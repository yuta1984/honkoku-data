"Tōkaidō Earthquake and Tsunami: Tales for Future Generations"

【Upper section, from left】
Fujikawa
After the earthquake, the tsunami destroyed more than half the houses, with many deaths.

Akasaka
Similarly, the tsunami swept away about 70% of the area, with an enormous number of deaths and injuries.

Goyu
The great earthquake caused many houses to collapse, with deaths and injuries. Afterward, the tsunami swept away more than half, again causing an enormous number of deaths and injuries.

Futagawa
After the earthquake, a tsunami came and destroyed more than half, with many deaths.

Shirasuka
Similarly, the tsunami destroyed about 60% of the area.

Arai
Half-destroyed. All boats large and small along the coast were swept away without exception. The checkpoint station was destroyed. Many deaths.

Maisaka
The great tsunami after the earthquake swept away the entire post town without exception.

Hamamatsu
The earthquake caused most houses to collapse, with many deaths.

【Lower section, from left】
Mitsuke
More than half was destroyed by earthquake and tsunami, but deaths were extremely few.

Fukuroi
The earthquake caused most houses to collapse, leading to fires and complete burning.

Kakegawa
The earthquake caused all houses to collapse, leading to fires and complete destruction without exception.

Nissaka
Though the earthquake was strong, there was only minor damage and it was safe.

Kanaya
The earthquake caused houses to collapse, leading to fires that burned 70-80% of the area.

Shimada
The earthquake caused many houses to collapse, and afterward the tsunami destroyed 70-80%.

Fujikawa
The great earthquake caused mountains upstream to collapse and create a dam, so the ferry crossing had no water and became a riverbed, with travelers crossing on foot.

Fujieda
The earthquake caused houses to collapse, leading to fires that destroyed half the area. Many deaths and injuries.

Okabe
After the earthquake, the tsunami left it half-destroyed.
