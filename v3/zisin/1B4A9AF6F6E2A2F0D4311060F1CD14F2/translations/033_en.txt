According to the Kaiki record of Kyōhō 9, there was once a blind man named Shikata Ichi who was a renowned sound-listener, and when he divined people's fortune and misfortune, he was never wrong. He would casually visit Ōyama frequently and attend court audiences, but in his later years he said: "I remember useless things and it troubles me greatly. Every day when I interact with people, I hear all their fortunes and misfortunes in my ears, and it is very noisy." His numerous achievements over time are countless. This Shikata rose early one morning, called his servant, and said: "What a terrible sound this is. With this sound, Kyoto will likely be destroyed. Quickly prepare some food and take me first toward Saga." With his usual efficiency, they immediately headed west to Saga, reached the foothills of Arashiyama at Ōi River plain, rested briefly, and he said: "The sound still hasn't improved—how frightening!
