　　Six: The Matter of the Ear Mound and the Collapse of Gojo Stone Bridge
In front of the Great Buddha Hall, to the south, there is what is called the Ear Mound. This dates back to when Taiko Hideyoshi conducted his Korean campaign—many foreign soldiers were killed by Japanese forces, and their heads were to be brought to Japan for Taiko's inspection. However, since the number of heads was so enormous, only the ears were cut off, packed in barrels, and transported. After Taiko conducted his inspection, [the owners of] these [ears] would become masterless spirits and ghosts of a foreign land. Though they were enemies, he felt pity for them, so he had them interred in a mound and erected a five-ring pagoda on top as an eternal memorial.
