This is a divine oracle. If you do not take precautions, you will be punished. Following the divine oracle, saying "Come now, let us take precautions and comfort the god's heart," they constructed huts with bamboo pillars and straw-mat roofing and moved into them. Their appearance was no different from a sudden gathering of beggars - truly unseemly sights.

At the banks of the Kamiya River in Nishi-no-kyō, when hot water was offered to the Emperor of Kitchi-chi, there was a divine oracle:
"How now, parishioners! In such troubled times you have given me hot water, not only easing my physical suffering but also refreshing my spirit - this brings me joy. So regarding this recent great earthquake that must have terrified you parishioners, with a carefree

heart I inquired of the gods of the great shrines. In ancient times too, when such shaking began, there were precedents of long periods of tremors. Nevertheless, while I think there should be no special calamities, I was told that even this is uncertain. Parishioners! Simply take precautions. Taking precautions means nothing special. From the eaves, the stones that hold down the roof tiles will fall. For small houses, use supporting props. If the ground seems likely to crack, lay down door boards. If you shut the doors and sliding screens tightly, they will warp and not open properly. Keep them open day and night. Young children will be frightened and suffer convulsions. Give them medicine for intestinal worms and soothe and comfort them. If houses seem about to collapse, flee quickly.
