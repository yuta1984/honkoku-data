When the clouds cleared, they rested their hearts a little, thinking that if they stayed for two or three days, the water might recede. However, after three days, another great flood occurred, one foot higher than the previous water. This was a great flood from the upper river, and most pitifully, the number of houses that were washed away at the place called Yogouchi was only fourteen houses, or about thirty buildings if you include the branch shrines. As for the number of people, it was thirty people total - eighteen from Yokouchi alone, two from Kamo no Ohara as we heard, making exactly twenty people, with the remaining ten from Furuichiba village. swept away in the time it takes to say "wah!" Even strong cattle - nine head died. They were bound to die. Looking at that scene, the water coming out from the valley scattered foam like balls and flowed faster than arrows - it was a great flood one jō and five shaku high, so there was no time to escape. In the time it takes to say "orya," they were swept away just like that. While grasping two children, one on the right and one on the left, trying to support themselves, they were gradually swept away, somehow trying to save the children.
