When the clouds scattered, they rested their hearts a little, thinking that if they stayed and lodged for two or three days, the water might recede. However, after three days another great flood occurred, one shaku (about 30cm) higher than the previous water. This was a great flood from the upper reaches of the river, and things were dire. In the place called Yokogōchi, there were only fourteen houses, or about thirty including the subsidiary shrines, with a population of thirty people. From Yokogōchi alone eighteen people, and from Kamo no Ohara two people, making exactly twenty people total. The remaining ten people - look at Furuichiba village. Thirty people were swept away in an instant with cries, and even the strong ones all died - they were bound to die. Looking at that scene, amazingly, the water rushing out from the valley scattered rocks like balls, flowing faster than arrows. It was a great torrent one jō five shaku high (about 4.5 meters), so there was no time to escape. In the time it takes to say "oriya," people were swept away as they were. While grasping two children, one on the right and one on the left, trying to hold their ground, they were gradually swept away, desperately wanting to save the children.
