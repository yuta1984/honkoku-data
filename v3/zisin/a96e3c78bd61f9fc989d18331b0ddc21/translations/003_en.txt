The collapsed Nakajima embankment - it was around the eighth hour of the night (approximately 2 AM), a time when sleep was deep, so people were completely unaware even in their dreams. When they heard the sound of water, thinking "this is strange," everyone was watching from inside their homes when suddenly the water surged. Its force was even more violent than the sound of thunder, and in an instant the water rose higher than the eaves, making the houses appear to sway as if they might collapse at any moment. Therefore, women and children cried out frantically, screaming "help us, help us," but thinking that one's own life was more important than others', and that others would only be a hindrance, people stripped naked and crawled out, jumping from rooftops onto boats. Amid the dirty waves and painful terror, barely escaping with their lives, they rowed boats to Hansha and moored them sideways. In the house rows of the four villages of Mine, people who would normally be dressed up when guests came - with white powder, layered kimono, and large obi sashes in elegant fashion - were now shamefully clad only in yukata wrapping cloth or loincloths, yet caring nothing about such things, they simply stood beside each other. The rest suddenly vanished, so let the stormy wind wipe away the tears.
