The collapsed Nakajima embankment gave way around the eighth hour of the night (around 2 AM), which was the time when everyone was sleeping, so people were unaware even in their dreams. When they heard the sound of water and thought "this is strange," everyone was tidying up inside their homes when suddenly the water surged. Its force was even more violent than the sound of thunder, and in the time it takes to say "oh no," the water rose higher than the eaves, making the houses sway as if they would collapse at any moment. Women and children cried out frantically, shouting "help, help," but everyone was more concerned with their own affairs than others'.

[Left page]
Saying it would be a hindrance, people stripped naked and crawled out, jumping from rooftops to boats. Dirty girls and those in terror barely escaped with their lives, rowing boats to Sakasunadome and mooring them there. In the house rows of the four villages of Mine, people who would normally receive guests dressed in white powder, layered kimono, and large obi sashes with graceful bearing, now stood only in bathrobe-like garments or loincloths, not knowing shame or anything else, just standing there saying "Oh, what a bother." Afterwards, overcome with tears, the storm wind blows away their tears.
