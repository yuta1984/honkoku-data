There are various songs, but setting those aside, the forty petitioners (Se-Anje)
The lords of Bizen and Bichu who handle the front barrier of the broken embankment
have sand transported and earthwork bags used, with tens of thousands
of laborers and workers employed daily. The officials
know nothing at all. With the good embankments of both groups requiring great expense, immediately
the seasonal payments become a great burden. With this year's great storms after three years,
and poor harvests in countries everywhere during this crowded year, rice prices are high.
Even without such expenses being needed, the times are already

bad and troubling, yet the magistrate who employs the laborers
yawns, saying "Well then, some tea sweets for lunch,"
and "Let's offer some sake," indulging in luxury and splendor.
When building embankments, if one is careless and negligent just once
and rushes the work, it will face another great flood. If it breaks this time, not even salt will flow.
If there are no embankments, then "Report this! Increase the workers!"
Not knowing at all the hardships below, to the mountain group: "Hurry the separation!
If there are no earthwork bags, then give up!" - and so on endlessly.
