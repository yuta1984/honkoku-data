Looking around, even the rural market towns have grown accustomed to luxury, and Kurashiki town in particular throws away even the finest Tosa bonito flakes after one use for cooking, discarding the first and second broths without eating them. They re-cook food that has already been cooked once - what absurd extravagance this is! Do they not know that long ago, Lord Saimyōji Tokiyori, though he was the master of Japan, drank sake with only miso as accompaniment? When mere townspeople of limited standing show no fear of heaven and indulge in luxury, heaven's blessings constrain their lives. If we trace this to its source, it all stems from everyone's arrogant behavior. If such luxury were restrained, there would be no such future hardships, the common people would live in peace, the five grains would flourish, descendants would prosper, and it would become the foundation of the people's well-being. Cut through worldly attachments with virtue - thus the record of high waters comes to an end.
