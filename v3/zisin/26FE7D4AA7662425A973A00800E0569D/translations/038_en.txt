○Same year, 4th month, 26th day: Wasa Daihachi of the Kishū domain household achieved the highest record in the tōshi-ya (arrow-shooting contest) in Kyoto at age 20, with 13,000 total arrows shot and 8,133 arrows passing through the target.

1. Jōkyō 4th year: Regarding the domain disturbance of Lord Arima Saemon-no-suke. Last year in the 9th month, 1,100 peasants armed with bows and firearms withdrew to the territory of Lord Akizuki Nagato-no-kami. Due to this, an official investigation was conducted and punishments were imposed:
Crucifixion <<annotation: ringleaders |>> 2 peasants; Death sentence: 2 peasants; Exile <<annotation: including scribes and masterless samurai |>> 6 peasants
Banishment <<annotation: district magistrate |>> Kajita Jūrozaemon <<annotation: local official |>> Ōshima Kyūemon. As a result of the above, the domain was confiscated and reassigned.

○Same year: The chamberlain of Lord Kōfu, Inaba Naizen and his son, and inspector Kosuga Jin'zaemon and his son were ordered to commit seppuku for their crimes. However, since there was no official announcement, the house elders were summoned and told: "Regarding Naizen, as he was someone appointed to the布衣 rank, there should be an official announcement." While all house elders should be ordered into confinement, this would cause difficulties, so it was decreed by the rōjū that one monthly duty elder should be ordered into confinement. When Lord Kōfu also inquired about refraining from activities, he was told this was unnecessary.

1. Jōkyō 5th year, dragon year, 9th month, 3rd day: During the visit to Sannō Shrine, at a townhouse within the great torii, a peasant from Katsuyama village in Echizen Province named Tamaki Matazaemon submitted a petition along the procession route. He was originally a peasant from Lord Matsudaira Hyōbu-dayū's new lands who had previously engaged in litigation in Echizen, and due to improper conduct was banished from that domain. Since his petition was not accepted at that time, both Matazaemon and the townsman who lodged him were ordered to prison. At that time, Lord Sugiura Ichizaemon, the ashigaru commander who was advance guard for Sannō, was ordered into confinement, and one group leader and five ashigaru were ordered into house arrest.

○Same year: The assistance for Chisokuin Temple construction was completed, and Lord Matsudaira Wakasa-no-kami and his retainers were granted rewards.

○Same year, 10th month: Domain reassignment with castle lands granted - Lord Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami

○Same year, 4th month: The sale of charred salamander powder was prohibited.
