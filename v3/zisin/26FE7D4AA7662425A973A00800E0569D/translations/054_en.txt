○Same year, May: <<New grant of 15,000 koku>> <<Lord Hōki-no-kami's younger brother, Lord Matsudaira Tatsunosuke>>

○Same year: Lord Mito Saishō's stipend was consolidated to 350,000 koku. This was because the portions of 20,000 koku each that had been granted privately to Lord Daigaku-no-kami and Lord Harima-no-kami were returned, resulting in the above.

○Same year: Lord Bishū Santomo passed away

○Same year, December 6th: Mito Kōmon Mitsukuni passed away, honored with the name Genyoshi-kō. He is also called the second lord of Mito, Lord Seizan.

○Same year: 3,000 koku increase making 10,000 koku total - Lord Kuroda Buzen-no-kami

1. Genroku 14 (1701), year of the snake, March 14th: Today there was an audience with imperial messengers, court nobles, and court officials. At that time, in the palace, Lord Asano Takumi-no-kami wounded Lord Kira Kōzuke-no-suke, a kōke official, with a blade. Lord Kōzuke-no-suke sustained injuries. Metsuke Kajikawa Zenbei restrained them and separated both parties. Lord Takumi-no-kami was placed in the custody of Lord Tamura Ukyō-daibu and ordered to commit seppuku the same day. The details of this incident are thoroughly recorded in various chronicles, so I omit them here.

○Same year, transfer: <<At original stipend to Takada in Echigo>> Lord Toda Noto-no-kami; <<10,000 koku increase>> Lord Matsudaira Ukyō-daibu; <<5,000 koku increase making 10,000 koku total>> Lord Honda Hōki-no-kami

○Same year: The Buddha statue from Zenkōji Temple in Shinshū came to Edo for special exhibition

○Same year: The petition by Ryūshōin for fundraising for the Great Buddha of Nanto was approved, and it was announced that daimyo should contribute one bu of gold per 10,000 koku

○Same year: Exile <<Goshoinban>> Lord Yamaoka Saburōbei. This was due to his improper guidance regarding Lord Sakuma Iori's urgent adoption request, which was deemed inappropriate conduct.

○Same year: <<Shingobanshu>> Lord Machino Sakunojō was placed in the custody of Lord Maeda Uneme due to his lack of filial piety toward his father

○Same year, January: This year being the 500th anniversary of Lord Nitta Yoshishige's death, 300 pieces of silver were granted to Nitta Daikōin Temple, and memorial services were held.

<<○Same year, February 6th: Great fire in the castle town of Kuwana in Ise Province. The entire castle interior burned down and the keep fell. Over 900 townhouses and more than half of the samurai residences were destroyed by fire>> [This line in red ink]

○Same year: Fukagawa Sanjūsangendō burned down. The temple keeper Kyūemon petitioned, and it was announced that daimyo with 10,000 koku or more should contribute five ryō of gold per 10,000 koku. Sanjūsangendō was originally in Asakusa and burned down; this time it was rebuilt in Fukagawa. The government provided timber.
