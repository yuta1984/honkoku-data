1. In the first month of Enpō 8 (Year of the Monkey, 1680), there was a great fire in Kagoshima in Satsuma Province, and 3,500 houses were destroyed.

○ On the 21st day of the 4th month of the same year, at the castle, Toraya Eikan's puppet performance of "Bonten-koku" in six acts was viewed by the shogun.

○ In the same year, books prepared by Lord Mito were presented: one volume of "Kuge Bunin Ketsu," ten volumes of "Ichidai Yōki," and thirty-three volumes of "Fusō Jūyōshū."

○ On the 6th day of the 5th month of the same year, [someone] became the adopted heir of Lord Tatebayashi Saishō.

○ In the 4th month of the same year, Lord Ōkubo Kaga-no-kami served tea at Ninomaru, and the decorations throughout the reception rooms were beyond description.

○ On the 8th day of the 5th month of the same year, the shogun passed away. His posthumous title was Gan'yū-in-den, and he was interred at Ueno. On the 14th of the same month, he was laid to rest at Ueno and memorial services were held. The administrators were Lords Ōkubo Kaga-no-kami, Itakura Iwami-no-kami, Matsudaira Yamashiro-no-kami, and Ōoka Gozaemon. The chief priest was the imperial prince of Bishamondō. ○ In the 8th month of the same year, the shogunal appointment was made.
