○Same year　Regarding Mori Tonomo's matter, although he had requested to become the adopted son of Mori Hōki-no-kami, due to his increasingly improper conduct, he was placed under the custody of Mori Mimasaka-no-kami. Furthermore, Hōki-no-kami's improper adoption request was deemed inexcusable, and therefore he was not granted succession to the family name. It was ordered that this decision be communicated to Mimasaka-no-kami.

○Same year　When Lord Date Mimasaka-no-kami was passing through Doki-chō, he encountered Lord Oka Hachirōbei from Lord Ogasawara Nagato-no-kami's unit, and the foot soldiers in the advance guard pushed Hachirōbei aside. When Hachirōbei drew his sword, a large group rushed him, seized his sword, and forced their way through. Hachirōbei pursued from behind but was not admitted through the gate. Since Hachirōbei was on duty, this matter was reported to the castle. From the castle, unit commanders, senior inspectors, and inspectors came and first had Hachirōbei return to his residence. Later there was an investigation, and Lord Mimasaka-no-kami was ordered into house confinement. Lord Hachirōbei was also confined for his improper handling of the unreasonable disruption of the procession. Subsequently, Lord Mimasaka-no-kami was recalled by directive from his main family.

○Same year　Eitai Bridge was completed. Length: 110 ken, width: 3 ken 1 shaku 5 sun. It was formerly called the Great Ferry of Fukagawa.
