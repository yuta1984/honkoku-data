Earthquakes do not shake the land through the conflict of yin and yang, or of water and fire, but are simply the movement of the earth. Moreover, as the principle of "earth generating metal" (土生金, *do shō kin*) holds, metal too moves and circulates along with the earth. As the common saying goes, "After rain, the ground hardens" — so too will the earthquake subside, bringing renewal and rectification to the world. The five grains will yield ever more abundant harvests, prosperity will flow down to all the common people, and the reign will be celebrated as "Manzairaku" — sung and cheered again and again with great joy.

　Settled as a land unmoved beneath the heavens,
　　the rough metal-bearing earth circulates its blessings.
