On the eighteenth day, heavy rain fell incessantly from the morning, with thunder and earthquakes intermingled, and the rain poured down as if washing away cart axles. In no time, the waters of the Kamo River, the Horikawa, the Nishiōin River, and the Katsura River all burst forth at once, flooding into the houses along the riverbanks, causing great commotion throughout the area. Here there is a small stream called the Otowa River, fed by the waters falling from the Otowa Falls at Kiyomizudera Temple in the eastern part of the capital, which ultimately flows into the Kamo River. The waters of this stream rose enormously, and the areas along this waterway — the Fushimi Highway, Sayamachi, Toiyamachi, and their surroundings — were especially struck by severe flooding. Water rose more than one shaku (approximately 30 cm) above the floor levels of people's homes. In the vicinity there was a large household that traded in oil, and water flooded into the oil jars buried in the ground, causing the whole area to reek of oil beyond all description. After the waters receded, oil flowed into nearby wells, leaving the residents utterly at a loss. It was also reported that the veranda (corridor) of Kiyomizudera Temple collapsed at this time. These events, too, were completely unprecedented occurrences, and everyone remarked, "Surely this must be the end of our misfortunes."
