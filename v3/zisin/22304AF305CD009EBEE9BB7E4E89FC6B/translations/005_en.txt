They lit horse lanterns and visited nearby friends and relatives to check on each other's well-being. It was quite remarkable that dogs would crawl up to the pillows of those sleeping wherever they had fallen. All the well water had become muddy. Those who had calmed down somewhat boiled tea on the main road, cooked rice, drew stream water, and finally managed to get food down their throats, though their hearts remained unsettled with the continuous rumbling and shaking. In each neighborhood, people strictly made their rounds for fire prevention, striking wooden clappers and split bamboo. From somewhere, elderly people came and offered magical charms. The song went:

　　Though it may loosen, the keystone shall never be dislodged
　　　　As long as the god of Kashima exists

Everyone copied this down and pasted it on doorway pillars or main support pillars. Reverently, they placed purification talismans from Amaterasu Omikami Shrine on their heads and tucked secret magical texts into their topknots. Especially for those caring for elderly people, the sick, and children, their anxiety was beyond description. Among them, some were crushed and fell under collapsing houses, others were trapped by objects and pressed against walls.
