The voices of those suffering in distress were clamorous, and as we heard and saw doctors rushing about from west to east, we were bewildered and greatly anxious. That night too we waited for the eastern sky to brighten, and on the dawning third day, with no change, we prayed for the trembling to ease even a little. People's complexions had changed, and what they called "pale faces" in common parlance, trembling as they were, was quite understandable. We simply took great care regarding fire prevention and thieves, remaining vigilant day and night without letting our guard down. There were those who spread strange theories and false rumors, and some were arrested. Among them, the villains who engaged in theft faced divine retribution right before their eyes. Throughout the towns, water pumps were set up along the rows of houses, water was poured on roofs, and it was quite remarkable to see men who normally wouldn't engage in rough work going barefoot to carry water. Those who owned groves carried food to the groves or fields, and some led elderly people and children by the hand.
