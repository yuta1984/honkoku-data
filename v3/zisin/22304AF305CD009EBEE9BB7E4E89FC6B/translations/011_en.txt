There were many such instances of soil sliding off storehouses, and signs warning "Caution on the road" (due to leaning buildings) could be seen here and there.

On the fourteenth day, there was quite strong shaking around midnight, and again rumbling at the seventh hour. This day was the seasonal settlement day (for collecting debts from credit sales), yet the debt collectors seemed more subdued than usual — how curious. On the fifteenth day, rain began to fall a little, and amid it, the rumbling continued from time to time without ceasing. By now people had grown accustomed to the earthquakes, and though they mingled with others in a distracted manner, their hearts were otherwise — they must have been praying inwardly. Rain continued on the sixteenth day as well.

Since this great calamity (the earthquake) some days past, roof tiles had been sliding off, and carpenters, tile workers, plasterers, helpers and the like were stretched thin across numerous jobs, unable to keep up, and many households suffering from rain leaking through damaged roofs could be seen. Moreover, in small houses built beside storehouses, many people were so frightened of the soil sliding down upon them that they worried themselves into spending night after night sleeping elsewhere. On the seventeenth day, rain was still falling heavily; around the eighth hour there was a brief clearing, and after dusk, a little lightning was seen to the west.
