Tengyō 1st year, 4th month, 15th day (mizunoe-tatsu): The earth shook violently, and all walls and fences
in the capital were destroyed. At the Bureau of Palace Kitchens, buildings collapsed and four people were crushed to death. The Bureau of Onmyō divined and reported
that there would be disturbances in the east and west.
22nd day (hinoto-tori): The Kamo Festival was suspended due to the defilement caused by deaths at the Bureau of Palace Kitchens. 
29th day (hinoe-uma): During the day there were rumbling sounds that continued repeatedly without stopping. From the 15th day until
today, earthquakes continued without rest for fifteen days.
5th month, 22nd day (tsuchinoe-tatsu): The era name was changed to Tengyō 1st year. This was done as a precaution against calamitous fortune, earthquakes, and
dynastic change. 26th day (mizunoe-saru): There was an earthquake. 6th month, 3rd day
(tsuchinoe-tora): The earth shook violently.
10th month, 9th day: Imperial offerings were made to Usa Hachiman Shrine to pray for the elimination of earthquake disasters.
Also, court rank was bestowed upon the Deputy Grand Shrine Administrator. 21st day (kanoe-uma): The earth shook frequently.

Tenryaku 3rd year (949 CE)
○Nihon Kiryaku - Emperor Murakami
Tenryaku 3rd year, 1st month, 26th day: [The Emperor] commanded the Chief Ritualist Ōnakatomi no Yorimoto to seclude himself at the Jingikan (Department of Shinto Affairs) and pray concerning
heavenly anomalies, earthquakes, military conflicts, epidemics, and annual harvests.
