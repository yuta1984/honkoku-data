5th month, day of Wugo: There was an earthquake. Day of Jiwei: There was an earthquake, and temples throughout the capital were ordered to recite the Suvarṇaprabhāsottama Sūtra for one period of seven days. Day of Gengshen: There was an earthquake. Day of Jiazi: There was an earthquake. Day of Yichou: There was an earthquake, and the four temples of Daian-ji, Yakushi-ji, Gangō-ji, and Kōfuku-ji were ordered to read the Mahāsaṃnipāta Sūtra for three periods of seven days (21 days). Day of Bingyin: There was an earthquake. Day of Dingmao: There was an earthquake. Day of Guiyou: There was an earthquake. Day of Yihai: There was an earthquake. This month earthquakes were extraordinarily frequent, with ground splitting in many places and water springs gushing forth.

16th year of Enryaku era
　○Ruijū Kokushi　《Section on Disasters and Anomalies》
　　Emperor Kanmu, 16th year of Enryaku, 8th month, day of Dingmao: There was an earthquake and violent wind, and many of the left and right ward gates and people's houses collapsed.

4th year of Tenchō era
　○Ruijū Kokushi　《Section on Disasters and Anomalies》
　　Emperor Junna　4th year of Tenchō, autumn, 7th month, day of Xinwei: There was a great earthquake and many buildings collapsed. In one day there was one great earthquake and seven or eight small tremors. Day of Guiyou: The ground motion did not cease. At the hour of the Boar there was a great earthquake, and every earthquake was accompanied by sound. Day of Jiaxu: There were tremors. Day of Yihai: There were earthquakes twice. Day of Wuyin: There were great earthquakes twice. Day of Gengchen: There was an earthquake. Day of Xinsi: There was an earthquake. Day of Guiwei at the hour of the Rabbit there was an earthquake, at the fourth quarter of the Horse hour there was an earthquake, and at midnight there was also an earthquake.
