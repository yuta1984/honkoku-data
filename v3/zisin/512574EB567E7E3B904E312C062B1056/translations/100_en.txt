【Right page】
- Yamanaka-shinmachi (a place with about 1,000 houses): collapsed, burned, and swept away, with 100 deaths. Until the following 25th day, water gradually accumulated, and sixteen villages upstream became submerged. Near Kumero Bridge in Minochi village, the water depth was approximately over 100 jō, the bridge had already floated up and drifted about 1.5 ri upstream, where it was moored at Hokari village. On the 13th day of the 4th month, the ponded water of the Sai River burst and caused flooding. The affected villages are as follows:

- Shimo-ōoka village: 12 houses swept away - Same group, Yasui village: 4 houses swept away
- Same group, Kawase village: 40 houses swept away - Wada village: completely swept away, bridge timber framework completely swept away
- Chihara group: completely swept away - Same group, Hongō village: completely swept away - Motohara Hongō: half swept away

The area around the above continues to be in a state of flooding even now.

【Left page】
- Shimo-ichiba village: completely swept away - Ri-hokari village: only about 5 storehouses visible, the rest swept away
- Shinmachi: about 100 storehouses visible, the rest underwater. From about the middle section downward still remains underwater
- Takefusa village and Taira village: mostly swept away
- Kamijō village Unsōji, Anyōji, and town group: completely swept away
- Minochi village Mushōdai group and Heisui group: completely swept away
- Yoshihara village Hashiba group: completely swept away
- Mizu village Hongō: about 50 houses swept away
- Hikuma village and Taira village: 5 houses swept away
