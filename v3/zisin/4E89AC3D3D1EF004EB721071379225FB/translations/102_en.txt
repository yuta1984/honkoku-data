In Ueda, the town magistrate Shirase Shirōzaemon fortified the castle town, and at a place called Kagagawa Bridge, one ri east of Ueda, he stationed the commanders Kimura Kanji, Yokota Chikiuchi, and Sugaya Hachirō—three men in total—along with seventy samurai retainers and one hundred fifty ashigaru foot soldiers. From a place called Nagaoka to Ōya village in the area of Niō Kawara, they began capturing prisoners alive from the ninth hour of the night on the 5th day of the 10th month. Even these formidable villains became confused and lost their way, fleeing and hiding in bushes here and under tree shadows there, but between the Komoro and Ueda forces, forty were captured by Komoro and forty-two by Ueda's forces. The disturbance was settled at the fourth hour of the morning on the 6th day.

Afterwards, upon investigation, those whose excuses were deemed acceptable were immediately released. The others who had stolen household goods, clothing, and other items were immediately thrown into prison. Thus, from Edo, the town magistrates Makino Ōsuminokami and Magaribuchi Kainokami, with their combined forces of about sixty men, were to capture those involved in the disturbances in Shinshū, Jōshū, and Yashū provinces. Four men from Makino's group in upper Shinshū and three from Magaribuchi's group—these seven men—established meeting places at two locations: Wakabayashi Jirōzaemon's and Hayashiya Heiemon's in Oiwake post station in Saku district.
