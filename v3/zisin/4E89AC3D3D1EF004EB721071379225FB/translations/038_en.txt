Record of the Great Earthquake in Shinano Province
　The ten districts of Shinshū: Saku, Ina, Takai, Hanishina, Chiisagata
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Minochi, Chikuma, Sarashina, Suwa, Azumi

This was fundamentally an extraordinary disturbance of heaven and earth, a battle between yin and yang. In the heavens it manifests as thunder and rain, and when lurking in the earth it reveals itself as earthquakes. Even with the protection of gods and buddhas, it is difficult to ward off such disasters effectively. Here, on the night of March 24th in the 4th year of Kōka (1847), year of the sheep, from the hour of the boar (around 10 PM), a great earthquake suddenly struck from around Minochi District in Shinshū. Mountains collapsed, water gushed forth, houses fell, fires broke out with flames burning like red lotus blossoms, and the loss of human life and horses was enormous. Relatives in distant provinces inquired about safety, and upon learning of deaths, their grief and pity were extraordinary.
