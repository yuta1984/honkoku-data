Due to such prices, in the Kawakami region as well as the areas of Hotchi, Nashizawa, and Omogae, there was absolutely no way to make ends meet, and many abandoned their homes and fled. Regarding straw rice cakes, notices were circulated to all villages under the jurisdiction of Komoro and Mikage domains. The method was as follows: first, soak straw properly in water to remove the lye, thoroughly remove and wash away the sand, cut the ears, and from the root end

finely chop it, steam it, dry it thoroughly, grind it with a parching mortar, make it fine and sieve it. Into one shō of this powder, add about two gō of rice powder, steam it like proper rice cakes, or boil it and add salt or miso to eat. If there is no rice powder available, kudzu powder, bracken powder, or red beans mixed in will also do. Since the Irikata area prepared and ate these cakes, straw and similar materials were exhausted around Kaijiri. Consequently, there was no feed for horses, so they fed pine needles to the horses. Although loans were provided from Hiraga four times since winter, the grain prices were so high that it was difficult to sustain livelihoods.
