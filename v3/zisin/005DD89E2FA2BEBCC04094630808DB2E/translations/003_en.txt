On the night of October 2nd in the 2nd year of Ansei (1855), year of the Rabbit, after the hour of the Boar had passed, a great earthquake suddenly struck. Fires broke out from collapsed houses in various places. The damage to daimyo residences, temples, townhouses, and storehouses throughout Edo was countless. Therefore, it is impossible to fully describe with brush and paper when informing relatives in distant provinces or business associates. Since I have recorded in detail all the collapsed houses, fires, and other damages without omission, please examine this carefully.

　　North of Nihonbashi

North of Nihonbashi: Takaramachi 1st, 2nd, and 3rd districts, Honcho-dori, around Surugacho, around Isecho, Jikken-dana, up to Honishicho, around Imagawa Bridge, around Kodenmacho - all suffered major damage. Eastward to Odenmacho-dori, same severe damage. The street, Hatago-cho, Abura-cho, Shio-cho, Bakuro-cho, Yokoyama-cho, Ryogoku Hirokoji, Yanagihara embankment - in this area about 300 people died instantly, with countless injured.

The County Magistrate's residence, Toshima-cho, around Kyuemon-cho, Hashimoto-cho, Benkei Bridge, Otamagaike, Lord Ichihashi's residence, Kanda Koyanagi-cho, around the rice storehouse, similarly Matsuda-cho, Shin-ishi-cho, up to Imagawa Bridge - all severely damaged. In this area, over 400 people died instantly, with an unknown number of injured. Within Sujikai Gate: Lord Sakai's residence, Lord Aoyama Shimotsuke-no-kami, Lord Doi Noto-no-kami, Lord Matsudaira Saemon-no-jo, Lord Inaba Tango-no-kami, Lord Tsuchiya Uneme-no-sho - from here to Mikawa-cho, all severely damaged.

The entire West Kanda area severely damaged, all storehouses heavily damaged. Also around Hama-cho, various lord residences damaged. Around Matsushima-cho severely damaged, with about 80 instant deaths in this area, countless injured.
