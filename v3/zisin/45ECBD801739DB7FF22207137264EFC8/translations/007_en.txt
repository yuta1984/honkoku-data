Item: From the 10th of last month, due to the Zenkoji Temple exhibition, an enormous number of pilgrims from various provinces stayed overnight. At one inn alone, 1,240 people stayed, of whom 1,200 paid their lodging fees while the remaining 40 were accommodated without charge. During this time of such crowding, the great earthquake struck, and Zenkoji town was devastated by the earthquake collapse, and moreover fires broke out, resulting in countless deaths. Approximately 3,000 pilgrims who had taken refuge in the Nyorai Hall safely evacuated, but

their clothing and travel money were all lost in the fires, and those from distant provinces were in particularly dire straits. Only the Nyorai Hall and the main gate remained, while the rest of the town center was completely burned down, as we have heard. Regarding the death toll, it is extremely difficult to determine, but rumor has it that in Zenkoji town alone, there may have been 20,000 to 30,000 instant deaths. It is rumored that of the town's registered residents, only about 200 to 300 remained on the morning of the 25th, though this may include those who fled, so the approximate numbers cannot be determined. In any case, it was a tremendous disaster.
