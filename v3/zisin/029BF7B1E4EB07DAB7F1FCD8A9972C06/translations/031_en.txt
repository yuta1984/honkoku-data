Title: Map of Fire-Damaged Areas from the Edo Earthquake

On the night of October 2nd, Ansei 2 (1855), at the fourth hour, after the Great Edo Earthquake, fires broke out in various locations. We surveyed these areas and documented them in detail. The earthquake's progression is illustrated in the map with color coding and marks indicating major, medium, and minor damage levels.

①Shin Yoshiwara Edo-cho 1st and 2nd districts, Kyo-cho 1st and 2nd districts, Kaku-cho, Yanagi-ya-cho, Fushimi-cho, and Naka-no-cho were completely burned. Fifty ken remained, but not a single storehouse in the quarter survived. ②Fire broke out from the Doteshita-ta-cho area. The northern part of Yamakawa-cho partially remained. Takemon Umamichi, Kita-shin-cho, Saruwaka-cho from 3rd to 1st district with three theaters, and the eastern side of Higashi-shindo remained. Yamano-yado, Katakawa, and Hanakawado had minimal damage before the fire was contained. Yabu-no-uchi, Iroha tenements, and about ten temples were burned. ③All pleasure houses in Kozukappara-cho were destroyed along with their lodgings. About half a district near Imado Bridge burned, and about half a district of Hashiba burned. ④Fire started from Komagata Yokocho, burning both sides of Komagata, Suwa-cho, Kurofune-cho on both sides, Miyoshi-cho, and was contained at Omaya riverside. ⑤Half of the area in front of Higashi Gomon ruins at Kikuya Bridge corner burned, and another area burned up to the temple front. ⑥Both sides of Shitaya Sakamoto 1st, 2nd, and 3rd districts burned. ⑦Fire broke out from the middle of Shitaya Kami-no-hirokoji, completely destroying Daimon-cho, Ueno-cho 1st and 2nd districts, Ito Matsusakaya, and nine storefronts with storehouses. Doho-cho, Shin-Kuromon-cho, Ueno retainer residences on Onarimichi, Inoue residence partially burned, Kurumasaka-cho, Choja-cho 1st and 2nd districts, Shitaya-cho residential area, samurai residences - approximately fifty buildings burned. ⑧On Onari-dori, Ishikawa Tonomo-no-kami and Kuroda Buzen-no-kami residences suffered secondary fires. ⑨Fire in Shitaya Ike-no-hata Kaya-cho 1st district was contained at the boundary near Inari shrine.

From here upward, the locations where the government established relief shelters are as follows:
- Saiwai Bridge inner and outer fields
- Asakusa Hirokoji  
- Fukagawa Seaside Shinden
- Same area, Hachiman shrine grounds
- Ueno Yamashita fire prevention area
- Toeizan relief shelter
- Same area, Yamashita field

Reports from nearby highways and villages:
Tokaido: Kanagawa, Hodogaya vicinity, Enoshima, Kamakura, Misaki, Uraga area. Nakasendo: up to Takasaki in Joshu. Along these routes, there are places where the ground split and water gushed out. Nikko-do: up to Utsunomiya. Major collapses occurred around Soka and Kawaguchi. Koshu-do: up to Hachioji with no particular incidents. Mito highway: up to Tsuchiura area, with some route collapses. Shimousa: up to Narita and Yokaichiba areas. Kazusa and Boshu areas had significant incidents.

Osaka experienced a major earthquake on September 28th causing severe damage. Owari, Mikawa, Mino, and Shinano provinces shook simultaneously with Edo. Other provinces had no particular incidents.
