Not only could they not eat meals, but that night too they fled a total of five times. Each time they fled, they carried bundles, worried about their parent's illness and felt distressed, and every time the earthquake rumbled, they would stand up and sit down wondering "what should we do," unable to sleep even for a moment. They spent the night outdoors in a wheat field beside where a portable shrine was placed, and even when they finally returned here, they could not enter their own home. Today too, all day long carrying luggage on their backs and holding furoshiki bundles, they fled a total of six times. Of course they could not eat meals, and without even having the usual sweets and fruits, they finally decided to make this their temporary dwelling.

However, they prepared themselves mentally that if water should come rushing in, they would abandon their belongings and flee. "I entrust these three items to you: the protective box, candles, and whatever coins we have on hand - about 100 mon. Even if further disasters occur and we must flee, no matter which direction we escape to, these are indispensable items," they were told. Understanding this, they shouldered the furoshiki bundle as a shoulder bag, tied their sandals securely with cord, and dozed leaning against the hut's enclosure just as they were. Finally...
