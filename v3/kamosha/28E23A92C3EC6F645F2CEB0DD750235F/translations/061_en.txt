Two hundred fifty me were accepted as agreed upon.

1. Today, a letter was sent to the Imperial Medical Office along with fifty hiki of copper coins requesting the preparation of toso (New Year's spiced sake) and byakusan (white powder) for use in the temporary shrine relocation. Three packages arrived immediately.

25th Day - Clear

1. The attendant monk Gyokusen-in came and brought a written schedule for the temporary relocations. What he stated at this meeting was that according to precedent, notifications would be submitted to the Imperial Messengers, Magistrates, and various offices. The record is as follows:
　　This month, 29th day, hour of the Dog - Seishin-ji Temple - temporary relocation performed
　　Same day, hour of the Rat - Jingu-ji Temple - temporary relocation performed  
　　3rd month, 2nd day, hour of the Dog - Honorable Sutra Reading Hall - temporary relocation performed
　　Same day, hour of the Boar - Small Sutra Hall - temporary relocation performed
　　The above matters are respectfully reported

　　　2nd month (Year of the Rooster)　　Temple Community
　　　To the Honorable Shrine

1. Today at the hour of the Dragon, regarding the dismantling of the temporary shrine, three-sun offerings were prepared and presented to the Main Shrine, temporary shrine, Kataoka Shrine, etc., by the duty officers.

27th Day - Clear

1. Today, temporary relocations of Shingu, Yamao, Fujiwo, Kawao and other shrines (at hours of Rooster, Dog, and Boar). For this, Head Administrator Jin Kenju, Ho Kan, roasting section chief, Ken-to and others appeared at the shrine precincts.

1. The same night, Goro-taro, a laborer from Hibarihara, was assigned fire watch duties and made rounds.

1. After breakfast, shrine treasure keepers Ho Reigen and Ho Seisoku went to the council chamber to prepare the necessary items for tonight's temporary shrine relocation.

1. Now, at the hours of Rooster, Dog, and Boar, temporary relocations of Shingu, Yamao, Fujiwo, Kawao and other shrines were performed. Accordingly, daily offerings to the Main Shrine and others continued as usual, with the duty shrine priest Kataoka Negi Kōkyū officiating.
