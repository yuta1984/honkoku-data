　　　　　　　Shrine priests wear ceremonial robes with upper ties【red】
1. On the kinoe-ne day, the ritual of lighting sacred lamps. The shrine priests
   go to the sacred hall, mark their seats, gather pine, wrap lamp grass and
   pass it to the shrine servants (jinni). When each has finished passing it, they announce the sacred procession and go before the sacred presence.
The shrine priests participate, light the sacred lamps from the hands of the shrine servants and present them to the various shrines.
   Before the sacred presence, the assistant head priest's celebrant serves as officiant, sets up the sacred offering table, and in order
starting with the shrine administrator, they make presentations. Afterwards they go to the connecting hall. Going to the minor shrines
   is as usual. At the lower shrines, celebrants all participate and make presentations.
