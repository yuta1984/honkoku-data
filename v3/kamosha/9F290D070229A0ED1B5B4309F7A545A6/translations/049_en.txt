Kimihisa
(Also known as Mochihisa)
　　　　　Appointed in the 11th month of Ōei 18 (1411), dismissed on the 24th day of the 9th month of Ōei 19 (1412). Served for ten months. This was due to neglecting the Shukuhō Setsuyō Shinji (celebratory ritual) on the 3rd day of the 1st month of that year. The head priest (shōshuku) at that time was Shigeki.

Hirohisa
(Son of Otohisa)
　　　　　Appointed on the 24th day of the 9th month of Ōei 19 (1412), promoted from gon-negi (assistant head priest) over Shōnegi Toyohira. At this time, Anshi-no-shō estate was attached as shrine administration funding because the rice allowance had fallen into arrears. Also, Ichihara field was attached to the shrine administration.
　　　　　In Ōei 20 (1413), there was a reconstruction of Ōta Shrine, with expenses covered by provincial taxes and building taxes. The ceremonial beginning was on the last day of the 9th month of the same year. The shrine administrator was Iio Mino-no-kami Nyūdō.

(Kibune-negi, fourth son of Yahei) There was a conflict between the peasants of Ichihara and the peasants of Hataeda that escalated to armed conflict.
Toyohira
(Son of Head Priest Otohira)

　　　　　Appointed on the 21st day of the 7th month of Ōei 21 (1414), dismissed on the 24th day of the 5th month of Ōei 26 (1419). Served for five years. He was replaced because on the 15th day of the 1st month of the same year, he overturned the sacred rice porridge offering in the inner sanctuary. Additionally, the shrine community reported that rituals during his tenure deviated from precedent.
　　　　　During this time, in Ōei 21 (1414), there was a relocation ceremony for Ōta Shrine. There was also a dispute regarding the boundary between Kibune and Futase-yama.

Hirohisa
(Second appointment)
　　　　　Reappointed on the 1st day of the 6th month of Ōei 26 (1419), seven days after Toyohira's dismissal. This was due to petitions from the assistant head priest and other shrine officials. Dismissed on the 21st day of the same month. Served for twenty days. This was due to imperial petition from the Muromachi Palace.
