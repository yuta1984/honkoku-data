Also, kemari (kickball) was performed in the imperial palace garden. The jōkyū was served by head priest Norihisa, the maritokunin role was performed by Yoshinobu-maru, the third son of chief negi Kazhira, and the mariashi were served by shrine officials and parishioners. The attire was optional, consisting of ho'i or jō'e. On the same day, gagaku court music was performed at the lower tower gate and continued throughout the day.

Though not yet having received court rank, it is said that an imperial decree was issued regarding Norihisa and Hisatoshi's administration of Kifune paddy fields.

Kazhira [eldest son of gon-negi Chikahira]
[Sixth-generation descendant of Korethira] On June 13th, Kōei 1 (1342), he received Norihisa's transfer and was appointed as head priest [at age 52]. On October 22nd, Jōwa 2 (1346), he resigned from his position [at age 56]. In Kōji 4 (1145), during this Kazhira's time, of one thousand kanmon, three hundred kanmon was allocated to Kazhira and seven hundred kanmon to former shrine administrator Norihisa, with both instructed to accomplish the merit of shrine repairs, so they carried out this directive. Subsequently, during his tenure as shrine administrator, the thirteen-bay long corridor collapsed. This Kazhira was a person who represented the interruption of four generations from ancestor Yukihira through Yorahira, Nobuhira, and Chikahira. His appointment as head priest as the son of a gon-negi was the first of its kind.
