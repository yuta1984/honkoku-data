Record of Appointments to the Shrine Administrative Position at Kamo Betsuraijinja Shrine
《Parentage unknown》

Otoko-doko: This Otoko-doko was a person who had no birthplace and came flying from the void riding on tree branches. He was appointed in the second year of Taika (646) and governed for 179 years until his death in the second year of Tenchō (825). However, before Otoko-doko's time, it is said that Kamo Shrine was first established during the reign of Emperor Kinmei, the 30th emperor. This Otoko-doko was the founding patron of Shōjin-ji Temple. He founded the temple in the fifth year of Wadō (712, year of the Metal Tiger) and built it in the eleventh year of Kōnin (820) following an oracle from this shrine.

Also during Otoko-doko's time, in the reign of Emperor Shōmu, on the 14th day of the 12th month of the second year of Tenpyō (730), one chō of imperial deputy rice fields was dedicated, and the imperial deputy meeting ritual began from this time. Subsequently, because the funds for year-round shrine rituals were insufficient, an additional chō was granted upon request. During the reign of Emperor Kōnin, the Kamo negi and hafuri first began carrying ritual batons in the fourth month of the first year of Ten'ō (781, year of the Metal Rooster). In the reign of Emperor Heizei, the shrine festival was first conducted in the second year of Daidō (807).
