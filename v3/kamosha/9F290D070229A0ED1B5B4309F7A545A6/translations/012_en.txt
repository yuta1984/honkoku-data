This Shigetsugu was the founding patron of Saichōju-ji temple at Sasaki-no. Therefore, it was established as the clan temple.

Yasuhisa (grandson of Naritugu, first son of Chief Priest Naruhisa): Appointed on the twenty-ninth day of the eleventh month of Ninpei 2 (1152) at age forty-five, died on the twentieth day of the sixth month of Ninpei 4 (1154) at age forty-five, served for two years, known as Dainyūdō no Kami-nushi (Great Lay Monk Shrine Master). During Yasuhisa's time, summer sacred vestments were prepared and presented in the fourth month of Ninpei 4. This was the first time someone who was not a shrine master's son was appointed to the shrine administration position.

Shigetada (fifth son of Sukesuke): Appointed on the fourteenth day of the sixth month of Ninpei 4 (1154) at age fifty-three, died on the fourth day of the tenth month of Jishō 4 (1174) at age seventy-three, served for twenty years, known as Sasaki-no no Kami-nushi (Sasaki-no Shrine Master). During Shigetada's time, in the Jishō era, a bell was installed at the shrine's Buddhist temple, a bell tower was constructed, and the bell was hung. As an imperial vow of Emperor Go-Shirakawa, Niishō Kifune in Tamba Province was donated as a perpetual nightly sacred lamp oil estate. Therefore, Shigetada's descendants should inherit this. The current sacred oil estate of Niishō is this one. During this shrine administrator's time, there was a reconstruction of the main shrine. In Emperor Takakura's reign, the ceremonial adze ritual began on the fifth day of the third month of Jishō 2 (1172), the roof-raising ceremony on the seventeenth day of the sixth month of the same year, and the reconstruction ceremony on the sixteenth day of the eighth month of the same year. This was thirty years after the previous reconstruction ceremony. Also during Shigetada's time, there is a diary record that summer vestments began in the fourth month of Jishō 4.

Iehira (first son of Narihira): Appointed on the thirteenth day of the tenth month of Jishō 4 (1174) at age seventy-two, died on the twenty-third day of the eighth month of Jishō 1 (1177) at age seventy-five, served for three years, known as Izumi no Kami-nushi (Spring Shrine Master).

Shigeyasu (second son of Shigetsugu): Appointed on the twenty-eighth day of the ninth month of Jishō 1 (1177) at age fifty-nine, died on the twelfth day of the first month of Kenkyū 2 (1191) at age seventy-three, served for fourteen years, known as Fujiki no Kami-nushi (Wisteria Tree Shrine Master). At this time, there was a reconstruction ceremony of Wakamiya Shrine on the twentieth day of the second month of Jishō 3 (1179). Also, the reconstruction ceremony of Ōta Shrine was on the seventh day of the fourth month of the same year. Next, Shigeyasu had Sasaki-no Saichōju-ji temple constructed according to his late father Shigetsugu's original intention.
