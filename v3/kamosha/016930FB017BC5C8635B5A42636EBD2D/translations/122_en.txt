While remaining secluded, this morning the religious ceremonies were concluded so I reported for duty. ○The palace religious lecture accounting from the 7th was conducted as usual.

13th Day, Hinoto-Ushi, Clear: For the Kagura lecture's 13th meeting, went to Okuda-ya at Sanbon-masu from morning, finished at the seventh half-hour and returned home.

14th Day, Tsuchinoe-Tora, Clear: For the first anniversary memorial service of the late Kawase Shizen, was invited at an irregular time by his son Shigenoshin and attended. Sent 3 monme of silver for incense offering. However, it was a temple service.

18th Day, Mizunoe-Uma, Clear: At the family gate today was the poetry meeting opening ceremony. Although I was in heavy mourning and should not have attended, since there was a shortage of people at this time, I had been told beforehand that despite the impropriety I should appear before His Lordship. Therefore, I reported for duty early in the morning, assisted with reception duties, and finished after the seventh hour.

19th Day, Mizunoto-Hitsuji, Clear: On father's death anniversary, made a grave visit in the afternoon.

20th Day, Kinoe-Saru, Cloudy then clear: Regarding the village's petition to build houses on the rice fields in front of Shinnyo-dō in Okazaki village domain, today for land inspection, assistant administrators Okamoto and Sakamoto, accountant myself, Okazaki and others, along with provisions manager Kozaburō, went from around the eighth half-hour, completed the inspection, and provided food and drink.

28th Day, Mizunoe-Tatsu, Clear: The festival distribution came and was handed over; received 1 koku as usual.

Third Month (small month)

3rd Day, Hinoto-Tori, Clear: The family gate's Jōshi celebration was conducted as usual with celebratory sake bestowed.

17th Day, Kanoto-I, Clear: For Lord Hosokawa Etchū-no-kami's visit to the capital, he passed through and entered the family gate, with the usual exchange of ceremonial cups, and 5 ryō of silver was bestowed as a souvenir. ○Regarding this year's remaining pilgrimage fees, the bid came to a low price of 90 monme 5 bu, so the 12 people of the fifth group received payment in rice, paid by Kyōsen. The divine amount was 5 koku 4 to 4 shō 8 gō, with 93 monme 5 bu worth 498 monme 4 bu 9 rin 2 mō. Since the administrator was indisposed today, after deducting recognition fees, mountain cutting money, etc., the remainder was 1 bu 2 shu in gold and 94 mon in coins per person. However, receiving payment in rice is an unprecedented example. From now on, should it continue according to previous regulations?

18th Day, Mizunoe-Ne, Cloudy: Since Yasuko was somewhat unwell after childbirth, she has been staying at Hirai since the 13th of this month.
