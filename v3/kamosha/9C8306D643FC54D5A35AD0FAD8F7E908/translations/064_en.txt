Next, again arriving at the appointed time (seeing the imperial police chief proceeding to the procession route, immediately one reporting official from Shimogamo returns to his locality; this is that time)
The shrine officials and clan members each form processions (in this ceremony, first thirty shrine attendants in two rows, then the head priest in one row, then twenty shrine officials in two rows, then thirty ceremonial offering bearers in two rows)
(then the yin-yang master in one row, then five purification leaders in one row, then sacred horses, then the stable master in one row, then twelve racing horses, then fourteen horse riders in two rows following)
They proceed toward the shrine precincts (in this ceremony, first when rising from seated position, they perform a seated bow, descend from the entrance and remove shoes with one bow, then when passing in front of Fujiki Shrine they perform a crosswise bow, then enter the first torii)

【Left Page】
(at the first torii one bow, then at the lower hem one bow and enter the second torii. However, from this point umbrella bearers and servants are excluded. Especially during rainy weather ceremonies, attendants take care of these)

Next the shrine officials take their seats at the earthen building (besides this, the purification leaders pass through Negi Bridge, Tama Bridge, the tower gate, etc. and proceed directly to)
(their seats in the offering hall. The ceremonial offering bearers and clan members likewise pass through the middle gate side doors and ascend to the feast hall, waiting for the shrine officials' arrival)
(However, the head ritualists - the negi and hafuri of Kataoka, Kifune, Shingu, Ōta, etc. - do not enter the second torii but separate and take their seats in the administrative building)
(The head ritualist takes his place in the main building. This is because there is the ceremony of the sacred treasure procession)

Next the sacred treasure procession, the divine offerings depart, and the head ritualist presents the sacred holly and katsura branches (the director passes these to him)
