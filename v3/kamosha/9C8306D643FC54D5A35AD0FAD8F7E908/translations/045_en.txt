Only human hands are to be involved. However, those who are brought along must not pass through the middle area. Wearing makiei (court cap ornaments), they proceed to the archery ground.

Next, the chief priest (shinmushi), negi, and hafuri take their seats at the curtained area (with the chief priest in the center, negi and hafuri to the right, all facing south. The diagram is as shown below).

Next, the shrine officials below them each take their seats. However, the negi sit on the western seats facing north and east, while the hafuri sit on the eastern seats facing north and west. The young arrow retrievers follow the same arrangement. During rain, the chief priest and others each sit in the御所屋 (palace building), and the ceremony follows the same protocol as in clear weather. The duty shrine officials also take their seats. After the daily offering ceremony is completed, they wait for the departure from the shrine head.

Next, the chief priest and hafuri stand together and shoot at the target (in this ceremony, they carry bows and arrows, go out to the edge of the lawn and shoot at the target. First the chief priest, then the hafuri shoot, and then return to their seats).

[Left Page]
They shoot two arrows each in alternation. From this point forward, they should lower their hakama hems. After finishing shooting, they return to their seats.

Next, the senior negi and shrine officials below stand in a line and shoot at the target (in this ceremony, starting with the senior negi, up to the clan deity hafuri, each shoots one arrow twice. Their standing positions are as shown in the diagram). After shooting, they return to their seats.

Next, young clansmen hold the arrows and present them to the shrine officials (in this ceremony, the shrine officials' attendants, servants, and those brought by the young men go to the target area to collect the arrows and present them to the young men, who then bring them and present them to the shrine officials).

Next, one献 (ritual offering) and reward goods are distributed (this is the duty of the toneri and shrine attendants. The arrow-wiping paper consists of five sheets of rough paper made into one bundle, seven sheets made into one bundle, and two sheets of sugiwara paper made into one bundle. These are now given to the arrow-retrieving young men. However, this arrow-wiping paper is under the jurisdiction of the betto-taifu and is the duty of the shrine attendants. The scattered rice for small earthenware vessels is under the jurisdiction of the shoshi-taifu and is the duty of the toneri).

Next, the chief priest and hafuri stand together as before and shoot at the target (in this ceremony, first the hafuri, then the chief priest shoots). After shooting, they return to their seats.
