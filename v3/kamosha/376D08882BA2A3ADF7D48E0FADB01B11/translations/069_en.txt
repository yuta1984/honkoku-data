Twenty-seventh day, Tsuchinoto-u (己卯), clear weather. For the Aoi Messenger's journey to Kyoto, both senior and junior members assembled as usual, but since the ninth attendant did not come for his shift, Chinki was temporarily requested and made to attend.

Twenty-eighth day, Kanoe-tatsu (庚辰), rain. The annual poetry gathering of the noble family began. All matters were completed as in previous years, and I served as secretary and intermediary.

Last day of the month, Mizunoe-uma (壬午), cloudy with occasional rain. Horikawa Ōmi-no-kami has been suffering from madness since this past New Year and is not recovering well. His cousin Katō [name unclear] was recommended by Shingū Ryōtei for adoption, and there have been private discussions about this matter since then. Today, Namba Iyo-no-kami, Shimohashi Yamato-no-suke, and Keisen went directly to Ryōtei's separate residence, met for the first time, discussed various matters privately, and sake was served.

Fifth Month, Long Month
First day, Mizunoto-hitsuji (癸未), cloudy. Horse racing preliminary selection as in previous years. However, in the tenth race, the pursuing horses Sunenaga and Shōsuke Akishiro Tadanao did not appear. At that time, the lead horse of Assistant Priest Motokiyo stopped at the exit in the middle of the reverse course, and the right rein came loose. Meanwhile, the pursuing horse ran out, so it was called back. Also in the sixth race, Tsuchida Shōaken Tokukachi fell from his horse near the victory post and was called back.

Third day, Kinoto-tori (乙酉), clear weather. The upper coating of the storehouse, which was started on the seventh day of the second month, was completed at this time. Accordingly, I paid plasterer Jirōsaburō seven ryō and one bu including previous advances, and gave eight kan-mon in coins to assistant Otoshirō. Also, since carpenter Zenbei was assigned to external work, I gave him five to of rice as an advance loan as requested, and also gave him two bu in gold today.

Fifth day, Hinoto-i (丁亥), cloudy. The sacred ritual was as in previous years. ○ The noble family's celebratory matters were as usual. The young lord made a visit, peers made courtesy calls, and the secretary accompanied as attendant. ○ The horse racing meet was as in previous years. In the third race, left side lead horse Kyōi-dai Senken, right side pursuing horse Fūken, when the caps touched hands, they separated and the match was incomplete, with the lead horse running and the pursuing horse holding back and stopping. Therefore, it was restarted. In the second attempt, the lead horse again broke away and it was restarted. In the third attempt, both sides called out, caps touched hands together, pursuing horse Fūken and lead horse stopped near the paper marker. Therefore, the right side became the loser.
