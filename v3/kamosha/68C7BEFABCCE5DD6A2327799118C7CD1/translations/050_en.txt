Thirty-four villages in total: ten villages of Higashi-Ono, fourteen villages of Nishi-Ono, and ten villages of Kita-Ono.

- Kan'ei 8th year (1631)
The three men Kageaki, Ujifusa, and Naonao petitioned the council of elders and youth for the first time this year, which led to the beginning of the practice called "tobi-nori" (flying mount) to replace "nori-jiri."

- Same 19th year (1642)
Flying mount was discontinued through the unanimous decision of the council of elders and youth.

- Keinan 2nd year (1649), September 11th
The three imperial residences - Hon'in, Shin'in, and Nyoin - made secret imperial visits, and seven rounds of special horse racing were held for their viewing. The horses at the imperial pavilion were provided by Lord Itakura Suō-no-kami.
