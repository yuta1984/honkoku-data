【Right Page】
They pass over the Ne Bridge (at this time performing one bow at shrines such as Hashimoto Suwa and Kataoka) and the Tama Bridge, then enter the Sakura Gate. At this time, under the northern eaves, the left and right kaika officials exchange courtesies, and the jōjiri do the same. After completion, those on the right remain in the western corridor.

(Circular seats are temporarily prepared in advance, with the assistant serving in this role, positioned facing north with east as the upper position. White curtains are also hung around. Initially, the riding attendants are organized.)

Next, the left-side kaika officials and jōjiri take their seats under the eaves of the Imperial Office.

The ceremony: After exchanging left and right courtesies under the tower gate eaves, they proceed, first performing one bow at Tanao Shrine. They enter the middle gate, perform one bow at the temporary shrine, then pass through the center of the main shrine and crouch, then perform one bow at Haji-no-o Shrine and take their seats under the eaves of the Imperial Office (facing north with east as upper position; however, the kaika officials sit about one ken apart from the jōjiri, at approximately the sixth pillar from the west).

(First shoe courtesy. At this time, they thrust out the right knee, turn left and take their seats. Bow, settle into seat, hold hem. At this time, they move the scepter to the left hand, press down the right sleeve, manipulate it, and smooth the flat cord.)

【Left Page】
Next, the jōjiri present the offerings.

Next, the kaika officials hold up the offerings, enter through the screen middle gate, advance to the small courtyard, and recite the ritual prayers to completion.

The ceremony: First, when the ninth jōjiri completes the offering presentation, the kaika officials rise (first seated bow, raise the right knee, turn right and rise, shoe courtesy). They turn left and remove their swords between Haji-no-o Shrine and the Imperial Office (having attendants hold them). They turn right and advance to near the fourth pillar east of the Imperial Office, face west and wait. Next, when the tenth jōjiri completes the offering presentation and holds up the offerings, they advance before the kaika officials (taking position). They present them. At this time, they insert the scepter (though sometimes they put it in their breast). They receive and hold the offerings, enter through the screen middle gate, advance to the center of the standing sand in the small courtyard, perform double bows in two stages, and recite the ritual prayers (according to oral tradition, remaining seated with shoes on; clapping hands is not required).
