Nobuhisa: Bunmei 7 (1475), former Shrine Master (red ink notation). According to the same records, served during Shrine Master Kagehisa's tenure (appointed Enkyō 2/1/25, abdicated Enkyō 3/4/25).

Shigechika: Eishō 7 (1510)/4th month. According to shrine administrative records, Kenmu 3/6/23, Chief Ritualist Yasunari, etc. Possibly served during the tenure of eleven generations: Tsunehisa, Tōhisa, Kagehisa, Munetada, Hisahisa, Fujimoto, Hisanō, Yūshin Hisada, Korohisa, Sadahisa, etc. If so, he served for 33 years from being called Chief Ritualist in Kagen 3 until Kenmu 3 (red ink notation).

Shigenō: Daiei 7 (1527)/9/2. According to construction records, Einin 4/3rd month, Sawada Ritualist Yasukiyo, etc.

Shigekuni: Keichō 6 (1601). Yasukiyo. According to the same records, served as Assistant Ritualist during the shrine relocation on Kagen 3/8/8, age 36, etc. (lower red notation). According to Tsunehisa's diary, served as Assistant Ritualist in Kangen 2/1st month (upper red notation).

Shigekata: According to certain records, promoted to Junior Fourth Rank Upper in Kenmu 2. According to shrine administrative records, same position on Kenmu 3/7/7. According to the same records, Bunwa 2, Chief Ritualist Yasukiyo, etc.

Shigetsugu: Keichō era, month and day [unspecified]. Yasutoshi. According to the same records, on Kōan 2 (same as Jōji 1)/1/3, Chief Ritualist Yasutoshi was ordered to perform divine offerings, etc., due to the absence of a Shrine Master. According to the same records, Jōji 4/11/7, Chief Ritualist Yasutoshi, etc.

Shigeyoshi: Keichō era, month and day [unspecified]. According to the same records, served on Ōan 3/5/24.

Shigesuke: Kan'ei 6 (1629)/11th month day. Shigechika. According to the same records, Jōji 4/10/12, Assistant Ritualist Shigechika, etc. Although no records are found, according to Jōji 4, he was called Assistant Ritualist, roughly like this (red ink notation).

Yasumasa: No records found, according to original document (red ink notation).

Iekage: No records found, according to original document. However, he was a second cousin of Kiyonori, grandson of Kiyoshi, ancestor of the Kiyoryū lineage. Kiyonori is recorded in the imperial register of Kakitsu 1. If so, the dates would correspond (red ink notation).

Shigetoshi: Same as above, according to original document (red ink notation). Either Shitoku 1 or Meitoku 1 (red ink notation).

[Separate Record] Shigeie: During Shrine Master Naohisa's tenure, at the time of the main shrine relocation, Shrine Master Naohisa and Ritualist Shigeie conducted the divine ceremonies, etc.
