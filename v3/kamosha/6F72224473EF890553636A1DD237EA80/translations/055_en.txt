According to the *Shoku Nihongi* (続日本紀):

　　Emperor Kanmu:
　　　On the 8th day (kanoe-ne) of the 11th month of Enryaku 4 (785 CE), an imperial decree was issued granting ten fuko (封戸; tribute households) each from Atago District to the Upper and Lower Kamo Shrines.

According to the shrine records:

　　Emperor Seiwa:
　　　On the 14th day of the 3rd month of Jōgan 6 (864 CE), through the Office of the Grand Empress Dowager's Household,

　　　by imperial decree, 45 chō, 9 tan, and 7,000 bu of fields in Warabino, Yamamoto-gō, Kawabe District, Settsu Province, were donated to Kamo Shrine. 《Marginal note: This is the Kometani Estate (米谷庄)》

　　　Also at this time,
　　　During the Jōgan era, 400 sheaves of rice for provisions for the Kamo Festival envoy were disbursed from the regular tax revenues (正税) of Yamashiro Province.
