【Left folio】
　　　　Petition — written on folded formal paper (hōsho, four-fold)

　　　　　　　A respectful memorandum of petition, humbly submitted

Regarding the Kamo Imperial Grand Shrine: since ancient times, the reverence shown to it by the Imperial Court has been unparalleled among other shrines. Accordingly, shinko (shrine households) and divine stipends have repeatedly been granted, and in addition to these, the shrine domains scattered across the six court ministers' territories and the various provinces altogether amounted to more than 99,080 koku, as recorded in the shrine's own records. Not only were the annual religious ceremonies carried out as a matter of course, but the daily food offerings (nichiku) stipulated by the pious vow of Emperor Horikawa were also solemnly prepared and presented. However, from around the Hōgen and Heiji eras, the world gradually fell into turmoil, and then, by the time of the Bunmei and Ōnin eras, powerful warlords came to occupy the four corners of the realm, and the greater part of the shrine domains across the provinces were plundered. As a result, the annual religious rites as well as the daily food offerings fell largely into disuse and neglect, a situation greatly lamented by all the shrine households. During the land surveys of the Tenshō era, a vermillion-seal grant of 2,572 koku was received from the Taikō (Toyotomi Hideyoshi), and matters stand in that condition to this day. However, since there are originally a great many shrine households, even this domain is insufficient to attend to all matters properly. We regretfully report that many of the annual rites and ceremonies have not been restored to their former standing, and in particular, the daily food offerings established by the pious vow of Emperor Horikawa — as mentioned above — are now prepared and presented only once or twice a month, a circumstance for which we feel the most profound and humble regret.
