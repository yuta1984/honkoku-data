- On the twenty-seventh day of the same month, he died of illness at the age of forty-five. His posthumous Buddhist name (*hōmyō*) was Isshin'in Shōyo Jōsen Shinji. His place of burial is the same as previously noted.

**Katsuichi (Katsutame)** *(his earlier names were Hotarō and also Kumegoro)*
- Natural father: Second son of Nagai Magodayū Toshiei
- Wife: Daughter of his adoptive father Sōjirō Katsuosa

- In the second month of the fourteenth year of Bunka (Year of the Ox, 1817), he was granted the inherited stipend (*atoshiki gochigyō*) of one hundred *koku* without any discrepancy, and was assigned to the Aizu Katte Ni-no-Kobushinigumi (the second reserve group under the Aizu household management) for the purpose of training in the martial and fine arts.
  - *Note: As there was a sick person in the household, upon petition, he remained in Edo and repeatedly served as a hired attendant (*oyatoi*) in the Shinbangumi (new guard unit).*

**[Left folio]**

- In the sixth month of the second year of Bunsei (Year of the Rabbit, 1819), having applied himself diligently to the martial arts and attained mastery in archery (*kyūjutsu*), he was assigned to the Aizu Katte Shinbangumi (new guard unit under the Aizu household management).

- In the ninth month of the same year, as his illness had become severe, he submitted a petition requesting that Ichishirō, the third son of Andō Ichinojō, be taken in as his son-in-law and adopted heir (*muko-yōshi*)… *(continued on the next page)*
