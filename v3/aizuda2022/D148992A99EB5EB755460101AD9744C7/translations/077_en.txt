**[Right folio]**
He was appointed to the role of *Shakura-kata* (granary management) and concurrently assigned to the *Heitei Shinden-kata* (embankment and new rice field administration).

One entry: In the third month of the second year of the same era (Year of the Dog), in connection with the domain administrative reforms (*go-kaisei*), he submitted opinions and proposals while serving on assignment at the rural district office (*gō-yakusho*), and for his diligent service, he was awarded five *ryō* of silver as a reward.

One entry: In the third month of the third year of the same era (Year of the Boar), concerning a dispute in which residents of Koto Village in the Kurokawa domain obstructed the transport of timber floated down from the three villages of Akaya, Tsunaki, and Takigaya in the Ogawa district toward Shibata, he traveled up to Edo on official business to make inquiries to the bakufu (central government).

**[Left folio]**
One entry: In the tenth month of the same year, he was appointed to the post of *Kōrikata Gimmi-yaku* (rural district inspector/examiner), and was granted the privilege of *dokurei* (the right to have a private audience with the domain lord).

One entry: In the same month, he was ordered to oversee the collection of *kowari jōsū* (fixed small-unit monetary allocations) at the rural district office, and was assigned to attend, supervise, and confirm the proceedings as an assistant to the *gimmi-yaku*.

One entry: In the ninth month of the fifth year of the same era (Year of the Ox), as the current Yukawa river construction project was an extraordinary undertaking differing from those of ordinary years, he was presented with ceremonial sake for his diligent and capable management of the work.

One entry: In the fifth month of the sixth year of the same era (Year of the Tiger), he was additionally appointed to concurrently handle the receipts and disbursements of the *kowari jōsū* (fixed small-unit allocations).
