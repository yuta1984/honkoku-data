- Same 11, Kanoto-hitsuji year (1799), tenth month:
For attending to examine Lord Ryōtokuin during his illness, one bundle of cotton was granted.

- Kyōwa 2, Tsuchinoe-inu year (1802), ninth month: For many years of diligent service, paying attention to health maintenance matters, providing broad medical treatment to both the lord's household and others, and performing surgeries, a stipend of thirty koku was granted.

- Same 3, Tsuchinoto-i year (1803), third month:
At the onset of Lord Kyōtei's smallpox, for paying careful attention to medical treatment and providing good care to prevent complications, he recovered well. This was recognized as the result of constant attentiveness, and therefore as reward, privately a crested haori and five pieces of silver were granted.

- Bunka 2, Kinoto-ushi year (1805), seventh month:
When Lord Kyōtei was indisposed, acting on private instruction and making it a personal request, he went down to Aizu and offered medicine. However, since the condition remained the same, it was decided to try a change of location therapy, so in the same month he returned to Edo.
