In the 1st month of Hōreki 7 (1757): He was appointed to the outer group (sotoyama-gumi) and joined Hori Hanemon's unit, serving castle guard duty.

In the 3rd month of the same year: His request to change his name to Magotayū was granted.

In the 2nd month of Hōreki 12 (1762): Group leader Hori Hanemon was relieved of his duties, and as his successor, he was assigned to Ueda Hachirōemon's unit.

In the 4th month of Hōreki 13 (1763): He was appointed to the lord's attendant guard (otomo-ban) and was granted an additional stipend of 10 koku and provisions for one person.

In the same month: His request to change his name to Shōhachi was granted.

In the 8th month of the same year: During the pilgrimage to Mine-yama Shrine, he accompanied the lord on an inspection tour of Sugawa, Noguchi, and Tsuboshitaguchi.

In the 4th month of Hōreki 14 (1764): He went to Edo as part of the attendant guard duty rotation.
Note: He was commended for his diligent service during the journey accompanying the lord.
