He was granted a stipend of two-person support within his allowance and summoned to serve as a samurai of Inawashiro, but since he was not assigned various duties for the purpose of studying arts, he was ordered to pay the kom普請料 (construction fee for unemployed samurai).

1. Bunka 1, Year of the Tiger, 2nd month (February 1818): For his diligent efforts in military drill, he was granted a new spear as a reward.

1. Bunka 3, Year of the Dragon, 4th month (April 1820): For attending the military academy without negligence, working particularly hard, and achieving academic progress appropriate for his age, he was granted one silver piece as a reward.

1. Same year, 5th month: His request to change his name to Genpachi was granted.

1. Same year, 10th month: Having mastered gunnery, he was exempted from the komushi fee, appointed to active duty, and assigned to guard duty at the castle.

Chūsaburō　　　Born on the 8th day of the 12th month of Bunka 4, Year of the Rabbit; died of illness on the 7th day of the 1st month of Bunka 5, Year of the Dragon. Buddhist name: Shunpō Zen Dōji. Buried at Anōnji Temple on Mt. Kaiji in Inawashiro.
