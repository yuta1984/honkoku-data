1. Hōreki 8 (1758), year of the tiger, 10th month: Due to faithful service for several decades since his father's time, one koku was deducted from his father's estimated yield, granted nine koku and stipend for two retainers, and summoned to serve as middle gate guard

1. Same 9th year (1759), year of the rabbit, 12th month: Granted an additional three koku and appointed as御徒 (o-kachi, foot soldier)

1. Same 10th year (1760), year of the dragon, 1st month: Served as tachigaeri escort when Lord Kyōtei traveled to Kyoto

1. Same year, 9th month: Went up to Edo for guard duty service, returned in the 4th month of the following mi year

　　However, because he was appointed as provisional inspector of foot soldiers in the field, he was granted one ryō in gold as compensation for his efforts

1. Meiwa 1 (1764), year of the monkey, 4th month: Received leave and went up to Edo for tachigaeri service

1. Same 2nd year (1765), year of the rooster, 6th month: Was appointed to inspect remaining rice in town and village storehouses and served in this capacity
　　However, from this time forward he was repeatedly appointed to inspection duties at various places and was given compensation for his efforts each time

1. Same 3rd year (1766), year of the dog, 10th month: When he went up to Edo for guard duty service, he was appointed to regular castle guard duty at Edo and received the standard compensation for his efforts
