One: Same 3rd year of the Dog (1766), 6th month, was appointed as go-kachi-shihai (supervisor of foot soldiers).

One: Same 4th year of the Boar (1767), 9th month, went up to Edo for guard duty service, returned in the 3rd month of the following year of the Rat (1768).
　　However, as customary grant items, hemp ceremonial dress (go-jōge) was bestowed.

One: Same 5th year of the Rat (1768), 11th month, was appointed as go-mochiyumi-gashira (head of bow carriers).

One: Same 6th year of the Ox (1769), 6th month, was appointed as koban-gashira (head of small guard unit) in the lord's presence.

One: Same 7th year of the Tiger (1770), 6th month, was appointed as go-yōnin (lord's administrator) in the lord's presence.

One: Same month, went up to Edo for guard duty service, returned in the 9th month of the same year.
　　However, as customary grant items, go-katabira (summer kimono), go-kosode (short-sleeved kimono), and hemp ceremonial dress were bestowed, and additionally, as irregular customary items during go-nando-harai (treasury clearing), various crested garments and other items were bestowed.

One: Same 8th year of the Rabbit (1771), 9th month, for his diligent service during his stay in the domain, was privately granted one crested lined haori jacket.

One: Same month, as requested, was relieved of his official duties and placed in non-duty status, with his seat ranking after Tōyama Iemon, appointed above the non-duty group.
