Commended for diligent and uninterrupted service

- In the fourth month of the same year, commended for diligent and uninterrupted service in shrine visit duties

- In the ninth month of the same year, although it was his first campaign, he was commended for diligent and uninterrupted service

- In the twelfth month of Tenmei 1 (1781), the year of the Ox, for his diligent service he was granted a stipend of three koku and one person's provisions as official salary

- In the sixth month of Tenmei 5 (1785), the year of the Snake, he was assigned to confidential duties

- In the ninth month of the same year, his request to change his name to Mataemon was granted

- In the second month of Tenmei 7 (1787), the year of the Sheep, he was ordered to serve in duties related to the Shogunate's official documents (go-hanmono), serving alongside current duties

- In the tenth month of the same year, he was assigned to legal affairs and ceremonial duties for both official and private matters

- In the first month of Kansei 2 (1790), the year of the Dog, he was appointed to the minor role of御納戸役 (go-nando-yaku, treasury official)
