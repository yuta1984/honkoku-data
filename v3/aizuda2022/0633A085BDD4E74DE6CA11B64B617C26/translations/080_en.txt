In the 3rd month of Bunsei 2, Year of the Rabbit (1819), after taking charge of Sagami Province, foreign ships frequently drifted ashore, and when they came to the offshore waters of the domain territory for the first time during the previous summer, he was immediately stationed as artillery personnel at Yokosuka Cape and remained in position for several days with great effort, for which he was bestowed 100 hiki of gold as a reward
1. In the 4th month of the same year, when foreign ships were sighted off Shirahama in Bōshū, it was proclaimed that his diligent service at various duties at the coastal batteries was recognized with favor
1. In the 6th month of the same year, he submitted his opinion that it would be appropriate to order rice shipments from Iwaki to be routed through Uraga
1. In the 4th month of Bunsei 3, Year of the Dragon (1820), it was proclaimed that he had served guard duty at the coastal batteries diligently and without negligence for eleven years up to that year
