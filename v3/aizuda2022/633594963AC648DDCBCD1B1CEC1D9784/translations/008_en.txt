1. In the 10th year of Kyōhō (1725, Year of the Snake), 10th month: He inherited the family headship and was granted 100 koku out of a 150-koku stipend, and was appointed to the gōzama-gumi (outer retainer group). His younger brother Gakubei was granted a divided stipend of 50 koku and was summoned as a martial artist specializing in Shinten-ryū swordsmanship.
   However, he served castle guard duty in Ohara Gorōemon's unit.

1. In the 12th year of the same era (1727, Year of the Sheep), 6th month: He petitioned to change his name to Yūhachi.

1. In the 18th year of the same era (1733, Year of the Ox), 1st month: When the head Ohara Gorōemon was relieved of his duties, he was transferred to Tanaka Kahei's unit as his successor.

1. Year and month unspecified: He petitioned to adopt Kansuke, the third son of Tsuchiya Katsusuke, as his son-in-law and heir.

1. In the 2nd year of Genbun (1737, Year of the Snake), 11th month: When his adopted son Kansuke died of illness, he petitioned to adopt Hikojūrō, the second son of Satō Rokudayū, as his son-in-law and heir.

1. In the 3rd year of the same era (1738, Year of the Horse), 4th month: He went to Edo for residential duty service and returned in the 10th month of the same year.

1. In the 2nd year of Enkyō (1745, Year of the Ox), 4th month: It was announced that he had mastered the martial arts he had long been devoted to.
