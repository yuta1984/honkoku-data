1. In the 11th month of the same year: As a celebration gift for having an audience with Lord Tokuō, he was granted one御召御小袖 (goshi okosode - a fine silk garment)

1. In the 7th year of the same era (1679), the Year of the Sheep, 12th month: Considering his family lineage, he was granted an increase of 200 koku and appointed as kumi-gashira (group leader)

1. In Tenna 2 (1682), the Year of the Dog, 6th month: Regarding the Kuruma Gentazaemon incident, due to improper conduct in his official duties, he was banned from entering Edo, Kyoto, Osaka, the Nikko-Aizu highway, and the residences of Lord Kōfu, the Three Houses, the Senior Councilors, and their family branches, and was ordered to be exiled from Seishidō Gate

1. On the 6th day of the 10th month of the same year: Died of illness at age 44. Posthumous Buddhist name: Hakuō Chūsei Koji. Burial place unknown. Stone pagoda at Kongōsan Erin-ji Temple

Daughter: Named Mutsu. Born on the 13th day of the 2nd month of Enpō 1 (1673), the Year of the Ox. Died of illness at age 2 on the 23rd day of the 7th month of Enpō 2, the Year of the Tiger. Posthumous Buddhist name: Shūren Dōjo. Buried at Shōunzan Zenryū-ji Temple.
　　Mother: Daughter of the Kuruma family

Daughter: Named Shichi (Marginal note: Born on the 13th day of the 12th month of Enpō 2, the Year of the Tiger). Adopted daughter of hatamoto Nagai Magodayū. Wife of Kita Yaemon of the Kishū domain retainers.
　　Mother: Same as above
