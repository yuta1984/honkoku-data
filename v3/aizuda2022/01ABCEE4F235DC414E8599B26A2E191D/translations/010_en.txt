Orinoshin Mituyasu (initial name: Jūnai): Born on the 11th day of the 6th month of Enpō 4 (1676), Year of the Dragon. Died of illness at age 19 on the 20th day of the 2nd month of Genroku 7 (1694), Year of the Dog. Posthumous Buddhist name: Shunrin Jōei Koji. Buried at Kongōsan Erin-ji Temple.
　　Mother: Same as above

Daughter named Kojirō (Marginal note: Born on the 6th day of the 4th month of Enpō 7 (1679), Year of the Sheep): Wife of Satō Hantaemon Shigeoki
　　Mother: Same as above

Daughter: Died of illness on the 23rd day of the 6th month of Kan'ei 19 (1642), Year of the Horse. Posthumous Buddhist name: Genjo Dōjo. Burial place in Ushū at Saijō-ji Temple, details unknown. Officiating priest from Ganjōju-ji Temple.
　　Mother: Same as above

Daughter named Wa: Born on the 27th day of the 5th month of Jōō 1 (1652), Year of the Dragon. Wife of Hori Chūzaemon Nagatomi.
　　Mother: Daughter of the Gyōda family. Died of illness at age 22 on the 18th day of the 11th month of Enpō 1 (1673), Year of the Ox. Posthumous Buddhist name: Shiun-in-den Baihō Myōkō Daishi. Buried at Kongōsan Erin-ji Temple. Memorial services performed due to the extinction of the Hori family line.

Heiya Mitsutada (Marginal note: Initial name Kōsei, initial name Matsunosuke, also Rokurōbee. Born on the 27th day of the 11th month of Jōō 3 (1654), Year of the Monkey)
　　Mother: Same as above
　　Wife: Daughter of Mitake Jūdayū Katsugen, named Teru. Born on the last day of the 7th month of Kanbun 4 (1664), Year of the Dragon. Died of illness at age 79 on the 26th day of the 6th month of Kanpō 2 (1742), Year of the Rabbit. Posthumous Buddhist name: Shōrin-in-den Kakushō Tan'en Daishi. Buried at Shōunzan Zenryū-ji Temple.

1. In the 8th month of Kanbun 7 (1667), Year of the Sheep: Was granted a divided inheritance of 200 koku from his late father's fief, appointed to the tozama-gumi (outer retainer group), and joined Numazu Kurōbee's unit.
