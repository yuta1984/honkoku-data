1. Same year, 7th month: Was granted permission to use a palanquin (norimono) in Aizu

1. Same 3rd year (Kanpō 3, 1743), Year of the Boar, 3rd month: Proceeded to Edo for guard duty service, returning in the 9th month

1. Enkyō 1 (1744), Year of the Rat, 9th month: Proceeded to Edo for guard duty service, returning in the 3rd month of the following Ushi year (Enkyō 2, 1745)

1. Same 2nd year (Enkyō 2, 1745), Year of the Ox, 1st month: Was announced that permission to use a palanquin (kago) was granted in Edo

1. Same 3rd year (Enkyō 3, 1746), Year of the Tiger, 1st month: Was appointed as Chief Administrator of the Accounting Department (moto-shimegata sōshi) in the lord's presence

1. Kan'en 1 (1748), Year of the Dragon, 12th month: Due to diligent service in current position, was granted an additional stipend of 100 koku

1. Same 2nd year (Kan'en 2, 1749), Year of the Snake, 3rd month: Proceeded to Edo for guard duty service, returning in the 9th month
　　During this guard duty service, the oath regarding palanquin permission was finalized at the residence of Lord Yagi Jūzaburō, the Shogunate Inspector. This should have been finalized when palanquin permission was granted in previous years, but due to obstacles, it was finalized on this occasion
