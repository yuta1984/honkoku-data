He was told to serve next in line for Lord Sadaaki's meal service (gozenban), and due to this, it would be difficult to grant his wish to go down to Aizu. For fifty days, he served continuously through both day and night vigils without sleep.

1. In the same year, intercalary 8th month: He was appointed to the temporary position of regular meal attendant (gozenban) for Lord Sadaaki.

1. In the same year, 9th month: Through Lord Kyōtei's thoughtful consideration, money that had been set aside was to be given to those who served diligently in personal attendance, as had been announced during his lifetime. Accordingly, he was given 3 ryō and 2 bu in gold.

1. In the same year, 11th month: As relics from Lord Kyōtei, he was given one court robe (katabira) and two small-sleeved garments (kosode).

1. In the same 3rd year, Tiger year, 1st month: When Lord Sadaaki's remains were taken down to Aizu, a symbolic representation (katashiro) was kept here [in Edo] and placed in the formal reception room. During the mourning period (chūin), he was appointed as temporary meal attendant and served continuous guard duty.

1. In the same year, 3rd month: He was relieved of his current duties, and his eldest son was appointed to a position. In recognition of his diligent service up to this point, he was awarded 2 pieces of silver and 2 bundles of cotton as a reward.
