1. In the same year, 7th month: He was ordered to take turns with Hara Hikokurō, to serve as Lord Sadaaki's attendant, and in the same 8th month he went up to Edo.

1. In Bunka 1st year, Rat year, 12th month: For his exceptional efforts in Lord Sadaaki's personal affairs, he was given one bundle of cotton.

1. In the same 2nd year, Ox year, 3rd month: Due to this occasion of [the lord's] return to the domain, he was ordered to handle affairs during his absence and was announced to remain in Edo.

1. In the same year, 8th month: When Lord Kyōtei passed away, he petitioned to go down to Aizu to pay his respects. While his request was deemed reasonable, since he was someone who understood the protocols here [in Edo], he was kept here out of [the lord's] special consideration. Due to this passing, a temporary head priest was appointed here to conduct daily worship services. For this, a page (kosho) had to be assigned to attend these services, which was unavoidable. At this time, there were particularly many guests and few personnel, causing various disruptions to official duties.
