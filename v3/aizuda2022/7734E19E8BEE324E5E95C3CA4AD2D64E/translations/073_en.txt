• Same 3rd year (1750), 1st month: Due to a peasant uprising (revolt) in the previous December, both his main and concurrent positions were revoked.

• Same 4th year (1751), 6th month: Regarding the matter of lending rice based on surplus land surveys from two years prior, although he had been serving at the government office for years, he had delegated all affairs to Saigō Niemon and had been extremely negligent in his duties. While punishment could have been imposed, out of mercy, 50 koku were confiscated from his domain stipend and he was assigned to minor construction duties (kobushin).

• Hōreki 3rd year (1753), 4th month: He went up to Edo and was granted an audience with the lord.

• Same year, 9th month: He was appointed to the Tozama group under Ohara Gorōemon's unit and served castle guard duty.

• Same 4th year (1754), 4th month: As requested, his son Bunzaemon was appointed as his replacement for guard duty.

• Same 11th year (1761), 9th month, 15th day: He died of illness at age 85. His Buddhist name was Hōgi-in Keishuku Nissō Daikoji. His burial place was the same as above.
