He went up to Edo for official financial administration duties, and when he returned after settling the accounts, he was granted ten ryō of gold as a reward.

First: Kanbun 2nd year of Tora (1662), 4th month - Together with Seki Tōemon, he went up to Edo for official financial administration duties. In the same 4th month, he returned together with Tōemon.

However, when he was granted leave to go up to Edo and returned, he was always granted old court kosode robes, katabira summer garments, and other items.

First: Same 12th year of Ne (1672), 7th month - Due to old age and infirmity, as he petitioned, he was relieved of his official duties and appointed to the tozama group under Harada Shume's unit, where he performed castle guard duties.

First: Enpō 4th year of Tatsu (1675), 12th month - As petitioned, his son Heinojō was appointed as his replacement guard.

First: Same 5th year of Mi (1676), 7th month, 2nd day - He died of illness at the age of 74. His posthumous Buddhist name was Jūsen'in Sōhaku Nisshō Koji, and he was buried at Kuozan Jitsujōji Temple.
