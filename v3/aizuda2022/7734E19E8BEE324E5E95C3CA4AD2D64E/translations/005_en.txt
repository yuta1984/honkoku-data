　　　　Subsequently, he served Lord Torii Sakage with a stipend of 250 koku. At that time,
　　　　he took the name Tōemon and served as zeni-taishō (money magistrate), residing in Mogami in Dewa Province.
　　　　Upon entering the domain, he received an increase of 100 koku, totaling 350 koku,
　　　　his duties were reassigned and he was appointed as teppō-taishō (firearms commander). Additionally, his second son Tōemon
　　　　Nariyoshi was granted 150 koku. Later, Tōemon Naruhide died of illness.

　　　　As inheritance, of the 350 koku, 300 koku was granted
　　　　to his eldest son Tomenosuke, and 50 koku was granted as additional stipend
　　　　to his second son Tōemon Nariyoshi. However, when the Torii house had its stipend reduced, they became rōnin.

　一　In the 13th year of Kan'ei, year of the rat (1636), 9th month, in Mogami in Dewa Province, according to
　　　　previous knowledge, he was granted 200 koku and summoned as an outsider vassal, and was
　　　　appointed as Yamanobe deputy magistrate.

　一　In the 14th year, year of the ox (1637), 3rd month, regarding the transfer of rice from Yamagata Castle to Tsuruga in Echizen
　　　　for official rice distribution, he was ordered to handle the maritime transport and management.
　　　　He went to Sakata, completed his duties, and returned in the 9th month of the same year.
