Around this time, he served Ashina Moriuji and resided at Naganuma Castle in Ōshū, ruling over 10,000 koku, and died of illness at age 63. His younger brother Shinkurō (某), whose son Ukon (某) became the adopted son of Ise Soji, the castle lord of Shirakawakonakamura, and took the name Konaka Ise (某). During the Tenshō period, when Date Masamune invaded Aizu, he died in battle at Suriagahara at the age of 16. His younger brother Kitarō (某) was initially adopted into the Konaka family together with his elder brother Ukon, but later came to reside in the local domain. He did not serve in an official position and died of illness at age 53. However, since there are no old records for verification, the actual facts of both their lives, including the dates of their deaths from illness, Buddhist names, and burial places, remain unknown.

Kitarō (某)'s son Kizaemon Mitsumasa had many siblings. The male children were Kokubo Gizaemon Kanari and Gotayū, who served Itakura Suō-no-kami. Of his two younger sisters, one married someone in the Yonezawa domain, and the other became the wife of Toshima Kakuzaemon Yoshishige.
