1. Twelfth month of Hōei 4 (1707), when his son Kuninosuke, who was serving as a substitute guard, absconded, [the father] petitioned to serve in his place and joined Hyūga Saburōemon's group to perform castle guard duties.

1. Second month of the same 5th year (1708), when he petitioned to adopt Kasuga Sawaemon's younger brother Mikisaemon as his son, he was told that although it would normally be difficult due to inappropriate lineage, since [Mikisaemon] had been in service since childhood, his petition would be granted out of compassion.

1. Tenth month of the same 6th year (1709), as petitioned, his son Mikisaemon was appointed as substitute guard.

1. Second month of Kyōhō 3 (1718), as petitioned, he was granted permission to retire.

1. Third month 19th day of the same 5th year (1720), he died of illness at age 79. His Buddhist name was Isshin-in Hōyo Gentsū Koji, and he was buried at Hōrin-ji temple on Mount Inadai.
