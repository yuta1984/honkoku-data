1. Second month of the same 4th year (1663), he received an additional stipend of two ryō in gold.

1. The same 5th year (1664), month unknown, when Yamada Sōetsu's eye disease treatment was ordered, he was commanded to assist. In the twelfth month of the same year, he received an additional two ryō in gold, with his stipend certificate increased to sixteen ryō.

1. Third month of the same 9th year (1668), he was ordered to perform his coming-of-age ceremony (genpuku). Regarding his service, he was ordered to serve in rotation at the御次 (go-tsugi). He was granted a fief of 150 koku, appointed to the page group (koshō-gumi), and served in the role of medicine preparation.
   However, he changed his name to Nuinodono-no-jō, though it is unclear whether this was a name change ordered from above.

1. Fourth month of the same 10th year (1669), he accompanied his lord on the journey down to the domain, and in the tenth month of the same year, he accompanied him back up to Edo.

1. Fifth month of the same 12th year (1671), he accompanied his lord down to the domain, and returned in the ninth month of the same year.

1. Twelfth month of the same year
   He accompanied Lord Dotsū's remains down to the domain. In the third month of the following Ushi year (1672), when the coffin was moved to Inawashiro, he served as an escort and continued his duties until the funeral ceremony. In the fourth month, he was given leave and returned to Edo.
