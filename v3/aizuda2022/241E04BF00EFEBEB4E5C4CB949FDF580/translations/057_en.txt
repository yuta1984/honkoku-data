One entry: In the sixth month of the same 2nd year of Genbun [Year of the Snake, 1737], he was granted the standard stipend equivalent (nami no o-gisaku) and was appointed as an escort guard (o-tomo-ban).

One entry: In the ninth month of the same year, he traveled to Edo for garrison duty (kinban).
　　Note: It was officially recognized that he had served throughout the campaign without any absences.

One entry: In the fourth month of the same 4th year [Year of the Sheep, 1739], on the occasion of the lord's entry into the domain (o-nyūbu), he traveled to Edo to return [to Aizu in attendance], and it was officially recognized that he had performed his duties admirably as an escort during the journey.

One entry: In the tenth month of the same 5th year [Year of the Monkey, 1740], he traveled to Edo for garrison duty.

One entry: In the ninth month of the same 6th year [Year of the Rooster, 1741], he traveled to Kyoto as an escort on the occasion of the lord's visit to the capital.

One entry: In the second month of the 2nd year of Kanpō [Year of the Dog, 1742], as requested, he was relieved of his escort guard (o-tomo-ban) duties and was appointed to the tozama-gumi (outer guard unit), placed in the unit of Hinata Saburōemon. However, due to illness, he expressed his wish to contribute the ko-fushin-ryō (a payment in lieu of active service), and his request was granted; he was then reassigned to the unit of Sugita Gorōbei.

One entry: In the tenth month of the 4th year of Enkyō [Year of the Rabbit, 1747], the unit commander (kashira) Sugita Gorōbei died of illness, and he consequently came to be placed in the unit of Suwa Isuke as the successor unit assignment.

One entry: In the first month of the same 5th year [Year of the Dragon, 1748], as requested, he was ordered to return to active duty and served as a castle guard (o-shiro o-ban).

One entry: In the second month of the 4th year of Hōreki [Year of the Dog, 1754], his request to take in Misaka Daishinō, a cousin of Misaka Genemon, as a son-in-law and adopted son (muko-yōshi), was granted.

One entry: In the eighth month of the same year, his request to change his given name to Sakuzaemon was granted.
