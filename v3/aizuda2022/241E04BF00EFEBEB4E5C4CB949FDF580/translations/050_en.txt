(Continued from previous page) — he was granted one hundred and fifty koku out of [a stipend of] one hundred koku, and was appointed to the Tozama-gumi (outside retainer unit). Fifty koku were granted to his nephew Hachibei. 
　　Note: The gisaku (provisional cultivation stipend) that had been granted until that time was revoked; he was placed into Sugiura Tōhachirō's unit and served as a castle guard (go-shiro go-ban).

One entry: In the second month of Shōhō 1st year [Year of the Monkey, 1644], he went up to Edo for duty rotation (kinban).

One entry: In the fourth month of the same year, in Edo, he was appointed as an escort guard (otomo-ban).

One entry: In the same month, due to the Miharu Disturbance, Lord Haniwa [Tsuchitsu-sama] made a journey down [to Aizu], and he accompanied him in escort, returning to Aizu.

One entry: In the fourth month of Keian 1st year [Year of the Rat, 1648], he went up to Edo for duty rotation; however, as Lord Haniwa made a pilgrimage (shazan) to Nikkō, he accompanied him to Nikkō.
　　Note: He served as escort guard (otomo-ban) for nineteen years without negligence, alternating years; however, the dates of his comings and goings for duty rotation are unknown.

One entry: In the eighth month of Kanbun 2nd year [Year of the Tiger, 1662], he was appointed to the post of o-nando-yaku (keeper of the lord's personal treasury/wardrobe).
　　Note: He served without negligence until the twelfth month of Enpō 1st year [Year of the Rat, 1673]; however, details regarding the comings and goings for duty rotation as well as items received as gifts are unknown.

[Marginal note in red ink:]
"One entry: In the twelfth month of Kanbun 11th year [Year of the Boar, 1671], as in previous years, he was granted a gift of used kosode (short-sleeved robes)."

One entry: In the twelfth month of the same [Kanbun] 12th year [Year of the Rat, 1672], Lord Haniwa passed away; from the following spring, he served at the Go-yōhai-den (Hall of Distant Reverence).

One entry: In the second month of Enpō 2nd year [Year of the Tiger, 1674], he was appointed as o-nando-yaku (keeper of the treasury/wardrobe) for Lady Hōshōin.

[Marginal note in red ink:]
"One entry: In the second month of Enpō 3rd year [Year of the Hare, 1675], as a reward for serving at the Go-yōhai-sho (Place of Distant Reverence) for three years, he was granted three pieces of silver (ginshi)."
