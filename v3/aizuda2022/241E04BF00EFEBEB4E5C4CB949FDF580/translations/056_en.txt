One entry: In the fifth month of the same 11th year [Year of the Horse, 1726] — [continued from previous page] — on the occasion of the Great Counselor's (Dainagon-sama's) haircut ceremony, and the hosting of the Senior Councilors (Rōjū), he performed his duties admirably, and was granted sake as a reward.

One entry: In the fourth month of the same 12th year [Year of the Sheep, 1727], while in Edo, he was appointed as an acting dining attendant (o-zen-ban kari-yaku), and having continued to fulfill this role even after returning to Aizu, he was granted one piece of silver as a labor reward (rōgin).

One entry: In the third month of the same 13th year [Year of the Monkey, 1728], having traveled to Edo in attendance, he was appointed as acting dining attendant for the journey (o-dōchū o-zen-ban kari-yaku).

One entry: In the third month of the same 14th year [Year of the Rooster, 1729], he was appointed as acting dining attendant, and in the same month, during the lord's return to Aizu in attendance, he continued to serve in this acting role in Aizu as well.

One entry: In the tenth month of the same year, he was granted the residential plot (ato-yashiki) previously belonging to Katamine Kuhachi.

One entry: In the eleventh month of the same year, he was granted three ryō as an expense allowance (hikin).

One entry: In the sixth month of the same 16th year [Year of the Boar, 1731], while in Edo, he was appointed as acting dining attendant.

One entry: In the ninth month of the same year, he was once again appointed as acting dining attendant.

One entry: In the second month of the same 17th year [Year of the Rat, 1732], he was appointed to the tozama-gumi (outer guard unit) and placed in the unit of Kasuga Nizaemon, where he served on guard duty.

One entry: In the ninth month of the same year, he was granted hemp formal wear (kami-shimo) and a small robe (ko-sode) from among the personal garments of Toku-ō-sama.

One entry: In the same month, on the occasion of the domain inspector's (o-kuni-metsuke) visit to Aizu, he provided instruction in the etiquette of serving and meal arrangement as an escort-guard (o-tomo-ban), and it was officially recognized that he had performed the serving and related duties admirably.
