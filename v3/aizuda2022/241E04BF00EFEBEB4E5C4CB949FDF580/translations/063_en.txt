Wife: Daughter of Hotta Dan'emon Tanekiyo — later separated (divorced)

Item: In the fourth month of Kansei 9 (1797, Year of the Snake), because his father had committed an improper act, the father's stipend (gisaku) was confiscated; however, out of compassionate consideration, he was granted five persons' worth of stipend (gonin-fuchi) and was summoned into service as an outside-rank retainer (kumigai no shi).

Item: In the ninth month of the same eleventh year (1799, Year of the Sheep), he participated in the Otorishigari (a domain-wide military hunting exercise).

Item: In the sixth month of the same twelfth year (1800, Year of the Monkey), his request to change his name to Tsuneemon was granted.

Item: In the eighth month of Kyōwa 3 (1803, Year of the Boar), he participated in the Otorishigari.

Item: In the ninth month of the same year, regarding the Otorishigari that year, as both rounds of the hunt were conducted with the units well-coordinated and proceeding favorably, it was deemed that the participants had applied themselves diligently, and the entire group received commendation.

Item: In the third month of Bunka 4 (1807, Year of the Rabbit), he was appointed to the role of supervisor of equestrian arts under Sasahara Seiroku, and duly served in that capacity.

Item: In the intercalary sixth month of the same fifth year (1808, Year of the Dragon), as one who had applied himself diligently in the martial arts and attended not only the regular practice sessions but also the evening sessions, he was deemed praiseworthy and was awarded one piece of silver as a reward.

Item: In the ninth month of the same sixth year (1809, Year of the Snake), it was heard that he had cultivated the martial arts and mastered several schools thereof; this was deemed praiseworthy, and he was awarded two pieces of silver as a reward.
