Item: In the eighth month of the same eighth year (1811, Year of the Sheep), he participated in the oidorigan (a formal falconry hunt).

Item: In the twelfth month of the same ninth year (1812, Year of the Monkey), he was appointed as an official of the御用所 (administrative office), was granted the rank of Gokinju Ni-no-yoriai (Second-class Attendant Companion Seat), his previous stipend was changed to four koku plus two persons' fuchi, and additionally five koku in role stipend (yakuryō) was granted to him.

Item: In the first month of the same tenth year (1813, Year of the Rooster), his request to change his name to Sakuzaemon was approved.

Item: In the fourth month of the same eleventh year (1814, Year of the Dog), it was proclaimed that, since this year marks the two-hundredth memorial anniversary of the ancestor Seizaemon Yoshikazu, who accompanied Lord Dōgi and died in battle during the Fall of Osaka in the Keichō era (1596–1615), a Buddhist memorial service would be held at Kenfuku-ji temple in his honor.

Item: In the first month of the same thirteenth year (1816, Year of the Rat), because he had devoted his energies over many years to the investigation and compilation of criminal records (keisho), conducted thorough inquiries, and successfully completed a substantial volume of documents, he was specially granted an increase of three koku from within his role stipend.

Item: In the eighth month of the same year, while retaining his current seat rank (mochiseki), he was appointed as Machikura-bugyō (Town Storehouse Magistrate).

Item: In the ninth month of the same fourteenth year (1817, Year of the Ox), he was appointed to serve as a Goryūsubi (garrison guard) armored soldier (kōshi).

Item: In the tenth month of Bunsei 1 (1818, Year of the Tiger), following the house confinement (heimon) imposed on Nagase Genji, he was appointed to serve as acting Machikura-bugyō, and served in that capacity until the first month of the following year (1819, Year of the Rabbit).
