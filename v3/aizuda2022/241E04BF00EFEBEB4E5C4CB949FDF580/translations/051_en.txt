Note: He served without negligence every other year for eight years until the fourth month of Tenna 1st year [Year of the Rooster, 1681]; however, details regarding the comings and goings for duty rotation and items received as gifts are unknown.

One entry: In the sixth month of Tenna 1st year [Year of the Rooster, 1681], having received a gracious directive (go-i) and having submitted a request citing family circumstances (teyori), he was relieved of his post; moreover, as a reward, he was granted five pieces of silver (ginshi) and was appointed to Shinoda Shichibei's unit.

One entry: In the fourth month of the same [Tenna] 3rd year [Year of the Boar, 1683], he was appointed to a unit (kumitsuke). Thereafter, as he became unable to perform guard duty (o-ban) due to illness, he petitioned for a substitute guard (banshiro); his request was granted, and his son Taro-bei was appointed as substitute guard (banshiro) in his place.

One entry: On the sixth day of the second month of Genroku 3rd year [Year of the Horse, 1690], he died of illness at the age of seventy-nine. His posthumous Buddhist name (hōmyō) was Sokuyō Ryōkū Koji. His burial place is not precisely known, but [it is recorded as] Unzan Zenryūji [temple].
