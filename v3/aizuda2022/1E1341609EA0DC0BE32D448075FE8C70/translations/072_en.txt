In Kyōhō 10, year of the Snake (1725), third month, he was appointed to the ōsōsha-ban (presentation officer corps).

In Kyōhō 11, year of the Horse (1726), third month, he went up to Edo for guard duty service and returned home in the ninth month of the same year.

In Kyōhō 12, year of the Sheep (1727), fourth month, he was appointed as o-kachi-shihai (commander of foot soldiers).

In Kyōhō 13, year of the Monkey (1728), first month, his position was relieved as requested in his private petition, and he was appointed as mono-gashira (military commander).

In the fourth month of the same year, in connection with the Shogun's pilgrimage to Nikko shrines, he went to Nanzan Gojūri village for guard duty.

In the fifth month of the same year, it was announced that he had diligently performed his guard duties at Gojūri village.

In Kyōhō 15, year of the Dog (1730), tenth month, he went up to Edo for guard duty service.

In the same month, he died of illness in Edo at the age of 36. His posthumous Buddhist name was Shinjuin Gigan Zenchū. He was buried at Chōkokuji Temple in Shibuya, Edo.
