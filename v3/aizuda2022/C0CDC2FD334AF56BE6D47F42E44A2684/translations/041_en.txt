Item: In the 12th month of the 12th year of the same era (Year of the Boar, Bunka 12), having gone up to Edo to serve as an attendant on guard duty, he accompanied the lord during the latter's visit to Kyoto in the 5th month of the following year (Year of the Rat).
　　Note: During the campaign, in addition to his primary duties, he diligently served as a guard in the o-hirōma (the great reception hall), and it was also declared that he had diligently performed the additional duties of o-sōjaban-sadaka (supplementary ceremonial announcer).

Item: In the 1st month of Bunsei 3 (Year of the Dragon), his request to change his name to Hanbei was granted.

Item: In the 8th month of Bunsei 4 (Year of the Snake), upon the death of Teikan-in, he was dispatched as a fast-post messenger to Edo, and at the time of the funeral procession, he was appointed to perform the o-daibai (proxy worship/obeisance on behalf of the lord) and to serve as a formal messenger to the shogunate.

Item: In the 12th month of Bunka 3 (Year of the Tiger), when foot soldiers and long-spear bearers were all turned out in full for the cleaning of the castle moat, he went out and worked himself; as the work in the mud during the cold-turning season was an especially arduous matter, he nevertheless pressed forward diligently and conducted himself with vigor and resolution — and for this, he was presented with sake.
