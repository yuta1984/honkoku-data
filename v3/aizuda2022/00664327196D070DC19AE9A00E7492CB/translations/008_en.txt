- In the first year of Kanpō era (1741), year of the rooster, fourth month, went up to Edo as a guard duty rotation
　　　In the eleventh month of the same year, accompanied the lord's return journey from Edo
　　　　However, since the lord received orders for a journey to Kyoto, he was ordered to serve as an attendant
- In the second year of the same era (1742), year of the dog, ninth month, accompanied the lord's journey to Edo for sankin-kōtai duty and returned
- In the third year of the same era (1743), year of the boar, fourth month, was appointed as temporary metsuke (inspector) and served in the same capacity as a regular official
- In the first year of Enkyō era (1744), year of the rat, fifth month, was appointed as temporary metsuke and served in the same capacity as a regular official until the twelfth month

　　　　For this service, he was granted one ryō of gold as compensation for his efforts
- In the third year of the same era (1746), year of the tiger, third month, accompanied the lord's sankin-kōtai journey and returned
- In the fourth year of the same era (1747), year of the rabbit, ninth month, served as attendant for the lord's inspection tour of Nanzan
- In the first year of Kan'en era (1748), year of the dragon, twelfth month, was appointed to assist with construction projects for one year, serving until the fourth month of the following snake year
　　　　For this service, he was granted two bu of gold as compensation for his efforts
- In the second year of the same era (1749), year of the snake, tenth month, was appointed to assist with tax collection duties
