While residing in Ōtawara, due to certain circumstances he left that house and went down to Ōshū Aizu where he wandered for a while, then later went up to Edo again. He spent his entire life as a rōnin (masterless samurai), never serving any lord, and continued to use the surname Itabashi. He died of illness at age 82 on the 6th day of the 3rd month of Hōei 6 (1709) <<Buddhist name Rigetsu-in Sōun, buried at Entoku-ji Temple on Jōshūzan in Mita, Musashi Province>>

His son Maruemon Narimori became the adopted son of Sonobe Gohei Nagatada <<The current Kensaemon is among his descendants>>. The second son Hachibei Shigesada is the first generation of my house. The third son Seitaku Rakumyō became the adopted son of Yamada Tarōemon Koreichi <<The current Yamada Seizō is in that branch family>>. The fourth daughter <<Tsure>> became the wife of the hatamoto Ōshima Un'ya Yoshifusa. The fifth son Junsai Kiyosono became the adopted son of Iida Kosaemon Masakiyo <<The current Iida Junsa is in that lineage>>
