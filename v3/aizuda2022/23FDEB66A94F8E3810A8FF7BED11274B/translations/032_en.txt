One: In the autumn of Genroku 1 (1688), he went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in the following Mi year (Genroku 2)

One: In the spring of Genroku 3 (1690), he went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in the autumn of the same year

One: In the winter of Genroku 6 (1693), he went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in the spring of the following Inu year (Genroku 7)

One: In the 4th month of Genroku 10 (1697), he went up as an attendant for a return journey
　　However, afterward he suffered from a chronic condition of rectal prolapse and became unable to mount and dismount horses, so his guard duty was exempted as requested

One: In Hōei 4 (1707) [month unknown], his official duties were exempted as requested, and he received ko-bushin allowance for recuperation

One: On the 6th day of the 9th month of Shōtoku 4 (1714), he died of illness at the age of 73
　His posthumous Buddhist name was Hōyo Shūen Koji, buried at Ganshōju-ji temple on Mount Kanō

Kyūtarō [Due to poor health, he declined heir status and was granted permanent leave as requested]
　Mother: daughter of the Ikuta family
