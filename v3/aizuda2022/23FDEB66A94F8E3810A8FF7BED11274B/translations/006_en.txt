- In the 3rd month of Hōei 7 (1710), he was appointed as a temporary employee at the accounting office.

- In the 5th month of Shōtoku 2 (1712), he was granted a stipend for three people and summoned to serve as a minor official at the Edo accounting office.

- In the 9th month of the same year, due to his late father's misconduct, his Edo stipend for one person was reduced, and he was granted four ryō and a stipend for three people.

- In the 7th month of Kyōhō 1 (1716), he was granted an increase of one person's worth of Edo stipend.

- In the 3rd month of Kyōhō 4 (1719), he was appointed as Edo warehouse administrator.

- In the 3rd month of Kyōhō 6 (1721), because he performed his duties as warehouse administrator diligently and well, he was granted an increase of one ryō in gold.

- In the 2nd month of Kyōhō 8 (1723), his official title was changed to "Ōmakanaiyaku" (Grand Provisioning Officer), and he was ordered to oversee various disbursements, establish proper procedures, and perform his duties with careful attention.

- In the 11th month of the same year, he was appointed as Edo accounting inspector, concurrently serving as Grand Provisioning Officer, and because he performed his provisioning duties well, he was granted three bu in gold as a reward.

- In the 8th month of Kyōhō 9 (1724), he worked diligently in both his primary and concurrent duties...
