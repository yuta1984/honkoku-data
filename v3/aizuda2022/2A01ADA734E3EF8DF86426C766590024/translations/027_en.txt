One entry: In the fourth month of Bunka 4, Year of the Rabbit (1807), since his son Naozō had recently died of illness, he petitioned to adopt Eigaku, the second son of Yajima Genshirō, as his heir, which was granted. Since there were also labor expenses for going out, he was granted 3 bu in gold as expense compensation.

One entry: In the same month, when firearms were to be sent down to Aizu this time, official documents bearing the lord's name and seal and countersigned by the Senior Councilors were to be sent to Nakata Checkpoint, so he carried them and presented them to the checkpoint. He was ordered to transport the said firearms and return via ferry at Bōkawa. He departed from Edo on the 23rd of the same month, presented the official documents to the checkpoint on the 24th, and after the inspection was completed, he dispatched the firearms and returned on the 25th.
Note: He was granted 500 mon in gold as expense compensation.

One entry: In the first month of Bunka 6, Year of the Snake (1809), having served diligently for several years and devoted himself wholeheartedly to both his assigned duties and emergency tasks, he was granted an additional stipend of 5 koku beyond his previous role stipend.

One entry: In the same month, since there were labor expenses for internal affairs, he was granted 3 bu in gold as expense compensation.
