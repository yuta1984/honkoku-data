- In the third month of Kanpo 3, year of the tiger (1743), he went up to Edo for guard duty as part of the lord's sankin-kotai procession

- In the ninth month of the same year, he was appointed to wear formal crested garments during the banquet held by the roju (senior councilors) on the occasion of the shogun's appointment ceremony

- In the same month, having served diligently and well during the campaign, he was granted three pieces of silver as a reward

- In the tenth month of the same year, he returned to the domain

- In the third month of Kan'en 3, year of the horse (1750), he went up to Edo in service to his lord

- In the tenth month of the same year, he accompanied the remains of Lord Dojo on the journey back to the domain

- In the first month of Kan'en 4, year of the sheep (1751), it was proclaimed that he had served diligently without delay during the previous year's campaign, and he was granted three pieces of silver

- In the fourth month of Horeki 2, year of the monkey (1752), he went up to Edo for guard duty

- In the ninth month of the same year, it was proclaimed that having served diligently in actual duties for several years, he was granted an increase of fifty koku

- In the fourth month of Horeki 5, year of the boar (1755), he went up to Edo for guard duty

- In the eighth month of the same year, he petitioned for permission to change his name to Monbei
