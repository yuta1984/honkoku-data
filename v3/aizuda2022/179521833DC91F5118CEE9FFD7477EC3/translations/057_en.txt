Regarding Lord Sadaaki, in his daily upper garments and clothing, he maintained frugality and economy in all things, and continuing Lord Kyōtei's aspirations, he set aside funds from his personal treasury, intending to use them for relief efforts. However, due to his unfortunate early death, his life was short and he was unable to accumulate what could be called reserve funds. Nevertheless, based on his intentions, what little he had left was added to Lord Kyōtei's personal treasury funds, and from the money distributed annually with increasing allocations to commoners' children who had difficulties with child-rearing, a small portion was set aside to be granted to those who served diligently in close attendance. This intention was proclaimed during his lifetime, and accordingly, 1 ryō and 2 bu in gold was granted.
