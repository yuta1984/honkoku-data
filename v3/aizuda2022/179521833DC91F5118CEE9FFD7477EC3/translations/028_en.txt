During times when he was ill and taking leave, he was repeatedly appointed
　　　　as a temporary replacement for the above position

- In the 5th month of the same year: For the first time, when the lord entered his domain and
　　　made a pilgrimage to Nikko-san shrine along the way, he served as an attendant and went down

- In the 8th month of the same year: For his devoted and diligent service during
　　　the long and busy period while the lord was residing in Edo, he was granted three pieces of silver
　　　as a reward, as announced

- In the 12th month of the same year: This time, regarding being ordered to serve
　　　as a messenger to Kyoto, he was to serve as an attendant walking alongside the palanquin,
　　　with meal attendants and regular attendants taking turns in pairs, just as during the lord's journeys to and from Edo,
　　　as announced

- In the 2nd month of the 13th year, Year of the Rat (1824): This time, for serving as an attendant during the lord's journey to Kyoto,
　　　he was privately granted three pieces of gold by his lordship's consideration,
　　　as announced through a retainer

- In the 4th month of the same year: He served as an attendant during the lord's journey to Kyoto,
　　　and during the stay in Kyoto, he served as advance guard attendant during
　　　imperial court visits and various pilgrimages to temples and shrines

- In the 12th month of the same year:
　　　Since there was a young lord, he endured various hardships,
　　　worked diligently in coordination with others, and even served night watch duties
