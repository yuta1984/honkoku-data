Entry: In the fifth month of Tenmei 5 (1785), he had driven stakes into the Tōrikawa river in front of his residence to soak cocoons. When Jūsuke from Higashikido village removed the stakes without permission, he reproached him. Jūsuke replied that it was acceptable since it was for water diversion. Considering this an unreasonable argument, he rashly struck him twice. If this was an unpardonable matter, he should have reported it to the proper authorities, but he did not.
