- In the 3rd month of Meiwa 4th year (1767), because he had served diligently in his duties for several years, served as sparring partner for martial arts together with his son, and his son Saemon had applied himself to martial arts instruction and academic studies, teaching reading and other subjects to many disciples, he was granted 25 koku and stipend for 4 retainers, and it was declared that he would be appointed to the lord's personal guard.

- In the 4th month of the same year, for the lord's return to the domain, he was appointed to lodging arrangements duties during the journey and went down after completing this service.

- In the 5th month of the same year, because he carried out the lodging arrangements without delay, he was given one piece of silver as a reward.

- In the 1st month of Meiwa 5th year (1768), both father and son were appointed to regular attendance duty.

- In the 2nd month of the same year, for the lord's journey to the capital, he was appointed to the entourage and moved residence to go up [to Edo].

- In the 4th month of the same year, during the journey when the rivers were in flood, because he served diligently, he was given two bundles of cotton as a reward.

- In the 12th month of the same year, because his son Hikogoro had recently died of illness, his request to make his third son Tomonosuke his heir was granted.
