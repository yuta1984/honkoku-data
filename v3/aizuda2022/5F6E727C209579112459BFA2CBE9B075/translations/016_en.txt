- In the 3rd month of Meiwa 7th year (1770), for the lord's attendance in Edo (sankin), he served in the entourage and went up [to Edo].

- In the 3rd month of the same year, regarding Lord Kyōjō's martial arts practice, because being stationed at the Shiba residence would make him too distant when suddenly summoned, it was declared that he would be stationed at the upper residence.

- In the 4th month of the same year, it was declared that he had served diligently in the lord's entourage during the journey without any absences.

- In the same month, because he had served diligently for several years, as an act of benevolence his son Tomonosuke was granted 25 koku and stipend for 4 retainers, and it was declared that he would be appointed to regular attendance guard duty.

- In the 6th month of the same year, he was appointed to temporary inspector (metsuke) duty and served until the intercalary 6th month of the same year.

- In the intercalary 6th month of the same year, he was appointed to temporary treasury keeper (nando) duty, and was ordered to appear for assistant entourage duty when his treasury duties were free.
  However, since he had already been appointed to oversee the Fukagawa residence, he continued to serve night watch duties and other responsibilities.
