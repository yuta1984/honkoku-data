1. In the third year of Genroku, year of the Horse (month unknown), when [Takahashi Banzō] was to travel to Kyoto regarding Moriyama Sakon'emon's archery skills, he was accompanied by Higuchi Tomouemon and traveled there by his own means. He performed a thousand shots at the temple grounds, and when he returned the following year of the Sheep, he was told in Edo that "despite being young, he served well" and was granted an audience with the lord.

1. In the sixth year of the same era, year of the Rooster, third month, because his archery skills were excellent, he was awarded five silver pieces as a reward.

1. In the eighth year of the same era, year of the Boar, twelfth month, because he diligently practiced archery without neglect, he was granted ten-person stipend, summoned to service, and appointed to a position above the minor guards (koban).

1. In the sixteenth year of the same era, year of the Sheep, ninth month, he was granted a standard stipend of twenty-five koku and four-person allowance, and appointed to the lord's escort guard (gokkyōban).

1. In the first year of Hōei, year of the Rooster, fourth month, for the lord's attendance in Edo (sankin), he served as escort guard, traveled to Edo, served one tour of duty, and returned.

1. In the second year of the same era, year of the Rooster, third month, when the lord returned from Edo, he traveled back to Edo for the return journey and came back as part of the lord's escort.

1. In the sixth year of the same era, year of the Ox, seventh month, [he received] the successor position of Higuchi Tomouemon...
