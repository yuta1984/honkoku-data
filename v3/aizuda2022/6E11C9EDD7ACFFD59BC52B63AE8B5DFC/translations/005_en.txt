One entry: In the third year of Genroku, the year of the Horse (month unknown), when Moriyama Sakonemon traveled to Kyoto for archery practice, [Takahashi Banzō] accompanied him along with Higuchi Tomoemon, traveling at his own expense, and performed one thousand arrow shots before the hall. Upon the party's return the following year (the year of the Sheep), while in Edo, he was commended for his fine conduct despite his youth, and was granted an audience (o-mimai) with the lord.

One entry: In the sixth year of the same era (Genroku 6, the year of the Rooster), in the third month, for his excellence in archery, he was awarded five pieces of silver as a reward.

One entry: In the eighth year (Genroku 8, the year of the Boar), in the twelfth month, for his diligent and unceasing archery practice, he was granted a stipend of ten persons' rations (jūnin fuchi), was summoned into service, and his rank was appointed above that of Kobан (small guard duty).

One entry: In the sixteenth year of the same era (Genroku 16, the year of the Sheep), in the ninth month, he was granted the standard allotment of 25 koku (gizaku) and a stipend of four persons' rations, and was appointed to the position of Otomo-ban (escort guard).

One entry: In the first year of Hōei, the year of the Rooster (1704), fourth month, in connection with the lord's alternate attendance (sankин), he traveled up [to Edo] to serve as escort guard, completed the first rotation of duty, and returned.

One entry: In the second year of the same era (Hōei 2), the year of the Rooster, third month, upon the lord's return journey, he traveled up [to meet the procession] and returned accompanying the lord's escort.
