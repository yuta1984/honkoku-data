The ancestor was called Kii-no-kami Sadachika, lord of Tokiwa Castle in Tamura District, Miharu, Ōshū. He governed twelve villages of Tokiwa and administered 20,000 koku of public rice fields. He had three sons and one daughter: the eldest son was Kai-no-kami, the second son was Gorōzaemon, the third son was Iga, and the daughter was married to a man called Misaka Echizen-no-kami. Kai-no-kami's son was Gorōzaemon, and his son was Tarōemon Morisada, who initially served Tozawa Etchū-no-kami-sama and later Torii Ukyōnosuke-sama. However, in the thirteenth year of Kan'ei (1636), the year of the rat, the Torii family was abolished.

[Left page]
As a result, he became a masterless samurai (rōnin), but in the eighth month of the same year, when Dotsū-sama entered Mogami domain, he was summoned into service.

1. In the eighth month of Kan'ei 13 (1636), year of the rat, he was granted 75 koku and summoned as a deputy administrator (daikan).

1. In the eighth month of Kan'ei 20 (1643), year of the sheep, when the lord transferred to Aizu, he accompanied him and was appointed as deputy administrator of Inawashiro.

1. In the tenth month of Shōhō 1 (1644), year of the monkey, he was appointed to the guard unit at Akadaniguchi in Ogawa-shō.
