Kan'en 1, year of the dragon (1748): When the Koreans returned to their country, he was ordered to provide escort horses to Maisaka in Tōtōmi Province and also serve concurrently as an accompanying envoy. In the 5th month of the same year, he went up to Edo, and in the 6th month, he handled the Korean departure from Edo without incident. At Hamamatsu in Tōtōmi Province, together with Iinuma Kanzaemon, he served as an envoy to the lodgings of Lord Sō Tsushima-no-kami, escorted them without error to Maisaka, handed over the Koreans at the harbor, returned to Edo in the same month, and came back down in the 7th month.

   Note: It was announced in Edo that he had handled the Korean mission without incident.

2. 3rd month of Kan'en 3, year of the horse (1750): When he went up for sankin-kōtai duty service, in the 9th month of the same year Lord Hoshina (Dotsū-sama) passed away, and in the 10th month, when his remains were brought down to Aizu, he served as guard at the main lodgings during the journey and accompanied the procession down.
   In the 12th month of the same year: For his diligent service during duty tour, he was granted 1 piece of silver as a reward.
