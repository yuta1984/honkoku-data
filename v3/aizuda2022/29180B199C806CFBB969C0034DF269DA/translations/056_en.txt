1. In the 10th month of the same year, due to official business, he was ordered to go up to Edo.

1. In the 11th month of the same year, he went up to Edo, was summoned to the lord's presence, and served well as a literary discussion partner. Since he remained there throughout the year, he was privately granted five ryō of gold, as announced.

1. On the 8th day of the intercalary 12th month of the same year, he died of illness at the age of seventy. His posthumous Buddhist name was Hakkōin Kanshō Nichiroku Koji, and he was buried at Kongōji Temple in Koishikawa, Edo.
