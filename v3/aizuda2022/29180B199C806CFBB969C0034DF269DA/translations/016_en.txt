...and he was appointed to hon-jin guard duty, as was proclaimed.

• In the same month, since he had advanced in years, his attendance duty during peacetime in Edo was excused, and it was proclaimed that he could attend at his own discretion when it was convenient for him.

• In the third month of the same year, for his diligent service during [the lord's] residence in the domain, he was granted two silver pieces as a reward.

• In the same month, as part of the lord's retinue for sankin-kotai duty rotation to Edo, he served as hon-jin guard during the journey to Edo.

• In the ninth month of the same year, when he requested through Ueda Denji to present a certificate of proficiency in horsemanship, [the lord] was greatly pleased and granted him sake.

• In the same month, after the formal dress inspection was completed, when sake was being consumed at the horse viewing grounds, he was granted an audience and given sake. On that occasion, he received a gracious word that the eel in the small dish from the meal was a delicacy for elderly nourishment, and [the lord] directly bestowed that delicacy upon him.

• In the same month, when [the lord] summoned horses during his rotation duty, for his diligent service, he was granted one silver piece as a reward.
