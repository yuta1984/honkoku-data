Nuinōnosuke Mitsuhiro　　(Initially Dainosuke, initially Tomoyuki, born in Genbun 1st year (1736))
　Mother: Daughter of the Namikawa family
　Wife: Suwa Isuke Yorinori　　(Died of illness on Kyōwa 2nd year (1802), 1st month, 25th day at age 61. Buddhist name: Bairin Shōkei Daishi, buried in the same location as above)

One　In Tenmei 1st year (1781), 3rd month, by gracious consideration was granted a three-person stipend.

One　In Enkyō 1st year (1744), 8th month, was granted his first audience as requested by his father.

One　In Hōreki 9th year (1759), 2nd month, inherited without discrepancy a 200-koku stipend and served castle guard duty under Sugita Gorōbei's unit.

One　In the same 10th year (1760), 3rd month, went up to Edo for guard duty rotation and completed one tour before returning.

One　In the same 11th year (1761), 4th month, was appointed to the lord's escort guard.
　　During this service, he frequently received commendation and was granted rewards.
