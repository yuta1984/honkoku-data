Since he had been appointed to the inspector role not long before, if he had any thoughts or concerns, he should speak freely without reservation so that official duties would not be hindered, as was announced.

- In the same month, because his regular duties were extremely busy, he was relieved of his temporary inspector role.

- In the 4th month of the 11th year of Kyōhō (1726), Year of the Horse, because Kajiwara Yasaburō was ill, he was appointed to the temporary inspector role, but since his regular duties were extremely busy, by his lordship's gracious consideration he was excused from this duty, as was announced.

- In the 6th month of the same year, he was working diligently and performing well, but since his regular duties were numerous and he was also ordered to consult freely regarding inspector duties without reservation, for his exceptional hardship and dedication he was granted three pieces of silver. Regarding the Mita duty inspector position, rotation would be implemented from now on, and although his lordship recognized that taking over would involve many busy tasks and be burdensome, he was ordered to discuss all matters openly without concealment.
