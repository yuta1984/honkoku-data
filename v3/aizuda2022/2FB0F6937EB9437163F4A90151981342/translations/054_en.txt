(Continued from previous page) Having accompanied Lady Shundō-in at her wedding ceremony, he was granted seven *ryō* of preparation money (*shitakukin*).

　　Note: He had repeatedly received rewards such as payments for ceremonial clothing (*chakufuku-dai*) and other such grants in connection with his service, but since the records thereof were lost in a fire, the details are unknown.

One: In the same fifth year, the Year of the Monkey (An'ei 5, 1776), on the sixteenth day of the twelfth month, he died of illness at the age of sixty-six.
　　Posthumous Buddhist name (*hōmyō*): Kōgakuin Shin'yo Ichidō Shinshi　Place of burial: Kōdai-in, Mikitani, Mita, Edo

Daughter (Soyo):
　　Mother: same as above (i.e., the wife mentioned previously)

　　　Childhood name: Hatsu
　　　She entered service with Lord Tsune of Toko [土常様], and later became the birth mother of Lady Zuikō-in. After being granted her leave of service, she became the wife of Nakamura Tadauemon Nagamasa, a retainer of Lord Nagai Hyūga-no-Kami.

Daughter (unnamed):　　　　Died of illness on the twentieth day of the seventh month of Hōreki 7, the Year of the Ox (1757).
　　　　　　　　　　　　Posthumous Buddhist name: Yōgyō Shōei Dōjo　Place of burial: same as above (Kōdai-in, Mikitani, Mita, Edo)
