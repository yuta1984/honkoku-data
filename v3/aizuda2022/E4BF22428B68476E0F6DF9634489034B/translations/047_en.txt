That matter

1. In the 9th month of Kanpō 1 (1741), Year of the Rooster, when his father was appointed to accompany the lord's journey to Kyoto, he petitioned and went up to the capital together with the party, serving in medical treatment for both serious and minor ailments during the journey and stay in Kyoto.

1. In the 3rd month of Enkyō 2 (1745), Year of the Ox, for the Wakamori ascent, he was appointed as an attendant and sent up the mountain.
　　However, for this service, he was secretly given a reward of 300 hiki of gold, and thereafter whenever appointed to such duties, he was given the same amount each time.

1. In the 8th month of Kan'en 1 (1748), Year of the Dragon, he was granted a hereditary stipend of 100 koku without change and was appointed as a physician. The training allowance that had been granted until then was withdrawn.

1. In the 1st month of Hōreki 1 (1751), Year of the Sheep, he petitioned to change his name to Kyōan, and this was approved as requested.

1. In the 2nd month of the same year, he was appointed to serve the domain inspectors (kuni-metsuke), and was ordered to serve not only during their residence but also as an attendant during their travels to and from Edo.
