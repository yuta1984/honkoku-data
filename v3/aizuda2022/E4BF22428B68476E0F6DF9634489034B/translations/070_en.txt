Fourth Generation
Chōhachi Shigenori (original name: Ushishirō, also called Chōhaku), born on the 19th day of the first month of Tenmei 1 (1781)

Mother: Same as above (daughter of the Higuchi family)
Wife: Tsuna, daughter of Komatsu Shichirō-zaemon Toshimichi (born on the 11th day of the first month of Tenmei 8, 1788)

1. Second month of Tenmei 8 (1788): As requested by his father's petition, he was granted an initial audience with his head completely shaved.

2. Twelfth month of Kansei 5 (1793): Due to his diligence in medical studies and his excellent reading at the Confucian academy, as well as his composition of poetry, he was granted the "Chūzō Zensho" as a reward.

3. Eleventh month of Kansei 7 (1795): Having been evaluated as one who excels in scholarship with good memory and skills superior to his age, he was ordered to concentrate on Confucian studies while concurrently practicing medicine. However, since the matter of returning to secular life was not an easy matter, he was to continue in his current status.
