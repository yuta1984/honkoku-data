[Continued from the previous page: was granted a stipend of 9 koku and a ration allowance for 2 persons] and was summoned to serve as a Junior Superintendent of Construction (普請小奉行).

1\. In the 7th month of the same 20th year, Year of the Ram, Kan'ei \[1643\], upon the lord's acquisition of the residence at Shiba in Edo, he and Funao Kuemon were both summoned to travel to Edo, and in the 8th month of the same year, he served in the escort party upon the lord's entry into Aizu and accompanied the procession back.

2\. In the 9th month of the same year, he was appointed to serve as a checkpoint guard (関所番) at Tochihori Village in Ogawa-no-shō.

3\. In the 11th month of Shōhō 3, Year of the Dog \[1646\], he was granted an increase of 6 koku and a ration allowance for 1 person, and was reassigned to the Akatani checkpoint (赤谷御番所).

4\. In the Year of the Rat, Kanbun 12 \[1672\] (month unknown), due to illness, his son Kyūzaemon was appointed to serve in his place.

5\. On the 8th day of the 12th month of Enpō 5, Year of the Snake \[1677\], he died of illness at the age of 72. Posthumous Buddhist name: Shingai Muhō Koji. Place of burial: Shōunzan Zenryūji Temple.
