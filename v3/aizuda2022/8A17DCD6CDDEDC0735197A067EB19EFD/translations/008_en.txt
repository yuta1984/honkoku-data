One item (continued from previous page): As his condition showed no sign of rapid recovery, he was assigned to *ko-fushin* (a reserve/inactive status for retainers) as requested.

One item: In the 8th month of Shōtoku 3 (1713), his son Umon was appointed as his replacement in the watch duty (*bandai*), as requested.

One item: On the 8th day of the 6th month of Shōtoku 4 (1714), he died of illness at the age of seventy-six. Posthumous Buddhist name (*hōmyō*): Shinjōin Hōjun. Place of burial: Hōshōzan Jōkōji temple. After death, following Confucian funeral rites, his posthumous honorific name (*shigō*) was revised to "Hitsu," and he is recorded as Koya Sōzaemon Shigefumi.
