One: In the sixth month of Kansei 7 (1795), for practicing foot movements in hunting, he was to attend the designated location to assist Kurokawachi Jūdayū in conducting combined formation drills with samurai and foot soldiers of the unit. It was announced that when Jūdayū was frequently unable to attend due to other official duties or obstacles, he should generally fill in and perform the duties.

One: In the twelfth month of the same year, during the foot movement practice for Obara Naiki's unit's hunting exercises, he managed the arrangements well by himself for several months, and in addition to his primary duties, he was also assigned to assist the military magistrate. It was announced that he had been diligent and performed extraordinary service since the practice sessions.

One: In the third month of Kansei 8 (1796), he handled the tozama samurai of his unit well and worked diligently to encourage military preparedness, for which he was granted three pieces of silver as a reward.

One: In the first month of Kansei 9 (1797), having served diligently for several years concurrently as head supervisor of military lectures, it was announced that he was granted three pieces of silver as a reward.
