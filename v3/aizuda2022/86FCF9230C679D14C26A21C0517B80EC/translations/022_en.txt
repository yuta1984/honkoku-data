1　In January of the 18th year (1733), as requested by his superior Kayano Gonbei, he was relieved of his duties and transferred to Ohara Gorōemon's unit as a replacement

1　In March of Genbun 1 (1736), as requested by his superior Ohara Gorōemon upon his retirement, he was transferred to Hinata Saburōemon's unit as a replacement

1　In the same month, he went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in October of the same year

1　In August of Kanpō 1 (1741), he was suddenly summoned to Edo and ordered to accompany the lord to Kyoto, serving as main camp guard during the journey. In September of the same year, he was appointed as a messenger and arrived in Kyoto ahead of the lord. After returning to Edo, he was granted leave and returned home in November

1　In March of the 2nd year (1742), he was granted the family inheritance of 400 koku of stipend without any reduction

1　In June of the same year, following the wishes of his great-grandfather Kan'emon to revere Shinto, his request to change the family's funeral rites to Shinto was granted, and memorial services for past generations were reformed through consultation with the Yoshikawa family

1　In March of Enkyō 1 (1744), when going up to Edo for guard duty as an attendant, he served on horseback and returned in October of the same year

1　In October of the same year, as it was a year of celebratory events with particularly many messengers

1　It was proclaimed that he had diligently served multiple times as an attendant and in employment for Lord Genshōin
