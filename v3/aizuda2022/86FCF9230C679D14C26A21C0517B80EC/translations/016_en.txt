Due to frequent illness, his request was granted and he was relieved of his duties as御近習 (gokinshu, close attendant) and appointed as御旗奉行 (gohata-bugyō, banner magistrate). Although his ceremonial rank was reduced by one grade, it was still an important position, so he was instructed to serve with proper understanding.

- In the 6th year, Ox year (Hōei 6, 1709), December: As requested, he was relieved of his duties, and his son Orinosuke was appointed as御番代 (goban-dai, guard substitute).

- Kyōhō 1st year, Monkey year (1716), May 27th: He died of illness at age 72. His Buddhist posthumous name was Ryōun Sōsei Koji, buried in the same location as above. Later converted to Shinto with the spirit name Gosō Reisha.

Sanshirō - Born in Shōhō 3rd year, Dog year (1646), died on July 7th of Kanbun 1st year, Ox year (1661) at age 16 from illness. Buddhist posthumous name: Jishō Genkyū Zen Jōmon, buried in the same place as above. Later converted to Shinto with spirit name Jishō Shinrei.
Mother: Daughter of the Tamura clan

Gidayū Sōbu (originally named Sōshige) - Separate lineage
Mother: Same as above

- Kanbun 10th year, Dog year (1670), July: As a favor to his father Kan'emon, he was summoned to serve as御供番 (gotomoban, escort guard) and granted standard stipend.

- Enpō 4th year, Dragon year (1676), March: According to the petition made during his late father's lifetime, he was granted a 200-koku stipend allocation, appointed to the外様組 (tozama-gumi, outer retainer group), and joined Hori Han'emon's unit.
Note: His previous stipend was withdrawn.
