One: The matter of being granted his first formal audience with his lord.

One: In the third month of the same year, he was granted the hereditary stipend of 400 koku without change and was assigned to Tanaka Kahei's unit.

One: In the sixth month of the same year, as his ailment would not improve, he petitioned to submit a koshin fee while convalescing, which was granted.

One: In the first month of Tenmei 2 (1782), as his ailment had recovered, he petitioned to return to duty, which was granted.

One: In the third month of Tenmei 8 (1788), he was appointed as bantō-soeyaku (assistant group leader), his seat was placed above the monogashira, and it was announced that he was granted the saihai (commander's baton) due to his official duties.
　　　However, in the eighth month of the following year of the Rooster, his title was changed to bantō-kumigashira (group head).

One: In the fifth month of Kansei 3 (1791), he was appointed to concurrently serve as head supervisor of military lectures. He was ordered to consult with Kurokawachi Jūdayū to take charge of various matters and handle administrative arrangements, and was granted three ryō of gold annually for expenses.
　　　However, due to this appointment, he was exempted from kyoyaku chakutō (residence duty attendance).

One: In the eighth month of the same year, as he had long been diligent in understanding the principles of the current martial school, it was announced that he was granted permission to wear the odoshi (laced armor) so that he could be appointed for emergency duties when needed.

One: In the third month of Kansei 4 (1792), he was ordered to participate in the tori-gari (bird hunting) at Hongō-hara. When he appeared with permission as a marksman, since he had been granted permission to wear odoshi, during unit hunting exercises he was to be present in each formation and pay attention to various signals from the command post, following the jo-ha-kyū (introduction-development-climax) sequence...
