**[Right folio]**
- In the tenth month of the same year (the third year of Jōō [1654]), he received an additional stipend of 5 *koku*.
- In the third month of the third year of Kanban, *Bō* year (1663), the provisional yield (*gizaku*) that had been granted for 33 *koku* of newly developed paddy land that he had reclaimed through his own efforts was dissolved, and the new land was formally assessed at 100 *koku*.
- In the fifth year of Kanbun, *Mi* year (1665) (month unknown), he was additionally appointed to the role of rewriting women's travel permits (*onna tegata kakikae*) at the Sekishō (barrier station) of Ishima and Akatani.
- In the sixth year of Kanbun, *Uma* year (1666) (month unknown), as he had advanced greatly in age and had become unable to mount and dismount a horse freely even for travel to Wakamatsu, he petitioned for permission to send his son Hikojirō up to the castle town to handle all affairs, including castle town duties, the wax and lacquer tax (*rōshitsu-yaku*) silver assessments, accounting, and all other matters. This petition was granted.

**[Left folio]**
- In the third month of the eighth year of Enpō, *Saru* year (1680), the 100 *koku* of newly developed land was formally converted into 100 *koku* of regular domain stipend (*chigyō*).
- In the second month of the first year of Tenna, *Tori* year (1681), he was provisionally appointed as district magistrate (*daikan*) of Ogawashō, with his son Hikojirō carrying out the duties. As a reward for this service, they were granted two pieces of silver (*ginsu nima*).
- On the twenty-third day of the tenth month of the second year of Jōkyō, *Ushi* year (1685), he died of illness at the age of 82. His posthumous Buddhist name (*hōmyō*) was Kotsugandōtetsu (骨巌道徹). His place of burial was Shōhōji temple in Tsugawa-machi.
