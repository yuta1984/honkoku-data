• Kyōwa 1st year, Tori cycle (1801), 8th month: This time, when Higuchi Jūdayū went up to Edo as duty officer to examine and manage various regulatory affairs and extraordinary revenue matters, he was ordered to go to Edo to perform these duties. He went up and completed his duties, returning in the 4th month of the following Inu year.
　　Note: At the time of departure, he was appointed as temporary assistant foot inspector and accompanied the court ladies on their journey.
• Same year, 10th month: For accompanying the court ladies to Edo without incident, he was privately granted two bu of gold as a reward.
• Same 2nd year, Inu cycle (1802), 8th month: For skillfully managing the chief administrator's regular coordination duties and for diligently performing regulatory coordination work during his trips to Edo, he was granted one person's stipend as official salary in addition to his current salary.
• Same month: Regarding the chief administrator's regular duties, in order to ensure smooth communication of official business between Edo and Aizu, he was ordered to serve joint internal duty shifts with personnel from the regulatory department starting from the autumn duty rotation. He departed in the same year's 9th month, but due to illness, he petitioned and returned in the 1st month of the following Hitsuji year.
