Two positions were reduced, and he was granted a five-person stipend and summoned to the Aizu Koban (palace guard).

- In Enkyō 3 (1746), 7th month, due to his diligent service, his current stipend allowance was increased to 15 koku and three-person stipend by direct order.

- In the 10th month of the same year, he went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in the 4th month of the following year of the Rabbit.

- In Kan'en 2 (1749), 6th month, he was appointed as personal attendant to Lord Kantsū'in.

- In the 3rd year (1750), 8th month, as requested, he was relieved of his official duties and reassigned to the Koban.

- In Hōreki 2 (1752), 10th month, he requested and was granted permission to adopt Junta, the son of Nagai Buuemon, as his son-in-law and heir.

- On the 3rd day of the same month, he died of illness at age 51. His Buddhist name was Ryōkaku'in Seiun, buried at the same place (Ansenji Temple).
