○Religious Sects
The monks of this country are not permitted to enter Tang China to transmit Buddhist teachings, so they come to Satsuma Province to study the Dharma. They wear clothing of a reddish-brown color and, in addition to the kesa (Buddhist stole), they wear another garment. This system is like a vest and is called "danzoku" (severing worldly ties). Their caps are like the hat-caps of Chinese people and are made of felt. The religious sects are only Rinzai Zen and Shingon, as recorded in the Chuzan Denshin-roku.

○Funeral Rites
All the people in the country practice cremation. In the houses of officials or influential people, they sometimes first bury the body as is, then after some time has passed, dig it up and cremate it. They also practice water burial, removing the decayed flesh, placing the white bones in jars, storing them in stone caves, and when conducting memorial services, they open them to view the remains.

○Coffins and Tombs
Coffins are made round, about three feet in height. They wash the deceased's knees with hot water, bend the legs, make them sit cross-legged, and place them in the coffin. For graves, they dig holes in mountains to bury the dead and pile up stones. Noble families have these stones finely polished,
