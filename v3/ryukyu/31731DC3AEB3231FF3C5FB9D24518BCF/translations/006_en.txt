Those are called "Chikudun no Oyakumo" (某筑登之親雲上). The 6th junior rank Jotokuro and 7th junior rank Jokoro are all called "Okite Oyakumo" (某掟親雲上). The 8th rank red hat officials are called "Sato no Ko" (某里之子), and those who hold territorial domains are called "Chi Satozhu" (某地里主). The 9th rank red hat officials are called "Chikudun" (筑登之), and those not yet in official ranks are called "Ko" (某子). None of them use family names. The specific listings are as follows:

State Minister (Kokuso) - 1 person, Senior 1st rank - Appointed from among the king's uncles who possess talent and strategic ability

Principal Lord (Genko) - Senior 1st rank - King's sons and brothers receive this peerage

Regional Lords and District Lords (Gunko Yuko) - Junior 1st rank - Descendants of Principal Lords receive this peerage. Those with merit are promoted and granted the same rank as Principal Lords

Legal Officers (Hoshi) - 3 people, Senior 2nd rank - Appointed from the king's maternal relatives and meritorious vassals. The Heavenly Bureau of Rites, Earthly Bureau of Agriculture, and Human Bureau rotate duties at the royal palace. For major matters, they convene to deliberate and report to the State Minister. Additional Legal Officers hold the same rank.

Purple Cap Officials (Shikin-kan) - Junior 2nd rank - Regional or district nobles serving as high ministers without fixed numbers. Some also hold additional Legal Officer titles.

Surveillance Officials (Jimoku-kan) - 4 people, Senior 3rd rank - Guest Affairs Surveillance Official (local name: Gosashigawa) 1 person, Protocol Surveillance Official (local name: Gososigamikuri) 1 person, Criminal Affairs Surveillance Official (local name: Hyotogawa) 1 person, Port Management Surveillance Official (local name: Tomari Jito) 1 person. All participate in political deliberations. All are called "Attendants" (local name: Moshikuchi-shu). Additional Attendants (local name: Moshikuchi-za) hold Junior 3rd rank with no fixed numbers.

Advisory Officials (Sangi-kan) - Senior 4th rank - Assist the Attendants as financial officials in deliberating various governmental matters.
