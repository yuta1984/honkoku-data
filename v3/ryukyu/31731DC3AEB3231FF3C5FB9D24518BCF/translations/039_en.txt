Jimoku-kan as well as doshi-sei and doshi-dōchi all [receive] forty shi, sangi-kan all receive stipend
　payments of sixteen shi
　Naha-kan fiefs are one county, with stipends of eighty shi
　Attari-kan receive stipend payments of ten shi
　The above are the numbers of fiefs, stipends, and salaries for the home country officials of Shuri, Tomari, and Naha
　Shikin-taifu fiefs are one village, with stipends of fifty shi, eighty shi, or one hundred twenty
　shi, all granted according to the magnitude of merit. For hereditary stipends, heirs are granted fiefs and stipends of fifty shi
　or forty shi, and for grandsons differences are made according to merit《annotation: In the late Ming period there was a shikin-taifu named Sai Ken who
　received a stipend of three hundred shi and whose heir was granted one hundred shi》
　《annotation: After the Kangxi kuimao year, this was reduced to fifty
　shi, grandsons forty shi, and now great-grandsons thirty shi》
　Seigi-taifu fiefs are one county, with stipends of twenty shi or thirty shi, with differences made according to merit
　Those not yet granted stipends receive annual salary payments of twelve shi
　Chūgi-taifu fiefs are one county, with annual salary payments of ten shi
　Chōshi fiefs are one county, with stipends of twenty shi
　Tsū-tsūji receive stipend payments of eight shi, some with fiefs and some without fiefs
　Fuku-tsūji receive stipend payments of five shi
　Tsūji receive stipend payments of four shi
　Shūsai receive stipend payments of two shi《annotation: Originally three shi, but after the Kangxi kiigai
　year reduced to one shi》
　Waka-shūsai [receive] one shi《annotation: Originally two shi, but after the kiigai year reduced to one shi
　Kume children become waka-shūsai at age seven and immediately receive stipends at Shuri》
