These two temples are located by the seashore and serve as places where the king makes vows and offers to Buddha, hence their rice stipends are second [in amount]. There are also Manju-ji, Shintoku-ji, Shōgen-ji, Ryūfuku-ji, and Ankoku-ji, which, regardless of the number of monks, receive eight koku of rice annually (one theory states this is rations for four people, with one koku, three to, and five shō per person, totaling five koku and four to).
