　Legal officials and below ordinarily use green ground printed cloth umbrellas, but now it is observed that each person uses floral rain umbrellas, with one person performing foot-stamping and bell-ringing in front of each person.
　　Sitting Cushions
　First rank: red-brown center, blue-brown border, lined with red felt
　Second rank: blue cloth center, red cloth border, lined with red felt
　Third rank: blue cloth surface, lined with red felt
　Fourth and fifth ranks: both use indigo cloth surface, lined with white felt
　Sixth rank and below: do not use sitting cushions, only use felt strips
　　Noble Clans

【Left Page】
　The four great surnames of Shuri are Shō, Ō, Mō, and Ba. The Shō clan is a branch family of the royal Shō surname, but being somewhat distantly related, they are called Shō to distinguish them. Therefore, they do not intermarry with the royal family through generations. Among the native people, only the three families Ō, Mō, and Ba have marriage relations with the royal family, serving as royal uncles and legal officials through generations. Currently there are three people serving as legal officials: Ba Kenzu, Ō Jidō, Shō Seikinkokujō, and Mō Kunshū《Note: maternal grandfather of current King Shō Kei; the queen consort is of the Ba clan》. The genealogies of all are unclear in detail, and we plan to continue investigation and list the family trees.
　The Kume Thirty-six Surnames were all Min people (Fujian people) granted during the Hongwu and Yongle reigns. By the Wanli period, only five surnames remained: Cai, Zheng, Liang, Jin, and Lin. In the 35th year of Wanli, two more surnames were granted: Ruan and Mao. The descendants of each surname have not flourished greatly. Many others have achieved status and become distinguished through naturalization, but they are not from the old granted-surname families. Currently examining the genealogies of the nine surnames, many are engaged in scholarly learning and national studies
