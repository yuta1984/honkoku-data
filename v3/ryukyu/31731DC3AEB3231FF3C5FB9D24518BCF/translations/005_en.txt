　　Government System
　The government system's rank structure roughly follows China, divided into nine grades of senior and junior ranks. High officials in important positions also have additional administrative roles. All officials, great and small, govern territories as fiefs. Royal brothers, royal uncles, and state ministers are all called princes of certain places, and those governing one prefecture are called按司 of certain places (under the old system, each prefecture was governed by one按司).
