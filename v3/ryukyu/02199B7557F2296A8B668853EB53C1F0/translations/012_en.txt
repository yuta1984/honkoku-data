Although Ryukyu was originally a tributary island of our country, it was a distant island nation, and since exchanges with that country had ceased early on, what occasionally appeared in written records was merely one or two entries, with nothing recording detailed information. In Kōbō Daishi's Shōryōshū, there is a passage stating "The southern wind fans in the morning and crushes the liver of Tamra's wolf-heart; the northern wind arises in the evening and causes Rukyū's tiger-nature to lose its courage." This was written on behalf of the Tang envoy Kanō to present to the Governor of Fuzhou, and this was in the 23rd year of Enryaku. Also, in the Konjaku Monogatari, in the biography of Chishō Daishi, on the 9th day of the 8th month of the 3rd year of Jinju, when the Song merchant Ryōki, who had been in Chinzei for years, was returning to Song, [the master] boarded his ship. The next day, around the hour of the dragon, they drifted to Ryukyu Kingdom. That country is in the middle of the sea and is a country where people eat people. At that time the wind died down and they did not know which direction to go. Looking far toward the land, they saw dozens of people wandering about carrying spears. Seeing this, Ryōki wept sadly.
