Incidentally, there exists a popular historical work called "Satsuma-Ryukyu Military Chronicles" (Satsuryū Gundan). Although the author of this book is not clear, it has circulated widely, and those who speak of the war between the two countries invariably use it as their source. According to this work, during the time of Shimazu Hyūga-no-kami Yoshihiro, lord of Satsuma Province, the supreme commander Niiro Musashi-no-kami Tadaemon, along with Tanegashima Taizen, Sano Tatewaki, and others, crossed the sea with a total force of 101,854 soldiers. It also mentions place names in that country such as Undan Port, Chikuko Castle, Beisō Island, and Ranja Bay, and records the names of military leaders including Chen Wenshuo, Meng Guiling, Zhu Chuanshuo, and Zhang Zhuwei. This is truly groundless fabrication, and one can tell without even examining the book's unreliable arguments.

Tribute Missions After Keichō

On the 9th day of the intercalary 7th month of Kan'ei 11 (1634), King Shō Hō of Chūzan sent congratulatory envoy Prince Sashiki, gratitude envoy Prince Kibu, and others to present tribute goods. 《Marginal note: Genkan Diary》 Since the Shogun had gone to Kyoto that year, they presented themselves at Nijō Castle. For this reason, the two envoys did not come to Edo.
