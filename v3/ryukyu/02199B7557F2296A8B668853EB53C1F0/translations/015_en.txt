Having received orders to subjugate [the target], on the thirteenth day of the third month of Kakitsu 1 (1441), Kabayama and others were dispatched as a punitive force with many soldiers under their command. At Eitoku-ji temple, a person named Yamada Shikibu killed the high priest (sōjō), and sent his head to the shōgun. It is said that Betsu Sanuki-bō, who was an official of the high priest, was also killed at this time. As a reward for this, the Ryukyu Kingdom was granted to the great lord of Satsuma Province, and commendation letters as well as swords were bestowed upon Kabayama and all the other soldiers who participated in the punitive expedition. From this time onwards, the Ryukyu Kingdom began its long relationship as a tributary state of Satsuma, maintaining annual tribute and diplomatic trade without interruption. 《Marginal note: Kyūdenshū, Sho Monzeki-fu》

Ryukyuans Come After Eikyō Era
In Bun'an 5 (1448), Ryukyuans came [to Japan]. 《Marginal note: Bunrui Nendaiki》
In the seventh month of Hōtoku 3 (1451), when a ship of Ryukyuan merchants arrived at the port of Hyōgo, the provincial governor (shugo) Hosokawa Keicho immediately sent people to select and take their merchandise without paying, and despite debts of four to five thousand kan from previous years remaining unpaid, he further confiscated their goods for sale. When the Ryukyuans petitioned about their difficulties, the kubō of the time appointed three magistrates: Fuse Shimotsuke-no-kami, Iio Yozaemon, and Iio Rokurō
