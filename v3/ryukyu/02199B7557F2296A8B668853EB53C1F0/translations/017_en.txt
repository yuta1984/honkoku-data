An official named Shana secretly consulted with Ming people, showed completely disrespectful treatment, and sent no tribute. Although Iehisa continued to send envoys to censure and question them, they would not comply. Therefore, having no choice, he requested permission to subjugate them and correct their crimes. In the spring of Keichō 14 (1609), he received imperial command. Kabayama Gonzaemon Hisataka was appointed as commander-in-chief, Hirata Tarōzaemon Masumune as vice-commander, the monk Ryūun as military strategist, and the Nanashima district magistrate as guide. Their total force numbered over three thousand men with over one hundred warships. On the twenty-second day of the second month, they weighed anchor and departed for Ryukyu Kingdom. On this occasion, each held farewell celebrations to honor the departure. Among them, the renowned warrior Niiro Musashi-no-kami Kazuuji, who in his later years became a monk and took the name Sessai, brought sake barrels and delicacies and saw them off as far as a place called Gion-no-su. When all the military forces were assembled and Kabayama Hisataka humbly declined to take the seat of honor, Niiro Sessai said, "Now, as you cross the sea as commander of the Ryukyu expedition, you are indeed the lord's representative. Please promptly take your place in the commander's seat." Thereupon, he took the seat of honor. Thus the officers and men of all forces naturally submitted in their hearts, and military orders were carried out effectively. From there they boarded ships at Yamakawa
