Thus, although the name Ryukyu is not recorded in the historical records, we should know that tribute missions from that country had already begun from the reign of Empress Suiko.

I believe this Tane Island also refers to Ryukyu. Because the names of the southern sea islands were not yet clear, since the shipping routes to Ryukyu passed through this Tane Island for travel back and forth, it was probably also called Tane Island. {{Annotation: This Tane Island refers to what is now called Tanegashima.}} Later, in the 7th year of the Wado era under Empress Genmei, it appears that all the southern sea islands submitted to imperial authority. {{Annotation: Nant≈ç-shi (Chronicle of the Southern Islands)}}
