On the twenty-eighth day of the seventh month of Bunshō 1 (1466), Ryukyuans came to the capital. This was the sixth time since the reign of Ashikaga Yoshimasa began. 《Marginal note: Saitō Chikamoto Diary》

In Tenshō 11 (1583), the Ryukyu Kingdom sent tribute. 《Marginal note: Wakan Gōun, Ikoku Ōraiki》

Upon consideration, given that the Ryukyuan commercial goods that came to Hyōgo in Hōtoku 3 are described as involving "debts from previous years," and that the visit to the capital in Bunshō 1 is noted as the "sixth time," it appears that people from that country came [to Japan] frequently after the Eikyō era. However, since records are scarce, the details cannot be thoroughly examined.

Lord of Satsuma, the Shimazu Clan, Subjugates Ryukyu

The Ryukyu Kingdom had been a tributary state of Satsuma for generations since the command of Ashikaga Yoshinori during the Kakitsu period. However, during the era of competing warlords around the Tenshō period, communication with Ryukyu was temporarily severed. Later, when the world became peaceful and the samurai and people enjoyed tranquility, the lord of Satsuma, Shimazu Iehisa, sent envoys to Ryukyu repeatedly requesting that tribute missions be resumed as before.
