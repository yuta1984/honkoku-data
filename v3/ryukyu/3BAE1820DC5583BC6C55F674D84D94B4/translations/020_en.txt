On December 13th, Tenmei 2nd year (1782), year of the Water Tiger, the ship captain Daikokuya Kodayu of the vessel Shinsho-maru from Shirako in Ise Province, along with sailor Isokichi, departed from that place and were blown adrift from off the coast of Suruga Province. On July 20th of Tenmei 3rd year, they drifted ashore on a Russian territory called the Aleutians, traveled through various islands to the Russian capital where they had an audience with the Empress, and were sent back to a place called Nemuro in Ezo on September 3rd, Kansei 4th year. On September 18th, Kansei 5th year of the Ox, they were presented for imperial inspection at Fukiage.

In Kansei 5th year of the Ox, sixteen crew members of the Wakamiya-maru from Samukasawa beach in Miyagi County, Oshu, encountered a storm on November 27th of that year and were blown to Russia. Of those sixteen people, Sahei, Tsudayu, Moheta, and Juro were delivered to Nagasaki in Bunka 2nd year of the Wood Ox.

This one volume of drift tales is presented to friends who appreciate antiquities, with sales prohibited and kept deeply secret.
