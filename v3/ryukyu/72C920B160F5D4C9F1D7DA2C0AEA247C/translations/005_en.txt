The envoy's record states: In the country's religion there exists a female king. She is of the royal family's sisters. Generation after generation they succeed each other through divine selection. When the five grains ripen, the female king crosses the sea to Mount Kudakajima. She searches for ripened grain ears and chews them. Only then do the various places dare to harvest. If someone harvests before she has tasted, if they eat it they will immediately die. Therefore, in the fields there are absolutely no thieves who steal crops.

The sixth month rice grand festival. An auspicious day is selected to worship the rice deity on the same day. There is also a sixth month festival. Throughout the country they steam glutinous rice to make rice. Families exchange it with each other. On this day they also do not do needlework or work in the fields. The same as the four festivals mentioned above. In this month on the night of the full moon, scholars and commoners all engage in tug-of-war contests to compete for victory.

The fifteenth day of the seventh month. The Bon festival to worship ancestors. In advance, on the night of the thirteenth day, every household lines up two torches outside their main gate to welcome the ancestral spirits. After the Bon festival on the fifteenth day, they send off the spirits.

The eighth month, every household worships the moon. The record of the Ming summer envoy Ziyang states that the custom has moon-waiting wishes. Generally on the nights of the third, eighteenth, and twenty-third of the month, they all prepare auspicious fruits and worship while waiting. On the night of the third, they burn incense and worship facing the moon. On the night of the eighteenth, they burn incense and wait standing. They wait for it to rise and become bright, then worship. After finishing worship, then they dare to sit. On the twenty-third day, they burn incense and wait sitting. They wait for the moon to rise, then worship. They say this can increase longevity and extend good fortune.

The White Dew is made the eighth month festival. Three days before and after, men and women all close their doors and do not engage in work. This is called "Guarding the Heavenly Grandson." During these several days, if there are disputes or various incidents, they will certainly suffer snake bites. The country's snakes emerge in the ninth month. If they bite a person, they die instantly. On the same day they steam glutinous rice mixed with red beans, make it into rice and exchange it with each other.

The twelfth month. When they encounter kanoe-ne or kanoe-uma days, the entire country makes glutinous rice cakes. They wrap them in palm leaves...
