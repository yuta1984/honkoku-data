For beasts, there are cattle, horses, sheep, pigs, dogs, cats, deer, monkeys, and wild boars. There are no tigers, rabbits, or roe deer. <<The Ming Dynasty's Yitongzhi states that the local products include bears, jackals, and wolves. Upon investigation today, none of these exist.>>

For domestic animals, there are chickens, ducks, and geese. Among unusual domestic animals is the Taihe chicken. Compared to ordinary chickens, it is particularly small with short legs and a long tail. The breed originates from the Seven Islands.

For birds, there are sparrows, crows, pigeons, herons, gulls, wild ducks, bulbuls, spotted doves, green doves <<arrive in the twelfth month>>, wild pigeons, quail, 鴗 <<commonly called "divine bird">>, field birds, pheasants, wagtails, cuckoos, and mandarin ducks. Swallows come in the seventh month but do not nest in human dwellings. Hawks drift over from the outer islands following the northeast winds in mid-ninth month. Geese occasionally appear but do not come regularly. Cranes sometimes appear, perhaps one at a time, but sightings are rare. Exotic birds include the koharuru and others.

**Koharuru**: Has golden-yellow plumage with a long beak and short tail. Sings in the fourth month.

**Maseki**: Wing feathers are green with white eyebrows. Arrives in the ninth month. Also, **Ishikyūshi** resembles the maseki.

**Karasubō**: Also called "Queen Mother Bird." Arrives in the fourth month.

**Konsatsu**: Plumage resembles a hawk but is somewhat smaller. Arrives in the eighth month.

**Yōzui**: Wings are grayish-brown with a black head. Arrives in the eighth month.

**Ishikyūdoku**: Plumage resembles a sparrow. Arrives in the tenth month and sings in spring.

**Bakudokuri**: Green plumage. Arrives in the tenth month.

For insects, there are mice, bats, and geckos that can make sounds like sparrows, doing so in both winter and summer. Lizards live in water ponds with red bellies and golden light on their backs. There are also small blue snakes with four legs that are commonly seen. The snakes in this country are extremely venomous. They emerge frequently in the ninth month.
