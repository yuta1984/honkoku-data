Utensils and Vessels
In rooms, everyone sits on mats spread on the ground. They do not use chairs or tables. All drinking and eating utensils are low and small for convenience of use. Those that differ in manufacture from mainland China are illustrated as shown on the left.

Serving Trays (Han)
These are vessels for placing bowls during meals and drinking, like the ancient ritual vessels zu and dou. The tray vessels are either square or round, all with legs attached. They are about five to six sun in height. When eating, several sets are arranged in front.

Smoking Sets (Smoking Stands)
A smoking set contains within one box: one fire brazier, one spittoon, and one smoking bowl. Several sets are placed in the room, with one set placed in front of each person. The products made for the royal palace are extremely refined and elaborate.
