Houses

When constructing houses, they are not built very high in order to avoid sea winds. They are necessarily raised three to four shaku (about 0.9-1.2 meters) from the ground to avoid ground moisture. When common people build houses, they extend tiled roofs in four directions for each room, resembling pavilions. The tiles are like Chinese ■-tiles, extremely strong and thick. Without these, they would not be able to withstand typhoons. There are no brick walls. Around all four sides of every house, boards are set up to serve as walls. For courtyard enclosing walls and outer enclosing walls, they use coral stone to build them up. The outer enclosing walls of great houses in Shuri are polished smooth on one surface as if cut, extremely solid and orderly. There is no brick paving on the ground. They mostly use raised wooden flooring, about three to four shaku high. Doors and windows have no hinges. Both upper and lower sills are carved with double groove tracks, and door panels are set within them. They slide left and right to open and close.
