They are called "certain Chikuto-no-親雲上." The Jotokurō of the sixth rank subordinate grade and the Jokōrō of the seventh rank subordinate grade are all called "certain Okite-親雲上." The eighth-rank red-cap officials are called "certain Satonoshi." Those who govern territories are called "certain regional Satunushi." The ninth-rank red-cap officials are called "Chikuto-no." Those not yet in the ranks are called "certain -ko." None use their family names. The specific listings are as follows:

Kokusō (National Minister), one position. First rank principal grade. The king's uncle with talent and strategy is appointed to this position.
Genkō (Prime Lord). First rank principal grade. The king's sons and brothers receive this peerage.
Gunko and Yūko (District and Village Lords). First rank subordinate grade. The descendants of Genkō receive this peerage. Those with merit are promoted and granted the rank of Genkō. The court rank is the same.
Hōji (Magistrates), three positions. Second rank principal grade. The king's uncles-in-law, meritorious retainers, and relatives are appointed to these positions. Tenchō oversees ceremonies, Jichō oversees agriculture, and Ninchō oversees civil administration. They serve in the royal palace in rotation. Important matters are discussed collectively and reported to the Kokusō. Additional Hōji positions have the same rank.
Shikin-kan (Purple Headband Officials). Second rank subordinate grade. Either Gunpaku or Yūhaku. High ministers of scattered ranks serving as assistant ministers. There is no fixed number. Some also hold additional Hōji titles.
Jimoku-kan (Eyes and Ears Officials) (local name: Mi-sasasoku), two positions. Third rank principal grade. Deputy Jimoku-kan, two positions. Third rank subordinate grade.
　One oversees guests, one manages treasures, one oversees criminal justice, and one manages the harbor.
Essha (Court Attendants) (also called Shinkō-kan), third rank subordinate grade. No fixed number. They participate in deliberative councils. They have no fixed duties. Additional Essha positions have the same rank.
Sangi-kan (Counseling Officials). Fourth rank principal grade. They assist the Essha and Doshi-kan in deliberating various governmental affairs.
