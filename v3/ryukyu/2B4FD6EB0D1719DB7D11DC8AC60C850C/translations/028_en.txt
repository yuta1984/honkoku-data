Postscript to the Ryukyu Shinshi

The ancestral deities of Ryukyu's creation, a male and female pair of gods, are called Amamiko. According to one text, the names of the two gods are Amamikyu and Shinerikyu. Initially, they descended upon the northernmost mountain of Ōshima, [Note: See the section on Ōshima in the Historical Records and Geography] sought out lands, arrived at present-day Okinawa Island, opened up the land, and sowed rice and millet. Their eldest son was called Tensōshi (Heavenly Grandson), their eldest daughter became a heavenly deity, and their second daughter became a sea deity, and so forth.

Upon examination, after our divine age's two honored deities (Izanagi and Izanami) gave birth to the eight great islands, they furthermore gave birth to six more islands, among which was Ōshima. The theories of earlier scholars do not specify its location in detail, but looking at Ryukyu's Ōshima today, it is even larger than Sado and Oki islands. Perhaps this is what was referred to.
