After the banquet ends, the vessels and dishes from the table are bestowed upon them. Each person carries these back with them. When the tribute envoys return to their country, they board reception ships for tribute missions. When tribute envoys have audience with the Qing emperor, they wear Ryukyu clothing. When the King of Chuzan receives investiture envoys, he wears Ming clothing. Since this was a gift from the Ming emperor in the time of their ancestors, the king is exempt from wearing it, but his retainers are not permitted to wear it. The circumference of Beijing's city walls is forty li (Tang dynasty li measurements). The Qing emperor's surname is Gioro, so many people with the Gioro surname rise to high official positions. The current Provincial Administration Commissioner of Fujian is also called Gioro Wu. The chief envoy for Ryukyu investiture is a Manchu, and the vice envoy is Han Chinese. From the above, one can see how the Qing state treats Ryukyu with special favor.
The matter of Ryukyu investiture originally arose when Yang Zai, a Herald of the Ming, was returning after inviting submission from our country [Japan] and passed by Lesser Ryukyu, persuading them that they should become internally attached to China. This did not originate from the Ming emperor's directive, it is also said.
However, the sea route from Fujian to Ryukyu is treacherous and dangerous, and when investiture envoys returned and spoke of these perils, those who heard were greatly alarmed and frightened. Therefore, there was ministerial discussion about stopping the dispatch of investiture envoys to that country and having tribute envoys carry back the imperial gifts instead, but the emperor would not listen and followed previous precedent.
