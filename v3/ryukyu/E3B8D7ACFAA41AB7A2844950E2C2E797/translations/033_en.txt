Ninth Month: Plums begin to bloom. Wutu fruits ripen 《Note: Name of a fruit called gubatisi, shaped like green fruit but flattened; also found in Fujian where it's called wutu》
Persimmons turn purple. Frost falls. Sea hawks arrive 《Note: There are no hawks in the country, but in this month northeast winds rise and they drift over from Japan》
Soybeans are harvested. Wheat seeds are sown. Thunder begins to quiet its voice 《Note: Sometimes it does not quiet until winter》 All fields are cultivated.
Mashiqiuzi birds arrive 《Note: Bird name, green wings with white eyebrows》 Snakes become violent 《Note: In this month when snakes emerge and bite people, death is immediate》

Tenth Month: Green seedlings emerge from water. Citrus fruits ripen. Field beans are sown. Cycad trees flower.
Snakes hibernate. Rainbows hide and are not seen. Pine dew enters soil and transforms into mushrooms 《Note: In soil under pine trees, this month has earth mushrooms shaped like taro. They are transformed from pine dew dripping into soil. Color is white; those appearing in cow dung are gray and inedible》 Paper kites fly.
Shiqiudu birds arrive 《Note: Bird name. There are also birds called moducishi and yishiqiuzi that all come in this month》

Eleventh Month: Narcissus blooms. Winter chrysanthemums flower. Wolfberries turn red. Earthworms call.
Canna lilies turn red. Fields have yellow flowers 《Note: Like Chinese dandelions, blooming throughout mountain crevices》 Heavy rains frequently occur.

Twelfth Month: Buddha's hand points to sky. Green doves arrive. Hibernating insects do not bow down. Mosquitoes do not quiet their voices.
There is no ice.

Products
Zhongshan's climate is mostly warm with little cold, no ice or frost, and snow rarely falls. Vegetation remains green year-round. Among local products, those same as China are marked only by name; different products have their forms described in detail. For flowers, their blooming times are also recorded as follows.

For grains, all six grains are available: six types of rice, three types of wheat, and legumes including mung beans, red beans, black beans, white beans, soybeans, and small beans. Soybeans are the same as Chinese yellow beans, with hairy pods growing in fields during the seventh and eighth months.
