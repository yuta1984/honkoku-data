Utensils and Dishes
In rooms, everyone sits on the floor without using chairs or tables. All eating and drinking utensils are made low and small for convenience of use. Those with manufacturing methods different from mainland China are illustrated as shown on the left.

Trays (Ban)
All utensils for placing bowls during meals are like ancient ceremonial vessels such as zu, dou, and pan, either square or round in shape, all with legs about five to six sun high. During meals, several pieces are arranged in front.

Smoking Stand
A smoking stand set includes one fire brazier, one spittoon, and one tobacco tray. Several sets are placed in the room, with one set placed in front of each person. Those made and used in the royal palace are extremely elaborate and refined.
