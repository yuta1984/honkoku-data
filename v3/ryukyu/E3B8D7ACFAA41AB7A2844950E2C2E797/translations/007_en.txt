Second Month - Wheat Ear Festival: The entire country worships the wheat god on the same day. On this day, women do not engage in needlework and men do not work in the fields. {{Note: The four grain festivals are all the same}}

Second Month, 12th Day - Flower Morning: Two days prior, each household cleans their wells, and women draw well water to wash their foreheads, believing this can prevent illness.

Third Month, 3rd Day - Shangsi Festival: Families make mugwort cakes to exchange as gifts. Officials and commoners all perform purification rituals at the seaside and drink. They also visit each other as part of the festival observances. On the same day of this month, they again worship the wheat god, calling this the Great Festival.

Fifth Month, 5th Day - Dragon Boat Racing: Three dragon boats {{Note: one from Tomari, one from Naha, one from Kume}} compete from the 1st to the 5th day. Zongzi dumplings and calamus wine are the same as in China. This is also a festival day. In this month there is the Rice Ear Festival, where an auspicious day is chosen to worship the rice god on the same day.
