The bow is over seven feet long, and when stood upright on the ground, it reaches as high as the eaves of a house. The arrows are considerably shorter than Chinese arrows, about one grip's length. When shooting, the bow is always planted on the ground, and when drawing the bowstring, archers grip not at the center but at the narrower part below to release the arrow. Everyone uses thumb rings (決) and arm guards (拾) according to ancient custom. The range is not very far; while old records mention "shooting beyond 200 paces," I have not witnessed this.

Monthly Observations (Seasonal phenomena)
First Month: Peach blossoms flourish 《Note: White peach, crimson peach, and cherry peach all bloom in this month》 Vinca flowers begin to bloom 《Note: Though they bloom in four seasons, this month is most abundant》
New broad beans are eaten. Bush warblers sing melodiously. Snakes emerge from their holes. Lightning begins. Thunder starts to sound. Loquats ripen 《Note: Same as in China, shaped somewhat long like jujubes. Eating the new fruit on New Year's Day leads all hundred fruits》

Second Month: Magnolia blossoms reach toward the sky. Crabapple blooms red. Kerria flowers bloom. Spring chrysanthemums are fragrant.
《Note: Lilies and poppies all bloom in this month》 Orange jasmine fruits turn red 《Note: Orange jasmine, also called "ten-li fragrance," blooms in the sixth month, and its red clustered fruits fill the trees in this month》
Duckweed begins to grow. Field beans are harvested. Crickets chirp. Dragons emerge from the sea after hibernation.

Third Month: Eria orchids are fragrant. Dianthus flowers bloom 《Note: Roses, wild roses, wisteria, and poppies all bloom in this month》 Perilla grows. The planting of various beans is completed. Trichosanthes appears in markets. Wheat ripens. Rainbows begin to appear.
