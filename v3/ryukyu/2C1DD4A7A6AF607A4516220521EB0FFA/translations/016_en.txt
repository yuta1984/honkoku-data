Kobateisu is a tree with a height of two to three jō and more, with leaves the size of persimmon leaves. The flowers have five petals, and in the eighth and ninth months, the fruit resembles green fruit but is somewhat flattened. It also exists in Fujian, where it is called bodo.

Kobateisu 《Also called bodo; the Japanese name is not clear. Upon investigation, this is probably the kime. The barbarian name is Katatsutesu》

Unō is a tree with a height of three to four jō and more, with leaves resembling those of the white paulownia. In summer it blooms with flowers like Chinese mallow from China, with yellow petals and a red center.

Unō 《Upon current investigation, in Satsuma dialect it is called ōba-gi (large-leaf tree)》

Jibun-boku has a height of five to six jō, with leaves resembling but smaller than grain leaves, and white flowers growing in clusters. It blooms in winter months. It is poisonous and can be used to poison fish.
