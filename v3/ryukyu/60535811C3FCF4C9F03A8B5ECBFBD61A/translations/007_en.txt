　　Geography

The Ryukyu Islands begin south of the southern sea islands of Kyushu, extend continuously in chains across the southern ocean, with their end trending southwest, terminating east of Taiwan Island. They extend from 24 degrees to 28 degrees 40 minutes north latitude, and from 122 degrees 50 minutes to 130 degrees 10 minutes east longitude. The northwest borders the East China Sea, and the southeast borders the Pacific Ocean. There are over forty large and small islands (traditionally counted as thirty-six islands, though this number does not match), with numerous other small island groups. The position and general configuration of these island groups are divided and clustered into three parts—central, southern, and northern—standing in opposition like a tripod (those who consider these to be the ancient territories of Chuzan, Nanzan, and Hokuzan are following an erroneous old theory).
