There are also many grass mats. ○Taketomi Island is located southwest of the main island, with a circumference of just under two ri. ○Kuro Island is also located to its southwest, with a circumference of three ri and a height of 120 jō. The two western subordinate islands are called Kami-hanare and Shimo-hanare. ○Hateruma Island is also located to its southwest, with a circumference of three ri and twenty chō. All of the above belong to Ishigaki.

Iriomote Island is named so because it is located west of Mount Omoto on Ishigaki. In the local dialect, deep interior places are called "iri," and "omote" refers to Omoto. It is also called Nishi-omote Island or Komi Island. It has a circumference of fifteen ri. It has two magiri, Iriomote and Komi, with thirty villages. The climate and products are the same as Ishigaki. It produces Iriomote orchids and wind orchids. The three ports - Komi in the east, Hikegawa village in the north, and Sonahi village in the west - are all shallow. In the western bay, there are two small islands, Uchi-hanare and Soto-hanare, but they have no houses. ○Kohama Island is adjacent to the east of Iriomote, with a circumference of three ri. The northeastern subordinate island Uyama has no houses. ○Hatoma Island is located to the north and belongs to Komi magiri. ○Aragusuku Island is located to the southwest. ○Yonaguni Island is located due west of Iriomote, far out at sea. The tidal currents in between are rapid. The island has a circumference of five ri and ten chō, and a height of seventy jō. It is the westernmost island of the Ryukyus and is adjacent to Taiwan Island (which Ryukyu people call Takasago). All of the above islands belong to Iriomote.

　Northern Islands　Located north and slightly east of the central islands. Ōshima is particularly large, with Tokushima, Erabu, etc. following in size. There are about ten islands in total, collectively called Ōshima. In ancient times they belonged to the Ryukyus, but after the Keichō era, they all came under the Shimazu clan, and now belong to Kagoshima Prefecture. ○Sometimes Ōshima, Kikai, and Tokushima are called the Three Islands.

Ōshima is called Ko-Ryukyu Island, and is the largest island after Okinawa among the islands.
