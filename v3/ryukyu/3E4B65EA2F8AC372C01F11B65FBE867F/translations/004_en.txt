He believed that what is obtained through hearsay cannot compare to the truth recorded by those who have witnessed it with their own eyes. The most honored among the country's officials is called the "Purple-Gold Grand Master." The person currently holding this position is the very one who, when Master Zhouci was previously sent as envoy, requested that the children of accompanying ministers be admitted to schools to study classical texts. His literary style is worth observing, and when speaking with him, he is eloquent and refined. What is now recorded here was all obtained from his oral accounts and the statements of his various ministers, verified against historical records, and is reliable and well-founded. How could the works of Ji Han's "Flora of the South" and Fan Chengda's "Records of Guilin and the Southern Regions" be worthy of envy in comparison? Those who compose imperial missions are creating works that people should properly keep as a volume at hand. Kangxi sixtieth year, preface by Wang Shihong, Left Consultant of the Left Spring Chamber and former scholar of the Southern Study.
