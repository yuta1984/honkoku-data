Iehisa selected his retainers, appointing Yokoyama Gonzaemon Hisataka as commander and Hirata Tarōzaemon as vice-commander, and dispatched over 8,000 soldiers and more than 100 warships to Ryukyu. The forces of the Chūzan Kingdom, numbering over 5,000 men, took position at Nihon-yama (Japan Mountain). This mountain is located about 100 ri from the Chūzan royal capital and is situated at the Japanese boundary.

The Shimazu forces brought their swords to the main mountain and launched a sudden attack with bows and firearms, whereupon many Ryukyuan soldiers were killed and the defeated army fled. In the fourth month of the same year, the Shimazu warships advanced and entered Naha port. The port's defensive ships stretched iron chains across the harbor mouth. The Shimazu forces landed at other ports and engaged in battle
