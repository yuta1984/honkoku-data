Ships

The style of tribute ships is roughly like the bird ships of Fuzhou. Oars are installed on both sides of the ship, two on each left and right side. The ship length is over eight
jo, width is two jo five to six shaku. Previously during the Ming Hongwu and Yongle eras, sea vessels were all bestowed by the emperor. Later envoys
requested to prepare their own construction materials and rebuild them in Fuzhou. Now the shipwrights of this country can also build them according to proper specifications.
The ships used for travel and transport between the various islands all have pointed bottoms, both large and small. The bottom planks overlap like scales. The Tai-
heizan ships are distinguished by decorative railings added as ornamentation. Therefore I illustrate them here. Small boats are all made by hollowing out single logs.
They are extremely light and swift. Village people and fishing households all use them. When one boat cannot bear the load, they lash two together for use.
