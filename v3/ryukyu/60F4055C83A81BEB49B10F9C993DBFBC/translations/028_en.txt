The length of the bow is over seven shaku. When stood on the ground, it reaches the height of house eaves. Compared to Chinese arrows, the arrows are about one grip shorter. When shooting, one must stand the bow on the ground. When holding the target, one aims not at the center but at the narrow part below. Drawing the bowstring and releasing the arrow, all use thumb rings and arm guards according to ancient custom. The range is not very far. An old record says: shooting beyond two hundred paces has never been seen.

First Month: Peach blossoms bloom beautifully {{Small text: Blue peaches, crimson peaches, and cherry peaches all bloom in this month}} Evergreen jasmine begins to flourish {{Small text: Blooms in four seasons, most abundant in this month}}
Fresh fava beans are eaten　Bush warblers sing　Snakes emerge from holes　Lightning appears for the first time　Thunder

Second Month: Magnolia blooms toward the sky　Begonias are red　Pear blossoms are splendid　Spring chrysanthemums are fragrant
{{Small text: Lilies and poppies both bloom in this month}} Orange jasmine fruits turn red {{Small text: Orange jasmine, also called ten-li fragrance. Flowers bloom in the sixth month. Fruits are red and clustered. By this month they fill the trees}}
Duckweed begins to grow　Field beans are harvested　Crickets chirp　Hibernating dragons emerge into the sea

Third Month: Millet orchids are fragrant　Carnations bloom {{Small text: Roses, wild roses, vines, and poppies all bloom in this month}}　Purple perilla
grows　All varieties of beans are planted　Snake gourds appear in the market　Wheat ripens　Rainbows are first seen
