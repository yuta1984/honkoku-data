**Nago Orchid**: The leaves are short and thick, same as column leaves. The size is barely like a finger. It blooms in the third and fourth months. It is no different from orchids. Eight or nine clusters bloom together on one stem. The fragrance is clear and increasingly superior. This orchid grows among the rocky cliffs of Mount Nago. It requires no water or soil. Sometimes it grows parasitically on tree branches, or is wrapped in palm fiber and hung. There is also the wind orchid. Its leaves are somewhat longer compared to regular orchids. The fragrance is like that of galangal and fennel. Bamboo is woven into baskets and hung in front of the wind. It multiplies extremely easily. The common people all prize orchids. They call them "Confucius flowers."

**Millet Orchid**: Also called Zhi orchid. The leaves are like phoenix tails. The flowers are like pearls. Among orchids there are also pine orchid, bamboo orchid, and stick orchid. *[Marginal note: Shaped like coral trees. Green colored with no leaves. Flowers emerge from between branches. Similar to orchids but rather small.]*

**Wild Peony**: Local name is 𦬯 flower. The leaves are no different from peonies. In the second and third months, flowers bloom forming clusters. They hang in clusters like bells. White petals with purple shading and sandalwood centers. About the size of bowls. Extremely fragrant and intense. The leaves are chewed and used as mouth freshener. This variety grows on Mount Daihei. There are also wild begonia, immortal staff, broom peach, and wild orchid. *[Marginal note: This is China's qing rang.]*

**Patterned Day-lily Flower**: Also called happy winter flower. The flowers are like day-lily flowers but particularly small. The leaves have blue and white alternating patterns.

**Mountain Su Flower**: Also called monkey mat (Saramushiro) flower. No flowers, no trunk. It emerges from soil and grows but does not reach a foot in height. Leaves are like plantain but smaller. Firm and thick with patterns.

**Thunder Mountain Flower**: Local name is old eggplant. Leaves are like iron-stem begonia. Flowers are like morning glory but somewhat smaller. Crow-jade color. Blooms in the fourth and fifth months. By the eleventh month, it produces bean-like seeds. The pods are like pomegranate chambers. They contain several dozen seeds. Can be planted.
