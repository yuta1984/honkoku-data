The envoy's record states: Among the deities of the country, there is a female king. She belongs to the royal clan sisters, and they succeed each other through divine selection from generation to generation. When the five grains ripen, the female king crosses the sea to reach Mount Kudakajima, seeks out the ripened grain ears, and chews them. Only then do the various places dare to harvest. If anyone harvests before she has tasted them first, they will die immediately upon eating. Therefore, there are absolutely no thieves in the fields.

In the sixth month's great rice festival, they select an auspicious day to worship the rice deity on the same day. There is also a sixth month festival. Throughout the country, they steam glutinous rice to make rice, and households exchange it with each other. On this day too, women do not apply rouge makeup and do not engage in field work, the same as on the four festivals mentioned above. When there are moonlit nights this month, both samurai and commoners engage in tug-of-war competitions.

On the fifteenth day of the seventh month is the Bon festival to worship ancestors. In advance, on the night of the thirteenth day, every household sets up two torches outside their main gate to welcome the ancestral spirits. After the Bon festival on the fifteenth day, they send off the spirits.

In the eighth month, every household worships the moon. The record of the Ming summer envoy Ziyang states: There is a custom of moon-waiting wishes. Generally on the nights of the third, eighteenth, and twenty-third of each month, they all prepare auspicious fruits and worship while waiting. On the night of the third, they burn incense and worship facing the moon. On the night of the eighteenth, they burn incense and wait standing. They wait until dawn breaks and then worship. Only after finishing worship do they dare to sit. On the twenty-third day, they burn incense and wait sitting. They wait for the moon to rise and then worship. They say this can increase longevity and extend good fortune.

The White Dew period is made the eighth month festival. For three days before and after, both men and women close their doors and do not engage in work. This is called "Guarding the Heavenly Grandson." During these several days, if there are any disputes or incidents, they will surely suffer snake injuries. The snakes of the country emerge in the ninth month. If they injure a person, death is immediate. On the same day, they steam glutinous rice mixed with red beans to make rice for exchange.
