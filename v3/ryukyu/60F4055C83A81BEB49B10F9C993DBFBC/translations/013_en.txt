The chess board is about one shaku high. The legs are two to three sun. The surface thickness is seven to eight sun. It is extremely sturdy and heavy, so that it will not tilt or fall to the side. Black stones are made by polishing lei stones. White stones are made by polishing the top bones of snails and clams. People are all skilled at go. They call it "enlightenment chess" (go-ki). When playing, they do not use the black and white positioning stones at the four corners. When the game ends, they count the number of empty intersections to determine victory or defeat. They do not count the actual stones. There is also xiangqi (Chinese chess).

Food Container (Kaku)
Scholar-official families have one food container. Some are vermillion, some are black. They are decorated with gold inlay and coloring. The craftsmanship is very refined. When dining in the countryside, each person carries one set. Food is placed in the four vessels inside. Alongside are placed one wine pot, one cup, and two chopsticks. All utensils are generally complete. Common people's food containers are either square or round. All are made in three or four tiers. They are made by hollowing out wood.
