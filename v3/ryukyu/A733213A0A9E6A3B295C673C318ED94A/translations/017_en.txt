Chronicle of Sho En, Volume Middle
King Nei

In Tensho 17 (Ming Wanli 17), Prince Urasoe ascended to the throne and became King Sho Nei. Sho Nei was the great-great-grandson of King Sho Shin. Initially, Sho Shin's heir apparent Sho Iko was deposed because he did not meet his father's expectations, and later lived in retirement in the countryside, where he fathered Sho Kogyo. Kogyo fathered Sho I, and I fathered Sho Nei. When King Sho Ei died without an heir, the court ministers consulted together and welcomed Sho Nei to the throne. At this time, Neiko was made chief minister. The magistrates Ba Seiei, Kin Kokutei, Mo Ryubun and others remained in office as before.
