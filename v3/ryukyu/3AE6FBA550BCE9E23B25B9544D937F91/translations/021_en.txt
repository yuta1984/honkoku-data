There are castle ruins in Oyadomari Village. This was the place where the King of Sanhoku held his territory. The broken walls still remain today. Inside there is the Juken Stone (Sword-receiving Stone). It is said that when the king met his downfall, he struck it in indignation with his sword.

The products include sugar, rice, wheat, sweet potatoes, sea fish, banana fiber cloth, etc. The banana fiber cloth is particularly famous.

Haneshi magiri has nineteen villages. Its eastern, southern, and western sides are divided by mountain ridges, bordering the four magiri of Kushi, Nago, Motobu, and Nakijin. The northwestern corner embraces a bay. The land is fertile, the fields are well developed, and it has the most rice paddies in the Kunigami region. It is about nineteen ri and ten chō from Naha.

Mount Taniyo stands towering in the southeastern corner. The Ōkawa River originates in the mountains at the border between Kushi and Haneshi magiri, flows north in meandering curves, reaches Goga Village and empties into the sea. Its length is about two ri and more.

Kanteino Port serves as the ferry landing for crossing to Yagaji Island.

The products are rice, wheat, indigo, timber, etc.

Ōgimi magiri has sixteen villages. Its southern, eastern, and northern sides are divided by mountains with the three magiri of Haneshi, Kushi, and Kunigami, while the western belt faces the sea. There are many mountain peaks everywhere and little flat land. It is about twenty-two ri and twenty-six chō from Naha.
