The land is fertile, and in terms of abundant products, it ranks first in the Nakagami region. The Nakagami government office is located in Ginowan village. It is approximately three ri and seven chō distant from Naha.

In Futenma village there is a cave. It is about five to six ken wide and seven to eight ken deep. Inside the cave there is a shrine called Futenma-gū. Worshippers come in an endless stream. On the cave ceiling, limestone stalactites have dripped down and solidified into pillar-like formations. Their number is countless. The short ones look exactly like hanging icicles. It is truly one of the wondrous sights of the southern islands.

Local products include rice, soybeans, sugar, sweet potatoes, indigo, tobacco, and others.

Nakagusuku Magiri has twenty-three villages. The east faces the sea, the west receives a corner of Chatan Magiri and intersects with Ginowan Magiri, the south borders Nishihara Magiri, and the north adjoins Misato and Goshikawa, the two magiri. The fertility of the land ranks second in the Nakagami region. It is approximately four ri and twenty-two chō distant from Naha.

The ruins of Nakagusuku Castle are located on a mountain in the center of this magiri. It is said to have been built by Gosamaru, a loyal retainer of the Shō clan. The castle walls still exist today. This location faces the sea to the east and also commands a distant view of the great ocean to the west, with an expansive panorama. One can observe the sun and moon rising and setting from the water's edge morning and evening. It is truly a place of magnificent scenery.

Local products include rice, wheat, sugar, sweet potatoes, tobacco, and others.
