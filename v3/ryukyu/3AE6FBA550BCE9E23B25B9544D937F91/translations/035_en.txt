The interior of the island consists only of rice fields and open fields, with no forests. Building materials for houses and such must be obtained from Naha or Yaeyama, it is said.

Extending northeast, it is about five ri east to west and about one ri and twenty chō north to south. Tidal waters constantly flow violently eastward. This is called Yae-bishi and is an extremely dangerous place.

Irabujima Island is located about one ri west of Harimizu Port. Its circumference is about four ri and twenty chō. To its southwest are the two islands of Taramajima and Minnajima. Ikemajima and Ōgamijima are scattered to the northeast, and Kurimajima is to the southwest. The larger ones have a circumference of about three to four ri. The smaller ones are no more than one ri.

Products include grains, indigo fine cloth, kasuri textiles, cattle and horses, fish and shellfish, etc. The indigo fine cloth and kasuri are especially famous.

The Yaeyama Islands are an archipelago located about sixty ri southwest of Miyako Island. They are divided into the three magiri of Miyara, Ishigaki, and Ōhama.
