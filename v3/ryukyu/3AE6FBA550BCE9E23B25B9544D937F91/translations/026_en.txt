The fertility of the soil is first-class in the Shimajiri region. It is about twenty-six chō from Naha.

Yarasa Point is an old fortress that juts out south of Naha Port. A parapet has been built on the sea, with arrow slits between the walls. It faces Mie Castle across the harbor entrance. Climbing up for the view is especially fine.

This magiri has a thriving weaving industry, and it is said that most of the kasuri (ikat) fabric shipped to the mainland is produced in this area.

The products are kasuri, cotton stripes, rice, wheat, soybeans, sugar, vegetables, fish, etc.

Tomigusuku magiri has twenty-one villages. To the southeast it connects to the three magiri of Kanegusuku, Kochinda, and Haebaru; to the west it faces the sea and adjoins Oroku magiri diagonally; to the north it borders Naha Bay and is interspersed with Mawashi magiri. It is about one ri and thirty chō from Naha.

The Nōha River comes from Ōzato magiri, flows west, and empties into Naha Bay.

The products are rice, wheat, beans, sugar, sweet potatoes, vegetables, etc.

Haebaru magiri has ten villages. To the southeast it connects to the two magiri of Ōzato and Kochinda; to the northwest it adjoins the two magiri of Tomigusuku and Mawashi as well as Shuri diagonally. The northeast corner borders Nishihara magiri in Nakagami. It is about one ri and thirty-one chō from Naha.
