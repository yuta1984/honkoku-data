Local products include rice, sugar, sweet potatoes, watermelons, vegetables, and others.

Nishihara Magiri has eighteen villages. The southeast faces Chinen Cape of Shimajiri across an embracing bay, the west borders Urasoe Magiri and Shuri, and the north connects to Ginowan and Nakagusuku, the two magiri. It is approximately two ri and twenty-nine chō distant from Naha.

In Kadekaru village there is a tile-roofed house called Uchima Palace. It is said to be the place where King Shō En once resided when he was the territorial lord of Uchima in ancient times.

Ben-no-dake (Ben Peak) stands majestically on the southern border, straddling Shuri and Haebaru Magiri. On its summit is a worship site of the Shō clan. Samurai and commoners who come to offer incense form an endless stream.

The Sueyoshi River originates from the border between Ishimine and Kuba villages, passes through Sueyoshi village, reaches Aja Port, and flows into the sea.

On a hill in Sueyoshi village stands Sueyoshi Shrine. The view is refreshing and expansive. To the west one can gaze upon the Kerama Islands, to the south Shuri Castle is visible, and the villages of Naha are nearby below. The panoramic view is quite beautiful.

Local products are the same as those of Urasoe Magiri.
