The south borders Shimajiri, and the north and south sides face the sea. Mountain ranges extend from northeast to southwest, but there are no remarkable peaks or steep ridges; rather, there is much flat land, and the fertility of the soil is the finest on the main island. This is divided into the two towns of Shuri and Naha, and eleven magiri: Urasoe, Nishihara, Ginowan, Nakagusuku, Katsuren, Yonashiro, Gushikawa, Misato, Goshi, Chatan, and Yomitanzan.

Naha is located at the southwestern end of Nakagami. To the east, it faces Shuri with Mawashi magiri in between; to the south, it faces the two magiri of Tomigusuku and Oroku across the harbor; and to the southwest it faces the sea on two sides. The topography is flat. It is divided into six villages: Nishi village, Higashi village, Kume village, Izumizaki village, Wakasa town village, and Tomari village. There are over 5,950 households with a population of over 24,310.

The urban area extends for half a ri along the coastal region facing the sea. There is a prefectural office, a judicial office, a police headquarters, a Naha government office, a normal school, and a medical clinic. Houses stand closely together like teeth of a comb, with samurai, commoners, and merchants living there in mixture. This is truly the foremost urban center in the country. In Higashi village there is a marketplace where thousands of people gather daily to buy and sell daily necessities. The excitement and bustle is particularly intense.

Naha Port is the foremost harbor in this country, where merchant vessels constantly gather like clouds. However, because there are many hidden reefs at the harbor entrance and the harbor interior is shallow, large ships anchor outside the harbor.
