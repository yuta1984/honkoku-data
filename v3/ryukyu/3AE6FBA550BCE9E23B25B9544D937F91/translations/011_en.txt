Benzaiten Pond is also called Enkan Pond. There is a small shrine in the center where the goddess Benzaiten is enshrined. To its east is Engaku Temple, which serves as the family temple of the Shō clan. It houses the memorial tablets of successive feudal lords from King Shō En onward. Its scale is grand, making it the greatest temple in Okinawa. Together with Tenkai-ji and Tennō-ji temples, these are called the three great temples.

Manzai Ridge is located outside Chūzan Gate. Pine trees flourish luxuriantly, and the scenery is most beautiful. There is a shrine hall within where Thousand-Armed Kannon is enshrined.

In Sakiyama village there is a villa of the Shō clan, located in the tatsumi direction (southeast) of Shuri Castle. Various flowers are planted throughout the estate grounds, and the scenery is quite profound and scenic.

Local products include shōchū (distilled liquor), silver crafts, tortoiseshell crafts, textiles, pigs, and others.

Urasoe Magiri has fourteen villages. The southeast borders Nishihara Magiri and Shuri, the west faces the sea, and the north connects to Ginowan Magiri. It is approximately two ri and two chō distant from Naha. Although there is Maki Port on the western coast, it is shallow and inconvenient for anchoring ships. It is said to be the place where Chinzei Hachirō Minamoto no Tametomo once raised his homeward sail in ancient times.

The Jirикuaku River originates in the mountains of Maeda village, flows west to Kowan village, and enters the sea.

In Nakama village there is Mount Gokuraku. On its summit are the remains of an ancient castle. It is said to be the place where the Urasoe Aji once made his stronghold in ancient times. There is a temple in the mountains called Ryūfuku-ji.
