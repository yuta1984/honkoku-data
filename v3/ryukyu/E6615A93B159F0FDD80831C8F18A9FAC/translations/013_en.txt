Utensils and Vessels

　　In the rooms, everyone sits on mats on the floor, with no use of chairs or tables. All eating and drinking utensils are made low and small for convenience of use. Those that differ in manufacture from mainland China are illustrated as follows.

　　Trays

　All utensils for placing bowls during meals are like the ancient ritual vessels zu, dou, and pan. They are either square or round in shape, all with legs, about five to six sun in height. During meals, several of these are arranged in front of each person.

　　Smoking Sets

　A smoking set consists of one case containing one fire brazier, one spittoon, and one tobacco tray. Several sets are placed in the room, with one set placed in front of each person. Those manufactured and used in the royal palace are extremely elaborate and beautiful.
