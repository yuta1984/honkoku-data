The bow is over seven feet long; when stood on the ground, it reaches as high as the eaves. The arrows are about one grip shorter than Chinese arrows. When shooting, the bow must always be planted on the ground, and when taking aim, the string is drawn not at the center but at the narrower part below. When releasing arrows, thumb rings and arm guards are always used according to ancient custom. The range is not very far; old records speak of shooting beyond two hundred paces, but this has not been observed.

Monthly Ordinances

First Month: Peach blossoms bloom 《marginal note: blue peach, crimson peach, and cherry peach all bloom in this month》 Vinca begins to flourish 《marginal note: blooms in all four seasons, but is especially abundant this month》 New broad beans are eaten Bush warblers sing melodiously Snakes emerge from their holes Lightning begins Thunder starts to sound Loquats ripen 《marginal note: no different from China, shape slightly elongated like dates; eaten fresh on New Year's Day as the first of a hundred fruits》

Second Month: Magnolia blooms toward the sky Begonias turn red Kerria flowers bloom Spring chrysanthemums are fragrant 《marginal note: lilies and corn poppies both bloom in this month》 Orange jasmine fruits turn red 《marginal note: orange jasmine, also called "ten-li fragrance," flowers bloom in the sixth month, fruits ripen red in clusters, filling the entire tree this month》 Duckweed begins to grow Field beans are harvested Crickets chirp Hibernating dragons emerge from the sea

Third Month: Anoectochilus orchids are fragrant Dianthus flowers bloom 《marginal note: roses, wild roses, trumpet vines, and poppies all bloom in this month》 Perilla grows Various beans finish being planted Snake gourds appear in markets Wheat ripens Rainbows first appear
