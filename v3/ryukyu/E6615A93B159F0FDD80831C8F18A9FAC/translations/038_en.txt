Flies all emerge in winter. Ants are the same as in China, but their abdomens shine like crystal, and when they die, they release droplets of water.

Scaled fish include sharks, carp, crucian carp, eels, loach, shrimp, goldfish, and silver-thread fish. Unusual species include hairy fish, needle fish, swallow fish, and others. Their green, red, green scales with red patterns, five-colored intermixed varieties, some round, some long, are countless. The local people call them by their color and shape, but none have formal names. When sea fish are cut fresh into slices and viewed in dark places at night, they all appear bright and translucent with green firelight, colored like the luminous wood of Rehe.

【Hairy Fish】Small and fine, appearing rotten on the outside but flavorful when chewed. Fujian people all value it as a delicacy. They appear in formation at sea for five days before and after the first day of the seventh month, and five days before and after the first day of the eighth month. They do not appear in other months.

【Needle Fish】Has a needle-shaped head. Also called sayori. 【Boot Fish】Has a long head shaped like a boot.

【Swallow Fish】Like a swallow with wings, capable of flight. Ancient name is ray, commonly called flying fish.

Shelled creatures include turtles, soft-shell turtles, and terrapins. Unusual ones include hawksbill turtles and others.

【Hawksbill Turtle】Shell like turtle or terrapin, with head and tail slightly pointed, head bearing light red color.

【Sea Horse】Horse head with fish body, no scales, flesh like pork. Quite difficult to obtain; those who catch them first present them to the king.

【Stone Cuttlefish】Round head with eight arms growing below, no legs. Local people all use it in cooking.

【Swimming Crab】Has the finest meat, tastes like crab but larger. Mild in nature. 【Crabs】Various species of different sizes. There are small five-colored crabs with two claws, left large and right small. The small one is for taking food, the large one for defense. Only the large claw is vermillion red, called "fire-lighter." Small crabs that live in snail shells are called "parasites."
