Ninth Month: Plum trees begin to bloom Wu-tu fruits ripen 《marginal note: fruit name is gubatisi, shaped like green fruit but flattened; also found in Fujian province where it's called wu-tu》 Persimmons turn purple Frost begins to fall Sea hawks arrive 《marginal note: there are no hawks in the country, but this month northeast winds arise and they drift over from Japan》 Soybeans are harvested Wheat seeds are sown Thunder begins to cease 《marginal note: sometimes it doesn't cease until winter》 All fields are plowed Ma-shi-qiu-zi birds arrive 《marginal note: bird name, with green wings and white eyebrows》 Snakes become violent 《marginal note: snakes emerge this month and wound people, causing immediate death》

Tenth Month: Green seedlings emerge from water Orange fruits ripen Field beans are sown Iron trees flower Snakes hibernate Rainbows hide and are not seen Pine dew enters earth and transforms into mushrooms 《marginal note: in soil under pine trees》 《marginal note: this month there are soil mushrooms shaped like taro, formed when pine dew drips into earth; white in color, those emerging from cattle dung are gray and inedible》 Paper kites are flown Shi-qiu-du birds arrive 《marginal note: bird name; another bird called mo-du-shi and yi-shi-qiu-zi all come this month》

Eleventh Month: Narcissus blooms Winter chrysanthemums flower Wolfberries turn red Earthworms chirp Canna lilies turn red Wild yellow flowers appear 《marginal note: like Chinese dandelions, blooming throughout mountain crevices》 Heavy rains frequently occur

Twelfth Month: Buddha's hand points skyward Green doves arrive Hibernating insects do not lie dormant Mosquitoes do not cease buzzing No ice forms

Products

Chuzan's climate is mostly warm with little cold, no ice or frost, and snow rarely falls. Vegetation remains green year-round. For local products identical to those of China, only their names are noted; for different products, their forms are described in detail. For grains, all six types of grain are available. There are six varieties of rice, three types of wheat, and legumes including mung beans, red beans, black beans, white beans, soybeans, and small beans. Soybeans are the same as Chinese yellow beans, with fuzzy pods growing in fields during the seventh and eighth months.
