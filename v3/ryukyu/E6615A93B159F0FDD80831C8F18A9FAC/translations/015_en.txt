Brazier

The water and fire brazier is made lightweight and simple, with a copper surface and tin lining. One is for placing fire, one for placing water. A small wooden frame is made on the outside to hold them, with two tiers of black lacquered cosmetic boxes below, three or four in number, containing tea utensils and storing tea. The Chinese king assigns two scholars to manage these, and when guests go out for recreation, they carry these along to accompany them.

Desk (Book Stand)

The curved reclining desk follows an ancient style, surrounding the body in a fan shape, about one shaku in height, with a cushion placed on top to lean against. The book stand is like a mirror stand with a small base, about half a shaku in height, used while sitting on the floor.
