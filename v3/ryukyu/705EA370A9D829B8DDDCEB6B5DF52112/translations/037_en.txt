Flies all emerge in winter. Ants are the same as those in China, but their abdomens shine like crystal, and when killed, they emit droplets of water.

Among scaled creatures (fish) are sharks, carp, crucian carp, eels, loaches, shrimp, goldfish, and silver-thread fish. Unusual species include hairy fish, needle fish, swallow fish, and others. Their green, red, green-scaled, and red-patterned varieties intermingle in five colors, some round, some elongated, in countless numbers. Local people call them by their colors and shapes, but they have no formal names. When sea fish are cut into raw slices and viewed in dark places at night, they all appear bright and transparent with a green fiery light, colored like the luminous wood of Rehe.

[Hairy fish] Small and fine, appearing rotten on the outside but flavorful when chewed. Fujian people all prize them as delicacies. They appear in formation in the sea for five days before and after the first day of the seventh month, and five days before and after the first day of the eighth month, but not in other months.

[Needle fish] Has a needle-shaped projection on its head, also called sayori. [Boot fish] Has a head long like a boot.

[Swallow fish] Like a swallow with wings, capable of flight. Its ancient name is ray, commonly called flying fish.

Among shelled creatures are turtles, soft-shelled turtles, and terrapins. Unusual ones include hawksbill turtles and others.

[Hawksbill turtle] Shell like that of turtles and terrapins, with head and tail somewhat pointed, head tinged with light red.

[Sea horse] Horse head with fish body, no scales, flesh like pork. Quite difficult to obtain; those who catch them first present them to the king.

[Octopus] Round head with eight arms growing from below, no legs. Local people all use them in cooking.

[Swimming crab] Meat is of the finest quality, tastes like crab but larger, with a warm nature. [Crabs] Various species of different sizes. There are small five-colored crabs with both claws, the left large and right small. The small one is used for feeding, the large one for defense. Only the large claw is vermillion red, called "fire-lighter." Small crabs living in snail shells are called "parasites."
