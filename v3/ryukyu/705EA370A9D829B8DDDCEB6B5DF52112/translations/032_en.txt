And it has luster. Also called tokiwa-gi (evergreen tree). The tree grows straight up to over ten feet tall and does not wither in any season. The leaves can be used to dye green color. It produces small yellow flowers and bears fruit like oranges that are edible. There is also another tree with the local name [arappo]. All its leaves resemble fukugi, also growing in opposite pairs, with white flowers resembling plum blossoms. It fruits in the eleventh and twelfth months. Both are called "gentleman trees." [Note: The leaf patterns are opposite and thread-like as if woven. The center edges are translucent when held up to sunlight, appearing golden yellow. The traditional fighting-carving tree with orange-like leaves mentioned in old records is likely this very tree.]

[Iron Tree] This is the sago palm, also called sea palm. The trunk is like banana leaves, with leaves that are strong and upright, growing in pairs, feathery like a phoenix tail. When held up to sunlight, the center line is translucent with no shadow. It does not wither in any season and is planted everywhere.

[Black Wood] Leaves are like cinnamon, growing straight up. The exterior appears no different from ordinary trees, but the heartwood is black. However, there are also those with white grain. There is also [Red Wood].

[Oil Tree] Leaves resemble orange trees, fruit is as large as oranges but inedible, used for pressing oil. There is also [Fukuman Tree]. The tree is several feet high, leaves resemble hibiscus but somewhat smaller, flowers cluster like small oranges in red color and are edible. There is another tree with leaves resembling holly, over ten feet tall, flowers cluster like jujube, growing like Chinese privet. Sweet and sour, edible, and can also be used to dye things blue-lotus color. Named yamamai, also called nomamako. This should be the blue essence plant.

[Kubatejisu] Tree height is over twenty to thirty feet. Leaves are large like persimmon leaves, flowers have five branches. In the eighth and ninth months, fruit resembles green olives but somewhat flattened, with fragrant sweet taste. This exists in Fujian and is called jutsu-do.

[Una] Tree height is over thirty to forty feet. Leaves resemble white paulownia, flowering in summer like Chinese autumn mallow with yellow petals and red center.
