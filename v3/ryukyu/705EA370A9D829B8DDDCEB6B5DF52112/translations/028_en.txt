The bow is over seven shaku long, and when stood on the ground, it reaches the height of the eaves. Arrows are about one grip shorter than Chinese arrows. When shooting, they must be stood on the ground, and when holding the target, they grasp not the center but the narrow part below to draw the string and release the arrow. All use thumb rings and arm guards according to ancient practice. The range is not very far. Old records speak of shooting beyond two hundred paces, but this has not been observed.

Monthly Ordinances

First Month: Peach blossoms bloom 《Note: Blue peach, crimson peach, and cherry peach all bloom in this month》 Perpetual spring begins to flourish 《Note: Blooms in all four seasons, but most abundant in this month》 Fresh broad beans are eaten Warblers sing sweetly Snakes emerge from holes Lightning begins Thunder sounds Loquats ripen 《Note: No different from China, shape slightly elongated like dates. Eating fresh ones on New Year's Day makes them the first of hundred fruits》

Second Month: Magnolia writes in the sky Crabapples turn red Cherry blossoms bloom Spring chrysanthemums are fragrant 《Note: Lilies and corn poppies both bloom in this month》 Citrus fruits turn red 《Note: Moon orange, also called ten-li fragrance, flowers bloom in sixth month, fruits red and clustered, filling the tree in this month》 Duckweed begins to grow Field beans are harvested Crickets chirp Hibernating dragons emerge from the sea

Third Month: Dendrobium orchids are fragrant Dianthus flowers bloom 《Note: Roses, wild roses, vines, and poppies all bloom in this month》 Perilla grows All beans are planted Snake gourds appear in markets Wheat ripens Rainbows first appear
