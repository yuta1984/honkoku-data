Pottery

Most bottles and jars are similar to those of China. The small differences are found in one or two types of tea utensils and braziers, which are illustrated here.

Cauldrons

All cooking pots and vessels have iron versions. Their manufacturing also differs considerably from China, with some having handles or grips attached. The old records of the Shoka mission state that "most cooking is done using conch shells," but this is no longer the case today. Occasionally, there are those who brew tea using large conch shells.
