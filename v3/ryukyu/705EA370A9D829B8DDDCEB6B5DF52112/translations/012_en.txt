Utensils and Vessels

In the rooms, everyone sits on the floor without using chairs or tables. All eating and drinking utensils are made low and small for convenience of use. Those with designs different from mainland China are illustrated as shown on the left.

Trays

All vessels used for placing bowls during meals are like the ancient ritual vessels zu and dou, or tray-like containers. They come in square or round shapes, all with legs about five to six sun in height. During meals, several vessels are arranged in front of each person.

Smoking Stand

A smoking stand set contains one fire brazier, one spittoon, and one tobacco bowl. Several sets are placed in a room, with one set placed in front of each person. Those manufactured and used in the royal palace are extremely refined and elegant.
