[Nago Orchid] Leaves are short and thick, the same size as cinnamon leaves, barely finger-like. It blooms in the third and fourth months, no different from orchids. Eight to nine flowers cluster on one stem, with a clear and outstanding fragrance superior to orchids. It grows among the rocky cliffs of Mount Nago, requiring no soil or water, either parasitic on tree branches or wrapped in palm fiber and suspended. There is also [Wind Orchid], with leaves relatively longer than orchids, fragrant like galangal and fennel. Using split bamboo as a pot and hanging it in the wind, it propagates extremely easily. Commonly, people all revere orchids and call them "Confucius flowers."

[Millet Orchid] Also called Aromatic Orchid. Leaves are like phoenix tails, flowers like pearl orchids. There are also [Pine Orchid], [Bamboo Orchid], and [Stick Orchid] [Note: Shaped like coral trees, green colored without leaves. Flowers emerge from branch joints, resembling orchids but smaller].

[Wild Peony] Local name is 𦬯 flower. Leaves are no different from peonies. Flowers bloom in the second and third months, forming clusters, hanging like bells, with white petals, purple halo, and sandalwood center as large as bowls, extremely fragrant. Chewing its leaves serves as mouth freshener. The species comes from Mount Daipei. There are also [Wild Begonia], [Immortal Staff], [Broom Peach], and [Wild Orchid] [Note: namely China's green vine].

[Patterned Daylily Flower] Also called Joy of Winter Flower. Flowers are like daylily flowers but particularly small, with leaves having blue and white alternating patterns.

[Mountain Su Flower] Also called Monkey Mat Flower. No flowers, no trunk, emerging from soil less than a foot tall, leaves like banana but small, firm and thick with patterns.

[Thunder Mountain Flower] Local name is Lucky Eggplant. Leaves like iron-stem begonia, flowers like morning glory but somewhat smaller, crow-blue colored. Blooms from fourth and fifth months until November, bearing seeds like beans, pods like pomegranate chambers containing dozens of seeds that can be planted.
