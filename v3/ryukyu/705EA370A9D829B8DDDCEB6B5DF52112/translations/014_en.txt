Brazier

The water and fire brazier is made for lightness and convenience, with a copper surface and tin lining—one holds fire, one holds water. A small wooden frame is made on the outside to hold them. The lower two tiers contain three or four black-lacquered cosmetic boxes that store tea utensils and hold tea. The Chinese king assigns two scholars to manage this, and when guests go out for pleasure, they carry it along with them.

Desk [Margin note: Book Stand]

The curved reclining desk follows an ancient style, with the body curved like a fan shape, about one shaku high. A cushion is placed on top to lean against. The book stand is like a mirror stand with a small base, about half a shaku high, used while sitting on the floor.
