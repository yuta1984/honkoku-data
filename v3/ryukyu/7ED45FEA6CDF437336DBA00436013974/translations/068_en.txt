Jimoku Officials, along with Doshi-shō and Doshi-dōchi, all receive forty koku; Sangi Officials all receive sixteen koku as salary support.

Naha Officials have fiefs of one county with stipends of eighty koku.

Etsudatsuri Officials receive ten koku as salary support.

The above are the numbers of fiefs, stipends, and salaries for the domestic officials of Shuri, Tomari, and Naha.

Purple Gold Grand Masters have fiefs of one village with stipends of fifty koku, or eighty koku, or one hundred twenty koku, all granted according to the magnitude of their merit. For hereditary stipends, heir sons are granted fiefs and stipends of fifty koku or forty koku, and for grandsons, differences are made according to measured merit. 《Note: In the late Ming period, there was a Purple Gold Grand Master named Sai Ken who received a stipend of three hundred koku and granted his heir son one hundred koku》《Note: After the Kangxi guimao year, this was reduced to fifty koku, grandsons forty koku, and current great-grandsons thirty koku》

Shōgi-dayu have fiefs of one county with stipends of twenty or thirty koku, with differences made according to measured merit. Those not yet granted stipends receive twelve koku annually as salary support.

Chūgi-dayu have fiefs of one county and receive ten koku annually as salary support.

Chōshi have fiefs of one county with stipends of twenty koku.

Tsūtsūji receive eight koku as salary support. Some have fiefs while others do not.

Fuku-tsūji receive five koku as salary support.

Tsūji receive four koku as salary support.

Scholars (Shūsai) receive two koku as salary support. 《Note: Originally three koku, but reduced to two koku after the Kangxi kihai year》

Junior Scholars (Waka-shūsai) receive one koku 《Note: Originally two koku, but reduced to one koku after the kihai year. Children of Kumemura become Junior Scholars at age seven and immediately receive salaries at Shuri》
