These two temples are located by the seashore and serve as places where the king makes vows and dedicates offerings to Buddha, so their rice stipends rank next in importance. Other temples include Manju-ji, Shintoku-ji, Seigen-ji, Ryufuku-ji, and Ankoku-ji, which receive eight koku of rice annually regardless of the number of monks (according to one account, this covers rations for four monks, with each receiving one koku, three to, and five sho, totaling five koku and four to).
