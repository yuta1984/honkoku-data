《Marginal note: Recorded based on genealogies, old tradition collections, political records, Nanpo literary collections, etc.》From this time forth, they have long observed our country's calendar system and maintained diplomatic ceremonies, marking the beginning of the current tribute system. Thereafter, whenever there was a shogunal appointment, the birth of a young lord, or the succession of a Chūzan King in that country, tribute envoys were invariably sent without fail.

In the fourteenth year of Keichō, during the conquest of Ryukyu, in the middle of the Amemizu crossing, an extraordinarily beautiful woman of unusual appearance came in a small boat and transferred to the boat of General Kabayama, saying: "I am Benzaiten, the guardian deity of the Ryukyu Kingdom. In this conquest, my wish is that you do not kill many people or cause suffering to the country. If you agree to this, I will guide you and quickly bring the Ryukyu Kingdom under your control." When she finished speaking and appeared to sit down, she had become a wooden statue of Benzaiten. What had appeared to be the boat she arrived in was actually a bamboo mat. Deeply moved by the remarkable divine spirit's thick protection of the country, they enshrined it in the boat, and after returning from the campaign, they reported the matter and built a shrine on an island in a pond where they worship and make offerings. 《Marginal note: Old Tradition Collection》
