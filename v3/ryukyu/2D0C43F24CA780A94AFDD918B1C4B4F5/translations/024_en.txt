In the ninth month of Keian 2 (1649), King Shō Shitsu of Chūzan had the gratitude envoy Gushikawa Aji and others present tribute goods. 《marginal note: Ryūkyū Jiryaku》They also paid respects at the shrine of Nikkō-zan.

On the twentieth day of the ninth month of Jōō 2 (1653), King Shō Shitsu of Chūzan had the congratulatory envoy Kunigami Aji and others present tribute goods. 《marginal note: Razan Bunshū, Wakan Gōun, Kinsei Buke Hennenryaku》They also paid respects at the shrine of Nikkō-zan.

On the twenty-eighth day of the seventh month of Kanbun 11 (1671), King Shō Tei of Chūzan had the gratitude envoy Prince Kin and others present tribute goods. 《marginal note: Manten Nikki》They also paid respects at the shrine of Nikkō-zan. 《marginal note: Ryūkyū Jiryaku, Rekidai Bikō》

On the eleventh day of the fourth month of Tenna 2 (1682), King Shō Tei of Chūzan had the congratulatory envoy Nago Aji, Onna Ōyakata and others present tribute goods. 《marginal note: Manten Nikki, Kanro Sō》

On the eighteenth day of the eleventh month of Hōei 7 (1710), King Shō Eki of Chūzan had the congratulatory envoy Prince Misato, Tomimori Ōyakata, gratitude envoy Prince Tomigusuku, Yoza Ōyakata and others present tribute goods. 《marginal note: Ryūkyū Heinshi Kiji》They also paid respects at the shrine of Tōei-zan. This marked the beginning of Chūzan envoys coming to Tōei-zan without going to Nikkō-zan.
