Upon consideration, regarding the Ryukyu merchandise that came to Hyōgo in Hōtoku 3, mentioning debts from previous years, and also noting that the visit to the capital in Bunshō 1 was the sixth time, it appears that people from that country came [to Japan] frequently after Eikyō, but since records are scarce, the details cannot be thoroughly examined.

Satsuma Lord Shimazu Conquers Ryukyu

Ryukyu Kingdom had been a tributary state of Satsuma for generations since the command of Ashikaga Yoshinori in the Kakitsu era. However, during the time of regional lords' struggles for power in the Tenshō period, communication with Ryukyu was temporarily severed.
