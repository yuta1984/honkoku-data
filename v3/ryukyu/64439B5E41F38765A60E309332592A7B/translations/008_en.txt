Inaba Province (Inaba no Kuni)

Geographic Layout of Districts in Inaba Province:
Western region: Keta District, Yagami District
Central region: Shikano, Takakusa District, Chizu District  
Eastern region: Tottori, Oumi District, Hōmi District, Iwai District, Hattō District
Cardinal direction indicators (North, South, East, West)

Detailed listing of villages and place names by district:

Keta District: Tahara, Kawachi, Kachihe, Aoya, Nagao, Shitsuhō, Hashizume, Hachiman, Semura, Imaichi, Takashita, Washimine-san, Suemochi, Shikano, Nikkō-ike, Hahaki

Takakusa District: Kawachi, Tanimura, Iwatsubo, Ishihoki, Matsugami, Uyama, Makitani, Yoshioka, Mori, Kanzaki, Mite, Ōtani, Hamauchi-zaki, Tategami-dani, Matsubara, Tsuzurao, Yokozaru, Kuike, Ozaki, Koyama-ike, Okamoto, Sewa, Ōki, Kamihara, Genzan, Fukui, Mikami, Ōtsu, Takebu, Ōtsuka, Takeno, Karushya, Karu, Koyama, Kaku

Tottori/Oumi District: Kuniyasu, Kitamura, Kotoyama, Akachi, Honzon, Nozaka, Kazutsu, Yoshikawa, Kakuma, Yasaka, Baba, Kamura, Yasunaga, Akisato, Tennō, Tomiyasu, Nanryū, Hashimoto, Kojima, Kurata, Miyanaga, Daikakuji, Yoshinari, Furuichi, Gyōda, Takashita, Wamura, Kume, Ōji, Kakuji, Reizen, Shimoyoshikata, Tottori, Ryūhōji, Kamifutari, Tōkōji, Hamasaka, and others

Iwai District: Yaehara, Yuyama, Ama, Ama-shima, Amano, Minamida, Ōmukai, Shichio, Matsutani, Yatani, Tokuda, Takaguchi, Yuyama-ike, Yasukata, Yamazaki, Iketani, Nagae, Minatoguchi, and numerous other villages including hot spring locations and temples

Hōmi District: Funaki, Hirooka, Sakuradani, Ugaki, Mikotani, Hyakutani, Nekitani, Katori, Takiyama, Machiya, Miyashita, Minazōji, Katsuragi, Kokubun-ji, Iwakura, and other locations including temples and mountain areas

Hattō District: Mandaiji, Hinoji, Kawakami, Itomura, Takashita, Ichinotani, Shimosaka, Nakamura, Hanafusa, Hanahara, Yamada, Fukui, Ōtsubo, and numerous other villages and temples including Wakasa, with many place names written in phonetic characters

Chizu District: Tochihara, Hosoo, Kotsuku, Hatamura, Okushina-no, Nishitokizuka, Sanuki, Koichi, Berekasaka, Unami, Nakata, Sunahara, Nohara, Mitsunari, Sakahara, Kihara, Mochigase, No, Takeda, and other settlements including mountain and river locations

Yagami District: Yotōge, Kanchi, Ōkawachi, Kamimura, Yamakami, Tōjū, Kotani, Hirato, Shikano, Motomei, Obata, Taniichiki, Shichikata, Sanuki, Yōkaichi, Fukurobara, Hotei, Takatsu, Iwata-Hyakui, Muikaichi, and other villages including temples and market towns

Border Crossings with Adjacent Provinces:
With Mimasaka Province: Western exits (Yoshihara-de, Kawakami-de, Matsuzaki-de, Kohama-de), Southern exits (Furumachi-de, Kageishi-de, Sakane-de, Takamaru-de, Monomide, Awa-de, Kuroki-de, Iwaya-de)
With Tajima Province: Northern and Eastern exits (Igumi-de, Ishibashi-de, Takeda-de, Hyō-no-yama-goe, Naraya-de, Tsuchikura-de, Higashikawachi-de)
With Harima Province: Via Tajima Province
With Hōki Province: Western and Southern borders
