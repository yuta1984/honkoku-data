Around that time, there was a hermit living on Mount Kurihara in Mino Province. This was a monk named Takenaka Hanbei Shigeharu (Shigeharu), who was intelligent and wise, and skilled in military strategy. Yoshitatsu repeatedly sent messengers to invite Shigeharu to join him. Originally, Shigeharu was a masterless samurai from the Takeda house who would not serve two lords, so he declined in various ways. However, since Ogaki Saburo repeatedly met with Takenaka and urged him in many ways, Shigeharu finally joined the Saito faction, and from then on, he won many battles.

Among the senior retainers of the Ashikaga house was Niki Danjosamon-no-jo Kuniyuki, whose son was called Taro Kunisada. His father Kuniyuki had lost two famous swords called "Oriryu-maru" and "Menryu-maru," and while he was in another province for the investigation, he fell into enemy hands. So Taro Kunisada left the capital to find the whereabouts of the famous swords and avenge his father, becoming a person of humble status in Mikawa Province. He secretly fell in love with Osome, the daughter of a man named Raisaku from Saiga-dake in the eastern provinces, and they formed a marital bond, carrying on their relationship in secret.
