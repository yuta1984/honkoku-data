Takenaka Shigeharu, wanting to probe Inukiyo's true intentions, said, "I am thinking of cutting the wisteria flowers, abandoning the Saito of the Fujiwara clan, and turning my heart toward the Oda side." Hearing this, Inukiyo was greatly pleased and revealed his true feelings: "Now the Oda allies are in dire straits, lacking even arrow shafts, provisions, and gunpowder. Soon they will likely be attacked and defeated by the Saito forces. Please somehow use your wisdom to support us and save the Oda." He pleaded earnestly. Shigeharu listened to this with great joy and said, "The defeat of the Oda retainers who gathered in loyalty must surely be due to some deep stratagem. Lately I have been refraining from battle to observe the enemy's condition. So the time has come to strike down Oda Harunaga, who has been weakened and reduced in force by our allies' courage." He signaled to his allies.

Inukiyo suddenly stood up: "So when you said you would turn your heart to the Oda side, it was a ploy to discover my true intentions and learn of our allies' desperate situation! How detestable!" Immediately drawing his sword, he struck at Takenaka with a single blow.
