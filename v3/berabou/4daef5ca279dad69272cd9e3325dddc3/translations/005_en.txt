This was during the Tensho era, when the country was like merchandise for sale, and there was no place in the streets without sake and turmoil. Lords gathered in various provinces to dispute over strategies. At this time, Oda Kazusa-no-suke Taira no Harunaga, lord of Azuchi Castle in Omi Province, conquered nearby provinces with great authority and captured the castle of Kusaka-yama in Noshu. Saito Shibuno-tayu Yoshitatsu, lord of Inaba-yama Castle in Dogo Province, feared Harunaga's brave warfare and deliberately surrendered. He sent his retainer Ogaki Saburo with 150 mounted soldiers to lie in ambush at the foot of Kusaka-yama, instructing him to join forces with Oda. Following these orders, Ogaki Saburo assisted Oda and helped capture Kusaka-yama Castle.

When Harunaga heard of this, he thought: "Yoshifusa of Kusaka-yama and Saito Yoshitatsu are close relatives. Yet they abandoned their friendship and, rather than helping during Kusaka-yama's fall, they joined our side to destroy Kusaka-yama together. This doesn't make sense - they are certainly not people whose hearts can be trusted." With this concern, he sent Miwa Goro Nagehide, who posed as Harunaga himself to meet with Ogaki Saburo, exchanged pleasantries as if nothing had happened, and then dismissed him.
