In this country, both men and women specialize in cultivating the arts, and there are over eighty people who are versed in the six arts. It was arrogantly recorded that among Confucius's disciples, seventy-two were versed in the six arts, but upon hearing of the abundance of artists in this country, they were so shocked that they offered boiled wild boar wrapped in kelp and dates wrapped in perilla as tribute, appearing to apologize - though this is an old fabrication. Even so, the number of artists may increase further still. Besides these artists, there are also poetry, linked verse, haikai, tea ceremony, incense appreciation, brewing, and flower arrangement. In general, whatever becomes popular does so tremendously, but things also fall out of fashion just as quickly. This is called "inkaze-gei" (fad arts). The meaning seems to be that what becomes popular will also cease to be popular. These are all arts performed to match the conversational mood with Japanese people. Since this is fundamentally a "shadow country," everything is loose, easygoing, and without strict regulation. In Japan, people curse others saying "let's make this guy popular." In this country, they say "let's make this guy unpopular."
