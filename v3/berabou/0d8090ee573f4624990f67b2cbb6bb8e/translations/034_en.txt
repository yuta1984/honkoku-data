《Kyōmachi Province》《Sixteen Districts》
The names of districts in all five provinces change with the times, but this province in particular has undergone various changes in district names. The large district called Miura District was divided into two, called Ryō-Miura (Both Miura), and there was Mount Takao, said to be the highest mountain in the five provinces, but it gradually crumbled away, and its soil reached the three-way junction of Japan to form a sandbar, or is said to have even reached Echigo Province. This corresponds to the present-day Okamoto, Kashiwaya, and Tamura Districts.
