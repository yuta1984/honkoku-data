In all five provinces, there is one magistrate for each district. These are called "naijo" and referred to as "danna-san." Being magistrates of their districts, they possess abundant wisdom, but since everything depends on each district's judgment, there are no fixed rules for the entire province, and everyone acts independently. There are many things that Japanese people cannot understand. This is said to be the national custom. Also, in each district there is one elderly woman who serves under the danna-san and oversees all regulations. She is called "yarite." This official wears a single pouch in front as something precious. Depending on how the district is governed, the contents of this pouch sometimes grow fat and sometimes thin. It may swell due to good governance, or it may also swell due to bad governance. When the mouth of the pouch appears to smile, her own mouth also seems to smile; when the pouch does not swell, her own cheeks puff out. In any case, one might say that a yarite's leather purse without substance is as worthless as a kappa's head without water. However, this is incomprehensible to Japanese eyes.
