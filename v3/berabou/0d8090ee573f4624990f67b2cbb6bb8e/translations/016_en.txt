When discussing human nature, women possess benevolence while men lack it. Women rescue their parents and siblings from poverty, are extremely harmonious among sisters, and work through countless hardships to enrich their master's household. However, men lack such consideration - not only the masters and procurers, but even young men constantly harbor thoughts of exploiting others, keeping torture devices at hand. Their sense of righteousness and duty is thin. Women, on the other hand, are extremely devoted to maintaining their honor. They perform finger-cutting ceremonies, hair-cutting rituals, write pledge documents, pull out their nails, and tattoo themselves with beauty marks, treating their bodies and hair as worthless. Before first-time customers, they will not eat even when starving, but once they become regular patrons, they will not find it disgusting even to share food scraps from a man covered in dental plaque. They show deep courtesy, earnestly offer sake, and are persistently attentive in serving tobacco. When a new courtesan makes her debut, she must always wear padded clothing, even in extreme heat.
