Originally, there were no proper houses either. They entered the darkness of the heavenly rock cave, and because they bumped their nose pillars together with a thud, they came to be called the "Two Pillar Deities." At this time, setting aside the matter of three futons, since it was an era when there was not even a Tentokuji temple, the divine couple lay flat on the bare earth and passed their time. Since it was excessively damp, retainers advised that they should wake up, as would be proper. This was the origin of what was then called "excessive dampness."

At this time, using a bird called the wagtail as their master instructor, they began practices involving heavenly curtain-opening and various intimate techniques, becoming the founding parents of Japanese lovemaking and the progenitors of yin-yang. Afterward, they crossed the floating bridge of heaven, smoothly drew out the heavenly reverse spear, and searched the abundant reed plains randomly.
