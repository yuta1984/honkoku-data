The Japanese laugh, saying that wisdom is like Nakahashi (middle bridge), but when Japanese people come to this quarter, no matter how wise they are, their sophistication is absorbed by the courtesans' skin, and their wisdom immediately becomes like Naka-no-cho (middle street). This must be the virtue of the pleasure quarters. However, the single character of "trust" (shin) is not easily understood carelessly. Though there is a text saying "square eggs," this is merely the vulgar view of children, all spoken from their own inadequacies. Recently there are only trivial word-plays and puns. There is a phrase: "No one tells lies like customers do to courtesans." This too represents one perspective. Confucius said, "If we are sincere, others will be sincere too." How shameless it is to put aside our own lies and take inventory of others' lies. According to Laozi's teaching, "If we are sophisticated, others will be sincere; if we are boorish, others will lie. It does not depend on our own truth or falsehood." Shakyamuni teaches that there is no concern for truth or falsehood - if the other party has money, even lies become truth; if the other party speaks truth but we have no money, it means nothing, and so on.
