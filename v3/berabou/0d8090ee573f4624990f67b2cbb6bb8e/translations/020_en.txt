Edo-chō Country

Throughout all five provinces, a single great river flows through the center, which is called Ryōgawa (Both Rivers). The counties on the right side of this river are called Ukawa (Right River), and those on the left are called Sakawa (Left River).

○ Matsuba-ya
▲ Ruins of Somenosuke Castle: The scenery of these castle ruins becomes more charming as the years pass, offering a pleasing view. According to a fictional tale: "It was said to compose a poem about the heart of the castle ruins by placing the characters 'Somenosuke' at the beginning of each line":

Growing upon the slope, how splendid the flowers are, even in the world to come
The scenery of this dwelling's ruins continues to add beauty

▲ Matsuno-i: This is famous spring water. It is promising that it will eventually become widely known to people. An old poem goes:

For a thousand generations, the prosperity that flourishes in the future at Matsuno-i
Even when drawn, the young green [water] is clearly known

▲ Hanamurasaki Rapids: These rapids reveal themselves in various interesting ways as the tide recedes. I think of it as a landscape of pathos.
