The Reed Plain found this so extremely ticklish that it rolled about laughing, and because of this, the drippings from the spear became like seed (vital essence), congealed at the base of the thighs, and formed a single island. This is what is called Nansenbushū, Toyoashihara, the Great Land of Japan. Moreover, the name "Onokoroshima" likely derives from the sound of that very "inoko" (seed/piglet). After this, the land was divided into sixty-six provinces, and within each province further divided into districts called "kōri" — all of which, dear readers, you are no doubt already aware.

Now, around that same time there were also divine husband and wife called Izanagi and Izanami. These deities were the seventh generation of gods descended from Hentekodachi no Mikoto, and served as the father and mother of the five generations of foreign deities. These gods were tremendous wanderers by nature, and moved by a spirit of rivalry, resolved: "I too shall try my hand at making a country."
