Because people passed through it as a road, it was called "the road of kamaboko," but in later generations this was mistakenly referred to as "the road of tamaboko" in poetic epithets. This kamaboko was thrown outside of Yoshiwara and thus was designated as Japanese territory, but since it was our kamaboko, it belonged within Getsu-hon (Moon Origin). From the dispute between Izanagi and Ko-Izanagi arose what we call "yakuza wo izakoza" (disputes among rogues) even to this day. However, because the place where Ko-Izanagi threw it was unsuitable, it was designated as Japanese territory, and to avoid confusion, it was named Nihon-zutsumi (Japan Embankment).

Though we say that the divine wind of Ise's beach pampas grass, changing its name to Yoshiwara or calling it Ashihara, are the same country, Ashihara's middle country is called "Hi-no-moto" (Origin of the Sun), while the middle district of Yoshiwara is the "Tsuki-no-moto" (Origin of the Moon). Therefore, Shinji also composed the verse "Yoshi atsuto tsukiyo kana" (What a fine, thick moonlit night). Now, Ashihara is the remainder of the spear's drippings...
