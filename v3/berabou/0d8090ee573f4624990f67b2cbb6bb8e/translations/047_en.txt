It is not inherently dark, but unless one steps away from the scene to observe, how can one properly evaluate things? This book is the same. I add the perspective of "onlookers see most clearly" to my own two eyes to evaluate this work. This is what ten eyes observe, without even the slightest personal bias. Though it may be like saying "is this fact or fiction?" about fictional works, even if I am called arrogant for this, I do not know the smell of my own body.
