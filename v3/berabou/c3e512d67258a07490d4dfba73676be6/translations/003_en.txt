(Continued from previous page) ...is not sought after. Therefore, disliking the idea of wasting several sheets of paper on a long and labored preface in the manner of an overly attentive author, I now change my approach and refrain from writing a formal preface. Instead, I take up the worn brush from my writing set and inscribe upon the cherry-wood printing block: "Do not let the Tenshō-bon (original text) go to waste." And yet, it cannot be said that there is no preface at all.

Written in the early spring (Mōshun) of Kansei 8, Year of the Bingchen (1796), in the spare moments of bookkeeping at a sundry goods shop.
At this time, Spring of Tenpō 6, Year of the Wood Sheep (1835)
　　　Kyokutei Bakin
