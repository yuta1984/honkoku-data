The Biography of Man'hachi

Man'hachi was a person from Chikushū (Chikuzen Province). His father was Meppō Yahachi. His mother was a courtesan named Kyogen (Falsehood) from the Damashiro-ya brothel. She dreamed of swallowing a square egg on New Year's Eve, and gave birth to Man'hachi on the winter solstice day in the sixth month of autumn in the year Tenpō 800, Otsu-riki. Man'hachi's childhood name was Senmitsu (One in a Thousand Lies). When he grew up, he was called Yajirō. He was naturally intelligent and his wit surpassed even that of birds and beasts. Therefore, he was extremely skilled at capturing birds and beasts alive. One year, on the third day of the first month, he crossed over to China, climbed Sutenho Peak, and shot tigers with a wild rifle. The number he caught was one koku, six to, two shō, and eight gō. The emperor was moved by such unbelievable deeds and enfeoffed him as the boss of Chochorara Country. At this time, the foreign barbarians sang: "The export from Chochorara is Japan's boss." He traveled through the eastern barbarians, southern barbarians, northern barbarians, and western barbarians. He spread lies by the sea of Tsukushi for a thousand years, lies on Mount Tsukuba for a thousand years, and on the Tōkaidō for nine thousand years, blowing around various harbors for three years and three months, lasting ninety-nine nights. His age accumulated to 10,008 years old. Therefore, he changed his name to Man'hachi after his age. Since this is a straightforward tale, there is not a single falsehood in it.

On the 30th day of the tenth month, Yomonya Hontarō Shōjiki
