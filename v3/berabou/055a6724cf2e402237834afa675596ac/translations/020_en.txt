Tai traveled to Nagasaki and entrusted his household-made pine soot to returning merchant vessels, sending it to China where the official craftsman Cheng Danmu of Huizhou manufactures it.

○Oil soot ink　The production of oil soot ink in our country began in medieval times at Nanto (Nara), where soot from lamps in the Buddha hall of Nitai-bo at Kofuku-ji Temple, which had accumulated and adhered to the roof tiles, was collected, mixed with glue, and formed into ink. This is said to be the beginning of Nanto oil soot ink. Some say that Kukai, after returning from China, taught the people of Nanto to make it. However, this is merely local lore not recorded in written traditions. Generally, common people tend to attribute the origins of various matters to Kukai as topics of conversation. These are all fabricated theories not worthy of belief. For medicinal use, pine soot is considered superior, with oil soot being secondary.

○Nitai-bo ink　This is made using the ancient method of creating Nanto oil soot ink. It is three sun long and eight bu wide. Its form is what the world calls the "oil soot shape," with "Mosquito Dragon" inscribed on the front and the three characters "Li Family Smoke" written in seal script on the back.

○Engi Bureau of Books ink　Five sun long and eight bu wide. This is manufactured according to the methods recorded in the Engi Shiki.

○Imperial Viewing Great Ink　Manufactured by Gentai, it has a diameter of one shaku six sun, thickness of two sun five bu, and weighs twenty-two kin. Its shape is circular. This ink being a great ink rare in the world, when it was summoned to the imperial court in former years, Nagata Sadayanagi composed the poem: "Not being the moon, yet rising above the clouds - what could be the reason for this?" This refers to precisely this ink.
