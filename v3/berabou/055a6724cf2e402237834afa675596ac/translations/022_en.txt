Metals Section

Gold　Japanese name: Kogane. In ancient times, it was not known that gold existed in our country. According to the Shoku Nihongi, on the day of Hinoto-mi in the second month of the twenty-first year of Tenpyo under Emperor Shomu, Mutsu Province first offered tribute of gold. Otomo no Yakamochi's poem "That our sovereign's reign may flourish, golden flowers bloom on Mount Mutsu in the east" was also composed at that time. In later ages, it came from various provinces.

△Placer gold　Japanese name: Nasuhigane. ○Ezo product is top grade. ○Wakasa product is top grade.

△Gold ore　"Ko" (ore) is also written as "ko." All five metals are produced within stones. What cannot be smelted and separated is called ore. Our predecessors read "ko" as "mabu." However, what is now called "mabu" at gold mines is the name for holes dug for gold and silver. Gold ore is called "hiishi," also called "ni." However, "ni" is a general term for both gold and silver. Copper ore is called "ku." ○Sado product is top grade. ○Musashi Chichibu mountain product is medium grade.

Silver　Japanese name: Shirokane.

On the seventh day of the third month of the third year of Emperor Tenmu, the provincial governor of Tsushima, Mamikai no Miyatsuko Okuni, reported: "Silver has appeared for the first time in our province and is hereby offered as tribute." For this reason, Okuni was awarded the court rank of Shokin-ge. This was the first time silver was found in Yamato country, as recorded in the Nihon Shoki. In later ages, it came from various provinces.
