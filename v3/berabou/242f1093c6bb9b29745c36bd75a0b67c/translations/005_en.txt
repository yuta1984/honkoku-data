Riding alone from the south, traveling a thousand leagues. The general is indeed establishing his great achievements. Where the dragon-men
offer no resistance and come forth to greet him, they bring offerings of food and jars of drink, filling the roads.
Soldiers ride long distances, bearing eagle feathers. Wherever Lord Tametomo turns, the warriors are fierce and valiant. The people of the islands
look up to him as their lord, as the people of old revered Tāng and Yǔ. He unifies the Ryūkyū Islands, and his fame resounds throughout Japan.

　　　　Written
　in the winter, the eleventh month of the year Kinoto-Ushi (乙丑) of the Bunka era [1805], by the Master of Kyokutei, at his desk, in the Chosakunodō.
