【Right Page】

Tametomo caught the arrows released with a mighty "hyo!" draw — one he sewed into the sleeve of his court robe, and the other, having no time to grasp it by hand, he seized firmly in his mouth, instantly crushing the arrowhead between his teeth. His speed was like heat shimmers rising from the earth, like lightning flashing across the sky — it seemed beyond any human capability. Those who witnessed it were as if intoxicated, and in their overwhelming admiration, they could not even raise their voices. Tametomo flung the arrows he had caught aside, left and right, and cried, "Now then, let me have that monk's head!" — and before he had even finished speaking, he leapt up onto the dais and was about to seize Shinzei, when his father Tameyoshi stepped between them and threw him straight down to the ground with a thud, scolding him: "Though you are born into a warrior household, and have managed by chance to deflect the arrow tips, there is nothing particularly remarkable about it. Yet without even reflecting on your own station, such base and reckless behavior — this is disgraceful, this is rash!" 

At this, Lord Yorinaga looked on and said, "Come now, Tameyoshi, do not scold him so harshly. Nor should Shinzei take this to heart. There is an old tale of two children who argued about whether the sun was nearer at rising or at setting, and even Confucius could not judge between them. That Michimori the Novice (Shinzei) cannot subdue Tametomo is the same kind of matter. Let us leave it at that for now. Everyone, please withdraw."

【Left Page】

With these words, taking neither one side nor the other, he soothed all parties. The Retired Emperor (Sutoku) had long harbored certain thoughts and had been quietly discussing them with the Minister of the Left, Yorinaga. Through a gap in the blind, he had observed Tametomo's conduct, and thinking, "That one could surely be of use," was greatly impressed and offered deep praise. Thus Tameyoshi was accorded unexpected honor, and father and son withdrew together. From this time forth, Tametomo's name became widely renowned throughout the world.

However, Shinzei harbored deep resentment toward Tameyoshi and his son, and repeatedly slandered them in attempts to bring about their ruin. At last, the Hogen Rebellion broke out, and the Retired Emperor reluctantly summoned Tameyoshi. Having no choice, Tameyoshi went to serve him, bringing six of his sons. But the battle went against them, and the Retired Emperor was exiled to Matsuyama in Sanuki Province. Then Michimori the Novice Shinzei, harboring such profound hatred for Tameyoshi and his son, reinstated the death penalty — which had long been abolished — had the body of the Minister of the Left, Lord Yorinaga, dug up from the Hannya-no Gosamai burial ground and beheaded, and proceeded to execute Tameyoshi and his son and all those who had fought on the side of the Retired Emperor…
