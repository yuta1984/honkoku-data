Preface

One day, the proprietor of the bookstore "Kōshodō" came to my humble cottage and, as usual, requested that I compile a book. I declined, reluctant to waste time and consume paper and ink.

The bookseller said: "The Book of Songs states: 'They are good at jesting and joking, yet they do not become cruel.' Your momentary diversions, Master, are the sustenance of my entire livelihood. How can this be merely a waste of ink and brush?"

At this point, I could not refuse. I recalled that there once existed a popular book called "Wasōbei," and decided to write a sequel to that work and give it to him.

Written by Santō Kyōden
In the first month of Kansei 9, Year of the Fire Snake (1797)
