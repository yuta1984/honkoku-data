"What a truly bitter thing I am hearing! Theater and sideshows that receive official permission from the authorities serve as techniques to harmonize people, illuminating the proper ways of lord and retainer, father and child, husband and wife, brothers, and friends. For example, Ōboshi Yuranosuke's actions serve as a mirror for loyal retainers, and Umegae in 'The Bell of Mugen' encourages women's chastity. Even the strange sideshows teach that a parent's sins are visited upon their children, that a hunter's child becomes deformed, and that evil's retribution is as fine as a needle's point - warning people not to be careless. But in recent years, people focus only on making money, and to devote attention to such places and moreover have a farting man as a sideshow is absolutely outrageous! Flatulence is not something to release in public. If one accidentally lets it slip in a place where it should not be released, a samurai would consider it shameful enough to commit seppuku. I have heard tell that in Shinagawa, some woman accidentally let one slip in front of a customer, and in that gathering was a merchant from Odawara-chō..."
