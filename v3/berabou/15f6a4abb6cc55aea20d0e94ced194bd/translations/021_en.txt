When the tide recedes and forms sandbanks, [the whale] is greatly troubled and releases helpless farts, which is why the receding of the tide is also called "hiru" (to dry up/recede). The deity who favors this way is called Hiruko and also Ebisu. Originally "bisu" was a mistake for "hebisu," which came to be corrupted through the phonetic correspondence between "aiueo" and "hahifuheho."
