When he entered and crawled into [the pond], the water became greatly agitated and he frequently farted, so he was given the nickname "General of Fart Pond." The record that was written is called "The Tale of Fart Pond." Writing it as "Heike" (平家) in later generations is a phonetic substitution.
Also, when Minamoto no Yoritomo, Assistant Captain of the Outer Palace Guards, was exiled to Izu Province, he was poor and constantly ate potato rice, and because he enjoyed farting, that place was named "Hiruga-kojima" (Fart Island). Farting in the fields is called "nobe," and farting in the mountains is called "yamabe."
