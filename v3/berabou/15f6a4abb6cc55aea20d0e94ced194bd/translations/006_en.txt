Hakuen's affected airs, Keishi's dance performances, Nakazō's skillfulness, Kinsaku's charm, Hiroji's musical timing, Sangorō's stage business. When Baikō overwhelmed Naniwa, Tomizō made his name known in the Eastern Capital.

Pilgrimages to Kawaguchi, crowds at Asakusa, sumo at Fukagawa, impromptu performances at Yoshiwara. Sashū spreads the fundamentals of Katō-bushi in Kobiki-chō, while Sumitayū recites the essence of gidayū-bushi in Fukiya-chō. There are also mechanical puppets, children's plays, gestures and vocal mimicry, street storytelling. Edo's prosperity is nothing new. Among these countless varieties of entertainment, recently around the Saikoku Bridge area, a "farting man" has appeared, and this has become the talk of the town, spreading as rumors throughout the various neighborhoods.

Reflecting carefully upon this, since humans are microcosms of heaven and earth, just as heaven and earth have thunder, humans have flatulence. It is the sound of yin and yang clashing against each other, sometimes released, sometimes restrained—this is natural.
