Hakuen's affected mannerisms, Keishi's performance pieces, Nakazō's skillfulness, Kinsaku's charm, Hiroji's rhythm, Sangorō's expertise. When Baikō overwhelms Naniwa (Osaka), Tomizō reveals his fame in Tōto (Edo). Pilgrimages to Kawaguchi, crowds at Asakusa, sumo at Fukagawa, sudden performances at Yoshiwara. Sashū spreads the fundamentals of Katō-bushi in Kobiki-chō, while Sumitayū narrates the essence of gidayū-bushi in Fukiya-chō. Or mechanical puppets, children's plays, gestures and vocal mimicry, street storytelling—such prosperity of Edo that did not begin today. Among these countless varieties, recently around Saikoku Bridge, a farting man has appeared, becoming the talk of the town and the rumor of every neighborhood.

Thinking this over carefully, since humans are microcosms, just as heaven and earth have thunder, humans have flatulence. It is the sound of yin and yang clashing against each other, sometimes releasing, sometimes letting loose—this is natural.
