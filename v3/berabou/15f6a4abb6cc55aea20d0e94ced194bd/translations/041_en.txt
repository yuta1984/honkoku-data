This extends [to the human body]. Now then, although Buddhism speaks of earth, water, fire, wind, and space as the Five Elements, space and wind are in a relationship of substance and function, so ultimately there are four great elements. Since this water, fire, earth, and air fill the space between heaven and earth, they are naturally present within the human body, and thus all four substances emerge from within the body.

Daily food becomes excrement and serves as fertilizer for the five grains. Is this not earth emerging from the human body? When it becomes urine and sweat, this is the body expelling water. What exists above as breathing is called flatulence below. This is the body expelling air.

Among these, fire is the foundation of the creation of all things. Its source is called the sun, and its end is called fire. That the Japanese readings for "sun" and "fire" are the same is also the natural principle of heaven and earth. Therefore, among the gods there is Amaterasu Omikami, and among buddhas there is Dainichi Nyorai. The Diamond Realm refers to the world above ground, and the Womb Realm refers to the world below ground. The Buddha of Infinite Life of ten trillion lands, reflecting upon oneself...
