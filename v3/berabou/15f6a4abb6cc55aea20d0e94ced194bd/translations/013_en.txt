"Though it may seem presumptuous, writing a pledge to save someone's life was also an elegant act. To hang a signboard on the roadside of a main street and expose to the public eye something that people consider shameful is the height of rudeness. The one who shows it makes money, and I think the viewers are fools, yet for you, teacher, to follow along with this is utterly disappointing. The water of Robber's Spring, the land of Shengmu - people hate even their very names. 'Hear no impropriety, see no impropriety' - this is the teaching of the sages," he argued with veins bulging.

I replied: "Your words are quite reasonable. However, you do not yet understand the greatness of the Way. Confucius did not even discard children's songs. I too have reason for taking up the matter of farting. All things that exist between heaven and earth naturally have ranks of noble and base, high and low. Among these, what is considered most extremely vulgar stops at urination and defecation. For base metaphors, the Chinese say 'dung and dirt,' and in Japan it is like 'shit'..."
