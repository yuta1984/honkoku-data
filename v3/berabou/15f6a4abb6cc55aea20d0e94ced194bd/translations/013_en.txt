"How considerate it was that they wrote a pledge and saved a person's life - was this not also an elegant act? To hang a signboard by the roadside on a major street and expose to public view what people consider shameful is the height of rudeness. The one who displays it makes money, while the viewers are fools, I think, yet for you, teacher, to follow along with this is truly disappointing. The water of Robber's Spring, the land of Shengmu - even their very names are detested. 'Hear not what is improper, see not what is improper' - this is the teaching of the sage," he argued with veins bulging.

I replied: "Your words are quite reasonable. However, you do not yet understand the greatness of the Way. Confucius did not discard even children's songs. I too have reasons for taking up the matter of farting. Indeed, all things between heaven and earth naturally have ranks of noble and base, high and low. Among these, what is considered most vulgar stops at excrement and urine. For base metaphors, the Chinese use the term 'dung and dirt,' while in Japan it is like 'shit'
