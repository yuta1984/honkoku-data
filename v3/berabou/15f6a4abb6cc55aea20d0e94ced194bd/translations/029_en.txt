When that precious jewel called "Facing Forward, Never Turning Back" was obtained, he was hired as a day laborer for sixty-four mon per day, and the fishermen joyfully pulled him up—this story was made into a noh song and frequently performed in theaters by nameless bit-part actors playing the fisherman, and he is the legitimate descendant of that fisherman. His mother dreamed of swallowing a worn-out fan and conceived him. After giving birth to this child, she revered the God of Poverty as her clan deity, quarreled with the Seven Lucky Gods, and left her hometown to reside in Edo. Thus, though they say "various skills are worth two hundred koku, no skills are worth nothing," this man had not mastered any single art, yet was not entirely without talent either. Being deficient in both respects, like a bamboo basket in the open sea that neither reaches the shore nor catches the waves, he was a drifting gourd.
