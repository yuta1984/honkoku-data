Do not use only flowers when leaves are available
○Plants that have no leaves during flowering season are exceptional
　(Summer narcissus, true name kintouka/golden lamp grass, leaves emerge in New Year and wither in April, flowers bloom in June-July) and　　　(Lycoris radiata/red spider lily, leaves emerge in September and wither the following April, flowers bloom during autumn equinox) etc.
○Toward the side where decorative implements of the room are placed
　Do not extend branches and leaves too long
○Though out-of-season flowers are taboo, there is tradition that
　Return flowers (kaeri-bana) should be used
○Both flowers and leaves submerged in water should be avoided
　That lotus and yellow pond lily are not avoided is also tradition
○Insect-eaten leaves, withered leaves, broken leaves are things to avoid
　Leaves cut with scissors should also be disliked
○Two flowers two branches, four or six stems should be avoided
　Also avoid bundling and tying when arranging
○Like viewing flower medallions and nail covers
　Avoid facing directly forward
○When arranging flowers and stems side by side
　Do not use the same colors on both sides
○Do not add other colors between the same colors
　Avoid bad-smelling ones, avoid those of unknown name
○Into the space where flowers and leaves are paired
　Adding other things should also be avoided
○Do not extend branches and leaves long toward the front
　Know that hiding hanging scrolls and name seals is also [taboo]
○Well ropes, ship chains, and basket handles too
