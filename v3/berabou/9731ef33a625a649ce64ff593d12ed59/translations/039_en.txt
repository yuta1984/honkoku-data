○In all matters of yin-yang, front-back, long-short
　Keep rhythm and balance in mind
○Arrange cleanly at the water's edge; from the flower basin
　Do not let too much debris fall outside
○Even without flower distribution, foot bamboo supports
　And bent insertions are also techniques
○For hanging flowers, drooping plants are good even without elegance
　It is good to arrange them hanging downward
○Drooping things do not suit standing arrangements
　Beginners should exercise discretion
○Be mindful of thick branches in narrow mouths, and large blossoms
　Under the table as well
○For under-table arrangements, so as not to obstruct the four corner legs
　It is good to arrange small flower forms
○For lion-mouth vases, understand not to obstruct the ring
　Removing the ring should be oral instruction
○In lion-mouth vases, plum, willow, and others
　That grow expansively should have their rings removed
○Upper part is wood, lower part is grass - this is the two-level cut
　There is tradition for wood below and grass above
○When arranging to represent landscape, use two-level cut
　Upper part for flowers, lower part for water only
○For two-level cut, if guests request, put water above
　The host should arrange it below in advance
