From the reading books of Hachimonjiya and the wrapping cloth of the lending bookstore, they emerged and came to the shop of a picture book artist from Kamigata (Kyoto-Osaka region) who was in the same store in Yanagihara, resembling a storyteller.

"Well, well, listen. I too came down from Kamigata and became popular in Edo, but in recent years, I have been constrained by the popularity of blue books (aobon) and especially by trivial things called sharebon (witty books). This is truly vexing," he complained.

The picture book artist from Kamigata, who was plotting to find fault with blue books and other local publications, said with an inward laugh, "Well, please leave this matter to me."

The picture book artist from Kamigata once invited red books (akabon) and black books (kurobon), and served them soup with various seasonings. Since red books turn red after drinking a little, he brought out dorayaki from Hikino-ya, and over casual conversation said, "I want to publish a critique of the blue books that came out last spring. Your poor sales are due to the publication of blue books, so please find fault with them. I have a plan that keeps my hands clean, and as someone from Kamigata, I cannot be careless."

Seeing this, both became indignant and envious of the blue books. "Even though we were once called the beginning of red books, do you think of red books and black books as demons from hell?"
