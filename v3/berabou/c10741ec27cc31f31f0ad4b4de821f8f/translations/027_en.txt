○Part Two

These days, around Itako and Karuizawa, it's the fashionable season. A group of men dressed hastily in matching yukata worn as undergarments, with dark brown-sheathed sleeves, and moegi-green sanada braid trim — the sort of fellows you'd spot around Nishi-no-Kubo's Gazen-bō Valley or around the Warigesui area of Honjo. Two or three "half-baked regulars" (nama-tsū) — men who frequent the pleasure quarters but are looked down upon even at the inn-brokerages (kurayado) — have split into two groups, strolling along the edge of the embankment. One of them, wearing a Japanese-style short sword, taps his pipe stem and says:

(Rifū): "Hey, hey, look at that! A three-man express palanquin is coming from the direction of Tanaka — why on earth would they be rushing it along at this hour?"

(Karyō?): "Well, who on earth could it be? I can't make heads or tails of this."

— As they say this, the express palanquin comes right up to the edge of the embankment, then turns back toward Tanaka. —

(Tomoji?rō): "Look at that — it's turning back!"

(Rifū): "Heh, got it. That's certainly someone practicing how to write the character for 'ko' (子)." [i.e., going back and forth in a curved path]

(Hana): "Indeed, now that you mention it, there's a strange fellow riding inside."

(Tomoji?rō): "Whatever one does, there's always something to learn. Hey, do you have any cash (kōhō) on you? I'd like to buy a booklet of kogiku paper."
