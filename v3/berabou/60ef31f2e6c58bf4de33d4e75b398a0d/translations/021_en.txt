**(Main text, continued)**

**[Kawaji / 川]** "There's money in the drawer that opens with the lock on the comb-box — take two *shu* from it and go buy some food. The key is in the drawer of the utility chest."

**[Yukino-ya / 雪]** "Yes, ma'am."

*(She goes to the drawer of the utility chest and catches her finger in it—)*

"Ouch!"

*(rubbing her finger)*

"The key isn't in here at all."

**[Kawaji]** "What? Nonsense, nonsense. Didn't you yourself put it in there last night?"

*(Meanwhile, she looks over the written list brought by the young attendant — a note from the yarite [the experienced woman who manages the courtesans] — and marks the items she fancies, sending the girl off to fetch them.)*

"Yobune-san, there should be *jyazen-mame* [spiced beans] and chili peppers in the tea cabinet. Bring them out here."

**[Yūgiri / 夕]** "Shall we not send for some scallop adductor muscles and make a dish of simmered scallops?"

**[Kawaji]** "Do send someone for them."

**[Yūgiri]** "Now, what's happened to the *yukihira* pot?"

**[Kawaji]** "Someone went and broke it long ago. Hey, Ashikano-san, what are the side dishes this morning?"

**[Ashikano-san / あし]** "I do believe it's taro and fried tofu, ma'am."

**[Kawaji]** "Good grief."

**[Yūgiri]** "My apologies."

*(And so, after much deliberation over their food preferences, they finally finish their morning meal. Yūgiri then takes her time rinsing her mouth, smokes two or three pipes of tobacco, and promptly goes off to the bathhouse. The young attendant follows along carrying her bathrobe. After they leave, a fabric merchant from Tamachi arrives—)*

**[Draper / 呉服屋]** *(arriving)* "Did the pattern book from yesterday meet with your approval?"

**[Kawaji]** "It was really quite good, I must say—"
