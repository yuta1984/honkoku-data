**[Kuchisato]** "...Since he never sent for us himself, it would be most ill-mannered to impose our own wishes upon the guest."

*(From this point the mood grows more complicated. The medicine merchant has tried various approaches, but it seems he is, after all, a man of means when it comes to the fee itself — yet stingy with bedding and clothing expenses. Meanwhile, in the room next door, the resident courtesan and her attendants appear to have only just woken up, and are gathered before their mirror primping and grooming. Into this scene enters the kamuro [child attendant] Tsunaji, coming up from below-stairs. She wears her standard-issue cotton wadded coat, dirty and worn to a dark greasy shine, with a narrow sash dyed in the dull, muddy colour of itajime resist-dyeing. She rubs her eyes, her face still heavy with sleep.)*

**[Kuruma-i]** "Tsunaji, is that you?"

**[Tsunaji]** "Yes'm."

**[Kuruma-i]** "Good timing. Light my pipe for me, there's a dear."

**[Tsunaji]** "Yes'm."

*(There being as yet no fire in this room, Tsunaji goes to the yarite-beya [the room of the matron/chaperone] to light the pipe there, and brings it back.)*

**[Kuruma-i]** "Ohh, for goodness' sake! The fire's gone out!"

*(She taps the pipe [in irritation]. At this moment, a male teahouse attendant comes hurrying in.)*

**[Teahouse attendant (male)]** "Excuse me, Oiran. I'm told that Lord Asei has left his tobacco pouch behind."

**[Kuruma-i]** "Have a look and see if it's in the bedding, won't you."

**[Teahouse attendant]** "Right away."

*(After searching:)* "Here it is, my lady."

**[Kuruma-i]** "Is he still staying on this visit?"

**[Teahouse attendant]** "Indeed he is."

*(Just as the attendant hurries off, a female teahouse attendant arrives.)*

**[Teahouse attendant (female)]** "Excuse me, Oiran. Please send your reply letter."
