**(Main text, continued)**

**[Kawaji / 川]** "It really is quite splendid. We shall go with that one."

**[Draper / 呉服屋]** "As for the plain fabric—"

**[Kawaji]** "Please make it a coordinated solid (*tomomuku*). And this time, please make the length of the young attendant's (*kaburo*) garment a little longer."

**[Draper]** "Certainly, ma'am."

*(He begins to leave—)*

**[Kawaji]** "Oh, excuse me, excuse me!"

*(calling him back)*

"While you're at it, please select some fabric for my *hakikake* [underskirt lining]. And also, please send over a length of purple *kanoko* [fawn-spotted] collar material."

**[Draper]** "Certainly, ma'am."

*(Just as he says this—)*

**[Young Attendant / 禿]** *(one arrives)*

"Yasobei-san says that the *oiran* should come over for a moment, she says."

**[Draper]** "And which house are you from, child?"

**[Kawaji]** "She's from Kurumawi-san's place."

*(The draper goes into the back.)*

**[Yūgiri / 夕]** *(returning from the bath before long, wiping herself down in her bathrobe)*

"Did you hear? It seems that Guchiri-san's client has been caught — so they say."

**[Kawaji]** "So I've heard. Let's go and see tomorrow, shall we? Yobune-san, please get something out so she can get herself ready."

**[Yūgiri]** "Ashikano-san, would you go upstairs and check whether the hairdresser, O-Kichi-san, hasn't arrived yet—"

---

**[Marginal note]**
"*Hakikake*" refers to the underskirt lining (*suso-mawashi*). This is a term used exclusively in this quarter [the Yoshiwara].
