**(Main text, continued)**

*(Yofune [lit. "night boat"] is wearing, like all the others, a padded cotton garment [nunoko] in indigo velvet as worn-looking as an oil shop's cleaning rag, with a narrow sash, and a collar that is jet black. Her face powder remains only in patches, like snow that has not quite melted away. Yūgiri is reading a reply letter that has arrived from a patron to whom she sent a request for money [mushin]. Kawatake has started cleaning on her own, holding a broom and peering over from behind.)*

*(Note: The appearance of these two women here is the very picture of a courtesan's idle, unguarded private self.)*

**[Yofune]** "The poem card [uta-garuta] that went missing last night has turned up from inside the sleeve of my nightrobe [yogi]."

**[(Woman)]** "There you have it, you see."

**[Kawatake]** "Now you lot — get yourselves off to the bath and be quick about it, then come back and wipe down the alcove and the lattice window. Everything is covered in dust — I've always said that corner smells dreadful."

*(Saying this, she picks out a lump of compressed ash from the smoking tray, tears up some scraps of old letters, and is wiping the rim of the brazier when the kamuro [young attendant girl] Yukino arrives.)*

**[Kawatake]** "Yukino, go and see who's in the bath."

**[Yukino]** "I just had a look, and there's no one from outside in there at all."

**[Kawatake]** "Oiran [Mistress Yūgiri], won't you come out now?"

**[Yūgiri]** "I'll be along in just a little while. There's something I'm thinking about just now."

**[Kawatake]** "Now then, Ashikano — while I'm away at the bath, would you set out the oiran's meal tray and mine as well, and perhaps put the kettle on for tea?"
