**（Postscript, continued）**

...there is a small but sincere intention behind it all. To take a familiar example: it is like soothing and admonishing a child by means of sweets and moxa cautery. The sweets, in other words, represent the parables and expedient means; the moxa represents the principles of benevolence, righteousness, and the Five Constant Virtues. If that is so, then one can hardly say there is no reason to place the two characters *kyōkun* ("moral instruction") at the head of this little volume.
To those who read this — please reflect upon it with discernment.
