**(Main text, continued)**

**[Sundries vendor]** "—if you could send along to me anything left over from those that didn't suit the oiran's taste, I can let them go at lower prices."

**[Yūgiri]** "Well, do as you please with it."

*(Just as he says this—)*

**[Kawaji / 川]** *(returning from the bath)* "One of my hairpins — the one I've been wearing lately — the setting has come loose, you see."

**[Sundries vendor]** "I'll fix it right up for you."

*(At that moment, from the back upstairs room, a prostitute called Adasaki — a heya-mochi rank courtesan — comes down.)*

**[Adasaki]** "Oh, please — could you polish up this comb for me? Please, I beg you, quickly!"

**[Sundries vendor]** "Certainly, ma'am."

*(He says this — though that comb, it seems, is the battered remains of one broken during a quarrel with a lover last night. And then yet another person arrives—)*

**[Furishin / Furisode-shinzō]** *(arriving)* "Is the finger-ring not finished yet?"

**[Sundries vendor]** "It'll be ready tomorrow. The crests were to be paulownia and *hanabishi* [diamond-flower], paired as *hiyoku* [double crests], weren't they?"

**[Furishin]** "Just make sure you say it exactly right, now. Good grief."

*(She says this with some embarrassment — this must be the greatest little pleasure a furisode-shinzō could have.)*

**[Sundries vendor]** "I must just pop in to see Ukisato's place."

**[Furishin]** "I believe Ukisato-san has a *itsuzuke* [extended-stay guest] right now."

**[Sundries vendor]** "Ah, right then."

*(And he heads off toward the back. ○ Meanwhile, in Yūgiri's sitting room, the furisode-shinzō has been laying out the chopstick box and a lacquered eight-sun tray, and arranging the tea bowls. The water in the earthenware kettle on the brazier is beginning to sing — chin-chin. Beside it, tobacco leaves are laid on top of a book called* Kogetsu-shū*, and the drawer of the tobacco tray has been pulled out to serve as a tobacco holder.)*

**[Kawaji]** "Now then, Yukino-ya — I say—"
