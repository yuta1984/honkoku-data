**(Main text, continued)**

**[Teahouse attendant (female)]** "I'll send someone."

**[Kuruma-i]** "Then wait just a moment, won't you. Tell me — is there any more of those pickled melon slices they always have at the place you're staying this time?"

**[Teahouse attendant (female)]** "There still is some. Shall I bring you some up?"

**[Kuruma-i]** "Then please bring it while I write the letter. I beg you."

*(Below, the shop's noren [dividing curtain], bearing the name "Yoshidaya" dyed boldly in indigo on a solid-coloured ground, is rolled halfway up. On the illuminated ox-stand [a kind of display stand or lantern platform], a kamuro [child attendant] sits perched precariously on the edge, fiddling with a kumquat or a physalis [hōzuki] as a plaything while having her hair dressed. It should be noted that a kamuro's hair is always done by a male hairdresser.)*

**[Hairdresser, Kippei]** "Now, will you please hold still! There's nobody who wriggles about like you do."

*(Nearby, another kamuro waits her turn.)*

**[Kamuro]** "Kippei-don, I want you to skip the yakko-shimada for me and go straight to the pin-set style [hari-uchi]. Oiran told me to say so."

**[Another kamuro]** "After her, it's my turn, you know."

**[Another kamuro]** "What nerve! What do you mean, 'your turn'? I got here first!"

**[Kippei]** "Oh, these children, honestly — it's too much. Bickering away first thing every morning — can't you be a little quiet?"
