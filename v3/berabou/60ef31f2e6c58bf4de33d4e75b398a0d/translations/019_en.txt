**(Main text, continued)**

"And then — now, now — get that done quickly, will you. Yukino, bring the rice-bran bag and a bathing robe, and a foot-wiper too. Then go around to the various places and collect the account slips [kakitsuke]. Be quick about it now. Nothing ever gets done unless I keep at it. Because the oiran is far too easygoing, everyone gets lazy and you can't help it."

*(Saying this, she heads off to the bath and is done with them. ○ Note: when going to the bath, she tucks a boxwood [tsuge] comb in upside-down at the nape — this is to train the hair into shape. ○ After she has gone, a traveling sundries-vendor [komamono-ya] arrives — with two or three hairpins stuck in his own head, carrying his pack on his shoulder — and peeks into the room.)*

**[Sundries vendor]** *(removing the single decorative artificial hairpin he had worn in his own hair)* "For the oiran — I've just had this hairpin made, and it's turned out exceptionally fine, so won't you take it and keep it for yourself?"  *(showing it to her)*

**[Yūgiri]** *(taking it and holding it up to the light to inspect it)* "My, this really is lovely. I want it desperately, but right now I'm not quite in a position to discuss it — if it hasn't sold by tomorrow, do bring it back."

**[Sundries vendor]** "Yes, of course. If it hasn't sold today, I'll bring it back tomorrow. Since it's to your liking—"

"The meaning of the term *kakitsuke* [account slip] is explained in detail in the previous volume, *Shijūhatte* — and so the annotation is omitted here."
