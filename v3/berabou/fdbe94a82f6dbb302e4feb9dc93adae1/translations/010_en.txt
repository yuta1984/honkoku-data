At Hikigaya where koto and shamisen are played, the
daughters who cannot let their guard down fall at Kofukurozaka
Making the shallows where Miura's shells float
A great commotion only at Takiguchi, the shadow of the ginkgo tree
remains in the theater turret's crest
Standing alongside the tachibana, from early morning
people enter the tiered seats at Dankazura
Climbing to the top of Kewai slope, in spring
flowers are planted in the middle town, in autumn lanterns
The prosperity cannot be fully described
Here, near the town where horses were provided for the Fuji hunting expedition in previous years
There was a wealthy townsman
called Urokoya. For years he had much business with
the various daimyo of Kamakura, today serving Kajiwara-sama
tomorrow for Wada-sama's banquet, with many orders for armor scales
Among them all, he had long been frequently visiting
the Hōjō family residence as a valued customer
The great lord Tokimasa-sama bestowed upon him
the character "masa" and he took the name Masabei
He fulfilled orders for goods and such, but the retainer
Sano Genzaemon-dono, rising from poverty
to sudden elevation, developed arrogance.
Well then, at the height of his prosperity on Kewai
slope, he was among the finest - Kagaya's Umegae,
Etchūya's Sakura, Kaminoya's
Matsue - this collection of beauties in one gathering
He was led by entertainers and jesters into banquets
and pleasures, and in the end
the golden three [scales] passed down in the Hōjō house
