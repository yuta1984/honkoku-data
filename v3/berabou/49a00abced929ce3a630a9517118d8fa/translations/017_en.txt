Assembly Diagram (continued)

Author Ichiku blows his nose with paper and says:
"Now, regarding the world diagram I have just presented, let me explain that all things - not only all humans but all phenomena - have their foundation or 'base.' In our country, Ebisu sits upon a rock even when placed in the tokonoma alcove, and Daikoku sits upon rice bales even when seated at a desk. Buddha too has his lotus pedestal. Not limited to this island, all people in the world have their bases. There are royal dynasties, samurai family positions, each inherited from ancestral generations - their succession is called 'generational change.' Townspeople too have their bases. Even with just a little capital, one can gradually build great wealth. Even to rent a shop, without shop rent money, one cannot remain seated there.

If there should be a scoundrel who loses his foundation, he becomes a helpless slave-like person forced to survive on meager rations of two-and-a-half cups of rice. In any case, not losing one's foundation is essential. This round object displayed here is an assembly diagram of the three thousand worlds. As ancient texts say, 'the world is like a single ball' - heaven is round and earth is square. This round thing is surrounded by the entire world, so Japan corresponds to the top of this ball, China is on the side, and India corresponds to the bottom of the ball. Humans are like ants all clinging to it, so Japanese people cling straight up, while Chinese people cling sideways. Though there's no reason they should stand sideways or upside down, since they all have invisible foundations, they can stand and walk whether upside down or sideways.

This foundation, to put it simply, is like this roly-poly doll (okiagari koboshi). Though it has no arms or legs, because it has a weighted base inside, whether placed on its side or upside down, it immediately stands upright. Both noble and humble people stand because they each have foundations appropriate to their social status. In any case, being careful not to lose one's footing on this foundation is the most important thing for humans. Do you understand?"
