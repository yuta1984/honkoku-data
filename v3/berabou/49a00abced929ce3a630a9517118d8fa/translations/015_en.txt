At the festivals on this island, families dress up their children and put them on flower floats. They use gold and silver leaf to decorate the platforms, and when pulling the floats, they sing songs called "kiyari" (work songs). However, when the children on these floats eat their midday meal, ladders are set up to deliver their lunch boxes. This scene resembles Fukurokuju doing his hair in reverse.

Those who have well-formed child trees display them at the festival just as they naturally grew, keeping them as siblings. Like the gesture of somehow trying to win praise, the daughter trees are also dressed accordingly.

Among the various costume ideas, including the Shakkyō (Lion Dance) outfit, the white heron costume that incorporated a fan showed particularly clever craftsmanship and appeared to be the work of quite a witty group on this island.
