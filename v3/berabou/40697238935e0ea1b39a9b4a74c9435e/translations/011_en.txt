Cotton one tan, specifically two jō six shaku, when bought for thirteen momme of silver, how much per one shaku? The answer is: it comes to five bu each.
By the method, two jō six shaku equals twenty-six. Thirteen momme equals thirteen. Even if you divide twenty-six by two, it's thirteen, so one shaku is five bu each. This can be known even by mental calculation.
Another question: cotton one tan measures two jō seven shaku five sun by whale measure, priced at nine momme seven bu. If this is measured as eight shaku three sun by bent measure, what is the price? The answer is: that should be seen by the draper's sales method.
