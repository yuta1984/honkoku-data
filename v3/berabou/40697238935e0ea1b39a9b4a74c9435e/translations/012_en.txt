One to of oil with a seven-shō measure
and a three-shō measure, dividing
five shō each - while this
can be done if you think about it,
it's just a waste of time that won't
help your fortune.
Set aside the seven-shō and three-shō measures
and borrow a one-shō measure
to do the measuring. If you and
another person are dividing and taking
five shō each, measure what you're
giving to the other person in the evening.
Oil increases in the evening
and settles in the morning,
so by morning the other person's
oil will be about four shō nine gō,
while your oil will be
five shō one gō or more.
However, if it's not oil you're taking
for yourself, do as you please at any time.

'On Excessive Rice Field Matters'

[Lower section]
○Fourth Level: Four means "doing" - speaking of how to practice economy
Conduct at the Age of Ear-Compliance: If you keep your spirit young, you'll spend money, so in all conduct, adopt the manner of an old person around sixty; "ear-compliance" refers to sixty years of age

Time for Salt-Cooking and Self-Made: Even in small empty plots, grow vegetables for soup ingredients by hand; also eat everything salted - this economizes on miso and soy sauce

Seventy-Year-Old Deliberation: While conduct should be like a sixty-year-old, deliberation should be like a seventy-year-old - withdrawing and contemplating everything is best

Single-Minded Labor Wages: Just sitting idle is a great loss; move your hands to earn money somehow and make earning wages your single focus

Government Money as Dust: Even when borrowing money, borrowing from blind musicians reduces you to nothing - truly fearsome

○Fifth Level: Five means "awakening" - when your fortune recovers and you awaken; from here, the mindset is to increase money
I-One Household Pawning: When asked to lend money, lend at five ryō one bu interest rate, taking household items as collateral

Five-Two Patience: Even if loaned money goes uncollected, if you can get five ryō two bu by lending five ryō, be patient and let it go

Five-Three Don't Lend: Five-three means three bu interest on five ryō - don't lend at such high interest rates; be cautious

Five-Four Lending: Don't lend large amounts; lend about five or four ryō at a time

Five Money Becomes Ten: Doing thus, five ryō immediately becomes ten ryō
