○Sixth Level: Six means stipend. Without stipend, it's dangerous. One should devise ways to seek stipend.
Stipend One - Scarecrow: Scarecrow refers to country bowmen, meaning rural samurai. Buying rice fields to make them your stipend and living without worry is first-class.

Stipend Two - Retainer Status: Retainer status means service in a lord's household. Though these are true samurai, they have worrisome duties, so this is second.

Stipend Three - Five Thousand Estates: Thousand estates means thousand-ryō properties. Having five thousand-ryō properties would be excellent, but there are more dangerous aspects than being a samurai, so this is third.

Stipend Four - Northern Province Doctor: Northern province doctor means a Yoshiwara physician. No learning required, comfortable, and good profit margin - this is fourth.

Stipend Five - Townspeople's Second: Village headman is townspeople's first; townspeople's second means a major landlord. This is also profitable but involves hardship, so it's fifth.

Stipend Advancement as Single Focus: Whatever stipend you have, focus on increasing it. Both advancing upward and retreating downward depend on single-minded determination.

○Seventh Level: This repeatedly emphasizes the losses from pawning, speaking tediously about pawning.
Pawn One - Mother: When in need, people first want to pawn mother's belongings. Doing this brings parental punishment, and one's health never recovers.

Pawn Two - Wife's Hidden Things: Second, people want to pawn the wife's belongings. This too ultimately flows away completely. Making new things afterward is a great loss.

Pawn Scattered - Impoverished Status: With pawning, if you can't redeem items and they scatter away, causing hardship, pawn either things attached to your body, precious items, or things you can definitely redeem.

Pawn Interest - Beginning, Middle, End: Nothing follows you around as annoyingly as pawn interest. Truly fearsome.
