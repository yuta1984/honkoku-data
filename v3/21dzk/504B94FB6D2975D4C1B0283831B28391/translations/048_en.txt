Body, and third is the transformation body. Question: What is called the transformation body? Answer: There are two transformation bodies: first is the Buddha-form transformation body, second is the five-destinies transformation body. Question: What does this mean? Answer: Descending from heaven, taking birth in the royal palace, practicing austerities for six years, and attaining enlightenment under the tree. While being the same as humans and gods, finally achieving Buddha form. After the Buddha's passing, manifesting five-destinies bodies, which are not Buddha forms. There are the body during the Buddha's lifetime and the body after the Buddha's passing, therefore there are two transformations.

Question: When one person generates the aspiration, cultivates, and attains the fruit, why must there necessarily be three bodies in the fruit? Answer: At the time of initial aspiration, two kinds of wisdom arise: first is the vow to seek bodhi, second is the vow to benefit others. By seeking bodhisattva wisdom, the dharma body arises. This is the true Buddha body, pervading throughout the dharma realm with no place it does not exist. It possesses the four wisdom categories and four nirvanas, is peacefully blissful, permanently abiding and unchanging. The three points and four virtues are perfectly complete. Through the wisdom of benefiting others, that true Buddha body transforms and creates two kinds of bodies: first is the enjoyment body for others, which transforms and creates this body for tenth-ground bodhisattvas and others. Second is the transformation body, which transforms and creates this body for those before the grounds, Two Vehicle practitioners, and ordinary beings. Because there are two kinds of beings to be transformed, wisdom has two kinds of bodies. Combined with the original true body, they make three bodies.

Question: Such three bodies are understood to have three distinctions through wisdom, but is it that if there are three in the cause, one attains three bodies? Or does one attain three bodies without separate causes? Answer: There are separate causes and conditions for attaining the three bodies, namely first is the producing cause, second is the understanding cause. That producing cause means from beginningless time there are naturally existing seeds which are superior grade. There are also the six perfections which assist the producing cause. Thus through the two producing causes of primary and auxiliary, one attains these three bodies. Question: Do those Buddha three

bodies come only from producing causes, or are there understanding causes? Answer: The principle dharma-body Buddha is attained from understanding causes, the rest are attained from producing causes. Question: Are the so-called two causes separate or the same? Answer: One cause becomes separately two. That is, in relation to the principle dharma body it serves as understanding cause, in relation to other Buddha bodies it serves as producing cause. Having the six perfections and naturally existing seeds, they divide into two in relation to the fruit.

Question: Among these three bodies, how many dharma gates are attained? Answer: The four wisdoms, four nirvanas, and other boundless dharma gates are completely possessed. Question: What are called the four wisdoms? Answer: First is great perfect mirror wisdom, second is equality nature wisdom, third is wonderful observing wisdom, fourth is accomplishing activities wisdom.

Question: What is called great perfect mirror wisdom? Answer: "Great" means vast and extensive. "Perfect" means completely round. "Mirror" means reflecting and manifesting, using analogy as the name. Like a great bright mirror manifesting various images, this wisdom likewise can manifest the wisdom reflections of generated bodies and lands, therefore it is called perfect mirror.

Question: Among the eight consciousnesses in the causal stage, which consciousness is transformed to attain this perfect mirror wisdom? Answer: The eighth consciousness is transformed to attain this wisdom.

Question: What is called equality nature wisdom? Answer: "Equal" means evenly balanced, "equality" means uniformly equal, "nature" means true nature, deriving the name from the object. Because it observes the equality nature of all dharmas and self-other sentient beings, it is called equality nature wisdom. Question: Which consciousness is transformed to attain this? Answer: The seventh manas consciousness is transformed to attain this wisdom, therefore it is called equality nature wisdom.

Question: What is called wonderful observing wisdom? Answer: "Wonderful" means supremely excellent, "observing" means contemplative illumination, "examining" involves careful investigation. The name derives from its function. Because it illuminates and understands nature wisdom with unobstructed functioning, it is called wonderful observing wisdom.

Question: What is called accomplishing activities wisdom? Answer: "Accomplishing"

The first two are dependent (possessive compound), the latter two are descriptive (descriptive compound).

Question: What do such four wisdoms take as their essence? Answer: In terms of self-nature, they take wisdom among the specific objects as essence. In terms of retinue, they take the five aggregates that are associated together as essence.

Question: How many dharmas are these four wisdoms associated with? Answer: Twenty-one dharmas are constantly associated. Including the mind-king, there are twenty-two dharmas. Question: What are the twenty-one dharmas? Answer: Five omnipresent, five specific objects, eleven wholesome, combining to make twenty-one. Including the mind-king makes twenty-two.

Question: The Ornament Treatise says "Transforming the five consciousnesses attains wonderful observing wisdom, transforming the mental consciousness attains accomplishing activities wisdom." Why is there disagreement with this explanation? Answer: That Ornament Treatise is a translator's error. Someone explains that it is to show function - is this not reasonable?

Question: At what stage do such eight consciousnesses transform and attain the four wisdoms? Answer: The two wisdoms of wonderful observing and equality begin transforming in the seeing path of the first ground. Regarding these two wisdoms, about perfect mirror and transformation wisdom theory, there are two explanations: one says only subsequently attained wisdom, another says there is also fundamental wisdom. The remaining three all possess both.

Question: Why doesn't the first ground transform the fifth and eighth, but rather two wisdoms? Answer: The eighth consciousness is the root of birth and death, so if it transformed in the first ground one would become Buddha, but then who would hold the contaminated seeds of the ten grounds? The five consciousnesses depend on the five sense organs which are still contaminated, so how could they be transformed?

Question: Why is equality nature wisdom associated with the seventh? Answer: The seventh consciousness, being in the causal stage and grasping at self, has self-other distinctions. Now with no self-grasping, there is equality great compassion etc., constantly associated together, therefore it is associated with this consciousness.

Question: Why is wonderful observing associated with mental consciousness? Answer: This sixth consciousness, in the causal stage, can cognize the individual and common characteristics of all dharmas, therefore in the fruit stage, associated with this consciousness, this wisdom wonderfully observes the individual and common aspects of dharmas to explain for others. The merit of perfect cause cannot be measured, the virtue of complete fruit has no limit. The four wisdom mind-groups, four nirvanas, three bodies, four virtues, three mindful abidings, Tathāgata's ten powers, three fearlessnesses, four kinds of fearlessness, exclusive dharmas, four kinds of all-种 purity, three-point great compassion, ten masteries, possessing the thirty-two marks of a great man, eighty secondary characteristics, atoms of non-forgetful dharmas, four wonderful wisdoms, completely possessing one hundred forty dharmas. Fearing that later generations would grow weary, I abbreviate. I wish that all wise ones would know this together.

Now I briefly praise and describe according to my ability. The ordinary mind does not understand what accords with principle. Offering to support our king for a thousand generations. Constantly turning the golden wheel, subduing ten thousand realms. Ministers and officials supporting the court. May the body be healthy, life long, peaceful and blissful. May all sentient beings share in this fortune. Quickly escape birth-death and realize nirvana.
