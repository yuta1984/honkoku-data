　　　Two-fold Middle Way
Question: Through what doctrinal gate does our school's intention call something "Middle Way"? Answer: In clarifying the principle of the Middle Way in the *Forest of Meanings Chapter*, there are two-fold meanings. First is the Middle Way of verbal expression. Namely, the essence of dependently arisen dharmas is not completely non-existent. Regarding this dependent arising, one falsely conceives all imaginatively constructed natures, but their essential nature is completely non-existent. Within this dependent arising there is only the emptiness-nature suchness that is far removed from all constructions. Regarding that emptiness-nature, one can also have these causally conditioned dharmas, therefore all dharmas are neither empty nor non-empty. Thus the three natures correlatively express and reveal the meaning of the Middle Way. Second is the Middle Way beyond language. Namely, although the essence of all dharmas is thus sometimes existent and sometimes non-existent, within true ultimate reality, because mind and language are cut off, it is called Middle Way. These present two levels discuss their dharma-essence without any further distinction. This is simply the dharma-gate of the three natures being neither identical nor separate. However, when expressing their meaning in contrast to other approaches: self and dharmas are not existent; emptiness and consciousness are not non-existent. Departing from existence and departing from non-existence, therefore called Middle Way. This is the gate of verbal expression. If dwelling in inner realization and stopping conceptualization, this is precisely the one reality Middle Way beyond language. Particularly, because the essence of the constructed is completely non-existent, it is not dharma-essence. That dharma-essence is simply the substantial other-dependent and consummate two natures. The above provisionally takes the constructed as empty and other-dependent and consummate as existent to discuss the general outline. If discussing in detail, even when called empty, emptiness is also precisely existence. Because real self and real dharmas appear to consciousness. Negating this aspect also becomes diminishment. However, speaking according to principle, their essence is completely non-existent. Not understanding this approach also becomes augmentation. One should know: imaginatively constructed nature is also neither empty nor existent. The nature of other-dependent and consummate, though called existent, can also be called empty. Because the essential nature of constructed, conventional, and ultimate is empty. If, because other-dependent and consummate are called existent, one then considers other-dependent and consummate to be one-sidedly existent, this is false attachment and therefore augmentation. However, the dharma-essence beyond language that is the realm of noble wisdom has essence that is not non-existent. Negating this also becomes diminishment. Therefore the two natures of other-dependent and consummate are also neither empty nor existent. This is still the general outline. If discussing their reality in further detail, within the principled non-existence of imaginative construction, there is also the meaning of neither empty nor existent. Question: What is now being discussed takes other-dependent and consummate as one in contrast to constructed emptiness as a doctrinal gate discussion. If other-dependent and consummate reality are discussed separately, can each also have Middle Way meaning? Answer: There is also that. Namely, because the essence of other-dependent is provisional, it is not real existence. It is also not complete non-existence. Because the essence of the wondrous principle of the consummate is without characteristics, it has no existent characteristics and also no non-existent characteristics. Therefore each one is all Middle Way. The layer-upon-layer meanings of each gate, and even the comprehensive gathering meaning of the various gates of phenomena and principle being neither identical nor separate, are all as above.
