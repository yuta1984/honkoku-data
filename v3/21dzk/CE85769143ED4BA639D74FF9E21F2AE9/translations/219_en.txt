【Hokke Kaiji-sho Volume 1, Chapter on Supernatural Powers, 436】

【Right page, upper section】
Regarding this matter. Also, these three types are the proper use of entrustment (fuzoku), therefore they are specifically interpreted. The others are general and not formal, so they are not explained.

The Suisho says: "The reason the treatise explains only three types is that among the eight types of supernatural transformations, the other five types are common to other sutras, but since they are not superior, they are not interpreted. Moreover, these three types are not found in any other sutras. The treatise commentator specifically interprets them. Also, these three types are precisely entrustment. Therefore they are specifically interpreted. The latter interpretation is superior. It also says, 'Excluding what is general and not formal' means excluding the five types found in other sutras. Light is like that in the Jōshō and Nirvana sutras, earth trembling is like that in the Prajñā sutra, the merging of canopies is like that in the Vimalakīrti sutra, and land transformation is also like that in the Vimalakīrti sutra. Since these are not the essential points of the entrustment section in this sutra, they are not explained."
