Four: The Gate of Determining Essence

Generally in our sect's understanding, judging the essence of all dharmas has roughly four gates. First, the gate of subsuming characteristics under nature. This gate's meaning uses true-thusness principle as all dharmas' essence. Though form and mind differ in myriad ways, their essence is all thusness. Beyond the nature-essence, there are no

characteristics or functions. Beyond the one thusness, there is no further essence. Second, the gate of subsuming objects under mind. All dharmas don't exceed mind-object inclusion. Those various objects follow from one mind, so only mind-consciousness exists with no other essence. Third, the gate of subsuming provisional under real. Among all dharmas, provisional and real differ. Since provisional apart from real has no essential nature, one subsumes all provisional under real essence. Fourth, the gate of separately discussing nature and function. The previous dharma-gates all use subsumption theory—their general purport is the non-difference gate within neither-one-nor-different. Now this gate is the separate-discussion gate's general purport—precisely the only non-oneness gate within non-difference. Therefore characteristics-nature, mind-objects, provisional-real dharmas each have distinct and clear differences, judging essence according to circumstances in various non-unified ways. Temporarily clarifying the four gates regarding doctrinal essence: if subsuming characteristics under nature, using thusness as doctrinal essence—the Tathāgata's teaching essence is one thusness. If subsuming objects under mind, various objects only return to consciousness—the Tathāgata's consciousness becomes doctrinal essence. If subsuming provisional under real, using sound as doctrinal essence—the Tathāgata's speech essence is sound. If separately discussing essence of nature and function, sounds, names, phrases, and words—Buddha's sounds, names, etc.—are what properly express meaning. Other various dharmas can be understood analogously.

Five: The Establishment of Aggregates

Form aggregate, sensation aggregate, perception aggregate, formations aggregate, consciousness aggregate make five. "Form aggregate" means gathering all form dharmas—the eleven forms among the hundred dharmas. "Sensation aggregate" means gathering all sensation factors—the sensation mental factors corresponding to the eight consciousnesses among the hundred dharmas. "Perception aggregate" means gathering all perception factors—the perception mental factors corresponding to the eight consciousnesses among the hundred dharmas.
