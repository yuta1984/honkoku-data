《Title: Kakumushō Hoketsu Hōmon》 Volume 1
　　　Establishment of the Vessel-World
Now, the vessel-world is what is obtained through the power of the karmic growth of all sentient beings. One tri-thousandfold world undergoes formation and destruction together. Such worlds are immeasurable and boundless, all created by the minds of sentient beings themselves. Karma means thinking, and obtaining means receiving retribution. Through the mind's thinking and receiving retribution, it manifests in this way. The tri-thousandfold world refers to the great thousandfold world. The great thousandfold world is the medium thousandfold world, and the medium thousandfold world is the small thousandfold world. The small thousandfold world consists of one thousand Mount Sumeru realms. A Sumeru realm is one four-continent world system, extending from the wind circle below up to the Highest Heaven above - this constitutes one realm. One thousand of these realms is called a small thousand. The wind circle refers to when the world first forms in the realm of space: due to the karmic power of sentient beings, a great wind suddenly arises that can support all things. Its extent is vast and wide, measuring countless yojanas. Its thickness is 1.6 billion yojanas. Regarding the order of the next two circles above and below, there is dispute among scholars within the Mahāyāna. Some say gold and water, others say water and gold. For now, we adopt the latter view. This is clearly stated in the Master's verse in the Kalpa Chapter. The text is evident, being identical with what is explained in the Abhidharmakośa and other texts. The "wind, gold, water" of the Yogācāra commentaries represents another master's interpretation. The true doctrine of our school is "wind, water, gold." The meaning of this doctrine is as follows: Rain falls abundantly on the great wind circle, measuring 1.12 billion yojanas. Of this, 800 million constitutes the water circle, and the remaining upper 320 million yojanas constitutes the gold circle. That is, the uppermost portion solidifies into gold. This gold circle is also called the earth circle. Therefore, the Yogācāra speaks of the "golden-natured earth circle."
