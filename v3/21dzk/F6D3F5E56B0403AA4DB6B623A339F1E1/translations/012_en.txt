That is, the previously mentioned essence-meaning and meaning both have universal-particular aspects, and individual characteristics and common characteristics follow in order without separate essences. All scriptures and treatises universally illuminate this. Third is the designation-non-designation contrast. What is not designated is called "inherent nature," and what is designated by verbal expressions is called "distinction." In texts like the Vijñānavāda, the inherent nature of all dharmas—since both conceptual knowledge and designation are not objects—connects to the previous two. If designated by verbal expressions, all are called "common characteristics," and what is not designated by verbal expressions is called "inherent nature." Fourth is the name-sentence contrast. What names designate is called "individual characteristics," and what sentences designate is called "distinctions." This is what texts like the Vijñānavāda mean by "names designate inherent nature, sentences designate distinctions." It also connects to the first two designation essences. Excluding universal names, when designation of inherent nature and meaning, sentence-designation of distinctions and name-designation are all viewed in relation to the third contrast, names and sentence-bodies all designate common characteristics. Through additional characteristics they can extend to others and are not the two individual characteristics, hence called "common." Excluding the principles of logic, this encompasses all explanations completely.

Various Types of Bondage

The fifth volume of Meaning Lamp says: Some explain two bondages—first, corresponding bondage; second, object-bondage. Others explain two bondages—first, subject-bondage; second, object-bondage. Still others explain two bondages—first, characteristic-bondage; second, gross-bondage. When the subject mind-king and mental factors exist simultaneously with afflictions, they cannot attain freedom because they are bound by corresponding dharmas. Correspondence itself is bondage, with "sustaining function" as the name. When the subject mind-king and mental factors are constrained by objects, this is called "object-bondage," extending to dharma-grasping mind. Subject-bondage means the bound objects bind the subject mind, like the name "ālaya." Characteristic-bondage means the mind being constrained by characteristics is called "characteristic-bondage." There are three types of characteristic-bondage: First (Sthiramati's mind theory, abbreviated). Second: contaminated characteristics are called characteristic-bondage, excluding only the uncontaminated, extending to the eight

Answer: Speaking of intermixing, one cannot eliminate characteristics. Gross-bondage: Generally encompassing all texts, there are four types of grossness: First, present contaminated mind and mental factors—the twenty-four types of grossness explained in the Abhidharmasamuccaya. Second, present functioning of two obstructions. Third, seeds of two obstructions. Fourth, habitual energies of two obstructions. Or regarding present functioning, generally speaking of contaminated three-nature mental dharmas. Seeds should not exist, because for wholesome and neutral seeds, the meaning of grossness is somewhat hidden, so various [texts] briefly do not explain. According to what is appropriate, all cultivate calm-abiding and insight to eliminate them. Question: Regarding "characteristic-bondage"—is it afflictive obstructions or cognitive obstructions? Answer: What fault would either have? Both have problems. If due to afflictive obstructions, two-vehicle arhats in contaminated mental states should have no characteristic-bondage because self-grasping is severed. If due to cognitive obstructions, post-emptiness wisdom should have characteristic-bondage. Two explanations: First, due to cognitive obstructions. If so, post-emptiness subsequently-attained [wisdom] should be bound. Answer: Because they do not correspond, according to Sthiramati's explanation, this is correct. Second, taking afflictive obstructions as the essence of characteristic-bondage. If so, two-vehicle arhats' contaminated wholesome and neutral minds should have no characteristic-bondage. Answer: Because this aspect-portion's essence is contaminated, being what they lead and perfume to form seeds. If contaminated characteristics each become characteristic-bondage, this extends to the eight consciousnesses. I privately question: Is the essence of characteristic-bondage the seeing-affliction obstruction, or is it the aspect-portion? If affliction obstruction, since it is already the cognitive subject, why call it characteristic-bondage? Why do explanations also take the aspect-portion? If aspect-portion, why say "afflictions are the essence," etc.? Answer: Characteristic-bondage refers to contaminated aspect-portions, not cognitive affliction obstructions. However, having the source of aspect-portion bondage returns to the fault of cognitive affliction obstructions. Explaining that source essence has two explanations—orthodox and heterodox as in the text. Question: If so, the cognitive subject would achieve itself. How does the aspect-portion have binding power? The essence and function of aspect-portions are originally like illusions. Not understanding this principle, one cannot attain freedom. This is merely mind's fault, not the object's fault. Answer: Speaking of the original cause, it is as questioned. Now not proceeding indirectly but directly pointing to what binds—hence aspect-portions. That is, the cognitive mind corresponds with two grasping types and cannot understand the meaning of illusion, repeatedly grasping them as real self and dharmas. Therefore transformed aspect-portions appear seemingly real. Since they seem real, they can bind the seeing-portion, making it unfree. This is the meaning of aspect-portions binding seeing. Question: If so, is that binding portion located in what is grasped or in other-dependence? Answer: The object's binding power is precisely located in other-

Question: If so, does this bondage exist only in minds where two grasping types are presently active? Answer: Not so. All contaminated minds carry this characteristic-bondage. The reason is that the deluded perfuming power of self-dharma two-grasping of sixth and seventh consciousnesses becomes the root source, so all resemble self-dharmas and all become deluded sentiments. Even if fifth and eighth consciousnesses have subtle delusion, they all carry the equal-flow seed-types of what is grasped. Though there are differences in gross-subtle and light-heavy, there is no time without carrying characteristic-bondage. If not so, why would one not understand illusory matters? Question: If so, does the characteristic-bondage of three-wheel grasping also exist in positions of wholesome minds like giving? Answer: Because that is heaviest, intermixed before and after, it is not in wholesomeness. What exists in wholesome positions is precisely that pervasive characteristic-bondage. Question: Do aspect-portions seem real due to the perfuming power of the past, or due to the deluded understanding of the present moment's cognitive subject? Answer: Through perfuming they seem [real], through seeming [real] comes deluded understanding; through understanding they seem [real], through seeming [real] comes perfuming. Beginninglessly it is thus—what is before and what after? Depending on capacity and following timing, explaining this has no fault. Question: Regarding present characteristic-bondage, among the self[-grasping] of sixth and seventh [consciousnesses], which self is it due to? Answer: Generally speaking, general characteristic-bondage is due to seventh consciousness self[-grasping]. Though three-wheel characteristic-bondage is also due to the seventh, it properly refers to the sixth. Hence the above text in the Lamp says: "Therefore speaking of characteristic-bondage exists in both types: first, extending to all characteristics, due to seventh consciousness self[-grasping]; second, grasping three-wheel characteristics—though also due to the seventh, properly refers to the sixth."
