Regarding the *Hossō Tōmyō-ki* (法相燈明記) on the right: I had previously heard that in earlier years it had been entrusted from the Hōin Dai-sōzu of Seson'in Shunsoku-bō to Tokue'in Senmyō Shunryō-bō. On this occasion, I secretly arranged to borrow it, and within just one or two days, I copied it in my own hand. I truly cannot contain my joy, and feel only deep gratitude for the abundant grace of the predecessors in the Dharma.

　The scroll of this original book is now held at Tokue'in. You must absolutely not speak of it to others or show it to others. These three texts are secret scriptures of the entire temple. They are to be revered and kept secret.

**[Left Page — Lower Section]**

While earnestly seeking an authoritative manuscript (*shōhon*), I was unable to fulfill my longstanding wish, and the days and months passed in vain. Now, fortunately, I have obtained this manuscript and have copied and preserved it. At this time, the lingering cold had not yet dispersed and my frozen hands could barely hold a brush; nonetheless, for the sake of the flourishing of the Buddha-Dharma, I suppressed my lazy disposition and applied [my] worn brush. I wrote this in the corner room facing the side of the *taiya* (annex) of Shūnan'in — that is all.
　　　　　　　　　　　　A humble student of the Ōri school: **Kōgyō** (eleven *rō* of precept seniority)
