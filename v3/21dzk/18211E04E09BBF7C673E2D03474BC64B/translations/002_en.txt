The *Hossō Tōmyō-ki* mentioned above: I had previously heard that in former years it was transmitted from Hōin Dai-sōzu Shunzoku-bō of Sezon'in to Senmyō-Shunryō-bō of Kudoku'in. On this occasion I secretly borrowed it, and within just one or two days I copied it in my own hand. I am truly overcome with joyful gratitude; I am only left feeling deep appreciation for the great benevolence of [our] revered predecessors.

　　The scroll of this work is now held at Kudoku'in. On no account speak of it to others or show it to others. These three texts are secret writings of the entire temple. They are to be revered and kept secret.

**[Left Page — Lower Section]**

　　[I had been] seeking an authoritative manuscript (*shōhon*) to verify [doubtful passages], but my long-cherished wish remained unfulfilled as the days and months passed in vain. Fortunately, I have now obtained this copy and have made a transcript for preservation. At this time, the lingering cold of late winter has not yet lifted, and my frozen hands could scarcely hold a brush. Nevertheless, for the sake of the flourishing of the Buddha-Dharma, I suppressed my laziness and wetted my worn brush. Written in the corner room of the facing building (*tsumaguki-no-tsubone*) of Shūnan'in. That is all.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　A humble student of the Ōri school (*Ōrishū*): Kōgyō (eleventh year of precepts)
