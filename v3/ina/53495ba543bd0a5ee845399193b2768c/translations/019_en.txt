In recent years, while rice prices have fallen, the prices of various commodities remain high, causing hardship for the people. For sake, vinegar, soy sauce, miso, and similar products made from rice and grains, it is natural that various commodity prices should be set based on grain prices at any given time. However, while rice prices have fallen, the prices of various commodities continue to rise gradually, which is improper conduct. Henceforth, commodity prices shall be reduced as much as possible in accordance with grain prices. Of course, even when prices are reduced, there is no point if product quality is compromised, so all transactions must be conducted properly.

[Left Page]
This applies to wholesale suppliers, intermediate buyers, and all others engaged in commerce. For those who still do not lower prices after receiving these orders, investigations shall be conducted through proper channels and strict punishment shall be imposed. Since this directive is being announced throughout all provinces and localities, if there are wholesale suppliers who do not reduce commodity prices or engage in hoarding, merchants under their authority must report such cases. Failure to do so will also be subject to punishment.
