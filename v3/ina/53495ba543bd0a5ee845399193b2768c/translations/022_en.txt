1. Regarding products produced within the domain, each should make an effort to send out goods in large quantities. Especially for the Fujisawa entrance to Noyatani Mountain, concerning the farmland and town areas, the collection of products should be carried out diligently without negligence. Although it was previously ordered in the 8th year of Bunka (1811), year of the sheep, that advance deliveries from rural areas were not permitted, this time it is reformed so that goods should be sent to the town for sale. When negotiating prices with the townspeople of this town, if discussions and adjustments cannot reach an agreement, the goods may be sold to either party. In such cases, those who sell to rural areas for export to other places may handle this at their discretion. However, freight should be sent to wholesalers with proper notification. Local products should be properly accepted and handled. Ultimately, because trading methods are immature, many merchants from other places have entered and are heard to be buying directly from rural areas. The townspeople are entirely avoiding the trouble, though there should be proper procedures for price matching. From such poor conditions, transactions naturally develop with outsiders.
