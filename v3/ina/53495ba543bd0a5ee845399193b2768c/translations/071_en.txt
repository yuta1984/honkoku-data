Since the remaining amounts have considerably decreased, regarding the system of providing travel expenses, for gold coins and below and silver coins that are difficult to submit in large quantities, since there are now many who submit them with expenses, henceforth for both old gold and silver coins and ni-shu coins, regardless of the amount submitted, even for small submission amounts, those whose distance exceeds five ri will be provided with travel expenses according to the previous rate based on the number of people and the amount submitted. Additionally, while travel expenses were not previously provided for genuine new ni-bu coins, henceforth, similar to old gold and silver coins, for those submitting genuine new ni-shu coins, when the distance from their residence to the gold mint or its nearest exchange office exceeds five ri, regardless of the amount submitted, travel expenses will be provided at a rate of five bu of silver per 100 ryō for each ri round trip, according to the number of people and the amount submitted.

In response, travel expenses shall be provided. Those possessing both old gold and silver coins and ni-shu coins should submit even small amounts to various nearby exchange offices. For shogunal territories, magistrates, and for private domains, lords and district heads should provide even more thorough assistance to ensure complete exchange by October of the year of the Dragon. If there are those in society who store and do not exchange their coins, after investigation, strict measures shall be taken. The above directive should be announced.
