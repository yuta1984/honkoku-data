For those submitting 10 kan-me or more, travel expenses will be provided at a rate of 1 rin per unit for the outbound journey: 5 bu of silver per 100 ryō of gold, and 3 bu of silver per 1 kan-me of silver, proportional to the amount of silver submitted. For shogunal territories (goryō), the magistrate (daikan), and for private domains, the domain lord or local administrator should investigate those requesting such expenses and submit applications through the Edo Gold and Silver Exchange. If individuals presumptuously attempt to apply directly to the Gold and Silver Exchange through their connections, this is also acceptable, so in any case, provide thorough assistance so that even those who have improperly handled old gold and silver can exchange their various items accordingly.

9th month of Bunka 14 (1817)

【Left Page】
Rivers throughout the provinces have become successively silted up, causing poor water flow and creating breaks in embankments. Fields have been flooded, water has entered houses, and in some places water levels continue to rise. For river courses with poor water flow, shoals should be restored and straightened, or channel improvements and dredging should be carried out. Using the excavated sand, regardless of whether they were previously official construction sites or private construction sites, embankments should be built up along the bank passages. If embankment work is carried out, the river channels will deepen, the embankments will become higher, water flow will improve, concerns about flooding will decrease, this will benefit forest management, and naturally flood damage can be avoided.
