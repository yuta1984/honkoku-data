Article: In the year of Boshinoe (1648), when the sacred portable shrine (mikoshi) of Gongen-sama was rebuilt, there were no mikoshi bearers available, causing confusion. Through the mediation of three men - Chūzaemon, Ichiemon, and Gonbei - it was arranged that six shrine priests and six members of Miyamachi would provide laborers, and the matter of participation fees (sanzen) was also settled for the time being. If the sacred palanquin became too heavy, it was agreed that only the shrine priests would carry it.

However, disputes arose from last year, and this time it became a legal matter. Through the mediation of Deputy Magistrate Yoshida Kinemon and the town elders, it was agreed that regarding the mikoshi participation fees, all would be collected by the shrine priests, but in exchange they would pay 600 mon as compensation to Miyamachi. As for mikoshi bearers, Miyamachi would provide six people as previously determined.

From now on, regardless of any grievances the shrine priests may have, six mikoshi bearers must be provided, and furthermore, all of Miyamachi must participate in the mikoshi procession. Should the sacred palanquin become too heavy, laborers need not be provided, and in that case the 600 mon compensation need not be paid either. Now that this has been determined, neither party shall violate this agreement. This document is written as evidence for the future.

Keian 4th year, Kanoto-u, 4th month, 3rd day (April 3, 1651)
