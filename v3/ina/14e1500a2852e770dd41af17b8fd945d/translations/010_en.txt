However, in the third year, the aforementioned Izumi-dono and Inaba-dono confiscated the mountain forests and temple grounds. Therefore, both the head priest and parishioners made various appeals saying "If things continue this way, the temple will be destroyed, so please grant us the lands as before," but this was not granted, and in the end the lands were not returned to us, causing us great distress.

As indisputable evidence, we possess several vermillion seal documents. Moreover, since the Hokoji Three Shrine Daigongen borrows and resides on Kenpuku-ji's mountain, they visit the temple annually as thanks. In ancient times, they also paid礼銭 (ceremonial money).

Now, we believe this is the proper time for this lawsuit. As Kenpuku-ji is increasingly heading toward destruction, we humbly beseech your mercy to hear and understand the aforementioned circumstances, and grant us the mountain forests and temple grounds as in ancient times. We earnestly implore your compassion.

Genroku 2nd year, 10th month, 18th day (October 18, 1689)
　　　　　　　　　　Takato-machi Kenpuku-ji
　　　　　　　　　　All Parishioners

To the Honorable Magistrate
