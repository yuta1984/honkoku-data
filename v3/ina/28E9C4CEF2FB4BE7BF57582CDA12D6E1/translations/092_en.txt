Morita

Morita Gen'emon

- Kan'en 3 (1750), 2nd month, 1st day: He served as a junior official in the investigation department with a stipend of 5 koku 9 to plus 3 to additional rice.

- Hōreki 3 (1753), 3rd month, 15th day: He was appointed as assistant foot soldier squad leader.

- Same year, 12th month, 23rd day: He was promoted to formal foot soldier rank, appointed as chief accounting clerk, and granted a stipend of eighteen hyō and two-person support.

- 9th year (Hōreki 9, 1759), 11th month, 4th day: He was granted attendant guard status and appointed to the purchasing department.

- Same year, 11th month, 20th day: He changed his name to Tokuzaemon.

- 11th year (Hōreki 11, 1761), 6th month: He changed his name back to Gen'emon.

- Same year, 11th month, 10th day: He sent his daughter to the Tateishi Yogoemon household.

- 13th year (Hōreki 13, 1763), 12th month: He was appointed to the storehouse department.

- Meiwa 8 (1771), 8th month, 28th day: He received an increase of four hyō, raising his stipend to twenty-two hyō and two-person support, with formal service duties.

- An'ei 8 (1779), 3rd month, 5th day: As requested, he was relieved of his official duties and appointed to rotating guard duty.

- 9th year (An'ei 9, 1780), 7th month, 20th day: As requested, he was granted retirement. Having served diligently for many years, he was awarded 200 hiki in gold.
    Service record

Son
    Morita Shōsuke

- Meiwa 9 (1772), 8th month, 1st day: He was summoned as a foot soldier, appointed to attendant service, and granted a stipend of sixteen hyō and two-person support, under the supervision of the foot soldier commander.

- An'ei 6 (1777), 2nd month, 11th day: He died of illness [in Edo].

Adopted son
    Morita Gen'emon
        Gen'jūrō

Kōshi year, 7th month, 20th day: Following his adoptive father Gen'emon's request for retirement, he inherited the family headship with a stipend of eighteen hyō and two-person support, and was appointed under the supervision of the rotating guard magistrate.

- Tenmei 7 (1787), 9th month, 21st day: He was appointed to attendant service.

- Kansei 3 (1791), 2nd month, 23rd day: He adopted his adoptive father's name, Gen'emon, having previously been called Gen'jūrō [in Osaka].

- 5th year (Kansei 5, 1793), 10th month, 1st day: He was appointed to the lumber and firewood procurement department.
