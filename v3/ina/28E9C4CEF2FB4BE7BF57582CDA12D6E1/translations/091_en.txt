- Hōreki 12 (1762), 4th month, 20th day: He inherited his adoptive father Tōsuke's position and was granted a stipend of eighteen hyō and two-person support, appointed as attendant foot soldier.

- Meiwa 5 (1768), 3rd month, 1st day: His request to marry the sister of Nishimura Tōhachi, a rural samurai at the magistrate's office of Lord Tsuji Genzagorō in Fuchū Tanida village, was approved.

- An'ei 4 (1775), 12th month, 13th day: He was appointed as temporary foot soldier inspector for attendant duty.

- 9th year (An'ei 9, 1780), 4th month: He was appointed as gate guard.

- Tenmei 4 (1784), 3rd month, 18th day: He successfully completed his service as gate guard for Lord Shōun'in and was reassigned to attendant duty.

- Same year, 5th month, 15th day: He was appointed as temporary clothing department officer.

- 7th year (Tenmei 7, 1787), 6th month, 28th day: He was appointed to the meal service for Lord Shōun'in while concurrently serving as temporary clothing department officer.

- Same year, 11th month, 24th day: He was relieved of clothing department duties due to vacancy.

- Same year, 12th month, 20th day: He was appointed as New Year ceremonial officer for Lord Shōun'in and granted 200 hiki for formal attire expenses.

- 8th year (Tenmei 8, 1788), 4th month, 1st day: He was promoted to formal attendant guard status with an increase of four hyō, receiving a total stipend of twenty-two hyō and two-person support, with duties remaining the same.

- Kansei 3 (1791), 2nd month, 28th day: His request to have his second son Tomihachi adopted by Imai Taemon, a retainer of Lord Naitō Tonomo-no-kami, was approved.

- 4th year (Kansei 4, 1792), 2nd month, 17th day: His request for his eldest daughter to marry Ikeda Ichizō, a minor guard of the Hitotsubashi house, was approved.

- 5th year (Kansei 5, 1793), 11th month, 20th day: He received an increase of two hyō, raising his stipend to twenty-four hyō and two-person support.

- 6th year (Kansei 6, 1794), 1st month, 7th day: He was ordered to exercise restraint, and was pardoned on the 21st day.

- Same year, 8th month, 12th day: He absconded, resulting in the dissolution of his house.

Son of Saichi
    Shinohara Tōsuke
        Originally Senzō

- Tenmei 5 (1785), 5th month, 15th day: He was appointed to child service and granted one-person stipend.

- Kansei 1 (1789), 4th month, 9th day: He changed his name to Tōsuke, having previously been called Senzō.

- 2nd year (Kansei 2, 1790), 3rd month, 21st day: He was appointed as assistant messenger for attendant service.

- Same year, 5th month, 28th day: He was granted an increase of one-person stipend.

- 4th year (Kansei 4, 1792), 3rd month, 12th day: He was appointed as inner monk attendant.

- 6th year (Kansei 6, 1794), 8th month: He absconded.
