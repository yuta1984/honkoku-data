- Bunsei 8 (1825), 7th month, 13th day: Granted formal ashigaru status with an allocation of sixteen hyō and two-person support, duties to continue as before

- Same year, 10th month, 13th day: Inherited his late father Nizaemon's position with twenty-two hyō and two-person support, granted formal escort duty status, duties to continue as before

- Bunsei 9 (1826), 7th month, 29th day: Temporarily appointed to provisional ashigaru inspector role

- Same year, 9th month, 28th day: Due to certain considerations, his official position was revoked and he was assigned to ashigaru messenger duty with eighteen hyō and two-person support, ordered to exercise restraint and conduct himself with strict caution

- Same year, 10th month, 14th day: Released from restraint and placed under chamberlain supervision

- Bunsei 11 (1828), 6th month, 1st day: Received an increase of two hyō and appointed as ashigaru inspector

- Same year, 9th month, 25th day: Due to illness, relieved of official duties as requested and appointed to messenger service, surrendered the previous increase, resulting in eighteen hyō and two-person support

- Same year, 11th month, 25th day: Absconded

Originally Takahashi, later Nakane Tadaemon

During the reign of Lord Genryūin

- Genbun 3 (1738), 6th month, 18th day: Employed as junior page-level secretary with thirty-five hyō and three-person support, later changed surname to Nakane

- Same year, same month, 28th day: Relocated to the upper mansion

- Same year, 7th month, 12th day: First audience with the lord

- Kanpō 2 (1742), 7th month, 9th day: Permission granted to marry the daughter of Ogawa Saburōbei, retainer of Lord Ōkubo Chikugo-no-kami

- Enkyō 2 (1745), intercalary 12th month, 1st day: Appointed to document duties for the official seal inspection

- Enkyō 4 (1747), 1st month, 15th day: Received an increase of one-person support, granted thirty-five hyō and four-person support

- Enkyō 5 (1748), 2nd month, 22nd day: Permission granted to adopt Kichizō, twelve-year-old son of a town physician of the Yamada school who was his cousin's relative

- Same year, 12th month, 19th day: Permission granted to take as second wife the daughter of Jusen, a physician serving Lord Koide Shinano-no-kami

- Kan'en 2 (1749): Appointed as junior page inspector and assigned to the service office
