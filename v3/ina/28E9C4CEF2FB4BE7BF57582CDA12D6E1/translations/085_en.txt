- On the 13th day of the 2nd month of Genbun 4 (1739), Year of the Sheep, his request to adopt as daughter the niece of his wife, who was the sister of Inoue Sakutayū, was approved.
- On the 8th day of the 3rd month of Kanpō 2 (1742), Year of the Dog, he took in an adopted son.
- On the 21st day of the 3rd month of the same year, he petitioned for his adopted son Shigehachi to inherit the family name, at which time he held the rank of lord's escort guard with 26 hyō and provisions for two people.
- On the 8th day of the 4th month of the same year, he died of illness.
　　　　　Service record

　　　　　　　　　　　Adopted son
　　　　　　　　　　　　Mikami Jūgorōemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Originally named Shigehachi
- On the 15th day of the 5th month of Kanpō 2 (1742), Year of the Dog, he inherited his late father Jūgorōemon's position with a stipend of 18 hyō and provisions for two people, was appointed to the foot soldier escort unit under the supervision of the foot soldier commander.
- On the 1st day of the 5th month of Kan'en 2 (1749), Year of the Snake, he was appointed to the temporary position of foot soldier inspector and moved to the upper mansion.
- On the 17th day of the 6th month of the same year, he was appointed to the permanent position of foot soldier inspector with an increase of 2 hyō, bringing his stipend to 20 hyō with provisions for two people.
- On the 18th day of the 7th month of Hōreki 5 (1755), Year of the Boar, he filed for divorce from his wife.
- On the 18th day of the 11th month of Hōreki 6 (1756), Year of the Rat, his request to take as second wife the sister of Mizuno Shūsaku, a retainer of Lord Sakakibara Shikibu-dayū, was approved.
- On the 13th day of the 10th month of Hōreki 7 (1757), Year of the Ox, there was a memorial service at Zama Sōchūji Temple, and Nagashima Shinemon was sent as an attendant, with 100 hiki granted as allowance.
- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Hōreki 8 (1758), Year of the Tiger, he was granted the rank of lord's escort guard and appointed as foot soldier sub-commander, with concurrent duties as personal guard commander, receiving an increase of 5 hyō for a total stipend of 25 hyō with provisions for two people.
- On the 1st day of the 10th month of Meiwa 1 (1764), Year of the Monkey, he was appointed as head of servants under the supervision of the chamberlain.
- On the 22nd day of the 3rd month of Meiwa 2 (1765), Year of the Rooster, foot soldier sub-commanders Manuri [possibly Makabe] Tomozaemon and Yamamoto Giemon were dismissed from their positions, causing a shortage in duties, so he consulted with Uchida Saburōemon and temporarily took charge of foot soldier affairs and served in local escort duties. On the 5th day of the 7th month of the same year, he was relieved of these responsibilities.
- On the 1st day of the 12th month of the same year, he was appointed as the year's ceremonial leader and granted hemp ceremonial dress.
