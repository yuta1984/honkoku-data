- Hōreki 7 (1757), 10th month, 23rd day: Two days ago at the hunting grounds at Katsumayama, successfully hunted a wild boar which provided entertainment for the lord, and was rewarded with 200 hiki in gold

- Hōreki 9 (1759), 3rd month, 2nd day: Appointed to temporary position as ashigaru inspector accompanying the young lord

- Hōreki 11 (1761), 10th month, 19th day: Appointed as ashigaru inspector with an increase of two hyō, bringing stipend to twenty hyō

- Hōreki 13 (1763), 5th month, 5th day: Ordered to make a pilgrimage to Myōgi shrine on behalf of the lord on the upcoming 11th day (day of the rabbit)

- Meiwa 3 (1766), 3rd month, 29th day: During the recent Kunō escort duty, lower-ranking soldiers made improper requests which were blocked by the supervising officers, but he insisted on pushing through with his own will. As an inspector, he should have been mindful of handling the minor officers properly, but lacking such consideration, he acted unilaterally in enforcing discipline. His position was therefore revoked and he was ordered to remain in confinement [at Edo duty station]

- Same year, 4th month, 19th day: Released from confinement. Due to his previous improper petition, his stipend support should have been revoked and permanent leave granted, but owing to his hereditary status and by the lord's mercy, his additional stipend for his position was revoked, reduced to eighteen hyō with two-person support, and reassigned to regular groom service ashigaru [in Edo]

- Meiwa 6 (1769), 11th month, 11th day: Granted formal escort duty status and appointed as head of servants and baggage supervisor, with an increase of six hyō bringing his stipend to twenty-four hyō with two-person support, under chamberlain supervision

- Meiwa 7 (1770), 3rd month, 11th day: Dispatched for the Suwa festival in the 4th month, granted two bu for formal dress allowance and 200 hiki in gold

- An'ei 8 (1779), 1st month, 15th day: Appointed as head of stores, in charge of equipment

During the reign of Lord Honryūin

- Tenmei 3 (1783), 7th month, 5th day: Due to illness, relieved of head of stores and equipment duties as requested, and appointed to regular shift duty

- Kansei 4 (1792), 4th month, 7th day: Died of illness
