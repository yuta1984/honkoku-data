　　　　　　　　　　　Seizaemon's son (?)
　　　　　　　　　　　　Ishii Sakudayū
1. Jōkyō period (1684-1688): Bowl ceremony official
1. Genroku period: 3 ryō, rice for 1 person, kitchen staff
1. Hōei period: 19 hyō, rice for 2 people. "Catering department" was crossed out and corrected to "reception hall clerk." Around this time he likely inherited his father's household
1. Same 2nd year, year of the rooster (1705), 3rd month, 7th day: Head clerk, foot soldier group leader rank
1. Same year, intercalary 4th month: By serving as record keeper, granted foot soldier rank
1. Same year, 9th month, 3rd day: Due to misconduct, granted permanent dismissal

　　　　　　　　　　　　Hayashi Jūtazaemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Initially named Jūtadayū

Reign of Lord Shinnyoin

1. Kyōhō 7, year of the tiger (1722), 12th month, 27th day: Appointed as foot soldier inspector, granted 1 ryō for haori coat from borrowed funds

1. Same 9th year, year of the dragon (1724), 4th month, 1st day: Appointed as personal attendant head, succeeding Mikami Jūgorōemon's rank, granted attendant duty, 1 ryō travel money, stipend of 6 ryō and rice for 2 people, under foot soldier commander supervision

1. Same 10th year, year of the snake (1725), 1st month, 1st day: Changed name to Jūtazaemon, previously called Jūtadayū

1. Same year, 10th month, 1st day: Appointed foot soldier sub-commander, increase of 1 ryō and rice for 1 person, granted 7 ryō and rice for 3 people, ordered to concurrently supervise personal attendants temporarily

1. Same 14th year, year of the rooster (1729), 1st month, 17th day: Requested to marry Okuda Kuzaemon's sister, approved and took her as wife on 2nd month, 12th day

1. Same 20th year, year of the rabbit (1735), 6th month: Died of illness

　　　　　　　　　　　　Hayashi Hanpei
1. Kyōhō 20, year of the rabbit (1735), 9th month, 1st day: Inherited deceased father Jūtazaemon's position, granted stipend of 5 ryō and rice for 2 people, appointed as attendant foot soldier
