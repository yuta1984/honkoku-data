Makabe Tomouemon
Original name: Yasuuemon

- On the 28th day of the 10th month of Enkyō 2 (1745), Year of the Ox, he was granted an additional allowance of 3 to of rice, receiving a total of 5 koku 3 to. He was appointed as an apprentice rural district secretary under the district magistrate group.

- On the 12th day of the 4th month of Enkyō 3 (1746), Year of the Tiger, he was promoted to lord's attendant ashigaru and granted 16 hyō with provisions for two people.

- On the 15th day of the 4th month of Enkyō 7 (1750), Year of the Ox, he was appointed as temporary ashigaru inspector while stationed in Edo.

- On the 15th day of the 8th month of the same year, he was appointed to the permanent position of ashigaru inspector.

- On the 19th day of the 4th month of Enkyō 8 (1751), Year of the Tiger, he was granted an increase of 2 hyō.

- On the 1st day of the 12th month of Enkyō 10 (1753), Year of the Dragon, due to the young lord's illness, he was appointed as proxy representative to Ise Province and granted 200 mon in gold while in Edo.

- On the 19th day of the 10th month of Enkyō 11 (1754), Year of the Snake, he was granted formal escort guard status and appointed as ashigaru squad leader, concurrently serving as personal attendant leader. With an increase of 5 hyō, he received 23 hyō with provisions for two people.

- On the 22nd day of the 3rd month of Meiwa 2 (1765), Year of the Rooster, he petitioned regarding misconduct during escort duty to Kunō, was dismissed from his position, and ordered into house confinement while stationed in Edo.

- On the 22nd day of the 4th month of the same year, regarding the previous improper petition by a commoner who conspired with others, and his own participation in group conspiracy and misconduct, his stipend allowance was to be confiscated and permanent dismissal granted. However, since all involved were hereditary retainers, and considering that his elderly father had served faithfully for many years without incident but would face destitution in his extreme old age, this was deemed pitiable. Through special mercy, while his additional allowances and escort guard rank were confiscated, he was granted 16 hyō with provisions for two people equivalent to a dependent son's status, and ordered to return to regular escort service.

- On the 4th day of the 9th month of Meiwa 3 (1766), Year of the Dog, he was appointed as proxy representative to Myōgi on the upcoming 12th day of the Rabbit.

- On the 11th day of the 8th month of Meiwa 4 (1767), Year of the Boar, he was appointed as secretary to the chamberlain.

- On the 5th day of the 5th month of Meiwa 8 (1771), Year of the Rabbit, he was granted formal escort guard status and appointed to treasury management, with an increase of 2 hyō to receive 18 hyō with provisions for two people.

- On the 11th day of the 4th month of An'ei 3 (1774), Year of the Horse, due to his father Kanroku's old age and infirmity, he inherited the family headship as petitioned, receiving 20 hyō with provisions for two people without change. His formal duties continued as before.

- In the 8th month of the same year, his petition to adopt Nejisuke, the second son of rōnin Kumebo Yagozaemon under Lord Mizuno Hayato-no-shō of Sajiki Village in the Matsumoto administrative territory, was approved.
