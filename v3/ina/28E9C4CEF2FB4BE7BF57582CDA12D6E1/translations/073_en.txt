Makabe Yogorō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Original name: Hannoshin

- On the 13th day of the 2nd month of Bunka 5 (1808), Year of the Dragon, he succeeded his late father Yoichirō with a stipend of 18 hyō and provisions for two people, and was appointed to formal ashigaru guard duty rotation.

- On the 23rd day of the 8th month of Bunka 6 (1809), Year of the Snake, he was appointed to escort duties.

- On the 13th day of the 7th month of Bunka 8 (1811), Year of the Sheep, the petition to make his sister the adopted daughter of Reida Uemon was approved (while in Edo).

- On the 18th day of the 6th month of Bunka 13 (1816), Year of the Rat, he was appointed as secretary to the senior councilors and chamberlains, under chamberlain supervision.

- On the 5th day of the 6th month of Bunpō 1 (1818), Year of the Tiger, he was granted formal escort guard status and appointed as deputy magistrate for rural administration under district magistrate supervision.

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunpō 3 (1820), Year of the Dragon, he received an increase of 2 hyō.

Reign of Lord Raiei

- On the 28th day of the 9th month of Bunpō 5 (1822), Year of the Horse, due to rural administration reorganization, he was smoothly relieved of deputy magistrate duties and appointed to guard duty rotation.

- On the 27th day of the 10th month of Bunpō 7 (1824), Year of the Monkey, he fled/deserted.


　　　　　　　　　　　　Nakayama Shinpachi

- On the 4th day of the 12th month of Kyōhō 8 (1723), Year of the Rabbit, he was appointed to kitchen service rotation or guard duties.

- In the 6th month of Genbun 2 (1737), Year of the Snake, he died of illness.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Nakayama Tōhachi

Reign of Lord Genryūin

- On the 25th day of the intercalary 3rd month of Kyōhō 20 (1735), Year of the Rabbit, he was recruited as escort ashigaru, granted 4 ryō and provisions for two people, and it was announced in Edo that he would serve under ashigaru captain supervision.

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kyōhō 21 (1736), Year of the Dragon, he was called to serve as rural district secretary without formal rank, and granted 3 ryō 2 bu and provisions for two people.

- On the 15th day of the 8th month of Genbun 2 (1737), Year of the Snake, succeeding his late father Shinpachi, since Shinpachi had served faithfully for several years, he was granted ashigaru rank with 4 ryō and provisions for two people, and would continue his duties as district magistrate secretary as before.

- On the 2nd day of the 12th month of Genbun 4 (1739), Year of the Sheep, he was appointed to chief accountant duties while continuing as secretary.

- On the 28th day of the 12th month of Genbun 5 (1740), Year of the Monkey, he received an increase of 2 hyō, making his total stipend 20 hyō.

- On the 1st day of the 11th month of Enkyō 2 (1745), he was appointed to formal escort guard duties with an increase of 2 hyō.
