- 15th year (Tenpō 15, 1844), 5th month, 3rd day: Due to administrative reorganization, middle page inspector position relieved without incident, supervision duties for young lords to continue as before

- Kōka 2 (1845), 4th month, 18th day: Due to illness, supervision duties for young lords' quarters relieved as requested, appointed to assist guard duties

- Same month, 28th day: Due to advanced age and illness, granted retirement as requested, family headship passed to son Zennosuke without issue
　　　　61 years of service
　　　　Recorded as extinct house due to separation from two adopted sons

　　　　　　　　　　　Adopted son
　　　　　　　　　　　　Actually third son of Ōta Yūemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　Ikegami Zennosuke
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Zenshirō

- Tenpō 12 (1841), 3rd month, 13th day: Appointed to employed service as retinue foot soldier, granted sustenance allowance of two-person stipend, under command of foot soldier captain

- 13th year (Tenpō 13, 1842), 1st month, 3rd day: Due to shortage of Buddhist attendants, appointed to temporary assistance, by directive placed under command of chamberlain during assistance period

- Same month, 4th day: Directive to observe inner quarters procedures

- Same year, 4th month, 8th day: Since father Zenshirō was appointed to retainer rank, henceforth to serve as second-seat retinue guard rank, temporarily to assist due to shortage of Buddhist attendants, henceforth under chamberlain command

- 14th year (Tenpō 14, 1843), 5th month, 18th day: Due to this time's financial reforms, employed service position unavoidably relieved without incident, thereby granted one-person stipend. Moreover, hereafter may occasionally be appointed to lower-ranking duties

- Kōka 1 (1844), 12th month, last day: Due to staff shortage, appointed to employed service in retinue and personal service, granted increased sustenance allowance of two-person stipend

- 2nd year (Kōka 2, 1845), 4th month, 28th day: Father Zenshirō granted retirement as requested, having served diligently for years, exceptionally granted family headship with stipend of thirty hyō and two-person sustenance allowance without issue. Appointed to ranked middle page retinue service

- Same year, 6th month, 23rd day: Appointed to lower residence service

- 4th year (Kōka 4, 1847), 8th month, 10th day: Name changed to Gentayū

- Kaei 1 (1848), 3rd month, 23rd day: Name changed to Zenshirō

- 3rd year (Kaei 3, 1850), 9th month, 15th day: Due to young lord's outing, appointed to intensive retinue service, thereby granted allowance of 2 ryō 3 bu in gold
