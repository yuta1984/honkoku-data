Itō Katsuuemon
　　　　　　　　　　　Changed name to
　　　　　　　　　　　　Baba Yasuuemon

During the reign of Lord Genryūin
1. 20th year of Kyōhō, Year of the Rabbit (1735), November 13th: In Edo, at Hoshino Saiin's residence, summoned from the position of chamberlain secretary which he had served until this time, granted three ryō and support for two retainers with the rank of foot soldier.

1. Same year, same month, 18th day: Changed name to Yasuuemon.

1. 2nd year of Genbun, Year of the Snake (1737), December 19th: Promoted to the rank of foot soldier, granted additional payment of two bu, with a total stipend of three ryō two bu and support for two retainers.

1. 5th year of same era, Year of the Monkey (1740), December 25th: Granted an increase of two hyō, total stipend of eighteen hyō.

1. 2nd year of Enkyō, Year of the Ox (1745), January 15th: Appointed to concurrent temporary purchasing officer position, granted one retainer's support as official duty allowance as customary.

1. 3rd year of same era, Year of the Tiger (1746), August 28th: Appointed as secretary to the senior councilors and elders, while remaining in the temporary purchasing officer position.

1. 4th year of same era, Year of the Rabbit (1747), December 29th: Due to ancestral connections through his mother's side, granted permission as requested to change his family name to Baba Yasuuemon.
