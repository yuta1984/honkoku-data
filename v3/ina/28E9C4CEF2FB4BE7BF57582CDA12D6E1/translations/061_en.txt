Nakamura Tazaemon
Originally named Magoshichi

During the reign of Lord Shinnyoin
- Kyōhō 10 (1725), 1st month, 15th day: As requested, summoned to escort service, placed under foot soldier captain's supervision with four ryō rice stipend and two-person support

- Kyōhō 12 (1727), 12th month, 1st day: As his father requested, retired and ordered to inherit headship, had audience on the 21st of the same month

- Kyōhō 13 (1728), 8th month, 23rd day: Appointed as magistrate's scribe

- Kyōhō 14 (1729), 10th month, 15th day: Dismissed from scribe duties and returned to escort service

During the reign of Lord Genryūin
- Enkyō 1 (1744), 12th month, 1st day: Appointed as escort guard and foot soldier inspector

- Enkyō 3 (1746), 1st month, 15th day: Appointed to concurrently serve as foot soldier sub-captain and personal guard captain, granted increase of one-person support

- Kan'en 3 (1750), 12th month, 1st day: Having served escort duties for several years and reaching old age, successfully discharged from escort service and appointed to secondary guard duty

- Hōreki 2 (1752), 8th month: His son Bansuke, now thirty years old, absconded from the Edo residence five years ago in the year of the Dragon and has been away for a long time; official notification to the government completed

- Same year, 10th month, 13th day: Ordered to change residence, granted the former residence of Okutani Genzaemon in Shimoda-machi

- Hōreki 3 (1753), 1st month, 12th day: Request approved to adopt Danji, younger brother of Komatsu Gunzō who serves as deputy to Lord Noro Iemon, and have him marry his daughter

- Same year, 11th month: Returned adopted son Danji due to incompatibility

- Hōreki 5 (1755), 9th month, 18th day: Appointed to oversee meeting hall storage duties

- Same year, 12th month, 19th day: Should serve secondary guard duty, but when there are duties related to meeting hall baggage, should withdraw from guard duty. During lord's absence, should make rounds of the retainer households

- Hōreki 7 (1757), 11th month, 4th day: Appointed as head storehouse keeper

- Hōreki 10 (1760), 1st month, 25th day: Request approved to adopt rōnin Ogiwara Yogorō, formerly of Lord Wakisaka Awaji-no-kami, and have him marry his daughter

- Hōreki 12 (1762), 4th month, 5th day: His son Satarō divorced

- Hōreki 13 (1763), 2nd month: Request to adopt Ōta Wataru approved

- Same year, 8th month, 18th day: Request approved as wished to send granddaughter to former adopted son Satarō

- Same year, same month, 28th day: Took in adopted son Wataru and renamed him Kōemon

- Same year, 9th month, 20th day: Request approved to have adopted son Kōemon marry his daughter

- Meiwa 1 (1764), 9th month, 15th day: As requested, discharged from head storehouse keeper duties due to old age infirmity and eye disease
