Appointed as head inspector

- Hōreki 7 (1757), 11th month, 18th day: Due to the dissolution of his previous adoption, permission granted to adopt Mataroku [age 10], third son of Okayasu Sawaemon, retainer of Lord Watanabe Etchū-no-kami

- Meiwa 4 (1767), 9th month, 1st day: His petition for official position was blocked by the officials

- Same month, 5th day: Died of illness
End of service

Adopted son
Nakane Kichizō

- Kan'en 1 (1748), 8th month, 11th day: Appointed as attendant with one-person support granted, no formal rank, treated as a child in all matters [age 12]

- Kan'en 3 (1750), 5th month, 5th day: Announced that he would receive two ryō in gold annually for paper handkerchief expenses, but later the adoption was dissolved

- Kan'en 4 (1751), 4th month, 25th day: By his father Tadaemon's request, granted permanent leave

Adopted son
Nakane Mataroku

- Hōreki 12 (1762), 11th month, 11th day: Appointed as attendant with one-person support granted, without formal rank

- Meiwa 4 (1767), 8th month, 29th day: Called up as escort ashigaru with sixteen hyō and two-person support granted, placed under ashigaru head supervision

- Same year, intercalary 9th month, 25th day: Inherited his late father Tadaemon's position with twenty-two hyō and two-person support granted, given escort rank and rotation duty, under chamberlain supervision, granted quarters at the lower mansion

- Meiwa 5 (1768), 2nd month, 1st day: Due to his late father Tadaemon's diligent and earnest service, granted formal junior page status with an increase of four hyō and one-person support, totaling twenty-six hyō and three-person support, appointed to escort duty

During the reign of Lord Zuiun'in

- An'ei 5 (1776), 5th month, 6th day: Appointed to upper mansion domestic affairs and granted Okayasu Uhei's former quarters

- Same year, 7th month, 1st day: Absconded
When ordered to relocate to the upper mansion, he left a note for his mother stating it was inconvenient

- Same year, 7th month, 26th day: Mataroku, forgetting the lord's great benevolence, absconded
