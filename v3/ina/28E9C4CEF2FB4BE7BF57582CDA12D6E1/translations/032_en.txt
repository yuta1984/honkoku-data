Son of Gonpei
　　　　　　　　　　　　Tomioka Kore□
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Original name: Yoshika
1. 9th year of Kyōhō, Year of the Dragon (1724), August 18th: Summoned as page, granted two ryō one bu as confectionery allowance, at that time 14 years old, on the 22nd renamed Kore□
1. Same 10th year, Year of the Snake (1725), September 28th: As usual, granted three ryō with support for two retainers
1. Same 16th year, Year of the Boar (1731), December 13th: Died of illness

　　　　　　　　　　　Adopted son of Gonpei
　　　　　　　　　　　　Tomioka Kyūzaemon
1. 18th year of Kyōhō, Year of the Ox (1733), July 12th: Summoned and granted three ryō with support for two retainers, to serve without rank in the cuisine department of the current lower residence tea house
1. Same year, August 1st: To serve in the cuisine department of the upper residence
1. Same 20th year, Year of the Rabbit (1735), April 28th: As petitioned by adoptive parent Gonpei, granted permanent leave, henceforth shall not enter or exit the residence nor loiter around the domain

　　　　　　　　　　　Adopted son of Gonpei
　　　　　　　　　　　　Tomioka Genshichi
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Original name: Chōzō
1. 3rd year of Enkyō, Year of the Tiger (1746), April 6th: Summoned as foot soldier, granted sixteen hyō with support for two retainers, appointed as secretary to the steward under steward supervision
1. Same year, June 17th: Renamed Genshichi
1. 4th year of Kan'en, Year of the Sheep (1751), August 21st: Appointed as secretary to the senior retainers and elders
1. 2nd year of Hōreki, Year of the Monkey (1752), July 21st: Succeeded late father Gonpei with twenty hyō and support for two retainers, appointed to attendant rank, duties as before
1. Same 6th year, Year of the Rat (1756), March 28th: Appointed as temporary purchasing agent in addition to other duties
1. Same 7th year, Year of the Ox (1757), January 29th: Submitted petition to be relieved of both positions. Regarding eye disease □ medical treatment would be appropriate. However, as document work causes difficulties, this hinders continuation and would particularly obstruct medical treatment. Therefore, secretary duties temporarily excused, but regarding purchasing duties, as these should not interfere with the eye condition, petition returned with instruction to continue temporary duties for two or three months
1. Same 8th year, Year of the Tiger (1758), January 15th: Appointed head of servants, concurrently serving as chief treasurer
