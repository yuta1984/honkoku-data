Two ryō, two bu, one person stipend
Son of Izawa Takebei of the advance guard unit
Special summons
Izawa Ihachi
Changed name to Hisayoshi
Also called Naouemon

During the reign of Lord Genryūin
- First day of the eleventh month of Genbun 2, year of the Fire Snake (1737): Summoned to serve as a monk
- First day of the second month of Kanpō 1, year of the Metal Rooster (1741): Granted five koku and two-person stipend
- Fourteenth day of the fifth month of Hōreki 14, year of the Monkey (1764): Appointed as tea ceremony monk inspector, granted seventeen hyō, and as ashigaru equivalent, granted three bu in gold for ceremonial dress
- Fifteenth day of the fifth month of Meiwa 5, year of the Earth Rat (1768): Given ashigaru regular rank, with one hyō increase
- Fourth day of the intercalary twelfth month of An'ei 4, year of the Wood Sheep (1775): Four hyō increase, given attendant rank, ordered to relocate to Edo

During the reign of Lord Honryūin
- Eighth day of the second month of the same 7th year, year of the Earth Dog (1778): Ordered to return to secular life
- Eighth day of the eighth month of the same year: Monk service for inspection office rest quarters duties
