Nakayama Isoemon
During the reign of Lord Yori'i
- Kansei 2 (1790), 3rd month, 23rd day: Promoted to ashigaru (foot soldier), stipend of 18 hyō and provisions for 2 persons, appointed to o-tsugiban
- Same 5th year (1793), 10th month, 1st day: Appointed to hayashi-takeyabu ginmi-yaku (forest and bamboo grove inspection role)
- Same 10th year (1798), 5th month, 23rd day: Due to illness, requested release from duties, returned to o-tsugiban
- Same 11th year (1799), 5th month, 8th day: Upon request, retired; family headship transferred to son Heizō
Service record

Nakayama Heizō
During the reign of Honryū-in-sama
- Anei 6 (1777): Appointed as bōzu joshin (monk's assistant) from the regular Edo group
- Tenmei 6 (1786), 9th month, 15th day: Appointed to serve as monk with additional oku responsibilities, granted stipend of 3 koku 5 to and provisions for 2 persons
During the reign of Lord Yori'i
- Kansei 4 (1792), 3rd month, 21st day: Appointed as provisional bōzu metsuke (monk inspector)
- Same 7th year (1795), 6th month, 28th day: Promoted to ashigaru by rank, stipend of 10 [koku]
