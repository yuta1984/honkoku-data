Inherited stipend of twenty-two hyō and food allowance for two persons, with rank of lord's attendant guard, concurrently serving as chūgen head and baggage supervisor
　　　　Service record
　　　　　　　　Izawa Rokurōemon
　　　　　　　　　　Suemon
　　　　　　　　　　Ihei

During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- Kansei 11, tsuchi-no-to hitsuji (1799), 12th month, 26th day: Temporarily assigned as assistant to monk attendants
- Same 13th year, kanoto-tori (1801), 1st month, 15th day: Appointed as hired monk attendant, thereby granted food allowance for one and a half persons
- Kyōwa 3, mizuno-to i (1803), 11th month, 20th day: Smoothly relieved from monk attendant duties, to serve exclusively as assistant scribe
- Bunka 6, tsuchi-no-to mi (1809), 3rd month, 13th day: Granted food allowance for two persons, appointed to full scribe position
- Same 7th year, kanoe-uma (1810), 2nd month, 15th day: Father Rokurōemon retired as petitioned, inherited family stipend of twenty-two hyō with food allowance for two persons, rank of lord's attendant guard, concurrently appointed as chūgen head and baggage supervisor
- Same 9th year, mizuno-e saru (1812), 1st month, 15th day: Ordered to serve during the Suwa Onbashira festival in the 4th month of this year

During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- Bunsei 9, hinoe-inu (1826), 5th month, 5th day: Increase of two hyō, duties to continue as before
- Same 10th year, hinoto-i (1827), 5th month, 25th day: As petitioned, relieved from concurrent duties as chūgen head and baggage supervisor, assigned to guard rotation
- Tenpō 6, kinoto-hitsuji (1835), intercalary 7th month, 1st day: Granted rank of chū-koshō, increase of food allowance for one person, total stipend of twenty-four hyō with food allowance for three persons
- Tenpō 13, mizuno-e tora (1842), 6th month, 28th day: Died of illness
