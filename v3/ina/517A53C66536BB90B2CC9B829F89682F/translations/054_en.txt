Reign of Lord Yorimoto
- On March 20th of Kansei 4, Ne year (1792), inherited his adoptive father Buzaemon's position with a stipend of 18 hyo and 2-person sustenance allowance, becoming a foot soldier in the lord's retinue
- On March 15th of the same 12th year, Saru (1800), appointed as foot soldier inspector
- On April 25th of Kyowa 1, Tori year (1801), granted 5 monme of silver for conch drum license
- On July 7th of the same year, as formal retainer guard received an increase of 2 hyo, appointed as head of servants and baggage supervisor
- On March 15th of the same 2nd year, Inu (1802), appointed as foot soldier squad leader and personal guard commander
- On the 3rd day of intercalary 8th month of Bunka 2, Ushi year (1805), placed under district magistrate supervision
- On March 25th of the same 4th year, U (1807), satisfactorily relieved of district magistrate duties and appointed to guard rotation
- On August 6th of the same year, Sato Chugoro'emon's formal position and stipend allocation were revoked, and he was placed in custody along with his household
　He was initially granted 4-person sustenance allowance as assistance
- On February 23rd of the same 8th year, Hitsuji (1811), appointed as temporary foot soldier squad leader and personal guard commander
- On July 10th of the same 13th year, Ne (1816), appointed to Otaki supervision duties

Reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- On September 18th of Bunsei 4, Mi year (1821), increase of 2 hyo, duties to continue as before
- On June 15th of the same 8th year, Tori (1825), appointed to serve at Lord Ryonoshin's residence
- On November 2nd of the same year, ordered to exercise restraint regarding his son Katsubei's matter
　On the 17th of the same month, relieved of restraint order
- On May 5th of the same 9th year, Inu (1826), promoted to formal middle page with increase of 2 hyo and 1-person sustenance allowance, duties to continue as before
- On July 28th of Tenpo 7, Saru year (1836), due to changes in Lord Ryonoshin's residence management and reorganization, satisfactorily relieved of residence duty
- On May 22nd of the same 14th year, U (1843), as requested due to illness, granted permission to retire, with succession granted to his son Katsubei
