Reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- Tenpō 7, Year of the Monkey, 5th month, 28th day (May 28, 1836): Due to staff shortage, appointed as assistant messenger on temporary hire, granted 1-person sustenance as assistance, second rank among monks

- Same 11th year, Year of the Child, 4th month, 5th day (April 5, 1840): Monk temporary hire, increase of 1-person sustenance, assistant messenger duties to continue as before; on the 6th day, due to staff shortage, notified of temporary assistant duties in the inner quarters

- Same year, 6th month, 13th day: As requested by father Zenzō, appointed as heir, henceforth appointed as foot soldier temporary hire

- Same year, 11th month, 28th day: Appointed to corridor duty assistance, inner quarters monk assistance to continue as before, granted 1 koku stipend rice

- Same 14th year, Year of the Rabbit, 5th month, 18th day (May 18, 1843): Due to financial reforms this time, unavoidably released from temporary hire without incident, accordingly granted half-sustenance, furthermore there may be occasions when appointed to duties of lower rank

- Kōka 1, Year of the Dragon, 12th month, 30th day (December 30, 1844): Due to staff shortage, appointed as foot soldier temporary hire and attendant service assistant, and due to monk department staff shortage, to also serve temporary assistance, granted increased assistance of 2-person sustenance

- Same 2nd year, Year of the Snake, 12th month, 23rd day (December 23, 1845): Inherited deceased father Zenzō's position, granted stipend of 22 hyō and 2-person sustenance, appointed to rank of attendant guard, disbursement and construction department subordinate

- Kaei 2, Year of the Rooster, 4th month, 10th day (April 10, 1849): Appointed to clothing department, others to continue as before

- Same 3rd year, Year of the Dog, 4th month, 8th day (April 8, 1850): Marriage proposal granted as requested

- Same year, 4th month, 15th day: Appointed as supervisor for Princess Iō's wedding disbursements

- Same year, 11th month, 13th day: Due to young lord's outings creating much office work, granted monetary reward for diligent service

- Same 4th year, Year of the Boar, 6th month, 15th day (June 15, 1851): Appointed to remittance department, others to continue as before

- Same year, same month, 20th day: Changed name to Zenzō
