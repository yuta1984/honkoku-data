- Kansei 1, Year of the Rooster (1789), 6th month, 20th day: Concurrently appointed as corridor duty monk inspector, granted an increase of 2 hyō
- Kansei 3, Year of the Boar (1791), 1st month, 15th day: Promoted to middle page (chūkoshō) with formal rank, granted an increase of 2 hyō and 1-person stipend

Reign of Lord Yorimochi
- Kansei 5, Year of the Ox (1793), 10th month, 1st day: Successfully relieved of monk inspector corridor duty, appointed to alternate duty
- Kyōwa 1, Year of the Rooster (1801), 11th month, 15th day: Granted 100 mon in gold for decades of diligent service
- Bunka 1, Year of the Rat (1804), 3rd month, 13th day: Retired upon request, family headship granted to his son Heigo. Granted 200 mon in gold for decades of service
- Same year, 4th month, 12th day: Died of illness (aged 85)

Arakawa Eiji (later renamed Jihei)
- Meiwa 7, Year of the Tiger (1770), 6th month, 17th day: Summoned to serve as katte ashigaru in Edo, granted 16 hyō and 2-person stipend
- An'ei 5, Year of the Monkey (1776), 5th month, 17th day: Due to illness, requested to return his stipend allocation. This was communicated to his relative Sehei in Takatō. For his diligent service thus far, granted 1-person stipend. Notification given to Jihei in Edo on the 10th of the same month

Arakawa Kanzaemon (second son, became heir after Jihei's death from illness) (former names: Mokuraō, Shinka, Heigo)

Reign of Lord Genryūin
- Meiwa 6, Year of the Ox (1769), 8th month, 28th day: Granted 3 koku 5 to and 1-person stipend, served as formal monk
- An'ei 2, Year of the Snake (1773), 6th month, 28th day: Increased to 5 koku for 2 people, stationed duty

Reign of Lord Honryūin
- An'ei 6, Year of the Rooster (1777), 9th month, 28th day: Ordered to return to lay life (secularization)
