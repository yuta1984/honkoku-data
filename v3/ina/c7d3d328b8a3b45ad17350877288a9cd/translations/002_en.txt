With such a large volume of cargo, this would not be an easy but profitable venture, so the entire district would work together to transport the goods. They also promised to assist each other when there were many official transport duties (goyūma) in the future, and generally wanted to maintain good relations. The two aforementioned people repeatedly stated this, and thinking it reasonable, we signed the agreement. However, Wada-shuku raised objections and filed a complaint with the Dōchū Bugyōsho (Highway Magistrate's Office), resulting in not a single load of cargo being allowed through. Therefore, we have continued to handle cargo forwarded from Kanazawa-shuku without interruption as before. However, in recent years, the passage of daimyo from western and central Japan has increased yearly, causing accumulated hardships. Our people and horses have become impoverished, many farmers have been ruined, and the situation has become unsustainable.
