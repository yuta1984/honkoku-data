but due to the nature of their occupation, their household members have been for a long time

【Left Page】

suffering hardship and are on the verge of
starvation, a situation unbearable to witness.
Therefore, relatives have petitioned
the temples expressing their distress, but regarding matters
under investigation, it is difficult to
make appeals for mercy. However, since the wives and children cannot
even survive today, and there have been
petitions submitted by three temples,
this is certainly not a matter of negligence. Regarding the
livelihood of the household members,
with special consideration for various matters,
relatives and community groups shall be appointed as guardians
on the condition that a written pledge of guardianship be submitted.
Furthermore, understanding well the circumstances under which
Manko-ji, Kenpuku-ji, and
Renge-ji temples have
appealed for mercy,
[they] must conduct themselves with strict prudence
in all matters.
Regarding the above, each person shall attend and
　　　　　　　convey [this message].
