　　　Akita
Akita-jōsuke Abe no Sanesue is said to be a descendant of Kuriyagawa Jirō Sadatō, the second son of Abe no Yoriyoshi of the six districts of Mutsu.《Note: According to his genealogy, his ancestors lived in Abeno Ikoma in Settsu Province, but the period is so ancient that the details are unclear. Around the middle period came Abe no Sadatō, and his descendants remained in the eastern provinces for over three hundred years, though their names are not certain. Looking at the "Aizu Shike Gōkō" (Four Houses of Aizu Combined Study), the description of this house appears somewhat detailed. This must have some basis. However, there are differences between what the official histories record and what the genealogies state, leaving considerable doubt. While this book records all doubts, taking the theory from the Gōkō to annotate is because the unbroken imperial succession of one hundred emperors in our land is spoken of as a rare occurrence even in foreign countries. Our land is a divine country. The divine succession (amatsuhitsugi) of the descendants of Amaterasu Ōmikami shall not cease, and it is natural that this should continue as long as heaven and earth endure. That the descendants of those who ruled this land before the divine descendants governed it should maintain their houses to this day and continue to govern districts and villages is unprecedented in other countries and is one of the mysteries of our land.
