Sanada greatly rejoiced and had the Echigo forces defend Ueda Castle, while he himself led 9,000 troops and took position at Onoyama, deploying forces here and there to await the enemy. Tokugawa-dono's military forces gained no advantage and finally withdrew, so the Echigo forces also returned. In the winter of the same year, Kimura again went to Echigo Province as Kampaku's messenger and lavishly bestowed gifts upon Kagekatsu and the chief retainers of his clan. This was understood to be a congratulatory embassy for the alliance between the two houses. In this year, Kagekatsu attacked and captured the castles of Sugihara, Niigata, and Numatari. {{Marginal note: These were all castles of Shibata (Katsuie's) supporters.}} When it was announced that he should visit the Kampaku in the coming Tensho 14, Hideyoshi greatly rejoiced and again sent Kimura as an envoy bearing various gifts.

【Left page】
Kagekatsu departed from Echigo on the 11th day of the fifth month, accompanied by Kimura. Ishida (Mitsunari), Masuda (Nagamori), and Otani (Yoshitsugu) went down the Hokuriku road making preparations here and there. On the 27th of the same month, Kagekatsu entered the capital. The Kampaku had already designated his lodging {{Marginal note: It was Honkoku-ji Temple}}. That night, he personally visited his lodging and celebrated his long journey to the capital. {{Marginal note: On this night, it is said that Kampaku-dono had Kagekatsu offer him a cup and drank sake, gave a cup to Naoe, and then took that cup to celebrate the visit to the capital.}} The next day, Kagekatsu visited Jurakudai Palace. Through the Kampaku's recommendation, Kagekatsu was appointed to Sangi (Counselor) and granted the Fourth Court Rank. Five retainers including Naoe, Fujita, and Yasuda were granted court ranks, and he returned to his home province in the seventh month of the same year.
