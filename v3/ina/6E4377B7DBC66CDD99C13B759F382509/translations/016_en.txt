The one who governs the authority of the realm, having crossed many difficult places in this age of warring states, came for two reasons: first, to form an alliance with Kagekatsu without breaking last year's oath, and second, relying on Kagekatsu's rare and admirable conduct. However, to strike him down in a surprise attack would tarnish the honor of archery that Kagekatsu has righteously maintained for many years. Rather, Kagekatsu should meet with him and either become close by acceding to his wishes, or else first send him back this time and raise an army again to decide the battle. With this decision, he took with him Naoe Yamashiro-no-kami, Fujita Noto-no-kami, Izumisawa Kawachi-no-kami, Yasuda Chikuzen-no-kami and other retainers, totaling twelve mounted warriors and sixty-odd

foot soldiers, and came to Ochimizu Castle around the hour of the Horse on that day to meet with Hideyoshi. After the formal greetings concluded, Ishida (Mitsunari) was at Hideyoshi's side while Naoe was at Kagekatsu's side, and they secretly deliberated for more than two hours. At the beginning of the hour of the Cock, Hideyoshi departed from Ochimizu, returned to Toyama Castle through the night, handled administrative affairs in Echizen Province, went to the capital, and was appointed Kampaku (Imperial Regent) in the seventh month of this year. In this month, Sanada Awa-no-kami Masayuki, a resident of Shinano Province, rebelled against Tokugawa-dono and sought Kagekatsu's assistance, offering his second son Genjiro as a hostage. {{Marginal note: Kagekatsu granted territory to Genjiro. This refers to the later Saemon-no-suke.}} Kagekatsu sent military forces to Shinano Province with Kawada, Yasuda, and Honjo in the vanguard, totaling 6,500 men.
