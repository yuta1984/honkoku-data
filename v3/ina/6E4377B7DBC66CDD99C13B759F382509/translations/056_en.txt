Nagatane, the seventh-generation descendant of Mitsutane, died without an heir. The Takahashi retainers—Yayama, Obara, Fukuda, and Itō—petitioned the Ōtomo to provide a clan member to succeed as head of the family. Saemon-no-suke Yoshishige, hearing of this matter, had the son of his kinsman Ichimanda Sakyō-no-dayū succeed to the house, taking the name Takahashi Mikawa-no-kami Ōkura Akitane. On the 10th day of the 4th month of Eiroku 2, saying that Hōman Iwaya was the family's main castle, he granted him over 2,000 chō of land and had him administer the affairs of the fifteen districts of Chikuzen Province. Later, Ichimanda committed crimes and was suddenly executed. Akitane, resenting Yoshishige, fortified himself in Hōman Castle in the spring of Eiroku 10 and conspired

with the Mōri of Aki Province, rebelling against the Ōtomo. Yoshishige, considering this an intolerable matter, sent military forces to attack the castle. In the 5th month of the same 12th year, when he heard that the Mōri forces had been routed at the Battle of Tatara-hama, Akitane lost his strength and finally came out as a surrendered person. His entire domain was confiscated, and he was granted only Kikukō district in Buzen Province, moved to Kokura Castle, and became a Buddhist monk, taking the name Sōsen. Sōsen's retainer Obara Iga-no-kami Shigehisa, having some grievance against his lord, left the province and came to the Ōtomo, saying: "I do not neglect to continue bearing resentment against my lord, the monk lord.
