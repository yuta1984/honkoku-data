《Note: This was a confrontation at the residence of Ōkubo Sagami-no-kami Tadachika》Sanesue stated in his defense: "Sanesue has never harbored any disloyalty toward your house (the Tokugawa). Therefore, when [enemies] sided with the Uesugi, I attacked and destroyed the enemies who had fortified themselves in Shizengo castle in Yashima-no-shō, Yuri district, Dewa province, and cut off over a hundred heads. I also besieged and captured the castle in Ogachi district where Ōmori Gorō, the younger brother of Onodera Tōtōmi-no-kami who was an adherent of Ishida, had taken refuge. If Sanesue had conspired with them, how could I have attacked allied castles and fought against allied forces?" Yoshimitsu, upon hearing this, said: "That he attacked and captured those two castles and cut off heads is of course true. However, when he heard that the imperial enemies from east and west had either perished or surrendered, he changed his previous agreements in order to escape future blame and

struck down his own supporters." The Great Lord (Tokugawa Ieyasu) asked: "When exactly did Sanesue capture those three castles?" Sanesue respectfully replied: "Indeed, sire. I destroyed Shizengo at the end of the eighth month, captured Ōmori on the fifth day of the ninth month, and Ishida's army was defeated at Sekigahara on the fifteenth day of the ninth month. Even if Sanesue had initially communicated with them and joined their conspiracy, how could it be possible to perceive from a thousand ri away that their grave destruction was imminent, repent of previous wrongs, and devise stratagems to avoid future punishment?" After this statement, there were no further inquiries. In the summer of the same seventh year, when the Satake were transferred to Akita, Sanesue [was assigned to] Ibaraki in Hitachi province
