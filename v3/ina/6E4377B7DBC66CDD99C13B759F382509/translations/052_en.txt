When Toyotomi Kanpaku (Chief Advisor) heard of this, he said: "At present, when Shimazu forces have invaded the Ōtomo domains and castles here and there have fallen, not only has Tōra prevented his own castle from falling despite his small forces, but he has achieved such incomparable military distinction. Moreover, he has defeated large enemy forces not just once—this is most admirable conduct." He granted an imperial letter and rewarded him. In the autumn of Tenshō 15 (1587), when the two islands of Kyushu were completely pacified, Tōra was soon summoned to become a direct retainer, granted the three lower districts of Chikugo Province, and given Yanagawa Castle. 《Said to be over 120,000 koku》 He was appointed Sakon-shōgen and changed his name to Muneshige. Muneshige assisted Sassa and destroyed the rebels one by one, pacifying that province. 《Sassa Mutsu-no-kami Narimasa incurred guilt at this time and was executed》 In Tenshō 16 (1588), Muneshige went to the capital. When His Lordship (Toyotomi Hideyoshi) summoned him close to his presence, he said: "Last year I intended to grant you Higo Province, but I heard that your household retainers disliked it, so I did not grant it. As a reward this time, if there are any vacant provinces among the four provinces of Shikoku or the nine provinces of Kyushu, state your desire. I shall grant it according to your request." Muneshige respectfully replied: "Muneshige has now received Your Lordship's benevolence and commands over 3,000 troops.
