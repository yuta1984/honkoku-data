When he said this, the Kanpaku was extremely displeased and declared, "What you say is reasonable. Why should you, Yoshinobu, wait for permission regarding those fellows who defy your orders? You should act according to your own judgment." Yoshinobu was greatly pleased and immediately returned to his home province. Under the pretext of holding a banquet, he summoned all thirty-three local lords, had them killed one by one, and annexed their lands, thereby becoming counted among the six great daimyo of the realm. 《marginal note: Tokugawa, Mōri, Uesugi, Maeda, Satake, Shimazu}} Since Yoshinobu's rise to such prominence as a daimyo was entirely due to Ishida's scheming, he thought to somehow repay this

Therefore, when the Taikō passed away and many daimyo formed factions to attack Mitsunari, Yoshinobu led his forces and rushed to Osaka, welcomed Mitsunari, and accompanied him to his residence in Fushimi. Later, in the autumn of Keichō 5, when Lord Tokugawa made his expedition to attack the Uesugi in the northern provinces, Yoshinobu, who had been in communication with Mitsunari's plots, did not respond when summoned by the lord's messenger. Instead, he led over forty thousand cavalry, passed through Ishinasaka, positioned his forces in Taga district, and plotted to attack from behind when Lord Tokugawa was heading north and the battle was at its height.
