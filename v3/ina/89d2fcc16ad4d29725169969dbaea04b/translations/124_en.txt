He came forward expressing a desire to sharecrop the aforementioned rice field. From Koka 2 (year of the snake) to the year of the monkey, he worked as a sharecropper for four years. Each year, he only received the sharecropping tribute rice as advance payment, and the shortfall in rice accumulated considerably. Therefore, we discontinued the sharecropping arrangement, and from the year of the rooster onward, we let others sharecrop the land. Regarding the unpaid tribute rice, we made repeated demands, but he made various excuses and refused to settle accounts at all, causing us extreme trouble. Although we had neglected such matters, we had postponed action with forbearance until now. According to the aforementioned certificate documents, there is absolutely no obligation to return the rice fields, dry fields, or donated land.

We are submitting copies of the two transfer certificates along with the account of overdue sharecropping rice. We humbly request your wise consideration and ask that you help the other party understand this matter. Furthermore, should there be additional inquiries, we will humbly respond orally.
If you would graciously grant our request as stated above with your benevolence, we would consider it a most grateful fortune. The above. Honmachi
