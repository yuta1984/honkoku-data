The sake brewing business was transferred to Daiji, who was from a branch family of Rihe, and they continued operating the sake brewing business using equipment jointly owned by Iemon and Rihe. However, during the current Jirōzaemon's time, in the first year of Genji (1864), they lent the entire sake brewing operation to Sangoro of Noguchi village, and because they allowed him to freely handle rent and taxes, they fell into financial difficulty and requested an investigation of the circumstances.

Jirōzaemon's response was as follows: When the previous generation lent their jointly-owned sake brewing license to Uemon, they were ordered to suspend operations. Regarding the license that this person later litigated over, Daiji, who was the younger brother of Takichi (Rihe's son) and had established a branch family, paid twelve ryō as transfer money and came to own it. Originally, the family had been on good terms and conducted business without problems. However, since they were to take over as a substitute for the suspended license held by both families, Daiji paid forty-eight ryō in license fees to both families, so they understood that the sake brewing license was completely owned by Daiji.
