(One thousand koku each to the princes) On April 7, Keian 3 (1650), six daimyo were selected and ordered that if there should be fires within or outside the castle and their force became intense, they should dispatch cavalry to provide swift rescue. Masuchika was chosen as one of them (this was the beginning of fire rescue by daimyo). On December 28, Jōō 1 (1652), he was granted court rank and appointed as Tosa-no-kami. He died on April 1, Kanbun 2 (1662), at the age of twenty-eight. Since he had no children, his younger brother Masuhide became his heir (19,000 koku). On December 28, Kanbun 4 (1664), he was appointed as Shinano-no-kami and granted the court rank of Junior Fifth Rank Lower Grade.

【Left Page】
　　　Kamei
Musashi-no-kami Minamoto Korenobu (later Bungo-no-kami) is said to be the descendant of the fifteenth generation from Gorō Yoshikiyo, the fifth son of Sasaki Minamoto Sanmi Hideyoshi, who was a descendant of Emperor Uda. Initially, Yoshikiyo was appointed to the position of jitō (land steward) of Izumo, Oki, and other territories and was appointed as Oki-no-kami. His descendants for generations received imperial edicts as provincial governors and were granted jitō positions, residing in Izumo Province. Masamichi, the sixth-generation descendant of Yoshikiyo, received a character from the name of Shōgun Yoshimasa of Jishōin. Later, during the time of Koretsuna's grandfather Koremune, he was particularly counted among the seven prominent local lords in Izumo Province, and by his father Nagatuna's time, they resided in Susa Castle in this province.
