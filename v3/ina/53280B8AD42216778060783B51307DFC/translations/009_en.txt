【Left Page】
　　Tozawa
Ukyōnosuke Taira Masamori (Upon examination, Masamori may be a son of Heikurō Mitsumori) was the son of Jibū-no-taifu Morinobu, and a descendant of Taira Kanemori. Kanemori first went down to Mutsu Province and resided in a place called Tozawa in Tekiseki-no-shō, Iwate District. Later he moved to Yamamoto District in Dewa Province. (According to genealogical records)

(It is said that Prince Owa's son, Hida-no-kami Taira Kanemori, lived in a place called Tozawa in Tekiseki-no-shō, Iwate District, Mutsu Province. However, upon examination, there appears to be no Prince Owa among the imperial princes of successive generations. Also, among those who were granted the Taira surname, none named Owa can be found. The 58th emperor Kōkō Tennō's first prince, First Rank Shikibu-kyō Koretada Shinnō, also called Nakano Shinnō. His son, Junior Fifth Rank Upper Yamashiro-no-kami Prince Okimasa, also written as Prince Okiga. His son, Daini Junior Fourth Rank Upper Atsuyuki, was granted the Taira surname. His son, Junior Fifth Rank Lower Taira Kanemori, was the one who won the poetry contest of Tentoku era with a poem about secret love, and was the real father of Akazome Emon. In the Gosen-shū he appears as Kanemori-ō. According to Kiyosuke's theory, since Kanemori was originally of princely lineage, he petitioned during the Tenryaku era to return to his original surname. This person appears in Yamato Monogatari as Echizen-no-gon-no-kami. According to some accounts, he was also Junior Fifth Rank Lower Suruga-no-kami. There is no record of him being Hida-no-kami. Also, this may not be the same person but rather a different Taira Kanemori, which remains unclear.) His descendants, our ancestor first...
