After the Battle of Sekigahara concluded, Takahashi (Ukon Shōgen) had already surrendered and been granted his original domain, so it was ordered that Miyazaki Castle, which the Itō had captured, should be returned to Ukon Shōgen. Shimazu was also granted confirmation of their original domain, so the lands of Mukasa, Kuraoka, Takaoka, Ayaai and others were returned to Ryūhaku Nyūdō (Shimazu Yoshihiro). All the lands they had personally fought for and conquered were returned entirely to their original lords, which must have been deeply disappointing. Thus, on the 11th day of the 11th month of this year (1600), Bungo-no-kami Sukehei's illness finally proved incurable and he died at the age of sixty. His eldest son Sakyō-no-suke Sukeyoshi received the original domain (said to be 57,000 koku) and was appointed Shuri-dayū. Yamato-no-kami Sukehisa inherited the house (54,000 koku), and his second son Shuzen-no-shō Suketomi was granted a division of his father's domain (3,000 koku, later receiving additional favor for a total of 5,000 koku). Yamato-no-kami Sukehisa died at the age of forty-nine on the 27th day of the 10th month of Meireki 3 (1657), and his eldest son Sakyō-no-suke Sukeyoshi succeeded his father (50,000 koku). His second son Shōgen Sukesane was granted a division of his father's domain (3,000 koku). On the 13th day of the 6th month of Kanbun 3 (1663), Sukeyoshi died young at the age of thirty-one. Having no children, he adopted his younger brother Kenbutsu Sukesane. Sukesane inherited his brother's house (54,000 koku, including all the domains he had personally been granted), and on the 28th day of the 12th month of Kanbun 3, he was ennobled and appointed Izumo-no-kami.
