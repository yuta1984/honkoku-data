All the enemy generals were listed on a notice board, which read "Whoever takes the head of so-and-so will receive such and such reward," and this was posted at the front of the camp. {{Marginal note: One theory says this notice board was erected by Araki Settsu-no-kami Murashige}} Se-hyōe-no-jō Hidehide at this time belonged to the forces of Araki Settsu-no-kami Murashige, a general under Lord Oda. Upon seeing this notice, he took up a brush and made a mark above Wada's name. Those who saw this found it strange, but that very night, around the hour of the rat (midnight), he indeed took the head of Iga-no-kami Koremasa and presented it. Murashige was greatly surprised and asked, "How did you so easily kill Wada?" Hidehide replied. What Hidehide had thought in his heart was: "The battle between enemy and ally will be decided tomorrow. Those who will be killed on both sides will not be few. If I throw away this life that will die the same way tomorrow during this night instead, taking Wada's head would be very easy.

【Left page】
The enemy will surely consider tomorrow's battle of great importance. They will certainly send experienced warriors tonight to survey the depths and shallows of the Yodo River. Koremasa is such a general. He should not rely only on scouts. He will surely come to see for himself. There can be no doubt that the general will set out. Ah, I will strike him down. Even if I cannot take Koremasa's head and am killed instead, if I die fighting after entering among many enemies attempting to take a general's head, that glorious reputation will not fade." Having made this resolve, he decided to cross the river and spy before the enemy drew near. He crossed the water and lay hidden in the shadow of willows on the far bank, waiting.
