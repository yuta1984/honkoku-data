【Right Page】
By official announcement on the 18th day of the 6th month of the year Hinoe-Saru (Tenpō 7/1836), 
from tomorrow the 19th day, prayers for abundant grain harvest at Gongen shrine
have been ordered. There is a record in the Saru year official ledger.

First day official duties:
　One magistrate (go-bugyō-sama)
When the above descends, [officials] wait on the eastern side outside the two-post stand
One squad leader (kogashira), then three of our people go out in formal dress (jōge)
To the west side-street valley, village officials with spear-bearers [go] to the next location
One town representative each appears in hakama and haori

When the administrator (go-shihai) and deputy magistrate (go-daikan) appear, we [wait] at the same location
or within the spear-bearer town for audience. The matter of town representatives is the same as above.

Middle days:
One administrator and two deputy magistrates appear
The final day is the same
Both magistrates and all deputy magistrate offices
without exception perform official duties

During proxy worship:
The head priest guides [officials] from the wide courtyard and hand-washing area,
and at the time of return, sees them off down to below the first torii gate
as is customary

The magistrates [station] their deputies east and west below the gate
The deputy magistrates of Kawashimo town and the administrator [station themselves] 
beside the first torii gate

Squad leaders, ourselves, and town representatives
come out to the sacred ground courtyard. Naturally, we
wear formal dress as representatives

Spear-bearing officials go out to the side-street entrance
