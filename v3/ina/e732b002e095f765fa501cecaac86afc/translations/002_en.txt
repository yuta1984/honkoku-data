Promissory Note for Borrowed Money Due to Overdue Tax Payments

One item: Six ryō in gold coins (Edo bunkin currency)

We hereby borrow the above amount for the certain purpose of paying overdue taxes to the authorities. As collateral for this loan, we deposit the original deed for the Yakushi-dō temple rice field that we received from Asaemon-sama. The interest rate is set at 15% per year, and we promise to settle both principal and interest by year's end without fail. Should there be any delay in repayment, the guarantors will assume responsibility, seize the aforementioned collateral, and deliver it to you, ensuring no inconvenience is caused. We affix our seals as proof thereof.

Hōreki 12th year, 12th month　　Borrower: Hyōzaemon of Deguchi Village
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Guarantor: Kinzaemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Same: Sōshichi

This amount was received and settled in the 12th month of the Year of the Rabbit.
