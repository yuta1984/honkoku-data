We were informed that [officials] were stationed at the mausoleum. After the gate master's representative Shūgaku-in arrived at the mausoleum, we were guided and proceeded via the outer route from the entrance. With only the steward, caretaker, and two sword bearers, we left our swords with the sword bearers outside Yaiba Gate, and from inside the same gate there were thin mats laid out. At that location we untied our bundles, and since there was a water basin in that direction, we used the water for purification. Then, guided by the lodging temple Hōmon-in, we entered the mausoleum. On the left and right of the Imperial Chinese Gate, the two deputy representatives and other monk-soldiers took their seats. Without need for formal greetings, we ascended the stairs. At this time, music began. Inside the doors, we performed a medium bow before the main altar, took our seats on the tatami mats to the right, and after a short while the sutra chanting began. This proceeded according to proper ritual form. At this time music began, and the gate master's representative Shūgaku-in entered the inner sanctuary, completed the incense offering, and Ryūkō-in made a bow. With that bow we stood and entered the inner sanctuary (placing our folding fans in front), lightly placed our hands on the kneeling cushion, and performed the incense offering (using aloes wood from the incense container).
