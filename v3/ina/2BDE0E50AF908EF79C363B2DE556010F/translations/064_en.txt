September 14th

After the rotating duty attendants completed their formal greetings, the assistant guard took his customary seat near the pillar at the corner of the veranda in the Fuyo-no-ma (Lotus Room). The senior councilors took their positions near the cedar sliding doors. Senior Inspector Hori Tōshū served as the measurer, and I (Oki) withdrew as usual from between the second and third pillars, then sat facing the second senior councilor with a space of two tatami mats between us. Then Lord Bizen-no-kami announced the following:

"In connection with the imperial succession, you are appointed as the imperial representative and deputy for the Nikkō Mausoleum."

After this proclamation was completed and the written appointment was to be presented, I stood up and came before His Lordship, whereupon the written appointment was handed to me. I immediately returned to my original seat, reviewed the document, and passed it to Oki, who examined it and returned it to me. I then stated the following:

"I humbly and gratefully receive this honor."

After stating this, I withdrew from between the first and second pillars, passed in front of the inspector's room, moved from the Tsutsuji-no-ma (Azalea Room) to the Naka-no-ma (Middle Room), and both of us took our seats as shown in the diagram.
