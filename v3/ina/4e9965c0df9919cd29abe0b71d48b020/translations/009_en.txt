One: "By all means, we shall proceed to Iida," 
　you graciously said, so we took our leave and departed.
　One: We stayed one night at Shioya Yaheiji's place.
One: The matters we consulted and inquired about with the Asuke village officials are 
　recorded on a separate sheet. Furthermore, in conversation:
　　About six or seven desks should be prepared. Inkstone boxes are
　　not needed. In Koromo, we hear rumors that officials
　　of about the rank of attendant (gokyu-ban) are dispatched, but
　　in Asuke, messenger officials (tsukai-ban) from above
　　come to the honjin. Also, when departing,
　　young retainers carry the document boxes (tsugibako)
　　dressed in formal attire, present their credentials, and
　　offer greetings—this we witnessed ourselves.

One: We understand that they stop at all places
　where there are local administrative offices (jinya) and such.
One: Even on the Mikawa roads, they separate and make
　their rounds, as recorded on the separate sheet.
One: Along all the routes they will travel, road improvements are being made.
　Even the mountain paths are being made beautiful
　by spreading sand.
One: In Asuke as well, though not saying it is
　everything, there are numerous houses
　with bamboo blinds hung.
