As transportation services will be conducted, from the same date all relay stations (temba-sho) and auxiliary post stations (sukego) shall be completely abolished. This is communicated for your understanding.
1. There have often been cases where local customs have been made into private laws based on old practices. For instance, when land was granted to those who served ancestors, their descendants are called "house retainers" (ie-kakae) and treated like family servants, not being regarded as equals with other villagers. Or when people move in from elsewhere, they are called "water drinkers" (mizunomi) and similarly

are not allowed equal social interaction. Such practices frequently occur and cause resentment among the people, going against the principles of harmonious social relations. Therefore, establishing family status based on such old customs is strictly prohibited.
1. Those who cleared wilderness in ancient times and established villages have been called "original pioneers" (moto wo kusamiri), and because they are old families, they despise later generations and often engage in improper behavior. While they may take pride in their ancestors' achievements, there is no reason why they should dominate others in the present day.
