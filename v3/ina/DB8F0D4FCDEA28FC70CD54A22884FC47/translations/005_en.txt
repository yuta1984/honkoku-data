The people from Karaki-sawa came and reported that they had heard from consultations with the landholders from Kamishima village, Imamura and others that they had agreed to recover the money according to the transfer documents and return the mountain forests to the original landholders. Even if this were to happen, among those who had gradually sold the land, some families had become extinct by this time, and some had lost or had their documents destroyed by fire, making the situation extremely complicated. Therefore, they petitioned that the domain forest be transferred to the grassland area that Karaki-sawa had taken over. When we discussed this, since it concerned other villages rather than matters within Tatsuno village, we responded that such arrangements would be difficult and unlikely to succeed. However, the landholders from the three villages all came together and petitioned with various difficulties. When they were allowed to examine the domain forest ledger, they understood for the first time and gradually comprehended the reasoning, acknowledging that it was entirely reasonable and unavoidable. They requested that, out of compassion, the current landholders be shown mercy so they would not face insurmountable difficulties, and accordingly they submitted a written pledge.
