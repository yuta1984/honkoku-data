【Left folio】

　4th Month, 20th Day　　Kamiya Jōzaemon
　　To: Satō Kyūuemon-dono

Regarding the above, the two net-casters (ami-uchi) were summoned and given their orders. Due to inclement weather (futenyaku), the visit was postponed; the visit accordingly took place on the 22nd day.

25th Day

One item: Today, a colleague (dōyaku) recovered from illness and returned to duty; it was agreed that he should resume his monthly duty rotation (tsukiban).

One item: Regarding seven oak trees (kashi) in the lacquer field on the Okabayashi slope, as four of them are to be replaced with new saplings, a request was submitted to have them cut down and sold for silver. Two officials from the Construction Office (o-fushin-kata) — Shimoyaku Yasubē-dono and the chief carpenter (daiku-gashira) — came to inspect the site, and upon inspection determined that two of the trees could be put to official use. As their findings differed from our own assessment, both parties returned to conduct a joint re-inspection. As a result, twelve trees were deemed serviceable for official use, and a written report was submitted stating that three trees were of poor grain quality. In any case, no official ruling (o-sata) was issued today.

One item: Regarding the net-casters going out for night fishing (yo-ami), (the necessary) items and supplies were prepared and dispatched. Henceforth, orders have been issued for them to go out for nightly net inspections/fishing.
As this has been duly arranged.
