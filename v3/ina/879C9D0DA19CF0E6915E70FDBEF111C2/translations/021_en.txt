【Sticky note】Timber, Tenryū River transport □
1st day
One Rain, fifth hour [approx. 8 a.m.]　　　　Sōhei
2nd day
One Regarding Ōkusa Kutarōzaemon-sama being of young age,
　this time again, we heard from Kuwaya
　Yoemon that he would be moving to Iijima; accordingly, as this
　appears to be the case, I instructed that the names of the
　officials who would be sending a messenger be inquired of,
　whereupon it was said that a messenger had been dispatched,
　and a message was sent to the Iijima town lodging.
　I reported this matter to Gonzaemon-dono,
　who replied that in that case, they would send over their seal
　impression, and that an express messenger would be sent.
　After making this inquiry, one middle-ranking attendant
　(chūgen) was immediately assigned; moreover, as traveling
　expenses were urgently needed, five hundred mon in coin
　was advanced and handed over, and instructions were given
　to settle the accounting.
One The wording of the letter is as follows:
One Kyūbei respectfully writes: As the weather has become
　warmer, I trust that all of you are continuing in good health
　and carrying out your duties; I hold this in great esteem.
　Furthermore, I have received word that the documents
　regarding the transport of timber down the Tenryū River
　have this time received the counter-seal (uradate)
　at your office. Henceforth, matters concerning the
　timber affairs here shall be brought to your attention;
　please arrange for them to be settled appropriately.
　Accordingly, one seal impression (hankan) is herewith
　forwarded...
