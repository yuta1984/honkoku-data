3rd Day

Item: Today, Matsudaya Rokushichizaemon presented two small promissory notes (ko-tegata) for sealing. I duly affixed my seal and returned them.

Item: Regarding the on-site inspection of the timber weir (ki-yana) on the river at Himochi, as ordered yesterday by Lord Jōzaemon — Kyūbei went to inspect, and found that the entry point (i.e., the inlet or approach to the weir site) was generally unsuitable. This was duly reported to Lord Jōzaemon, who responded that, while he had intended to proceed with the plan, it should be cancelled.

Item: As there was no other suitable weir river available, it was suggested that the Benten Kawara (Benzaiten Riverbank) timber-and-fuel river (o-maki-kawa) be inspected. Kyūbei was accordingly dispatched to examine the site. Upon inspection, it was found that at this location, a channel-clearing (sarai) of the Katanosuke fuel-wood river was currently underway, and it appeared that the irrigation/dam channel (o-tsuki-kawa) could be suitable. It was assessed that a weir could be erected and the work completed with a workforce of sixty to seventy persons in a single operation. Kyūbei returned from the inspection and this finding was immediately reported to Lord Jōzaemon. The matter from the previous report was also duly noted and recorded.
