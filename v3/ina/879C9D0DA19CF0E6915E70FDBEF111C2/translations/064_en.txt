【Right folio】

　　7th Month　　(In charge:) Kyūzaemon

2nd Day
Item: Today, I was summoned by Jōzaemon-dono who wished to have a meeting. Upon attending, he raised the matter of a fish weir (yana) to be installed on the Fujisawa River. It was reported that the Construction Office (Ofushin-kata) had previously submitted a request to install a replacement weir for official use, and that the senior councilors (karō), elders (otoshiyori), and administrators (oyōnin) had convened about this matter. In response, it was conveyed that while the weir would serve an official purpose, an earlier inspection related to the assembly had resulted in a decision to refrain from proceeding. Further inquiries were made regarding other possible weir locations, but it was reported that all nearby sites had already been applied for. It was then suggested that, excluding the previously designated non-holding (himochi) area, there was a small tributary fork (mata) slightly downstream from the Lower Daimyōjin pool (Shimo Daimyōjin-fuchi). Upon hearing this, Jōzaemon-dono instructed me to send a subordinate official to inspect the site and to estimate how many laborers would be needed, taking into account water flow and other conditions. I duly dispatched Kyūbei to carry this out, and gave my regards, reporting that I would provide a full answer the following morning, before returning.

Item: Today, someone came from the Matsuda residence to collect a small certificate (kohyōkata/kotekata). I accordingly sent the seal/stamp to them.

2nd Day
Item: In the 5th month, during Kyūzaemon's rotation as duty officer (tsukiban), a report was submitted on the number of fish caught by net fishing. As this did not match the written record submitted by the Provisions Office (Makanai-kata), Jūzō-dono instructed that the matter be investigated and the written record corrected. Accordingly, after consulting with the Provisions Office, it was reported that a reply would be submitted by the 5th, the next official business day (oyō-bi). Upon carefully reviewing the net fishing ledger (ami-uo-tōri), it was found that there was a slight discrepancy in the count of "sako" (a type of small fish/catch category), and it was reported that this discrepancy was not of any great significance.
