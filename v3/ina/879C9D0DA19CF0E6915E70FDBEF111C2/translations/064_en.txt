【Right folio】

　　7th Month　　Kyūzaemon (on duty)

2nd Day

Item: Today, having been summoned by Lord Jōzaemon for a personal consultation, I duly proceeded to present myself. The matter concerned the Fujisawa River, and it was desired that a meeting be held with the senior councillors (karō), the elders (toshiyori), the executive officials (yōnin), and the guard attendants — along with additional personnel as needed — to deliberate on the matter. It was reported that the Works Office (Fushin-kata) had already submitted a formal request regarding this. I reported that while the fish weir (yana) in question was designated for Lord Jōzaemon's use, there were precedents for gatherings of this kind, and (the lord) expressed concern about following such precedents. I was also asked about other possible weir locations, and I reported that all nearby sites had already been applied for. Thereupon, I reported that — if that were the case — one option might be to exclude Himochi Village and direct the water downstream from the Shimo Daimyōjin Pool toward the Koumi area. In response, (Lord Jōzaemon) instructed that lower-ranking officials be sent for an on-site inspection, to assess whether water rights could be secured and whether additional personnel such as role officers could be arranged. I was directed to convey this, and accordingly gave instructions to Kyūbei. I then offered my respects, stating that I would report back with a definitive answer the following morning, and (Lord Jōzaemon) departed.

Item: Today, a small promissory note (ko-tegata) was received from the Matsuda estate (■■), and I duly affixed my seal and sent it (on its way).

2nd Day (same or another entry for the 2nd)

Item: In the 5th month, during Kyūzaemon's turn as monthly duty officer, the tally for the rope-catch (o-tsuna-uchi) was reported with the actual count. However, when the written record (kakitsuke) was produced, it was found to be inconsistent with the report, and Lord Jūzō ordered that the matter be investigated and the document corrected and resubmitted. Accordingly, after consulting with the Provisions Office (Makanai-kata), it was reported that Kyūbei would submit the corrected report on his next official duty day. Upon repeated investigation, it was found that the count of "sako fish" (likely salmon or trout) showed a slight discrepancy, and this discrepancy was reported with regret.
