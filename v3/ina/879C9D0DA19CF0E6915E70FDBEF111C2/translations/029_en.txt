　　March 14th　　Kyūzaemon

14th day
One, Yoemon, a dyer (kōya) of Miroku village, who has until now operated four indigo vats, submitted a petition requesting to increase to five vats beginning this spring. The matter was forwarded to Jōzaemon-dono, and it was settled with instructions to acknowledge and approve the petition. Notice was accordingly sent to Heibei.

Same day
One, Tokuemon, a dyer of Yokkaichi-ba, finding himself in financial difficulty, wishes to sell all his tools and equipment to a man called Zenzaemon of Kurosawa village, after which Zenzaemon desires to operate the dyeing business in his place. A petition was submitted and forwarded to Jōzaemon-dono, who issued instructions to acknowledge and approve it as requested. Accordingly, a letter was sent to summon him and the above decision was conveyed.

One, Kitaya Yoemon, regarding the floating of timber down the river from Nakazawa mountain, has been coming monthly to obtain seal impressions on small vouchers. Since on months when we are off-duty we would still need to appear for official duty, a request was made to Heijizaemon-dono that the said seal impressions might instead be handled by those residing on duty. He acknowledged the full details and replied that he would convey the matter to the monthly duty officer.

15th day
One, a letter arrived from Zenhachi-dono. It stated that for the day after tomorrow, the 17th, two rope-braiders (tsunauchi) should be dispatched from early morning and instructed to return by around the hour of the Rooster in the afternoon (around 2 PM). The two men were accordingly notified.
