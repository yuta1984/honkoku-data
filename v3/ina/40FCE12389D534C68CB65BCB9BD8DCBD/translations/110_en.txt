Misawa Hijūrō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　《Later renamed Gohei》
1. On the 13th day of the 4th month of Bunka 4, year of the Fire Rabbit (1807), he was appointed to go-soku temporary service.
1. On the 18th day of the 5th month of Bunka 11, year of the Wood Dog (1814), he was appointed go-konando go-meshimono-kata.
1. On the 13th day of the 8th month of Bunka 12, year of the Wood Pig (1815), he was appointed acting go-zenban, with other duties continuing as before.
1. On the 8th day of the 10th month of Bunka 13, year of the Fire Rat (1816), he was appointed go-kinjū metsuke, with go-konando and other duties continuing as before.
1. On the 2nd day of the 6th month of Bunsei 1, year of the Earth Tiger (1818), he died of illness.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Fifth generation
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Tominaga Kihachirō
1. On the 15th day of the 5th month of Bunka 4, year of the Fire Rabbit (1807), he was appointed go-soku service from his concurrent positions as go-fushin bugyō and go-bugu bugyō go-soeban.

1. On the 20th day of the 8th month of Bunka 5, year of the Earth Dragon (1808), he was successfully dismissed from go-soku service, continued as oshiai-yaku as before, with his seat rank as jōza of go-kachi-gashira.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Third generation after inheriting the family name, actually fifth generation
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Okada Kujūku (Tsukumo)
1. On the 3rd day of the 10th month of Bunka 4, year of the Fire Rabbit (1807), he was appointed to serve the young lord from go-bangata.
1. On the 10th day of the 3rd month of Bunka 7, year of the Metal Horse (1810), he was granted the rank of go-tsukai-ban and appointed concurrently as provisional go-konando go-meshimono-kata for the young lord.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunka 13, year of the Fire Rat (1816), he was appointed oshiai-yaku.
