1. On the 25th day of the 10th month of Tenpō 8, Hinoto-Tori (1837), from ōmetsuke with concurrent go-bugu-bugyō, he was appointed go-zenban go-kinshū-metsuke go-konando concurrent duties. His rank position remains as before.

1. On the 23rd day of the 9th month of Kōka 2, Kinoto-Mi (1845), he was appointed go-soku-gashira and temporary go-soku-goyōnin while residing in the castle. Due to having many duties, he was satisfactorily relieved of go-mochizutsu-gashira and temporary ōmetsuke positions. However, his moto-shime role remains as before.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Second generation
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Okamura Gonshirō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　(marginal note: later Kanjūrō)

1. On the 15th day of the 9th month of Bunsei 5, Mizunoe-Uma (1822), he was appointed go-soku service from go-bangata employed service (marginal note: while stationed in Osaka).

1. On the 19th day of the 3rd month of Bunsei 8, Kinoto-Tori (1825), since Matabei of the same family name was appointed to domain domestic affairs, he should similarly relocate. Moreover, given the circumstances of his attachment and the regulations established in the domain, his employed service was satisfactorily terminated and he was granted go-gōryoku one-person stipend.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Fourth generation
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Tamura Ushizō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　(marginal note: later Yoichizaemon)

1. On the 10th day of the 10th month of Bunsei 6, Mizunoto-Hitsuji (1823), he was appointed retired lord's go-soku service from go-bangata.

1. On the 28th day of the 2nd month of Bunsei 9, Hinoe-Inu (1826), due to illness, as requested, he was relieved of retired lord's go-soku service and appointed to return to regular service.
