Fourth Generation
Fukaya Shigenosuke
　　　　　　　Later: Kakuemon

1. On the 13th day of the 9th month of Tenpō 4, Mizunoto-Mi (1833), he was appointed to go-soku service for the retired lord from go-bangata.

1. On the 20th day of the 10th month of the same 12th year, Kanoto-Ushi (1841), he was appointed go-konando go-meshimono-kata for His Lordship.

1. On the 1st day of the 8th month of the same 14th year, Mizunoto-U (1843), with ō-metsuke rank, he was appointed go-zenban and go-kinshū-metsuke for His Lordship. Go-konando metsuke-kata remained as before.

1. On the 13th day of the 10th month of Kōka 3, Hinoe-Uma (1846), he was appointed to concurrently serve as go-tsuki go-yōyaku and go-shimoyashiki go-torishimari for His Lordship. Go-zenban was temporarily suspended.

1. He died of illness on the 20th day of the 6th month of Kaei 1, Tsuchinoe-Saru (1848).

Son of Fifth Generation Sōbei
Masuda Kumatarō
　　　　　　　Later: Kogenji

1. On the 20th day of the 9th month of Tenpō 4, Mizunoto-Mi (1833), he was appointed go-soku service from go-tomogata go-chūkoshō go-yatoizutome.

1. On the 21st day of the 1st month of the same 11th year, Tsuchinoto-I (1840), as requested, he was exempted from go-soku service. Due to certain implications, his stipend was confiscated and he became stipend-less.

1. That same night, he fled.
