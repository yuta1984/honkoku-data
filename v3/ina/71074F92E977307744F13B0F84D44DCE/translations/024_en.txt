28th day
1. The woodcutter inspector Yosoji and Genji from Kashiwagi Village completed their survey of the mountain in Haruchika Township last night and returned. Both men reported to their lodgings, and the details were received and recorded.
　　Genji's travel days: 6 days, mountain inspector Miyata Kitawari [crossed out: Genbei (Kyusaemon)] [unclear] 2 days
　　They were sent back from the woodcutter hut, and from there the inner mountain routes were guided by the head woodcutter, as reported.

　　　　　May　To Lord Agusuke Saemon　　　　Tohei

1. Based on the mountain survey map of Kurokawa Mountain in Haruchika Township, after Yosoji and Genji returned, the mountain timber valuation and wood estimation documents were submitted, and today this was reported to the duty officer. Furthermore, we

　　will conduct our own inspection and make inquiries accordingly, as reported, and this was also discussed with the district magistrate's chief accounting office.
　　According to what the woodcutters reported, contrary to expectations, the various trees in the mountain are small trees that have regrown since the timber sales official cleared them in previous years. Moreover, regarding the cypress, there appears to have been excessive cutting, and this matter was reported.
