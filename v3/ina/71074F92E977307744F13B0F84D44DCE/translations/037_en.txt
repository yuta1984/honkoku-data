　　There was a conference with the district magistrate about wood types. For lower tools, Japanese hemlock, larch, and chestnut varieties would be used, and for handrails, pine would also be acceptable. Since these wooden tools would be expensive if obtained from distant places, plans were discussed to cut them from remote government forests, and this was also communicated to the mountain officials [...] [...]. Wood selection personnel were gradually sent to suitable locations. For the temporary bridge, pine was planned for all parts.

1. When subordinates of the mountain officials go out to their localities, it has been customary practice to go without notifying the rural districts about personnel, horses, and village provisions. However, from now on, for personnel and horses as well as village provisions, proper notification should be given to the rural district clerks each time. Lord Okamura Jūbei conveyed this message, and it was immediately communicated to Uemon's group to inform everyone, and also announced to the subordinates.
