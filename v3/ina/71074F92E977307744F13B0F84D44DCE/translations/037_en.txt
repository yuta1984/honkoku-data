　　There was a conference with the district magistrate, and regarding the types of wood, for the lower tools hemlock, larch, and chestnut types would be used, and for the handrails pine would be acceptable. Since these wooden tools would be expensive if obtained from distant places due to high transportation costs, plans were discussed to cut them from government forests as far away as possible, and this was communicated to the mountain officials, gradually sending people to suitable locations to select timber. For the temporary bridge, the plan is to use pine entirely.

1. When subordinates of the mountain officials went to outlying areas, it had been customary for them to go without notifying the rural districts regarding horses, men, or village provisions. However, Okamura Jūbei announced that henceforth, not only for horses and men but also for village provisions, notification should be given to the rural district scribes each time and reported. This was immediately communicated to Uemon's group to inform everyone, and also announced to the subordinates.

Hiranoya Gihei, Jiwataya Gihei, Onozawa village Zenzaemon, Kohaku, Masuyama, Tsuguyama and others have been submitting various petitions and reports, but henceforth they should petition only after thorough examination. Since these have already become upper mountains and regarding tribute mountains, they should be examined and harvested as thoroughly as possible. The mountain officials Kobayashi and Sugi Zaemon were instructed to communicate these points to the head supervisors.
