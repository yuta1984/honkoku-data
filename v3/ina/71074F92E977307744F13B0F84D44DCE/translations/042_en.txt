　　　The above concerns the allocation of disposal proceeds from Miyata Kurokawa mountain in Haruchika district. We humbly request to deliver acknowledgment according to the apportionment as stated above.
　　　　　　June 20th　　　　　Satomi Tōbei
　　
　　　The above two matters have been reported to the duty officer for consideration.
1. Regarding four larch trees for Benzaiten bridge timber for official use, since there are no imperial forests nearby and logging from distant locations would result in excessive costs, we will purchase from village forests and deliver to the district magistrate, which has been reported to the duty officer.
[Left page]
1. At Himochi mountain enclosed forest, Ryōzō climbed the mountain today to mark logged areas of official timber, then proceeded to Mizoguchi village and was ordered to handle matters there in the same manner.
1. Kitahara Giemon reported that delivery of flood control timber for Omoteki village was completed and he returned yesterday evening.
1. A circular notice came stating that from Fujisawa river entrance to Nakamura village, hunting is prohibited in the river during the lord's residence in the castle, and this was communicated to subordinates.
1. A circular notice came that since Lord Kametarō will privately enter this area, samurai should refrain from wearing tall geta and conduct themselves with restraint, and gate guards need not prostrate themselves but should appear in the lower reception room. This was also communicated to subordinates.
