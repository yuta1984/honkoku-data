　　　　　　　Memorandum
　　　One hundred ryō in gold
　　　　　Of which:　　30 ryō　to be paid at this time
　　　　　　　　　　　20 ryō　to be paid during autumn
　　　　　Remaining:
　　　　　　　　　　　50 ryō　in three annual installments from the Year of the Dog
　　　
　　　The above concerns the reallocation proportions for the disposal proceeds of Miyata Kurokawa Mountain in Haruchika district. We humbly request acknowledgment and delivery as detailed above.
　　　　　　June 20th　　　　　Satomi Tōbei
　　
　　　The above two matters have been reported to the duty officer for consideration.
1. Regarding four larch trees for Benzaiten bridge timber for official use, since there are no imperial forests nearby and logging from distant locations would
[Left page]
　　incur excessive costs, we have reported to the duty officer that we will purchase from village forests and deliver to the district magistrate.
1. Today Ryōzō climbed the restricted Himochi Mountain to mark the logging sites of official timber, then proceeded to Mizoguchi village with orders to handle matters in the same manner.
1. Received report that Kitahara Giemon completed the delivery of flood control timber for Omoteki village and returned yesterday evening.
1. A circular notice came stating that the river from Fujisawa River entrance to Nakamura is designated as a hunting prohibition zone while the lord is in residence at the castle. This was communicated to those under jurisdiction.
1. A circular notice came stating that since Lord Kametarō is privately visiting this area, the retainers' ceremonial dress is excused and they should conduct themselves with restraint, and that gate guards need not prostrate themselves but should appear in the lower reception room. This was also communicated to those under jurisdiction.
