In the autumn of Keichō 5, Hidehisa followed Tokugawa-dono and set out for Ōshū. When the capital region was again in turmoil and Chūnagon-dono (Tokugawa Hidetada) attacked up the mountain route, Hidehisa served under his command and returned from Shimotsuke Province. At this time, Sanada of Shinano joined the western forces and fortified himself in Ueda Castle, attempting to coordinate with allies from east and west. The daimyo of this province were all detained to fight against Sanada. Hidehisa also remained in his own Komoro Castle and did not participate in the western campaign.
