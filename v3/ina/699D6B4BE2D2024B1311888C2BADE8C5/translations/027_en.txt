【Left page】
　　　Tōyama
Kyūbei-no-jō Fujiwara Tomomasa was a descendant of Minister of the Left Uona of Kawabe and a descendant of Tōyama Kageasa, son of Katō-ji Kagenori, who was the eighth-generation grandson of Commanding General of the Defense of the North Toshihito. For generations his ancestors lived in Tōyama in Mino Province, and since the lineage divided into seven branches, they were called the "Seven Tōyama." Tomotsugu succeeded his forefathers at Naegi Castle, but later lost his domain and fled to Mikawa Province, where he served Lord Tokugawa. Suganuma Kodaizen was affiliated with the Ashikaga faction. 《marginal note: According to one document, since Tomotsugu initially belonged to Saitō Uhyōe-no-suke Tatsuyoshi, the guardian of Mino, he was destroyed by Nobunaga, but this is questionable. In Tenshō 10, Kiso of Shinano turned against the Takeda and allied with Naegi Kyūbei to communicate with Nobunaga, as seen in the Azuchi Nikki and Nobunaga-ki. Since Nobunaga was also killed that year, Kyūbei may have remained at Naegi until the end of Nobunaga's era. Another theory states that he lost his domain during Hideyoshi's time.
