　　　Kuwayama　　Attached: Iga-no-kami Naoharu
Shurinoshō Fujiwara Kazuharu is the eldest grandson of Jibukyo Hōin Sōei. A descendant in the twelfth generation from Yūki Shichirō Tomomitsu, the resident of Toki District in Mino Province, Shuridayū Sadahisa, served the Takauji Shōgun's side and had his original domain of Kuwayama-shō confirmed. {{marginal note: In the intercalary second month of Kannō 2, there was an official document from Takauji confirming his original domain, and also in Bunna 1, there is a letter sent to Toki Samanosuke stating that there should be no disturbances to Sadahisa's original domain}} {{marginal note: One theory says that Kuwayama was originally written as Kuwayama (鍬山)}}. The seventh-generation descendant of Sadahisa, Shuridayū {{marginal note: Korenori was at Kamiko Castle in Owari Province. His son Shuridayū}} Shigeharu, after becoming a Buddhist monk, took the name Sōei, and later was made Jibukyo Hōin. Sōei initially served the household from when Toyotomi Hideyoshi was still at Nagahama Castle in Gōshū, and when Hideyoshi and Shibata fought, he defended the strategic positions of the province.
