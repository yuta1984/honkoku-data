　　　Mizoguchi
Hōki-no-kami Minamoto Hidekatsu was the son of Mizoguchi Hikozaemon-no-jō Katsumasa, a resident of Owari Province. Long ago, Hemmi Matatarō Yoshishige was appointed to the position of jitō (estate steward) of Ōkuwa-no-gō in Mino Province for his military service in the Jōkyū War, and his descendants lived in that province for generations. Later they moved to Owari Province and lived in the area of Mizoguchi, from which they took the name Mizoguchi.
When Hidekatsu was still called Takemaru and was a child, he served Niwa Gorozaemon-no-jō Nagahide, and after his coming-of-age ceremony, he was called Kin'emon-no-jō. On the sixteenth day of the fourth month of Tenshō 9, he was unexpectedly summoned by Oda-dono and joined his retainers, and [was given] Takahama Castle in Wakasa Province
