His son Kunai-no-shō Sadataka, his eldest son Kunai-no-shō Kiyotaka, and second son Uma-no-jō Yoshitaka (who later became Ōsumi-no-kami). In Kiyotaka's time, the Seven Islands group sought reinforcement from Lord Kitabatake (namely, the Ise Provincial Governor) and attacked the Kuki castle of Tashiro. Because Kiyotaka was exceptionally brave in battle, the castle was never taken, but he died in the fighting. His son Yagoro Sumitaka, together with his uncle Yoshitaka, held up in Tashiro castle and fought defensively, but being outnumbered, they could not prevail and retreated to Mount Asama. Afterwards, there were battles repeatedly, and they recaptured Tashiro, but he died soon after, so Uma-no-jō Yoshitaka inherited the house and in Ise Province became

Eventually, Oda Kazusa-no-suke Nobunaga, seeking to subjugate Ise Province, departed from Owari Province in the autumn of Eiroku 12 (1569) and headed toward Ōkōchi Castle. Uma-no-jō Yoshitaka and others betrayed the provincial governor and sided with Oda. The Kitabatake forces were unsuccessful, and Gon-chūnagon Tomonori-kyō made peace with Nobunaga, adopting Oda's second son and transferring the house to him. What was called Kitabatake Zen-naidaijin Nobuo-kō was at this time known as Chasen Onzōshi. Thus Yoshitaka served under Nobunaga and achieved distinguished service many times.
