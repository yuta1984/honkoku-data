　　　Sengoku

Echizen-no-kami Minamoto Hidehisa was initially called Gonbee-no-jō. When Toyotomi Taikō was still known as Hashiba Echizen-no-kami and ruled Harima Province, in the winter of Tenshō 9, he received orders from Oda-dono to attack Awaji Province. Atagi Kawachi-no-kami surrendered, so there was no need to newly appoint a guardian for that province. In the summer of the same tenth year, Nobunaga passed away, and when Hideyoshi came to control the affairs of the realm, he granted Hidehisa Sumoto Castle in Awaji Province. This was in recognition of his many years of military service.
