Izu-no-kami Minamoto Yoshikatsu《Marginal note: his original name was Magozaemon》was the second son of Hōki-no-kami Hidekatsu. In Keichō 6, at the age of 18, he first had an audience with the Ōgosho, and in this year was granted court rank and appointed Izu-no-kami. In the same tenth-odd year, from the Shogun's house

he was granted domain in Kanra District, Kōzuke Province《Marginal note: 2,000 koku》. In the 15th year his father died, and his elder brother Hōki-no-kami Nobukatsu tried to divide and grant him domain, but Yoshikatsu declined and would not accept it. Nobukatsu finally reported this matter to the Shogun's house. By [the Shogun's] command, Yoshikatsu combined this with his own domain and ruled it《Marginal note: He was divided 12,000 koku, which combined with his original domain totaled 14,000 koku that he ruled》. In the campaigns before and after Osaka, he served as the Shogun's house's vanguard. His eldest son Tosa-no-kami Masakatsu received his father's inherited domain in Kan'ei 11《Marginal note: 10,000 koku ○ The year, month, and day of Yoshikatsu's death are not yet clear. Since it says the inherited domain was granted in Kan'ei 11, he probably died in this year》and divided the domain among his younger brothers《Marginal note: Second son Gonsuke Sukekatsu 3,000 koku, third son Kujūrō Naokatsu 1,000 koku》. Masakatsu died on the 28th day of the 1st month of Kanbun 11 at the age of 62. His son Iyo-no-kami Masatane succeeded him. His son Taitō was actually the second son of Katō Kuranosuke Akitomo.
