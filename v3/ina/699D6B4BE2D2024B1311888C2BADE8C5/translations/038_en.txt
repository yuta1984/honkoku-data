He was granted Takeda Castle in Tajima Province {{marginal note: 10,000 koku}}. Later, attached to his younger brother Dainagon Hidenaga, he distinguished himself in battles in various places and came to rule Wakayama Castle in Kii Province {{marginal note: 30,000 koku. It is said that the Hōin first built and resided in this castle}}. In Bunroku 4, when the incident involving Kanpaku Hidetsugu occurred, because he defended Fushimi Castle, Hideyoshi granted him additional new lands {{marginal note: 20,000 koku. These lands are said to be in Izumi Province}}. His heir Kurōgorō Kazushige died at Takeda Castle at the age of twenty-two. After the Hōin retired, since [Kazuharu] was the legitimate grandson, he transferred those domain lands to Kazushige's son Shurinosuke Kazuharu {{marginal note: Combining Takeda Castle in Tajima and lands in Kii Province, totaling 20,000 koku. One theory says 16,000 koku}}. He divided domain lands to his second son Iga-no-kami Naoharu {{marginal note: Said to be 10,000 koku. One theory also says 16,000 koku}}. After Shurinosuke Kazuharu inherited his grandfather's domain, he followed the army to Korea and, together with Katō, Tōdō and others, defended the guard ships of that country and was wounded. When the Kii Province uprising attacked Tanabe Castle, Kazuharu provided rear support and defeated the enemy. In the autumn of the same fifth year, when the Kamigata army rose up, together with his grandfather the Hōin, he sided with Sekigahara [Eastern Army] and defended Wakayama Castle. Receiving orders from Tokugawa-dono, he proceeded toward Shingū Castle and attacked and forced the surrender of Awa-no-kami inside the castle. In this year, the domains of his clan were moved to Yamato Province and he ruled Fuse in Katsuge District {{marginal note: Kazuharu 16,000 koku, Naoharu 25,000 koku, Sakon Sadaharu 3,000 koku, it is said}}. On the twenty-eighth day of the second month of the same ninth year, Kazuharu died at the age of thirty-three. His younger brother Saemonnnosuke Kazunao inherited the house. Kazunao had served Tokugawa-dono from the age of nineteen {{marginal note: His original name was Kuhachirō}}. At the Battle of Sekigahara, he charged ahead, took enemy heads, and received [the lord's] appreciation. In the eighth month of the year he inherited Kazuharu's position, he was granted court rank.
