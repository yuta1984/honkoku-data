《marginal note: This was probably around the time when Lord Nobutaka was killed. Since I have not seen his genealogy, the details are unclear. Although Naegi is mentioned in various places in the Kōyō Gunkan, it is not clear》In the autumn of Keichō 5 (1600), it was heard that Ishida and others had raised troops and would attack down the Tōsandō and Tōkaidō routes. Tomotsugu hurriedly rushed to his home province, gathered local people, and expressed his desire to attack and destroy enemy fortifications, open mountain roads, and welcome the forces of the Chūnagon. When his proposal was deemed admirable and permission was granted, he rushed up day and night and assembled the local people. Since he was a hereditary local lord, people flocked to him without even being summoned. Tomotsugu was greatly pleased and led his forces to attack and destroy enemy fortifications here and there. 《marginal note: The various records of Sekigahara describe different accounts. One theory states that at this time Naegi Castle was controlled by Kawajiri Higo-no-kami Naotsugu. Kawajiri was in Osaka at the time, with only a small garrison force remaining at the castle. Tōyama attacked and fought, finally capturing the castle. Another theory states that Naegi Castle was defended by Seki Gihei, and when the Chūnagon》
