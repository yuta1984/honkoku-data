The daimyo of Kyushu removed their helmets and unstrung their bows, surrendering in an endless stream, but Shimazu still did not surrender. On the twenty-third day of the fourth month of the same year, at the Battle of Mimikawa in Hyuga Province, Miyabe Hōin defeated Shimazu's forces and eagerly pursued the fleeing enemy to finish them off, but Bitō firmly restrained him, so Miyabe's army could not advance. When the Kanpaku heard of this, he was extremely displeased and said, "What cowardly behavior from Bitō! It would be one thing if he simply did not fight like Miyabe, but how could he fail to strike down the fleeing enemy? Take that man's province as well!" and confiscated Sanuki Province from him too. Shimazu finally could no longer continue fighting and surrendered, so Kyushu was
