He died at the age of eighty. When the Osaka campaigns arose, Kazunao rushed from Yamato Province and at Konda in Kawachi Province personally took the heads of two enemy riders and presented them. When war broke out again, at the Battle of Dōmyōji he cut seventeen heads, and on the day the castle fell he cut another 119 heads and presented them. In Kan'ei 10, he received orders to inspect both the Hokuriku and San'in circuits, and in the same thirteenth year, eighth month, he died at the age of fifty-nine. His son Shurinosuke Kazuyuki followed his father, retired on the twenty-third day of the eighth month of Enpō 6, and his heir Mimasaka-no-kami Kazutada inherited the house {{marginal note: 15,000 koku.
