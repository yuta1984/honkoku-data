Since this was the hereditary domain of his ancestors for generations, he petitioned through Kanamori Hōin to the Kantō authorities that if the Gujō district were granted to him, he would swiftly capture Hachiman Castle.《marginal note: Hōin's son Izumo-no-kami Yoshishige was Yoshitaka's son-in-law》Lord Tokugawa issued a written guarantee for the security of his domain and approved the assistance of Hōin's son Izumo-no-kami Yoshishige. Working day and night, they coordinated with Yoshitaka who was advancing rapidly, and finally captured Hachiman Castle. Since it was nearby, he first reported this to Lord Chūnagon's camp at Suwa, received a reply, and was rewarded for his swift achievement. Even Kohachirō Tanenao, who was Yoshitaka's clansman and son-in-law but had joined the western forces, was ultimately attacked and forced to surrender. Lord Tokugawa soon invaded this province and established his camp at Akasaka, so Yoshitaka rushed to the camp

During the Battle of Sekigahara, he is said to have secured the rear guard》After the Sekigahara campaign ended, following the terms of the written guarantee, he was granted Hachiman Castle and once again had his hereditary domain confirmed. 《marginal note: 25,000 koku》In the same ninth year, he was ennobled and made Tajima-no-kami. He rushed to the Osaka campaign, fought, and presented sixty-eight severed heads. Yoshitaka lived to the age of eighty-two and died on the twenty-first day of the third month of Kan'ei 9 (1632). His son Nagato-no-kami Yoshikatsu died in Genna 1 (1615) at age twenty-eight, predeceasing his father. Since the son of Miki Ukon-dayū Naotuna, who was the son of the former Hida Governor Anekōji Dainagon Yoritsuna, was Yoshitaka's grandson through his daughter, he was adopted to continue the family line. This was Tajima-no-kami Yoshitoshi. Yoshitoshi's son Bizen-no-kami Tsunesue, and Tsunesue's son Geki Tsuneharu was appointed Uemon-no-suke and awarded the court rank of Jugoi-no-ge (Junior Fifth Rank Lower).
