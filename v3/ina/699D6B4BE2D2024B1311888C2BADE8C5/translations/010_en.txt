Thus, when it was heard that the entire realm had submitted to the Kanpaku (Toyotomi Hideyoshi), Masayuki once again made contact with his retainer Otani Gyobu-shoyu Yoshitsugu and secretly requested to serve His Lordship (Hideyoshi). In the same year, Tensho 17 (1589), in the eleventh month, when Kagekatsu had an audience with the Kanpaku and went up to the capital again, Masayuki seized this opportunity to call back Yukimura and sent him to serve the Kanpaku's household. Kagekatsu was greatly angered and frequently petitioned that Genjiro Yukimura should definitely be returned to him, but when His Lordship (Hideyoshi) expressed his detailed concerns, Kagekatsu had no choice but to comply with his wishes. In this year, the Kanpaku's household transmitted an imperial command urging the Hojo father and son to come to court. Ujimasa and Ujinao stated the conditions they had previously agreed upon with Lord Tokugawa, hoping that they should be granted Numata Castle in Kozuke Province
