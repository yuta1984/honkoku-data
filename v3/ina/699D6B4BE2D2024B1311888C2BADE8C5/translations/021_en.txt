Needless to say, there was no instance of them not participating in battles in the Kinai and San'yo regions. In the same year's second month, when Sanmi Chunagon-dono (Oda Nobutada) invaded Kai Province, Nagachika led 3,000 men, departed from Echizen Province, and headed toward Kai Province. In the sixth month of this year, when Lord Oda and his son were killed, Nagachika's son Chujiro Naganori (aged nineteen) served Nobutada-kyo and committed seppuku at Nijo Palace. Before long, Shibata (Katsuie) and Hashiba (Hideyoshi) became hostile, and when war was about to break out, Nagachika, together with the Maeda and Fuwa clans, mediated between the two houses. In the following eleventh year's second month, Katsuie and Hideyoshi's forces finally clashed and fought at Shizugatake in Omi Province. The people of the Hokuriku region

In the same fourteenth year, they were granted Hida Province and resided in Takayama. This was a reward for their years of military merit. Thereafter, Nagachika and his son never failed to participate in campaigns east and west.
