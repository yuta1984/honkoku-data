He boarded many small boats, pushed out from Ise Bay, reached Kumano Bay in Kii Province where he engaged in naval battle with the Saiga people, captured over thirty warships, reached Sakai Bay in Izumi Province, and blocked the sea routes used by forces from Shikoku and Kyushu traveling to and from Osaka. On the 6th day of the 11th month of the same year, over 600 warships from the western provinces approached Kizu Bay. Kuki assembled his warships and sailed out to meet them. Allied forces positioned on land lined the water's edge densely to watch. In the initial engagement, Kuki appeared to be losing and was pushed back. After Nobunaga's death, he betrayed the Kitabatake and became Hideyoshi's ally, took Kanie Castle in Ise, and attacked Ono Castle, but was defeated by Lord Tokugawa's forces and retreated. When the Kanpaku attacked Shikoku and Kyushu and invaded Korea, he consistently served as the naval commander.
