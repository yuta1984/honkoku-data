1. On the 25th day of the 3rd month of the same 12th year, Kanoe-saru (1800), he was appointed to the position of inspector of mountain areas, forest areas, and bamboo groves (yamakata-hayashikata-takeyabu ginmi-yaku).

1. On the 3rd day of the 4th month of Kyōwa 3, Mizunoto-i (1803), permission was granted to adopt Kōkichi, the adopted son of Sakai Yahei, a masterless samurai of Lord Wakisaka Awaji-no-kami from Tera village.

1. On the 5th day of the 5th month of the same year, due to a longstanding private request, he was granted a change of residence to a vacant official residence on the east side of Fukuro-machi.

1. On the 8th day of the 11th month of Bunka 4, Hinoto-u (1807), he was granted the rank of gokūban-kaku and appointed as head of the firewood department (makikata tōdori). Everything else remained as before.

1. On the 20th day of the 2nd month of the same 5th year, Tsuchinoe-tatsu (1808), he was relieved of his position as head of the firewood department due to administrative reforms (go-shuhō go-kirikae).

1. On the 28th day of the 6th month of the same year, he was appointed as head of attendants and baggage supervisor (chūgen gashira nimotsu sewa-yaku).

1. On the 25th day of the 3rd month of the same 7th year, Kanoe-uma (1810), due to illness, he was relieved of both positions as requested and appointed to alternate duty (go-jiban).

1. On the 28th day of the 5th month of the same 12th year, Kinotono-i (1815), he died of illness.

1. On the 20th day of the 7th month of the same year, his son Gensuke was appointed to official position.

Service record

Kitahara Gensuke

1. On the 20th day of the 5th month of Bunka 6, Tsuchinoto-mi (1809), he was appointed as assistant priest (bōzu jokin).

1. On the 15th day of the 2nd month of the same 7th year, Kanoe-uma (1810), he was assigned to inner priest employment (oku-bōzu yatoi-kin) and granted relief stipend of 1.5 persons' rations.

1. On the 13th day of the 10th month of the same 8th year, Kanotono-hitsuji (1811), he received an increase of half a ration and was appointed to employment as attendant foot soldier (gokūkata go-ashigaru go-yatoi-kin).

1. On the 27th day of the 7th month of the same 12th year, Kinotono-i (1815), he inherited his late father Giuemon's position with a stipend of 18 hyō and 2 persons' rations, and was appointed as attendant foot soldier.

1. On the 5th day of the 7th month of the same 13th year, Hinoe-ne (1816), he was appointed to the young lord's attendant corps, specifically for Edo duty.

1. On the 15th day of the 10th month of the same 14th year, Hinoto-ushi (1817), as he was suffering from an incurable illness with little prospect of recovery, permission was granted as requested to adopt Tashichi, second son of Yoneyama Kanuemon.

1. On the 15th day of the 12th month of the same year, due to illness, he was granted retirement as requested.
