Ina Ichi

During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- Tenpō 2 (1831), 5th month, 25th day: Newly appointed with the rank of tea ceremony assistant, granted a stipend of 16 hyō and provisions for two retainers, assigned to Edo service

- Kōka 2 (1845), 2nd month, 3rd day: Promoted to second rank of attendant guard format, granted an increase of 2 hyō

- Same 4th year (1847), 11th month, 20th day: Due to good understanding and diligent practice of acupuncture treatment, promoted to regular rank of attendant guard, granted an increase of 2 hyō

- Kaei 4 (1851), 1st month, 15th day: Granted an increase of 2 hyō

- Same year, 6th month, 13th day: Appointed as attendant guard upon the lord's return to the castle

- Same 7th year (1854), 8th month, 13th day: Due to illness, by special compassion sent to his home domain, to move with dependents as preparations are completed. Arrived at home domain on the 24th of the same month

- Same day: Due to prolonged illness, by divine mercy assigned to home domain service, but as there is further compassionate consideration upon recovery from illness, temporarily returned to ancestral home for thorough convalescence. Subsequently...
