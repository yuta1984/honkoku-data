- Kaei 1 (1848), May 24th: Appointed to Edo residence duty,
　　and appointed as temporary ashigaru inspector during residence duty
- Same 2nd year (1849), October 13th: Appointed as temporary inspector for
　　firewood, mountain affairs, forest affairs, and bamboo grove inspection
- Same 3rd year (1850), September 9th: Appointed to Edo residence duty,
　　and appointed as temporary ashigaru inspector during residence duty
- Same year, October 1st: On this occasion,
　　appointed to intensive escort duty for the young lord's departure,
　　and therefore granted money as special allowance
- Same 4th year (1851), December 1st: Appointed as ashigaru inspector
- Ansei 2 (1855), July 3rd: Granted status of escort attendant rank, appointed as
　　ashigaru squad leader and personal guard commander, granted increase of 2 hyō, total 20 hyō and provisions for two retainers
- Same year, October 2nd: Died in fire　　This night after 4 o'clock, great earthquake, fires broke out everywhere,
　　　　　　　　　　　　the mansion suffered fire damage, residence quarters and barracks
　　　　　　　　　　　　collapsed, particularly at the Edo residence post
　　　　　Service Record
　　　　　　　Sato Aizaburō
- Ansei 2 (1855), November 18th: Regarding Sato Sōbei's death during the Edo earthquake,

　　through divine mercy the petition of his relatives was heard and granted, and although still
　　of young age, he was granted succession to the family name, awarded 18 hyō and provisions for two retainers,
　　with status of lowest-ranking ashigaru, to be assigned duties upon maturity, for the time being
　　appointed to reserve child attendant duty under the supervision of the chief retainer
