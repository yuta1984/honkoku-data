- 13th day, 4th month, Kaei 3 (1850): Loaned to Lord Naitō Awanokami and appointed to accompany him to Nagasaki, arriving in Edo on the 25th day of the same month.

- 1st day, 5th month, same year: Appointed to the position of junior administrator under the page rank in Lord Awanokami's household accounting department.

- 15th day, 12th month, Kaei 4 (1851): While on loan to the clan relative Lord Awanokami, he accompanied him even to distant provinces and performed his official duties well, earning good reputation. Therefore, he was granted the formal rank of escort attendant, appointed to oversee both catering and cooking departments, granted an increase of 3 hyō for a total stipend of 20 hyō with provisions for two people, and placed under the supervision of the chief retainer.

- 11th day, 11th month, Kaei 5 (1852): Appointed to Edo garrison duty upon arrival. During garrison duty, appointed to attend the young lords' quarters and instructed to also understand the duties of meal service assistance.

- 23rd day, 3rd month, Kaei 6 (1853): During garrison duty, appointed to handle catering for the lord's wife, gate security duty, and cooking department duties concurrently.

- 8th day, 3rd month, Kaei 7 (1854): Released from garrison duty.

- 3rd day, 7th month, Ansei 2 (1855): Appointed to Edo garrison duty.

- 13th day, 7th month, same year: During garrison duty, instructed to understand the duties of attending the rest quarters for meals and handling affairs for the young lords.

- 10th day, 4th month, Ansei 3 (1856): Released from garrison duty.
