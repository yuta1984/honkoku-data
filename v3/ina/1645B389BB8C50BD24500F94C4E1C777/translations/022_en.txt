During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- Bunka 2 (1805), July 15th: Appointed as assistant monk
- Same 4th year (1807), August 6th: There was a matter reported regarding his father Tadagoemon,
　　and release from temporary employment was announced
- Same 6th year (1809), September 29th: His father Tadagoemon had previously been placed
　　under the custody of Kise Sogo regarding the shortage of funds in remittance service,
　　but as his conduct was proper, out of further compassion his son Keisuke was granted
　　a stipend of 4 koku and provisions for two retainers in the advance guard unit

- Same 11th year (1814), January 15th: Appointed as official monk with 1 koku additional rice
- Same year, June 25th: Due to administrative arrangements,
　　temporarily appointed as assistant personal attendant monk to Lord ◯nosuke
- Same 12th year (1815), June 15th: Ordered to serve as official monk
- Same 13th year (1816), May 3rd: Promoted to ashigaru with a stipend of 18 hyō and provisions
　　for two retainers, appointed to attendant service
During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- Bunpō 4 (1821), July 13th: Appointed to rest chamber duty
　　under the supervision of the chamberlain
- Same 7th year (1824), February 20th: Appointed to palace duty for Lord Ryōnoshin,
　　granted formal rank as attendant guard
- Same 8th year (1825), June 15th: Due to Lord Ryōnoshin's change in administrative method,
　　the title of palace duty was discontinued, appointed to residence duty with morning and evening
　　attendance, managing clothing and various detailed duties, continuing as before
- Same 12th year (1829), March 1st: Granted attendant guard seniority ranking with 2 hyō
　　increase, duties continuing as before
- Same year, September 23rd: Appointed to handle rest chamber duty provisions and other
　　expense matters, senior Edo resident duty
