Permanent Transfer Document for House and Premises

Article 1: Located on the north side of Sagari-machi, as recorded in the official survey of Shotoku 4, Year of the Metal Horse (1714), extending eastward from the Honmachi boundary, with a frontage of 7 ken and 5 shaku, along the existing lateral road and with a depth of 31 ken extending to the large boundary, including all standing trees without exception, are hereby permanently transferred to you. As compensation for this transfer, I have received 70 ryo in new gold, which I acknowledge in truth and clarity.

Article 1: Regarding official duties for the government, please carry them out within the town. Concerning this house and premises, neither relatives nor any outside parties will interfere or obstruct, extending to all descendants for generations to come. Should any troublesome matters arise, we will personally appear anywhere necessary with witnesses present to resolve the matter promptly, ensuring no inconvenience to you, with no deviation whatsoever from these established terms. This transfer document is hereby executed for future reference.
