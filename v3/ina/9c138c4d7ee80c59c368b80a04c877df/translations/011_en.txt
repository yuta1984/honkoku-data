Regarding the Permanent Transfer of House and Property

1. Within the northern side of Sagarimachi, according to the official survey of Shōtoku 4 (1714), Year of the Horse, from the border of Honmachi eastward, with a frontage of seven ken and two shaku five sun, including the existing lateral street and extending thirty-one ken to the rear boundary, including all standing trees without exception, I hereby permanently transfer this property to you. As payment for this, I have certainly received seventy ryō in new gold. This is true and clear.

1. Official duties for the government may be performed in the town. Regarding this house and property, not only relatives but any outsiders who might raise objections or cause interference shall not exist through generations of children and grandchildren. Should any difficult problems arise, we shall go anywhere necessary with witnesses present to resolve matters promptly, causing you no trouble, and there shall be no deviation whatsoever from what has been agreed upon. This transfer document is created for future reference. Therefore, it is as stated herein.

Kyōhō 15 (1730), Year of the Dog, 7th month
