While we were discussing various matters within the neighborhood, time passed and the situation dragged on indefinitely. Afterward, there were occasionally people who brought up the matter of these duties, but since they were townspeople, everyone expressed their own opinions and disliked disputes, so the matter was neglected.

Then, about eight or nine years ago, he applied to remove the large doors of the rental property and renovate it into a display room (mise-zashiki). We told Heijūrō that he should fulfill his neighborhood duties, but again on that occasion he would not comply. Therefore, we respectfully submitted a petition, and the town officials promptly investigated the matter. They ordered that the various duties for the rental property must definitely be performed and immediately conveyed this to Heijūrō, but he requested a three-day extension.

[Left page]
During that time, we heard that he summoned a man named Genbei from Akasugi village and made various arrangements with him. Moreover, there were rumors that he would make those from the neighborhood who had brought up these duty matters guilty of false accusations, and we were also told by the town officials that the lord's disposition had changed in the meantime. Therefore, we had no choice but to let the matter rest as it was.

However, this spring, everyone in the neighborhood petitioned that town duties should be performed uniformly by proper personnel according to the religious registry (goshūchō). We negotiated with Heijūrō about the rental property duties, but he completely refused to consider it.
