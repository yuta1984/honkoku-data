Duties of the Seat Magistrates (Gozaseki Bugyō) during formal meals for the daimyo lords (kunimochi-shū) at the White Shoin

1. After the third performance of Noh is completed and the ceremonies of koshiashi (ceremonial items) and hirobuta (ceremonial covers) are finished, three officials confer and proceed to the White Shoin. Soon a squad leader (bantō) arrives. When the formal meal is served, four officials enter together into the designated viewing room's veranda and sit in formation as illustrated.

1. When the formal meal service is completed, the elder retainers come forward to offer greetings. After this, the lord makes his rounds, and the four seat magistrates together go to the designated location as shown in the diagram to relax, declare "we humbly partake," and then return to their original positions.

1. The second round of sake is served, with the elder retainers performing their duties.

1. When the formal meal for all the daimyo lords is completely finished, everyone comes forward before the seat magistrates to express gratitude, saying "we are grateful for having partaken of the meal," and then withdraw from their seats.

1. When appointed as seat magistrates, regarding the matter of partaking of the meal early, this should be communicated in advance to the inspector (metsuke) in charge of the White Shoin.
