I write this urgent letter to you. In this season of autumn chill, I trust that you are all together in good health and faithfully performing your duties, which I find most gratifying. We here are also doing well without incident. Though this may be presumptuous, I hope you will consider this matter favorably. Now, regarding the matter of the visiting Yugyō Shōnin, as it draws ever nearer, I imagine you will be quite busy with various preparations. The other day, Mr. Yazawa went out to Matsumoto, and Seiemon also went there and had a leisurely conversation. On that occasion there was discussion, and I have learned that this time our lord has presented ten bags of white rice to the Yugyō Shōnin as provisions for meals. However, is this arrangement made at a rate of one-third contribution? Regarding matters between your area and our locality, we would like to handle everything in the same manner. I am sending detailed information written on a separate sheet, so please review it. Also, I understand that there were some inquiries regarding the Kanō area previously.
