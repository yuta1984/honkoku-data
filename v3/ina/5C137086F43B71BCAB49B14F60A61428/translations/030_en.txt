- Kōka 2, Year of the Wood Snake (1845), 9th month, 9th day: Granted seniority among ashigaru foot soldiers, appointed as monk inspector, continuing culinary duties as before, with an increase of one hyō of rice
- Same 4th year, Year of the Fire Goat (1847), 8th month, 18th day: Appointed as assistant for Lord Ryōnoshin's palace attendance, appointed to prepare morning and evening meals to be served, appointed as temporary inspector of firewood, mountain affairs, forestry, and bamboo groves, granted one koku of service rice on the same day
- Same year, 9th month, 18th day: Due to reorganization, temporarily appointed as assistant for rest quarters attendance, Lord Ryōnoshin's palace attendance and other duties continue as before
- Same 5th year, Year of the Earth Monkey (1848), 1st month, 10th day: Edo residence duty for rest quarters meals and other necessary arrangements, concurrently appointed to attend Lords Kōkichi and Osada
- Kaei 2, Year of the Earth Rooster (1849), 2nd month, 28th day: Due to the heavy workload of Lord Ryōnoshin's palace attendance, relieved without delay from temporary duties as inspector of firewood, mountain affairs, forestry, and bamboo groves
- Same year, 10th month, 13th day: Appointed to rest quarters attendance while in residence at the castle, Lord Ryōnoshin's palace attendance continues as before
- Same 4th year, Year of the Metal Boar (1851), 10th month, 13th day: While serving rest quarters attendance during castle residence, also appointed to handle Lord Kōkichi's affairs
- Same 6th year, Year of the Water Ox (1853), 3rd month, 8th day: Appointed to Edo residence duty
- Same year, 4th month, 1st day: During residence duty, appointed to culinary service for the Young Lady, ordered to also handle rest quarters assistant duties when needed, specifically for Edo residence
