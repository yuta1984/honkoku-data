Hara Yozō

During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- 1st month, 25th day, Kansei 9 (1797): Succeeded Hara Sadashichi in his position
- 4th month, 5th day, Bunka 10 (1813): Appointed as inspector (metsuke) in Naitō Heigorō's group
  Three to of additional rice allowance
- 1st month, 15th day, Bunka 11 (1814): Appointed as subordinate official in the armory department
- 8th month, 28th day, Bunka 12 (1815): Appointed as junior inspector, three to of additional rice allowance

During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- 1st month, 15th day, Bunpō 9 (1826): Granted 3 kan-mon of copper coins for diligent service over many years
- 7th month, 20th day, Bunpō 11 (1828): Three to additional rice allowance and appointed as clerk-equivalent, exempted from horse duty
- 1st month, 15th day, Tenpō 11 (1840): Promoted to the rank of foot soldier (ashigaru), granted 17 hyō rice stipend and provisions for two people, assigned to guard duty rotation under the supervision of chamberlain
- 9th month, 3rd day, Ansei 3 (1856): Due to old age and illness, granted retirement as requested, his son Yoshizō succeeded with 5 koku rice stipend and provisions for two people, appointed as head temple servant

(Red ink notation) "Hara"
