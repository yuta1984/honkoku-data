Regarding your inquiry about those who practice this, I respectfully submit the following. Concerning those who play the shakuhachi, these are individuals who have long served the sect, and even after spending years returning to serve former masters, when this proves impossible and they enter the sect together with their parents, wives, and children, they rely on relatives and continue to practice as komusō (wandering Zen monks). However, as it is difficult to maintain the practice of relying on others for extended periods, they may petition the fukiai-sho (playing assembly) to return to lay life for such unavoidable reasons, particularly when they need to support wives and children, and we grant such requests. Also, when someone's health is poor and they find it difficult to fulfill sectarian duties, and they petition the fukiai-sho or wish to make shakuhachi crafting their livelihood, since this is also a means of sustaining life, both temples acknowledge and approve such requests. Also, playing flutes in accompaniment with shamisen, kokyū, and other instruments is not part of our sectarian regulations, and even among junior members, though such things should not occur, when individuals privately use their skills to accompany shamisen or kokyū, if we do not see or hear of it, we have no means to investigate. However, when we do learn of such matters, we immediately investigate and handle them according to temple law. It should be noted that we have strictly prohibited the creation of new musical pieces outside of our regulations.
