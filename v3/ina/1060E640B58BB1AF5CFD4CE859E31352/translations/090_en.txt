Koizumi Jihei
During the reign of Raiji-kun
- Bunka 11, Year of the Dog (1814), 5th month, 4th day: His elder brother Watōji had previously become deranged and his line was severed, but now the family name of their father Wasuke was restored. He was granted the rank of personal guard (goryōban) with 20 hyō and support for two people, appointed to personal attendance service, and assigned to the domestic affairs of the lower residence.

- Same 13th year, Year of the Rat (1816), 7th month, 5th day: Appointed to the young lord's personal attendance.

- Bunsei 2, Year of the Rabbit (1819), 4th month, 28th day: Appointed concurrently as catering officer, gate guard, and kitchen officer for the new lady, and thereby assigned to the domestic affairs of the upper residence.

During the reign of Raiyasu-kun
- Same 7th year, Year of the Monkey (1824), 6th month, 28th day: Due to eye disease, he was relieved of his duties as catering officer, gate guard, and kitchen officer for the inner quarters as requested, and appointed to general service duties.

- Same 9th year, Year of the Dog (1826), 3rd month, 1st day: Due to prolonged eye disease making attendance difficult, he was granted retirement as requested, and his son Kichijirō was designated to inherit the family headship.
　　End of service

Koizumi Katsurokū
　　Original name: Kichijirō
- Bunsei 3, Year of the Dragon (1820), 1st month, 15th day: Appointed to serve as hired monk, and thereby granted assistance in the form of support for two people.

During the reign of Raiyasu-kun
- Same 6th year, Year of the Sheep (1823), 9th month, 1st day: Appointed as hired foot soldier, and was to serve both as inner monk and messenger.

- Same 9th year, Year of the Dog (1826), 3rd month, 1st day: His namesake Jihei was granted retirement due to prolonged eye disease as requested, and he inherited the family headship with 18 hyō and support for two people, appointed as foot soldier in personal attendance. Other duties remained the same, but he came under the supervision of the foot soldier commander.

- Same year, 7th month, 18th day: Appointed to lower seat attendance, continuing as inner monk with one koku of duty rice. He came under the supervision of the chamberlain.

- Same 13th year, Year of the Tiger (1829), 4th month, 10th day: Appointed as apprentice to the kitchen staff for butchering duties.
