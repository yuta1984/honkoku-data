Service Record

Tanaka Gisuke
　Jirōtayū
　Jōemon

During the reign of Lord Genryū-in
- On October 1st of Hōreki 4, year of Inu (1754), he was summoned as an escort foot soldier and received 16 hyō and rations for 2 people
- On December 11th of Hōreki 5, year of I (1755), he inherited his deceased father Jirōtayū's position as escort guard with 22 hyō and rations for 2 people in the escort department
- On March 29th of Hōreki 8, year of Tora (1758), he was appointed as assistant clerk
- On May 4th of Hōreki 14, year of Saru (1764), he was appointed to the permanent clerk position
- On August 11th of Meiwa 4, year of I (1767), he was appointed to the tax collection department and received an increase of 2 hyō
- On April 7th of Meiwa 5, year of Ne (1768), he was appointed to concurrent clerk duties
- On August 28th of Meiwa 8, year of U (1771), he was formally appointed as middle page inspector with 26 hyō and rations for 3 people
- On August 9th of An'ei 4, year of Hitsuji (1775), he was appointed as deputy administrator and received role stipend of rations for 2 people {{Marginal note: On April 10th of Meiwa 9, year of Mizunoe-Tatsu (1772), his request to marry his sister to Tominaga Matazaemon was approved}}

During the reign of Lord Honryū-in
- On November 11th of Tenmei 5, year of Mi (1785), he received an increase of 4 hyō and was appointed middle page inspector while concurrently serving as deputy administrator
- On February 23rd of Tenmei 7, year of Hitsuji (1787), having fulfilled his financial duties without delay, he was dismissed and received monetary reward
- On March 4th of Kansei 1, year of Tori (1789), his role was dismissed, he returned to middle page duties, and his 4 hyō increase was revoked

During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- On May 20th of Kansei 9, year of Mi (1797), he was appointed to the accounting department
- On November 28th of Kansei 11, year of Hitsuji (1799), he was appointed deputy administrator under district magistrate supervision
- On August 20th of Bunka 2, year of Ushi (1805), having fulfilled his deputy administrator role without delay, he was dismissed, maintained his current rank with rotating duty, and was placed under chief retainer supervision
- On March 8th of Bunka 3, year of Tora (1806), he was appointed temporary head of servants for cargo management
- On August 15th of the same year, he was appointed middle page inspector with an increase of 2 hyō and temporarily oversaw the servants' cargo department
- On January 15th of Bunka 6, year of Mi (1809), having served for several decades, he received an increase of 2 hyō and was appointed to assist with stipend-receiving retainer guard duties
