Yasuda Denzaemon

During the reign of Lord Shinnyoin
- On March 9th of Kyōhō 12, year of Hinoto-Hitsuji (1727), he was ordered to return to service, appointed to the rank of ashigaru-kaku (foot soldier rank) and uma-yaku (horse keeper), and granted a stipend of 4 ryō in gold and rations for 2 people
- On April 6th of the same year, he received an additional grant of 1 ryō in gold and was elevated to otomo-ban-kaku (attendant guard rank)

During the reign of Lord Genryūin
- On December 28th of Genbun 1, year of Hinoe-Tatsu (1736), he was made a chū-koshō (middle page), received an additional grant of 1 ryō and rations for 1 person, bringing his total to 6 ryō in gold and rations for 3 people
- On December 26th of Genbun 5, year of Kanoe-Saru (1740), he received an increase of 3 hyō of rice, bringing his total to 33 hyō and rations for 3 people
- On January 18th of Kanpō 2, year of Mizunoe-Inu (1742), he was appointed to chū-koshō-metsuke-kaku (middle page inspector rank)
- On July 11th of Enkyō 2, year of Kinoto-Ushi (1745), he was appointed as chū-koshō-metsuke (middle page inspector), concurrently served as uma-yaku (horse keeper), and received an increase of 3 hyō, bringing his total to 36 hyō
- On November 1st of Enkyō 4, year of Hinoto-U (1747), he was promoted to kyūnin (stipend recipient) status and received an increase of 4 hyō
"(Red ink notation) Yasuda"
