Family succession
　　　　　　　　　Foster brother under the care of Iida Ichitayū
　　　　　　　　　Takuma
　　　　　　　　　 Kanzaki Tōgo

During the reign of Raiin-kun:
1. On the 2nd day of the 7th month of Kansei 12, Monkey (1800), he was appointed as assistant to the horse training department, granted support allowance for two retainers, and instructed to understand his position as equivalent to the lord's attendant rank.
1. On the 20th day of the 10th month of Bunka 2, Ox (1805), when Kanzaki Samanosuke was punished and his family line was discontinued, the family name was reestablished and he was appointed to inherit it, with a stipend of 22 hyō and support for two retainers, continuing his duties as lord's attendant as before.
1. On the 9th day of the 5th month of Bunka 5, Dragon (1808), he was appointed to the lord's attendant corps, continuing his assistance with horse training as before.
1. On the 23rd day of the 3rd month of Bunka 7, Horse (1810), regarding the matter of the servant Genuchi borrowing money through an intermediary, he was reprimanded for negligent conduct and ordered to exercise strict caution as requested.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunka 11, Dog (1814), he was granted the formal rank of middle page, appointed to the lord's attendant corps, given an increase of 2 hyō and support for one retainer, with other conditions remaining as before.

1. On the 5th day of the 7th month of Bunka 13, Rat (1816), he was appointed as attendant to the young lord.
1. On the 18th day of the 1st month of Bunka 14, Ox (1817), he was appointed to horse duty, given an increase of 2 hyō, bringing his total to 26 hyō with support for two retainers.
1. On the 25th day of the 7th month of Bunsei 6, Sheep (1823), he died of illness.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Kanzaki Bunzaemon

During the reign of Raiei-kun:
1. On the 25th day of the 1st month of Bunsei 4, Snake (1821), he was appointed as employed clerk assistant and scribe apprentice, also able to serve as messenger, and accordingly granted support allowance for two retainers.
1. On the last day of the 9th month of Bunsei 6, Sheep (1823), he inherited his late father Tōgo's position with a stipend of 22 hyō and support for two retainers, continuing his formal duties as lord's attendant as before.
1. On the 10th day of the 9th month of Bunsei 8, Rooster (1825), he was appointed to full clerk duties with formal rank (continues below).
