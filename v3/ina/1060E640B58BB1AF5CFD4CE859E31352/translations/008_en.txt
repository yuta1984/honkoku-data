Sanpei Shigakura
- In Tenpō 6, Year of the Sheep (1835), on the 7th day of the 7th month, he had his first formal audience
- In the 7th year, Year of the Monkey (1836), on the 20th day of the 4th month, he was appointed as a temporary middle page in the escort service and granted a support allowance for 3 persons
- In the 10th year, Year of the Boar (1839), on the 20th day of the 10th month, due to illness, his temporary employment was terminated and he was permitted to surrender his allowance and retire as requested


　　　　　　　　　Second Son
　　　　　　　　　 Sanpei Tansaburō
Reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- In Tenpō 11, Year of the Rat (1840), on the 27th day of the 3rd month, he was appointed to temporary service in escort and personal attendant duties, and granted a support allowance for 2 persons. His rank was set as second seat among escort guards
- On the 23rd day of the 7th month of the same year, since his father Moriemon's son Shigakura was serving, it was announced as requested that the second son Torajirō would become the heir

- On the 25th day of the same month of the same year, he was appointed to temporary middle page duty in escort service, and accordingly received an increase of one person's allowance, being granted a support allowance for 3 persons in total
- In the 12th year, Year of the Ox (1841), on the 20th day of the 10th month, he was appointed to temporary personal attendant service to the Retired Lord
- In the 13th year, Year of the Tiger (1842), on the 25th day of the 8th month, his father Moriemon's stipend was confiscated and house arrest was imposed. When the family name faced extinction, his grandfather Seiemon's lineage was restored, he was granted 40 hyō and allowance for 3 persons, and appointed to guard duty according to stipend rank. Due to his father's disgrace, he was also sentenced to house confinement
- On the 26th day of the 9th month of the same year, his house confinement was lifted
- On the 25th day of the 10th month of the same year, he was appointed to guard duty in the formal audience hall
- In the 14th year, Year of the Rabbit (1843), on the 3rd day of the 8th month, he was appointed to personal attendant service to the Retired Lord
- In the 15th year, Year of the Dragon (1844), on the 15th day of the 10th month, due to staff shortage in the formal service, his personal attendant duties to the Retired Lord were smoothly terminated and he was ordered to return to regular rotation
