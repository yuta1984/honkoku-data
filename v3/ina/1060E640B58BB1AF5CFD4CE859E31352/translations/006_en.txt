- In the 8th year, Year of the Sheep (1811), on the 8th day of the intercalary 2nd month, he was appointed to serve concurrently as Chief Inspector (ōmetsuke) and Head Supervisor (motojime)
- In the 11th year, Year of the Dog (1814), on the 7th day of the 7th month, he was appointed as Assistant Caretaker (rusui soezaku), with his other official duties remaining as before
- In the 13th year, Year of the Rat (1816), on the 5th day of the 7th month, he was appointed as attendant (kaisoe) for the young lord's outings
- In the 14th year, Year of the Ox (1817), on the 28th day of the 8th month, he received an increase of 10 koku, making his stipend 70 koku and 3 person rice allowance, with his duties remaining as before
- In Bunsei 1, Year of the Tiger (1818), on the 8th day of the 6th month, due to the heavy workload of both official positions, he was successfully relieved of his Assistant Caretaker role
- In the 2nd year, Year of the Rabbit (1819), on the 15th day of the 1st month, he was appointed to the rank order of ashigaru commander (monotō), serving above Fukaya Chūzaemon

Reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- In the 3rd year, Year of the Dragon (1820), on the 8th day of the 12th month, due to the heavy workload of his main duties, he was exempted from monthly rotation duties as Chief Inspector. However, he was to continue with regular duties and, when necessary, monthly rotation duties as discussed and arranged
- In the 6th year, Year of the Sheep (1823), on the 5th day of the 6th month, having been unable to serve for a long time due to illness, he submitted a petition requesting relief from both official positions, but this was rejected
- On the 23rd day of the 12th month of the same year, he submitted various petitions requesting relief from official duties due to illness. His Chief Inspector position was granted relief without issue, but his Head Supervisor position was again rejected
- In the 7th year, Year of the Monkey (1824), on the 3rd day of the 6th month, due to administrative reforms in Edo, he was relieved of his Head Supervisor role without issue, his rank status remained as before, and he was appointed as an intermediary (otoritsugi). For his diligent service as Head Supervisor for several years, he was awarded a silk garment
- On the 4th day of the same month and year, he died of illness
