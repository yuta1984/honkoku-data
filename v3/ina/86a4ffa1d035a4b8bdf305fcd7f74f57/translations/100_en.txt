With utmost respect,
Concerning Sangoro of Noguchi Village under Ina Prefecture's jurisdiction who wishes to engage in sake brewing business: From the first year of Genji era (1864) to the year of the dog (Meiji 2, 1869), the entire sake brewing operation was lent out under the name of Daiji, and business was conducted during that period. Although we humbly received the recent directive to return to the home village according to the current policy, given the present circumstances where substantial loans continue to increase, withdrawal proves difficult. We humbly request that Sangoro's petition be mercifully granted as requested.

Regarding this matter:
September, Meiji 3, year of the metal horse　　Nakamachi
　　　　　　　　　　Petitioner: Hirose Jirozaemon
　　　　　　　　　　Group Head: Yaemon  
　　　　　　　　　　Town Representative: Ichiroemon
To the Magistrate's Office

The above petition was to be delivered by Nakamachi town representative Ichiroemon, but since this concerns the sake brewing of both the Iemon and Jirozaemon households, both parties should have submitted the petition together. When Iemon was consulted, he stated that Sangoro's matter was not under the management of the main house, so he would submit an oral statement from his side, and thus petitioned accordingly. Therefore, Jirozaemon's petition endorsement was not made, and only Iemon's oral statement was taken by Kinzaemon and Shobei to the Civil Administration Bureau and presented to Lord Ikeda Sadato. Regarding this, although Sangoro prepared two separate petition documents, they were not submitted. Following instructions regarding the oral statement, both parties intended to submit petitions and took them back. However, since Sangoro brought an accompanying letter from Ina Prefecture, Lord Takahashi Genshichi instructed that both parties should be summoned during the term period, so the petition was handed over to the Nakamachi town representative.
