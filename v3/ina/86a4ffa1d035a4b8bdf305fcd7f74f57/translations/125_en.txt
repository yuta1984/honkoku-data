Humbly submitted
When we replaced the well located on the north side of our district on the 16th of this month,
we found a small unglazed ceramic jar at the bottom of the well, and inside it were
the two deities Ebisu and Daikokuten. As these are fortune deities who are the ancestors of commerce,
we are all rejoicing that they form the foundation for our district's prosperity, and we earnestly wish
to invite them for worship. To the east of Masugata, there is
vacant land entrusted to our district by the authorities,
so we wish to build a small shrine at that location and
invite the deities for worship. Therefore, we humbly submit this petition.
Regarding the above matter
Meiji 3, Year of the Metal Horse, 11th month　　　Aioi-chō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Group head
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Tanaka Gondaburō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　District representative
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kitahara Shinemon
　To the Magistrate's Office
When this was submitted on the 22nd at the government office through Mr. Takahashi's intermediation, on the 24th
Mr. Kondō informed us that approval was not granted, and the items mentioned in the petition were also returned.
By official order, the items were to be retrieved and kept with the district representative, so immediately
on the morning of the 25th, we summoned district representative Shinemon and conveyed this instruction.
