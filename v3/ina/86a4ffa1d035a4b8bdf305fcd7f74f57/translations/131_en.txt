Respectfully submitted,

First, regarding a property I own adjacent to where I reside, where Yamazumiya Heiji currently resides as his dwelling. Concerning this property, my grandfather Chūzaemon acquired the house and property that was owned by Izumiya Shintarō of Honmachi during the Tora year of Bunsei 13 (1830), and rented it to a person called Wataya Yahei. This Yahei was the nephew of my grandfather Chūzaemon. Since my grandfather had no biological children, he thought of him as his own son and allowed him to stay without charging rent or setting any terms. However, this Yahei died of illness during the Inu year of Tenpō 9 (1838). After that, the deceased man's widow and three children, four people in total, were taken in and cared for by my adoptive father Chūzaemon. During this time, a man named Mokichi from Hiyama village was adopted as a son-in-law to the widow's daughter and made to succeed the Yahei family line. However, during the Hitsuji year of Ansei 6 (1859), this widow also

passed away. Subsequently, about ten years ago, this Mokichi abandoned the family name of Wataya Yahei and succeeded to the extinct family line of Yamazumiya Heiji in the same town, changing his name to Yamazumiya Heiji. As a result, there were no longer any relatives of Wataya Yahei, so it became difficult to continue lending my property without rent. I consulted about this matter with my adoptive father Chūzaemon while he was still alive. Then during the I year of Bunkyū 3 (1863), [the property] was damaged by a spreading fire, and Wataya Kichichi, acting as the representative of Yahei's relatives, earnestly requested that I lend him the land so he could build a house again on my property. Having no choice, I received a stamped document from him and lent him the land.
