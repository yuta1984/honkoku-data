Respectfully submitting a petition by oral statement

In the 12th month of Bunkyū 3, Year of the Boar, the blacksmith Masahachi of Shimo-machi wished to transfer his property on the east side of Hokomotichō, with a frontage of 5 ken 1 shaku 5 sun, depth of 7 ken, and one storehouse attached. Upon receiving this request from Masahachi and his presentation of official documents bearing the joint seals of inn town officials and town elders, we decided to accept the transfer and purchased the property. This matter concerns property held by Fujiya Ginzō of Hokomotichō, who is related as a nephew through family connections, and where the same person intended to reside,

However, due to living together in Yosaburo's household, the purchase document was received under the relative's name. As there is no discrepancy in this matter, we are sending the formal documents with accompanying letters. This does not mean complete personal freedom over the property. This is merely a nominal arrangement until a separate household can be established in the future. When we previously requested that ground rent be paid to the house builder Miyoshiya Bunshirō, he stated that he had long-standing concerns about the said property and asked that it be sold to him, but no agreement could be reached.
