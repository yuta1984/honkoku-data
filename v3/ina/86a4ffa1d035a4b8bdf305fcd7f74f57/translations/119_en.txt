We are gratefully conducting our artisan and merchant businesses, but since this is a new town, our means of earning are limited and we are declining day by day. Moreover, due to recent successive crop failures, rice prices and all commodity prices have become extraordinarily high. Construction and repairs cannot be properly maintained, and all of us are falling into decline. Each household no longer resides in their own homes, and as years pass, even when houses are damaged by wind or suffer from rain leaks, there is no one to repair them. No matter how hard we work at our means of earning, it is insufficient for today's livelihood expenses. We are in inevitable hardship, and finally, having received the benevolent favor mentioned above and borrowed money respectively, we have fallen behind in repaying the loans to the government.

We have absolutely no intention of forgetting the original beneficence, but in our current state, only those who maintain residences as members of the town's religious community remain, while the rest are increasingly moving back to their original towns and villages, and the population is gradually decreasing. We consider this an extremely lamentable situation. Therefore, we humbly request that with special compassion, for the prosperity of both towns, we be allowed to pay transportation taxes to the government, and that a certain number of inn operators be designated, with three serving women per establishment being pardoned, which we earnestly petition for.
