## Record of Words and Deeds of the Naito Family

Compiled with reverence by your servant, Toshimasa.

The lineage of the Naito family originates from Fujiwara no Hidesato, the Chinjufu-shōgun (Commander of the Pacification Headquarters).
Hidesato's great-great-grandson held the title of Sakon-shōgen, and his personal name (imina) was Yoritoshi. Yoritoshi was appointed to the position of Utoneri (Inner Chamberlain). Yoritoshi's son was called Yukitoshi. Yukitoshi was appointed as the Kengyō (Inspector/Administrator) of Ōmi Province (Gōshū), and was referred to as the "Naitō Kengyō." Because Yoritoshi had been appointed as Utoneri (内舎人), the character "Nai" (内, "inner") was used to distinguish this branch from the other Fujiwara lineages (Shofuji), and it is said that herein lies the origin of the restored surname (Naito). Among the descendants born after Yukitoshi was one known as Bungo Shirō Saemon, whose personal name (imina) was Iekiyo. [Continues on the next page...]
