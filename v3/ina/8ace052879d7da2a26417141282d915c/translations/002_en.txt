The aforementioned three persons (Chōgorō, Ichibei, and Kanzaemon) had developed and cultivated the land, but it was again washed away by floods thereafter. Consequently, Chōgorō and Ichibei of Ina Village transferred it again to Hyōemon and Gonosuke of I Village, who took over the cultivation. Under these circumstances, Sakai Kichibei fell into repeated financial difficulties and found it hard to continue farming. He believed that if he could redeem the mortgaged land with the original principal and cultivate it, it would provide a foundation for rebuilding his livelihood. For several years, he made requests for financial assistance (mushin), but his pleas were not heeded, and years passed in this manner.

However, during the flood in the previous Snake year (Mi year), the land was washed away again, returning to the same state as when it was transferred to Chōgorō and Ichibei of Ina Village and Kanzaemon of I Village in the 4th year of Bunsei (1821), Snake year. Despite repeatedly requesting in good faith that they return the land for the original principal amount, his requests were not accepted. Sakai Kichibei has fallen into poverty and is in dire straits. Though it may be presumptuous, he humbly petitions for compassionate consideration to be given to the current owners - Hyōemon, Kanzaemon, and Gonosuke - so that Kichibei may continue farming and maintain his livelihood as a peasant permanently.
