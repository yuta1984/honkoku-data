1. Swordsmanship
1. Ittō-ryū school: Ōya Kanji, a disciple of Asari Matashichirō, and
　　Endō Goheta of the Kiso Yamamura Jinbei family were from the same school and were
　　famous swordsmen.
1. Jikishinkage-
　ryū school: Tagaya Tadaemon served as instructor and was
　　a fellow disciple of Kawamo Kotōta. Due to certain circumstances, violence occurred
　　and Tadaemon was killed.
1. Shintō Munen-
　ryū school: During Lord Yoriyasu's time, the Edo swordsman Saitō Yakurō
　　was invited to teach swordsmanship to the domain's retainers in their territory. At this time
　　Katsura Kogorō accompanied him. Kogorō was a
[Left page]
　　retainer of Chōshū domain and a meritorious subject of the Restoration, Marquis Kido
　　Takayoshi. At that time he became Saitō's disciple and served as head student.
　　The accompanying group numbered ten people (names recorded on separate paper).
　　Lord Yoriyasu had domain retainers Yamagata Jintayū and Izawa Enta
　　enter Saitō's academy for training. After several years they obtained full
　　licenses, and upon returning to the domain were appointed as swordsmanship instructors,
　　that is, teachers.
