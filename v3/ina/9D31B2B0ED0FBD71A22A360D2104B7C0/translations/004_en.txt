Furthermore, although investigation into his father, mother, and siblings is necessary, there are no documents whatsoever at either our domain house or Zama Soshuji Temple, and with only the four characters "Takeda Soshu," we have been unable to grasp the essential facts to this day. It appears that his relatives and others deliberately kept matters concerning Lord Soshu himself secret. Therefore, in my humble opinion, Lord Soshu may perhaps have been a surviving child of the Koshu Takeda family. If so, when the Takeda family was destroyed, the Tokugawa Ogosho (retired shogun) would have regretted the extinction of this distinguished bloodline and secretly issued an order. This can be inferred from the fact that the Ogosho at that time consistently protected and employed former retainers of the Takeda. Lord Soshu resided in Zama for his entire life, passed away there, and established three temples in the same location, called Soshuji Temple. In the ○○th year of Meiji, there was a 300th memorial service, which Lord Yorisuke attended, and I accompanied him.
