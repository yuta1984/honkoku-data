The son Den'ichirō was taken into service. Den'ichirō had reached
　　the "heta" (lower) rank, it is said.
　　For spearmanship, all domains in Southern Shinano took the Suganuma as their teacher. Therefore, Suwa
　　invited Master Suganuma, Iida made a member of the Suganuma clan an
　　adopted son of the Andō family, and Takatō took
　　Den'ichirō into service.
　　At the time when Den'ichirō was taken into service, one day Asami Kisui
　　invited Suganuma Den'ichirō and entertained him with food and drink. When the banquet reached its height,
　　Kisui went to his own sitting room in a separate chamber and
　　committed suicide. This was likely due to some inner thoughts. The family's shock
　　was extraordinary, but Kisui's wife dealt with
　　the matter calmly and composedly, and as if nothing had happened, told Den'ichirō that "my husband has unfortunately developed stomach pains,
　　so"

　　until seeing him off upon his departure. Even after Den'ichirō's departure,
　　she still did not announce mourning due to the suicide. The next day
　　she reported that he had suddenly fallen ill and died, and
