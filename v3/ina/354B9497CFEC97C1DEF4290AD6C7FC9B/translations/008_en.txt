——to enter the capital. As the troops of Hikone and Ōgaki were holding and guarding the key positions, [the rōshi] changed course and headed toward Echizen Province. At this time, the troops of Kaga domain were guarding the fortifications in Ōmi Province. Takeda and his companions sent a messenger to petition the Kaga forces, saying: "We intend to present our circumstances before the capital. We humbly ask that you open a path for us." The Kaga troops refused to comply. Takeda and his companions, their stratagems exhausted, ultimately surrendered to Kaga domain. Kaga reported this to the shogunate. The shogunate distributed Takeda and the others among various domains, where they were kept under confinement, and the following spring they were executed by beheading.

Taishō 5 [1916], 12th day of the 7th month
