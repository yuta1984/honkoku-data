The Mito Rōshi and Takeda Kōunsai Pass Through the Ina Highway
Handwritten Record by Okada Kazumichi

The rōshi (masterless samurai) of the Mito domain, led by Fujita Kojirō and others, raised troops in the provinces of Hitachi and Shimotsuke with the primary aim of expelling foreigners (jōi), and established their base at Mount Tsukuba. Takeda Kōunsai joined them and together they plotted to travel to Kyoto to appeal their cause. In the eleventh month of Genji 1 (1864), they marched up the Nakasendō highway with an estimated force of eight hundred men. The shogunate ordered the domains along the route to intercept and attack them. As they advanced, Takeda's forces defeated the troops of various Kōzuke (Jōshū) domain forces and entered Shinano Province, where they were about to cross the Wada Pass. 〇On a certain day in the eleventh month, the Suwa domain attacked them at Wada Pass; a great battle ensued, with casualties on both sides. 〇 Kazumichi, acting under orders from the Takatō domain, departed from Miyaki post station by express palanquin at a certain hour on that same day and month, arranged a relay at Ono post station, and arrived at Shiojiri post station at a certain hour that same night, where he conducted reconnaissance of the situation in that area.
