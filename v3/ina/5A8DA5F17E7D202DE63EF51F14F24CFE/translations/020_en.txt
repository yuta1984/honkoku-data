Ina Village

Tenhaku (currently called Shibabayashi [grass forest])
One plot: Bamboo grove, 1 se 2 bu (4 ken × 8 ken)
　　　　　　　　　　　Grove keeper contractor: Ichiroemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Currently: Ichiroemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kichibei

The above: By petition in Kansei 4 (1792), field crops were planted,
with 2 to of rice paid annually as tribute, collected by the officials.
In Bunsei 10 (1827), it was transferred to Ichiroemon. The location called Shibabayashi-Ha-no-36, lower field
8 se 13 bu, was exchanged for the same person's existing land. On the 28th day of the 2nd month of Tempo 6 (1835), 
the petition for mountain dedication was approved as requested, commendable conduct was proclaimed, and permanent grove keeper duties were assigned. In the same year,
seed bamboo planting was ordered.
