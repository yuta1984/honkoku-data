One: When someone appointed to extraordinary garrison duty finds it difficult to serve due to illness or other reasons and petitions not to report for garrison duty during that year, naturally the full year's deductions shall apply. For example, if payments were made without deductions for both spring and summer periods, the rice deduction amount shall be paid back in three installments from that winter through the following year's spring and winter. One: When a regular attendant remains behind from service due to illness or other reasons for several months of convalescence and petitions that garrison duty is difficult to perform, if by the lord's consideration he is graciously ordered to rest and recuperate first, and consequently does not report for garrison duty during that year, then two-thirds of the usual annual rice deduction amount shall be waived and paid to him.
