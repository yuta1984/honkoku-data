However, even for cases where collection deadlines and judicial sanctions have already been set, the magistrate's office shall not investigate settlement methods in the future.

Regarding the matter of interest on gold and silver, as announced in the previous Year of the Tiger, this should be thoroughly understood, with the facilitation of financial circulation in society as the primary concern. All provinces should ensure smooth and sincere lending without hindrance. Taking excessive miscellaneous fees under various pretexts beyond the stated interest is absolutely forbidden.

1. Regarding gold and silver lending, even in old cases, both parties should engage in discussions with sincerity. There is no need to readily resort to legal suits and judicial sanctions. It appears that many cases arise from frivolous lawsuits due to insincerity on both sides. Now that mutual settlement has been decreed, all matters should follow what was communicated regarding gold and silver transactions in the 9th year of Kansei (1797), strictly observing these rules and conducting business with complete sincerity. Those who expect that lawsuits to the magistrate's office will not succeed and therefore plan to accept losses, or those who craftily manipulate situations with greed to create disputes, or those who file lawsuits concerned only with profit, shall be dealt with accordingly.
