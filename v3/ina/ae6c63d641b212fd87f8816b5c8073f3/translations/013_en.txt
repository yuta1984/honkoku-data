The late Kato Shinbei owned a house and residential plot within the town district. Since he had debts to Fujiya Toemon, in the fifth year of Bunsei, year of the Horse (1822), the house and plot were put up as collateral. After Shinbei's death, the matter was suspended for some time, and furthermore, in the fifth year of Tenpo, year of the Horse (1834), it was destroyed by fire spreading from nearby, leaving only vacant land. Since Toemon's side found it difficult to fulfill the various municipal duties, they decided to surrender the land to the town district. After consultation among the town officials, in the second year of Kaei, year of the Rooster (1849), through the efforts of the town officials, house construction and repairs were carried out, and even the Tenno Shrine was established there.
