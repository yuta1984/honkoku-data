Fukushima

Saemon-no-dayū Minamoto no Masanori was the eldest son of a certain Fukushima Yozaemon-no-jō, a resident of Owari Province. Initially, when Toyotomi Taikō was the guardian of Harima Province, Masanori was employed as a close attendant from his childhood (it is said that Masanori was a person connected to the Toyotomi family, but the details are not known). In Tenshō 6, when Miki Castle in Harima Province was attacked, Masanori, then eighteen years old, distinguished himself in battle for the first time. From then on, there was no battle at Tottori, Yamazaki, and other places where he did not achieve merit, and as the first of the seventh among the seven vanguard riders at Shizugatake, he received special rewards. In the eleventh month of Tenshō 11, he was granted court rank and made Saemon-no-jō (during this battle, his childhood name was Ichimatsu, and he was the first to take a head and present it, receiving a grant of 5,000 koku of land). He participated in the campaigns at Komaki and Saiga, and after Shikoku was pacified in the summer of the same thirteenth year, he was granted land in Iyo and ruled Imabari Castle (100,000 koku).
