This is an essential matter, and since there have been official regulations from long ago,
when conducting new construction or repairs,
one should make them rather compact,
and building fixtures should prioritize
durability over elaborate workmanship, keeping
costs reasonable for long-term use.
If there is an unavoidable reason to expand even by one tsubo,
one should submit a petition to the administrative office
as stipulated in the Bunka-era
proclamation and follow their instructions. Should there be anyone who is misguided
and constructs elaborate houses
prioritizing appearance,
after investigation, demolition and other measures
shall be ordered.
One, Regarding tobacco pouches, paper cases and other textile-covered items,
high-quality and expensive goods, as well as gold and silver
crafted items, and naturally colored or bronze
items which appear luxurious, should be strictly avoided
as previously ordered in proclamations. Furthermore,
unnecessary tools and implements should not be acquired.
One, Paper-covered parasols, snake-eye umbrellas, and
garden path coverings
shall continue to be prohibited as before.
