Twenty tsubo each for two sides [gives] forty tsubo, which is subtracted and discarded. The small corner is two-times-two giving four tsubo [self-multiplied], multiplied by the height of three ken, giving three-four of twelve tsubo, which is subtracted and cleared — [the length/width is thus] known to be 12 ken. Adding one ken to the height [gives] 13 ken, as stated.
