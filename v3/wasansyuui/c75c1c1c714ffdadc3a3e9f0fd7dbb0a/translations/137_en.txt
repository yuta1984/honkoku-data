**Ten Mindsets for Calculation** (continued)

Third: Consider whether multiplication or division is more appropriate.

Fourth: Carry out calculations calmly and deliberately.

Fifth: Verify and carefully reconsider the quotient (result of division).

Sixth: Pay attention to discrepancies in the terminology used for placement and division procedures.

Seventh: When assigning names (to numbers/units), pay attention to discrepancies in the tens place.

Eighth: In the case of division, multiply back by the divisor; in the case of multiplication, divide back — and verify whether the result returns to the original number, thus checking for errors.

Ninth: Both when first placing numbers and when arriving at the final quotient, pay attention alongside the original reference value to whether there are any "tobi-ki" (skipped or misaligned digit positions).

Tenth: Take care to avoid errors in writing and errors in reading.
