The methods set forth in these three volumes — upper, middle, and lower — all illuminate calculation from its very foundations and employ their methods in thorough detail. Because of this, they may appear to be a roundabout way of doing things. Nevertheless, methods for creating reference points and taking shortcuts do exist within each type of calculation. That said, if one relies solely on a method that has merely been constructed and assembled on the surface, without knowing its underlying roots, then while the calculations one has written down may be completed successfully, it will be difficult for a beginner to apply that same understanding to carry out other, various calculations.

For example: if one were to receive a flowering branch from another person's tree and simply gaze upon it, that pleasure would last only as long as the flowers remain before they fall — once fallen, no trace remains, and one would have no way of knowing that the flowers will bloom again on another branch. But if one were to desire the tree from its roots and plant it oneself, then not only would one enjoy it for as long as it is in bloom, but it would become a sight to behold in the coming spring as well, and would become one's own lasting treasure.

In this way, the ready-made, shortcut methods are like receiving a flowering branch from another. Practicing only those calculations will make it difficult to be of use in other calculations. But to clearly set forth and record the foundations of calculation is like planting a flowering tree together with its roots. One is not limited to the enjoyment of a single branch; from a fallen branch new blooms may emerge, and the pleasures are many...
