He published two volumes and taught them to his disciples. Although his methods did not extend to the square root extraction method (kaiheihō), they served as a bridge for later mathematicians. At this time, the whole realm enjoyed peace and prosperity, and many people devoted themselves to the arts. Yoshida Mitsuyoshi appeared and created the Jinkōki, which became widely popular in the world. However, his methods did not go beyond square root extraction, cube root extraction, surplus and deficit, and equations. Next, Sawaguchi Kazuyuki appeared and, obtaining Zhu Shijie's Introduction to Mathematical Studies, demonstrated the celestial element method and published Ancient and Modern Mathematical Methods. Furthermore, Master Takakazu wielded his outstanding talent, extensively studied various reduction methods and secret techniques, and his inventions surpassed previous generations, causing mathematics to flourish greatly. He can truly be called a once-in-a-generation talent. From then on, the disciples emerging from his school are too numerous to enumerate. Masters Takebe Matsunaga, Kurushima, and Nakane appeared and devoted themselves to this way. Someone like myself has drawn from those tributary streams and has barely managed to glimpse over the hedge. Most people do not discuss the true purpose of mathematical learning, but instead of focusing on its economic benefits, they use it merely as a convenience for brokerage and calculation, making it part of the daily life of merchants and traders. Therefore, every household without exception possesses an abacus. Those court nobles and high officials view this as vulgar commercial practice. How appropriate this is! When one examines their methods, they are all techniques for profit-making, truly the commercial activities of merchants and traders—indeed, this is the surplus benefit of great peace.
