The Origins of Mathematical Learning
The child asks: "In what age did mathematics begin? And how has it flourished and declined?" The answer is: "Long ago, it is said that Leishu began it in the time of the Yellow Emperor. However, Fuxi's Eight Trigrams should be considered the true beginning.
When Duke of Zhou asked his minister Shang Gao 'From where do numbers arise?', Shang Gao replied: 'The method of numbers arises from circular methods. Circles arise from squares. Squares arise from the carpenter's square. The carpenter's square arises from nine times nine equals eighty-one.' In the Rites of Zhou it states: 'The Guardian instructs the king through remonstrance and nurtures the sons of nobles through the Way, teaching them the six arts,' among which are the nine numbers. Liu Hui of the Han dynasty created the Nine Chapters on Mathematical Arts. The group including Xu Shang, Du Zhong, Zhuo Mao, Liu Xin, Ma Rong, Zheng Xuan, He Xiu, Zhang Heng, Chen Zhi, and Wang Can all excelled at this.
