《Title: Tea Discussions with Master Araki Hikoshiro Murahide》
Among the mathematicians of old times, there was a man called Mori Kanbei Shigenō. He was a person of Osaka Castle, but after the unification [of Japan], he became a masterless samurai (rōnin) in Edo. He had three disciples: Imamura Jihei Chishō authored the "Tateigairoku"; Yoshida Shichibei Mitsuyoshi authored the "Jinkōki," "Koreki Binran," and "Wakan Gōun"; and Takahara Shōzaemon Yoshitane later called himself Ichigen. Imamura's student, Hiraga Kanzaemon Yasuhide, served Lord Mito Mitsukuni and held a stipend of 500 koku.
