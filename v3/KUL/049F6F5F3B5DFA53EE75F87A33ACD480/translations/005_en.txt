As described above, dozens of vessels were deployed daily to Jishima, and as night gradually fell and the sea became calm, the ships went out into the offing of Jishima. First, boat captains went out for reconnaissance in various directions and observed that since daytime the Chinese ship had moved closer and could be seen about three to four ri offshore from Yamaga. They rowed back and reported this. From around the hour of the Ox (1-3 AM), they gradually approached the Chinese ship and eagerly awaited daybreak.
