This was the etiquette of the time. My petition to return home was also promptly granted. Now, regarding the structure of the royal palace, it does not look like a castle at all - it is built like an earthen storehouse made of brick, or piled up five or six layers deep with stone. On the second and third floors of the houses, they construct artificial hills and water features, and create flower gardens. The foundation is lined with copper and soil is placed on top. As for house construction, there is not much difference between the royal palace and commoners' residences.

Question:
What about fires?

Answer:
As mentioned above, since houses are mostly made of brick and such materials, fires are extremely rare.

【Left Page】
During my stay in that land, there were two fires. A fire on the second floor would go unnoticed on the third floor, and neighboring houses would be even less aware of it. In short, entire buildings do not burn down. Only the furnishings inside the house or interior fittings are destroyed by fire. However, I heard that places built with wood do have quite a few fires.

Question:
I observed that there is a large self-ringing bell (clock) on top of the castle tower - is this correct?

Answer:
It is an extraordinarily large construction. The size of the gears appeared to be about as large as the wheels of watermills made in this country.
