Officials have various decorations according to their rank, but they are probably much like the Russians who came this time. Women's clothing also has tube sleeves, fitted narrow from the waist up, with wide hakama from the waist down, and they consider it proper to wear long trailing hems. They sprinkle white powder on their hair when styling it. At such times they do not wear hats. Ordinarily they wear small hats.

The Tatars are dark-skinned, decorate their hair with embroidered silk, wear small hats, and their clothing appears similar to that of Russians. Those of middle class and above wear woolen cloth, while those of lower status wear leather

The people of the "Siberian" region are dark-complexioned and robust, with black hair and stout builds, generally coarse in appearance. Their clothing is made of leather and hemp, and they wear hats in winter but not in summer. They wear leg coverings and shoes. Women wear garments that cover from the head down to the thighs, along with leg coverings and shoes. Even within "Siberia," the "Platzke" and "Yakōde" are extremely base, and in Russia they despise immoral people by saying they are "like Yakōde."

Regarding food, both Russia proper and the "Siberian" regions make bread from wheat as their staple food, and eat cattle, pigs, wild cattle, birds, and fish.
