Those dispatched to the great assembly hall are appointed from among those unanimously recommended by the people. Generally, council officials serve for seven years and local officials for two years as a rule. Legal ordinance Type A is compiled by local officials, reviewed by council officials, and when implementing it among the people, council officials directly subordinate to the alliance leader also process it. The "President" commands both naval and land forces and takes measures to ensure that already completed laws are necessarily enforced. He also oversees diplomatic ceremonies with other nations and the minting of currency. His vice-administrators include "Minister" council officials. These officials are subordinate to the "President," managing many cases and consulting to make decisions. Above the "Senate" there is an official called "Vice President," and if there are unavoidable circumstances, he handles affairs in place of the "President." The great assembly hall of all the states is located in "Washington," which was established as the foundation of the nation and constructed, named "Columbia." Generally, lawsuits undergo primary hearings in the various states, then are resolved by the upper oversight in "Washington" and lower oversight in "[unclear]" to judge right and wrong and determine punishment. The lower oversight also handles matters of peace and lawsuit resolution through adjudication. These judicial officials are separate from political officials and do not interact with each other. Even for death sentences, most often death is commuted to a term of years, or prison is given for lifelong labor service. Their clothing and provisions are fully supplied without shortage
