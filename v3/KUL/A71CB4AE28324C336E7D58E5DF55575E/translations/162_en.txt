Merchant vessels from various nations gather at "Oahu," bringing tools necessary for various industries to trade with the island's local products. Therefore, Western implements have increased on the island year by year. By now, the islanders' craftsmanship nearly matches that of the West. Their products such as rope, sailcloth, and fishing gear are sturdy and durable, said to surpass British products. The sailors and craftsmen employed by the king are all British.

Two
The "Sandwich Islands" consist of eleven islands, two of which are uninhabited. "Cook" discovered them on his third voyage and initially named them after the British naval captain "Sandwich" who pacified these islands. The climate is similar to the West Indies (America) and is even more temperate. Ponds, marshes, and streams are always filled with water, with no concern for drought or desiccation.

Local products include pigs, ducks, breadfruit ("Brotfrüchten"), sugarcane, pineapple ("Ananas"), "Pataten" potatoes ("Blummel"), coconut trees ("Kokosbaum"), marble ("Almur"), whetstones ("Schleifstein"), pearls ("Hisank"), etc. According to "Cook's" account, the native race is "Malay" and the population numbers four hundred thousand. The islanders have robust physiques and are darker in complexion than "Tahitian" people. Their nature is gentle and intelligent. They skillfully handle various tasks and are particularly diligent in cultivation, bird and animal hunting, and fishing. The manufacture of sails and rigging has a unique mastery among the natives, being so strong and durable that Atlantic products cannot match them. Their flower mats are also fine and beautiful, able to withstand long-term use.
