○For whale hunting, six men are loaded onto one cedar plank, operating three boats
○Rescued by a whaling ship and after six months anchored at "Sasuitchi." Whales are killed by stabbing the throat, then brought alongside the main vessel and rotated
○Traveling around the area, the foremost in prosperity is "Sasuitchi." The island has seven parts, and the guests resided on an island called "Owaho," where the chieftain also lives. There are over two hundred households, not including the foreign shops in this count. There are two Guangdong shops
○Also rode a steamship for one day. Its operation runs from four in the morning until eight in the evening, round trip, resting for about half an hour at the destination. The water distance is twenty ri, making forty ri for the round trip
○While staying in "Sasuitchi," saw "Manowa" ships. Of the three vessels, two were "Marekai" and one was a French warship. I entered and closely observed the French vessel. "Manowa" means patrol ship
○Theft and arson are unheard of. Have never witnessed disasters from dancing horses either
○Chimneys are made of single-layer copper, and the upper layer of roofs is constructed with stone and mud. They emerge layer by layer above the rooftops
○The washing of clothing is extremely frequent. They wash once every seven days. For nobles, they wash three times every seven days
○House construction is three-story buildings made by stacking stones. The stones are three feet wide
