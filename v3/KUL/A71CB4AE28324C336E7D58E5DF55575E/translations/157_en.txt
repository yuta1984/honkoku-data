When calculating the population growth of the inhabitants: in 1790 《marginal note: Kansei 2, year of kanoe-inu》 there were 3,929,000 people; in 1800 《marginal note: Kansei 12, year of kanoe-saru》 there were 5,281,000 people; in 1817 《marginal note: Bunka 14, year of hinoto-ushi》 there were 10,250,000 people. When Spain ceded Florida, this reached 20 million.

In the American mainland, many tribes that have lived on that land since ancient times still reside there. In the western part of the Mississippi, grasslands still flourish today, with vast wilderness, great marshes, huge swamps, and such. In the Savannas there are areas where wild grasses have grown wild and desolate, where "Indian" natives have come to dwell. Therefore, calculating all the American states together, there are 50 million people.

The united states each conduct their own governance and issue edicts. Their superior officer is called "Government" 《marginal note: that is, "President"》. Among the states, only a President is appointed, and some also have council officials 《marginal note: "Senate," also called "Lord"》 as co-signatories. To deliberate on matters of great importance to the states in general - such as military affairs, peace negotiations, diplomatic missions, trade, naval affairs, taxation, coinage and other such items - two "Lords" each are appointed in advance from the various states to the great assembly hall. "Lords" are also sent to local government offices. The number of members is not fixed but varies according to the size of the population.
