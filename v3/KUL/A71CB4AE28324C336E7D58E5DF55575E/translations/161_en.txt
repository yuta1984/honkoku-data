Previously, Westerners taught these various arts to "Australia," and from "Australia" they were transmitted again to these islands. The islanders' clothing consists of fur cloaks and loincloths, and high officials wear magnificent crowns during formal occasions. Both men and women apply tattoos to their bodies and decorate their hair and necks with tortoiseshell and bird feathers. Their character is honest and trustworthy, and they treat Western visitors with great hospitality. The women are more headstrong than those of "Tahiti" and less submissive. However, they are chaste and virtuous with little licentious behavior. Noble ladies often have weaving rooms. Their religious doctrine is the same "Christianity" practiced in "Tahiti." At noble funeral rites, they sometimes use human sacrifice. The priests' attire resembles that of physicians. The largest of all the islands is called "Oahu" Island. In 1799 《Kansei 11, Year of the Boar》, "Cook" had his boat seized by the natives, which greatly opened disputes, and he ultimately died in battle. On this island the supreme king establishes his residence and governs the neighboring islands. Under the king are several administrative officials called "Erics" who convey the king's commands to the various lords. Below them are attached the common people of farming, crafts, commerce, etc. 《called "Tautaus" in the native language》. Now the king seeks to civilize the natives and invites many Europeans. The king's palace and fortress system is the same as in the West, equipped with cannons large and small, and its defenses are extremely strict. The navy also follows Western models. They also have shipbuilding skills and navigate the South Seas.
