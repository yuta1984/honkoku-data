Regarding industry: In this place, they buy sand gold that is dug out and brought from the mountains, calling it "hain," and they smelt this gold to make round coin-like pieces with human heads on them. People bring the sand gold they have dug to the smelting place. When they bring sand gold, they exchange it for these coins. If they bring one ounce of sand gold by weight, they receive sixteen or seventeen coins. Although by weight it should be eighteen coins worth, they deduct about two coins for manufacturing costs and other miscellaneous expenses. Manjiro also exchanged the sand gold he dug for coins. He did not hear about other industries. This place appears to be full of merchants, and since merchants from various states come by ship, any kind of goods are available without difficulty.

1. Regarding farming: He observed the planting of wheat (large and small varieties), sweet potatoes, taro, etc.

1. Regarding celebrations: No celebratory scenes were observed.

　　　　The situation at the California gold mines

1. Regarding people: Similar to New Bedford, but no women live there. It is generally an unsafe place, and he heard that there are murders on the way from Sacramento, with people frequently being shot and killed with pistols, though he did not see any murder victims himself. He witnessed people gambling at the foot of the gold mountain, with large groups gathering in circles, using gold, silver, and coins as stakes, playing with card-like items made of paper for gambling.
