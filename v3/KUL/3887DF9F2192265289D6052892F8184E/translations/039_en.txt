The houses are built with four pillars, and the roofs are made by arranging coconut palm leaves. These leaves are about two to three shaku long and narrow in width. The land is sandy with low mountains, and houses are sparsely scattered. Since they only approached by small boat and did not land, they do not know the details about food and other aspects of the land.

　　　　　The Condition of Kian Island

Regarding the people: Both men and women are of ordinary height, and their facial features and eye color show no particular difference from Japanese. Men cut their hair and wear hats made like tea stands. Those who appear to be officials have come from Spain to reside there. They also have cut hair and wear clothing of woolen cloth and such. All their clothing and hair styles are similar to those of Oahu (Hawaii).

Regarding food: Through ship provisions, they heard that this place has many fish, but they did not see any.

Regarding house construction: The pillars are set directly into the ground, and some roofs are thatched while others are shingled with boards. There are no merchant-type people; all are farmer-type houses, and the house construction and land conditions are all similar to Oahu (Hawaii).

No castle fortifications or temple-like structures were observed.

Regarding vegetation: There are many coconut and citrus trees, and many other trees as well, but they do not remember clearly.

Regarding birds and animals: They saw chickens, pigs, cattle, horses, and such.

Regarding the seasons: They were detained there repeatedly from around the first month to the third month.
