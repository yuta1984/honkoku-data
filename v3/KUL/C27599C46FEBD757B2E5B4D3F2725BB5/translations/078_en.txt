Message to Lord Gaiki:
1. Concerning the Chinese ship that drifted to Kishiura in Lord Matsudaira Hizen-no-kami's domain, we have made repeated inquiries during this time. If the ship has no official documents and there are no instructions from the magistrate regarding forwarding it, it should be expelled according to previous written orders. This was conveyed to Tokunaga Yajiemon in the written order of the 13th of last month. When we made subsequent inquiries, we were told to follow what had been previously ordered, as conveyed to Tokunaga Yajiemon in the written order of the 15th of the same month. We humbly acknowledge this.

1. We were ordered to report in detail the circumstances of when we first received documents from the Chinese ship. As reported in writing in response to your inquiry of the 17th of last month, this differs from bad drifting vessels and has nothing suspicious about it. It appears to be a ship that drifted ashore unavoidably due to rough seas. For ships that have drifted ashore unavoidably from the past, the island guards from that direction would meet with them, learn of the situation, inquire whether they carried official documents, and if the Chinese ship presented documents, they would receive them and report to the castle town. Then officials would be sent from the castle town to conduct written dialogue, inquire in detail about official documents and other matters, and report these matters to the Nagasaki magistrate in due course. This time as well, the Himeshima station guard met with them, received documents, and reported to the castle town.
