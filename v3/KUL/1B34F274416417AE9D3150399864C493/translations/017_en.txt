When questioned, he would answer in their country's language, "By the great king's permission, we may pass anywhere," or when going to government offices, "We go there by official order." Such responses would consistently earn them polite treatment. This young man, who was extremely inarticulate when speaking Japanese and had a very poor memory, now appeared eloquent and quick-witted in all matters when conversing with people from both countries. Observing this, one might wonder if the gods deeply pity those who wander at the ends of the earth and bestow divine protection upon them. If anyone were to converse with this boy in Japanese, they would think "What a simpleton he is," yet this was indeed a mysterious phenomenon.
