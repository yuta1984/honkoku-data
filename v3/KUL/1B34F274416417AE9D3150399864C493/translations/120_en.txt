When the ship arrived at Nagasaki, we dropped anchor and remained moored for a while, when a surveillance ship came out from the guardhouse, followed by twenty tugboats that pulled us in, and we were finally able to set foot on land.

Indeed, having once been caught in the calamity of wind and waves, unexpectedly drifting to a foreign land ten thousand miles away, spending three years amid miasmatic vapors and mist, experiencing countless hardships and difficulties, and being able to see again the mountains, rivers, and vegetation of our great Japan - this must surely be due to the invisible protection of our eight million gods. When I think of this, what we should yearn for is only our land of Japan. What we should be grateful for is the divine blessing. Reader, if you temporarily put yourself in the position of a castaway, and after midnight when people have quieted down, close your eyes and contemplate deeply, you will surely feel something naturally.
