Fifth Tale: A person from Chikuzen drifts to Borneo and escapes from certain death to return to his homeland

In Karadomari-ura, Shima District, Chikuzen Province, there was a ship called Isemaru with a capacity of 1,600 koku, captained by Jūemon. Together with the sailors, twenty men were blown away to the southern seas, and all crew members died except for one sailor named Magoshichi, who endured nine years of hardship and returned to his hometown of Karadomari on August 26th of Meiwa 7, year of the Metal Tiger (125 years ago from now). Hearing the story of that time: in the final years of Hōreki era, they built a new ship and named it Isemaru. Since this was its maiden voyage, with Jūemon as captain, on October 6th of the year Jinba, they loaded rice from Fukuoka domain's storehouse, celebrated this auspicious departure, and transported it to Osaka without any hindrance. On their return journey, they welcomed the New Year at Nakatsu port in Buzen, and on the 6th day of mid-February, they again loaded rice from Nakatsu domain's storehouse and sailed to Edo. From April 6th, they loaded rice from Ōshū Tsugaru at Aomori and transported it to Edo, where they stayed for a while repairing ship equipment, and by then the era name had already changed to Meiwa.
