The six men had already become Japanese people again. They escaped being buried in the bellies of fish and sea turtles, and avoided becoming soil on a desolate island. By asking the local people, they finally learned the calendar date. From the magistrate and village headman, they were given one to and two shō of barley from six villages, and for the first time could eat grain meals. After staying about ten days, their strength returned to normal, so whether they knew or not about the ancient story of Qu Yuan of China throwing himself into the Miluo River, on the same date of the fifth day of the fifth month in early morning, they set sail from the bay of the same island, and around noon on the seventh day, they reached Susaki in Izu. When they reported the circumstances, they were ordered to sail the ship around, so after three days they sailed around, completed the investigation at the magistrate's office, returned to Susaki again, and finally became free. So they sold that ship at this place, and of the two logs they had cut and brought, they gave one to the Hachijojima magistrate as he desired, but the remaining one they left with innkeeper Riemon, traveled overland westward, and in the eighth month of Kanbun 10 (1670), successfully returned to their hometown.
