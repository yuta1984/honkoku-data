From within the island, about two natives boarded a small boat and came toward them. Using gestures, they asked where the harbor was, and the natives pointed south, saying it was over there. Following their directions, they rowed there and indeed found an inlet suitable for mooring ships.

So they dropped anchor in this inlet and moored their ship. That night, the island natives secretly came in a small boat and cut one of the anchor ropes. While they were thinking what outrageous behavior this was, hundreds of natives came pressing toward them in numerous small boats, seizing cargo indiscriminately, destroying even the tender boat, taking the crew members to shore, surrounding them with many people, and shouting abuse at them. Thinking their lives would end here, they steeled themselves for death. After a while, the natives stripped the clothes from all fifteen men, made them completely naked, knocked them down left and right, took even their undergarment sashes, seized the small amounts of gold, silver and copper coins they possessed, and departed to who knows where.

On the morning of the 8th, two natives at a time took their hands and held their feet, trying to drag and pull them away. Even though they had given up their lives, they thought they should not simply submit to whatever these people might do, so they all refused to go. Some were bound, others were beaten, and finally they were taken one by one to the individual dwellings of the natives. The scene at that time appeared as miserable as being whipped by demons of retribution and dragged to the gates of hell.

Now the fifteen men had already been made slaves of the natives. Their padded cotton clothing was completely stripped away, unraveled and made into single-layer garments for them to wear. Daily they were driven to the mountains to cut trees and gather firewood, forced to work the fields planting sweet potatoes and cutting grass. For their three daily meals, they were given only sweet potatoes and no other grains. After spending more than ten days like this, everyone could no longer endure this meaningless hardship.
