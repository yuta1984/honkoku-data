...a monk of great spiritual power. Both held the same rank of Third Stage Bodhisattva. Since both their prayers were equally strong, there was no clear victor in the battle. In the void, arrows flew back and forth, and when arrowheads clashed, the arrows would shatter and fall to the ground. Black clouds swirled, obscuring the light of the sun and moon, thunder occasionally roared and rumbled, and lightning flashed. The earth trembled in a most fearsome manner. For seven days, both Tō-ji and Sai-ji temples continuously maintained their goma fire altars without cease as they prayed—truly a terrifying sight. At this point, Kūkai devised a stratagem...
