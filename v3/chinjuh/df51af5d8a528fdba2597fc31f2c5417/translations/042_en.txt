When water is drawn continuously day and night through a ditch with a water depth of two feet, it could support rice fields worth three or four thousand koku. Therefore, even without converting all the newly reclaimed land to rice fields, at least one-third could safely be made into rice fields, I thought. However, since I am unfamiliar with the local customs and do not know whether this is suitable land for retaining irrigation water, this should not be taken as reliable, though I have shown my thoughts without reservation in the diagram.

From the newly reclaimed land to the sea surface, about seven to eight chō consists of tidal flats, with many formations of attached sandbanks visible. Within a few years, these will likely be added to the newly reclaimed land. The reed beds are places with low tribute taxes that should become newly reclaimed fields, so even if made into salt fields during high tide, this coastal area would become excellent land.
