The water flowing out from the Tone River and Nakagawa River mixes with seawater within a range of fourteen to fifteen chō, so it cannot be drawn in to make salt fields. However, if seawater is collected by boat from places where the tide flows in, it would be free of salt content.
There are calculations for how many thousand koku of seawater should be put into 300 tsubo of salt field land to produce salt (note: I do not know this). Since this is land with low tribute taxes, even if high tide is used with transport boats, rather than gaining profit from the land through vegetables, millet, barnyard millet, wheat cultivation, etc. in dry fields, using high tide and investing in the costs to gain profit from salt production would result in great benefit. This is not land that should be left as it is now. The soil quality is extremely good.
