It is blue in color [with diagram] and has this much radiance. It is said that there were once three stars, but two stars flew away and now only one star remains. In front of the garden there is also something called "the pine where the star descended" and there is also "the shrine of three stars." I have seen things called "stars" many times in various provinces, and since they have no evil sins, they are made into temple treasures with stories like "dropped by the prayers of such-and-such high priest" or "such-and-such dharma seal." Whatever one examines, the substance is like the above. Upon reflection, they appear to be what the world calls relics (shari), and these things called relics are mostly Tsugaru stones, Arito stones, or shell beads. In any country they have no particular efficacy, but they are good as decorative objects for Buddhist altars.

Shimo-Hirai Village [present-day Hirai 1-7 chome, Edogawa Ward]
In this area there is a commoner's house with the hereditary name Genemon, and the ancestor six generations back named Genzaemon was an excellent large man of great strength, so the villagers nicknamed him "O-Dai" (Great Big). He was skilled at fishing and had mastered the technique of casting a thirty-fathom hand-net even from atop a large boat. Therefore, at the hawking sessions of Lord Daiyuin, for entertainment he was summoned close to the august presence, where he demonstrated netting distant ducks and diving deep into the water to catch carp for the lord's viewing. On one occasion, he was granted a boat cushion as a reward, which is still preserved and treasured by the family. On another occasion, he was granted sake at the august location.
