Regarding the origin of this residence, though the period is unknown, it is a hall built several hundred years ago by some ancestor of the village headman, an ancient site that has existed for five hundred years, and is said to house a Kannon statue cast from natural gold that Murasaki Shikibu treasured. I stayed overnight at this headman's house, and the master's oral tradition was as described above - they had lived wealthy lives in the past, though this is not apparent now. Large antique items such as sword boxes were visible, and there was a bow and arrows on a spear rack that appeared extremely old, perhaps passed down for hundreds of years. When I asked to examine them, they were bow and arrows as shown in the diagram.

I am fond of ancient artifacts and have seen weapons from ancient times in various provinces, but I have never seen arrows like these. Since I do not know the sukagari shooting technique, I do not know what purpose these arrows served. They might be arrows used in hikinome archery, but in any case, they should be called rare.
