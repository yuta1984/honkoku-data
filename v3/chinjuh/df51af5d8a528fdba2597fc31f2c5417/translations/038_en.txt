I saw that girl using a naginata against her brother of about twenty years old as her opponent, leaping here and running there, calling out as they fought. The vigor was extraordinary and made for first-class entertainment. Though women's martial arts, especially among peasants, might not be something to praise, I could sense a spirit like that of men. Women's martial arts is something completely unknown even in dreams in the upper provinces, central provinces, and western provinces - the difference in customs is beyond description. Since ancient times, Kanto warriors have been known for their valor, and the saying "one Kanto warrior equals five warriors from the Kamigata region" is indeed true. In these peaceful times, not forgetting martial arts is called true warriorship, yet even among those born into samurai families, many lack proper dedication. "Floating world" is indeed an apt name.

Until not so long ago, the topography appears to have been as shown in the diagram. During frequent floods, the Tone River cut straight through as it does now, so it appears that the main rice fields of Horie Village in Shimosa Province became submerged in the sea. The villages recorded here are ancient, and the details of olden times are unclear. The other villages and settlements appear to have been established over the past two hundred years by developing reclaimed land from the sea or tidal marshes into rice fields and settlements.
