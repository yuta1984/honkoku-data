There was also an imperial command saying "let him take that cup as well," and it was granted to him. This too is preserved and treasured by the family. When I examined these two items, the imperial cushion was made of unfamiliar silk that at first glance appeared to be domestic weaving, but upon closer inspection was Chinese brocade of extremely rare quality. The imperial cup has maki-e lacquerwork depicting cranes, tortoises, pine, bamboo, and tachibana oranges, indescribably magnificent. In addition to these, it is said he also received an imperial boat. This person called "O-Dai" must have been blessed with extraordinary fortune to receive items that would become family treasures for generations to come, bringing honor to his descendants—truly enviable.

There is also a license permit for using hawks, written in a mixed script of Chinese characters and kana, in extremely fine calligraphy. There is a large imperial seal that appears to be the national seal. This document does not detail the circumstances of why it has been preserved and transmitted.

Tohoku Matsukawa Village [area spanning present-day Higashi-Komatsukawa, Matsue, Matsushima, Chuo, Osugi and other districts in Edogawa Ward]
Jukakuzan Genpo-ji is a Jodo sect temple with Amida Buddha as the principal deity, founded by Fukko Kanchi Daishi, with a vermillion seal grant of six koku. It is only known to be an old temple; its origins are unknown and it has no notable treasures.

Iouzan Zensho-ji is a Shingon sect temple of new establishment, with Dainichi Nyorai as the principal deity and a vermillion seal grant of six koku. Even when asked about its origins, they are not clear, though there must be some reason for its high status. This temple's vermillion seal land is extensive, yet with only six koku in assessed value, there are sixteen farming families attached to the temple.
