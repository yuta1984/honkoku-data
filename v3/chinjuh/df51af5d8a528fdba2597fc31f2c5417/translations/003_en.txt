Sumida Village [Sumida Ward]

The local people call it "Suma Village." The Sumida River, from which the name derives, forms the boundary between Adachi District and Katsushika District north of this village. It is quite distant from the current Sumida River, and now it has been filled in with only a small stream remaining. However, the fact that people from all villages call it "Old Sumida River" suggests that this is the true site of the Sumida River that was celebrated as a famous place in our province and featured in ancient poems.

The Edo Sunago records the Arakawa as the Sumida River. Since it is a river that flows along Sumida Village, it seems reasonable that the name Sumida River was attached in this area. However, considering what the local people say, we should conclude that the Old Sumida River is the true site.
