Adachi and Katsushika Districts are villages that are eighty percent paddy fields, and in the dry fields they cultivate many vegetables specifically for sale to Edo. Since wheat cultivation is minimal, about half of the middle-class and above peasants eat rice as their main meal. Regarding clothing, particularly among women, the differences in social status are very pronounced. Also, in this temple precinct there are many large trees, and nearby there are places where herons have built their nests, with young birds visible. In the lower branches, many sparrows roost, and sparrow nests can be seen here and there. Finding this curious, I asked the resident monk, "What is the reason for so many sparrows near the heron nests?" He told me this story: "The sparrows eat the parasites from the herons' feathers, which pleases the herons. Their relationship is very good. Particularly, ants gather at heron nests, causing difficulty for the heron chicks, so the herons rely on the sparrows to ward off this ant problem. When snakes come to the sparrow nests to take their young, the herons protect them. They depend on each other mutually—herons and sparrows are quite harmonious." This was the first time I had heard such a thing about birds and beasts...
