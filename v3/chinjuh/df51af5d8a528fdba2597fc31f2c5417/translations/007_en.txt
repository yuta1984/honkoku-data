Shibue Village [parts of Higashi-Tateishi, Yotsuki, and Higashi-Yotsuki in Katsushika Ward]
Chōetsuzan Saikōji Temple, Shingon sect. This temple contains the grave of Kasai Saburō Kiyoshige. Due to repeated floods, it has collapsed and the ancient grave is damaged. Now it is called Kiyoshige Inari and has a small shrine. Kasai Saburō is recorded in the "Azuma Kagami" and was a warrior who prospered during the time of the Kamakura Right General (Minamoto no Yoritomo). He held dominion over this Kasai region and appears to have been born here. These seem to be genuine graves.

Kami-Kinegawa Village [The local people call it "Kinegawa." It should be "Kigegawa." Also called "Kimōgawa"] [around Higashi-Yotsuki 1st-3rd districts, Katsushika Ward]
Seiryūzan Jōkōji Temple, Tendai sect, founded by the Great Master Jikaku, with principal deity Yakushi Nyorai created by Great Master Dengyō. Shogunal seal of five koku, the temple grounds are tax-exempt land and extremely vast. They pay several dozen koku of tribute rice.

When Tōshō-gū-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) conducted the conquest of Kasai, this place became his military headquarters, and all that land was granted to this temple, which is why it is so expansive. One-third of the village appears to be the temple's tax-exempt land. Among the temple treasures, there are precious portraits of Gongen-sama (Ieyasu) and Taitoku-in-sama (Hidetada) painted by Daiyū-in (Iemitsu) and granted to this temple, according to the resident monk. Out of reverence, I did not view them. There is also a bronze Shō-Kannon excavated from the ground, said to be an ancient Buddha statue.
