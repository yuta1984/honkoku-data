Although all things with form naturally possess innate nature, those with form have hearts, and where there are hearts, there is both wisdom and foolishness. However, ability and inability are matters of particular circumstance: herons, being large in form, cannot ward off small ants; sparrows, being small in form, cannot defend against the threat of snakes and so rely on herons. That they help each other's shortcomings with their abilities is truly admirable. While such things exist, even though I have reached seventy years of age, the fact that I still consider my own thoughts to be correct and am not without ego is a matter of great shame. 《Marginal note: Herons have no crying voice, but make many sounds with high pitch from their throat. The ancients called this "crying" and also "boiling beans"》

Kami-Chiba Village
Mount Nissho, Fugen Temple, Shingon sect, founded by Dharma Master Hōkū during the Kōan era

Until this year, over five hundred years have passed. The principal Buddha image is called Kasuga and is the work of a Buddhist sculptor—Yakushi Nyorai (Medicine Buddha). Within the temple grounds stands a stone pagoda of a person called Kasai Rokurō Shigeuji. Besides this, there are other old stone pagodas, but all their inscriptions have worn away and disappeared. He was probably of the Kasai Saburō Kiyoshige clan. In ancient times this was temple grounds with extensive temple holdings, and neighboring villages such as Fugen Temple Village were said to be part of this temple's sustaining lands.
