Having completed the tour of East Kazusai, when I reflect deeply on the merits and demerits of the local climate and soil, Adachi District's natural conditions are markedly better than those of Toshima District, and Katsushika District is even better than Adachi District. Particularly, the natural environment of East Kazusai consists of the finest land in the country, where everything from the growth of various grains to vegetables surpasses other provinces. The colors of trees, grasses, and flowers are like those of the Kinai region, and moreover, the annual tax burden is lenient compared to the Kansai and Chugoku regions due to benevolent governance, so it should be called the finest land in Japan. However, some villages appear prosperous, while others do not seem particularly so, and among them are some where the overall condition of the village is not good at all. Though they share the same natural environment and derive the same benefits from the land, when I inquired thoroughly into why there are differences in prosperity among farmers, I learned that when village customs are bad and there are just three to five people in the village whose conduct is improper, people easily fall into bad ways. Before long, the young people of the entire village also adopt these customs, becoming not only negligent in their work but also falling into nighttime entertainment. Their profits from rice fields and farmland diminish, and within five to seven years, their land becomes barren and their fields are taken by other villages, gradually leading to poverty. In any country, one must never be careless about customs and morals.
