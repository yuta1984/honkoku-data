The fact that the river has become elevated due to sediment accumulation becomes a disaster for villages during floods, but at least during droughts like this, one should devise ways to easily draw water by taking advantage of the river flowing at a high level, ensuring not even a single grain of rice loses the benefit of the land. The area of Nishi-Kasai is difficult to rescue from flood damage, but it is a place that never suffers from drought damage. Despite flowing near the mouth of the great Tone River, fearing drought and not utilizing the land's advantages is like the common people's proverb about "storing unhulled rice yet starving and thirsting." When one considers the dedication of the people who squeezed sweat from their bodies, cut grass, and tilled the fields, it is too painful to watch and one must close their eyes and pass by.

　　Upon reflection, while the height of floodwaters cannot be calculated as a human affair and thus is difficult to remedy, for droughts there are several methods to provide relief in this area that can be applied for years to come.
　　　　Shimo-Koiwa Village [present-day Higashi-Koiwa 1-5 chome and Minami-Koiwa 1-8 chome, Edogawa Ward]
Seijūzan Zen'yōji is a Shingon temple of the New Doctrine school, serving as the head temple for thirty-one branch temples, with a vermillion seal income of ten koku, and Jizō Bodhisattva as its principal deity. It has a Fudō hall in its garden and is a fine temple with spacious grounds. Among this temple's treasures is something called "hoshi" (star), constructed like a relic pagoda with the object housed inside the tower. At first glance, it resembles the jewels that come from Shari Beach in Tsugaru, Ōshū.
