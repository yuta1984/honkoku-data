Same village [Sumida Village - around Mukōjima, Sumida Ward]
Seiryūzan Rengeji Temple, New Shingon sect, principal deity Amida Nyorai, shogunal seal of seven koku of rice. This temple houses a statue of Prince Shōtoku. According to the temple's founding record, it was the protective deity of Hōjō Tsunetoki. Originally, this temple was located in Sasuke Valley in Kamakura, but Tsunetoki's son Yorisuke became a monk and, since this location was his father Tsunetoki's domain and he preferred quiet places, moved the Rengeji Temple from Kamakura to this location and enshrined the statue of the Prince. In ancient times it had magnificent temple buildings, but during the Tenshō era (1573-1592), the Chiba family of Shimōsa split into two factions and fought repeatedly, leading to the temple's decline from that time.
