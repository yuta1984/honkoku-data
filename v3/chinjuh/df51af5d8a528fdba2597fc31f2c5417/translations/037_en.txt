He had lost his father early and served his mother with great devotion. When I inquired in detail, I learned that although he had two older brothers, the family was poor and had no wives, so Tora performed women's work, drawing water and cooking rice to help ease his mother's burden. Hearing of this man's great size and strength, a sumo wrestler named Tamagaki Gakunosuke came repeatedly, wanting to take him as a disciple and make him a professional wrestler. Friends also urged him repeatedly, but since his brothers were absent, he thought of his mother's hardships and would not accept their urgings. His mother, since he was her youngest child, loved him deeply, calling "Tora, Tora" and treating him like a small child. Though Tora was a large man,

he would say "Yes, yes" and never disobeyed his mother's words. Those who saw this did not laugh but were moved with respect. His mother also passed away about ten years ago, and now he has gone to Edo where he engages in small trade as his occupation. I heard he is a man of thirty-some years this year. It is said that loyal retainers necessarily come from the homes of filial children - he is a reliable man, and moreover, being of great strength, there would be many uses for him. I do not know the details of his current conduct. The above was heard in an evening conversation with the village headman, and it would be regrettable to let it pass unrecorded, so I write it down here.
