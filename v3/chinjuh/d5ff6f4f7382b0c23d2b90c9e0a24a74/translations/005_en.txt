○Hirai Shōten: Located in Shimo-Hirai Village, Katsushika District. The bettō (administrator) is Myōgasan Tōmyōji Temple of the Shingon sect. Three ri from Edo. It is a branch temple of Konrenin in Kanamachi Village. The Shōten Hall faces south and is two and a half ken square, with a worship hall of six by three ken. There is a plaque of Kangi-ten, written by someone in the fifth month of Bunka 11 (1814). Behind the main shrine is an offering hall, two ken by one and a half ken. To the side is a small Daikoku shrine made of stone, facing east. In front are stone Niō (guardian kings) and a stone torii. North of Daikoku is the grave of Eryū, the hōin (high priest) who revived this temple. There are four torii for the main shrine. The first torii is

made of plain wood with a Kangi-ten plaque by an unknown calligrapher. The second torii is vermillion. The third torii is stone with a Kangi-ten plaque, also by an unknown calligrapher. The fourth torii is located above the ferry crossing of Shimo-Hirai Village, facing west, with a "Shōten-gū" plaque written by Tenchū Ikei. Beside the second torii is a shrine to Suwa Myōjin, facing east, four by three shaku, under a large ginkgo tree. There is spring water to the east, with a wisteria arbor above. Suwa's torii is made of stone and faces east. The Inari shrine is outside the third torii and is a small shrine. Tōmyōji Temple is located to the east of Shōten. The main hall faces south and is nine by seven ken. The principal deity, Fudō Myōō, is said to be made by Priest Rōben, the same as the principal deity of Mt. Ōyama in Sagami Province.
