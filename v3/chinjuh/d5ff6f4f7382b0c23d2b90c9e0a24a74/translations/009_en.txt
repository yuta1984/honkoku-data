It is called Enmei-in. It is a branch temple of Enmyō-in in Hikonari Village. Enmei-in is also a branch of Enmyō-in in Wakaoka Village, Shimōsa Province. Tsumakoi Gatatsu is a branch of Tōfukuji in Fudesaki, Shimōsa Province. The Kokūzō statue is said to be carved by Kōbō Daishi and to have emerged from a place called "Chiyoba-ga-fuchi" on the old Tone River. The hall is four ken square in Kyoto measurements, with verandas one ken wide on each side, tiled roof, and of good construction. The principal image is only opened for viewing in the years of the Ox and Tiger; it is not normally permitted to be viewed, and a standing statue of Kokūzō is placed in front of the tabernacle. There is a plaque with the three characters "Kokūzō" on the front worship area, inscribed "Buppō Shoki Sesshū Gokoku Shitennōji Shari Hōdōin Onjun haishyo."

To the right facing the Kokūzō Hall is the main hall, connected by a corridor. The principal deity is Fudō Myōō, and in front of the hall is a small Matsuo shrine with a stone torii and stone lanterns. Also behind the Kokūzō Hall is a Shōtoku Taishi Hall, one ken square with a stone statue. There is also a small Inari shrine. The bell tower is to the left facing the front of the Kokūzō Hall. Many people make pilgrimages on the 13th of each month. Hikokura Village is a two-and-a-half gō territory at the edge of the old Tone River. Villages called Sashikura, Hikone, Hikoito and others are all located along the riverbank. There are six ferry crossings near this river: Hanawada-no-watashi, Hikoe-no-watashi (from Nichōme Village), Hikokura-no-watashi (from Tsurusone), Kami-Hikona-no-watashi (from Hachijō), Dōba-no-watashi (from Kakinoki), and so forth. Boats also run from Edo to Hikokura via the Nakagawa route.
