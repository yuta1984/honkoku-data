The claim that it was the tutelary shrine of the Handa family's estate is erroneous. According to the old records of Sensōji Temple, in Kanpō 1 (1741), a legal dispute arose when Handa Inari was enshrined at Kongōin within Sensōji Temple. The gist of what is recorded in the documents is as follows:

○Kanamachi Konren-in: Located in Kanamachi Village, Katsushika District, called Hōgosan Kongōhōji of the Shingon sect. It is a branch temple of Sanpōin in Kyoto, with Dainichi Nyorai as the principal deity and Senju Kannon nearby. There is also a Taishi Hall within the temple grounds, and it is one of the four Shingon-Shingi temples of Kasai. It is a little over three and a half ri from Edo.

○Kanamachi Katori: Located in Kanamachi Village, Katsushika District, the main shrine is three ken by two ken. The connecting hall is one and a half ken by two ken, the worship hall is three ken by two ken, and there are many subsidiary shrines. The bettō is Kichijōin, a Shingon temple in the same village. Puppet theater performances are held on the shrine grounds, and sometimes curtains bearing the imperial crest are hung. It is three ri and twenty chō from Edo.

○Yūgao Kannon: Located in Iizuka Village, Katsushika District, the principal deity is Shō Kannon, and the hall is five ken square with a thatched roof. There is a plaque reading "Nō kyū sekan ku" (able to save worldly suffering) with the donor listed as Master Okuda. There is no bettō; it belongs to the village headman named Sekiguchi Sōtarō, who assigns a hall keeper. The site of the Kannon Hall is said to be the burial place of Muraoka Gorō Yoshifumi. It is three ri and thirty chō from Edo.

○Hikokura Kokūzō: Located in Hikokura Village [present-day Misato City, Saitama Prefecture], of the Shingon sect, Myōjōzan.
