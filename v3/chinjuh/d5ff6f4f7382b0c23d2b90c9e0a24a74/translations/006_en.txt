There are monthly exhibitions on the 3rd and 28th of each month. The calligrapher of the "Myōgasan" plaque on the main hall is noted as Kengatsu Sōta. The main hall faces south, and to the southeast of this is a Jizō hall facing west, three ken on four sides. The mountain gate is on the south front of the main hall and measures three by two ken.

North of the fourth torii is the house of village headman Taguchi Genzaemon, where it is said that Tōshō-gū (Tokugawa Ieyasu) once rested. The family preserves his portrait along with portraits of meritorious retainers and a waka poem in the hand of High Priest Tenkai. Taiyū-kō (Tokugawa Iemitsu) also stopped by, bestowing a sake cup and cushion, which are also preserved. Every year on April 17th, these are displayed for public viewing.

○Shibamata Taishaku: Located in Shibamata Village, Katsushika District. It belongs to the Nichiren sect as a branch temple of Hokkeji Temple in Nakayama, Sōshū, and is called Kyōeisan Daikyōji Temple. It was founded during the Kan'ei era (1624-1644). Around the Enpō period (1673-1681), a person called Shōzenba from Nakayama's sub-temple administered this temple concurrently and accomplished its revival. It is handed down that this temple had a carving by Saint Nichiren and a principal deity for prayers, but their whereabouts were lost. However, in the first year of Tenmei (1781), when the ninth-generation priest Nikkei was repairing the dilapidated buildings, he found an old board measuring two shaku five sun in length, one shaku five sun in width, and about five bu in thickness from above the ridge of the main deity hall.
