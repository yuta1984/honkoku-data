Therefore, casual worship is not permitted. There are many pilgrims on kōshin days. The festival is held on February 6th with thousand-scroll dhāraṇī practice, music, and children's memorial services. It is five ri from Edo.

○Handa Inari: Located in Kanamachi Village, Katsushika District, three ri and twenty chō from Edo. The bettō (temple administrator) belongs to the Tendai sect as a branch of Mokuboji Temple in Suda Village, called Nisshōzan Sanpōin.

The main shrine is two ken square, and the sacred body is said to be a figure wearing armor. The honji (original Buddhist form) is Eleven-faced Kannon, and the doors are opened annually on June 22nd for mushboshi (airing against insects). The connecting hall is one and a half ken by one ken, the offering hall is three ken by two ken, and the worship hall is six ken by three ken, with a painting of a dragon on the ceiling. It bears the inscription "Eikō Fujiwara Norinaga kin[...] kō kai kō Fujiwara Yasutoyo kei." To the left of the shrine is a fox hole with a small shrine built there. The reason it is called Handa Inari is that during the time of head priest Junkyō, a person called Yōjin, the younger brother of Handa Tan'ami, fell ill and requested prayers from Junkyō. When the shrine under Sanpōin's administration fell into ruin, he had a new shrine built and donated it for the purpose of these prayers, which is how it came to be so named.
