He possessed countless soldiers and his skill with bow and arrow was limitless. Depending on one's perspective, prosperity is indeed endless. However, this is merely temporary worldly glory in this lifetime.

Now, so that the great matter of the afterlife might be peaceful, he arranged his flowery sleeves and served as companion twenty-one times. After this, there was no doubt that he would visit the Pure Land of Amida Buddha.

Here at Yasaka Shrine lived a great villain named Emon Saburō. When Kōbō Daishi visited Yasaka Shrine and sat down to rest in the worship hall, Emon Saburō broke the Master's begging bowl into eight pieces. Grieving over this bowl, the Master renamed Yasaka Shrine as Hachiōji Shrine.

Emon Saburō had eight children, and though the Master initially thought to kill them all in one day, he decided that such a great villain should be made to suffer longer, so he killed the eight children over eight days. The Eight Mounds, as they are called, are located in Ebara in Iyo Province. Seeing this, Emon Saburō shed tears of sweat, became even more agitated, threw himself westward, awakened religious devotion, and served as companion twenty-one times. Being such a great villain, the Master would not show his face to him. Thinking this would not suffice, Emon Saburō finally circled seven times, at which point Kōbō Daishi showed his face and said, "Though I am one who has committed great evil deeds, this matter of circling in forward and reverse order..."
