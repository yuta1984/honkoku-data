[Concerning the Two Eminent Disciples]

Among the many disciples Kūkai had, there were two who were particularly outstanding. Their conduct and wisdom were such that the world could not fathom them, and no person could measure their depth. One possessed wisdom beyond comprehension, surpassing all people. Kūkai said to him: "My secret treasury [of teachings] lies at the bottom of that box of the three robes. Seek out its profound meaning." The other spent his entire life seated beneath a tree and upon a stone at Kongōchō-ji Temple in Tosa Province. Even when storms came and the wind raged, his mind of meditation did not stir from its stone seat; birds built nests upon him, and wild animals became tame around him — the Chan stream and the cave window remained long and tranquil. One should know that the two virtues of samādhi (concentration) and prajñā (wisdom) were manifested in these two holy men. Now, since Tosa was Kūkai's birthplace, he made a journey down to Sanuki Province.
