【On the Reappearance of Huiguo Following His Passing into Nirvāṇa】

Now, this venerable monk [Huiguo] was a teacher and guide to the Emperor himself, and was as a father and mother to the nation. That all people—high and low alike—should grieve and mourn was no more than what was to be expected; indeed, even the very grasses and trees seemed to take on a hue of sorrow. And yet, on the very night of his passing into nirvāṇa, there came a wondrous and most rare divine visitation. Kūkai, overwhelmed by his grief, entered the hall of practice, and holding back his crimson tears of sorrow in the sleeves of his robes, concentrated his heartfelt devotion to the very depths of his being, and throughout the entire night continued his devotional recitation without pause.
