They drove everyone onto the beach, showing no signs of compassion whatsoever, and posted guards to watch them strictly. At that time, Kanō said to Kūkai, "Though we send letters to the provincial governor, they are not accepted. What will become of us? This is deplorable. Our situation has reached an impasse. At this point, please, Master, try sending a letter yourself." When he said this, Kūkai replied "Very well then," and took up the brush in place of Kanō. His writing was concise, and its reasoning was sublime. When the provincial governor opened and read it, tasting its meaning again and again, he realized it was truly the brushwork of an incarnate sage, and said, "What a foolish ordinary being I am to have been tormenting such an excellent transcendent person during this time." He repented his previous wrongs and immediately released the ship from surveillance, and instead treated the people with care and comforted them from their fatigue of the sea journey.
