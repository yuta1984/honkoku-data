【On the Granting of Tō-ji Temple to the Great Master】

On the nineteenth day of the first month of the same fourteenth year (of Kōnin, 823), the imperial envoy Fujiwara no Ason Yoshifusa — holding the ranks of Dainagon, Junior Third Rank, acting Commander of the Right Imperial Guards, and Minister of Popular Affairs — was dispatched to bestow Tō-ji temple upon Kūkai. This temple had originally been founded by Emperor Kanmu at the beginning of his transfer of the capital to this site, for the purpose of protecting and pacifying the royal capital. Thus, the granting of the temple to the Great Master at this time was also done with the intent that he would serve as guardian of the royal capital. Kūkai thereupon constructed a great sutra repository, within which he enshrined in full all the implements transmitted from the Eight Patriarchs, along with the Mandalas of the Two Realms, the paintings of the Assembly of the Ocean of Sages, sutras, commentaries, and Dharma writings.
