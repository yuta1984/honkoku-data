And so, from the Emperor himself down to the common people, there was not a single person who did not press their palms together and bow their head in reverence to Kūkai. So moved was the Emperor that he appointed Kūkai to the position of Lesser Sōzu (Junior Bishop). His Imperial devotion grew ever deeper. That the Way of Shingon came to be so profoundly revered was, in one respect, born from this very moment. After the successful completion of the ritual, Kūkai ceremonially sent off the assembled sacred beings. The true Zennyo Ryūō (the Good-Woman Dragon King) was thereupon kept and enshrined in the pond of Shinsen'en, and a solemn covenant was made, beseeching her: "Until the dawn of the three assemblies under the Dragon Flower Tree [when Maitreya descends], protect this land and protect our Dharma."
