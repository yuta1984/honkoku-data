Thereafter, following the instructions of that hunter, Kūkai made his way to Kii Province and arrived at the bank of the Ki River. This is the site of what is now the Jisonin of the Administrative Office (Mandokoro). As he traveled about surveying the mountains and fields, the black-and-white dogs that the hunter had led in the land of Yamato appeared from nowhere and, upon seeing Kūkai, came to him with familiarity and affection. Feeling all the more encouraged and heartened, he took the dogs along as his companions and climbed up into the mountains. Before long, he arrived at the edge of a wide marsh on a broad plain. Surveying the character of the land, he saw that to the east and west, the mountains ranged in the shape of a reclining dragon, with waters flowing eastward; and to the north and south, the mountains coiled like a crouching tiger——
