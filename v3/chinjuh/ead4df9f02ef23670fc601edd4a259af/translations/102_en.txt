After this, in the first month of Kōnin 4 (813 CE), the Venerable Dengyō sent the Venerable Enchō as his emissary and formally became a disciple of Kūkai. Thus the bond of master and disciple between Kūkai and Dengyō was no shallow thing, and Saichō was recorded as a disciple in the dharma (*juhō no deshi*).

Regarding this matter, in later generations there have been many who say things such as "Dengyō was not a disciple of Kūkai," but this is not so. In the degenerate age of the latter days (*mappō*), people's spiritual faculties have become clouded, and they place their biased attachments foremost, compounding fault upon fault and mocking one another over these matters. This is truly lamentable. This is truly to be feared. In ancient times, the world was honest and upright, and people's hearts were profound and subtle. Those possessed of virtue recognized virtue in others, and they mutually held one another in the highest esteem, so it is told.

One was a Buddha, the other a Bodhisattva. One became the master, the other the disciple. Know that between them there was not even a hair's breadth of separation.

Now, the Venerable Kūkai increasingly propagated the Esoteric teachings with great vigor, and he remained at Jingoji Temple on Mount Takao.
