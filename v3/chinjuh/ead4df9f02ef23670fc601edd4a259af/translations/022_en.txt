Thereafter, at the age of twenty-two, he ascended to the ordination platform hall (kaidan-in) of Tōdai-ji Temple in the southern capital (Nanto), and received full ordination (gusoku-kai). He again changed his name and was called Kūkai. His dharma transmission became increasingly profound and venerable, and deeply contemplating the precious nature of Buddhism, he wrote about the inadequacies of secular teachings, compiling a work called "Rōko Shiki" in three volumes to show to people. This is the present-day "Sangō Shiiki" (Indications of the Goals of the Three Teachings).
