It seems so. "Come, first show it to me," I said, but he replied that it was hidden in a chest and he didn't have it here. "Just write a preface for me. I'll place it at the beginning," he said. I thought to myself: opening texts to write prefaces and viewing mountains to compose scenic poetry has been what people do since ancient times. But to hide the content and ask me to write a preface—what an absurd thing for a person to say! However, since this was a moment that pierced my heart tonight, I couldn't tell if it was a dream or reality. I took up my brush before that person. Truly, even with a brush as large as Mount Sumeru and an inkstone as vast as the ocean, it would not be sufficient. Surely what I can record amounts to but a single speck of dust in the kalpa of dust motes. I only wish to avoid errors and stick to truth, to guide the innocent ones of this world and quickly lead each of them forward.
