The Retired Emperor, too, was deeply grieved, and he sent an imperial rescript to the disciples, including Jitsue, offering heartfelt condolences in the form of an imperial elegy. It is said that the words of the decree were most august and solemn indeed.

Furthermore, the many eminent monks each went their separate ways — some took up the role of guardian priests (*goji*) for noble households, while others resolved to take up residence at various branch temples (*bessho*) — and they departed one by one from the cloud-capped peaks of Kōyasan. However, in accordance with Kūkai's dying wish, it was Sōjō Shinzen alone who remained behind on Mount Kōya.
