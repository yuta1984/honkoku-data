【On the Debate of the Eight Schools, and the Matter of the Grand Master Manifesting as a Golden Tathāgata】
【Illustration】

After dwelling at Tōdai-ji Temple for a time, the master Kūkai returned once more to the capital, where he continued his ritual practices and spread the teachings of Shingon's esoteric mysteries. Now, this teaching, it is said, represents the secret treasury of the Tathāgata and the innermost realization of all the Buddhas. Kūkai presented to the throne a memorial setting forth the doctrine of attaining Buddhahood in this very body (*sokushin jōbutsu*), and proclaiming that in governing the nation and benefiting the people, this teaching surpassed the Seven Schools.

At this time, the scholars of the various schools, who were engaged only in the exoteric teachings and had never tasted the waters of the Dharma that is esoteric Buddhism, raised objections and refused to accept this claim. It was as though insects of summer were doubting the existence of ice. And so Kūkai was summoned to court, and the eminent and virtuous monks of the various schools were likewise summoned, and in the great hall each was to set forth and debate the profound principles of his own school.
