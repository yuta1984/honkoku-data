The monkey placed his wife and daughter at a waste paper storage area, so with the family business they did washing and laundry to raise the daughter. Seeking marriage prospects, he rented a shop in Ryogoku Yonezawa-cho 8-chome. There was a beautiful geisha in the neighborhood who would go to mansions today and to pleasure boats tomorrow, constantly changing her large-sleeved kimono and silver chest ornaments and combs, with hair ornaments as large as carrying poles. The family envied this lifestyle, and with parental bias thinking their daughter was as good as anyone, though not in the Fujima or Nakamura dance styles, they had her learn dancing and made her practice with the intention of sending her to work at mansions and pleasure boat excursions to earn money.

At the oil merchant's, there was an only daughter named Osome, and [someone called out] "Sister, don't fall!"
