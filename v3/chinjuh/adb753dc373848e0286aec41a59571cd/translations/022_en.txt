【Temple No. 66 - Unpen-ji】
Thousand-armed Kannon
No. 66 - Unpen-ji - 2.5 ri
Coming from far away to this temple at the edge of clouds
Now I see the sun and moon from the foot of the mountain
Bessō. Tsuji

【Temple No. 67 - Komatsuo-zan Fudōkō-in Daikō-ji】
Yakushi Buddha
No. 67 - Komatsuo-ji - 1 ri
Looking upon Komatsuo-ji where small pines were planted
The wind of dharma teachings blows
Hara. Shutsusaku Kannon-ji

【Temple No. 68 - Kotohiki Hachiman-gū → Shippō-zan Shinkei-in Sai-kondō】
Amida Buddha
No. 68 - Kotohiki - 2 chō
The sound of flutes, wind through pines, playing the koto
Singing and dancing are all voices of dharma

【Temple No. 69 - Kannon-ji】
Kanzeon Bodhisattva
No. 69 - Kannon-ji - 1 ri
Since Kannon's great compassionate power is strong
Please lift up even heavy sins
Kōka. Nagareoka. Yoshioka

【Temple No. 70 - Motoyama-ji】
Batō Kannon (Horse-headed Kannon)
No. 70 - Motoyama-ji - 3 ri
Who planted these flowers at Motoyama?
Though spring may end, they become offerings
In this area there are many Nichiren sect lay practitioners, lodging is difficult
