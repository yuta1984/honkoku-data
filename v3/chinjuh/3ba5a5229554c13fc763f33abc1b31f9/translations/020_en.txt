The area between the Ayase River spans about one ri, and from the middle section to Shimōsa Province is about one and a half ri, or roughly one ri and twenty chō. The land is as flat as if tatami mats were spread out. There are tens of thousands of chō of expansive rice fields, and now during the height of rice planting season, old and young, men and women, even children are working together, singing and chattering. This scene brings such joy to the eyes that even the people under the sage kings Yao and Shun could not have been like this. My old body felt as if it had returned to youth, and I composed this poem:

What is there to lament, even though I grow old? / To encounter such an auspicious reign

Kyūzaemon Shinden [Adachi Ward]
In this place there is an old tree called the "Bent Pine" with fine character.
It is said to be a pine tree that was praised by Lord Tokudō-in (Tokugawa Yoshimune).

Sano Shinden [Adachi Ward]
On the banks of the Naka River there is a small shrine called Yanagi Inari. Many willow trees flourish there, and it overlooks the flowing water, creating a somewhat scenic spot. However, it is nothing particularly special. The route through Adachi District covers only one-fifth of the district, so it does not reach famous places, historical sites, or scenic locations, which is regrettable. Particularly in this area, everything consists of newly developed rice fields (shinden), so there are no historical sites whatsoever.
