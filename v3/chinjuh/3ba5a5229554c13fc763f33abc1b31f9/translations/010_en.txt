Shimonu-mada Village [Adachi Ward]
Miyagisan Emyō-ji, Shingon sect of new tradition, with a vermillion seal grant of twenty koku. This area has good land and yields several koku of tax rice. However, the resident monk has no knowledge whatsoever of what historical connection merited the granting of the vermillion seal. Perhaps it was received in a dreamlike state. There are no treasures to speak of from the beginning.

Miyagi Village [Adachi Ward]
Ryūtō-zan Shōō-ji, Jōdo sect, with a vermillion seal grant of ten koku. The principal image of this temple is the Six Amida. When it was carved, the monk Gyōki used the leftover wood from the main carving to sculpt a seated Amida Buddha statue and presented it to the manor administrator of Adachi. A stone marker was erected designating it as the "Six Amida Original Amida Buddha." It looks like a pharmacy signboard. Looking at the temple's origin story, it differs greatly from what was first heard in Toshima Village. The only daughter of a person called Adachi manor administrator Miyagi Saisho was sent as wife to Toshima Saemon, but she was criticized by her mother-in-law for the inadequacy and paucity of her dowry gifts. Ashamed before her husband, she threw herself into the Arakawa River and died. As a result, twelve lady attendants also followed her in death. The place where these corpses were found and recovered is called the Forest of the Twelve Devas.
