The seventh prince of Emperor Seiwa was exiled to this province and passed away in this place. Ryouhon buried him in the land of Ushi Gozen. As a memorial sign, he erected a stone Buddha of Shakyamuni. In Yowa 1 (1181), Minamoto no Yoritomo (holding the court rank of nii) managed this shrine, and Chiba Tsunetane donated rice fields. Hojo Ujinao commanded his senior retainer Odawara Kageshū to donate shrine lands according to precedent. The land called "kyūmen" currently in front of the main shrine is that very land. On the 28th day of the sixth month of Tenbun 7 (1538), Emperor Go-Nara bestowed the imperial title "Ushi Gozen."

The above founding legend records the outline of the printed version.
