In Edo, all money changers are made to contribute three sen daily (at the exchange rate of seven thousand mon per one ryō of gold, this amounts to three mon in permanent currency, about twenty-one mon in today's money, they say). Half of this is offered to the authorities annually, and half is set aside. With these funds, regular dredging of the moats within the castle's kuruma (defensive perimeters) is to be carried out. Recently there are those who have brought this matter to the attention of higher authorities, saying this plan will surely be implemented. Also, Lord Matsudaira Aki-no-kami performed a financial assistance of seventy thousand ryō four years ago. However, this year again he has been ordered to provide another seventy thousand ryō for river construction projects in Shinshū and Kōshū provinces. Consequently, his household is beginning to complain of financial strain. I, having connections to families in Kai province, engage in such casual conversation to record examples of the state of the world in these times for future remembrance. The particularly cold weather we experienced yesterday and today was due to snow and hail that had accumulated in the areas around Fuefuki, Matsumida, Kōshū, Enshū, and Izushū, as I later heard. Particularly at Mount Nikkō, they say about three shaku of snow has accumulated. This is why it was so cold.
