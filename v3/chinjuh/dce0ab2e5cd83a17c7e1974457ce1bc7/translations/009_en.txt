　Record of Pilgrimage to Handa Inari【Katsushika Ward】
On the 15th day of the 6th month of Bunka 14, year of the Ox【1817】, intending to visit Handa Inari, I finished breakfast and left the inn at Hamacho, crossed Ōkawa Bridge, and from the residence of the Lord of Mito at Koume, took a single road along the irrigation canal heading east for some time. On the roadside there was a signpost reading "Right: to Edo Ōkawa Bridge, 30 chō; Left: to Shinjuku Matsudo, 2 ri." Here there is a path - going south leads to Kinegawa Yakushi, and going north leads to Umewaka Sannō, as can be seen. Passing this point and continuing further, there is a crossroads. Going straight east leads to Shinjuku, going south and crossing the irrigation canal bridge slightly, then following the southern edge of the water leads to Ichikawa. The left side behind, toward the northwest, is the Hashiba Highway.
