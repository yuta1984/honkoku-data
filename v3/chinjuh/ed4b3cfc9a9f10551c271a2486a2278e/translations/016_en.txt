The Emperor heard of this situation and said, "It would be good if there were prayers that could bring rain not only to the capital but to the entire realm. Summon Kūkai." Kūkai of Tō-ji Temple came to the imperial court. He then decorated an altar in front of Shinsen-en Garden and prayed, but even after three days no rain fell, and even after praying for seventeen days, not a single drop fell. Kūkai found this strange and entered into meditation to observe the situation. He discovered that Shubin had sealed away all the dragon deities of the world and confined them in a single vessel. Here there was Zennyo Ryūō (Good Woman Dragon King), who resides in the Anavatapta Pond in India and is a bodhisattva of higher rank than Shubin. Shubin is a bodhisattva of the third stage, while Zennyo Ryūō is a bodhisattva of the [Anavatapta] Pond
