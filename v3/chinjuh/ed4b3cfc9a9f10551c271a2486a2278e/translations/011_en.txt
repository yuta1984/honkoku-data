Emperor Saga, in the 11th year of Kōnin, granted Kūkai the position of Sōzu with the rank of Dentō Daihōshi. In the same year, in the first month of the 14th year, he granted Tōji Temple to Kūkai and Saiji Temple to Shubin. However, Kūkai was in the midst of ascetic training throughout the provinces. During this time, Sōzu Shubin served close to the Emperor and displayed various miraculous powers.

On one occasion when Shubin appeared before the Emperor, raw chestnuts were served as sweets. Shubin took some and put them in his robe. After a short while, when he took them out, they had become roasted chestnuts. Beginning with the Emperor, all the court nobles and retainers regarded this as miraculous. The Emperor requested tea.
