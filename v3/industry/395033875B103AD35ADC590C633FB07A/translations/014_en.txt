Although I have recorded the usual methods of sericulture as above, one should carefully adapt according to the customs of each town and household.
However, if women do not dismiss their traditional practices all at once, but rather consider the preceding instructions to become well-versed in the general principles of sericulture and apply their accumulated skills and experience over many years, there will be no occasional crop failures, cocoons will be abundant, and profits will be substantial.
