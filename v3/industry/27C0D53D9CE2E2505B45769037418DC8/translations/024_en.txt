Generally, cocoons whose excrement is not removed become moldy and rot, resulting in poor cocoons. Mulberry leaves should be fed abundantly regardless of the prescribed amount. If done so, even though seven days is mentioned, they will begin spinning in five or six days. Such good cocoons can be produced. However, while raising them, all incense burning and any odors, good or bad, should be avoided in the house. Particularly, the smell of eel causes the insects to die, and the smell of sardines causes severe illness. If mulberry leaves touched by hands that handled tobacco are fed to them, the color will inevitably change. Although there are many such taboos, the essential point is to strictly avoid anything with fragrance.

Also, if they show poor coloration and suffer during the final seven days before spinning, sprinkle just a little sake on the mulberry leaves with the tip of a tea whisk and feed them once or twice to observe the results.
