When such homes succeed in raising them, it is said that those households will surely prosper. In homes where the family is not impure, or where upper-class people raise cocoons, gold silkworms will certainly grow well.

1. Generally speaking, the practice of raising and nurturing silkworms has been most abundant through generations and has spread year by year. However, in distant countryside humble homes, there are mostly only "earth cocoons" - cocoons that are large in form and flatly drooping. These have cotton-like fibers resembling fur that grows on moldy, rotted matter, standing up woolly and clinging in countless numbers, with dull colors - the white ones are truly dull, or they are yellowed, and in any case

Earth cocoons are also called "cotton child cocoons," but not limited to these - generally, those that are large and poorly formed, with several insects inside and having cotton fibers, or cocoons with holes that cannot be drawn into fine silk threads, are all commonly called "cotton cocoons."
