The furnace bedding paper should be touched by hand to test - after stroking it about three times, your hand should be so hot as to be nearly unbearable. However, there will be various skilled practitioners regarding this point.

First, cocoons dried for about one day during good weather when the sun is strong, when silk is drawn directly from them as they are, produce thread with particularly good color and luster, clear and magnificent. As mentioned above, cocoons put over fire during bad weather lack luster and are inferior to fresh cocoons.

male butterflies continuously mate starting from the edges. After about three hours of mating, separate them and remove all the male butterflies completely. The female butterflies will then walk around laying eggs on the paper. Leave them for about six hours then discard them. On the remaining paper will be tiny objects like poppy seeds packed densely. These are next year's cocoon seeds. This is called seed paper. Initially when arranging the insects for mating, it should be called fukusa paper (silk paper). Writing it as "grass paper" is wrong. The meaning here is that white is read as pure, and cloth is read as modest.
