Writing about "exchanging with fukusa paper" also refers to this matter. Another document states that the silk merchant and the brocade merchant are two people who are star spirits of women engaged in sericulture. Therefore, when these insects lay eggs, the way the eggs are attached - making them encircle in a round pattern - is called "making stars." What was mentioned above about laying on leaves is careless. There is also something called "layered making." This occurs with poor-quality seeds when the insects are in a bad mood and reluctantly lay eggs on top, piling them up in one place. These seeds are called "whitened seeds" and are also known as diseased seeds. However, they need not be discarded
