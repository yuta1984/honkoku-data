Writing "exchanging with hakushi paper" also refers to this matter. Also, in certain texts, it is said that the two figures Kurehatori and Ayahatori are the spirits of the stars Kengyū and Shokujo (Altair and Vega), and therefore when these insects lay eggs, arranging them in a circular pattern is called "making stars." Saying that eggs are laid "on leaves" in the upper areas is inappropriate. There is also something called "layered laying," which occurs with inferior seed quality when the insects are in poor spirits and reluctantly lay eggs on top of each other in one place. This seed is called "whitened seed" or "diseased seed." However, there is no need to discard them as they naturally wear away and fade over time. Compared to humans, they are like premature babies - seeds that will not develop. However, the way to prepare paper is to fold back about three to four bu on all four sides of one sheet to make it like a box for placing the butterflies. The paper that merchants use for counting has about one sun folded and hidden, but ultimately, eggs laid densely to the very edges of the paper are poor for storage and look unsightly. Also, paper to be stored until next year should have small amounts placed between roof rafters and such places.
