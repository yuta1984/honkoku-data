First, regarding the timing of selecting worms mentioned above and the quantity to place in cocoon frames: Generally, for one cocoon bed, the appropriate amount is about one handful of worms held in both hands, or about one large tea bowl full. This proportion is good. Lower-class farmers say about one rice bowl full, but when so many are placed together, many defective cocoons such as dumpling cocoons, coarse cocoons, and attached cocoons are produced, and many do not become proper silk cocoons. As for cotton cocoons, if raised with great care, even these golden drum cocoons produce cotton that becomes the finest material for high-quality clothing and the top grade of inner cap cotton. It is best to raise them with utmost attention. However, even among silk cocoons, depending on weather conditions, and also with repeatedly

Silk is drawn with water, while cotton is processed by peeling with lye - there are various methods for this preparation. For lye, mugwort is the best, or early rice straw. Millet husks are medium quality. Ordinary straw is the lowest grade. Generally, lye made from this produces poor results, so it is not used.

First, the method of constructing cocoon beds is difficult to teach through writing. It should be transmitted orally. However, one can understand by seeing just one example.
