Generally, while they are small and cannot be picked up by hand, use something like thin chopsticks to carefully move them without causing injury. When the insects grow large, hand-picking becomes manageable. However, hands should be thoroughly washed before handling. Cocoons caught with hands that have oil or salt from scratching the head or stroking hair will inevitably be poor quality. On the seventh seven days (49th day), from this point they enter their peak feeding period, so feed them mostly with whole leaves. For mulberry leaves, use thirty-four to thirty-five cups in the basket over seven days, adjusting the amount as needed for feeding. During this time, change their location five times.
