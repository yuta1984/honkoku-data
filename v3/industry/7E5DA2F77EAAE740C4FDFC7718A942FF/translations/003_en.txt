Mulberry: A shrub cultivated in fields, whose leaves are harvested and used as food for silkworms. There are many varieties, but those with large leaves are considered the best.

Kaimei Yōsan Ōrai (Enlightened Sericulture Correspondence)

The origins of sericulture began in China during the imperial dynasty of Emperor Huangdi of the Youxiong clan. In our country, it started from the age of the gods with the deity Ukemochi-no-kami and continued until the era of human emperors as a pastime of the imperial court. However, as the world progressed, it became a means of providing relief to the common people and furthermore developed into an important agricultural occupation for farming households.
