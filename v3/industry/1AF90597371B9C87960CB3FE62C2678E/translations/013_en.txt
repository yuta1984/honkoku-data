【Lower Right, Main Text】
The first rest period, which they dislike until after being given mulberry leaves, is best at seven to eight days. Too early is not good. When silkworms emerge, those that are black and elongated are good. Even if black, those that are thick and short are bad silkworms. When red-colored ones or those with only large heads emerge as hairy silkworms, all in the rearing bed will die. Furthermore, when raising silkworms,

【Lower Left】
one should thoroughly pick up the excrement, place mulberry leaves where it was removed, continue picking for about two days, then place fresh mulberry leaves without leaving any and raise them up. Most importantly, cocoons should be large, well-formed, and resilient, yielding reliable dry cocoons of five to ten kanme per sheet of eggs. Also, silkworms that are raised slightly young show only good qualities upon examination.
