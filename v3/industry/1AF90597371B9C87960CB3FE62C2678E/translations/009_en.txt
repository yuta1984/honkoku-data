Covering with night robes or futons, or placing braziers near fire or inside paper partitions to provide strong warmth - silkworms treated this way appear excellent until the fourth molting, but from the fifth stage onward they have difficulty eating mulberry leaves, become uneven, and result in poor harvests. This should be deeply understood. Generally, in the way of sericulture, failures due to negligence are extremely numerous. Therefore, although women are meticulous...

Illustration of handling cocoon clusters

Sometimes fresh cocoons are soaked in salt. In large jars, inside and outside, on bamboo, paulownia leaves are laid down, salt is sprinkled over them, and the top is sealed with mud. They are removed after a day, put in a cauldron, hung on frames, and reeled.

...these are non-viable seeds. Also, those that are too beautifully water-colored are late-harvested. They must be understood as inferior quality. One should learn not to create such quality variations. In recent years, many merchants, not knowing their seed sources, mistake poor seeds for high quality and make their living this way, causing grief to countless people.

This is a way of not fearing divine retribution. How great must such sin be? Many cultivators do not understand weather irregularities, nor can they distinguish between their own mistakes and poor seeds. With utmost honesty and sincere heart, they should conduct business so that sericulture may prosper eternally.
