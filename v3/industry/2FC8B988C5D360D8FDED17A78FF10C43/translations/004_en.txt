When there is no moisture in summer and the seed color becomes pale, but autumn brings moisture and the seeds become plump, these are good seeds.

△Seeds should be soaked in snow water drawn during the cold season, placed in buckets or basins. Change the water the next day, and after three or four days have passed, on a clear day, lift the seeds and dry them on grass mats or spread straw and place them on top. Most importantly, turn the underside of the seeds toward the sun. In the evening, wrap them in dry white paper and move them to the area near the hearth inside the house, using bamboo as shelving to dry them high up. When making fire, ensure that the heat does not directly reach the seeds. There are many taboos regarding seeds.

Do not place lamps or braziers under the seeds. Also, do not brew tea. After approximately seven to eight days have passed, the seeds will be thoroughly dried. Place these seeds in paper bags and store them in small boxes. It is also good to hang them high on the back boards of the bedroom. Avoid the influence of metal. If storing them in a chest, never place them together with swords or short swords. The seeds will die. If tea is brewed nearby, the seeds will die.
