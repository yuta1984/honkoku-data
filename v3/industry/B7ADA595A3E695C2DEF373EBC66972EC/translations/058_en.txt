In such regions, they naturally settle into silk thread production and pay no attention to silkworm egg production, believing that silkworm breeding cannot succeed unless it is in riverside areas.

　　[Note] Silkworms originally have the natural disposition bestowed by creation to form cocoons, transform into pupae, become moths, and leave egg varieties for the following year. When they occasionally become maggot-like (defective silkworms), this is an aberration. Although there may be slight variations depending on the quality of mulberry leaves, there is no principle that prevents them from transforming into moths. If one were to establish silkworm rooms and cultivate mulberry by selecting open land, not sparing fertilizer, working diligently at cultivation, and applying great care to raising silkworms, then compared to other regions that produce silkworm eggs,

That is to say, rather than spending money on fertilizer and laboring in cultivation to produce silkworm eggs, only to unfortunately face ruin, it would be better to use naturally flourishing mulberry trees to produce silk thread without debate over moths or maggots, and find security in this occupation.
