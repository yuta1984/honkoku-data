　　[Note] Silkworms by nature form cocoons, transform into pupae, become moths, and leave egg seeds for the following year - this is their fundamental nature endowed by creation. That they occasionally become maggots is an aberrant transformation, and while there may be slight variations due to the quality of mulberry, there is no principle stating that moths cannot develop. If one establishes silkworm rooms, selects open and accessible land for mulberry cultivation, spares no expense on fertilizer, endeavors in cultivation, and applies great care in raising silkworms, then there would be no great difference from other regions that produce silkworm eggs
