　　　Chapter 8
The silkworm egg papers that have already finished laying should be attached with string and hung in a cool, quiet room. At that time, some among the egg varieties may accidentally hatch in response to the season. This is called "re-silkworms" (sai-san). Those that have hatched should be inspected and thoroughly swept clean with a broom, then hung as before, and one must not neglect to prevent mice and ward off foul air.

　　　Chapter 10
Some people immerse silkworm egg papers in cold water for storage. This is merely a common custom. The method is to first weigh each paper to determine how many monme it weighs and record this, then select a clear day during the cold season and immerse in clear water for three days, then hang in a quiet room for several days until thoroughly dried, then store in baskets. At this time, weigh each paper again, and if it matches the weight initially recorded, one knows the moisture has been completely removed.
