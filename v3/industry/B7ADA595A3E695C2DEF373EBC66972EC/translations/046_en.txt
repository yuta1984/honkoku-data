　　　Chapter 8
The silkworm egg papers that have finished laying are attached with string and hung in a cool, quiet room. Then, some eggs within respond to the season and hatch unexpectedly. This is called "re-silkworms" (saisan). When such hatching is observed, sweep thoroughly with a broom, hang as before, and never neglect to prevent mice and ward off foul air.

　　　Chapter 10
Some people immerse silkworm egg papers in cold water for storage. This is merely common custom. The method is as follows: first, weigh each paper and record the amount, then choose a clear day in the cold season and immerse in clear water for three days. Then hang for several days in a quiet room until well dried, and store in boxes. At this time, still weigh each paper, and when it returns to the weight initially recorded, you know the moisture has been completely removed.
