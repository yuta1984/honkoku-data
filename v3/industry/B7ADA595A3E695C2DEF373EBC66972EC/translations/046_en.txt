　　　Chapter 8
The silkworm egg papers that have finished laying are attached with strings and hung in cool, quiet rooms. Sometimes among the egg seeds, some respond to the seasonal conditions and hatch unexpectedly. This is called "re-silkworm" (sai-san). When such hatching is observed, thoroughly sweep with a broom, hang as before, and never neglect to prevent mice and ward off foul air.

　　　Chapter 10
Some people immerse silkworm egg papers in cold water for storage. This is merely a common custom. The method is: first measure and record the weight of each paper, choose a clear day during the cold season, immerse in clear water for three days, then hang for several days in a clean, quiet room until thoroughly dried, then store in baskets. At this time, measure the weight of each paper again—if it matches the initially recorded weight, one knows the moisture has been completely removed.
