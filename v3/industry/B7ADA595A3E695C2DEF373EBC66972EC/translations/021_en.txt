　　　Chapter Twenty-Two
For the natural mulberries in mountain valleys, no one knows how many years they have weathered. From about two hundred years ago until about one hundred years ago, a variety called Bandō mulberry was cultivated, but because its nature was coarse and poor, none have been cultivated for the past hundred years. Its remnant varieties grew in mountain valleys and became large trees over the years, but they are of the poorest quality, so no one deliberately harvests them. However, when mulberry leaves are insufficient and inconvenient, they are reluctantly used,

　　　Chapter Twenty-Three
In cultivating low stumps, there are naturally differences depending on their height and low positioning, varying in character according to the terrain. This can be understood from the fact that even in Kōzuke Province, Sai County favors low ones, while other mountain villages prefer high ones.
