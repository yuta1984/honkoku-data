　　　Chapter Two
When this is inspected the following morning, if approximately twenty to thirty, or even one to two hundred downy silkworms per paper ([Note] there are variations in timing and quantity depending on temperature) are observed to have hatched, then use a feather broom to sweep them off the paper most gently, sprinkle millet grains, spread them appropriately in baskets so the downy silkworms are not harmed, and feed them finely chopped mulberry leaves that have been sifted and had their stems removed with a winnowing basket (the size of the finely chopped mulberry leaves should be approximately one bu).

　　　Chapter Three
