Section 1: Construction of Silkworm Rearing Rooms
Silkworm rearing rooms should face southwest or east-west. They must not face northwest, as there is concern about dampness and humidity. The room should neither be extremely large nor extremely small. If too large, it will be difficult to protect against cold; if too small, it will be inconvenient for dealing with heat. Therefore, partition it to about two and a half ken (approximately 4.5 meters), drill an opening of more than four shaku (about 1.2 meters) in the ceiling with provisions for opening and closing to regulate the yin and yang qi. There should be windows and doors on both the south and north sides. When the south wind is fierce, open the north door to release the sultry air; when the north wind is cold, add heat from fire to warm the room. The temperature of the silkworm room should be limited to between 60 and 70 degrees. The southeast should be kept open and unobstructed, while the west should be shaded. If there is no shade, protection must be provided during times when the evening sun blazes intensely, as it may harm the silkworms. However, if proper care of the mulberry leaves is maintained, this harm may perhaps be avoided.
