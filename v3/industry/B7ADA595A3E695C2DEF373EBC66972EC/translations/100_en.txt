First: Construction of the silkworm rearing room - Southwest or east-west orientation is good. It should not face northwest.
　There is concern about dampness and humidity. The room should not be too large, nor should it be too small.
　If too large, it is difficult to prevent cold; if too small, it is inconvenient to escape heat. Therefore,
　partition it to about 2.5 ken in size, open about 4 shaku of the ceiling area and install opening and closing mechanisms
　to regulate the yin and yang qi. There should be windows and doors on both north and south sides. When the south wind is strong,
　open the north door to release stagnant humid air, and when the north wind is cold, add fire to
　warm it. The temperature of the silkworm room should be limited to between 60 and 70 degrees.
　The southeast should be open and bright, while the west should be shaded. If there is no shade,
　the intense evening sun must be blocked as it may harm the silkworms. If the mulberry
　care is well-maintained, this concern may perhaps be avoided.

Second: Method of selecting silkworm eggs - Generally, silkworm eggs should be laid uniformly across the base paper, with dense
　texture, and the adhering egg grains should be plump and moist, not dried out.
