　　　Chapter 9
When the silkworms have settled into sleep, feeding them once after giving no mulberry for about a day and night is called "naka-kuwa" (middle feeding). Giving mulberry when about 80% appear ready to awaken is called "furi-kuwa" (shaking feeding), and feeding after another half day has passed is called "kuwa-tsuki" (mulberry attachment). Even if 80% have not awakened uniformly, early is preferable and late is harmful. Indeed, not only for the first sleep but for all other sleep-awakenings, it is best to be early.
