Following the third day method. On the fifth day, divide into three sections. Give mulberry four times, with the same amount. If warm, it's acceptable to give five times. Until the eighth day, follow the above example. On this day, divide into six sections. After about nine days, sleeping silkworms will appear. Once sleeping silkworms are already visible, reduce the amount of mulberry and give it frequently. When awakening silkworms begin to appear slightly, do not give immediately, but wait for all to awaken uniformly, then give little by little. Generally follow the conditions of egg cleaning. This is called "mulberry attachment." On the second day of mulberry attachment, remove the silkworm bed. At this time, divide into six sections.

Section 6: It is bad to feed young silkworms with mulberry flowers. Mulberry wet with rain and dew should not be used for young silkworms, nor for mature or old silkworms.

Section 7: From lion rest to hawk rest is nine days, from hawk rest to boat rest is six days, from boat rest to garden rest is seven days, from garden rest to cocoon making is seven days. From egg cleaning to cocoon making takes thirty-six to seven days, up to forty-two to three days. This is the general outline, but there are seasonal temperature variations, different mountain and river climates by region, varying skill levels among rearers, and the frequency of mulberry feeding also changes accordingly. Therefore, it is difficult to provide uniform examples. The silkworm bed must not be allowed to accumulate excessively.

When discarding this, until the third molting, use feather dusters and such to gather the silkworms and move them to separate containers before removing. For boat and garden moltings, do the same by hand.

Section 8: Procedure for Raising
Select old silkworms whose throats have become transparent, bind bamboo to both sides of mat-like or straw bag-like materials so they cannot bend, arrange straw rings continuously inside, and store in the ceiling. Place the selected old silkworms in this device, and after they finish making cocoons, wait four to five days before harvesting them.

Section 9: The time limit for transformation into pupae is four days if warm, extending to five or six days if cold.

Section 10: The time from becoming pupae to transforming into moths varies in speed depending on seasonal temperature. Pupae that become maggots always have black spots. Those with visible black spots invariably become maggots within about ten days. The cause of becoming moths versus maggots lies entirely in the mulberry used during rearing. Mulberry from sandy, gravelly, thin soil along rivers produces many moths. High, fertile land produces many maggots. It also depends on wet or dry land and that year's drought or heavy rain.
