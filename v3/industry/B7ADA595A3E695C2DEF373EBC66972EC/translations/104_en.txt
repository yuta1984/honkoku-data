【Right Page, Upper Section】
Ichihei Mulberry

The leaves are thin and round, the stems are dense with
many leaves

【Right Page, Lower Section】
Yanagida Mulberry

The leaves are thick and long, the stems are sparse with
　few leaves. The budding is the
　earliest. Depending on that year's climate,
　　there is concern that mulberry fruits may be
　numerous and leaves
　　few

【Left Page】
Kobata Mulberry

The stems are long and the leaves are thin and long.
Budding is late and soft.

This is a late mulberry that does not mind the richness or poorness of the soil.
After the "garden rising" period, if this mulberry is not used, many silkworms
become maggots. However, since there are also
geographical and wet-dry climatic conditions of the land,
what can be done about the fact that this is not necessarily always the case?
