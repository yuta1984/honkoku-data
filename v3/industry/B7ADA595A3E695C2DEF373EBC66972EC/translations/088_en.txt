　　　Chapter 2
Figure No. 2 shows how, as some time passes and the egg is exposed to the atmosphere, it gradually develops a silkworm body, causing the external surface to display its form and color. There are two depressions in the egg body, one large and one small. The large one naturally forms a depression and becomes oval-shaped because the silkworm body is curved inside, while the small one is connected to the silkworm's tail with a thread, designed for the silkworm to bite through and crawl out when it emerges. Most importantly, albuminous mucus is stored between the silkworm body and the surface skin.
