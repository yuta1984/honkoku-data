Up to this point, from one sheet of seed called "Chū Nyorai," applying two settings, producing 1.5 to 1.6 koku of upper, middle, and lower grades,
of which about 1.2 koku comes out as silk cocoons. Following this standard, the cocoons are uniform and the silk
quality is also good. In all the years up to now, there has not been a single year of failure.
This is all due to the principle of handling the sericulture sequence without mistakes, and the good or bad of each year
depends entirely on one's dedication. Therefore, I use my family's sericulture as
an example.
One: During sericulture, one should make keeping the household quieter than usual the foundation, and even servants
should refrain from quarreling and such improper things. If there is any carelessness, that year's
sericulture will not be profitable.
One: During sericulture, one should avoid death pollution. One should avoid going in and out of houses with misfortune or eating and drinking there
