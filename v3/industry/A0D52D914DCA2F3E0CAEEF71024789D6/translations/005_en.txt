Record of Silkworm and Mulberry Cultivation for Common Households, Volume 2
　　　　　　　　　　　　Tōoku [Northern Region]　　　　Written by Yamauchi Dōkō
　Generally speaking, in sericulture, selecting silkworm eggs is of primary importance. Generally in Ōshū,
　those produced from the main production area of Date, or those from
　Sawada Village in Kurihara District (coming from the house of a person
　called Heibei) are considered good. Generally, people who raise silkworms obtain silkworm eggs during the summer
　of that year, place them in white paper bags with one opening at the bottom (strings are attached to the edge of the silkworm egg paper for hanging.
　This is for ventilation),
　and hang them either above the floor of the house or in a clean location away from walls, leaving them there until early autumn.
　Then they are stored away in boxes, chests, and similar containers. After the beginning of the cold season of that year,
　a day of the Horse is selected (the use of Horse days in sericulture may be based on
　the annotation in Zhou Li by Zheng Xuan stating that "silkworms share the same qi as horses").
