Regarding this matter, the cargo should first be sent by English or French steam mail ships to the port called Marseille in France. Although the mail ships have high fares, other shipping routes take many days for the voyage, and if the opportunity is missed, there is the concern that the silkworm eggs will deteriorate, so this cannot be substituted for the difference in freight costs. These mail ships stop at Shanghai, Hong Kong, and if it's a French ship, also Saigon, Singapore, Ceylon, Aden, the Suez Canal, from where they travel by steam train to Alexandria, then cross the Mediterranean Sea again, arriving in Marseille in approximately fifty-two to fifty-three days. The shipping cost is approximately 135 francs for one box containing 250 sheets. (Since approximately 5.5 francs equals one dollar, this amounts to about 25 dollars per box. Regarding shipping costs, there is a recently published book called "Western Travel Guide, Foreign Edition" that should be consulted for reference.)
