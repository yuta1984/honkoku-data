The silkworm-raising regions of Western countries extend from Spain to France, from the Rhine River area to Italy, and to the vicinity of Constantinople, the capital of Turkey. These countries have climates that are generally similar and are considered suitable lands for sericulture.

Before our country opened its ports, in the West, Turkish products were considered first-class, followed by those produced in Italy, Spain, and Portugal, which were used as standard varieties. However, in recent years, as the silkworm eggs produced in Italy have greatly deteriorated, Japanese varieties have become even more superior, and silkworm egg production in the West is said to be gradually declining.

Through several years of recent experience, our country's silkworm eggs have become of world-class quality. In Italy and other places, when raising ten sheets of silkworm eggs, three or four of them must absolutely use Japanese silkworm varieties, and people have come to boast to each other about using many Japanese silkworm varieties.
