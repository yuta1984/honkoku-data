For coloring materials, the five plants - safflower, indigo leaves, dyer's knotweed, purple gromwell root, and madder root - are considered primary, and there are many other types of plants and trees used for dye solutions. Some combine this and that to make one color, or add two or three ingredients to create one color. As for intermediate colors, there are countless variations. Some have been devised by people throughout history and spread among society. All of these can be understood through inference.
