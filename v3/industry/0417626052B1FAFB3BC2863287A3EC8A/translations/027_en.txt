Kiori Ihen Volume Two
　　　How to Plant Hemp Seedlings
One: In fertile land, take one se (about 991 square meters), plow three times, then use three shō
　of seeds. Black-spotted seeds are considered the best. Early March is optimal,
　early April is moderate, and early May is the lowest grade. Generally,
　leave one plant every two shaku (about 60 cm). If planted too densely, do not cultivate but keep constantly clean.
　If left wild, it will bear fruit, and once it has bloomed, pull out the male plants.
　However, if you remove the male plants before they bloom, no seeds will
　form.
One: Prepare the field and plant in late February to early March in conjunction with rain.
　When hemp grows and spreads its leaves, weed it. Generally five shaku tall, when each plant reaches
　one shaku in height, use silkworm droppings as fertilizer - three shō per plant. If there are no silkworm droppings, aged
　human waste from the toilet works well as fertilizer. One shō per plant. During droughts, provide flowing water to each plant
