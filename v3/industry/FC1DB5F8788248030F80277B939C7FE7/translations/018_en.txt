Chapter Three《Marginal note: Procedure for mulberry seedling planting, with instructions on layering and planting methods》

For mulberry seeds, seek sufficiently high-quality mulberry seeds, plant one tree within the property grounds, keep it upright, and when the fruits fall, collect them in a silk sieve or similar, soak in water and wash by rubbing, then add plenty of ash and mix thoroughly so there are no clumps. However, for the seedbed, wrap with stable manure during winter, and in spring apply night soil, enrich the soil, level the ground, and at intervals of about one shaku, draw furrows with the tips of your toes, sow the mulberry seeds thinly, cover with about two to three bu of soil, place a little straw as a cover on top, and when the mulberry has sprouted

remove the cover. Also, the following spring during transplanting, plant at intervals of about three to four sun, add stable manure or compost and cover with soil. When the shoots emerge, apply night soil, and at that time leave one shoot per mulberry plant and cut off the tips. For layering, after rain dig to a depth of about seven sun according to the height of the mulberry tree, leave about five sun of the tip exposed and cover with soil, tamp down well, and in the second or third spring when plenty of roots have formed, carefully dig up without damaging the roots, cut leaving about three to four sun of root from the mulberry base, plant these mulberry seedlings at intervals of four to five sun like transplanting, with the cut end slightly exposed and covered with soil, and when shoots emerge
