Chapter 15 《Subtitle: Regarding the superior, medium, and inferior grades of the second molting rest period, and how to distinguish when various diseases appear in silkworms》

From the initial spreading, mulberry should be given frequently, and those that rest in the second molting on the seventh or eighth day should be considered superior. Those that rest on the sixth day would be medium grade. Those that rest on the fourth or fifth day should be understood as inferior silkworms. However, silkworms have various diseases. First, during the second molting rest period, when many silkworms appear white in color with nodes that release water when touched, one should know that none will remain good by the fourth molting. This occurs in silkworms that have been warmed from about the fourth day after initial spreading and rest early. Also, before the second molting, when raised coolly,

when applying mulberry and separating them, dead silkworms may sometimes be seen at the rear end of the silkworms. These are silkworms that were placed near the shoji screens and were affected by the garden's grain waves. One should understand that about 60% will survive to maturity. Also, during the bamboo tray period, many floating silkworms appear, and even when mulberry is applied, their heads become thin and translucent with a soot color. Even though mulberry is given, they use it as bedding mulberry and do not progress - none of these will remain good at maturity. This may be due to poor vitality of the breed, or it may occur in silkworms that were strongly warmed before the second molting. Also, during the tray period, when mulberry is applied, shriveled silkworms can be seen - four or five per basket.
