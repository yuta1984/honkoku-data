When the great wind began to blow, light appeared everywhere as if fires had broken out. From the upper town, it appeared as if fires had started in the lower town around Shichikenmachi area, and also appeared around one or two blocks from the harbor, and light was also seen around the Ichige area. From the lower town, it appeared as if fires had broken out around the upper town area, and the same was reported at the harbor. Perhaps the light was visible everywhere due to the force of the earth's energy being released. In rural areas, there were reportedly places where fireballs appeared to fly down to about the height of house eaves.

The same occurred in Edo, and around Yukiume area fireballs reportedly flew about.

The regular express messenger was passing through Koganehara during the great wind when he saw straight beams of light seven times in the direction of Edo. The same reportedly occurred during last year's earthquake.

From around Oyama toward the Shimotsuke region there was no particularly strong wind, and around Aizu it was reported to be so calm that the wind barely blew at all. To the north, [this calm extended] as far as the Iwaki region.
