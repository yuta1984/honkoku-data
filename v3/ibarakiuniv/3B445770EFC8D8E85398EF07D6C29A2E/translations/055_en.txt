Lightning struck the warehouse. The sake brewery building, which was about 12-13 ken long, was struck in the middle of its ridge. Although the carpenters tried to restrain [the owner] regarding the ridge beam that had been struck by lightning, Kihei would not listen and used it anyway - truly a frightening thing.

Lightning also struck the enoki tree at Kōtaiji Temple and the new residence area. In the lower town, it also struck Taishidō.

Also struck within the Hachiman Shrine grounds.

25th day: Scattered clouds in the morning, cool, clear weather (70 degrees)

26th day: Good weather from morning, with black clouds in the east. Clear and hot from after 10 AM (76 degrees, earthquake at dawn).

The lightning on the 24th also struck the Katō residence at Sakashita, and about three places in Minato. Two places in Ōnuki, two people and one horse in Nukada, a fish seller in Sugimura - all died instantly from lightning strikes. Also struck Mount Iwayoshi at Takisaka.

27th day: Morning fog. Hot from morning, great heat, as is usual during the dog days of summer (80 degrees, earthquake at dawn, 95 degrees at 8 o'clock).

28th day: Morning fog. Strong sunshine and great heat from after 10 AM, no wind. Almost unbearably hot. No wind even at night. Slight thunder in the evening (morning 82 degrees, 95 degrees at 8 AM, earthquake in morning, earthquake at 4 o'clock at night).
