2nd day: There were scattered clouds in the morning, and from past the 5th hour it became cloudy with intermittent rain. It rained all day with cold air, and the rain intensified after nightfall.

3rd day: It rained from evening with cold air. After the 7th hour the rain stopped and it became stormy weather.

4th day: Rain began falling from the 7th hour of morning with cold air. It stopped from the 7th hour.

From the 4th hour of night on August 25th, strong southeast (tatsumi) winds began, with thunder that gradually became a great storm, flying stones and roof tiles, toppling large trees, collapsing temples and shrines. A great tsunami brought seawater 4-5 feet above floor level at the seaside, washing away houses, with countless dead. Tsukuda Island, Horie, Nekozan, and Katada were completely swept away. Around Shiba Daimon, 5-6 chō burned. Offshore boats were driven ashore at Takanawa, Shiba, Tsukiji, and Teppōzu. At Tsukuda Island, several large ships were wrecked. The middle section of Eitai Bridge collapsed. The temple at Tsukiji was destroyed with many deaths. Fukagawa, Susaki, Sanjūsangen-dō, and the Kiba lumber district suffered extensive damage from high water, and 4-5 government boat warehouses collapsed. The buildings of the Odaiba fortifications off Shinagawa could not be seen from Takanawa. Along the Sumida River, riverside mansions and townhouses collapsed with extensive property damage. Various halls of Sensō-ji Temple were damaged, and several large trees in Okuyama toppled. Shitaya, Sakamoto, Negishi, Ueno, and Yanaka areas suffered major damage. Hongō, Koishikawa, Ushigome, Yotsuya, Kōjimachi, Akasaka, and Ichigaya areas [suffered] from the great wind...
