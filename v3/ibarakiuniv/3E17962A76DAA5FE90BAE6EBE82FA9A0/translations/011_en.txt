16th day: Frost falls, lingering cold, clear, strong west wind
(Marginal note: 35 degrees)
At daytime 9 o'clock (midnight), fire breaks out from the residence of carpenter Hanzō in Barōmachi [Bakurōmachi] 3rd district.
At that time the west wind was fierce and fire spread to the north side, burning up to the embankment edge of the south side 1st district. Tokiwa back street burned completely. Gion [crossed out "temple"] south burned completely. Eseki hall and hermitage remain. Flying sparks reached Shimogane-machi area and Nemoto-yamashita area, so they were preparing their houses [crossed out "yo"]. Fire subsided after 7 o'clock (after 4 AM).
64 houses on the main street and 23 houses in the back burned down.

17th day: Clear from morning, strong west wind. Wind stops in the evening.
(Marginal note: Color of sun | red)
Fire spread to Gion temple gate front and Tokiwa back street - number of houses burned unknown, said to be over 30 hearths.

From around night 5 o'clock (around 8 PM) on February 10th, fire broke out from around Anjin-machi in Nihonbashi, Edo. At that time the northwest wind was fierce, and the fire was stopped at Ise-machi, Nagahama-machi, Odawara-machi, Setomono-machi [crossed out "ame"], and Minami riverside. From the burning of Muromachi 1st and 2nd districts' east side to Hon-funa-machi, south of there is Yokkaichi, with 6-7 houses remaining on the north side. Edo-bashi warehouse compounds all burned down. Okina Inari shrine burned. Aomono-machi, Yorozu-machi, Komatsu-machi, Otowa-machi
