Fifth Month

1st Day　Rain from evening, heavy rain and cold at the fifth hour, unprecedented heavy rain from after half past the ninth hour until evening, rain stopped at dusk with north wind
(50 degrees)

2nd Day　Clear weather from morning, good weather all day

Nakagawa River flooded, boats stopped in the morning, water overflowed at Takisaka area and Nemoto area covering the entire plain, water reached Akanuma area, flooding similar to that of the Horse Year

3rd Day　Cloudy from morning, cold with north wind, light rain during the night

Due to the cold weather at this time, an official notice was issued stating that wearing lined kimono would be acceptable even on ceremonial days during this month

4th Day　Light rain and cold at dawn, light rain again from the fifth hour, stopped at half past the fourth hour, cloudy all day, extremely cold with northeast wind, strong northeast wind and rain during the night continuing all night
(50 degrees, 51 degrees after the ninth hour)
(New sake lees from Iso, one bale per one ryō, about 80%)

5th Day　Rain with northeast wind from evening, earthquake at half past the fifth hour, extremely cold, strong northeast wind, rain stopped at half past the ninth hour
(43 degrees, 40 degrees at the ninth hour)
(On the 5th, it is said that 2-3 ri inland from Ōta had 2-3 sun of snow, around Sukagawa and Yabuki had over 2 shaku of snow, and it snowed considerably in the Ōshū region)
(Frost falls)

6th Day　Frost and cold, clear weather, southeast wind blowing with scattered clouds, cold all day
(41 degrees, snow visible around Nasu mountains)

7th Day　Cold and cloudy from morning, northeast wind blowing, cold weather
(49 degrees)
