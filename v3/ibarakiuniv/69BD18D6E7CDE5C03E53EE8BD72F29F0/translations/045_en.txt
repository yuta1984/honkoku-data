The day before yesterday, the 26th, Lord Katata, Lord Satō, Lord Ichikawa, and other gentlemen from the Kōdōkan academy - some 600 to 700 people - suddenly went up to Edo. They brought weapons such as spears, guns, bows, and other armaments.

28th day: Light rain fell from evening. Humid and hot. Rain stopped from the hour of the Rabbit (around 6 AM), becoming lightly warm. Rain fell from the hour of the Dog (around 8 PM).
A merchant from Shinshū province says that the Shinshū area is extremely peaceful.
Brown rice: 4 to 3-4 shō per koku
All goods are extraordinarily expensive compared to this area.

29th day (Hange-shō): Heavily overcast until morning, becoming hot. Hot weather from half past the hour of the Cock (around 10:30 AM), hot in the evening.
More academy students are gradually going up [to Edo].

Last day of the month: Deep mist in the morning, then cloudy. Rain began falling from half past the hour of the Cock. Repeatedly raining and clearing.
Four or five people were arrested around Kasama. They were said to be from Mito.
In Edo, goods continue to be expensive. Brown sugar costs about 70 ryō - truly rare in all of history. Sesame oil is also extremely expensive, so nowadays people mix 4 gō of top-quality vinegar with 6 gō of oil for lighting, and it works just the same, so they buy from oil merchants.
