　　　　　　　　　　　　2. Markets
　The Fish Market Co., Ltd., which handles fresh seafood and other marine products, has annual transactions amounting to 715,420 yen. The Maruichi Toyohashi Marine Products Market for salted and dried fish reaches 69,581 yen, the Toyohashi Consignment Transport Co., Ltd.'s produce market has annual transactions of 401,000 yen, and the Tosan Produce Market has transaction volumes of 99,000 yen.
　For daily necessities retail markets, there are two public markets in Shinkawa and Matsuba with sales totaling 394,018 yen. Additionally, there are private markets in Daiichi-Toyohashi, Hatchō, Takashi, Hanada, and Higashida with annual sales of 361,454 yen, directly serving citizens' needs, though this is still not considered adequate.

　　　　　　　　　　　　3. Rice Exchange
　Its origins are very ancient, beginning with the price exchange office (sōba kaisho) of the Meiwa period. After many twists and turns, it obtained government licensing in Meiji 27 (1894) and was established as a joint-stock company, combining settlement markets and warehousing operations. Recent annual trading volume in actual commodities has reached 399,526 yen, and it is regarded as an important economic institution not only within the city but throughout the Eastern Mikawa region.
