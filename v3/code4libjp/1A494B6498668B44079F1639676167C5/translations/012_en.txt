Through the city's encouragement of these activities, the numbers have gradually increased in recent years, with 1,446 cattle-raising households owning 1,578 head of cattle, and 581 pig-raising households with 1,191 head of pigs.
　Poultry farming has seen increasing demand with urban expansion and is mainly conducted by specialized farmers. There are 3,445 poultry-raising households with 504,347 birds, valued at 502,977 yen. With improvements in chicken breeds and advances in rearing techniques, this industry shows a trend toward further development. Annual egg production amounts to 52,143,455 eggs valued at 1,042,869 yen. The city serves as a distribution center for Mikawa eggs, with daily shipments to Tokyo and Hokkaido averaging no less than 28 tons, and now exports have begun even to the Philippines in the South Seas.

　　　　　　　　　　　　5. Fisheries
Since the western part of the city faces Atsumi Bay, fishing consists of shallow-sea fisheries. There are 1,158 fishery workers, and the main coastal catches include shrimp (12,720 yen), mullet (9,855 yen), and eel (6,358 yen). Manufactured products include kamaboko and chikuwa totaling 469,399 yen, dried nori seaweed (283,646 yen), and others. Grilled chikuwa is renowned nationwide for its excellent flavor and is famous as a specialty of the city. Kamaboko also surpasses other leading regions in both quality and quantity. Nori seaweed was previously shipped to the Tokyo area as raw material for Asakusa nori, but several years ago it began being marketed as Mikawa nori and processed into seasoned grilled nori for sale throughout the country, where its excellent flavor is welcomed by everyone.
　In freshwater fish cultivation using pond water, eel ranks first with an annual value of 278,310 yen, followed by carp (95,860 yen), Asanori (87,324 yen), and clams (50,676 yen).
　Furthermore, nori seed nets and clam seedlings from the cultivation farms are shipped not only within the prefecture but also to distant areas in Mie and Shizuoka prefectures. The total annual fisheries production amounts to 1,361,300 yen.

　　　　　　　　　　　　6. Forest Products
The city's forest products consist of lumber, bamboo, fuel wood, and other forest products from the eastern region, valued at 13,665 yen.

　　　　　　　　　　　　7. Mineral Products
These consist mainly of limestone, gravel, and clay for tile production, with annual production valued at 136,608 yen, showing a yearly increasing trend.
