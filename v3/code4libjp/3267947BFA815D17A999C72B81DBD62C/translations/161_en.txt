○Takashi Mountain (Takashiyama)　●Matsuba-shū (vol. 5) ●Ruiji Meisho-shū (vol. 2) ●Utamakura Aki no Nezame ●Moshiogusa (vol. 4, folio 23)
　　　　　　●Utamakura Nayose (vol. 19) ●Ruiji Meisho Hoyoku-shō (vol. 3) etc. classify it as being in Tōtōmi Province
　●Mikawa Moshiogusa states: It is the geographical feature of Suseyama Village in Yana District. According to one theory, it refers to the mountain east of Tateiwa.
　　Considering according to ancient records, it is located in Suseyama Village of Yana District, and is a border mountain between Tōtōmi and Mikawa provinces.
　　The western face is considered a famous place of Mikawa Province, and the main face a famous place of Tōtōmi Province. Both provinces have
　　poems about this famous place. According to oral tradition, the Mikawa section of Kokumei Fudoki writes it as "Takaishi Mountain." There are places with the same name
　　in other provinces as well. Someone says that Takashi Mountain is a famous place of Tōtōmi Province, not knowing that there are many places with the same name
　　throughout the provinces.
　　There are cases where provinces differ but famous places have the same name. This type is extremely numerous. The aforementioned Takashi Mountain has places with the same name
　　in four provinces: Mikawa, Tōtōmi, Settsu, and Izumi. All have famous place poems, appearing in Wakaura-yu
　　Chidori-shū. ●The theory in Sanpo-matsu agrees with this　●Futaba-matsu does not include Takashi Mountain.
　●Nihon Kanoko (vol. 6) states: Takashi Mountain is between Futagawa post station and Shirasuka post station. To the north are mountains, to the south is the sea.
