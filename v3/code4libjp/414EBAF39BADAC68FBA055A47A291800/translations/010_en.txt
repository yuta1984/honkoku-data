　4. Patient room glass doors shall not be completely closed except when instructed by medical staff
　5. Haircuts, bathing, cold water bathing, cold water massage, sunbathing, deep breathing, watering, exercise, etc. shall not be performed without permission from medical staff
　6. Loud voices and other noisy activities should be avoided as they disturb others' rest and are also disadvantageous to one's own treatment
　7. Moderate reading according to the degree of illness is acceptable, but excessive concentration or reading aloud is not advisable. Permission from the attending physician must be obtained
　8. Alcoholic beverages and smoking are harmful and useless and shall be completely discontinued
　9. Clothing, especially undergarments, should always be kept clean
　10. One's personal surroundings should always be kept clean and tidy, not soiling indoor or outdoor areas. In particular, pasting pictures on walls with paste is strictly prohibited as it damages the building. Also, do not confuse indoor and outdoor footwear, and do not discard paper scraps, fruit peels, etc. outside designated containers
　11. Buildings and equipment should of course be handled carefully, and trees, flowers, and animals in the garden should be protected
　12. Sputum should be spat into designated spittoons and never scattered elsewhere. When going out, collect it in paper and deposit it in designated containers after returning to the hospital
　13. Do not enter the nurses' station or other unauthorized areas, and do not freely visit other patient rooms
　　　Also, do not enter areas outside the walking zone within the hospital grounds
　14. Be sure to be in the patient room at prescribed times for examination, temperature taking, medication, etc., and especially maintain quiet for 10 minutes before temperature taking
　15. When there are abnormalities in a roommate's condition, immediately notify the nurse
　16. Visits and outings have many disadvantages for treatment, so they should be avoided as much as possible
　17. When unavoidably going out, obtain a leave permit from medical staff through the nurse and return it immediately upon return to the hospital
　18. Recreation equipment and books provided in the recreation room may be used freely, but may not be monopolized for long periods or taken outside the hospital. After use, they should be properly organized
　19. Do not possess books that are disturbing or harmful to morals, and avoid vulgar speech and behavior
　20. Do not engage in disturbing speech and behavior, distribute documents, incite others, or act in organized groups
　21. Do not engage in gambling, lending or borrowing money, or exchanging food and beverages
　22. Valuable items should not be carried if possible. Should any be lost, the hospital shall not be held responsible
　23. Always wash hands before and after meals
　24. Meals should be taken in the designated dining hall except for those with medical permission, and should not be arbitrarily taken back to one's room
　25. Do not place dishes and milk bottles directly over fire
　　　(Dishes that are burned and scorched are not easy to clean and are the greatest cause of dish uncleanliness. Also, milk bottles break easily when placed over fire, thus causing milk prices to rise, ultimately disadvantaging all patients. Therefore, value public spirit and be mindful of each other)
　26. Dishes after meals should be moderately organized, not left in disorder
　27. Maintain proper etiquette toward hospital staff, visitors, and consolers
　28. Do not give any gratuities to nurses or others
　29. If there are matters you wish regarding the sanatorium, please feel free to speak up

　　　　　　　　　　　　Guidelines for Fire Prevention and Natural Disasters
The greatest care must be taken with fire, and the complete absence of accidental fires must be ensured. Therefore, the daily requirements to be observed are as follows:
　　1. The use of open flames is most dangerous and is strictly prohibited
　　2. Lanterns are very dangerous, so leaving them in unoccupied rooms should be strictly avoided
　　3. Use only braziers provided by the hospital
　　4. Do not make excessive fires in braziers
　　5. Do not place braziers close to walls or place flammable items (such as wastepaper baskets, rags, etc.) near braziers
　　6. Remove fire from braziers and cover them by 8 PM
　　7. When using braziers all night due to medical conditions, notify the duty nurse and pay extra attention to fire safety
　　8. When temporarily leaving after lighting a brazier, always cover it
　　9. Portable stoves, etc. shall not be used except in designated locations
