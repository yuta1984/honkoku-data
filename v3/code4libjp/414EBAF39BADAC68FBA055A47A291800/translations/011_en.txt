◎Notice
　Those who violate these guidelines and are deemed unable to achieve the purpose of treatment may be discharged from the hospital.

　　　　　　　3. Patient Care Status
　　　　　　　　　　　1. Treatment
Inpatients are separated into different rooms according to the severity of their symptoms and gender. For severe patients who are deemed to require attendants, attendants are provided. The main focus is on nutrition, fresh air, and rest therapy. Moreover, through detailed and comprehensive guidance and treatment methods, and with constant perseverance and patience, efforts are made in mental training so that patients can endure long-term treatment.

　　　　　　　　　　　2. Meals
The greatest emphasis is placed on nutritional value, with attention paid to fresh cooking. Menus to be prepared are thoroughly assessed in advance using weekly menu plans before being provided. Items that are difficult to digest are avoided, and efforts are made to meet patients' needs as much as possible. In addition to regular meals, one chicken egg is distributed every other day each week, and one cup of milk is distributed every Wednesday.

　　　　　　　　　　　3. Comfort and Recreation
As mental guidance, a magazine called "Takayama" is published within the hospital. Contributions and criticisms are discussed from a position of freedom, serving as an aid to recreation. In addition to radio, phonograph, books, and go, for mild cases who need appropriate exercise, fishing in nearby ponds (officially permitted as hospital annexes) is available, or flower beds are set up in the garden to comfort hearts with seasonal flowers. Sometimes lectures on spiritual cultivation by pastors and others are also given. On holidays and memorial days, nutritious bread is provided to serve both comfort and cultivation.
