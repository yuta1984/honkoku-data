Preface to the Biography of Master Ono Kozan

Master Ono Kozan was not merely a great poet of our nation, but truly a great man possessed by our homeland, a patriotic loyalist during the final days of the Tokugawa shogunate. However, it is deeply regrettable that biographical works about him that are widely known to the world are extremely rare. It seems that the Master, in his daily life, did not wish to speak to others about his personal experiences, and only through his numerous poetry collections can we occasionally glimpse fragments of his life. By chance, there is a biography of "Kyōkyō Sensei" in Gamō Shigeaki's collection of biographies of great men, which describes the Master's youth in some detail, but it cannot yet be said to be complete. I too, when writing the historical accounts of Toyohashi City some years ago, endeavored to collect materials related to the Master, but most things went contrary to my intentions, and I ended up not achieving even half of what I had hoped.

Recently, Brother Ono Hōsui has conducted deep research into the Master's literary remains, and has further examined previously unknown secret manuscripts through the surviving family members, now completing this biography of the Master.
