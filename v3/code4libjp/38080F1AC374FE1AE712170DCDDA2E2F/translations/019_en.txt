　　　Transportation and Communication
　The current state of industry is generally as described above, and when observed from a broad perspective, it is still developing, but considering Toyohashi's history, remarkable rapid progress and development has been achieved in these twenty-odd years, creating a completely transformed impression. This is primarily due to the influence of transportation facilities, and Toyohashi has in fact become the economic and cultural center of eastern Mikawa.

　The recent freight and passenger conditions at Toyohashi Station, which serves as the main gateway, as well as other stations, generally show an annual increasing trend. The development of automobiles within the city is remarkable, and buses run extensively throughout the area with the city as their center. Our industrial sector continues to develop, and the improvement of citizens' economy is also notable. The Horaiji Railway, which could be called a sister line to the Toyokawa Railway, operates between Miwa Village and Kawai in Kita-Shitara District from Nagashino Station, the terminal of the Toyokawa line. The Taguchi Railway runs from Horaiji-guchi Station via Horaiji Village and Ebi-cho to Taguchi Town in Kita-Shitara District. The Atsumi Electric Railway, which runs through the Atsumi Peninsula, operates from in front of Toyohashi Station through Tahara Town to Kurokawara. The Nagoya Railway's city entry line starting from Nagoya has been completed, and city trams run from Toyohashi Station south to Yagyu Bridge and east to Higashida, providing great convenience. The Sanshin Railway, which had been a pending issue for over thirty years, saw the opening of its entire line in August of Showa 12, crossing between maritime Japan and mountainous Japan, reaching Iida City in Shinshū in just over three hours, connecting to Tatsuno Station on the Chūō Line via the Ina Electric Railway. As a trunk line connecting the back of Japan with the front of Japan, not only devout men and women visiting Toyokawa Inari, Horaiji, Zenkoji and other temples, but also general passengers frequently travel this route.
