　　　　Religion and Education
　The education of our Toyohashi City has recently reached a stage of remarkable progress and development, but regarding the general situation, particularly concerning facilities, we are still far from being satisfied. As of the end of April this year, the city has Prefectural Toyohashi Middle School, Prefectural Toyohashi Second Middle School, Municipal Commercial School, Municipal Higher Girls' School, Municipal Girls' Commercial School, and twenty-four elementary schools, as well as a commercial specialized school and youth school established for supplementary education. In addition, there are private schools such as Toyohashi Higher Home Economics Girls' School, Toyohashi Shoso Girls' School, Toyohashi Higher Practical Girls' School, Aichi Higher Japanese-Western Girls' School, Toyohashi School for the Blind and Deaf, Toyohashi Speed Arithmetic School, and Nursing and Midwifery School, as well as Toyohashi Kindergarten, Hanazono Kindergarten, and Sayuri Kindergarten. Furthermore, there are the Municipal Library and Zoo, and the Municipal Education Association and Education Society that plan and implement educational projects or directly aim at educational research.

　The Toyohashi Scholarship Foundation was established in October of Showa 2 (1927). To cultivate talented individuals for the future, it widely solicits scholarship funds and provides loans to those who would otherwise have to abandon their studies due to financial reasons. It has gone further to establish dormitories in the six major cities across the nation for these scholarship recipients and students from Toyohashi, reducing their individual burden and facilitating their pursuit of learning. It also advocates for lifestyle improvement and makes extremely significant contributions to society. Many other educational and academic research activities are conducted, all achieving considerable results.

　Regarding religion, how exactly has the religious sentiment of Toyohashi citizens been cultivated? While it is extremely difficult to describe this concretely here, they generally maintain an attitude of free faith under relatively sound critical judgment.
