construction from around the 9th year of Enpō (1681). Passing through Tenpaku and Godo stations to enter Tahara, here there are the ruins of Tahara Castle built by Toda Munemitsu during the Meiō era. The grave of Watanabe Kazan, a senior retainer of Tahara Domain who excelled in calligraphy and painting, was skilled in poetry and prose, and was well-versed in foreign affairs, is located in the grounds of Jōhōji Temple in the same town. The prefectural shrine Tomoe Shrine, which enshrines Kojima Takanori, ancestor of the Miyake clan, is situated in a corner of the old castle site. Also in the same town is the grave of Murakami Hansei, who served as chief administrator of Tahara Domain, researched artillery techniques, excelled in shipbuilding methods, and later rose to control domain administration. Katahama Beach is eighteen chō from the town. Other places including Shinmei Shrine, Ashi Shrine, Chōkōji Temple, Ōmu Stone in Izumi Village, Senpukuji Temple in Fukue, the stone gate of Cape Irago, Muramatsu, and at the mouth of the Toyokawa River, Jinno Shinden and Maeshiba - all have value as famous scenic spots and historical sites of Mikawa worthy of wide introduction.
