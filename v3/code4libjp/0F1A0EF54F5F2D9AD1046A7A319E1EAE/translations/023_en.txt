Hot winds (Heete Vluscruiende, meaning "scorching") come from vast desert lands. In the country of Egypt, this wind blows from the south or southwest direction. This is called "Khamsin." In Mecca it blows from the east. In Surat it blows from the north. In Basra it blows from the northwest. In Baghdad it blows from the west. In Syria it blows from the southeast. Among these, the most fearsome are those called "Samum" in Arabia and "Samgel" in Turkey. Also, the "Harmattan" of Guinea is of this type of wind.

Chapter 6: Land and Mountains

In general, the surface of land is extremely uneven. What protrudes upward becomes mountains and hills, what sinks becomes valleys, and what extends flatly becomes plains. Among the land, the lowest places are called seashores and the highest places are called interior lands.
