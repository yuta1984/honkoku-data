○Method for Tying Mountain Yam
Cut mountain yam thinly, sprinkle salt over it for a while, tie it up, then soak in water to remove the saltiness, and boil before using.

○Method for Making a Substitute When There Is No Wasabi
Mix ginger and mustard in equal parts and grind well. When used, the flavor is no different from wasabi.

○Recipe for Kōsen (Aromatic Tea)
Burnt rice 《Note: Remove from the pot, place on a grill over charcoal fire, roast well from both sides, grind with a mortar and make into fine powder》
White sesame powder (roasted)
Japanese pepper powder
Grind these three ingredients into fine powder for use. It is no different from commercial kōsen.

○Method for Pickling Okara (Soy Pulp)
When pounding and preparing miso, spread six to seven shō of tofu lees at the bottom of the miso tub, prepare the miso on top of it, and after gradually using up all the miso, take out the aforementioned lees. It becomes an exquisitely delicious taste.

○Method for Green Yuzu Pickling
Grate whole green yuzu with a wasabi grater, mix one shō of that pulp with two and a half gō of salt, and coat unblemished green yuzu with this pulp...
