○Method for Salt Proportions When Preserving Fish in Brine
When preserving fish, poultry, vegetables, or fruits in brine, the salt proportion is of utmost importance. The method is to place one chicken egg into the brine, and it will sink to the bottom. Gradually add salt from the side, and when the salt proportion becomes correct, the egg will naturally float up. Use this as a guide to stop adding salt, then preserve items in this water. Vegetarian foods can be preserved for one to two years. Fish and poultry can be preserved for about a month.
