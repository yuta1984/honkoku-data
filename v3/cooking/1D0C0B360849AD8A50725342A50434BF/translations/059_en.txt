○Same as Wax Grilling (Rōyaki)
Cut sea bream into pieces of any size as desired, lightly salt them, skewer them, and grill both sides well. Then mix beaten eggs with a small amount of wheat flour, stirring well to proper consistency, and use this mixture for glazing while grilling. It is important to grill over distant heat so as not to burn. The egg mixture should be applied about four to five times. It is also good to mix only egg whites with a little wheat flour and grill. It is also good to mix egg whites with red cinnabar powder and a little wheat flour, grilling over distant heat as described above.

○Same as Quick Ground Fish (Haya Surimi)
For this preparation, fillet into three pieces as usual, scrape the flesh, knead it with a knife on the cutting board, take an appropriate amount and boil it. It may also be deep-fried in oil.

○Pike Conger Bone-Cutting (Eso Honekiri)
Fillet into three pieces, make numerous cuts down to the skin level, skewer, and grill with Japanese pepper soy sauce.

○Same as Dengaku
This preparation is the same as bone-cutting. Japanese pepper miso and mustard miso varieties are good.

○Same as Pressed Fish (Yose-mi)
Fillet into three pieces as usual, scrape off the flesh with a knife, remove small bones thoroughly, and place in a tea bowl or similar vessel
