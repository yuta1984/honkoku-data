Whitebait Kamaboko (Shirauo Kamaboko)
Clean whitebait removing all dust and debris. For 1 shō of fish, add 1 shō of water and soak from morning until evening when the water becomes cloudy. Boil this cloudy water in a pot and use it to make kamaboko. Scoop it up, pour kudzu starch sauce over it, and serve with grated wasabi. When serving many people, use plenty of fish. This is a modest dish.

Grilled Yuzu Jerky (Yaki Yuzu-boshi)
Remove the flesh from yuzu citrus, take 1 gō each of wheat flour, walnut powder, and rice cake flour, add a little black sesame, about 30 monme of miso, and a little ginger. Put into chestnuts and boil, let cool, then cut thick and grill.

Buddhist Vegetarian Silver Sushi (Shōjin Gin-zushi)
Sow grapes in sand, warm about 1 sun of water over them
