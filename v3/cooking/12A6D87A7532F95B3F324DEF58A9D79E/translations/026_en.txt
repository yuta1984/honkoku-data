【43】How to Make Daikon Red Miso Soup
Cut the daikon into pieces about one inch long and grate it with a grater, which is the best method. Do not wash this with water, but cook it directly in red miso soup. However, use less miso, and when cooking, add a little sake, remove the lid, cook on low heat, dissolve green mustard and add it as a garnish when serving. The flavor is extremely light and very good.

【44】How to Make Daikon Soup (One Variety)
For this, take the ring-pattern daikon mentioned earlier, cut it into rounds, boil well, and drop thin kudzu starch into clear soup. This is called "kake-kuzu." It should feel soft and smooth in the mouth. Make the soup slightly sweet, put the daikon in a bowl, add the soup, and top with grated daikon. Also, knead pepper powder and roll it into balls about this size, then squeeze the juice from the grated daikon, cut it small by hand like this, and add one pepper ball.
