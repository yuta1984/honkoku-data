Kudzu-yaki　　Knead kudzu powder with boiling water, add sugar and an paste,
　　　　　　place a copper mold on top of the frying pan, pour the mixture
　　　　　　into it and bake, then flip with a metal ladle and cook both sides.
　　　　　　Make it sour enough to taste. Oral tradition: it's good to add
　　　　　　egg white to the kudzu.

Kinuta-maki　　Strain kudzu powder with water and spread it out, add white sugar,
　　　　　　make thin pancakes in a frying pan, lift with a metal ladle and roll,
　　　　　　then bake a little more in the frying pan to make it roll tightly.
　　　　　　However, when adding an paste and eggs, use turmeric for yellow
　　　　　　color to make it look good, with an paste coating.

Kudzu-tsutsumi　Like the above kinuta-maki. Finely chop ginkgo nuts,
　　　　　　wood ear mushrooms, pounded chestnuts, walnuts, etc.,
　　　　　　wrap each portion in this, tie the opening with gauze,
　　　　　　and quickly deep-fry in sesame oil.

　　About Salt-Cooking Adjustments

Sea Bream Scraps　Rub the fish with salt and let it sit, put salt in the pot,
　　　　　　rub and burn it into the pot with a metal ladle,
　　　　　　add dashi and bring to a boil, then wash the salt off the fish well,
　　　　　　add it and season by reducing the heat slightly.

Same　　　　This is the adjustment when there's too much sake. In this case,
　　　　　　blanch the fish in salt water and wash well with water, then set aside.
