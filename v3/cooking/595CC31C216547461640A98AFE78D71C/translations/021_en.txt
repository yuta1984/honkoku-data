Pigeon Sake　　　Pound pigeon well, dissolve with sake, add a little
　　　　　　　　miso to the pot, fry until fox-colored, then add
　　　　　　　　both pigeon and sake. Add a little black pepper
　　　　　　　　powder and sansho pepper powder.

Wing Node Sake　Cut the tips beyond the nodes in pheasant wing
　　　　　　　　feathers finely, add a little salt and sake, fry,
　　　　　　　　add the meat as well, warm the sake to proper
　　　　　　　　temperature and serve.

Tsukami Sake　　Take pheasant intestines, add a little miso,
　　　　　　　　pound well together, then skewer each pheasant
　　　　　　　　with a small skewer, stuff the pounded mixture
　　　　　　　　into the cavity, and when grilled, it swells well.

　　　　　　　　When it bulges like a chestnut and overflows,
　　　　　　　　cut from the edge of the cavity, pound again,
　　　　　　　　add a little more, pour in sake, warm it and serve.

　　　　　　　　However, the grilling technique requires much
　　　　　　　　oral instruction.

Kneaded Sake　　Mix egg white and white sugar with cold sake,
　　　　　　　　knead well together until smooth and creamy,
　　　　　　　　then heat in an earthenware pot while kneading.

　　　　　　　　Use sake with water, two egg whites, white sugar

Ginger Sake　　　Grate ginger, add a little miso, grind in the
　　　　　　　　pot and fry, then add sake.
