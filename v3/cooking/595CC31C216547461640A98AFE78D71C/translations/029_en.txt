Goji-mochi　　　Both are prepared the same way - boil well and pound, squeeze out the juice,
Ukogi-mochi　　　knead four parts glutinous rice and six parts regular rice flour, boil and pound.

Chimaki　　　　This also uses four parts and six parts flour kneaded softly with water,
　　　　　　　add white sugar, wrap in wild rice grass or bamboo leaves,
　　　　　　　and boil well.
　　　　　　　　　For yellow color, knead with gardenia water.

Gosho-sama-mochi　Mix grated mountain yam with four parts and six parts flour, make small
　　　　　　　flat pieces, simmer well in miso soup, lift out,
　　　　　　　serve only the mochi, and pour boiled sugar syrup over it.
　　　　　　　　However, use one shō of sugar with four gō of water and boil well.

Konoe-sama Yuki-mochi　Two monme white rice, one monme poria, two monme Chinese yam, one monme lotus seeds,
　　　　　　　two monme ginseng - grind these well into powder and mix with four parts and six parts flour.
　　　　　　　　Add eight monme white sugar, mix well, spread cloth in steamer,
　　　　　　　put in and steam thoroughly, then cut. This is truly an incomparable item.

　　　Same - About Making Bean Paste

Superior An　　Use good azuki beans from the new rice season, grind well, soak in water,
　　　　　　　strain to remove hulls, put in cloth bag and squeeze out water
　　　　　　　completely. Use one kin of finest black sugar with just enough water
　　　　　　　to make proper consistency, boil while stirring, remove foam, then add refined white sugar
　　　　　　　syrup and mix with the aforementioned azuki powder, simmering until thick.
　　　　　　　　　Using only refined white syrup will not provide sufficient
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　sweetness.
