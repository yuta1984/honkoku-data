Removing Sand from　　　Not only hatsutake mushrooms, but also enoki and shimeji - even when well washed,
　Hatsutake Mushrooms　sand stuck in the gills under the cap is difficult to remove. To remove this,
　　　　　　　　　　　turn the hatsutake upside down, hold the stem with chopsticks
　　　　　　　　　　　and tap the top of the cap with the other chopstick, then all the sand will fall out
　　　　　　　　　　　As shown in the diagram, this works for all mushroom varieties

　from Bamboo Shoots　　To remove the bitterness, add whole red chili peppers when boiling
　　　　　　　　　　　and the bitterness will disappear

How to Boil Seaweed　　Wash very thin seaweed well, put in boiling water
　　　　　　　　　　　using a copper ladle or pot lid, also add
　　　　　　　　　　　four or five bamboo leaves and boil, then remove and wipe
　　　　　　　　　　　with ash. However, there are two types of seaweed - thick ones are
　　　　　　　　　　　not good. The thinner ones have better color and appearance
　　　　　　　　　　　and also better taste

How to Wash Mozuku　　Whether using salted mozuku raw or in warm water, rub
　　　　　　　　　　　with ash and wash. This removes the sliminess well

How to Wash Matsuba　　Bury in hot ash for a while, then remove and wash
　　　　　　　　　　　thoroughly with water while shaking

Making Old Buckwheat　　When kneading buckwheat, knead with boiling water and add
　Flour Like New　　　　octopus pressing liquid, then aroma emerges like new buckwheat

How to Grill Whitebait　When grilling whitebait, use a grill but they stick and won't come off
　Same for Young Ayu　　If you try to remove them they break apart and cause trouble. The grilling method is
　　　　　　　　　　　to arrange on the grill as usual and grill, not worrying about those that stick
