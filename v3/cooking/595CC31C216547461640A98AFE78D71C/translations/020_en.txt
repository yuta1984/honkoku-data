Mino-ni　　　　Beat eggs with a ladle, add a little to hot water
　　　　　　　without letting it become stringy, and serve as
　　　　　　　clear soup.

Iri-gaki　　　Heat salt in a pot, add oysters and cook them.
　　　　　　　However, if the oysters lack color, add some dashi.

Mikuni　　　　Serve Noto seaweed in clear broth.

White Chrysanthemum　Cut tofu into squares about one inch on each side,
　　　　　　　　　　make fine cross-cuts, place in a bowl, and when
　　　　　　　　　　hot water is poured from the edge, it opens
　　　　　　　　　　sequentially like chrysanthemum flowers.
　　　　　　　　　　Cutting method: Do not cut through completely,
　　　　　　　　　　leave as is and pour hot water.
　　　　　　　　　　Season the hot water with grilled salt. Serve
　　　　　　　　　　with soba noodles as a base.

Futami Tofu　　This is the above hot tofu served with miso, hence
　　　　　　　called "Futami" (two views). It's also good to serve
　　　　　　　with buckwheat flour dumplings.

Yamakage Tofu　This refers to hoppai tofu topped with grated
　　　　　　　　mountain yam. It's good to thin the yam with
　　　　　　　　squeezed daikon radish juice.

　　　About Seasoning Cooking Sake

Egg Sake　　　Use one egg per three sake cups.
　　　　　　　First crack the egg and mix it into cold sake.
　　　　　　　If the sake taste is too strong, add a little
　　　　　　　white sugar.

Yam Sake　　　Grate white mountain yam, grind it well, mix
　　　　　　　thoroughly with cold sake, and continue mixing
　　　　　　　until it becomes smooth.
