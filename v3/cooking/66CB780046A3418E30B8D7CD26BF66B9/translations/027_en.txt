▲Arrowhead duck eater (katsura-mugikui)
This is slightly smaller than the true barley eater. When used as a substitute for duck, it equals two true ducks or about one small duck. As soup it serves twenty-one to twenty-two people, as roasted bird seventeen to eighteen people. The above refers to both breast meat and both thighs only. The fat content is the same as the true eater. This bird is scarce locally; it comes little by little from Edo and Owari. However, it is somewhat inferior to the true eater.
▲White wild goose
This is used quite extensively in Edo cuisine. Among wild geese, it is a lower-grade bird. The bird's size is the same as true wild geese. When prepared as split bird, it equals two true ducks.

▲Sea goose (umigan)
The bird is small, the same as wild goose. The flavor also lacks good broth. For festive cooking eleven to twelve servings, as roasted bird eight to nine servings. When used as duck substitute, it equals one true duck plus one small duck. The fat is thin. Depending on timing, it can be good.
