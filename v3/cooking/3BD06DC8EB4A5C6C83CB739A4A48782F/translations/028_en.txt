　　Transmission of Sesame Tofu
First, peel white sesame seeds, roast appropriately in a parching pan, grind very well in a mortar, add five gō of kudzu to one shō of sesame, add about two shō of water and put in a pot, simmer very well until thickened, lay cloth on a lid for cutting and pour, cool briefly then cut. Fire control is oral tradition.
Walnut tofu uses the same method. Edamame tofu also uses the same method.

　　　Transmission of Glazed Simmered Yuzu
First, peel the yuzu, make about six knife cuts in the yuzu, boil well and put in water, remove seeds and flesh, squeeze out water well, simmer again very well with mirin and sake, add rock sugar, simmer again briefly, add roasted salt and finish cooking.
First, mandarin oranges use the same method, cutting the mandarin into rounds and simmering.
First, kumquats use the same method but are simmered whole.

　　　Transmission of Crystal Kelp
First, boil confectionery kelp very well, scrape both front and back of the kelp with a knife, cut the white interior into strips, simmer very well with mirin, and at the finish add roasted salt for seasoning.
