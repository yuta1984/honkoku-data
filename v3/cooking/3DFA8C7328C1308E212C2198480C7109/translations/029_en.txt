"Sasa-mochi" (bamboo leaf rice cakes): Make regular rice into finest white rice, pound well into flour, and divide the flour into three grades. First grade: pound roughly, first sift, and set aside that flour. Second grade: pound well and sift finely. Then knead with water, form into small balls, put in pot and boil. When they float up and sink again, finish boiling. Remove and pound well in a mortar, then tear into various colors. For yellow use gardenia, for blue use mugwort juice - this is good. There are oral instructions for green soybean flour. Hot water leaves.

"Gosho-sama Mochi" (Imperial Palace Style Rice Cakes): Transmitted from Lord Nambu. Four parts regular rice, six parts glutinous rice, grind well into flour, grate mountain yam and knead together. Form into small flat pieces, boil well in miso soup, serve only the rice cakes, and pour boiled sugar syrup over them. Add four go of water to one sho of sugar and boil - this is good.

"Konoe-sama Yuki-mochi" (Lord Konoe's Snow Rice Cakes): Two ryo white atractylodes, one ryo poria, one ryo Chinese yam, two ryo lotus seeds, two ryo well-roasted ginseng. Grind all of these into powder well, combine and grind with four parts regular rice flour and six parts glutinous rice flour, eight ryo sugar. Mix all of these ingredients well together, spread cloth as usual and steam, then cut and serve. This is incomparable.

**Chapter 19: Tea Section**

"Nara Tea": First roast tea lightly and put in a bag, weigh the adzuki beans and tea and decoct. Then add half portions each of beans and rice - this is good. For beans, crack and discard the hulls - this is good. Also good to add broad beans, water chestnuts, roasted chestnuts, etc. There are proper proportions for Japanese pepper powder and salt. Proper cooking timing is crucial for everything.

"Goji Tea": Pick young goji berries, steam them, and place on a roasting basket. Boiling is also fine. When grinding, it's good to add one-third regular tea.

"Turmeric Tea": This is also prepared like goji. Mulberry tea is also good prepared in this same manner.

**Chapter 20: Various Records Section**

"One-Night Sushi Preparation": Wash sweetfish, make the rice saltier than the usual salt proportion, stuff into the fish, wrap in grass bundles, build a fire in the garden, roast together with the bundles, wrap the top with straw mats two or three times, place on top of where the fire was built, and apply weights strongly. Alternatively, it's good to tie around a pillar and bind tightly. It will be ready in one night. Do not remove the salt.
