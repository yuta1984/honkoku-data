【Cooking Method】
Quick vinegared dish (haya-namasu)
or hand salt (teshio)
　Fillet into three pieces and soak directly in vinegar, apply
　strong grilled salt and sprinkle plenty of bonito flakes
　　　No accompaniments

【Cooking Method】
Hon-jiru (main soup)
　Whether red miso or medium white miso, grill young sweetfish over fire and
　add to make soup, with bamboo shoots, thin bamboo,
　wakame seaweed, yomena (wild chrysanthemum)　Select from these types as appropriate

【Cooking Method】
Large bowl
　Grill young sweetfish freshly with white salt
　Pour simmered mixed vinegar sauce over and serve
　　　Or put mixed vinegar in a large sake cup for dipping while eating
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　which is also good

【Cooking Method】
Inkstone lid
Assorted arrangement
　Colored grilled　or simmered　also tempura
　　However, the tempura method is detailed in
　　the oil cooking section at the back of this book

【Ingredient】
Sculpin (kajika)
　Sansho pepper miso fish dengaku　also soy sauce grilled　or dried grilled
　ones with bonito flakes simmered
　Also grill fresh ones with dried turnip etc. in red miso soup
　Also used in main soup　Also simmered with green vegetables and served in flat bowls

【Ingredient】
Willow dace (yanagi-baya)
　Pound and form into balls, fry in sesame oil
　Use as accompaniment for flat bowls etc.　Also good grilled with grated daikon
　and soy sauce　Otherwise has fishy smell
　and is not good

【Ingredient】
Kuki (a type of small fish)
　Found in the Karasu River and Kanna River areas of Jōshū　This also has much
　fishiness and is not delicious, but prepare like yamame trout and
　sweetfish to have ready for times
　of shortage

【Ingredient】
Giant salamander (sanshō-uo)
　A medicinal fish, good for feeding
　to small children, but its form is different and difficult
　to use in cooking　Therefore omitted
