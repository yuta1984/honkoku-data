【Ingredient】
Whale
　Red bean-colored meat, iri-gara, pressed whale　　However, when cooking with oil, some parts may be burnt, so one should examine carefully before use.

【Cooking Example】
Soup
　As usual
　　◦Board-cut daikon radish　◦Shaved burdock root
　　◦Round-sliced eggplant　Garnish: green onion and chili pepper

【Cooking Example】
Sashimi
Mustard miso
　Slice thin and blanch in boiling water before use
　　　Accompaniments　◦Dried vegetables　◦Scallions　◦Julienned green onions
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　These types are good

【Cooking Example】
Rice Bowl
Three-flavor vinegar pickle
Also for bowl dishes
　Make the white meat into thin strips, wash many times in clear water, and pickle in three-flavor vinegar
　　　Accompaniments　White melon　Wood ear mushrooms　Japanese ginger
　　　　　　New ginger　Yuzu citron　Black pepper

【Cooking Example】
Flat Plate
　Slice thin and blanch in boiling water, then lift out, add seasoned greens and simmer.
　Sprinkle with bonito flakes.

【Ingredient (Whale Iri-gara)】
Same, iri-gara
　This is a specialty item that is always available in Kyoto, but rarely found in the Edo vicinity. Since the flavor is light, it's good to simmer with green onions, daikon radish, etc.

【Ingredient (Pressed Whale)】
Same, pressed
　This is also a specialty item, flattened like domestic Mishima seaweed. When hot water is poured over it and rubbed, it becomes hair-like. Good as an accompaniment to sashimi, or as a single dish with seasoned vinegar.

【Ingredient (Whale Kabura-bone)】
Same
Kabura-bone
　This is also a famous product. When sliced thin and used as decoration for water bowl dishes or vinegared dishes, it becomes translucent and looks magnificent.
