　　　　　Recipe for Kuzumochi (Arrowroot Mochi)
1.　Add 5 gō of water to 1 shō of arrowroot starch, dissolve it well, add the arrowroot starch, and
　　knead it. Regardless of the quantity, adjust using the above proportions.
　　Then cool thoroughly in water, and serve with
　　roasted soybean flour (kinako) and sugar. End of recipe.

　　　　　Lord Nagata Hyūga
　　　　　　　Recipe for Zuiki (Taro stems)
1.　Remove the skin from the zuiki (stems of taro), and cut into lengths of approximately 2–3 sun (about 6–9 cm).

　　Boil them in the proportion used for su-zuiki (vinegared zuiki), add wasabi to simmered sake (irizake),
　　and serve in any vessel you prefer, or alternatively place wasabi on the side —
　　serve according to taste. When serving as a pickled dish (nukasusume), it is best to
　　keep the soy sauce, vinegar, and sake separate and bring them to a light, quick boil.
　　It is best to use zuiki that are well-fattened (thick and plump);
　　the root of the zuiki that has a slight whitish □ (tinge/appearance)
