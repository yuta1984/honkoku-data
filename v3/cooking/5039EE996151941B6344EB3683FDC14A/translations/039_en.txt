　　　　Method for Dried and Pickled Owari Daikon
1. During winter, cut Owari daikon to about 3 bu thickness,
　　vary the length according to your preference, sprinkle lightly with salt,
　　after about half a day, lay straw and stand them to dry in the sun for about two days.
　　When done this way, wash well and pour sake over them.

　　　　Method for Simmered Sansho Pepper
1. Before the green sansho pepper berries harden, simmer with soy sauce
　　like ordinary simmered sansho. Soaking in water overnight
　　to remove bitterness is recommended.

　　　　Transmitted by Lord Koide Yamato-no-kami

【Left Page】
　　　　Method for Salt-cured Sea Bream
1. Take fresh sea bream, remove scales, and open the belly.
　　Rub salt well on both front and back sides, salt-cure for one night,
　　then dry well. The color becomes beautifully white,
　　and it's better than salmon. End.

　　　　Transmitted by Lord Koide Yamato-no-kami
　　　　　　Method for Eggplant Kasu-pickles
1. Roast five sho of black rice for eating and let cool. While still slightly warm,
　　mix in five sho of koji, put in a jar and press down,
　　cover and leave for four or five days to become amazake (sweet rice wine).
