　　　　　　How to Make Inuma Mochi
1.　　Pound glutinous rice well, soak it in water to soften, and sift the resulting
　　　flour through a sieve in the usual manner.
1.　　Cook adzuki beans slightly firmer than usual boiled adzuki beans,
　　　transfer them onto a board, and cut finely with a thin-bladed knife.
1.　　Moisten the above mochi flour with water and shape into large,
【Left page】
　　　well-formed balls. On the cloth lining of a steamer,
　　　spread the finely cut adzuki beans from above, and arrange the rolled
　　　mochi balls on top so they do not stick, then steam them. Over-steaming
　　　is not good. It is good to split a mochi with chopsticks
　　　and turn it inside out to check. Alternatively, finely cut
　　　yam (yamaimo) may be used as the lining instead.
