Change the boiling water two or three times, then cut into pieces about one sun (3cm) long. For fish ingredients, cut kamaboko into thin strips, and also cut kikurage mushrooms into very fine strips. Add finely chopped dried citrus peel and a little walnut meat, mix udon flour with equal parts sake and water, and knead well together. It should not be too soft. Then add the daikon, mix very well, form into small balls, and deep-fry in sesame oil. It is good to add a bit extra daikon. Then serve with soy sauce mixed with chili pepper, finely chopped garlic, or finely chopped green onions. This is excellent as tea cuisine, drinking appetizers, or sweet soup dishes.

(11) Jōshū Tatebayashi Specialty Daikon Soba Method
First, take spicy daikon and, as with rin-maki daikon, peel it thinly, roll it tight, and cut it thinly crosswise. Put this into soba flour, sprinkle water little by little from above, and stir the flour - the soba flour will stick to the daikon. Let this rest overnight, and the next day sprinkle soba flour on a board, also sprinkle it on the daikon, and gently tap with a pestle - it will stick well. Then put a little rice bran in a pot, bring water to a boil, and add the daikon
