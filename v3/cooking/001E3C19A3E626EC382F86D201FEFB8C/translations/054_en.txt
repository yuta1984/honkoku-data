How to Cut Wachigai (Ring-shaped) Daikon Radish

First, select a sufficiently large daikon radish that has no hollow core (spongy interior), and remove the red leaves and tough outer leaves. Cut as shown in the diagram. However, leave the soft green leaves. Prepare lukewarm water and set it aside.

Apply this water to both the knife and the daikon before peeling. The knife should be well-sharpened. Make horizontal cuts on both the front and back surfaces of the daikon to remove these sections, then cut away the vertical ridges on both sides. After that, cut away the four corners, and it will become as shown in the diagram that follows.
