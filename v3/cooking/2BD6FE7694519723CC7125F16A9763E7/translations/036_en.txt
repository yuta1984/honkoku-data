○Method for Rolled Rice Cakes (Maki-mochi)
One portion wheat flour with white sugar added to make it quite sweet, knead in a bowl with water until it becomes smooth and thick. Lightly oil the bottom of a frying pan, then pour in the mixture and fry to about the thickness of two layers of thick cloth, lifting with a spatula. Set aside the cooked portion, then fry another sheet, lifting the edges from the pan and leaving it in the pan. Sprinkle with walnuts, black sesame, persimmon, etc. as desired, then combine with the previously cooked sheet to make two layers, lightly fry both sides and cut as desired.

○Method for Sweet Bean Paste Dumplings (An-dango)
One portion kneaded like regular dumplings, put sweet bean paste inside, and steam in a steamer. The steaming is done until the rice cake develops a glossy color. Add strained flour to the sweet bean paste.
