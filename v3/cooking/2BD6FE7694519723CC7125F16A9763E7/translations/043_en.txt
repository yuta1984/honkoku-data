One: Kudzu powder - 2 gō, One: Non-glutinous rice powder - 1 gō, One: Salt - a little
Mix these well together and grind thoroughly in a mortar. Then
put 220 monme of brown sugar in a pot and simmer,
strain through a moji as if straining lacquer, mix this with the above
bean paste and knead well together. Then lay two layers of cloth
in a steamer, spread directly and level well, place one layer of cloth
on top and steam. For steaming doneness, place soybeans
on top - when these beans are cooked to an edible degree, it's ready.
Cool for one night then cut. Of the brown sugar,
mix one-third with white sugar, making a total of two hundred and
twenty monme.

○Method for Rakugan (Dry Confection)
One: Put white sugar in water until just covered, when it becomes hot water,
strain through cotton, then again in the same pot

【Left Page】
The kneading consistency should be such that when you scoop it with a tea spoon
and drop it down, it falls in a nice dripping manner.
For baking, bake just enough so it doesn't burn.

○Method for Gyūhi Candy
One: White sugar - 1 kin, rock sugar in good proportion
One: Candy - half kin, One: Wheat flour - 50 monme
Using a bronze pot, simmer the sugar and
remove impurities. Then add 1 shō 5 gō of water and the above two ingredients,
keep the fire quite low and knead until concentrated. Judge the degree
by observation. Then place on a tray, and cut like mochi
using kudzu powder. In any case,
cutting and chopping until you understand is the ultimate oral tradition for this.
