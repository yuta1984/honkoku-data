One: Take hatsutake mushrooms that are not fully opened, cut off the stone base, boil them well and drain thoroughly, then pickle with very white salt so they don't rub against each other. It's good to make holes in the bottom of the tub with an awl.

　　○Method for Pickled Shimeji Mushrooms
One: Cut off the stone bases of fresh shimeji mushrooms, select ones without insect damage and boil them well, arrange on boards overnight to cool, then pickle so they don't rub against each other. Place a light weight on top.

　　○Method for Pickled Yuzu Citrus
One: Add three sho of salt to one to of water, knead and crush well, put in an iron or copper pot and decoct. Also put in an iron or copper bowl to cool, leave for two or three days until well chilled, then put in a jar and pickle the yuzu. Similarly, iron utensils are good.

【Left Page】
One: Cut yuzu (about twenty out of a hundred) into rounds.
For small green yuzu about the size of plums, do it this way; for yuzu about the size of mikan oranges, cut twenty out of a hundred. Put them in the salt water mentioned above, bury the jar in the ground with about half sticking out. Of course, wrap the mouth of the jar well - this will keep until next year.

　　○Method for Pickled Bamboo Shoots
One: Remove the thin tips of bamboo shoots before pickling. Similarly, young bamboo shoots that are green are good. Whether split in two or cut into rounds with the nodes removed, pickle them. With very white salt so they don't rub together - for rounds with nodes removed, pack salt tightly inside the hollow tubes.
