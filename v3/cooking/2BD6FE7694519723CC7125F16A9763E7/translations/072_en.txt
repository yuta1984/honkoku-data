　　　　○Echigo Roe Sac
One: For ten salmon, put one to of salt into one to two shō of
　water, knead and crush well, strain out debris, boil this water down to
　one to, cool well, and soak
　the salmon in this water. Remove the salmon's intestines, and if there is little roe, mix in other
　roe, sew the thin flesh parts
　with thread so the roe doesn't fall out, soak in the aforementioned water for seven days, then
　take it out and tie it in about three places with thin rope, or
　wrap it round and round - in any case, attach rope and hang it in shade.
　If you want it soft, about ten days is
　sufficient. If you want to make it hard, hang it for twenty or thirty days.

【Left Page】
　　　　○Salt-Cured Salmon Without Roe (Ōshū style)
One: Open the salmon's belly, salt inside and outside,
wrap in straw matting
　and leave for seven days. On the eighth day, wash off the salt and
　hang inside the house. To make salt-cured salmon hard, let it blow
　in the wind. To make it soft, wrap it in straw matting.
　　　　○Echigo Salmon Fillet
One: Put one shō of salt into one to two shō of water, knead and crush well,
　boil down to one to of water, cool well, open the salmon
　and put it in the salt water for about four to five days, then
　take it out and hang in shade until it hardens.
　To eat it quickly, soak in salt water for about three days.
