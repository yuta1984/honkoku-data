　　○Method for Pickled Apricots
One: For one hundred apricots, use one sho of salt, one to of water, and one sho of ash. Combine these and pickle for one day and night, then wash well with that salt water. After that, use one sho of refined white sake and one kin of sugar - pickle the apricots with these two ingredients and place in a jar. They will be well pickled in about twenty days.

One: Wash dried plums with water and soak in water for about one hour to remove some saltiness, then put in a jar. Pour the above sugar-sake mixture while hot, add plenty of sake to cover the dried plums, cover with a lid and leave overnight - it will be ready the next day. 　　○Method for Dried Plums
One: Mix three sho of salt with one to of plums that have turned slightly in color, put in a tub, cover with water and leave overnight. During the day, dry in the sun; at night, place back in the salt water. Repeat this many times until the plums become soft and wrinkled.
