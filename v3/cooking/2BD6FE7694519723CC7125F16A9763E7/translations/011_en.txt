　　　○Method for grape wine
1. Crush grapes one by one, squeeze the juice well
　and collect it, put it in a pot and bring to one boil
　over charcoal fire, then let it cool well, and when cold, add
　about one-third of either shochu or awamori.
　Use both sour and sweet grapes together.

　for about fourteen days until it develops a color like soy sauce.
　At that time, remove the longan flesh, squeeze it with cloth,
　and discard the pulp. Mix the above two types according to taste.
　It becomes like chinta (Chinese liquor). If you store the two types
　separately in jars as described above, they
　will keep indefinitely.
　　　○Method for yam wine
1. Peel the yam and cut into pieces about 5 bu thick,
　put in a bamboo strainer, boil water in a pot, and soak the strainer
　in the hot water for about the time it takes to drink
　two or three sips of hot tea, then remove as is, let it drip,
　grind well on a tray, and when cooled, add sake and knead, stretch it
　like sake and store in a tokkuri bottle, warming it
　each time you use it. It will keep for about five days.
