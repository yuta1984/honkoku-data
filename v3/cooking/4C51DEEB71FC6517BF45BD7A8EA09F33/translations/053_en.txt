Butterbur sprouts
　　　Sugar simmered
Appetizer　Matsukaze arrowhead
　　　Matsutake potato

　　　△For butterbur sprouts, remove one layer of outer skin, simmer in light soy sauce and sake, add white sugar and cook. △For matsukaze arrowhead, peel the arrowhead, grate with wasabi grater and steam, add ground sugar and a little salt, put in egg pan and flatten, when the surface is well cooked, flip it over and cook. Sprinkle with poppy seeds and cut. When it gets colored, open and turn over, sprinkle poppy seeds on both sides, let cool and cut to appropriate size. △For matsutake potatoes, wash with skin on, simmer in sake and soy sauce, cut from the end and stack.

　　　White hair udo
Vinegared dish　Rolled yuba
　　　Green thread konjac
Served directly with mustard vinegar miso

　　　△For white hair udo, remove skin and cut to about 1.5 sun, cut thinly lengthwise, then stack and cut very finely lengthwise, place in water and drain. △For rolled yuba, prepare at yuba shop with light seasoning, also deep-fry in sesame oil, cut from the end and stack. △Green thread konjac is available at konjac shops, boil briefly in salted water and season lightly. 〇The cutting method for thread konjac is also found in the eighth menu.

　　　△Peel pears, depending on the size of firm pears, cut like mozuku seaweed to about 1.5 or 2 sun, and twist from both sides in opposite directions. △For kunenbo, cut off front, back and four sides to make square shape, then cut like daikon radish. △For black arrowhead, wipe with cloth and scrape well, remove front, back and four sides to make square shape. Leave the skin and cut in half or slice from the end.
