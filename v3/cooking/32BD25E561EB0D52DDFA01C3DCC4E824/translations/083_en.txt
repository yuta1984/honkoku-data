Mix three shō of vinegar, one shō of sake, and seven gō of salt, boil this mixture until it comes to a boil twice, let it cool well, and pickle any vegetable preparation in it. Make this during the cold season and use throughout the year.

　　　Chakin-dōfu (Tea Towel Tofu)
Deep-fry one block of tofu whole in oil, cut one side open, and remove the inside. Put fish, fowl, wood ear mushrooms, and egg inside, tie the opening with kelp, simmer in sake, and serve with grated wasabi on top. The egg should be filled to about seven-tenths capacity.

　　Haya-udo (Quick Udo)
Grind young udo shoots and add barley miso, grind again, boil thin daikon radish well and mix together. Use this when udo is not in its fragrant season.

　　Saimi-nama (Twice-prepared Raw Fish)
Put ground fish into toko-bushi shells, steam and grill, then salt.
