Nishiki Yukan (Brocade Citron Confection)
Knead wheat flour, steam it, dye it with red coloring and roll it out. Separately, mix wheat flour with miso, steam it, layer it on top of the red-dyed layer, roll it out, then roll it up from one end and slice.

Kakusan-zuke (Square Mountain Pickle)
Wash daikon radish, drain the water and immediately pickle it. When pickling daikon, sprinkle with salt, sprinkle with rice bran, lay straw over the entire surface, then layer the daikon as described above in tiers and place weights on top.

Hajiki-gusa (Popped Taro Method)
Use small taro with well-developed flesh, steam thoroughly with skin on. Cut the top thinly, pop out the flesh, chop it finely and use in soup.
