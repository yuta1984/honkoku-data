Salmon sauce (sake-bishio)
　　Scrape the good parts of salmon flesh with the blade of a plane knife,
　　mix thoroughly by pounding with high-quality sake lees, apply to small boards
　　and layer in inkstone lids and such
　　　　　However, aim for a color similar to pickled plum sauce

　Tea bowl
　Senba style
　　Or flat plate
　　　Take salmon flesh in good portions, parboil well in hot water,
　　　change the water, add a little sake, salt, and soy sauce
　　　and season to taste　Accompaniments: {{julienned daikon radish｜first mushrooms and such are good}}

　Sashimi
　　Long plate
　　　Slice thin, arrange as cut and serve
　　　　　Or it's also good to pour white sake over the top

　Overnight pickle
　Shaved sushi
　　Soak in water to remove some salt, slice flesh thin and large,
　　add wood ear mushrooms, sea lettuce, julienned ginger or
　　good-colored pickled daikon cut into strips, mixed with rice
　　and pressed　However, shaved sushi means mixed with rice and pickled

　Large sake cup
　　Or small rice bowl
　　　Chill only salmon skin well in water, remove algae,
　　　squeeze out moisture and deep-fry in sesame oil,
　　　pour over with daikon juice mixed with soy sauce
　　　　　　Accompaniment: deep-fried yam bulbils and such are good

　Vinegared dish plate
　　Split salmon head and take the cartilage, slice thin,
　　soak in vinegar, grate daikon for eight servings, mix with
　　vinegar and soy sauce　Or udo shoots cut like white hair are also
　　　　　　　　　　　good

　Drying rack preparation
　　Take flesh from dried salmon, place on drying rack and dry well,
　　dissolve kudzu powder in water, coat with this
　　and place on drying rack again to dry well
