Items like salted matsutake mushrooms are commonly bought and sold, so I do not include them, but only record the traditional methods for preserving rare items.

1. I present a general listing of regional specialties such as salted fish pastes, pickled items, dried goods, etc.

In the appendix, while there are many varieties of Ōbaku cuisine, fucha (vegetarian temple cuisine), and shippoku cuisine, since the matter would become complex, I present the general methods and teach their procedures. However, by combining items from the various dishes in the previous two volumes and following this style, one can create countless variations.
