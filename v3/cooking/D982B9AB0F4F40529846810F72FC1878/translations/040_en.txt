Someone says that since the food for going to lower estates doesn't differ much from regular meal arrangements, it would be fine to pack them in cutting containers rather than stacked boxes. This is indeed reasonable. However, even at lower estates, there are often parents who have retired there or important people in residence. On such occasions, since these items will be shown to those people, one should arrange them properly in stacked boxes so they don't look unsightly. Also, when invited to someone's villa, if bringing food as a courtesy gift, it should be done as described above. Even at one's own villa, if distinguished parents or elders are present, incorporating foods they usually prefer into these preparations is another important consideration.

In general, when invited to a lower estate or going there for cooking, first observe the seasonal fruits, flowers from the garden, and vegetables from the tea garden that are available there, and use them appropriately as garnishes for soups or side dishes when the time is right. If there are rare flowers or vegetables, do not treat them carelessly. Praise them, request to use them, and employ them in a way that highlights their special nature. Let everything else follow this example.
