Eel (Unagi)
　Mountain and river eels are oily with tough skin and poor taste
　However, making them into kabayaki (grilled with sweet sauce) is common
　　　　Or　preparing kabayaki with sansho miso
　　　　　　　this method is superior to using soy sauce

　Vinegared Fish Plate (Namasu-zara)　《Peel the skin and cut thinly lengthwise for use in vinegared dishes｜Also combined in live arrangements　However, the theory that eel vinegar is poisonous is false》
　Main Soup (Hon-jiru)　《With red miso, crumbled tofu, eel peeled and cut into｜cylinders or split and cut to appropriate size》

Catfish (Namazu)
　This is also commonly made into kabayaki
　Peel the skin and make into a thick red miso soup for serving in
　flat bowls　Add dried sansho pepper

　Fish cake (Kamaboko)　《Slice the fish meat thinly, pound it, mix in starch｜and egg whites to set it》
　　　However, if too little egg is added, it won't set properly　Also when fish
　　　is out of season and hard to set, add a little high-quality wheat flour
