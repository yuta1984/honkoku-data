Travel Reception Stacked Boxes (Sake barrels should be carried separately) - Upper Class

【First Tier - Seven Varieties】
　△Pressed sea bream
　　Shrimp dumpling (appears throughout the book)
　　Grilled pike conger (with green skewers)
　△Whiting rice crackers
　　Abalone (glazed)
　　Umbrella-shaped pine mushrooms
　　Higo taro stems
　　　　Vinegar pickled

【Second Tier - Sashimi】
　　Shark fin ray (prepared frost-style)
　△Same, spiral cut
　　Lettuce leaves (finely chopped)
　　Seaweed jelly grass
　　Green island seaweed
　　　　　　(Red pepper)
　　　　　　　Red miso

【Third Tier - Accompanying Dishes】
　　Medium sea bream (filleted, beach-grilled with sprinkled salt)
　　Kawasemi insects (plenty)
　　Long bracken (at joints)

【Fourth Tier】
　　Grilled rice
　　Sesame rice balls (best to add plenty of ground sesame)

For travel reception boxes, use plenty of fish dishes, and especially pay careful attention to sake and soy sauce varieties to compensate for the hardships of travel - this should be the first priority.

Same - Middle Class (However, when seeing off departing travelers for upper, middle, and lower classes, this elaborate preparation is unnecessary. Just one type of side dish with drinking accompaniments should suffice)

【First Tier - Five Varieties】
　　Thick grilled egg
　　Rolled squid
　　Sparrow (grilled bird)
　　Tied winter flounder
　　Chinese yam (grilled then boiled)

【Second Tier - Accompanying Dishes】
　　Flounder (glazed and simmered)
　　Hen burdock root
　　　　　　With plum sauce
　　River ginger - plenty

【Third Tier - Sashimi】
　　Flounder
　　　　　　Or bonito
　　　　　　　　Yellowtail
　　　　　　　　Mullet
　　　　　　　　　Any of the above types
　　Soft roe
　　Kelp
　　　　　　Vinegar miso

【Fourth Tier】
　　Grilled rice (in nested containers with pickled vegetables, vegetable pickles, Nara pickles)

However, when there are many people, separate the upper and lower classes, and for the lower portion, use individual split-bamboo lunch boxes. Information about split-bamboo boxes appears at the end.
