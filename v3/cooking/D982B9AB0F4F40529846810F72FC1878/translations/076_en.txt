Clear soup
　Suimono (clear soup)
　　Cut red jellyfish into rectangles or diamond shapes
　　　　Accompaniments　○Miruusa (seaweed) ○Kamoji nori (thin seaweed) ○Mozuku (seaweed)
　　　　　　　Combine with the above types. However, wasabi may be
　　　　　　　added to the clear soup. Dark [soup] is also good

Sea urchin
　Add to clear suimono. Garnish with wasabi
　Also mix with miso for tofu dengaku to make one dish
　Also good on its own. All domestic products are the same

Karasumi (salted mullet roe), salted shrimp, konowata (sea cucumber entrails), fukudami (pufferfish sauce), salted shrimp paste
　　　Others　All domestic salted preserved foods are used in the same way

Salted birds
　Crane, wild goose, duck, and any other birds - if the salt is well removed,
　they are used the same way as regular birds
　　　　However, for salted sparrow, add squeezed daikon juice to the soup

　　　Dried fish preparation section
Dried sea bream
　Soak in plain water for four to five days. Change the water daily
　When well softened, remove and drain well
　Also sun-dry for about half a day, then prepare
　in various ways

　Red miso
　Hon-jiru (main soup)
　　Cut the sea bream prepared as above with skin attached into pieces about
　　eight bu square, grill both sides until well charred, thoroughly
　　pre-cook dried greens, serve in red miso hon-jiru with
　　the sea bream as oka-ire (solid ingredients)

　Hachi-zakana (bowl fish dish)
　　Cut the same sea bream to proper size, cook with mirin and sake using only
　　grilled salt. When the liquid becomes syrup-like,
　　serve in a bowl with plenty of plum branch denbu
　　heaped like a mountain and buried
