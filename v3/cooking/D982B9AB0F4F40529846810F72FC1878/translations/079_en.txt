Dried pufferfish (hifugu)
　There are two types: glossy skin and dried pufferfish, both have
　their uses. Both should be soaked in water before use

　Boiled-out miso
　Clear soup
　Also scallop miso
　　Add only the glossy skin, or combine with
　　peeled whole daikon radish. However, peeled whole daikon should be pre-boiled before use
　　Light miso is better. Use dashi from
　　bonito flakes　　　Aromatics: dried citrus peel, scallions

　Regular miso
　Same method
　Called "May soup"
　　Use dried pufferfish. Cut both skin and flesh to appropriate size, combine with grilled tofu,
　　dried daikon, new burdock root, all cut small
　　　　No aromatics needed

　Small bowl
　　Cut glossy skin finely, simmer with mirin, sake and
　　soy sauce, then mix into
　　plum-flavored dish

　Roasted dish (hoiro-mono)
　　Cut glossy skin into strips or diamond shapes, simmer in sake and
　　soy sauce and set aside, sprinkle with a tea whisk,
　　grill over distant fire, or place on a roasting pan

Dried sweetfish (ayu)
　Soak in water and wash well before use. There is also grilled ayu,
　prepared the same way. There is also domestic salted ayu, but
　the preparation method is poor, so it's omitted

　Medium bowl
　　Add plenty of bonito flakes to the above sweetfish and make into nimono
　　　　　Accompaniments: ○Royal fern ○Ise winter radish ○Types of coltsfoot stems
