Shigure Potato
　　　　Boil high-quality long yams well, peel and grate them, then strain
　　　　through a sieve. Add corn flour, white sugar and tamari
　　　　and mix well. Place a frame inside the steamer, lay down
　　　　a cloth, add the yam mixture and press flat, then add corn flour,
　　　　add a thin layer of yam, then a thin layer of corn mixture,
　　　　then white yam again, then corn yam, and steam.

Steamed Karukan
　　　Mix medium wheat flour with white sugar and salt, add lye
　　　and knead. Also add wheat flour to red miso with white sugar
　　　and grind well. Cut yuzu in half and remove the flesh, stuff this with the miso.
　　　Also peel mandarin oranges and carefully remove the pith for one piece. Pack these two
　　　items with the kneaded wheat flour into the steamer and steam.
　　　When the bamboo slats stand up as a sign, let cool, remove and slice from the end.

Steamed Millet
　　　Soak the wheat flour in gardenia water, add white sugar
　　　and salt to taste, knead with lye water, and using the mold as shown in the diagram,
　　　make filling with red miso, sansho pepper, and sugar,
　　　roll into balls, wrap with the wheat flour mixture,
　　　shape with the mold, arrange in steamer and steam.

Camellia Miso
　　Grind red miso well, add bracken starch, white sugar
　　and a little water, place in a frame inside the steamer,
　　distribute shelled walnuts on top and
　　steam. Cool and cut into squares. As shown in the diagram,
　　place one walnut in the center of each piece when cutting.

Nameless
　　　Mix domyoji rice, grated long yam, and white sugar.
　　　For red parts, add red miso and knead, place in a frame
　　　inside the steamer and steam. The steaming time is the same
　　　for all varieties.

Same (Alternative method for Nameless)
　　Steam sweet potatoes, peel them, strain through a sieve,
　　add wheat flour and white sugar, season with salt and
　　knead well. Arrange cogon grass on a board, use this as a core and attach
　　the potato mixture like kamaboko. Also mix red coloring with
　　small potatoes, add white sugar, and wrap the top as shown in the diagram,
　　steam and slice from the end.
