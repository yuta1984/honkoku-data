For late-night guests, or guests when there are no prepared dishes, or
　　when all the prepared dishes have been served and nothing remains,
　　when one or two dishes must be hastily prepared, these are simple
　　ideas presented for reference

Clear Soup
　Regular miso soup simmered and strained of sediment, old pickled eggplant from rice bran
　pickles cut thinly lengthwise from the small end, salt boiled out,
　add two or three pieces, knead high-quality kudzu starch with hot water and pinch in appropriate amounts,
　flatten by hand and add. However, it's best to dissolve the kudzu in hot water before adding

Clear Soup
　Make the dashi thin, wash large pickled plums well
　add one piece with grated wasabi
　　　　Also good to add enoki mushrooms, shimeji mushrooms, or shiitake mushrooms

Clear Soup
　Mix grated mountain yam with a little kudzu powder and add by pinches, cut the top off a yuzu
　citron and hollow it out to make one serving, can be made with either red miso or clear broth.
　Crumble dried Asakusa nori seaweed and sprinkle lightly

Rice Bowl Dish
　Deep-fry rice crackers in sesame oil, dilute scallion miso
　with sake until loose and pour over

Small Side Plate
　Mix grated wasabi with pickled plum flesh and finely
　shaved bonito flakes, pound well and add a little
　soy sauce. This is called nishiki-gi

Similar Garnish
　Roast high-quality tea well and crumble finely,
　grate bonito flakes with the edge of a tea bowl until
　powdery. Mix this together and add a little soy sauce

　　　However, like plum porridge, use very little soy sauce
　　　just enough to moisten. It's best to
　　　cook briefly over low heat
