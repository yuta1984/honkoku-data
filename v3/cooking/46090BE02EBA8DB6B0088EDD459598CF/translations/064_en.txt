○In chapter 38 of "Water Margin" (Suikoden), there is a passage where a person named Dai Zong drinks vegetarian wine with ground tofu seasoned with sesame and chili pepper. It appears to be a type where tofu is ground up and then reformed into tofu again. This shows that various methods of preparing tofu existed in China as well.

○During the Shōtoku era (1711-1716), a Kyoto poet named Kasahara Genba (given name Ryōrin, pen name Unkei) composed comic poetry about the Gion Niken Chaya (two tea houses). It has long been a topic of conversation among people:
"Walking through all of Gion's spring. Left and right, all are pleasure-seekers. The tofu is mottled like tortoiseshell, and fresh amber pools in the kudzu sauce. Dumplings scatter soft as snow, and flower-like chili peppers are sprinkled in bamboo tubes. After drinking several cups, a young woman arrives carrying scales."

Remove the cloth pattern from the tofu, place it on this vessel, hold the outside with your left hand, and cut from right to left.
