27. Ogasawara Tofu　　Cut to appropriate size, cook in kudzu starch broth with good seasoning,
add spice to taste, make kudzu starch sauce and place bonito flakes
all over so thickly that the kudzu cannot be seen

28. Dried Tofu (commonly called Rokujō)　　Cut one block into about eight thin slices, sprinkle with
salt and expose to the blazing summer sun　○Buddhist monks
use it shaved as a substitute for bonito flakes　○Some people
falsely sell the hard tofu left after making yuba
as dried tofu, which contains much nigari (bittern) and is poisonous. If you buy
and use it, you should examine it very carefully
○There is one type of dried tofu made at Mount Kōya. Place one block of tofu
on a grilling net lined with straw, roast over a low charcoal fire, then
dry it and use it shaved

29. Matsukaze Tofu　　Six parts tofu, three parts bag miso, one part egg,
add powdered Japanese pepper and grind well together, season appropriately
with sake and salt, oil a frying pan, spread the ground tofu
mixture evenly and sprinkle with poppy seeds, then fry
Cut to appropriate size and use as sake accompaniment
