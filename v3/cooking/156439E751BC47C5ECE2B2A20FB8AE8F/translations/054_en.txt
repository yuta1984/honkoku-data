Make the bag, pour in the aforementioned eggs, and tie the opening with thread. Then boil water in a pot, put this into it, remove the lid, and cook very well. Then take it out, put it in water, and when you tear off the bag, the eggs inside will have become hard-boiled eggs. Wrap this in bamboo grass to make chimaki, put in a steamer and steam - the fragrance of bamboo grass will permeate, which is good. For usage: one for soup bowls, one for flat dishes made small for clear soup, first or second course soup, one for appetizers, and if large, serve on a tray together with the bamboo grass, and arrange others as appropriate.

　　　Method for Field-cooked Eggs
First, place a seven-times-fired earthenware vessel directly over fire, crack about three eggs and beat well, put into the earthenware vessel, then finely chop and add a little tree buds - in winter use celery stems, in spring use pine needles with the outer coating and skin removed and finely chopped. This greatly improves the flavor. Fish, fowl, and other ingredients may also be added.
