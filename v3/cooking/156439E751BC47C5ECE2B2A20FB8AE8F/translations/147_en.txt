　　　How to Make Miso-Pickled Trout

First, scale the fresh trout, open the belly and remove the innards, wash well with water, and dry slightly. Use plenty of salt, apply pressure, and let sit for one or two days. Then take it out, wash with water two or three times, let the water drip off, and expose to air for about half an hour. Add a little sake to the miso as well. Now, into one kan (3.75kg) of this miso, add 160 monme (600g) of white sugar. Also sprinkle a little sugar inside the belly of the trout, and apply sugar to both the front and back surfaces. Into about one kan of miso, pickle three trout about one shaku (30cm) long. If you have more, add more miso, and like sushi, stuff the miso into the belly cavity as well, press down firmly, and it will keep. Whenever needed, you can take it out for cooking, and it can be used just like fresh trout. Whether for grilled dishes, sashimi, prepared fish, small plate dishes, or other...
