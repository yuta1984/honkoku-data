Add about fifteen [egg whites], decoct them, strain through a water bag, decoct again, and when foam rises, make a hole in the thin part of a bamboo tube's joint, pour the aforementioned egg mixture slowly into this, over the boiling white sugar water, scoop up with a golden mesh ladle, put into water, and cool. However, when scooping up as mentioned, add about one ladleful of water to the aforementioned pot with the golden ladle, then scoop.
Usage: for sashimi, main soup, clear soup, second soup, third soup, and various other suitable applications.

　　　Method for Egg Soba
To about one shō of mountain buckwheat flour, add fifteen local eggs - seven with yolk included, mixed in, and eight with only whites added, mix well, add a little rice bran to the dusting flour, roll out thinly, and for boiling, when it boils vigorously about twice, remove, cool in water, and from this point on, treat like regular soba.
Usage: for sashimi, second and third soups, clear soup, others same as before.
