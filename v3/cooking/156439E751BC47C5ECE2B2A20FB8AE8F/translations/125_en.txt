Kisu Fish Cracker Preparation
1. Remove the tail and head of the kisu, scrape off the scales well, and from the belly to the tail area, carefully remove the central bones without cutting through to the back, also remove the dorsal fins, wash well, sprinkle with light salt, and when the salt has penetrated well, wash thoroughly with water, wipe well, coat with kudzu powder, gently pound with a pestle until it becomes thin and flat. Steam in a bamboo steamer lined with straw, let it air dry slightly, and when it becomes cool, shape it round, trim the edges, place in a paper lantern, and toast slowly until it looks just like real rice crackers. However, it is best if the kisu skin is visible on the back. Usage: good for large inkstone lid combinations, dai-hiki dishes, and mixed grilled dishes. Beyond this, use as appropriate for the occasion.
