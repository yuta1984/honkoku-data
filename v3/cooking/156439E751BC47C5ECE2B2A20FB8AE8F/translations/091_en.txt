First, take fresh crucian carp about 2-3 sun (6-9 cm) long that have drunk flowing river water, soak them in thick amazake (sweet rice wine) for about two days, remove on the third day, grill them lightly over a distant fire as they are, let them cool, then pickle them in white miso together with scales, gills, and entrails. First, in spring, upstream crucian carp are good - pickle for about three days. In summer, downstream crucian carp are good - pickle for about two days. In autumn, Yodo River crucian carp are good - pickle for about five days. In winter, also use Yodo River crucian carp and pickle for about seven days. The methods of use are the same as for the carp in the previous section. It is also good to sauté with soy sauce. This is primarily medicine for men and women.

　　　Method for Pickling Crucian Carp in Rice Bran Miso

First, as in the previous section, take 3-4 sun (9-12 cm) crucian carp while fresh, thoroughly remove the scales, completely remove the entrails, gills, etc. from the belly, wash with water, dry well so there is no moisture.
Then warm sake to drinking temperature and soak the fish in it. Use plenty of sake, soak for one day and night, then remove. For the appropriate rice bran miso, cook black rice - for about two sho (3.6 liters) of rice bran miso, cook five go (900ml) of rice, let it cool thoroughly, add a bit more than five shaku and a half of salt into this mixture, mix well together, pickle the crucian carp so they cannot be seen, place a paper lid on top, put a wooden lid over that, and place a light stone
