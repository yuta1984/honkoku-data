　　Method for Inside-Out Eggs (Kimi-gaeshi Tamago)
Take fresh local eggs, make a small hole in the head with a needle, then bury well in rice bran miso for about five days, remove and wash well with water, then hard-boil - the yolk will be on the outside and the white will be on the inside. This is called "inside-out eggs." Usage is the same as before.

　　Method for Flower Eggs (Hana Tamago)
Like the red hard-boiled eggs, remove the shell and soak thoroughly in hot water for a while, remove, wrap in paper, use chopsticks or bamboo to press in shapes, tie the top, cool well, then slice. Usage is the same as before.

　　Method for Tea Towel Eggs (Chakin Tamago)
Take hard-boiled eggs while still hot, wrap in single-layer paper, then wrap the top in cloth, twist tightly, and press lightly to make them like chakin mochi (tea towel-shaped rice cakes). Usage is the same as before.
