Take the egg whites and discard the yolks without using them. Then crack three more eggs into a mortar and grind well. Into this, gradually add the aforementioned eggs while grinding together, add half a go of sweet cooking wine, and grind together very thoroughly. Then put into a box or bowl and steam. The usage is the same as before, but for individual servings, take out and let cool, then cut and stack in layers. For tray service, likewise cool first. This can also be made into dengaku, which is especially good.

　　　Kokura Specialty Egg Mochi Method
First, for this method, when pounding mochi with roughly five go of rice, add about fifty monme of finest white miso and pound well together with the mochi. Add one cup of aged sake and pound this together well. Then put in a bowl and steam. Crack about twenty-five fresh eggs and beat well. Keep the mochi from cooling in the steamer, put a little mochi at a time into tea bowls, pour the eggs over it, and serve immediately. It's best to also place the tea bowls in hot water beforehand - using heated vessels eliminates the fishy smell of the eggs.
