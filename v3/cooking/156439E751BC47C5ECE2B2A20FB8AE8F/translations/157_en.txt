Well-washed [shellfish] should be boiled once, then cut into thin strips and added to the aforementioned sake to simmer. Now, when you rub the cooled squid with your hands, it becomes fine and fluffy. Next, soak wheat gluten (fu) in sake for one day, remove the next day, coat with salt and toast over fire, then immediately soak in water and rinse well. Cut this too into strips as described above, combine with the skewered shellfish, and simmer in sake. However, keep the fire moderate, and remove when it begins to reduce. Wash the pot thoroughly, add equal parts aged sake and soy sauce, then add the squid along with the skewered shellfish and toasted wheat gluten. Note that both the skewered abalone and toasted wheat gluten should be cooled, rubbed by hand, then added to the pot to simmer. Also cut kaya grass into strips, chop about twenty roasted chestnuts finely, add two cups of hemp seeds and two cups of the finest ground Japanese pepper powder to the mixture, and simmer everything uniformly. However, if the liquid reduces too much, add two cups of sake, one cup of soy sauce, one cup of water, and two cups of roasted salt, and simmer thoroughly. When this too has reduced sufficiently, remove the pot from heat and let it cool slightly before adding white sugar.
