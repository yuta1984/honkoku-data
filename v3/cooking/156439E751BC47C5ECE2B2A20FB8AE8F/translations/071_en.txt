　　　Method for Egg and Sea Urchin Dengaku　This is a specialty of Echizen
First, make hard-boiled eggs, remove the shells, and soak briefly in hot water. Then take fresh sea urchin and grind it well in a mortar,
adding aged sake to taste and a little white sugar. Then take the eggs, wrap them in cloth, and press them flat on a board.
Skewer them on bamboo sticks, hold them over charcoal fire to lightly brown,
spread the sea urchin paste on them, brown them a little more,
and serve. This makes an excellent side dish and is good for use in tea houses.

　　　Method for Matsutake Simmered Eggs
First, take medium-sized matsutake mushrooms at the peak of autumn season, cut off all the stems,
leave the skin as is, place them cap-side up over charcoal fire. While grilling, crack eggs, beat them well, and pour into the mushroom caps
and cook. It's fine to add the eggs little by little, several times. Uses: as an appetizer or as a formal course dish.
