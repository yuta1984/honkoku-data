　　Fish Pickle Various Sections

　　　How to Make Nanban-zuke (Southern Barbarian Pickle)

First, take three cups of finest vinegar, two cups of aged sake, and one cup of salt. Boil these three ingredients over charcoal fire until they bubble twice, then cool well. Use with small sardines, sweetfish, other river fish, or ginger, jellyfish, and Japanese ginger.

　　　How to Make Koji-Pickled Pointed Mackerel

First, remove the tail and head of mackerel from around July, discard the bones and cut lengthwise. Scrape off the salt from the mackerel with a bamboo spatula, combine one shō of koji with three gō of aged sake, pickle thoroughly, cover with paper, and after five or six days, remove and soak again in sake. Afterwards, wash with water, cut into rounds, and use for sashimi, vinegared salad, achara-zuke, vinegar pickle. For small dish items, use with mustard miso, clear soup, or ichijū-nisai soup, matching appropriately.

　　　How to Make Sake-bune-zuke (Sake Boat Pickle)

First, combine koji with sake and water, making the koji just covered with liquid, and pickle any salty fish in this mixture.
