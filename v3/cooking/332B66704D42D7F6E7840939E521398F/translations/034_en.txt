Tofu　fluffy　Bring to a boil once or twice with dashi-soy sauce
　　　and serve quickly
Seasoned tofu　Add dashi to kneaded miso; good
　　　For toppings　bonito flakes, chestnuts, ginger, black sesame,
　　　walnuts, etc. are good to place on top
For coloring preparation　Add vinegar to dashi-soy sauce; anything is good
Itoko-ni (mixed vegetable stew)　Add adzuki beans, burdock root, taro, daikon radish, tofu, roasted
　　　chestnuts, arrowhead, etc. and season with medium miso; good. Since ingredients are
　　　added one by one gradually, it is called itoko-ni
Hibari korobakashi (lark tumble)　Good to put egg and kamaboko inside
Tofu egg　Grind tofu, lightly color with
　　　gardenia, add a little kudzu starch, and with dashi-soy sauce

　　　make it fluffy like eggs; it becomes
　　　like real eggs
Crucian carp aspic　Simmer in dripping miso until the shadow falls and the bones
　　　become tender, then place in the wind and it will
　　　set into aspic within an hour. In summer, it's good to
　　　add seaweed like tokoroten
Crucian carp bone candy　Deep fry crucian carp in oil, cut into large pieces
　　　and prepare like duck cuisine; good
Korokoro (round dumplings)　Pound small birds like senba and quickly
　　　boil, then drain and set aside. Grind kamaboko well
　　　into soapnut-sized portions, wrap with the bird meat, season
　　　with dashi-soy sauce, simmer and serve
