Add vinegar to soy sauce until lukewarm with good seasoning
　　　this is also called "black simmering"
Cherry Searing　Use only octopus tentacles, cut very thinly, in dashi
　　　with soy sauce, simmer quickly
Vinegar Searing　Put only salt in dashi and simmer, when serving add a little
　　　vinegar, which is good; horse mackerel, mackerel, bonito and such are good
Five-Color Simmering　White-grill Spanish mackerel and simmer
　　　in dashi with soy sauce
Pufferfish Searing　Cut sea cucumber large, make dashi-soy sauce deep,
　　　add it dashi-style and serve as is; also called "vinegar tsubaki"
　　　abalone and squid are also good
"Zorori-koto"　Chop iri-ko (dried small fish) very fine, boil well
　　　pound in small birds, duck, etc., also add mountain yam, simmer
　　　in dashi with soy sauce; if using only iri-ko
　　　it's called "thousand iri-ko"
Salmon Sear-Grilling　Make like sugi-yaki, take the roe
　　　make half into paste and set aside, half into balls and set aside
　　　season with dashi-soy sauce, add intestines and liver to the flesh
　　　and simmer; when it comes to a boil, add both the balls and paste, stir
　　　together and serve immediately; adding whole peppercorns is good
Simmered Medley　Good dashi-soy sauce with salmon skin, thin
　　　flesh also added a little, black beans, dried skin, pickled plum,
　　　prepared wood ear mushrooms, apricot kernels, ginkgo nuts, etc., simmer
　　　then place egg soboro on top, which is good; in summer
