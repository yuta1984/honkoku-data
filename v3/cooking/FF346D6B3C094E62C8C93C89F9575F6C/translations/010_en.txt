○The main purpose of this book is to have our countrymen learn foreign cooking methods and serve as an aid to international exchange. It is by no means intended as an introductory textbook for Western learning. Therefore, passages written in foreign languages are translated in small print arranged in two lines, while the main text mixes Chinese characters and hiragana, using colloquial language for the pronunciation guides of Chinese characters, following the example of popular fiction books and illustrated novels that prioritize accessibility for common readers.

○Regarding the clauses recorded in foreign languages in the original book, here we retain foreign words only in the headings of items, while in the main text we translate "soup" as suimono (clear broth), record "cucumber" as kyūri, and translate "macaroni" as sōmen (thin noodles), thus consolidating two linguistic systems into one to make it easier to read. However, words like "butter," which would require the lengthy translation "something made from cow's milk," are retained in their original form.
