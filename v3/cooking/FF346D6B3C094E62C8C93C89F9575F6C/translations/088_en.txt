**Method for the Above Recipe**

Remove the skin of a white melon (shirouri) and boil it for approximately one hour (half a "toki"). Remove it, take out the seeds, break 2 eggs and beat them well, then use a brush-like implement to coat the melon with the beaten egg. Sprinkle breadcrumbs over the top, heat oil in a frying pan until boiling, and deep-fry the melon for 10 minutes.

**Chapter 8**

**○Item No. 103　Puff Paste, Baked (meaning: baked pastry) — Ingredients**

Place flour (wheat flour) in a bowl and flatten it with a rolling pin, sprinkling flour gradually as you work the dough. Roll it out, dot the surface with butter (gyūraku) and beef suet, roll it up into a cylinder, then flatten it again with the rolling pin. Dot with butter once more and repeat the same process. Be sure to dust the rolling pin with flour when using it. (This is to prevent the dough from sticking to the rolling pin.)

---

**○Item No. 104　Puff Paste, Boiled (meaning: boiled/steamed pastry) — Ingredients**

Wheat flour: 1 kin; Sugar: 2/16 of 1 kin; Butter (gyūraku): 3/16 of 1 kin; Boiled (warmed) milk: 2 gō. If milk is unavailable, hot water may be substituted.
