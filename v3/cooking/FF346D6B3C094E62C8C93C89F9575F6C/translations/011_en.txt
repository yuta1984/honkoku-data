○Abbreviating "karōbo" (carrot) to "ninjin" when there are numerous product names and character meanings helps reduce the labor of the typesetters. The pronunciation guides also follow this example, rendering "chau/chou" as "tefu/teu" and "chuu/juu" as "chiu/jiu"—such sloppiness is indeed extreme, one might say. This is because we planned for swift publication, and since the manuscript was completed hastily, we could not give the copyists time for re-proofreading. Although we feel ashamed before the scrutiny of learned individuals, what else could be done? Alas.
