(...May God, who protected my ancestors, once again extend His protection over me.)

From the moment the King uttered these words, the hearts of the people were greatly stirred, and ultimately the nation rose up in unison, contributing one hundred and twenty million Taler in war bonds. ○ In France, however, the surge of enthusiasm was by no means as great, and the legislators were likewise not in agreement. Since this country had always had a large democratic political faction, dissenting voices arose, arguing that as this war was being waged entirely for the sake of the Napoleonic dynasty, it would bring no benefit to their own party. Nevertheless, beyond these dissenting factions, the general populace of France was greatly stirred, eager to bring glory to their nation and to secure victory...
