On July 6th, the French government sent a dispatch to its Ambassador residing in Berlin, Count Benedetti, stating as follows: "Prussia must forthwith abandon the proposal to elect Prince Leopold, and moreover must remove his name from the register of candidates for succession to the Spanish throne. As Spain is by nature a country that ought to defer to us as its superior, there is no reason whatsoever to so readily permit another power to place someone upon its throne. You are therefore to act in full accordance with this intent, and ensure that the throne is treated with due respect and not lightly bestowed upon any person."
