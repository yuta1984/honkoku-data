Now, Prince Leopold, disregarding the King of Prussia's disapproval of the proposed accession, falsely spread word that the King of Prussia had in fact consented to the plan. On July 4th, 1870, General Prim notified Señor Olózaga, the Spanish Ambassador residing in Paris, that the resolution to elect Prince Leopold had been passed in the Cortes.
