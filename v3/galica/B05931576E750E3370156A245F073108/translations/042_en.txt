The draft treaty proposals contained in the aforementioned circular letter were all abolished in 1866, notwithstanding France's attempts to intimidate and overwhelm the other powers. As for its articles, they were newly revised and rewritten in light of the changes that resulted in the loss of Belgium and the South German states. However, these two territories did not come entirely under the possession of Prussia. Nevertheless, for the sake of national tranquility, it was deemed best to escape from under Napoleon's jurisdiction. Although this was requested, no reply permitting it was yet received.
