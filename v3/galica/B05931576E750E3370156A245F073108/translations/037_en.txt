At Headquarters
　　　　Napoleon
　　To the Soldiers

While the armies of both nations were being mustered at the front, and before the exchange of arms had yet begun, the Prime Minister of Prussia, Count Bismarck, took the secret document containing the terms that had been sent by Napoleon III to Prussia in May of eighteen hundred and sixty-six, added minor revisions thereto, and attached a letter setting forth the various matters that had led to the provocation of the present war.
