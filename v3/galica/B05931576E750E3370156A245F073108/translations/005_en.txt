At the end of May and the beginning of June, General Prim met with the French Emperor (Napoleon III) at Biarritz, and after completing their discussions and returning, he promptly determined that Prince Leopold, the second son of Prince Karl Anton of the House of Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen, should be placed on the vacant Spanish throne. When his name was put forward in the Spanish assembly (the Cortes), the majority of the delegates approved his selection. The reason this prince had gained support was entirely because he had previously advocated to the members of the assembly that there would be a petition for his election to that position.
