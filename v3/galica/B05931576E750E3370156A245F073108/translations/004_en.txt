Maintaining cordial relations with Prussia and frequently exchanging courtesies was likely a strategy to alienate France and provoke its jealousy and suspicion. General Prim of Spain, who was on particularly close terms with the French Emperor (Napoleon III), indicated his intention to place the eldest son of the House of Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen on the Spanish throne. When one of the Hohenzollern princes expressed a desire for the position, Prim informed the Prussian minister residing in Spain of his intention to place this person on the Spanish throne, and sought to gauge his response.
