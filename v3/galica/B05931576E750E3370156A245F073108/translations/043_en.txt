The Luxembourg affair was set aside for the time being, and France drew up a clear written document concerning the arrangement whereby France would take Belgium as its own possession and Prussia would take the South German states as its own, reiterating and elaborating upon the previous articles. It was said that these revised articles were drafted by Count Benedetti alone on his own authority. One cannot but doubt the claim that the Emperor did not approve them and that the ambassador wrote them up on his own initiative, because they are identical in nature to what was fabricated four years earlier — threatening with military force in order to manipulate and select the terms of the agreement.
