Mr. George Ripley of the Tribune, being in Berlin at this time, was able to learn the facts of the exchange between the Ambassador and the King. He thereupon exercised his remarkable literary talent to record their dialogue. His account is exceedingly detailed and precise, and may therefore be taken as reliable evidence of the true facts of the matter.
