Also, a document called the Hyakuren-shō records that on the eleventh day of the first month of Shōji 1, Yoritomo became ill and took Buddhist vows, and died on the thirteenth day. That his end came so swiftly may also serve as evidence. Yoritomo's son Yoriie was killed by his younger brother Sanetomo, and Sanetomo was in turn stabbed by his nephew Kugyō, who also died immediately thereafter, so that the house was finally succeeded by the Fujiwara clan.
