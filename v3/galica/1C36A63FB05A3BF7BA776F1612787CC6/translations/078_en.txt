Kitakusan Seikōji Temple - Originally located in Sakura village, temple lands of fifty koku {{Marginal note: Nineteenth year of Tenshō, Kanoto-u, eleventh month}} Jōdo sect, a branch temple of Sanen-zan in Edo. The principal image is Amida Nyorai, and the founding priest is called Geppō Shōnin. According to the temple's tradition, during the Tenshō era, the second-generation priest Musan-shi carried the sacred tooth relic of Lord Daiju-ji and descended to Kantō, traveling to various places. Due to karmic connection, he came to reside at this temple, selected a pure place within the temple grounds, and had it buried there. Later, when the Divine Lord went hawking to Tōgane, he heard that the priest Musan was residing in this province. Upon inquiry, [the priest] immediately went to pay respects at Tōgane and reported that the sacred tooth relic had been buried at this place.
