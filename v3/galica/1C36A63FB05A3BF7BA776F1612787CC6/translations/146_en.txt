○According to the "Record of Military Disturbances in Sagami Province" (Sōshū Hyōranki), Volume 3, "The Battle of Oyumi Yoshiaki," when Yoshiaki of Oyumi Palace's power became extensive, being naturally an extravagant person, it was heard that he plotted to subjugate the Kantō region, surpass the main family line, and become the leader of Kantō. Therefore, there was a private request from the Lord of Koga to Ujitsuna to subjugate Lord Oyumi. Ujitsuna also thought that since Yoshiaki's power was strong, it would not bode well for himself either, so he immediately accepted the request and prepared to march against Oyumi with the combined forces of his domain territories. At this point, the various houses of the eight provinces petitioned, saying, "Yoshiaki is an unparalleled great general of recent times and a person who should inherit the Kubō's position, so how can you subjugate him? Simply make peace and in the future establish him as a lord and install him in Kamakura." However, Ujitsuna ultimately did not heed this advice, and it was heard that he had already set out. When Yoshiaki heard this, he commanded, "Quickly rush to intercept them midway," appointing his younger brother Motoyori and his son, the Young Lord of Oyumi, as vanguard generals, designating Satomi Yoshihiro as vice-general, mobilizing military forces from Bōshū and both Sōshū provinces, setting up camp at Kōnodai in the same province, and waiting with Ichikawa positioned in front.
