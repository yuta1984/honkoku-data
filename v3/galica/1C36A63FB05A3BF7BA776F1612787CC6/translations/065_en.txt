1. Taneyori (Tō Rokurō Dayū, later Junior Fifth Rank Lower Grade)

2. Shigetan (Tarō Hyōe-no-jō, Buddhist name Kakuzen, later Heita Mushadokoro Hyōe-no-jō Nyūdō. Received transmission of the Way of Poetry from Dainagon Tamie and Udaijin Sanetomo)

In the second year of Genkyū (Kinoto-ushi, 1205), on the 22nd day of the first month, during the subjugation of Hatakeyama Shigetada, with General Hōjō Yoshitoki, vanguard Kuzu Hyōe-no-jō, rear guard Sakai Heiji, Nin(mu) Tsunehide, Ōsuka Shirō Tanenobu, Kokubu Gorō Tanemichi, and Sōma Gorō Yoshitane, Shigetan achieved military merit in battle at Futamatakawa in Musashi Province.

On the 27th day of the fourth month of Kennin 4, following the example of his father who had served since coming of age, he expressed his desire to serve. On the 27th day of the seventh month of Kenpō 2 (Kinoe-inu, 1214), he served as escort at the dedication of Ōkura-ishi Daijiji Temple. On the 27th day of the fifth month of Jōkyū 6 (Tsuchinoe-uma, 1218), he served as escort for the Right General's ceremonial visit to Tsurugaoka. On the 27th day of the first month of Jōkyū 7 (Tsuchinoto-u, 1219), he served as escort for the Shogun Right Minister's ceremonial visit.
