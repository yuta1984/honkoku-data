What Napoleon III should leave as a legacy to France is an enormous national debt. In 1853, that debt was 1,133,238,940 yuan. By 1868, this number had increased by one and a half times, namely to 2,766,344,622 yuan. This is the combined total of principal borrowed from reserve treasuries and gold and silver deposit dealers, plus military funds and debts from other sources, amounting to approximately 173,200,000 yuan.
