They showed little loyalty to France, instead fighting the enemy recklessly, and in their desire to satisfy their greed, they obtained opportunities to plunder their own countrymen. Having gained such reckless military forces, the field armies and the garrison troops of "Paris" and other cities combined would have reached a total of one million. However, due to casualties, prisoners, and desertion, it is believed that by around October 15th, France's military personnel had been reduced to 650,000 men.
