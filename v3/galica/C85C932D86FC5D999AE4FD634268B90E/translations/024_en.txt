Prussia's tax revenue and expenditures have remained almost constant and unchanged since 1665, and the fact that their annual accounting calculations show no deficit is truly considered a miracle among the ledger books of all European nations. This tax revenue reached the large sum of 31.5 million yuan in 1869 through direct and indirect taxes, and reached the high amount of 14,180,444 yuan through customs duties.
