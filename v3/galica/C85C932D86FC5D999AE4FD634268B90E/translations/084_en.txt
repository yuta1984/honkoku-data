The Chassepot rifle, though needle-fire in its mechanism, differs greatly from the needle-fire rifle manufactured in Prussia. This rifle was a new invention of 1863 (though improvements were added to it in 1866) by one "M. Chassepot," who served as the Inspector-General of Arms for France. The reason this rifle came to be manufactured was that the French government had long desired to develop a new type of rifle that, while differing from Prussian weapons, would be equal to them in capability.
