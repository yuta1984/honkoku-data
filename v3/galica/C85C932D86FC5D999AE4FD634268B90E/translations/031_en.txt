Regarding whether the peoples of Prussia and France can foster friendship above this, their situations differ greatly. Education in the German states, particularly in Prussia, has spread throughout almost the entire country. In 1867, the population of the North German Confederation was 29,653,038, the population of South Germany was 8,869,328, for a total of 38,522,366 people. Among them, those who cannot read books and write are very few, and most have received adequate education in government schools.
