Four days after the battle, a correspondent from the "New York Tribune" (the name of a newspaper company) who had remained on the battlefield carefully gathering details of the engagement filed a report written with such skill that it reads as though one is witnessing the battle firsthand. This is from the newspaper dated August 11th. The text reads as follows:

　　Yesterday I passed through the battlefield of "Spicheren." This place was the site of the fierce battle that occurred last Saturday, and those engaged on both sides at this battlefield are said to have numbered over 20,000 men each. ○ Taking the road from Saarbrücken to Forbach, I ascended a hill.
