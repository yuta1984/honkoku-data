...being unable to make practical military preparations is truly pitiable. In contrast, how magnificent are Prussia's military preparations! Their soldiers are selected by searching throughout the country for those with strong, healthy bodies, making them soldiers who have passed selection tests. Their officers are chosen only from those who have been trained in geographical and topographical studies as well as military arts sufficient to command their respective units, and they are given their positions after passing selection examinations. Military preparations consisting of such officers and soldiers are truly fearsome, and how could they consist merely of the needle-fire rifles and improved artillery units that the French Emperor constantly fears and dreads?
