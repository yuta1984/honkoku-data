Louis Napoleon had few visitors during his stay in America, and moreover his relatives - "Joseph Bonaparte" in Bordentown and "Patterson Bonaparte" in Baltimore - both treated him with great coldness. Louis Napoleon had acted thoughtlessly in the Strasbourg uprising, and since his usual associates were all of poor character, he had no way of entering respectable society.
