Since his elevation to the position of president, and especially after the restoration of imperial rule and the establishment of the empire, the French people's desire to reclaim all the provinces that Napoleon I had conquered became intense, and the momentum to forcibly seize them began to flourish for the first time.

When the new French emperor ascended to the throne and issued proclamations celebrating the eternal peace and prosperity of the empire, he also plotted to wage a war of revenge at "Waterloo" in order to achieve the objectives he had long harbored in his heart, and furthermore conspired to expand France's borders eastward to the Rhine River and southeastward to the Adriatic Sea.
