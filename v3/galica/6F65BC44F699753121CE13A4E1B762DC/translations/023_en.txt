"General Edmond Leboeuf" of the French army
held the office of "Minister of War" and "Chief of Staff"
at the beginning of the war. Although this person was not
of the highest honor, he was one with considerable reputation
among the French generals. This person was born
on October 5, 1809, entered the École Polytechnique
in Paris, remained at the military school in "Metz"
to study military science, and at the age of twenty-eight became
a "Captain," then after nine years became a "Major"
of the artillery corps. In 1848 he became "Vice-Director"
