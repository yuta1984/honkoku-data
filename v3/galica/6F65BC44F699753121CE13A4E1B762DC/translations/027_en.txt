Having resigned from his position, both the people and soldiers regretted being unprepared for war. However, he deliberately did not tell the Emperor about this incident because it would only increase his own fault.

"Charles Guillaume Marie Apollinaire Antoine Cousin-Montauban, Comte de Palikao" replaced "Lebœuf" as Minister of War and later became Prime Minister. He was an elderly man of distinguished family reputation, superior to his predecessors. He wielded the power of life and death, was righteous and incorruptibly honest. However, having acquired the customs of the "Algerian" war, he naturally possessed a harsh and cruel disposition.
