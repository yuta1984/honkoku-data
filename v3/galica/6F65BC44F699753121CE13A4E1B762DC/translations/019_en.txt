The position of Chief of Staff is of utmost importance and possesses the authority of independent judgment. However, under the government of Napoleon III, all such officials became like instruments of the Emperor, merely following orders. Thus, ministers like Ollivier or Rouher, and the military administration generals like Leboeuf or de Palikao, also became like the Emperor's hands and feet, their employment and dismissal subject entirely to the Emperor's will.

As for the character of Ollivier and Rouher, there is nothing particularly noteworthy to observe. However, while Rouher was a person of notable authority, his views were narrow and rigid, and he flattered Napoleon III
