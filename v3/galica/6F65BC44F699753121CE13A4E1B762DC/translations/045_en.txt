He rescued "Marshal Niel" from danger and truly exerted himself at the battle of "Solferino," achieving victory for the French army. In this war, his great contribution led the French army to triumph. By virtue of his position as "Marshal" (an office to which he was appointed in 1856), he became a member of the French council. With this authority, in March 1861, he vigorously opposed the matter of financial support to the Pope of Rome. From June to August 1862, he commanded the camp at "Chalons." Thereafter, at "Lyon," he was promoted to "command" of the fourth army. This position lasted for two years
