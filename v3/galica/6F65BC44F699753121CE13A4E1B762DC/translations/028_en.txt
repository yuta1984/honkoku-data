He entered the École Polytechnique for his education, became a cavalry officer at age 28, and made his name in "Algeria." At age 40, he became a "Major," and after nine years became a commander of the "Spahis" (the name of an African irregular but formidable cavalry unit). In 1851, he was promoted to "Brigade" commander (unit name), with the responsibility of divisional commander. He was also promoted to the rank of "Major General."
