At a young age, he was wounded in the war of "Danzig." In the distinguished battle at "Warnitzwalde," a bullet shot off one of his fingers, and another bullet severely penetrated his thigh. However, being a brave and fierce general by nature, even though walking became inconvenient for him, in May 1813 he hoped to mount a horse and march out to the war at "Bautzen." Also, in 1814 he fought almost everywhere in the battles in "France." And he entered "Paris" with the allied troops.
