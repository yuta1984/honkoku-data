Moreover, the salary of those who served as soldiers in "Prussia" at that time was extremely meager. Nevertheless, his determination never wavered, and he endeavored to master all the modern languages of "Europe." This would later prove to be of great utility. From that time, "Moltke" studied science, and his fellow student "Alexander von Humboldt" particularly did not favor studying, nor did he care for the academic subjects such as climate and geology that this accomplished young man (referring to Moltke) studied.
