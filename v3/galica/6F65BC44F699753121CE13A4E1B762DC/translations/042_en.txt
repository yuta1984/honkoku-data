In 1841, he went again to Africa. There, leading a dragoon unit of the "Light Infantry" and soldiers of the 64th "Regiment" of the "Rhine," he suppressed the rebellion of "Bougie." After continuous battles lasting eight months, he forced many enemies of the "Kabyle" tribes to surrender. He fought in various regions of northern Africa for nearly eight years. With his courage and boldness, he led the brave forward and penetrated into the interior.
