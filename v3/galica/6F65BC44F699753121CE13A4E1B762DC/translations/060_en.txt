However, the general of the Prussian division was appointed to Prince Friedrich Karl on December 15, 1863. It was already thought that the fortified position of Düppel was one of the largest Danish fortresses, and also one of the positions through which the German armies should advance to the Danish border, and this reasoning was unanimous. Therefore, it was decided to besiege and assault that position. This location being in the northern latitude, the severe bitter winter greatly hindered their plans, and the sequence of first firing artillery and second destroying the fortifications was obstructed until April 1864.
