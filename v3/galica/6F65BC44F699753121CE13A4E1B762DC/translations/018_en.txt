At the time when the Empire fell, France had no one among its prime ministers and generals who possessed both diplomatic authority and skill like Bismarck and von Moltke. However, such diplomatic officials were not considered necessary in France, because the Emperor himself presided over diplomatic authority, and the exchange of correspondence was not entrusted to ministers.
