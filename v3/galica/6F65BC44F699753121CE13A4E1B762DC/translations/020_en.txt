In a certain year he became the leader of that party, but from 1863 onwards, when he was suspected by his party of harboring divided loyalties, he sought by all means to curry favor with the Emperor. After being treated with considerable favor by the Emperor, he was appointed Prime Minister in the winter of 1870, succeeding to Rouher's position. In executing his policies, all matters were handled with procrastination and nothing was accomplished. Moreover, being of poor character and having an irascible temperament, he received merciless humiliation from his former associates, namely the democratic political party.
