After six years of continuous warfare against the "Kabyle tribes" and other warlike peoples, "Bazaine" received orders to join foreign military units. In order to suppress uprisings, he departed for that region in 1837. In 1839, he participated in expeditions toward "Miliana" and "Morocco," went to "Algeria," and for several years served as governor of "Tlemcen," a small territory in "Arabia." During the "Crimean" War, his military prowess and talent in his duties seemed to distinguish him remarkably.
