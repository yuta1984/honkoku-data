Shortly before the Franco-Prussian War was about to break out, he returned from Algeria to his homeland and worked very hard to rebuild his military forces. Marshal MacMahon is a brave and courageous warrior with a reputation for loyalty and righteousness. Even though he had been trained for such a long time in Algeria, he would probably be unsuitable for modern civilized warfare. Moreover, if it were a war requiring wisdom and strategy, how could he be fit for such a task? Nevertheless, he should be considered one of the most well-regarded among France's military officers.
