He was newly ennobled with the title of "Count" of "Palikao." However, this matter was strongly rejected by the "Corps Législatif" (Legislative Body) and was not carried out. In 1865, he became commander of the Fourth Corps, and in 1870 became a member of the Privy Council. Since this person had previously distinguished himself in battles against the "Kabyles," "Berbers," and "China," it would certainly be impossible for him to engage in combat against such a civilized and formidable army. Moreover, the "Prussian" people declared emphatically that his strategies were like those of the barbarous and uncivilized warfare of "Turkey" and "Persia."
