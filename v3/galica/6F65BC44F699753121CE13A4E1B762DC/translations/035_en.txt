As he rode closer to the ravine, he saw "Arabs" from their encampment pursuing him from all eight directions. At this point, he thought that he must choose one of two options: either leap into the abyss or throw himself upon the enemy. He adjusted his horse's direction, gave it one lash of the whip, and leaped over the treacherous path of the ravine. The pursuing "Arabs" were frightened and did not chase him far, but the brave soldiers fired bullets like a torrential rain from behind him. Due to this, his horse's hooves received wounds, and it is said that this was communicated to the other side as well. When it came to attacking "Constantine," his honor became increasingly remarkable, and due to his transcendent talent, his good reputation spread rapidly, and he received abundant rewards
