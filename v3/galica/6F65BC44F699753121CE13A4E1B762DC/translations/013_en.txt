Moltke performed various duties in a short period of time, rising to the position of deputy commander, and finally became the administrative supervisor of Prussia. At this point, Moltke used his talents with careful attention to produce plans for reforming the Prussian military system. This success became of great utility to the Prussian army in later years. In 1866, at the beginning of the war between Austria and Prussia, the geographical knowledge he had acquired proved to be of immense effectiveness.
