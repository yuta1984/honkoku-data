The Great Ming refers to China. Because they change their dynastic names generation after generation, there is no fixed title. The people themselves call their land "Chūka" (Middle Kingdom). It was the Taizu Emperor of the Great Ming who established fifteen provinces and set up two capitals and thirteen circuits.
The reason Japan calls China "Tōdo" (Tang Land) is because during the Great Tang period, friendly relations with Japan were flourishing. Also, the reason for calling it "Kara" is that the first people from foreign countries who came to Japan in ancient times were from the Great Gaya Kingdom, one of the Three Han kingdoms, so foreign countries are referred to as "Kara."
