After some time had passed, they did not grant permission, and in May 1846, when "Louis Napoleon" escaped from "Ham" where he had been imprisoned, the French government completely refused the matter of "Louis's" succession. "Louis Napoleon" disguised himself as a workman through the mediation of his physician "Conneau," and carrying a plank on his shoulder, walked out of the fortress, crossed the Belgian border, and finally reached England.

Now, "Louis Napoleon" had demonstrated evidence of literary talent in books published during his years of confinement, and on the other hand, had greatly made his name known through the uprisings in both "Strasbourg" and "Boulogne"
