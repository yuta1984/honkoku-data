The brave "Maximilian" who had been lured into the enterprise was unfortunately executed, and the "Carlota" party raised false grievances, leading to unending turmoil. Consequently, "Napoleon" developed great fear about inciting such rebellion and was greatly troubled in spirit.

At this point, Napoleon III attempted combat by engaging in a small war with "Annam" and "China," but the results were insufficient to compensate for the military expenses incurred, and there were no benefits adequate to satisfy the desires of the "French" people.
