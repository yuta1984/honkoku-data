Kitabatake Dainagon (Major Counselor) Minamoto no Chikafusa served as an advisor to the Southern Emperor. Tōin Saneharu and Shijō Takasuke handled and reported all matters to the throne. Chikafusa was the father of Akiie. He was widely learned and talented in scholarship, and authored many works. His son Akiuobu was appointed provincial governor (kokushi) of Ōshū, and his next son Akinori was appointed provincial governor of Ise.

In the seventh month of the second year (of Ryakuō/Engen), Wakiya Yoshisuke gathered troops from the northern provinces and captured Kuromaru Castle in Echizen. The castle's lord, Takatsune, requested reinforcements, and as a large force set out from Kyoto, Yoshisuke was defeated and fled to Mino Province, then made his way to Yoshino. The castles in the northern provinces that had sided with Yoshisuke all fell. Only the warrior Hata Tokiyoshi, with a mere twenty-seven men, held out at Takasu Castle. Though they endured for a long time, they too were eventually defeated as the months passed, and the northern provinces were pacified.

In the twelfth month, Ichijō Tsunemichi resigned from the position of Minister of the Left (Sadaijin). Kujō Udaijin (Minister of the Right) Michitaka replaced him.
