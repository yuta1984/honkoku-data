(Continued from previous page) ...Shōben Tsunetō of Nakamikado was dispatched to the Kantō (Kamakura) to offer an apology (on behalf of the court regarding Munetaka's return to the capital). As the warrior house (the Bakufu) raised no particular objection, peace was restored throughout the land. Munetaka had been in his position from the fourth year of Kenchō (1252) until this year, a tenure of fifteen years. Meanwhile, in Kamakura, through the arrangements of Tokimune and Masamura, Munetaka's son Koreyasu—who was a mere three years of age—was installed and established as lord. In the same month, Koreyasu was appointed Seii Taishōgun (Shogun) and conferred the Junior Fourth Rank (Jushiihon).

In the first month of the fourth year (of Bun'ei, 1267), Fuyutada resigned as Inner Minister (Naidaijin). Ietsune, the son of Regent (Kanpaku) Sanetsune, was appointed Inner Minister. In the tenth month, Retired Emperor Go-Saga made an imperial visit (miyuki) to Kasuga (Shrine). A dedication ceremony (kuyō) was held for the Yuishikiron (Vijñaptimātratāsiddhi-śāstra) written in the Retired Emperor's own hand. In the same month, the former Chancellor (Sadaijin) of Saionji, Kinsuke, passed away. He was forty-five years of age. His father, the lay-monk Chancellor Sanetō, was still living at this time. In the twelfth month, Ichijō Sanetsune resigned as Regent. Konoe Sadaijin (Minister of the Left) Motohira became Regent.
