His Majesty (Emperor Go-Daigo) had already been restored to the throne, and dismissed Takatsukasa Fuyunori from the position of regent (kanpaku), dismissed Imadegawa Kanesue from the position of grand minister of state (daijō-daijin) and demoted him to former right minister. Many others were also dismissed from their posts. Right Minister Suehira, Interior Minister Minamoto no Michiaki, and others even took the tonsure. Nijō Michihira was reappointed as left minister and made head of the Fujiwara clan, putting him in charge of all affairs. However, the position of regent was not filled, presumably because His Majesty intended to exercise direct imperial rule himself. Kuga Nagamichi was reappointed as right minister, and Tōin Kinkata was reappointed as interior minister. All those who had been exiled during the Genkō Rebellion returned to the capital.
