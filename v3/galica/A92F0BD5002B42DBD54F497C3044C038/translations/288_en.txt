In the first year of Manjū era, third month, there were many robbers in the capital. The Imperial Police (kebiishi) captured them. In the ninth month, there was an imperial visit to Yorimichi's mansion. In the twelfth month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Kintō retired. He was the son of Yoritada and a master of poetry and music. The Wakan Rōeishū was compiled by this person.

In the second year, eighth month, Court Lady Fujiwara no Kishi was Michinaga's third daughter. She served Crown Prince Atsuyoshi and was favored by him. She gave birth to a prince but died after three days. She was nineteen years old. From the reign of Emperor Ichijō onward, only fortunate events had continued for Michinaga, but at this point he encountered sorrow. Kishi was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank.
