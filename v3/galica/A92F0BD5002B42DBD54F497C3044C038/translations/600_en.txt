In the first month of the second year (of Ōan, 1369), Kusunoki Masanori submitted a request to surrender to the warrior government (the shogunate), and in response, Yoshimitsu sent him an official letter (mikyōsho).

In the third month, in order to support Kusunoki (Masanori), Hosokawa Umanosuke Yorimoto and Akamatsu Hangwan and others were dispatched from Kyoto to the southern region.

In the fourth month, Masanori entered the capital (Kyoto), first meeting with Yoriyuki, and afterwards had an audience with Yoshimitsu.

Also in the fourth month, the monks (shuto) of Mount Hiei petitioned to have Nanzenji temple destroyed. As neither the court nobles nor the warrior government issued a ruling, the monks staged a riotous appeal (gōso), carrying the sacred palanquin (mikoshi) of Hiyoshi Shrine to the Imperial Palace, threatening to abandon it there and set it on fire. Sasaki Sōei prevented this, and so the mountain monks returned to their mountain and the Imperial Palace was unharmed. A document written in the Emperor's own hand (shinkan) was bestowed upon Sōei in recognition of his service. The sacred palanquin was moved to Gion Shrine, and later returned to the mountain.

In the ninth month, Shiba Yoshimasa fought a battle against Momoi Naotsune in Etchū Province. Naotsune was defeated and took refuge in Matsukura Castle. The local warriors (kokujin) all pledged their allegiance to the shogunate.
