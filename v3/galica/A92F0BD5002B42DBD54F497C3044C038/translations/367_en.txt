In the second year, second month, Imperial Consort Taira no Tokuko became Empress. In the third month, there was an imperial visit to Hiyoshi Shrine.

In the seventh month, the Cloistered Emperor moved to the newly constructed palace at Sanjō Karasumaru. Naruchika, Middle Counselor and administrator of the Cloistered Emperor's affairs, was promoted from Third Rank to Junior Second Rank. In the tenth month, there were imperial visits to Inari and Gion shrines.

In the twelfth month, Motofusa resigned from his positions as regent and chancellor to become kampaku (chief advisor to the emperor).

In the third year, fourth month, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo shrines. In the tenth month, there was an imperial visit for the consecration ceremony of Saishōkō-in, the temple built at the request of Kenshunmon-in. Munemori was promoted to Junior Second Rank as a reward for organizing the ceremony.

In the fourth year, first month, there was an imperial visit for audience with both the Cloistered Emperor and Kenshunmon-in. In the third month, Ushiwaka, the youngest son of the late Left Horse Bureau Chief Minamoto no Yoshitomo, secretly left the capital and went to Mutsu Province, where he stayed with Fujiwara no Hidehira. Ushiwaka came of age and took the name Kurō Yoshitsune. He was sixteen years old at the time. In the seventh month, Major Counselor Taira no Shigemori also assumed the position of Right General.
