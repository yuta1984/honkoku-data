Emperor Kōtoku was the younger brother of Emperor Kōgyoku. After Iruka was executed, he ascended to the throne, receiving the abdication from his elder sister Kōgyoku. At this time, Ōtomo no Nagatoko and Inukami no Takebe, wearing golden quivers, stood to the left and right before the throne. All officials paid their respects in rows. They presented an honorific title to Kōgyoku, calling her Kōsobo no Mikoto (Imperial Grandmother). Prince Naka no Ōe was made Crown Prince. Abe no Kurahashi Maro was made Left Minister, and Soga no Kurayamada Maro (referred to as Ishikawa in the Nihon Shoki) was made Right Minister. This was the beginning of the Left and Right Ministers. Kamatari was granted the golden crown and given the office of Inner Minister, with increased stipends, placing him above all officials and entrusting him with governing the realm. That the nation was peaceful after Iruka's destruction was due to this man's merit. Later he was also granted the purple crown and his stipends were further increased. Takamuko no Genri and the monk Min were made hakase (learned advisors). Both had entered Tang China and studied there. For the first time, an era name was established, called Taika year one. The names of the eight ministries and hundred offices were all decided at this time. Nineteen types of caps were created, and ranks were determined by their colors. The capital was moved to Naniwa...
