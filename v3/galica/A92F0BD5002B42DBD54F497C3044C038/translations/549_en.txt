In the tenth month of the third year [of Karyaku, 1328], the former Shogun Prince Hisaaki passed away. He was fifty-five years of age.

In the twelfth month, Sonun Hōshinnō, the son of the Emperor, was appointed Tendai Zasu (Chief Abbot of the Tendai sect). This Prince favored martial valor and secretly harbored the ambition to strike down Kamakura. He is none other than the Prince of Tōtō-no-Miya (Prince Daitō).

In the sixth month of the first year of Gentoku [1329], Sanjō former Shōkoku Saneshige passed away. He was seventy-one years of age.

In the twelfth month, Koga former Shōkoku Minamoto no Michikatsuo passed away. He was seventy-three years of age.

In the first month of the second year [of Gentoku, 1330], Nijō Michihira resigned as Kampaku. Konoe Ufu (Minister of the Right) Tsunetada became Kampaku.

In the second month, Naidaijin Mototsugu was appointed Ufu (Minister of the Right), and Koga Dainagon Minamoto no Nagatōru became Naidaijin (Minister of the Interior).

In the third month, Nagatōru resigned, and Tōin Dainagon Kinkata was appointed Naifu (Minister of the Interior). In the same month, [the Emperor] made imperial visits to Tōdai-ji, Kōfuku-ji, and Enryaku-ji, secretly conspiring with the monks there to plot an attack against the warrior houses (the shogunate). Sonun Hōshinnō was the ringleader of this scheme.
