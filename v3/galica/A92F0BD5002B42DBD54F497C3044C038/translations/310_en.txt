"It would be proper to issue the decree today," and thus the decision was made immediately. Emperor Go-Reizei ascended to the throne, and Takahito was appointed Crown Prince at the age of twelve. Yoshinobu was made the Crown Prince's tutor (Tōgū no Dayū). Although Emperor Go-Suzaku passed away shortly after, since the Crown Prince had already been determined, there were no particular complications. Yoshinobu was Yorimichi's younger brother. In the 4th month of Chiryaku 4 (1068), Emperor Go-Reizei died, and Takahito ascended to the throne at the age of thirty-five. From the time this emperor was Crown Prince, he summoned a learned man named Ōe no Masafusa to study with him. He also listened to the Buddhist teachings of Tendai and Shingon schools. Fujiwara no Yorimichi had served as regent and kampaku for three reigns since Emperor Go-Ichijō, controlling the government for fifty years. Since the emperor had been displeased with him from his time as Crown Prince, after his accession, Yorimichi submitted a petition and withdrew to his villa in Uji.
