In the third year, on the second day of the first month, there was an imperial visit for the New Year audience. In the same month, Gyōbukyō Taira no Tadamori died at the age of fifty-eight. His eldest son Kiyomori succeeded him. In the fourth month, a mysterious bird called a nue flew over the Imperial Palace crying repeatedly. Hyōgonokami Minamoto no Yorimasa received an imperial command and shot it down. Yorinaga, receiving the imperial order, bestowed an imperial sword upon Yorimasa. It is said that he also bestowed upon him the court lady Ayamemae. Yorimasa was a descendant of Settsu no kami Yorimitsu and was a warrior accomplished in both military arts and Japanese poetry.

In the first year of Kyūju, in the ninth month, Kuga Udaijin Minamoto no Masasada took Buddhist vows at the age of sixty-one. He died some years later. This person called his villa residence "Nakain." In the eighth month, Udaishō Fujiwara no Sanenō was transferred to Sadaishō, and Yorinaga's eldest son, Chūnagon Fujiwara no Kanetsune, concurrently held the position of Udaishō. He was seventeen years old at the time.
