From his advanced age, he had children, and after his abdication, he had many male and female children from various consorts year after year. He also secretly visited a sister from a different mother, and an imperial princess was born. Even after taking the tonsure, he had many children. In the seventh month, former Daijō-daijin Tokudaiji Kimunori passed away at the age of fifty-three. In the twelfth month, Takatsukasa Udaijin Fuyuhira was transferred to Sadaijin, Konoe Naidaijin Iehira was promoted to Udaijin, and Ichijō Dainagon Sanieie was appointed Naidaijin after serving as Jun-daijin. In the same month, former Udaijin Saionji Kinhira was stripped of his fiefs in Izu and Iyo provinces as well as the Sama-ryō by imperial censure from Retired Emperor Go-Uda. In reality, this was carried out upon the request of the military government.

In the first year of Tokuji (1306), in the second month, Kinhira received imperial pardon and resumed court service, and all his domains were returned to him. This was because the military government petitioned on his behalf. In the sixth month, Ichijō Sanieie resigned from Naidaijin. In the twelfth month, Iemasa was appointed Daijō-daijin. Nijō Dainagon
