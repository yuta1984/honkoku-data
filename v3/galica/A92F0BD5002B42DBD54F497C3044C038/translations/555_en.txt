[Continued from the previous page] While his son Yoshitaka threw himself before the enemy and died in battle, Prince Moriyoshi managed to escape and hid deep in the mountains. After that, troops from all directions surrounded the fortress at Chihaya and attacked it. Their numbers reached several hundred thousand. Masashige devised various ingenious stratagems to defend against them, causing great losses among the attacking forces. Nitta Yoshisada was also among the besieging force, but secretly communicated with Prince Moriyoshi and returned to Kōzuke Province, intending to raise a righteous army. In the same month, Akamatsu Enshin advanced to Maya Castle in Settsu Province, and in Iyo Province, Doi, Tokuno, and others raised a righteous army. In the third month, Akamatsu Enshin attacked and advanced on the capital. Because of this, the new Emperor made a progress to Rokuhara. Both (northern and southern) Rokuhara headquarters repeatedly engaged Akamatsu in battle. Around this time, in Tsukushi (Kyushu), Kikuchi Jakua, Shōni Myōe, and Ōtomo Gukan conspired to attack the Tandai (Deputy), Hōjō Hidetoki. However, the other two instead came to Hidetoki's rescue, and Kikuchi was defeated and killed. In the same month, the Retired Emperor Go-Daigo secretly escaped from Oki Province, made his way to Hōki Province, relied upon Nawa Nagatoshi, and entered Mount Funakami (Funagamiyama).
