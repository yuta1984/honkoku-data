In the first year of Eishō (1046), first month, Right Minister Fujiwara no Sukesuke died at age ninety. He was the grandson of Saneyori. He was called the Right Minister of Ono. The records he made are called "Shōyūki." In the seventh month, Princess Shōshi, daughter of Emperor Go-Ichijō, was made Empress.

In the second year, eighth month, Norimichi was transferred from Interior Minister to Right Minister, and Major Counselor Yorimune became Interior Minister. Both were younger brothers of Yorimichi.

In the fourth year, eleventh month, there was a poetry competition in the palace. This had been a tradition carried on generation after generation since the reign of Emperor Murakami. In the twelfth month, there was an imperial visit to Kasuga. Buddha relics were deposited one grain each at shrines throughout the provinces.
