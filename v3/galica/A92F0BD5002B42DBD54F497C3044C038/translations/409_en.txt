Sukemori and his younger brother Gorō Tokimune secretly entered Kudō Suketsune's residence at night and killed him, avenging their father. Afterward, they burst into Yoritomo's lodgings and wounded about ten people with their swords. Since this was a sudden incident in the middle of the night, it caused great commotion. When they encountered Nitta Tadatsune, Sukemori was killed. Since Tokimune continued to advance, Yoritomo himself was about to come out. While Ōtomo Yoshinao restrained him, a man of great strength called Gorōmaru captured Tokimune. Yoritomo directly questioned him about the circumstances and was about to pardon him, but at the request of Suketsune's son, Tokimune was sentenced to death.

Originally, Sukemori's father was called Kawazu Sukeyasu. Sukeyasu's father was called Itō Sukechika. When Suketsune's father died, since (Suketsune) was still young, Sukechika took charge of his domain out of family loyalty. However, after Suketsune came of age, disputes arose between them over territorial rights. Suketsune directed his anger toward Sukeyasu and secretly killed him, pretending not to know anything about it. But as Sukemori and Tokimune gradually grew up, (they learned) that he was their father's enemy.
