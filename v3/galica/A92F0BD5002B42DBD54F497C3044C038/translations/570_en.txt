In the first month of Ryakuō 1 (1338), Akiie, Yoshioki, and others marched on the capital. Along the way, at Aonogahara in Nōshū (Mino Province), they engaged in battle with Takauji's general Momoi Naōtsune and others. In the same month, Yoshisada and Yoshisuke emerged from Somayama and captured the castle of Echizen-fu. Ashikaga Takatsune fled and took refuge in Kuromaru Castle. Yoshisada's power flourished in the northern provinces, and he harbored ambitions of attacking Kyoto.

In the second month, as Akiie was proceeding toward Yoshino, he fought multiple battles at Yawata and Nanto (Nara) along the way against Kō no Moronao and Momoi Naōtsune and others.

In the fifth month, Akiie was defeated in battle and died fighting at Abeno in Senshu (Izumi Province). He was twenty-one years old. Akiie's younger brother Akiuobu, along with Nitta Yoshioki, took refuge in Yawata.

In the sixth month, Moronao attacked and defeated the forces at Yawata. Konoe Mototsugu resigned from the position of Kampaku. Ichijō Safu (Minister of the Left) Tsunemichi became Kampaku.
