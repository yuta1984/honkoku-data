Fujiwara no Tokihira was appointed to Dainagon (Major Counselor) and concurrently served as Sadaishō (General of the Left). Minamoto no Hikaru and Minister Kan were made Gondainagon (Provisional Major Counselors). Minister Kan concurrently served as Udaishō (General of the Right) and administered government together with Tokihira. The ministerial posts were left vacant and not yet filled. On the 2nd day of the 7th month, the Emperor abdicated his throne to Crown Prince Atsuhito and retired to Suzaku-in. He was thirty years old at the time. He is also called Teiji-in. Later, because he shaved his head, he is also called Kanpyō Hōō (Dharma Emperor Kanpyō). Initially, in the year following his enthronement, the era name was not changed, and Ninna 4 of Emperor Kōkō was used. Thereafter, the Kanpyō era lasted nine years. In total, he reigned for ten years.

60th Emperor
Emperor Daigo - He was the first imperial son of Uda. His personal name was Atsuhito. His mother was called Fujiwara no Taneko (Inshi), daughter of Middle Counselor Takatō. In Kanpyō 5, he became Crown Prince. In the 9th year, on the 3rd day of the 7th month, he came of age at thirteen years old. On the same day, he received the abdication and ascended the throne.
