The Eighty-First Emperor

Emperor Antoku: Son of the Takakura-in, his personal name was Tokihito. His mother was Kenreimon-in Taira no Tokuko, daughter of the Grand Minister of State and Lay Priest Kiyomori. Born in the eleventh month of Jishō 2 (1178), he received the abdication from the Takakura-in in the second month of Jishō 4 (1180) and ascended the throne at age three. Kiyomori and his wife received imperial decrees granting them quasi-imperial prince status. There was no Chancellor or Regent, and the Cloistered Emperor Go-Shirakawa remained under house arrest at Toba Palace. Though the Retired Emperor Takakura was called the "New Retired Emperor," he did not conduct governmental affairs. The regency existed in name only, and all matters of state, great and small, were entirely at Kiyomori's discretion.

In the third month, the New Retired Emperor made a pilgrimage to Itsukushima in Aki Province. Since this was the deity revered by the Taira clan, Kiyomori was pleased, thinking that if his heart softened, there might be a possibility of releasing the Cloistered Emperor from Toba.
