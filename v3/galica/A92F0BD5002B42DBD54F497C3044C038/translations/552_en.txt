[The Emperor] came out from the mountain and fled, but was captured along the way and taken into Rokuhara. Furthermore, troops were raised to attack the fortress at Akasaka. Masashige held out for a time, then secretly left the castle and hid on Mount Kongō. Sonun hid near the Totsukawa River. Close retainers such as Fujifusa and Suefusa were all taken prisoner. The First Prince, Takayoshi, and all other imperial princes below him were likewise captured alive.

The era names of this reign were: Ōan 2, Genkō 3, Shōchū 2, Karyaku 3, Gentoku 2, and Genkō 1 — totaling thirteen years on the throne.

　　　The 96th Sovereign
Kōgon-in: Personal name (imina), Kazuhito. He was the first son of the Gofushimi-in. His mother was the Kōgimōn-in, daughter of Saionji Kinhira, Minister of the Left. When Emperor Godaigo ascended the throne, it was arranged by Takatoki that Kazuhito be installed as Crown Prince.
