[The Emperor] came out fleeing. He was captured on the road and brought into Rokuhara. 
Again [the Kamakura forces] dispatched troops to attack Akasaka castle. Masashige defended for a while, then secretly left the castle and hid in Mount Kongō. Son'un hid in the vicinity of Totsukawa. The close retainers Fusafusa, Suefusa and others were all imprisoned. The First Prince Takayoshi and the other imperial princes were all taken alive as prisoners. The era names of this reign were Gennō (2 years), Genkō (3 years), Shōchū (2 years), Karyaku (3 years), Gentoku (2 years), and Genkō (1 year).
Total reign: thirteen years

96th Emperor
Emperor Kōgon: His personal name was Kazuhito. He was the first son of Retired Emperor Go-Fushimi. His mother was Kōgimon'in, daughter of Saionji Left Minister Kimihira. When Go-Daigo ascended the throne, by Takatoki's arrangement, [Kazuhito] was made Crown Prince.
In the tenth month of the first year of Genkō, when Go-Daigo was brought from Kasagi to Rokuhara.
