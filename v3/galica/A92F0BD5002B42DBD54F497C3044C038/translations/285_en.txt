He resigned from the position of Minister of the Left, transferred Right Minister Akimitsu to Minister of the Left, made Inner Minister Kinsue Right Minister, and appointed Dōchō's eldest son, Major Counselor Yorimichi, as Inner Minister. Dōchō also transferred the regency to Yorimichi. Yorimichi was twenty-six years old at that time. Yorimichi's younger brother, Middle Counselor Norimichi, became Left General. On the third day of the fifth month, Dōchō set up temporary structures on Ichijō street and distributed alms to over three thousand people. On the ninth day of the same month, Retired Emperor Sanjō passed away at the age of forty-two. On the twenty-seventh day of the sixth month, thieves entered Dōchō's storehouse and stole over 1,300 ryō of gold dust before fleeing. After a month had passed, the thieves were captured in Harima Province. In the eighth month, Crown Prince Atsuakira, due to evil spirits, voluntarily abdicated his position and took the title Koichijō-in, being treated equivalent to a Retired Emperor. His younger brother Atsuyoshi was made Crown Prince. This too was likely the arrangement of Dōchō and his son. The two imperial lines of Reizei and En'yū had alternated on the throne, but at this point the Reizei imperial lineage was severed.
