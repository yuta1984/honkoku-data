In the fifth month of the second year [of Shōchū, 1325], Hino Chūnagon (Middle Counselor) Suketomo and Hino Ushōben (Junior Assistant Controller of the Right) Toshimoto were arrested and taken to Kamakura. This was because these two men were close retainers of the Emperor who were involved in a conspiracy to destroy the Kantō (shogunate).

In the seventh month, Madenokōji Dainagon (Major Counselor) Nobufusa was dispatched to Kamakura, and a written declaration was presented to Takatoki as an apology. As a result of this, Suketomo was exiled to Sado, while Toshimoto was pardoned and returned to the capital. The Imperial Court was thus spared from harm.

In the eighth month, the Zen monk Soseki was appointed abbot (jūji) of Nanzen-ji. From this time onward, the Emperor became deeply devoted to Zen Buddhism.

In the tenth month, the former Shōgun Prince Koreyasu passed away. He was sixty-two years of age.

In the twelfth month, the former Kampaku (Imperial Regent) Ichijō Utsunori passed away. He was thirty-six years of age. He was given the posthumous name Fundarike-in.

In the third month of the first year of Karyaku [1326], Crown Prince Kuniyoshi passed away. He was twenty-four years of age. In the same month, Hōjō Takatoki, due to illness, shaved his head and took the Buddhist name Sōkan. He was twenty-four years of age. He passed the position of Shikken (Regent) to his younger brother, Sakon no Daibu Yasuie, and [Yasuie served] jointly with Kanazawa Sadaaki as Renshō (co-signatory).
