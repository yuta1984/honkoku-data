However, because his father, the lay priest Tadazane, was still alive and loved Yorinaga while hating Tadamichi, he arranged for this to happen. The Retired Emperor made no clear pronouncement either way, and though the Emperor was still young, he was troubled by this matter. After this occurred, though there was a Kampaku, it was as if there were none, and Yorinaga's power flourished. Tadamichi composed poetry and waka, and his calligraphy was extremely excellent. Yorinaga disliked this and constantly devoted himself to learning, becoming well-versed in both Japanese and Chinese classics, ancient and modern. He gathered Fujiwara no Michinori (lay priest Shinzei) and other scholars to hold lectures and discussions. The records he produced were extremely numerous. However, because he was arrogant and sought to push aside Tadamichi and wield power alone, the people of the world called him "Aku-sadaijin" (the Evil Minister of the Left).

In the second year, on the seventh day of the third month, there was an imperial visit to Toba Palace to celebrate the Retired Emperor's fiftieth birthday. The stay lasted until the next day, and there were entertainments including court music and dance.
