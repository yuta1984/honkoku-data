People armed themselves and rushed to gather at Yoshimitsu's Hana no Gosho (Flower Palace). Yoshimitsu dispatched Nikaidō Nakatsukasa and Matsuda Tango no Kami as envoys to the residence of Hosokawa Musashi no Kami Yoriyuki, ordering him to resign from the post of kanrei (deputy shogun) and proceed to Shikoku. Yoriyuki's younger brother Yorimoto and other members of the Hosokawa clan all incurred the shogun's displeasure. When Yoriyuki departed from Kyoto, he shaved his head and changed his name to Tsunehisa. The following day, the Zen monk Myōha (known by the religious name Shun'oku) returned to the capital from Tango Province. This monk was a disciple of Musō (Soseki). At the beginning of Yoshimitsu's reign, when the Sanmon (Enryakuji) had staged a protest march (gōso) with the intent of destroying Nanzenji, Myōha had grown angry that Yoriyuki's handling of the matter was sluggish, and had been living in seclusion in Tanshū (Tango Province) for a long time. It seems that Myōha returned to Kyoto following Yoriyuki's departure from the capital. In the same month, Shiba Yoshimasa was appointed kanrei. In the sixth month, Myōha was appointed abbot of Nanzenji. In the seventh month, during the congratulatory ceremony for Yoshimitsu's appointment as Right Grand Commander (Udaishō), Yamana Mimbu Yoshiyuki, serving as the tōshoku of the Samurai-dokoro, accompanied the procession at the vanguard with a hundred cavalry.
