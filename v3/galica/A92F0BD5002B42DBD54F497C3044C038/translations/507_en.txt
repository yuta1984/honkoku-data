...Naritoki served as co-signatory (kajō). Sadatoki's maternal grandfather, the Akita no Jō (Castellan of Akita) Yasumori, was appointed Governor of Mutsu. None could rival his power and influence. In the same year, it was reported that Hōjō Tokikuni at Rokuhara harbored treasonous intentions; he was summoned down to the Kantō, exiled to Hitachi Province, and subsequently executed. In the seventh month, the Former Regent (Kanpaku) Ichijō Sanetsune passed away, at the age of sixty-two. His posthumous temple name was Enmyōji. In this same year, the Yuan court sent an envoy named Wang Jiweng (Ō Sekiō), accompanied by a Zen monk named Ruzhi (Nyochi), to Japan in order to observe the customs of our country. However, Wang Jiweng was killed by someone on board the same vessel during the voyage, and thus the mission came to nothing. This was likely because word had reached foreign lands that the Retired Emperor of Kameyama (the New Retired Emperor) as well as the Hōjō of Kamakura were enthusiastic devotees of Zen Buddhism.

In the second month of the eighth year [of Kōan, 1285], the celebration of the ninetieth birthday of the Kitayama Jugo (Quasi-Empress Dowager), Junior First Rank Sadako, was held. She was the daughter of the Washio Great Counselor Takahira, and the consort of the late Saionji Chancellor Saneuji.
