In the same year, rumors were heard that Hōjō Tokikuni was plotting rebellion at Rokuhara, so he was summoned back to the east, exiled to Hitachi Province, and later executed. In the seventh month, the former Kanpaku Ichijō Sanetsune died at the age of sixty-two. [His temple] was called Enmyō-ji. This year, the Yuan court sent an envoy named Ō Sekiō along with a Zen monk named Nyochi to Japan to investigate the customs of our country. En route, Ō Sekiō was killed by fellow passengers on the ship, so the matter came to nothing. This was probably because rumors had reached foreign lands that the Retired Emperor Kameyama and the Hōjō of Kamakura favored Zen Buddhism at this time.

In the second month of the eighth year of Kōan (1285), the ninetieth birthday celebration was held for Kitayama no Jungo (Empress Dowager) Teishi, holding the court rank of junior first rank. She was the daughter of Washino-o Dainagon Takakiyo and the wife of Saionji Sanekuni. As the mother of Ōmiya Nyoin, she was [the grandmother of] both the Main Court (Emperor Go-Fukakusa) and the New Court (Emperor Kameyama)...
