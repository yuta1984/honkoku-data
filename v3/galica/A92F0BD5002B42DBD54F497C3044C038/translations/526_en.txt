In the ninth month, [Hōjō] Sadatoki built the Saishōon-ji temple and held a dedication ceremony. The Shōgun, Prince Hisaaki, paid a visit to the temple. In the eleventh month, Tokudaiji Kinryō, the Udaijin (Minister of the Right), was appointed Daijō Daijin. Kujō Fuyuhira, the Naidaijin (Minister of the Interior), was transferred to the position of Udaijin, and Ichijō Uchinori, the Dainagon (Major Counselor), was appointed Naidaijin.

In the tenth month of the first year of Kagen (1303), Hōjō Motoki returned from Kyoto to Kamakura. In the eleventh month, Hōjō Tokinori came up to the capital as his replacement. This year, Takatoki, the son of Sadatoki, was born — though some say he was born in the first year of Einin (1293).

In the third month of the second year [of Kagen] (1304), Tokudaiji Kinryō resigned as Daijō Daijin. In the seventh month, the Cloistered Emperor Go-Fukakusa passed away at the Tominokōji Palace at the age of sixty-two. The commanders of both Rokuhara posts, [Hōjō] Sadaaki and Tokinori, led their troops to the gate of the palace and stood guard, seated on their camp stools (shōgi). The funeral rites were held at the Fushimi Palace. In the twelfth month, Ichijō Uchinori, the Naidaijin, died at the age of twenty.
