A person named Okiyo-ō told Masakado: "Whether you plunder one province or seize all of Bandō, the crime would be the same." Masakado agreed with this reasoning and immediately led his troops to attack Shimotsuke Province, driving out the provincial governor. From there he moved to Kōzuke Province, brought Kazusa, Shimōsa, Musashi, and Sagami under his control, and established his capital at Ishii-gō in Sarushima District of Shimōsa Province. Since Masakado was a fifth-generation descendant of Emperor Kanmu, he reasoned that there would be no problem with ascending to the imperial throne, so he styled himself Prince Taira, or alternatively called himself the "New Emperor" (Shinnō). He appointed left and right ministers and all the hundred offices below them, but only lacked a calendar scholar. He administered rewards and punishments at will. It is also said that he built a royal castle in Sōma District of Shimōsa Province. At the same time, Fujiwara no Sumitomo gathered pirates and sailed out from Iyo Province, captured Bizen no suke Kodaka, took alive Harima no suke Shimada no Koremoto, plundered the Nankai region, and attempted to seize Sanyō, San'in, and Saikai.
