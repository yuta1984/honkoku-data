The Eighty-Ninth Sovereign

Kameyama'in; his personal name was Tsunehito. He was the sixth son of Emperor Gosaga, and the younger brother of Emperor Gofukakusa by the same mother. In the eighth month of the second year of Shōka, he was installed as Crown Prince, and in the eleventh month of the first year of Shōgen he ascended to the throne, at the age of eleven. Takatsukasa Grand Minister of State Kanehira served as Regent (Kampaku). The Retired Emperor Gosaga administered governance from within the Retired Emperor's Court, and was referred to as "Ichiin" (the [principal] Retired Emperor). The Retired Emperor Gofukakusa was referred to as "Shin'in" (the New Retired Emperor) and resided at the Tominokōji Palace.

In the second month of the first year of Bun'ō, the daughter of the late Konoe Kanetsune descended to Kamakura, became the adopted daughter of Saimyōji Tokiyori, and was given in marriage to Prince Munetaka.

In the seventh month, the monk Nichiren arrived in Kamakura and had an audience with Tokiyori. Nichiren gathered many followers and founded a new Buddhist sect.

In the eleventh month, the Former Chancellor Saionji Saneuji took the tonsure and took the name Tokiwai Nyūdō.
