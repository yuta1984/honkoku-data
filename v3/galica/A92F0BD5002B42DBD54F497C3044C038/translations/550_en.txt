They were captured and sent down to Kamakura. This was because they had received imperial edicts to subjugate the military government. All were exiled to distant lands. Hino no Suketomo, who was at his place of exile in Sado, was killed by a warrior named Honma who received orders from Takatoki. Suketomo's son, a child called Anii, killed Honma and avenged his father. In the sixth month, Hōjō Shigetoki was appointed as regent (shikken). He was the son of Hirotoki. In the seventh month, Hino no Toshimoto was summoned to Kamakura again and killed. Both Suketomo and Toshimoto were particularly involved in the conspiracy.

In the eighth month, Konoe no Tsunetada resigned from the position of regent (kanpaku). Takatsukasa no Fuyunori became regent.

In the ninth month, because Nagasaki Takasuke wielded excessive tyrannical power, Takatoki secretly ordered Takayori, who was from Takasuke's clan, to kill Takasuke. When the plot was revealed, Takayori was instead exiled to Mutsu Province, and Takasuke became even more arrogant. Kamakura's government declined and all people turned against it. Hearing this, the Emperor again plotted against Kamakura.
