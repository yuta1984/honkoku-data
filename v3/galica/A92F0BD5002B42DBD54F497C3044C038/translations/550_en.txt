[Continuing from the previous page: the monks En'kan, Mon'kan, Chūen, and others were] arrested and sent down to Kamakura. This was because these individuals had received an imperial decree and attempted to subjugate the warrior houses through esoteric rituals. They were all sentenced to distant exile. Hinō Suketomo, who had been residing at his place of banishment on Sado Island, was killed by a warrior named Hōma (Honma), who acted under orders from Hōjō Takatoki. Suketomo's son, a young boy called Anii, killed Hōma and avenged his father's death.

In the sixth month, Hōjō Shigetoki was appointed Shikken (Regent). He was the son of [Hōjō] Hirotoki.

In the seventh month, Hinō Toshimoto was once again summoned to Kamakura and killed. This was because both Suketomo and Toshimoto had been deeply involved in the secret conspiracy.

In the eighth month, Konoe Tsunetada resigned as Kampaku. Takatsukasa Fuyunori became Kampaku.

In the ninth month, because Nagasaki Takasuke's abuse of power had become extreme, Takatoki secretly ordered Takasuke's kinsman Takayori to kill Takasuke. When the plot was discovered, Takayori was instead banished to Ōshū (the northeastern provinces), and Takasuke grew even more arrogant. The governance of Kamakura fell into decline, and all people turned against it. Upon hearing this, the Emperor once again plotted against Kamakura.
