Yoshihira was the younger brother of Yoshitsune.

In the first year of Karoku (1225), in the sixth month, Ōe Hiromoto, the lay monk Kakua, died at the age of eighty-three. He had been an advisor on warrior government affairs since the time of Yoritomo. In the seventh month, the Second Rank Nun Taira Masako (Buddhist name: Nyojitsu) passed away at sixty-nine. Since she had governed after Sanetomo's death, she was commonly called the Nun Shogun. In the ninth month, the former Chief Abbot of Tendai and former Great Prelate Jien died. He was posthumously named Jichin. He was the son of Hōshōji Chancellor Tadamichi and was a monk renowned for his waka poetry. In the eleventh month, Yoritsune of Kamakura underwent his coming-of-age ceremony at the age of eight. Yasutoki served as his guardian and devoted his heart to governance. In the twelfth month, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo shrines.

In the second year (1226), in the first month, Yoritsune was appointed Sei-i Taishōgun, granted the court rank of junior fifth rank lower grade, and appointed Right Minor Captain. In the fifth month, in Mutsu Province, there was a rebel who called himself the Zen Master Kōkyō. Yūki Tomohiro and others captured and executed him.
