On the eleventh day of the seventh month, Yoritsune left Kamakura and returned to the capital. He arrived in Kyoto on the twenty-eighth day and resided at Rokuhara. The warriors who had accompanied him on the journey all returned to Kamakura, but Miura Mitsumura alone grieved deeply for the parting, shedding tears and saying he wished to return to Kamakura once more before departing. Mitsumura was Yoshimura's second son and Yasumura's younger brother. He had been Yoritsune's close retainer since childhood. Regarding Yoritsune's return to the capital, there had been discussions about this since Tsunetoki's time, but it seems Tokiyori hastily implemented the plan due to Mitsutoki's incident. Miura Yasumura was a great lord of many generations, and moreover, being Yasutoki's son-in-law, he was consulted on governmental affairs. Akita Jōsuke Yoshikage, also being close to Tokiyori, competed for influence with Yasumura. At this time, Hōjō Shigetoki had long been in Kyoto and was well-versed in political affairs, so Tokiyori proposed calling him down to Kamakura to discuss various matters.
