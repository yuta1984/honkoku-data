Thus it is said. In the same month, Minamoto no Yorimasa was appointed to the Senior Third Rank. He had long desired this but the matter had not progressed, but Kiyomori took pity on his obscurity and made a petition to the throne. Fujiwara no Naritsune and Taira no Yasuyori received pardons and returned to the capital. Shunkan was not pardoned due to the gravity of his crimes and died on the island.

In the second month of the third year, Munemori resigned from the position of Major Captain of the Right. In the third month, Shigemori resigned from the position of Inner Minister. In the same month, the monk Mongaku of Takao committed crimes and was exiled to Izu Province. In the fifth month, whirlwinds blew through the capital and many houses collapsed. This summer, Shigemori returned to the capital from his pilgrimage to Kumano. In the seventh month, he fell ill, and died on the first day of the eighth month. He was forty-three years old. From the Cloistered Emperor down, all mourned his loss. In the eleventh month, there was a great earthquake. In the same month, Kiyomori came up to the capital from Fukuhara and enumerated various grievances he held against the Cloistered Emperor. He exiled Chancellor Matsudono Motofusa to Bizen.
