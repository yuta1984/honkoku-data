In the twelfth month, the title of Great Master (Daishi) was posthumously granted to Chishō.

In the sixth year, sixth month, Ono no Tōfū was summoned to write about the virtuous conduct of wise kings and renowned ministers of Chinese dynasties on the wall of the southern eaves of the Seiryōden. Tōfū was a renowned master calligrapher.

In the seventh year, eighth month, there was a great flood, fields were washed away, and many people died. In the ninth month, Ono no Tōfū was made to write the names on the paintings of the Kenshō sliding doors.

In the eighth year, sixth month, twenty-sixth day, black clouds arose from the direction of Atago, suddenly there was great thunder, and lightning struck the top of the Seiryōden. Major Counselor Fujiwara no Kiyotsura, Assistant Middle Controller Taira no Mareyo, and several other court attendants were burned to death by the lightning fire. The Emperor avoided the fire and moved to the Jōnei-den. It was said and passed down through the world that this was the work of Minister Kan's vengeful spirit. This is doubtful.
