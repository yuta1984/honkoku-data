Mount Hiei appealed to the court nobles, requesting that Morotaka be exiled and Moretsune be imprisoned. However, since Saikō was favored by the Cloistered Emperor, the petition from the mountain monastery (Enryaku-ji) was not granted.

In the first year of Jishō, first month, Inner Minister Moronaga resigned from the position of Left General. Major Counselor Taira no Shigemori was transferred to Left General, and Middle Counselor Taira no Munemori was appointed Right General. Around this time, Tokudaiji Major Counselor Fujiwara no Sanesada and Kazanin Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Kanemasa both desired the general positions because of their distinguished Seiga family lineage. Also, the new Major Counselor Fujiwara no Narichika, who served as superintendent (bettō) of the Cloistered Emperor's office, relied on his own authority and persistently sought a general position, but his wish was not granted. Through Kiyomori's machinations, the brothers Shigemori and Munemori were placed side by side as Left and Right Generals. Narichika's younger sister was Shigemori's wife. Shigemori's eldest son Koremori was Narichika's son-in-law. Although there were close family ties, Narichika detested the Taira family's arrogance and constantly appealed to the Cloistered Emperor, plotting with Saikō's faction to destroy the Taira house.
