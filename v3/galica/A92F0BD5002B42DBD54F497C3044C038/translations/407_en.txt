In the third month, Yoritomo's residence in Kamakura and Tsurugaoka Hachiman Shrine were destroyed by fire. All of these were newly constructed. Inner Minister Kanefusa was appointed Grand Minister, and Nakayama Major Counselor Fujiwara no Tadachika was made Inner Minister.

In the fourth month, the monk Eisai returned from Song China and began spreading Zen Buddhism for the first time. In the tenth month, Hōjūji temple, which served as the Retired Emperor's residence, was constructed. Yoritomo had Hiromitsu Chikanō and others serve as construction supervisors. In the twelfth month, Kanezane resigned as regent and became chancellor.

In the intercalary month, former Minister of the Left Tokudaiji Sanesada passed away at the age of fifty-three.

In the first month of the third year [of Kenkyū], a Taira retainer named Kazusa Gorōbē Tadamitsu secretly hid in Kamakura, plotting against Yoritomo. Around this time, a new hall was being constructed at Matsufukuji temple, and when Yoritomo came to supervise the construction, Tadamitsu covered his left eye with fish scales to disguise himself as a one-eyed person, concealed a sword in his robes, and mingled among the construction workers.
