Gien (義円) returned to secular life, entered the Muromachi Palace (室町殿), and took the name Yoshinobu (義宣). On the same day, he was granted the court rank of Junior Fifth Rank, Lower Grade (従五位下), and appointed Head of the Left Stables (左馬頭). He was thirty-five years of age at this time.

In the same month, Hino Sukekuni (日野資国), Acting Grand Minister (准大臣) and maternal grandfather of the reigning Emperor, passed away.

In the fourth month, the Bakufu held its inaugural ceremonies: the First Council (評定始め), the First Document Signing (判始め), and the First Horse Riding (乗馬始め), among others. Yoshinobu was promoted to Junior Fourth Rank, Lower Grade (従四位下). In the same month, Hino Sukenori (日野資教), of the rank of Gidō Sanshi (儀同三司), passed away at the age of seventy-three. He was a sibling of Sukekuni.

In the fifth month, the former Minister of the Right of the Takatsukasa family (鷹司), Fuyuie (冬家), passed away.

In the sixth month, the former Grand Minister (太政大臣) of the Tokudaiji family (徳大寺), Kintoshi (公俊), passed away at the age of fifty-eight.

On the twentieth day of the seventh month, the Emperor passed away at the age of twenty-seven. During this reign, there was no change of era name following the imperial accession. From the twentieth year of Ōei (応永) to the thirty-fourth year — fifteen years in total — and adding the one year of Shōchō (正長), the reign amounted to sixteen years in total. It is said and handed down that this Emperor practiced esoteric rites (魔法) and always observed ritual abstinence (潔斎), and for this reason left no imperial heir.
