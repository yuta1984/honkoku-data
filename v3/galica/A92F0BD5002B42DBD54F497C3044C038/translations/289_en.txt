In the eleventh month, Dōchō (Fujiwara no Michinaga) fell ill. Both Jōtōmon-in and the Middle Palace Consort performed various prayers. On the twenty-sixth day, there was an imperial visit to Hōjōji Temple to inquire about Dōchō's illness. On the first day of the twelfth month, Dōchō passed away at the age of sixty-two. For three reigns, he governed the realm as regent and chancellor for over thirty years. Emperor Ichijō, Emperor Sanjō, and the current Crown Prince (later Emperor Go-Ichijō) were all his sons-in-law. His sons all became regents, chancellors, ministers, and court nobles. The prosperity of the regent house reached its peak here. The forty-volume tale written by Akazome Emon largely records the glory of Dōchō. On the fourth day of the same month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Yukinari died at the age of fifty-six. He was a master calligrapher and the founder of the Sonshōji house, which produced skilled calligraphers for generations.

In the first year of Chōgen (1028), in the fourth month, Higo Governor Takashina no Narishō, Fujiwara no Tokitō, Taira no Tameyuki and others attempted to engage in private warfare. Their crimes were determined. In the sixth month, former Kazusa Deputy Governor Taira no Tadatsune raised a rebellion in Shimōsa Province. Minister of the Right Sanesuke reported this.
