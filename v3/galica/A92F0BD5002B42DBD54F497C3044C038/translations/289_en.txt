In the eleventh month, Michinaga fell ill. Both Jōtōmon-in and the Empress performed various prayers for his recovery. On the twenty-sixth day, there was an imperial visit to Hōjōji Temple to inquire after Michinaga's illness. On the first day of the twelfth month, Michinaga died at the age of sixty-two. For three generations, he served as regent and chancellor, governing the realm for over thirty years. Emperor Ichijō, Emperor Sanjō, the current emperor (Go-Ichijō), and the Crown Prince (Prince Go-Suzaku) were all his sons-in-law. His sons all became regent chancellors, great ministers, or court nobles. The prosperity of the regent family reached its peak here. The forty-volume tale written by Akazome Emon largely records the splendor of Michinaga's glory. On the fourth day of the same month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Kōzei died at the age of fifty-six. He was a master calligrapher and the founder of the Sesonji family, which produced skilled calligraphers for generations.

In the first year of Chōgen era, fourth month, Higo Governor Takashina no Nariakira, Fujiwara no Tokitō, Taira no Tameyuki, and others attempted to engage in private warfare. Their crimes were determined. In the sixth month, former Kazusa Assistant Governor Taira no Tadatsune raised a rebellion in Shimōsa Province. Right Minister Sanesuke supervised the response.
