In the first month of the second year of Kōchō, Sanjō Kimitika resigned as Inner Minister (Naidaijin). Takatsukasa Mototada was appointed Inner Minister. (Interlinear note: He was the son of Kanehira.) In the seventh month, Kinsuke resigned as Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin).

On the twenty-eighth day of the eleventh month, Han'en, the founder of the Ikko sect, passed away. He was ninety-one years of age. This was Shinran. He was a member of the Hino family.

In the second month of the third year, the Retired Emperor Gosaga was residing at the Kitayama Kameyama Palace, and the reigning Emperor paid a visit (gyōkō), as did the Shin'in (Retired Emperor Gofukakusa) (on a御幸). A poetry gathering and entertainment were held.

In the same month, in Kamakura, Hōjō Masamura held a one-day, one-thousand-poem waka gathering. Perhaps because the Shogun Munetaka excelled at waka, Masamura too developed a fondness for it during breaks from the affairs of government.

In the third month, Fujiwara no Saneo resigned as Left Minister.

In the eighth month, the former Regent of the Ichijō house, Sanetsune, was reappointed as Left Minister — a most unusual precedent.
