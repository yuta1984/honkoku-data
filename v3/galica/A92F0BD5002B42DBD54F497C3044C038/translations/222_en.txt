In the first month of the seventh year, envoys from Balhae led by Pei Ting and others arrived in Kaga Province. By imperial edict, they came to the capital in the fourth month and entered the Kōrōkan (guest house for foreign envoys). Minister Kan (who at this time was a Professor of Literature but temporarily became Assistant Minister of Civil Administration) received the Balhae envoys. The Ministry of Civil Administration is the office that handles affairs with foreign countries. Pei Ting had literary talent, and there were exchanges of poetry between him and Minister Kan. In the fifth month, Pei Ting and others were summoned to the imperial palace, granted a banquet, and shown horseback riding and archery. Afterwards, they returned to their country.

In the eleventh month, the Emperor developed a fondness for horseback riding, had horses kept in the imperial palace, and constantly brought lowly persons close to him for riding, leading to many breaches of proper conduct. When Mototsune heard of this, he came to court and expelled the petty courtiers and close attendants like Ono no Kiyokazu and others. After this, the Emperor became mentally disturbed - sometimes he would gather frogs to have snakes swallow them, and sometimes he would make monkeys and dogs fight each other.
