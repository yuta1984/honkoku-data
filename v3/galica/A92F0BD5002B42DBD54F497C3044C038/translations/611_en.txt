From Kyoto, Hosokawa Ukyō no Daibu Yorimoto, Yamana Shuri no Daibu Yoshimasa, Yamana Mutsu no Kami Ujikiyo, and Ishidō, Akamatsu, and others advanced to Kishū (Kii Province). As the enemy was defeated and collapsed, the Kyoto forces returned to their positions. In the twelfth month, Yoshimitsu was granted the Junior Second Rank (Jū-nii).

In the same month, the Southern faction rose up again, and Yoshimitsu himself went out as far as Tō-ji Temple, dispatching Yamana Yoshimasa, Ujikiyo, and others to suppress them.

In the first month of the first year of Kōryaku [1379], Yoshimitsu became the Director of the Imperial Stables (Maryō no Mikan). In the same month, Yamana Yoshimasa, Ujikiyo, and others attacked and defeated the enemy forces in Kishū, capturing the castle of Tsuchimaru. They also captured the castle of Yuasa. In the second month, Uesugi Ujimitsu, the Kanryō (Deputy) of Kamakura and Head of the Left Stables (Sama no Kami), showed signs of rebellion and attempted to attack Kyoto. Uesugi Noriharu remonstrated with him, but Ujimitsu would not yield. In the same month, in response to a complaint from the monastic warriors (shuto) of Nanto (Nara), in order to suppress a certain Tōichi of Yamato Province, forces were sent out from Kyoto under Shiba Saemon no Suke Yoshimasa, together with Isshiki, Togashi, Akamatsu, and others, reinforced by troops from Ōmi Province and the Toki forces from Mino Province.
