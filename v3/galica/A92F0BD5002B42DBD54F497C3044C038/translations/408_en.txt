In the third month, Retired Emperor Go-Shirakawa passed away at the age of sixty-seven. His reign was only three years, but through cloistered government (insei) he conducted political affairs for about forty years, spanning the reigns of emperors Nijō, Rokujō, Takakura, Antoku, and the current emperor (Tsuchimikado). During this period, after the Hōgen Rebellion, he was troubled by Nobuyori, Kiyomori, and Yoshinaka. Although there was temporary peace thanks to Yoritomo's achievements, the transfer of political power from the imperial court to the military houses began at this time.

In the seventh month, His Majesty (Emperor Tsuchimikado) began governing personally for the first time and appointed Yoritomo as Seii Taishōgun (Barbarian-Subduing Generalissimo). Imperial messengers Nakahara no Kageyoshi and Yasusada brought the imperial edict to Kamakura. Yoritomo had Miura no Suke Yoshizumi receive the edict in the shrine precincts of Tsurugaoka Hachiman.

In the eighth month, Yoritomo's second son Sanetomo was born. His mother was the same as Yoriie's (Masako).
