In the third month, Retired Emperor Go-Shirakawa died at age sixty-seven. His reign was merely three years, but from the eras of Nijō, Rokujō, Takakura, Antoku, through the current reign, he had conducted government affairs from the retired emperor's court for about forty years. During that time, after the Hōgen Rebellion, he was troubled by Nobuyori, Kiyomori, and Yoshinaka, and though there was temporary peace thanks to Yoritomo's achievements, the transfer of imperial government to the military houses began from this time. In the seventh month, His Majesty began conducting government personally for the first time, and appointed Yoritomo as Seii Taishōgun (Barbarian-subduing Generalissimo). Imperial messengers Nakahara no Kageyoshi and Yasusada brought the imperial edict down to Kamakura. Yoritomo had Miura no suke Yoshizumi receive the edict at the shrine courtyard of Tsurugaoka. In the eighth month, Yoritomo's second son Sanetomo was born. His mother was the same as Yoriie's.
