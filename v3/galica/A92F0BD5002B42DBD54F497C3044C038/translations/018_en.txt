His military might gradually grew stronger and more prosperous, and he completely destroyed numerous great enemies, beginning with Nagasune-hiko and including Uda no Eukashi, Yasotakeru, and Eshiki. Ten years after departing from Hyuga Province in the year of Kinoe-Tora (Wood-Elder Tiger), in the year of Kanoto-Tori (Metal-Younger Rooster), he cut open Mount Unebiyama in Yamato Province, built the first imperial palace, and ascended to the imperial throne. This was called Kashihara Palace. This was precisely the first year of Emperor Jinmu's reign. Umashimachi-no-Mikoto and Michi-no-Omi-no-Mikoto, both having distinguished military achievements, led troops and guarded the imperial palace. The troops under Michi-no-Omi-no-Mikoto's command were called the Kumebu, and those under Umashimachi-no-Mikoto's command were called the Mononobe. The fact that warriors are called "mononofu" to this day began from this. Ama-no-Taneko-no-Mikoto and Ama-no-Tomi-no-Mikoto served at his left and right sides and conducted government affairs.
