Regent Fujiwara no Moroie died. In the same month, Yoritsune returned to Kamakura.
In the first month of En'ō 1 (1239), Chancellor Yoshihira took Buddhist vows at age fifty-six. In the second month, Retired Emperor Go-Toba died in Oki Province at age sixty.
In the second month of Ninji 1 (1240), imperial envoy Major Counselor Fujiwara no Kinsuke made a pilgrimage to Ise. Kinsuke was the grandson of Kinetsune and son of Saneuji. In the ninth month, Sanjo Sanechika resigned as Right Minister. In the tenth month, Oinomikado Ietsugu resigned as Inner Minister. Major Counselor Sanetsune was appointed Right Minister, and Kinugasa Major Counselor Fujiwara no Ieyoshi was appointed Inner Minister. Ieyoshi belonged to a branch family that had separated from the Konoe house. In the eleventh month, Yoritsune ordered warriors to light watch fires and guard and fortify street corners in both Kyoto and Kamakura. In the twelfth month, Regent Kanetsune became Chancellor. This year, Hōjō Tokifusa died at age sixty-six.
