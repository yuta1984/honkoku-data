Empress Shōtoku (Female Emperor) - This is Emperor Kōken. She pushed aside the deposed emperor and ascended to the throne again. This second reign is called Shōtoku. This follows the precedent of Emperor Kōgyoku-Saimei, where one emperor had two reign names.

In the first year of Tenpyō-jingo, tenth month, she granted Dōkyō the position of Daijō-daijin-zenji (Prime Minister and Buddhist Master) and had all civil and military officials pay their respects. In the eleventh month, Right Minister Fujiwara no Toyonari of the First Rank passed away at the age of sixty-two.

In the second year, first month, Fujiwara no Nagate was appointed Right Minister. Kibi no Makibi was made Dainagon (Major Counselor). In the tenth month, Dōkyō was granted the position of Hōō (Dharma King). Fujiwara no Nagate was made Left Minister, and Kibi no Makibi was made Right Minister. This person (Kibi no Makibi) had traveled to Tang China twice and, due to his reputation for extensive learning, gradually rose from humble origins to become a minister. He is the one known to the world as Minister Kibi.

In the first year of Jingo-keiun, second month, the Shakuten ceremony was held. The Emperor personally made an imperial visit to the Daigaku-ryō (Bureau of Education).
