In the first year of Jōgen (1207), in the first month, Sanetomo was promoted to Junior Fourth Rank Upper Grade. Hōjō Tokifusa was appointed governor of Musashi Province. In the second month, Udaijin Takatada was transferred to Sadaijin, Naidaijin Tadatsune was appointed Udaijin, and Dainagon Fujiwara no Michitsune became Naidaijin.
In the same month, the monk Genkū was exiled to Sanuki Province. Genkū was known as Hōnen-bō and lived in Kurodani, where he first propagated the Jōdo (Pure Land) sect. His followers were extremely numerous. Some of the retired emperor's court ladies received Buddhist precepts and became nuns. The retired emperor became angry and exiled Hōnen, and had his disciples Anraku and Jūren executed. In the fourth month, the former kanpaku and daijō-daijin of the Kujō family, Fujiwara no Kanezane, died. He was sixty-one years old. He was known as Tsukiwa-dono.
