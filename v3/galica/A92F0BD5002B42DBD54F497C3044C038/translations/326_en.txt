In Kanji 1 (1087), Kampaku Moromichi was promoted to Junior First Rank and positioned above Left Minister Toshifusa. In the summer of this year, something called dengaku became popular in the capital, and people of both high and low status all went to see it. It was even summoned to the retired emperor's palace for viewing. In the eighth month, the retired emperor took Buddhist vows, with Ryūmyō as his ordination master. From this time he was called Cloistered Emperor Shirakawa. From this point forward, he continued to conduct government affairs from the cloister.

In Jōtoku 1 (1097), in the first month, Major Counselor Minamoto no Tsunenobu died at Dazaifu at the age of eighty-two. In the fourth month, there was an imperial visit to Gion. In the tenth month, there was an imperial visit to the residence of Kampaku Moromichi.

In the second year, in the seventh month, there was an imperial visit to Hōshōji. In the ninth month, Middle Counselor Ōe no Masafusa was appointed Provisional Governor-General of Dazaifu and went down there. Therefore, Masafusa is called "Kōsui" (River Commander).

In Kōwa 1 (1099), in the first month, Kakugyō, the Cloistered Emperor's son who was the imperial abbot of Ninnaji, received the imperial prince declaration.
