The Cloistered Emperor made an imperial visit to Mount Kinpu. In the eleventh month, the Engi Regulations (Engishiki kyaku) were compiled.

In the sixth year, eighth month, the Emperor read the Shiji (Records of the Grand Historian). In the seventh month, the Grand Counselor and Right General Fujiwara no Sadakuni passed away. He was the Emperor's maternal uncle. In the ninth month, there were bandits on Mount Suzuka in Ise Province; sixteen of their ringleaders were captured and executed. In the twelfth month, having finished reading the Nihongi, a banquet was held and poetry composition was ordered.

In the seventh year, tenth month, the deity of Kumano in Kii Province was granted the Senior First Rank. The Cloistered Emperor made an imperial visit to Kumano.

In the eighth year, first month, the envoy Hai Qiu (Haikiū) from Balhae came to court. In the fourth month, he returned to his country.

In the ninth year, fourth month, the Left Minister Fujiwara no Tokihira passed away. He was thirty-nine years old. He was posthumously granted Senior First Rank and the title of Grand Minister of State. He was given the posthumous name "Minister of the Hon'in."

In the tenth year, there were droughts, celestial anomalies, strange occurrences, and other such events.

In the thirteenth year, third month, the Right Minister Minamoto no Hikaru passed away at the age of sixty-nine. In the eighth month, there were great winds.
