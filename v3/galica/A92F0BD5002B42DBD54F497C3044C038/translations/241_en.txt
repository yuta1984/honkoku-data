The Retired Emperor made an imperial visit to Mount Kinpusen. In the eleventh month, the Engi-kyaku (Engi Code) was compiled.

In the sixth year, eighth month, the Emperor read the Records of the Grand Historian (Shiki). In the seventh month, Major Counselor and General of the Right Fujiwara no Sadakuni died. He was the Emperor's maternal uncle. In the ninth month, there were bandits at Mount Suzuka in Ise. Sixteen ringleaders were captured and executed. In the twelfth month, having finished reading the Nihon Shoki (Chronicles of Japan), a banquet was held and poetry was composed.

In the seventh year, tenth month, the deity of Kumano in Kii Province was granted the court rank of Junior First Rank. The Retired Emperor made an imperial visit to Kumano.

In the eighth year, first month, the Balhae envoy Pei Qiu came to court. He returned to his country in the fourth month.

In the ninth year, fourth month, Minister of the Left Fujiwara no Tokihira died at the age of thirty-nine. He was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank and the title of Grand Minister of State. He was called the Minister of Honin.

In the tenth year, there were droughts, celestial phenomena, and strange omens.

In the thirteenth year, third month, Minister of the Right Minamoto no Hikaru died at the age of sixty-nine. In the eighth month, there was a great storm.
