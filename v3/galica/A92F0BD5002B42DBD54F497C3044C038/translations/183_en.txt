He composed poetry and also excelled in calligraphy. He became crown prince during Emperor Saga's reign.
In the fourth month of Kōnin 14 (823), he received the abdication and ascended to the throne. He made Prince Masayoshi, son of Emperor Saga, the crown prince. Emperor Saga became the retired emperor. Emperor Heizei was called the former retired emperor. In the fifth month, Middle Counselor Yoshimine no Yasuyo concurrently served as Right General. This was the emperor's younger brother. He was granted the surname Yoshimine and served the court. He was a person of exceptional talent and skill. His maternal grandfather Fujiwara no Momokawa was posthumously granted the rank of Chancellor and Senior First Rank. In the ninth month, the retired emperor (Saga) moved to Saga. Although the current emperor suggested that the formal ceremony of imperial progress should be used, the retired emperor declined and rode on horseback instead of using a carriage. In the eleventh month, the Great Thanksgiving Festival (Daijōsai) was held. In the twelfth month, the former retired emperor (Heizei) entered the capital to hunt. The emperor presented him with various gifts. Gifts were also given to his attendants.
