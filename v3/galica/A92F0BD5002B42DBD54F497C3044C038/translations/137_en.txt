(For an emperor to shave his head) began with Shomu. By the will of the Retired Emperor, Prince Doso was made Crown Prince. He was a grandson of Emperor Tenmu and son of Prince Nitta.

In the first year of Tempyo-hoji era, Tachibana no Moroe died at the age of seventy-four. He was called the Minister of Ide. In the third month, Crown Prince Doso was deposed and sent back to his residence. Right Minister Fujiwara no Toyonari protested, saying "How can this be done when it was the emperor's dying wish?" However, because it did not accord with the current emperor's intentions, it was done thus. In the fourth month, when there was deliberation about which prince should be made Crown Prince, although Toyonari and others expressed their opinions, they were not accepted. Based on what Fujiwara no Nakamarō proposed, Prince Ōi was made Crown Prince. He too was a grandson of Emperor Tenmu and son of Prince Toneri.

In the fifth month, after Emperor moved to Tamura Palace where Fujiwara no Nakamarō had relocated, she granted Nakamarō the office called Shibi Naishō (Purple Palace Inner Minister) and had him oversee all military officials both inside and outside the court.
