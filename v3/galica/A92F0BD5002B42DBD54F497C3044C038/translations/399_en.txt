Koremori's son Rokudai, though he was the legitimate heir of the Taira clan, was pardoned due to the intercession of Mongaku. Yoritomo also sent Doi Sanehira and others to the western seas to guard them. Yoritomo petitioned through Tokimasa to become the General Pursuer (sōtsuibushi) of all provinces. The Cloistered Emperor granted permission. From this time, Yoritomo placed military governors (shugo) in the provinces and stewards (jitō) on estates, so that all sixty-odd provinces came under the control of the military houses, and the imperial court declined day by day. In the twelfth month, through Yoritomo's petition, Right Minister Fujiwara no Kanezane received the imperial decree for the position of nairan (chief advisor).

In the second year, first month, they celebrated the Cloistered Emperor's sixtieth birthday. In the third month, Sessho Motomichi was dismissed and Right Minister Kanezane was made sessho.
