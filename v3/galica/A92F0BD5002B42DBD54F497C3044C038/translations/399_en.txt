Rokudai, the son of Koremori, was the legitimate heir of the Taira clan, but he was pardoned due to the intercession of Mongaku. Yoritomo also sent Doi Sanehira and others to the western seas to guard them. Yoritomo petitioned through Tokimasa to become the General Constable (sō-tsuibushi) of all provinces. The Retired Emperor granted permission. From this time, Yoritomo placed military governors (shugo) in all provinces and stewards (jitō) on estates, so that all sixty-odd provinces came under the control of the warrior houses, and the imperial court declined day by day. In the twelfth month, through Yoritomo's petition, Right Minister Fujiwara no Kanezane received an imperial decree appointing him to the position of nairān.

In the second year, first month, they celebrated the Retired Emperor's sixtieth birthday. In the third month, Regent Motomichi was dismissed and Right Minister Kanezane was made regent.
