One thousand ryō of gold and one thousand bolts of silk were donated to Tōdai-ji Temple. Warriors were ordered to guard the four gates, but a dispute arose between the warriors and the temple monks. Yūki Tomomitsu pacified the situation and it ended peacefully. After the dedication ceremony concluded, the Emperor returned to the palace, and Yoritomo made a formal visit to the court. In the same month, Nakayama Naidaijin (Interior Minister) Tadachika passed away. The records he created are called the Sankai-ki, and the Mizukagami was also his work.

In the fourth month, Yoritomo toured the temples and shrines throughout the capital. Imperial envoy Chūnagon (Middle Counselor) Fujiwara no Tsunefusa came to the Rokuhara residence and discussed state affairs with Yoritomo. In the fifth month, Yoritomo visited the court and deliberated on government matters with Regent Kanezane. In the sixth month, Yoriie visited the court. In the same month, Yoritomo, Yoriie, and Masako all returned to Kamakura. In the eleventh month, Dainagon (Major Counselor) Fujiwara no Yoshitsune became Naidaijin (Interior Minister). He was Kanezane's second son. Yoshitsune's wife was the daughter of Yoshiyasu and Yoritomo's niece.

In the fourth month of Kenkyū 7 (1196), Sanjō Sadaijin (Minister of the Left) Sanefusa resigned from office and became a Buddhist monk. In the eleventh month, Kanezane resigned as regent, and Konoe former regent Motomichi became regent.
