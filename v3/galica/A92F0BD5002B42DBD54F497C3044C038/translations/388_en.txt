Kanezane and other court officials climbed Mount Hiei and had an audience with the Cloistered Emperor. On the twenty-eighth day, the Cloistered Emperor returned to the capital. Yoshinaka escorted him with fifty thousand mounted warriors. Yukiie, being at odds with Yoritomo, had joined Yoshinaka since the northern campaigns and had likewise come to the capital. The Taira could not remain even at Fukuhara and finally fled to Tsukushi (Kyushu).

In the eighth month, the Cloistered Emperor stripped the Taira of their court ranks and distributed their domains to the Minamoto. To Yoshinaka he granted Iyo Province, appointed him Samanosuke (Assistant Director of the Left Imperial Stables), and he was called the Asahi General. To Yukiie he granted Bizen Province and he was titled Governor of Bizen. The power of these two men gradually grew. On the twentieth day of the same month, a son of Emperor Takakura (Narinari, later Emperor Go-Toba) ascended the throne. From this time there were two emperors, one in the capital and one in the countryside. For this reason, Emperor Antoku came to be called the Former Emperor.

Eighty-second Emperor
Emperor Go-Toba　Fourth son of Emperor Takakura. His personal name was Narinari. His mother was Fujiwara no Shokushi.
