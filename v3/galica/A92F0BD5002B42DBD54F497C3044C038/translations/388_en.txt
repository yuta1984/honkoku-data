Kanezane and the court officials climbed Mount Hiei and had an audience with the Cloistered Emperor. On the twenty-eighth day, the Cloistered Emperor returned to the capital. Yoshinaka accompanied him with fifty thousand cavalry. Yukiie, being at odds with Yoritomo, had joined Yoshinaka since the northern campaigns and had likewise come to the capital. The Taira could not remain even at Fukuhara and finally fled to Tsukushi (Kyushu).

In the eighth month, the Cloistered Emperor stripped the Taira of their court ranks and distributed their lands to the Minamoto. To Yoshinaka he granted Iyo Province, appointed him to the position of Sama-no-kami (Head of the Left Division of the Bureau of Horses), and gave him the title of Asahi Shogun. To Yukiie he granted Bizen Province and gave him the title of Bizen-no-kami (Governor of Bizen). The power of these two men gradually increased.

On the twentieth day of the same month, a son of Emperor Takakura (Prince Takahira, later Emperor Go-Toba) ascended the throne. From this time forward, there were two emperors, one in the capital and one in the countryside. For this reason, Emperor Antoku came to be called the "Former Emperor."

Eighty-second Emperor
Emperor Go-Toba: Fourth son of Emperor Takakura. His personal name was Takahira. His mother was Fujiwara no Shokushi.
