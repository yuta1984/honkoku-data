In the first month of the seventeenth year (of Ōei, 1410), Minamoto no Yoshitsugu was appointed Chūnagon (Middle Counselor). In the fourth month, Yoshimochi made a pilgrimage to Mount Kōya. In the same month, Motoori (Motonori), the son of Nijō Kanpaku Mitsumoto, changed his name to Mochimoto. Following precedent, he was granted a character from Yoshimochi's personal name (imina). In the sixth month, Hatakeyama Mitsunori was appointed kanrei. In the twelfth month, Kanpaku Mitsumoto passed away. Ichijō Tsunetsugu was once again appointed kanpaku.

In the fourth month of the eighteenth year (1411), Imagawa Udaijin Kimiyuki was appointed Sadaijin (Minister of the Left). Takatsukasa Dainagon Fuyuie was appointed Udaijin (Minister of the Right). In the ninth month, Minamoto no Takakazu was ordered to suppress Fujiwara no Itsuna (Tatsuna), the provincial governor of Hida. In the twelfth month, Yoshitsugu was appointed Dainagon (Major Counselor).

In the same month, Uesugi Norizada, the Governor of Awa in Kamakura, died. His second cousin, Uemon no Suke Ujinori, took his place and became the chief retainer (karō) of Mochiuji. Ujinori was the son of Tomofusa and the nephew of Tomomune. Ujinori took Buddhist vows, changing his name to Zenshū. He took the title of Inuakari Nyūdō (the lay monk of Inuakari).
