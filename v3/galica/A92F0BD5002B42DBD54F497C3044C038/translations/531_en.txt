Through the arrangement of the military government (Bakufu), the second imperial prince of the Retired Emperor-Priest Go-Uda, Takaharu, was established as Crown Prince (Tōgū). In the tenth month, the era name was changed to Enkyō. In the eleventh month, Morotaka stepped down as sesshō (regent), and Takatsukasa Minister of the Left Fuyuhira became regent.

In the third month of the second year of Enkyō [1309], Fuyuhira resigned from the position of Minister of the Left. The former Minister of the Right of the Saionji house, Kinhira, was transferred to the position of Minister of the Left. In the sixth month, Kinhira declined [the position]. In the tenth month, Ichijō Sanehira retired from the position of Daijō Daijin (Grand Minister of State), and the former Naidaijin (Minister of the Center) of the Ōinomikado house, Nobatsugu, was appointed Daijō Daijin. Konoe Minister of the Right Iehira was transferred to Minister of the Left, Nijō Naidaijin Michihira was appointed Minister of the Right, and Iehira's younger brother, the Great Counselor Tsunehira, was appointed Naidaijin.

In the fourth month of the third year [1310], Horikawa Great Counselor Minamoto no Tomomori was granted the Junior First Rank and became Jun-Daijin (Honorary Minister). In the eleventh month, Hōjō Sadafusa died at Rokuhara. Hōjō Tokiatsu entered the capital as his successor. In the twelfth month, Nobatsugu resigned from the position of Daijō Daijin. The Regent Fuyuhira was appointed Daijō Daijin.
