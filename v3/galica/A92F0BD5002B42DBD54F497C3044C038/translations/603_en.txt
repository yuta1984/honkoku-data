Jōji 6th year; Ōan 4th year; Total reign of twenty years.

The Hundredth Sovereign:
Go-En'yū-in: His personal name (imina) was Ōhito. He was the eldest son of Go-Kōgon-in. His mother was Sōken Mon'in, the daughter of Fujiwara no Kanetuna, the Great Counselor (Dainagon) of Yotsutsuji.

In the third month of Ōan 4 (1371), he received the abdication (of the throne), at the age of fourteen. The Retired Emperor Go-Kōgon exercised governance from his retired-emperor's residence (in'chū). At this time, both the Kōmyō-in (Retired Emperor Kōmyō) and Sōkō-in (Retired Emperor Sukō) were still alive and residing in Fushimi. Regarding the matter of the imperial succession, Retired Emperor Sukō had previously consulted Yoriyuki, expressing that his son Prince Yoshihito was the rightful heir. However, since Go-Kōgon also relied deeply on Yoriyuki, the wishes of the Fushimi Palace (Retired Emperor Sukō) were not fulfilled. From this point on, Retired Emperors Sukō and Go-Kōgon became estranged from each other, despite being brothers.

In the fifth month, Hosokawa Umanojo Yorimoto advanced toward the Southern (Court). In the same month, Hosokawa Musashi no Kami Yoriyuki, for reasons of his own, resigned the post of Kanrei (Deputy Shogun) and withdrew to Saihō-ji Temple on the western hills (of Kyoto).
