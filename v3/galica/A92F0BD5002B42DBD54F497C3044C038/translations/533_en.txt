Enki and Akita Jōsuke Tokiaki, who was Takatoki's maternal uncle, received Sadatoki's dying instructions and assisted Takatoki. Enki was the son of a man called Mitsutuna, who was the nephew of Taira Saemon Yoritsuna. Tokiaki was the grandson of a man called Akimori, who was the brother of Shirakawa Mutsu-no-kami Yasumori.

In the first year of Shōwa, sixth month, Hōjō Munenori died. Hirotoki alone affixed his seal and administered all affairs. Enki and Tokiaki gradually began to wield power.

In the second year, seventh month, former Takatsukasa Kanpaku Motonada passed away at the age of sixty-seven. Kanpaku Fuyuhira resigned from his current position due to his father's mourning period. Konoe Iehira became Kanpaku. In the tenth month, Retired Emperor Fushimi transferred governmental affairs to Retired Emperor Go-Fushimi and took the tonsure. Since His Majesty (Emperor Hanazono) was originally Go-Fushimi's adopted son, ceremonies such as court visits followed the etiquette of father and son. In the twelfth month, Konoe Iehira resigned from Sadaijin (Minister of the Left). Nijō Michihira transferred from Udaijin (Minister of the Right) to the left, and Konoe Tsunehira from Naidaijin (Minister of the Center) to the right
