...Enki [Nagasaki Enki], and Takatoki's father-in-law, the Akita no Jōnosuke (Governor of Akita Castle) Tokiaki, received Sadatoki's dying instructions and served as assistants to Takatoki. Enki was the son of a man named Mitsunori, who was the nephew of Taira no Saemon Yoritsuna. Tokiaki was the grandson of a man named Akimori, who was the younger brother of Jō, Governor of Mutsu, Yasumori.

In the sixth month of the first year of Shōwa [1312], Hōjō Munenori died. Hirotoki administered all affairs alone as Shikken. Enki and Tokiaki gradually grew in power and influence.

In the seventh month of the second year [of Shōwa, 1313], the former Kanpaku of the Takatsukasa house, Mototada, passed away. He was sixty-seven years old. The Kanpaku Fuyuhira resigned from his post due to mourning for his father. Konoe Iehira became Kanpaku.

In the tenth month, the Retired Emperor Fushimi transferred governance to the Retired Emperor Go-Fushimi and entered the Buddhist priesthood. Since the Emperor [Hanazono] had originally been the adopted son of Go-Fushimi, the ceremonies of court attendance and the like followed the proprieties of a father-son relationship.

In the twelfth month, Konoe Iehira resigned as Minister of the Left. Nijō Michihira transferred from Minister of the Right to Minister of the Left, and Konoe Tsunehira [was promoted] from Minister of the Interior (Naidaijin) to Minister of the Right...
