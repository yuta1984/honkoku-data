In the second year of Chōgen (1029), in the fifth month, Chancellor Yorimichi invited ministers and below to his Shirakawa villa, where horse racing and court music performances were held. After Dōchō's death, Yorimichi succeeded him and conducted politics as he pleased. In the tenth month, Grand Minister Fujiwara no Kimsue died at the age of seventy-two. He was enfeoffed as Duke of Kai and posthumously named Duke Jingi. After this, there were no more deliberations on posthumous titles, because both sovereign and subjects had院号 (in-gō temple names). Kimsue was called the Minister of Kan-in. His descendants formed three Seiga noble lineages: Sanjō, Saionji, and Tokudaiji. In the twelfth month, Imperial Police Commissioner Nakahara no Narimichi was recalled to the capital because he had no success in defeating Tadatsune.
