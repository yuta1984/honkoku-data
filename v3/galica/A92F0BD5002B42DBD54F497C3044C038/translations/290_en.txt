The court dispatched Inspector Police Officers Taira no Naomasa and Nakahara no Narimichi, mobilizing forces from the Tōkaidō and Tōsandō circuits to suppress this (Taira no Tadatsune's rebellion).

In the second year of Chōgen (1029), in the fifth month, Chancellor Yorimichi invited ministers and below to Shirakawa Betsugyō (a villa at Shirakawa), where horse racing and court music were performed. After Dōchō's (Michinaga's) death, Yorimichi successively conducted politics as he pleased. In the tenth month, Grand Minister Fujiwara no Kinsue passed away at the age of seventy-two. He was enfeoffed as Duke of Kai and given the posthumous name Duke Jingi. From this point forward, there was no further consideration of posthumous titles, as both rulers and ministers had院号 (temple names). Kinsue was called the Kan'in Minister. His descendants formed three distinguished lineages: Sanjō, Saionji, and Tokudaiji. In the twelfth month, Inspector Police Officer Nakahara no Narimichi was recalled, as he had achieved no success in suppressing Tadatsune.
