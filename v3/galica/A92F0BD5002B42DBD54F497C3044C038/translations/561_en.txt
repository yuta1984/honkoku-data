In the seventh month, several generals of the besieging forces at Chiken-ha surrendered. They were all executed.

In the eighth month, Takauji was promoted to Junior Third Rank and appointed as Governor of Musashi Province. He was granted the character "Taka" and changed his name from Takauji to Takauji. His Majesty had already implemented a government under the unified control of the court nobles. Due to the appeals of Quasi-Empress Renshiko and others who enjoyed imperial favor, rewards and punishments were not administered fairly. The realm instead yearned for military rule. Middle Counselor Fujifusa repeatedly remonstrated, but was not heeded.

In the tenth month, Kitabatake Sangi Minamoto no Akiie was appointed as Governor of Mutsu Province and departed for his post. Both Dewa and Mutsu provinces submitted to him.

In the first month of the first year of Kenmu, Takauji was promoted to Senior Third Rank. In the same month, construction of the Great Imperial Palace commenced.
