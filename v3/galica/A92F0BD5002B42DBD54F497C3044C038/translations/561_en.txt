In the seventh month, several generals of the besieging forces at Chihaya surrendered, but all were executed. In the eighth month, Takauji was promoted to Junior Third Rank and appointed Governor of Musashi Province. He was granted the character "Taka" and changed his name from Kōuji to Takauji. His Majesty had already implemented a government unified under the court nobility, but due to the interference of favorites like Quasi-Empress Renshi, rewards and punishments were not administered fairly. The realm instead came to long for military rule. Middle Counselor Tōbō repeatedly remonstrated, but his advice was not accepted.

In the tenth month, Kitabatake Akiie, Counselor and member of the Minamoto clan, was appointed Governor of Mutsu Province and proceeded to his post. Both Dewa and Mutsu provinces submitted to him.

In the first month of Kenmu 1 (1334), Takauji was promoted to Senior Third Rank. In the same month, construction of the great imperial palace began. In the second month, Minamoto no Nagamichi resigned as Right Minister. Konoe Tsunetada was reappointed as Right Minister. In the spring of the same year, Takauji [received] Musashi...
