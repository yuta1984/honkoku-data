Through their machinations, he unexpectedly ascended to the throne at the age of sixty-two. Originally, during the Jinshin War, Prince Ōtomo was killed and Emperor Tenmu ascended the throne, causing the descendants of Emperor Tenji to decline and become insignificant. However, at this point, the royal descendants of Emperor Tenmu had instead died out, and the legitimate lineage of Emperor Tenji inherited the imperial succession.

In the tenth month of Hōki 1 (770), Senior First Rank was conferred upon Junior First Rank Left Minister Fujiwara no Nagatte. Before this, Tachibana no Moroe and Emi no Oshikatsu had been promoted to Senior First Rank. Including Nagatte, only these three were promoted while still alive - all subsequent promotions were posthumous honors. In the eleventh month, his father Prince Shiki was given the posthumous name Prince Tahara. In the twelfth month, Nagatte was granted a district of two hundred chō within Yamashiro Province.

In the second month of the second year (771), Left Minister Nagatte died at the age of fifty-eight. He was the grandson of Lord Ōmi and son of Fusasaki. In the third month, Right Minister Kibi no Makibi retired from office. Ōnakatomi no Kiyomaro was made Right Minister. Fujiwara no Yoshitsugu was made Inner Minister.
