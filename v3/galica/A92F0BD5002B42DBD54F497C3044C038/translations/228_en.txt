"What desires do you have?" The eldest son Koretada said, "I would like to be granted Tsukushi (Kyushu)." The second son Koresada said, "Grant me the Tōkaidō region." The third son Sadayoshi said, "I want to become Crown Prince." Later, when Emperor Kōkō ascended the throne, Sadayoshi was appointed to the position of chamberlain. During Emperor Kōkō's illness, upon the recommendation of Mototsune and others, Sadayoshi was made Crown Prince. Soon after, Emperor Kōkō passed away. Mototsune guided the Crown Prince to the Daigokuden (main hall of the imperial palace) and had him ascend to the throne. He was twenty-one years old. This occurred on the seventeenth day of the eleventh month of Ninna 3 (887). Mototsune submitted a memorial returning governmental authority. The Emperor declared, "I am now alone. If you do not become my assistant and attend to governmental affairs, I will abandon my position and retreat to the mountains and forests." Because of this, Mototsune again became regent.

In the fourth month of Ninna 4 (888), there was a drought in Sanuki Province. The provincial governor Kan-jōsō (Sugawara no Michizane) prayed for rain to the deity of Jōzan mountain in that province. In the eighth month, Ninna-ji temple was constructed. A memorial service was conducted with the monk Shinnen from Mount Kōya serving as the officiating priest.
