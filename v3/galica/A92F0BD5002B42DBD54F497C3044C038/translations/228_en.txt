The third son Sadayoshi said he would want to become Crown Prince. Later, when Emperor Kōkō ascended the throne, Sadayoshi was appointed as chamberlain (jijū). During Emperor Kōkō's illness, on the recommendation of Fujiwara no Mototsune and others, Sadayoshi was made Crown Prince. Soon after, Emperor Kōkō passed away. Mototsune escorted the Crown Prince to the Daigokuden and had him ascend to the throne. He was twenty-one years old. This was the 17th day of the 11th month of Ninna 3. Mototsune submitted a memorial returning governmental authority. The Emperor declared, "I am now alone. If you do not become my assistant and listen to governmental matters, I will abandon my position and enter the mountains and forests." Because of this, Mototsune again became regent (kanpaku).

In Ninna 4, 4th month, there was a drought in Sanuki Province. The provincial governor, Minister Kan (Sugawara no Michizane), prayed for rain to the deity of Shiroyama in that province. In the 8th month, Ninna-ji temple was built. The monk Shinnen from Mount Kōya served as the officiating priest for the dedication ceremony. Shinnen was a disciple of Kōbō Daishi (Kūkai).
