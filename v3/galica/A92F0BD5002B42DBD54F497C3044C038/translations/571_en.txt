On the second day of the intercalary seventh month, Yoshisada attacked Kuromaru Castle and was struck by a stray arrow and died. He was thirty-seven years old. Yoshisuke gathered the remnants of the defeated forces and returned to Echizen-fu. Yoshioki returned to the eastern provinces, and Akiuobu proceeded to Ōshū (Mutsu Province).

In the eighth month, Takauji was granted the Senior Second Rank (Shōnii) and received the imperial proclamation appointing him Seii Taishōgun (Shogun). Tadayoshi was granted the Junior Fourth Rank Upper Grade (Jushii-jō) and appointed Captain of the Left Division of the Military Guards (Sahyōe no Kami). According to one account, Tadayoshi was granted the Junior Fourth Rank Upper Grade and appointed to the same post. According to another account, it is said that Tadayoshi received an imperial decree appointing him Vice-Shogun. Kō Moronao was appointed Governor of Musashi and assigned to the position of Chief Steward (shitsuji) of the warrior house, and together with his younger brother Moroyasu, they wielded great power. Takauji did not personally handle government affairs, leaving their execution to Tadayoshi. The Kō brothers were not on good terms with Tadayoshi.

In the same month, Emperor Go-Daigo of the Southern Court passed away at Yoshino. He was fifty-one years old. It had been six years since his restoration to the throne. The seventh imperial prince, Yoshinaga, ascended to the throne. This was Emperor Go-Murakami. His mother was the Jungo Renshi, a woman deeply beloved by the late emperor.
