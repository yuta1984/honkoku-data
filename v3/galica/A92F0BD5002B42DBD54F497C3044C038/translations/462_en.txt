Hōsuke, the head abbot of Mount Hiei Egen, and the chief administrator of Mii-dera Gyōshō were also sons of Michiie. The precedent of entering Ninna-ji temple without being an imperial prince began with this. Former Chancellor Saionji Kintsune was Michiie's father-in-law. Therefore, the power of the imperial court lay with Michiie and Kintsune. However, all of this was through the arrangement of Hōjō Yasutoki.

In the first month of Tenpuku 1 (1233), Yoritsune was appointed to the position of Provisional Middle Counselor. In the fifth month, Former Regent Konoe Motomichi died at the age of seventy-four, taking the posthumous name Fugen-ji. In the ninth month, Sōheki Mon-in died at the age of twenty-five.

In the third month of Bunreki 1 (1234), Yasutoki's eldest grandson Tokiyoshi came of age, with Yoritsune himself performing the capping ceremony. In the fifth month, the deposed Emperor Kujō died at the age of seventeen. In the eighth month, Emperor Go-Horikawa died at the age of twenty-three. From this point on, Michiie and Kintsune acted increasingly according to their own will. In the twelfth month, Yoritsune was promoted to the Third Rank.
