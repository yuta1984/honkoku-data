In the first year of Jinki, second month, Right Minister Prince Nagaya became Left Minister. In the tenth month, there was an imperial visit to Tamatsushima in Waka-no-ura, Kii Province.

In the fourth year, imperial envoys were dispatched to all provinces to reform the administration of provincial governors. The good and evil deeds of all officials were also recorded.

In the fifth year, envoys from Balhae came and presented tribute. Balhae was a remnant tribe of Goguryeo. Before this, the kingdom of Goguryeo had been destroyed by the Tang Dynasty. Those who barely survived were called the kingdom of Balhae.

In the first year of Tenpyō, first month, there were rumors that Left Minister Prince Nagaya harbored treasonous intentions, so Minister of Civil Affairs Fujiwara no Umakai and others were dispatched to surround Prince Nagaya's residence, and Prince Toneri and others were sent to interrogate him about his crimes. Prince Nagaya committed suicide. His wife and children were all killed. Prince Nagaya was the grandson of Emperor Tenmu and the son of Prince Takechi.

In the eighth month, Fujiwara no Kōmyōshi was made Empress. She was also a daughter of Fuhito.
