In the first month of the fourteenth year, Minor Secretary Sugawara no Michizane and others were made to receive the Balhae envoys. Michizane is the Kan Shōjō (later known as Sugawara no Michizane). In the second month, Right Minister Fujiwara no Ujimune died. In the third month, Yoshifusa fell ill, so he was granted 500,000 coins for prayer expenses. He was also granted eighty ordinands. Senior Monk Shinga was appointed to the Bureau of Buddhist Affairs. In the fifth month, the Balhae envoys entered the capital and were lodged at the Kōrokan, where they were granted banquets. During their stay in the capital, Ariwara no Narihira and others went as imperial messengers to comfort them. Other literati also went to meet with them. Miyako no Yoshika also performed reception duties. Yoshika was a man of great learning. Afterward, the envoys were granted imperial letters and, without need for court audience, returned to their country from the Kōrokan. The Kōrokan was part of the Bureau of Barbarian Affairs, a place where foreigners were lodged. It was located near Rajōmon Gate of Tōji Temple. In the eighth month, Minamoto no Tōru became Left Minister and Fujiwara no Mototsune became Right Minister.
