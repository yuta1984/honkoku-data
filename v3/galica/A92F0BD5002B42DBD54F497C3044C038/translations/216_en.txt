In the fourteenth year, first month, Minor Secretary Sugawara no Michizane and others were made to receive the Balhae envoys. Michizane was (the later) Minister Kan. In the second month, Right Minister Fujiwara no Ujimune passed away. In the third month, Yoshifusa fell ill and was granted 500,000 coins to use for prayers. He was also granted eighty ordinands. Senior Monk Shinga was appointed to the Bureau of Buddhist Affairs. In the fifth month, the Balhae envoys entered the capital and were lodged at the Kōrōkan, where they were granted a banquet. During their stay in the capital, Ariwara no Narihira and others went as imperial messengers to show appreciation for their labor. In addition, men of letters went to meet with them. Miyako no Yoshika also performed greetings. Yoshika was a person of great learning. Afterward the envoys received an imperial letter and, without proceeding to court audience, returned to their country from the Kōrōkan. The Kōrōkan was part of the Bureau for Barbarian Affairs, a place where foreigners were housed. It was located near Rajō Gate of Tō-ji temple. In the eighth month, Minamoto no Tōru became Left Minister and Fujiwara no Mototsune became Right Minister.
