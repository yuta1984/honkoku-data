In the first month of the fourteenth year, Minor Secretary Sugawara no Michizane and others were made to receive the envoys from Bohai. Michizane was the Minister Sugawara. In the second month, Right Minister Fujiwara no Ujimune died. In the third month, Yoshifusa fell ill and was granted 500,000 coins for prayer expenses. He was also granted eighty ordinands. Senior Priest Shinga was appointed to the Office of Monastic Affairs. In the fifth month, the Bohai envoys entered the capital and were housed at the Kōrokan where they were granted banquets. During their stay in the capital, Ariwara no Narihira and others went as imperial messengers to comfort them. In addition, men of letters went to attend meetings with them. Miyako no Yoshika also conducted reception for them. Yoshika was a man of great learning. Afterwards the envoys received imperial edicts and returned home from the Kōrokan without participating in court audiences. The Kōrokan was under the Bureau of Barbarian Affairs. It was a place where foreign people were lodged. It was located near Rajō Gate of Tōji Temple. In the eighth month, Minamoto no Tōru became Left Minister. Fujiwara no Mototsune became Right Minister
