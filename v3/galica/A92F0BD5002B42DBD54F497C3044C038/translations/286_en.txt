In the ninth month, Dōchō made a pilgrimage to Iwashimizu Hachimangū. Court nobles and below accompanied him, and entertainers came out to greet them. When crossing the Yodo River, there were over fifty boats. One of them sank, and over thirty people died. In the twelfth month, Dōchō was appointed as Chancellor (Daijō-daijin). Regent Yorimichi served as the imperial messenger.

In the second year, on the third day of the first month, the Emperor underwent his coming-of-age ceremony. Dōchō performed the capping ceremony, and Yorimichi performed the hair-cutting ceremony. In the third month, Dōchō's daughter Ishi entered the imperial court and became an imperial consort (nyōgo). She later became Empress (chūgū). In the tenth month, there was an imperial visit to Dōchō's mansion.

In the third year, in the third month, Dōchō took Buddhist vows. He was fifty-four years old. People of the world called him Nyūdō-dono (the Lord Monk). When other people shave their heads, they refrain from calling themselves nyūdō out of deference. In the fourth month, over fifty ships of foreign pirates invaded Iki Island and killed the island governor Fujiwara no Masatada. Based on this report from Dazaifu, government forces from the headquarters were dispatched to subdue the bandits.
