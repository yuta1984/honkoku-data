On the fifth day of the same month, the Southern Emperor transferred the Three Sacred Treasures to the imperial palace. King Hiroshige (Go-Kameyama) received the honorary title of Retired Emperor and came to be known as the Cloistered Emperor Go-Kameyama. From the second year of Engen, when Emperor Go-Daigo had entered Yoshino, fifty-six years had now passed, and for the first time the Northern and Southern Courts were unified. Nevertheless, it is said that a small number of Southern Court remnants still remained deep in the Yoshino mountains.

In the twelfth month, Yoshimitsu was reappointed as Minister of the Left, owing to Tokudaiji Sanetoki's resignation from that office. In the same year, an envoy from Korea came and requested the renewal of friendly relations. Yoshimitsu granted this request.

In the fourth year (1392), fourth month, the Retired Emperor Go-Enyū passed away at the age of thirty-six. A funeral was held at Sennyūji temple, and Yoshimitsu also attended to pay his respects. In the eighth month, Yoshimitsu made a pilgrimage to the Iwashimizu Hachimangū for the Hōjōe ceremony. In the ninth month, Yoshimitsu resigned as Minister of the Left and made a pilgrimage to the Grand Shrine of Ise. In the same year, Shiba Yoshimasa was appointed Kanrei once again.
