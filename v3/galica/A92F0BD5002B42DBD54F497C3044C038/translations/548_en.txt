[Takaie] attempted to have [Yasuie serve as Shikken with Kanazawa Sadaaki as Renshō], but Nagasaki Takasuke refused to agree. Yasuie, angered by this, shaved his head and took the Buddhist name Eishō. Sadaaki also shaved his head. Hōjō Moritoki and Hōjō Koresada took up the positions of Renshō and Shikken respectively, but carried out their duties according to Takatoki's wishes.

In the seventh month, Kazuhito, the son of the Retired Emperor Go-Fushimi, was installed as Crown Prince. Although the Emperor had many imperial children of his own, the appointment of the Crown Prince was determined by the Kantō (shogunate), and thus the matter could not be decided according to the Emperor's own wishes.

In the ninth month, Kanazawa Sadamasa came up to the capital and resided at Rokuhara. After some time, he returned to Kamakura.

In the eleventh month, Saionji Naifu (Minister of the Interior) Sanehira passed away. Dainagon (Major Counselor) Fujiwara no Mototsugu was appointed Naidaijin (Minister of the Interior).

In the first month of the second year [of Karyaku, 1327], Takatsukasa Kampaku Fuyuhira passed away. He was fifty-five years of age. In the same month, the Zen monk Shōchō came from Yuan-dynasty China and took up residence as abbot of Kenchō-ji in Kamakura. Shōchō is none other than Seissetsu [Shōchō].

In the second month, Nijō Michihira was reappointed as Kampaku.

In the third month, Kujō Fusazane passed away. He was thirty-eight years of age.

In the tenth month, Hōjō Koresada died.
