The retired emperor (Sutoku), though he had become a monk, was exiled to Sanuki Province at the age of thirty-eight. Prince Shigehito also became a monk. Yorinaga's sons Kanenaga, Moronaga, Norinaga, and the monk Hannō were all sentenced to exile. Tameyoshi and Tadamasa surrendered, but Kiyomori reported this to the throne and executed Tadamasa and his sons. Because of this, an imperial command was given to Yoshitomo to kill Tameyoshi. Tameyoshi's eight sons were all captured and killed. Tametomo alone, being a warrior of unparalleled prowess, had his death sentence commuted and was exiled to Ōshima in Izu. The other party members were either executed or exiled. The lay priest and minister Fujiwara no Tadazane (Tomike no Nyūdō Sōkoku) had also favored Yorinaga and was on the retired emperor's side, so there was discussion that he should be exiled. However, Tadamichi petitioned through Shinzei that "if my father is exiled, I cannot remain as regent," so Tadazane was pardoned. When Tadazane heard this, father and son were reconciled for the first time. In this battle, both lords and vassals, high and low alike...
