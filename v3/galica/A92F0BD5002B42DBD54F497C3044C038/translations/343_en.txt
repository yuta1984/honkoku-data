Because [Sanenō] was Right General, [the emperor] thought favorably of Saneyuki in various ways and had suppressed [the appointment of] Masasada. However, one night the retired emperor suddenly visited the imperial palace and spoke directly [to the emperor], so Masasada finally became Left General. The superintendency of Shōgaku-in and Jun'na-in is a position held by the head of the Minamoto clan. By decree of the retired emperor, it was permanently assigned to Masasada's house.

In the first year of Eiji (1141), in the third month, the retired emperor took Buddhist vows at Toba Palace. He was called Retired Emperor Toba. He was thirty-nine years old. In the twelfth month, through the machinations of the retired emperor, the emperor abdicated for no particular reason and yielded the throne to his younger brother, Crown Prince Narihito. From this time, [the emperor] was at odds with the retired emperor. The era names were: Tenji 2 years, Daiji 5 years, Tenshō 1 year, Chōshō 3 years, Hōen 6 years, Eiji 1 year. Total reign: eighteen years.

Seventy-sixth Emperor
Emperor Konoe - The eighth son of Retired Emperor Toba. His personal name was Narihito. His mother was Bifukumon-in.
