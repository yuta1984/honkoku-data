Since [Sanenō] was Right General, [the emperor] rather favored Saneyuki and tried to suppress Masasada's appointment. However, one night the retired emperor suddenly visited the imperial palace and spoke directly [to the emperor], so Masasada finally became Left General. The superintendency of both Shōgaku-in and Jun'wa-in academies was a position held by the head of the Minamoto clan. By the retired emperor's decree, it was permanently attached to Masasada's house.

In the third month of Eiji 1 (1141), the retired emperor took Buddhist vows at Toba Palace and was called Toba Retired Emperor (Hōō). He was thirty-nine years old. In the twelfth month, through the retired emperor's machinations, the emperor abdicated without any particular reason and yielded the throne to his younger brother, Crown Prince Narihito. From this time, [the emperor] became estranged from the retired emperor. The era names were: Tenji 2 years, Daiji 5 years, Tenshō 1 year, Chōshō 3 years, Hōen 6 years, and Eiji 1 year. Total reign: eighteen years.

76th Emperor
Emperor Konoe　The eighth son of Toba-in. His personal name was Narihito. His mother was Bifukumon-in.
