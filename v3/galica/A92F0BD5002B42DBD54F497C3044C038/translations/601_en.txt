In the third month of the third year (of Ōan, 1370), Momoi Naotsune's grandson Naokazu and others advanced to Nagasawa in Etchū Province. They fought a battle against Shiba Yoshimasa, in which Naokazu and others were killed, and the remaining forces collapsed and fled.

In the same month, Regent Moroyoshi was transferred to the position of Sadaijin (Minister of the Left). Kujō Dainagon Tadamoto was appointed Udaijin (Minister of the Right). Sanjō Sanetsugi resigned from the position of Naidaijin (Minister of the Center). The former Dainagon of Kajūji, Fujiwara no Tsuneaki, was appointed Naidaijin. He was seventy-three years old and had rendered meritorious service since the reign of the Cloistered Emperor Kōgon.

In the fourth month, Yoshimitsu made pilgrimages to the New Hachiman Shrine at Rokujō, as well as to Kitano and Gion shrines.

In the eleventh month, Wada (某, name unknown) and other warriors of the southern forces attacked Kusunoki Masanori's stronghold and engaged in battle. Yoriyuki mustered a large army and advanced southward, drove off the enemy, and left Yamana Ujikiyo stationed in Kawachi Province as a check against the southern forces, after which Yoriyuki himself returned to the capital.
