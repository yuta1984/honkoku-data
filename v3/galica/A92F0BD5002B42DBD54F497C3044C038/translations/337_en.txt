(continuing from previous page) ...there was rebellion. His father Yoshiie was appointed Governor of Mutsu Province and there was the rebellion of Takehira and Iehira. Now if Tameyoshi were to be appointed, there would certainly be warfare with Fujiwara no Motohira, so imperial permission was not granted. Motohira was the son of Kiyohira. Tameyoshi was indignant and was not appointed to any provincial governorship.

In the third month of the third year (Daiji 3, 1128), Enshō-ji temple was constructed as a pious wish of Taiken Mon'in. In the tenth month, there was a memorial service for the complete Buddhist scriptures at Iwashimizu, and the Retired Emperor made a pilgrimage there. In the twelfth month, Regent Tadamichi was appointed Chancellor.

In the third month of the fourth year (Daiji 4, 1129), pirates arose in the San'yō and Nankai circuits, obstructing travel, so an imperial decree was issued to Taira no Tadamori, Governor of Bizen Province, ordering him to capture the bandits. In the sixth month, Tadamichi resigned as regent and became kampaku (chief advisor to the emperor). This was due to the emperor's coming of age ceremony. On the seventh day of the seventh month, Retired Emperor Shirakawa died at the age of seventy-seven. This retired emperor had personally conducted government from the time of his reign, and even after abdicating continued [to rule] through the reigns of Horikawa, Toba, and the current era...
