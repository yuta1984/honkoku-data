If Tameyoshi were to be appointed now, there would certainly be warfare with Fujiwara no Motohira, so imperial permission was not granted. Motohira was the son of Kiyohira. Tameyoshi was indignant and was not appointed to any provincial governorship.

In the third year (1128), in the third month, Enshōji Temple was constructed as a votive offering by Empress Taikemon'in. In the tenth month, there was a memorial service for the complete Buddhist scriptures at Iwashimizu Shrine, and the Retired Emperor made an imperial visit. In the twelfth month, Regent Tadamichi was appointed Chancellor.

In the fourth year (1129), in the third month, pirates arose in the San'yō and Nankai regions, obstructing travel, so an imperial decree was issued to Taira no Tadamori, Governor of Bizen, ordering him to capture the bandits. In the sixth month, Tadamichi resigned as regent and became kampaku (chief advisor to the emperor). This was because of the emperor's coming-of-age ceremony. On the seventh day of the seventh month, Retired Emperor Shirakawa died at the age of seventy-seven. This retired emperor had personally administered government from the time of his reign, and even after abdicating continued [to rule] through [the reigns of] Horikawa, Toba, and the current era.
