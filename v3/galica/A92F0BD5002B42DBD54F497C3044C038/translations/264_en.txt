In the first month of Tenroku 1, Fujiwara no Arihira was appointed Minister of the Left. Fujiwara no Koretada was appointed Minister of the Right. Koretada was the eldest son of Morosuke and the emperor's maternal uncle. Arihira was originally from a Confucian scholarly family. During the reign of Emperor Murakami, he established his house through scholarship, and because his daughter became an imperial consort and received imperial favor, he was promoted to high office. Apart from Kibi no Makibi and Minister Sugawara [no Michizane], Arihira was the only person from a Confucian scholarly family to rise to the rank of minister. In the fifth month, Regent and Grand Minister Fujiwara no Saneyori passed away at the age of seventy-one. He was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Owari, and given the posthumous name Seishin-kō. Minister of the Right Koretada became regent. In the tenth month, Minister of the Left Arihira passed away at the age of seventy-nine.

In the third month of the second year, the Iwashimizu Rinji Festival was held for the first time. Imperial messenger Middle Captain Tadakiyo and others attended. In the eleventh month, Koretada was appointed Grand Minister. Minamoto no Kaneakira was made Minister of the Left, and Fujiwara no Yorichika was made Minister of the Right. Kaneakira was an imperial prince of Emperor Daigo. Yorichika was the son of Saneyori.
