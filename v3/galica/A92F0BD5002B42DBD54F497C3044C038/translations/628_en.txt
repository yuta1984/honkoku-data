In the third month of the first year of Ōei (1394), Minamoto no Tomomiichi resigned as Minister of the Right. Former Minister of the Center (Naidaijin) Imaōgawa Kinjika was appointed Minister of the Right. In the sixth month, Ichijō Naidaijin Tsunetsugu was appointed Minister of the Left. Former Minister of the Left Tokudaiji Sanetoki was appointed Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin). Quasi-Minister (Jun-daijin) Minamoto no Yoshinari was appointed Naidaijin (Minister of the Center).

In the ninth month, Yoshimitsu made a pilgrimage to the Hiyoshi Shrine. In the eleventh month, Nijō Morotsugu resigned as Regent (Kampaku). Ichijō Tsunetsugu became the new Regent.

On the seventeenth day of the twelfth month, Yoshimitsu's eldest legitimate son Yoshimochi came of age (genpuku) at the age of nine. He was granted the Junior Fifth Rank Lower Grade, appointed as Middle Captain of the Left (Sachūjō), and was permitted the privileges of wearing forbidden colors (kinshoku) and ascending to the imperial audience hall (shōden). Yoshimitsu relinquished the title of Seii Taishōgun (Shogun) to Yoshimochi. Generally speaking, regarding the ceremony of rank bestowal (joshaku), members of the Sekke (the Five Regent Houses) receive the Junior Fifth Rank Lower Grade, while others, even those of the Seiga houses, receive only the Junior Fifth Rank Lower Grade of the outer rank. On this occasion, Yoshimochi's rank bestowal was conducted in accordance with the standard for the Sekke. In the same month, Tokudaiji Sanetoki resigned as Grand Minister of State. On the twenty-fifth day, Yoshimitsu was appointed Grand Minister of State, at the age of thirty-seven.
