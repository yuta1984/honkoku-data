The eldest son of former Minister of State Saneuji, Major Counselor Kinsuke, and his younger brother Major Counselor Kinmoto became Left and Right Generals respectively, and eventually were all appointed as ministers. In the eighth month, Dōgen, the founding abbot of Eiheiji in Echizen, passed away. This marked the beginning of the spread of the Sōtō Zen sect in Japan. In the tenth month, there was an imperial visit for court audience. Around this time, Retired Emperor Go-Saga was residing at Toba Detached Palace. In the eleventh month, Tokiyori built Kenchōji temple and held its consecration ceremony. The Song dynasty monk Dōryū served as the officiating priest. Dōryū was also called Rankei - this is Daikaku Zenji. Dōryū was the first foreign monk to come to Japan.

In the sixth year, eleventh month, Ashikaga Samanosuke Yoshiuji died. He was the great-grandson of Yoshikuni, the third son of Yoshiie. Yoshikuni had two sons: the elder was called Nitta Yoshishige and became the ancestor of the Nitta clan. The younger was called Ashikaga Yoshiyasu.
