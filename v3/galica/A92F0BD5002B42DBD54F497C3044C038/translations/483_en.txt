The former Chancellor (Shōkoku) Saneūji's eldest son, the Great Counselor (Dainagon) Kinsuke, and his younger brother, the Great Counselor Kinmoto, were appointed side by side as the Left and Right Commanders of the Imperial Guards, and eventually both were appointed as Ministers of State. In the eighth month, Dōgen, the founding abbot of Eiheiji Temple in Echizen, passed away. This was the beginning of the spread of the Sōtō sect of Buddhism in Japan. In the tenth month, there was an imperial procession for a visit to the Retired Emperor (chōkin no gyōkō). Around this time, the Retired Emperor Go-Saga was residing at the Toba Detached Palace. In the eleventh month, Tokiyori constructed Kenchōji Temple and held a dedication ceremony. The Song-dynasty monk Dōryū served as the officiating priest. Dōryū is also known by the name Rankei. He is none other than the Zen Master Daikaku. The arrival of Dōryū marks the first time a foreign Zen monk came to Japan.

In the eleventh month of the sixth year, Ashikaga Yoshiuji, the Master of the Left Stables (Samanokame), passed away. He was the great-grandson of Yoshikuni, the third son of Yoshiie. Yoshikuni had two sons: the elder was called Nitta no Yoshishige, who was the founding ancestor of the Nitta clan, and the younger was called Ashikaga no Yoshiyasu.
