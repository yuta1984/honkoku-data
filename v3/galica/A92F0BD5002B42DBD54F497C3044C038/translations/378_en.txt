Hearing this, he secretly went to Fukuhara, relied on Fujiwara no Mitsuyoshi to petition for an imperial decree from the Retired Emperor, then returned to Izu and presented it to Yoritomo. In the eighth month, Yoritomo sent his father-in-law Hōjō Tokimasa, along with the Sasaki brothers and others, to kill Yamamoto Hōgan Taira no Kanetaka, the deputy governor of Izu. After this, Yoritomo himself advanced into Sagami Province with more than three hundred mounted warriors and established a position at Ishibashiyama. Ōba Kagechika, a warrior of that province, had originally been a retainer of Yoshitomo, but having recently received great favors from the Taira clan, he attacked with more than three thousand mounted warriors. Yoritomo was defeated, and Sanada no Yoichi Yoshitada was killed in battle. Yoritomo retreated into Sugiyama. When Kagechika pursued him, Sasaki Takatsuna and others resisted. In the meantime, Yoritomo escaped. Those who followed him to one place were Tokimasa, Doi Sanehira, Okazaki Yoshizane, Adachi Morinaga, Tsuchiya Muneto, Shinkai Tadauji, Doi Tōhira, and others.
