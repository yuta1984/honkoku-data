Upon hearing this, he secretly went to Fukuhara, relied on Fujiwara no Mitsuyoshi to obtain an imperial decree from the Retired Emperor, returned to Izu, and presented it to Yoritomo. In the eighth month, Yoritomo dispatched his father-in-law Hōjō Tokimasa, along with the Sasaki brothers and others, and killed Yamamoto Hangan Taira no Kanetaka, the deputy governor of Izu. Then Yoritomo himself advanced into Sagami Province with over three hundred horsemen and set up camp at Ishibashiyama. Ōba Kagechika, a warrior of that province, had originally been a retainer of Yoshitomo, but having recently received great favors from the Taira clan, he came to attack with over three thousand horsemen. Yoritomo's army was defeated, and Sanada no Yoichi Yoshitada was killed in battle. Yoritomo retreated and entered Sugiyama. Kagechika pursued him. Sasaki Takatsuna and others resisted him. During this time, Yoritomo escaped. Those who followed him to one place were Tokimasa, Doi Sanehira, Okazaki Yoshizane, Adachi Morinaga, Tsuchiya Munetō, Shinkai Tadauji, Doi Tōhira, and others.
