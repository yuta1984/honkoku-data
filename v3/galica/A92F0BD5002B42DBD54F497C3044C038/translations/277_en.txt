Koremasa (Fujiwara no Koremasa, the eldest son of Michitaka) was made Naidaijin (Minister of the Center).

In the first year of Chōtoku, first month: There was an imperial visit to pay respects to the Nyōin (empress dowager). Third month: Michitaka took Buddhist vows due to illness. After reporting this to the emperor, his son Koremasa was made acting Kanpaku (regent). Soon after, Michitaka died at age forty-three. Fourth month: Udaijin (Minister of the Right) Michikane was made Kanpaku. Koremasa became angry, and relations between uncle and nephew became strained. He performed magical rituals against Michikane. Fifth month, seventh day: Sadaijin (Minister of the Left) Minamoto no Shigenobu died at age seventy-four. Eighth day: Kanpaku Michikane died. He is known as the Awata Kanpaku. Eleventh day: Michikane's younger brother, Sadaishō (Major Captain of the Left) Michinaga, was made Kanpaku. Upon hearing of Michikane's early death, Koremasa rejoiced. He had thought he himself would become Kanpaku, but when Michinaga was appointed according to the Nyōin's wishes, he became even more discontented. He again performed magical rituals against Michinaga. However, there was no effect. At this time an epidemic spread, and many from court nobles down died of illness. Seventh month: Michinaga became Udaijin. From this time, Michinaga [controlled] court politics as he wished...
