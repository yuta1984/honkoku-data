His military power was strong, and Taira no Naomasa was also recalled due to lack of success. The court commanded Kai Governor Minamoto no Yorinobu to gather forces from Bandō (eastern Japan) to suppress Tadatsune.

In the fourth year of Chōgen (1031), in the fourth month, Yorinobu led troops to attack Tadatsune's fortress. Since the fortress was located by the seaside, Tadatsune had previously given orders to hide all the boats completely. Therefore, Yorinobu had no way to cross. However, as Yorinobu possessed both wisdom and courage, he estimated where there might be shallow waters and drove his horse into the sea. Among his soldiers, there was someone who knew the location of the shallow crossing, but initially he remained silent and did not speak. However, seeing his general's brave demeanor, he guided them to the shallow waters, and all the troops crossed the sea on horseback. Seeing this, Tadatsune was frightened by their威勢 (military prowess) and, realizing he could not win, surrendered. Yorinobu immediately took Tadatsune with him and went to the capital. Tadatsune died of illness in Mino Province.
