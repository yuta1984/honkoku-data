He was the great-grandson of Emperor [Kan]mu and the son of Prince Shioyaki. His mother, Imperial Princess Fuwa, was exiled to Awaji Province. Since Emperor Kōnin had passed away and the court was in mourning, the death penalty was commuted to exile. Among the court nobles and officials, those who were accomplices and relatives were all sentenced to exile.

In the fifth month, there was a divine oracle at Usa Hachiman Shrine, declaring that [the deity] should be called Great Bodhisattva.

In the sixth month, Left Minister Fujiwara no Uona was found guilty of crimes, dismissed from office, and sent down to Kyushu. He was later pardoned, returned to the capital, and died there. Fujiwara no Tamaro became Right Minister. During this period, there was only one person serving as either Left Minister or Right Minister, not both positions simultaneously. A person holding the rank of Major Counselor assisted the Minister in government affairs.

In the third month of the second year, Right Minister Fujiwara no Tamaro died at the age of sixty-two. 《He was the son of Umakai》

In the seventh month, Fujiwara no Korekimi became Right Minister.

In the tenth month, the Emperor made an imperial visit to Katano for falconry.
