However, through Sanehira's stratagem, he deliberately scattered Tokimasa and others, while Yoritomo hid in the fallen trees. Sanehira accompanied him. Kajiwara Heizō Kagetoki, a member of Kagechika's clan, came searching and claimed to have found where Yoritomo was hiding, but he deliberately pretended not to see and said "there are no human traces in this mountain," then departed for elsewhere, taking Kagechika with him. Yoritomo barely escaped with his life and entered Mount Hakone. Tokimasa and others also met up with him. Tokimasa's eldest son Munetoki was killed on the road. Miura no suke Yoshiaki sent his son Yoshizumi, his legitimate grandson Wada Yoshimori, and others with three hundred cavalry to Ishibashi, but since Yoritomo had already been defeated and his whereabouts were unknown, on their way back to Miura they encountered Hatakeyama Shigetada at Kotsubo and fought a battle, achieving victory. Shigetada again assembled a large force and attacked Miura's Kinugasa Castle, so Yoshiaki died in battle at the age of eighty-nine. He had Yoshizumi, Yoshimori and other clan members search for Yoritomo. Yoritomo went from Hakone to Doi.
