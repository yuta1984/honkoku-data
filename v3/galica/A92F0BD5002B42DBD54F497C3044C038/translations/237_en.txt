Because he devoted himself entirely to the Buddhist Way, he was called Hōō (Dharma Emperor). This was the beginning of the title Hōō.

In the third year, on the third day of the first month, the Emperor paid a formal visit to Suzaku-in. Minister Kan accompanied him. He presented a poem and was granted imperial robes. As his favor and glory flourished day by day, Tokihira harbored feelings of jealousy. According to one theory, at this time there was a secret imperial intention to make Minister Kan the Kanpaku (regent), but he firmly declined, and this was never publicly announced. In the same month, Takafuji was appointed Naidaijin (Minister of the Center). Since Emperor Kanmu's time, this office had been vacant. In the third month, Takafuji died at the age of sixty-three. He was posthumously granted the rank of Daijō-daijin (Chancellor) with the court rank of Shōichii. In the seventh month, a comet appeared. In the tenth month, there was a person called Miyoshi no Kiyotsura. He was learned and a master skilled in mathematics. At this time he held the office of Monjo Hakase (Professor of Literature), and he submitted a letter to Minister Kan stating that the following year would be the year of Kanoto-tori (Metal Rooster).
