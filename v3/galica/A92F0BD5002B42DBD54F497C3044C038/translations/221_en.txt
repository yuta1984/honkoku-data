On the eighth day of the eleventh month, Udaijin Mototsune ceased being regent and became kanpaku (chief adviser to the emperor). This was the beginning of the kanpaku system. From the end of this month, the Retired Emperor fell ill.

On the fourth day of the twelfth month, Mototsune was appointed as Daijō-daijin (Chancellor). With the father and son Yoshifusa and Mototsune succeeding each other as regent, kanpaku, and chancellor, all power in the imperial court came to belong to the Fujiwara clan. The Retired Emperor passed away at the age of thirty-one. Various memorial services were held.

On the second day of the first month of the sixth year, the Emperor came of age. Mototsune performed the ceremony of placing the cap. Dainagon Minamoto no Ōshi served as the one who arranged the hair. The ceremony was solemn and dignified. Minamoto no Ōshi became Udaijin. Fujiwara no Yoshiyo and Fujiwara no Fuyuo became Dainagon. Ariwara no Yukihira and Minamoto no Yoshiari became Chūnagon. In the second month, Mototsune was granted the rank of Jun-sangū and awarded zuijin (personal attendants) and heijō (ceremonial weapons).
