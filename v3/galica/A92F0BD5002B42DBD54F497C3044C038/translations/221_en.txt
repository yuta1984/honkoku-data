He was a master of Japanese poetry and a person of amorous disposition. On the eighth day of the eleventh month, Udaijin Mototsune ceased being regent (sesshō) and became chancellor (kanpaku). This was the beginning of the kanpaku system. From the end of this month, the Retired Emperor fell ill. On the fourth day of the twelfth month, Mototsune was appointed Grand Minister of State (dajō-daijin). With the father and son Yoshifusa and Mototsune succeeding each other as regent-chancellor and prime minister, all power in the court came to belong to the Fujiwara clan. The Retired Emperor passed away at the age of thirty-one. Various memorial services were performed.

On the second day of the first month of the sixth year, the Emperor came of age. Mototsune performed the cap-adding ceremony. Dainagon Minamoto no Tōru performed the hair-cutting ceremony. The ritual was conducted with great solemnity. Minamoto no Tōru became Udaijin. Fujiwara no Yoshiyo and Fujiwara no Fuyuo became Dainagon. Ariwara no Yukihira and Minamoto no Yoshiari became Chūnagon. In the second month, Mototsune was granted the treatment of Jun-Sankyū and awarded zuijin guards with weapons.
