From the reign of Emperor Toba to the present reign, he served as Regent and Chancellor, was appointed Minister of State again, and administered the government for about forty years. In the eighth month, Sutoku-in died in Sanshū at the age of forty-six and was buried at Shiramine. In the tenth month, Chancellor Motazane resigned from the position of Minister of the Left, and Munenō resigned from the position of Minister of the Center. Motofusa was transferred to Minister of the Left, and Fujiwara no Tsunemune became Minister of the Right. Kanezane, the younger brother of Motofusa and a Grand Counselor, was appointed Minister of the Center. Motofusa and Kanezane concurrently held the positions of Left and Right Generals. Motazane was the founder of the Konoe house. Motofusa was called Matsudono, and Kanezane was called Tsukimaro and also called Kujō-dono. These three ministers were all sons of Tadamichi.

In the first year of Nin'an (1166), in the second month, Ōmiya Chancellor Koremichi died at the age of seventy-three. In the third month, Minamoto no Tametomo, an exile on Izu Ōshima, crossed over to Onigashima and seized control of that territory. In the fifth month, Taira no Shigemori was appointed Counselor (Sangi).
