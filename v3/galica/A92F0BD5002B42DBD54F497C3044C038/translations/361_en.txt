Tadamichi died at the age of sixty-eight. He was called Hōshōji-dono. From the reign of Emperor Toba to the present reign, he served as regent and chancellor, was appointed chancellor (taikokudaijin) twice, and held control of court administration for about forty years. In the eighth month, Former Emperor Sutoku died in Sanuki Province at the age of forty-six. He was buried at Shiramine. In the tenth month, Chancellor Motomichi resigned from the position of Minister of the Left, and Muneyoshi resigned from Minister of the Interior. Motofusa was transferred to Minister of the Left, and Fujiwara no Tsunemune became Minister of the Right. Motofusa's younger brother, Major Counselor Kanezane, was appointed Minister of the Interior. Motofusa and Kanezane concurrently held the positions of Left and Right Palace Guards Commanders. Motomichi is the ancestor of the Konoe house. Motofusa was called Matsudono, and Kanezane was called Tsukino-wa and also called Kujō-dono. These three ministers were all sons of Tadamichi.

In the first year of Eiman, second month, Ōmiya Chancellor Michimichi died at the age of seventy-three. In the third month, the exile Minamoto no Tametomo from Izu Ōshima crossed to Onigashima and took control of that territory. In the fifth month, Taira no Shigemori was appointed Middle Counselor.
