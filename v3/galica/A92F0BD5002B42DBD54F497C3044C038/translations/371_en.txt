Major Counselor Tokitada ascended the mountain as an imperial messenger to pacify the warrior monks. Morotaka was exiled and Moritsune was imprisoned. On the twenty-eighth day of the same month, a great fire broke out in the capital. The wind was fierce, and the entire Great Imperial Palace was consumed by flames.

In the fifth month, the Cloistered Emperor, indignant at Mount Hiei's forceful petitions, exiled the head abbot Meiun to Izu Province. Since there were rumors that this was due to Saikō's slander, the warrior monks rose up and seized Meiun on the road, taking him back up the mountain. The Cloistered Emperor became even more enraged and ordered Naritsune and others to attack Mount Hiei. At that time, a man named Tada Kurōdo Yukitsuna, who had been asked by Naritsune and had promised to become a general for the subjugation of the Taira, suddenly had a change of heart. On the twenty-ninth day of the same month, he went to Rokuhara and reported Naritsune's secret plot. Kiyomori was greatly enraged, and on the first day of the sixth month, he arrested Naritsune, Saikō, and all their associates. Saikō was immediately executed. His sons Morotaka and Moritsune were also sentenced to death.
