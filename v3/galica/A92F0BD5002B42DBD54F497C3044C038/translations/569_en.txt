Kōmyō-in: His personal name was Toyohito. He was the fourth son of Emperor Gofushimi.

In the eighth month of Kenmu 3 (1336), he ascended the throne through the arrangements of Takauji (Ashikaga Takauji). At this time, the Hanazono-in was given the title of Hon'in (the Senior Retired Emperor), and the Kōgon-in was given the title of Shin'in (the Junior Retired Emperor).

In the third month of Kenmu 4 (Southern Court: the second year of Gendai under Emperor Go-Daigo, 1337), the generals of Takauji attacked and captured Kanegasaki Castle. Yoshisada (Nitta Yoshisada) and Yoshisuke secretly took refuge in Uryū's Somayama Castle. Yoshisada's eldest son, Yoshiaki, took his own life. Prince Tsuneyoshi also took his own life. The Crown Prince, Tsuneyoshi (Tsunenaga), returned to Kyoto. Thereafter, Tadayoshi (Ashikaga Tadayoshi) killed him.

In the fourth month, Konoe Kampaku Tsunetada proceeded to the Southern Court. Tsunetada's cousin, the former Naifu Motitsugu, became Kampaku.

In the seventh month, Tōin Kinkata resigned from the position of Ufu (Minister of the Right). Ichijō Naifu Tsunemichi was appointed Safu (Minister of the Left), Kujō Dainagon Michitomo was appointed Ufu (Minister of the Right), and Takatsukasa Dainagon Morahira was appointed Naifu (Minister of the Center).

In the eighth month, Kitabatake Akiie, the Provincial Governor of Mutsu, raised an army.

In the twelfth month, he attacked Kamakura. Yoshisada's second son, Yoshioki, joined this campaign.
