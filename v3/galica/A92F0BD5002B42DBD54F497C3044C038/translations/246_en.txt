In the third year, first month, bandits arose in the capital. In the second month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Nakahira became Right Minister. He was the elder brother of Tadahira. In the twelfth month, more than ten courtiers went falconing at Oharano, displaying the utmost beauty in their attire.

In the fourth year, pirates arose in the San'yō and Nankai regions. Officials were ordered to capture them.

In the fifth year, Jiang Chengxun, a man from Wuyue of Tang China, came and presented sheep.

In the sixth year, third month, there was a small archery competition (ketsuban) at Hikōsha. In the sixth month, the ringleader of the Nankai pirates, Fujiwara no Sumitomo, gathered his followers and assembled more than two thousand vessels at Hiburi Island in Iyo Province, plundering official goods traveling by sea. Because of this, Ki no Yoshito was dispatched as Governor of Iyo. Since Yoshito pacified them with benevolence, the pirates were temporarily subdued. In the seventh month, Jiang Chengxun of Wuyue came to Dazaifu. In the eighth month, Tadahira sent a letter to the King of Wuyue in Great Tang.
