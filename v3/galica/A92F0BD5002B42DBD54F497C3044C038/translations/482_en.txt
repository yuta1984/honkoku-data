(The former Shogun Yoritsugu) was removed from his post and returned to the capital. From the second year of Kangen to the third year of Kenchō, his reign lasted eight years. As Munetaka was a prince of the imperial blood, two or three nobles and courtiers attended closely upon him, and the ceremonial protocols were carried out with great solemnity, surpassing those of previous generations. The reverence shown by Tokiyori and his associates grew ever greater. The former Shogun's residence was torn down, and a new palace was newly constructed and offered for the Prince's residence. In the tenth month, Konoe Kanetsune resigned as Regent (Sesshō). His younger brother, the Minister of the Left Kanehira, became Regent. This was the beginning of the Takatsukasa family. Prior to this, Michiie's eldest son Norisane had succeeded to the Kujō house, his second son Yoshizane had taken the name Nijō, and his third son Sanetsune had taken the name Ichijō. Now, a further branch split off from the Konoe to become the Takatsukasa. From this time on, they came to be called the Five Regent Houses (Go-Sekke). Could it be that the warrior house had arranged this so as to divide the power of the regency? In the first month of the fifth year, the Emperor came of age (genpuku). He was eleven years old. As the Saionji clan, due to their power as maternal relatives of the Emperor, and with the backing of the Hōjō, they advanced in court rank and office as they pleased…
