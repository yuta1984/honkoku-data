Since Munetaka was an imperial prince, two or three court nobles and courtiers attended him closely, and the ceremonies were more solemn and magnificent than in previous eras. The reverence of Tokiyori and others also greatly increased. The former shōgun's residence was demolished and a new palace was built for him to move into. In the tenth month, Konoe Kanesane resigned from the position of regent (sesshō). His younger brother, Minister of the Left Kanehira, became regent. This was the beginning of the Takatsukasa house. Before this, Dōke's eldest son Norizane succeeded the Kujō house, his second son Yoshizane took the title Nijō, and his third son Sanetune took the title Ichijō. Now Konoe split off to become Takatsukasa. From this point they were called the Five Regent Houses (go-sekke). Perhaps this was arranged by the military government (bakufu) to divide the power of the regent houses.
