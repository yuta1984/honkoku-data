He ascended the throne on the twenty-eighth day of the fourth month of Tengyō 9. At that time he was twenty-one years old.

The emperor was naturally intelligent and composed both poetry and waka.

On the fourth day of the first month of Tenryaku 1, there was an imperial visit to Suzaku-in for the court ceremony of chōkin. He had an audience with his mother Empress Dowager Onshi and the Retired Emperor. In the fourth month, Fujiwara no Saneyori was transferred to Left Minister and concurrently served as Left General, while his younger brother Morosuke was appointed Right Minister and concurrently served as Right General. Their father Tadahira had already been Regent and Grand Minister for many years. At this point, the father and his two sons—three men from one family—simultaneously held the three highest court positions, an unprecedented prosperity. Tadahira was called Lord Koichijō, Saneyori was called Lord Onomiya, and Morosuke was called Lord Kujō. Morosuke's daughter Yasuko was the emperor's consort. In the sixth month, Counselor Fujiwara no Tadafumi died at age seventy-five. He was posthumously granted the rank of Middle Counselor. This man had served as general for the subjugation of Masakado and went down to the eastern provinces. Though he returned from the journey without achieving military merit, he was still granted rewards.
