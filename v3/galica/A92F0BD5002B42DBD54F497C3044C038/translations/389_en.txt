She was the daughter of Shichijō Shuridaifu Nobutaka. Being favored by Emperor Takakura, she gave birth to two imperial princes, Morisada and Narinari. In the seventh month of Juei 2 (1183), when the Former Emperor (Antoku) departed for the western seas, the Cloistered Emperor (Go-Shirakawa) summoned his two grandsons for an audience. Morisada was five years old and cried when he saw the Cloistered Emperor. Narinari was four years old but climbed onto the Cloistered Emperor's lap. The Cloistered Emperor was pleased and placed him on the imperial throne.

Fujiwara no Motomichi, though he was Kiyomori's son-in-law, remained in the capital and continued as regent as before. All governmental affairs were conducted under the Cloistered Emperor's direction, while the security of the capital was maintained by Kiso Samanosuke Yoshinaka. In the ninth month, the Former Emperor made an imperial visit to Usa Hachiman Shrine in Buzen Province.

The Taira had temporarily settled at Dazaifu, waiting for an opportune moment, when Ogata Saburō Koreyoshi of Bungo Province raised troops and attacked Dazaifu. Unable to remain even in Kyushu, they took the Former Emperor with them and headed for Shikoku. When passing Yanagi-ga-ura in Buzen Province, Sachūjō Kiyotsune threw himself into the sea and died. He was Shigemori's third son.
