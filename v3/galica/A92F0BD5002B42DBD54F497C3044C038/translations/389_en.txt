She was the daughter of Shichijo Shuri-no-taifu Nobutaka. Beloved by Emperor Takakura, she gave birth to two imperial princes: Morisada and Takahira. In the seventh month of Juei 2 (1183), when the Former Emperor (Antoku) departed for the western seas, the Cloistered Emperor (Go-Shirakawa) summoned his two grandsons for an audience. Morisada, who was five years old, cried when he saw the Cloistered Emperor. Takahira, who was four years old, climbed onto the Cloistered Emperor's lap. The Cloistered Emperor was pleased and had him ascend to the imperial throne.

Although Fujiwara no Motomichi was Kiyomori's son-in-law, he remained in the capital and continued as regent. All government affairs were conducted under the Cloistered Emperor's authority, and the security of the capital was entrusted to Kiso Sama-no-kami Yoshinaka.

In the ninth month, the Former Emperor made a pilgrimage to Usa Hachiman Shrine in Buzen. The Taira had temporarily settled at Dazaifu, waiting for an opportune moment, when Ogata Saburo Koreyoshi of Bungo raised troops and attacked Dazaifu. Unable to remain even in Kyushu, they departed for Shikoku, taking the Former Emperor with them. When passing Yanagi-ga-ura in Buzen, Sachu-jo Kiyotsune threw himself into the sea and died. He was Shigemori's third son.
