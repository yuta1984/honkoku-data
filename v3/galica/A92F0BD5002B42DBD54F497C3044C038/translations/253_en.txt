In the second month, a great amnesty was proclaimed. This was due to the pacification of military rebellions in the eastern provinces and western seas.

In the third month of the fifth year, envoys bearing offerings were dispatched to Ise and Usa shrines, and for the first time there was an imperial visit to Kamo Shrine. This was because the military rebellions had been pacified.

In the fourth month of the seventh year, Fujiwara no Saneyori was appointed Right Minister. He was the eldest son of Tadahira.

In the ninth month of the eighth year, Left Minister Fujiwara no Nakahira passed away at age seventy-one. He was known as the Biwa Left Minister.

In the fourth month of the ninth year, the emperor abdicated in favor of his younger brother Nariakira and moved to reside at Suzaku-in. He received the honorary title of Retired Emperor. The era names spanned from Jōhei 7 to Tengyō 9, with a total reign of sixteen years.

62nd Emperor
Emperor Murakami: The fourteenth son of Emperor Daigo. He was the younger brother of Emperor Suzaku by the same mother. His personal name was Nariakira. Since Emperor Suzaku had no children, Nariakira was made crown prince and received the abdication.
