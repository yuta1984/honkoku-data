In the second month, a general amnesty (daisha) was proclaimed, on account of the pacification of the military disturbances in the eastern provinces and the western seas.

In the third month of the fifth year (of Tengyō), imperial envoys bearing offerings (hōheishi) were dispatched to the shrines at Ise and Usa, and for the first time, an imperial visit (gyōkō) was made to the Kamo Shrine. This too was because the military disturbances had been quelled.

In the fourth month of the seventh year, Fujiwara no Saneyori was appointed as Minister of the Right (Udaijin). He was the eldest son of Tadahira.

In the ninth month of the eighth year, the Minister of the Left (Sadaijin), Fujiwara no Nakahira, passed away. He was seventy-one years old. He was known by the posthumous title "Biwa Sadaijin" (Minister of the Left of Biwa).

In the fourth month of the ninth year, the Emperor abdicated in favor of his imperial younger brother, Nariakira, and took up residence at Suzaku-in. He was given the honorary title of Retired Emperor (Daijō Tennō). The era names spanned Jōhei (year seven) and Tengyō (year nine), with a combined reign of sixteen years.

　　The Sixty-Second Sovereign
Emperor Murakami: The fourteenth son of Emperor Daigo, and the younger brother of Emperor Suzaku by the same mother. His personal name (imina) was Nariakira. Since Emperor Suzaku had no children, Nariakira was designated Crown Prince, and the throne was passed to him.
