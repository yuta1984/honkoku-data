In the first year of Chōwa, first month, Dōchō's daughter Kenshi was made Chūgū (Empress Consort). On the twenty-first day of the third month, offerings were made to twenty-one shrines to pray for abundant harvests of the five grains.

In the second year, ninth month, the Emperor made an imperial visit to Dōchō's mansion. In the eleventh month, he visited Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine. In the twelfth month, he visited Kamo Shrine. From this time forward, successive generations of emperors would make imperial visits to both shrines. In the same month, a man called Fujiwara no Masanobu, who was a minor official (shōshin) in the Chūgū's household, was killed by Fujiwara no Korekane, a samurai from the same office. Dōchō was enraged and had Korekane arrested and imprisoned.

In the third year, second month, ninth day, the Imperial Palace burned down. In the fifth month, there was an imperial visit to Dōchō's mansion, with entertainments including horse racing and mounted archery. In the sixth month, Genshin, the monk-administrator (sōzu) of Eshin-in on Mount Hiei, passed away.

In the fourth year, ninth month, the reconstruction of the Imperial Palace was completed. In the tenth month, Dōchō's fiftieth birthday was celebrated. In the eleventh month, the Imperial Palace caught fire again.
