The Uesugi clan bore the Fujiwara surname and was a cadet branch of the Kajūji lineage. A man called Uesugi Shigehusa accompanied Prince Munetaka to Kamakura, and from that time the clan resided in the Kantō region. His son was called Yorishige, and Yorishige's daughter Kiyoko was the mother of Ashikaga Takauji and Tadayoshi. Kiyoko's siblings were called Norihusa and Shigeatsu. Norihusa's son, Noriatsu, the Minbu no Taifu (Senior Assistant Minister of Popular Affairs), was the ancestor of Norizada and Norimoto. This branch was called Yamanouchi (Yamanouchi-Uesugi), and was the head family (tōryō) of the Uesugi clan. Noriatsu's younger brother Norifuji, the Danjō Shōhitsu (Junior Assistant of the Censorate), was the ancestor of Zenshū. The descendants of Shigeatsu were called the Ōgigayatsu-Uesugi. Beyond these, the clan branched out further: some resided in Echigo, some lived in Shiroi in Kōzuke province, and others traveled back and forth between Kyoto and Kamakura. From Motoujii through Ujimitsu, Mitsukane, and Mochiuji, they wielded power throughout the Kantō region by relying on the support of the Uesugi clan.
