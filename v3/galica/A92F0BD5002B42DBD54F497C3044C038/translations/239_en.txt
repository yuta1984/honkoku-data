They consulted with Fujiwara no Sugane and others, sometimes performing religious rituals to curse Minister Suga, and sometimes adding various slanderous accusations against him. The Emperor's younger brother was called Prince Saisei, who was Minister Suga's son-in-law. Therefore, Tokihira reported to the Emperor that the reason Suga had previously prevented Emperor Uda's abdication was a scheme to make Saisei the crown prince. Since the Emperor was only seventeen years old this year, perhaps he could not judge the truth or falsehood of these claims. Tokihira, as a hereditary regent, wielded strong authority and governed despotically, as was heard. Minamoto no Hikaru was made Right Minister in place of Minister Suga. When Retired Emperor Uda heard of this and tried to mitigate the crime of Minister Suga's demotion by visiting the court on the last day of the same month, the guards would not open the gates. Though he stood at the imperial gates all night, there was no one to report his presence to the Emperor. At dawn on the first day of the second month, the Retired Emperor returned in vain.
