In the first year of Ōhō (1161), in the second month, there was an imperial visit to Kasuga, and imperial visits to various other shrines in the capital region as well. In the eighth month, Tokudaiji Minister of the Right Kimiyoshi died at the age of forty-seven. Motofusa became Minister of the Right, and Grand Counselor Fujiwara no Munenō became Minister of the Center. Taira no Kiyomori was appointed Middle Counselor (Chūnagon). In the eleventh month, Bifukumon-in died.

In the second year, in the first month, there was an imperial visit to pay respects to the Retired Emperor. In the same month, the former Chancellor Munesuke died. Taira no Kiyomori became the Director (Bettō) of the Imperial Police (Kebiishi). In the sixth month, the Lay Priest and Minister of State (Nyūdō Saikoku) Fujiwara no Tadazane of the Fuke family died at the age of eighty-four. This was the Chisokuin Regent.

In the first year of Chōkan (1163), in the first month, Taira no Shigemori was granted the court rank of Junior Third Rank (Jūsanmi).

In the second year, in the second month, the construction of Renge-ō-in, the Retired Emperor's temple of prayer, was completed.
