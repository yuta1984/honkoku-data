[The text continues]. In the fifth month, Retired Emperor Moritei passed away. He was given the posthumous name Retired Emperor Go-Takakura. In the tenth month, Ietsuki resigned from the regency and became chancellor (kanpaku).

In the second month of Gen'nin 1, a drifting ship from Goryeo (Korea) washed ashore in Echigo Province. The implements from the ship were sent to Kamakura. On the thirteenth day of the sixth month, Hōjō Yoshitoki died of illness at the age of sixty-two. According to one theory, it was sudden death. According to another theory, he was stabbed to death by a young attendant.

Since Genkyū 2, he had served as regent (shikken) for twenty years. At this time, Yasutoki was in Kyoto, but he hurriedly traveled down to Kamakura together with Tokifusa. Iga Mitsumune and others, who were brothers of Yoshitoki's wife, secretly conspired with Miura Yoshimura to push aside Yoritsune, kill Yasutoki, make Yoshimura's son-in-law, Minister of the Left and Middle Captain Fujiwara no Sanemasa, the lord of Kamakura, and make Masumura, Yoshitoki's legitimate son, the regent.
