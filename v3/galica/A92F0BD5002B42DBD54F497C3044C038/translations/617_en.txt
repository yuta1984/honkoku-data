His post as Right General (Udaishō) remained unchanged. After some days, he was further transferred to Left General (Sadaishō). The Great Counselor (Dainagon) Fujiwara no Sanetoki was appointed to the post of Inner Minister (Naidaijin). In the intercalary first month, Yoshimitsu was appointed as the head (bettō) of the Kurōdo-dokoro (Imperial Secretariat). In the third month, Yoshimitsu was granted permission to ride in an ox-drawn carriage (gissha). In the fourth month, the Emperor abdicated in favor of his son, Prince Motohi (幹仁). During this reign, the first three years used the era name "Ōan" (応安) from the previous reign, followed by Eiwa (four years), Kōryaku (two years), and Eitoku (two years). The total length of reign was eleven years.

　　The 101st Emperor
The Retired Emperor Go-Komatsu — His personal name (imina) was Motohi (幹仁). He was the eldest son of Emperor Go-Enyū. His mother was Tsūyōmon-in, Fujiwara no Itsuko, the daughter of the Inner Minister (Naidaijin) Kintada of the Sanjō family. In the fourth month of Eitoku 2 (1382), he acceded to the throne at the age of six. Sanjō no Motoie, former Regent and Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin), Yoshimoto, served as Regent (Sesshō). The Retired Emperor Go-Enyū conducted state affairs from within the In (Retired Emperor's court).
