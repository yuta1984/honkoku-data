Minamoto no Makoto was made Left Minister, and Yoshisuke was made Right Minister. Makoto was the Emperor's uncle.

After Ōtomo no Takamichi, Oshikatsu, and Dōkyō, the position of Grand Minister had remained vacant. Now Yoshifusa was appointed. From this time on, members of the Fujiwara clan would continue to advance in succession. In the third month, Yoshifusa was permitted to wear a sword when attending court. This followed the precedent of the Chinese Han minister Xiao He. Yoshifusa repeatedly declined this honor. In the sixth month, a rebellion occurred on Tsushima Island and the provincial governor was killed. Troops were dispatched from Dazaifu to suppress it. In the eleventh month, the posthumous rank of Dai-Sōjō (Grand Monk) was bestowed upon Kōbō (Kūkai) at the request of his disciple, the monk Shinsai. In the twelfth month, the first prince Koretaka came of age in the imperial presence and was granted the fourth rank. Yoshifusa and others were summoned and a banquet was held. The Emperor loved this prince, and while Korihito was still young, he wished to temporarily establish Koretaka as crown prince first.
