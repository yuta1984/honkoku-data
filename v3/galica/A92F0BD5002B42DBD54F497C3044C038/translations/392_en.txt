When Yoritomo heard of this in the east, he sent his brothers Kaba no Kanja Noriyori and Kurō Yoshitsune with sixty thousand mounted warriors to march on the capital and attack Yoshinaka. Although Yoshinaka tried to resist at Uji and Seta, Sasaki Takatsuna and Kajiwara Kagesue, who followed Yoshitsune, crossed the Uji River, followed by Hatakeyama Shigetada, and won the battle. Since Yoshitsune quickly entered the capital and guarded the Cloistered Emperor's palace, Yoshinaka fled the capital and fought repeatedly around Seta, with all his retainers being killed. He was struck by a stray arrow and died at Awazu Plain at the age of thirty-one. Imai Kanehira also died in battle at the same place. Higuchi Kanemitsu had been in Kawachi trying to attack Yukiie, but when he heard this news, he returned to the capital and surrendered, but was executed by beheading. In the same month, Regent Moroie was dismissed, and Mototō became regent again. The Taira clan, during Kiso's period of evil rebellion, pacified much of the western provinces and controlled the Settsu Province area
