Without the Grand Minister's directive, it would be difficult to undertake this task," and immediately sent a messenger to inform Yoshifusa. Yoshifusa was greatly alarmed and said, "During the previous emperor's reign, when the current emperor was crown prince, it was through this person's (Minamoto no Makoto's) efforts that the imperial succession was secured. Therefore, the Left Minister is a meritorious retainer. What crime could he have committed? If the Left Minister is to be charged with a crime, then Yoshifusa should be executed first." This was reported to the throne. Due to this, the Left Minister was safe.

On the third day of the eighth month, there was a man named Ōyake no Takatori who accused that "the Ōtenmon Gate was burned by Yoshio and his son, who secretly went there in the middle of the night and set it on fire, then falsely reported that it was the Left Minister's doing." When Sangi Minabuchi no Toshina and Fujiwara no Yoshitsuna were ordered to investigate this matter, it became clear beyond doubt that it was indeed Yoshio's doing. Though he should have been sentenced to death, his punishment was reduced by one degree and he was exiled to Izu Province.
