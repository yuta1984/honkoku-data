Without the knowledge of the Grand Minister, I cannot accept responsibility for this," and immediately sent a messenger to inform Yoshifusa. Yoshifusa was greatly surprised and said, "During the previous emperor's reign, when the current emperor was crown prince, it was through this person's (Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto's) efforts that the imperial succession was secured. Therefore, the Left Minister is a meritorious retainer. What crime could he have committed? If the Left Minister were to be charged with a crime, Yoshifusa should be executed first." This was reported to the emperor. As a result, the Left Minister was safe. Then on the third day of the eighth month, there was a person named Ōyake no Takatori who accused them, saying "The Ōtenmon Gate was burned by Yoshio and his son, who secretly went there in the middle of the night and set it on fire, then falsely reported that it was the Left Minister's doing." Therefore, Sangi Minamibuchi no Toshina and Fujiwara no Yoshitsuna were ordered to investigate thoroughly, and it was clearly determined that it was indeed Yoshio's doing. Although he should have been sentenced to death, his punishment was reduced by one degree, and he was exiled to Izu Province.
