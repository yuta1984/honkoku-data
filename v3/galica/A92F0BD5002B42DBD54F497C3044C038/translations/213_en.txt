[Yoshimi said] he devoted himself to Buddhism and did not involve himself in politics, so he did not yet know about this matter. Mototsune said, "This is a matter of great importance to the realm. Without the Grand Minister's knowledge, it would be difficult to accept responsibility for this." He immediately sent a messenger to inform Yoshifusa. Yoshifusa was greatly surprised and said, "During the previous emperor's reign, when the current emperor was crown prince, it was through this person's (Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto's) power that the imperial succession was secured. Therefore, the Left Minister is a meritorious retainer. What crime could there be? If the Left Minister is to be punished, then Yoshifusa should be executed first." This was presented to the throne. Because of this, the Left Minister was cleared of suspicion. Then, on the third day of the eighth month, a man named Ōyake no Takatori came forward and accused [Tomo no Yoshio], saying that "Yoshio and his son secretly went [to the Ōtenmon Gate] in the middle of the night, set it on fire and burned it, then falsely accused the Left Minister of the deed." Sangi Minamibuchi no Toshina and Fujiwara no Yoshitsuna were ordered to investigate, and it was clearly determined that it was indeed Yoshio's doing. Although he should have been sentenced to death, his punishment was reduced by one degree, and he was [exiled] to Izu Province.
