(Yoshimi said:) "...devoted himself to Buddhism and does not concern himself with government affairs, so he does not yet know of this matter." Mototsune replied, "This is a matter of great importance to the realm. Without the Grand Minister's direction, it is difficult for me to comply," and he immediately sent a messenger to inform Yoshifusa. Yoshifusa was greatly alarmed and said: "During the reign of the previous emperor, when the current emperor was still Crown Prince, it was through this man's [Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto's] efforts that the imperial succession was secured. Therefore, the Left Minister is a meritorious retainer. What crime could he have committed? If the Left Minister is to be punished, then Yoshifusa should be executed first." He presented this to the throne. As a result of this, the Left Minister was unharmed. In due course, on the third day of the eighth month, a man called Ōya no Takatori came forward with an accusation, stating that Yoshio and his son had secretly gone to the Ōtenmon Gate in the middle of the night, set fire to it, and then reported it as the work of the Left Minister. Sangi Minabuchi no Toshina and Fujiwara no Yoshitsuna were ordered to conduct a thorough investigation, and it became undeniably clear that it was indeed the work of Yoshio. Although he deserved the death penalty, his sentence was reduced by one degree, and he was exiled to Izu Province.
