Without the Grand Minister's directive, it would be difficult to proceed," and immediately sent a messenger to inform Yoshifusa. Yoshifusa was greatly alarmed and said, "In the previous emperor's time, when the current emperor was crown prince, it was through this person's (Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto's) efforts that his position was secured. Therefore, the Left Minister is a meritorious retainer. What crime could he have committed? If the Left Minister is to suffer punishment, Yoshifusa should be executed first." This was reported to the emperor. As a result, the Left Minister was cleared of wrongdoing.

Later, on the third day of the eighth month, there was a person named Ōyake no Takatori who reported that "Yoshio and his son secretly went to the Ōten-mon gate in the middle of the night, set it on fire and burned it, then instead reported that it was the Left Minister's doing." When Sangi Minabuchi no Toshina and Fujiwara no Yoshitsuna investigated this matter, it became clear beyond doubt that it was Yoshio's doing. Though he should have been sentenced to death, his punishment was reduced by one degree and he was exiled to Izu Province.
