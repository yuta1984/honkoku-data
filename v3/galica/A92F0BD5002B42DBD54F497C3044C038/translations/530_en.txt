[Continued from the previous page: Though holding only the name of supreme military commander, with all matters great and small decided by the Hōjō clan,] perhaps because so many years had passed, a change of position was made. Hisaaki's son, Prince Morikuni, was a mere seven years of age, yet he was elevated to the position of Seii Taishōgun (Barbarian-Subduing Generalissimo) and made lord of Kamakura. As Sadatoki had already taken the tonsure, Hōjō Morotoki and Hōjō Munenori were appointed as Shikken (regent) and Renshō (co-signatory) respectively. Morikuni's mother was the daughter of [Prince] Koreyasu.

In the eighth month, the Emperor [Go-Nijō] passed away at the age of twenty-four. His reign had encompassed the era names Kengen (first year), Kagen (third year), and Tokuji (second year and beyond), totaling more than six years on the throne.

The Ninety-Fourth Sovereign:
Emperor Hanazono — his personal name (imina) was Tomihito. He was the second son of the Fushimi-in (Retired Emperor Fushimi). His mother was Kenshine Mon'in, Fujiwara no Atsuko, daughter of the Minister of the Left, Sanemao. During the reign of Emperor Go-Nijō, he was established as Crown Prince (Tōgū) through the arrangement of the Kantō (the Bakufu).

In the eighth month of the third year of Tokuji [1308], Emperor Go-Nijō passed away. The Crown Prince ascended to the throne at the age of twelve.
