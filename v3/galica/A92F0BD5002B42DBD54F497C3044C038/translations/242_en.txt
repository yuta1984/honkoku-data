In the fourteenth year, first month, over six hundred houses in the capital burned down. In the sixth month, there was a great flood. In the seventh month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Tadahira became Minister of the Right. He was the younger brother of Tokihira.

In the sixteenth year, third month, seventh day, there was an imperial visit to Shujaku-in to celebrate the Retired Emperor's fiftieth birthday. On the fifth month, seventh day, Prince Sadazumi died. He was the sixth son of Emperor Seiwa and was Prince Momozonoo, the ancestor of the Minamoto clan. On the twenty-first day of the same month, the wind and rain were fierce, and Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Sadakata and Fujiwara no Kiyotsura inspected the embankments of the Kamo River.

In the seventeenth year, there was a great drought, and the ponds in the capital dried up.

In the twentieth year, fifth month, the Balhae envoy Pei Qiu came to court again. He was granted the court rank of Senior Third Rank and returned to his country.

In the twenty-first year, tenth month, Minor Counselor Taira no Koresuke was sent as an imperial messenger to Mount Koya, and the title of Daishi (Great Master) was posthumously granted to Kobo.
