He sought to subjugate the northern provinces. This was the grandson of Tameyoshi and son of Yoshikata. He was separated from his father at age two and had long resided in Shinano. He was Yoritomo's cousin. Nitta Ōi no suke Yoshishige was at Terao Castle in Kōzuke Province. Being the legitimate grandson of Yoshiie and son of Yoshikuni, he had aspirations of independence and for a while did not submit to Yoritomo, but later he surrendered. Yoritomo constructed and restored Tsurugaoka Hachimangū shrine in Kamakura, which had been established by Yoriyoshi. Since Hachiman was the tutelary deity of the Genji clan, he particularly venerated it. Yoritomo was exiled at age fourteen, and after more than twenty years, he restored his family's fortunes. This year he was thirty-four years old. When Kiyomori heard of this, he became greatly enraged and made his legitimate grandson Shōshō Koremori the general, with his younger brother Satsuma no kami Tadanori as vice-general, along with Kazusa no kami Tadakiyo, Saitō Sanemori and others - thirty thousand cavalry in total - to attack Yoritomo.
