He sought to subjugate the northern provinces. This was the grandson of Tameyoshi and son of Yoshikata. He was separated from his father at age two and had long resided in Shinano. He was Yoritomo's cousin. Nitta Ōi no suke Yoshishige was at Terao Castle in Kōzuke Province. Being the legitimate grandson of Yoshiie and son of Yoshikuni, he harbored ambitions of independence and for a while did not submit to Yoritomo, but later he surrendered. Yoritomo reconstructed and restored Tsurugaoka Hachiman Shrine in Kamakura, which had been established by Yoriyoshi. Since Hachiman was the tutelary deity of the Genji house, he particularly venerated it. Yoritomo was exiled at age fourteen, and after more than twenty years had passed, he revived his family's fortunes. This year he was thirty-four years old. When Kiyomori heard this, he became greatly enraged and made his legitimate grandson Shōshō Koremori the general, his younger brother Satsuma no kami Tadanori the vice-general, and along with Kazusa no kami Tadakiyo, Saitō Sanemori and others totaling thirty thousand cavalry, he had them attack Yoritomo.
