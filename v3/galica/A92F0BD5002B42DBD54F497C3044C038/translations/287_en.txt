In the first year of Manjū (1024), in the third month, there were many robbers in the capital. The Imperial Police (kebiishi) arrested them. In the ninth month, there was an imperial visit to Yorimichi's mansion. In the twelfth month, Major Counselor Kintō retired from office. He was the son of Yoritada and was skilled in poetry, music, and string instruments. The "Collection of Japanese and Chinese Poems for Recitation" was compiled by this person.

In the second year, in the eighth month, Imperial Attendant (naishi-no-kami) Fujiwara no Yoshiko was Dōchō's third daughter. She went to serve Crown Prince Atsunaga and was favored by him, giving birth to a prince, but died after three days. She was nineteen years old. Since the time of Emperor Ichijō's reign, Dōchō had experienced only fortunate events in succession, but at this point he encountered sorrow. Yoshiko was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank.
