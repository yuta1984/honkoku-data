He was also appointed as Awa no Kami (Governor of Awa Province), and in his arrogance, he not only treated Sadatoki with contempt but Yoritsuna also secretly plotted to install Awa no Kami as Shōgun. Yoritsuna's eldest son, Munetsuna, revealed this plot to Sadatoki. As a result, both Yoritsuna and Awa no Kami were put to death. Munetsuna was exiled to Sado Province. He was later pardoned and recalled, and was made to serve as Kanryō (administrative regent). He was subsequently exiled again to Kazusa Province due to another offense. In the twelfth month, Ichijō no Moto-Sesshō (Former Regent) Ietsune passed away. He was forty-six years of age. He was posthumously known as Kōmyōbuji.

In the second year (of Einin, 1294), eighth month, Takatsukasa no Moto-Kampaku (Former Chief Regent) Shōkoku Kanehira passed away. He was sixty-seven years of age. He was posthumously known as Shōnen-in.

In the third year (of Einin, 1295), sixth month, Hōjō Kanetoki returned to Kamakura from Chinzei (Kyushu). In the ninth month, he died of illness at the age of thirty-five.
