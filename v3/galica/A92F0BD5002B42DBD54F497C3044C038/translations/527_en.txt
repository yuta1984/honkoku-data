In the first month of the third year [of Kagen] (1305), Konoe Iehira, the Dainagon (Major Counselor), was appointed Naidaijin (Minister of the Interior). Around this time, two men — Hōjō Motoki and Hōjō Tokimura — were serving as regents (shikken) as deputies on behalf of Sadatoki. Hōjō Munekata was a grandson of [Hōjō] Tokiyori and was vying for power against Motoki. However, since Tokimura was also close to Motoki, their combined influence was strong. Therefore, Munekata planned to first kill Tokimura and then plot against Motoki; he secretly gathered troops under the false claim that it was by order of the Shōgun, Prince Hisaaki, and launched a night attack, killing Tokimura. Tokimura was sixty-four years old at the time. Sadatoki, enraged, dispatched Hōjō Munenori and Utsunomiya Sadatsuna to kill Munekata. All of his associates were also put to death. Munenori was appointed to assist Motoki as co-administrator of the regency (shikken kajō).

In the fourth month, Nijō Kanemooto stepped down as Kampaku (Imperial Regent), and Kujō Moronori, the Sadaijin (Minister of the Left), became Kampaku. [Moronori] resigned from the post of Sadaijin.

In the ninth month, the Cloistered Emperor Kameyama passed away at the age of fifty-seven. At the time of the funeral procession, the Retired Emperor Go-Uda also attended in attendance.
