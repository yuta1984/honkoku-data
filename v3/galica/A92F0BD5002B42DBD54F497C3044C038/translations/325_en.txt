In the third month of Kahō 1 (1094), Morozane resigned from the position of kampaku (regent). His son, Minister of the Interior Moromichi, became kampaku. However, for a while he was positioned below Left Minister Minamoto no Toshifusa. At this time Morozane was fifty-three years old and was called the Kyōgoku Kampaku. Moromichi was thirty-three years old and later was called the Nijō Kampaku. In the sixth month, Major Counselor Minamoto no Tsunenobu was transferred to the position of Provisional Governor-General of Dazaifu and went down to Kyushu. At this time he was seventy-nine years old. In the same month, Counselor Fujiwara no Michitoshi and Ōe no Masafusa were appointed to Middle Counselor. In the ninth month, Right Minister Akifusa died at the age of fifty-eight. This person excelled in waka poetry. His elder brother Toshifusa was superior in literary talent. Because Akifusa was the maternal grandfather of the current emperor, the retired emperor intended to promote him and appoint him as kampaku before Toshifusa, but Ōe no Masafusa remonstrated, and first Toshifusa was appointed minister, then Akifusa was promoted next.

In the fourth month of the second year, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo. In the eighth month, the emperor suffered from malaria, and because there was efficacy from the prayers of High Priest Ryūmyō, he was permitted the use of a palanquin.
