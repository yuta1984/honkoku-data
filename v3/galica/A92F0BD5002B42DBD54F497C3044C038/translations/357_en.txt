The soldiers came, dug up the earth, severed Shinzei's head, and returned to the capital. All of his children were sentenced to exile. They confined the Retired Emperor near the Imperial Palace and placed the sovereign in the Kuroto Palace. Nobuyori made himself minister and general, resided in the forbidden precincts, sat in the imperial seat at court, wielded power arbitrarily, and bestowed rewards upon Yoshitomo and others. When Kiyomori heard of this, he hastily returned to the capital and went back to his mansion at Rokuhara. On the night of the twenty-sixth day of the same month, through the arrangements of Major Counselor Fujiwara no Tsunemune and the Retired Emperor's administrator Fujiwara no Koremasa, the sovereign secretly proceeded to Rokuhara. The Retired Emperor secretly went to Ninna-ji temple. All court officials went to Rokuhara. On the twenty-seventh day, with Kiyomori's eldest son Shigemori as general, they attacked Nobuyori and Yoshitomo. Nobuyori was cowardly and could not fight. Although Yoshitomo and his son Akugenda Yoshihira fought several battles, the Minamoto were ultimately defeated and fled to the eastern provinces. Nobuyori was captured and executed.
