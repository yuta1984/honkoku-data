In the first month of the eighth year, the Emperor's evil deeds became increasingly severe. When Mototsune came to court and observed the situation, he saw the Emperor making people climb trees and then stabbing them to death from below, laughing and enjoying himself. Mototsune thought that at this rate the imperial throne would be in danger, so he approached closely and reported: "Your Majesty appears to be bored, so shall we organize horse racing entertainment? Perhaps Your Majesty would like to make a royal visit to observe it?" The Emperor was pleased and said, "Set a date."

On the fourth day of the second month, the Emperor boarded the imperial carriage and departed the palace. Mototsune posted guards at all the gates of the inner palace and had them keep strict watch. He directed the imperial carriage to a place called Nijō Yōzei-in and made his announcement: "Since Your Majesty suffers from madness, it is not appropriate for you to remain on the imperial throne.
