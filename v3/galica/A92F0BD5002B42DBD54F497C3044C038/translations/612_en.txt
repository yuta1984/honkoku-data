At that time, the capital (Kyoto) fell into turmoil. Because of this, the forces that had been dispatched to Nanto (Nara) were recalled. However, Yoshimasa and the Toki forces retreated and dispersed along the way. Yoshimasa returned to Kyoto from Ōmi Province (Gōshū). As rumors of a conspiracy surrounded Toki Daizen no Daibu, Yoshimitsu dispatched official missives (mikyōsho) to the various provinces, ordering them to punish the Toki clan.

In the third month, upon hearing of the conspiracy in Kamakura, Yoshimitsu sent a personally written letter to Uesugi Noriharu, directing him to maintain peace throughout both the capital and the provinces. Though Noriharu admonished Ujimitsu, Ujimitsu would not abandon his treacherous plot. Consequently, Noriharu took his own life. Ujimitsu, shocked and remorseful, finally abandoned his rebellious intentions. Noriharu's younger brother, Awa no Kami Norikata, was appointed to oversee government affairs. It was at this time that Norikata first took up residence at Yamanouchi in Kamakura.

In the same month, Toki Daizen no Daibu was pardoned and sent an envoy to Kyoto. However, Sasaki Daizen no Daibu blocked the road and refused to let the envoy pass. Upon hearing this, Yoshimitsu became all the more forgiving toward the Toki clan, while resolving to punish the Sasaki clan.
