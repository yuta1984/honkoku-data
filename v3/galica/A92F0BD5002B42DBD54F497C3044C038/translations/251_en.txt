Masakado's several brothers and their associates, including Fujiwara no Harushige and Prince Kōse, were all killed in various places. Sadamori was the son of Kunika. Since Masakado was his father's enemy, he was particularly motivated in his military achievements. Hidesato initially intended to serve under Masakado and went to his residence. Masakado joyfully welcomed him. However, Hidesato perceived that Masakado's character was frivolous and that he would not be able to achieve his true ambitions, so he ultimately joined forces with Sadamori and accomplished great deeds. Since the Bandō region was already pacified, on the ninth day of the third month, Hidesato was granted the court rank of Junior Fourth Rank Lower. He was appointed as governor of both Shimotsuke and Musashi provinces. This Hidesato is the one known to the world as Tawara Tōta. Later, both Hidesato and Sadamori became Chinjufu generals. Sadamori was promoted to Senior Fifth Rank Upper and appointed as Assistant Director of the Right Division of Bureau of Horses. On the twenty-fifth day of the same month, Masakado's head arrived in Kyoto. In the fourth month, Tadafumi and others returned to the capital from Kiyomi Barrier in Suruga Province.
