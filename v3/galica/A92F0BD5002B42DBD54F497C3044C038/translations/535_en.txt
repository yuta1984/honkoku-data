In the first month of the fifth year of Shōwa [1316], the former Naidaijin Minamoto no Tokomori passed away. In the seventh month, Hōjō Takatoki appeared for the first time at the deliberative council (hyōjō) as the Shikken of Shogun Morикuni, being then fourteen years of age. Hōjō Mototoki resigned as Shikken. He later shaved his head, took the religious name Shinnin, and adopted the temple name Fuonji. In the eighth month, Fuyuhira resigned as Kanpaku. Nijō Minister of the Left Michihira became Kanpaku and resigned as Minister of the Left. In the tenth month, Konoe Minister of the Right Tsunehira was transferred to Minister of the Left; Tōin Naidaijin Saneyasu was appointed Minister of the Right; and Imadegawa Dainagon Kinkiyo was appointed Naidaijin.

In the third month of the first year of Bunpō [1317], Takatoki, at the age of fifteen, was appointed Governor of Sagami. Although it was said that he was by nature not suited to the role of Shikken, following the precedents established since [Hōjō] Yasutoki's time, Akita Jōnosuke Tokiaki and Nagasaki Enki sought to establish him in the position. In the sixth month, Tōin Saneyasu resigned as Minister of the Right. Imadegawa Naidaijin Kinakira was appointed Minister of the Right.
