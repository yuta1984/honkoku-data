In the sixth month, Dainagon (Major Counselor) Fujiwara no Fuyuji was appointed Naidaijin (Minister of the Center).

In the same month, the Emperor summoned his officials, and a scholarly debate was held on the Five Classics and Three Histories. Around this time, the Retired Emperor Go-Uda (in holy orders) sent Dainagon Fujiwara no Sadafusa to the eastern provinces (Kamakura), and declared his wish to retire to a quiet life, entrusting governance to the reigning Emperor. As the warrior government (bakufu) raised no objection, the Retired Emperor withdrew to Daikakuji.

In the eighth month, the Minister of the Left Saneyasu and the Naidaijin Fuyuji resigned their offices. Fujiwara no Fusazane of the Kujō house was transferred from the position of Ufu (Minister of the Right) to that of the left, and Dainagon Fujiwara no Kanesue of Imadegawa was transferred to Ufu (Minister of the Right). Dainagon Noriyoshi of the Takatsukasa house was appointed Naifū (Naidaijin, Minister of the Center). Kanesue was the youngest son of Saionji Sanekane. Because there were chrysanthemums (kiku) in his garden, he took the name "Kikutei" (Chrysanthemum Garden). It is said that this was later amended and called "Kikutei" (Chrysanthemum Arbor).

In the same month, Shiren of Tōfukuji presented the Genkō Shakusho to the Emperor. Shiren is known by the name Kokan.

In the ninth month, the former Regent and Chancellor (Shōkoku) Saionji Sanekane passed away. He was seventy-four years of age.
