On New Year's Day of the sixth year, the Emperor came of age. He was fifteen years old. Thirteen children of the Fujiwara clan also came of age at the same time. In the second month, there was an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence. The Emperor viewed flowers and enjoyed entertainment. Also at the archery range, the Emperor himself shot his bow and hit the target. He also commanded Ki no Imamori, Governor of Yamashiro, to bring farmers and have them demonstrate plowing fields for the Emperor's observation. This was likely so that the Emperor might understand the hardships of the people.

In the fifth month, Mount Fuji erupted, and the fire did not extinguish for more than ten days. Rocks from the mountaintop collapsed and filled the sea for about thirty ri. Many houses also collapsed. Initially it erupted from the Asama direction, and later the fire spread toward Kai Province.

In the fourth month of the seventh year, Wake no Tsunenori was sent as imperial envoy to Iwashimizu Hachiman shrine to dedicate shields, spears, saddles and other items.
