In the sixth year, on New Year's Day of the first month, the Emperor came of age. He was fifteen years old. Thirteen young boys of the Fujiwara clan came of age at the same time. In the second month, the Emperor made an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence. He viewed the flowers and enjoyed entertainment. Also at the archery ground, the Emperor himself shot his bow and hit the target. He also commanded Yamashiro Governor Ki no Imamori to bring farmers and have them demonstrate plowing fields for the Emperor's observation. This was presumably so that His Majesty might understand the hardships of the people.

In the fifth month, Mount Fuji erupted and the fire did not die out for more than ten days. Rocks on the mountaintop collapsed and filled the sea for about thirty ri. Many houses also collapsed. Initially it erupted from the Asama side, and later the fire spread toward Kai Province.

In the seventh year, fourth month, Wake no Tsunenori was sent as imperial messenger to Iwashimizu Hachiman shrine, where shields, spears, saddles and other items were dedicated. In the eighth month, one hundred koku of rice and one hundred koku of beans were granted to Tsushima Island to have them dig silver mines. Due to continuous rains, the mine shafts (were damaged).
