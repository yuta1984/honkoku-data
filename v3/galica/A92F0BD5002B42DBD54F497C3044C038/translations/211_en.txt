In the first month of the sixth year, on New Year's Day, the Emperor came of age. He was fifteen years old. Thirteen young boys of the Fujiwara clan came of age at the same time. In the second month, there was an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence. The Emperor viewed the flowers and enjoyed entertainment. Also at the archery range, the Emperor himself shot arrows and hit the target. He also commanded Kino Imamori, the Governor of Yamashiro, to bring farmers and demonstrate plowing fields for the Emperor to observe. This was likely so that the Emperor might understand the hardships of the people.

In the fifth month, Mount Fuji erupted and the fire did not extinguish for more than ten days. The massive rocks on the mountain collapsed and filled the sea for about thirty ri. Many houses also collapsed. Initially it erupted from the Asama side, and later the fire spread to the Kai Province side.

In the fourth month of the seventh year, Wake no Tsunenori was sent as imperial messenger to Iwashimizu Hachiman shrine to dedicate shields, spears, saddles and other items.
