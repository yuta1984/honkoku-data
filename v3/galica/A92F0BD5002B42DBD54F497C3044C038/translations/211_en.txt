On New Year's Day of the sixth year, the Emperor came of age. He was fifteen years old. Thirteen children of the Fujiwara clan came of age at the same time. In the second month, the Emperor made an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence. He viewed flowers and enjoyed entertainment. Also at the archery range, the Emperor himself drew his bow and hit the target. He also commanded Yamashiro no Kami Ki no Imamori to bring farmers and have them demonstrate plowing fields for the imperial viewing. This was likely so that His Majesty might understand the hardships of the people.

In the fifth month, Mount Fuji erupted and the fire did not go out for more than ten days. Rocks from the mountaintop collapsed and filled the sea for about thirty ri. Many houses also collapsed. At first it erupted from the direction of Asama, and later the fire spread toward Kai Province.

In the fourth month of the seventh year, Wake no Tsunenori was sent as an imperial envoy to Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine to dedicate shields, spears, saddles, and other items. In the eighth month, one hundred koku of rice and one hundred koku of beans were granted to Tsushima Island to have them dig for silver mines. Due to continuous rains, the mine shafts were (affected).
