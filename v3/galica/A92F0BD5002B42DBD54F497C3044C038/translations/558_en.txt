Nagasaki Takashige was the grandson of Enki. He was an outstanding warrior who fought many times, but finally committed suicide. Takatoki also committed suicide at Tōshōji Temple on the twenty-second day. Hōjō Shigeki, being the incumbent regent, committed suicide in the palace at Kamakura. The Kanazawa Sadaaki nyūdō, the Tokiwa Norisada clan, Jō Enmyō, the retainers of Nagasaki Enki and others all committed suicide. On the same day, Shogun Morukuni shaved his head and became a monk at age thirty-three (he died in the seventh month of the same year). Takatoki's eldest son Kunitoki was captured and killed. His second son Tokiyuki fled to Shinano Province. Eshō fled to Ōshū.

In the same month, armies arose in Kyushu, and Tandai Hideki was also killed by the Shōni and Ōtomo clans. The Tandai of Nagato, Hōjō Tokina, surrendered. In addition, members of the Hōjō clan living in various provinces were either killed or went into hiding and perished.
