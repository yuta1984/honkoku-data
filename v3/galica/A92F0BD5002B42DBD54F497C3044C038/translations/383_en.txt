In the first month of Yōwa 1 (1181), Retired Emperor Takakura passed away at the age of twenty-one. In the second month, Kiyomori petitioned the court and appointed a person called Jōnosuke Chō of Echigo Province as governor of Echigo to have him attack Kiso Yoshinaka. Around this time, not only in the eastern and northern provinces, but also in the western and southern seas, armies arose. In Tsukushi, Ogata no Koreyoshi, and in Shikoku, Kōno Tōmokiyo and his son Tōnobu joined the Genji side and fought against the Heike forces. Yoritomo's uncle Yukiie had already advanced as far as Owari Province. Although Tomomori, Koremori and others from the Heike were supposed to depart for the eastern and northern provinces, some returned claiming illness, while others lingered on the road and did not advance.

On the fourth day of the intercalary second month, Kiyomori died at the age of sixty-four. That night, the mansion at Nishi-Hachijō caught fire. Munemori had already succeeded Kiyomori as the head of the clan and conducted government affairs. He returned the Cloistered Emperor to his original residence at Hōjūji.
