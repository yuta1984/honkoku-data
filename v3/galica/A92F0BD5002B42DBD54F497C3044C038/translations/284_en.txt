In the fifth year, first month, the Emperor suffered from an eye disease and abdicated the throne to Crown Prince Atsunari. He was given the honorary title of Retired Emperor (Jōkō Tennō). His reign lasted five years. The era name was Chōwa.

68th Emperor
Emperor Go-Ichijō　Son of Emperor Ichijō. His personal name was Atsunari. His mother was Empress Consort Fujiwara no Shōshi (known as Jōtōmon-in), daughter of Minister of the Left Dōchō. When Emperor Sanjō ascended the throne, Atsunari became Crown Prince. In the first month of Chōwa 5, he received the abdication and ascended the throne at nine years of age. His maternal grandfather Dōchō became regent, with ceremonies following the precedent of Minister Tadahito. Prince Atsuakira, son of Emperor Sanjō, was made Crown Prince.

In Kannin 1, on the night of the twenty-second day of the first month, bandits entered the Imperial Palace. Fujiwara no Nagasuke, a guard (toneri) of the Takiguchi, and Fujiwara no Yoshitaka, a personal attendant (zuishin) of Dōchō, went out and shot them dead. Both men were rewarded. In the third month, Dōchō became Minister of the Left.
