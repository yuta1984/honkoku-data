Since then, they became warriors, and up until his father Masamori, they lived in the countryside. Because they lived in Ise Province for a long time, they were called the Ise Taira clan. However, Tadamori found favor with both Retired Emperors Shirakawa and Toba, and raised his family's status, so everyone envied him. In the tenth month, there was an imperial visit for the dedication ceremony of Hōshōgon-in. In the same month, the retired emperor made a pilgrimage to Kōya.

In the third year, first month, imperial visits to Kasuga and Hie shrines. Fifth month, imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo shrines.

In the second year of Hōen (1136), third month, there were imperial visits for the dedication ceremony of Toba's imperial hall, Shōkōmyō-in. In the fifth month, Left Minister Ietada died at age seventy-five. He was the second son of former Regent Morozane and the ancestor of the Kazanin family. Ietada's younger brother, Major Counselor Tsunezane, was the ancestor of the Ōimikado family. Both lineages are seishinka noble families. In the twelfth month, Right Minister Minamoto no Arito was transferred to the left (became Left Minister), Inner Minister Munetada was promoted to Right Minister, and Major Counselor Fujiwara no Yorinaga became Inner Minister at the age of seventeen.
