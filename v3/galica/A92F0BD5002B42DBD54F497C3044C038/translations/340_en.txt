Since then, having become warriors, up to his father Masamori they had lived in the countryside. Because they had long resided in Ise Province, they were called the Ise Taira clan. However, because Tadamori pleased both Shirakawa and Toba retired emperors and raised his family's status, everyone envied him. In the tenth month, there was a consecration ceremony at Hōshōgon-in with an imperial visit. In the same month, the retired emperor made a pilgrimage to Kōya.

In the third year, in the first month, there were imperial visits to Kasuga and Hiyoshi. In the fifth month, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo.

In the second year of Hōen (1136), in the third month, there was a consecration ceremony at Toba's imperial hall Shōkōmyō-in, with imperial visits by both the emperor and retired emperor. In the fifth month, Left Minister Ietada died at the age of seventy-five. He was the second son of former Regent Morozane and the ancestor of the Kazanin family. Ietada's younger brother, Major Counselor Tsunezane, was the ancestor of the Ōinomikado family. Both lineages belong to the seiga noble families. In the twelfth month, Right Minister Minamoto no Arito was transferred to the left, Interior Minister Munetada was promoted to right minister, and Major Counselor Fujiwara no Yorinaga became interior minister at the age of seventeen.
