Some were killed in battle, some committed suicide, and some were captured alive and then executed. Mitsuchika, along with Dainagon Fujiwara no Tadanobu, Chūnagon Fujiwara no Arimasa, Fujiwara no Muneyuki and other court officials who were close attendants were captured and sent down to the Kantō region, but were killed along the way. Only Tadanobu was pardoned because he was related to Sanetomo. Yasutoki and Tokifusa resided in the Rokuhara mansion and administered rewards and punishments. This was the beginning of the two Rokuhara offices.

In the seventh month, the new emperor Kanari abdicated and withdrew to Kujō-in. In the same month, with Yasutoki's eldest son Tokiuji serving as magistrate, Retired Emperor Go-Toba was exiled to Oki Province. Retired Emperor Juntoku was exiled to Sado Province. Go-Toba's son Prince Masanari was exiled to Tajima Province, and Prince Yorihito was exiled to Bizen Province. As for Retired Emperor Tsuchimikado, since he had remonstrated against this recent affair, there was discussion that he should be allowed to remain in the capital as is, but he too was exiled to Tosa Province.
