The second son, Samanotō Tokimune, succeeded as head of the family at the age of thirteen and became Regent (Shikken), being appointed Governor of Sagami. Masamura and Nagatoki assisted him in this role. Tokimune's father-in-law, Akita-no-shiro-no-suke Yasumori, also wielded considerable influence.

In the third month of the first year of Bun'ei (1264), Enryakuji Temple was engulfed in flames. This was said to have been set ablaze by the mountain monks themselves, in fury at the appointment of the Tennōji bettō (superintendent) being assigned to Miidera Temple.

In the fifth month, (the forces of) Sanmon (Enryakuji) descended upon Miidera and burned it to the ground entirely. Also in that same year, the monks of Nanto (Kōfukuji in Nara) harbored grievances against the imperial court, causing the sacred sakaki tree to be brought into the capital (nyūraku) and proceeding to lodge a forceful protest (gōso).

In the eighth month, Hōjō Nagatoki died. He was thirty-five years of age.
