In the same month, the Retired Emperor Go-Komatsu made an imperial visit to Shōkoku-ji, and Yoshikazu accompanied him in attendance. Yoshimochi had already relinquished governance to Yoshikazu, with the kanrei (deputy shogun) Hatakeyama Mitsuie serving as his regent and assistant. Yoshimochi was touring various places around the capital at leisure. That year, Kujō Mitsunori resigned as regent (kanpaku), and Nijō no Safu Mochimoto was appointed kanpaku, while Ichijō no Naifu Kaneyoshi was appointed to the Right Minister's position. Tōin Dainagon Mitsusue was appointed to the position of Naifu (Minister of the Center/Middle Counselor).

In the thirty-second year [of Yoshimochi's rule], in the first month, Yoshikazu was conferred the rank of Shō-shii-no-ge (Senior Fourth Rank, Lower Grade). On the sixteenth day of the second month, the second prince of the Retired Emperor passed away. On the twenty-seventh day of the same month, the Sei-i Taishōgun, Sangi, and Chūjō, Minamoto no Yoshikazu, passed away at the age of nineteen. He was posthumously titled Chōtoku-in. His tenure in office was a mere three years. In the fourth month, the close attendants of Yoshikazu had an audience with Yoshimochi. The kanrei Hatakeyama Mitsuie, together with Ise Inaba-nyūdō Shōshin, reported in succession that the Ise no Kami was a descendant of a branch of the Taira clan, and had served as a close attendant to the Muromachi family for generations, administering matters within the shogunal compound. In the same month, a memorial Buddhist service was held at the Sentō (the Retired Emperor's palace) for the thirty-third anniversary of the death of Retired Emperor Go-En'yū.
