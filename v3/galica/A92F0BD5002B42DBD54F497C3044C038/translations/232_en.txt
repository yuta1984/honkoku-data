From Dazaifu, Bun'ya no Yoshitomo, Governor of Chikuzen, went to Tsushima as general and killed over three hundred people, seizing their ships as well as weapons and equipment.

In the 12th month, monks Yakushin and Shōbō jointly took charge of religious affairs. Yakushin resided at Ninnaji Temple, and Shōbō resided at Daigo. Both were renowned monks of the Shingon sect. In the same month, an envoy from Balhae, Hai Bunseki, arrived. There was a reception at the Kōrokan. This was the same person as Hai Tei who had come in the 7th year of Gangyō. This person, upon seeing poetry composed by Minister Kan, said it resembled that of Bai Letian of Tang China.

In the 7th year, 3rd month, there was an imperial visit to Shinsen-en Garden to view the cherry blossoms. Minister Kan and others attended the emperor. In the 8th month, Left Minister Minamoto no Tōru passed away at the age of seventy-three. This person built the Rokujō-Kawarain estate and dug a large pond in the garden.
