[Prince Ninsuke] not only took complete charge of Buddhist monastic affairs, but also participated in court politics. In the seventh month, Hōjō Tsunetoki was appointed as Governor of Musashi Province. In the tenth month, Saionji Nyūdō-Saikoku Kintsune made a pilgrimage to Kumano, accompanied by his second son Major Counselor Saneō, his eldest grandson Major Counselor Kinsuke, and other court nobles including Major Counselor Tamie, along with about thirty courtiers of fifth rank and above, making it almost like an imperial progress.

In the eleventh month, memorial services for the thirteenth anniversary of Emperor Tsuchimikado's death were performed. On the first day of the twelfth month, there was an imperial visit to Iwashimizu [Hachiman Shrine]. Regent Yoshizane, Left General Fujiwara no Tadaie (grandson of Dōka, son of Yoshizane), and Right General Fujiwara no Sanemoto (son of Tokudaiji Kintsugu) attended on horseback, and the procession was conducted with great solemnity. There was also an imperial visit to Kamo [Shrine]. This year, Dōka built Tōfuku-ji Temple and made Enni its founding abbot. This is [the monk known as] Shōichi Kokushi.

In the spring of the second year, many natural disasters occurred in Kamakura, and Shōgun Yoritsune performed various prayers. In the fourth month, Yoritsune's son Yoritsugu came of age at six years old, and both the capping ceremony and hair-cutting [were performed]
