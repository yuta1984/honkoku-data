Since Naomata was Tokimasa's great-great-grandfather, this connection is not without significance. In the ninth month, Chōshige, the younger brother of Jōnosuke Chō, was appointed as governor of Echigo and mobilized troops to attack Kiso Yoshinaka. Yoshinaka fought and achieved a great victory. Chōshige fled. From this time, Yoshinaka's prestige spread throughout the Hokuriku region. His retainers Imai Kanehira, Higuchi Kanemitsu, Tate no Chikahada, and Nei no Yukichika were called the Four Heavenly Kings. Kanehira was particularly outstanding. In the tenth month, Munemori was appointed as Minister of the Interior and granted military authority. At this time, Yorimori was a major counselor. Norimori and Tomomori were middle counselors. Tsunemori was a councilor. Norimori and Tsunemori were also brothers of Kiyomori. Munemori's eldest son Kiyomune held the rank of senior third court rank and served as chamberlain. Shigehira and Koremori both held the rank of junior third court rank and served as middle captains.
