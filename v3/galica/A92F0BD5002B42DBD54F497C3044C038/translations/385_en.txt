Since Naomata was Tokimasa's great-great-grandfather, there was not without connection [between the Minamoto and Taira clans]. In the ninth month, Nagamochi, the younger brother of Jōnosuke Chō, was appointed governor of Echigo and gathered troops to attack Kiso Yoshinaka. Yoshinaka fought and achieved a great victory. Nagamochi fled. From this time, Yoshinaka's power spread throughout the Hokurikudō. His retainers Imai Kanehira, Higuchi Kanemitsu, Tate no Chikachū, and Nei no Yukichika were called the Four Heavenly Kings. Kanehira was particularly outstanding. In the tenth month, Munemori was appointed Minister of the Interior and given military authority. At this time, Yorimori was a Major Counselor. Norimori and Tomomori were Middle Counselors. Tsunemori was a Minor Counselor. Norimori and Tsunemori were also younger brothers of Kiyomori. Munemori's eldest son Kiyomune held the rank of Senior Third Rank and the position of Chamberlain. Shigehira and Koremori were both Junior Third Rank and Middle Captains.
