In the second month of the first year of Kōchō, the daughter of the Saionji Left Minister of State Kinsuke entered the imperial court and became Chūgū (Second Consort/Empress). In the third month, Kinsuke resigned from the position of Left Minister. Saneo became Left Minister. Konoe Motohira became Right Minister — he was the son of Kanetsune. Sanjō Kimitika became Inner Minister (Naidaijin).

In the fourth month, Kanehira resigned as Regent. The former Left Minister of the Nijō house, Yoshizane, again became Regent.

In the sixth month, a man called Risshi Ryōken, son of the late Miura Yoshimura, plotted a rebellion and was captured alive in Kamakura; a search was conducted for his associates.

In the eleventh month, the former Governor of Mutsu, the lay monk Hōjō Tokitoki, passed away at the age of sixty-four. He was known by the name Gokurakuji. He was the third son of Yoshitoki. His descendants took the name Akahashi.

In the twelfth month, the former Left Minister Saionji Kinsuke was appointed Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin).
