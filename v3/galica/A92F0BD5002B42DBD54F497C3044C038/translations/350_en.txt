Emperor Konoe's early death was suspected by Bifukumon-in to be the result of a curse by the retired emperor (Sutoku), and she harbored suspicion and resentment. She appealed to the retired emperor (Toba) and prevented Prince Shigehito from being enthroned. Because of this, the retired emperor (Sutoku) became increasingly displeased.

After the emperor had already ascended the throne, Takamatsu Palace was made the imperial residence. The first prince, Prince Morihito, was made crown prince. Princess Shōshi, the daughter of the retired emperor, was made the foster mother of the crown prince, and although she was not elevated to empress, she was titled Hachijō-no-Nyōin. Her younger sister, Takamatsu-in, was made the crown prince's consort. These two imperial princesses were both born from Bifukumon-in's womb. In the winter of the same year, the retired emperor made a pilgrimage to Kumano.

On the second day of the seventh month of the first year of Hōgen, Retired Emperor Toba passed away at the age of fifty-four. After abdicating the throne, he had governed from the imperial court for thirty-four years. From the beginning of the emperor's reign, Tadamichi continued as before to serve as regent. Although Yorinaga remained head of the Fujiwara clan as before, he was dismissed from his position as nairan. Because of this, did he harbor resentment toward the current emperor? Or perhaps he thought to rule the realm by himself? Increasingly, he allied with Retired Emperor Sutoku...
