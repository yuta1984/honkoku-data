In the third month of the third year (of Kōkoku), Shionoya (Enya) Hangan Takasada of Izumo was executed due to slanderous accusations by Kō no Moronao. In the fourth month, Wakiya Yoshisuke, having received an imperial edict from the Southern Emperor, proceeded to the southern seas to attempt the pacification of Shikoku. In the fifth month, Yoshisuke died of illness in Iyo Province. Hosokawa Yoriharu, a general under Takauji, raised an army, defeated those who had followed Yoshisuke, and Shikoku was pacified. In the seventh month, Minamoto no Tomochika resigned from the position of Naidaijin. Nijō Dainagon Yoshimoto was appointed Naifu (Naidaijin). In the twelfth month, the former Udaijin Koga, Minamoto no Nagatsu, was appointed Daijō-daijin (Grand Minister of State). This year, at the urging of the Zen monk Soseki, Takauji and Tadayoshi founded Tenryū-ji temple. Soseki was established as its founding abbot. He is the National Teacher Musō.

In the first month of the fourth year (of Kōkoku), the lay-monk former Regent Nijō Morotada passed away. He was eighty-nine years of age. He was the great-grandfather of Yoshimoto.
