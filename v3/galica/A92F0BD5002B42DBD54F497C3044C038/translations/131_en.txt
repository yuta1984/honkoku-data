Under fierce attack, Fujiwara no Hirotsugu was defeated in battle and attempted to flee by ship to a foreign country, but was captured alive by Abe no Kuromaro, one of the government forces, in Nagano Village, Matsuura District, Hizen Province, who immediately beheaded him. Tsunate was also killed. According to one account, Hirotsugu rode a swift horse and leaped into the sea, and because his spirit caused curses, a shrine was built in Matsuura where he is worshipped as a deity. Hirotsugu was the son of Fujiwara no Umakai.

During Hirotsugu's rebellion, the Emperor made an imperial visit to Ise Province. He made offerings to the Grand Shrine of Ise, passed through Mino and Iga provinces, moved the capital to Sagara District in Yamashiro Province, and built a new palace. This was called Kuni Palace. The crimes of Hirotsugu's associates were determined, and ranks were bestowed upon Ōno no Azumahito, Sakanoue no Iimaro, Abe no Tsunehito Mushimaro, and others.

In the fourteenth year, there was an imperial visit to Shigaraki Palace in Kōka District, Ōmi Province. From there, Right Minister Tachibana no Moroe was dispatched to the Grand Shrine of Ise.
