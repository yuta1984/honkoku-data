In the twenty-second year, there was a drought.

In the first year of Enchō (延長), third month, Crown Prince Yasuakira passed away. He was posthumously named Prince Fumihiko. As it was said to be caused by the vengeful spirit of Minister-Chancellor Sugawara no Michizane, his offices and ranks were posthumously restored.

In the second year, first month, Tadahira was transferred to the position of Minister of the Left, and Major Counselor Fujiwara no Sadakata was appointed Minister of the Right. Sadakata was the younger brother of Sadakuni. In the same month, a celebration was held for the Emperor's fortieth birthday.

In the third year, sixth month, the Emperor contracted smallpox.

In the fourth year, twelfth month, a celebration was held in honor of the Retired Emperor's sixtieth birthday.

In the fifth year, eleventh month, Minister of the Left Tadahira compiled and presented the fifty volumes of the Engishiki. The codes and regulations (kyakushiki) had first been compiled during the reign of Emperor Saga, revised during the reign of Emperor Seiwa, and were completed in full during this reign. The Fudoki (records of local culture and geography) of the sixty-six provinces, though they had begun to be compiled since the reign of Empress Genmei, were corrected through successive reigns and brought to completion during this reign.
