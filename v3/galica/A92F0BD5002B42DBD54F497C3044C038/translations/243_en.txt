In the twenty-second year, there was a drought.

In the first year of Enchō, third month, Crown Prince Yasuakira died. He was posthumously named Prince Bunken. Because it was said to be caused by the vengeful spirit of Minister Kan (Sugawara no Michizane), his court rank was restored.

In the second year, first month, Tadahira was transferred to Minister of the Left, and Major Counselor Fujiwara no Sadakata was made Minister of the Right. Sadakata was the younger brother of Sadakuni. In the same month, the Emperor's fortieth birthday celebration was held.

In the third year, sixth month, the Emperor contracted smallpox.

In the fourth year, twelfth month, the Retired Emperor's sixtieth birthday was celebrated.

In the fifth year, eleventh month, Minister of the Left Tadahira compiled and presented the Engi-shiki in fifty volumes. The legal codes (kyaku-shiki) were first compiled during Emperor Saga's reign, underwent revisions during Emperor Seiwa's time, and were completed in this reign. The provincial gazettes (fudoki) of the sixty-six provinces had been compiled since Emperor Genmei's time, but they were revised through successive generations and finally completed in this reign.
