[The retired emperor] was angered that [the Hōjō] held power and caused the imperial authority to decline. After abdicating the throne, when not occupied with waka poetry and music, he focused on practicing martial arts and established the Western Guards (Saimen no samurai) in addition to the existing Northern Guards (Hokumen no bushi) in the imperial court, gathering warriors around him. After Sanetomo's death, when Yoshitoki acted as his retainer yet controlled the realm as he pleased, the retired emperor became furious. When a warrior from Shinano Province named Nishina Moretō was summoned to serve as one of the Western Guards, Yoshitoki confiscated his territory. The retired emperor granted the Kurahashi estate in Settsu Province to the shirabyōshi dancer Kamegiku, but when the estate's steward (jitō) treated Kamegiku with contempt, he ordered Yoshitoki to dismiss the steward. Yoshitoki did not comply. The retired emperor became increasingly enraged and sent the Northern Guard Fujiwara no Hideyasu to the residence of the warrior Miura Taneyoshi, who was in Kyoto at the time, to discuss the pursuit and punishment of Yoshitoki.
