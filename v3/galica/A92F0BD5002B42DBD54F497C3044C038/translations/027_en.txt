The imperial army pressed forward even more, saying that the consort and prince should be released, but Saho-hiko would not agree. Yatsunata set fire to attack and capture the fortress. The prince was taken and saved. Saho-hiko perished together with the consort. This prince, even after reaching adulthood and until the age of thirty, was unable to speak. One day, seeing a swan crying as it flew, he said "What is this thing?" and spoke for the first time.

In this reign, there was a man of great strength called Taema-no-Kuehaya in Yamato province. There was also a warrior called Nomi-no-Sukune in Izumo province. These two men were summoned to compete in strength. Nomi was superior in strength, broke Kuehaya's ribs, and trampled his back to kill him. This was the beginning of sumo wrestling in Japan. Nomi was given Kuehaya's territory and was kept in the capital to serve at court. This man was put in charge of making human figures and various other vessels from clay.
