In the second month of the third year of Kyūan, Minister of the Left Minamoto no Arihito passed away at the age of forty-five. He was called the Hanazono Minister. In the sixth month, the Retired Emperor and the New Retired Emperor made an imperial visit to Mount Hiei. In the eighth month, they held a dedication ceremony for an Amida statue at Toba Palace, with an imperial visit.

In the sixth month of the fourth year of Kyūan, the Imperial Palace burned down. In the seventh month, Regent Tadamichi built Hōshō-ji temple and held its dedication ceremony. The Retired Emperor made an imperial visit. In the eighth month, there were imperial visits to Hirano and Ōharano.

In the third month of the fifth year of Kyūan, there were imperial visits for the dedication of Enshō-ji temple. In the seventh month, Minister of the Interior Yorinaga was appointed Minister of the Left. Major Counselor Sanesuke became a minister. Minamoto no Masasada became Minister of the Interior. In the tenth month, there were imperial visits to Matsuo, Kitano, Inari, and Gion. In the same month, Regent Tadamichi was appointed Chancellor. This was the first time someone was reappointed after resigning from the position of Chancellor.

In the first month of the sixth year of Kyūan, the Emperor came of age. In the third month, Tadamichi resigned from the position of Chancellor. In the same month, Tashi, daughter of Tokudaiji Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Kinnō, [was married to] Minister of the Left Yorinaga
