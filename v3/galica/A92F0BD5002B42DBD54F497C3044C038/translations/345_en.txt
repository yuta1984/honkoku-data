In the third year, second month, Left Minister Minamoto no Arihito died at the age of forty-five. He was called the Hanazono Minister. In the sixth month, the retired emperor and the new retired emperor made an imperial visit to Mount Hiei. In the eighth month, the retired emperor held a dedication ceremony for an Amida Buddha statue at Toba Palace. There was an imperial visit.

In the fourth year, sixth month, the Imperial Palace burned down. In the seventh month, Regent Tadamichi built Hōshō-ji temple and held its dedication ceremony. The retired emperor made an imperial visit. In the eighth month, there were imperial visits to Hirano and Ōharano.

In the fifth year, third month, there was the dedication ceremony of Enshō-ji temple, with imperial visits by both the emperor and retired emperor. In the seventh month, Interior Minister Yorinaga was appointed Left Minister. Major Counselor Saneyuki became Right Minister. Minamoto no Masasada became Interior Minister. In the tenth month, there were imperial visits to Matsunoo, Kitano, Inari, and Gion. In the same month, Regent Tadamichi was appointed Chancellor. This was the first time someone was reappointed to the position of Chancellor after having resigned from it.

In the sixth year, first month, the emperor came of age. In the third month, Tadamichi resigned from the position of Chancellor.
