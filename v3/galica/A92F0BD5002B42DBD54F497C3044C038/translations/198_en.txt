Because he was a retainer, he shaved his hair to become a monk and changed his name to Henjō. He who was later called the Monk Supervisor of Kazurayama was this person. The era names during the emperor's reign were Jōwa for fourteen years and Kajō for three years, totaling seventeen years.

The 55th Emperor
Emperor Montoku was the son of Emperor Ninmyō. His personal name was Michiyasu. His mother was Empress Junshi, daughter of Left Minister Fujiwara no Fuyutsugu. What the world calls Gojō Empress refers to Junshi. The emperor became crown prince in the ninth year of Jōwa under Emperor Ninmyō.

In the third year of Kajō, Emperor Ninmyō passed away. In the fourth month, the emperor ascended the throne. In the fifth month, the Empress Dowager of Emperor Saga passed away. Since she was Emperor Ninmyō's mother, she was the emperor's grandmother. This empress dowager loved Buddhism and built Danrin-ji temple, so she was called Empress Danrin. She sent a monk named Egaku to Tang China to inquire about Zen teachings. She also loved learning and built the Gakkwan-in academy, having members of the Tachibana clan engage in reading and writing.
