## Nihon Ōdai Ichiran, Volume 3

### The 56th Sovereign
**Emperor Seiwa** — He was the Crown Prince of Emperor Montoku. His personal name (*imina*) was Korehito. His mother was the Consort of Somedono, Fujiwara no Akiko, daughter of the Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin) Fujiwara no Yoshifusa. He was designated Crown Prince only nine months after his birth. In the eighth month of Tennan 2 (858), Emperor Montoku passed away. In the eleventh month of the same year, the Crown Prince ascended to the throne at the age of nine. His maternal grandfather Yoshifusa assumed the role of regent (*sesshō*). This was the beginning of the Fujiwara clan's regency. Emperor Seiwa was the first in Japan to ascend to the imperial throne at such a young age. Notice of the enthronement was conveyed to the Grand Shrine of Ise and to various imperial mausolea. The Senior First Rank (*Shō-ichii*) was posthumously conferred upon his maternal grandmother, Minamoto no Kiyohime. Kiyohime was the daughter of Emperor Saga, the wife of Yoshifusa, and the mother of the Consort of Somedono. In this same year, Chishō Daishi (the monk Enchin) returned from Tang China.

In the first month of Jōgan 1 (859), the New Year's banquets and ceremonies were all cancelled, as the court was still in the period of imperial mourning (*ryōan*). In the second month, the Senior First Rank was conferred upon the Miwa Myōjin and other deities of Yamato Province.
