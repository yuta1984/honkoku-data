Chronicles of Japanese Sovereigns, Volume 3

The 56th Emperor
Emperor Seiwa was the crown prince of Emperor Montoku. His imperial name was Korehito. His mother was Empress Somenodono, Fujiwara no Akiko, daughter of the Chancellor Yoshifusa. He was made crown prince nine months after his birth. In the eighth month of Tennan 2 (859), Emperor Montoku passed away. In the eleventh month, the crown prince ascended to the throne at the age of nine. His maternal grandfather Yoshifusa became regent. This was the beginning of the Fujiwara regency. This emperor was the first to ascend to the imperial throne at such a young age in Japan. Reports of the enthronement were made to Ise Grand Shrine and various imperial mausoleums. The rank of Senior First Court Rank was posthumously granted to his maternal grandmother, Minamoto no Ketsuki (Kiyohime). She was the daughter of Emperor Saga, wife of Yoshifusa, and mother of Empress Somenodono. In this year, Master Chishō returned from Tang China.

In the first month of Jōgan 1 (859), New Year ceremonies and festivals were all cancelled because it was during the period of mourning. In the second month, Senior First Court Rank was granted to Miwa Myōjin of Yamato and other deities.
