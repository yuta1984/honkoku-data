Chronicles of Japanese Imperial Reigns, Volume 3

The 56th Emperor
Emperor Seiwa was the crown prince of Emperor Montoku. His personal name was Korehito. His mother was Empress Someden-no-Kisaki, Fujiwara no Akiko, daughter of Chancellor Yoshifusa. He was made crown prince at nine months of age. In the eighth month of Ten'an 2 (859), Emperor Montoku passed away. In the eleventh month, the crown prince ascended to the throne at age nine. His maternal grandfather Yoshifusa served as regent. This was the beginning of the Fujiwara regency. In Japan, ascending to the imperial throne at a young age began with this emperor. The accession was announced to Ise Grand Shrine and various imperial mausolea. Positive First Rank was posthumously granted to his maternal grandmother, Minamoto no Ketsuki. She was the daughter of Emperor Saga, wife of Yoshifusa, and mother of Empress Someden. This year, Chishō Daishi returned from Tang China.
In the first month of Jōgan 1 (859), New Year ceremonies and banquets were all cancelled as it was during the period of imperial mourning. In the second month, Positive First Rank was granted to Miwa Myōjin of Yamato and other deities.
