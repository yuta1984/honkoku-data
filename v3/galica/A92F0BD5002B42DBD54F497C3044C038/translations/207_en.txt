Chronicle of Japanese Sovereigns, Volume Three

The Fifty-sixth Emperor
Emperor Seiwa: He was the crown prince of Emperor Montoku. His personal name was Korehito. His mother was Empress Somenodokoro, Fujiwara no Akiko, daughter of Chancellor Yoshifusa. He was made crown prince at nine months of age. In the eighth month of Ten'an 2 (859), Emperor Montoku passed away. In the eleventh month, the crown prince ascended to the throne at age nine. His maternal grandfather Yoshifusa became regent. This was the beginning of Fujiwara regency. In Japan, ascending to the imperial throne at a young age began with this emperor. The Ise Grand Shrine and various imperial mausoleums were notified of the enthronement. His maternal grandmother, Minamoto no Kiyohime, was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank. She was the daughter of Emperor Saga, wife of Yoshifusa, and mother of Empress Somenodokoro. In this year, Master Chishō returned from Tang China.

In the first month of Jōgan 1 (859), the New Year's court ceremonies were all suspended because it was during the mourning period. In the second month, Senior First Rank was conferred upon the Miwa deity of Yamato and others.
