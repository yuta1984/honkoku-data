Chronicle of Japanese Imperial Reigns, Volume 3

The 56th Emperor
Emperor Seiwa was the crown prince of Emperor Montoku. His personal name was Korехito. His mother was Empress Somedonо, Fujiwara no Akiko, daughter of Grand Minister Yoshifusa. He was made crown prince at nine months of age. In the eighth month of Ten'an 2 (859), Emperor Montoku died. In the eleventh month, the crown prince ascended to the throne at age nine. His maternal grandfather Yoshifusa became regent. This was the beginning of Fujiwara regency. Emperor Seiwa was the first emperor in Japan to ascend to the throne at such a young age. The enthronement was announced to Ise Grand Shrine and various imperial mausoleums. The first court rank was posthumously granted to his maternal grandmother, Minamoto no Ketsuki (Kiyohime). She was the daughter of Emperor Saga, wife of Yoshifusa, and mother of Empress Somedonо. In this year, Chishō Daishi returned from Tang China.

In the first month of Jōgan 1 (859), New Year ceremonies and banquets were all suspended because it was still within the mourning period. In the second month, the first court rank was granted to Miwa Myōjin of Yamato and other deities.
