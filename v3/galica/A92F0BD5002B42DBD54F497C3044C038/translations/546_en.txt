In the third month of the third year [of Genkō, 1323], Ichijō Utsunori resigned as Kampaku (Imperial Regent). Kujō Fusazane became Kampaku.

In the fifth month, Minamoto no Michio resigned as Daijō-daijin (Grand Minister of State). The former Kampaku Takatsukasa Fuyuhira was reappointed as Minister.

In the first month of the first year of Shōchū [1324], the former Naidaijin (Minister of the Center) Sanjō Kimimochi passed away. He was forty-one years of age.

In the third month, there was an imperial visit (gyōkō) to Iwashimizu [Hachimangū Shrine]. In the fourth month, there was an imperial visit to Kamo [Shrine]. In the same month, Naidaijin Takatsukasa Noriyoshi was appointed Minister of the Left. Dainagon Konoe Tsunetada was appointed Minister of the Right. Dainagon Saionji Sanehira was appointed Naidaijin (Minister of the Center).

In the fifth month, the former Kampaku Konoe Iehira passed away.

In the sixth month, the Retired Emperor Go-Uda (in holy orders) passed away. He was fifty-eight years of age.

In the eighth month, Hōjō Koresada returned from Rokuhara to Kamakura.

In the ninth month, Toki Yorisada and Tajimi Kuninage and others, having received a secret imperial decree from the Emperor with the aim of destroying [the Kamakura shogunate], were exposed in their conspiracy. Norisada of Rokuhara dispatched troops and killed both Yorisada and Kuninage.
