In the eighteenth year, on the tenth day of the fourth month, the Daigokuden (Great Audience Hall) and many other palace gates and buildings burned down. Due to suspicions of arson, brave warriors were ordered to patrol and inspect the capital. In the fifth month, imperial messengers were dispatched to Ise Shrine as well as Kamo and Matsuo shrines to report the matter of the Daigokuden fire. In the seventh month, the Daigokuden was reconstructed.

In the eleventh month, the Emperor abdicated the throne to his first son, Prince Sadaakira. Having Right Minister Mototsune serve as regent followed the precedent of Chūjin-kō (Fujiwara no Yoshifusa). In the twelfth month, the honorary title of Daijō-tennō (Retired Emperor) was bestowed upon Kiyowa. Because he later entered Mount Mio, he is also called the Mio Emperor. The era name was Jōgan, and his reign lasted eighteen years.
