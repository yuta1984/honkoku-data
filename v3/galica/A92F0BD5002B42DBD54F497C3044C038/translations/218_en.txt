In the eighteenth year, on the tenth day of the fourth month, the Daigokuden (Great Audience Hall) and many other palace buildings and gates burned down. Due to suspicions of arson, warriors were ordered to patrol and inspect the capital. In the fifth month, imperial messengers were sent to Ise Shrine as well as Kamo and Matsuo shrines to report the matter of the Daigokuden's destruction by fire. In the seventh month, the Daigokuden was rebuilt.

In the eleventh month, the Emperor abdicated the throne to his first son, Prince Sadaakira. Having Right Minister Mototsune serve as regent followed the precedent of Chūjin-kō (Fujiwara no Yoshifusa). In the twelfth month, Emperor Seiwa was given the honorary title of Daijō Tennō (Retired Emperor). Because he later entered Mount Mio, he is also called the Mio Emperor. The era name was Jōgan, and his reign lasted eighteen years.

Fifty-seventh Emperor
Emperor Yōzei was the crown prince of Emperor Seiwa. His personal name was Sadaakira. His mother was Empress Dowager Fujiwara no Takako, daughter of the late Middle Counselor Nagara and sister of Right Minister (Mototsune).
