In the eighteenth year, on the tenth day of the fourth month, the Daigokuden (Great Audience Hall) and many other palace halls and gates burned down. Due to suspicion of arson, brave warriors were ordered to patrol the capital. In the fifth month, imperial messengers were dispatched to Ise Shrine as well as Kamo and Matsuo shrines to report the burning of the Daigokuden. In the seventh month, the Daigokuden was rebuilt.

In the eleventh month, the Emperor abdicated the throne to his first son, Prince Sadaakira. Having Right Minister Mototsune serve as regent followed the precedent set by Chūjin-kō (Fujiwara no Yoshifusa). In the twelfth month, the honorific title of Daijō Tennō (Retired Emperor) was bestowed upon Seiwa. Because he later entered Mio-yama mountain, he is also called Mio-tei (Emperor Mio). The era name was Jōgan, and he reigned for eighteen years.
