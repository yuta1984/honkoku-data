In the fourth month of the eighteenth year, on the tenth day, the Daigokuden (Great Audience Hall) and many other palace buildings and gates burned down. Due to suspicions of arson, brave warriors were ordered to patrol and inspect the capital. In the fifth month, imperial messengers were dispatched to Ise Shrine as well as Kamo and Matsunoo Shrines to report the matter of the Daigokuden fire. In the seventh month, the Daigokuden was rebuilt.

In the eleventh month, the Emperor abdicated the throne to his first prince, Prince Sadaakira. Having Right Minister Mototsune serve as regent followed the precedent of Chūjin-kō [Fujiwara no Yoshifusa]. In the twelfth month, Emperor Seiwa was given the honorary title of Daijō Tennō (Retired Emperor). Because he later entered Mount Mizunoo, he is also called the Mizunoo Emperor. The era name was Jōgan, and his reign lasted eighteen years.
