In the eighteenth year, on the tenth day of the fourth month, the Daigoku-den Hall and many other palace gates burned down. As arson was suspected, soldiers were ordered to patrol the capital. In the fifth month, imperial envoys were dispatched to the Ise Shrine and the Kamo and Matsunoo Shrines to report the destruction of the Daigoku-den Hall. In the seventh month, the Daigoku-den Hall was reconstructed. In the eleventh month, the Emperor abdicated in favor of his first imperial prince, Prince Sadaakira. Right Minister Mototsune was made regent, following the precedent set by Chūjin-kō (Yoshifusa). In the twelfth month, the honorific title of Daijō Tennō (Retired Emperor) was bestowed upon Emperor Seiwa. As he later entered Mount Minoo, he is also referred to as the Emperor of Minoo. The era name was Jōgan; his reign lasted eighteen years.
