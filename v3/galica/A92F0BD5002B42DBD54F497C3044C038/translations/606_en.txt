In the same month, Sasaki Dōyo passed away. In the ninth month, the two emissary monks from Ming China returned to their country.

In the tenth month, it was decreed that the appointment of head abbots (*jūjishoku*) of the Five Zen Temples of Kamakura should be decided by Yoshimitsu, while other temple regulations should be handled by the Kamakura *kanryō* (deputy), Ujimitsu.

In the eleventh month, Yoshimitsu was granted the Junior Fourth Rank, Lower Grade (*jushii no ge*), appointed as a Counselor (*sangi*), concurrently assumed the post of Middle Captain of the Left (*sachu-jō*), and the post of Head of the Left Stables (*samanokamì*) was conferred upon Ujimitsu of Kamakura.

In the twelfth month, there was a council deliberating Yoshimitsu's military expedition to Kyushu. Consulting with Yoriyuki, they summoned Uesugi Danjō Tomofusa of Kamakura to serve as guardian of the capital, had Nikiyo-shichō suppress the Kitabatake of Ise, had Yamana Ujikiyo suppress the Wada and Kusunoki clans of the southern faction, and had Takeda and Ogasawara suppress Kanaya and others of Iyo Province. Furthermore, they mustered military forces from the provinces, bounded by Izu to the east and Echigo to the north.
