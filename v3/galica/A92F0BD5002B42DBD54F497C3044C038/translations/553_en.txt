Through the arrangements of the military government, they consulted with Saionji Dainagon Kinmune and enthroned Kazuhito (as Emperor Kōgon). They made Yasuhito, son of Kuniyoshi who was the grandson of Emperor Go-Nijō, the Crown Prince.

In the first year of Shōkyō, third month, Tokiwa Norisada resigned from his position at Rokuhara. Hōjō Echigo no Kami Nakatoki and Hōjō Sakon Shōgen Tokimasu were appointed to Rokuhara and went up to the capital. Nakatoki was at the northern headquarters and Tokimasu at the southern headquarters. Norisada returned to Kamakura. In the same month, Takatoki's messenger Nagai Takafuyu went up to the capital, and through consultation between both Rokuhara headquarters, the former emperor Go-Daigo was exiled to Oki Province. First Prince Takayoshi was exiled to Tosa. Myōhō-in Prince Son'chō was exiled to Sanuki. Son'un hid here and there, returned to secular life, changed his name to Morinaga, and shut himself up in Yoshino Castle. In the fourth month, Kusunoki Masashige again captured Akasaka Castle.
