By the arrangement of the warrior houses, Saionji Dainagon Kimmune was consulted, and Kazuhito ascended the throne. Yasuhito, the son of Kuniyoshi — grandson of the Gonijō-in — was made Crown Prince.

In the third month of Shōkyō 1 (1332), Tokiwa Norisada resigned his post at Rokuhara. Hōjō Nakatoki, Governor of Echigo, and Hōjō Tokimasu, Sakon no Shōgen, were appointed to Rokuhara and went up to the capital. Nakatoki took charge of the Northern Rokuhara, while Tokimasu took charge of the Southern Rokuhara. Norisada returned to Kamakura. In the same month, Takatoki's envoy Nagai Takafuyu went up to the capital, and after consultation between the two Rokuhara offices, the retired Emperor Godaigo was exiled to the province of Oki. The First Prince, Imperial Prince Takayoshi, was exiled to Tosa. Myōhōin Imperial Prince Sonchō was exiled to Sanuki. Sonun (Prince Morinaga) hid here and there, returned to lay life, changed his name to Moriyoshi, and shut himself up in the fortress at Yoshino.

In the fourth month, Kusunoki Masashige again captured the fortress at Akasaka. In the fifth month, the close retainers of the retired Emperor were either killed or exiled.
