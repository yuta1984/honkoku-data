Through the arrangements of the military government, they consulted with Dainagon Saionji Kinmune, and Kazuhito ascended the throne. They made Yasuhito, son of Kuniyoshi and grandson of Retired Emperor Gonijō, the Crown Prince.

In the first year of Shōkyō, third month, Tokiha Norisada resigned from his position at Rokuhara. Hōjō Echigo-no-kami Nakatoki and Hōjō Sakon-shōgen Tokimasu were appointed to Rokuhara and went up to the capital. Nakatoki was at the northern headquarters, and Tokimasu was at the southern headquarters.
Norisada returned to Kamakura. In the same month, Takatoki's messenger Nagai Takafuyu went up to the capital, and through consultation between the two Rokuhara offices, the former Emperor Go-Daigo was exiled to Oki Province. Prince Takayoshi was exiled to Tosa. Myōhōin Prince Son'chō was exiled to Sanuki. Son'un hid here and there, returned to secular life, changed his name to Moriyoshi, and shut himself up in Yoshino Castle. In the fourth month, Kusunoki Masashige again captured Akasaka Castle.
