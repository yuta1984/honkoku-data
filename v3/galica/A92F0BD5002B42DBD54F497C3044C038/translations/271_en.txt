He became Crown Prince under Emperor En'yū, and on the twenty-seventh day of the eighth month of the second year of Eikan, he received abdication and ascended the throne. At that time he was seventeen years old. Yorichika continued to serve as regent as before. At this time, both Emperor Reizei and Emperor En'yū were still alive and were both called Retired Emperor.

In the first year of Kanna, fourth month, Fujiwara no Tokiaki and his younger brother Yasusuke, as leaders of a criminal gang, wounded Fujiwara no Suetaka and Ōe no Masahira with blades, cutting off a finger of Masahira's left hand. Tokiaki and Yasusuke fled with their whereabouts unknown. Orders for their pursuit were sent to all provinces, and Tokiaki was executed in Takashima District of Ōmi Province.

When the emperor ascended the throne, he summoned three women to become imperial consorts: the daughter of Regent Yorichika, the daughter of Prince Tamehira, and the daughter of Major Counselor Fujiwara no Tomomitsu. He also summoned Tsuneko, daughter of Major Counselor Fujiwara no Tamemitsu, placed her in Kōki Palace, and made her an imperial consort. She was extremely favored.
