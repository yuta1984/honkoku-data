In the sixth month, Silla pirates came to Hakata and disrupted tribute ships from Buzen Province. The Dazaifu dispatched troops to capture them, but the pirate ships quickly fled and escaped.

In the first month of the twelfth year, Fujiwara no Ujimune became Right Minister. Minamoto no Tōru and Fujiwara no Mototsune became Major Counselors. Tōru was a son of Emperor Saga.

In the second month of the thirteenth year, the Emperor went to the Shishin-den and for the first time personally attended to governmental affairs. Before Emperor Ninmyō, the sovereign would go to the Shishin-den every day. From the reign of Emperor Montoku onward, this ceremony had ceased. Now that the old protocol was restored, everyone rejoiced. In the fourth month, Yoshifusa was granted additional stipends and awarded personal guards with weapons, and was accorded the status equivalent to the Three Princely Houses. The rank of Jun-sankyū (quasi-three princely houses) began from this time.

In the eighth month, Right Minister Ujimune received orders to compile and present the Jōgan-shiki legal code.

In the ninth month, Empress Gojō, Fujiwara no Junshi, passed away. She was the empress of Emperor Ninmyō and mother of Emperor Montoku. In the twelfth month, envoys from the kingdom of Balhae, Yang Chenggui and others, arrived at Kaga...
