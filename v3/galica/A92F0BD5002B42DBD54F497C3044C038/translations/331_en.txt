Yoshi was the legitimate grandson and, having already become an adopted son, succeeded to the house.

In the second year, first month, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo shrines.

In the first year of Ten'ei, fifth month, there was a memorial service for the golden-lettered Tripiṭaka at Hōshōji temple. The emperor also made an imperial visit.

In the second year, first month, there was an imperial visit for audience with the retired emperor. In the seventh month, Ōe no Masafusa died at the age of seventy-one.

In the third year, twelfth month, Regent and Right Minister Tadazane was appointed as Chancellor.

In the first year of Eikyū, on New Year's Day, the emperor came of age at eleven years old. In the fourth month, Tadazane ceased being regent and became kampaku. In the eighth month, there were imperial visits to Matsuo and Kitano.

In the tenth month, there was an imperial visit to Hiyoshi. In the eleventh month, there were imperial visits to Inari and Gion.

This year, there was a dispute between Mount Hiei and Kōfukuji temple. Kōfukuji resented the court, and the monks rose up in revolt, putting the sacred tree of Kasuga at the front, with several thousand people
