The records he compiled are called the Nishinomiya-ki. Chiharu was the son of Hidesato. According to one theory, Takaaki had no treasonous intentions, and it was Mitsunaka's slander that Saneyori took as truth and acted upon. In the eighth month, due to the emperor's illness, he abdicated the throne to his younger brother Morahira and moved to Reizei-in, taking the title of Retired Emperor. From this time forward, all emperors have had imperial retreat names. The era name was Anna, and his reign lasted two years.

The Sixty-fourth Emperor
Emperor En'yū　He was the fifth imperial son of Emperor Murakami. His personal name was Morahira, and his mother was the same as Emperor Reizei's. He ascended the throne in the ninth month of Anna 2 at the age of eleven. Saneyori served as regent. He was granted permission for personal guards, weapons and guards, and ox-drawn carriages, and received an imperial edict for nairan (internal oversight). In the tenth month, Minister of the Left Morotada passed away. In the twelfth month, they celebrated Saneyori's seventieth birthday.
