The co-conspirators were arrested throughout the various provinces. Mitsunaka and Yoshitoki were rewarded for their service. Takaakira was a person widely versed in the old records of Japan as well as in precedents and court customs. The collection of records he compiled is known as the *Saigūki* (西宮記, "Records of the Western Palace"). Chiharu was a son of Hidesato. According to one account, Takaakira had no treasonous intent, and it was a slanderous accusation by Mitsunaka that Saneyori had taken as truth and acted upon accordingly.

In the eighth month, the Emperor, due to illness, abdicated in favor of his younger brother Morehira, retired to Reizei-in, and took the title of Daijō Tennō (Retired Emperor). From this point onward, all retired emperors carried an "in" (院) title. The reign era was Anna; the reign lasted two years.

　　The Sixty-Fourth Sovereign

Emperor En'yū: The fifth imperial prince of Emperor Murakami. His personal name was Morehira; his mother was the same as that of Emperor Reizei. He ascended to the throne in the ninth month of the second year of Anna, at the age of eleven. Saneyori served as regent (sesshō), and was granted permission to be accompanied by imperial guards (zuijin), armed escorts (hyōjō), and an ox-cart, and received an imperial decree granting him the privilege of nairan (prior inspection of official documents). In the tenth month, the Minister of the Left Morotada passed away. In the twelfth month, a celebration was held for Saneyori's seventieth birthday.
