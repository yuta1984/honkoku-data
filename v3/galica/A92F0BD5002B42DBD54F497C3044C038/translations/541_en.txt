Chronicle of Japanese Imperial Reigns, Volume Six

The Ninety-Fifth Emperor
Emperor Go-Daigo: His personal name was Takaharu, and he was the second son of Emperor Go-Uda. His mother was Dantenmon-in Fujiwara no Tadako, daughter of Hanayama-in Minister of the Interior Morotsugu, but actually the daughter of Counselor Tadatsugu. The Emperor was initially appointed as Dazaifu no Sochi (Governor-General of Kyushu), so he was called Sochi no Miya (Prince Sochi). When Emperor Hanazono ascended the throne, he was made Crown Prince through the arrangement of the military government.

In the second year of Bunpo (1318), he ascended the throne in the second month at age thirty-one. Nijo Michihira became regent. Retired Emperor Go-Uda administered governmental affairs. The Kamakura shogun was Prince Morikuni. The regent was Hojo Sagami no Kami Takatoki. In the third month, Kuniyoshi, son of Emperor Go-Nijo, was made Crown Prince.
