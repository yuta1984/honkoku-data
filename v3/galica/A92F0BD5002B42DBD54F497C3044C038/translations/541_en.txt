## Nihon Ōdai Ichiran, Volume Six

### The 95th Sovereign
**Emperor Go-Daigo**　His personal name (imina) was Takaharu. He was the second son of Emperor Go-Uda. His mother was Tatsu-Tenmon-in, Fujiwara no Tadako, daughter of Morotsugu, Minister of the Interior of the Kazanin family — though in fact she was the daughter of Counselor (sangi) Tadatsugu. As the Emperor was initially appointed to the post of Dazai no Sochi (Governor-General of the Dazai府), he was referred to as "Sochi no Miya" (Prince of the Dazai). When Emperor Hanazono ascended to the throne, Go-Daigo was established as Crown Prince through the arrangement of the military government (bakufu).

In the second month of Bunpō 2 (1318), he ascended to the throne at the age of thirty-one. Nijō Michihira served as Regent (kanpaku). The Retired Emperor Go-Uda (hōō) administered state affairs. The shogun in Kamakura was Prince Morikuni. The regent (shikken) of the Hōjō clan was Hōjō Sagami no Kami Takatoki. In the third month, Kuniyoshi, son of the In of Go-Nijō, was established as Crown Prince. In the sixth month, Konoe Tsunehira, Minister of the Left, passed away.
