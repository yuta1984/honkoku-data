Emperor Kōkō was the third son of Emperor Ninmyō. His personal name was Tokiyasu. Having lived through the three reigns of Montoku, Seiwa, and Yōzei, he held the title of First Rank Prince and Minister of Ceremonies. When Emperor Yōzei abdicated, through Mototsune's arrangement, he unexpectedly ascended to the throne on the twenty-third day of the second month of Gangyō 8 (884). He was fifty-five years old at the time. Mototsune served as regent (kanpaku).

In the third month, he posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank to his maternal grandfather Fujiwara no Fusatsugu. In the fourth month, the Emperor read literary selections for the first time, with Tachibana no Hirosuke serving as imperial tutor. In the fifth month, Minister of the Left Minamoto no Tōru, having received an imperial edict, summoned scholars including Minister Suga (who was at that time a Doctor of Literature), Yoshifuchi Nagasada, and Ōkura no Yoshiyuki, and asked them: "Since the office of Grand Minister serves as the teacher and model for the Emperor, should it not have specific administrative duties? Or, being the highest of the three ministers, should it oversee the governance of the realm?" The scholars each presented their opinions. Since Mototsune's authority was at its peak at this time, they said that the Grand Minister had no specific duties, attempting to diminish his power...
