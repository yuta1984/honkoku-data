A banquet was held and stipends were bestowed. In the third month, Iji no Azamaro, an Emishi from Mutsu Province, raised a rebellion, killed Provincial Inspector and Counselor Ki no Hirozumi, and plundered government property throughout the province. Government forces under Ōtomo no Masutatsu, Ki no Kosami, and others were dispatched to Mutsu. Government troops were also sent to Dewa Province for defense. Large quantities of military provisions were transported from various provinces to Mutsu. In the ninth month, Fujiwara no Oguro-maro was appointed commander-in-chief for the Mutsu campaign. The Emishi rebels gradually expanded their power and were not easily defeated.

In the second month of the first year of Ten'ō era, 100,000 koku of rice were sent to Mutsu Province. In the third month, the Emperor fell ill. In the fourth month, he abdicated the throne to Crown Prince Yamabe and had him ascend to the throne. This was Emperor Kanmu. An envoy was sent to Ise Grand Shrine to announce the enthronement. In the sixth month, Right Minister Ōnakatomi no Kiyomaro retired. Interior Minister Fujiwara no Uona was appointed Left Minister. In the eighth month, Fujiwara no Oguro-maro pacified the rebels in Mutsu and returned to the capital. He was awarded the rank of Senior Third Rank.
