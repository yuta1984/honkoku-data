Hitachi and Shimōsa provinces were granted [to Takauji]. To Yoshisada were granted Kōzuke and Harima provinces. To Tadayoshi was granted Tōtōmi province. To Yoshisada's younger brother Wakiya Yoshisuke was granted Suruga province. To his eldest son Yoshiaki was granted Echigo province. To Masashige were granted Settsu and Kawachi provinces, and to Nagatoshi were granted Inaba and Hōki provinces. Many other rewards were also granted. Only Akamatsu Enshin did not receive any reward. Because of this, he bore resentment against the imperial court.

In the fifth month, Prince Morinaga was exiled to the Kantō region. Tadayoshi took custody of him and imprisoned him in the jail at Nikaidō in Kamakura. This was because his stepmother, the quasi-empress, had been persuaded by the Takauji brothers to make slanderous accusations. Prince Morinaga's close retainer, the Dharma Seal Ryōchū and others were killed. His Majesty made his eighth prince, Prince Nariyoshi, the Shōgun (Barbarian-Subduing Generalissimo), appointed Tadayoshi as shikken (regent) and governor of Sagami province, and had him reside in Kamakura.

In the seventh month, a strange bird cried overhead above the Shishin Hall. Oki Hiroyuki shot it down.
