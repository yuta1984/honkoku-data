[Continued from the previous page] ...Hitachi and Shimōsa (provinces) were granted (to Takauji). To Yoshisada were granted Kōzuke and Harima. To Tadayoshi was granted Tōtōmi. To Yoshisada's younger brother, Wakiya Yoshisuke, was granted Suruga. To (Yoshisada's) eldest son Yoshiaki was granted Echigo. To Masashige were granted Settsu and Kawachi, and to Nagayoshi were granted Inaba and Hōki. There were many other rewards besides. However, Akamatsu Enshin alone received no reward, and on account of this, he came to harbor deep resentment toward the imperial court.

In the fifth month, Prince Moriyoshi (Morinaga) was exiled to the Kantō. Tadayoshi took custody of him and confined him in the prison at Nikaido in Kamakura. This was because his stepmother, the Lady Jugo (Fujiwara no Renshi), had slandered him at the behest of the Takauji brothers. Moriyoshi's close retainer, Hōin Ryōchū and others were killed. His Majesty's eighth prince, Prince Nariyoshi (Narinaga), was appointed Sei'i Taishōgun (Shogun), and Tadayoshi was made Shikken (regent), appointed Governor of Sagami, and installed at Kamakura.

In the seventh month, a strange bird flew crying over the Shishinden (the Ceremonial Hall of the Imperial Palace). Oki no Hirōari shot it down with an arrow.

In the ninth month, Tōin Kinkata resigned from the post of Minister of the Interior (Naidaijin). Yoshida Dainagon (Major Counselor) Sadafusa (was appointed as Minister of the Interior...)
