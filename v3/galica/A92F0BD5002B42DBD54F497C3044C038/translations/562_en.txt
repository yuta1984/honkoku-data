[Takauji received] Hitachi and Shimōsa provinces. Yoshisada was granted Kōzuke and Harima provinces. Tadayoshi was granted Tōtōmi Province. Yoshisada's younger brother Wakiya Yoshisuke was granted Suruga Province. [Yoshisada's] eldest son Yoshiakira was granted Echigo Province. Masashige was granted Settsu and Kawachi provinces. Nagatoshi was granted Inaba and Hōki provinces. Many other rewards were also bestowed. Only Akamatsu Enshin received no reward, and because of this bore resentment toward the court.

In the fifth month, Prince Morinaga was exiled to the Kantō region. Tadayoshi took custody of him and imprisoned him in the jail at Nikaidō in Kamakura. This was because his stepmother, the Empress Dowager, had been asked by the Takauji brothers to make slanderous accusations. Prince Morinaga's close retainer, the Dharma Seal Yoshitada, and others were killed. His Majesty appointed the Eighth Prince, Prince Narinaga, as Seii Taishōgun (Barbarian-Subduing Great General), and Tadayoshi as shikken (regent), appointing him Governor of Sagami Province and having him reside in Kamakura.

In the seventh month, a strange bird cried overhead above the Shishin Hall. Oki Hiroyuki shot it down.
