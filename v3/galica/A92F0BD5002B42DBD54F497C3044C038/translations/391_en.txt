[The cloistered emperor] ordered [the warrior-monks] to plot against Yoshinaka. When rumors of this reached them, Imai Shirō Kanehira said, "By all means, we should apologize to the cloistered emperor for our crimes," but Yoshinaka would not listen. He led his troops to attack the Hōjūji Palace, set it on fire, and confined the cloistered emperor to the Gojō Palace. The Tendai chief abbot Meiin, Mii-dera head administrator Prince Enkyō, and many others were killed—the number of those slain reached several hundred. All the court officials fled barefoot in confusion, and many suffered humiliation. Yoshinaka personally took control of Tanba Province and became the son-in-law of former Chancellor Matsudono Motofusa. He stripped forty-nine people, including Regent Motonichi and Minister of the Left Sanesada, of their court ranks. He made Motofusa's son, Middle Counselor Moroie, who was only twelve years old, into Minister of the Left and regent.

In the twelfth month, Yoritomo killed Kazusa-no-suke Hirotsune. Later, realizing that [Hirotsune] was innocent, he is said to have regretted this.
