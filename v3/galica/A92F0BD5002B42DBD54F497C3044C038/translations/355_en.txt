The Seventy-eighth Emperor
Emperor Nijō: The first son of Go-Shirakawa. His personal name was Morihito. His mother was Fujiwara no Yoshiko, daughter of the posthumously appointed Chancellor Tsunezane of the Ōinomikado family. When Go-Shirakawa ascended the throne, Morihito was appointed Crown Prince. In the eighth month of the third year of Hōgen, he received the abdication and ascended the throne. Tadamichi resigned as regent, and his son, Right Minister Motomi, became regent at the age of sixteen. All governmental affairs of the realm were administered by Retired Emperor Go-Shirakawa. Shinzei increasingly gained imperial favor and wielded authority.

In the first year of Heiji, on the third day of the first month, there was an imperial visit to pay respects to the Retired Emperor. On the twenty-first day of the same month, an inner banquet was held. After this, it was again discontinued and not performed.
