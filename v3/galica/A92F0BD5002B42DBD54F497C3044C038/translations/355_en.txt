The Seventy-eighth Emperor
Emperor Nijō　The first son of Go-Shirakawa. His personal name was Morihito. His mother was Fujiwara no Yoshiko, daughter of the posthumously appointed Chancellor Fujiwara no Tsunesane of the Ōinomikado family. When Go-Shirakawa ascended the throne, he was appointed Crown Prince. In the eighth month of the third year of Hōgen, he received the abdication and ascended the throne. Tadamichi resigned as regent. His son, Right Minister Motomi, became regent at the age of sixteen. All governmental affairs of the realm were administered by Retired Emperor Go-Shirakawa. Shinzei became increasingly influential as a close advisor and wielded great authority.
In the first year of Heiji, on the third day of the first month, there was an imperial visit to pay respects to the Retired Emperor. On the twenty-first day of the same month, an inner banquet was held. After this, it was again discontinued and not performed. Around this time, there was a man called Fujiwara no Nobuyori, who held the positions of Middle Counselor and Right Gate Guard Commander.
