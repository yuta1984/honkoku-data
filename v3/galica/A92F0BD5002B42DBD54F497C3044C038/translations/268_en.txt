Sari was a master calligrapher known even in foreign countries. In the tenth month, Kanemichi, due to illness, yielded the position of regent (kanpaku) to Yoritada. Kanemichi submitted a memorial stating that his younger brother Kanei's daughter was favored by Retired Emperor Reizei and bore him a child, and therefore had intentions to restore him to the throne. With this slander, he stripped Kanei of his positions as major counselor and general of the right, demoting him to minister of justice. Still not satisfied, he petitioned for death penalty or exile, but imperial permission was not granted. On the eighth day of the eleventh month, Kanemichi died at the age of fifty-one. He was granted the title Duke of Tōtōmi and posthumously named Duke Chūgi (Loyal and Righteous).

In the eighth month of the first year of Ten'en era, Kanei's daughter Senshi was summoned to serve in the Plum Court and made an imperial consort. Before this, Kanemichi's daughter had been empress. Therefore, during Kanemichi's lifetime, no other man's daughter entered the palace. Due to Kanemichi's death, Senshi entered the palace. Soon after, she gave birth to an imperial prince. In the tenth month, Yoritada became grand minister of state. Minamoto no Masanobu became left minister. Kanei became right minister.
