Later, Tenmu sent Ōtomo no Shima as an envoy to persuade Prince Takasaka, who was the governor of Yamato, but as he would not join their cause, they hastily departed Yoshino before the matter could be exposed. Being a sudden departure, there was no imperial carriage available, so Tenmu rode on horseback while Empress Jitō rode in a palanquin. His sons Prince Kusakabe and Prince Oshinokabe, along with more than twenty close retainers and more than ten women, followed on foot. On the road, more than twenty hunters came and joined the imperial procession. They also acquired fifty horses that were carrying tribute rice from Ise, discarded the rice, and had the people on foot ride the horses. During this journey, when passing through Yamashiro Province, a stray arrow came and struck Tenmu in the back. That place is called Yasena [Note]. They fled into the mountains and tied up their saddled horses at a place now called Mount Kurama.
