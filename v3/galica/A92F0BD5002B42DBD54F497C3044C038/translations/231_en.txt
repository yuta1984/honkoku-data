In the fourth year, fifth month, Tokihira became the Chief Administrator of the Imperial Police. The Emperor issued an edict to Minister Sugawara to compile the Ruijū Kokushi (Classified National History).

In the fifth year, second month, Tokihira was appointed Middle Counselor and concurrently served as Right General. Minister Sugawara was appointed Councilor. Minister Sugawara had inherited the family profession and was particularly outstanding in his extensive learning and literary talent, so the Emperor promoted him to office. In the seventh month, Middle Counselor Ariwara no Yukihira died at the age of seventy-five.

In the sixth year, eighth month, Minister Sugawara was appointed as Ambassador to Tang China, and Ki no Haseo was made Vice-Ambassador. Although they were granted these offices in recognition of their talents, perhaps because Tang China had become a chaotic country at this time, there was no implementation of the mission to enter Tang. Haseo was also a person of great talent who had read the Book of Han, the Selections of Refined Literature, and various other books. In the ninth month, about fifty pirate ships from Silla came to attack Tsushima Province.
