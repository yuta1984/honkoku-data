He was the founding abbot of the temple.

In the fourth year, fifth month, Tokihira became Director of the Police and Judicial Commission. The Emperor issued an edict to Minister Sugawara to compile the Ruijū Kokushi.

In the fifth year, second month, Tokihira was appointed Middle Counselor and concurrently served as Major Captain of the Right. Minister Sugawara was appointed Councilor. Since Minister Sugawara had inherited the family profession and was exceptionally gifted in erudition and literary talent, the Emperor promoted him to office. In the seventh month, Middle Counselor Ariwara no Yukihira died at the age of seventy-five.

In the sixth year, eighth month, Minister Sugawara was appointed as Ambassador to Tang China, and Ki no Haseo was made Vice-Ambassador. Though they were granted these offices in recognition of their talents, perhaps because Tang China had become a country in turmoil at this time, there was no implementation of the mission to China. Haseo was also a person of great talent, having read the Book of Han, the Literary Selections, and various other books. In the ninth month, about fifty pirate ships from Silla attacked Tsushima Province.
