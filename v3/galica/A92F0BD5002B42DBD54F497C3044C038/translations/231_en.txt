In the 4th year, 5th month, Tokihira became the director of the Imperial Police. The Emperor commanded Minister Kan (Sugawara no Michizane) to compile the Ruiju Kokushi (Classified National History).

In the 5th year, 2nd month, Tokihira was appointed as Middle Counselor and concurrently held the position of Right General. Minister Kan was appointed as Imperial Advisor. Since Minister Kan had inherited his family's scholarly tradition and was exceptionally learned and talented in literature, the Emperor promoted him to office. In the 7th month, Middle Counselor Ariwara no Yukihira died at the age of seventy-five.

In the 6th year, 8th month, Minister Kan was appointed as chief envoy to Tang China, and Ki no Haseo was made vice-envoy. Although they were granted these offices in recognition of their talents, since Tang China had become a chaotic country at this time, there was no implementation of the mission to enter Tang. Haseo was also a person of great talent, having read the Book of Han, the Selections of Refined Literature, and various other books. In the 9th month, about fifty pirate ships from Silla came to Tsushima Province.
