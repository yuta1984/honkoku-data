Morotada of the Nijō clan served as Regent (Kampaku). At this time, there were three Retired Emperors (Daijō Tennō). Retired Emperor Gofukakusa administered governance and was styled "Ichi-in" (the Senior Retired Emperor) or "Hon'in" (the Principal Retired Emperor). Retired Emperor Kameyama was styled "Naka-no-in" (the Middle Retired Emperor). Retired Emperor Gouda was styled "Shin'in" (the New Retired Emperor). In contrast to earlier times, (the retired emperors) refrained from interfering in any matters of state.

In the fourth month of the first year of Shōō, Regent Morotada resigned as Minister of the Left. In the sixth month, the daughter of Fujiwara no Sanenori, the Grand Counselor (Dainagon) of the Saionji clan, entered the imperial court as a consort. Sanenori was the son of Kinsuke. In the seventh month, Tadanori, the Minister of the Right (Ufu) of the Kujō clan, transferred to become Minister of the Left, and Iemoto, the Inner Minister (Naifu) of the Konoe clan, was appointed Minister of the Right. Minamotono Michimoto, the Grand Counselor of the Koga clan, transferred to become Inner Minister.

In the ninth month, Michimoto became the superintendent (bettō) of the Shōgakuin and Junna'in academies, and also head of the Minamoto clan (Genji Chōja). The superintendency of the two academies had long been transmitted within the Koga family, but the formal designation as head of the Minamoto clan is said to have originated from this point onward.
