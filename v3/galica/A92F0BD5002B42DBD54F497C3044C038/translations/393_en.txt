[The Taira] built fortifications in the valley and gathered one hundred thousand men. Because of this, the former emperor (Emperor Antoku) made an imperial progress from Yashima to Fukuhara. Among the nearby warriors who had sympathies for the Genji, Noto-no-kami Noritsune defeated them all completely.

In the second month, Noriyori and Yoshitsune divided into two forces and attacked the Taira. Yoshitsune first defeated Taira Sukemori and others who had set up camp at Mount Mikusa, then headed toward the rear approach to Ichi-no-tani. Noriyori headed toward the main approach at Ikuta Forest. At the main approach, the Kawara brothers died fighting in the vanguard, while Kajiwara Kagetoki and his son Kagesuye distinguished themselves with two cavalry charges. At the rear approach, Kumagai Naozane and Hirayama Sueshige led the vanguard, followed by Dohi Sanehira.

However, when the fortress proved too strong to break, Yoshitsune circled around the mountain behind it, attacked through Hiyodorigoe, and set fire to the Taira camp. The Taira suffered a great defeat, and Echizen-no-sanmi Tsumorimori, Satsuma-no-kami Tadanori, Bitchū-no-kami Moromori, Musashi-no-kami Tomoakira, Owari-no-kami Kiyosada, Awaji-no-kami Kiyofusa, and Kōgōgū-no-suke Tsunemasa [were killed].
