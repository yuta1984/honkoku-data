In the tenth month, Right Minister Minamoto no Tōru passed away at the age of fifty-nine. He was a son of Emperor Ninmyō. In the eleventh month, the Daijōsai (Great Thanksgiving Festival) was performed.

In Kanpyō 1, on New Year's Day of the first month, the Shihōhai (worship of the four directions) was performed. From this time on, this became an annual practice. Grand Councilor Fujiwara no Yoshiyo became Left General. Middle Councilor Minamoto no Yoshiari became Right General. Yoshiyo was the younger brother of Yoshifusa. Yoshiari was a son of Emperor Montoku. In the fifth month, Prince Takamochi was granted the Taira surname. He was the great-grandson of Emperor Kanmu, grandson of Prince Kazurawara, and son of Prince Takami. He was the ancestor of Taira no Kiyomori and also of the Hōjō clan. In the tenth month, Retired Emperor Yōzei's madness manifested, and he bound women with koto strings and submerged them in water. At times he would mount a horse and ride out chasing people.
