In the first year of Ninna (885), in the first month, hunting grounds within Settsu Province were granted to Mototsune. In the fourth month, the Emperor celebrated Mototsune's fiftieth birthday. In the eighth month, the Emperor made an imperial visit to Shinsen'en, where he fished and viewed horses. In the eleventh month, High Priest Henjō was summoned, and the Emperor celebrated his seventieth birthday.

In the second year, in the first month, Mototsune's eldest son Tokihira, at sixteen years old, came of age in the Imperial Palace, with the Emperor personally performing the capping ceremony. Mototsune presented various gifts in gratitude. On the day of hinoto-hitsuji in the eighth month, the Sekiten ceremony was held as usual. A scholar lectured on the I Ching. Mototsune came and paid respects to Confucius. On the fourteenth day of the twelfth month, there was an imperial visit to Serikawano for falconry. The Emperor was fond of hunting and frequently made such excursions.
