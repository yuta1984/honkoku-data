He was an older brother from a different mother of the [current] Retired Emperor. Since he resided at Sanjo Takakura, he was called Takakura-no-miya (Prince Takakura). Yorimasa's son, Izunokami Nakatsuna, had always maintained cordial relations with Shigemori, but with Munemori it was not so, and there were many unpleasant incidents. Therefore, father and son together encouraged this prince, and the prince granted imperial commands to the Minamoto clans throughout the provinces. These were distributed through Nakatsuna's official letters. Consequently, they made Minamoto no Yukiie, the youngest son of Tameyoshi known as Jūrō, their messenger. Yukiie first went down to Izu Province and contacted the exiled former Uemon no Suke Minamoto no Yoritomo, then gradually made his rounds from there.

In the fifth month, Yorimasa's secret plot was discovered. He led Prince Takakura to take refuge at Mii-dera temple, persuaded the monks of that temple to join them, and also summoned the warrior-monks of the southern capital [Nara]. On the route from Mii-dera toward Nara, they temporarily rested the prince at Byōdō-in temple in Uji, when the Taira forces, led by Tomomori and others as generals, sent an attacking force against them.
