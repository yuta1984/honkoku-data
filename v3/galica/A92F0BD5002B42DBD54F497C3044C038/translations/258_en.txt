The Kebiishi (Imperial Police) were dispatched to suppress this conflict. Kan'shin-in is Gion. In the same month, Morosuke made a pilgrimage to Kasuga Shrine. From this time forward, ministers of the Fujiwara house frequently made pilgrimages to Kasuga. Kasuga is the ancestral deity of the Fujiwara clan.

In the fourth year, fifth month, fourth day, Right Minister Fujiwara no Morosuke passed away at the age of fifty-three. Born with a benevolent nature, he never showed joy or anger in his expression, and all people mourned his loss. In the eighth month, Fujiwara no Akitada was appointed Right Minister. He was a son of Tokihira. In the ninth month, the Imperial Palace caught fire. Since the capital was moved to Heian-kyō, this was the first fire after thirteen generations of emperors. Many treasured imperial objects handed down from ancient times were also lost in this fire. The sacred mirror was in the Onmyō-den, but it flew out on its own and came to rest on a cherry tree in the Nanden, where a court lady received it in her sleeves. The name "Naishi-dokoro" (Bureau of Court Ladies) for the sacred mirror began from this time. In the eleventh month, [the Emperor] moved to Reizei-in.

In the first year of Ōwa, eleventh month, [the Emperor] returned from Reizei-in to the newly constructed Imperial Palace.
