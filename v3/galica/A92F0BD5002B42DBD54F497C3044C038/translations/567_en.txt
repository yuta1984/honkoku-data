In the same month (the fourth month), Takauji (Ashikaga Takauji) and Tadayoshi (Ashikaga Tadayoshi) set out from Tsukushi at the head of a great army. In the fifth month, Yoshisada (Nitta Yoshisada) fell back and encamped at Hyōgo. An imperial decree was issued to Masashige (Kusunoki Masashige), commanding him to go to Yoshisada's aid. Although Masashige submitted a plan of strategy, it was not accepted, and so he proceeded to Hyōgo, joined forces with Yoshisada, and gave battle against Takauji. Masashige died in combat at the Minatogawa River, and Yoshisada was defeated and returned to the capital. The Emperor once again proceeded to Mount Hiei (Eisan), and Takauji entered the capital. He summoned the Retired Emperor Hanazono to Tōji Temple and sought to establish the imperial line of the Jimyōin-tō (the Northern Court faction).

In the sixth month, Takauji dispatched Kō no Moroshige and others to attack Mount Hiei, but the attacking force was defeated, and Moroshige was taken prisoner.

In the seventh month, although Yoshisada sortied from the capital several times to give battle, the imperial forces gained no advantage on each occasion. Nawa Nagatoshi died in battle. Yoshisada himself advanced on Tōji Temple and challenged Takauji to decide the outcome in single combat. Takauji refused, and Yoshisada, in anger, returned to the mountain.

In the eighth month, Toyohito, the younger brother of the Retired Emperor Kōgon, was, by Takauji's arrangement, [enthroned] in the capital.
