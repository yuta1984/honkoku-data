In the tenth month, Sanjō Saneshige resigned from the post of Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin). The former Minister of the Interior, Minamoto no Michio, was appointed Grand Minister of State. Minamoto no Michishige resigned from the post of Minister of the Interior. Kazanin's Moronobu was appointed Minister of the Interior.

In the fifth month of the second year [of Gen'ō] (1320), Hōjō Tokiatsu of the Rokuhara [Tandai] died. In the seventh month, Kujō's former Regent, Norinori, passed away. He was forty-eight years of age.

In the second month of Genkō 1 (1321), Imadegawa's former Minister of the Right, Fujiwara no Kinaki, passed away. In the fourth month, the Retired Emperor Go-Uda (now a Buddhist monk, Hōō) established the kondō (main hall) of Daikaku-ji temple. In the fifth month, an imperial visit (gyōkō) was made to Daikaku-ji. In the sixth month, Hōjō Kanetoki, the Chinzei Tandai (Deputy for Kyushu), died. That summer there was a great drought. The Emperor ordered Fujiwara no Tsunenobu, the Chief of the Kebiishi (Imperial Police), to distribute millet to relieve the poor. Furthermore, the Emperor had the wealthy residents of the capital sell their stored rice at low prices to relieve the famine. The Emperor himself went to the Kirokusho (Records Office) to adjudicate petitions and lawsuits. In the eleventh month, Kazanin's Minister of the Interior, Moronobu, passed away.
