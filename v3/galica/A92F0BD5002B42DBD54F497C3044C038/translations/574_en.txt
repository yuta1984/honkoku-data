In the first month of the first year of Kōei, Ichijō Tsunemichi resigned from the position of Regent (Kampaku). Kujō Michitaka became Regent. In the second month, Koga Nagatsu resigned from the position of Daijō-daijin.

In the ninth month, Toki Yoritō encountered the procession of the Retired Emperor Kōgon and committed an outrage against it. Tadayoshi, enraged, executed Yorito. In the eleventh month, Kujō Michitaka (sic) resigned from the position of Regent. Takatsukasa Udaijin Morahira became Regent. In the twelfth month, Takauji's mother, Fujiwara no Kiyoko, passed away. She was the daughter of Uesugi Yorishige. It was because of this connection that the Uesugi clan wielded great influence.

In the fourth month of the second year (of Kōei), the former Udaijin of the Tōin house, Fujiwara no Kinkata, was appointed Sadaijin (Minister of the Left). Nijō Naifu Yoshimoto was appointed Udaijin (Minister of the Right). Sanjō Dainagon Sanetada was appointed Naifu (Naidaijin).

In the first month of the third year (of Kōei), Takauji made a pilgrimage to Iwashimizu (Hachimangū Shrine). In the ninth month, Tadayoshi was granted the Junior Third Rank (Jushānmi).

On the twenty-ninth day of the eighth month of the first year of Jōwa, the dedication ceremony (kuyō) of Tenryū-ji was held. Takauji and Tadayoshi attended.
