The Cloistered Emperor also made an imperial visit and heard about these matters. In the third month, Moronaga was appointed directly from Inner Minister to Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin). Shigemori was appointed Inner Minister while remaining Left General as before. For someone outside the regent houses (Sekke) and distinguished families (Seiga) to hold both minister and general positions concurrently was unprecedented, and everyone was astonished. A grand banquet was held with Left Minister Tsunemune as the honored guest. At this time, besides Shigemori and Munemori, Kiyomori's younger brother Yorimori held the position of Middle Counselor. Norimori was a councilor (sangi). Tsunemori and Munemori's younger brother Tomomori held the rank of Junior Third Rank.

In the fourth month, the warrior monks of Mount Hiei repeatedly petitioned regarding the crimes of Morotaka and Moritsune, but since there was no judgment from the Cloistered Emperor, on the thirteenth day of the same month, the monks raised the sacred palanquins (mikoshi) of Hiyoshi Shrine and entered the capital, attempting to enter the Imperial Palace. The Taira clan under Shigemori and others, along with Minamoto no Yorimasa, were ordered to guard the palace gates.
