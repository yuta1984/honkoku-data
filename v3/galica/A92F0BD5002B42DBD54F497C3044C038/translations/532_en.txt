...it was the custom to appoint [the person who performed the Kakan ceremony] as Daijō Daijin (Grand Minister of State).

In the first month of the first year of Ōchō [1311], the Emperor [Hanazono] underwent the coming-of-age ceremony (genpuku). He was fifteen years old at the time. Fuyuhira performed the Kakan (placing of the ceremonial cap), and Konoe Minister of the Left Munehira performed the Rihats (ritual hair-dressing). In the third month, Fuyuhira was reinstated and became Kanpaku (Chief Advisor to the Emperor). In the same month, the former Daijō Daijin Nobatsugu passed away. He was seventy-six years old. In the eighth month, the former Minister of the Left of the Saionji house, Kinhira, shaved his head [entered the Buddhist priesthood]. There had been word that this person was to be appointed as Daijō Daijin, but he declined and did not take the position. He adopted the Buddhist name Chikurin'in. In the ninth month, Hōjō Morotoki died suddenly. He was thirty-seven years old. On the twenty-sixth day of the tenth month, Hōjō, Governor of Sagami, Sadatoki passed away. He was forty-one years old. His posthumous Buddhist name was Saishōonji. From the seventh year of Kōan [1284] to the third year of Shōan [1301], he served as Shikken (regent of the Bakufu) for eighteen years; after shaving his head, ten more years — twenty-eight years in total. His legitimate son Takatoki was barely nine years old. Hōjō Munenori and Hōjō Hirotoki jointly served as Shikken and Rensho (co-signatories).
