It was customary to appoint him as Daijō-daijin (Grand Minister of State).

In the first year of Ōchō, first month, when His Majesty (the Emperor) came of age, he was fifteen years old. Fuyuhira performed the capping ceremony. Konoe Sadaijin Munehira performed the hair-cutting ritual. In the third month, Fuyuhira returned to office and became Kanpaku (Chief Advisor to the Emperor). In the same month, former Daijō-daijin Nobitsugu passed away at the age of seventy-six. In the eighth month, former Saionji Sadaijin Kinhira took the tonsure. There was discussion that this person should be appointed as Daijō-daijin, but he declined and was not appointed. He took the alternate name Chikurin-in. In the ninth month, Hōjō Morotoki died suddenly at the age of thirty-seven. On the twenty-sixth day of the tenth month, Hōjō Sagami-no-kami Sadatoki died at the age of forty-one. He was given the posthumous name Saishōen-ji. From the seventh year of Kagen to the third year of Shōan, he served as shikken (regent) for eighteen years, and for ten years after taking the tonsure, totaling twenty-eight years. His heir Takatoki was only nine years old. Hōjō Munenori and Hōjō Hirotoki served as shikken and rensho (co-signer) respectively. Hirotoki was the grandson of Tokimura and son-in-law of Sadatoki.
