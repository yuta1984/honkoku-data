In the seventh month, Fujiwara no Munesuke resigned from the position of Chancellor (Daijō-daijin). In the same month, the Minister of the Center (Naidaijin) Fujiwara no Kiminori died. The former Chancellor Fujiwara no Saneyuki also died at the age of eighty-four. In the eighth month, the Minister of the Left (Sadaijin) Koremichi was appointed as Chancellor. The Regent and Minister of the Right (Udaijin) Motosada was appointed as Minister of the Left, Grand Counselor (Dainagon) Kimiyoshi was appointed as Minister of the Right, and Motosada's younger brother, Grand Counselor Motofusa, was appointed as Minister of the Center. Taira no Kiyomori was appointed as Middle Counselor (Sangi) and granted the court rank of Senior Third Rank (Shōsanmi). With Yoshitomo already executed and the Minamoto clan weakened, Kiyomori finally seized control of the realm. In this year, Fujiwara no Tashi was summoned and made Empress. She had been the consort of Emperor Konoe. After Konoe's death, she had been living in quiet seclusion, but when the Emperor heard of her beauty, he issued an imperial command to her father, Minister of the Right Kimiyoshi, to summon her. Both the Retired Emperor thought this matter inappropriate, and the court ministers remonstrated against it, but there was no imperial consent [to their objections]. The Empress also made various attempts to decline, it is said.
