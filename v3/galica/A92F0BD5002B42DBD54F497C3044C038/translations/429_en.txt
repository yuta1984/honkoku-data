Yoriie's close retainers plotted rebellion. Hōjō Sagami no Kami Yoshitoki had warriors suppress and pacify them. In the tenth month, Hōjō Masanori, Yūki Tomomitsu, Hatakeyama Shigeyasu and others traveled from Kamakura to the capital and welcomed the daughter of Bōmon Dainagon Fujiwara Nobukiyo to become Sanetomo's consort. In the twelfth month, when returning to the east, Masanori died of illness while still in the capital. In the same month, Yorisane resigned from the position of Grand Minister of State. Regent Yoshitsune was appointed Grand Minister of State. Konoe Udaijin Iemitsu was appointed Sadaijin (Minister of the Left). Naidaijin Takatada was appointed Udaijin (Minister of the Right). In the same month, the retired emperor composed waka poetry and dedicated them to Iwashimizu, Kamo, and Sumiyoshi shrines, thirty poems to each shrine.

In the second year, first month, His Majesty came of age at eleven years old. In the same month, Sanetomo was appointed to Junior Fifth Rank Lower and Udaijū (Middle Captain of the Right), concurrently serving as Kaga no suke (Assistant Governor of Kaga). In the third month, great winds blew in Kyoto and the capital region. There were rumors that this was a curse brought about by the monk Eisai's introduction of Zen Buddhism for the first time, and an imperial edict was issued ordering Eisai's expulsion.
