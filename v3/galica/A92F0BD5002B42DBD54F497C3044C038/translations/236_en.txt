There was an imperial visit. In the second month, Fujiwara no Tokihira was appointed to the position of Minister of the Left (Sadaijin). He remained as before in his position as General of the Left. Minister Kan (Sugawara no Michizane) was appointed Minister of the Right (Udaijin). He remained as before in his position as General of the Right. Minamoto no Hikaru and Fujiwara no Takafuji became Great Counselors (Dainagon). Hikaru was a son of Emperor Ninmyō. Takafuji was the Emperor's maternal grandfather. Minister Kan, rising from a scholarly family background, was positioned above imperial princes and maternal relatives. Therefore, he again submitted a memorial declining the position of Minister of the Right, but the Emperor did not grant permission.

In the ninth month, Princess Yasuko became the Saigū (sacred princess) of Ise. The Emperor attended and the ceremony was held. Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Kunitsune was ordered to escort the Saigū.

In the tenth month, the Retired Emperor (Emperor Uda) took the tonsure at Ninnaji Temple. Yakushin served as the precept master. His Buddhist name was Kongōkaku. In the eleventh month, he received abhisheka at Tōdaiji Temple, returned the honorary title of Retired Emperor, and toured famous mountains in various places. Only one person named Tachibana no Yoshitoshi accompanied him. No one regularly knew his whereabouts. The Emperor sent imperial messengers to search for him, but they could not meet. After some time had passed, he returned to the capital.
