Later, Yoshiie's younger brother, Shinra Saburō Yoshimitsu, who was serving as Hyōe-no-jō and was on night duty at the Imperial Palace, heard about the battles in Mutsu. Although he requested leave, he did not receive imperial permission, so he left in the middle of the night and went down to Mutsu. Yoshiie was delighted, saying it was as if his father Yoriyoshi had come again, and gaining strength from this, he gathered troops and attacked Kanazawa. Kamakura Gon-no-Gorō Kagemasa, at sixteen years old, advanced to the front line and, even after being shot in the left eye, shot an arrow and killed that enemy. In addition, warriors such as Miura Tametsugu and Ban-no-Sukekane also distinguished themselves in battle. In the daily battles, they established rankings of kō (first class) and otsu (second class), placing brave warriors in the kō seat and cowardly ones in the otsu seat. A man named Fujiwara no Hidekata never once sat in the otsu seat.
