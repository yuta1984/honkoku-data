Imperial envoys were dispatched. In the eleventh month, the Daijōsai ceremony was held. Its rituals were fully prepared. In this year, the monk Gyōkyō made a pilgrimage to Usa and reported that there was an oracle from Hachiman Daijin saying that the deity would come to the royal capital to protect the imperial throne. For the first time, a shrine was built at Iwashimizu on Mount Otokoyama in Yamashiro Province to venerate the deity.

In the second year, first month, University Doctor Kasuga no Otsugu presented the Classic of Filial Piety to the Emperor. From this time forward, the Emperor's reading instruction generally began with the Classic of Filial Piety.

In the third year, second month, the Emperor made a royal visit to the residence of Grand Minister Yoshifusa. All court officials attended. Yoshifusa's retainers were all granted court ranks. In the third month, a memorial service was held because the repair of the Great Buddha at Tōdaiji was completed. In the fifth month, a Balhae envoy ship arrived in Izumo Province. Because there were violations of proper diplomatic protocol, they were sent back from there. In the sixth month, children's sumo wrestling was viewed in the front hall. In the eighth month, the Emperor read the Analects, with Kasuga no Otsugu serving as imperial lecturer.
