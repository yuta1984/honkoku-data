Imperial envoys were dispatched. In the eleventh month, the Daijōe (Great Thanksgiving Festival) was performed. The ceremonies were complete. In this year, the monk Gyōkyō made a pilgrimage to Usa and reported that there was an oracle from Hachiman Daijin saying that the deity would come to the imperial capital to protect the imperial throne. For the first time, a shrine was built at Iwashimizu on Mount Otoko in Yamashiro Province to venerate the deity.

In the second year, first month, University Doctor Kasuga no Otsugu presented the Classic of Filial Piety to the Emperor. From this time forward, the Classic of Filial Piety was generally used for the imperial reading ceremonies.

In the third year, second month, there was an imperial visit to the residence of Grand Minister Yoshifusa. All court officials attended. Yoshifusa's retainers all received court ranks. In the third month, a memorial service was held as the repairs to the Great Buddha of Tōdaiji were completed. In the fifth month, a Bokkai envoy ship arrived in Izumo Province. Because there were violations of protocol, they were sent back. In the sixth month, children's sumo was viewed in the front hall. In the eighth month, the Emperor read the Analects, with Kasuga no Otsugu serving as lecturer.
