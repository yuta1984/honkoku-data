Imperial envoys were dispatched. In the eleventh month, the Daijōsai (Great Tasting Ceremony) was performed. The ceremonies were complete. In this year, the monk Gyōkyō made a pilgrimage to Usa, and reported that there was an oracle saying that Hachiman Daijin would come to the imperial capital to protect the imperial throne. For the first time, a shrine was built at Iwashimizu on Mount Otoko in Yamashiro Province and was venerated.

In the first month of the second year, University Doctor Kasuga no Otsugu presented the Classic of Filial Piety to the Emperor. From this time forward, the Emperor's reading initiation ceremonies generally employed the Classic of Filial Piety.

In the second month of the third year, there was an imperial visit to the residence of Chancellor Yoshifusa. All court officials attended. Yoshifusa's retainers were all granted court ranks. In the third month, a memorial service was held because the repair of the Great Buddha at Tōdaiji was completed. In the fifth month, a Bokkai envoy ship arrived in Izumo Province. Because there were violations of protocol, they were turned away. In the sixth month, children's sumo wrestling was observed in the front hall. In the eighth month, the Emperor read the Analects, with Kasuga no Otsugu serving as lecturer.
