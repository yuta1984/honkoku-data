Imperial envoys were dispatched. In the eleventh month, the Daijōsai (Great Thanksgiving Festival) was held. Its ceremonies were complete. In this year, the monk Gyōkyō made a pilgrimage to Usa and reported that there was an oracle saying that Hachiman Great Deity would come to the imperial capital to protect the imperial throne. For the first time, a shrine was built at Iwashimizu in Otokoyama, Yamashiro Province, and was worshipped.

In the first month of the second year, University Doctor Kasuga no Otsugu presented the Classic of Filial Piety to the Emperor. From this time onward, the Emperor's reading ceremonies generally used the Classic of Filial Piety.

In the second month of the third year, the Emperor made a royal visit to the residence of Chancellor Yoshifusa. All court officials attended. Yoshifusa's retainers were all granted court ranks. In the third month, a memorial service was held due to the completion of repairs to the Great Buddha of Tōdaiji Temple. In the fifth month, ships of Balhae envoys arrived in Izumo Province. Because there were violations of proper protocol, they were turned back. In the sixth month, children's sumo wrestling was viewed in the front hall. In the eighth month, the Emperor read the Analects, with Kasuga no Otsugu serving as lecturer.
