The Emishi rebel called Takamaru from Mutsu Province rose up from Tagaya Cave and attacked as far as Kiyomi Barrier in Suruga Province. The Seii Taishogun (Great General for Subduing the Barbarians) Sakanoue no Tamuramaro received the ceremonial sword and departed for battle. Takamaru retreated and withdrew to Ōshū, and Tamura continued to attack into Ōshū, engaging in battle and shooting Takamaru to death at a place called Kaguraoka. He also pacified the rebel called Akuro-ō (King Akuro). All of Ōshū was completely subdued. Tamura built a Hachiman Shrine in Isawa District and deposited his bow and arrows there, and also in front of Tagaya Cave, he modeled after Kurama Temple of Yamashiro and enshrined a statue of Tamonten (Vaiśravaṇa). In the eleventh month, when Tamura returned to the capital, the Emperor declared: "In recent years Ōshū has been in rebellion several times, but Tamura has now completely subdued it all. His merit is great," and awarded him the court rank of Ju-sanmi (Junior Third Rank).
