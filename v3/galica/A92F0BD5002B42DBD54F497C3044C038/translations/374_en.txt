Though he was at this time still Middle Captain of the Second Rank, he was immediately appointed Inner Minister and made Chancellor, placing him above Left Minister Tsunemune and Right Minister Kanezane. Motomichi was twenty years old at this time. They even surrounded the Cloistered Emperor's palace, Hōjūji-dono, and Kiyomori ordered Munemori to confine the Cloistered Emperor in the detached palace at Toba. Leaving Munemori in the capital, Kiyomori returned to Fukuhara. Kiyomori's evil deeds cannot be fully recounted, but while Shigemori was alive, he was restrained and endured patiently. Now that there was no one to admonish him, things became as they were.

In the second month of the fourth year, the Emperor abdicated and ceded the throne to the Crown Prince. He moved to Kan'in-dono [Note]. He received the honorary title of Retired Emperor. The era names were Kaō 2 years, Jōan 4 years, Angen 2 years, and Jishō 4 years, for a total reign of twelve years.
