Imperial visit to Kasuga Shrine. In the sixth month, former Kampaku Fujiwara no Yorichika died at the age of sixty-six. He was granted the title of Suruga-kō and given the posthumous name Ren'gi-kō. In the eighth month, a great wind caused many gates of the imperial palace and numerous shrines to collapse. In the twelfth month, Kanetsuna was appointed Daijō-daijin (Chancellor).

In the first year of Shōryaku (990), in the first month, the Emperor came of age at eleven years old. In the fifth month, due to illness, Kanetsuna shaved his head and became a monk, taking the name Higashi-Sanjō no Daiundō. He made Michitaka regent to govern in his place. On the second day of the seventh month, Kanetsuna died at the age of sixty-two. Since he became a monk during his illness, he received no posthumous name. His mansion was converted into a temple called Hōkō-in. This marked the beginning of the practice of temple names for the Sekke (regent families).

In the second year, in the second month, Retired Emperor En'yū died at the age of thirty-two. In the ninth month, Right Minister Fujiwara no Tametaka was made Daijō-daijin. Minamoto no Shigenobu was made Right Minister. Fujiwara no Michikane was made Inner Minister. Shigenobu was the younger brother of Left Minister Masanobu. Michikane was the younger brother of Michitaka. In the tenth month, Empress Dowager Senshi of Umitsubo became a nun...
