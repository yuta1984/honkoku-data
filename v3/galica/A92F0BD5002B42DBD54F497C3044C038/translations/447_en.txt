Kitajō Tokifusa went to the capital and brought Yoritsune to Kamakura, where he was revered as lord. All the warrior vassals paid their respects and followed him. Since Yoritsune was only two years old, Masako acted as his representative in conducting government affairs. All matters of the realm, great and small, were handled by Yoshitoki's arrangements.

Yoritomo's elder sister married Middle Counselor Yoshiyasu, and the daughter she bore married Gokōgoku Yoshitsune and gave birth to Michiie. Michiie married the daughter of Saionji Kintsune and had Yoritsune. Due to this lineage, it is said that Yoritsune was welcomed because he had some connection to Yoritomo. Through this relationship, the Saionji and Hōjō families developed close ties.

Around this time, in Suruga Province, there was a man called Ano Kanja Tokimoto. Being Yoritomo's nephew and the son of Zenjō, when it was heard that he harbored ambitions to become lord of the Kantō, Yoshitoki sent warriors and killed Tokimoto.
