(He was the younger brother of Mototsune. She is the one known to the world as the "Nijō no Kisaki" [Empress of Nijō].) This Emperor was born in the tenth year of Jōgan, became Crown Prince in the eleventh year, and in the eleventh month of the eighteenth year received abdication from Emperor Seiwa. At the dawn of the first year of Gangyō, on the third day of the first month, the Emperor ascended the throne. As the Daigokuden had not yet been rebuilt, the ceremony was held at the Burakuden. Since he was only eight years of age, Mototsune served as regent. The title of Minister of the Left, Senior First Rank, was posthumously bestowed upon his maternal grandfather, Fujiwara no Nagara. In the second month, an envoy from Balhae arrived and was sent home from Izumo Province. In the sixth month, a drought continued and rain had not fallen for a long time. Prayers were offered at various shrines including Ise, Hachiman, and Kamo. In the eleventh month, the Daijōe (Great Thanksgiving Ceremony) was held. In the twelfth month, Gangyō-ji Temple was constructed.

In the second year, second month, Yoshibuchi no Yoshinari was ordered to read aloud the Nihongi. In the third month, over one thousand barbarian brigands from Dewa Province rose up and burned and broke through Akita Castle. The provincial governor Fujiwara no Okiyo attempted to repel them but suffered a military defeat, with over five hundred men reportedly killed.
