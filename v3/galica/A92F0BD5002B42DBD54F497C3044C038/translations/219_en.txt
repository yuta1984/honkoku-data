[Fujiwara no Takako was] the younger sister of [Fujiwara no] Mototsune. She is known to the world as the Nijō Empress. This emperor was born in the tenth year of Jōgan, became crown prince in the eleventh year, and in the eleventh month of the eighteenth year, Seiwa abdicated the throne to him. On the third day of the first month of the following year, the first year of Gangyō, the emperor ascended the throne. Since the Daigokuden had not yet been rebuilt, the ceremony was held in the Burakuden. Since he was only eight years old, Mototsune served as regent. His maternal grandfather Fujiwara no Nagayoshi was posthumously granted the rank of Minister of the Left, Senior First Rank. In the second month, envoys from Parhae arrived. They were sent back via Izumo Province. In the sixth month, there was a drought and it did not rain for a long time, so prayers were offered at various shrines including Ise, Hachiman, and Kamo. In the eleventh month, the Daijōsai ceremony was held. In the twelfth month, Gangyōji Temple was built.

In the second month of the second year, Yoshibuchi no Yoshinari was ordered to lecture on the Nihon Shoki. In the third month, more than a thousand Emishi rebels in Dewa Province rose up and burned and destroyed Akita Castle. Provincial governor Fujiwara no Okiyo tried to defend against them but was defeated in battle, and it is said that more than five hundred were killed.
