She was the younger sister of Mototsune. She is the one known to the world as Empress Nijō. This emperor was born in the tenth year of Jōgan, became crown prince in the eleventh year, and received the abdicated throne from Kiyowa in the eleventh month of the eighteenth year. On the third day of the first month of the following first year of Gangyō, the emperor ascended to the throne. Since the Daigokuden had not yet been constructed, the ceremony was held in the Burakuden. As he was only eight years old, Mototsune served as regent. The posthumous rank of Minister of the Left, Senior First Rank was bestowed upon his maternal grandfather Fujiwara no Nagara. In the second month, envoys from Balhae came and were sent back via Izumo Province. In the sixth month, due to drought with no rain for a long time, prayers were offered at various shrines including Ise, Hachiman, and Kamo. In the eleventh month, the Daijōsai ceremony was held. In the twelfth month, Gangyō-ji temple was built.

In the second year, second month, Yoshibuchi no Yoshinari was ordered to read the Nihon-ki. In the third month, over a thousand Emishi rebels in Dewa Province rose up and burned down Akita Castle. Provincial governor Fujiwara no Okiyo attempted to defend against them but suffered defeat, and it is reported that over five hundred people were killed.
