She was the younger sister of Mototsune. This is the person known to the world as Nijō-kōgō (Nijō Empress). This emperor was born in the tenth year of Jōgan, became crown prince in the eleventh year, and received the throne from Emperor Seiwa in the eleventh month of the eighteenth year. On the third day of the first month of the following year, Gangyō 1, the emperor ascended to the throne. Since the Daigokuden had not yet been built, the ceremony was conducted at the Burakuden. As he was only eight years old, Mototsune served as regent. Left Minister and Senior First Rank were posthumously granted to his maternal grandfather Fujiwara no Nagara. In the second month, an envoy from Balhae arrived. He was sent back via Izumo Province. In the sixth month, there was a drought and no rain fell for a long time. Prayers were offered at various shrines including Ise, Hachiman, and Kamo. In the eleventh month, the Daijōe ceremony was held. In the twelfth month, Gangyōji Temple was built.

In the second year, second month, Yoshibuchi no Yoshinari was made to read the Nihon Shoki. In the third month, over a thousand Emishi rebels in Dewa Province rose up and burned down Akita Castle. Provincial Governor Fujiwara no Okiyo attempted to defend against them but suffered defeat, and it is said that over five hundred people were killed.
