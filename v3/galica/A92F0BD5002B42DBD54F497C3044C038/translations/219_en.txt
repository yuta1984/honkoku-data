[She was] the younger sister of Mototsune. What the world calls the Nijō Empress refers to this person. This emperor was born in the tenth year of Jōgan, became crown prince in the eleventh year, and in the eleventh month of the eighteenth year, Seiwa abdicated the throne to him. On the third day of the first month of the following first year of Gangyō, the emperor ascended the throne. Since the Daigokuden had not yet been rebuilt, the ceremony was held in the Burakuden. As he was only eight years old, Mototsune served as regent. His maternal grandfather Fujiwara no Nagayoshi was posthumously granted the rank of Minister of the Left, Senior First Rank. In the second month, an envoy from Balhae arrived and was sent back via Izumo Province. In the sixth month, there was a drought with no rain for a long time, so prayers were offered at various shrines including Ise, Hachiman, and Kamo. In the eleventh month, the Daijōsai ceremony was held. In the twelfth month, Gangyō-ji temple was built.

In the second year, second month, Yoshibuchi no Yoshinari was ordered to read the Nihon Shoki. In the third month, over a thousand Emishi rebels in Dewa Province rose up and burned down Akita Castle. Provincial Governor Fujiwara no Okiyo defended against them but was defeated, and it is said that over five hundred men were killed.
