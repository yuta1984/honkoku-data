Fujiwara no Tokushi. She was the daughter of Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Nagasane. [Emperor Konoe] was born in Hōen 5 (1140). He was immediately made Crown Prince. He ascended the throne in the twelfth month of Eiji 1 (1141), at the age of three. Chancellor Tadamichi served as regent. At this time, Toba Retired Emperor was called Ichi-in (First Cloister). Retired Emperor Sutoku was called Shin-in (New Cloister). All governmental affairs were conducted through the retired emperor's arrangements, while the new retired emperor was not involved in anything and spent his years in displeasure with all matters.

In the first month of Kōji 2 (1143), the retired emperor made an imperial visit to the empress for the morning audience. In the fifth month, the retired emperor received ordination at Tōdai-ji and also received ordination at Mount Hiei.

In the seventh month of Ten'yō 2 (1145), because a large comet appeared, the era name was changed to Kyūan. In the eighth month, Taiken Mon-in, the mother of the new retired emperor, passed away. In the same year, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo.

In the second month of Kyūan 2 (1146), there was an imperial visit to Toba Palace. In the twelfth month, they celebrated Regent Tadamichi's fiftieth birthday.
