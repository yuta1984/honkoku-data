In the same month, Yoritomo divided forces from various provinces into three groups and launched a campaign against Oshu. Yoritomo advanced via the Tosando route, with Hatakeyama Shigetada as the vanguard. Chiba no Suke Tsunetane and Hatta Tomoya advanced via the Tokaido route, and Hiki Yoshikazu advanced via the Hokurikudo route. Miyoshi Yoshinobu, Sasaki Tsunetaka, and Oba Kageyoshi were left as guardians of Kamakura. Yasuhira left Hiraizumi mansion and set up camp at Kokubun Plain. He built a fortress at Mount Atsukashi and had his elder brother Kunihira and others defend it. In the eighth month, Yoritomo crossed the Shirakawa barrier, reached Idate District, and stormed the Atsukashi fortress. Wada Yoshimori released an arrow and shot down Kunihira. One of Shigetada's retainers obtained his head. In subsequent battles at various places, Yoritomo achieved victory every time. Tsunetane and Tomoya also joined from the Tokaido route, and Hiki Yoshikazu entered Dewa from the Hokurikudo route and killed Yasuhira's retainers. Yoritomo (led) a large army
