In the same month, Yoritomo divided the troops from various provinces into three forces and launched a punitive expedition against Ōshū. Yoritomo advanced via the Tōsandō route, with Hatakeyama Shigetada as the vanguard. Chiba no suke Tsunetane and Hatta Tomoya advanced via the Tōkaidō route, while Hiki Yoshikazu advanced via the Hokurikudō route. Miyoshi Yoshinobu, Sasaki Tsunetaka, and Ōba Kagenō were left to guard Kamakura. Yasuhira left his mansion in Hiraizumi and set up camp at Kokubun-hara. He constructed a fortress at Mount Atsukashiyama and had his elder brother Kunihira and others defend it. In the eighth month, Yoritomo crossed the Shirakawa barrier, reached Dateno district, and attacked and destroyed the Atsukashi fortress. Wada Yoshimori shot an arrow and struck down Kunihira. Shigetada's retainer obtained his head. In the subsequent battles at various locations, Yoritomo achieved victory every time. Tsunetane and Tomoya also joined from the Tōkaidō route, and Hiki Yoshikazu entered Dewa from the Hokurikudō route and killed Yasuhira's retainers. Yoritomo (led) a great army.
