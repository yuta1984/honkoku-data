Twenty men were taken prisoner alive. The mountain monks returned to their mountain in anger. In the eleventh month, Fujiwara no Teika presented a secret manuscript of the Man'yōshū to Sanetomo.

In the second month of the second year, Sanetomo suffered from hotohori illness after drinking. The chief monk Eisai of Jufuku-ji temple offered tea to ease his hangover afflictions. In the third month, there was an imperial visit to Kasuga. In the fourth month, the monk-warriors of Mount Hiei burned Onjō-ji temple. Sanetomo had it reconstructed.

In the first month of the third year, Hōjō Tokimasa died of illness in the mountains of Izu. He was seventy-eight years old. In the sixth month, the monk Eisai died. Some say he died at Kennin-ji temple in Kyoto, while others say he died at Jufuku-ji temple in Kamakura. In the eighth and ninth months, there were frequent major earthquakes in Kamakura. In the tenth month, Fujiwara no Kintsugu resigned from the position of Minister of the Right. In the twelfth month, Inner Minister Michii was appointed Minister of the Right, and Major Counselor Fujiwara no Kinefusa became Inner Minister.
