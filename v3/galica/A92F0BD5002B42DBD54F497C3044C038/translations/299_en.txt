In the first year of Tenki, sixth month, Yorimichi's mother Minamoto no Rinshi died. She was ninety years old. She was the emperor's great-grandmother (and also his wet nurse).

In the fifth year, ninth month, Yoriyoshi fought against Abe no Yoriyoshi in Mutsu Province. Yoriyoshi was struck by an arrow and died. Sadatō gathered the remaining forces and fortified himself in the Kawasaki stockade. It is also called Kawai Castle. In the eleventh month, Yoriyoshi led over 1,100 men to attack Sadatō. Sadatō led 4,000 men in defensive battle. At that time, wind and snow were fierce, the government army's provisions were exhausted, and they suffered a great defeat with several hundred dead. Yoriyoshi, along with his eldest son Yoshiie, retainers Fujiwara no Kagemichi, Ōyake no Mitsutō, Kiyohara no Sadahiro, Fujiwara no Norisue, and Fujiwara no Noriaki, was reduced to only seven horsemen and surrounded by a great enemy force. Yoshiie was about thirty years old at the time, a skilled archer and elite warrior who shot and killed very many enemies. Mitsutō and the others fought defensively, making light of their lives.
