In the first year of Tenki (1053), sixth month, Yorimichi's mother, Minamoto no Rinshi, passed away at the age of ninety. She was the great-grandmother of the Emperor.

In the fifth year (1057), ninth month, Yoriyoshi fought against Abe no Yoritoki in Ōshū. Yoritoki was struck by an arrow and died. Sadatō gathered the remaining forces and fortified himself in the Kawasaki stockade, also called Kawai Castle. In the eleventh month, Yoriyoshi led over 1,100 men to attack Sadatō. Sadatō defended with 4,000 men. At that time, fierce wind and snow raged, the imperial army's provisions were exhausted, and they suffered a great defeat with several hundred dead. Yoriyoshi was reduced to just seven horsemen: his eldest son Yoshiie, and his retainers Fujiwara no Kagemichi, Ōyake no Mitsutō, Kiyohara no Sadahiro, Fujiwara no Norisue, and Fujiwara no Noriaki, and they were surrounded by a great enemy force. Yoshiie was about twenty years old at the time, a skilled archer and elite warrior who killed very many enemies with his bow. Mitsutō and others fought desperately, making light of their lives.
