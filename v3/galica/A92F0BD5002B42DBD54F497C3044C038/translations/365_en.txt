On the eleventh day, Kiyomori took the tonsure due to illness and changed his name to Jōkai. He was fifty-one years old at the time and was called Nyūdō Sōkoku (Lay Priest Chancellor). Sometimes he resided at Rokuhara, sometimes at Nishi-Hachijō, and sometimes at his villa in Fukuhara in Settsu Province. His wife Taira no Tokiko was called Hachijō Nii-dono, his eldest son Shigemori was called Komatsu-dono, and his younger brother Yorimori was called Ike-dono. All his other sons and brothers achieved glory in official ranks.

In the first year of Kaō (1169), in the third month, Retired Emperor Go-Shirakawa made a pilgrimage to Mount Kōya. In the sixth month, the retired emperor took Buddhist vows and was called Cloistered Emperor. In the twelfth month, due to complaints from the Buddhist community of Mount Hiei, Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Nariyoshi was exiled to Bingo Province. Since Nariyoshi was a close retainer of the cloistered emperor, he was soon recalled.

This year, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine and Kamo Shrine.

In the second year, in spring, Kanō no Suke Shigemitsu of Izu Province came to the capital and reported that the exile Minamoto no Tametomo was seizing control of various islands and encroaching upon Shigemitsu's territories, so [an order was issued] that he should be pursued and attacked.
