Since the retired emperor (Sutoku) had originally harbored the ambition to regain power, he was greatly pleased and conspired secretly with Yorinaga. Taking the opportunity of the retired emperor's (Toba's) death, they summoned and gathered soldiers from nearby provinces. Therefore, before even seven days had passed since the death, both the capital and its outskirts were in turmoil. The retired emperor (Sutoku) proceeded from Tanaka Palace in Toba to his residence in Shirakawa. Left Minister Yorinaga also went there. To the imperial palace came Regent Tadamichi and others in attendance. Among the warriors, Minamoto no Yoshitomo, Governor of Shimotsuke, and Taira no Kiyomori, Governor of Aki, and others guarded the imperial palace. Yoshitomo's father Tameyoshi and Kiyomori's uncle Taira no Tadamasa, Assistant of the Right Division of Bureau of Horses, and others went to Shirakawa Palace upon the retired emperor's summons. Among Tameyoshi's sons, all except Yoshitomo were on the retired emperor's side. Among them, Chinzei Hachirō Tametomo was an unparalleled warrior with a strong bow and military prowess. On the night of the eleventh of the same month, the lay priest and Minor Counselor Shinzei, receiving imperial command, had Yoshitomo, Kiyomori and others attack the retired emperor's residence.
