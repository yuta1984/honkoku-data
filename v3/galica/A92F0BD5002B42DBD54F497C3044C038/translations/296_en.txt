In the fourth year, summer, there was a drought. The monk Ninkai prayed for rain and it was efficacious, so he was granted permission to use an imperial palanquin.

In the first year of Kantoku (1044), tenth month, Retired Empress Jōtōmon'in fell ill, so ten thousand monks were assembled and memorial services were performed.

In the second year, first month, eighteenth day, the Emperor died at age thirty-seven. Though he was born intelligent, all governmental affairs were handled by Yorimichi, so things did not go according to the Emperor's wishes. The era names were Chōryaku for three years, Chōkyū for four years, and Kantoku for two years, making a total reign of nine years.

Seventieth Emperor
Emperor Go-Reizei   The eldest son of Emperor Go-Suzaku. His personal name was Chikahito. His mother was Fujiwara no Kishi, daughter of Michinaga. When Go-Suzaku ascended the throne, Chikahito was made Crown Prince.
