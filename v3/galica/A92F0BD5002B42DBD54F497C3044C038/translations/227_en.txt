In the third year, in the fourth month, imperial messengers bearing offerings were dispatched to Ise, Iwashimizu, Hiyoshi, and other shrines. In the fifth month, Ōharano in Yamashiro Province was presented to Retired Emperor Yōzei as hunting grounds. In the eighth month, various strange phenomena occurred in the Imperial Palace. On the twenty-sixth day of this month, the Emperor passed away at the age of fifty-eight. Previously, Emperors Heizei, Saga, and Junna had favored composing Chinese poetry, so there were many literary courtiers at that time. This Emperor (Kōkō) favored Japanese waka poetry, so from this time forward, people throughout the realm considered it essential to dedicate themselves to waka composition. Reign: three years. Era name: Ninna.

Fifty-ninth Emperor
Emperor Uda - The third son of Emperor Kōkō. His personal name was Sadayoshi. His mother was Empress Hanshi, daughter of Prince Nakano. Before Emperor Kōkō's accession to the throne, he was playing with his children and said, "If I should ascend to the imperial throne, what would you all..."
