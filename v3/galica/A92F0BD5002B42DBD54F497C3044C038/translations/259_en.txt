In the second year, second month, imperial messengers (hōheishi) were dispatched to Ise Shrine, Kamo Shrine, Matsunoo Shrine, Hirano Shrine, and Kasuga Shrine. To Kamo and Matsunoo shrines, ten sacred horses each were offered. Imperial messengers were also dispatched to various other shrines.

In the third year, second month, the Crown Prince came of age (genpuku ceremony) in the Shishin-den. Saneyori performed the capping. Councillor Fujiwara no Asatada performed the hair cutting. In the eighth month, Saneyori made a pilgrimage to Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine. From this time onward, ministers of the Fujiwara house frequently made pilgrimages to Iwashimizu. In the same month, Genshin of Mount Hiei and Chūsan of Nanto were summoned to conduct religious debates in the Seiryō-den.

In the first year of Kōhō, fourth month, Empress Fujiwara no Anshi passed away. The Empress's younger sister was called Tōshi. She was the wife of Prince Shigeakira, the Emperor's elder brother. Due to her beautiful appearance, when she came to visit the Empress, the Emperor had a secret affair with her. At this time, Shigeakira had already died, and when the Empress also passed away, Tōshi was summoned to the Imperial Palace and favored. From this time, Emperor Murakami's government began to decline.
