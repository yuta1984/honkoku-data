The [Left] General was Major Counselor Fujiwara no Moronaga (son of Yorinaga).

In the first year of Angen, second month, Kuga Inner Minister Minamoto no Masamoto died at the age of fifty-eight.

In the eleventh month, Fujiwara no Moronaga was appointed Inner Minister. This year, Shigemori sent three thousand ryō of gold to Great Song China and donated it to Mount Yuwan.

In the second year, third month, there was an imperial visit to the Cloistered Emperor's palace to celebrate his fiftieth birthday. In the seventh month, Emperor Rokujō died at the age of thirteen. In the same month, Kenshunmon-in died. Around this time, Kaga Provincial Governor Fujiwara no Morotaka had a dispute with the warrior monks of Mount Hiei. Morotaka's father was called the monk Saikō, who had been a retainer of Shinzei. After Shinzei's death, he served closely as a member of the Cloistered Emperor's personal guard (hokumen) and wielded considerable power. Morotaka was appointed Governor of Kaga Province. His younger brother Moretsune served as deputy (mokudai) and resided in the province. There was a legal dispute with monks from Ukawa, a subsidiary temple of Mount Hiei, which led to the burning of temple buildings and eventually armed conflict between both sides.
