Saneyuki was called Sanjō. The second son, Middle Counselor Michisue, was called Saionji. The third son was Sanenō, called Tokudaiji. The descendants of all three branches were of the Seige (noble) rank. Because they descended from Minister Kinsue of the Kan'in line, the three branches together are called the Kan'in family.

In the second month of the sixth year, former regent Tadazane was permitted to use a imperial palanquin for his comings and goings. In the fifth month, the warrior monks of Mount Hiei burned Miidera temple. In the sixth month, Tadazane was granted additional stipends with the honorary rank of Jun-Sangū (quasi-imperial prince) and his retainer guards were increased. In the tenth month, Tadazane took the tonsure at his villa in Uji at age sixty-two. In the twelfth month, Inner Minister Yorinaga resigned from the position of Left General. Although it was said to be the emperor's intention to appoint Major Counselor Saneyuki, the elder brother of Right General Sanenō, as Left General, the retired emperor commanded that Major Counselor Masasada, son of Minister Masazane of Kuga, should be appointed instead.
