Saneyuki was designated as Sanjō. The second son, Middle Counselor Michisue, was designated as Saionji. The third son was Sanenō, designated as Tokudaiji. The descendants of all three lineages held the status of seika (high noble families).

Since they were descendants of Minister of the Closed Palace Kinsue, the three lineages together are called the Kan'in House.

In the sixth year, in the second month, former Regent Tadazane was granted permission to travel in and out using a palanquin. In the fifth month, the monks of Mount Hiei burned Mii-dera temple. In the sixth month, Tadazane was granted the status of jun-sankyū (quasi-three palace rank) with additional food fiefs, and his retainer guards were increased. In the tenth month, Tadazane took the tonsure at his Uji villa at the age of sixty-two. In the twelfth month, Interior Minister Yorinaga resigned as Left General. Although the emperor intended to appoint Major Counselor Saneyuki, the elder brother of Right General Sanenō, as Left General, the retired emperor commanded that Major Counselor Masasada, son of Kuga Minister of State Masazane, should be appointed instead.
