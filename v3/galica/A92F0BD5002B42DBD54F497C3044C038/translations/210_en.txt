In the third month of the fourth year, Ariwara no Narihira was appointed to the rank of Senior Fifth Rank Upper Grade. He was the grandson of Emperor Heizei and the son of Prince Abo.

In the first month of the fifth year, Major Counselor and General of the Right Minamoto no Sadamu died. He was a beloved son of Retired Emperor Saga. In the same month, his elder brother Major Counselor Minamoto no Hiromu died. This person loved learning, was skilled in calligraphy, and was fond of instrumental music. In the fifth month, at Shinsen-en Garden, the vengeful spirits of Emperor Sudō, Prince Iyo, Fujiwara Lady Yoshiko, Tachibana no Hayanari, Bunno-no-miya Tamaro and others were enshrined. This is called the Goryō-e (Spirit Pacification Ceremony). In recent years, epidemics had continued to spread, and from the spring of this year they were particularly severe, with many people dying. Since there were those who said this might be the work of these evil spirits, this ceremony was performed. In the tenth month, Yoshifusa was summoned and granted a banquet to celebrate his sixtieth birthday. In this year, Yoshifusa received orders and had Harusumi no Yoshinawa compile the Shoku Nihon Kōki (Later Chronicle of Japan Continued).
