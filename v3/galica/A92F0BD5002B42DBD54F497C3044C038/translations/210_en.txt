In the third month of the fourth year, Ariwara no Narihira was appointed to the court rank of Junior Fifth Rank Upper Grade. He was the grandson of Emperor Heizei and the son of Prince Abo.

In the first month of the fifth year, Major Counselor and Commander of the Right Guards Minamoto no Sadamu died. He was the beloved son of Retired Emperor Saga. In the same month, his elder brother Major Counselor Minamoto no Hiromu died. This person loved learning, was accomplished in calligraphy, and was fond of music. In the fifth month, at Shinsen Garden, they held rituals for the vengeful spirits of Emperor Sudo, Prince Iyo, Fujiwara Lady Yoshiko, Tachibana no Hayanari, Bunroom Prince Tamaro, and others. This was called the Goryō-e (Spirit Pacification Festival). In recent years, epidemics had continued to spread, and from the spring of this year, they became particularly severe, with many people dying. Because some said this might be the work of these evil spirits, this festival was performed. In the tenth month, Yoshifusa was summoned and granted a banquet to celebrate his sixtieth birthday. In this year, Yoshifusa received orders and had Harusumi Yoshinawa compile the Shoku Nihon Kōki (Later Chronicles of Japan Continued).
