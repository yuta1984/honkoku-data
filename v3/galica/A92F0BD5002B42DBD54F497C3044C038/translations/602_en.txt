There were those who devoted their loyalty to (the Southern Court). In this year, the Southern Court abolished the Shōhei era name and established the new era name of Kentoku.

In the second month of the fourth year (of Ōan, 1371), Yamana Tokiuji died. In the same month, since Kikuchi Takemasa and others of the Southern Court faction rose up in Chinzei (Kyushu), Imagawa Iyono-kami Sadayo, the lay monk known as Ryōshun, was appointed as the Tandai (Deputy) of Kyushu and dispatched there, with Ōuchi Yoshihiro assigned to accompany him. Around this time, the Kikuchi clan brought Kyushu under their sway, and the Southern Court prince whom they had installed and promoted was called Prince Kansei (Kanenaga), known as Ryōkai. (Ryōkai) dispatched an envoy with a ship to the Great Ming (China). The letter carried by this envoy bore the inscription "Ryōkai, King of Japan." He also intercepted envoys coming from the Great Ming to the King of Japan in Tsukushi (Kyushu), preventing them from reaching the capital, and sent back carefully composed replies in their stead. As a result, the Great Ming believed Ryōkai to be the true king of Japan.

In the third month, the Sovereign (retired Emperor Sukō) abdicated in favor of Crown Prince Ōhito.

Era names: Bunna 4th year; Enbun 5th year; Kōan 1st year.
