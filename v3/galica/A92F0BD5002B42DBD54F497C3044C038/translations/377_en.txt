Nakatsuna and his younger brother Kanetsuna were killed, and Yorimasa committed suicide. Prince Takakura fled toward Nara, but was struck by a stray arrow at a place called Kōmyōzan and died at the age of thirty. Kiyomori sent Tomomori and others to attack and burn down Mii-dera temple.

In the sixth month, through Kiyomori's scheme, the capital was moved to Fukuhara in Settsu Province. The Emperor made an imperial progress there, and both the Retired Emperor and the Former Retired Emperor also made the journey. Though the court officials were not all of one mind, they proceeded to Fukuhara. Since Emperor Kanmu established Heian-kyō, there had been no precedent for moving the capital. The Retired Emperor was confined to the palace at Fukuhara on account of the Prince Takakura affair. Because of Yorimasa's actions, there were deliberations that Kiyomori declared all the Minamoto throughout the provinces should be killed. Hearing of this in Izu Province, Minamoto no Yoritomo made Tōkurō Morinaga his messenger to consult with the retainers of the eastern provinces and plotted to raise a righteous army.
