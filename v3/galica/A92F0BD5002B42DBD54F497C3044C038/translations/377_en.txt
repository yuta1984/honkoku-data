Nakatsuna and his younger brother Kanetsuna and others were killed in battle, and Yorimasa committed suicide (at the age of seventy-five). Prince Takakura fled toward Nara, but was struck by a stray arrow at a place called Kōmyōzan and died. He was thirty years old at the time. Kiyomori again dispatched Tomomori and others to attack and burn down Mii-dera Temple. In the sixth month, through Kiyomori's machinations, the capital was moved to Fukuhara in Settsu Province. His Majesty (Emperor Antoku) made an imperial progress there, and the Retired Emperor and Former Emperor also made their imperial visits. All the court officials reluctantly proceeded to Fukuhara against their will. Since Emperor Kanmu established Heian-kyō, there had been no precedent for moving the capital. The Retired Emperor was confined again in the palace at Fukuhara due to the matter of Prince Takakura. Because there were reports that Kiyomori had declared that all Minamoto throughout the provinces should be killed due to Yorimasa's affair, Minamoto no Yoritomo heard of this matter in Izu Province and made Tōkurō Morinaga his messenger to win over the retainers of the Kantō region and plotted to raise an army of righteousness.
