[Continued from the eighth month of the sixth year of Jōhei:] ...Tadahira sent a letter to the King of Wuyue in Tang China. In the same month, Tadahira was appointed Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin). Nakahira was appointed Minister of the Left. Fujiwara no Tsunesuke was appointed Minister of the Right.

In the seventh year, first month, the Emperor came of age (genpuku).《Age fifteen》 Tadahira presided over the ceremony. In the twelfth month, a celebration of the seventieth birthday of Retired Emperor Yōzei was held.

In the first year of Tengyō, third month, fourth day, ten bouts of cockfighting were held in the Imperial presence. From the fifteenth to the twenty-ninth day of the fourth month, there were major earthquakes every day. In the fifth month, the Minister of the Right, Tsunesuke, passed away. He was the son of Yoshiyo.

In the second year, first month, a celebration of Tadahira's sixtieth birthday was held. In the fourth month, barbarian bandits (izoku) rose up in Dewa Province. On the twenty-second day of the eighth month, a Kōshin gathering was held in the Imperial Palace. In the eleventh month, the Emperor read the Shiji (Records of the Grand Historian), and Fujiwara no Arihira and others served as lecturers in attendance. In the twelfth month, Taira no Masakado raised a rebellion in the Kantō region, invaded Hitachi Province, killed his paternal uncle, Hitachi no Daijō (Senior Secretary of Hitachi) Taira no Kunika, and seized control of the entire province.
