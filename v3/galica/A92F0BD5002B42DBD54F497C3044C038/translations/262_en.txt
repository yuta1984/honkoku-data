Several hundred of Morotada's retainers rose up and broke into Kaneie's residence. Kaneie was the third son of Morosuke and was Morotada's nephew. In the third month of the same year, Samanosuke (Assistant Master of the Left Stables) Minamoto no Mitsunaka and Musashi no suke (Assistant Governor of Musashi) Fujiwara no Yoshitoki secretly reported that Nakatsukasa no shōsuke (Junior Assistant Minister of Central Affairs) Minamoto no Shigenobu had been plotting a rebellion. It was said that this was a scheme by the Minister of the Left Takaakira to force the Emperor to abdicate and install Tamehira on the throne. As a result, Saneyori and Morotada presented this matter to the Emperor, and Takaakira was demoted to the position of Dazai Gon no Sochi (Acting Governor-General of Dazaifu), had his head shaved, and was exiled to Tsukushi (Kyushu). Morotada was appointed Minister of the Left, and Fujiwara no Arihira became Minister of the Right. Investigators (kebiishi) were dispatched to arrest Shigenobu and the monk Remo, who were subjected to interrogation under torture, and confessed. Since there were also reports that Fujiwara no Chiharu was among the conspirators, the investigator Minamoto no Mitsusue (younger brother of Mitsunaka) was dispatched and arrested Chiharu along with his soldiers, who were all imprisoned. The commotion within the Imperial Palace was extreme. In the fourth month, the residence of Takaakira at Nishimiya was burned down. Chiharu, Shigenobu, and Remo were all sentenced to exile.
