Several hundred retainers rose up and destroyed Kanemie's residence. Kanemie was the third son of Morosuke and nephew of Morotada. In the third month of the same year, Sauma-no-suke Minamoto no Mitsunaka and Musashi-no-suke Fujiwara no Yoshitoki secretly reported that Nakatsukasa-no-shōyu Minamoto no Shigenobu was plotting rebellion. They claimed this was a scheme by the Minister of the Left Takaaki to force the emperor to abdicate and install Prince Tamehira on the throne. Based on this report, Saneyori and Morotada petitioned the throne, resulting in Takaaki being demoted to Provisional Governor-General of Dazaifu, having his head shaved, and being exiled to Tsukushi. Morotada became Minister of the Left, and Fujiwara no Arihira became Minister of the Right. They dispatched kebiishi (police officials) to arrest Shigenobu and the monk Renmo for interrogation. After they confessed, Fujiwara no Chiharu was also suspected of complicity, so they sent kebiishi Minamoto no Mitsusue (younger brother of Mitsunaka) to arrest Chiharu and his followers and imprison them. The disturbance in the imperial palace was extremely severe. In the fourth month, they burned down Takaaki's residence in Nishinomiya. Chiharu, Shigenobu, and Renmo were all exiled.
