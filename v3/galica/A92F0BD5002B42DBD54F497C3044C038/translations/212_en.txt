On the first day of the intercalary third month, there was an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence, with various entertainments. On the night of the tenth day of the same month, the Ōten-mon gate burned down. Though there was discussion about whether it might be arson, the cause of the fire was unknown. Around this time, Yoshifusa occasionally attended court and left governmental affairs to Yoshimi. At this time, there was a person called Dainagon Tomo no Yoshio. He aspired to become a minister, but since there was no vacancy, he thought that if Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto were removed, Yoshimi would become Left Minister and he himself would become Right Minister. So he accused the Left Minister of burning down the Ōten-mon gate. Yoshimi believed this and, together with Yoshio, went to the council chamber and called Sangi Chūjō Mototsune, saying "The Left Minister harbors treasonous intentions and burned the Ōten-mon gate. Go quickly and investigate this matter." When Mototsune heard this, he asked, "Does the Grand Minister know about this?"
