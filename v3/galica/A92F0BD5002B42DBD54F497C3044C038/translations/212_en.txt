In the third month of the eighth year, the Emperor made an imperial visit to the Hyakka-tei pavilion of the Minister of the Right, Yoshimi. On the first day of the intercalary third month, he made an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence, where various entertainments were held. On the night of the tenth day of the same month, the Ōtenmon Gate was destroyed by fire. There was talk that it might have been arson, but the cause of the fire remained unknown. Around this time, Yoshifusa only occasionally attended court, and the affairs of government had been entrusted to Yoshimi. At this time, there was a man called Tomo no Yoshio, a Major Counselor. He desired to become Minister, but there being no vacancy, he thought that if he could remove the Minister of the Left, Minamoto no Makoto, then Yoshimi would become Minister of the Left and he himself could become Minister of the Right. He therefore accused the Minister of the Left of being responsible for burning the Ōtenmon Gate. Yoshimi believed this, and together with Yoshio, went to the Jinnoза (the guards' chamber) and summoned Consultant Middle Captain Mototsune, saying: "The Minister of the Left harbors treasonous intent and has burned the Ōtenmon Gate. Go there at once and conduct an investigation." Upon hearing this, Mototsune asked: "Is the Grand Minister aware of this matter?" Yoshimi replied: "These days, the Grand Minister is devoted exclusively to Buddhism..."
