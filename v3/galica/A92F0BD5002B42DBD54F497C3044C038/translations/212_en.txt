On the first day of the intercalary third month, there was an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence, with various entertainments. On the night of the tenth day of the same month, the Ōtenmon Gate burned down. There was discussion about whether it was arson, but the cause of the fire was unknown. Around this time, Yoshifusa occasionally attended court and left politics to Yoshimi. At this time, there was a man called Dainagon Tomo no Yoshio. He aspired to become a minister, but since there was no vacancy, he thought that if he could remove Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto, Yoshimi would become Left Minister and he himself would become Right Minister. So he accused the Left Minister of being responsible for burning down the Ōtenmon Gate. Yoshimi believed this and, accompanying Yoshio, went to the council chamber and called Sangi Chūjō Mototsune, saying "The Left Minister has treasonous intentions and burned down the Ōtenmon Gate. Go quickly and investigate this matter." Hearing this, Mototsune asked, "Does the Grand Minister know about this matter?" Yoshimi replied, "Lately the Grand Minister has devoted himself entirely to Buddhist faith..."
