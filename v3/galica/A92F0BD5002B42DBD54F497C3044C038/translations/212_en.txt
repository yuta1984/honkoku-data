On the first day of the intercalary third month, there was an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence, with various entertainments. On the night of the tenth day of the same month, the Ōtenmon Gate burned down. There was discussion about whether it might have been arson, but the cause of the fire was unknown. Around this time, Yoshifusa only occasionally attended court, leaving government affairs to Yoshimi. At this time, there was a man called Dainagon Tomo no Yoshio. He desired to become a minister, but since there was no vacancy, he thought that if Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto were removed, Yoshimi would become Left Minister and he himself would become Right Minister. He accused the Left Minister of being responsible for burning the Ōtenmon Gate. Yoshimi believed this and, together with Yoshio, went to the council chamber and called Sangi Chūjō Mototsune, saying "The Left Minister harbors treasonous intentions and burned the Ōtenmon Gate. Go quickly and investigate this matter." Hearing this, Mototsune asked, "Does the Grand Minister know about this matter?" Yoshimi replied, "Lately the Grand Minister has devoted himself exclusively to Buddhism (and does not involve himself in government affairs)."
