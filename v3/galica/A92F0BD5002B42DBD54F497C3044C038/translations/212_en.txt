On the first day of the intercalary third month, there was an imperial visit to Yoshifusa's residence, with various entertainments. On the night of the tenth day of the same month, the Ōtenmon Gate was destroyed by fire. There was speculation that it might have been arson, but the cause of the fire was unknown. Around this time, Yoshifusa occasionally attended court and left political affairs to Yoshimi. At this time, there was a person called Dainagon Tomo no Yoshio. He hoped to become a minister, but since there was no vacancy, he thought that if Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto were removed, Yoshimi would become Left Minister and he himself would become Right Minister. So he accused the Left Minister of being responsible for the burning of the Ōtenmon Gate. Yoshimi believed this and, together with Yoshio, went to the council chamber and called Sangi Chūjō Mototsune, saying, "The Left Minister harbors treasonous intentions and burned the Ōtenmon Gate. Go quickly and investigate this matter thoroughly." Hearing this, Mototsune asked, "Does the Grand Minister know about this matter?" Yoshimi replied, "Recently the Grand Minister has been devoted exclusively to Buddhism..."
