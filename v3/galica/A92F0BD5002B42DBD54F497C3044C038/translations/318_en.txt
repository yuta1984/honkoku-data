In the third year, eleventh month, [Emperor Shirakawa] abdicated the throne to Crown Prince Yoshihito. He was honored with the title of Retired Emperor. Initially, the year following his accession did not change the previous emperor's era name and was called Enkyū 5. Subsequently there were Jōhō 3 years, Jōryaku 4 years, Eihō 3 years, and Ōtoku 3 years, totaling fourteen years of reign.

Seventy-third Emperor
Emperor Horikawa - The second son of [Emperor] Shirakawa. His personal name was Yoshihito. His mother was called Empress Kenshi. She was the daughter of Minister of the Right Minamoto no Akifusa, but was adopted by Regent Fujiwara no Morozane who arranged her entry into the palace. [Yoshihito] was born in Jōryaku 2. In Ōtoku 3, eleventh month, he received the abdication from Shirakawa and ascended the throne. He was eight years old at the time. Morozane served as regent. However, Retired Emperor Shirakawa, for the first time conducted all governmental affairs from the retired emperor's residence.
