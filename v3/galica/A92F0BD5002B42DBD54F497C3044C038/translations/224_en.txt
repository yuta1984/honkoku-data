He shed tears and grieved, but it was unavoidable. Immediately, he was given the honorary title of Retired Emperor (Jōkō). He was seventeen years old at the time. At this time, Mototsune's authority and power were extremely strong, and all the court ministers feared him. However, only the Minister of the Left, Minamoto no Tōru, appeared to be contemplating what should be done about this matter. A person named Fujiwara no Morokutsu placed his hand on his sword and said, "Who would dare oppose the will of the Grand Minister?" Upon hearing this, Tōru fell silent. Due to this, no objections were raised.

According to one theory, since Tōru was also an imperial prince, he had hopes of ascending to the throne, but because he had already joined the ranks of court officials, Mototsune would not permit it. According to another theory, due to the Emperor's frequent illnesses, he gave Mototsune an edict written in the imperial hand and abdicated of his own accord. This was likely done out of consideration for the court, concealing and avoiding mention of the Emperor's evil deeds.

The Emperor reigned for eight years. Era name: Gangyō
