In the same month, the monks of Mount Hiei responded to Morinaga's will and raised an army to attack Kyoto. They fought with the warriors of Rokuhara, but the mountain monks were defeated. From Mount Senjo, the former emperor sent Chigusa Chunagon Tadaaki with additional troops to attack Kyoto. They fought with Rokuhara, but Tadaaki's army was not successful. In the same month, Takatoki made his clansman Nagoe Owari-no-kami Takaiie the general and Ashikaga Jibu-no-taifu Takauji the vice-general, and sent them to march on Kyoto. Since Nagasaki Enki suspected Takauji, Takauji used his younger brother Tadayoshi's strategy and wrote a sworn statement to show him, so Takatoki did not suspect him. After the march to Kyoto, Takaiie fought with Akamatsu and was killed in battle. Takauji became aligned with the former emperor's side and joined hearts with Akamatsu and others. Takauji temporarily avoided Kyoto and went to Tanba Province. Many warriors from neighboring provinces followed him.
