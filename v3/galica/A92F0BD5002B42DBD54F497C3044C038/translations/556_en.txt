[Continued from the previous page] Warriors from the provinces of Sanyō and San'in came in great numbers and submitted to him (Emperor Go-Daigo).

In the same month, the warrior monks of Mount Hiei, responding to the wishes of Prince Moriyoshi, raised arms and attacked the capital. They engaged the warriors of Rokuhara in battle, but the mountain monks were defeated. The Retired Emperor (Go-Daigo), from Mount Funakami, sent Chigusa Chūjō Tadaaki at the head of troops to attack the capital. They fought against Rokuhara, but Tadaaki found no advantage in the battle. In the same month, Hōjō Takatoki appointed his kinsman Nagoshi, the Governor of Owari, Takauji (Takake), as commander-in-chief, and Ashikaga Jibu no Taifu Takauji as deputy commander, and ordered them to march on the capital. As Nagasaki Engki was suspicious of Takauji, Takauji made use of his younger brother Tadayoshi's stratagem and presented a written oath, which caused Takatoki to cease his suspicions. After arriving in the capital, Takake fought against Akamatsu and died in battle. Takauji then sided with the Retired Emperor and joined forces with Akamatsu and others. Takauji temporarily withdrew from the capital and proceeded to Tanba Province. Many warriors from nearby provinces followed him.
