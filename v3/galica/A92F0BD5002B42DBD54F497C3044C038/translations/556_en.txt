They fought with the warriors of Rokuhara, but the mountain monks were defeated. The former emperor sent Chigusa Chūjō Tadaaki from Mount Senjō with military forces to attack Kyoto. They fought with Rokuhara, but Tadaaki's army gained no advantage. In the same month, Takatoki made his clan member Nagoe Owari no Kami Takaiê the general and Ashikaga Jibu no Taifu Takauji the vice-general, sending them to Kyoto. Since Nagasaki Enki suspected Takauji, Takauji used his younger brother Tadayoshi's strategy to write and show a written oath, so Takatoki did not suspect him. After going to Kyoto, Takaiê fought with Akamatsu and was killed in battle. Takauji joined the former emperor's side and united in spirit with Akamatsu and others. Takauji temporarily avoided Kyoto and went to Tamba Province. Many warriors from nearby provinces followed him.
