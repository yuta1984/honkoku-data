When the Emperor came of age, he loved waka poetry. Those renowned in this art, such as Minamoto no Toshiyori and Fujiwara no Mototoshi, constantly attended upon him. Court ladies such as Suō no Naishi and Ise no Taifu were among the many palace women who composed poetry. He was also accomplished in instrumental music and refined songs, and was particularly skilled at playing the flute. He summoned a person called Tokimoto to play the shō (reed pipe) for his listening pleasure.

Kanji 1 (1087), 5th month: Retired Emperor Shirakawa made an imperial visit to Uji.
2nd year, 1st month: [Emperor Horikawa] made a formal visit to pay respects at the retired emperor's palace. 2nd month: The retired emperor visited Tōdaiji and Kōfukuji temples. From there, he ascended Mount Kōya and opened the portrait hall of [Kūkai] Kōbō Daishi to worship. 10th month: Imperial visit to Mount Hiei. 11th month: Minister of the Left Nobutaka retired from office (at age 67). 12th month: Regent Morozane was appointed as Chancellor.
3rd year, 5th month: The retired emperor visited Mount Hiei. [He] stayed at the central hall for seventeen days.
