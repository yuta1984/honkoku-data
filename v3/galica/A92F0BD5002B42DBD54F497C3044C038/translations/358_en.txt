In the first month of the following year, on the third day, Yoshitomo was killed by his retainer Osada Tadamune in Noma, Owari Province. He was thirty-eight years old. Tadamune's son-in-law, Kamada Masakiyo, who was Yoshitomo's chief retainer, was also killed along with Yoshitomo. On the tenth day of the same month, there was a change of era name to Eiryaku. Yoshitomo's eldest son Yoshihira was an unparalleled warrior, but when he secretly entered the capital to target Kiyomori, he could not succeed and was eventually captured alive and executed. He was twenty years old. His second son Asanaga was wounded when fleeing after losing the battle in the capital, and died at Aohaka in Mino Province. His third son Yoritomo fled as far as Owari Province, where he was captured alive by the Taira warrior Munekiyo and brought to the capital. Although he should have been sentenced to death, through the petition of Kiyomori's stepmother, the nun Ike, he was exiled to Izu Province. Yoritomo was fourteen years old at the time.
