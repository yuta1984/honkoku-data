In the second month, the Emperor returned from Mount Hiei (Eisan). Once again, Masashige (Kusunoki Masashige) devised various clever stratagems and distinguished himself with meritorious deeds. In the same month, Yoshisada (Nitta Yoshisada), Akiie (Kitabatake Akiie), Masashige, and others pursued Takauji (Ashikaga Takauji), and a battle took place at Teshima in Sesshū Province. Takauji was defeated and fled to Tsukushi (Kyushu). Yoshisada returned to the capital and was appointed as Middle Captain of the Left (Saちゅūjō). Kikuchi Taketoshi raised troops from Kyushu and attacked Takauji, and a battle was fought at Tatara-no-hama in Chikuzen Province. Kikuchi was defeated, and Kyushu came under Takauji's control. At this time, Yoshisada became captivated by a beautiful woman known as Kōtō no Naishi, and while his departure to the western provinces was delayed, Akamatsu Enshin (Norimura) and the warriors of the western provinces all rallied to Takauji's side.

In the third month, Akiie was promoted to Middle Counselor (Chūnagon) and appointed Commander of the Pacification Headquarters (Chinjufu Shōgun), and set out once more for Ōshū (the northeastern provinces). Yoshisada was granted the governorship (kanryō) of the sixteen provinces of the San'yō and San'in regions and headed west, first attacking the castle of Akamatsu in Harima Province.

In the fourth month, the Retired Emperor Go-Fushimi passed away at the age of forty-nine. Before his death, he had secretly bestowed an imperial decree (inzen) upon Takauji.
