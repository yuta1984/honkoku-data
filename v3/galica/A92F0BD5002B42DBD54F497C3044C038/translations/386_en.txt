In the second month, Munemori was promoted to Junior First Rank and resigned from the position of Minister of the Interior. Tokudaiji Sanesada became Minister of the Interior. In the third month, Noriyori and Yoshinaka became antagonistic and were about to engage in battle. Yoshinaka sent his eldest son, Shimizu Kanja Yoshitaka, as a hostage and sought reconciliation, so Yoritomo accepted this and returned to Kamakura, making Yoshitaka his son-in-law. In the fourth month, Taira no Koremori and Michimori served as generals, with Tadanori, Tsunemasa, Kiyofusa, and Tomonori as vice-generals, leading 100,000 horsemen toward the northern provinces to attack Yoshinaka.

In the fifth month, the Taira forces and Yoshinaka fought in the northern provinces. Though it is said that the Taira won the battle at Hiuchi Castle in Echizen, in the battles at Tonami Mountain and Kurikara Valley in Etchu, Shiho Mountain, and Shinohara in Kaga, Yoshinaka was victorious each time. As a result, Tomonori and many Taira retainers were killed, their forces reduced to merely 20,000 horsemen, and Koremori and the others returned to the capital.
