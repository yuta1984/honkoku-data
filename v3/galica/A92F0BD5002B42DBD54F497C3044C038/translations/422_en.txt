In the third month, Hōjō Tokimasa was appointed as Tōtōmi no kami (governor of Tōtōmi). In the seventh month, Kazanin Sadamasanokami (Left Minister) Kanemasa passed away at age fifty-three. In the tenth month, Yoriie was promoted to Junior Third Rank and appointed as Saemon no kami (Chief of the Left Palace Guards). In the eleventh month, a warrior of Ōmi Province named Kashiwabara Yasaburō defied imperial orders, so Yoriie commanded warriors to execute him. Around the same time, a man called Shibata of Ōshū Province rebelled. Yoriie dispatched warriors to execute him. In the first month of Kennin 1 (1201), the Emperor (Tsuchimikado) made an imperial visit to the Retired Emperor's palace for morning audience, and Oyama Tomomasa, the protector of Kyoto, and Sasaki Sadatsuna also attended. At this time, a man called Jō Nagashige attacked Tomomasa's residence. When Tomomasa's retainers defeated him, Nagashige rushed into the Sentō Palace and petitioned to "destroy Kamakura." Since there was no imperial permission, he fled. Tomomasa searched for him, captured him at Yoshino, and killed him.
