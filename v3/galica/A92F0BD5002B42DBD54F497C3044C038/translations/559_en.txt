After that, [the Hōjō clan] perished seven years later. [Takatoki] died at the age of thirty-one. From the fourth year of Jishō when Yoritomo entered Kamakura until this year, there were nine generations of shoguns and eight generations of Hōjō regents, totaling one hundred fifty-four years. This emperor (Go-Daigo) reigned for only two years. The era name was Shōkyō.

Emperor Go-Daigo's restoration to the throne occurred in the fifth month of the second year of Shōkyō. When Takauji, along with Tadaaki, Enshin, and others reported from their ship that Rokuhara had been captured, Go-Daigo immediately entered the capital. At Mount Shosha in Harima Province, Yoshisada reported the matter of Takatoki's destruction. Kusunoki Masashige welcomed the emperor at Hyōgo. In the sixth month, [the emperor] arrived in Kyoto. Takauji was appointed as Chinjufu Shogun, appointed to Jibu-kyō, and granted the court rank of Junior Fourth Rank Lower Grade. Naoyoshi was appointed as Sama-no-kami. Takauji was ten generations descended from Minamoto no Yoshiie and was the great-great-great-great-great-great grandson of Ashikaga Sama-no-kami Yoshiuji. Although he was of the main Seiwa Genji lineage, because his family had maintained close relations with the Hōjō clan through marriage alliances for generations, his landholdings were extensive.
