His head was severed and sent to the capital, where it was exposed at the prison gate. In the tenth month, Retired Empress Jōtōmon'in made a pilgrimage to Hachiman and Sumiyoshi shrines.

In the sixth year, eleventh month, there was a celebration for the seventieth birthday of Minamoto no Rinshi, holder of the Senior First Rank. She was Michinaga's wife and mother of Retired Empress Jōtōmon'in, the Empress Consort, and Yorimichi, making her both the emperor's maternal grandmother and aunt.

In the seventh year, ninth month, Ōnakatomi no Sukechika went as imperial envoy to Ise Shrine. He found a blue jewel within pine nuts and returned to the capital to present it.

In the eighth year, sixth month, Senshi, the Imperial Princess who served as Kamo Priestess, passed away at age seventy-two. She had been very close to Retired Empress Jōtōmon'in.

In the ninth year, fourth month, seventeenth day, the Emperor passed away at age twenty-nine. Middle Counselor Minamoto no Akimoto, being a close retainer, mourned deeply and became a monk at Ōhara. In the same year, ninth month, Empress Consort Ishi also passed away. The era name changed from Kannin year four to Chian.
