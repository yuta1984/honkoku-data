Not only did four people - father, sons, and brothers - hold positions in the kuaimon (highest court ranks), but at this time, among the four current dainagon (major counselors), Yoshinobu and Nagaie were Yorimichi's younger brothers. Minamoto no Morofusa was his brother-in-law (his sister's husband). Nobuie was Yorimichi's nephew and Noritoshi's son.

In the eleventh month of the fourth year, they celebrated Yorimichi's seventieth birthday. In the twelfth month, Yorimichi was appointed as daijō-daijin (chancellor). In the seventh month, there was an imperial visit to Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine and Kamo Shrine.

In the spring of the fifth year, since Minamoto no Yoriyoshi's term as governor of Mutsu Province had ended, Takashina no Tsuneshige was appointed as governor and sent down to the province. However, fearing Sadato's power, and moreover because all the warriors in the province followed Yoriyoshi, Tsuneshige returned to the capital. In the seventh month of the same year, Kiyohara no Takenori, a resident of Senpoku in Dewa Province, gathered ten thousand soldiers and reinforced Yoriyoshi.
