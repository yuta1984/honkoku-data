In the twelfth month, Munetaka summoned Minamoto no Chikayuki, the Governor of Kawachi, and had him lecture on *The Tale of Genji* (*Genji Monogatari*). Since all matters of governance were handled at Tokiyori's discretion, Munetaka passed his days in the pursuits of Japanese poetry (waka) and kemari (court football).

In the third month of the seventh year, there was an imperial pilgrimage to Kumano. The Inspector of the Three Mountains (Sanza Kengyō), the Dharma Prince Kakujin, served as the leading guide (sendachi). This was the first time a prince of the blood served as a leading guide.

In the third month of the first year of Kōgen, Hōjō Shigetoki, the Governor of Mutsu, resigned from his post. His younger brother Masamura took his place and co-signed documents together with Tokiyori. In the fourth month, Hōjō Nagatoki resigned from his post at the Rokuhara and returned to Kamakura. His younger brother Tokishige went up to the capital (Kyoto) to take his place. He was twenty-seven years old at the time. Both men were sons of Shigetoki.
