A man named Takehaniasu-hiko rebelled and attacked the capital, but the imperial army fought against him and Takehaniasu-hiko perished. Since the nearby provinces were already pacified, he had Prince Toyoki no Mikoto govern the eastern provinces. He granted the office of Ōmuraji to a retainer named Takemoro-wake no Mikoto and had him administer the government. An envoy came from the country of Mimana and presented tribute. This country was probably within the Three Han kingdoms. This marks the beginning of foreign countries offering tribute. According to one theory, the person who came from Mimana had horns on his forehead and arrived by ship at Kehi-no-ura in Echizen. Therefore, that place was named Tsunoga. Kehi is present-day Kehi. Tsunoga is present-day Tsuruga. He reigned for sixty-eight years before passing away at age one hundred and twenty.

The Eleventh Emperor
Emperor Suinin - He was the crown prince of Sujin. His mother was called Mimaki-hime, daughter of Ōhiko no Mikoto. He established his capital at Makimuku in Yamato province and resided at Tamaki Palace. From the country of Silla
