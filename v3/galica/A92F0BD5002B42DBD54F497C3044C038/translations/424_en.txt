The Retired Emperor was fond of waka poetry and was most accomplished in that art. Originally, during Emperor Daigo's Engi era, he commanded Ki no Tsurayuki and others to compile the Kokin-shū. Later, during Emperor Murakami's reign, five people - Ōnakatomi no Yoshinobu, Minamoto no Shitagau, Kiyohara no Motosuke, Ki no Tokifumi, and Sakanoue no Mochiki - were commanded to compile the Gosen-shū. During Emperor Ichijō's time, Fujiwara no Kintō compiled the Shūi-shū. During Emperor Shirakawa's time, Fujiwara no Michitoshi compiled the Goshūi-shū. During Emperor Sutoku's time, Minamoto no Toshiyori compiled the Kin'yō-shū. During Emperor Konoe's time, Fujiwara no Akisuke compiled the Shika-shū. During Emperor Go-Shirakawa's time, Fujiwara no Shunzei compiled the Senzai-shū. Now again, the Retired Emperor commanded Minamoto no Michitomo, Fujiwara no Arie, Fujiwara no Teika, Fujiwara no Ietaka, and Fujiwara no Masatsune to compile the Shin Kokin-shū. From the Kokin-shū to this point, they are called the Eight Imperial Anthologies (Hachidai-shū).
