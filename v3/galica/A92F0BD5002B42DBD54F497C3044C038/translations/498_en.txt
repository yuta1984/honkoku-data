In the fifth month of the tenth year (of Bun'ei, 1273), Takatsukasa Mototada resigned as regent (kampaku). Kujō Tadaie became regent. In the same month, Hōjō Masamura died at the age of sixty-nine. He was the fourth son of Yoshitoki. In the sixth month, Tokimune had Hōjō Yoshimasa affix his seal as co-signatory of the shogunal regency (shikken). He was Masamura's replacement. This Yoshimasa was the fourth son of Shigetoki. In the eighth month, the former Minister of the Left of Yamashina, Fujiwara no Saneo, passed away at the age of fifty-seven. He was the father-in-law of both the reigning Emperor (Kameyama) and the Retired Emperor (Go-Fukakusa), and the maternal grandfather of (the future Emperors) Uda and Fushimi. Furthermore, being a member of the Saionji family, he wielded great influence in the world of his day. In this same year, the Mongol envoy Zhao Liangbi came to Japan. He was permitted to enter neither the capital nor Kamakura, and was turned back from Dazaifu.

In the first month of the eleventh year (of Bun'ei, 1274), the reigning Emperor (Kameyama), at the age of twenty-six, abdicated in favor of his son Yohito. His reign spanned the eras of Bun'ō (one year), Kōchō (three years), and Bun'ei (eleven years), totaling fifteen years on the throne.
