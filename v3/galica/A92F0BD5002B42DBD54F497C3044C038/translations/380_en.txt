He boarded a ship and crossed to Awa. At sea, he encountered Yoshizumi, Yoshimori and others rowing. Through this, he gathered the forces of Awa Province and proceeded to Shimousa in the ninth month. Chiba no suke Tsunetane brought his entire clan and followed him. He sent Hōjō Tokimasa to Kai Province to win over the Genji clans of that province. At the banks of the Sumida River, Kazusa no suke Hirotsune led twenty thousand cavalry and had an audience with Yoritomo. Yoritomo was angry at his late arrival and made him wait in the rear guard. Hirotsune originally intended to test Yoritomo, but sensing his grave dignity and capacity as a ruler, he submitted wholeheartedly. In the tenth month, when they arrived in Musashi, the warriors throughout the province submitted. Hatakeyama Shigetada also surrendered. After this, Yoritomo, on Tsunetane's recommendation, entered Sagami Province and resided in Kamakura. This place was the former site of his ancestor Minamoto no Yoriyoshi. All eight provinces of the Kantō submitted and revered him as Kamakura-dono. Around this time, Kiso Kanja Minamoto no Yoshinaka rose up from Shinano, passed through Kōzuke, proceeded to Echigo, and headed north...
