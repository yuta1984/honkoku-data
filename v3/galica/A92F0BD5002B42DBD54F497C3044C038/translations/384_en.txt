[Yoshihiro] gathered troops from Mutsu and Shimotsuke provinces and rebelled against Yoritomo. Oyama Tomomasa defeated him. In the third month, [the Heike] made Shigehira the general and sent him eastward. At the Sunomata River in Owari Province, he fought with Yukiie, and the Heike gained victory. Minamoto no Gien was killed in battle. This was Yoshitsune's elder brother by the same mother. In the fifth month, Yoritomo built Tsurugaoka Wakamiya shrine.

In the sixth month, Jōnosuke Chō raised an army to attack Kiso Yoshinaka, but Chō suddenly died at the time of departure. In the seventh month, Munemori sent his retainer Sadayoshi to Tsukushi to pacify the rebellion. In the eighth month, through the Heike's machinations, [the court] issued an imperial decree to Fujiwara no Hidehira, the governor of Mutsu, to attack Yoritomo, but Hidehira did not comply.

In the second month of Juei 1 (1182), Yoritomo dedicated sacred treasures to Ise Grand Shrine. In the sixth month, Regent Motomichi resigned from the position of Minister of the Interior. In the eighth month, Yoritomo's eldest son Yoriie was born. His mother was Taira no Masako, daughter of Hōjō Tokimasa.
