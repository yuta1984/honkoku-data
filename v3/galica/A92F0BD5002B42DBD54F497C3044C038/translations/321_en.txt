...there was a man [named Fujiwara no Tsunekiyo]. He was a descendant of Hidesato. His son was called Kiyohira. Tsunekiyo was executed at the same time as Sadatō. His wife married a man called Arakawa Tarō Taketada. Kiyohira also became Taketada's son and inherited his position. Taketada was probably a younger brother of Takenori. Alternatively, it is said that Takenori seized Tsunekiyo's wife and she bore Iehira, making Kiyohira and Iehira half-brothers of different fathers. After Takenori's death, there was a dispute between Takehira and Iehira on one side and Kiyohira on the other, creating discord. Under these circumstances, around the second year of Eihō (1082), Yoshiie was appointed as Chinjufu-shōgun and governor of Mutsu and went down [to the region]. Kiyohira came out to meet him. Takehira also had no objections. Iehira was in Dewa and did not submit. When Yoshiie tried to enter Dewa Province, Iehira prevented this and would not let him in. Yoshiie returned to Mutsu for a while.
