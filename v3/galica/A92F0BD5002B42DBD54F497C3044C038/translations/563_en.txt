In the second month of the second year [of Kenmu, 1335], the Minister of the Left (Sadaijin) of the Nijō clan, Michihira, passed away. He was forty-nine years of age. Fuyunori of the Takatsukasa clan, the Minister of the Right, was appointed Minister of the Left. The former Minister of the Interior (Naidaijin) Kinkata of the Tōin clan was appointed Minister of the Right. Sadafusa of the Yoshida clan resigned from the post of Minister of the Interior. Tsunemichi, Major Counselor (Dainagon) of the Ichijō clan, was appointed Minister of the Interior. A "dragon horse" was presented to the court from Izumo Province.

In the third month, Middle Counselor (Chūnagon) Fujifusa renounced the world (and entered religious life). The Major Counselor (Dainagon) Kinmune of the Saionji clan, together with Eishō, the younger brother of Hōjō Takatoki, conspired to raise a rebellion. Eishō returned to lay life and took the name Tokioki. Takatoki's son Tokiyuki raised troops in the Kantō, and his kinsman Namikoshi Tokikane raised troops in the northern provinces. When the plot was uncovered, Kinmune was executed.

In the seventh month, Tokiyuki marched from Shinano Province and attacked Kamakura. Tadayoshi, escorting Prince Nariyoshi (Narinaga), fled Kamakura. At this time, (Tadayoshi) dispatched a messenger and had Prince Moriyoshi (Morinaga) killed. Takauji, in response to an imperial edict, (set out to subjugate Tokiyuki...)
