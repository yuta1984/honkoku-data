In the fifth month of Kenji 2 (1276), Michimasa, Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin) of the Kazanin clan, passed away. He was forty-four years of age. In the twelfth month, Regent Kanehira was reappointed as Grand Minister of State.

This year, Mongol envoys arrived in Nagato Province but were summoned to Kamakura and beheaded.

In the first month of Kenji 3 (1277), the Emperor (later Emperor Kameyama's imperial son, Emperor Gouda) came of age in the genpuku ceremony. He was eleven years old. Kanehira performed the kakan (crowning) role. The head chamberlain (Tō no Chūjō) Tomoaki served as the hairdresser (rihats) for the ceremony.

In the same month, there was an imperial visit (gyōkō) to the Retired Emperor Kameyama for the chōkin (filial audience) ceremony. In the third month, there was an imperial visit to Iwashimizu [Hachimangū]. In the fourth month, there was an imperial visit to Kamo [Shrine].

In the same month, Kanehira resigned as Grand Minister of State. In the fifth month, Hōjō Yoshimasa resigned from his role as co-signatory regent (shikken kahan), shaved his head, and retired to Shiota in Shinano Province. Tokimune assumed sole authority (ichihan), issuing directives on all matters great and small.

In the twelfth month, Crown Prince Hirohito came of age in the genpuku ceremony. The Left Minister of State Morotada of the Nijō clan, who served as the Crown Prince's guardian (Tōgū no Fu), performed the kakan (crowning) role. Minamoto no Tomomori, the Master of the Crown Prince's Household (Tōgū no Daibu), served as hairdresser.

In the first month of Kōan 1 (1278), Hōjō Tokimura went up to the capital and assumed charge of the northern division of the Rokuhara [Tandai]. (He was the son of Masamura.)

In the twelfth month, Kanehira resigned as Regent and became Chancellor (Kanpaku).
