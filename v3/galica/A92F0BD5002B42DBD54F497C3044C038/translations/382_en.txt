As [the Genji forces] grew ever larger, Koremori, Tadanori and others, fearing Yoritomo's military might, fled to Fukuhara without engaging in battle. With this momentum, Yoritomo pacified Suruga and Tōtōmi provinces and returned to Kamakura. At that time, at the post station of Kisegawa, Kurō Yoshitsune came from Ōshū and met with Yoritomo. Ōba no Kagechika also came out as a surrendered person, but was executed. Itō no Sukechika was captured alive and entrusted to Miura no suke Yoshizumi. This Sukechika had harbored resentment from the time when Yoritomo was an exile. However, since he was Yoshizumi's father-in-law, he was pardoned after years had passed, but Sukechika committed suicide. Others who had been enemies at the Battle of Ishibashiyama were mostly pardoned.

In the eleventh month, Yoritomo went to Hitachi Province and killed Satake Hideyoshi. Returning to Kamakura, he made Wada no Yoshimori the administrator (bettō) of the samurai office (samurai-dokoro). In the twelfth month, Kiyomori moved the capital back from Fukuhara to Heian-kyō. The Emperor, Retired Emperor, and Cloistered Emperor all returned to the old capital.
