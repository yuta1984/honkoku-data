Wakasa-no-kami Tsunetoshi, Kurōdo-no-taifu Narimori, and Taifu Atsumori were all killed in battle. Sanmi-chūjō Shigehira was taken alive as a prisoner. The number of other retainers who died is unknown. According to one account, Noto-no-kami Noritsune was also killed in this battle.

Munemori, Tomomori, Norimori, Tsunemori and others barely escaped and, accompanied by the former emperor (Emperor Antoku), Kenreimon-in, and the Nun of Second Rank, headed for Yashima. Noriyori and Yoshitsune returned to the capital, reported to the Cloistered Emperor, and sent Shigehira to the east (Kamakura).

In the third month, Taira Koremori left Yashima, visited Kōya and Kumano, and threw himself into the sea at Nachi, dying at the age of twenty-seven. Alternatively, it is said that he hid deep in the mountains and lived out his natural life.

In the same month, Yoritomo was promoted to Junior Fourth Rank Upper Grade. In the fourth month, Yoritomo killed Shimizu-no-kanja Yoshitaka. His wife was Yoritomo's daughter. Being virtuous and faithful, she never remarried. In the fifth month, Taira Yorimori went to Kamakura and met with Yoritomo. He was granted many estates and returned to the capital. In the sixth month, Minamoto Noriyori was appointed as Mikawa-no-kami.
