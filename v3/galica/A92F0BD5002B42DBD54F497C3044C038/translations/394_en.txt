Wakasa no Kami Tsunetoshi, Kurōdo no Taifu Narimori, and Taifu Atsumori were all killed in battle. Sanmi no Chūjō Shigehira was taken alive as prisoner. The number of other retainers who died is unknown. According to one account, Noto no Kami Noritsune was also killed in this battle. Munemori, Tomomori, Norimori, Tsunemori and others barely escaped and, accompanying the former Emperor (Antoku), Kenreimon'in, and Nii no Zenni, headed to Yashima. Noriyori and Yoshitsune returned to the capital, reported to the Cloistered Emperor, and sent Shigehira to the east (Kamakura). In the third month, Taira no Koremori left Yashima, visited Kōya and Kumano, and threw himself into the sea at Nachi, dying at the age of twenty-seven. Alternatively, it is said he hid deep in the mountains and lived out his natural life.

In the same month, Yoritomo was appointed to the court rank of Jōshii (Senior Fourth Rank). In the fourth month, Yoritomo killed Shimizu no Kanja Yoshitaka. His wife was Yoritomo's daughter, who remained chaste and never remarried. In the fifth month, Taira no Yorimori went to Kamakura, met with Yoritomo, was granted many estates, and returned to the capital. In the sixth month, Minamoto no Noriyori was appointed as Mikawa no Kami (Governor of Mikawa).
