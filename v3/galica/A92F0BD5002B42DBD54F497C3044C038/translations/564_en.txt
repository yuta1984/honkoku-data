[Takauji went] to the eastern provinces for the purpose of pursuit and subjugation. According to one theory, it was at this time that he was granted the character "taka" (尊). Takauji met Tadayoshi on his way up from the capital in Tōtōmi Province, and they traveled together to Kamakura.

In the eighth month, fighting more than ten battles across Tōtōmi, Suruga, Izu, and Sagami provinces, Tokiyuki was defeated and his whereabouts became unknown. Tokikane perished in the northern provinces. From this point on, all the warriors of the eastern provinces submitted to Takauji. Takauji styled himself Seii Taishōgun (Barbarian-Subduing Great General). Due to his long-standing rivalry with Nitta Yoshisada over military accomplishments and their resulting discord, he petitioned to attack Yoshisada. Yoshisada, in turn, petitioned that Takauji harbored treasonous intentions. At this time, it became clear that Tadayoshi had arbitrarily killed Prince Morinaga, so the sovereign flew into a rage and ordered Yoshisada to attack Takauji.

In the eleventh month, Takatsukasa Fuyunori resigned from the position of Left Minister. Right Minister Konoe Tsunetada was appointed as Left Minister. In the same month, Yoshisada left the capital for an eastern campaign. First Prince Takayoshi was appointed as deputy (kanrei) for the eastern provinces and likewise departed for the east.
