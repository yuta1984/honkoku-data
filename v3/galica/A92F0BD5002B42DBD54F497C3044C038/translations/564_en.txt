[Takauji] proceeded to the eastern provinces for the purpose of subjugation. According to one theory, it was at this time that he was granted the character "Taka" (尊). Takauji met Tadayoshi coming up [from Kamakura] in Tōtōmi Province, and they traveled together to Kamakura.

In the eighth month, fighting more than ten battles in the areas of Tōtōmi, Suruga, Izu, and Sagami provinces, Tokiyuki was defeated and his whereabouts became unknown. Tokikane perished in the northern provinces. From this point on, all the warriors of the Kantō region submitted to Takauji. Takauji proclaimed himself Seii Taishogun (Barbarian-Subduing Generalissimo). Having long competed for merit with Nitta Yoshisada and being at odds with him, [Takauji] petitioned to attack Yoshisada. Yoshisada also petitioned that Takauji harbored treasonous intentions. At this time, when it became clear that Tadayoshi had arbitrarily killed Prince Morinaga, the sovereign was enraged and had Yoshisada attack Takauji.

In the eleventh month, Takatsukasa Fuyunori resigned from the position of Left Minister. Right Minister Konoe Tsunetada was appointed as Left Minister. In the same month, Yoshisada left the capital and marched eastward. First Prince Takayoshi was appointed as supervisor (kanrei) of the eastern provinces and likewise proceeded eastward.
