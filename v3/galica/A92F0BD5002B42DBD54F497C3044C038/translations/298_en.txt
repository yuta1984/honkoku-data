In the sixth year, Yorimichi built Byōdō-in temple in Uji. This year, a barbarian rebel in Mutsu Province named Abe no Yoriyoshi raised a rebellion and plundered throughout the province, so Minamoto no Yoriyoshi was appointed Governor of Mutsu and concurrently Commander-in-Chief of the Defense Headquarters, and was sent on an eastern expedition. Yoriyoshi was the grandson of Mitsunaka and son of Yorinobu. Since Yoriyoshi had already achieved military merit from the time when Yorinobu defeated Tadatsune, all the warriors of the Kantō region held him in high regard. When Yoriyoshi entered Mutsu Province, Yoriyoshi became frightened and surrendered, and the province quickly became pacified. However, when Yoriyoshi's son Sadatō violated the law and was about to be punished, Yoriyoshi became angry and together with Sadatō withdrew to Koromo River fortress, refusing to obey Yoriyoshi. Because of this, [Yoriyoshi] gathered an army of ten thousand men, besieged Koromo River, and fighting continued without end.
