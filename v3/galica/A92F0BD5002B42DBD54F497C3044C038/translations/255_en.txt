Consequently, Tadafumi became enraged, harboring resentment toward Saneyori, and declared that the rewards should be ceded to Morosuke; thereupon he starved himself to death. It is transmitted that, owing to his vengeful spirit, the descendants of Saneyori declined, while the descendants of Morosuke flourished — yet, given that several years had passed since Masakado's defeat when Tadafumi died at over seventy years of age, what the common people say in this regard seems doubtful.

From the eighth month onward, smallpox spread throughout the realm, and offerings (hōhei) were made to various shrines; sutras were also read and prayers offered. In the ninth month, a shrine (byō) dedicated to Chancellor Sugawara (Michizane) was erected at Kitano. In the eleventh month, an imperial excursion (gyōkō) was made to Uji for hunting.

In the second year (Tenryaku 2, 948), there was a great drought in summer and heavy rains in autumn. On the twenty-fourth day of the eighth month, the sun and moon were seen side by side.

In the third year (Tenryaku 3, 949), in the first month, Grand Minister of State (Daijō Daijin) Tadahira retired from office (chiji) on account of illness. Saneyori and Morosuke jointly administered the affairs of government. On the fourteenth day of the eighth month, Tadahira passed away. He was seventy years of age. He was posthumously awarded the Senior First Rank (Shō Ichii). He was enfeoffed as the Lord of Shinano (Shinano-kō), and was given the posthumous name (okurina) Teishin-kō.
