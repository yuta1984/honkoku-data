There was [an imperial visit]. In the third month, Ochi no Taichō died. This person was the one who opened Mount Hakusan in Echizen.

In the seventh month, the monk Shōdō first opened Mount Futaara in Shimotsuke Province. This is Mount Nikkō.

In the tenth month of the second year, Kashiwade no Omi Ōoka, Assistant Professor of the Imperial University, made a memorial stating: "The Emperor of Great Tang honors Confucius with the posthumous title of King Bunsen (Wénxuān). Therefore, in Japan as well, following that example, we request to address him as King Bunsen." This was then granted imperial permission.

In the eleventh month, Kasuga Shrine was first established on Mount Mikasa in Yamato Province, enshrining Takeikazuchi no Mikoto, Ame no Koyane no Mikoto, Iwahinushi no Mikoto, and Hime-Ōkami.

In the first month of the third year, Dōkyō resided in the western palace of the imperial court. All officials from ministers down attended court.

In the second month, there was an imperial visit to the residence of Left Minister Nagate. There was also an imperial visit to the residence of Right Minister Kibi. In the fifth month, Princess Fuwa, the Emperor's sister, was the wife of Prince Shioyaki. After Prince Shioyaki was killed, she had secret discussions with his son Hikami no Shikeshi Maro, and because it was revealed that they had cursed the Emperor, the Princess was [banished] from the capital.
