In the second year, first month, there was an imperial visit to pay respects to Retired Empress Jōtōmon'in. In the winter of the same year, Myōson, the high priest of Miidera, was made Tendai head abbot.

In the second month of the third year, the monk-soldiers of Mount Hiei submitted a petition to Yorimichi, arguing that "Myōson belongs to the Chishō school lineage. Since he is not of the Jikaku faction, he should not be appointed as head abbot." Yorimichi replied that "regardless of which school lineage, it should depend on the person." The mountain monks became angry and came in great numbers to Yorimichi's residence, staging a forceful appeal and breaking down the gate pillars. Yorimichi became angry and had Taira no Naomata defend against the mountain monks. They fought each other, and there were many casualties. They captured Jōsei, the ringleader of the mountain monks, and imprisoned him. In the fifth month, Retired Empress Jōtōmon'in took Buddhist vows, with Myōson serving as her ordination master. On the twenty-second day of the eighth month, imperial messengers for offerings to the twenty-two shrines were appointed. The twenty-two shrines are: Ise, Iwashimizu, Upper and Lower Kamo, Matsunoo, Hirano, Inari, Kasuga, Ōharano, Ōmiwa, Isonokami, Yamato, Hirose, Tatsuta, Umenomiya, Yoshida, Hirota, Gion, Kitano, and Niū.
