Ono no Yoshifuru, Fujiwara no Yoshiyuki, Ōkura no Harusane and others were appointed as generals and departed for Iyo Province leading more than 200 warships. Official documents were also issued to both the Tōkaidō and Tōsandō circuits, announcing that rewards would be given for military achievements. On the first day of the second month, Fujiwara no Hidesato, the military governor (ōryōshi) of Shimotsuke, and Taira no Sadamori, the secretary (jō) of Hitachi, gathered forces from Mutsu and Shimotsuke provinces and led 19,000 men in battle against Masakado in Shimotsuke Province. Several hundred of Masakado's soldiers were killed and he retreated. Sadamori and Hidesato pursued him and arrived in Shimōsa Province on the thirteenth day. Masakado fortified himself at Mount Shimahiro. Sadamori set fire to and burned the houses of Masakado and his followers. On the fourteenth day, Masakado came out himself and fought at a place called Karashima. An arrow shot by Sadamori struck Masakado and he fell from his horse.
