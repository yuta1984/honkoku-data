(Fujiwara no Tadafumi was appointed Seii Taishōgun,) and his younger brother Fujiwara no Tadanobu, together with Minamoto no Tsunemoto and others, were appointed as vice-generals and dispatched to the Kantō region. Ono no Yoshifuru, Fujiwara no Yoshiyuki, and Ōkura no Hanemori were appointed as generals, leading a fleet of over two hundred warships and setting out toward Iyo Province. Furthermore, official documents (kanpu) were issued to both the Tōkai and Tōsan circuits, announcing that those who rendered military merit would be rewarded accordingly.

On the first day of the second month, Fujiwara no Hidesato, the Ōryōshi (military commissioner) of Shimotsuke Province, and Taira no Sadamori, the assistant official (jō) of Hitachi Province, mustered the forces of Mutsu and Shimotsuke, leading nineteen thousand men, and engaged Masakado in battle in Shimotsuke Province. Several hundred of Masakado's troops were struck down and retreated. Sadamori and Hidesato pursued them, and on the thirteenth day arrived in Shimōsa Province. Masakado took refuge in the mountains of Shimahiro. Sadamori set fire to the dwellings of Masakado and his followers. On the fourteenth day, Masakado himself came out and fought at a place called Karashima. An arrow shot by Sadamori struck Masakado and he fell from his horse.
