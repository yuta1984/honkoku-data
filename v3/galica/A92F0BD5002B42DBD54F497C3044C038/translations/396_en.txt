They were unable to set sail. Yoshitsune led only five vessels, braving the wind and waves, and unexpectedly arrived at Yashima, setting fire to the imperial palace. Munemori and others took the former emperor and fled to the sea. They had Noritsune defend against Yoshitsune. Although Minamoto warriors including Satō Tsuginobu, Kamada Mitsumasa and others were shot and killed by Noritsune, the Taira were ultimately defeated and retreated to Nagato. The Taira samurai general Tauchi Saemon Noritaka was attacking Kōno in Iyo Province, but Yoshitsune pursued and forced him to surrender. Yoshitsune finally pacified Shikoku and advanced toward Nagato. The Taira attempted to flee to the nine provinces of Kyushu. Noriyori was in Bungo, blocking their path. On the twenty-fourth day of the third month, Yoshitsune arrived at Akamagaseki and fought with the Taira. Awa Minbu Shigenō, who had been loyal to the Taira for many years, suddenly surrendered to the Minamoto at this time. Due to this, the Taira were defeated. The Ni-no-Ama (Second Rank Nun) held the sacred mirror, wore the sacred sword, embraced the former emperor, and entered the sea.
