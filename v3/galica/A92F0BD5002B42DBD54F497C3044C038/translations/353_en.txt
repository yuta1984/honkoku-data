It was an unprecedented event where relatives and blood kin fought against each other. The authority of the imperial court (ōbō) gradually declined from this time. Since Fujiwara no Nakanari was executed during Emperor Saga's reign, no death sentences had been carried out, but this time many were sentenced to death at Shinzei's recommendation. Shinzei possessed both Japanese and Chinese learning and was a scholar of great erudition. Though he had long remained in obscurity, he was promoted by the current emperor (Go-Shirakawa) and served close to the throne, conducting governmental affairs. Due to their military merits in this campaign, Yoshitomo was appointed to the position of Samanotō (Head of the Left Horse Bureau), and Kiyomori became Governor of Harima Province. The leadership of the Fujiwara clan was restored to Regent Tadamichi as before. In the ninth month, Inner Minister Saneyoshi was appointed Left Minister, and Major Counselor Fujiwara no Munesuke was appointed Right Minister. Major Counselor Fujiwara no Koremichi was appointed Inner Minister. As the realm had already been pacified, the Emperor began to conduct government personally. Following the precedent of Emperor Go-Sanjō, he established a Records Office (Kirokusho) to adjudicate lawsuits.
