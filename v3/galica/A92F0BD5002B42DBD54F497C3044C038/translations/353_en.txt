Relatives and family members fought against each other - this was unprecedented. The authority of imperial law (ōbō) gradually declined from this point onward. Since the time of Emperor Saga, when Fujiwara no Nakanari was executed, there had been no death sentences carried out, but this time many were sentenced to death due to Shinzei's recommendations. Shinzei possessed talent in both Japanese and Chinese learning and was a scholar of great erudition. Having long remained in obscurity, he was promoted by the current emperor (Go-Shirakawa) and served close to the imperial presence, conducting governmental affairs. Due to his military achievements in this campaign, Yoshitomo was appointed as Samanosuke (Chief of the Left Division of Bureau of Horses) and Kiyomori became Governor of Harima Province. The position of head of the Fujiwara clan was granted to Regent Tadamichi as before. In the ninth month, Interior Minister Sanenō was appointed Left Minister. Major Counselor Fujiwara no Munesuke was appointed Right Minister. Major Counselor Fujiwara no Koremichi was appointed Interior Minister. As the realm had already been pacified, the Emperor personally attended to governmental affairs. Following the precedent of Emperor Go-Sanjō, he established a Records Office (Kirokusho) to adjudicate lawsuits.
