Go-Fushimi-in: His personal name (imina) was Tanenari; he was the Crown Prince of the Fushimi-in. His mother was the Empress Consort Eifukumon'in, daughter of Saionji Sadaijin (Chancellor) Sanekane. In truth, he was born of Keishi, the daughter of Consultant (Saisho) Fujiwara no Tsuneuji, but was raised by the Empress Consort and installed as Crown Prince.

In the seventh month of Einin 6 (1298), he ascended to the throne at the age of eleven. Takatsukasa Kanpaku (Imperial Regent) Kanetada served as regent (sesshō). At this time, the retired emperors Go-Fukakusa, Kameyama, Go-Uda, and Fushimi were all still living, and there were four imperial retired residences (in no gosho). In the eighth month, Kuniteru (Kuniharu), son of the Retired Emperor Go-Uda, was installed as Crown Prince (Tōgū). He was the Emperor's second cousin. In the twelfth month, Kanetada retired from the regency, and Nijō Sadaijin (Minister of the Left) Kanemoto became regent.

In the first year of Shōan (1299), fourth month, Regent Kanemoto resigned from the position of Safu (Minister of the Left). Kujō Moronori transferred to the position of Minister of the Left, and Saionji Kinhira was appointed Ufu (Minister of the Right). Takatsukasa Dainagon (Senior Counselor) Fuyuhira was appointed Nafu (Minister of the Center). In the sixth month, the former Nafu Tōin Kinmori was appointed Daijō-daijin (Grand Minister of State).
