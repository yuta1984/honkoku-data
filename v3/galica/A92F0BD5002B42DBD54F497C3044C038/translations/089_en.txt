The capital was moved to Toyosaki in Nagara, and a new imperial palace was constructed. On New Year's Day of Taika 2, the ceremony of court officials paying their respects was held. For the first time, governors were appointed in the capital region and various provinces, checkpoints and post stations were established, mountains and rivers were demarcated, the size of districts was determined, chiefs were appointed to villages, and matters including the number of households and people, annual tribute and local products, military equipment and horse gear were all considered and decided. From places with one hundred households, one uneme (court lady) each was to be provided. Uneme refers to selecting suitable women from those places to serve at the palace. Envoys were sent to various provinces to evaluate the good and bad conduct of provincial governors and reward or punish them accordingly. Storehouses were also built in various provinces to stockpile military equipment. Right Minister Soga no Kurayamada Maro, receiving imperial orders, commanded the court officials to present their counsel. Besides these, many court ceremonies were established at this time. This was likely discussed between Crown Prince Naka no Ōe and Inner Minister Nakatomi no Kamatari. In Taika 5, Left Minister Abe no Kurahashi Maro passed away.
