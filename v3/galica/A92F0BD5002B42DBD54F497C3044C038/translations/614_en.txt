Court nobles (gekkyō and unkaku) also attended in the retinue. In the eighth month, Kujō Tadamoto resigned as kampaku (regent). Nijō Morotsugu became kampaku. In the ninth month, Yoshimitsu issued a mikyōsho (shogunal directive) proclaiming that the monk Yoriyuki, known in retirement as Tsunehisa, should be put to death. However, perhaps because Yoriyuki was without guilt, he was pardoned and remained in his home provinces as the sōkan (general administrator) of the four provinces of Awa, Awaji, Sanuki, and Iyo.

In the first month of the second year (of Kōryaku, 1380), the monk Myōha was granted the title of Kokushi (National Teacher) and was appointed to the post of Sōroku-shi (Administrator of Monks). The office of Sōroku is said to have begun from this time. In the same month, Yoshimitsu was elevated to Junior First Rank (Jūichii). In the second month, Yoshimitsu's younger brother Mitsunori was appointed Samanosuke (Head of the Left Stables). Ujimitsu of Kamakura was appointed Sahyōe no Kami (Commander of the Left Imperial Guards). In the fifth month, in the Kantō region, Oyama Shimotsuke no Kami Yoshimasa and Utsunomiya Shimotsuke no Zenji Motozuna fought a battle, in which Motozuna was killed. To pursue and suppress the Oyama clan, Ujimitsu dispatched Uesugi Norimasa. In the same month, Ōuchi Shinsuke fought a battle in Seishū (Ise Province) against his younger brother Saburō, and more than two hundred people were killed.
