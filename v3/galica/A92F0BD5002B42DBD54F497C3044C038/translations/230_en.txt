This was because when the Emperor was still a chamberlain, the matter of his future enthronement had been announced in advance to this deity. In the same month, Mototsune was permitted to ride in a palanquin when entering and leaving the palace, and Minamoto no Tōru was permitted to ride in an imperial carriage.

In the 2nd year, on the 15th day of the 1st month, it was established as a regular practice to offer rice porridge with seven herbs. In the 11th month, Mototsune fell ill, and the Emperor made an imperial visit to comfort him. Chishō from Miidera came and performed ritual prayers.

In the 3rd year, on the 13th day of the 1st month, Regent and Grand Minister Fujiwara no Mototsune passed away at the age of fifty-six. He was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Echizen, and given the posthumous name Shōsen-kō.
