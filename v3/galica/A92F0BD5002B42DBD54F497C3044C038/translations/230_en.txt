He would break into the residences of court officials, or enter the mountains to hunt wild boar and deer. In the eleventh month, the Kamo Special Festival was performed for the first time. This was because when the Emperor was still a chamberlain, the matter of his future enthronement had been reported in advance to this deity. In the same month, Mototsune was permitted to ride in a palanquin when entering and leaving the imperial palace, and Minamoto no Tōru was permitted to ride in an imperial carriage.

In the second year, on the fifteenth day of the first month, it was established as a regular practice to present seven-herb rice porridge. In the eleventh month, Mototsune fell ill, and the Emperor made an imperial visit to comfort him. Chishō from Miidera came and performed ritual prayers.

In the third year, on the thirteenth day of the first month, Chancellor and Grand Minister Fujiwara no Mototsune died at the age of fifty-six. He was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Echizen, and given the posthumous name Shōsen-kō. In the third month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Yoshiyo became Right Minister. Tokihira, the eldest son of Shōsen-kō, was appointed as Councilor. In the tenth month, Chishō passed away. Mii[dera]...
