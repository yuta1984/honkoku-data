In the eleventh month, the Kamo Rinji-sai (Special Kamo Festival) was held for the first time. This was because when the Emperor was still a chamberlain, the intention for him to ascend to the throne had been reported in advance to this deity. In the same month, Mototsune was permitted to ride in a waist palanquin when entering and leaving the imperial palace, and Minamoto no Tōru was permitted to ride in a hand-cart.

In the second year, on the fifteenth day of the first month, it was established as a regular practice to offer seven-herb rice porridge. In the eleventh month, Mototsune fell ill, and the Emperor made an imperial visit to console him. Chishō from Mii-dera came and performed prayers for his recovery.

In the third year, on the thirteenth day of the first month, Regent and Grand Minister Fujiwara no Mototsune passed away at the age of fifty-six. He was posthumously granted the rank of Senior First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Echizen, and given the posthumous name Shōsen-kō. In the third month, Senior Counselor Fujiwara no Yoshiyo became Right Minister. Tokihira, the eldest son of Shōsen-kō, was appointed as Councilor. In the tenth month, Chishō passed away.
