In the twelfth month, at the retired emperor's Bureau of Japanese Poetry, a celebration for the ninetieth birthday of Fujiwara Shunzei Nyūdō Shakua was held. He was the pride of the house of Japanese poetry. The following year, Shakua passed away.

In the first year of Genkyū, first month, Sanetomo's reading ceremony (dokusho hajime) was held, using the Classic of Filial Piety. Nakahara Nakayoshi served as the reader. He was granted a sword and placer gold. In the second month, the retired emperor made a pilgrimage to Tennōji. In the third month, Sanetomo was appointed to the position of Minor Captain of the Right. In the fourth month, remnants of the Taira clan - Tomita Motonori and Miura Moritoki and others - plotted rebellion in Ise Province and captured Iga and Ise. The Kyoto protector, Musashi no Kami Minamoto no Asanaga, raised troops and killed Motonori, Moritoki and others, destroying their associates. Asanaga was Tokimasa's son-in-law. Particularly because his wife was a daughter of the Lady of Maki, he wielded considerable authority.
