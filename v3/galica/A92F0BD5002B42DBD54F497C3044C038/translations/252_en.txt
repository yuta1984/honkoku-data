In the fifth month of the fourth year, Ono no Yoshifuru and others engaged in battle with Sumitomo at Hakata Port in Chikuzen. Fujiwara no Yoshiyuki and Ōkura no Haruzane fought desperately, risking their lives, and set fire to burn the rebel ships. In the sixth month, Sumitomo was defeated in battle, and some of his followers either surrendered or fled and disappeared, so Sumitomo escaped by small boat back to Iyo Province. Tachibana no Tōyasu, who was stationed as a guard in that province, killed Sumitomo and his son Jūtamaro and sent their heads to the capital. It is also said that Sumitomo was captured alive and died in prison. In the eighth month, Ono no Yoshifuru returned to the capital. In the eleventh month, Tadahira resigned from the position of regent. By imperial decree, he was made kanpaku (chief advisor to the emperor), and it was ordered that all matters of government should first be entrusted to Tadahira before being reported to the emperor. This followed the precedent of Duke Shōsen.
