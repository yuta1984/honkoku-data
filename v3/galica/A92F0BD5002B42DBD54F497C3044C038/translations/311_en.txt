Minister Morozane and Interior Minister Minamoto no Morofusa concurrently held the positions of Left and Right Generals. Because the emperor was wise, he curtailed the power of the regent families for the first time and made decisions on all important matters of government himself. Moreover, he established a Records Office to hear complaints from the people and alleviate their sufferings. During the final years of Emperor Go-Reizei's reign, the world was not peaceful, but after this reign began, within less than a year, the government was conducted properly and all the people rejoiced.

In the 7th month of Enkyū 1 (1069), Kampaku Norimichi resigned from his position as Minister of the Left, Morozane was transferred to Minister of the Left, Minamoto no Morofusa was promoted to Minister of the Right, and Fujiwara no Nobunaga became Interior Minister. Nobunaga was Norimichi's son. In the 8th month, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu, Kamo, and Kasuga shrines. From this reign, for the Iwashimizu Hōjō-e (release of living beings ceremony), court officials such as sangi (counselors) and deputy commanders of the palace guards were dispatched for the first time. The ceremonies were conducted with great solemnity. In the same month, there was an imperial visit to pay respects to his mother, Empress Dowager Yōmei-mon'in.
