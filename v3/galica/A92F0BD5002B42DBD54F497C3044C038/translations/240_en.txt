Ōkura no Yoshiyuki received an imperial edict and compiled and presented the fifty-volume "True Records of the Three Reigns of Seiwa, Yōzei, and Kōkō." Yoshiyuki was Tokihira's teacher and had reached the age of seventy this year. In the twelfth month, the Cloistered Emperor received abhisheka at Tōji Temple. He built the Imperial Residence (Omuro) at Ninna-ji Temple. This was the beginning of the Omuro. The term "Monzeki" (Imperial Prince's Temple) that arose in later generations also originated from this. Since it was the place where Cloistered Emperor Uda resided, it means "the traces of the Imperial Gate."

In the second year, third month, there was a Fujiwara banquet at Hikōsha.

In the third year, second month, twenty-fifth day, Minister Kan died in Tsukushi at the age of fifty-nine.

In the fourth year, second month, Prince Yasuaki was installed as Crown Prince. He was two years old at the time. His mother was Fujiwara no Yasuko, Tokihira's younger sister.
