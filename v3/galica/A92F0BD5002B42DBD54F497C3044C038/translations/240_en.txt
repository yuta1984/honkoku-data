Ōkura no Yoshiyuki, following imperial command, compiled and presented the fifty-volume Jitsuroku (True Records) of the three reigns of Seiwa, Yōzei, and Kōkō. Yoshiyuki was Tokihira's teacher and reached the age of seventy this year. In the twelfth month, the Retired Emperor received abhisheka (esoteric Buddhist initiation) at Tōji Temple. The Omuro was built at Ninnaji Temple. This was the beginning of the Omuro. The later term "Monzeki" (imperial temple) also originated from this. Since it was the place where Retired Emperor Uda resided, it means "the trace of the imperial gate."

In the second year, third month, there was a Fujiwara banquet at Hikōsha.

In the third year, second month, twenty-fifth day, Minister Suga died in Kyushu at the age of fifty-nine.

In the fourth year, second month, Prince Yasuaki was made Crown Prince at the age of two. His mother was Fujiwara no Inshi, Tokihira's younger sister.

In the fifth year, first month, third day, there was an imperial visit to Ninnaji Temple. On the fourth day, there was a grand banquet at Tokihira's residence.
