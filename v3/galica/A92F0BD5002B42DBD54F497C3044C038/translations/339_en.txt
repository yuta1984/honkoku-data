In the first year of Tenshō (1131), in the seventh month, on the first anniversary of Shirakawa-in's death, the Hokke Hakkō (Eight Lectures on the Lotus Sutra) was held at Hōshōji Temple. In the twelfth month, Right Minister Ietada became Left Minister. Inner Minister Arito became Right Minister. Major Counselor Fujiwara no Munetada became Inner Minister. In the same month, former Kampaku Tadazane had an audience with Retired Emperor Toba. Perhaps because he had been at odds with Retired Emperor Shirakawa, after resigning from the position of kampaku, he had remained in seclusion for twelve years, and now for the first time he emerged. He was granted armed retainers and weapons. Although he was already advanced in age, thereafter he participated in government affairs, and discord arose between him and his son Tadamichi. His second son Yorinaga, who was twelve years old this year, he loved deeply.

In the first year of Chōshō (1132), in the first month, Tadazane received an imperial edict appointing him to nairan. In the third month, the retired emperor's votive temple Tokujujuin was established, a hall of thirty-three bays was built, and 1,001 Buddhist statues were installed. Because Taira no Tadamori served as the supervisor of this project, he was granted Tajima Province and permitted to ascend to the imperial presence.
