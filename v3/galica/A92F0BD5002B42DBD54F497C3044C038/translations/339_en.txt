In the first year of Tenshō (1131), in the seventh month, on the first anniversary of Shirakawa-in's death, the Eight Lectures on the Lotus Sutra were held at Hōshōji. In the twelfth month, Right Minister Ietada became Left Minister. Interior Minister Arito became Right Minister. Major Counselor Fujiwara no Munetada became Interior Minister. In the same month, former Kampaku Tadazane had an audience with Retired Emperor Toba. Perhaps because he had been at odds with Retired Emperor Shirakawa, after resigning as kampaku he had remained in seclusion for twelve years, and now appeared at court for the first time. He was granted bodyguards and weapons. Though he was already considered a court attendee, from this time forward he participated in government affairs. Relations between Tadazane and his son Tadamichi were strained. His second son Yorinaga, who was twelve years old this year, he loved greatly.

In the first year of Chōshō (1132), in the first month, Tadazane received an imperial decree appointing him to nairan. In the third month, Tokujuin, a temple built at the retired emperor's vow, was established. A thirty-three bay hall was erected and 1,001 Buddhist statues were installed. Because Taira no Tadamori served as the supervisor of this project, he was granted Tajima Province and permitted to ascend to the imperial palace.
