In the second year (1125), in the fifth month, Gyōson of Miidera was appointed to the position of Daisōjō (Great Monk Supervisor) and was permitted to use ox-drawn carriages. This monk became the first superintendent (kengyō) of the three sacred mountains of Kumano and became involved in matters related to mountain ascetics and Shugendō. In the tenth month, there were imperial visits to Iwashimizu and Kamo shrines. After this, there were also imperial visits to shrines such as Hirano, Ōharano, Matsunoo, Kitano, Inari, and Gion.

In the second year of Daiji (1127), in the second month, former Chancellor Kuga no Masazane died at the age of sixty-nine. In the tenth month, Retired Emperor Shirakawa and Retired Emperor Toba traveled together to Mount Kōya. In this year, Shinra Saburō Minamoto no Yoshimitsu died at the age of seventy-two. He inherited the military arts of archery and horsemanship from his elder brother Yoshiie and became the ancestor of a branch of the Minamoto clan. His descendants are numerous in the provinces of Kai, Shinano, Hitachi, and others. In this year, Minamoto no Tameyoshi was appointed as a police commissioner (kebiishi) and granted the court rank of Junior Fifth Rank Lower. Tameyoshi desired to be appointed as Governor of Mutsu.
