In the second year [of Kōho, 965], in the fourth month, the Minister of the Right (udaijin) Fujiwara no Akitada passed away. He was sixty-eight years old. In the twelfth month, a celebration was held for the Emperor's fortieth year.

In the third year [of Kōho, 966], in the first month, Minamoto no Takaakira was appointed Minister of the Right. He was a son of the Engi Emperor [Emperor Daigo]. In the eighth month, the Vinaya Master (ritsushi) Ryōgen became the Tendai Seat-Master (zasu, i.e., head abbot of the Tendai sect). This is the monk known as the Jie Sōjō.

In the fourth year [of Kōho, 967], on the twenty-fifth day of the fifth month, the Emperor passed away. He was forty-two years old. The reign spanned the eras of Tenryaku (ten years), Tentoku (four years), Ōwa (three years), and Kōho (four years), with a total reign of twenty-one years.

　　The Sixty-Third Sovereign
Emperor Reizei　The second imperial son of Emperor Murakami, his personal name (imina) was Norihira. His mother was the Empress Consort Yasuko, who was the daughter of the Minister of the Right Morosuke. He was born in the fifth month of the fourth year of Tenryaku [950], and became Crown Prince in the seventh month. From the second month of the fourth year of Kōho [967], he suffered from an illness caused by evil spirits (jaki), and his mental condition was not normal. In the fifth month, Emperor Murakami passed away, and the Crown Prince acceded to the throne at the Gyōkasha. He was ten years old [at the time of accession]...
