In the fourth month, when they fought the rebels again and the imperial army was defeated, in the fifth month, Fujiwara no Yasunori was dispatched to Dewa, and troops from nearby provinces were gathered to subjugate them. In the sixth month, Ono no Harukaze was sent to Ōshū as Chinjufu-shōgun (commander-in-chief of the defense headquarters) to gather troops. Forces were also sent from the provinces of the Tōkaidō region as reinforcement. In the seventh month, Yasunori arrived in Dewa and fought the rebels, achieving a small victory. However, the rebels still filled the lands of Tsugaru and other areas. In the ninth month, there was a great earthquake in the Kantō provinces.

In the third year, first month, the Emishi rebels of Dewa surrendered, and it was reported that the province was peaceful. In the fifth month, Retired Emperor Seiwa took Buddhist vows. In the tenth month, the Daigokuden was completed. A banquet was held.

In the fourth year, third month, the Retired Emperor toured the famous mountains and Buddhist temples of Yamashiro, Yamato, and Settsu provinces, and entered Mizunoo-dera temple in Tanba Province. He devoted his heart to Buddhism
