Therefore, in the fifth month, Fujiwara no Yasunori was dispatched to Dewa Province, where he was ordered to mobilize troops from neighboring provinces to subjugate the rebels. In the sixth month, Ono no Harukaze was sent to Ōshū as the Chinjufu-shōgun (Commander of the Pacification Headquarters) and ordered to mobilize troops. Reinforcements were also sent from the provinces along the Tōkaidō. In the seventh month, Yasunori arrived in Dewa and engaged the rebels, achieving a minor victory. However, the rebels still filled the regions of Tsugaru and surrounding areas. In the ninth month, a major earthquake struck the provinces of the Kantō region.

In the third year, first month, the Emishi rebels of Dewa Province surrendered, and it was reported that peace had returned throughout the province. In the fifth month, the Retired Emperor Seiwa took Buddhist vows. In the tenth month, the Daigokuden was completed, and a banquet was held.

In the fourth year, third month, the Retired Emperor toured the famous mountains and Buddhist temples of Yamashiro, Yamato, and Settsu Provinces, and entered Minoo-dera temple in Tanba Province. He devoted his heart to Buddhism...
