In the fourth month, when the imperial army was again defeated in battle with the rebels, in the fifth month, Fujiwara no Yasunori was dispatched to Dewa, and troops from neighboring provinces were assembled to subjugate them. In the sixth month, Ono no Harukaze was appointed as Chinjufu General and sent to Mutsu Province to muster troops. Reinforcements were also sent from the provinces of the Tōkaidō circuit. In the seventh month, Yasunori arrived in Dewa and fought the rebels, achieving small victories. However, the rebels still filled the lands of Tsugaru and other areas. In the ninth month, there was a great earthquake in the Kantō provinces.

In the first month of the third year, the Emishi rebels of Dewa surrendered, and it was reported that the province was peaceful. In the fifth month, Retired Emperor Seiwa took Buddhist vows. In the tenth month, the Daigokuden was completed, and a banquet was held.
