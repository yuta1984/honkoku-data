In the fourth month, the government forces again fought the rebels and were defeated, so in the fifth month, Fujiwara no Yasunori was dispatched to Dewa, and troops from neighboring provinces were summoned to subdue them. In the sixth month, Ono no Harukaze was sent to Ōshū as Chinjufu General to muster troops. Reinforcements were also sent from the provinces along the Tōkaidō. In the seventh month, Yasunori arrived in Dewa and fought the rebels, achieving a small victory. However, the rebels still filled the lands of Tsugaru and other areas. In the ninth month, there was a great earthquake in the Kantō provinces.

In the first month of the third year, the Emishi rebels of Dewa surrendered, and it was reported that the province was peaceful. In the fifth month, Retired Emperor Seiwa took Buddhist vows. In the tenth month, the Daigokuden was completed. A banquet was held.

In the third month of the fourth year, the Retired Emperor visited famous mountains and Buddhist temples in Yamashiro, Yamato, and Settsu provinces, and entered Mizunoo-dera temple in Tanba Province. Turning his heart to Buddhism
