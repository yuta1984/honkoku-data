In the seventh month, when Empress Dowager Someden fell ill, Sōō of Mount Hiei prayed for her recovery with efficacious results, so both Dengyō (Saichō) and Jikaku (Ennin) were posthumously granted the title of Daishi (Great Master). Sōō was a disciple of Jikaku. Jikaku passed away in the sixth year of Jōgan.

In the tenth month of the ninth year, Right Minister Yoshimi died.

In the twelfth month of the tenth year, Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto died. He was a person accomplished in scholarship, calligraphy and painting, music, horsemanship, and even falconry.

In the fourth month of the eleventh year, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Ujimune, along with Counselor Ōe no Otohito, Minister of Justice Sugawara no Koreyoshi and others respectfully compiled and presented the Jōgan Code. Otohito was also a person of great learning. The Sugawara and Ōe families stood alongside each other as hereditary masters of Confucian learning for generations. In the fifth month, there was a great earthquake in Ōshū, and over a thousand people died.
