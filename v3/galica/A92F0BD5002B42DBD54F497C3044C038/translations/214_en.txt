[Tomo no Yoshio was] exiled. His children and accomplices were all sentenced to exile as well. Mototsune was the adopted son of Yoshimi. He was the person who was later called Duke Shōsen. In the seventh month, when Empress Dowager Somedon fell ill, Sōō of Mount Hiei prayed for her and it proved efficacious, so both Dengyō (Saichō) and Jikaku (Ennin) were granted the posthumous title of Daishi (Great Master). Sōō was a disciple of Jikaku. Jikaku passed away in Jōgan 6.

In the ninth year, tenth month, Right Minister Yoshimi died.

In the tenth year, twelfth month, Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto died. He was a person accomplished in learning, calligraphy and painting, music, horsemanship, and even falconry.

In the eleventh year, fourth month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Ujimune, along with Middle Counselor Ōe no Otondo and Minister of Justice Sugawara no Koreyoshi, respectfully compiled and presented the Jōgan Codes. Otondo was also a person of great learning. The Kan house and Gō house stood side by side as hereditary masters of Confucian learning for generations. In the fifth month, there was a great earthquake in Ōshū, and over a thousand people died.
