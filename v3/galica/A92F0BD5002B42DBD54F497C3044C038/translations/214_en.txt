In the seventh month, when Empress Dowager Somedono fell ill, Sōō of Mount Hiei prayed and achieved miraculous results, whereupon Dengyō (Saichō) and Jikaku (Ennin) were both granted the posthumous title of Daishi (Great Master). Sōō was a disciple of Jikaku. Jikaku passed away in the sixth year of Jōgan.

In the ninth year, tenth month, Right Minister Yoshimi died.

In the tenth year, twelfth month, Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto died. He was a person accomplished in learning, calligraphy and painting, music, horsemanship, and even falconry.

In the eleventh year, fourth month, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Ujimune, along with Sangi Ōe no Otondo and Minister of Justice Sugawara no Koreyoshi, compiled and presented the Jōgan-kyaku (legal codes). Otondo was also a man of great learning. The Sugawara house (Kan-ke) and the Ōe house (Gō-ke) stood side by side as hereditary masters of Confucian studies for generations. In the fifth month, there was a great earthquake in Ōshū, and over a thousand people died.
