In the seventh month, Empress Dowager Somei fell ill, and when Sōō of Mount Hiei prayed for her recovery with efficacious results, both Dengyō (Saichō) and Jikaku (Ennin) were posthumously granted the title of Great Master. Sōō was a disciple of Jikaku. Jikaku had died in the sixth year of Jōgan.

In the tenth month of the ninth year, Right Minister Yoshimi died.

In the twelfth month of the tenth year, Left Minister Minamoto no Makoto died. He was a person accomplished in learning, calligraphy and painting, music, horsemanship, and even falconry.

In the fourth month of the eleventh year, Major Counselor Fujiwara no Ujimune, together with Sangi Ōe no Otondo and Minister of Justice Sugawara no Koreyoshi, received orders to compile and present the Jōgan-kyaku legal code. Otondo was also a person of great learning. The Sugawara house and the Ōe house stood side by side as hereditary masters of Confucian learning for generations. In the fifth month, there was a great earthquake in Ōshū, and more than a thousand people died.
