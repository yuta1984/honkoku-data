Although our country has had exemplary empresses since ancient times, some had short lifespans and could not witness their sons' reigns, while others outlived their imperial sons and regretted their longevity. Ōmiya Nyoin was the mother of three generations of emperors, having the current sovereign and Crown Prince as grandsons, with their mother still alive and the family prospering in wealth - this was indeed rare good fortune. At this celebration, the sovereign made a royal visit to Kitayama, both retired emperors also visited, and the Crown Prince attended, with various entertainments taking place.

In the fourth month, former Kanpaku Takatsukasa Mototada was appointed to the position of Daijō-daijin (Chancellor). His father Kanehira had been a former minister and was currently serving his second term as Kanpaku. Such simultaneous glory of father and son was unprecedented. In the same month, Hōjō Sadatoki was appointed Sagami-no-kami. Akita-jō-suke Yasumori wielded power as he pleased, borrowing the influence of his position as [Sadatoki's] maternal grandfather.
