From there they crossed into Ise Province, where the provincial governor Miyake no Muraji gathered five hundred soldiers and blocked Suzuka Pass. Tenmu's sons, Prince Takechi and Prince Ōtsu, who had all been in Ōmi, secretly escaped and joined Tenmu in the territories of Iga and Ise. At this time, Tenmu worshipped the Great Goddess Amaterasu from afar at the banks of the Ato River. Meanwhile, Murakuni no Oyori reported that he had mobilized three thousand soldiers from Mino Province and blocked Fuwa Pass, so Tenmu sent Prince Takechi to Fuwa to defend it. He also sent messengers to the Tōkaidō and Tōsandō routes to mobilize armies. Both Tenmu and Jitō rested temporarily in Kuwana District of Ise Province. According to popular accounts, Tenmu was pursued by Ōtomo's forces and fell into dire straits whether in Kusu of Yoshino Province, in Shima Province, or at Sunomata in Mino.
