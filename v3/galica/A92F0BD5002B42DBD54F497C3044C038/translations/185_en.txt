The Lotus Sutra was donated to Sai-ji Temple, and dharma talks were held. In the seventh month, Left Minister Fuyutsugu of the Second Rank died at the age of fifty-two. He was posthumously awarded the First Rank and given the title of Left Minister of Kan'in.

In the eleventh month, Kōbō Daishi petitioned the court and erected a pagoda at Tō-ji Temple.

In the fourth year, fifth month, imperial orders were given to Shigeno no Sadanushi to collect contemporary poetry and prose. This was called the "Keikoku-shū" and consisted of twenty volumes. Sadanushi was a person of great learning. During this period, both rulers and subjects favored poetry and prose, and there were many talented people.

In the fifth year, second month, Yoshimine no Yasuyo was appointed as Major Counselor. At the same time, Fujiwara no Mitsumori and Kiyohara no Natsuno were both appointed as Major Counselors and participated in government.

In the ninth month, Ono no Takamura became Senior Secretary. He was also a person of exceptional talent and intelligence.

In the sixth year, fifth month, Yoshimine no Yasuyo received imperial orders to have people in various provinces construct waterwheels as an aid to cultivation.
