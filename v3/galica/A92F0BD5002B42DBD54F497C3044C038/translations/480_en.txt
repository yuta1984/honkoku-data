In the third year of Kenchō [1251], in the seventh month, Yoritsugu was granted the Junior Third Rank (Jushō-i) and appointed as the Middle Captain of the Left (Sachūjō). Tokiyori was granted the Senior Fifth Rank, Lower Grade (Shōgoi-no-ge). These were rewards for the construction of the Kan'in Imperial Palace. In the twelfth month, Sasaki Ujinori, Mutō Kagetora, and others captured alive a man called the monk Ryōgyō and presented him to Tokiyori. Upon interrogation, it was revealed that the former Shogun Yoritsugu had a plot to throw the realm into disorder while in Kyoto. As a result, all of his associates who were in the Kantō were punished.

In the fourth year of Kenchō [1252], in the second month, Tokiyori and Shigetoki dispatched a messenger to Kyoto, and requested that the first prince of the Retired Emperor Go-Saga, Prince Munetaka, be received and made the lord of Kamakura. In the same month, the former Regent of Kōmyōbuji, Michiie, passed away at the age of sixty-one. Because this man was the father of Yoritsugu, he had been highly regarded even by the warrior houses during the time of Yoshitoki and Yasutoki, and his authority was very nearly that of an emperor. However, after Yoritsugu went up to the capital [Kyoto, i.e., after being sent back], he harbored a grudge against the Hōjō clan and had reportedly been in communication with Miura no Mitsumura as well.
