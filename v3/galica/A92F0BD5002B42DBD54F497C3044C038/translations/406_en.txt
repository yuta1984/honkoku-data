There were various gifts presented to both the Emperor and the Retired Emperor. Afterwards Yoritomo made a pilgrimage to Iwashimizu shrine and offered swords and horses. At this time, Kazanin Right Minister Kanemasa resigned as Right General, and Yoritomo was made to concurrently hold the position of Right General. In the twelfth month, Yoritomo made formal visits to the palace and retired emperor's court, resigned from Major Counselor and Right General, and returned to Kamakura.

In the first month of the second year, there was an arrangement of Yoritomo's governmental affairs. Ōe no Hiromoto became head (bettō) of the Administrative Board (Mandokoro), Fujiwara no Yukimasa became assistant administrator, Kamata no Toshinage became secretary (anju), Nakahara no Mitsuke became house administrator (chikaji), Miyoshi no Zennobu became secretary of the Judicial Board (Monchūjo), Wada Yoshimori became head of the Board of Retainers (Samuraidokoro), and Kajiwara Kagetoki became director of the Board of Retainers. Fujiwara no Chikayoshi, Fujiwara no Toshikane, Miyoshi no Yasukiyo, Miyoshi no Nobuhira, Taira no Moritoki, Nakahara no Nakanari, and Kiyohara no Sanetoshi were made administrators of public affairs. In addition, the protector of Kyoto was Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Yasuyasu. The administrator of Chinzei was Amano Tōkage. In the second month, Yasuyasu became head of the police commissioners (kebiishi)
