There were various gifts presented to both His Majesty and the Retired Emperor. Afterwards, Yoritomo made a pilgrimage to Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine and offered swords and horses. At this time, Kazanoin Minister of the Right Kanemasa resigned from the position of Major Captain of the Right, and Yoritomo was made to concurrently hold the position of Major Captain of the Right. In the twelfth month, Yoritomo made formal visits to the Imperial Palace and the Retired Emperor's palace, resigned from Major Counselor and Major Captain of the Right, and returned to Kamakura.

In the first month of the second year [of Kenkyū], Yoritomo established governmental administrative procedures. Ōe no Hiromoto became the director (bettō) of the Administrative Board (Mandokoro), Fujiwara no Gyōsei became the decree officer and document authenticator, Kamada Toshinage became the chief clerk (anshu), Nakahara no Mitsuke became the household administrator (chikaji), Miyoshi Zenshin became the executive (shitsuji) of the Judicial Board (Monchūjo), Wada Yoshimori became the director of the Samurai Office (Samuraidokoro), and Kajiwara Kagetoki became the administrator of the Samurai Office. Fujiwara no Chikayoshi, Fujiwara no Toshikane, Miyoshi Yasukiyo, Miyoshi Nobuhira, Taira no Moritoki, Nakahara no Nakanari, and Kiyohara no Sanetoshi were made public affairs administrators (kuji bugyō). In addition, the protector (shugo) of Kyoto was Middle Counselor Fujiwara no Yoshiyasu. The administrator of Chinzei [Kyushu] was Amano Tōkage. In the second month, Yoshiyasu became head of the Imperial Police (kebiishi)
