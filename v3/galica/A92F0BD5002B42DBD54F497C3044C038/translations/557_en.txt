They coordinated with Uraryōchū and others and destroyed Rokuhara by assault. On the eighth day, both Rokuhara deputies Nakatoki and Masumasu took with them the new emperor as well as Retired Emperors Go-Fushimi and Hanazono, left Kyoto and headed for the Kantō region. Masumasu was struck by a stray arrow and died. When they reached Banba in Ōmi Province, enemies had already blocked the road, so Nakatoki and his followers all committed suicide. The new emperor and both retired emperors were all captured and returned to Kyoto. The attacking forces at Chihaya also withdrew and fled to Nanto.

On the eighth day of the same month, Nitta Yoshisada raised a righteous army in Kōzuke Province and attacked Kamakura. Takatoki had his younger brother Eshō and others defend against this. There were several battles in Musashi Province. Since all the military forces of Kantō had turned against Takatoki, Eshō was finally defeated and returned to Kamakura. Yoshisada subsequently attacked Kamakura. Hōjō Moritoki and Hōjō Motoki committed suicide. Moritoki was the grandson of Nagatoki and was called Akahashi. Takauji's wife was Moritoki's younger sister.
