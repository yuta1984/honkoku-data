On the eighth day, the two Rokuhara (commanders) Nakatoki and Masumasu took the new emperor along with Retired Emperors Go-Fushimi and Hanazono and left Kyoto for the Kantō region. Masumasu was struck by a stray arrow and died. When they reached Banba in Ōmi Province, enemies had already blocked the road, so Nakatoki and all his followers committed suicide. The new emperor and both retired emperors were all captured and returned to Kyoto. Chigusa (Tadaaki) and the attacking forces also retreated and fled to Nanto.

On the eighth day of the same month, Nitta Yoshisada raised a righteous army in Kōzuke Province and attacked Kamakura. Takatoki had his younger brother Eshō and others defend against this. They fought several battles in Musashi Province, but when it was heard that all of Kamakura's military forces had turned against Takatoki, Eshō was finally defeated and returned to Kamakura. Yoshisada continued to attack and entered Kamakura. Hōjō Moritoki and Hōjō Motoki committed suicide. Moritoki was the grandson of Nagatoki and was called Akahashi. Takauji's wife was Moritoki's younger sister. Motoki was (a member of the clan of) Nakatoki of Rokuhara.
