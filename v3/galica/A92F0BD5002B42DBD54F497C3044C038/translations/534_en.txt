In the eleventh month of the third year of Shōwa [1314], Hōjō Sadaaki returned from Rokuhara to Kamakura.

In the third month of the fourth year [of Shōwa, 1315], Minamoto no Tokomori resigned as Naidaijin. Tōin Dainagon Saneyasu was appointed Naidaijin. In the seventh month, Hōjō Hirotoki died. Hōjō Mototoki and Hōjō Sadaaki served as Shikken and Rensho respectively. Mototoki was the grandson of Nariatoki. Sadaaki was the great-grandson of a man called Saneyasu, the fifth son of Yoshitoki. Saneyasu's son, the Governor of Echigo, Sanetoki, resided in Kanazawa and took the name Shōmyōji. His son was called the Governor of Echigo, Akitoki, and was the father of Sadaaki. Since they all lived in Kanazawa, the family name came to be called Kanazawa. Within the precincts of Shōmyōji, a library (bunko) was established and Japanese and Chinese books were collected. Books were stamped with a seal reading "Kanazawa Bunko." Black seals were used for Confucian works, and vermilion seals for Buddhist works. These historic remains are transmitted to this day.

In the ninth month, Takatsukasa Minister of the Left Fuyuhira became Kanpaku. In the same month, Hōjō Koresada went up to the capital, [taking charge of] the southern sector of Rokuhara...
