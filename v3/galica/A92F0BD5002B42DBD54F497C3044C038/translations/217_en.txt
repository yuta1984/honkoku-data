He was posthumously granted Senior First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Mino, and given the posthumous name Chūjin-kō. Although Minamoto no Tōru and [Fujiwara no] Mototsune served as Left and Right Ministers respectively and conducted government affairs, the authority and power rested entirely with Mototsune.

In the second month of the sixteenth year, a new prayer hall was built and named Jōgan-ji Temple. A great ritual assembly was established there. In the fourth month, Jun'na-in Palace burned down. The fire spread and reached the Imperial Palace. Mototsune and others hurriedly came to prevent the fire, and it was quickly extinguished. Jun'na-in was the place where Emperor Jun'na resided after he abdicated the throne.

In the first month of the seventeenth year, Reizen-in Palace burned down. This was Emperor Saga's retirement residence. All the documents stored there and other treasures were destroyed. A man named Ōhara no Ohiro died in the flames while trying to fight the fire. In the fourth month, the Emperor read the Five Classics, the Records of the Grand Historian, and the Essentials of Government from Various Books. Sugawara no Koreyoshi, Sugano no Sukuyo, Ōe no Otondo and others instructed him.
