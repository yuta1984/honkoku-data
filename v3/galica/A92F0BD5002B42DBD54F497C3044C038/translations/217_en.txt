In the ninth month, second day, Grand Minister of State Senior First Rank Fujiwara no Yoshifusa passed away at the age of sixty-nine. He was posthumously granted Junior First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Mino, and given the posthumous name Chūjin-kō. Although Minamoto no Tōru and [Fujiwara no] Mototsune served as Left and Right Ministers respectively and administered the government, the authority and power rested entirely with Mototsune.

In the second month of the sixteenth year, a new prayer hall was built and named Jōgan-ji Temple. A great Buddhist ceremony was held. In the fourth month, Junna-in Palace burned down. The fire spread and reached the Imperial Palace. Mototsune and others rushed to the scene and had the fire suppressed, and it was quickly extinguished. Junna-in was the place where Emperor Junna resided after abdicating the throne.

In the first month of the seventeenth year, Reizen-in Palace burned down. This was Emperor Saga's retirement residence. The documents stored there and all other treasures were destroyed. A man named Ōhara no Ohiro died in the flames while trying to fight the fire. In the fourth month, the Emperor studied the Five Classics, Records of the Grand Historian, and the Qunshu Zhiyao. Sugawara no Koreyoshi, Sugano no Sukuyo, and Ōe no Otondo and others served as his instructors.
