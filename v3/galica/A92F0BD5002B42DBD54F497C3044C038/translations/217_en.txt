He was posthumously granted Supreme First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Mino, and given the posthumous name Chūjin-kō. Although Minamoto no Tōru and Mototsune served as Left and Right Ministers to conduct government, the authority was exclusively held by Mototsune.

In the sixteenth year, second month, a new Buddhist hall was built and named Jōgan-ji Temple. A great Buddhist ceremony was held. In the fourth month, the Junna-in Palace burned down. The fire spread and reached the Imperial Palace. Mototsune and others hurriedly came to fight the fire and it was quickly extinguished. The Junna-in was the place where Emperor Junna resided after he abdicated the throne.

In the seventeenth year, first month, the Reizen-in Palace burned down. This was the retirement residence of Emperor Saga. All the documents stored there and other treasures were destroyed. A person named Ōhara no Ohiro died fighting the fire. In the fourth month, the Emperor read the Five Classics, Records of the Grand Historian, and the Essentials of Government from Various Books. Sugawara no Koreyoshi, Sugano no Sukuyo, Ōe no Otohito and others respectfully instructed him.
