In the ninth month, second day, Grand Minister of State Fujiwara no Yoshifusa of Senior First Rank died at the age of sixty-nine. He was posthumously granted Senior First Rank, enfeoffed as Duke of Mino, and given the posthumous name Chūjin-kō. Although Minamoto no Tōru and Mototsune served as Left and Right Ministers executing government affairs, authority rested exclusively with Mototsune.

In the sixteenth year, second month, a new temple hall was built and named Jōgan-ji Temple. A great Buddhist service was held. In the fourth month, Junna-in Palace burned down. The fire spread to the Imperial Palace. Mototsune and others hurried to the palace and had the fire suppressed, and it was quickly extinguished. Junna-in was the residence where Emperor Junna lived after his abdication.

In the seventeenth year, first month, Reizen-in Palace burned down. This was Emperor Saga's retirement residence. All the documents stored there and other treasures were destroyed. A man named Ōhara no Ohiro died in the flames while trying to fight the fire. In the fourth month, the Emperor studied the Five Classics, Records of the Grand Historian, and Essential Techniques of Government. Sugawara no Koreyoshi, Sugano no Sukeyoshi, Ōe no Otondo and others provided instruction.
