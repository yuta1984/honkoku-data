Furthermore, he kept fish, birds, and insects, and planted grasses and flowers in numbers beyond counting. He was called the Left Minister of Kawara. In the 10th month, Minister Kan was appointed to Middle Counselor.

In the 8th year, on the 6th day of the 1st month, there was an imperial visit to Unrin-in, where the Ne-no-hi festivities were held. Imperial princes and court nobles attended. In the 7th month, Fujiwara no Yoshiyo became Left Minister. Minamoto no Yoshiari became Right Minister. In the 9th month, the Nijō Empress (Seiwa's empress, Yōzei's mother) was discovered to have had an illicit affair with the monk Zen'yū of Tōkōji Temple. The empress lost her position, and Zen'yū was exiled to Izu. The empress was fifty-five years old at this time. In the 12th month, Left Minister Yoshiyo retired from office at the age of seventy-four. Right Minister Yoshiari took charge of government. Yoshiari was an imperial prince of Montoku and was skilled in the martial arts of archery and horsemanship. Prince Sadazumi, the ancestor of the Minamoto family, was this person's son-in-law and inherited the martial arts tradition.
