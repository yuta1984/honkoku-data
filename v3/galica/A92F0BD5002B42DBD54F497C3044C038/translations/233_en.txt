In the tenth month, Minister Sugawara was appointed to the position of Middle Counselor.

In the eighth year, on the sixth day of the first month, there was an imperial visit to Unrin-in Temple, where the Ne-no-hi festivities took place. Imperial princes and court nobles attended. In the seventh month, Fujiwara no Yoshiyo became Left Minister, and Minamoto no Yoshiari became Right Minister. In the ninth month, it was revealed that Empress Nijō (Empress of Emperor Seiwa, mother of Emperor Yōzei) had been having an illicit affair with the monk Zen'yū of Tōkō-ji Temple. The Empress was stripped of her position, and Zen'yū was exiled to Izu. The Empress was fifty-five years old at this time. In the twelfth month, Left Minister Yoshiyo retired from office at the age of seventy-four. Right Minister Yoshiari took control of government. Yoshiari was a son of Emperor Montoku and was accomplished in the martial arts of archery and horsemanship. Prince Sadazumi, an ancestor of the Minamoto clan, was this person's son-in-law and inherited the martial arts traditions.
