In the first year of Chōkyū (1040), ninth month, the imperial palace burned down. The sacred mirror was burned. However, since it still manifested light, the ashes were collected and enshrined. The Emperor moved to Tōhoku-in. This palace was built by Retired Empress Jōtōmon'in and is located beside Hōjō-ji temple.

In the second year, third month, fourth day, there was a flower banquet. Literary men presented poetry and their talents were tested. This was modeled after the Chinese examination system and had been conducted annually since the time of Emperors Saga and Junna, continuing without interruption until this time.

In the third year, third month, the daughter of Major Counselor Minamoto no Morofusa became a consort. Morofusa was the grandson of Emperor Murakami, son of Prince Tomohira, and son-in-law of Michinaga. This family is called the Murakami Genji and belongs to the Seiga nobility. They are the ancestors of the present-day Kuga and Nakain families.
