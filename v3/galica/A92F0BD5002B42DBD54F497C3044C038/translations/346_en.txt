In the sixth month, Regent Tadamichi adopted Teishi, daughter of Major Counselor Fujiwara no Koremichi, and had her enter the court as Chūgū (Second Empress). There was already discord between the brothers Tadamichi and Yorinaga, but from this point they competed even more fiercely for power. However, the Emperor was always close to the Chūgū and distant from the Empress. In the eighth month, Minister of the Right Sanesuke (Saneyuki) was appointed Chancellor. Minister of the Interior Masasada was appointed Minister of the Right. Major Counselor Sanenō was appointed Minister of the Interior. Empress Tashi was Sanenō's granddaughter (marginal note: Kinnō was Sanenō's son). In the ninth month, Tadamichi became clan leader. In the twelfth month, Tadamichi resigned as Regent and became Kampaku (Chief Advisor to the Emperor). This year, Minamoto no Yoshikuni went down to his villa in Ashikaga in Shimotsuke Province. He was the third son of Yoshiie and the ancestor of both the Nitta and Ashikaga families.

In the first month of the first year of Ninpei, Minister of the Left Yorinaga was granted armed attendants, became clan leader, and took charge of affairs of the Great Council of State. Later he received an imperial decree for nairan privileges. Ujinochōja (clan leader) refers to the head of the Fujiwara clan. Nairan refers to the privilege of reviewing official documents before they are presented to the Emperor.
