The former Minister of the Interior, Saneshige, was appointed Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin). The former Minister of the Right of the Tōin family, Saneya­su, was appointed Minister of the Left. Kazanin's Major Counselor (Dainagon), Iesada, was appointed Minister of the Right. Ichijō's Major Counselor, Uchitsune, was appointed Minister of the Interior. In the twelfth month, through the arrangement of the Kantō (Kamakura shogunate), Nijō Michihira stepped down as Regent, and Ichijō Uchitsune became the new Regent.

In the first month of Gennō 1 (1319), Hōjō Takatoki was appointed Acting Master of the Office of Palace Repairs (Shurinokon-no-daibū). In the fourth month, Iesada resigned from the post of Minister of the Right. In the sixth month, Regent Uchitsune resigned from the post of Minister of the Interior. Major Counselor Minamoto no Arihusa was appointed Minister of the Right. In the seventh month, Arihusa passed away. Kujō's Major Counselor Fusazane was appointed Minister of the Right, and Major Counselor Minamoto no Michishige was appointed Minister of the Interior. In the eighth month, Jushi, daughter of Saionji Sanekane, the former Grand Minister of State, became Chūgū (second consort of the Emperor). Renshi, daughter of Middle Captain of the Imperial Guard Ano Fujiwara no Kinkado, entered the palace in attendance upon the Chūgū. The Emperor greatly favored Renshi, and she was given the title "Sanmi no Tsubone" (Lady of the Third Rank). She later became Jun-daijō Tennō (Honorary Retired Empress). Beyond these, there were many ladies-in-waiting at court, and the Emperor fathered numerous imperial princes and princesses by various mothers.
