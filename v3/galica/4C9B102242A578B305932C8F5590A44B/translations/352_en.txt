Kamome Island (Seagull Island) ≪Located between Matsushima and Hirashima, a single giant rock stands upright, protruding above the water surface. Seagulls constantly gather on top of it, hence the name. It is also called Togame Island (Angry Turtle Island)≫

Ki Island ≪Located north of Shinojima, with no houses, only grasses and trees flourishing≫

Tsukumi Island ≪Located east of Shinojima, with many pine trees. All of the above islands have precipitous cliffs and are extremely difficult to approach by boat. Therefore, it is said that few people climb them≫

Nezumi Island ≪Located north of Himakajima, with particularly many pine trees≫

Ōiso Island ≪Located east of Himakajima, consisting only of rocks. At high tide, only its peak remains visible≫

Koiso Island ≪Located north of Shinojima, with very luxuriant vegetation≫

Washiwatari Island, Tobisaki Island, Naichi Island ≪All three islands are located north of Himakajima. They are hidden at high tide and emerge at low tide≫

Ebisu Island ≪A single rock located on the coast of Morosaki, with a solitary pine tree on top≫

Nakaten Island ≪Located between Tsukumi Island and Koiso Island, with no houses, only vegetation≫

Byōbu Island ≪Located west of Hantōsaki, also without any dwellings≫

The "Fubokushō" mentions that there is a Motome Island in Owari Province, and the "Matsuba-shū," "Aki-nezame," and other works also list "Motome Island" as a famous place in Owari.
