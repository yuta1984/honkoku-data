Ōhara Family: The same book records that they are descendants of the Baekje King, who was a grandson of Emperor Bidatsu.

Nagaoka Family: The same book and the Kōin Shōunroku show that Nagaoka Okanari, a prince of Emperor Kanmu, was granted the family name Nagaoka Asomi in Enryaku 6.

Isobe Family: They are descendants mentioned in the Shinsen Shōjiroku as "Isobe-no-omi are descendants of Prince Honyawake-no-mikoto, son of Emperor Chūai."

Hayashi Family: They are descendants mentioned in the same book as "Hayashi Asomi are descendants of Takenouchi-no-sukune."

Matsuoka Family: They were military commanders who accompanied Prince Yamato Takeru on his eastern expedition. Though the national histories omit their names, their descendants became shrine officials and took the name Matsuoka-no-mahito.

Mikuni Family, Wakayama Family, Kagami Family, Kikuta Family, Minematsu Family: All serve various shrine duties respectively. The types of their duties are extremely numerous and cannot be recorded here.

Kizuyama Jingūji Daiyakushi: Located in Katamachi, west of the bridge, 25 chō outside Kaizō Gate. This temple was built by imperial edict of Emperor Ninmyō and was founded by both great masters Dengyō and Kōbō. It served as the temple office for the shrine monks of Nyohōin, as can be seen in the Daijōkan proclamation of Jōwa 14, 3rd month, 7th day, making it no ordinary sacred precinct. However, it fell into decline and ruin due to years of military conflicts, so Right Minister Toyotomi Hideyori restored it, constructing a tile-roofed Buddha hall with a 10-ken beam and 13.5-ken length during the Keichō period. But this too fell into decay, remaining without resident monks for many years and receiving no repairs.
