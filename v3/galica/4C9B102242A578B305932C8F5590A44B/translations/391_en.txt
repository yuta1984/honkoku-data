Mount Mannen Sōshinji Temple (located in Tokoname Village, Sōtō sect, branch temple of Enjōji in Hora Village, Mino Province) was founded in the first year of Genna (1615) by the nun Kaei Sōshin (the nun was the daughter of Mizuno Shimotsukenomori and the second wife of Mizuno Kenbutsu). The founding abbot was Priest Tengai (Tengai was the son of the nun Sōshin).
The temple lands were donated by Lord Ieyasu. Additionally, in the mountain behind the temple, the thirty-three sacred sites of the Saigoku pilgrimage have been recreated with Kannon statues installed. ○Main image (wooden statue of Shakyamuni) Sacred treasures (personal letter from Lord Ieyasu, poem by Lord Daiyūin (Tokugawa Iemitsu), letter from Lord Nobunaga, Jizō statue made by Kōbō Daishi, etc.)

Mount Hōko Tensaku-in (in the same village, Sōtō sect, branch temple of Kenkon-in in Ogawa Village) The founding abbot of this temple was Priest Shūtei, but the exact date of founding is unknown. ○Main image (Eleven-faced Kannon)

Tokoname Castle Site (in the same village, the residential castle of Mizuno Kenbutsu Naomori, commonly called Tokoname-den. Now it has become rice fields. In Sōboku's "Tōgoku Kikō" it records "hurrying to Tokoname, whereupon a messenger came from Mizuno Kenbutsu-no-jō," and in Sōchō's notes it mentions "Tokoname Mizuno Kisaburō's lodging." That Kisaburō was Naomori's childhood name)

Mount Ryūshō Shōjū-in (located in Hōjō Village, Jōdo sect, branch temple of Jōrakuji in Nariwa Village) Founded during the Entoku era (1489-1492) by Master Kūkan Eikaku
