Long bridges, short bridges, willows drooping at the water's edge where herons roost, ripples stirring where fish leap. Everything that meets the eye serves as poetic material. This is unlike wandering through the busy marketplace.

Moonlit Night at Biwa Bridge, Returning Drunk　　　　　　Okuda Tōen
The bridge divides two districts, twin dragon shadows. The water carries an island within, white silk light. Returning drunk on foot, the coolness can be cupped in one's hands. In the cricket's song, the moon faintly illuminates the flowers.
