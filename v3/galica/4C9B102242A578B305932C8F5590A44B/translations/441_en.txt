Eleventh day, Dinghei, clear weather. Today there was a stay at Kayazu post station, due to the lingering effects of last night's indisposition. Afterwards, the floating bridges of both rivers were repaired (and so forth).

In the "Jōō Kaidō-ki" it appears: "The dim moonlight appeared, and when the people at the inn had settled down, I spread a grass pillow and stayed at Kayazu inn." In "Meisho Hōgaku-shō" it appears: "Kayazu Plain is about one ri south of Orizu. It is commonly called 'Kaizu inn'" - thus it is an ancient post station.

Long ago, the sea route descending eastward from Kyoto passed through Sunomata River, as well as Tamai, Kuroda, Ichinomiya, Shimotsu, and Kayazu, then proceeded to Furuwatari, Atsuta, Narumi, and from there passed the foot of Futamura Mountain to reach Yatsuhashi in Mikawa.
