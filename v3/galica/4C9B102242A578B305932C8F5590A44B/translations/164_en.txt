Takada Honbō {{Located behind Kamihata, it is a branch temple of Takada Isshindenji Senshūji}} was originally called Mount Rinkō Shingyō-in, but during the Genbun era (1736-1741), it became the main temple through the wishes of Prince Kankishin'in-no-miya, with monk Gidō as its founder. When the principal Buddha image of Tenpai Ikkō Sanzon from Mount Takada Senshūji in Shimotsuke Province comes for special exhibition, religious activities are always conducted at this temple. Pilgrims from all social classes visit in great numbers, so various entertainments are brought here, creating great prosperity.
