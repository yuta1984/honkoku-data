Hachiman Shrine 《Small text: Located in Tsuchida village. The enshrined deity is Emperor Ōjin, with Tamayori-hime and Empress Jingū enshrined on the left and right. It is commonly called Tsuchida no Hachiman》According to the shrine's tradition, this Hachiman Shrine was established during the reign of Emperor Go-Toba, in the first year of Kenkyū (1190), year of the Metal Dog, in the ninth month, when Lord Yoritomo was traveling to the capital and passed through this area. He saw a white hawk among the trees and declared this to be an auspicious omen of Hachiman's protection. Later, he established the shrine at that location, inviting the deities from Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine in Kuze District, Yamashiro Province, along with three other subsidiary shrines and branch shrines, creating a magnificent grand shrine. However, it fell into ruin over the years. On the third day of the fifth month of Keichō 4 (1599), year of the Earth Boar, the shrine's head priest Hōin Shōsei undertook reconstruction. In Keichō 11 (1606), year of the Fire Horse, the domain lord also carried out major repairs, bestowing official notices and making it a dignified shrine. ○The head priest is from Hōshō-zan Hōdō-in 《Small text: Shingon sect, branch temple of Mantoku-ji in Nagano village》Shrine priest 《Small text: Hirose family》
