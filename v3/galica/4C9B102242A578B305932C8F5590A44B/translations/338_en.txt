Sub-temples (There are three buildings: Shinkōin, Jōdoji, and Kyōkōbō) Temple treasures (A wooden statue of Amida made by Prince Shōtoku. There are many other rare items, but they are omitted here)

Takeo Tenjin Shrine (Located in Nagao Village, commonly called Tennō. The enshrined deity is Susanoo-no-Mikoto, with Ōnamuchi-no-Mikoto and Sukunahikona-no-Mikoto enshrined in auxiliary halls. This is what appears in the provincial records as "Junior Third Rank Takeo Tenjin")
(It is indeed a great shrine, and the mystical nature of its grounds and the majesty of its divine power are most venerable) Festival days (June 14th and 15th) Subsidiary shrines (There are very many shrines both within and outside the precincts, including Imori Shrine, Yagorō Shrine, Empress Jingū Shrine, Hachiman Shrine, Wakamiya Shrine, Mountain God Shrine, Hakusan Shrine, Tenmangū Shrine, as well as Mine Shrine and Ōhirume-no-Mikoto Shrine) Shrine priests (The Iwata family. Their ancestor was called Iwata Katesada, who was said to be the castle lord of this village. However, the exact period is unclear as the chronology is not detailed. The castle ruins remain on the mountain in this area. There is also something called Iwata Mound, which is said to be Katesada's tomb, with a single hiiragi tree beside it)

Toyoshi Tenjin Shrine (Located in a pine forest by the seashore of Ōashi Village, this is what appears in the provincial records as "Junior Third Rank Toyoshi Tenjin." The shrine grounds are mystical. In summer months, fireworks are launched over the sea in this area, drawing enormous crowds of spectators, and the scenery of the fireworks reflected on the water and sky is truly indescribable)

Mibu Tenjin Shrine (Located in Kofu Village, this is what appears in the provincial records as "Junior Third Rank Mibu Tenjin")

Fuki Castle Site (Located in Fuki Village, where Toda Magohachirō resided. His benevolent influence overflowed to the people below, and many who depended on the high agricultural profits here became wealthy, so from that time the village came to be called by this name)

Anashi Tenjin Shrine (Located in Yanashi Village, commonly called Yatsurugimiya. This is what appears in the provincial records as "Junior Third Rank Anashi Tenjin")

Konowata (Sea cucumber intestines) (A specialty product of Ōi Village, offered annually by the domain lord to both the Imperial Court and the Shogun's family. The beginning of its production dates to when there was a holy monk of strict discipline in this area who refined the technique of preparing intestinal sauce [shiokara])
