Karuta (playing cards)　　Also called　Kotsuhai
Dutch people were
playing these around the Kan'ei era,
and the people of Nagasaki imitated them
and made them into a pastime.
The manufacturing method is not the same
as in ancient times. The ones played today
are black on the outside
and white on the inside. What is depicted:
　Blue color　Pau (clubs)　called
　　Red color　Isu (swords)　called
　　　Round shape　Oul (coins)　called

[Illustration of a man drawing patterns on karuta cards]
　Semicircle　Kopp (cups)　called—four suits,
each with twelve cards, making forty-eight cards total.
Namely, the first has an insect shape called Zum,
from two to nine
the number of pips
are drawn. Ten is a monk figure,
　eleven is a mounted rider,
　　twelve is a military commander.
Also there are ○Kau and
　　　　○Unsun, etc., and all are
words from the
　　Nanban countries.
