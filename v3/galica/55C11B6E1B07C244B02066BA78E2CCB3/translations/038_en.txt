【Right page, upper section】
○The parrot (ōmu) is a bird that speaks well. It is white and blue, and there are also five-colored varieties. It has blue feathers and a red beak. It is a bird from Tang China.

○The bamboo partridge (yamashigi) resembles the francolin but is smaller, with a brown color and mottled red patterns. It has no tail and eats ants. It lives near water.

【Left page, upper section】
○The crested myna (hakuchō) has a form similar to a crow but smaller, and it well imitates human speech. This is also a bird from Tang China.

○The bat (kōmori) has a form similar to a mouse, with wings like stretched paper. From summer to the end of autumn, it flies around every night eating mosquitoes. During the day it hides in caves. It has hooks at the tips of its wings with which it hangs.
