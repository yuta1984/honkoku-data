【Right Page, Upper Section】
○Wisteria (fuji) blooms at the end of March. The purple-colored ones bloom late, and the flower clusters reach three to four shaku in length. White flowers bloom early and are short. Also called "invitation bean wisteria" (shōzutō).

○Dendrobium (sekikoku) grows on stones. It calms stomach qi and removes evil heat from the skin. Also called "stone bamboo" (sekichiku).

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○Japanese kerria (yamabuki) has yellow flowers, both single and double varieties. It blooms in March. Sometimes called "ground tang flower" (jitōka).

○Selaginella (iwahiba) is also called "ground cypress" (jihaku). It grows between rocks. Used fresh, it breaks up blood; when roasted, it stops bleeding.

○Sedum (mannengusa) is also called "ten-thousand-year pine" (mannenshō). The long ones are called "stone pine" (sekishō) or "jade sui" (gyokusui).
