【Right page, upper section】
○The lesser cuckoo (kakkō) is brown in color and first calls after Kokuu (Grain Rain) in the third month. When eaten, it calms the spirit. It is also called tsutsu-dori. This is also a type of dove, and it is said that beans are sown upon hearing its call around the third month.

○The domestic pigeon (iebato) is a dove that gathers and lives in large numbers at temples and pagodas. It regulates vital energy, increases qi, heals malignant sores, and neutralizes medicinal toxins. One should not eat too much of it.

【Left page, upper section】
○The thrush (tsugumi) is about the size of a brown-eared bulbul. Its feather color is brown and beautiful. This is eaten at year's end. The taste is good.

○The shrike (mozu) is slightly smaller than the brown-eared bulbul, is brown in color with a hawk-like head, chases small birds, and is carnivorous. When a small child is slow to speak, it is good to strike with a branch where a shrike perches.

○The siskin (hiwa) has a form about the size of a sparrow. Its feather color is black mixed with yellow feathers. It comes when spring arrives.
