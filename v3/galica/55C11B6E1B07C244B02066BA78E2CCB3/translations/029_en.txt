○Sparrowhawk (etsusai) and honey buzzard (sashiba) are both names of hawks that catch small birds. There are forty-eight varieties of hawks. Adding kites, shrikes, and owls makes forty-eight species. However, hawks used for hunting are given names by their keepers. Also, famous hawks from ancient times all have special names. Moreover, hawks that came from foreign countries must have particularly unique varieties.

【Left page, upper section】
Chinese hawks, Korean hawks, southern barbarian hawks, Ryukyu hawks - even within Japan, there are differences between those from the eastern provinces, western provinces, northern provinces, Shikoku, Chugoku, and Tsukushi. A hawk's wing feathers number twenty-four per wing, forty-eight for both wings combined, and the tail has twelve feathers. Each has a name. An eagle's tail has fourteen feathers.
