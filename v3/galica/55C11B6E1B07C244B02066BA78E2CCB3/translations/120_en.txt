【Right Page, Upper Section】
○Reed (ashi) grows near water. Those that have not yet produced ears are called "ro." When they grow long, they are called "i." The leaves resemble bamboo, and the flowers are like miscanthus.

○Lotus (hachisu) has both red and white flowers. The leaves are called "ka," the roots are called "gū," the flowers are called "fuyō," and the seeds are called "renteki."

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○Sweet flag (shō) - those with nine nodes per inch are called "shōbu" (iris). They first sprout fifty-seven days after the winter solstice.

○Wild rice (makomo) grows near water. It resembles iris. Also called "kōsō" (reed grass) or "shōsō" (sedge grass).

○Cattail (gama) grows near water. It can be woven into mats. The yellow pollen from the flower spike of the cattail club is called "hōō" (cattail pollen).

○Duckweed (ukikusa) floats on water and has no roots. It has a blood-like color.
