【Right Page, Upper Section】
There are distinctions between white sandalwood, purple sandalwood, red sandalwood, and black sandalwood.
《Supplement》Kyara (aloeswood) and jinkō (agarwood) are formed when this wood decays.

○Chinese honey locust (saikashi) has leaves resembling the pagoda tree. Its branches have thorns. In summer, thin yellow flowers bloom. It is also written as saikakushi.

○Brushwood (shiba) consists of small trees and miscellaneous timber. Commonly called shiba.

○Firewood (takigi): coarse wood is called shin (firewood), and fine wood is called shō.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
Also called "tsumagi."

○Bamboo has sixty-one varieties. Once every sixty years, it flowers, bears fruit, and dies.
《Supplement》This is called "shizen-koiri" (natural death cycle). The flower is called "sasameguri." After dying completely, it grows again.

○Bamboo shoots (takanna) are also written as jun, referring to the same thing. When eaten, they clear chest congestion, eliminate phlegm, refresh the stomach, and facilitate urination.
