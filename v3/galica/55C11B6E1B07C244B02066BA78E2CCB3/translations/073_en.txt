○滑虫 (abura-mushi, cockroach) is also called 蜚蠊 (hiren). They are numerous around kitchen stoves. They have wings and fly. Their color is black.

○殻 (kara, shells) is the general term for shells of the 蚌螺 (bōra) family. Clam shells are called "genmyōfun" and treat phlegm. The operculum of clams is called "kōkō" and is also written as "厴." It is added to incense. The shell of abalone is called "sekiketsumei" and treats eye pain and stops nosebleeds. Also, oyster shells are named "borei" and effectively stop night sweats.

○蛻 (monuke, molted skin) refers to snake molts, which are the shed skins of snakes. Also called "jahe" (snake skin) or "jatai" (snake molt). When made into charcoal powder and used with sake, it is good for difficult childbirth. Cicada molts are also called "sentai" or "kosen." When powdered and mixed with oil and applied to ear discharge, it heals.

○甲 (kō) refers to turtle shells. In ancient times, turtles were burned and the patterns on their shells were read to divine good and bad fortune. This was called "kiboku" (turtle divination). Also, when used as medicine, it supplements the kidneys and eliminates blood stasis. Soft-shell turtle shells disperse blood stasis and reduce swelling.
