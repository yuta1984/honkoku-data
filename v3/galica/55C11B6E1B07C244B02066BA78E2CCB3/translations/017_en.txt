【Running title】Tōsho Zōho Kinmō Zui 12, 8

【Right page, upper section】
○The ox is livestock that
plows fields. In China,
they kill oxen
to offer them in sacrifices.
There are wild oxen and water
buffalo. What is offered
as sacrifice is called
dairō (great offering).

○Toku is a calf,
the young of cattle. Since
the calf's nose resembles
male genitalia, male genitalia
are called tokuhi
(calf's nose).

【Right page, lower section - Illustrations】
Toku (reading: koushi)
Gyū (reading: ushi) 《explanations: tokushi (bull), meushi (cow), ameushi (yellow ox), hoshimadaraushi (spotted plow ox)》

【Left page, upper section】
○Ro is called a rabbit-like horse.
It is a horse with long
ears. In China
they use these.
They are horses that do
not exist in Japan.

○Da has a flesh saddle on
its back that is like
a peak. Its neck is long
and its legs are tall.
Its fur is warm,
warmer than
fox fur.
In summer it is
cool.

【Left page, lower section - Illustrations】
Ro (reading: usagiuma)
Da (reading: rakuda no uma)
