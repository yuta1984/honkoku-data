【Right Page, Upper Section】
○The bow is called the art of archery. Those born into samurai families must learn the art of archery. Samurai are called "bow-bearers" (yumitori). In China, there was a master archer named Yang Youji who could step back a hundred paces and shoot willow leaves without missing a single leaf. In our country, Chinzei Tametomo, Noto-no-kami Noritsune, Nasu-no-Yoichi, and others were master archers. Besides these, there were many other skilled warriors who were expert shooters.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○The horse refers to the methods of horsemanship. Since this is an essential way for warriors, it is crucial to receive instruction from a master and learn properly. Depending on whether the trainer is skilled or unskilled, even a fine horse can develop bad habits. It is said there are a hundred different kinds of such habits. There are established methods for training horses and correcting their bad habits, and since ancient times there have been eight riding schools. In the present age, the Otsubo school is primarily employed as the essential way for warriors. Easily climbing steep mountain precipices and crossing great rivers - all of this is due to the virtue of fine horses.
