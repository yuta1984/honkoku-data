【Right page, upper section】
○The crane has a body length of three shaku, height of 
over three shaku, and a beak length of four to 
five sun. The crown, eyes, and cheeks are red,
the legs are blue, the neck is long, the toes are
slender, the feathers are white, and the wings are
black. It cries at midnight
with a clear and beautiful voice. When pregnant,
its droppings turn to stone.

○The stork resembles a crane but its crown
is less red, its neck is long,
its beak is red, its color is grayish-white, and its wings are
black. It nests in tall trees.

○The saukwatsu is also called saukei,
which refers to the red-crowned crane.

【Right page, lower section - Illustrations】

【Left page, upper section】
○For geese, the large ones are called
kō and the smaller ones are called gan.
If eaten over a long period,
they stimulate qi and
strengthen bones.

○The kō (bean goose) is a large type
of goose. Because they gather in large numbers
at rivers and sandbars, it is written as kō.
It benefits the five organs and
neutralizes the poison of cinnabar.

○The koku (swan) is larger than geese,
with white feathers and flies high. Its taste is
sweet and it has no toxicity. It increases human
vitality and [strengthens] the internal organs.

【Left page, lower section - Illustrations】
Goose (kari)
Kō (hishikui)
Koku (kugui, hakuchō)
