【Right page, upper section】
○The meadow bunting (hōjiro) is a painted eyebrow bird (gabi-chō). Its form is about the size of a sparrow. Its feather color is also similar. Its cheeks are white with black markings.

○The jay (kashitori) is larger than the brown-eared bulbul. It has blue-shining black star-like feathers on its wings. It comes and calls from late autumn through the winter months.

○The lesser cuckoo (hototogisu) is larger than the brown-eared bulbul, is yellowish-black in color, and has a red beak. It calls in the night during the fourth and fifth months. It is the same as the duyu and zigui.

【Left page, upper section】
○The brown-eared bulbul (hiyodori) is a crested myna (kuyoku). It is also called haha-chō. Both its body and head are light gray with black markings. It eats the fruits of various trees. Many come in autumn and winter.

○The wagtail (sekirei) has a thin beak and long tail. When flying it calls, and when perched it moves its tail. Those with white feathers and black backs are called seguro, and those that are blue and yellow are called kisekirei.
