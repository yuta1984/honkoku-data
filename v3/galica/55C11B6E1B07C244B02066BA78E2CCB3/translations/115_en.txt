【Right Page, Upper Section】
○Blackberry lily (karasu-ōgi) is also called fan iris (hi-ōgi). The shape of its leaves resembles a folding fan. The flowers are yellow-red and bloom in May and June. It is the same as wushan (usen) and wusha (usau).

○Iris (kochōka) is a type of blackberry lily (karasu-ōgi). White flowers bloom in March with yellow in the center. Commonly called yaburan. "Shaga" is a mispronunciation of the sound "shakan" (for iris).

○Self-heal (kakosō) grows abundantly in the fields. It produces light purple flowers.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○Roof iris (ichihatsu) has leaves similar to blackberry lily (karasu-ōgi). The flowers are purple. The flowers are called purple umbrella (shirasan). It blooms in April.

○Iris (barin) grows near marshes. It has an unpleasant smell. The flowers resemble iris (ayame) but are thinner and lighter in color. Also called baren. It blooms in early summer.
