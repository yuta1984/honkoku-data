【Right Page, Upper Section】
○Also called Vimalakirti Layman. Holding a fly whisk in his hand, he decorated eight lion seats in all directions within a square chamber and admitted three thousand assembled people to preach the Dharma.

○The Amida of Mountain-Crossing refers to when Amida Buddha manifested his noble form on the peak of Yokawa at Mount Hiei, which the priest Eshin Sozu reverently observed and copied.

○Prince Shotoku was the son of Emperor Yomei, the 32nd human emperor. He served as regent during the reign of Emperor Suiko, the 34th emperor. He is the founder of Japanese Buddhism. He destroyed Moriya and established Tennoji Temple in Settsu Province.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○The Buddha Leaving the Mountains refers to how the Tathagata left home at seventeen years of age, and at thirty years old, on the eighth day of the twelfth month, when the morning star appeared, he achieved sudden great enlightenment and attained perfect awakening.

○The Birth Buddha refers to how Shakyamuni Tathagata was born on the eighth day of the fourth month at the hour of the Tiger, took seven steps, and pointing up and down with his left and right hands, declared "Above heaven and below heaven, I alone am honored." His parinirvana was on the fifteenth day of the second month.
