Sanbō Kōjin (Three Treasures Fierce Deity)
The Three Treasures refer to Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha. [This deity] protects and maintains the Three Treasures. Kōjin refers to the spirit of po (corporeal soul).

Sanbō Kōjin (Three Treasures Fierce Deity)
This is Kojima Kōjin. Because he punishes evil people, he is also called Ran Kōjin (Chaotic Fierce Deity). Also, because he protects the Three Treasures, he is called Sanbō Kōjin.

Kichijōten (Śrī-Lakṣmī)
She is the younger sister of Vaiśravaṇa. According to the Kōmyō, she is called the deva of accomplished威徳 and great merit in all affairs. In Sanskrit, she is called Śrī-mahādevī-vihāra.

Bodaijushinten (Bodhi Tree Spirit Deva)
This is the place where the Tathāgata achieved enlightenment. This name exists because [this deity] protects the Bodhi tree.

Hisōten (Compassionate Appearance Deva)
According to the Shurin, [this deity] exists among the thirty-two types of devas. [It is] within the devas of the three realms.
