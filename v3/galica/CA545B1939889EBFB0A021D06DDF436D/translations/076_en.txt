Nichi-getsu Tōmyō Buddha (Sun-Moon Lamp-Light Buddha)
10th Day

According to the Lotus Sutra commentary, the sun represents wisdom (e), the moon represents concentration (jō). Concentration and wisdom are the virtues of self-practice. Lamp-light represents the virtue of teaching others. Also, sun, moon, and lamp represent the three wisdoms.

Tenshō Kōtai-jin (Amaterasu Ōmikami)
10th Day

Enshrined in Watarai County, Ise Province. The Inner Shrine was established in the 25th year of Emperor Suinin's reign, and the Outer Shrine in the 22nd year of Emperor Yūryaku's reign. The original ground (honji) is Dainichi Nyorai.

Kanki Buddha (Joy Buddha)
11th Day

According to the sutra, dwelling in the ground of joy, able to save all sentient beings. Therefore so named.

Hachiman Daimyōjin
11th Day

Enshrined at Kyūhō (Dove Peak) in Yamashiro Province. The original ground is Amida Buddha. When the monk Gyōkyō was in retreat at Usa in Chikuzen, through divine oracle he transferred the deity to present-day Mount Hachiman. This is the spirit of Emperor Ōjin.

Nanshō Buddha (Hard-to-Conquer Buddha)
12th Day

According to the sutra, in wisdom, compassion, practice, and virtue, surpassing other honored ones. Therefore so named.

Kamo Daimyōjin
12th Day

Enshrined in Atago County, Yamashiro Province. The original ground is Shō-Kannon (Sacred Avalokiteśvara). The deity Wakeikazuchi-no-mikoto. During the reign of Emperor Kinmei, ceremonies for the upper and lower shrines were first established.
