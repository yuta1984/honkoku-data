【Upper Right】
【Title】
Samantabhadra Bodhisattva (Fugen Bosatsu)
【Description】
In Sanskrit "Viśvabhadra" or "Samantabhadra," translated as "Universal Worthy." The Karuṇāpuṇḍarīka Sūtra states: "My practice will surely surpass all bodhisattvas."
The Buddha said: "Because of this cause and condition, I now change your name and call you Samantabhadra."

【Lower Right】
【Title】
Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva (Darani Bosatsu)
【Description】
In Sanskrit "dhāraṇī," translated in this country as "total retention." In the Mingyi Ji, total retention means holding onto good without losing it, and holding evil without letting it arise.

【Upper Left】
【Title】
Dharmasvararāja Bodhisattva (Hōjizaiō Bosatsu)
【Description】
The so-called "dharma" refers to Buddha-dharma, and "sovereign king" both carry the meaning of freedom without obstruction. This is another name for Mañjuśrī. In Sanskrit "Mañjuśrī," translated as "Wonderful Virtue." The Karuṇāpuṇḍarīka Sūtra calls him "Ānandagarbha-maṇi-ratna-kūṭa Buddha," and the Śūraṅgama Sūtra calls him "Nāgavaṃśa-agrarāja Buddha."

【Lower Left】
【Title】
Śvetahastirāja Bodhisattva (Byakuzōō Bosatsu)
【Description】
The white elephant immediately represents and manifests principle. "King" means freedom without obstruction, and refers to the fundamental nature of the bodhisattva being endowed with all dharmas, and for the sake of saving sentient beings, displaying the awesome manifestation of wielding the wonderful function of freedom.

Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas:
Samantabhadra Bodhisattva (Fugen Bosatsu)
Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva (Darani Bosatsu)  
Dharmasvararāja Bodhisattva (Hōjizaiō Bosatsu)
Śvetahastirāja Bodhisattva (Byakuzōō Bosatsu)
