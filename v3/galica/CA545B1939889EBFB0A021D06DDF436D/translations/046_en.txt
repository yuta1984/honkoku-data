【Upper Right】
Samantabhadra Bodhisattva

In Sanskrit called Vișubhadra or Samanvabhadra, translated as Universal Worthy (Fugen). The Compassionate Lotus Sutra states: "My practice will surely surpass all other bodhisattvas."

The Buddha said: "Due to these causes and conditions, I now change your name to Universal Worthy (Fugen)."

【Lower Right】
Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva

In Sanskrit dhāraṇī, translated in this country as "total retention" (sōji). According to the Collection of Names and Meanings, total retention means holding good without losing it and holding evil without allowing it to arise.

【Upper Left】
Dharma Sovereign King Bodhisattva

The so-called dharma refers to Buddha-dharma, and sovereign king both mean freedom without obstruction. This is another name for Mañjuśrī. In Sanskrit Mañjuśrī, translated as Wonderful Virtue (myōtoku). The Compassionate Lotus Sutra calls him "Joyful Treasury Maṇi Jewel Accumulation Buddha," and the Śūraṅgama Sutra calls him "Dragon Seed Supreme Honored King Buddha."

【Lower Left】
White Elephant King Bodhisattva

The white elephant represents and manifests principle (ri). King means freedom without obstruction. This refers to the majestic appearance of one who, having perfected all dharmas in the essential nature of a bodhisattva (sattva), manifests wonderful functions of freedom for the sake of saving beings, and is named accordingly.
