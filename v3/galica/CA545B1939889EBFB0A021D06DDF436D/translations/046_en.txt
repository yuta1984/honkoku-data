【Upper Right】
【Title】
Samantabhadra Bodhisattva (Fugen Bosatsu)
【Description】
In Sanskrit called Biśupattra (Hiyuhatsuda) or Samantabhadra (Sanmanhatsuda), translated as "Universal Worthy" (Fugen). The Karuṇāpuṇḍarīka Sūtra states: "My practice will surely surpass that of all bodhisattvas."

The Buddha said: "Because of this cause and condition, I now change your name to Samantabhadra (Universal Worthy)."

【Lower Right】
【Title】
Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva
【Description】
In Sanskrit called dhāraṇī, here translated as "total retention" (sōji). According to the Fanyi Mingyi Ji, "total retention" means to maintain good without losing it and to restrain evil without generating it.

【Upper Left】
【Title】
Dharmasvāmirāja Bodhisattva (Dharma Sovereign King Bodhisattva)
【Description】
The so-called "dharma" refers to Buddha-dharma, and "sovereign king" both carry the meaning of freedom without obstruction. This is an alternate name for Mañjuśrī. In Sanskrit called Mañjuśrī, translated as "Wonderful Virtue" (Myōtoku). The Karuṇāpuṇḍarīka Sūtra calls him "Ānandagarbhamaṇiratnacitta Buddha," and the Śūraṅgama Sūtra refers to him as "Nāgavaṃśāgraśrīrāja Buddha."

【Lower Left】
【Title】
Śvetahastirāja Bodhisattva (White Elephant King Bodhisattva)
【Description】
The white elephant represents and manifests principle (ri). "King" means freedom without obstruction. The bodhisattva's essential nature encompasses all dharmas, and for the sake of liberating sentient beings, manifests the wonderful function of freedom. The name follows this awesome aspect.

Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas:
Samantabhadra Bodhisattva (Fugen Bosatsu)
Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva (Darani Bosatsu)
Dharmasvāmirāja Bodhisattva (Hōjizaiō Bosatsu)
Śvetahastirāja Bodhisattva (Byakuzōō Bosatsu)
