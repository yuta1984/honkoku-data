Fifth King: Yama-rāja (Enma-ō)
In Sanskrit called "Yama-rāja," in this country it means "obstruction and cessation." It signifies obstructing and preventing the creation of evil. His true form is Jizō Bodhisattva. He governs the fifth seven days (35th day).

Sixth King: Hensei-ō (Transformation King)
"Hen" (transformation) means creation and change, different from when the original Buddha-body manifests. That is, true suchness (tathatā) follows conditions and becomes a king's body, hence called "transformation." His practice is the teaching of "non-accomplishment is accomplishment." His true form is Maitreya Bodhisattva. He governs the sixth seven days (42nd day).

Seventh King: Taizan-ō (Mount Tai King)
This is the crown prince of Yama-rāja. Also called Taizan-fukun, and also one who expresses reverence, also called Jinsha Great King. His true form is Bhaiṣajyaguru (Medicine Buddha). He governs the seventh complete seven days (49th day).

Eighth King: Byōdō-ō (Equality King)
The sutra says: "When I observe all things, they are universally equal, without any mind of love or hatred." This is the meaning. Because he views good and evil deceased persons with compassion as if they were his own children, he is named Equality King. His true form is Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. He governs the 100th day.

Ninth King: Toshi-ō (Metropolitan King)
"Toshi" - both characters mean "market." "To" means the capital's castle and palace. "Shi" means marketplace. His true form is Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva. He governs the first anniversary.

Tenth King: Godō-tenrin-ō (Five Paths Wheel-Turning King)
He dwells at the crossroads of the five paths, turns the wonderful Dharma wheel, and examines the evil karma of sentient beings, hence called the Five Paths Wheel-Turning King. His true form is Amida Buddha. He governs the third anniversary.
