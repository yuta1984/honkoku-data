The Twenty-Eight Attendants of Avalokiteśvara

Great Brahmā Heaven King: He is the lord of the first dhyāna heaven in the form realm and also the lord of the great trichiliocosm. Because he possesses vitarka and vicāra (initial and sustained thought), he rules above and stands supreme below, hence he is called "lord." The Vibhāṣā-śāstra states: "The expanse of the first dhyāna is like that of the four continents."

Maheśvara King: The Śāstra Commentary states: "Maheśvara is here called 'Great Sovereign.'" He is the lord of the highest heaven in the form realm and lord of the three realms, hence called "supremely venerable."

Indra Heaven King: The Lamp of Understanding states: "A name like Indra's is called 'Thousand-Eyed,' but this does not mean he has a thousand eyes. It is because he can see a thousand dharmas at one time."

Eastern Direction Heaven: This refers to Dhṛtarāṣṭra. He commands the gandharvas and piśācas and protects the people of the eastern continent.

Golden Peacock King

Virūḍhaka (Southern Direction Heaven): This refers to Virūḍhaka. He commands the kumbhāṇḍas and pretas and protects the people of the southern continent.
