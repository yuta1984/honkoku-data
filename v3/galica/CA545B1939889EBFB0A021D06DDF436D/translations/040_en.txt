【Upper Right】
【Title】
Seven-Character Mañjuśrī
【Description】
The first volume of the Commentary on the Perfect Enlightenment Sutra states that this is also called "Wondrous Leader" and also called "Wondrous Auspiciousness." In the Commentary on the Vimalakīrti Sutra, in Qin it is called "Wondrous Virtue." The sutra states that he has already attained Buddhahood in the past and is called "Dragon-Seed Honored One."

【Lower Right】
【Title】
Akṣayamati Bodhisattva
【Description】
The Collection of Names and Meanings states that "Akṣayamati" is rendered as "Inexhaustible Intent." Tiantai says that because one knows that all dharma-natures are inexhaustible, the bodhisattva's arousal of bodhicitta is also inexhaustible.

【Upper Left】
【Title】
Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva
【Description】
The sutra states that he is called "Great Compassionate Ākāśagarbha" who can fulfill all wishes. He holds a sword in his right hand and holds a wish-fulfilling jewel in his left hand.

【Lower Left】
【Title】
Fukuichi Man-Kokūzō (Merit-Fulfilling Ākāśagarbha)
【Description】
"Ākāśa" (void/space) represents that great compassionate benefit for others is inexhaustible. The Nirvana Sutra states that ākāśa has three meanings: first, no change or transformation; second, no boundaries or limits; third, no obstructions or hindrances.
