■Seven-Character Mañjuśrī
【Lower Section】
The Yuanjue jing shu (Commentary on the Perfect Enlightenment Sutra), volume 1, states: "Here it is called 'Wonderful Head' and also called 'Wonderful Auspiciousness.'" The commentary on the Vimalakīrti Sutra states: "In Qin [dynasty Chinese] it is called 'Wonderful Virtue.'" The sutra says: "He has already attained buddhahood and is called 'Dragon-Seed Honored One.'"

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Akṣayamati Bodhisattva (Inexhaustible Intent Bodhisattva)
【Lower Section】
The Mingyi ji (Collection of Name Meanings) states: "Akṣayamati, here called 'Inexhaustible Intent.'" Tiantai says: "Because one knows that the nature of all dharmas is inexhaustible, the bodhisattva's awakening mind is also inexhaustible."

【Upper Section】
■Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Space Treasury Bodhisattva)
【Lower Section】
The sutra states: "Able to fulfill all wishes, named Great Compassion Space Treasury." In the right hand holds a sword, in the left hand holds a wish-fulfilling jewel.

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■Fukuichi Mankokūzō (Merit-Fulfilling Space Treasury)
【Lower Section】
"Space" represents that great compassionate benefit to others is inexhaustible. The Nirvāṇa Sutra states: "Space has three meanings: first is non-change, second is boundlessness, third is non-obstruction."
