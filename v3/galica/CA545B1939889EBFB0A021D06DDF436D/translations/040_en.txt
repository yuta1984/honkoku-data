【Upper Right】
【Title】
Seven-Character Mañjuśrī
【Description】
The Commentary on the Perfect Enlightenment Sutra, Volume 1, states: "This is called Wonderful Head (Myōshu), and also called Wonderful Auspiciousness (Myōkichijō)." In the Commentary on the Vimalakīrti Sutra, it says "In Qin it is called Wonderful Virtue (Myōtoku)." The sutra states: "Having already attained Buddhahood, he is named Dragon Seed Honored One (Ryūshu-son)."

【Lower Right】
【Title】
Akṣayamati Bodhisattva
【Description】
The Collection of Name Meanings states: "Akṣayamati, this is called Inexhaustible Intent (Mujin'i)." Tiantai says: "Because one knows that the nature of all dharmas is inexhaustible, the bodhisattva's awakening of the aspiration for enlightenment is also inexhaustible."

【Upper Left】
【Title】
Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva
【Description】
The sutra states: "Named Great Compassionate Ākāśagarbha, who can fulfill all wishes." He holds a sword in his right hand and a wish-fulfilling jewel in his left hand.

【Lower Left】
【Title】
Fukuichi Man-Kokūzō (Full Ākāśagarbha of Fortune)
【Description】
"Ākāśa" (empty space) represents that great compassionate benefit to others is inexhaustible. The Nirvana Sutra states: "Empty space has three meanings: first is non-transformation, second is boundlessness, and third is non-obstruction."
