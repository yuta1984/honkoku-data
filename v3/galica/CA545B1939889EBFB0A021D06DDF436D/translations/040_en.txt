**Seven-Character Mañjuśrī**
The Yuanjue Jing Shu (Commentary on the Perfect Enlightenment Sutra), Volume 1, states: "Here it is called Wonderful Head (Myōshu), and also called Wonderful Auspiciousness (Myōkichijō)."

The Commentary on the Vimalakīrti Sutra states: "In Qin [dynasty Chinese] it is called Wonderful Virtue (Myōtoku). The sutra says: '[Mañjuśrī] has already universally become a Buddha, named Dragon Seed Honored One (Ryūshu Son).'"

**Akṣayamati Bodhisattva (Inexhaustible Intent)**
The Collection of Names and Meanings states: "Akṣayamati - here it is called Inexhaustible Intent (Mujin'i)."

The Dharma-Fulfilling Seal Bodhisattva says: "Because [this bodhisattva] knows that all dharma-nature is inexhaustible, the bodhisattva's aspiration for enlightenment is also inexhaustible."

**Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Space Treasury)**
The sutra states: "Named Great Compassion Space Treasury, able to fulfill all wishes."

[This bodhisattva] holds a sword in the right hand and a wish-fulfilling jewel in the left hand.

**Merit-Fulfilling Space Treasury**
"Space" (虚空) represents that great compassionate benefit to others is inexhaustible.

The Nirvana Sutra states: "Space has three meanings: first, no change or alteration; second, no boundaries or limits; third, no obstructions or hindrances."

**Pronunciations**
