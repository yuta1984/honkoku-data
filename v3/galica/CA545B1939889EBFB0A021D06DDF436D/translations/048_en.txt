【Upper Right】
Light King Bodhisattva

First, this light represents wisdom. King means venerable leader. Because my light surpasses the light of the sun, moon, and all buddhas, this bodhisattva is called Light King. This is also mentioned in the Abbreviated Commentary on Perfect Enlightenment.

【Lower Right】
Mountain-Ocean Wisdom Bodhisattva

The Xuanzan commentary, volume 8, states: "By possessing treasure-house wisdom that is deep and unfathomable like the ocean, and difficult to look up to like a mountain, this bodhisattva is named Mountain-Ocean Wisdom." The meaning is that the wisdom and virtue being lofty and profound like mountains and oceans becomes the name.

【Upper Left】
Vajragarbha Bodhisattva

In Sanskrit, vajra, which is translated as "diamond" or "vajra." Vajra means both firm and sharp. This bodhisattva's wisdom is likewise. That afflictions and external demons cannot invade or disturb shows firmness; that this bodhisattva can destroy various obstacles and break through people's doubts shows sharpness. Because this bodhisattva possesses inexhaustible merit, the name includes "garbha" (treasury).

【Lower Left】
Flower Adornment Bodhisattva

In the section on the upper direction in the Amitabha Sutra, it mentions "Buddha Adorned with Variously Colored Real Flowers." In the causes of self-practice, flowers are like jewels that adorn the body of fruition virtue (as seen in Dayu's commentary). Ultimately, this bodhisattva's name demonstrates the principle of cause and effect.
