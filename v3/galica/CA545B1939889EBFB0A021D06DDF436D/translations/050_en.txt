【Right Upper】
Lion's Roar Bodhisattva

"Lion" is called "kara" in Sanskrit, which is translated here as "lion," or sometimes rendered as "fearless." This name was given because [this bodhisattva] attained the awesome virtue of fearlessness in preaching the Dharma and saving beings. The lion is the king of beasts and feels no fear when roaming about.

【Right Lower】
Great Awesome Virtue Bodhisattva

"Great" means universal and extensive; "awesome" means majestic power; "virtue" means meritorious virtue and merit; "king" means sovereignty. Ultimately, [this bodhisattva] is called Great Awesome Virtue King because the moral virtue of great majesty is clear, and the great compassion for saving beings is free and immeasurable.

【Left Upper】
Meditative Sovereignty King Bodhisattva

"Meditative" refers to dhyana meditation, which together means freedom without obstruction.

【Left Lower】
Great Sovereignty King Bodhisattva

The Nirvana Sutra states: "Great means that its nature is vast and extensive, like empty space." The Infinite Life Sutra says: "In regard to all things, [one is] sovereign according to one's will." This is because [this bodhisattva] is greatly sovereign with unobstructed supernatural transformations.
