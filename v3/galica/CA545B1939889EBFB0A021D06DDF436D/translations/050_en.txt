【Upper Right】
Lion's Roar Bodhisattva

"Lion" is called "kara" in Sanskrit, which is translated here as "lion," or sometimes rendered as "fearlessness." This name is given because this bodhisattva attained the awesome virtue of fearlessness in preaching the Dharma to liberate sentient beings. The lion is the king among beasts and has no fear when roaming about.

【Lower Right】
Great Awesome Virtue Bodhisattva

"Great" means universal and vast; "awesome" means majestic power; "virtue" means virtuous righteousness and merit; "king" means sovereignty. In essence, this is called "Great Awesome Virtue King" because the moral virtue of great majesty is clear, and the great compassion that liberates sentient beings is free and immeasurable.

【Upper Left】
Concentration Sovereignty King Bodhisattva

"Concentration" means meditation (dhyana), and signifies freedom without obstruction.

【Lower Left】
Great Sovereignty King Bodhisattva

The Nirvana Sutra states: "Great means that its nature is vast and broad, just like empty space." The Infinite Life Sutra says: "In all phenomena, one is sovereign according to one's will." This is because of being greatly sovereign and having unobstructed supernatural transformations.
