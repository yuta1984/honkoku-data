The Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas (continued)

**Shishiku Bodhisattva (Lion's Roar Bodhisattva)**
"Lion" is called "kara" in Sanskrit, which is translated as "lion" or sometimes as "fearless." Therefore, this name is given because this bodhisattva obtained the awesome virtue of fearlessness in teaching the Dharma and saving sentient beings. The lion is the king among beasts, traveling without fear.

**Daiitoku Bodhisattva (Great Awesome Virtue Bodhisattva)**
"Dai" (great) means universal and vast; "i" (awesome) means majestic power; "toku" (virtue) means moral virtue and merit; "ō" (king) means sovereignty. Ultimately, this bodhisattva is called "Great Awesome Virtue King" because his great majestic moral virtue is clear and his great compassion for saving sentient beings is free and immeasurable.

**Jōjizai-ō Bodhisattva (Concentration Sovereignty King Bodhisattva)**
"Jō" (concentration) refers to dhyāna (meditation), meaning freedom and non-obstruction.

**Daijizai-ō Bodhisattva (Great Sovereignty King Bodhisattva)**
The Nirvana Sutra states: "Great means that its nature is vast and broad, immeasurable like empty space." The Infinite Life Sutra says: "In accordance with intention, there is sovereignty over all things." This is because of great sovereignty and unobstructed supernatural transformation.
