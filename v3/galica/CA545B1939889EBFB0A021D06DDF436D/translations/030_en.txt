【Upper section】
■Birth of Shakyamuni
【Lower section】
His father was King Shuddhodana of the kingdom of Kapilavastu in Central India, and his mother was Lady Maya. He was born in the tiger hour on the eighth day of the fourth month in the thirtieth year of King Shuddhodana's reign. He took seven steps in each of the four directions, pointed up and down with his hands, and said, "In the four directions, above and below, I alone am most honored." He was called Sarvarthasiddha. Details are found in the "Dharma Garden Pearl Forest" and "Abbreviated Ancient Records," among others.

【Lower Right】
【Upper section】
■Shakyamuni Leaving the Mountain
【Lower section】
At age nineteen, on the eighth day of the second month, the prince left the royal palace and went to Mount Dandoku. After twelve years of difficult ascetic practices, at age thirty, on the Vajra Seat at the Bodhi Ground in Magadha, in the thirty-third year of King Zhao, on the eighth day of the twelfth month of the year of Wu-Yin, when the morning star appeared, he suddenly achieved great enlightenment and attained perfect awakening.

【Upper Left】
【Upper section】
■Shakyamuni of Perfect Enlightenment
【Lower section】
The Avatamsaka teaching for twenty-one days, the Agama teaching for twelve years, the Vaipulya and Prajnaparamita teachings for thirty years, the Lotus teaching for eight years, and the Nirvana teaching for one day and night. The "Blue Cliff Record" states: "Shakyamuni, for forty-nine years in the world, in 360 assemblies, expounded both sudden teachings and expedient truths." This is called the complete teaching of one lifetime.

【Lower Left】
【Upper section】
■Nirvana of Shakyamuni
【Lower section】
At age eighty, by the Hiranyavati River near Kushinagara, between the twin sal trees, at midnight on the fifteenth day of the second month, he entered nirvana. This corresponds to the thirty-sixth year of King Mu of Zhou, the year of Ren-Shen.
