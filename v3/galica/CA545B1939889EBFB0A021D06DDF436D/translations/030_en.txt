**The Life of Buddha**

**Birth**
In the kingdom of Kapilavastu in Central India, born with King Śuddhodana as father and Queen Maya as mother. In the thirtieth year of King Śuddhodana's reign, on the eighth day of the fourth month at the hour of the tiger, he was born. He took seven steps in each of the four directions, pointed to heaven and earth with his hands, and declared "Above heaven and below heaven, I alone am honored," and was named Sarvārthasiddha. This is detailed in the Fayuan Zhulin, Keiko Ryaku, and other texts.

**Leaving the Mountain**
At the age of nineteen, on the eighth day of the second month, the prince left the royal palace and went to Mount Dandaka, where he practiced severe austerities for twelve years. At the age of thirty, on the Vajra Seat at Bodh Gaya in Magadha, in the thirty-third year of King Zhao (year of Wu-yin), on the eighth day of the twelfth month, when the morning star appeared, he achieved great enlightenment and attained perfect awakening.

**Perfect Awakening and Teaching the Dharma**
He taught the Avatamsaka for thirty-seven days, the Agamas for twelve years, the Vaipulya and Prajñāpāramitā for thirty years, the Lotus Sutra for eight years, and the Nirvana Sutra for one day and one night.

According to the Blue Cliff Record, Shakyamuni resided for forty-nine years and held 360 assemblies to expound the teachings. The sudden teachings, provisional teachings, and true teachings—these are called the teachings of one lifetime.

**Nirvana**
At the age of eighty, by the Hiranyavati River near Kushinagar, between the twin sal trees, he entered nirvana at midnight on the fifteenth day of the second month. This corresponded to the thirty-sixth year of King Mu of Zhou (year of Ren-shen).
