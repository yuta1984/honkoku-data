■The Birth of Shakyamuni
【Lower section】
His father was King Śuddhodana of Kapilavastu in Central India, and his mother was Queen Maya. In the thirtieth year of King Śuddhodana's reign, on the eighth day of the fourth month at the hour of the tiger, he was born. He took seven steps in each of the four directions, pointed above and below with his hands, and said, "In the four quarters and above and below, I alone am most honored." He was called Sarvārthasiddha. Details can be found in the "Fayuan Zhulin" and "Shakuko Ryaku."

【Lower right】
【Upper section】
■Shakyamuni Leaving the Mountain
【Lower section】
At nineteen years old, on the eighth day of the second month, the prince left the royal palace and went to Mount Dandaka. After twelve years of austere practices, at age thirty, at the Vajra Seat in the Bodhi Grove in Magadha, in the thirty-third year of King Zhao's reign, year of Wuyin, on the eighth day of the twelfth month, when the morning star appeared, he suddenly achieved great enlightenment and attained perfect awakening.

【Upper left】
【Upper section】
■Shakyamuni of Perfect Awakening
【Lower section】
The Avatamsaka teaching for three times seven days, the Agama teachings for twelve years, the Vaipulya and Prajnaparamita teachings for thirty years, the Lotus Sutra teaching for eight years, and the Nirvana teaching for one day and one night. The "Blue Cliff Record" states: "Shakyamuni and Laozi dwelt in the world for forty-nine years, holding 360 assemblies to expound sudden teachings, provisional and true." This is called the lifetime teachings.

【Lower left】
【Upper section】
■The Nirvana of Shakyamuni
【Lower section】
At eighty years old, by the Hiranyavati River near Kushinagar, between the twin sal trees, he entered nirvana at midnight on the fifteenth day of the second month. This corresponds to the thirty-sixth year of King Mu of Zhou, year of Renshin.
