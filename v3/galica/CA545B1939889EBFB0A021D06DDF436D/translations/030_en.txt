【Upper Right】
■The Birth of Shakyamuni
King Śuddhodana of Kapilavastu in Central India was his father, and Queen Maya was his mother. In the thirtieth year of King Śuddhodana's reign, on the eighth day of the fourth month at the hour of the tiger (3-5 AM), he was born. He took seven steps in each of the four directions, pointed to heaven and earth with his hands, and declared "In the four quarters, above and below, I alone am the World-Honored One." He was called Sarvārthasiddha. Details can be found in the "Forest of Pearls in the Garden of Dharma" and "Brief Records of Ancient Times."

【Lower Right】
■Shakyamuni Leaving the Mountain
At nineteen years old, on the eighth day of the second month, the prince left the royal palace and went to Mount Daṃḍaloka. After twelve years of austere practices, at thirty years old, at the Vajra Seat in the Bodhi Grove in Magadha, in the thirty-third year of King Zhao (year of Wu-Yin), on the eighth day of the twelfth month when the morning star appeared, he suddenly attained great enlightenment and achieved perfect awakening.

【Upper Left】
■The Perfectly Awakened Shakyamuni
The Avatamsaka teaching for thirty-seven days, the Agama teaching for twelve years, the Vaipulya and Prajñāpāramitā teachings for thirty years, the Lotus teaching for eight years, and the Nirvana teaching for one day and one night. The "Blue Cliff Record" states that Shakyamuni lived in the world for forty-nine years, holding 360 assemblies to expound the Dharma. The sudden teaching and provisional-ultimate teaching—this is called the teaching of one complete lifetime.

【Lower Left】
■The Nirvana of Shakyamuni
At eighty years old, by the Hiranyavati River near Kuśinagara, between the twin śāla trees, he entered nirvana at midnight on the fifteenth day of the second month. This corresponds to the thirty-sixth year of King Mu of Zhou (year of Ren-Shen).
