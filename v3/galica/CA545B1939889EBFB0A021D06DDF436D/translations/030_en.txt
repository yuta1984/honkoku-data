【Upper Section】
■The Birth of Śākyamuni

【Lower Section】
With King Śuddhodana of Kapilavastu in Central India as his father and Lady Māyā as his mother. In the thirtieth year of King Śuddhodana's reign, on the eighth day of the fourth month at the hour of the Tiger, he was born. He took seven steps in each of the four directions, pointed with his hands above and below, and said: "In the four directions, above and below, I alone am the most honored." He was called Sarvārthasiddha. Details are found in the "Fayuan Zhulin" and "Keiko Ryaku."

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Śākyamuni Leaving the Mountain

【Lower Section】
At nineteen years old, on the eighth day of the second month, the prince left the palace and went to Mount Dandoku. After twelve years of ascetic practices, at thirty years old, at the Vajra Seat of the Bodhi Place in Magadha, in the thirty-third year of King Zhao (year of Wu-yin), on the eighth day of the twelfth month when the morning star appeared, he suddenly achieved great enlightenment and attained perfect awakening.

【Upper Left】
【Upper Section】
■Śākyamuni of Perfect Awakening

【Lower Section】
The Avataṃsaka teaching for thirty-seven days, the Āgama teaching for twelve years, the Vaipulya and Prajñāpāramitā teachings for thirty years, the Lotus Sutra teaching for eight years, and the Nirvana Sutra teaching for one day and one night.
The "Blue Cliff Record" states: "Śākyamuni lived in the world for forty-nine years, holding 360 assemblies and expounding sudden teachings and provisional truths." This is called the teachings of one lifetime.

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■The Nirvana of Śākyamuni

【Lower Section】
At eighty years old, by the Hiraṇyavatī River near Kuśinagara, between the twin śāla trees, he entered nirvana at midnight on the fifteenth day of the second month. This corresponds to the thirty-sixth year of King Mu of Zhou, the year of Ren-shen.
