【Upper section】
■Birth of Shakyamuni
【Lower section】
He had King Śuddhodana of Kapilavastu in Central India as his father, and Queen Maya as his mother. In the thirtieth year of King Śuddhodana's reign, on the eighth day of the fourth month at the hour of the Tiger, he was born. He took seven steps in each of the four directions, pointed above and below with his hands, and said, "In the four directions, above and below, I alone am most honored." He was called Sarvārthasiddha. Details can be found in the "Forest of Pearls in the Garden of Dharma" and "Abbreviated Records of Shakyamuni," among others.

【Lower right】
【Upper section】
■Shakyamuni Leaving the Mountain
【Lower section】
At age nineteen, on the eighth day of the second month, the prince left the royal palace and went to Mount Dandaka, where he practiced severe austerities for twelve years. At age thirty, at the Bodhi-place Diamond Seat in Magadha, in the thirty-third year of King Zhao (year of Wu-yin), on the eighth day of the twelfth month, when the morning star appeared, he demonstrated great awakening and achieved perfect enlightenment.

【Upper left】
【Upper section】
■Shakyamuni of Perfect Enlightenment
【Lower section】
The Avatamsaka teaching for three times seven days, the Agama teaching for twelve years, the Vaipulya and Prajnaparamita teachings for thirty years, the Lotus teaching for eight years, and the Nirvana teaching for one day and one night. The "Blue Cliff Record" states: "Shakyamuni, for forty-nine years in the world, held 360 assemblies, expounding sudden teachings and provisional and ultimate truths." This is called the "teachings of one lifetime."

【Lower left】
【Upper section】
■Nirvana of Shakyamuni
【Lower section】
At age eighty, by the Hiranyavati River near Kushinagar, between the twin sala trees, he entered nirvana at midnight on the fifteenth day of the second month. This corresponds to the thirty-sixth year of King Mu of Zhou (year of Ren-shen).
