Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas

【Upper Right】
【Title】
Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva (Kanzeon Bosatsu)
【Description】
In Sanskrit called Avalokiteśvara (Abarokitaishihara),
in Chinese translated as "Guanzizai" (Perceiver of Freedom). In the Fahua Wenju it is
Bālakiteśa (Barugiteisei), and in the Ōjō Yōshū it is considered
an attendant of Amida Buddha.

After the True Dharma perishes, Avalokiteśvara will attain perfect enlightenment
and be called Samantaraśmiprabhasaśrīkūṭarāja Tathāgata (Universal Light Merit Mountain King Tathāgata).

【Lower Right】
【Title】
Bhaiṣajyarāja Bodhisattva (Medicine King Bodhisattva)
【Description】
In Sanskrit called Agada (Akaun). This Bodhisattva uses great compassionate
healing arts to remove the sufferings of illness caused by various karmas of all sentient beings
and freely bestows joy upon them, hence
named Medicine King.

Also burned his body as an offering to Buddha, burning for
twelve hundred years without being extinguished. This is recorded in the Mahāprajñāpāramitāśāstra, volume 16.

【Upper Left】
【Title】
Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva (Great Power Bodhisattva)
【Description】
In Sanskrit called Mahāsthāmaprāpta, translated as "Great Power Attained." In the Śūraṅgama
Sūtra it states: "I, in my original causal ground, with the mind of Buddha-recitation entered the tolerance of no-birth, and now
in this world I gather those who recite the Buddha's name and guide them to return to the Pure Land."

In the Vimalakīrti Sūtra it is called "Attained Great Power."

【Lower Left】
【Title】
Bhaiṣajyasamudgata Bodhisattva (Superior Medicine Bodhisattva)
【Description】
In the past, during the time of Vaiḍūryaprabhāsa Buddha, there was a monk named Sūryagarbha
who listened to the teachings of an elder named Nakṣatraprabhā,
and offered haritaki and various other medicines.
Therefore there was a younger brother called Medicine King,
and the one called Vidyutprabhā offered supreme fine medicine of ghee,
hence called Medicine Superior.

Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas:
Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva (Kanzeon Bosatsu)
Bhaiṣajyarāja Bodhisattva (Yakuō Bosatsu)
Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva (Daiseishi Bosatsu)
Bhaiṣajyasamudgata Bodhisattva (Yakujō Bosatsu)
