【Header, horizontal text】
Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas

【Upper Right】
【Title】
Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva (Kanzeon Bosatsu)
【Description】
In Sanskrit "Avalokiteśvara," translated into Chinese as "Guanzizai" (Observer of Freedom). In the Hokke Mongu it appears as "Bōrukiteisei," and in the Ōjō Yōshū as "Mida."
After the True Dharma perishes, Avalokiteśvara will achieve perfect enlightenment and be called "Fukō Kudoku Sannō Nyorai" (Universal Light Merit Mountain King Tathāgata).

【Lower Right】
【Title】
Bhaiṣajyarāja Bodhisattva (Yakuō Bosatsu)
【Description】
In Sanskrit called "Agada." This bodhisattva uses great compassionate arts to remove the sufferings and illnesses caused by various karmas of all sentient beings and freely grants them happiness, hence the name "Medicine King."
Also burned his body as an offering to the Buddha, continuing to burn for twelve hundred years without being extinguished (as seen in the Mahāprajñāpāramitā-śāstra, volume 16).

【Upper Left】
【Title】
Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva (Daiseishi Bosatsu)
【Description】
In Sanskrit "Mahāsthāmaprāpta," translated as "Great Power Attainer." The Śūraṅgama Sūtra states: "In my original causal ground, I entered the tolerance of non-arising through the mind of Buddha-recitation, and now in this world I gather those who recite the Buddha's name and lead them back to the Pure Land."
In the Vimalakīrti Sūtra it is called "Tokudaiseishi."

【Lower Left】
【Title】
Bhaiṣajyasamudgata Bodhisattva (Yakujō Bosatsu)
【Description】
In the time of the past Buddha Vaiḍūryaprabha, there was a monk called "Nichizoō." He listened to the Dharma teaching of an elder called "Seishukukō" and offered haritaki and various medicines. Therefore there was a younger brother called Yakuō, named "Denkōmyō." Because he offered supreme daigo medicine, he is called "Yakujō."

Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas:
Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva (Kanzeon Bosatsu)
Bhaiṣajyarāja Bodhisattva (Yakuō Bosatsu)
Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva (Daiseishi Bosatsu)
Bhaiṣajyasamudgata Bodhisattva (Yakujō Bosatsu)
