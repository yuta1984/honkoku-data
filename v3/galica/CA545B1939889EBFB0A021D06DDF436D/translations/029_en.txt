Master Fu of the Liang Dynasty
His name was Xi, his courtesy name was Xuanfeng, also called Great Master Shanhui, and also called Great Master Dongyang. At age sixteen, he married a woman of the Liu clan and had two sons: Pucheng and Pujian. Once, when the Great Master looked at his reflection in water, he saw a circular halo and jeweled canopy. Pujian pointed to it, and Pucheng saw it and smiled. The "Record of Buddhist Patriarchs" states that he resided at Shuanglin Temple in Wushang County, Dongyang Prefecture. Because there were so many catalogs of Buddhist scriptures that it was troublesome to view them, he constructed a box that could be rotated eight times around a single pillar, creating a sutra repository that remained until later generations. He passed away on the twenty-seventh day of the fourth month of the first year of Dajian in Benzhou.
(Marginal note: This corresponds to the己丑 year, the thirtieth year of Emperor Kinmei of our country)
