【Upper Section】
Master Fu of the Liang Dynasty
His name was Xi, his courtesy name was Xuanfeng, also called Great Master Shanhui, and also called Great Master Dongyang. At sixteen years old, he married Lady Liu and had two sons: Pucheng and Pujian. Once, when the Great Master was contemplating his reflection in water, he saw a circular halo and precious canopy. When Pujian pointed at it, Pucheng saw it and smiled. According to the Records of Buddhist Patriarchs, he resided at Shuanglin Temple in Wushang County, Dongyang Commandery. Finding it laborious to consult the numerous Buddhist scriptures in the catalogs, he constructed a revolving bookcase that could rotate eight times around a single pillar, which has been preserved as a "wheel repository" until the present age. He passed away in his home province on the 27th day of the 4th month of the first year of Dajian (corresponding to the 30th year of Emperor Kinmei's reign in Japan, the year of Ji-Chou).
