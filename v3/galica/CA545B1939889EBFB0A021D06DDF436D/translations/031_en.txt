**The Teaching of Nine Grades of Birth**

**Superior Grade, Superior Birth**
First: Those who practice loving-kindness without killing, observe various moral precepts, and recite the Mahayana Vaipulya sutras.

Third: Those who practice the Six Perfections (pāramitā), dedicate merit, and make vows to be born in that land (the Pure Land).

**Superior Grade, Middle Birth**
Those who need not necessarily uphold and recite the Vaipulya sutras but have good understanding of their meaning and principles.

Amida Buddha, surrounded by a great assembly, comes before the practitioner carrying a purple-gold platform.

**Superior Grade, Inferior Birth**
Those who believe in cause and effect, and simply generate the aspiration for enlightenment (bodhicitta) and wish to be born in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.

At the time of death, Amida Buddha, Avalokiteśvara, Mahāsthāmaprāpta, and five hundred transformation Buddhas praise them, saying: "O child of the Dharma, you have now generated a pure mind."

**Middle Grade, Superior Birth**
Those who uphold the five precepts, eight precepts, and various precepts, do not commit the five grave offenses, and wish to be born in the Western Pure Land.

Amida Buddha appears with various monks, and upon paying homage to the Buddha, one is immediately born in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.
