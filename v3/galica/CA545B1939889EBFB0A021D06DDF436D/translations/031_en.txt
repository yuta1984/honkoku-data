【Upper Frame - Horizontal Text】
The Nine Grades of Amida

【Upper Right】
【Upper Section】
■Superior Grade, Superior Birth (Jōbon-jōshō)
【Lower Section】
Those of superior grade, superior birth are: first, those who have compassionate hearts and do not kill, possessing various precepts and practices; second, those who read and recite Mahayana Vaipulya sutras; third, those who practice the six mindfulnesses, dedicate merit, and make vows to be born in that land (the Pure Land).

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Superior Grade, Middle Birth (Jōbon-chūshō)
【Lower Section】
Those of superior grade, middle birth do not necessarily need to uphold and recite the Vaipulya sutras, but they well understand their meaning and principles. Amida Buddha, surrounded by a great assembly, holds a purple-gold dais and comes before the practitioner.

【Upper Left】
【Upper Section】
■Superior Grade, Lower Birth (Jōbon-geshō)
【Lower Section】
Those of superior grade, lower birth also believe in cause and effect, but simply generate the aspiration for enlightenment and are born in the Land of Bliss. At their end, Amida Buddha, Avalokiteshvara, Mahasthamaprapta, and five hundred transformation Buddhas praise them, saying "Dharma-child, you are now born."

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■Middle Grade, Superior Birth (Chūbon-jōshō)
【Lower Section】
Those of middle grade, superior birth uphold the five precepts, eight precepts, and various precepts, do not commit the five grave offenses, and wish to be born in the Western Direction (Pure Land). Amida Buddha appears with various monks, and upon paying homage to the Buddha, they are immediately born in the Land of Bliss.
