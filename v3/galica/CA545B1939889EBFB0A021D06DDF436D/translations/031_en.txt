**The Nine Grades of Amida**

**Highest Grade, Highest Birth**
Those of the highest grade, highest birth are: first, those who practice loving-kindness without killing, observe various precepts, and recite Mahayana Vaipulya sutras. Third, those who practice the six paramitas, transfer merit, and vow to be born in that land (the Pure Land).

**Highest Grade, Middle Birth**
Those of the highest grade, middle birth do not necessarily need to uphold and recite the Vaipulya sutras, but they understand well their meaning and principles. Amida Buddha, surrounded by a great assembly, comes before the practitioner holding a purple-gold platform.

**Highest Grade, Lowest Birth**
Those of the highest grade, lowest birth believe in cause and effect, and simply generate the aspiration for enlightenment, wishing to be born in the Land of Bliss. At the time of death, Amida Buddha, Avalokiteshvara, Mahasthamaprapta, and five hundred transformation Buddhas appear and praise them, saying "Dharma-child, you are now about to be reborn."

**Middle Grade, Highest Birth**
Those of the middle grade, highest birth uphold the five precepts, eight precepts, and various other precepts, do not commit the five grave offenses, and wish to be born in the Western Pure Land. Amida Buddha comes with various monks, and after paying respect to the Buddha, they are immediately born in the Land of Bliss.
