【Upper frame, horizontal text】
Seven Kannon

【Upper Right】
Thousand-Armed Kannon

The thousand hands are actually forty arms. Each of the twenty-five existences has forty arms, and when combined together they make a thousand hands.

Root mudra, Nine-Headed Dragon mudra (also Great Compassion Avalokiteśvara)

【Lower Right】
Horse-Head Kannon

The profundity of this venerable one's responsive benefits is like a horse that thinks only of water and grass and knows nothing else.

【Upper Left】
Eleven-Faced Kannon

The front three faces show compassion, the left three faces show wrath, the right three faces have upward-pointing fangs - they rejoice when seeing good and mock and laugh when seeing evil. The main central face neither laughs nor shows anger, being non-dual regarding good and evil. The Buddha on the crown is the past Samyak-praṇidhāna-jñāna Tathāgata. This is called "manifesting causative form in response to fruition."

Also Great Light Universal Illumination Avalokiteśvara

【Lower Left】
Sacred Avalokiteśvara

In the left hand holds an unopened lotus, representing the form of trying to make the wonderful Dharma lotus of all sentient beings, which has not yet opened, to bloom.

The right hand shows the mudra of great compassion bestowing fearlessness

Also Great Compassion Avalokiteśvara
