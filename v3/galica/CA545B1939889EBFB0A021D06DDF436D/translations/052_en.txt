The Seven Forms of Avalokiteśvara

**Thousand-Armed Avalokiteśvara (Sahasrabhuja)**
The thousand hands are actually forty arms. Since each of the twenty-five realms of existence has forty arms, when combined they total a thousand hands.
The fundamental mudrā is the Nine-Headed Dragon seal. Also called Great Compassion Avalokiteśvara.

**Horse-Headed Avalokiteśvara (Hayagrīva)**
The profound nature of this deity's responsive benefits is like a horse that thinks only of water and grass, as if knowing nothing else.

**Eleven-Faced Avalokiteśvara (Ekādaśamukha)**
The front three faces show compassion, the left three faces show wrathful anger, and the right three faces have upward-pointing fangs. They show joy when seeing good and laugh mockingly when seeing evil. The main central face neither laughs nor shows anger, representing the non-duality of good and evil. The Buddha on the crown is the past Samyakprabha Tathāgata, which is called "manifesting cause while facing the result." Also called Great Light Universal Illumination Avalokiteśvara.

**Sacred Avalokiteśvara (Āryāvalokiteśvara)**
In the left hand holds an unopened lotus flower, representing the form that will cause the wonderful Dharma lotus of all sentient beings, which has not yet bloomed, to open. The right hand forms the mudrā of great compassion bestowing fearlessness. Also called Great Loving-Kindness Avalokiteśvara.
