Dipankara Buddha (Jōkō-butsu)
First Day

According to the Treatise on Great Wisdom (Chirin), the Burning Lamp Buddha is Dipankara Buddha. The Records of Buddhist Patriarchs (Busso Tōki) states that the Burning Lamp Buddha dwelt in the world for 8.4 trillion years before entering nirvana.

Atsuta Daimyōjin
First Day

The Thirty Guardian Deities are the protective deities of the Tendai school. When the Great Master Jikaku (Ennin) was practicing the Śūraṅgama Sutra at Ryōgon Cedar Cave, one deity would manifest each day for his protection. This deity is enshrined in Aichi District, Owari Province.

Lamp Light Buddha (Tōmyō-butsu)
Second Day

According to Jōchin, what is called Sun-Moon-Lamp-Light and Cloud-Sovereign-Lamp-King are the same entity with different names. The appearances of sun and moon should be combined as one body.

Suwa Daimyōjin
Second Day

Enshrined in Suwa District, Shinano Province. The original ground (honji) is Samantabhadra Bodhisattva.

Prabhūtaratna Buddha (Tahō-butsu)
Third Day

Prabhūtaratna Buddha of the Pure Treasure World in the East dwells in a great stupa of seven treasures, 500 yojanas in height, and emerged from the great earth of Vulture Peak. (This appears in the Essential Meaning of the Lotus Sutra)

Hirota Daimyōjin
Third Day

One of the five shrines of Nishinomiya in Muko District, Settsu Province. The original ground (honji) is Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva.
