【Header, horizontal text】
Thirty Days of Secret Buddhas

【Upper right】
【Upper section】
■Dīpaṅkara Buddha 《margin note: 1st day》
【Lower section】
According to the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Śāstra, Dīpaṅkara Buddha is the same as Dingguang Buddha. According to the Fozu Tongji (Chronicle of Buddha and Patriarchs), Dīpaṅkara Buddha dwelt in the world for 8.4 trillion years before entering nirvana.

【Lower right】
【Upper section】
■Atsuta Daimyōjin 《margin note: 1st day》
【Lower section】
【Upper right corner】
Thirty Guardian Deities
【Main text】
The Thirty Guardian Deities are the protective deities of the Tendai school. When the Great Master Jikaku was performing the Śūraṅgama Sūtra practice in the cedar cave, each day one deity would manifest to protect him. Enshrined in Aichi District, Owari Province.

【Center upper】
【Upper section】
■Dīpaṅkara Buddha 《margin note: 2nd day》
【Lower section】
According to Jōchin, what is called Candrasūryapradīpa and what is called Meghasvararāja are the same essence with different names. One should consider the unified appearance of cloud and moon together.

【Center lower】
■Suwa Daimyōjin 《margin note: 2nd day》
【Lower section】
Enshrined in Suwa District, Shinano Province. The original ground (honji) is Samantabhadra.

【Upper left】
【Upper section】
■Prabhūtaratna Buddha 《margin note: 3rd day》
【Lower section】
Prabhūtaratna Buddha of the Pure Jewel World in the east dwells in a great stupa of seven treasures, 500 yojanas in height, and emerged from the great earth of Vulture Peak (as seen in the Hokke Daii).

【Lower left】
【Upper section】
■Hirota Daimyōjin 《margin note: 3rd day》
One of the five shrines of Nishinomiya in Muko District, Settsu Province. The original ground (honji) is Mahāsthāmaprāpta.
