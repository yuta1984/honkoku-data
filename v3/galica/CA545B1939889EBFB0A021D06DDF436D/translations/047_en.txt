【Upper Right】
【Title】
Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Kokūzō Bosatsu)
【Description】
Being the dharma-body of the Five Dharmas and Five Buddhas, there is nothing in the realm of empty space (ākāśadhātu) that cannot attain enlightenment. Just as we call empty space a "storehouse," that realm of emptiness has no boundaries or locations. This venerable one similarly has infinite wisdom of great compassion for benefiting beings, and is named by comparing this limitless quality to the storehouse of empty space.

【Lower Right】
【Title】
Guṇagarbha Bodhisattva (Tokuzō Bosatsu)
【Description】
This bodhisattva is a great being (mahāsattva) who possesses the merit of vast great compassion. Following the various spiritual capacities of all sentient beings, he opens millions of storehouses of merit and great compassion to guide all sentient beings. Therefore, he is called Guṇagarbha Bodhisattva.

【Upper Left】
【Title】
Ratnagarbha Bodhisattva (Hōzō Bosatsu)
【Description】
Treasure (ratna) refers to various precious jewels. Storehouse (garbha) means treasury. Just as worldly merit and precious treasures are stored in earthen storehouses to provide for all needs, this great being also opens the great treasure house of merit and great compassion according to the wishes and aspirations of all sentient beings, liberating them.

【Lower Left】
【Title】
Suvarṇagarbha Bodhisattva (Konzō Bosatsu)
【Description】
Gold is of an indestructible nature—it does not drown when entering water, nor does it change color when entering fire. This bodhisattva, responding to the merit of reciting the name during the causal stage, becomes part of the holy assembly of the Western Pure Land and obtains the virtue of indestructible gold. Therefore, he is called Suvarṇagarbha (Gold Storehouse).
