Twenty-Five Bodhisattvas (continued)

【Upper Right】
■Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Space Treasury)
The five dharmas and five buddhas are the dharma-body, so in the dharma realm of empty space, there is nothing that does not attain enlightenment. For example, just as we call empty space a "treasury," that realm of emptiness has no limitations or locations. This honored one similarly has infinite wisdom of great compassion for benefiting beings, and is named by analogy to the treasury of empty space for having no limitations.

【Lower Right】
■Guṇagarbha Bodhisattva (Virtue Treasury)
This bodhisattva is a great being with the merit of vast great compassion. According to the myriad capacities of all sentient beings, he opens the millions of storehouses of merit and great compassion to guide all sentient beings. Therefore he is called Virtue Treasury Bodhisattva.

【Upper Left】
■Ratnagarbha Bodhisattva (Jewel Treasury)
"Jewel" refers to the seven kinds of precious treasures. "Treasury" means storehouse. For example, just as the precious treasures and blessings of the world are stored in earth storehouses to provide for all matters, this great being also opens the great treasure house of merit and great compassion according to the wishes and hopes of all sentient beings to save them.

【Lower Left】
■Suvarṇagarbha Bodhisattva (Gold Treasury)
Gold has an indestructible nature - it does not sink when entering water and does not change color when entering fire. This bodhisattva, responding to the merit of invoking the name in the causal stage, becomes part of the holy assembly of the Western Pure Land and attains the virtue and meaning of indestructible gold. Therefore he is called Gold Treasury.
