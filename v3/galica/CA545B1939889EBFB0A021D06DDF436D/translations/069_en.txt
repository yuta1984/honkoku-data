■《Side note: Eastern Direction》Trailokyavijaya Wisdom King
【Lower section】
The original ground is Yakushi (Bhaiṣajyaguru). The so-called "three worlds" refers to greed, anger, and ignorance, and subduing these
three poisons is called "conquering the three worlds." Stepping on Maheśvara with the left foot represents
severing the afflictive hindrances.
　　【Illustration】
Stepping on
Umā with the right
foot represents severing the cognitive hindrances. Since afflictive
hindrances are coarse, they are likened to the male and stepped on strongly; since cognitive hindrances are subtle, they are likened to the female and stepped on gently.

【Upper section】
■《Side note: Western Direction》Yamāntaka Wisdom King
【Lower section】
The original ground is Amida (Amitābha). In the Blue Dragon Commentary, it states that he subdues all evil poisonous dragons.
The Luminous Commentary says that Yamāntaka means his virtue surpasses all others,
and he strikes fear into all dragons.

■Buddha Mother Great Peacock Wisdom King
【Lower section】
The Peacock Sutra says that those who recite the divine mantra of the Peacock Wisdom King will have a life-
span of one hundred years, living for a hundred autumns.
