**Cintāmaṇicakra Avalokiteśvara (Wish-Fulfilling Wheel)**
The text of the fundamental vow states: "I vow that if even one person is not saved within great compassion, I will not follow the願 of the two worlds. If they do not return to original enlightenment from false sins and transgressions, I will abandon great compassion." Cintāmaṇicakra is the transformative lord of the heaven realm. The wish-fulfilling jewel is indeed a supreme treasure of heaven. Also called Great Brahma Profound Avalokiteśvara.

**Cundī Avalokiteśvara**
The Śamatha-vipaśyanā states: "Avalokiteśvara enters samādhi such as the Youthful samādhi and others, breaking all three obstacles in the human realm, subduing pride and arrogance, and revealing the Buddha nature." Also called Celestial Being and Great Man Avalokiteśvara.

**Amoghapāśa (Unfailing Lasso)**
The Thousand-Armed Sūtra states: "The Buddha said, 'This bodhisattva is named Avalokiteśvara Īśvara and is also named Pāśa (Lasso).'" The meaning is that in the great ocean of birth and death, the bait of the wonderful Dharma lotus is scattered, and with the lasso of unwavering mindfulness, the fish of sentient beings are pulled up and sent to the other shore of bodhi.

**Willow Avalokiteśvara**
Avalokiteśvara, with a responsive heart of compassion, adapts in every way to the pleasurable desires of beings' spiritual capacities, like a willow swaying in the spring breeze. Therefore, this extends throughout all six forms of Avalokiteśvara.
