## The Seven Kannon (continued)

---

**Nyoirin Kannon (Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Avalokiteśvara)**

The *Nyoi Honzei Mon* (Scripture of the Original Vows of Nyoirin) states: "Within my vow of great compassion, I swear that not a single person shall fail to follow the vow of the two existences [this world and the next] and return [to Original Enlightenment]; I shall not allow them to remain lost amid falsehood and transgression, but shall return them to Original Enlightenment."

〔Figure〕

(Nyoirin Kannon) vows never to abandon great compassion (until all beings return to Original Enlightenment). Nyoirin is the transforming master (*keshu*) of the heavenly realm (the highest of the Six Realms), and the wish-fulfilling jewel (*nyoi no tama*) is the supreme treasure of the heavens.
○ Also known as: Daibōn Jin'on Kanzeon (Kannon of Great Brahma and Profound Depth)

---

**Juntei Kannon (Avalokiteśvara of Seventy-Seven Kotis of Buddhas)**

The *Shikan* (Mohe Zhiguan; *Mahāśamatha-vipaśyanā*) states: "Entering samādhis such as the 'Kannon-like-a-child samādhi' (*Kannonnyoyū zanmai*) and others, (this Kannon) completely destroys the Three Obstacles (*sanjō*: the obstacles of affliction, karma, and retribution) for all beings in the human realm,

〔Figure〕

subdues arrogance and pride (*kyōman*), and causes the Buddha-nature to be clearly revealed."
○ Also known as: Ten'nin Jōbu Kannon (Kannon, the Great Person of Heavenly Beings)

---

**Fukū Kenjaku (Amoghapāśa — Avalokiteśvara of the Infallible Lasso)**

The *Senju-kyō* (Thousand-Hand Sūtra) states: "The Buddha said: This bodhisattva is called Kanzeon Jizai (Avalokiteśvara). Also called Kenjaku (Lasso)."
○ The meaning is this: in the great ocean of birth and death (*shōji no taikai*), (this Kannon) scatters the bait of the Wondrous Dharma Lotus (*myōhō renge*), and with the lasso (*nawa*) of *shinen fukū* (the infallible [power of] mindful thought), hooks the fish of sentient beings and sends them to the far shore of Bodhi (enlightenment).

〔Figure〕

---

**Yōryū Kannon (Willow-Branch Avalokiteśvara)**

Kannon, with a heart of responsive compassion (*ōshin jūhi*), in whatever way it may be, responds and conforms to the desires and wishes (*gyōyoku*) of sentient beings according to their capacity (*ki*),

〔Figure〕

just as the willow branches sway and bend in the spring breeze. For this reason, (Yōryū Kannon) encompasses all Six Kannon in totality.
