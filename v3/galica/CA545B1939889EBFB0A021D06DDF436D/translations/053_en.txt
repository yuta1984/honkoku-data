【Upper Right】
Cintāmaṇicakra Avalokiteśvara (Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Kannon)

The text of the fundamental vow states: "In my vow, if even one person in the great compassion does not achieve the wish of two worlds, I will not return to original enlightenment amidst false sins and transgressions, and will abandon great compassion." Cintāmaṇicakra is the transformation lord of the heavenly realm. This wish-fulfilling jewel is the supreme treasure of heaven. Great Brahma Profound Avalokiteśvara

【Lower Right】
Cundī Avalokiteśvara

The Śamatha-vipaśyanā states: "Entering samādhi such as Avalokiteśvara's illusion-like samādhi, all in the human realm break the three obstacles, subdue pride, and are shown the Buddha-nature." Celestial Being Great Man Avalokiteśvara

【Upper Left】
Amoghapāśa (Infallible Lasso)

The Thousand-Armed Sūtra states: "The Buddha said, this bodhisattva is named Avalokiteśvara Īśvara, and also named Pāśa (Lasso)." The meaning is to scatter the bait of the wonderful Dharma lotus in the great ocean of saṃsāra, and using the lasso of unfailing mindfulness, fish up the fish of sentient beings and send them to the other shore of bodhi.

【Lower Left】
Willow Avalokiteśvara

Avalokiteśvara, with responsive compassionate mind, adapts in all ways to the desires and inclinations of beings, like a willow swaying in the spring breeze. Therefore, this generally extends across the six Avalokiteśvaras.
