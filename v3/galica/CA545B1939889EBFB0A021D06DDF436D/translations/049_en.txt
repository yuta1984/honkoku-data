【Upper Right】
Sun-Illumination King Bodhisattva

Because this bodhisattva illuminates and destroys the delusion and darkness of sentient beings with the sunlight of wisdom, the name was obtained through wisdom-virtue. The Avatamsaka Sutra states: "The wisdom-sun illuminates the world and eliminates the clouds of birth and death."

【Lower Right】
Myriad Treasures King Bodhisattva

"Shu" (myriad) is read as "moromoro." "Ho" (treasure) is interpreted as "chin" (precious) and read as "takara." "O" (king) means sovereignty. Therefore, the meaning is "King of Myriad Treasures." The treasures refer to the seven types of precious treasures and the seven types of royal treasures. Not limited to these, this name encompasses all treasures.

【Upper Left】
Moonlight King Bodhisattva

Because this bodhisattva perfectly possesses virtues that surpass the three virtues of the moon-disc in this world, this is directly manifested in the name-title that is proclaimed.

【Lower Left】
Samadhi Bodhisattva

Because this bodhisattva attains sovereignty among all samadhis, this name is proclaimed. That samadhi is correctly called "samadhi" (three-摩-地). This is translated as "right concentration" and rendered as "the place where the right mind practices."
