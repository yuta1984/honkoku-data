■Lower Grade, Lower Birth (Gebon-geshō)
【Lower Section】
Those of lower grade, lower birth are people who have committed the five grave offenses. At the time of death, when a good spiritual friend recites the nembutsu (Buddha's name), each recitation removes the sins of life and death, and immediately a golden lotus flower appears before that person like the solar disc, and they immediately attain birth in the Land of Bliss.

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Amida of Wonderful Observing Wisdom (Myōkanzatsu-chi no Mida)
【Lower Section】
The Collection of Essential Sutras states: His father was the wheel-turning sage king Getsujo, his mother was Shushō Myōgan (Supremely Excellent Beautiful Face), and he was also the red lotus-colored Amida. His name was called Kōshika, and he abandoned his royal position to become a monk, taking the name Dharma-store Bhikkhu (Hōzō Biku).

【Upper Section】
■Mandala Amida (Mandara no Mida)
【Lower Section】
The mudra of the three bodies: the right palm is revealed and the left palm is concealed. There is a swastika symbol on the chest, and the thumb is joined with the ring finger and middle finger. This is the mudra of the dharma-body and reward-body. The right hand joins the thumb with the index finger - this is the mudra of the response-body.

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■Amida of Five Kalpas of Contemplation (Gokō Shiyui no Mida)
【Lower Section】
The three characters "Muryōju" (Immeasurable Life) represent the three truths of emptiness, provisional existence, and the middle way of all Buddhas of the three periods. The name resulting from five kalpas of contemplation is the fundamental source of the eighty-four thousand dharma treasures.
