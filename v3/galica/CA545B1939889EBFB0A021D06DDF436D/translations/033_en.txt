■Lower Grade, Lower Birth (Gebon-geshō)
【Lower Section】
Those of lower grade, lower birth are people who have committed the five grave offenses. At the time of death, a good spiritual friend recites the Buddha's name, and with each recitation the sins of life and death are removed. Immediately a golden lotus flower appears before that person like a sun disc, and they immediately attain birth in the Land of Bliss.

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Amida of Discriminating Wisdom (Myōkanzatchi no Mida)
【Lower Section】
The Collection of Essential Sutras states: His father was King Getsujo Ten'rin Seiō (Moon-Superior Wheel-Turning Sacred King), and his mother was Shushō Myōgan (Exceptionally Superior Wonderful Face). He is also called the Red Hariri-colored Amida. His name was called Kōshika, and abandoning his royal position, he became a monk and was named Hōzō Bhikkhu (Dharmakara Monk).

【Upper Section】
■Mandala Amida (Mandara no Mida)
【Lower Section】
The mudra of the Three Bodies shows the right palm and hides the left palm. There is a swastika symbol on the chest, and the thumb is joined with the ring finger and middle finger. This is the mudra of the Dharma Body and Reward Body. Joining the thumb and index finger of the right hand—this is the mudra of the Response Body.

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■Amida of Five Kalpas of Contemplation (Gokō Shiyui no Mida)
【Lower Section】
The three characters "Muryōju" (Infinite Life) represent the three truths of emptiness, provisional existence, and the middle way of all Buddhas of the three time periods. The sacred name of Five Kalpas of Contemplation is the fundamental source of the eighty thousand dharma treasures.
