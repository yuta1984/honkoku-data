**Lowest Grade, Lowest Birth**
Those of the lowest grade, lowest birth are people who have committed the five grave offenses, and at the time of death, a good spiritual friend encourages them to chant the name of Buddha. By reciting the Buddha's name, the sins of life and death are eliminated, and immediately a golden lotus flower appears before that person like the sun disc, and they can immediately be reborn in the Land of Bliss.

**Amida of Wonderful Observing Wisdom**
The "Collection of Essential Sutras" states that his father was King Getsujo Dai-tenrino-sho-o (Great Wheel-Turning Sacred King Moon-Above), and his mother was Shusho-myogan (Supremely Excellent Wonderful Countenance). Together with the second Harishikimi-da, his name was called Koshika. He renounced the throne and became a monk, taking the name Dharma-store Bhikkhu (Hozo Biku).

**Mandala Amida**
The mudra of the three bodies shows the right palm and hides the left palm. There is a swastika symbol on the chest, and the thumb, ring finger, and middle finger are joined. This is the mudra of the two bodies - the Dharma body and the reward body. Joining the thumb and index finger of the right hand is the mudra of the response body.

**Amida of Five Kalpas of Contemplation**
The three characters "Muryoju" (Infinite Life) represent the three truths of emptiness, provisional existence, and the middle way of all Buddhas of the three times. The name resulting from five kalpas of contemplation is the source of the eighty thousand Dharma treasures.
