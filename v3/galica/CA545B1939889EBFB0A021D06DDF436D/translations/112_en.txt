Brahmā (King of Brahmā Heaven)
According to the Zhūlín, the name of Brahmā is Yuèqì, and in this land he is called Dàxiàng, or alternatively Dàmén.
According to the Abhidharmakośa, he has no separate dwelling place but resides in a high pavilion among the auxiliary devas.

Sūrya (Sun Deva)
According to the Guǎngyǎ, he is named Guāng Líng, and is also called Xiānmíng, Dàmíng, and Yáng Wū. He is also called Rì Yù Yì Hé.
In the Abhidharmakośa, the circular light is a sphaṭika jewel.

Īśāna Deva
This is the deity Izanagi no Mikoto. He is one of the twelve celestial deities who protect the Buddhist teachings.
He holds a dish in his left hand and a halberd in his right hand.

Śakra Devendra (Indra, King of the Devas)
According to the Madhyamāgama, because he becomes king of the Thirty-three Heavens, he is named Yīntílì.
According to the Mahāprajñāpāramitā-śāstra, Śakra should properly be called Devānām according to the Sanskrit pronunciation.

Agni (Fire Deva)
Reddish flesh-colored, dwelling within great flames, with four hands holding a sage's staff, ritual vessel, prayer beads, and knowing the mudrā of fire.

Yama Deva
Called Yama Heaven, which is fully translated in this land as "Good Division." [Ninnō-kyō Commentary]
In the Zhūlín, he is named Miàoshàn (Wondrous Good).
He holds a staff topped with a human head and knows the mudrā of Yama.
