【Upper Section】
■Tathāgata Beyond the Mountains
【Lower Section】
It is transmitted that when the sacred form of Amida appeared on the peak of Yokawa on Mount Hiei, the monk-supervisor Genshin worshipped this vision and then depicted and preserved it on the sleeve of plain silk. For details, there is a painted image he copied (along with its origin story) at Eshin-in temple in Yokawa.

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Tathāgata Many Treasures
【Lower Section】
The Lotus Sutra says: Long ago in the past, in the eastern direction beyond immeasurable thousands of myriads of millions of asaṃkhyeya worlds, there was a land called Treasure Pure. In that place there was a Buddha called Many Treasures.

【Upper Left】
【Upper Section】
■Dainichi of the Diamond Realm
【Lower Section】
The Diamond Realm arises from the fruit-wisdom of Vairocana Buddha. Diamond is the bodhi-realm, which is intrinsic nature. According to the "Fruit Treasure Commentary on the Dainichi Sutra," Vairocana Buddha is another name for the sun, meaning to dispel darkness and illuminate universally.

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■Dainichi of the Womb Realm
【Lower Section】
In Sanskrit it is called Vairocana, which is translated here as "Supreme Manifest Wide-Eyed Treasury Tathāgata." The Womb Treasury originally arises from the place of sentient beings' afflictions and desires, employing the wisdom seal of the Tathāgata.
