■Yamagoshi Nyorai (Buddha Who Appeared Over the Mountain)
【Lower Section】
It is transmitted that when the revered form of Amida appeared on the peak of Yokogawa at Mt. Hiei, the monk Genshin bowed to it and immediately depicted and preserved it on the sleeve of white silk. For details, there is the image he copied (along with its origin story) at Yokogawa Eshin-in.

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Tahō Nyorai (Many Treasures Tathagata)
【Lower Section】
The Lotus Sutra states: In the past, in the eastern direction, in immeasurable thousands of millions of billions of asamkhya worlds, there was a land called Treasure Purity. In that land there was a Buddha named Many Treasures.

【Upper Left】
【Upper Section】
■Dainichi of the Diamond Realm (Kongōkai no Dainichi)
【Lower Section】
The Diamond Realm arises from the wisdom of Vairocana Buddha's enlightened state. Diamond represents the nature of the bodhi realm. According to the Dainichi-kyō Fruit Treasure Commentary, Vairocana Buddha is another name for the sun, meaning the removal of darkness and universal illumination.

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■Dainichi of the Womb Realm (Taizōkai no Dainichi)
【Lower Section】
In Sanskrit it is called Vairocana, which translates as Supreme Manifest Wide-Eyed Treasury Tathagata. The Womb Realm originally arises from the place of sentient beings' afflictions and desires, employing the wisdom mudra of the Tathagata.
