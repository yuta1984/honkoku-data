**Yamagoshi Tathagata (Buddha Appearing Over the Mountain)**
It is transmitted that when the venerable form of Amida appeared on the peak of Yokawa on Mount Hiei, the monk Genshin reverently beheld it and immediately depicted and preserved it on plain silk sleeves. For details, there is the image he copied at Yokawa Eshin-in (along with its origin story).

**Taho Tathagata (Many Treasures Buddha)**
The Lotus Sutra states: "In the past, in the eastern direction, beyond immeasurable thousands of myriads of billions of asamkhya worlds, there was a land called Treasure Purity. In it there was a Buddha named Many Treasures."

**Dainichi of the Diamond Realm**
The Diamond Realm arises from the fruit-wisdom of Vairocana Buddha. The vajra (diamond/thunderbolt) is the nature of bodhi (enlightenment).
Wisdom Mudra
Commentary on the Dainichi Sutra: Vairocana Buddha is another name for the sun, meaning the elimination of darkness and universal illumination.

**Dainichi of the Womb Realm**
In Sanskrit, Vairocana Buddha; here translated as "Supreme Manifest Wide-Eyed Treasury Tathagata."
Dharma Realm Concentration Mudra
The Womb Realm originally arises from the place of sentient beings' afflictions and desires, employing the wisdom mudra of the Tathagata.
