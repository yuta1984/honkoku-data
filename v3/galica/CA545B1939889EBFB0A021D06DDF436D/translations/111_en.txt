When conferring the Buddha-nature samaya precepts, screens depicting the Twelve Devas (Jūni-ten) are erected to manifest the forms of their descent.

Prithvi (Earth Deva)

Chandra (Moon Deva)
According to the Zhūlín, the Moon Deva has five hundred lights that shine downward and five hundred lights that travel sideways to illuminate. Therefore, the Moon Deva is also called "Thousand Bright Lights" and is also named "Cool and Cold Bright Light."

Vaiśravaṇa (Bishamonten)
Regarding Vaiśravaṇa, it means to draw [beings] with the hook of desire into the Buddhist path.
Regarding Vaiśravaṇa, it means the vow to protect by destroying monks who break the precepts.

Vāyu (Wind Deva)
Reddish flesh-colored, holding a staff and banner.
