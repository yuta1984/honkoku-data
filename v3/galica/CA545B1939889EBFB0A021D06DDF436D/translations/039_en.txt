**Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva**
This is the great teacher from the distant past, the mother of wisdom when buddhas of the three times appear in the world.

This is the bodhisattva (sattva) who awakened the aspiration for enlightenment among the Tathāgatas of the ten directions.

**Child Mañjuśrī**
The Nirvana Sutra states: "In the buddha-lands where buddhas exist in the worlds of the ten directions, all the propagation and exposition of the Mahāyāna [teachings] is through the power of Mañjuśrī."

**Samantabhadra Bodhisattva**
The spreading of virtuous benefits everywhere is called "universal" (fu), and benevolent compassion and wise awakening are called "worthy" (ken).
The Flower Ornament Sutra's Chapter on Worthy Conduct and Vows states: "I vow that when I am about to meet my life's end, I will completely remove all obstacles and obstructions, face and behold Buddha Amitābha directly, and immediately attain rebirth in the Land of Peace and Bliss."

**Child Samantabhadra**
The Commentary on the Principle and趣 Sutra states: "Following conditions and reaching everywhere universally is called 'universal' (fu), and leading beings into the Buddha Way is called 'worthy' (ken)."

**Pronunciations**
