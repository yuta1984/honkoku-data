【Upper Right】
【Upper Section】
■Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva
【Lower Section】
This is the great guide from the distant past, the wisdom-mother of all buddhas who appear in the three times (past, present, and future). This is the bodhisattva who inspires the awakening mind of the Tathāgatas of the ten directions.

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Child Mañjuśrī
【Lower Section】
The Nirvāṇa Sūtra states: "In the ten directions of worlds, whether there are buddhas or not, in the Mahāyāna of buddha-lands, all that is propagated is the power of Mañjuśrī."

【Upper Left】
【Upper Section】
■Samantabhadra Bodhisattva
【Lower Section】
The pervasive spread of virtue and benefit is called "universal" (fu), and benevolent compassion and wise awakening is called "worthy" (gen). The Avataṃsaka Samantabhadra's Vows states: "I pray that when I approach the time of death, I will completely remove all obstacles and obstructions, face and see Buddha Amitābha, and immediately attain rebirth in the Land of Peace and Bliss."

【Lower Left】
【Upper Section】
■Child Samantabhadra
【Lower Section】
The Rishukyo-shō states: "Following conditions to reach everywhere is called 'universal,' and leading beings into the Buddha-path is called 'worthy.'"
