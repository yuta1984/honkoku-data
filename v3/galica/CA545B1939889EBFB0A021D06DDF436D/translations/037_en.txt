■Nichigaku-Tōmyō-Butsu (Sun-Moon Lamp Light Buddha)
【Lower Section】
The first volume of the Lotus Sutra states: "In the past, in immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable asaṃkhyeya kalpas, at that time there was a Buddha called Sun-Moon Lamp Light Buddha World-Honored One."

【Lower Right】
【Upper Section】
■Niman-Tōmyō-Butsu (Twenty Thousand Lamp Light Buddha)
【Lower Section】
According to Hokke-Teichin's commentary, the meaning represented by twenty thousand is ten thousand practices and ten thousand good deeds. Also, when a thousand tathāgatas are arranged according to the ten good deeds, it becomes ten thousand. This is made twenty thousand by dividing into provisional teachings and true teachings.

【Upper Left】
【Upper Section】
■Sanman-Tōmyō-Butsu (Thirty Thousand Lamp Light Buddha)
【Lower Section】
This represents the three truths of emptiness, provisional existence, and the middle way. In the Lotus Sutra, it appears as Twenty Thousand Lamp Light Buddha.

■Fukū-Jōju-Butsu (Amoghasiddhi Buddha)
【Lower Section】
The Bodhicitta-śāstra states: "The Northern Amoghasiddhi Buddha is called Karma-jñāna (Action Wisdom) because he accomplishes the wisdom of all-accomplishing action."
