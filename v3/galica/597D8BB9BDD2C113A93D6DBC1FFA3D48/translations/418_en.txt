Though they were given their swords (daishō), they were not people who would flee, but going outside the gate was unnecessary. [Mizuno] laughed, saying "Might this situation not be rather stifling?" He said they should rest peacefully with their pillows high, and assigned five or six page boys to attend them. For himself, he placed armor in his sleeping quarters, stationed five or six samurai nearby, and most importantly assigned night guards. Horses were kept ready day and night, and other precautions were strictly maintained.
