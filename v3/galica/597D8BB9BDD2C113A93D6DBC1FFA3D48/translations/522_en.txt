Shinō Genmu Zen Jōmon　　Makino Shunsai
　　As noted above, there are grave sites beside the stone pagoda of Lord Kōzuke-no-suke within the grounds of Banshō-in temple.

　The funeral rites having already been completed with the head reattached, Lord Kōzuke-no-suke's ancestor from four generations past, Kira Wakasa-no-kami, was killed by Oda Kazusa-no-suke Nobunaga on the 14th day of the 12th month during the Tenshō era. His head was recovered and reattached near the Ōi River, and he was buried at a temple in Mikawa Province. He was given the posthumous Buddhist name Enzan Jōkō. Since the date was the same, and because retainers who had served his domain for generations made pilgrimages to the temple in Mikawa, Lord Kōzuke-no-suke was also given the posthumous name Enzan Jōkō.
