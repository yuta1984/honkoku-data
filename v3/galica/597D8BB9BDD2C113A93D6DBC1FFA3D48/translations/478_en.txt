Despite being without material possessions, he did not misunderstand the true intention of the warrior's way, abandoned gold, silver, treasures, and family, and died for righteousness.

1. Muramatsu Kihē took Buddhist vows and called himself Kurahara Ryūen, becoming an excellent physician in peacetime and living with this as his occupation. Though he was already over sixty years old, when he heard news of the siege of Akō, he was the first to rush there and attack. A few days before, he had also sent farewell poems to someone, being a person of refined culture.

1. Dōshi Sandayū was a person who constantly cultivated the way of the warrior. When Ryūen heard news of planning to hold up in Akagi, Sandayū also set out together with him. Ryūen restrained him, saying "You should remain and show filial piety to your mother.
