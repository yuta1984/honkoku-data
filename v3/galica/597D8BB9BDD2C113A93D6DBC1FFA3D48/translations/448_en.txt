○One: When seppuku was determined for that day, Chikara said, "If there is no impediment, I would like to send a final letter to my father Kuranosuke." As there was no impediment, he sent word saying that for both of them to meet death on this day was beyond their fondest wish, and that he looked forward to seeing his father's face in the underworld. When Kuranosuke read this, he found his child's handwriting dear, and thinking of various things—how though they had not spoken face to face for several days, the writing style was vigorous—he shed tears for a while. To fulfill the duty to their deceased lord was their deepest desire. Today, to regard this fleeting life as the height of joy and to commit seppuku with purity was proper. There should be no concern whatsoever about worldly matters, was his reply. Chikara also read this letter three times, it is said.

【The correspondence in this account is likely fabricated】
