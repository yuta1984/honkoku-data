【Heading, boxed section】Kinsenka (Golden Coin Flower) 《Marginal note: 《Also》Kinsanka (Golden Cup Flower) | also called》
【Outside the box】The flower shape is like a chrysanthemum. There are single, double, and thousand-petaled varieties. The flowers bloom in mid-period without fully opening, blooming upright. The leaves resemble kuyō-sō (nine-star grass). Flowers are present in four seasons, with colors of red, yellow, orange, leaf-green, yellow, and blue in light shades. Length is about one shaku. Blooms from around August-September, with spring March being the peak season. Calling this kinsanka or kinsenka are also Chinese names. According to the Shū-gafu sōmoku no fu (Collected Pictorial Records of Plants), kinsenka is a colloquial name, and kinsanka is called so because it resembles small cups. Kinsenka refers to goshi-ka (noon flower). Kinsenka is called goshi because it blooms at the hour of the horse (noon). The red ones are called shigo-ka (child-noon flower), and the yellow ones are called kinsenka. Also, the original name of kinsanka is kagyoku-ka 《also》 kinsan-sō 《also》 chōshun-giku (long spring chrysanthemum).
【Dividing line】
Chōshun-giku: The common name is kai-kai-giku (Hui-Hui chrysanthemum). Has flowers in four seasons. Even when planted between hedges and fences, it can naturally be admired.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Kan-giku (Winter Chrysanthemum) 【Outside the box】Flowers are yellow. The flower center is yellow with tubular stamens rising up. Petals are white. Single-flowered and sparse. Leaves grow thick and turn red from October, becoming crimson-like until December. Flower color is like gold. Also, suisen-giku (narcissus chrysanthemum) flowers are single with white, bright petals that are blue. Leaf color is gray on the back, blue-green on the front. Present in September-October. Called winter chrysanthemum.

【Left Page】
【Heading, boxed section】Mandara-ke (Mandala Flower)
【Outside the box】Grows from seed in spring, with a form like eggplant seedlings, gradually producing branches, with leaves also like eggplant. Hence the alternative name yama-nasubi (mountain eggplant). Flowers bloom in autumn. Color is white, large-flowered. Flower shape is robust and unusual like morning glory, so commonly called tōjin-bue (Chinese flute). Most fitting for its shape. Planted in flower beds and called Chōsen-asagao (Korean morning glory). Opens in morning and closes in evening. ○ Shi-chin says: Mandara-ke grows seedlings at homes in spring and grows in summer. A single stem rises straight up, height four to five shaku. Leaves and stems are like eggplant. White flowers open in August, lasting until September. Leaves are large like morning glory flowers. Opens in morning and closes in evening. Both wood and stems are black-red. Fruit is round with thorns.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Ō-yatsushiro-sō (Great Yatsushiro Grass) 《Marginal note: August-September | blooms》【Outside the box】Flowers resemble yatsushiro-sō, like bellflower, with similar colors. Flowers bloom at crossroads. Each leaf produces flowers. Resembles sasa-rindō (bamboo gentian). Later than yatsushiro-sō. Found in mountains.

【Heading, boxed section】Aki-botan (Autumn Peony) 《Marginal note: from August |》
Kibune-giku (Kibune Chrysanthemum) 《Marginal note: September》
Kaga-giku (Kaga Chrysanthemum) 《Marginal note: until | flowers present》
【Outside the box】Leaf shape resembles chrysanthemum leaves with shallow cuts and hardness. Flowers are also like chrysanthemums with purple-red color. Double and single blooming, also with white flowers. Both flowers and leaves are the same. Also called Kaga chrysanthemum. Original chrysanthemums have veins in their petals, but this chrysanthemum has no veins in its petals. Like peonies, flowers open with stamens surrounding the fruit. Like single poppies with poppy heads.
