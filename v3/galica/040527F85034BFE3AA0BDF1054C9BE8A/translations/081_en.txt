【Heading, boxed section】Tall Chrysanthemum (Jōkiku)【Outside box】Sunflower (Himawari) grows seven to eight shaku tall, with flowers seven to eight sun in diameter. The color is yellow, resembling spring chrysanthemums. The shape appears like layered rocks but is soft. This flower faces east in the morning, turns south at midday, and faces west toward the setting sun, following the sun. Therefore, it is also called "sun wheel." The silk tree closes its leaves in the evening and opens them in the morning. Among others

Many flowers open in the morning and fold their petals in the evening. Only the tall chrysanthemum turns following the sun. This is like Tōba's poem: "Li Ling and Wei Lu died at Mount Yin, unlike the sunflower that knows the sun." It blooms in the seventh and eighth months.

【Heading, boxed section】
Wallflower (Araseitō) 《March》
Purple orchid flower (Shiranka) 《flowers bloom》
【Outside box】The leaf shape is like willow leaves but broader, with powder on the leaf surface. The stem is thick with tall nodes. The flower has four petals, reddish-purple in color with luster, white at the base. They bloom in clusters on the leaves. Pods form afterward, shaped like cowpeas, and are used as a substitute for early cowpeas.

Seven-ring grass
Nine-ring grass (Kurinsō)
Banner joint flower (Seisetsuka)
《flowers bloom in March》
【Outside box】The flower shape resembles primrose with thick petals. The stem stands upright and flowers bloom around it. The stem is thick, standing tall, blooming in tiers. There are several colors: white, red, or snow-white genji, and also pale-colored varieties. The type with red base and white petal edges is called Genji nine-ring grass.

【Heading, boxed section】Rashōmon 《flowers, March》【Outside box】The flowers are lapis lazuli blue, like aconite flowers. The leaves resemble those of dancing grass but are slightly longer and thinner. The stem is vine-like; when laid down, it sends out roots from the nodes and spreads.
