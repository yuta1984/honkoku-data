【Right Page】
【Heading, boxed section】Miyagino-hagi (Miyagino Bush Clover)【Outside box】Flowers bloom in July and August. Flower colors include white, purple, pale colors, and variegated patterns.
The flowers hang down long, blooming like princess wisteria. There are also yellow flowers, but these are the reverse (underside) of white ones. Mountain bush clover also has white, purple, and pale colored flowers. Miyagino grows to about one jō in length, while mountain bush clover grows to about six shaku.

【Heading, boxed section】Morning Glory (Asagao)
【Outside box】Flowers bloom from April-May through July-August. The flower shape resembles bindweed, with triangular leaves.
There are many flowers, thirty to forty blooms. Colors include lapis lazuli blue, white, pale red, and pale yellow, with variegated and絞り patterns. Korean morning glory has lapis lazuli colored flowers; when seeds are sown and grown,
flowers bloom when two leaves appear. Also called dwarf morning glory or water morning glory, it is a mountain plant. Its original name is Kengyū (Ipomoea nil).
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Bindweed (Hirugao)【Outside box】Flowers bloom in April-May. The color is pale red, and there are also white ones with snow-white large blooms.
Morning glories bloom every morning. Bindweed flowers bloom all day long. Nanking bindweed has cherry-colored small blooms. The leaves are like buckwheat, and the flowers resemble morning glories but are somewhat smaller. It spreads vines in fields and mountains.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Evening Glory (Yūgao)【Outside box】Flowers bloom in June-July. The flower shape is like morning glory, with large, round leaves. The flower color is white. The original name is hisago (bottle gourd). Also called hyō-yūgao (gourd evening glory). This is also the same with white flowers. Both varieties bloom in the evening. Also called sen-nari (thousand fruits). Hisago is small and a field crop.

【Left Page】
【Heading, boxed section】Daruma Chrysanthemum (Daruma-giku)
Hotei Chrysanthemum
Valley Chrysanthemum
Satsuma Chrysanthemum
【Outside box】The leaves are thick with fine hairs. The plant stem is thick, and the flower shape is like single chrysanthemums with pale purple flowers. They bloom at the base of leaves. There are also white flowers. When touched, they are soft like velvet, with leaves present in all four seasons. Also called daruma grass, hotei chrysanthemum, or satsuma chrysanthemum. Blooms from August to September, a plant of mountain valleys.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Gentian (Rindō)《Interlinear note: From July to August》【Outside box】The flower shape stands upright like balloon flower. The leaves are like bamboo grass.
Flower colors include white, pale crimson, purple, and lapis lazuli - four varieties. Found in mountains and fields.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Bakkatu (China Root)
【Outside box】A type of briar. The leaves are round like small persimmon leaves,
with five veins in the center of the leaf. Leaves fall in winter and emerge in spring. Has red berries in autumn. Commonly called sankiraï. Also called sarutori-ibara, but this is incorrect. Sarutori-ibara has leaves shaped like Japanese pagoda tree leaves, with flowers colored like turmeric, growing and spreading about one to two shaku. Also called kame-ibara. Found in riverbanks and mountain valleys. Also found in fields together with sasa-rindō. Flowers bloom in May, with red berries in September-October.
