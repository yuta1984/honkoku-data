On the fifteenth day, [Napoleon's forces] crossed the Sambre River from Charleroi, so the Prussian side received word of this, and Blücher and Wellington organized their armies. On the twelfth day, the French forces deployed 120,000 infantry and 22,000 cavalry and launched an assault against the Prussian side. The Prussians fought with all their military strength in a bloody battle, but the French side had much advantage. The Duke of Brunswick also died in battle here, and General Blücher fell from his horse and was already in danger, but was barely saved and withdrew.
