**Kōji (malted rice/fermentation starter)** — Its flavor and nature are sweet and warm; it is non-toxic. It dissolves digestive stagnation (*shaku*), breaks down food, and neutralizes the toxins of fish and fowl.

**Washing water from rice (*hibantoginoshiromizu*, rice-rinsing water)** — Its flavor and nature are sweet and cool; it is non-toxic. It cools heat, relieves irritation and thirst, promotes urination, and clears stagnant bad blood (*oketsu*). It can also serve as a useful aid when used in the cooking of fish and fowl.

**Glutinous rice (*mochigome*)** — There are varieties differing in red/white and large/small. Its flavor and nature are sweet and warm; it is non-toxic. It strengthens the spleen and stomach, warms deficiency-cold (*kyokan*) conditions, stops diarrhea, checks frequent urination, and suppresses spontaneous sweating (*jikan*). It helps to bring out rashes in cases where smallpox eruptions are slow to appear, and draws out and raises *yin*-type carbuncles and swellings (*yōshu*) to promote healing.

Because its nature is warm and sticky, if consumed in excess it will cause digestive stagnation (*shaku*), congeal phlegm (*tan*), stir up wind (*fū*), and generate heat. If eaten habitually over a long period, the body will become soft, the sinews will loosen, and one will lack strength. It should also be prohibited for those suffering from phlegm, fire, wind diseases (*fūbyō*), or excess heat (*jitsunetsu*), as well as for young children with toxic heat (*dokunetsu*) from smallpox.

**Rice cakes (*mochi*)** — According to the ancient customs of our country, rice cakes are made in the shape of a large round mirror (*kagami mochi*) and offered to the gods (*jingi*). They are also offered to suits of armor kept in storage. Moreover, people of all four classes (*shimin*)—old and young, male and female alike—offer them to one another to celebrate the New Year. After wedding ceremonies (*kongi*), the two families also send them to each other as a gesture celebrating the bond of firm, lasting, and imperishable union.
