Truly it is the foremost among all medicines; yet for that very reason, it is also the source of a hundred harms. Its flavor is sweet and pungent, and it possesses great heat and some toxicity. It circulates the qi, harmonizes the blood, relieves stagnation, expels stagnant blood (血瘀), wards off cold, disperses heat, dissipates wind-dampness, kills evil influences, opens the water passages, smooths the large intestine, resolves the toxins of fish, fowl, vegetables, and fruits, and conducts medicinal efficacy throughout the body. It also effectively dispels sorrow, releases frustration, loosens the tongue, and expands the mind. Its virtues are too numerous to enumerate. However, if one habitually drinks to excess, the body becomes afflicted, the spirit grows dim, the lifespan is shortened, the qi is thrown into disorder, the blood is agitated, phlegm is generated, and internal fire is stirred.
