**Sugar**　Sugar did not exist in ancient times in either Japan or China. In China, it is said to have first been brought as tribute from foreign lands during the Tang dynasty. In Japan, until recent times, only imported goods were treasured. During the Kyōhō era, sugarcane (*Saccharum officinarum*) from the Ryūkyū Kingdom was introduced via Satsuma domain, but the production methods were not yet well understood. Later, around the Hōreki era, knowledge of these methods gradually became more refined, and today domestic sugar has in fact come to surpass imported varieties.

●Overview of sugar products:
Sugarcane (*kib*i) · Cane syrup (*sato-mitsu*) · White refined sugar (*taihaku*) · Medium white sugar (*chūhaku*) · *Shimi* (a type of granulated white sugar) · Brown sugar (*kuro-satō*) · Rock candy (*kōrizatō*) · *Aruheiro* sugar (a Portuguese-derived sugar confection) · *Konpeitō* (star-shaped sugar candy) · *Karameru* (caramelized sugar / caramel) · Candied preserves (*satōzuke*)
