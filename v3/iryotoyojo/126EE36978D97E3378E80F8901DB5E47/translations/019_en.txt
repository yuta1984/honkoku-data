It is advisable for elderly persons to drink a small amount of warm sake, then lie down to rest after the qi has settled. For women who have recently given birth or whose blood and qi have become depleted, it is effective to finely shred dried bonito (*katsuobushi*) into miso soup, add rice cakes made in the twelfth month (*shiwasu no mochi*), simmer them until soft, and eat the mixture.

Wheat, millet, sorghum (*morokoshi*), sesame, adzuki beans, black soybeans, young mugwort (*yomogi*), and cudweed (*hahakogusa*)—among these and many other ingredients, there are numerous things that can suitably be combined with rice cakes. Each of these also possesses its own distinct efficacy and potential toxicity.

**Glutinous steamed rice (*kowa-ii*)** — Excessive consumption obstructs qi and is difficult to digest. It is not suitable for sick persons or young children.

**Dried cooked rice (*hoshi-ii*)** — The variety from Dōmyōji in Settsu Province and that from Sendai in the far north have long been celebrated as distinguished products. It was originally meant to be stored for long periods and used as military provisions. Aged (dried) rice of this kind is harmless for both sick persons and children.

**Ice mochi (*kōri mochi*)** — This is produced in cold regions,

and among these, the product of Shinshū (Shinano Province) is considered the finest. Its nature is good, and sick persons in particular should use the more thoroughly dried variety.

**Rinsing water from glutinous rice (*mochigome no shiromizu*)** — Its flavor and nature are sweet and cold; it is non-toxic. It stops thirsty irritation (*hankatsu*) and acute gastrointestinal disturbance (*kakuran*). When one has eaten wild duck (*kamo*) or domestic duck (*ahiru*) and cannot digest it, drinking one cupful (*issen*) will immediately dissolve the blockage.

**Barley (*ōmugi*)** — There are many varieties. Generally speaking, early-ripening varieties are considered inferior; middle and late-ripening varieties are said to be better. Its flavor and nature are sweet, balanced, and cool; it is non-toxic. It loosens tightness in the chest, directs qi downward, cools heat in the blood, dissolves digestive stagnation (*shaku*), stimulates appetite, stops excessive thirst (*shōkatsu*), and eliminates heat.
