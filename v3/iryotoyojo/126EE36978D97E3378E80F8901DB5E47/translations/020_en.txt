On the other hand, a popular saying holds that eating it in excess makes one thin and prevents one from gaining weight for life. Upon reflection, given differences in a person's innate constitution, whether their body type tends toward deficiency or excess (*kyojitsu*), or their particular illness, neither view is without reason. The matter of being fat or thin cannot be discussed in blanket terms. ● **Mugi-meshi** (barley rice): This is especially beneficial to people. ● **Wari-mugi** (cracked/split barley) rice: This is the easiest of all to digest. However, those with *kyodatsu* (physical debilitation and collapse of vital energy) should adjust the amount they consume accordingly. ● **Ōmugi-kiri** (sliced/cut barley, i.e., barley noodles): Its nature is cool, surpassing that of *udon*, whose nature tends toward dryness and heat. ● **Iri-mugiko** (roasted barley flour; also called *mizu no ko* or *mugi-kogashi*): Its nature is good. ● **Mugi-no-moyashi** (barley sprouts, i.e., malted barley / *bakuga*): Used as a medicine to aid digestion and in the making of *ame* (malt syrup/candy). Its flavor and nature are sweet, salty (*kan*), and warm, with no toxicity. It has many efficacies. In particular, it is highly effective at dissolving and dispersing glutinous rice cakes (*mochi*).

**Small wheat / Wheat** (*komugi*): There are also many varieties, with early-ripening and late-ripening types. The late-ripening kind is said to be superior. ● Its flavor and nature are sweet (when fresh, it is *hot* in nature; when aged, it is *neutral/balanced*), with slight toxicity. ● It removes vexation-heat (*ikiri*, a feeling of heat and irritation in the chest), stops thirst, suppresses excessive sweating, promotes urination, stops hemorrhage (*roketsu*) and spitting of blood (*daketsu*), and nourishes the qi of the heart and liver. ● This substance cannot be prepared as a cooked rice dish. It is used only by grinding it into flour, and then making *komugi-mochi* (wheat cakes), *udon* (thick wheat noodles), *sōmen* (thin wheat noodles), *namafu* (raw wheat gluten), and similar products. Furthermore, many of the Chinese and foreign (*kaban*) cold confections (*higuashi / kanka*) are made from wheat flour. Moon cakes (*Yuèbǐng*, read *Epin*), longevity peach buns (*shōtō*), steamed buns (*Zhēngbǐng*, read *Jiutarō-pan*), "Hiridzu," and "Bōru" are among these. ● **Shōfu** (starch paste): This is originally a food. It is also used by mounters and paper craftsmen (*kyōshiya*) to prepare fermented/decomposed paste (*kusari-nori*). It has many functional properties. ● **Yaki-fu** (baked/grilled wheat gluten): Causes no harm to people. ● **Udon**: Its nature is warm with slight toxicity. It warms the interior of the body and stops diarrhea caused by cold. Excessive consumption, however, obstructs the qi, causes phlegm blockage, generates heat, and stirs up digestive stagnation (*shaku*).
