However, there is also a popular belief that eating it in excess will make a person thin and prevent them from gaining weight for their entire life. Upon reflection, given a person's innate constitution, whether they tend toward deficiency or excess (*kyojitsu*), or depending on their particular condition, neither theory is without some merit—the matter of weight gain and loss cannot be generalized.

**Barley rice (*bakuhan*)** is especially beneficial for people. **Cracked barley rice** (*wari-mugi meshi*) is the most easily digestible. However, those who are in a state of exhaustion (*kyodatsu*) should moderate their consumption.

**Cut barley** (*kitta ōmugi*): its nature is cooling (*ryō*), which is superior to that of udon, whose nature carries dry heat (*sōnetsu*).

**Roasted barley flour** (called *mizu-no-ko*; this is *mugi-kogashi*, roasted barley powder): its nature is good.

**Barley sprouts/malt** (*mugi no moyashi*, i.e., *bakuga*, malted barley): used as a digestive medicine and for making maltose syrup (*ame*). Its flavor and nature are sweet, salty, and warm; non-toxic. It has many beneficial functions and is especially effective at breaking down and digesting rice cakes (*mochi*).

**Wheat (*komugi*)** — There are many varieties, with early and late-ripening types. The late-ripening variety is said to be superior. Its flavor and nature are sweet (fresh wheat is heating, aged wheat is neutral), with slight toxicity. It removes vexation and irritation in the chest, stops thirst, checks perspiration, promotes urination, stops bleeding and blood-spitting, and nourishes the qi of the heart and liver.

This food cannot be prepared as boiled rice. It is only used by milling it into flour and making wheat cakes (*komugi mochi*), udon noodles, sōmen noodles, wheat gluten (*namafu*), and the like. Also, many of the cold confections (*higashi* / *kanguwa*) of China and foreign lands are made from it. Moon cakes (*yuèbǐng*, read *ebin*), longevity peaches (*shōtao*), steamed buns (*zhēngbǐng*, read *jiū-tarō-pan*), "Hiruidzu," and "Bōru" are all of this kind.

**Wheat starch paste (*shōfu*)** — This is fundamentally a foodstuff. It is also prepared by mounters and bookbinders (*kyōshya*) as a fermented paste glue. It has many beneficial properties.

**Baked wheat gluten (*yakifu*)** — Not harmful to people.

**Udon noodles** — Their nature is warm with slight toxicity. They warm the interior of the body and stop cold-induced diarrhea (*hie-kudari*). Excessive consumption will close off qi, cause phlegm blockage, generate heat, and stir up digestive stagnation (*shaku*). Fresh grated daikon juice, malted barley (*bakuga*), and hawthorn berries (*sanzashi*) effectively counteract these effects...
