**Wild Radish** (*ko-ōhone* / *Hadano daikon*)

On examination, the small daikon of the Kamigata (Kyoto-Osaka) region and the Hadano daikon of Sōshū (Sagami Province) are all of the same variety. They grow in wild fields, with seeds falling and sprouting naturally. They can also be cultivated with good results. Both are tender and delicious. ●Flavor—acrid and sweet; non-toxic. Some sources say they have a slight toxicity and should not be consumed by the sick.

There are two varieties—large and small—and the larger should be used medicinally.
