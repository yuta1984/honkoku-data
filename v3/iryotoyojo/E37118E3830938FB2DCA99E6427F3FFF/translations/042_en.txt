Vegetable Section

○Lettuce (Chisha)　　　Has mild toxicity. Hardens muscles and bones. Good when young leaves are small. Eating together with honey causes diarrhea.

○Amaranth (Hiyu)　　　Supplements qi and removes heat. Facilitates bowel movements. Should not eat much. Should not eat together with soft-shell turtle.

○Chili Pepper (Tōgarashi)　　　Breaks cold accumulation and treats hernia. Sick people should avoid.

○Bracken (Warabi)　　　Removes heat from the bladder and promotes urination. Bad for sick people.

○Royal Fern (Zenmai)　　　Facilitates urination and removes swelling. 《Note: Eating bracken and royal fern together with bird shellfish should be greatly avoided》

○Goosefoot (Akaza)　　　Has slight toxicity. Not good for people with spleen and stomach deficiency-cold.

○Bracken Starch Cake (Warabimochi)　　　Should be used by people without illness. Cool in nature and causes diarrhea easily. Eating together with mung beans harms people.

○Taro (Imo)　　　Though said to regulate the middle, should not eat much.

○Taro Stems (Imo no Kuki)　　　Cook softly and use a little even for sick people. Dried ones made into soup are even better. Chinese taro stems are considered the best.

○Taro Head (Imogashira)　　　Same as taro bulbs.

○Chinese Yam (Yamanoimo)　　　Because its nature is cool, using too much causes diarrhea. Sweet potato (tsukuneimo) is the same.

○Bulbil (Mukago)　　　Beneficial for weak people. Generally the same as Chinese yam.

○Bamboo Shoots (Takenoko)　　　Opens the chest and removes phlegm. Mōsō bamboo has a gentle nature and is good for eating. Contraindicated for qi stagnation. Sugar is also contraindicated.

○Eggplant (Nasubi)　　　Has mild toxicity. Removes cold and heat and soothes blood pain.
