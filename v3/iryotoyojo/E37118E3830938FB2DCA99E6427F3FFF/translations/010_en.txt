Minimize sadness, minimize thoughts, minimize lying down, and minimize sleep. In daily life, keep your mind quiet without being noisy, relaxed without being hurried, and soft without being rigid. Do not raise your voice or laugh loudly. Always keep your heart joyful and do not become angry. Regret things that cannot be undone, and if there are mistakes, blame yourself once but do not regret twice.

If joy is excessive, the qi opens too much and diminishes vital energy. If worry and sadness are abundant, qi becomes knotted and blocked. All of these harm the original qi.

Body fluids are the moisture of the entire body, and their source is one with kidney water. Always treasure them and do not spit them out, but swallow them. They transform into essence and blood. Immortals call this "golden sweet dew and jade liquor" and consider it precious. Phlegm should be spat out as it does not become nourishing moisture.

It is good to regularly tap the teeth. They will not fall out early and this also removes heat from the mouth. Every night before sleep, gargle with salt tea.

In health cultivation, there are "five benefits": it is good to comb the hair frequently, it is good to keep hands on the face often, it is good to tap the teeth frequently, it is good to regularly swallow saliva, and it is good to keep qi always calm.

When lying down at night, lie on your right side with both thumbs bent and grasped by the four fingers, with the right hand bent while lying down. Gripping the hands prevents the chest from being blocked and attacked. Later this becomes habit, and the hands will not open even during sleep.

The head should follow "head north, face west, lying on right side," meaning sleeping with head to the north and face to the west. However, many people avoid sleeping with head to the north and face to the west. This shows ignorance of health cultivation. In immortal scriptures it says "East-poverty, South-illness, West-fortune, North-longevity," stating that sleeping with head to the north brings long life. Confucius slept with his head to the east because east is the beginning of yang, and the head is also where yang gathers, being the beginning of form. Though it says "East-poverty," sages do not desire wealth or poverty but take principle as their guide. The choice should be according to each practitioner's heart.
