Principles for Maintaining One's Body
There are seasonal principles for maintaining one's body. However, this does not necessarily mean one must do exactly this every morning and evening—I am merely explaining the general principles.

○In the three months of spring, yang energy rises and generates all things, so one should understand that human hearts naturally become spirited and gain vitality. Rise especially early in the morning and diligently attend to work, and when there is free time, go out into a spacious garden and see the grass and trees sprouting and becoming full of yang energy, thus delighting the heart and keeping the mind relaxed. However, this does not mean being lackadaisical as commonly said. Especially in the first month, it is essential to rise early in the morning, work diligently, and maintain the mindset described in the winter section.

○In the three months of summer, because yang energy is vigorous, one should maintain one's heart so that it naturally becomes vigorous, rise early in the morning, maintain one's energy throughout the day, and diligently attend to work with good vigor. On days when there is leisure, one should stay in cool places and engage in idle talk to comfort one's spirit. Common people take afternoon naps from around the fifth month, but this is greatly harmful to health. Farmers dislike going out to till the fields under the scorching sun during midday, so they call it "midday rest" and do indoor work such as making rope or straw sandals. However, it is a mistake to understand this "midday rest" as meaning taking a nap. Also, seeking coolness by going out to riverbanks every night is greatly harmful to health. I know someone who, finding his house cramped and the buzzing of mosquitoes troublesome for reading, borrows the riverside platforms of the Kamo River every night from after sunset until midnight...
