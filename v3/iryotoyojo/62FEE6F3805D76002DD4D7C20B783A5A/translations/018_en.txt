　Chapter Three: On Not Employing Fashionable Doctors and So-called Famous Doctors
An ancient person said, "A truly skilled doctor cannot compete with a fashionable doctor." This means that even a learned doctor cannot match one who has the momentum of being fashionable. Therefore, various books record the harm caused by fashionable doctors and so-called famous doctors. First, a "fashionable doctor" refers to one who, though lacking in learning, is employed due to temporary good fortune. A "famous doctor" refers to one who, though originally lacking the qualifications of a physician, is employed by borrowing the reputation of their parent or teacher. Nowadays, many of these types
