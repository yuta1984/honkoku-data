For mochi, anything wrapped inside is called "an" (bean paste). Since it cannot be seen from the outside and heaven and earth have not yet been separated, it is "an" (darkness). "An" means "darkness," and because it is dark, it is probably called "an."

▲Imasaka Mochi
There was a wise man called Imagawa So-and-so who constantly taught his son, saying "In any case, those who are human beings must observe the five constants of benevolence, righteousness, propriety, wisdom, and trustworthiness, and above all, sake is a bad thing," as taught in the Imagawa-jō

【Left Page】
As he spoke according to the teachings, many other disciples also carefully observed this and never drank sake. When a certain disciple offered mochi filled with five-colored bean paste, the master said "This is a splendid mochi." The disciple then answered, "The five colors represent benevolence, righteousness, propriety, wisdom, and trustworthiness, as well as blue, yellow, red, white, and black, as you constantly teach, master, so I made it accordingly." The master was greatly pleased. At that time, everyone found this five-colored mochi remarkable and
