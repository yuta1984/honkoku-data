▲Furthermore, from this point he devised and turned out vessels for eating rice and soup. Originally, people had made do with earthenware. There is also something called "goki" (five vessels). This refers to "teppachi" (iron bowls) made of iron in sets of five during the Buddha's lifetime in India. These were used by lighting fire directly underneath, serving as pots and such, and were called "goki" because they came in sets of five. The master's innovation was a set of four that served as both vessels and lids. He wanted to name this as well, so he asked the usual teacher.

When he inquired, the teacher said, "Since you have devised various vessels this time to leave for posterity and use, it would be good to attach your name directly to these utensils and leave them for future generations." Using Wake no Muneharu's initial character:
▲"Wan" - This should still be in use today.
▲"Choko" - This has become customary to call "inoshishi no kuchi" (boar's mouth). Indeed, since it is a small pot-like object, it should be called "choko."
