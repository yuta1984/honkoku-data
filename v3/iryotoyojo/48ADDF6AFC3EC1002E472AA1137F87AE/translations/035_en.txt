This person combined wheat flour with sugar, baked it, and let it cool appropriately before serving it. Therefore, it became an amusement for noble people.
For this person too, Sen no Rikyū gave permission to use the character "sen" (thousand),
and thus the confection came to be called "Sen no Kōbei." Eventually, the character "kō" was omitted and
it came to be called "senbei."
These days, returning to the old ways, "Kōbei" and kuchitori have also
