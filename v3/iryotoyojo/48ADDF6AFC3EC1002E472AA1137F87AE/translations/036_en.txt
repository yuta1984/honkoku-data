【Right Page】
Seeing that he ate only sugar, [the Dutch resident] processed that sugar to make food and
offered it to him, whereupon [Uhei] said it was extremely delicious. When asked how best to make it,
[the resident] first removed the impurities from the sugar, kneaded it
in a copper pot until thick, stretched it out, and let it cool before giving it to him. From this point, gradually
red and green stripes were added to make it into confections that were also given to other people. It was simply called "uhei-tō," but eventually
the "u" was changed to be pronounced "aru,"
thus becoming "aruheii-tō."

【Left Page】
Adding two-colored stripes may also be connected to the name's origin. As [Uhei] became an elder and his back
became bent, this was called "ōkoshimage" (great back-bending), and twisting it to resemble the tied yokozuna's
shimenawa rope, among other techniques—
these remain in the name of this uhei [candy].
