Then divide the roots and harvest them for eating. Also, in April after the seeds form, if you cut away the stems, new leaves will grow from the remaining roots, which can also be eaten. Also, if you harvest and eat them during winter, new leaves will grow from the stumps and become plump by spring. It is good to harvest in both winter and spring. If you harvest while leaving the root stock, they will last a long time and can be continuously harvested. Generally, both large and small onions are suitable for soft, sandy soil. Liquid fertilizer should be poured frequently. Human urine is especially good. However, if there is too much moisture, cutworms will appear, and snails will also emerge and may destroy everything. One should be careful.

Small onions (scallions) - Sow seeds in August and harvest in May. The planting method is the same as for carrots. The Allium mentioned in herbalism texts is not the same as scallions.

Garlic - The Qimin Yaoshu states that soft, good soil is best. Plow thoroughly three times and plant in early September. The method for planting seeds is to make ridges and plant by hand. It is good to plant one bulb every 5 sun (about 15 cm). ○ The Complete Treatise on Agricultural Administration states that small garlic should be planted in the 6th and 7th months, and large garlic should be planted in the 8th month. It also states that when planting garlic, make ridges, apply liquid fertilizer, and plant on top of the ridges. It also states that when planting, a small amount of wheat bran should first be placed under each clove. The land should be soft.
