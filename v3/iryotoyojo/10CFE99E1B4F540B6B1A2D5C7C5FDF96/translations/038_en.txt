Those that age and remain vigorous for a long time exist. These are considered good. Should be eaten until May. Other varieties age in April and bolt early.

Planting method: Till sandy soil well, spread manure and till again, soak seeds in water, mix with sandy soil, manured soil or ash and sow. After growing, transplant. Afterwards, frequently pour liquid manure and urine. Should sow early in July. Also, if sown gradually over time, they will be available constantly. If sown in shade, they grow quickly. In cold weather they do not grow. Seeds ripen in June. After bolting, bend them over and leave them - this produces good seeds. During long droughts, the flower stalks do not elongate and wither. Also, if too many leaves are harvested, the flower stalks will not develop. If taking seeds, do not harvest leaves after March. Till the soil well. If rain does not fall for a long time, water should be applied. The stems can be peeled and made into vinegared salad, or added to pickled plums. There is something called Nanking lettuce. It has large leaves and yellow flowers. The taste is not good. Dutch lettuce blooms with blue flowers in April and May. The leaves have no luster and are not beautiful. They open in the morning and wilt in the evening. In winter they should be wrapped with straw. The leaves grow thickly and white, and can be eaten raw.
