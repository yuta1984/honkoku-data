Also, when dried, both the leaves and roots are superior to daikon radish. In the first and second months, flower stalks emerge with the finest taste. To collect seeds, do not break the stems. Remove the branches. Kyoto mizuna has excellent flavor. Next, Ōmi greens have large roots and good taste. Tennōji greens and Edo greens are also good. Edo greens have long roots resembling daikon radish. There are also Chinese cabbages with good flavor. There is also one late variety that does not thrive until the first and second months, but grows long and flourishes luxuriantly after turnips and bok choy are exhausted in the third and fourth months. Much manure should be applied. The leaf color is deep blue-green. The taste is slightly inferior, but it remains long after other bok choy varieties are finished, which is good.

○ Pickled greens from Ōmi and pickled sour greens (suina) from Kamo are famous specialties. Their taste is excellent.
