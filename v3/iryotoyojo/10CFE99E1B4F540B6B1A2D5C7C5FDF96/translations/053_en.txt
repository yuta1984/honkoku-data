According to the Agricultural Complete Book (N≈çsei Zensho), to grow large gourds, in early February, dig a hole three feet wide and three feet deep, mix fertilizer and soil in equal parts and put it in the hole, tamp it down with your feet, water it, and plant ten seeds. Cover with fertilizer again, and when they grow to over two feet tall, gather the ten stems together in one place, wrap them with cloth, and cover with mud. Within a few days they will fuse together to become one stem. Keep the strongest one among them and cut away all the rest. Keep two fruits and remove the excess. Dig small ditches around the hole 4-5 inches deep and fill with water. Do not put water in the hole itself. If done this way, what would hold one to (about 18 liters) will grow to hold about one koku (about 180 liters). Upon reflection, this theory has not yet been tested and seems doubtful.

Tsuke-uri (pickling melon): Plant the same way as sweet melons. Use the same way as asa-uri.

Asa-uri (morning melon): A type of pickling melon. Planted the same way. Larger than pickling melons. Better taste than pickling melons. Will not bear much fruit if the soil is poor. Common in Kyoto, rare in other provinces. Eaten raw as namasu (vinegared salad). Good for soups, dressed dishes, salt pickling, dried melons, and sake lees pickling. Also made into sushi.
