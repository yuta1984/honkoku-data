The above is recorded in the "Tatsusho-roku" (Record of Safe Birth).

○Eating the meat of beasts is not preferable. In China, people of all social ranks commonly eat it as part of their national custom, so there may be no harm, but perhaps because of this, there are also many who develop carbuncles and suffer from abscesses. As for our Japan, I do not know about ancient times, but looking at the present era, those who are vigorous in blood and qi and eat meat accumulate impure blood and often develop swellings.

【Left Page】
Some eat soft-shell turtle and swan, developing scalp sores, or experience blood rushing to the head causing hair loss and scabies. Even if young men eat wild boar and deer and feel no harm, not a few suffer from carbuncles as they age. However, for elderly people whose legs and hips are cold, using a little is also not to be prevented. Indeed, regarding four-legged animals, Shinto prescribes days of ritual impurity, and it is said that if one receives chopsticks from Suwa Myojin and eats, there is no impurity.
