【Right Page】
The mistakes of drunken madness are not few. Because of this, one neglects official duties, errs in matters, inflames sexual desires, confuses the mind, forgets home and harms the body. The proverb "At first, man drinks wine; in the middle, wine drinks wine; at the end, wine drinks man" refers to such people. Therefore, the ancient sages established wine etiquette, as appears in the "Book of Rites." It is natural that both Japan and China strictly prohibit alcohol when facing important matters. For those who think of prudence, there should be no such conduct as alcohol-induced disorder or drunken madness.
