○ Stomach Reflux (Also called "kaku")

There are two types of this condition. The condition treated with my Yakuo-gan involves spleen-stomach deficiency. Even though food settles in the spleen once, after half an hour to one hour, or even after one or two days, everything is vomited up completely, causing emaciation and weakness - this is called stomach reflux. It is a condition that can be confused with "kaku." Though it is an extremely difficult condition, all cases can be completely cured. "Kaku" refers to food stagnating in the chest, not descending, and being vomited immediately. This condition cannot be cured, so do not use [the medicine] for it. Kaku occurs only in adults and is rare in children. Stomach reflux occurs in both adults and children.
