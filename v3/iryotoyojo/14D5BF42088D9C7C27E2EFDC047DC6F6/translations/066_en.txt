Kikunojō moved closer and asked with great concern, "Why do you appear so lost in melancholy thought?" But that man only hung his head further, offering no response save tears to any words. Rokō's heart was also unsettled, so he said with a resentful air, "Then perhaps my attentions have somehow displeased you? Why do you hide something from me when we have become so intimate?"

Then that man wiped away his tears and said, "To waste such deep sincerity from you by remaining silent would also be painful. Your single phrase about whether my heart fails to please you, layering upon all this kindness like Musashi stirrups, echoes in my chest, so I shall reveal the truth. You must not be startled by any means. I am not actually human. I am what is called a kappa, one who dives through waves and has made his home at the bottom of the water."

Hearing this, Rokō was astounded, but wondering what circumstances could be involved, he calmed his heart and listened. Yet he felt an unexpected chill run through him and a sense of being seen through completely. Gradually he steadied his chest, chanted prayers in his heart, and continued listening to the situation.
