In the end, many people came saying "forget it," but as society gradually became more turbulent, eventually there were no more pleasure-seekers. From the high-ranking courtesans to the lattice girls, from the tea house girls to the riverside prostitutes, even the many regular customers who had once been so numerous disappeared completely—like the wind of Shinato blowing away the eightfold clouds of heaven, like clearing thick undergrowth at the base of trees with a sharp burning sickle. Since no one came to visit anymore, the brothel proprietors suffered headaches and called together their young employees, saying "Well now, what should we do?" Four of them wrinkled their brows and shook their heads, engaging in various discussions that came to nothing. Though they spoke of various rumors, their eyes saw no customers. Tea houses and inns with debts had no one to press for payment, saying "Please pay, please settle your accounts." The paper flowers (tips) that the brothel managers had received were now being used for divination with the I Ching hexagrams. As for others, high and low alike, the only ones whose finances were doing well were rats and men who loved sleeping in—no one else. Thinking that society would fall into complete chaos at this rate, the eight million gods assembled at the banks of the Heavenly Yasukawa River and held various discussions, but no particularly sound ideas emerged. When someone suggested having stonemasons submit bids to cut open the Heavenly Rock Cave, others said "No, no!
