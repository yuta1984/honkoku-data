All those who make wild, base, and inferior courage their business through fighting spirit—Mencius calls this the petty courage of common men, and Zhuangzi compares it to the fighting of roosters and the biting of dogs. If this is so, then the striking with bamboo swords by swordsmen of today's world is merely equivalent to children's play. Even if well learned, it is merely individual competition between one person and another, and is not a skill that a gentleman should practice. The true method of swordsmanship is to unite this Way with righteousness, making uprightness the technique, being internally enlightened and externally gentle and compliant. Sweeping away the evil thoughts of competition and humiliation, the false techniques of provocation and deception, letting the mind dwell in a realm of tranquil emptiness,
