Since it was decided that he would go to the capital, he was greatly delighted and petitioned the chief retainer to arrive at Hara station one day earlier than his lord. He went to Shoin-ji temple and met with Hakuin. Since it would be only a brief conversation, he earnestly requested to receive the essence of Zen teachings. Hakuin said, "Your aspiration is truly admirable, so I shall indeed speak directly and transmit the essence of Zen to you. When I was in my prime years, I was traveling on foot around Kyushu, and while I was staying for a while in a certain castle town, a peasant uprising occurred. This happened because there was a clever person in that domain who conducted a land survey throughout the territory and discovered an additional yield of over 100,000 koku. The farmers were enraged by this and united together to
