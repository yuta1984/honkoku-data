The perception of fragrances through the nose follows the same principle as sound.

In the depths of the ear, there is what is called the eardrum, which is like the stretched skin of a drum. When sound travels through this medium and strikes this eardrum, it is like striking a drum with a drumstick to produce sound. Following the degrees of long-short and high-low tones of the stringed and wind instruments outside, the violent vibrations produced here are perceived by consciousness, which discriminates and recognizes them. The Abhidharmakośa calls this the "ear-faculty" because in that narrow depth of the ear, the ability to hear accurately without error is due to the workings of the極微 (atoms) that collectively form the human body - it is a miraculous instrument of sympathetic response that discriminates and recognizes all sounds: the resonance of metal, stone, strings, and bamboo instruments, human speech, and the cries of birds and beasts. This applies not only to the ear.
