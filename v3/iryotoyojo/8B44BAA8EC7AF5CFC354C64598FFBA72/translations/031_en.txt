Perceiving fragrances through smell follows the same principle entirely.

At the bottom of the ear, there is what is called the eardrum, like a drumhead stretched over a drum. When sound travels through this atmospheric medium and strikes this eardrum, it is just like striking a drum with drumsticks to produce sound. Following the rhythmic measures of long-short and high-low tones from stringed and wind instruments outside, this creates vibrations here that consciousness perceives and understands. What the Abhidharmakośa calls "ear-atoms" refers to how, in that narrow ear cavity, each sound can be heard without error through the workings of the many atoms that collectively form the human body - a mysteriously responsive instrument that can distinguish the resonances of metal, stone, string and bamboo instruments, human speech, and the cries and roars of birds and beasts.

This applies not only to the ear. The supreme subtlety and minuteness of creative transformation is truly astonishing, but merely discussing empty theories without clarifying them through actual principles will never lead to thorough understanding.
