**[F/K (辛)]**
**Solid**

It is only upon reaching the solid that form is first truly determined. Among solids, the cube is taken as the standard (正). Therefore, the fact that musical pitch (ritsu) emerges from this cube—with its top, bottom, four sides, and six equal faces—was discovered independently, without relying on Tomonobu's book on mathematical principles, which is likewise a remarkable thing.

**A diagram showing that the triangle is the beginning of all mathematical operations.**

Four lines · Four lines (long and short) · Five lines · Six lines (long and short) · Eight lines
[The ways in which multiple lines form shapes are various, as shown here.]

Any figure of four lines or more can be divided into triangles; but when a triangle is divided, only lines remain, and no shape is formed. Therefore it cannot be further divided. Thus, the triangle is a naturally occurring shape that inherently contains the principles (ri) of all shapes. It is for this reason that three gives rise to all things. Astronomy, calendar science, solar and lunar eclipses, and all forms of arithmetic make use of this principle of three—this is an immovable, fixed principle of heaven and earth, and it is the very reason why the study of pitch and tuning (ritsuryō) takes heaven and earth as its model.
