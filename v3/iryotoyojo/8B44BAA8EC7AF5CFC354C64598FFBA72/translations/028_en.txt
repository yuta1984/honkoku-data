{The Abhidharmakośa calls this "correspondence" (taihō), meaning balance - the movement of heaven and earth, the good and evil fortune and misfortune of human bodies, today's undertakings, and all manner of functions are all due to this correspondence (balance). Regarding matters of this ki's gravity and pressure force, these can be verified using pneumatic tubes and air pumps, as recorded in the Yōjō Yōryaku.}

Because this atmosphere fills the sky completely with not even a hair's width of gap, when one takes a fan and moves it, wind is immediately generated. Therefore, when still it is called ki, and when moving it is named wind. When a cannon's iron ball flies and produces thunder-like sound, this is because the air that was displaced gathers together again

and strikes violently together to produce sound. In wind cannons, when this ki is gathered and compressed, one can see that shooting projectiles becomes like using gunpowder - thus it is naturally clear that this ki has expansive force. These phenomena are matters that should be included among evidential studies, so I have only recorded their outline - the details are difficult to explain exhaustively. {Moral duty, technical arts, and all undertakings are entirely things within this atmosphere, so there is nothing easier or more direct than recognizing this fundamental source and cultivating it accordingly. The monk Nichiren said: "There are shadows in darkness, fish have paths in water, birds have tracks in the void. Among all things in the four continents, not one is lacking. People do not see this, but the Buddhas of the three worlds illuminate and observe it. Nichiren also infers this." Taking this as the fundamental meaning of his doctrine, he explained the textual meaning of the Lotus Sutra that all under heaven and within the four seas return to the Wonderful Law. That profound teaching is also the working of this ki.}
