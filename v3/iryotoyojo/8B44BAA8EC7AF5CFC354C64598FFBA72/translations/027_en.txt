Now, qi as a substance is called alongside water, fire, and earth. Buddhist scholars call these the four great elements, or also the four realms, while astronomers call them the four fundamental forces. "Great" means the same as Mencius's "vast qi" - referring to something vast and without boundaries. "Realms" refers to things that are distinct and separate. "Fundamental forces" refers to [the basic principles] of all things.
