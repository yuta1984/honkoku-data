When everyone found this strange and asked about it, [the master] said: "Indeed, climbing that slope is not particularly difficult, and there was no need to rush up it. The one who galloped showed poor judgment of the situation and was very dangerous. Looking back with visible pride shows a lack of humility. 【Left page】

In the "Appended Statements" of the I Ching, it says: "Harnessing oxen and riding horses, drawing heavy loads to distant places, thereby benefiting all under Heaven - this is taken from the hexagram Sui (Following)." Sui is read as "shitagau" (to follow). The reason horses are mentioned in relation to this hexagram of Following is that horses are originally wild animals, and when humans use them, we should ride them in accordance with their nature without forcing them unnaturally. However, since they are beasts that originally galloped freely across wide plains according to their own will and were raised in that manner, they are difficult to use without proper control. In the hexagram Sui, the upper trigram Dui is a yin trigram, and the lower trigram Zhen is a yang trigram, representing the image of yang leading and yin harmonizing - the principle of "we move and they rejoice."
