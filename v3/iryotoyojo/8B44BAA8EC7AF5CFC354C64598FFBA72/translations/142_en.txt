On the principle that cultivating qi is the art of benevolence and the foundation of employing people

Governing a country and military strategy both fundamentally rely on benevolence as their foundation. Whether commanding armies of three legions or forces of a million, the deployment of light and heavy troops comes from just one person's

benevolent heart, and when this vast qi achieves freedom in its contraction and extension, appearance and disappearance, advance and retreat, this is called qi-ji (the mechanism of qi). "Ji" means the pivotal mechanism - whether clarifying bright virtue to govern the realm, moving heaven, earth, ghosts and spirits, or achieving complete control over the military forces led by a great general as if using one's own hands and feet - all depend on this pivotal mechanism of qi. The other three mechanisms of military strategy - geographical advantage (chi-ji), situational advantage (ji-ji), and force advantage (ryoku-ji) - all arise from this qi mechanism. {{Interlinear note: The details of the four mechanisms appear in the Wu Zi.}}
A great general who possesses the three virtues of wisdom, benevolence, and courage must command great armies with this upright, great, and enlightened qi mechanism, breaking whatever they face and crushing whatever they strike. Their momentum is like raging fire and splitting bamboo
