[The concubine] was moved to emotion and secretly tried to become a nun, but when Sadako overheard this, she was greatly alarmed and firmly prevented it. Wife and concubine lived harmoniously together, serving their husband and displaying the virtue of chastity. Regarding the matter of Emperor Ichijō, though it would be presumptuous to discuss the benevolent heart with which he, from his supreme position, showed compassion even for the poorest people, the fact that a single woman like Sadako could move her [husband's] concubine with such a pure and principled heart was because she had attained what was right and proper.

Yōki-setsu, Volume Two
