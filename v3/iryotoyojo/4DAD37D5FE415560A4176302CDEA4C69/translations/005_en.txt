Preface
Forty years ago, when I was still a child, I followed my teacher
and made reading books my daily practice. By nature I was slow-witted, and what I
read in the morning I would forget by evening. It was exactly like carving in ice or
painting on water. My old teacher grew weary of instructing me, and finally
transmitted this method of memory orally to me. From this point I gradually progressed, and
was able to memorize a phrase or half a chapter.
