1. The concept of Five Elements (Wu Xing) is not unusual, but the "five" refers to Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water. "Xing" (行) carries the meaning of the character "jin" (陣, formation), read as "tsurauru" (to penetrate). It is called Five Elements because the five elements form a formation.
