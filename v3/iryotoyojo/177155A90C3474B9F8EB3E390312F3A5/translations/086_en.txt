An illusionist came and made a wager out of receiving gunshots on his naked body, taking much money. Since Suō and Nagato are places where there are many master gunners, young people were deceived by this illusionist and lost much of their wager money, with no one able to defeat the illusionist. The illusionist was already about to leave and return from that place. The people who had shot at him felt frustrated and regretful, spoke to their instructor, and requested the master's ultimate technique. Though the instructor declined, since many people begged and pleaded, he had no choice but to face the illusionist, and when he fired with a snap, he hit without fail and

defeated him, and the illusionist died in vain. One should observe the difference in skill between this master and his disciples. The disciples aimed solely at the naked body, while the master shot at the clothes that had been removed. This is the same as a monkey trainer placing a mirror in a boat. The naked body was an illusory form; the real body was inside the clothes. The master had thought through and understood this point. In our medical practice, there is the theory of root and manifestation (hyōhon) in the Inner Canon. In treating disease, first seek its root. If the root is urgent, first treat the root, then necessarily treat the manifestation afterward. If the manifestation is urgent, first treat the manifestation, then treat the root afterward.
