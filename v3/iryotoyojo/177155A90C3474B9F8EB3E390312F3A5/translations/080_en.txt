He violently destroyed the household shrine, cursed the smallpox deity with foul language, and wildly slashed around with a blade, crying and wailing. The next morning, early, when a man from the house across the street opened his door to look outside, he saw a terrifying old woman, covered in blood, emerge from that smallpox-stricken house and rush into four or five neighboring houses. The children in the houses she entered contracted smallpox from that very day. If this is so, does the smallpox deity truly exist? And if it does exist, how strange that it was invisible to the eyes of those in that household, yet visible to the eyes of those in the house across the street," he asked.

Fungokusai answered, saying: "Indeed, the smallpox deity exists. It is of the same type as what people call epidemic deities. Originally, smallpox was a disease that did not exist in ancient times, and in China it began from the Tang dynasty. In Japan, it was transmitted from foreign countries around the time of Emperors Tenji and Tenmu.
