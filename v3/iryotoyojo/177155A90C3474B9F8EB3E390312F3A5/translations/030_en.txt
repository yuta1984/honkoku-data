Today's physicians favor literary studies and do not even pick up Dosan's kirigami "Complete Nine Collections," claiming that reading the voluminous works of various Chinese authors is essential. However, when attending to patients, they are not clear. What Shuhe said - "clear in mind but not clear under the fingers" - refers to this very thing. Particularly, many physicians in recent years do not know that there are old and new school traditions. The old school is said to have been transmitted from Ōanamuji-no-mikoto through the Wake and Tanba lineages, continuing for hundreds of reigns and generations, and in recent times the Wake and Tanba became one family called the Nakarai house. The new school refers to the Dosan house. This Dosan was from the time of Lord Nobunaga. At that time, there were three people named Dosan throughout the realm. One was Dosan from five generations before Nakarai Tsūsen'in Roan. One was Manase Ikkeiō Dosan. Another was Saitō Yamashiro-no-kami Dosan. This one was a warrior. Ikkeiō Dosan was born in Rakuyō Yanagihara. At age ten, he entered Zōshūken in the temple kitchen of Shōkokuji and became a kasshiki, took the name Tōhaku, memorized poetry collections of Tōba Sankoku and others, and at twenty-two went to the Kantō region, lodged at the Ashikaga school, served Bunpaku as his teacher and broadly studied various books. At that time in Kamakura there was one called Koga no Sanki Tōdō, who had entered the Great Ming, transmitted the medical methods of Zhang Yuehu, studied abroad for twelve years, returned to Japan, lived in Kamakura and vigorously practiced medicine.
