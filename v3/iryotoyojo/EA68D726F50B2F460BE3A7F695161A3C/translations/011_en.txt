【Right Page】
Those who are distant from matters of sex - in any case, those who are distant from sexual matters in their youth will surely preserve their longevity well, and even in old age, there are very many people who still love and enjoy sexual matters. Those who do not restrain themselves in sexual matters when young often suddenly develop illness in their prime years and die young. The teachings of the sages are truly clear. What one learns in early childhood, regardless of what skill it may be, is never forgotten throughout life. I myself learned a little singing as a small child, but even now I still remember both the words and the melodies. My wife also learned the shamisen as a child, and even in old age she still has not forgotten it. What one learns after reaching adulthood is all forgotten if one neglects it for a while. What is learned while one's vital energy is still soft and not yet settled seems to soak into the body and cannot be extracted.

【Left Page】
Everything should be learned in early childhood. Moreover, if one has something as one's main focus in mind, one will not be distracted by other things. For young people, restraining oneself in sexual matters is unsurpassed for learning. An acquaintance of mine, a person called Suetaka of Kamo, learned waka poetry in his youth and eventually gained fame in the world. He was distant from sexual matters in his youth, and thus preserved his longevity well. Even in old age he still loved sexual matters, and at his seventieth birthday celebration, he used a clay figure with a vigorous male organ as a decorative object on the tokonoma, and hung a scroll with his own kyōka (comic poem): "Confucius said one stands at thirty, but at seventy, standing is rare since ancient times."
