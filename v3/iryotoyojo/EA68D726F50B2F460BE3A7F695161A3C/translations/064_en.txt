My predecessor entered the school of Kagawa obstetrics, and I also learned the technique from my predecessor. Kagawa Gen'etsu of Ichikan-cho in Kyoto, in his youth practiced acupuncture and abdominal massage as his profession. He had a faithful character and a deep aspiration to save the world. When there were cases of breech birth, transverse presentation, stillbirth, or difficult labor caused by fetal movement triggered by shock, he would visit personally to provide rescue, and eventually created surgical techniques for obstetric revival. Later, he visited my former teacher Minagawa Kien, related his discoveries, and announced his intention to spread this knowledge to the world. Since he was poor at writing, he requested Kien to write on his behalf, and compiled something called "Shigenshi Sanron" (Shigenshi's Theory of Obstetrics) and published it to the world.

[Left page]
After making it public, his name spread throughout the country and he became the founder of obstetrics, with many of his disciples practicing throughout the world. Gen'etsu's son Gengo had an outstanding and unrestrained character, different from his father, and eventually did not inherit his father's house but established himself independently. At first he enjoyed fishing and netting, but when he reached mature years, he inherited the family profession, and his skills were no less than his father's, becoming established in the world, thus forming the two Kagawa houses. This is the ancestor of the present Aburakoji line. Shigenshi had a daughter, and he took a son-in-law for her and made him continue the profession as an adopted son, called Genetsu. My former teacher Kien always praised Shigenshi's character when speaking to me.
