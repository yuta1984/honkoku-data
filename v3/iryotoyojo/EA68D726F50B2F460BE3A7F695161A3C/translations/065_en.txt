Shigenshi created obstetric delivery surgical techniques and rescued patients from the suffering of difficult and critical births. His achievements should be called great. In medicine, when tonic therapy is practiced excessively, it becomes a harmful practice of tonic therapy; when aggressive therapy is practiced excessively, it becomes a harmful practice of aggressive therapy. This is true not only of medicine but also of Buddhism. When nembutsu (Buddhist chanting) is practiced excessively, it becomes a harmful practice of nembutsu. When Zen is practiced excessively, it becomes a harmful practice of Zen. When Master Nichiren declared "nembutsu leads to hell, Zen to demons, Shingon to national ruin, Ritsu to treason," since nembutsu, Zen, Shingon, and Ritsu are all teachings of the Buddha, he was not denying those methods themselves but criticizing the harmful effects of those methods.

In the present world, obstetric techniques are widely practiced. When women become pregnant, they entrust themselves to obstetricians, taking only medicines, neglecting proper daily activities and movements, drowning in the indulgent love of parents, not being shown what they should strive for. When they reach full term and labor, they cannot endure labor pains and eventually suffer difficult births, leading to untimely death. This is not the fault of obstetrics, but the harmful effect of society relying only on obstetrics. If during pregnancy one is unfortunately afflicted with breech presentation, transverse presentation, stillbirth, or eclampsia, or fetal movement caused by shock, leading to difficult labor with no other recourse, then one has no choice but to use obstetric surgery. This is not something to be used in normal circumstances. I pray that in all things one applies oneself to health preservation, properly maintaining moderation to preserve one's lifespan.
