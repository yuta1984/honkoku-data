The legs should be extended; bending them is not good. If one suddenly gets up and moves about while the belly has not yet returned to normal after giving birth, both chest and abdomen will be pulled upward, causing blood dizziness (cerebral congestion). Not only does blood rise up, but the entire chest and abdomen move and impact the heart. Understanding this principle, one should conduct postpartum recovery. Do not rush to pick up the child. After childbirth, one should generally be careful in all things. Even if the birth was easy, one must never be careless. Do not rush to bathe. Generally, one should bathe after twenty days. For thirty days after birth, one should lie in bed and keep rising and sitting quiet. During the ten months of pregnancy, the belly had been expanding outward day by day,
