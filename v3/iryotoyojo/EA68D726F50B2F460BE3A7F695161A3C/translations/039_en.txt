It is not that I begrudge my formulas, but I do not transmit them out of respect for human life. If it is medicine to be taken for a long time, one should request a prescription from a good doctor and take it.

Recently, both doctors and Confucian scholars have greatly declined, and moral standards deteriorate daily. Among Confucian scholars, it's not that there are no men of letters, but many engage in elegant learning, and those who pursue learning in moral principles that is useful to the world are rare. Among them are some with such lack of virtue that they are inferior even to illiterate people, beyond description in words. Doctors too have become negligent in applying their hearts to the healing arts, focusing instead on profit and monetary gain, flattering and pandering to the wishes of patient families, seeking only the number of patients and medicines, not knowing how to discern whether patients will live or die, be cured or not, and communicate this to the families. while laypeople appear to respect doctors on the surface, since they do not truly trust doctors in their hearts, they naturally come to despise and doubt doctors gradually, and their behind-the-scenes criticism of doctors is like that of geishas and dancing girls. Recently, they compare doctors to theater actors or sumo wrestlers when discussing their reputations. Though one might expect them to be indignant at having doctors compared to people of low status, they do not become indignant but rather greatly rejoice. They should rejoice. This is not the doing of laypeople, but as I have secretly heard, it is the doing of quack doctors who wish to sell false reputations for profit. If you look at the printed publications of these reputation-makers, their authors are clearly known.

Even among our colleagues who are quack doctors, they receive official permission for family names, palanquins, and white ceremonial robes
