【Right Page】
When he was seventeen years old, he examined a patient with beriberi and said "This is an incurable condition," declining treatment and returning home. Afterward, two or three elderly doctors also exhausted their efforts, but ultimately could not save the patient, who died. Sankaku's reputation from that incident became famous throughout the world - this was a story told by an elderly person I knew well. I heard that in those days, patients' families also paid attention and judged doctors' skills. Recently, such stories are not heard, and when illness is still mild initially, families do not inform doctors about the patient's natural constitution, usual behavior, or condition. Doctors also do not thoroughly apply the four diagnostic methods, ultimately making errors in treatment that worsen the illness, causing families to panic and change doctors due to the disease.

【Left Page】
Since families think that simply changing doctors will bring immediate cure, when five or six days pass with the same condition, they change doctors again. When the situation is similar or slightly different, they change doctors again. When changing two or three doctors shows no quick effects, patients suffer thinking they might die, losing appetite, which causes families to panic. Relatives and acquaintances gather with various conflicting opinions - consulting divination, checking auspicious directions for doctors, taking wonder drugs, performing prayers - becoming confused in various ways until finally reaching complete chaos.
