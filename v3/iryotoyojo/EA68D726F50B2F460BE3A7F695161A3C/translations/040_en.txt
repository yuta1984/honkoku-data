That people in the world do not trust doctors is perfectly reasonable. Therefore, those who do not trust doctors say things like "How can doctors, who do not crawl inside the patient's belly to look, know what illness it is just by looking from the outside?" Though there are such people, this is not entirely the case. Even we quack doctors can see if we apply our minds to it. For example, whether the menuki ornament wrapped around a sword hilt is pure gold, fired gold, or plated gold - though it may seem impossible to tell from the outside, it can be known. However, those who have no interest in swords and do not handle them at all cannot tell even when they look.

Those who handle them can naturally see the difference. Moreover, since doctors handle patients just as sword dealers handle swords, the inside of the belly can also be seen from the outside.

Also, when laypeople speak of doctors' misdiagnosis, this too is misguided. In the layperson's mind, some colleagues think that once they know "this is what disease, this is what medicine" - once the disease name is identified, the medicine is naturally determined - but this is not so. For a single disease, there may be hundreds of medicinal formulas, countless in number. Disease names like typhoid fever, cold damage, heat stroke, rheumatism, and syphilis are named according to their causes. Disease cause refers to the origin from which the illness comes. All other disease names are designated according to the observed symptoms and manifestations.
