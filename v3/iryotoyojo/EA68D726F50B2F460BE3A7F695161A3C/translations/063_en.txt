As evidence for this, they say that when you split a persimmon and look at its seed, the head where the sprout emerges faces down, and no sprout emerges from the calyx end above, so the persimmon seed is also positioned upside down. This is a biased theory. Initially, the persimmon has its calyx facing down and the sprouting end of the seed facing up. As the fruit gradually ripens, the calyx moves to the top, so the sprouting end of the seed faces down. Rice ears and such also initially have their tips pointing up. As the grain gradually ripens, the ear tips become heavy and face downward. In the pregnant womb, initially the child has its head up and feet down, which is proper and follows the principle of heaven and earth.
