When one changes doctors one after another, sometimes the medicine happens to hit the mark and provides a cure. This can hardly be called a good doctor. If there are effects after thoroughly investigating the principles behind those effects, then one can truly call it a good doctor. Without investigating those principles, even if there are effects, it is merely a chance hit. Even if one errs in treatment, if one reflects on oneself and understands that mistaken treatment, even having made mistakes, one should be called a good doctor. When one makes treatment errors without knowing they are treatment errors, how much less can laypeople understand this. The skill or clumsiness of doctors is difficult to discern. The first thing to guard against is laypeople's obsession with medicine. This fundamentally arises from stinginess and foolishness - thinking that if they take a doctor's medicine the medicine fees will be expensive, so they make their own preparations. and of course for themselves, they give medicine to servants and maids. Then they look at medical books with phonetic annotations and gradually compound medicines. Even doctors do not understand how medicine can harm people, so how could laypeople understand? Among people I know who had this harmful practice, one used self-compounded calomel preparations for his own syphilis, which later became an intractable condition. He summoned various famous doctors, but finally his nose collapsed and his eyes became ulcerated, and he ultimately died an unnatural death. Even so, he had no idea whatsoever that he died an unnatural death. I have seen many other people who became obsessed with medicine, developed serious incurable diseases, and died unnatural deaths. Even in this discourse on health preservation, though I have medicinal formulas from my experience that I would like to transmit to the world, I fear this harmful practice and do not reveal even one formula.
