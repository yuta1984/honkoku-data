During this time, the father places the child in front of him, and when the mother returns to the loom, the father smokes tobacco for a while and plays with the child. When he goes out to farm work again, the nursemaid has also finished eating and is washing everyone's rice bowls. Here the mother again lifts the child onto the loom and gives breast milk, and when the nursemaid finishes tidying up the area, she ties the child to her back again and goes outside. By raising the child in this manner, the child grows very healthily without any illness. When this child reaches five or six years of age, they desire various things, but being originally poor, they cannot give sugary foods, and only manage to obtain peaches, persimmons and such to

give to the child. Therefore, the child does not get tooth decay or suffer from childhood digestive disorders. Even when contracting major illnesses like smallpox or dysentery, they can generally overcome them. Their clothing barely protects against the cold, but being accustomed to thin clothing, they rarely catch colds. The middle class and above are completely opposite to this. However, even for the middle class and above, I do not say they should necessarily do likewise. While they should raise children according to their social station, seeing how healthy poor children are, I am explaining why wealthy people's children would also become quite healthy through such child-rearing methods.
