Li Dongyuan treated one person who, due to excessive alcohol consumption, developed small sores below the brain and above the nape of the neck, called "star-white sores." After several days, great pain arose, he said. According to Zhu Danxi's theory, one woman who was fond of alcohol developed an abscess in her brain. In the Classified Cases of Famous Physicians, a Confucian scholar who drank alcohol well had coughing with phlegm, stiff neck, and dry skin. This was a lung carbuncle. Also, one person drank distilled liquor one evening and attempted sexual intercourse but could not complete it, and his scrotum swelled and became painful. This was a scrotal carbuncle, it states. Also, a person named Zhang Biya became drunk, engaged in sexual intercourse, and developed a lumbar abscess. A famous physician named Xue Ji was unable to treat this condition, it states. Thus, if rich foods or alcohol and sex are both excessive, they will necessarily cause sores and swellings. The Treatise on Disease Origins states that "if one engages in sexual intercourse while drunk, it will cause malignant sores." The Three Causes Formulary also speaks of carbuncles and abscesses being caused by alcohol. In today's world, many who suffer from these conditions all experience things as described in the aforementioned theories. When one constantly restrains oneself from heavy drinking, one should not develop these diseases. The Inner Canon states that "the formation of pus and blood that generates carbuncles and abscesses does not descend from heaven nor emerge from earth, but arises from the accumulation of minute things."
