Discourse on Alcohol and Health Preservation, Volume One
　　　　　　Busō　　Edited and revised by Sōshū Moribu Masayoshi
　　General Theory of Alcohol Discourse, Part One
○Alcohol appeared in ancient times, and many sacred scriptures and biographies of sages discuss it,
so it is said. The Strategies of the Warring States records that long ago, the emperor's daughter had Yidi create alcohol,
and it was delicious, which was then presented to Yu. The Book of Han states that alcohol is heaven's
beautiful bounty, the means by which emperors nourish all under heaven, used in sacrificial rites to pray for blessings, to support the declining,
and to nourish the elderly; gatherings of a hundred blessings cannot be conducted without alcohol. In sacrificial rites,
it is used to serve ancestors, and in banquets it is used for honored guests
