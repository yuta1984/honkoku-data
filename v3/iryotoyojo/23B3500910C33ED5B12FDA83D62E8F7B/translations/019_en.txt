Generally speaking, among those who discuss health maintenance, none surpass medical practitioners, and what medical practitioners hold as their foundation has nothing above the Inner Classic. The Yellow Emperor's Inner Classic, Basic Questions, and Spiritual Pivot refer to dialogues between the Yellow Emperor Xuanyuan-shi—a sage emperor among what are known as the three ancient sovereigns—and his retainer Qibo and other ministers, broadly covering everything from heaven, earth, and yin-yang to the ways of human bodily care, health maintenance, disease, and treatment.

They discussed these matters thoroughly, leaving nothing incomplete. In the first chapter of that Inner Classic, "Discourse on Natural Truth in High Antiquity," the Yellow Emperor asked the Heavenly Master, saying: "I have heard that people of high antiquity all lived beyond one hundred years of age without their movements declining. People of today all decline in their movements by age fifty. Is this because times have changed, or have people lost this [way of health maintenance]?" Qibo answered: "Among the people of high antiquity, those who knew the way followed yin and yang, harmonized with mathematical principles, had moderation in food and drink, regularity in daily activities, and did not labor recklessly. Therefore, they could maintain both body and spirit together, completely fulfilled their natural lifespan, and departed after living beyond one hundred years. People of today are not like this.
