　Treatise on Alcohol-Related Diseases, Part 1
　　Discussion of Apoplexy
○Apoplexy (chūfū) refers to sudden collapse (sottō), meaning falling down suddenly; violent muteness (bōin), meaning inability to speak; stupor (mōmai), meaning clouding of mental faculties; facial paralysis (kaheki), meaning distortion of eyes and mouth; left hemiplegia (nan), meaning inability to use the left half of the body; right hemiplegia (kan), meaning inability to use the right half of the body; loss of consciousness, meaning inability to recognize things; speech impediment (kenshū), meaning stammering and halting speech; excessive phlegm and saliva (yōsei), meaning abundant blockage of phlegm in the throat.
