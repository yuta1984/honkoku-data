(Continuation of Zhu Danxi's theory) had a robust constitution and enjoyed drinking hot alcohol, but suddenly fell ill with blindness. This occurred because hot alcohol injured the stomach qi, and dirty blood stagnated within, he states. All of these show that eye diseases are caused by alcohol. Generally, when eye diseases are caused by alcohol, their symptoms are various and not uniform, so it is impossible to discuss them all. Longmu Zen Master said, "A person having both eyes is like heaven having the sun and moon as twin luminaries - they are truly the supreme treasure of one's body." Therefore, not only for those whose eye diseases are caused by alcohol, but even for other symptoms, when one develops even a slight illness, one should abstain from alcohol and treat it well. There is a popular saying: "Since alcohol passes below the eyes, using it for eye diseases causes no harm," which may sound plausible.

　　Discussion on Mouth and Nose

○The Inner Classic states: "The yellow color of the center enters and connects to the spleen, opening its orifice at the mouth." It also says: "The mouth and nose are the gateways of qi." The Treatise on Disease Sources states: "Spleen qi connects to the mouth. When the mouth is harmonized, it can well distinguish the five flavors. The five flavors enter the mouth and are stored in the spleen. The spleen therefore transforms and transports body fluids to nourish the five organs."
