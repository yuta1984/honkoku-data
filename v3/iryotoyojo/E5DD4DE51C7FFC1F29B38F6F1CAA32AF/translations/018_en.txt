　　　Explanation of Fetal Position During Pregnancy

Generally, the location where pregnancy occurs in a woman's abdomen is in the space between the bladder (behind) and the large intestine (in front), where there is a sac called the uterus that holds the fetus. It has no opening at the top, but below it connects to the genital opening with a mouth. Normally it is contracted and narrow, storing menstrual blood inside, but when there is a fetus, it naturally expands according to the fetus's growth. Within it is a sac called the membrane, and the fetus hides its body inside, positioned upside down. This membrane is within the uterus.
