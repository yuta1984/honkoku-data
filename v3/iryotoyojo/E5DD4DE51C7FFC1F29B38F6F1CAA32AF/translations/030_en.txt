I have long grieved that women in remote villages and narrow alleys suffer from difficult births, and there are often those who lose their lives. Therefore, I have written this treatise, wishing to save those who die untimely deaths. This too is the way of benevolence. If one truly knows that such people exist and give birth to such people, it is truly not by chance.

Kōjutsu late winter, one day before the full moon (1790)
　　　　　Disciple　　Masuda Hakugen, respectfully inscribed

　　　　　　　　　Written by Konata Akira
