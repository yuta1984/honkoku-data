When the stubborn one who sank in the Miluo River thinks of white miso from saying "my body is pure and white," and when the withdrawn and naive person who knows nothing of the world says "I will not bow to the petty people of my hometown," this must be the beginning of five-to miso. I once wrote "Nenashigusa" (Rootless Grass). Though it was light-salted, thin miso, it suited contemporary tastes, and with the reputation that the neighbor's rice-bran miso would be fragrant, it became known in all directions, people came seeking it from far and near, and over three thousand copies
