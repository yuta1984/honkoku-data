Long Song

What is tobacco exactly? There are no definite characters to describe it, and though it is written about in various ways in the world, when we inquire into its essence, it is known to be everyone's personal conception. When we hear its origins from books, this grass did not exist in this country in ancient times, but in recent times of long-lasting joy, it was brought to Japan from the southern barbarian countries.

The first to learn to smoke was Sakura-baba, and seeds were planted here and there, cultivated and popularized. People's various preferences spread widely, and there was no place from deep mountains to seashores where it was not grown, nor was there anyone who did not smoke it. Truly marvelous is this grass - many people make their living from it. Those with little capital simply engage in

cutting tobacco, gathering tobacco from a thousand ri, though they become as poor as children, they continue cutting it. Those famous for excellent color and fragrance are Hattori, Nitta, Tanba perhaps, Komatsu, Hagiwara, Takasaki, Kokubu, Yoshino - thus they are celebrated.

In any case, the world celebrates this grass, and when guests come, the tobacco tray comes before tea. They reach for the "love and respect grass," filling the pauses in conversation with pipes. They puff thoughtfully and blow smoke that rises like clouds - how noble it is. The "longing grass" whose destination is unknown, the "what-shall-we-do grass," the "deliberation grass" for consultations, when stuck for words they put the pipe through their mouths - behold the "golden thread smoke." In all matters it is the "go-between grass," but if consumed carelessly it becomes "fool's grass." Those who call it a wasteful thing name it "poverty grass." When compared to sake, it is not so much of an expense...
