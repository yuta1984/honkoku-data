When consumed with proper understanding, it nourishes the vital energy and is therefore good, so it is named "longevity grass" and also written as "life-extending grass." Having the virtue of stopping sleep, it is called "soul-returning grass." The medicinal properties recorded in books are numerous indeed.

Try binding it - it is miraculous for stopping sudden bleeding. The stems are for stopping coughs and killing leeches. Birds, livestock, fish, and all kinds of insects dislike it. Only monkeys enjoy eating it. Though plant-eating insects also dislike it, the fact that there are tobacco-loving insects is indeed mysterious.

If this were a world where Buddha exists, the poetic sentiment says it should be called not the "five pungent plants" but the "six pungent plants." For those who serve Buddha, is this perhaps an admonishment that it should be considered a forbidden thing? Before the gods and Buddha, it should be avoided. When one reflects deeply on this...

The spread of this grass is not ordinary. Due to our lord's deep benevolence, in this well-governed era when the people's hearts are also peaceful and prosperous - moon viewing, flower viewing, and boat excursions, even when snow falls it is delightful, and people await the coming spring. That they rejoice in this auspicious era is still more than enough.

In any case, since cut tobacco has become a pleasure of the present world, in this wise era, may it continue forever without distinction between high and low, men and women, even children enjoying it as they please, letting smoke rise.
