Bitter Gourd (Japanese name: nigahisago)
This is a type of bottle gourd (yugao), Japanese name narihisago, with bitter taste and toxicity that makes it inedible. Therefore it is also called bitter bottle gourd. What is commonly called hyotan (bottle gourd) is this plant. When the fruit is dried in the sun and ground to fine powder, using about 4-5 bu to 1 sen will effectively induce vomiting. When melon pedicle (guati) is unavailable, this should be used as substitute. When this plant is made into pills for use, it has the effect of purging water. Therefore, medical texts also record its use for edema, facial swelling, jaundice, and abdominal distension, effectively draining water. It is also said to expel roundworms, so this should be tried. Since bottle gourd and bitter gourd are originally the same plant, when bitter gourds are planted near where many bottle gourds grow in a garden, even just one stem of bitter gourd will cause all the bottle gourds to transform into bitter gourds. I have actually experimented with this myself before. The non-toxic, mild-flavored bottle gourd that is edible
