　　　Treatment of sword wounds
All sword wounds should be quickly washed with clean water. Lye is also good. Lime stirred into water and filtered through silk may also be used. A certain surgeon called this a water medicine for sword wounds and made it his secret transmission, but later, due to Western discoveries, water came to be used. In ancient times, only shōchū (distilled spirits) was used, but since shōchū causes intense pain that is unbearable, lime water was used instead, and eventually

water came to be used. Rather than using shōchū, raw vinegar is better, so it may be used for small wounds. For all sword wounds, whether they can be healed or not is determined by their location and depth, but in any case, it is essential to quickly prevent excessive bleeding. If blood spurts out and will not stop, it is because an artery has been severed, so after washing well, one should search for the blood vessel from which blood is spurting, cut the surrounding flesh, pull out the blood vessel, 《marginal note: forceps are used for pulling out》 and tie it with thread. When there are many blood vessels and they are difficult to tie, one should burn them with a cautery iron to seal the vessel ends.
