　The "Rōdan Ichigon-ki" states that a physician of Katō Kiyomasa once said:
　During the Korean campaign, being unaccustomed to the local climate and water, he thought the
　Shōki-san prescription would be appropriate, so he prepared those medicines and crossed over, but as expected,
　everyone fell ill, so he had them take
　Fukankin-shōki-san, but it had no effect. That physician said there was truly a medicine that should be given
