I wish to select methods and supplement what has been omitted, continuing the tail of Toride-gusa. Though this is truly like a dog following the sable (an inferior continuation), and cannot escape the mockery of great scholars, I merely hope that it may provide small assistance during emergencies at military gates.

The "Rōdan Ichigon-ki" (Record of an Old Man's Words) states that a physician of Katō Kiyomasa once said: During the Korean campaign, since [the soldiers] were not accustomed to the local climate and water, he deemed the Shōki-san formula appropriate and prepared and distributed those medicines. As expected, everyone suffered ailments, so he had them take Fukangen Shōki-san, but it had no effect. That physician said there was truly a medicine that should be given,

[Left page]
and first gave medicine to one person, whereupon there was immediate effect. Thereupon, when he gave it to the others as well, all were subsequently cured. When asked about the formula, it was Kōso-san. When asked the reason, it was because they suffered from qi depression while in camp. From then on, the retainers of other generals were all able to cure their illnesses with Kōso-san. Alas, during the Korean campaign, the officers and soldiers all exposed themselves to wind and rain and endured frost and snow, their courage being like that of tigers and bears. Yet still, upon entering foreign soil, they developed symptoms of qi depression. How much more so in times of lasting peace...
