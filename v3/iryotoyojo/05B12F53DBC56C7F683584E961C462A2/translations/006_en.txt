Preface
Using medicine to achieve miraculous effects after a disease has become dangerous is not true medical art. Teaching people how to avoid illness from the beginning is what constitutes a true good physician, as can be seen in ancient texts. In today's world, driven solely by the desire to greedily seek resources for one's own clothing and food, physicians compete in performing only eye-catching acts with strong medicines and unusual techniques.
