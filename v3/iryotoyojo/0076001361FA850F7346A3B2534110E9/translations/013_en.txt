Bakagai (ark shell)　　馬珂
　Sweet and slightly warm in nature. Non-toxic. Opens the stomach and promotes appetite. Eating too much
　aggravates parasitic accumulation.

Swan　　hakuchō
　Sweet and very warm in nature. Toxic. Eating too much causes various blood disorders, eye diseases,
　head fever, or may trigger malignant sores and leprosy.

Frog　　ban
　Sweet and neutral in nature. Non-toxic. A high-quality food item.

Dove/Pigeon　　hato (general term)
　Sweet and neutral in nature. Non-toxic. Benefits qi, supplements the kidneys, and treats food obstruction

Chinese chives　　nira　
　Pungent and warm in nature. Non-toxic. Disperses stagnant blood at the stomach opening and treats chest obstruction. Eating too much
　damages eyesight. Should especially be avoided after drinking alcohol.

Garlic　　ninniku
　Pungent and warm in nature. Slightly toxic. ▲Caution with honey combinations.

Carrot　　ninjin
　Sweet, pungent, and slightly warm in nature. Non-toxic. Moves qi downward, supplements the middle burner, and affects the chest and intestines
