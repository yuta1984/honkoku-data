Bakagai (arc clam)　　馬珂
　Sweet and slightly warm in nature. Non-toxic. Opens the stomach and promotes appetite. Eating too much
　agitates parasitic accumulations.

Swan　　hakuchō
　Sweet and greatly warm in nature. Toxic. Eating too much causes various blood disorders, eye diseases,
　head fever, or malignant sores and leprosy.

Frog　　ban
　Sweet and neutral in nature. Non-toxic. It is a noble food.

Dove/Pigeon　　hato (general term)
　Sweet and neutral in nature. Non-toxic. Benefits qi, supplements the kidneys, and treats food obstruction in

Chinese chives　　nira　
　Pungent and warm in nature. Non-toxic. Disperses stagnant blood in the stomach opening and treats dysphagia. Eating too much
　damages eyesight. Should especially be avoided after drinking alcohol.

Garlic　　ninniku
　Pungent and warm in nature. Slightly toxic. ▲Caution with honey food combinations.

Carrot　　ninjin
　Sweet, pungent, and slightly warm in nature. Non-toxic. Moves qi downward, supplements the middle, and affects the chest and intestines
