Also, during illness, after illness when not fully recovered, when swellings have not healed, when qi and body are fatigued, after great drunkenness, overeating, and all anger, worry, sadness, shock, and exhaustion—one should abstain.

The "Book of Rites" states: "Men should take wives at thirty, women should marry at twenty." The teachings of sages are all mandated by Heaven and should be respectfully observed. When not observed, there may be the error of shortening life and weakening wisdom. One should carefully consider and cultivate this.

○An ancient saying states: "If one cannot restrain desire for even a short time, great misfortune will arise. When transgressing, it is as minute as autumn down, but when it becomes illness, it is as heavy as Mount Tai." It also says: "When healthy, constantly remember the suffering of sick days, defend against external pathogens of wind, cold, heat, and dampness, moderate internal desires for alcohol, food, and sexual pleasure, and be careful with the body's rest, movement, and stillness—then illness will not arise." An ancient poem says: "In comfort, always think of times of illness and suffering." Also, "If small desires are not restrained, they become great illness. Small desires are easy to restrain, but great illness brings much suffering." This should be kept in mind beforehand.

○The "Qianjin Fang" states: "Do not seek extreme warmth in winter or extreme coolness in summer. Momentary pleasure inevitably becomes future disaster."
