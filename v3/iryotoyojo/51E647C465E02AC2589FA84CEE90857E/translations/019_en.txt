○After long journeys or strenuous work, when hungry and thirsty, one must not suddenly eat large amounts. The abdomen may become distended and cause illness. One should be cautious. After laboring the body, one must not eat while still sweating. Wait until the sweat subsides before eating.

○The "Gujin Yitong" (Ancient and Modern Medical Compendium) states: "Most premature deaths from various diseases are caused by food and drink, and the harm from food and drink exceeds that of sexual desire." Sexual desire can be abstained from, but food and drink cannot be abstained from for even half a day.

○The "Qianjin Fang" (Precious Prescriptions) states: "Mountain people lack fish and meat, therefore they are free from disease and long-lived. Coastal people eat much fish and meat, therefore they suffer from many diseases and are short-lived."

○Rice nourishes vital energy. When eaten in large quantities, it damages vital energy and obstructs qi. Well-cooked rice is good. Hard rice or rice lacking stickiness should be avoided. Re-boiled rice and rice with water drained off are good for people with accumulation disorders and qi stagnation, those with weak spleen and stomach, elderly people, and children. People recovering from illness should also eat these. Rice that is too sticky and paste-like is not good for people with esophageal obstruction and qi stagnation. New grain has strong properties and harms qi, so it is forbidden for various diseases. Late-season and old grains have mild properties and are good for various diseases. Porridge should be eaten warm every morning. It nourishes the intestines and stomach, warms the body, and is good for various diseases.

○Various fish and meats should be eaten in small amounts. They cause stagnation easily.
