○That sweet things are poisonous for children is something that today's doctors often say, and it has become common knowledge among people. I too mistakenly wrote this earlier in my health manual. However, when my grandson was born and around eighteen or nineteen months old, at the time of what is commonly called weaning, I took him into my home to raise. Though I thought it was not good, I had no choice but to

give him rice cakes and dried sweets daily, always adding sugar to his food, and letting him eat as he pleased. However, I refrained from black sugar because I heard it was hardened with lime. In the year of Kōshin when he turned ten years old, there was not the slightest harm from sugar, nor did he ever become ill. For this reason, afterwards I let my grandchildren eat as they pleased, and every one of them grew up healthy without illness. Through this experience, I have recently discovered for the first time that sugar is not poisonous.
