Ezo Sōshi (Notes on Ezo)
　　　　Introductory Rules: Fifteen Articles

Article 1: The domain of Matsumae administers only certain islands within the Ezo territories as its domain. Within each island, allocations are made between places belonging to the lord (directly controlled by the domain) and places belonging to retainers. "Basho" (場所) refers to a domain or territory. Each basho covers only a stretch of coastline, roughly fifty to seventy ri in extent, with territories distributed at various points along the coastline of an island. Only the coastal areas are included; the interior is entirely unoccupied land, consisting solely of deep mountains and vast wilderness with no human habitation. Merchants from the town of Matsumae would petition their respective domain lords (jitō), expressing their wish to look after the Ezo (Ainu) people of the land. Permission was granted based on consideration of the amount of the transport/operation tax (un'yōkin) offered. This arrangement is called "basho ukeoi" (contract management of trading posts).
