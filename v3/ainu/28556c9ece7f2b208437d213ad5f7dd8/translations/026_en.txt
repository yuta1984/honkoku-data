There is a fish whose shape is like a sea ray (ei). It has oil intestines in its belly, which is considered a delicacy by the Ainu people. When they catch a Kinahō, they cut open its belly and take out the oil intestines, then put inao in their place and release it back into the sea. It will not die this way. The proof that it doesn't die is that they have frequently caught Kinahō that had inao in their bellies. Inao are made by carving wood into human-like forms. These are boat spirits of the Ainu, revered like gods. In the western sea there are no unusual fish. Whales are numerous. They also go out to the southern sea in spring and summer, and return to the northern sea from autumn through winter. According to one theory, during winter they hide in the marshes of Taraikai, and in spring they go south. Therefore, on their backs

it is said that oysters grow. When looking at large whales floating in the distance, their backs already appear white. This is said to be because oysters have grown on them. Also, cranes, pheasants, wild geese and such return north in spring. In the marshes of the deep mountains of Ezo territory, summer birds also hide in the shade of the shores and live in flocks. When passing through the large and small marshes of Mount Uchirabe during the dog days of summer, cranes, pheasants, ducks and others were living in flocks. It is said there are even more in the marshes of Shiribetsu and Shikotsu.

One: The reason rice cultivation is not done in Matsumae may be because it is a cold country and the land is not suitable. Or perhaps because the season for rice cultivation conflicts with the herring fishing season, no one has attempted farming since ancient times. Recently, people from Tsugaru came and tried planting a small amount at a place called Oyobe, and the growth was quite good.
