Payment at the market rate of the time has been the established law since ancient times. In addition, there is ship entry tribute rice, and rice sent from merchants to farmers. Since this is a place where rice is not produced, rice instead flows in from various regions. With abundant rice, even the lower classes do not eat barley but only know to eat rice. With abundant charcoal and firewood, they use high-quality tea from Goshu province, and the water is clean and precious. Since salt is not made by burning seawater locally, it is sent from Tsugaru Sotogahama. The route to Kyoto uses cargo ships from Echizen Tsuruga that constantly travel back and forth, arriving in six or seven days with favorable winds, so Kyoto customs are surprisingly well known. Since there is no ship route to Edo, it feels more distant than Kyoto.

【Left page】
1. In the country, the eastern and southern areas are mountainous, while the western and northern areas are flat. The mountains have many rocks and peaks that rise like sheer cliffs. Most high mountain summits are burned and crumbled by gold and silver vapors or sulfur vapors. The abundance of gold in this land is unparalleled compared to other provinces. Until seventy years ago, placer gold was collected annually, paid as tribute to the lord, and shipped in large quantities to Kyoto and Osaka. These placer gold sites include Senmi-ga-take and Shiriuchi in Matsumae domain, and Kunnu, Kensui, Unbetsu, Yuhari, Shutsu and others in eastern Ezo territory. Some single locations span dozens of ri, making them vast. In the west is Pahoro, where placer gold comes up from the sea.
