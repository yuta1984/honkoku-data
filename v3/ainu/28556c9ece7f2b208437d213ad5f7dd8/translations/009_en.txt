Kan'ei 11
2nd day of the 5th month    Imperial seal

One: The samurai class is wealthy and has few worldly concerns. Even when wild dancing becomes popular, they do not indulge in pleasure-seeking and remain simple and honest. The national laws are lenient, emphasizing compassion above all, and tax collections are also lenient. Those who commit crimes are banished from the domain at most, and it does not extend to the death penalty. Because of this, in recent years, wicked people from other domains have infiltrated and caused disturbances, but

investigations and examinations are not conducted, and matters are settled as they are, which sometimes causes trouble for the local people. The merchants in the castle town are all from other domains - many are from Ōtsu and Hachiman in Ōmi Province, Satsuma village, etc., and there are also those from the coastal areas of Kaga, Noto, and Dewa. Half of the farmers are from Matsumae, and half are from Tsugaru and Nanbu. The merchants are all travelers, while those called farmers are all natives of the domain. The farmers' work does not involve rice cultivation; they only catch herring, paying one-fifteenth as tribute (unjo) and using the surplus for clothing and food. This is the main tax revenue of Matsumae domain. This herring fishing must truly be the greatest catch in all Japan. That is why the dried herring used as fertilizer is employed in domains from Nanbu, Tsugaru, Dewa, the northern provinces, to Ōmi, and herring roe is used throughout the entire country, though it is scarce.
