One: In the early days of the domain's founding, the Matsumae lord's rank was treated as that of a guest,
　　and during sankin (alternate attendance), he used post horses for travel, and had special privileges
　　compared to other daimyo of court noble rank. In the middle period, there was a young lord who neglected sankin duty, so
　　the protocol changed and was never reformed again.
　　From the time of Kenmyō (Shogun Ienobu), it was changed to the current rank. Of the former privileges that lasted until the end,

One: Although this domain does not produce the five grains, the profits from mountains and seas are double those of other domains. The samurai and people are
　　simple and honest and do not make doubling profits their business. They truly have the customs of ancient people.
　　After a hundred years of peaceful governance, luxury increases and court nobles become impoverished, and the various people
　　become unable to bear the labor - this is an ancient and modern custom. Perhaps because this domain's development was late,
　　the customs are sincere and there are no signs of decline. However, where the profits of mountains and seas
　　reach the people, comparing past to present, it has decreased by half, they say.
