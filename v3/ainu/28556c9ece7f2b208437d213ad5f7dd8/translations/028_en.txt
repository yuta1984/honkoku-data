There are large specimens, and those commonly seen have leaves about three shaku (approximately 3 feet) wide. In Ezo-chi (Hokkaido), there are even larger ones, said to have leaves five to six shaku wide. Bracken, wild yams, and Chinese yams are abundant. Wild burdock and carrots are also plentiful. Though I cannot identify the various medicinal plants, aconite and Solomon's seal are known to the locals and are abundant.

1. In the castle town, there are Ō-Matsumae and Ko-Matsumae, both serving as harbors. Eighteen ri west of Matsumae is a place called Esashi, where ships from various provinces dock, making it the most prosperous place in Matsumae. The provinces whose ships enter include Tsugaru, Nambu, Akita, Sakata, Kaga, Noto, Echigo, Echizen, Sado, and others. Ships from Osaka also come, and sometimes ships from Hizen bring pottery and ceramics. At this Esashi, something unusual happened the year before last.

【Left Page】
From early July, rain continued to fall, and it finally cleared around the twentieth day. On the twenty-sixth day, the weather was clear and fine, and the villagers went out to sea to gather kelp. Men and women mingled together on the beach, catching up on work that had been neglected during the many rainy days. Around the hour of the Sheep (approximately 2 PM), the mountains suddenly began to shake, and they looked back thinking it was thunder, but since it was clear sky without a single cloud, they thought it strange. While watching, the coastline suddenly began to collapse, so they panicked and fled into the sea. Then the rocks on the sea floor naturally protruded upward, and an island appeared. Several men and women who climbed onto it were so shocked they fainted. After a while, people gathered in excitement, witnessing this coastal collapse and the appearance of a new island...
