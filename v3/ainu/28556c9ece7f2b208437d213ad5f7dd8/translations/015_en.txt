Even in Sado province, the placer gold that comes from a place called Nishi-Mikawa is only a small amount. In Matsumae and Ezo territory, not only mountains and rivers but even plains contain placer gold. It certainly does not flow out from gold mines, but is placer gold that occurs naturally across the entire land. However, since the sources of rivers where gold occurs uniformly across the land should have gold-bearing veins, when this was investigated, a gold mine was found in the riverbed of the Kunsui placer gold site. The evidence was examined and already confirmed. Knowing that this land is based on placer gold, people do not know to follow the gold-bearing veins and enter various locations. Therefore, there has been no digging of mountains from before. Though there are some rare traces of digging that can be seen now, these were dug after finding placer gold, not dug targeting gold-bearing rock. For this reason, silver mines, copper mines, and such
