They told a story that once, when they were blown out several hundred ri offshore by a fierce wind, they finally found an island and approached it with their boat. The people of that island, both men and women without distinction, all had only one eye, so thinking it terrifying, they tried to push off their boat. A large crowd came out to the shore and said various things, but they could not understand what they were saying at all, and language did not get through. This was apparently an island located north of Kiitappu. It is also said that among the islands to the east, there is a hot country where there are Ainu people with curly hair.

1. Twenty ri before Kiitappu there is a place called Akkeshi. It produces large timber. Eight years ago, a merchant from Nanbu named Tsuji Bunzaemon was the first to enter the mountains of Akkeshi and cut timber. All of it is used for ship masts.

At this time, they sailed directly from Akkeshi to Edo and unloaded the timber at a lumber wholesaler in Teppozu. Because it was large timber, they waited for the price, and it is said some still remains today. This sea route comes across the great ocean and is said to be very direct. This makes sense. The shape of Ezo territory extends from north to east. The terrain extends horizontally into the eastern sea from the land facing Nanbu, corresponding to the northeast direction, for about two hundred ri. The maps handed down by the ancients are all crude. This is perhaps inevitable given the remote location. Even maps of Japan proper have inadequacies when examined in detail. Satsuma is at the western extremity of the western provinces, and Sendai is at the eastern extremity of the eastern provinces—they are completely unrelated like the wind and horses and cattle—yet there are disputes between fishing boats from offshore Satsuma and fishing boats from offshore Kinkazan in Sendai.
