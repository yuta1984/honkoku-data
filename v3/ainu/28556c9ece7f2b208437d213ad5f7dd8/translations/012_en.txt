Though connected by land, numerous mountain ranges stand in layers blocking any overland routes, so these are only maritime distances, making it difficult to state precise distances. When I inquired of seafarers experienced in eastern and western sea routes, they said it was about 270-280 ri to Sōya and about 300 ri to Kiitappu. This seems reasonable. These eastern and western locations are places where merchant ships from Matsumae go to trade. Though it is said that ships do not travel beyond these points, the north continues with Ainu settlements and villages, and they come out to Sōya and Kiitappu to trade. This remote territory where ships do not travel extends about 150 ri, and the northern limit is a place called Urayashibe. This land where ships do not travel is also called "Oku-Ezo" (Inner Ezo). 1. The Ainu people in places accessible by ship are all subjects of Matsumae. This territory of several hundred ri, except for the lord's directly administered lands, has been entirely allocated as fiefs to retainers, leaving no land unused. Each Ainu village has a chief called "otona." They receive orders from Matsumae, engage primarily in fishing and hunting, and pay tribute in marine products. Merchants contract for these nearby Ainu villages, pay operating fees (unjō) to Matsumae, and build trading posts at their contracted locations. These are called "unjō-ya" (operating houses). They control the Ainu people to conduct fishing and hunting, collect their catch, and send it to other provinces for trade.
