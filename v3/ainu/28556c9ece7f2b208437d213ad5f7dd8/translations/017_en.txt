【Right Page】
It is said to be about fifty ri overland from Uya. North of this Taraikai there is a place called Santan-Manchiu. The people of Matsumae say this is Northern Korea. Manchiu is probably Manchuria. Santan is unclear. Brocade, blue gems, etc. are also brought to Taraikai from these two countries. The people of Taraikai go to trade in both countries and constantly engage in commerce. Their language is different from that of the Ainu and cannot be understood, so they send young people from Taraikai to both countries to learn the languages and use them as interpreters. Both Santan and Manchiu have writing systems and good people, and have rice, sake, and tobacco, so they desire iron goods in trade. They trade for pots, kettles, kitchen knives and such. A ship captain named Yamada Kyūemon

【Left Page】
went to Sōya, and it happened that people from Santan came at the same time and they conversed. This Kyūemon had crossed to Ezo for two years and was knowledgeable about both the eastern and western seas of Ezo territory, so he could communicate in their language. He spoke with the Santan people, and they praised his abilities and gave him a scroll. When he opened it to look, it had large and small characters written together, most appearing to be barbarian script resembling Sanskrit. There were also Chinese characters written in standard script. It was written like the oracles of the three shrines of our country, arranged in upper, middle, and lower sections with large vermillion seals stamped on them. Since they could not understand what it said, he returned to Matsumae and presented it to the lord. Learned monks were gathered for examination, but because it was barbarian script, it was difficult to decipher and impossible to understand, so the document was stored with the lord.
