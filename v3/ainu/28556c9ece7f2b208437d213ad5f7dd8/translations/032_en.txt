They also wear deer hide, but their unconcerned appearance regarding clothing is because they are indifferent to heat and cold. They leave their hair disheveled with no head coverings, and ordinarily do not wear belts. When food comes from the sea, they eat it immediately with no preparation for storage. They have no fixed meal times morning and evening, eating just one meal from around evening time. Their features include deep brows, long eyes, and long sideburns, with hair growing all over their bodies like bears. From whatever country this custom was transmitted, they wear earrings or headbands. Though they appear robust at first glance, none are particularly strong. When made to wrestle with Japanese, they all lose from the waist down with no winners. They have strength in their arms and heads, and carry heavy loads using a strap against their foreheads. By nature they greatly favor sake and tobacco,

One: Women cut their hair short all around like being bald. They are fair-skinned and wear earrings. When marriage arrangements are settled, tattooing the lips and hands is an ancient custom. Around the lips they create various patterns as they please, like netting or wild geese formations. Therefore, having many characters on the hands seems to be a source of pride. They wear belts over their attushi, hang strings of blue beads like rosaries from their collars, and also wear something called shitoki - mirror-like objects with cords attached - on their chests. When going to the beach, they wear aprons and work alongside their husbands, appearing quite industrious. The husbands fish and hunt while the women gather firewood and make attushi
