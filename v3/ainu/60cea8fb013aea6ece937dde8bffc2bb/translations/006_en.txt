○ The Ainu people never wear straw sandals (zōri) or straw-soled footwear (waraji), nor do they wear rain capes (mino) or sedge hats (kasa). Even when traveling on the road, they wear only a single-layer attush robe. For a sash, they use whatever is at hand — even rope or wisteria vine will do. Their traveling equipment consists solely of a karo-futo (a hanging fire-starting kit), bow and arrows, a pipe, and a tobacco pouch.

○ Gold, silver, and coin currency do not circulate in the Ezo islands. Trade between one Ainu person and another is conducted by exchanging swords, small tools, quivers, and the like.

[Left page]
These items are treasured possessions in that land, carefully stored away deep in the mountains. Trade between the Ainu and the people of Matsumae involves exchanging Japanese products — rice, rice malt (kōji), sake, cotton cloth, used clothing, and garments — as well as old household goods and bladed tools, for Ezo products such as fish oil and dried fish. Unlike the Japanese, since gold, silver, and coin currency do not circulate among them, there are no wealthy individuals; everyone lives hand to mouth, accumulating no material goods whatsoever. Furthermore, since there are no land taxes or levies, they have no reason to apply themselves diligently to labor. They spend their days in play and leisure. Viewed from the perspective of being free from the pursuit of fame, profit, and reputation, theirs is a peaceful and contented existence.
