The vastness of this island (Karafuto/Sakhalin) is even greater than that of the island where Matsumae is situated (Hokkaido). The shape of the island is said to resemble a kite (bird) spreading its wings. Since I myself have not been there, I shall for the time being follow the account of the indigenous people. To the west of this island, separated by approximately ten ri of open sea, there is a country called "Santan." Its people are different from the Ezo people. From there, the land continues to Manchuria without break, and thus it is also connected overland to Beijing in China. The islands of "Kunashiri" and "Etorofu" resemble the shape of the character 人 (hito/person) written in standard script (kaisho). The "丿" stroke corresponds to "Etorofu" Island, and the "乀" stroke resembles "Kunashiri" Island. 　　　　　On the Great Rivers

Large countries with vast lands have great rivers, while narrow lands tend to have many small rivers. We may infer this from the fact that Japan's Saikaidō (Western Circuit) has great rivers, and the Tōkaidō (Eastern Circuit) likewise has many great rivers. The Ezo lands are an even more expansive territory than Japan proper, and so they too have many great rivers.
