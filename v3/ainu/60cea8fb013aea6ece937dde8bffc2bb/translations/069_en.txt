## On Local Products

**Gold Mines**
Among the islands where Matsumae is located, Senken Mountain, Kunnui Mountain, Horo Mountain, and others are recorded in various writings, but all of these yield what is called "shibashita-kin" (sub-surface gold) — that is, placer gold mixed within soil and sand. No actual gold veins exist. There is also the site of a former gold mine at a place called "Urakawa," and it seems likely that gold would be found if one were to dig there. There are also deposits around Erimo and on Ratsuyu Island. There may also be gold deep in the mountains, but as this is a vast land yet to be opened up, it is difficult to conduct detailed surveys. One should achieve this when the opportunity arises.

**Silver Mines**
There have been no accounts of silver mines since ancient times. There are said to be some at "Garatami Mountain" and "Kafuwotsuf Mountain." Western Ezo has many deep mountains, and the interior is of great interest, but as I have not yet reached there myself, it is difficult to report anything beyond hearsay.

**Copper Mines**
There is one in the deep mountains of Shebetsu in Eastern Ezo. There is also one in the mountains near Hakodate.

**Lead Mines**
Rahoma Peak in the interior of Miichi Village is said to be the finest. In a recent year, when people from Esashi Village dug there, approximately three hundred units were produced in a single year. There are also deposits in Akagami Village, Esashi Village, and other places.

**Iron Mines**
There are deposits in Omori Village, Ishizaki Village, and other places near Hakodate. They are also found in numerous other locations.

**Brass / Yellow Copper**
Found on islands called "Mesosui," "Ōsu," and "Torofu." This metal is not seen in Japan; it is golden in color in its raw state. It appears to be a kind of copper similar in nature to brass. A "Sekijin" (a red-haired foreigner, likely a Russian) crossed the sea and described it to me in detail.

**Emergency Rations (Preserved Food)**
Found at a place called "Shotsunecha" on Etorofu (Iturup) Island. It is stored and used as provisions from time to time. The color is white, it resembles mochi (rice cake), and it is sweet in taste. When I crossed to this island, I became separated from my companion vessel, and having neither rice nor miso, I boiled grass roots and added this substance to make a meal. It was remarkably light and good in flavor.
