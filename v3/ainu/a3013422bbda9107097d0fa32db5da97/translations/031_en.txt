**Illustration of Chishiyukina (a plant)**

This grass grows abundantly on plains and open fields. Upon tasting it, the flavor is slightly sweet and salty. The Ainu people harvest its roots, crush them into pieces the size of rice grains, and boil them down to make a fermentation starter (koji). Additionally, they separately prepare another variety by boiling it, then combine it with the fermentation starter to brew an alcoholic beverage. Its taste is equivalent to Japanese unrefined sake (doburoku), and it produces intoxication in the same manner.
