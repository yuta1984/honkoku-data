【Sketch of Heroki (Herring)】

In the dialect of Matsumae, it is called "Nishimu," and a Chinese character was devised, using the character "鯡" (herring).

Fishing takes place from the spring equinox until around the fourth month.
It is produced more abundantly here than anywhere else in Ezo.
The herring of the western Ezo region are large, long, and of superior quality.
Forty fish are bundled together to make one bunch, and the back meat is
cut and dried (sun-dried, "hikake"). The roe (kazunoko) is obtained from within them.
The raw flesh is soft but
does not taste good. The people of this island make their living by
harvesting herring and kelp, and appear to have
no knowledge of farming.
