Bunka 7 (1810)

In Edo, to Shibuya Gonzaemon:
○Regarding the annual memorial services for children who passed away in their early years, henceforth the following shall apply:
　One: Two pieces of silver - for memorial service offerings
　One: 200 hiki of gold
　　　Condolence money from the current lord, with proxy worship performed by retainers
　Additionally:
　　100 hiki of gold - for memorial tablet fees
　　1 ryō - for meal expenses
As ordered above, formal attendants shall be discontinued, and rear palace staff shall also be reduced as much as possible
　One: Kōgen-in-sama
　One: Yōgan-in-sama
For these two lords, it shall be ordered as before
　　February 1st
　　The separate document is the same as above
　　　　　　　To Yamaguchi Sakuemon

In Edo, to Minagawa Toneri:
○Regarding the ceremonial dress for attendants during the lord's journey, if arrival at the castle is in May, lined garments shall be used; if it extends to June, unlined summer garments shall be used, as ordered. Henceforth, understand this and make arrangements accordingly
　　The separate document is the same as above, to Kajikawa Hanbei
　　　May 1st

Same:
○Those permanently stationed during the lord's absence should be prudent in all matters, and especially the younger members should devote themselves even more to martial arts, according to his lordship's wishes. It has been ordered that this matter should be communicated from time to time
However, regarding the above, what the senior councillor Mizutani Oribe communicated was that since this has now been ordered, it should be conveyed to each respective person. Therefore, it was communicated to the junior guard units and above, and for future reference, it should also be conveyed to the inspectors
　　Same day, 2nd
