Over a period of more than ten years, the majority of our country's gold and silver was reduced, making it difficult for the nation's finances to circulate as they had initially. Consequently, during the Genroku era, the laws governing gold and silver were reformed and reestablished, doubling the amount of gold and silver in circulation in our country. However, since the quality of this gold and silver fell far short of what Toshogu had established, merchants and artisans looked down upon the value of the newly minted gold and silver, and in their schemes to avoid losing profits, they increased the prices of all goods in their trade, resulting in

the prices of all goods becoming more expensive year by year, while the value of gold and silver became cheaper year by year, until finally both public and private sectors, noble and humble alike, fell into hardship. In foreign countries, it has been common since ancient times for the quality of precious currencies to vary, and particularly since medieval times, it is heard that they have used paper money (treasury notes) to replace gold and silver for circulation throughout their realms, continuing to this day. Even though the gold and silver since Genroku may be of lower quality, they should not be compared to foreign paper money.
