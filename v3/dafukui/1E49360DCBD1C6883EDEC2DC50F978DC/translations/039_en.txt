Regarding the tax collection offices in rural areas where deputy officials are staying, in order to ensure that the peasants' work is not hindered, in addition to the established three-person stipend for their own provisions, they shall receive an additional one-person stipend during the period from the 9th to the 12th month, for 100 days of service.

1. Regarding deputy magistrate assistants: for those with fiefs, one person per 2,500 koku of assessed value; for magistrate officials receiving rice stipends, one person per 2,000 koku of assessed value. All shall receive 13 koku of rice stipend and three-person provisions as

1. Regarding peasants under magistrate jurisdiction: not only upper magistrates, but also lower deputies and subordinates shall not accept any gifts whatsoever.

1. Peasants under magistrate jurisdiction shall serve the magistrate personally one day per year per household room. If they cannot serve due to other duties, they shall pay daily wages of 5 bu of silver per day. When sending laborers as substitutes for peasants, one horse shall be equivalent to two laborers. Private use of post-horse laborers beyond the established regulations is prohibited.
