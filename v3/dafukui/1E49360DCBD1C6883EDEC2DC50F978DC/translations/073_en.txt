　　He was ordered from his lodgings at Kimoto to depart from Fukui on the 14th
1. On the 14th day of the intercalary second month, because Dewa-no-kami (Naomasa) had been unwell last winter, female attendants who had been sent by Matsudaira Nagato-no-kami (Hideyuki) to visit him were returning to Edo. Regarding the matter of travel permits for women to pass through Imakiri checkpoint, a message came from Dewa-no-kami (Naomasa)'s chief retainer Asahi Tamba (Shigemasa), so it was sent through the elder Koma Takumi-no-jō (Takasumi)
