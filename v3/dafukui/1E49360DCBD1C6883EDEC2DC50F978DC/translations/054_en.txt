　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Sakai Ukyō-no-suke
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Tadayo (signed and sealed)
　　　　　　　　　　　Matsudaira Iyo-no-kami-dono

1. December 7th
　　　　The following was decreed by official document from the government:
　　　　　　　1. Regarding silk and pongee: One tan in length shall be three jō
　　　　　　　　two shaku by the carpenter's measure, width one shaku four sun
　　　　　　　1. Regarding hemp cloth and cotton: One tan in length shall be by the carpenter's measure
　　　　　　　　three jō two shaku, width one shaku three sun

【Left page】
　　　　　　　　For the above textiles, those who sell silk and
　　　　　　　　cloth that fall short of the prescribed length and width measurements shall be
　　　　　　　　suspended from the first day of the fourth month of next year (Year of the Rabbit), and appropriate action shall be taken.

1. Same month, 21st day: Five formal court robes were presented as year-end congratulatory gifts,
　　　　whereupon an imperial letter was delivered at the castle.

　　　　　　　　I have duly acknowledged the arrival of five court robes
　　　　　　　　as year-end congratulations.
　　　　　　　　Sakai Ukyō-no-suke will convey further details. Respectfully,

　　　　　　　　　December 27th　　Iemitsu　(signed and sealed)
