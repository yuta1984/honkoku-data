An envoy, assistant keeper of the Edo residence Takasaka Buemon Yoshifumi, was dispatched.
　　However, although [the lord] should have attended both baileys of the castle to express gratitude, due to illness an envoy was dispatched instead.

1. On the same day, regarding the same matter, due to illness it would be difficult to attend the castle tomorrow, so an envoy and keeper of the Edo residence was dispatched to duty administrator Honda Nakatsukasa-dayū Tadayoshi for official notification.

1. On the sixth day of the sixth month, since there were Noh performances, one folding lunch box was presented through envoy and keeper of the Edo residence Sakata Shichiemon Nagatsune (a written notice was given by the duty administrator on the second day of last month).

1. On the eleventh day of the same month, since [the lord's] illness had recovered, notification was made that he could participate in future emergency deployments in case of fires around Tōei-zan Temple. Envoy and keeper of the Edo residence Sakata Shichiemon Nagatsune was dispatched to duty administrator Honda Nakatsukasa-dayū Tadayoshi.

1. On the thirteenth day of the same month, for the celebration of the seventh night ceremony, [the lord] attended the West Bailey and then the Main Bailey of the castle to express congratulations (notification was given on the eleventh day of last month).

1. On the same day, Kasuya Jidayū Okifumi was sent as a proxy pilgrim to both Ise Shrines to offer prayers of gratitude. To the Inner Shrine: 500 hiki of initial offering money, 2 ryō for kagura music, and 5 silver pieces for prayer fees. To the Outer Shrine: 500 hiki of initial offering money. Jidayū departed from Fukui today.
　　This proxy pilgrimage was arranged because the Fujinami shrine priest had communicated that prayers had been conducted for eleven years since a prayer document was sent in Kyōhō 11 (1726), continuing until this year.
