- On the sixth day of the first month, the first and second sets of ceremonial equipment (for Lady Katsuhime's wedding procession) were dispatched via Ōseki Shingozaemon (Nao-■), the Sugigata Spear Magistrate (Sugigata-yari-bugyō), and Sakakibara Sukizaemon (Masafusa), an御使番 (inspector/messenger official).
  Note: The third and subsequent sets of equipment were to be sent on a day-by-day basis depending on weather conditions.

- On the seventh day of the same month, as a congratulatory gesture for the Young Greens Festival (Wakana), a messenger was sent to present, as customary, a ceremonial fish offering (sea bream/tai).

- On the ninth day of the same month, the senior retainer (karō) Koma Moku (Kōshō) had departed from the home domain on the twenty-eighth day of the previous twelfth month for matters related to Lady Katsuhime's wedding, and having arrived in Edo today (the ninth day), he was granted an audience (omemie).

- On the tenth day of the same month, as Honda Shuri (Masataka) in the home domain was gravely ill, a letter of inquiry and well-wishing was sent.

- On the seventeenth day of the first month, in order to formally notify the duty official (goyōban) Matsudaira Izunokami (Nobutoki) of Lady Katsuhime's procession to the residence of Matsudaira Daizenno-daibu (Munehiro) on the following eighteenth day, the following written statement was submitted:

　　　　　　Memorandum of Oral Statement

　As I communicated to the duty official last month, the wedding of my younger sister to Matsudaira Daizenno-daibu has been arranged for the eighteenth day. I hereby notify you of this through a messenger. Respectfully submitted.

　　　　　　Seventeenth day of the first month

　Note: A similarly worded document was also submitted to Matsudaira Notonokami, a Senior Councilor (rōjū) of the Nishinomaru (the heir apparent's palace); however, as the content is identical, it is not recorded here.
