Article 1: The order of ceremonial seating is detailed in the section on various ceremonial protocols
　Article 1: From the original ancestor's Kōchi-chū to guard organization and above, the head position is at the second tatami mat from the paper threshold upward. For ceremonial occasions of yoriki officials, the head position is at the first tatami mat from the paper threshold upward, with the master of ceremonies and inspector in attendance

　Article 1: The matter of Kōchi-chū seating is determined by the lord's will at the time
　　　However, according to current practice, appointments are made to the third position, two ranks below the father's position - around the Kōka era
　Article 1: For yoriai bangai (assembly outside members), appointments are made to the fourth position, three ranks below the father's position
　　This was established through Honda Shuri's inquiry regarding Sakai Takurō's seating in the first year of Shōtoku (1711), year of the rabbit. However, there may be slight variations depending on circumstances
　Article 1: The heir to an elder councilor's position is appointed to the senior seat of the assembly
　　　However, currently, appointments are made to the fourth position, three ranks below when continuing from that official position to the assembly - around the Kōka era
　Article 1: The heir of a retired guard captain enters their parent's original seat
　Article 1: When a chamberlain from regular succession seating is appointed as palace guard captain, if there is an unemployed assembly member above them, they are promoted by one seat. If a guard captain is above them, they are appointed while maintaining their current seat
　Article 1: When regular succession seating appointees become caretakers, palace guard captains, or great guard captains, if there is an unemployed assembly member above them, they are promoted by one seat. If someone already serving in an official role is above them, they remain in their current seat
　　　However, this article states that on the 18th day of the 11th month of Bunka 12 (1815), all three positions - palace guard captain, great guard captain, and caretaker - should be promoted by one seat.
　　For example, someone promoted by one seat when appointed as great guard captain would not need further promotion when later appointed as palace guard captain or caretaker, as announced
