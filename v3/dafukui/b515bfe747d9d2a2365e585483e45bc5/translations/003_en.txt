Official proclamation issued on the 15th day of the first month

【Red inscription】This document was issued around the 28th day of the first month until Kyōhō 9 (1724), the year of the Dragon. Since the authorities took care of this matter and the inspectors frequently issued proclamations, the number of servants increased greatly. Therefore, it was decided that from next year, the year of the Snake, the proclamation should be issued at the beginning of the first month, with additional notices to follow.

Memorandum

【Red inscription】This document first appears in the record of various matters in Kyōhō 9, the year of the Dragon.

Article 1: Since a shortage of servants in the lord's household would cause difficulties for everyone, it was proclaimed to the administrators that people from towns, rural areas, and even those under direct administration should not be sent carelessly to other provinces for service.

Article 1: When hiring servants according to regulations, one should inquire whether there are any issues and should confirm the amount of salary when notifying the previous master. If any previous master falsely reports the salary, this should be reported immediately to the inspectors, and punishment will surely be imposed. If a master, through misunderstanding, takes pity on a retainer and overstates the salary to someone who inquires, that master will be held accountable in the future. Since salaries vary depending on the servant, they should be hired with slight increases or decreases. For any type of servant, the salary should be limited to two ryō and one bu. As servants' salaries increase year by year, causing difficulties for masters, proclamations have been issued repeatedly since previous years. However, perhaps because people lack such consideration, it is strictly forbidden to tolerate servants who make selfish demands or devise ways to obtain high salaries. Everyone should understand this principle and report any misconduct promptly to the inspectors. If dismissing retainers who have served until now on the 2nd day of the second month would cause problems, they should be employed immediately.
