　Proclamation issued on the 15th day of the first month
【Red ink note】"This document was issued around the 28th day of the first month until Kyōhō 9 (year of the dragon). Since the authorities took care of this matter and the magistrates frequently issued proclamations, the number of servants increased. Therefore, it was decided that from next year (year of the snake), proclamations should be issued at the beginning of the first month as additions."
　　　　　Memorandum
【Red ink note】"This document first appears in the miscellaneous records of Kyōhō 9, year of the dragon"
1. Since a shortage of servants in the domain households would cause difficulties for everyone, people from towns, villages, and territories under direct control should not be sent carelessly to serve in other provinces. This has been proclaimed to their respective administrators.
1. When hiring servants according to regulations, there is no problem, but when reporting to the former master, the amount of salary should be confirmed. If a former master falsely states the salary, this should be reported immediately to the magistrates. Punishment will certainly be imposed. If a master, out of misguided sympathy for a retainer, tells excessive salary amounts to inquirers, this will be the master's fault thereafter. Since salary levels vary depending on the servant, hiring should be done with slight increases or decreases. For any type of servant, wages should be limited to two ryō and one bu. As servants' salaries increase yearly, causing difficulties for masters, proclamations have been issued repeatedly since previous years. Perhaps because everyone lacks such consideration, it is strictly forbidden to be lenient with servants who make selfish demands or devise ways to obtain high salaries. Everyone should understand this principle and report unruly individuals to the magistrates immediately. If there is a shortage due to giving leave to currently employed retainers on the 2nd day of the second month, they should be employed immediately.
