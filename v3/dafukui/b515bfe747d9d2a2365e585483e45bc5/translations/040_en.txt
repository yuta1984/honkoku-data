**10th day of the 6th month — To All Concerned / From the Inspectors (Metsuke)**

*Addendum:* Regarding the large drainage ditch behind the Edo-machi (town) area, although dredging was ordered this spring, upon subsequent inspection it was found that weir stones had been placed from behind the residential estates, and repairs to drainage outlets were observed from behind the town as well. Within the next day or two, inspectors will be dispatched to dredge the drainage channels, remove weir stones and the like from drainage outlets, and set the waterway to the prescribed width. Please be informed accordingly. When notice is given, each party is to send one servant/retainer as before. The town ward will be directed to supply laborers; please be aware of this. That is all.
*(This addendum was circulated only to those on the north side of Edo-machi. Note: Notices regarding the water supply (jōsui) are recorded in the "Kyūrei-kō" and "Kinrei-kō" registers of the water supply administration and are therefore omitted here.)*

○ Regarding hunting/killing of animals in the castle moats: this has long been prohibited, and notice boards (seisatsu) have been posted accordingly, so all should be well aware that such activities are not permitted. It appears, however, that lower-ranking persons unfamiliar with the rules may be engaging in such conduct. Therefore, it is appropriate that the prohibition at these designated locations be strictly communicated even to one's servants and retainers.

**28th day of the 6th month**

*(Regarding the above matter):* As it came to be heard that people had been casting nets and the like into the castle moats at dusk, this was discussed among fellow inspectors, and today at the Main Keep (Honmaru), the matter was conveyed to those currently on monthly duty by the month-duty officer Atsumi Shinuemon, and all acknowledged their understanding.

○ Although the customary notices for the Bon festival season have already been issued, it is further directed that people must not be sent to the open areas of the castle town where crowds gather — particularly to Katsumi. Those who have jurisdiction over subordinates should once again make this known to them.

**7th day of the 7th month**

The above was conveyed at the Honmaru to the high-ranking retainers (kōchi) and all those on monthly duty.

○ As the pigeons at the castle gates have become fewer, everyone is strictly ordered, down to the lowest-ranking persons, not to catch pigeons within their estates. Even tame pigeons kept within the grounds must not be caught. Please be informed accordingly. That is all.

**Kyōhō 18, Year of the Ox (1733)**

○ Written Notice Regarding Fire Safety:
*(This is recorded in the section of orders issued by the Senior Elders (Karō) and is therefore omitted here.)*

○ As has been decreed by written notice, the matter of fire safety is to be strictly communicated to all, down to the lowest-ranking persons. Furthermore, since it is established practice to carry a lit lantern when traveling about from the fifth hour (approximately 8 p.m.) onward, this rule is to be strictly observed, and all are to be ordered to carry lit lanterns after the prescribed hour. Should there be any negligent conduct, corrections will be made immediately. Naturally, on windy and dry nights, each person's servants are to be ordered to patrol the vicinity of their estate throughout the night. Additionally, since those left behind in Edo may be short-staffed, arrangements should be made with relatives or neighboring households to watch out for each other with due diligence. Be aware that duty officers will be patrolling both day and night.
