The retainers employed until now should continue to be employed as before. After explaining the above matters, if there are those who make various complaints and refuse to understand, the circumstances should be reported to the inspectors.

Article 1: When lower-class people change employment, they falsely claim urgent business or pretend to withdraw into seclusion for service, temporarily hiding around to create a shortage of servants and demand high wages. Therefore, since previous years, it has been ordered to the town magistrates and district magistrates that those who make claims from the first month to engage in commerce, or those who withdraw for rest, even if they are parents, children, or siblings, should not be allowed to stay if they extend their absence for five to seven days.

Additionally, servants who are ill and unable to serve from spring and withdraw should not be sent outside their designated town (for townspeople) or village (for rural people) for the entire year, even if they recover during summer. If someone withdraws due to unavoidable circumstances, and the master writes a report about this matter and submits it to the authorities, it should be approved. Retainers should not be carelessly taken in without proper investigation, nor should dismissal letters be submitted to the authorities.

Following the above proclamation, those who do not employ servants because lower-class people demand high wages should be ordered to employ farmers instead. Generally, anyone who acts selfishly will be punished immediately upon discovery. The above instructions must be communicated to all retainers. The above.
