Current retainers should continue to be employed as they are. After explaining the above matters, if there are those who make various claims and do not understand, the reasons should be reported to the magistrates.

1. When lower-ranking people change positions, some claim unavoidable business or withdraw into seclusion under the pretense of service, temporarily hiding around to create a shortage of servants and demand higher salaries. Therefore, since previous years, town magistrates and district magistrates have been ordered that from the first month, those who make claims and come as merchants, or those who withdraw for rest, even if they are parents, children, or siblings, should not be allowed to stay for five to seven days. This is strictly prohibited.

　　　Note: Servants who are ill and cannot serve from spring and withdraw should not be sent outside their town (for townspeople) or village (for rural areas) during that year, even if they recover by summer. If someone withdraws due to unavoidable business and the master writes down the details and consults with the administrator, they should be allowed to stay. Retainers should not be carelessly entrusted without proper examination, nor should dismissal notices be submitted to administrators.

Since the above has been proclaimed, those who do not hire servants because lower-ranking people demand high salaries will be ordered to employ farmers instead. Generally, if there are those who act selfishly, punishment will certainly be imposed as soon as it becomes known. The above principles must be strictly communicated to all retainers. End.

　　　　　　　Memorandum
1. Regarding the replacement of lower-ranking people, while it is acceptable for masters to dismiss servants through mutual agreement, it is strictly forbidden for servants to request dismissal on their own initiative. When making replacements, they should be hired without high salaries.

1. As for public service, it goes without saying, but if domain residents wish to abandon their fields and withdraw, their request should be acknowledged and reported to the district magistrate through the domain administrator, and dismissal should be granted.

1. Regarding replacements, if lower-ranking people make selfish demands, especially seeking high salaries, or if there are those who attach themselves to supervisors and cause various obstructions, this should be reported immediately to the magistrates. Thorough investigation and punishment will certainly be ordered.

1. For tool-bearers taken to Edo, wages should be limited to two ryō and one bu, and sandal-bearers and attendants should be employed lightly according to the same standard. Hiring high-salary individuals is strictly unnecessary. Generally, servants should be hired more modestly according to their positions.
