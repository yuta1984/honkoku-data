The retainers employed until now should continue to be employed as they are. After explaining the above articles, if there are those who make various complaints and do not understand, the reasons should be reported to the magistrates.

1. When lower-ranking people change positions, some claim unavoidable business or engage in reclusive service, temporarily hiding around to create a shortage of servants and demand high salaries. Therefore, since previous years, town magistrates and district magistrates have been ordered that from the first month, those who make such claims and come as merchants, or those who withdraw for rest, even if they are parents, children, or siblings, must not be allowed to stay for more than five to seven days.

Additionally, servants who are ill and cannot perform service from spring and withdraw should not be allowed to leave their town (for townspeople) or village (for rural people) for the entire year, even if they recover during summer. If someone withdraws due to unavoidable business, and the master writes down the details and reports to the authorities, they should be allowed to stay. Retainers should not be carelessly entrusted to others, and dismissal letters should not be submitted to authorities without proper investigation.

Since the above has been proclaimed, those who do not employ servants because the lower classes demand high salaries will be ordered to employ farmers. Generally, if there are those who act selfishly, punishment will certainly be imposed as soon as it becomes known. The above principles must be strictly communicated to all retainers. The above.
