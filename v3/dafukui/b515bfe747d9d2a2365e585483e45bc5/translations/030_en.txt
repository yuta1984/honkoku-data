One: Kyōhō 3, Year of the Dog (1718)
　○It has been heard since last winter that there are those among the lower ranks of the domain retinue who appear to be engaging in gambling. As gambling has long been strictly prohibited, it is utterly outrageous that such conduct is occurring. Should an investigation reveal such activity, the master of the household concerned shall be held responsible for negligence. Therefore, each person must conduct thorough investigations and strictly order that nothing even resembling gambling be permitted. The above.

　　　Additionally, those with supervisory authority over groups must ensure this order is conveyed without fail. The above.
　○Regarding fire hazards, orders have previously been issued on this matter, but as His Lordship is currently absent, it has been further ordered that strict fire precautions be enforced. Understand this intent and convey it strictly to subordinates. The above.

One: Kyōhō 4, Year of the Boar (1719)
　○Regarding the spring servant changeover for lower-ranking persons, notice was given previously [marginal note: "This is a standing official notice"], but it has since been heard that there are those who, in connection with the new gold and silver currency, are selfishly demanding higher wages, which is utterly outrageous. Regarding wages, whether in new gold or dry gold, servants are to be engaged by mutual agreement with their master based on the previously notified wage standards. If there are those who engage servants at higher wages, the master shall be held responsible for negligence. Furthermore, if there are those who make various demands from below, the details should be reported to the inspectors, who will investigate and issue orders accordingly. The above.

　　　　　　　Memorandum
　One: Engaging domain household servants at high wages has been prohibited since before, yet it is heard that year after year servants continue to seek higher wages, which is utterly outrageous. Up until now, this has been permitted because of the fluctuation in the value of gold and silver currency, but as new gold will be in circulation from next spring, servant wages are to be set in accordance with the standards that applied during the circulation of Keichō gold coins. However, because of the recent shortage of servants, the situation has been left as is. At the time of the spring changeover next year, servants should be engaged at the equivalent rate, calculated in terms of dry gold, of what was paid during the current Year of the Boar. If servants are engaged at higher wages in violation of this prohibition, the master shall be held responsible for negligence. Understand this accordingly.
　One: At the time of the servant changeover for lower-ranking persons, there are those who cite unavoidable business or feign withdrawal from service, lying low for a time to create a shortage of servants, and then demand higher wages. Therefore, from this year's year-end, the town magistrates and district magistrates have been instructed that, starting from the first month of next year, those who have made such declarations and become merchants, or who have withdrawn ostensibly for rest, shall not be permitted to remain in that status beyond fifty days, even if they are parent, child, or sibling.
