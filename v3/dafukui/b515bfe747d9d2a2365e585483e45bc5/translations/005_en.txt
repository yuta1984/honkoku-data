【Red ink】"From Genroku like this"

○If there are those who go to serve in other households, it is forbidden for village headmen to act as sub-guarantors. Except for parents, children, and siblings, no matter what kind of relatives they may be, when they are employed elsewhere, they must not act as sub-guarantors or even oral guarantors. If there are unavoidable circumstances requiring parents, children, or siblings to act as sub-guarantors, masters or those with authority should report to their superiors and act according to their instructions.

The above principles should be strictly communicated to retainers. Those with group authority should furthermore convey this, so this intent should be understood. The above.

○In recent years, there has been a shortage of servants, and moreover they demand high salaries, so proclamations have been issued repeatedly. However, in the past year or two, both male and female servants have been making various claims during times of position changes, waiting to see masters, or withdrawing for rest, and many such cases have been reported. Therefore, in recent years【Red ink margin note】"Hōreki 4th year this spring" "same 5th year last spring" "from same 6th year became recent years", written orders have been issued anew to town and rural area supervisors stating that such people, even if they are parents, children, or siblings, should not be allowed to stay at home even temporarily. If those in rural areas withdraw unavoidably due to other administrative hindrances, they should provide substitutes. Most importantly, they must never be sent to serve in other places or domains. Moreover, both in towns and rural areas, there are many children, and those who have returned to live with parents and siblings, or those in rural areas who engage in small trade and earn daily wages, should be sent out for service with strict orders to each respective area.
