　　　　　　4th month, 4th day [Note: reads "8th month, 4th day" in original]

One: Kyōhō 7, Year of the Tiger (1722)

◯Gambling has long been strictly prohibited. Furthermore, all those who oversee groups, as well as their retainers, are to be instructed that there must be no conduct even resembling such activities. Those in charge are to make rounds among members of their groups, and in cases of suspicious locations, the master himself is to conduct an investigation. The above provisions are to be firmly communicated in advance. The above.　5th month, 16th day　To all　Additionally to groups～　Inspectors

◯Even when dispatching retainers and others to Kyoto, they are to be instructed not to lodge for more than one night. Until a further notice is issued, the above is to be strictly observed. The above.　8th month, 28th day　To all　Additionally to groups～　Inspectors
　　　This notice was issued because a proclamation had been issued throughout the capital's districts that persons carrying swords were prohibited from lodging in the capital for more than one night, and this was communicated to the local magistrates by Yoshizawa Kanzaemon's office, prompting this instruction.

One: Kyōhō 8, Year of the Rabbit (1723)

◯Since gambling has long been a serious prohibition, any activity resembling gambling in which outcomes are determined—even in small matters—is to be prohibited, even as a form of amusement. Should such activity come to light, the matter will be thoroughly investigated and appropriate action will certainly be taken. Each party is to understand this intent and strictly instruct their retainers to observe the same. The above.　2nd month, 9th day　To all　Additionally to groups～　Inspectors

【Left page】

　　　　　　Verbal Statement

◯As the ladies-in-waiting are moving to the garden pond area today, henceforth when persons are to ascend to the roof or other elevated places, they must first obtain permission from the officials attending the ladies-in-waiting and follow their instructions. Subordinates are to be strictly instructed not to ascend to elevated places without proper authorization. The above.
