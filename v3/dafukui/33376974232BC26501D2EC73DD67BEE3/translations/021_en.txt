【Right Page】
　　Regarding the passing of the Retired Emperor at the Imperial Palace on the 19th day past, a letter was submitted to Toda Echizen-no-kami Tadamasa, the Kyoto Deputy (Shoshidai).
One　8th Month, 25th Day: Regarding the same matter as above,
　　messenger Ito Jōzaemon was dispatched to Edo to inquire after His Lordship's well-being.
　　Departed Fukui today.
　　　　However, the date of Jōzaemon's arrival in Edo is unknown. A reply letter was delivered on the 7th day of intercalary 8th month.
One　Same month, 27th Day:
　　Regarding Tsunamasa-sama's appointment as Minor Captain (shōshō), messenger Tsuchiura Ichirōbei Noriaki was dispatched to Edo
　　for thanks, and one type of ceremonial fish was offered. Ichirōbei departed Fukui today.

【Left Page】
　　　　However, the date of Ichirōbei's arrival in Edo is unknown. A reply letter was delivered on the 10th day of intercalary 8th month.
One　Intercalary 8th Month, 2nd Day:
　　A letter of congratulations was submitted regarding the completion of the shogun's imperial appointment (senka) on the 23rd day of last month.
　　　　However, a reply letter was delivered on the 16th day of the same month.
One　Same month, 12th Day:
　　For congratulations on the completion of the shogun's imperial appointment, through messenger Kageyama Yohei, a ceremonial sword
　　and horse substitute (ten ryō of gold) were offered.
　　　　　However, while this messenger appeared to be dispatched from the domain, it was actually presented through Yohei who was stationed in Edo.
