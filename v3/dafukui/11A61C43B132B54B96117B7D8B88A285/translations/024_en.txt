First, Bunsei 12th year, Tsuchinoto-ushi (1829):
　　Isshugin (one-shu silver coins) began circulation

First, Tenpō 2nd year, Kanoto-u (1831), March 4th: Around New Year, over 40 monme
　　Due to high rice prices causing hardship among the common people, rice was distributed 
　　from the domain storehouse at 37 monme per hyō. Around the 12th, the rice market 
　　price was 39 monme for buying and 39 monme 5 bu for selling.
　　Accordingly, from around June, Akita rice was purchased and distributed.

First, Same 3rd year, Mizunoe-tatsu (1832):
　　Nishukin (two-shu gold coins) began circulation

First, Same 4th year, Mizunoto-hitsuji (1833), August:
　　Isshukin (one-shu gold coins) circulation ceased

First, Same year:
　　Nationwide famine. Around December, one hyō cost 45 monme.
　　Relief rice distribution to townspeople was implemented.

First, Same 5th year, Kinoe-uma (1834), February 15th:
　　Promissory note: one hyō for 44 monme 5 bu

First, Same year, around November:
　　Promissory note: one hyō for approximately 32 monme 5 bu

First, Same 7th year, Hinoe-saru (1835), June:
　　Ōban gold coin transactions ceased
