Due to rumors that the domain would abandon the half-stipend reduction policy, the entire country fell into great turmoil. However, since the amount of paper money in circulation was enormous, exchange proved difficult to implement. New paper money was ordered to be issued, reduced to one-third of the original value, with 100 mon being exchanged for 33 monme of new certificates.

First, Enpō 3rd year, Kinoto-u (1675):
　Great famine struck all provinces

First, Genroku 8th year, Kinoto-i (1695):
　Gold and silver recoinage; changed to gold coins with the character "Gen" (元)

First, Same 15th year, Mizunoe-uma (1702):
　Silver certificates in circulation; Kanaya Yasuke was appointed as head administrator

First, Hōei 1st year, Kinoe-saru (1704):
　Hō-character silver coins were minted

First, Same 4th year, I year (1707):
　Silver certificates suspended in all provinces, then resumed
