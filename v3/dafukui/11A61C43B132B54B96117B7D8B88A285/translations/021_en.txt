First, Genbun 1st year, Hinoe-tatsu (1736), June:
　　Bun-character gold and silver were issued. Gold coins were exchanged at a 65% increase
　　Silver at a 50% increase
　　　Old gold: 1,650 ryō for 1,000 ryō; old silver: 150 kan-monme for 100 kan-monme

First, Hōreki 5th year, Kinoto-i (1755):
　　From midsummer of this year, continuous rain caused flood damage in various regions, and rice borer insects damaged crops
　　By year's end, rice was 40 monme per hyō

First, Meiwa 5th year, Tsuchinoe-ne (1768), March:
　　Brass four-mon coins began to be minted

First, Tenmei 3rd year, Mizunoto-u (1783):
　　Poor harvests in various provinces. In autumn, about 34 monme per hyō, rising to 36-37 monme by year's end
　　Lending and borrowing became difficult in society
　　　Mount Asama erupted, ash fell in various provinces - this occurred in June

First, Same 4th year, Kinoe-tatsu (1784):
　　　At New Year's it became 40 monme, around summer premium rice was 50 monme, retail at 8 gō per monme
　　　Salt: 35 mon per shō; rice bran: 15 mon; red beans: 15 mon per shō
　　　Sake: 1 monme 5 bu per shō
