First, Hōei 5th year, Tsuchinoe-ne (1708):
　Ten-mon coins were newly minted

First, Same 7th year, Kanoe-tora (1710):
　Kan-character gold, medium silver, Sanpō, Yonpō, and Hō-character silver were issued

First, Shōtoku 4th year, Kinoe-uma (1714): New gold and silver were issued
　　　　　　　Those issued in Hōei 7th year
　For Keichō gold one ryō: exchanged for two ryō of Kan gold
　　　　　　　　Similarly
　For Keichō silver one kan-monme: exchanged for two kan-monme of medium silver, Sanpō, Yonpō, and treasure silver
　Those issued in Genroku 7th year
　For Gen-character silver one kan-monme: exchanged for 1,600 mon of medium silver, Sanpō, Yonpō, and treasure silver
　Silver from early Hōei period
　For Hō-character silver one kan-monme: exchanged for 1,300 mon of the aforementioned silver

First, Kyōhō 4th year, Tsuchinoto-i (1719):
　Circulation of Kan gold was suspended

First, Kyōhō 6th year, Kanoto-ushi (1721):
　Gold and silver exchange began

First, Same 15th year, Kanoe-inu (1730):
　Countries that previously had silver certificate circulation could be granted permission upon petition
　Accordingly, a 25-year petition was granted from this year, with circulation beginning from November 1st

First, Same 16th year (1731):
　Due to falling rice prices in various provinces, 50,000 hyō (one-third of stored rice in Edo) were purchased
　At the rate of one koku nine to two shō per gold ryō
