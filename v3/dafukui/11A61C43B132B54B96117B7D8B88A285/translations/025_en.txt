First, Tenpō 7th year, Hinoe-saru (1836), August:
　　Around this time, grain prices were 70 monme per hyō, retail 6.5 gō per 1 monme
　　As usual, hulled rice and barley were granted to those in hardship. Around late October, 75 monme.
　　Rice was distributed at 1 monme 3 bu per 1 shō. Shelters for outcasts were built in 
　　Hama-machi, Kawara, Fukawa, and Dō-mae, where gruel was served. Rice gruel relief 
　　was also provided in various other places.　　Famine throughout all provinces

First, Same 8th year, Hinoto-tori (1837), from around February: Around New Year, 105-106 monme
　　Extremely numerous deaths from starvation among outcasts《marginal note: holes were dug behind Fukawa temple and in Koyama valley for burial》
　　Also from around August, epidemic diseases spread with extremely numerous deaths. Accordingly, protective amulets were bestowed.
　　Around April, over 120 monme per hyō. From mid-May to early June,
　　from about 125 monme to about 127 monme
　　Millet 45 monme　Soybeans 75 monme　Red beans 110 monme
　　Glutinous rice 125 monme　　Buckwheat 70 monme
　　　Although sake brewing was prohibited, sake dealers secretly brewed in private, so punishment was ordered.
　　　The price, which had been 4 monme per shō, was fixed at 2 monme 8 bu.
　　November: 50 monme per hyō　Salt 4 monme 5 bu per hyō
　　Sake 1 monme 9 bu per shō　　Oil 7 monme per shō
　　　1 ryō of gold = 85 monme
