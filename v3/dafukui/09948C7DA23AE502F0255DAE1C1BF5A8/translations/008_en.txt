One: There may be townspeople within the domain who possess firearms for buying and selling purposes. There may also be firearms held as pawned items. For such pawned firearms, if they are to be returned to their owners with proper notice, both parties (the owner and the pawnbroker) should be registered. For other firearms being bought and sold, they should be left as they are, with detailed records kept in ledgers for each town, noting which firearms of what bullet weight are held by which individuals in each town. Most importantly, certificates should be received and kept according to written instructions. In the capital, these examinations should be conducted as instructed through the town magistrates.
