1. Regarding oath papers for the foot inspector role
　1. On the 29th day of the 7th month of Kyōhō 5 (1720), foot inspector Kawai Budayū was ordered to take a temporary role oath paper today at the council hall. Record keeper Nambu Jiemon was called out to handle the proceedings.
　　The aforementioned oath paper was entrusted to office clerk Harada Chōemon.
1. In the 8th month of Genroku 16 (1703), all officials were newly ordered to take oath papers.
1. On the 2nd day of the 3rd month of Kyōhō 10 (1725), Ōta Saburōbei was appointed as temporary road inspector on the 29th of last month, but since it was a purification day, the oath was postponed. Since he was to depart for Edo on the 5th, today he was invited to the residence of monthly duty officer Tsuchiya Jūrōemon to prepare the oath paper, with stamp witness Jūrōemon verifying it. This was naturally done after consulting with the senior councilors.
1. On the 10th day of the 6th month of the same 12th year, various checkpoint guard personnel were summoned to the accounting office and ordered by the magistrates to take oath papers. For replacement checkpoint guards at various locations, Koma Ichiemon's unit was dispatched, but due to unit shortages, the reserve guard unit was investigated. Since there were no available personnel, a record was kept of the communication to the advance monthly guard.
1. On the 5th day of the 11th month of the same 15th year, five Buddhist monks took oath papers at the side of the accounting office entrance. The head monk led them out, and since foot inspectors had previously attended such proceedings, Sakabe Matahachi attended.
1. From the 3rd month of the same 18th year, senior councilors' sons were appointed as council hall trainees, and oath papers were taken in the senior councilors' chambers. (Recorded in the castle town section of the accounting office records)
1. On the 8th day of the 9th month of Genbun 5 (1740), Ōtani Sukegata was appointed to senior councilor position, and his son Kyūma was ordered to attend council business days in the senior councilors' chambers. Therefore, he was ordered to take an oath paper, and it was announced that all colleagues should attend at that time.
1. On the 25th day of the 8th month of Kanpō 2 (1742), temple administration official Ōsaki Shichidayū and others, appointed on the 16th, were ordered to take oath papers at the council hall. However, since Mitsuoka Sukeemon was returning to the temple administration magistrate role, his previous oath paper was used. Colleague Sawaki Tokozaemon attended the oath ceremony only to hear the text and was told by Matsudaira Shume that he need not perform a new blood seal.
