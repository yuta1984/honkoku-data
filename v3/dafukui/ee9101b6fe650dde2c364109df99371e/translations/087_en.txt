On the 11th day of the 7th month of Bunka 3 (1806), when Murao Shinbei was appointed as ashigaru metsuke, although we contacted him to come to the Council Chamber (hyōjōsho), since the council session had ended, the appointment was announced at Saburōbei's residence with Nosaka Genemon in attendance.

1. On the 10th day of the 6th month of Bunka 7 (1810), regarding ashigaru Yoshikawa Kyūbei having official business, we contacted the ashigaru leader to have him come to the lord's quarters immediately, and with ashigaru metsuke Makino Kazaemon in attendance, we announced that the aforementioned Kyūbei was appointed as ashigaru metsuke.
Note: While it states that the monthly rotating magistrate and ashigaru leader should also be notified, such notification is unnecessary.

1. On the 5th day of the 7th month of Bunka 9 (1812), minor official kitchen inspector (odaidokoro metsuke) Mizuno Seibei and ashigaru Shimada Kurōzaemon were appointed to ashigaru metsuke positions as announced by Koma Taitō-kata, so this was announced at the Council Chamber with Nosaka Genemon in attendance.
Note: On the 13th of the same month, the appointment of these two men as ashigaru metsuke was reported in the corridor when the senior councilors were leaving.

1. At the end of the 5th month of Genbun 5 (1740), Matsudaira Shōbei's office informed us that ashigaru Aoyama Sen'suke was to be appointed as ashigaru metsuke, but since he was stationed in Edo, he should be summoned. We were told to consult with Akashi Shōgen about this matter. When consulted, Shōgen said that since there were few ashigaru stationed in Edo, a replacement should be sent before summoning him. We arranged that when a replacement could go with about 10 days' preparation, he would be summoned then.

1. On the 29th day of the 4th month of Kyōhō 16 (1731), there is a record in the miscellaneous affairs register that the ashigaru metsuke gathered and submitted a written report about investigating whether there was any disorder in society. There are no such records before or after this.
I surmise this may have been a previous practice. This is the first time seen in the official duty records. The monthly officer was Atsumi Shinemon.

1. When ashigaru metsuke serve diligently for over 20 years, they should be appointed as leaders (tōdori) and granted official stipends, and after about 25 years, they should be promoted to caretaker positions (rusuban).
I surmise that around the beginning of the Kōka era, domestic ashigaru metsuke entered the ōgōban after about 23 years, while residential ashigaru metsuke entered the ōgōban after about 24 years.

1. Nosaka Genemon entered the ōgōban in the 1st month of Bunsei 2 (1819) after 26 years as ashigaru metsuke, having become leader in the winter of his 21st year.
1. Residential ashigaru metsuke Yamagami Hachirōemon entered the ōgōban in the 1st month of Bunsei 12 (1829) after 24 years, having become leader in the spring of his 21st year.
[Marginal note] Residential Yasumura Kozaemon became leader in his 20th year and entered ōgōban in his 24th year.

1. On the 16th day of the 12th month of Kōka 3 (1846), ashigaru metsuke leader Takeshita Jōdayū entered ōgōban after 23 years, having become leader in the 10th month of the winter of his 20th year.
1. On the 18th day of the 1st month of the 4th year (1847), residential ashigaru metsuke leader Otobe Yūhachi entered ōgōban after 24 years, having become leader in the 9th month of the autumn of his 20th year.
Note: The above Yūhachi and Jōdayū had the same years of service, and moreover Yūhachi was senior to Jōdayū, but following recent precedent as described in the main text, Jōdayū was promoted the previous year and Yūhachi the following spring, resulting in Jōdayū becoming senior to Yūhachi.

1. For those who cannot manage within 20 years due to various circumstances, should they be given shinban status and transferred to other positions?
1. On the 20th day of the 11th month of Bunsei 12 (1829), Mizuno Seibei entered shinban in his 18th year and was transferred to arako-gashira, having become leader in the spring of his 17th year.
1. On the 29th day of the 1st month of Tenpō 4 (1833), Seki Yūemon entered shinban in his 20th year and was transferred to kobu-shin-kata.
Note: This was his second term as ashigaru metsuke, totaling 20 years. In Bunsei 11 (1828), in his 15th year, he was transferred to warehouse magistrate grain department, the following year to policy office, three years later dismissed for office misconduct, and the same year resumed ashigaru metsuke duties, returning to his original position.
1. In the 1st month of Tenpō 12 (1841), Tsuda Denshichi entered shinban in his 12th year as ashigaru metsuke and was relieved of ashigaru metsuke duties due to his devoted attention to family business.
1. In the 7th month of Tenpō 5 (1834), Toda Shichibei was dismissed from his position due to errors in regulatory duties but retained his stipend and was placed in minor official status, in his 19th year as ashigaru metsuke. The following year he was appointed to river works inspector, in Tenpō 8 (1837) 7th month resumed ashigaru metsuke duties returning to his original position, and in Tenpō 9 (1838) 7th month entered shinban as assistant inspector, totaling 21 years.
1. In the 4th month of Bunsei 9 (1826), Shimada Kurōzaemon was placed without position in minor official status after 15 years as ashigaru metsuke, resumed duties the same year returning to his original position, and after a total of 19 years was appointed as minor official kitchen inspector.
1. In the 11th month of Bunsei 12 (1829), Iwasa Heidayū became a minor official in his 14th year, serving as assistant to the palace keeper, then kitchen inspector, warehouse magistrate, etc. In the 8th month of Tenpō 5 (1834), while remaining a minor official, he resumed ashigaru metsuke duties, and in the 7th month of Tenpō 8 (1837) entered shinban as assistant to palace keeper, totaling 18 years as ashigaru metsuke.
