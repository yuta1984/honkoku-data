Artisans

1. From Meiwa 7, year of the Tiger (1770), gunsmith platform craftsman Takase Rinemon came under metsuke supervision. Details will be provided later.

1. Sword smiths: Yamashiro (Shimada Kichizaemon), Shimosaka (Buemon), and gunsmith Wakabayashi - these three men are under metsuke supervision.
   However, Wakabayashi Shōgorō was placed under metsuke supervision from the 20th day of the 6th month of Kan'en 3, year of the Horse (1750), and until then was under town magistrate supervision. Details will be provided later.

1. Stipend-receiving smiths: Yamashiro, Shimosaka, sword polisher Itō - these are under metsuke supervision.
   The above was announced to Niwa Hachizaemon and Nishio Denbei on the 16th day of the 8th month of Jōkyō 4, year of the Rabbit (1687).

1. On the 4th day of the 6th month of Kyōhō 15, year of the Dog (1730), Shimada Kuhachirō petitioned that his ancestors had worn swords for generations, but he was troubled by the recent prohibition on artisans wearing swords. He requested permission to wear a sword but was refused, as recorded.

1. In the 5th month of the same 20th year, year of the Rabbit (1735), Shimada Kuhachirō submitted a written request to send his daughter as an adopted daughter to a man named Shinzaemon in Tarōmaru village, which was under direct domain control. Since Tarōmaru village was under direct control, the written request was submitted to and reviewed by Matsudaira Shume's office, which responded that if it was a matter of marriage arrangement, it could be handled at their discretion.

1. On the 28th day of the 5th month of Kanpō 4, year of the Rat (1744), Shimada Shinemon's repeated improper conduct regarding official sword business was deemed extremely inappropriate, so strict house confinement was ordered. He was told to strictly observe restraint and remain in seclusion. Shimosaka Ichizaemon served as intermediary.
   However, records show that like samurai who refuse mediation, he was instructed to refuse repeatedly.

1. On the 11th day of the 10th month of Kan'en 1, year of the Dragon (1748), Shimosaka Ichizaemon died of illness, and on the 9th day of the intercalary 10th month of the same year, family headship was granted to his son Buemon.

1. In Enkyō 3, year of the Tiger (1746), Shimada Shinemon's son Shinbei was removed from heir status and ordered to live in a separate residence.

1. On the 19th day of the 9th month of Hōreki 2, year of the Monkey (1752), Shimada Shinemon died, and on the 13th day of the 11th month, his son Hikohachi was granted succession exactly as had been given to Shinemon, and his seating rank was also appointed the same as Shinemon's, as recorded.

1. When Yamashiro, Shimosaka, and their sons wish to make pilgrimages to Ise or take hot spring baths at places like Kaga Province's Yamanaka, they consult with the chief retainers.
