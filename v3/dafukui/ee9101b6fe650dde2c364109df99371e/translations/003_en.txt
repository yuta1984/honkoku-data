　Four Classes and Vagrants Section
　　　　　Examination for Official Positions
1. When there are vacant official positions, and the senior retainers (karo) instruct that qualified candidates should be examined from among the samurai and written reports submitted, the list of names should first be presented for inspection before being reported to the senior retainers.
　　　However, even though such written reports are inspected, this matter must be kept secret from the senior retainers.
1. When senior retainers instruct that examination documents should be submitted in advance of personnel changes, if there are suitable candidates for inspector positions among colleagues, oral recommendations are acceptable, but written recommendations should be avoided, as instructed by the senior retainers.
1. As proclaimed in the sixth year of Kyoho (1721), Year of the Ox, regarding examinations when official positions become vacant: until now, those who had been dismissed from positions once did not submit written reports, but henceforth, those dismissed from positions they were unsuited for should be included in examination documents, along with their approximate age. (Marginal note: When submitting examination documents for vacant positions on the 8th day of the 10th month of the Ox year, the age of such persons should be recorded. If not precisely known, it should be written as "approximately X years old" as instructed to Otani Gizaemon through Sakai Tange. According to Happyakuri's analysis, this is how it appears in the records of various matters)
1. Regarding treasury guard samurai: initially four were appointed from the ranks, later increased, then reduced to six, and in the sixth year of Meiwa (1769), Year of the Ox, two more were added, making a total of eight.
1. For positions attached to financial administration, selection matters are first assigned to the magistrate, and the documents submitted by the magistrate are passed from the senior retainers to the inspectors, who conduct detailed examinations before reporting.
