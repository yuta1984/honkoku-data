1. On the 6th day of the 3rd month of Kyōhō 16 (1731), Hiramoto Sakunoemon (side attendant head) and Hasebe Koemon (foot soldier head) were appointed as close attendant inspectors during their Edo duty assignment, as announced by the senior retainers. On the same 16th day, written oaths were required. While regular appointments are made in the presence of senior retainers, for temporary appointments, the inspectors verify the signature authentication.

1. On the 19th day of the 9th month of the same year, regarding the temporary supervision by Ugai Hachiemon for the succession of Deputy Niwa Yoichieemon's group, the magistrates communicated this and consulted with the senior retainers, who instructed that this should be conveyed to the magistrates.

1. On the 7th day of the 9th month of the same year, when Edo-assigned Inspector Kōzuki Buzaemon was sent back to the domain for Yōjuin-sama's private affairs, Side Attendant Head Hiramoto Sakunoemon was appointed as acting inspector. Written oaths were also required, with inspectors verifying the signature authentication.

1. On the 14th day of the 7th month of Kyōhō 17 (1732), due to hasty handling of official documents, three magistrates were ordered into house arrest. Among them, monthly duty magistrate Amemori Giemon was not to attend the lord's residence but could perform duties at the accounting office and senior retainers' residences. Consequently, Deposit Territory Magistrate Senbon Tōzaemon was appointed to additional duties for household financial matters.

1. On the 19th day of the 9th month of the same year, Matsuda Yaichieemon, formerly a secretary, was appointed to succeed Document Magistrate Ikoma Shōzō and was assigned to the page guard unit. However, on the 24th day, he reported that Chief Retainer Sasaji Gonemon had informed him that he was ordered to serve additionally in maintaining records that the lord had previously kept in his own handwriting.

1. On the 5th day of the 9th month of Kyōhō 18 (1733), during Town Magistrate Ōmiya Hikoemon's absence for hot spring treatment, the monthly duty inspector should handle his official duties.

1. On the 25th day of the 2nd month of Kyōhō 20 (1735), due to household financial difficulties, Kanazu Magistrate Suzuki Hikotayū, being skilled in his previous duties, was ordered to consult with other magistrates going forward.

1. On the 6th day of the 1st month of Temmei 3 (1783), while Front Wardrobe Keeper Yoshiki Tanai was under punishment and his colleague Kiuchi Ichirōbei was unable to serve due to illness from today, from the 7th day Accounting Investigation Officer Yokoyama Jūrōbei was appointed as temporary concurrent official to serve the duty rotation.

1. On the 16th day of the intercalary 7th month of Genbun 5 (1740), when Town Magistrate Tsuchiya Jūrōemon died of illness, Inspector Nishio Gentazaemon was appointed as acting magistrate. However, until the acting appointment was made, the gates were closed out of respect for superiors, and opened once the acting appointment was confirmed.
(In previous years when Kaifuku Ihei served as town magistrate and was under house arrest due to reprimand, the gates were not closed and the assistant group members attended the office daily. Later, during Ōmiya Hikoemon's absence for mountain hot spring treatment, the same procedure was followed, with duties performed under the acting inspector's direction. Therefore, this time too, the front gates were not closed. Detailed records exist.)

1. On the 22nd day of the 12th month of Kanpō 1 (1741), regarding who should be appointed as acting inspector for Reiganjima in Edo next year, following an internal communication from the advance guard head and inquiry to Edo, it was decided to appoint Eguchi Jirōbei, and this decision was conveyed to Jirōbei. Detailed records exist.

1. On the 5th day of the 3rd month of Kōka 2 (1845), Magistrate Yokota Sakudayū was dismissed and placed under house arrest, along with Ichimura Kanemon, Sasaki Kozaemon, and Okada Kinzaemon. Consequently, Deposit Territory Chief Administrator Akita Sangozaemon was appointed as acting concurrent magistrate.
However, only Kinzaemon was released from house arrest on the 11th day and ordered to remain in careful conduct except for official duties.

1. On the 16th day of the 9th month of Kōka 1 (1844), Unit Head Atsumi Shinemon maintained his position while being additionally appointed to handle family genealogy initial transcription duties. His monthly duty rotation service was suspended from the 29th day of the 11th month.
