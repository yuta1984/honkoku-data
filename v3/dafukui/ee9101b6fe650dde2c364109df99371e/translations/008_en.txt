However, regarding dates and names, the inner scribes (御内御右筆) would first record them, and individual written seals would also be prepared first at the treasury office (御勝手). Before the senior councilors (御家老中), only blood seals would be prepared, and real names need not be recorded. Also, at that time, all officials would attend that seat. Naturally, all inspectors (御目付) would be seated in attendance. Around the Kōka period.

Additionally, when there are those among the officials who are ordered to submit oath papers (誓紙), after completing the oath papers, they need not separately attend that seat. Regarding the inspectors, once the oath papers are completed, they would attend their own seats while still wearing their formal attire and be seated. Around the Kōka period.
