1. Shoe box carriers, umbrella bearers, and raincoat carriers need not remain constantly attached to their horses while at the lodgings. They should proceed to the entrance of the lodging in advance and attach themselves to their respective horses from the time of departure from the lodging. However, rainy weather is an exception.

1. In previous years, there were many complete sets of horse equipment and travel chests, causing severe congestion as we have heard. This time, it was previously announced that only a few spare riding horses should be brought and many are unnecessary, so this should be followed accordingly. Regarding horse equipment, it is not necessary to bring only spare sets, so it is thought that bringing just a few extra items and avoiding excess would be appropriate. If this is done, there should be no congestion, so this principle should be thoroughly understood. Furthermore, the aforementioned spare equipment and travel chests should not depart at will. Early morning departure is strictly forbidden.

1. In general, regarding grooms, saddle replacers, equipment attendants, and even the baggage of lower-ranking personnel, they should wait briefly after the three envoys and the Lord of Tsushima have completed their departure, then set out afterward to avoid congestion.

1. Hired personnel and horses at various lodgings should not be brought in the same manner as those of the three envoys and the Lord of Tsushima's party. They should be brought to the lodgings after the three envoys and the Lord of Tsushima have departed.

1. At travel lodgings and such, one should be mindful not to obstruct the envoys and the Lord of Tsushima.
