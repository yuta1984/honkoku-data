1. On the same day, the government foot-inspector (o-kachi-metsuke) went to the front gate of the residence and conveyed the following to the caretaker Ōtani Ichiemon Yoshitsugu regarding the matter of the Korean embassy's upcoming visit and castle ascent:

Following the precedent of the Kyōhō era, maintain strict security. At places with side streets, according to the status of the residence, deploy military officers and foot soldiers for strict security. Those of lesser rank should coordinate and deploy four to five foot soldiers for strict security. In general, ordinary water buckets need not be provided; stack water buckets in front of checkpoint stations and the like. Moreover, lanterns must not be displayed from the previous night. Hang blinds on windows of viewing areas, keep the front gates of residences open, and there is no need for locking devices. Where there are places that allow visibility through, hang available folding screens or curtains.

However, for those of lesser rank who find it difficult to provide personnel for their residences along this route, arrangements should be made for higher-ranking neighbors to provide three foot soldiers.
