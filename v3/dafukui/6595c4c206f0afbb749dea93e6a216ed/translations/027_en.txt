However, when the above was submitted, the next day on the 19th, the caretaker was summoned to the same place, and through a steward was told that the presentation should be made as inquired. In addition, regarding Lord Matsudaira Satsuma-no-kami and his heir Masunosuke-dono's visits to the Great Interior, although documents are customarily submitted with additions, this was not recorded.

Item: On April 23rd, for the Boys' Day celebration, through messenger and steward Daidōji Genkurō Shigekyo, one iris decoration, one box of dried sea bream, seven banners, and one type of delicacies were presented to Lord Giyoshi-maru.

Item: On the 25th of the same month, regarding the matter of escort horses for the Korean envoys' return journey, caretaker Tsuda Kuuemon brought and submitted the following written document to Lord Inaba Tango-no-kami Masamoto, who was in charge of this affair:

　　　　　　For the Korean envoys' return journey, riding horses are to be provided by Hyōbu-tayū's domain from Tōtōmi Arai to Yamashiro Province Yodo. Therefore, how many days after the Korean envoys arrive at this location should we instruct them to reach Arai? As this concerns a long journey, we humbly inquire about this matter in advance.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Retainer of Matsudaira Hyōbu-tayū
April 25th　　　　　　　　　　　Tsuda Kuuemon
