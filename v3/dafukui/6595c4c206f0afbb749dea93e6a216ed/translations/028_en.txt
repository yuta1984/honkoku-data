Separate document
Regarding the date when personnel and horses are to be dispatched to Arai for the Korean envoys' return journey, as soon as the orders are issued,
we will promptly communicate and dispatch them from our domain. We humbly request that the orders be issued approximately twenty days before the required dispatch date. As this concerns a distant province, we respectfully convey this internal discussion. Above.
　　　　　　　　　April 25th　　Tsuda Kuuemon

Item: On April 27th, a notice came from Lord Inaba Tango-no-kami Masamoto, who was in charge of the Korean envoys' visit.
When caretaker Tsuda Kuuemon went to the Ōte Accounting Office, Lord Itsumi Dewa-no-kami and Lord Horie Arashirō were present, and regarding the inquiry document of the 25th, the following written instruction was given:
　　　　　　Regarding the saddled horses to be provided from Tōtōmi Arai to Yamashiro Yodo for the Korean envoys' return journey, they should arrive at Arai within approximately ten days from the date the envoys arrive in Edo.
　　　　　　　　　April

Item: On the 28th of the same month: Arrival at the castle

Item: On the same day, as gratitude for the above, messenger Takochiba Hakubu Yukie Hirokane was dispatched to Edo. As customary, formal paper, dried sea bream, and sake barrels were presented. Yukie departed from Fukui today.
