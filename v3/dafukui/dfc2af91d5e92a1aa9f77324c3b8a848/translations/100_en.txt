○Regarding peasants and townspeople's commercial goods: traveling using borrowed travel permits and letters of introduction from imperial princes, court nobles, and other high-ranking households is not permitted. Also, merchants' goods should not be mixed with courier packages. This has been proclaimed repeatedly since the Shōtoku and Enkyō eras. Recently these rules have become lax, with people disguising commercial goods as belongings of military houses, imperial princes, court nobles, etc., or peasants and townspeople falsely claiming to be retainers of such households while traveling, and mixing commercial goods with courier packages. This is improper conduct. Henceforth, anyone engaging in such practices will be investigated and strictly punished, including those who lend out travel permits, advance notices, and transport fee ledgers.
