No violations should occur, and this should be communicated accordingly. Inspections should also be conducted on the highways, and if cargo weight is excessive or if the number of packages is arbitrarily increased [unclear portion], the matter should be promptly reported to the highway magistrate. After investigation, if there are suspicious matters, not only the cargo agents but also the contractors should be punished.

●1. When there are improper acts by personnel at highway stations, it has been heard that travelers sometimes summon station masters and elders from two or three days' journey away, or they accompany them for litigation purposes. Even if station personnel behave improperly, summoning station masters and elders reduces manpower at those stations and interferes with official business. Henceforth, summoning station masters and elders is unnecessary, and accompanying them for litigation should also be stopped. Such matters should be reported to the highway magistrate, who will investigate and issue strict orders from the magistrate's office.

Regarding the above articles, recently at highway stations, horses and porters have been required beyond regulations, and there have been other illegal acts by travelers. Not only the stations but even the auxiliary villages have petitioned that they are suffering from poverty. After detailed investigation, these orders are issued. Henceforth, the contents of this document must be strictly observed. Even if there are illegal acts by group supervisors and retainers, everything should conform to the intentions of their duty officers and masters.

Shōtoku 2, 3rd month of the Dragon year - The above was delivered to the caretakers at Lord Abe Bungo-no-kami's residence on the 10th day of the 3rd month. Sakai Tenzen instructed the inspectors to communicate this to the retainer band.

Although the above was communicated in Shōtoku 2, year of the Dragon, it has been heard that recently things have become lax. From now on, these principles should be observed even more strictly. If anyone violates them hereafter, they will be punished after investigation.

The above - Enyō 4, year of the Rabbit, 3rd month - This should be communicated as above.

Memorandum

●1. If travelers violate regulations and commit illegal acts, they should be corrected according to the proclamation. If they do not comply, the matter should be reported to the local lord's officials, who should explain this to the travelers. If they still object, they should be detained and the case reported to Edo.

●1. Matters concerning magistrate territories should be handled according to the same principles.

●1. It has been heard that townspeople's cargo tags are being given to make samurai cargo appear as merchant cargo. From now on, this is strictly prohibited.

●1. Recently there have been criminals at stations who extort fees from couriers and visit travelers' lodgings to extort drinking money. From now on, if such individuals are found, they should be arrested at the location. In government territory, report to the magistrate; in private domains, report promptly to the domain lord or local administrator.

●1. Regarding horse and porter fees, those with official seals and government business should pay. Stations should keep detailed daily records, and not only the station towns but also auxiliary villages should maintain accounts, which transport agents should always keep in mind.

●1. Regarding lodging and rest, whether for those who set dates well in advance or those who make last-minute arrangements, even light travelers should be accommodated without incident, which main inns and lodging houses should strictly understand.

●1. After this communication, if station personnel make improper demands to travelers, frequently charge fees unnecessarily, or detain travelers, strict measures will be taken.

The above - Enyō 4, year of the Rabbit, 3rd month - Communicated as above.
