○Regarding the prohibition issued in the intercalary fourth month of this year stopping peasants and townspeople from using gold and silver items: For items that peasants and townspeople have been possessing due to misunderstandings or other reasons up until now, there will be no particular punishment. These items should be promptly submitted to the Gold and Silver Exchange (kinginza). In return, the exchange will provide appropriate monetary compensation. All such items must be submitted without concealment. However, for those in locations distant from the Gold and Silver Exchange, items may be collected at the territorial magistrate's office (jitō yakuba) for submission to the exchange, or alternatively submitted to the nearest distant province magistrate's office (ongoku bugyōsho), local deputy official (daikan), or deputy territory office (oazukari-sho yakusho), from where they will be forwarded to the exchange and appropriate compensation will be provided. Understanding this directive, domain lords and territorial magistrates must ensure complete compliance throughout their territories and holdings without exception. This directive shall be promulgated as stated above. Sixth month
