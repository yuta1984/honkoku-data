**1. Shōtoku 5, Year of the Sheep (1715)**
Copy of the Book of Laws and Prohibitions

●〇 It has come to our attention that in recent years, a practice called "mikasatsuke" has been spreading throughout villages across the country. As this is equivalent to gambling, it shall be strictly prohibited in both shogunal demesne (goryō) and private domains (shiryō). In all matters, should anyone be found organizing such activities, the ringleaders and those who facilitated or hosted such events shall be apprehended, and the matter reported without delay.
　Ninth month, Year of the Sheep

Regarding the above: on the 21st day of the ninth month, at the office of Senior Inspector (Ō-metsuke) Yokota Bitchū-no-kami, the written decree was distributed to the domain's Edo representatives (rusui), with instructions to enforce the prohibition strictly throughout the domain's residences and territories.
　On the 2nd day of the tenth month of the same year, Honda Shuri directed the inspectors (metsuke) to disseminate this to all domain retainers as well as to towns and villages.

〇 The shogunate has issued a decree from Edo, the contents of which are set forth on an attached document. All parties are to understand its meaning and must not engage in mikasatsuke. This prohibition applies not only here in our domain but also when traveling elsewhere; such matters are to be strictly refrained from. Furthermore, as has long been prohibited by law, any entertainment resembling gambling is equally forbidden. The foregoing shall be strictly conveyed to those of lower station. The above.
　2nd day of the tenth month　To All　(Also to be forwarded to unit commanders)　Inspectors

**1. Kyōhō 4, Year of the Boar (1719)**

〇 Although the Mikasa gambling game has already been prohibited, there are reports that said gambling is being conducted in samurai residences as well as in temples and shrines. Such conduct is especially impermissible. Whereas until now only the townspeople's quarter (machiyata) had been subject to inspection, henceforth inspections shall extend to samurai residences and temple and shrine precincts alike; should any such violations be found, strict investigation shall be undertaken. This decree has been issued, and all parties are to take note and maintain firm prohibitions at all times.
The above shall be disseminated accordingly. The above.
　Seventh month, Year of the Boar

〇 The prohibition on Mikasa gambling recently issued by the shogunate is as described in the attached document. All parties are to understand its meaning; in particular, those traveling to Edo are to be fully cognizant of the matter. It goes without saying that gambling has long been prohibited by law, and any activities resembling gambling are likewise strictly forbidden. The above.
　12th day of the eighth month　To All　(Also forwarded to unit commanders)　Inspectors

**1. Kyōhō 3, Year of the Dog (1718)**

Memorandum on the Circulation of New Gold and Silver Coin from the Eleventh Month of the Current Year of the Dog

1. As the reminting of gold coins has been ordered and production has proceeded accordingly, as previously announced, the circulation of Kanji gold coins (kanjikin) shall cease at the end of the coming Year of the Boar (亥年). Henceforth, while the negotiation of prices for various goods by mutual agreement (aite) shall be a separate matter, for matters such as tribute payments, disbursements, stipends, loans, outstanding payments, and the like—all of which have been conducted according to previously established denominations—circulation shall proceed as stated below.

　　Addendum: Even where transactions were previously conducted in terms of a certain number of ryō in Kanjikin, from the eleventh month of the current Year of the Dog, transactions shall be denominated in the equivalent amount of new gold coin. During the period when Kanjikin remains in circulation, it may, at the discretion of the parties involved, be exchanged for new gold coin at the prescribed exchange rate.

1. Although gold has been reminted to full purity (shōmi) and no supplement was necessary, there is a shortage of raw silver (nama-gin), and therefore the reminting of silver using haifuki-gin (silver refined by the cupellation method) has been ordered, with circulation to proceed according to the prescribed quantities as stated below.

　　Addendum: Even where transactions were previously conducted in terms of a certain number of coins or monme in circulation silver, from the eleventh month of the current Year of the Dog, transactions shall be denominated in the equivalent amount of new silver coin. During the period when old circulation silver remains in circulation, it may, at the discretion of the parties involved, be exchanged for new silver coin at the prescribed exchange rate.

1. The exchange period for Kanjikin shall be limited to five years, from the current Year of the Dog through the coming Year of the Tiger. The exchange period for Genroku gold coins shall be limited to the coming Year of the Tiger.

　　　　　Rules for Exchanging New Gold and Silver Coin

The exchange of Kanjikin and Genroku gold coins for new gold coin shall proceed without change as before.
