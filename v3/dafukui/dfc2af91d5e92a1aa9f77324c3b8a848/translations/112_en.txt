Tenth month

The above should be communicated to all those with domains of 10,000 koku or more.

○ Regarding the moat between Ichigaya Gate and Ushigome Gate, it is reported that in recent years there have been people fishing there indiscriminately. There are even posted notices prohibiting the taking of life, yet this is most improper behavior. Hereafter, anyone found fishing in these locations will be strictly punished.
As stated above, although notices were issued during the Kyōhō era, it is reported that people are still fishing indiscriminately in the said moat in recent years, which is improper. Henceforth, the regulations issued during the Kyōhō era must be strictly observed, and the taking of life is firmly prohibited. Anyone who violates this will be strictly punished. Tenth month

○ Regarding the establishment of gold and silver exchange offices until the tenth month of the coming year of the Tiger.
Item: The recast gold and silver of the Bunsei era shall have their circulation suspended immediately as announced in the previous year of the Rat. The grass-script two-bu coins and recast two-shu silver will also have their circulation suspended before long, so exchanges should be carried out diligently.
Tenth month

○ Item: Expensive confections and dishes that require unnecessary labor shall henceforth be prohibited. Even those previously prepared should be discontinued.

Item: Some Noh costumes appear extremely elaborate, so henceforth simpler items should be used.

Item: For hamayumi (ceremonial bows), iris decorations, armor, swords, boxes, boards and similar items, the use of gold, silver, metal, and foil is prohibited.

Item: Hina dolls and toy dolls exceeding eight sun in size shall be prohibited. For those below this size, crude gold containers and simple costume accessories are acceptable.

Item: For hina doll accessories, both nashiji lacquer and maki-e are prohibited except for family crests.

Item: The buying and selling of expensive potted plants is prohibited.

Item: The use of gold and silver on tobacco pipes and similar recreational items is prohibited, as well as elaborate carving, inlay work, and maki-e decoration.

Item: Elaborate woven and sewn clothing for women shall be prohibited. Even with gold thread embroidery, the limit shall be 300 momme of silver for one kosode outer garment, and 150 momme of silver for one dyed pattern kosode. Items more expensive than this shall not be bought or sold. The same applies to summer garments (katabira).

Item: Townspeople shall uniformly avoid ostentation. From now on, if any townspeople, male or female, wear elaborate items beyond their station, or use elaborate hair ornaments, members of their neighborhood group who notice this shall investigate their residence and name, have town officials accompany them, and immediately bring them to the magistrate's office for interrogation. This should be understood accordingly.

Item: For combs, kōgai (hair ornaments), and hairpins, gold is naturally prohibited, and elaborate tortoiseshell work is also prohibited. Combs shall be limited to 100 momme of silver, and kōgai and hairpins shall be made inexpensively according to the same standard.
