As stated above, although notices have been issued repeatedly from previous years, it appears that in recent years there are some who have failed to make the required reports, which is improper. Henceforth, the previous regulations must be strictly observed. When ronin (masterless samurai) take up residence, they must submit documentation. For areas under the jurisdiction of deputy administrators (daikan), as well as samurai residences, temples and shrines, when housing ronin, documentation must be obtained and submitted to the responsible inspector (metsuke). This directive should be communicated to all relevant parties.
Fifth month

○ That the honorable lady of Lord Arikimi shall be addressed as Princess (Himegimi-sama). Twenty-eighth day of the fifth month

○ Now that strict economy measures have been ordered, it is desired that not only the shogunate but also all daimyo reduce expenses in all matters so that each may serve with peace of mind. Although we speak of service, matters relating to military affairs are originally each person's responsibility, so even if unexpected expenditures occur, there is nothing particular to report. However, it is desired that wasteful expenses for ordinary service and tribute goods be reduced as much as possible. Regarding tribute goods, although orders have been repeatedly issued during the Kyōhō and Kansei eras to make them simpler, prices for the same items fluctuate according to circumstances. Therefore, if it would be more convenient to replace items previously procured in Edo with local products, or to replace local products with items procured in Edo, such requests may be made without hesitation. Matters that do not interfere with official duties may be approved as requested. Furthermore, regarding annual regular and special celebratory occasions, when presenting fresh sea bream during hot weather or consecutive days of wind and rain, procurement is difficult and costly, causing much concern. During hot seasons especially, there would be even greater concern, but since time passes after presentation, the items may not serve their purpose well. In any case, this represents wasteful expenditure for the various houses. Therefore, following the precedent of sake barrel fees and mackerel fees, henceforth for fresh sea bream presentations: domains of 100,000 koku or more shall pay 2,000 mon in gold, those of 50,000 koku or more shall pay 1,000 mon, and those below 50,000 koku shall pay 500 mon as monetary payment.
Ninth month

○ Regarding Princess Yasu, since she has been taken into the castle, it is ordered that henceforth she shall be treated the same as princesses were before the relocation. This should be communicated to all parties for their information. Ninth month

○ Regarding sake brewing in the provinces: although an order was issued in the previous year of the Rat to reduce production by half, since this year of the Ox appears to have good harvests throughout the provinces, until further notice, sake production should use two-thirds of the rice amount used until the previous year of the Snake, reducing it by one-third. Naturally, supervision should be conducted as before to ensure there is no hidden or excessive brewing, and should be carried out even more strictly. If hidden or excessive brewing occurs, not only the perpetrator but also the local officials will be investigated and severely punished. There must be no misunderstanding of these rules.
These directives should be promptly communicated without fail to shogunal lands, private domains, and temple/shrine lands.
Ninth month

○ Although poor harvests continued in recent years, crops have been mostly good for the past two or three years, so preparations for years of famine and emergency may naturally become neglected. Since rice reserves have been ordered even for shogunal territories, private domains should also set aside rice reserves at a rate of 100 koku of hulled rice per 10,000 koku of assessed income for five years from this year of the Ox until the year of the Snake. While all should practice frugality and may have useful preparations in their territories, this corresponds to preparations for the realm and brings peace of mind to His Lordship. Regarding the grain reserves ordered with the generous intent of the Kansei period, for unavoidable expenditures where replenishment has not yet been completed, replenishment should be made as much as possible in this year of the Ox...
