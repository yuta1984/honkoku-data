Regarding the above matters, although edicts were issued during the Kyōhō and Kansei eras and thereafter, over the years society has become increasingly extravagant. People of all ranks compete in splendor without understanding their proper station, and moreover, even when outward appearances seem modest, there are those who indiscriminately buy and sell expensive items in reality. Even when there are no recreational items involved, treating the repeatedly issued edicts as temporary matters has ultimately led to negligence and violation of prohibitions, which is most improper. However, past matters shall be specially pardoned without previous punitive measures being taken, for which we are grateful. Now that edicts have been issued with profound imperial intent to reform customs, this must not be taken lightly. Since there are likely items already prepared, strict prohibition shall take effect from the coming year of the Tiger. When there are those who violate the intent of these public edicts, officials shall be sent around to investigate thoroughly, and strict punishment shall be imposed without exception. However, those who conduct suspicious inspections or arrest and examine people traveling on the roads shall absolutely not exist. If such persons are found, they shall be detained and reported immediately. From now on, if there are those among the warrior class who order extravagant and expensive items, they must consult with the magistrate's office and follow their instructions.

As stated above, this shall be announced to all towns. Understanding this intent, elaborate and expensive items shall not be ordered in general. The profound intent of this time should be thoroughly understood, and all extravagant matters should be avoided.
Tenth month

▲ In rural areas, during Shinto festivals and ceremonial occasions, or events named as crop pest-sending ceremonies, wind festivals and the like, theatrical and sideshow-like events are held, costumes and equipment are prepared, spectators are gathered, and gold and silver are spent, which we hear is improper conduct.

Those who make a living from such gatherings are of course [prohibited], but others including ill-behaved traveling lodgers and riverbank people must absolutely not enter villages. Learning frivolous and indolent bad practices, naturally neglecting cultivation, leads to much wasteland and poverty, ultimately becoming the foundation for population dispersal. Therefore, one should be mindful to understand these circumstances well.

Henceforth, performing arts, shadow plays, dancing girls, jōruri, dancing and similar activities - all gatherings like theatrical performances - shall be strictly prohibited. Even after this announcement, if such activities do not cease, strict punishment shall be imposed without exception.

As stated above, this was announced in Kansei 11, year of the Sheep, but recently we hear that such activities have become rampant, which is improper. Hereafter, the intent of the edict must be strictly observed, no crowd-gathering activities shall be conducted, indolent customs shall be reformed, and cultivation should be the primary concern. If there are those who violate this, after investigation, strict punishment shall be imposed.

Regarding the above, for imperial lands the deputy officials (daikan) and local magistrates, for entrusted lands and private domains the lords, land stewards, and temple and shrine lands shall all announce this without exception and conduct investigations without negligence. For small stipend areas, the nearest deputy officials should also pay constant attention.
Eleventh month

○ Recently, many people have been making head coverings that conceal their faces and wearing them while traveling, which causes confusion with wanted persons sought by the magistrate's office. Except for the traditional round and square head coverings that have existed previously, no other head coverings shall be worn. Although this was announced in previous years, we recently hear that there are those wearing unusual head coverings that conceal their faces, which is improper conduct that should not occur.
