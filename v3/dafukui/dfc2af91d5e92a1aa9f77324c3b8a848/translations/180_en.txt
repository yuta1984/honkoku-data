1. Hōei 5, Year of the Rat (1708)
　　Official document transmitted by Lord Inoue Kawachi-no-kami

　　　　Memorandum

In recent years, there have been increasing expenditures required by the shogunate. Furthermore, following last winter's accumulation of sand in villages across the three provinces of Musashi, Sagami, and Kazusa, a special domain-assessment tax (takayakukin) has been levied throughout the country as part of a relief effort. From all territories—both shogunal demesne (goryō) and private domains (shiryō)—a sum of two ryō of gold per one hundred koku of assessed yield is to be collected from villages and remitted to the authorities.

As the distances to various domains differ and collection from villages will naturally take time, those with holdings of 10,000 koku or more shall advance the amount themselves, with a deadline of the third month of this year for payment into the shogunal treasury (o-kinzō) in Edo. Those with holdings below 10,000 koku shall submit payment by the sixth month.

For those with a group leader (kumigashira), payment shall be made by the group leader, accompanied by a receipt ledger for each group. Those without a group leader shall notify the relevant authorities in advance of their payment.

Fractional assessed yields of fifty koku or less shall be exempt from the tax.

Temple and shrine lands are excluded. The above is hereby decreed.

　　　　Intercalary First Month

Intercalary First Month, 14th Day:
Copies of the above were distributed one each at the audience hall (o-zashiki) to the district magistrates (kōri bugyō) Muso Gonzaemon and Suō Inozaemon, with instructions to notify the villages, and further to consult with the magistrates and urgently complete collection by the middle of next month.

On the following day, the 15th, the same instructions were relayed to Kaifuku Sazaemon, acting Kanazu magistrate (Kanazu bugyō kari-yaku), and the document was handed over.

Naruta Kakubei, a retainer of Honda Mago-tarō, was summoned. As Mago-tarō was at the time residing in Fuchū, instructions were sent by messenger, and the document was handed over. Note: for Tamuramura, one of Mago-tarō's enfeoffed villages, the collection was to be handled by Mago-tarō's side, while the remaining portion [obscured character] was to be collected by the district magistrates.
