▲○ Regarding sake brewing rice in various provinces: Up to the quantities established in Genroku 10 (1697), new sake and cold brewing could be conducted freely, and for suspended breweries wishing to brew sake, they could report to local magistrates (or governors for direct shogunal lands, or local lords for private domains) and brew at their discretion, as proclaimed in Hōreki 4 (1754). However, in recent years there have been no years of low grain prices, and this year too rice prices are high, causing hardship for common people. Therefore, until grain prices fall and further notice is given, all provinces should cease brewing half of their previous sake brewing rice quota, brew with the remaining half, and suspended brewing licenses should remain inactive. Any secret brewing will result in strict punishment not only for the violators but also for local officials. To avoid misunderstanding, magistrates and governors of direct shogunal lands, and lords and local administrators of private domains should investigate previous brewing quotas individually and enforce half-production quotas.
This should be communicated to all domains and fiefs, both large and small, in rural and urban areas. September of the Horse year
