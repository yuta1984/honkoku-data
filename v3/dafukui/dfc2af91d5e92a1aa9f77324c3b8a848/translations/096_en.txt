Copy of a notice from Magistrate of Finance (Kanjō-bugyō) Yabe Suruga-no-kami

Proclamation

○ Regarding the reduction of sake brewing in various provinces, it was previously announced that production should be reduced to one-third. However, following increasingly strict orders, the five provinces of Mikawa, Tōtōmi, Kai, Shinano, and Mutsu within the shogunal domains (goryōsho) are ordered to completely cease sake brewing, while other provinces should reduce production to one-quarter. This has been communicated to the intendants (daikan) and administrators of entrusted territories. In accordance with previous edicts, ensure that this directive is thoroughly communicated to the villages in your domains.
This notice is issued after consultation with Ōkubo Kaga-no-kami. Eleventh month

Copy of an edict from the Senior Inspectors (Ōmetsuke)
○ In recent years, there have been crop failures in various regions, resulting in insufficient rice shipments to Edo. Rice and grain prices have become extremely high, causing great hardship to the common people. As the shogunate is providing relief measures and other policies to improve grain distribution will be announced soon, all daimyo households should arrange to have their stipend rice (fuchigome) sent from their respective domains and fiefs. However, areas with complete crop failure or locations where transportation is difficult are exceptions. For other areas, refrain as much as possible from purchasing in Edo and make arrangements accordingly. Furthermore, set aside the amount equivalent to the extra stipend rice sent, and refrain from selling the remainder in the city markets.
This directive applies to all those with domains and fiefs, whether above or below ten thousand koku. Eleventh month. Instructions to magistrates
