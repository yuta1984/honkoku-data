To the Chief Inspector

▲○ Regarding the matter announced last winter that calling oneself "wholesale guild associations" and such should be discontinued: while wholesale business alone may be conducted at one's discretion, since they still use the title "wholesaler," they seem to think that even the guilds need not be dissolved. We hear that this causes interference with those in the same trade who sell at lower prices or amateurs who purchase goods for resale. Since the large monetary tribute offerings have also been exempted, they should gratefully observe [the regulations], but the absence of such observance is extremely improper. Therefore, hereafter, not only guild associations but also calling oneself "wholesaler" is strictly prohibited. Rice merchants should only call themselves "rice shops," charcoal merchants "charcoal shops," and oil merchants "oil shops." In business practices, they should not only wholesale to middlemen but should primarily engage in retail, and during times of commodity shortage, even if wholesaling is suspended, retail should continue without hindrance. Furthermore, they must not collude with middlemen to set high prices from wholesale to retail. If there are those who do not follow these instructions and claim that business cannot function without guilds, or who secretly collude to submit petitions, they will be subjected to strict investigation without delay and punishment will be imposed.

1. Although it was announced that using titles such as stock certificates, wholesale associations, guilds, and such is not permitted, we hear that some may have misunderstood this to apply only outside the Ten Guilds, which is improper. As previously announced, understand that even outside the Ten Guilds, stock certificates, wholesale associations, guilds, and such are absolutely not permitted. Since all previous tribute obligations such as free deliveries, unpaid labor, river dredging, and emergency services are hereby exempted, each should conduct business properly. Even if more people enter the same trade, they must never interfere. Regarding deliveries and labor services that have been customary for government use, these will be investigated by the appropriate authorities and announced later.

1. While it is acceptable to purchase goods for oneself and sell them gradually, sending advance payments to other provinces to monopolize purchases, postponing shipments, and hoarding goods locally constitutes market manipulation and is improper. Hereafter, such practices are prohibited. If word comes from outside that such practices continue, severe punishment will be imposed.

1. Since bathhouse and hairdressing businesses are not affected by general price regulations, the matter of discontinuing guild associations was not addressed for them. However, we hear that when some in these trades lower their fees, guild members claim this causes interference, which is improper. Therefore, hereafter, those in these trades are also prohibited from using stock certificates and calling themselves guild associations. No matter how many shops of the same trade open in a neighborhood or elsewhere, they must never interfere. This matter should be announced to all neighborhoods without omission.
Third month

Since the above was communicated to the town magistrates, it should be announced to all relevant parties.
However, from your office, this should be sent to Kanazu town, the Three Districts under direct administration, Fuchū, and other places.

○ Since we recently hear that there are those conducting gambling in samurai residences, we previously communicated that strict enforcement should be implemented in accordance with what was announced during the Kansei era. While it is of course necessary for each to impose strict prohibition on their servants and enforce this severely, if there is any negligence, we have ordered that when town magistrates and fire and theft investigation teams make their rounds, they should raid residences and make arrests. Understanding this, it should be communicated to all relevant parties.
