【Section previously hidden by attached paper】
○When a mother absconds and her whereabouts become unknown, if her child remains as a "heya-zumi" (heir-in-waiting who has not yet inherited), even if the child is young and does not understand the circumstances, it would be difficult for them to succeed to the family headship. It would also be difficult to send such a child as an adopted heir to another family. This was communicated in the fourth year of Enkyō, year of the Rabbit (1747). However, henceforth, even if a mother absconds, there shall be no impediment to her child's succession to family headship or being sent as an adopted heir to another family. This directive shall be communicated to all relevant parties. Eleventh month.

【Attached paper】
▲○Previously, vermillion ink has been sold from the vermillion guild (shuza) in the same manner as vermillion. However, in recent years, reports have come that inferior vermillion ink is being manufactured and sold in various quarters, which is improper. Those who conduct vermillion ink business shall purchase from the vermillion guild and conduct business as before. Henceforth, if there are those who manufacture inferior vermillion ink, they shall be strictly punished. This was proclaimed in the nineteenth year of Kyōhō, year of the Tiger (1734). However, perhaps having forgotten this directive, reports have come that inferior vermillion ink is being purchased from various sources for commercial purposes, which is improper. All the more so, vermillion and vermillion ink sold from the vermillion guild shall be purchased for business as before. Henceforth, those found to be purchasing from various other sources shall be strictly punished after investigation. This shall be proclaimed accordingly. Twelfth month of the year of the Rabbit.
