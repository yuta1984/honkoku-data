[Continuation of the attached note from Frame 150]

Item: In recent years, it has come to our attention that when official duties are assigned in Edo, hired persons are being added to the retinue of servants to fulfill said duties. Henceforth, this practice is strictly prohibited. Furthermore, now that orders have been issued regarding the number of attendants, the prescribed number must without fail be brought along. Even if there should be a slight surplus in the number of people available, they shall not be presented. That said, duties shall not be assigned in places that are unsuitable. In the event that attendants are urgently required, it goes without saying that people shall be summoned from within the domain, and military service obligations shall be strictly fulfilled as prescribed.

**Required numbers by domain size:**

- **200,000 koku and above:**
Mounted warriors: 15 to 20 horsemen (including oneself)
Foot soldiers (ashigaru): 120–130 men
Porters and attendants (chūgen/ninsoku): 250 to 300 men

- **100,000 koku:**
Mounted warriors: 10 horsemen
Foot soldiers: 80 men
Porters and attendants: 140–150 men

- **50,000 koku:**
Mounted warriors: 7 horsemen
Foot soldiers: 60 men
Porters and attendants: 100 men

- **10,000 koku:**
Mounted warriors: 3–4 horsemen
Foot soldiers: 20 men
Porters and attendants: 30 men

Item: For places where duties have until now been fulfilled with a smaller number of people, that arrangement shall remain as it is.

Item: For those with domain revenues below 200,000 koku whose holdings fall between the figures listed above, they shall understand and conduct themselves in accordance with the above prescribed standards on a proportional basis.
