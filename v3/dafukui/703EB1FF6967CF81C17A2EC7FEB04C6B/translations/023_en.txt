When funds are granted by the government for official use as substitute money or silver, any type of gold or silver should be employed according to the established rates.
In society, the laws governing circulation among all social classes should all conform to this example.

1. Regarding large gold coins (ōban): Even those reminted during the Genroku years, when compared to Keichō-era large gold coins, have not significantly declined in quality, nor do they break or become damaged. Therefore, when the currently circulating gold was decreed in Hōei 7, this matter was not included in that directive. In the present case as well, there should be a directive after the koban and ichibuban coins are reformed. During the interim, whether granted by the government, offered as tribute, or used privately, all should continue to use the large gold coins that have been in use until now.

1. Regarding silver offered as tribute to the government and silver granted by the government: This should also use the current silver calculation system. Whether using silver that has been in use until now, or using the new silver decreed this time according to the established rates, there should be no distinction between new and old in circulation.
