The offerings to the Hōō (Retired Emperor) Palace and the Nyōin (Imperial Lady) Palace were presented at the武家御式台 (samurai reception area), accompanied by the assistants (雑掌) of both Imperial Transmitters (伝奏), received and accepted, and the matter was concluded. The offerings to the Naishi-dokoro (内侍所, Imperial Regalia Hall) were also duly presented.

　　　　　　Note: Uemon (Matsubara Yoshitaka) departed Kyoto on the first day of the second month and arrived in Fukuei on the fifth day of the same month.

　　One entry: On the fourth day of the second month, upon a summons from Senior Councilor (老中) Inoue Kōzuke-no-kami (Masamine), the resident deputy (留守居) Murakami Sandayū (Masamine) appeared. He was questioned via a written document regarding the matter of the offering of incense money (香奠) at the fiftieth memorial service of Tōshō-gū (the deified Tokugawa Ieyasu) in the fifth year of Kanbun (1665), the Year of the Snake, as follows:

　　　　　　　　Kanbun 5, Year of the Snake
　　　　　　　　17th day of the 4th month

　　　　　　Regarding the memorial Buddhist service for the fiftieth anniversary of Gongen-sama's death:

　　　　　　　　[Presented] a sword with an inventory list — Matsudaira Echizen-no-kami

　　　　　　At that time, due to mourning restrictions (服忌), a messenger was not sent to Nikkō. After the mourning period ended, was the sword and inventory list offered at the head temple of Tōeizan (東叡山, Kan'ei-ji in Ueno)? Please submit a written account of the circumstances at that time.
