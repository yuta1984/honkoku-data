Therefore, from now on, when exchanging these two types of gold at the exchange offices, the supplementary gold (step-gold) system shall continue to follow the same precedent as before.

1. Keichō old silver shall have a ten-wari increase over currently circulating silver. For the aforementioned Keichō old silver (commonly called "ancient silver" in society), one kan-monme shall be exchanged for two kan-me of currently circulating silver. The new silver ordered this time has the same quality as this old silver, therefore its rate increase is also the same.

　　Note: Currently circulating silver, roughly the varieties produced since Hōei 7 (commonly called "middle silver," "sanpō," "shihō," etc. in society), shall be used uniformly without distinction.

1. Genroku silver shall have a six-wari increase over currently circulating silver. For the aforementioned Genroku silver (commonly called "gen-character silver" in society), one kan-me shall be exchanged for one kan six hundred me of currently circulating silver.

1. Early Hōei silver shall have a three-wari increase over currently circulating silver. For the aforementioned early Hōei silver (commonly called "hō-character silver" in society), one kan-me shall be exchanged for one kan three hundred me of currently circulating silver.
