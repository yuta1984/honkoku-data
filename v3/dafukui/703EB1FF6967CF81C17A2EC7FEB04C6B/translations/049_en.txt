[Continued from previous page] ...in the presence of assembled lords, he was notified of his appointment as Junior Captain of the Left Imperial Guard (Sakonoe-shōshō). Thereafter, as an expression of gratitude, he made courtesy rounds to the Senior Councilors (Rōjū), the Sobayōnin (Senior Chamberlains), and the Wakadoshiyori (Junior Councilors), while dispatching messengers via the御使番 (messenger officials) to the Sobashū (Chamberlains).

One entry: On the same day, at the summons of Senior Councilor Abe Bungo-no-kami (Masataka), the domain's Rusuiyaku (caretaker official) Ōzeki Shingorōemon (Naomi) attended, whereupon he was handed a written notice stating that the previously communicated matter of the name change could be carried out at his lordship's convenience.

One entry: On the same day, in response to last year (the Year of the Snake) having seen high rice prices, 300 bales of rice had been lent to the townspeople of the castle town upon their petition, and repayment had been completed by last month. However, since this year also saw high prices for rice and various goods, with many people in hardship, a petition was submitted requesting a loan of 600 bales of rice. After deliberation, 300 bales were granted as relief rice (kōgyōmai), and an additional 200 bales were provided as a loan.

One entry: On the 19th day of the 12th month, his lordship changed his name to "Iyo-no-kami-sama," and in the御座之間 (audience chamber), Honda Shuri (Tsunehisa) and Ogino Magouemon (Masamuku) were invited to share a celebratory meal of red rice (sekihan) and soup, and were presented with cups of sake.

One entry: On the same day, his lordship paid his respects to the duty Senior Councilor Matsudaira Kii-no-kami (Nobuyasu) and to Inoue Kawachi-no-kami (Masamine), conveying his gratitude for the official appointment and announcing his name change. He also paid a visit to Abe Bungo-no-kami (Masataka) to announce the name change. To the remaining Senior Councilors, Sobayōnin, and Wakadoshiyori, he dispatched messengers through the Rusuiyaku and messenger officials.

One entry: On the same day, in connection with the above, a letter was dispatched to the Kyoto Shoshidai (Kyoto Deputy), Mizuno Izumi-no-kami (Tadamoto).
