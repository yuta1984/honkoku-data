Shōtoku 4, Year of the Horse (1714), Fifth Month, Fifteenth Day
[End mark]

Regarding the matter of returning the quality of gold and silver to the standards of the Keichō era as has now been decreed: This follows the directive issued on the 11th day of the 10th month of the year of the Dragon two years ago during the previous reign, and since it was ordered for the sake of the realm and future generations, matters such as the government's expenses are not worth discussing. However, since the gold and silver produced from mines throughout the various provinces in recent times is not as it was in the past, it will take many years to completely reform all gold and silver from the Genroku era onward. Therefore, the articles defining the laws for gold and silver circulation until this transition is completed are as follows:

Article 1: The new gold and silver now decreed, as well as the old gold and old silver from the Keichō era through Genroku 7, not to mention all Genroku and Hōei gold and silver, shall be put into circulation. However, regarding Genroku and Hōei gold and silver, while the government's official rate sets one ryō of gold as equivalent to sixty monme of silver following the Keichō standard, in private transactions additional premiums (step-gold, step-silver) have been added in circulation...
