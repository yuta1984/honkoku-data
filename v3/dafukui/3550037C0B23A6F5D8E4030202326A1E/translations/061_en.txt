These provisions are the established laws of the western provinces.

1. When going to inspect ships in an emergency, one should prepare guns of about one hundred monme, include small guns, and bring grappling hooks, ropes, etc. Spears should have their sheaths removed when proceeding. Having many people on one boat is inadvisable. Large cannons are also inadvisable. It is essential to bring sailors and assistants.

1. There are official orders to fire warning shots with loaded cannons. In such cases, shots should be fired to the left and right of the ship to observe the situation.

1. When warships gradually approach the shore with the intention of landing, signals should be established so that people from nearby coastal areas can raise their voices and rush to gather. This has been ordered as the established law of the western provinces.

1. There is an established law to arrest Japanese ships that follow foreign ships and guide them through waterways. If such persons are found and arrested, it is considered loyal service. Those who act as intermediaries should especially be arrested.

1. It is essential to strictly prevent foreigners from landing on shore from their ships by any means. If they do land, it will lead to even more difficult situations throughout.
