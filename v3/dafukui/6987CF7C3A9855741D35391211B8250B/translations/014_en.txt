One: On the 12th day of the first month, since the smallpox had been cured, there was a celebratory ceremony with sake-yu (sake bath).

One: On the 15th day of the same month, since there were no complications after the smallpox, attendance at the castle was declined.

One: On the 25th day of the second month:
The Emperor contracted smallpox, so messenger Ichihashi Magoheiji was dispatched to Kyoto to inquire about His Majesty's condition.
Note: The date of Magoheiji's arrival in Kyoto is unknown.

One: On the 6th day of the third month:
It was announced that the Emperor's smallpox had been cured and that sake-yu had been performed on the first day of last month, and attendance at the castle was commanded for congratulations. However, due to illness, an inquiry was made through the castle caretaker to the duty officer Lord Kuze Yamato-no-kami Hiroyuki, who instructed that a messenger should be sent as a representative.

One: On the same day, regarding the above matter, messenger Saito Minbu Sadayuki was dispatched to the castle for congratulations. After the formal audience of various daimyo, he had an audience with duty officer Lord Kuze Yamato-no-kami Hiroyuki in the Hinoki-no-ma (Cypress Room).

One: On the 19th day of the same month:
For congratulations that the Emperor's smallpox had been cured and sake-yu had been performed, a ceremonial sword and horse substitute (thirty pieces of silver) was presented to the Imperial Palace through Kyoto caretaker Matsue Zenbei Fusayuki.
