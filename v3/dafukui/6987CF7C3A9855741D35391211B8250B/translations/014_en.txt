- January 12th: Since the smallpox had been cured, a sake-yu (recovery celebration) was held.
- January 15th: As there were no complications after the smallpox, attendance at the castle was cancelled.
- February 25th: 
  Because His Majesty the Emperor had smallpox, messenger Ichihashi Magoheiji was dispatched to Kyoto to inquire about his condition.
  Note: The date of Magoheiji's arrival in Kyoto is unknown.
- March 6th:
  His Majesty the Emperor's smallpox was cured, and orders were issued to attend the castle to celebrate the sake-yu ceremony held on the first day of last month. However, due to illness, an inquiry was made through the deputy to the duty officer Kuze Yamato-no-kami Hiroyuki, who instructed that a messenger should be sent as a representative.
- Same day: Regarding the above matter, messenger Saito Minbu Sadayuki was dispatched to the castle for congratulations. After the audience with various daimyo, he had an audience with duty officer Kuze Yamato-no-kami Hiroyuki in the Hinoki-no-ma (Cypress Room).
- March 19th:
  To celebrate His Majesty the Emperor's recovery from smallpox and the sake-yu ceremony, a tachi-umashiro (ceremonial sword and horse substitute - 30 pieces of silver) was presented to the Imperial Palace through messenger and Kyoto deputy Matsue Zenbei Fusayuki.
