The plan was to hold a drinking party using the gifted larks as soup, but as the meal was declined, this did not take place. After Chuemon's departure, [the lord] went to the castle to express gratitude, and afterwards dispatched a guard captain as messenger to Senior Councilor Sakai Kawachi-no-kami (Tadaaki) who was making his rounds, as well as to the junior councilors.

One: July 21st　Lady Kiyohime moved from the Torigoe residence to the Reiganjima residence, and preparations for the wedding were completed in the inner quarters.

One: Same month, 22nd　For the same reason, as celebration for the third day [after the wedding], 580 rice cakes were presented to Lady Manhime [daughter of Tadamasa] through messenger Saito Hanzaemon. Lady Manhime also presented the same through her messenger Fujita Kazaemon (Nobukatsu).

One: Same day　For the same reason, Lord Tsunamasa and Lady Kiyohime entered Lady Manhime's residence and presented ten pieces of gold and three kinds in two loads to Lady Manhime. Lady Kiyohime presented one hundred bundles of cotton and two kinds in one load.

One: August 1st　For celebration, [the lord] went to the castle and presented a ceremonial sword and horse substitute.

One: Same month, 3rd　As there was a third anniversary memorial service for Lord Kōganin at Tōeizan, five pieces of silver were offered as incense money through messenger Shimada Seizaemon (Shigesue).

One: Same month, 9th　When [the lord] went to the castle, the Shogun appeared in the Black Study, and [the lord] presented five summer kimono (two with vertical dyeing, one light persimmon, one light blue, one white), three single-layer garments (one with twill stripes, one with silk crepe, one with figured satin), and two lined kimono (one formal striped, one with refined stripes and vertical dyeing with red lining), and expressed gratitude for the marriage.
