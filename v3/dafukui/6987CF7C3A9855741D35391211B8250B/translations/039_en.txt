On the 27th day of the 11th month, regarding Nakane Chōzaemon, who arrived in Fukui yesterday on the 26th, letters were sent to Lord Watanabe Ōsumi-no-kami Tsunasada and Lord Abe Shirōgorō Masayuki requesting that they report to the Council of Elders that arrangements had been made according to the shogunate's orders

1. On the 29th of the same month, envoy Ōta Awa went to the castle to express gratitude for the gifted crane. He received a reply letter, and on that occasion Awa was granted a court robe and haori jacket

1. On the same day, the wild geese and ducks caught and raised by the hawk that was bestowed this summer were presented through envoy Hasegawa Hachirozaemon Katsumori. He departed from Fukui today
Note: The date of Hachirozaemon's arrival in Edo and other details are unclear. A reply letter was received on the 13th of the same month

1. On the 3rd day of the 12th month, at Kezōji Temple, the seventh memorial service for Lady Kōshōin was conducted from the 1st of this month until today. The same service was also conducted at Nansen Temple in Yanaka

1. On the 9th day of the same month, due to the cold season, an envoy was sent to Edo to inquire after His Lordship's well-being
