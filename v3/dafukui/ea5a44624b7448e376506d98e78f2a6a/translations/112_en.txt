　　　　　　　△　Hibi

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, at Takada in Echigo Province
　Summoned, year unknown
　　● Hibi Hanbei Naomasa{{Home province unknown, born in Owari Province｜Family name Fujiwara, original name Matsushita Hanbei}}
　　　　Naomasa's elder brother named Hibino Kageyu had served since Lord Hideyasu's reign,
　　　　so Naomasa was also heard of and summoned. Regarding ancestors, around the Eiroku era a man named
　　　　Hibino Shimotsuke-no-kami led over three thousand cavalry in the Battle of Moribe in western Mino,
　　　　fought against Lord Nobunaga and killed a man named Tsunekawa Kyūzō, but was killed in battle at that time. His
　　　　son Hibi Shimotsuke-no-kami, for certain reasons called himself Shirae Shimotsuke-no-kami, and was lord of Hachiman-yama Castle in Ōmi Province,
　　　　but was killed by rebels at a place called Nanayashiki in Ōta Village, Owari Province.
　　　　His son Hibi Shimotsuke-no-kami was the father of Hanbei Naomasa and served Regent Hidetsugu,
　　　　initially as lord of Hachiman-yama in Ōmi Province, but later became lord of Kiyosu in
　　　　Owari Province with domain holdings of seventy thousand koku. Shimotsuke-no-kami's daughter named Ako
　　　　was the mother of Lord Hidetsugu's son Prince Senmaru. Because of this, when Lord Hidetsugu
　　　　committed seppuku, Shimotsuke-no-kami also accompanied him and committed suicide at Myōkaku-ji Temple in Kyoto
