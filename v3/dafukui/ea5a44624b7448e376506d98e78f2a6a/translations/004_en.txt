Those whose ancestors were summoned by Lord Hideyasu in Echizen during his reign

△Takada
Summoned in the 8th year of Keichō, year of the Water Rabbit, during Lord Hideyasu's reign
● Takada Tōtōmi Kazuhide 《Native province: Settsu, Birth province: Tōtōmi, Family name: Sugawara, Original name: Kozaemon》

During the time of Lord Toyotomi Hideyoshi, he served under Masuda Uemon-no-jō, lord of Koriyama Castle in Yamato Province. At that time, his stipend was 10,000 koku. During the Battle of Sekigahara, although Masuda Uemon was on Ishida's side, he did not go to the battlefield himself but sent Takada Tōtōmi as a deputy commander with troops. When his lord was defeated, Uemon-no-jō was granted clemency and retired to Mount Kōya. At that time, Honda Sado-no-kami and Ii Hyōbu-shōyū issued orders that Takada Tōtōmi should sever ties with Uemon-no-jō, as this was the shogun's will. Accordingly, he escorted Uemon-no-jō to the foot of Mount Kōya, then went to Koriyama to surrender the castle, arranged for Uemon-no-jō's wife and children to be settled elsewhere, and stayed in Kyoto. There, under the direction of Honda Sado-no-kami and Ii Hyōbu-shōyū, he was summoned by Lord Hideyasu in the first month of the 8th year of Keichō. He requested and still possesses the letter sent by Honda Sado-no-kami to the senior retainers of the household as proof of this event.
