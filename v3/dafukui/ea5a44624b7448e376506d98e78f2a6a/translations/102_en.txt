【Left page】
　　　　　　　　△　Nakane

　Summoned in Echigo during the Genna era (1615-1624) in the reign of Lord Tadamasa
　　● Nakane Shingorōzaemon (real name unknown)《Both home province and birthplace unknown, surname unknown, original name: Nanjō Jinzaemon》
　　　　Shingorōzaemon was formerly called Nakane Jinzaemon and relied on Nakane Ōsumi-no-kami for
　　　　his livelihood, being cared for as a masterless samurai, but when his livelihood matters were not
　　　　resolved, he complained and left, thus earning hatred and making it even more
　　　　difficult to make a living, so he withdrew to Shinshū and changed his name to Nanjō Jinzaemon. Meanwhile,
　　　　Jinzaemon's younger brother, whose name was [blank], served as Lord Tadamasa's foot soldier and during the Siege of Osaka
　　　　accompanied his lord and died in battle before the lord's horse. After that, there was an inquiry
　　　　stating that if there were any relatives of this person, they would be summoned. When it was learned that Nanjō Jinzaemon
　　　　was living as a masterless samurai in Shinshū, he was summoned in Echigo Province,
　　　　accompanied [the lord] to Echizen, and was gradually granted increases in stipend. Later he changed his name to Nakane Shingorō-
　　　　zaemon. After his eldest son Inobei inherited the family headship, in the 3rd year of Jōkyō, hinoe-tora year (1686), during the personnel reshuffling,
　　　　he was granted leave, and the main family line was discontinued.
