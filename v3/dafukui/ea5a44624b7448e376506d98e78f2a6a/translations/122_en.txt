【Left page】
　　　　　　　　△　Takeso

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, summoned at Takada in Echigo Province
　Year unknown
　　● Takeso Gonzaemon Motoyasu《Both home and birth province Echizen｜Minamoto clan》
　　　　Ancestor said to be descendant of Takeso Kanja Nobumasa, great-great-grandson of Hachiman Tarō Yoshiie. Motoyasu's
　　　　grandfather, called Takeso Gyōbu Shōyū, served the Asakura house and for generations resided
　　　　in a place called Yachi in Sakai District of this province with a stipend of 10,000 koku. Gyōbu Shōyū's eldest son,
　　　　called Uneme, was Motoyasu's father. After the fall of the Asakura house, [Uneme] followed Maeda Matazaemon
　　　　Toshiie and died of illness at Oyama in Kaga Province. His son Gonzaemon Motoyasu
　　　　served Maeda Chikuzen-no-kami Toshinaga and participated in the Siege of Osaka, performing spear work
　　　　at Okayama front. He still preserves documentary evidence from those who fought alongside him at that time. Later,
　　　　when Lord Tadamasa heard that [Motoyasu] had left the Maeda house, he summoned him in Echigo.
　　　　Thereafter he accompanied [the lord] to this province, and in the 5th year of Kan'ei, tsuchinoe-tatsu year (1628), during the
　　　　rotation for the construction work of Tayasu Gate in Edo, he performed official duties and received written commendations from Lord Tadamasa, and
　　　　he still possesses the written documents also granted by Lord Tajima-no-kami.
