　　　　　　People whose ancestors were summoned during Lord Tadanao's reign

【Left page】
　　　　　　　△　Ōta

　Summoned during Lord Tadanao's reign, year unknown
　　● Ōta Sōbē Nobushige《Home province: Musashi　Birth province: Ise｜Family name: Minamoto》
　　　　Nobushige's father was called Ōta Tango-no-kami and served Kitabatake Tomonori,
　　　　the governor of Ise Province, but became a rōnin after the fall of the Kitabatake house.
　　　　The ancestor was a descendant of Ōta Dōkan and has inherited Dōkan's long sword
　　　　through generations. Regarding the family crest, the ancestors originally used the
　　　　bellflower (kikyō), but changed it to nine stars (kuyō) when serving the Kitabatake house,
　　　　as has been passed down.

　Inherited the family headship during Lord Tadamasa's reign, year unknown
　　　Ōta San'ya Nobusumi《Birth province: Echizen》
　　　　Shōhō 2, Year of the Wood Snake (1645), 1st day of the 8th month: Lord Tadamasa passed away,
　　　　and on the 19th day of the same month, [Nobusumi] committed junshi (ritual suicide following his lord)
