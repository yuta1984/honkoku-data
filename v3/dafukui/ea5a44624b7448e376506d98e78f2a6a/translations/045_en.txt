【Left page】
　　　　　　　△　Nomura

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, in the 12th year of Keicho, year of Hinoto-Hitsuji (1607),
　summoned at Anezaki in Kazusa Province
　　● Nomura Jutayu Tomoaki《home province: Omi, birth province: Echizen, family name: Fujiwara》
　　　　His ancestor was Michimasa, the seventh son of Minister of the Left Yorimichi,
　　　　who built a mountain villa in Ogurisu, Yamashina-go, Yamashiro Province during the Eisho era
　　　　and was known as Lord Nomura. His son Tomoushi served as administrator (shoshi) in the capital
　　　　and governed 3,500 cho of Umezu domain. His son Ujihisa distinguished himself
　　　　at the Shirakawa battle during the Hogen era and at the night battle of Taiken Gate during the Heiji era.
　　　　His son Kageyu-no-zaemon Tomohisa governed 2,300 cho across thirteen villages in Nomura, Omi Province.
　　　　During the Wada rebellion in Kamakura, he killed Wada Yoshishige and Yoshinao,
　　　　received rewards, and was appointed as land steward (jito) of 300 cho
　　　　at Bajomen-den in Musashi Province. Furthermore, he received the character "Tomo"
　　　　from Lord Sanetomo and was granted permanent exemption. A descendant thirteen generations
　　　　after this Tomohisa was also called Kageyu-no-zaemon Tomohisa. He served Asakura Yoshikage
　　　　and governed 500 kan within Ikeda District, Echizen Province. When Yoshikage campaigned
　　　　to Odani in Goshu, he quarreled over battle formations, betrayed the Asakura house,
　　　　joined under Lord Nobunaga's command, and advanced in the vanguard during Lord Hideyoshi's campaigns
