　　　　　Those ancestors who served since Hitachi Province Shimotsuma during Lord Tadamasa's reign

【Left page】
　　　　　　　△　Ozeki

　Summoned when Lord Tadamasa received the fief of Shimotsuma in Hitachi Province during his reign
　　● Ozeki Sukesaemon Hisayoshi《home province: Shimotsuke, birth province: Hitachi, clan: Tanji, original name: Yamaguchi Sukesaemon》
　　　　Hisayoshi's grandfather, Ozeki Uemon Sukemasu《later changed name to Mimasaka-no-kami, religious name: Mian》, resided in Kurobane in Shimotsuke Province and
　　　　held a domain of 20,000 koku. His son, Yamaguchi Kazue Tsuneharu《later changed name to Shingozaemon》,
　　　　for some reason did not succeed to his father's headship and went to stay with his maternal grandfather, Yamaguchi Izumo-no-kami Tsunemitsu,
　　　　lord of Yatabe Castle in Hitachi Province, and used the Yamaguchi surname. It is said that he
　　　　distinguished himself in the Battle of Ushiku in the same province. Afterward, he received protection from
　　　　the lord of Kando Castle in the same province《name unknown》 and lived in that area,
　　　　where he died. His son, Sukesaemon Hisayoshi, lived as a ronin in Kando and,
　　　　being fond of falconry, kept hawks. It is transmitted that he was summoned by Lord Tadamasa
　　　　and served as head falconer. In Genna 4, year of Tsuchinoe-Uma (1618), he received a vermillion seal document for new knowledge and
　　　　changed back to Ozeki, which was his original family name.
