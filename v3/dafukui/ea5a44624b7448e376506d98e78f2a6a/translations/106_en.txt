　　　　He was granted 300 koku and appointed to the position of Osaki-bushō-gashira (military commander).
　　　　However, his feeling that the stipend was insufficient reached His Lordship's ears, and when his
　　　　second son Tahei reached fourteen years of age, he was summoned and granted a new stipend of 200 koku.
　　　　Grateful for this, he served diligently. When a vacancy arose in the position of Banner Magistrate (Hatamoto-bugyō),
　　　　sealed bids were called for from the retainers for the successor position. Since Tōbei's bid was unsuccessful,
　　　　he was appointed to the Banner Magistrate position. His eldest son Sehei was granted succession
　　　　during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, and Sehei's son also inherited, but the line later became extinct.
　　　　The second son Tahei was also granted leave along with others in the 3rd year of Jōkyō, hinoe-tora year (1686).
