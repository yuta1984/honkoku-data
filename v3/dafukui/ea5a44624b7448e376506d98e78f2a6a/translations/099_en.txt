【Left Page】
　　　　　　　　△　Ōta

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, summoned to service in Echigo《Note: Era name｜unknown》
　　● Ōta San'emon Masatsugu《Note: Home province Ōmi, birth province Kaga｜Family name unknown, original name Iguro, Buddhist name Kyūshin》
　　　　His ancestors lived in a place called Iguro in Gōshū (Ōmi Province). When Masatsugu was a youth, the
　　　　Iguro estate was destroyed in warfare, and he fled under the care of retainers. Afterwards, he served
　　　　under Murakami Shuubō-no-kami Yorikatsu in Echigo Province and possessed documents from Yorikatsu and Tadakatsu.
　　　　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, it was thought that pronouncing the Iguro surname was inauspicious, so he was ordered
　　　　to change it and adopted the name Ōta. The origins of the Ōta designation have not been handed down and are unknown.

　Summoned to service during Lord Tadamasa's reign and served thereafter
　During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, Kanbun 12, year of the water rat (1672), granted family succession
