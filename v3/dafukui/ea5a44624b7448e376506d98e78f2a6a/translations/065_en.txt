　　　　　　　　△　Toda

During Lord Tadamasa's reign, the second year of Genna, hinoe-tatsu year,
summoned to service at Matsumoto Castle in Shinano Province
　　● Toda Shichibei Narimasa《Note: Both home and birth provinces Mikawa, family name Fujiwara, original name Gozaemon》
　　　　Narimasa's great-grandfather, Toda Shuzen Tadatsugu, resided at Tahara Castle in Sanshu Province.
　　　　In the sixth year of Keichō, kanoto-ushi year, ninth month, Narimasa got into a fight at a bathhouse in Kōji-machi, Edo,
　　　　killing on the spot two men from the shogunate's kobito-shū (minor officials), two from the o-kachi-shū (foot soldiers), and four accompanying samurai—eight men total—
　　　　and severely wounding six others who had joined in, all of whom died the following day.
　　　　For this, Narimasa was summoned to the magistrate's office, ordered to tend to his wounds, and after investigation into the circumstances of this quarrel,
　　　　he was placed under custody. However, in the eighth year, mizunoto-u year,
　　　　by the supreme command of Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), he was summoned by Kazusa-no-suke-sama (Matsudaira Tadamasa), and when the Osaka campaign began,
　　　　he was granted armor and served for both years, distinguishing himself in battle at the Yamato-guchi front.
　　　　He was given a written certificate. After Kazusa-no-suke-sama's exile, he was summoned to the household (Matsudaira family),
　　　　placed in charge of sailors, and later put in charge of the ancestral archery group, sumo wrestlers, and others.
