　　　　　　　△　Oguri

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned in Echizen
　Year unknown
　　● Oguri Jiuemon Shigemasa (home province and birthplace both Mikawa, family name Taira)
　　　　Regarding his ancestors, they were hereditary retainers of our lord's ancestral family for generations, serving in military campaigns in various places. Shigemasa served Okazaki Saburō-sama, and after his death served under Kuroda Josui (he still possesses the written warrant). He accompanied [Josui] to the Odawara campaign, Korean campaigns, etc. Later, during the Ōtomo rebellion, at the Battle of Tateishi in Bungo Province, he fought under Josui's command and killed and captured the military commander Yoshihiro Kahyōe Muneyuki. He still preserves Yoshihiro Muneyuki's sword, Kuroda Josui's commendation letter from that time, and copies of battlefield records from the enemy side. During both years of the Osaka campaigns, he served Lord Tadanao, and when he went as a messenger to the main force, he was summoned before Lord Gongensama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) and received direct responses. On that occasion, he was granted a key-spear, which he still possesses.
