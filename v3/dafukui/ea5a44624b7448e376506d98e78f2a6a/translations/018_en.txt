　　　　　　　△　Ōida

　Summoned in Echizen Province during Lord Hideyasu's reign (year unknown)
　　However, there is a vermillion seal document from Keichō 8 (1603)
　　● Ōida Kenmotsu Fusanaka (both home province and birth province: Echigo, family name: Minamoto, Buddhist name: Dōmu)
　　　　Initially served Uesugi Kagekatsu, and together with yoriki-chi held a fief of 20,000 koku as lord of Sado
　　　　Castle, according to tradition. Later summoned by Lord Hideyasu, during Lord Tadanao's
　　　　reign he served in the Osaka campaigns. During Lord Tadamasa's reign he requested leave to become a monk
　　　　and took the name Dōmu, but through various kind words he was recalled, and furthermore his two
　　　　sons were summoned, and later Fusanaka was also granted increases in stipend and appointed
　　　　to the position of flag magistrate, serving in official capacity despite his monastic status. During Lord Mitsumichi's reign he was
　　　　ordered to retire and granted 200 koku for elderly support. His heir Uemon had been
　　　　granted a separate fief of 300 koku during Lord Tadamasa's reign and was serving, but died while Dōmu was still alive, and his son
　　　　Yasaburō succeeded him, but he too died young without an heir, and the main family line was discontinued.
　　　　Documents recording Dōmu's military exploits at various places while with the Uesugi house and the circumstances of leaving that house,
　　　　written records from the time of the Osaka campaigns in service to our domain, and other scroll documents of the Ōida genealogy, etc.
