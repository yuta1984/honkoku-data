　　　　　　　△　Ōida

　Summoned in Echizen during Lord Hideyasu's reign (year unknown)
　　However, there exists a vermillion seal from Keichō 8 (1603)
　　● Ōida Kenbutsu Fusanaka (home province and birth province both Echigo, family name Minamoto, Buddhist name Dōmu)
　　　　Initially served under Uesugi Kagekatsu as a retainer, holding a fief of 20,000 koku together with yoriki-chi, and was reportedly the lord of a castle in Sado. Later summoned by Lord Hideyasu, he participated in the Siege of Osaka during Lord Tadanao's reign. During Lord Tadamasa's reign, he requested leave to become a monk and took the name Dōmu, but was called back through various kind words, and his two sons were also summoned. Later, Fusanaka was granted additional stipends and appointed to the position of banner magistrate (ohata-bugyō), serving while maintaining his monastic status. During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, he was ordered to retire and granted 200 koku for his old-age support. His eldest son Uemon was granted a separate fief of 300 koku during Lord Tadamasa's reign and served, but died while Dōmu was still alive. His son Yasaburō succeeded him but also died young without an heir, causing the main family line to become extinct. Records of Dōmu's activities in various places while he was with the Uesugi house, items from when that family departed, documents from the time of the Siege of Osaka under our domain, and other scroll paintings of the Ōida family genealogy, etc.
