　　　　　　　　△　Suō

　In the 5th year of Genna, tsuchinoto-hitsuji year (1619) during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　Summoned in Echigo Province, year unknown
　　● Hara Chōbei Yoshikiyo《Home province Ise, birth province unknown｜Family name Taira, original name Suō》
　　　　Ancestor Suō Kunai-shōyū Masasuke, his son Suō Hyūga-no-kami Sasatsuna, his son Suō Sa-
　　　　do-no-kami Masatsuna, his son Suō Kōzuke-no-suke Yoshimasa served the Koga Kubō house for generations.
　　　　Kōzuke-no-suke Yoshimasa was the second son of Lord Koga Takamoto and served Ujitomo
　　　　of the Gosho at Setatani in Musashi Province; the official document still exists. After the fall of Odawara, he withdrew to Hitachi Province
　　　　and lived there. He was called Hara Dōshunsai. The surname Hara comes from his mother's side. His son
　　　　Hara Chōbei Yoshikiyo first served Sengoku Hyōbu-shōyū Tadamasa, and there is a written record
　　　　that he led foot soldiers into battle during the Siege of Osaka. He was later summoned to serve the domain.
