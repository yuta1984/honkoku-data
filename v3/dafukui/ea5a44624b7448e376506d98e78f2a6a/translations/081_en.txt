　　　　　　　　　△　Sano

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, summoned to service in Echigo Province
　Year number unknown, there is a vermillion seal from the 8th year of Genna (1622)
　　● Sano Shikibu Kagetoshi 《Note: Home province is Shimotsuke, birth province is Ise, family name is Fujiwara, original name was Naizen》
　　　　His ancestor was a descendant of Tawara Tōta Hidesato, who was ten generations removed from Ōshikokan Kamatari's eighth-generation descendant, specifically Ashikaga Matatarou
　　　　Tadatsuna. His son Sano Tarō Aritsuna resided in Sano-no-shō in Shimotsuke Province, and thus came to be called Sano-no-
　　　　shōji (Sano estate administrator), and from this they adopted Sano as their
　　　　family name. Eighteen generations later, a descendant named
　　　　Sano Echizen-no-kami Hidetsuna, during the time of Ashikaga Mochiuji, around the Ōei era, held domain over
　　　　ninety thousand koku in Sano-no-shō in Shimotsuke Province. The castle site was at a place called Tochimoto, where the ruins of Karasawa
　　　　Castle still exist today. His descendants continued to serve the Ashikaga shogunal house,
　　　　and around the Eiroku era, one named Sano Umanosuke Tadayoshi resided in Matsusaka in Ise Province.
　　　　His son, named Sano Tōemon Tadanao, served as chief retainer in the house of Seki Nagato-no-
　　　　kami Kazumasa, lord of Kameyama Castle in Ise Province. His son Sano Naizen Kagetoshi served in the same position
　　　　and distinguished himself at the Odawara campaign, the Kashiro village uprising in Shinano Province, and the Battle of Sekigahara,
