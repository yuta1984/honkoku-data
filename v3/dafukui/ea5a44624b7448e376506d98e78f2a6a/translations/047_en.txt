【Left page】
　　　　　　　△　Kadono

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, in Keicho 14, year of Tsuchinoto-Tori (1609),
　summoned at Anezaki in Kazusa Province
　　● Kadono Tarozaemon, personal name unknown《home province: Shinano, birth province unknown, family name unknown》
　　　　He held territory at a place called Kadono near Ashida in Shinano Province, hence the surname.
　　　　He served as an attendant during both years of the Osaka campaigns.

　During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in Keian 3, year of Tsuchinoto-Ushi (1650), granted family headship
　　　Kadono Kuuemon Ieshige《birth province: Shinano》
　　　　Fifty koku from his father's inherited land was confiscated, and furthermore his younger brother called Kadono Hanbei was granted
　　　　a fief allocation. As both became impoverished, he petitioned for leave through the connections of his home province,
　　　　appealing to Kato Sogetsu. That same year, the fifty koku was returned to him, and later
　　　　in Manji 1, he received an increase in stipend. His younger brother Hanbei later became sickly,
　　　　requested leave, and took Buddhist vows.
