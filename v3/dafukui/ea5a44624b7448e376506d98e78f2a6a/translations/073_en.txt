【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△　Nishimura

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, at Matsushiro Castle in Shinano Province
　Summoned to service (year unknown)
　　● Nishimura Rokubei Sadehide《Note: Original province unknown, birth province Shinano, family name Minamoto》
　　　　His ancestor, a man called Nishimura Shimōsa-no-kami Sadatsugu, served under Uesugi Kagekatsu and
　　　　when confronting Date Masamune, Shimōsa-no-kami suddenly attacked Masamune's camp, causing Masamune's defeat, and
　　　　seized the equipment and curtains at the battlefield. Afterward, the Date family requested to exchange the Shiroishi domain of 40,000 koku
　　　　for the curtains, but the Uesugi family's
　　　　reply was that they could take any amount of territory with spear points, but the curtains were needed
　　　　so they would not return them. Those curtains remained with the Uesugi family, and because of this,
　　　　the Nishimura Shimōsa-no-kami family crest also came to bear bamboo and sparrows, as has been passed down
　　　　Sadatsugu's son, Nishimura Seisuke Sadetoshi, did not serve anywhere and remained
　　　　a village samurai his entire life. Among the clan, men called Nishimura Sansuke and Sanpei served during
　　　　Lord Hideyasu's reign. Seisuke Sadetoshi's son Rokubei Sadehide was summoned by Lord Tadamasa and
　　　　later was appointed as a retainer and guardian during Lord Mitsumichi's childhood, and in his old age
