【Left Page】
　　　　　　　　　△　Tsuda

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, Genna 9, Year of the Water Boar (1623)
　Summoned to service at Takada in Echigo Province
　　● Tsuda Gen-no-jō Shigenari 《Note: Both original and birth provinces are Kii｜Family name is Tachibana　Original name is Nanjō》
　　　　His ancestor Nanjō Kenmotsu Kazunaga was a descendant of
　　　　Kusunoki Masashige's second son Jirōzaemon Masayoshi. His son, a man named Nanjō Kenmotsu Kazumasa, had military merit, so Lord Oda Nobunaga,
　　　　wishing to establish family ties, granted him the status of a family branch, the Tsuda surname,
　　　　and the maku crest. He was granted dominion over more than half of Kii Province, Handa-gō in Uchi-gun of Yamato Province, and
　　　　two districts within Izumi Province. Later, when Shibata Katsuie was destroyed, the Tsuda family
　　　　clan fought against Lord Hideyoshi, and seventy-three members of the family all died in battle together. At this time, the entire clan
　　　　was completely destroyed, but Tsuda Shirōdayū Arinao, son of Tsuda Kenmotsu Kazunaga,
　　　　was ill at the time and did not go to battle, thus surviving alone. His son Gen-no-jō Shige-
　　　　nari was initially summoned to serve Lord Shimōsa-no-kami Tadayoshi, but after His Lordship's death became a rōnin,
　　　　then was summoned by Lord Tadamasa.
