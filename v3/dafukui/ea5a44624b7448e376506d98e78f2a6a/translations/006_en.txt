　　　　　　△　Sasaji

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned in Echizen《Note: Year unknown｜》
　　● Sasaji Hyōgo Shigemasa《Note: Home province Ise, Birth province Owari｜Surname Minamoto, Original name Katō Shōjirō》
　　　　His ancestor was Shibata Owari-
　　　　no-kami Ieuji, eldest son of Ashikaga Kunai-shōyū Yasushi, seven generations from Hachiman-tarō Yoshiie, and four generations later Ashikaga Owari-no-kami Takatsune, his son Shibata Jibu-dayū Yoshimasa
　　　　《Note: Commonly known as｜Buei》his son Jibu-dayū Yoshishige, his second son Sasaji Hyōbu-no-sakan Yoshiharu《Note: Changed his title at this time｜Lived in retirement in Seshū》
　　　　Hyōgo Shigemasa was Yoshiharu's great-grandson. Shigemasa first became a retainer of Kingō Chūnagon Hideaki-kyō and
　　　　lived in Okayama in Bizen Province. During the Battle of Sekigahara, as Hideaki-kyō's vanguard,
　　　　he captured Hokkemaru Castle, the stronghold of Ishida Jibu-shōyū《Note: The armor he wore at that time｜and his battle standard still exist today》After Hideaki's
　　　　return from battle, he was granted a fief of 20,000 koku and became a military commander, but after Hideaki's downfall, he went to Kyoto and
　　　　was summoned to the (Matsudaira) house. During Lord Tadanao's expedition to Osaka, he was ill and
　　　　did not accompany him. Hyōgo Shigemasa's eldest son, called Saemon, received a separate
　　　　stipend during Lord Tadanao's reign but died young thereafter. His second son, called Inobei Shigenari, received an
　　　　allocation during Lord Tadamasa's reign, but was granted leave during Lord Mitsumichi's reign.
