　　　　　　　△　Shiōten

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, Keichō 6 (1601), year of Kanoto-ushi, in Echizen
　summoned to service
　　● Shiōten Matahei Masazane (home province Musashi, birth province Tanba, family name Fujiwara of the Kodama clan)
　　　　In ancient times, Kodama Musashi-no-suke Koreyuki, a descendant of Ōshikouchi Kamatari, distinguished himself in military campaigns in Ōshū, and received a fan from Minamoto no Yoriyoshi which became the family crest. Koreyuki's great-great-great-great-great-great-grandson, Shikata Saburō Hiroyoshi, resided in a place called Shikata in Kodama District, Musashi Province, and took this as his title. His son Gorō Hirotsuna defeated Taira no Tomoaki at Ichi-no-Tani in Settsu in the first year of Genryaku (1184), and following Yoritomo, was granted additional favors in Ise Province. Thirteen generations after Hirotsuna, his descendant Shikata Tajima-no-kami Masanaga, during the reign of Emperor Tsuchimikado, experienced tribulation but was appointed Kurōdo Saemon-no-jō, received an imperial decree, and changed his name to Shiōten. Later, Masanaga's great-great-great-great-grandson Shiōten Matahei Masazane served under Akechi Hyūga-no-kami Mitsuhide and performed military duties in various places. In Tenshō 10 (1582), when Lord Oda Nobunaga was killed, he slew Mori Ranmaru Nagayasu. After the fall of the Akechi, fearing Toyotomi Hideyoshi, he hid in various places, and when he was in Yuasa in Kii Province, the domain lord Aoki Kii-no-kami Hidemochi, out of old friendship, took care of him and granted him a fief of 300 koku.
