　　　　　　△　Ishikawa

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign 
　in the Keicho era at Anezaki in Kazusa Province
　　● Ishikawa Jinza'emon Mitsushige《home province and birth province both Mutsu, family name: Taira》
　　　　The ancient matters of his ancestors are unknown. Mitsushige initially served under Naito Tachi, 
　　　　then left that house and went to Edo in Musashi Province, where he was teaching archery. Among his disciples, 
　　　　a man named Oguri Hyokichi recommended Mitsushige to Lord Tadamasa during a conversation, 
　　　　and he was summoned. He served as an attendant during the two-year Osaka campaigns. He died in Shoho 1, year of Kinoe-Saru (1644), 
　　　　and his inherited stipend was divided between his heir Jinza'emon Shigeoki and second son Chuzaemon Takekiyo. 
　　　　However, Jinza'emon Shigeoki requested leave during Lord Yoshishina's reign, and the main family 
　　　　line was discontinued.
