　　　　　　　　△　Yamamoto

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, in Keichō 6 (1601), year of Kanoto-ushi, in Echizen
　Summoned
　　● Yamamoto Seiuemon (true name unknown; both home province and birthplace Ōmi, family name Minamoto)
　　　　He served Takeda Shingen, and was later summoned by Lord Ieyasu and temporarily
　　　　placed under Lord Ii Hyōbu. During this time, when the attacks on Nagakute and Kanie castles were conducted, among the forty to fifty Kai province warriors
　　　　that Lord Ii Hyōbu took charge of, Seiuemon was included and distinguished himself.
　　　　He struck down an enemy commander who was retreating on horseback leading about twenty foot soldiers, and when he showed this to Lord Hyōbu
　　　　, it was deemed the first head taken at Kanie that day. When this was reported directly to Lord Ieyasu,
　　　　he was summoned to his camp stool at the border post, where Lord Ieyasu was partaking of early melons,
　　　　and was granted half of that melon, which he received with gratitude, as has been passed down. Later, while
　　　　he was with the Sanada family, during the attack on Ueda Castle in Shinshū, among the castle defenders, both Yoda Hyōbu and Yama-
　　　　moto Seiuemon sortied and fought valiantly. Yoda was killed in action, and at that time Seiu-
　　　　emon was also wounded in several places, but he surrounded Yoda's corpse and prevented his head from being taken.
　　　　Later, Lord Makino Umanosuke offered a sword in tribute to that Yoda.
