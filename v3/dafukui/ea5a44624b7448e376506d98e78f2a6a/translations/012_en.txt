【Right page】
During Lord Tadamasa's reign, he was appointed to the position of head falconer, but he declined the appointment, stating that there was no precedent of serving in such a role. As a result, he defied the lord's will, had half of his domain confiscated, and was granted 65 koku. Later, the assessed yield of Hikime Village became 142 koku, 6 to, and 9 sho through rope measurement. Of this, 100 koku was granted to Yoshishige under the vermillion seal in the 7th year of Kan'ei (1630), and 42 koku, 6 to, and 9 sho was granted to his cousin Kishi Sōbei Mitsukiyo. In the 14th year of the same era (1637), an additional grant of 50 koku was made to Yoshishige.

During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in the 2nd year of Jōō (1653), family headship was granted.
Kishi Rihei Yoshitsugu (birthplace: Echizen)

During Lord Tsunamasa's reign, Yoshitsugu's succession was granted to his legitimate son Rihei Yoshitatsu, but during Lord Yoshishina's reign, he defied the lord's will, had his stipend confiscated, and his son Jūsuke was summoned to serve as a falconer.

Yoshitsugu's second son
Current: Kishi Mozaemon Masanobu (birthplace: Echizen)

At Hikime Village, the residence, mountains, and fields that had been held as fiefs by ancestors and where his grandfather Yoshishige had lived are still possessed by the family to this day. The vermillion seals and precedential documents are held by his nephew Kishi Jūsuke.

During Lord Yoshikuni's reign, in the 4th year of Kyōhō (1719), he was separately summoned to service.
