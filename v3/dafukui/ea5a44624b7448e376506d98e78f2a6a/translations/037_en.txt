　　　　　　　△　Ishikawa

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, Keichō 8th year, mizunoto-u (1603), in Echizen
　He was summoned
　　● Ishikawa Sōzaemon Narizuna《home province: Mikawa, birth province: unknown, clan name: Gen, childhood name: Yohachirou, later: Tsushima-no-kami》
　　　　He was the eldest son of Ishikawa Hōki-no-kami. Due to certain circumstances, the family headship was
　　　　given to Ishikawa Hyūga-no-kami, the younger brother of Hōki-no-kami, while Narizuna was separately
　　　　granted 5,000 koku and served Tokugawa Ieyasu from childhood.
　　　　He was appointed as a guard captain (bantō). Later he was assigned to serve Lord Okazaki Saburō, but
　　　　after Lord Saburō's death by suicide, he was again summoned to serve close to Tokugawa Ieyasu as a personal attendant.
　　　　However, during the mobilization for the Odawara campaign, when the retainers were assembled at Suruga Castle and
　　　　the lord's address stated that each should show loyalty during the current campaign, while Narizuna was serving,
　　　　a man named Kagawa Gyōbu spoke ill, saying "His lordship's judgment is unclear." When Narizuna responded
　　　　"That is because you have not performed deeds worthy of his lordship's notice," Gyōbu clicked his tongue, so
　　　　considering this behavior insolent, after reporting to the assembled retainers, he waited outside the gate and
　　　　killed Gyōbu with one strike, then withdrew to Mikawa province. It is said that afterward, Tokugawa Ieyasu
　　　　also inquired about what had become of him. His son, Gorobei Otonari, was a member of the Ishikawa clan
