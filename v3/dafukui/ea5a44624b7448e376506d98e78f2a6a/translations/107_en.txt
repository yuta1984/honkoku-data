　　　　　　△　Hasegawa

　Summoned in Echigo during Lord Tadamasa's reign, year unknown
　　● Hasegawa Hachirozaemon Kazuyasu《Both home province and birth province unknown｜Family name unknown　Original name Yasojūrō》
　　　　Family lineage unknown. Kazuyasu's father, a man called Hasegawa Hachirozaemon Katsuke, was living
　　　　as a masterless samurai (rōnin) in Kanaya, Tōtōmi Province, when Lord Hideyasu was residing in Sunpu.
　　　　Word of Katsuke's skill in gun forging reached His Lordship's ears, and there was an informal indication
　　　　that he should be taken into service. However, regarding the matter of accepting this offer, he had some
　　　　reservations and while he delayed, he soon died of illness. Subsequently, his eldest son Zenpachi Katsumasa
　　　　also died young in succession, so the second son Yasojūrō Kazuyasu was summoned.

　During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, 8th year of Kanbun, tsuchinoe-saru year (1668), granted succession
　　　Hasegawa Hachirozaemon Katsumori《Born in Echizen Province｜Original name Buemon》
