　　　　　　　△　Kojima　Ōki

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned in Keichō 6th year, kanoto-ushi (1601)
　　● Kojima Yogorōemon (personal name unknown) 《home province: Mikawa, birth province unknown, surname unknown》
　　　　During the Korean campaign, he was summoned at Lord Hideyasu's military quarters 
　　　　in Nagoya, Hizen, and had an audience with him, as has been passed down. During 
　　　　Lord Tadayoshi's reign, he accompanied him to both Osaka campaigns. Later, while 
　　　　serving as gatekeeper at Kawara Gate of the current castle, there were rumors that 
　　　　Lord Takada (Tadayoshi) might secretly withdraw to the Kantō region. He was ordered 
　　　　to strictly inspect those entering and leaving the gate, which he faithfully carried 
　　　　out, thus earning Lord Tadayoshi's approval. However, after Lord Tadayoshi's exile, 
　　　　due to this matter, he fell out of favor with Princess Takada, and was therefore 
　　　　dismissed. He went to Sunpu, where he was placed in service under Lord Suruga and 
　　　　died of illness.
　　　　His eldest son Kojima Tonpachi was specially summoned during Lord Hideyasu's reign 
　　　　in Keichō 8th year, mizunoto-u (1603), and served in that capacity. During the Osaka 
　　　　campaigns, he served together with his father Yogorōemon, but later died of illness, 
　　　　and the main family line was discontinued.
