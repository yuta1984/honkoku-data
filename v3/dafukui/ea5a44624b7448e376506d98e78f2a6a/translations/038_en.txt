【Right page】
　　　　He served under Nagato-no-kami, and during the Battle of Sekigahara, he took three noses and one head of enemies. 
　　　　A person named Atsumi Hanemon served as witness to this deed. Later, when Narizuna 
　　　　had gone to Kōshū, Lord Hiraiwa Kazue-no-kami received a written message from Lord Hideyasu 
　　　　stating: "If you learn the whereabouts of Tsushima-no-kami and his son, tell them to 
　　　　come to Echizen immediately." Following this, father and son went to Echizen together, and the following year he was summoned.
　　　　During Lord Tadanao's reign, he served in the Osaka campaigns. Lord Tadanao bestowed 
　　　　two war-picture fans upon Narizuna. One of them bore a commendation letter written 
　　　　in the lord's own hand, which is still preserved today.

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, Keichō 12th year, hinoto-hitsuji (1607), he was granted a separate fief
　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, Kan'ei 15th year, tsuchinoe-tora (1638), he was granted hereditary succession
　　　Ishikawa Sōzaemon Otonari《birth province: unknown, original name: Gorobei》
　　　　During the Osaka campaigns, he served together with his father Narizuna
