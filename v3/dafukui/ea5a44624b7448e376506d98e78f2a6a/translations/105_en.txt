【Left page】
　　　　　　　　△　Mizuno

　Summoned at Takada in Echigo Province during the reign of Lord Tadamasa
　Year unknown
　　● Mizuno Tōbei Shigetoshi《Home province unknown, born in Shinano Province, surname Sugawara》
　　　　Shigetoshi's father, called Mizuno Shinzaemon, held domain over a place called Mizuno-daira in Shinano Province,
　　　　and had served there for generations. He possessed ancestral documents and various commendation letters,
　　　　but all were lost in a fire, leaving no trace, and thus the details of his ancestors are unknown from oral tradition.
　　　　When serving Uesugi Kagekatsu, he distinguished himself in battle, climbed mountain paths, and was granted
　　　　the privilege of using a boar standard, which his family had used for generations. After leaving Kagekatsu's service,
　　　　he served Lord Kazusanosuke (Yūki Hideyasu) and distinguished himself at Osaka as well, it is said.
　　　　After Lord Kazusanosuke's death, he became a rōnin and sought service with Lord Ogasawara Ukon-shōgen,
　　　　with the promise of receiving a stipend of 1,000 koku and command of ashigaru troops. While traveling
　　　　through Echigo Province on his way there, he stopped to meet with Matsudaira Bizen due to their
　　　　old friendship as former colleagues. When Lord Tadamasa heard of this, he declared that "those who
　　　　served under Lord Kazusanosuke are all considered part of our retainer lineage, so he should not
　　　　be sent to serve Ukon-shōgen." He immediately ordered Matsudaira Bizen to detain him and grant him domain
