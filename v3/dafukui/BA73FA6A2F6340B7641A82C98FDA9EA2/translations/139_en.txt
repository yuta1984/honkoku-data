At the cemetery near Ipponmatsu, connected to Asuwa Hill (Asuwa-yama), a memorial service was held before the burial mound of those who died in battle at Aizu.
Also, at Katsumi Riverbank, beacon fires (signal fires) and a fireworks display were held.

[Written in red ink] "The 20th day"

21st day: Cloudy then sunny, southerly wind. Assisted with drying reed mats, carried out soybeans and various other goods.
　Kansichi and Yohei did millstone grinding. Yesterday, the 19th, two houses burned down in Ota village.
　Last night, Lord Honda Konosuke of Takefu returned to Fukui from Tokyo.
　Heavy rain at night.

21st day [appears twice in original]: Rain then sunny, northerly wind. Stayed home ill with a cold.
　Koshichi, Rihei, and Kyuhei did millstone grinding. Hail fell at night.

22nd day: Rain then sunny, northerly wind. Same as above (stayed home with cold). In the afternoon, washed two shoji panels and replaced one.
　Jinsuke did millstone grinding. Frost fell at night; it was cold.

[Marginal note] Eggplant: 135

23rd day: Clear skies. Dried reed mats, set up frames for drying buckwheat and soybeans.
　Jisanji and Kahei did millstone grinding.

24th day: Rain, a little thunder. Went out to Fukui in the morning. Miscellaneous work in the afternoon.
　Heijiro did millstone grinding.

25th day: Rain then sunny, strong northerly wind. Cleaned the house; in the afternoon, pounded eight portions of glutinous rice.
　[Red ink] "Evening: soup dumplings, one serving"

26th day: Sunny, southerly wind. Thunder heard to the northwest. Early-harvest millet stacked — four koku.
　[Red ink] "Fried dish, two servings"

　Risshū-tō (Start of Winter), the solar term for the 10th month. At seven hours and one minute before dawn today. Daytime: 43.5 units; nighttime: 56.5 units (night lengthening).
　Mohei and Yahichi did millstone grinding. At night.
　At night, heavy thunder, frost, and rain. West wind, then north wind.

27th day: Violent storm (continuing from last night). Copied the prefectural officials' directory for Honpo. Staying home due to cold.
　Last night: northerly wind and hail. Today: heavy rain and violent wind.
　[Red ink] "Dried squid and eggplant simmered together, one serving"

28th day: Cloudy with some sun, slight northerly wind. The village held a general rice harvest. Rested due to cold.
　[Red ink] "Midday: soup dumplings"
　△ In Shimo-Emori village, the elderly mother of Naemon and the young child of Tasaemon passed away from illness.

29th day: Sunny. Rested due to cold. In the afternoon, transferred straw from drying frames into storage, dried reed mats, and assisted with setting up frames for late-harvest rice.
　△

Last day of the month (30th): Sunny. Finished drying reed mats, transported soybeans from Doho, finished treading the field of sixty steps at Oshima.
　Performed the annual "Kami-okuri" (sending-off of the deity) ritual as customary, offering sacred sake and red-bean rice.
　[Red ink] "Night: red-bean rice, dried squid and eggplant simmered together"
　At the Oshima field, received the name of "Ao no Myoyaku" (a blue/green medicinal remedy), associated with Mr. Sugisaka of No. 4, Fukui Shinshiki.
　Last night, just past five o'clock, a large fire broke out on the second floor of Yamada Goroemon's shop on Shimo-Gofuku-cho street in Fukui, causing great commotion.
　Work began on clearing the stone walls of the outer fortifications of Fukui Castle, starting with the Sakura and Yanagi gates.
