Genroku 7, Year of the Wood Dog (1694)
- 4th month, 13th day: Having recovered from smallpox, a celebratory sake-yu ceremony was held

Genroku 12, Year of the Earth Rabbit (1699)
- 2nd month, 11th day:
  Upon Lord Tsunamasa's death, she took the tonsure and came to be called Lady Seishōin

Genroku 13, Year of the Metal Dragon (1700)
- 9th month, 26th day: Departed Edo accompanied by Princess Toyohime (formerly Princess Masahime), traveling via the Hokuriku road to the home domain (arrived in Fukui on the 3rd day of the 10th month, escorted by Sakai Tenzen)
  Note: She took residence at the former accounting office site behind the Iron Gate

Shōtoku 2, Year of the Water Dragon (1712)
- 7th month, 14th day: Passed away (aged fifty)
- Same month, 16th day: Grand funeral held at Ungōji Temple on Mount Shingan, with the posthumous Buddhist name
  Seishōin-den Gaku-yo Jōe Kyōkū Daizen-ni
