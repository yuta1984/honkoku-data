　　　　　　　　　　　　　Memorandum

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Yūki domain — Zen sect
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kōken-ji Temple
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Same domain — Zen sect
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Jōkoku-ji Temple
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Same domain — Jōdo sect
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kōkyō-ji Temple
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Same domain — Zen sect
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Anon-ji Temple

　　　　　　All of the above received their black seals (kokuin) on the same day — the twentieth day of the first month of Bunroku 5 (Hinoto-Saru year) — during the period when the Lord Chūnagon held the domain of Yūki. Furthermore, it appears from the family records (kahu) that the black seals issued to other temple landholdings, shrine landholdings, and retainers were likewise, for the most part, issued on that same day.

　　　　　　　　　　The above is as stated.

Item: On the twenty-fourth day of the fifth month, the messenger for the return-to-domain ceremony, Komamoku (Saneyoshi), appeared before the Duty Councillor Honda Nakatsukasa-no-taifu (Tadayoshi) and presented the formal letter.

Item: On the same day, regarding the verification of the official seal, the domain's resident representative (rusui) Ōtani Ichiemon (Yoshitsugu) brought and presented Hideyasu-sama's seal specimen (inkan) to Honda Kii-no-kami (Masayoshi). (As had been communicated the previous day, the twenty-third, by Akimoto Settsu-no-kami.) The examination was conducted with Akimoto Settsu-no-kami (Ryōchō) also in attendance. It was thereupon declared that the black seal in the possession of Anon-ji Temple was without doubt the black seal of Hideyasu-sama, and the seal specimen was returned.
