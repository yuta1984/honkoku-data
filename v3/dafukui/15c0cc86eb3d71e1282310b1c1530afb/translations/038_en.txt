A circular notice was distributed to the Rusui (caretaker officials) as follows:

　　On this occasion, when receiving the honorable written decree (御判物) and the honorable seal (御来印), each Rusui is expected to wait assembled in the Sotetsu-no-ma (Sago Palm Chamber). After all the daimyō who have been waiting to receive their lord's written decree have dispersed, they are to proceed from the main entrance (御玄関) in groups of approximately ten, as guided, and then depart.

　　Item: The long chests (長持) and narrow boxes (狭箱) in which the said written decrees are placed are to be left outside the Naka-no-mon (middle gate).

　　The above should be understood accordingly, and departure should be conducted so as to avoid crowding. Furthermore, this notice is to be communicated to those of the same assembly (御同席).

　　　　　　　　Tenth month

Item: On the eleventh day of the tenth month, Rusui (caretaker official) Ōtani Ichiemon (Yoshitsugu) called upon the Duty Councilor (御用番) Hotta Sagami-no-kami (Masasuke). There, he received the following written notice (said to have been issued from the same office the previous day):

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Matsudaira Hyōbu-no-taifu

　　　　As there is official business, one representative (名代) is to dress in noshi-me (熨斗目) and half-hakama (半袴), and is to proceed to the castle at the hour of five-and-a-half (approximately 9 a.m.) tomorrow. Furthermore, a written statement naming the representative is to be submitted.
　　　　　　　　　　Eleventh day of the tenth month

Item: On the same day, in accordance with the above, Rusui Ōtani Ichiemon (Yoshitsugu) personally carried and submitted to Duty Councilor Hotta Sagami-no-kami (Masasuke) the written statement naming the representative (名代).
