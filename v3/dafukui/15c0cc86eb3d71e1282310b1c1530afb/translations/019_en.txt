Item: On the twenty-fifth day of the third month, [the lord] met with Senior Councillor Honda Nakatsukasa-no-taifu (Tadayoshi) and fulfilled his official duties.

Item: On the twenty-seventh day of the same month, at Tenryū-ji Temple in Shinagawa, the advance observance (otorikoshi) of the twenty-third memorial service (o-ki) for Hōsen-in-sama was conducted with a tea ceremony (o-chayū).

Item: On the fourth day of the fourth month, as the Inspectors of the Provinces (shokoku junken-shi) — Lord Ōkubo Eshichihei (Norihira), Lord Yamaoka Gorōsaku (Kageyuki), and Lord Tsukushi Uhei (Tsūmon) — were passing through, the head of the advance guard unit (o-saki mono-gashira), Tsutsumi Shichidayū (Nobutsugu), was dispatched to greet them as far as Kinome-tōge [pass].

Item: On the fifth day of the same month, as Lord Karasumaru, the former Middle Counselor (Mitsuyoshi/Kōei), was to travel down to Edo as an Imperial Envoy (chokushi), [the lord] had his residential representative (o-rusui), Tsuda Kuemon (Morihiro), deliver the following written oral communication to the Duty Councillor, Lord Sakai Uta-no-kami (Tadayuki):

　　　　　　　　　　Memorandum of Oral Communication

　　　　　　On this occasion, the former Middle Counselor Karasumaru is traveling down from Kyoto. He is my cousin's son-in-law. Accordingly, during his stay, I wish to call upon him at his lodgings and also to invite him to my residence. I humbly request your guidance regarding the above matter. Respectfully submitted.
　　　　　　　　　　Fifth day of the fourth month　　　　　　　　Matsudaira Hyōbu-no-taifu

Item: On the sixth day of the same month, a summons was issued by the Duty Councillor, Lord Sakai Uta-no-kami (Tadayuki), and the residential representative Tsuda Kuemon (Morihiro) attended. He was informed — via an attached note (o-fudasatsu) — that, regarding the inquiry submitted the previous day concerning Lord Karasumaru, the former Middle Counselor (Kōei), visiting him during his stay: the matter of calling upon him at his lodgings was to be handled at [the lord's] own discretion, but that the matter of extending an invitation [to his own residence] was unnecessary (i.e., should not be done).
