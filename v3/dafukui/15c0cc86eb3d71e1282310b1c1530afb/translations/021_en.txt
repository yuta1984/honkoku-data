Seventh day of the fourth month　　　　Matsudaira Hyōbu-no-taifu

After the above had been submitted, on the following eighth day, [the lord] was summoned to the same place, and a written notice (tsukefuda) was issued granting permission to proceed as one saw fit in both matters.

Item: On the eleventh day of the fourth month, as the Inspection Envoys (junken-shi) arrived in Fukui, the Chief Elders (karō) and officials went out to Mitsubashi-guchi to receive them. Messengers were dispatched to the lodgings of Lord Ōkubo Shichihyōe (Noribira) (lodging: Keishō Gorōemon's establishment), Lord Yamaoka Gorōsaku (Kageyuki) (lodging: Tenka-ya Shōzaemon's establishment), and Lord Tsukushi Uhyōe (Michikado) (lodging: Kanaya Yasuke's establishment): specifically, the Inspector (yoriai) Suganuma Kunai (Tsugitsune) (accompanied by the Town Magistrate Iwakami Kajidayū), Sakai Heima (Naritomo) (accompanied by the Inspector Nara Gonzaemon), and Makino Orinosuke (Morisato) (accompanied by the Vanguard Unit Commander Tsutsumi Shichidayū).

Item: On the twelfth day of the same month, as the Inspection Envoys departed Fukui, the Chief Elders and officials went out as far as the Kaga Gate exterior to see them off.

Item: On the sixteenth day of the same month, through the Senior Councillor (rōjū) Lord Sakai Uta-no-kami (Tadayuki), acting as special envoy (jōshi), permission to return to the home domain (o-itoma) was formally announced. [The lord] received the customary gifts of silver and a scroll.
　From the Great Retired Shōgun (Ōgosho-sama) as well, through Lord Uta-no-kami, ten scrolls were received as gifts.
　From the Middle Counselor (Dainagon-sama) as well, through the attending Senior Councillor Lord Nishio Oki-no-kami (Tadahisa), ten rolls of crepe silk (chirimen) were received — this being the first time such a gift had been bestowed. (The protocol for the special envoy was as customary.) In gratitude for the above, [the lord] made a round of courtesy calls (mawakin) to the Senior Councillors.

Item: On the seventeenth day of the same month, the following written notice (kirigami) arrived from the Duty Councillor, Lord Sakai Uta-no-kami (Tadayuki):

　　　　　　　Tomorrow, at the fifth half-hour (approximately 8:00 a.m.), you are to proceed to the castle and present your formal thanks for the permission to return home. Respectfully submitted.
　　　　　　　　　Seventeenth day of the fourth month　　　　　Honda Nakatsukasa-no-taifu
