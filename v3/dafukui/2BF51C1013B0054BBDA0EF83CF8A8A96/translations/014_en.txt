Distant Fires and Local Fires

1. June 25th, Hōreki 12 (Year of the Horse) - All the long houses at Kanazu Tea House were completely destroyed by fire. An assistant magistrate and one group member were immediately dispatched, and on the following 26th, Magistrate Sugiura Kōemon was sent. He returned immediately, and Kanazu Magistrate Arakawa Sōemon was ordered to suspend his duties from the 26th until the end of the month.

1. Dawn, 7:30 AM, March 10th, Meiwa 7 (Year of the Tiger) - When the Kanazu long houses caught fire and were destroyed, Sugiura Kōemon consulted with Duty Officer Inaba Uneme. This time, no magistrate was dispatched, but by consultation with the senior retainers, Assistant Magistrate Akao Sobē and two official group members were sent. Kanazu Magistrate Hiramoto Tōshichirō was suspended from duty, and orders were given as requested.

1. July 26th, Bunka 1 (Year of the Rat) - Great fire at Yoshizaki Bay, 170 houses. Official notice boards were taken out and stored, but all were destroyed by fire. Since this was also at the domain border and the checkpoint station was also destroyed, an assistant magistrate and group members were dispatched.

1. For fires in rural areas, the district magistrate reports to the monthly duty magistrate with a written document stating "this document shall be recorded in the general record book." House fires alone do not reach the lord's ears, but when there are injuries to people or horses, it should reach his ears. However, for great fires involving hundreds of houses or any extraordinary incidents, it should reach the lord's ears, and during the lord's absence years, reporting directly to the senior retainers follows the same procedure.

1. Late March, Hōreki 5 (Year of the Boar) - Regarding the great fire in Sabae, there were even reports that the lord's mansion was in danger, so there were deliberations about countermeasures, but since the fire was extinguished in the meantime, such measures were not necessary. At this time, two group members were dispatched to investigate the fire's origin.

1. April 17th, Hōreki 12 (Year of the Horse) - Group members reported that there were discussions about a great fire in Fuchū. When this matter was reported to the senior retainers, two magistrates were immediately dispatched to that location to investigate the fire's origin.

1. Past 7 AM on the 18th - From Kuranoske's residence, through Senior Retainer Uesaka Nakaemon, a private consultation was requested regarding the Fuchū great fire. This morning, there was a gathering of colleagues at Duty Officer Okabe Zōsuke's residence, and all fellow officials attended.
