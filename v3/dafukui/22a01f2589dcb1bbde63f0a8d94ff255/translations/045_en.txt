【Right page】
　Granted succession stipend during Lord Tsunamasa's reign in Tenna 1, year of the metal rooster (1681), and again
　summoned into service during Lord Yoshikuni's reign in Shōtoku 3, year of the water snake (1713)
　　Adopted son
　　Current head: Takamura Genshirō Norimori《Birth province: Echizen, Actual father: Fukui Shōzaemon Masashige》
　　　　After Norimori was adopted into Narifusa's family, Narifusa's biological son Suminari was born.
　　　　Therefore, regarding succession after Narifusa's death, since it concerned his biological son, Norimori petitioned that it should be
　　　　granted to Suminari. However, since Suminari was still young, it was ordered that Norimori
　　　　should be granted the succession stipend. Accordingly, Suminari was made an adopted son and kept in reserve.
　　　　Later, during Lord Yoshishina's reign, he was summoned as a page and served for many years, so in
　　　　Shōtoku 3, Norimori petitioned again that his stipend was ultimately the succession of Suminari's actual father Narifusa's
　　　　position, so he wished to transfer it. The petition was granted as requested, and
　　　　Norimori was separately granted rice stipend and support allowance and summoned into service. Regarding Norimori's actual father and
　　　　ancestors: Kamakura Samanosuke Ujikane, great-great-great-grandson of Lord Takauji, his son Fukui Shirō
　　　　zaemon-no-jō Mitsusada, and seven generations later Fukui Inaba-no-kami Sadamasa, lord of Aribe Castle in Tanba Province
【Left page】
　　　　His son Nagato-no-kami Masatsuna, both father and son, committed suicide at Aribe Castle in Tenshō 4, year of the fire mouse (1576) for Lord Nobunaga.
　　　　Masatsuna's son, Fukui Hyōbu Shigemasa, was summoned during Lord Tadamasa's
　　　　reign. His son, Fukui Shōzaemon Masashige, was the actual
　　　　father of the current Genshirō Norimori. Masashige's succession passed to his son Shōzaemon Masafusa, who was granted leave during Lord Mitsumichi's reign
　　　　and the line was discontinued.

　After being summoned in Genroku 10, year of the fire ox (1697) during Lord Yoshishina's reign,
　Norimori was granted a stipend in Shōtoku 3, year of the water snake (1713) during Lord Yoshikuni's reign
