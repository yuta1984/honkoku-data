　　　　　△　Kōzuki (clan)

　Summoned in Shōhō 1, Kinoe-saru (1644) during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　● Kōzuki Hachirozaemon Kagji《Home province: Harima, Birth province: Owari｜Surname: Gen (Minamoto)》
　　　　A ninth-generation descendant of Prince Tomohira, the seventh son of Emperor Murakami, and descendant of the Seisai Shōgun and Harima Province Governor
　　　　Suefusa《Akamatsu clan founder}}. From the fourth generation descendant Kōzuki Jirō Kagemori onward, they were successive castle lords within Kōzuki Sayō,
　　　　and his descendant Kōzuki Bungo-no-kami Shigekage held a domain of 70,000 koku. However,
　　　　after the fall of their home province, they retreated to Mihara in Bingo. His son, a man called Kōzuki Kyūsaburō Kageyoshi, served
　　　　Fukushima Saemon-dayū Masanori. Kagji was the second son of the aforementioned Kyūsaburō and received
　　　　an allocation of 1,000 koku. After Saemon-dayū's house fell, through the kind intentions of Lord Tōdō Daigaku-
　　　　no-kami, he was to be placed under the custody of Lord Ikoma Kobōshi and Lord Ikino-kami, but
　　　　was summoned by Lord Tadamasa through the agency of Hiraoka Ukon. Kagji's brothers entered service with Lord Hosokawa
　　　　Etchū-no-kami, and their descendants still serve the Hosokawa house today.
