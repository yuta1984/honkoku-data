　　　　severed, he was invited by Kyōgoku Wakasa-no-kami based on his Sasaki family lineage and
　　　　went to serve in Matsue, Unshū. After Wakasa-no-kami died in Kan'ei 14, all retainers became
　　　　rōnin, but in the same 18th year he was summoned to service during Lord Tadamasa's reign. Muneji's eldest
　　　　son, while living as a rōnin in Unshū, was summoned by Dewa-no-kami Naomasa,
　　　　and his descendants continue the line to this day.

　　　　Kansuke and went down to Hizen, where he performed some
　　　　meritorious service under Inoue Chikugo-no-kami, as has been passed down. In Keian 4, Kanoto-u year (1651) during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, for the
　　　　assistance with the Nikkō construction project, when his father Kan'emon went as construction magistrate,
　　　　he fell ill, so Yoshiyoshi petitioned to go and care for him. However, Kan'emon died of illness in Edo,

　　　　appointed to his father Kan'emon's former position and served in that role.
