【Left page】
　　　　　　△　Nakagawa

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　● Nakagawa Shōgen Naoshige《Original province: Harima　Birth province: Kawachi｜Surname: Minamoto》
　　　　The ancestor was Majima Yamato-no-kami, lord of Hirano Castle in Harima Province, who died in battle
　　　　at the Battle of Shimamura, having killed one called Shimamura. This Yamato-no-kami was a descendant
　　　　of Prince Tomohira, son of Emperor Murakami, eight generations later, and descendant of
　　　　Majima Jirō Hisauji, grandson of Akamatsu founder Sanmi-chūjō Suefusa by seven generations.
　　　　The third son of Yamato-no-kami was Majima Izumo-no-kami, lord of Hayashida Castle in Harima Province,
　　　　whose youthful name was Shinsuke. For warrior training,
　　　　he traveled through various provinces together with one called Kawasaki Miyawaki Han'nyū from Tsukushi, settled in Kawachi Province,
　　　　and later changed his title to Kodera. His son Nakagawa Izumo-no-kami Kazushige, whose original name was
　　　　Gentarō, served Lord Hideyasu and accompanied him from Yūki,
　　　　receiving the title Nakagawa. During Lord Tadanao's reign, due to his connection
　　　　to Lady Seiryōin, he was served with great favor, but due to certain circumstances, by the Shogun's order
　　　　he was placed under custody in Shinano Province. At that time, Shinano Province was under the domain of Lord Suruga Dainagon
