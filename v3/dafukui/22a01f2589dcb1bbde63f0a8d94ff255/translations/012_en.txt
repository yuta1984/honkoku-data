【Left page】
　　　　　　△　Nakagawa

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　● Nakagawa Shōgen Naoshige 《Home province: Harima　Birth province: Kawachi｜Surname: Minamoto》
　　　　His ancestor was the lord of Hirano Castle in Harima province, called Mashima Yamato-no-kami, who died in battle
　　　　at the Battle of Shimamura, reportedly killing one called Torimura. This Yamato-no-kami was a descendant
　　　　eight generations removed from Prince Tomohira, son of Emperor Murakami, and was a descendant of 
　　　　Mashima Jirō Hisauji, seven generations from Akamatsu's ancestor, Sanmi Chūjō Suefusa.
　　　　Yamato-no-kami's third son, lord of Hayashida Castle in Harima province, called Majima
　　　　Izumo-no-kami, whose youthful name was Shinsuke, traveled through various provinces
　　　　for warrior training together with one called Kawasaki Miyawaki Han'iri from Tsukushi, and
　　　　settled in Kawachi province. Later he changed his family name to Kodera. His son, Nakagawa Izumo-no-kami Kazushige (whose
　　　　original name was Gentarō), served Lord Hideyasu and accompanied him from Yūki.
　　　　He was granted the family name Nakagawa. During Lord Tadanao's reign, he continued to serve with special favor
　　　　due to his connection to Lady Seiryōin, but due to certain circumstances, by order of the Shogun's house,
　　　　he was placed under custody in Shinano province. At that time, Shinshū was under the domain of Lord Suruga Dainagon
