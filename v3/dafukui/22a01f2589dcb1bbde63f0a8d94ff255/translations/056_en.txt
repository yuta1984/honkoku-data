【Left page】
　　　　　　　△　Inoue

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　● Inoue Hachihei, personal name unknown 《Home province: Owari, Birth province unknown; Family name: Sugawara》
　　　　His ancestor, a man called Hori Sōemon, was the youngest son of Hori Kyūtarō Hidemasa. After Kyūtarō's
　　　　death, he was taken in by Kyōnenji temple in Mino province, a Ikkō sect temple that was related to the family,
　　　　and changed to his maternal family name Inoue. Hachihei came to this province because a man called Ino-
　　　　ue Magodayū, who served the domain, was a relative, and he was summoned during Lord Tadamasa's
　　　　reign. His son Inoue Yoshidayū inherited the stipend, and his son Gonpei was also granted succession
　　　　and served, but in Jōkyō 3, year of the Fire Tiger (1686), they were all granted leave, and the main line was severed.
