　　● Kawase Kan'emon Muneji《Both ancestral domain and birthplace are Ōmi Province, family name Gen, original name Sasaki》
　　　　Kawase Samanosuke Takafusa, a descendant of Tsushima-no-kami Ujinobu, the fourth son of Sasaki Nobutsuna, who was eleven generations from Emperor Uda,
　　　　built a new castle in Kawase-no-shō, Inukami District, Gōshū in Ōei 18 (1411) and
　　　　resided there. Due to this, he first changed his title to Kawase. He possessed
　　　　genealogical records transmitted from ancient times. Kawase Echizen-no-kami
　　　　Ieka, the castle lord of Kawase in Gōshū and great-great-grandson of Takafusa, belonged to the Rokkaku family and had numerous military achievements. Letters of commendation and
　　　　correspondence have been transmitted from the time of Sasaki Sadayori onwards. During the Battle of Echi River, he achieved the distinguished feat of taking three enemy heads himself, and
　　　　when this was verified, Shogun Yoshizumi showed his appreciation. At that time, there was a tea garden near the shogun's camp stool,
　　　　and in jest he took three tea seeds and said, "The number of heads from this achievement is like this—
