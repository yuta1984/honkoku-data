　　　　　　　△　Betsui

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign in Kan'ei 13, year of the Fire Rat (1636)
　　● Betsui Kihei Sadatoshi 《Home province and birth province both Kawachi; Family name unknown》
　　　　Sadatoshi served under Katō Kazue-no-kami Kiyomasa, received a stipend of 350 koku, and served as
　　　　construction magistrate. During the Korean campaigns, he also accompanied [his lord] and distinguished himself
　　　　in that land. As a reward, he was granted a wakizashi sword by Aishū Akihiro, which has been
　　　　passed down and is still possessed [by the family]. The commendation letter from that time, as well as
　　　　captured swords and Kiyomasa's vermillion seals were lost in a fire and are no longer possessed.
　　　　During the time of Katō Higo-no-kami Tadahiro, the house collapsed and all retainers became
　　　　masterless samurai. Afterwards, during Lord Tadamasa's reign, he was granted relief rice of 200 hyō
　　　　and stipend for ten men, and was summoned [to service].

　Granted succession during Lord Tadamasa's reign in Kan'ei 19, year of the Water Horse (1642)
　　　Betsui Hachirōbei Sadanao 《Birth province: Higo》
