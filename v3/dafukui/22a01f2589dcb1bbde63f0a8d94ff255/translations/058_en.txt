　　　　　　　△　Betsui

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign in Kan'ei 13, year of the Fire Rat (1636)
　　● Betsui Kihei Sadatoshi 《Home province and birth province both Kawachi; family name unknown》
　　　　Sadatoshi served under Katō Kazue-no-kami Kiyomasa, received a fief of 350 koku, and served as
　　　　construction magistrate (sakuji-bugyō). During the Korean campaign, he also accompanied [his lord] and
　　　　distinguished himself in that land. As a reward, he was granted a wakizashi by Sōshū Akihiro,
　　　　which has been passed down and is still possessed to this day. The commendation letter from that time, along with
　　　　a captured sword and Kiyomasa's vermillion seal, were lost in a fire and are no longer in possession.
　　　　When it came to the time of Katō Higo-no-kami Tadahiro, the house collapsed and all the retainers
　　　　became rōnin. Afterward, during Lord Tadamasa's reign, he was granted relief rice of 200 hyō
　　　　and stipends for ten men, and was summoned [to service].

　Granted succession during Lord Tadamasa's reign in Kan'ei 19, year of the Water Horse (1642)
　　　Betsui Hachirōbei Sadanao 《Birth province: Higo》
