【Left page】
　　　　　　　△　Nakayama

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign in the 2nd year of Genna, year of Fire Dragon (1616)
　　● Nakayama Umanojō, personal name unknown《Home province unknown, birth province Hitachi｜Family name unknown, original name Shirōemon》
　　　　When Umanojō was seventeen years old, during a battle at Tsuchiura in Shimōsa Province, he used a spear to pull in
　　　　a boat carrying over a hundred men and fought valiantly. At that time, he received a
　　　　letter of commendation from Lord Satake Shuridayū, and he still possesses the armor, spear, and other equipment he wore that day. The family crest was
　　　　originally bellflower, but because he achieved the greatest feat that day, the crest of a circle with the character "ichi" (one)
　　　　has been used since that time, according to family tradition. Initially he was with Tagaya Sakon, but
　　　　after Sakon's house was abolished, Umanojō's deeds came to His Lordship's attention, and after being summoned he became a banner magistrate.
　　　　Because his son Chūsuke was granted leave due to some incident, Umanojō's succession rights were granted to his legitimate grandson Tōemon,
　　　　but during his grandson Bunshichirō's generation, in the 3rd year of Jōkyō, year of Fire Tiger (1686), they were granted leave along with others,
　　　　and the main family line was discontinued.
