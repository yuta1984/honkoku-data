【Left page】
　　　　　　△　Amemori

　Summoned in the 3rd year of Kan'ei, Hinoe-tora (1626) during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　● Komori Shōbei (given name unknown)《Home province: Ōmi, Birth province: Echigo｜Surname unknown, Original name: Amemori》
　　　　An ancestor named Amemori Hyōgo-no-kami resided in Ōmi Province for generations, held a domain of fifteen
　　　　thousand koku and served under Asai Bizen-no-kami. After the fall of the Asai house, he went
　　　　to Echigo Province and served Murakami Suō-no-kami Tadakatsu, receiving a stipend of 350 koku,
　　　　for which the vermillion seal still exists. His son Amemori Giemon also served Suō-no-kami, and later
　　　　served Hori Tango-no-kami, receiving a stipend of 350 koku, for which the vermillion seal still exists.
　　　　His son Komori Shōbei took leave from Tango-no-kami's service, came to this province, and was summoned to serve during Lord Tadamasa's
　　　　reign. During the Shimabara Rebellion in Hizen Province in the 14th year of Kan'ei (1637), he was
　　　　dispatched as an envoy to Lord Mizuno Hyūga-no-kami together with Takayanagi Jintayū, fought in Hyūga-no-kami's formation,
　　　　and returned after the fall of Shimabara in March of the following year. Regarding his military service at that time,
　　　　Lord Hyūga-no-kami reportedly sent commendation in writing. Furthermore, there are documents of mutual certificates of battlefield service exchanged with the retainers of Lords Hyūga-no-kami and Mimasaku-no-kami,
　　　　father and son.
