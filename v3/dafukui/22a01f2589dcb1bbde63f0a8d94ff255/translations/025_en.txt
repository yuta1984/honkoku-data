　　　　He was added to the besieging forces of Tanabe Castle in Tango by Lord Hideyori and proceeded there. On that route,
　　　　he captured a fortress castle called Nakayama and immediately proceeded to Tanabe. At that time,
　　　　a letter of commendation arrived from Osaka. This too is still preserved to this day. Tadaoki was
　　　　accompanying Lord Gongen (Tokugawa Ieyasu) on his journey to the Kantō region, while his elderly father Yūsai was
　　　　holed up in Tanabe Castle. However, due to the Emperor's intentions, all the besieging forces retreated. After the subsequent
　　　　suppression of the rebels, following Tadaoki's petition, the besiegers of Tanabe Castle were punished, or had their
　　　　domains confiscated, with various punishments imposed. Among these, regarding Kawachi-no-kami, because his father-in-law Nishio Bungo-
　　　　no-kami had shown loyalty, it was declared that he would be placed under Bungo-no-kami's custody. The official letter
　　　　granted to Bungo-no-kami from the Kantō at that time is still in possession today. Later, Kawachi-no-kami was assigned to
　　　　the Chikuzen Chūnagon with 5,000 hyō of support rice, fifty mounted warriors, and thirty
　　　　foot soldiers, and was appointed as a unit commander. However, when Chūnagon
　　　　died, Inaba Sado-no-kami later kindly took care of Kawachi-no-kami's affairs,
　　　　but he also died of illness. However, since there was a dying wish left to two retainers, chief retainer Tanabe Gondayū and Matsubara
　　　　Gozaemon, they consulted with Sugita Gorobē who was in service to the house,
　　　　and he was summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign.
