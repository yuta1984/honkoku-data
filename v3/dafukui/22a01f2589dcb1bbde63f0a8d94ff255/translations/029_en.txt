　　　　　　△　Oka

　Summoned in Kan'ei 15, Tsuchinoe-tora (1638) during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　●　Oka Hachirōbei Hisanori《Home province and birth province both Bizen｜Surname Fujiwara, original name Togawa》
　　　　　Hisanori's grandfather was called Oka Echizen-no-kami and was a retainer of Lord Ukita Chūnagon Hideie.
　　　　　He died at Busan Bay during the Korean campaign in Bunroku 1, Mizunoe-tatsu (1592). His son
　　　　　Echizen succeeded to the family headship, but due to certain circumstances, Hideie's chief retainers were placed under custody in various locations.
　　　　　As for Echizen, he was placed under Masuda Uemonnosuke's custody, and after the Ukita domain was abolished, Echizen's son
　　　　　Hisanori stayed with Lord Tsugaru Etchū-no-kami for a while before being summoned to service in our domain
　　　　　　Among the Ukita chief retainers, Togawa Higo-no-kami and Hanabusa Shima-no-kami were
　　　　　　summoned by Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu). Higo-no-kami was said to be the elder brother of the former Oka Echizen-no-kami
