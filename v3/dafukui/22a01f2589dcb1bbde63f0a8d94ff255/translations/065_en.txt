　　　　　　　△　Katō

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, 15th year of Kan'ei, year of Earth Tiger (1638), summoned to service
　　● Katō Shozaemon Shigenao《Home province: Mino, Birth province: Higo｜Family name: Fujiwara, Original name: Heima》
　　　　His ancestor was Katō Unosuke Shigenari, younger brother of Katō Higo-no-kami Kiyomasa's father, 
　　　　Danjō Uemon Hyōe Kiyotada. Shigenari's son, Katō Yozaemon Shigetsugu, served Katō Kiyomasa,
　　　　receiving a stipend of 17,650 koku and being entrusted with Sashiki Castle in Higo Province.
　　　　In the same province, when battling Amakusa Izu-no-kami, he achieved the honor of being first to charge, and during the Korean campaigns,
　　　　he served as a commanding officer of Kiyomasa's advance guard second unit. During the Battle of Sekigahara, he defended Sashiki Castle
　　　　and earned great honor. After Kiyomasa's death, when his son Higo-no-kami Tadahiro made his formal visit to Edo and
　　　　had an audience with Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), Yozaemon was also summoned before him,
　　　　received his gracious words, and was granted the name Yamato, it is said. His son, Katō Uchi-
　　　　kurasuke Shigemasa, served Katō Higo-no-kami Tadahiro, receiving a stipend of 1,030 koku and
　　　　serving as head page. When Higo-no-kami Tadahiro's house was abolished and he became a rōnin,
　　　　through the good offices of Mizuno Hyūga-no-kami Katsunari, in the 15th year of Kan'ei, year of Earth Tiger (1638)—
