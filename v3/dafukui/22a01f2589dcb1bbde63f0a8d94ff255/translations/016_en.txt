【Left page】
　　　　　　△　Yamada

　Summoned during Lord Tadamasa's reign, Kan'ei 2, Kinoto-ushi year (1625)
　　● Yamada Jirōdayū Fusayoshi《Original province: Mino, Birth province: Ise｜Surname: Minamoto, Original name: Yasuuji》
　　　　The ancestor was Toki Danjō Shōhitsu Ujimasa, lord of Gujō Castle in Mino Province. When the Toki family was destroyed,
　　　　Ujimasa's son Toki Rokurōbei Masakata was still young, so he retreated to Yamada-shō in Ise Province.
　　　　Later, Masakata died of illness in Yamada. Masakata's son Jirōdayū Masafusa was born in
　　　　Yamada-shō, so he changed his family name to Yamada. Later, before the Osaka campaigns,
　　　　he was summoned by Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) and accompanied him to Osaka, where at Yao he killed
　　　　a Chōsokabe retainer named Kae Kurando and achieved other military exploits. After
　　　　returning from the campaign, he served with particular favor, but Jirōdayū
　　　　mistook someone else while trying to avenge his brother and killed four members of Taitoku-in-sama's (Tokugawa Hidetada's) guard unit.
　　　　For this he should have committed seppuku, but Gongen-sama secretly gave him word to
　　　　flee wherever he could. He immediately fled and hid here and there among his relatives.
