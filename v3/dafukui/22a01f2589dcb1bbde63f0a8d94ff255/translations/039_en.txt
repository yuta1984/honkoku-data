　　　　　　　　△　Takasaka

　Summoned in Echizen during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　● Takasaka Kin'emon, personal name unknown《Original province: Ōmi, Born in same province, Surname: Minamoto, Original name: Uesaka》
　　　　There is a written record stating that he was a descendant of one called Uesaka Gontarō Moretada, 
　　　　the adopted son of Sasaki Saburō Moritsuna from ancient times. Kin'emon's father was Uesaka Mizu-no-kami 
　　　　Tsunafuyu, the lord of Uesaka Castle in Gōshū. He reportedly held domain over Uesaka, Tatsumi, Yanase, Kimoto, Shimosaka, 
　　　　Shimoe, Yuge, Suemura and other villages within the northern district of Gōshū.

　Summoned in Kan'ei 20, Mizunoto-hitsuji year (1643) during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　　　Takasaka Buemon Shigeyoshi《Born in Echizen Province》
　　　　His father Kin'emon's headship was inherited by the eldest son Kin'emon Toshihide, but while
　　　　serving as an attendant to Lord Maskatsu and working in Matsuoka, he became ill and
　　　　requested leave, becoming a rōnin.
