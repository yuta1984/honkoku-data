　　　　　　△　Hori

　In the 8th year of Kan'ei, Kanoto-hitsuji (1631) during Lord Tadamasa's reign
　Summoned
　　●  Hori Jūbei Nobutomo《Home province: Mino, Birth province: Iga｜Surname: Fujiwara》
　He is a descendant of Hori Gon-no-tayū Suetaka, who was the eighth-generation grandson of the ancestor General Toshihito《Family tree exists》. Nobutomo's
　great-grandfather Hori Kambu-tayū served under Saitō Yamashiro-no-kami in Mino Province, held the territory of Kami-Shimo
　Akanabe village in Atsumi District, and achieved military merits repeatedly. Kambu-tayū's eldest son Hori Tarōzaemon-
　no-jō Hideshige served Lords Nobunaga and Hideyoshi, and later also served Gongen-sama (Lord Ieyasu). After
　the Battle of Sekigahara, he was granted 10,400 koku in Echigo Province. Hideshige's grandson Hori Saemon-
　no-kami Hideharu received the entire province of Echigo in the 3rd year of Keichō, Tsuchinoe-inu (1598), and his son Echigo-no-kami Tadatoshi
　inherited the family headship. However, in the 15th year of Keichō, Kanoe-inu (1610), he was ordered to be stripped of his domain, and the entire clan scattered.
　Kambu-tayū's second son Hori Gonnosuke Shigenobu became a monk due to illness and stayed with Hori
　Kyūtarō Hidemasa in Echizen. Shigenobu's son Hori Iori Nobuie《Birth province: Ōmi｜Father of Jūbei Nobutomo》 moved from Echizen to
　Echigo Province, where《Under the direction of the Saemon-no-kami in Echigo｜he married the younger sister of Kyūtarō Hidemasa》. After the Hori house was abolished, Nobuie
　served Lord Tōdō Izumi-no-kami《He was granted a stipend of 1,500 koku and resided in Iga Province, where his descendants still exist｜His activities during the Osaka campaigns are also recorded in the Namba-ki》. Nobuie
