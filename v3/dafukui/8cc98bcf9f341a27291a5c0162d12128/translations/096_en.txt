Those summoned to Fukui during the previous reign before Lord Yoshishina

　　　　△　Kuwayama

Summoned in the fourth year of Enpō, year of the Fire Dragon (1676) during Lord Yoshishina's reign
　　●　Kuwayama Sōan Masasuke 《margin note: home province Owari, birth province Yamashiro｜secular name Kuwayama Jūzaemon》
　　　　His ancestor, Kuwayama Shuri-dayū Junior Fifth Rank Lower Fujiwara no Shigeharu, after taking Buddhist vows became known as
　　　　Kahōin Jibu-kyō Hōin Sōei. He served Lord Hideyoshi, and when fighting Shibata Katsuie in northern Ōmi,
　　　　Shuri-dayū was defending a castle in the Shizudake area. Later he served
　　　　Yamato Dainagon Hidenaga, and during the attack on Senoo Castle in Mino Province, Shigeharu led the vanguard.
　　　　Also, when attacking mountain castles in Kii Province, Shigeharu achieved military distinction by taking enemy heads, and received a commendation
　　　　from Lord Hidenaga. Later he moved to Wakayama Castle in Kii Province and was granted a fief of 30,000 koku. In the fourth year of Bunroku
　　　　(1595), during the conspiracy of Kampaku Hidetsugu, Hideyoshi assigned the Hōin
　　　　to guard the main gate of Fushimi Castle. This service pleased Hideyoshi and he was granted a fief of 10,000 koku.
　　　　He also held lands in Izumi Province. In the fifth year of Keichō, year of the Metal Rat (1600), during Ishida Mitsunari's rebellion,
　　　　Tokugawa Ieyasu sent official letters to the Hōin and his son Kuwayama Shuri-dayū Kazuharu,
　　　　ordering them to defend Wakayama Castle in Kii Province. His son Kuwayama Ichizaemon Shigemasa
