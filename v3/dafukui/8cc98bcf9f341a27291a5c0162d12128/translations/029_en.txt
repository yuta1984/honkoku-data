　　　　　△　Matsuo
In the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, summoned to service in the 2nd year of Jōō, Mizunoto-Mi year (1653).
　●Matsuo Shinsuke Kiyonao《Note: Home province Yamato　Birth province Echizen｜Family name unknown》
　　His ancestor, a man called Sakoma Iga-no-kami, held a position worth over 20,000 koku in Yamato Province, but died of illness during the Korean campaign. Kiyonao's father, Matsuo Rokurōemon Kiyosato, served under Sugita Gorōbei as a rōnin retainer receiving a stipend of 170 koku. His eldest son, called Matsuo Kinzaemon, also inherited this position and subsequently served as a yoriki (assistant) in Gorōbei's group. Kiyonao was Kinzaemon's younger brother.

In the reign of Lord Yoshishina, granted inherited succession in the 12th year of Genroku, Tsuchinoto-Uma year (1699)
