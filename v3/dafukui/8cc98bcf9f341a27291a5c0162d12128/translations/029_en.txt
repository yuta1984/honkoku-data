In the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the second year of Jōō, Mizunoto-Mi year (1653),
he was summoned to service.
　　●Matsuo Shinsuke Kiyonao《Note: Home province Yamato, Birth province Echizen｜Family name unknown》
　　　　An ancestor named Hasama Iga-no-kami held a position worth over 20,000 koku in Yamato Province, but died of illness during the Korean campaign. Kiyonao's father, Matsuo Rokurōemon Kiyosato, served under Sugita Gorōbei as a masterless samurai (rōnin) receiving a stipend of 170 koku. His eldest son, called Matsuo Kinzaemon, also succeeded him in service and later served as a yoriki (assistant) in Gorōbei's unit. Kiyonao was Kinzaemon's younger brother.

In the reign of Lord Yoshishina, in the twelfth year of Genroku, Tsuchinoto-Uma year (1699), he was granted succession.
