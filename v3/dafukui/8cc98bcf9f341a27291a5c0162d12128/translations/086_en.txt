When Lord Yoshishina was at Yoshie, summoned in the 2nd year of Meireki, year of the Fire Monkey (1656)
　●　Shimokawa Yajirōemon Masanaga (home province unknown, birth province: Echizen, clan unknown)
　　　　His ancestor, a man called Shimokawa Kinzaemon, served during Lord Hideyasu's reign and went as an attendant to
　　　　Lord Matsudaira Dewa-no-kami. His son, a man called Shimokawa Kinzaemon Nagasho, was granted three
　　　　hundred koku and served, but later requested leave and became a physician, calling himself Shimokawa Jūan.
　　　　While he was in Kyoto, he was summoned by Lord Matsudaira Tajima-no-kami and granted one hundred hyō of rice and
　　　　stipend for ten men. Later he requested leave and became a rōnin. His son, Shimokawa
　　　　Yajirōemon Masanaga, became a rōnin together with his father, and later was in Kanazu in this province, after which
　　　　he was summoned
