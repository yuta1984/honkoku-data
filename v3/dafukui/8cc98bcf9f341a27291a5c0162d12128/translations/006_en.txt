　●　Murakami Santadayū Masamine《marginal note: original province Tanba, native province Musashi | family name Minamoto》
　　His ancestors were Murakami Shinano-no-kami, his son Jirōzaemon, and his son Murakami Uhyōe Yoshimasa. In the 2nd year of Genroku (1599), he served Lord Hideaki, Middle Counselor of Chikuzen, and in the 7th month of the 5th year of Keichō, Year of the Metal Rat (1600), during the attack on Fushimi Castle, when fire arrows were shot from within the castle, Matsuno Shume and Murakami Uhyōe Yoshimasa rushed to extinguish them. Lord Hideaki was so pleased with their achievement that he bestowed upon them a sword of Iemitsu.
　　At the moment of the castle's fall, Uhyōe Yoshimasa was the first to charge in at Ebisu with a banner bearing a crane's antler design, and at this time he received a commendation letter, which is still held by the Murakami Sanemon family. In the 9th month of the same year, during the Battle of Sekigahara, when Lord Hideaki's vanguard was routed by Ōtani Gyōbu's forces, Yoshimasa held his ground, restrained his allies, and drove back the enemy. Lord Hideaki was so impressed that after returning from the campaign, he appointed him as commander of fifty mounted warriors of the vanguard and one thousand arquebusiers. After Lord Hideaki's death, he was summoned by Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) and granted a thousand koku stipend, and just five days later was granted an additional five hundred koku, for a total of fifteen hundred koku with a vermillion seal certificate.
