△ Murakami
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in the 12th year of Kanbun (1672, Year of the Water Rat), summoned to service:
●Murakami Santayu Masamine《Original domain: Tanba Province, birthplace: Musashi Province, family name: Minamoto》
　His ancestor was Murakami Shinano-no-kami, his son Jiro Zaemon, and his son Murakami Uhyoe Yoshimasa, who in the 2nd year of Genroku served Lord Chikuzen Chunagon Hideaki. In the 5th year of Keicho (1600, Year of the Metal Rat), in the seventh month, during the siege of Fushimi Castle, when fire arrows were shot from within the castle, Matsuno Shuma and Murakami Uhyoe Yoshimasa rushed to extinguish the fires, and Lord Hideaki was so pleased that he bestowed a sword of Iemitsu. At the moment of the castle's fall, Uhyoe Yoshimasa was the first to charge in with an Ebisu-tsuru banner with horn-shaped paper insignia, and at this time received a commendation. The Murakami Sanemon family still possesses it today. In the same year, ninth month, during the Battle of Sekigahara, when Otani Gyobu's forces attempted to rout Lord Hideaki's vanguard, Yoshimasa held firm, restrained his allies, and repelled the enemy, which so impressed Lord Hideaki that after returning from the campaign, he was appointed head of fifty mounted samurai of the vanguard and one thousand arquebusiers. After Lord Hideaki's death, he was summoned by Lord Gongen (Tokugawa Ieyasu) and granted one thousand koku, and after merely five days, was granted an additional five hundred koku, receiving a vermillion seal for a total of fifteen hundred koku.
