　　　　　△　Samukae (Sagae)

Summoned to service in the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the first year of Meireki, Kinoto-Hitsuji year (1655)
　●Samukae Jinzaemon Nobutada《Note: Home province Dewa, birth province same｜Family name Ōe》
　　Regarding his ancestors, from Masafusa, the Provisional Middle Councilor who was ten generations from Emperor Heizei, and ten generations from Sagae Ōkura-shōyu Tokiuji, the Ōshū kanrei (deputy governor of northern Japan), came Sagae Kunai-shōyu Takashige, who became a senior retainer of the Mogami family. His son was Sagae Yamato Hiroshige, his son was Yamato Hiroaki, and his second son was Kenmotsu Chikatoshi, who was Nobutada's father. For generations as stated above, they served the Mogami family, witnessing until the time when Mogami Suruga-no-kami's fortunes collapsed and his castle lands were confiscated. Afterward, he served Lord Matsudaira Chikuzen-no-kami for a while. At that time, the Shimabara Rebellion broke out in Hizen Province, so he went with Lord Chikuzen-no-kami's forces. On the twenty-seventh day of the second month, during the castle assault, he climbed over the fence at Ōe-guchi, mounted the stone walls of the western main gate's honmaru quarters, and engaged in spear combat at the fence. During the fighting, he was repeatedly knocked down by stones, his battle standards were broken, and he received multiple stone wounds. After fighting all day, as evening approached, orders came to withdraw the troops, so they retreated. At that time, those who had fought exchanged words with each other and exchanged certificates of proof.
