He exchanged words with the hatamoto messenger Honda San'ya and obtained proof, swiftly reporting the details of his military service
　　　to the higher authorities. Afterwards, he was summoned before both Generals and received sake cups,
　　　two commendation letters, a horse, and gold. Furthermore, when the Gongen-sama rose from his seat,
　　　the gracious words from above were: "He thrust the first spear, and particularly his fighting on that occasion, especially killing
　　　a single commander and taking his head, plus his seven days of fighting - all are without comparison." Honda San'ya
　　　reported these details thoroughly to the higher authorities, who were greatly pleased. At that time, Lord Doi
　　　Ōi-no-kami and Lord Aoyama Ōkura were waiting in the next room and heard these circumstances, saying "The present
　　　words from above are not ordinary matters, but accord with a warrior's divine favor," and expressed their envy.
　　　It is said that Chitaka was offered sake and drank it. His son Hatta Kinemon Chi-
　　　aki was the third son of the aforementioned Chitaka. During the Kan'ei years, he entered service under Ii Yukie-no-suke Naotsugu,
　　　and when Naotsugu went into seclusion at Hyakusaiji Temple in Ōmi Province, he served faithfully throughout. After his master's death,
　　　he became a masterless samurai, but was summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's reign.

During Lord Yoshishina's reign, he was granted succession in the 3rd year of Jōkyō (1686), year of the Fire Tiger

　　　Regarding the matter of ancestral military achievements, during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, there was an inquiry made to
　　　grandfather Kinemon Chiaki, and he submitted a written report. Later, when Lord Ii Kambu-no-kami received an official inquiry from
　　　the government about the Osaka campaigns, those with the same family name residing in Hikone
　　　submitted written accounts of their ancestors' military service, as we have heard. The commendation letters and other old documentary evidence
　　　are preserved by the main family residing in Hikone, so we only possess copies.
