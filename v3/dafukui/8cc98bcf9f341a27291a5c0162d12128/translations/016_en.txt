He exchanged words with Hatamoto Honda Sanpei, who served as messenger, to obtain proof, and quickly reported the details of his military service to the higher authorities. Afterward, he was summoned before both Shogun lords and received a ceremonial sake cup, two letters of commendation, a horse, and gold. Furthermore, Lord Gongen (Ieyasu) rose from his seat and gave gracious words to the effect that "combining the first spear thrust, particularly the fighting on that occasion, especially killing and taking the head of a general of one year's standing, plus seven days of fighting - all of this is without parallel." Honda Sanpei reported these details thoroughly to the higher authorities, who were deeply moved. At that time, Lord Doi Ōi-no-kami and Lord Aoyama Ōkura were waiting in the next room. Upon hearing these circumstances, they said "the purport of the current imperial words is not ordinary but befits the divine favor of a warrior," and enviously requested sake from Tomotaka and drank it. It is said that his son Hatta Kinemon Tomo[character unclear] was the third son of the aforementioned Tomotaka. During the Kan'ei era, he served under Ii Yukie-no-suke Naoshige, and when Naoshige went into seclusion at Hyakusai-ji temple in Ōmi Province, he served him faithfully throughout. After Naoshige's death, he became a rōnin, but was summoned to service during Lord Mitsumichi's reign.

　　Hatta Kinemon Eiji (born in Ōmi Province, Buddhist name Kinchū. Actual father was Tomoaki's elder brother, Hatta Sakon-emon Tomona, lay priest name Suisei)
　Regarding ancestral military achievements, during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, there was an inquiry to grandfather Kinemon Tomoaki, and a written statement was submitted accordingly. Later, when there was an official inquiry to Lord Ii Kamon-no-kami regarding matters from the Osaka campaigns, written statements about ancestral military service were submitted by relatives of the same name residing in Hikone, as we understand. The letters of commendation and other ancient proof items are preserved by the main family line in Hikone, so we possess only copies.
