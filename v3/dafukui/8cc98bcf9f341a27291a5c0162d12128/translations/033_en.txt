　　　　　Noji
Summoned to service during the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the 8th year of Kanbun, year of the Earth Monkey (1668).
　●Noji Jokan Muneeki《Note: Home province Echizen, Birth province Yamashiro｜Family name unknown》
　　　Muneeki's great-grandfather, called Noji Munehachi, served Asakura Yoshikage. During that time, he distinguished himself in the Battle of Motooriguchi in Nomi District, Kaga Province, and possessed a letter of commendation from Yoshikage. After Yoshikage's downfall, when Kanamori Hōin established his castle at Ōno in this province, he served as chief retainer. His son Noji Shichirōemon Munenaka remained in the Kanamori family, and when Hōin retired to Kyoto, he accompanied him there. His son called Noji Joun was Jokan's father and resided in Kyoto.

During the reign of Lord Yoshishina, in the 15th year of Genroku, year of the Water Horse (1702), he was granted succession.
　Current generation Noji Munehachi Muneaki《Note: Birth province Echizen》
