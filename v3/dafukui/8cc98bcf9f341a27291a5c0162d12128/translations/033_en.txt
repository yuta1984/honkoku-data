　　　　　Noji
Summoned to service during the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the 8th year of Kanbun, year of the Earth Monkey (1668).
　●Noji Jokan Sōeki《Note: Home province Echizen, Birth province Yamashiro｜Family name unknown》
　　Sōeki's great-grandfather, called Noji Sōhachi, served Asakura Yoshikage. At that time, he distinguished himself in the battle of Honoriguchi in Nomi District, Kaga Province, and possessed a commendation letter from Yoshikage. After Yoshikage's downfall, when Kanamori Hōin established his castle at Ōno in this province, he served as chief retainer. His son Noji Shichirōemon Munanaka remained with the Kanamori family, and when the Hōin retired to Kyoto, he accompanied him there. His son, called Noji Joun, was Jokan's father and resided in Kyoto.
During the reign of Lord Yoshishina, in the 15th year of Genroku, year of the Water Horse (1702), he was granted succession.
　Current generation Noji Sōhachi Sōmei《Note: Birth province Echizen》
