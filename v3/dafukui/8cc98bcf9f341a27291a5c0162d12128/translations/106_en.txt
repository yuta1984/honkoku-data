At the time of the handover, when Izu-no-kami employed various retainers, Tōbei became the chief commander (sōgashira), led troops, and carried out the castle handover. On that occasion, he received a letter of gratitude from Yamato-no-kami-sama, which is still in possession today. His son Tōbei Sadamasa served as chief retainer (karō) during Magotarō's time. He was the father of Sadaemon Sadatake.

△ Sakanoue
Summoned in the era of Lord Tsunamasa, in the 6th year of Enpō, year of the Earth Horse (1678):
● Sakanoue Matahei Takemitsu <<Original province: Unknown, Born in: Kaga | Surname: Fujiwara>>

Regarding his ancestors, it is said they were descendants of the ancient Sakanoue no Tamuramaro, and the genealogy shows succession from one called Sakanoue Bingo-no-kami Yoshimasa. Matahei Takemitsu's father, called Sakanoue Zenemon Yoshihiro, received a stipend of 250 koku from Lord Matsudaira Hizen-no-kami and served in construction work (sakuji-kata) in the page group (koshō-gumi). It was a family with hereditary carpenter's square transmission. When Takemitsu was eighteen years old, he got into a fight, but handled it well. Hizen-no-kami declared: "Though you are young, your performance was outstanding. There is an opponent involved, but I will spare your life, so depart to anywhere you wish." This was conveyed to his father Zenemon. Accordingly, when he came to this province, he was summoned by Honda Kuranoske and granted a stipend of 100 koku. However, desiring direct service to the lord, he took leave and remained as a rōnin, until he was summoned in Lord Tsunamasa's era.
