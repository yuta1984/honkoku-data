At the time of the castle transfer, since Izu-no-kami employed various retainers, Tōbei became the chief commander, brought his men, and carried out the castle handover. On that occasion, he received a letter of gratitude from Yamato-no-kami-sama, which he still possesses today. His son Tōbei Sadamasa served as acting chief retainer under Magotarō. He was the father of Sadaemon Sadatake.

In the era of Lord Yoshikuni, he was granted succession in the 2nd year of Shōtoku, year of the Water Dragon (1712)

△ Sakaue
Summoned in the era of Lord Tsunamasa, in the 6th year of Enpō, year of the Earth Horse (1678)
● Sakaue Matahei Takemitsu <<Original province: Unknown, Born in: Kaga | Surname: Fujiwara>>

Regarding his ancestors, according to the genealogy, he descended from the ancient Sakaue Tamura-maru lineage, continuing from one called Sakaue Bingo-no-kami Yoshimasa. Matahei Takemitsu's father, called Sakaue Zenemon Yoshihiro, received a stipend of 250 koku from Lord Matsudaira Hizen-no-kami and served as acting construction supervisor in the page corps. This was a family with hereditary carpentry skills. When Takemitsu was eighteen years old, he got into a fight, but handled it well. Hizen-no-kami declared: "Though you are young, your performance was outstanding. There is the matter of your opponent, but I will spare your life, so depart to wherever you wish," and handed him over to his father Zenemon. Consequently, when he came to this province, he was summoned by Honda Kuranosuke and granted a 100-koku stipend. However, wishing for direct service [to the lord], he took leave and was residing [elsewhere] when he was summoned in Lord Tsunamasa's era.
