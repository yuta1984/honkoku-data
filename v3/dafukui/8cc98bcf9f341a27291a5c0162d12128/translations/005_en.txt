△ Hanaki
Summoned to service during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in the 5th year of Kanbun, year of the Wood Snake
○Hanaki Geki Yoshitoki《Original domain: Ōmi, Birthplace: unknown｜Family name: Minamoto》
　His ancestor was a descendant in the ninth generation of Emperor Uda, being the twelfth generation from Rokurō Zaemon-no-jō Shinji, who was the fourth generation descendant of Sasaki Tarō Sadatsuna《possesses a genealogical chart showing blood lineage succession》. In the first year of Bun'ō, second month, Shinji first took the surname Hanaki by command of Tokiyori Nyūdō Saimyōji and adopted a fan with flowers as the family crest. Yoshitoki's father, Geki-no-shin Yoshishige, served Lord Hotta Kaga-no-kami, and Yoshitoki also served as chief retainer during the time of the same Kōzuke-no-suke. After Kōzuke-no-suke's downfall, he withdrew to Hino-no-shō in Ōmi Province. Having connections with Lady Seichiin, he petitioned for service, and since there were also people among the Edo hatamoto who could vouch for him, he was summoned to service and granted his original stipend the following year.
