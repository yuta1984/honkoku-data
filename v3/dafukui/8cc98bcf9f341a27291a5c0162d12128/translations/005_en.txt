　　　　△　Hanaki
Summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in the 5th year of Kanbun, Year of the Wood Snake (1665)
　○Hanaki Geki Yoshitoki《marginal note: original province Ōmi, native province unknown | family name Minamoto》
　　　His ancestor was a descendant of the ninth generation from Emperor Uda, the fourth generation from Sasaki Tarō Sadatsuna, namely Rokurō Zaemon-no-jō Nobuji, and Yoshitoki is the twelfth generation from Nobuji《marginal note: he possesses the genealogical record of blood succession｜in his home province》. In the 1st year of Bun'ō (1260), February, by command of the lay priest Tokiyori Saimyōji, Nobuji first took the surname Hanaki and adopted a flower on a fan as the family crest. Yoshitoki's father, Geki-no-shin Yoshishige, served Lord Hotta Kaga-no-kami, and Yoshitoki also served in a senior retainer capacity during the time of the same Kōzuke-no-suke. After Kōzuke-no-suke's downfall, he retreated to Hino-no-shō in Ōmi Province, where he petitioned about his connection to Lady Seichiiin, and furthermore, since there were people among the Edo hatamoto who possessed [records of this connection], he was summoned to service and granted his original stipend the following year.
