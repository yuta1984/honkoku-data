△ Hanaki
Summoned to service during Lord Mitsumichi's reign in the 5th year of Kanbun (1665, Year of the Wood Snake)
○Hanaki Geki Yoshitoki《Original domain: Omi Province, birthplace unknown, family name: Minamoto》
　His ancestors were descendants of Emperor Uda in the ninth generation, and from Sasaki Taro Sadatsuna's fourth generation descendant Rokuro Zaemon-no-jo Nobuji to Yoshitoki spans twelve generations《possessing a bloodline succession genealogy chart》. In the first year of Bunno (1260), in the second month, Nobuji, by command of Tokiyori Nyudo Saimyoji, first adopted the title Hanaki and used a fan with flowers as the family crest. Yoshitoki's father, Geki-no-shin Yoshishige, served Lord Hotta Kaga-no-kami, and Yoshitoki also served as acting chief retainer during the time of the same Kozuke-no-suke. After Kozuke-no-suke's downfall, he withdrew to Hino-sho in Omi Province, where he petitioned based on his connections to Lady Seichiiin, and furthermore, since there were people among the Edo hatamoto who possessed [relevant credentials], he was summoned to service and granted his original stipend the following year.
