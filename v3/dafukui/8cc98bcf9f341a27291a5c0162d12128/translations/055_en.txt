Among those who were summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's reign and later dismissed in subsequent reigns, but were summoned back again

　　　△　Kumagai
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, Meireki 1, year of kinoto-hitsuji (1655)
Summoned
　●Kumagai Kohyōe Masamoto《Note: Home province Ōmi, birth province Yamashiro｜Surname Minamoto, original name Sasaki》
　　His ancestor was the eldest son of Sasaki Danjō-shōhitsu Sadayori, a descendant of Emperor Uda - Sasaki Sakyō-dayū Yoshikata, priest name Bakkaisai Jōtei. He received orders from Ōmi-no-kami Sasaki Yoshihide as a branch family member, was entrusted with the yakata title, became a guardian, and managed neighboring provinces. His son Sasaki Yoshirō Masatsugu allied with Akechi Hyūga-no-kami Mitsuhide upon his request and died in battle at Tennōji. His son Kumagai Hayato-no-shō Yoshiyuki was separated from his father Masatsugu in his childhood, and changed his title to Kumagai when he reached adulthood. Later he was summoned by Lord Hijikata Kawachi-no-kami, served as chief retainer, and also went to the Osaka campaigns. In his old age, before Lord Hijikata's house fell, he withdrew and lived in Kyoto, dying as a rōnin. His son Kumagai Kohyōe Masamoto served Oda Nobuo (priest name Jōshin) in his youth, later served Lord Sakai Utanokami, then served Lord Matsudaira Mutsu-no-kami.
