　　　△　Kumagai
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, Meireki 1, year of kinoto-hitsuji (1655)
Summoned
　●Kumagai Kohyōe Masamoto《Note: Home province Ōmi, birth province Yamashiro｜Surname Minamoto, original name Sasaki》
　　　His ancestor was Sasaki Danjōshōhitsu Sadayori, heir of Emperor Uda's descendant, whose eldest son was Sasaki Sakyō-no-dayū Yoshikata, the monk Bakkaisai Jōtei. He received orders of kinship from Ōshū lord Sasaki Yoshihide, was entrusted with the lord's title and became guardian, governing neighboring provinces. His son Sasaki Yoshirō Masatsugu cooperated at the request of Akechi Hyūga-no-kami Mitsuhide and died in battle at Tennōji. His son Kumagai Hayato-no-kami Yoshitaka was separated from his father Masatsugu in childhood and changed his title to Kumagai in his prime years. Later he was summoned by Lord Hijikata Kawachi-no-kami and served as chief retainer, also participating in the Osaka campaigns. In his old age, before Lord Hijikata's house fell, he withdrew to Kyoto where he died as a rōnin. His son Kumagai Kohyōe Masamoto served Oda Nobuo (monk name Jōshin) in his youth, later served Lord Sakai Utanokamii, then served Lord Matsudaira Mutsu-no-kami.
