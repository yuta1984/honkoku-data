At that time, he went as an envoy from Lord Mutsu-no-kami to Lord Izu-no-kami. On February 28th, relying on his uncle Ogasawara Bizen within Lord Hosokawa Higo-no-kami's retinue, Masamoto joined Higo-no-kami's forces and distinguished himself in battle at the main gate of the honmaru during the castle assault. Subsequently, a man named Yoshioka Rokunosuke from Lord Mizuno Hyūga-no-kami's retinue also distinguished himself in the castle assault. They exchanged names within the castle, returned to their quarters, and exchanged written certificates as proof. On the same day, he participated in castle assaults twice in total. Regarding these two occasions, he obtained written certificates from Matsuzaki Sukeemon of Lord Hosokawa Higo-no-kami's retinue. After the fall of Shimabara, he received an increase in stipend from Lord Mutsu-no-kami and served with a stipend of 960 koku. However, he had a quarrel with a colleague and took leave over this matter. Though there were complications, during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, through Ishitani Doiri-rō's mediation, he was summoned back.

During Lord Yoshikuni's reign, Shōtoku 5, year of kinoto-hitsuji (1715), received family headship
　Adopted son, actually Masanaga's younger brother
　Current generation　Kumagai Kohyōe Masayoshi《Note: Masamoto's birth province was Echizen》
　During Lord Tsunamasa's reign, after Masamoto was granted a stipend allocation in Tenna 3, year of mizunoto-i (1683), he was granted leave during the reorganization in Jōkyō 3, year of hinoe-tora (1686). The following year, he accompanied Masanaga when he returned to service and became Masanaga's adopted son. Later, in Genroku 8, year of kinoto-i (1695), he was specially summoned and temporarily served in the lord's personal guard. Subsequently, his adoptive father Masanaga was ordered to retire, and he received family headship.
