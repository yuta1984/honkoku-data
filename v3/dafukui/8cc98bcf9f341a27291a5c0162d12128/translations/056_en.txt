At that time, he went as an envoy from Lord Mutsu-no-kami to Lord Izu-no-kami. On the 28th day of the second month, relying on Masamoto's uncle named Ogasawara Bizen within Lord Hosokawa Higo-no-kami's retinue, he joined Higo-no-kami's forces and performed castle assault at the main gate of the honmaru, achieving distinction under spear combat. Subsequently, a man named Yoshioka Rokunosuke from Lord Mizuno Hyūga-no-kami's retinue also achieved distinction in castle assault, so they identified each other within the castle and returned to their quarters to exchange certificates of proof. On the same day, he performed castle assault twice in total. For both occasions, he obtained documentary evidence from Matsuzaki Sukeuemon of Lord Hosokawa Higo-no-kami's retinue. After the fall of Shimabara, he received an increase in stipend from Lord Mutsu-no-kami and served with a stipend of 960 koku. However, he had a quarrel with colleagues, and regarding this matter, he requested leave. Though there were complications, during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, through Ishitani Donyūrō's mediation, he was summoned back.

During Lord Yoshikuni's reign, Shōtoku 5, year of kinoto-hitsuji (1715), granted family headship
　Adopted son　Actually Masanaga's younger brother
　Current Kumagai Kohyōe Masayoshi《Note: Masamoto's birth province was Echizen》
　　　During Lord Tsunamasa's reign, after Masamoto was granted a fief allocation in Jōkyō 3, year of mizunoto-i (1683), he was granted leave during the new regulations of Jōkyō 3, year of hinoe-tora (1686). The following year, he accompanied Masanaga when he returned to service and became Masanaga's adopted son. Later, in Genroku 8, year of kinoto-i (1695), he was specially summoned and served as a personal attendant. Subsequently, his adoptive father Masanaga was ordered to retire, and he was granted family headship.
