At that time, he went as a messenger from Lord Mutsu-no-kami to Lord Izu-no-kami. On February 28th, he relied on his uncle named Ogasawara Bizen within Lord Hosokawa Higo-no-kami's retainer group, joined Higo-no-kami's forces, participated in the castle assault at the main gate of the honmaru, and achieved distinguished service with his spear. Subsequently, a person named Yoshioka Rokunosuke from Lord Mizuno Hyūga-no-kami's retainers also achieved distinguished service in the castle assault, so they exchanged names within the castle, returned to their quarters, and exchanged certificates of proof with each other. On the same day, he participated in castle assaults twice in total. Regarding these two occasions, he obtained written certificates from Matsuzaki Sukeemon of Lord Hosokawa Higo-no-kami's retainers. After the fall of Shimabara, he received additional stipend from Lord Mutsu-no-kami and served with a 960-koku stipend. However, he had an argument with his peers and took leave over this matter. Though he had regrets, during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, through the mediation of Ishitani Donyūrō, he was summoned back.

During Lord Yoshikuni's reign, Shōtoku 5, year of kinoto-hitsuji (1715), received family headship
　Adopted son, actually Masanaga's younger brother
　Current　Kumagai Kohyōe Masayoshi《Note: Masamoto's birth province was Echizen》
　During Lord Tsunamasa's reign, in Tenna 3, year of mizunoto-i (1683), after Masamoto received his stipend allocation, in Jōkyō 3, year of hinoe-tora (1686), during the "new regulations," he was given leave. The following year, when Masanaga returned to service, he accompanied him and became Masanaga's adopted son. Later, in Genroku 8, year of kinoto-i (1695), he was especially summoned and temporarily served in the lord's personal guard. Subsequently, his adoptive father Masanaga was ordered to retire, and he received family headship.
