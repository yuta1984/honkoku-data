　　　　　△　Yokoyama
Summoned to service in the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the 3rd year of Keian, Kanoe-Tora year (1650).
　●Yokoyama Jūrōbei Norikei《Note: Home province Musashi, birth province Tosa｜Family name Ono》
　　The ancestor called Yokoyama Sagami-no-kami Tokisue resided in Yokoyama Castle in Musashi Province and changed his title to Yokoyama. His descendant called Yokoyama Iga-no-kami Toki[?] resided in Ueta Castle in Tosa Province as a retainer under the Kira family around the Eishō era (1504-1521), according to tradition. His branch families still exist in the households of Lord Matsudaira Tosa-no-kami and Lord Inaba Tango-no-kami. Iga-no-kami's eldest son Yokoyama Izu Tokiyuki served Chōsokabe Motochika and was entrusted with Kamashima Castle in Tosa Province. His son Yokoyama Yahei Tokitake's branch family still exists in the household of Lord Tōdō Izumi-no-kami. Yahei's eldest son Yokoyama Kurōbei Hirotake inherited the family headship and served the Chōsokabe family, but after that house was destroyed, he became a rōnin and served Ikeda Terumasa. However, due to certain circumstances, he left that house and lived in retirement in Tosa Province until his death. His son Yokoyama Jūrōbei Norikei was summoned to service in Lord Mitsumichi's reign. Norikei enjoyed sumo wrestling from his youth and became famous in the world
