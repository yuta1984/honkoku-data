　　　　　△　Yokoyama
Summoned to service in the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the third year of Keian, Kanoe-Tora year (1650).
　●Yokoyama Jūrobei Norikei《Note: Home province Musashi, birth province Tosa｜Family name Ono》
　　The ancestor, a man called Yokoyama Sagaminokami Tokisue, resided in Yokoyama Castle in Musashi Province and changed his title to Yokoyama. His descendant, a man called Yokoyama Iganokami Tokioki, resided in Ueta Castle in Tosa Province as a retainer under the Kira family around the Eishō period (1504-1521), according to tradition.
　　His branch families still exist in the household of Lord Matsudaira Tosakami and in the household of Lord Inaba Tangonokami. Iganokami's eldest son, Yokoyama Izu Tokitsugu, served under Chōsokabe Motochika and was entrusted with Kamashima Castle in Tosa Province. His son Yokoyama Yaheiji Tokitake's branch family still exists in the household of Lord Tōdō Izuminokami. Yaheiji's eldest son, Yokoyama Kurōbei Hirotake, inherited the family headship and served the Chōsokabe family, but after that family's destruction, he became a rōnin and served Ikeda Terumasa. However, due to certain circumstances, he left that household, retired to Tosa Province, and died there. His son, Yokoyama Jūrobei Norikei, was summoned to service during Lord Mitsumichi's reign.
