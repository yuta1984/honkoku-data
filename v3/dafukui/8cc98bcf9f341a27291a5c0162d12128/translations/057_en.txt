　　　△　Tsuge
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, summoned in Kanbun 10, year of kanoe-inu (1670), granted leave in Jōkyō 3, year of hinoe-tora (1686)
During Lord Yoshishina's reign, recalled in Genroku 3, year of kanoe-uma (1690)
　●　Tsuge Iemon Kagerin《Note: Home province Owari, birth province Bungo｜Family name Fujiwara》
　　　His father was called Tsuge Heikurō Motoshige, lay monk Nyokyū. He initially served Lord Nakagawa Sehyōe and had military achievements during the Hizen Shimabara Rebellion. Afterward, he requested leave and when he departed from the Nakagawa house, there were complications, so he took refuge in Sakai in Izumi Province. During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, he was granted assistance of fifty-man stipend, and his son Iemon Kagerin was granted 500 koku. Later, in Tenna 2 (1682), both father and son went to Echizen, and Kagerin served in official duties. However, during the reorganization in Jōkyō 3, year of hinoe-tora (1686), all were granted leave together, and later in Genroku 3 (1690), he was recalled. Regarding his ancestors, a man named Tsuge Tarōzaemon Yukishige served Lord Oda Nobunaga and was the castle lord of Yamashiro in Owari Province. His son, Tsuge Genbanokami, inherited and held Yamashiro Castle in Owari Province. However, in Eiroku 3 (1560), when Imagawa Yoshimoto advanced toward Owari Province, while many retreated from Ōdaka Mizukake and several other castles, Genbanokami defended his residence castle of Yamasaki and achieved military distinction when Lord Nobunaga went to battle.
