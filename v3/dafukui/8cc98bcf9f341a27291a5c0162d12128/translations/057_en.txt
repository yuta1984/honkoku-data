　　　　　　△　Tsuge
　During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, summoned in Kanbun 10, year of kanoe-inu (1670), granted leave in Jōkyō 3, year of hinoe-tora (1686)
　During Lord Yoshishina's reign, recalled in Genroku 3, year of kanoe-uma (1690)
　　●Tsuge Iemon Kagebayashi《Note: Original province Owari, birth province Bungo｜surname Fujiwara》
　　　　His father was called Tsuge Heikurō Motonari, lay monk Nyokyū. Initially he served Lord Nakagawa Sehyōe and distinguished himself during the Hizen Shimabara uprising. Afterward, he requested leave and departed from the Nakagawa house, but due to complications, he resided in Sakai in Izumi Province. During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, he was granted assistance of fifty-man stipend, and his son Iemon Kagebayashi was granted 500 koku. Later, in Tenna 2 (1682), both father and son went to Echizen, and Kagebayashi served faithfully. However, in Jōkyō 3, year of hinoe-tora (1686), during the new regulations, all were granted leave, and later in Genroku 3 (1690) he was recalled. Regarding his ancestors, a man named Tsuge Tarōzaemon Yukishige served Lord Oda Nobunaga and was reportedly the lord of Yamashiro Castle in Owari Province. His son Tsuge Genba-no-kami inherited and held Yamashiro Castle in Owari Province. In Eiroku 3 (1560), when Imagawa Yoshimoto advanced toward Owari Province, while castles at Ōdaka, Kutsukake, and several other locations were abandoned, Genba-no-kami defended his stronghold at Yamazaki and achieved military merit when Lord Nobunaga went to battle.
