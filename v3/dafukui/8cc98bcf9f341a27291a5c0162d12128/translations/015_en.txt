　　　　△　Hatta Family
During the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the third year of Kanbun, Mizunoto-U year (1663), summoned to service
　●Hatta Kinemon Tomoaki (home province Ise, birthplace Ōmi, surname Fujiwara)
　Around the Eiroku era (1558-1570), when a man named Hatta Iga-no-kami Tomoyuki was lord of Hatta Castle in Ise Province, during the Miyoshi Rebellion when Shogun Yoshiteru was overthrown, he conspired with an ikki leader named Yūki Shuzen-shō to fight against the rebels and died in battle, leading to the fall of Hatta Castle. His son, Hatta Yajibei Tomoyoshi, was only two years old when he was carried away in his wet nurse's arms and took refuge in Yoshida in Mikawa Province. After reaching adulthood, he served under Ii Naomasa. During Hideyoshi's campaign against the Hōjō family in Tenshō 18 (1590), he achieved distinction by charging into the kuruwa of Odawara Castle, also fought during the Kunohe Rebellion in Ōshū, participated in the Battle of Sekigahara, and also fought at Osaka. His son, Hatta Kinjūrō Tomotaka, also participated in the Osaka campaigns alongside his father Tomoyoshi. At the Battle of Wakae, he was the first to thrust his spear, killed the enemy archery commander Iijima Tarōzaemon, also killed the enemy vice-general Yamaguchi Samanosuke and took his head, and brought a golden war fan for inspection by both Lord Shōgun (Ieyasu and Hidetada). The next day, as the first of the Ii forces, he charged into the castle and killed three enemy warriors.
