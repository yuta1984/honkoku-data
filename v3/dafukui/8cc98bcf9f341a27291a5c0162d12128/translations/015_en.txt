　　　　△　Hatta
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, he was summoned in the 3rd year of Kanbun (1663), year of the Water Rabbit
　●Hatta Kinemon Chiaki (original province: Ise, birth province: Ōmi | surname Fujiwara)
　　　Around the Eiroku era, a man called Hatta Iga-no-kami Chigyo was the lord of Hatta Castle in Ise Province.
　　　During the Miyoshi Rebellion when Shogun Yoshiteru fell from power, he joined with a rebel leader called Jōshu Shōzen-no-shō,
　　　fought against the insurgents and died in battle, causing Hatta Castle to fall. His son Hatta Yajibee Chiyoshi, at age 2,
　　　was carried away in his wet nurse's arms and took refuge in Yoshida in Mikawa Province. After coming of age,
　　　he entered service under Ii Naomasa. In Tenshō 18 (1590), when Lord Hideyoshi pursued the Hōjō family,
　　　he rode into the bailey and achieved great fame. He also fought during the Kunohe Rebellion in Ōshū, had achievements at the Battle of Sekigahara,
　　　and also fought at Osaka. His son Hatta Kinjūrō Chitaka also
　　　served alongside his father Chiyoshi at the Siege of Osaka, thrust the first spear at Wakae,
　　　killed the enemy archery commander Iijima Tarōzaemon, and also killed the enemy vice-general Yamaguchi Samanosuke,
　　　taking their heads and a golden war fan. This was verified by both Lords,
　　　and the next day he was the first of the Ii house to charge into the castle, killing three enemy warriors.
