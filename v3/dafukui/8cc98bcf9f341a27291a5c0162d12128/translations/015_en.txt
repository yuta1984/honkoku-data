　　　　△　Hatta
Summoned in the 3rd year of Kanbun (1663, Year of the Water Rabbit) during Lord Mitsumichi's reign
　●Hatta Kinemon Tomoaki《original province: Ise, birthplace: Ōmi｜family name: Fujiwara》
　　　Around the Eiroku era, a man called Hatta Iga-no-kami Tomoyuki was the lord of Hatta Castle in Ise Province.
　　　During the Miyoshi Rebellion when Shogun Yoshiteru was overthrown, he joined with a man called Jōshu Zenshō who had organized an uprising,
　　　and they fought against the rebels and died in battle, causing Hatta Castle to fall. His son Hatta Yajibei Tomoyoshi, at the age of two,
　　　was carried away in his wet nurse's arms and took refuge, hiding in Yoshida in Mikawa Province. After coming of age,
　　　he entered service under Ii Naomasa. In the 18th year of Tenshō when Lord Hideyoshi pursued the Hōjō family,
　　　he rode into the Jō-kuruwa (castle compound) and achieved distinguished service. He also fought during the Kunohe Rebellion in Ōshū, had achievements at the Battle of Sekigahara,
　　　and also fought at Osaka. His son Hatta Kinjūrō Tomotaka also
　　　served alongside his father Tomoyoshi at the Osaka Campaign, and at Wakae he was the first to thrust his spear,
　　　killed the enemy archer commander Iijima Tarōzaemon, and also killed the enemy vice-general Yamaguchi Samanosuke,
　　　taking their heads and a golden war fan. This came to the attention of both Lords, and
　　　the next day, as the first from the Ii family, he rode into the castle and killed three enemy samurai.
