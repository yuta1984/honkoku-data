　●Atsumi Shinemon Tomonobu《original province: Mikawa, birthplace: Kii｜family name: Fujiwara, Buddhist name: Kokusetsu-sai》
　　　Tomonobu's great-great-grandfather Atsumi Tarōbē Tomomoto had resided in Atsumi District, Mikawa Province for generations of ancestors. In the 1st year of Kyōroku (1528, Year of the Earth Mouse), he entered service under Lord Kiyoyasu, distinguished himself in battle at various locations multiple times, and received commendation letters and salary increases. His son Atsumi Tarōbē Tomoyoshi distinguished himself in battle before Lord Hirotada, but due to certain circumstances fled to Owari Province and served Lord Nobunaga, achieving distinguished service twice and receiving commendation letters. Later, due to an unexpected conflict with his unit members, he fled and resided around Kiyosu. In the 3rd year of Eiroku (1560, Year of the Metal Monkey), when Lord Gongen entered Ōdaka Castle, he coordinated with Hattori Sakyō to provide military provisions. On this occasion he received a letter of appointment from Lord Sakai Saemonjo, was summoned into the castle the following evening, and was given duties《has a gold placement crest》. In the same year he was summoned at Okazaki and granted a fief of 500 koku. During New Year celebrations and such occasions, he was seated at the end among the lord's family members, and was always included in military councils before battle formations.
