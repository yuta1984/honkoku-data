　　　Tomonobu's great-great-grandfather, Atsumi Tarōbē Tomomoto, had ancestors who lived
　　　in Atsumi District of Mikawa Province for generations. In the 1st year of Kyōroku (1528), he came
　　　to serve Lord Kiyoyasu, distinguished himself in battle at various locations numerous
　　　times, and received letters of commendation and increased stipends. His son, Atsumi Tarōbē Tomoyoshi,
　　　performed meritorious service in the presence of Lord Hirotada, but due to certain circumstances retreated to Owari Province and
　　　served Lord Nobunaga, achieving fame twice and receiving letters of commendation. Later, due to an unexpected
　　　dispute among his group members, he retreated and stayed in the Mino Province area. In the 3rd year of Eiroku (1560), when Lord Gongen
　　　entered Ōdaka Castle, he coordinated with Hattori Sakyō to supply provisions. At this time,
　　　he received a letter from Lord Sakai Saemonnosuke, was summoned into the castle the following evening,
　　　and was given duties《has a gold placement crest》. In the same year, he was summoned at Okazaki and granted a stipend of 500 koku.
