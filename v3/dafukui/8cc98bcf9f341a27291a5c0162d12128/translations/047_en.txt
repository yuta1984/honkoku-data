　　　　　△　Katsuki
Summoned to service during Lord Mitsumichi's reign in Manji 2, year of tsuchi-no-to-i (1659)
　●Katsuki Goemon Nagayoshi《Note: Original domain unknown, born in Echizen｜Family name unknown, original name Fujishima》
　　His ancestors served Asakura Yoshikage. There were two warrior-monks called Sōteki and Santeki, and among them, the one called Fujishima Santeki Shōnaga was the grandfather of Katsuki Goemon Nagayoshi. The family crest was the wheel design. When Asakura Yoshikage was at the Yanagase encampment, during a meal on the battlefield when there were no chopsticks available, Santeki Shōnaga presented chopsticks attached to a military fan. When Yoshikage asked what wood they were made from, he answered "katsuki wood." Yoshikage was greatly pleased, that day's battle was also victorious, and Santeki's service was excellent, so Yoshikage gave him rewards and words of gratitude. From that time, the Fujishima surname was changed to Katsuki, and the family crest was also changed to depict chopsticks attached to a cypress fan.
