　　　　△　Ōkubo
When Lord Yoshishina was at Yoshie, summoned in the 1st year of Jōō, year of the Water Dragon (1652)
　●　Ōkubo Jidayū Moritsugu (home province: Kōzuke, birth province: Echizen, clan: Fujiwara, initially called Tanabe)
　　　　Moritsugu's father, a man called Ōkubo Seiemon, came to this province around the Keichō and Genna eras (ca. 1596-1624).
　　　　He relied on acquaintances and lived in seclusion as a rōnin in the Kawakita region until his death. His son, a man
　　　　called Ōkubo Sadanonoshin, was also a rōnin. This man's wife, a woman named Ako, was
　　　　Moritsugu's mother. However, when Sadanonoshin died while Moritsugu was still young,
　　　　Ako remarried to a man called Matsubara Sadenji, and later gave birth to a daughter
　　　　named Yotsu. Around that time, when Lord Yoshishina was young and they were searching for a wet nurse,
　　　　the aforementioned woman Yotsu was summoned and offered her breast milk. At that time, Jidayū was still
　　　　young and was being raised by a relative named Tanabe Sōken
　　　　when he was summoned. At that time, due to the lineage of his foster parent, he took the surname
　　　　Tanabe, but later changed back to Ōkubo according to his original name.
