　　　　△　Takada
In the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, summoned to service in the 1st year of Kanbun, Kanoto-Ushi year (1661).
　Current　Takada Saburōzaemon Tomoyoshi《Note: Home province Hitachi, birth province Shimōsa｜Family name Minamoto, originally called Miyata》
　　The ancestor, grandson of Oda Chikuzen-no-kami Tomoyoshi, Takada Saburōzaemon-no-jō《Note: Shuri｜no-suke》 Tomotsugu was the lord of Takada village in Hitachi Province for five generations. The sixth generation, Yamato-no-kami Tomoyuki, was skilled in archery and was summoned by Lord Yūki Naomitsu, who granted him the Yana domain in Shimotsuke Province. Three generations preceded this. In the reign of Lord Ujitomo, Takada Magosaburō Yoshitane was temporarily appointed to the warrior's office (musha-dokoro), and during the Battle of Yūki, his eldest son Samanosuke and the father-son family all died loyal deaths.
　　In the reign of Lord Ujihiro, Samanosuke's younger brother Hyōgonosuke《Note: Yamato｜no-kami》 Tomonao was granted the Yana domain. His descendants served the Yūki house with military loyalty through generations until Yamato-no-kami Yoshimichi. Yoshimichi's grandson Ryūshin Tomoyo was blind but had received the transmission of the Tale of Genji, so he was summoned by Lord Mitsumichi in the 2nd year of Jōō, Mizunoto-Mi year (1653). The current Saburōzaemon Tomoyoshi, son of Ryūshin's son-in-law Takada Magobei Tomoyoshi, was summoned upon Ryūshin's petition and came from Yūki.
