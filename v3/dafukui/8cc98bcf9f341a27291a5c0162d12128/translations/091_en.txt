　　　　△　Katsuraoka
When Lord Yoshishina was at Yoshie, summoned in the 2nd year of Manji, year of the Earth Pig (1659)
　●　Katsuraoka Jinemon Masanobu (home province unknown, birth province: Echizen, clan unknown)
　　　Masanobu's ancestors had served for generations as head priests of Wada Gyōmyōji Temple. Their honorary title was also called Minowa. During
　　　the Kaga-Etchu Ikki rebellion, Gyōmyōji's Shōgan found it difficult to employ military tactics while in monk's form, so he
　　　changed his name to Minowa Genuchi and killed Tomita Hitachi at Mount Chōsenji, achieving unparalleled
　　　military fame. A letter of commendation from Honganji Kennyo Shōnin is still possessed by Gyōmyōji Temple. Regarding the
　　　surname Katsuraoka, it is an alternative name from the maternal side. Masanobu's mother was
　　　the younger sister of a man named Yoshida Hyōnosuke. Hyōnosuke served
　　　Lord Tadanao and is said to have taken three heads during the Siege of Osaka
During Lord Yoshikuni's reign, inherited the position in the 1st year of Kyōhō, year of the Fire Monkey (1716)
　Tō　Katsuraoka Sōzaemon Motoki (birth province: Echizen)
