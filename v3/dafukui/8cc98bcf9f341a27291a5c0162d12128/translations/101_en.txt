△Higuchi

He served from Lord Tsunamasa's childhood, continued to serve during his period of retirement, and after his death was summoned by Lord Yoshishina in the 12th year of Genroku, year of the Metal Rabbit (1699).
The current Higuchi Kizaemon Yasutaka 《native province: Shimotsuke, birth province: Musashi, original name: Kozaemon》
Regarding his ancestors: for generations they served the Nagao family, who were regents (shikken) to the Uesugi family, held the fief of Egawa in Shimotsuke Province, and resided at the fortress of Sugata in the same province. After the fall of Odawara, they took refuge in Ashikaga, and his son Saito Kohyoe was a country samurai of Ashikaga. His son Higuchi Kozaemon Yasunobu went as an adopted son to a man named Higuchi Zenzaemon who served Lord Matsudaira Awaji-no-kami, but after a biological son was born to that family, he apologized and withdrew. Takigawa Yosanzaemon, the uncle of a man named Higuchi Kizaemon who served Lord Matsudaira Uemon-no-suke, was serving as a hatamoto in Edo. He went to this man and received upbringing from him, during which time he was asked to carry out revenge killings, which he accomplished successfully twice. Later, through the good offices of a man named Tezuka Mataemon who served Lord Matsudaira Tajima-no-kami, he was summoned by Lord Masakatsu and served him, but later died of illness. His son, the current Kizaemon Yasutaka, was granted succession while still young, and later in the 7th year of Kanbun, year of the Fire Sheep (1667), during Lord Tsunamasa's childhood...
