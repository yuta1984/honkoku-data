　　　△　Kanō
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, summoned in Kanbun 8, year of tsuchinoe-saru (1668)
　●Kanō Hokkyō Senseki Ōshin《Note: Original domain Izu, born in Yamashiro｜Family name Fujiwara, secular name Nasu Seiemon》
　　His ancestor, one Nasu Echizen-no-kami Takanobu, served in the imperial court during the reign of Emperor Go-Nara, and
　　it is said that at that time he was granted imperial permission to use the chrysanthemum crest. Takanobu's main family line is currently
　　the imperial physician Nasu Genchiku Hōin in Edo. The second son's family line: Nasu Takaizumi, his son Nasu Sō-
　　izumi, his son Nasu Rihyōe Sadaharu was Senseki's father. He served Lord Matsudaira Iga-no-kami,
　　and his descendants continue to serve that house to this day. Senseki studied
　　painting from childhood, and when he was summoned into service, he was appointed
　　as a disciple of Kanō Hōgen Eishin, took the tonsure, and by his lord's wishes applied for his master's family name, changing his name to Kanō Senseki.
　　Later, during Lord Tsunamasa's reign, in Tenna 3 (1683), he was appointed to official rank and became Hokkyō,
　　receiving an imperial edict.
