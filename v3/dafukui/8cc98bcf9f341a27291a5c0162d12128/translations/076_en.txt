In Lord Yoshishina's time, at Yoshie during the Meireki years (1655-1658), summoned
　●　Suganuma Heibei (real name unknown) (home province: Ōmi, birth province: Tango, clan name: Minamoto)
　　　　Around the Tenshō period (1573-1592), an ancestor named Oka Kozaemon Munemitsu, in his old age called himself Dōmi Nyūdō
　　　　In his prime, he served Kampaku Hidetsugu-kyō, and afterwards went to various places, also
　　　　serving at battle camps with distinguished service, and memoranda are preserved to this day. His son, named Oka Gonemon,
　　　　served Lord Kyōgoku Tango-no-kami during the Kan'ei years (1624-1644), and at Tango-no-kami's inquiry, submitted memoranda about his brief service during the Keichō
　　　　period (1596-1615), copies of which also remain to this day. His son, named Oka Jirōzaemon,
　　　　later took the name Suganuma Heibei and was summoned
