Daizen died of illness, and his son Tashiro Uzaemon was placed under the custody of Lord Doi Ōi-no-kami, lord of Sakura Castle in Shimōsa Province. Later, when he was transferred to the custody of the subsequent castle lord, Lord Hotta Kaga-no-kami, his punishment was pardoned and he served directly under Kaga-no-kami. However, since he had no legitimate heir, he adopted the legitimate son of Tashiro Yōzan, the current Yōgen. When Lord Hotta Kōzuke-no-suke fell from power, he became a rōnin. Later Uzaemon died of illness, and while Yōgen was in Edo, he was summoned during the era of Lord Yoshishina.

Regarding his real father Yōzan: when his grandfather Daizen took the tonsure, he transferred the medical practice to his adopted son Tashiro Yōsen. During the era of Lord Tadamasa, Daizen petitioned and Yōsen was summoned at Takada in Echigo. His son Yōzan was granted succession, and his son Dōhaku was granted inheritance, but after his death there was no legitimate heir and the main family line was discontinued.

During the era of Lord Yoshishina, in the 15th year of Genroku, year of the Water Horse (1702), he was summoned and served with distinction, but due to certain circumstances his stipend was withdrawn.

Later, during the era of Lord Yoshikuni, in the 3rd year of Shōtoku, year of the Water Snake (1713), he was summoned again and granted a stipend.
