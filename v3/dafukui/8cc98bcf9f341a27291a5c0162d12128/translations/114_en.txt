Daizen died of illness, and his son Tashiro Uzaemon was placed under the custody of Lord Doi Ōi-no-kami, the lord of Sakura Castle in Shimōsa Province. Later, when custody was transferred to the subsequent castle lord, Lord Hotta Kaga-no-kami, his confinement was pardoned and he directly served Lord Kaga-no-kami. However, as there was no legitimate heir, he adopted the current Yōgen, who was the eldest son of Tashiro Yōzan. When Lord Hotta Kōzuke-no-suke fell from power, he became a masterless samurai. Subsequently, Uzaemon died of illness, and while Yōgen was in Edo, he was summoned to service during Lord Yoshishina's reign.

His real father Yōzan, when his grandfather Daizen took the tonsure, had transferred the medical practice to his adopted son Tashiro Yōsen. During Lord Tadamasa's reign, at Daizen's request, Yōsen was summoned to service in Echigo Takada. His son Yōzan received the hereditary stipend, and his son Dōhaku received the succession. However, after his death, there was no legitimate heir, and the main family line was discontinued.

During Lord Yoshishina's reign, in the 15th year of Genroku, year of the Water Horse (1702), he was summoned to service and served particularly well, but for some reason his stipend was revoked.

Later, during Lord Yoshikuni's reign, in the 3rd year of Shōtoku, year of the Water Snake (1713), he was summoned again and received a stipend once more.
