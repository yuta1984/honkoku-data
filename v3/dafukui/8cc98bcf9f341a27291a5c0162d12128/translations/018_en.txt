During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, 1st year of Meireki (1655), year of the Wood Sheep, came from Echigo
to temporarily serve as an intermediary for Lady Seichiin
　●Ōsaki Sadayū Masaaki (home province and birth province both Echizen | surname unknown, Buddhist name: Hōi)
　　　Masaaki's father, Ōsaki Shichidayū, served from Lord Hideyasu's reign through Lord Tadanao's reign,
　　　teaching archery within the household, as has been handed down. Afterwards he accompanied Lord Echigo-no-kami
　　　and went to Echigo. Masaaki was actually Shichidayū's younger brother, but had been made his adopted son
　　　for some time. When a biological son was born in Echigo, Masaaki was separately
　　　summoned by Lord Echigo-no-kami to serve. Later, he was appointed as an attendant during Lady Seichiin's wedding ceremony,
　　　accompanying her palanquin procession and serving. After Lady Seichiin's death, he shaved his head
　　　and was immediately ordered to retire
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, 11th year of Kanbun (1671), year of the Metal Boar, granted succession

　　Ōsaki Sadayū Masatomo (birth province: Echigo, Buddhist name: Onsei | biological father: Uchida Kyūzaemon, retainer of Lord Echigo-no-kami)
