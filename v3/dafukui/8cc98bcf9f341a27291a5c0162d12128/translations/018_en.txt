During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, Meireki 1, Kinoto-Hitsuji year (1655), from Echigo
Came to serve temporarily as an intermediary for Lady Seichiin
　●Ōsaki Sadayū Masaaki (home province and birth province both Echizen. Family name unknown, Buddhist name Hōi)
　　Masaaki's father, Ōsaki Shichidayū, served from Lord Hideyasu's reign through Lord Tadanao's reign
　　and is said to have taught archery within the household. Afterward, he accompanied Lord Echigo-no-kami and
　　went to Echigo. Masaaki was actually Shichidayū's younger brother, but had been made an adopted son
　　beforehand. However, when a biological son was born in Echigo, Masaaki was separately
　　summoned by Lord Echigo-no-kami to serve. Later, he was appointed as an attendant during Lady Seichiin's wedding ceremony,
　　accompanied her bridal procession to Echizen and served there. After Lady Seichiin's death, he took the tonsure and
　　was immediately ordered to retire

　　Ōsaki Sadayū Masatomo (born in Echigo, Buddhist name Onsei. Actual father: Uchida Kuzaemon, a retainer of Lord Echigo-no-kami)
