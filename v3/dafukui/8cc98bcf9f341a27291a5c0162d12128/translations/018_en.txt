During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, 1st year of Meireki (1655), year of the Wood Goat, came from Echigo
to serve as intermediary for Lady Seichiin
　●Ōsaki Sadayū Masaaki (home province and birth province both Echizen | surname unknown, Buddhist name: Hōi)
　　　Masaaki's father, Ōsaki Shichidayū, served from Lord Hideyasu's reign through Lord Tadanao's reign
　　　and instructed archery within the household, as tradition tells. Later he accompanied Lord Echigo-no-kami
　　　to Echigo. Masaaki was actually Shichidayū's younger brother, but had been made an adopted son
　　　previously. When a biological son was born in Echigo, Masaaki was separately
　　　summoned by Lord Echigo-no-kami to serve. Later, on the occasion of Lady Seichiin's wedding, he was appointed as an attendant,
　　　accompanied her palanquin procession and served faithfully. After Lady Seichiin's death, he shaved his head
　　　and was immediately ordered to retire
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, 11th year of Kanbun (1671), year of the Metal Boar, granted succession

　　Ōsaki Sadayū Masatomo (birth province: Echigo, Buddhist name: Onsei | biological father: Uchida Kyūzaemon, retainer of Lord Echigo-no-kami)
