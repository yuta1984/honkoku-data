　　　　△　Hagiwara
In Lord Yoshishina's reign, at Yoshie in the 3rd year of Shōhō, year of the Fire Dog (1646)
summoned
　　●Hagiwara Inoheе Masataka《Original province: Hitachi, birth province the same｜Family name unknown》
　　　　His ancestor was Kawanobe Genba, whose son called Kawanobe Tosa was a
　　　　village samurai of a place called Yoshimaru in Hitachi Province. Tosa's younger brother called Hagiwara Chōbei
　　　　was summoned at Yūki in Lord Hideyasu's reign and granted a stipend of 200 koku, but
　　　　died before long and his line was discontinued. Tosa's eldest son called Kawanobe Sōgi
　　　　served as head of the monk attendants' group in Lord Daiyūin's reign, and his descendants
　　　　exist to this day. Masataka was Tosa's second son, and
　　　　petitioned stating that his uncle Chōbei had served the house in Lord Hideyasu's reign,
　　　　and was thus summoned.
