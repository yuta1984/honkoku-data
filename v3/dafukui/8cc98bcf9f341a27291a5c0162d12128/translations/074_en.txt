In Lord Yoshishina's time, at Yoshie in the 3rd year of Shōhō, year of the Fire Dog (1646)
Summoned
　●Hagiwara Ihei Masataka (home province: Hitachi, birth province the same, clan name unknown)
　　　Ancestor Kawanobe Genba, his son called Kawanobe Tosa was a
　　　rural samurai of a place called Yoshimaru in Hitachi Province. Tosa's younger brother called Hagiwara Chōbei
　　　was summoned at Yūki during Lord Hideyasu's reign and granted a stipend of 100 koku. However, he died before long and his
　　　line was discontinued. Tosa's eldest son called Kawanobe Muneyoshi served as
　　　head of the monk attendants during the reign of Daiyūin-sama (Tokugawa Iemitsu), and his descendants continue to this day. Masataka was Tosa's second son.
　　　He petitioned claiming that his uncle Chōbei had served the house during Lord Hideyasu's reign and was thus
　　　summoned.
