　　　　　△　Mōri
In Lord Yoshishina's time, at Yoshie in the 4th year of Kanbun, year of the Wood Dragon (1664)
summoned
　●　Mōri Yoshichi Nobu■ (home province: Owari, birth province: Tosa, family name: Fujiwara, original name: Mori)
　　　When Nobu■ was summoned, he was called Mōri Matazaemon, but when he was temporarily
　　　appointed to Edo Castle service, because it would conflict with the titles of
　　　Lord Mōri Nagato-no-kami and Mori Naiki-sama and others,
　　　he was ordered to change his family name and was called Kuwabara Jibu-emon,
　　　but later when he went to Fukui he became Mōri Kōshichi. Regarding his ancestors,
　　　there was one called Mōri Wakasa-no-kami Yoshikatsu who was Nobu■'s grandfather and served closely
　　　under Lord Hideyoshi. The brother of one called Mōri Hyōkitsu, named Mōri Minbu-shōyū, was the ancestor of Lord Mōri Suō
　　　-no-kami. His brother Mōri Wakasa-no-kami Yoshikatsu was one of the Yellow Standard Bearers (ki-horo no shū) of Regent Hidetsugu.
　　　His son Mōri Hayato Katsuyoshi was the father of Kōshichi Nobu■ and
　　　served Yamauchi Tsushima-no-kami.
