　　　　　△　Mōri
In Lord Yoshishina's reign, summoned at Yoshie in the 4th year of Kanbun, year of the Wood Dragon (1664)

　●　Mōri Yoshichi Nobuoki《Original province: Owari　Birth province: Tosa｜Surname: Fujiwara　Original name: Mori》
　　　When Nobuoki was summoned, he was called Mōri Matazaemon, but when he was appointed to serve at Edo Castle,
　　　his name conflicted with the titles of Lord Mōri Nagato-no-kami and Mori Naiki-sama and others,
　　　so he was ordered to change his family name and became known as Kuwabara Jibu-emon,
　　　but later when he went to Fukui he became Mōri Kōshichi. Regarding his ancestry,
　　　Mōri Wakasa-no-kami Yoshikatsu was Nobuoki's grandfather and served closely under Lord Hideyoshi.
　　　The brother of Mōri Hyōkitsu, called Mōri Minbu-shōyu, was the ancestor of Lord Mōri Suō-no-kami.
　　　His brother Mōri Wakasa-no-kami Yoshikatsu was among the yellow standard-bearers of Kampaku Hidetsugu.
　　　His son Mōri Hayato Katsuyoshi was Kōshichi Nobuoki's father and
　　　served Yamauchi Tsushima-no-kami
