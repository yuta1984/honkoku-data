He also achieved military distinction at the Battle of Nagakute in Tenshō 3 (1575). His son Tsuge Ichihyōe served Lord Niwa Gorōzaemon, and later served Lord Nakagawa Sehyōe. He gained fame during the attack on Kōnokisawa Castle in Banshū in Tenshō 6 (1578), and also performed well during the attack on Kameyama Castle in Seshū in the same 11th year (1583). His son was Tsuge Kazuma, whose son was Tsuge Heikurō Nobunari, whose son was Iemon Kagerin.

●　Fukuda Hachirōzaemon Yoshihiro《Note: Home province Shimōsa, birth province Musashi｜Family name unknown》
It is handed down that an ancestor named Fukuda Jinhyōe served the Yūki house. His son was Fukuda Hachirōzaemon, and his younger brother was called Fukuda Shichirōzaemon. This Shichirōzaemon was the son-in-law of a man named Kawahara Kiemon. This Kiemon served Lord Nabeshima Tangonokamii, but during Lord Daiyūin's reign was summoned as a direct retainer and falconer. However, as he had no biological son, he petitioned to adopt his son-in-law Fukuda Shichirōzaemon (who at that time served Lord Matsudaira Yamatonokamii) and called him Kawahara Kihyōe. Hachirōzaemon Yoshihiro was the elder brother of this Kihyōe, and was summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's reign.
