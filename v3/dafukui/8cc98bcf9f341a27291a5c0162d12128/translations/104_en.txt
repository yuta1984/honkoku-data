△ Hirao
Summoned in the era of Lord Tsunamasa, in the 1st year of Tennā, year of the Metal Rooster (1681):
Current: Hirao Shingobei Noritomo <<Original province: Buzen, Born in: Musashi | Surname: Fujiwara>>

He possesses genealogical records and memoranda stating that his ancestor Gotō Hyōe Sanemoto was a descendant of the thirteenth generation. His great-grandfather, called Hirao Magobei Fujinori, served Lord Kyōgoku Tango-no-kami and resided in Tanabe in Tango Province. His son, Hirao Tahei Shigenori, took leave during the Siege of Osaka, departed from Tango, had an audience with Lord Hosokawa Genba on the road, and served as an attendant during the Osaka campaigns. Lord Genba granted him a stipend of 150 koku, and the certificate is still in his possession. His son, Hirao Yaichirōemon Motonori, inherited his father Tahei's position but later took leave and resided in Edo. He was the father of the current Shingobei Noritomo.
