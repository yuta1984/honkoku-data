Those summoned to service during Lord Mitsumichi's reign

△ Tsuneoka

Summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's reign in the Keian era (1648-1652)
●Tsuneoka Yukie Munehisa《Original domain unknown, birthplace: Yamashiro Province, family name unknown》
His ancestor, one called Tsuneoka Genzaburo, served Ashikaga Tadafuyu and had military achievements during the Kyoto battles. Letters of commendation from Mafuyu from the 9th and 10th years of Shohei era still exist today. Munehisa's grandfather, one called Tsuneoka Genba, died in battle at Sekigahara. His father, Tsuneoka Kyuemon, was in poor health, lived in seclusion, and died as a masterless samurai. Munehisa's elder brother, one called Tsuneoka Shumanosuke Hisayuki, was summoned to service during Lord Tadamasa's reign and, by lord's command, inherited the Ajioka family line and was called Ajioka Jinzaemon. Munehisa was initially summoned by Lord Masakatsu, but later was transferred to Lord Mitsumichi, received gradual salary increases, and due to his lord's great benevolence, became a monk after Lord Mitsumichi's death, remaining as Zangi-ho.
