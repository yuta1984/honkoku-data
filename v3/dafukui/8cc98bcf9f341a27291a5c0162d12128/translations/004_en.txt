Those summoned to service during Lord Mitsumichi's reign

△【written in red】Tsuneoka

Summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's reign in the Keian era (1648-1652)
○【written in red】Tsuneoka Yukie Munehisa《marginal note: original province unknown, native province Yamashiro | family name unknown》
His ancestor, one called Tsuneoka Genzaburo, served Ashikaga Tadafuyu and had military achievements during the battles in Kyoto. He still possesses commendation letters from both occasions in the 9th and 10th years of Shohei from Mafuyu. Munehisa's grandfather, one called Tsuneoka Gemba, died in battle at Sekigahara. His father, Tsuneoka Kyuuemon, was in poor health, lived in seclusion, and died as a masterless samurai. Munehisa's elder brother, one called Tsuneoka Shumanosuke Hisayuki, was summoned to service during Lord Tadamasa's reign and, by his lordship's will, inherited the Ajioka family name and was called Ajioka Jinzaemon. Munehisa was initially summoned by Lord Masakatsu, but was later transferred to Lord Mitsumichi, who gradually granted him increases in stipend. Due to such generous favor, after Lord Mitsumichi's death, he renounced the world and became Zangi-ho...
