　　　　△　Wanibuchi
In Lord Yoshishina's time, at Yoshie in the 1st year of Meireki, year of the Wood Sheep (1655)
summoned
　●　Wanibuchi Kotarō Masayuki (home province: Izumo, birth province: Echizen, family name: Fujiwara, original name: Takaya Kin'emon)
　　　His ancestors were of the Sasaki family. They reportedly served under Ashikaga Owari-no-kami Takakyō.
　　　One called Wanibuchi Shōgen Masahiro served Asakura Yoshikage. His son Jirōzaemon
　　　distinguished himself in battle on October 13th of the 1st year of Kōji, year of the Wood Rabbit (1555), at Origuchi in Nomi District, Kaga Province,
　　　and the commendation letter given by Yoshikage still exists today. Later, on August 14th
　　　of the 1st year of Tenshō, year of the Water Rooster (1573), he died in battle at Tane in Ōmi Province during Asakura Yoshikage's desperate last stand. His
　　　son, called Wanibuchi Kantarō Masahiro, was the father of Kotarō Masayuki. He served
　　　Lord Aoyama Shuridayū, the castle lord of Maruoka in this province. [The family] possesses
　　　a written record concerning the Wanibuchi ancestors.
