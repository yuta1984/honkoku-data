　　　　△　Wanibuchi
　In Lord Yoshishina's reign, summoned at Yoshie in the 1st year of Meireki, year of the Wood Sheep (1655)

　●Wanibuchi Kotarō Masayuki《Original province: Izumo　Birth province: Echizen｜Surname: Fujiwara　Original name: Takaya Kin'emon》
　　　　The ancestors were of the Sasaki family and reportedly served under Ashikaga Owari-no-kami Takanori.
　　　　Wanibuchi Shōgen Masahiro served Asakura Yoshikage. His son Jirōzaemon
　　　　distinguished himself in battle at Origuchi in Nomi District, Kaga Province on the 13th day of the 10th month of the 1st year of Kōji, year of the Wood Rabbit (1555),
　　　　and a commendation letter from Yoshikage still exists today. Later, on the 14th day of the 8th month of the 1st year of Tenshō, year of the Water Rooster (1573),
　　　　he died in battle at Tane in Ōmi Province during Asakura Yoshikage's desperate final battle. His
　　　　son Wanibuchi Kantarō Masahiro was Kotarō Masayuki's father and
　　　　served Lord Aoyama Shuridayū, the castle lord of Maruoka in this province. He possesses
　　　　a memorandum concerning the Wanibuchi family ancestry.
