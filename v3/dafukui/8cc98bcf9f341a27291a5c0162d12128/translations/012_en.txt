After leaving the Tōdō family and withdrawing to Yamato Province, he was summoned by Matsudaira Tajima-no-kami Naotomi and appointed to the position of chief retainer (karō). Due to this, he became known to members of the main family branches and received written communications from them, which are still preserved today. However, when Toyohide was four years old in the spring, his father Kaga-emon died. Although he was granted succession, being still very young, his relatives petitioned for leave and he was taken in and raised by his uncle Imadate Rokūemon Masatane, who served in the household. After coming of age, he was summoned again and changed his family name to Imadate.

△ Matsunami

Summoned in the 3rd year of Keian, kanoe-tora year (1650) during Lord Mitsumichi's reign

● Sakurai Den'emon Masayoshi 《Marginal note: Home province Mikawa, birthplace Settsu | Surname Fujiwara, Sakurai title from mother's side, religious name Jinyū》

His ancestor was Matsunami Mikawa-no-kami Dexing, said to be the 17th generation descendant of General Toshihito, who was six generations from Minister of the Left Kawabe Uona. He served during the reign of Emperor Go-Hanazono and had military achievements. There is a portrait of Dexing at Shōkoku-ji Temple in the capital, and these achievements are recorded in the inscription of that portrait. Copies of this and genealogical records are preserved. Thereafter, generations held domain within Mikawa Province and served at the Imperial Palace. Five generations after Dexing, Matsunami Gagaku-no-kami Mitsumasa served Oda Nobunaga. His son Matsunami Heiemon Masaoki 《Marginal note: Masayoshi's grandfather》 served Tokugawa Ieyasu, and his descendants continue to serve as hatamoto retainers to this day. Masayoshi's father, Matsunami Den'emon Masatsugu, was the second son of the aforementioned Masaoki, but had been sickly since youth. He was under the protection of his father Masaoki and his uncle Matsunami Gorōemon, who served as chief magistrate of the five provinces around Osaka in Settsu, but passed away. After that, Masayoshi was summoned to service.
