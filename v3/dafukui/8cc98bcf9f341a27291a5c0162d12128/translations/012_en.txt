　　　　△　Matsunami
Summoned in the 3rd year of Keian (1650), year of the Metal Tiger, during Lord Mitsumichi's reign
　●Sakurai Den'emon Masayoshi (original province: Mikawa, birth province: Settsu | surname Fujiwara, Sakurai title from mother's side, Buddhist name Jinyū)
　　　His ancestor was Matsunami
　　　Mikawa-no-Kami Dekō, the seventeenth-generation descendant of General Toshihito, who was six generations from Minister of the Left Kawabe Uona. He served during the reign of Emperor Go-Hanazono and had military achievements. His
　　　portrait is housed at Shōkoku-ji Temple in the capital, and the inscription on that portrait records these achievements.
　　　He possesses copies of this along with genealogical records. Thereafter, generations ruled parts of Mikawa Province and
　　　served at the Imperial Court. Dekō's fifth-generation descendant, Matsunami Utanokamimi Mitsumasa,
　　　served Lord Nobunaga, and his son Matsunami Hei'emon Masaoki (Masayoshi's grandfather) served Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu),
　　　whose descendants remain as direct shogunal retainers (hatamoto) to this day. Masayoshi's father, Matsunami Den'emon
　　　Masatsugu, was the second son of the aforementioned Masaoki, but from a young age he was sickly
　　　and was cared for by his father Masaoki and his uncle Matsunami Go-
　　　rō'emon, who was the head magistrate (daikan-gashira) of the five Kinai provinces in Settsu Osaka, until he passed away. Thereafter, Masayoshi was summoned.
