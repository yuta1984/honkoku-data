　　　　△　Suzuki
During the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the 2nd year of Enpō, year of the Wood Tiger (1674),
he was summoned to service.
　Current generation Suzuki Jindayū Munekiyo《Note: Home province unknown, Birth province Yamashiro｜Family name unknown》
　　　Ancestral matters are unknown. Munekiyo's father, called Suzuki Seijirō Masanari, was a masterless samurai (rōnin)
　　　residing in Kyoto, where he died of illness. Munekiyo's maternal grandmother, called Tamechū,
　　　came from Kyoto during the reign of Lord Tadamasa and served as a personal attendant to Lord Mitsumichi. As a result,
　　　Munekiyo's elder brother, called Suzuki Kahē, was summoned from Kyoto and became Tamechū's adopted son.
　　　He was then appointed as a page (koshō). After that, Munekiyo also came from Kyoto and resided with Tamechū,
　　　from where he was summoned to service. The aforementioned Kahē was granted leave during the reorganization
　　　in the 3rd year of Jōkyō.
