　　　　△　Suzuki Family
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign in Enpō 2, year of the Wood Tiger (1674)
Summoned to service
　Current　Suzuki Jintayū Munekiyo《Note: Original domain unknown, born in Yamashiro｜Family name unknown》
　　Ancestral matters are unknown. Munekiyo's father was a man called Suzuki Seijirō Masanari, who was a
　　masterless samurai (rōnin) living in Kyoto but died of illness. Munekiyo's maternal grandmother, called
　　Tamechū, came from Kyoto to Echizen during Lord Tadamasa's reign and served as Lord Mitsumichi's personal attendant.
　　Because of this, Munekiyo's elder brother, a man called Suzuki Kahei, was summoned from Kyoto and made
　　Tamechū's adopted son, whereupon he was appointed as a page. Later, Munekiyo also came from Kyoto to Echizen
　　and was living with Tamechū when he was summoned to service. The aforementioned Kahei was granted leave
　　during the reorganization in Jōkyō 3 (1686).
