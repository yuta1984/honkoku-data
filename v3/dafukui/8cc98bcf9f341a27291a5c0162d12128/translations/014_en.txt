During the reign of Lord Yoshikuni, in the first year of Kyōhō, Hinoe-Saru year (1716), granted hereditary stipend
　Horie Genzaemon Hisasada (born in Echizen province, originally named Denzaemon)

During the same reign, in the fourth year of Kyōhō, Tsuchinoto-I year (1719), granted hereditary stipend
　Adopted son
　Current head: Horie Denshichi Hisashige (born in Echizen province, actually younger brother of Hisasada)

　　　　△　Kawazu Family
During the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the first year of Meireki, Kinoto-Hitsuji year (1655), summoned to service
　●Kawazu Jūrōemon Sukesato (home province Izu, birthplace Musashi, surname Fujiwara)
　　　His ancestor was a descendant of Daishokkan Kamatari, and he possessed the genealogical records of Kawazu Jirō Sukeoya (grandfather of Soga Jūrō and Gorō), though the last two or three generations were missing. Sukesato's father was called Kawazu Kyūsai Sukeyoshi, who served at Momijiyama in Edo when he was summoned.

During the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, in the second year of Kanbun, Mizunoe-Tora year (1662), summoned to service
　　Kawazu Zentayū Takasuke (born in Shimotsuke province)
　　　In the first year of Kanbun, Kanoto-Ushi year (1661), after his father Sukesato died under certain circumstances, he was summoned the following Mizunoe-Tora year and served. Later, after Lord Tsunemasa's illness, he served at Torigoe, and after [Tsunemasa's] passing, he was summoned during the subsequent reign of Lord Yoshishina.
