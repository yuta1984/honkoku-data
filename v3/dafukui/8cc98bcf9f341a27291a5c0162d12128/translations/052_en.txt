　　　△　Yasumi
During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in Keian 4, year of kanoto-u (1651),
summoned
　●Yasumi Jukō Yūken《Note: Original domain Kii, born in Echizen｜Family name unknown》
　　　Yūken's father, one Andō [surname unknown], served in the Kishū domain household, but due to certain circumstances, in Kan'ei
　　　5 (1628) he left Kishū and came to this province (Echizen), changing his name to Yasumi. His son Yūken was
　　　summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's reign and was ordered to become a disciple of one Jusen, who was
　　　a disciple of the renowned physician Juzō of Suruga. He devoted himself to studies in Kyoto for five years, practiced
　　　acupuncture techniques, and upon returning home, served in official capacity
During Lord Yoshishina's reign, in Hōei 5, year of tsuchinoe-ne (1708), granted succession
　Current Yasumi Jukō Yoshizumi《Note: Born in Echizen｜Actual father Yasumi Juhaku》
　　　His actual father Juhaku was specially summoned as Yūken's adopted son and served, but due to certain circumstances
　　　he died, so his son Yoshizumi was made the adopted son of his grandfather Yūken
