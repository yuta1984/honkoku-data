　　　　△　Hikosaka
In the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, summoned to service in the 5th year of Kanbun, Kinoto-Mi year (1665).
　●Hikosaka Matabei Shigetsune《Note: Home province Mikawa, birth province same｜Family name Minamoto　　　》　　The ancestor Hikosaka Shuzen Shigemasa served for generations as a hereditary retainer of Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu). Shuzen was assigned as a page to Lord Owari Satsuma-no-kami (Matsudaira Tadayoshi) and at age fifteen accompanied him to the Battle of Sekigahara where he also performed meritorious service. After the death of Satsuma-no-kami, he served under Lord Matsudaira Shimotsuke (Yūki Hideyasu), and after the fall of Aizu, he was summoned by Lord Owari Dainagon (Tokugawa Yoshinao). The descendants of his son Hikosaka Mataemon Shigezumi continue to serve in Owari to this day. Matabei Shigetsune was the second son of the aforementioned Mataemon Shigezumi. He was summoned to service in Lord Mitsumichi's reign through the good offices of Lord Dewa-no-kami Tsunataka. Shigetsune's succession was granted to his eldest son Asaemon, but it was discontinued due to certain circumstances.
