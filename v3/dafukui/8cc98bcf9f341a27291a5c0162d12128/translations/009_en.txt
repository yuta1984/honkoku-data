△ Iinuma

Summoned during Lord Mitsumichi's time as heir apparent《year unknown》
●Iinuma Kanbē Masayoshi《original province unknown, birthplace Hida｜family name unknown》

Masayoshi's grandfather Iinuma Jūrōemon Masayasu had served the Kanamori family in Hida Province, but was summoned during Lord Hideyasu's reign. His son Genzaemon and his son Jūrōemon served in succession. This third-generation Jūrōemon was granted leave, and the main family line was extinguished. Masayoshi was among the children of Jūrōemon Masayasu who had been sent as an adopted child to one Utsunomiya Hyōbu in Hida Province, and was the third son of one called Utsunomiya Shirōzaemon. Therefore, though he was Masayasu's grandson, Masayasu had taken him in and raised him from childhood, and he was summoned into service while Lord Mitsumichi was still heir apparent.

Granted succession rights in the fourth year of Enpō (1676, Year of the Fire Dragon) during Lord Tsunamasa's reign:
