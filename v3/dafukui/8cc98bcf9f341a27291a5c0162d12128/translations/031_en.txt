　　　　△　Enjō
Summoned to service in the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, but the year is unknown.
　●Enjō Hikozaemon Masanori《Note: Home province Izumi, Birth province unknown｜Family name unknown　Initial name Takamatsu Gonnosuke》
　　　Masanori's father, a man named Takamatsu Munemon, served Lord Matsudaira Suō-no-kami and received a stipend of 150 koku, serving as a steward (nando-yaku). His eldest son, called Gondayū, inherited his father's headship. Since Masanori was the second son, he desired separate service and went to Kii Wakayama to serve as a foot soldier (o-kachi) under Lord Kii Dainagon. However, having certain thoughts, he took leave. Afterward, he served Lord Kyōgoku Bitchū-no-kami, but again took leave. Later, he came to serve the (Fukui) house. He was summoned as a foot soldier and served in attendant duties and sumo wrestling services. When a foot soldier named Naoi Seiemon was punished, he was ordered to carry out the execution and performed this duty. Afterward, he was granted a new stipend.
