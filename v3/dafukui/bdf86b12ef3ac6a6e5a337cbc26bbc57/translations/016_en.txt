　　　　　△　Hanihara

Summoned in Genroku 11, year of the water horse (1698) during Lord Yoshishina's reign
　Current Hanihara Jirōemon Kiyokatsu (home province Shinano, birth province Yamashiro, family name Taira)
　　　Kiyokatsu's great-grandfather Hanihara Kaga-no-kami Chōbei (birth province Shinano, former name Kunai) initially served Takeda Shingen. During the Tenbun era, he set out on a pilgrimage to inspect the area around Owari Province's Kiyosu, where Oda Nobunaga's castle was located, but was detained by Lord Nobunaga who found him suspicious, and ultimately became a retainer of Lord Nobunaga. In Eiroku 12 (1569), when Lord Nobunaga constructed the Nijō Palace for Shogun Yoshiaki, Hanihara Kunai served as the chief magistrate, and at this time was granted court rank and appointed Kaga-no-kami. Nobunaga's ninth son, called Otono, was the nephew of Kaga-no-kami's wife, and due to this connection, when Otono was born, he was made Kaga-no-kami's heir (granted a fief of 1,000 kanmon), later taking the name Hanihara Utanosuke Nobushige, and then changing to Sakyō-no-suke Nobusada (this was the father of Oda Kazue-no-kami Sadaoki). Kiyokatsu's grandfather Hanihara Jirōemon Chōsen (birth province Mino, original name Chōjirō, later called Juan) was actually the third son of Nobunaga's retainer Hirate Nakatsumunosuke Taidayū (holding a fief of 1,500 kanmon). Since Kaga-no-kami had no natural heir, he was adopted to continue the family line (fief of 1,500 kanmon). After Lord Nobunaga's death, he served Lord Hideyoshi, retired during the Keichō era, and went to Izumi Province
