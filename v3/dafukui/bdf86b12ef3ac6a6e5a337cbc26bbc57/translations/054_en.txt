△ Yoshida

Summoned to service in the third year of Shōtoku, year of the water snake (1713), during the reign of Lord Yoshikuni.

The current Yoshida Genmori Kōchiku 《Home province: Shimōsa, birth province: Echizen, family name: unknown》

His great-grandfather, called Yoshida Matahei, accompanied Lord Hideyasu from Yuki to Echizen and served there. His son, Yoshida Matahei, accompanied Lord Echigo-no-kami (Tadanao) to Echigo and served there, but later became ill, requested leave, went to Kyoto, and died of illness. This Matahei was related by marriage to Kaifuku Sazaemon, who served the domain. Therefore, when he went to Kyoto as a masterless samurai from Echigo, he left his wife and children in Echizen. Matahei's eldest son Gon-no-jō, following his father's deathbed instructions, became a physician and was in Kyoto, but later came to this province (Echizen), became a disciple of Tashiro Yōzan, took the name Yoshida Ekian, and practiced as a town doctor. His son is the current Genmori Kōchiku.
