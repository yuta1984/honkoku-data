△ Tada

Summoned in the 1st year of Kyōhō, Year of the Fire Monkey (1716) during Lord Yoshikuni's reign
　Current Tada Denhachi Jutei (home province: Kōzuke, birth province: Echizen, family name: Sugawara; the Tada designation comes from his maternal grandfather's side)
　　　His ancestor Maeda Shōjirō Kyūshō served Lord Horio Yamashiro-no-kami, but when that house collapsed, he became a rōnin along with the others and came to this domain. Namikawa Heiemon, having distinguished lineage, came to this province and was staying in a place called Nakamura near Fuchū, when he was invited by Lord Honda Hida-no-kami to serve him, but died of illness. His son, called Nakamura Sakubei, was raised from childhood by Namikawa Heiemon and remained in this area. Later he became a town magistrate yoriki in Heiemon's group and served in that capacity, but subsequently became a rōnin. His son is the current Denhachi Jutei.
