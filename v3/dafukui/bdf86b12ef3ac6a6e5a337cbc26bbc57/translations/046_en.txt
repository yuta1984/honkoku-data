Emon lived in seclusion in Osaka, Settsu Province, and later went to Edo where he died as a rōnin for life. 　　　　　△　Tada

Lord Yoshikuni's reign, 1st year of Kyōhō, year of the Fire Monkey (1716)
Summoned
　Present: Tada Den'hachi Jutei (home province: Kōzuke, birth province: Echizen, family name: Sugawara; the Tada title comes from his maternal grandfather's side)
　　　The ancestor Maeda Shōjirō Kyūshō served Lord Horio Yamashiro-no-kami, but when that house collapsed, he became a rōnin along with others and stayed with our house. Since Namikawa Heiemon had connections, he relied on this to come to this province and stayed at a place called Nakamura near Fuchū. He was then invited by Lord Honda Hida-no-kami, served him, and died of illness. His son, called Nakamura Sakubē, was raised by Namikawa Heiemon from childhood and stayed in this area. Later, he became a yoriki (police assistant) under Heiemon's group of town magistrates and served, but afterwards became a rōnin. His son is the present Den'hachi Jutei.
