He resided in Sakai-no-tsu. His eldest son Hanibara Hachizō and second son Sanjūrō both served Lord Hideyori, and when Osaka fell, the brothers committed seppuku together. After that, the retired Kisen once went to Nagoya in Owari, where Lord Yoshinao (Lord Owari Dainagon) granted him a residence. With Lord Yoshinao's notification to Itakura Iga-no-kami, the Kyoto Shoshidai, he took up residence in Kyoto. Kisen's third son, Hanibara Jirōemon Kiyomasa (born in Yamashiro, father of Kiyokatsu), was still young during the Osaka campaigns and stayed with his father, but lived his entire life as a rōnin and died of illness in Kyoto. Kiyokatsu went to Edo and stayed with Oda Sadaoki, from where he was summoned to service at Sadaoki's request.

△ Niwa

Summoned in the 10th year of Genroku, year of the Fire Ox, during Lord Yoshishina's reign
●Niwa Yozaemon Yoshifusa (home province: Owari, birthplace unknown, surname: Fujiwara)
His ancestor, one Niwa Samanosuke Muneshige, served Lord Nobunaga and was appointed as guardian to Hasegawa Hideichi, serving when he was lord of Tōgō domain in this province. Later, during Lord Hideyoshi's time, he accompanied the Korean campaigns as a warrior commander and died in battle at Jinju in Korea. He was granted a domain worth 3,000 koku. His son, Niwa Zenemon Munetora, went to Korea together with his father Samanosuke and was appointed to succeed his father's position after his death in battle. However, after Lord Hasegawa Tōgoro died of illness in Hizen province, his son Lord Takenosuke inherited the family headship but died young soon after, leading to the extinction of the line, and all the retainers became rōnin. Since there were family connections with Niwa Gorozaemon Nagashige, lord of Komatsu Castle in Kaga province, he went to Kaga and later joined Niwa Kanbei, who was serving Lord Hideyasu. During the Osaka campaigns, he borrowed equipment from Honda Izu-no-kami Tomimasa and fought, killing Makino Jirozaemon, a foot soldier captain under Fukushima Masanori, along with his standard bearer, achieving military distinction. He then served Lords Tadanao and Tadamasa.
