(Left page)
　　　　△ Akautsu
Served from when Lord Yoshikuni was heir apparent
　● Akautsu Oribe Tomire 《Interlinear note: Home province Shinano, birth province Yamashiro | Family name Minamoto, original name Magojirou》
　　　His ancestor, five generations descended from Shinra Saburou Yoshimitsu, was the lord of Ooi Castle in Shinano Province, Ooi Shinano-no-kami 
　　　Mitsunaga. Six generations later came his descendant Ooi Mujijiro Mitsumasa, who came to reside at Akautsu Kameda Castle in Yuri District, 
　　　Dewa Province. From this time the family name was changed to Akautsu. From this 
　　　Mitsumasa, for three generations until Akautsu Magojirou Mitsutaka, they resided at Kameda Castle 《Interlinear note: They possess genealogical records | Mitsutaka's younger brother Okumura》
　　　《Interlinear note: Called Zusho, served during Lord Tadamasa's reign. His son Kunai requested leave during his generation | and departed for other provinces. Zusho's second son Tsugawa Yazaemon served the household until the time of reorganization》. This Mitsutaka's eldest son
　　　Akautsu Momo-no-kami Mitsuyuki's father and descendants incurred the displeasure of Lord Taitokuin,
　　　so they went into seclusion in Saga, Yamashiro Province. His son, called Ooi Kenzan-no-Jun, also 
　　　resided in Saga. Kenzan's son Oribe Tomire was summoned by Lord Masakatsu at Matsuoka in the 11th year of Kanbun, year of the Metal Boar,
　　　and afterward was assigned from the time when Lord Yoshikuni was heir apparent.
