In the 2nd year of Manji, year of the Earth Boar (1659), he was ordered to return to service and served accordingly. After that, following Lord Mitsumichi's death, he requested leave and died as a rōnin. Gentazaemon Yukiaki was separately summoned at age nine during Lord Mitsumichi's reign, and later at age seventeen was appointed to otemawarishū (personal guard). Later, during the previous reign of Lord Yoshishina, he requested leave and went to Edo, where he served Lord Honda Nakatsukasa-tayū. During the time of his son Lord Kichijūrō, due to the reduction of the house, he was granted leave and was living as a rōnin in Edo when he was summoned in the 4th year of Shōtoku, year of the Wood Horse (1714).

Granted succession in Lord Yoshikuni's reign, 6th year of Kyōhō, year of the Metal Ox (1721)
　Present: Sanada Gorōbē Nobusato (birth province: Echizen)
　　　In his youth, he received gōryoku-mai (support rice) from Lord Honda Nakatsukasa-tayū and served, and during Lord Kichijūrō's time, he was granted leave together with his father.

　　　　△　Kōno

Summoned in Lord Yoshikuni's reign, Kyōhō year of the Metal Rat (1720)
　Present: Kōno Ichirōzaemon Michiaaki (home province: Iyo, birth province: Settsu, family name: Ochi)
　　　The ancestor was Emperor Kōrei's twentieth generation descendant Tamasumi (also called Umataryō and Kinoshita-ōkami), who resided at the foot of Takanawa in Kazahaya District. The reason the clan is called Kōno is that when someone searched for water aboard a ship off Bitchū, saying "this water flows from Takanawa Mountain in my village," the four characters "ka-sui-yo-sato" (可水予里) were shortened to two characters to form "Kōno" (河野). Eighteen generations from Tamasumi, Kōno Shirō Michinobu (who achieved numerous merits in the Genpei War), during a banquet at Yui Beach, when Lord Yoritomo wrote numbers one, two, three on three small folding trays, designating one for Yoritomo, two for Tokimasa, and three for Michinobu. From this time, the various daimyo were seated in order, and from this occasion, the character "正" (shō) in the folding tray was used as the family crest. Ten generations from this Michinobu, one called Kōno Gorōzaemon Michinawa served Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu). His son Kōno Matsuan Hōin and his son Ryōi Hōin (each having reasons to study medicine) served successive generations as御匙役 (御matchmaker/physician).
