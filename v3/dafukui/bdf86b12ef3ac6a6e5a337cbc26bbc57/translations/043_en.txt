　　△　Umeda　

Summoned in the 2nd year of Kyōhō, Year of the Fire Rooster (1717) during Lord Yoshikuni's reign
　Current Umeda Danemon Yoshiyoshi (original province: Ōmi, birth province: Musashi, family name: Minamoto)
　　Yoshiyoshi's great-grandfather, Wada Ise-no-kami Harimasa, when Lord Oda Nobunaga advanced against him, found battle untenable and committed seppuku in his castle. At that time, all his relatives and retainers died in battle. His son, Umeda Busaemon Okioki (birth province: Ōmi), was one year old when his father was killed, and hid in Umeda Village in Iga Province, which was their ancestral domain. After reaching adulthood, he changed his title. Okioki's son, Umeda Buhei Okifusa (birth province: Iga, Yoshiyoshi's father), served Lord Kuchiki Minbu-shōyū and served as a vanguard commander. Yoshiyoshi also served during the era of Lord Kuchiki Iyo-no-kami. Later, he went to serve Lord Matsudaira Suruga-no-kami, and during the era of his son Lord Uneme-no-kami served as a chief retainer, but for certain reasons took leave. In that same year, he was summoned to our domain (Fukui han).
