　　△　Umeda　

Summoned in Lord Yoshikuni's reign, 2nd year of Kyōhō, year of the Fire Rooster (1717)
Present: Umeda Dan'emon Yoshiyoshi (original province: Ōmi, birth province: Musashi, family name: Minamoto)
　　Yoshiyoshi's great-grandfather, a man called Wada Ise-no-kami Harumasa, when Lord Oda Nobunaga advanced [against him], was unable to offer proper resistance and committed seppuku in his castle. At that time, all his relatives and retainers died in battle. His son, Umeda Buzaemon Haruoki (birth province: Ōmi), was one year old when his father was killed, and hid in Umeda village in Iga Province, which was their original domain. After reaching adulthood, he changed his title. Haruoki's son, Umeda Buhē Harufusa (birth province: Iga, father of Yoshiyoshi), served Lord Kuchiki Minbu-shōyū and performed the duties of a captain of the vanguard. Yoshiyoshi also served during the time of Lord Kuchiki Iyo-no-kami, but later went to serve Lord Matsudaira Suruga-no-kami and performed the role of chief retainer during the time of his son Lord Uneme-no-shō. However, due to certain circumstances, he took leave and was summoned into [Fukui] service in that same year.
