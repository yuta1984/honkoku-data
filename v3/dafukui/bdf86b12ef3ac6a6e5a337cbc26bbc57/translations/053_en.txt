△ Ōtsuki

Summoned to service in the sixth year of Kyōhō, kanoto-ushi year (1721), during the reign of Lord Yoshikuni.

Current head: Ōtsuki Sōan Junō (both home province and birth province: Echizen | surname: Taira)
Regarding his ancestors, it is said that they are descendants of Chichibu Rokurō Shigekiyo, the eldest son of Hatakeyama Shōji Jirō Shigetada. In recent times, they served the Asakura family and lived for several generations in Ichijōdani in this province, holding dominion over Ōtsuki village, hence adopting "Ōtsuki" as their family name. The remains of their residence can still be found in the depths of Shihi-tani Ichinonomura. His great-great-grandfather, Ōtsuki Bitchū-no-kami Kagehide (originally named Ōtsuki Saburō Zaemon), held a family stipend of 1,000 koku under the Asakura family. After the fall of the Asakura family, having no intention of serving two lords, he resided in Kitanoshō, later took the tonsure and called himself Ōtsuki Zenryū, practicing medicine as his profession. Subsequently came Ōtsuki Jusai, then his son Ōtsuki Saian, then his son Ōtsuki Gentsu, who was Sōan's father.
