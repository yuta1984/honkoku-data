　　△　Ōtsuki

Summoned during the reign of Lord Yoshikuni in Kyōhō 6, year of the metal ox (1721).
　The current Ōtsuki Sōan Jun'ō 《marginal note: home province and birthplace both Echizen | surname Taira》
　　Regarding his ancestors, it is handed down that they are descendants of Chichibu Rokurō Shigekiyo, the eldest son of Hatakeyama Shōji Jirō Shigetada. In recent generations, they served the Asakura family and resided for several generations in Ichijōdani of this province, holding domain over Ōtsuki village, hence taking Ōtsuki as their family name. The ruins of their residence still remain in the inner part of Ichinono village in Shihi valley. His great-great-grandfather Ōtsuki Bitchū-no-kami Kagahide (original name Ōtsuki Saburō Zaemon) held a stipend of 1,000 koku under the Asakura family. After the fall of the Asakura family, having no intention of serving two lords, he resided in Kitanoshō, later took the tonsure and called himself Ōtsuki Zenryū, making medicine his profession. He was succeeded by Ōtsuki Jusai, whose son was Ōtsuki Saian, whose son was Ōtsuki Gentō - this was Sōan's father.
