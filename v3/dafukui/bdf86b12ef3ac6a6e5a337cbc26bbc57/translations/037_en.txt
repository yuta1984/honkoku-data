　　　△Tomita
Summoned to serve from Lord Yoshikuni's time as heir apparent
　Current Tomita Heizō Nobuhari (home province: Echizen, birthplace: Musashi, surname unknown)
　　　In the 6th year of Genroku, Mizunoto-Tori year (1693), he was summoned when Lord Masakatsu was residing as heir apparent at his mansion. Nobuhari's ancestor was the sixth-generation descendant of Tomita Yarokurō Nagehide.
　　　Nagehide initially served under Asakura Yoshikage's command, but in the 3rd year of Genki, Mizunoe-Saru year (1572), when Lord Oda Nobunaga advanced to Gōshū (Ōmi Province) and faced off against Azai Bizen-no-kami Nagamasa, Yoshikage received a request for reinforcements from Nagamasa. Consequently, the Asakura house dispatched clan members led by Shikibu-shōsuke Kagekage.
　　　Though Nagehide also joined, he had always considered Yoshikage's governance unjust and harbored resentment against Kagekage, so he changed allegiance. Taking his retainers Keya Inosuke and Masui Jinuchi with him, he joined Lord Nobunaga's forces. During this time, he performed distinguished service repeatedly, and eventually Nagamasa was executed and Lord Nobunaga returned to the capital. Nagehide remained in Echizen and resided in Fuchū with orders to hunt down the remaining enemy forces. In the 1st year of Tenshō, Mizunoto-Tori year (1573), when Lord Nobunaga advanced to Nagashima in Seshū and withdrew to Nōshū (Mino Province), many enemies followed in pursuit.
