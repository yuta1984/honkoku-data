[He made this his] uma-jirushi (horse banner) and used the nine lanterns as his ko-uma-jirushi (small horse banner) (this has been transmitted to the present day). From this time forward, various families began using large and small horse banners. In the same year, [Hōjō Ujiyasu] granted Kawagoe Castle to Suruga-no-kami. After the destruction of Takeda Katsuyori in the 10th year of Tenshō (1582), Suruga-no-kami used military strategy to advance into Kōzuke and Shinano provinces and brought them under Hōjō control. Due to these military achievements, Ujimasa granted him the castle grounds of Matsuida in Kōzuke Province, and he received additional favors in various other places. Kawagoe Castle and the old domains were granted to his eldest son Magokirō. In the 18th year of Tenshō (1590), during the siege of Odawara, Suruga-no-kami was besieged in Matsuida, but after the fall of Odawara and Ujimasa's suicide, Suruga-no-kami also committed suicide. Suruga-no-kami Masashige's son, Ōdōji Magokirō Naoshige (born in Musashi, Yūzan's grandfather), accompanied Ujinao when he went to Mount Kōya in Kii Province after the fall of Odawara. There, he received the inner intention of Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) and was granted a sword directly from his hand. From this time forward, during Ujinao's lifetime, official duties were entrusted to Magokirō (he still possesses the written order from that time). After Ujinao's death, through the petition of the princess of Nishi-no-kōri, Naoshige was summoned by Taitoku-in-sama (Tokugawa Hidetada) and served in the pages' corps. However, in the 7th year of Keichō (1602), when he got into a fight with a guard named Ōno Mohē at Fushimi Bungo Bridge, since the opponent had many supporters, both Naoshige and a man named Maruge Ushioke joined forces to cut them down. At that time, Mohē mistakenly stabbed Naoshige with a spear, and because the wound was deep, he died (Mohē fled the scene immediately). Naoshige's son, Ōdōji Magokirō Sukeshige (born in Musashi, Yūzan's father), was taken in by his maternal grandfather Yura Shinano-no-kami after Naoshige's death. Later, through Taitoku-in-sama's inner intention, he was summoned by Kazusa-no-suke-sama, but after the lord's downfall, he ended his life as a rōnin. As for Yūzan, in his youth he served Lord Asano Inaba-no-kami (Nagateru), and during the Genroku era he received a stipend as a rōnin from Lord Matsudaira Higo-no-kami (Masayoshi), but later he declined this and left that house to be summoned into [Fukui] service. His eldest son Shigetaka was granted new lands, and even afterward, a stipend was granted for the support of his old age.
