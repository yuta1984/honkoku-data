In the same year, Kawagoe Castle was granted to Suruga-no-kami. In the 10th year of Tenshō (1582), after the destruction of Takeda Katsuyori, Suruga-no-kami, through his military prowess, brought Kōzuke and Shinano under Hōjō control. Due to these meritorious deeds, he was granted the castle grounds of Matsuida in Kōzuke Province by Ujimasa, and received additional favors in various places. Kawagoe Castle and his former territories were given to his eldest son Magonokurō. In the 18th year of the same era (1590), during the Siege of Odawara, Suruga-no-kami was besieged at Matsuida, but after the fall of Odawara and Ujimasa's suicide, Suruga-no-kami also committed suicide. Suruga-no-kami Masashige's son, Dōdōji Magonokurō Naoshige (born in Musashi, Yūzan's grandfather), accompanied Ujinao on his pilgrimage to Mount Kōya in Kii Province when Odawara fell. There, he received the inner intentions of Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) and was personally granted a sword by him. Thereafter, during Ujinao's lifetime, official duties were entrusted to Magonokurō (the official letter from that time is still preserved). After Ujinao's death, through the petition of the Princess of Nishi-no-kōri, Naoshige was summoned by Taitoku-in-sama (Tokugawa Hidetada) and served in the page corps. However, in the 7th year of Keichō (1602), when he quarreled with a guard named Ōno Mohē at Fushimi Bungo Bridge, since the opponent had many allies, Naoshige and another man named Maruge Ushinosuke fought together to drive them off. During this fight, Mohē mistakenly stabbed Naoshige with a spear, and due to the deep wound, he died (Mohē fled the scene immediately). Naoshige's son, Dōdōji Shūgoku-suke Shigehisa (born in Musashi, Yūzan's father), after Naoshige's death, was taken in by his maternal grandfather Yura Shinano-no-kami. Through Taitoku-in-sama's inner intentions, he was summoned by Kazusa-no-suke-sama, but after the family's abolition, he died as a rōnin. Regarding Yūzan, in his youth he served Lord Asano Inaba-no-kami (Nagaharu), and during the Genroku era received a stipend as a rōnin from Lord Matsudaira Higo-no-kami (Masayoshi), but later declined this arrangement, left that house, and was summoned to our domain. His eldest son Shigetaka was granted new lands, and thereafter, provisions for his elderly care were also granted.
