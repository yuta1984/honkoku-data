　　　　　△　Murakami

Summoned to service in the sixth year of Shōtoku, year of the Fire Monkey (1716), during the reign of Lord Yoshikuni
　　Current Murakami San'etsu Yoshishige《Note: Both home province and birth province are Echizen｜Family name is Chinshi》
　　　　The ancestor Kondō Yukei Shigemichi, commonly known as Kondō Saburōemon,
　　　　served Asakura Yoshikage. The ancestor was Chinese, named Chin Gairō Tōchōkō,
　　　　and thus the family name has been Chinshi (Chen clan). The Gairō family has resided in Kyoto for generations.
　　　　After the fall of the Asakura house, Saburōemon lived in Maruoka in this province, changed
　　　　his name to Yukei, and made his profession as an eye doctor, having a secret family remedy for eye diseases.
　　　　Yukei had no biological children, so he adopted the son of a certain Shida,
　　　　a senior retainer of Kurosaka Bitchū-no-kami, lord of Nagasaki castle in this province, naming him Murakami Sōgen Shigemasa. Since this Shida
　　　　died in battle during the Kaetsu Conflict, Sōgen wandered and temporarily
　　　　resided in Murakami in Echigo Province, thus adopting Murakami as his family name. His son was Murakami Yuen Shigekatsu, his son was Mura-
　　　　kami Genshō Shigekiyo, his son was Murakami Yusetsu Shigenobu, and his son is the current Murakami San'etsu Yoshishige.
　　　　The Gairō families of Kyoto, Edo, and Odawara all share the same surname.
