After the battle, [Nagahide] relied on Maenami Kurōbei-no-jō Yoshitsugu to request rewards for himself and his allies. However, since Maenami and Nagahide were constantly at odds, this matter was not pursued. In the same year, when Lord Nobunaga executed Miyoshi Sakyōdaibu Yoshitsugu, Nagahide did not go to Kyoto. Since Maenami supported and reported this matter, [Nagahide] was placed in Kitanosho, and an official letter was sent down instructing him to coordinate with Tsuda, Akechi, and Kinoshita. Upon hearing this, Nagahide attacked Ichijōdani, Maenami's residence, in the first month of the same second year of the monkey-dog, killed Yoshitsugu, and also attacked Kitanosho. When mediation occurred, the three magistrates of Kitanosho were sent to Nōshū. From then on, Nagahide's authority shook the entire province.

However, for whatever reason, Nagahide killed Uozumi Bingo-no-kami Kagekota and his son, who were on his side. At this point, the various clans throughout the province became suspicious, and those who agreed with Kagekota joined together and sent assistance to Kashū. Kennyo Shōnin sent Shichiri Mikawa-no-kami as general, and other Ikkō sect monks and peasant rebels conspired together, advanced on Fuchū, and repeatedly fought at Hoyama riverside, where Nagahide achieved victory. However, his retainer Kobayashi Saburōjirō changed sides and collaborated with the enemy, launching a night attack, resulting in Nagahide's death in battle.

His son Tomita Yajūrō (then two years old, name unknown) was deeply hidden and resided in Nishibukuro village in Echizen province. His son Sukegoro Nagatika and his son Sukemon Enshi served the Mizuno Shimotsuke-no-kami family successively. Enshi's eldest son, Tomita Sukemon Entomo, was summoned at Yoshie during Lord Yoshishina's reign (afterwards he was given leave and the line was discontinued). Entomo's daughter (Kōzaki) served Shōryōin-sama and Jōshōin-sama. Nobuharu was the grandson of the aforementioned Kōzaki's daughter line, so he was made an adoptive son and inherited the Tomita family name from his mother's side. Nobuharu's biological father was Tajōsho Rizaemon Shigenobu, who served Lord Doi Shinano-no-kami and was Kōzaki's son-in-law. When Nobuharu was young and Shigenobu died, Kōzaki took him in as an adopted son.
