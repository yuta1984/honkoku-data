After the campaign, [Nagehide] relied on Maenami Kurōbyōe-no-jō Yoshitsugu and requested rewards for Nagehide and his retainers.
However, since Maenami and Nagehide were constantly at odds, this matter was not addressed. That same year, when Lord Nobunaga executed Miyoshi Sakyō-dayū Yoshitsugu, Nagehide did not go to the capital. Since Maenami supported this matter, an official letter was sent down stating that [Nagehide] should be stationed at Kitanosho and coordinate with Tsuda, Akechi, and Kinoshita. Upon hearing this, in the same 2nd year, Mizunoe-Inu year (2nd year of Tenshō, 1574), in the first month, Nagehide attacked Ichijō-no-tani where Maenami resided and killed Yoshitsugu. When he also attacked Kitanosho, mediation was arranged and the three magistrates of Kitanosho were sent to Mino Province. From then on, Nagehide's authority shook the entire province. However, for whatever reason, Nagehide killed Uozumi Bingo-no-kami Kagekata and his son, who were his allies.
At this point, the various clans throughout the province became suspicious, and those who sympathized with Kagekata banded together and sent reinforcements to Kaga Province. Kennyo Shōnin dispatched Shichiri Mikawa-no-kami as general, and other Ikkō sect monks and peasant rebels conspired together, advanced on Fuchū, and fought repeatedly at Hoyama riverbed. Though Nagehide achieved victory, his retainer Kobayashi Saburōjirō changed allegiance and conspired from within, launching a night attack. Thus Nagehide finally met his death. His son Tomita Yajūrō (then two years old, real name unknown) was hidden deeply and made to reside in Nishibukuro village in Echizen Province. His son Gosukerō Nagataka and his son Sukeuemon Nobitsugu succeeded and served Lord Mizuno Shimotsuke-no-kami. Nobitsugu's eldest son Tomita Sukeuemon Nobutomo was summoned at Yoshie during Lord Yoshishina's reign (this lineage was later dismissed and became extinct). Nobutomo's daughter (Kōzaki) served Shōryōin-sama and Jōshōin-sama. Since Nobuhari was the grandson through Kōzaki's daughter's line, he was made an adopted son and inherited the Tomita surname through his mother's side. Nobuhari's real father was Tayui Riizaemon Shigehari, who served Lord Doi Shinano-no-kami and was Kōzaki's son-in-law. When Shigehari died during Nobuhari's childhood, Kōzaki took him in as an adopted son.
