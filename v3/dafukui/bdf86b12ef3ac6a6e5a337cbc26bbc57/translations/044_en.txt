　Present: Umeda Sadahachi Takatake (birth province: Musashi)
　　　　His real father was the son of Ōnishi Kahē, a yoriki (assistant) of the Kōgi Ōban (shogunal guard). He also served as a kinshū (personal attendant) to Lord Matsudaira Uneme-no-shō, and together with his [adoptive] father Yoshiyoshi, obtained leave and came to the domain (Fukui).

　　　　△　Sanada

Summoned in Lord Yoshikuni's reign, 4th year of Shōtoku, year of the Wood Horse (1714)
　●　Sanada Gentazaemon Yukiaki (home province: Shinano, birth province: Echizen, family name: Shigeno, Buddhist name: Nyūtokusai)
　　　　The affairs of ancient generations are recorded in the genealogy. His great-great-grandfather Sanada Danjōchū Yukitaka, lay priest Ittokusai, served under Takeda Shingen's banner and held a domain of about 300,000 koku. His second son Sanada Hyōbu-shōyū Masateru served under Takeda Katsuyori's banner and held a domain of about 100,000 koku. During the Battle of Nagakute, he died in battle at age twenty-six. His son Sanada Chikara-no-suke Nobumasa served Lord Kōzuke-no-suke (Yūki Hideyasu), and after that house fell, when Lord Tadamasa was residing in Echigo, he was summoned in the 6th year of Genna, year of the Metal Monkey (1620), granted 600 koku, and when [the lord] entered Echizen, he was appointed as advance scout. His son Sanada Gorōbē Yukihiro, in Lord Mitsumichi's reign in the 1st year of Jōō, year of the Water Dragon (1652), inherited his father Chikara-no-suke's position and was granted 500 koku. When Lord Sanada Iga-no-kami petitioned Lord Mitsumichi requesting his service, he was sent there, received a stipend of 2,000 koku and served as chief retainer.
