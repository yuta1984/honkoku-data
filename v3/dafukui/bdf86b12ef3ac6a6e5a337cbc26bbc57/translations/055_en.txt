△ Miyoshi

Summoned to service in the third year of Shōtoku, year of the water snake (1713), during the reign of Lord Yoshikuni.

The current Miyoshi Gentō Jitō 《Home province: unknown, birth province: Yamashiro, family name: unknown》

His grandfather, called Miyoshi Tarōbei, was a member of Lord Miyoshi Tango-no-kami's clan and served as chief retainer (karō), residing in Settsu Province. After Lord Tango-no-kami was transferred to Suruga and died, he became a masterless samurai and lived in Kyoto. His son Miyoshi Sahei, when his father Tarōbei died while he was still young, gave up his position as a samurai and studied medicine, became a disciple of Genji Hōin, took the tonsure, and called himself Miyoshi Genryū. This person is the father of the current Gentō.
