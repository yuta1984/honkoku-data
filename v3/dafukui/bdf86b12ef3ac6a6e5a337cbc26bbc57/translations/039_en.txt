　　　　　△　Kobayashi

Summoned to serve from the lord's heir's retinue during Lord Yoshikuni's reign
　　Current Kobayashi Mataemon Sadaei (home province: Kōzuke, birth province: Musashi, family name: Minamoto)
　　　　Great-grandfather Kobayashi Yazaemon Yoshikata was a fifth-generation descendant of Hachiman Tarō Yoshiie, and the son of Horiguchi Nuinodono Sadayuki, who was seven generations from Nitta Kurandotarō Masayoshi. After the Hōjō rebellion, Sadayuki retired to Kōzuke province and became a rural samurai under Yūki domain. His son Kobayashi Yoshikata (changed his title due to his residence) served under Ina Bizen-no-kami during the Osaka campaigns. His son Kobayashi Shichizaemon Yoshitake succeeded him and served the Ina family. Yoshitake's son, Tajima Mohei Yoshishige (changed his title for certain reasons), was summoned by Lord Masakatsu in Manji 3, year of the metal rat (1660). His son Sadaei was separately summoned and assigned from the lord's heir's retinue.
