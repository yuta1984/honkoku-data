　　　　　△　Kobayashi

Summoned to serve from the lord's heir's household during Lord Yoshikuni's reign.
　　Current Kobayashi Matauemon Sadahide (home province: Kōzuke, birth province: Musashi, family name: Minamoto)
　　　　Great-grandfather Kobayashi Yazaemon Yoshikata was a fifth-generation descendant of Hachiman Tarō Yoshiie, and the son of Horiguchi Nuinodono Sadayuki, who was seven generations removed from Nitta Kurōdo Tarō Masayoshi. After the Hōjō rebellion, Sadayuki retired to Kōzuke province and became a rural samurai under Yūki domain. His son Kobayashi Yoshikata (changed his title due to his residence name) served under Ina Bizen-no-kami during the Osaka campaigns. His son Kobayashi Shichizaemon Yoshitake inherited and served the Ina family. Yoshitake's son Tajima Mohē Yoshishige (changed his title for certain reasons) was summoned by Masakatsu in Manji 3, year of the metal rat (1660). His son Sadahide was separately summoned and assigned from the lord's heir's household.
