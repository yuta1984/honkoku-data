People summoned during Lord Yoshikuni's reign

△ Dōdōji

Summoned during Lord Yoshikuni's reign in the 4th year of Shōtoku, Year of the Wood Horse (1714)
●Dōdōji Yūzan Shigenobu (home province: Yamashiro, birth province: Echigo, family name: Taira, original name: Magonokurō)
His ancestor was a twenty-first generation descendant of Emperor Kanmu: Ise Tarō Sadatsugu, son of Ise Ise-no-kami Ujisada's son Suruga-no-kami Teruyasu. His son was Dōdōji Shigeni (founder of the Dōdōji family, initially called Ise Tarō, great-great-grandfather of Yūzan), who resided in a place called Dōdōji in Tsuzuki District, Yamashiro Province (from this time, the place name was adopted as the family name). Due to certain circumstances, Shigeni became a retainer of Hōjō Shingorō Ujishige (originally named Ise Shingorō, later called Sōun). When Ujishige established his castle at Odawara in Sagami Province, Shigeni was stationed at Amanawa in the same province and repeatedly distinguished himself in military service. Shigeni's son, Dōdōji Suruga-no-kami Masashige (born in Sagami, originally called Magonokurō), served three generations of the Hōjō: Ujiyasu, Ujimasa, and Ujinao. In the 13th year of Tenbun (1544), when Kantō Kanrei Uesugi Norimasa attacked Kawagoe Castle in Musashi Province where Hōjō Saemon-dayū Tsunashige was besieged, Hōjō Ujiyasu marched out from Odawara and drove away both Uesugi forces. At this time, a military supervisor of the Uesugi forces named Honma Ōmi threw a banner with nine golden stars on a lantern into the Dōdōji formation, entreating "May things not go badly as long as the Hōjō house exists," and then died in battle. Though an enemy, his words showed great valor, so [Masashige] changed his own horse banner to (black character "Dai" on a gold quarter) large horse...
