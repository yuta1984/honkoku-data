People summoned during Lord Yoshikuni's reign

△ Ōdōji

Summoned during Lord Yoshikuni's reign in the 4th year of Shōtoku, year of the Metal Horse (1714)
●Ōdōji Yūzan Shigeyuki (home province: Yamashiro, birth province: Echigo, family name: Taira, original name: Magokirō)
His ancestor was a descendant of Emperor Kammu in the 21st generation, Ise Ise-no-kami Ujisada's son, the eldest son of Suruga-no-kami Teruyasu, Ise Tarō Sadatsugu's son, Ōdōji Shigeni (the founding ancestor of the Ōdōji family, originally called Ise Tarō, great-great-grandfather of Yūzan).
He resided in a place called Ōdōji in Tsuzuki District, Yamashiro Province (from this time forward, the place name was used as the family name). For certain reasons, Shigeni served under Hōjō Shinkirō Ujishige (original name: Ise Kirō, later called Sōun). When Ujishige established his castle in Odawara, Sagami Province, Shigeni was stationed at Amanawa castle in the same province and distinguished himself in military service many times. Shigeni's son, Ōdōji Suruga-no-kami Masashige (born in Sagami, original name: Magokirō), served three generations of the Hōjō: Ujiyasu, Ujimasa, and Ujinao. In the 13th year of Tenbun (1544), when Kantō Kanrei Uesugi Norimasa attacked Kawagoe Castle in Musashi Province where Hōjō Saemon-daiyū Tsunashige was besieged, Hōjō Ujiyasu marched out from Odawara and drove off both Uesugi forces. At this time, Uesugi's military supervisor, a man named Honma Ōmi, threw golden nine-lantern battle standards into Ōdōji's formation and, entrusting them with the words "as long as the Hōjō house exists, may things not go badly," died in battle. Though an enemy, because he was a man of great courage, [Ōdōji] made his own uma-jirushi (golden quarter with large black character) the great horse...
