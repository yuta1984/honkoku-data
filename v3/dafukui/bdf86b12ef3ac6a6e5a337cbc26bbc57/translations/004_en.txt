△#1 Utsunomiya

《Margin note: Summoned in the 3rd year of Genroku (1690), year of the Metal Horse, during Lord Yoshishina's reign》
Current【Written in red】Utsunomiya Jibuzaemon Shinshin《Margin note: Home province Buzen or Shimotsuke　Birth province Buzen｜Family name Fujiwara　Original name Tōichi》

The clan ancestor Daishokkan Kamako, twelve generations later Awata Kaihaku Michikane, five generations later Kazusa-no-Ōkami Munetsuna《Margin note: From this point called Utsunomiya｜Also Oda and Hatta》. When the eastern barbarians Abe no Sadato and Muneto rebelled, he joined the imperial army and Munetsuna went down to the eastern provinces. Thereafter, Hitachi military governor Kino Yoshitsukasa《Margin note: Called Mashiko Tarō》and Shimotsuke military governor Kiyohara Yorikiyo《Margin note: Called Haga Gorō》served under Munetsuna, and from then on they were called the Ki and Sei factions《Margin note: Using these two clans as strategic reserves》. His eldest son Yamato-no-kami Nobufusa, during the suppression of the Taira remnants in the first year of Bunji, received imperial permission for the forbidden crest of chrysanthemum and paulownia and was appointed as a military commander in Kyushu. In the sixth year of Kenkyū, he received a certificate of confirmation from Lord Yoritomo, and from then on the family served as retainers of the shogunal house for generations《Margin note: Resided in Kagura Castle in Buzen Province and added Utsunomiya Daimyōjin to their worship》. Seven generations from Nobufusa, Utsunomiya Hitachi-no-suke Iesuna achieved numerous military merits during the Kenmu disturbances. Nine generations from Iesuna, Utsunomiya Hitachi-no-suke Shigefusa, and his son Daizen-no-dayū Tomofusa, in the 15th year of Tenshō when Lord Hideyoshi went down to Kyushu, were summoned up from Bungo Province and told they would be granted Iyo Province. However, when Tomofusa petitioned that regarding his old domain, he wished it to remain as it was, he incurred Lord Hideyoshi's displeasure, had his domain confiscated, and after that became a rōnin...
