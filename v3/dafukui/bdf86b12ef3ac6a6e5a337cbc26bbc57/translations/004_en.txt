△ Utsunomiya

Summoned in the third year of Genroku, year of the Metal Horse (1690), during Lord Yoshishina's reign
Current [written in red] Utsunomiya Jibuzaemon Shinshin 《Home province: Bunzen or Shimotsuke, Birth province: Bunzen | Surname: Fujiwara, Original name: Tōichi》

The ancestral founder was Sōkō, five generations after Awata Kaibyaku Michikane, who was twelve generations after the great minister Kamako. 
《From this point called Utsunomiya | also Oda and Hatta》 When the eastern barbarians Abe no Sadatō and Muneto rebelled, he joined the imperial army. Munetsuna went down to the eastern provinces, and thereafter Hitachi Deputy Governor Ki no Yoshitsukasa 《styled Mashiko Tarō》 and Shimotsuke Deputy Governor Kiyohara no Yoritada 《styled Haga Gorō》 served under Munetsuna. From then on they were called the Ki and Sei parties 《using these two clans to prepare both conventional and surprise military formations》. His heir, Yamato no kami Nobufusa, during the first year of Bunji (1185) when suppressing the remnants of the Heike clan, was appointed by imperial permission to the military commanders of Kyushu with the forbidden crests of chrysanthemum and paulownia. In the sixth year of Kenkyū (1195), he received a certificate of confirmation from Lord Yoritomo, and from then on, generation after generation served as retainers of the Shogun's house 《residing at Kagura Castle in Bunzen Province and worshipping Utsunomiya Daimyōjin》. Seven generations after Nobufusa, Utsunomiya Hitachi no suke Ietuna achieved numerous military merits during the Kenmu rebellion. Nine generations after Ietuna, Utsunomiya Hitachi no suke Shigefusa and his son Daizen no dayū Tomofusa, when Lord Hideyoshi went down to Kyushu in Tenshō 15 (1587), were summoned from Bungo Province and told they would be granted Iyo Province. However, Tomofusa petitioned that since it concerned his ancestral domain, he wished to remain as before.
