　　　　　△　Kawachi

Summoned during Lord Yoshishina's reign in Genroku 5, year of the water monkey (1692)
　Current Kawachi Jiemon Takenori (home province: Mino, birth province: Echizen, family name: Fujiwara, original name: Ujiie)
　　　The ancestor Ujiie Hitachi-no-suke Tomokuni, Buddhist monk name Bokuzen, served Toki Daizen-no-
　　　dayū Noriyori of Mino Province, also served Saitō Yamashiro-nyūdō Yoshitatsu, and later when serving Lord Oda Nobunaga,
　　　on the twelfth day of the fifth month of Genki 2, year of the metal sheep (1571), during the Owari Nagashima Ikkō sect
　　　uprising, he was appointed as advance guard commander and died in battle. His son Ujiie Sakyō-no-suke Tsunikuni, and his
　　　son Ujiie Sama served Lord Nobunaga, guarding Araki Castle as a check against the Uesugi.
　　　His son Ujiie Heima, during the Keichō era, had ancient connections to Ujiie Village in Nyū District of this province,
　　　so he withdrew there to live in retirement, at which time he changed his family name to Kawachi.
　　　Even now in Ujiie Village there are places called the Kawachi residence and Heima's grave mound. His son Kawachi Gon-
　　　nai Korimori also lived in retirement among the common people in Ujiie Village. His younger brother, called Kawachi Tatsunosuke,
　　　during the Keichō era was summoned by Lord Kaga Hizen-no-kami and granted a domain of one thousand koku. His descendant
