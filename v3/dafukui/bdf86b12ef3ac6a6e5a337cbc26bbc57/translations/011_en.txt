　　　　　△　Kawachi

In the reign of Lord Yoshishina, in the 5th year of Genroku, Mizunoe-Saru year (1692), he was summoned.
　Current Kawachi Jiuemon Takenori (home province: Mino, birthplace: Echizen, family name: Fujiwara, original name: Ujiie)
　　　The ancestor Ujiie Hitachi-no-suke Tomotuni, lay priest Bokzen, served Toki Daizen-daiyū Noriyori of Mino Province, then served Saitō Yamashiro lay priest Yoshitatsu, and later when serving Lord Oda Nobunaga, on the 12th day of the 5th month of Genki 2, Kanoto-Hitsuji year (1571), during the uprising of the Ikkō sect at Nagashima in Owari Province, he was appointed as advance guard commander and died in battle. His son Ujiie Sakyō-no-suke Tsunikuni, and his son Ujiie Sama served Lord Nobunaga, guarding Araki Castle as a defense against the Uesugi. His son Ujiie Heima, during the Keichō era, withdrew and went into seclusion due to ancient connections with Ujiie Village in Nyū District of this province, changing his title to Kawachi at that time. Even now, in Ujiie Village there are places called Kawachi residence and Heima mound. His son Kawachi Gonuchi Kōrimori also went into seclusion among the common people of Ujiie Village. His younger brother, called Kawachi Tatsunosuke, was summoned by Lord Kaga Hizen-no-kami during the Keichō era and granted a domain of 1,000 koku.
