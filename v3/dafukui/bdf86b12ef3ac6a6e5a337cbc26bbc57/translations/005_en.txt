[The Utsunomiya clan] engaged in numerous battles with Kuroda Nagamasa and Mōri Terumoto, with Shigebusa and his son achieving victory. In the 17th year of the same era, following Hideyoshi's command, they made peace and married Shigebusa's daughter to Kuroda Nagamasa. However, Shigebusa was ultimately killed during a banquet at Nakatsu Castle. Tomofusa was surrounded and attacked by Katō Kiyomasa at Oniba-juku in Higo Province, and Tomofusa committed suicide. Utsunomiya Shimōtsuke-no-kami Kunitsuna, a clan member stationed at Utsunomiya Castle in Shimotsuke Province, was exiled for acting willfully after the destruction of Shigebusa and Tomofusa. Thus, the Utsunomiya house was completely destroyed.

Utsunomiya Jibu-no-saemon Tomasue (childhood name Daisuke, mother was the daughter of Akizuki Nagato-no-kami Tanesane, grandfather of Shinshin), the only son of Daizenno-daibu Tomofusa, was born at Mount Hiko in Buzen Province after Tomofusa's death and lived in hiding within their former domain. During the Battle of Sekigahara, being still young, he sent his retainer Haga Gonnosuke to serve under Gongen-sama's banner, and this man died in battle. After Tomasue grew up, he resided in Kyoto, and through the mediation of Konoe Nobumoto and Lord Tadamasa, in the 19th year of Keichō, he was granted an audience with Gongen-sama and received 300 ryō of gold and a sword (by Raikunitoshi).

Thereafter, he presented the family's traditional bows, arrows, spears, and arrowheads, and received various items from Taitokuin-sama in Edo. During the journey to the capital that same year, having a retinue of 700 people, he was granted provisions for 700 people. However, Tomasue suffered from a toxic swelling and could not accompany the Osaka campaign. The following year, by Gongen-sama's command, he presented a ceremonial bow to Taitokuin-sama (made in the style of ancient sacred offerings). In the fifth month of the same year, to celebrate the fall of Osaka, he presented a ceremonial sword and also offered one to Lord Tadamasa. From the shogunate he received 1,000 hyō of rice, 50 court robes, 300 pieces of silver, and his two retainers received silver and court robes. He also received various gifts from Gongen-sama. Tomasue spent ten years at Sakamoto for his health, where he eventually died of illness. During this time, he received 1,000 hyō of rice from the shogunate each spring.

When Tomasue's son Utsunomiya Sakon Tanefusa (childhood names Yasaburō and Daisuke, Shinshin's father) was two years old, Tomasue died. Although the shogunate continued to provide the customary stipend rice, a retainer named Yasutake Ichirōbei collected it in Kyoto and immediately absconded. Moreover, he stole Gongen-sama's certificates and hereditary documents before fleeing. As a result, the stipend was discontinued from that time, and mother and son returned to Buzen Province, where he was raised by loyal retainers. Later, he went to Edo and submitted petitions to the senior councilors, serving in various positions until his death from illness. As for Shinshin, he was summoned to serve the domain during his father Sakon's lifetime.
