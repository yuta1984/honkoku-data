They engaged in several battles with Kuroda Nagamasa and Mōri Terumoto, with Shigebusa and his son achieving victory. In the same 17th year [Tenshō 17, 1589], following Hideyoshi's orders, they made peace and married Shigebusa's daughter to Kuroda Nagamasa. However, ultimately Shigebusa was killed during a banquet at Nakazu Castle, and Asafusa was surrounded and attacked on all sides by Katō Kiyomasa at Oniba-juku in Higo Province, leading to Asafusa's suicide. Utsunomiya Shimōsa-no-kami Kunitsuna, a clan member stationed at Utsunomiya Castle in Shimotsuke Province, was sentenced to exile for acting willfully after the destruction of Shigebusa and Asafusa. Thus, the Utsunomiya house was completely destroyed.

Utsunomiya Jibuzaemon Asasue, the only son of Dazendaibu Asafusa, was born at Mount Hiko in Buzen Province after his father's death and lived in hiding within their former territory. During the Battle of Sekigahara, being still young, he sent his retainer Haga Gonnosuke to serve under Ieyasu's banner, and this man died in battle. After Asasue came of age, he went to Kyoto where Konoe Nobumoto served as intermediary, and Lord Tadamasa also interceded for him. In Keichō 19 [1614], he was granted an audience with Ieyasu and received 300 ryō in gold and a sword (by Raikunitoshi).

Thereafter, he presented the family's hereditary bow, arrows, spear, and arrowheads, and received various items from Hidetada in Edo. That same year, during the journey to Kyoto with a total of 700 men, he was granted provisions for 700 men. However, Asasue suffered from gangrenous swelling and could not accompany the Osaka campaign. The following year, by Ieyasu's order, he presented ritual items to Hidetada (made in the style of ancient sacred streamers). In the fifth month of the same year, to celebrate the fall of Osaka, he presented a ceremonial sword and also offered one to Lord Tadamasa. From the government he received 1,000 koku of rice, 50 sets of court dress, 300 silver coins, and his two retainers also received silver and court dress. He also received various gifts from Ieyasu. For ten years, Asasue stayed in Sakamoto for medical treatment but eventually died of illness. During this period, he received 1,000 koku of rice from the government each spring.

Utsunomiya Sakon Tanefusa, Asasue's only son, was two years old when Asasue died, but continued to receive the usual stipend rice from the government. However, a retainer named Yasutake Ichirōbee collected it in Kyoto and immediately absconded. Moreover, he stole Ieyasu's written guarantee and hereditary documents before fleeing. Consequently, the stipend was discontinued from that time. Understanding this, Tanefusa went down to Buzen Province with his mother and was raised by retainers. Later he went to Edo, submitted petitions to the senior councilors, and served in various positions before dying of illness. As for Nobumasa [Shinshin], he was summoned to serve the domain while his father Sakon was still alive.
