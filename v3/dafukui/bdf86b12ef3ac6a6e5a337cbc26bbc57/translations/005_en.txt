[The Utsunomiya forces] engaged in battle several times with [Kuroda] Nagamasa and Mōri Terumoto, with Shigefusa and his son achieving victory. In the same 17th year [of Tenshō], they made peace on Hideyoshi's orders, and Shigefusa gave his daughter in marriage to Kuroda Nagamasa. However, Shigefusa was ultimately killed at a banquet in Nakatsu Castle, and Tomofusa was surrounded and attacked from all sides by Katō Kiyomasa at Oniba-juku in Higo Province, whereupon Tomofusa committed suicide. A clan member who had been stationed at Utsunomiya Castle in Shimotsuke Province, Utsunomiya Shimōtsuke no kami Kunitsuna, was sentenced to exile for acting willfully after the destruction of Shigefusa and Tomofusa. Thus the Utsunomiya house was completely destroyed.

The only son of Daizen no dayū Tomofusa, Utsunomiya Jibuzaemon Tomosue 《childhood name Daisuke; his mother was the daughter of Akizuki Nagato no kami Tanesane; he was Shinshin's grandfather》, was born at Mount Hiko in Buzen Province after Tomofusa's death and lived in hiding within the former domain. During the Battle of Sekigahara, being still young, he sent a retainer named Haga Gonnosuke to serve under Lord Ieyasu's banner, and this man died in battle. After Tomosue came of age, he was in Kyoto under the patronage of Lord Konoe Nobumoto, and Lord Tadamasa also provided patronage. In Keichō 19 (1614), he was granted an audience with Lord Ieyasu and received 300 ryō in gold and a sword 《made by Rai Kuniyoshi》. Thereafter, he presented the family's hereditary bows, arrows, spears, and arrowheads, and received various gifts from Lord Hidetada in Edo. That same year, during the journey to Kyoto, having a total of 700 men, he was granted rice stipend for 700 men.

However, Tomosue suffered from a wind-poison swelling and could not accompany the Osaka campaign. The following year, by Lord Ieyasu's command, he presented ceremonial offerings to Lord Hidetada 《these were sacred streamers from the age of the gods》. In the fifth month of the same year, to celebrate the fall of Osaka, he presented a ceremonial sword and also gave one to Lord Tadamasa. From the government he received 1,000 bags of rice, 50 court robes, and 300 pieces of silver, and his two retainers also received silver and court robes, while from Lord Ieyasu he received various gifts. For his health, Tomosue stayed in Sakamoto for ten years and eventually died of illness. During this time, every spring he was granted 1,000 bags of rice from the government.

Tomosue's only son, Utsunomiya Sakon Tanefusa 《childhood names Yasaburō or Daisuke; Shinshin's father》, was two years old when Tomosue died, but the government continued to provide the customary rice stipend. However, a retainer named Yasutake Ichirōbei collected it in Kyoto and immediately absconded. Moreover, he stole Lord Ieyasu's certificate and hereditary documents when he fled. Due to this, it was understood that the stipend matter was no longer known and was recalled. [Tanefusa] went down to Buzen Province with his mother and grew up under the care of retainers. Later he went to Edo and submitted petitions to the rōjū (senior councilors), serving in various places before dying of illness. As for Shinshin, he was summoned into service while his father Sakon was still alive.
