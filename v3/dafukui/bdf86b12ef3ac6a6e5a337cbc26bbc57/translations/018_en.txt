He was included in the official inspection (go-jikken), had an audience at that time, and was summoned by Lord Tadamasa. He still possesses copies of records documenting these military achievements. Zenemon Munetora's son, Niwa Anzaemon Yoshitsugu, was summoned and served from the time of Lord Mitsumichi's youth, and was granted leave after the collective dismissal in the 3rd year of Jokyo, year of the Tiger (1686). His son Yozaemon Yoshifusa previously served under Mizuno Hayato-no-sho and was later summoned to service.

In the 1st year of Kyoho, year of the Monkey, during Lord Yoshikuni's reign, he was granted succession.
Current generation: Niwa Yozaemon Shigetora (birthplace: Echizen)

△ Seki

Summoned in the 10th year of Genroku, year of the Ox, during Lord Yoshishina's reign.
Current generation: Seki Jingoemon Gyogen (home province unknown, birthplace Echizen, family name unknown)

His ancestor, a man named Seki Wakasa Takebuta, served Ashida Gemon's ancestor Ashida Kozuke-no-kami Nobumori, held a fief of 25 kan in Saku District of Shinano Province, and resided in a place called Yamabe, southwest of Ashida-juku in the same province. His son Seki Sozaemon Katsumoto inherited the headship and served Ashida Gemon Nobushige and his heir Matsudaira Gemon-no-tayu Yasumasa (later called Kato Sogetsu), and went to Echizen. Katsumoto's son Minbu-gemon Takenari and his son Minbu-gemon Nagateru both served as retainers (yoriki) of the Ashida family for two generations. Nagateru's son is the current Gyogen.

Summoned in the 2nd year of Kyoho, year of the Rooster, during Lord Yoshikuni's reign.
Current generation: Seki Heidayu Tadahide (birthplace: Echizen)
