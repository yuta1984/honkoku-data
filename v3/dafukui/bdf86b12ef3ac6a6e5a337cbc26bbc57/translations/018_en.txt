He was included in the formal inspection, had an audience at that time, and was summoned by Lord Tadamasa. He still possesses copies of records documenting these military achievements. The son of Zenemon Munetora, Niwa Yazaemon Yoshitsugu, was summoned to service from Lord Mitsumichi's childhood and served until he was granted leave after the unification in the third year of Jokyo, year of the Fire Tiger. His son Yozaemon Yoshifusa previously served Lord Mizuno Hayato-no-sho and was later summoned to service.

In the first year of Kyoho, year of the Fire Monkey, during Lord Yoshikuni's reign, he was granted succession.
Current: Niwa Yozaemon Shigetora (born in Echizen)

△ Seki

Summoned to service in the tenth year of Genroku, year of the Fire Ox, during Lord Yoshishina's reign.
Current: Seki Jingoemon Gyogen (home province unknown, born in Echizen, family name unknown)
His ancestor, a man called Seki Wakasa Taketon, served Ashida Gemon's ancestor Ashida Shimotuke-no-kami Nobumori, held a fief of 25 kan in Saku District of Shinano Province, and resided in a place called Yamabe, southwest of Ashida-juku in the same province. His son Seki Sozaemon Katsumoto inherited the headship and served Ashida Gemon Nobushige and his heir Matsudaira Gemon-no-tayu Yasumasa (later known as Kato Sogetsu), coming to Echizen. Katsumoto's son Minbu-emon Takenari and his son Minbu-emon Nagahide both served two generations as retainers of the Ashida family. Nagahide's son is the current Gyogen.

Summoned to service in the second year of Kyoho, year of the Fire Rooster, during Lord Yoshikuni's reign.
Current: Seki Heidayu Tadahide (born in Echizen)
