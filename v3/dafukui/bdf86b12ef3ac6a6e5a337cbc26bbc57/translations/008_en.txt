　　　　△　Kajikawa

Summoned during Lord Yoshishina's reign in Genroku 11, year of the earth tiger (1698)

Present Kajikawa Tōbei Shigeyasu (home province: Mino, birth province: Echizen, family name: Tachibana, father: Mizoguchi Ichizaemon Takashige)
　Regarding his ancestors, a man named Kajikawa Heikurō Nobutoki was a member of the Oda family clan and resided in a place called Ikejiri in Mino Province, as seen in the genealogy. His great-grandfather, a man named Kogure Tōbei Masatake, served Hōjō Ujinao, and when Ujinao fought at a place called Koizumi, he killed a man named Kanai Sensuke and received a commendation letter from Ujinao, which he still possesses today. Besides this, there are still memoranda and signed documents of his service in various places. After the fall of the Hōjō house, he was summoned by Lord Hideyasu and appointed to Tabuse Kenbutsu's unit. Later, during Lord Tadamasa's reign, he was appointed as a direct retainer and granted a vermillion seal, which is still preserved today. At that time he was called Kogure Umanosuke. His son Kogure Tōbei Katsuke, later called Kajikawa Tōbei, succeeded and served. Later, he was appointed as an attendant to Lord Yoshishina, went to Yoshie to serve, but died of illness.
