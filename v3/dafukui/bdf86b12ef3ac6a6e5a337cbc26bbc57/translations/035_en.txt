△Akautsu
Served from the time when Lord Yoshikuni was heir apparent
●Akautsu Oribe Tomire (home province: Shinano, birth province: Yamashiro, family name: Minamoto, original name: Magojiro)
His ancestor was the fifth-generation descendant of Shinra Saburo Yoshimitsu, and he descended from Oi Shinano-no-kami Mitsunaga, lord of Oi Castle in Shinano Province. Six generations later came Oi Mujijiro Mitsumasa, who resided at Akautsu Kameda Castle in Yuri District, Dewa Province. From this time, the family name was changed to Akautsu. For three generations from Mitsumasa until Akautsu Magojiro Mitutaka, they resided at Kameda Castle (they possess a family genealogy. Mitsutaka's younger brother was Okumura Tosho, who served during Lord Tadamasa's reign. His son Kunai requested leave and went to other provinces. Tosho's second son, Tsugawa Yazaemon, served the house until the time of the new regulations). From Mitsutaka's eldest son, Akautsu Mominokami Mitsuyuki's father onward, there was an incident that incurred the displeasure of Lord Taitokuin, so they went into retirement in Saga, Yamashiro Province. His son, called Oi Kanzan-no-jo, also resided in Saga. Kanzan's son, Oribe Tomire, was summoned by Lord Masakatsu at Matsuoka in the 11th year of Kanbun, year of the metal boar (1671), and thereafter was attached to Lord Yoshikuni from the time he was heir apparent.
