　　　△　Funaoka

Summoned in the 2nd year of Shōtoku, Mizunoe-Tatsu year (1712) during Lord Yoshikuni's reign

Current　Funaoka Hidehaku Norinobe《Native province: unknown　Birth province: Echizen｜Family name unknown　Original name: Kurihara》
　　　His grandfather, called Kurihara Jiemon, is said to have had ancestors who served Takeda Shingen. His son
　　　Funaoka Hidehaku Noritsune was the father of the current Hidehaku Norinobe. He became a disciple of 
　　　a physician named Tashiro Mankyū from Owari and studied medical arts. Later he came to this province (Echizen) and
　　　lived in a town residence, practicing medicine. During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, he was summoned to accompany the lord to Edo, serving in an ashigaru (foot soldier) capacity without stipend,
　　　and was frequently summoned before the lord's presence.
