△ Funaoka

Summoned to service in the second year of Shōtoku, mizunoe-tatsu year (1712), during the reign of Lord Yoshikuni.

Current head: Funaoka Shūhaku Jōen (home province unknown, born in Echizen | original surname unknown, original name Kurihara)

His grandfather, called Kurihara Jiuemon, is said to have had ancestors who served Takeda Shingen. His son, Funaoka Shūhaku Jōyō, who was the father of the current Shūhaku Jōen, became a disciple of a physician named Tashiro Mankyū from Owari Province and studied medical arts. Later, he came to this province (Echizen) and resided in the town quarters, practicing medicine. During the reign of Lord Mitsumichi, he was summoned to accompany the lord to Edo, served in the ashigaru (foot soldier) rank without stipend, and was frequently called before the lord's presence.
