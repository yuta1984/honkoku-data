　　　　△　Nomoto

Summoned to service in the 6th year of Kyōhō, Kanoto-Ushi year (1721) during Lord Yoshikuni's reign
　Current　Nomoto Genzaemon Tsunetaka 《Native province: Musashi　Birth province: Echizen｜Family name: Taira　Original name: Iwaki》
　　　　Great-great-grandfather Nomoto Shōgen 《Personal name unknown｜Native province: Musashi》 was for generations a local samurai of Nomoto village in Musashi Province,
　　　　serving Ōta Sanraku, lord of Iwatsuki Castle in the same province 《From that time, changed｜his family name to Nomoto》. During the Battle of Kōnodai in Shimōsa Province,
　　　　he distinguished himself in battle, and afterward served Hōjō Jūrō, the adopted son of Sanraku. When Odawara
　　　　fell, Lord Hideyasu summoned both Shōgen and his son
　　　　Ukon together at the field headquarters in Odawara, and they served as regular attendants in Fushimi. Ukon Masanori thereafter
　　　　served during the expedition to Nagoya in Hizen Province, and accompanied Lord Tadanao during both
　　　　Osaka campaigns. On May 7th of the 1st year of Genna (1615), when charging into Sakura Gate,
　　　　he encountered Oyado Kanbei Masatomo, exchanged words with him, both dismounted from their horses and engaged
　　　　in spear combat, taking his head. During the head inspection before both Their Lordships, it appeared that U-
　　　　kon had been wounded, but by the gracious command of His Majesty Gongensama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), he personally bestowed medicine,
　　　　and furthermore granted two pieces of judge's gold and hakama trousers of iris leather. Taitokuin-sama (Tokugawa Hidetada)
