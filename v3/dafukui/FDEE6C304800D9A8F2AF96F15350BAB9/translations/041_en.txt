Regarding the 33rd memorial service for Ryūhō-in-sama, silver offerings of three pieces each were bestowed through messenger Arakawa Jūemon (Naomori) to the widows of those who committed junshi (ritual suicide) upon her death: Saitō Minbu (Sadayuki), Yamauchi Kotarō, Ōta Mitsuya (Nobumasu), Taki Kazue (Akinori), Suzuki Tamiya (Naotaka), Mizuno Zenza-emon (Katsumitsu), and Yamamoto Genza-emon (Tsunezumi).
From Masachika-sama as well, one silver piece each was bestowed through messenger Ogawa Jihei (Hidenaka).

1. On the 16th of the eighth month, for the monthly inquiry after the Shogun's health, messenger Kusakabe Zendayū was dispatched to Edo, presenting one type of delicacies. Zendayū departed Fukui today.
Note: The date of Zendayū's arrival in Edo is unclear. A reply letter was delivered on the 29th of the same month.

1. On the 27th of the same month, at Sannomaru, from today all ashigaru were ordered to practice firearms and archery, with one koshōgashira attending each session.

1. On the 4th of the ninth month, for the Chōyō (Double Ninth Festival) congratulations, kosode robes were presented through messengers as usual.

1. On the 12th of the same month, to escort Kiyohime-sama, Sakai Genba (Mototomo) and others were dispatched to Kyoto. They departed Fukui today.
Note: They arrived in Kyoto on the 16th of the same month.

1. On the 26th of the same month, Kiyohime-sama departed Kyoto and proceeded to Edo via the Tōkaidō road. Sakai Genba (Mototomo), two monogashira, ōgoban samurai, okachi, and others accompanied her.

1. On the 28th of the same month, Shōbei (Masatsune), son of Matsudaira Shume (Masaakira), was summoned with a stipend of 1,000 koku.
