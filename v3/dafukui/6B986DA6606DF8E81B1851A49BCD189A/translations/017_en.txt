• December 1st: Through Niwa Tōtōmi-no-kami Nagamori, a petition was submitted to Abe Bungo-no-kami Masatake (the duty magistrate), requesting that Princess Toyo be sent as an adopted daughter to Asukai Saemon-no-suke and arranged in marriage with Kazanin Dainagon.

• 2nd of the same month: Regarding the above matter, letters were sent to Kyoto Deputy Matsudaira Kii-no-kami Nobuyasu and Asukai Uemon-no-suke.

• 4th of the same month: As construction of the Reiganjima residence was completed, there was a celebration for the relocation.

• 5th of the same month (past 5 o'clock): When [the lord] attended the castle (yesterday on the 4th, an official document signed by all the senior councilors had arrived from the duty magistrate), the senior councilors were present in assembly, and Duty Magistrate Abe Bungo-no-kami Masatake announced that the petition regarding sending Princess Toyo as an adopted daughter and the marriage arrangement had been approved as requested. As thanks for this, [the lord] made courtesy visits to the senior councilors, chamberlains, magistrates, and junior councilors.

• Same day: Regarding the above matter, a letter was sent to Kyoto Deputy Matsudaira Kii-no-kami Nobuyasu.

• Same day: Regarding the marriage arrangement between Princess Okitsu (sister of Takumi-no-kami Masahira) and Ōtawara Hida-no-kami Kiyonobu, which was approved as petitioned, an envoy was sent to the senior councilors, chamberlains, and magistrates as thanks.

• 7th of the same month: Due to the cold season, a courtesy visit was made to Duty Magistrate Abe Bungo-no-kami Masatake to inquire about his well-being.

• 9th of the same month: Due to the passing of Mito Chūnagon Mitsukuni in his domain (on the 6th), [a visit was made] to inquire about [the shogun's] well-being.
