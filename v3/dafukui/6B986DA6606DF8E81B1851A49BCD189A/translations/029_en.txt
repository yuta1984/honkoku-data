One: June 15th - Due to the summer heat, for inquiry into [His Lordship's] well-being, messenger Negoro Seitayū (Harusada) was
     dispatched to Edo. One type of delicacy was presented. Seitayū departed from Fukui today.
          Note: A reply letter was received on the 29th of the same month.

One: Same month, 18th - An official letter regarding the presentation of Boys' Day congratulatory gifts was received
     at the castle. Retainers' granted items, etc. were as usual.

One: Same month, 20th - Regarding the 18th, during the Hakusan shrine relocation ceremony, peasants from Kaga Province's Ozoe Village and Ōno District's Ushikubi Village and
     Fūarashi Village engaged in a dispute resulting in injuries, so the matter of reporting to the senior councilors was
     conveyed to Lord Ōoka Mino-no-kami (Tadataka). Subsequently, after settlement, the following was again
     conveyed:

                    Memorandum
               One: The relocation ceremony guides for Hakusan Main Shrine's Ōmikami and the two Betsusan shrines were
                    conducted by Genjōin of Echizen Heisenji. The relocation ceremony guide for Ōnamuchi shrine alone
                    was conducted by monks from Kōya.
               One: The Ōmikami relocation ceremony was completed without incident during the morning of June 18th.
                    The Ōnamuchi relocation ceremony was to take place the same day, but people from Kaga's Ozoe Village and those from Echizen's
                    Ushikubi Village and Fūarashi Village quarreled and fought on the mountaintop, so
                    that day was postponed. Due to heavy wind and rain on the 19th, it was further delayed, and the Ōnamuchi relocation ceremony was
                    completed on the 20th. Accordingly, the Betsusan relocation ceremony was also completed on the 20th.
