One: October 9th - His lordship went out to Yoshie for falconry (returned to the palace on the 16th of the same month)

One: Same day - A messenger was dispatched to Edo to inquire about his lordship's well-being as a monthly custom, and offered
     one type of fish as tribute. The aforementioned messenger departed from Fukui today.
     However, a reply letter was received on the 21st of the same month.

One: Same month, 21st - An inner letter regarding the tribute for the Chrysanthemum Festival celebration was
     received at the castle. Gifts bestowed upon retainers, etc., were as usual.

One: Same month, 25th - His lordship went out to Kanazu for falconry (returned to the palace on the last day of the same month)

One: November 4th - His lordship went out to Yoshie for falconry (returned to the palace on the 7th of the same month)

One: Same day - Messenger and guard captain Kunieda Koichirō Meishin was dispatched to Edo
     to inquire about the timing of sankin attendance. He departed from Fukui today.

One: Same month, 7th - This time (on the 25th of last month), as requested by Lord Wakasa-no-kami Naoaki, he was granted retirement, and his eldest son Sahyōe-no-suke Naotsune
     was definitively granted succession to the family headship. Also, as requested by Lord Dewa-no-kami Tsunachika, his younger brother Tanomo Norinori
     was granted a domain allocation (10,000 koku of new rice fields within Unshū). For gratitude regarding these matters, messenger Nagasaka Shigenosuke was
     dispatched to Edo. He departed from Fukui today.
     However, a reply letter was received on the 19th of the same month.

One: Same month, 18th - Due to a summons from the duty officer, messenger Kunieda
     Koichirō Meishin, who had gone to inquire about the timing of sankin attendance, received an official document
     stating that the lord should proceed to the capital during April of next year.
