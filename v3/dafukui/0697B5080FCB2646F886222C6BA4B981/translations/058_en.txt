1. Nothing shall be given as gifts to clerks, minor officials, and others. In general, providing hospitality to them is strictly prohibited.

1. Puppet shows, strange dances, courtesans, and other spectacles and entertainment of any kind shall not be allowed in the village.

1. Regarding clothing, food, and all other matters, one shall not indulge in luxury beyond one's station, but should maintain frugality at all times.

The above articles shall be copied and kept at each village headman's office, and read aloud to all villagers twice a year without fail, on the 7th day of the 1st month and the 7th day of the 7th month. Furthermore, village headmen and senior farmers shall keep this in mind and regularly inform the village farmers, ensuring strict compliance. Therefore, this order is given as above.

　　Jōkyō 4, Year of the Fire Rabbit, 11th month

1. On the 28th day of the 11th month, a person from Shimbo-ura who had traveled to Ōshū Nanbu in the 4th month of this year for ship trading encountered a storm off the coast of Noto on his return voyage and drifted to Korea in the 5th month. Therefore, this ship was sent from Lord Tsushima-no-kami Yoshimasa to Osaka, where it arrived under the jurisdiction of the town magistrate Odakiri Kihyōe Toshimasa.
