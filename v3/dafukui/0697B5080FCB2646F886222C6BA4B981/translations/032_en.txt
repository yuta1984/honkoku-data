1. May 2nd: For the Boys' Day celebration, through an envoy, presented the customary summer kimono and unlined garments as usual, and offered five pieces of silver to the Lord's wife

1. Same month, 4th day: Made formal visit to the castle to celebrate the completion of the imperial enthronement

1. Same month, 6th day: Regarding the above matter, through envoy and castle keeper, presented two varieties in one package to the Shogun

1. Same month, 25th day: Through the imperial messenger and senior councilor Abe Bungo-no-kami [Masatake], received permission to return to the home domain, and was granted 300 pieces of silver, lined garments, and thirty items

1. Same day: Made formal castle visit to express gratitude for the leave permission and received a horse as a gift

1. June 9th: Due to the summer heat, through an envoy presented the customary local products of somen noodles and dried sea bream

1. Same month, 10th day: Departed from Edo, traveling via the Tōkaidō route

1. Same month, 13th day: At the residence of monthly duty senior retainer Honda Sahyōe [Naoshige], regarding the recent order to destroy silver certificates, the retainers including Takachi and various other samurai unanimously petitioned either orally or in writing that they wished to contribute rice from their stipends. When this reached His Lordship's attention, the following was ordered, which Sahyōe conveyed to all monthly duty officers:
　　　　Regarding the recent order to destroy silver certificates, rice should be contributed as a gesture of loyalty
