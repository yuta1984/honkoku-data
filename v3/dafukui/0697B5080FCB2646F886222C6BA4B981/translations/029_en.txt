1. February 4th: Purification ceremonies and prayers at Ise and Nishinomiya were requested and entrusted to Kanri-in temple
　　Note: Misawa Jinemon carried these, but his arrival in Edo is unclear

1. Same month, 7th day: A new guard duty assignment was made for Edo, henceforth establishing two groups of palace guards (goshoin-ban), four groups of great guards (ō-goban), and two groups of caretakers (go-rusui). This matter was announced to the guard commanders today

1. Same day: When samurai retainers submit requests for deathbed adoptions, from now on the guard commander shall examine the original document of the petition, understand its contents, and report accordingly. Should the guard commander be ill or otherwise indisposed, the matter shall be handled through the secretary (fudetsugu), as was announced

1. Same month, 12th day: At Zuigen-ji temple, memorial services for the first anniversary of Lady Shōkei-in were conducted from dawn on the 10th until this morning. At Nanzen-ji temple in Yanaka as well, memorial services were conducted from the evening of the 11th until today

1. Same month, 27th day: Inspector Murakami Santayū (Masamine) was dispatched from Edo, and Heizō, son of Taki Kazue (Akinori), was ordered to commit seppuku

1. March 28th: At Jōkō-in temple, the 27th anniversary memorial service for Lady Seichi-in was conducted from the evening of the 27th until this morning. At Tentoku-ji temple as well, memorial services for Lady Fuyo-hime [daughter of Mitsumichi] were conducted from the 26th until today
