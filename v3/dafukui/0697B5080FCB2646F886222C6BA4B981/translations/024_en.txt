They proceeded to the formal reception hall, where御長蚫 was served, and after the ceremonial attendants and other officials assembled,
Noh performances (Oimatsu, Tamura, Tōhoku, Miwa, Shūgen) were presented, followed by formal cuisine (three soups and nine dishes). Ceremonial gifts (sliced kamaboko and fuko) were also presented.
Lord Kaga-no-kami personally escorted them to their seats, and Lord Chūmu-no-taifu Masatoki was also
escorted to his seat by Lord Yamashiro-no-kami, who brought additional delicacies (grilled river fish).
Lord Yamashiro-no-kami escorted them to their seats, and after the sake ceremony was completed,
the lord's family members attended and formal greetings were exchanged. Lord Kaga-no-kami offered sake cups to his retainer Koma
Mokunosuke Teizumi and other officials, including the caretakers. Lord Yamashiro-no-kami similarly offered
sake to his retainer Matsudaira Shōbei Masatsune and other officials, including the caretakers.
After the banquet concluded, Lord Kaga-no-kami offered sake cups to Mokunosuke and others, including Taki Kazue Akinori,

Minobe Sukesaku Shigefusa, Hagino Gen'emon Masafū, and Arakawa Zen'emon Masatake.
Lord Yamashiro-no-kami similarly offered sake to Shōbei and others, including Takaya Iori Nagafusa, Nagami Jibu'emon Yoshimasa,
and Inaba Hikoshirō. When they departed, they were escorted back to the same place where they had been initially welcomed, and afterward, courtesy visits were made to Lords Kaga-no-kami,
Yamashiro-no-kami, and Aki-no-kami as expressions of gratitude.
