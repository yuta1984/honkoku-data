1. On the 16th day of the 6th month, [the lord] went to the castle for the celebratory ceremonies of Katei

1. On the 17th day of the same month, due to official government business, the provincial village register was submitted
　　to the Ote accounting office through a messenger

1. On the 18th day of the same month, the provincial map and castle map were submitted through a messenger to
　　the Nakanokuchi gate of the castle, and were received by Lord Hikosaka Iki-no-kami Shigetsugu

1. On the 23rd day of the same month, because it became difficult to repay money to those from the Kamigata region who had
　　provided financial assistance from long ago, the following was communicated to the intermediaries through
　　the Kyoto caretaker Matsue Zenbei Bokyū and Ogi Rokurōbei Sadauji stationed in Ōtsu, as instructed by the senior retainer

　　　　　　　　　Memorandum
　　　　　There have been various borrowed silver through intermediaries since the previous generation of Lord Echizen-no-kami, but due to continued
　　　　　poor harvests in the domain, and subsequent unavoidable circumstances with financial difficulties, the initially
　　　　　agreed-upon repayments could not be made properly, causing long-standing hardship
　　　　　Now Lord Echizen-no-kami's domain has been confiscated, and Lord Hyōbu-no-taifu has been
　　　　　granted new domain, but since the yield has decreased, there is no way to make repayments
　　　　　hereafter, resulting in the loss of a considerable amount of gold and silver
　　　　　We can imagine how troublesome this situation is, but those who continue to manage finances
　　　　　under these circumstances must handle business for several years with special consideration
