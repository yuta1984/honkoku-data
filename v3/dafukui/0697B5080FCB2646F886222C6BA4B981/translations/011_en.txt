1. 4th month, 25th day: For the second abstinence-ending ceremony, fish 《marginal note: sea bream》 (one box) was presented through a messenger
　　　　Note: The above fish offerings for abstinence-ending ceremonies on the 9th, 18th, 21st, and 25th of each month
　　　　　are regular practice during years of residence in Edo, so they will not be recorded hereafter
1. Same day: Regarding travel permits for women at checkpoints, when Imakiri checkpoint magistrate Matsudaira Shume-no-suke inquired
　　with the rōjū, instructions were given that they should pass through with the seals of senior retainers as before.
　　Therefore, seal samples of the senior retainers were exchanged and submitted to
　　the checkpoints at Imakiri in Enshū, Fukushima in Shinshū, and Yokogawa in Jōshū
　　　　Note: To Imakiri, [the samples] were sent with a letter to Shume-no-suke; to Fukushima, they were sent via
　　　　　ōban Shimazaki Ichirōzaemon who had returned from Edo; to Yokogawa,

　　　　　they were sent through a messenger to the residence of the administrator at Edo
1. 29th day of the same month: Inspector Nishio Den'bei 《marginal note: Masatora》 was sent from Edo on official business and
　　arrived in Fukui today, so a meeting of senior retainers and officials was held
　　at the residence of senior retainer Saitō Minbu 《marginal note: Sadayuki》
1. 5th month, 4th day:
　　As there was a Buddhist service for the seventh anniversary of Lord Gen'yūin's death, to inquire after [the shogun's] well-being,
　　kudzu (one box) and yam (one basket) were presented through a messenger. An official letter stating that the above presentation had been
　　completed came from rōjū Ōkubo Kaga-no-kami 《marginal note: Tadatomo》
　　　　　　　　Addressed to:
　　　　　　　　　Matsudaira Hyōbu-dayū
