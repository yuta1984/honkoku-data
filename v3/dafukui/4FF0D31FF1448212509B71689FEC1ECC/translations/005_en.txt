[The matter] was announced as requested.

One: On the 3rd day of the 3rd month, Eiheiji [temple representatives] came to the castle and presented ten scrolls in one bundle, having an audience in the Tattan-no-ma. Afterwards, a meal (three soups and nine dishes) was served, gifts were personally bestowed, and Kōkenji and Chintokuji [temples] were ordered to accompany [them].

One: On the 4th day of the same month, regarding Dewa-no-kami Tsunachika-sama's request being granted for Minbu-shōyū Yoshitōru-sama to become his adopted son (announced on the 22nd day of last month), a letter of thanks was sent to Edo.

One: On the 15th day of the same month, regarding the 10,000 koku that had been granted to Minbu-shōyū Yoshitōru-sama being returned to Dewa-no-kami Tsunachika-sama, a letter of thanks was sent to Edo.

One: On the 23rd day of the same month, there was a summons from duty officer Inaba Tango-no-kami Masamichi-dono, and when caretaker Murakami Sandayū Masamine went to attend, he was ordered to assist with the construction work on the castle's surrounding stone walls, and an official letter was presented.
　However, this official letter was sent via foot messenger to [the lord] during his journey.

One: On the same day, regarding Naizen Naotomo-sama being granted court rank and renamed Naizen-no-shō, a letter of thanks was sent to Edo.

One: On the 26th day of the same month, [the lord] departed from Fukui and traveled via the Nakasendō route.

One: On the 28th day of the same month, the official letter ordering assistance with the construction work around Edo Castle arrived at Akasaka post station in Mino Province, and it was reviewed at that station.
