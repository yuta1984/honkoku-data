One: On the 18th day of the 5th month, regarding the desire to offer large stones (thirty pieces) located at Reigenjiima for construction work, a private inquiry was made to Senior Councillor Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami Masanao, who gave instructions to proceed as convenient.

One: On the 19th day of the same month, regarding the desire for Lord Masasato to also visit the assisted construction site for inspection, an inquiry was previously made to Senior Councillor Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami Masanao. This morning, during the audience with Sagami-no-kami, instructions were given to proceed as inquired.

One: On the 21st day of the same month, accompanied by Lord Masasato, a visit was made to the construction site for inspection.
　　Note: On the 27th day of the same month, a similar visit was also made.

One: On the last day of the same month, as requested by Lord Dewa-no-kami Tsunachika, he retired and his adopted son, Minbu-shōyu Yoshitō, was formally appointed as head of the house without question.
　　Note: There should have been ceremonial thanks for this matter, but the details are not clear in the records.
　　One: On the 5th day of the 6th month, Dewa-no-kami changed his name to Geki, and Lord Minbu-shōyu changed his name to Dewa-no-kami.

One: On the 10th day of the 6th month, due to the death of Naiki Masazumi, the younger brother of Lord Takumi-no-kami Masahira, mourning was observed from today until the 12th for the adoptive cousin.

One: On the same day, regarding construction duties for the same reason, an inquiry was made to Senior Councillor Akimoto Tajima-no-kami Takatomo, who gave instructions to suspend duties during the mourning period.
