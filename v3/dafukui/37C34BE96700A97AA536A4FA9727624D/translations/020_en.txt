1. January 28th: Due to having a painful ailment, [the lord] declined to proceed to the castle.

1. February 5th:
   Through messenger Sakai Tenzen Tomofusa, Lord Yoshikuni presented betrothal gifts to Princess Ume.
   In connection with this,
   Lord Yoshishine, for the same celebratory purpose, through messenger Matsubara Hachizaemon Yoshio, presented to Hinonishi Chunagon Kunitoyo: tachi sword, gold horse substitute, formal paper (10 bundles), two kinds and one load, sake barrel money (1000 hiki); to Lord Sakumaru: tachi sword, gold horse substitute, two kinds and one load; to Princess Ume: hair paper (50 booklets), two kinds and one load; to Chihoin-sama and Nishidai-sama: one box of kelp each, one box of dried sea bream each, and sake barrel money of 1000 hiki each.
   Note: Hachizaemon departed from the domain on the 26th of last month and returned to Fukui on February 10th.

1. 26th of the same month: Internal letters and formal documents regarding year-end felicitation presentations were handed over at the castle, and gifts to retainers were as customary.

1. March 6th:
   As Lord Yoshikuni arrived in Edo yesterday on the 5th, [Lord Yoshishine] served together with him to the duty officer Okubo Kaga no Kami Tadamasu.

1. 15th of the same month: For the same reason,
   Together with Lord Yoshikuni, [they] proceeded to the castle and completed the formal greetings, then together proceeded to the Western Citadel where formal greetings were also completed. After leaving the castle, [they] made courtesy visits to the senior councilors, chamberlains, and administrative officers.
