　Granted succession stipend
　Main
　　Current Matsudaira Shume Masatsune (Birth province: Echizen, original name: Shōbei; Masatsune was granted separate stipend from his status as a dependent and served as chief retainer)
　　　　During Lord Yoshishina's era, Hōei 6th year, Year of the Earth Ox, Masatsune was ordered to retire, and his eldest son Katsuemon
　　　　Masamura was granted the family headship, while his second son Kurōzaemon was granted an allocation. Masatsune
　　　　shaved his head and took the name Bokūō, but in Shōtoku 2nd year, Year of the Water Dragon, Katsuemon died, and the following Shōtoku 3rd year, Year of the Water Snake,
　　　　Kurōzaemon also passed away. Since the eldest grandson Kinzō was still young, by gracious intention
　　　　the brothers' granted lands were combined and given again to Masatsune, who was ordered to continue the succession

【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△Honda

　During Lord Tadamasa's era, Kan'ei 1st year, Year of the Wood Rat
　Summoned to service
　　● Honda Minbu Shigekata (Home province: Mikawa, Birth province: Kazusa; surname: Fujiwara, original name: Chikara, religious name: Jōnyū)
　　　　Since Honda Hida-no-kami Narushige had many sons, Lord Tadamasa expressed his intention
　　　　to have one of them serve, and when this matter was reported to higher authority, there was an imperial command to present
　　　　the fifth son Chikara. Subsequently, audience with Lords Taitoku-in and Taiyū-in was
　　　　commanded, and accompanied by Shimada Danjō, both father and son went to the Edo mansion,
　　　　where they were promptly granted audience and received sake cups, and at that time his name was changed to Minbu
　　　　Regarding his ancestors, his great-great-grandfather Honda Sakuzaemon Shigetsugu served under Lord Nobumitsu
　　　　of our lord's legitimate lineage, as Shigetsugu's father Sakuzaemon had served faithfully. Shigetsugu was
　　　　summoned at age seven, and thereafter during Gongen-sama's first campaign at the siege of Terabe Castle in Sanshū,
　　　　he fought according to his station, and subsequently had various military exploits. In Tenshō 2nd year, Year of the Wood Dog, before Lord Hideyasu's birth,
　　　　due to Lady Tsukiyama's jealousy, it became difficult for Lord Chōshō-in to remain within the castle, so at the request to take refuge with Sakuzaemon, he was granted the fief of Sanme
