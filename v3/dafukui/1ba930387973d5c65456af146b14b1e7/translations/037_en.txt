【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△　Makino

　From His Excellency Gongen-sama to Lord Hideyasu
　Served as an attendant from childhood
　　● Makino Tonomo-no-suke Yasusada (home province: Tōtōmi, birth province: Mikawa, surname: Minamoto, original name: Kogengō, Tango)
　　　　Yasusada's father was called Makino Seibē. He served His Excellency Gongen-sama and still
　　　　possesses the vermillion seal document from Eiroku 12 (1569). When Yasusada was young, His Excellency Gongen-sama personally
　　　　inquired, "Should you serve Lord Hideyasu or should you serve
　　　　Lord Takechiyo" (referring to Lord Taitoku-in)? When this imperial will was expressed, Yasusada respectfully replied, "Since you have
　　　　shown me such daily kindness, I would like to serve Lord Hideyasu." At this,
　　　　he laughed and said "You shall be appointed as you wish," and attached him
　　　　to Lord Hideyasu, as has been passed down. During Lord Tadamasa's expedition to Osaka, he served in the second left
　　　　unit of the advance guard. Later, when Lord Tadanao was exiled to Bungo, he remained
　　　　in this province, and when Lord Tadamasa entered the domain, he went out to meet him at Kanazu plateau, where
　　　　he received gracious words and was immediately ordered to guide them
　　　　directly to the castle interior, and thus accompanied them.
