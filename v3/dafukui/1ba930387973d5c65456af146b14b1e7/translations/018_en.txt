【Left Page】
　　　　　　△　Ashida

　In the 5th year of Keichō, year of the Metal Rat (1600), during Lord Hideyasu's era
　Summoned during the Yūki domain period
　　● Katō Shirōbei Yasuhiro (Both home province and birth province: Shinano　Surname: Minamoto　Original name: Yoda Ashida　Initial name: Matsudaira Shinrokurō Uemon-dayū　Buddhist name: Sōgetsu)
　　　　Yasuhiro's father, Ashida Uemon-no-suke Nobutsura, was a descendant in the 27th generation of Uemon-no-jō
　　　　Shimotsuke-no-kami Minamoto no Mitsunobu, the fifth son of Prince Tsunemoto, the Rokuson-ō. For generations they
　　　　had their castle in Ashida village, Yoda-shō, Shinano Province. At that time they served the Takeda family, but after Katsuyori's downfall in the 10th year of Tenshō, year of the Water Horse (1582),
　　　　they served Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), and because Takeda remnants were holed up in various places, they were ordered to completely
　　　　pacify both Kai and Shinano provinces and were granted a golden war fan (still transmitted today). Thereupon Nobutsura first
　　　　had Sanada Masayuki join our side, and when the pledge document that Sanada presented was submitted, Nobutsura's
　　　　messenger, a man called Yoda Jūrozaemon, was granted a rifle (with thunder inlay, still transmitted today).
　　　　In July of the same year, both Suwa and Saku districts of Shinshū were granted to Nobutsura (the certificate document is still transmitted).
　　　　In the same year, he captured the castles of Iwamura-da, Maeyama, Takatana, Odai, and others. The following year, when attacking Iwao Castle,
　　　　Nobutsura was killed in battle.
　　　　　His heir, the eldest son Takefuku-maru (fourteen years old at the time), was appointed to succeed him and was granted an honorary name, title, and sword.
