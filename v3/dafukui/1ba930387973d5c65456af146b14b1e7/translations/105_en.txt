【Left Page】
　　　　　　　　△　Kaneko

Summoned during Lord Hideyasu's reign <<year unknown>>
　　● Kaneko Rokuemon, personal name unknown <<home country Kōzuke, birth country unknown, surname Minamoto>>
　　　　Rokuemon's eldest son, called Kaneko Sanshirō, was granted a stipend for one hundred men and one hundred pieces of silver each,
　　　　and was summoned as a page (koshō). Rokuemon was granted a domain of 300 koku as a retiree, and while Sanshirō
　　　　was serving, it was decreed that the Kaneko designation was unsuitable for the page role and should be changed.
　　　　When asked about his maternal lineage, he reported that she was the daughter of Aoki Kai-no-kami, whereupon
　　　　he was given the name Aoki Kogengō and served, but died of illness in Edo. Regarding his
　　　　ancestors, Kaneko Sakyōshin Masayoshi, a descendant of Kaneko Jūrō Minamoto no Ietada, eldest son of the Sasaki noble house,
　　　　died in battle with Naoe Mototsugu in the 18th year of Tenshō. His younger brother,
　　　　Kaneko Mino-no-kami <<Rokuemon's father, personal name unknown>>, after Sakyōshin's death in battle, controlled the house retainers and yoriki.
　　　　He was lord of Kaneko castle in Gunma district, Kōzuke province, with a domain of approximately 35,000 koku.
　　　　Letters from Uesugi Kagekatsu, Naoe Yamashiro-no-kami Kanetsugu, and others including Kunitsuna Yoshihisa Shigetsune and Nunozawa Kōzuke-no-suke Ujitoshi are preserved to this day. Rokuemon
