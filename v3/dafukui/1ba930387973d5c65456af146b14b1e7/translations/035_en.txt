【Left Page】
　　　　　　△　Okabe

　From Gongen-sama, during Lord Hideyasu's reign
　Attendants
　　● Okabe Bungo-no-kami, personal name unknown (home province: Mikawa, birth province unknown, family name: Fujiwara)
　　　　He served during Lord Hideyasu's reign, and later was assigned to Lord Tadamasa, accompanying him to the Siege of Osaka.
　　　　He died in battle on the 7th day of the 5th month of Keichō 20 (1615). On the previous day, the 6th, in the presence of Gongen-sama,
　　　　when Lord Tadamasa requested to lead the vanguard the next day, permission was not granted, and furthermore,
　　　　the shogun expressed that if there were to be an advance attack the next day, it would cause trouble for the senior retainers.
　　　　At that time, Bungo-no-kami was also present and, upon hearing this,
　　　　said "If that is the shogun's will, then the matter is settled, so you should now withdraw,"
　　　　and accompanied his lord back to the camp. On the following 7th day, Lord Tadamasa made his advance attack, and
　　　　due to the previous day's imperial instruction, Bungo-no-kami died in battle, as is told. At that time, when
　　　　news of Bungo's death in battle reached the shogun's ears, it is also told that he said "Such a thing would happen."
　　　　Regarding the Okabe surname, there was an elderly lady-in-waiting called Okabe who served Taitokuin-sama,
　　　　and she was Bungo-no-kami's mother. Because of this, Okabe became the family name.
