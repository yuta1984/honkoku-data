【Left Page】
　　　　　△Honda

　When Lord Hideyasu was twelve years old, he was assigned by Lord Ieyasu (Gongen-sama)
　From Tensho 13 (1585, Year of the Wood Rooster), he served faithfully
　　● Honda Izunokami Tomimasa (Both his ancestral domain and birthplace were Mikawa; family name Fujiwara; childhood name Genshiro, later called Shima, then changed to Tanba-no-kami; Buddhist name Genkaku)
　　　　Tomimasa's father, Magozaemon Shigetomi, was a descendant in the 24th generation of the ancestor Tankai-ko, and was a resident of
　　　　Bungo Province descended from one called Honda Sukehide, being a descendant of the 10th generation, with several generations residing in
　　　　Mikawa Province and serving there, as has been passed down. It is also passed down that he was of the same family line as Honda Sado-no-kami Masanobu. Since Shigetomi was
　　　　assigned to serve Lord Nobuyasu of Okazaki, he went into retirement thereafter. Tomimasa
　　　　served as a substitute for Senchiyo, the son of Shigetomi's younger brother Honda Sakuzaemon Shigetsugu, and was assigned to Lord
　　　　Hideyasu and sent to Osaka (he was fifteen years old at the time). On this occasion, he was granted an audience with Lord Ieyasu,
　　　　who personally bestowed upon him kind words. Thereafter, he faithfully accompanied Lord Hideyasu on
　　　　every military campaign. When Lord Ieyasu's retirement residence was built in Suruga, Lord Hideyasu
　　　　sent Tomimasa there, and for his service in the logging operations on Mount Fuji, as a reward
　　　　Lord Ieyasu personally bestowed upon him a sword with "Daiza" inscription. This was the sword that he had
　　　　worn at the Battle of Sekigahara, and though he treasured it greatly, for this occasion's reward
