　　　　　△Honda

　When Lord Hideyasu was twelve years old, he was assigned by Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu)
　From Tenshō 13 (1585), he served faithfully in official service
　　● Honda Izu-no-kami Tomimasa (native province: Mikawa, family name: Fujiwara, childhood name: Genshirō, middle period called Shima, later changed to Tamba-no-kami, Buddhist name: Genkaku)
　　　　Tomimasa's father, Magosaemon Shigetomi, was a descendant of the 24th generation of ancestor Ōmi-kō, a resident of Bungo Province
　　　　It is said that he was the tenth-generation descendant of one Honda Sukehide, and for several generations resided in Mikawa Province
　　　　serving there according to tradition. It is also handed down that he was related to Honda Sado-no-kami Masanobu. Shigetomi
　　　　was assigned to Lord Okazaki Saburō Nobuyasu, but after that went into retirement. Tomimasa
　　　　became the adopted son of Senchiyo, son of Shigetomi's younger brother Honda Sakuzaemon Shigetsugu, was assigned to Lord Hideyasu
　　　　and was sent to Osaka (at that time he was fifteen years old). At this time, he was granted an audience with Gongen-sama,
　　　　and received direct gracious words from him. Thereafter, he faithfully served as escort
　　　　every time Lord Hideyasu went on campaign. When Gongen-sama built his retirement residence in Suruga,
　　　　Tomimasa was sent by Lord Hideyasu for the timber cutting at Mount Fuji, and as a reward
　　　　Gongen-sama personally bestowed upon him a sword with the inscription "Ōsa." This was a sword that had been worn
　　　　at the Battle of Sekigahara and was treasured by him, but for this reward
