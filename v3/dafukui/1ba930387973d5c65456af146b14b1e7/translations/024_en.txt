　　　　　　△　Sasaji

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, at Yuki in Shimōsa Province
　Summoned to service (year unknown)
　　● Sasaji Daizen Masatoki (Home province and birth province both Owari, family name Minamoto, Buddhist name Keien)
　　　　His father was the son of one called Uemura Sahei, who was an illegitimate child
　　　　of Yamagata Saburōbei Masakage, a retainer of Takeda Shingen. Sahei lived in Owari Province and died as a masterless samurai.
　　　　After Daizen was summoned to service, he was ordered to become part of the lineage
　　　　of Sasaji Hyōgo Shigemasa, and accordingly changed his family name to Sasaji.
　　　　　　Masatoki had an elder brother called Yamagata Takumi, who during the Osaka campaigns accompanied Lord Tadanao
　　　　　　and at Tennōji charged into Sanada's formation and performed the first spear attack. Regarding
　　　　　　the lack of reward for this service, he petitioned several times from Lord Tadanao's reign through
　　　　　　Lord Tadamasa's reign, but was put off. Still expressing dissatisfaction, he requested
　　　　　　dismissal from service, which was granted, and Takumi's house was terminated.
