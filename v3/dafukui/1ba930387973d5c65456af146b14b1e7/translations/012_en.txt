【Left Page】
　During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in Kanbun 9 (1669)
　He was granted succession of the house at one year of age
　　　 Honda Magotaro Nagazane (birthplace: Echizen)
　　　　In Genroku 11 (1698), the Edo Asakusa mansion was
　　　　confiscated for shogunate official use, and replacement land was granted in Kita-Honjo, as
　　　　conveyed by the monthly senior councilor Lord Abe Bungonomkami.
　　　　Honda Daizen received the Honjo mansion as Nagazane's representative.
　　　　During Lord Yoshishina's reign, in Genroku 17 (1704), Nagazane was first in Edo
　　　　as an attendant. When he had an audience with Lord Tsunayoshi (Jokoin-sama), he proceeded by palanquin up to the dismounting area within the castle, and
　　　　also brought his own caretaker into the palace halls, and while in residence, had someone carry a ceremonial umbrella in Edo.
　　　　During his residence, there was a memorial service for Lord Ienobu (Ganyuin-sama), and on that occasion, at the main hall of Toeizan,
　　　　when the assembled hatamoto participated in worship, Nagazane performed the ritual worship and presented incense offerings.
