【Right Page】
　　These are excluded because stipend records exist
1. For ancestors who were initially summoned in low positions but later received promotions, use
　　the era name from when they were first summoned
　　　However, for those whose grandfathers, fathers, etc. served the lord but had not yet
　　　entered the guard units, do not establish these as founders
1. For ancestors whose service to the lord's house was interrupted but who did not serve other houses,
　　and whose descendants were later summoned and returned to service, record them at the end of the reign when the ancestor was
　　summoned, and record the reason for the interruption at that
　　location. The same applies when someone was once granted leave and later recalled

【Left Page】
　　The same treatment applies
　　　However, for those who served other houses briefly after becoming ronin, they are treated as
　　　new retainers, so record them under the reign when they returned to service
1. For those who inherited titles from the maternal side and therefore submitted
　　genealogical records of their maternal grandfather, even if they are second or third sons of the main house,
　　due to the principle of separate houses, place them in the section of the reign when they themselves were summoned
