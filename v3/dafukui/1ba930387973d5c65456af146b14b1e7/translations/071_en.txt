　　　　　　　△　Ōtō

　Summoned at Yūki during Lord Hideyasu's reign
　　● Ōtō Kotarō, personal name unknown (home province and birth province both Sagami, surname Minamoto)
　　　　The ancestor was originally named Takeda in ancient times, but when serving the Hōjō family, since Kōshū Takeda Shingen and the Hōjō family were in conflict, both the title and curtain crest were changed.
　　　　He was called Ōtō Shikibu Daiyū, but his formal name is not known. His Buddhist name was Kinya. Previously for generations they resided in Odawara in Sagami Province. By virtue of documents granted by the Koga Kubō family, two letters bearing the name Dōtetsu are preserved to this day. Also, when he went to Lord Yūki Masakatsu as reinforcement from the Hōjō family together with Matsuda, Daidōji and others, leading over ten thousand troops, he received commendation letters from Lord Masakatsu, as well as commendation letters from Takeda Shingen, generations of the Hōjō family, the Imagawa family, and others - in total about twenty old letters and old documents are preserved to this day. After the fall of the Hōjō family, while living as a masterless samurai, he was summoned to Lord Hideyasu at Yūki with exceptional promises, and afterward was granted a high stipend of one thousand koku in Echizen, with the verbal promise that the promised fief would be granted again...
