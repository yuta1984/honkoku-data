　　　　　　　△　Ōfuji

　Summoned at Yūki during Lord Hideyasu's reign
　　● Ōfuji Kotarō, personal name unknown (home province and birth province both Sagami, clan name Minamoto)
　　　　The ancestor in ancient times had the original name Takeda, but while serving the Hōjō family, when Kai Province's Takeda　　　
　　　　Shingen and the Hōjō family became enemies, he changed both his title and crest on banners,
　　　　and was called Ōfuji Shikibu-no-taifu. The full name is not clearly known, but his Buddhist name was
　　　　called Kanaya. Before that, generations had resided in Odawara in Sagami Province. There is a letter
　　　　granted by the Koga Kubō house with "Dōtetsu" written on it, and two letters are still preserved today. Also, when
　　　　he went to assist Lord Yūki Masakatsu on behalf of the Hōjō family, leading over ten thousand
　　　　troops together with Matsuda, Daidōji and others, he received commendation letters from Lord Masakatsu and others, plus
　　　　commendation letters from Takeda Shingen, successive generations of the Hōjō family, the Imagawa family, etc., altogether about
　　　　twenty old letters and ancient documents are still preserved today. After the fall of the Hōjō family, while living as a rōnin,
　　　　he was summoned by Lord Hideyasu at Yūki with exceptional promises, and afterward in Echizen was granted
　　　　a high stipend of one thousand koku, with the promise that the promised fief would be granted again, as stated in his lordship's words
