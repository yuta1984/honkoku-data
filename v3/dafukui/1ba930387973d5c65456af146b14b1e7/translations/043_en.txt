【Left Page】
　　　　　　　　△　Hohokabe

　Attendant from Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) to Lord Hideyasu
　　● Hohokabe Yukie Ieshige (both home province and birth province: Tanba, surname: Minamoto, original names: Jūrōsaburō, Kuhei)
　　　　The ancient affairs of ancestors are unknown. Ieshige's father was Hohokabe Gon-no-kami Takaiie (with the title Ippōken), and his son
　　　　Ieshige was granted an audience with Gongen-sama during his imperial visit to Kyoto at Fushimi (on that
　　　　occasion he presented ten bundles of true feathers), and when he expressed his gratitude, he received the lord's command to be summoned to service, accepted respectfully,
　　　　and served as an attendant. Later he was assigned to Lord Hideyasu and accompanied him to Echizen.
　　　　During Lord Tadanao's reign, he served in the two-year Osaka campaigns, and at that time was entrusted with
　　　　one unit of mounted retainers and served accordingly. Later he was appointed to the assistant castle warden role and served
　　　　through successive reigns until Lord Mitsumichi's reign, Keian 3 (1650, year of the Metal Tiger), serving until the age of ninety-seven.
　　　　His eldest son, called Heisaburō Shigemasa, died in battle during the Battle of Sekigahara in Keichō 5 (1600, year of the Metal Rat).
