　　　　　　　△　Tagaya

　Served from Yuki during Lord Hideyasu's reign
　　● Tagaya Gyōbu Murahiro (birth province and home province both Shimōsa, family name Taira, original name Gondayū)
　　　　His ancestors served as hereditary retainers of Yuki for several generations. His grandfather Tagaya Oki-no-kami Tamehiro
　　　　resided at Oguri Castle in Yuki. His son Tagaya Aki-no-kami Masahiro also
　　　　resided at Oguri Castle. During the Tenshō era, when Lord Hideyoshi returned from his Ōshū campaign and
　　　　entered Lord Harutomo's residence, perhaps because he was a castle lord under Yuki's command,
　　　　he was specially granted a vermillion seal. His son Tagaya Gyōbu Murahiro, during Lord Hideyasu's reign,
　　　　distinguished himself in military service during the Sekigahara conflict, and because Tsuchiura in Jōshū was a strategic fortress location,
　　　　he was appointed as castle deputy. During Lord Tadanao's reign, at the time of the Ōsaka campaigns, he served as captain and
　　　　accompanied his lord. During Lord Tadamasa's reign, when Lord Masataka was young,
　　　　he was appointed as guardian and served for ten years.
