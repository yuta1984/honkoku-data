　　　　　　　△　Hasebe

　From Lord Ieyasu to Lord Hideyasu
　Attached retainer
　　● Hasebe Uneme (given name unknown, home province: Echizen, birth province unknown, family name unknown, Buddhist name: Kyūraku)
　　　　It is said by tradition that [he was] a descendant of Hasebe Chōhyōe-no-jō Nobuzura from ancient times, but the details of succession are
　　　　unknown. Around the Eiroku era (1558-1570), while serving under Asakura Yoshikage, [he] got into an unexpected quarrel and
　　　　killed his opponent, then fled to Noto Province. Due to ancestral connections with the same family name, [he] took
　　　　refuge with Chō Kurōzaemon. Later [he] went to Kyoto and learned the instruction of Noh chanting and
　　　　matters of etiquette. At that time, Lord Hideyasu was still called Lord Ogimaru and
　　　　had become the adopted son of Lord Hideyoshi. While residing in Kyoto, [Uneme] provided instruction in Noh chanting
　　　　and had regular access [to the residence]. Around Tenshō 12-13 (1584-1585), when Lord Ieyasu came to the capital,
　　　　Lords Hideyoshi and Hideyasu were seated together with [Ieyasu], and Uneme was
　　　　summoned before them. It was said that Lord Ogimaru's conduct was excellent and [Ieyasu] was well pleased,
　　　　so as a reward, Lord Ieyasu bestowed a Bizen Kanemitsu sword, and Lord Hideyoshi also
