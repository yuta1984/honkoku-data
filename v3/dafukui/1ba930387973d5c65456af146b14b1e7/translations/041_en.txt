【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△　Hasebe

　From Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) to Lord Hideyasu
　Attendant
　　● Hasebe Uneme, personal name unknown (home province: Echizen, birth province unknown, family name unknown, Buddhist name: Kyūraku)
　　　　It is said that [he] was a descendant of the ancient Hasebe Chōhyōe-no-jō Nobitsura, but the details of succession
　　　　are unknown. Around the Eiroku era, while serving under Asakura Yoshikage, [he] got into an unexpected fight,
　　　　killed his opponent, and fled to Noto Province. Due to ancestral connections with the same family name, [he]
　　　　hid at Chō Kurōzaemon's residence. Later [he] went to Kyoto and learned the arts of Noh instruction and
　　　　etiquette. At that time, when Lord Hideyasu was still called Lord Ogimaru and had
　　　　become the adopted son of Lord Hideyoshi while residing in Kyoto, [Hasebe] gave instruction in Noh
　　　　and had access [to the residence]. Around Tenshō 12-13 (1584-1585), when Gongen-sama made his imperial visit to Kyoto,
　　　　the three lords - Hideyoshi, and Hideyasu - sat together and Uneme was summoned
　　　　before them. Being greatly pleased with Lord Ogimaru's conduct over a long period,
　　　　as a reward Gongen-sama bestowed a Bizen Kanemitsu sword, and Lord Hideyoshi also
