　　　　　　△　Koma

　Served since Lord Tadamasa's childhood
　　●  Koma Ise Takasumi (Both home province and birth province: Yamato; Surname: Fujiwara; Original name: Moku; Served as chief retainer through the reigns of Lords Tadamasa and Mitsumichi)
　　　　His ancestor Koma Yamashiro-no-kami Ieyoshi and his son Yamashiro-no-kami Iemasa, these two generations resided in
　　　　a place called Koma village near Kizu in Yamashiro Province. Even today there is a mountain with castle ruins in that area, and they
　　　　held a domain of 20,000 koku. Since Iemasa had no heir, he adopted a descendant of the Kujō family (name unknown)
　　　　as an adopted son-in-law to succeed to the family headship. His son was Koma Settsu-no-kami Sanechika (the family surname was Ariwara, but since his real father was from the Kujō family,
　　　　it was changed to Fujiwara. The family crest was a circle with the character "大", but
　　　　at this time it was changed to a wheel treasure. Sanechika is considered the restorer of the family). He resided in a place called Iwasaka in Hase-gō, Shirono-kami District, Yamato Province, and
　　　　changed his territorial name to Koma (since he moved from Yamashiro Province to Yamato Province,
　　　　details are unclear). He is said to have achieved military
　　　　merit many times in Yamato Province and other nearby provinces. Sanechika's son, Genba-no-jō Sanehide (Takasumi's
　　　　father), built his castle in Koma village in Yamato Province,
　　　　attacked and conquered Kishida Minbu-shōyū, lord of Kishida Castle in the same province, and came to hold both Koma and Kishida castles,
　　　　totaling over 60,000 koku. During the time of Miyoshi Shuri-dayū and Matsunaga Danjō-shōhitsu, he
　　　　distinguished himself in battle many times, but after Danjō's death in battle, he followed and served
　　　　Kitabatake Tomonori, the governor of Ise Province. Around that time in Yamato Province, Akiyama Tōtōmi-no-kami, Kaishige Kai-no-kami, Sawa Mikawa-no-kami, Koma
