【Right Page】
　　　　He was called Matsudaira Genjirou Yasukuni and resided in Komoro Castle in the same province. Later he was appointed to the position of Shuri-dayuu.
　　　　Nobutsura's second son Fukuchiyo-maru (thirteen years old) came of age in the fourth month of the 14th year of Tenshou, year of the Fire Dog (1586), in the presence of Gongen-sama. He personally cut his hair and granted him an honorary name, title, sword, hair ornaments, and other items (each of these are still transmitted today). He was called Matsudaira Shinrokurou Yasumasa (later renamed Yasuhiro). In the 18th year (1590), when over a thousand ronin staged an uprising in Aegi Valley in Shinshu, Yasukuni and Yasuhiro attacked and defeated them, killing their leader Tomono Gyoubu along with over 380 soldiers (the letter of commendation from the Taiko is still preserved today).
　　　　In the same year, during the destruction of the Hojo family, Nishimaki Castle in Joshu was defended by Hojo retainers Tatome Suo and Otani Tatewaki Zaemon, but Yasukuni and Yasuhiro captured it with a single force and captured Tatome and Otani (the letter of commendation is still preserved today). They also captured Ishikura Castle. However, Yasukuni met with misfortune and died. Therefore, his legacy was granted to Yasuhiro (the certificate document still exists today). When the eight Kanto provinces became domains, he was ordered to transfer to Fujioka in Kozuke Province and was granted both Midorino district in the same province and Hanzawa district in Musashi Province. After this he was ennobled and appointed to the position of Uemon-dayuu. In the 5th year of Keicho, year of the Metal Rat (1600), for certain reasons he was in confinement, but during the confrontation between Lord Hideyasu and Uesugi Kagekatsu, he was summoned to the military camp at Utsunomiya in Shimotsuke Province. At this time he was called Kato Shirobei, and was immediately ordered to defend Nikko. When entering Echizen Province, he was granted land at Kinomoto in Ono District.

【Left Page】
　　　　He resided there. At this time, he was granted long-barreled rifles (two) with the instruction to maintain the route between Fukui (these are still possessed today). When Lord Hideyasu passed away, he shaved his head and took the name Sōgetsu.
　　　　When Lord Tadanao set out for Osaka, there was a command stating that Ono District would become a place of uprising, so he served as castle keeper of Ono.
