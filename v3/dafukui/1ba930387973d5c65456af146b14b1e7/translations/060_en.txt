　　　　　　　△　Nagami

　Summoned at Yuki during Lord Hideyasu's reign (year unknown)
　　● Nagami Shima Yoshitsugu (home province unknown, birth province Mikawa, surname Fujiwara, original name Menju Chūzaemon)
　　　　Yoshitsugu's grandfather served as chief retainer from Lord Tadamasa's reign through Lord Mitsumichi's reign.
　　　　Menju Kozaburō (formal name unknown) is said to have been the lord of Tahara Castle in Mikawa Province. His descendant Hyōzaemon
　　　　lived in retirement at Koyama in Mikawa Province. His wife was the daughter of Nagami Awa-
　　　　no-kami Yoshihide, younger sister of Chōshō-in-sama. Their son Shima Yoshitsugu was summoned by Lord Hideyasu.
　　　　When Lord Tadamasa was five years old, he was appointed as guardian. Before the Osaka campaigns, at Fuchū in Suruga,
　　　　by order of Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), he changed to the surname Nagami as an in-law of Lord Hideyasu.
　　　　At this time, by gracious order, he was granted a shikoro hood (light yellow damask) and shoulder garment (brown damask).
　　　　Later he was granted aloes wood (with a handwritten note). Upon returning from the Osaka campaigns, he was summoned before Gongen-sama,
　　　　who directly inquired about the locations of Lord Tadamasa's distinguished military achievements.
