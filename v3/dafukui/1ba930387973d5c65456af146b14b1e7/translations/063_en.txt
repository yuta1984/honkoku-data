　　　　　　　　△　Nagami

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, Tenshō 19, year of Metal Rabbit (1591), summoned to service at Yūki
　　● Nagami Awaji-no-kami Yoshikatsu (both ancestral domain and birth province: Mikawa, surname: Fujiwara)
　　　　　Yoshikatsu's father, Awaji-no-kami Yoshihide, was lord of Chirifu Castle in Sanshū with a domain of 50,000 koku.
　　　　　When Gongen-sama entered and stayed at Chirifu Castle, he later attacked castles such as
　　　　　Hirose and Iho in Sanshū, and Yoshihide provided military assistance, displaying loyal service.
　　　　　Perhaps for this reason, when Gongen-sama expressed his desire to become related by marriage,
　　　　　Yoshihide promised his daughter (Lady Chōshōin) who was then two years old.
　　　　　Later, during the Genki years when she had grown up, she was taken in marriage.
　　　　　It is said that during the Tenshō years, due to certain circumstances, Yoshihide met his downfall
　　　　　and Chirifu Castle fell. Consequently, Awaji-no-kami Yoshikatsu became a masterless samurai
　　　　　and, having connections to the head priest of Chirifu Daimyōjin shrine, took refuge there.
　　　　　Through Lady Chōshōin's recommendation, he was summoned to service by Lord Hideyasu.
　　　　　During Lord Tadanao's reign, he served in the Osaka campaigns.
