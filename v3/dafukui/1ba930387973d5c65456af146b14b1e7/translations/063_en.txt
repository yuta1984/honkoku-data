　　　　　　　　△　Nagami

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, Tenshō 19, year of Metal Rabbit (1591), summoned to service at Yuki
　　● Nagami Awaji-no-kami Yoshikatsu (both home province and birth province: Mikawa, surname: Fujiwara)
　　　　　Yoshikatsu's father, Awaji-no-kami Yoshihide, was lord of Chirifu Castle in Sanshū with a domain of 50,000 koku.
　　　　　When Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) entered and stayed at Chirifu Castle, and afterwards when he attacked
　　　　　the castles of Hirose and Iho in Sanshū, Yoshihide provided military assistance and rendered loyal service.
　　　　　Perhaps for this reason, when Ieyasu expressed his desire to become related through marriage, at that time
　　　　　Yoshihide promised his two-year-old daughter (later known as Chōshōin-sama), and later during the Genki
　　　　　years when she had gradually grown up, she was taken in marriage. It is said that during the Tenshō years,
　　　　　due to certain circumstances, Yoshihide committed suicide and Chirifu Castle fell. As a result, Awaji-no-kami
　　　　　Yoshikatsu became a rōnin and, having connections to the chief priest position of Chirifu Daimyōjin shrine,
　　　　　he relied on this and remained there. However, through the request of Chōshōin-sama, he was summoned
　　　　　to serve Lord Hideyasu. During Lord Tadanao's reign, he accompanied the lord to the Siege of Osaka.
