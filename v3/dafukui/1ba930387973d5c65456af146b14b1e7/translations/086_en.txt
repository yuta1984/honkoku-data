【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△　Atsumi

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned at Yūki (year unknown)
　　● Atsumi Muteemon, personal name unknown (home province Mikawa, birth province Tōtōmi, family name Fujiwara, original name Sukezaemon)
　　　　Muteemon's father was called Atsumi Kawachi, who served for generations under Ogasawara Mimasaka-no-kami, lord of Yokosuka Castle in Enshū, and Kawachi was granted a stipend of 1,000 koku. After Mimasaka-no-kami died in battle, he became a rōnin. During the Ōshū campaign, he borrowed troops from Lord Ii Hyōbu-shōyū and participated in the battle, performing distinguished service in the vanguard at Kunohe. Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) was impressed and, saying "he fought well even without hands," gave him the name Muteemon and summoned him to service. Later, due to certain circumstances, he was placed under the custody of Lord Ii Hyōbu and remained there for a while, but Lord Hideyasu summoned him at Yūki. When Echizen Province was granted to him, Muteemon was appointed to oversee castle construction, went ahead to Echizen, and served in this capacity until the construction was completed. When Lord Tadamasa entered Echizen, he had received prior instructions to remain in the province and immediately entered into service.
