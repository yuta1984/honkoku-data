　　　　　　　△　Suganuma

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned to service at Yūki (year unknown)
　　● Yamamoto Kuranokami Narimoto (original province unknown, birth province Mikawa, surname Minamoto, original name Tsushimanokami, Buddhist name Dōkyū)
　　　　It is said that the lineage continued from ancient times from Kamo Jirō, and in recent times from Yamamoto Kansuke Nyūdō Dōki,
　　　　but since the genealogy was lost in a fire, the details are unclear. He was summoned at Yūki, accompanied [his lord] to the
　　　　Osaka campaigns and served as a unit commander. What kind of distinguished service he performed on that occasion
　　　　is unclear, but it is said he received a Fujishima short sword from Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), which is still preserved to this day.
　　　　His eldest son Yamamoto Kunainosuke was granted a separate stipend and served, but died young.
