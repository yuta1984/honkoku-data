　　　　　　　△　Kamitsukawa

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned at Yūki
　　● Kamitsukawa Saemon Saneki (home province and birth province both Shimotsuke, surname Fujiwara, original name Utsunomiya, has separate genealogy)
　　　　　His ancestor was a descendant in the 19th generation of Taishokan Kamatari, the second son of Utsunomiya Yasaburō Yoritsuna, named Kamitsu-
　　　　　kawa Etchū-no-kami Yorinari. Thereafter generations lived in Kamitsukawa in Shimotsuke Province. Hence this became their title.
　　　　　In Keichō 2nd year, year of the Fire Rooster (1597), when the Utsunomiya clan fell into ruin, Kamitsukawa Aki-
　　　　　no-kami Narinari became a masterless samurai. His son Saemon Saneki was summoned
　　　　　at Yūki in the early Keichō period, accompanied [the lord] to Echizen in the 6th year, and in the 12th year when Lord Tadamasa received
　　　　　Anegasaki in Kazusa Province and relocated there, along with Okabe Bungo, Nagami Shima, Menju Shōgen and others,
　　　　　over ten retainers were ordered to accompany him and served there. He accompanied [the lord] to the Osaka campaigns, and in Genna 1st year, year of the Wood Rabbit
　　　　　(1615) when Osaka Castle fell, when Lord Tadamasa led the vanguard and was first to enter the main keep, he served as
　　　　　banner magistrate and planted the banner. Also at that time, his personal banner bore the design of a galloping horse,
