　　　　　　　　△　Amagata

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned at Yūki (year unknown)
　　● Amagata Yamashiro-no-kami Michinori (home province: Sagami, birth province: Tōtōmi, family name: Fujiwara)
　　　　His ancestor's original name was Shuto, and for generations they resided in a place called Yamauchi in Sagami Province, hence they used Yamauchi as
　　　　their title. They served the Tokugawa house and became lord of Kakegawa Castle in Enshū.
　　　　Later they were granted domain over a place called Amagata in the same province, but due to circumstances withdrew to Mount Kōya,
　　　　where Lord Hideyasu summoned him at Yūki, and he accompanied the lord to Echizen. During the Osaka campaigns,
　　　　he served as guard captain (ban-gashira) and was assigned under Lord Dewa-no-kami to perform military duties.

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, Kan'ei 2, year of Wood Ox (1625)
　Granted succession to family headship
　　　Amagata Gorōzaemon Michitomo (birth province: Echizen, original name: Kin'ya)
