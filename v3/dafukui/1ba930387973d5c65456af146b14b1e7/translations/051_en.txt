【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△　Mizutani

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, in the sixth year of Keichō, year of Kanoto-Ushi (1601)
　Summoned to service
　　● Mizutani Oribe Masayoshi (home province and birth province both Hitachi, family name Minamoto)
　　　　His ancestors served the Yuki house for generations. Masayoshi's grandfather Banryūsai Masamura was the lord of
　　　　Shimodate Castle in Hitachi Province. His son Mizutani Hōki-no-kami Katsuyoshi was the second son of Banryūsai Masamura, but
　　　　died of illness in his prime. Therefore, Masamura's eldest son Mizutani Ukyō-dayū Katsutoshi was Masayoshi's uncle,
　　　　so following him, he accompanied Lord Hideyasu when he entered Echizen Province, arriving as far as Aiwatari in Nōshū.
　　　　However, when Ukyō-dayū Katsutoshi was summoned from Edo for official business and returned,
　　　　he sent his nephew Oribe Masayoshi as his representative to directly accompany the lord to Echizen. During the Ōsaka
　　　　campaigns as well, he served in attendance upon Lord Tadanao.

　During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, in the third year of Keian, year of Kanoe-Tora (1650), granted family headship
　　Adopted son
　　　Mizutani Jizaemon Ujiyoshi (home province: Hitachi, birth province: Echizen, actual father: Sasa Jizaemon Shigenao)
