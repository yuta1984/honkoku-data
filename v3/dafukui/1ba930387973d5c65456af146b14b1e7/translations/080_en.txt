　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, Shōhō 1st year, year of the Wood Monkey (1644) Granted inherited domain
　　　Takaya Rokurōzaemon Yorinaga (birth province Echizen)
　　　　Yorinaga died in Kanbun 13th year, year of the Earth Ox (1673), and the inherited domain was
　　　　granted to his eldest son Zenemon Nagahide. He passed away in Kyōhō 1st year, year of the Fire Monkey (1716), and his eldest son Rokurōzaemon Nagaharu succeeded him,
　　　　but in Kyōhō 5th year, year of the Metal Rat (1720) he was confined due to illness, and the main family line was extinguished.

　During Lord Yoshikuni's reign, Kyōhō 5th year, year of the Metal Rat (1720)
　Summoned
　　Current head　Takaya Genshichi Naganori (birth province Echizen)
　　　　During Lord Yoshishina's reign, Enpō 1st year, year of the Water Ox (1673), his father Yorinaga was granted a domain assignment at Yoshie and served there,
　　　　but in Genroku 9th year, year of the Fire Rat (1696) he did not meet expectations, was granted leave, and was ordered to live in confinement
　　　　at his elder brother Zenemon Nagahide's residence. The following year his son Sakujūrō Nagamasa was summoned,
　　　　but died young shortly after and the line was extinguished. Naganori was raised under the care
　　　　of his nephew Rokurōzaemon, but after Rokurōzaemon's illness, the family name was extinguished, so he was summoned again.

【Left Page】
　　　　　　　　△　Okabe

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned at Yūki
　　● Okabe Gorōbē, personal name unknown (home province Suruga, birth province unknown, surname Fujiwara)
　　　　His ancestor was a descendant of Taishokan Kamatari-kō, of the lineage of Okabe Gon-no-kami Kiyotsuna, according to genealogical records.
　　　　In recent times, Okabe Tamba-no-kami Mototsuna served the Imagawa family for generations, and therefore
　　　　still possesses several documents bearing the Imagawa family seal. Later he served under Takeda Shingen and his son Katsuyori,
　　　　and therefore still possesses certificates of the Okabe family's headship (with Katsuyori's direct seal) as well as licensing documents
　　　　for service vessels. Later he also served the Hōjō family, as evidenced by commendation letters from Ujiyasu and Ujimasa.
　　　　Subsequently, when he served under Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), there still exists a
　　　　document written by Deputy Governor Ina Nōzō.
