　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, Shōhō 1st year, year of the Wood Monkey (1644) Granted inherited domain
　　　Takaya Rokurōzaemon Yorinaga (birth province Echizen)
　　　　Yorinaga died in Kanbun 13th year, year of the Earth Ox (1673), and the inherited domain was
　　　　granted to his eldest son Zenemon Nagahide. In Kyōhō 1st year, year of the Fire Monkey (1716) he passed away, and his eldest son Rokurōzaemon Nagataka succeeded, but in
　　　　Kyōhō 5th year, year of the Metal Rat (1720) he went into confinement due to illness, and the main family line was discontinued.

　During Lord Yoshikuni's reign, Kyōhō 5th year, year of the Metal Rat (1720)
　Summoned
　　Current head　Takaya Genshichi Naganori (birth province Echizen)
　　　　During Lord Yoshishina's reign, Enpō 1st year, year of the Water Ox (1673) his father Yorinaga was granted a domain assignment at Yoshie and served there, but in
　　　　Genroku 9th year, year of the Fire Rat (1696) he did not meet expectations and was given leave, ordered to live in confinement
　　　　at his elder brother Zenemon Nagahide's residence. The following year his son Sakujūrō Nagamasa was summoned but
　　　　died young shortly after and the line was discontinued. Naganori was raised by his nephew Rokurōzaemon, but
　　　　after Rokurōzaemon's illness the family name was discontinued, so he was summoned again.

【Left Page】
　　　　　　　　△　Okabe

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned at Yūki
　　● Okabe Gorōbē, personal name unknown (home province Suruga, birth province unknown, family name Fujiwara)
　　　　His ancestor was a descendant of Ōshikonokami Kamatari, and according to genealogy he is a descendant of Okabe Gon-no-kami Kiyotsuna.
　　　　In recent times, Okabe Tamba-no-kami Mototsuna served the Imagawa family for generations, and because of this he still possesses
　　　　several documents from the Imagawa family. Later he served under Takeda Shingen and his son Katsuyori,
　　　　so he still possesses certificates of the Okabe family's headship and documents with Katsuyori's personal seal, as well as boat service licenses.
　　　　Later he also served the Hōjō family, as evidenced by commendation letters from Ujiyasu and Ujimasa. Subsequently when he served Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu), there remains a
　　　　document written by deputy Ina Nōzō.
