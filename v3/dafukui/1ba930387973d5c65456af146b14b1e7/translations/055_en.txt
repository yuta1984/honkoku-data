【Left Page】
　　　　　　△　Minagawa

　Summoned to service at Yuki during Lord Hideyasu's reign (year unknown)
　　● Minagawa Heiuemon Katsuteru (both home and birth provinces: Shimotsuke, surname: Fujiwara)
　　　　Katsuteru's father, Minagawa Yamashiro-no-kami Hiroteru, was a descendant in the ninth generation after Taishokkan (Fujiwara no Kamatari), 
　　　　and a descendant of Tawara Tōta Hidesato. The eldest grandson of Nagamuma Gorō Munemasa, second son of Oyama Shirō Masamitsu, 
　　　　was called Minagawa Saemonnosuke Munekazu and first took the name Minagawa. His younger brother Saemonnosuke Muneyasu's 
　　　　descendant in the tenth generation was called Hidemune, served Kamakura Kanrei Mochiuji, lived in Minagawa in Shimotsuke Province, 
　　　　and ruled over fifty-odd villages. Hidemune's great-great-grandson was called Minagawa Yamashiro-no-kami Toshimune, captured 
　　　　the two castles of Awano and Suwayama in the same province, and his son Yamashiro-no-kami Hiroteru succeeded 
　　　　to the family headship. In the eighteenth year of Tenshō, he received an honorable command from Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) through Nakagawa Ichizaemon 
　　　　to join his side, and from then began his service. In the fifth year of Keichō, year of Kanoe-Ne, he served in attendance during the campaign against 
　　　　Uesugi Kagekatsu, and in the same year, when Lord Hidetada advanced to Ueda Castle, the residence of Sanada Masayuki, 
　　　　he also served in attendance. His son Minagawa Heiuemon Katsuteru had his own intentions, and transferred his father Hiroteru's stipend to his younger brother Yamashiro-no-kami Takatsune,
