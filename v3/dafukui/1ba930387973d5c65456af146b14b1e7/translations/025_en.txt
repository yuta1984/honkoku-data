【Left Page】
　　　　　　△　Sugita

　Summoned to service while Lord Tadamasa was still heir apparent
　　● Sugita Gorōbei Mitsumasa (home province and birth province both Yamato, family name Fujiwara, original name Gonnosuke, Iki)
　　　　Mitsumasa's grandfather, Sugita Gonnosuke, served as chief retainer to Kaishige Kai-no-kami,
　　　　lord of Kaishige Castle in Yamato Province. However, when his admonishments were not accepted, he resigned his position and retired,
　　　　going into seclusion in Awadono village in the same province and changing his name to Sukigara. The site of his residence remains to this day. Mitsumasa's father,
　　　　Sugita Shindayū, went to Korea during the Korean campaigns as a masterless samurai commanding a hundred horsemen but died of illness
　　　　in the field. When Mitsumasa was young, he served as a page in the household of Kanamori Hōin. At that time,
　　　　Lord Hideyoshi was invited to Hōin's residence, and Gongensama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) also attended for a go gathering.
　　　　When Mitsumasa came out to serve tea, Lord Hideyoshi observed him and said, "This child is no ordinary person.
　　　　Hōin should raise him well." Gongensama also gave similar words, as has been passed down. When he came to Echizen as a masterless samurai, Koma Furuise,
　　　　based on connections from the same province, acted as intermediary for his appointment. He served in both years of the Osaka campaigns, and in Genna 2 (year of the fire dragon),
　　　　in the seventh month, was granted new territory at Shimotsuma in Hitachi Province (with official documents). Abandoning Echizen, he served as chief retainer.
