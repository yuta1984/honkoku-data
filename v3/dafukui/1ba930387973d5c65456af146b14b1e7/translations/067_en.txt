　　　　　　　△　Nishio

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, Bunroku 2, year of Water Snake (1593)
　Summoned to service at Yuki
　　● Nishio Jinzaemon Munetsugu (home province: Tōtōmi, birth province unknown, surname unknown, original name: Hisaku, [also called] Zusho)
　　　　He was the son of one called Miyachi Kyūemon, initially taking the name Miyachi Hisaku. Later,
　　　　he was adopted by a masterless samurai named Nishio Koretaka, a resident of Enshū, and changed his name to Nishio Hisa-
　　　　ku. At Takatengjin in Enshū, he served under Yokota Jinemon, and elsewhere
　　　　he wandered and served in various places. Lord Hideyasu heard of him and summoned him to service. During Lord Tadanao's
　　　　reign, he accompanied [his lord] to both Osaka campaigns. During the winter campaign, he pierced with his spear the helmets of two
　　　　enemies hiding behind a fence. Those helmets bear spear wounds and he still possesses them today. During the summer campaign,
　　　　he crossed spears with Sanada Saemon-no-suke Yukimura and killed him. At that time, he was granted an audience with both Their Lordships
　　　　and received gold, ceremonial robes, and other gifts. From Lord Tadanao as well, he received
　　　　swords, gold, and other rewards.
