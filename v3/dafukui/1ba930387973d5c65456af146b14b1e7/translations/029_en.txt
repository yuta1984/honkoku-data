　　　　　　△　Ōtani

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, in Kan'ei 3 (year of the fire tiger)
　Summoned in Kyoto
　　● Ōtani Sukeroku Shigemasa (both home province and birth province: Yamashiro, family name: Ariwara, original name: Usami Shuri, Buddhist name: Ryōi)
　　　　His ancestor, eight generations from Prince Takechi, son of Emperor Tenmu: Bizen-no-kami Moronao (actually son of Ariwara no Narihira)
　　　　Ten generations from Moronao: Kōgōgū-daijin Yasusada (Ōtani ancestor), fourteen generations from Yasusada: Ōtani
　　　　Jibu-taifu Yasuchin, his son Ōtani Gyōbu-shōyū Yoshitaka (one of Hideyoshi's five magistrates, lord of Tsuruga Castle in Echizen Province, stipend of 50,000 koku)
　　　　His son Ōtani Jibukyo Yasushige (served as a masterless samurai at Shōren-in Temple), Sukeroku Shigemasa is the
　　　　son of the aforementioned Yasushige

　During Lord Tsunamasa's reign, in Tenna 3 (year of the water boar)
　Granted succession to territory
　　Current: Ōtani Sukeroku Shigeteru (birth province: Echizen)
