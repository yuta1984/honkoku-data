【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△　Iwagami

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, continued service from Yuki
　Accompanied to Echizen
　　● Iwagami Etchū Tomoyoshi (home province: Shimotsuke, birth province unknown, family name: Minamoto, original name: Sakyō)
　　　　Tomoyoshi's father, Miura Oki-no-kami Yoshikata, during Lord Harutomo's reign, changed his original name from Miura
　　　　and was ordered to take the name Iwagami, a branch line of Oyama. He was granted one character from the lord's name and the tomoe family crest,
　　　　and thereafter was called Iwagami Oki-no-kami Yoshikata.

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, granted family headship (year unknown)
　　Adopted son, actually the younger brother of Sasa Jidaizen-shō Toki
　　　Iwagami Sakyō Yoshitsugu (birth province: Owari, served together with his adoptive father during both Ōsaka campaigns. Later, Yoshitsugu's heir lineage became extinct)

　During Lord Tadamasa's reign, granted allocated lands (year unknown)
　　　Iwagami Shichinojō Tomochiha (birth province: Echizen, original name: Baiyō)
　　　　It reached the lord's attention that Tomochiha was Tomoyoshi's actual son, and he was granted allocated lands
