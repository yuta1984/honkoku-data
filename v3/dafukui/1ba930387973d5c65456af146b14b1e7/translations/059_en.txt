【Right Page】
　During Lord Mitsumichi's reign, Kanbun 11, year of Kanoto-I
　Granted family headship
　　　Suganuma Ichizaemon Ietsugu (birth province Echizen)
　　　　From his father Yoichirō's inherited stipend, an allocation of 400 koku was granted to his younger brother Yamamoto Chūzaemon,
　　　　but he died young and the line was discontinued. Since many of the brothers died young, Lord Mitsumichi
　　　　ordered that the family name be changed, and it became Suganuma after the maternal lineage.
　　　　Ietsugu's mother was the daughter of Suganuma Oribe, lord of Zeze Castle in Enshū.

【Left Page】

　During Lord Yoshishina's reign, Genroku 4, year of Kanoto-Hitsuji
　Summoned to service
　　Current Suganuma Yozō Tsugutaka (birth province Echizen)
　　　　He was summoned to service when young. His grandmother Chishōin was the wife of Nagami Taitō and
　　　　the younger sister of Keijuin-sama, so she had previously been granted a support stipend. In Genroku
　　　　15, year of Mizunoe-Uma, when Chishōin passed away, the said support stipend was converted to stipend rice,
　　　　and combined with the stipend that had been granted to Chishōin's heir and to Tsugutaka from before.
