【Left Page】
　　　　　　　△　Takaya

　During Lord Hideyasu's reign, summoned at Yūki
　　● Takaya Echigo, personal name unknown (home province and birth province both Mino, family name unknown)
　　　　His father was called Andō Iga-no-kami. Known in the world as one of the "Three Men of Mino." Since Echigo was
　　　　Iga-no-kami's third son, he was adopted into the family of a man called Takaya Shirōbē
　　　　in Mino Province, hence he came to use the surname Takaya. During Lord Hideyasu's reign, he was summoned
　　　　at Yūki and accompanied him to Echizen. During the Siege of Osaka, he served Lord Tadanao,
　　　　joining the forces under Honda Izu-no-kami. He was given battlefield regulations which are
　　　　preserved to this day. It is said that he took nineteen heads at various places during the campaign. Echigo's eldest
　　　　son Shichirōzaemon succeeded to the family headship but died young, causing the main family line to end.
