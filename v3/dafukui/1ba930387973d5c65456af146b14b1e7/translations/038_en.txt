【Left Page】
　　　　　　△　Tominaga

　From His Excellency Gongen-sama to Lord Hideyasu
　Attendant
　　● Tominaga Gyōbu Katsuyasu (home province unknown, birth province unknown, surname: Minamoto)
　　　　His ancestor Tominaga Yamashiro-no-kami Masanao served three generations:
　　　　Hōjō Sōun, his son Ujitsuna, and his son Ujiyasu, and resided in Edo Castle in Musashi Province. He had many military achievements, and commendation letters are still preserved today.
　　　　Masanao's eldest son, Tominaga Jinshirō Naokatu, succeeded him and resided in Edo Castle, and also
　　　　held Kasai Castle concurrently. In Eiroku 7 (1564, year of the Wood Rat), he died in battle at Kōdai in Shimōsa Province. His son, Yama-
　　　　shiro-no-kami Masatatsu, succeeded him and resided in Edo Castle. He received the character "masa" from Hōjō Ujimasa, and also
　　　　received a license for the commander's war fan, with documentary evidence still existing today. Masatatsu's eldest son, Jinshirō Nao-
　　　　nori's descendants serve as direct retainers of the Shogun's house. The second son, Gyōbu Katsuyasu, served
　　　　Lord Hideyasu. At this time, the ancestral vermillion seal documents and commendation letters were distributed between the main and branch families, and descendants
　　　　still preserve and pass them down today.
