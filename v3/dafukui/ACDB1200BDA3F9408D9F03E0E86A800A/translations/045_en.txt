Accordingly, this matter was communicated to Rokuzaemon and Shukai, and regarding this matter, a letter was also sent from the chief retainer to Daitokuin at Mount Kōya.

1. On August 9th, Higuchi Kozaemon Yasutaka was dispatched as an envoy to Edo for the monthly inquiry into [the Shogun's] well-being, presenting one type of delicacy. Kozaemon departed from Fukui today.
Note: A reply letter was delivered on the 21st of the same month.

1. On the 16th of the same month, at the Council Chamber (Hyōjōsho), the monthly chief retainer Matsudaira Shōbei Masatsune issued instructions regarding economy measures to all departments. Written orders as follows were distributed, one copy each:

"In recent years, various expenses have accumulated, and this year our finances have reached a critical point of difficulty. Therefore, further simplification is ordered. All officials in their respective roles must carefully examine all matters concerning expenses down to the smallest details and ensure there is no waste. Although there is no change from the intent of the simplification measures ordered in previous years, these instructions are nevertheless issued as written."

To the Armory Supervisors:
For armory repair duties, the silver allowance shall be reduced to half of the previous stipulation.
