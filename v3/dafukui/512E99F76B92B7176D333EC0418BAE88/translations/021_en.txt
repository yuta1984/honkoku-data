One: All religious donations (hōka) are strictly prohibited, and Buddhist ceremonies should be conducted with minimal expense.

The above matters have been proclaimed previously, but now that simplification has been reported to the shogunate, within the domain as well, all affairs should be conducted even more modestly. Should there naturally be those who violate these rules, there will be official proclamations, so these rules must be strictly observed.
　9th month, [day]

One: 10th month - Regarding the death by illness of the daughter of Kashiwaya Kihei, a relative of Christians, notification was sent to the Christian magistrates.

One: 11th month, 3rd day
　Chōkitsu-sama's birth mother, Shōjuin-dono, passed away.

One: Same month, 7th day - Due to the first snow, Kaga Tōzaemon (Narioki), the Edo residence keeper, was sent as a messenger to the castle to inquire about [the lord's] well-being.

One: 12th month, 10th day - Through envoy Ōshima Unshirō-dono, falcons were graciously received. In gratitude for this, [the lord] went to the castle and then made rounds to visit the senior councilors.

One: Same month, 11th day - An official letter regarding the presentation of gifts for the Chrysanthemum Festival was delivered at the castle, and gifts to retainers followed the usual custom.

One: Same month, 18th day - Due to the cold season, to the senior councilors to inquire about [their] well-being
