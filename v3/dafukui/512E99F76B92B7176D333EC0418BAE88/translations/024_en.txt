One: January 3rd
　　Chōkitsu-sama made his first castle visit for New Year congratulations, presented ceremonial sword and horse substitutes (ten ryō of gold), offered his respects, and received silk garments as gifts.

One: Same day - For the New Year poetry recital celebration, sake cups and sake money were presented through a messenger as customary.

One: 7th of the same month - For the first castle ascent celebration, ceremonial fish were presented through a messenger as customary.

One: 17th of the same month - Fresh cod for presentation arrived again yesterday evening, but due to an inauspicious day today, the presentation was difficult to make. Although days had passed, not wanting to cause concern, this matter was reported to the duty official Abe Bungo-no-kami Masatake, and no presentation was made.

One: 26th of the same month - Due to the construction of the Confucian temple at Shōheidaka this time, the Tsūkan Setsuyō, Gyokuendō calligraphy copybooks, and ten sets of deep robes were donated, and they were delivered to the temple through the messenger and deputy castellan Kaga Tōzaemon Nariyoshi.

One: February 21st - Orders were strictly given at the deputy's office that farmers of the domain should not commit rudeness, assaults, or other offenses against samurai in the castle town. When a subordinate of Deputy Morohoshi Kuranosuke went to Town Magistrate Atsumi Shin'emon Tomonobu to discuss this matter, Shin'emon confirmed there was no disagreement. Consequently, although this had been previously proclaimed, it was [ordered] that not only the deputies but even their subordinates should show no discourtesy when encountering [people] in the fields, [extending this order] to towns and countryside.
