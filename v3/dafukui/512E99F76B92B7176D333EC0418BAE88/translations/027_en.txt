One: May 3rd - For the congratulations of Tango no Sekku (Boys' Day), through a messenger, as usual, summer court robes and
　　unlined garments were presented, and silver was offered.
One: 4th of the same month - Departed from Edo, traveling via the Tōkaidō route.
One: 16th of the same month - Arrived at the castle.

One: 18th of the same month - For the same reason as above, as a gesture of gratitude, messenger Kōchi Sugita
　　Danjō Santake was sent to Edo. Departed from Fukui today.
One: 26th of the same month - Due to the illness of Sakai Genba Moto■, Kyoto physician Arima
　　Ryōkyū was summoned, and to request treatment for Genba, messenger
　　Minagawa Yoichirōemon ■■ was sent by express horse to Kyoto. Departed from Fukui today.
　　　　However, Ryōkyū arrived in Fukui on June 9th.
One: Same month (date unspecified) - Through an official letter, the first mackerel of the local products was presented as usual.
One: June 1st - For gratitude regarding the return to the domain, through messenger Sugita Danjō Santake,
　　as usual, ceremonial paper along with kelp, dried sea bream, and sake barrels were presented.
