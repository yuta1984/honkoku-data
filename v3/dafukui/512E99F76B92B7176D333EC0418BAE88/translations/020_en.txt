One: When inviting senior retainers (karō) for succession celebration banquets, the cuisine should be the same as for wedding celebrations.

One: Regarding clothing for those accompanying [the lord] to Edo, when there are guests or when going as messengers, special attire is appropriate, but when serving regular duties, simple clothing should be used. Even on ceremonial days, changing formal attire is acceptable. However, clothing brought from home may be of any type without restriction, and available family crests may be worn. Additionally, instructions should be given that even servants and lower-ranking attendants should wear clothing as simple as possible.

One: Entertaining by individuals while residing in Edo is prohibited. However, light meals for regular gatherings are exceptional, and light hospitality should be provided when receiving guests from elsewhere.

One: When serving as messengers to Edo, both high and low-ranking retainers may bring lighter retinues than their status would normally allow. Spears should also be carried according to the number of attendants. When serving as envoys to other domains, retinues should be appropriate to one's status. Depending on the type of mission, consultation should be made with the inspectors (metsuke) for guidance on the number of attendants.

One: Correspondence within the domain should be prepared on thin hōsho paper or lower quality. Correspondence with outsiders is exceptional.
