One: When inviting senior retainers (karō) for succession celebration banquets, the cuisine should be the same as for wedding celebrations.

One: Regarding clothing for those accompanying missions to Edo, when there are guests or when departing as messengers, special attire is appropriate, but when serving guard duty, plain clothing should be used. Even on ceremonial days, changing formal wear is acceptable. However, brought clothing may be of any type without restriction, and available family crests should be worn. Additionally, even servants and lower-ranking attendants should be instructed to dress more simply.

One: Banquets by retainers while residing in Edo are prohibited. However, light meals for regular gatherings are exceptional. When receiving guests from elsewhere, entertainment should also be modest.

One: When serving as messengers to Edo, both high-ranking and low-ranking retainers may bring fewer attendants than their stipend would normally allow. Spear-bearers should also be brought according to the number of personnel. When serving as envoys to other domains, attendants should be brought according to one's status. Depending on the type of mission, personnel matters should be reported to the inspectors (metsuke) for guidance.

One: Correspondence within the domain should be written on thin hōsho paper or lower quality paper. Correspondence with outsiders is exceptional.
