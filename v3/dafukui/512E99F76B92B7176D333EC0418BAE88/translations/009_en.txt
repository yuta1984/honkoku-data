One: On May 21st, regarding the matter of frugality, an official notice was issued.
　　However, the details of this official notice are unclear in the records.

One: On the 26th of the same month, the estate adjacent to the Torigoe residence belonging to Lord Yamato-no-kami Naonori was received, and today the transfer took place. The caretaker Kaga Tōzaemon Nariyuki and the attached inspector Kawazu Zentayū Takasuke went to receive it.

One: On the 29th of the same month at four o'clock, when he made his castle visit (yesterday on the 28th, an official note came from the duty councilor Lord Abe Bungo-no-kami), with the Senior Councilors in attendance, it was announced as requested that Chōkichi-sama (the third son of Lord Daizen-dayū Tsunahiro, born on December 4th of Enpō 6), younger brother of Matsudaira Nagato-no-kami Yoshinari, would be adopted as heir. This was conveyed by the duty councilor Lord Abe Bungo-no-kami Masa[?], and upon leaving the castle, courtesy visits were made to the Senior Councilors, close retainers, and junior councilors to express gratitude.
　　However, since Nagato-no-kami was in his domain, the messenger Iwamura Denemon was sent to Chōshū for the congratulatory ceremony.

One: On the same day, regarding the same matter, a letter was sent to the Kyoto Deputy Lord Naitō Yamato-no-kami Shigeyori.

One: On the same day, regarding the same matter, the messenger Ihara Genbei was sent to the home domain and departed from the Edo residence today.
