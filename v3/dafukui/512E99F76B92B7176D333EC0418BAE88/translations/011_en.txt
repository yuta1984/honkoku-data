Lord Chōkichi presented a sword (Miiike, worth six gold pieces), which Lord Shōgen brought out and presented. After this concluded, Sukenoshin was summoned.

Lord Masaaki bestowed a sake cup and presented a sword (Naoe Shizu, worth three gold pieces). The sake cup was offered before him, and after this was completed, he appeared in the front study. A meal was served together with the guests (three soups and eight dishes). Music and dance performances were ordered, and there was a sake ceremony with the guests. After this concluded, the chief retainer was summoned and given a sake cup, which was then offered before the lord. At the middle study, an audience was granted to those who had accompanied Lord Chōkichi, and ceremonial gifts were bestowed, completing the proceedings.

Lord Chōkichi entered the front reception room from the main entrance (temporary residence at the corner tea house), and his attendant Hababe Koemon Yorihiro was summoned and given a sword (worth three gold pieces). Other attendants were also given ceremonial gifts.

One: On June 5th, to announce the completion of the move, Lord Chōkichi made courtesy visits to the Senior Councilors, chamberlains, and Junior Councilors.
　　However, to the Senior Councilors and Lord Makino Bingo-no-kami Sadatei, Lord Chōkichi personally brought ceremonial swords and gold horse substitutes.
