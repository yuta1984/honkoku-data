All tools and implements should not be sought.

One: Regarding clothing, silk and pongee should be worn. Particularly those of lower status may also use cotton and paper clothing. However, these should not be worn in Edo.
Old clothing of any kind is acceptable. In general, plain clothing should be used in both summer and winter. Additionally, women's clothing should also be correspondingly plain garments.

One: When attending official duties, cotton-lined kamishimo should be worn exclusively. Old kataginu or any substitute for kamishimo is acceptable. However, when there are guests or messengers, appropriate attire according to their status should be worn.

One: Regarding the clothing of those under each group's supervision and male and female servants, orders should be given to wear plain clothing in accordance with the official proclamation standards for house retainers.

One: Entertainment is prohibited. However, when naturally attending gatherings, as previously established, light meals of one soup and three dishes with pickles, along with three rounds of sake and one type of light appetizer, should be served. Food should consist of available soup and one type of light appetizer. Particularly, sake should be treated the same.

One: Regarding New Year, the five seasonal festivals, and celebratory occasions in general, household arrangements should be conducted modestly in all matters. The exchange of gifts for celebrations is strictly prohibited.
