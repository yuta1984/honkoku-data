Messenger Tanaka Jozaemon was dispatched to Edo, and one type of ceremonial fish was presented. Jozaemon departed Fukui today.

One: On January 13th, to inquire about the timing of the lord's attendance in Edo (sankin), messenger Senbon Choemon Hisanaga was dispatched to Edo. He departed Fukui today.
　　However, the date of Choemon's arrival in Edo is unknown.

One: On the 27th of the same month, there was a summons from the Senior Councilors, and when messenger Senbon Choemon Hisanaga appeared, he was given an official letter stating that the lord should come to the capital within the fourth month of this year.

One: On February 9th, for the monthly inquiry into the lord's well-being, messenger Yanagihara Sukesaemon Takahiro was dispatched to Edo, and one type of ceremonial fish was presented. Sukesaemon departed Fukui today.

One: On the same day, as the lord had completely recovered from his illness, his sickbed was removed today.

One: On the 15th of the same month, for the first time after his illness, the lord moved to the main castle, and in the formal audience chamber, ceremonial long sake cups were bestowed upon Honda Magotar​ō Nagataka and other senior retainers, as well as Sakai Genba Motonaga (eldest son of a senior retainer). After this was completed, in the Tasshi-no-ma chamber, ceremonial long sake cups were bestowed upon Takachi, Mito Hyōbē Tomohide, and Yamakawa Naizen Tomonari. In the great hall, group audiences were granted to all members from the yoriai-bantō down to the new guards.
