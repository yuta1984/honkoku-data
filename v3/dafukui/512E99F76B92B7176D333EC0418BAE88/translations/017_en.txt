The 33rd memorial service was held. At Dentsū-in in Koishikawa as well,
it was similarly conducted by Lady Tenshō-in.

One: August 18th, regarding the presentation of Boys' Day congratulations, a private letter was
delivered at the castle. Gifts to retainers, etc., were as usual.

One: September 3rd, for the Double Ninth Festival congratulations, through an envoy, kosode robes were presented as usual, and silver was offered.

One: September 5th, to the Bun'in Academy (later renamed to Tō[?]rin, Daigaku-no-kami),
a request was made for the selection of Lord Chōkichi's formal name and characters.

One: September 10th
Accompanied by Lord Chōkichi, he had an audience with the duty officer Lord Abe Bungo-no-kami Masanao and performed his duties.

One: September 25th, regarding the matter of frugality, as it was recently reported to the shogunate,
the house retainers should also observe greater frugality. At the council chamber, monthly duty senior retainer Koma Moku-no-jō Teizumi conveyed this to all monthly duty officers. The following written directive was distributed:
　　One: Among the house retainers and samurai, house construction beyond one's means should be
　　　considered unnecessary, and should be done modestly.
　　One: Care for weapons and horse equipment should be considered essential; items other than military equipment are unnecessary
