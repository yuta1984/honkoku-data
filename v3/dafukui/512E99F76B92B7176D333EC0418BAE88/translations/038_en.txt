One: April 26th - From his private quarters, he presented two pairs of folding screens (one pair depicting cherry blossoms, pheasants, willows, and herons by Kano Doun, one pair depicting red and white plum blossoms by Kano Yoboku).
　　After seeking permission, he sent them through his envoy, the resident representative
　　Hara Heizaemon (Masatake) to Senior Councilor Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami (Masanao). A formal notification of the completed presentation came
　　from Sagami-no-kami:
　　　　　　Regarding the folding screens you presented, I consulted with Makino Bingo-no-kami, and
　　　　　　the presentation was successfully completed in the private quarters, so please be
　　　　　　at ease. I am writing to inform you of this matter. The above.
　　　　　　　　April 26th　　　　Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami
　　　　　　　　To Matsudaira Hyobu-daifu

One: May 3rd - At Toeisan Temple
　　For the 13th anniversary memorial service of Genyuin, to inquire after his health,
　　he sent envoy and resident representative Kaga Tozaemon (Narioki) with one set of cypress boxes
One: Same month, 8th - For the same matter
　　As the Shogun was making a pilgrimage to Toeisan Temple, he performed advance preparatory duties
One: Same day - For the same matter, a tea ceremony was conducted at Manpukuzan
One: Same month, 9th - For the same matter, through envoy Nagami Jiroemon (Yoshimasa, wearing deep court dress and long hakama),
　　he presented ten pieces of incense offering silver to the sutra hall at Toeisan Temple
　　Monomimi-sama sent envoy Idebuchi Heibei (Morikyo) with three pieces of incense offering silver
