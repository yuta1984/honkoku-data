Matters to be reported

1. Kanpō 2, Year of the Dog: What was announced to Okabe Sazen's section was that when there are those granted domain confiscation or dismissal, relatives should go to that person's place and report immediately without delay, and this should be acknowledged and reported to the senior councilors.

1. On the 11th day of the 11th month of Genbun 5, Year of the Monkey: When Tsukaya Gengorōemon was granted dismissal, there were instructions from Okabe Sazen's section to handle it the same as domain confiscation.
　　　Jinpei considers that Tsukaya Gengorōemon had his domain confiscated - this clause is suspicious

1. 　　When Nishio Jinbei was granted dismissal, he returned home from the council chamber, went to the lower section, and two group members were sent.

1. 　　Ban Shingorōzaemon had his domain confiscated. The group leader announced this at the council chamber with inspectors in attendance, obtained signatures on the confinement document, and for retreat to the requested temple Honmyōji, went to the lower section at eight o'clock and surrendered his swords, so foot soldier inspectors were not sent, only group members were dispatched.

1. Suyama Yogorōzaemon and Tagawa Kintayū: Ordinary dismissal was granted and handled according to regulations. Kintayū had two group members assigned from the council chamber until his return to Matsuoka, and from there to the domain border, both surveillance and estate guards from Matsuoka group served.

1. On the 29th day of the 10th month of Kyōhō 9, Year of the Dragon: Karasawa Uemon, Kuwano Kaemon, and Hyōsuke were granted dismissal. Group leaders announced this at the council chamber with inspectors in attendance, obtained signatures on confinement documents and pledges to remain in the domain territory. For Hyōsuke, foot soldier leaders announced this in the map room with inspectors in attendance and obtained signatures on confinement documents. All three did not return to their lodgings; from the council chamber to the checkpoint, two surveillance group members each were assigned, group members were also assigned to residences, and the residence magistrate was also notified. {Margin note: The aforementioned Kaemon retreated to Fukushōji temple in Yoshie Ushiya village, but committed suicide on the night of the last day of the following month. Although those granted dismissal should not be confined, since it was shortly after dismissal, to check if there might be written notes, two group members were dispatched for investigation.}

1. Genroku 14, Year of the Snake: Takano Jinzaemon was granted dismissal for non-attendance at duty.

1. Genroku 17, Year of the Monkey: Okajima Tahei and his two sons had their domains confiscated, with confinement orders of ten thousand koku or more for retreat to three directions.

1. Same year: Ideura Gengorōemon [Red correction] Shinemon was granted dismissal and resided in Tochiizumi village, but when it became known that he had died of illness, this was reported to his lordship. The aforementioned Shinemon □□

　Assistant Okuyama Shichirōemon was summoned, and regarding his illness since last winter and the course of medical treatment, a verbal report was prepared carefully by Shichirōemon for review. Shichirōemon affixed his seal to this verbal report.

1. Hōei 2, Year of the Rooster: Group leader Kawagoe Uemon [Red notation] was granted dismissal as inspector and remained in Kaminaka village.

1. Hōei 3, Year of the Dog: A court lady attending Seishōin made a proxy visit to Kitano village Tenjin shrine, then went to view wisteria at Oka. Since Matsue Zenbei did not report to the monthly inspector, his position was revoked and Ōfuka Sen'emon was granted dismissal.

1. Same year: Nakanishi Kansuke reported chronic illness and frequently left his post without informing the group leader, so was granted dismissal. Moreover, he did not attend festival duties and rode in a palanquin without permission during the memorial service for Daian-in.

1. On the 29th day of the 1st month of Shōtoku 2, Year of the Dragon: Edo permanent residence court acupuncturist Satō Teijun had misconduct and was granted dismissal, returned to the official residence, packed up various tools, and before Teijun's departure, group members were assigned as before, and watchers were also dispatched to Teijun's hut.
　However, until this time, those with domain confiscation, banishment, and ordinary dismissal were all handled the same way, so it was said that Koma Ise's section should be consulted. When Ōno Sanzaemon made inquiry, he was ordered to establish distinctions for such cases, so regarding Teijun's matter, it was reported in official correspondence as above.

1. Shōtoku 3, Year of the Snake: Ishiguro Nasaemon repeatedly submitted petitions showing disrespect for superiors, which did not meet with approval, so was granted dismissal.
　　　However, this was stricter than ordinary dismissal, requiring retreat to a temple and confinement documents with signatures. The original document was kept by head falconer Kishi Shigezaemon.

1. Shōtoku 4, Year of the Horse: Yoshii Kanemon's son Tauemon absconded and claimed to be a deserter, going to relatives in Kaga province for help. However, he was told he could not be accommodated without Kanemon's guarantee and returned. At that time, since he was an offender, a petition for banishment was considered, but the handling was not accepted.
