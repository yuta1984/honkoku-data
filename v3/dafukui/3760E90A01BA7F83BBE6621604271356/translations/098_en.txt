1. On the 13th day of the 1st month of Shōtoku 1, Rabbit year (1711), peasants from Fuchū domain set nets within the falconry grounds, so the senior councilors notified Magotarō's group, imprisoned them in Fukui town jail, and there are records of successive deliberations on this matter.

1. During the punishment on the 26th day of the 6th month of Kyōhō 3, Dog year (1718), Hanbyōe of Honshōji gate was sentenced to banishment from the domain as an accomplice of thieves. This person shall never be pardoned even under any great amnesty in the future, and the senior councilors informed Town Magistrate Ōmiya Hikoemon of this yesterday on the 25th.

1. On the 21st day of the 8th month of the same year, it was announced to Ōmiya Hikoemon: "Regarding banishment from the castle town, until now there was no interference if they stayed around Katsumi area since it was outside the castle town, but hereafter regarding castle town banishment, they shall be ordered not to enter within two ri (about 8 km) in all directions from the castle town."

1. On the 11th day of the 6th month of the same 6th year, Ox (1721), it was announced to the district heads that banished persons shall not hereafter take side roads beyond Itatori in the north and Hosorogi in the south.

1. On the 21st day of the 11th month of the same year: Head shaved, branded, banished from domain - Jinemon of Ōtani village, a returnee, in prison. (This was ordered to Town Magistrate Ōmiya Hikoemon, and there are other banished persons)

However, nose-cutting banishments were numerous in the Genroku era and appear in various records. All were for gambling. In the 12th month of Kyōhō 7, Tiger year, there was banishment from two ri around the castle town.
According to Jinpei's investigation, on the 21st day of the 6th month of Kyōhō 2, Rooster year, three persons had their noses cut and were banished from the domain - all were returnees from domain banishment.
Also, on the 22nd day of the 2nd month of Genbun 3, Horse year, nose-cutting followed by banishment from thirteen ri in all directions - Takaya Genemon, for operating a gambling house within the Edo residence gate compound.
On the 5th day of the 7th month of Kyōhō 6, Ox year, Mataemon of Ikube village was banished two ri in all directions from his home village.

1. On the 25th day of the 4th month of the same 7th year, Tiger (1722), Eguchi Jirōbyōe reported that in the villages under government custody there were many groundless disputes causing much expense and trouble, so after adjudicating disputes, if penalty fees were imposed on the unreasonable party as in the domain territory, there might be no more groundless disputes in the future. The village headmen made this proposal, and when this was reported to the senior councilors, they were told to consult with Suzuki Hikodayū and Inoue Handayū.

1. On the 5th day of the 6th month of the same year, in Tajima village under government custody, a man named Tōshirō severely beat another man named Seizaemon of the same village. As penalty, Tōshirō was made to provide rice for the period from the day Seizaemon was beaten until he recovered and could go out.

1. On the 29th day of the 4th month of the same year, because unauthorized pilgrimages were prevalent and discipline was lax, it was decided after inquiry to order village heads in government custody villages to collect fines from the parents of those who made unauthorized pilgrimages. On the 11th day of the 7th month, it was further decided after inquiry that parents' fines should be 2 monme, and for servants, masters should pay fines of 3 monme each. However, on the 5th day of the 7th month of the same 12th year, Sheep (1727), it was decided that children 15 years and under would be exempted from fines as they lack judgment.

One ri banishment in all directions is rare, so I record it here.

1. On the 21st day of the 9th month of the same 12th year, Sheep, Gonsuke of Ikejiri village, who had been banished one ri in all directions in previous years, was pardoned upon petition - there is a record of this.

1. On the 29th day of the 5th month of Kyōhō 6, Ox year, Buemon of Yoshie Nishiban village was banished one ri from Yoshie in all directions, including Fukui government custody area and Matsuoka.

1. On the 5th day of the 9th month of Genbun 1, Dragon year (1736), Magistrate Ogawa Jibyōe announced: Hereafter, when village people who are landholders serving in samurai households fall behind in tax payments and become delinquent, resulting in village dispersal and banishment, the local deputy should report to the magistrates for consultation. The magistrates should then inform the district magistrates, release stipends, and deliver them to the deputy - this procedure was decided upon as announced by the senior councilors.

1. On the 27th day of the 9th month of Tenpō 2, Rabbit year (1831), Asano Kyōsai sent word by letter that Naitō Ribyōe was ill and wished to request medical treatment, but since he was currently under punishment, he communicated this through an intermediary, Nunoya Tazaemon. He wished to bring this Tazaemon this evening. Although such reports were not made during townspeople's punishments before, this time there was an intermediary so he reported it, and he stated that he would not report every time in the future - please take note of this point.

1. On the 10th day of the 10th month of Genbun 5, Monkey year (1740), for the 33rd memorial service tea ceremony for Lord Jōken'in, as amnesty: prison release, tattooing on forehead, expulsion thirteen ri in all directions within the domain - Chōzaemon of Higashi-uo-machi, a returnee.

1. On the 10th day of the 10th month of Kyōhō 5, Rat year (1720), for the amnesty of the 13th memorial service for Lord Jōken'in: four persons in prison who committed arson were permanently banished from the domain, with no interference with wives and children. However, they shall not be pardoned even in future great amnesties - this was conveyed to Town Magistrate Ōmiya Hikoemon and recorded at the end of the amnesty document.

1. On the 29th day of the 1st month of the same 10th year, Snake, Seijirō of the Matsumoto Yokochō [certain] shop family was expelled thirteen ri from the castle town, permanently expelled hereafter, not to submit petitions even during memorial services, etc. According to investigation, there are many others as well.

1. On the 21st day of the 10th month of Tenmei 1, Ox year (1781), for amnesty of memorial service, life spared, tattooed on forehead, banished thirteen ri in all directions within the domain - Jibyōe, homeless person born at Seigenji temple grounds.

1. On the 12th day of the 12th month of Kyōhō 9, Dragon year (1724), for amnesty of the first anniversary of Lord Gen'kō-in, prison release, banished six ri in all directions from castle town - Ichibyōe, retainer of Kanemon of Nabe-ya in Shimo-gofuku-machi.

1. On the 8th day of the 6th month of Enpō 7, Sheep year (1679), finger cut off, banished from domain - Kizaemon of Ōtsu town, [certain] mountain in Ōmi province. This was for pickpocketing in the middle shops.
