1. On the 29th day of the 11th month of Enpō 5 (Year of the Snake): Inaba Uneme instructed town magistrates Hasebe Rokuuemon and Namikawa Heiuemon that those granted dismissal should not visit the four family temples: Jōkōin, Kōkenji, Tōkōji, and Kezōji.

　　　　　　Domain Confiscation, Dismissal, and Domain Banishment

1. For samurai who have committed improprieties and are granted dismissal, the matter should be handled at the council chamber or at the captain's residence with inspectors present, and the captain should announce it according to the written directive. For places under restriction (or written directives), the inspectors should deliver the written notification and affix their seals. For those who do not bring their seal, a written signature should be taken.
[Red notation] However, for those granted dismissal in the antechamber of the council chamber, their short swords should be collected by ashigaru inspectors who come out to meet them. The same applies to those in custody.

1. For those of ban-gai rank and above who are ordered domain confiscation, dismissal, house confinement, etc., the matter should be announced through the inspectors. Regarding chief retainers and senior councilors: this was announced to duty officer Sakai Tenzen's section on the 14th day of the 12th month of Genbun 5 (Year of the Monkey) through Hahakabu Hachizaemon.

1. On the 16th day of the 2nd month of Shōtoku 2 (Year of the Dragon): Regarding court physician Nakarai Tamétake, when [unclear] matters reached his lordship's ears, domain confiscation was ordered. According to the standard written directive, restriction was imposed jointly with domain-holding daimyo, and inspectors were instructed to obtain seal confirmations. When Tamétake stopped by Kezōji to prepare, town magistrate Kaifuku Ihei inquired with Honda Shurinosuke's section, and was told that those granted dismissal should not visit family temples, but preparing within the temple grounds before departure would not be problematic. This was conveyed to Ihei.
It is standard law to leave the domain directly from that location.

1. Those granted dismissal should return to their private residences and leave the domain. They should not enter the domain thereafter.

1. Those whose domains are confiscated should directly enter temple life from that location. Ashigaru inspectors are not dispatched.

1. When someone is granted dismissal, two ashigaru inspectors should go out to collect and present their short swords, etc.
　However, even when announcements are made at captains' residences, two ashigaru inspectors should go. The same applies to those in custody.

1. When someone is granted dismissal, six group members should be summoned to the council chamber:
　2 people: To watch when the person goes to the council chamber and be sent to observe the residence
　　2 people: To follow behind when the person returns from the council chamber and observe until the residence
　　　However: When leaving directly from the council chamber to a temple, two people should also follow to the temple and observe until departure
　　2 people: When the person leaves their residence, to observe until the domain border
　　　However: The same when leaving from a temple
