1. In the 7th month of the same year: Banishment of the grandmother of horse keeper Anzai Gengozaemon
[Below] When Gengozaemon died of illness in the 4th month of this year, there was no adopted heir, and since this grandmother was an improper person, she was ordered banished from a five-ri radius of the castle town. The family members were told to inform the grandmother and carry out the banishment, as ordered through Nara Gonzaemon as assistant.
However, on the 5th day of the 9th month of the same year, Kanzō, the second son of Teraki Genzaemon, was appointed as adopted heir, given a five-person stipend, and the family name was reestablished.
The following year, when this grandmother returned, she was imprisoned, and on the 21st day of the 5th month was ordered banished from a thirteen-ri radius.

1. In the 6th month of the same year: Gate expulsion of Sōtadayū, son of Matsuda Sōbee of the new guard unit in Edo
However, this should have been announced to the father Sōbee, who would return and inform Sōtadayū, but due to circumstances, Sōtadayū was called to the metsuke hut for the announcement. His swords were confiscated, sealed in the presence of ashigaru-metsuke Kosan, and placed with the antiquities office.
Later, when they were given to the father Sōbee, Sōbee received them directly from the antiquities office under ashigaru-metsuke handling.
This was handled at the metsuke hut because there was no captain of new guards in Edo, falling under metsuke jurisdiction.

1. In Kansei 2 (Year of the Rooster): Minowa Bunuemon

1. On the 25th day of the 10th month of Kansei 4 (Year of the Rat): Takasaka Kinuemon's adopted son Magoshichirō, Kiuchi Yojibee's adopted son Yoichirō, Kōji Kinpei, and adoptive grandfather Hyōuemon had their samurai status stripped and were banished. The handling was that father and son were called together to the hyōjōsho, the senior retainers withdrew, and at the next seat, father and son together had the written order read aloud by the captain. Also, regarding Hyōuemon, since he had retired status, he was not called to the hyōjōsho but was ordered through the family, with notification delivered to the family member's residence. At that time, two ashigaru-metsuke went there.
However, both family members were also summoned and given the order.

1. On the 25th day of the 6th month of Bunka 8 (Year of the Sheep): Itō Kanbee, head of servants of the new guard unit, had his samurai status stripped and was ordered banished from a thirteen-ri radius within the domain.

1. In Genroku 14 (Year of the Snake): Ono Gonuchi reported a long illness, but when the captain later inquired about his condition, there were improper matters, so his hair was shaved at the custodian's place and he was banished.

1. In Shōtoku 5 (Year of the Sheep): Atsumi Gonbee had his hair shaved for banishment, announced by the new guard captain who showed the restriction notice, but stated there were no restrictions since it was not under samurai law.
However, when Gonbee returned from Edo, two magistrate group members were stationed at Itadori, provided guidance in the evening to the hyōjōsho where magistrates and metsuke appeared, and magistrate group members were stationed at Yamasaki entrance. Three metsuke group members were deployed in case he said anything at that time, but this was kept secret.
