1. In the 3rd month of Kyowa 1 (1801), when Murakami Hikohachiro, who was residing with Iwasaki Kurozaemon of the accounting office, was ordered into confinement, Kurozaemon's mother became ill during this period and a request was made to call a physician. This matter is recorded in the section on illness during punishment.

1. On the 21st day of the 11th month of Bunka 8 (1811), when Otani Junzaemon was ordered into confinement, since his son Takehisa was co-residing, an inquiry about restraint was submitted. When the magistrate asked how this should be handled, a reply was given that if it concerns co-residence, he should be ordered to remain in restraint.

1. Regarding cases where those whose adoption requests have been completed go to their adoptive families due to their birth families' punishment, or where adoptees remain in their birth families for restraint because they have not yet moved despite being punished, many such cases are recorded in the section on various matters related to punishment.

1. Cases where family members staying behind in Edo are taken into custody when punishment is ordered, and these family members are immediately placed under restraint together or separated, are recorded in the same section. Reference should be made to the regulations from Kansei 2 (1790) in the section on punishment of those remaining in Edo.

1. On the 25th day of the 10th month of Kyoho 13 (1728), Sakabe Sangoemon's adopted son Kanroku was ordered into exile. Regarding this Kanroku matter, Imaeda Jingobee was in house arrest after inquiry, and foot inspector Tsuzuki Kaemon, who resided with Jingobee, informed him to take note that there were people coming and going from outside to Kaemon's residence.

1. On the 19th day of the 12th month of the same 24th year, when construction office carpentry head Masui Rokuemon (new guard) and his son were granted leave and ordered to withdraw from the castle, new guard Niwa Zentayu (Rokuemon's son) was residing with Rokuemon. He quickly vacated Rokuemon's residence, first went to reside with Teraki Genzaemon, and was in house arrest after inquiry. However, on the 20th of the same month, Matsudaira Shobee's office informed new guard captain Kumagai Kohee that since Zentayu's staying at Genzaemon's place for a long time seemed inappropriate, he should quickly find separate lodgings and be in house arrest there. On the 22nd of the same month, he rented a house owned by someone called Choho-in in Koyasu-cho, and through an intermediary, requested permission from the senior retainers to move there that very night, and relocated.

1. In the 7th month of Kansei 3 (1791), during the confinement of Ichikawa Ryoemon, deputy of Kanatsu and caretaker of Kawai Hikosaku's residence, orders were given respectively to surrender Hikosaku's residence and impose confinement.

1. On the 13th day of the 8th month of Kanpo 2 (1742), when ■ guard (probably great guard) Sato Hanzaemon was ordered dismissed, a person named Muranaka Yashichi, deputy of Toda Yajibee, was residing by renting Hanzaemon's residential land. When Yajibee inquired about what should be done with Yashichi's wife and children, this was communicated to the stable office, who replied that the house should be vacated immediately and the family should be made to leave. This was communicated to Yajibee.
