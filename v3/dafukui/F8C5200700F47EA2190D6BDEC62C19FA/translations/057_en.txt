The messenger was sent to convey that regarding Kenmotsu-dono's matter, a petition was submitted yesterday,
　　　　　　　so [this] was reported for your information. As for the response, it is entirely
　　　　　　　up to your discretion. [The message stated that] you conveyed your thoughts to
　　　　　　　Sagami-no-kami-dono, and that you mentioned taking [him] back to your residence first.
　　　　　　　Since this is a matter of unofficial intention, there may be ways for you to respond,
　　　　　　　but I believe it would be appropriate to refrain [from commenting].
　　　　　　　Your desire to hear what kind of response there will be after submitting the petition
　　　　　　　is quite reasonable. Surely Yamashiro-no-kami-dono will convey [this matter]
　　　　　　　to Mimasaka-no-kami-dono's retainer office. Since Daizen-dayū has made a request,

　　　　　　　if the rōjū (senior councillors) instruct that [he] should be returned,
　　　　　　　I believe it would indeed be best to proceed accordingly. I look forward to meeting you in person. The above.
　　　　　　　　June 1st

1. On the same day, [he] wrote to Toda Yamashiro-no-kami Tadamasa-dono and Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami Masanao-dono,
   who were the officials on duty last month.
   [The letter] reported that the negotiations with Matsudaira Daizen-dayū Tsunahiro-dono regarding Kenmotsu-sama's matter had been completed.

1. On the same day, regarding the same matter, following a request from Matsudaira Daizen-dayū Tsunahiro-dono, an escort was sent tonight,
   and [Kenmotsu] was taken to Daizen-dayū-dono's residence.
