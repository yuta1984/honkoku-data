[He] made ceremonial visits to the Great Elder, Senior Councilors, and Chamberlains to inquire after the Shogun's health.

December 12th: For the same reason, [he] went to the castle to inquire after [the Shogun's] health.

Same day, evening: To inquire after [the Shogun's] health, deputy Hori Jūbei Kyūritsu was sent as messenger to Duty Councilor Ogasawara Sado-no-kami Nagashige. On that occasion, it was communicated through a retainer that due to the recent fire damage to the residence, [he] was exempted from fire guard duty at Mt. Tōei.
　　　　　　However, regarding this announcement, while the deputy was supposed to be summoned and informed directly, since a messenger was fortunately being sent anyway, it was conveyed as additional business, as the retainer explained.

December 13th: [He] went to the castle to express gratitude for the exemption from fire guard duty, had an audience with the Senior Councilors, and upon withdrawal made ceremonial visits to the Great Elder, Senior Councilors, and Chamberlains.

Same day: For condolences regarding relatives, [he] went to the castle to inquire after [the Shogun's] health and had an audience with the Senior Councilors.

Same day: For the same reason, a messenger was sent to the Duty Councilor and Chamberlains to inquire after [the Shogun's] health.
　　　　　　However, for the same reason, a messenger was also sent on the 15th.

December 14th: For the same reason, to inquire after [the Shogun's] health, deputy Matsubara Zensaemon Norinari was sent as messenger to present one set of cypress containers (inquiry from the 12th).

Same day: For the same reason, [he] performed duties to the Duty Councilor and Chamberlains to inquire after [the Shogun's] health.

December 15th: For the same reason, [he] went to the castle to inquire after [the Shogun's] health.

December 17th: During the mourning period, to inquire after [the Shogun's] health, [a messenger was sent] to Duty Councilor Ogasawara Sado-no-kami Nagashige...
