1. June 12th, regarding the same matter, at the residence of monthly duty karō Sakai Tenzen Tomofusa, Tenzen conveyed the purport of the aforementioned document to Honda no Magotarō Nagataka and others, from the samurai retainers up to the physicians.

1. Same month, 13th, regarding the offering for the Boys' Festival celebration, an inner letter was presented at the castle, and retainer gifts were given as usual.

1. Same month, 22nd - Returned to the castle.

1. Same month, 23rd, regarding the same matter, for the purpose of expressing gratitude, Takachi Saitō Sakunosuke Sadafusa was dispatched to Edo. As usual, ceremonial paper, kelp, dried sea bream, and sake barrels were offered. Sakunosuke departed Fukui today.
   However, the date of Sakunosuke's arrival in Edo is unknown.

1. Same month, last day, due to the summer heat, through a letter, local specialty wheat noodles and dried sea bream were offered as usual.

1. July 6th, for the birthday celebration, gold was offered through a messenger as usual.

1. Same day, messengers were sent to Honda Magotarō Nagataka's residence - karō Matsudaira Shōbei Masatsune and Sakai Yozaemon Motonobu. Since Magotarō had no legitimate heir, they conveyed the purport of the lord's intention to appoint Asajirō-sama as his adopted son. Magotarō accepted the proposal and immediately went to the lord's quarters to express his gratitude. He was granted an audience and received abalone as a gift.

1. Same month, 9th, messenger Hori Kinshichi Naomasa arrived in Edo to inquire after [the shogun's] well-being during the summer heat (departed from the domain on the 25th of last month) and presented a letter to the duty officer.
   However, a reply letter was delivered the following 10th.
