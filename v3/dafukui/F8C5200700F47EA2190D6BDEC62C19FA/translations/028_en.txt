Regarding the request to borrow the provincial map (kuni-ezu) that had been submitted in the Tiger year, yesterday on the 24th, the Finance Magistrate Matsudaira Mino-no-kami Shigeryo sent word that someone should come to collect it from the caretaker. Today, Kaga Tōzaemon Nariyoshi and Matsubara Zenzaemon Norinari went to the map storage house at Honjō, where they received the maps of the twelve districts of the province that had been submitted in Shōhō 2, along with two village registers.
The provincial maps were brought back to the domain by caretaker Takayanagi Denzaemon, accompanied by six men from the foot soldier groups, arriving in Fukui on March 6th.

On the same day, regarding Ryūsenji Temple in Fuchū: After the landholding was confiscated from the late Honda Izu-no-kami Tomimasa, one hundred koku of temple land was donated from within Ōta Village, and subsequently red-seal documents were granted by successive lords. In Jōkyō 3 (Tiger year), it became public domain, but after the magistrate made inquiries to Edo, it was maintained as before. However, since the domain transfer last year (Ox year), this temple land has not been paying taxes. Kuzuno officials received various appeals from Ryūsenji Temple. Recently, there were disputed matters regarding waste land within Ōta Village between these officials and Ryūsenji's branch temples. During the investigation, the temple's rice tax was kept in the hands of the farmers, but since the investigation could not be concluded quickly, the officials instructed the farmers to first pay the rice tax to Ryūsenji Temple. This was reported by Ryūsenji Temple.

On February 26th, the caretaker Murakami Santayū Masamine visited Senior Councillor Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami Masanao to request the purchase through private negotiation of Makino Sanuki-no-kami Hidenari's estate located in Fukagawa.
