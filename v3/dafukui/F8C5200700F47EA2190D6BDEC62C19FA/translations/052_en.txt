[The lord] was granted leave, and as customary received white silver and summer robes. As thanks for this, he made courtesy calls to the Senior Councilors and chamberlain attendants.
However, when the envoy came, he went out to meet him at the gate entrance.

1. May 15th - [The lord] ascended to the castle to express gratitude for the leave granted, and received a horse as customary.
   Upon leaving, he made courtesy calls to the Grand Elder, Senior Councilors, chamberlain attendants, and Junior Councilors.

1. Same month, 18th - The gifted horse was brought by a messenger from Senior Councilor Toda Yamashiro-no-kami Tadamasa.

1. Same month, 22nd - At Jōkō-in, from the evening of the 20th until this morning (two nights and three days), the 100th day memorial service for Lady Seijō-in was conducted.

1. Same month, 25th
   Regarding Lord Kenmotsu's illness, the following was conveyed to Lord Matsudaira Daizen-no-taifu Tsunahiro through Lords Fujikake Uneme Eiji and Nishio Shichibee:

Record of Verbal Message
Regarding Kenmotsu, as you know, he has been ill for three years now. Given his condition at that time, this is not an illness that would allow him to attend court and perform his duties, which is a difficult situation. Although his complexion has improved since last year, I believe that attending court and performing duties would be presumptuous toward his superiors. Therefore, I intend to request adoption at the end of my life.
