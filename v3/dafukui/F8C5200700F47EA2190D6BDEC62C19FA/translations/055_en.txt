[The previous speaker] had stated their desire to send [the person] away, but since there is no succession of the family headship, if Matsudaira Daizen-dayū wishes to take [the person] back, it would be proper to return [them]. This matter should be communicated. The above.
　　　　　　　　　　May 28th

1. May 29th, regarding the same matter mentioned above, the following letter came from Ōoka Mino-no-kami Tadataka:

　　　　　　　　　　Furthermore, if this is Daizen-dayū's wish, it would be
　　　　　　　　　　reasonable to proceed accordingly, so please be sure to convey this message.
　　　　　　　　Yesterday I had the honor of hearing your thoughts. The matter you mentioned at that time
　　　　　　　　　　should be conveyed by Sagami-no-kami this morning, so it would be most appropriate for you to understand this intention.
　　　　　　　　When I went to Sagami-dono and explained the details, his thinking was the same,

　　　　　　　　but if this is Daizen-dayū's wish,
　　　　　　　　in any case it would be appropriate to return [the person], so he said to convey this point.
　　　　　　　　Of course, this matter should be discussed with your colleagues.
　　　　　　　　Even someone of my humble status believes that if Daizen-dayū-dono
　　　　　　　　wishes to take [the person] back, it would be appropriate to proceed accordingly.

1. Regarding the matter of the adopted son from Kaga that was announced, when I inquired with Sagami-no-kami-dono,
　　[he said that] all matters should be reported to the monthly duty rōjū (senior councilors),
　　so this should be done accordingly, and through a courtesy visit, the above matter [should be communicated].
