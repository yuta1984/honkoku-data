Generally, to memorize all kinds of intangible things, use tangible
things as analogies, and to memorize tangible things,
use intangible things as analogies. This is the one great
essential secret strategy of this method. Tangible things refer to humans, birds and beasts,
utensils and goods, plants and trees, clothing and food, palaces and residences—things that can be clearly seen
with the eyes. Intangible things refer to language, quantities, seasons, abstract concepts
and similar categories that cannot be seen with the eyes.
　　　Complex Texts (multiple items)

【Left Page】
Complex texts refer to having numerous items. Lists of royal dynasties and era names,
or lists of people's names, or the titles of the fifty-four chapters of the Tale of Genji,
or the critique titles of the Mōgyū, as well as the names of the sixty-four hexagrams of the I Ching—
these are intangible things that nevertheless have
a sequential order. If you wish to memorize these, the human body has too few
locations. Therefore, it is essential to expand the range of memory locations.
You may use the architectural structure of houses and such.
