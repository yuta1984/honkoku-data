If you leave them planted without digging them up and take seeds that way, the seed quality will be poor. You should plant the dug-up roots deeply. After planting, pour diluted liquid manure on poor soil. After they grow, you should set up bamboo stakes so they won't fall over in the wind. When the seeds are well-formed, hang them under the eaves to dry, remove the skin, and store the seeds.

According to one theory, when taking seeds from daikon radish and burdock, cut off the lower third of the root before planting. Since the energy goes upward rather than downward, the seeds will form well. Some say that for seed collection, you should cut off from the lower half of the root, below where the small roots are, and plant it. It doesn't matter if you cut below the lower third.
