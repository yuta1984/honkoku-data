Bamboo shoots (takenoko) - Bamboo shoots from light bamboo (hachiku) grow early. The taste is especially good. Bitter bamboo shoots come out late. Though their taste does not match light bamboo, they are also good. Bamboo grass shoots are bitter. Generally, bamboo shoots should be eaten fresh on the same day they are harvested. Those kept long lose their flavor. When exposed to wind or put in water, the taste becomes bad. They should be boiled long together with their husks. Even when pickled in salt for several days, the taste remains good. When cooking bamboo shoots, other ingredients should not be added. Using only one type gives the best flavor. When planting bamboo, water should not be used. Plant those with long horizontal roots. If you plant those without horizontal roots, they will wither. Plant during the rainy season. If planted on May 13th, they will surely grow. This is called "bamboo intoxication day." When shoots first sprout, plant the roots. If sesame meal is scattered around bamboo roots, the bamboo will decrease. If buckwheat hulls are placed, shoots will not grow.
