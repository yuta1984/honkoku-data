The roots can be strung on thread and hung in windy places to dry for eating. There is also wild carrot (ya-korafuku). It appears in the Honcho Komoku under the entry for carrots. It is commonly called "yabu-ninjin" (wild carrot). It resembles carrots and is edible, though the taste is inferior. The roots are small. It grows luxuriantly and is also good for hedges.

Chinese Chives (Nira): The Piya states, "Chives mean 'long-lasting' - once planted, they last forever." The Erya Yi says, "In the first month when the color turns yellow and they have not yet emerged from the soil, the taste is best. They are called 'chive yellow.'" The Zhongshu Shu states, "When planting chives, dig deep furrows and plant in hollowed places. This is for watering and applying manure." Li Shizhen said, "Divide the roots for planting, and also plant seeds." The Nongzheng Quanshu states, "Collect seeds in the ninth month, sow seeds in late second month, and divide and plant in the ninth month. In the tenth month, cover the chives with straw ash about three inches deep, and place thin soil on top so the ash won't scatter in the wind. After Risshun (beginning of spring), harvest the sprouts that emerge from within the ash for eating."
