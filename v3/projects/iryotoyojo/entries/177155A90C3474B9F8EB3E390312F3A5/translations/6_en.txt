Emperor Yan Shennong appeared and tasted all varieties of plants and trees, establishing 365 types of medicinal substances to match the number of days in a year. He compiled the Bencao (Materia Medica) in three volumes (upper, middle, and lower), which has been transmitted to the present day. Five hundred years after Shennong, the Yellow Emperor Youxiong appeared and, together with his ministers Qibo, Guiyuqu, and others, discussed the theory of human body structure, the five organs, six bowels, twelve meridians, and established methods for treating all diseases. He created the Inner Canon consisting of the Plain Questions in nine volumes and the Spiritual Pivot in nine volumes, totaling eighteen volumes. Since one must study the works of these three sages (Fuxi, Shennong, and Yellow Emperor), it is said that a doctor should not practice without mastering these three traditions. Three thousand

years after the Yellow Emperor, which corresponds to fifteen hundred years before the present, during the Later Han dynasty, Zhang Zhongjing, the governor of Changsha, appeared and compiled the "Treatise on Cold Damage and Miscellaneous Diseases" in sixteen volumes, establishing 113 medicinal formulas and 397 treatment methods. From this time, the medical way flourished greatly, and by the Sui, Tang, Song, Yuan, and Ming dynasties, medical books became so numerous that they could make oxen sweat and fill storage houses to the rafters. This concerns Chinese affairs.
