Taking the character "道" from "道" and "三" from "三喜," he called himself "Mitsukara Dōsan" and returned to Rakuyō (Kyoto), where he had an audience with Lord Kōgen-in Yoshiteru and received especially generous patronage. He was also warmly received by Hosokawa Katsumoto, Miyoshi Shuri, Matsunaga Danjō, and others, and thus his name became known throughout the realm. The current dosage method for taking medicine—adding one and a half cups of water and boiling it down to one cup, the method called "noble-constant"—originated with this Dōsan. Therefore, the new school is called the "current school" and the old school the "other school." Regarding wrapping forms, both current and other schools formerly made all small packages as incense wrappings, but since Katahiji Roan wrapped them with one hand, the Nakarai school uses mountain-shaped wrappings.

When wrapping the outer package in sword form, making the right side short and left side long creates the shape of the character "出" (exit). This is used to promote dispersal and expel all diseases. Making the left short and right long creates the shape of the character "入" (enter). This is used to treat diseases such as gastric reflux, esophageal obstruction, loss of appetite, and parasitic accumulation. Generally, medical treatment focuses primarily on the patient's taboos and prayer rituals. In general, licorice is added to medicine to moderate and soften harsh medicinal flavors. Ginger is added for surface expression and guidance. Jujube is added to support and nourish spleen qi. Dōsan's principle of "pinch of licorice, slice of ginger, one jujube" means that too much licorice over-moderates and weakens other medicines' effects, too much ginger causes adverse rising harm, and too many jujubes cause chest congestion and loss of appetite.
