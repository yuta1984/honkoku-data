Trismus is a disease of newborns, and at first, they cry incessantly

without stopping, gradually lose their voice and breathing becomes labored, then they clench their mouth shut and cannot open it. Therefore it is also called "tsubokuchi" (closed mouth). It is an acute serious illness, and while trying various treatments, the hands and feet become cold and the child dies. One must notice early, open the mouth, and examine inside and outside the gums. There are many small blood blisters, tiny as millet grains, round and red like winter cherry. These should be broken with fingernails or needles to release blood, then apply a mixture of five parts fine borax powder, two parts refined cinnabar or native cinnabar, and about one part sal ammoniac with a brush tip, and quickly give large amounts of purple pills to purge.
