○Sake thickens qi and causes it to rise. It is said to have yang properties. Sake is also called "the chief of a hundred medicines." If drunk in small amounts, there are benefits without harm. An old saying goes, "For sake, slight intoxication is best; for flowers, half-bloom is best." When drunk in moderation, it deepens human relationships, nourishes qi and blood, assists the power of medicines, and is useful in both auspicious and inauspicious matters. When drunk excessively, it damages the internal organs, causes vomiting of blood, hemorrhoids from drinking, and various diseases, or leads to madness and severs human relationships. One should be cautious and fearful of this.

○The "Goko Manbun" states: "People who drink much sake have short lives. All long-lived people do not drink sake." It says, "There are no long-lived people among those who drink sake."

○Method of post-meal care: After some time has passed following a meal, stroke downward from chest to abdomen, also massage around the area of the "kyomon" (京門), do the same to the lower back and tap lightly. This is nourishment.

○Hua Tuo's words say: "One should engage in moderate labor after meals. Through labor, grain qi is digested and blood vessels circulate properly. When digestion is poor, it becomes the root of various diseases."

○The "Lüshi Chunqiu" states: "Flowing water does not rot, and door hinges are not eaten by insects." This is because they are constantly in motion.
