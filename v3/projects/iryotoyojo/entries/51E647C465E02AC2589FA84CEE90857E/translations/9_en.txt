For evening meals, fish and poultry are not suitable. Light, easily digestible foods, dried fish, sesame miso, and similar items should be served. Sake should also be served in small amounts after meals. This helps the food's qi circulate well. Freshly pounded mochi (rice cakes) are not good. Elderly people and children sometimes choke on them and die. They should be well-cooked before serving. Hard mochi or grilled mochi are also not suitable. They should be grilled, then soaked in hot water to soften before serving. Hard grilled mochi often causes throat blockages.

Also, it is not good for elderly people to travel to distant places, as this fatigues the body. If they must go due to unavoidable circumstances, and if they have no children, relatives should accompany them. This is to reduce mental stress. No matter how robust an elderly person may be, if they intend to travel ten ri, they should go six or seven ri; if they intend to go six or seven ri, they should stop at four or five ri. Forcing long-distance travel causes bodily pain and is not good. In all matters, one should be moderate. Generally, elderly people have weak intestines and stomachs.
