　　　Care of the Elderly

○Serving one's parents with filial piety is the foundation of descendants' prosperity
As a child, respecting and treasuring one's parents is of utmost importance. First, one should devote oneself wholeheartedly to pleasing one's parents in all matters. During morning and evening, and at bedtime, even the care of the parents' bedding should not be left to servants, but the married couple should handle raising and lowering it themselves. Also, during meals, the couple should personally serve the meal trays.

Especially if the parents have reached the age of sixty, every night before sleep, one should wash their feet with appropriately warm water. This warms them well, and this is particularly desirable during cold seasons. During hot weather, one should keep them sufficiently cool and pay attention to their morning and evening living spaces. Food should be soft and flavorful, and if they normally eat three bowls, serve two and a half bowls; if they eat two bowls, serve one and a half bowls—in any case, reduce the amount by about half a bowl. This is not for economy but for health, and this should be well explained to them. The scripture says, "Those who moderate their food and drink can well avoid illness—this is a good method."
