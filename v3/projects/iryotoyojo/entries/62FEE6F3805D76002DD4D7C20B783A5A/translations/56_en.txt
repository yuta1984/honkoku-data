【Right page】
　　Old Friends　Friends in old age
Listening to dharma talks and sightseeing are difficult for the elderly, so intimate companions should converse and comfort each other

　　Holding and Avoiding　Keeping what is good and avoiding what is bad
What is good to hold for oneself should simply be held, and what brings ease when avoided should always be avoided

　　Reducing Desires　Lessening one's desires
With a body limited like dew, if you follow boundless desires, you will quickly scatter and fall

　　Difficult Practice　That which is difficult to practice
People who hear discretion with their ears and understand it in their hearts but do not practice it are worthless

【Left page】
　　Poetry Sages　About poetry
The path of Shikishima (Japanese poetry) reveals traces unknown to people, even where immortals have frequently traveled

　　Forgetting Worries　Not dwelling on anxious thoughts
The nourishment that rests the body and extends the heart lies in forgetting all worldly matters

　　Envying Sufficiency　Knowing one's proper place
The joy of not envying and knowing sufficiency brings days and nights of old age without calamity

　　Jar Mouth　Being cautious with one's words
In any case, like a jar's mouth that depends on people, there is no disaster whether things come out or go in
