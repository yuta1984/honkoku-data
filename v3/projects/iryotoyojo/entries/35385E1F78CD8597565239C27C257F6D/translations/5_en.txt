I have been sickly since childhood, so I have always focused on regulating and maintaining my body, and having gained some effectiveness from this, I came to believe in the way of cultivating one's nature from middle age. As I nourished my body, I gradually became aware of my body's robustness, and now, already over sixty years of age, I do not feel my eyesight has declined—I can write small characters by candlelight. I know this is the virtue of regulation and life cultivation. Therefore, I have extracted the necessary parts from books on life cultivation that I have been fond of for many years, rendered them into kana script, hoping to help people avoid the sufferings of premature death and illness, and to assist acquaintances and relatives, thus naming it "Shōgai Yōjinshū" (Lifelong Precautions Collection).

Written at Katsushika Roan on a spring day of Bunsei 6, year of the Water Sheep
