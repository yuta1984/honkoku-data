There are various symptoms, and in some cases they do not heal at that time, in some cases they persist for several days, in some cases the aforementioned symptoms exist but the patient does not die and persists for several years, in some cases there are those who recover completely, in some cases they do not heal in middle age, and in some cases they heal in old age. It differs according to each person's constitution (deficiency or excess) and the severity of the disease. There are types such as sudden stroke (sotchū), true stroke (shinchū), and stroke-like conditions (ruichū). Generally, the cause from which a disease arises is called "in" (cause). Regarding the causes of apoplexy, what has been discussed since ancient times: physicians like Zhang Zhongjing and Sun Simiao attributed it to being struck by pathogenic wind, while in later generations from the Song and Yuan dynasties onward, Liu Hejian attributed it to fire, Li Dongyuan attributed it to deficiency of central qi, and Zhu Danxi attributed it to damp phlegm.
