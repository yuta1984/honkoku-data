The explanation is that the nature of alcohol is to promote circulation (xuan tong) and does not stagnate or accumulate, therefore one becomes drunk and then sobers up again. This is because it follows the flow and dispersion of blood vessels. Now, when people have stagnation of nutritive and defensive qi and accumulation of phlegm and water, and also drink alcohol without reaching great intoxication but vomit greatly, the alcohol and phlegm strike against each other and cannot be dispersed and dissolved. Therefore the abdomen becomes full and cannot digest.

When one drinks alcohol excessively and the abdomen is constantly distended and large, this is as stated in the Inner Classic and also as described by Master Chao. Initially one does not realize this and does not practice proper care, and after a long time eventually develops the disease of distension and fullness. Such cases are mostly difficult to cure. Generally, there should be many examples of distension and fullness caused by alcohol.

【Left page】
In recent times, according to Zhu Danxi's account, one man aged fifty who was fond of alcohol suffered from malaria for half a year and then developed distension. His limbs became thin while his abdomen became large, resembling the shape of a spider. He took medicine and strictly observed precepts, and was cured after half a year. Another man over forty years old by nature was fond of alcohol and sometimes saw blood during bowel movements. He suffered from distension and fullness, his complexion became black and his abdomen large, resembling the shape of a demon. He took medicine and became well after one month. All of these show that alcohol causing distension and fullness has been so since ancient times.
