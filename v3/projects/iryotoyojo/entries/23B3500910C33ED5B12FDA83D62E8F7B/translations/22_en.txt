If so, then koji is first a corrupted substance. Water and grains are combined with this and further corrupted to become sake. The sourness of vinegar also comes from the decay of water and grains. Water and grains originally do not intoxicate people. Because decay produces dampness and heat, generating damp-heat toxins, they can intoxicate people well. Being drunk means being poisoned and suffering from toxins. When one is intoxicated by other substances,
