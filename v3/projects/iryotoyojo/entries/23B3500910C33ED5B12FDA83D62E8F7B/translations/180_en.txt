The tongue and teeth also belong to diseases of the mouth. These also have various pathological symptoms, and it is impossible to enumerate them all. Regarding the occurrence of oral diseases, their causes are also various, but according to ancient theories, most are attributed to heat in the spleen and stomach. This stomach heat is invariably caused by imbalanced flavors and excessive consumption of rich foods. Generally, while many foods can cause stomach heat, none is more severe than alcohol. The fact that one inevitably desires to drink water after consuming alcohol shows that it generates stomach heat. Due to stomach heat, various oral diseases arise. According to Li Dongyan's theory,

oral erosion is caused by those who enjoy drinking alcohol. According to Luo Qianfu's theory, halitosis is caused by heavy drinking and qi of great heat. Regarding dental caries, Wang Haicang's theory states that those who enjoy alcohol to excess contract this disease. The Zhengzhi Zhunsheng states that gum bleeding involves blood coming from the tooth roots - one man had blood flowing from his tooth roots filling a basin, occurring once a month, which was attributed to his fondness for alcohol. When teeth constantly ache, become loose and fall out early, this mostly occurs in heavy drinkers. The tongue also has various pathological symptoms including double tongue, wooden tongue, tongue sores, tongue bleeding and others, which can also be caused by alcohol. According to Zhang Zihe's theory, one person suffered from muteness and the tongue could not speak - he had fallen asleep drunk and been exposed to wind, and the wind struck his meridians causing muteness.
