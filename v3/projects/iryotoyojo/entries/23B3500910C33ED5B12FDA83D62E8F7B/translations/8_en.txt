This work was created out of concern that excessive drinking harms one's life. It first presents a general discourse on abstaining from alcohol to establish the main principles, then records the symptoms of various diseases to enumerate the details. The discussion is thorough and the selection is precise—truly admirable and beyond further praise. Alas, how great is alcohol's involvement with human beings! If one does not drink, that is the end of it, but once one drinks, merit and harm become interrelated, and the matter becomes far from simple. The reason for this is that this substance,
