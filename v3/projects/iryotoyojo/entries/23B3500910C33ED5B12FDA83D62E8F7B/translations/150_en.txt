On Blood Loss
○Blood loss refers to hematemesis and bloody stools - all forms of bleeding. What comes out from the mouth as pure blood vomiting is called hematemesis; what comes out mixed with saliva is called salivary bleeding; what comes out with coughing is called cough-bleeding; what comes out mixed in phlegm is called phlegm-bleeding; what comes out from the tongue is called tongue bleeding; what comes out from the gums is called dental bleeding; what comes out from the nose is called epistaxis; what comes out in urine is called hematuria; what comes out in feces is called bloody stool. There are also distinctions between distant blood and near blood. What comes out through sweat is called bloody sweating.
