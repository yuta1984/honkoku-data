　　Heat Reversal (Netsuketsu)
○Reversal refers to qi flowing upward in reverse - when the hands and feet become cold it is called "cold reversal," and when the hands and feet become hot it is called "heat reversal." The Inner Classic states: "When alcohol enters the stomach, the collateral vessels become full while the meridian vessels become empty. When yin qi becomes deficient, yang qi enters, and when essence qi is exhausted, it cannot nourish the four limbs. Such a person inevitably gets drunk frequently and engages in sexual activity, causing kidney qi to weaken daily while only yang qi flourishes. Therefore the hands and feet become hot." Chunyu Yi said: "Heat reversal is obtained by drinking alcohol and becoming greatly intoxicated." Zhang Zihe said: "What is obtained through intoxication is called 'alcohol reversal.'" The Kokon Itō states: "Alcohol reversal occurs in those who enjoy drinking large quantities of alcohol."

　　Wandering Joint Wind (Rekisetsufū)
○Wandering joint wind is a condition where the body's joints ache and one cannot bend or extend them. In severe cases it is called "white tiger wandering joint wind," which is like being bitten by a tiger. While this can have other causes, it can also be caused by alcohol. The Theory of Disease Origins states: "This is caused by drinking alcohol, which opens the skin texture, causes sweating, and then being exposed to wind or entering water."
