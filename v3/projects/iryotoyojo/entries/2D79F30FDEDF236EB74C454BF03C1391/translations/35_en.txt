I penetrated through completely and obtained great joy about six or seven times. As for other small enlightenments and moments of such elation that I forgot myself in ecstatic dance, they were countless. For the first time I understood what Myōki (Dahui Zonggao) meant when he said "great enlightenment eighteen times, small enlightenments countless." Truly, this did not deceive me. In the past, even wearing two or three pairs of tabi (split-toe socks), my soles always felt as if soaked in ice and snow, but now, even on the severely cold days of the three winter months, I wear no tabi and use no hearth.
