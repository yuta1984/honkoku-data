In a world that is neither this world nor any other, between paradise and hell, there lives the venerable King Enma. This great king makes rounds through the three thousand worlds, so beginning with the Ten Kings, there are countless court retainers, and many who govern their respective offices. Therefore it stands to reason that in human society, each of the four classes—warriors, farmers, artisans, and merchants—are busy without respite. King Enma's palace was not so busy in the old days, but in recent years people's hearts have become hardened, and there are many who commit various evil deeds. The number of sinners increases daily without limit, so the existing hells are entirely insufficient in terms of ground space. Taking advantage of King Enma's distress, speculators compete to ingratiate themselves privately, offering bribes and flattery to officials, submitting various petitions. They survey land here and there within the ten billion lands of the Paradise Highway, clearing areas up to the borders of Jizo Bodhisattva's domain and the eggplant fields, digging pools hundreds of ri long, boiling sappanwood to create pools of blood, building mountains and planting sword seedlings, and since the mortars for beating sinners are beyond the reach of the hell guards' hands, they install waterwheels.
