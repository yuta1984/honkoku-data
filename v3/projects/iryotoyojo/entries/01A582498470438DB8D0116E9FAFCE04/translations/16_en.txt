Asiatic dayflower　Also called tsukikusa (moon grass). It is bamboo joint vegetable. Pick young seedlings and boil with leaves, then season
　with soy sauce to eat. Master Genko says that in the south it is called light bamboo leaf and is prized. The materia medica states its nature is
　bitter and very cold, non-toxic, and treats snake and dog bites. Grind both stems and leaves and apply around the wound,
　cover the wound with magnolia bark leaves so wind cannot enter, and when fat and water flow
　out from the wound and pain subsides and swelling reduces, remove the magnolia bark leaves and apply only dayflower as a covering to heal.
　Dog bites are not to be taken lightly and should be considered life-threatening illness; seek good medical prescriptions.
Winter cherry　Also called lantern child or hanging golden lantern. Take leaves, boil, soak in water to remove bitterness,
　season with soy sauce and eat. When fruit ripens, pick and prepare the same way for food.

【Left Page】
Nipplewort　Should be boiled and eaten.
Japanese knotweed　Treats retained lochia after childbirth. Pregnant women should not eat it. When eating the several types mentioned above, do not add salt.
Summer cypress　Boil seedlings and leaves, rinse and eat.
Dogtooth violet　The Chinese characters are not yet clear. Nanbu in Ōshū produces the best quality. Inō Jakusui says this is probably drought
　lotus root. Its powder is like rice, sweet in taste, and eating it supplements and benefits people. The manufacturing
　method is like trichosanthin powder. The detoxification method is the same as mentioned before.
Job's tears　Not the thick-shelled round type, but the pointed form with thin shell. Its grains are white
　like glutinous rice. Should be made into porridge, rice, or noodles for food. Like rice, it can be made into alcohol.
　When made into rice or noodles and eaten, one knows no hunger. However, since it is hard to break down when boiled,
　it is best to powder it and make steamed rice cakes or dumplings.
