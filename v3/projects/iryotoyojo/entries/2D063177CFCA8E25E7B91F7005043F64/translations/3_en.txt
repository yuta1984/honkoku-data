Preface to Continued Torite-gusa (Continued Fortress Grass)

This year, Mizunoto-Ushi (1853), foreign envoys came to Japan. They have opened quite alarming signs of war. For this reason, Kayū Genjun, a domain physician of Tahara Domain, compiled medicinal formulas for military emergency treatment and authored one volume of writings. He named this "Continued Torite-gusa." Recently, he sent the manuscript from afar and requested that I write a preface.

When I examined his book, all that was recorded were foreign medical prescriptions. I sighed and said: "Alas, those who wound warriors and soldiers are foreign captives and bandits. Those who heal their wounds use foreign medicinal formulas. If that is the case, then even though such people may be greatly detestable, must we not accept their knowledge?"

I have long known Genjun to be a physician of Chinese medicine. Yet why does he now compile foreign prescriptions? Probably he does not do so out of preference, but given the present circumstances, there is something inevitable about this course of action. Why? Because in the current warfare with foreign barbarians, military force is not prioritized...
