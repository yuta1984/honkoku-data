One should nurture [the patient's spirit]. Also, there are numerous preventive medicines in the world, but one must never be misled by them. Kyūgo-zeu said: I have ten children, both boys and girls. As an experiment, six of them used preventive antidotes, and their smallpox was actually severe. Four did not use medicine, and their smallpox was actually mild. From this I learned that preventive antidotes are all medicines that damage the spleen and stomach, causing harm with no benefit. Seeing these cases, one should not rely on medicine for smallpox. One should well understand the principle that once the fixed period passes, it naturally heals.
