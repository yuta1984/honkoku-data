They consider these matters as secondary. The arts of archery, horsemanship, spear, and sword also do not strive to cultivate their true martial valor, but only make quick techniques and competition their business. The entire realm flows in this manner because there are no virtuous teachers in these ways, and those with insight are few. In all matters, when one does not seek from the foundation of natural moral principles and does not investigate from practical experience, these become mostly superficial empty theories. When one studies these carelessly, they instead become entanglements that bind one's hands and feet. Therefore, one must thoroughly understand and distinguish these matters.

Even when Mencius wished to explain this qi, he first discussed the courage that relies merely on blood and physical energy, such as that of Beigong Yu and Meng Shishe, and then later presented the great courage that arises from moral principles, as exemplified by Zengzi.
