One: Mencius's technique of not moving the heart was entirely obtained through cultivating this qi (vital energy). However, when he spoke of achieving the accomplishment of not moving his heart at age forty, this is because generally when people reach around forty years of age, having experienced many things, their will becomes refined and mature, the Way becomes clear and virtue elevated, and their life's direction is settled at this time of life. Even Confucius, being a great sage, said "At forty I was not perplexed." This is because no matter how intelligent and virtuous a person may be, during their youth, following the fullness and vigor of their blood and qi, they are prone to mistakes and errors in judgment. The ancient sages also established forty as the age for entering government service, saying "At forty one is called strong for service."

When one reaches forty, one's thinking naturally becomes more precise, so from this settled insight that does not waver, Mencius also spoke of not moving the heart at forty. However, this achievement of not moving the heart came entirely from the power of learning and cultivation during one's youth. It does not mean that the heart moved in various ways before forty and then suddenly stopped moving upon reaching forty. Confucius's saying "At forty I was not perplexed"...
