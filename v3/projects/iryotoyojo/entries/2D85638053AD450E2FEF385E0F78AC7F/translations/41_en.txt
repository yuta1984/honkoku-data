【Right page】
In the main text, it states that for traumatic wounds where arteries are severed and blood spurts out uncontrollably, one should tie the blood vessels with thread or cauterize them with a hot iron.
　However, by using these bandages (wrapped cotton) to block the pathways through which blood flows to the extremities,
　　most bleeding will gradually stop. Therefore, here we present diagrams to show
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　an outline of this method.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Twist cotton
　　　　　　　　　　　　　firmly, and
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　apply pressure like this—
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　it serves as a cushion.
　　　　　　　　　　For the upper limb,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　same as for bandaging,
　　　　　　　　　　　　use wood carved round.
Use a thin stick—　　　　　　　　　　This is called spiral binding.
　chopsticks broken in half
　　work well for emergency situations.
　　　When twisting and tightening with this stick, it compresses and stops the blood flow through vessels.

【Left page】
　　Board splint　　　　　　　　　　For a fractured tibia,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　applying the above apparatus
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　looks like this. Other cases should be understood
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　following the same principle.

　The board should be four to five bu wide and two to three bu thick, with the front and
　　　rear ends rounded, and the four corners also rounded
　　　　and smoothed, wrapped with paper and pasted with glue. Use torn cotton
　　　　　　　　　edges or hemp to weave it together.

　　Bamboo blind

　　　　　　　　These two types are for
　　　　　　　　　applying to fractured bones
　　　　　　　　　　and bandaging them—
　　　　　　　　　　　　this should be examined
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　together with what is
　　　　　　　　　　　　recorded in the main text.


　Carefully shave the front, back, and four sides
　　　of bamboo, and weave with hemp.
　　Make whichever of these two types is available to you.
