Regarding hemostatic medicines
For ordinary hemostasis, quicklime should be ground to fine powder, kneaded with egg white, dried in the sun, ground again to very fine powder, and sifted before use.

While it stops blood well, when ash powder enters the wound opening, it often causes suppuration, so when using this, ensure that the ash powder does not get trapped in the wound opening. Brazier ash also stops blood well, so if one intends to use it, it should be ground to fine powder and sifted before use. Mabotsu, commonly called hokoritake, is also a good substance that stops blood well. It grows in bamboo groves and such places and is available at pharmacies. These should also be stored and used. However, they cannot match the blood-stopping effectiveness of hahasodake. Hahasodake is a fungus that grows on oak trees. Its nature is soft like leather.
