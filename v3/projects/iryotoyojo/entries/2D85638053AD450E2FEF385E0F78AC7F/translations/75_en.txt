Expose only the face above ground, pack it down well, and leave it as is for a while. If the body becomes soft, vital energy will surely return. This is because the slight yang energy remaining in the body attempts to expand toward the skin and muscles in opposition to the pressure of cold surrounding the entire body. When there are signs of this softening, one should determine that revival is certain and apply subsequent treatment methods. Before burying in snow like this, it is best to first massage with the palms from the extremities up to the shoulders, back, chest, and abdomen, kneading and softening each joint, then proceed with this method. It is also good to draw cold water from beneath ice and pour it abundantly from the head over the body, or to fill a basin with cold water for immersion. Sitting immersion is preferable, but if the basin is too shallow to reach the shoulders, it is better to elevate the head and have the patient lie supine with the head and face above water.
