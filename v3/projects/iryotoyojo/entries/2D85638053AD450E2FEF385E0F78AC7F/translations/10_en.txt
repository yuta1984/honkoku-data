Rolling down from high mountains—such battle momentum will naturally surge forth, treating even large cannons and fire attacks as nothing, and there will surely be many who display their brave might and advance forward—this is inevitable. Therefore, when such brave young warriors suffer damage from firearms and fire attacks, receive wounds from fierce strikes and assaults, or sustain bruises and sprains, it would be an aid to the spirit of loving people if generals, officers, and unit commanders could learn the techniques to treat these conditions and quickly rescue them from suffering and calamity. Having resolved this, I suddenly followed the intentions of predecessors and wrote down methods for treating urgent crises in a way that even laypeople could immediately comprehend.

I selected economical medicines that are easy to manufacture and compiled them into a small booklet. This is merely my humble loyalty from the grasslands, like rain gear during a drought.

　　　On Burns
Fire techniques include howitzers, fire bottles, thunder cannons, thunder tubes, fire chests, fire ships, explosive shells, and other weapons to threaten the enemy. For extremely light burns where the skin has not reached the point of festering, quickly pouring abundant water on the injured area will immediately ease the pain. However, for somewhat severe cases, if the injured area is on hands or feet, quickly immerse them in lamp oil for a while. For the whole body, use wine from a wine barrel.
