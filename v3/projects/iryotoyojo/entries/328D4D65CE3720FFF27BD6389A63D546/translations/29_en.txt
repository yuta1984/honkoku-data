○Two Hundred and Tenth Day
The two hundred and tenth day from Risshun. Around this time is when autumn storms blow, so fearing damage to rice crops, this is used as a precautionary marker. The two hundred and twentieth day is also mentioned. However, it doesn't mean wind blows only on this specific day; it's generally the same before and after.

○Shanichi (Shrine Days)
Shanichi are the previous Tsuchinoe days of the second and eighth months. There are two days per year. These are days when the field gods and the gods who first sowed the five grains are worshipped and celebrated in China. It's called Shanichi meaning to worship the gods of earth and grain. In our country, they're called Shanichi-kō, spring holidays, autumn holidays, etc., where people worship their tutelary deities and celebrate individually without moving earth.
