○Food injury and spleen-stomach deficiency are similar but differ for the following reasons: Those who become ill from being temporarily injured by food can be easily treated if there is vomiting and diarrhea, while those whose spleen and stomach become deficient due to food are difficult to treat. Medical texts say that "abdominal pain before vomiting and diarrhea, with the abdominal pain decreasing after vomiting and diarrhea, is food stagnation. If there is abdominal pain after vomiting and diarrhea, it is spleen-stomach deficiency." One should distinguish this oneself and

Elderly people and children have difficulty digesting food, and if they eat hard or tough things, they will pass them directly in their stool without digesting their properties. The spleen and stomach are also easily injured. Special care should be taken in daily life. In general, one should know that raw, cold, dry, and hard foods damage the stomach and are not digested.
