falling ill. Since food is the foundation for preserving human life, ordinary prudence is of primary importance. In ancient times, there was no fixed number for daily meals. High-ranking noble officials who governed the people from above ate twice a day, while farmers and common people of lower status who engaged in cultivation and manual labor ate four or five times a day. It is appropriate for men's daily activities to involve multiple meals, while women's less frequent activities require fewer meals. Even today, this custom exists among farmers and agricultural households.
