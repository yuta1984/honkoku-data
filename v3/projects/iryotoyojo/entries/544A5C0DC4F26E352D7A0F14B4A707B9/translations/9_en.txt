What great matter would it be? However, the gentleman considered the distant future and restrained this. That trend indeed spread from those above to those below who emulated them, leading to the use of beaten silver and polished unicorn horn as chopsticks. In the Tsurezuregusa, it tells how Taira no Nobutoki came to visit Saimyōji-dono for evening conversation, and when there was no accompaniment for sake, rather than wake the servants, he went to the kitchen himself and found a small earthenware dish with miso stuck to it in the corner of a shelf, saying "this will do perfectly," and brought it back to use as an accompaniment while pleasantly sharing many cups.
