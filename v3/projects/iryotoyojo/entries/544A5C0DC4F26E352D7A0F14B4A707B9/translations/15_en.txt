The seven emotions of love, hatred, and desire, whether intense or mild, are universal human feelings without distinction. However, as also shown in the Doctrine of the Mean, there should be a proper degree to these, and when these emotions become excessive, the qi becomes bound up because of this and causes the circulation of blood to stagnate. When people stand in this world, there are all manner of worldly affairs that constantly burden the mind. Being able to make good decisions and handle matters appropriately - this too should be the foremost principle of health cultivation.
