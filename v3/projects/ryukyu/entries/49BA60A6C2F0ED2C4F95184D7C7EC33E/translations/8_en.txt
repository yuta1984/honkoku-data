Also, it records that in ancient times the Imperial Court called it "Uruma no Kuni," but this is an error. In the Kintō Kyōshū it states "People from Shiraki's Uruma island came, but they could not understand what the people here were saying," so it appears to have been a place belonging to Silla.

The "iroha" practiced on that island is mentioned in the Denshin-roku, but among them, the twelve characters "tochiri kayo neko hayo emo" are transmitted incorrectly. The katakana are well transmitted, with only the character "wa" being different, while characters like "a" are particularly well preserved. The character "kyō" is not yet present, which is remarkable (the explanation about the absence of the character "kyō" is detailed in the critical examination of Waji Seiran).
