Joo 3 (1654)

On the 6th day of the 8th month of Keicho 15 (1610), Shimazu Iehisa arrived in Sunpu accompanied by the King of Ryukyu. On the 8th day of the same month, by order of the shogun, they went to the castle and had an audience with the Ogosho (Tokugawa Ieyasu). The King of Ryukyu presented one hundred bolts of damask silk, twelve hiro of orangutan fur, two hundred bolts of taiheifu cloth, one thousand pieces of silver, and one tachi sword. On the 18th day, he was granted a feast and ordered to perform noh, and Iehisa was bestowed with a Sadamune sword and a wakizashi of the same maker. On the 20th day of the same month, Iehisa and the Ryukyu King departed from Sunpu and arrived in Edo on the 25th day. On the 26th day, an envoy was sent from the Shogun's house and one thousand bales of rice were bestowed. On the 28th day, Iehisa went to the castle and presented a Nagataka tachi sword, one hundred bolts of damask silk, ten tiger pelts, and one thousand pieces of silver. He also presented one tachi sword, one horse, and one hundred kin of crimson to Takechiyo-sama. On the 3rd day of the 9th month, he was granted a feast.
