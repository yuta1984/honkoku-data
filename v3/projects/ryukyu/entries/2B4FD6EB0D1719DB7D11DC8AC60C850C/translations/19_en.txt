It is granted for meritorious service - some for one lifetime, some for a fixed number of years or generations, and some permanently. ○ For the calendar, interpreter officials prepare and use calculated calendars based on perpetual almanacs in advance, and when they go to Qing China, they bring back one hundred copies of the Qing calendar each year, following which they distribute them throughout the country. ○ For distance measurements, they use the thirty-six chō-ri system. ○ There are celebrations on New Year's Day, the Lantern Festival, and the winter solstice. On the king's birthday, there is a grand celebration ceremony with appointments and general amnesty. Other ritual ceremonies for ancestral temples, altars of state, and similar institutions are substantially established. ○ Military preparedness is quite weak. Generally, civil officials concurrently hold military positions. The military system integrates soldiers with farmers - five households form a squad (go), and five squads are linked together. Those called Foster Parents of the Clouds and Chikudun all train in military arts and go out to fight when there are incidents. Weapons include armor, helmets, spears, swords, bows, arrows, lances, and cannons. Most are prepared for naval warfare with ships. Within the country, there are no fortresses outside the royal castle. Naha has training grounds and gun batteries. ○ For criminal law, there are three death penalties: dismemberment, beheading, and crucifixion. There are five light punishments: exile, exposure to sun, pressing between boards, cangue, and flogging. When someone commits a crime, officials report it to the Legal Officers, who decide right from wrong without delay. The judicial system is extremely strict, not avoiding even high officials, and is said not to bend justice even for fathers, sons, and brothers.
