"The matter of your relocation having been completed has been acknowledged, and I consider it most joyful. I sit thus to express words of congratulation. With fear and respect, respectfully stated.
　　　4th month, 3rd day　　　　　　　　　Chūzan King Shō On (seal)
　　　　To Lord Shimazu Noboru"
　(It is conjectured that the name Shō On does not appear in the genealogies. One document mentions "Shō On gratitude envoy" in Kansei 8, possibly the same person as Shō Sei - awaiting future investigation)

During the Tokugawa period, from Keichō to Kaei, court visits totaled approximately twenty times, as follows:

　　Keichō 15 (1610)　　King Shō Nei's court visit
　　Same 16　　　　　　Envoy's court visit
　　Kan'ei 11 (1634)　　Shō Hō, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Sashiki, gratitude chief envoy Prince Kin
　　Shōhō 1 (1644)　　　Shō Ken, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Kin, gratitude chief envoy Prince Kunigami
　　Keian 2 (1649)　　　Shō Shitsu, gratitude chief envoy Prince Gushikawa
　　Jōō 2 (1653)　　　　Same, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Kunigami
　　Kanbun 11 (1671)　　Shō Tei, gratitude chief envoy Prince Kin, vice-envoy Goeku oyakata
　　Tenna 2 (1682)　　　Same, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Nago, vice-envoy Onna oyakata
　　Genroku 7 (1694)　　Shō Eki, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Misato, gratitude chief envoy Prince Tomigusuku
　　Shōtoku 4 (1714)　　Shō Kei, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Yonagusuku, gratitude chief envoy Prince Kin
　　Kyōhō 3 (1718)　　　Same, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Goeku, vice-envoy Nishihira oyakata
　　Kan'en 1 (1748)　　　Same, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Gushikawa, vice-envoy Yonabaru oyakata
　　Hōreki 2 (1752)　　　Shō Boku, gratitude chief envoy Prince Nakijin
　　Meiwa 1 (1764)　　　Same, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Yomitanzan
　　Kansei 2 (1790)　　　Same, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Ginowan, vice-envoy Kōchi oyakata
　　Same 8 (1796)　　　　Shō Sei, gratitude chief envoy Prince Ōgimi, vice-envoy Yasumura oyakata
　　Bunka 3 (1806)　　　Shō Ken, gratitude chief envoy Prince Yomitanzan, vice-envoy Oroku oyakata
　　Tenpō 3 (1832)　　　Shō Iku, gratitude chief envoy Prince Tomigusuku, vice-envoy Takushi oyakata
　　Same 13 (1842)　　　Same, congratulatory chief envoy Prince Urasoe, vice-envoy Zakimi oyakata
　　Kaei 3 (1850)　　　　Shō Tai, gratitude chief envoy Prince Tamagawa, vice-envoy Nomura oyakata
