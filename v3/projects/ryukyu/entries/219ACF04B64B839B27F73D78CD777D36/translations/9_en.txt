In September, King Sho Tai dispatched his uncle Sho Ken to Tokyo to congratulate the Emperor on his personal rule. In this month, the vassal Sho Tai was ranked among the nobility as a domain lord. The Shimazu clan's jurisdiction was abolished and made into a direct territory, implementing the abolition of domains and establishment of prefectures. Lord Goto of Nishikigawa served in the Ministry of Home Affairs and, together with Senior Secretary Matsuda of the Ministry of Home Affairs, went to Okinawa to convey orders. Subsequently, Lord Goto again went there on official business, became increasingly familiar with island affairs, and wrote the three-volume "Nanto Kiji" (Records of the Southern Islands). Former prefectural governor Lord Nishimura, in his spare time from governing the people, personally recorded what he observed and heard, creating a separate two-volume edition that was also published. Sadayoshi's entries into Okinawa totaled four times in all. He also once wrote the five-volume "Okinawa-shi" (Okinawa Records). However, the institutional systems and criminal administration of that time were in the hands of the king, and when island officials carried out their duties, many kept secrets and did not reveal the truth.
