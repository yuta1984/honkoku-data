Ōganeku Village is the most prosperous place in the Kunigami region, with the Kunigami government office and medical examination facility. There is also a Nago police station in Agarie Village.

The products are rice, wheat, sweet potatoes, firewood, sea fish, and so forth.

Motobu magiri has eighteen villages. It is a peninsula jutting out into the sea. The northwest is surrounded by seawater and faces Ie Island, while the southeast is separated by mountain forests and borders the three magiri of Nakijin, Haneji, and Nago. It is about twenty-one ri and eighteen chō from Naha.

On the border with Nago magiri to the southeast are Mount Yae and Mount Gushikawa. Both are high and steep. What towers above them is called Mount Katsuu. It is the highest mountain on the main island.

The Toguchi River originates from Mount Ōarashi and flows west into Toguchi Port.

In Toguchi Village there is a harbor of the same name. Ships traveling between Naha and Kunigami invariably enter this bay and wait for the wind to calm before raising their sails. In the northwest of the bay are two islands, large and small. The large one is called Sesoko Island, and the small one is called Minna Island. Both belong to this magiri.

The products are rice, wheat, sugar, indigo, timber, firewood, and so forth.

Nakijin magiri has twenty-one villages. To the southwest it borders the three magiri of Haneji, Nago, and Motobu through mountain forests, and on two sides to the northeast it faces the sea.
