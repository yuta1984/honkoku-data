In Aka Village there are two waterfalls. One is called Kuhirihanta and one is called Hige-mizu. Both hang from precipices. Each is over four jō high. They are constantly stirred by sea winds and soar into the sky for over ten jō. When viewed from afar, they appear like clouds and mist. They present an extremely spectacular sight.

Products include pongee silk and rush mats, which are most famous, as well as lumber, nago orchids, sponges, grains, fish and shellfish, etc.

The Two Sakishima Islands

The Sakishima Islands begin from about ninety ri southwest of Naha Port on the sea, and to the southwest are not far from Taiwan Island.

There are about twenty islets. These are divided into the two major islands of Miyako and Yaeyama.

Miyako Island has a circumference of about eleven ri. It is divided into the three magiri of Sunakawa, Shimoji, and Hirara. There are over 5,630 households with a population of over 26,690. It is about ninety-four to ninety-five ri from Naha.

Nishizato Village in Sunakawa magiri is where the Miyako Island government office is located, and a medical clinic is also established there.

The main island has a triangular topography, with its northwestern end protruding into the sea.
