In Sobe Village, there is a cave. Its width is about ten ken, and inside there is a spring. It is about two ken square and five to six jo deep. This is called the "dark river of Sobe." It serves as drinking water for more than 260 households in this village.

The products are sugar, wheat, soybeans, sweet potatoes, firewood and charcoal.

Kunigami

Kunigami is located in the northern part of the main island. To the south it borders Nakagami, and on three sides—east, north, and west—it faces the sea. There is no place that is not mountainous. There are only small areas of flat land along the coast. This region is divided into nine magiri: Onna, Nago, Motobu, Nakijin, Haneji, Ogimi, Kunigami, Kushi, and Kin.

Onna magiri has twelve villages. It is a narrow, elongated area along the coast, positioned to the west of Kin magiri, separated by mountain ridges to the east. The northwest faces the sea on two sides, and to the south it meets Yomitan magiri. It is about eleven ri and twenty chō from Naha.

Mount Onna is on the eastern border. Its height nearly rivals Mount Katsuu. Its range extends and runs north, connecting to the various mountains of Kushi and Henoko.

The coastline has protruding capes and inlets, with Cape Onna jutting out in the center. Although there are ports at Hiru, Onna, and Afuso, they are all shallow and inconvenient for anchoring ships.
