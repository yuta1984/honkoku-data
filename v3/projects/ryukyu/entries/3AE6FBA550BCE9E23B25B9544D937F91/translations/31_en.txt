Iwo Torishima is located about eighty ri north of Naha. Its circumference is about one ri. It is a volcanic island that produces sulfur. Therefore, many islanders make their living by mining sulfur. There are over seventy households with a population of over 550.

The Kerama Islands are also called Mount Bashizan. This is an island group beginning seven ri west of Naha, including the islands of Tokashiki, Zamami, Aka, Yakabi, Geruma, Amuro, Gishibu, Fukaji, and Keruma. These are divided into front and rear sections, forming the two magiri of Tokashiki and Zamami. There are over 390 households with a population of over 1,670.

Tokashiki Island is the largest, with a circumference of about three ri. The others range from over one ri for the larger ones to just a few ch≈ç for the smaller ones. Each island has mountains and forests everywhere, with luxuriant tree growth. The soil is barren and rocky, unsuitable for cultivation.

On Zamami Island there is Ago Bay, a natural good harbor that can accommodate several large vessels. Therefore, when storms arise, ships from Naha Port all take shelter in this bay.

The products are fish and shellfish, sea pine, deer, firewood, etc.
