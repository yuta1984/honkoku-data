The climate, being close to the tropical regions, is warm throughout the four seasons, with the scorching heat of midsummer being particularly severe. The Fahrenheit thermometer rises and falls between fifty and ninety-six to ninety-seven degrees. Even in the depths of winter, frost and snow are not seen. Vegetation remains constantly green and lush.

The products include kasuri (splashed pattern fabric), banana fiber cloth, pongee silk, fine ramie cloth, sugar, sweet potatoes, distilled spirits, rush mats, indigo, cycad, ebony, lacquerware, tortoiseshell, and pork being the most renowned, along with grains, tobacco, pottery, cattle and horses, fish and shellfish, fruits, and so forth.

　　　　Main Island

The main island has a circumference of approximately one hundred and ten ri. It extends diagonally from northeast to southwest, stretching about forty ri in length. Its width varies, sometimes one or two ri, sometimes eight or nine ri, not uniform. The topography resembles a dragon floating on the sea. It is broadly divided into three regions: Kunigami, Nakagami, and Shimajiri. Kunigami is also called Hokuzan (Northern Mountain), Nakagami is called Chuzan (Central Mountain), and Shimajiri is called Nanzan (Southern Mountain). It is further subdivided into the two towns of Shuri and Naha, and thirty-five magiri (administrative districts). The northeastern tip is called Cape Hedo, and the southwestern tip is called Cape Kyan. The eastern protrusions are called the two capes of Katsuren and Chinen, and the western protrusion is called Zanpa Cape.
