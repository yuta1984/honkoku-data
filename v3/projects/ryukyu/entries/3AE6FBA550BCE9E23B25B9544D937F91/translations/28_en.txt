In Ōzato village there are castle ruins called Nanzan Castle. This was the place where the Nanzan King once held territory in ancient times. The broken castle walls still remain today.

In Yoza village there is a spring called Yoza River. It is the finest clear spring in the Shimajiri region.

The products are the same as those of Kochinda magiri.

Gushikami magiri has seven villages. To the east it connects to the two magiri of Ōzato and Tamagusuku, to the northwest it diagonally adjoins the three magiri of Mabuni, Makabe, and Kochinda, and the entire southern side faces the sea. It is about five ri and eighteen chō from Naha.

The products are rice, miscellaneous grains, sugar, sweet potatoes, marine fish, etc.

Mabuni magiri has six villages. To the southeast it connects to Gushikami magiri and faces the sea, while to the northwest it adjoins at right angles with the two magiri of Kyan and Makabe. The terrain is small and narrow, and the soil is poor and thin. It is about five ri and eight chō from Naha.

The products are the same as those of Kochinda magiri.
