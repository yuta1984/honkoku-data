Kyan magiri has five villages. To the northeast it connects to the two magiri of Mabuni and Makabe, while to the southwest it is surrounded by seawater, with Cape Kyan protruding into the sea. This is indeed the southern tip of the main island. It is about six ri and five chō from Naha.

The products are miscellaneous grains, sugar, sweet potatoes, etc.

Kanegusuku magiri has nine villages. To the east it adjoins Kochinda magiri, to the west it faces the sea, to the south it neighbors Takamine magiri, and to the north it borders Tomigusuku magiri. It is about three ri and nine chō from Naha.

Itoman village is located on the coast south of the Hōtoku River, with over nine hundred households in a narrow area. Many villagers make their living through fishing. Therefore, the abundance of fishing makes this place the foremost in the country for this activity.

The products are rice, wheat, beans, sugar, sweet potatoes, fish and shellfish, etc.

　　　　Subordinate Islands

Ie Island is located in the sea about two ri northwest of Motobu magiri. Its circumference is about four ri and more. In the center stands a single stone mountain called Ie Castle, which resembles the form of a tray garden stone. The terrain is high and flat, and since there are no rivers flowing throughout the entire island, there are no rice paddies. However, the soil is fertile
