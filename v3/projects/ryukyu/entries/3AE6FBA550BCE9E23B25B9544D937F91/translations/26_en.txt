The Kokuba River has its source in Miyagi village, and the Asato River originates from Ben-no-take (Mount Ben), both flowing west into Mawashi magiri.

The products are miscellaneous grains, sugar, sweet potatoes, cotton, etc.

Ōzato magiri has twenty villages. To the southwest it connects to the four magiri of Sashiki, Tamagusuku, Gushikami, and Kochinda; to the north it borders Haebaru magiri; to the east it faces the bay; and the northeast corner adjoins Nishihara magiri in Nakagami. It is about two ri and nineteen chō from Naha.

Although Yonabaru Port can accommodate ships of five to six hundred koku capacity, because there are many reefs in the harbor and the bottom is shallow, when wind and waves arise, ships cast off their mooring lines and take shelter in Tsuha-ko Bay in Sashiki magiri.

The products are rice, miscellaneous grains, sugar, sweet potatoes, cotton, etc.

Sashiki magiri has nine villages. To the northwest it adjoins Ōzato magiri, and to the southeast it connects to the two magiri of Chinen and Tamagusuku. Seawater penetrates into the center of the magiri. There are salt fields along the bay shore, and the topography forms a ring shape.

The old castle is in Sashiki village, also called Ue-gusuku. Various trees grow densely there. This is the castle site from when King Shō Hashi, who destroyed the three kingdoms and unified the main island, was the Sashiki aji. It is about three ri and twenty-eight chō from Naha.

The products are rice, wheat, sugar, sweet potatoes, tobacco, salt, etc.
