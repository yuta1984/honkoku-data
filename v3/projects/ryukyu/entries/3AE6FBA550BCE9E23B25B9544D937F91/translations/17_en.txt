In the mountains of Afuso Village, there is a waterfall called In Falls. Its height does not exceed one jō, and its width is one jō and five shaku. Its water flows and becomes the Maebukuro River.

The products are rice, wheat, sweet potatoes, firewood, sea fish, and so forth.

Nago magiri has thirteen villages. To the south, east, and north, it is separated by mountain ridges from five magiri: Onna, Kin, Haneji, Nakijin, and Motobu, and to the west it faces the sea. The terrain is bow-shaped, naturally forming a harbor. The soil is rather fertile with many rice paddies. It is about seventeen ri and twenty-four chō from Naha.

Mount Nago is to the southeast. It faces Mount Taniyo in Haneji magiri. To its south is Mount Ishi.

Koheisoko in Kyoda Village is a good port convenient for anchoring ships.

In Sukuta Village there is a waterfall called Todorobaru Falls. It is three jō high and five to six shaku wide. Deep forests grow luxuriantly on all four sides, and one feels cold even in summer.

Although there are several streams, they are all small and narrow, with only the Yabu River being somewhat large. It originates from the mountains in the north, flows south, reaches Yabu Village, and enters the sea.
