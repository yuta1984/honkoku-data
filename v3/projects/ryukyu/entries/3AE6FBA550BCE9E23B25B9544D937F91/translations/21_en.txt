Miyagi Island is a small island with a circumference of about twenty chō. The various rivers of Nerumei, Nōha, and Neshyamei originate in the southeastern mountains and all flow westward into the sea.

The products are sweet potatoes, grains, firewood, etc.

Kunigami magiri has sixteen villages. To the south, it borders intricately with the two magiri of Ōgimi and Kushi through mountain forests, and is surrounded by the sea on three sides - east, north, and west. It is a peninsula protruding from the northeastern corner of the main island and is the largest magiri. Everywhere there are layered peaks and ridges with scarce arable land. There are only a few rice paddies along the seashore. It is about twenty-five ri and thirty-four chō from Naha.

There are several steep slopes within the magiri: Yona-no-takasaka, Henoki-saka, Zanaka-saka, Bumi-saka, and Ōkawa-saka. Among these, Zanaka-saka is particularly famous for being precipitous.

Mount Hedo and Mount Nishime stand side by side in the northernmost land, their ridges extending down to form a cape point - this is Cape Hedo. To its west lies a treacherous place called Kayauchi-bantan. Cliffs rise precipitously to a height of several dozen fathoms, with constantly fierce sea winds. Therefore, when bundles of thatch grass are thrown down from the summit, they inevitably scatter apart midway. This is the origin of the name.
