The Tengan River originates from Yamashiro village in Misato Magiri, flows eastward in meandering curves, reaches Uken village and empties into the sea. Its length is approximately two ri and twenty-seven chō. Its width ranges from about four to five ken to seven to eight ken. It is said to be the largest river on the main island.

Local products include sugar, grains, and others.

Misato Magiri has nineteen villages. To the southwest it borders Goeku and Nakagusuku, the two magiri; to the east it intersects with Gushikawa Magiri; to the north it connects to Kin and Onna, the two magiri. Its southeastern corner faces the sea, controls Awase Island, and faces distant Tsuken Island. It is approximately six ri and twenty-five chō distant from Naha.

Awase Island has a very flourishing salt-making industry.

Local products include sugar, grains, salt, charcoal, and others.

Goeku Magiri has ten villages. To the south it borders Nakagusuku Magiri, to the west it adjoins Chatan Magiri, and in the northeast directions it connects to Misato Magiri. It is approximately six ri and twenty-two chō distant from Naha. There are no particular geographical features worth describing.

Local products include sugar, grains, sweet potatoes, mandarin oranges, bayberries, charcoal, and others.
