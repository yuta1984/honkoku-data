All islanders engage in agriculture as their occupation. However, because hills and mountains overlap throughout the island with little flat land, there are few products.

Aguni Island is located about seven to eight ri northwest of Tonaki Island. Its circumference is about two ri. There are over 660 households with a population of over 4,080.

The island has no forests, and the terrain is flat. The threat of typhoons is particularly severe. Therefore, it is said that house construction is extremely low-built.

Products include silk floss, mulberry juice extract, and miscellaneous grains.

Kume Island is located about forty-eight ri west of Naha. Its circumference is six ri and twenty chō. It is divided into the two magiri of Nakazato and Gushikawa. There are over 1,210 households with a population of over 4,860.

Masha Village in Nakazato magiri is where the Kume Island government office is located. There is also a medical clinic established there. Masha Village has a port of the same name. However, there are many hidden reefs, making it inconvenient for anchoring ships.

Kanegusuku Port is in Gushikawa magiri. It is suitable for mooring vessels.

In the center are various mountains including Uegusuku, Ōtake, and Nakamori. There is also Shimaziri Mountain at the southern tip. Trees grow luxuriantly. However, there is much flat land, and the fertility of the soil is said to be first among the outlying islands.
