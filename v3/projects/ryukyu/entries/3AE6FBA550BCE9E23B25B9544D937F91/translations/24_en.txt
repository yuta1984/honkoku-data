This is divided into fifteen magiri: Mawashi, Oroku, Tomigusuku, Haebaru, Ōzato, Sashiki, Chinen, Tamaki, Kochinda, Takamine, Gushichan, Mabuni, Makabe, Kyan, and Kanegusuku.

Mawashi magiri has twelve villages. To the northeast it connects to Haebaru magiri and to Shuri and Urasoe magiri, and to the southwest it adjoins Tomigusuku magiri and Naha. The topography forms a bow shape, and the soil is most fertile. It is about one ri and four chō from Naha.

In Kohagura village there is a former military camp of the Kumamoto garrison detachment. There is also an agricultural experiment station where Japanese, Chinese, and Western vegetables are cultivated.

The Kokuba River comes from Haebaru magiri, passes through Madama Bridge in Tomigusuku magiri, and enters Naha Port. The Asato River also originates from Haebaru magiri, curves northwest, and flows into Tomari Port.

In Shikina village there is a villa of the Shō family. Within the estate, pine trees and various trees grow luxuriantly. A large pond has been dug in the garden where lotus are planted. A stone bridge spans the center of the pond, and flowering trees and fragrant plants are planted around the perimeter. Clear springs gush forth from rock caves at the pond's edge. The scenery is quite secluded and profound.

The products are rice, wheat, soybeans, sugar, sweet potatoes, tobacco, etc.

Oroku magiri has fifteen villages. To the southeast it connects to Tomigusuku magiri, to the west it faces the sea, and to the north it faces Naha across a bay.
