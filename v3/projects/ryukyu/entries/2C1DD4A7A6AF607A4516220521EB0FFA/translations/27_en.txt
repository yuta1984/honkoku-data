Raizan flower, local name kichika, leaves resemble iron-stemmed begonia, flowers like morning glory but somewhat smaller. Of elegant emerald color, blooming in the fourth and fifth months, bearing fruit by the eleventh month. The fruit is like bean pods or pomegranate chambers, containing several dozen seeds that can be planted.
　　　Raizan flower 《Also called kichika, Japanese name is Ryukyu wood morning glory》

Kikoura, also called kahō, commonly planted on house walls to ward off fire. The stem resembles haōben grass, leaves resemble chinkakausa. Flowers resemble yellow chrysanthemums, but there are also red ones, which are named fukurokuboki.

　　　Kikoura 《Also called kahō, also named fukurokuboki. In Satsuma dialect called □renka》

Among fruits there are lotus root sugarcane, watermelon, cucumber, papaya, mandarin orange 《several varieties》, bitter orange, kumquat, Buddha's hand citron, lychee, longan, grapes, cherry, Chinese bayberry, raspberry 《shaped like Chinese bayberry》, horse chestnut, walnut, loquat, and plum 《small and like longan》. Among exotic products there are banana fruit and others.
