Wild orchid 《This is China's seikyō, Japanese name is kōyaran》

Striped daylily, also called kangutōka, flowers resemble daylily but are particularly small. The leaves have a bluish-white color with alternating patterns.
　　　Striped daylily 《Also called kangutōka, in Musashi dialect called isaha kanzō. According to examination, this is what the "Kyūkō Honzō" calls funjourji》

Mountain rush flower, also called saruyō flower, has neither flowers nor stems, emerges from the soil with length not reaching one shaku. Resembles banana plant but smaller, firm and thick with patterns.
　　　Mountain rush flower 《Also called saruyō flower, Japanese name unknown》
