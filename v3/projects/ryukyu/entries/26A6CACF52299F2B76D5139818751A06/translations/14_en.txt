Map of Ryukyu
Ryukyu was originally named "Liuqiu" (流虬). 《Marginal note: The Chūzan Seikan states: When the Sui Dynasty sent the cavalry commandant Zhu Kuan to the country, amid the myriad waves, they saw that the topography resembled a dragon floating in the water, hence the name.》When it first appears in the Sui Shu, it is written as "Liuqiu" (流求). The Song Shi followed this usage. The Yuan Shi calls it "Liuqiu" (瑠求). During the Hongwu era of the Ming, it was changed to "Liuqiu" (琉球). The country is located 1,700 ri due east of Fuzhou in Fujian, and 3 ri to the south. Its topography is narrow from east to west, with the widest areas being only several tens of ri. The north-south length is 440 ri. From Chūzan Shuri, going south to the seashore of Kiyamu takes a day and a half of hurried travel. Going north to the seashore of Kunigami takes three and a half days of hurried travel. Before the Yongle era of the Ming, the country was divided into three parts: Chūzan, Sannan, and Sanhoku. During the Xuande period, they were unified into one. It was divided into three provinces. Chūzan became Nakagami Province with fourteen affiliated prefectures.
