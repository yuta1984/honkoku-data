On the eighteenth day, a banquet was bestowed upon Iehisa and Chūzan King Shō Nei, and they were made to watch sarugaku performances. Yorinobu (ancestor of Kishū) and Yorifusa (ancestor of Mito) performed dances. During this time there was a drinking banquet, and Tōshō-gū bestowed upon Iehisa a Sadamune sword and short sword. On the nineteenth day, Iehisa and Shō Nei received permission to depart and

were given gifts. On the twenty-fifth day, Iehisa brought Shō Nei with him and arrived in Edo. On the twenty-sixth day, by order of Taitoku-in-dono, acknowledging Iehisa's efforts in bringing the Chūzan King from distant lands, an envoy was sent to Iehisa's residence in Sakurada.
