The islanders have dark skin. ○ Okinoerabu Island lies to the southwest of the above. It is called Okinoerabu. Circumference: ten and a half ri, with three magiri: China, Oshiro, and Tokutoki. Wadomari is a shallow port on the east coast. Among its products is sugar, and it also produces unusual fish. There is the Erabu eel, slender and about one jō long, which is dried and used as medicine (it is a large sea snake). The people and customs resemble those of Okinawa, while the products resemble those of Oshima. ○ Yoron Island lies to the south of the above. Circumference: three ri five chō, with two villages: Bukiya and Akasa. Akasatomari in the southwest has a shallow harbor entrance where large ships cannot enter. It produces sugar, and has abundant banana plants and oak trees. To the southwest, it faces Unten Port in Okinawa.

Osumi Islands - Territory of Kumage and Gomu counties (Appendix follows below)
Tanegashima has a circumference of thirty-seven and a half ri. The island's shape extends north-south, located in the southern sea of Osumi, and belongs to Kumage County. To the west lies Mageshima, a small island belonging to the same county. Yakushima lies to the southwest of Tanegashima and belongs to Gomu County. Circumference: over sixteen ri, with a round island shape. Motomiyama at the southern tip has a height of 635 jō. Kuchinoerabujima lies to the west of the above. It is called Kuchinoerabu and belongs to Gomu County. Circumference: six and a half ri, with an island shape extending east-west. Mountain height: over 200 jō.
