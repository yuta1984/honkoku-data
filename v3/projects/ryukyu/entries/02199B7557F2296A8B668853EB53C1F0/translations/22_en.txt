In the ninth month of Keian 2 (1649), King Shō Shitsu of Chūzan had the gratitude envoy Gushikawa Aji and others present tribute goods. 《Marginal note: Ryūkyū Jiryaku》 They also paid respects at the shrine of Mount Nikko.

On the twentieth day of the ninth month of Jōō 2 (1653), King Shō Shitsu of Chūzan had the congratulatory envoy Kunigami Aji and others present tribute goods. 《Marginal note: Razan Bunshū, Wakan Gōun, Kinsei Buke Hennen Ryaku》 They also paid respects at the shrine of Mount Nikko.

On the twenty-eighth day of the seventh month of Kanbun 11 (1671), King Shō Tei of Chūzan had the gratitude envoy Prince Kin and others present tribute goods. 《Marginal note: Manten Nikki》 They also paid respects at the shrine of Mount Nikko. 《Marginal note: Ryūkyū Jiryaku, Rekidai Bikō》

On the eleventh day of the fourth month of Tenna 2 (1682), King Shō Tei of Chūzan had the congratulatory envoy Nago Aji, Onna Oyakata and others present tribute goods. 《Marginal note: Manten Nikki, Kanro Sō》

On the eighteenth day of the eleventh month of Hōei 7 (1710), King Shō Eki of Chūzan had the congratulatory envoy Prince Misato, Tomimori Oyakata, gratitude envoy Prince Tomigusuku, Yoza Oyakata and others present tribute goods. 《Marginal note: Ryūkyū Heinshi Kiji》 They also paid respects at the shrine of Mount Tōei. The practice of Chūzan envoys coming to Mount Tōei without going to Mount Nikko began at this time.
