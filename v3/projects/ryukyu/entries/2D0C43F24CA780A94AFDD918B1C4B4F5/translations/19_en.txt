They could not resist and, being overwhelmed with fear, all came out to surrender, leading to the castle's fall. Meanwhile, at Naha castle, they waited with arrows ready, but when not a single enemy ship appeared, they wondered what was happening. Suddenly attacked from behind and hearing that the royal castle had already fallen, they surrendered without even a single battle. Thus, they quickly achieved military victory and Ryukyu was immediately pacified. When this was reported to the Shogun's house by fast ship, he praised it greatly. Since that year they had missed the season of favorable winds, the various forces remained in Ryukyu, and in the fifth month, twenty-eighth day of the following year, they returned in triumph bringing along Chūzan King Shō Nei. Likewise in the eighth month, the lord of Satsuma came to Sunpu with the Chūzan King and entered the castle. At that time, the Chūzan King presented one hundred rolls of silk damask, twelve fathoms of orangutan hide, two hundred bolts of Taihei cloth, ten thousand ryō of silver, and one sword. Since a foreign country had come under control at the beginning of his reign, the Shogun was extraordinarily pleased, and as a reward, he granted a sword and the kingdom of Ryukyu. The Chūzan King also received gifts. From this time, Ryukyu became a long-standing vassal of Satsuma.
