The route for both envoys' ascent to the castle: This morning they proceeded to Lord Matsudaira Satsuma-no-kami's Kōbashi residence, then via the side of Lord Matsudaira Tango-no-kami's residence, passing by the side of Lord Sakai Iwami-no-kami's residence, through between Lord Soma Sanuki-no-kami's and Lord Abe Minbu's residences, passing through Kasumigaseki slope, from the back gate of Lord Ii Kamon-no-kami through the outer moat area via Sakurada Gate, from in front of Lord Akimoto Tajima-no-kami's and Lord Manabe Echizen-no-kami's residences via Wadakura Gate, through the back street of Lord Inoue Kawachi-no-kami's residence rest area, and entered the castle through Ōte Gate. The retainers and accompanying clan members in procession followed the route designated by Satsuma-no-kami, with two groups of御徒 (castle guards) providing security, and guards from the various residences along the route came out to provide security in front of their residences.

1. The chief envoy rode in a palanquin while his retainers were on horseback. They dismounted beyond Ōte Bridge and followed the messenger. The chief envoy dismounted from his palanquin in front of Gejō Bridge. However, the flag bearers, spear bearers, and other officials all remained at the Ōte rest area.
