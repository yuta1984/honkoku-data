【Left page main text】
Because they flattered the Ming dynasty and did not send tribute to Japan, the lord of Satsuma requested permission to punish them. Several thousand troops and horses were sent, and finally King Shō Nei was taken captive and brought to Satsuma as a hostage for three years. He repented his former errors and vowed to be a vassal for all eternity thereafter. Therefore he was pardoned and allowed to return. This was in the 17th year of Keichō under Emperor Go-Mizunoo, the 40th year of Wanli of the Great Ming.
