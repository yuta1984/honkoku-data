Lord Chōkichi presented a sword (Miiike, worth six gold pieces), which Lord Shōgen brought out and presented. After this was completed, Sukenoshin was summoned.

Lord Masaaki bestowed a sake cup and presented a sword (Naoe Shizu, worth three gold pieces). The sake cup was brought before his presence and the matter was concluded. He appeared in the front study and dined together with the guests, with a meal of three soups and eight dishes served. Musical entertainment and dance performances were ordered, and there was a sake cup ceremony with the guests. After this concluded, the chief retainer was summoned and given a sake cup, which was then brought before his presence and the ceremony ended. In the middle study, those who had accompanied Lord Chōkichi were granted an audience and given lengthy gifts, and the matter was concluded.

Lord Chōkichi entered from the main entrance to the front quarters (as temporary residence in the corner tea house). His attendant Hababe Koemon Wa[?] was summoned and given a sword (worth three gold pieces), and the other attendants were given lengthy gifts.

One: On June 5th, to announce the completion of the moving, Lord Chōkichi accompanied [the lord] on courtesy visits to the Senior Councilors, chamberlains, and Junior Councilors.
However, to the Senior Councilors and Lord Makino Bingo-no-kami Narisada, Lord Chōkichi's ceremonial sword and gold horse substitute were brought as gifts.
