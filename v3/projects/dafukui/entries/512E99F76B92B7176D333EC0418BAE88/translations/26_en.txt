One: May 3rd - For congratulations on the Boys' Festival, through messengers, the customary summer kimono and
　　unlined garments were presented, and white silver was offered up.
One: 4th of the same month - Departed Edo, traveling via the Tōkaidō route
One: 16th of the same month - Arrived at the castle

One: 18th of the same month - For the same reason as above, messenger Takaoka Sugita
　　Danjō San[?] was sent to Edo for expressions of gratitude. Departed Fukui today.
One: 26th of the same month - Due to the illness of Sakai Gemba Moto[?], Kyoto physician Arima
　　Ryōkyū was summoned, and because it was desired to request Gemba's treatment, messenger
　　Minagawa Yoichiemon [?] was dispatched by express courier to Kyoto. Departed Fukui today.
　　　　However, Ryōkyū arrived in Fukui on June 9th.
One: Same month (day not specified) - Through a letter, the customary first mackerel of the province was presented as usual.
One: June 1st - For gratitude regarding the return to the domain, through messenger Sugita Danjō [?],
　　the customary formal paper, kelp, dried sea bream, and sake barrels were presented.
