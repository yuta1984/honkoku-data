One: July 29th - Through a letter, the first cod of the local products was presented as usual
One: August 1st - For celebratory purposes, a ceremonial sword and horse substitute were
　　presented through a messenger as usual
One: 9th of the same month - For the monthly inquiry into His Lordship's well-being, through messenger Isobe Kinzaemon,
　　one type of ceremonial fish was presented. Kinzaemon departed from Fukui today
One: 17th of the same month - Regarding field inspection attendants, the ōban samurai who had served in the eighth shift until now
　　were ordered to serve in the twelfth shift going forward, and child attendants were to serve in the tenth shift
One: 19th of the same month - Regarding economy measures for the kitchen quarters, orders were issued through written instructions
　　　　However, the details of the aforementioned written orders are unclear in the records

One: 25th of the same month - Due to economy measures, the number of horses was reduced, and it was ordered that
　　henceforth both in Edo and the domain, the total should be set at fifty horses
　　　　However, later by His Lordship's will, it was set at sixty horses
One: 11th of the intercalary eighth month - Regarding the main castle and gate guard duties, the following was
　　ordered:
　　　　One: The senior ashigaru commanders who had been serving guard duty at Taiko Gate were relieved,
　　　　　　and were to join and serve at both Geba Gate and Kitte Gate guard posts
　　　　　　　However, Geba Gate became the eighth shift and Kitte Gate the sixth shift
　　　　One: The front guard samurai were to cease guard duty at the main castle and guard only the lord's quarters
