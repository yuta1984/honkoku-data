A letter was written to the castle deputy.

One: July 4th
Accompanied by Lord Chōkichi, he had an audience with the senior councilors and performed his duties.

One: On the 6th of the same month, for the celebration of the birth viewing jewel ceremony, gold was presented through an envoy as customary.

One: On the 28th of the same month, the first cod from the domain's local products was presented through an envoy as customary.

One: August 1st, for congratulations, he went to Edo Castle and presented a sword and horse substitute as customary.

One: On the 7th of the same month
When he went to Edo Castle accompanied by Lord Chōkichi (yesterday on the 6th, a written order came from Lord Tsuchiya Sagami-no-kami, who was on duty, instructing that he should go to Edo Castle accompanied by Lord Chōkichi), it was announced that as congratulations for the completion of the enshrinement at Mount Nikko, there would be Noh performances on the coming 12th and he should attend to view them.
Upon departure, as thanks, he made courtesy visits to the senior councilors and close attendants.

One: On the 11th of the same month, regarding a Christian-related family member named Okeya Taemon who had been residing in Misurao Village, Sakai District, in the deputy's territory and died of illness last year, Taemon's wife and five daughters together wished to move to the home of Taemon's elder brother, lacquerer Hachibe, in Jinguji-machi, and this matter was reported to Deputy Tsuzuki Chōzaemon.
