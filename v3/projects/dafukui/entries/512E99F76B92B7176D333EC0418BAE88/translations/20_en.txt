One: All miscellaneous contributions (hōka) must not be accepted. Buddhist ceremonies and other religious events should also be conducted modestly.

The above matters have been previously decreed, but since this time simplification has been reported to the shogunal government, within the domain as well, all affairs should be conducted even more modestly. Those who harbor subordinates who naturally violate these rules may receive specific decrees, so these regulations must be strictly observed.
September [day]

One: October - Regarding the death by illness of the daughter of Kashiwaya Kihei, a relative of Christians, a report was made to the Christian magistrates.

One: November 3rd
The biological mother of Chōkitsu-sama, Shōjuin-dono, passed away.

One: Same month, 7th day - Due to the first snow, as an inquiry into [the lord's] well-being, a messenger to the castle, the castle keeper Kaga Tōzaemon, was dispatched.

One: December 10th - Through the envoy Ōshima Un'shirō-dono, a hawk's mist-net was received as a gift. For this honor, [the lord] went to the castle, and then made rounds to the senior councilors.

One: Same month, 11th day - Regarding the presentation of Chrysanthemum Festival congratulations, an inner letter was delivered at the castle, and retainers received gifts as usual.
