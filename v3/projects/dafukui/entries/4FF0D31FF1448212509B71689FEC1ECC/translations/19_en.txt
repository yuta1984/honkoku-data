The necessary funds were supposed to be procured through financial arrangements in Kyoto, but due to discrepancies in the arrangements, financial shortfalls were inevitable. Therefore, additional service funds were once again requested from the towns and villages as follows. Although it was recognized that requesting additional funds after having already collected substantial service money would cause hardship for the common people, since disruption to the construction project would be a serious matter, this unavoidable request was made. Yozaemon conveyed instructions to the town magistrates and district magistrates through the following written directive, stating that this matter should be communicated thoroughly to all levels and that everyone should apply themselves diligently.
