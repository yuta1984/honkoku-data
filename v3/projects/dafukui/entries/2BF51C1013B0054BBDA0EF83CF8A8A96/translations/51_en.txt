1. The official positions exempt from construction silver (taxes) are as follows:
   - Castle Deputy, Substitute Guard Captains
   - Town Magistrates, Magistrates
   - New Guard Captains, Inspectors
   - District Magistrates, Construction Magistrates
   - Finance Magistrates, Document Office Magistrates
   - Treasury Officials, Secretaries
   - Road Magistrates, Library Magistrates
   - Horse Officials, Falconry Officials
   - Boat Bridge Officials, Gold Magistrates
   - Master Officials, Instructors
   - Court Physicians

1. Similarly, the official positions with the same exemptions are as follows:
   - Senior Councillors, Middle-rank Elders
   - Record-keeping Captains, Attendants
   - Great Guard Captains, Personal Guard Leaders
   - Navy Captains, Messenger Guards
   - Meal Service Guards, Sword Magistrates

For these official positions, construction silver exemptions are maintained upon return to Edo. When there are repeated position changes, regardless of how many years since retirement, calculations should be made at 7 rin 5 mō per position, with two years each allocated for current and retired positions.

Ordered in Meiwa 4 (1767):
1. When construction and repair work is ordered and inspectors are assigned to oversee duties, they shall not wear formal hakama but shall wear white robes and haori jackets.
   Note: The same applies to foot soldier inspectors. One inspector and two foot soldier inspectors each, the same for both Edo and the domain.

1. When making the same type of inspection rounds, they may wear their regular clothing.
   Note: The same applies to foot soldier inspectors.

1. When inspecting construction and repair sites, they should examine for fire hazards and other suspicious matters, verify appropriateness or inappropriateness, and investigate to ensure there are no fights or disputes.
   - From Meiwa 4 to spring of Meiwa 5 (1768), Ichiyanagi Shinkurō served as duty overseer.
   - In the 6th year, Tateiwa Heiemon served.
   - In Anei 9 (1780), in preparation for Princess Hisa's birth, from the 11th month of the previous year, Akita Shichibei was appointed as overseer for the reconstruction of the women's quarters enclosure, and two foot soldier inspectors were assigned to patrol that area. All were to appear in white robes and haori jackets.
