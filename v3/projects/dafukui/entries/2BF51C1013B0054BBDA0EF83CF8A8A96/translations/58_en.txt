1. In the 9th month of Kyōwa 2, year of the dog (1802), minor official Mizuno Kyūdayū of Esashi-chō petitioned for and was granted an exchange of his residence for tea garden land in Tawara-chō. However, since the Tawara-chō residence had a larger ground area, the excess over the area of the Esashi-chō residence was to be treated as received land, and on the 5th day of the 10th month, the following was decreed at the Council Chamber:

　Regarding the petition by record-keeper Mizuno Kyūdayū for exchange of his granted residential land, it is granted as petitioned, and hereafter the land rent代 shall be faithfully performed as shown in the separate document. The senior retainers conveyed that this should be communicated to Kyūdayū.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Saitō Ukon

　Separate document:
　1. 117 bu　　　　　　　Tahara-chō tea garden land
　　Of which 101 bu 2 fun 5 rin　　　Granted in exchange for Esashi-chō granted residence
　　Remainder
　　　11 bu 7 fun 5 rin　　　Remaining portion of tea garden

　Tahara-chō land rent
　　　　This rice: 1 to 7 shō 1 gō 2 shaku　Kyō-masu

　1. 101 bu 2 fun 5 rin　　　　Esashi-chō granted residence exchanged for tea garden land
　　　Of which 2 fun 5 rin　　　Granted as custodial land

　Esashi-chō land rent
　　　　This rice: 2 to 8 shō 4 gō 8 shaku
　　　　　　However, to be collected and paid annually by Kyūdayū

　The above land rent rice, two accounts totaling 4 to 5 shō 6 gō, to be paid annually by Kyūdayū

　The above to be communicated to Mizuno Kyūdayū
