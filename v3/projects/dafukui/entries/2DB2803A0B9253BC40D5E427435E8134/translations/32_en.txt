They dislike showing [their sleeping posture] to people.

1. Most utensils are made of tin and similar materials. Rice containers are also made of tin. Ladles are made from something like hollowed sword bean pods.

1. The staple foods are taro and sweet potatoes. Among these, wheat is extremely precious. Rice is shipped from various countries but is not considered high-quality. Among fish, small sea bream and other migratory fish are no different from Japan. There are many reef fish that do not exist in Japan.

1. Both Hawaii and America have no miso or soy sauce. They season food with salt.

1. People of high class do not drink alcohol. Even if they do drink, it is only slightly for medicinal purposes. Lower-class people drink like Japanese people. They detest and despise drunk people, considering them very base.

1. America is currently a developing new country, and its scholarship is said to become more precise year by year.

1. American politics are generally the same as Japan's, with twelve articles of law, and there are no troublesome matters.

1. The American king's residence is built as a mansion on flat land. The castle above resembles ramparts stacked like mirror rice cakes, with stone walls extending slightly in all directions with entrances, and cannons are installed throughout as fortifications. According to one theory, a large mirror is placed above the king's house, and everything within a hundred ri is reflected in that mirror and can be seen as if in the palm of one's hand.

1. There are many among the daimyo class who have built large castles.

1. Officials and others never display authority while traveling. Social class distinctions are made by the color of clothing. Even farmers can be promoted and employed based on their scholarship. Those with good scholarship are permitted to wear high-class clothing even if they are not officials.

1. The people are sincere and benevolent, doing no evil, with no murderers or bandits... (continues)
