Examination and Deliberation
1. In Kyōhō 9 (Year of the Dragon), when accounting for the duties of miscellaneous affairs officer Nozawa Kyūdayū and assistant Satō Ichizaemon, previously foot inspectors attended, so the magistrates requested submission. However, if deliberation is assigned to inspectors, then submit. This time only confiscation of household goods was ordered, so foot inspectors do not attend the accounting session. Later, inspectors will not be involved during release either.
1. Samue Kyūzaemon
1. In Genbun 2 (Year of the Snake), there was a shortage in gold magistrate Naruse Heizaemon's promissory note circulation, and a written statement was submitted. Magistrate Senmoto Tōzaemon and inspector Nakamura Hachidayū were ordered to examine Heizaemon's case and record the statement. Tōzaemon conveyed the matter and had a seal affixed under Heizaemon's name. The aforementioned statement concerns the seal matter during Samue Kyūzaemon's statement recording.
1. Rice magistrate deputy Kataoka Shichirōemon and Yumoto Bunzaemon were ordered to deliberate with magistrates present, and inspector Ōtani Gizaemon was in charge. They were confined to the accounting office dormitory with guards assigned from the long-spear bearers, and meals were arranged through the mountain teahouse. During deliberations at the accounting office, one foot inspector was assigned. Two members each from the magistrate group and inspector group were sent to both men's residences, various items were sealed, wives and children were left as they were, and sealed items were entrusted to Kabē.
