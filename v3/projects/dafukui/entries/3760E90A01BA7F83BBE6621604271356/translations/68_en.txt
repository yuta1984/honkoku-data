1. Matters of discretionary confinement inquiries for petty officials and below appear in the section on punishments for petty officials and below.

1. Matters of teachers' discretionary confinement inquiries due to their disciples' punishments appear in the supervision section. On June 5th, Kyōhō 12th year, when Shimada Kuhachirō's disciple Yosuke was banished from the domain, Kuhachirō inquired about discretionary confinement but was told it was unnecessary.

1. On December 25th, Kyōhō 12th year, over ten rooms of Honda Ōkura's construction barracks in the capital caught fire on the night of the 22nd and burned down. However, the main building surroundings and other areas were unaffected. Nevertheless, since it was within the compound, they inquired with the senior councilors whether discretionary confinement was necessary, but were told it was unnecessary, which Uemon reported to Gizaemon.

1. On February 29th, Kyōhō 15th year, smoke appeared above the kitchen of Tashiro Mansada's residence and was quickly extinguished, but the firefighting personnel did not respond. However, regarding Mansada's discretionary confinement inquiry, Okabe Genpaku went to the council chamber to inquire, and the senior councilors said that since it was a minor matter that did not require firefighter response, discretionary confinement was unnecessary.

1. On June 25th, Kyōhō 17th year, regarding a discretionary confinement inquiry due to misconduct by someone in Construction Magistrate Suzuki Genbee's unit, Katō Shozaemon reported at the council chamber that it was unnecessary. This also appears in the council records.

[Lower section]
1. On February 5th, Bunsei 3rd year, a person named Tsuchida Ichirōbee from Atobe Kazue's unit committed misconduct.
After reporting this to the senior councilors, monthly duty officer Suzuki Jūzaemon summoned Kazue to his residence and informed him through speech that Ichirōbee had committed misconduct and was ordered to be replaced, and that the aftermath would be handled under his personal custody, so he should handle it appropriately. However, when Kazue submitted a discretionary confinement inquiry, he was told it was unnecessary.
　　However, it was established that henceforth, for matters ordered through inspector transfers without official documents, inquiries need not be submitted.
