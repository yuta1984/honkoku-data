House Arrest and Inquiries Regarding House Arrest

1. When ordering the cessation of house arrest, whether ordered from above or inquired from below, it is the same matter. At this time, gates should not be barred, and it does not apply to people passing through.延享 4th year, final month.
   However, according to ancient custom, gates were half-closed for house arrest, but on the 15th day of the 6th month of 寛政 4th year (Year of the Rat), during the family succession ceremony, Chamberlain Inaba Oribe made mistakes in handling the ceremony seating. After inquiry, house arrest was ordered. From this time forward, unlike before, gates should not be barred for house arrest, and constant closure is not necessary. Hereafter this was reformed and ordered, as communicated by Koma Shikibu.

1. Regarding house arrest upon inquiry of magistrate's restraint in cases of prison breaks, this appears in the section on imprisonment.

1. Regarding house arrest upon inquiry of restraint due to forgetting congratulatory letters, this appears in the section on felicitations.

1. In the 9th month of 寛政 8th year (Year of the Dragon), regarding the handling of men under Senior Ashigaru Commander Aizawa Hachirou-emon, upon inquiry of restraint, house arrest was ordered. This appears in the section on punishment of group members.

1. On the 7th day of the 5th month of 寛保 2nd year (Year of the Dog), Hahabekabe Hachizaemon reported: "Today during the temple visit, the sandal-bearer was absent from the entourage. The palanquin attendants should have noticed this, but they failed to do so - this was extremely careless.
   However, regarding the return to the castle, light treatment was given, and the matter of communication through the senior councilors was stopped. Through Hahabekabe Hachizaemon, it was ordered that palanquin attendants on duty Matsuda Zen-emon and Takida Yazaemon be placed under house arrest. This was communicated to Akashi Shogen. Since this is house arrest, there should be no need to request permission for passage, and this was also discussed with Shogen, who was told to understand accordingly." The next day, both men were pardoned from house arrest, as Hachizaemon reported.
   It is thought that this was the beginning of house arrest.

1. On the 19th day of the 8th month of the same year, regarding Inspector Suzuki Juzaemon's group leader's handling of the lookout group members, there was misconduct, and confinement with scolding was ordered. When inquired whether Juzaemon should also be placed under house arrest for similar misconduct, it was deemed unnecessary.

1. On the 24th day of the 10th month of the same year, Magistrate Kato Buemon reported: "Regarding Mimura Shinhachi, there was consideration for succession to his father Matasaku's position, and he was appointed to minor official status. Upon inquiry of restraint, it was ordered that he be placed under house arrest," and this was formally reported.

1. On the 27th day of the same month, District Magistrate Ajioka Jinzaemon failed to properly handle matters regarding exiled persons under his jurisdiction, and therefore was ordered to remain under house arrest.
