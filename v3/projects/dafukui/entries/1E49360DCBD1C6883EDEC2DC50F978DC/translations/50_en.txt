　　　　　　　　　　Matsudaira Iyo-no-kami-dono
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　To all concerned
　1. June (day unclear)
　　　　In connection with the journey to Kyoto by the Shogun and Retired Shogun,
　　　　Lord Tadamasa departed from Fukui, traveled through western Ōmi, and went up to Kyoto (lodged at Daiunji Temple at Shijō, a subsidiary temple of Chion-in)
　1. August 19th: In Kyoto, he was appointed to the court rank of Shōshi-i-ge (Senior Fourth Rank Lower Grade) and the position of Sangi (Counselor) (at age thirty)

【Left page】
　　　　　　　　However, this was a promotion from Jushi-i-ge Jijū (Junior Fourth Rank Lower Grade, Chamberlain)
　1. Same day: Dewa-no-kami Naomasa-sama was appointed Chamberlain, and Yamato-no-kami Naomoto-sama was granted the rank of Junior Fourth Rank Lower Grade.
　　　　(Until now they had been called the Yūki group, but from this time they came to be called Matsudaira) Chōkōmaru Naoyoshi-sama was appointed to Junior Fourth Rank Lower Grade and Tosa-no-kami
　1. September (day unclear): In connection with the upcoming imperial visit, orders were issued
　　　　to provide saddled horses, so five horses were provided
　1. 6th of the same month
　　　　His Majesty made an imperial visit to Nijō Castle. At this time,
　　　　[Lord Tadamasa] attended in the entourage of the Shogun (he received court dress and wore
　　　　a court robe with clove and arabesque patterns, accompanied by six retainers in court dress, two horse attendants, two stable boys, one halberd bearer, and one hat bearer)
