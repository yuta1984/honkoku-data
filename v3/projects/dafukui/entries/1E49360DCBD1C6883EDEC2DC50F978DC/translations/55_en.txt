【Right page】
　　　　The captains, standard-bearers, messenger guards, inner and outer guards, and irregular personnel wore half-hakama and
　　　　brought 100 hiki each as sake barrel fees to pay their respects, received sake cups, and were served long abalone through
　　　　the intermediation of the master of ceremonies.
　　1. On the same day, as New Year's congratulations, a sword and horse fee (20 ryō of gold) were presented through a messenger.
　　1. On January 2nd, Buddhist priests gathered in the document chamber to bring swords and formal documents to pay
　　　　their respects.
　　1. On the same evening, there was a celebration for the first Noh chanting of the year, and from Honda Izu-no-kami Tomimasa down to the Kawara Gate guards,
　　　　sake cup stands were presented. Afterwards, those who had changed their names brought swords and formal documents to pay respects for their name changes.
