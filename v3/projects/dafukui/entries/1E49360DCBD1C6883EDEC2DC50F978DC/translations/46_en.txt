【Left page】
　　1. Date: When entering the domain, [Lord Tadamasa] brought along Zuiun, the head priest of Tōkōji Temple, and
　　　　established one temple (at the former site of Oyamada Tamon's residence, now at Kami-no-hashi bridge), relocating
　　　　Fujisan Tōkōji Temple from Takada in Echigo Province, and transferred the sacred bones of
　　　　Lord Ōbaiin and Lord Kōgenin. A stone pagoda for Lord Ōbaiin was constructed, and
　　　　the remains of Lord Kōgenin were also enshrined to the left of this stone pagoda.
　　1. Date: Honda Izunokami Tomimasa went to Edo and expressed gratitude for the increase in domain holdings.
　　　　At that time, he was granted an audience with the Shōgun, and on that occasion,
　　　　Lord Tadamasa's succession was officially approved. As chief retainer, [he was to continue] administration as before...
