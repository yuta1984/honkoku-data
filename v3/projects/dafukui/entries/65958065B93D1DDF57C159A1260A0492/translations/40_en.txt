　　　　　　　　Memorandum of oral instructions
　　　　　Junshi (following one's lord in death) has been admonished since ancient times
　　　　　as unrighteous and useless, but because no official proclamation was issued, in recent years many have committed
　　　　　追腹 (seppuku following a lord's death). Henceforth, for those who may harbor such intentions,
　　　　　their masters must constantly and firmly agree that they should not commit junshi. If
　　　　　anyone does so in the future, it will be considered a transgression due to the deceased lord's lack of foresight, and the heir's

1. May 25th: Through the jōshi (imperial envoy) Senior Councilor Abe Bungo-no-kami Tadaaki, permission to return to the domain was
　　granted, and as customary, 1,000 pieces of silver and 100 silk robes were bestowed. As thanks for this
　　service was performed to Bungo-no-kami, and messengers were sent to the remaining Senior Councilors
1. Same month, 26th (5 o'clock): Attended the castle, expressed gratitude for the leave permission, and received a horse as customary
　　The accompanying karō (chief retainer) was granted an audience, and after completion, departed the castle. Subsequently
　　courtesy visits to the Senior Councilors were conducted
