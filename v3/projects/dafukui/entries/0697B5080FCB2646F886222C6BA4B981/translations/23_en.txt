They proceeded to the formal reception hall, donned formal hakama, and after the master of ceremonies and other officials had assembled,
Noh performances (Oimatsu, Tamura, Higashitokoro, Miwa, Shūgen) were performed followed by formal cuisine (three soups and nine dishes). Ceremonial gifts (hemp cloth) were also presented.
Lord Kaga-no-kami personally escorted [the guest] to that seat, and Lord Nakatsukasa-no-taiyu Masatoki was also
escorted to that seat by Lord Yamashiro-no-kami, who brought additional delicacies (grilled salmon).
Lord Yamashiro-no-kami escorted [the guest] to that seat, and there was a sake ceremony.
After the family branch lords attended and formal meetings concluded, Lord Kaga-no-kami bestowed sake cups upon his retainer Koma
Moku-no-jō Teizumi and other officials down to the caretakers. Lord Yamashiro-no-kami similarly bestowed [sake cups] upon
his retainer Matsudaira Shōbei Masatsune and other officials down to the caretakers.
After the banquet concluded, Lord Kaga-no-kami bestowed sake cups upon Moku-no-jō and others including Taki Kazue-no-kami Akinori,

Minobe Sukesaku Shigefusa, Hagino Gen'emon Masafū, and Arakawa Zen'emon Masatake.
Lord Yamashiro-no-kami similarly bestowed [sake cups] upon Shōbei and others including Takaya Iori Nagafusa, Nagami Jibu'emon Yoshimasa,
and Inaba Hikoshirō. Upon their departure, they were escorted to the same place where they had initially been received, and from there, as expressions of gratitude, [the lord] paid formal visits to Lords Kaga-no-kami,
Yamashiro-no-kami, and Aki-no-kami.

　each. To Junior Councilors Lords Akimoto Tajima-no-kami, Matsudaira Iga-no-kami, and
　Kitami Wakasa-no-kami, one type of delicacy each was sent.
