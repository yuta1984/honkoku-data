Endorsement
　　　　　　　　　　　　Ōkubo Kaga-no-kami
　　　　　　The kudzu (one box) and yam (one basket) that were presented
　　　　　　have been successfully announced. Respectfully and humbly submitted.
　　　　　　　5th month, 4th day　　　　　　Tadatomo (seal)
　　　　　　Note: When there are offerings for inquiries after [the shogun's] well-being and such occasions,
　　　　　　　　such official letters are issued each time, but as the content is similar, they will not be recorded hereafter
1. 5th month, 8th day: For the same Buddhist service mentioned above,
　　the Shogun made a pilgrimage to Tōeizan, so preliminary worship service was performed
　　　　Note: Incense offerings should have been presented for the Buddhist service, but details are not clear in the records
1. Same day: For the same reason mentioned above, a tea ceremony was held at Manpukuzan
1. Same month 《marginal note: day unclear》: For the Boys' Day celebration, summer robes 《marginal note: three》
　　and unlined garments 《marginal note: two》 were presented through messengers and residential representatives. Later, an inner letter was presented at the castle

　　　　Five pieces of summer robes and unlined garments have arrived
　　　　as Boys' Day celebration gifts. [I] am pleased to receive them.
　　　　Details will be explained by Ōkubo Kaga-no-kami.
　　　　　　5th month, 18th day　Black seal
　　　　　　　　　　　　　To Fukui Jijū
