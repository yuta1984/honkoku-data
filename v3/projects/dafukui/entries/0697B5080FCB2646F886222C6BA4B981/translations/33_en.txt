It immediately reached His Lordship's attention, and he was particularly pleased. Since this is a matter of these times, everyone would likely face hardship, so it should not be accepted. However, since the order to destroy the paper money cannot be easily handled by the domain finances alone, there are remaining rice from past harvests and paper money stored in the treasury. Of these, for those with one thousand koku or more, two bu per unit, and for those with nine hundred koku or less, one bu and two rin - following this rate, these amounts shall first be set aside and then the paper money destruction shall be ordered. Those portions shall be distributed promptly.

June 13th: Regarding the same matter, if all paper money is ordered to be destroyed without exception, the townspeople had previously petitioned that they should contribute small amounts according to their means, even if only small portions. Today it was ordered that they should contribute as requested in their petition.
　　However, since the townspeople were ordered as per their petition, it was also ordered that rural residents should contribute paper money according to their means.
