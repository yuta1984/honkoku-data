Fire Fighting and Fire Response Procedures

Kyōhō 18, Year of the Water Ox (1733)
Regulations for Fire Fighting at Ueno Sacred Grounds

1. This time, regarding the appointment of fire watch duty at Tōeizan, it was previously ordered that people in that area should exercise caution. In the event of a fire breaking out within the sacred mountain, the townspeople in front of Tōeizan's main gate and the townspeople and farmers residing in the Yanaka area will rush to extinguish it. Since our personnel will come from the Reiganjima residence, there may be delays compared to those people. However, when our officials rush to the scene, even if those people are engaged in firefighting, they should vacate the area and hand it over to us. In such cases, they should withdraw from the firefighting area and leave it as is, and for firefighting purposes, our personnel should be deployed wherever deemed appropriate. Also, there may be separate location instructions from the duty officers during this period, and in such cases, the names of those giving such instructions should be noted. If the location is narrow, firefighting may proceed with people working together. Of course, even townspeople and farmers should avoid causing disturbances or making excessive statements, and this should be strictly communicated regularly not only to the officers themselves but down to all subordinates under group command.

1. If there are matters at the location that are difficult to tolerate, such circumstances should be reported to the inspectors. Later, these will be investigated and corrected under official scrutiny. Under no circumstances should matters be handled according to one's personal judgment.
