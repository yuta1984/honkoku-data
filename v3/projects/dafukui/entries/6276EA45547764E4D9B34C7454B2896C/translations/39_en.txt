Regulations for Guard Posts at Intersections of the Upper Residences
Articles

1. Regarding intersection guard duty: This shall be performed diligently day and night without negligence. Even during the night, the front doors shall be kept open and sleepless watch maintained, with continuous patrol of the designated areas. Should any troublemakers, wounded persons, or generally suspicious individuals appear, guards shall go out to detain them, immediately report to the supervising officials, and notify the inspectors (metsuke) of each respective residence to receive instructions. Even if it is not a designated patrol area, nearby guards shall immediately respond and handle the situation, then transfer custody to the guards of the patrol area.

1. In case of fights, street attacks, and similar incidents, the same procedures as above shall be followed.

1. Regarding intersection guard personnel: For those serving guard duty with stipends from 10,000 to 19,000 koku, three guards during the day and five at night shall serve.
   Note: Those with stipends of 10,000 koku or more who serve intersection guard duty without group partnerships shall not transfer their guard shifts to contractors.

1. For those serving guard duty with stipends of 20,000 koku or more, four guards during the day and six at night shall serve.
   Note: Even in group partnerships, the predetermined number of personnel shall serve.

1. The prohibitions issued by magistrates and night patrol inspectors shall not be violated.
   Note: Idle gossip shall not be spread to other locations.
