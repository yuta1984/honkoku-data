Five-ryō coins begin circulation
First, Tenpō 9th year, Tsuchinoe-inu (1838)　　《marginal note: Around April, 1 monme of silver = 70 mon》
　　From May 16th, gold became valued at 90 monme per 1 ryō
First, Same 10th year, Tsuchinoto-i (1839), November
　　From the 6th, gold became valued at 120 monme per 1 ryō, with exchange fees of 2 monme,
　　totaling 122 monme. Daily price fluctuations occurred. Paper silver: 1 monme = 
　　60 mon
First, Same year, December
　　Rice approximately 70 monme per hyō　Gold approximately 135 monme per 1 ryō
First, Same 13th year, Mizunoe-tora (1842)
　　From August 13th, new currency exchange
　　New paper silver 65 monme per 1 ryō of gold《marginal note: However, for gold payments, exchange fee is 5 bu per 1 ryō》
　　Old currency 130 monme per 1 ryō of gold《marginal note: Same as above, exchange fee is 1 monme each》
　New currency: 104 mon per 1 monme, old currency: 52 mon per 1 monme
First, Same year, September 10th
　　Bunsei period inscribed gold and silver coins, cursive script ni-bu coins, ni-shu silver,
　　and isshu silver were all discontinued
First, Same 14th year, Mizunoto-u (1843), November 15th
　　Government rice stipend certificates purchased in bulk: 45 monme per hyō, for townspeople
