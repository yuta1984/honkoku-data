First, Ansei 3rd year, Hinoe-tatsu (1856), February
　　　Rice certificates one hyō: over 60 monme
　　　Gold one ryō: 142-143 monme

First, same year, September
　　　New note exchange

First, same year, same month, Finance Office proclamation
　　　During September: 55.5 monme per hyō　During October: 54.5 monme
　　　During November: 54 monme
　　Villages should complete their tax payments according to these official rice broker prices

First, same 4th year, Mi year (1857), April
　　　Regarding the official exchange office, money changing had been suspended since previous years, but from April 17th
　　　money changing began as a trial
　　　However, for one ryō of gold: 129 monme plus 1 monme cutting fee, town market rate per ryō:
　　　　129 monme, at this time 50 mon per 1 monme of coins
　　　However, when exchanging gold for silver notes at the official exchange office, it is 129 monme

First, same year, December
　　　By year's end, certificate prices gradually became expensive, one hyō: 84-85 monme
　　　Rice one hyō: about 88-89 monme

First, same 5th year, Uma year (1858)
　　　February 15th, rice certificates at about 81.5 monme per hyō, but gradually becoming bullish
