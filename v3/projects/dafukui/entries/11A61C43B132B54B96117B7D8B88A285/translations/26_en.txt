First, Tenpō 14th year, Mizunoto-u (1843)
　　From December 3rd to 10th, new currency exchange for hatamoto retainers and other domains, from the 11th to 25th of the same month
　　New currency exchange for our domain territory
　　　However, during the previous Tora year (Tenpō 13th) August 15th silver-free new currency exchange, old currency 10 monme
　　　　became new currency 5 monme, but this time it reverted: new currency 5 monme became additional print
　　　　currency 10 monme
　　　First, the fan-shaped additional print silver notes exchanged and distributed within our domain territory
　　　　shall be used only within our domain territory. However, if commoners
　　　　use them privately for trade with other domains, there shall be no interference
　　　First, the square-marked additional print silver notes exchanged and distributed to other domains this time, even if they
　　　　circulate within our domain territory, shall be used without hindrance
First, Kōka 3rd year, Hinoe-uma (1846), June
　　　Due to high rice prices, as relief measure, payment at 63 monme
First, same year, September
　　From the 1st to the last day of the month, fan-shaped silver notes used in our domain territory exchanged for new notes
　　From October 15th to November 10th, square-marked notes used elsewhere exchanged for new notes
First, same year, November 22nd
　　The square-marked印 silver notes exchanged and distributed to other domains during the previous U year (Kōka 3rd) due to the currency office reform,
　　from today the 22nd, return to original exchange rate: 65 monme per 1 ryō of gold,
　　money exchange rate: 100 mon per chō
