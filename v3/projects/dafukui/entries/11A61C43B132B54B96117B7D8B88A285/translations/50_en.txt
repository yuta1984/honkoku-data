Each time they were replaced, the shape of the small boards would also be changed according to the regulation - for example, if they had been square boards until then, this time they would be gourd-shaped, and the next time diamond-shaped, and so on, changing the shape of the small boards according to the rules. Therefore, when a tofu seller without this brand mark was walking around, it was agreed among the eighteen establishments that his tofu box would be confiscated. After the abolition of the han system and establishment of prefectures, the number of tofu merchant houses increased, and such old customs came to an end.
