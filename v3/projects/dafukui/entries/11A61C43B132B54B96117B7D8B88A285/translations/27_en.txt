First, Kaei 2nd year, Tsuchinoto-tori (1849), November
　　　Certificate for one hyō: 82-83 monme

First, same 3rd year, Kanoe-inu (1850), around August
　　Rice about 95 monme

First, same year, November 15th
　　The existing square-marked silver notes up to now, according to the previously established agreements, for various
　　tax payments and other transactions, within our domain territory silver note transactions shall
　　continue to be used without any hindrance
　　　However, regarding square-marked note exchange: 64 monme per one ryō

First, same 4th year, Kanoto-i (1851), February
　　　Certificate for one hyō becomes over 100 monme

First, same year, November 15th
　　Rice stipend certificates purchased at 72 monme per hyō　2 monme higher

First, same 6th year, Mizunoto-ushi (1853), December
　　Rice one hyō about 80 monme

First, Ansei 1st year, Kinoe-tora (1854)
　　One shu silver begins circulation

First, same year, December
　　Rice one hyō about 80 monme
　　　Silver 1 monme: 50 mon exchange rate
