29th day: Heavy snow falls from evening, heavy snow all day until after 7:30 (after 5 PM).
(Marginal note: 32 degrees)
Snow accumulates 1 shaku 5-6 sun to over 2 shaku, with drifts on the north side reaching over 3 shaku.

The Americans who had been staying in Edo since last year returned to Shimoda in Izu on the 21st day of the first month.

On the same day, Senior Councilor Hotta Bitchū-no-kami departed for Kyoto.

Second Month

1st day (hinoe-hitsuji): Clear from morning, strong cold weather. Clear all day with strong lingering cold.
(Marginal note: 25 degrees | brown rice 5 to 7 shō)
(Marginal note: 100 mon 7 gō 5 shaku | superior 7 gō)

2nd day: Clear from morning, strong cold weather. Light rain falls from 8 o'clock (2 PM) but stops immediately.
(Marginal note: 24 degrees)

3rd day: Clear from morning. Light rain falls from after 8 o'clock (after 2 PM), continuing until midnight.
(Marginal note: 32 degrees)

4th day: Partly cloudy weather in morning, west wind blows until 4 o'clock (10 AM) in the night.
(Marginal note: 34 degrees | hikan)

5th day: Thin overcast from morning, lingering cold. Overcast all day, snow falls from night 5 o'clock (8 PM).
(Marginal note: 29 degrees | Edo area)
(Marginal note: 100 mon for white rice | 5 gō 7 shaku)
