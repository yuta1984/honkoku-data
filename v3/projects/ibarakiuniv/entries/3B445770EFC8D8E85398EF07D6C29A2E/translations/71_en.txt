[The number] is unknown, but [ships] were washed ashore. The Hama Palace and the Tsukiji waterfront were mostly damaged. The main hall of Nishi Hongan-ji temple collapsed, and the temple buildings were mostly damaged, with a large ship washed up in front of the main gate. The entire Teppōzu area and Tsukuda Island suffered great damage. Around Reigan Island and Eitai Bridge, large ships caused major collapses from the middle section, stopping all traffic. Ship masts drifted to the Tayasu family estate, while sterns were washed up in front of the Andō family gate. The long houses suffered major collapse, and about ten tea boats were washed ashore in that area. From there, the Fukagawa waterfront was mostly damaged by the tsunami, temporary houses and pleasure quarters collapsed greatly, and a pleasure house called Ōnoya was completely destroyed. Around Susaki, the lumber yards were completely [destroyed], and two ship warehouses were also damaged. Besides this, the Honjo area suffered great damage from extensive flooding. In the northern area, the entire Kanda district, Bakurō-chō, and Ryōgoku area, as well as Asakusa, Okura-mae street, and the Kannon Hall were all thoroughly [damaged], and the entire temple grounds were destroyed. The pleasure house called Matsudaya outside the Yadaijin gate completely collapsed. Around Hanakawado, Sanya, and the theater district, all three theaters suffered great damage. The entire Senju area [was damaged], and in the western area, the residences of various daimyo in Marunouchi were completely destroyed in the entire district. The entire Shitaya area [was damaged], fires broke out around Sakamoto, parts of Yoshiwara burned, the entire Hongō district and Hakusan area suffered great damage, and besides this, the entire Yamanote area [was damaged]. Apart from the above, the entire city center suffered great damage, so I humbly submit this report for your inspection.

August 29th　　　　　Hashimoto Zenbei
