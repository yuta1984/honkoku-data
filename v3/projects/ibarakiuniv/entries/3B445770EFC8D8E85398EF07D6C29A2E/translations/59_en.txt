22nd day: Clear weather from morning with scattered clouds

23rd day: Rain from the 5th hour of morning (9 AM), stopped after the 4th hour (10 AM), cloudy all day

24th day: Cloudy from morning, cold, rain all day, rain extremely heavy during the night

25th day: Heavy rain from evening, light rain from dawn, cold, rain all day. Around the 4th hour of night (10 PM) south wind began to blow, sky turned pitch black with fierce lightning and heavy rain, thunder three or four times. From the 9th hour (midnight) a great southern gale unprecedented in history, turning to southeast wind by dawn and gradually calming down. Until dawn, we felt as if we were barely alive.

(Reference: On the 1st day of the 8th month of Tenpō 4, Year of the Snake, from the 4th hour of day (10 AM) to past the 8th hour (2 PM), there was heavy rain, strong wind, and great storm with many large trees uprooted and broken. This was said to be an unprecedented great storm, but this time's great wind was even more severe than that of the Snake year, beyond the memory of even elderly people.)
