The temple hall at the former site of Tsukiji Gate was destroyed. Shiodome Bridge half-collapsed, and iron [cannons] flew about. Many houses were destroyed by the large timber blown up at Shiodome.

Throughout Edo, fire watchtowers and the like were completely destroyed without exception, and there were reportedly many injured people.

7th day: Extremely clear from morning, cold. Around noon, scattered clouds appeared.

8th day: Extremely clear in the morning, scattered clouds appeared after midday.

9th day: Rain began falling from dawn at half-past the fifth hour (around 9 AM) and continued heavily all day. Cold air. Rain was exceptionally heavy during the night.

10th day: Rain cleared from dawn, fine weather, warm air. It was exceptionally hot, so single-layer garments and single-layer haori were worn. Cloudy from evening, rain fell at the fifth hour of night (around 8 PM) with thunder. Stopped after the fourth hour (around 11 PM).

11th day: Scattered clouds in the morning, the sky's appearance is troublesome.
