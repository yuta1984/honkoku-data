Officials should control the situation as appropriate. When circumstances require that people not be stopped, and if there are matters that cannot be resolved, instructions should be given by the attendants.

1. People on the streets should pass through as usual, but standing still to watch is of course forbidden. All improper behavior should be avoided, and nothing should obstruct traffic.

1. It is forbidden for towns to set up scaffolding and invite others to engage in elaborate spectating.

1. It is strictly forbidden for large crowds to come out carelessly from side streets and back alleys to stand and watch on the main roads.

1. Climbing up to second floors, laundry-drying areas, fire watchtowers, and similar places to watch is strictly forbidden.

1. Pointing fingers, loud laughter, etc. during the passage is forbidden. All improper behavior should be avoided.

1. When large carts and pack horses pass through, town officials should control and restrain them after they have passed.

1. Beggars and outcasts should not be allowed to appear along the route.

1. Town merchants may display their goods as usual, but items displayed on the streets should be taken inside. However, spears, long swords, other swords, armor, and other weapons should be taken inside and not left in front of shops.

1. During their stay, towns should pay special attention to fire prevention.
