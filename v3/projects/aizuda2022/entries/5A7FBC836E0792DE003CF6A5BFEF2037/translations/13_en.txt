Kuranonosuke (4th generation) Takanori (initially Takamōsu, initially Saemon, also Naizen)
　Mother: daughter of the Kitahara clan
　Wife: adopted daughter of Kitahara Uneme Mitsuyoshi
　　Actually daughter of Kasuya Heiuemon Takemune

One: 1st year of Kanpō (1741), 2nd month, because parent Denbei had served diligently with great effort for ten years, out of gracious consideration granted a fief of 200 koku and appointed to messenger guard (tsukaiban).

One: Same year, 4th month, went up to Edo for guard duty service, served in the imperial journey to Kyoto in the 9th month of the same year, and returned accompanying the lord's return journey in the 11th month of the same year.

One: Same 2nd year (1742), 8th month, for family succession granted a fief of 800 koku without change, ordered to continue serving as messenger guard as before, and the previously granted 200 koku fief was to be recalled.

One: Same 3rd year (1743), 6th month, appointed as messenger for the midsummer seasonal inquiry, went up to Edo and returned in the same month.
