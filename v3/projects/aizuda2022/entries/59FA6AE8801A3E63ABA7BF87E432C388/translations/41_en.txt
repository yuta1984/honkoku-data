- In the 10th month of Jōkyō 3 (1686), he went up to Edo for guard duty service and returned in the 3rd month of the following Jōkyō 4 (1687)

- In the 7th month of Hōei 4 (1707), he was appointed to concurrent duty as deputy magistrate

- In the 8th month of Hōei 6 (1709), as petitioned, he was relieved of his duties and served castle guard duty

- In the 9th month of Shōtoku 2 (1712), he was appointed as a permanent Edo-stationed outer vassal

- In the 5th month of Shōtoku 3 (1713), he was appointed to the position of chief treasurer (ō-onando)

- In the 5th month of Kyōhō 5 (1720), as petitioned, he was relieved of his duties, and his son Shozaemon was appointed as substitute guard

- In the 2nd month of Kyōhō 6 (1721), as petitioned, he was granted permission to retire

- On the 18th day of the 11th month of Kyōhō 9 (1724), he died of illness at the age of 72; posthumous Buddhist name: An'yo Rakubetsu Yūken Koji; burial site: Senshin-ji Temple at Matsumiyayama, Mita, Musashi Province
