• In the 7th year of Meiwa (1770), fifth month: As requested by his father Sekiemon, he was relieved of his gisaku (agricultural management) position. Five koku and one person's stipend were reduced from his father's gisaku allowance, and he was granted fifteen koku and three persons' stipend. His previous gisaku position was taken away.

• Same year, tenth month: He went up to Edo as a kinban (duty officer) and returned the following year (Boar year) in the fourth month accompanying the lord's return journey.

• In the 9th year of Meiwa (1772), sixth month: He was appointed to provide instruction in water arts (swimming) at the tea garden.

• In the 2nd year of Tenmei (1782), third month: The lord inspected the iai practice hall of his students.

Note: After this, such inspections were conducted many times.

• Same year, tenth month: He went up to Edo as a kinban, served in attendance upon Lord Sadaaki, was appointed to castle guard duty, and performed his duties without fault, for which he was awarded two bu in gold.

• In the 3rd year of Tenmei (1783), fourth month: He was appointed as ashigaru inspector (otsukami metsuke) in Edo and returned accompanying the lord's descent.

• In the 4th year of Tenmei (1784), third month: He was appointed to grain embargo duties in Inawashiro and served accordingly.

• Same year, fourth month: He had been appointed to instruct the lord's retainers in water arts, and it was announced that he had performed with diligence.
