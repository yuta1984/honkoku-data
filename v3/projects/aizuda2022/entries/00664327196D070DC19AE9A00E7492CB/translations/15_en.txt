• In the 2nd year of Kansei, 12th month (December 1790): He properly supervised bow and gun practice for the ashigaru (foot soldiers), and as a reward, he was granted five ryō of silver.

• In the 3rd year of Kansei, 11th month (November 1791): He was appointed as yoriki gashira (assistant commander) under a bantō (group leader), and because he diligently instructed two schools of iai and hyōhō (martial arts), he was granted dokudan status (independent audience privilege).

• In the 4th year of Kansei, intercalary 2nd month (1792): Since there were no current operational expenses required, he was assigned to the 4th yoriai group under a karō (chief retainer), and it was announced that he had served diligently up to this point.

• Until the 3rd month of the same year, he participated in hunting expeditions.

• In the 5th year of Kansei, 12th month (December 1793): His Ryūki school swordsmanship was observed by his lord.
Note: It was announced that he properly instructed a large number of disciples.

• In the 9th year of Kansei, 1st month (January 1797): He instructed both swordsmanship and iai schools and properly trained a large number of disciples, so as a reward he was granted one silver piece.

• In the 1st year of Kyōwa, 9th month (September 1801): Having instructed swordsmanship for several years and properly trained a large number of disciples, he was appointed as an instructor at the Bugakuryō (martial arts academy)...
