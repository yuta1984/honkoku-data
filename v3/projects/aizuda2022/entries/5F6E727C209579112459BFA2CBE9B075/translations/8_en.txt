[The lord] declared that his and his disciples' iaijutsu was performed well.

- In the 11th month of the same year, because Shibuya Genshichi fell ill and went down [to the domain], he was appointed to emergency guard duty, went up to Edo in the 12th month of the same year, and in the 4th month of the following Kanpō 4th year (1743), came down accompanying the lord's return journey.

- In the 4th month of Kanpō 3rd year (1743), it was declared that he served diligently with perfect attendance during guard duty.

- In the 5th month of the same year, it was declared that he served diligently with perfect attendance as escort during the lord's travels.

- In the 3rd month of Enkyō 1st year (1744), when the lord observed the Ryūgi-style iaijutsu military arts, it was declared that his and his disciples' iaijutsu was performed well.

- In the 4th month of Enkyō 2nd year (1745), he returned to meet [the lord] and went up [to Edo], then came down accompanying the lord's return journey.

- In the 5th month of the same year, it was declared that he served diligently with perfect attendance as escort during the lord's travels.

- In the 8th month of the same year, it was declared that he devoted himself to martial arts training.

- In the 3rd month of Enkyō 3rd year (1746), for the lord's attendance at court (sankin), he went up to Edo to serve guard duty as an escort.

- In the 4th month of the same year, [it was declared] that he served diligently with perfect attendance during the lord's travels.
