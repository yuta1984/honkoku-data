However, when the lord practiced iaijutsu, if his chronic illness flared up, he was told to take care of his health and still come to serve as able.

- In the 1st month of Genbun 5th year (1740), for the New Year beginning of iaijutsu practice, he was ordered to observe, and furthermore received the lord's kind words and was granted celebration gifts in the lord's private chambers.

- On the 18th day of the 7th month of the same year, he died of illness at age 64. His posthumous Buddhist name was Yūshō-in Akiya Nichie Koji, with the chief priest from Hōshōzan Jōkō-ji temple, buried at Ōkubo-yama.

Second Generation
　Sawaji Toshiuji　　　　　　　Born on the 15th day of the 4th month of Shōtoku 3rd year (1713)
      　　　　　　　　　
　　Mother's lineage unknown　

　　Wife: daughter of Obara Uemon Toshitomo, died of illness on the 7th day of the 4th month of Kansei 10th year (1798), posthumous Buddhist name Ryūtan-in Minpō Chiho Daishi, buried at Seiyui-in temple in Edo　　　　

- In the 8th month of Genbun 4th year (1739), as petitioned by his father, he was appointed as guard replacement and attached to the outer retainer group, joined Miyake Magobei's unit, and served castle guard duty.

- In the 8th month of Genbun 5th year (1740), he was granted the hereditary stipend of 100 koku without change.

- In the 11th month of Kanpō 1st year (1741), he was appointed to escort duty.

- In the 3rd month of Kanpō 2nd year (1742), the lord observed the Ryūgi-style iaijutsu demonstration.
