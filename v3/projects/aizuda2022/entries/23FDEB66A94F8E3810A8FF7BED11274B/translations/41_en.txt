One: In the 2nd month of the same year (Kyōwa 2, 1802), it was announced that he was appointed to handle confidential documents.

One: In the 4th month of Bunka 1 (1804), he served as an attendant, returning and going up to the capital, concurrently serving as guard at the official lodgings during the journey.

One: In the 11th month of the same 2nd year (1805),
　As a memento of Lord Kyōtei, he was granted one hemp ceremonial outfit (kamishimo) and one striped small-sleeved kimono.

One: In the 8th month of the same 3rd year (1806),
　As a memento of Lord Sadaaki, he was granted one hemp ceremonial outfit.

One: In the 12th month of the same year,
　When Lord Kinbun took up residence in the inner quarters, for working diligently as a companion, he was privately granted five bundles of tissue paper.
　　However, this continued to be granted yearly until the same 6th year (1809), and in addition, various items such as fans and tissue paper were frequently granted.
