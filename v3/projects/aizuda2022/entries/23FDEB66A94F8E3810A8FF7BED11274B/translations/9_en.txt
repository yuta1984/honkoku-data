He was summoned before Lord Doi Ōi-no-kami,
who inquired about his predecessor's service, and was told to submit
his thoughts on the matter. When he submitted his response in writing,
he received gracious words from his lordship.

- In the 1st month of Hōreki 2 (1752), when the domain inspectors had
previously come down and conducted their inspection tour, he went out to the gold mine contracting site
and responded to various inquiries with proper greetings. For this, he was commended for
his diligent service in contracting matters.

- In the 12th month of Hōreki 5 (1755), having fallen ill,
he submitted a petition to resign, but was ordered
to recuperate and continue his service.

- In the 11th month of Hōreki 6 (1756), as requested, he was relieved of his current duties,
and his son Jinji was appointed as his replacement in guard duty.

- On the 26th day of the 2nd month of Hōreki 7 (1757), he died of illness at the age of sixty-six.
Buddhist name: Shingyōin Gen'jō Nichigi, buried at Hōshōzan Jōkōji Temple.

Daughter (born in Shōtoku 4 (1714)) - wife of Baba Yojizaemon Aritaka
