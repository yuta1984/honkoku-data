- For his diligent service, he was granted a direct stipend certificate and an increase of three koku, plus an increase of one person's worth of Edo stipend, making his total allowance thirteen koku and stipend for five people.

- In the 2nd month of Kyōhō 10 (1725), because he performed his duties as inspector diligently and well, he was granted one silver piece as a reward.

- In the 4th month of Kyōhō 11 (1726), because he performed well in investigating official matters, although it was a weighty matter, his seat was assigned next to Muramatsu Den'hachirō, and he was appointed as a permanent inspector continuing his previous duties overseeing financial matters.

- In the 5th month of the same year, for his diligent and excellent service in official duties related to invitations and entertainment for the senior councilors, he was granted three bu in gold as a reward.

- In the 8th month of the same year, for his diligent work and faithful performance of various official duties, he was granted two silver pieces as a reward.

- In the 11th month of the same year, for his competent handling of official duties related to Lady Baien-in's shrine visit, he was privately granted three bu in gold as a reward.

- In the 2nd month of Kyōhō 12 (1727), for his careful attention to detail and diligent performance of inspection duties, he was granted an increase of five koku for his public service...
