Regarding his departure to Aizu, as requested, he was relieved of his duties as companion, and in the formal audience chamber received gracious words, and was granted one roll of silk before his lord.
　However, during the aforementioned service, he was granted items from the lord's storehouse on two occasions, but the details of these items are unclear.

- In the 5th month of Enkyō 2 (1745), he was appointed to document duties, and as he performed well, he was granted one roll of silk as a reward.

- In the 12th month of Hōreki 6 (1756), as requested by his father, he was appointed as substitute guard and entered Suwa Isuke's unit to serve as castle guard.

- In the 4th month of the 7th year (1757), he was granted his inherited stipend of 100 koku without any reduction.

- In the 1st month of Meiwa 1 (1764), when captain Suwa Isuke became substitute guard, he was transferred to serve under Numazu Gonbei's unit in the succeeding role.

- In the 11th month of the 5th year (1768), when captain Numazu Gonbei became substitute guard, he was transferred to serve under Ichinose Naiki's unit in the succeeding role.

- In the 8th month of the 8th year (1771), as requested, his son Jinkurō was appointed as substitute guard.
