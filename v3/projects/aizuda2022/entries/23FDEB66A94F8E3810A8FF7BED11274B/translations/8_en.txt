Because of his service, he was granted direct ownership of the 100 koku stipulated land that had been provisional until then.
However, this was granted through the gracious consideration of Lord Tokuō.

- In the 8th month of Kyōhō 17 (1732), during the construction at the Shiba estate, he attended the construction site every other day, paid careful attention to matters of profit and loss, and served well, for which he was granted one summer robe (katabira).

- In the 7th month of Kyōhō 18 (1733), as requested, he was relieved of his current duties and appointed to an unassigned position in Aizu finance, returning to Aizu in the 8th month of the same year.

- In the 9th month of the same year, his rank was elevated to deputy head of accounts.

- In the 3rd month of Kyōhō 20 (1735), he was appointed as chief head of accounts.

- In the same month, he was granted the former residence of Tanaka Jirōbei.

- In the 11th month of Kanpō 1 (1741), because of his excellent service over many years, he was assigned to the external retainer group and appointed to serve concurrently as head of accounts, joining Hyūga Saburōemon's group.

- In the 5th month of Enkyō 2 (1745), he was appointed as gold mine magistrate.
