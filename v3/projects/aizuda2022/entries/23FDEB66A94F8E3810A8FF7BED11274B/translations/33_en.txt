One: In the 9th month of Enkyō 1 (1744), he was appointed as personal attendant (懐守) to Lord Kyōtei and granted a stipend of 15 koku and provisions for 3 retainers

One: In Enkyō 2 (1745) [month unknown], due to his diligent service, he was granted one bolt of silk annually as announced

One: In the 3rd month of Kan'en 1 (1748), when Lord Kyōtei made his formal visit to Edo, he accompanied him as duty guard and was commended for his diligent service during the journey

One: In the 7th month of the same year, he was appointed as regular personal attendant

One: In the 9th month of Kan'en 3 (1750), it was announced that his title was changed to御近習番 (personal attendant guard)

One: In the 6th month of Hōreki 2 (1752), his request to adopt Yamakawa Namie's younger brother Jibu as his heir was granted

One: On the 17th day of the 7th month of the same year, he died of illness at age 50. His posthumous Buddhist name was Kōzan Ryōchū Koji, and he was buried at Seiganjin within Seishōji Temple in Edo
