- In the 7th month of the same year, he was appointed as Edo Chief Administrator and Head of Accounts, and was also appointed to concurrent duties as inspector.

- In the 3rd month of Kyōhō 14 (1729), because he handled administrative duties well through proper consultation and diligent service, he was granted an increase of seven koku and stipend for one person, making his total twenty-five koku and stipend for five people. The stipend for one person was converted from the Edo stipend he had been receiving to a permanent direct stipend.

- In the 11th month of the same year, for his diligent and excellent service, he was granted one set of ceremonial hemp garments (kamishimo).

- In the 9th month of Kyōhō 15 (1730), he was relieved of his inspection duties and appointed to serve as chief inspector, with an annual salary of five silver pieces to be granted each year.

- In the 12th month of the same year, he was granted an increase of Edo stipend for two people, and it was announced that he would receive ten ryō annually for guest entertainment expenses.

- In the 11th month of Kyōhō 16 (1731), he was appointed to serve in addition to his other duties as construction magistrate for the ongoing construction at the Shiba estate and for future projects.
