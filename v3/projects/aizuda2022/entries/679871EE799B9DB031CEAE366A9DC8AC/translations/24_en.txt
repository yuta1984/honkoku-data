Zentayū Moriyuki　　《Marginal note: Initially Jintarō｜Born in Kyōhō 13 (1728), year of the monkey》

Mother: same as above

Wife: Daughter of Higuchi Kizaemon Mitsutaka, born on the 11th day of the 7th month of Genbun 4 (1739), year of the sheep
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Died of illness on the 12th day of the 8th month of Bunsei 6 (1823), year of the sheep, at age 85. Buddhist posthumous name: Jōshinoin Myōgetsu Nissho Daishi. Officiating priest from Daihōji Temple on Mount Myōryō. Burial place: Mount Daichō.

1. In the intercalary 12th month of Enkyō 2 (1745), year of the ox, due to his father's misconduct, he had 2 koku reduced from his stipend, was granted 5 koku and rations for 2 people, and was summoned to serve as a minor official in the Finance Office.

1. In the 12th month of Meiwa 1 (1764), year of the monkey, he was granted a service allowance of 2 koku.

1. In the 2nd month of the same 3rd year (1766), year of the dog, he departed for garrison duty in Yamagata and returned in the 9th month of the same year.

1. In the 10th month of the same 8th year (1771), year of the rabbit, he went up to Edo for garrison duty, but due to staff shortage, he was ordered to extend his stay and returned in the 6th month of the following year of the dragon.
　　Note: It is said that he performed garrison duty two more times after this, but the dates of one tour are unknown.

1. In the 2nd month of An'ei 3 (1774), year of the horse, he was appointed to the position of Finance Inspector, and his service allowance of 2 koku continued to be granted as before.
