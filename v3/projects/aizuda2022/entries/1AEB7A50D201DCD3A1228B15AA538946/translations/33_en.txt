In the twelfth month of the same year, he was appointed to御次番 (palace guard duty) and granted an increase of five koku of rice stipend.

In the eighth month of Kanpō 2 (1742), he accompanied his lord to Edo for sankin-kōtai service, ascending to serve guard duty, and descended with his lord's retinue when they returned in the fourth month of the following year.
However, after this he served two tours of guard duty, and once received items from the lord's personal storehouse, though the specific nature of these items is unclear.

In the ninth month of Enkyō 3 (1746), though he was scheduled for guard duty, due to illness his request to be excused from guard duty was granted.

In the twelfth month of Kan'en 2 (1749), having served diligently and well, his previous stipend of twenty koku and provisions for three retainers was directly increased to a fief of one hundred koku.

In the twelfth month of Kan'en 3 (1750), he was granted clothing as a memento from Lord Dojō.
However, the specific items are unclear.

In the third month of Hōreki 3 (1753), having served three tours of duty in Edo with great diligence, he was granted one roll of bleached cloth in place of hemp formal wear as a reward.

In the seventh month of the same year, his son Sukeshichi was appointed as a guard substitute as requested.
