1. In the sixth month of the first year of Jōō (1652, year of the dragon), he was granted a standard stipend of 10 koku and provisions for two attendants, and was summoned to serve as a foot soldier permanently stationed in Edo.

1. In the third month of the third year of Kanbun (1663, year of the rabbit), he was appointed to handle Aizu domain finances.
1. In the ninth month of the sixth year (1666, year of the horse), he was appointed as inspector of foot soldiers (o-kachi-metsuke) and granted the accompanying stipend and rice allowance.
1. In the fourth month of the seventh year (1667, year of the sheep), as a reward for his service in managing rice transport, he was granted one silver piece.
1. On the last day of the same month, he died of illness. His posthumous Buddhist name was Teigen Kyōshitsu Shinji, and he was buried at Ganshōji Temple on Mount Kanō.
