He was granted without objection and was ordered to continue serving as attendant guard as before.

In the ninth month of Meiwa 1 (1764), since there was no male heir, his request to adopt Tomekichi, the fourth son of Shirai Gorōdayū, as an adopted son-in-law was granted.

In the ninth month of the same year, he went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in the fifth month of the following year accompanying the lord's return to the domain.
　　However, it was announced that he had served diligently and without incident in the garrison.

In the second month of Meiwa 2 (1765), his request to change his name to Kan'emon was granted.

In the fourth month of the same year, he was appointed as temporary gate guard and served until the fifth month of the same year.

In the fifth month of the same year, when the lord returned to the domain and made a pilgrimage to Nikkō Shrine, he accompanied him.
　　However, it was announced that he had served diligently as an attendant.

In the fourth month of Meiwa 4 (1767), he went up to welcome the lord's return to the domain.
　　However, it was announced that he had served diligently in this attendance.

In the third month of Meiwa 7 (1770), he accompanied the lord for sankin (continues)
