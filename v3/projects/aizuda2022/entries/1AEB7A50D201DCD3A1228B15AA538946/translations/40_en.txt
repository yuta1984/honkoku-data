In the fourth month of Kansei 5 (1793), he was granted a hereditary stipend of 100 koku without change.

In the eleventh month of the same year, his request to adopt Matsushirō, the fourth son of Kanao Kogenji, as son-in-law was granted.

In the ninth month of Kansei 7 (1795), he participated in the hunting expedition (tsuitori-gari).
Note: He participated four more times after this.

In the twelfth month of the same year, during the hunting expedition, his formation order, marching pace, and movements were flawless, and it was deemed that he had been diligent in his private training, for which his entire unit received commendation.
Note: He continued to receive commendations on every subsequent occasion.

In the first month of Kansei 9 (1797), it was announced that he had performed his castle guard duties without negligence.

In the tenth month of Bunka 1 (1804), his request to change his name to Kan'emon was granted.

In Bunka 2 (1805), as requested, his son Hayato was appointed as guard substitute.

On the sixth day of the sixth month of Bunsei 1 (1818), he died of illness at age sixty-four, posthumous name Honsei-in Kakuyo Eishō Koji.
