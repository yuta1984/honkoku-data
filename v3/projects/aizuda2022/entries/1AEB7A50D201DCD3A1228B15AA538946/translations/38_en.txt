In the sixth month of Tenmei 6 (1786), due to his chronic illness affecting his memory and eyesight becoming weak, he petitioned to be relieved of his current duties and requested that his son Tomekichi be appointed as his substitute. However, he was ordered to focus on recuperating while continuing his service.

In the tenth month of Tenmei 7 (1787), his petition was granted and he was relieved of his duties, with his son Sukeshichi appointed as his substitute.

In the fourth month of Kansei 5 (1793), his petition was granted and he was permitted to retire.

He died of illness on the ninth day of the fourth month of Kyōwa 2 (1802) at the age of seventy-nine. His posthumous Buddhist name was Shinkaku-in Kaiyō Seisen Koji. Burial site: same as above.
