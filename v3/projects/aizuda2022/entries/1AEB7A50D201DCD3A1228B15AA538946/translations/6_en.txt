Mother: Daughter of the Nojima clan
　Wife: Daughter of Takahata Kuemon (name unknown)

1. In the eleventh month of the eighth year of Kanbun (1668, year of the monkey), he was granted provisions for three attendants and summoned to serve in the meeting hall tea room.
1. In the twelfth month of the eleventh year (1671, year of the boar), his three-person stipend was supplemented with a certificate for six koku of rice, and he was appointed as a minor official in the investigation office.
1. In the third month of the eighth year of Enpō (1680, year of the monkey), he was granted an additional four koku.

1. In the twelfth month of the third year of Tenna (1683, year of the boar), he was appointed to concurrently serve as a rice transport official.
1. In the third month of the first year of Jōkyō (1684, year of the rat), when rice was being transported to Osaka, he traveled to Niigata in Echigo Province on official business related to ship loading, returning in the seventh month of the same year.
1. In the ninth month of the second year (1685, year of the ox), he was granted an additional five koku.
1. In the same year [month unknown], he went up to Edo for rice transport accounting purposes, completed the balance calculations, and returned.
　　However, each time he went up for such official business, he was granted ten ryō of gold.
1. In the same year [month unknown], upon his request, he was relieved of his concurrent duties in rice transport.
