Entry: Same 14th year, year of the ox (1817), 3rd month, regarding the boundary dispute between rice fields of Yamazaki village in Keitoku group and Aoki village in Aotsu group, he went to that location with the standing meeting of foot inspectors and arranged an internal settlement

Entry: Bunsei 1st year, year of the tiger (1818), his father's stipend assessment was called up, and although he was an adopted son, due to the distinguished service of both father and son, through special consideration his stipend assessment was not reduced, and he was granted 21 koku and 3 person stipends as before, and was appointed to continue serving as regular temporary rod leader for land survey officials

Entry: Same 3rd year, year of the dragon (1820), 1st month, he was appointed as rod leader for land survey officials

Entry: Same month, he was appointed to official duties for boundary dispute inspection and mediation between Hōfuku-ji temple in Uragō village, Miura district, Sagami province, and the village headman Jirōzaemon of the same village. When he went up and performed these duties, in the 4th month of the same year, as reward for performing these official duties, he was granted 7 ryō of silver

Entry: Same 4th year, year of the snake (1821), for the boundary dispute inspection duties at the disputed riverbed area between the distant fields of Aotsu group Tachikawa and Shiokawa group, he performed multiple tours of duty, conducting land surveys and investigations, always together with the foot inspectors
