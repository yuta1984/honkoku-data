Entry: Anei 3rd year, year of the horse (1774) (month unknown) - Of the 3 ryō of gold in compensation, 2 ryō were converted to a stipend for two persons

Entry: Same 7th year, year of the dog (1778) (month unknown) - Granted a stipend of 4 koku 4 to 4 shō for two persons, and summoned as a lacquer and wood inspector

Entry: Tenmei 2nd year, year of the tiger (1782), 12th month - As requested in his petition, he was promoted to the drama performance office

Entry: Same 4th year, year of the dragon (1784), 5th month, 23rd day - Died of illness at age 74　Posthumous Buddhist name: Jōzen-in Ennyū Nichigo Koji　Burial site: Same as above

Yagoemon Yasuyoshi (4th generation, adopted son)　Initially called Tetsunosuke, later Tsuneemon, then Tsuneemon　Born on Hōreki 11th year, year of the snake (1761), 1st month, 29th day
　Biological father: Eldest son of Watanabe Naosuke Teruji
　Wife: Daughter of Watanabe Jūsuke Takahisa

Entry: Anei 7th year, year of the dog (1778), 7th month - Appointed to employment as a lacquer and wood inspector, and awarded 1 ryō of gold

Entry: Same year, 12th month - Went on official duty for border road control and apprehended smuggled lacquer, for which he was awarded 3 bu of gold as a reward

Entry: Same 9th year, year of the rat (1780), 12th month - For going on the aforementioned official duty, he was awarded 3 bu of gold as a reward

Entry: Same year (month unknown) - His petition to change his name to Tsuneemon was approved
