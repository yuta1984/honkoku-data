一In the second year of Tora (1782), third month: For four years of diligent daily service and perfect attendance, he was granted one set of old hemp formal wear (kamishimo)
一In the fourth year of Tatsu (1784), ninth month: He was appointed to the position of gozenban (lord's meal attendant)
一In the fifth year of Mi (1785), fifth month: For continued diligent service and perfect attendance for four years, he was granted one old katabira (summer garment)
一Same year, sixth month: He was granted one ryō in gold as position allowance
一In the eighth year of Saru (1788), eighth month: He was appointed as temporary okuban (inner guard)
　　Note: Thereafter, he was repeatedly appointed to this position in both Edo and Aizu
一Same year, twelfth month: In addition to his previous position allowance of one ryō in gold, he was directly granted an increase
一In the first year of Kansei, Tori (1789), ninth month: For several years of perfect attendance at busy posts, he was granted one old summer garment
一In the second year of Inu (1790), first month: He was granted the hereditary stipend of 100 koku without change, and was ordered to continue serving as okoshō (page) as before
一Same year, ninth month: After sanpū (going to Edo), for diligent service and good performance in managing the lord's quarters, he was granted two [units of] cotton as a reward
