One: In the 8th year of the same era, 9th month (September 1771), he went up to Edo for guard duty, and returned in the 3rd month of the following Dragon year.
One: In the 2nd year of An'ei, 1st month (January 1773), having worked diligently for several years, he was granted an increase in stipend and awarded regular gisaku status.
One: In the 6th year of the same era, 12th month (December 1777), his request to change his name to Rokurōemon was granted.
One: In the 4th year of Kansei, 3rd month (March 1792), he participated in the bird hunting at Hongōhara.
　　Note: He participated five more times after this.
One: In the same month, this bird hunting was the first attempt, but he was seen to have worked diligently, the formations were well-coordinated, and regarding the group warriors, although there should not have been any formation training before the above directive was issued, and there was indeed no such training, they performed well as observed, and the entire group was praised.
　　Note: He continued to receive praise on every subsequent occasion.
