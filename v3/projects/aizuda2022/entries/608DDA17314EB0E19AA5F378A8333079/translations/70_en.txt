1. Same year, 12th month: His request to change his name to Tōgorō was granted.

1. Same [year] 12, 5th month: He was appointed to the gaisama-gumi and placed in Jinbo Kuranosuke's unit.
　　　However, due to illness, he did not serve castle guard duty.

1. Same year, 9th month: Because he had no male heir and his illness had worsened, his request to adopt Yogorō, the second son of Kasuga Ogiemon, as son-in-law and heir was granted.

1. Same year, 10th month, 2nd day: He died of illness at age 45. His posthumous Buddhist name was Shugakuin Giten Nichiō Shinji, and his burial place was Hōshōzan Jōkōji Temple.

　Tōgorō Takahide (first name: Yogorō, born Kyōwa 2, 1st month)
