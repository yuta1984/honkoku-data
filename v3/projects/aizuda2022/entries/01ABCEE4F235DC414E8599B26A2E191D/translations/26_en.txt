He was granted [the position] and appointed to the hired service of assistant district magistrate (gun-bugyō soe-yaku), given a stipend of seven men's portions, and additionally granted expense allowance equivalent to that of an assistant magistrate. He was ordered to attend the assistant magistrate's seating during New Year ceremonies, lord's castle arrivals, and formal ceremonies.

1. Same month: Regarding Itō Jūbei's matter, since he was appointed to serve in Niigata, he was instructed to handle all affairs in the same manner as his previous official duties. Concerning this, since he would also specialize in procurement matters, if it became necessary to send correspondence to financiers in the future, and if expenses for final seals and such were required during such negotiations, he should also provide the final seal. He was ordered to consult thoroughly with Jūbei and handle matters appropriately.

1. Same year, 3rd month: He was granted a replacement loan of the former residence of Ibuka Jūrōzaemon in Yonedai San-no-chō.

1. Same year, 6th month: He was appointed to concurrently serve as head clerk of the accounting office (kanjō-sho shu-yaku) and granted fifteen silver pieces annually.

1. Same 4th year (1792), Year of the Rat, 5th month: He was appointed to divided duties as head clerk of the accounting office. Even when not handling assigned duties, he was ordered to consult with others without concealment as before, handle affairs appropriately, and manage all official business efficiently.
