Hikojūrō Kanenō, originally called Chōjirō
　　　　　　Born on the 10th day of the 12th month of Shōtoku 5 (1715, Year of the Sheep)
　Actually the second son of Satō Heitayū Tadatsune
　Wife: daughter of adoptive father Yūhachi Kanemichi　　Died of illness on the 12th day of the 1st month of Kan'en 2 (1749, Year of the Snake), posthumous Buddhist name Dōsei-in Myōkyō
　　　　　　　　　　　　Shinnyo, buried at Chōshōzan Kyūfuku-ji temple
　　Daughter of Higuchi Jūemon Mitsuo　Died of illness on the 28th day of the 11th month of Hōreki 3 (1753, Year of the Rooster), posthumous Buddhist name Jigen-in
　　　　　　　　　　　　Myōtatsu Shinnyo, buried at Shōun-zan Zenryū-ji temple
　　Daughter of Kurokawauchi Tōemon Tomohide　Born in Kyōhō 18 (1733, Year of the Ox), died of illness on the 12th day of the 4th month of Bunka 9 (1812, Year of the Monkey)
　　　　　　　　　　　　　at the age of 81, posthumous Buddhist name Shinshō-in Myōri Nichiryō Daishi
　　Daughter of Arakawa Kyūbei Taneshige

1. In the 11th month of Genbun 2 (1737, Year of the Snake): As requested by his father, he was granted his first audience with the lord.
1. In the 8th month of Enkyō 2 (1745, Year of the Ox): He was assigned to copying books from the lord's library.
1. In the 12th month of the same 4th year (1747, Year of the Rabbit): As requested by his father, he was appointed as a guard substitute and served castle guard duty in Tanaka Kahyōe's unit.
1. In the 8th month of Hōreki 1 (1748, Year of the Sheep): When the unit head Tanaka Kahyōe was transferred to another position, he was assigned to Hori Hanemon's unit as his successor.
1. In the 7th month of the same 3rd year (1750, Year of the Rooster): He was granted a hereditary stipend of 100 koku without any changes.
1. In the 2nd month of the same 12th year (1759, Year of the Horse): He was ordered to transfer to Sugita Gorobē's unit.
