He was ordered to continue serving as before, but when summoned, if his chronic illness flared up, he should rest for recuperation and return to service when his condition improved.

First, in the 7th year of Hōreki, 7th month (July 1757): Having practiced archery for several years, this time he brought Saka Tetsuma with his own resources and climbed up [the mountain/castle], performing tsūya (consecutive arrows through targets) well. This was considered admirable, and as a reward he was granted one old imperial summer garment and three pieces of silver.

First, in the 9th year, 8th month (August 1759): He was granted retirement as requested.

First, on the 1st day of the 12th month of the 12th year (December 1, 1762): He died of illness at the age of 84. Buddhist name: Benchūin Hakusen Dōsetsu Koji. Burial place: same as above.

Saemon (adopted son) Mitsuhiro - Born in the 5th year of Hōei, child year (1708)
Actually the second son of Saka Tsunbei Naonori
Wife: daughter of adoptive father Tanzaemon Mitsushige - Died of illness on the 12th day of the 2nd month of the 3rd year of Kansei, boar year (February 12, 1791). Buddhist name: Jikō Myōgen Daishi. Burial place: Shōuzan Zenryūji Temple.
