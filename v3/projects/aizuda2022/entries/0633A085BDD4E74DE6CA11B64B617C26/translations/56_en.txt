He was granted a fief of 150 koku without change, and served guard duty at the castle in Yamazaki Sasuke's unit.

First, in the 3rd month of Meiwa 2, rooster year (March 1765): He went to Yamagata for guard duty and returned in the 9th month of the same year.

First, in the 9th month of the same 3rd year, dog year (September 1766): He was appointed to escort duty.

First, in the 12th month of the same year (December 1766): He went up to Edo for emergency guard duty, and returned in the 2nd month of the following boar year due to illness.

First, on the 27th day of the 2nd month of the same 4th year, boar year (February 27, 1767): He died of illness at the age of 25. His posthumous Buddhist name was Tōsen-in Shunpō Ryōki Koji. Burial site: same as above.
