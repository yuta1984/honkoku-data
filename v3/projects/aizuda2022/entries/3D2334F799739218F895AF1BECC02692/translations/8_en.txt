1. In the 5th month of Shōtoku 4 (1714), he petitioned to change his name to Naotadayū and was granted permission

1. In the 2nd month of Kyōhō 2 (1717), he moved to the Wadakura-muki residence and was appointed to serve as a regular fire brigade member (jōbikeshi)

1. In the 6th month of Kyōhō 7 (1722), due to his diligent service in the fire brigade, he was granted 2 pieces of silver as a reward

1. In the 5th month of Kyōhō 13 (1728), since the Muki residence was surrendered, he moved to the Shiba residence and continued his regular duties. He was then appointed as one fire brigade member for the upper residence (kami-oyashiki) and moved there to serve in the 9th month

1. In the 8th month of Kyōhō 15 (1730), due to his diligent service in the fire brigade, his stipend as an attendant of the same rank as gokinshu was increased, and it was announced that he would receive 3 pieces of labor silver each time

1. In the 6th month of Kyōhō 18 (1733), he was appointed as an attendant to Lords Baien-in and Ganshō-in, with his seat rank appointed above that of ometsuke-ban

1. In the 10th month of Kyōhō 20 (1735), due to his diligent service, he was granted 50 koku as official salary
