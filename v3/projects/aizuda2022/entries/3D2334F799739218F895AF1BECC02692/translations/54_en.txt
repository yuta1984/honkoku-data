Married a daughter of Shimizu Shichirobyōe (a retainer of Masuyama Tsushima-no-kami) <<died of illness on the 29th day of the 4th month of Hōreki 14 (1764), posthumous Buddhist name Chimyōin Kangyoku Emyō Daishi, buried in the same location as above>>

1. In the 2nd month of Hōei 3 (1706), in accordance with his father's petition, he was appointed as a substitute guard (banday) and assigned to the outer retainer group (gaikesama-gumi-tsuki), joining Hori Hanemon's unit to serve guard duty at the castle.

2. In the 6th month of the same year, he was granted the hereditary stipend (ato-shiki chigyō) of 200 koku without any reduction.

3. In the 10th month of Hōei 4 (1707), he went up to Edo for guard duty (kinban) and returned in the 5th month of the following year.

4. In the 11th month of Hōei 6 (1709), he was appointed to the lord's escort guard (go-tomobam).

5. In the 4th month of Hōei 7 (1710), he went up to Edo for escort guard duty and returned in the 10th month of the same year.

6. In the 4th month of Shōtoku 1 (1711), he went up to Edo as part of the lord's escort for the return journey.

7. In the 10th month of Shōtoku 4 (1714), when he went up to Edo for guard duty, he was appointed to permanent Edo duty (Edo jōzume) in the 1st month of the following year. He returned in the 4th month serving as escort, then went back up to Edo in the 9th month bringing his sons with him.
