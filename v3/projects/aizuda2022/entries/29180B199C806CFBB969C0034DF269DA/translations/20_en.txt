However, during the service, when the upper mansion was damaged by fire, he worked well and this was commended.

• In the third month of An'ei 2 (1773, Year of the Snake), he was relieved of his duties as a page, was made into a proper heir, and was ordered to practice horsemanship. It was announced that he would continue to receive a five-person stipend as before.

• From the fifth month of the same year, he was ordered to serve as an attendant in the same manner as a stable superintendent.

• In the third month of the third year (Year of the Horse), because he served diligently during the lord's stay in the domain, he was granted two bundles of cotton as a reward.

• In the same month, when his father went up to Edo for guard duty service as an attendant, since his father had been ill for a long time before departure, he was granted permission to accompany him to look after him during the journey. While staying together with his father, he was ordered to serve not only during formal dress occasions but also when attending to guests' horses.
　　　However, in the fourth month, since his father's illness was completely cured, he returned home.

• In the fifth month of the fourth year (Year of the Sheep), he was granted a hereditary stipend of 100 koku without change, and his father Jōemon had served as an opponent for several years...
