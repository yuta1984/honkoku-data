- In the first month of Shotoku 4, year of the horse (1714), at the age of fifteen, he was granted eight ryo of gold and stipend for four retainers, and was summoned to serve as a page (okosho)

- In the fourth month of the same year, he went up to Edo for advance guard duty (osaki-ban)

- In the tenth month of the same year, due to illness, he requested and was granted permission to return to the domain

- In the sixth month of Kyoho 1, year of the monkey (1716), he went up to Edo for advance guard duty

- In the fourth month of Kyoho 2, year of the rooster (1717), he returned from advance guard duty

- In the first month of Kyoho 3, year of the dog (1718), he was granted semi-coming-of-age ceremony (han-genpuku) as requested

- In the fourth month of the same year, he went up to Edo for rear guard duty (oato-ban)

- In the fourth month of Kyoho 4, year of the boar (1719), he returned from rear guard duty

- In the fourth month of Kyoho 5, year of the rat (1720), he went up to Edo for advance guard duty

- In the second month of Kyoho 6, year of the ox (1721), he was granted full coming-of-age ceremony (hon-genpuku) and was ordered to continue serving as a page as before

- In an unspecified month of the same year, he was appointed as temporary attendant for the lord's meal service during travels (omichi gozenshoku kariyaku) and accompanied the lord on his return to the domain

- In the first month of Kyoho 7, year of the tiger (1722), he was appointed as meal attendant (gozenshoku)

- In the fourth month of the same year, he served as attendant during the lord's journey to Edo

- In the same period, he was granted a monthly stipend increase of one ryo of gold

- In the fourth month of Kyoho 8, year of the rabbit (1723), he returned from advance guard duty

- In the fourth month of Kyoho 9, year of the dragon (1724), he served as attendant and went up to Edo

- In the second month of Kyoho 10, year of the snake (1725), he was granted Takekoshi Hyakusuke's residence
