Therefore, he accompanied [the lord] and served during the new funeral ceremonies. Afterward, he took turns with Saigō Tanomo, Hyūga Saburōemon, and Nakazawa Rokuemon, serving at Mount Mine for twenty days each.

1. In the fifth month of the second year of the Tiger (Enpō 2, 1674), in special consideration of his advanced age, he was granted permission to use a palanquin within Aizu domain.

1. In the eighth month of the third year of the Rabbit (Enpō 3, 1675), he was assigned duties in connection with the relocation ceremony of the shrine at Mount Mine. For this occasion, as Yoshikawa Koretari came down from Edo, he was ordered to arrange the hospitality, and they all traveled back and forth to Inawashiro.

1. In the ninth month of the same year, he was granted prepared crane that had been shot with the lord's rifle.

1. On the fourth day of the first month of the fourth year of the Dragon (Enpō 4, 1676), he died of illness at age sixty-eight. His posthumous spiritual name was Sadahiko Reisha, and he was buried at Mount Ōkubo.
　　　However, since he had previously learned and received transmission of Shinto practices from Yoshikawa Koretari, he had requested a burial site at Mount Ōkubo in advance. After his death, the funeral rites were conducted according to Shinto practices, and his spiritual name was also bestowed by Koretari.
