　　　Genealogy of Niwa Orinojō《Surname: Sakanoue｜Home Province: Owari》　《Family crest: Two horizontal lines in circle｜Alternative crest: Character "Yoshi" in circle or coiled dragon｜Shogunal service crest: same as above》

Niwa Kanemon Munesada (eldest son of Niwa Kantarō Yoshimune)　《Original name: Hansaburō｜Born in Keichō 14, year of the rooster (1609)》
　　Mother: daughter of Ōhashi Zenemon [name unknown]　《Died of illness on 9th month, 17th day of Kan'ei 3, year of the tiger (1626), Buddhist name: Tenshitsu Myōyū Shinnyo, later changed to Shinto with spirit name: Chōkei-hime Shinrei, burial place: unknown》
　　　Shogunal retainer
　　Wife: Matsu, daughter of Tamura Suketarō Nagatake　《Born in Genna 3, year of the snake (1617), died of illness on 4th month, 18th day of Jōkyō 1, year of the rat (1684) at age 68, Buddhist name: Jushōin Matsuganiritei Shinnni, burial place: Kōganji Temple on Mt. Seidō, later changed to Shinto with spirit name: Tayo-hime Shinrei》

　　[This family] are descendants of Sakanoue no Tamuramaro, and formerly used the surname Tamura, but in medieval times
　　when they came to reside in Niwa District of Owari Province, they changed their name to reflect their place of residence
