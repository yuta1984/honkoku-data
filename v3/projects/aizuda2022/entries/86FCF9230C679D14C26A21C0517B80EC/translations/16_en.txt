Gōemon Naoharu (originally named Hanya, then Munenao, born in Jōō 2, year of the boar) - adopted son of Tamaru Gōemon (retainer of Lord Nanbu Daizen-no-Dayu)
　Mother: Same as above

Gonshichirō (born Manji 3, year of the rat; died of illness on the 26th day of the 11th month of Jōkyō 3, year of the tiger, at age 27; Buddhist name Zenzan Jōhon Zen-jōmon; burial place same as above; later changed to Shinto with memorial spirit name Jōhiko Shinrei)
　Mother: Same as above

Daughter Tora (born Enpō 1, year of the ox; died of illness on the 12th day of the 7th month of Enpō 2, year of the tiger, at age 2; Buddhist name Sō-dōjo; burial place same as above; later changed to Shinto with memorial spirit name Orihime Shinrei)
　Mother: Daughter of the Nakane family

Kushichirō (born Enpō 2, year of the tiger; died of illness on the 22nd day of the 9th month of the same year; Buddhist name Gen-shū-dōjo [possibly an error for "dōshi"]; burial place same as above; later changed to Shinto with memorial spirit name Chise-hiko Shinrei)
　Mother: Same as above

Sukenosuke Munemichi (originally named Tatsunosuke, born Enpō 4, year of the dragon)
　Mother: Same as above
- Genroku 3, year of the horse: As requested by his father, he was granted his first formal audience (omemie)
- Genroku 13, year of the dragon, 27th day of the 11th month: Died of illness at age 25; Buddhist name Eiin Sōchi Shinji; burial place same as above; later changed to Shinto with memorial spirit name Chihiko Shinrei
