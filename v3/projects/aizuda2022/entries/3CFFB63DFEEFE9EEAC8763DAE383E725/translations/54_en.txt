Since the time of Hattori Ankyū, he has faithfully served in all shrine duties, and in recognition of the special status of the shrine, he was granted 100 koku from his late father's fief and appointed as chief priest of Minemiyama Shrine.

1. In the 10th month of Genbun 5 (1740), he went up to Edo and studied Shinto theology under Yoshikawa Genjūrō, received the transmission, and returned.

1. In the 11th month of the same year, his request to change his name to Kurando was granted.

1. In the 1st month of Enkyō 1 (1744), he was given two silver pieces for performing prayers for safe childbirth the previous winter, and two silver pieces for performing prayers for the healthy growth of Lord Kyōtei, presented at the chamberlain's office.

1. In the 11th month of the same year, he was granted a crown bestowed by Lord Doitsu.

1. In the 10th month of Enkyō 3 (1746), he was given 300 hiki of gold as congratulatory money for Lord Kansōin.
