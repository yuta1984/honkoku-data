One: Kyōhō 14, year of the Rooster (1729), 5th month - Since his father Geki had served diligently and performed well, although [the son] was still young, it was announced that he would be granted an audience with the lord, and he was granted his first audience.

One: Kanpō 3, year of the Boar (1743), 6th month - While still a dependent living in the family residence (heya-zumi), he was granted a fief of 200 koku and appointed to the position of messenger (oshiban).

One: Enkyō 1, year of the Rat (1744), 3rd month - He went up [to Edo] as part of the lord's retinue for guard duty and returned in the 10th month of the same year.
　　However, as requested by his father, since he served with his own men and horses both in the domain and in Edo, it was announced that while in Edo he would be treated as cavalry class, receiving stipends, horse allowances, and miscellaneous expenses equivalent to those of a 300-koku retainer.

One: Same year, 10th month - He was appointed to the position of chief announcer (osōjaban) and granted an additional stipend of 100 koku.

One: Same 2nd year, year of the Ox (1745), 3rd month - He went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in the 10th month of the following year of the Tiger.

One: Same 4th year, year of the Rabbit (1747), 9th month - He went up to Edo for guard duty and returned in the 3rd month of the following year of the Dragon.
