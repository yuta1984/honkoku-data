• Enpō 4, Year of the Dragon (1676), 10th month: Went up to Edo for guard duty service, returning in the 4th month of the following Year of the Snake.

• Same 6th year, Year of the Horse (1678), 12th month: Was granted the hereditary stipend of 250 koku without any changes.

• Same 7th year, Year of the Sheep (1679), 4th month: When the head Jinbo Mozaemon was relieved of his duties, he was transferred to Ibuka Shirōbei's unit as successor.

• Tennā 2, Year of the Dog (1682), 6th month: When the head Ibuka Shirōbei was ordered into exile, he was transferred to Kanno Gonbei's unit as successor.

• Genroku 4, Year of the Sheep (1691), 10th month: When appointed as inspector (metsuke), both his legs were in pain and he could not properly perform the inspector duties, so he did not formally accept the appointment and apologized, whereupon he was relieved of the position as requested.

• Same 14th year, Year of the Snake (1701), 6th month: As requested, his son Kurōemon was appointed as guard replacement.

• Kyōhō 3, Year of the Dog (1718), 11th month, 13th day: Died of illness at age 69. Buddhist posthumous name: Yū'yo Kakuen Koji. Burial site: same as above (Jōan-ji Temple on Mt. Kodate).
