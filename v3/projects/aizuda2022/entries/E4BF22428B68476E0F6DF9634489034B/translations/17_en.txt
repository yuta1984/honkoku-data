- In the leap month of June of the same year, his son Shinjūrō was appointed as his substitute for guard duty as requested.

- In June of Hōreki 2 (1752), because his son Shinjūrō had been ill for a long time, he (the father) was ordered to return to active duty as requested and served castle guard duty.

- In January of Hōreki 5 (1755), considering that he had become aged and found it difficult to perform guard duties, he petitioned to resign from guard duty by submitting the koshugi stipend, and his request was granted.

- In September of Hōreki 10 (1760), because his son Shinjūrō had completely recovered from his illness, he petitioned to have his son appointed as his substitute for guard duty, and this was granted.

- On the 5th day of the same month, he died of illness at age 67. His Buddhist name was Kakuyo Tokushin Koji, and his burial place was the same as mentioned above.
