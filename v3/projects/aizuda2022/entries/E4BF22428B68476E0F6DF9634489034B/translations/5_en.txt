On the 25th day of the 12th month of Keichō 4 (1599), year of the Boar, he died of illness at the age of over seventy (his Buddhist name was Saikan Zentaku Koji, burial place unknown). At Masanao's deathbed, by imperial command, his second son Magoemon Masatsugu was ordered to succeed him (his descendants served as direct retainers of the shogun, and were called Magotarō or Magoemon through the generations). The eldest son Masahiro had been wandering in various places, but when Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) marched to Takatenjin in Tōtōmi Province, he joined under the banner of Ōsuka Gorozaemon-sama. When climbing over the castle walls, he was shot by two bullets but his life was not in danger.

Since Gongen-sama was near his direct retainers, he witnessed this and asked "Who is that?" When Gorozaemon-sama replied "His name is Magoemon," he said "Tend to that man." Afterwards, Gorozaemon-sama ordered someone called Kondō Busuke to draw his sword and remove the bullet lodged near Masahiro's left ear, whereupon they found another bullet inside his helmet.
