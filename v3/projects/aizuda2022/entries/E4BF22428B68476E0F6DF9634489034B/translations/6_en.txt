This sword had saved his life, so it was given to him with the words "return to service again and achieve even greater military honors." 《One of the bullets is kept by the Otake Magotaro family》《One was kept in the house but was lost in the great fire during the Kanbun era》

After that, he was not pardoned from his lord's displeasure for a long time, so he temporarily served Oda Nobunaga, and later served Torii Hikouemon Mototada as a guest retainer. During the Tensho era, he also participated in campaigns at Kurokoma in Koshu, Iwatsuki in Musashi, and Nanbu in Oshu, achieving military honors repeatedly. Because of this, Lord Mototada showed him much compassion and favor, but while he was in service, he died of illness on March 25th of Genna 3rd year.

《Buddhist name: Osen Koshin Koji, burial place unknown. Later a grave was erected at Kogan-ji Temple on Mt. Seido》He had three children: eldest son Magouemon Masamichi, second son Gozaemon Masashige, and one daughter. Masashige served Gongen-sama (Tokugawa Ieyasu) and was granted 200 koku. During the Summer Campaign of Osaka in the first year of Genna, he accompanied his lord and achieved military merit, so in the following year (Year of the Dragon) in January, he was granted an additional 200 koku. Later he served Mito Chunagon Yorifusa and was granted 1000 koku, serving as commander of archers (mochiyumi-gashira).
