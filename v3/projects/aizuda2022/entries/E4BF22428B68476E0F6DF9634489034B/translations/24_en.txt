- In the 10th month of the same year,
　　he transmitted one matter to Lord Kyōtei, delivering the oral instructions as a substitute lecturer for Lord Kikkawa Genjiurō, and the completion banquet proceeded without delay, for which he was privately granted two pieces of silver.

- In the 2nd month of the 10th year [Kansei 10, 1798], at the request of Lord Kikkawa Tominosuke, he went up to Edo to discuss matters of Shinto with Tominosuke,

　　as ordered, going up in the form of regular duty service and returning in the 9th month of the same year.
- In the 4th month of the 12th year [Kansei 12, 1800], at the request of Lord Kikkawa Tominosuke, he was ordered to go up in the same manner as before to engage in discussions on Shinto matters, but while he was scheduled to return in the 9th month of the same year, he was detained and returned in the 1st month of the following year.

- In the 10th month of Kyōwa 1 (1801),
　　he transmitted two matters to Lord Kyōtei, delivering the oral instructions as substitute lecturer for Lord Kikkawa Tominosuke, and the completion banquet proceeded without delay.
