　- In the 8th month of the 2nd year [Kyōwa 2, 1802], having devoted his heart to Shinto and Japanese learning for many years,
　　　performing various duties including lectures before his lord, and at the request of the Kikkawa family, going up to Edo for the return transmission of Shinto teachings, and furthermore successfully training many students, he was granted a stipend of two-person support as official salary and summoned to service.
　　　　However, he was given the official title of Shinto instructor, with his seat ranking placed below Uchida Makitarō and above Urakami Kijirō.
