He was ordered to make arrangements for the benefit of his lord.

Ninth year (of the era), year of the Snake, second month: Since he served diligently on ordinary days and had perfect attendance for four years, he was privately granted one complete set of old hemp formal dress (kamishimo) as a reward.

Same month: When he was appointed to economizing duties, since particularly significant reductions were observed, he was granted three pieces of silver as a reward.

Sixth month of the same year: He was appointed to duties overseeing construction work within the castle and on official lands, as well as kitchen affairs, tea ceremony matters, bird-keeping duties, and all economizing measures for various quarters and expenditures. For matters that he and Kajiwara Keizo jointly arranged and procured, they were to request approval from the appropriate departments and also report to the chief retainers, and were ordered to make arrangements for the lord's benefit.

Ninth month of the same year: This time, Lord Kyotei made a formal request and went up to Edo, but it was announced that he would be left in charge during his lord's absence until next spring.

Tenth year, year of the Horse, fifth month: He was granted family succession with a stipend of 150 koku without change, and was ordered to continue serving as a page (kosho) as he had until now.
