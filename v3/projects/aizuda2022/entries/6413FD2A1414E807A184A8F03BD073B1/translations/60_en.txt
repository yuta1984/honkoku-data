In the 2nd month of Anei 1 (1772): Due to a fire that started from Meguro Gyōninzaka, when the upper residence was damaged by the spreading fire, he had been stationed in advance at the Shiba residence, but since all his clothing and household goods were completely burned, making it difficult to continue his duties, he was granted leave until he could prepare new belongings and returned to Aizu. In the 8th month of the same year, having completed his preparations, he returned to Edo.

In the 5th month of Anei 6 (1777): He was appointed as a regular provisional dining attendant (gozenban teikeyaku).

In the 12th month of the same year: He was granted the former residence site of Momozawa Dan'emon in Suishu-machi.

In the 12th month of Anei 9 (1780): He was granted the former residence site of Yokoyama Kazuma in Yoneyo 2-chōme.

In the 12th month of Tenmei 4 (1784): In connection with the presentation of winter ducks to the shogun, he was appointed as a domain messenger and served as an envoy to both castle compounds (Honmaru and Nishinomaru), beginning with the senior councilors, young elders, colleagues of equal rank, and various other houses.

In the 10th month of Tenmei 5 (1785): He was appointed to serve Lord Hoshina Echizen-no-kami, and was subsequently appointed to serve Lord Akita Shinano-no-kami, Lord Mizoguchi Izumo-no-kami, Lord Katō Tōtōmi-no-kami, Lord Kuki Shikibushōyū, and Lord Ōzeki Iori.
