He died on the 6th of an unknown month and was buried at the same temple.

1. Twelfth month of Meireki 1 (1655)
   He was summoned as a page (kosho) to Lord Dotsū (Hoshina Masayuki) at age fourteen,
   and was granted a stipend of eight ryō and provisions for five people.

1. Eighth month of Manji 2 (1659), he was appointed as御膳番 (gozenban - meal attendant) and served as an inner page (oku-kosho). In the twelfth month of the same year, he received an additional stipend of two ryō.

1. Third month of the same 3rd year (1660), he was ordered to perform the coming-of-age ceremony (hair-raising), and was also ordered to assist with ceremonial dress. He practiced under Ōishi Ne Gohei to fulfill these duties.

1. Kanbun 2 (1662), month unknown
   When Lord Dotsū's eye disease was being treated at Kakagyokuin temple, he was ordered to assist during the treatment period and fulfilled his duties.

1. Fourth month of the same year
   Because he worked diligently during Lord Dotsū's illness, he received two summer robes as a reward from Lady Hōshōin.

1. Twelfth month of the same year, for his service during the aforementioned treatment period, he received an additional stipend of two ryō in gold, and furthermore was granted three ryō in gold as a special reward.
