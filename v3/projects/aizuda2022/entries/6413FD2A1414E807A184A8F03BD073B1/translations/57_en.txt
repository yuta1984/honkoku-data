In the third year of Kyōhō, third month (1718):
When Lord Sesshōin went up to Edo, he was appointed as temporary attendant guard and served one term of guard duty in Edo before returning.

In the tenth year of the same era, twelfth month (1725):
He was appointed as a Inawashiro samurai.

In the second year of Hōreki, ninth month (1752):
His request to adopt Tetsuzō, the second son of Ban Iori, as his heir was granted.

In the ninth year of the same era, intercalary seventh month (1759):
His request to return his adopted son Tetsuzō to the Iori family due to the latter's recent poor health was granted.

In the same year, ninth month:
His request to adopt Iwasaburō, the third son of Onoda Sukezaemon, as his heir was granted.

In the tenth year of the same era, first day of the ninth month (1760):
He died of illness at the age of 73. His posthumous Buddhist name was Jōtai-in Jūyo Shudō Koji, and he was buried at Ganseishōji Temple on Mount Kanō.
