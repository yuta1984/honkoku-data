On that day, due to bad weather and poor road conditions, his service as an attendant was particularly arduous, so he was granted sake as a reward.

In the sixth month of the same year, during the lord's return journey to the domain, it was announced that he had served without absence as an attendant and intermediate officer during the journey, and had also performed duties as御次番 (escort guard) and御戸番 (gate guard).

In the eleventh month of the same year, it was privately announced that he had served diligently during the lord's illness.

In the third month of the third year of An'ei (1774), he went up to Edo as a rear guard attendant for the lord's journey to the capital.
　Note: From this time forward, he served as both advance guard and rear guard attendant for every such journey.

In the twelfth month of the same year, it was privately announced that he had diligently performed his duties in direct service to the lord.
　Note: Thereafter, he was repeatedly commended for his diligent performance of these duties.

In the second month of the fifth year of An'ei (1776), he was appointed as an apprentice御膳番 (meal service attendant).

In the third month of the sixth year of An'ei (1777), he was granted one used formal jacket from the lord's wardrobe as a reward from the御納戸 (wardrobe office).

In the fifth month of the same year, he was appointed as a temporary御膳番 (meal service attendant).
　Note: He was repeatedly appointed to this position thereafter.
