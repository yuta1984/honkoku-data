He served as a page (kosho) in Aizu with a stipend of 300 koku and worked as a construction magistrate (fushin-bugyō). When Lord Shikibu-shōyū Akinari fell from power, he became a rōnin and withdrew to live in that village. His surname at that time is unknown. After retirement, he shaved his head and became a monk. Although he supposedly possessed genealogical records and such, they were lost during the Kyōhō era, according to oral tradition only. The spear that this Sōgen possessed bears the signature of Hizen Kanehiro and is still in the family's possession today.

1. During the Jōō era, he was summoned to serve as a scribe at the town office and served in various roles for over fifty years, but since no service records have been passed down, the details remain unclear.

1. He died of illness on the 29th day of the 8th month of Hōei 4, Year of the Boar, at the age of over eighty. His Buddhist name was Kōgakuin Shakushu Ichiō Sōhaku Koji, buried at the same place as mentioned above.
