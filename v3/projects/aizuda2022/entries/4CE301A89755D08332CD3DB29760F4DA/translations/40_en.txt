His stipend was reduced by eight koku and two-person rice allowance, he was summoned as assistant physician, and appointed to both gairyo (external medicine) and hondo (internal medicine) departments.

- Same year, fourth month: His seat was placed above Hoshino Un'i

- Same year, sixteenth year Kinoto-i (1731), fourth month: Traveled up to Edo to welcome his lord on the journey down from Edo

- Genbun 2, Tsuchinoto-mi year (1737), eleventh month: Appointed to dokurei (independent audience) status

- Same year 4, Tsuchinoto-hitsuji year (1739), third month: Appointed as御番医 (attending physician on duty)

- Kanpo 1, Tsuchinoto-tori year (1741), ninth month: Appointed to accompany his lord on the journey up to Kyoto

- Enkyo 1, Kinoe-ne year (1744) (month unclear): When Lady Jushin-in had a breast tumor, he provided medical treatment and was commended for his excellent service

- Kan'en 3, Tsuchinoe-uma year (1750), eleventh month: When Lord Kyotei succeeded as head of the family, he was granted 300 pieces of gold as celebration money

- Horeki 1, Tsuchinoto-hitsuji year (1751), sixth month: When the domain inspectors came down from Edo, he was appointed for related duties
