His wife was the daughter of his adoptive father Buhachirō Chikayoshi.

- In Hōreki 2 (1752), 12th month, three koku were reduced from his predecessor's assessed income, and he was granted twelve koku and three-person stipend, and summoned to the Aizu Koban.

- In Meiwa 4 (1767), 9th month, as requested, he went to Yamagata for guard duty, and in the 3rd month of the following year of the Rat, he completed the transfer duties and returned to the domain.

- In the 12th month of the same year, due to his diligent service, he was granted an increase of three koku.

- In the 6th year (1769), 10th month of the Ox, he was granted the former residence of Shimizu Hachirōji in Kazuko-machi.

- In Tenmei 1 (1781), 9th month of the Ox, he requested and was granted permission to adopt Hideyuki, the second son of Kuroda Sōhachi, as his son-in-law and heir.

- In the 5th year (1785), 8th month, he was appointed as magistrate (daikan).

- In the 10th month of the same year, he was assigned a rank below the chief inspector, above the personal attendants, and above Nishimura Kōsuke.

- In Kansei 5 (1793), 5th month of the Ox, [he was assigned] to handle commoner affairs...
