He was assigned to the no-duty group (muyaku-gumi).

- In the 4th year (Shōtoku 4, 1714), 2nd month, when he petitioned to be appointed to a group assignment (kumitsuki), he was told to remain as he was.

- In the 5th year (Shōtoku 5, 1715), 3rd month, after petitioning again, he was appointed to an external group assignment (tozama-kumitsuki), entered Ueda Hachirōemon's group, and served castle guard duty.

- In Kyōhō 1 (1716), 9th month, 11th day, he died of illness at age 55. His Buddhist name was Shingen-shin-in Yuikan Nissei. Burial place: same as above.

Zensuke [a certain person]  《Initially Yasaburō, later Chōtarō, later Tamiya》
　Actually the second son of Koizumi Tsuneemon [a certain person]

- In Hōei 1 (1704), 2nd month, while staying at his birth family, he was granted the standard stipend of 8 ryō and support for 4 people, and was called to serve as page (koshō).

- In Shōtoku 2 (1712), 7th month, as petitioned, he was appointed as adopted son and was to continue serving as page as before.
