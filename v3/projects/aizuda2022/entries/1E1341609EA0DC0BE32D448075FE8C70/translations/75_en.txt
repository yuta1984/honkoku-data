In the seventh month of Hōreki 1 (Mi year), he petitioned to change his name to Seizaemon, and this was granted.
In the ninth month of the same year, he went up to Edo for guard duty, and in the twelfth month of the following year was appointed 御徒支配 (go-ashigaru-shihai, commander of foot soldiers), returning home in the third month.
　As per custom, he was granted one roll of bleached cloth as a substitute for formal court dress, and this continued to be granted for each subsequent guard duty assignment.
In the ninth month of the third year (Tori), he went up for Edo guard duty, returning home in the third month of the following year (Inu).
In the ninth month of the seventh year (Ushi), he went up to Edo for guard duty, returning home in the third month of the following year (Tora).
　As he had diligently performed four tours of guard duty without incident, he was granted a formal kimono as a substitute for the usual reward.
In the ninth month of the ninth year (U), he went up to Edo for guard duty, and in the third month of the following year (Tatsu) accompanied the lord on his journey to Kyoto.
