1. In the ninth month of Kyōhō 4 (1719), he was appointed as kumigashira (group leader).

1. In the third month of Kyōhō 5 (1720), he was relieved of his position as kumigashira and appointed as the head of a group that required no expenses.

1. In the tenth month of the same year, he went to Edo for guard duty, and in the fourth month of the following year of the Ox (Kyōhō 6, 1721), he served as kibauuchi (mounted attendant) in the lord's retinue and returned home.

1. In the second month of Kyōhō 7 (1722), he was appointed as monogashira (military officer).

1. In the fourth month of Kyōhō 8 (1723), he went to Edo for guard duty. From that month until the seventh month of the same year, he served as acting otoritsugu (intermediary) at the reception hall of the Mita residence. Afterward, he received a set of formal court dress from Lord Shōsenin, and returned home in the tenth month of the same year.

1. In the fourth month of Kyōhō 14 (1729), he went to Edo for guard duty and returned home in the tenth month of the same year.

1. In the second month of Kyōhō 17 (1732), his request to adopt his younger brother Shichizaemon as his heir was granted.

1. In the twelfth month of Kyōhō 19 (1734), as requested, his son Shichizaemon was appointed as obandai (guard substitute).

1. In the third month of Genbun 5 (1740), his request for retirement was granted as wished.
