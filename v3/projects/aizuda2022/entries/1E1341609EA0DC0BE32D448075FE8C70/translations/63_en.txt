First, in the 6th month of the same year: He was summoned before his lord, and his diligent service over two years was acknowledged with gracious words. He was granted leave to return to Aizu and received a kosode (short-sleeved kimono) as a customary parting gift.

First, in the 9th year of Genroku, 7th month (1696): He was granted one piece of hardship money.

First, in the 10th year of Genroku, 3rd month (1697): He went up to Edo for duty rotation and returned in the 9th month of the same year. However, during his tour of duty, he was granted one set of formal court dress.

First, year and month unknown: In recent years, his memory had become poor and he was additionally incompetent in his duties, so he requested to resign from his official position. However, he was ordered that his resignation would not be accepted.
