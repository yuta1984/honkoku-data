The Cuckoo
Director Gosho Heinosuke's full-scale representative work of this year
The mountains of Ikaho in early spring were wrapped in the quiet beauty of fresh greenery...
The newlyweds Kawasaki Takeo and his wife Namiko spent particularly happy days together, strolling in the shade of trees, whispering under the moonlight, with each day passing more quickly than expected. However, this peaceful newlywed life was disrupted by the appearance of Chijiiwa Yasuhiko. Despite his pure and lovely wife Namiko's devoted care, he courageously yet reluctantly departs on a ship bound for Marshall Islands patrol duty...
Among the many film adaptations of "The Cuckoo," this definitively claims the throne - Kamata's pride, a special production film with a stellar main cast, production staff, and an all-star cast in this Shochiku Kinema deluxe edition...
Chojiro, who starred in "The Golden Demon," once again creates a contemporary film for Kamata movies. The director is Kamata's sole romantic director, Gosho Heinosuke. Furthermore, his first co-starring role with the cheerful Kawasaki Hiroko, filmed with fresh locations in Ikaho, Izu, and Atami.
