◇Grand Public Opening of Luxurious Shochiku Period Films Coming Soon◇
Now the three great masters of Shochiku Cinema period films have completed their most devoted works…………The three great films of mystery, interest, and chivalry that will cause ripples in the film world's grand finale are coming - behold the refreshing spirit and gallant formation, with our greatest expectations……………

Nezumi Kozo Jirokichi [Second Part] - Kyoto Shimogamo Special Production Film
・Original work by Osaragi Jiro・Serialized in Kodan Club magazine・
・Starring Hayashi Chojiro in dual roles・Special appearance by Onoe Eigoro・
Appearing and disappearing like a ghost!! What happens next in the story of the phantom thief Nezumi Kozo who continues his superhuman leaps within Edo's security network……The strange tale of Jirokichi and Kotani Shinkuro, cornered in the dark tenements as crisis approaches, Shinkuro raging on the rooftop, Nezumi Kozo appearing and disappearing at will on the ground, and the great leap of ronin Tachimi Yuichiro

Chivalrous Yakuza Way - Shochiku Cinema Kyoto Shimogamo Period Film
・Original story and screenplay by Suimon Okichi・Directed by Hirose Goro・
・Starring Bando Kotaro・Co-starring Iizuka Toshiko………・
In the world of traveling gamblers bound by duty and human sentiment, Kitaro of Nanao burns with chivalrous tears and the blood of gallant spirit in this endlessly thrilling sword film. Original work by Suimon Okichi's specialty yakuza stories, directed by Hirose Goro, starring new star Bando Kotaro and co-starring Iizuka Toshiko in this period drama.

Muto Taheita - Ichikawa Utaemon Productions Super-Special Period Drama
・Original work by Sasaki Mitsuzō・Published in Sunday Mainichi Special Issue・
・Co-starring Ichikawa Utaemon and Oe Michiko…………………・
Combined with the bored man who appeared in Sendai, this masterpiece of Utaemon films based on Sasaki Mitsuzō's original work and directed by Shirai Sentaro………Serialized and written in Sunday Mainichi magazine, this grand period film draws its material from the turbulent light and shadow of the Meiji Restoration and the end of the Edo period.
