【Right Page】
The "Jiwen Leiju Qianji" (Classified Collection of Literary Matters, First Series) states that on the Dragon Boat Festival, sweet flag is used as hat cords or hair ornaments, or crushed finely and floated in wine. Also today, women playfully insert sweet flag in their hair and wrap it around their waist. It is customarily said that doing so will ward off various illnesses. The "Suiji Zaji" (Miscellaneous Records of Seasonal Events) states that on the day of the Dragon Boat Festival, sweet flag and mugwort are carved into small human figures, or made into gourd-like shapes, and wearing these will repel evil spirits. Also, the "Wang Yigong Tiezi" states: "Tomorrow morning, knowing this is the festival of the heavenly center, I will carve sweet flag to ward off evil spirits."

【Left Page】
It is recorded that hanging mugwort and sweet flag above doors on the fifth day of the fifth month will dispel poisonous vapors. Also, sweet flag wine is said to support positive energy and extend one's lifespan. Based on these principles, the practice of attaching mugwort and sweet flag to everyone from mounted attendants down to servants and pack-horse handlers may have this meaning.

　　　Regarding Swords
One: Concerning the use of leather scabbard tips for large swords. Fundamentally, large swords are primarily worn in pure places such as before shrines and temples.
