《Marginal note: On the 5th day, when the shoshi becomes senior in age to the betto, the shoshi takes the superior position [in red ink]》

1. Seating arrangement below the steps: On the 5th day, the betto-dayū, shoshi-dayū, and mokudai take their positions. However, when the shoshi-dayū becomes senior in age to the betto, the shoshi takes the superior position. In Kōji 3rd year (1557), the Ōmi-no-kami as shoshi took the superior position. However, on the 1st day, the betto-dayū takes the western seat.

1. When it rains, both left and right sides bring up horses from the horse grounds as in the preliminary selection on the 1st day. There is a saying that "six hangs" at the western eaves of the imperial pavilion.
   In Tenbun 15th year, year of the horse (1546), during the time of shoshi Kumachiyodayū Ujiyori's tenure, when Hyōbu-shōyū Takahira served, Yamamoto Minbu-no-jō Ujishige expressed the opinion that it was thus in ancient times, and this practice began from that year. The shrine administrator was Takeuchi Sanmi Akihisa-kyō.

1. On the 1st day, the riders: those passing through the southern pillars of the imperial pavilion pay respects to the shrine administrator; those at the western cut lawn are for audience and announcement to the shoshi-dayū. This is not a formal ceremony.
《Marginal note: Regarding notification when the imperial horse is changed on the 5th day [in red ink]》

1. When the imperial horse is changed on the 5th day, first there is notification from the horse office to the negi office. Also, from the negi office there is notification to the twenty riders and the shoshi.
