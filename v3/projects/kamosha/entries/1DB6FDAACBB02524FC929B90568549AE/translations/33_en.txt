At that time the weather was clear. On the 1st day, Suga-no-miya's party made an imperial visit, and with the attendance of magistrate Itakura Suō-no-kami, viewing stands were erected west of Kokyōsho. On the 5th day there was no imperial visit.
《Marginal note: Regarding Suga-no-miya's imperial visit and viewing》【Red ink】

Shigemochi notes that according to a certain record, on both the 1st and 5th days of the 5th month of Kan'ei 19th year, the Crown Prince 《Marginal note: Later Emperor Go-Kōmyō》 made an imperial progress to Kamo Shrine for the purpose of viewing the horse races. The route passed through Nakā-ōji road and arrived at the first torii, where viewing stands were provided west of Kokyōsho at the riding area. His Majesty viewed the ceremony of offering sacred objects, and the imperial seat was in the eastern room of the imperial registry building.

Shōhō 1st year 《Marginal note: The era name was changed on the 16th day of the 12th month of Kan'ei 21st year to become Shōhō 1st year》
《Marginal note: After the deputy wrote an entry in the April Ōmawari, the horse riding could not be accomplished》【Red ink】

This year, Hyōgo-no-suke Nariai was the deputy. He had formally submitted a written entry for the April Ōmawari but forgot about it and accepted responsibility for the tenth group's riding. When it came to the middle appointment, it suddenly became impossible, so there was no rider until the final selection. However, since there was no precedent for the deputy to ride, he made a formal report. It was evident that if he had made the entry for Ōmawari, it would not have been possible.

《Marginal note: Because Yasusuke went into light mourning, Yasuka rode in his place》【Red ink】

Mino-no-kami Yasusuke suddenly went into light mourning on the 3rd day, so in his place Umano-jō Yasuka (Yasukazu was later changed to Yasuka) rode. He was one rank below Seigen. However, following precedent, without changing the seating arrangement, he rode as Mino-no-kami's substitute. This was an example of riding while wearing purification robes.
