The three monks Onsei-dō, Kunsei-dō, and Teisei-dō, who were involved in the wealth matter, were henceforth prohibited from coming to the palace and attending memorial services.

29th day, Mizunoe-Inu (Water Dog): Rain. Heavy mourning ends today.

Sixth Month
1st day, Mizunoto-I (Water Boar): Clear. Early morning, for the end of mourning, I untied my topknot, bathed, and hung sacred ropes at the gate. I completed the household purification ritual as usual. I offered first fruits and ceremonial meals to the main shrine's eight shrines, subsidiary shrines, Ebisu-Daikoku deities, and Sanbō-Kōjin as usual, and performed worship. However, I refrained from shrine visits for both today and tomorrow. For court service, I remained in regular clothes without changing attire.

2nd day, Kinoe-Ne (Wood Rat): Clear and pleasant. Due to the end of mourning, I visited intimate acquaintances and neighbors in the local area to offer greetings.

3rd day, Kinoto-Ushi (Wood Ox): Clear. This morning, after the end of mourning, I visited the shrine, performed the ritual sequence at the tutelary deity shrine as usual, completed services up to Ōta Shrine, then visited Chigo Shrine.

5th day, Hinoto-U (Fire Rabbit): Clear. Due to the death of Kii Province's Great Retired Lord Taishin-den, I visited the Great Dowager's quarters for condolences, wearing formal hemp attire. He was the Great Dowager's father, aged eighty-five. As he was the Great Lord's great-grandfather, there is no temporary mourning. However, on the 2nd, upon hearing of this irregular situation, the lord sent his close attendant Murakami Yagara to Kii Wakayama for condolences. The 10th meeting of the family financing association was held at Okudaya as usual, going from half past the Snake hour (9:30 AM), finishing at the Rooster hour (6 PM), and returning home at the Boar hour (10 PM). For the first anniversary of the late Shūko on the coming 10th, I sent memorial gifts to about six households of higher rank in the local area.

7th day, Tsuchinoto-Mi (Earth Snake): Clear. Planning meeting for the family association.

8th day, Kanoe-Uma (Metal Horse): Clear. For the Osaka association meeting, retainer Okamoto and I took the noon boat down to Osaka today, arriving in the evening. Lodging as usual. Nakano had gone down to Osaka one or two days earlier and was staying at the same place.

9th day, Kanoto-Hitsuji (Metal Sheep): Clear. At Tennōji Ukise, there was the third meeting of the association with three ryō gold stakes, going from before noon and finishing at the Dog hour (8 PM).

10th day, Mizunoe-Saru (Water Monkey): Cloudy. At the same place as yesterday, there was the fourth meeting of the association with one bu gold stakes, going at noon and finishing at half past the Dog hour (8:30 PM).
