Yoaken, Enbo, and Hokaku sealed [the amulets], making a total of 100 pieces, receiving two pieces each.

- Today, Fukunishi completed the aoi paintings with brush and ink, so Hodo inscribed the aoi crests on both front and back. Since these sacred amulets were sealed, there was an evening meal today.

- Regarding the council custody box in Misei's care, one box was sent to Fushin's place, and after consulting about detailed examination, it was sent today. Hodo and Gakuken went for the sealing, with Hodo going to cut the pair. Moreover, both Kishu and Tetsuken attended and inquired together with Fushin. This concerns whether there might be any good documents regarding the current water rights dispute at Ide.

- Until the thunder protection amulets were completed, sake was offered on the platform of the council hall and the head priest recited prayers. Therefore ritual vestments were worn.

- Today is the vigil for the first anniversary memorial service for Uji■. Sweets, incense offerings, etc. were sent from Kyoto, and Suishinan came to visit.

- Today Hokaku brought a fir tree to be planted. The payment was 100 copper coins. Since it arrived around dusk last night, it was planted today. The hired worker was Isuke.

Fifth day, Day of the Rabbit - Clear

- Today is the farewell banquet for the Aoi Messenger's departure. The attendees for the evening meal at the council hall were as follows:
Aoi Messenger Hoshow, Sankō, Head Priest Masahisa, Monthly Duty Officer Naruhisa, Yoaken, Hoshika, Enbo, February duty officer, Council Head Kaneta (Hoshow serves concurrently), and six other officials.
Additionally, ■■ Fukunishi was invited because he was commissioned to write the aoi designation tablets this time.

The menu was as follows:
Grilled dishes, simmered and pickled crucian carp, soup (with fine-cut vegetables and kamaboko)
Raw fish salad (with ginger, jellyfish, ark shell), pickled vegetables, rice
