18th day, Mizunoto-Tori, clear weather: It was the palace storehouse ceremony. All matters proceeded as usual, with about 100 koku of rice being delivered.

19th day, Kinoe-Inu, clear weather: Tsunenori had been occasionally falling ill since some days ago, and yesterday afternoon became quite unwell, so Doctor Goto came early in the morning for an examination. Later, on the 1st day of the 7th month, Chiko was sent back in a palanquin, and the details were reported to Shinano-no-suke Yasufumi.
Consequently, afterward Michie submitted letters of apology, and through Yasufumi repeatedly apologized. Moreover, she went to Suzuki Rokuro of the imperial kitchen staff and similarly offered apologies, explaining everything in detail. However, as the Inokawa family would not agree at all, there was no recourse and inevitably the marriage was broken. As a result, the male child born this time was called an abandoned child, and today two men and one wet nurse from the Inokawa family came to take him back. Chiko's grief and tears were limitless - there was nothing to be done, it was truly pitiful. Although I had requested through Yasufumi that we send the child from our side, he replied that this would not be appropriate and Tsunechika would not agree, so they came to collect him unexpectedly this morning.
