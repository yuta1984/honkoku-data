1. Igarashi Sagoemon arrived at Misono-guchi around the hour of the Sheep (2 PM), wishing to meet with the representative immediately. When Kanemoto went out to meet him, [Igarashi] said: "Just now, orders have been given regarding stopping cattle and horses on the road up to now due to today's shrine relocation. The road I just traveled has been arranged, so please arrange for cattle and horses coming from the inner area to stop." Therefore, workers were sent to Nikken-chaya, and from Nikken-chaya, horses from the inner area were sent back, horses from the entrance were directed to areas where they would not cause obstruction, and other horses returning from Kyoto were directed to have people placed on the upper bank to lead them up the embankment.

1. Once the shrine relocation is completed, representative Yasukiyo alone shall promptly go to report to the Imperial messenger. The record is as follows:
