New Year's Day Inner Sanctuary Divine Offering Arrangement　Secret tradition, therefore recorded separately
First, the head priest (shinshū) and ritual assistant (hafuri) enter the inner sanctuary, make one bow before the sacred curtain stand, and place the large eight-legged stand on the sacred platform.

　　The above refers to vermillion lacquer sacred platforms (or trays) - two legs constantly present before the sacred curtain stand. The black lacquer large eight-legged stand is constantly at the southeast corner {{Note: length is｜north-south}}. After the head priest and ritual assistant make one bow, they carry it by hand and place it on the sacred platform.

Next, roll up the sacred curtain {{Note: after the shrine officials of the eight shrines finish bowing｜one ritual assistant rolls it up}}. Next, offer the divine offerings on the large eight-legged stand.
　　As seen in the diagram, especially the round trays are only for divine offerings held by the chief negi and assistant negi priests; the round trays used for these do not enter the inner sanctuary.
