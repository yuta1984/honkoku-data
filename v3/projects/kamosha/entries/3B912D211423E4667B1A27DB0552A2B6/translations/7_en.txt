Body purification《Note: Like the divine offerings, first the head priest, then the chief liturgist and the twenty-one ritual officials stand in order facing the yin-yang master to purify their bodies》. Next, one bow at Kataoka and Suwa shrines, then the liturgist faction crosses the jeweled bridge, enters the tower and middle gates, performs one prostration at Tanao Shrine, enters the middle gate and approaches the divine presence through the Chinese gate.

The ceremony involves first performing the lowering of hems and shoe-removing bow before the Chinese gate, then entering with the right foot first, ascending the Chinese gate, ascending to the transparent corridor, facing diagonally toward the divine presence for one prostration, advancing on knees to the round seat for a seated bow, then arranging the hem《Note: with left hand (or right hand?)》, then taking proper seat. However, the head priest sits facing south in the central transparent corridor between the sliding boards of the main authority hall, while the chief liturgist and below each sit in a row facing east with northern precedence.

Note that for annual divine ceremonies and below, the arrangement of round seats is the duty of the junior deputy according to the deputy's instructions.

The ritualist faction crosses the jeweled bridge, and when passing through the center, they bow, then one bow at Suwa and Kataoka shrines, pass through the connecting hall for one bow at Kawao Shrine, then enter the new shrine gate, first one bow at New Shrine, Fujio, and Yamao shrines, then enter the Chinese gate for one prostration at Wakamiya, then approach the divine presence from Wakamiya worship hall.

The ceremony involves first performing the lowering of hems and shoe-removing bow below the worship hall, entering the eastern connecting hall to face diagonally toward the divine presence for one prostration,

advancing on knees to the round seat for a seated bow, arranging hems, each facing south with western precedence. However, from the chief ritualist to the new shrine ritualist form one row facing south with western precedence, while from Ota ritualist to the clan deity ritualist face north with western precedence, because the connecting hall space is narrow.
