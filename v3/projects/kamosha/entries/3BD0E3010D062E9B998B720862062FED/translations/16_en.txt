　Fourth month section　　　　　　　
First day sacred festival　The shrine priests go to Kibune (during the journey, they wear formal court dress)
Both officials《marginal note: wear sokutai｜don summer garments》participate. The shrine priests wear court caps and robes, each with upper garments tied up.
When they come before the sacred presence, there is a ceremony of opening the sacred doors《marginal note: ceremonial attendants｜music is performed｜rhythm is beaten》
The sacred door opening is performed and sacred offerings are presented as usual. However,
this should be in a separate record. The details of this should be known thoroughly.
Going to the sacred interior and also before the sacred doors. The head priest makes congratulatory words. During this, the priest
takes the sacred streamers and makes two bows, then two more bows and advances them. When the congratulatory words end,
the sacred offerings are removed. The sacred interior's offering tables etc. are brought out, and each person's
offerings are presented and they return. In various ways, in the past even those of lower rank would come out,
but when the head priest left the sacred sword behind and presented it, there was an incident of cutting hands.
From that time, only the sacred offering tables are brought out. When things end and the shrine priests
withdraw, there is banzairaku music. In the past, after that, they would return and participate and

there would be wild dancing, but from the time of father Eibun, it was established to be done this way.
This is during the shrine administrator's time. The shrine priests in formal dress go to their seats at what is called the administrative office where
there is a building for conducting sacred rituals. Now they sit at that site. When it rains,
it can also be on blue ground. The seating arrangement has横座 (cross seats) to the north.
Individual seats, the senior official should be placed to the west, the celebrants to the east. This is the protocol for
the arrangement of the lower seats. Around the middle of the proceedings, two eight maidens with hanging headdresses participate.
Each receives a headdress. These eight maidens have great presence. This is the usual ceremony.
When things end, each withdraws. At Ichiharano there is entertainment with dance and music.
That place is along the western mountain, under the forest, facing south, where each person
sits. Ceremonial skins are spread. In case of rain, screens are used.《marginal note: This place, recently called｜料の料 (ryo no ryo)》
