Article 21
For village-owned mountains, forests, and suburban fields where land values are difficult to determine, certificates recording only the place name (azana) and acreage along with the traditional tribute amounts shall be issued as public land. Understanding this, those who possess such land shall investigate and submit [the information].
However, the same applies to various types of ponds and marshes.

Article 22
For mountains and fields jointly used by multiple villages (iriai), those villages shall form an association, and using the same method as the previous article, certificates shall be issued recognizing them as public land of such-and-such villages. The certificates shall be managed by the associated villages through annual rotation or other appropriate arrangements as stated in writing.
