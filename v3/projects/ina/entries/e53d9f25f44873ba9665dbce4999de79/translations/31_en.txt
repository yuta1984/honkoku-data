Article 23
For mountains, forests, and fields where it is difficult to distinguish the acreage (tanben), they shall first be submitted without acreage designation, accompanied by pictorial maps (ezumen).

Article 24
For all such categories of land, the boundaries shall be recorded on the certificates. For example, they should be recorded in detail such as "East: cultivated land, West: such-and-such mountain in [place name], South: certain river, [bounded by] certain village, [place name] field."

Article 25
Even for previously tax-exempt land where residents exist, when the land quality

【Left Page】
is of low grade and there are no applicants from elsewhere, making it difficult to establish a land price standard, the land price shall be set at ten yen or more per tan-bu (that is, land of 300 bu).

Article 26
Land that was originally exempt from land tax (chishi-men) shall not be subject to these regulations.

Article 27
Once the land verification is completed, [certifying] that there are no omitted lands, all landowners shall affix their seals, and sworn statements shall be received from the village headman (kocho) and below.
