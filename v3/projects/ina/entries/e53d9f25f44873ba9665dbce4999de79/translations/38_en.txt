Therefore, from now on such appellations shall be prohibited, and arrogant behavior shall not be permitted.
1. While some have prohibited engaging in commerce alongside agriculture, this is now permitted freely.
1. Regarding people's privately held land, when digging ponds, marshes, rivers, ditches, etc., or rerouting roads for personal or others' convenience, applications must now be submitted and official approval received in all cases.
1. Establishing shrines and temples (such as Jizo halls and Inari shrines) without prior application shall remain prohibited as before.

1. There have been cases of people burying remains arbitrarily at the edges of their cultivated fields, which is completely unacceptable, so this is now strictly prohibited.
1. Regarding riverside ports (kawagishi-ba), while some have been prohibited from establishing new ones, henceforth they may be permitted upon application and after examination.
1. For those who have been conducting periodic redistribution of indefinite lands with set terms, henceforth the landowners must be designated and reported.
1. Regarding free cultivation of rice fields and dry fields, this was already permitted in the eighth month of the previous year Shinbi (1871).
