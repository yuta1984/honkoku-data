Regarding those involved in purchasing medicinal herbs and sundry goods brought by Chinese ships, as well as cargo and joint guarantees, we have heard that recently they are hesitant about trading and their financial circulation is not good. Not only the townspeople engaged in this trade, but also all those who handle such goods in various provinces should conduct transactions without hindrance, even if they are under investigation for legal disputes or conflicts. If someone possesses genuine Chinese goods or keeps them under joint guarantee arrangements, even if only the person in question (not their family) faces punishment, those goods should not be confiscated but should be given to their wives and children. Even if household goods are inspected and sealed during investigations, genuine Chinese goods are exempt from sealing, so people should conduct trade extensively in the future without any concern.
