10th month of Bunka 14 (1817)

1. Regarding national public works (kokuyaku-fushin) for rivers throughout the provinces: when embankments and river maintenance works have suffered major damage and are left abandoned, and the situation at those locations has become hopeless, such cases are beyond the capacity of domain lords and local administrators, and exceptional cases should be handled as local public works. Therefore, frivolous requests should not be made, as was proclaimed in the previous year of the Dragon (Bunka 5, 1808). However, recently there have been many such requests, and people are even reporting damage that is not particularly severe, which goes against the previously issued directives and is problematic. As annual tax collection coordination is being reformed and government expenses are high, for domains of 10,000 koku or more, national public works shall first be applied during the period of current rice fields...
