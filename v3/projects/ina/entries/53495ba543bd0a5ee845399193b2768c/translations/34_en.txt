12th month, 8th day of the Year of the Boar

One: When filing lawsuits regarding loans of gold and silver, outstanding debts, and similar matters, some people falsely claim that those experienced in legal proceedings are their cohabiting relatives, dependents, or servants and present them as such. Or they file suits without properly consulting with the opposing party, resulting in frequent cases where the opponent has died, absconded, or there are mistaken identities. Moreover, when rural people are sued over trivial outstanding amounts, they dislike the various expenses of coming to Edo, and some believe there are ways to settle matters appropriately even when there are actually no outstanding balances.

With this in mind, they frivolously file formal lawsuits. Additionally, regarding loans, some take advantage of the ignorant and impoverished by keeping loan documents with the intention of lending against land, or they change high-interest loans to the pretext of tenant rice payments, which also appears to be under investigation. Recently, formal lawsuits have become frivolously frequent, and the attitude of treating magistrates' endorsements lightly is a misunderstanding. Therefore, while immediate family members and servants are exceptional cases, when it comes to filing formal lawsuits, one must never present those experienced in legal proceedings as representatives. To ensure there are no cases of the opposing party's death, disappearance, extinction, mistaken identity, etc., one should thoroughly consult not only with the opposing party themselves but also with their village officials before filing a petition.
