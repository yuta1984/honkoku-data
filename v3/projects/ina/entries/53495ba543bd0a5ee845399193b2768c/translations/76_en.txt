Those who receive summons papers without paying courier fees shall, upon arriving in Edo, negotiate with the Edo lodging house that handles the aforementioned papers and pay the fees in Edo. This policy has been communicated to Edo lodging houses in general, so they should understand this arrangement.
　However, if one notices suspicious summons papers, the person carrying such papers should be detained on the spot and reported to the appropriate authorities.

As announced in the 7th year of Tenmei (year of the Sheep) and the 12th year of Bunka (year of the Tiger), recently there have again been reports of people handling fake summons papers to fraudulently collect fees. This ultimately occurs because rural people are naive about such matters and mistake fake papers for genuine ones, overlooking the deception. After investigation, punishment will be imposed, so from now on, even if those carrying summons papers claim they lack travel funds, courier fees must never be paid on the spot. 　4th month

Regarding koban gold coins: although orders have been given to recast them more thoroughly to prevent flaws, even if cut gold or flawed gold appears as years pass, such cut or flawed pieces may be used in circulation without concern. Regarding lightweight gold, pieces up to 4 monme lighter than standard koban shall be accepted in circulation, and nibuban (half-piece) and ichibuban (quarter-piece) coins that are light or flawed according to this measure shall also be circulated without delay.
