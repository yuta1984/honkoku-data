[The adoption was completed as] four branches. Bitchū-no-kami Takeyoshi succeeded to Takataka's house and was appointed to the position of Ichimasa. His children were Mokusuke Toyotaka and Yukie [a certain person].

Kamon-no-kami Minamoto Takeyoshi was the second son of Kawachi-no-kami Takehisa. In Keichō 9, at age nine, he went to the Kantō region and served close to the Shogun's house, receiving a portion of his father's inherited domain. He participated in the battles before and after Osaka and presented eight severed heads. On the 28th day of the 9th month of Genna 8, his domain was increased (5,000 koku were added in Iwaki, making his total holdings approximately 20,000 koku: 10,000 koku in Noto Province and 10,000 koku in Ōshū). He died on the 29th day of the 12th month of Kan'ei 5 at age 37.

His son Kawachi-no-kami Taketsugu succeeded his father. His heir was Yamashiro-no-kami Takenao, and his second son was called Minbu [a certain person].
