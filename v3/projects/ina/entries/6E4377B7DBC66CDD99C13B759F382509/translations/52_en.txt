When he expressed this wish, the Kanpaku was greatly moved and soon had it formally petitioned, so he was granted the rank of Junior Fourth Rank Lower Grade and became a chamberlain. 《The Kanpaku always said with admiration, "In the eastern provinces there is Honda Heihachirō Tadakatsu, and in the western regions there is Tachibana Sakon-shōgen Muneshige. They are peerless warriors under heaven."》 At the beginning of Bunroku (1592), he crossed over to Korea and defeated the army of the Ming general-in-chief Li Rusong, making his name known in the three countries (Japan, Korea, and Ming). In the autumn of Keichō 5 (1600), following orders from Osaka, he led his forces, attacked and destroyed Fushimi Castle, fortified Seta Bridge, and captured Ōtsu Castle. When he heard that his allies' army had been defeated at the Battle of Sekigahara, he returned to his home province and fortified himself in his castle. When he heard that Nabeshima's forces were

advancing, he sallied forth from the castle and fought desperately in defense. At such a time, Kuroda Nyūdō Josui and Katō Higo-no-kami Kiyomasa came rushing and sent envoys to both camps to stop the border fighting. The two men gave various advice to Muneshige, had Kiyomasa station troops to defend the castle, made Muneshige lead the vanguard, and sent him toward Satsuma Province. He established his position at Sashiki and Minamata between Higo and Satsuma, but since Shimazu Yoshihiro Nyūdō Ryūhaku had already sued for surrender, there was no need to decide the battle, so they withdrew. Although Kuroda and Katō pleaded on his behalf in place of their own rewards, Muneshige's crimes were not pardoned and his domains were completely confiscated.
