Suzuki Hachirobei
- Enpō era: Thirteen hyō with two-person support
- Jōkyō era: Kitchen inspector
- Genroku era: Same stipend of eighteen hyō
- Same era: Base stipend of fifteen hyō, around this time promoted to foot soldier
- Genroku 16 (1703), 6th month, 7th day: As cook, granted an increase of one hyō
- Hōei era: Twenty-one hyō with two-person support
- Kyōhō 3 (1718), 3rd month, 19th day: Appointed to attendant rotation and meal service
- 4th year (Kyōhō 4, 1719), 4th month, 15th day: Appointed to lower residence meal service
- 9th year (Kyōhō 9, 1724), 3rd month, 1st day: As requested, relieved of duties and appointed to rotating guard duty
- Same year, same month, 11th day: As requested, permitted to retire

    Suzuki Kyūetsu

Second son
    Suzuki Seijirō
        Originally named Shinzō
- Kyōhō 9 (1724), 3rd month, 11th day: Upon his father Hachirobei's retirement as requested, inherited the family headship with a stipend of seventeen hyō and two-person support, appointed as messenger foot soldier, and moved to the upper residence during Edo service
- 10th year (Kyōhō 10, 1725), 1st month, 9th day: Under foot soldier commander supervision

- Enkyō 1 (1744), 12th month, 1st day: Granted an increase of three hyō and appointed as foot soldier inspector
- Hōreki 2 (1752), 10th month: Family line terminated due to desertion
