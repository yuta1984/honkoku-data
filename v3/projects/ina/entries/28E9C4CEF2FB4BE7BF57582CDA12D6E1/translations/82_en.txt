- On the 15th day of the 3rd month of Kansei 12 (1800), Year of the Monkey, he was appointed to mountain affairs, fire brigade, and clothing management during Edo residence duty.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Kitahara Gonnosuke
During the reign of Lord Honryūin
- On the 7th day of the 3rd month of Kansei 1 (1789), Year of the Rooster, based on Nagasaka Zenzō's petition, he was recalled and appointed as assistant head of servants, assistant head of attendants, and bowl manager, with salary, provisions, and role stipend as before: 6 kan 500 mon, provisions for one and a half persons, and role stipend of 1 bu.
During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- On the 15th day of the 2nd month of Kansei 4 (1792), Year of the Rat, he was promoted to the rank of foot soldier with a stipend of 3 koku 5 to and provisions for two people, with duties as before overseeing culinary matters.
- On the 20th day of the 11th month of Kansei 5 (1793), Year of the Ox, he was appointed as temporary assistant in culinary duties for Lord Honjuin, with the main kitchen duties continuing as before.
- On the 1st day of the 12th month of Kansei 6 (1794), Year of the Tiger, he was appointed as guard of the lower lock entrance of the rest quarters.
- On the 15th day of the 12th month of Kansei 10 (1798), Year of the Horse, he was granted the rank of scribe with an additional 1 koku 5 to of rice, making his total stipend 5 koku with provisions for two people, with duties continuing as before.
- On the 18th day of the 6th month of Kansei 11 (1799), Year of the Sheep, he was appointed to concurrently serve as guard of the lower lock entrance of the Eastern Palace, continuing culinary duties as before, and was successfully released from his position as assistant head of the main kitchen attendants.
- On the 20th day of the 4th month of Bunka 1 (1804), Year of the Rat, he was granted an additional 6 to of rice.
- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunka 8 (1811), Year of the Sheep, he was promoted to foot soldier rank with a stipend of 17 hyō and provisions for two people, with duties continuing as before under the supervision of the chamberlain.
- On the 2nd day of the 12th month of Bunsei 4 (1821), Year of the Snake, he died of illness.
