- On the 28th day of the 7th month of Kan'en 2 (1749), Year of the Snake, he was appointed to the position of ō-nando-yaku (chief storehouse keeper).
- On the 5th day of the 12th month of the same year, he was again ordered to serve annually as New Year ceremonial attendant.
- On the 15th day of the 4th month of Hōreki 2 (1752), Year of the Monkey, he was granted a residence. He was given a new residence facing Hara Ichirōemon in Shimoda-machi.
- On the 18th day of the 9th month of Hōreki 5 (1755), Year of the Boar, he was appointed to bunichi-kata (tax collection duties).
- On the 19th day of the 12th month of the same year, he could serve as deputy from his off-duty months. During the lord's absence for sankin-kōtai, he was to patrol the retainer quarters.
- On the 26th day of the 2nd month of Meiwa 3 (1766), Year of the Dog, his younger sister's petition to marry physician Kakinoki Ryōsu of Aramura village in Fujisawa district was approved.
- On the 2nd day of the 5th month of Meiwa 4 (1767), Year of the Boar, his second son Sukeichi absconded.
- On the 11th day of the 8th month of the same year, regarding timber inspection duties at Shimokawa, though this was a primary duty of his position, due to inadequate supervision and consistently poor performance, his bunichi-yaku position was revoked and he was ordered into confinement.
- On the 11th day of the 9th month of the same year, his confinement was lifted and he was appointed as deputy.
- On the 5th day of the 5th month of Meiwa 8 (1771), Year of the Rabbit, due to residence reassignment, he was given a vacant residence north of Akabane Senzaemon in Shimizu-bukuro-machi.
- On the 5th day of the 6th month of Meiwa 9 (1772), Year of the Dragon, his second daughter's petition to become the second wife of Tateishi Yogorōemon was approved.
- On the 11th day of the 4th month of An'ei 3 (1774), Year of the Horse, due to old age, he retired as requested and headship was granted to his son Kan'emon.
- On the 1st day of the 6th month of An'ei 9 (1780), Year of the Rat, out of compassion, he was granted sustenance provisions for one person for life.
- On the 4th day of the 1st month of Tenmei 3 (1783), Year of the Rabbit, he died of illness.

- On the 18th day of the 4th month of Hōreki 10 (1760), Year of the Dragon, he was summoned as a foot soldier in the attendant corps and granted a stipend of 16 hyō and provisions for two people.
- On the 15th day of the 4th month of Hōreki 11 (1761), Year of the Snake, since the young lord has recently arrived and there is a shortage of personnel locally, he was ordered to extend his garrison duty into the coming Year of the Sheep. {{Interlinear note: In Edo}}
- On the 11th day of the 11th month of the same year, for his diligent service day and night during the shogunal seal escort journey, he was granted soup and sake.
- On the 4th day of the 1st month of Hōreki 12 (1762), Year of the Horse, he was appointed to serve as proxy for the pilgrimage to Myōgi on the coming 9th day, the day of the Rabbit.
- On the 5th day of the 3rd month of Meiwa 7 (1770), Year of the Tiger, his petition to take the daughter of Ikegami Riemon, a wholesale merchant in this town, as wife was approved. {{Interlinear note: Father Kyūzaemon and Riemon are cousins once removed}}
