Reign of Lord Yoriyoshi

1. Same 5th year, year of the horse (1822), intercalary 1st month, 23rd day: Promoted to attendant foot soldier, granted 18 hyō and rice for 2 people, placed under foot soldier commander supervision

1. Same year, 4th month, 8th day: Changed name to Gorōuemon, his great-grandfather's name 《marginal note: in Edo》

1. Same 6th year, year of the sheep (1823), 11th month, 25th day: Due to debt matters in Osaka, became castle deputy group member with stipend of 5 koku and rice for 2 people

1. Same year, 12th month, 15th day: Died of illness

Ikegami Namihachi
Initially named Dōtarō

1. Bunsei 6, year of the sheep (1823), 12th month, 28th day: As replacement for deceased father Gorōuemon, granted 5 koku and rice for 2 people, became member of advance guard group

1. Same 8th year, year of the rooster (1825), 5th month, 27th day: Fled 《marginal note: from Edo stationed post》

Iwamoto Kihanji

Reign of Lord Genryūin

1. Meiwa 1, year of the monkey (1764), intercalary 12th month, 11th day: Appointed as middle page, granted 35 hyō and rice for 4 people, summoned as secretary, assigned to upper mansion administrative duties. Former lord Aoyama Yamato-no-kami's home province was Echigo, birth province was Musashi, son-in-law of Ichikawa Chūdayū's sister

1. Same year, same month, 21st day: First audience with lord

1. Same 5th year, year of the rat (1768), 8th month, 9th day: By lord's will, stipend was revoked and permanent dismissal granted. All service matters are no concern. However, must not loiter near the domain territory or both Edo mansions

Former Lord Sakai Yamato-no-kami
Ishii Tazaemon

1. Genroku 16 (1703), domain registry: 7 ryō, rice for 5 people, district administrator

1. Same 17th year, year of the dragon (1704), 2nd month, 10th day: Though his service performance was poor, through clemency was granted satisfactory dismissal

Note: Records show that when he administered Upper Ina district, he was sued by the district residents for misconduct
