- On the 28th day of the 7th month of Meiwa 1 (1764), Year of the Monkey, the request submitted by his father Takuzaemon was received and acknowledged, and he was appointed to succeed to his father Takuzaemon's house, with duties and rank remaining as resident status.
- On the 21st day of the 10th month of Meiwa 2 (1765), Year of the Rooster, he was granted an increase of provisions for one person.
- On the 15th day of the 5th month of Meiwa 5 (1768), Year of the Rat, he was appointed to personal attendant duty and granted an increase of 7 hyō, making his total stipend 25 hyō with provisions for three people.
- On the last day of the 1st month of Meiwa 7 (1770), Year of the Tiger, due to the death by illness of his son Tadaremon, his petition to make his second son Suzujirō the heir was approved.
- On the 5th day of the 5th month of Meiwa 8 (1771), Year of the Rabbit, he was to temporarily handle the catering duties alone, and accordingly was exempted from overnight stays during castle residence and also exempted from the monthly five-day duty rotation.
- On the 6th day of the 2nd month of Meiwa 9 (1772), Year of the Dragon, his petition to give his eldest daughter in marriage to Daiemon, son of Hyōtō Kihei, was approved.
- On the 15th day of the 1st month of An'ei 2 (1773), Year of the Snake, he was appointed as proxy pilgrim to Ise Province.
During the reign of Lord Honryūin
- On the 28th day of the 2nd month of An'ei 6 (1777), Year of the Rooster, he was released from catering and culinary duties. For the attendant rotation, culinary staff were to be appointed on a rotating basis, and he was appointed as head of servants and baggage manager.
- On the 21st day of the 10th month of An'ei 7 (1778), Year of the Dog, Yamashita Ryōsuke was appointed to concurrent catering and culinary duties, Shirokura Tamehachi was appointed to concurrent culinary duties, and regarding instruction in previous service duties, Yoshihara Yosōji was ordered to oversee culinary matters and provide instruction to be useful in official duties.
- On the 4th day of the 1st month of Tenmei 3 (1783), Year of the Rabbit, his wife died.
- On the 28th day of the 1st month of Tenmei 8 (1788), Year of the Monkey, as requested, he was released from official duties and assigned to attendant rotation.
- On the 28th day of the 5th month of Kansei 2 (1790), Year of the Dog, his petition to adopt Wasaburō {{Interlinear note: 25 years old}}, second son of Ōkuma Genjun, retainer of Lord Suwa Inaba-no-kami, together with his daughter, was approved.
- On the 24th day of the 4th month of Kansei 3 (1791), Year of the Boar, he died of illness.

- On the 15th day of the 6th month of Kansei 3 (1791), Year of the Boar, he inherited his late father Takuzaemon's position with a stipend of 18 hyō and provisions for two people, and was appointed as foot soldier in personal attendant service.
During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- On the 23rd day of the 1st month of Kansei 6 (1794), Year of the Tiger, he changed his name to Sezaemon, previously Wasaburō.
