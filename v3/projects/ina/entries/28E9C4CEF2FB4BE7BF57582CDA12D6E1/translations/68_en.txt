- On the 28th day of the 9th month of Enpō 4 (1675), Year of the Sheep, he was summoned as a secretary with a stipend of 10 ryō and provisions for 4 people.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Muraoka Danzō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Original name: Heijirō

- On the 9th day of the 6th month of Genbun 1 (1736), Year of the Dragon, his father Yaichirōemon was granted retirement as petitioned, and being young in age, he was granted relief provisions for 4 people and appointed as yobitsuki ashigaru.

- On the 20th day of the 4th month of Genbun 2 (1737), Year of the Snake, the petition for his younger brother Heizō to be adopted by a rōnin named Saki Yasuemon was completed.

- On the 28th day of the 6th month of Kanpō 2 (1742), Year of the Dog, having come of age, he was appointed to guard rotation duty succeeding his late father with 18 hyō and provisions for 2 people, and was to understand that his daily duties would continue as before. This was also yobitsuki.

- On the 28th day of the 3rd month of Enkyō 1 (1744), Year of the Rat, the petition for his younger brother to be adopted by Masuo Jinsuke, a retainer of Lord Kuchiki Shuri, was completed.

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Enkyō 2 (1745), Year of the Ox, he was appointed as assistant secretary to the senior councilors, elders, and pages, and moved to the upper residence. {{Marginal note: yobitsuki}}

- On the 26th day of the intercalary 12th month of the same year, he took back Kuribayashi Kanzaemon's adopted son due to dissolution of the adoption.

- On the 24th day of the 12th month of Hōreki 3 (1753), Year of the Rooster, he was appointed to escort guard rank with an increase of 2 hyō, receiving 20 hyō and provisions for 2 people, concurrently serving as secretary.

- On the 1st day of the 11th month of Hōreki 4 (1754), Year of the Dog, he was appointed to account keeping duty with secretarial staff under him.

- On the 29th day of the 6th month of Hōreki 10 (1760), Year of the Dragon, regarding his younger brother Masuo Kansuke who had been sent as an adopted son to Masuo Kanzaemon, a retainer of Lord Kuchiki Oribe of the hatamoto, he absconded on the evening of the 20th, so formal separation was performed and notification was given that it should be handled as petitioned to the shogunate.

- On the 9th day of the 3rd month of Hōreki 11 (1761), Year of the Snake, the petition to marry the daughter of Udagawa Masaemon, a rural samurai of Shimohagikubo Village in Musashi Province within the Sannō domain, was completed.

- On the 22nd day of the intercalary 4th month of Hōreki 12 (1762), Year of the Horse, he absconded.
　　　　　　He left a written statement that he was departing due to financial difficulties. At this time he held the position of secretary.
