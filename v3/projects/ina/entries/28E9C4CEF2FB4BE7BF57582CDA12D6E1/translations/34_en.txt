1. Same 5th year, Year of the Monkey (1740), July 18th: During the recent period of being entrusted with duties, when there were few personnel available, worked diligently day and night without negligence, and for this dedication was granted soup and sake

1. Kanpō 3rd year, Year of the Boar (1743), February 14th: Marriage proposal for younger sister to Assistant Inspector Terajima Tsutaemon approved

1. Enkyō 3rd year, Year of the Tiger (1746), May 9th: Granted salary increase of three hyō

1. Kan'en 1st year, Year of the Dragon (1748), intercalary October 28th: Returned to secular life, granted rank among foot soldiers, appointed as page inspector

1. Same 2nd year, Year of the Snake (1749), March 26th: As hair had grown properly, to shave head on the first day of next month, and accordingly changed name to father's name Kin'emon as petitioned

1. Hōreki 4th year, Year of the Dog (1754), November 28th: Appointed to assist with treasury payment accounting

1. Same 5th year, Year of the Boar (1755), September 18th: Granted rank of escort guard, duties as before, exempted from page rotation duties, to serve as Yoneyama Senzaemon has been serving, announced at Takatō

1. Same 8th year, Year of the Tiger (1758), April 29th: Granted salary increase of three hyō

1. Same 14th year, Year of the Monkey (1764), February 26th: As land damage and new field development administrator was appointed, ordered to assist

1. Same year, May 14th: Appointed administrator, granted salary increase of one hyō and support for one retainer, total twenty-four hyō and support for three retainers, under district administrator supervision, granted administrative allowance of two retainers as usual

1. Meiwa 1st year (1764), October 24th: Ordered residence change, granted Takahashi Naozaemon's former residence at Kubokawa

1. Same day: Appointed administrator of Fujisawa village

1. Same 8th year, Year of the Rabbit (1771), August 28th: Granted rank of junior page, salary increase of two hyō, total twenty-six hyō and support for three retainers

Reign of Lord Honryūin
1. Tenmei 3rd year, Year of the Rabbit (1783), July 5th: Appointed senior administrator chief with rank of junior page inspector, above Takada Rokuemon, stipend of thirty hyō and support for three retainers

1. Same year, December 28th: Ordered to inspect due to poor rice harvest this autumn, worked diligently with continued effort in accounting duties, granted three hundred mon in gold

1. Kansei 1st year, Year of the Rooster (1789), March 5th: Due to old age, as petitioned, relieved of official duties, to continue serving in guard rotation maintaining current rank, having served rural administration for many years with great effort, granted three hundred mon in gold
