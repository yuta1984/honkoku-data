　　　　　　　　　　　　Nakazaki Budayu

During the reign of Lord Genryu-in
- On the 13th day of the 3rd month of Enkyō 4 (1747), Year of the Rabbit, his petition to send his daughter as an adopted daughter to Naito Tomoshichi, a retainer of Lord Hikosaka Goroemon, was approved.

- In the spring of the same year, he divorced and took back Matsuyama Jūsuke's wife.

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kan'en 2 (1749), Year of the Snake, he was promoted from deputy head of the caretaker group to ashigaru, granted 18 hyō and provisions for 2 people, appointed to guard duty rotation, and placed under magistrate supervision. {{Marginal note: Previously 5 koku 9 to}}

- On the 5th day of the 3rd month of the same year, his petition to adopt Hanji {{Marginal note: 23 years old}}, second son of Kato Hanemon, a retainer of Lord Yagi Saburo, was approved.

- On the 23rd day of the 9th month of Hōreki 3 (1753), Year of the Rooster, he died of illness.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Nakazaki Masuemon

- On the 15th day of the 11th month of Hōreki 3 (1753), Year of the Rooster, he inherited his deceased father Budayu's position without change, was granted 18 hyō and provisions for 2 people, appointed to ashigaru guard duty rotation, and assigned to the lord's lower residence.

- On the 24th day of the 12th month of the same year, he was appointed to concurrently serve as deputy secretary under the head clerk, and was to remain at the upper residence as before.

- On the 17th day of the 3rd month of Hōreki 5 (1755), Year of the Boar, he was placed under the supervision of Nakano Shikiemon.

- On the 27th day of the 11th month of the same year, he died of illness. Having no son and serving only briefly, a petition was made for succession through adoption into the family.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Nasuno Sohei

- On the 1st day of the 4th month of Kampō 3 (1743), Year of the Boar, as petitioned due to old age and infirmity, he retired with a replacement.
　　However, until then he had been deputy head of Kamei Gorobei's group, with a stipend of 3 ryō 3 bu before the Genbun era.
