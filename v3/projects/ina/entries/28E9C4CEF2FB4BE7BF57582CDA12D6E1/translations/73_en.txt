- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Enkyō 3 (1746), Year of the Tiger, he was appointed as deputy magistrate, with an increase of 4 hyō and provisions for one person, making his total stipend 26 hyō with provisions for three people, plus additional role allowance equivalent to provisions for two people.

- On the 29th day of the 9th month of Kan'en 2 (1749), Year of the Snake, his petition to marry the daughter of Tamura Yoichizaemon was approved.

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kan'en 3 (1750), Year of the Horse, he was appointed to formal middle page status.

- In the 1st month of Hōreki 5 (1755), Year of the Boar, his petition to send his sister's daughter as wife to Kitahara Bungoemon was approved.

- On the 4th day of the 11th month of Hōreki 7 (1757), Year of the Ox, his administrative jurisdiction was changed from Haruchika and Fujisawa villages to Nakazawa and Irinotani villages.

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Hōreki 8 (1758), Year of the Tiger, he received an increase of 4 hyō, making his total stipend 30 hyō.

- On the 15th day of the 10th month of the same year, his petition to send his second son Yūzō as adopted son to Hyōdō Kihei was approved.

- On the 28th day of the 4th month of Meiwa 3 (1766), Year of the Dog, he was appointed to middle page inspector rank and granted an increase of 3 hyō.

- On the 11th day of the 12th month of Meiwa 6 (1769), Year of the Ox, his petition to send his daughter as wife to Itō Sadenji was approved.

- On the 11th day of the 1st month of Meiwa 9 (1772), Year of the Dragon, due to his diligent service in managing both villages, he was granted a ceremonial hemp hakama.

- On the 28th day of the 12th month of An'ei 4 (1775), Year of the Sheep, he was appointed as head deputy magistrate, relieved of his administrative territory, appointed to middle page inspector sequential rank above Nakayama Jizaemon, and granted an increase of 2 hyō, making his total stipend 35 hyō.

Reign of Lord Honryūin

- On the 5th day of the 7th month of Tenmei 3 (1783), Year of the Rabbit, due to illness, he was relieved of head deputy magistrate duties as petitioned and appointed to guard duty rotation. Having served faithfully for several decades under chamberlain supervision in rural administration until extreme old age, he was granted 500 mon in gold.

- On the 1st day of the 11th month of the same year, he was granted retirement as petitioned, and family headship was passed to his son Hachirozaemon.

- On the 10th day of the 7th month of Kansei 1 (1789), Year of the Rooster, he died of illness.
