Ikegami Zenshirō
During the reign of Lord Honryūin
- Tenmei 5 (1785), 5th month, 3rd day: Employed as monk with support allowance of one-person sustenance granted
- Kansei 2 (1790), 3rd month, 10th day: Upon parent Yohei's retirement request, succeeded with substitute rank of 5 koku and two-person sustenance, under chief inspector supervision
- Same year, 5th month, 28th day: Apprentice scribe under chamberlain supervision

During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- 6th year (Kansei 6, 1794), 6th month, 15th day: Promoted to ranked foot soldier with eighteen hyō and two-person sustenance granted, duties to continue as before
- 11th year (Kansei 11, 1799), 7th month, 20th day: Additionally appointed as construction department assistant
- 13th year (Kansei 13, 1801), 1st month, 15th day: Appointed as apprentice secretary, scribe duties to continue as before, construction department assistant position temporarily maintained
- Bunka 1 (1804), 3rd month, 10th day: Ranked attendant secretary full position, granted increase of two hyō, scribe and construction department assistant duties to continue as before
- 5th year (Bunka 5, 1808), intercalary 6th month, 8th day: Due to frequent castle visits as part of duties, granted position allowance of one-person sustenance
- 9th year (Bunka 9, 1812), 1st month, 15th day: Granted increase of four hyō  
- 13th year (Bunka 13, 1816), 5th month, 28th day: Granted increase of two hyō and one-person sustenance
