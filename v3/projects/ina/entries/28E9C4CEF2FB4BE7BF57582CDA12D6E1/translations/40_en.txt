Ōhira Gunbei

During the reign of Shinnyo'in-sama
• Kyōhō 1, 9th month, 13th day (September 13, 1716): Granted an increase of 1 ryō and provisions for one person, appointed as middle page
• Kyōhō 5, 3rd month, 1st day (March 1, 1720): Appointed as middle page inspector, with 3 ryō allowance money

During the reign of Genryū'in-sama
• Genbun 5, 12th month, 25th day (December 25, 1740): Granted increase of 1 hyō to total stipend of 40 hyō with provisions for three people, relieved of attendant duties
• Kanpō 3, 4th month, 15th day (April 15, 1743): Promoted to stipend-receiving retainer status and appointed to guard duty
　　　　Note: Since stipend-receiving retainers and above are recorded in the previous register, details are omitted here

Retainer of Mizuno Hayato-no-shō-sama
《Marginal note: Home province Mikawa, birth province Shinano}} Age 32　　　　Ōsuga Heihachi
Younger brother of Kotani Mozaemon　　　　Originally Shinemon

During the reign of Genryū'in-sama
• Enkyō 5, 4th month, 1st day (April 1, 1748): Entered service at Takatō, appointed as middle page secretary with stipend of 30 hyō and provisions for three people, assigned to Edo service; changed name to Heihachi on the 2nd day of the same month
• Kan'en 3, 6th month, 22nd day (June 22, 1750): Request completed to take the daughter of Kido Kyūbei as wife
• Meiwa 1, intercalary 12th month, 14th day (January 1765): As he had no male heir, request completed to adopt the second son of Hosaka Sōemon, retainer of Sugaya Hyōgo-sama
• Meiwa 2, 1st month, 18th day (January 18, 1765): Died of illness
　　　　Service record

Adopted son
Ōsuga Onozō
• Meiwa 2, 3rd month, 10th day (March 10, 1765): Inherited his late father Heihachi's position with stipend of 22 hyō and provisions for two people, appointed to formal attendant duty and alternate duty
• Same month, 24th day: Temporarily appointed to local escort duty, relieved on the last day of the same month
• Same year, 5th month, 11th day: Appointed as assistant to middle pages during absences
• Meiwa 3, 9th month, 17th day (September 17, 1766): Scribe to senior councilors and elders
