1. 9th year of same era, Year of the Dog (1826), January 15th: Due to changes in the management system of both stables, appointed concurrently as stable superintendent, with other duties continuing as before.

1. Same year, June 28th: Since his duties frequently required him to appear at the castle, granted one retainer's support as official allowance.

1. 11th year of same era, Year of the Rat (1828), June 23rd: Full appointment as page inspector, increase of four hyō, other duties as before.

1. 3rd year of Tenpō, Year of the Dragon (1832), Intercalary 11th month, 20th day: Having served diligently and faithfully, and like his father Shinouemon having devoted himself to Confucian studies and given lectures, and having worked diligently in teaching elementary reading to domain retainers, granted stipendiary status with an increase of four hyō, duties as before.

1. 4th year of same era, Year of the Snake (1833), May 28th: Due to administrative reorganization, smoothly relieved of stable superintendent duties.

1. 9th year of same era, Year of the Dog (1838), Last day of April: During the recent fire at the upper residence, there may have been some inappropriate handling of matters, and due to these inadequate circumstances, ordered to refrain from duties; on intercalary 4th month 13th day, the restraint was lifted.

1. 11th year of same era, Year of the Rat (1840), March 24th: Increase of five hyō.

1. 13th year of same era, Year of the Tiger (1841), December 20th: Appointed as assistant financial magistrate, with page inspector and secretary duties continuing as before.

1. 14th year of same era, Year of the Rabbit (1843), March 1st: As requested, his father Shinouemon was granted retirement, and he inherited the family headship with a stipend of 35 hyō and support for three retainers without change. Stipendiary status, assistant financial magistrate, and other duties as before.

1. 2nd year of Kōka, Year of the Snake (1845), June 15th: Died of illness at age 57.

　　　　　　　　　　　　Yamashita Inosuke
Reign of Lord Yoriyasu
1. 13th year of Tenpō, Year of the Tiger (1842), April 8th: Appointed as employed attendant in guard formation service, granted support for two retainers.

1. 14th year of same era, Year of the Rabbit (1843), May 18th: Due to financial reforms, unavoidably relieved of employed service without incident. Accordingly, granted support for one retainer. However, in the future there may be occasions when lower-ranked duties are assigned.
