- On the 11th day of the 6th month of Enkyō 4 (1747), Year of the Rabbit, his elder brother Jinsaemon had his stipend confiscated by lordly decree. However, in consideration of their father Jinsaemon's diligent service, he was summoned as an ashigaru (foot soldier) to succeed the former Jinsaemon's position, granted a stipend of 18 hyō and provisions for 2 people, and placed under the supervision of a magistrate.

- On the 15th day of the 4th month of Hōreki 2 (1752), Year of the Monkey, he was granted lodgings in the former quarters of Hyōdō Kihei in Aramachi.

- In the intercalary 2nd month of Hōreki 4 (1754), Year of the Dog, he petitioned to change his name to Gen'ya.

- On the 3rd day of the 5th month of the same year, he was appointed as an attendant ashigaru under the supervision of the ashigaru commander.

- On the 18th day of the 10th month of Hōreki 10 (1760), Year of the Dragon, he was banished for making an improper petition.

- On the 19th day of the intercalary 4th month of Hōreki 12 (1762), Year of the Horse, he was newly summoned as an attendant ashigaru, granted 18 hyō and provisions for 2 people, and assigned to Edo service.

- On the 12th day of the 6th month of Hōreki 13 (1763), Year of the Sheep, he was appointed as temporary purchasing officer with additional provisions for 1 person, and ordered to serve as a messenger when off duty.

- On the 23rd day of the 2nd month of Meiwa 6 (1769), Year of the Ox, having served for many years, he was smoothly relieved of his temporary purchasing officer duties.

- On the 21st day of the 6th month of Meiwa 8 (1771), Year of the Rabbit, his marriage to the sister of Masuko Giemon, a retainer of Lord Sakakibara Shikibu-no-Taifu, was approved.

- On the 21st day of the 6th month of Meiwa 9 (1772), Year of the Dragon, he was appointed ashigaru inspector with an increase of 2 hyō, making his total stipend 20 hyō.

- On the 21st day of the 12th month of An'ei 1 (1772), he changed his name to Kōzaemon, having previously been called Ihachi.

- On the 24th day of the 5th month of An'ei 2 (1773), Year of the Snake, he was reappointed as temporary purchasing officer with additional provisions for 1 person as position allowance.

- On the 22nd day of the 2nd month of An'ei 5 (1776), Year of the Monkey, he changed his name to Tomosaemon, having previously been called Kōzaemon.

- On the 24th day of the 4th month of the same year, due to illness and incompetence, as petitioned, he was relieved of his duties as ashigaru inspector and temporary purchasing officer and reassigned to attendant service.

- On the 21st day of the 6th month of An'ei 7 (1778), Year of the Dog, he was assigned to service at the lord's lower residence.
