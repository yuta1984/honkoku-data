Nakayama Iemon
Original name: Kazuemon

- Kyōhō 9 (1724), 12th month, 4th day: Appointed under the command of the ashigaru captain
- Same year, 12th month, 6th day: Changed name to Iemon
During the reign of Lord Genryūin

- Genbun 2 (1737), 3rd month, 19th day: Transferred from groom service to castle guard duty under the supervision of the chamberlain. To proceed to the lord's residence once preparations are complete. From Edo duty station

- Same year, 12th month: Permission granted for his daughter's marriage arrangement with farmer Jiroemon of Ashizawa village

- Kanpō 2 (1742), 2nd month, 6th day, night: His son Gōemon absconded and is working at Katsugami Gen'emon's place

- Kanpō 3 (1743), 12th month, 21st day: Permission granted for his wish to have his daughter join Tsuchiya Tomoya's adoption arrangements

- Kanpō 4 (1744), 2nd month, 15th day: Appointed as ashigaru inspector with an increase of two hyō, bringing his stipend to twenty hyō

- Hōreki 2 (1752), 7th month, 15th day: Died of illness
- Same year, 9th month, 12th day: Succession rights granted to adopted son Ryōemon
