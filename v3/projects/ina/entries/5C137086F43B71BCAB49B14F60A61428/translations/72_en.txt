Takahashi Yōzaemon
(Also known as: Kanzaemon, originally Seisaburō)

- Kyōwa 3 (1803), 9th month, 25th day: Due to his father Rinzaemon's illness, appointed as substitute guard upon request, granted a stipend of five koku and two-person rations
- Bunka 6 (1809), 2nd month, 28th day: Appointed to oversee the chief administrator's department
- Bunka 11 (1814), 1st month, 15th day: Granted additional rice stipend of three to
- Same year, 4th month, 18th day: Appointed as tree manager while remaining under chief administrator's supervision
- Same year, 9th month, 8th day: Appointed to oversee mountain affairs, forestry, and bamboo groves, other duties remain as before
- Bunpō 3 (1820), 1st month, 15th day: Three to additional rice stipend, duties remain as before

During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu:
- Bunpō 7 (1824), 7th month, 18th day: Group inspector level, firewood affairs, bamboo grove management, mountain and forest affairs remain as before, joined rural group
- Bunpō 9 (1826), 9th month, 10th day: Due to frequent business trips to rural areas, granted three to of allowance rice
- Bunpō 11 (1828), 7th month, 20th day: Appointed as assistant inspector
- Tenpō 2 (1831), 9th month, 3rd day: Appointed as assistant in public works department, three to additional rice, minor leader level remains as before, total stipend became five koku nine to

- Tenpō 5 (1834), 9th month, 13th day: Appointed as clerk level, high stipend exempted
- Tenpō 7 (1836), 10th month, 3rd day: Due to illness, dismissed from public works assistant role upon request, appointed as assistant to castle deputy group, rank remains as before, henceforth under chief inspector's supervision
- Tenpō 8 (1837), 10th month, 15th day: Appointed as assistant in military equipment department
- Tenpō 10 (1839), 12th month, 10th day: Appointed as rural group leader
- Tenpō 11 (1840), 6th month, 28th day: Regarding continuing instruction in Araki-ryū martial arts to Itō Genji, as he has devoted himself for several years and achieved full mastery, he should especially cultivate current students and work to ensure the school's tradition continues unbroken
- Kaei 5 (1852), 1st month, 15th day: For several years of diligent service, granted three kan mon of copper coins as reward
- Kaei 6 (1853), 1st month, 15th day: For several years of diligent service without negligence, promoted to foot soldier rank, appointed as inspector of supply and payment of various items in the public works office, accordingly granted salary, seventeen koku in bags and two-person rations granted, henceforth under magistrate's supervision
