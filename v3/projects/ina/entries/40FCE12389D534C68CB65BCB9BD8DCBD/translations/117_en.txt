　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Sixth generation
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Miura Shinhachirou
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　{{Marginal note: Later Tadauemon}}
1. On the 10th day of the 6th month of Bunka 12, Kinoto-I (1815), he was appointed to serve the young lord (waka-tonosama) as go-soku-kin from go-bangata.
1. On the 8th day of the 10th month of Bunka 13, Hinoe-Ne (1816), his go-soku service to Lord Ryōnoshin was successfully completed and dismissed, and he was appointed to go-omote go-soku service.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunsei 3, Kanoe-Tatsu (1820), he was appointed go-konando go-meshimono-kata.
1. On the 17th day of the 2nd month of the same year, as the lord's retirement was granted as requested, he was appointed go-tsuki go-soku service. The manner of service remained the same as before.

1. On the 23rd day of the 6th month of Bunsei 4, Kanoto-Mi (1821), he was granted go-shiban rank and appointed temporary go-zenban to the retired lord. Go-konando go-meshimono-kata continued as before.
1. On the 3rd day of the 11th month of Bunsei 6, Mizunoto-Hitsuji (1823), he was granted ō-metsuke rank and appointed permanent go-zenban to the retired lord. Go-konando go-meshimono-kata continued as before.
1. On the 15th day of the 9th month of Bunsei 12, Tsuchinoto-Ushi (1829), due to illness and as requested, he was dismissed from go-zenban and go-konando go-meshimono-kata to the retired lord, and ordered to return to regular duty (kiban).
