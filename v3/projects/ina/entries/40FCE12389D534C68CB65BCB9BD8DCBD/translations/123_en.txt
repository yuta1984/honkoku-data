1. On the 20th day of the 9th month of Tenpō 4, Mizunoto-Mi (1833), he was granted the rank of ō-metsuke and appointed go-zenban. Go-konando duties continued as before.
　　　　　　However, he was relieved of go-kachi-gashira
1. On the 15th day of the 5th month of Tenpō 5, Kinoe-Uma (1834), he was appointed go-kinshū-metsuke as an additional duty.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kōka 2, Kinoto-Mi (1845), he was appointed go-yōyaku. He continued to concurrently hold moto-shime-yaku go-sentemono-gashira as before.

Fifth generation

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kōno Shinsaburō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　{{Marginal note: Later Sōzaemon, Hachirōemon}}
1. On the 15th day of the 4th month of Bunsei 2, Tsuchinoto-U (1819), he was appointed go-soku service from go-bangata go-yatoi.
1. On the 17th day of the 2nd month of Bunsei 3, Kanoe-Tatsu (1820), this time due to succession, he was appointed go-soku service to the lord.
1. On the 15th day of the 3rd month of Bunsei 9, Hinoe-Inu (1826), due to arrangements, he was appointed go-soku service to the retired lord.
　　　　　　However, he was to serve at go-zaishō-zaiban first
1. On the 25th day of the 7th month of Bunsei 11, Tsuchinoe-Ne (1828), he was appointed go-konando go-meshimono-kata to the retired lord.
1. On the 25th day of the 9th month of Bunsei 12, Tsuchinoto-Ushi (1829), he was appointed go-shiban-rank go-kinshū-metsuke to the retired lord. Other duties continued as before.
1. On the 18th day of the 10th month of Tenpō 2, Kanoto-U (1831), he was appointed oshiai-yaku.
