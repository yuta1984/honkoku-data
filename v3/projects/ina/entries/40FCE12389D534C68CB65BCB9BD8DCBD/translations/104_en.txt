《marginal note: originally named Itsutōji》
                     　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Fukaya Isamu
1. On the 15th day of the intercalary 6th month of Kansei 6, year of the Wood Tiger (1794), he was appointed to go-soku go-yatoikin (temporary attendant service).
1. On the 22nd day of the 9th month of Kyōwa 3, year of the Water Boar (1803), he was appointed to go-fushin bugyō (construction magistrate), while continuing his concurrent position as go-kin bugyō (treasury magistrate) as before.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Third generation
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Yoshida Gakuya
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　《marginal note: later named Hikosaburo》
1. On the 8th day of the 4th month of Kansei 8, year of the Fire Dragon (1796), he was appointed to go-soku go-yatoikin service.
1. On the last day of the 5th month of Kyōwa 2, year of the Water Dog (1802), having performed his go-soku duties without incident, he was dismissed and appointed to go-bangata.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Second generation

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Saeki Sadayoshi
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　《marginal note: later named Junpachi》
1. On the 9th day of the 2nd month of Kansei 9, year of the Fire Snake (1797), he was appointed from go-tomogata go-chū-koshō go-yatoikin to go-soku service.
1. On the 18th day of the 7th month of Bunka 3, year of the Fire Tiger (1806), he was elevated to kyūnin status and appointed to go-konando and go-meshimono-kata.
1. On the 15th day of the 6th month of the 5th year, year of the Earth Dragon (1808), he was appointed go-kinjū metsuke, with other duties continuing as before.
1. On the 10th day of the 7th month of the 6th year, year of the Earth Snake (1809), he was appointed temporary go-zenban.
1. On the 28th day of the 3rd month of the 10th year, year of the Water Monkey (1813), he was appointed assistant go-zenban.
1. On the 7th day of the 7th month of the 11th year, year of the Water Rooster (1814), he was appointed to full go-zenban position, with other duties continuing as before.
1. On the 28th day of the 7th month of Bunsei 5, year of the Water Horse (1822), he was appointed as go-yōnin-kaku (chamberlain rank) attached to the retired lord, while temporarily continuing as go-kinjū metsuke.
