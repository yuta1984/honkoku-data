Fourth Generation by Name Succession, Actually Eighth Generation
Okada Kinpei

1. On the 1st day of the 4th month of Tenpō 8, Hinoto-Tori (1837), he was appointed to go-soku service from go-bangata go-yatoizutome go-soeban. Go-soeban duties continued as before.
1. On the 15th day of the 6th month of the same year, due to illness, as requested, he was exempted from both go-soku service and go-soeban duties and ordered to return to regular guard duty.
1. On the 23rd day of the 10th month of the same 9th year, Tsuchinoe-Inu (1838), he was appointed to go-soku service from go-bangata.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of the same 12th year, Kanoto-Ushi (1841), he was appointed to go-tsukaiban rank, go-konando go-meshimonogata concurrent duties, and go-kinshū-metsuke as temporary assignment.
1. On the 28th day of the 6th month of Kōka 2, Kinoto-Mi (1845), he was appointed to ō-metsuke rank, go-zenban, and go-kinshū-metsuke as permanent positions. Go-konando go-meshimonogata duties continued as before.
