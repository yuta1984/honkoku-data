1. On the 21st day of the 2nd month of Tenpō 4, Mizunoto-Mi (1833), he claimed illness and expressed his desire to request exemption from his duties. His colleagues consulted among themselves and repeatedly encouraged him to return to duty, but he paid no heed whatsoever, became obstinate in his willfulness, secluded himself, and acted improperly. As a result, three-person stipend was confiscated from his allowance, he was exempted from retired lord's go-soku service, and was ordered to return to regular duty under house confinement.
