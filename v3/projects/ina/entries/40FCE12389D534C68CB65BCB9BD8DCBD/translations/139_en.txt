Fourth Generation
Okuda Kanji
(later Kyūzaemon)

1. On the 15th day of the 6th month of Tenpō 8, Hinoto-Tori (1837), he was appointed to go-soku service from go-bangata.
1. On the 2nd day of the 2nd month of the same 11th year, Kanoe-Ne (1840), due to illness, as requested, he was exempted from go-soku service and ordered to return to guard duty (at go-zaisho zaiban-saki).
1. On the 5th day of the 10th month of the same year, he was appointed to go-inkyo-sama go-soku service from go-bangata.
1. On the 15th day of the 10th month of Kōka 3, Hinoe-Uma (1846), he was appointed to go-inkyo-sama go-kinshū-metsuke.
1. On the 28th day of the 5th month of the same 4th year, Hinoto-Hitsuji (1847), due to illness, as requested, he was exempted from go-inkyo-sama go-soku service and go-kinshū-metsuke and ordered to return to guard duty.

Sixth Generation
Fukuhara Tadazō

1. On the 25th day of the 10th month of Tenpō 8, Hinoto-Tori (1837), he was appointed to go-soku service from go-bangata.
1. On the 12th day of the 10th month of the same 9th year, Tsuchinoe-Inu (1838), he died of illness (while stationed at Edo zaiban-saki).

Fourth Generation by Name Succession, Actually Sixth Generation
Araki Tomotarō
(later Hyōzō)

1. On the 18th day of the 3rd month of the same 12th year, Kanoto-Ushi (1841), due to go-kuriawase, he was exempted from go-soku service without incident and appointed to go-bangata.
