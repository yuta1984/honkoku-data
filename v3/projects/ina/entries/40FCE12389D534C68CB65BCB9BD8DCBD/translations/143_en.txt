Re-established lineage, 5th generation, actually 9th generation
Kaminami Yaichirō
《Marginal note: later called Hanzō》

1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Tenpō 12, Kanoto-Ushi (1841), he was appointed to go-soku service from go-bangata.
1. On the 11th day of the 7th month of the same 13th year, Mizunoe-Tora (1842), regarding the matter of his relative Tomozō, there were certain considerations, and he was ordered to retire and granted Egō-kaku status. Due to the special consideration for his old family lineage, he was granted 150 koku without fail as private stipend, was relieved of go-soku service, and appointed to go-bangata ranked position according to stipend order.

First generation of the lineage, actually 7th generation
Sanpei Torajirō
《Marginal note: later called Tansaburō》

1. On the 20th day of the 10th month of Tenpō 12, Kanoto-Ushi (1841), he was appointed to go-inkyo-sama go-soku service from go-tomogata go-chū-koshō go-yatoi service.
1. On the 25th day of the 8th month of the same 13th year, Mizunoe-Tora (1842), due to misconduct by his father Mori-emon, his stipend allocation was confiscated and he was ordered into house confinement, leading to the extinction of the family name. However, through special consideration, his grandfather Seiemon's lineage was reestablished for him, he was granted a stipend of 40 hyō and support for three retainers, and appointed to go-bangata ranked position according to stipend order.
1. On the 3rd day of the 8th month of the same 14th year, Mizunoto-U (1843), he was appointed to go-inkyo-sama go-soku service from go-bangata.
1. On the 15th day of the 10th month of the same 15th year, Kinoe-Tatsu (1844), due to shortage of personnel in omotegata, he was relieved of go-inkyo-sama go-soku service without incident and ordered to return to regular service.
