1. On the 7th day of the 7th month of Bunka 11, Kinoe-Inu (1814), he was summoned to serve as kyūnin go-kinshū and granted a stipend of ten men's rations.
1. On the 25th day of the 6th month of Bunka 13, Hinoe-Ne (1816), he was appointed to serve the young lord (waka-tonosama) as go-soku-kin. Having served with familiarity up to this point, he was promoted to go-tsuki through the lord's favor.
1. On the 15th day of the 4th month of Bunsei 2, Tsuchinoto-U (1819), he was appointed go-kin-bugyō. He was successfully dismissed from go-soku service.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunsei 4, Kanoto-Mi (1821), he was appointed from go-kin-bugyō to go-soku go-konando go-meshimono-kata.
1. On the 17th day of the 5th month of Bunsei 6, Mizunoto-Hitsuji (1823), (retired due to) illness.

1. On the 5th day of the intercalary 11th month of Bunka 10, Mizunoto-Tori (1813), he was appointed go-soku service from go-bangata.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunka 13, Hinoe-Ne (1816), he was appointed assistant go-tsuki to Lord Sukenosuke.
1. On the 25th day of the 6th month of the same year, he was appointed go-shiban, concurrently serving as go-soku to the young lord and probationary go-konando, particularly for Edo affairs {{Marginal note: At the Edo residence}}.
1. On the 17th day of the 2nd month of Bunsei 3, Kanoe-Tatsu (1820), he was granted ō-metsuke rank, and furthermore, in connection with the succession, was appointed go-zenban. Go-konando continued to be held concurrently as before.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Bunsei 4, Kanoto-Mi (1821), he was appointed with concurrent go-kinshū-metsuke duties.
　　　　　On the 28th day of the 12th month of Bunsei 12, Tsuchinoto-Ushi (1829), he was granted succession to the main family line as requested.
1. On the 24th day of the 9th month of Tenpō 4, Mizunoto-Mi (1833), his family lineage was recognized favorably, and furthermore, having served with distinguished devotion, he was appointed go-yō-yaku.
