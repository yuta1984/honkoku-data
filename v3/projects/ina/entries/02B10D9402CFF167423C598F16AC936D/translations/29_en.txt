Article: When there are wind-broken or fallen trees within the government forest, we shall immediately report this to the authorities. Even village officials and forest guards must not arbitrarily take liberties in private. If they overlook or ignore those who commit misdeeds within the government forest, and during subsequent inspections new cut stumps are found and we face investigation with no viable excuse, it goes without saying that even the village officials will be subject to whatever punishment is decreed.

Article: Regarding the bamboo groves, because the village officials have neglected supervision for many years and left them as they were, bamboo grass and shrubs have grown thick within the government bamboo groves, and good bamboo does not grow. Again, we have no excuse for these matters of concern. When next spring arrives, we will construct sturdy fencing on all four sides and perform maintenance so that good bamboo will sprout and grow. Until now, Shukeiji Temple served as the grove guardian, but following this official investigation, it has been decreed that this should be done by someone from among the farmers.
