Okuda Saheiji
Later Kyūzaemon
Renamed Yōkei

During the reign of Lord Shinnyoin
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kyōhō 11, year of hinoe-uma (1726), promoted from assistant page (ochū-koshō soban) to page inspector (ochū-koshō metsuke), granted a stipend of 10 ryō and provisions for 3 people
1. On the last day of the 3rd month of the same 16th year, kanoto-i (1731), relieved of duties by lord's discretion

During the reign of Lord Genryūin  
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Genbun 4, tsuchinoto-mi (1739), appointed as acupuncturist due to his dedication to acupuncture medicine, granted an increase of 3 hyō for a total of 33 hyō and provisions for 3 people, ranked equivalent to ashigaru physician, renamed Yōkei
1. On the 28th day of the 3rd month of Hōreki 3, mizunoto-tori (1753), retired as requested
End of service record
