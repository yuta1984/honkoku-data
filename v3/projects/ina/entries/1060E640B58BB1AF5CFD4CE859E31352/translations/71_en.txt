1. On the 13th day of the 10th month of the 8th year, Boar (1779), he was appointed as assistant duty officer and granted support allowance for three retainers.
1. On the 13th day of the 11th month of the 9th year, Rat (1780), as his late father Mokutayū had requested, he inherited the family headship without issue and was granted a stipend of 100 koku, taking his place in the guard duty rank order.
1. On the 8th day of the 7th month of Tenmei 3, Rabbit (1783), by special consideration, although he could have been granted permanent leave, through extraordinary compassion he was retained in the house, granted a stipend of 50 koku and support for three retainers, and appointed to manage local affairs.
1. On the 12th day of the 8th month of Tenmei 7, Sheep (1787), he was appointed to fire watch duty for ancestral tablets.
1. On the 10th day of the 5th month of Kansei 3, Boar (1791), he was appointed as military equipment magistrate.

During the reign of Raiin-kun:
1. On the 28th day of the 4th month of Kansei 4, Rat (1792), he was ordered to relocate to Edo.
1. On the 28th day of the 5th month of the same year, he was appointed as senior inspector, construction magistrate, and chief administrator concurrently.
1. On the 7th day of the 7th month of Kansei 5, Ox (1793), he received an increase of 50 koku, restoring his former stipend to 100 koku.

1. On the 15th day of the 6th month of Kansei 6, Tiger (1794), he was appointed as advance guard captain, holding three positions concurrently.
1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kansei 8, Dragon (1796), he was appointed as administrative officer, and for his diligent service in financial matters since the Rat year, he was granted a ceremonial robe and relieved of his three concurrent positions.
1. On the 2nd day of the 2nd month of Kansei 12, Monkey (1800), by special consideration he was relieved of administrative duties and appointed as messenger and intermediary.
1. On the 29th day of the 8th month of Kyōwa 2, Dog (1802), his official duties and stipend allocation were revoked, he was ordered into house confinement, and his entire household was placed under the custody of Iida Ichitayū.
1. On the 23rd day of the 3rd month of Bunka 1, Rat (1804), his custody was lifted, he was granted quarters in the lower residence, and given support allowance for six retainers.
1. On the 22nd day of the intercalary 8th month of Bunka 2, Ox (1805), he died of illness (at age 45).
1. On the 25th day of the same month, the order lifting his house confinement was issued posthumously.
1. On the 20th day of the 10th month of the same year, Iida Takuma was appointed to inherit his family name.
