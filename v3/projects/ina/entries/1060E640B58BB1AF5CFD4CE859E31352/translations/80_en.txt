Tanaka Jōzaemon
Original name: Jizaemon

During the reign of Lord Shingo-in
- In December of Hōei 2, year of Tori (1705), he was promoted to the rank of o-kachi (foot soldier) and received an additional stipend of 2 bu in gold, bringing his total to 4 ryō
　Note: It is unclear when he began his service, but it appears that he served as a subordinate in the accounting department (go-kanjō-kata) at the end of the Genroku era, with a stipend of 3 ryō 2 bu and rations for 2 people

During the reign of Lord Shinnyo-in  
- On November 29th of Shōtoku 4, year of Uma (1714), he was appointed to escort duty and accounting department (go-tomōban go-kanjō-kata), received an additional 1 ryō, bringing his total to 5 ryō. He was under the supervision of a chief retainer, previously under Okutsu Seizō's supervision
- On August 15th of Shōtoku 5, year of Hitsuji (1715), he was ordered to transfer to the accounting department in Edo and received an additional 2 ryō, bringing his total to 7 ryō
　Note: Due to financial difficulties and many burdensome responsibilities, he declined and appears to have traveled annually from his home domain to fulfill his duties
- On December 7th of Kyōhō 3, year of Inu (1718), he was appointed as assistant to the revenue collection department (bunichi-kata soe-yaku)
- On May 20th of Kyōhō 5, year of Ne (1720), he was appointed to the main position in the revenue collection department (bunichi-kata hon-yaku)
"(In red ink) Tanaka Jō"
