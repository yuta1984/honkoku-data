Kawanishi Kobei
During the reign of Shingo-in-sama:
1. On the 5th day of the intercalary 5th month of Shōtoku 3, year of the Water Snake (1713), he was appointed to succeed his late father without change, but due to his young age, he was assigned to the small construction corps.

During the reign of Shin'nyo-in-sama:
1. In Kyōhō 1, year of the Fire Monkey (1716), he requested and was granted permission to relocate to the lord's residence (Takato), arriving at Takato on the 12th day of the 3rd month.

1. From the beginning of Kyōhō 10, year of the Earth Boar (1725), he changed his name to Sajiemon. Previously he had been called Sajirō.

1. From the 7th day of the 3rd month of Kyōhō 20, year of the Metal Rabbit (1735), he changed his name to Kobei.

During the reign of Genryū-in-sama:
1. On the 6th day of the 4th month of Genbun 1, year of the Fire Dragon (1736), he was ordered to relocate to Edo, arriving in Edo on the 7th day of the 5th month.

1. On the 7th day of the 10th month of Genbun 2, year of the Fire Snake (1737), he was appointed as head of foot soldiers.

1. On the 18th day of the 9th month of Genbun 3, year of the Earth Horse (1738), he was appointed to personal attendance while concurrently serving as head of foot soldiers.

1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Genbun 6, year of the Metal Rooster (1741), as head of foot soldiers, he was henceforth appointed to the senior position among the front palace officials.

1. On the 15th day of the 1st month of Enkyō 3, year of the Fire Tiger (1746), he was granted the rank of inspector-general, continuing as head of foot soldiers but being relieved of personal attendance duties.

1. On the 5th day of the 4th month of the same year, due to circumstances involving his nephew Shioda Yohachiō, he requested permanent leave, but since the nephew was young, he was granted leave for one or two years as requested for supervision purposes.

1. On the 20th day of the 1st month of the following year of the Rabbit, he was ordered to return to service with his original stipend of 170 koku and was assigned to guard duty.

1. On the 6th day of the 6th month of year 5, year of the Earth Dragon (1748), he was granted inspector-general rank and ordered to return to the position of head of foot soldiers.

1. On the 17th day of the 7th month of Kan'en 2, year of the Metal Horse (1749), he was appointed as assistant inspector, relieved of the position of head of foot soldiers, but his administrative authority remained as before.

1. On the 16th day of the 6th month of Hōreki 2, year of the Water Monkey (1752), he died of illness.
