- On August 19th of Kyōhō 6, year of Ushi (1721), he was appointed to the rank of o-chū-koshō (middle page)代官役 (deputy administrator) and received a role stipend of rations for 2 people
- On December 23rd of Kyōhō 10, year of Mi (1725), by special consideration his role was dismissed and he was placed under the supervision of chief retainers

　　His years of service are unknown
　- Jirōtayū was summoned as a messenger foot soldier (o-tsukai kachi) on April 14th of Shōtoku 1, year of U (1714), and was told he could also serve in record-keeping duties
　- On December 4th of Kyōhō 9, year of Tatsu (1724), he was placed under the supervision of the foot soldier commander
　- On March 9th of Kyōhō 11, year of Uma (1726), he inherited his deceased father's position with a stipend of 5 ryō and rations for 2 people, with duties continuing as before
　- On July 23rd of Kyōhō 12, year of Hitsuji (1727), he was appointed to the purchasing department
　　Note: This article was researched and recorded here, continuing from this point forward

Original names: Hanshichi
Tanaka Jirōtayū　　　Shōemon

During the reign of Lord Shinnyo-in
- On August 28th of Kyōhō 19, year of Kinoe-Tora (1734), he was appointed as temporary secretary to the chief retainers
- On August 7th of Kyōhō 20, year of Kinoto-U (1735), he was appointed to the permanent position
- On December 28th of the same year, he was granted escort guard rank and appointed to return to the purchasing department

During the reign of Lord Genryū-in
- On December 23rd of Genbun 2, year of Hinoto-Mi (1737), he was appointed to the accounting department, dismissed from the purchasing department, and assigned to regular rotating duty
- On January 15th of Genbun 6, year of Kanoto-Tori (1741), he was appointed head of servants and received 22 hyō (rice measure) and rations for 3 people
- On November 1st of Enkyō 2, year of Kinoto-Ushi (1745), he received a formal rank as middle page with an increase of 4 hyō, bringing his total to 26 hyō, and was appointed to supervise the rice purchasing coordination office
- On September 18th of Hōreki 5, year of Kinoto-I (1755), his role was dismissed as requested
- On December 11th of the same year, he retired as requested
