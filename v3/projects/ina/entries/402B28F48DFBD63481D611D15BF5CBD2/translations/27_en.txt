The time for this to be granted has arrived. However, the mother and child should reform their hearts and apologize for their mistakes, and therefore the mother should be sent to remain somewhere between駿府 (Sunpu) and Edo. Masanori, having received the favor of the Kantō for many years, harbors no duplicitous thoughts, and his wife and children are also in the Kantō. If you do not allow them to submit, then Masanori will stand against the forces of the realm, charge forward, and swiftly defeat your forces. Even if you come to regret this when that time arrives, it will be of no avail. Even if it is not yet time for your family fortune to come to an end, it depends on your decision."

Thus it was written. On the same sixteenth day, Masanori's messenger again rushed to the Ōgosho's camp at Okazaki and presented a letter. In this, Masanori requested to be given the vanguard so he could charge forward and swiftly punish those parties. The Ōgosho said, "What Masanori states is admirable. However, he should remain in the Kantō as he is, and send all his wife and children into the castle," and sent the messenger back. On the twenty-second day, Takenaka Izu-no-kami Shigetoshi was summoned to the camp at Nagahara, and ordered to hurry down to Masanori's domain and, together with Bingo-no-kami Masakatsu, raise those forces and head toward Osaka.
