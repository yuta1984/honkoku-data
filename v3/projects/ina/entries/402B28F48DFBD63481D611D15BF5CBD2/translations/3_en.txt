　　　　Gamō　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Hankanfu Volume 12, Part 1

Jijū and Hida-no-kami Fujiwara Hideyuki was the eldest son of Sangi and Hida-no-kami Ujisato.
Masatoshi, six generations after Chiharu, the second son of Chinzei-fu-shōgun Hidehira and himself a Chinzei-fu-shōgun (not shown in the genealogy), was the first to come up to the capital from Mutsu and was granted Gamō District in Ōmi Province, taking the name Gamō Tarō (the Gamō-ki states this was during the Heike period, while the genealogy shows it as during Lord Yoritomo's time. Upon examination, the genealogy and Gamō-ki differ in many of their accounts). His son Toshikata served the Right General of Kamakura (the genealogy shows him as Uma-no-jō). (Toshikata, son of Hidetoshi, was initially called Koretaka, it is said.) Toshikata served the Right General of Kamakura and belonged to the Ashikaga house, performing military service.
