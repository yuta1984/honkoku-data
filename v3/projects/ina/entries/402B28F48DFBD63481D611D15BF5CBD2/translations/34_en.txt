Mitsumasa became angry and said that capturing people in the castle town at will without even informing the magistrate was extremely unreasonable. When Masayori heard this, he replied, "What problem could there be with Masayori pursuing and capturing Masayori's own retainers? How can one accept such a strange matter?" The Great Retired Lord heard of this and had the ropes removed from the captured retainer, sent him away, and imprisoned those who had pursued and captured him. Soon after, the Osaka campaign began, so there was no further judgment on the matter.

In the fifth month of Genna 1 (1615), after the Osaka affair ended, at the end of the sixth month of the same year, Kobori Shinsuke [marginal note: later Tōtōmi-no-kami] and Nakabō Sakon were sent as messengers to Masayori with the message: "According to what your retainers have reported, your crimes are not light, but because of the meritorious service of your elder brother Masanori, there was no punishment. Yet you have again committed crimes carelessly, and moreover spoke rudely to one who would be a magistrate - your crimes have already accumulated. However, through separate consideration, these crimes are being pardoned," and his domain was confiscated. [Marginal note: From this time, Uda Castle fell into ruin] [Marginal note: Masayori had two sons who were said to be summoned by the Shogun's house]
