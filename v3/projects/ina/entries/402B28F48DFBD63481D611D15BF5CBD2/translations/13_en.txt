The Taiko rose from his seat and withdrew into the inner chambers. Jibu-no-shō Mitsunari came forward and addressed Hideaki's retainers Sugihara Shimōtsuke-no-kami and Yamaguchi Gemba-no-jō, saying, "His lordship is not in good humor. You should first escort him back to his residence." Upon hearing this, Hideaki showed signs of wanting to strike down that fellow, taking up his sword and standing. Tokugawa-dono restrained him, calming him down in various ways and accompanying him to his residence.

There, the Taiko's messenger, the nun Kō Kuranosuke, arrived and conveyed the following message: "Your recent frivolous conduct at the Battle of Ulsan, and now your present statements, are extremely rash. You must immediately return and surrender Chikuzen Province and move to Echizen Province." The Chūnagon (Hideaki) became greatly enraged and said, "Hey, nun! If there is any fault in Hideaki's conduct, my life would be taken immediately. I am not aware of any crime. As long as I live, things should remain as they were. You may have my head cut off immediately!" The nun departed after saying this.

Tokugawa-dono addressed Kō Kuranosuke, saying, "You should have replied, 'I humbly accept your command.'" The nun acknowledged this and said, "In this matter, it must be left to the Naidafu's discretion. I must also report this matter to the Mandokoro," and then withdrew. Tokugawa-dono said to Hideaki, "In any case, it would be best if you followed the command. If the Mandokoro is made to grieve, the Taiko will not remain so resolute." "Well then..."
