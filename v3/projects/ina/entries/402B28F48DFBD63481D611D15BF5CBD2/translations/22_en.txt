{{Note: Ina was the commander of ashigaru (foot soldiers) in the vanguard of the Tokugawa forces}} Masanori became increasingly unable to contain his anger and sent back all the heads, saying: "Generally speaking, people in this world have distinctions between high and low status. The noble do not receive the same treatment as the lowly, and the lowly receive different treatment from the noble - who could not know this? When I sent the head of Masanori's retainer, all the heads I received in return appear to be those of ashigaru foot soldiers. Their ranks are completely unequal. Although I, Masanori, am of humble ability, there is no doubt that as an ally of Lord Tokugawa, I led the vanguard and achieved considerable merit. This was not Masanori's merit alone, but entirely because my household retainers and followers threw away their lives and did not care for themselves. Yet now, to have Masanori's retainer treated as equivalent to ashigaru belonging to the Tokugawa house means that Masanori has lost face before Heaven. Moreover, the head of the retainer that Masanori sent was only one. I do not wish to receive many heads in return. There is no point in receiving these heads, so I shall return them promptly." Hearing this, Ii and Honda were greatly surprised and said, "What Masanori says is perfectly reasonable. In that case, we shall cut off the head of a mounted retainer belonging to Ina's forces and send it to you. For Ina and you to reconcile would be a great fortune for both public and private matters - what could surpass this?" However, Masanori had become estranged from his household retainers and followers, making it undesirable to undertake serious military action again, and now even with Lord Tokugawa...
