2. Hossu (Buddhist ceremonial whisk) - One piece
This hossu was transmitted from the two masters Ingen and Mokuan of the Ōbaku sect through Zen master Ryōkō to Kōrin, the founding abbot of our temple's restoration, and has since been treasured by our temple. While it is clear that it was originally manufactured in Tang China, it is not known in detail from which era or by whose hand it was made.

3. Hanging scroll with Sanskrit shadow image of Yakushi Nyorai - One scroll

[Left page]

This hanging scroll is on paper and written by the brush of Kōbō Daishi (Kūkai). It was originally a hereditary treasure of the Ōgiya Tokuemon family of Takato town for generations, but during the time of the sixth generation Tokuemon Hisatomi, it was entrusted to our temple due to a spiritual dream revelation. Later, in November of Meiji 6 (1873), it was permanently donated to our temple by Komatsu Tokue, the current head of that family. While the exact date of the Great Master's calligraphy is not known in detail, it possesses an elegance that evokes ancient refinement.

4. Temple bell - One piece
This temple bell has a mouth diameter of two feet and five inches. It was cast on September 6th of Shōhei 10, Kinoto-Hitsuji year, that is, Bunwa 4 of the Northern Court (1355). It originally belonged to Ruishūzan Kai[seiji temple] in Igara village, Shimo-Ina district.
