It is hoped that present and future resident monks will also accumulate virtue and strive not to shame the past of our temple.

Our temple has possessed several branch temples since ancient times, but now it is a small head temple having two branch temples - namely Hōonzan Tōsenji and Ankyozan Shōzuiji - along with over two hundred parishioners. Now, in this great era of Meiji, when the Way of Buddha's Law is increasingly flourishing, from now on Ryūtakuzan Keisenjin, as a small head temple of the Sōtō sect possessing two branch temples and many parishioners,

While it is reverent to speak of the Buddha ancestors and sect patriarchs, we look up to the inherited virtue of the founding patriarch and successive generations of resident monks, and wish to transmit this legacy for a long time into the future, just as the famous Katsurai spring water flows clear and never ceases. How venerable this is!

1. Standing statue of Yakushi Nyorai (Medicine Buddha) - One body
   This statue of Yakushi Nyorai is made of wood and measures two feet and four inches in height. It is attributed to the work of Gyōki Bosatsu.
   It was originally the principal image of Hokutōzan Kōseiji, a former branch temple of our temple.
