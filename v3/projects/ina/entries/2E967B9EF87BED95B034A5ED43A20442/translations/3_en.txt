Regarding the matter of humbly submitting an oral statement with utmost reverence

First, concerning my sake brewery, storehouse, land, and sake brewing licenses that I possess in Honmachi, in response to the petition submitted by Iseya Zensuke, I have been ordered to provide a reply. Since there are discrepancies in each of the points raised by said person, I humbly submit the following articles.

First, regarding the matter of the sake brewery premises being lent by Rokubei to Heihachi of Shimo-Oshima Village in previous years and subsequently becoming abandoned land - this matter does not concern me, so I will not provide an answer.

First, in the 8th month of Tempō 8, Year of the Cock (1837), after Iseya Rokubei's residential property in Honmachi was destroyed by fire in the Year of the Horse, and since he could not rebuild, he earnestly requested my father Risaemon to allow him to mortgage the residence and other properties, and to begin sake brewing business once the house and storehouse were completed. Accordingly, for a term of 10 years, with a frontage of 8 ken and 3 shaku, extending to the rear boundary, plus one grain shop, I lent him 50 ryō as deposit money and kept the term contract document. There was absolutely no instance of beginning this business due to entreaties or such. The claim of a 15-year term is greatly at variance with the facts. It was a 10-year term.
