1. In the 11th year, Kinoe-Inu (1814), 1st day of the 9th month: Since Otaki, daughter of Takaki Kihachi, was to be sent elsewhere, he was granted the Izawa Ichijūrō residence and accordingly appointed to a caretaking position.

1. In the 13th year, Hinoe-Ne (1816), 7th day of the 7th month: His caretaking duties for Otaki were successfully completed and dismissed, and he was assigned to a residence change.

1. Same year, intercalary 8th month, 18th day: Appointed as head of servants and baggage caretaker concurrently, with rest quarters duties continuing as before.

1. In the 14th year, Hinoto-Ushi (1817), 8th day of the 10th month: Appointed as a mid-ranking page (gochūkoshō), granted an increase of two hyō and one person's stipend, and successfully dismissed from rest quarters duties. However, head of servants position continued as before.

Reign of Lord Yoriyasu
1. Bunsei 5, Mizunoe-Uma (1822), 28th day of the 7th month: Appointed as acting inspector of mid-ranking pages during the lord's absence in Osaka.

1. Same 9th year, Hinoe-Inu (1826), 25th day of the 2nd month: Since town residents of good reputation were neglecting their duties to their masters, he was appointed as their supervisor. Other duties continued as before.

1. Same 11th year, Tsuchinoe-Ne (1828), 10th day of the 5th month: Granted an increase of two hyō.

1. Same 13th year, Kanoe-Tora (1830), 18th day of the 3rd month: Appointed as head of the great treasury clothing department concurrently. However, his additional duties as head of servants, baggage caretaker, and supervisor of direct retainers' quarters were successfully dismissed.

1. Tenpō 2, Kanoto-U (1831), 15th day of the 1st month: Appointed to the rank of inspector of mid-ranking pages, successfully dismissed from concurrent head of great treasury clothing department, and appointed to the next watch duty.

1. Same 8th year, Hinoto-Tori (1837), 23rd day of the 11th month: Due to illness, retired as requested, and succession was granted to his son Genta.
