Heir of Yoshida Tazaemon
Yoshida Toyohachi
Later named Kanbei
Also named Kakuemon

During the reign of Lord Genryuin
- September, 5th year of Meiwa (1768): Appointed as yobitsugu (page/attendant), granted one-person stipend

During the reign of Lord Honryuin  
- June 20th, 1st year of Kansei (1789): Appointed to Buddhist monk duties, granted two-person stipend
  Ordered to adopt the s≈çhatsu hairstyle
- July of the same year: Appointed to serve concurrently in the inner quarters (oku-tsutome)
- January 15th, 3rd year of Kansei (1791): Ordered to have head shaved in Osaka,
  granted 100 hiki of gold

During the reign of Lord Yorimoto-kun
- March 23rd, 4th year of Kansei (1792): Ordered to return to secular life
- June 1st of the same year: Due to administrative changes, 
  relieved of duties, granted one-person stipend without service
- August 23rd, 5th year of Kansei (1793): Re-employed, appointed to inner quarters monk duties,
  stipend increased by one person, making it two-person stipend total
