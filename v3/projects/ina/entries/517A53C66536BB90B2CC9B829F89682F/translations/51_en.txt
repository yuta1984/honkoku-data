Yoshise Zen'emon
Changed name to Kisaku
Also Buzaemon

During the reign of Lord Genryuin
- On August 11th of Genbun 4, Kinoto-hitsuji year (1739), he was granted 6 koku 2 to and 2-person stipend as successor to his deceased father's position, and was appointed to serve as a monk
- On February 15th of Kanpo 4, Kinoe-ne year (1744), he was appointed as御供方御徒士 (attendant foot soldier) and granted 18 hyō and 2-person stipend
- On August 14th of Meiwa 4, Hinoto-i year (1767), he was appointed as inspector of foot soldiers with an increase of 2 hyō
- On April 28th of the same 7th year, Kanoe-tora (1770), he was given the rank of attendant guard with an increase of 4 hyō

During the reign of Lord Honryuin
- On April 21st of An'ei 7, Tsuchinoe-inu year (1778), he was appointed to concurrently serve in provisions management, while continuing as foot soldier inspector under the supervision of the magistrate
- On March 15th of Tenmei 6, Hinoe-uma year (1786), he was honorably relieved of his position as foot soldier inspector
- On September 21st of the same 7th year, Hinoto-hitsuji (1787), he was relieved of provisions management duties and assigned to the next guard rotation
