Yoshise Katsubei
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Later called Buzaemon

Reign of Lord Yorimoto
- On August 23rd of Bunsei 2, Tsuchinoto-u year (1819), appointed as temporary foot soldier
　Accordingly granted 1.5-person sustenance allowance as assistance

Reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- On August 8th of the same 3rd year, Kanoe-tatsu (1820), appointed as assistant scribe, granted an increase of half sustenance allowance
- On November 25th of the same 6th year, Mizunoto-hitsuji (1823), relieved of temporary service due to personnel changes, granted half sustenance allowance
- On October 15th of the same 10th year, Hinoto-i (1827), appointed as temporary assistant due to shortage of inner attendants. Though of equivalent rank, ordered to consider himself as foot soldier
- On June 15th of the same 11th year, Tsuchinoe-ne (1828), satisfactorily relieved of temporary service, granted monetary reward
- On December 15th of the same year, when the scribal department is short-staffed or when there are many documents to process, appointed to work in the scribal office as document assistant
- On April 15th of the same 12th year, Tsuchinoto-ushi (1829), appointed to rural affairs office duty to familiarize himself with service matters, as the rural department is constantly busy. Must assist with investigations by deputy magistrates, including forestry and firewood department investigations, and serve in any other capacity as needed. Under district magistrate supervision
- On January 15th of Tenpo 2, Kanoto-u year (1831), appointed as concurrent scribe for senior councilors and chamberlains in temporary service, promoted to junior position in formal retainer guard rank, granted increase of 1.5-person sustenance allowance and 2-person assistance allowance
- On October 13th of the same 5th year, Kinoe-uma (1834), promoted to regular position in formal retainer guard rank, granted allocation of 18 hyo and 2-person sustenance allowance, additionally appointed as supervisor of "Nao" character room staff. Scribal duties to continue as before
- On November 23rd of the same 8th year, Hinoto-tori (1837), granted increase of 2 hyo
- On February 9th of the same 13th year, Mizunoe-tora (1842), daughter born
- On May 22nd of the same 14th year, U (1843), father Sogo granted retirement as requested due to illness. Katsubei, having served diligently for several years, granted succession with stipend of 24 hyo and 2-person sustenance allowance. Promoted to formal retainer guard, duties to continue as before
- On October 28th of the same 15th year, Tatsu (1844), additionally appointed as treasury and stable supervisor. Scribal duties to continue as before for the time being
- On January 28th of Kaei 2, Tori year (1849), appointed to supply management
