Yoshise Kitayu
- On December 26th of Hoei 3, Tsuchinoe-inu year (1706), promoted to formal foot soldier (kakushiki o-ashigaru)
　　Note: Around the Hoei period, he served as secretary to District Magistrate Kono Hachiro'emon, and at that time appears to have been a formal foot soldier. Stipend: 4 ryo 2 bu and 2-person sustenance allowance
- On June 28th of Shotoku 4, Hinoe-uma year (1714), appointed as foot soldier inspector with an increase of 2 bu, bringing total stipend to 5 ryo
- On March 12th of Kyoho 1, Hinoe-saru year (1716), appointed to purchasing duties with an increase of 2 bu, bringing total stipend to 5 ryo
- [2 bu?] granted 1-person sustenance allowance for official duties
- [Same 5th year?] Ne year (1720), November 25th, relieved of purchasing duties and appointed to matters concerning lacquer [?] and candles
- [Genbun?] 4th year, Tsuchinoto-hitsuji (1739), April 22nd, relieved of foot soldier inspector position upon his own request due to old age and infirmity
- July 5th of the same year, died of illness
