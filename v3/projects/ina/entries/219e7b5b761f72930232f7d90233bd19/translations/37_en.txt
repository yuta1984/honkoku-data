First, when buying and selling houses and residential lots, the seller must petition in advance, then prepare a deed and submit it unsigned to the town representative office. After that, they must petition the monthly duty town elder, and in the presence of witnesses, compare the official duties, measurements, etc. with the deed to confirm there are no discrepancies before approval is granted. Both the seller and buyer, along with the guarantors, must appear at the town office

【Left Page】

to revise the names in the register and affix their seals to the deed. After that, an endorsement with seal impression shall be made and delivered. At that time, the deed shall be transferred from the seller to the buyer. Naturally, if there are subsequent transfers in later years, and furthermore if there are transferees thereafter, they must attach the property deed to the new deed and keep them together.
