Regarding official government affairs, although immediate levying of special taxes (goyōkin) could be ordered, as everyone has gradually come to understand, through His Lordship's profound benevolence and extraordinary nurturing care for the townspeople, the hardships of the common people are of utmost concern to him. This is the primary intention, and regarding the matter of special taxes, without issuing such orders, after thorough deliberation, concerning the debts that cannot be immediately settled, four persons have been entrusted with this responsibility. The intention is to minimize external borrowing as much as possible, so that if future domain expenses can be met within the domain itself, wasteful money will not flow to other places, naturally enriching the local community and improving financial circulation.
