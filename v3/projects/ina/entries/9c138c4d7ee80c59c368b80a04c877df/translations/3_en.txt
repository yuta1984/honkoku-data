Regarding the matter of Daizō and Gengorō-emon, detailed instructions were given in writing. One written document was delivered to Gengorō-emon's side, and one written document was given to Daizō's side.

Written statement from the 11th year, Year of the Horse
1. When I went to Edo this spring, regarding the matter of my great-uncle Matsumoto Jūrō-emon's nephew being struck down, after investigation I received a pardon, and I was told that I may reside anywhere without any restrictions, for which I am most grateful. This was announced in the presence of Inspector (metsuke) Ogasawara Heibē and Town Magistrate Suwa Mino-no-kami, and having received these instructions, I have returned.

    7th month, 8th day
                          Hongō Daizō [seal]
    To the Magistrate's Office

1. Hongō Daizō returned on the night of the 7th day of the 7th month, and when this was reported to the South Town Magistrate's Office the following day, after investigation it was found that when he went to Edo he gave no prior notice, and moreover he had written in the name of his representative, so he was ordered into house confinement (hissoku). This matter was announced to him.
