1. On the 20th day of the 9th month of Kan'en 3 (1750), permission was granted for his sister to marry Kawamura Kōsuke, a retainer of Lord Ōta Settsu-no-kami.

1. On the 23rd day of the 12th month of Hōreki 3 (1753), he was appointed to the rank of gokūban-kakushiki (attendant guard rank).

1. In the 1st month of the same 5th year (1755), permission was granted to take the daughter of Nakayama Tōhachi as his second wife.

1. On the 1st day of the 9th month of Meiwa 5 (1768), as the 6th day would fall on a rabbit day, he was ordered to perform proxy worship at Myōgi.

1. On the 28th day of the 4th month of the same 7th year (1770), he was appointed as gokūkata gochūkoshō (attendant page), granted an increase of 8 hyō and 1 person's stipend, receiving a total of 30 hyō and 3 persons' stipends, under the supervision of the goyōnin (administrator).

1. In the 2nd month of the same 9th year (1772), his son Ginjirō underwent the maegamitori ceremony and changed his name.

1. On the 11th day of the 8th month of An'ei 2 (1773), having served faithfully in Edo duty for several decades, he was honorably relieved of his attendant guard duties and sent back to his home domain. His duties would be determined at his home domain.

1. On the 3rd day of the 4th month of Kansei 1 (1789), his daughter was divorced from Yoneyama Senji.

1. On the 1st day of the 12th month of the same 3rd year (1791), his adopted son Banzō died.

1. On the last day of the 8th month of the same 4th year (1792), as his adopted son had recently died of illness, permission was granted to adopt Tō'uemon, younger brother of Kawakami Kuuemon (a masterless samurai of Lord Mizuno Hayato-no-shō currently residing in Shiojiri post town), and to arrange his marriage with his daughter.

1. On the 3rd day of the 12th month of the same year, due to misconduct in his household, his rank and stipend allocation were revoked, and he was granted relief stipend of 2 persons' rations, with the promise that his family line would be restored later.

1. On the 28th day of the 9th month of the same 5th year (1793), regarding the adoption matter, it was announced through the lord's mercy as requested by Kojima Hachirōzaemon.

1. In the same year, he died of illness.

Adopted son
Kitahara Giuemon
Original name: Jiuemon

During the reign of Lord Yorimochi
1. On the 11th day of the 10th month of Kansei 5 (1793), although his adoptive father Bun'gorōemon had previously been punished, through exceptional mercy his position was restored, and he was appointed as gokūkata goashigaru (attendant foot soldier) with 18 hyō and 2 persons' stipends, under the supervision of the ashigaru head.

1. On the 28th day of the 12th month of the same year, he was ordered into house confinement due to his connection with Hyōdō Daiuemon, who was sentenced to death.

1. On the 19th day of the 2nd month of the same 6th year (1794), he was released from house confinement.
