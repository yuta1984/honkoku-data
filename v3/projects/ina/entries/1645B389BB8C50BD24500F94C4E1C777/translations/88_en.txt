Granted a stipend of one nin-buchi (rice rations for one person)
During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- On the last day of the 9th month of Bunsei 6 (1823), year of the sheep, he was appointed to hired monk duty, given an increase of one nin-buchi for a total of two nin-buchi, with duties to be performed following the instructions of the meal service attendants. His hair was ordered to be shaved. For this, he was granted 100 hiki in gold.
- On the 15th day of the 4th month of Bunsei 8 (1825), year of the rooster, as requested, his stipend was suspended and he was released from hired service to pursue medical studies, but due to special consideration, he continued to receive one nin-buchi.
- On the 5th day of the 6th month of Bunsei 10 (1827), year of the boar, a petition submitted while his father Shun'yō was still alive reached his lordship's attention. It was heard that Shun'yō had served diligently for many years, and although this person was still inexperienced, his dedication to studying both the family profession and medicine was profound. Due to both these special circumstances, he was granted succession with a stipend of six nin-buchi, appointed to the lowest seat of the lord's attendant guard rank, and would henceforth be summoned for audiences and assigned various duties.
　　By this decree, he was to work even harder in his studies to become useful for future service. He was placed under the supervision of the chamberlain.
- On the 6th day of the 7th month of Bunsei 13 (1830), year of the tiger, he died of illness at age 23
　　　　End of service
　　　　　　　Adopted son succeeding to the name
　　　　　　　　Uchiyama Sekigorō
- On the 8th day of the 2nd month of Tenpō 2 (1831), year of the rabbit, according to the deathbed wish of Uchiyama Shun'ei and the petition of his relatives: although the deceased father Shun'ei served only briefly, his deceased grandfather Shun'yō had served diligently for many years in medical treatment of their lordships. Due to these special circumstances, he was appointed to succeed to Shun'yō's name, granted a stipend of 5 koku and two nin-buchi, and appointed as a tonsured monk.
- On the 23rd day of the 7th month of Tenpō 3 (1832), year of the dragon, he was promoted to ashigaru (foot soldier) and appointed to attendant duty. Accordingly, he was granted provisions amounting to 18 hyō and two nin-buchi, under the supervision of the ashigaru commander.
- On the 10th day of the 10th month of Tenpō 8 (1837), year of the rooster, he was appointed as a lower official in the construction department, henceforth under the supervision of the construction magistrate, [assigned to] the lower mansion kitchen
