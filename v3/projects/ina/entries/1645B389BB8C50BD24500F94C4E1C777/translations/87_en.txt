Shun'yō
During the reign of Lord Yorimoto
- On the 8th day of the 6th month of Bunka 11 (1814), year of the dog, he was employed as a ashigaru (footsoldier) physician specializing in acupuncture treatment, granted a stipend of 7 nin-buchi (rice rations for 7 people), given 3 ryō in gold for needle treatment fees, and appointed to the position of second seat among the Ina-zatō in the lord's attendant guard rank.

During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu  
- On the 3rd day of the 2nd month of Bunsei 9 (1826), year of the dog
　　Due to matters concerning the retired lord's intentions
　　He was ordered to refrain from audience with the lord
- On the 18th day of the 7th month of the same year, the restraint was lifted
- On the 10th day of the 4th month of the 10th year of Bunsei (1827), year of the boar, he died of illness at the age of 54
　　　　End of service
