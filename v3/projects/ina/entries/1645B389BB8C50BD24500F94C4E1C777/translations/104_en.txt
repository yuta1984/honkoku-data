《Marginal note: Retainer of Lord Matsudaira Tamba-no-kami | Third son of Tarai Yasōzaemon》　　Kakinoki Sanjūrō

During the reign of Lord Yoriyasu
- Tenpō 11 (1840), 6th month, 13th day: At Aramachi village, as per the petition of Kojima Gunpei to inherit the family name of the physician Kakinoki Ryūtaku, it was granted as requested. Since there had been prior private petitions regarding this matter, he was newly employed with special consideration, granted a stipend of 20 hyō and rations for two persons, and appointed to the rank of gokūban-kaku gojiban.

- Kōka 2 (1845), 1st month, 15th day: Appointed as secretary (shoyaku).

- Kaei 4 (1851), 6th month, 13th day: Posted to Edo duty station.

- Same year, 7th month, 18th day: Temporarily appointed to attend the young lords' quarters and instructed to also report to the escort division.

- Kaei 5 (1852), 6th month, 23rd day: Appointed as temporary assistant secretary (goyūhitsu tōbun sukekin). All other duties remain as before.

- Same year, 12th month, 1st day: Relieved from stationed duty.

"(Red ink) Kakinoki"
