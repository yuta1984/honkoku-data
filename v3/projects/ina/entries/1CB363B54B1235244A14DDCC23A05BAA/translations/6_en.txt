One: Even in areas adjacent to contracted mountain sites where designated woodcutters enter the mountains, woodcutters shall not carelessly enter non-contracted areas.
　　Addendum: One's representatives at contracted mountain sites shall not lend their authority, and no timber, however small the amount, shall be transported. Most importantly, official seals shall not be lent or borrowed, nor shall head supervisors be appointed without proper authorization. Official seal holders, woodcutters, and day laborers shall not be left in charge, nor shall employed persons act at their own discretion.
One: Contracted mountain sites shall be divided by area and transferred on an annual basis.
　　Addendum: Inspection officials shall be dispatched to contracted mountain sites at irregular intervals.

One: During downstream transport, regarding riverbank rice fields and other fields, it goes without saying, but logs shall be floated so as not to damage bridges and river embankments. Should damage naturally occur at any location, the timber owner shall, without fail and according to previous practice, promptly make repairs and deliver compensation to the village headman.
One: Regarding advance payments, sustenance rice, and other loans to woodcutters and day laborers, promissory notes shall be exchanged, and at the time of later settlement, accounts shall be clearly balanced. Even if negligence occurs and delays arise later, matters without evidence shall be resolved through mutual agreement, and [disputes brought] to the government office...
