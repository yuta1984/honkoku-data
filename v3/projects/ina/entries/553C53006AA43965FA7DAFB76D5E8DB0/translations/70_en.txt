Appointed as district magistrate (daikan) with an increase of 2 hyō, under the supervision of the county magistrate (gundy)

- On the 3rd day of the 11th month of the 9th year, Inu (1835): Appointed to inspect crop failures of that year. After going out for several days and working diligently with various efforts, granted 200 mon as compensation for his service

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of the 10th year, Tsuchinoto-i (1839): Appointed to escort guard rotation and granted an increase of 2 hyō

- On the 28th day of the 1st month of the 11th year, Kanoe-ne (1840): For handling numerous ceremonial and official matters on this occasion, granted 100 mon as celebration money

- On the same day: For special handling requiring day and night efforts, granted crested hemp formal wear (kamishimo) as extraordinary recognition

- On the 25th day of the 12th month of the same year: Due to particular financial difficulties at year's end, reluctantly ordered to arrange special financing during his stay in the domain. Since his handling was thorough and resulted in exceptional financial arrangements, granted 100 mon through special channels

- On the 13th day of the 4th month of the 12th year, Kanoto-ushi (1841): Dismissed from district magistrate position, appointed to alternate duty under steward supervision, with restraint and consultation as directed

- On the 20th day of the 1st month of the 13th year, Mizunoe-tora (1842): On the 16th, while on alternate duty rotation, when there was waiting at the catering office, immediately assembled and worked diligently on various matters

This diligent service was exemplary, therefore granted soup and sake. This matter has been reported in advance, so it should be understood accordingly

- On the 28th day of the 2nd month of the same year: Reappointed to district magistrate position under county magistrate supervision

- On the 9th day of the 9th month of the 15th year, Tatsu (1844): Granted new construction at outer elevated site due to residence relocation

- On the 3rd day of the 12th month of the same year: Dispatched to Nagoya for rural district business, therefore granted 200 mon

- On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kōka 2, Mi (1845): During financial difficulties, worked hard in consultation, investigation matters were completed, granted 200 mon through special channels

- On the 20th day of the 11th month of the same year: For inspection efforts due to poor harvest that autumn, granted 100 mon

- On the 15th day of the 4th month of the 3rd year, Uma (1846): Appointed to rear guard duty during lord's journey to Edo

- On the 13th day of the 4th month of Kaei 3, Inu (1850): Loaned to Lord Naitō Awa-no-kami, appointed to escort duty to Nagasaki, arrived in Edo on the 25th of the same month, appointed as head retainer and financial administrator under Awa-no-kami on the 1st day of the 5th month

- On the 15th day of the 12th month of the 4th year, I (1851): Loaned to the related Lord Awa-no-kami, served as escort even to distant provinces. Since his official service performance was excellent and enhanced external reputation, granted the rank of middle page (chū-koshō), duty assignment remained the same, with an increase of 2 hyō and rations for 1 person, totaling 24 hyō and rations for 3 people
