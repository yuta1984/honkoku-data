During the reign of Lord Raiei-kun
- On July 13th, Bunsei 4, Kanoto-mi (1821), appointed as foot soldier in the escort unit
- On November 2nd, Bunsei 8, Kinoto-tori (1825), ordered to house confinement for hosting a drinking gathering, pardoned on December 3rd
- On April 15th, Bunsei 12, Tsuchinoto-ushi (1829), assigned to rural administration office to gain experience in service duties, as the rural office is constantly busy. In addition to official business investigated by deputy magistrates, also to assist with investigations of mountain and firewood affairs, and serve in any other matters as required. Under the supervision of the district magistrate
- On November 18th of the same year, appointed as head of firewood section and inspector of mountains, forests, and bamboo groves
- On November 18th, Tenpō 3, Mizunoe-tatsu (1832) (intercalary month), granted the rank of escort guard, with an increase of 2 hyō, satisfactorily relieved of duties as head of firewood section and inspector of mountains, forests, and bamboo groves, temporarily appointed to guard rotation under chief retainer supervision
- On August 3rd, Tenpō 4, Mizunoto-mi (1833), appointed as assistant captain of foot soldiers with additional duties as personal guard captain, under foot soldier captain supervision
- On January 15th, Tenpō 9, Tsuchinoe-inu (1838), appointed as head of servants and baggage supervisor, continuing as assistant foot soldier captain and personal guard captain as before, but under chief retainer supervision
- On May 3rd of the same year, for diligent service during the recent inspection tour passage, his efforts were reported to the lord who was pleased, and granted soup and sake
- On February 28th, Tenpō 13, Mizunoe-tora (1842), due to the heavy burden of concurrent duties as head of servants and baggage supervisor, satisfactorily relieved of temporary concurrent duties as assistant foot soldier captain and personal guard captain
- On March 8th of the same year, for the April Suwa Onbashira festival security deployment, assigned as assistant to Onodera Tōemon, granted 1 ryō as allowance and an additional 1 ryō 2 bu
- On January 25th, Tenpō 14, Mizunoto-u (1843), due to illness and as requested, relieved of duties as head of servants and baggage supervisor, appointed to guard rotation
- On July 13th, Kōka 2, Mi (1845), appointed as assistant captain of foot soldiers with additional duties as personal guard captain, henceforth under foot soldier captain supervision
- On April 15th, Kōka 3, Uma (1846), satisfactorily relieved of duties as assistant foot soldier captain and personal guard captain, appointed to guard rotation under chief retainer supervision
