Miyata Town District
Minamimichi-bara　　Currently called Nambayashi
One plot: Mixed forest 8 se (40 ken × 6 ken)
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Currently: Sukesaemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Tōzaemon

This land previously became replacement land for part of Hakushinji Temple's precincts due to the temple's petition in an unknown year. An annual tribute of 3 shō of rice was paid as undergrowth fee, but this fee was eliminated when the land became newly developed in Bunka 14, year of Ushi (1817).
In Bunsei 11, year of Tsuchinoe-ne (1828), it was sold to Tōzaemon. On the 23rd day of the 12th month of Tenpō 12, year of Kanoto-ushi (1841), as per the mountain dedication petition, it was announced with commendation, and the previously developed portions along with embankments were allowed to remain in exchange for tribute rice, with mountain guard duties permanently assigned. Sukesaemon was exempted.

Ōtagiri Hyūga-haha passage
One plot: Mixed forest 7 tan 3 se 2 bu (274 ken × 8 ken)
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Currently: Sukesaemon
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kihei

This land was entirely sold to Kamiho Village in Kansei 12, year of Kanoe-saru (1800).
In Bunsei 11, year of Tsuchinoe-ne (1828), it was sold to Kihei, and in the same 13th year, Kanoe-tora (1830), officials went to inspect the boundaries and established boundary markers consisting of 14 pine trees, 4 stones, and 3 mound locations.
On the 23rd day of the 12th month of Tenpō 12 (1841), as per the mountain dedication petition, it was announced, and through payment of grassland tribute rice, mountain guard duties were permanently assigned, and furthermore, official certification was granted. Sukesaemon was exempted.
