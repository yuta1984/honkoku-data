In the autumn of Keichō 5 (1600), Nagato-no-kami Moritaka was following Lord Tokugawa and rushing to join the campaign in Ōu when warfare broke out again in the Kamigata region. Ishida Mitsunari, knowing that Yoshitaka had long harbored resentment against Lord Tokugawa, immediately sent him a letter. Yoshitaka first had his son seize Toba Castle, gathered pirate ships, raided the ports and harbors throughout the Tōkai region, seized provisions and supplies, and fortified the castles of Mino and Owari provinces. He summoned Horiuchi Awa-no-kami Ujizen, a resident of Shingū in Kii Province, to defend Toba Castle, while he himself besieged Inaba Kurōdo's Iwate Castle. Ujiie Naizen-no-shō Sumitoshi sortied from Norina Castle and attacked Azenori. Moritaka defeated Ujiie, took many heads, and sent them to the Kantō, arriving at the camp at Nakaizumi. Since these were the first heads offered after Lord Tokugawa had headed west on this campaign, his appreciation was especially profound. When the Battle of Sekigahara resulted in the complete defeat of the western forces, Yoshitaka accompanied [the forces] to Shingū and fled to Kii Province.
