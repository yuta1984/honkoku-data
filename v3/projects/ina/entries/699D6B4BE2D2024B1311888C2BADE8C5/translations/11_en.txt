Without doubt, the western army had risen again. Masayuki received orders from Hideyori to join his side (the Toyotomi side). Now, what do you think about this? he asked. Hearing this, Izu-no-kami (Nobuyuki) said: "Since you, father, have long been on unfavorable terms with Lord Tokugawa, you will likely follow Hideyori's orders. Saemon-no-suke (Yukimura) has also become Otani's son-in-law, so he too will surely go to the western side. Nobuyuki has received Lord Naifū's (Tokugawa Ieyasu's) favor for many years, and moreover, having married Honda's daughter, through this blood relationship we have become close. To turn against him with a backstab at this moment would stain our ancestors' reputation and leave eternal shame for our descendants - this would be truly unfilial. In conclusion, as for Nobuyuki, I should serve by siding with the eastern faction (Tokugawa side)

Since Nobuyuki went east, Masayuki and Yukimura returned to Shinano and followed the western side. When Lord Tokugawa heard of this, he sent a letter expressing his extraordinary admiration for Nobuyuki's loyalty. When Chūnagon-dono (Tokugawa Hidetada) advanced through the mountain route, Masayuki and Yukimura plotted to delay his forces. Nobuyuki was granted the vanguard and captured the Isesaki fortress, but then firmly declined to continue as vanguard. Receiving permission, he did not proceed to Ueda Castle.
