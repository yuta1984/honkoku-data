"It would be regrettable to be accused of abandoning the Sanada by pushing them away. In any case, Kagekatsu should help them," he said and sent back the messenger. When it was heard that the Sanada had already defected (from Tokugawa), Yoda Uemon-no-suke Nobushige departed Komoro Castle with 1,500 troops and advanced on Yazawa Castle. Yazawa Tajima came out to fight, and Yoda was defeated and retreated. Kagekatsu left Shibata and sent a combined force of 6,500 troops from Echigo and Shinano to Ueda Castle, with Kawada Honjo as the commanding general. Also, thinking that Ogasawara might advance on Kawanakajima, he positioned 1,300 troops from Echigo under General Fujita Noto-no-kami at a position short of Sarugahaba. Meanwhile, when it was heard that Lord Tokugawa's forces were approaching, Masayuki first confined the Echigo forces in Ueda

Castle, and he himself took position at Mt. Onoyama with his own forces of over 9,000 men, dividing his troops among Maruko, Atsuko, Yazawa, Toishi, Urano, and Iseyama to fight, ultimately never suffering a single defeat. Also, when Hojo forces attacked Numata Castle, his eldest son Nobuyuki fought and defeated the attacking forces. Since Lord Tokugawa's forces gained no advantage and retreated from Ueda Castle, the Echigo forces also returned to their home province. Masayuki was greatly pleased and sent his second son Genjiro Yukimura, who had reached eighteen years of age, to Echigo Province. Kagekatsu soon granted Yukimura the village of Yashiro in Shinano Province, worth 1,000 kan.
