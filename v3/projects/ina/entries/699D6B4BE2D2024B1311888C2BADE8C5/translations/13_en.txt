Izu-no-kami Shigeno Nobuzumi was the son of Kawachi-no-kami Nobutaka and the eldest grandson of Izu-no-kami Nobuyuki. His father Nobuyoshi distinguished himself in the Osaka campaigns at age 15, and when war broke out again, he served as the Ōgosho's vanguard and took 27 heads to present as tribute. When he died before his father, his younger brother Nobumasa inherited the house, and his son Nobuzumi was granted Numata Castle and received court rank on the 26th day of the 12th month of Meireki 2 (1656). Nobuzumi's son Nobunari was granted court rank on the 25th day of the 12th month of Kanbun 9 (1669) and appointed to the position of Danjō-no-chū.

Kuki
Nagato-no-kami Fujiwara no Moritaka was the son of Ōsumi-no-kami Yoshitaka. His ancestors were among the Eight Shōji of Kumano in Kii Province, and their descendants took the name Kuki from the place where they resided. {{Annotation: Upon examination, Yukawa, Tamaki, Shingū, Yasuda, Imose, Nakatsugawa, Nonagase, and Yuasa are called the Eight Shōji of Kumano. It is unknown which shōji the Kuki ancestors were among them}} In the time of Takaryo, Moritaka's ancestor eight generations back, he crossed over to Ago District in Shima Province and fought against the Stone River Seven Islands group, finally subjugating the lands of Namikiri, Tanabe, Tategami and others. His grandson Yamato-no-kami Takatsugu also combined and ruled Tōshi District, and in the time of his son Yamashiro-no-kami Yasutaka, he first built a castle at Kamo-no-gō Tashiro and resided there.
