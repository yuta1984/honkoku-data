One should use the day of kinoe-saru (31st in the sexagenary cycle). Even when in haste, if not kinoe, one must invariably use a saru (monkey) day. Also, a day without strong wind and without rain should be considered a good day. In spring, one begins late and finishes late; in summer, one begins early and finishes early. For spring days, cloudy weather without wind is called the finest day; for summer, sunny weather with a light breeze is called a good day. Generally speaking, for devotees, one should not divide the seasons. Did people of old play kemari even under moonlight or lamplight? Also, when the Supplementary Middle Counselor was away for seven thousand days, the balls left there would not deteriorate during the four seasons. It would be most unseemly for anything disappointing to occur deep in autumn.

1. On the Suspended [Trees]
The proper arrangement uses four trees: willow, cherry, pine, and cockscomb tree. In addition, plum is also commonly used. Willow is placed in tatsumi (southeast), cherry in ushitora (northeast), pine in inui (northwest), and cockscomb tree in hitsujisaru (southwest). Plum may be placed in any corner where there is advantage toward the edge. At the four corners, one should begin planting from the tree of the northeast direction.
