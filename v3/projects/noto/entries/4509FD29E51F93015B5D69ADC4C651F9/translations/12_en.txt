The assessed rice yields were: 60,000 koku within Nei District, 20,000 koku within Nomi District, and 20,000 koku within Niikawa District. Of these, 16,800-plus koku were from the Urayama area, and 3,170-plus koku were from seven villages in the Toyama area. These seven villages were Koizumi, Ōmachi, Nezuka, Isobe, Fuse, Hane, and Takada. These seven villages later became part of Toyama domain. The total above was 100,000 koku.

This is recorded in the "Masutoyama-kyūki" (Old Records of Masutoyama). Also, according to the allocation of Shōhō 4 (1647), there were 60,000-plus koku throughout Nei District and 10,000-plus koku within Niikawa District. The number of villages included seven villages in the Toyama area, which constituted the seven villages of the Nei District circle.
