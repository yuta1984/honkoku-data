According to a left annotation in the chronicle, someone said that when Lord Chōmyō established the separate domains of the Toyama and Daishōji houses, initially the domain yields were not consolidated but were deliberately distributed throughout various locations within the twelve districts, with tax collection across the three provinces of Kaga, Etchu, and Noto. However, after Lord Chōmyō's death, Lord Toshinaga Awaji-no-kami made various proposals and consulted with Lord Toshiharu Hida-no-kami, gradually petitioning the main house

to allow both houses to consolidate their tax collection. This led to village exchanges, and through secret negotiations with Senior Councilor Lord Sakai Utanokamī, they finally received the red seal in Kanbun 4. It was precedent for branch family domain holders to receive red seals.

Upon consideration, the territories of both houses were previously distributed as scattered lands across the three provinces within the twelve districts, but this may perhaps be a misconception.
