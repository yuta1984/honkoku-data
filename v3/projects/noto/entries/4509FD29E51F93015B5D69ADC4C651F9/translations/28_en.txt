To Lord Maeda Ukon-no-taifu

1. The genealogy of the Nanukamachi house states: The founder Toshitaka, common name Magohachiro, was the younger brother of Lord Toshiie, the fifth son. His birth mother, Okowa, was the daughter of Yamamoto Wakasa-no-kami, lord of Ishiura Castle in Kaga Province. In Keicho 9 (1604), he went to Edo in Musashi Province and served under the Tokugawa. He participated in the siege of Osaka Castle during the Keicho era, was assigned to the vanguard, and achieved military merit. In Genna 2 (1616), he was granted 10,014 koku at Nanukamachi in Kanra District, Kozuke Province, and was appointed to the court rank of Junior Fifth Rank Lower Grade, Yamato-no-kami, etc.

1. Aochi Reikan's genealogical summary of the main domain states: Toshitaka was born in Bunroku 4, year of the boar (1595). In Genna 1, year of the rabbit (1615), he fled to the Eastern Capital without anyone's knowledge, accompanied only by Saito Gonroku and Fujii Sakuemon. The Tokugawa lord granted him a fief of 10,014 koku at Nanukamachi in Shimotsuke Province. The following year he was appointed Yamato-no-kami. From this time forward, he did not get along well with Lord Chomiyo for the rest of his life. He died at age 43.
