In the mountains owned by the villages, there are many rock caves approximately four shaku square. Wind constantly blows through them.

1. Leaf Stones (Konoha-ishi)
In the mountains owned by Iguchi Village and Higashinishihara Village of the same group, there is a place called Akasobu Mountain on Myōjin Mountain. Red mineral water constantly springs forth there. When tree leaves fall into it, they naturally turn to stone, and the patterns and veins of the leaves can be seen vividly.

1. Saimyōji Pagoda
Located in Saimyōji Village of Taizaemon Group, Ōtaki Village. It is said to be the site where Lord Saimyōji rested during his pilgrimage around the provinces in ancient times. It is located about three chō from the village.

1. Masugata Stone (Square-shaped Stone)
In the valley river within the territory of Yanami Village of the same group, there is a rock four ken square and about one jō six shaku high.

1. Kannon Falls
In the territory of Bessho Village of the same group, there is a waterfall about seven shaku high. Beside the falls stands a Kannon hall that the same village reveres as their tutelary deity.

1. Ryūgū Abyss (Dragon Palace Deep)
In the Konaderikawa River within the territory of Moriya Village of the same group, there is a deep pool about fifteen ken long and nine ken wide. Rain ceremonies are performed here during droughts.

1. Shunkan's Tomb
In the mountains owned by Kurisu Village of the same group, there is a burial mound eight ken by three ken and about five shaku high. It is a high mound built with Hōtatsu stone.

1. Unohana Mountain
Located in the mountains owned by Dōrinji Village of Denemon Group, Uryū Village, about twenty chō south of the main road.

1. Tonami Mountain
Located in the mountains owned by Matsuo Village of Kinemon Group, Kanaya Hongō Village, twenty-four to twenty-five chō south of the main road, near Unohana Mountain.
