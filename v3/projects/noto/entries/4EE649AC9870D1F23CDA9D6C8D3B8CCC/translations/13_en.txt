According to the biography of Great Master Daitō Keijo: In Tensho 6 (1578), when Lord Toshiie governed Noto Province, he founded Chōrei-ji in Nanao to pray for the repose of his mother's soul.

1. According to the genealogical record of Kōtoku-ji in Nanao from Jōkyō 2 (1685): The founding of our temple was in Bun'ei 11, year of Kinoe-inu (1274). When Lord Toshiie came to Noto Province, the thirteenth-generation abbot Kenjō inquired about the temple's history, and in Tensho 6, when moved to Shoguchi and the castle was built, he was granted a temple site in present-day Nanao.

According to the genealogical record from Jōkyō 2 by Gonbei of Ōsawa Village, Hōshi District, Noto Province: When Lord Kōtokuin (Toshiie) came down to Noto in Tensho 8 (1580), Kurotaki Yoichi of the Echigo forces had taken refuge in Anamizu Castle, so he attacked it. At that time, our ancestor Naiki promptly went out, served as guide, and performed his duties.

According to the "Chronicle of the Seven Hokuriku Provinces": In the intercalary third month of Tensho 8, Shibata Katsuie invaded Kaga Province in rebellion, defeated all enemies, and pacified Kaga. He placed Sakuma Genba-no-jō at Oyama Castle, Tokuyama Gohei at Miyukizuka, and Haigō Gozaemon at Daishō-ji. He ordered Maeda Matazaemon Toshiie to govern Noto Province and sent him to Noto accompanied by two men: Sugaya Kuemon Nagataka and Fukutomi Heizaemon Yukikiyo. From then on, Maeda Toshiie resided at Iiyama Castle in Noto, Sugaya resided at Nanao Castle, and Fukutomi resided at Tomiki to govern the province
