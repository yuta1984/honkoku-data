Postscript to the New Annotated Edition of Mathematical Enlightenment
　　The three volumes of Mathematical Enlightenment compiled by Zhu Shijie of the Pine Court. In the past, the senior fellow disciple Kuda Gentetsu carved it into woodblocks and spread it to infinity. Numbers are one of the six arts, yet few reach mastery in it.
　　The ancients said that numbers are the foundation of principles. How true these words are! Below the Supreme Ultimate, we establish the heavenly unity, divide it into positive and negative, using red and black counting rods of two types. When like signs are multiplied, the result is positive; when unlike signs are multiplied, the result is negative. Sometimes we subtract like and add unlike, sometimes subtract unlike and add like, canceling each other out, and thus all principles naturally exist within in perfect harmony.
