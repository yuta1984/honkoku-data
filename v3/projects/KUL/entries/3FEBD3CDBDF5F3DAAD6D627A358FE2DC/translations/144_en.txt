There were twenty children. ○ During my stay on the island for twenty days, thinking of the beginning, I was greatly moved and shed tears. I also left behind and gave them all kinds of implements. Generally these were weapons, gunpowder, lead bullets, as well as clothing and various daily necessities. I also left one craftsman, one blacksmith, and two men skilled in various trades. These were all people from my home country. I also made the laws and criminal codes strict, appointed a provisional king, divided them into military units, and had them manage various implements. ○ From here I sailed to "Brazil" and bought a ship called a "bark," loaded some men and women aboard and sent them to this island, pairing them according to each one's desires. I also sent along daily necessities as well as cattle, sheep, chickens, and dogs. ○ The island's climate is mild and very easy to govern. ○ Later, three or four hundred villains came to attack. However, the islanders had guns while the enemy had none, and they were greatly defeated. The islanders killed many enemies. When they were about to fight again,

A fierce gust of wind blew from the sea and capsized all the enemy boats, leaving none. Taking advantage of this opportunity, they achieved great success and killed many bandits. The defeated bandits fled into the mountains and forests to hide, trying to cut down trees and build boats, but lacking food, they eventually starved to death. Since then, fearing that wind, they dare not invade the island. The islanders prosper more and more, each diligently working, never seeing weapons of war. It is truly a kind of isolated realm separated from the human world, which could be called a domain where noble people avoid the vulgar world. ○ I developed this island, sailed to various countries, and finally returned to my home country. Reflecting carefully on past events, my life has truly been a collection of strange and astonishing experiences. When I reached those places where human footsteps had never trod, I encountered a hundred worries and ten thousand hardships, to the point where my very life nearly ended. Having experienced such dangers and refined my spirit for thirty-five years, yet the great fortune of my old age is also rare among people.
