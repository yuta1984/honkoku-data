They returned. After a while, five or six people in a small boat came and beckoned to us from afar, so it seemed they were telling us to board this boat. We thought that if we boarded, we didn't know what kind of hardships we might face again, but since we were destined to die anyway, we decided that we should board their boat, and we all got on together. Then they brought food in baskets and gave it to us. This food consisted of sweet potatoes and taro that had been boiled. What they continued to give us on the boat was glutinous rice mixed with potatoes

[Left page]
for us to eat. Then they rowed the boat out and traveled on the sea for about four days, arriving at what appeared to be the castle town of this country. We later heard that this was a place called Karaka within South India. In this Karaka, the towns stretched on for a long distance, appearing to be quite a vast settlement. Thus one of the boat people went ashore specifically to report our arrival, it seemed. Officials from various places, two people who appeared to be government officials, came and took all twenty of us through the castle gate to what appeared to be an entrance hall, where a person who seemed to be a leader asked us, "Are you Japanese?"
