【Right Page】
Everyone scooped up water with both hands and drank their fill, thinking that the taste of sweet dew or sacred springs must be like this. That day they lay down on the rocky shore, using rocks as pillows and dew as bedding. Compared to the suffering of the previous days, it felt better than jade pillows and fine robes, and the night's sleep they enjoyed was a pitiful sort of happiness.

The next morning, not awakened by roosters heralding the dawn, but only by the sound of waves on the rough shore, when everyone got up, somehow Kanzaemon had already breathed his last. Everyone gathered around, both shocked and saddened, wondering why their luck was so poor. Having lost someone who had sworn to share life and death with them, they had no coffin to prepare, not even a single stick of incense to offer, and lamented the sadness of having to bury him like a dog or cat. For a while they were overcome with weeping, but since they could not remain that way, they performed a humble funeral service, buried him nearby, and somewhat comforted his wandering soul with the nembutsu chanted by each person.

At this time, their bodies were extremely fatigued, and they could not thoroughly explore the conditions of the island, spending each day on the island...
