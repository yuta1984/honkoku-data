They nodded and, imitating the cry of birds, answered that when the roosters crowed at dawn, they would come here as guides, then pointed toward the land and departed.

Thus, when night gradually gave way to dawn, the three men came as promised. They were again given food and drink, and while fourteen men were left on the ship, the other forty-four were all lured by them up the mountain. After climbing what seemed to be about ten chō, they heard the sounds of people stirring in the shadows of trees here and behind rocks there, creating an inexplicably frightening atmosphere. Their steps naturally began to falter when suddenly, numerous bandits armed with bows and arrows rushed together from all directions, surrounded them, and shot at them relentlessly without pause. "Ah, how mortifying! Though we have been deceived by these scoundrels, we are Japanese men - how can we show our backs in retreat? We shall display our daily-practiced skills and show them what we're made of!" Though their hearts were brave, their swords, short swords, and all personal belongings had been thrown into the sea as offerings when they encountered the severe storm earlier, so now they had not even an inch of steel on their persons
