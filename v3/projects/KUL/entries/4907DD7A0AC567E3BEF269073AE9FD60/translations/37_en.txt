First, regarding what I saw and heard after landing in Canton and Macao within China, the details are as follows:

First, the climate in Canton felt considerably warmer than Japan.

First, Macao is called "Yakau" in foreign languages, while the Chinese call it "Oomon."

First, Macao appears to be a port for foreign ships, and I saw many foreigners there. At this place I saw Indians, Annamese, Malays, Americans, English, and others.

First, the Indians leave a little hair at the back of their heads, or leave hair on one side,

while shaving off all the rest. They wear something like an eboshi cap and grow their mustaches long. Their clothing consists of loose garments with tube sleeves that hang down from the waist, and below they wear something like hakama trousers.

First, the Annamese are similar to the Chinese in all respects, but they dye their teeth black.

First, the Malays have full hair on their heads, cut to about five sun in length, with cloth wrapped around like a hand towel. They have no mustaches but wear clothing that reaches to their shins and wear trousers.

First, the Africans are extremely black all over their bodies. Their clothing and everything else is the same as Americans, with full hair on their heads that is black and universally curly.
