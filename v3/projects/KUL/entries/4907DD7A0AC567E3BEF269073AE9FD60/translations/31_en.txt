There are also varieties resembling coconuts, betel nuts, pears, pomegranates, bitter oranges, and citrus fruits, and there are many unfamiliar fruits as well. I do not see varieties like bayberries, persimmons, mandarin oranges, or pears.

First, regarding grains, there is an abundance of corn and millet, and there are also varieties resembling wheat and barley with somewhat larger grains. There are also beans resembling salted beans with grain size as shown in the diagram ○, which are grown in large quantities. Rice is provided here, but I have not seen where it is cultivated. I have heard that there are places within this country where it is grown abundantly. I do not see wheat or soybeans. No fertilizer whatsoever is applied to any of the crops.

First, sugar cane is the same as in Japan, planted abundantly to make sugar. I have heard that they also squeeze sweet grass and use that juice to make shochu (distilled liquor). However, I have not observed the sugar manufacturing process.

First, I hear that they make sake and shochu from grapes, and also make shochu from coconuts.

First, perhaps because it is a warm country, salt naturally crystallizes and hardens on the beach. They break off pieces to take home and use. The hardened pieces they break off are like crystal.
