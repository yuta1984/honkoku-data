"Tarō," they read Matsunosuke as "Sontsūzō," and Tatsuzō as "Shinzon." They all made reading errors like this, but it was resolved through the interpreter's arrangements. The interpreter said, "Since ancient times, there have been burial mounds of four Japanese people at this place, and this time too we will follow the precedent." They performed a splendid funeral service. Now, according to the country's law, they perform instrumental music for both funerals and weddings, and the manner of becoming a monk is no different. we visited a temple. It was a large temple with Kannon (Goddess of Mercy) and Jizō statues, where we lit candles, burned incense, and prayed. Time passed, and on the 26th day of the 12th month, the local official informed us of favorable winds, saying "The day after tomorrow, on the 28th, a ship bound for Japan will definitely set sail." When this was decided, Sentarō and Tatsuzō went together to Matsunosuke's grave to bid farewell. After that, today they held a magnificent feast and provided us with a hundred delicacies.
