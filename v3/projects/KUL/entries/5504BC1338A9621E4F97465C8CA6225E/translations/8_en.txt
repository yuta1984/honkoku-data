They do not use any fertilizer at all. When the corn is ready in autumn, they put about five to (approximately 90 liters) into sacks made of cowhide, making them into bales, attach two to each horse, and with about twenty horses go to sell them twice a year as far as Los Angeles. The farming tools such as Chinese plows used for oxen are the same as in Japan.

1. The mountains in this area are all covered with two types of trees called "Silvoera" and "Vicuya."

1. For sugar cane, they leave the root stocks as they are each year, make furrows in the fields without changing them, draw water, and it grows twice yearly. The sugar manufacturing method is generally the same as in Japan, but they boil down the extracted juice well and spread it over thick boards about one ken long and two shaku wide,

【Left page】
with round holes about three sun in diameter carved into them. When the boiled-down juice is poured in, it gradually hardens inside. Using oxen to extract sugar cane juice is the same as in Japan.

1. They grow sweet potatoes in abundance. Their shape is the same as in Japan.

1. They grow excellent winter onions, not using the leaf tips but eating the white roots.

1. The pepper plants are about nine sun around and three to four shaku tall, and are the same type of hot peppers.

1. For fruits, there are many varieties including pomegranates and those for making citrus. Pomegranates are not at all like those in Japan
