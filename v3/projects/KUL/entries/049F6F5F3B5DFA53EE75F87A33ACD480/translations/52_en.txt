"Right Volume"
A message came requesting that supervisors be sent in advance to Kokura. There was a directive stating that personnel should be dispatched upon the arrival of ships. [Marginal note: "Particularly regarding meals, when Chinese ship expulsion officials are sent out to meet, on this day, before Tango-no-kami's arrival at Kokura - Katō Naoemon, head of ashigaru foot soldiers, Iga Uemon, artillery officer Usuki Yaemon, cannon unit supervisor"] On October 7th, they went as far as Tobata in Wakamatsu to await the arrival, and on the 13th, news came of the arrival at Kokura. The next day, the 14th, these men went to Kokura. [Marginal note: "They lodged at the place of one called Wakamatsu Yaemon"] Then, the domain's ship expulsion officials went to the envoy's lodging, where official documents were reviewed. The business was completed around midnight on the 14th, after which they immediately returned to Wakamatsu, arriving back in Fukuoka on the 16th.

"Left Volume"
1. Regarding Chinese ships drifting to the waters off Chikuzen and Nagato, expulsion orders have been issued repeatedly, but they do not appear to return to port. Guns are fired at them, but it is heard that the current methods do not inflict much damage. As previously instructed by Ishikawa Tosa-no-kami at Nagasaki, from now on during expulsion operations, the amount of ammunition should be increased from what has been used until now. Somehow, even with four or five shots, the Chinese ships should be made to suffer damage.

1. If many ships can surround the Chinese vessels, damage should be inflicted on the ships in any way possible. However, if few ships are sent out, care should be taken that the Chinese ships do not recognize them as pursuit vessels.
