Regarding punishments, I have not seen or heard of the death penalty being carried out. Tattooing is done in three places on the face: the forehead and both cheeks. Flogging starts from about ten lashes, with the number gradually increasing according to the severity of the crime. Those who are beaten severely have their back skin torn and blood flowing. There are also punishments involving cutting off the nose.

Regarding the land, there are many places with good soil, and some sandy areas as well. There are no rice fields at all, only dry fields where they mainly cultivate buckwheat, barley, wheat, hemp, and other crops.

Regarding vegetation, there are many five-needle pines among the trees, as well as cherry trees that do not bloom, oak trees, and other miscellaneous trees, though I do not

remember them in detail. There is no bamboo at all. There are various grasses, but I did not see any particularly rare ones. Flowering plants are generally few, and even plum and cherry trees are kept in pots and placed beside heating sources to barely make them bloom.

Regarding mountains and rivers, there are no particularly high mountains, and it is mainly plains. Rivers are also relatively few compared to the vastness of the area.

Regarding birds and beasts, there are many cattle, horses, pigs, wild cattle, sheep, and they raise various types of chickens and domestic ducks. There are occasionally tigers as well. I did not see any cranes. Geese and such are only there during summer. Everything else is no different from Japan.
