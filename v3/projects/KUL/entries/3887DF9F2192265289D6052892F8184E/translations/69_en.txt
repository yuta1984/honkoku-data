　We report below what the castaways witnessed and heard:
1. In Honolulu within Oahu, there is a leader whose name is King Kauikeaouli. The residence of this chief has mountains to the north and faces the sea to the south, spanning approximately 300 chō square. His retainers live within the compound, with the main gate facing south. There are three guard posts to the east and north, each manned by two ashigaru-like guards carrying swords and guns who stand guard and maintain positions, rotating every hour.
1. When this chief goes out and walks one or two ri, he rides a horse. His attire is American-style clothing made of wool, decorated with gold tassels, and he is accompanied by many attendants. Once or twice a year throughout the country
