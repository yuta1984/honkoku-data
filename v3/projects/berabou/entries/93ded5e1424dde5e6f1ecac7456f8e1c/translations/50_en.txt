　　　　㊆ About chishiki (critical periods) and their calculation
One, two, nine, ten correspond to Ne, Uma, U, Tori; three, four, five correspond to Ushi, Hitsuji, Tatsu, Inu;
Six, seven, eight correspond to Tora, Saru, Mi, I
　For the above, the calculation differs depending on whether it falls in the first, middle, or last ten days of the month. Children born during the critical period have short lives. If not born during the hour of Ne at night, it should be understood as the hour of Uma the following day.

　　　　㊇ Items to prepare before childbirth
One: Oshioke (pressing tub) - This is what is commonly called the placenta tub. Formally, twelve are needed, but one is sufficient. Diameter: seven sun, nine sun, or six sun; Height: six sun, seven sun, or sometimes eight or nine sun. It has a lid decorated with cranes, turtles, pine, and bamboo.
　When using twelve pressing tubs, twelve small knives are also needed. If there is one pressing tub, then one small knife. The above pressing tub, small knife, hemp fiber, and three earthenware dishes should be prepared in advance. Press against the earthenware dish, cut the umbilical cord with the small knife, then wrap the end in paper and tie it with hemp fiber.
　In the pressing tub, sometimes twelve mon coins and a little rice are placed. Also, sometimes one hemp strand, five straw strands, and one noshi (ceremonial gift decoration) are included. Calculate the directions to find the favorable direction
