For bride-taking ceremonies, the server should take two steps when adding sake. The adding requires six steps. For groom-entering ceremonies, it's one step, and adding is seven steps - this should be understood. The long handle should be wrapped in twelve places. In leap months, it's wrapped in thirteen places. Rest the left hand, hold the spout of the long-handled sake vessel with the right hand positioned to your right, hold the long handle horizontally, and pour the sake forward. For the formal san-san-kudo ceremony, all servings should be added three times each.

In regular sake serving, "tying" (musubi) is greatly taboo. For example, when the cup goes to the right, the server should go to the left. When passing the cup through the "tying" space, one should face right. If at this time one faces left with the upper seat behind them, this is called "tying." One should not put the upper seat behind them all the way to the lowest seat. Generally, when going to the opposite seat, one should face toward the upper seat on both left and right sides, even to the lowest seat. During wedding ceremonies, all servings are "tying servings."
