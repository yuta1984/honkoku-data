About the maternity belt: Take eight shaku of raw silk, fold it into four layers, and pass it from the man's left sleeve to the woman's right hand. This is the custom.
After birth, attach a kanitori (protective charm) to this silk, dye it light yellow, and use it as baby clothes. It is also auspicious to receive a belt from someone who has given birth to many children. Regarding beginning to wear the belt, it should be done facing a favorable direction after the sankon celebration. The aforementioned belt should later be replaced with another belt. The auspicious days for the belt are:
Kinoe (Ne, Inu days)　Kinoto (Ushi, Hitsuji, Tatsu days)　Tsuchinoe (Uma, Ne, Inu days)　Tsuchinoto (Ushi days)
Kanoe (Saru, Inu days)　Kanoto (U, Tori days)　Hinoe (Uma, Inu days)
　　If you begin wearing the belt on the above days, there will be no difficult labor

On Tatsu, Inu, Ushi, Hitsuji days, face southeast
