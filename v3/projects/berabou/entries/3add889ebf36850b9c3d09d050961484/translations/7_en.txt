This should not be called a difference in kana. In detail, it seems easy to confuse.

One: What should be understood when taking up a topic is that when the topic is refined, one should compose in a vulgar manner. When the topic is vulgar, one should compose in a refined manner.

For example, if one composes crudely and vulgarly on a topic like "privy," it will sound like an ordinary thing. If one composes dirty words cleanly, they will sound unexpectedly elegant.
