Even great wealth becomes like a single branch of Guilin's cassia trees, and here the beauty of Shang Jun or Yang Guifei becomes equal to but a fragment of jade from Kunshan. This is a place of golden refuse heaps, a dumping ground for melancholy, where steam rises vigorously, and the rudeness of wearing headbands and sitting cross-legged goes unnoticed as befitting the local customs. The vulgar voices crying "Hurry, hurry!" truly disturb the elegant court music, causing even loyal retainers to lose their way and filial sons to become frivolous. Regardless of age or status, whether Gonsuke or Hachibei, people recklessly come and go. Some are spurned while others are favored; some strike while others are struck. Those who travel by land are rare, while those who go by boat are many. The departing boats show a spirited air, while the returning boats display a contemplative mood.
