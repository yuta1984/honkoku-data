Needless to say, farts [can imitate various sounds]: fulling blocks, bamboo flute music, Sanbaso dance, Mitsu-ji music, seven herbs song, Gion festival music, dogs barking, chicken farts. The sound of fireworks deceives [the audience into thinking they're at] Ryogoku, and the sound of water wheels resembles the Yodo River. Dojoji, Kiku Jido, "houtameri yasu," Ise folk songs, ichu-bushi, hanchu-bushi, bungo-bushi, Tosa bunya-bushi, hantayu-bushi, geki-bushi, kato-bushi, osatsuma-bushi. Even the long pieces of gidayu-bushi—Chushingura, Yaguchi no Watashi—are performed as requested. Each act matched to shamisen and joruri music, a peerless master has appeared.

Rather than just hearing about it without seeing, which would be pointless, let's go and have a look! So two or three companions set out together from Yokoyama-cho toward Ryogoku Bridge's main street. Without crossing the bridge but turning right, there was an ostentatious banner reading "Ancient Tales: The Flower-Blooming Man," with monks and laypeople, men and women pushing and shoving against each other. Looking first at the signboard from among the crowd, there was a strange man with his buttocks raised up, and behind him, painted in light ink with kumadori makeup, were numerous theatrical pieces like Dojoji and Sanbaso all drawn together in one place...
