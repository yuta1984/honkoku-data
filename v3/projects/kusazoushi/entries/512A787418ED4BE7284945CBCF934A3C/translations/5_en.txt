The first man to sleep was about forty years old, appearing quite honest and upright. Without anyone knowing, he took out a paper wallet from his bosom, and found one bu of silver he didn't remember putting there. "This is strange. There's no way this should be here. I can't understand it at all," he pondered various possibilities, then decided, "I might as well consider this money as non-existent and buy one lottery ticket. If it doesn't win, I'll just think of it as paying for my first visit to a courtesan." When he bought one ticket, it was the grand prize of 100 ryō. This was

【Left page】
such outrageous good fortune that he bought four more tickets with another ryō, thinking "surely not again." Though he didn't go on the day of the drawing, when he went to check about ten days later, all four tickets had won consecutively - one, two, three - incredibly, and he had made money. While he was enjoying himself wondering how this could happen, [someone said] "It's already noon (time to wake up)."
