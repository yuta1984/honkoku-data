What remains after death to experience suffering and pleasure is taught as follows. Therefore, there are various types of souls. First, the spirit of plants and trees is called anima vegetativa. Anima vegetativa refers to spiritual life that possesses only the functions of generation, nourishment, flowering, and leaf-falling. Also, the spirit of birds and beasts is called anima sensitiva. Anima sensitiva is spiritual life that possesses not only the function of generation but also functions such as perception and movement.

It is spiritual life equipped with these faculties. For example, it is the spiritual life by which birds and sparrows recognize a hawk as their enemy when they see it, and feel hunger, thirst, pain, itching, and so forth. These two types of anima emerge from material existence and are spiritual lives that function only in correspondence to material existence. Therefore, they are the fundamental life force that perishes together when returning to the four great elements of material existence. Now, the human mind is called anima rationalis.
