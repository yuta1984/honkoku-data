Because of these circumstances, Nobunaga did not quickly proceed to destroy the new sect, and the two padres continued to slander the various Buddhist sects and attack the Buddha-dharma, freely propagating the new sect throughout Nobunaga's reign. However, on the second day of the sixth month of Tenshō 10 (1582), Lord Nobunaga and his son met their tragic death in Kyoto at the hands of Akechi Mitsuhide. Hashiba Hideyoshi immediately came to the capital, defeated Mitsuhide, destroyed Shibata, and seized control of the realm. Thus the Nanban temple new sect flourished for eighteen years from Eiroku 11 (1568) to Tenshō 13 (1585). However, this year in Tenshō 13, Hideyoshi suddenly destroyed this sect.
