Record of the Rise and Fall of the Nanban Temple 《End》

This book is a summary of a work called "Kirishitan Kongen-ki" (Record of the Origins of Christianity). At the beginning of that book, it explains the geography of Christian countries, stating that the great king of a country called Kōshinbi, in order to seize Japan, sent one of his ministers called the Minister Koki to a country called Kirishitan located three thousand ri to the west. In that country, on a peak called Tenrin-mine, there was a tree called a sandalwood tree, and living there was an Urukan Furaten, a padre who had mastered the teachings of a secret sect called Hizō-shū and gained supernatural powers. He commanded this person to cross over to Japan and use the secret sect's teachings to subjugate the Japanese people and place them under control, then launch a unified attack to conquer the country. Thus they first sent Urukan across, along with the sequential machinations of the other ministers...
