A matter concerning an informant appearing who reports that the Southern Barbarians seek to subjugate Japan

Around the Genna era, a monk from Higo Province came up to Suruga and made a report to the elder councilors, saying: "In Higo Province, there is what they call a Christian temple, a temple established by Konishi Tsukami-no-kami, but they posed difficulties to me and expelled me. I too was called an irmão and gave sermons. Their intention is that, as a means for the Southern Barbarian king to subjugate Japan, they sent many padres to spread Buddhism [Christianity], directing the territories of five or ten countries within that nation toward expenses for Japan,

Every year, disguising themselves as merchant ships, they deliver silk and various goods. It is not uncommon for them to distribute and send these to temples in Kyoto and the countryside. Moreover, they create great ledgers recording how many hundreds or thousands of people from Japan were persuaded to join their sect each year, and send these to the Southern Barbarians. This is a stratagem to take the country without bow and arrow warfare. Before our very eyes, in New Spain, Luzon, and such places, they install governors from their country, changing every three years, having them transport various affairs. Spreading the law was a stratagem. Please quickly send someone to Higo to summon my opponent. Let there be a confrontation in your presence."
