Even if one contracts a dangerous illness and recovers again, the soul never truly heals and becomes an even worse person than before. This is nothing else but the result of accustoming oneself to bad habits accumulated over the years. For those whose conduct is never upright, who do not fear committing sins, and who constantly engage in meaningless matters, it is rare for their end to be with right mindfulness, as recorded in Eusebius's writings.

Therefore, Saint Jerome also reflected on how dangerous it is to try to achieve the path of penance at the end of life without having pursued it throughout one's lifetime. In the Book of Job, where it says "What hope can a person who steals from others for fame and profit have? Will God hear his cry at his end?" Saint Gregory commented on this passage, saying "Those who did not hear the Lord's voice in peaceful times, God will not hear their voice in times of trouble either." Proverbs 28 states that "The prayers of those who stop their ears to avoid hearing God's voice will never be accepted."

Therefore, pointing to those who do not constantly perform good deeds but seek to beg for divine mercy at their end, the venerable Job asks "Will God indeed hear his cry?" as seen in Job chapter 6. Also in Matthew 25, the foolish virgins come at the end saying "Lord, Lord, open to us," but He truly answers "I do not know you." You shall hear the words befitting you.
