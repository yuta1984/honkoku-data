If [God] creates and distinguishes between the souls of animals and humans, then why does He add evil hearts to human souls and drop them into hell? If this is so, then dropping humans into hell is entirely the work of Deus. When Shakyamuni Buddha appeared in this world, heretical sects flourished in India. Although they had vast wisdom, established various views, and their explanations of principles resembled the Buddha's Dharma, their own vision was unclear and consisted only of verbal explanations. The Samkhya school established twenty-five principles (tattvas) to judge all phenomena of the world.

They name the first one the "unmanifest principle" (avyakta). Before heaven and earth were separated, it was unaffected by fortune and misfortune, and beyond the reach of seeing, hearing, feeling, and knowing. Though difficult to name, they forcibly call it the "unmanifest principle." This is eternal and unchanged by birth, duration, change, and extinction. They name the twenty-fifth principle the "self principle" (purusha). This is called the "ordinary mind" and is what is referred to as the soul. They say this too is eternal.
