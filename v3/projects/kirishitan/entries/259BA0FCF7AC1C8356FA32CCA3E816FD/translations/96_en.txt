They were told to look and descend from below the stone wall, but if they were to be pushed down, there were numerous spears and naginata of several people below the stone wall, and being pushed down would be terrible. First and foremost, so as not to displease His Lordship regarding the excellent conduct, Toki Takumi sent word three times, so they said "What shall we do then?" and the aforementioned men consulted together and climbed up all at once. In Tachibana-dono's forces, the military law directive stated "Individual glory-seeking is unnecessary; strike them down on the spot," so they were ordered accordingly. At that time, since they hesitated to mention the aforementioned matter, they did not seek individual glory. When Jidayū and Takumi came together to where Lord Ishitani Jūzō and Lord Itakura Monnōkami were present and asked "Did you observe the situation just now?", they replied "We witnessed the excellent conduct," so when they said "Please do not forget this in the future," they received the word "We shall not forget this again." A man named Toyoda Kyūbei in the same unit said to both of them, "Yesterday too we quickly attacked the stone wall, and we have firmly witnessed today's performance, so we should certainly make a written record." They agreed with each other and wrote names with the tips of their short swords. Then Nomura Yoemon and Jidayū also made written records on their helmets.
