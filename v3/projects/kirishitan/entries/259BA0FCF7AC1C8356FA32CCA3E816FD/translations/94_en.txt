From a distance of five ken (about 9 meters), they set fire with a single fire arrow. Both lords expressed that this was a satisfying affair. At that house, Jinzaemon took the head of Shirō. Today at the hour of the boar (around 10 PM), they completely conquered up to the main citadel, and all the besieging forces raised victory cries. Lord Tadatoshi deployed his troops.

According to the "Shimabara Subjugation Record," on February 28th, Jinzasaemon, a retainer of Hosokawa Etchū-no-kami Tadatoshi, killed the rebel leader Amakusa Shirō. A wooden model diagram of the situation at Arima was submitted.

According to the Arima Records, on February 28th, Tadasato and others each took many heads and died in battle. On this day the castle finally fell, and Tadasato immediately went to Edo.

By imperial decree, he was renamed Nakatsukasa-shōyū, using his old name Tarui for this reason.

According to the Nabeshima family records, on February 28th, all forces together attacked and captured the inner citadel. At this time, a retainer named Nabeshima Daizen cut down the banner of Amakusa Shirō, the leader of the rebellion. These two men died and were wounded under Shinano-no-kami's command. Also, from the day of the siege works until the fall of the castle, a total of 989 men died or were wounded. Additionally, from the arrow exchange until the fall, 17 naval forces died in battle.

Total battle deaths and wounded: 3,654 people
