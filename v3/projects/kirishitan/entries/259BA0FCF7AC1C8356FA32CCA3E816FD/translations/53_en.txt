Since it was right before his eyes, [Lord Hideaki] witnessed it. Afterward, at the iron gate below the great drum tower, [Namikawa] was first to break through and helped Yoshida Kobei, who had come from behind and been wounded. When a messenger was sent from the main camp, he withdrew. Though he had broken military law and should have faced strict punishment, he was secretly given an increase of 100 koku, raising his stipend to 500 koku. There was an implicit understanding that he would receive further advancement in the future. He still possesses the certificate and the document of stipend increase from that time.

Also on the same day, Namikawa Saburōhyōgo was first to plant his banner on the side stone wall of the main citadel. His retainers Nakanishi Mataemon and Rokuzaemon, both men, entered together. A little later, the banners of Banner Commissioner Yamada Shōgen and Hyōgonokamidono were also planted. There was some dispute about precedence regarding this. However, the banner that Mataemon planted was Saburōbei's own banner, while Shōgen's banner was Hyōgonokamidono's banner, so there was already a difference. That day, Yanagimoto carried an unsheathed sword in his left hand, and when it got slightly nicked, he quickly claimed to be wounded and withdrew. Shibata had both thighs shot through by musket fire, but sitting on a narrow flying box, he continued giving orders to his assigned foot soldiers.
