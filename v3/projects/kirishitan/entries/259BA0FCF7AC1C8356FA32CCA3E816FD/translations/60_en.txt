According to the Araki Chronicle: Sama Muratsune went to military service in Shimabara, Hizen Province in the spring of Kan'ei 15 (1638), arriving there on February 27th. He immediately joined under Hosokawa Higo-no-kami and devoted his efforts. At this time, he first met with the official envoy Matsudaira Izu-no-kami Nobutsuna, who questioned him very thoroughly about the enemy, and afterward returned to Edo.

According to Tales of Ancient Military Houses: Noguchi Sasuke Kazunari, the spear captain of Kuroda Nagamasa, was a masterless samurai from Noguchi village in Kako district, Harima Province. In the spring of Tenshō 2 (1574), he became a retainer of Kuroda Yoshitaka. At that time he was seventeen years old and was first called Hikoshirō, then Tōkurō, and later changed his name to Sama. When Nagamasa entered Chikuzen Province, he was granted 2,500 koku and made chief of firearms. During the time of Nagamasa's eldest son Tadayuki, 500 koku was added, making it 3,000 koku.
