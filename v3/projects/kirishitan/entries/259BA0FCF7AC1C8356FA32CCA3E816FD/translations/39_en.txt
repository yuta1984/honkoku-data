Both Yanagida Kinuemon and Irie Tokuzaemon also rushed down to the base of the stone wall. Kada Kyūbei had sustained one spear wound, but since it was shallow, he attempted to climb the stone wall again. However, the enemy struck Kyūbei down with stones. At that time, his left and right hands (palms) were wounded in two places, so thinking that further fighting would be difficult, he informed his comrade Kajimura Sukejirou of this situation and withdrew into the main body of troops. Tsuchida Nuinodono, who was positioned behind, observed Kada Kyūbei's wounds and spoke words of admiration. Afterward, Tsuchida's retainer and Tomita Ichinosuke also said to Kyūbei, "Your performance in battle is incomparable."

In addition to this, dozens of advance troops desperately attached themselves to the base of the stone wall, attempting to break into the main citadel. However, the enemy fired arrows and guns at them and threw stones and timber, resulting in not a few casualties. Particularly as evening gradually approached, Matsudaira Izunokami sent military messengers to all fronts with orders that the main castle should be taken tomorrow. Therefore, the advance troops withdrew from the base of the stone wall and established defensive positions some fifty to sixty ken to the left and right. Arima Naiki was concerned about Inanami Nabenosuke's whereabouts, uncertain of his life or death, when Kōzuki Yozaburou spotted Inanami's identifying banner and said, "Nabenosuke-dono is there at the base of that stone wall."
