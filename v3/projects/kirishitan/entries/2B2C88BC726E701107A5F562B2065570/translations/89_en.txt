Kisaemon, Matsui Shuma, and Ido Chūsaburō accompanied them.

The marginal notes in red ink in the "Yaso Seibatsu-ki" (Record of Christian Suppression) state: Although Nobutsuna Ujitetsu and all the retainers of his house said the wind was fierce, it was a favorable wind, so they departed from Terai in Hizen at the hour of the Rabbit on the third day of the first month. The entire force traveled twenty-three ri by sea route and arrived at Arima-hara at the hour of the Monkey. Nobutsuna Ujitetsu crossed the sea directly for eighteen ri and arrived at Shimabara. There he heard that Itakura Shigemasa had been killed in action on New Year's Day.

The "Hosokawa-ke Shimabara Gunki" (Hosokawa Family Record of the Shimabara Campaign) states: On the third day of the first month, Lord Mitsutoshi moved his camp to Shimabara Hino. Reports of this matter were also sent to Lord Kawakatsu Tanba-no-kami and Lord Sasa Gonbee. Now, with Lord Mitsutoshi's fresh large army, the main attack position at Hara Castle was handed over by the imperial commissioners.

The "Yūkō-ki" (Record of Brave Deeds) states: Lord Hosokawa Higo-no-kami had been at Amakusa since last winter as reinforcement for the Karatsu forces, but since there were no incidents in that area, Lord Matsudaira Jinzaburō came to Arima, and Higo-no-kami deployed his forces to Kumamoto. However, upon hearing this news, he led an army of over twenty thousand troops, set sail from Misumi Bay, and arrived at Arima around the hour of the Monkey on the third day of the first month.
