The Kurume forces also abandoned the castle and showed themselves being pressed by the enemy, but Tadasato's hatamoto remained completely unmoved, so the various forces saw the banners and quickly gathered. Arima Naiki was indignant that the military forces had withdrawn and still remained below the walls, when a servant of Hoshino Soubei's retainers spotted Naiki and reported this to his master Soubei. Soubei rushed to Naiki's side and said, "Since the troops have already withdrawn, you should quickly retreat. I shall accompany you," and helped the seated Naiki to his feet. Then the enemy aimed matchlocks through the loopholes, and when Naiki's retainer Katou Denbei tried to grab the gun barrel, the enemy fired the matchlock, but Hoshino skillfully deflected it with an arrow, causing only a slight graze to Naiki. Since it was only a scratch, Naiki was not counted among the wounded. Thus Naiki followed Hoshino's advice and withdrew from the castle vicinity, saying "This is enough." Inanashi Nabenosuke had remained below the walls until then, but sent his retainer Ogawa Izaemon to Naiki's group, saying, "Since all the military forces have withdrawn, should we also retreat from here? I seek guidance." The reply was, "Not necessarily so, you should go quickly to the main camp." When Nabenosuke's retainer quietly went to the base of the main wall and conveyed Naiki's response, Nabenosuke said, "Then let us withdraw" and retreated from below the walls.
