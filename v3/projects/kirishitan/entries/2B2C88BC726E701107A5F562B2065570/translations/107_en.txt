After that, it appeared that they were adding more barriers (seirou) on top. By the 27th of that month, they were able to advance the siege works (shiyose) to within three or four ken (approximately 5-7 meters) of the enemy's palisade.

6th Day, Kanoe-Uma (Day of the Metal Horse)
According to a memorandum by Betto Mokuzaemon, on the 6th day of the first month, the following lords arrived at Arima villages: Matsudaira Izu-no-kami and his son, Toda Samon and his son, Hosokawa Etchu-no-kami and his son, Matsudaira Jinzaburo, Makino Denzo, followed by Kuroda Uemon-no-suke, Kuroda Kai-no-kami, Kuroda Ichisho, Terazawa Hyobu-no-kami, Arima Saemon-no-suke and his son, and Mizuno Hyuga-no-kami. These forces took their positions and rearranged the battle formations at the attack points.

The record also states regarding the deployment of men and horses: at the main attack point (oteguchi) was Hosokawa Etchu-no-kami, followed that night by Tachibana Hida-no-kami, then Matsukura Nagato-no-kami and Matsukura Ukon, next were Kuroda Uemon-no-suke, Kuroda Kai-no-kami, and Kuroda Ichisho. Along the continuation of the mountains were Ogasawara Ukon and Ogasawara Shinano-no-kami, followed by Arima Saemon-no-suke in this arrangement. Behind Hosokawa Etchu-no-kami's camp was positioned Matsudaira Izu-no-kami, to his west was Toda Samon, next to him was Mizuno Hyuga-no-kami, and nearby the camp positions for the inspectors (ometsuke) were established.
