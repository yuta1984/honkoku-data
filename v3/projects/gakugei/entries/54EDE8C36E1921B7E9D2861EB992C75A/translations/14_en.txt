The divine image of Tenjin is said to have been created
by Jigen Daishi of Mount Toei.

Heikasan Jodoji Temple　Also called Genshoin. Located in the same area, about half a cho south of Ryusenji Temple,
on the same side. It belongs to the Jodo sect under Enzan. The principal Buddha, Amida Nyorai, is a seated statue
over four feet tall, maker unknown. The founding priest was called Kyoyo Seiko Shonin. The restorer was called Genren
Shahon'yo Rikaku Itsuko. This temple was formerly located near Hirakawaguchi within the castle grounds
but was moved to its current location in Genki 3 (1572).

Ichigyosan Senshushi Temple　Located in the temple district of the same area. This temple also belongs to Enzan of the Jodo sect,
and its principal Buddha, Amida Nyorai, was carved by Eshin Sozu 《Note: over three feet tall》. The founding priest was called Jakurensha
Ton'yo Shonin. Originally it was in Aoyama as a grass hut
enshrining Seishi Bosatsu, but during the Eiroku period the founding priest established it as a proper temple, and later it moved to the vicinity of Akasaka Hikawa
Myojin, then during Kanei it was relocated within the same temple district, and finally
during Genroku it moved to its current location. 《Note: The miraculous image of Seishi Bosatsu enshrined since ancient times still exists at this temple. It is made of gilt bronze and is an extremely ancient Buddha statue》
