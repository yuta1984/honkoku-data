Ishi Kannon Hall (Stone Kannon Hall) - Same location
Located about seven chō south of Heiken-ji Temple, this is a Tendai sect temple called Mt. Enichi Myōchō-ji. The principal image is a stone statue of Nyoirin Kannon (Cintāmaṇicakra Avalokiteśvara). Hence it is called Stone Kannon. On the 17th day of every month, monks and laypeople hold all-night vigils. The Sacred Turtle Stone refers to a stone water basin located beside the fence on the left inside the gate. According to local tradition, this stone is a sacred stone that emerged from the sea bottom in the autumn of Kyōhō 18 (1733). When local fishermen tried to pull it up, two or three sacred turtles appeared and helped the fishermen lift it up together. Recognizing this as the divine power of Kannon, they finally placed it in front of the hall on the last day of the seventh month of that year. However, this stone is now cracked and damaged, making it difficult to hold water.

Nitta Daimyōjin Shrine - Located about seven chō south from Horinouchi Sannō Shrine across farmland, on the right side of the road to Watada Village. (Watada was formerly written as Wadata.) The regular festival is on July 2nd. Local people say that every year at dawn on New Year's Day and July 2nd, the neighing of war horses can definitely be heard. According to tradition, this is because the divine spirit of Yoshioki-kō (Prince Yoshioki), who is enshrined at Yaguchi Village in Kawakita, comes to visit this shrine.

Principal deity of this shrine - The spirit of Nitta Sachūjō Minamoto no Yoshisada Ason. According to tradition, Lord Yoshisada met with no success in the battle at Asuwa village in Echizen Province on the intercalary 7th month, 2nd day of Engen 2 (1337), and was finally killed by a masterless arrow.
