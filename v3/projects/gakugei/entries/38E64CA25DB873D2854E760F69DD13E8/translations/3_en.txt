Horiuchi Sannō Gongen Shrine, Kawasaki - About two chō to the left from the middle of Kami-Shinjuku highway
　Located to the south. According to tradition, it was established during the reign of Emperor Kinmei. It is the tutelary shrine of Kawasaki
　and has sacred lands. The shrine priest Suzuki family serves it 《Annotation: The ancestor of the Suzuki family was called Saburō Takashige, descended from the Suzuki family of Kumano》
　Main shrine　Enshrined deity: Takemikazuchi-no-mikoto　Co-enshrined deities 《Annotation: Futsunushi-no-mikoto, Kukurihime, Izanagi-no-mikoto, Izanami-no-mikoto》 Five deities are enshrined together
　On January 3rd, there is a yabusame (horseback archery) ritual. June 15th is the grand festival, and from the 13th to the 16th it becomes very lively. During this time, there is a divine procession to the temporary shrine at the seashore of Watada village 《Annotation: Called Uba-ga-mori, there is a purification pond, with a small Benzaiten shrine nearby. Also at the same location there is a horse track eight chō long, said to have been donated by the Nitta family》
　《Annotation: Local people say that all fish and insects living in this purification pond have only one eye》On the 15th, when the portable shrine makes its procession, seven sacred wands are carried in front.
　According to tradition, in Kōan 4 (1281), a person named Kawabata Sakuragawa Sakonnosuke received an imperial edict and came to this shrine as an imperial messenger, and these are the sacred wands from that time, making them the shrine's most precious
