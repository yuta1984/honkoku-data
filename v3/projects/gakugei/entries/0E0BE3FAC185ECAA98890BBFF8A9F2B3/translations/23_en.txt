[continuing from previous page: frogs] live in water. Those with blue color are called tree frogs (amagaeru). Those with red color are called red frogs. It is good to feed these to small children.

○Tadpoles (kaeru-ko) are the young of toads. They live in water. Kato and kato are the same thing.

○Leeches - large ones are called horse leeches. They readily suck human blood.

○Earwigs resemble centipedes in shape and are black in color. If they urinate on a person's private parts,

[Left page, upper section]
that person will develop sores.

○Centipedes have black-green backs, red legs, and yellow bellies. Those who are stung should apply black-boned chicken droppings or garlic.

○Toads have white bellies with black patterns. They have warts on their backs, and the oil from these is called toad venom (senso) and is used as medicine.

○Tree frogs have black spots on their backs. Their bodies are small and they jump well. They transform into quails.
