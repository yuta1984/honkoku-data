However, since ancient times, the principles behind earthquakes have not been clearly understood. Some theories claimed that clouds form at the bottom of the earth and generate thunder, causing tremors. Others proposed that there are many large cavities at the bottom of the earth - one filled with water and another filled with combustible materials like saltpeter and sulfur, which ignite due to underground fire, heating the water to create steam, and the rising vapors cause earthquakes.
