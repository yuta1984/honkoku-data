[Upper section]
○The Gawabi (bunting) is the Kawabichou (painted bunting).
Its form is about the size of a sparrow,
and its feather coloring is similar. Its cheeks are white
with black hair.
○The Kashidori (jay) is larger than the bulbul,
with wings having blue-edged black
star-like spots on its feathers. It comes from the end of autumn
through the winter months and calls.
○The Token (cuckoo) is larger than the bulbul,
yellow-black in color with a red mouth.
It calls in the night darkness
around the fourth and fifth months. It is the same as Tou and Shiki.
○The Hi (bulbul) is the Kuyoku.
It is also called Hahachou.
Both body and head are light
mouse-colored with black spots.
It eats the fruits of various trees.
Many come in autumn and winter.
○The Sekirei (wagtail) has a thin beak
and a long tail. When flying it calls,
when perched it moves its tail.
Those with white feathers and black backs are called Seguro,
and those that are blue and yellow are
called Kisekirei.
