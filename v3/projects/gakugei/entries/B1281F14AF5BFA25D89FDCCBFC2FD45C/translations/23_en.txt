Types of Stone
　Stone is the skeleton of mountains. According to natural philosophy, the essence of earth becomes stone. Stone is the nucleus of qi (vital energy). The way qi generates stone is like the sinews and claws of humans. However, the quality of stone is not uniform across all countries and mountains. This is due to variations in climate and geography, meaning it is generated through qi.

　Also, plants, trees, fish, and shellfish can all transform into stone. The pharmacological texts mention pine fossils, the Song records mention cypress fossils, and unofficial histories mention bamboo fossils. According to the Daisui-hen, in a clear stream north of Mount Fuyo in Yangquan, when plants and trees are submerged for several dozen steps, they all transform into stone. Also, in one country within Italy, there is a miraculous spring where whatever falls into it develops a stone skin within half a month, encasing the object.

　Also, in a western country of Europe, there is a lake where if wood is inserted with part in the ground, that part transforms into iron, and the part in water transforms into stone. Japan also has many such places; generally, the seashores and lakeshores of cold countries are all like this. One should know that all fossils of implements and objects depend on the nature of their location.
