Shitaya-oka (Shitaya Hill): Generally refers to the entire area around Ueno. 《According to ancient documents of the Odawara Hōjō family, Ōtani Jūrōzaemon held territory at Hishinobun in Shitaya during his rounds of Edo》

The remaining volumes of the Musashi Province Gazetteer state: "Shitaya-oka in Toshima District provides tribute of deer, foxes, rabbits, badgers, wagtails, pheasants, sparrows, etc., and also provides tribute of yams and pine resin" 《etc.》

Gojō Tenjin Shrine (Gojō Tenjin-gū): Located on the land of the Segawa family at the southeastern foot of Mount Tōei. The enshrined deity is Sukunahikona-no-mikoto, one seat 《the ancestral deity of Japanese medicine, called Gojō Tenjin》. Kitano Tenmangū is enshrined as a co-deity 《the statue of the Kan deity was consecrated by Great Master Jigen in the 18th year of Kan'ei and enshrined as a co-deity of this shrine》.

This shrine was originally within Mount Tōei, but when Kan'ei-ji Temple was established, it was moved to the residential land of the court poet Segawa Masaoku 《According to Kikuoka Senryō, the old site was around the main temple quarters of Ueno》. Every year on the night of Setsubun, the Okera Shinji (white atractylodes ritual) is performed.

The Hokoku Kikō states: "At the end of the first month, I wandered leisurely at Shinobugaoka on the border of Musashino. The enshrined shrine is called Gojō Tenjin. At that time, they were burning the withered miscanthus fields."
"Having made a promise, who would not wait for the spring's first grass, the dew beneath the sprouting plants of Shinobi Hill" - Gyōe

Hōōzan Jōrakuin (Mount Hōō, Jōraku Temple): Called Chōfukuju-ji, a Tendai sect temple. Located south of Gojō Tenjin, across the Shinobu River. The principal image, Amida Nyorai, was created by Great Master Gyōki and is the fifth of the Six Amida Buddhas. It becomes very lively during the equinoxes in February and August.
