Food Preparation Methods for the Elderly

○To make all sea fish soft down to the bones, add 2-3 hawthorn berries when boiling. Even the head and backbone become soft, and it neutralizes fish toxins, preventing food poisoning.

○To soften all river fish, boil them in rice washing water with an appropriate amount of soy sauce. Both head and bones become soft, and even small bones won't irritate the tongue.

○To soften abalone, remove the meat from the shell and pound it well with daikon radish. It becomes very tender.

○To soften octopus, add fragments of a Bizen pottery mortar when boiling. Alternatively, boil it in sencha tea with an appropriate amount of soy sauce. Either method makes it very tender and easy to cut with chopsticks.

○To cook kelp until tender, first put a whole piece unwashed into water and boil well. When cooked, wash thoroughly with water, dry lightly, then wrap crucian carp or similar fish and boil together.

○Method for Eternal Miso
One sho of soybeans, five go of white rice - steam these two ingredients well, cover with a box or mat and let rest. When koji mold appears, mix with fine sake and soy sauce totaling one sho four go, add five go of koji, mix well, prepare in an airtight container, and consume as it matures.

○Method for Pear Paste
Grate pears with a wasabi grater, squeeze out 100 monme of juice, add 20 monme of ginger juice and one kin of rock sugar. Put these three ingredients in an earthenware pot, cook over charcoal fire, and continuously stir the bottom with a ladle without rest. It becomes like candy.
