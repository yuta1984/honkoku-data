Pearls are stored in the spaces between them.
○The "tai" (belt) has characters shaped like
connected jade ornaments. There are stone belts,
lower belts, and hanging belts.
○The "hou" is a long undergarment.
Now, the clothing worn when attending
court is called "hou." Also, clothing stuffed
with old cotton is called "on-hou," and dyed
ones are called "su-hou."
○The "san" is a small undergarment. It is underwear.
Those without sleeve ends are called "san-i." There are also
"sha-san," "fu-san," and "hen-san,"
which are of the same type and serve as undergarments for court dress.
○"Hakama" are leg coverings. There are also "ōguchi"
hakama. They have pleats. Commonly called
"kamishimo," the upper part is called "shū" and the lower
part is called "hakama."
○"Kutsu" are leather shoes. Officials

【Left Page Upper Section】
wear these. The shoes of Sekikō and the shoes
Li Bai wore in the palace hall are of this type. In Japan,
kemari (kickball) shoes are of this type. The footwear of officials and
monks differs.
○"Kyo" (hem) is what hangs down at the back
of clothing. It is commonly called
a kite's tail.
○"Kun" is the lower garment that
women wear. It should be written as "kun." In China
too, since they dye it red, it is called
"akane-kun" (madder skirt).
○"Han-hi" is found in musicians' clothing or Noh costumes.
The sleeves are made short
so that half the elbow shows, hence
the name.
○"Nuko" are hakama worn pulled up.
They are hakama worn by female attendants
in the imperial palace. Those worn by women are red in color.
