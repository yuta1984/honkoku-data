【Right Page Upper Section】
Playing in reverse from below is called "hi," and playing forward from above is called "pa." It is also called kokin. Wang Zhaojun of the Han dynasty played it.

○Genkan has four strings and ten bridges, or five strings and thirteen bridges. It is the same as gekkin (moon lute).

○Sangen is the shamisen. It is called sangenshi. It is said to be a musical instrument that came from the Ryukyu Kingdom.

○Bachi (plectrums) include biwa bachi, sangen bachi, and katsuko bachi, all differing in shape and written characters. Rei is the same.

○Chū (bridges) are called chū on the biwa and kotoji on the koto. There are slight differences in shape.

○Shin are kinshin (tuning pegs) and tenju. Both biwa and shamisen have them.

【Left Page Upper Section】
○Fū are drumsticks for large drums. They can also be written as fū. Rei and hatsu are plectrums for biwa and also for shamisen.

○Keisō are fingerpicks for koto. They are called kakezume. They are the same as gikō and kakō.

○Dōbachi are called kin in Buddhist temples. Kin is Chinese pronunciation.

○Katsuko are musical instruments. Emperor Xuanzong of Tang played them well to encourage flowers to bloom.

○Yōko are drums held at the waist. Ordinary drums are called shiko.

○Dōra are what we now call dōra. They are musical instruments. One theory says they are gongs with navels.
