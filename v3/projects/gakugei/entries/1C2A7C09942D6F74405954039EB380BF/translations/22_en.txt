○Kamen (masks) are what we now call dance masks. They are also called taimen or kimen. They are worn in Noh theater and gagaku court music.

○Unban are bell plates. They are struck to gather the assembly at meal times.

○Sanō are flutes called taiheishō. Sano and sano are the same.

○Rappa and dōkaku are both Chinese flutes. In Chinese, they are also called charumera.

○Fūtaku are hōtaku. They are also called entaku and hang from the eaves of temples.

○Go (the board game) was first created by Emperor Yao and taught to his son Danzhu. The black and white stones represent day and night,

【Left Page Upper Section】
and the 360 points represent the number of days in a year. Go stones are called goshi, and the go containers are called goren.

○Hei are go boards. They are also called kikyoku. The intersections on the go board are called ro, the go stones are called shi, and the containers are called ren.

○Rokusai is sugoroku. The black and white stones represent day and night, and the twelve points represent the twelve months. The board is called kyoku.

○Sai represent the sun and moon, and the four corners represent the four directions. Tōshi are cylinders, the same as tōshi.

○Shōgi was created by Duke of Zhou and taught to King Cheng. There are large, medium, and small shōgi, and there is also something called makada shōgi.
