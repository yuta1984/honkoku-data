[I] wish to open [these foundations of learning]. If one does not diligently study, one cannot understand the way of benevolence, righteousness, propriety, wisdom, and trustworthiness. If one does not understand the way, one will spend merely fifty years of life in vain as a mediocre and foolish person. This is truly lamentable.
Therefore, in response to the request of the bookseller, I speak thus.
　　In the spring of the year of the Ox　　　Tōtei Kazenshiki
