【Right Page】
[Those with] swords wear happi coats (flower-colored silk) with rinbō (wheel treasure) family crests, and plain momohiki (leg coverings)

Sacred bows: Fifty bows
Twenty-five men each in two rows, guards the same. Black-lacquered quivers attached, bearers' happi coats same as above

Sacred spears: Fifty spears  
Twenty-five men each in two rows, guards the same. Plain sacred spears with red-lacquered sheaths, bearers wear flower-colored happi coats with white komochi stripes

Armored warriors: One hundred men
Fifty men each in two rows, others the same. Red-threaded armor with large sleeves, leg guards, helmets all golden. Wearing swords, with navy-blue and white horizontal-striped tachitsuke

Child attendants: Twelve children, six each in two rows, two guards the same
Wearing flower garlands with the twelve zodiac animals on their heads, red robes of fine silk with gold patterns. Wearing ōkuchi with gold-printed crests on white ground

【Left Page】
Subsidiary shrine deities: Masked figures
Fifty people, twenty-five each in two rows, guards the same. Bright red pointed hoods, sleeveless garments of the same material, wearing tachitsuke with white scale patterns on navy ground underneath, each wearing various strange masks and carrying weapons. About six feet long, black-lacquered, with gilt metal fittings above and below

Sacred fans: Four fans, carried by four shrine attendants in two rows
These are large military fans. About eight feet long with mother-of-pearl lacquered handles and gilt metal fittings. The fan surface is covered with red figured gauze with gold imperial crests on both sides

Sacred sword-bearing shrine family: One person on horseback in fourth-rank court dress, served by the first-ranking shrine family member
The sacred sword is placed in a bag of red Yamato brocade and carried on the back with crimson cords. One person in plain robes and four in white robes accompany

Sacred banner-bearing shrine family: One person on horseback in fourth-rank court dress, served by the second-ranking shrine family member
The sacred banner is also placed in a bag of red brocade and carried on the back with crimson cords. One person in plain robes, white-robed [attendants...]
