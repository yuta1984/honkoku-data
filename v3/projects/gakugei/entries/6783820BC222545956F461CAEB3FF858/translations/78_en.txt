Bell Tower: Located to the right of the stone torii gate. Four-pillared structure. The bell was cast in Shōhō 4 (1647).
Niō Gate Tower: Copper-roofed, painted red with polychrome decorations and carvings. Approximately two by three ken in size. The front houses Sahō and Uhitsu [guardian deities], while the back houses the two heavenly kings of wind and thunder. Under the eaves of the upper story hangs a plaque reading "Nyotai Chūgū" written by Kōbō Daishi's own hand. A reproduction is shown next.
