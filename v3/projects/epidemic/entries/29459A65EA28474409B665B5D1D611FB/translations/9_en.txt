With no break in the long rains, the tedious days continue. Without any schemes for economizing, though unable to overcome children and illness, he mutters to himself that this sickness is fortunately convenient as an excuse for creditors, sitting by the door which goes knock-knock-knock - a girl rushing in with nowhere else to turn. Here even the water rail is an old thing, constantly pushing back and forth, while lying down

his responses while still lying down are those of his usual lazy self, so others naturally forgive him. Looking at the face of the entering guest, it's mottled like a fawn's spots and covered in pot soot. His complexion entirely jet black - this is a resident of Shinano Province who boasts of being led by an ox to Zenkoji Temple, said to be a descendant of Taishokukan, the neighbor called Jinta who is a rice-cooking fellow.
