　　　The first arrival of smallpox in Japan
Our country was originally a divine land with no strange diseases and was peaceful, but in the 8th year of Tenpyō during Emperor Shōmu's reign, smallpox first arrived and became epidemic. At this time, from summer to winter, many people died. Since this was unprecedented, it appears that both medical treatments and methods of home nursing care were unfamiliar. This disease moves the vital energy of the five organs and is caused by susceptibility to seasonal miasma. When this disease becomes epidemic, there is no one who does not fear it. The beginning of the illness resembles typhoid fever, and there are various types. If the symptoms are mild, one takes medicine
