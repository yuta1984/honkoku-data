　　Concerning the judgment of good and bad during the three days of pus penetration (mountain raising)
At this time it is commonly called "mountain raising." If it proceeds smoothly as before, it will be fine. If the care of the upper half is good, the mountain will naturally rise sufficiently. The resemblance of smallpox to beans should be recognized during the mountain-raising time. Seven days after the smallpox emerges, when its form becomes round, red, and full with light and moisture, appearing like green water, gradually becoming waxy in color and appearing to rise up, this is called pus penetration.
