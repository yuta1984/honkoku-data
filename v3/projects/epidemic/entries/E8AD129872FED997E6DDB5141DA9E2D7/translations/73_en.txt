- Chinese cabbage (hatakena)　This plant has many varieties and belongs to the turnip green family. It is non-toxic.
　　　　　It moves qi downward, harmonizes the abdomen, and promotes urination. It should be well-prepared
　　　　　as a vegetable for eating. Also, it may be mixed with salted miso and cooked well for eating,
　　　　　which is considered good. However, from the sixth to tenth day, it should not be given to wet nurses or children.
　　　　　After the tenth day, there is no harm.
- Mizuna (Japanese mustard greens)　This plant has many varieties. The seeds vary depending on the region. It is non-
　　　　　toxic. However, one theory states it has slight toxicity. It should not be given during smallpox. After smallpox,
　　　　　after fifteen days, it may be given to both children and wet nurses. Also,
　　　　　it should be prohibited after the spring equinox. There is a theory that it has great toxicity, disturbing qi and
　　　　　causing leg diseases. Many people in Kyoto suffer from beriberi.

- Garland chrysanthemum (kikuna)　Also called spring chrysanthemum or wolf fang chrysanthemum. It is non-toxic. This plant
　　　　　should not be given carelessly from the sixth to tenth day during smallpox.
　　　　　Simply boil it well, rinse with water, and mix with miso and soy sauce
　　　　　before giving. After the tenth day, it may be given to both children and wet nurses
　　　　　without harm.
