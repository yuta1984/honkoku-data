- Corbicula clam (shijimi)　　　Non-toxic. It removes dampness from the stomach, eliminates heat toxins, promotes urination, treats jaundice, and clarifies the eyes. However, it should not be given during smallpox. After thirty days following smallpox, it may be given. It is also said that after smallpox has healed, when small sores develop within the skin resembling lacquer rash that cause unbearable itching, if one soaks about one cup of corbicula clams with their shells in water for four to five days and washes the itchy areas with that water, it will heal immediately.

- Ocean sunfish (ukiki turtle)　《Commonly called ukiki turtle》This fish is found in the seas of Mito. Its nature is warm, and it effectively warms the spleen and stomach while removing cold qi from the abdomen. Therefore, during the seventh, eighth, and ninth days of smallpox, when there is diarrhea,

- Croaker (ishimochi)　Non-toxic. It opens the stomach, benefits qi, and treats food stagnation. However, it should be given during the first seven to eight days from initial onset. Its effects are similar to sea bream, and since it has the ability to cause eruptions, it should be avoided for one hundred days after smallpox. If used mistakenly, it will trigger residual toxins.
