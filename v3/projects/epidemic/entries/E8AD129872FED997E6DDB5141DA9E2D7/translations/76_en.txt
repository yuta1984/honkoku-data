- Japanese ginger (myōga)　Myōga shoots or flowers are called myōga children. Non-toxic. It removes evil qi and
　　　　　inauspicious influences. During and after smallpox, it should be steamed or roasted, or
　　　　　boiled, or blanched in hot water and mixed with salted miso before giving. It should not be
　　　　　used raw.
- Five-addition sprouts (ukogi buds)　Non-toxic. Young buds should be well-blanched in hot water, rinsed in cold water, and
　　　　　mixed with salted miso before giving. It removes wind-dampness from the skin. It may be used during smallpox
　　　　　without harm.
- White plum (pickled plum)　Non-toxic. When used regularly, it resolves food poisoning, calms the heart qi, and
　　　　　stops thirst. If used for patients or during smallpox, first the pickled plum should be
　　　　　boiled well in cold water once or twice, the liquid discarded, and then

　　　　　again boiled well in fresh water, the liquid discarded, then soy sauce
　　　　　added and simmered together with sugar added before giving. During smallpox,
　　　　　from beginning to end, there is no harm.
- Bitter lettuce (chisha)　Slightly toxic. Should not be used raw. It should be well-blanched and mixed with salted miso
　　　　　before eating. It should not be given during smallpox. After fifteen days following smallpox,
　　　　　it may be given to both children and wet nurses without harm. This plant has many varieties.
　　　　　Red and purple varieties are toxic and should not be used for patients or children.
- Turnip greens (kabuna)　Both roots and leaves may be eaten. Non-toxic. When used regularly, it harmonizes the interior,
　　　　　circulates qi, nourishes and strengthens people, and makes the body light. Most importantly,
　　　　　it should be forbidden during smallpox. After fifteen days following smallpox, both children and wet nurses [may use it].
