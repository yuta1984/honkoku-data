- Burdock root (gobō)　Non-toxic. It should be well-boiled, rinsed in water, mixed with salted miso,
　　　　　and also cooked thoroughly to be regularly used as a vegetable.
　　　　　This plant is a fine product among vegetables. Its nature is extremely mild.
　　　　　Therefore, it may be given during and after smallpox without harm. Also,
　　　　　if burdock seeds are roasted and used, their potency is extremely strong.
　　　　　The root and seeds have greatly different natures. Those who use them in the future
　　　　　should make this distinction.
- Lotus root (hasune)　Non-toxic. It stops thirst, stops diarrhea, aids digestion,

　　　　　and neutralizes alcohol toxicity. It treats various types of bleeding. It should be well-cooked and
　　　　　mixed with salted miso for eating. One theory states it causes various sores,
　　　　　but this is also considered incorrect. It particularly has the function of stopping bleeding. During smallpox,
　　　　　after ten days, it may be used by both children and wet nurses. Also, recently there is
　　　　　a new variety of lotus root starch that has been imported. It may be given to children and patients without harm.
- Broad beans (fava beans/green peas)　Non-toxic. When the pods are young, they should be cooked and eaten with the pods. Also, these
　　　　　beans have thick skins and should be cooked thoroughly with the skins removed before eating. Especially
　　　　　children with weak spleen and stomach should not be given large amounts. After twelve days of smallpox,
　　　　　they may be given to both wet nurses and children. Also, roasted beans
　　　　　should be avoided for one hundred days.
