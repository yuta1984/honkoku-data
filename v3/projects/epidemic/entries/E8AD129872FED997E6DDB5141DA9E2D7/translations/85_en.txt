- Pomfret (manakatsuo)　Non-toxic. It regulates stomach qi, moves the qi of the five organs downward, removes stomach wind, and digests grains. Avoid using it during smallpox. After twelve days following smallpox, it causes no harm. It should be grilled or mixed with salted miso and thoroughly cooked before giving. It should not be used raw.

- Flounder (hirame)　Non-toxic. It should not be used during smallpox. It should be given after twenty-one days following smallpox. No harm. It should be thoroughly cooked or steamed before use.

- Righteye flounder (karei)　Has minor toxicity. Should not be given during smallpox. After smallpox, for those without residual toxicity, it may be used by both children and wet nurses after twenty-one days.

- Cod (tara)　　　Non-toxic. It is said to supplement and nourish qi well, but fresh fish has soft flesh and causes no harm when given to patients and children. Salted cod and dried cod

　　　　　have very hard flesh that is difficult to digest. Therefore, they should not be given to those who are weak. After fifteen days following smallpox, they may be given to wet nurses.

- Ark shell (akagai)　Non-toxic. It benefits the five organs, strengthens the spleen and stomach, warms the interior, and has the ability to digest food well. However, it should not be used during smallpox. After twelve days following smallpox, it should be mixed with salted miso, thoroughly cooked, and then given.

- Hard clam (hamaguri)　Non-toxic. It moistens the five organs, stops thirst, and opens the stomach. However, it should not be used during smallpox. After thirty days following smallpox, it may be given without harm. It should be mixed with salted miso and steamed before giving. Grilling should be most avoided.
