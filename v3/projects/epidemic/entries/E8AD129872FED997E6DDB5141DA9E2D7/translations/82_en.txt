- Dried bonito flakes　　Non-toxic. It harmonizes all flavors and makes them sweet. For people weakened by
　　　　　chronic illness, when regularly mixed with salted miso and added to various dishes, the
　　　　　flavor becomes sweet and delicious, effectively promoting appetite. Therefore, it should be regularly
　　　　　used to regulate the spleen and stomach, strengthen and nourish the muscles, and may be used during and after smallpox.
　　　　　However, fresh bonito has great toxicity and should not be used. Also, shaved bonito flakes
　　　　　mixed with soy sauce should not be given to patients and children, as there is a theory that it
　　　　　generates intestinal worms.
- Sea cucumber (dried small fish/sea cucumber)　　Non-toxic. It supplements vitality, benefits the five organs, and treats all types of chronic fatigue and
　　　　　deficiency damage. It should be well boiled and then thoroughly cooked again in salted miso
　　　　　before giving. Particularly for smallpox, from initial outbreak until the tenth day, sake and salt may be

　　　　　added. After the tenth day, alcohol should be avoided. It should be thoroughly cooked in thin soy sauce
　　　　　with a little white sugar added.
　　　　　This ingredient may be given throughout the entire course of smallpox without problem.
- Halfbeak fish (sayori)　　Non-toxic. It removes the toxins of epidemic diseases. It may be given throughout smallpox without problem.
　　　　　It may be soaked in grilled soy sauce, salt-grilled, or steamed.
　　　　　It should not be given raw.
- Eel　　Non-toxic. It treats all types of sores. It kills all kinds of parasites. It treats chronic fatigue and damage.
　　　　　However, it loosens the bowels, so it is forbidden during smallpox. After smallpox,
　　　　　fifteen days later, for those with hard stools, an eel six to seven inches long should be grilled and
　　　　　given. Large ones are toxic and should not be given to patients and children.
