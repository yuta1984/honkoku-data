Children who have not yet had smallpox should be vaccinated promptly, and even those who have already been vaccinated should still be vaccinated repeatedly.

○While there is a smallpox patient, children from that household shall not be permitted to enter school.

○Not only the clothing of smallpox patients, but also all daily utensils kept in their rooms should be known to be contaminated with the disease poison. Therefore, until the following purification methods are carried out, these items must not be taken elsewhere or used by others.

○Clothing and bedding worn during illness should be immediately removed and [treated with] carbolic

acid water [made by adding 32 monme of crude carbolic acid to 3 shō 2 gō of water], or potassium permanganate, or water mixed with medicinal water made by dissolving 4 monme of chlorinated lime in 3 shō 2 gō of water, or strong alum solution [an excess amount of crude alum should be thrown into water, stirred well, and the supernatant liquid should be taken for use]. After soaking in these solutions, pour hot water over them, wash them, and hang them on the roof or in an empty room to dry.

However, it is better to burn and discard crude items that are contaminated. [Hand towels, paper scraps, etc. must be burned and discarded.]
