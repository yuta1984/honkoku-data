○Whooping cough attacks occur 10 to 12-13 times in a day and night, and in severe cases, attacks occur every 30 minutes. As the sticky phlegm becomes increasingly viscous, the cough

continues even longer, and unless the phlegm is expectorated, the cough will never stop. Children with some understanding can spit out phlegm, but babies without sufficient intelligence tend to swallow the sticky phlegm that appears in the back of their mouth immediately, and it often retreats back to the larynx, causing the cough to persist and creating great fatigue. Therefore, caregivers should hold the child's body during attacks, bend them slightly forward, hold the child's head against their own (the caregiver's) chest, wrap a piece of cloth around their index finger, insert it into the (patient child's) mouth, and carefully wipe away the expectorated phlegm.
