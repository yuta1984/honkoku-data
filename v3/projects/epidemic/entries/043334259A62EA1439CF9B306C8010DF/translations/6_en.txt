　　Chapter Five: On Bathing
Bathing, whether hot water bathing or cold water bathing, cleanses the skin, softens muscles and bones, opens pores, removes dirt, promotes blood circulation, and maintains healthy constitution - it is the best method for these purposes. Therefore, bathing should not be neglected in any of the four seasons. Particularly in summer, it should especially not be neglected. During hot weather, perspiration naturally increases, so one must bathe daily to keep the body clean.

Hot water baths should not be excessively hot. When bathing in water that is too hot,

[Left page]
it is rather harmful.
After bathing, one should cleanse the body with rinse water. This is especially important for communal baths. Also, immediately after bathing, one must not expose oneself to wind or fan oneself with a fan.

Post-bath cooling should be done with caution and not taken to excess.
Immediately bathing in cold water after a hot bath causes great harm to health.
This is because when one bathes in cold water just as the pores have opened from hot bathing and perspiration is rising, it immediately stops that perspiration.
