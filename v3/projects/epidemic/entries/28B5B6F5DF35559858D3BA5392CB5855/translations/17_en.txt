　　　Guidelines for Nursing Care

One: In smallpox, the initial treatment is most crucial. Common physicians do not know how to distinguish between the fever of early smallpox and the fever of typhoid or common cold, and often prescribe medicine incorrectly. Even when eruptions are already visible, they still do not recognize it as smallpox, and while they deliberate back and forth, it becomes too late to treat, resulting in symptoms that cannot be remedied later. When in doubt, one should quickly consult a skilled physician.
