○Japanese iris (kakitsubata) grows in water. The flowers are large and beautifully colored like bellflower blossoms. They bloom in early summer.

○Iris (ayame) has flowers similar to Japanese iris but smaller, with thinner leaves as well. The plant called shōbu is a different species that has no flowers. There is also a type called flowering sweet flag.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○Joseph's coat (momijigusa) has leaves that turn red in June and July. Those with yellow-green mixed in are called ten-pattern brocade. Those that turn red when wild geese arrive are called wild goose red. Commonly called leaf cockscomb.

○Bellflower (kikyō) has purple and white flowers. There are single and double varieties. They open in June. Also called stem grass (kōsō).
