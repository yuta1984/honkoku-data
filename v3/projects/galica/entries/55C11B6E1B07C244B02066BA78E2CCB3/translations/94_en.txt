【Right Page, Upper Section】
○Rose of Sharon (mukuge) resembles hibiscus flowers but is smaller. It comes in light red and white colors. There are both double and single-flowered varieties. It blooms in July. It is also called nikkyū.

○Hibiscus that grows in water is called water hibiscus, which is the lotus flower. The tree variety is called tree hibiscus. It is also called kibachisu. The flowers open in July and August.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○Clerodendrum (kusagi) blooms with purple flowers in autumn. There are black fruits within the flowers. The root is called jōzan. Around June, the leaves are picked and eaten, though some say they are poisonous.

○Privet (nezumochi) endures winter without withering. Therefore, it was named in comparison to women's chastity. It is also called rōju (wax tree).

○Japanese holly (mochinoki) remains green even in winter months. Therefore it is called tōsei (winter green). The leaves are somewhat round.
