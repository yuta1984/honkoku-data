○Peony (shakuyaku) has three branches and five leaves. The flowers resemble tree peonies but are slightly smaller. They bloom in early summer. There are red, white, and purple varieties. It is called "kashōshōri" (flower minister's parting). The roots are used medicinally.

○Primrose (sakurasō) has leaves like turnip greens but harder. The flowers are purple and white. They bloom in March.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○Hollyhock (aoi) is a general name. The leaves are large and the flowers are red or purple. They bloom in May. The fruit is finger-sized, dry, and flattened.

○Hollyhock (shokki) is Chinese hollyhock (kara-aoi). The flowers are double-petaled with deep red color, particularly beautiful. Same as kenki. Also called shūki.

○Mallow (kinki) is small hollyhock (ko-aoi). Also called zeni-aoi (coin hollyhock). Same as keiki.
