○Rice cakes (mochi) are rice cakes and noodle cakes.
Gao (kō) refers to flour cakes, which are dumplings.
Rice balls (handan) are botamochi (sweet rice balls covered with bean paste), millet cakes are millet rice cakes, and mugwort cakes are rice cakes made with mugwort.
○Sugar (tō) refers to candy/syrup. Yi (candy) is the same. Wet sugar refers to soft candy, and tin candy refers to hard candy.
Both nourish the elderly.
There is one type called rehmannia decoction.
At that time, it was used especially for children during summer months.
