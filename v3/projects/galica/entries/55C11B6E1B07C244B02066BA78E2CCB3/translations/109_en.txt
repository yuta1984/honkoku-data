【Right Page, Upper Section】
○Hibiscus (fuyō) has leaves like hollyhock, with red and white flowers. There are single and double varieties; the single ones resemble rose of Sharon but are larger. They are pure and beautiful. They bloom in July.

○Gentian (rindō) has flowers colored like bellflower blossoms, and leaves like bamboo grass. They bloom at the end of September. Commonly called rindō.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
○Sunset hibiscus (ōso) is also called yellow hibiscus (ōshokki) or side golden cup (sokkinsan). The leaves are pointed, narrow, and serrated. In autumn, pale yellow flowers bloom. Commonly called tororo-aoi.

○Barnyard grass (yū) resembles Japanese millet but has no grain. Also called dog's tail grass (kubisō). It grows among millet crops. Commonly called enokoro-gusa.

○Marigold (kinsenka) is also called noon flower (gojika). It blooms in autumn. It is a deep red color and beautiful. Also called midday flower (shigoka).
