There is skin under their feet, and their fur does not get wet even when touching water. They are called "ajika."

○Water buffalo are blue in color with large bellies, and their heads are
pointed, resembling wild boars in shape.
When eaten, they stop excessive thirst (diabetes),
nourish the spleen and stomach, supplement
weakness, and treat edema.

○Orangutans are beasts that live in the sea.
Their fur is yellow and they resemble monkeys.
Their ears are white, and their faces and feet are like humans.
They love alcohol. Their blood is taken for dyeing.

○Baboons are said to be monkeys that become baboons
after accumulating years. Their shape is like humans
but large. Their lips are long and they wear
inverted heel hair, run swiftly and eat people. When they
see people, they laugh loudly.

【Left page, upper section】
○Mice have four teeth but no fangs. They have four
claws in front and five claws behind. They treat
convulsions and epilepsy in small children.

○Kei are harvest mice. They are small
mice. When they bite people, it doesn't
hurt but becomes a sore.

○Moles (uguro-mochi) are transformed
from shrikes. They resemble mice but their heads are
like wild boars and they have no tails. Their fur color is
yellowish-black. They dig through the ground and
eat earthworms. They fear the light of sun and moon.

○Weasels are larger than mice with long bodies,
short legs and large tails. Their color is
yellow and red. They are good at catching mice.

○"Kaku" (horn) is read as "争う" (to fight), referring to beasts
fighting with their horns.
