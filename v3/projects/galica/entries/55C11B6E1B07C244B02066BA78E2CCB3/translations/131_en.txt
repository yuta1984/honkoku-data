【Right Page, Upper Section】
○Man (蔓, vine) means creeping vine. Woody vines are called fuji (wisteria), while herbaceous vines are called tsuru (vine).

○Hō (苞, bract) means flower bud. Bairai (蓓蕾, bud) is the same.

○Zui (蕋, stamen) refers to the stamens of flowers. Zui (蕊) and zui (蘃) are the same. It is also called kashin (flower center).

○Gaku (萼, calyx) means flower cluster. Katai (flower calyx) and kafu (flower base) are the same.

【Left Page, Upper Section】
　　Niō Kongō (Two Guardian Kings)

○The right one is called Uhitsu Kongō (Right Assistant Vajra). He rejoices in people's good deeds. He is also called Naraen Kongō (Nārāyaṇa Vajra).

○The left one is called Saho Kongō (Left Assistant Vajra). He rejoices in people's cessation of evil. He is also called Misshaku Kongō (Guhyapāda Vajra). Since they are guardian deities of Buddhism, they are enshrined at the sanmon (temple gate).
