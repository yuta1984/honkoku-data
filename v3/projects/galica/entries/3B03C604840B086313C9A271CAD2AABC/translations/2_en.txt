Urashima

Long ago, in the province of Tango, there lived a man named Urashima. He had a son called Urashima Tarō, a young man of twenty-four or twenty-five years of age. Day and night he caught fish in the sea to support his father and mother. One day, he went out intending to fish. He fished at various bays, islands, and inlets—there was no place he did not visit—and he picked up shells, harvested seaweed, and so forth. At a place called Ejima-ga-iso, he caught a turtle.
