○Among the同心 (fellow conspirators), three died of illness: Kinemon (Okano Kinemon, also known as Shinya), Yagashira Chōsuke, and Hashimoto Heizaemon.
This Heizaemon was the son of Hashimoto Mozaemon. Heizaemon committed double suicide with a courtesan at Shijimi River in Osaka's pleasure quarter. On the night of the 15th day of the 7th month of Genroku 15, around seven o'clock, at a teahouse called Awajiya, he stabbed to death a courtesan named Hatsu and then took his own life. He was eighteen years old at the time.

○On the 18th day of the 7th month of Genroku 15 (1702), when Lord Asano Daigaku's house confinement was pardoned, the monthly duty magistrate Abe Bungo-no-kami-dono Masatake (Fourth Court Rank Lower Grade, Chamberlain, 100,000 koku, residing at Oshi Castle in Musashi Province) sent word that a senior retainer should be dispatched to Lord Aki-no-kami. Accordingly, Akashi Kichidayū was sent. Bungo-no-kami-dono then conveyed:
"Since Asano Daigaku's confinement has been pardoned, Lord Aki-no-kami should return with his wife and children."
