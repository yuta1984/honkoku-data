▢One: In the domain of Lord Matsudaira Iyo-no-kami in Bitchū Okayama, great care was taken in cleaning the roads. A boat bridge was constructed over the Itsu River, three teahouses were erected on the riverbank, grass was removed even from the roofs along the roadway, brushwood fences were installed in various places, and water buckets were placed along the road. This was done to entertain the imperial envoy, and it was ordered that goods be sold at reduced prices.

▢One: At the border between Bitchū and Akō, at a place called Mitsuishi and Yoke-chi, samurai are stationed and record the comings and goings on the highway in inspection registers. There are also watchtowers at sea.

▢One: People from Bitchū domain do not go to Akō, and people from Akō are not permitted to enter Bitchū at all.
