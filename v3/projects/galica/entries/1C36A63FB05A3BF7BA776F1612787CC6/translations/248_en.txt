Senda-no-shō: There is Senda Village in Katori District, and the surrounding fourteen villages are called Senda-no-shō. Hara-no-gō: There is Harakata Village in Sōsa District. Nakamura-no-gō is Nakamura in Katori District. Kanehara-no-gō: There is Kanehara Village in the same district. Usui-no-shō: There is Usui Village in Inba District, and the surrounding twenty villages are called Usui-no-shō. Shimada Village, Makino Village, Hirato Village, and Komuro Village are in Chiba District. Hachiman-no-shō: There is Hachiman Station in Katsushika District, and the surrounding [unclear] villages are called Hachiman-no-shō. Soya Village and Akiyama Village are in the same district. Kōshōji Temple is called Matsuo-san and was founded by Nichiyū. The matter is detailed in the Ōzōshi section cited earlier. Honmyōji Temple also appears earlier. Tanesada was the son of Munetane, called Senda Tarō, and became Ōsumi-no-kami. In the first year of Genkō, he followed Chiba-no-suke Sadatane to advance to Hishū and achieved military merit. In the third year of Kenmu, he followed Takauji and went down to Kantō, but fell ill on the way and died in Sanshū at age forty-nine. His posthumous Buddhist name was Shuntoku-in Nichiei. Mitani-dō refers to Hōrinji Temple in Katori District. Iitaka Village is divided into three parts: Nekoya Valley, Nakadai Valley, and Matsuwada Valley, the so-called three valleys.
