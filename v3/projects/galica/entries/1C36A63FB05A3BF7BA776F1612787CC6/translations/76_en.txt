According to the "Sengaku-shū," on the first day of the sixth month of the first year of Daiji, Hinoe-uma (1126), construction of Chiba began. In total, there were approximately 16,000 houses.
The front streets had 8,000 houses, the back streets had 8,000 houses, and there were over 580 alleys in front and back combined. The seven ri distance from Sohaka Daimyōjin to the front of Otatsuhō Inari Shrine was the honorable lodging area. From Sohaka to Hirokōji and Yaheta, there were residences of various samurai throughout the province. This was within the moated area of Lord Ikeda Kaburaki, and the honorable lodging was for the honorable clan members. To the east of the lodging was the Enjōji clan family style, and to the west was the Hara clan family style. From the bridge facing toward Otatsuhō were the residences of lodging people. Therefore, the area across the river is called the marketplace. The guardian deities of Chiba are Sohaka Daimyōjin, Gozu Tennō of the moated area, Shinmei of Yūki, Inari Daimyōjin of Otatsuhō, and Ryūzō Gongen of Chiba-dera. The archery deities are Myōken Hachiman and Marishiten Daibosatsu. The sacred festivals of Chiba began on the sixteenth day of the seventh month of the second year of Daiji, Hinoto-hitsuji (1127). This was during Lord Tsuneshige's reign. The temporary shrine for the imperial procession had eight shrine priests, eight shrine families, and four maidens. The festival boats were the responsibility of the elders of the lodging area. The offerings were from thirteen kan worth of places in Chiba Nakano. Similarly, the toll money was contributed by the various samurai groups. At one checkpoint, shrine priests were given the offerings from the temporary shrine, and at another checkpoint, elders were given them to conduct the festival.
