The second gate is now at the location of the torii of Shinmei Shrine. During the Chōroku period, from when Sukétane, son of Makuwari Yasutane, moved here from Chiba, through nine generations to Shigetane, the castle fell along with Odawara in the seventh month of Tenshō 18. In the eighth month, Kuno Saburōzaemon-jō Muneyoshi (now a retainer of the Kishū house; at that time he was a retainer of Mantchiyo-dono) was enfeoffed with this land. During the Keichō years, his son Minbu-shōyū Munehide was transferred to Enshū Kuno, and the castle was abandoned (later in Keichō 14, Doi Toshikatsu was enfeoffed with this land, but he rebuilt the castle at Kashima. This is the present Sakura Castle).
