**Ichikawa Former Castle Site**: This is the grounds of Sōneiji temple in Kōnodai village.

○According to the Kamakura Ōsōshi (Volume 2), Uesugi Noritada raised Sanetane and Tane, two sons of Nakatsukasakyū Nyūdō Ryōshin, who fortified themselves in Ichikawa castle in Shimōsa Province. In the first month of Kōshō 2nd year, Minami Zusho, Yanada Dewa-no-kami and many others were dispatched, and after several battles, the castle was finally conquered on the 19th day of the same month. Yanada Kawachi-no-kami advanced from Sekiyado, seized more than half of Adachi District in Musashi Province, and took Ichikawa castle.

※Ichikawa castle was also called Kōnodai castle. However, the statement in "Edo Meisho Zue" that the site of Ichikawa castle is now unknown is an error due to not knowing that Kōnodai was originally part of Ichikawa. ○The "Gikeiki" states that on the 11th day of the 9th month of Jishō 4th year, [Yoshitsune] arrived at a place called Ichikawa in Matsudo-no-shō, on the border between Musashi and Shimōsa. ○Ichikawa appears to be a place name near the provincial capital, as the name Ichikawa also appears near Hitachi Fuchū. There are likely others elsewhere as well. ○In the "Natsume-ki," in the section on Kagekatsu and Toshiie's land survey of the two provinces of Ōu, it states "crossing the Ichikawa ferry at the border of Musashi and Shimōsa." Considering these sources, calling the west side of the Edo River "Musashi" appears to be an ancient practice.
