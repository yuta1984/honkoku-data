For this reason, there was a common saying among the common people in the Kanto region about inferiors surpassing superiors: "Chiba commands a hundred horsemen, Hara commands a thousand" or "After Chiba comes Hara, after Hara come Takagi and the two Sakai." During the Odawara campaign in Tensho 18 (1590), Shikibu-no-shosuke Tanenari, serving as military deputy for Senkakumaru Shigetane, commanded eight thousand men and defended the Yumoto entrance in Sagami province. However, with the fall of the Hojo house, the Chiba house also fell into ruin. After the Great Divine Lord (Tokugawa Ieyasu) entered the Kanto region, Shikibu-no-shosuke's heir Monto-no-suke (later renamed Heinai), though of modest means, was summoned as a bannerman and added to the ranks of close attendants. However, due to the crime of believing in the Christian faith, he was sentenced to punishment in the autumn of Keicho 19 (1614) and his line was extinguished. Also, since Chiba-no-suke Shigetane was still young, his retainers could not maintain Sakura Castle and surrendered it to Honda Tadakatsu and Sakai Ieharu, forcing them to flee their hereditary domain.
