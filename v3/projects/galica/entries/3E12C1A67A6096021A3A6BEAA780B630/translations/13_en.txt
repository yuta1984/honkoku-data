Carving (Horimono)

In the world, the Goto clan is renowned for this craft. According to tradition, Goto Shirobei Sukemasa was a retainer of Lord Yoshinori of Fukouin. Once he incurred Lord Yoshinori's anger and was imprisoned. It was during the scorching heat of June, unbearably hot, but the jailer took pity on him and gave him a peach. Sukemasa was greatly pleased and carved on the surface of the peach pit the twenty-one shrines of Sanno and sixty-six monkeys. The precision was beyond human capability. When Lord Yoshinori heard of this and saw it, he deemed it truly marvelous, pardoned his crime, and immediately ordered Sukemasa to create decorative fittings for swords. The divine skill made birds, beasts, plants, and human figures appear as if alive, and thus he founded the house of carving. The peach pit he carved is now
