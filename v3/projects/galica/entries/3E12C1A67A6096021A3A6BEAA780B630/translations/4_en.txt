Cap-maker

The origin of court caps began when Emperor Itoku, the fourth sovereign emperor, established the three caps of heaven, earth, and man. Later, during the reign of Emperor Tenmu, men and women first began to arrange their hair in formal styles. From this time, lacquered caps were used and

[Left page]
[Figure of a man holding an eboshi court hat]
ceremonial etiquette was established. Today's gauze caps and eboshi hats also have their origins here. Also, from the time of Emperor Monmu, thick-fronted, thin-fronted, and transparent-fronted caps were instituted. The transparent-fronted cap had gauze stretched across its crown like a crescent moon, and was also called "moon-fronted" in the world. That boys of fifteen years and above should seek to display their vigor through these is a manifestation of His Majesty's gracious favor - how awesome and fearful to contemplate! All court officials receive these with proper dignity and ceremony.

There are so many varieties of eboshi that one has no time to count them all. Among them, the wind-bent eboshi is said to have originated with Ariwara no Narihira, the Middle Captain of the Fifth Rank.
