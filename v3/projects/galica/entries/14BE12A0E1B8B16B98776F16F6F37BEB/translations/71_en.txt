When they confessed that they were the two men Kokushu Mōjumon, this became clear, so on the 19th day of the 9th month of Tenshō 16 (1588), they were executed by crucifixion at Awadaguchi. From this time, orders were transmitted not only to Kyoto and Osaka but to all provinces, and those who secretly and privately hung images of the heavenly emperor and chanted mantras in worship were strictly investigated. At this time, this religious sect was largely eradicated.
