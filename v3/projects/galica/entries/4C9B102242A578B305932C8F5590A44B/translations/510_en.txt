The soldiers in the castle had become like empty cicada shells, and moreover, their arrows and gunpowder were exhausted. When Takigawa Kazumasu consulted with his retainers about surrendering and retreating, they replied that it would be most reasonable. He immediately surrendered the castle and on the 27th day of the same month retreated to Kitsukuri Castle in Ise Province. Previously, both he and Tomita Sakon-no-jō had been stationed at this castle, but Tomita said, "Regarding the retreat from Kanie Castle, what kind of firm promise could there be? I cannot enter this castle." Takigawa Kazumasu found himself in a situation where he belonged neither in heaven nor on earth, and later met his end at a place called Gobuichi in Echizen Province.

According to the Chronicle of Toyotomi Hideyoshi: "Takigawa Sakon-shōgen Kazumasu, after the destruction of Shibata (Katsuie), was living in hiding in Echizen. Hideyoshi, valuing his military reputation, had him reside in Kanbe in Ise. When Kazumasu heard that Hideyoshi and Nobukatsu had joined forces, he immediately sent an envoy to Maeda Yojūrō, lord of Kanie Castle in Owari Province, saying: 'You should be loyal to Hideyoshi. If you do so, your rewards will be substantial.' Maeda listened to this. Thereupon, Kazumasu, together with Kuki Uma-no-jō Yoshitaka, boarded ships and entered Kanie Castle. The Great Sovereign (Tokugawa Ieyasu), together with Nobukatsu, heard of this and immediately led troops to attack them. Sakai Tadatsugu and Sakakibara Yasumasa..."
