Mt. Kajō Kōzen-ji Temple 《Small text: Located across from Kiyomizu Temple, it is a branch temple directly under Nishi Hongan-ji》 was originally located in Kajō village, Kaisai District,
It was a Tendai sect temple and an imperial prayer temple of Emperor Kanmu, but in Tenbun 23rd year Kinoe-Tora, the monk Enshō changed it to the current sect according to tradition. Alternatively, Rennyo Shōnin is considered the restorer, and according to the Biyō Zakki, Rennyo Shōnin's son Jitsue and his son Shōkyō obtained permission from the head temple to serve as abbots. In medieval times the temple was moved to Kiyosu, moved again to Nagoya during the Keichō era, and changed from the Eastern to Western branch during the Kanbun era. ○Main deity 《Small text: Standing statue of Amida》
