Nobemai Kaisho (Rice Exchange) {{Located on the west side of Horie-machi. During the Genna era (1615-1624), a man named Chōzaemon, who lived as an adopted brother in the house of Ōhashi Sukekunō in Funairi-machi, assessed the abundance or scarcity of rice in various provinces and purchased rice. He borrowed Ōhashi's storehouse and conducted small-scale rice transactions, unexpectedly earning substantial profits. Whether buying or selling, he always doubled his profits, so other people imitated this practice. Later, they traded vast quantities of rice with minimal capital, eventually petitioning the authorities to establish an exchange office, which has prospered increasingly day by day as it is today}}

Gakkan Meirintō (School Hall) {{Extending from Katahana-suji Nagashima-machi to Chōja-machi. To the east is the Seidō Gate, to the north is the lord's passage gate, and to the west is the main gate of the school}} Lord Genkei first established a place of learning south of Ōtsu-machi, later moved it west of Kinshita Gate, renamed it Meirintō during the Kan'ei era (1624-1644), and hung a plaque written in the lord's own hand. During the Tenmei era (1781-1789), it was moved to the present location, where the Seidō {{the plaque for the Hall of Ancient Sages was written by Lord Genkei}}, lecture hall, school building, and storehouses were newly constructed. The spring and autumn ceremonial rites are conducted with great solemnity.
