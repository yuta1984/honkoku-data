After his defeat, he took refuge with Ōi, a wealthy man of Aobaka in Mino Province, and from there intended to go down to the eastern provinces. Ōi treated him cordially and suggested, "You should spend the New Year here and then quietly proceed on your journey." However, saying "This place is on the main highway, so it would be inconvenient. You should quickly go to Noma in Owari and rely on Osada," he urged haste. But the local people had already heard the news, and two or three hundred gathered and pressed forward. Seeing this, Sado Shikibu-no-taifu Shigenari resolved to fight to the death to let his lord pass through. Mounting his horse, he scattered the attacking forces thoroughly, entered the Koyasu Forest, and crying out "Samanosuke Yoshitomo commits suicide here!" he peeled off the skin of his face, cut open his belly, and died in vain. During this distraction, Yoshitomo relied on Ōi's younger brother, Washisu Genkō, and under cover of night boarded a small boat and went down the Kuise River. On the 29th day of the 12th month (the 28th according to the illustrated biography), he arrived at the residence of Osada Shōji Tadamune (sometimes written as Tadamune) of this place. His retinue consisted of four men: Kamata Hyōe Masukiyo (sometimes written as Masatada or Masaie), Hiraga Shirō Yoshinobu, Shibuya Konjōmaru, and Genkō.
