However, in the 5th year of Kenkyū (Kinoe-Tora, 1194), in early April, when Hōjō Shirō Tokimasa traveled from the Kantō region to the capital, he made various prayers to the principal deity of this temple. When he returned the following year, he stayed at this temple for a while and, firstly for the wishes of Lord Yoritomo (the right shogun), and secondly as gratitude for the fulfillment of his own cherished願, he rebuilt the temple halls and donated temple lands. Several certificates of this donation still remain today. ○ The principal deity is Yakushi Nyorai and the Twelve Divine Generals, all carved by Bodhisattva Gyōki. The temple treasures include a two-volume origin record (copied in the 2nd year of Enkyō) and a temple bell (inscribed "Bishū Kaitō-gun Tomita-shō Jōgan-ji temple bell, Great Patron: wife of Kawasaki Ukyō-no-suke Tsūsada, 18th year of Bunmei, Hinoe-Uma, 11th month, 13th day").

Godai-san Anyō-ji Myōgen-in (located in Majima village, a branch temple of Noda Mitsuzō-in of the Tendai sect). The founding of this temple was in the 21st year of Enryaku during the reign of Emperor Kanmu, established as a sacred site by Saint Shōen. Initially, the saint received an auspicious dream and built temple halls and pagodas on this site, enshrining the sacred statue of Yakushi Nyorai previously carved by Bodhisattva Gyōki.
