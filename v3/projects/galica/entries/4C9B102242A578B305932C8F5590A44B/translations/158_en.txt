Tanakasan Hōzōji Temple《Marginal note: Located south of Hōshūji Temple. Honganji sect Eastern branch, subsidiary to Shōman-ji Temple in Harisaki, Mikawa Province. This temple was founded in Chōji 2 (1105), when Minamoto no Kyōmori, second son of Kamakura Gon-no-gorō Kagemasa, built it at the time of his tonsure at Samegai in Ōmi Province》
《Marginal note: In Kashō 1 (1106), it was moved to Otai village in Kasugai district of this province. Later, due to certain circumstances it fell into ruin, but in Meiō 7 (1498), Kakugen, a disciple of Master Rennyo, restored it. In Kan'ei 3 (1626), it was relocated to Tanaka within Nagoya village. Around this time, Bashō stayed at this temple during his travels in this province, and the hokku he composed then was carved in stone and still stands in the temple grounds today.
