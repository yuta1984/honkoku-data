[Heading, boxed section] Yama-hototogisu (Mountain Cuckoo)
Hototogisu-sō (Cuckoo Grass)
[Outside box] The flower shape is that of a regular hototogisu, with colors having speckled patterns like the feathers of that bird. Compared to regular hototogisu, the leaves are rounder and flowers bloom earlier. Flowers bloom in the seventh and eighth months. The Chinese name is Sanpaku-sō (Three White Grass).
[Dividing line]
[Heading, boxed section] Karukaya Chinese name: Enbaku (Wild Oats)
[Outside box] Leaves are like chyahikigusa (brome grass) and flowers are the same color. Flowers, leaves, and stems are all black and hard. Flowers bloom in the seventh month. In waka poetry it is also called "tatakaya," and variously sung in poems as "shiromekaya."
[Dividing line]
[Heading, boxed section] Heikōtai [marginal note: flowers in 6th, 7th, 8th months]
[Outside box] Leaves resemble hōōna with deep indentations, and at the tips of flower stalks bloom round flowers in lapis purple color shaped like thistles. It is an extremely unusual flower. It is also called "hirai-sō" or "hikotae."
[Dividing line]
[Heading, boxed section] Ukon-gusa
Ukon (Turmeric)
[Outside box] Leaves resemble bijin-shō (canna lily). The flower shape is like butterbur flowers grown tall, blooming within the leaves. The color is white with a slight color variation visible. Flowers bloom in the eighth and ninth months.
[Dividing line]
[Heading, boxed section] Aoi (Hollyhock) [marginal note: flowers in 5th month]
Kenki
Kara-aoi [marginal note: both called]
[Outside box] Crimson with double and single varieties. Flower shape is like cotton flowers. Purple in single and multiple-petaled varieties. Snow white in multiple-petaled and single varieties. Genji (light purple) with white flower edges. Double and single varieties exist. Bottom-white with double and single varieties. The center of the flower is white.

[Left page]
[Heading, boxed section] Shokki (Chinese Hollyhock)
[Outside box] Also called "bottom-red" has red centers. Light ink color and light mouse color with double and single varieties.
[Dividing line]
[Heading, boxed section] Seki-ran (Rock Orchid)
[Outside box] Leaves are like hakuran or usuran, with rock-like rootstock rising 2-3 inches above the soil. Numbers increase yearly, eventually becoming like rock formations. Between these, flowers extend 7-8 inches. Flower shape resembles large orchids with turmeric color. Called ganseki-ran (rock-stone orchid). Flowers bloom in the sixth and seventh months. Orchids bloom at the same time.
[Dividing line]
[Heading, boxed section] Nago-ran
[Outside box] Leaves are narrow, long and thick, with rounded tips. Leaf color is verdigris, like painted with blue lacquer. Flowers are pale white with slight blue tint, light yellow in color. Extending about 4-5 inches, opening in stages. Flower shape resembles orchid flowers with five petals, with one projection in the flower center having dark purple spotted patterns - a lovely flower. Blooms in the seventh and eighth months.
[Dividing line]
[Heading, boxed section] Fū-ran (Wind Orchid) [marginal note: flowers from 5th-6th months]
Osa-ran (Reed Orchid) [marginal note: until 8th month]
[Outside box] Leaves are like willow leaves, narrow, thick, glossy and small. Flower color is white, small-wheeled like orchid flowers. There are also "nago-ran" and "kaya-ran." Nago-ran has large leaves and yellow flower color like small orchids. Blooms between leaves. The roots rise up creating a very attractive appearance. Kaya-ran has opposite leaves growing densely, shaped like a weaving reed (osa). Hence called osa-ran.
