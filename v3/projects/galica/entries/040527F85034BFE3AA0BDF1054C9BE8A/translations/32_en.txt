【Heading, boxed section】Tokei-ran (Also called: Sawa-ebine, or "grass")
【Outside the box】The leaves are like those of ebine but shorter.
The flower shape resembles ebine but is smaller, with a blackish-red color.
Star patterns appear on the leaves - some have white stars,
others have purple stars. It is also called Mizuma-ebine, Ko-ebine,
or Sawa-ebine. Flowers bloom in April and May.

【Heading, boxed section】Sawa-kikyō (Marsh Bellflower)
【Outside the box】The flowers resemble bellflowers with deep cuts and long petals. The color is also
the same as bellflowers. The leaves are also similar. It grows to a height of about 2-3 shaku and grows near water. Flowers bloom in June, July, and August.

【Heading, boxed section】Chōji-sō (Clove Grass)
Sankō-sai (Mountain Bellflower)
【Outside the box】The leaves resemble willow leaves, and the flowers are indigo-colored with a shape like
a clove and small blooms. They bloom between the leaves. Flowers appear in March.

【Heading, boxed section】Katako-grass
(Katako-yuri)
Kangū (Drought Lotus Root)
【Outside the box】The leaves resemble bamboo grass leaves, and the flower shape is like a lily with a light pink
color. This is the katakuri mentioned in the Man'yōshū, and it has been
cultivated for a long time. Horticulturists call it hatsu-yuri (first lily). Flowers appear in February.

【Heading, boxed section】Mayu-haki-gusa (Eyebrow Brush Grass) / Aki-azami (Autumn Thistle)
Tamura-gusa (flowers bloom in February and August)
【Outside the box】Both flowers and leaves resemble thistles, with thin leaves that have
no thorns. The flower stems stand upright and branch into small stems with many flowers

blooming. It is called autumn thistle. What is called eyebrow brush is not limited to autumn thistle
but also refers to spring thistle. The flower shape resembles an eyebrow brush. One variety has thick
leaves with deep cuts, and the flowers are also larger than ordinary autumn thistles.

【Heading, boxed section】Kugai-sō
【Outside the box】The flower shape is like a tiger's tail with a light purple color. The leaves are
arranged in whorls, emerging in tiers. Flowers bloom in May.

Chrysanthemums arise from autumn and last until autumn, never ceasing throughout the year. The virtue of their leaves
resembles that of pine. When autumn grass flowers are gathered, they are in full bloom, and become even more
abundant as winter approaches. Though they share the single name "chrysanthemum," they have many appellations. The flowers also come in various sizes.
First, those with a diameter of one sun or less using a metal measure are classified as small blooms. There are two grades of small blooms.
Five to six bu or less are considered small blooms. One sun to two sun are medium blooms. Two sun to three sun are
large blooms. From three sun two bu corresponds to a small handle, so it's large-large. Three sun five bu and
above should be called large-large-large. First, what is recorded here includes white Suiyōbi, yellow Suiyōbi, Daihan'nya, yellow Daihan'nya, Nuresaki, and among the small blooms: Ko-shōjō, Ko-gane-menuki, and Seiken-ji sakiwake.
