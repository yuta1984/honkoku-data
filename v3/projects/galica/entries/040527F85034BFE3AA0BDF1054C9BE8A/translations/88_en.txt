【Heading, boxed section】Oka-kōhone (Yellow Pond Lily)
【Outside the box】The flower stalk resembles that of kōhone. The color is yellow with a slight reddish tinge. The flower stem is long and has branches. The leaf arrangement resembles camellia but has round serrations and is glossy. There is redness at the base of the axis. There are also pouch-like leaves at the base of branches. Blooms from April to May.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Hōsenka (Garden Balsam)
【Outside the box】The flower shape is like a bird spreading its wings. The color is red. There are deep red and pure white varieties. There are peach-colored ones. There are purple ones. There are also striped varieties. There are also speckled ones. The leaf shape also resembles peach and is long. The plant growth varies in size. The height is about 3-4 shaku. There are also 1-2 shaku ones. Some are 4-5 sun tall. Some have branches, some don't. Present from March, and also from August to September. Grows in mountain villages. The proper name is kinpōke (Golden Phoenix Flower). Commonly called honenuki-gusa (bone-removing grass).

【Heading, boxed section】Kōka (Safflower)
【Outside the box】Leaf color is light green. Both the receptacle and stem are shallow. In April-May there are crimson flowers. The leaves resemble thistle but have no serrations. There are thorns, and they grow thickly with bristles on the leaves, making them

hard. Also, the flowers are red in color, and the buds resemble thistle. The thorns and leaves grow so thickly they wrap around one's hand, and the flowers open only slightly. Also, the stamens extend and the tips split into five parts. The height extends to about 3 shaku, with many branches. Also, many buds attach alongside the leaves.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Mizu-aoi (Water Plantain)
Mizu-nagi
Fushoku
Sawa-kikyō
【Outside the box】The leaf shape resembles nagi and is glossy. Emerges coiled around the stem. Those with leaves resembling aoi are called mizu-aoi. The flower axis stands upright, with 6-petaled flowers blooming in succession. Three varieties: light color, white, and purple. Like kikyō, there is color at the flower tips. Resembles ruri, fuji, and kikyō but smaller, with deeply cut petals. Flowers bloom individually. Present in June, July, and August.
【Dividing line】
【Heading, boxed section】Sagi-sō (White Egret Flower) 《Small text: Omodaka》
Hakugaku 《Small text: resembles》
【Outside the box】Leaves resemble bamboo grass leaves and are narrow, emerging coiled around the stem. The flowers are snow-white, shaped like a small bird in flight, or resembling the shape of an egret in flight. Found at the edge of marshes. It is an aquatic plant. Present in July and August.
