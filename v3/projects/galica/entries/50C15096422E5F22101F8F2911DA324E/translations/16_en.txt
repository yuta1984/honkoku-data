Captain came before him. Looking at this princess, he said, "How strange! How mysterious that she looks exactly like the prince from whom we parted empty-handed!" As he gazed upon her, he wept profusely. The princess said to the Middle Captain, "What matter are you grieving over?" The Middle Captain replied, "When I look upon your appearance, you are exactly like the prince from whom I parted empty-handed. Remembering him now, tears of deep sorrow flow forth." When she asked, "Could it be that prince?" the Middle Captain said, "That person was sixteen, I was seventeen when we parted. It has been fourteen years now. You were abandoned on a lotus pedestal,

and I myself found you and entrusted you to the lady-in-waiting. How could you be that person?" The princess said, "Because you have offered so many memorial services, I was reborn in the Western Paradise. Brahma, Indra, and all the bodhisattvas gathered and said, 'This person made false vows in the secular world, but afterward will attain enlightenment,' and thus I was born in this way. I myself had no place to return to, and longing for you, my beloved, I took residence in that womb. I was abandoned because I was said to be disabled. I have the light blue obi that we divided when we parted from you. Please look at this," she said.
