○ Ryukyu (Three Provinces, Thirty-six Islands) - A national map is made separately on one sheet and attached to this volume
Ryukyu, also known as Ryūkyū. Sometimes called Akikina Island (Okinoshima). Also called Yakinya (Okino) Island
The country extends five and a half days' journey north to south (about sixty ri by Japanese roads), and a little over one day east to west (fourteen to fifteen ri by Japanese roads). Beyond this, there are thirty-six islands
Chūzan is called Nakagami Province, with fourteen subordinate districts
Sannan is called Shimajiri Province, with twelve subordinate districts  
Sanhoku is called Kunigami Province, with ten subordinate districts
The country is located at 25-26 degrees north latitude, in the celestial region of the Nü and Niū constellations
The country is located 140 ri south of Satsuma
Kikaijima, Ōshima, Tokunoshima and others are called "road islands" by the people of Satsuma
