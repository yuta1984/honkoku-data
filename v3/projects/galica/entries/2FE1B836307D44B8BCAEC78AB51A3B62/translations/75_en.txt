On the 23rd and 24th, they continued their bombardment day and night, whereupon the castle's garrison was exhausted and raised white flags (marginal note: this is a sign of surrender) to request surrender. Barbanègre led out his 1,900 subordinates, laying down their arms and opening the castle, so when the Archduke accepted their surrender and entered the castle, the magistrate welcomed him in, allowed the inhabitants to return to their respective hometowns, and dispatched the main troops to the rear of the Baden River to pacify the area.
