In the tenth month, Sanjo Saneshige resigned from the position of Chancellor. Former Minister of the Interior Minamoto no Michitaka was appointed as Chancellor. Minamoto no Michishige resigned from Minister of the Interior. Hanayama-in Moronobu was appointed as Minister of the Interior.

In the second year, fifth month, Hojo Atsutoki of Rokuhara died. In the seventh month, former Regent Kujo Moronori passed away at the age of forty-eight.

In the first year of Genko (1321), second month, former Minister of the Right Imadegawa Fujiwara no Kimiaki (Ki no Akiaki) passed away. In the fourth month, Retired Emperor Go-Uda built the golden hall (kondo) of Daikaku-ji temple.

In the fifth month, there was an imperial visit to Daikaku-ji. In the sixth month, Chinzei Tandai Hojo Kanetoki died. This summer there was a great drought. The Emperor commanded Kebiishi-betto Fujiwara no Tsunenobu to distribute millet to provide relief to the poor. He also made wealthy people in the capital sell their stored rice cheaply to relieve the famine. The Emperor personally went to the Records Office to decide on legal appeals.
