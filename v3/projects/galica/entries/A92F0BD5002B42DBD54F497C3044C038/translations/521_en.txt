Emperor Go-Fushimi: His personal name was Tanehito, and he was the crown prince of Emperor Fushimi. His mother was Empress Eifukumon-in, daughter of Saionji Minister Sanekane. In reality, he was born to Fujiwara no Tsuneko, daughter of Minister Fujiwara no Tsuneuji, but was adopted by the Empress and made crown prince. He ascended the throne in the seventh month of Einin 6 (1298) at the age of eleven. Takatsukasa Regent Kanetada served as regent.

At this time, the four retired emperors Go-Fukakusa, Kameyama, Go-Uda, and Fushimi were all still alive, so there were four retired emperor palaces. In the eighth month, Prince Kuniharu, son of Retired Emperor Go-Uda, was made crown prince. He was a second cousin of the current emperor. In the twelfth month, Kanetada resigned as regent, and Nijō Minister of the Left Kanenobu became regent.

In the first year of Shōan (1299), in the fourth month, Regent Kanenobu resigned as Minister of the Left. Kujō Moronori transferred to the left, and Saionji Kinohira was appointed Minister of the Right. Takatsukasa Major Counselor Fuyuhira was appointed Minister of the Center. In the sixth month, former Minister of the Center Dōin Kinmori was appointed Grand Minister. In the same month, former Minister Saionji Sanekane took Buddhist vows at age fifty-one.
