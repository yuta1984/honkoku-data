Former Minister of the Left Minamoto no Sadazane was appointed to the position of Chancellor (Daijō-daijin). He was a favorite retainer of Retired Emperor Go-Uda, and his sons Dainagon Masafusa and Chūnagon Chikasada were all promoted to high positions.

In the same month, Hōjō Mototoki came up to the capital and took position as the Northern Deputy (Kitakata) of Rokuhara. He was the grandson of Naritoki. In the eighth month, Tomihito, the second son of Retired Emperor Fushimi, was made Crown Prince. In the same month, Hōjō Sadatoki took the tonsure and adopted the Buddhist name Sūen. He transferred the position of Shikken (regent) to his son-in-law Hōjō Morotoki. Morotoki was the grandson of Tokiyori.

In the ninth month, Hōjō Nobutoki also took the tonsure. Hōjō Tokimura, being the son of Masamura and advanced in age, was appointed by Sadatoki's arrangement to assist Morotoki as co-regent (shikken kahan). Since Tokimura's grandson Hirotoki was also Sadatoki's son-in-law, Morotoki and Tokimura maintained very harmonious relations and conducted affairs according to Sadatoki's wishes.
