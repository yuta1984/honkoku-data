Mori (continuing from previous page) resigned as Grand Minister. In the eleventh month, Nijō Regent Kanenobu was appointed Grand Minister. In the twelfth month, Kinohira resigned as Minister of the Center, and former Minister of the Center Tokudaiji Kintaka was appointed Minister of the Right.

From New Year's Day of this year, the Zen monk Issan came to court. This was because he had received secret orders from that country's (Yuan's) emperor to serve as a spy in Japan. Hōjō Sadatoki perceived this and captured Issan, exiling him to Izu. Although he was later pardoned, Issan did not return to his home country but remained in Japan, spreading Zen teachings and becoming the abbot of Nanzen-ji temple. Among the Zen monks who came to court around this time, there were probably many more like Issan.

In the first month of the second year, His Majesty came of age, with Regent Kanenobu performing the capping ceremony. In the fourth month, Kanenobu resigned as Grand Minister. In the seventh month, Hōjō Sanemasa became Chinzei Tandai and went down to Kyushu. He was a fifth-generation descendant of Yoshitoki.
