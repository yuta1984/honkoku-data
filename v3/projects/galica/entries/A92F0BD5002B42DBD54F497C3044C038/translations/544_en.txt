In the same year, the Emperor summoned his courtiers for scholarly debates on the Five Classics and Three Histories. Around this time, Retired Emperor Go-Uda sent Major Counselor Fujiwara no Sadafusa to the Kanto region and expressed his wish to entrust politics to the current Emperor and live in retirement. As the military government had different intentions, he was made to retire to Daikakuji Temple.

In the eighth month, Minister of the Left Sanetai and Minister of the Center Fuyuuji resigned from their offices. Minister of the Right Kujō Fusazane was transferred to Minister of the Left, Major Counselor Imadegawa Fujiwara no Kanesue was transferred to Minister of the Right. Major Counselor Takatsukasa Fuyunori was appointed Minister of the Center. Kanesue was the youngest son of Saionji Sanekane. Because there were chrysanthemums in his residence, he was called Kikutei (Chrysanthemum Garden). It is said and passed down that this was later changed to Kikutei.

In the same month, Shiren of Tōfukuji Temple presented the "Genkō Shakusho" (Buddhist History of the Genkō Era). Shiren was also called Kokan.

In the ninth month, former Minister Saionji Sanekane passed away at the age of seventy-four.
