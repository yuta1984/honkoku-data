Yoritsugu died in Kyoto at the age of eighteen. In the eleventh month, Tokiyori transferred the position of shikken (regent) to Hōjō Musashi-no-kami Nagatoki, took the tonsure, retired to Yamanouchi, took the religious name Dōsū, and was called Saimyōji. He was thirty years old at the time. Many people shaved their heads at the same time. While they said this was to show their unwavering loyalty to Tokiyori, it seemed like independent action, so he had them cease their court service. Since Tokiyori's son was still young, Nagatoki served as his representative and co-signed documents with Masamura. However, none of them failed to receive Tokiyori's instructions.

In the first year of Shōka, second month, the daughter of former Minister Saionji Saneuji became chūgū (empress consort). Since she was the emperor's aunt, she was much older than him. In the same month, for Tokiyori's succession, Tokimune came of age at seven years old, with Munetaka personally performing the capping ceremony and Nagatoki serving as the hairdresser.
