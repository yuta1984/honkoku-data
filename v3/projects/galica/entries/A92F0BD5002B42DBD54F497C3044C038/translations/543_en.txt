Hojo Takatoki had his kinsman Tokiwa Suruga-no-kami Norisada reside at Rokuhara, and made Hojo Hidetoki the Tandai of Chinzei. Around this time, Takatoki's chief retainer (naikakuryo) Nagasaki Enki, due to his advanced age, transferred his position to his eldest son Takasuke. Takasuke became arrogant, treated Takatoki with contempt, and wielded tyrannical power.

In the second year, first month, there was an imperial visit for audience with the Retired Emperor, and court music performances were held.

In the fifth month, there was a dispute between Ando Goro and Matataro in Oshu province. Because Nagasaki Takasuke took bribes from both sides and showed personal favoritism, Ando rebelled. Also around the same time, men called Watanabe of Sesshu, Yasuda of Kii, and Ochi of Yamato and others turned against the military government. For over a hundred years since the Jokyu Disturbance, defiance of the Hojo family's orders began with Ando. The Emperor had long resented being at the complete mercy of the military houses. Hearing that Takatoki indulged in wine and women, and that Takasuke's tyrannical power went against the will of the people, he secretly planned with his close retainers to destroy Kamakura
