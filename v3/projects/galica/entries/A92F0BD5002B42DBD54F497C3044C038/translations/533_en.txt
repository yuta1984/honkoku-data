In the third year, eleventh month, Hōjō Sadaaki returned from Rokuhara to Kamakura.

In the fourth year, third month, Minamoto no Tomamori resigned from Naidaijin. Dōin Dainagon Sanetai was appointed to Naidaijin. In the seventh month, Hōjō Hirotoki died. Hōjō Motoki and Hōjō Sadaaki became Shikken (Regent) and Rensho (Co-regent). Motoki was the grandson of Naritoki. Sadaaki was the great-grandson of a man called Sanetai, who was the fifth son of Yoshitoki. Sanetai's son, Echigo-no-kami Sanetoki, resided in Kanazawa and was called Shōmyōji. His son was called Echigo-no-kami Akitoki, who was Sadaaki's father. Since they all lived in Kanazawa, their family name was called Kanazawa. They established a library within Shōmyōji and collected numerous Japanese and Chinese books. They stamped it with a seal reading "Kanazawa Bunko." They used black seals for Confucian books and red seals for Buddhist books. Those historic remains are still preserved today. In the ninth month, Takatsukasa Sadaijin Fuyuhira became Kanpaku. In the same month, Hōjō Koresada went up to the capital and [became] the southern commissioner of Rokuhara
