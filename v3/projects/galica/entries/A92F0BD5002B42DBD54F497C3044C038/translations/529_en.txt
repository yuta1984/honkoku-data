Perhaps because many years had gradually passed, they changed the position. Prince Morikuni, son of Hisaaki, was only seven years old when he was appointed as Seii Taishōgun and made lord of Kamakura. Since Sadatoki had already taken the tonsure, Hōjō Morotoki and Hōjō Munenori were made Shikken (regent) and Rensho (co-signatory). Morikuni's mother was the daughter of Koreyasu.

In the eighth month, His Majesty passed away at the age of twenty-four. The era names were Kengen one year, Kagen three years, and Tokuji two years and some months, totaling a reign of six years and some months.

94th Emperor
Emperor Hanazono: His personal name was Tomihito. He was the second son of Emperor Fushimi. His mother was Kenshin Mon'in Fujiwara no Atsuko, daughter of Sadaijin Saneo. During Emperor Go-Nijō's reign, he was made Crown Prince through the arrangements of the Kantō (Kamakura).
