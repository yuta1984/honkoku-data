In the first month of the fourth year, His Majesty (Emperor Go-Saga) abdicated the throne to Crown Prince Hisahito. He was granted the honorary title of Daijō Tennō (Retired Emperor). The era name was Kangen. His reign lasted four years.

The Eighty-Eighth [Emperor]
Go-Fukakusa-in: His personal name was Hisahito. He was the second son of Go-Saga-in. His mother was Empress Fujiwara no Kitsuko, daughter of Saionji Saneuji. Born in the first year of Kangen, he ascended the throne in the first month of the fourth year at age four. Government affairs were decided by Retired Emperor Go-Saga from within the imperial palace. Saneuji, leveraging his position as maternal grandfather, cultivated relations with the Hōjō clan and monopolized power. Michiie was still alive and governed jointly with Saneuji.
