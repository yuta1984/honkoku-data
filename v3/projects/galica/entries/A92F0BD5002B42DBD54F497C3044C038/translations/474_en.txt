On the eleventh day of the seventh month, Yoritsune left Kamakura and returned to Kyoto. On the twenty-eighth day he arrived in the capital and took residence at Rokuhara. The warriors who had accompanied him on the journey all returned to Kamakura. Miura Mitsumura grieved deeply over the parting, shed tears, and said "I wish to return to Kamakura once more" before withdrawing. Mitsumura was the younger brother of Yasumura, the second son of Yoshimura. He had been a close retainer of Yoritsune since childhood. Although there had been discussion about Yoritsune's return to the capital since Tsunetoki's time, Tokiyori likely hastened its implementation due to the Mitsutoki incident. Miura Yasumura was a great lord of hereditary standing, and moreover, being son-in-law to Yasutoki, he was consulted on governmental affairs. Akita-jō-no-suke Yoshikage was also close to Tokiyori and thus competed with Yasumura for influence. At this time, Hōjō Shigetoki had long resided in the capital and was well-versed in governmental affairs, so Tokiyori proposed that he should be summoned down to Kamakura for consultation on various matters.
