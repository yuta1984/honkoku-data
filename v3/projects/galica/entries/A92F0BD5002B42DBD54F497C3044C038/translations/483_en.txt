[Yoshiuji] was Tokimasa's grandson and was quite capable, not inferior to other warriors, so the Hōjō clan also respected him. Takauji was a descendant of Yoshiuji. In the twelfth month, Munetaka summoned Kawachi-no-kami Minamoto no Chikayuki and had him discourse on the Tale of Genji. Since governmental affairs were all at Tokiyori's discretion, Munetaka spent his days with waka poetry and kemari.

In the seventh year, third month, there was an imperial pilgrimage to Kumano, with Sanzankengyo Prince Gakunin serving as the guide. This was the first time a prince served as a guide.

In the first year of Kōgen, third month, Hōjō Mutsu-no-kami Shigetoki resigned from his position. His younger brother Masamura took his place and co-signed documents with Tokiyori. In the fourth month, Hōjō Nagatoki resigned from his position at Rokuhara and returned to Kamakura. His younger brother Tokishige went to the capital as his replacement, at the age of twenty-seven.
