[They tried] to conclude [the arrangement]. Nagasaki Takasuke did not agree, so Yasumura became angry, shaved his head, and took the Buddhist name Eshō. Sadaaki also shaved his head. Hōjō Moritoki and Hōjō Korisada became rensho (co-signatory) and shikken (regent), but Takatoki gave his approval and continued to exercise authority.

In the seventh month, Kazuhito, son of Retired Emperor Go-Fushimi, was installed as Crown Prince. Although the Emperor had many sons, the establishment of the Crown Prince was arranged by the Kantō (Kamakura shogunate), so it was not according to His will. In the ninth month, Kanazawa Sadamasa came to the capital and resided at Rokuhara. After some time, he returned to Kamakura. In the eleventh month, Minister of the Interior Saionji Sanetsune passed away. Major Counselor Fujiwara no Mototsugu was appointed to the position of Minister of the Interior.

In the first month of the second year, Regent Takatsukasa Fuyuhira passed away at the age of fifty-five. In the same month, the Zen monk Seichō came from the Yuan dynasty. He served at Kenchō-ji temple in Kamakura. Seichō is [also known as] Seisetsu. In the second month, Nijō Michihira was reappointed as regent. In the third month, Kujō Fusazane passed away at the age of thirty-eight. In the tenth month, Hōjō Korisada died.
