Through the arrangements of the warrior class, Prince Takaharu, the second son of Retired Emperor Go-Uda, was made Crown Prince. In the tenth month, the era was changed to Enkyō. In the eleventh month, Moronori resigned from the regency, and Takatsukasa Sadaijin Fuyuhira became regent.

In the second year of Enkyō, third month, Fuyuhira resigned as Sadaijin. Former Udaijin Saionji Kinhira was transferred to Sadaijin. In the sixth month, Kinhira resigned. In the eleventh month, Ichijō Saneyasu resigned as Daijō-daijin, and Ōinomikado former Naidaijin Nobitsugu was appointed as Daijō-daijin. Konoe Udaijin Iehira was transferred to Sadaijin, Nijō Naidaijin Michihira was appointed as Udaijin, and Iehira's younger brother Dainagon Tsunehira was appointed as Naidaijin.

In the third year, fourth month, Horikawa Dainagon Minamoto no Tomamori was promoted to Senior First Rank and appointed as Jun-daijin (Associate Minister). In the eleventh month, Hōjō Sadafusa died at Rokuhara. Hōjō Tokiatsu entered the capital as his replacement. In the twelfth month, Nobitsugu resigned as Daijō-daijin.
