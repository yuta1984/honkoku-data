Former Minister of the Interior Saneyoshi was appointed as Chancellor (Daijo-daijin). Former Minister of the Right Toin-in Sanetai was appointed as Minister of the Left. Major Counselor Hanayama-in Iesada was appointed as Minister of the Right. Major Counselor Ichijo Tsunetsune was appointed as Minister of the Interior. In the tenth month, through the arrangement of the Kanto (Kamakura bakufu), Nijo Michihira was removed from the position of regent, and Ichijo Tsunetsune became regent.

In the first year of Gen'o (1319), in the first month, Hojo Takatoki was appointed as Shuri-gon-daibu (Deputy Director of Palace Repairs). In the fourth month, Iesada resigned from Minister of the Right. In the sixth month, Regent Tsunetsune resigned from Minister of the Interior. Major Counselor Minamoto no Arifusa was appointed as Minister of the Right, and Major Counselor Minamoto no Michishige was appointed as Minister of the Interior. In the eighth month, Hashiko, daughter of former Chancellor Saionji Sanekane, became Empress (Chugu). Yasuko, daughter of Yasuno Chunagon Fujiwara no Kinkado, entered the palace following the Empress, and the Emperor favored her greatly, giving her the title "Sanmi no Tsubone" (Third Rank Lady-in-waiting). She later became Jungo (quasi-empress). There were many other court ladies, and numerous princes and princesses born from different mothers.
