Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva
16th Day

According to the Kōen Hōgi (Lecture Ritual), the Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva is namely Kannon (Avalokiteśvara).

Hirano Daimyōjin
16th Day

Enshrined in Kadono County, Yamashiro Province. This is the mausoleum of Emperor Nintoku. The original ground is Shō-Kannon (Ārya-avalokiteśvara).

Nāgārjuna Bodhisattva
17th Day

The thirteenth patriarch, Nāgārjuna Bodhisattva, was a descendant of brahmins from South India. He appeared seven hundred years after the Buddha's passing. On the day of his birth, he was under a tree, and because he entered the dragon palace and first attained enlightenment there, he is called Nāgārjuna (Dragon Tree). This is recorded in the Tōki (Chronicle).

Ōhiei Gongen
17th Day

This is the main shrine of the seven shrines of Sannō in Ōmi Province. It is Kunitokotachi-no-mikoto. The original ground is Shakyamuni Buddha.

Kanzeon Bodhisattva (Avalokiteśvara)
18th Day

In the commentary on the Kannon Sutra, observing the circumstances of stimulus and response is called "kan" (observation), and the realm of what is observed is called "seon" (world-sound). This is for the 100-day memorial service.

Kohiei Gongen
18th Day

The second shrine of the seven Sannō shrines in Ōmi Province. The original ground is Yakushi Nyorai.
