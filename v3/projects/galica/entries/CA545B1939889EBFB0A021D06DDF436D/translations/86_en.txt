Konda Hachiman
This is Emperor Ōjin, the son of Emperor Chūai. His mother is Empress Jingū. At the time of his birth, four white banners and four red banners descended from heaven, hence he is called Hachiman. His original ground (honji) is Amida Buddha.

Zenzai Dōji (Sudhana)
In the Avatamsaka Sutra, he is the son of the wealthy merchant Fukujō. When he first entered the womb, a seven-jeweled tower appeared in the residence, and there was a treasure vault underground. Hence he is called Zenzai Dōji. He is the first among the five hundred boy attendants of Manjushri.

Uhō Dōji (Rain Treasure Boy)
In the Age of Gods scroll, Izanagi-no-mikoto and Izanami-no-mikoto deliberated together, saying "Why do we not give birth to one who would be the ruler of our Great Eight Islands?" The deity thus born is Amaterasu Ōmikami.

Kongō Dōji (Vajra Boy)
Bajara, this is called vajra (diamond/thunderbolt).

Aka Dōji (Red Boy)
Ame-no-Oshihomimi-no-mikoto, the ancestral deity of the Ōnakatomi clan, the Imperial Grandson, Kasuga Daimyōjin. He was a supporting minister when [Ninigi] descended from heaven to Mount Takachiho in Hyūga Province.

Zenni-shi Dōji
This is an attendant (wakidachi) of Bishamonten.
