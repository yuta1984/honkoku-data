Monju Bodhisattva (Mañjuśrī)
25th Day

In the Kegon Ongi (Huayan Sound and Meaning Commentary), it says "Mañjuśrī," translated as "Myōtoku" (Wonderful Virtue), and also called "Myōkichijō" (Wonderful Auspiciousness).

Sekizan Daimyōjin
25th Day

Sekizan is called Taizan Fukun (Mount Tai Lord) in China. Now located at the western foot of Mount Hiei. The original ground is Jizō.

Yakujō Bodhisattva (Bhaiṣajyarāja)
26th Day

In the Mongu (Commentary on the Lotus Sutra), volume 10, it states this refers to King Śubhavyūha.

Takebe Daimyōjin
26th Day

Enshrined in Shiga County, Ōmi Province. The original ground is Amida.

Rushana Buddha (Vairocana)
27th Day

Here called "Jōman" (Pure and Complete) - pure because it covers all evils, complete because all virtues are perfect. This is the Sambhogakāya (reward body). Also called "Kōmyō Henshō" (Light Illuminating Everywhere), as stated in the Daichido Ron.

Mikami Daimyōjin
27th Day

Enshrined in Ōmi Province. During the Yōrō era of Empress Genshō, descended from heaven to this place. Called Japan's second sacred fire. The original ground is Senju (Thousand-Armed Kannon).
