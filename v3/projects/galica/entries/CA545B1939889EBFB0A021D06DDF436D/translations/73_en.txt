Akṣobhya Buddha
Fourth Day

The Vimalakīrti Sūtra states that there is a country named Abhirati (Wonderful Joy), and the Buddha is called Akṣobhya (Immovable). The commentary explains that "a" means "not" and "kṣobhya" means "moving."

Kehi Daimyōjin
Fourth Day

Enshrined in Tsuruga District, Echizen Province. This is Emperor Chūai. The original ground (honji) is Dainichi Nyorai (Mahāvairocana).

Maitreya Buddha
Fifth Day

Also called Ajita. The Engaku Commentary states that here it is called Jishi (Benevolent Clan). "Ji" (benevolence) is his family name and clan. His name is Ajita (Invincible). Because his virtue surpasses that of others, he is now called by his family name, Maitreya.

Keta Daimyōjin
Fifth Day

Enshrined in Hakui District, Noto Province. The original ground (honji) is Amida Buddha. According to another theory, it is Shō Kannon (Ārya Avalokiteśvara).

Twenty Thousand Lamp Light Buddhas
Sixth Day

In the records of Nanyue Huisi and Nanshan Daoxuan, it also mentions twenty thousand koṭi (billion) Buddhas. Details are annotated previously.

Kashima Daimyōjin
Sixth Day

Enshrined in Kashima District, Hitachi Province. The original ground (honji) is Eleven-faced Kannon.
