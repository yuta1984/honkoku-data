Metsu-akushu (Destroyer of Evil Realms)
The Jūō-kyō (Ten Kings Sutra) states: "Also called destroyer of evil realms," and further states "removes all evil realms." This is the honored one who, dwelling in the great compassion samadhi, destroys the three evil realms including hell.

Jōgyō Muhen-gyō (Superior Practice and Boundless Practice)
According to the Hōshō-ki, "superior practice" means practicing the supreme great dharma. "Boundless" refers to boundless merit.

Darani Bosatsu (Dharani Bodhisattva)
In the Yūyo Mandara-shō, it states this is the inner realization Avalokiteśvara. This bodhisattva especially has compassion for hell beings and guides sentient beings with karmic connections.

Jōgyō Anryū-gyō (Pure Practice and Established Practice)
"Pure practice" is so named because true suchness is pure. "Established practice" is so named because the three thousand [realms] are perfectly manifest. This refers to the four [bodhisattvas] of original transformation in the Lotus Sutra commentary.
