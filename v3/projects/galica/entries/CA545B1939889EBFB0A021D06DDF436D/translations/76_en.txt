Kokūzō Bodhisattva (Ākāśagarbha)
13th Day

According to the Vimalakirti Sutra, the wisdom treasury of true reality is like empty space. In the Ten Kings Sutra, this is the memorial Buddha for the 33rd year. According to the sutra, existing in the world of all fragrant gatherings.

Matsuo Daimyōjin
13th Day

Enshrined in Kadono County, Yamashiro Province. The shrine was established in the first year of Taihō era (701). Called Ōyamakui-no-kami. The original ground is Bibato Buddha.

Fugen Bodhisattva (Samantabhadra)
14th Day

According to the Dainichi Sutra, "Fu" means the principle of all places, "Gen" means the most wonderful principle. In the Ten Kings Sutra, this is the memorial Buddha for the 28th day (4×7 days).

Ōhara Daimyōjin
14th Day

Enshrined in Yamashiro Province. In the first year of Nin'ju era during Emperor Montoku's reign (851), the Ōharano festival was first performed. The original ground is Yakushi Nyorai.

Amida Buddha
15th Day

According to the Shōsan Jōdo Sutra, therein dwells the World-Honored One, named Immeasurable Life and Immeasurable Light. The Amida Sutra states it has been ten kalpas since achieving Buddhahood. The Lotus Sutra speaks of dwelling in the Land of Bliss, Amida Buddha's Pure Land.

Kasuga Daimyōjin
15th Day

Located at Mount Mikasa in Yamato Province. Called the Four Shrine Deities. The original ground is Shakyamuni Buddha. The first are Kashima, Katori, Ame-no-Koyane-no-mikoto, and Amaterasu Ōmikami.
