　　　　That this was the only way he knew.
Everyone agreed with the opinion of the elder:
Nothing seemed more beneficial to them all.
The difficulty was in attaching the bell.
One said: "I'm not going, I'm not so foolish;"
Another: "I couldn't do it." So that without doing anything
　　They parted. I have seen many councils
　　That were thus held in vain;
Councils not of rats, but councils of monks,
　　　　Even councils of canons.

　　　　When it comes only to deliberating,
　　　　The court abounds in counselors:
　　　　When execution is needed,
　　　　One no longer finds anyone.
