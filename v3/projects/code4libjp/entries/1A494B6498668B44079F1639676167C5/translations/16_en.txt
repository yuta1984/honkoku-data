　　　　　　　　　8. Transportation and Logistics

It goes without saying that transportation and logistics have a tremendous relationship with urban development.

Toyohashi City is strategically located on the Tokaido route and serves as the cultural and economic center of East Mikawa. The Tokaido Main Line runs through the southwestern part of the city, and Toyohashi Station has 122 train arrivals and departures per day.

There is the Nagoya Railway connecting the city with Nagoya City via Okazaki City, and the Toyokawa Railway, Taguchi Railway, and Horaiji Railway connecting the northern part of East Mikawa, which are about to link up with the Sanshin Railway, playing a crucial role as a connecting network in central Japan. Additionally, there is the Atsumi Electric Railway running through the Atsumi Peninsula, and the Toyohashi Electric Railway running through the city.

Freight traffic at Toyohashi Station in Showa 9 (1934) consisted of 89,011 tons shipped and 105,948 tons received, averaging 244 tons shipped and 290 tons received per day. The number of passengers was 796,347 boarding and 798,711 alighting, averaging 2,180 each per day. For suburban trains, there were 2,073,853 passengers boarding and 2,082,457 alighting, with freight shipments of 191,238 tons and arrivals of 246,963 tons. The movement of passengers and freight by automobile is too numerous to count.
