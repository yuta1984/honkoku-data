　　　　　　　　　3. Industry
　　　　　　　　　　　　1. Industrial Products and Their Values
　The city's annual industrial production value amounts to 26,115,748 yen, with the main products being raw silk, silk yarn, hemp sennit, writing brushes, sanitary ceramics, fishing nets, soy sauce and tamari, wooden products, soft drinks, confectionery, candy, sake, rock sugar, leather products, etc. Recently, silk textiles and reprocessed silk thread along with their processed goods are remarkably flourishing.
　Among these, the sericulture industry occupies the leading position, with 916 silk mills, 15,651 reeling basins, and 15,911 workers, with an annual production value of:
　　　　Raw silk　　　　　　　　　　　10,928,478 yen
　　　　Silk yarn　　　　　　　　　　　 4,765,317 yen
　　　　Hand-reeled silk　　　　　　　　　 651,918 yen
　　　　Waste silk　　　　　　　　　　　　 920,917 yen
　　　　　Total　　　　　　　　　　　17,266,630 yen
Cocoons transported into the city from various production areas throughout Japan amount to as much as 7,295 tons as raw materials. Particularly, silk yarn surpasses that of Joshu (the traditional center) and has become a specialty known as Toyohashi silk yarn. The city is known both domestically and internationally as one of the two major centers of Japan's silk industry, along with the Suwa region of Nagano Prefecture. In industrial areas such as the Hanada-cho district in the city, one can see factory chimneys standing like a forest, with black smoke constantly rising to the sky.
　The main industrial products are listed as follows:

　　　　Sugar　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　709,668 yen
　　　　Stationery　　　　　　　　　　　　　418,620 yen
　　　　Wooden products　　　　　　　　　　　413,894 yen
　　　　Machinery and equipment　　　　　　　　392,906 yen
　　　　Miso　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　392,033 yen
　　　　Textiles　　　　　　　　　　　　　　363,615 yen
　　　　Soy sauce, tamari　　　　　　　　　　352,929 yen
　　　　Sake　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　309,067 yen
　Regarding the distinctive products among these:
　Hemp sennit is one of the important products of the city. It is made from Manila hemp imported from the South Seas as raw material, connected and braided with silk thread, and is actively exported to European and American countries for hat-making. This is a recently developed industry
