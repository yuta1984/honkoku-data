　Writing brushes, which are included among stationery items, are also specialty products of the city with the oldest history. They were already being made as side work by ashigaru (foot soldiers) of the Yoshida Domain during the Bunka-Bunsei period, and since then have developed steadily with their reputation gradually rising. They progressed rapidly after the First Sino-Japanese War, and consequently, adapting to the trends of the times and conducting strict product inspections, an industry association was organized with the purpose of selling only high-quality products to the market, which was established in Showa 3 (1928). Now they enjoy the foremost reputation in the nation and have distinctly distinguished themselves through superior quality and low prices, with a production value of 403,420 yen.

　　　　　　　　　　　　2. Factories and Workers
　Since silk manufacturing occupies the first position in the city's industry, both the number of factories and workers tend to increase year by year. Currently there are 523 factories with 15,242 workers. Among these, there are 2,701 men compared to 12,541 women, which is a result of the many silk factories.
　There are 180 factories employing 5 or more workers, 212 with 10 or more, 63 with 30 or more, and 34 factories each with 50 or more and 100 or more workers.
　By category, the spinning industry has 383 factories with 13,757 workers, the food industry has 45 factories with 427 workers, and lumber and wooden products follow these.
　Of the 523 factories, 493 use mechanical power while 30 do not. From this, one can roughly understand the trends of modern industry.
　Labor wages in the city are generally low compared to other cities. Looking at silk factories, which constitute the majority, daily wages range from a maximum of 1 yen 5 sen, minimum of 25 sen, and typically around 60 sen.

　　　　　　　　　4. Commerce
　　　　　　　　　　　　1. Overview
　The number of commercial establishments in the city is 5,113, and the distribution of food products such as rice, vegetables, fruits, fish, meat, and seasonings, daily necessities such as textiles, industrial goods such as cocoons, raw silk, silk yarn, lumber, coal, oil, cement, copper and iron, and production materials such as fertilizer and feed is increasingly flourishing.
　The city's commercial transactions are not limited to domestic trade alone; raw silk, silk yarn, hemp sennit, fishing nets, eggs, sanitary ceramics, etc. are all exported overseas and have gained fame.
