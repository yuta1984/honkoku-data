　　　　　　　　　　　　2. Agricultural Products
Agricultural products in Shōwa 9 (1934) totaled 3,428,766 yen, with rice and wheat being the main products, followed by sweet potatoes. The main production amounts for the most recent year are as follows:

Vegetables of increasingly superior quality have come to be produced, and forced cultivation using greenhouses includes muskmelons, tomatoes, and cucumbers. This began around Meiji 40 (1907) when Mr. Nakajima Komaji operated an 8-tsubo greenhouse, and since then has made remarkable progress, currently reaching 9,143 tsubo. The products are mainly shipped to central wholesale markets in Tokyo, Osaka, and other cities, and are also actively transported to Hokkaido. The city is particularly famous as a sweet potato production area, and due to favorable climate and cultivation methods, it produces large quantities that are shipped not only to the Keihanshin region but also as far as the Sanin region.

　　　　　　　　　　　　3. Sericulture
The city is called "Sericultural Capital" and is said to be the nation's foremost in sericulture. There are 9 silkworm egg producers, with both original and regular silkworm eggs valued at 144,942 yen.
Currently there are 2,398 sericulture households, with silkworm egg hatching totaling 450,441 grams. The resulting annual production amounts to:

　　　　　　　　　　　　4. Livestock
The annual livestock production amounts to 1,695,011 yen, and the raising of livestock and poultry must never be neglected in agricultural management.
