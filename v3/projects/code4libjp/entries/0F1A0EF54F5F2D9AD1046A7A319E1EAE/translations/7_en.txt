The ecliptic is divided into twelve parts, each assigned a symbol. Each part is divided into thirty degrees.

Aries　　　Latin: Aries　　　　Dutch: Ram　[Margin note: Ram means lamb]
Taurus　　　Latin: Taurus　　　Dutch: Stier
Gemini　　　Latin: Gemini　　　Dutch: Tweelingen　[Margin note: refers to twins]
Cancer　　　Latin: Cancer　　　Dutch: Kreeft　[Margin note: Kreeft refers to crab with claws]
Leo　　　　Latin: Leo　　　　　Dutch: Leeuw
Virgo　　　Latin: Virgo　　　　Dutch: Maagd　　Virgin
Libra　　　Latin: Libra　　　　Dutch: Weegschaal　　Scales
Scorpio　　Latin: Scorpius　　Dutch: Schorpioen　　Scorpion

Sagittarius　Latin: Sagittarius　Dutch: Schutter　　Person shooting a bow

Aquarius　　Latin: Aquarius　　Dutch: Waterman　　Water bearer
Pisces　　　Latin: Pisces　　　Dutch: Vissen　　　Fish

From the two points where the ecliptic is most distant from the equator, a line is drawn from each. They are twenty-three degrees away from the equator. These two circles are called the tropics (Keerkringen, namely solsticial lines). When the sun travels along these two circles, it is at the extreme point of departure from the equator, and from there it returns again toward the equator.
