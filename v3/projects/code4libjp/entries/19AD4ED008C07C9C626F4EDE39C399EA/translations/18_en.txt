　　　　　　　　　61　Army Memorial Day　(10th day)
　During the Russo-Japanese War of 1904-1905, on March 10, 1905, the Japanese army completely crushed the Russian army in the great battle of Mukden to the point where they could not rise again, achieving a great victory and hastening the peace treaty. This is a national memorial day that all citizens can never forget.
　　　　　　　　　62　Kotohira Pilgrimage　(10th day)　Old custom
　This day is the festival of Kotohira Shrine in Tajiri, Iwata-machi, which draws many worshippers from neighboring areas, and a plant market is held.
　　　　　　　　　63　Higan (Equinox Week)　(Seven days around the spring equinox)
　The spring equinox day is called the middle day, with three days before as the beginning and three days after as the end. This one week is called Higan. People generally visit graves and make Higan dango to distribute to relatives and acquaintances.
　　　　　　64　Spring Imperial Spirit Festival　(21st or 22nd day)
　On the spring and autumn equinox days, imperial ceremonies are held at the Imperial Spirit Hall in the palace to worship the spirits of successive emperors, empresses, imperial consorts, and imperial relatives. On this day there is also a Shrine Festival at the palace shrine where the Emperor personally worships the Eight Deities as well as the heavenly and earthly deities.
　The origin of this Imperial Spirit Festival was in the spring of Meiji 4, and it became a biannual spring and autumn observance from Meiji 11.

　　　　　　　　　65　Shanichi Pilgrimage　(Shanichi day)
　Shanichi refers to the day of the earth element (tsuchinoe) closest to the spring or autumn equinox. It is said that visiting seven shrines with stone torii gates on this day prevents chronic illness, so this practice continues today. This day is said to be when various seeds should be sown and the rice field deity and earth deity worshipped. Swallows are said to arrive on this spring Shanichi.
　A. Visitors are given sweets called Tsumame (made by pinching). (Ushikawa)
　B. Volunteers visit ten shrines with stone torii gates. (Ueda)
　　　　　　　　　66　Miei-ku (Memorial Service for Kōbō Daishi)　(21st day)　Old custom
　This is the true death anniversary of Kōbō Daishi's entering meditation, and Shingon sect temples enshrine the master's portrait and hold memorial services. Recently other sects also observe this. There are countless pilgrims, especially at places with the 88-temple pilgrimage route, where an endless stream of devout men and women walk the circuit, creating crowds. On this day, the faithful provide hospitality with rice cakes, sweets, sweet rice wine, flags, etc. September 21st is similarly bustling.

　　　　　　　　　68　Mizuguchi Festival　(Late in the month)
　Mizuguchi is the inlet where water is drawn into the rice seedbed. Since rice seedlings will die if water cannot enter here, this is worshipped when rice seeds are sown.
