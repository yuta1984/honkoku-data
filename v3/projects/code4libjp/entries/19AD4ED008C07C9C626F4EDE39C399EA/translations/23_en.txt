　　　　　　　　　84　Rice Field Preparation Mochi　(Late May - Early June)
　Generally, farming families make mochi rice cakes to celebrate when rice field preparation is completed.

　　June
　　　　　　　　　85　Changing of Clothes　(1st day)
　In the past, people changed from winter clothes to lined garments from May 1st to Boys' Day, but since we now use the new calendar, on this day people change from lined garments to single-layer summer clothes.
　　　　　　　　　86　Warding Off Evil Diseases　(1st day)
　On this day, hydrangeas are hung from the eaves to ward off evil diseases.
　　　　　　　　　87　Mountain Opening　(6th day)
　On this day, Mount Ōmine is worshipped. A hōin (Shingon Buddhist priest) makes sacred wands, burns sacred fire (goma), and performs day-waiting rituals. (Tsuda)
　　　　　　　　　88　Time Memorial Day　(10th day)
　When Emperor Tenji was crown prince, he first created something called a water clock (rōkoku) and placed it at the new astronomical observatory, striking drums and bells to inform the people of the time. This was the beginning in our country, and since this corresponds to June 10th, this day has been designated as "Time Memorial Day," with efforts focused on spreading awareness of time concepts.
