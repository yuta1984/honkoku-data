　　　Finance
　The municipal assets of Toyohashi City are classified into land, buildings, ferry boats, securities, and cash, but the amounts of cash, securities, and ferry boats are extremely small.
　The city's revenue and expenditure budget for Showa 14 (1939) was 1,778,318 yen, which represents a decrease of 144,477 yen compared to the previous year's total budget. Needless to say, the main source of revenue is municipal taxes, which account for more than 40%. When examining recent municipal tax revenues: Showa 11 (1936) was 844,587 yen, Showa 12 (1937) was 842,966 yen, Showa 13 (1938) was 823,354 yen, Showa 14 (1939) was 833,013 yen, and the Showa 15 (1940) budget is 792,379 yen. Breaking this down in detail:
　　Land tax surcharge　　　　　71,438 yen
　　Special land tax surcharge　　6,981 yen
　　Business income tax surcharge　120,152 yen
　　Income tax surcharge　　　　　30,928 yen
　　House tax surcharge　　　　　398,790 yen
　　Business tax surcharge　　　　16,500 yen
　　Miscellaneous tax surcharge　　147,590 yen

These are the proportions. Since the survey at the end of Showa 14 showed that Toyohashi City had 27,499 households with a population of 147,831 people, the tax burden for Showa 14 amounts to 28 yen and 81 sen per household, or an average of 5 yen and 36 sen per person.
　Next, regarding major expenditures, the regular section includes elementary school expenses of 414,330 yen and municipal office expenses of 173,349 yen as the largest items, while the extraordinary section includes public bond expenses of 449,308 yen, elementary school maintenance expenses of 80,373 yen, and various expenses related to the current incident of 30,363 yen.
