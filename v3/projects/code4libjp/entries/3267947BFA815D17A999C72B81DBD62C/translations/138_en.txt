○Toyokawa River　　●In the Wamyō-shō under the section on Hōi District, Toyokawa (Toyokawa)
　　　　　●The Kokumei Fudoki (Volume 1, folio 12) states: Regarding Toyokawa, there was a wealthy man of Ichisaka, and if you look at this,
　　he built his residence upstream of this river. The houses flourished for twenty ri, and because the homes of those people prospered abundantly,
　　that waterway was called Toyokawa (Abundant River).
　●The Meisho Hōgaku-shō (folio 59) states: Toyokawa is north of what is commonly called the Imabashi post station. It is close to a place
　　called Hoshino and such. The north of Mikawa is continuous mountains. From Imabashi post station one goes to Takashi Plain. There is a great mountain to the north,
　　and at its foot is the Toyokawa River.　●Moshiogusa (Volume 5, folio 7) Mikawa dō (Volume 6, folio 20) Toyokawa-gō (Mikawa)
