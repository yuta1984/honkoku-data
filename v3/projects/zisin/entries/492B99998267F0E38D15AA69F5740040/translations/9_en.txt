【Right Page】
When the sun tilts toward the western mountains and the evening bell pierces the ears, countless tens of thousands of items of every description—some illuminated by candles, others by lanterns—create an especially lively night market scene. Not to mention the lights from peep shows and lottery stalls, the crowds of worshippers illuminate with their lanterns, and the flames reflect and shine in the sky, making it brighter than clear daylight, indeed surpassing it. The people coming and going astound the eyes and ears, running west and walking east, with even north and south unclear in such bustling activity that has no equal.

【Left Page】
According to ancient custom, on the ninth day of the third month at the first hour of the monkey (around 3-5 PM), there is a procession departing from the main gate of the head temple. A beautiful procession is arranged from there to the main hall, with the portable shrine of the standing Buddha image and the brocade curtains around the portable shrine of the sacred seal gleaming brightly in a ceremonial parade. Guard monks and various officials protect the front, back, left and right, and when the signal bell is struck, all the monks of the mountain come out to the edge of the temple grounds to welcome and escort it, respectfully transferring it to the main hall. Flower offerings and ritual items are prepared...
