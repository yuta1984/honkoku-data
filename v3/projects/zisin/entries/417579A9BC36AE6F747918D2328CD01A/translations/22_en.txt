【Right page】
Without letting time pass or allowing any intervals, the earth shook incessantly. On the first day of the fifth month, there were fifty-six tremors during the day, and forty-seven more during the night. At the beginning of the shaking, it was still relatively mild, but with such violent trembling, people worried about what kind of major earthquake might come and cause houses to collapse and be crushed. The great Keichō earthquake of old also split the earth and mud gushed up, and in even more ancient times, fires broke out and many people died. People wondered what might happen after this great earthquake, clasping their hands and lifting their feet off the ground, unable to remain calm whether awake or asleep, unable to bear standing or sitting, and every time the shaking began, people in every house raised their voices to announce the time.

【Left page】
Young children cried and screamed. The depths of the earth rumbled thunderously, and with all the commotion and uproar throughout Kyoto, no other sounds could be heard. In any case, all the townhouses would surely be shaken to pieces. Saying that life was most precious, people of all social ranks - high and low - set up shelters in front of temple halls and in graveyards, or in town squares and at crossroads, laying door planks on the ground, binding bamboo poles with rope, and stretching treated paper and rain cloaks overhead. The various temples and shrines of Kyoto were also overflowing with people beyond capacity.
