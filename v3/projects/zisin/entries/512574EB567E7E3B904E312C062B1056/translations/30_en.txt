Jishō 1 / An'gen 3
○Gyokuyō　Jishō 1 (An'gen 3), 10th month, 27th day, mizunoto-mi, around the hour of the ox
　There was a great earthquake. It is said there has been no such earthquake since the Hōen era. The great bell of Tōdai-ji was shaken and fell. Also, some of the spiral curls (rahotsu) of the Great Buddha fell.

○Hyakurenshō　Emperor Takakura
　Jishō 1, 10th month, 27th day, hour of the ox - Great earthquake
　Tōdai-ji reported that during the earthquake, two spiral curls of the Great Buddha fell before the Kannon statue, and spiral curls from the crown also came loose. The great bell's suspension cord broke and it fell to the ground. Also, the northeast corner of the sutra repository collapsed. This strange phenomenon occurred due to the sinful rebellion of Binkaku.

○Akihiro-ō-ki　An'gen 3, 11th month, 24th day - Offerings to the Nine Shrines
　Dispatched from the Jingi-kan (Department of Divinities), with Senior Noble Sanesada as chief, and Gon-u-chūben Chikamuneshin as secretary. These are shrines in the direction that caused the curse from the previous earthquake. The Ise messenger is Kaneyasu. Nakatomi Naganaka, Inbe Tomohira, Urabe Kanesada. For Hachiman shrine, Gon-chūnagon Masayori; for Kamo, Yorisada; for Matsuo, Sadakiyo; for Hirano, Udaiben Nagakata, etc.
