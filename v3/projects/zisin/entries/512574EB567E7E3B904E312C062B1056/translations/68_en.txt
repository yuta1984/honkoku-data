In the Kesei En'gi, Kong Mo states: "When yang energy lies dormant beneath yin and feels pressed by yin, unable to rise, it leads to earth movement." Thus, when steam lies hidden underground and tries to emerge, it is suppressed by yin energy and cannot escape, causing violent clashing and oscillation within the earth. Similarly, Bo Tang-fu's words in the Zhou Yu section of the Guo Yu, and others from ancient times, all expound this theory.

It has holes all around that connect with each other, like a bee's nest or like fungal valves, with water and fire qi lurking within.
