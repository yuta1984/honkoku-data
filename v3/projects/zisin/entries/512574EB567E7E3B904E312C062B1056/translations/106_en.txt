Additionally, Kawarazaki and one wing of Yoko-daimon belonged to the outcasts. At that time, the temporary shrine of the Honored Nyorai was located in the fields called Horikiri in the northeast direction, guarded by the head administrator Daikanjin of the three temples and related officials, and the exhibition proceeded without incident.

- Deaths by crushing in the temple precincts: 8 resident monks (breakdown: 3 among the senior clergy, 5 among the middle clergy)
- Deaths by crushing in the city: over 2,000 people

【Left page】
- Along the Hokuriku road from Zenkoji, places destroyed by earthquake: Yokoyama completely destroyed; Ainoki village destroyed with dozens of houses burned; Oshigane village and Yoshida village mostly destroyed. Villages and post towns destroyed all the way to Sekikawa post town in Echigo province. The distance from Zenkoji to Sekikawa is about 10 ri. Along this route, near a place called Yoshimura, mountains collapsed and residents were buried in the earth. Echigo Takada was half-destroyed. Ima-machi suffered four earthquakes starting from the 24th day of the third month, was completely destroyed, and then on the 29th of the same month was completely burned by strong winds and great fire.

- From Zenkoji to Iiyama is 8 ri. Along this highway, Sansai village, Ishi village, and the remaining villages were half-destroyed. Among the villages, rice fields and farmland suffered water damage from flooding.
