○Same year　Lord Date Tōtōmi-no-kami was granted 100,000 koku with an additional 30,000 koku of new rice fields.

○Same year　The monk Senpaku of Ōbaku-san had an audience with the shogun upon visiting the castle, and was granted 100 pieces of silver. Five people - his disciples Raishō, Sekimon, Ten'yū, and the retired Unzan of Ushigome Ryūkō-ji - held a dialogue in the shogun's presence.

○Same year, September　During a severe windstorm and rain, Lord Matsudaira Aki-no-kami's residence collapsed. The residences of castle monks Noguchi Genki and Okazaki Junsa were destroyed. Genki's mother and wife and children, along with Junsa, were crushed, but Junsa did not die while four of his wife and children perished. There were also deaths and injuries among the townspeople in the neighborhood.

○Same year, September　Lord Kuroda Buzen-no-kami was granted an increase of 1,500 koku to his existing 5,500 hyō, making his domain 7,000 koku total.

○Same year　Regarding a duck that fell after being struck by an arrow outside Koishikawa Gate, there was an investigation that became increasingly complicated, involving the imprisonment of guards from the Mito domain.

1. Genroku 10 (1697), 2nd month　At the residence of Hiyoshi Daizen, head priest of Sannō Shrine, Lord Abe Shima-no-kami's unit captain Fukushima Iori came and killed Daizen. Daizen died instantly from eleven wounds, while Lord Iori sustained six wounds but did not die. He told Daizen's retainers that he had gone mad and asked them to stop him, so the retainers tried to stop him. The reason is said to be unclear.

○Same year　The creation of maps of all provinces was ordered. ○Same year　Sake transportation tax was ordered <<However, in Shōtoku 6 (1716), the tax was exempted and subsequently discontinued>>.

○Same year　Lord Ogasawara Sado-no-kami received an increase of 10,000 koku; from April, Lord Matsudaira Kii-no-kami became Kyoto shoshidai.

○Same year, June　Domain transfers: <<From Uzen Kaminoyama to Mino Gujō>> Lord Kanamori Izumo-no-kami, <<With 100,000 koku to Utsunomiya>> Lord Abe Tsushima-no-kami, <<To Tajima Izushi>> Lord Matsudaira Iga-no-kami, <<To Tango Miyazu>> Lord Okudaira Kumataró.

○Same year　Officials appointed to oversee the creation of maps of all provinces: <<Jisha-bugyō>> Lord Nagai Iga-no-kami, <<Ōmetsuke>> Lord Sengoku Hōki-no-kami, <<Machi-bugyō>> Lord Kawaguchi Settsu-no-kami, <<Okanjō-gashira>> Lord Ido Tsushima-no-kami.

○Same year, April 12　The shogun visited the Kishū lord. On this occasion, 30,000 koku each were granted to Kuranosuke-sama and Chikara-no-kami-sama.

○Same year, June 20　Due to the death of Lord Mori Mimasaku-no-kami, his requested adopted son Shikibu came down from his domain, but went mad at Kuwana during the journey. Consequently, Mimasaku Province was confiscated, and Lord Mori Naiki was newly granted 20,000 koku.
