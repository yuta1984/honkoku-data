Same year, 5th month: Regarding the dispute between the Gyōnin faction and the Hijiri faction at Mount Kōya, an investigation was conducted and the matter was settled. A directive bearing the joint seals of the Senior Councilors was issued to both parties, placing the Gyōnin faction under the authority of the Hijiri faction.

Same year, 5th month, 17th day: A vendetta killing occurred at the foot of Tsukudo Myōjin slope. The victim was Sawaoka Tōemon (age 53). Tōemon had previously served Lord Hōjō Dewa-no-kami as a low-ranking stipend holder, but later became a masterless samurai. The killer was Furusawa Chūjirō (age 23). Furusawa Heizaemon had served Lord Honda Etchū-no-kami as a low-ranking stipend holder but later became a rōnin and lived in Yamashina village in Enshū Province. Heizaemon's wife's younger brother was Furusawa Ichizaemon (around 50 years old). Six years earlier, on the night of the 16th day of the 3rd month in the year of the Boar, Tōemon had conducted a night attack on Heizaemon's residence, killing Heizaemon and his wife, their eldest son Yahei, and two servants before withdrawing. At the foot of Tsukudo Myōjin slope, when both sides confronted each other and tried to restrain the situation, local residents intervened to stop them. However, declaring this was a vendetta to be recorded in the government registers, [the avengers] withdrew toward the hatamoto residences.

Same year, 6th month, 19th day: It was reported that the chigi (ornamental roof finials) of the main hall of Ise Outer Shrine had fallen over. First, [the sacred objects] were moved to an auxiliary building, after which repairs were to be made and everything restored to its former state according to imperial will, as conveyed by the court liaison officials to the imperial audience. It was decreed that all matters should not proceed according to former precedents.

Same year, 7th month: The length measurements for one tan of silk, pongee, cotton, and cloth were decreed. Silk and pongee: one tan = 3 jō 4 shaku (width: 1 shaku 4 sun). Cloth and cotton: one tan = 3 jō 4 shaku (width: 1 shaku 3 sun). These are calculations using carpenters' curved rulers.

1. Kanbun 4 (1664), 6th month: From Lord Uesugi Harima-no-kami's former domain of 300,000 koku, the Yonezawa domain of 150,000 koku was granted to his adopted son Saburō, representing a reduction. This Saburō was the son of Lord Kira Kōzuke-no-suke. This matter was communicated to Lords Kira Wakasa-no-kami and Kōzuke-no-suke, as well as to the Banshū domain's chief retainers (Chūjō Echizen, Chisaka Hyōbu) and other Banshū chief retainers (Fukamatsu Iemon, Yasuda Hyōgo).

1. Kanbun 5 (1665): Okutsu Myōkakuji and Zōshigaya Hōmyōji temples were placed under the custody of Lord Kyōgoku Hyakusuke for establishing the Fuju-fuse sect. This custody was decided after deliberation. (Same year, 12th month, 27th day: Great earthquake in Takada, Echigo Province)

Same year, 1st month, 2nd and 3rd days: Severe thunderstorms in Osaka. On the 2nd day at the hour of the Dog, lightning struck the first story of the castle keep, and the remaining structure burned down. On the 6th day, this was reported to Edo.
