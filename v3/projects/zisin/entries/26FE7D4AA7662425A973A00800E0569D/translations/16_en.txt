1. Kanbun 6 (1666): In Nakamura domain, Hata District, Tosa Province, there were three major floods with heavy rains on the 4th, 11th, and 15th days of the 7th month. The damage included 2,000 houses, 37 human deaths, and 579 cattle and horses.

Same year, 1st day of 8th month: Mount Hebiyama in Tajima Province experienced seismic activity, creating a fissure 3 ken wide and 56 ken long of unknown depth. Nineteen houses collapsed and over ten people died. Since it occurred at night and was not visible, people wondered if snakes had emerged.

Same year: A house with an 18-ken ridge and 28 ken in length, with pillars of 1 shaku 4 sun square, drifted to Mera Bay in Bōshū Province. It arrived undamaged, and this was reported to the Finance Office.

Same year, 12th month, 8th day: Great fire in Osaka. It was extinguished at the fourth hour on the 9th day. (The fire source was in Keiseichō, this village, at Itoya of Junsei) 9,933 townhouses burned down. The castle governor was Lord Aoyama Inaba-no-kami.

1. Kanbun 6, 2nd month, 6th day: Lady Tenjuin passed away.

Same year: A rōnin named Yamaga Jinzaemon created and circulated a book called "Seikyō Yōroku" (Essentials of Sacred Teachings). The government took issue with this, and he was placed under the custody of his lord, Asano Takumi-no-kami.

Same year: Strange birds appeared in the mountains of Kishū domain. They were difficult to capture, so poison bait was scattered to kill them. The mountain birds had characters like the following on their chests: "揜抬揜抱" (said to be possibly a floating rumor).

Same year: About 200 Ainu people came attacking Matsumae. The Matsumae people prepared defenses and killed about 100 of them, capturing one leader alive. There were also casualties among the Matsumae people.

Same year, 1st month, 14th day: Tōdaiji Temple in Nanto (Nara) caught fire. The fire started from inside the Nigatsudō hall, and the main hall burned down.

1. Kanbun 7 (1667): The reconstruction of Izumo Grand Shrine was completed.

Same year, 6th month, 22nd day: Lightning struck seven locations in the domain of Lord Mizuno Kenmotsu in Okazaki, Mikawa Province. At one location where lightning struck, there was a round object shaped like a stone weighing 670 monme.

Same year, 9th month: A sword dedicated to the Outer Shrine in Ise Province went missing. There was a strict investigation, and it was found that former chief official Higaki Yadayū and sword polisher Ihei had stolen it. Ihei fled, and Yadayū was captured at Toba Bay.

Same year, intercalary 2nd month: Kongō Dayū performed a benefit performance of "Kanjinchō" at Honjo. Various lords had box seats, and the Senior Councilors attended as spectators.
