Because their father Sahei was improper, custody was decreed, and therefore his sons were also placed in custody.

○Same year, 2nd month, 12th day: The New Imperial Palace, granted the posthumous title Go-Sai-in, passed away.

○Same year, 2nd month, 15th day, latter part of the hour of the rooster: A great meteor crossed from east to west. Its size was like a dipper, and its trail left a white

pathway of qi in the middle sky that was visible for a short time. Its brilliance fell upon the faces of observers with trembling, and it was seen simultaneously throughout all provinces. It is said that such a thing has not been heard of for a hundred years.

○Same year: Lord Kanamori Tatewaki of the pages of the inner palace, while stationed in Sunpu, was found to have falsified documents during investigations of suspicious writings, and had various other improper conducts. Furthermore, his manner of killing a retainer on the road was improper and unbefitting a samurai, therefore he was sentenced to death and his three children were ordered to commit seppuku. Regarding this matter, <<annotation: page of inner palace |>> Lord Kurokawa Dewa-no-kami, <<annotation: same |>> Lord Isshiki Kichibei, <<annotation: same group leader |>> Lord Inaba Kazuma, having improper persons during their stationed service and failing to report written apologies, were deemed improper, therefore all three were placed in custody. More than fifty members of their group were ordered into house confinement.

○Same year: Exile to Hachijōjima: Shinagawa Chūshō-in
This was exile due to devising evil deeds. The court lady called Yamazaki, who served Princess Tsuru, was mediated by Chūshō-in, resulting in banishment. Yamazaki's son, called Yamazaki Daisuke, who had been summoned in previous years, had his stipend withdrawn and was banished this time. The aforementioned Chūshō-in is said to be the younger brother of Lord Ogawa Bōjō Dainagon.
