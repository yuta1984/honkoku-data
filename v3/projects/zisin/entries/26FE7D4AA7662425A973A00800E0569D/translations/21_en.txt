Same year [5th month]: Military strategists and masterless samurai (Shinozaki Shinnosuke and Ishikawa Chobei) - both men were formerly retainers of Lord Hori Mimasaka-no-kami and now live as ronin in Azabu. At Shinnosuke's residence, the two men quarreled, and Chobei killed Shinnosuke and fled. Shinnosuke's disciple Watanabe Shichinosuke (18 years old) pursued and killed Chobei on the spot. Shinnosuke was a grand-disciple of Obata Kanbei, a military strategist and master craftsman who had carefully made over 2,000 warrior dolls and various military implements.

Same year, 8th [9th] month: Five Dutch ships and nineteen Chinese ships entered Nagasaki port, bringing large quantities of silk thread, cotton goods, scrolls, and rapeseed.

Same year, 9th month: In Lord Hosokawa Etchū-no-kami's domain in Higo Province, Mashiki District, peasants from Butsubara Village, Shimōno Village, Yasunaga Village, and Takatsuki Village gathered at the home of one Shobei from Butsubara Village and plotted evil deeds and spread false rumors. As punishment, they were sentenced to beheading and gibbet, males to execution, and females to slavery.

1. Enpo 3 (1675), Rabbit year, around 6th month: A report stated that southeast of Hachijojima there was an uninhabited island with rare trees and birds. Since a Kishu merchant ship had previously drifted to this island and reported on it, last year in the 5th month a Chinese-style ship was prepared, sailed to the island, and returned this day, bringing back rare items from the island to present.

Same year, 4th month: Yamaga Jingoemon, who had been placed under the custody of Lord Asano Mataichi, was pardoned this time.

Same year, 5th [9th] month: Floods in Aki Province. Same year: Famine in various provinces. In Yamato Province, Yoshino and nearby areas, 18,600 starving people received relief rice, one day's portion (3 go for men, 2 go for women) distributed from the deputy's granary. In Settsu and Kawachi provinces, 30,600 starving people similarly received relief rice.

Same year, 11th month, 25th day: Great fire in Kyoto. Fire broke out from Aburakoji Ichijo sagaru-machi, burning the temporary Imperial Palace, temporary New Retired Emperor's Palace, main Imperial residence, Konoe Palace, Hachijo Palace, and many other court nobles' residences and townspeople's areas.

Same year, 12th month: Lord Hoshina Higo-no-kami (at this time Chikuzen-no-kami) presented the following books: One volume of "Aizu Fudoki" (complete), one volume of "Dō Jinja-shi," one volume of "Gyokukō Furoku," one volume of "Nitei Chikyōroku," one volume of "Iraku Sanshi Denshin-roku."

Same year, 6th month, 2nd day: A great earthquake struck the domain of Lord Kamei Noto-no-kami in Iwami Province, Tsuwano. The main bailey, second bailey, third bailey, retainers' quarters, town, and countryside all collapsed completely, with numerous deaths extending about 8 ri to the Chōshū border.

Same year, during summer and autumn: Floods caused crop damage in the provinces of Bizen, Kawachi, Izumi, Ōmi, Yamato, Harima, and Aki.
