Moreover, fires broke out and everything was completely burned down. There were enormous numbers of instant deaths among travelers and others, but the exact numbers are unknown. According to rumors, there were 2,000 to 3,000 instant deaths. It is believed that the other post stations and villages that were burned down naturally had many deaths as well.

Item: Regarding the Saigawa (Sai River) in Shinshū, the water flow stopped from the time of the great earthquake on the night of the 24th, and there was no flowing water at all until we departed from that area around midday on the 26th. This was because bridges were thrown up from around Tanbajima-juku upstream for seven to eight ri to the bridge on the Mino road, and landslides occurred in the areas before and after, blocking the Sai River.

【Left page】
It was impossible to predict when and where flooding might occur, and naturally those along the river, including the paper manufacturers, gradually fled. Due to this, officials were dispatched from Ueda, Matsushiro and other places to the highways, and travel toward Zenkōji was prohibited. According to rumors, the backed-up water of the Sai River formed a lake-like body near Matsumoto in Shinshū, the water pushed open toward a place called Kikyōgahara, and from there would flow into Lake Suwa and then into the Amanogawa River. However, most believe that if sufficient water accumulates in the collapsed areas, it will break through and cause a sudden great flood.
