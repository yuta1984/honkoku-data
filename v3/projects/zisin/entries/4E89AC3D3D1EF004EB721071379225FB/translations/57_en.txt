Prior report regarding land changes in Tawarano Village and three other villages in Tango Province
　Total rice fields and dry fields: 1 chō 5 tan 3 se and more
　Breakdown: Rice fields 1 chō and more experienced ground elevation
　　　　　Rice fields and dry fields 5 tan 3 se and more suffered sand intrusion

The above concerns villages near the coast of Tawarano Village and three other villages in Takeno District, Tanshū, which are under my administration as deputy governor. Between the night of the 15th day of the first month of this year and the 16th day, rice fields totaling more than 1 chō rose in elevation by about 8 shaku or 1 jō, with irrigation channels and riverbed areas all suddenly experiencing ground elevation. Additionally, rice fields and dry fields totaling more than 5 tan 3 se suffered sand intrusion, creating damaged and collapsed land. I hereby report this matter. While I will provide detailed information later, I first submit this report.

　Second month of the Hitsuji year　　　　　　Okazaki Kenzaburō

Within my domain, Kizu Shinden in Takeno District, Tango Province
　Rice fields: 8 tan 9 se 7 bu　　　Wada Ueno portion
　Rice fields: 5 tan 2 se 24 bu　　　Nakadate portion
The above is a village near the coast. On the night of the 12th day of the first month of this year, sand dunes suddenly subsided, causing land changes in the rice field areas. The combined rice field area of the aforementioned two villages, totaling 4 tan 2 bu, experienced ground elevation ranging from about 1 shaku to 1 jō, creating wasteland equivalent to a yield of 17 koku 9 to 7 shō 2 gō. This has been reported to me by retainers from the local area, so I hereby submit this report. The above.
