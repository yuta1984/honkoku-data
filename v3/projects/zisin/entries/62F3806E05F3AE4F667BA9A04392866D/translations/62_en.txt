○Since the Genroku kinoto-hitsuji year (1703) and after, the high and low classes in Edo have not experienced severe earthquakes for a long time, so while they constantly fear fire disasters, their fear of earthquakes has weakened. 《Note: Regarding the Genroku earthquake, the outline is recorded in Elder Hakuseki's "Oritaku Shiba no Ki," Shōhō Jiryoku, Wakan Gōun, and Bukō Nenpyō, but not in detail. Other records occasionally seen are only used as topics for daily conversation, with no one truly fearing or worrying about them》

Therefore, they rely on storehouses, building many of them not only for their own homes but also for neighboring houses as a key measure for fire prevention. Building shop-storehouses or warehouses at entrances is particularly common in the commercial districts.

The magistrate's office also issued orders recently circulated throughout the neighborhoods to rebuild board-roofed buildings into earth-walled storehouse-style plastered houses to avoid the danger of fire spread. However, during this recent earthquake, not only the shop-storehouses but also those at entrances caused wall earth to crumble and fall when people tried to escape, injuring their bodies, sometimes causing loss of life, and furthermore extending the disaster to adjacent walls and neighboring houses.
