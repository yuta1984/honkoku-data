Last year's great earthquake on the 2nd day of the 10th month caused countless people to lose their lives or livelihoods, and the world thought it a terrifying event. However, this year on the 25th day of the 8th month of Ansei 3 (1856), there was an unprecedented great wind and rain storm that toppled houses, and storm surges flooded the seashore, submerging houses and causing loss of life. A general account of these events is recorded below.

○On the 23rd day of the 8th month there was light rain; on the 24th and 25th days light rain continued, becoming increasingly heavy from evening onward. The great southern wind became particularly severe from around the 7th-8th points of the hour of the dog (around 8-10 PM), and gradually subsided after the hour of the ox at dawn (2-4 AM). At the beginning there was thunder, which soon ceased, but during this wind and rain there were also earthquakes. Some people did not notice them because they were confused with the swaying of houses caused by the strong wind. From the morning of the following 26th day, it cleared up.
