"Tōkaidō Earthquake and Tsunami: Tales for Future Generations"

【Upper section, from left】
Mariko
After the great earthquake, fires broke out and could not be prevented, resulting in complete burning.

Fuchū
The earthquake caused houses to collapse, fires started from Egawa-chō and burned about 30% of the area.

Ejiri
Similarly, fires broke out and completely burned the area. Many deaths and injuries.

Okitsu
After the earthquake, the tsunami destroyed more than half, with many deaths and injuries.

Yui
Though the earthquake caused much damage to houses, it was safe overall.

Kambara
The earthquake caused houses to collapse and fires to break out. Half remained after the burning, but a great tsunami swept in and pulled all the remaining houses into the sea.

Yoshiwara
After the earthquake, fires broke out and could not be prevented, resulting in complete burning.

【Lower section, from left】
Hara, Numazu, Mishima
These three post towns experienced very strong earthquakes but were safe.

Hakone
The earthquake was severe, three honjin collapsed, the mountains were greatly disturbed, and travel was suspended.

Odawara
The earthquake was strong, some houses collapsed, and there were quite a few damaged buildings.

Ōiso, Hiratsuka, Fujisawa, Totsuka, Hodogaya, Kanagawa, Kawasaki, Shinagawa
All seven of these post towns had slight differences, but generally the same as Odawara.

Edo
On November 5th, the earthquake affected many daimyo residences and townhouses. About ten chō of Saruwaka-chō caught fire, and all three theaters burned down, but there were no injuries.
