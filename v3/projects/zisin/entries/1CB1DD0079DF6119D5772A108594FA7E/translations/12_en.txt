If it proves difficult, that matter should be reported immediately. Also, I pray that the calligraphy plaque by His Lordship Nijō, which was given to us by my elder brother in response to our earnest entreaties over the years, is a precious item. The hanging scroll also in his own hand, the Six Immortal Poets plaque written by court nobles - all of these are items of the same importance, so I hope somehow they will remain safe. Take care not to get injured, and certainly do not approach danger. If there is anything you are uncertain about, turn back and cease your actions.

I have been gravely ill for the past four or five years, with my belly in such turmoil, chest pains and heart troubles, and severe blood rushing to my head, so tonight my fragile life is uncertain. O-Jun should support her mother with her heart and combine their strength so as not to cause worry, and I entrust Kenzō's upbringing to you. Also, if there should be a fortunate marriage match, women should always make prudence and discretion their constant practice. Speaking in a rough manner is unpleasant to hear - one should regard one's husband as if he were heaven itself. As I have always said, women face the seven grounds for divorce. What inadequate talk I spend time on - Kenzō is still young, but "the soul at three lasts until one hundred" as they say. Revere your mother, and for males, learning and reading should come first.
