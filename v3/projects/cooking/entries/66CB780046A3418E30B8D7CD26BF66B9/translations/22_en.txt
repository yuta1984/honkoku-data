▲About the wood for chopsticks
Plum wood for spring, red wood for summer
Boxwood for autumn, black persimmon for winter
These are about the carp chopsticks of the four seasons
For ordinary carp chopsticks, any wood
is acceptable. For four-season carp
even exotic wood is acceptable, understand this meaning.

▲About striking cuts (uchimi)
On the 21st day of the 7th month of Tenpyō 7 (735 CE), Minister Tachibana no Moroe, during a great banquet, regarding the carp that was prepared, the Sadaiben (Left Major Controller) said with his shaku (ceremonial baton) "Make this part and present it"
From this it was named "uchimi" and has been passed down, according to one theory.

▲About cutting two-soul carp
Do not cut ryōmi-oroshi (filleting both sides) or katami-oroshi (filleting one side).

[Header Left]
Other than that, anything may be cut. However, cut one at a time.
Place the first one at the ceremonial seat, place the later one in front. When using the front one, push aside the one at the ceremonial seat and make the later one the main cut. The first carp may be broken up without problem. Similarly, the front one should remain undisturbed. This is because when someone who doesn't know knife work requests carp, or when someone equally knowledgeable requests it, since two carp should not be cut, the two-soul carp was included in the Shijō diary. Since this is crucial to our school, it is kept secret in this manner.
