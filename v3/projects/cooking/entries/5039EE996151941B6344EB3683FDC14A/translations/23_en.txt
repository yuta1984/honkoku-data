　　Knead the above very well, put in a mortar, press down and let rest. In hot weather, about one hour; in cold weather, let rest for about two hours. After that, pound gently as if pounding mochi, and it will become fu (wheat gluten). After that, knead and wash many times in water to remove the dregs. Remove small bits of dregs with a thin blade, and tear into well-formed pieces. Then boil in hot water

【Left Page】
　　How to boil: heat water so it doesn't come to a rolling boil, put the fu in it, stir many times with a ladle, and when the water starts to boil, add cold water several times to keep it from boiling hard - this works well. The fu is properly cooked when it all floats to the surface. In general, whether kneading or washing the fu, still river water works best.
