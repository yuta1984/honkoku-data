18. How to make mizore daikon (grated daikon radish)
First, peel the outer skin and slice thinly, about half the thickness of regular rounds.
Then sprinkle high-quality kudzu powder over the daikon, and on the cutting board, gently tap with a pestle.
The kudzu powder will stick to both the top and bottom of the daikon. However, it is good to
coat the daikon with sake first. Then bring water to a boil and keep it boiling,
then add the daikon. It is best to keep the pot lid open.
Then scoop up what has boiled up and cool it in water.

Then cut into small cubes or tear apart for use. For clear soup, thin
kudzu soup, clear broth, red miso or sashimi, namasu (vinegared salad), small side dishes,
garnish for prepared fish, rice bowl dishes, sweets bowls, and anything else
will do well.
