Raw Vinegar (Kisu)
　　For any fish or ingredients, mix vinegar with salt and marinate,
　　then squeeze out that vinegar and pour fresh vinegar over - this is what it refers to

Blended Vinegar (Awasesu)
　　Mix soy sauce and vinegar in equal parts, simmer
　　and cool before using

Three-Cup Vinegar (Sanbaisu)
　　One cup vinegar, one cup sake, one cup soy sauce - mix together and use
　　Also, simmering the above and cooling before use is even better

Seven-Cup Vinegar (Shichihaisu)
　　One cup vinegar, one and a half cups sake, add seven parts Akō roasted salt
　　and use. May also be simmered

White Vinegar (Shirasu)
　　Squeeze water well from tofu, roast white sesame well and
　　grind together, thin with vinegar, and strain through a water bag

Green Vinegar (Aozu)
　　Boil spinach and squeeze out water well, grind thoroughly
　　and thin with vinegar, strain through a water bag

Black Vinegar (Kurozu)
　　Toast kelp and let cool, rub well and
　　grind finely in a mortar, add to vinegar

Water Pepper Vinegar (Tadesu)
　　Rub water pepper with vinegar to extract, discard the husks and use
　　Also good to remove the stems from water pepper leaves and grind them in

Sugar Vinegar (Satōsu)
　　Add about 2 monme of white sugar to 1 gō of vinegar, simmer well
　　and strain through silk cloth before using

Mandarin Vinegar (Mikansu)
　　Squeeze juice from Kii Province mandarins, combine with sugar and
　　salt, simmer and strain through silk cloth

Heated Vinegar (Yakisu)
　　This is for urgent situations. Pour vinegar
　　into a tea bowl, add charcoal fire and strain
