Korean-style Simmering　Heat salt in a pot, put whole medium sea bream with hot water and aged sake
　　　　　　　　　　　enough to cover, simmer until alcohol evaporates, add rice cooking water
　　　　　　　　　　　reduce heat and season. Any ingredients can be added together
　　　　　　　　　　　Matsutake mushrooms, bamboo shoots, or leeks

Skin Sautéing　　　　Sauté goose or duck skin in a pot, add bonito flakes and bones
　　　　　　　　　　simmer and add a little raw sauce for seasoning
　　　　　　　　　　However, can also be made without bones. Accompaniments are water dropwort
　　　　　　　　　　matsutake mushrooms, burdock root and such

Green Liver　　　　　Mince pheasant innards, add a little regular miso to pot
　　　　　　　　　　sauté until fox-colored, add dashi and set aside
　　　　　　　　　　to use as soup

　　　　　　　　　　Any seasonal ingredients work well for additions
　　　　　　　　　　　　However, green miso is only for springtime

Suruga Simmering　　Simmer sea bream with bonito flakes, soy sauce, sake and vinegar added
　　　　　　　　　　Also done with octopus in the same way, called black simmering

Sororiko　　　　　　Beat dried fish roe well in hot water, mince small birds
　　　　　　　　　　add mountain yam and simmer

Salmon Roe Sautéing　Prepare salmon meat, make ground paste from roe and set aside
　　　　　　　　　　When the meat simmered with bonito and soy sauce comes to boil
　　　　　　　　　　stir in the ground roe and fish balls and simmer
　　　　　　　　　　　　Innards and liver can also be added
　　　　　　　　　　　　Whole peppercorns are also good

Simmered Medley　　　Salmon skin, black beans, inner skin, pickled plums, small dried fish
　　　　　　　　　　wood ear mushrooms, ginkgo nuts, walnuts etc. mixed and simmered together
