Chunky Bean Paste　This is made by boiling azuki beans and grinding them without draining the liquid,
　　　　　　　grinding in a mortar with equal parts black and white sugar
　　　　　　　mixed together.
　　　　　　　　This is good for skin-mochi dumplings.

Superior White Paste　This is made by boiling white cowpeas and grinding them in the same manner as superior paste.
　　　　　　　However, use less black sugar and more white sugar,
　　　　　　　simmering until thick.
　　　　　　　Double-layer cherry blossom mochi is made the same way.

Green Paste　　　Boil green soybeans, remove the outer skin and inner skin, grind and
　　　　　　　mix with white sugar.

　　　About Soup Stock

Raw Drip　　　Add two shō of water to one shō of miso, knead well and let drip through a bag.

Dripped Miso　　Add three shō five gō of water to one shō of miso, simmer until
　　　　　　　reduced to about three shō, then put in a bag and let drip.

Bonito Stock　　Shave bonito flakes, add two shō five gō of water to one shō,
　　　　　　　simmer until the flavor is extracted and pleasantly sweet, then remove.
　　　　　　　If over-extracted and bitter, add a little more
　　　　　　　water.

Vegetarian　　　Ise dried gourd strips, kelp, white beans, glutinous rice
　Stock　　　　Boil these in water to make stock for use.

About Kage　　　"Dropping the shadow" means adding a little soy sauce
　　　　　　　to either miso soup or clear soup.
