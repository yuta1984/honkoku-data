Kasutera-fu　　Add many eggs to ground fish paste, put in a baking pan,
　　　　　　place fire above and bake
　　　　　　　However, the pan should be as shown in the diagram.
　　　　　　　The fire control is described in detail within the volume.

Denpō-yaki　　On a tile for grilling, spread white root of negi (scallion)
　　　　　　that has been pounded into a decoction, place over fire.
　　　　　　When slightly cooked, prepare bonito and tuna as for sashimi,
　　　　　　arrange on top and grill. When the color changes,
　　　　　　　flip over and apply the prepared base sauce.

Giseiyaki　　　Add mountain yam, egg white, and salt to ground fish paste,
　　　　　　combine and shape, brush with sesame oil and grill
　　　　　　　However, one side should be browned and the other
　　　　　　　should be fox-colored. May also be cut and stacked alternately.

Rō-yaki　　　Pile salt in a roasting pan, salt-cure the fish, arrange
　　　　　　on top of the salt, cover with another roasting pan
　　　　　　and place fire above and below to grill.

Hama-yaki　　Stand tiles in the earthen floor, wash sea bream well,
　　　　　　spread salt thickly in the belly, arrange the sea bream,
　　　　　　cover with tiles from above, seal front and back with tiles,
　　　　　　place many charcoal fires on top of the tiles for
　　　　　　steam-grilling. When cooked, burn bamboo grass in fire
　　　　　　and mark attractive spots with the burn marks.
　　　　　However, when in a hurry, skewer with large bamboo skewers
　　　　　and lean against the rim of a brazier to grill.
　　　　　That is the grilled style.

Tsukuri-yaki　Fillet sea bream, cut thin, salt and grill.
　　　　　　Serve with squeezed daikon radish juice poured over.

Ara-shio-yaki　Horse mackerel, small sea bream, carp, sweetfish and
　　　　　　similar fish, coarsely salt and grill, sprinkle with salt
　　　　　　and serve. Grated daikon radish is also good.
