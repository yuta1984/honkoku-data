Iriko Namasu (Dried Sardine Namasu)
　With blue vinegar dressing
　　Wash dried sardines well, boil them, simmer with dashi and soy sauce,
　　then dress with green bean vinegar.

On the Preparation of Clear Soups

Regular Clear Soup
　　Add sake and soy sauce to dashi to adjust the flavor. Also,
　　for the base of flounder and such, thicken by coating with soy sauce.

Raw Clear Soup
　　This is used for soups after sake has been served.
　　Made with only water and soy sauce.

Miso Clear Soup
　　Same as above. Make regular miso soup, place in a bowl,
　　and let cool so the miso settles and the liquid clears. Use the clear portion.

Salt Clear Soup
　　Add roasted salt to soy sauce, add appropriate amount of water and re-boil,
　　pour into bowl, let settle, and ladle the clear portion.

Also
　Salt-based
　　Made with only roasted salt. However, it's good to add quality pickled plums
　　and simmer. Having slight acidity is also good.

Squeezed Clear Soup
　　Prepare as raw clear soup, and at the end add squeezed daikon juice.
　　Can also be made with salt-based soup.

Wasabi Clear Soup
　　Same as above, using squeezed wasabi juice.

Mustard Clear Soup
　　Mix with mustard miso, prepare as thin miso soup,
　　let cool and settle, then ladle the clear portion for use.

On Named Simmered Dishes

Sea Bream Kaki-iri
　　Roast salt in a pot, thinly slice sea bream flesh and add, pour aged sake
　　to barely cover and simmer. When the alcohol aroma rises, add third-grade white water
