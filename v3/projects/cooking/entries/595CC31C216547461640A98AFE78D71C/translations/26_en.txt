Ramen　　　　　To one shō of buckwheat flour, add ten beaten eggs, knead and roll
　　　　　　　　out.
　　It's also good to grind tofu and add it. It's also good to grate
　mountain yam and add it.

Yamakake soba　　This involves adding mountain yam to the base of zaru soba,
　　　　　　　　serving the soba, heating the base and pouring it over, then topping with the yam.

Mugi-kiri　　　　This is barley flour. The method is the same as udon.
　　　　　　　　However, udon is cut rather wide. Kuzu-mugi-kiri is rolled thin.

Suiton　　　　　Combine finest udon flour with kudzu flour, knead with boiling water,
　　　　　　　　bring to a boil in broth and pinch pieces into it.

Soba-gaimochi　　Bring finest buckwheat and miso soup to a boil and knead until firm,
Also called　　　add a little more boiling water, put it in, and when it's ready
soba-gaki　　　　ladle and cut it to serve. However, the miso soup should
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　be thin.
　　　　　　　　　Serve with soba-kiri base or tsuyu.

Keiran　　　　　Six parts glutinous rice, four parts regular rice flour, knead with water, wrap
　　　　　　　　fine brown sugar inside, form into kumquat-sized balls and
　　　　　　　　boil. For the broth, add tamari stock to thin miso and
　　　　　　　　simmer. However, the flour should be ground quite fine.
