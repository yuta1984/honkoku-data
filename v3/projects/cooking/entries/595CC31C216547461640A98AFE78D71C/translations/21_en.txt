Tsubaki Sake　　Roast one sho of black beans and let cool, add one sho
　　　　　　　　five go of good sake and let sit. When the beans
　　　　　　　　become soft and swollen, it is ready.

Iri-zake　　　　Cut one sho of bonito flakes, prepare large pickled
　　　　　　　　plums with salt removed appropriately, add two sho
　　　　　　　　of sake, a little tamari, and adjust with a little water.
　　　　　　　　For quick iri-zake, use one sho of sake with five go
　　　　　　　　of small plums, or use one sho of the decoction.
　　　　　　　　Also, with mirin sake, add only roasted salt, decoct
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　with plums and tamari.

Vegetarian Iri-zake　Cut tofu to dengaku size, grill it, then decoct
　　　　　　　　pickled plums and dried turnips in aged sake,
　　　　　　　　strain and use.
　　　　　　　　　Alternatively, boil only pickled plums in the
　　　　　　　　　aforementioned mirin sake, strain and use.

　　　Names of Sashimi Dishes

Ogawa-tataki　　Fillet bonito, pound well, arrange in a bowl,
　Iri-zake　　　pour hot water over it and prepare it pale.
　　　　　　　　Firm sea bream or skipjack tuna are also good.

Shimofuri　　　　This refers to briefly blanching any fish or fowl
　　　　　　　　in boiling water, then placing in cold water
　　　　　　　　before preparing as sashimi.
