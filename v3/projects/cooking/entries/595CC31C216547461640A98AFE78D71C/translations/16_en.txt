Items: Kudzu Section

Kudzu-uchi　　　Knead Kyusuke kudzu in boiling water until it reaches
　　　　　　　　the right consistency, then do the hot water cooking with
　　　　　　　　water removal by hand

Kudzu-take　　　Same method. Shape the kneaded kudzu into the forms of
　　　　　　　　early mushrooms and pine mushrooms, boil in hot water
　　　　　　　　and transfer to cold water. Knead Kyusuke hard in sugar
　　　　　　　　syrup, spread white kinako (soybean flour) on a wooden bowl,
　　　　　　　　pour it on top and knead while sprinkling kinako - this
　　　　　　　　allows free shaping and cutting. Then mix kinako with
　　　　　　　　sugar and roasted salt in proper proportions and sprinkle
　　　　　　　　on top before serving.
　　　　　　This method can also make warabi-mochi. For accompaniments,
　　　　　　good takuan pickles and stem pickles work well

Kudzu Manju　　　While the kudzu is hot from kneading, apply sugar and
　　　　　　　　water in proper amounts, scoop the kudzu with a spatula,
　　　　　　　　and while adding water, stretch it to the right thickness,
　　　　　　　　prepare superior an filling and wrap it.
　　　　　　　　The method for superior an is given later

Kudzu-kiri　　　Add half a kin of white sugar to one sho of kudzu powder,
　　　　　　　　knead with boiling water, roll out like soba with a rolling pin,
　　　　　　　　then pass through boiling water again before serving
　　　　　　　　Note: The base should be sweeter than soba. Use sugar
　　　　　　　　with mirin

Kudzu Somen　　Dissolve kudzu in water, add sugar in proper amount,
　　　　　　　　strain through the back of a fine sieve and set aside.
　　　　　　　　Using a copper plate or bowl-like vessel with holes,
　　　　　　　　place in a barrel and scoop into the hot water above.
　　　　　　　　The kudzu comes out through the holes in the vessel,
　　　　　　　　entering the hot water below and forming thin
　　　　　　　　somen-like strands
