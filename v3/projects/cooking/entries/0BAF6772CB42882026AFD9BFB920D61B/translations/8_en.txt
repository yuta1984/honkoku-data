Hashi no Uchikawa (Inner Skin of Beak) - The lower lip is called "tana" (shelf). The inner skin of the shelf peeled to about three bu in thickness is called the inner skin of the beak. It is gray in color.
○Attach white meat thinly and make fine cuts, pour lukewarm water over it, and use with raw soy sauce, iri-zake (heated sake with seasonings), or sanbaizu (three-flavor vinegar).
○Combine with vegetables and use as clear soup.

Mimi (Ears) - This refers to the edge parts. The edge of the upper skin of round cuts, the edge of the lower skin, and also the upper edge of the lower lip. Young whale that has been salted is best.
○Cut the salted meat thinly, wash well in water to remove the salt. Pour hot water over it, wash several times in water, and eat with vinegar miso, raw soy sauce, or iri-zake.
○Prepared as above, combine with vegetables and use as clear soup.

Sunoko (Bamboo Grating) - In right whales and sei whales, from around the lower jaw to about one shaku eight sun above the belly base, the ridged and wrinkled underside of the belly skin is called "Une." The Une skin is about three to four bu thick, and the meat inside that has reddish flesh with whitish meat is called sunoko
