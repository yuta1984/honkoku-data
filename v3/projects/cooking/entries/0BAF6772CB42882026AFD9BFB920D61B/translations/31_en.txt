Needle bone　　There are two on both sides below the abdominal bones.
Saddle (kura) bone　　There are twelve attached above and below the round-cut bone.
Kase bone　There are two on the left and right next to the key bones of the fins.
Ring (wa) bone　　There are two on both sides of the genital opening.
Clam (hamaguri) bone　　Above the genital opening, inside the flesh, there are
　　　　two about the size of tea bowls.

The above eighteen types of bones are extremely hard and should not be used for food. Those
with high oil content are chopped and rendered into oil, and the residue is made into bales for
fertilizer in rice fields and farms, sent to various provinces to be sold. Those with low oil content are chopped
and made into bales as they are, to be sold as fertilizer material.

Lice (shirami)　These grow on baleen whales and right whales, most abundantly on young whales. They are
　　about the size of bell crickets, resembling flattened spiders.
　　Their color is pale white. Those attached to black areas are pale black
　　in color. They also bite humans. They are not food.
　　They are abundantly attached to right whales. Their color is white. There is flesh inside
　　the shell, and the flesh is boiled and eaten.
