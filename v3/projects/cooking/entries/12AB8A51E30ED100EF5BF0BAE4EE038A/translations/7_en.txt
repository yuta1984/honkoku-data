Same - Lower Grade. However, cherry blossom viewing boxes should contain only spring items. For chrysanthemum viewing, autumn leaf viewing, and wisteria viewing, one should adjust the various items according to each season.

First Box, Seven Varieties:
- Needlefish (grilled in sweet soy glaze)
- Octopus simmered with cherry blossoms
- Taro potatoes
- Carrots
- Bracken ferns
- Burdock root with soil attached (preparation method found in the vegetarian cuisine section)
- Wood ear mushrooms (savory simmered)

Second Box, Accompanying Dishes:
- Steamed flounder (colored grilling)
- River snails (mixed with tree buds)

Third Box, Rice Cake Selection:
- Bush warbler rice cakes
- Fulling block rolls
Or alternatively:
- Imasaka variety

Fourth Box:
- Red rice (celebratory rice with azuki beans)

Separately, one should bring dried sweets, a sake barrel, and cups.
Packing seasonal viewing boxes appropriately in this manner is most essential as a means of sustaining oneself through long outings.

Boat Excursion Tiered Boxes - Upper Grade. Note: these are boxes given as greetings to those involved in boat excursions.

First Box, Seven Varieties:
- Amber eggs
- Dutch tempura
- Japanese whiting grilled with salt (deboned, head and tail removed, salted)
- Beaten jellyfish
- Small eggplant (honey-simmered), rolled pickles
- Grilled seaweed, radish sprouts

Second Box, Fresh Selection:
- Small sea bream
- Leaf-shaped flounder
- Ayu sweetfish variety
- Flowering yuzu citrus

Third Box, Sashimi:
- Blanched fish, soft roe in large cups with cooking sake
- Agar jelly (pulled thin and yellow)
- Rock mushrooms
- Wasabi (formed into needles, domestic)

Fourth Box, Final Course:
- Arrowroot noodles (boiled)
- Fine sweet bean paste
- White sugar
(However, packing instructions for the boxes should be written on cards and provided)
