Illustration of the Preliminary Work at the Storehouse Area of Misaki, Ikitsuki

Every year from mid-August, shipbuilding begins. Each year, three seko boats (hunt boats), one motsoū boat, and one sōgai boat are newly built and added, rotating and discarding the old ones. Ship equipment is handled in the same manner. The number of ships prepared for one group is as stated below.

Seko boats (hunting boats): 20 vessels (ranked from No. 1 boat to No. 20 boat in order)
　Equipment for one vessel: 1 rudder, 8 oars, 1 paddle, 16 oar pegs, 16 storage containers, 16 hawsers
　　1 four-tier sail, 1 mast, 1 joint hook, 6 mats, 1 water bucket, 2 wing kettles, 1 iron bath
　　1 mountain knife, 1 chisel, 1 marker, 1 small finger marker, 1 pole, 1 rice bucket, 1 ladle, 1 long handle
　　1 firebox, 1 water pole, 2 multi-purpose harpoons, 2 feather-arrow harpoons, 1 sword, 1 file, 1 pattern cutter, 1 root hemp
　　1 binding tool, 3 signal devices, 2 projecting tools, 2 arrow ropes, 1 long handle (this long handle is carried only by boats No. 1, 2, and 3), torches (number variable)

Motsoū boats (twin-support boats): 4 vessels
　Equipment for one vessel: 2 body ropes, 2 end restraints, 2 middle restraints, 6 reef attachments, 2 screen hooks
　　Twin-support pillars (2 pillars used for 4 boats), 5 binding tools, other equipment same as seko boats

Sōgai boats (net boats): 6 vessels
　Equipment for one vessel: nets (19 tan), 1 throwing mooring line, 1 rowing pillar, 1 rowing rope, 3 iron anchors
　　1 stern support, 1 chest support, 1 water-crossing rope, other equipment same as seko boats

Sōgai-support boats: 6 vessels (equipment for one vessel same as seko boats)
Group leader's boat: 1 vessel (equipment same as seko boats)
Storehouse boats: 2 vessels (equipment for one vessel same as seko boats)
Storehouse transport boat: 1 vessel

Total of 40 vessels. New construction or repaired boats are used alternately. They are painted and decorated with red lead, white powder, ink, etc., creating various patterns. Emergency replacement equipment is prepared for all these various tools and gear, which can truly be called thoroughly convenient preparations.
