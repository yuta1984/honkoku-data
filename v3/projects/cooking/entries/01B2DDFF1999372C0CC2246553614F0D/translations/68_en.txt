**Tampo**
Made of copper, holds two gō. Used for scooping oil when filling barrels to leave a little room. To reduce a four-to barrel by one shō, five scoops with this are taken.

**Oil Pot (Abura-tsubo)**
An iron cauldron that holds two koku of oil. Buried about seven-tenths deep and arranged in the oil storehouses of the main warehouse, small warehouse, bone warehouse, etc., with boiled oil poured in through gutters.

**Oak Bark Boiler (Kashiha-ki-taki)**
The oak bark boiler is a large iron cauldron. The mouth diameter is 3 shaku 5 sun, depth about 3 shaku 5 sun. It boils out oak bark to dye net ropes. New ones are dyed twice, old ones once. They are dyed because fish are frightened by white color. It also makes them strong and water-repellent.

**Rendering Cauldron (Neri-gama)**
Mouth diameter 3 shaku, depth 1 shaku 2 sun. Small-cut blubber is put in to render oil.

**Cut Meat Pole (Kiri-mi-bō)**
Hooks are attached to the rim of small cutting tubs as shown in the diagram for carrying them to the side of the rendering cauldrons where oil is boiled.

**Small Cutting Tub (Ko-giri-oke)**
Holds two to of small-cut blubber, weighing about 60 kin. Rendered oil yields: top-grade fish 1 to 6 shō, medium-grade fish 1 to 2 shō, low-grade fish about 1 to.

**Fish Basket (Uo-kago)**
Used inside the warehouse to carry skin and meat from fish shelves to the small cutting area.

**Organ Basket (Zō-kago)**
Used to carry organ meat from the beach to the warehouse, or when loading from the warehouse to selling boats, transported in this basket.

**Lever/Crowbar (Manriki)**
This crowbar is driven in when carrying bones.

Holds two to.

**Large Oil Barrel (Abura-ō-taru)**
Holds four to. Oil is put in these and sold to various places. About two thousand barrels of various sizes are prepared.
