【Cooking Example (Dried Sweetfish, Dried Ayu)】
Soup　
　Cut into tube shapes, about two pieces; if with roe, even better.
　Goto miso or barley miso are good choices.
　　　　Soup garnish ◦Butterbur sprouts　◦White part of scallions
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Choose from these types accordingly

【Ingredient】
White Dried Small Sweetfish　
　Commonly called "chirimen jako" (dried young sardines).
　Simply use one type, sprinkle with sake and salt or seasoned vinegar.
Also crumble and add to tofu for soup.

【Ingredient】
Pressed Sardines　
　These are dried shirasu (whitebait) fish, which are
　different in type from the small sweetfish above, so should not be prepared the same way.
　　Sprinkling with soy sauce and grilling is the usual method.

【Cooking Example】
Clear
Soup
　If pressed sardines are left in water for about two days, they return to their fresh color
　and separate individually. Good for soups with udo (Japanese spikenard) etc. as substitute for early season whitebait.
　　　　　　　　Soup garnish　Asakusa nori seaweed, crumbled

【Ingredient】
Namaribushi (Half-dried Bonito)
　Tosa-bushi　For both, remove oil well and then
　　　　　　　use them
　　　Also　Glossy-spicy【continues on next page】

【Cooking Example】
Flat Bowl
　Cut namaribushi into large rectangular pieces, simmer in light soy sauce
　and serve with kudzu starch sauce
　　　　　　Wasabi　or mustard

【Cooking Example】
Medium Plate
　Similarly, cut thick and large, grill, sprinkle with salt
　and pour tade vinegar (smartweed vinegar) over it

【Cooking Example】
Bowl Dish
　Similarly, cut thicker than small rounds, make red miso thick
　by grinding, thin with sake, strain through wheat gluten, add pepper
　powder and simmer
