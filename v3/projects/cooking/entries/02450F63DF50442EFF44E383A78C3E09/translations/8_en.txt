【Cooking Example (Salted Mackerel)】
Rice Bowl Dish
　Draw out a little salt, sear with grill marks, steam, and sprinkle with sesame salt.
　　Accompaniments: fresh ginger, pickled shiso seeds, vinegar-pickled small eggplant, and other suitable items

【Cooking Example】
Grilled Dish
Or Mukōzuke (appetizer)
　Take large pieces of flesh while still salted, grill, pass through hot water and squeeze, then pour seasoned sake over.
　　Garnishes: thinly shaved small turnips, lotus root varieties are good

【Ingredients】
Salted Yellowtail
　Use the prime cuts of Tango yellowtail.

【Cooking Example】
　Bowl Side Dish
Or Long Plate
　　Cut flesh into cubes, simmer in hot water and set aside, pour white sake over and serve.
　　　Garnishes: citron fragrance, miso-pickled eggplant, these types are good

【Cooking Example】
Large Bowl Dish
　Take large pieces of flesh, thoroughly draw out the salt by boiling, add slight grill marks, add plenty of sake with a little soy sauce and simmer, then serve.
　Accompaniments: simmered daikon radish, taro varieties

【Ingredients】
Salt-Cured Salmon, also Salmon Roe (kokomori), or Dried Salmon

【Cooking Example】
Hōroku Grilling
　Cut salt-cured salmon as-is into large dice, put in earthenware pot (hōroku), simmer with sake only, beat eggs and mix, pour over the top and cover.

【Cooking Example】
Koji Pickle
　Remove skin and make fine strips from the good parts of the flesh, dissolve koji in sake and pickle in this for about one day, use with the koji still on. However, for inkstone lids and such, wrapping in bamboo leaves and layering shows good technique.
