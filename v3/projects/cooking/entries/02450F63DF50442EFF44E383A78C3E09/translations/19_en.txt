【Cooking Example (Namaribushi)】
Large Sake Cup
　Cut namaribushi into small dice, mix with black sesame
　miso paste
　　　Green herb dressing is also good

【Cooking Example】
Tosa-bushi
　Pickle in miso for more than ten days, then remove
　and slice thinly crosswise, arrange on hand-salt dishes or
　on inkstone lids

【Cooking Example】
Glossy-spicy
　When Tosa-bushi is shaved with a proper plane, it becomes
　wide and thin shavings which are simmered and
　used for arranged dishes. Also available domestically

【Ingredient】
Small Migakinishin (Processed Herring)
　Though inferior quality, soak in plain hot water for 4-5 days,
　frequently changing the water to thoroughly wash away the oil
　before using

【Cooking Example】
Medium Plate
　After soaking migakinishin well in water, wash thoroughly, add plenty of
　bonito flakes and make simmered dish

【Cooking Example】
Small Bowl
　Wrap with green kelp, simmer well with
　sake and soy sauce
　　　In Kyoto at Nijo-Takakura, this is called nishin-kobu-maki
　　　and is a specialty. Wrapping with arame seaweed is also good

【Ingredient】
Herring Roe
　There is also domestically produced clustered roe
　For both types, soak in water for 4-5 days, frequently changing water
　until well softened, then remove and use
　　　However, it is customary to use with bonito flake broth poured over

【Cooking Example】
Bowl Dish
　Take chilled roe, thoroughly wipe off moisture
　and dress with mustard vinegar miso
