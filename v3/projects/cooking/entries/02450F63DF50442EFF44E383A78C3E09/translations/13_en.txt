【Cooking Example】
Senba-ni (Boiled Preparation)
　Same as above, blanch well with repeated boiling, change the water,
　add a little sake and a little soy sauce, then season with pickled plum.
　　　　Accompaniment: Thinly sliced daikon

【Cooking Example】
Vinegar Stir-fry (Su-iri)
　For this, lightly salted is good. Method same as senba-ni.
　Add vinegar before serving.
　　　　Accompaniment: Root parsley

【Cooking Example】
Tea Bowl Dish
　Same as right, remove salt slightly, cut to proper size,
　steam and pour long yam sauce over it.

【Ingredient】
Horse Mackerel (Moro-aji)
　These come from Izu and Suruga provinces, arriving in large quantities
　in places like Kofu around springtime. The flavor is extremely good; grill and pour
　tade vinegar over, or simmer in light clear broth,
　both are good.

【Ingredient】
Salted Abalone
Attached: Simmered Shellfish
　Salt shellfish should be soaked in water for two to three days, then removed at the right time
　for use, but difficult to use except for water shellfish or vinegar shellfish.
　Simmered shellfish are already cooked in soy sauce from the beginning, so even when made into stuffed preparations,
　they have such strong flavor there's no good way to use them.

【Ingredient】
Pickled Jellyfish
　There are three colors: red, white, and black, all domestic products.
　Depending on the combination, any color may be used.

【Cooking Example】
Long Plate
　Soak in water to remove salt well, slice thin and
　arrange, with wasabi vinegar or seasoned sake, or three-flavor vinegar
　　　　Accompaniment: Anything is fine; also as partner for fresh arrangements
　　　　　　or as partner for water dishes

【Cooking Example】
Tea Bowl Dish
　Cut very fine and simmer in light clear broth,
　strain and transfer to tea bowl, pour kudzu starch sauce over.
　　　　Also good with seasonings added to soba noodle base
