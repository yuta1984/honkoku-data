【Cooking Example (Salt-Cured Salmon)】
Salmon Paste (Sake-bishio)
　Scrape the good parts of salmon flesh with the blade of a plane knife, mix well by pounding with high-quality salmon sake lees, apply to small boards and layer in inkstone lids.
　However, one should aim for a color similar to plum miso.

【Cooking Example】
Tea Bowl Dish
Senba style
Or Flat Plate
　Take salmon flesh in appropriate sizes, parboil well in hot water, change the water and season with a little sake, salt, and soy sauce. Accompaniments: shaved bamboo-style daikon radish, early bamboo shoots are good.

【Cooking Example】
Sashimi
Long Plate
　Slice thin and serve arranged as is. Alternatively, pouring white sake over the top is also good.

【Cooking Example】
Overnight Pickle
Kokera Sushi
　Soak in water to draw out some salt, shave the flesh thin and large, mix with wood ear mushrooms, sea lettuce, julienned ginger or attractively colored pickled daikon cut into strips, and pickle mixed with rice. Kokera sushi refers to mixing with rice and pickling.

【Cooking Example】
Large Sake Cup
Or Small Rice Bowl
　Chill only the salmon skin well in water, remove moss and squeeze out moisture, deep-fry in sesame oil, and pour over a mixture of squeezed daikon juice and soy sauce.
　　Accompaniment: deep-fried yam bulbils are good.

【Cooking Example】
Vinegared Dish Plate
　Split the salmon head, remove gills, slice thin and pickle in vinegar, cut daikon into eighths and simmer until soft, then mix with vinegar and soy sauce. Alternatively, julienned aralia shoots are also good.

【Cooking Example】
Drying Rack Dish
　Take flesh from dried salmon, place on drying rack and dry well, dissolve kudzu powder in water and coat with this, then place on drying rack again and dry well.

【Cooking Example】
Same with Salmon Roe (Sujiko)
　Soup: Pass through boiling water, break apart and add to clear tofu broth.
　Hand Salt: Break apart and pour wasabi vinegar over.
