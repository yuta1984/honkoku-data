Cooking Third Volume: Yamaga Collection
　　　General Rules
1. Since the composition of meal arrangements has been thoroughly recorded in the previous two volumes, what more is there to say now? This volume simply prepares salt-preserved foods and dried fish for use during times when fresh fish is unavailable.
1. In general, salted fish and dried fish have poor flavor and are difficult to prepare, making them increasingly hard to use for food. Here I classify various salted and dried fish and describe in detail below how to prepare them with good flavor.
1. For the freshwater fish section, I present small fish starting with sweetfish (ayu). This is because they can be found in any mountain stream. Carp, crucian carp, salmon, and trout are not available in some locations, and moreover, since cooking methods for these have already been listed in the previous volume, I omit them here.
