After that, gradually pound it like making mochi, and it will become fu (wheat gluten). Then wash and knead it many times in water to remove the dregs. Remove the small bits of dregs by cutting with a thin blade, and tear into good-sized pieces. Then prepare hot water. Heat the water so it doesn't come to a full boil, put the fu in it, stir many times with a ladle, and when the water starts to boil, add cold water many times to prevent it from boiling over. The proper cooking point is when it floats to the surface. Generally, for both kneading fu and washing it, flowing river water is best.

　　　△Methods for Long-term Preservation of All Things
　　　　　○How to Store Chestnuts
One: In a jar or pot, place one earthenware piece face down at the bottom, put chestnuts on top of it, and seal the opening well. Place it so the earthenware doesn't break.

　　　　　○Same Storage Method - Secret Transmission
One: Arrange chestnuts in a tea kettle with no moisture, cover well, and place the whole thing in a box. Done this way, insects won't eat them, stains won't penetrate, and they will keep until next year. However, if you heat fire tongs and burn the pointed sprouting area at the bottom of the chestnuts, they won't sprout either.
