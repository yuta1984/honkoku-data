You can also pickle them by removing the contents from both cut ends. You can also pickle them cut in half. Arrange one hundred melons in layers in a tub, add the above water, place weights, and leave as is for three days. On the second day, loosen the weights slightly. On the fourth day, remove the weights, wash well with the pickling liquid, dry in good weather for three days, then leave in shade for another three days to cool thoroughly, and pickle in lees. If the lees are hard, add sake to make it liquid-like for pickling. Add plenty of lees so the melons don't rub against each other.

　　○Method for Dried Melons
One: Cut white melons into six or eight pieces depending on size, remove the contents, arrange in a tub, sprinkle with salt, repeat this process several times, and place weights overnight - water will come out. The next day, wash well with that salt water, arrange on bamboo screens, dry in good weather for just one day, brush the inside lightly with old sake, and store in jars. The color will keep well until June of next year.
