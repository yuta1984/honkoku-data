○Method for Horse Chestnut Rice Cakes
One serving of glutinous rice (1 shō)　　One serving of horse chestnut powder (3 gō)
Removing the toxins from the horse chestnuts and making powder is quite difficult. Now, quickly boil the glutinous rice, place it in a steamer, sprinkle the horse chestnut powder over it and mix together. Add a little water - just enough so that when you grip it in your hand, it holds together in your palm. Then steam well and pound like regular rice cakes. For the steaming process, steam as thoroughly as possible, and the pounded cakes can be used as they are. They can also be made into sliced dried rice cakes.

○Method for Kudzu Rice Cakes
One cup of kudzu powder (undissolved kudzu)
One cup of white sugar　　One cup of water
Mix these three ingredients together and put into a bamboo tube. For the bamboo tube,

○Method for Uirō Rice Cakes
Non-glutinous fine white rice (7 gō, to be powdered)
Glutinous fine white rice (3 gō, to be powdered)　　White sugar (a little)
First combine the two types of rice together, then divide into two equal portions. Knead one portion with gardenia juice and the other with water, achieving a crumbly consistency. Sift through a coarse rice sieve, put into a steamer and steam. Between the yellow and white layers, arrange thinly sliced Mino hanging persimmons and steam together.
