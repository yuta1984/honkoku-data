○Method for Chidori (Plover-shaped confection)
One: White miso 160 monme (grind well and strain), One: Poppy seeds 10 monme
One: White sesame 10 monme, One: Japanese pepper powder 10 monme
Mix the above well together, spread a very coarse cloth on a cutting board, sprinkle a little poppy seeds and sesame on top, spread the above miso little by little and smooth with a thin blade, sprinkle poppy seeds and sesame on top as well, then spread cloth over that, smooth with the palm of your hand, flip it over, remove the cloth, cut into pieces about 4-5 sun long and 2-3 sun wide, and place on a hōroku (earthenware pan). If the fire is too strong it will burn, so generally roast the edges and cut as desired. After cutting, if you place it on the hōroku again, the edges will roast.

○Method for Kase-ita (Pear confection board)
One: Among wood pears, take good pears, peel them, cut into quarters, remove the hard parts inside, add just enough water to cover, boil until quite soft, place on a bamboo strainer to remove moisture, grind very finely in a mortar, strain through a cloth, and set aside separately. To 1 kanme of the above prepared pears, add 600 monme of white sugar dissolved in about 2 shō of the pear cooking liquid, strain through cloth, mix well with the above prepared pears, put in a pot, and slowly cook down over gentle heat. The consistency should be about as firm as kuzu-mochi. Then prepare a bent cedar container with an opening of 6 sun and depth of about 1 sun 5 bu to 2 sun, fill it completely, and smooth the top beautifully.
