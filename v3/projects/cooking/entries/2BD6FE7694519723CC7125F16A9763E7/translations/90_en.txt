One: Skewer the sweetfish and grill without burning, then dry in the sun for about 14-15 days until hard, and hang them up. From around Shimotsuki (November), it's fine to store them in boxes. They can be prepared with the intestines, or if the intestines are removed, washed well and grilled, they will dry faster. No matter when made, they keep for about thirty days. ○Mock Pheasant
One: Wash bonito well, fillet into three pieces, and remove the dark meat, thin flesh, and skin. Pour hot water over and shred, pour hot water again, then cool with water and drain well. Then add vinegar to irizake (seasoned sake) and simmer over charcoal fire, add the bonito, chestnuts, and ginger, stir and finish. Adding about as much wasabi and citron vinegar is good.

○Plum Kaka
One: Scrape bonito flakes very roughly, rinse quickly with water - one cup

One: One and a half cups sake, half cup water, dried plums with salt still attached - if regular plums, twelve or thirteen; if large plums, seven or eight. Combine these three ingredients and simmer once, add salt at the right time to prepare the broth. Simmer down and repeatedly add broth while simmering.
