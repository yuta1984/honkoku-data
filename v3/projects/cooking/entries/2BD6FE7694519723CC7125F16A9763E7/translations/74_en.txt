　　　○Small Clam Shiokara
One: For one shō of small shucked clam meat, add three gō of salt and mix together. Put in a bamboo strainer
　and shake - the bad water will come out and the sliminess will be removed. Wash well and let drip,
　then mix two gō of sake lees as is, two gō of salt, two gō of
　old sake, and a little yuzu leaves as described, put in a jar and
　set aside. Use after about seven days have passed.
　△Types of Namasu (Vinegared Fish)
　　　○Carp Namasu
One: Roasting the roe is generally bad practice. Boil it and drain the water,
　then set aside. Cut the flesh as finely as possible, warm the irizake (seasoned sake),
　and dress with irizake only. Add plenty of wasabi, chestnuts,
　ginger, etc., add a little vinegar, and mix in the roe when serving.

【Left Page】
　　　○Crucian Carp Namasu
One: Before adding the roe, sprinkle a little sake and
　dress once with that, then drain off that sake. This is done
　to remove the fishy smell. Dress with vinegar, irizake, and salt. The grilled bones and roe
　are mixed in afterward.
　　　○Mock Crucian Carp Namasu
One: Cut sea cucumber as finely as possible, warm irizake until lukewarm
　and dress with it. Use any fish
　grilled bones finely ground, add wasabi and dress, then add vinegar.
　　　○Sea Cucumber Namasu
One: Put fish bones in vinegar and boil to make a broth, remove the bones, add sake,
　add salt, add dried bonito flakes and let cool to lukewarm. Cut sea
　cucumber, fish, and bird feathers all finely and dress together.
