○Method for Corn Rice Cakes
During the cold season, or actually at any time, remove and discard the rough hulls of corn by grinding with a stone mortar. Then grind the good parts finely with a stone mortar again and put through a steamer basket. Mix with water, put into a cotton bag, knead out in water, change the water two or three times to remove the lye, let it settle and discard the water on top, then dry in the sun. When making rice cakes, powder the hardened material, knead with hot water, boil in water, and when they float to the surface, coat with bean powder or azuki beans. It doesn't matter if you don't add rice cake powder. When adding rice cake powder, add one-third the amount of rice cake powder, knead until crumbly,

○Method for Chestnut Rice Cakes
Crush chestnuts in a mortar, remove and discard the rough shells. Then grind with a stone mortar and sift through silk. Mix with one-third glutinous rice powder (made by cold-soaking), knead until crumbly, steam well in a steamer, put in a mortar and pound. Coat with azuki beans or soybean powder.

○Method for Water-Dried Rice Cakes
Soak finest white glutinous rice appropriately in water, grind in a grinding bowl, put in a cotton bag, knead out in water, discard the clear water on top, dry in the sun, and prepare like cold-soaked powder. However, cold-soaked rice can also be made into water-dried rice cakes, but cold-soaked rice cakes are not as white as those made from regular rice.
