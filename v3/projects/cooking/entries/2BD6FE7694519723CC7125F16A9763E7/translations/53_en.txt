　　○Method for Quick-Pickled Daikon
One: Daikon radish (however, half should be left with leaves attached, half with leaves cut off, dried for a full day until slightly wilted)
One: Salt - one to; Koji - four sho
Mix the above well together, sprinkle one layer at the bottom of the tub, place daikon, and layer salt-koji between and on top at frost-falling intervals, stacking many times. Apply quite strong weights, then later loosen the weights slightly. The amount of salt depends on the quantity of daikon.

　　Same Method
One: Daikon - 100 pieces; Rice bran - one to
One: Salt - two sho; Koji - three sho

【Left Page】
Mix the above three ingredients well together; the pickling method is the same as above.
This quick pickle works very well. When there is a large quantity of daikon, adjust the proportions of bran, salt, and koji lightly as estimated.

　　○Same Water Quick-Pickle Method
One: Pickle seventy daikon pieces with one sho of salt. Apply weights, and after three days pass, remove the weights, add water until just covered, and apply weights well again. The above quick pickle maintains good flavor even when kept until May or June.

　　○Thin Daikon Pickle Style (Pickled Vegetables)
One: Rub thin daikon with salt, and when the salt has penetrated well, dry in the sun for one day, then pickle in sake lees.

　　○Daikon Overnight Pickle (Pickled Vegetables)
One: Leave about one sun of stem on thin daikon, cut off the leaf tips, wash thoroughly, remove moisture completely, and pickle in miso overnight.
