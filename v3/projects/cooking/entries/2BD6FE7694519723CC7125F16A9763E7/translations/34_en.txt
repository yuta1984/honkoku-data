○Method for Satsuki Rice Cakes
One portion fine white non-glutinous rice, wash three times with water, place in a bamboo basket to dry well, clean the mortar and pound, then sieve through a fine sieve. Knead the flour with water to about the consistency of gripping the base of your thumb. Then tear into pieces about the size of large Buddha rice cakes, flatten them and steam in a steamer. To judge the steaming, when you break one open and the inside has the same glossy appearance as the outside, that's good. At that time, pound well in a mortar. Plain water is good for hand-wetting. After that, stretch by hand many times and cut with string in any manner desired. Then arrange in a single layer in a grilling bowl,

Float the bowl on water to cool. Also, to add mugwort, the day before remove the mugwort stems and prepare well, boil in straw lye, soak in water, and the next day pound well before making the rice cakes, then pound together with the rice cakes.
However, when adding mugwort, make the initial kneading consistency slightly firmer. Also, to make yellow color, crack gardenia seeds, extract the yellow with water, and strain through a water strainer. Knead with that yellow liquid (for red, use safflower).

○Method for Wheat Gluten Rice Cakes
One portion wheat flour, 1 shō　One portion white sugar, 1 kin
One portion aged sake, five cups in an ordinary sake cup
Mix the above well together, grind well in a mortar, then spread cloth in a steamer and steam. Steam for about one hour. Then when well cooled, cut in any manner desired.
