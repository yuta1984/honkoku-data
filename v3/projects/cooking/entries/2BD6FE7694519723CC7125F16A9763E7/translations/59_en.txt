Dig directly in a place without moisture, spread bran evenly on the bottom, and lay dried smartweed on top of it. Arrange chestnuts and layer them with smartweed like making sushi, pressing down well in multiple layers, then place about 5 sun (about 15cm) of soil on top and press down. When needed, take them out and press down the remaining area again. The smartweed should be dried when it's at its peak. This will keep until next year.

　　　○Same Method for Ground-Stored Chestnuts
One: Soak chestnuts with their shells in boiling water for about three days to remove the lye, then take them out and dry in good weather for six or seven days, then peel. The astringent skin will fall off if rubbed lightly by hand.

　　　○Method for Preserving Mandarin Oranges
One: Spread ground bran on the bottom of a lacquered stacked box, place the finest mandarin oranges one by one so they don't touch each other, cover with ground bran on top, put the lid on, place the whole thing in a box, and store in an earthen storehouse. However, even done this way, about twenty out of a hundred will be damaged.

　　　○Same Method - Secret Transmission
One: Cut reeds to about 5 sun (15cm) long and insert them into the finest mandarin oranges from the stem end. Use one reed per mandarin orange. Then dig about 2 shaku (60cm) deep in dry ground, insert the mandarin oranges with reeds into the ground, with one mandarin orange per reed
