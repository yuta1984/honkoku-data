Forty-eight: A Type of Yamabuki (Yellow Rose) Tofu　　Cut into large dice, shake in a bamboo strainer to round the corners, color with gardenia, and grill with thin soy sauce marinade. ○There is also yamabuki tofu mentioned in item twenty-four, unohana tofu.

Forty-nine: Kasumi (Mist) Tofu　　Squeeze water from tofu, grate daikon radish and also squeeze water well. Mix thoroughly in a ratio of six parts tofu to four parts daikon, scoop into boiling water and it will coagulate well. Scoop with a mesh ladle into a bowl, add a little Asakusa nori seaweed, and serve with fresh simmered soy sauce mixed with red pepper powder.

Fifty: Nanban (Foreign-style) Dengaku　　Roast sesame seeds, Japanese torreya nuts, and Japanese pepper well, grind to powder, then stir-fry in oil again and beat into miso. ○Alternatively, mix with miso and stir-fry the miso mixture in oil, then beat. Thin with sake and salt to make dengaku.

Fifty-one: Aratama (Rough Jewel) Tofu　　Hard-boiled chicken eggs whole, especially freshly boiled ones, serve in a tea bowl, and on top add the kaminari tofu from item ten of the previous volume
