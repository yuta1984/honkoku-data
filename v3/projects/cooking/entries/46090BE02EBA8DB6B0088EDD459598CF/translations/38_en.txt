Eighty-one: Gōkan (Silk Tree) Tofu　　Cut tofu and mochi into the same size, flat pieces, boil them separately, and serve in a good flat tea bowl with the mochi layered on top. ○Pour kudzu starch sauce over it, and top with squeezed ginger juice and dried bonito flakes.

Eighty-two: Gansetki (Rock) Tofu　　Squeeze the water well from the tofu, add quail meat and a little kudzu starch powder. Grind each ingredient separately, then combine and grind well together. Form into appropriate sizes and boil. It's essential to squeeze the water from the tofu well, or it won't bind with the meat. ○Separately prepare dashi and soy sauce with good seasoning balance, adding spice to taste. ○Duck can also be used, but quail is the best. ○This is a favorite of Lord Katagiri Sekishū. Junior Fifth Rank, Lower Grade, Governor of Iwami Province, Sadamasa, with the tea name Sōkan.

Eighty-three: Nanjing (Nanking) Tofu　　Cut the white parts of scallions into five-bu pieces, stir-fry with sake, season with soy sauce, then add tofu by breaking it up with your hands and simmer. ○Top with roughly chopped green chili peppers.
