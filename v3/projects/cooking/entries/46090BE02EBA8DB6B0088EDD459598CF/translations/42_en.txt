Ninety-two: Success Dengaku　　Order from a tofu maker to add powdered shukusha when grinding the soybeans. The proportion should be one ryō of shukusha per box of tofu. For the miso, mix in about two bu of finely ground Kōchi cinnamon. The dengaku preparation follows the usual method. Although the tofu is a bit firm, this dengaku has excellent flavor and causes no digestive problems.
○This is one of the seven types of cuisine mentioned in the ancient practices of Shitennō-ji Temple in Naniwa (Osaka).

Ninety-three: Dried Tofu　　Take the pressed tofu from recipe fifteen of the previous volume, stir-fry it with light soy sauce, let it cool, cut it into one-inch squares about three bu thick, deep-fry in oil, then season again with soy sauce. ○Serve with irizake and grated wasabi.

Ninety-four: Torreya Nut Oil-Fried Tofu　　Deep-fry in torreya nut oil. There is nothing special about the preparation method. The flavor is of the highest quality. Season according to preference.
