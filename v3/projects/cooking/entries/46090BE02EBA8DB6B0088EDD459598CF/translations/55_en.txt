Thirty-two: Grilled Cutlassfish　　Fillet a large cutlassfish into three pieces, lightly salt the flesh, mix fresh okara with a little salt, marinate the fish meat like pickles, place under a good weight for about one night, then remove and grill without brushing off the okara. This has a simple, refined taste.

Thirty-three: Tanuki Soup　　Tear konnyaku into small pieces, deep-fry in sesame oil, and use this as ingredients for a miso soup made with well-ground okara.

Thirty-four: Long-awaited Okara　　Pack tofu okara as-is into the bottom of a miso barrel, add whole red peppers in appropriate numbers, and leave until the upper layer of miso is gradually used up as usual. When the miso is exhausted and you reach the bottom, take out the okara and add seasonings as desired, such as sanshō pepper, kaya nuts, hemp seeds, white sesame, and powdered citrus peel, in addition to the original red peppers. This makes an excellent accompaniment to sake.
