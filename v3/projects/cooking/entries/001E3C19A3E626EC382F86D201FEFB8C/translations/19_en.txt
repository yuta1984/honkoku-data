Boil water and parboil the daikon three or four times. Then put plenty of ground sea bream or flounder into a mortar, grind well, add salt to taste, add a little sake, add good quality miso as appropriate, also add high-quality kudzu and mix well by grinding, extending it to about the consistency of grated yam. When the daikon is cooked, lift it out with a mesh ladle, drain off the drops, put it in the mortar, mix well, let it rest for a while, then serve. Adding ground red pepper or grated ginger is also good. This is a specialty of Inba District.

(17) Kōya-ni Daikon Method
《Side note: This is a specialty of Mount Kōya in Kishū. In this province, daikon does not grow very well, so it is brought from other provinces. Generally, dried daikon from Bishū is used in various ways》
First, if using fresh daikon, cut into rounds about 5 bu (approximately 1.5cm) thick with the skin on, fry small pieces of kelp in sesame oil, put about one gō of high-quality miso in a tea bag, place it among the daikon, combine water and soy sauce, add this and simmer the daikon. If you remove from heat after one boil, it will cook well. The flavor is extremely good, and one can eat any amount of it.
