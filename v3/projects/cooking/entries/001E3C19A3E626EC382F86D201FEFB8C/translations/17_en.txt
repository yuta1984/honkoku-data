(13) Daikon Tōshun-kin Method
First, peel this in the same manner as above, spread on a cutting board, and sprinkle udon flour. Then spread red peppers, remove the seeds, and cut into thin strips. Sprinkle this on top, also cut yuba into thin strips and sprinkle. Similarly sprinkle udon flour. On top of this, sprinkle thin strips of kaya nuts and thin strips of Japanese pepper bark, sprinkle high-quality kudzu starch, and again sprinkle udon flour. Roll tightly from the edge, apply kudzu paste, cut into pieces about 5 bu (approximately 1.5cm), heat oil, and deep-fry one by one. This can be used for anything. However, place in a small dish, pour soy sauce over it, and top with grated spicy daikon to serve.

(14) Rikyū Daikon Nikoroshi Soup
First, peel daikon thinly to about 3 sun (9cm) long, then cut to about 2 sun (6cm), arrange the cross-sections, stack 5-6 pieces at a time and cut thinly and long. Make plenty of this, blanch briefly, and simmer in three-year miso soup. Or with available grated daikon, including the skin
