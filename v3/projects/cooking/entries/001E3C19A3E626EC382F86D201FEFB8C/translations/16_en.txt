(12) Nishiki Daikon Method
First, cut this into pieces about three sun (9cm) long, peel the outer skin. Peel thinly and long so it doesn't break, spread it out on a cutting board, and apply udon flour to the surface of the daikon. Similarly, mince green leaves and scatter them on top. Also soak iwatake mushrooms in water, squeeze them well and spread on top, then sprinkle a little udon flour. Also place fresh flat yuba (tofu skin), and sprinkle udon flour again. However, sprinkle only in certain places. From the end of the peeling, roll it up tightly and firmly, wrap the top with cloth or paper, tie with thin string, steam in a steamer, let it cool well, and cut into rounds. Depending on usage, you can also cut it while hot and put it in bowls. For nuta dishes and sashimi, use the chilled version
