○Method for Homemade Sesame Oil
Grind one shō of sesame seeds in a mortar until extremely fine, wrap in cloth pieces, put plenty of water in a pot, wash the mortar well and add that water too, and when boiled over strong fire, oil will float to the surface of the hot water. Continuously scoop up that floating oil with a metal ladle, completely removing it until there is no oil left, then discard the water from the pot and simmer only the scooped oil. The moisture will naturally evaporate and only oil will remain. This is called homemade sesame oil.

○Method for Storing Matsutake Mushrooms
Take quite fresh matsutake mushrooms that are medium-sized and opening, carefully remove the stem ends, make a bag from thick paper, put them inside, seal the opening well so that smoke cannot enter, always hang in a place without smoke, take out when needed, soak in rice washing water and use. The aroma does not fade and they are like fresh ones.

○Method for Storing Persimmons
If you soak quite fresh persimmons in fresh astringent juice, not even one in a hundred will spoil, which must be the principle of "like seeks like." The sympathetic response among similar things should be called wondrous.
