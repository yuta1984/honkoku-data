Put it in and cover well, let it rest for about 3 hours. However, test by squeezing it in your hand - when the water drains out and the remaining glutinous rice husks become dry and rattling, that is the proper degree. Then put it as is into a cloth bag and squeeze, then let it rest again for a while.
At this time, add 2 monme of dried alum. Strain the water from which the dregs have been removed through sand.
Put that strained water into a kettle and boil it down. However, for the boiling degree, determine it by scooping with a shell ladle - when it remains like thin paper, that is the proper boiling degree. At that time, quickly remove from heat, transfer to a separate vessel, cool and store.

○Second batch of the same
Add water to just cover the dregs from the first batch, let it rest for about half an hour, put in a bag and squeeze again, then boil down. In summer use water, in winter use body-temperature hot water.

○Confection - Flower Mochi
Kudzu powder　　1 shō
Udon flour　　　3 gō

Mix these two ingredients well together. For red color add crimson, for blue color add powdered tea, for yellow color add gardenia juice. Knead firmly and make various flower shapes, steam thoroughly, cool and serve by placing in a bowl with water. However, whether in small dishes or anything else, add finest white sugar and scoop and serve with a mesh ladle on top.
