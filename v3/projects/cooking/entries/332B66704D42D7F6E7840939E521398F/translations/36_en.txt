　　　△Chapter 15: Cooking Sake Section
Egg sake　Crack eggs and gradually add cold sake,
　　　mix well, add a little salt, warm and serve.
　　　For one egg, add three cups of sake separately - this is good
Yam sake　Grate very white mountain yam finely,
　　　mix this well with cold sake until smooth,
　　　add a little salt and stir until properly warmed - this is good
Dove sake　Pound dove well and mix with sake, add a little miso
　　　to the pot, stir-fry until fox-colored, then add both dove
　　　and sake - this is good. Add a little sansho pepper powder, black pepper powder, or wild
　　　ginger - this is good. Can also stir-fry with soy sauce
Wing-joint sake　Finely pound the tips beyond the joints of pheasant wing feathers,
　　　add a little salt and a little sake, stir-fry, add any of the above
　　　spicy seasonings, warm the sake properly and serve.
　　　When eating the meat, adding a little soy sauce is good
Grasping sake　Take pheasant intestines and add a little thick miso,
　　　pound together well, then pierce each foot of one bird with skewers,
　　　stuff the pounded mixture between the toes
　　　and roast - this grips well. The inside also appears
