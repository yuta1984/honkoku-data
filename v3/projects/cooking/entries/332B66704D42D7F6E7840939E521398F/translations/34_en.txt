△ Chapter 13: Grilled Dishes Section
Hama-yaki (beach grilling)　For large sea bream, only scale and clean, sandwich between bamboo
　　　add katana-me (decorative cuts), sprinkle with salt and grill, then pour
　　　sake-salt sauce with shadow dropped and serve
Makuri-yaki (roll grilling)　Fillet sea bream, slice thinly, add
　　　salt and grill
Ara-shio-yaki (coarse salt grilling)　Apply only salt to crucian carp and grill,
　　　pour sauce of sake-salt with shadow dropped and serve
Kotori-yaki (small bird grilling)　Take crucian carp about 3-4 sun long and fillet into three pieces,
　　　skewer and apply sansho miso paste,
　　　then grill. Carp and catfish are also good

Ki-no-me-yaki (tree bud grilling)　Apply salt to dried squid, wrap in hot water leaves,
　　　then wrap over that in paper and steam-grill, remove
　　　and pour sauce over before serving
Kiji-yaki (pheasant-style grilling)　Cut tofu into small pieces, apply salt,
　　　add and grill
Shigi-yaki (snipe-style grilling)　Boil eggplant, cut at the right time,
　　　skewer and apply sansho miso paste, then grill
Sake-yaki (sake grilling)　For the sauce, use cloudy sake or clear sake
　　　with soy sauce added, squeeze in hot water vinegar and pour over;
　　　good to add fish roe and pour over white shellfish
Yaki-takenoko (grilled bamboo shoots)　Remove the nodes from bamboo shoots, put kamaboko
　　　and egg formed into balls inside, and grill with the skin
