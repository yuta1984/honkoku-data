There are oral instructions for when this is made.
Kudzu grilled mochi: One sho each of kudzu, water, and sugar, three
　　　colors, knead well together, form into mandarin-sized balls, grease the pot
　　　lightly with oil, and grill while turning frequently.
Kudzu mochi: Add one sho five go of water to one sho of kudzu powder and knead
　　　to prepare. For any mochi, sprinkle with soybean flour, salt, and sugar
　　　for good taste. Also, kudzu powder and bracken fern powder should be
　　　ground well with a medicine grinder before kneading.
Bracken fern mochi: Add one sho six or seven go of water to one sho of powder,
　　　dissolve well and knead. Same for the powder.
Snow mochi: Take one sho of regular rice and three go of glutinous rice, grind into
　　　fine powder, moisten with water on the outside, lay cloth in a steamer,
　　　sift in the rice powder and steam well. In between, add
　　　dried persimmons, chestnuts, torreya nuts, etc. To make it yellow,
　　　moisten the powder with gardenia juice.
Sugihara mochi: Also called "meguri." Finely steam sugihara paper,
　　　boil mountain yam leaves and remove the veins and stems, then knead flour made from
　　　six parts glutinous rice and four parts regular rice, boil, and pound the three colors together well
　　　at once. This is suitable as an offering to great ministers or lesser retainers
　　　during the summer doyo period in the sixth month.
Goji berry mochi: Boil goji berries, pound well, and squeeze out the juice,
 　　　then knead flour of four parts glutinous rice to six parts regular rice, boil and pound
　　　well. This can also be added directly.
Five-leaf aralia mochi: Same as goji berry mochi.
