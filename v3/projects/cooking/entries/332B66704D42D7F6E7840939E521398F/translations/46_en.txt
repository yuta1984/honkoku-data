Chimaki: This too uses flour in four-to-six ratio, knead softly with water,
　　　wrap in wild rice leaves or bamboo leaves and boil well.
　　　For yellow color, add gardenia juice, sugar, soybean flour, and salt.
Sasa mochi: Use the finest white regular rice, pound well into flour,
　　　taking the flour in three stages. First, pound roughly and
　　　sift, setting aside that flour. Second, pound well and sift
　　　finely. Then knead with water into small balls
　　　and put in pot to boil. Boil until it rises and sinks again.
　　　Remove and pound well in a mortar, then tear into various colors.
　　　For yellow use gardenia, for blue use mugwort juice. There are
　　　oral instructions for green soybean flour. Boiled leaves.
Gosho-sama mochi: Transmitted from Nambu lord. Four parts regular rice,
　　　six parts glutinous rice, grind well into flour, grate mountain yam and knead
　　　together, form into small flat pieces, boil well in miso soup,
　　　then serve only the mochi with boiled sugar poured over.
　　　Boil one sho of sugar with four go of water.
Konoe-sama snow mochi: Two ryo white atractylodes, one ryo poria,
　　　two ryo dioscorea, two ryo lotus seeds, two ryo coix seeds.
　　　Grind the above well into powder and mix with flour of four parts regular rice
　　　and six parts glutinous rice, grind with eight ryo of sugar. Mix everything well,
　　　lay cloth as usual and steam, then cut and serve.
　　　This is incomparable.
