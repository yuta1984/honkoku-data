Iri-yaki (stir-grilling)　Prepare duck in large pieces, pour soy sauce over and set aside,
　　　stir-fry the skin, sandwich the meat inside, and grill
　　　arranged in a single layer in a pot. If there is too little liquid,
　　　add a little of the soy sauce that was set aside
Sogi-yaki (shaved-wood grilling)　Prepare as above, arrange in a single layer
　　　on cedar shavings and grill

　　　△Chapter 14: Clear Soup Section
Unohana　Make diagonal cross cuts on the back of squid,
　　　cut finely, then cut to a good size,
　　　boil shiitake mushrooms and add nori seaweed as garnish to the dashi,
　　　add the topping, bring to a boil and serve as clear soup
Mino-ni (straw-coat simmering)　Crack eggs and catch with a ladle without breaking,
　　　put into boiling water. Various garnishes and broth prepared the same way
Oysters　Add salt at the right time, leaving some salt,
　　　add oysters and when it comes to a boil, serve as clear soup. If there is little broth,
　　　dashi or even water is acceptable. Sometimes prepared without adding salt.
　　　Adding sake-salt is good
Konowata (sea cucumber intestines)　Cut at the right time, add dashi to thin miso,
　　　when it comes to a boil add the intestines, serve the clear soup as is
Mikuni-ha　This is Noto seaweed. Prepare with dashi and soy sauce.
　　　Good to add river shrimp. Use powdered pepper as a flavor accent
Matsutake　Stir-fry refreshingly with aged sake, with sake poured over
