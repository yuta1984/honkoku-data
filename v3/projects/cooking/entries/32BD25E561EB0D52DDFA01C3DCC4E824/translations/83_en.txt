　　　Grilled White Fish (Yaki-shirauwo)
Arrange white fish on a net and grill, then flip the entire net over to grill the other side. Grill well so they don't fall apart, then after grilling, put the net into water and the fish will separate from it.

　　　Flower Matsutake (Hana-matsutake)
Remove the caps of matsutake mushrooms, cut only the stems to about six or seven bu in length, dry them, and when spring arrives, cut them into flower shapes, simmer until well-seasoned, slice into rounds and use.

　　　Brocade Sea Bream (Nishiki-dai)
Take a fresh medium-sized sea bream with scales intact, remove the gills and intestines, and peel the skin from the back side in one piece without tearing it. Leave it attached at the belly side, treat both front and back the same way, heat salt water to boiling, and cook the aforementioned sea bream in it.
