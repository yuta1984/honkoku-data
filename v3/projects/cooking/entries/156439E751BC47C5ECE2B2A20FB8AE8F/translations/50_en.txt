First, take egg whites, add powdered ice sugar in proportion, strain through fine cloth, put in a deep bowl, and whisk vigorously with thin bamboo skewers. The egg whites will expand considerably and gradually foam up. Spread this on a thin board like kamaboko, about 6 bu thick and flat, put in a steamer and steam. This steams quickly. Then remove it, and while hot, draw striped patterns with safflower juice, green vegetable juice, or gardenia juice. Then remove from the board, cut and tie it, or cut into thin strips - leave it to your preference. Put in chopstick containers and use for chopstick picking. One serves as finger food, another as confectionery accompaniment.

　　　Method for Cold Egg Yōkan
First, soak one stick of large red agar in water to soften it, put in a pot with two gō of water and boil, strain once quickly through cloth, put back in the pot and heat. Crack ten eggs into this, mix very well, and strain this through cloth as well. Then into the above, add fifty monme of brown sugar and a little sake. Strain this through cloth as well.
