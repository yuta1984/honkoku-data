Method for Uzura Eggs (Quail Eggs)
Use quail, dove, bulbul, skylark, snipe, or any other small bird. Chop the small bird very finely, put in a mortar, grind well like grinding conger eel, add a little aged sake, grind and spread like a fine paste. For one gō of this quantity, add ten eggs, breaking them in one by one, grinding together. Put this also in a bowl or box and steam. Steam well before using. Usage is the same as before. Especially good for tea ceremony cuisine.

　　Method for Corn Eggs
Take about three gō of winter-bleached corn flour, mix well with water, dissolve to the consistency of grated yam soup. Add ten eggs to this, mix very well together, then add about two gō of mirin wine, and steam in a steaming basket. This is also especially good for tea ceremony cuisine.
