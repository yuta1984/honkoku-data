Remove the skin, sprinkle a little wheat flour on the underside of the matsutake, crack an egg and add it little by little with a metal spoon, place it on a metal grill, and cook gently over a distant fire. When the egg starts to sizzle, add more egg in the same manner, and when it puffs up, put it in a steamer. Fill it with egg until it's full, add a little soy sauce, and steam. However, it's best to keep the fire under the steamer pot small.

After steaming for a while, take it out and cut it into rounds to serve. Put nanban-zu (spiced vinegar), iri-zake (seasoned sake), yuzu vinegar, or soy sauce in small sake cups and serve on a large inkstone lid for an excellent sake accompaniment.

Uses: dai-biki (formal serving), bird appetizers, portable tiered boxes, clear soups, flat plate dishes, sweets bowls, tea bowl miso dishes, sashimi, small plate dishes.
