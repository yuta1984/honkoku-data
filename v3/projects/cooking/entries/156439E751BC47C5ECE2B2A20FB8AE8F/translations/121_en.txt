Place the cut surface of the meat in the center of the previously pounded round piece, attaching it about 5 bu deep. Gather the edges of the round meat placed on the body from all around, and wrap and tie with paper cord about two layers thick. Stand the tail upright in the center. Put it in hot water and boil thoroughly. Let the drops fall, then dissolve red powder thickly in either ginger salt broth or plum vinegar. While the shrimp is still warm, dip it in this red liquid, turn it around, then lift and cool. It will look like a real peony flower. For usage: dai-hiki dishes, flat plate dishes, confection bowls, and anything else should be left to the cook's preference.
