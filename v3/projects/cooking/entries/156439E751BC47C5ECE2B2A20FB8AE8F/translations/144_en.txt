First, fillet the bonito into three pieces, scrape away the dark meat, and salt it heavily for about half a toki (1 hour). When pickling, wash off the salt thoroughly, and let it dry for a while. Mix salt with the sake lees and pickle like sushi. However, sprinkle salt on each piece of fish individually. If you want to use it quickly, apply a little pressure. It will be pickled in about one day. When serving, wash off the lees thoroughly, add a little vinegar to soy sauce, bring to a boil, and sprinkle with grated daikon radish before serving. For sashimi, quickly pour hot water over the bonito, cool in water, dry again, and arrange in overlapping slices. For ankake (thick sauce dishes), cut into slightly thick overlapping pieces, about six slices together, and briefly blanch in hot water before using. For vinegared dishes and daihibiki, cut diagonally, skewer with green bamboo skewers, and serve with sanshō miso or wasabi miso.
