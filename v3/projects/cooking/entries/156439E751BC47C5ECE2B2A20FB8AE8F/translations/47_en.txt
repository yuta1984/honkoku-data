For raw pickling, if you pickle fresh eggs in the aforementioned sake lees, after about fifteen days you can remove them and make them into hard-boiled eggs.

　　　Method for Making Strained Powdered Eggs
First, remove the yolk from hard-boiled eggs, knead finely by hand, place in a roasting pan over distant heat, gradually add old sake while roasting well in the pan, then grind thoroughly in a mortar and sieve through a fine sieve. Mix this with warmed sake, combining white sugar and the aforementioned sieved egg in equal parts, and drink.
This is primarily a miraculous medicine that regulates the spleen and stomach and increases vital energy. For culinary uses: sprinkle this over hot hard-boiled eggs and serve. It is also good for tea cuisine. Sprinkle over clear soup or over soup for sipping and serve. Also sprinkle over rice and serve. In Nagasaki, they sprinkle this powder over daikon rice and serve it with clear soup.
