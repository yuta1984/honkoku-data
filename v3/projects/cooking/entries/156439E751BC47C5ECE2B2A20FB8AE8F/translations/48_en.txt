First, crack about five fresh eggs, remove the whites, and use only the yolks. Take one gō of high-quality white miso, grind it well, spread it on the bottom of a metal mizunō, remove the bean residue, mix the aforementioned miso with half a gō of aged sake and blend well, then roast this mixture over charcoal fire, stirring constantly. Then add the aforementioned five egg yolks directly into this mixture, stirring gently with a small spoon to prevent burning. Next, add both the white and yolk of a chicken egg and cook thoroughly, then add one gō of black sesame seeds that have been roasted and ground well, and also add fifty monme of white sugar. If this is also prepared during the cold season, it will be particularly excellent. Store it in a jar, and it can be preserved until the dog days of summer the following year. For usage: mix with carp pickled in miso, which is good. Also, finely cut thin slices of sea bream and mix, which is good. Also mix with roasted chestnuts, prepared udo (Japanese spikenard), matsutake mushrooms, pine shoots, and anything else as appropriate. The aforementioned miso is medicine for vital energy.
