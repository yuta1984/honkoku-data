　　　Method for Eggs Pickled in Amazake (Sweet Rice Wine)
One: If prepared during the cold winter period, it will keep until the following year. Make amazake thick and after about three days, hard-boil the eggs and peel them while hot. Pickle them in this amazake. Use about fifteen eggs per one shō (1.8 liters) of amazake. Then, at the end of the New Year period, boil the amazake once, let it cool well, add the eggs again, cover tightly, and keep away from wind. From around March, take them out, place in small dishes, and serve sprinkled with white sugar. In Nagasaki, honey pickles and similar items are of this type. This serves as a tonic medicine.

　　　Method for Egg Ushioni (Salt-Simmered Eggs)
One: Prepare kombu dashi and season with roasted salt. In a separate pot, boil water and crack eggs in one by one to poach them. Put warmed sake in a bowl, briefly dip the eggs in it, then lift them out with a mesh ladle, place in serving dishes, and add the kombu dashi broth before serving. For clear soup, Asakusa nori seaweed makes a good accompaniment.
