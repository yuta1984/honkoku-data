When it is well-cooked, take it out and let it cool, then break it from the bamboo joint side and remove the egg. Cut this to an appropriate width and length, skewer it on bamboo sticks, lightly heat it over fire, and serve with wasabi miso, Japanese pepper (sanshō) miso, or plum kernel miso. Kudzu starch sauce is also exceptionally good. For flat plate presentation and other uses, follow the same methods as before.

　　　Method for Making Egg Senbei (Rice Crackers)
Take the whites of about five eggs, add the yolks of about three eggs, mix well together, add about twenty monme of white sugar, strain and squeeze through fine cloth, then in a tamago-nabe (egg pan), pour in with a metal spoon, spread well and bake. Sprinkle a little poppy seeds or black sesame on top, bake without burning, making them thin and round, cut standing, put in a chopstick container, and use. Whether made large or small and round, it should be left to the intended use.
