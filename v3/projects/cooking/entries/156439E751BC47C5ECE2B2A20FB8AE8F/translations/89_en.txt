First, fillet fresh carp from any season into three pieces, remove the scales, ensure the flesh has no moisture, soak it briefly in warmed sake, then remove it and let the sake drip off. Use regular miso for this. Into about one sho (1.8 liters) of this miso, add one cup of premium soy sauce and one cup of aged sake, mix thoroughly, add the carp, pack miso well both above and below, place kelp on the top lid, put a wooden lid over that, weight it down with stones for about three days. This timing is good for spring; for summer, one day and night; for autumn, two days and one night; for winter, about five days.

Methods of use: first, as sashimi; second, in soup; third, the second soup should be clear broth; fourth, as accompaniment for sake; fifth, as individual servings; sixth, in clear soup with garnish ingredients; seventh, for grilled dishes, use light soy sauce for glazing. Cut according to the dish. For grilled items in tea ceremony cuisine, rich soy sauce is good. ○Also, wash off the miso well and on a good sunny day, string it up and dry it thoroughly. When needed, shave it like bonito flakes, add to thin miso, simmer, add first-frost kelp and serve - this is also good. There are many other recipe variations, but these are well-known methods. ○First, this is medicine for people with cold constitution. It is also kidney medicine.
