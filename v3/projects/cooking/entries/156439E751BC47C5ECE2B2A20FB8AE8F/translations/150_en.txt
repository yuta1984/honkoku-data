How to Make Go-hai-zuke (Five Cup Pickle)

First, take two cups of sake, two cups of vinegar, and one cup of salt. In this liquid, put thirty grains of mustard in a cloth bag, place it at the bottom of the container, and pickle any kind of fish.

　　　How to Make Oranda-zuke (Dutch Pickle)

First, boil one shō of vinegar, and while hot, add three gō of salt, then add whole mustard seeds for pickling. The pickled items are salted mackerel or sardines, or lotus root, eggplant, burdock root, Japanese ginger, or myōga buds. Alternatively, it's also good to mix a little sweet cooking sake with plum vinegar for pickling.

　　　Salted Sardine Dried Moromi Pickle

First, take 100 monme of finest white miso, three gō of sake lees, one gō of salt, and five gō of rice bran, roast them and mix well together. Pickle so that the sardine's belly doesn't split. The sardine's salt should be mild. When using, wash off the dried moromi well. Then steam the sardines and use with Japanese pepper miso, garlic miso, or if using kudzu starch sauce, with grated ginger, grilled plain, pickled and grilled, or as a side dish - match with anything appropriate. Or cut into five-bu pieces for clear soup, with yuzu or udo finely chopped as garnish, or pepper.
