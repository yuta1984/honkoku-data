　　　Section on Seasoned Miso for Dressed Dishes
　　　Method for Yamabuki (Golden Yellow) Miso
First, take only the yolks from about ten hard-boiled eggs and grind them in a mortar. Add one bowl of white miso, two cups of mirin, and five bu of roasted salt, and grind them together thoroughly. The way to use this is to stuff it into the belly of fish paste, small sea bream, or into kisogo fish, pigeons, quail, snipe, small ducks - remove all the bones and stuff this mixture where the bones were, then steam. It can also be used for dressed dishes and other applications as you see fit, for whatever comes to mind. For vegetarian dishes, use fresh yuba instead of eggs. This too should be prepared with miso, roasted salt, and sugar in proper proportion, but it should not be watery.
