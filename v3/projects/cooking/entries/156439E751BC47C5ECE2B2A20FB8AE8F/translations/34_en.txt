　　Method for Yose-tamago (Steamed Egg Custard)
Break eggs and beat well. If the egg quantity is one gō, add one gō and five shaku of dashi stock, mix well together, put in a bowl or box, and steam. The proper steaming is when there is no liquid on top, indicating the center is well steamed. Usage: flat dishes, sweet bowls, taihei dishes, notsuhei, tea bowls with kuzu sauce, Nagasaki-style dishes and soups. For sashimi, cool well first, then arrange in layered cuts.

　　Method for Teika Eggs
Break eggs and beat well. For one gō of this quantity, add about one small shell cup of bean flour. Using five or six bamboo skewers, whip vigorously in a deep bowl. When whipped, it will rise considerably and foam up. When foaming, put in a steaming basket and steam. The steamed egg will have honeycomb-like holes. Scoop and serve with a gold spoon. Usage is the same as before.
