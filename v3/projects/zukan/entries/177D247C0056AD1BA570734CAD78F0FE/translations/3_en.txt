From "Jiuhuang Bencao" (Famine Relief Materia Medica):
　　　Spoon-head Vegetable (Hitōzai)　Thousand Spear Plantain　　I say that the spoon-head vegetable sends up flower stems in both spring and autumn seasons.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　In spring, flowers like those in this illustration bloom; in autumn, the
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　buds attempt to open but ultimately do not open. The stems number
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　in the dozens and resemble spears, hence the name "thousand spears." The details of how
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　one root produces flower stems in two seasons are recorded
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　in Volume 3 of the Autumn section.
