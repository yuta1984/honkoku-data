　　　　　　　　　The sacred spider lily of Kumano Bay, layered a hundredfold,
　　　　　　　　　such are my thoughts of love, yet I cannot meet you directly

　　　　　　　　In the Senkaku Commentary, it states that hamayu (beach lily) is a small grass resembling banana plants. Its stems
　　　　　　　　are layered countless times. When peeled, it is white with
　　　　　　　　separations like paper. For grand banquets of ministers, it is said that
　　　　　　　　they would order it from Mikumano Bay to wrap
　　　　　　　　bird legs (a delicacy).
