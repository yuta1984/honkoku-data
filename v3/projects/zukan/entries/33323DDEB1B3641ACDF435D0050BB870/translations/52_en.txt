Even flower specialists say it is difficult to distinguish. Edo cherry has long stems, with buds that are crimson when beginning to bloom, becoming light pink when they open. The layering of the flower rings is thick, the petals droop, and it blooms late.

Edo-Hōrin-ji
[Illustration]
○Igan-sai says: It resembles Hōrin-ji but is especially abundant. The thickness of the petal layering is deeper than that of Edo cherry. The leaves are also thick, large, and beautiful. This is a tree that originated from Hōrin-ji temple in Musashi Province. There are also separately called Hōzen-ji and Taizan-fukun.
