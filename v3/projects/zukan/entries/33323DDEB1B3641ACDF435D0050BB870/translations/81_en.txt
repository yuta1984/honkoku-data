Ōzen Cherry (Ōzen-zakura)
[Illustration]
○ Donnei says: "It is slightly smaller than the Hōraji cherry. The color is deep red. The stem is long and the tips of the petals are thin. It has a thousand petals. The flower resembles the shape of the yamabuki (Japanese rose) flower. The etymology of this name is not clear."

Ise Cherry (Ise-zakura)
[Illustration]
○ Igan-sai says: "It is double-flowered, deep purple-red in color. The base of the inner petals is white."
○ According to Kassho-ō's Cherry Catalog: "The color is crimson and it does not open very much. This flower blooms the latest. It was named Ise with the meaning of being close to Owari."
