Sen'ōge (Lychnis senno)　Produces seedlings in spring from old roots. The form resembles kansara (ganpi) but the leaves are smaller and deep green with fine white hairs. In the sixth and seventh months the stem height reaches about 2 shaku, with nodes on the stem where two leaves grow opposite each other. Several flowers cluster at the branch tips. Five petals, more beautiful in color than ganpi, like vermillion. The petal tips are deeply split appearing like narrow petals. There are also white flowers, white-red variegated, lip-red, yellow, brown, and double-flowered varieties, with many other cultivars besides these. There is also Ogura-sennō, a small plant that flowers early. There is also one variety called Matsumoto (Chinese name: jiǎn chūn luó). Also called Matsumoto-sen'ōge. It flowers earlier than ganpi, beginning to bloom in the fourth month. Deep red color, and also dozens of varieties in light red, white, yellow, mixed colors, double flowers, etc. All are cultivars of ganpi.

Primary properties
△ Nature, flavor, and therapeutic uses are the same as ganpi
