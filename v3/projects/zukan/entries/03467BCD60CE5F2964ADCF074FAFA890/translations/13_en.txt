Kayatsurigusa　Grows in spring and withers in winter. Very abundant in fields and roadsides. The leaf shape resembles Suge
　but smaller, width one bu or one and a half bu, length eight to nine sun. Those in fertile soil
　reach one to two shaku, forming a triangular shape. Deep green color with luster.
　In summer, stems emerge up to one shaku, those in fertile soil reach over two shaku. Triangular
　with three leaves growing at the top. There are long and short varieties, the long ones are longer than the leaves attached to the stem.
　Among them, spikes form and small flowers cluster together. Very similar to Igusa flowers.
　Brown in color and not attractive to look at. Same family but different species from Kōbushi.
　Kōbushi has small shoots with root tubers that don't wither. Mosasō has large shoots
　with no root tubers and withers.
　　　　　Main properties
　　△Sweet taste, slightly cold, non-toxic. Disperses qi stagnation and reduces phlegm-heat.
