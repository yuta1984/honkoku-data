According to Yamato Honzō:
　　　　Aoki (ahaki)　Also called　Ten-thousand-year branch
　　　　　　　　　　Also　Winter green
　　　　　This flower is called
　　　　　　Ten-thousand-year flower
　　　　　　　Also aoki

　　　　　　　　　　　　　Peach-leaf coral is large-leaf aoki, small-leaf aoki,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　and long-leaf aoki are also of the same type. Snow coral
　　　　　　　　　　　　　is called frost-descent aoki in common parlance. Its leaves are mostly
　　　　　　　　　　　　　large-leafed, not variegated but solitary growing ones

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Mizunoe-Uma year, Kōsen month, 6th day

According to Wakan Sansai Zue, plectrum plant:
Buddhist monks take its stem to make
staffs for peach lanterns. Since it repels mosquitoes and flies, it's called
life-protecting grass. Its taste is sweet. According to Shi Kuang,
when the year desires abundance, sweet grass grows first <<glossed: sweet grass refers to shepherd's purse>>
when the year desires hardship, bitter grass grows first <<glossed: bitter grass refers to draba>>
It's one of the seven herbs in the vegetable porridge of the 7th day of the first month.
It bears triangular fruit, not yet large, with a narrow base resembling
a shamisen plectrum. When children rub two of them together,
they make sound, hence called three-string grass.
