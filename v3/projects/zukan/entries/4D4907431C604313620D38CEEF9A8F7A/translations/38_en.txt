According to the Wakan Sansai Zue, in the section on wet grasses:
　　　　Japanese Forsythia (Warenkiyō)　The Honzō states that small forsythia grows in southern regions. What
　　　　　　　　exists now in Japan is this variety. The original forsythia
　　　　　　　　once existed but no longer does. Throughout all provinces it is simply called forsythia.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　According to my investigation, what the Honzō calls small forsythia
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　is indeed grass. It is what is called grass giyō.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　The harmonious explanation of Japanese and Chinese forsythia
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　is this. Japanese forsythia should be examined.
