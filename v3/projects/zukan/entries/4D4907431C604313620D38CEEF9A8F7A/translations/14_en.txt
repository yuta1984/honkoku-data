According to Yamato Honzō:
　　In the Nihongi (Chronicles of Japan), in the thirteenth year of Emperor Tenmu,
　　third month, a person from Yoshino named Umae no Atai
　　presented a bow made of white camellia (tsubaki). The Engi-
　　shiki also writes tsubaki as
　　"kaisekiru" (sea pomegranate). The Jun Wamyō-shō
　　is the same, saying that because its leaves are thick, it means
　　"thick-leaf tree."

　　　Tsubaki is a character with the tree radical, and the tall tree type
　　　called chochun is completely different from kaisekiru
　The Man'yōshū, imperial codes, and Wamyō-shō all
