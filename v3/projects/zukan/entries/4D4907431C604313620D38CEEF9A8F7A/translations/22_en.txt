　　　According to Honzō Kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica), onions have leaves that grow straight outward with hollow centers; what grows in spring is called onion needle,
　　　the leaves are called onion green, the outer layer onion robe, the stem onion white, and the mucus in the leaves is called
　　　onion. They can be seeded and cultivated.
　　　Winter onion (also called compassionate onion, frozen onion, or large palace onion) flourishes in late winter
　　　Chinese onion (also called original onion) has actually hard stems, forms clusters of blue-white color before flowering in spring
　　　According to Wakan Sansai Zue commentary, because the leaves are round with no branches, it is called hitomoji (one character)
　　　I believe winter onion and Chinese onion are originally the same; seeds are planted in the 10th month, leaves harvested in the 1st month are eaten, and can be
　　　cut repeatedly, hence commonly called kari-negi (cut onion). This is the so-called Chinese onion. After experiencing frost, the taste becomes extremely
　　　sweet and delicious, with warming properties that can dispel cold qi. This is the so-called winter onion. Though one plant, it appears like many varieties
