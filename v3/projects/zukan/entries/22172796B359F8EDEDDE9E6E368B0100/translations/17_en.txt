Recorded in the "Zōho Tashikihen" (Supplementary Multi-Knowledge Compilation), Mountain Herbs section:
　　　Epimedium　Japanese name: Umukina, also called
　　　　　　　　　　Yamadori-gusa (mountain bird grass)
　　　Alternative names: Senreihi (Chinese Pharmacopoeia), Hōjō-sō (Nikka),
　　　Kijō-sō (same), Senryōkin (same), Kankeikin
　　　(same), Ōrenso (same), Sanshikyūyō-sō (Illustrated Classic),
　　　Gōzen (Basic Classic)





The "Wamyō Ruijushō" states:
　　　Senrei-sō　From Tao Yinju's Pharmacopoeia
　　　(Yamadori-gusa)

　　　　　　　　Tao Yinju's pharmacopoeia commentary states: Epimedium (Japanese name umukina, also called yamadori-gusa)
　　　　　　　　When sheep eat this herb, they mate a hundred times in one day, hence the name. Another name is Gōzen.
　　　　　　　　Su Jing said: What is commonly called Senrei-sō is this plant.
　　　　　　　　In Chinese it is called Senrei-sō (Japanese name) miratakari-gusa
　　　　　　　　The "Yamato Honzō" states: Epimedium, also called Ikari-sō (anchor grass)
　　　　　　　　The "Chikinshō" on Epimedium flowers states: (kumokiri-gusa, ikari-gusa)


　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　On the fourth day of early Koshō in the year Kinoto-Tori,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　drawn from life after breaking off a branch
