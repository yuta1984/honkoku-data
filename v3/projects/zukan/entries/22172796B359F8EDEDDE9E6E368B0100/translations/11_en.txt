　　　　　　　　　The "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia), Mountain Fruits section, states:
　　　　　　　　　　　　Cherry (Yutsuramume)　Nightingale-bird peach　Containing peach　Jing peach
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　The "Wamyōshō" says "Nightingale fruit: Uku hisu no ki no mi"
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Commonly called: Yusura plum

　　　　　　　　　"Mōshi Hinbutsu Zukō" (Illustrated Study of Plants and Objects in the Book of Songs)
　　　　　　　　　　　　Yū (Melancholy)　Garden plum 〇 Corresponds to Yutsuramume


　　　　　　In my opinion, the "Honzō" reads it as "yusura" and 
　　　　　　reads mountain cherry as "niwasakura," or sometimes reads it as "niwaaume."
　　　　　　The forms of these two plants are largely similar with minor differences, yet their species are the same.
　　　　　　Using "nightingale fruit" for "yusuume" in the "Wamyōshō" is
　　　　　　an error. Nightingale fruit is the fruit of kirishi (Lycium).
