The Zōho Tashikihen (Expanded Multilingual Dictionary), section on five fruits, states:
　　　Apricot　Japanese name: Karamomo (Chinese peach), commonly called　Kanbai (sweet plum)
　　　　　　　　Also called Anzu

The Wamyō Ruijushō states:
　　　Apricot　The Bencao states "apricot"　The upper pronunciation follows the "sa" line
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Japanese name is Karamomo

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Year of Kinoto-tori, Jiāzhōng (2nd month), 16th day
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Breaking a branch to draw one illustration

Those with sweet, sandy flesh are called "sha-kyō" (sand apricots)　　Those yellow and tart are called "baikō" (plum fragrance)
Those green tinged with yellow are called "ri-kyō" (plum apricots)　　Those large like pears, yellow and resembling oranges are called
　　　　　　　　　　　　　"kin-kyō" (golden apricots)

　　　　How did they first begin to emit fragrance in Japan?
　　　　　　　The flowers of Karamomo that are not native to our land　Minister Kinugasa
