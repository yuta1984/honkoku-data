The "Zōho Tashiki-hen" (Expanded Multi-knowledge Compilation) in its section on fragrant plants states:
Peony (botan) Japanese name: fukami-gusa (deep-beauty grass), also
　　　　　　called: hatuka-gusa (twenty-day grass)
　　　　　Alternative names: Shuko (Materia Medica), Rokui (same text),
　　　　　Hyakuryō-kin (Tang Materia Medica), Moku-shakuyaku
　　　　　(Compendium of Materia Medica), Kaō (Flower King)


　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Three varieties of peonies, Jishō 3rd year, kinoto-tori, 25th day
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Painted from life at Kagaku-dō




In Ouyang Yongshu's (Ouyang Xiu) peony catalog,
he recorded over ninety varieties
of peony names. Today, the varieties
possessed in Japan are extremely numerous and difficult to depict with the brush.
