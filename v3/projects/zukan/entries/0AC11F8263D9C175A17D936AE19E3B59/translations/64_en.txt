【Left Page】
Matsu-ōji (Pine mushroom)

Illustration from the Kinshi (Mushroom History). According to the same book, it grows on the roots or branch tips of old pine trees. Its shape resembles matsutake, with the cap spread and facing upward. It is white with a slight yellow tinge. Small ones are one to two sun in size, while large ones approach one shaku. It is resilient and smooth, slightly bitter with a pine fragrance. It has the efficacy of stopping abdominal pain and diarrhea. There are also specimens with yellow on the stem and black spots on the cap.
