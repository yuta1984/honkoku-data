Yabu shimeji (Forest shimeji)

An illustration from the Kinshi (Mushroom History). According to the same book, it resembles shimeji but has a thinner quality, with white gills and a reddish-brown cap surface, hollow center, wilts easily, and grows abundantly in bamboo groves during summer and autumn.

【Left Page】
Shimo shimeji (Frost shimeji)

An illustration from the Shin'yō Kinpu (Shin'yō Mushroom Catalog). It grows in shaded mountain areas after frost falls. The taste is said to be inferior to shimeji mushrooms.
