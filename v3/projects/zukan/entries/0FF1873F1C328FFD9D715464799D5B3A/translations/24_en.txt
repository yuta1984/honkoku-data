　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kinoto-Tori year, 6th month, middle, 9th day

The "Honzō Wakai" states:
　　　Patchouli　Alternative names: Torōbakō appears in the Śūraṅgama Sūtra
　　　　　　　　　Tama-torō incense appears in the Lotus Sutra
　　　　　　　　　Hattara incense appears in the Suvarṇaprabhāsa Sūtra
　　　　　　　　　Kasan incense appears in the Nirvana Sutra
　　　　　　Japanese name: Kawamitori

　　　　　　　　　　　　　The same book states:
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Duan Chengshi's "Youyang Zazu" and Emperor Yuan of Liang's
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　"Jinlou Zi" record that people of the southern countries say that the five fragrances
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　all come from one tree: its roots are all sandalwood, the nodes are agarwood,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　the flowers are clove, the leaves are patchouli, and the resin is
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　frankincense. Formerly it was classified under trees, but now
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　what is used is an herb. The stem is square with nodes in the middle.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　The leaves are like eggplant, or similar to ○ leaves but
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　thinner. They resemble perilla leaves and are blue-green. Cotton
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　leaves, eggplant leaves, and ramie leaves all closely resemble it, etc.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　I say that this explanation corresponds well with the "Chijinshō"
