Kanari states:
　　　Returning-soul grass
　　　　　　Grows in the mountains of the eastern barbarian sea islands
　　　　　　The barbarians harvest it, dry it in the sun,
　　　　　　and bring it to China as trade goods

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kinoto-Tori year, 6th month, 25th day
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Field garden, one drawing of broken stem

The same book states:
　　　Tobacco is a foreign dialect term. Some say when it first came to Japan, the powder was processed and cut from Tamba province, hence
　　　it was called Tamba powder (tabako). This theory is false.
Chen Fuyao of the Qing Kangxi period wrote in "Flower Mirror":
　　Smoke flower　One name: Tanbako
　　　　　　　　Initially appeared overseas and seeds were transmitted to Zhangquan. Now found
　　　　　　　　everywhere. The plant resembles Harufu-rō (ōokarasu) but is larger. It blooms with purple-white fine
　　　　　　　　flowers. When the leaves age, they are dried in the sun and cut thread-like, then beautifully
　　　　　　　　named
　　　　　　　　　　　Golden thread smoke
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　One name: Returning-soul smoke　One name:担不帰
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　People happily buy the smoke and inhale it
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Even when falling down drunk, they do not resent it

　　　　　　　　　　　In the "Tashikihen," calling henbane "tobacco" refers to
　　　　　　　　　　　a different species, with the Japanese name utaitoko. Because it
　　　　　　　　　　　makes people sleep well, it is included in opium medicine. Commonly called sleeping medicine.
