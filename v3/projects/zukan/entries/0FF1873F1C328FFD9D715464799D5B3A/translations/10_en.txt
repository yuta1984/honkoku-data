Listed in the soft and smooth plants section of "Zōho Tashiki-hen"
　　　Moku-shuku (Alfalfa)　Japanese name is Ō-nomi (large flea)　Alternative name is Moku-soku (wood millet)　In Kōmoku it's called Kōfū-sō (light wind grass)

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　1st day of the first month of summer, year of the Wood Rooster
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Broke a stem among the grass and drew from life

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　According to "Yamato Honzō," the alfalfa grass is probably
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Sendai bush clover. Sendai bush clover is
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　different from this, however, there are
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　differences in the size of the grass forms and they resemble each other well.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　This theory is not far off the mark.

According to "Wamyō Ruijushō"
　　　Moku-shuku (alfalfa)　Su Jing's herbal commentary says
　　　　　　Moku-shuku　Two pronunciations: moku and shuku
　　　　　　Japanese name　Ohohi

　　　According to "Nōsei Zensho," alfalfa leaves resemble pea leaves but are smaller, with three
　　　leaves growing in one place, and purple flowers blooming in between, though this is uncertain.
