The "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia) in the section on moisture-loving plants states:
Toad lily (toketsu-sō) - commonly called by its original name
《hototogisu》 Details unclear
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Listed in "Yamato Honzō"
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　(Japanese Materia Medica)
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　in the flowering plants section

The "Bukō Sanbutsu-shi" (Record of Musashi Province Products) states:
Oil-spot grass (hototogisu)

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　On the fifth day of early eighth month, Hinoe-Inu year
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Looking toward Mount Dōkan, broke off a stem
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　and drew one illustration

According to my (Mōri Baien's) observation, there are two types of toad lily flowers.
The white-colored ones with light purple spots are called mountain toad lily (yama-toketsu). 
Examining the illustration in the "Chikin-shō" (Botanical Manual), 
what was depicted here is the mountain toad lily. Those that are simply called 
"toad lily" are slightly different, but they are probably not 
separate species.
