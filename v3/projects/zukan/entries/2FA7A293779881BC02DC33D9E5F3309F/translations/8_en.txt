Mountain Ginger (Sankyō)
　Hanamyōgai (?)

Found in Tōshū Takada District as well as Seishū, Kishū, Yoshū, Sunshū and other provinces. The leaves resemble ginger and have slight hairs. The stems are reddish-purple in color, over one shaku (30cm) in height, withering in winter. Flowers bloom between the stems. The shape resembles kumatakeran but is shorter and smaller, and red in color. The fruit is round and red. The roots resemble ginger but are thin and hard.
