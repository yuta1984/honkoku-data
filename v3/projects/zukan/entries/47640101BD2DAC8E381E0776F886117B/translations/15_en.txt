According to the "Chikinsho Furoku" (Supplementary Records of Earthly Brocade):
　　　Jiran (Orchid species) - requires careful investigation

According to various books:
　　　Tato-so (Summer Calanthe) - one variety

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Someone says that what is called suzuran (lily of the valley) is a different plant.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　It is also called kiran or kozanran. Its form is quite different. It has yellow flowers,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　and its leaves grow attached to the stem. Is jiran also called suzuran? There are
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　several varieties of tato-so, with those blooming in spring, summer, and autumn
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　being called respectively spring kairou-kon and summer kairou-kon according
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　to their flowering season.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Copied on the 13th day of the 5th month, year of Hinoto-i

　　　Small Rock Chrysanthemum (ko-iwagiku)
　　　　Someone says:
　　　　　Stone Chicken Intestine (iwa-yomena) - common name

　　　　　　　　　　This plant grows among rocks in the mountains, and its leaves do not wither in winter.
　　　　　　　　　　The plant's form and flower-leaves resemble those of chicken intestine (yomena).
　　　　　　　　　　This plant is not found in various botanical texts. It may be a type of rock chrysanthemum
　　　　　　　　　　as mentioned in the "Yamato Honzo."

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Copied on the 9th day of the 5th month, year of Hinoto-i
