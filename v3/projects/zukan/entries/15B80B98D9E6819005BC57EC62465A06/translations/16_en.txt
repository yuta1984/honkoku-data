[Miyagino Bush Clover (Miyagino-hagi)] Flowers bloom in July and August. The flower colors include white, purple, light colors, and mottled varieties.
The flowers hang long and drooping, blooming like princess wisteria. There are also yellow flowers, but these are the reverse side of white ones. Mountain bush clover also has white, purple, and light-colored flowers. Miyagino grows to about one jō in length, while mountain bush clover grows to about six shaku.

[Morning Glory (Asagao)]
Flowers bloom from April-May through July-August. The flower shape resembles bindweed and the leaves are triangular.
There are many flowers, numbering thirty to forty. Colors include lapis lazuli, white, light red, and pale yellow, with mottled and絞り patterns as well. Korean morning glory has lapis lazuli-colored flowers; when seeds are sown and grown, flowers bloom once two leaves emerge. Also called dwarf morning glory or water morning glory, it is a mountain plant. The original name is Kengo (morning glory).

[Bindweed] Flowers bloom in April and May. The color is light red, and there are also white varieties with snow-white large blooms.
Morning glories are at their peak in the morning, but bindweed flowers bloom all day long. Nanking bindweed has cherry-colored small blooms. The leaves are like buckwheat, and the flowers resemble morning glories but are slightly smaller. It grows vines in fields and mountains.

[Evening Glory (Yugao)] Flowers bloom in June and July. The flower shape is like morning glory, with large, round leaves. The flower color is white. The original name is Ko (bottle gourd). Also called Hisago Evening Glory. This also has white flowers. Both varieties bloom in the evening. Also called Senari. Hisago are small field crops.

[Daruma Chrysanthemum
Hotei Chrysanthemum  
Valley Chrysanthemum
Satsuma Chrysanthemum]
The leaves are thick with fine hair. The plant stem is thick, and the flower shape is like single chrysanthemums with light purple flowers blooming at the leaf bases. There are also white flowers. The name derives from being soft like spread felt, with leaves present in all seasons. Also called Daruma grass, Hotei chrysanthemum, or Satsuma chrysanthemum. Blooms from August to September; a mountain valley plant.

[Gentian (Rindou) <<July to August>>] The flower shape blooms upright. The leaves are like bamboo grass.
Flower colors come in four varieties: white, light crimson, purple, and lapis lazuli. Found in mountain fields and plains.

[Smilax (Bakkatsu)]
A type of thorn bush. The leaves are round like small persimmon leaves, with five central veins in each leaf. Leaves fall in winter and emerge in spring. Has red berries in autumn. Commonly called Sankiraï. Also called Sarutori-ibara, but that is incorrect. Sarutori-ibara has leaves shaped like Japanese pagoda tree leaves, with turmeric-colored flowers. It grows one to two shaku tall with spreading growth. Also called Kame-ibara. Found along riverbanks, in mountains and valleys, together with wild roses and bamboo grass gentian. Flowers bloom in May, with red berries in September and October.
