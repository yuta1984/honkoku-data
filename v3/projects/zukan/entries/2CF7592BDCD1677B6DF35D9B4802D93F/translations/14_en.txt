【Right Page】
Yamato Honzō states:
　　Tenmyōsei (Carpesium abrotanoides)
　　　The seeds are called kakushitsu
　　　Japanese name is Hamatakana
　　　Also called Inoshirikusa, commonly
　　　called Yabutabako. Its leaves
　　　resemble tobacco

Honzō Wagekai states:
　　Kakushitsu

Alternative names: Tenmyōsei, Tenmansei (Heavenly turnip)
　　　Tenmonsei, Bakkukyō (Barley phrase ginger)
　　　Senjōran (Toad orchid), Gama-ran (Toad blue)
　　　Tonshu (Pig head) 〈Reading: Teirō〉, Katsuroku-sō (Live deer grass)
　　　Ryūkaku grass, Shūmen (Wrinkled face) grass, Boshiyokai (Sow mustard)
　　　The root is named tongyūshitsu, the seed is named kakushitsu
　　　Japanese names:
　　　　　Hamatakana
　　　　　Inunoshiri
　　　　　Inoshirikusa
　　　Tenmyōsei is also named chisō (ground cabbage)
　　　Its leaves resemble sō (Chinese cabbage), the seedlings are green
　　　in color with a fox-like smell, which
　　　matches well with this plant
