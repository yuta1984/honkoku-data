Monkshood (uzu) (Torikabuto) From "Compendium of Materia Medica"
　　　Extremely poisonous. There are two types: those that grow wild in mountains are called grass monkshood (yama-torikabuto). The poison is extremely
　　　strong. In spring, shoots emerge from perennial roots. Height is 3-4 shaku (approximately 90-120cm). The stem is weak and does not stand upright. The leaves
　　　branch and grow alternately. In autumn, flowers bloom between each leaf. Deep blue-green color, generally in clusters of
　　　two or three flowers. The shape roughly resembles a court musician's bird helmet. There are also deep purple and white varieties. The white
　　　variety is called "hanazuru." Those currently cultivated in homes are called river monkshood (kabutosou). The poison is
　　　somewhat weaker than grass monkshood. The leaves are thick, deep green with a glossy appearance. The stem is strong and straight―
