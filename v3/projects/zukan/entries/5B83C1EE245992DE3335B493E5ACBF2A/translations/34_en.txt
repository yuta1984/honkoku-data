Enbi (Iris) [Ichijutsu] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It is poisonous. It is cultivated in gardens everywhere. The leaves do not wither even through winter and produce new leaves in spring. They resemble the young leaves of Shakkan (Belamcanda) and are soft, not standing upright. From the center it extends stems and opens six-petaled purple flowers. There are also white-flowered varieties, which are one type of this species.

Gyūhen (Aconitum) [Reijinsō] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It is poisonous. It grows beside mountain ravines. The leaves resemble those of Sōuzutō (Yamatorikabuto) and have serrated edges. In summer it extends stems with alternately arranged leaves, and in autumn opens flowers at the stem tips. They are pale purple in color and form fruits after flowering.
