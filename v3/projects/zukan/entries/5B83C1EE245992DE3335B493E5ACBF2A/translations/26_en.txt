Jinma (Stinging Nettle) [Irakusa] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It has great toxicity. It grows in shaded areas in the mountains. Height is two to three shaku. The leaves resemble ramie (choma) [karamushi], are deep green in color, and grow in opposite pairs. The stems and leaves have hair-like spines. Flowers grow between the leaves, resembling ramie. When the spines prick a person, they cause pain and swelling. Fish die immediately upon eating the juice of this plant.

[Upper portion obscured by attached note, difficult to read] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It has toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. Leaves are three-pointed and light green in color. Flowers bloom from summer to autumn. The flowers have five points, are blue-green in color with white tubular parts. After flowering, it produces fruit with three longitudinal ridges containing seeds inside. White ones are called white morning glory (haku-ken-gyu), black ones are called black morning glory (koku-ken-gyu). No insects grow on these leaves.

[Seeds are] pressed to make lamp oil, which keeps all insects away. Cotton seeds are also pressed for oil. Cotton seeds are poisonous.
