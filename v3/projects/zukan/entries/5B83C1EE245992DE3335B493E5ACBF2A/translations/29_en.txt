Kinugasa-so (Silk Umbrella Grass) 《Side note: Sao-kyu (Paris polyphylla), Compendium of Materia Medica | Chinese name unclear》One variety
It has toxicity. It is a mountain product. In spring, it produces one stem from one root. Height is about one shaku. At the stem head, it produces eight or nine leaves in an umbrella-like shape. In summer, it produces a stem from the center and opens one flower. It has ten petals of light purple color with deep purple patterns and drooping yellow stamens. The details of the Japanese variety of Sao-kyu are not yet clear.

Hakurakukai (Macleaya cordata) [Takenigusa/Plume Poppy] - Compendium of Materia Medica, Castor Bean Appendix
It has great toxicity. It grows in mountains. In spring, it produces shoots from old roots with round stems that have no nodes and are hollow inside. Height reaches five to six shaku. The leaves resemble chrysanthemum leaves but without pointed tips, with size close to one shaku. Both stems and leaves are white-green in color, and when cut, they emit red-yellow sap. In summer, it opens small white flowers at the stem head in spike form. Later it produces pods that are flat and thin, pointed at both ends, containing small black seeds inside. This plant is used for boiling bamboo.
