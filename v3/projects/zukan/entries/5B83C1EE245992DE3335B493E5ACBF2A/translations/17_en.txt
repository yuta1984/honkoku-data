Phytolacca acinosa [Yamagobo]　Compendium of Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. It grows in shady and moist places. The leaves resemble young tobacco leaves, being pointed and hairless. In summer, it blooms white flowers at the stem tips opposite to the leaf stems, forming spikes. It should not be eaten together with water dropwort. Those that bloom purple flowers are called "sekisho." The toxicity is extremely strong. It must never be eaten. There are also those with yellow flowers. These are varieties of the same species.

Clematis terniflora [Senninsou]　Famine Relief Herbal
　It is poisonous. It is a climbing vine that produces leaves in spring. At each node, two stems grow oppositely, with each stem bearing two leaves at the base and three leaves at the tip. It uses these leaf stems to wind around trees and bamboo. In autumn, it blooms white flowers in clusters. If one mistakenly chews this, it will damage the teeth.
