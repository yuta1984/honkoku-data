Chiyū Kinren (Ground-emerging Golden Lotus) - Secret Transmission Flower Mirror
[Zazensō - Skunk Cabbage]
It is poisonous. It grows in water marshes in valleys of deep mountains. It produces buds from winter and opens flowers in early spring. The height is about 2-3 sun (6-9 cm), and the shape resembles the flower of Tennansho (wild arum) but is shorter, purple-black in color with a white center tinged with red. After flowering, it produces leaves. The shape resembles the leaves of Gyokusō (hosta), being round and thick. It bears fruit which turns red when ripe, like the fruit of Tennansho.

Batōrei (Aristolochia) - Compendium of Materia Medica
[Tsumbokusa - Birthwort]
It is poisonous. It is a climbing vine that produces leaves in spring with hair on them. In summer it opens flowers that are green tinged with purple. In autumn it bears fruit shaped like a bell. Inside there are flat seeds.
