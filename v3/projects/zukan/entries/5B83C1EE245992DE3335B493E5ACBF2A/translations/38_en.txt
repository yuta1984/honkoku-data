Kojō (Polygonum cuspidatum) - Compendium of Materia Medica
[Itadori - Japanese Knotweed]
It is poisonous. In spring it grows from old roots. Initially it resembles bamboo shoots, with round stems that are hollow inside. The leaves grow alternately, and in autumn it forms spikes between the leaves with small white flowers clustering together. The fruit resembles rhubarb in shape. If pregnant women eat it, their children will have body odor.

Ichiben-ren (One-petal Lotus) - Secret Transmission Flower Mirror
[Mizubashō - Asian Skunk Cabbage]
It is poisonous. It grows beside ponds and marshes in deep mountains. The leaf stems are short and soft, light green in color. At the end of spring, from morning to evening, short stems emerge from between the leaves and open single-petaled white flowers. They are roughly like the tip of a spoon. There are green stamens in the center. There are also varieties with black spots on the leaf surface, which are one type of this species.
