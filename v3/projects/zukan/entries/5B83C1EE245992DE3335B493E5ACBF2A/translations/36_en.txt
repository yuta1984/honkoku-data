Baryō (Polygonum) - Compendium of Materia Medica
[Inutade]
It is poisonous. In spring it grows beside ditches in fields and plains. There are many varieties. It lacks pungent taste and is unsuitable for food. The one described here reaches a height of three to four shaku, has large thick leaves with black spots on the leaf surface, and in autumn opens small white flowers forming spikes. After flowering it produces small seeds, and the seedlings wither in winter.

Bōki (Glehnia littoralis) - Compendium of Materia Medica
[Botanbōfū]
It is poisonous. It grows on seashores in warm regions. The leaves resemble peony leaves, are smooth-textured, and the leaf surface bears a white powder. In summer it reaches a height of three to four shaku, opening white flowers densely clustered at each branch tip. They are like the flowers of angelica (tōki) and cnidium (senkyū). After flowering it produces fruit.
