Lycopus lucidus [Yama-azami]　Compendium of Materia Medica
　It has mild toxicity. It grows from old roots that overwinter. It is reddish-purple in color, and reaches over one shaku in height by spring. The leaves grow alternately, and both stems and leaves exude white sap. At the stem tip, five leaves emerge and divide into five branches, at whose ends two leaves grow oppositely, blooming four-petaled flowers and bearing fruit. Its roots exude yellow sap.

Herbaceous Lycopus [Sawa-urushi]　Compendium of Materia Medica, Lycopus Collected Explanations
　It is poisonous. It grows beside rivers and around lakes and marshes. It sprouts early in spring. It is pale reddish in color, and when mature reaches two to three shaku in height. The leaves resemble those of Yama-azami and are pale green in color, becoming yellow toward the tips. Five leaves grow in pairs, dividing into five branches, at whose ends three leaves grow in pairs, blooming five-petaled pale yellow flowers. Its roots exude white sap.
