Senbō (Curculigo) - Compendium of Materia Medica
[Sanbaizasa]
It is poisonous. Chinese varieties are introduced and cultivated. In spring it produces leaves from old roots. They are pale green in color, and in summer thin stems grow beside the leaf stems, opening deep yellow six-petaled flowers. Details of its form are recorded in the Seifu.

Hakukutsusai (Chelidonium majus) - Jihuang Bencao
[Kusanōō]
It is poisonous. It grows in groves and forests everywhere. It has seedlings throughout the four seasons. The leaf surface is pale green and the back is white. The stems and leaves are hairy. From spring and summer until autumn it opens four-petaled yellow flowers and forms pods. When the stems or leaves are cut, yellow sap emerges with a foul odor.
