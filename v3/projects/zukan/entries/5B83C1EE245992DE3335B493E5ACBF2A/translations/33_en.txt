Kanshū (Dryopteris) [Hosobakinojio] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It is poisonous. It grows in deep mountains. From one root it produces dozens of thin leaves. The length is one to two shaku and deep green in color. It does not wither even through winter. In summer, separate leaf-like thin stems of brown color grow one or two at a time. These are actually its flowers. According to one theory, if women take these leaves, they will not become pregnant. Therefore, it should not be used carelessly. What is called "kusasotetsu" (false bracken) is one variety of this.

Sekishaishisha (Solanum) [Kuchinawajōgo] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It is poisonous. It grows abundantly in deep mountain ravine sides and shady places. The stem is diagonal and does not stand upright. The base is dark reddish in color. The leaves resemble those of Sōyōju and grow alternately. In summer it opens small white flowers between the leaves in clusters. When snakes overeat, if they consume these leaves, digestion occurs immediately.
