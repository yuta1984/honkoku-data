Impatiens balsamina [Tsumakurenai] - Compendium of Materia Medica
　It has mild toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. Both leaves and stems are soft, with nodes on the stems. It blooms in summer. There are red, white, and mixed colors, including double-flowered varieties. The shape resembles a bird with wings. After flowering, it bears fruit that splits lengthwise and curls back when twisted. If mistakenly eaten with vinegar, it is highly toxic. There is also one called "Horagai-sou" that grows in deep mountains. There are varieties with yellow flowers (Kitsuri-fune) and purple flowers (Yoko-murasaki), which are species of the same genus.

Belamcanda chinensis [Hiougi] - Compendium of Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. It produces leaves in spring. When young, the leaves resemble those of iris and are covered with white powder. In summer, it branches at the stem tip and blooms. The flowers have six petals with purple spots, or are sometimes yellow, and some have no spots. There are varieties with different leaf shapes.
