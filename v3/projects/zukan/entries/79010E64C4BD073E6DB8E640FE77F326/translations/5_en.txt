【Right Page】
In the clear morning, one should stroke it with hands that have oil and grime from combing hair. Women's hands are most suitable.
Therefore, it is commonly said that "orchids prefer sensuality." To nourish them, one should pour rainwater, pond water, or water with oil and grime from washing hair. Freshly drawn water is not good. Bath water
produces worms and earthworms. If one pours the washing water from eels, it will eliminate worms and earthworms. When the cultivation is improper, white spots appear on the leaves. This is called orchid lice 《Kashi Sahen》. If one occasionally brushes and washes with a brush tip,
they will naturally disappear. They are extremely difficult to cultivate. Their form is elegant and refined. The "Flower Mirror" states: The teaching of cultivating orchids says: Do not expose in spring 《to avoid harm from frost, snow, and cold winds》, do not sun in summer
《most detesting scorching heat and blazing sun》, do not dry in autumn 《pour much fertilizer water or bean juice》, do not moisten in winter 《should be stored in warm rooms or earth pits》. The method of planting orchids is also found in "Zunsheng Bazhen"
and "Runan Garden History" among others. In winter, store in warm rooms, and there is a proper amount for watering. [Ritsu-]

【Left Page】
Around the time of early summer's Xiaoman, one should divide the plants. If one takes old bulbs and nurtures them, they will produce leaves.
If not taken, they will not grow. The "Xinchu Ji" states: If orchid leaves are soaked in oil, they will blacken hair and prevent it from falling.
Their nature is to enjoy oil and grime, and they are also good for hair. However, there are theories that they are poisonous. The "Shiwu Ganzhu"
states: Orchids are extremely beautiful but poisonous. A person from Shu once, being drunk and thirsty, drank orchid
flower water from a jar and died from vomiting and diarrhea. All flower arrangement water is poisonous. It should not be limited
to orchid flower water alone. The sword orchid has leaves reaching two to three feet, hard and firm, deep green in color, standing upright,
and has no fragrance. The root is called Duzokdan 《Kyokoku Orchid Theory》. Its essence carries oil. In summer,
tender shoots emerge from beside the root bulbs. The bracts bear purple color, and the form roughly resembles bamboo shoots.
Four to five leaves emerge and grow to maturity by late autumn. The flowers emerge from beside the previous year's root bulbs...
