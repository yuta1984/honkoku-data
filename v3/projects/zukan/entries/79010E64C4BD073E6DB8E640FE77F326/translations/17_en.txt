Auspicious Grass (Kichijōsō), Kannon Grass (Kan'onsō) 《Bencao Shiyi》
It grows in mountains, clustering in shady and moist places, spreading horizontally over the ground. The leaf shape
resembles Rohdea japonica with light green color. It does not wither in winter, produces new leaves in summer, and in autumn extends purple stems between the new and old leaves, opening small five-petaled flowers. The front surface is white, the back surface is purple with yellow stamens forming spikes. The fruit gradually ripens by the winter of the following year, turning reddish-purple. It is commonly said that this flower is rare. When it flowers, that household will surely have auspicious events. The "Min Shu" states that when a household has fortunate events, the flower naturally blooms. Therefore it is named "auspicious." Also, the "Flower Mirror" says that the flower does not easily bloom, but when it opens, it governs joy. Some say it blooms once every ten years. Indeed, when it flourishes, it has flowers and is not rare. Also
