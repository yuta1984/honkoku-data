Tiger Lily (Kentan/Oniyuri) "Bencao Gangmu Lily Corrections"
In spring, seedlings grow from old bulbs (aged bulbs). These bulbs overlap each other and are white in color. By summer and autumn, the stem branches at the tip and opens six-petaled flowers. They are reddish-yellow with purple spots. All curve backward and hang downward. In midsummer, small bulbs develop between the leaves. They are purplish-black in color, and their shape roughly resembles the root bulbs. When severely weakened, the plant produces no flowers, only the small bulbs between the leaves. The old bulbs divide into two bulbs in autumn. There are one or two similar species, listed on the left.

Pinellia (Karasubishaku) "Bencao Gangmu"
In spring, it grows wild in various places in gardens. It produces three leaves at the stem tip. The leaves roughly resemble those of wild rose or water oats. It produces small bulbs at the base of the three leaves on the stem. They also grow in the middle of the stem. The flowers grow separately and bloom at the stem tip. They resemble the flowers of Arisaema (yamagonyaku).

Bulbil Ginseng (Chinese name: details unknown)
It grows in spring. Initially it has three leaves, gradually lengthening to produce five leaves, reaching three to four shaku in height. It opens small white flowers at the stem top. It produces small bulbs like bulbils between the leaves.

Autumn Begonia "Secret Mirror of Japanese and Chinese Names"
It grows in spring. Both stem and leaves are soft. In autumn, it opens pale pink flowers with two long petals and two small petals, forms pods, and separately produces seeds between the leaves.

Tamafuki (Chinese name: details unknown)
It grows in spring. The leaves resemble coltsfoot leaves but smaller. In autumn, it opens white flowers at the stem tip forming spikes, and produces seeds between the leaves. The rest is omitted.

The Bencao Gangmu Lily Corrections states: Shizhen said, "The stems and leaves resemble those of mountain lily but are taller, the red flowers have yellow tinge and hang in four directions, with black spots above. Those that first have offspring between the branches and leaves are tiger lilies."
