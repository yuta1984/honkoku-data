Since rapeseed and cotton seeds produced are sent to Osaka for sale, and oils pressed there are then purchased by various provinces to meet domestic needs, this results in double expenses. Moreover, oil cake used as fertilizer for rice fields and dry fields cannot be obtained freely, creating various inconveniences. Therefore, regarding the oil pressing methods and the selling and distribution of oil seeds for daily use in the aforementioned provinces, the following should be understood:

Article 1: At this time, new seed commodity wholesalers have been newly established in both Sakai and Hyogo. Rapeseed and cotton seeds sent from various provinces shall be received collectively by the seed commodity wholesalers at these two locations as well as the Osaka seed commodity wholesalers. The provinces should understand this arrangement and send their products to one of these three locations to sell to the respective seed commodity wholesalers.

Article 1: Whereas Osaka general cargo wholesalers have been purchasing oil seeds up to now, not only such purchasing but also accepting oil seeds as payment for other commercial transactions is henceforth prohibited. The same prohibition applies to general cargo wholesalers in Sakai and Hyogo, so cargo owners from various provinces should be aware of this. Furthermore, while seed commodity wholesalers in Osaka, Sakai, and Hyogo may provide advance silver payments to facilitate oil seed transactions at their discretion, borrowing and lending of interest-bearing silver under this pretext is strictly prohibited.
