1. Regarding cooking and food stalls in villages and coastal settlements

These establishments are not the kind of shops located along the north-south highways that provide lodging for travelers or daytime provisions, but rather shops within villages and settlements that have become gathering places for day laborers and local young men. Many such establishments have recently appeared, where day laborers squander their wages and salaries, and the young men of the village have developed lazy and weak moral habits. Since this has arisen from the flourishing of such commercial activities, if all such establishments were prohibited entirely, those without family inheritance would face hardship in making their living. Therefore, it is acceptable to allow those who conduct proper trade in goods appropriate to their locality to engage in commerce after investigation by village officials. However, if it is reported that anyone disobeys these orders by gathering people day and night, selling food and drink, and engaging in improper activities, they shall be strictly punished. Moreover, among those operating traveler inns along the highways, if any engage in improper gatherings of people, such commerce shall be prohibited and they shall be strictly punished.
