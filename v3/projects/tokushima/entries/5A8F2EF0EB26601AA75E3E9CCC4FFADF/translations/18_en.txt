They made preparations, but Kinchō had only two retainers with him as master and servant. He pondered how to defend themselves, when suddenly he thought of a stratagem and whispered to Taka. He stuffed straw into their beds to make it appear that both men were lying there, while he himself climbed up into a good position in a large cedar tree nearby, and Taka hid in the forest across the way, carrying large trees and stones. "Come quickly, you rogues. I'll kill every last one of you without exception," he said, holding his breath as he lay in wait.

Completely unaware of this, at Tsuda's side, Rokuemon led Kyūzaemon, Sakuemon, Hachibe, Yakuemon, and other strong evil tanuki—about sixty in total—and past the hour of the Ox that night, they departed from their various holes at Kannon and pressed toward Kinchō's dwelling. Moreover, that night it was raining and dark, and thinking "darkness is our specialty," they prepared large and small stones and easily reached his dwelling. When they looked, it appeared as though there had been a drinking party in the evening, with things scattered about the area, and Kinchō and Taka appeared to be lying unconscious, unaware of what was happening around them. Rokuemon was greatly delighted and shouted, "Now!"
