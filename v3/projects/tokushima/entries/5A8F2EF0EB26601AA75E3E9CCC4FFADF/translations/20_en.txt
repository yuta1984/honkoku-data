Some had their heads smashed by stones as they fled, when from the forest on the right appeared another retainer of Kinchō who identified himself as Taka of Fujunoki Temple, hurling large stones like rain. This caused Tsuda's forces to panic even more, scrambling to be first to flee up the mountain, with some pushing and shoving against their own comrades in their haste, retreating in complete disorder. At this moment, a thick and robust old tanuki emerged from Tsuda's group and shouted loudly: "The enemy is only two creatures, yet we have great numbers and still we retreat—what pathetic fellows you are!

Follow Kawashima no Kyūzaemon, who is called the strongest among the kinfolk of Ana Kannon!" He dodged the flying stones and leaped toward Kinchō. Encouraged by these words, many of the clan rallied back and fought by throwing small stones. Since Taka was an extremely valorous warrior, he charged alone into the crowd, fortunately knocking down and biting to death those he encountered, displaying his courage as he rampaged wildly—it was just like releasing a tiger among sheep.
