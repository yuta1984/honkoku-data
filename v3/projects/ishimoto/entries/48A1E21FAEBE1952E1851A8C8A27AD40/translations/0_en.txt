Fires broke out at 7-8 locations in government and private buildings, with particularly severe tremors in the northwestern areas of the prefecture. The damage to local taxes is immeasurable, said to exceed one million yen.

Disaster Report: So far throughout the prefecture - 1,533 deaths, 2,400 injured, over 1,500 houses collapsed.

◯Earthquake in Gifu
Around 6 o'clock on the same day, in Akasaka, Mino Province, the ground was observed moving up and down by 1 shaku 5-6 sun. Ground fissures 7-8 sun wide appeared in various places, with muddy water and saline soil erupting to a height of over 1 shaku. Fires began in Ogaki with countless buildings completely burned. The Nagara River railway bridge collapsed about 300 shaku. Eastbound trains did not depart from Tarui. Near the Kiso River, Tamano-i collapsed for a length of 20 jo, with severe flooding.

◯Earthquake in Fukui
Beginning at 6:37 on the same day, 240 houses collapsed in the city, 50 casualties, over 10 fire locations. Tremors continue even now.

In Shiga Prefecture, the damage in Osaka, Hikone and other areas near Kyoto was from a truly unprecedented great earthquake. The number of injured at Namba Spinning Company and other companies is in the tens of thousands, still unknown.

Summary: In Hikone - 50 houses, 15 deaths, 60 injured
　　　　Kyoto - High walls collapsed but no casualties
　　　　Osaka - Collapses but no deaths
　　　　Ishikawa, Toyama, Nagano, Mie, Wakayama, Kochi prefectures also experienced earthquakes of various magnitudes, but damage was limited to injuries. We await further reports.
