Fire Site Directional Map, November 29, Meiji 9

Today at 11:20 PM, a fire broke out from the house of Suzuki Teizo in Sukiyacho, Nihonbashi. The north wind was fierce, and the fire was extinguished at 5:40 AM.

Yesterday, the 29th, after 11 PM, a fire broke out from the residence of one Suzuki, a horse keeper by trade, located at 2 Sukiyacho, 6th Sub-district, 1st Major District. The fire spread to Hinokimono-cho, Moto-Daiku-cho, Kami-Maki-cho, Kawase-Ishi-cho, Shin'uemon-cho, Hakumasa-cho, Hakuya-cho, Shimo-Maki-cho Streets 3rd and 4th chome, Minami-Denma-cho 1st to 3rd chome, Nakabashi-Hirokoji-cho, South and North Maki-cho, Oke-cho, Minami-Daiku-cho, Minami-Kaji-cho, Gorobei-cho, Tatami-cho, Kita-Konya-cho, Nakabashi-Izumi-cho, Daiko-cho, Minami-Saya-cho, Matsukawa-cho, Suzuki-cho, Inaba-cho, Tokiwa-cho, Gusoku-cho, Yanagi-cho, Sumi-cho, Hon-Zaimoku-cho 3rd chome, Sandai-cho, Kitajima-cho 1st and 2nd chome, Kamejima-cho 1st and 2nd chome, Mizutani-cho, Okazaki-cho 1st and 2nd chome, Hatchobori-Naka-cho, Nagashima-cho, Hibiya-cho, Sachi-cho, Nagasawa-cho, Moto-jima-cho, Matsuya-cho 1st, 2nd and 3rd chome, Takadai-cho, Hon-Hatchobori 1st to 5th chome, Minato-cho, Shintomi-cho 1st to 7th chome, Minami-Hatto-bori including 3 chome, Irifune-cho including 8 chome, Shinei-cho including 7 chome, Shinminato-cho including 7 towns, and extending to Akashi-cho and other areas. The fire was fierce and spread from east to west. As this supplementary report is being printed, the fire has not yet been completely extinguished. There are likely many casualties and injured, but the details are not yet known. Therefore, detailed information will be provided tomorrow, and this is an urgent preliminary report.
