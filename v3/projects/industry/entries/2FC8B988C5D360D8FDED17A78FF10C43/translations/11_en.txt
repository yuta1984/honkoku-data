Do not spread the straw mats, and from this point, do not use bird feathers but gather them by pushing and take them with fingers. Also, after five to six applications, perform bottom removal, making one straw mat into two. Also, perform bottom removal when approaching molting, and do not spread the straw mats. Up to this point, every time bottom removal is done, scatter rice bran inside the straw mats and spread the silkworms thinly. For the fourth waking, do not use rice bran. If rain falls and the cold is severe on the day of bottom removal, first spread rice bran evenly over the existing bran, then apply mulberry on top, and all silkworms will climb onto the mulberry. This properly blocks the bad air from heat.

If it rains, apply this technique and perform bottom removal the next day. If all fourth-stage garden silkworm bottom removal is completed, there will be no harm from rain and humidity, and no hindrance from south winds. Indeed, for fourth-stage molting silkworms, the skill or lack of skill of the caretaker determines how many cocoons are obtained. If the weather is cold, gently light a fire to rouse the silkworms. If it is warm, remove the fire and do not let it become hot.
