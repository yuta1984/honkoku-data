Within the third molting, the one day of mulberry stopping is for the silkworms to wake up and align together. For the fourth waking, regardless of this matter, do not let the previously awakened silkworms reach hunger. They wake up and mulberry is applied. At this time, apply all remaining uncut mulberry without leaving any, and immediately proceed to bottom removal. Silkworms that have not yet awakened are at the bottom of the bamboo mats; pick them up and move them to separate straw platforms. They will soon awaken. From this, one should know that it is proper to perform bottom removal on silkworms. From the fourth waking onward, block the furnace in the silkworm house, prohibit lighting fires, and keep it cool.

If the south wind blows making it humid and hot, quickly open the north windows, or remove the partition doors to keep it cool. Regardless of whether in cold or warm regions, wind and coolness are essential. Among fourth-stage silkworms, if more days are taken, more silk threads will be obtained. At this time, if the silkworms fill the house and much mulberry is gathered, it is good to apply mulberry three times. On the second day after waking, do not perform bottom removal but apply cut mulberry. On the morning of the third day, apply uncut mulberry thickly and perform bottom removal.
