Because of this, the silkworms weaken and some die. Seeing this, people don't realize they have been defeated by the warmth, but think only that they are becoming fuzzy because it's still cold, so they wrap them even more. Therefore, many have plenty of silkworms but never see the shape of cocoons. Originally, silkworms follow their country and location, developing early where warm weather comes

early, and developing late where it comes late. Since they are insects that grow naturally, they should never be artificially warmed. If this doubt is hard to dispel, place a few silkworms on the mulberry in your estate and observe their condition. In one night they will eat through all the leaves and grow steadily, after which there will be no continuing silkworms. However, they should be brought inside to raise from the third instar onward, because there are enemies such as ants and various birds.
