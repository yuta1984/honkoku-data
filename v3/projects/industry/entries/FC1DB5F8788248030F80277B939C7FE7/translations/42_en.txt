The most important thing is to beat mulberry leaves and have all silkworms mount without exception. The cocoons will become large and well-formed with good firmness and elasticity, and five kan of dried cocoons from one seed card is reliable. Also, silkworms that mount when still slightly young only have good appearance but are loose, with little weight and no elasticity.

For example, even though one pays attention to cultivation throughout the year, when the harvest season arrives and one sees the tips of rice ears slightly ripened and scattered by wind, one becomes alarmed. At harvest time there will be great profit and loss. If one waits a little longer and harvests when even the base ears are fully ripened,

No matter how much mulberry or cocoons are produced, one should not be surprised. The remaining young silkworms will feed and grow, producing twenty percent more weight. Also, those that spin slightly in the evening should have their rear ends pulled out, be given mulberry leaves without concern, and be inserted into the shelves. They do not spin at night.
