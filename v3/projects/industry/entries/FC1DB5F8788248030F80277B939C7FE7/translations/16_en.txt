Chapter Two: Things to Keep in Mind Regarding Various Silkworm Tools
First, those who think to raise silkworms with great care should prepare plenty of shelves, bamboo, mats, and baskets during autumn and winter. If containers are insufficient,

the silkworms will naturally be overcrowded and the cocoons will turn out poorly. However, large baskets are difficult to manage. Generally, they should be about three feet wide and four feet long, and should be prepared with the plan of placing two per ken of space. First, they do not take up much space and are easy to manage, keep the house cool and do not give heat to the silkworms, and there is benefit in terms of labor. Also, the frame shelves for small-leaf feeding should be six feet high with shelf spacing of about seven sun. Generally, prepare about eighty of the above-mentioned baskets per sheet of eggs, and when the silkworms from one sheet of eggs grow up, spread them thinly across seventy baskets, keeping the remaining ten baskets as extras.
