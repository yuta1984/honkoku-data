From the beginning of silkworm hatching, one should always keep oneself in unlined clothing to feel cold and judge the weather accordingly. Even if it's the hatching season, if it's warm, without worry, open all high windows or partition doors completely and hatch the silkworms in cool conditions. However, for three days after hatching, they should be raised in cool conditions. This is the secret of sericulture. Also, while one may imitate skilled people in matters like feeding with small leaves, one should not imitate others regarding weather conditions. For example, seeing others close their doors and windows, one should not close

There are cases where closing doors in someone's cool house and opening doors wide in one's own warm house achieve the same result. Generally, trees should be planted in rows on the west side of the house to block the evening sun when raising silkworms. Also, during rain and such, there may be terribly cold conditions, and even after layering unlined and padded clothing, it may still be cold.
