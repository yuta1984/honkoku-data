At that time, one must distinguish between the fine and coarse qualities of mulberry trees. Mulberry is originally intended to serve as silkworm feed, and while silkworms will eat any mulberry given to them, depending on its quality, there are differences in the amount and suitability of the nutrients contained, and ultimately the beauty or ugliness of the cocoon varieties becomes naturally apparent. Moreover, even if the mulberry's nature is good, if one does not carefully observe the soil quality of the garden and fields and make efforts in cultivation and plowing, it will not even match wild varieties. Layering, seeding, high stumps, low stumps, tall trees, mountain mulberry—each has different characteristics according to the terrain. This must be carefully observed. Therefore, this volume first presents the varieties of mulberry trees and the quality of their nature, then addresses matters of field soil quality and irrigation, and in the middle shows the various types of silkworm eggs and methods of nurturing,
