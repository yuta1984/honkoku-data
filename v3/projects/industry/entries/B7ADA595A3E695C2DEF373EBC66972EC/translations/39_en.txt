From the third sleep onward, it goes without saying that when expanding, carefully selected mulberry leaves should be fed evenly without thick or thin spots. Five feedings per day must not be missed.
　　　Chapter 12
The fourth sleep (called "niwa no yasumi" - garden's rest) also involves mulberry attachment in the same manner as the previous three sleeps, raising them most carefully. However, covering with nets is not used; they are picked up by hand. Simply sprinkling rice hulls and feeding mulberry, carefully observing when one or two parts of sleeping silkworms appear in one basket, making eight hundred or nine hundred silkworms per basket, feeding mulberry thinly, and working to remove the silkworm litter.

　　　Chapter 13
When all have entered sleep and after giving no mulberry for about a day and a half, when five or six parts have awakened, middle mulberry should be fed, and when eight or nine parts have awakened, mulberry attachment should be done immediately. If the weather is cold and the timing is delayed, even if eight or nine parts have not awakened, mulberry attachment may be done.
　　　Chapter 14
Thus on the third day the silkworm litter is removed, and from this time whole-leaf mulberry is fed, and removing silkworm litter daily must be most diligently practiced.
