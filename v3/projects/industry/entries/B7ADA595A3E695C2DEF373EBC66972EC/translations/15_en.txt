　　　Chapter Five
For root stocks from which branches have been cut, dig around the circumference to a depth of five to six sun, and appropriately apply a mixture of mulberry leaves left uneaten by silkworms and silkworm excrement 《Marginal note: called "kaikoura" in local dialect》.

　　　Chapter Six
For fertilizer, use soybeans 《Marginal note: boiled soft or pounded into powder and mixed with ash》, sake and soy sauce lees, fish entrails, sugar, rice, and similar materials as convenient for cultivation.

　　　Chapter Seven
For mulberry seedlings, there are differences between sowing 《Ruby text: torine》 and layering methods. Layering can be used for silkworm raising in three years. Sowing can finally be used after five years. Furthermore, with layering, good varieties will always remain good varieties without losing their original quality. With sowing, many undergo changes. Therefore, layering is considered more convenient.

　　　Chapter Eight
Generally, layered plants can serve as silkworm feed after three years, and when seven to ten years have passed, old stocks should be removed and new layered plants planted.

　　　Chapter Nine
When cultivating layered plants, one plant should be grown per tsubo 《Marginal note: six shaku square》 of land.
