　　　Chapter Two
There is some reason to the common saying that what is produced in one's own household is good. The reason is that, primarily when manufacturing original stock, for those intended for one's own use, one should first select well-developed moths and have the number of moths that should lay eggs on half a sheet of paper lay eggs on one full sheet instead. The time period is from four o'clock in the evening until three o'clock the following morning, after which they are removed and transferred elsewhere. By doing this, not only is the seating space wide, but since only those that laid eggs when their vigor was at its peak are used, although the egg granules may not be dense, when spring comes the number that hatch is

　　　Chapter Three
The moths transferred elsewhere will continue to lay eggs the following day, but they gradually weaken, and naturally some will not hatch. Even if they do hatch, since it is logical that their nature will be weak after hatching, they must be removed.
