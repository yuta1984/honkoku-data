What is illustrated next shows the silk glands after removing the stomach and intestines, where two strands of silk material run parallel and reach the silk-spinning pores under the jaw. This raw material is gum-like (Note: When tested, it dissolves when mixed with water and solidifies when put into alcohol). Initially, when spinning silk, it forms a viscous liquid state, and it is also expelled with a kind of mucus, but due to the external air, the two threads combine to form one strand.
