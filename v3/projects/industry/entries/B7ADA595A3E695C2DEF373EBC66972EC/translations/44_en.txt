However, moths that have been improperly raised from the beginning will not lay eggs as desired at this stage, no matter how much effort is made. Generally, to densely fill one sheet of paper requires 90 to 120 superior moths, laying eggs from 2-3 o'clock in the afternoon until 4 o'clock the following morning. After 4 o'clock in the morning, move them to other paper.
　　[Note] Once morning arrives, their strength is nearly exhausted and they produce inferior eggs, so these are discarded elsewhere.
