Figure No. 1 shows the eggs laid by moths. Initially they are oval-shaped and pale yellow in color, but after approximately three days they change to a blue-gray color, which is their true form. The enlarged version is a diagram depicting what was observed through a microscope. The surface of silkworm eggs is essentially a thin white membrane
