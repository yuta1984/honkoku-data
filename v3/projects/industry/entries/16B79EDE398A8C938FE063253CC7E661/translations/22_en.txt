○Generally, large mulberry trees are considered best. Although cutting and using branches from young trees seems easy, they have few leaves. Particularly when silkworms are small, soft leaves are good, but after the silkworms have grown fat, if you do not feed them thick leaves from old trees, the worms will not fatten and will not produce much silk. This means using only young tree leaves while the silkworms are small. Also, trees on mountain sides often develop various diseases in their leaves. Only mulberries by large rivers are best. The silkworms also grow well without disease, and good silkworm eggs are produced, making silkworm seeds valuable items. Generally, for raising many mulberries, in the western provinces one should learn the detailed production methods in the Tango and Tajima regions, and when there are many trees, hire men and women from famous places to thoroughly apply their techniques.
