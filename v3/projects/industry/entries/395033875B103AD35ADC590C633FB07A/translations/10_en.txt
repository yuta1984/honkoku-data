However, the size of the nets should be made according to each household's containers. All three types should have thin bamboo tied to both sides so they can fit inside the silkworm baskets, and should be handled as previously described. For example, when raising ten trays of silkworms, twelve or thirteen nets should be made.

1. The method of seme-kuwa (intensive feeding): when the silkworms appear to be in the shishi sleep stage, mulberry should be fed strictly seven or eight times per day. This is called seme-kuwa. At this time, even if silkworms entering the sleep stage go under the mulberry and leave some uneaten, it doesn't matter - keep persistently sprinkling mulberry on them.
