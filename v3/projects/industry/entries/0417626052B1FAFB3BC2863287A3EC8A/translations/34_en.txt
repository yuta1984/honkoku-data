　This is how it is. From one kan-me of cotton, about three monme can be produced.

One: The loss in cotton beating is ten percent. When beating one hundred me, it results in ninety me. 
　Of course, this depends on the quality of the cotton.

One: Cotton that was ginned and stored from last year is difficult to beat. However, 
　it is better to store it as seed cotton, dry it in the sun during the dog days to prevent insects, then gin it.

One: Cotton that was ginned and stored from last year is good.

One: To examine ginned cotton, pull it apart by hand - cotton that separates 
　smoothly and softly is superior. Cotton that twists with a chiriri sound when pulled apart is bad.

One: Cotton that is straight and firm is considered good. Also, cotton that becomes 
　soft for no particular reason is bad.

【Left Page】
One: For beaten cotton to be spun into thread, it is good to leave it as beaten 
　without board pressing.

One: The bow should be about four shaku six to seven sun in length, limited to eight sun. The wood should be 
　made from sawara cypress, which is good. It is also made from hinoki cypress. The weight 
　should be about five hundred fifty me. It can be made from deep mountain azalea wood, 
　or black persimmon wood is also good. Wood with coarse grain 
　is bad. With coarse-grained wood, the string gets damaged.

One: The mallet should be coated with Chinese wax several times throughout the day. If the string gets 
　damaged, wax should also be applied to the string.

One: If there are knots in the string, no sound is made even when the mallet is applied. It vibrates and tangles, 
　making it impossible to beat.

One: Bows made in Settsu Province, Osaka, are considered good. The same applies to strings. Also, Nagasaki
