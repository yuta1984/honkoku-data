　　　Method of making rouge (beni)
1. Safflowers should be picked in early summer morning when they bear dew. If they encounter storms or prolonged rain, the flowers will fall off, so they should not be picked the day after rain.
　Yellow-brown colored flowers are considered superior, while those with a reddish tinge are secondary. Those called "red flowers" are inferior and are only used by medical practitioners, not by dye houses.
1. Pick the safflowers, put them in a bucket with water, and knead them well in running water until no more yellow water comes out. When the yellow juice stops coming out, immediately put them in a box, pour water over them, and after a night has passed and they have become soft, make them into cakes.
　Make them round about the size of loquat fruit, then press them flat. This is called "coin-shaped cake." Dry these under clear skies. In rainy weather, dry them by hearth fire, but this does not compare to sun-drying.
