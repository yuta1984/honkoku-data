Mulberry leaves cannot be stored for longer than 4 to 6 days, and the fresher they are, the better. To raise the aforementioned silkworms, the number of mulberry trees required is:

　1,000 trees of 11 years old are needed. Or 360 trees of 13 years old, or 120 trees of 15 years old, or 20 trees of 20 years old are required.

　　Upon consideration, in that country they use large mulberry trees, which differs from our practice of harvesting leaves from young trees. One should consider whether this is due to climate and soil conditions, or perhaps due to customs.

Sometimes, to supplement insufficient mulberry leaves, small amounts of rice powder or sugar powder are sprinkled on the mulberry leaves when feeding. If the quantity is small, it causes no harm.
