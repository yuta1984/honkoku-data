Addendum
Before the opening of the ports, people generally disliked commerce and communication with foreigners, viewing them like dogs and pigs, thinking of them as enemies. After the opening of the ports, ignorant common people merely compete for trivial profits, without planning for lasting national benefits, and without considering matters related to national dignity, they peddle crude and inferior goods, thinking themselves profitable. As a result, even high-quality goods have lost their reputation, and this has instead given foreigners the opportunity to seize profitable advantages.

The isolationist theories of people in the past were not necessarily correct, nor are the commercial methods of people today entirely wrong. Comparing both, all are evils resulting from lack of understanding of foreign circumstances, and China's precedent of failure must also be taken as a warning. China paid enormous reparations in the Opium War and, defeated in the Battle of Tianjin, made a humiliating peace treaty - their decline reached its extreme. Indeed, Westerners first came to China beginning in the late Yuan period, and though several hundred years have already passed, the Chinese people remain ignorant, unable to understand foreign circumstances, not knowing methods of trade, and despising them
