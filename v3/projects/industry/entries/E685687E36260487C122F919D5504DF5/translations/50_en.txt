What must be most carefully noted is that during the voyage and certainly when in foreign lands, one must never engage in frivolous conversation at the dining table. Moreover, belching is something that Westerners greatly detest. Such behavior considerably tarnishes the culture of our country.

Now, regarding the procedures for selling silkworm egg cards, it is advisable to sell them at the port of Marseilles. This is because expenses are not excessive, and since merchants from various countries gather there, it is extremely convenient and suitable for transportation to various countries. Upon arrival there, brokers called "commissaires" (similar to the selling agents in Yokohama) will come inquiring in large numbers when they hear that Japanese have brought silkworm eggs.

Therefore, whether to sell or not according to market prices is at one's discretion, but when requesting these brokers to act as intermediaries, it is customary to pay a commission of two bu. There are also those who take no commission but collect it from the buyers instead. However, if one brings large quantities, there are numerous silk textile manufacturing establishments in Lyon, France (located between Marseilles and Paris)
