In France it is called soie, in England silk, and in Holland zijde.

○Silkworm　In Germany Seidenraupe or Seidenwurm, in France ver à soie, in southern France magnian, in England silkworm, in Holland zijderups or zijdeworm, Latin name Bombyx mori

○Cocoon　In Germany Kokon or Gallette, in France cocon, in England cocoon, in Holland cocon or tonnetti or hoesje

○Silkworm eggs　In Germany Grains or Samen, in France graines, in England seeds or grains or silkworm eggs, in Holland eitjes or zaadjes, meaning eggs or seeds.

Silk thread is a type of extremely fine thread with a unique luster different from other threads, produced when the insect called the silkworm grows and becomes a pupa
