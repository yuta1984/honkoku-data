Also, for items that exist rarely in our country but have not reached widespread production, we should measure the suitability of the land and teach the local people to produce increasingly more of these items. Furthermore, for products like those for which our country has previously produced nothing and has only used imported goods—if they are not difficult to cultivate and raise in accordance with our domestic land and climate—we should seek seeds from foreign countries and

　learn methods of cultivation and nurturing, thereby preventing the harmful practice of the people relying on other nations for daily necessities. If the people are thus fully satisfied, from the Imperial Court above down to the common people in remote border areas, all will achieve prosperity and wealth. Only then can we make our national prestige shine across the five continents. This is the true intention of the author of Kaibutsu Shinsho.
