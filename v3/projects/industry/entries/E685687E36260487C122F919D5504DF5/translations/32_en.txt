Thus, when the silkworm egg papers brought out by the various domains were not purchased by the Westerners, the merchants could only stand idle with no recourse but to wait for them to rot and then discard them, as there was no other option available. This was as exemplified in the Ushi-Tora year mentioned earlier.

In that Ushi-Tora year, the price of silkworm egg papers was initially two to three dollars per sheet, but eventually dropped to a mere five or six cents, and in the end reached the point where six sheets of silkworm egg papers could be bought for one Tenp≈ç coin, with some reportedly being thrown into ditches. However, at this point our merchants were also at fault. The reason was that they deceived foreigners by calling summer silkworm eggs spring silkworm eggs, or treated silkworm eggs with glue and such, producing many extremely inferior products in an attempt to gain immediate short-term profits. Consequently, their deception was eventually discovered, and instead they suffered difficulties, with many going bankrupt.
