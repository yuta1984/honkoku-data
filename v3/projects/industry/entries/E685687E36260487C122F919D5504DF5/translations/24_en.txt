The number of days for growth is shorter than previously mentioned. In places like the East Indies, there are locations where silkworms are raised four times per year. This is because mulberry production is extremely abundant in those regions, and the leaves grow rapidly, providing ample opportunity to raise silkworms repeatedly.

Japan has two varieties - spring silkworms (haruko) and summer silkworms (natsuko) - allowing for two cultivation cycles. This is likely due to the warmer climate compared to the West and the abundance of mulberry trees.

Also, in the Lombardy region of Italy, there is a type of silkworm that molts only three times. Therefore, the rearing period is said to be four to five days shorter than other silkworms.
