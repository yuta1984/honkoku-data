On Mulberry Cultivation Methods

【Left Page】
Illustration of silkworm eggs laid on paper hatching
The eggs laid on paper hatch. In mid-March, around the time of Grain Rain (kokuu), they emerge, which is called "hatching" (kaeru). Once hatched, they are divided into first instar, second instar, etc., placed in chopping boxes, and fed finely chopped mulberry leaves. These are also called "black larvae" (kuroko) or "first instar" (hitotsu sube).
