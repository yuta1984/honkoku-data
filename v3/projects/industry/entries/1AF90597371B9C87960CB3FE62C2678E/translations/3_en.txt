For mulberry trees, during the cold season, apply night soil, and additionally sake lees, horse manure, and similar types of fertilizer abundantly to the fields, burying them at the roots of the mulberry trees. If done this way, the young leaves in spring will be tender, sprouting early and growing broad. However, during spring and summer, fertilizer must never be applied. If late frost falls, mulberry leaves should be thoroughly washed with water and dried before giving them to the silkworms. Flowers are always bad.
