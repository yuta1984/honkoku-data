The various texts adopted for the textual variants in this book were examined repeatedly in an effort to completely eliminate errors, but due to the circumstances of the owners, some had to be surveyed within an extremely short time and were not permitted the opportunity for re-examination. Examples include the Yokoyama text among the Aobyoshi texts and the Hosaka text among the separate texts. Furthermore, for the convenience of manuscript preparation, various texts discovered after 1938 were omitted.
　　　　　　　　Base Text
In selecting a base text from among the aforementioned various texts for marking textual variants, after repeated rigorous examination, it was decided to use Fujiwara no Teika's Aobyoshi text for this purpose. For the three chapters Hana-chiru-sato, Kashiwagi, and Sawarabi, the extant Teika text was used. For the other chapters, the Oshima text, which is considered to most faithfully preserve the form of the Teika text among existing texts, was used. However, since the Oshima text for Kiritsubo and Yume-no-ukihashi are supplementary copies, Hatsune is from a separate text lineage, and Ukifune is missing from the Oshima text, for these chapters the Ikeda text, which holds the position second to the Oshima text, was used.
