The various texts adopted for the textual variants in this book have been repeatedly investigated to eliminate errors, but due to the circumstances of the owners, some had to be examined in extremely short periods without the opportunity for re-examination. Examples of this include the Yokoyama text among the Aobyoshi texts and the Hosaka text among the variant texts. Furthermore, for the convenience of manuscript preparation, texts discovered after 1938 have been omitted.
　　　　　　　　Base Text
In selecting a base text from among the aforementioned texts to mark the textual variants, after rigorous examination, it was decided to use Fujiwara no Teika's Aobyoshi text as the base. For the three chapters Hanachiru-sato, Kashiwagi, and Sawarabi, the extant Teika text was used. For the other chapters, the Oshima text, which is considered to most faithfully preserve the form of the Teika text among existing texts, was used. However, since the Oshima text is a supplementary copy for the two chapters Kiritsubo and Yume-no-ukihashi, the Oshima text for Hatsune belongs to the variant text lineage, and the Oshima text lacks Ukifune, the Ikeda text, which holds the position next to the Oshima text, was used for these chapters.
