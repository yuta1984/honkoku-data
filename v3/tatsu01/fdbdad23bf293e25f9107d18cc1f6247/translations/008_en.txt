From around Genna 2 (1616) until Genna 7, [someone] relied on the monk Seietsu as a teacher of military arts, staying close to him day and night,
and this is a tale that detailed records of the fall of Takadachi that he heard morning and evening. In it
it says that the provincial lord, Middle Counselor Masamune, once summoned the monk Seietsu and said, "How is it, monk? You have lived long in this world.
Also, I hear you possess writings of Lord Yoshitsune. Might I not see them?" Seietsu respectfully immediately took out a vermillion-
lacquered box, pushed open the lid, and brought out the documents. When he
offered them up for viewing, Middle Counselor Masamune descended from his seat and knelt to view
them, and ordered that the people should also be allowed to see them. Gently, Seietsu put away the scroll and took his seat. When [Masamune tried] to grant Seietsu
a domain, Seietsu said, "This is a most gracious offer, but I have lived many years in this world and
am now close to my final moments—what would I do with a domain? That I have lived peacefully
in Hiraizumi until now, this itself I consider your lordship's benevolence and domain. Without serving even slightly in return for this favor," he said, appearing absolutely determined not to accept any domain whatsoever. Thereafter, though there were many summons,

[Masamune] never again spoke of granting domains, and so forth, it appears. This Seietsu Monogatari also has variant versions in two volumes.
Seietsu's ancient tomb is located in a place near the village called Usukinu. Also, Yamakita is very ancient, and in medieval times it was also written
as Senboku or Senboku, and in the rice certificate from Gentaji temple that Yoshiie Ason had Kamakura Gongoró Kagemasa write, it appears as Senboku.
Senfuku appears in the calligraphy of the priest Muju and also on the sacred mirror of Sugi Shrine. Seietsu Kaison's longevity came from fishing in the Koromo River and on his
return journey eating meat called "nimukamu" at a mountain home, after which he became long-lived. Also, in a book called "Ryōkei Ezo Gundan,"
it is written in detail that all the people thought to have died in battle at Hiraizumi actually hid themselves and
escaped to cross over to Ezo country. In that book,
in the section titled "Hitachibon Kaison Encounters Yoshitsune," it relates: "Yoshitsune returned to his main camp... setting fire to the hall, and in the confusion of smoke
he stole away toward Tsugaru, hoping to catch up with his lord, and when passing through Komagata Pass, he encountered an old man. His appearance was strange and very noble, and this mysterious person, seeing him, said, 'I will teach you a technique.
Follow me.' Though he was puzzled... [the man] gave him a scroll and said... you will obtain arts of divine transformation...
Kaison will ultimately return victorious to Japan." In this passage, "Hitachibon Kaison said to Yoshitsune, 'I shall take my leave from here...'"
