However, when Lord Toyotomi Hideyoshi conducted his campaign against the Hōjō of Odawara in Sagami Province, Sanboku was seriously ill and could not join the battle, so he sent his chief retainer as his representative. When the battle ended and it became known that Lord Sanboku had not participated in person, he was exiled to western provinces. His entire estate was given to his chief retainer. Thus [the retainer] governed Senboku and Akita, and around Keichō 7 (1602) there was a transfer to Mutsu Province. It is said that the one called Akita Jōsuke is a descendant of Lord Sanboku's chief retainer. Also, the monk Jōtokubō Kaison

was still alive in the Senboku domain. However, he had become senile and no longer spoke of the past. Also, in Kan'ei 7 (1630), Jōtokubō died. He always carried a small box wrapped in bamboo bark, never separating it from his body, wearing it around his neck morning and evening. When they opened it after his death, they found it contained a document in Lord Yoshitsune's own hand with his seal, addressed "To Jōtokubō." Seeing this, everyone knew he was indeed Jōtokubō, and so on, it is said. This Seietsu narrative tells how, around the time of the battle at Koromo River in Isawa District, Mutsu Province, after all who served under Judge Yoshitsune died in battle, only these four people survived: Seietsu, Jōtokubō, and two other close attendants.
