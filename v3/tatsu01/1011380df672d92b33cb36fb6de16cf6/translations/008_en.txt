【Right page】
【Main text】
There is a hermit sage on Mount Komagata, and because this divine hermit resides there, it is said that "Sanhoku" (mountain north) was rewritten with the characters for "Senboku" (hermit north). Even now there is a divine hermit on Mount Komagatake, and this place is also called Sennin Gongen, with this deity being enshrined in various locations. This hermit is Hitachibō Kaison. Occasionally, mountain woodcutters and others say they have seen him. Also, the "Seietsu Monogatari" states: "Concerning Yoshitsune's fate... his funeral was determined to be held at Numakura within Samnohazama, the residence of Lord Ōkura. Yoshitsune's remains were carried in a portable shrine, with Yoritomo on horseback, and the scene was beautiful up to the pillow. The retainers accompanying him were Benkei, the Suzuki brothers, Satō Saburō Yoshinobu, and Satō Shirō Yoshitada,

【Marginal note】
In southern Michinoku there is a mountain called Sennin Pass, and in the mountain village of Ikada in Hiraka District of Dewa Province, there is a shrine to Sennin Gongen. The reason for this is unclear.

【Left page】
【Main text】
Kanefusa - these ten people's remains were also treated the same as Judge Yoshitsune's funeral, and after completing this, Yoritomo departed. On the 9th day of the 7th month he went up to the capital, Yoritomo became Seii Taishōgun, and returned on the 25th day of the 10th month. On the 19th day of the 7th month of Bunji 5 (1189), General Yoritomo set out again for this Hiraizumi. His forces numbered 284,000 cavalry - this was to punish the descendants of Hidehira. Nishikido Tarō was killed on the 3rd day of the 9th month, and Senboku was captured. The General issued orders asking through messengers why this Mutsu faction had not engaged in battle within thirty days of opposing Yoritomo.
