General Rules (Hanrei)
1. Within the domain's fifteen counties (gun), there are boundaries both large and small in extent. For the extensive ones, one county extends across two volumes, while for the narrow ones,
   five counties are compiled into one volume. The county boundaries are marked with fine writing above the border lines as indicators.
1. The great temples in the illustrations are mostly those that have existed for over a thousand years since their founding. Following the changes of the times,
   during periods of national and regional turmoil, many either fell into ruin or suffered from fires. Therefore,
   the illustrations depict present-day scenic beauty, while their origins are recorded based on old records. Examples of this are
   Kōfuku-ji Temple and Yakushi-ji Temple.
1. Among the illustrations, there are large-scale depictions of people. Drawing the spirit of ancient poems serves to
   express the atmospheric qualities of those places. Also, illustrating actual events serves as an aid for the easy understanding of children and beginners.
   Kasuga Plain and Ryōben Cedar are examples of this.
1. Newly constructed halls and newly erected inscribed monuments are omitted here. We select and include those that are
   elegant, refined, and tasteful.
