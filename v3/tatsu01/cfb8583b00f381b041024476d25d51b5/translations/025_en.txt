The Haraitono Shrine (Shrine of the Purification Deity) enshrines Seoritsuhime《Note: The Hōei Record also calls this the Kumano Gongen》
　《Note: The stone lantern before the Haraitono Shrine is renowned throughout the land; total height: 6 shaku 1 sun 5 bu; the lantern chamber is hexagonal》【Illustration of stone lantern】

Kamigaki Forest and Kamigaki Mountain are north of the Haraitono Shrine
　《Note: Fūga Collection》
　　The leaves of grass in the divine fence forest have scattered and spread; only the pampas grass remains on the Kasuga Plain　In'e no Hyōe no Kami
　《Note: Gyokugin Collection》
　　On the day I cross mighty Kamigaki Mountain, will it be the final limit of my love-sick grief?　Ietaka

Chakutōden (the Arrival Hall) is to the right of Kamigaki Forest, built in the sixteenth year of Engi《Note: The attendants following the imperial envoy to the Kasuga Festival record their arrival and》
　《Note: then proceed to worship before the shrine》. Jigoku Valley (Hell Valley) is to the south of this《Note: Among the disciples of the Holy Priest Gedatsu, there was a scholar of great learning called the Priestly Superintendent Shōen. After his death, he supposedly possessed a woman's body and among various things he spoke through her:》
　《Note: "Through the compassionate means of our Great Deity, anyone who has had any encounter with the deity, no matter how grave and deep their sins, shall not be cast into the hells of other realms. A hell is established beneath Kasuga Plain,》
　《Note: where they are gathered, sprinkled with purified water, and made to hear sutras and dharani, so that they may be saved. Though I myself sank into the demonic realm, the purified water of compassionate means entered my mouth, freeing me from the three torments of heat,》
　《Note: and touching my ears with the teachings of the harmonious light manifest in traces, I have been able to reach the utmost joys of the afterlife—is this not a grace beyond measure?" Those who heard this all expressed deep admiration, as recorded in the Shasekishū》

The Enomoto Shrine is to the east of this. It is said to be the shrine of the local earth deity of Kasuga Mountain. The deity enshrined is the god Sarutahiko. The Seiryū Bridge (Blue Dragon Bridge) is

　　The deity who crossed the Blue Dragon Bridge—may they rejoice receiving the sacred water; instantly becoming Buddha in this very body　Holy Priest Gedatsu

Kensaki-ishi (Sword Tip Stone) is in Kamigaki Forest. It is passed down that if anyone—even forgetfully—should tread upon this stone, misfortune will follow, though the reason for this is unknown. To the east of this is the Fuji no Torii (Wisteria Torii)《Note: In ancient times, wisteria grew around this torii, blooming in magnificent profusion every spring, which is how it came to be called by this name》
　《Note: From the New Senzai Collection, composed for a scene depicting the Kasuga Festival ceremonies on a monthly folding screen for the enthronement ceremony in the third year of Genkō:》
　　If you should stop here, officials too must be mindful—beneath the shade of flowers at the Wisteria Torii　Emperor Go-Daigo
　《Note: The Hōei Record says, a poem composed on the naming of this torii as Bodai-mon (Gate of Enlightenment):》
　　For those who pass through the divine torii standing amid divine power—all ten thousand sins vanish away
