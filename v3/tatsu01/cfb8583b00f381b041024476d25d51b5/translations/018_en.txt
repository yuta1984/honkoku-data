【Right Page】
Because this is a female deity, she is enshrined in the auxiliary hall of Hiraoka Myōjin. According to the Shoku Nihongi, in the 9th month of Kashō 3, Councilor Fujiwara no Tasuku was dispatched with an imperial edict to bestow court ranks: Senior First Rank to the two great deities Takemikazuchi and Iwainushi, Junior First Rank to Ame-no-koyane-no-mikoto, and Senior Fourth Rank Upper to Hime-no-kami. Based on these court ranks, the theory that she is a wife deity may be reasonable.

Six small shrines of the Middle Courtyard (the area outside the sacred fence is called the Middle Courtyard):
Iwamoto Shrine (located southwest of the main shrine, dedicated to Sumiyoshi Myōjin), Shingo-ji (to the east), Aozakaki Shrine (south of Shingo-ji, with blue cloth offerings), Karazakaki Shrine (south of Aozakaki, with white cloth offerings), Anakuri Shrine (south of Karazakaki, dedicated to Anatsugi deity), Ikuri Shrine (south of Anakuri, dedicated to Takamusubi-no-mikoto)

Two small shrines of the Inner Courtyard (the area inside the sacred fence is called the Inner Courtyard):
Tachikarao deity (southern shrine), Hirai Tenjin (northern shrine, Ame-no-minakanushi-no-mikoto)

The Naorai Hall is where imperial envoys and high court officials conduct ceremonies. Also called Hakkō-ya because the Lotus Sutra Eight Lectures were performed there (the Eight Lectures began in Emperor Murakami's Tenryaku 1, held twice yearly. The chief was Minister Teishin, and the superintendent was Great Monk Hyōgen. The "Great Naorai Day" mentioned in the Kokin-shū also refers to this place).

Offering Hall (adjacent to Naorai Hall, where imperial envoys present offerings on festival days), Dance Hall (connected to Naorai Hall, built in Jōgan 1)

Deer Running (according to Kanbun records, located between the corridors. Every year on the night of the 5th day of the 2nd month, officials from the Konparu, Kongō, and Hōshō schools visit and perform the Okina dance. This is called Deer Running. At this time, sacred sake offerings emerge from the third shrine hall)

Apple Garden (in front of the Offering Hall, where offerings are presented on Kasuga Festival days), Purification Stream (refers to the small stream between the corridors. On Kasuga Festival days, imperial envoys perform ablutions here)

First Rank Bridge (refers to the bridge beyond the gate tower), Second Rank Bridge (refers to the bridge in front of the gate tower)

From Kasuga Koki, a poem about First Rank Bridge:
"Pray, people, for in this sorrowful world's glory, seek the path of health, longevity, and good fortune"

First Torii (according to Hōei records, this gate is called Nehan Gate, also known as Purple Gate), Sacred Platform (inside the corridors, according to Kanbun records, where biwa priests who recite the Earth Deity Sutra retreat)

【Left Page】
Transfer Hall (north of Hakkō-ya, according to Kanbun records, the place for temporary enshrinement during shrine reconstruction), South Gate (called the gate tower. According to Kanbun records, it was formerly a torii with a plaque, but lightning struck in summer of Hōki 3. Afterwards it became a two-story gate tower. Some say Fujiwara no Mitsuyori built it)

Manifestation Stone (south of the South Gate, the manifestation stone of the Red Youth), Wish-fulfilling Stone (south of the Manifestation Stone. In Kōhō 4, a bottomless hole opened here, so Head Priest Suketo deposited purification rituals for the complete Daihannya Sutra in ten thousand volumes), Fushō Bridge (also called Oai Bridge)

From Kasuga Koki:
"How grateful! Crossing the Fushō Bridge, how joyful to hear the offering kagura music"

Sake Hall (west of the corridors, where ritual sake is brewed. Contains Sake Hall Shrine and Hearth Hall Shrine, two seats), Offering Preparation Hall (southwest below the Court Ladies' Gate, built in Jōgan 1)

Shunki Cherry Tree (between the Court Ladies' Gate and Offering Preparation Hall, according to Kanbun records, called a famous tree unparalleled under heaven)

Kasuga Wakamiya enshrines the child of Ame-no-koyane, Ame-no-oshikumo-no-mikoto, as recorded in the Myōhō-yōshū (namely, the Wakamiya of Hiraoka Myōjin in Kawachi Province is Ame-no-oshikumo-no-mikoto). According to certain records, the Yoshida family records name Ninigi-no-mikoto, but this is only a secret teaching of the Wakamiya head priest family and unknown to others (Kasuga Shaki). The enshrinement of Wakamiya occurred on the 3rd day of the 3rd month of Chōhō 5, when the deity manifested between the second and third shrine halls. Nakatomi-no-muraji Koretada, fifth-generation descendant of Tokikaze, moved the deity to the third hall and offered prayers. After 130 years, on the 27th day of the 4th month of Chōshō 4, Sukefusa, eighth-generation descendant of Tokikaze, separately constructed a shrine hall and enshrined the deity. This is the present Wakamiya Great Myōjin.
