General Principles (Hanrei)
1. Within the territory, the fifteen counties have boundaries of varying sizes. The extensive ones span two volumes for one county, while the smaller ones
   consolidate five counties into one volume. The county boundaries are written in small characters above the divination diagrams to serve as markers.
1. The grand temples and monasteries depicted in the illustrations have existed for over a thousand years since their founding. Following the changes of the times,
   during periods of national and regional turmoil, some have fallen into ruin or have been destroyed by fire. Therefore,
   the illustrations show the current scenic beauty, while their origins are recorded based on ancient records. Examples of this include
   Kōfukuji Temple and Yakushiji Temple.
1. Among the illustrations are large depictions of human figures. Drawing the spirit of ancient poems serves to
   express the local atmosphere and scenery. Also, depicting actual events serves as an aid for the young and uneducated to understand easily.
   Kasugano and Ryōben Cedar are examples of this.
1. Newly constructed halls and newly erected inscribed monuments are omitted here. We select and include
   those things that are elegant and refined.
