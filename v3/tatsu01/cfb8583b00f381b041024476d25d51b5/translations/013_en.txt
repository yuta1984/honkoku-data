The capital of Heijō (Nara) was first built by Empress Genmei, the mother and consort of Emperor Monmu, in the second year of Wadō (709 CE).
《Note: Nara was also written as 那羅, 諾樂, 寕樂, 乃樂, 平, 楢》The capital was moved there in the third year, and seven emperors《Note: Genmei, Genshō, Shōmu, Kōken, the deposed emperor, Shōtoku, and Kōnin》established their imperial residence in Nara. Emperor Kanmu moved the capital back to Nagaoka Palace in Yamashiro Province in the third year of Enryaku (784), and in the thirteenth year (794) relocated to Heian-kyō. The Left Capital refers to present-day Nanto (Nara), while the Right Capital refers to Saikyō.

《Man'yōshū》
Blue-clayed Nara, the capital, is like fragrant blossoms in full bloom now

《Shin Chokusenshū》
In blue-clayed Nara's capital, the lodge built of plain wood—I cannot tire of dwelling there　　Emperor Shōmu

《Shūishū》
When will I send word by the messenger geese flying through the sky to Nara's capital?　Hitomaro

The site of the imperial palace is not in present-day Nara town, but in a place called "Tsuiji-no-uchi" (Within the Earthen Walls) located southeast of the southern road of Nijō Village in Chōshōji-gō, west of Kōfukuji Temple. Even now no rice is cultivated there, and there is a small shrine called "Dairi-no-miya" (Palace of the Imperial Residence).

《Kokinshū》　　《Poem by the Emperor of Nara; according to the commentary, Emperor Heizei, the Daidō Emperor》
In what has become the old village of Nara's capital, unchanged in color, the flowers still bloom

《Hōan Hyakushu, Violets》
Of Nara's capital where violets bloomed, only the foundation stones remain as mementos　　Minamoto no Nakamasa

《Zoku Shūishū》
Let me tell of the ancient times of the palace that bears the name of Nara's leaves, ages that have grown old　Hōin Kōchō

【Left Page】
Kasuga Field refers to the area from the Great Torii eastward to Kasuga Shrine. This place is named Kasuga because in the age of the gods, when Amaterasu Ōmikami subdued seven billion nine thousand evil deities and restored them to righteous hearts, bringing peace to all people, Amaterasu Ōmikami's divine heart was pure and peaceful like the gentle spring day, and she rejoiced in naming this land Kasuga, also naming the village Kasuga.

Moreover, Koyane-no-Okina of Kototama-subi-no-Mikoto was granted this Kasuga Forest and honored as Kasuga Palace. At the base of Mount Mikasa's lower rocky foundation, palace pillars were erected stoutly, and the four-pillar shrine halls were built magnificently. Worshippers visit constantly without cease, and divine miracles occur anew each day.
