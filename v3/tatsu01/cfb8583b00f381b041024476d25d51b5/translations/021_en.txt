Inner Sanctuary Minor Shrines: "Two shrines within Wakamiya's inner sanctuary" - Tachikarao no Kami shrine: "Enshrined to the south" - Tsugō no Kami shrine: "The spirit of Nakatomi no Sukefusa Ason, who moved Wakamiya here"
"After moving it, he died on the 24th day of the 12th month of Jinpei 2. Twenty-seven years later, in Jishō 2, through divine oracle he was venerated as Tsugō no Kami. Also, this shrine has what is called the Kasuga Mandala, a drawing from the vision of Lord Motomichi of Fugenji-dono during the Juei years"

"Izanagi no Mikoto and Izanami no Mikoto" - Saraki Shrine: "Hiruko deity" - Kii Shrine: "South of the outer sanctuary, four deities enshrined: Hinokuma deity, Isotakeru deity, Oya-hime, Kotsu-hime" - Benzaiten Shrine
"According to the Hōei Records, this Benzaiten was invited by Kōbō Daishi from Tenkawa. To its east is the place where the Great Master performed fire ceremonies" - Sueishi Stone: "South of the Thirty-eight Places. Said to be where Master Gedatsu worshipped the deity, also called Master Myōe"
"When Master Gedatsu invited the deity to his hermitage at Kasagi, it appeared in the form of a child and showed itself to the master with a divine poem, as recorded in the Shasekishū"

I shall go and protect the Hannya Platform, as long as the Buddha's teachings remain

Worship Hall: "According to the Kanbun Records, Emperor Toba first performed kagura in this hall on the 14th day of the 3rd month of Hōan 3. Also, Emperor Sutoku first built the hall's corridors in Chōshō 1. A poem from that time:"
How wonderful, today's eight shrine maidens of Kasuga - surely the gods too feel joy - Fujiwara no Tadafusa
"The poem screens in the worship hall were painted by Emperor Go-Kōmyō's imperial brush with colored costumes and court dress, with poems written by various families and secretly placed on the stone pavement of the Great Shrine. When the chief clerk became suspicious and reported to the court, the emperor decreed it a donation to the Four Place Deities, so it was housed in this worship hall. The screens are donated by Yahata Takimoto-bō"

Imperial Corridor: "According to Kanbun Records, the north side of the worship hall is called the Imperial Corridor. Buddha relics are housed within, representing the dual Shinto. The space between this corridor and the worship hall is called Hosotono"

Worship House: "According to Kanbun Records, located east of the worship hall. Various shifts serve at Wakamiya festivals. This building was constructed in Jishō 2 under Emperor Takakura. Every year, noh performances with torches are held at the South Gate for two days, and on the third day there are two-day noh performances on these stones. When Konparu Tayu is on duty, sacred sake is bestowed from Wakamiya shrine at the worship hall. For other tayus"

Five Houses: "Shinzō-ya, Kawara-ya, Kami-ya, Nishi-ya, and Moto-dangi-ya. Adding Chūin-ya makes six houses in total" - Shinzō-ya: "According to Kanbun Records, west of Moto-dangi-ya"
"Also called the House of Kasuga Deity's Manifestation. The main Buddha, Amida, is said to be the work of An'ami's lifetime. There is also a Daikokuten, An'ami's work, belonging to the Matsura merchant, said to have Saint Shinran's seal on the sole of its foot"

Kawara-ya: "According to Kanbun Records, houses Shakyamuni Buddha" - Kami-ya: "According to Kanbun Records, has invited the Sixteen Protector Deities" - Nishi-ya: "Houses Jizō deity"

Moto-dangi-ya: "According to Kanbun Records, the main object is the ten-volume Yogācāra-vijñānavāda text by Vasubandhu from Buddha's birthland as the Buddha body. In the treasure house to the east are various precious vessels. Every year on July 15th, these are displayed for people to worship. The imperial fan granted to Lecturer Chūsan. This fan was given in Ōwa 3 under Emperor Murakami, when there was a Lotus Sutra lecture at Seiryōden with twenty-five distinguished scholars from Nara and Mount Hiei holding five days and nights of debates"
"Lecturer Chūsan's eloquent words clearly revealed the essence of doctrine, so the emperor, deeply moved, bestowed this fan. It bears imperial poems:"

May your reign be like celestial robes rarely worn, eternal as summer rocks
Though white jewels keep their natural color, on autumn leaves they become crimson dew

"Kōbō Daishi's authentic handwriting of the Lotus Sutra, one sheet eight volumes in small characters; Mandenokōji Nobufusa's single-character incense flower three-prostration Five Great Vehicle Sutras (this sutra also appears in the Taiheiki), same hand's Yogācāra text; Minamoto no Yoshiie's five-tone威 armor and crimson-threaded armor with sleeve guards and front plates; armor donated by Minamoto no Yoshitsune; Taira no Munemori's votive text; armor donated by Taira no Koremochi; Kusunoki Masashige's armor; summoning bell said to be brought by Monk Hōzō from Enma's palace; screens painted by Sumiyoshi Hōgen depicting Kamo horse races"
