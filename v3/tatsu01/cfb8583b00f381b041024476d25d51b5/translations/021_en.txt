Inner Shrine Subsidiary Shrines (Naiin): Inner shrine of Wakamiya; there are two shrines. Tachikarao-no-shin: enshrined on the southern side. Tsugō-no-shin: the spirit of Nakatomi no Sukefusa Ason who moved the Wakamiya shrine here.
After this, he died on the 24th day of the 12th month of Jinpei 2 (1152). Twenty-seven years later in Jishō 2 (1178), through divine oracle he was revered as Tsugō-no-shin. Also in this shrine there is something called the Kasuga Mandala, which is a drawing from the dream vision of Fugen-ji-dono Motomichi-kō during the Jueietsu era.

Outer Shrine Subsidiary Shrines (Geiin): The outer shrine of Wakamiya. Hirose Shrine: commonly called Kishimojin. Kakehashi Shrine: Katsuraki deity. Thirty-eight Shrines: Kan-yamato-iware-hiko-no-mikoto.
Izanagi-no-mikoto and Izanami-no-mikoto. Saraki Shrine: Hiruko deity. Kii Shrine: located south of the outer shrine, with four deities enshrined - Hinokuma deity, Isotakeru deity, Ōya-hime deity, and Kotsu-hime deity. Benzaiten Shrine.
According to the Hōei Record, this Benzaiten was invited from Tenkawa by Kōbō Daishi. To its east is the place where the Master performed goma fire rituals. Sueishi (Sitting Stone): located south of the Thirty-eight Shrines, said to be the place where Gedatsu Shōnin (also called Myōe Shōnin) worshipped the deity.
It is said that when Gedatsu Shōnin invited the deity to his hermitage at Kasagi, the deity appeared in the form of a child and showed himself to the master, composing a divine poem recorded in the Shasekishū:
"I will go and guard the Hannya platform as long as Shakyamuni's teachings exist"

Worship Hall (Haiden): According to the Kanbun Record, Emperor Toba first had kagura performed in this hall on the 14th day of the 3rd month of Hōan 3 (1122). Also, Emperor Sutoku first built the corridor of the worship hall in Chōshō 1 (1132). The poem from that time:
"How wonderful, today's eight shrine maidens of Kasuga - surely even the gods must feel joy" - Fujiwara no Tadafusa

The poem immortals of the worship hall were painted by Emperor Go-Kōmyō's imperial brush with colored costumes and dress, and poems were written by various families. When these were secretly placed on the stone pavement of the Great Shrine, the chief clerk found it suspicious and reported to the imperial court, whereupon the emperor decreed they be donated to the Four Shrine Deities and placed in this worship hall. The folding screens were donated by Yahata Takimoto-bō.

Imperial Corridor: According to the Kanbun Record, the north of the worship hall is called the Imperial Corridor. Buddha relics are enshrined within, representing the Ryōbu Shinto. The space between this corridor and the worship hall is called Hosodono.

Hai-no-ya: According to the Kanbun Record, located east of the worship hall where various officials of Wakamiya take turns serving. This building was constructed in Jishō 2 (1178) during Emperor Takakura's reign. Every year, Takigi Nō is performed at the South Great Gate for two days, and on the third day there is a two-day Nō performance on these stones. When Konparu Tayū is on duty, sacred sake is bestowed from the Wakamiya shrine at the worship hall. Other masters do not receive this honor.

【Left Page】
Kureai Bridge: According to the Kanbun Record, there is a small bridge on the narrow path behind the worship hall.

Five Buildings (Itsutsu-no-ya): New Construction Building, Tile Building, Upper Building, West Building, Original Sermon Building. Including the Middle Court Building, these are also called the Six Buildings.

New Construction Building: According to the Kanbun Record, located west of the Original Sermon Building. Also called Kasuga Deity Manifestation Building in the world. The principal Buddha is Amida Buddha, said to be An'ami's masterwork. There is also a Daikokuten, An'ami's work that belonged to Matsura Chōja, said to have Saint Shinran's seal on the sole of its foot.

Tile Building: According to the Kanbun Record, enshrines Shakyamuni Buddha. Upper Building: According to the Kanbun Record, invited the sixteen protective deities. West Building: installed Jizō deity.

Original Sermon Building: According to the Kanbun Record, the principal object is the ten-volume Yuishiki-ron (Consciousness-only treatise) by Seshin Bodhisattva of Buddha-birth country used as the Buddha body. In the treasure house to the east are various precious vessels, which are displayed for public viewing every year on the 15th day of the 7th month.

Imperial brush fan bestowed upon Chūsan I-kō. This fan was given during Emperor Murakami's reign in Ōwa 3 (963) when there was a Lotus Sutra lecture at Seiryōden, with twenty-five eminent monks from Nanto and Hokurei conducting five days and nights of doctrinal debates. Because Chūsan I-kō eloquently revealed the essence of doctrine, the emperor bestowed this fan in great admiration. There are imperial poems:
"Your reign is like heavenly robes rarely coming, an eternal rocky peak even in summer"
"Whether it be white jewels or their own color, when placed among autumn leaves, they become crimson dew"

Various treasures include: Kōbō Daishi's authentic Lotus Sutra in small characters on eight scrolls, Manri-koji Nobufusa-kyō's one-character incense flower three-reverence five-section Mahāyāna sutra (also mentioned in Taiheiki), same author's Yuishiki-ron, Minamoto no Yoshiie's five-tone威鎧 and crimson-threaded armor with front plates, armor of Minamoto no Yoshitsune, prayer document by Taira no Munemori, armor of Taira no Koremochi, armor of Kusunoki Masashige, summoning bell brought from Enma King's palace by Hōzō Sōzu, folding screen painted by Sumiyoshi Hōgen depicting Kamo horse racing.

Pine Building: the Shogun family's building. Tajima Building: Konoe-dono's building; only ruins remain of these two buildings now. Funato Building: Kujō-dono's building. According to Kanbun Record, first built by Tsukiwa Kanesane-kō in the 1st month of Kenkyū 1 (1190). There is a Funato shrine that protects sea voyages.

Sugi Building: One-jō-in temple head's building. Tsubaki Building: Great Shrine priest's building. Three Great Shrines Building.
