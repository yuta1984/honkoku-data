**Inner Precinct Minor Shrines** (The inner precinct of the Wakamiya; two shrines)

**Tachikara-o no Kami** (Enshrined to the south)

**Tsūkō no Kami** (The spirit of Nakatomi Sukefusa Ason. After the Wakamiya was transferred here, he passed away on the 24th day of the 12th month of Ninpei 2 [1152]. Twenty-seven years later, in Jishō 2 [1178], by divine oracle, he was enshrined and venerated as Tsūkō no Kami. Also at this shrine is what is called the "Kasuga Mandala," a picture that appeared in a dream to Lord Motomichi of Fukenji-dono during the Juei era [1182–1184].)

**Outer Precinct Minor Shrines** (The outer precinct of the Wakamiya)

**Hirose Shrine** (Commonly called Kishimojin, the deity of children)

**Kakehashi Shrine** (Deity of Katsuragi)

**Thirty-Eight Shrine** (Deities: Kamuyamato Iwarehiko no Mikoto [Emperor Jinmu], Izanagi no Mikoto, and Izanami no Mikoto)

**Saraki Shrine** (Hiruko no Kami)

**Kii Shrine** (Located to the south of the outer precinct. Four deity seats are enshrined: Hinokuma no Kami, Isotakeru no Kami, Ōyatsuhime no Kami, and Kotsuhime no Kami)

**Benzaiten Shrine** (According to the Hōei Record, this Benzaiten was invited [by prayer] by Kōbō Daishi from Tenkawa. To the east is the place where the Daishi performed goma fire rituals.)

**Suei-ishi (The Resting Stone)** (Located to the south of the Thirty-Eight Shrine. It is said to be the spot where the holy monk Gedatsu Shōnin worshipped the deity; others say it was Myōe Shōnin. When Gedatsu Shōnin invited the deity to his hermitage at Kasagi, the deity appeared in the form of a child and presented himself before the monk, offering a divine poem. This is recorded in the Shasekishū.)

> "I shall go, go and protect,
> the Hannya platform,
> For as long as Śākyamuni's sacred teachings endure."

**Haiden (Worship Hall)** (According to the Kanbun Record, on the 14th day of the 3rd month of Hōan 3 [1122], during the reign of Retired Emperor Toba, kagura was performed here for the first time. In Chōshō 1 [1132], during the reign of Retired Emperor Sutoku, the corridor of the haiden was first built. A poem composed at that time:)

> "How wondrous— the eight maidens of Kasuga today;
> Surely even the gods rejoice at the sight."
> — Fujiwara Tadafusa

(The poet immortals [歌仙] of the haiden were drawn in the imperial hand of Emperor Gomyōin [Go-Kōmyō], with colored costumes and robes. The poems were written by various noble families, and were secretly placed upon the paving stones of the great shrine. The official on duty at the time found this suspicious and reported it to the imperial court. The Emperor issued a decree that these were offerings to the Four Shrines of the Myōjin, and they were accordingly enshrined in the haiden. The folding screen was donated by Takimoto-bō of Yawata.)

**Go-Rō (Sacred Corridor)** (According to the Kanbun Record, the area north of the haiden is called the go-rō. Buddhist relics are enshrined within, expressing the Ryōbu Shinto tradition. The space between this corridor and the haiden is called the Hosodono.)

**Hai-no-ya** (According to the Kanbun Record, this is located to the east of the haiden. The shrine officials of the Wakamiya take turns on duty here. This building was constructed in Jishō 2 [1178], during the reign of Emperor Takakura. Each year, takigi nō performances are held for two days at the South Great Gate, and on the third day, two days of nō are performed on this stone. When the Komparu troupe head is on duty, sacred sake is received from the Wakamiya sanctuary in the haiden. For other troupe heads,)

**Kure-ai no Hashi (Twilight Bridge)** (According to the Kanbun Record, there is a small bridge along a narrow path behind the haiden; this is what is referred to.)

**Five Houses (Gokano-ya)** (The New House, the Tile House, the Upper House, the West House, and the Original Lecture House—these five. Adding the Middle Precinct House makes six houses in total.)

**Shinzō-ya (New House)** (According to the Kanbun Record, it is located to the west of the Original Lecture House. It is also commonly known as the "House of Kasuga Deity's Manifestation." The principal image is an Amida Buddha, said to be the life work of An'ami. There is also a Daikokuten, also An'ami's work, which was the personal Buddhist image of Matsura Chōja; it is said that the seal of Shinran Shōnin is on the sole of its foot.)

**Kawara-no-ya (Tile House)** (According to the Kanbun Record, a Śākyamuni Buddha is enshrined here.)

**Kami-no-ya (Upper House)** (According to the Kanbun Record, the Sixteen Guardian Deities of the Dharma were invited here.)

**Nishi-no-ya (West House)** (A Jizō Bodhisattva is enshrined here.)

**Moto-dangi-ya (Original Lecture House)** (According to the Kanbun Record, the principal image is the Vijñaptimātratā-siddhi (Yuishikiron/Cheng Weishi Lun) in ten volumes, composed by Vasubandhu Bodhisattva of the Land of the Buddha's Birth, enshrined as the buddha-body. In the treasure repository to the east are numerous sacred treasures. Every year on the 15th day of the 7th month, these are displayed for all people to worship. A fan written in the imperial hand was bestowed upon Chūzan Ikō. This fan was presented by Emperor Murakami in Ōwa 3 [963], when a Hokke-kō [Lotus Sutra lecture] was held at the Seiryōden, with twenty-five eminent monks from Nanto and Hokurei debating for five days in five rounds. Chūzan Ikō's words were brilliantly eloquent, beautifully illuminating the essence of the teaching, and so the Emperor, overwhelmed with admiration, bestowed the fan. On it are poems in the imperial hand:)

> "Your reign— like the feathered robe of heaven,
> rarely worn yet ever unfading,
> like the enduring ancient rock."

> "Is it a white jewel? Its color remains the same,
> yet when placed upon the autumn leaves,
> it becomes a crimson drop of dew."

(Genuine handwritten manuscript of the Lotus Sutra by Kōbō Daishi—one sheet per volume, eight volumes in fine script. The Ichiji Kōke Sanrai Gobu Daijō Kyō [Five-Part Mahāyāna Sūtra] by Lord Madenokōji Nobufusa—one character per flower offering—mentioned also in the Taiheiki. The Yuishikiron in the same hand. Minamoto no Yoshiie's five-cord laced armor [goion-odoshi] and crimson-laced armor [hi-odoshi] with breastplate. Armor donated by Minamoto no Yoshitsune. A written prayer by Taira no Munemori. Armor donated by Taira no Koremochi. Armor of Kusunoki Masashige. A summoning bell said to have been brought from the Palace of Enma by the monk Hōzō Sōzu. A folding screen painted by Sumiyoshi Hōgen depicting the horse races at Kamo.)

**Matsu-no-ya (Pine House)** (The house of the Kubō [Ashikaga shogunal] family)

**Tajima-no-ya** (The house of the Konoe family. Both of these two houses now exist only as their former sites.)

**Funato-no-ya** (The house of the Kujō family. According to the Kanbun Record, Lord Tsukiwa Kanezane first had it built in the 1st month of Kenkyū 1 [1190]. There is a Funato Shrine within, which protects those at sea.)

**Sugi-no-ya (Cedar House)** (The house of the Ichijōin monzeki [princely abbacy])

**Tsubaki-no-ya (Camellia House)** (The house of the Ōmiya head priest)

**Sandaisha-no-ya (House of the Three Great Shrines)**
