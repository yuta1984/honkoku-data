This occurred in the 6th year of Kaei, year of the Metal Ox (1853), on the 2nd day of the 2nd month, at the 9th hour of the day (around noon). Beginning with the castle town of Odawara in Sagami Province, the earthquake affected: to the east - the Tamura River area, Atsugi, Hagino Mountain, Naka District, Koza District, Aya District, Godo, Oiso post station, Nakamura, Kaneko, Suzukawa, Minowa, Usui, Isehara, Koyasu area, Oyama area, Osumi District and nearby mountains, Kamimura, Yaimura, Okamoto, Hayakawa, Ishibashi Mountain, Futago Mountain, Hakone and all seven hot spring resorts, Yumoto, Hatajuku, Yamanaka, Mitsutani area; to the west - Atami area in Izu Province, Mishima post station, Umijiri Pass, Iwawatari Pass, and in Suruga Province as far as the Numazu area, including Hara post station and Yoshiwara post station area, all experiencing considerable shaking. To the north, in Aiko District's Mimasu River village, the mountains in this area were greatly disturbed, and in Tsukui District's Aonohara, Doshi River, Nezumizaka, and Hashimoto area experienced some damage. Even the Obara area and Seki area felt significant tremors. The shaking continued until past the 9th hour of night (around midnight), with countless tremors, but there were five major shakes before it finally stopped, bringing relief to all people. Therefore, to inform relatives and acquaintances in various provinces of this event, I record the details on this single sheet.
