Ansei 2, October 7th
Great Edo Earthquake and Fires - Main Investigation

From around 10 PM on the 2nd of this month, there was a great earthquake that seemed to turn heaven and earth upside down. About 70-80% of houses and storehouses collapsed, and fires broke out in various places among them. The number of dead and injured is countless, with 38 locations becoming sources of fire. Reports continue to come in as follows:

Asakusa Umaya-gashi, Kaya-cho, Kawara-cho, Zaimoku-cho, Okura-mae, Kurofune-cho, Suwa-cho, Komagata-cho, Namiki-cho, Hanakawado, Yama-no-yado, all three theaters in Saruwaka-cho without exception, from Imado area to Mitsuya, all of Yoshiwara, Tamachi, Kanasugi area, Shitaya, Ueno Hirokoji, Onari Kaido, Soto-Kanda, Sakuma-cho area. In the inner palace grounds, fires broke out in four locations, with more than half of the great and lesser daimyo residences suffering from secondary fires. Outside Toranomon, around Kyobashi, Shiba Zojoji temple and Shinmei shrine suffered great damage. All of Fukagawa, Eitaiji temple burned down, Honjo area, Eko-in temple, then Kameido Tenjin, Yanagishima, Koume area, Gohyaku Rakan, Tsukiji Monzeki, Teppozu, extending to Tsukuda area all burned. Two locations caught fire below Atago, Shibaguchi, Takanawa, Sengakuji, Tokaiji, Mita, Shirokane, Meguro area also suffered great damage, Dentsu-in, Hakusan, Nezu, Komagome, Oiwake area - all suffered great devastation with countless dead and injured. Among the remaining houses outside these areas, some are warped or half-collapsed, and everyone is sleeping outdoors.

Although the earthquakes have not yet stopped shaking, the fires were finally extinguished around 6 AM on the 4th day. This is an unprecedented great disaster. This main investigation report came as the fourth additional report last night and is hereby submitted. The remainder will be presented in more detail in a later volume.
