Great Tsunami of the Three Hokuriku Prefectures - Collapse Song
Printed: July 30, 1896 (Meiji 29)
Published: August 3, same year
Artist, Printer and Publisher:
Kusuyama Kichijiro
2 Shinyoshicho, Nihonbashi Ward

[Upper Section]
〽The state of the great tsunami disaster chaos in the Hokuriku region
　　　is so pitiful one cannot bear to look　Ho-kai
〽The terrifying, fierce force of the great tsunami
　　　leaves only five minutes to escape　Ho-kai
〽The ultimate tragedy of this great tsunami
　　　will be told for generations to come　Ho-kai
〽Before one's eyes, houses collapse and are swept away
　　　people standing without even chopsticks in hand　Ho-kai
〽The shocking, unprecedented water disaster
　　　struck Aomori, Iwate, and Miyagi prefectures　Ho-kai
〽The fishermen who were saved by being on boats had strong luck
　　　truly it must be divine fate　Ho-kai
〽Though we resign ourselves to it being the times, we still complain
　　　such is the wretchedness of ordinary people　Ho-kai
〽More frightening than earthquakes are great tsunamis
　　　even the escape routes become fields of water　Ho-kai
〽Some children cry separated from their parents
　　　while others are separated from wives and husbands in life　Ho-kai

[Lower Section]
〽How tragic
　　Over 60,000
　　　casualties
　The remaining wives and children
　　　are pitiful
　　　　　　　Ho-kai　
〽The blessing is the government's
　　merciful relief assistance
　All people shed tears
　　　　over the rice
　　　　　　　Ho-kai
〽All within the four seas
　　are brothers, and so
