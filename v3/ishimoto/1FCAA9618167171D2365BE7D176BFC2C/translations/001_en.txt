Fire Prevention
Songs of Fire Prevention

For fire sources: Those in charge should patrol and take precautions to prevent fires from starting within
For fire sources: Even in summer, do not be careless; especially be cautious of burning mosquito incense
For fire sources: At construction sites with wood shavings, be careful after burning scattered debris
For fire sources: Be careful with pipes, lanterns, flint boxes, lamp wicks, kindling, and kotatsu
For fire sources: Be careful with discarded ashes, fire extinguishing pots, and places related to fire
For fires: Prepare ladders, ropes, buckets and well-pulleys; those with storehouses should prepare earth
For fires: Keep children, towels, sashes, belts, medicine and pocket money always ready
For fire storehouses: Entering for inspection is problematic, so block mouse holes and be careful
For fires: Prepare a change of clothes in advance for when emergencies arise
For fires: Always maintain hand buckets, water jars, and garden ponds without letting them dry up
For fires: Do not leave bath water flowing at night; let it cool in the morning - this too is precaution
When fire occurs: Help elderly, children, and sick people escape without injury
When fire occurs: Do not hastily put fire-containing braziers and fire containers into the storehouse
When fire occurs: Protect treasures, death registers, various ledgers, and documents from burning
When fire occurs: If something is extremely important, take precautions without putting it in the storehouse
When fire occurs: There are many thieves, so look at faces carefully when handing over belongings
When fire occurs: Do not sacrifice precious life by focusing too much on gold and silver
