Oil-pressing licensed operators are prohibited from sending purchase orders for rapeseed and cotton seeds to provinces along the Hokurikudo, San'indo, San'yodo, Nankaidō, and Saikaidō routes, excluding Harima Province from the remaining provinces, and direct sales are also forbidden in those provinces, which should understand this directive.

Article 1: Since the production of daily lamp oil in the aforementioned provinces is permitted, the designations "hand-made" and "hand-pressed" shall be discontinued henceforth. Oil seeds grown anywhere - whether in main rice fields, dry fields, vacant land, or anywhere in the domain - may be used. For oils needed by domain lords and for daily use by samurai families, both in directly controlled territories and private domains, annual consumption targets shall be established. Oil pressing should be conducted according to local convenience, not limited to water-powered or manual methods, ensuring no hindrance to oil seeds that should be sent to Osaka, Sakai, and Hyogo. Various types such as white-pressed oil and plum blossom oil may also be produced at will to meet domestic needs.

Article 1: At this time, an oil collection center has been established at Hommachi Hashizume-cho in Osaka, and those previously called oil dealers, Edo-guchi dealers, and Kyoto-guchi dealers have all been renamed as oil wholesalers. Oil trading shall be conducted at this collection center, so if there is surplus oil beyond domestic use, it should be sent to this collection center and sold to the oil wholesalers.
