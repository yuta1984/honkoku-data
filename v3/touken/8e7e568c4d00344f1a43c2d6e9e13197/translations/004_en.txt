1. Tomomitsu 《Marginal note: Lower-Upper [rank]》 Same reign, same period

The shape of the tachi has a refined, austere quality; the shinogi is wide and high. The forging is masame-hada (straight grain), extremely fine. The ground color is dark. It has a small irregular (komidare) temper pattern. From the base (koshi-moto), it is tempered out in a fine, straight manner, and around the mono-uchi (striking area) it becomes a broad temper (hiro-ba). While the thin-straight (hoso-sugu) section from the base is superior, the level/quality of the hamon itself is slightly inferior. The color and luster of the hamon is skillfully done. There are oral traditions.

1. Kunitsugu　During the reign of Emperor Go-Daigo, around the Gentoku era (1329–1331), the nephew (son of a sister) of Kunigo (possibly Bingo Province)

The shape of the tachi has a whitish-yellow appearance; the left side of the kissaki (point) has a deep iori (ridge). There is generally a thick hi (groove). The cross-sectional appearance (men-buri) resembles that of Kuniyuki. The forging is itame-hada (wood-grain). This work also has midare-ba (irregular temper) tempered at the base, and toward the tip it transitions to a yellowish straight temper (suguha) with ashi (extensions) worked in.
