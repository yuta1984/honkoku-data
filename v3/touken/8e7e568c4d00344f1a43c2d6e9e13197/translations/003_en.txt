1. Tenkoku 《Marginal note: Superior Imperial Treasure》 From the reign of Emperor Monmu, around the Taihō era (701-704), Yamato Province

Regarding the shape, appearance, and oral tradition of this tachi sword: The forging is itame-hada (wood-grain pattern), extremely fine. The ground color has a bluish tint and is clear. The底 (bottom) shows the character of絶え (fade), like sand left unwashed by returning waves at a sandy point where waves crash against the shore - such is the forging. The ground metal is completely without cloudiness. The hamon (temper line) has abundant nie (martensitic crystals), the kissaki (point) is small and burned tight. It has komidare-ba (small irregular temper pattern) with ashi (legs/extensions) tempered in.
