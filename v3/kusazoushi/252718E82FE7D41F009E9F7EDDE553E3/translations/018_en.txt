From then on, Shakyamuni was celebrated as the Great Universal World-Honored One, and having grown weary of the pleasures of the Pure Land, he now turned his attention toward the Tatsumi, Shinjuku, and southern districts. However, as this would make for too long a tale and become tedious, the author decided to defer these three locations to a sequel and pause the entertainment for now.
