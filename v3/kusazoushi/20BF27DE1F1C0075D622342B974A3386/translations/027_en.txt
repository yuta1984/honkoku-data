Understanding these as mere words, he humbly bows on the hanamichi (runway stage) 《No, no》- these are not words of praise at all! The words I, Jūrokubeei, called out in my performance were words of reproach 《Listen》That Tachibana-sama there - last night you filled my hungry belly, and in gratitude for that kindness, I offered an unnecessary donation to your troupe of plants and trees, and from the sidelines Jūrokubeei butted in with his unnecessary arguments - what is the meaning of this? Like the skin of bottle gourd and evening glory, speaking of "one cup, one country" - things that cannot be said in flowery Edo - even this is like a bat in a village without birds

A carefree existence in the mountains. Though this is old-fashioned, taking the old and making it new, adding antiquity through novelty - using this as the seed of kyōgen (comic theater), forcing connections in the plot, showing it as one thing but it being another, showing it as something while making it known as that something, working the lips subtly or performing with deep profundity - without this, whether in jōruri or kyōgen, there are no gaps at all. Just wanting desperately to show something new, like putting wings on a dog
