The Curtain Rises on Monster Extermination

Pine and cypress do not lose their integrity even in snow, and brave warriors do not bend their hearts even in hardship—so they say. Ah, indeed so! Though I don't possess even a single mon coin, my heart is that of a stalwart warrior. Using straw sandals and starlight as my lantern, I am a traveling crow with no fixed lodging. With my foolish nature but proud temperament, I continued my journey and crossed through the castle town of Odawara, arriving at Mieda Bridge. From here to the right lies Mount Yu, famous for the Seven Hot Springs of Hakone. Since this is a renowned place, I thought to take a look as a diversion for my aimless travels. Ah, the sun has already reached the Hour of the Monkey. Jūrokubeē climbs impetuously, entering deep into the unfamiliar mountaintop. White clouds encircle the peaks like a sash around the mountain's waist, and the waters of Nunobiki Waterfall Princess are like ten thousand bushels of bright pearls, leaping from the steep cliffs like a single strip of white silk hanging from the perilous peaks.
