Respectfully submitted
Articles regarding the deputy administrator position for the Tōji temple estate of Taira-shō in Wakasa Province

1. Having been appointed as deputy administrator by the temple's grace as I requested, I shall not consider any selfish or negligent matters, whether internal or external.

2. Regarding this appointment's imperial messenger and official documents' reports, as well as other new administrative matters and below, I will responsibly collect and transport the annual tribute, spring harvest, summer harvest, autumn land rent and other monetary payments without delay beyond their due dates.

3. When transporting the annual tribute of spring harvest and below, I will necessarily submit them with official documents bearing the co-signatures of the public officials.

4. When developing new rice fields from riverbanks and flood plains, or cultivating new dry fields and below, even if the amount is small, I will not keep it secret but will report it and maintain fairness.

5. Regarding the peaceful market, I will especially maintain fairness, and even for small amounts, without private interests or selfish considerations, I will question sellers and proceed accordingly. The deputy administrator's most important duty lies solely in this matter, which I will especially keep in mind.

6. Regarding the land holdings of this estate, whether assigned to temples and shrines or to village headmen and peasants, I will never grant them arbitrarily but must always seek proper consultation.

7. When natural disasters such as floods occur in the local area, regardless of the appeals of village headmen and peasants, I will especially maintain fairness and report according to the actual situation.
