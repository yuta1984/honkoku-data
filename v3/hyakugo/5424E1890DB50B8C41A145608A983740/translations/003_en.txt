Articles concerning the position of deputy official for the ryōke side of Tarashō in Wakasa Province, domain of Tōji Temple

Article 1: Having requested appointment and been installed as deputy official through the temple's benevolence, one must not engage in arbitrary or negligent conduct, whether in internal or external matters.

Article 2: Following the intent of the imperial messenger of Kōshō 2 (1456) and the reports of public officials, as well as all new operations, the annual tribute (nengu) and substitute money for spring crops, summer crops, and autumn land rent must be transported without exceeding one's allocated portion, taking full responsibility.

Article 3: When transporting the annual tribute of spring crops and other items, one must submit them with documents bearing the joint seal of public officials.

Article 4: When developing new rice fields and dry fields including riverbank fields and offering fields, one must not conceal anything, however small, and must maintain fairness.

Article 5: Regarding markets, one must especially maintain fairness, not engage in any private manipulation however small, and conduct business by engaging in dialogue with merchants. The most important duty of a deputy official lies solely in this matter, which must be kept foremost in mind.

Article 6: Regarding the landed property of this estate, whether assigned to temples and shrines or to village headmen and peasants, one must not make grants arbitrarily but must always seek proper consultation.

Article 7: When natural disasters such as floods occur in the locality, one must not rely on the statements of village headmen and peasants alone, but must especially maintain fairness and report according to actual conditions.

Article 8: Regarding expenses for courtesy toward the provincial governor (shugo), even a single sen must be handled fairly without private considerations.

Article 9: When legal disputes arise in the locality, one must report them immediately and not act arbitrarily.

Article 10: Even if dismissed from the deputy official position due to failure to submit annual tribute, the outstanding amount shall be collected through collateral of equivalent value. Therefore, regarding collateral such as residences and privately held rice fields, if the outstanding amount becomes enormous, these shall become the permanent property of the temple.
