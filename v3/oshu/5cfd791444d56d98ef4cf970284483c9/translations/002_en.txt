[The name was changed to] Koyanagawa Uneme Moritsune.

In the fourth year of Ansei (1857), he was appointed as a companion/attendant to his lord, and received various gifts on that occasion. In the first month of the third year of Keiō (1867), he was appointed as the head of the lord's personal attendants (go-kinjū-bantō). During the Boshin War, he served as commander of the Toyama encampment, concurrently serving as head of the rifle corps, and later was promoted to Senior Guard Commander (ō-bantō).

In the first year of Meiji (1868), upon the occasion of the Restoration of Imperial Rule, the return of governance by the Tokugawa Shogunate, and the return of lands and people (hanseki-hōkan), he surrendered his fief lands and was registered among the shizoku (former samurai class). He took up temporary residence in Yanagawa Village, Esashi District, Rikuchū Province — his former domain — in the area of Notezaki, restored his original family name, and from this point changed [his surname] to "Date."

His wife was the eighteenth daughter of Kakuda Ishikawa Yamato. Her common name was San.

　Son: Ainnosuke, later known as Moriyasu.
　　　Succeeded the branch family of Koyanagawa Sukugorō.
　　　Later relocated to Hokkaidō.

　Son: Yūnoshin, later known as Moriyuki.
　　　Succeeded the family of Itō Gentai of Mizusawa.
　　　His wife was Se.

　Son: Fusanoshin
　　　Born in the eighth month of the fourth year of Ansei, the year of the Snake (1857); died at one year of age.
　　　Posthumous Buddhist name: Seigan-in Kōtoku Suiko
　　　Interred at Butsuganji Temple, Aramachi, Sendai.
