[The subject] changed his name to Koyanagawa Uneme Moritsune.
　In the fourth year of Ansei (1857), he was appointed as a companion to the lord (domain head), and received various gifts. In the first month of the third year of Keiō (1867), he was appointed head of the lord's personal attendants (go-kinjū-bantō). During the Boshin War (1868), he became the commander of the Tomiyama mountain encampment and concurrently served as head of the rifle corps. [Marginal note: He was later promoted to ō-bantō (senior guard commander).]

　Following the Restoration of Imperial Rule, in conjunction with the return of governing authority from the former Tokugawa shogunate and the return of lands and people [to the Emperor], he surrendered his stipend lands (saichi) and was placed in the rank of shizoku (former samurai). He took up residence in Yanagawa Village, Nozaki, Esashi District, Rikuchū Province — his former domain — and reverted to his original family name, henceforth changing it to Date.

His wife was the eighteenth daughter of Kakuda Ishikawa Yamato. Secular name: San.

　Son: Ainōsuke, later renamed Moriyasu

　Son: Yūnoshin, later renamed Moriyuki
　　　Succeeded the household of Itō Genshin of Mizusawa
　　　Wife: Se

　Son: Fusanoshin
　　Died in infancy in the eighth month of the fourth year of Ansei, the year of the Snake (1857), at the age of one.
　Buddhist posthumous name: Seigan-in Kōtoku Mizuko
　　Buried at Butsuganje Temple, Aramachi, Sendai [Transcription note: As the current Butsuganje Temple retains the traditional character 佛, it will be transcribed as 佛 unless otherwise noted.]
