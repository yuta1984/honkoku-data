## Junior First Rank Minister of the Interior; First Granted the Fujiwara Surname in the Eighth Year of Hakuhō (679 CE)

**Taishokkan Kamatari** (Fujiwara no Kamatari)

**Fuhito** — Left Minister of State; granted Senior First Rank and the title of Grand Minister of State (Daijō-daijin)

**Fusasaki** — Consultant (Sangi); Junior Second Rank; Minister of Ceremonial (Shikibu-kyō); granted the title of Left Minister of State and Head of the Fujiwara Clan (Uji no Chōja); further granted Grand Minister of State and Senior First Rank

**Okuna** — Left Minister of State; General of the Left; known as "Minister of Kawabe"; Senior First Rank

**Washitori** — Junior Assistant Minister of the Central Affairs Ministry　　**Fujitsugu Takafusa** — Governor of Echizen

**San'in** — Middle Counselor (Chūnagon)

### Genealogy of the Fujiwara Clan — Date Lineage

Descendant of Okuna, the fifth son of Fujiwara no Asomi Fusasaki, Middle Counselor San'in
*(Interlinear note: Son of Takafusa, Governor of Echizen)*

- Born in the fifth year of Tenchō (828 CE), the year of Tsuchinoe-Saru, during the reign of Emperor Junna
- Rose to prominence during the reigns of Emperors Ninmyō, Montoku, Seiwa, Yōzei, and Kōkō
- Died at the age of sixty in the first year of Kanpyō (889 CE), the year of Tsuchinoto-Tori, during the reign of Emperor Uda
- During the Jōgan era (859–877 CE), enshrined the deity Kasuga Myōjin and founded the Yoshida Shrine. Furthermore, it is said that this person is the originator of the culinary arts (the art of the kitchen knife) in Japan
