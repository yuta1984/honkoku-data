Kanesanji (兼三) — Governor of Mutsu (陸奥守)
Nakamasa (中正) — Junior Fourth Rank, Governor of the Left Capital (左京大夫) / Governor of Settsu (摂津守) | Yasuchika (安親) — Senior Third Rank, Councillor (参議)
Tamemori (為盛) — Junior Fourth Rank, Lower Grade / Governor of Echizen (越前守) | Sadatō (定任) — Junior Fourth Rank, Upper Grade; Governor of Chikuzen (筑前守); Governor of Hizen (肥前守) / Governor of Iga (伊賀守); Governor of Yamato (大和守)
Sanemune (実宗) — Junior Fourth Rank, Lower Grade; Assistant Governor of Hitachi (常陸介) / Governor of Noto (能登守); Governor of Hizen (肥前守) | Suetaka (季孝) — Junior Official of the Empress's Household (皇后宮少進)
Iehiro (家周) — Official of the Bureau of Attendants (大舎人) | Mitsutaka (光隆) — Unofficial Chamberlain of the Taikenmon'in (待賢門院非蔵人)
Tomomuné (朝宗) — Unofficial Chamberlain of the Takamatsuin (高松院非蔵人). In the middle of Bunji 5 [1189], he first traveled down to the Ōshū (Mutsu) region and took up residence in Date District. This marks the beginning of the annual records of successive generations of this family. Those who accompanied Tomomuné from Kyoto: Harada, Horikoshi, Kan, and Iwano.
Munemura (宗村) — Entered the Buddhist priesthood as the Lay Monk of Nakamura-Hitachi (中村常陸守入道), with the Buddhist name Nensei (念西). Passed away at age 71 at the local Katsuji Temple (蒲勝寺). His second wife was the Zen nun Ganzen (願善禅尼). Founded Kōmyōji Temple (光明寺). Died on the 2nd day of the 10th month of Kenchō 3 [1251], the year of Kanoto-I (辛亥), at age 60.
Yoshihiro (義廣) — Known as Awano Jirō Kurōdo-no-Tayū (粟野次郎蔵人太輔), with the Buddhist name Kakubutsu (覚仏). Founded an Avalokiteśvara Hall (観音堂) and had 33 images of Avalokiteśvara created to match the height of Kakubutsu himself. He renounced the world and retired. Passed away on the 23rd day of the 9th month of Kōgen, the year of Hinoto-U (丙辰) [1256], at age 72.
Yorichika (政依) — Known as Kurōdo-no-Tayū (蔵人太夫), with the Buddhist name Ganzai (願西). At the Tōshōji estate, he first designated five temples: Tōshōji (東昌寺), Kōmyōji (光明寺), Katsuji (蒲勝寺), Kannonji (観音寺), and Kōfukuji (光福寺). The founding abbot of Tōfukuji's Shōgakuan (東福寺正覚庵) was the Zen Master Butchi (仏知禅師). On the 9th day of the 7th month of Shōan 3 [1301], the year of Kanoto-Ushi (辛丑), he passed away at age 76. He is regarded as the founding patron of Tōshōji. Yorichika also died at age 76 on the same year, same day, and at the same hour.
Munetsuna (宗綱) — [childhood name] Kotarō (小太郎)
Motomune (基宗) — [childhood name] Itotarō (糸太郎)
Yukimune (行宗) — Known as Kunaitayū (宮内太輔), with the Buddhist name Ennyo (円如). Formed a favorable marriage alliance with the Tamura clan. Passed away on the 9th day of the 5th month of Furitori 4 [戊子, 1348], at age 58.
