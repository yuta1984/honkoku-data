Sarue Marishiten Shrine
The shrine has remarkable spiritual efficacy, and worshippers never cease to visit. For its origins, please refer to the Supplementary Edo Famous Places Illustrated.

【Main text, continuation of Gohyaku Rakan Zen Temple】
Priest Shoun is revered as the founding patriarch.
Buddha Hall: The principal image is a statue of Shakyamuni Buddha holding a flower (16 shaku tall, with large stones stacked beneath to form a pedestal). The attendant figures are Manjushri and Samantabhadra (each 8 shaku tall), and Ananda and Kashyapa (each 9 shaku tall). The 500 Arhat statues arranged on the left and right platforms are each life-sized and were all carved by Priest Shoun.

【Main text continued】
The Origin of Creating the Five Hundred Arhats
Priest Shoun was from the capital (Kyoto), generous in spirit and possessed of some true faith. (Some say that Priest Shoun was originally a Buddhist sculptor whose lay name was Kyubei.) In Kanbun 9, year of the Earth Rooster (at age 22), he entered Zuiryu Shoja in Settsu Province, followed Zen Master Tetsugen, shaved his head and became a monk.
