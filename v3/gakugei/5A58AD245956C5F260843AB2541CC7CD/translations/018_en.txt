【Right page, upper section】
○Grave (bo) has the meaning of the character "bo" (to yearn for), which means to long for something. It represents descendants yearning for their ancestors. Mound (tsuka) has the same meaning. The grave of an emperor is called "misasagi" (imperial mausoleum). "Ei" means the same thing. "Ko" refers to a grave pit.
【Supplement】Swamp (numa) refers to a large pond. It also refers to something with little water and muddy soil. Ponds, marshes, and swamps belong to the same category. There is a large swamp in Fushimi, Yamashiro Province, where many reeds grow and waterbirds dwell.
【Supplement】Thicket (yabu) refers to a bamboo grove. Two types are used: madake (timber bamboo) and hachiku (henon bamboo). Because of their hard nature, they are made into thickets and used countless times for construction and utensils.

【Left page】
《Title: Kashiragaki Zoho Kinmo Zui, Volume 3》
Dwellings (kyosho) 《This section contains characters related to palaces, gates, walls, fences, gardens, windows, and all matters concerning homes and residences》

○Palace (ten) is a hall that is high and large. The place where the emperor resides is called a palace. Drawing seaweed on the ceiling of a palace represents the intention to ward off fires, since seaweed is an aquatic plant.
○Ridge (mune) is the peak of a roof. The roof ridge is called "iraka." Shibi (ornamental roof tiles) are shoe-shaped, and shifun (onigawara) are demon tiles.
○Eaves (en) is the same as "enu," referring to the encircling eaves. It has been written in poetry as "dripping like the sound of koto and chiku (musical instruments)." It is also sung about in poems as "iris of the eaves" and "jeweled water of the eaves."
