[Upper section]
○The Chin (mythical poisonous bird) resembles a hawk. It is purple-black in color with a red-black beak. Its neck is seven to eight sun long, and it is a highly poisonous bird that eats snakes. It fears the phoenix.
○The Kiji-bato (oriental turtle dove) resembles domestic pigeons, with light black-red feathers having brown spots. It lives in bamboo groves and calls longingly throughout the year.
○The Shaku-nagi (curlew) has a brown body overall, with a long neck and long legs. It lives near the seashore.
○The Miyako-dori (black-headed gull) has a black head and back, with a white belly. Its beak and legs are red; it is a water bird.
○The Inko (parrot) comes in various sizes. Large ones are about the size of pigeons, while small ones are about the size of small birds. They come in red colors and five-colored varieties. It is a bird from China.
○Ha (feather) is the same as rei and kan. Kaku refers to wing feathers and quills. Kō refers to flight feathers, the short feathers above the kaku.
