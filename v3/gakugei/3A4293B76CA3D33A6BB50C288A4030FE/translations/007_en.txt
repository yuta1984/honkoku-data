【Right Page】
The shrine hall is maintained, what was severed is reconnected, and what had declined is revived. However, when compared to the magnificent spectacle of old, not even one-tenth has been achieved. Only by offering sacred hemp and paper, presenting offerings, placing the imperial throne in peace like Mount Tai, protecting the eternal military fortune like enduring stones, and praying for the prosperity and happiness of all people.

When Tokugawa Ieyasu entered Edo, he inquired about the origins of this shrine. Later, the third shogun Tokugawa Iemitsu granted shrine lands and bestowed a red seal document. However, in the summer of Genroku 15 (1702), the year of the Water Horse, the wise mother, Senior First Rank Keishoin, heard of the shrine's history and, lamenting the shortage of portable shrines, donated several pieces of gold to newly construct one. 《At this point, three portable shrines were completely furnished》 From this time on, the divine power became brilliantly manifest, and the management of the shrine buildings also became magnificently splendid, surpassing the grand spectacle of old times. 《According to Nankotei Sawa, the old site of Ichigaya Hachimangu was inside Ichigaya Gate, at the corner north of where the current guard station is located, in a plot with a large zelkova tree at the corner of the Yamamoto family estate. It was moved to the present location during the Kanei era, which is why this zelkova is called a sacred tree》

【Left Page】
Inariyama Yakuoji Temple, also called Tokoin. Located northwest from the same area, in Kawada-kubo. It belongs to the Shingon school of New Doctrine and is affiliated with Gokokuji Temple in Otsuka. The founding priest is called Hochin Chokaku. The statue of the principal deity, Yakushi Nyorai, is said to be a sacred image carved by Kobo Daishi from sacred stone obtained from the Shiming Cave on Mount Tendai. At the beginning of the Jokyo era, a certain member of the Suda family enshrined it at this temple. 《This temple was formerly called Aizen-in》

Inari Shrine 《Located within the temple grounds. According to tradition, it was established by Ota Dokan and was formerly located near the present Ichigaya Gate. Around the Genna era, it was moved about two cho north of this temple, and later moved again to this location to serve as the temple's guardian deity》

Shokakuzan Gekkeiji Temple. Located about three cho away to the southwest. It is a Rinzai Zen temple affiliated with Engakuji in Kamakura. As one of the ten great Kanto temples, it was founded by Shibue Tsugenjin Tessai and serves as the mortuary temple of the Kitsunekawa family. The plaque reading "Shokakuzan" hanging at the main gate was written by Zen Master Fusai Sokan of Nanzenji. The plaque reading "Kao-kaku" on the bell tower was written by Lord Kozan. This temple was founded during the Bunroku period with Zen Master Setsuzan as its founding abbot.
