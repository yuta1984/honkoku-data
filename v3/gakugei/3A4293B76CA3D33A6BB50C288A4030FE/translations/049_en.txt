Mount Sanmyō Senjuin Temple - Located in the same area, Shichiken-teramachi. It belongs to the Shingon sect. The founding abbot was called Shunki Hōin. The statue of the principal deity Senju Kannon (Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara) measures 8 sun and 9 bu in height. The two attendant deities, Tamonten and Jikokuten, are both made of red sandalwood and are said to be works of Bishukatsumaten. According to tradition, this was originally located at Mount Anko in Echigo Province, but during the Tenshō era when Lord Toyotomi Hideyoshi fought against Shibata Katsuie, a retainer of Gamō Ujisato named Tonoike Genba received it through divine inspiration. Later, during the Genna era, after the fall of the Gamō house, Tonoike served the Hotta family, lords of Sakura Castle in Shimōsa Province. Due to certain circumstances, it was passed down to a certain member of the Tominaga clan and later enshrined at this temple.

Mount Shōjō Kōkokuji Temple - Located in the same area, Haramachi. It belongs to the Nichiren sect and is affiliated with Tanshōji Temple in Kominato. The founding abbot was called Superior Nichikan. The statue of Great Master Nichiren enshrined at this temple is known to the world as the Nunobiki Sacred Image. According to tradition, in the seventh year of Bun'ei, year of the Metal Horse (1270), when the sect founder Great Master was in Kamakura, an epidemic had been spreading for several months throughout the provinces and districts of Bōsō.
