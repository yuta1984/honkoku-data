Nichien-zan Myōhō-ji Temple Located in Horinouchi village, it is a temple of the Nichiren sect Itchi school and is quite prosperous. The sacred image of the sect founder Nichiren Daishi is called the Yakuyoke no Miei (Image for Warding Off Evil) and was created by Saint Nichirō. Originally it was at Myōhokke-ji Temple in Himonya, but during the Genroku era, when that temple was converted to the Tendai sect for certain reasons, this sacred image was moved to this temple. 《Note: This was during the time of the temple's resident priest, Master Nichishō》According to tradition, in the first year of Kōchō (1261), year of the Metal Rooster, Saint Nichiren 《Note: age 40》 was exiled to Itō in Izu.

Saint Nichirō tried to accompany him to that place, but this was not permitted. Therefore, following the saint's instructions at that time, Master Nichirō remained at Yui Beach in Kamakura and prayed day and night for his master's pardon. One evening, on the same ocean, he mystically obtained a sacred piece of wood and hand-carved a true image of Saint Nichiren, constantly serving it without negligence. 《Note: This sacred image was the beginning of creating images of the sect founder and great master》When the time of divine response arrived, in the fifth month of the third year of Kōchō (1263), year of the Water Boar, a pardon was granted and Saint Nichiren returned to Kamakura. At that time, seeing this sacred image with great joy, he said: "My spirit shall now transfer into this wooden image. Forever until the future, the benefits of saving and protecting sentient beings shall be infinite. Since I have already received salvation at age 42, this wooden image shall be called 'Yakuyoke' (Ward Off Evil)," and he personally performed the eye-opening ceremony.

Kaji-fu (Protective Talismans) 《Note: Faithful people who face this talisman with proper mindfulness, chanting the title and reciting sutras for 21 days will have their prayers fulfilled, so many people receive them. When suffering from illness, they paste them on the wall near the sickbed or on house pillars. Hence, common people call them "hari-gofū" (pasted sacred talismans). According to tradition, when Saint Nichiren was at his place of exile in Itō, Izu, a man named Hachirō Uemon was suffering from a serious illness. Therefore, this miraculous talisman was bestowed upon him and there was a divine response. Later, Master Nichirō transmitted this practice, and from then on it has been passed down through the generations》

Although this temple is far removed from the capital, because of its remarkable spiritual efficacy, people do not mind the distance (and come to worship).
