Ōmiya Hachiman Shrine - Located in Wada Village, it is also called Wada Hachiman Shrine. Its temple administrator belongs to the Shingon sect and is called Hankōzan Taiku-ji Temple. <<In the past, Hōsen-ji Temple in Nakano served as its shrine keeper.>> The festival is held on September 19th. <<For three days until the 21st, markets are set up and it becomes lively.>> The divine body is Emperor Ōjin, and there are also two other deities on the left and right, but due to ancient wars the old records were lost, so their divine names are not clear. It is suspected they might be Emperor Nintoku and Kōra no Omi. All are spiritually wondrous and strange, without added ornamentation, having an ancient simple style, and the carving is not particularly skillful. <<For some reason, from the end of the Genroku era the divine shrine was sealed with nails and could not be worshipped, but during the Tenmei years, the temple administrator Yūshō Hōin performed seven days of ritual practice, then carefully opened it to worship the divine images. Recently, a person named Tatebu-shi Masamichi, to give to devout people, personally drew the divine image and had it carved into woodblocks.>> According to tradition, this shrine was originally established by Tada no Mitsunaka. Later, when Minamoto no Yoriyoshi went to battle in the Ōshū campaign, various miraculous signs appeared and he received divine images. In the sixth year of Kōhei, upon his triumphant return, he built the shrine buildings and made it the guardian deity of the Minamoto family. Therefore, Right General Yoritomo also renovated the shrine buildings and monk quarters equally to those at Tsurugaoka in Sagami Province, showing the deepest devotion.

During the Tenshō period, Ōishi Shinano-no-kami investigated the shrine's antiquity and built a shrine building. In the nineteenth year of the same era, the Great Deity Lord graciously visited this place, recognized it as the protective spirit deity of the Minamoto family through the generations, and newly granted shrine lands.

Hatagaya Fudō Myōō - Located in Hatagaya Village, enshrined at Shingon sect Kōmyōzan Shōgon-ji Temple. The statue of the principal deity Fudō Myōō is the work of Chishō Daishi. Every year from April 8th to the 18th, inner worship is permitted. According to tradition, this is a miraculous statue carved in ancient times when Chishō Daishi founded Mii-dera Temple in Ōmi Province. During the Tenkyō years, Taira no Masakado was in the eastern provinces displaying rebellious power and troubling the Emperor, so Taira no Sadamori and Fujiwara no Hidesato received imperial decrees to pursue and punish him and set out for the eastern provinces. At that time, they carried this principal deity from Mii-dera Temple into their military camp.
