Ōmiya Hachiman Shrine　Located in Wada Village, it is also called Wada Hachiman Shrine. The bettō (head priest) belongs to the Shingon sect and is called Bankōzan Daikū-ji Temple. 《It is said that Hōsen-ji Temple in Nakano used to serve this shrine in the past》The regular festival is held on September 19th. 《For three days until the 21st, markets are held and it becomes lively》The divine body is Emperor Ōjin, and there are also two deities on the left and right, but because the old records were lost in ancient warfare, their divine names are not clear. It is suspected they might be Emperor Nintoku and Kōra no Omi. All are spiritually mysterious and wondrous, with no added ornamentation, having an ancient simple style with the most skillful carving. 《For some reason, from the end of the Genroku period, the divine shrine was nailed shut and could not be worshipped, but during the Tenmei years, after Bettō Yūshō Hōin performed seventeen days of religious practices, he carefully opened it and worshipped the divine images. Recently, a person named Tatebe Masamichi, being devout, drew the divine images himself to give to faithful people and carved them on woodblocks for printing》According to tradition, this shrine was originally established through the petition of Tada no Mitsunaka. Later, when Minamoto no Yoriyoshi went on expedition to suppress Ōshū, there were various miraculous signs and he received divine images. In the sixth year of Kōhei, upon his triumphant return, he built the shrine buildings and made it the guardian deity of the Minamoto family. Therefore, Right General Yoritomo also rebuilt the shrine halls and monk quarters equally to those at Tsurugaoka in Sagami Province, showing the deepest devotion

During the Tenshō period, Ōishi Shinano no Kami investigated the shrine's antiquity and built new shrine buildings. In the same nineteenth year, the Great Divine Ruler (Tokugawa Ieyasu) graciously visited this place, recognized that it was the spiritual deity that had protected the Minamoto family for generations, and newly granted shrine lands.

Hatagaya Fudō Myōō　Located in Hatagaya Village, enshrined at Shingon sect Kōmyōzan Shōgon-ji Temple. The image of the principal deity Fudō Myōō was created by Chishō Daishi. Every year from April 8th to the 18th, inner worship is permitted. According to tradition, this is a miraculous image carved when Chishō Daishi founded Mii-dera Temple in Ōmi Province in ancient times. During the Tenkyō years, when Taira no Masakado was in the eastern provinces wielding rebellious power and troubling the Emperor, Taira no Sadamori and Fujiwara no Hidesato received imperial edicts to pursue and punish him and set out for the eastern provinces.
