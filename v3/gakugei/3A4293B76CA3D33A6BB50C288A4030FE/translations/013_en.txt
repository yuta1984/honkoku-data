The azaleas (eizan-beni) of Okubo reach their peak at the end of March. There are several bushes over a meter tall, and people who love their beautiful crimson color gather here to enjoy themselves. Though the individual flowers are small, they bloom in clusters, covering the branches and stems. It appears as if crimson has been poured throughout the entire garden, and reflecting in the evening sun, they form a forest like brocaded embroidery.
　The magnificent scenery of this area
　　　is truly splendid
