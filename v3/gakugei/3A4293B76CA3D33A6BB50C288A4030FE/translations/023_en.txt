Nakano Pagoda

【Left Page】
What is called [the seven pagodas] may also be of that same type. Also, there is a three-story pagoda within the grove on the right side of Nakano thoroughfare. Could this be one of the seven pagodas? According to tradition, it was built by Nakano Choja Suzuki Kurou Shoren, and while it was formerly within the grounds of Jogan Temple, it was moved to the present location in later times. Currently it enshrines Dainichi Nyorai as the principal image (the former principal image was Shakyamuni Buddha, which became the principal image of Jogan Temple in later times). Inside, what are called portraits of the wealthy Suzuki family couple are enshrined.

Myoouzan Hosen Temple, called Mudoin. It has temple lands. It belongs to the Kogi Shingon sect and is located on the right side toward the west in the same area. It is said to have been founded by Priest Roben. The principal image is a life-sized statue of Kobo Daishi, created by Gangyo. The restoration founder is called Priest Shoei. In ancient times it was a great temple located in Asagaya, about twenty cho north of this place, but during the Ashikaga period it was moved to the present location. However, around the Taiei period it was caught in military fires and the Buddha hall and monks' quarters were all reduced to scorched earth. Therefore, the old records from that time were also lost, so details about the time of founding etc. are not clear. A sacred image of Fudo-son is enshrined in Fumonin within the temple grounds. It is said to be either the work of Priest Roben or the work of Gangyo.
