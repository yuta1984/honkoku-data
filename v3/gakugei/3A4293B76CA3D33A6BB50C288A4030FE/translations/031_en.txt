【Left Page】

The ancient pine trees standing before this shrine rise magnificently, as if sweeping away the very clouds, bearing the dignified appearance of several hundred years. Master Arai Hakuseki also praised these pines, and in his *Shin'an Shukan* wrote that "beginning with Ōu (the Tōhoku region) and extending to Bōsō (Kazusa and Shimōsa), Tōsō (Izu and Sagami), along the entire Tōkaidō route, the Kinai region, and the provinces of Nōbi (Mino and Owari)—never have I seen so great a number of such tall and magnificent pines as these."

Furthermore, the great road in front of the shrine is the ancient Kamakura Highway, which the local inhabitants today call the "Shōyō Kaidō" (Shōyō Highway). It is said that what is called "Kamakura Bridge" in Kami-Takaidō also derives its name from this old designation as a former highway of ancient times.
