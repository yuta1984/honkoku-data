【Right Page】
It is said that on this mountain there were originally two ancient pine trees, and around that time mountain doves would come and play daily on the branches of these pines. Taking this as an auspicious sign, they temporarily established a small shrine to Hachiman Daishin and made these pine trees sacred trees. 《According to Nankōtei, this place was formerly part of Waseda village called Nakajima. There lived a wealthy man named Aoyagitsu Rokubei, who in ancient times served the Hōjō family as a samurai, and these were the mountain forests passed down by that person》 This place was formerly called Mt. Amida, but no one knew the reason why. In the summer of Kan'ei 18 (1641), there was a monk named Ishōin Ryōshō among the disciples of Hōin Shūyū of Hōsenji temple in Nakano. He was from Suō Province and was a parishioner of Yamaguchi Hachiman. 《As a child he served a certain retainer of the Mōri family named Enomoto, but after Enomoto's death, at age nineteen he renounced the world, climbed Mt. Kōya, became a disciple of Hōin Shunzan of Hōshōin, completed twelve years of religious practice, and from age thirty-one set his heart on pilgrimage throughout the provinces, during which he is said to have manifested various miracles》 Therefore they welcomed this monk to serve as the shrine's priest. In the autumn of that same year, on August 3rd, while cutting open the mountainside to build a hermitage, they discovered a sacred cave. Within that cave, on a stone, stood one sacred image of Amida in gold and bronze. 《About three inches in height》 This was the honji (original ground) Buddha of Hachiman Shrine, and moreover it corresponded to the mountain's name, making it miraculous. 《The name "Ana-Hachiman" (Cave Hachiman) originated here. Its former site still exists beside the slope》 Also, on this day the Shogun's heir 《Lord Gen'yū》 was born, so people increasingly recognized its divine power. 《According to Edo Meisho-ki, it was August 9th of the same year》

【Left Page】
《They cordoned off an area of one chō (about 109 meters) square around the shrine precincts, cleared the land, moved the main shrine to the base of the sacred pine tree, and built an eight-fold fence around it. At that time, the Lord of Kaga Province sent hundreds of workers to help fortify the ground. Therefore it was completed without delay. On the 14th, they performed the ceremony of shrine relocation. Matsudaira Shingozaemon-no-jō led his yoriki retainers to set up curtains around Mt. Mato and erected a proper small target. Since the divine archery was customary, a twelve-year-old son of someone named Koike performed this duty》 Later, during the Genroku era, the shrine buildings were constructed as they are today, with complete magnificence. 《In Nankōtei's Tea Talks, it states that Lord Gen'yū especially revered this shrine, and after his vows were fulfilled, he established this shrine. The rear gate was donated by Naitō Buzen-no-kami, the Fugen Hall by Matsudaira Sakon-shōgen, and the ritual water basin fence by Masuyama Hyōbu-shōyū and others. Also, books such as Kōfu Jinja Ryakki and Wakan Sansai Zue state that there was reconstruction by Lady Keishōin during the Genroku period》

Wakamiya Hachiman Shrine 《Located to the front left of the main shrine》
Tōshō Daigongen 《Enshrined alongside at the same location. Many people visit on April 17th every year》
Himuro Myōjin Shrine 《Facing the main shrine. It displays a plaque carved with the two characters "Seitoku." The deity enshrined is Ōnamuchi-no-mikoto. On December 22nd, Genroku 2, Makimura Naoyoshi, age sixteen, suffered from smallpox. On January 2nd, Genroku 3, a resident of Kanakiwa named Watanabe received a divine dream response and enshrined this deity. Naoyoshi prayed to this deity and recovered. It is said that around Genroku 7, the deity was first enshrined here》
Light Pine 《Located on a side path of the slope between the temple administrator's quarters and the main shrine. The old pine died during the Enkyō period, so what exists now is a young tree planted later. According to Nankōtei, this place was formerly a mountain forest where pine trees flourished, and among them was one pine tree that sometimes manifested auspicious light during dark nights, so that tree was called the Light Pine. Also, when Hachiman Shrine was first established here in Kan'ei 13, mountain doves came to play in this tree》
Hōjō Pond (Life-Release Pond) 《Located below the stone steps. Clear spring water drips down from the mountainside. This truly deserves to be called a miracle corresponding to the name Iwashimizu (Rock Clear Water)》
Apparition Site 《Located along the cliff on the mid-slope. This is the former site of the ancient sacred cave. Until recently, there was a hall called Shutsugen-dō where an image of Amida Nyorai of the lower grade, upper birth among the Nine Grade Buddhas was enshrined, but it is no longer visible》
