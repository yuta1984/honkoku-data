**Inryōzan Saishōji Temple** — Located in Enokimachi, in the same district. It is a Zen temple belonging to the Myōshinji branch of Rinzai Zen from Kyoto. [Marginal note: *In former times, the head priest was appointed in rotation from Myōshinji.*] The principal image enshrined here is Śākyamuni (Shaka Nyorai). The founder (*kaisan*) is the Zen master Shin'in Shōden Zenji, and the founding patron (*kaiki*) is the nun Soshin-ni. This nun was a daughter of Makino Hyōbu-no-shōyū Masaharu, and together with Kasuga no Tsubone, she served as a lady-in-waiting in close attendance upon the Shogunal household. The temple has an *Ofutsuden* (Shogunal Buddha Hall), administered by the Hōshin'in sub-temple. [Marginal note: *This temple was founded by the nun Hōshin-ni.*] The pond in front of the *Ofutsuden* is called the Hōōchi (Phoenix Pond). The Reiki-sui (Auspicious Tortoise Spring) is located within the precincts of Hōshin'in, and it is said that during the Kan'ei era, water was drawn from it for the Shogun's tea.

The founder's memorial stupa (*kaisantō*) is under the care of the Yōshun'in sub-temple. Altogether, there are six monks' quarters, along with a sutra hall, a bell tower, a kitchen quarters, a bathhouse, and other structures — all standing in magnificent rows, presenting an imposing and splendid appearance. [Marginal note: *The tablet on the Sanbutsudō (Three Buddhas Hall) reading "Tenka Inryō" ("Shade and Cool for All Under Heaven") is in the personal brushwork of Imperial Prince Zuishii'in-no-Miya, First Rank, Jugo Kōkei Hōshin'nō.*]

**Site of the Former Residence of Bungo Kojijū Ōtomo Yoshinobu** — The temple [Saishōji] itself is identified as the site of his former estate. According to tradition, in the second year of Bunroku (1593), Ōtomo Yoshinobu took part in the Korean campaign, but was punished by the Taikō Toyotomi [Hideyoshi] for negligence in military preparedness, was transferred to this province (Musashi), and was placed under house confinement (*chikkyō*) at this location. This place is said to be the remnant of that former residence.

[Marginal note: *The* Nankō Sawa *states: Ōtomo Saheie-no-kami Yoshimune, during the Korean campaign of the Bunroku years, had his domain confiscated for dereliction of duty, and thereafter died in Hitachi Province. His eldest son, Sōgorō Yoshinobu, resided at this place. Because Yoshinobu was raised to Junior Fourth Rank and appointed as Jijū (Chamberlain), he was styled "Bungo Kojijū" (the Minor Chamberlain of Bungo). After the Battle of Sekigahara in the fifth year of Keichō (1600), he was granted 3,500 koku of land in Tsukuba District, Hitachi Province, but died young. Also, the booklet known as the* Edo Kanoko *refers to him as "Yoshinori," which appears to be an error for Yoshinobu.*]

**The Ōtomo Pine** — Said to be located on the grounds of the Omochidzutsu-gumi (Musket-bearer Corps) retainer of the Takano clan, to the east of Tenjin-machi in the same district. It was formerly the pine tree that stood in the garden of Ōtomo Yoshinobu's villa, but was subsequently lost to fire. The owner of the land, lamenting the loss of this historic remnant, planted a young tree in its place. [Marginal note: *One person relates: According to a tradition preserved in the Ōtomo family, it was the pine tree standing before the tea-ceremony arbour (*sukiya*) built by the retainer Kira Den'zaemon (whose personal name is unknown), who followed Ōtomo Sōgorō Yoshinobu when he was relocated to Musashi Province; and it is said that the name of Inryōzan Saishōji temple itself derives from this very pine tree.*]

---

**Ōtomo Inari Shrine** — Located in the same district. It is also said by tradition to have been established (*kanjō*) by Yoshinobu.
