Inari Shrine (Located within the temple grounds. The founding abbot Seikan could create all kinds of Buddhist statues and regularly used bellows to make various crafts. For this reason, he established an Inari shrine within the grounds, and on November 8th held a bellows festival. This tradition continues to this day annually as a remaining custom.)

Weeping Cherry Tree (Located in front of the main hall. This is what Kikuoka Sen'ryo named "the so-called unknown cherry." Additionally, a small ditch running across the temple grounds serves as the boundary between Toshima District and Ebara District. This fact is also recorded in the inscription on this temple's bell.)

Kanagawa River - Refers to the small river that flows from in front of Ana-Hachiman at the same location toward Waseda (now called Furukawa - Old River).
The water source originates from within the Toyama Imperial Garden. During the Bunmei era, when Ota Dokan was hunting and encountered sudden rain at this location, the river was much wider in those days. At that time it was called Kanagawa or Kanogawa (sometimes written as Kanigawa - Crab River).

Takada Hachiman Shrine - The main tutelary shrine of Ushigome, located in Takada (commonly called Ana-Hachiman). This place is called Totsuka. The betto (temple administrator) belongs to the Shingon sect and is called Koshoyama Hoshoe-ji Temple (the former name was Isho-in Naka-no-bo).
(The festival is held on August 15th with a hoshoe ceremony. The temporary shrine is located on the middle slope of Kagurazaka in Ushigome.)

According to the shrine records, in Kan'ei 13 (1636), Matsudaira Shingozaemon-no-jo Minamoto no Naotsugi, head of the imperial archery corps, had his yoriki subordinates construct a target mountain at this location for archery practice. Since Hachiman Shrine is the ancestral shrine of the Minamoto clan and also the guardian deity of bow and arrow, they decided to establish it at this location.
