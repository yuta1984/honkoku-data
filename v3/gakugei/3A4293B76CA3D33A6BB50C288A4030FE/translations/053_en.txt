Inari Shrine: Located within the temple grounds. The founding priest Seikan was skilled at creating Buddhist statues and regularly used bellows to make various crafts. For this reason, he invited an Inari deity to the temple grounds and held a bellows festival on November 8th. This tradition continues annually even today.

Weeping Cherry Tree: Located in front of the main hall. This is the tree that Kikuoka Senryo named "Yurusanu Sakura" (Unforgiven Cherry). Additionally, the small ditch flowing across the temple grounds serves as the boundary between Toshima District and Ebara District. This fact is also recorded in the inscription on this temple's bell.

Kanagawa River: Refers to the small river that flows in front of Ana-Hachiman toward Waseda at the same location (now called Furukawa - Old River). The water source originates from within the Toyama Imperial Garden. During the Bunmei era, Ota Dokan encountered sudden rain while hunting in this area, and the river was much wider in ancient times. In those days it was called "Kanagawa" or "Kanogawa" (sometimes written as "Kanigawa" - Crab River).

Takada Hachiman Shrine: This is the main tutelary shrine of Ushigome, located in Takada (commonly called Ana-Hachiman - Cave Hachiman). This area is called Totsuka. The temple administrator belongs to the Shingon sect and is called Koshoyama Hoshoe-ji Temple (formerly called Isho-in Naka-no-bo). The festival is held on August 15th with a life-release ceremony (hoshoe). The temporary shrine is located midway up Kagurazaka in Ushigome.

According to the shrine records, in Kan'ei 13 (1636), the leader of the imperial archery unit, Matsudaira Shingozaemon-no-jo Minamoto no Naotsugi, along with his yoriki retainers, built a target mound at this location for archery practice. Since Hachiman Shrine is the ancestral shrine of the Minamoto family and also the guardian deity of archery, they decided to invite the deity to this location.
