【Right Page】

**Inari Shrine**　[Inset note] Located within the temple precincts. The founding abbot Seikan was skilled at crafting all manner of Buddhist images and always used a bellows (fuigo) to produce various works of metalwork. For this reason, he invited Inari to be enshrined within the precincts, and on the eighth day of the eleventh month, a Fuigo Festival (Bellows Festival) was held. This custom continues to this day, with the event taking place every year. [End of inset note]

**Weeping Cherry (Shidare-zakura)**　[Inset note] Located in front of the main hall. This is the cherry tree that Kikuoka Senryō named "Shiranu Zakura" (The Unrecognized Cherry). Additional note: The small channel running horizontally through this temple's precincts serves as the boundary between Toshima District and Ebara District. This fact is also recorded in the inscription on the temple bell. [End of inset note]

**Kanakawa (River)**　In the same area, this refers to the small river that flows from in front of Ana Hachiman shrine toward Waseda. [Inset note] It is now called Furukawa (Old River). [End of inset note] Its source originates from within the Toyama御庭 (Toyama Garden). During the Bunmei era, when Ōta Dōkan was out hunting and was caught in a sudden rainstorm, it was at this very location; in those days, the river was said to have been much wider. At that time it was called Kanakawa or alternatively Kanōkawa. [Inset note] It is also written as Kanikawa (Crab River). [End of inset note]

**Takada Hachiman Shrine**　The chief tutelary shrine (sō-chinjusha) of Ushigome, located in Takada. [Inset note] It is commonly known as Ana Hachiman (The Pit Hachiman). [End of inset note] This area is called Totsuka. The bettō (administrative temple) is of the Shingon sect, known as Kōshōzan Hōjōe-ji. [Inset note] Its former name was Ishōin Nakanobō. The festival is held on the fifteenth day of the eighth month, at which a Hōjōe (life-releasing ceremony) is performed. The temporary shrine resting place (tabisho) is located on the hillside of Kagurazaka in Ushigome. [End of inset note]

According to the shrine records: In the thirteenth year of Kan'ei (丙子, 1636), the head of the Archery Unit, Matsudaira Shingozaemon no Jō Minamoto no Naonori, together with his subordinate yoriki, constructed an archery target mound (matoyama) at this location for the purpose of practicing archery. Since Hachiman Shrine is the ancestral shrine of the Minamoto clan and moreover the guardian deity of bow and arrow, it was decided to enshrine it at this location…
