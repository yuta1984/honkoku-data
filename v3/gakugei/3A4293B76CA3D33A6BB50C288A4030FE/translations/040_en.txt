The sect is called Shōreizan Muryōji Temple.
The enshrined deities are Emperor Ōjin, Empress Jingū, and Emperor Chūai - these three in total. According to tradition, during the reign of Emperor Saga, an old man lived in this place who constantly revered Hachiman Shrine. One time, the deity of this shrine appeared in the old man's dream and said he wished to leave his traces in this land for eternity.
The old man thought this strange. The next day, he saw auspicious clouds gathering above a pine tree like banners. *Note: It is said that the name Shōunzan (Pine Cloud Mountain) originates from this* At that time, a white dove came and roosted in the branches of the same tree. The villagers heard of the old man's divine dream and immediately built a sacred fence around the base of this tree to worship it as Hachiman Shrine. Much later, when Great Master Jikaku was traveling through the eastern provinces, he established a small shrine with Amida Nyorai (carved by Great Master Dengyō) as the honjibutsu. Later, during the Bunmei era, Uesugi Tomooki, lord of Edo Castle, renovated the shrine buildings and made it the tutelary deity of this area. *Note: According to one document, this shrine's location was originally the site of a fortress of the former regent Uesugi Tokiuji, who established the Hachiman shrine with his bow and arrows*

During the reign of the Nara Emperor, a person named Ono no Misago was appointed as Governor of Musashi and came down to this province.
At that time, there was a woman in this place called Sanekazura who was very beautiful.
Misago fell in love with her and took her as his wife. After some time had passed, Misago was summoned by the Emperor and went up to the capital of Nara, where he lived at the foot of Mount Wakakusa, but died not long after. At that time, Misago said, "After I die, my remains must be sent to Musashi Province and buried near where Sanekazura lives." However, since the borders were so far apart, he was buried at the foot of Mount Wakakusa in Yamato Province. That place came to be called Musashino, and the burial mound was called Musashi-zuka. *Note: According to the tradition of the local elders, Musashi-zuka is the ancient tomb of Dainagon and Musashi Governor Yoshimine no Yasuyo* After this, Sanekazura, not knowing that Misago had died, continued to love and long for him alone, praying to the gods and making vows to Buddha, lamenting and grieving day and night. One night she received a revelation in a dream, so she came to this place and indeed met Misago.
