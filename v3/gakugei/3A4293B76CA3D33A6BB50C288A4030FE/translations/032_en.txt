The Kurakake Pine (Saddle-hanging Pine) is located outside the residential compound along the main road at Babazaki in front of Ōmiya Hachiman Shrine. It grows luxuriantly and densely. Since it curves about one jō (approximately 3 meters) up from its roots, local people call it the Wada Curved Pine, or sometimes the Hip-resting Pine. According to tradition, when Hachiman Tarō Yoshiie Ason was subjugating the rebels of Ōshū, he hung his saddle on the branches of this pine, which is how it got its name. Also, about two or three chō (200-300 meters) east of this place, beside the road, there stands an old pine tree. Local people call it the Single Pine. It is said to be the former site where the first torii gate of Hachiman Shrine once stood.

Iguchisan Jikōji Temple is located on the right side of the Kawagoe Highway in Ōmiya-mae Shinden. It belongs to the Nichiren sect, was founded during the Kanbun era, and its founding priest was called Nichiken Shōnin. The Three Treasures are enshrined as the principal object of worship.

The statue of Nichiren Daishi enshrined in this temple is the work of Nichirō Shōnin. According to tradition, on the 12th day of the 5th month of Kōchō 1, kanoto-tori, when Daishi was exiled to Itō in Izu, Master Nichirō, regretting the parting from Daishi, obtained sacred wood and carved two portrait statues of Daishi. {{One statue was seated and was initially at Hokke Temple in Himonya, but was later enshrined at Myōhōji Temple in Hori-no-uchi. The second is a standing statue enshrined in this temple. This sacred image is that very one. Because it has the appearance of someone on a journey, it is also called in the world the Sacred Image of Kōmyōboku Tabidachi (Radiant Wood Departure).}} After Daishi returned to Kamakura, the eye-opening ceremony was performed.
