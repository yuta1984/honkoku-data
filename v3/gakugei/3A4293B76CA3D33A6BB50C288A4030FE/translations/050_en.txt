A Buddhist sculptor was commissioned to create an image of himself, and he wrote the sutra title on white cloth and hung it on the hands of the image, entrusting it with the words "This is indeed Nichiren." Therefore, when this sacred image was moved to that place, the plague that had been afflicting the area immediately retreated. Thus, this sacred image was enshrined at Tanjoji Temple in Kominato, but later, for the propagation of the sect, it was moved to this temple on the 16th day of the 2nd month of Kanei 7 (1630). This temple was founded by Kato Higo-no-kami Kiyomasa, and it is said that the sacred image of the sect founder changes its clothing according to the seasons, just like at Ikegami Honmonji Temple (for certain reasons, the clothing is annually provided by a certain member of the Abe family).

Shinmei Shrine - Located in the large rice fields of Waseda. The enshrined deities are the three seats of Amaterasu, Kasuga, and Hachiman. It is jointly managed by Togakuji Temple, which serves as the betto (administrator) of Akagi Myojin in the same area. The festival is held on September 16th. The year of its establishment is not clear (it is said to have been moved from Enoki-machi in the same area in Tenwa 2 [1682]. The current residence of a certain Mr. Hayashi of the O-koban group is said to be on the former site).

Former site of Akagi Myojin - Located along a small stream beside the rice field ridges in the same area. This is the place where the Ogo clan first invited Akagi Myojin. Therefore, on festival days, the portable shrine is carried to this place.

Honmyosan Kantsushi Temple - Located on the hill south of the riding grounds below Ana-Hachiman in Takada. It belongs to the Nichiren sect.
