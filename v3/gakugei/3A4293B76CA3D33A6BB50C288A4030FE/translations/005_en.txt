Ichigaya Hachiman Shrine　Located outside Ichigaya Gate, the bettō (head priest) is called Tōen-ji Temple, which belongs to Kōya-san Kongōbu-ji Temple in Nanki and follows the Kogi Shingon sect.

The main shrine deity is Emperor Ōjin (the divine body is armor, and according to tradition, it was a sacred form revered by Tada no Mitsunaka, which was originally at the Tada shrine in Settsu Province but was transferred here by Ōta Mochisuke. The honji buddha is Aizen Myōō). To the east is Empress Jingū (mother of Emperor Ōjin), and to the west is Hime-ōkami (the Emperor's elder sister, Hōman Bosatsu) - three deities are enshrined.

Inari Shrine (the tutelary deity of this shrine, located on the left side of the middle tier of the stone steps. Commonly called Chanoki Inari, but its origins are unreliable so they are omitted here. The parishioners of this deity do not drink tea from New Year's Day to the third day every year. Those suffering from eye diseases abstain from tea for a set period of one or three weeks while praying, and it is said that the divine efficacy is remarkable and all wishes are fulfilled without fail).

According to shrine records, during the Bunmei era (1469-1487), Ōta Mochisuke invited the great deity Hachiman from Tsurugaoka in Sagami Province to protect Edo Castle, and created Tōen-ji Temple with attached mountain forests and shrine fields (the mountain name Tōrei comes from the fact that there was originally an Inari shrine on this land serving as the tutelary deity. Tsurugaoka was also formerly Inari shrine land. This likely follows that precedent). He also personally planted pine and chinquapin trees as shrine trees, praying that both the shrine altar and castle would prosper together (locally called Dōkan Pine. The branches and leaves flourish luxuriantly and still exist today).

Later it was destroyed by the fires of war during the Tenshō era (1573-1592), but during the Keichō era (1596-1615), the bettō priest Gen'kū Shōsōzu was indignant at this ruined foundation and spent his own private funds to inspect the ruins of about a hundred paces, tying grass for eaves and cutting wood for doors to rebuild one building.
