The sacred jewel for safe childbirth《Marginal note: Enshrined at this temple. It is said that the wife of Shogun Ashikaga Takauji possessed this. Women who worship this sacred jewel are said to have no worries about difficult childbirth and greatly revere it. The temple was initially called Heianji, perhaps with the meaning of peaceful childbirth》

Seikōzan Anyōji Temple is located in Ichigaya Tanimachi and is called Rinsenin. It belongs to the Jōdo sect and is affiliated with Kyoto's Chion-in. It was founded in the second year of Tenshō (1574), and its founder is called Shinrensya Shin'yo Shōnin Teikō Oshō. The standing statue of Amida Nyorai, the principal image, is three shaku and three sun tall and was carved by Eshin Sōzu, said to be made from the same wood as the principal image of Kyoto's Shin'nyodō《Marginal note: According to tradition, during the Tenchō period, Jigaku Daishi obtained sacred wood from Naoka Myōjin in Ōmi Province, and when he split it, the wood grain naturally formed the shape of a Buddha body. From one piece of wood, two Amida Buddha statues were carved and installed at Hiyoshi Nembutsu-dō and Raku no Shin'nyodō. Later, Eshin Sōzu received divine inspiration and obtained the remaining wood to create this principal image》. According to tradition, when the founding abbot sought to establish a monastery, he found a place where clear spring water bubbled up from beneath the forest《Marginal note: Ichigaya Fujimi-zaka was that old location, which is now said to be within the Owari lord's residence》.

There was also a small cave nearby, from which a white fox appeared and seemed to pay respects to Shin'yo Shōnin. Recognizing this as sacred ground, he obtained the land from its owner, one Shimada, and built a temple there《Marginal note: In the second year of Meireki (1656), it moved to its current location》.

Inari Shrine《Marginal note: Located within the temple grounds. On New Year's Day night of the first year of Manji, an old man in white robes appeared in the dream of the resident monk Shūyo Shōnin and made an announcement. After awakening, Shūyo Shōnin reflected and thought it must be the white fox that had appeared to the founding abbot in ancient times, so he immediately invited Inari Myōjin. Also, around that time, a blacksmith named Uda Kunimune lived in this area and deeply revered the deity, and because he was protected from fire disasters by this god's protection, it is commonly called "Fire Prevention Inari"》

Hachiman Shrine《Marginal note: Also located within the temple grounds. It was originally revered as the guardian deity of Amako Iyo-no-kami Tsunehisa's castle in Unshu Province, but for certain reasons was transferred here. The honchi Amida Buddha is a work by Jōchō and was created in the first year of Chian by imperial decree. It was later passed down in the house of Tsukinowa-denka Kanesane-kō and became the guardian deity of Tsunehisa's castle. This temple also houses the Kōkō Buddha that was possessed by Shunkan Sōzu from Hōshōji Temple and a Jizō statue made from the same wood as that of Mibu-dera in Kyoto》

Shippōzan Yakuōji Temple is located to the southwest of the same area, separated by about four chō. It is a Zen temple of the Ōbaku sect affiliated with Manpukuji Temple in Uji, Yamashiro Province. It was originally an old temple of the Shingon sect, but in medieval times it greatly declined and was reduced to merely the form of a grass hermitage. During the Genroku period, Zen Master Ryōun restored it《Marginal note: Ryōun Oshō was from Shinshū Province, born to a daughter of Takeda Tenkyū, making him truly Tenkyū's grandson. He was tonsured at Kaion-in Temple of the Sōtō sect in Komoro of the same province and later became Ōbaku. He came to Edo and sought to transform various grass hermitages into new temple compounds, but originally the new construction of temples was officially prohibited》
