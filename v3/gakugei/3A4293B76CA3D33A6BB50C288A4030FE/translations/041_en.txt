Kagurazaka - Same location. This refers to the slope outside Ushigome Gate. On the right side of the slope's mid-section is the tabisho (temporary shrine) of Takada Ana-Hachiman. During festivals, the mikoshi (portable shrine) is carried to this place. It is called this because kagura (sacred music and dance) is performed at that time. *Note: Some say that when Tsukudo Myōjin was relocated from Tayasu to its current location, kagura was performed*
*Note: on this slope, hence the name. It is also said that because Wakamiya Hachiman Shrine is nearby, the sound of kagura can always be heard reaching this slope*

Wakamiya Hachiman Shrine - Same location. Located in Wakamiya-chō, at the top of Wakamiya slope. *Note: Sometimes also called Wakamiya-kōji* The bettō (temple administrator) is called Fumon-in of the Tendai sect. According to tradition, in autumn of Bunji 5 (1189), Right General Yoritomo set out to subjugate Yasuhira of Ōshū. At that time he made a vow that after pacifying Ōshū, he would establish this shrine and enshrine Wakamiya Hachiman from Kamakura's Tsurugaoka. *Note: Wakamiya was Emperor Nintoku. Later it was changed to enshrine Emperor Ōjin* During the Bunmei era, Ōta Dōkan re-established this shrine for the protection of Edo Castle

According to the origin story of the main deity, after the Battle of Ishibashiyama, Right General Yoritomo passed through Awa and Kazusa, then crossed from Shimōsa Province to this province, and spent the night in vigil before the sacred image. That night in a dream, Yoritomo saw himself hanging this sacred image from his collar and opening the military fortune of the Genji clan. Later he indeed unified the realm, so it came to be called Yoritomo's Collar-Hanging Sacred Image.

Ushigome Castle Site - Same location. It is said to be the old site in the upper area of Waratana. During the Tenbun era, Ushigome Kunaishōyū Katsuyuki lived in this place, and these are said to be the remains of those castle fortifications.
