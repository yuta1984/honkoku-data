【Right Page】
According to one person's theory, Ichigaya was formerly a place where markets were held, hence it was written as "Ichikai" (market buying). However, the details are not clear.

Upon consideration, in an old document by Ashikaga Motouji dated the 22nd day of the 12th month of Enbun 3 (1358), preserved at Kamakura Tsurugaoka Hachimangu, it states: "The temple administrator of Tsurugaoka Hachiman reports that regarding the matter of Kanasoki Hikosaburo and Ichiya Shiro of Musashi Province, the seizure by Edo Awaji-no-kami should be stopped, and in accordance with the donation document of the 11th day of the 8th month of Showa 1 (1312), it should be handled through the shrine family." This should serve as evidence.

On the shrine grounds there are theater stages and archery ranges, making it constantly lively. Also, the main road in front of the shrine is the thoroughfare to Yotsuya, with people coming and going incessantly.
