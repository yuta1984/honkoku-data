【Right Page】
Elephant Bones: In Kyōhō 13 (1728), year of the Earth Monkey, a person named Tei Taii from Cochin China brought two elephants, male and female, born in Guangnan, and presented them to Japan (According to Hayashi Nobuyuki's "Jibutsu Kenyo," they came from Daini Country. The female elephant died in Nagasaki on September 11th of the same year). They arrived in Nagasaki on June 13th of the same year (landed on the 19th; two elephant keepers named Tansō and Tanmen, two interpreters named Ri Shakumei and Chin Abō from Guangnan accompanied them). The following year (Kyōhō 14, 1729), on March 13th, they departed Nagasaki, arrived in Osaka on April 16th, entered Kyoto from Fushimi on the 26th, and on the 28th were granted an imperial audience (Since there was no precedent for entering the imperial palace without court rank, even though it was an animal, it was granted the rank of Junior Fourth Rank and called "Guangnan Junior Fourth Rank White Elephant"). On May 25th they were welcomed to Edo, and on the 27th the shogun viewed them. Afterward, an elephant stable was built in Nakano where it was kept, but after more than twenty years, it died around the Kan'en era (The bones preserved at this temple are those of the male elephant).

Male elephant (7 years old): Entirely gray in color, head length 2 shaku 7 sun (the head could not bow down or turn around). Trunk length about 4 shaku (some say 3 shaku 3 sun), circumference 1 shaku 5 sun (at the tip about 6 sun. Two nostrils, deeply recessed at the ends and opening and closing well. Inside are small flesh claws that can pick up needles and pinch mustard seeds; it drinks water and sips sake with its trunk, and when eating also uses its trunk to roll food in. All the body's strength is in the trunk; when rising to walk, it first supports itself on the ground with its trunk, then moves its feet. The mouth is hidden beneath the chin, far from the ground, and usually not visible). Tusk length about 1 shaku 2 sun (some say 1 shaku 4 sun, circumference at the base about 1 shaku 6 sun).

【Left Page】
Eye length 3 sun (some say 1 sun 5 bu, shaped like bamboo grass leaves). Ear width over 8 sun (some say 1 shaku 3 sun, shaped like bat wings or ginkgo leaves).

Body length 7 shaku 4 sun, circumference 1 jō, back height 5 shaku (some say 5 shaku 7 sun). Leg length 2 shaku 2 sun, circumference 1 shaku 5 sun (some say 3 shaku 5 sun, circumference 2 shaku 5 sun. Legs are cylindrical with no fingers; five claws shaped like chestnuts. Though the whole body is plump and fat, it climbs steep mountains and descends winding paths swift as lightning, and crosses deep water nimbly. Its nature is to become well accustomed to people and understand their intentions, so elephant keepers straddle its head and guide it with iron hooks to make it advance, retreat, turn, and move left and right). Tail length 3 shaku 3 sun (some say 2 shaku 7-8 sun, shaped like a cow's tail).

Female elephant (5 years old): Entirely gray, head length 2 shaku 5 sun, trunk length 2 shaku 8 sun, body length about 5 shaku, circumference 8 shaku 6 sun, back height 4 shaku 7 sun (some say 4 shaku). Tusk length about 5 sun, otherwise same as the male (this female elephant died while in Nagasaki. Only the male came to Edo).

Feed (daily: 200 kin of fresh vegetables, 150 kin of bamboo leaves, 2 banana plants excluding roots, 8 shō of foreign rice with about 4 shō made into cooled porridge, hot water about 2 to per time. 50 steamed buns, 50 oranges, 30 kunenbo citrus, and occasionally boiled and cooled soybeans. Among green plants, it particularly favored what common people call "sumo wrestler grass." When there was no green grass, it was fed rice with stems and ears, or straw and daikon radish. It also enjoyed drinking sake).

Imperial poem: "When the time comes, even beasts from foreign lands bring joy when seen today in the nine-fold palace"
