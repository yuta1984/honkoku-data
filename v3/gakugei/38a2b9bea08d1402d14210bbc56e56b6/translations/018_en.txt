**A Method for Quickly Removing Ink to Make Paper White Again**

- Ink absorbed into paper fiber (*susa*) is generally difficult to remove quickly. Soak wastepaper (*hōgu*) in water and set it aside. Dig a hole in the ground, wring out the soaked wastepaper slightly, wrap it in rush matting (*komo*), and bury it in the ground. After about a day, rub the ink on the wastepaper with your finger.

---

**[Left Page — Upper Section]**

…and you will find that the ink separates from the paper and adheres to your finger. If the ink has not yet come free, leave it buried as it is. When the ink begins to come off upon rubbing with a finger, take the paper out, beat it well, and rinse it with water. The ink will be entirely removed and the paper will become white again. **[Right Page — Lower Section]**

*(Continued from the previous page — On Bending and Binding Timber and Alcove Posts)*

…even for freshly cut wood, the method of binding is as follows. Collect a large quantity of rat droppings, spread them over a thick layer of ash, cover them with rush matting (*komo*), and leave it overnight. The rat droppings will transform into horse maggots (*kejikezi*). Place these maggots into a pot, add water, and bring it to a boil. Put the wood into this boiling water and simmer it for a while — the wood will become pliable. Hold the wood with a hand towel and bind it as you wish, and it can be freely bent and tied into shape. When building large vessels, bending large, thick pieces of timber is done in the same manner — produce the horse maggots and…

---

**[Left Page — Lower Section]**

…apply them to the wood, and it can be bent freely.

**On Making the Eyes of Dolls and Painted Figures Emit Light**

- To cause the eyes of a painted figure or doll to emit light: grind *shimi* (silverfish) — *(marginal note: an insect that breeds in paper)* — into *gofun* (powdered shell white), and use this mixture to color the eyes. The effect of light emanating [from the eyes] will be remarkable.
