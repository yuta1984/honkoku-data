Perhaps because they do not particularly use cherry blossoms, there are not many poetic compositions by poets on this theme. In Han Wuling's poem "White Cherry Tree":
　I remember when flowers bloomed and snow filled the branches. The gentle wind harmonizes with butterflies carrying flowers as they move. Just now the flowers fall and the playful bees depart. This only causes the master to compose melancholy poetry.
This appears in volume eight of "Ten Thousand Tang Quatrains."
