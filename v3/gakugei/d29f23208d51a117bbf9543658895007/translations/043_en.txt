(Sakate-zakura, when viewed from a distance,) has a suishoku (flushed reddish tint). While this characteristic can be used to distinguish it (from Shibayama-zakura), there are also cases where one of the six petals is twisted and stands up like a raised sail. For this reason, it is easily confused with Hotate-zakura (Sail Cherry). However, Hotate-zakura is white in color, while Sakate-zakura has a slightly more reddish tinge than Hotate-zakura. Shibayama, Hotate, and Sakate differ from one another only by a small degree. They can only be distinguished by their color. Because one of the six petals is twisted, this variety is commonly called "Sakate" (literally, "reverse hand").

【Lower section】
○Iganai said: "Hotate-zakura belongs to the same variety as Shibayama-zakura and has six petals. Furthermore, because one of the petals stands up like a raised sail, it is named accordingly. It is easily confused with Sakate-zakura; however, while Sakate-zakura has a slight suishoku (flushed reddish tint), Hotate-zakura shares the same white color as Shibayama-zakura. It should be noted, however, that it is a different variety from Hokake-zakura."
