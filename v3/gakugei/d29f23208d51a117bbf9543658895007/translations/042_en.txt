As this matter seemed doubtful, I made inquiries at various places, and came to conclude that Shibayama and Washinoo are indeed one and the same thing under two different names. This tree is said to have either originated from the Shibayama family, or to grow abundantly in the Shibayama area. However, it is difficult to say with certainty whether it is double- or single-petaled. Leaves begin to appear while the flowers are still in bloom, and once the leaves are fully out, the flowers fall.

Sakate-zakura (Sakate Cherry)

【Lower section】
○Iganai said: "Sakate-zakura belongs to the same variety as Shibayama-zakura. It is single-petaled, and the shape and size of the flowers are exactly the same as Shibayama-zakura. However, the distinguishing feature is that it has a 'suishoku' (a flushed, slightly reddish tint, as if blushing with drink). Shibayama-zakura is white in color, while Sakate-zakura has a pale reddish hue. Viewed up close, it appears the same as Shibayama-zakura, but when viewed from a distance, Sakate-zakura (appears different)..."
