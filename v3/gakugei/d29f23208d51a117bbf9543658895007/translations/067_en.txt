【Right folio】
○Igan-sai states: Multi-petaled (senyo), white in color. The flowers cluster and droop at the tips of the branches. This is, in effect, a variety of Ō-temari (Large Temari Ball) in which the branches hang downward.

○Don'ei states: The flower form stands upright. In color, the buds are deep, and as they open, they become noticeably paler. Those with three or four layers of large petals are also called "Ō-chōchin" (Large Lantern Cherry).
