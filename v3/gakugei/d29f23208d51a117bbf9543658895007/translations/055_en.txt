This is indeed the Edo-Hōrinji cherry itself; it is not a separate variety but rather one plant bearing two names. However, while it broadly resembles the Edo cherry, the Edo cherry has thin petals, whereas the Edo-Hōrinji cherry has thick petals and is rich and full in its flowering.

○Ikansai states: The flowers are small and roughly similar to those of the Ko-Edo (小江戸) cherry, and they are double-petaled (yaezaki). The color is beautiful and lustrous. The stems are shorter than those of the Ko-Edo cherry. The form of the flower especially resembles that of the Kiridani cherry, though the blooms are smaller than those of the Kiridani cherry.
