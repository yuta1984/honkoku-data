　Mountain cherry blossoms bloom ahead of spring. Red buds fill the frost-covered branches. In secluded places, who will ultimately see them? The fragrant heart knows only itself in vain. It seems to divide and illuminate the morning sun, as if fearing and doubting the evening wind's blowing. If one wishes to ask about the meaning of its colorful beauty, it would likely startle frivolous people.

Thus it is written. This appears in "Twelve Poetry Selections." In Song poet Wang Anshi's poem on mountain cherry blossoms:

　Mountain cherry embraces stone and shades pine branches. Compared to other flowers, its blooming is the most
