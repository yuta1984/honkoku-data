【Right folio】

The flowers resemble those of the double-petaled red plum (*yae no kōbai*) and are deeply crimson. The leaves, trunk, and bark are in every respect identical to those of the cherry. This variety is found at Sennyū-ji and Hiden-in in Higashiyama, but the flowers have not yet bloomed there. This is because it is a tree of a warm region, transplanted to the cold climate of Kyoto, and thus the tree has failed to grow vigorously. It is a different variety from the Hi-zakura of the capital. I myself visited Hiden-in and witnessed this with my own eyes.
