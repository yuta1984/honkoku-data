Uba-zakura (Hag Cherry)
【Lower section】
○Igansei says: "It is the same as
equinox cherry. However, it was named
for having no leaves. 'Ha' (leaf) and 'ha' (tooth) have the same pronunciation in Japanese.
This appears in Elder Kassho's Cherry Catalog."
○According to Elder Kassho's Cherry Catalog: "The stem is short,
flowers grow densely and closely attached, with no leaves.
Named after the meaning of an old hag having no teeth,

【Left page】
this is how it was named. The flowers bloom later than equinox cherry."
○Donnei says: "The flowers open later than Kirigayatsu cherry. They bloom
at the same time as scarlet cherry and Edo cherry. However, the
Uba-higan mentioned in the equinox cherry section and this Uba-zakura are different varieties."
