Edo Cherry (Edo-zakura)
Large / Small (indicating size variations in the illustration)

【Left folio】
○Ikansai states: This cherry has double blossoms (yaezaki) with long stems, and its flower clusters hang downward. It closely resembles the double-blossomed Kiritani cherry, and is indeed a variant (mutation) of the Kiritani. Its color is deeper, but the layering of the petals is less dense than that of the Kiritani. Past its peak bloom, the flowers do not scatter but instead wither and cling to the branches. The overall appearance of the flower is the most opulent and noble among all flowers. It blooms late, flowering after the Kiritani. Furthermore, what is called "Ō-Edo" (Great Edo) refers specifically to the variety found at Hōrinji Temple, which has shorter stems.
