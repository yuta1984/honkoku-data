　　After [the temple was established in] Nosho Tahara Village, Edo Shimotsuke-nyudo Doshin moved this temple to Kanasugi Village in Kohinata-go, Edo-sho, Musashi Province. Later, during the Bunmei era, Ota Saemon-nyudo Seishoken Shuen Dokan restored it. In ancient times it belonged to the Rinzai sect, with Fuo Kokushi as the founding priest, Kyoshu Osho as the second generation, and Zen Master Shukuetsu as the restorer. In the 6th year of Eisho (1509), it was converted to the Soto sect. This was recorded on July 10th of the 10th year of Eisho (1513) by the current resident monk Jitsuzanso of Kongo-ji.

　Jizo Hall《Located on the same mountaintop. The principal image is a Tenjiku Buddha that was enshrined behind Engaku-ji Temple in Kamakura by Lord Yoritomo. During Lord Sanetomo's time, a temple hall was built in Hatano and the statue was moved there, then later transferred to this location together with Kongo-ji Temple》

　This temple is a monastery built in the 2nd year of Kencho (1250) in Tahara Village, Hatano-sho, Sagami Province by Hatano Nakatsukasa Tadatsune《In the Azuma Kagami he is called Nakatsukasa-no-sho Tadatsuna. According to various family genealogies, he was Hatano Nakatsukasa-no-sho Junior Fifth Rank Lower Tadatsuna, who later changed his name to Tadatsune》to pray for the repose of Kamakura Shogun Sanetomo's soul. Later, Edo Shimotsuke-nyudo Shinbutsu moved it to the present location. During the Bunmei era, Ota Dokan renovated the temple and made Zen Master Shukuetsu the head priest《According to notes by Elder Cho in the Baika Mujinzo-den, Zen Master Shukuetsu was Dokan's uncle》. Therefore, memorial tablets and portraits of Lord Sanetomo and Dokan are enshrined here.
　The plaque reading "Enichi-san" on the main gate was written by Obaku monk Sokuhi《According to Master Hakuseki, from the Baika Mujinzo》
