Taiun-ji Temple
　　Historical Site

【Left page】
Ōryūzan Taiun-ji Temple　Located in Kami-Ochiai in the same area, this is an Ōbaku sect Zen temple belonging to Manpuku-ji in Kyoto. The principal image of Nyoirin Kanzeon is said to be a natural stone Buddha that emerged from the ground at this temple. The founding priest was called Hakuō Dōtai Oshō. 《He was a dharma successor of Mokuan Oshō and the teacher of Ryōnen-ni》The second abbot was Ryōnen-ni. Later, Hōun-in Genkō-ni 《daughter of the Sanada family and lady-in-waiting to Tōfukumon-in, who became a dharma disciple of Tetsuzen Oshō》restored this temple, and Tetsuzen Oshō, teacher of Senzan Oshō, is regarded as the founder of its revival. The plaque reading "Taiun-ji" hanging at the main gate was written by Ōbaku's Mokuan Rōjin. The temple's second abbot Ryōnen Zen-ni was called Taiun-in Gensō Oshō. Her birth family was the Kusuyama clan; she was the daughter of a man called Chōjirō, son of Kusuyama Jūrō Yoshihisa, the head priest of Fuji Shrine in Suruga Province. 《Chōjirō lived in retirement in front of Senyu-ji in Kyoto, enjoyed tea ceremony, and appraised ancient paintings. At that time he was known to the world as "Emi no Chōji" (Chōji the Painting Appraiser). The Matsuya Sōwa states he was from Owari Province, and also that Ryōnen was the mother of a Confucian retainer named Ueyama Jūzō, which should be investigated》Initially she served the Ōuchi family, where she was called Yadorigi. Later she left service and returned home. 《The Edo Sunago records that Ryōnen-ni was a court lady who served Tōfukumon-in, but became a nun after the Empress's death》
　Someone arranged a marriage for her with a physician named Matsuda. 《The Edo Sunago gives his name as Matsuda Bansui》She bore three children, both sons and daughters. 《The Shin Chōmonshū states that at age thirty-something she gave birth to three children, male and female, and the eldest son later became Kusuyama Chōjūrō》
