【Right Page】
It belongs to the Shingon sect and is called Shinjo-in. The divine body is a sacred statue hand-carved by Sugawara no Michizane himself, said to be one sun and eight bu (about 5.4cm) tall. According to tradition, during the Kan'ei period, the shogun bestowed this divine statue upon Ohashi Rikkei. 《This Rikkei was skilled in calligraphy and was the founder of the Ohashi school of calligraphy, as stated by Kikuoka Senryo. Upon investigation, his son Ohashi Chozaemon Shigemasa emerged from the Oie school to establish his own school. This is what is commonly called the Ohashi school.》 Therefore, Rikkei built the current shrine and recorded this purpose on the door curtains hung before the deity. 《The former site of this shrine was near Saisho-ji temple in Ushigome, in what is now called Tenjin-cho. A booklet called Hokai Maidan records events from the seventh year of Kan'ei, stating that the shogun's secretary Ohashi Rikkei was granted the Takada Otomo estate, and records that there was a Tenjin shrine on that land. This should serve as evidence.》 It is also said to house sutras written in Sugawara no Michizane's own hand. The stone statues of dragon gods and Kishimojin in front of the shrine are said to have been erected for protection when there was a sutra repository on this site in ancient times.

《Upon investigation, the shrine's tradition states that Ohashi Rikkei enshrined the statue of Sugawara no Michizane received from the shogun in a single shrine, with the former site being Tenjin-cho. Local people say that the site of Saisho-ji temple was formerly the residential land of the Ohashi family. The Nanko-tei Sawa states that Otomo Sogoro Yoshinobu relocated Dazaifu Tenmangu to his own residential land, called that place Tenjin-cho, and later moved it to Takada. The paintings of the thirty-six immortal poets donated by Ohashi Chozaemon still exist today. Upon further consideration, the Edo Kanoko published in the second year of Genroku mentions over two hundred years, so considering that Otomo Yoshinobu was a person from the Bunroku period, the shrine was probably established on his residential land at that time, and later the Ohashi family resided on that site and enshrined the divine statue received from the shogun in that shrine. From Kan'ei to the present Kansei period, it has not yet exceeded two hundred years.》

Takada Horse Ground (Takada Baba) - Located to the north of the same area. Called "oimawashi" (riding ground), it has two sections. Vertically it extends six cho east to west, and the horizontal width is over thirty ken north to south. According to tradition, this is the historic site where Right General Minamoto no Yoritomo came from Sumida River to this place and held a military muster in ancient times. According to local accounts, during the Keicho period, this was grassland opened for the recreational use of the mother of Echigo Shosho 《Lord Tadateru》, called 《Lady Takada》, but in the thirteenth year of Kan'ei, the horse ground was constructed as it is today and made into a place for archery and horsemanship training. 《Some say it was constructed under the supervision of Hayashi Tanba-no-kami Katsumasa, Kato Sanai, Kawamura Kichizaemon, and others. It is also said that the northern horse ground is the historic site where Takeda Shingen tested horses when attacking the Hojo family of Odawara. The row of pine trees to the north were planted by imperial command during the Kyoho period as windbreaks. During the Enpo and Tenwa periods, groups from Zoshigaya would come to this place, and target shooting, large targets, small targets, mounted archery, and other entertainments like noh music, Tosa geki, and hoka performances would create a lively atmosphere.》

【Left Page】
At the beginning of each shogun's reign, as an auspicious custom for prayers for national security, yabusame (mounted archery) ceremonies are held at this location. The costumes and beauty are perfected. The illustrated explanations of these ceremonies are stored at Hoshoe-ji temple, the administrator of Ana Hachiman shrine. The text was written by Kanda Hakuryushi.

《Upon investigation, calling this place Takada has been done for a long time. In the Odawara Hojo family's domain register, among Ota Shinrokuro's territories, lands such as Takada-uchi Akasawa-bun and the same Soeda-bun are noted and added. Also, Edo Takada, which was ruled by Akasawa Senju, was attached to the Akasawa widow during Senju's minority. It is also seen in the same document that a person named Nakamura Heijiro also ruled this land.》
