Also, as it states that there is a Fudo Hall on top of a mound at 《Ruby text: Murasaki no Hitomoto》, perhaps the current site of Namikiri Fudo-son is the historic site called Otsuka. According to tradition, it is a mound built by Ota Dokan for raising signal fires for coordination. Therefore, it was formerly called Ota-zuka, according to one theory. Alternatively, when Kamakura Shogun Prince Morikuni fled from rebellion and died in Otsuka village in Hiki district of Musashi Province, his mausoleum was called O-zuka (Royal Mound), and the naming of this place as Otsuka may be of this type, though the details are unclear.

《Note: There are many places named Otsuka within Edo. This requires further investigation》

Taiho-san Hondenji Temple　Located on a side street in Otsuka-cho, it belongs to the Nichiren sect and is affiliated with Ren'eiji Temple in Suruga Province. It was formerly of the Zen sect and was called Jukosan Zenshoji Temple. During the Genna era, Zen Master Zuio converted to the current sect, changed his own name to Hosen'in Nichigyo, and also changed the temple name to Hondenji.

Sutra-reading Nichiren Daishi　According to the temple's founding history, long ago, when Nichigyo, the restoring founder of this temple, was first called Zen Master Zuio, he examined the doctrines of Master Ren (Nichiren) and discovered that the essential path to enlightenment was limited to the Lotus Sutra, and attempted to convert the sect, but his heart was still undecided
