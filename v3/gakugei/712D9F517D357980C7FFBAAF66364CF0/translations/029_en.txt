In ancient times, this area was part of the Kamakura Highway, hence the name "Michiyama" (Road Mountain). In medieval times it fell into great ruin, with only a small shrine remaining under the sacred hackberry tree. A shrine priest named Masayasu of that time built and maintained the shrine as it appears today. {{According to local legend, around the Enpo era (1673-1681), the spirit of a golden horse appeared and ravaged the fields in this area. The villagers witnessed this numerous times. When pursued, it would hide in the mountain valleys. That valley is called Koma-ga-tani (Horse Valley). Also, since people would lose sight of the horse from atop the bridge, that bridge is called Komadome Bridge (Horse-stopping Bridge)}}

Mejiro Fudo Hall: Located to the east of the same area, facing the edge of Sekiguchi. It belongs to the Shingon sect and is called Tofusan Shin-Chokokushi Temple. {{It serves as a lodging temple for Koikeho of Hase}} The sacred image of the principal deity Fudo Myoo {{eight sun tall}} is a work by Kobo Daishi. The three large characters "Tofusan" on the main gate plaque are written by Nangaku Etsuzan.

According to the temple's origin story, after Kobo Daishi returned from Tang China, when he was secluded at Mt. Yudono in Ushu Province, Dainichi Nyorai suddenly transformed into the form of Fudo Myoo and appeared at the base of a waterfall, telling the master: "This land is the pure land of the inner realization and secrets of all Buddhas, so it abhors the defiled fires of conditioned existence.
