Yamabuki no Sato (Kerria Rose Village) refers to the area around private houses north of Takada Horse Ground. Long ago, when Ōta Mochisuke was at Edo Castle, one day he was hawking near Kanagawa in Totsuka. Caught in a sudden downpour, he entered a nearby farmhouse and asked to borrow a rain cape. A young woman came out from inside and, without words, offered Mochisuke a single branch of kerria roses in full bloom. This was her answer based on a waka poem by Prince Kaneakira found in the Goshūi-shū: "Though kerria roses bloom in layers seven and eight deep, how sad that not even one bears fruit."

Her wit is still praised today and passed down through generations as a fine tale.
