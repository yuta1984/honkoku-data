Methods for Removing Stains from Dyed Fabrics
〇When oil gets on silk, sprinkle talc powder,
　lay paper over it, and rub many times with
　a heated iron—it will come off
〇For washing the same, put one gō of salt
　in two shō of water, boil well and cool slightly, then
　pour over the oily spot. When much water
　is needed, adjust the salt proportion
　accordingly
〇When fish broth or miso soup gets on fabric,
　crush turnip and rinse with
　that juice
〇When ink gets on fabric, sprinkle pinellia powder
　and wash with water held in the mouth
〇When iron solution or tooth-blackening liquid
　gets on fabric, boil rice vinegar
　and wash with it
〇When phellodendron or gardenia juice
　gets on fabric, rinse with
　plum vinegar
〇When mold or blackening occurs from dew or rain,
　boil plum leaves and
　wash with the liquid
〇When blood or vermillion gets on
　white fabric, rub with rice grains
　and wash. 〇Also, if you peel ginger
　thinly and place it on
　the stained area, it will transfer and remove
　the stain
〇When lacquer gets on fabric, if you
　boil miso, clarify it and wash,
　it will come off well
〇When persimmon tannin gets on fabric, boil
　bonito flakes and
　wash with the liquid
〇For indigo-dyed fabric, put lime in water and
　boil the cloth—it will become white
〇For tea-dyed fabric, add sake to water and boil—
　it will become white
