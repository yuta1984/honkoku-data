Even if one is a person of great learning and many talents, if one lowers oneself about fifty percent below one's actual learning and abilities and behaves with humility and prudence, people will gladly employ such a person. Even if one is completely illiterate, if one considers oneself fifty percent lower than one's actual illiteracy, saying "I am the greatest fool of all ages" and feels shame for one's ignorance, people will praise such a person as clever and love them. One should understand this principle. No matter how beautiful one's appearance, how inventive, wealthy, learned, or talented one may be, if one becomes even slightly arrogant beyond one's natural disposition, one will be detested by others and invite misfortune. No matter how unattractive, humble in appearance, poor, foolish, or unlearned one may be, when one keeps one's pride about fifty percent lower than one's natural disposition, people will love such a person, one's life will be comfortable, and the gods and Buddha will show divine favor. Now that you have lowered your pride, this is the compassion that improves the beauty of your heart
