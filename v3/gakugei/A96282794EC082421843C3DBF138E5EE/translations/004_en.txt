【Left Page】
　　　General Principles
1. The name "Jinkouki" originates from a phrase "jinko raiji shigō fuhekidatsu" 
from a work compiled by Mr. Yoshida 250 years ago in Kan'ei 4 (1627). 
I also follow this tradition.
1. "Ka" (addition) means to gather and put in ○ "Gen" (subtraction) means to take away
1. "Jō" (multiplication) means to gather the same numbers, referring to multiplying
1. "Jo" (division) means to divide into equal numbers, referring to dividing
1. "In" refers to multiplication with single-digit numbers
1. "Ki" refers to division with single-digit numbers
1. In conventional eight-operation arithmetic, calculations like 120,000 often 
lack their division terminology. This compilation now supplements this.
