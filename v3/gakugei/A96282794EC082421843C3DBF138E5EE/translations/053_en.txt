　Place 1 *sen* — paired with the quantity sought and of the same kind as ㈠. Multiply ㈡ (21) by ㈢ (1 *sen*) to get 21 *sen*. Divide by ㈠ (14), and one learns that for 1 *sen*, one gets one and a half persimmons. That is, the half yields five (*rin*).

○Further, when the price of one persimmon is 3 *rin* 5 *mō*, and one asks the price of 28 persimmons:
　Set up as "㈠ one item　㈡ 3 *rin* 5 *mō*　㈢ 28 — how much?", placing one at the first position as ㈠; place its paired price, 3 *rin* 5 *mō*, as ㈡; and in ㈢, place 28 — paired with the quantity sought and of the same kind as ㈠. Multiply ㈡ (3 *rin* 5 *mō*) by ㈢ (28), and the price is found to be 9 *sen* 8 *rin*. That is, since ㈠ is one — a quantity of one — no division is performed.
