Whether it be rin, 1 sen, 10 sen, 1 yen, 1 koku, 1 hyo, 10, or 100, they are all the same - each represents the number one. Similarly, two beads can represent 2 sen, 2 yen, 20, or 200 - all representing the number two. Three, four, five, and so forth follow the same principle. Also, since one upper bead represents five, it can be used for 5 rin, 5 sen, 50 yen, or 500 yen - all representing the number five. Therefore, when an upper bead is combined with one lower bead, it becomes the number six; when combined with two lower beads, it becomes seven. When combined with three lower beads, it becomes eight; when combined with four lower beads, it becomes nine. Therefore, first take the abacus, move all upper beads to the upper side, move all lower beads to the lower side [abacus diagram], and move the beads representing the desired number toward the center bar.
