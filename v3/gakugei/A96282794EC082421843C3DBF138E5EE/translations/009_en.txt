【Right Page】
Whether it represents rin, one sen, ten sen, one yen, one koku, one hyō, ten, or one hundred, all are numbers representing one. Also, two can be used as two sen, two yen, twenty, or two hundred - all representing the number two. Three, four, and five are all similar. Also, one upper bead represents five. Therefore, this can be used as five rin, five sen, fifty yen, or five hundred yen - all representing the number five. Therefore, combining the upper bead with one lower bead makes six, combining the upper bead with two lower beads makes seven. Combining three lower beads makes eight, and combining four lower beads makes nine. Therefore, first take the abacus, move all upper beads to the upper side, move all lower beads to the lower side【abacus diagram】, and move the numbers you wish to place toward the center rod.
