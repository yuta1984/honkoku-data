Whale shaku (equivalent to one shaku two sun five fun of the carpenter's square) - this is called the merchant's shaku
Western "Elle" shaku three shaku two sun nine fun five rin and more, "Palm" three sun two fun nine rin
Chinese "Cubit" one shaku zero one fun seven rin, English "Foot" one shaku zero zero three rin weak

　　　Volume Numbers (Units of Capacity)
　Koku (meaning ten to, above are large numbers), To (meaning ten shō), Shō (meaning ten gō, same below), Gō, Shaku, Sai,
　Zu (meaning four shō), Ku (meaning four zu, one to six shō), Fu (meaning five ku, eight to), Ri (meaning two fu, sixteen to), Shō (meaning five ri, eighty to), I (meaning two shō, one hundred sixty to)
　Tō (meaning five to or two to five shō, said to be changed to koku after the Han dynasty)
Kyō-masu (mouth diameter four sun nine fun, depth two sun seven fun, capacity sixty-four sun eight fun two rin seven mō) - the commonly used measure today
Ko-masu (mouth diameter five sun, depth two sun five fun, capacity sixty-two sun five fun), Musha-masu (equivalent to eight gō of kyō-masu, a private measure in common use)
Kōshū-masu (equivalent to three shō of kyō-masu, considered as one shō) - In old times there were officially licensed measure guilds
Western: Kan (five gō five shaku eighteen and more), Ton (six koku five to three shō seven gō weak)

【Left Page】
　　　Weight Numbers (Units of Weight)
　Kan (meaning ten hyaku-me, above are large numbers), Hyaku (meaning ten jū-monme), Jū (meaning ten monme), Sen (meaning ten fun, using the monme character), Fun (below same as small numbers)
　Kin (not fixed depending on goods, generally as follows): Tō-me (160 me), Shiro-me (230 me)
　　Fundō-me (300 me), Hirano-me (220 me)
　Ryō (not fixed depending on goods, using four monme or five monme)
Western: Pound (268 monme three fun two rin and more)

　　　Celestial Degrees (Numbers used in astronomy)
　Shū (meaning twelve kyū, 360 degrees), Kyū (meaning thirty degrees), Do (meaning sixty fun), Fun (meaning sixty byō), Byō (meaning sixty bi, follow this pattern below)
　Bi, Sen, Kotsu, Bō, (In ancient times one degree was divided into one hundred parts and one part into one hundred seconds, this was called the hundred-part calculation)

　　　Earth Measure (Numbers used in geography)
　Ri (using thirty-six chō, or fifty chō), Chō (sixty ken), Ken (considered six shaku, or there are six shaku three sun, six shaku five sun)
