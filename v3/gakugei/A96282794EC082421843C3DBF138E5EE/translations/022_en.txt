【Left Page】
　　　Division and Multiplication (除 means to divide, 乗 means to multiply. The common term "ken'ichi" refers to the beginning of this chapter)

Ken'ichi (Visible One): Division recitation uses the sounds of the one times table throughout. When one-advance one-ten cannot be done, it is called "ken'ichi mutō sakukyū," and when subtraction is not possible, it is called "ki'ichi bai'ichi," returning one to the next position.

When 100 me of silver is divided among 16 people, how much per person?　　Answer: 6 monme 2 bu 5 rin

Explanation: Dividing by a divisor of more than two digits is commonly called "ken'ichi." First, divide the first digit of the dividend by the first digit of the divisor. This is called the initial quotient (the result of division). Match that quotient with the number in the next digit of the divisor, recite using the multiplication table, and subtract from the dividend starting from the next position of the quotient. Continue this process sequentially for each digit of the divisor (this completes the division for the initial quotient).
