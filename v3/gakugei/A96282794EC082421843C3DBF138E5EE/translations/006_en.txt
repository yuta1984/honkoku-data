　One, Ten, Hundred (meaning ten tens), Thousand (meaning ten hundreds), Ten thousand (meaning ten thousands), One hundred thousand,
　One million, Ten million, Oku/Yi (meaning ten thousand times ten thousand), Ten oku, One hundred oku, One thousand oku,
　Chō/Trillion (meaning ten thousand oku), Ten chō, One hundred chō, One thousand chō, Kei (meaning ten thousand chō), Ten kei,
　One hundred kei, One thousand kei, Gai (meaning ten thousand kei), Ten gai, One hundred gai, One thousand gai,
　Beyond these: shi, jō, kō, kan, sei, zai, kyoku, etc., all of which can be inferred. These are called the places of large multiplication.
　This is what is commonly used today. Besides these, there are names such as gōgasha and asōgi, but
　most of these appear in Buddhist teachings.
　　　Small Numbers　　Numbers below one
　Fun (meaning ten rin), Rin (meaning ten mō), Mō (meaning ten shi), Shi (meaning ten kotsu, follow this pattern for the rest), Kotsu, Bi,
　Sen, Sha, Jin, Ai, Byō, Baku,
　Beyond these: moko, shunjun, suyu, etc., exist without limit, all appearing in Buddhist teachings.

【Left Page】
　Generally, the limit of small numbers becomes emptiness (kū), and the limit of large numbers becomes void (kyo). When reaching these limits,
　they are beyond what each person's vision can comprehend. Also, in Sūri Seion, "futsu" is recorded
　after "mō." Therefore, calendar books often use the character "futsu."
　　　Degree Numbers (Units of Length)
　Jō (meaning ten shaku), Shaku, Sun, Fun, Rin, Mō,
　Tan (from 2.5 or 2.6 to 3 jō in whale shaku, with variations in length according to quality, all terms for silk cloth), Hiki (meaning two tan),
　Shi (meaning eight sun), Jin (meaning four shaku), Hiro (meaning two jin, which is eight shaku), Jō (meaning two hiro), In (meaning ten jō),
Kanejashi/Carpenter's square (equivalent to eight sun in whale shaku) - The name comes from the curved
　rectangular tool used by craftsmen. Its reverse scale divides one shaku four sun one fun four rin
　and more into one shaku increments, and its origin comes from square root extraction. The diagonal of a square
　one shaku measures one shaku four sun one fun four rin and more. Therefore it is called sumigane.
