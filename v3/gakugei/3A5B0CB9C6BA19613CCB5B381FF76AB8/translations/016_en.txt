○As mentioned above, some have names but no poems, while others have poems but no names. There are various cases of this type as well, but now I have taken words from their poems to make their names, or named them after their residences, creating temporary names. ○In all books, it is a general rule to give the highest official rank for people with court positions. However, now I have not followed this example, writing "Prince Genji" where "Rokujō-in" should be written, and "Kashiwagi Emon-no-kami" where "Kashiwagi Gon-dainagon" should be written—there are many such cases. This is to present the forms that have reached the ears of people in the world even slightly more widely, making them easier for children to remember.
