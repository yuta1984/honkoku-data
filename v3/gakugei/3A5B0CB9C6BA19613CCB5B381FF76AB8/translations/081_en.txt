Gengen-tei (Ladder to Word Origins) - 1 volume - Written by Ōishi Chibiki

(This book examines and determines the original meanings of words in detail. Substantives use only clear sounds while predicates also have voiced sounds; there are no examples of voicing at the beginning of words; it also details sino-Japanese vocabulary and words mixing on'yomi and kun'yomi readings. The arrangement follows the fifty-sound order for convenient reference)

Kana Benran (Kana Handbook) - 1 volume - Compiled by Ōno Hiroshiro

(This book is called "Categorical Kana Handbook for Euphonic Changes, Sino-Japanese, and Voiced Words," detailing kana from elegant language to sino-Japanese and colloquial words. It clarifies confusing cases where the euphonic change of "hi" resembles "i" and "wi," and "he" resembles "e" and "we")

Kokin Wakashū Shinkōsei (New Revision of the Kokinshū) - 2 volumes - Examined by Elder Kamo, re-edited by Elder Suzuya

(This is a good edition of the Kokinshū)

Gosen Wakashū (Later Collection of Waka) - Small format - 2 volumes

(The printed editions of the Gosen Collection circulating in the world are only in large volumes, inconvenient for carrying, so this was made into small volumes with corrections added using secret editions from various masters' houses, specifically prepared for the convenience of gatherings and travel)

【Left Page, Upper Section】
Shūgai Kasen (Poetry Immortals Outside Collections) - 1 volume - Selected by Emperor Gen'na, also called "Modern Poetry Immortals"

(This contains the thirty-six poetry immortals that the august Retired Emperor Go-Mizunoo selected and had painted on Empress Tōfukumon'in's folding screens. The authors' names are appended at the end)

Shōgo Kanazukai (Correct Kana Usage) - 1 volume - Compiled by Kamo Suetaka Agatanushi

(This book is based on the Kojiki, Nihon Shoki, Man'yōshū, Wamyō Ruijushō, etc., arranging word kana in iroha order for convenient reference)

Genji Hyakunin Isshu (One Hundred Poets from Genji) - 1 volume - Written by Master Kurosawa Okimaro

(This book follows Teika's Ogura poem cards, selecting one hundred people from The Tale of Genji with one famous poem each, adding annotations for easy understanding by children and women. Since the main work is lengthy, one should first understand the general meaning of Genji through this book)

【Right Page, Lower Section】
Tanki Yōryaku (Essentials of Single Cavalry) - 5 volumes - Compiled by Murai Masahiro

(This book details armor wearing procedures, the attachment of horo cloaks, helmet cord tying, back banner placement, etc., with diagrams for each, and clarifies the traditions of hand-carried spears, swords, and equipment, exhausting the essentials of single cavalry tactics. Not only military families but also those studying court customs should definitely keep this at their side. Master Murai is the author of "Shinbu Tekisei Bugaku Sen'nyū" and others, and is highly renowned)

Ashikaga-ke Bukan (Military Directory of the Ashikaga House) - 1 volume - Edited by Master Kanshō

(This book is a military directory of the Ashikaga shogunal house, thoroughly researched using records and family crest registers from that period, greatly assisting scholars)

Kyūsen Zushiki (Illustrated Guide to Bow and Arrow) - 1 volume - Written by Master Kurihara

(This book is one section on bows and arrows from the master's work "Burin Hōryō." Military families must definitely read this book)

【Left Page, Lower Section】
Shōchū Kotō Meikan (Pocket Guide to Famous Ancient Swords) - 1 volume - Compiled by Retainer Danen

(While there are many previous mei-zukushi catalogs, this book differs by detailing contemporary and same-period works, exclusive dual works, transmission lineages, rare swordsmiths, Yoshino era signatures, striking swordsmiths, blade measurements, tang, ridgeline, width variations, point styles, grain patterns, nie, file marks, construction methods, Sanskrit characters and engravings, appraisal meeting bid responses, smiths' official names, craftsman rankings, smith genealogies and name registers, etc. Difficult items are illustrated, doubtful matters omitted - truly a rare and precious book)

Katchū Zushiki (Illustrated Guide to Armor) - 1 volume - Written by Master Kurihara

(This book is the second volume of "Burin Hōryō," clarifying armor illustrations)

Buki Sode-kagami (Sleeve Mirror of Weapons) - 1 volume - Same author

(This book illustrates all types of weapons in diagrams with detailed commentary on military matters)

Buki Sode-kagami Kōhen (Sleeve Mirror of Weapons, Second Volume) - 1 volume - Same author

(This book covers helmets, half-helms, throat guards, horse equipment, and battle flags)
