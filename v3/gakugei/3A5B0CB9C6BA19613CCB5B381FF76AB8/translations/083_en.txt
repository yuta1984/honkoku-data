(Regarding the Man'yōshū, Kokinshū, and Imperial anthologies, those interested in poetry study have long kept this on their desks as reference poetry, as everyone knows. However, good editions were lost early on, and circulated versions contained many errors. Fortunately obtaining the corrected and annotated editions by the masters of Keien-in, Abbot Keichū, and Kamo no Agata-nushi, with additional annotations by Master Yamamoto Meisei, this is the premier rare book for reference poetry. The volumes are divided into approximately 25 categories with a total of 516 topics. Volume 1 covers seasonal and celestial phenomena, Volumes 2-3 cover terrestrial features, Volume 4 covers love, celebration, parting, and miscellaneous forms, Volume 5 includes miscellaneous thoughts, and Volume 6 covers plants, insects, trees, birds, and fish - all topics meaningfully arranged. The collection includes 193 poets total, each with ancestral names listed. The total number of poems is 4,518)
