Kinkadō Publishing Catalog　　　　　　Suharaya Sasuke (Nihonbashi Minamiōdōri 4-chōme)

Genji Shinobugusa (The Tale of Genji Study Guide) - 5 volumes - Preface by Narushima Kō
(This book explains the general meaning of the entire Tale of Genji in familiar language so that beginners can easily understand it. Those who study the Genji should definitely read and savor this book first)

Man'yō Nara no Ochiba (Fallen Leaves of Nara from the Man'yōshū) - 5 volumes - Compiled by Masaki Chikan
(This book categorizes interesting and rare words from the Man'yōshū for beginners who want to compose poetry and write, creating five volumes with topics. Volume 1: Celestial phenomena; Volume 2: Geography; Volume 3: Shinto deities, Buddhism, human relations, provinces, residences; Volume 4: Clothing, food, utensils; Volume 5: Birds, beasts, fish, insects, plants. A book to keep at hand for poetic studies and textual research)

Chidori no Ato (Traces of Plovers) - 1 volume - Written by Nakatomi Chikamitsu
(This book establishes the proper handling of poetry drafts, paper for copying poems, and poem cards based on examination of ancient masters' authentic works and old books)

Minki Ibun (Last Days of Ming Dynasty) - 4 volumes - Written by Qing scholar Zou Xishan
(This book is a compilation by Qing's Zou Xishan, detailing the facts about Koxinga from the beginning and end of Li Zicheng's rebellion at the end of Ming through to the Qing's pacification of Fujian and Guangdong)

Rekidai Teiō Shōtōfu (Genealogy of Dynastic Imperial Succession) - 1 folding book - Edited by Kihan Shunsen
(This book creates genealogies of all the rulers of successive Chinese dynasties from Tang and Yu through the Qing Daoguang Emperor, providing convenience for those reading history)

Sōsei Waiben (Comprehensive Analysis of Cursive Script Masters) - 8 volumes - Traced by Qing's Zhu Jialing, character verification by Japan's Nagane Bunpō
(Among the many books collecting cursive calligraphy styles throughout Chinese dynasties, none surpasses the excellence of this compilation. Even the calligraphy of our country's Prince Kaneakira is included in this compilation. There is a character index from two strokes to thirty strokes by which characters can be searched. Volume 8 includes observations on cursive script fundamentals. This is a book that gentlemen studying cursive script cannot help but treasure)
