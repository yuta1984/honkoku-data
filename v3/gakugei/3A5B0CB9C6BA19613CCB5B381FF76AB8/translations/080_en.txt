Kokon-sen (Ancient and Modern Selection) - 3 volumes - Compiled by Master Motoori, edited by Murata Haruki

(This book contains poems specially selected by Master Motoori from the Twenty-one Imperial Anthologies for those learning to compose poetry, chosen as models for poetic form and diction to be constantly recited and appreciated)

Ruidai Waka Hoketsu (Supplementary Collection for Categorical Waka) - 6 volumes - Selected by Katō Kofū

(This book laments that the commonly circulated categorical waka collections contain only topics with 2,900 missing poems, and broadly collects from successive imperial anthologies, family collections, and poetry contests, while correcting erroneous characters in topics - a book that readers should definitely keep at their desks)

Kara Monogatari (Chinese Tales) - 1 volume - Written by Monk Saigyō, annotated by Shimizu Hamaomi

(This book renders Chinese historical anecdotes in an interesting manner using Japanese language. It is said to be written by Monk Saigyō, and indeed the literary style and poetic vocabulary seem to be from that era, according to Master Hamaomi's theory)

Cursive Script "Anterior Red Cliff Rhapsody" - 1 volume - Written by Master Tenmin

(This book contains the Anterior Red Cliff Rhapsody written by Master Shibutsu. The calligraphy style is of one school's manner, neither agitated nor strained, suitable as a copybook)

Shōgaku Daiji (Elementary Learning Preface) - 1 volume - Written by Master Ryūtaku

(This book contains the Elementary Learning Preface by Song Dynasty's Zhu Wengong written by Master Ryūtaku. The brush strength shows vigor and has the style of Tang calligraphers)

Takimoto Kisei-jō (Takimoto Clear Weather Album) - 1 volume - Authentic work by Shōjō-ō

(This book contains excerpts of poems and songs from the Rōei Collection beautifully written by Shōjō-ō)

Tsutsumi Chūnagon Kashū (Poetry Collection of Middle Counselor Tsutsumi) - Written by Kino Tsurayuki

(This book contains the poetry collection of Middle Counselor Tsutsumi Kanesuke written by Kino Tsurayuki, rarely transmitted and carved into woodblocks. The kana follows ancient methods and is undoubtedly authentic)

【Left Page, Upper Section】
Kana-kō (Study of Kana) - 2 volumes - Written by Okada Masumi, Chinese preface by Master Hōsai, kana preface by Master Hamaomi

(This book examines in detail, following the order of the fifty sounds, those kana characters whose original Chinese sources became difficult to identify, since kana originally flowed from cursive script styles. It also includes ancient calligraphy styles in the headers, making it highly beneficial for those studying calligraphy)

Shin Rōeishū (New Collection of Recited Poetry) - 1 volume - Compiled by Shinkai Kashiwagi, edited by Sodō Yamamoto

(This book selects figures from Emperor Monmu above to around the Hōgen era below, correcting the chronological order. Songs are selected to match the poems, so regardless of the authors' periods, they are divided into the four seasons of spring, summer, autumn, and winter, just like the original collection. There is a bibliography at the beginning and seasonal topics written in large characters at the end)

Kasen Eshō (Illustrated Commentary on Poetry Immortals) - 1 volume - Written by Fujiwara Masaomi, paintings copied by Kita Takesei

(This book uses the poets' genealogies and the essence of their poems as headers. The illustrations are from Master Takesei's hand based on original drawings - truly a beautiful edition)
