【Right Page】
Now, if I were to live on, would there come a time when I would long for these current days that I once saw as painful?

This expresses one's inner feelings.
The meaning is: if one continues to live in this world, there may come a time when one would fondly remember these current days that seem so painful now, in some future yet unseen.
The times that were once thought of as a painful world have now become something to be missed.
The word "shinobaremu" in this poem means "will be fondly remembered."
This is not the "shinobu" (endure) of "hidden endurance."
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Fujiwara no Kiyosuke Ason

【Left Page】
During the time when I am lost in thought all night long, even the gaps in my bedroom seem cold and distant, as dawn refuses to break.

This is a love poem. The meaning is:
Because the one I love is cold and distant, during the time when I spend all night in contemplation, though I wish for dawn to come quickly to end my suffering, it does not come, and not only is my beloved distant, but even the gaps in the bedroom's wooden doors [text unclear] as well.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Priest Shun'e
