【Right Page】
Waves that approach the shore of Sumiyoshi—
even in my dreams, must I be concerned
about prying eyes on the path of sleep?

This is a love poem lamenting that there are so many prying eyes that even in dreams one must be wary of being seen. The meaning is: "To visit the woman I truly love, I avoid prying eyes, but why must I avoid people's gaze even on the path through dreams? How truly shameful this is." This also uses what is called jo-kotoba (introductory words), and "waves that approach the shore of Sumiyoshi" has no particular meaning. The word "yoru" (approach) in "yoru nami" (approaching waves) leads to the word "yoru" (night) in "yoru sae ya."

Fujiwara no Toshiyuki Ason

【Left Page】
Naniwa Bay—not even for the brief span
of a short reed's single joint
should I pass through this world without meeting you

A love poem
The meaning is: "Am I to pass through this world without meeting even for the brief moment of a short joint of the reeds growing by Naniwa's seashore? What a heartless person you are." This was probably composed and sent to someone who, while the poet felt deep love, remained cold and indifferent and would not come even when sought.
