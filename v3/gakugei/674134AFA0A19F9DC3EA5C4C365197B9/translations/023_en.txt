【Right Page】
Even if I exhaust myself at Naniwa,
since I am in such anguish, I now think the same—
that I would meet you.

This poem was composed and sent when his secret visits to the lady known as Kyōgoku no Miyasudokoro became known, creating difficulties, and it became impossible for them to meet again. The meaning is as follows: Having become unable to meet like this and spending my days in lamentation, now there is no point in living, and death would be equally painful. Therefore, even if I am further censured by people and become unable to live, it would not be painful. I wish to meet you even if it means casting away my life. "Miotsukushi" refers to the wooden stakes set up as markers for ship routes in Naniwa Bay. This is used as a metaphor for "exhausting oneself." To exhaust oneself means to lose one's life, to cast away one's life.

Prince Motoyoshi

【Left Page】
Just because you said "I will come now"—
throughout the long ninth month
I have waited until the dawn moon appeared.

This is also a love poem.
The meaning is: Relying only on a letter from my beloved saying "I will come right away," I waited and waited through these long September nights, not even entering my bedchamber, until I saw the dawn moon appear. The person who made the promise never came at all—such is my lament. The dawn moon would be the moon around the twenty-fourth or twenty-fifth day of the lunar month.

Sōsei Hōshi
