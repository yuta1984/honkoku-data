【Right Page】
"My hermitage lies / In the southeast of the capital— / Thus quietly I dwell / On what people call Mount Uki-yo (Weary-of-the-world)"

The meaning of the poem is:
My hermitage is located on Mount Uji in the southeast direction from Kyoto, and living in such quiet seclusion, people say that "Kisen has become weary of the world and chosen to live as a recluse." The expression "yo wo ushi" (weary of the world) is used as a wordplay (combining with Uji mountain).

Monk Kisen

【Left Page】
"The color of flowers / Has faded away— / While I spent my days in vain / Gazing during the long rains / As my life passed by in this world"

This expresses how, while spending empty days absorbed in various worldly worries, long rains fell, and the flower colors became poor without even being seen once—what a regrettable thing. "Utsuru" (to change/fade) refers both to the passage of time and to the changing of colors. "Itazura ni" (in vain) means the same as "munashiku" (emptily). "Nagame" refers to gazing at the sky while lost in thought. Since long rains are also called "nagame," the poem combines "growing old in the world" with the addition of spring's long rains.
