【Right Page】
As we part ways at Mount Inaba's peak
where the pine trees grow—
if you should "wait" for me,
I will return at once

This poem was composed at the time of parting when he went down to serve as governor of Inaba Province. The meaning is: "Now I part from everyone and go to Inaba Province, but like the pine trees growing on the peaks of that province's mountains, if I hear that you 'wait' for me (playing on the word 'matsu' meaning both 'pine' and 'wait'), I will immediately return." This was composed to console the hearts of those who were sad about the parting.

Middle Counselor Yukihira

【Left Page】
Even in the age of the gods,
with all their supernatural power,
never was it heard that the Tatta River
bound its waters in Chinese crimson

This poem was composed about a painting on a folding screen showing autumn leaves flowing in the Tatta River. The meaning is: "In the age of the gods there were many various wondrous things, but even in the age of the gods, no one ever heard of the Tatta River binding its waters in crimson tie-dye." The fluttering fall of autumn leaves is likened to crimson kanoko shibori (fawn-spot tie-dye). "Chihayaburu" is a makura-kotoba (pillow word) for "god" and has no particular meaning. "Karakurenai" means a supremely beautiful crimson color. "Kukuru" refers to binding silk in various places and dyeing it—what we now call shibori dyeing.

Ariwara no Narihira Ason
