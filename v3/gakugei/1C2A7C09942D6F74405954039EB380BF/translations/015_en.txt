【Right Page Upper Section】
○Coral is a marine gem that is red in color. It is harvested using iron nets. It is one of the seven treasures.

○Whetstone (to) is a fine grinding stone. It can also be written as kei. Yellow whetstone (ōto) refers to finishing stones.

○Grindstone (rei) is a coarse grinding stone. It refers to rough stones and can also be written as arato.

○Gauze (sha) includes gold gauze, silver gauze, patterned gauze, and others - all thin fabrics. There are also types called hora-ro and modoshi.

○Noshime is a striped textile. It is formal wear for samurai on celebratory occasions, and is also worn by Noh actors.

○Brocade (kin) is made by weaving threads of five colors. It is similar to what is commonly called gold brocade (kinran).

○Embroidery (shū) refers to decorative stitching in five colors. It means needlework.

○Felt (jū) is fine woolen cloth. The finest quality is called velvet (birōdo). Kassu, fura, and toramen are all woolen fabrics.

【Left Page Upper Section】
○Crimson dyeing (momizome) produces red colors. Varieties include plum-red, scarlet, peach, medium red, and madder - all red colors.

○Kaga silk is silk woven in Komatsu, Kaga Province.

○Koku is crinkled gauze. It refers to what is now called chirimen (crepe), commonly written as chirimendan.

○Shusu (satin) comes in five colors and also has striped patterns.

○Shuchin comes in five colors and is woven using silk threads.

○Twill (ryō) is diagonal weave. It is also called ayako (rinzu). Flower twill is patterned rinzu, and lustrous twill is smooth rinzu.

○Shō is a cool fabric called suzushi. It should be written as raw shō, while cooked silk refers to degummed silk.
