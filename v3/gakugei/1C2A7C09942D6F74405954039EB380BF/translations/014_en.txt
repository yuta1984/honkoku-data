Treasures and Currency《This section compiles all treasures including gold and silver, pearls and gems, copper and iron, stones and shells, brocade and embroidery, twill and gauze》

【Right Page Upper Section】
○Gold includes purple-polished gold, yellow gold, and placer gold. In Japan, it was formerly produced in Ōshū Province. Gilding refers to gold plating.

○Silver is white silver. There are varieties like nanryō silver and bei silver. Commonly called hebuki. Silver plate refers to sheet metal.

○Lead is blue metal. Tin is white lead, commonly called tin-lead. White solder is the same. Lead is heated to make red lead oxide.

【Left Page Upper Section】
○Giant clam is the name of a gem and one of the seven treasures. It resembles stone gems. Also called sea fan, with the Japanese name itayagai.

○Tortoiseshell is the name of a turtle whose shell has patterns. It can be made into vessels, such as combs, hairpins, and incense boxes.

○Lapis lazuli is the name of a gem, a luminous stone. It is one of the seven treasures and blue in color.

○Amber is formed when pine resin falls to the ground and becomes amber after a thousand years. It is a gem that attracts dust well. Its color is yellow.

○Crystal is the name of a gem and one of the seven treasures. It is a gem from western countries. Can also be written as hari.

○Langgan is a luminous gem. There are langgan trees on Mount Kunlun. Its color is blue.
