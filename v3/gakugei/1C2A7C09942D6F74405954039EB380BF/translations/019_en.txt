They are called seki, which refers to what we now call account books.

○Calendars are said to have been created by the Yellow Emperor. It is also said that Yōsei created them, or that Gika created them.

○Fu refers to split tallies called fukei or fushin. Bamboo is cut to six sun in length and split, then matched together as proof of trust. They are also made of wood.

○Ki refers to what we now call desks. They are also armrests called kyōsoku, or hyōki. They are also written as ki (desk).

○San (counting rods) are six sun in length and are used for calculation with calendar numbers. In the time of the Yellow Emperor, Reishu created arithmetic. "San" (筭) is incorrect and should be written as "san" (算).

○Candles are made by mixing oil into wax, kneading it, putting it in bamboo tubes and hardening it to make light. There are silver candles and vermillion candles.

【Left Page Upper Section】
○Ruyi are made of wood, bamboo, ivory, or tortoiseshell. They are items held in the hand with notes written on them so as not to forget things. They are what Manjusri Bodhisattva holds.

○Ei are items held up behind the emperor. This is the duty of court ladies.

○Futsujin refers to dusters. In Zen Buddhism they are called hossu. They are tools for giving directions. They are made from the tails of zhu deer or white yak hair.

○An refers to what we now call desks. They are also called writing desks or tables, and are also called ki-an.

○Bells (struck bells) use the ōshiki tone among the twelve tones as the best. Therefore they are called shō.
