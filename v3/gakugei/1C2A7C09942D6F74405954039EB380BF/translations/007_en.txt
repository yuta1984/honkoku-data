【Right Page Upper Section】
○The "boku" is a Chinese cap that Emperor Wu of Northern Zhou first created.
It consists of a single width of cloth worn on the head with four legs protruding.
○The "i" is something that covers both ears.
It is the cord of the cap. It is what hangs down below the collar.
○The "kin" is a head covering. In its construction, items of
the same square shape are called "kin," and round
ones are also called "bou" (hat).
○The "bou" is head clothing. In China, from high-ranking
officials to low-ranking officials,
all wear hats. It is worn under the cap.
○The "boushi" is a monk's cap. It is worn during Buddhist gatherings
and religious services.
○The "kotsu" is a hand tablet. The emperor uses jade,
feudal lords use ivory, ministers use fish fins or patterned
bamboo, and samurai use wood with ancient characters carved on it—all
use them. It is what officials hold in their hands.
○The "eboshi" is made of paper and painted with lacquer.

【Left Page Upper Section】
The left-folded type is worn by courtiers of chamberlain rank and above.
The right-folded type is worn by those of fifth court rank and above.
Chamberlain rank and above use silk cords, while fourth
rank and below tie them with paper cords.
○The "kon" is the emperor's ceremonial robes.
First: dragon, second: mountain, third: flowering insects, fourth:
fire, fifth: tiger—these are on the upper garment. Sixth:
seaweed, seventh: rice grains, eighth: fu (ax pattern), ninth: futsu (paired pattern),
these are on the lower garment. This is called the nine-symbol
imperial robe.
○"Shou" refers to the upper part being called "i" (garment) and the lower part being called
"shou" (skirt). Regarding the patterns on the skirt—seaweed,
rice grains, fu, and futsu. These are within the nine symbols.
It is the skirt of the emperor's ceremonial robe.
○"Hai" is what officials wear at their waist.
Above are twin horizontal pieces, with each horizontal piece being five
sun long and one sun wide. Below are twin curved pieces.
Each curved piece spans three sun. Hin
