○Ink is made by combining soot with glue.
There are oil soot and pine soot varieties. A person
named Zilu is said to have begun making it.
○Books in ancient times were made by weaving
bamboo strips and carving on them with small knives,
and this was called sho (writing).
Therefore they are also called kan (scroll) or satsu (volume).
○Hyō refers to book covers. It is the
outer paper of books. Hyō is the same. Sen refers to the title label.
○Ga means pictures. Colored ones are
called e (paintings). In China there was Shun, in
Japan there was Sesshū, and now there is the Kanō school
and other masters.
○Chitsu is the outer wrapper for books. Chitsu is
the same. There are also fumaki and bunkō. Also,
books in general are called chitsu.

【Left Page Upper Section】
○Sei is the seal of a ruler. It is
made of jade. Commoners make them
of metal and stone.
○Fans are said to have been created by Shun, or also
by King Wu. In Japan,
Empress Jingū created them during the conquest
of the Three Korean Kingdoms, having seen
bat wings.
○The shaku (foot) originates from millet. Ten grains of millet
make one bu, ten bu make one sun, and ten
sun make one shaku. The shaku is measured
using the human body. By arranging fingers one knows
the shaku, by extending the elbow one knows the hiro.
A hiro is eight shaku.
○Bo is a writing tablet. It is something for writing
and recording matters. Bosho, bohō.
