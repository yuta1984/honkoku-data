There are also kei. The items used to hang kei are called shuankyo.

○Ritsu are musical instruments. There are six yang-ritsu and six yin-ritsu, making twelve ritsu in total. They are also called rokritsu rokryo. They were created by a retainer of the Yellow Emperor.

○Kin (zithers) originally had fifty strings. Later they became twenty-five strings, and now have thirteen strings. In Japan, they began making sounds by arranging heavenly fragrant bows and stringing them.

○Shitsu vary in the number of strings. Large shitsu have fifty strings. Shun created these. Both are musical instruments. Large ones are called shitsu, small ones are called kin.

○Sō were invented by Meng Tian of the Qin dynasty. They are one shaku long with thirteen strings, and the bridges are

three sun high. The eleventh, twelfth, and thirteenth strings are called toikin.

○Ken are made by firing clay. They have six holes and are blown as musical instruments.

○Tō are the same musical instruments as chō. They are also called yōko. They are hand drums.

○Hichiriki are also called kakukan. They are musical instruments. Barbarians blow them to startle horses.

○Gyo are wooden tigers. Notches are carved into the back, and wood is used to scrape them to stop music. They are sasara. They can also be made by splitting bamboo.

○Biwa are three shaku five sun long with four strings.
