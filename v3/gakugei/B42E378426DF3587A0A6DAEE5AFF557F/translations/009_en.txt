In "Kaikoku Zakki": "At a place called Katahira inn:
When shall I come to change my travel clothes in the wind-chilled village of Katahira - Dōoku Jungō"

In "Kamakura Kikō": "From this side of that village, going left is the road that leads to Kanazawa. When I asked the name of that village and heard it was Katahira village:
What would the white earth be like on frosty mornings? I shall come see it in summer, the village of Katahira - Takuan"

Katahira River: Flows at the entrance of Shinmachi station house, south of Shimo-Katahira (the river width is about 15 ken). The plank bridge spanning this flow is called Katahira Bridge. This river originates from the vicinity of Shirane in Tsuzuki District of the same province, reaches this place, and downstream flows through Tobe Village in Kuraki District to meet the sea.

Hodogaya Shinmachi: One of the official stations on the Tōkaidō (combining the areas of upper and lower Katahira-chō, upper and lower Iwama-chō, upper and lower Kamito-chō, etc., to form one station).

(Some say Keichō, others say Keian [Kaibara sets it as the 2nd year of Keian], and still others say the Man'ji period - the theories are not consistent. In the Hōjō family's estate register, there is mention of "Hodogaya of Kozukue" in the holdings of a person called Saburō, so this land also probably belonged to Kozukue in ancient times.)

The distance from Kanagawa to this place is 2 ri and 9 chō. Station inns line up eave to eave, making it a prosperous place.

Kamito River: A small river between Kamito and Kami-Katahira, with a plank bridge about 2 ken long (called Kamito Bridge, but locals call it Naka-no-hashi). The water source is field water that collects and flows, running behind the right side from Shinmachi, reaching this place, and eventually circling behind the left side of Kamito and Iwama before entering Katahira River.

Daijingū: Located in the Kamito area. A torii is erected on the right side of the highway. The shrine is located about 3 chō beyond the main gate. The Okada family serves as chief priest. Festivals are held on the 16th of both June and September, with September being the grand festival. According to tradition, in ancient times the deity of this shrine manifested at Hatagatani Peak in Mikuriya Manor, Musashi Province, but was later moved to places like Kawai, Futamata River, Miyabayashi (same location), and Yasaka, but due to divine oracle, finally on September 16th of the 2nd year of Karoku...
