Nōken Hall (Nōkendō)　Located on the mountain top to the northeast of Shōmyōji Temple in Kanazawa, it is a hermitage of the Zen sect (Sōtō school).
　The principal image of Jizō Bodhisattva is said to be the work of Priest Eshin and measures one sun and eight bu.
　　In later times, a standing statue of Jizō Bodhisattva measuring about two shaku and five sun was made, and the sacred image is said to be
　enshrined within its interior. Therefore, this hermitage is called Jizōin. The present
　temple buildings were built by Lord Kuse Washū Minamoto no Hiroyuki in recent times. The two plaques for Mount Tekihitsu and
　Nōken Hall were both written by Zen Master Shin'etsu. According to tradition, long ago a painter
　named Kose no Kanaoka attempted to capture the true scenery but found his brush inadequate...
