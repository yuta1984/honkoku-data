The water source of what is called the downstream originates from Kamakura, and at its end flows from Sansō Village to Shiohama, where it joins the bay. The bridge that crosses the Seto Highway is called Shijū Bridge. The meaning of its name is as stated in the section on Abura-tsutsumi. Crossing this bridge, the right road leads to the crossroads of the border Jizō between Musashi and Sagami provinces and is the highway to Kamakura. The southbound road is the route to Miura Misaki. The left road along the river leads to Sansō Bay and other places in Sagami Province such as Sakai Bay Village.

Jōkenzan Kōdenji Temple - Same location
Located on the right side from the northern path, alongside the Shijū River. It belongs to the Jōdo sect and is affiliated with Kōmyōji Temple in Kamakura. The wooden statue of the principal deity, Amida Nyorai, is a standing figure about four shaku tall. The creator is unknown. The founding priest is called Tokurensha Nin'yo Reiden Shōnin. Inside the gate on the right side is a Jizō hall. The principal deity, Jizō Bosatsu, is a standing figure about six shaku tall and is said to be the work of Unkei. It is called Chifukuzan Sōkōji.
