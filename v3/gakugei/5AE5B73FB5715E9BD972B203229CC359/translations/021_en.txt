●[40] A Type of Ōbaku Tofu　Combine thin soy sauce with sake and salt and bring to a good boil,
　　　　in a separate pot, bring plenty of oil to a boil. Cut tofu into flat dice
　　　　and place in a metal wire basket, dip in oil and shake two or three times, then immediately
　　　　put into the pot of boiling soy sauce and simmer with good timing. ○According to one theory, squeezing out water well
　　　　to make it like [No. 10] Kaminari Tofu is also called Ōbaku Tofu.

●[41] Seigai Tofu　Simmer scooped kinugoshi tofu in kudzu broth
　　　　with good timing. ○Separately prepare fresh nikkaeshi soy sauce, and use it
　　　　as dashi substitute by adding it to the bowl as sashi-shoyu, then sprinkle roasted aonori seaweed
　　　　that has been ground very finely and sifted.

【Left page】

●[42] Asaji Dengaku　Make with thin soy sauce glaze and grilling, spread with plum miso and
　　　　densely sprinkle with roasted poppy seeds.

●[43] Sea Urchin Dengaku　Dissolve sea urchin in sake to proper consistency and use. Same as regular
　　　　dengaku. ○Sea urchin from Tsushima and Hirado in Hizen province are considered
　　　　the finest quality. That from Aikawa in Echizen province is second best.

●[44] Cloud-topped Tofu　Cut to appropriate size, dust with kansarashi mochi flour,
　　　　steam and top with wasabi miso. ○The preparation of wasabi miso is [No. 82]
