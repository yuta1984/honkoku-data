Myōhōzan Hokkeji Temple is located in Himonya, about half a ri south of Yūtenji Temple. It is called Kisshōin. It belongs to the Tendai sect under Tōeizan. The main hall's principal image is Shakyamuni Buddha, with Manjusri and Samantabhadra as attendant bodhisattvas 《Note: According to local tradition, the current buildings were constructed by a master carpenter from Hida》.
Kannon Hall 《Note: Located to the left in front of the main hall, the principal image is a standing statue of Eleven-faced Kannon, and pilgrims spend the night in vigil in this hall》. Enoki tree 《Note: Located behind Shaka Hall along the left fence, it is an extremely old trunk. Said to date from the temple's founding, a fence has been built around its base》. The two statues of Kongō and Misshaku at the Niō Gate are said to be works of the Buddhist sculptor An'ami

This temple was originally founded by Jikaku Daishi as an ancient Tendai sect temple, but later converted to Nichiren's teachings, with Priest Nichigen as the restoration founder. Eventually in Genroku it returned to its former tradition and again proclaimed the original Tendai sect 《Note: The statue of Nichiren Daishi now enshrined at Myōhōji Temple in Horinouchi is said to have been moved from this temple》. The temple grounds have many cherry and maple trees, creating spectacular views in both spring and autumn.

Himonya Hachiman Shrine is located in the same area, about one chō to the south across cultivated fields. According to tradition, this is the deity revered by Hatakeyama Shigetada 《Note: The sacred body is said to be secret. Some say it is a bronze statue in court dress》.
The bettō (temple administrator) is from the Tendai sect, with Shingūin, a subsidiary of Hokkeji Temple, serving as the shrine 《Note: In the past, among the local farmers, a person of the Miya family served as shrine priest. This Miya family is said to be distant descendants of Shigetada's retainers.
