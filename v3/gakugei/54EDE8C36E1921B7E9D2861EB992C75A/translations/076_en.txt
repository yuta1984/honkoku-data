The statue serves as the principal object of worship. It is said to have been founded by Lord Hōjō Tokiyori, and the temple buildings bear the three-scale crest. 《It is said that the old records were lost due to flood damage around the Genroku era.》

It is called Fushōzan Jugenji Yuishōmyōin Temple. Located on the mid-slope of a hill in Minami-Kase village, it belongs to the Jōdo sect. The 46th abbot, Nen'yo Kakuei, built the current temple buildings and enshrined a seated Kannon statue of jōroku size. According to the inscription on the temple's ridge plaque, it was founded in the first year of Kenmu, Kinoe-Inu (1334), and in ancient times it was called Kaseyama Chiekōin Shinnyoraiji Temple.

The founding priest is called Ryōzan Shōnin. During the time of the 11th abbot Ryōsatsu Shōnin, in the first year of Kanshō, Kanoe-Tatsu (1460), it was destroyed by the fires of war.

《The Azuma Kagami states: "On the 14th day of the 6th month of the 3rd year of Jōkyū, Kanoto-Mi, among those wounded in the Battle of Uji Bridge, Kase Sakon Shōgen and his second son died," etc.》

Kosugi Palace Grounds: The land behind a farmhouse near the great gate of Saimyōji Temple is said to be its former site. Imperial construction took place in the 13th year of Keichō (1608), and it was later demolished in the 3rd year of Manji (1660). It is said to have been a palace established in this area for provincial cultivation.
