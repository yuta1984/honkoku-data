　　　Classifications of Poets

Among the classifications of poets that have been grouped together, there are the Two Sages, the Six Poetic Immortals, the Five Poets of Nashipitsu, and the Five Poetic Immortals of Nashipitsu.
The Two Sages of waka refer to the two poets Hitomaro and Akahito.
The Six Poetic Immortals refer to Sōjō Henjō, Ariwara no Narihira, Bunya no Yasuhide,

Kisen Hōshi, Ono no Komachi, and Ōtomo no Kuronushi—these six people.
The Five Poets of Nashipitsu refers to these five: Ōnakatomi no Yoshinobu, Kiyohara no Motosuke, Minamoto no Shitagō, Ki no Tokibumi, and Sakanoue no Mochiki.
The Five Poetic Immortals of Nashipitsu are all court ladies who served Jōtōmon-in. They refer to these five: Akazome Emon, Izumi Shikibu, Murasaki Shikibu, Uma no Naishi, and Ise no Taifu.

　　　Hyakunin Isshu (One Hundred Poems by One Hundred Poets)

The book called Hyakunin Isshu was created when a person named Kyōgoku-chūnagon Fujiwara no Teika, in his old age, resided at his mountain villa in Ogura. From among ancient and contemporary poems, he selected one hundred poems that pleased his heart, wrote them in the shikishi format, and had them pasted on the sliding doors of that villa. This was transmitted to the world as the shikishi of Ogura Sansō
