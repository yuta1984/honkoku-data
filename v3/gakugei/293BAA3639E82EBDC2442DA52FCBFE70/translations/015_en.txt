Rules of Waka Composition
Among the various rules of waka composition, there are the six principles (rokugi), six forms (rokutai), ten forms (jūtai), seven faults (shichihei), eight faults (hachihei), four schools' methods (shike no shiki), and poetry contest rules (uta-awase no shiki). These are established laws.
The six principles refer to: fū (wind), fu (exposition), hi (comparison), kyō (evocative image), ga (elegance), and shō (hymn).
