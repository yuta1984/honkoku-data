The book called "Wa Rongo" (Japanese Analects) was created by Kiyohara no Yoshinari upon receiving an imperial edict. At the beginning of the volume, it contains various divine oracles, in the next volume it gathers wise edicts from successive generations, next it records the words of many sages, then the words of brave warriors, then the words of wise women. At the end of the volume, it gathers the words of the Shaku-shi (Buddhist teachings). All are words that teach people to be honest and wise.

"Onna Shisho" (Four Books for Women) refers to the collection of four works: "Jokōkyō" (Classic of Filial Piety for Women), "Jo Rongo" (Analects for Women), "Naikun" (Inner Instructions), and "Jokai" (Admonitions for Women).

This collection is called "Onna Shisho" (Four Books for Women). "Jokōkyō" was written by the wife of Chen Miao of the Tang dynasty, Lady Zheng, and was rendered into Japanese in this country. "Jo Rongo" is a book written by a woman named Cao Dagu of the Han dynasty, which was translated and explained in this country. "Naikun" is a book created by the empress of Emperor Taizong of the Ming dynasty. It was translated and written in this country. "Jokai" was also written by Cao Dagu of the Han dynasty and was translated and written in this country. All of these are books that teach and guide women to have righteous hearts and pure conduct.
