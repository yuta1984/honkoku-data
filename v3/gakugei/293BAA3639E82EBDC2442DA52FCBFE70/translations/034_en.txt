　　　Ten Varieties Incense (Jikko)
The practice called "Ten Varieties Incense" - when did it begin? Among friends of the same circle, gathering together to enjoy and play with wonderful fragrances has become a most interesting pursuit. Therefore, even in foreign countries, the practice of enjoying incense can be found mentioned in ancient writings. This can be understood from the existence of books such as "Kōshi" (Incense Records), "Kōroku" (Incense Chronicle), "Kōfu" (Incense Genealogy), and "Meikōfu" (Famous Incense Genealogy).
The excellent books in the way of Ten Varieties Incense include "Kōawase no Shiki" (Rules of Incense Competitions), "Shino Sōshin Hikki" (Notes by Shino Sōshin), "Setsugetsu Kashū" (Collection of Snow, Moon, and Flowers), "Sōon Meikōki" (Sōon's Record of Famous Incenses), "Takebe Takayoshi Kō no Ki" (Takebe Takayoshi's Incense Records), "Tokumi Kō no Ki" (Records of Ten Group Incense), "Kurabuyama," and "Kōdō Hiden Shō" (Secret Teachings of the Way of Incense).
