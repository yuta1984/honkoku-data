**Koishikawa**

This is the general name for the area from Suidōbashi (Water Supply Bridge) to the vicinity of Soto-Hakusan. It is said to have been named thus because in ancient times several small streams with many pebbles (koishi) flowed through the area. [Marginal note: A work called the *Edo Meishō Shi* states that it was a noted place called "Azuma River," but this claim appears to lack sufficient basis.] It is also said to have been named after the Hakusan Shrine of Ishikawa District in Kaga Province, which is enshrined here, though the details remain unclear. [Marginal note: The Hakusan Gongen of Koishikawa was only established as recently as the first year of Genna (1615).]

In the second year of Eiroku (1559), the land register (*shoryō yakuchō*) of the Odawara Hōjō clan records a place name "Koishikawa Honjō" within the holdings of a certain Sakurai, and also notes that a man named Shimazu Magoshirō held land here known as the Hōrin'in Shōgetsu parcel. [Marginal note: According to Kikuoka Senryō, the waterway that flows beneath Negi Bridge and Matahashi Bridge to the northwest of Sugamo, passing south of Koishikawa Palace, behind Dentsu'in Temple, and through Yanagi-chō, then running through the estate of the Lord of Mito (Suifukō), and joining the Kanda River above Suidōbashi — this is said to be the original course of Koishikawa.]

**Kaikoku Zakki** [*Miscellaneous Records of Travels through the Provinces*], "Composed at a place called Koishikawa":

　Thinking ever more deeply of my homeland,
　O Koishikawa — when shall the shallows appear
　that will let me ford this river of longing?
— Dōkō Jugo

**Kōyō Shū** [*Collection of Autumn Leaves*], "Composed at a place called Koishikawa, while visiting Edo":

　How cool is this lodging where I view the moon —
　and cooler still for the waters of Ishikawa flowing nearby.
— Karasumaru Mitsuhiro

　A passing shower — / is it pebbles raining down? / Koishikawa
— Bashō

　A cool breeze — / if I were to linger yet longer, / Koishikawa
— Sōin
