【Right Page】
Imado-yaki (Imado pottery)

In this vicinity there are ceramic artisans and pottery craftsmen, and many households make this their livelihood. It is commonly called Imado-yaki.

From "Sumidagawa Kikō" (Sumida River Travelogue), 7th month, 3rd day, Genroku 2:
Kneading clay and learning to make roof tiles, drying them—
But while not yet fired,
Do you dislike the dew,
O under-tiles?
Sanpū

【Left Page】
Reikisan Keiyōji Temple　Also to the south, located at the north end of Imado Bridge. It is a Sōtō Zen temple, with the founding priest called Meizan Ryōsatsu Oshō. 《Formerly it was originally next to Saikuji Temple in Torigoe, later moved to Honjo, and since the Jōkyō era has been located in this place》The three characters "Reikisan" on the main gate plaque are written by Isai. A Benzaiten shrine is located within the temple grounds. The principal image is said to be a sacred statue that Kōbō Daishi brought from Tang China. 《When this temple was originally in Torigoe, two warriors named Itami Sakyō and Funakawa Uneme fought and killed each other for certain reasons, which is detailed in a text called "Mokuzumonogatari" among the temple's treasures》

Matsuchiyama　Located at the south end of Imado Bridge, also written as "Maichi" or "Shinchi." In the Man'yōshū it is written as "Matauchi." 《The monk Keichu says that writing it as "Matauchiayama" and reading it as "Matsuchiyama" is because it is the inversion of "tau"》

《From the Man'yōshū》
Crossing Matsuchiyama at evening, at Iomasaki on Sumida riverbank, shall I sleep alone? — Benki

《From the Kenpō Meisho Hyakushu》
Tonight too, who will lodge at Iomasaki on Sumida riverbank in the autumn moonlight? — Sutokuin

《Same》
Where the moonlight shines at Iomasaki, crossing the Sumida River from the valley of Matsuchiyama — Ietaka

《From the Shin Senzaishū》
To whom shall I ask for lodging? Crossing Matsuchiyama at evening, there is no one to meet — Sadezane

《From the Akikaze-shō》
Crossing Matsuchiyama at evening, the wind is cold, and plovers cry on Sumida riverbank — Suehiro

《From Kaikoku Zakki: Among the many famous places along the way, at a place called Matsuchiyama》
How can I not place my trust in home? Today I have come to Matsuchi mountain on the eastern road — Dōkō Jun'gō
