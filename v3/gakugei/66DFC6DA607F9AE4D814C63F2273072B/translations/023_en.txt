Gochisan Sojiji Temple　Located in Nishiarai Village. It belongs to the Shingon sect and is called Henshoin. It was founded by Kobo Daishi, and the principal image and the sacred statue of Kobo Daishi are also his work. The spiritual power is remarkable, and on the 21st of every month there is an opening ceremony with very many pilgrims. 《Marginal note: Someone says that the sacred statue of Kobo Daishi at this temple was originally enshrined at Hokuso Mamasan Kobo-ji Temple, but when it converted to the Nichiren sect, this statue was moved to the current temple》

Akai Well《Marginal note: Located to the left of the main hall. This is Kobo Daishi's blessed water. When used for eye washing and medicine, it has efficacy. It is said that the place name Nishiarai comes from this well》

Hachiman Shrine　Located in Rokugatsu Village. The temple administrator is called Enten-ji. According to tradition, when Hachiman Taro Yoshiie was conducting his campaign to suppress the northeast, wild warriors of this province blocked the road. Since it was the blazing heat of June, his allied forces were exhausted and showed no sign of wanting to fight. Yoshiie prayed in his heart to Kamakura Hachiman Shrine, and mysteriously the sun seemed to circle around, casting light on their backs. The enemy wild warriors, facing the sun, were blinded and suffered great defeat. Therefore, he established a Hachiman Shrine in this place. For this reason, the village is called Rokugatsu (June), the temple is called Enten (Blazing Heaven), and it is also called Hanshozan.

Mantokusan Myooin　Called Bairin-ji Temple. Located in Umeda Village. It belongs to the New Shingon sect and enshrines Jizo Bodhisattva as the principal image.
