Heavy things sink. The reason why bath water heats from the bottom when fire is lit below and the water bubbles up from above is that when the water at the bottom of the bath receives heat, that water expands and increases in volume, becomes lighter and floats upward, while cold water from above sinks downward. Also, why does bamboo make noise when it burns? This is the sound of air trapped between bamboo joints expanding and bursting through the bamboo. Also, during cold periods, when boiling water is poured into a cold tea bowl, it sometimes breaks. This too is because heat causes things to

Originally, pottery conducts heat slowly, but when hot liquid is poured into it, the inside of the tea bowl heats up and tries to expand rapidly, while the outside has not yet had time to expand, causing it to break. The reason why thin tea bowls are less likely to break is that the inside and outside receive heat simultaneously
