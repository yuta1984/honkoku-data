○Silverfish are the white fish found in books. They are also called hei. Commonly they are called togyo.

○Fleas develop from soil under floors.

○Lice infest human bodies. They also appear in hair and body hair, with varying forms.

○Among ants, the large ones are called hifu and the small ones are called ki. Ants have the righteousness of ruler and subject relationships. Therefore the character for "righteousness" is written.

[Left page, upper section]
○Among spiders, the jorō spider is called kachirō (flower spider). The long-legged spider is called kishi. In ancient times, Taikō saw spiders and conceived the idea of weaving nets.

○Silkworms are insects that spin silk. They bow down three times and rise three times, aging in twenty-seven days. The Yellow Emperor's principal consort, Lady Seiriō, was the first to raise silkworms and make silk.

○Dung beetles skillfully move dung soil to form circles. Dung insects are the same.
