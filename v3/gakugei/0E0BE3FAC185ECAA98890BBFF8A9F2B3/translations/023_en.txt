○The horsefly (abu) - large ones are called wood horseflies. They make a buzzing sound with their wings that sounds like "buu-buu," hence the name.

○The moth (ga) is formed when silkworms transform into moths. It is a type of lamp moth.

○The thread-waisted wasp is also called scorpion or snail, thin-waisted bee, or cattail rush. It is commonly called jigabachi (potter wasp).

○The stink beetle (heppiri-mushi) is also called kōya (night traveler). Its wings are short and it cannot fly far.

[Left page, upper section]
○The black fly (buyo) lives in fields and meadows, flies before and after rain, and stings human skin. The marks are hard to heal.

○The mosquito (ka) is formed from the transformation of mosquito larvae. The leopard-legged type is the bush mosquito.

○Mosquito larvae (bōfuri-mushi) are born from stagnant, rotting water. They transform into mosquitoes. Also called nail-toppling insects.

○Frog (kawazu) is a general term. Those with mouse-colored bodies and small patterns on their backs are called kawazu. Their cry is beautiful. Water
