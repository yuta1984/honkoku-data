[Right page, upper section]
○Doko (earthworm-like creature) resembles a leech and is yellow in color. Its head is like an ear pick. It is commonly called "ear-pick leech." This insect has great poison.

○Wood lice develop from trees and bamboo. They resemble lice and are round, bluish-gray in color. Wall lice are the same. They are commonly called taniko.

○Flying insects are also called water lice. They develop in soil in wet areas and fly well. They are good to apply to eye sties.

○Water striders are also called water measurers. They swim on water surfaces. Because water is light, they fly. They are about one inch long and have four legs.

[Left page, upper section]
○Newts live in water. Their backs are black and bellies are red, and they have four legs.

○Geckos are also called "palace guards." When this insect is killed and applied to palace women's elbows, it will peel off if they commit adultery with men, but won't peel off if they don't. Hence they are called "palace guards." Wall tigers and scorpion tigers are the same.

○Lizards live in soil. They are poisonous. They are also called stone dragons and mountain dragons.
