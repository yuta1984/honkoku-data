○Oats (karasu-mugi) are sweet and neutral in nature with no toxicity, rescue from hunger, and make the intestines smooth. Also called sparrow wheat (jaku-baku).

○Ears of grain (ho) refer to rice ears. Awns (bō) are the bristle-like appendages, and blighted grain (hi) refers to sterile husks. Upon present consideration, there are three segments.

○Straw (wara) refers to rice straw. Grain stalks (ka-kan), grain chaff (ka-jō), and rice grass (tō-sō) are all the same. Stalk pith (kan-shin) is straw core, and stubble (kai), cut stalks (katsu), and stalk remnants (katsu) are all the same.

○Grain (koku) refers to unhulled rice. Rice, hemp, millet, wheat, and beans are called the five grains. Seeds (shu) are seeds, and husks (fu) are rice hulls.

○Bean stalks (ki) refer to bean stems. 𧯯 (ki) is the same. Cao Zhi of the Wei dynasty composed poetry about this.

○Pods (kyō) refer to bean pods, also called bean horns (zu-kaku). Bean leaves (kaku) are bean foliage that horses eat.

○Steamed buns (manjū) originally used meat filling. Those with adzuki bean paste are called plain buns (so-man). Those without filling are called steamed cakes (jō-hei). Now there are various new preparations, including Tang-style buns and rice cracker buns.

○Cooked rice (han) refers to rice meals, also called "meshi." Hard rice (kō-han) is firm rice, red rice (seki-han) is adzuki bean rice, dried rice (kan-pan) is dried cooked rice, and water rice (sui-han) is rice with hot water poured over it. Supplement: barley rice (baku-han) is barley rice, millet rice (zoku-han) is millet rice.
