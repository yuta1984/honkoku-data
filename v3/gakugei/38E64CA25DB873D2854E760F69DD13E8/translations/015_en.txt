Former Residence Site of Watari Shinzaemon-no-jō Hayakatsu: Located more than half a chō west of the temple gate, on the left side of the road. This land was Watari Shinzaemon's fief during the Genkō era (1331-1334), and he is said to have resided here. Even after Hayakatsu's death, the village people did not forget his past kindness, built a shrine, enshrined Hayakatsu's spirit, and revere it as Goryō Gongen. Nearby stands Hayakatsu's tomb, a stone pagoda about three shaku (approximately 90cm) in height.

Ubaga-mori (Old Woman's Forest): Located about seven to eight chō south of Jōju-in, by the seashore. This is a temporary shrine (tabisho) for Sannō within the moat, extending westward and retaining the form of a horse training ground. 《Marginal note: Local people say this was a horse training ground donated by Yoshisada. The Mitarashi purification pool is within the forest, barely retaining its original form》
