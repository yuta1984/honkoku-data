It is located seven ri southwest of Nikko. The family name is Ishihara. According to tradition, their ancestors were descendants of Myōdōki, who served En no Ozunu (En no Gyōja). They have lived in this place since ancient times, and it is customary for ascetic practitioners of our mountain to go to their house for one night's lodging before entering the mountains for spiritual training. Various folk tales are passed down, but nothing certain is known. In recent years, the family has been divided, and one who calls himself Mondo also lives in the same place.

Toko no Shinji (Floor Ritual): This is a Shinto ritual performed every year from after 6 o'clock on the evening of January 2nd.
It is performed on the same day and night respectively at various sacred sites including the Main Shrine, Shingu, Hongu, Takino-o, Jakko, and Chuzenji. This ritual is said to be a fire hearth festival.

On the night of January 2nd, after the Saitō Goma ritual ends at the Dairakuin Lower Offering Hall, a shakujo (monk's staff) and chūkei (folding fan) are placed on the desk, and holding these, people take turns dancing and singing (there are hymns). When the dance ends and "goban goban" is called out, lay people appear in various forms, some wearing masks, and dance. After finishing, sacred sake is bestowed upon those in attendance, and then copper

bowls filled with zenzai (sweet red bean soup) and rice cakes are served to everyone in the large tea room of Dairakuin. After this ceremony ends, they announce that the service will be changed, and then lay people come out and force rice cakes upon people while dancing.

When this also ends, various miscellaneous items are placed in large wooden bowls - toy papier-mâché figures, vegetables, sweets, and many offering rice cakes, with gold coins also mixed in so they cannot be seen, and these are scattered about. Everyone rushes out at once, competing to pick things up before dispersing. This concludes the sacred ritual, and formal cuisine is served to the attending officials in the study hall.
