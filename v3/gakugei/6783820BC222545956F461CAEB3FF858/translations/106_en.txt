Records of Mount Nikko, Volume Three
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Compiled by Ueda Mōshin

Temple Halls and Monk Quarters within the Sacred Mountain Precinct: One Gakutō (head of learning) of the mountain - one temple; Shuto (monk disciples) - twenty temples; Bessho (branch temples) - four temples; additionally one temple
　Total of twenty-six temples - these are called the Taishū (great assembly) of one mountain; additionally there are eighty monk quarters
Gakutō: Called Shūgaku-in, located in Hotoke-iwa Valley. The front gate of this temple is said to be the gate of an ancient palace. In the fourth year of Genroku, year of the sheep, fifth month, it was placed under imperial custody. Later, during the Kyōhō era, the gate was bestowed upon Shūgaku-in. Since it was a gate that had been relocated from construction by the Muromachi family and built under imperial management, it is craftsmanship rare in the world. The local people call it the "Two-story Imperial Gate."
Twenty Temples of Shuto: Located in Higashiyama Valley, Hotoke-iwa Valley, and Nakayama
　Nanshō-in, Ango-in, Nichizō-in, Yūjō-in, Kyōjō-in, Sakuramoto-in, Zenchi-in
　Yuishin-in, Fujimoto-in, Iō-in, Gokō-in (the above in Higashiyama Valley), Yōgen-in, Kezō-in
