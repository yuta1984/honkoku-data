Revival Period
　Great Master Jigen (personal name: Tenkai)
　　　　In the 14th year of Keichō, 12th month, he was appointed to provisional high priest rank; in the 16th year of the same era, he was transferred from provisional to regular appointment; in the 18th year of the same era,
　　　　he became the head priest of this mountain temple; in the 2nd year of Gen'na, 7th month, he was transferred from regular rank to great high priest; in the 20th year of Kan'ei, on the 2nd day of the 10th month,
　　　　he passed away at Tōeizan, and on the 11th day of the 4th month of the 1st year of Keian, he was posthumously granted the title Great Master Jigen.
Concurrent Position
　Great High Priest Kōkai of Kuon-jū-in, Quasi-Third Imperial Prince rank (former Great High Priest, head of Bishamon-dō temple)
　　　　The eldest grandson of Minister of the Left Sadahiro of the Hanayama-in house, son of Assistant Captain of the Left Tadanaga; later
　　　　by imperial command he became the adopted son of Regent Yukiie of the Kujō house; he received the position in the 20th year of Kan'ei, resigned in the 3rd year of Jōō,
　　　　year of Kinoe-Uma, and passed away on the 16th day of the 10th month of the 8th year of Genroku, year of Kinoto-I.
