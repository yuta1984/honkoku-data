【Right Page】
Tahō Iron Pagoda: Located to the left of the main shrine. It is a one-ken square hall on four sides, with an iron pagoda placed inside. Within the pagoda is enshrined a bronze statue of Fugen Bodhisattva. The doors are finished in black lacquer, and the inscriptions and diagrams are shown next.

Treasures:
One grain of Buddha's relic (placed in the treasure pagoda)　　Six-character sacred name in one scroll by Kōbō Daishi
One large priest's staff - donated by Amida Shōnin of Tsukushi on March 15, Kenkyū 3 (1192)
One scroll of Takio Establishment Record - written by Dōchin Sōzu, disciple of Shōdō Shōnin
One stone sword (in a gold brocade bag)　　Three swords
Hannya mask - donated by Ōshima Tango-no-kami Munehisa in April, Tenshō 12 (1584)
Mask by Jōjun - donated by Kiyohara no Noriharu in Eiroku 3 (1560)
Amida Sutra one volume (120 lines) - imperial calligraphy by Emperor Fushimi
Keijō-yu Chapter one volume (337 lines) - imperial calligraphy by Emperor Go-Fushimi
Fukyō Chapter one volume (138 lines) - imperial calligraphy by Emperor Go-Daigo

Besides these, there are many other treasures, but there is no time to enumerate them all.

Sujikai Bridge: This place marks the boundary of the sacred precincts, and from here begins the downward path. This bridge crosses the official waterway, and since this is the entrance to Takio, defecation, urination, and other impurities are forbidden.
