As spring was drawing to a close, violets and other flowers bloomed heavily with dew, and I thought there might be some tinged with purple connections, which made my sleeves wet with tears again. With great effort, they arrived at Odawara, where the Buddhist service was conducted most solemnly.
　The 19th day was the same as yesterday.
　On the 20th day, as they passed through the shores of Koyorogi, the blue sea stretched far into the distance, the waves hitting the rocks looked like snow, and the clouds swaying over the beach appeared like flowers.
　Even the fisher maidens gathering shellfish on the shore set aside their pearl-curtain worship and bowed to this sacred palanquin. Then they arrived at the palace in Nakahara, where the Buddhist service was conducted even more solemnly. This place is near Rokusho-no-miya, which has been inherited as the provincial shrine of the entire province.
　Could there be any divine light of Tosho Daigongen that does not reach even the snow of Shirane in Koshi from the west and south? (No, it surpasses even that.)

【Left Page】
　On the 21st day, they arrived at the palace in Fuchu, and on the following day, various sacred Buddhist assemblies and precious ceremonies were held at the same location.
　On the 23rd day, they entered into Musashino, where even the mountain edges were unknown. What emerges from the grass is not only the moon. The sun pointing to those peaks also shines gently from the same miscanthus fields, its shadow peacefully filtering through the spring mist—a view beyond description. The wings of geese returning behind their companions were also pitiful, so the High Priest (Tenkai) composed:
　　Unexpectedly, I wet my ink-black sleeves with tears from both the departing and returning geese
　They passed with Horikanē-no-i well to their right. This should float in the "determined knowledge of nearby water and heart." Today they stayed at Senba Great Hall until the 26th of the same month.
　This place was originally developed by the hermit Senpo, restored by Great Master Jigaku, and later revived again by High Priest Sonkai. There are imperial plaques and sacred sites from several generations, and because this is such a sacred place, the topic presented here for debate was "Entering Perfect Enlightenment in One Lifetime." The questions and answers were profound.
