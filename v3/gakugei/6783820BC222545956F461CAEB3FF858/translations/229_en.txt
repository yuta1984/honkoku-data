　The eaves have gilt imperial crests attached, and the ceiling has polychrome carved flying dragons on the rafters, which are painted black. Both the foundation stone edges and girder edges of the stone pillars are wrapped with gilt metalwork. On the back side of the water basin stone is an inscription:
　"Respectfully donated sacred ablution basin stone, Nabeshima Shinano-no-kami Hizen-no-jijū Fujiwara Katsushige, Genna 4th year, 4th month, 17th day"

Bronze Torii Gate - Erected in front of the sacred water house. It has no sacred plaque, but five gilt imperial crests on the kasagi (top crossbeam). Height is approximately two jō.

Sutra Repository - North of the sacred water house. The hall is five and a half ken on four sides, with double roof and copper roofing. In the central rotating repository, the complete Buddhist canon is enshrined, with a wooden statue of Fu Daishi in front and wooden statues of Fusei and Fuken on the left and right. Hōgyō-zukuri style construction. The hall interior has stone flooring with doors on four sides, and the rear section on both left and right has one ken-wide raised floors with tatami.

Lanterns Donated by Various Houses - Total of 118 units. Breakdown: 15 bronze, 2 iron, 101 stone. Year marks and donor names are omitted.
