Record of the Eight Lotus Sutra Lectures　　　　　Reizei Tamekage

　How joyful, on that peaceful beginning month and day,
　It being the 33rd memorial service for Tōshō Daigongen, the realm was shaken and
　resounded with noise, so there was a decree that Eight Lectures should be held
　at Nikkō Shrine, and from the Former Regent on down, all important personages were dispatched. This followed the precedent of memorial services for Tōji-in, the posthumously appointed Left Minister (Ashikaga Yoshimitsu). His Majesty's (Emperor Go-Mizunoo's) imperial composition
　recalled the ancient poems of that Great Counselor of Bishamondō, using frost as warp and dew as
　weft to weave a broad brocade, expressing the imperial heart completely, and the
　skillful oral tradition of this embroidery circulates like an endless jeweled ring.
　This must be like the words that Jakuran wove with swaying hand-jewels, but how

　beautiful they appear! I too, joining their
　ranks like anyone else, having resolved to journey far in Kiso hemp robes, feel as though the
　saba flies of a qilin's tail were galloping a thousand ri. Crossing ships over great seas and building
　suspension bridges over mountains, the path ahead is not difficult to follow. The sound of bells on the correct life-transmitting
　post roads also seems to guard the flowers, and knowing the poignancy of their traces,
　I departed the capital on the twenty-first day of the past third month.
　From then, on the third day of this fourth month, I finally arrived at Ubatama no
　Kurokami Mountain. Along that journey, having passed so many famous places and composed
　Chinese and Japanese poems, the words became tedious so I omit them. This entire mountain was originally
　the site of Mangankongen and was named Mount Fudaraku. Generally, Saint Shōdō climbed
　three times before first establishing a temple. Hence it is called Shingū Shōja.
　Or it is also said that Master Ennin founded it. For details, in Master Shiren's writings
