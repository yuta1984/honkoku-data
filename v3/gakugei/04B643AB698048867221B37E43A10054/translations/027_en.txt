Koyasu Yakushi Nyorai　Enshrined at Shingon-sect Shōkō-in temple, located to the south
　The statue of the principal Buddha, Rurikō Nyorai, is said to be carved by Eshin Sōzu and was the principal Buddha for prayers at the time of Emperor Ichijō's birth

Zuisen-zan Shōun Zen-ji　Located in Hiroo-machi 《According to the Hōjō family's land registry, Okitsu Kaga-no-kami held the Hirao lands within Sakurada, and this place also seems to have belonged to Sakurada》
　This is a Zen temple of the Kyoto Daitoku-ji sect, with Shakyamuni Buddha enshrined as the principal Buddha. The founding priest was Ryūgaku Dai-oshō, and the founding patron was Matsudaira Chikuzen-no-kami Nagamasa 《Shōun is his Buddhist posthumous name》. There are eight sub-temples
　《Long ago it was located at the domain residence in Akasaka, but was moved to the upper part of Azabu Tani-machi, and finally relocated to the present site in Meireki 4》

Bishamonten　About four chō from the same location in the tatsumi direction, enshrined at the Zen temple called Tamon-zan Tengen-ji on the north bank of Shibuya River. The sacred statue of the principal Buddha Bishamonten is carved from a single camphor tree and is said to be the work of Prince Shōtoku 《Its height is three shaku one sun》.
