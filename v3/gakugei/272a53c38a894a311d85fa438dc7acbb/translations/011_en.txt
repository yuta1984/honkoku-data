(Section 16, continued)

The friend, upon seeing this, was deeply moved, and sent bedding along with a poem:

*Toshi dani mo / tō to te yottsu wa / heni keru wo / ikutabi kimi wo / tanomi ki nuran*
(Even as forty years have passed with you counting ten upon ten — how many times over those years have I depended upon you?)

In reply to this, (Aritsune wrote):

*Kore ya kono / ama no hagoromo / mube shi koso / kimi ga mikeshi to / tatematsuri kere*
(Can this be the celestial robe of the heavens? Indeed, how fitting it is that this should be offered to you as your own garment.)

Unable to contain his joy, the friend composed another poem:

*Aki ya kuru / tsuyu ya magau to / omou made / aru wa namida no / furu ni zo ari keru*
(I wonder if autumn has arrived, or if this might be mistaken for dew — but what falls is, in truth, nothing other than my own tears.)

【Section 17】

A person who had not been in contact for many years came to view the cherry blossoms at their peak. The host composed:

*Ada nari to / na ni koso tatere / sakurabana / toshi ni mare naru / hito mo machi keri*
(Cherry blossoms, known by reputation for their fickleness and scattering — yet even they have been waiting for a person as rare as you, who comes but once in many a year.)

(In reply:)

*Kyō kozuba / asu wa yuki to zo / furi namashi / kienu wa are domo / hana to mimashi ya*
(Had you not come today, by tomorrow the blossoms would have fallen like snow. Even if they had not yet scattered, could one truly call them flowers in their glory?)
