Sentai Kōjin Hall (Thousand-Body Fierce Deity Hall) - Located about half a chō south of the same place, on the right side of the same coastal road, in a Zen temple called Kaiun-ji. The sacred statue of the principal deity Kōjin is a genuine work of Viśvakarma Deva. Long ago it was located in a place called Amakusa (with the reading note: Kōjin-no-hara) in Higo Province, Kyūshū, but when the Christian rebels destroyed the shrine during the Christian uprising, it was transferred to this temple for various reasons. People constantly visit for worship believing in its miraculous powers. The 28th day of each month is designated as the festival day. The main festivals are on the 28th day of both the third and eleventh months.

Fudasan Kaian-ji Temple - Located about one chō south of the same place, on the right side of the coastal road. It is a Zen temple of the Sōtō sect belonging to Kōun-ji in Mita. It claims Hōjō Sagami-no-kami Taira-no-Tokiyori as its founder and Daikaku Zenji as its founding abbot, with Kosan Oshō as the second generation. Tentsō Keison Oshō restored this temple in the first year of Keichō (1596) and is considered the restorer.
