《Although the meaning of this poem is difficult to understand, since it has been passed down from ancient times, we cannot omit recording it here, and for now we merely record and add it》

The founding master Zen'e Shōnin 《initially called Gedatsu》, whose Buddhist name was Shōkū, was of Minamoto clan by secular family name, a descendant of Emperor Murakami (Tenryaku Seishu), and son of Chikasuē, the provincial governor of Kaga Province. 《The name Shōkū was formed by taking characters from his father Chikasuē's Buddhist name Shōken and his master Genkū's title》 He was born on the ninth day of the eleventh month of Jishō 1, year of the Metal Rooster (1177). Minister of the Left Kuga no Michichika adopted and raised him as his son. From his youth he dwelt in the bodhi-mind, so he went to Master Yoshimizu 《this refers to Genkū》. At fourteen he took the tonsure and took the name Zen'e. His nature was brilliant, and hearing one thing he would understand a thousand. In the spring of Kenkyū 9 (1198), when Master Genkū composed the Senchakushū at the request of Lord Tsukino-wa, Zen'e also participated in examining and determining the textual meaning. Once the lord said, "After the master's death, if there are doubts about this book, who shall resolve them?" The master replied, "The profound meaning of the Pure Land and the essential teachings of this book are all entrusted to Zen'e. He is no different from me." Thereupon the lord's veneration of Zen'e became extremely deep. The master established temples in both the capital and provinces, twelve locations in total, and also drew Pure Land mandalas to be housed in various places, opened printing blocks for Buddhist scriptures, and provided benefit for future scholars. Finally, on the twenty-sixth day of the eleventh month of Hōji 1, year of the Fire Sheep (1247), he passed away at Yuikōin in Shirakawa. He was seventy-one years old.

【Left Page】
Because he resided at Zenshin-ji in Nishiyama and vigorously propagated the religious teachings, he is respectfully called Nishiyama Shōnin. 《He is called the great founder of the Seizan branch of the Jōdo sect. His life's conduct is detailed in the master's biography》

This temple in ancient times was a great temple complex, called the Kōyasan of the Kantō region, where people erected stone memorial towers for deceased ancestors and for merit-making practices, and many pilgrims visited. Perhaps for this reason, old stone monuments and stone Buddha statues still exist here and there. 《Six or seven chō east of the temple's main gate is a place called Gomadō Estate》《It is said that when impure, there would be curses, so no one cultivates the fields, and it has become overgrown》

Kōmyō-ji Pond: Adjacent to the south of Kōmyō-ji. It is said this was formerly the course of the Yaguchi River. Now the water flow has changed and flows closer to the south. The pond's length from east to west is over two hundred ken, and its width from north to south is about fifty ken. 《Village elders tell that when Zen Master Kishu was the head priest of this temple, he caught the carp from this pond, wrote the sacred name in red ink on their heads》《and released them back to their original place. Their descendants remain, and even now they occasionally surface. On the twenty-fifth day of the first month, during the Gyoki Nenbutsu-e ceremony, many of these fish are said to surface on the water》

Nitta Daimyōjin Shrine: Five chō south of Kōmyō-ji, located in Yaguchi village. The temple administrator follows the old Shingon sect and is called Shinpuku-ji. It belongs to Hōtō-in in Takahata. The deity enshrined is the spirit of Nitta Saheē-no-suke Yoshioki Ason. The tenth day is the monthly festival day. Only the worship hall is maintained.
