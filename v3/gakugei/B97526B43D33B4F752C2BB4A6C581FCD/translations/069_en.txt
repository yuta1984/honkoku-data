The enshrinement of this deity dates back to very ancient times. This land was formerly a highway to the northern provinces (Ōshū), and it is said to be the historic site where Prince Yamato Takeru conducted archery practice during his campaign to subdue the eastern barbarians. 《Note: The place name "Yaguchi" (Arrow Mouth) is also said to derive from this event.》

《Note: Across the Rokugō River in the land of Inage, there is a village name called Yamuke (Arrow Direction), which is also said to be named after the place where arrows were directed at that time.》

Furukawa Yakushi Nyorai Hall - Located in Furukawa Village, about twenty chō southeast of Nitta Myōjin Shrine. It is called Mount Iō, Seson-in 《Note: Sometimes written as Tōkō-in》 Anyōji Temple. It belongs to the Shingon sect of the New Doctrine and is affiliated with Hōtō-in in Takahata Village. In ancient times it was called Tōkōbō. The three large characters "Mount Iō" on the plaque of the main hall are written by Ōbaku Kōsen.

The main hall houses the principal image of Yakushi Nyorai 《Note: five shaku five sun tall》, with Amida and Shakyamuni Buddha on the left and right, each five shaku three sun tall. The images of the twelve divine generals and four heavenly kings on the side altars are all works by the bodhisattva Gyōki. 《Note: From a ginkgo tree that existed at this temple in ancient times》《Note: It manifested auspicious light, so that tree was used as material for the statues.》

Ginkgo Trees 《Note: Two trees stand side by side in front of the main hall. Many people whose wives have no milk come to pray beneath these sacred trees, believing in their efficacy. The hanging breast-like formations extend about four to five shaku, or sometimes over three shaku.》

Sugimoto Sacred Spring 《Note: Located under a cedar tree to the right in front of the main hall. Those suffering from eye diseases who wash with this sacred water find it efficacious.》

Gochi Hall - Also contains images of the Ten Kings and Aizen Myōō. It stands alongside the main hall to the right.
