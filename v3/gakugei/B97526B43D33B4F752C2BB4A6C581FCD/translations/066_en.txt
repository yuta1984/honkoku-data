To Lord Hata Hizen-no-kami

《The rest, including weapons, ancient pottery, and temple treasures, are all omitted here》

According to the Taiheiki, Nitta Sahyōe-no-suke Yoshioki was 《a son of Yoshisada's concubine who lived in Kōzuke Province. When Kitabatake Akiie, the provincial governor of Ōshū, advanced to attack Kamakura, warriors from Musashi and Kōzuke who supported the Nitta family made this Yoshioki their general. With over 30,000 horsemen, they attacked and captured Kamakura together, then went to Yoshino. The former emperor (Emperor Go-Daigo) was greatly pleased and said he was a worthy vessel to restore Yoshisada's house. The boy, who was then called by the childhood name Tokushū-maru, was given his coming-of-age ceremony and named Nitta Sahyōe-no-suke Yoshioki. His abilities surpassed others, and his wisdom and strategy excelled among many. In the seventh year of Shōhei, at the Battle of Musashino and the Kamakura campaign, he defeated great enemies, and his prowess against ten thousand soldiers was unparalleled in all ages》. After his father, the Left Middle Captain (Yoshisada), died in battle, he was in Echigo Province. However, those in Musashi and Shimotsuke provinces who supported the Nitta family secretly maintained communication, so their power gradually began to emerge between the two provinces. When this became known in Kamakura, the Kanrei Ashikaga Sama-no-kami Motouji and Hatakeyama Tayū Nyūdō Dōsei were greatly alarmed. They searched for Yoshioki's whereabouts and repeatedly sent forces against him, but Yoshioki thought nothing of them and defeated them all. His transformations were endless - truly beyond human capability.

Therefore, unable to handle this situation, Dōsei secretly plotted with Takezawa Ukyō-no-suke and sent Takezawa to serve under Yoshioki. From then on, he used various poisonous schemes to try to kill Yoshioki, but the time had not yet come. 《Takezawa Ukyō-no-suke had previously belonged to Yoshioki's forces during the Battle of Musashino and had shown loyalty, but after later joining the Kamakura side, he forgot old friendships and engaged in such treacherous behavior》.

Finally, using a beautiful woman to soften his heart and becoming trusted as his most loyal ally, Takezawa falsely devised a plot to destroy Kamakura and greatly enticed Yoshioki. Yoshioki followed his suggestion and, on the dawn of October 10th in the third year of Enbun, departed secretly with only thirteen master and retainers. When they reached Yaguchi Ferry and boarded the boat, Takezawa had prepared his scheme beforehand. The ferryman pretended to drop the oars and rudder, saying he would retrieve them, and falsely entered the water. He then removed the wooden plugs that had been blocking two holes previously bored in the boat's bottom. As river water poured in and the boat began to sink, Edo Tōtōmi-no-kami's ambush forces on the opposite bank arose along the riverbank and raised war cries.

At this point, Yoshioki first realized the plot and, greatly enraged, cut open his own belly and perished. I-no-Danjo also followed by committing suicide, and the others - Serata Uma-no-suke, Ōshima Suwō-no-kami, Yura Hyōgo-no-suke, and Shin Saemon-no-jō - grappled with enemies and killed each other, or mutually struck off each other's heads.
