[This temple] was made into a place of dharma propagation by Saint Shinran. The fifteenth abbot of this temple is called Kūzen. He was the fourteenth generation descendant of the lay monk Haga Zenka, the eldest son of Haga Hōki-no-kami Junior Fifth Rank Upper Grade Kiyohara no Mahito Motonori. His secular name was Musashi Gorō Saikō, and his childhood name was Hakuōmaru. The reason this temple is now called Saikō-ji is probably because it was named after this Saikō. Among the temple treasures is what is called Takeda Shingen's campaign vest. In the garden there is an old tree called the Daigo Cherry, which has single-petaled flowers that begin blooming around the seventieth day after the beginning of spring. There are several other old single-petaled cherry trees, and when in full bloom they present a magnificent sight. This place is the foremost cherry blossom viewing spot in the area.

Taisei-zan Kōfuku-ji Temple　Located about one chō southwest of Saikō-ji, this temple also practices the dharma propagation of the Lotus Master, and its principal deity, the statue of Amida Nyorai, is said to be the work of Prince Shōtoku. This place is the old site where Saint Ryōkai, who restored Azabu-san Zenpuku-ji temple, was born (details are given in the third volume under Azabu Zenpuku-ji). This temple also has old cherry trees that present a magnificent sight in spring.
