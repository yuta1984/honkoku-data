Fukudensan Renge-ji Temple: Located in Renshō village. 《Marginal note: This area belongs to Rokugō. Around Eiroku 2 (1559), Kishita Shinzaburō held dominion, as seen in the Hōjō family's land registry》This is an ancient temple of the Shingon sect. Ehara Hyōbu Ariharu, a local lord of Ehara District, became a monk, took the name Renshōbō, and founded this temple. The statue of the Eleven-faced Kannon Bodhisattva is the work of Great Master Gyōki. In ancient times it was a magnificent large temple, but the local lord Namekata Danshōnochu, who revered Nichiren's teachings, destroyed temples of other sects.

Onna-zuka (Women's Mound): Located on the land of a farmer named Tazaemon in Onna-zuka village. According to tradition, long ago Takezawa Ukyōnosuke, in order to harm Nitta Yoshioki, summoned from the capital a high-ranking court lady called Shōshō-dono from the imperial side, a beautiful woman of about sixteen or seventeen years, and presented her to Yoshioki. Later, on the night of September 13th, when he plotted to invite Yoshioki to his residence, this woman stopped him, saying there were bad omens. Therefore Takezawa, angry that his plan could not succeed, ordered his retainers to take this woman to this place and kill her. So the local people, taking pity, hid her corpse and built a mound. However, since her name was unknown, it has been called only "Onna-zuka" (Women's Mound).
