By Manji 2, the 20,000 koku of Nomi District were exchanged for 16,800 koku in Niikawa District. Considering this, the 100,000 koku of Nei and Niikawa districts were not yet fixed at this time. The vermillion seal certificate for 100,000 koku in the two districts was granted by the fourth shogun Ietsuna in Kanbun 4. The "Hankanfu" record stating it was Shōhō 2 is incorrect.

1. "Honpō Joji-kō" states: In the sixth month of Kan'ei 16, when Lord Toshinao received 100,000 koku and Lord Toshiharu received his divided grant, the locations of both lords' domains were not yet determined and were scattered in various places. They were consolidated as they are today from Manji 3 onward. It also states that the fifth lord Matsun-kō received the retirement domain of Chōmyō-kō in the intercalary twelfth month of Manji 1. In the same third year, both lords of Toyama and Daishōji conducted village exchanges and land transfers. "Toyama Old Records" records this as Manji 2.

1. Regarding the territorial exchange between Toyama and Daishōji domains as per the above conditions, there was a petition to the shogunate requesting vermillion seal certificates, and also that the Ōmi Province domains of Imazu and Hirokawa be included in the vermillion seal certificates.
