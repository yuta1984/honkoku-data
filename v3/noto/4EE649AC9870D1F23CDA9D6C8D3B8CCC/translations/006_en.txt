　Upon examination, Akaza Kobōshi later took the name Akaza Bingo Yoshiie. He initially resided in Imajō in Echizen Province, and after the Battle of Sekigahara, served the Maeda family. His son Tosa Takaharu took the name Nagahara, and his descendants returned to the Akaza surname.
　The above two documents date from the time of entry into the fief domain. Therefore, they are recorded here.

As recorded in Murai Nagaaki's personal memoir "Jinzen-roku," it is recorded as follows:
1. In Echizen Province, when Shibata Shuri-dono received his grant, and the three men - His Excellency the Dainagon, Sasa Kuranosuke-dono, and Fuwa Kawachi-dono - held the 100,000 koku of Fuchu District, Toshiie-sama came out together with Fuwa Kawachi-dono and hosted a feast for one day and night.
　Upon examination, Fuchu in Echizen Province is the ancient site of the provincial government office, being what the ancient saibara songs refer to as "the provincial office (kōfu) of Takefu." Therefore, at the time of the Meiji Restoration, it was renamed Takefu Town. According to the Echizen-shi and Echizen Meisai-en, the villages of the 100,000 koku of Fuchu are now located within Imadate and Nanjō Districts.
