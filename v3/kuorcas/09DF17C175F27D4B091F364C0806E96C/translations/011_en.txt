This verse is a country folk song for the time of the wheat harvest. Now the story changes: at Yoichibei's ramshackle hut, Kanpei has now drifted here and taken shelter, temporarily living together with him. Kanpei's wife, the beautiful Katsuko, rose early this day, wanting to comb her disheveled hair from sleep, and opened her comb box. Thinking of how her husband has not yet returned home even once, her many emotions are like tangled hair, difficult to untangle, and to whom can she tell the sorrowful suffering in her heart? Taking her comb to brush it, sweeping away the oil and ash, neatly and orderly, she arranged her hair into a three-part coiffure. Looking at her, one could pity such beautiful features
