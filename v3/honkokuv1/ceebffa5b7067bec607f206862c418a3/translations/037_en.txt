One account written by someone states that on the night of the second day of the tenth month of Ansei 2 (Year of the Wood Rabbit, 1855), after the hour of the Rat (around 10 PM), the sky suddenly began to shine brilliantly, flashing like lightning. It appeared as if electric light was bursting forth from the edges of clouds, but immediately afterward a great earthquake occurred, reaching across thirty ri in just two or three breaths. Considering all these accounts together, it is factual that the sky emitted light before the earthquake that night. However, since this occurred after 10 PM, few people witnessed it, and moreover, no one spoke of it due to the confusion of the earthquake. According to the earlier account by the fishermen of Uraga, the shape was indescribable. What kind of mysterious fire could it have been? I should continue to inquire with eyewitnesses and record their accounts.

Another previous record states that the death toll from this disaster reached approximately 120,000 people, but in my estimation it likely did not reach even half of over 100,000, perhaps only about one-third. If there truly had been deaths numbering over 100,000, commodity prices would not have risen so high after the earthquake. According to this theory, the temple surveys I recorded earlier claiming over 190,000 deaths must be completely unfounded. I should pursue the truth and record it properly.

However, among the casualties from crushing in this disaster, there were not few illegitimate children and daughters of various daimyo families who were crushed to death, and there were many among the hatamoto as well. Yet all of these were officially reported as deaths from illness, and the deaths were generally kept hidden. Even among the cases I know of families of five or six people with only one or two survivors, there are not few, so the number of deaths from crushing must certainly be great. However, the exact number cannot be determined. Nevertheless, Edo's population must have decreased by one-third to one-quarter.
