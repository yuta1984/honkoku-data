There were numerous lodgers, and at night two or three people would wrap themselves in a single futon, barely managing to ward off the cold. Despite proclamations from the prefectural government, there were those who took advantage of this disaster for various schemes. Prices soared dramatically: white rice rose from 10 to 13 sen per sho, beef (which arrived from Kyoto for the first time that day) cost 30 sen per kin, chicken cost 30 to 35 sen, and eggs cost over 2 sen each. Lamp oil, candles, and similar items also increased in price accordingly, with vegetables being particularly expensive. (These were the market prices from the day of the earthquake disaster until around November 2nd or 3rd; prices gradually declined afterward but still had not returned to normal levels.)
