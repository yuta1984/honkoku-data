◎Report from the Meteorological Observatory regarding the Great Earthquake
The Central Meteorological Observatory's report on this great earthquake is as follows. However, the statement in the text that this earthquake was caused by the collapse of Neo Village, which then propagated seismic waves in all directions, is difficult to believe. This is because this earthquake had no epicenter, and it was not like throwing a stone into a pond where the ripples create concentric circles spreading in all directions. Please refer to the causes of earthquakes described at the beginning of this volume.

This earthquake appears to have been caused by the collapse of land with a diameter of over one ri in Fujitani within Ōaza Nōgō, Neo Village, Ōno District, in the northwestern part of Mino Province, Gifu Prefecture, which then propagated seismic waves in all directions. The seismic area extended southwest to cover all of Chūgoku and Kyushu, and northeast to reach as far as the Mutsu region, covering a vast area with a total surface area of 15,750 square ri (which is slightly more than six-tenths when compared to the total area of Japan at 24,794 square ri).
