◎ Report from the Meteorological Observatory Regarding the Great Earthquake

The report from the Central Meteorological Observatory regarding the recent great earthquake is as follows. However, the claim within the text that "the earthquake occurred as a result of the land subsidence at Neo Village, with the resulting seismic waves propagating outward in all directions" is difficult to accept. This is because the present earthquake did not have a single epicenter in the manner of ripples spreading outward in concentric circles when a stone is thrown into a pond. The reader is also directed to consult the discussion of the causes of earthquakes presented at the beginning of this volume.

The present earthquake appears to have been caused by a subsidence of land measuring more than one ri in diameter at Fujitani, within the village of Nōgō in Nishinone Village, Ōno District, in the northwestern part of Mino Province, Gifu Prefecture, with seismic waves propagating outward in all directions as a result. The affected zone extended as far southwest as the Chūgoku region and the entirety of Kyushu, and as far northeast as the distant Mutsu region, making it an extremely vast area. Its total area amounted to 15,750 square ri (compared to the total area of Japan of 24,794 square ri, this represents more than six-tenths of the country). The intensity of the tremors, classified by degree, is as follows:

In the above seven provinces, land subsidence, collapse of mountain cliffs, cracking of the ground, eruption of spring water, damage to sturdy buildings, and deaths and injuries of people and livestock occurred, making these the most devastated areas.
