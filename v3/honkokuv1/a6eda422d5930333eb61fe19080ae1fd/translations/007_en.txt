# Earthquakes in Tosa, Past and Present
**Lecture by Teraishi Masaji, Tosa Historical Society**

## Part One: The Earthquake of Hakuhō Year 13

### (A) General Overview

That a great earthquake struck Tosa Province in the thirteenth year of the Hakuhō era during the reign of Emperor Tenmu is a fact clearly recorded in the *Nihon Shoki* (Chronicles of Japan), which may be called the oldest official history of our country, and is widely known to the public. Since the circumstances of that earthquake offer many points of reference for the study of later earthquakes, I shall first attempt a detailed description.

### (B) Meteorological Conditions Prior to the Great Earthquake

In general, the histories of ancient times did not neglect to record the occurrence of earthquakes, thunder, solar eclipses, comets, and other such phenomena, owing to certain superstitious associations attributed to them. It is, one might say, a fortunate coincidence that later scholars have been able to derive valuable materials for astronomy and earth sciences from these very records. It is particularly noteworthy that in the *Nihon Shoki*, records of such phenomena are interwoven throughout with accounts of human affairs, filling the annals entirely. Upon reflection, according to what the same work records, the Hakuhō era was truly a year of widespread disaster from earthquakes across the entire country, and the great earthquake in Tosa Province in the thirteenth year was by no means a sudden, isolated event. Some members of the public have doubted the text of the *Nihon Shoki*, dismissing certain passages as errors made by the ancient writers.
