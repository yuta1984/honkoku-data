...all of which was felt across more than thirty provinces of the Nankai and Tokai regions. The Tosa earthquake of this time was most clearly recorded as the most violent in terms of seismic force among preceding and subsequent earthquakes. Although the number of deaths was comparatively small—perhaps because the population was not as dense as today—the amount of farmland and buildings swept away by the tsunami was unexpectedly large, and the overall damage in statistical terms was likely among the top one or two in all the provinces.

**(B) Unusual Signs Before and After the Great Earthquake**

The Hōei era, like the Hakuhō era, was a year of recurring natural disasters and calamities, and the records of the time all convey this directly. However, since the events of this era are already matters of recent history, well known to those with discerning minds, I shall avoid the trouble of citing each record one by one as I did when writing about the Hakuhō earthquake, and will not compile meteorological observations from across the country before the earthquake for reference purposes.

Therefore, in this chapter, I shall only discuss the unusual signs before and after the great earthquake in a narrow sense—that is, the locally observed precursory and subsequent signs in the single province of Tosa regarding the great earthquake of that time.

**① Meteorological Phenomena**

(A) The records of the Inamo family state: The year of the great earthquake had a remarkably warm winter; until the 12th day of the 11th month, people could get by with only a single lined garment or a single unlined garment, and even after the new year the cold did not come. The aforementioned ash (while aftershocks continued for about two months after the great earthquake, Mt. Fuji erupted and produced what is known as Mt. Hōei; this refers to the ash from Mt. Hōei, of which an account appears in the earlier section of the same document) began to fall on the 26th day of the 12th month and continued falling until the 3rd day of the 12th month [Note: likely an error in the original], accumulating to about five or six sun (approximately 15–18 cm) on flat ground.

(B) Also stated: From the 9th through the 11th month of Hōei 4, there was a long drought; on the night of the 8th day of the 12th month, heavy snow fell and accumulated to about two shaku (approximately 60 cm). It fell for three days, accompanied by extraordinary cold. Aftershocks from the earthquake continued little by little for about three years.

**② Underground Rumbling**

(C) Also stated: Tremors did not cease until the 2nd month of the following year; while not as strong as at first, they sounded like the firing of large cannons. They occurred five or six times daily, and the same at night. Every time the ground rumbled, an earthquake followed.

(D) Also stated: Until the 10th month of the following year (Hōei 5), earthquakes occurred from time to time; rumbling never ceased, but earthquakes no longer occurred as frequently as before.

**③ Drying Up of Springs and Wells**

(E) Also stated: Since the great earthquake, wells and springs dried up completely, causing great hardship to the people; but from around the middle of the 2nd month of the following year, heavy rains fell, water returned, and the people rejoiced.

Now, the aftershocks following the great earthquake continued for a further three years, and minor tremors are said to have continued for several more years beyond that. Below I record the strong earthquakes felt in this province (Tosa) before and after the main event, based on the Miyaji Family Diary and the *Akikure Dokuken-shū*:

- (1) Hōei 4, 9th month, 3rd day, Hour of the Snake (approx. 9–11 a.m.): Earthquake
- (2) Same year, 10th month, 4th day, Hour of the Goat (approx. 1–3 p.m.): Great Earthquake *(this is the main earthquake that is the focus of this book)*
- (3) Same year, 11th month, 16th day, middle of the Hour of the Rooster (approx. 6 p.m.): Earthquake *(the strongest aftershock following the great earthquake of the 4th day of the 10th month)*
- (4) Same year, 5th year, 1st month, 1st day: Multiple earthquakes
- (5) Same year, 6th year, 3rd month, 11th day, Hour of the Hare (approx. 5–7 a.m.): Somewhat large earthquake
