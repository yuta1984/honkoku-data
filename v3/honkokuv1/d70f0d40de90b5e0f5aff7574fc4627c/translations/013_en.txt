Looking around, there were only human footprints and horse hoofprints. Wondering if she had been taken away, he first took the infant out from the silk wrapper and looked, finding that the mirror had been broken with a poem written on the back. This must be a vow that if this princess were somehow raised to adulthood, they would meet once again. She is of noble lineage - how could he abandon her? Though he was a man, he pressed the infant, still wrapped in the silk cloth, into his bosom and followed the tracks of people and horses, pursuing their trail westward up the mountain, which seemed reassuring. Eventually traveling on, he arrived at

a place called Ashigara in Sagami Province. Here lived a person called Shiman Chōja (the Forty-Thousand Wealthy Man), who owned forty thousand chō of rice fields, which is why people called him Shiman Chōja. When the retainer stopped at the wealthy man's front gate, the wealthy man saw this and thought, "What an unusual traveler this is." He called for his wife (kita no kata), and when he told her about this situation, the couple approached the retainer and asked what circumstances had brought him there. "This is my lord's child.
