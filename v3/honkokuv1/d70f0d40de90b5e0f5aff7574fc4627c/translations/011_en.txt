Thus, the one who came out was a kind-hearted and good retainer. Soon catching up, he said, "My lady, if I accompany you, your heart should be at ease." Finally traveling on, they arrived at Kawagoe in Musashi Province. Pitifully, the princess said to Yukiharu, "I feel unwell. What will become of my condition?" No sooner had she spoken than she showed signs of going into labor. Oh, how sad! In such a place at the edge of the wilderness, in the mountains where no one lives, what is this situation? Things will surely become very difficult. How wretched this is, she said, and rested for a while in the shade of trees. Yukiharu said,

"I understand. Even if it be at the edge of the wilderness or deep in the mountains, I will first protect your life." The princess was pleased and said, "Then bring me water." The retainer acknowledged this and went out to seek water. Oh, how mysterious! Perhaps the Buddha, gods, and Three Treasures took pity on her? The provincial lord Genkan-kō, having been appointed as lord of Suruga Province, was first going up to the capital, but decided to take a look at Musashi. Passing through this place, he saw the princess and thought, "How strange. In such a desolate plain, how could such a woman be living alone? Could she be some kind of supernatural being? Well, whatever the case, I have come from the capital..."
