The Lady of the North (Kita no Kata) also wished to pray for the soul of the magistrate, and had ninety-nine golden Buddha statues made. Then she went to Kiyomi Barrier, visited the site of Shaken Chōja's residence, held memorial services for the magistrate's body, erected 88,000 stupas, and made offerings of 60,384 golden Buddha statues, accumulating good deeds beyond description. Originally, through Amida's original vow, their attainment of Buddhahood was truly blessed. With the vow that the entire clan and retainers would become gods to protect sentient beings, there is a festival on March 3rd for Lord Dayū. This is called the March Assembly. The lord became the Great Deity of the Main Shrine, and the Lady of the North manifested as Asama Daibosatsu. This is said to be connected to the legend of Princess Konohana-sakuya-hime. The princess became the deity of Yamagu Shrine, and there are festivals every year on the first Day of the Monkey in April, September 15th, and the first Day of the Monkey in November—a total of forty-five festivals per year. The Yoman Chōja couple, who had raised the princess until she was thirteen, also ascended the mountain, planted thousand-year pines at Miho Bay, and became the Five Great Deities. Also, the Goman Chōja couple from Shimotsuke ascended as well, and the parent-child relationship was revealed.
