And immediately, [the deity] appeared as the Lower Shrine in the field of Fuji. According to the words of Asama Daibodhisattva, "When I departed from Shimotsuke Province, I placed a thousand-tan saddle on a dapple-gray horse named Sentan, so until the end of time, dapple-gray horses should be kept as sacred horses." Now, the eight retainers who served the provincial lord became the Eight Princes, and the thirteen ladies-in-waiting each became deities in their own way. In this manner, having become deities, they left their traces in Suruga Province through the divine connection with Princess Sakuya, who from her early childhood devoted herself to Buddhism,

and gained merit through reciting the Lotus Sutra. Among all these, the evidence of the lord's loyal service remains an unprecedented matter even now and for future generations. Therefore, those who can read this divine origin story themselves should read it three times each month. Those who cannot read should have others read it for them and listen at least once a month. Those who listen once will have the understanding of seven rebirths. Never doubt this. If anyone harbors doubt, their wishes will not be fulfilled in this life, and in the future they will fall into the evil realms.
