They put a saddle called "sentan" on the horse and sent it to the princess, saying, "From today on, do not think you have parents. Mount this horse and go wherever you will. If I hear you are in this country, I will take your life," he said angrily. As for the mother, since this concerned her only daughter, no matter how reluctant she felt in her heart and how she felt like fainting away, the father's anger was so strong that without even giving proper instruction, if the princess were to go to an unknown country and unfamiliar village, their bond in this world would end here. Through the gaps in their tears, trying to look at each other one last time, she gave her an eight-shaku sash, a five-shaku wig, a Chinese mirror,

incense shells, seven fans with gold ribs and silver ground, twelve hand-boxes made of gold, twelve layers of small sleeves, and other treasures. The princess, in her overwhelming sadness, cried without restraint, saying, "Until now, I had relied on mother as my support, but even that was in vain - how sad it is to receive these keepsakes!" The ladies-in-waiting also wept together, saying "Is this the end? How heartless!" and fell down sobbing. From the father's quarters came a messenger saying, "It would be bad if you delay.
