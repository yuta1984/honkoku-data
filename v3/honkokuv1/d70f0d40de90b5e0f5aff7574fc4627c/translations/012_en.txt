"I traveled all this way east to find such a woman. How could I abandon her?" Immediately, he had her board a palanquin and they departed toward Suruga - truly a wondrous turn of events.

At that time, the princess spoke: "Ah, noble traveler, though I am ashamed to say it, I have just given birth. I shall explain the circumstances later. Should anyone come searching for me at this place, I will leave behind a keepsake. Thus I shall obey your words." Taking up the infant, she said, "Ah, my precious child. Though you are abandoned with little fortune, if you live long, we may meet again," like morning dew. "I leave this as a memento," and she took out a Chinese mirror given to her by her mother, broke it in two, and wrote a poem on the back:

　　Together with your father and ancestors,
　　　May you surpass others as a woman of this land

The princess wrapped it in silk cloth, placed it upon pampas grass, and tearfully departed. Taking the keepsake with them, she ascended to Suruga with the provincial lord. Meanwhile, the retainer finally found water and returned to the original spot, only to find the princess was no longer there. "Was this the work of demons?" he thought, and could not stem his flowing tears.
