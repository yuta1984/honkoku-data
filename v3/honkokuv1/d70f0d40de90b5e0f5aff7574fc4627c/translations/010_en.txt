Praying in her heart "Please let me meet my father and mother once more," she was made to mount the horse. What a changed and pitiful state this was! One who had ridden in palanquins and carriages even for casual moon-viewing or flower-viewing was now mounted on such a frightening horse, wandering off not knowing what country to rely upon. Her parents and the ladies-in-waiting appeared to be on the verge of fainting. Whether they knew her or not, there was no one who did not lament. The princess, in her overwhelming sadness, composed a poem:

　From now on, who will take pity on me?
　　The flowers of mother's forest have scattered

Thus she composed. Also, seeing the various flowers blooming in profusion along the hedge at that moment, she composed thus:

　Though flowers of many colors bloom fragrantly in their prime,
　　Why must I be the first to scatter?

Having composed this, she was made to straddle the horse and tearfully departed from home. Yukiharu (her attendant) found her so pitiable that he thought, even if he should incur the displeasure of the wealthy couple, how could he let a woman go alone?
