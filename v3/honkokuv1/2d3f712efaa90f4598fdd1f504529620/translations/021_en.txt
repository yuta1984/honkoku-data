(Continued from previous page) Within that orbital path, the sun's gravitational force, together with the atmosphere, fills the entire circle and revolves, ensuring that the movements of all the planets do not fall into disorder—truly demonstrating that the sun's gravitational force is supremely powerful and unyielding. If a comet were to enter this orbital zone, how could it possibly avoid being restrained by this mighty force and becoming a star belonging to the sun's domain? This is because it would be like trying to ferry a boat sideways across the raging current of a great river. Yet according to the theories of the Western barbarians, a comet comes from far beyond the outermost planetary heavens, breaks through the several layers of atmosphere of the planetary orbits, penetrates and shatters the sun's powerful gravitational force, approaches very close to the sun, then in an instant breaks through the several atmospheric layers of the planets again and departs back to where it came from beyond the outermost planetary heavens—but what principle or reason do they claim accounts for this? Where, then, is the vaunted theory of natural philosophy they have so proudly proclaimed? If one argues in this manner, even if clever tongues exhaust every stratagem, the barbarian factions' reckless arguments will collapse of their own accord without even needing to be attacked.

**Third Difficulty: The Ebb and Flow of the Tides**　　The Western theory states that the morning tide and evening tide are caused by the gravitational forces of the sun and moon. The *Kikai Kanran* (folio 39) states: "Among celestial bodies there exists gravitational attraction. The most conspicuous example is that of the sun acting upon the earth. The ocean tides rise and fall on account of this. (Omission) ...the tides rise and fall twice, generally rising over six hours and falling over six hours, alternating without cease." This is to use the ebb and flow of the tides as proof that the sun and moon possess gravitational force. The text further states: "At the new moon (*saku*), the moon and sun are in conjunction; therefore not only the moon's gravitational force but also the sun's gravitational force acts together. At the new moon, these two forces are combined, and the tide swells to an especially large degree." This explains that the reason the tides at the new moon are twice as great as on ordinary days is that the combined forces of both the sun and moon pull in the same direction.
