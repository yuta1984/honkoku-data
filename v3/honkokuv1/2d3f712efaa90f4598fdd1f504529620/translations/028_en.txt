"Tantiandi" (The Englishman Wylie brought Western books in the year Xianfeng Jiwei and translated them together with the Chinese scholar Li Shanlan.) Yet people universally praise Western astronomical theories as precise calculations and use them to determine actual diameters—what kind of ignorance is this? (This is the first unreasonable point.) Moreover, it has been known since ancient times that the five planets enter within the moon. However, when examining volume 12 of the "Xinpian," it measures the moon's diameter as 7,630 li. Compared to the diameters of the five planets, their relative sizes are clear. (There is a diagram showing the sizes of the seven luminaries and their distances from earth earlier.) Yet how is it that the five planets, with their large diameters, can enter within the small-diameter moon, and their stellar forms appear no different in size from the stellar bodies we commonly observe?
