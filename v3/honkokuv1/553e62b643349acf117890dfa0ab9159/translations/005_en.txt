The country called "Herculaneum" sank entirely underground due to an earthquake, and those trapped could neither escape nor return; it was unclear whether they were dead or alive. Centuries later, people in the lands now known as "Italy" and "Germany," having heard of this event through old traditions, excavated the underground and reportedly obtained vast quantities of precious treasures, famous paintings, gold and silver vessels, and great riches. This occurred because a cavity formed underground, and under the weight of castle structures the land collapsed below, while great mountains and layered ridges fell upon it, making it level like flat ground. The author Tenzan notes that the book *Konkyo Zushiki Ho* (Supplementary Illustrated Records of the Earth's Geography) states that in ancient times, a famous city of "Rome" was involved in this disaster and became entirely buried in the earth, and that it was excavated again 1,650 years later — but this refers to a different event. That case was caused by a volcanic eruption.
