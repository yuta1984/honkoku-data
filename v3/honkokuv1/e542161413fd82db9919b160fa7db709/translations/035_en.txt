Language

Human races can also be distinguished by language. When we examine various national languages today, we find that many bear similarities to one another, and we can determine that their origins lie in a single type of language, which we designate as one family. When we combine those that are similar to each other, we form a second family, and we call these the two great language families. The first family is called the Semitic family, which includes Hebrew, Aramaic (namely Babylonian and Syrian languages), Arabic, and (Ethiopian is close to this family). The second is called the Indo-European family, to which the languages of European and Asian countries belong. This can be further divided into six types. The first is Sanskrit, which includes all the dialects of the Indian states. The second is Zend, namely Median-Persian, and all the dialects of Persia and Armenia belong to this language.
