On the night of October 2nd, 1855 (Ansei 2, year of the Rabbit), at the fourth hour of the night (around 10 PM), a great earthquake suddenly struck, causing the provinces of Kanto to collapse all at once. The damage to civilian houses and temples and shrines was severe, and the shaking was particularly intense within Edo.

To the north, half of Senju-juku post station collapsed, Kozukappara-cho was completely burned, and there were many collapsed houses in the areas of Sanya, Asakusa-cho, and Shin-torigoe.

In the riverside towns, the Zenza (mint) burned, and Imado-cho had many collapses, with fifteen or sixteen houses burning from near the bridge. Sosenji Temple in Hashiba suffered minor damage, and the temple grounds also had minor damage.

In Shin-Yoshiwara, all five districts collapsed at once. Particularly, a fire broke out from Kyomachi 2-chome and spread throughout the area, with fires also starting in various other locations, burning everything down. The number of deaths among courtesans and other men and women was tremendous and countless. Not a single storehouse remained, and everything became open fields. The Nihon-zutsumi embankment cracked in places. The official notice board remained unharmed, and the fifty-ken road on this side survived the fire.

From Tamachi 2-chome to 1-chome, Yamakawa-cho, from Takemon to Kita-shin-cho (Shoten-yoko-cho), Kita-bado, and all the temples in the Kitadani and Minamidani areas of Sensoji Temple grounds burned completely. Minami-bado partially survived the fire. Shoten-cho and Yama-no-yado had many collapses, while Mamechiyama remained safe. Most of Kawara-cho collapsed.

The Kawarasaki-za in Saruwaka-cho 3-chome, the Ichimura-za in 2-chome, the Nakamura-za in 1-chome, theater buildings, and Shindo all burned completely. Parts of Yakusha-shindo survived. Nakamura Fukusuke, Onoe Eijiro, Bando Takesaburo, Bando Kichiya, Morita Kanya, and other tea houses and amateurs also survived. From Yama-no-yado to the Hanakawado Tozawa tenements across from Kuhonji Temple burned down.

Now, the main hall of Kinryuzan Kannon remained unharmed, but subsidiary shrines and temple buildings collapsed in places. Rental houses on the temple grounds were greatly destroyed. There were many collapsed houses in the areas of Namiki-cho and Tahara-cho, and Naka-cho and Sanken-cho also had many collapses.

A fire started from the middle of Komagata and stopped at Suwa-cho, Kurofune-cho, Miyoshi-cho, and Umayagashi. The imperial mausoleum storehouse, though much damaged, remained safe. There were many collapses around Kaya-cho, Fukui-cho, Odaichi, and along the Kanda River.

The main hall of Higashi Honganji Temple, though greatly damaged, remained safe. The rear gate collapsed, and many temples on the grounds were destroyed. About half a block beyond Kikuya Bridge burned. Temples in Shintera-cho collapsed in places, but Seigan-ji and Hoon-ji remained safe. The area in front of Yugyo-ji Temple behind them was damaged in places. The mansion of Lord Kato Okura-shoyu of Tanba had many collapses, with damage to the main ridge and enclosures.
