In the Denma-cho street outside Yotsuya Gate, there was extensive damage in various side streets. On the main street, the Mangaku-toi (permanent water conduit) of the Tamagawa water supply collapsed from underground, causing water to overflow onto the road, and many people fell into it. Yotsuya Shinjuku and Naruko-cho had little damage, while the areas of Sendagaya, Okubo, Gondahara, and Rokudo had no particular damage. Samegabashi had much collapse, and the Aoyama area had little damage.

In Akasaka, there was much collapse in Denma-cho, Tamachi street, and Hitotsugi area. Azabu also had much damage but nothing exceptional. Higakubo and Ryudo had little damage. However, regarding earthen storehouse walls, there was no place that did not collapse.

From Tameike outside Tora-no-mon to the Ichibei-cho area, damage was relatively light. Nishi-kubo had some damage, Yotsutsuji was severely damaged, and Mamiana, Juban, and Akabane had severe damage. Mita and Iikura had some damage. From Kanasugi to Ushi-machi, Tamachi street also had little damage.

From Hijiri-zaka to the Isarago area, damage was severe. Takanawadai-cho, Nihon-enoki, and Shirokane had little damage. In Takanawa-cho and Shinagawa area, only about five or six houses collapsed.

The temple grounds of Shiba Zojoji were safe. Chumon-mae, Katamon-mae, and Hamamatsu-cho had little collapse. Shinmei-cho and Mishima-cho had major collapses with many destroyed houses, but the Shinmei shrine grounds were safe.

On the street side, from Udagawa-cho to the waterfront mansions and the Shinginza area, damage was severe. Shibai-cho had one entire block burn down. In Rogetsu-cho, Gensuke-cho, and Shibaguchi, the residences of the Sendai lord and Wakisaka Awaji-no-kami had little collapse, and the Kubo-cho area also had little damage.

Between Shinbashi and Kyobashi, damage was light, and Sukiyabashi had some damage. From Kobiki-cho to the Tsukiji area, only the plaster walls and earthen storehouses of the mansions collapsed, but the houses were safe. The main hall of Nishi Honganji was safe, but there were collapsed temple buildings within the grounds.

From Minami-Hacho-bori to Teppozu, many collapsed. In Funamatsu-cho, the residence of Matsudaira Awaji-no-kami burned down. On Tsukuda Island, the fishermen's quarter burned. On Reigan Island, more than two blocks burned in three locations: from Shio-cho to Hama-cho and Okawabata-cho. Tomishima-cho, Kawaguchi-cho, Nagasaki-cho, and Minato-cho areas had much collapse, and Kita-Shinbori and Hakozaki also had much collapse.
