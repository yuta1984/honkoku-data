After suffering major collapse, the area also burned. From there, along Kōtokji Temple front street, many temples in the back streets were damaged, and areas around Yamazaki-cho, Okitte-cho, and Iriya were largely collapsed in places.

Sakamoto 2nd and 3rd districts burned. Fires spread to both Tako-ya Yokocho and Otansu-cho. Near Kanasugi Daionji Temple front, Minowa-cho, and Shin-cho suffered many collapses. Negishi had relatively light damage but still had some collapsed houses.

Ueno's Tōeizan (Kan'eiji Temple) collapsed in places but was generally safe. The five-story pagoda at Yanaka Tennōji had its finial broken and buried in the ground. Temples in this area suffered minor damage. Nezu Shrine was unharmed, but all buildings in front of the gate collapsed. Misaki and Sendagi areas had few collapses. Komagome collapsed in places, and Hongō had few warehouse collapses. Koji rooms (for sake brewing) collapsed in various places, and the roads became depressed, requiring logs to be laid across for passage.

Generally, from Yamada-cho along the riverside area, Tozaki-cho, Masu-cho, splitting through Shimotomi-zaka, the mansions on the left had many collapsed houses. Hakusan and Sugamo had few collapses. Around Koishikawa Dentsūin Temple front street, Otansu-cho, Ōtsuka, and Otowa areas were half-destroyed. From Koishikawa Suwa-cho to the Mito lord's hundred-unit tenement and the lower street collapsed, while the opposite side burned sporadically. Both sides along the Edo River had many collapses.

Ushigome Baba had few collapses. From Akagi-shita, Kaidai-cho, Kohinata Suidō-cho to the Sekiguchi area had many collapses. The Ichigaya area also had many severely damaged places, but the Hachiman shrine there was safe.

Iida-cho, Manaita-bashi, and areas near Ogawa-cho had much damage. Both inside and outside Koishikawa Gate suffered severe damage. Within that area, the mansion of Matsudaira Suruga-no-kami burned. All mansions in this vicinity burned, and the rest also suffered severe collapses.

Reaching Ogawa-cho, the mansions of Inaba Nagato-no-kami, Ōya Uneme-no-shō, Matsudaira Saemon-no-jō, Doi Noto-no-kami, and other nearby lord mansions suffered much damage. Toda Ōi-no-kami's mansion burned but the front tenements remained. Endō Suruga-no-kami suffered similarly with the front gate tenements remaining. Starting with Hotta Bitchū-no-kami, all nearby areas burned completely. Nakarai Izumo-no-kami's mansion remained. Most mansions in this area burned.

From there, the lord mansions around front Jinbō-kōji, from the fire brigade official residences to Jinbō-kōji, all burned.

Along Hitotsubashi Gate street, the hatamoto residences remained, and toward Hara area, Sakakibara Shikibu-tayū's mansion half-burned and completely collapsed. Honda Bungo-no-kami's mansion also half-burned. Toda Kaga-no-kami's mansion burned.

Along Kiji-bashi Gate street, starting with Hongō Tango-no-kami, all lord mansions in that area burned completely, stopping at Sanban-hara.
