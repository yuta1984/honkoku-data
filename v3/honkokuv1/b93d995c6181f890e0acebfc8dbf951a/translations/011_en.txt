Among these, the gold, silver, treasures, clothing, and furnishings consumed in the fires were completely destroyed in an instant, turning everything into scorched earth and wasteland - truly a lamentable disaster. ○ Afterwards, earthquakes continued to occur frequently day and night, causing people great fear. Someone spread the rumor that "there will certainly be a severe earthquake tonight," and this spread throughout the populace without anyone knowing who started it. To avoid this calamity, people used sliding doors as roofs in any open spaces at street corners, or enclosed themselves with baggage and folding screens, spreading straw mats below to camp outdoors. This was widespread throughout the towns. However, nothing significant happened thereafter, so people returned to their homes around mid-October. But those who lost their homes to fire had nowhere to return, so they remained in makeshift huts covered with straw mats, and one can imagine their hardships during heavy rains. Therefore, the government built relief huts for the destitute and issued an official notice that people could apply as needed. The homeless gathered there, receiving daily rice distributions and carrying on their livelihoods - truly grateful mercy of this benevolent era.
