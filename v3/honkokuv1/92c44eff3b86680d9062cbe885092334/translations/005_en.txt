Preface to Brief Remarks on Hot Springs
Hot springs exist in the earth, with fiery vapor rising upward, creating bubbling torrents. Some are warm, some are hot, and some are half-cold and half-warm. Within a distance of just a few steps, water and fire coexist. It is unknown what causes this phenomenon. Those who theorize say that sulfur stone is the root cause, or that wherever hot springs exist, there must be alum, cinnabar, and sulfur as the fundamental sources, which then evaporate and flow.
