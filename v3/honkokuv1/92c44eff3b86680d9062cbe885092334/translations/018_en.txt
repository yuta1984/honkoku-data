When you feel that you have warmed up and the hot water has penetrated your body, you should immediately get out. Staying immersed for a long time causes vital energy to leak out and bodily fluids to become depleted, which is greatly harmful. Now, after getting out of the hot spring, you must not expose yourself to wind. After bathing, the hot spring vapors permeate the body and you do not feel cold, so many people sit for long periods wearing only a yukata and catch cold. Moreover, since daily bathing opens the surface energy, you are susceptible to catching cold even when not immediately after bathing, so you should be very careful about this. You must never take afternoon naps or doze off. If you absolutely must nap, you should put on proper nightwear and set up a folding screen around yourself before lying down. During hot spring therapy, alcohol should be prohibited. Sexual relations are of course out of the question. Even after hot spring therapy, you must abstain from these for a while
