Those suffering from scabies, syphilitic sores, and all manner of skin ailments who bathe in this spring will have their harmful toxins expelled and their stagnant suppurations brought to the surface. The various sores will become remarkably active, and after a period of nine days, or fourteen days, or twenty-one days, the sufferer will recover completely and in good spirits. This is truly a hot spring of the highest and most supreme quality. For this reason, bathers come from many neighboring provinces. However, those who are not versed in the principles of hot springs may offer various criticisms, thinking that it is considerably inferior to springs such as those at Arima — but this is not so. People in this world merely esteem what they hear by reputation and look down upon what they see with their own eyes; they yearn for what is distant and neglect what is close at hand. This is their common nature. It is truly lamentable.

Long ago, the monk Renzen came all the way down to this spring for a curative bath, and upon completing his stay and making his return journey, he composed the following verse at Dan-no-ura in Nagato Province:

　At night, thinking of my distant homeland, I at last enter into dreams;
　The skies clearing, I gaze upon a solitary island — how small it appears.
