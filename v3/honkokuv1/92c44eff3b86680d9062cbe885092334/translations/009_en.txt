In past years, when I was treating my illness at hot springs in Dewa, I followed solely the methods recorded in the "Shōgen" (Small Words) and achieved complete recovery. I first relied on this book and gained benefits for my body. This is truly the virtue of Sōkei extending its influence. At that time, while passing through Shimōsa, I met Onda Taiga, a Confucian retainer of Koga, who was a disciple of Master Sōkei. We discussed this matter, and I secretly rejoiced in the purity of his devotion to his friend, finally confirming that the words of Tōgai and Hikoshō were indeed true. As the poem says, "When I think of the ancients, they truly fulfill my heart." Such are my feelings toward Sōkei as well. When Confucius died, his disciples wanted to serve Yūjaku as they had served Confucius, but when strongly urged by Zengzi, Zengzi would not consent. There are also the words about "Jianghan and autumn sun." Moreover, Mencius, though a great sage like Yanzi, still did not dare to wish to emulate him directly, saying instead "I wish to learn from Confucius." From this time onward, those who study Confucianism invariably take Confucius as their master teacher. Therefore, disciples call him "Master," which even five children know.
