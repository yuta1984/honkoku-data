One: Recently, I had the opportunity to see a book called "Yakusen" (Medicine Selection) written by Kagawa Taichū, a disciple of Gotō Saichi of Kyoto. In its sequel, he discusses hot springs and does not accept the theory that hot springs boil due to sulfur. Instead, he cites Ina Jakusui's theory, thinking as follows: there are water channels and fire channels in the earth, and when the fire channels meet the water channels, hot springs are formed. While this theory is correct to some extent, it is nevertheless very crude. Moreover, this is something collected from Yūshi Roku's theories, and it is not a theory that Wakamizu first propounded. However, since "Tenkei Wakumon" is a recently imported book, Wakamizu might not have seen it and his theory might have coincidentally matched.
