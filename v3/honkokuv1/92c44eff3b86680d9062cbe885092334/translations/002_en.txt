Hot Springs Treatise - Complete

This book is beneficial not only for doctors but for all people who take hot spring cures to possess a copy. It explains in detail the methods of hot spring therapy, and moreover teaches the distinction between diseases that are cured by hot spring treatment and those that are not. It also explains symptoms for which hot spring therapy can be harmful depending on the illness, and discusses various Japanese and Chinese theories about how hot springs emerge, thoroughly refuting their errors and recording all the ancient matters and origins of hot springs.

Preface to "Brief Remarks on Hot Springs"
Harako Kōyō of Heian made Confucian studies his profession, and his life's journeys covered half the realm. From what he personally experienced and witnessed with his own eyes, he wrote "Brief Remarks on Hot Springs" in one volume, discussing the nature and primary therapeutic effects of hot springs, their indications, contraindications, and proper regimens. Regarding hot spring matters, he left almost nothing unexplored. That he named it "brief remarks" was probably out of modesty.
