In the midst of such suffering, violent tremors occurred from Ōmi Province through Iga and Ise, with Shima also experiencing terrible conditions. The injured and dead lay piled upon one another. The same occurred in Yamashiro Province, where hills and Kawachi cracked open, and from Izumi, Settsu, and Wakasa to Tanba - within just 22 provinces, the number of dead and injured was incalculable.

In Nagoya city, 2,585 houses collapsed. All brick buildings crumbled without exception, shattered to pieces. Among all this destruction, only the main tower of Owari Nagoya Castle remained unharmed without losing even a single roof tile - a truly remarkable miracle of this disaster.

Great Earthquake Ballad (Part 2)
Now then, the great devastation of the earthquake occurred around Ōgaki in Gifu Prefecture, Mino Province, where over 6,000 houses were destroyed or burned. According to the tally of the dead, there were approximately 974 people. In addition, the number of injured was incalculable.

Particularly tragic was the textile company, whose building was a three-story brick structure measuring thirty ken by thirty ken in a area of three chō. When the unexpected earthquake struck while 74 officials and over 500 female workers were inside together, there wasn't even time to cry out "ah!"
