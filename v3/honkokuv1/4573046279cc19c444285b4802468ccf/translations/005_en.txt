How pitiful it was - people writhing and suffering under the collapsed brick rubble, like the torments of hell. Even those fortunate enough to escape with their lives became half-dead and wounded. While about twenty factory girls and officials were saved, over five hundred people were crushed, presenting a tragic and horrific scene. When parents and siblings heard the news and came rushing, tumbling and rolling in their haste, they saw brick debris piled up like mountains. From the machinery areas, fires blazed up, and all they could do was cry out helplessly - it was a terrible story to hear. Also, at the post stations of Okazaki and Asuke,

The railway collapsed and sank more than two jō (about 6 meters), telegraph poles were broken and scattered for more than three ri (about 12 kilometers), cutting off communications. The damage to the railway company and telegraph bureau exceeded 100,000 yen, with over 60,000 houses burned, and the total death toll from various locations reaching approximately 73,000 people. From the morning of the 28th for ten days, the number of earthquakes that shook day and night was approximately 534 times. People everywhere could only cry out in despair, feeling barely alive. Then, around sunset on November 11th, rain began to fall, and after two days and one night of heavy rain, the earthquakes finally began to subside.
