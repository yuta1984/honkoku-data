The Book of Changes (I Ching) states: "It is stirred by thunder and lightning, moistened by wind and rain, and the sun and moon move in their courses, bringing alternating cold and warmth." This passage speaks of how trembling swiftly calls forth changes between cold and warm. Since Lesser Yang (少陽) is oriented toward the east and corresponds to mid-spring among the four seasons, it is only natural that yang energy rises, bringing transformation and warmth.

Examining the historical record of great earthquakes since ancient times——

Seven reigns after Emperor Jinmu:
In the fifth year of Emperor Kōrei's reign (Boar year), it is recorded that the province of Ōmi split open in a single night to become a lake, and simultaneously Mt. Fuji in Suruga Province erupted; however, upon consulting the Man'yōshū and other sources, this account appears doubtful and is difficult to accept as fact.

The twentieth reign:
In the fifth year of Emperor Ingyō's reign, Hei-shin year (436 CE), in the seventh month, a great earthquake caused all palace buildings and residences to be completely destroyed.

The thirty-fourth reign:
In the seventh year of Empress Suiko's reign, Kinoto-Hitsuji year (599 CE), in the fourth month, a great earthquake struck.

The fortieth reign:
In the fourth year of Emperor Tenmu's Hakuhō era, Kinoto-I year (675 CE), in the eleventh month, an earthquake occurred. In the same reign's thirteenth year, Tsuchinoe-Tora year (678 CE), on the fourth day of the tenth month, a great earthquake struck Tsukushi (Kyushu): the earth cracked for more than 3,000 jō in length and more than 3 jō in width. Not only were people's dwellings destroyed, but mountains collapsed extensively, and in the eleventh month of the same year the shaking was especially strong, causing landslides and flooding rivers throughout the provinces, with deaths among the people and their livestock...
