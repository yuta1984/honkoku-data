Using the *son* (southeast, ☴) direction of the board as a pivot point, sight back (*mikaeshi*) to the right side of the target from the north of the board diagonally and draw an ink line ⑥. Using the northern end of that ink line (this is the ink line drawn by sighting back to the right of the target) as the pivot, sight directly (*makane*) — (when the width of the far face is very large, sight back diagonally as well) — to the left side of the target (note: the left of the target is the right from the home station; all subsequent items follow this convention), sight back and draw an ink line following the straightedge (*jōgi*).

【Boundary Line (界)】
Now, using the *senban* method (specialized board operation), draw a boundary line diagonally from the *son* (southeast) intersection of the board across to the intersection of the ink lines for sighting and line-of-sight to the left target, thus completing the full diagram (*taisei*) of the board face.
The "three" (三) on the north of the board now visible is the reduced scale of the opening distance (*kaijo*: right direct opening, 30 *ken*). The diagonal "three" (*shasan*) in the center of the board (the *shasan* refers to the boundary line drawn across using the *senban* method) is the reduced scale of the sought distance (*kyūtei*: the width of that sloped face). (The "four" on the right is the reduced far-distance to the left of the target; the "four" on the left is the reduced far-distance to the right of the target.) Measure the "three" on the north of the board against the opening distance of 30 *ken*, and using that square measure (*kane*: the square measure of the opening distance of 30 *ken*), measure the diagonal boundary line "three" in the center of the board; it yields one span and one-third (*ikkyo sanbun'ichi*). (One span equals 30 *ken*; one-third equals 10 *ken*.) One span and one-third is thus 40 *ken*. This is the number of *ken* of the sought distance (the width of the sloped face on the far side: 40 *ken*).

【Direct-Face, Forward-Backward Method (正面前後方)】
(Here the method for "forward opening" (*maeateki*) is recorded. "Backward opening" (*ushiroateki*) should be understood by the same principle.)

This technique is used when the terrain of the home station is narrow — a road, embankment, or similar confined land — making it difficult to perform the opening measurement (*kaijo*) to the left or right, in order to measure the width of the face directly ahead. The method involves advancing or retreating the opening position forward or backward to find the measurement, and then measuring the sought distance. It is broadly the same format as the "forward-backward opening in the distance technique" (*enkinjutsu zengōkai*). Refer to and coordinate with that section.

The technique states (described with reference to the diagram below): Following the prescribed procedure, after completing each preliminary calculation ①, set the board squarely at the home station. Sight directly (*makane*) from the east of the board to the right side of the target (note: the right of the target is the left of the board) ②. Place the board so it does not move. Using the *gon* (northeast, ☶) direction of the board as the pivot, sight diagonally to the left of the target, draw an ink line following the straightedge, and set a remaining marker (*nokoshi shirushi*) at the home station. From there, determine the number of *ken* going straight ahead to the far side and seek the opening distance (since it is difficult to open to the left or right, one advances to find it; retreating to find it uses the same technique), and have a line-of-sight marker (*mitōshi no shirushi*) set up. (Take care to set it such that the right of the target, the straightedge of the east of the board, and this marker form a single straight line at all three points. Since this is the step of "sighting," there is no need to separately perform a line-of-sight check.) ② Move to the opening ground (*kaichi*), re-sight the remaining marker to orient the board correctly. (At this point, for certainty, it is also acceptable to sight directly to the right of the target once more.) ③
