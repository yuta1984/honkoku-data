## Liu (柳, Willow) — Lunar Mansion

**[Star Chart]** The eight stars of Liu govern food and the harmonizing of flavors. They also govern thunder and rain. When bright, the people are abundantly provided with food and drink; when their luster is lost, famine spreads along the roads — and within no more than three years, this omen will surely manifest.

People born under this star will preserve good fortune and prosperity throughout their lives, yet they will frequently find themselves in conflict with others. Furthermore, if they change their place of residence, they will witness the death of others.

---

## Xing (星, Star) — Lunar Mansion

**[Star Chart]** The seven stars of Xing govern garments and embroidered textiles. If Mercury transgresses them, the people will be at peace; if Mars transgresses them, drought will follow.

People born under this star will enjoy much good fortune, and their plans and aspirations will come to fruition as desired. However, they should expect mental strain and worry in their old age.

---

## Zhang (張, Extended Net) — Lunar Mansion

**[Star Chart]** The six stars of Zhang govern the Celestial Kitchen, food and drink, and tribute offerings. When bright, the nation flourishes and grows strong.

People born under this star will fulfill their ambitions of advancement and success, and things will go as they wish. Those of noble standing will rise in official rank, and their stipends will also increase.

---

## Yi (翼, Wings) — Lunar Mansion

**[Star Chart]** The twenty-two stars of Yi govern foreign peoples, travelers from distant lands, performers, and entertainments. When bright, ritual and music flourish, and the peoples of the four directions come as honored guests.

People born under this star are, for the most part, poor. And if they are not poor, they will be short-lived. However, if they cultivate a generous spirit and show compassion to others, they will preserve their good fortune and attain a long life.

---

## Zhen (軫, Chariot) — Lunar Mansion

**[Star Chart]** The four stars of Zhen govern chariots and cavalry, and correspond to the image of the Bright Fire (離明) of the South. When bright, all under heaven is at great peace, the people live in ease and comfort, and the four seas submit to the transforming influence of the sovereign.

People born under this star are blessed with good fortune and divine protection. Moreover, in their old age they will attain great auspiciousness in all matters.
