It is like the movement of blood vessels and pulse. Thunder and lightning also follow the same principle. Therefore, in lands close to the North Pole, because it is extremely cold, heat cannot be generated, so there may be slight trembling but no major shaking. Below the equator is where the sun's power is strongest, so the qi there disperses easily. When it breathes, there may be some slight trembling. Also, when the qi is sparse and not confined, there is slight trembling. In muddy soil, since it cannot store qi in emptiness, it trembles slightly. In warm regions with many stones, if there are cavities underground, hot qi blows in and contracts the cold qi, and when this reaches its limit, it expands and is released, violently striking that land. Therefore, the earth does not shake uniformly. Shaking means that each place, each qi, and each movement is different. Thus, while three directions may shake, the whole does not shake together. Sometimes land is moved to other places, sometimes mountains are moved and changed, sometimes the earth splits and castles and villages completely sink, sometimes new mountains appear in places where there were no mountains, new islands appear in seas where there were no islands, sometimes rivers reverse their flow and scatter to the sides, or new spring flows are opened while old flows are blocked - all these occur because there are variations according to the severity of the shaking. After great shaking, dry qi in the underground presses fiercely and becomes hot.
