## [Wei 尾 – The Tail]
[Star Chart]
If the nine stars of Wei shine equally bright, the inner palace (imperial harem) will be well-ordered and many descendants will flourish. If the stars cluster together, it portends flooding and water disasters.

A person born under this star will appear to have good fortune and prosperity to their satisfaction; however, they will encounter fire disasters and lose their wealth.

---

## [Ji 箕 – The Winnowing Basket]
[Star Chart]
The four stars of Ji govern disputes arising from words and the winds of all eight directions. If they shine brightly and are straight (well-aligned), the five grains will ripen; if dark, the price of the five grains will rise.

A person born under this star will have difficulty settling in a fixed place of residence. They may encounter misfortune in old age; however, if they possess a compassionate heart toward others, their wishes will be fulfilled in old age, and they will obtain good fortune.

---

## [Dou 斗 – The Southern Dipper]
[Star Chart]
The six stars of the Southern Dipper (Nandou) are the foremost lunar mansion of the north. Their shape resembles the Northern Dipper (Beidou/Big Dipper), penetrating through the center. They govern rewards and commendations, the promotion of worthy persons, and the bestowal of noble ranks and stipends.

A person born under this star will have thin fortune and prosperity. Throughout their life they will experience mental hardship and find it difficult to govern themselves properly. However, if they are warm and friendly toward others and treat people with respect, it will bring good fortune.

---

## [Niu 牛 – The Ox]
[Star Chart]
The six stars of the Ox (Qianniu/Altair region) resemble the shape of an ox and have two horns. They govern matters of ritual sacrifice. Furthermore, the central star governs oxen; if it moves, there will be much calamity for oxen. If this star appears curved, the price of rice will rise; if it is not bright, the five grains will fail to ripen.

A person born under this star will be endowed with fortune and prosperity, but will be short-lived. If they do live long, they will be poor and burdened with much mental hardship. Nevertheless, by maintaining an honest and sincere heart, being warm toward others, and by venerating the gods through ritual offerings, they may avoid such misfortunes.

[Star Chart]
The four stars of Nü govern the cutting and sewing of cloth and silk, and matters of marriage and betrothal. If these stars are bright, the world will be prosperous and women's handiwork and crafts will flourish.
