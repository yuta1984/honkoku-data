## The Directions of the Twenty-Eight Lunar Mansions (Cited from the Shiji/Records of the Grand Historian)

○ Eastern Direction: Jiao (角), Kang (亢), Di (氐), Fang (房), Xin (心), Wei (尾), Ji (箕) — 7 mansions, 32 ri
○ Western Direction: Kui (奎), Lou (婁), Wei (胃), Mao (昴), Bi (畢), Zi (觜), Shen (参) — 7 mansions, 51 ri
○ Southern Direction: Jing (井), Gui (鬼), Liu (柳), Xing (星), Zhang (張), Yi (翼), Zhen (軫) — 7 mansions, 64 ri
○ Northern Direction: Dou (斗), Niu (牛), Nü (女), Xu (虚), Wei (危), Shi (室), Bi (壁) — 7 mansions, 35 ri

### The Mansions that Govern the Four Seasons
○ Spring: Kui (奎)　　Summer: Lou (婁)
　　　　　Doyo (Midseasonal Period): Bi (壁)
　Autumn: Zhang (張)　　Winter: Fang (房)

## The Keys to Good and Bad Fortune of the Twenty-Eight Lunar Mansions

○ The Twenty-Eight Lunar Mansions move continuously through the four pillars of year, month, day, and hour. Therefore, by means of these mansions, one can know the fortune and misfortune, longevity and untimely death in a person's life — this is the essence of astrology. Here, key passages have been extracted and compiled from military treatises, and the keys to misfortune are set forth.

【Star Chart】 The eight stars of the Shi (室, Chamber) mansion govern matters of earthworks and construction. When these stars are bright, the nation flourishes; when they are not bright, there will be much pestilence; when they move, earthworks and construction will occur.

A person born under this mansion (Shi/Chamber) will experience misfortune in youth, but will find good fortune upon reaching old age. Furthermore, at some point in their life, they will encounter a thief while traveling and suffer the loss of their belongings. Travel and journeys to the eastern direction are auspicious. In general, those born in winter under this mansion are of half-auspicious fortune.
