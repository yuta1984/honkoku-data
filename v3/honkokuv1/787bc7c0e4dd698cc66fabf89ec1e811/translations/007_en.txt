On the Theory of the Annual Virtue Deity (Nentokushin)

According to the "Almanac Forest Dialogue" from Kamo's region, when employing matters of yin-yang, it is considered good to align with virtue. Therefore, the direction of annual virtue is the direction of virtue for the entire year. These are all virtues of the Ten Heavenly Stems. However, among the ten stems, five are considered yang virtue: Kō (甲), Hei (丙), Bo (戊), Kō (庚), and Jin (壬). Five are considered yin virtue: Otsu (乙), Tei (丁), Ki (己), Shin (辛), and Ki (癸).

The annual virtue of Kō is in the eastern palace in the direction of Kō, the annual virtue of Hei is in the southern palace in the direction of Hei, the annual virtue of Bo is in the central palace in the direction of Bo, the annual virtue of Kō is in the western palace in the direction of Kō, and the annual virtue of Jin is in the northern palace in the direction of Jin. The annual virtues of these five stems are all yang virtue, so they are in their respective directions.

Also, the annual virtue of Otsu is in the western palace in the direction of Kō, the annual virtue of Tei is in the northern palace in the direction of Jin, the annual virtue of Ki is in the eastern palace in the direction of Kō, the annual virtue of Shin is in the southern palace in the direction of Hei, and the annual virtue of Ki is in the central palace in the direction of Bo. Otsu, Tei, Ki, Shin, and Ki are yin stems, so naturally they have no virtue of their own. They form virtue by pairing with yang stems. Therefore, Ki is made the wife of Kō and they unite, so the annual virtue of Ki is in Kō. Shin is made the wife of Hei and they unite, so the annual virtue of Shin is in Hei. Otsu is made the wife of Kō and they unite, so the annual virtue of Otsu is in Kō. Ki is made the wife of Bo and they unite, so the annual virtue of Ki is in Bo.
