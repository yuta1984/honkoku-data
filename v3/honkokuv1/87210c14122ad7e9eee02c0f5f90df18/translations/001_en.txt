# Overview of the Kanto Flood

## Locations of Levee Breaches

### Tone River Basin

◯Karasu River: Kamigun, Jinbohara Village — 1 breach, over 30 ken; same village, Hatchōgawara — 3 breaches
◯Tone River: Kodama District, Fujita Village — 1 breach, 50 ken; Hara District, Onuma — 1 breach, 60 ken; same district, Shonuma Village — 1 breach, 50 ken; same district, Nagai Village — 2 breaches; Kita-Saitama District, Suga Village, Shimochūjō — 1 breach, 70 ken; same district, Mugikura Village — 1 breach, over 90 ken; same district, Kawabe Village — 1 breach, over 100 ken
◯Watarase River: same district, Zen Village — 1 breach, 15 ken
◯Gongendo River: Kita-Katsushika District, Miyuki Village — 1 breach, 25 ken

### Ara River Basin

◯Ara River: Osato District, Yoshimi Village, Tamatsukuri sluice gate — 7 ken; Yokomi District, Higashi-Yoshimi Village, Daisan temporary closure
◯Iruma River: Koma District, Nahoso Village — 1 breach; same district, Kashiwabara Village — 1 breach
◯Koma River: Iruma District, Sakado Village — 1 breach

---

The damage from levee breaches in Saitama Prefecture was already a great disaster as described above, but unfortunately, due to the violent storm on the 30th, the region was struck by flooding a second time. Since the initial inundation on the 23rd, through the combined efforts of government officials and residents, and with the gradual receding of the river waters, conditions had been slowly improving. However, even the temporary repaired levee at Shimochūjō — the most critical breach point — was, pitifully, swept away all at once, rendering all defensive efforts in vain. Raging, churning waves poured violently through the breach. What could withstand such force? Once again, the entire area became a sea of mud. In particular, around Gyōda, only the remains of Oshi Castle stood like a solitary island, while an endless muddy expanse stretched beyond the eye's reach. Within it, disaster victims, covered in mud and driven to the brink of starvation, presented a scene of such misery that it was truly unbearable to witness.
