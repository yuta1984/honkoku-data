The Earth is enveloped by air, which absorbs the yang (positive energy) of the sun and becomes filled with it. Yin (negative energy) emanates from the Earth itself, and when combined with yang, it becomes wind, rain, and clouds. When yin rises with great intensity, it becomes thunder. When yang descends with great intensity and enters into the Earth, the Earth trembles. This is what we call an earthquake.
Although all people are aware of this, no one is able to predict when the shaking will occur. For this reason, earthquakes are accompanied by fires and cause many injuries. It is understood that earthquakes since ancient times have all shared this same nature. Here, we intend to compile a complete record of earthquakes from antiquity for the reader's perusal.
