**Entry 24:**
Starting from the Dōtetsu gate at Asakusa Umamichi (Horse Road), fires spread through Yanaka, the precinct before Tennō-ji temple gate, Daichi-chō, and Yamakawa-chō. To the west, the fire stopped at the large-scale collapse of several temples. On the western side of the southern area, a small section near Fuji survived, but beyond that, Kichijō-in, Takaura, Tokuo-in, Kōshin-dō, Enmei-in, Jizō-dō, Seishin-in, Mudō-in, Tori-dera, Kyōzen-in, Saru-dera, and others all had their hundreds of back tenements completely burned. To the south, fires reached as far as the taverns in front of Asakusa-dera's Zuishin Gate, and from the opposite side westward to Minami-Umamichi (South Horse Road), a stretch of one and a half chō (approximately 165 meters) burned. In total, from the aforementioned Dōtetsu gate to this point, more than nine chō (approximately 990 meters) in the north-south direction were destroyed by fire.

△ Asakusa-dera's main hall suffered no damage. The various halls and monks' quarters were severely damaged. △ The nine-ringed finial (kurin) atop the Five-Story Great Pagoda was bent. Also on the eastern side of Umamichi in the same area, fires spread southward as far as Benten-dō, Seiryū-in, Hyaku-Kannon-dō, Senryō-in, Senzō-in, Fuji-miya, Shūzen-in, Tora-Yakushi, and Myōtoku-in. To the south, fires reached Iō-in, the Twenty-Third Night Hall, Kontara-dō, the historic site of Ubagaike (Old Hag's Pond), and the First Gongen Shrine. Along the eastern side of Zuishin Gate avenue, Kongō-in, Kakuzen-in, Hōzen-in, Myōon-in, and Kenshō-in burned, as did Jishō-in and Jizō-dō on the southern side.

**Entry 25:**
In Saruwaka-chō, the theater district, the following establishments were destroyed by fire: the Nakamura Kanzaburō theater (1st block), the Ichimura Uzaemon theater (2nd block), the Kawarasaki Gonnosuke theater (3rd block), as well as the puppet theaters of Ōsatsuma Kichiemon and Yūki Magosaburō. The backstage alleys, actors' alleyways, and hundreds of back tenements in the area were also completely burned.

**[Character Grid for Fortune-Telling / Social Observation after the Great Fire]**

This section consists of three groups of nine Chinese characters arranged in 3×3 grids, connected by vertical, horizontal, and diagonal lines from the center character outward. They describe the social conditions and fortunes of various groups following the disaster:

**Grid 1 (Movement/Chaos):**
- Great fire moves — townspeople and land flee — earth trembles greatly — houses and spirits collapse
- Parents fear the earth — suffering in the midst of fire — household fortunes prosper — gold and fortune enter
- Things arise and crack — dreams are buried and people run — giving stops and things solidify — storehouses labor and topple

**Grid 2 (Finance/Trade):**
- Lending depletes and renews — dark and cold every night — great wrongdoing overflows — dark blue [merchants] sleep deeply
- Cash is scarce — day and night things move — benevolent guidance — cuts [in goods] sell
- Borrowing swells and empties — deep shock and flashes of light — water without remorse cracks — [night] watchmen are busy with desires

**Grid 3 (Recovery/Prosperity):**
- Main luxury goods bustle — performing arts of the dead revive — long roles earn — the year and world become bright
- New homes are built — craftsmen rejoice — doctors are busy — townspeople become prosperous
- Temporary shelters burn — wounds are hastily healed/revived — the poor have time to weep — the day turns straight to dawn
