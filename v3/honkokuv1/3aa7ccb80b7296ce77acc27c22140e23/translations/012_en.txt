## [Damage within the Grounds of Sensōji Temple]

Within the precincts of Sensōji Temple, the gabled roof (hafu) on the western side of the Kannon Hall was severely damaged. The nine-ringed finial (kurin) atop the Five-Storied Pagoda was bent out of shape.

The Arasawa Hall, the Inner Inari Shrine, the West Shrine Inari, Nichion-in, the Grand Imperial Shrine, the Konpira Shrine, the Matsuo Shrine, the Old Woman Benten — all the halls and shrines within the precincts were, without exception, destroyed.

The Thunder God statue at the Kaminari-mon (Thunder Gate) tumbled and fell. The outdoor stone Buddha (nure-botoke, "wet Buddha") toppled over. The collapse of the monks' quarters and sub-temple buildings throughout the precinct was severe.

The sacred image of the Kannon Bodhisattva was reverently relocated to the Hanayashiki pleasure garden in Okuyama [the area behind the temple].

The fires that broke out from Tamachi or from Shōten Yokochō were contained at Umamichi [Horse Road] and did not enter the temple precinct at all.

---

## [Damage to Temples in the Yanaka and Asakusa Environs]

The temples of Kitatani and Nakatani were entirely destroyed and furthermore burned to the ground. Minamitani suffered many collapses, but was spared from fire.

Through the middle of the tenth month, great numbers of people took to sleeping outdoors in Okuyama.

Furthermore, as for Saruwaka-machi [the theater district], perhaps because the buildings were newly constructed, there was little damage beyond the earthen storehouses — and thus it is all the more to be lamented that the area suffered destruction by spreading fire.
