How magnificent is the virtue of the art of land surveying! Without ascending, one can measure the vast heights of the heavens; without traveling there, one can determine the thickness and breadth of the earth; without entering, one can know the depth and expanse of the sea. As for measuring the height, depth, breadth, and distance of mountains, valleys, rivers, plains, hills, fortifications, and buildings — it is as easy as pointing to something in the palm of one's hand. Through this art, one can observe the movements of the sun and moon to create calendars; float ships upon the great ocean to chart the nations of the world; measure the distance to enemy positions to aim firearms; and determine differences in elevation to open waterways. There is nothing among these that does not rely upon this art. What it strives for achieves the distant through the most immediate means, and what it upholds exhausts the vast through the most concise methods. Is it not truly an art of essential and wondrous technique?

While it may appear that there are numerous methods of land surveying in this world, their teachings broadly fall into five categories: first, the Compass-Needle Method (盤針術); second, the Surveying-Board Method (量盤術); third, the Celestial-Sphere Method (渾発術); fourth, the Arithmetic-Calculation Method (算勘術); and fifth, the Mechanical-Ingenuity Method (機転術). It must be said, however, that these methods cannot be considered equal in quality and standing. Students should choose carefully when deciding what to study. The so-called Compass-Needle Method is the orthodox technique of the ancient kings of China, and it ranks first among them. The so-called Surveying-Board and Celestial-Sphere Methods are the skillful techniques of the Dutch, and they offer a swift and direct path. The so-called Arithmetic-Calculation Method is what mathematicians practice, and it is roundabout and indirect. The so-called Mechanical-Ingenuity Method is what craftsmen and carpenters do, and it ranks last. While it may appear that all of these ultimately arrive at the same unified truth when one reaches their innermost depths, each method has its own principles, and each technique has its own practice.
