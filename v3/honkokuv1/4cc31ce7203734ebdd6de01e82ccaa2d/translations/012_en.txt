Therefore, using this method of preliminary measurement as a foundation for practicing the main techniques has been considered essential teaching since ancient times.

The intervals for surveying measurements are also determined based on this. [Marginalia: The method for determining surveying intervals is described in detail in the chapter below] Also, in urgent situations where there is no time to perform the main techniques, this method alone is often used to measure distances, so it should be mastered most thoroughly.

Generally, the practice of preliminary measurement involves testing distances and widths during daily walks on roads, observing heights and depths while viewing mountains and valleys during excursions, applying one's mind in daily life, and constantly directing one's eyes to observation. Through this, one naturally becomes skilled in the technique.

Also, this method has the practice called "shi, kan, satsu" (seeing, observing, examining). This refers to the mental attitudes during the beginning, middle, and end stages of preliminary measurement. Although these three characters appear in classical texts and their etymology is unclear,

when speaking of their meaning as borrowed for use in this method: "Shi" (seeing) means knowing through immediate visual measurement without engaging in various deliberations. [Marginalia: "Not engaging in various deliberations" means not using personal thoughts and judgments. "Immediate visual measurement" means knowing through a single surface glance. Therefore, although this method seems easy, because it is done with selflessness, depending on the item and situation, it has advantages that surpass even the two methods of observation and examination. The daily practice mindset is most important.]

"Kan" (observing) means knowing by exploring the origins of this place and making detailed comparisons with that place. [Marginalia: "Origins of this place" means exploring the reasons behind the target points and base positions. "Making detailed comparisons with that place" means comparing the distances of other places with the measurements of this location. Therefore, this method should be understood as having a somewhat deeper mindset than the seeing method.]

"Satsu" (examining) means knowing by considering the good and bad aspects of geographical features, examining the appropriateness of astronomical conditions, and applying deep mental attention. [Marginalia: Geographical features include height, depth, breadth, distance, steepness, difficulty, flatness, and ease. Astronomical conditions include clear skies, cloudy weather, post-rain, snowy days, etc. Therefore, this method surpasses the two methods of seeing and observing and is quite profound. When one becomes well-versed in this examining method, one can know in advance the general distances, widths, heights, and depths without waiting for the main techniques. Students should strive diligently.]

Generally, these three methods are the essential points of preliminary measurement. When one is not clear about these points, each technique lacks accuracy.
