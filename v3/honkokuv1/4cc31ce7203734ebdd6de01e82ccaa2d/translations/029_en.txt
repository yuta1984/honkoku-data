## Diagram of the Disassembled Surveying Board (Kenban)

(An exploded/disassembled view of the surveying board and its components)

Component names:
- Hozo-zan (tenon-type measuring rod)
- Ushi-tome (pin hole for securing the support brace)
- Hashira-tome (pin hole for securing the column/pillar)
- Ban-ura (back face of the board)
- Hashira (column/pillar)
  - Ryūban san-mizo (groove for the measuring rod on the vertical board), ushi-iri-ana (hole for the brace), dai-iri-hozo (tenon for the base)
  - Ushi-iri sen-ana (pin hole for the brace)
  - Heiban san-mizo (groove for the measuring rod on the horizontal board), heiban sen-ana (pin hole on horizontal board), ryūban sen-ana (pin hole on vertical board)
- Ushi (support brace/diagonal strut)
  - San-iri sen-ana (pin hole for the measuring rod)
  - San-hasami (measuring rod clamp), hashira-iri sen-ana (pin hole for the column)
- Sen (pins/pegs)
- Dai (base/stand)
  - Hashira-ana (hole for the column)
  - Kiri-kumi (interlocking joint/notched joint)

## Diagram of the Compass (Konhatsu) in the Closed Position

- Length: 5 sun 5 bu; thickness: slightly over 3 bu (square cross-section)
- When closed, the shape resembles a square pyramid (square cone)
- The head is shaped like a dragonfly's head; however, this may be adjusted according to preference

## Diagram of the Compass (Konhatsu) in the Open Position

Component names:
- Kaname (pivot/fulcrum)
- Sumi-mizo (ink groove)

## Ruler (Jōgi)

- Length: 1 shaku 8 sun 6 bu, graduated with carpenter's square (kanejaku) markings
- Width: slightly under 1 sun
- Thickness: 3 bu
