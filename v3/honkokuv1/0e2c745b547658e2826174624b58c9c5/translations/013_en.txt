Damage to Villages Along the Riverbanks: Regarding the flooding of the Tama River, while there are certainly no small number of victims not only in Kita-Tama District but also in the villages along the banks, since the elevation of the land is not uniform, it did not sweep uniformly from one side to the other as in Minami-Katsushika District, causing widespread damage to the majority of houses in all villages. Even when an embankment on one side collapsed, the flood was held back by continuous hills or stopped by pine forests; and even when damage was done to fields and farmland, it did not easily reach the point of inundating houses, and on the rare occasions when it did, the extent was not so great. This naturally made rescue operations somewhat more manageable. Nevertheless, since these are all residents of the prefecture's towns and villages, the people of the prefecture must never view them with any sense of discrimination or preferential treatment. If anyone asks what the scene of the flooding looked like, the torrential rush of the mountain streams surpasses even the gentler overflow at Mukōjima. One might, in place of enjoying leisurely ayu (sweetfish) fishing, purposely take up a walking stick and go to view the traces of the flood.
