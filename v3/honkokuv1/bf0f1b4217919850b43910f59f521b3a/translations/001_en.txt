**Legend for Daimyo Residences**
■ = Upper residence (Kami-yashiki) ▲ = Middle residence (Naka-yashiki) ● = Lower residence (Shimo-yashiki)

Item 1: On the 15th day of the 1st month of Kōka 3 (1846), the year of Hinoe-Uma, at around the middle of the Hitsuji hour (approximately 2:00 PM), a fire broke out from Maruya­ma in Hongō. At the time, a fierce northwest wind was blowing, causing the fire to spread into a great conflagration. The fire was finally extinguished near Kyōbashi on the following day, the 16th, at the Saru hour (approximately 4:00 PM). This is a matter deserving the utmost caution.
Even the smallest smoldering tobacco ember must be handled with great care; one must never be negligent with fire. Guarding against fire diligently is of the utmost importance. The blessings of this peaceful age must never be forgotten.

Item 2: By the order of the Lord (the Shogun), relief shelters were established at three locations for the assistance of all those affected, and food was distributed both day and night. This is truly an immeasurable act of great benevolence, for which we are deeply grateful.

Item 3: Locations of the three relief shelters:
Nihonbashi Yoka-ichi, Hatchōbori, Matsuyachō, and Kanda Sakuma-chō
