The flames blazed up, winds arose and waves became turbulent, frightening all manner of scaled sea creatures in the corners of the sea. It was as if snow were being scattered across the blue sky and lights were being lit between the waves. Various sea creatures touched by those flames raised their scales in vain, scattering wave-flowers, and the foul odor spread throughout the coastal area. Between the year Kanoto-Hitsuji of Emperor Ninken (491 CE) and the first year of Taihō, Kanoto-Ushi of Emperor Monmu (701 CE), there lived in the capital a novice monk named Chijin who had one son. From the time the child was crawling about in swaddling clothes, he refused meat and fish in his mouth and declined fine silk garments for his body. Both parents found this strange, so they had him enter the Buddhist priesthood as a disciple of the elderly priest Genri. He recited the Hōkō Sutra daily, and having already completed ten thousand volumes of sutras, he was given the title "Priest Mangen" and became the fourth patriarch of Hakone. In the first year of Tenpyō-shōhō, Tsuchinoto-Ushi (749 CE) during the reign of Empress Kōken, Mangen made a pilgrimage to the sacred shrine of Kashima in Hitachi Province.
