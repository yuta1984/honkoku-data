## The Origin of the Hot Springs of Atami in Izu Province

In the fourth year of the reign of Emperor Ninken, a man known as Kashima no Bobeno Kimi was found guilty of a crime and died in prison. However, the Emperor's wrath was not yet appeased, and by imperial decree, his remains were to be cast into the depths of the coastal waters of Kamo District in Izu Province (Tōshū). The officials, setting their poles to their boats and navigating against the swirling, surging tides, attempted to sink the body in the waters — whereupon it instantly burst into fierce, blazing flames, and with scorching fire…
