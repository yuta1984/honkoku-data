○Methods of Bathing and Internal Consumption
Although differences in temperature, frequency of bathing, duration, and so forth must inevitably be made according to differences in symptoms and physical constitution, following for now the explanation of Dr. Hoffmann, the appropriate temperature is generally between 98 and 100 degrees Fahrenheit (that is, 29 to 30 degrees Celsius, or 37 to 38 degrees on the Réaumur scale), bathing once to two or three times daily, with a duration of approximately 15 to 16 minutes up to 30 to 40 minutes being considered appropriate. For those who take it internally, the daily dosage should be 4 to 12 ounces (since 1 ounce equals approximately 7 monme, 5 fun, 6 rin, and 5 mō in Japanese units, the appropriate daily drinking amount should be from approximately 1 gō 5 shaku up to 4 to 5 gō), taken once or twice daily. It should be taken either straight or diluted with water, in the morning or morning and evening.

After bathing, one should dress quickly and absolutely avoid immediate exposure to the outside air. Bathers are cautioned not to seek momentary trivial pleasures in a way that would diminish the therapeutic effects of the hot spring cure. There is also something to which bathers must pay careful attention — namely, what is commonly known as "yu-atari" (hot spring shock).
