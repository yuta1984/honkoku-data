○Site of the Former Imperial Lodge　Located south of Shinshuku. According to the "Izu-shi," the Third Shogun (Tokugawa Iemitsu) wished to bathe in the hot springs, and in Kan'ei 3 (1626), he ordered the Sakuma clan to construct facilities for this purpose. Although construction was completed, due to certain circumstances, the shogun's visit never materialized, and later he ordered the structures to be demolished. Prior to this, in Keichō 9 (1604), the Third Month, the Divine Ancestor (Ieyasu) took Gorotamaru Yoshitoshi and Chōfukumaru Yorinobu with him on his journey to Kyoto, passing through Atami and bathing in the hot springs for seventeen days. Perhaps the Third Shogun was following this precedent. Documents related to the construction of that time are said to be preserved by the Watanabe family. I examined them through copies held by the Uchida family. The site covers more than five tan, located in the center of Atami village, flat with particularly excellent views. Old trees line the northwest side, and outside them are traces of moats (locally called "hori"). There are also traces of a riding ground nearby. Recently, Atami School has been built on this historic site. The school building is magnificent and academic activities appear to be somewhat flourishing.

The land of Atami is extremely narrow, surrounded by mountains on three sides, all of which are barren peaks. One side opens to the sea, but this is limited to fishing. Agricultural land remains scarce. How much less could there be room for producing other goods?
