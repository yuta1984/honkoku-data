There are also several types of chopsticks made from Japanese blue oak (aoki) and nagi wood. All can be manufactured in any way to meet the needs of those staying at the hot spring baths.

○ Ganpi Paper: Made using jimmen (ganpi fiber). Jimmen is called "kago" in Japanese and commonly referred to as "ganpi." The tree resembles cherry and produces leaves in the fourth month. This tree grows in various mountainous areas, but cannot be used until it is ten years old. Therefore, mitsumata and kozo are mixed with it for papermaking, though what is called "usuyō" (thin paper) uses pure jimmen alone. The beginning of papermaking in Atami originated from the creative idea of Shibano Ritsuzan, carried out by Imai Handayū, also known as Sensai or Tokuō. He established this industry, opened a shop in Edo Honchō, and distributed the paper for sale through Kinkadō, Haibara, and other establishments. Since then, the number of people engaged in this trade has gradually increased, and currently Imai Handayū, Edoya Kichibei, Uematsu Gohei, Kamikado Zenkichi, Akutagawa Yoshigorō and others are said to be devoting their full efforts to this work.

○ Wall Clay: The local mountains, valleys, fields and gardens of Atami produce this soil everywhere. There are approximately seventeen varieties including blue, yellow, red, white, and other intermediate colors. All create beautiful and durable wall coatings. Among them are gold and silver-colored varieties that add brilliant luster sufficient to enhance the beauty of palace rooms.
