○Tenjin Shrine: Located in the eastern part of Hamamachi, enshrining Sugawara no Michizane. According to tradition, when Sugawara no Michizane was exiled to Tsukushi (Kyushu), he carved seven self-portraits. The image enshrined in this shrine is said to be one of them. The shrine was originally located on Tenjinyama in Wada, but has been moved to its present location since early modern times. The Uchida family manages the religious ceremonies.

○Shrine of Ki no Kakinomoto no Hitomaro: Located south of Onaribashi in Yokocho, enshrining Kakinomoto no Hitomaro. According to local tradition, the monk Shinzei was once implicated in an incident involving Empress Somedono, was exiled here, and eventually died. This tradition is greatly mistaken. According to monastic records, Shaku Shinzei was from Yamashiro Province, the son of Danjō-daihitsu Ki no Misono, and one of the ten great disciples of Kōbō Daishi. During the Jōwa era, he received imperial orders to travel to Tang China, and upon his return was appointed head priest of Tōji Temple, became a monk-administrator (sōjō), and passed away at Takao Jingoji Temple on February 25th of Jōgan 2.

The "Honchō Kōsōden" (Biographies of Eminent Japanese Monks) states: "People say that Shinzei was deluded by lust and became a demon, but I have always doubted this. [omitted] After reading the 'Zenke Hiki' cited in the 'Shingon Den,' my doubts were finally resolved. According to it, a monk from Mount Kinpu, while using incantations to cure Empress Fuji's illness, saw her face and fell in love, becoming a demon and entering her sleeping quarters. Kiyoyuki was a great scholar of his time and his observations were not mistaken. It is certain that this matter was unrelated to Shinzei." This explanation is sufficient to correct the false tradition. However, it is still unclear why this monk is enshrined in this place. [Perhaps it derives from a corruption of the story of Ki no Hiroaki; this still requires investigation.]

As for the claim that a certain stone in the precincts of the Tenjin Shrine in Hamamachi is the tomb of Empress Somedono, such extreme fabrication is hardly worth discussing.
