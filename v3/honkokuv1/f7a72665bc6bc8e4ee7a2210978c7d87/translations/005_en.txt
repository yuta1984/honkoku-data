People lit their paper lanterns and went around to check on nearby friends and relatives—a gesture that was mutual among all. It was quite a curious sight to see dogs crawling up to people who had collapsed and were sleeping in the streets. The water in all the wells had turned muddy. Those who had regained some composure boiled tea, cooked rice, and shared sake in the main streets, and were finally able to get some food down their throats. Yet their hearts remained unsettled, as the ground continued to tremble incessantly with a rumbling "doro-doro" sound. Throughout the neighborhoods, people were strictly required to patrol the streets, striking wooden clappers to warn against fire. From somewhere, an elderly person came and offered protective charms. The verse they shared read:

　　Though it shakes, it shall never come loose, the keystone,
　　　　As long as the god of Kashima abides.

Everyone copied this verse down and pasted it on the pillars of their doorways or on the central pillars of their homes. With great reverence, people placed the sacred paper amulets of the Grand Shrine of Amaterasu upon their heads, and tucked protective secret inscriptions into their hair-topknots. Those with elderly persons, the sick, or young children to care for were overcome with indescribable anxiety. Among the people, some were crushed under collapsed houses, others were caught between fallen objects, pushed against walls...
