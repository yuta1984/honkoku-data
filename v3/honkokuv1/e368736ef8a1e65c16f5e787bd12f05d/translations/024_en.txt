○Figure 1 illustrates that the visible range in each of the four directions spans ninety degrees. Since each direction covers ninety degrees, the four directions together total three hundred and sixty degrees. This figure remains unchanged in Chinese, Western, and all other astronomical theories.

○Figure 2 demonstrates that the arc from the zenith down to the horizon also spans ninety degrees. This has been created purely for the convenience of measurement by the ordinary human eye, and does not mean that the fundamental nature of the vast heaven and earth is fixed in such a manner. Nor does it mean that the four directions above and below actually form such a circular shape; rather, the diagram is drawn here simply to convey the degree of the range of vision.

○What is shown in Figure 3 is, first and foremost, that a single glance can take in ninety degrees of view. For example, inside a square house, if one first looks to the East, then to the South, then to the West, and then to the North — after looking four times in total, one will have seen all four sides of the house, with nothing remaining
