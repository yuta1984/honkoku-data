【Left Page Main Text】

Objects appear larger, as shown in the previous figure. Furthermore, at the position of the focal point (chikiri), since the upper and lower lines of sight form a cross, objects cannot be seen. Beyond this focal point, objects of any size will appear upside down. The same applies to left and right.

Figure 9 demonstrates the evidence for the focal point (chikiri) position shown in the previous figure. First, pierce a small hole of slightly over two bu in a board, carefully determine the position of the focal point, and place it there. As shown in the figure, the upper and lower lines of sight converge at the center, form a cross, and emerge through the small hole, spanning broadly from earth to sky, allowing many objects to be seen. Consequently, no matter how large a mountain may be, it will appear entirely inverted when viewed through the small hole.
