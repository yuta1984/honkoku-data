**[Left page: Main text]**

(Continued from previous page) ...if made into two faces [of glass], it demonstrates that one object appears as three objects. First, through the central face of the three faces, one sees the actual fire (flame). The line of sight directed toward the upper face is bent and refracted by the thickness of the glass, so that the line of sight, which should have gone toward the sky, is deflected and instead sees the actual fire in the middle (at the person's level). Likewise, the line of sight from the lower face directed toward the ground is also bent toward the actual fire, and the lines of sight through all three faces converge at one point. Nevertheless, the upper and lower lines of sight do not perceive that they are seeing the actual fire, and one still believes there to be fire in three separate places — in the sky, on the ground, and at the middle (human) level.

○ Figure 11 is a diagram for viewing hidden objects. Place a *tenmoku* (tea bowl) on the far side of a folding screen, and step back until the *tenmoku* is no longer visible. Then, if a triangular prism of glass is stood upon the top of the folding screen, the *tenmoku*, which was hidden and invisible, will appear to rise up high and become visible. This is based on the same principle as the previous figure. The two should be studied together.
