【Left page, main text】

As shown in the diagram, with only the glass prism labeled 卯 (Mao), the celestial image at point 甲 (A) appears at point 乙 (B). When the glass labeled 寅 (Tora) is added and stacked upright, the image at 甲 appears at point 丙 (C). When the glass labeled 丑 (Ushi) is further added and stacked, the image at 甲 appears at point 丁 (D). When the glass labeled 子 (Ne) is also added and stacked, the image at 甲 appears at point 戊 (E). This is a point of great importance in the discussion of astronomy, and one should be careful to keep it in mind.

○ Figure 17 illustrates that even a single triangular glass prism exhibits changes in the apparent position of an image depending on the angle at which it is held. As shown in the diagram, when held perfectly upright, the displacement of the observed celestial image is small. When this glass is tilted, however, the displacement of the image becomes considerably greater.
