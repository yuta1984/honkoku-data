They went out to meet them and waited in anticipation. When the general with the white chrysanthemum banner advanced to Nagashima, two suspicious men came running frantically and said, "The bandits have already passed through Kawakubo and are heading to Sasai village. There is no need to worry about Iseyama. Please hurry to Sasai," then ran back. So they decided to divide their forces - they sent forty-odd men with the wisteria banner to Iseyama, while fifty-odd men with the white chrysanthemum banner headed toward the Sasai entrance. This intelligence was later realized to have been false information spread by spies sent by the bandits.

Meanwhile, the general with the wisteria banner rushed to Iseyama, guided by the beacon fires. However, intimidated by Rokuzō's strategy, the bandits hesitated to advance and stood still for a while. At that moment, the general with the wisteria banner charged straight through and cut down the banner that had been advancing ahead. Thinking their general had been killed, they fled downstream along the riverbed. At Sasai bridge, both generals with the rice ear and white chrysanthemum banners sat on camp stools giving commands, so they fled further downstream. When they tried to cross the pursuit bridge, two local militia generals with forty-odd men were waiting with beacon fires lit.
