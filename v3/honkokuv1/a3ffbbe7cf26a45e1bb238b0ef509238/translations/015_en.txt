The flames rose up. By the light of that fire, [the rioters] shouted "Let's cross over Iseyama and head to Ueda!" and departed in a tumultuous procession, roughly with heaven leading the way. This fire was visible all the way to Ueda.

The fifteen samurai—Yoshida, Horitani, Sasaki, Sato, Kobayashi and others—who had previously requested permission to defend [the domain] came forward and said: "We have just observed the situation, and it appears that the bandits have burned down Yokoo and are crossing Iseyama to advance toward the castle town. There is no time to spare. Please grant us fifteen men leave of absence so that we may work to our full capacity, eliminate all of those insolent ruffians, and restore peace and tranquility to the domain. Even if something should happen to us, since this would be the work of masterless samurai, it should not cause any significant harm to our lord."

While the castle was deliberating on this matter, reports from the domain came in one after another like the teeth of a comb. The disturbance had already completely burned down Sanada and Yokoo and appeared to be advancing over Iseyama and Kawakubo. Successive urgent reports requested that defensive forces be dispatched immediately. The chief retainer then declared: "Now there is no other choice. [The rioters] will enter the domain and likely advance all the way to the castle town. Let each [of you] do as you wish."
