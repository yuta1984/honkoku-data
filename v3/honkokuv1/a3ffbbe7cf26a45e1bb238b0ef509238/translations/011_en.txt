"This would be a disgrace lasting for generations," he said, taking out twenty ryo of gold and seeing them approaching with their banners raised. He called out in a loud voice, "Everyone, you have worked so hard. Though it is modest, I offer twenty ryo of gold as congratulations, so please return peacefully. If you are still dissatisfied, that too would not be tragic. As long as this old man's arms can endure, it would be my heart's desire." Hearing this, two gentle men stepped forward and said, "We gratefully receive your admirable words and thoughtfulness," took the gold, restrained the crowd, and departed.

At the Yajima administrative office, there was only one magistrate present, but he could do nothing and remained with clenched fists. These rioters have not slept for four days and four nights from the first day until today, yet they feel no drowsiness. Like storms blowing through Mount Muro and the Tatsuta River, or in the depths of Mount Tamuke in Kokura where stag calls echo clearly across the waterfall shrine - the end is unknown. At dawn's sacred fence, this very arm entices them, and they trample between the male and female stones, creating an uproar as they climb up, peering here and there around Hara village, heading toward Sanada with pity.
