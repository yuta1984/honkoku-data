At this point, the Great Elder said: "This disturbance has not involved any violence against the domain lords or local magistrates, but is merely peasants stirring up trouble due to some grievance among them. We should not particularly take up arms to defend against this. To employ military force and make a great show of defense in such matters would seem rather cowardly. If a great army were to rise up, what would we use to defend against that? Of course, even if they are common people, if they plot rebellion against the realm, or if any domain lord or magistrate faces opposition, it would be proper to defend against and capture them. But there is no reason to mobilize military force when there is no such situation." Upon hearing this, a group captain stepped forward and said: "Indeed, while peasant uprisings may not be trivial matters, great troubles arise from small ones, and as the saying goes, 'even a dyke can collapse from a single ant hole,' so leaving this alone might lead to incalculable future regret. Moreover, would it not show insufficient military authority to fold our arms and watch while the peasants in our domain are being trampled?"
