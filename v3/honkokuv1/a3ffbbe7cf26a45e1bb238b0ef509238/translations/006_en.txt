As they passed through, young samurai secretly came out and, seeing this, clamored to disperse the crowd. However, it was difficult to go against their lord's will, so they suppressed their feelings and held back. When the rebels quickly left Shinmachi, one young samurai secretly emerged and fired a gun, causing everyone to panic and flee while wearing straw mats. A reclusive scholar in the castle town composed the following verse, modeling it after Li Bai's "Crows Cawing at Night":

Broken Platform - Confusion by Profit
Enemies around Komoro Castle seek to enter, then fly back to stand in the marketplace
Actors remove their caps to fortify the town, holding back cavalry to intimidate distant people
When one alone fires an iron cannon, they flee like rain

The disturbance soon pressed toward Shinbari. Under cover of night, the rebels again gathered from various directions, becoming an exceptionally large force. Here, Sozaemon the sake dealer was a famously wealthy man, so his main residence, including the storehouse and toilet, were all built with earthen walls, and his reception rooms were perfected in both goodness and beauty. When he heard the disturbance was coming, he called together young men from the neighborhood who frequented his establishment, placing a large number on the roof.
