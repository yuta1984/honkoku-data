When they demanded "bring out the sake," the proprietor of Yomogiya brought out what sake he had on hand, but they said "this amount of sake isn't even enough to wet our lips," seized the proprietor and threw him about, beating him up. They crushed Yomogiya like moxa, trampled and scattered everything, threw miso, soy sauce, and all household implements into the well with a splash, and looted and scattered all miscellaneous goods without leaving anything behind.

After that, they decided to "smash up" Watanabe Bunemon's house as well, but Bunemon fled near the bridge. However, their hearts were fierce as demons or the Rashomon gate, using bridge ropes as stern ropes of empty boats, determined to cross even by tying them together, and they pressed forward. Bunemon had hidden his household goods outside beforehand, concealed the rice and wheat he had stockpiled under the veranda, and stored clothing and household items in the storehouse. Thinking they wouldn't cross the river, he hesitated for a moment, but when a large crowd quickly approached and tightly surrounded him with bamboo poles, thinking he would be killed, he felt as if demons had seized his arms and fled for dear life.

When Watanabe fled, his wife ran around clutching a frying pan, spinning about dangerously in a comical manner. Afterward, the large crowd rushed in chaotically, piled up sliding doors, paper screens, and rice bales in front of the storehouse, and set fire to it from all directions.
