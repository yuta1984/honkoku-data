Furthermore, even if they were to send defensive forces now, these were merely ignorant peasants and servants. Using blades is not the way of the warrior. Therefore, they should try as much as possible to persuade them to return. However, if they proved stubborn and committed violent acts, then at that time they would deal with it as necessary - with this understanding, deployments were ordered. First, to Mount Ise and Kawakubo, over a hundred cavalry were sent under two generals bearing the white chrysanthemum and wisteria banners. To the bridge at Sasai Village, two generals from the rural areas were sent with forty-four men. To Akezawa Great Bridge, a single mounted warrior called "First Warbler Song in Winter Sky" was sent with over fifty men in firefighting gear.

The total force consisted of six mounted warriors and over two hundred common soldiers, all wearing leather haori jackets and leather hakama, or war hats and leather hoods, each equipped with lamellar armor. They departed Ueda on the night of October 5th around the Hour of the Boar, heading east - a scene both bitter and valiant.

Here, the village headman of Mount Ise, a man named Rokuzō who was quick-tempered, saw the riot approaching. He had the elderly, wives, and children light fires in several hundred places on the mountain plateau to make it appear like a large army, while he himself led a large group of young men toward the bridge at Kawakubo.
