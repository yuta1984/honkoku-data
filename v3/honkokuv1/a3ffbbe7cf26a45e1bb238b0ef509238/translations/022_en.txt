They mistook [the situation] and [thinking] "Now it's a disturbance!" rushed with great vigor all the way to Hachiman and pressed forward. Their spirit was like Sekigahara, Nitta's departure was like Yashima, Dannoura, Mount Oeyama - though they knew not the rivers, [they fought like] Hirai no Yasumasa, Tsuna, Kimitoki at Umakutsuzawa, feeding horses, planning to ambush the Takeda forces and Koshu forces as they headed upstream toward the river. Ashida Hachigaya broke through the boat landing at Shigezawa, and when they heard that a disturbance had come, Ushiga Pond also emerged and shot thin arrows, chasing toward Nagase and pressing forward to Nagase. Seeing this, Nagase Maruko cried out that it was Koshu forces, and Yaehara sent reinforcements in multiple layers to various directions. Seeing this, the Shiokawa forces [thought] "The enemy is in front, Koshu forces behind - what shall we do?" and panicked. Iinuma Nakayama said forces were pressing from Shioda and prepared meals, set up small pots, and pushed up to Sunahara Pass. The townspeople thought these were Koshu forces and roused the five villages, throwing stones to skewer them. Ishigami Village rang bells and emerged.
