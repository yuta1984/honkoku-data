He declared that if it came to going out resolutely to defend, they should capture the rioters one by one. The young samurai and group members agreed with this, all wanting to go out to defend and demonstrate their military prowess, each becoming excited and clamorous. Although the Great Elder tried to restrain them, they would not listen. The group commander said, "In any case, please command us to defend according to our wishes. If there should be any fault in this matter, I alone will bear the blame and commit seppuku." The Great Elder reluctantly said, "Then I will leave it to everyone's judgment. However, armor is not appropriate. Use firefighting equipment to fortify Yoraguchi."

Accordingly, they set up curtains at Yoraguchi, arranged various weapons, kept bows and guns at the ready, removed the sheaths from spears and long swords, attached fuses to guns, and waited in anticipation - appearing quite formidable. On October 4th, the riot passed from Shionada through Mimitori, trampled through Moriyama, and headed toward Komoro, reaching as far as Rokudohara. There, the defenses at Yoraguchi were extremely strong, and the sound of guns echoed through the mountains and fields.
