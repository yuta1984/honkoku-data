They set fires and with this light, they noisily crossed back over the Chikuma River. Swallows know the autumn days and leave their nests to depart, and chrysanthemums brave the rain to bloom for the Double Ninth Festival. Those who do not leave when they should leave, do not bloom when they should bloom, and do not know the proper timing for advance and retreat will often regret it later. As the saying goes, "A skilled hawk hides its talons," but the golden eagles with long talons had accumulated interesting spoils - grasping here and clutching gems there. The gold and jewels hidden beneath the jaw of the poisonous dragon they had seized were drawn out, and the various precious items scattered like souls flying away when the cord of jewels was cut, without knowing how fleeting it all was.

In the hour of the Tiger when the night wind blew wildly, the horizontal clouds parted and as dawn faintly broke over eastern Ueda, before the light of the morning sun could shine through, everyone scattered and fled. The remaining forty or fifty people finally broke into the house of Kodama Jōemon, a grain merchant, smashing forty or fifty sliding doors and paper screens. They destroyed Sōemon, the village headman of Karasu and Koeda villages, and since he dealt in paper, they damaged white paper, sliding doors, and Chinese paper. Paper pavilions and such were treated like waste paper, inner feelings were crumpled and scattered like wrapping paper and crepe paper, and they trampled over the tatami mats with muddy feet.
