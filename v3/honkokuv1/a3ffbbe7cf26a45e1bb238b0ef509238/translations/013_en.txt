As things developed, the retainers Yoshida, Horinoya, Sakuki, Kato, Kobayashi, and Ito stepped forward and said: "There is no need for deliberation about what we have or lack. We should immediately go out and capture them one by one to rescue our domain from this crisis. In Komoro, they are being too cautious and letting things pass as they are. The rebels, emboldened by their success, will certainly storm even the castle. In any case, we request that the fifteen of us be appointed to defend against them." However, the chief retainer and elder councilors all spoke in unison: "First, let us hold back for a while and observe the situation. If they press close to our vicinity, then we can deal with it somehow. There must be good reason why Komoro has let things pass as they have. There is no justification for us alone to rashly mount a defense." Thus they were sternly restrained.

However, the rebels rose up even more vigorously, growing to over two thousand people, and stormed into the house of Imajiro, a grain merchant in Sanada village. Imajiro was the wealthiest family in Sanada village and moreover lived on an elevated estate.
