The retainers Yoshida, Horino-ya, Sakuki, Kato, Kobayashi, and Ito stepped forward and argued: "There is no need for deliberation — we should immediately sally forth, arrest them one by one, and relieve the hardship in the domain. In Komoro, they made too great a matter of it and simply let them pass through. It is certain that these rioters, emboldened by their momentum, will break into the castle itself. We humbly request that we fifteen men be ordered to go and defend against them."

However, the senior councilors and elders all replied in unison: "Wait and observe the situation for the time being. If they press close, we can deal with it then. The fact that Komoro let them pass must surely have had its reasons. It would be improper for us to rashly attempt to block them on our own initiative," and firmly held them back.

Nevertheless, the rioters grew ever more bold, swelling to over two thousand men, and forced their way into the home of Imanijiro, a grain merchant in Sanada village. Imanijiro's was the largest household in Sanada village, situated on a slightly elevated plot of land. At dusk on the fifth day of the tenth month, a great mob of rioters surrounded the house, shouting that they would tear it apart.
