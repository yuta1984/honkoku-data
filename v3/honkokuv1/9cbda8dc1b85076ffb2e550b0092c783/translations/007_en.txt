During the time of Ise Hōjō, a bridge was constructed along the mountain path from Sōun-ji Temple to this hot spring, and Ujitsuna, Ujiyasu and others would bathe here when they occasionally visited Sōun-ji Temple.

○Nature of the Hot Spring: Contains iron
○Primary Therapeutic Effects: General physical weakness, muscle and bone relaxation, cold injuries, constipation, bruises, sprains, lower back pain, hernia

○Bathing Methods
Those who bathe in hot springs must strictly avoid overeating, moderate their food and drink, abstain from sexual activities, and engage in appropriate exercise. ○Generally, elderly people should bathe once a day, while young people should bathe three times a day as standard practice.
○Regardless of age, the appropriate temperature for hot springs is generally between 90 and 100 degrees Fahrenheit on the thermometer. ○After bathing, one should thoroughly dry the body with a dry cloth and never expose oneself to wind.
○When hot spring water is taken internally, it causes gentle diarrhea, which promotes intestinal and gastric function, aids digestion, and refreshes the spirit. Therefore, it is highly effective for those with constipation and plethoric conditions. ○The amount for internal consumption varies according to spring quality and symptoms, but generally one should not exceed 1 cup (equivalent to about 2 sake cups) to 5-6 cups per day. If one intends to drink 2-3 cups at once, first drink one cup and engage in light exercise...
