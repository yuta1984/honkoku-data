The Jizō Buddha statue was originally located near where the Ōchō inscription stone now stands, but during the medieval period it was moved to this upper road, making this the main path.

Shōji's Pond: Also called the Pond of Life and Death. Located below the Jizō Buddha statue, it measures four to five chō in width and over one chō in length. There is an ancient stone monument on the shore of the pond, called the Ōchō inscription stone, which people make paper rubbings of and treasure greatly. Its calligraphy is soft and extremely beautiful, appearing to be written by a woman's hand.

Twelve-Light Buddha: This was also originally beside this path, but now has been moved to beside the west side of the main highway. It is the central statue among the five halls, called the White Jizō.

Tomb of the Eight-Hundred-Year-Old Nun: The locals call it the Nun's Mound. It is located across from the Ōchō inscription stone, on the shore of Shōji's Pond. Recently, it is said that the local people use it as a surveying marker when measuring land.

Komagata Shrine: This was located on the summit of Mount Koma, but was recently moved to the west of Hakone Station. On the mountain summit, there remains a large stone with a horse carved into it, known as the Komagata inkstone.

Taikō's Saddle-rest Stone and Shoe-rest Stone: Located on Mount Koma, this is a strange rock that protrudes about three ken from the summit. It is said that when one tries to climb it, clouds immediately arise and one cannot see the shape of the mountain.
