From Tonosawa, a new road was opened down the mountain (with a width of three ken), allowing carriages and rickshaws to travel freely to Miyanoshita without having to cross steep cliffs. About one cho before Miyanoshita, there is a milestone, and from here the mountain path down to Dogashima is only three to four cho, but it is particularly steep with switchbacks. Dogashima is located in an extremely hollowed-out area, with the Hayakawa River flowing around it on three sides, giving it the appearance of an island. Across from it flows the stream from Mount Myojin-ga-take, and the Shiraito waterfall quietly cascades down from the mountainside. It is truly a place of deep mountains and secluded valleys, with a character quite different from the other hot springs of the Hakone Seven Springs. Recently, a new road was opened that emerges near the Naraya inn in Miyanoshita. This location is much flatter than the old road and the distance is shorter.

Map of Dogashima

This place originally had five hot spring inns, but currently there are three:
　　Yamatoya - Tametaro　　Omiya - Koto　　Edoya - Magosaburo

Additionally, the original hot spring inn Naraya run by Rokubei has apparently changed business, and the site of that establishment has become a villa belonging to a certain Hiramatsu (banker). It is an elegant and quiet residence that is most excellent. Also, a five-story building was constructed along this new road going up.
