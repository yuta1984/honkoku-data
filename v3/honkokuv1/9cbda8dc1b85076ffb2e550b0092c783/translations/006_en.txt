There is a bridge called Asahi Bridge. Once you cross the bridge, you reach Yumoto. This location is open to the east, with the steep peaks of Tō-no-mine rising to the north, Yamaishi-gakeyama visible to the south, and Shiroyama surrounding it to the west, with hot spring valleys bubbling up at its foothills. In front, the clear flow of the Hayakawa River runs north of the houses, creating particularly beautiful scenery.

This place has only 18 households, of which only two families, Fukuzumi and Ogawa, are called "uchiya" (private bath houses). The remaining 6 or 7 establishments are all "sotoyu" (public baths) and are quite quiet.

Private Bath Houses: Ogawa Manemon, Fukuzumi Kuzō

This Fukuzumi recently constructed a Western-style three-story stone building, sparing no expense on its architectural beauty, naming it Kinsenrō, and also serves Western cuisine.

Map of Yumoto

The hospitality for travelers is most cordial and attentive. Therefore, it is said that there is never a day without bathers.

Sōyu (Main Public Bath): Located between Kumano Gongen mountain and the residence of Ogawa Manemon, the interior is divided into four bathing areas.

Since everyone except the two private bath houses bathes here, it is called Sōyu (main bath). This bath was formerly called Sōun-ji Ashiarai-yu (Sōun Temple Foot-washing Bath).
