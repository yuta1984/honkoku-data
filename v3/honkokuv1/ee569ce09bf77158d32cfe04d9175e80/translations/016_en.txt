Some people avoid three-tatami rooms even when they face auspicious directions, saying that three tatami represents the number of fire. As an example of using water-related elements in dwellings, many roof tiles are made with tomoe (comma-shaped) patterns, which represent the form of waves. Blue ocean wave patterns are also common on entrance decorations. However, while six-tatami rooms represent the water number, when I examined a narrow house with two six-tatami rooms, the yin energy predominated and it was extremely inauspicious.

When moving to a new house, one must also avoid fire-related elements, so days of three, and the days of hinoe, hinoto, mi, and uma should be avoided. The days of mizunoe, mizunoto, i, and ne represent water and are auspicious. During such occasions, grilled fish should be avoided, and for hanging scrolls, ink paintings and calligraphy works are good.
