[Publisher/Location information in split writing: Sanchōme, Tachibana-chō] [Second generation] Takaoka Kon'gen; [Kihara shop, Nihonbashi] Shikei Jirō, the Right-Way Hermit, author. "Takuso Sokuden" (Quick Transmission of House Physiognomy). Almanac selections. Recently published.

Some people avoid rooms of three-mat (tatami) size even in auspicious directions, considering three to be a number of Fire. It is customary to use water associations in the home — for example, many roof tiles are made with the tomoe (comma-shaped) design representing waves, and the aonami-ha (blue wave) pattern is often used for entryway decorations as well. However, six-mat rooms correspond to the number of Water; yet when I tested a narrow house that had two six-mat rooms, it was excessively yin and extremely inauspicious.

When moving into a new house, since fire associations are to be avoided, one should avoid the days of "three," as well as the cyclical days of Hinoe (丙), Hinoto (丁), Mi (巳), and Uma (午). The days of Mizunoe (壬), Mizunoto (癸), I (亥), and Ne (子) belong to Water and are auspicious. On such occasions, grilled fish dishes are unnecessary; hanging scrolls, ink paintings, and calligraphy are appropriate. Furthermore, regarding the directions of Ryōkonjin, Tsune-konjin, Saiha, Fusagari, and the master's Honmei-tekisetsu, one should move on an auspicious day that has been selected beforehand.
