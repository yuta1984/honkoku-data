First, water should be carried in; second, fire, horses, swords, attendants, and miscellaneous goods; and then the master of the house enters. Not a single person should enter empty-handed.

For people of middle or lower social standing, the order is: water, fire, miscellaneous goods, attendants, living creatures, and finally the master of the house.

When the monk Kūkai (Kōbō Daishi) was forty-two years old, he received an imperial command to write the plaque for the Daigokuden (Great Audience Hall). It is said that the character "dai" (大, meaning "great") was split in the middle, making it read "Kagokuden" (火極殿, "Fire Extreme Hall"), and because of this, the imperial palace subsequently burned down.

On commonly used land, even if the width is narrow, alternately placing stone bridges and earthen bridges would serve as preparation for emergencies. Wheeled vehicles may not even be necessary.

The above shows only the major inauspicious years among the Nine Luminaries. The Mars Star does not represent a fatal directional conflict, but there will be fire disasters, illness, and disputes. Even if one encounters other stars' inauspicious years, if one performs the star festival (hoshi matsuri) at the winter solstice each year, practices the releasing of living creatures (hōjō), and performs hidden virtuous deeds (intoku), the Seven Calamities will undoubtedly be immediately extinguished.
