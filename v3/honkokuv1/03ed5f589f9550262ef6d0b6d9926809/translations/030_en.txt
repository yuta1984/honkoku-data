**On the Phenomenon of Luminous Objects**

Someone asked: On summer nights, there are things called "luminous objects" (hikari-mono), which are seen frequently from time to time. Furthermore, there is what the common people call "hitodama-bi" (spirit-fire / will-o'-the-wisp), a fire that flies through the sky. Many people say that in the place where it falls, there is something resembling charcoal. What sort of thing might this be?

In response: It is a fact that things called "luminous objects" do exist. I myself have seen them from time to time. These are the great yang fire-energy (火気) that exists underground, which bursts and splits forth, rising from below upward toward the heavens. As it rises and there is no moisture above, when it becomes hot and dry, it takes on a form and becomes what people today commonly call "yobai-boshi" (shooting stars / night-flowing stars). These "yobai-boshi" are not stars; they are yang fire that thrusts upward from the earth. Their height is not at the same level as the sun and moon — they are far lower than the sun and moon. For this reason, one cannot observe them by changing one's location. They are visible only in the place where that yang energy rises. They are like the halo around the moon.
