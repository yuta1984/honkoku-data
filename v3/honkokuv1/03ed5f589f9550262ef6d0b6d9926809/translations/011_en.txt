Further question: If thunder is so venerable, why do people fear and dread it?

Answer: Thunder is a medicine for the circulation of vital energy and blood. However, when it falls, it causes harm. This is like how medicine can cause harm when it does not suit the illness. That people fear it greatly depends on their innate disposition, but mostly it is young people who particularly fear it. The reason young people fear it is that yang energy is in excess within them, and they therefore dislike the yang energy from outside. There is also such a thing as an innate aversion. Even with medicines one takes regularly, some people do not particularly dislike them, while others dislike them intensely. In general, whether one fears thunder or not cannot be attributed to a person's bravery or timidity. Even a warrior as courageous as Cao Cao of Wei deeply feared thunder, and it is said that when thunder rumbled while he was eating, he would forget to put down or pick up his spoon. This is an innate aversion. This is not to say that fearing thunder is good, nor that facing it boldly is good either. Already……
