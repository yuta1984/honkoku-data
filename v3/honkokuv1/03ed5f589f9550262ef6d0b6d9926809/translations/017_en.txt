**On the Theory of the Moon**

A questioner asked: All people in the world say that the moon belongs to the realm of yin and is a form made of accumulated water-vapor. However, there is such a thing as a lunar eclipse. Or, regarding the "cassia tree of the moon" (the dark patterns on the moon's surface), there are dark regions within the moon. Furthermore, even though the moon is always the same moon, the moon of the fifteenth day of the eighth month is specially called the "harvest moon," and both Japan and China celebrate it by composing Chinese verse and Japanese poetry, admiring it with exceptional appreciation—what is the reason for this?

In answer, it was said: The moon is the essence and vital energy of the Great Yin and Water. Generally speaking, the fire-energy beneath the earth rises up to become the sun, and the water-energy rises up to become the moon. As for lunar eclipses, there exists a method of astronomical calculation (suiho no jutsu), and if one applies arithmetic to examine them, one can predict them without error even to the finest degree.
