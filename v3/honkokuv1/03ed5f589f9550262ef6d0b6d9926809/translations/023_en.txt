Furthermore, there is a popular belief that "a house over which a rainbow hangs will surely suffer misfortune," but there is no reason why good or bad fortune should be determined by where a rainbow reaches. Long ago, there was a certain commoner whose conduct was habitually bad, and who was despised by the people of his village. One day, an official came to summon him on a matter of public business. The man thought to himself, "Surely my misdeeds have become known to the authorities, and I am being called in to have my crimes investigated," and he was greatly frightened. Unable to refuse, he went to the magistrate's office. However, his speech was halting and hesitant, and his manner appeared somehow suspicious. Therefore, the magistrate, setting aside the original official business, questioned him about the suspicious nature of his words, whereupon his evil deeds were exposed, and he ultimately fell into punishment. The official had summoned him for an entirely different matter of public business, but because the man's own accumulated wrongdoings weighed upon his conscience, he brought his own crimes to light.
