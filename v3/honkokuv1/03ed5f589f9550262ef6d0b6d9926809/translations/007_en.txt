There are those who claim to have seen the form of what fell, with some saying it resembled a cat, and others saying it resembled a wild boar. What is particularly strange is that in the vicinity where something has fallen, various objects said to be "drumsticks" (bachi) are sometimes found scattered about. Depending on the occasion, axes and wedges and such things may be found lying there. There are so many different theories that the confusion only deepens. What exactly is thunder?

In answer, it is said: Although thunder has been described in various ways through oral tradition, one should know that none of these explanations represents the true account. Master Cheng (Cheng Yi) stated:

Thunder is the mutual grinding of yin and yang, while lightning is the mutual striking of yin and yang. Thus both thunder and lightning are of the yang principle. However, because the accumulated and concentrated yin energy wraps around the yang, the yin and yang grind against each other, producing sound. The force of the yang trembling within the enveloping yin becomes light. To use an analogy: when using a flint to strike fire, the sound of stone and metal grinding together is like thunder, and the spark that flies out is like lightning. This is what is meant by the saying "when thunder peals swiftly, the lightning is also fierce" —
