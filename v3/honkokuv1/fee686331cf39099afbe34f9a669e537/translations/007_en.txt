1. During a fire, evacuation sites for elderly people, wives, and children should be predetermined according to wind direction. Otherwise, one will suffer hardship wandering about searching for a place to go. When evacuating, the master should carry a spear, the wife should carry a naginata or wooden sword, or perhaps a stick. Even women may wear swords at their waist. Of course, what should be carried out first are the most important items: official red seals, black seals, documents, records, commendations, certificates, and gold and silver, etc. These should be put in a bag and hung around the neck so the cord cannot be seen (the bag's cord should also be well-twisted so it won't break, because there are many people with evil intentions). In addition, one should take along spare kosode robes, food, money, etc. in appropriate amounts. One must never evacuate empty-handed. If there are many people, a banner should be raised, and at night, lanterns bearing the family crest should be used to keep everyone well together in one group during evacuation.
