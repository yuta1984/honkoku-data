Three-shaku hand towels, footwear, etc., one set per person, should be tied with an obi sash and placed in advance in a bamboo basket (tsuzura), with a sign written in large characters saying "Change of clothes for when fire breaks out" pasted on top, so as not to panic in case of sudden fire.

1. Those who do not have leather hoods should wear cotton-lined hoods with neck guards attached. Women should cover their heads with wet cotton hand towels and wrap their stomachs with three-shaku hand towels. Otherwise, if hair catches fire, it cannot be extinguished and will cause great difficulty, so this should always be kept in mind.

1. Both men and women should always keep three-shaku hand towels, cotton tabi socks, thin rope, and footwear within easy reach.

1. When the wind is strong, morning and evening meals should be finished early. Of course, from spring until the end of April, extra food should be prepared for both morning and evening meals.

1. During spring, pots and kettles that are not in regular use should be filled with water. In case of fire, they will not be damaged even if left as they are...
