1. There is an excellent substitute for earthen doors called "doro-momen" (mud cotton). Take three layers of old cotton cloth, sew them together in a grid pattern of vertical and horizontal lines at intervals of two to three sun, calculate four to five sun extra beyond the measurements of the opening it is to cover, attach a thin drawstring around the perimeter as a hem, and prepare it so that the hemming cords can be hung evenly on nails on all four sides. In the event of a fire, put water (one to), salt (one sho), and dried powdered earth (one sho) into a large half-cask or old barrel, mix them together, soak the aforementioned cotton cloth in this mixture, hang it over the opening, and if time permits, repeatedly pour water over it from both inside and outside. It is superior to earthen doors.
During normal times, fold it up and attach a label indicating its corresponding opening on a wooden tag - it will not be in the way, and it is most convenient
