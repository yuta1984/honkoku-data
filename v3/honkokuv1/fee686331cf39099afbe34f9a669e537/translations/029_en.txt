These experiences have been collected and written down somewhat randomly to be passed down and taught to descendants. Fire is the most serious among all disasters, so one must be extremely careful day and night without fail. Since it concerns the peace of the nation and is for the sake of both others and oneself, this booklet should be read aloud to household members two or three times each month to ensure their understanding, and they should teach others as well. In daily life, one must never be careless, and when a fire breaks out, one should quickly help prevent it with sincere care, making no distinction between close and distant acquaintances.

Such good deeds would be limitless. Should a great fire occur, tens of thousands of people would suffer grief, and how much wealth would be lost? Therefore, one should prevent fires as earnestly as if rescuing flames that had caught one's own hair. By doing so, one would surely communicate with the divine spirits and receive help from Heaven without doubt.
