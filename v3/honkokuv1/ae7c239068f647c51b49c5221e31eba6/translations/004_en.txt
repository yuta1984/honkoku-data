Because of this, the explanation becomes all the more difficult. When this model is used for explanation, there is no need to imagine the positions of lines, planes, and solids in space, and I believe that understanding these phenomena will thereby become considerably easier.

The size of each part of this model is impossible to construct in proportion to its natural size. Objects such as water droplets and ice crystals have been made considerably larger in order to make the path of light rays easier to see. Also, since all distances have been shortened, it is likewise unavoidable that the angles formed between the directions of the light rays may become slightly larger or slightly smaller than they would be in nature.

One further point worth mentioning here is that this model was made entirely for the purpose of demonstrating to beginners, and for those who have studied even a little solid geometry, it is not particularly necessary.

　　　Explanation of the Rainbow

A rainbow is a luminous arc that appears on the side of the sky opposite the sun when there are rain clouds present, and is a phenomenon that displays the seven colors of the spectrum. The violet is on the inner edge of the arc, and red is on the outer edge. The diameter of this arc is always constant, and its center lies on the straight line connecting the center of the sun and the observer's eye. Therefore, the lower the sun is, the higher the rainbow should appear, and when the sun rises to a certain height above the horizon, no rainbow will form. The rainbow referred to here is the first rainbow, or the inner rainbow. Sometimes a second rainbow, namely the outer rainbow, can be seen.
