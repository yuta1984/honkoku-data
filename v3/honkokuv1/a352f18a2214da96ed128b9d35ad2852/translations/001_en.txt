Great Fire of Osaka

On the 25th day of the 2nd month of Ansei 5 (1858), at the upper hour of uma (around 11 AM), a fire broke out from a theater at the corner of Dotonbori. At that time, southwest winds were blowing violently, causing numerous flying sparks. Then from the upper hour of tori (around 5 PM), northeast winds became severe, and the fire was extinguished at the hour of ushi (around 2 AM).
