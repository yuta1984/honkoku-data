## Seeds of Tales for Generations: The Great Earthquake

**Doshomachi, Sendan-no-ki-suji (Chinaberry Tree Street)**
A high earthen wall collapsed for approximately ten ken (roughly 18 meters).

**Junkeimachi, southeast corner of Donike**
Two private residences suffered major damage.

**Temma, Fudoji Temple**
Suffered major damage. The principal Buddhist image (honzon) tilted into a diamond/rhombus shape.
The surrounding area saw widespread damage including structural warping, collapses, and destruction.

**Ebisu Shrine, Horikawa**
Minor damage to the shrine precinct. The surrounding area sustained scattered minor damage in all directions.

**Temma Myoken (Shrine)**
The votive tablet hall (emado) collapsed.

**Awajimachi**
The earthen storehouse (dozo) on the western side collapsed.

**Nagahori, north end of Sanoya Bridge**
Seven or eight tenement row houses (ura nagaya) suffered major collapse.

**Temma Tenjin Shrine**
The well-house structure and earthen storehouse collapsed. Further east into the temple district (teramachi), numerous temple gates and walls were damaged or destroyed. In this vicinity, many buildings sustained incremental damage and were left partially collapsed, with many rendered uninhabitable.

**Temma, Nishi-teramachi (West Temple Quarter)**
The votive tablet hall of Konpira Shrine collapsed. Several additional locations sustained damage.
