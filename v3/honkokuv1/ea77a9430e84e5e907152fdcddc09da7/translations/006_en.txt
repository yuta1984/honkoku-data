A Copy of Arima Hot Spring Instructions
Generally, when entering the hot springs, first cleanse yourself with pillow bath (light bathing), recite one volume of the Heart Sutra, the sacred name of Yakushi (Medicine Buddha), and the sacred name of Kannon (Goddess of Mercy), and then enter the bath. If there is urgent business, one should recite only the name of Yakushi. Hot springs exist in various provinces, but some are like water or have various flavored waters. However, this Arima is a salt spring, and if bathed in lightly according to one's condition, it warms coldness, removes dampness,

repels wind ailments, corrects blood circulation, increases vitality, and activates areas affected by lung disease - nothing surpasses the virtue of this hot spring. However, just because it is medicinal, if one bathes too intensely, sweat will pour out continuously, blood circulation will become agitated, the chest will feel congested, the heart will be distressed, vitality will be depleted, and harm will occur immediately. For example, sake has various medicinal properties, but in excess it becomes poison; salt is flavorful, but excess causes it to lose its taste.
Those who greatly engage in health cultivation should bathe about twice a day
