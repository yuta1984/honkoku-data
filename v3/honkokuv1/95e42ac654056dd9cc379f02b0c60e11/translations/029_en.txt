**○ Most Deaths Occurred During the Second Wave**
Those who had managed to escape initially, upon seeing the first wave recede, turned back to rescue their children or retrieve their belongings. At that very instant, the second great tsunami struck, and the death toll is said to have doubled at this moment.

**○ Complete Annihilation**
Households in which not a single family member survived numbered twenty-three in the sub-district of Kawamuki alone; the rest can be imagined accordingly.

**○ Incident at a Yamada Town Inn**
A traveler (aged about twenty-four or twenty-five) staying at Motonjuku Yahachi's inn heard that the tsunami was coming and tried to flee, but by then the seawater had already reached chest height, making even walking alone extremely difficult. At that moment, four women clung to him from both sides, leaving him utterly unable to move. Though he cried, "Let go! It's dangerous!" the women only clung more tightly, until he finally fell into the water. The group of five—one man and four women—were swept along six or seven meters before fortunately coming to rest atop a collapsed house, and all are said to have survived with their lives.

**○ A Tragic Incident**
Shiraito Sentaro of Yamada and three married couples in his household were cornered three times by houses swept toward them by the waves, but ultimately managed to escape. However, his aged father lost the use of his legs, and his wife suffered a miscarriage and died three days later.

**○ Corpse Found in a Rice Field**
After the disaster, when the mud in the rice paddies was dredged, the body of a woman clutching an infant was discovered. Though her face had changed, her clothing identified her as a woman named Man. It is said that when the tsunami struck, she fled at full speed, but as she was carrying an infant, she accidentally fell into the mud and mire, became unable to move freely, and was struck by the raging waves, meeting her end in this manner.

**○ A Tale of Utter Sorrow**
In Yamada there was a widow who turned forty that year. Having lost her husband, she had lived for over three years finding purpose in raising her five-year-old daughter and two-year-old son, looking forward to watching them grow. When the tsunami struck, the five-year-old was swept away by the waves while trying to escape, and the mother, clutching her two-year-old, was hurled into a rice paddy. All three—mother and children—were found lying dead one after another, which was truly the most pitiable of sights.

**[Lower Right Section]**

**○ The Devastation of Kamayado**
This is a sub-district at the northern tip of Yamada. Houses were destroyed, bodies were scattered everywhere, and the nearby rice paddies were completely washed away. The scene of devastation was too horrible to behold.

**○ Theft of Belongings and Money**
Having barely survived but having lost all food, clothing, and shelter, and falling into the depths of misery, some of the poor, as is unfortunately common in such circumstances, immediately lost their moral sense. Upon seeing chests of drawers and other items washed up at the foot of the mountains, many individuals immediately plundered money and clothing from within them. Given the magnitude of the disaster, police presence was insufficient, and dealing with this situation was said to be extremely difficult.

**○ Losses Among Village Office Staff**
Assistant Mayor Kando Tsutoya perished. Revenue Officer Abe Teruharu died just three days after taking up his post, and two additional clerks met the same fate.

**○ Various Tragic Accounts from Kawamuki, the Most Heavily Damaged Area of Yamada**
A physician named Seki Gentatsu lost thirteen family members while he alone survived unharmed. Kudo Gensada lost all nine family members. Suzuki Zennosuke lost all five members of his household, with only himself surviving. Tamura Heigoro lost fourteen out of fifteen relatives, with only his adopted son Moshichi surviving. Ota Gorouemon had only two survivors out of sixteen family members.

**○ Conditions in the Affected Areas**
Ioka is situated on low ground in the southern part of Yamada Town and bore the full brunt of the tsunami attack. The portion adjacent to the sea was completely swept clean of people, livestock, and property, while the portion near the higher ground was buried under layers of shattered houses and boats reduced to splinters, presenting a scene of devastation that defies description. The damage in the low-lying northeastern area of Yamada was much the same as Ioka—too horrifying to look upon. The bodies being dug out here and there had already been lying for some days, and the faces, hands, and feet had all decomposed, making it impossible to identify who they were; the sight could not but cause involuntary tears of grief.

**● Osawa Village**
Osawa Village was a fishing village on the northern shore of Yamada Bay, with 180 households and a population of 853. The great tsunami destroyed or washed away 174 of those households and killed 474 people. Village headman Fujiwara Kumakichi and his wife, revenue officer Abe Gorosaboro, and others all perished; as the clerical positions were also vacant, the village was left in a state of anarchy. A temporary village headman was appointed, but as all official documents had been washed away, there was said to be nothing that could be done.
