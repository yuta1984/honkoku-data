○On the matter of abandoning one's parents and encountering disaster

When we hear stories of earthquakes from various provinces, sometimes the earth splits and mountains collapse, or the courses of rivers change. Sometimes land sinks into the sea, high places become low, or water springs forth from level ground. These are not particularly rare occurrences. However, in Edo, such strange phenomena have never occurred since ancient times. As I mentioned in the previous volume, earthquakes are very rare in Edo. We heard that there was a great earthquake in the 16th year of Genroku, but the only written record of it is found in Master Hakuseki's book "Oritaku Shiba," where he wrote about it only briefly.

However, he did not record the state of the city center, nor where the most damage occurred from which area to which area.
