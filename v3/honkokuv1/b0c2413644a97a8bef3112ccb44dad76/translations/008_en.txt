## Editorial Guidelines (Hanrei) for the Newly Printed Edition of "Tenkyō Wakumon"

Article 1: "Tenkyō Wakumon" (Questions and Answers on Heavenly Classics) is, in essence, the model text for the study of astronomy. Therefore, in accordance with the request of侍医法眼 (Court Physician of the Hōgen rank) Nagao Buntetsu, we have carefully collated and revised the original text, added punctuation, and committed it to woodblock print. Now, the study of astronomy encompasses four paths: Ri (principle/theory), Sū (mathematics), Ki (instruments), and Shō (celestial phenomena). Therefore, even the most learned Confucian scholars cannot thoroughly comprehend its true essence without the guidance of teachers and friends. For this reason, we have composed a separate volume entitled "Tengaku Meimoku" (Glossary of Astronomical Terms), with the aim of enabling students to understand the main path and not be reduced to weeping at crossroads, making the subject ever easier to comprehend. This has been appended to the end of the main work and likewise serves as a ladder and boat (i.e., a guide) into the study of astronomy. We respectfully ask that learned gentlemen not ridicule this work as mere colloquial speech or trivial tales, and rashly discard and destroy it.

Article 2: The errors and unfounded theories of various scholars are, of course, not few in number.
