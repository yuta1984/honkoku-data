In ancient times, in foreign lands, the division and naming of numerous stars such as the Three Enclosures, Twenty-Eight Mansions, miscellaneous constellations, the Milky Way, and various arrays was done as follows: Wu Xian of the Shang dynasty named forty-four constellations with 144 stars, Gan De of Qi divided 118 constellations with 511 stars, and Shi Shi of Wei considered and named 128 constellations with 810 stars. The three schools combined totaled 300 constellations with 1,465 stars.

However, claims that when Dongfang Shuo was born the Jupiter star disappeared from heaven, or that when the Venus star was hidden Li Bai was born, are attempts to exalt their virtue that instead slander heaven. If it were true that such stars actually disappeared, it would be mere flattery by officials of the Bureau of Astronomy.

As stated in the Ming scholar You's "Questions on Celestial Classics": "If a single star fell to earth, it would crush and overturn the great earth." Since stars are so enormously large, they are eternally unchanging, and the absurd notion that falling stars entrust their lives to humans is thus made clear.

In Zhuangzi's "Great and Venerable Teacher" chapter, it says "Fu Yue obtained this and became prime minister to Wu Ding, greatly preserving all under heaven, riding on the Eastern Dimension, mounted on Winnowing Basket and Tail, comparing himself to the array of stars." To interpret this as saying Fu Yue was the essence of a star would be quite wrong. This is merely a flight of literary fancy based on the existence of a star named Fu Yue.

Also, the story of the immortal Zhang Bowang ascending a river to reach the Milky Way and encountering the two stars of Ox-boy and Weaver-girl is complete nonsense. Do they think the Milky Way is actually a river? In no text do we find that there is a river in heaven that connects to earth. Upon examining the strangeness of immortal arts, there is mention in one text of illusion techniques in the Western Regions, which this probably refers to.

Buddha also had arhats learn these techniques for the sake of expedient means. The intention was simply to change customs and correct the temperament of immoral people in India, but since ignorant common people would not change their ways unless they witnessed something miraculous, these were expedient means for encouraging virtue, not for flying through heaven and diving underground to perform wonders.

Among the several arhats including Ananda, Kasyapa, and Maudgalyayana, many learned these techniques. In later generations, while Buddhist teachings, expedient means, and punishment of evil were set aside, many transmitted and learned only the illusion techniques, entering waves and riding clouds to confuse the ignorant people. The strangeness of immortal arts flows from this tradition.

Should we envy or honor the achievements of false immortals who deceive the people and obstruct benevolence and righteousness through evil arts? The sage does not speak of strange powers and chaotic spirits, and attacks heterodox teachings because they bring only harm. Though their loftiness may surpass great learning, without truth...
