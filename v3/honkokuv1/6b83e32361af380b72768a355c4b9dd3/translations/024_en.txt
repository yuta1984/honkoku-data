When this too is examined through the lens of Buddhist teaching, its principle becomes brilliantly clear. This is because the sutras such as the *Dīrgha Āgama*, *Zhengfa Nianchujing* (Saddharma-smṛty-upasthāna Sūtra), and *Loutanjing*, as well as treatises such as the *Mahāprajñāpāramitopadeśa* (Zhidu Lun) and the *Abhidharma on the Establishment of the World* (Lishi Apitan Lun), explain that Asuras (also called Ashulun) and Indra (Taishakuten) and other heavenly beings engage in battle from time to time. During these battles, they move up and down through the terraced levels of Mount Sumeru and the Seven Golden Mountains (Saptaratna), and must pierce through the complex layers of air standing in their way. This is why such phenomena appear, and it is also inevitable that, given their northward direction, there would be thunderous roaring and violent lightning strikes. As the proverb says, "A thousand accounts heard by a wise man are no match for a single direct observation by a fool." What a hundred Western heroes could not discover even after racking their brains in rational inquiry becomes perfectly clear through the single glance of the divine eye (the supernatural sight of buddhas and bodhisattvas).

**Difficulty No. 22: The North Pole is Lower in the South (The Problem of Polar Altitude and Latitude)**

The questioner asks: Western surveying determines degrees of latitude entirely by the elevation of the North Pole (i.e., the altitude of Polaris). Previously, using this principle, Buddhist teachings were criticized with the argument that "if the earth is flat, the further south one goes, the wider the degrees of latitude become, and the further north one goes, the narrower they become." In response to this criticism, the Western explanation was invoked to establish the principle of a flat earth, which in turn ended up shattering the Western theory of a spherical earth. However, all of this consists solely of theoretical argument and does not constitute *genyō* (現量, direct perception and empirical evidence). If we are to say that using the elevation of the North Pole to determine latitude is mistaken, then while the theory of a spherical earth would be difficult to sustain at all, unless this is verified through direct empirical evidence, I cannot give my assent to it. I ask: is there proof of this?
