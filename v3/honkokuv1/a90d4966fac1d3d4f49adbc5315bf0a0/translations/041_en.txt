Question: What distance can be measured by right ascension?
Answer: Three hundred sixty degrees, that is, one complete revolution around the entire sky.

Question: What is the latitude circle of the celestial sphere?
Answer: It is a great circle that passes through both ecliptic poles and intersects the ecliptic plane at right angles.

Question: What is the latitude of a celestial body?
Answer: It is the north-south distance of a celestial body from the ecliptic, measured in celestial latitude.

Question: What is the longitude of a celestial body?
Answer: It is the eastern distance of a celestial body from the first degree of Aries, measured along the ecliptic.

Question: What angle does longitude indicate?
Answer: It indicates the angle between the meridian circle where the celestial body is located and the meridian circle of the first degree of Aries.

Question: Where does this angle occur?
Answer: The various meridian circles intersect each other at the ecliptic pole, creating this angle.

Question: What distance can celestial longitude measure?
Answer: Three hundred sixty degrees.

Question: What is called the vertical circle?
Answer: It is a great circle that extends from the zenith to the nadir and cuts the horizon circle at right angles.
