Q: When was Neptune first discovered?
A: It was first discovered in 1846 by "Galle," an astronomer (described here as a physician) in Berlin.

Q: Who was it that made the existence of this planet known to the world and indicated its location to "Galle"?
(Note: Mr. "Le Verrier" had not yet observed the planet directly himself; he had only deduced the reasoning behind its existence and estimated its location.)
A: It was the renowned French mathematician, Mr. "Le Verrier."

Q: Was the location indicated by Mr. "Le Verrier" close to the actual position?
A: It was found within one degree of the location he had indicated.

Q: What is the planet's full diameter?
A: Approximately 350,000 ri.

Q: How does it compare in size to Earth?
A: It is approximately 80 times larger than Earth.

Q: What is its distance from the Sun?
A: Approximately 2,850,000,000 ri.

Q: How long does it take to rotate on its axis?
A: This is not yet known in detail.

Q: How long does it take to complete one orbit around the Sun?
A: 166 years.

Q: How many satellites does it have?
A: Only one. There are claims that there may be others, but this is not confirmed.
