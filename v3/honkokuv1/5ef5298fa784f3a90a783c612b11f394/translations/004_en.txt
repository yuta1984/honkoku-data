The ash and debris continued to accumulate, and it was not until the evening of the 8th that the thunder finally ceased. Nakazato Village suffered similar damage, but being three ri distant from the mountain, the accumulated ash was only about five to six sun in depth. Such a terrible disaster has not occurred in over one hundred years. Without distinction between rice fields and dry fields, everything has become like wasteland, and we are living in utter desperation. Needless to say, all standing trees and bamboo groves, as well as all green vegetation in the fields and mountains, have completely disappeared. Naturally, we are severely short of fodder for horses, resulting in extreme hardship.

Furthermore, even if we were to clear away the ash and gravel from the fields, we do not believe it would be ready in time for this year's farming. We are deeply saddened by this situation. Until the afternoon of the 8th, we could not even leave our homes, and it was difficult to make any requests for help. In our helplessness, families gathered together to recite sutras and resign themselves to their fate. We earnestly request that you conduct an inspection as soon as possible so that we may sustain our lives and that all the peasants of the village may be able to resume farming. The above is respectfully submitted.
