Karuizawa-juku, Saku District, Shinano Province on the Nakasendo Road

This post station is located at the foot of Mount Asama. From the 29th of last month, Mount Asama began erupting violently with earthquakes and lightning so severe that houses shook violently, and the peasants fled one after another. From around the fourth hour of the night on the 7th of this month, large stones began falling heavily. A large burning stone fell on the roof of the house of an elderly man named Matahachi, immediately bursting into flames, and from there the fire spread across four or five areas, burning down the entire post town completely. The village headman named Rokurouemon and his father desperately worked to retrieve the water registers and other official documents, risking their lives. While doing so, large stones struck his bamboo hat twice, knocking him down, but he managed to get back up and evacuate. Rokurouemon's daughter, grandchild, and two or three maidservants went somewhere, but because it was the middle of the night, their whereabouts were unknown. Rokurouemon said they were probably struck by stones and killed. The number of other injured and dead is incalculable, according to the report.
