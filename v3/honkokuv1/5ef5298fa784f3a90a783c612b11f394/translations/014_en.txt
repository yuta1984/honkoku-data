However, when a messenger sent to those three locations on the morning of the 8th passed by the Maebashi river area around 8 o'clock, approximately eighty houses had been swept away, and the Tone River water was immeasurable. Within about half an hour, the water level rose to about 5-6 jo (15-18 meters), and human corpses, broken into pieces, came flowing down in great numbers. Among them, 3-4 people who were not dead but piled on top of corpses came floating down, crying "Help us!" But there was no way to save them, and people fled desperately for their lives. Around 7 o'clock in the evening, the tremors suddenly became stronger, and large and small stones

came flowing while still burning, flames rose up from the water, and the smell of smoke entered our noses and eyes, making the situation unbearable. This situation is truly impossible to fully describe with pen and paper. On the morning of the 9th, before 5 o'clock, mud about one jo (3 meters) deep pushed into a place called Shiba-shuku. In this flow were countless dead bodies floating on top of the mud - corpses that were either decomposed or burned. There were many dead women and children. Among them were six people who were half-dead and half-alive, so the local people gathered together to pull out these six people and provided various medical care. However, since they couldn't distinguish between men and women, they washed them with hot water and carefully combed their hair.
