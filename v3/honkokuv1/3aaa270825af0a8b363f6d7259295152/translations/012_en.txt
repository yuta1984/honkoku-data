Then, on the evening of the fourth day, he woke up by himself and sat up, unaware that he had been lying down for four days, asking "What time is it now?" At this moment, the innkeeper's wife was beside him and gave the old man a meaningful look, saying "It must be around half past seven." The woman smiled and said, "How delightful this is. For the first time in six years, you have slept peacefully for a while and your heart has become clear and refreshed." When he asked if the people had taken their evening meal and requested food for himself, the old man rejoiced greatly and urged "Come, quickly!" The woman innkeeper again gave the old man a meaningful look and said, "Since you have slept so well for the first time in six years, let us joyfully serve you rice porridge," and hastily prepared and offered porridge. He took up his chopsticks more comfortably than usual, and his manner of speaking to the serving maid and such was different from usual - he had become like an ordinary person, so the old man beside him rejoiced without limit. Thus, gradually his sleeping and eating became comfortable and normal, and he seemed to forget his previous aversion to meeting people's faces, even beginning to converse with fellow female guests with whom he had little familiarity. After bathing more than three cycles, what had been called a strange illness was completely cured. Not only the old man but also his sister and even the attendants were filled with joy and celebration. Both the old man and his sister forgot their illness and returned safely to their hometown - so the story is told. Following this tale, I heard many stories during rainy idle hours about how a certain person in Edo was mysteriously cured of a long-standing tumor, and tales of various illnesses being cured at other inns, but I omit them here as there are too many.

○Bathing Method (How to Bathe)
Bathing in hot springs to cure illness is the same as taking medicine, so if the method is not appropriate, it is difficult to achieve results. For those bathing for the first time: on the first day, twice morning and evening - whether hot or warm depends on one's preference, but beginners should not enter water that is too hot. When about to enter, first splash the face and moisten the body, then enter the hot water. Those with painful areas should massage those spots, and when the whole body has warmed, exit the water, dry the body, enter again briefly, then get out. This constitutes one bathing session. On the second day: three times before meals. On the third day: three times before meals, once at bedtime. The fourth day: the same. The fifth day: six times day and night (four times during the day, twice at night). The sixth and seventh days: the same.
