Hanashiki: The first layer consists of rock formations exposed in various places with a slight covering of red soil. The second layer is also rock.

Dryness/Wetness of the Land
The humidity is not particularly severe.

Cleanliness/Contamination of the Land
Since there are not many patients (hot spring visitors), the uncleanliness is not particularly severe.

Original Spring Source
Both Ōtoku and Hanashiki emerge beside the Agatsuma River. In the case of Ōtoku, it springs from a location more than one jō (about 3 meters) below the riverbed, in a courtyard area mixed with round stones and gravel.

In the case of Hanashiki, there is one large rock on the riverside at the same level as the river, where the rock has cracked and water springs forth. Half flows into the river, and half enters the hot spring pool at a distance of three to four shaku (about 1 meter).

Clarity/Turbidity
Both locations are clear and transparent.

Elevation of the Land
Ōtoku: Approximately 3,000 shaku above sea level, barometer reading 26 inches 9 bu
Hanashiki: Approximately 3,400 shaku above sea level, barometer reading 26 inches 6 bu 8 rin
