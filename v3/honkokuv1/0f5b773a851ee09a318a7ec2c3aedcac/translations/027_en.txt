The answer is: The Book of Documents (Shang Shu), in the Hong Fan section, states "When the sun and moon move correctly, there is winter and summer."

The movement and transformation of climate is organized as follows: five days make one period (kou), three periods make one season (ki), twenty-four seasons make one year (sai), and the four seasons are established. The 365-plus days of the celestial cycle represent the beginning and end of the sun's complete circuit around the heavens. The changes in cold and warmth all result from the sun's circulation and the rise and fall of yin and yang.

Speaking of lands like Japan and China: when the sun comes to the north of the equator, it is close to the zenith and hot; when it goes south of the equator, it is distant and cold. When it follows the equator, it is between cold and hot - warm and cool. However, there is no reason for all countries to have the same climate. Since cold and heat vary according to whether the sun moves far away or comes near to each particular land, the vastness of all countries varies greatly.

Some countries divide cold and heat into half-year periods, while others are "eight-season countries" with four seasons occurring twice. Therefore, the space between heaven and earth cannot be rigidly explained with fixed principles. According to the climate, plants, birds, beasts, insects, and fish flourish or decline, appear or hide. The energy of spring and summer flies upward, and things transform accordingly - like green caterpillars changing into butterflies, or water insects transforming into dragonflies. In autumn and winter, energy descends and hides. The transformation of things follows the same pattern - sparrows entering great waters to become clams, pheasants becoming mirages. This is the supreme mystery of natural heaven and earth.

For detailed explanations of the five-day period changes, see my separately authored booklet titled "Japanese-Chinese Seasonal Periods for Children's Education," which will be published around cherry blossom time. When dividing one solar year of 365 days and 25 moments into twenty-four seasons, each season has 15 days, 21 moments, and 87.5 minutes. Removing the 15 days, the remainder of 21-plus moments becomes the surplus fraction of one season. Accumulating twenty-four gives 5 days and 25 moments - this is the seasonal surplus of one year. The lunar year has 354 days and 37 moments. This is because the sun retreats one degree of celestial measurement per day, and the moon's revolution is slower.

The energy of spring rises from below, so spring colors appear first in the open fields. The energy of autumn descends from above, so autumn colors appear first in the tall forests. Though plants and trees are without feeling, they do not err in the timing of the four seasons.
