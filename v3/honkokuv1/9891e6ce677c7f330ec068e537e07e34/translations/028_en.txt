Q: How wide is Venus's tropical zone?
A: 150 degrees, being 75 degrees on each side of the equator.

Q: How many degrees is it from the pole to the tropic line?
A: 15 degrees.

Q: How many degrees is it from the polar circle to the equator?
A: 15 degrees.

Q: What is the central diameter (width) of the polar circle?
A: 150 degrees.

Q: Does Venus have seasonal changes?
A: Over the course of one year, the equatorial region experiences two summers and two winters, while the polar regions experience one summer and one winter.

Q: When viewed through a large telescope, how does Venus appear?
A: It appears in the same way as the Moon (i.e., showing phases).

Q: Is there a distinction in the way planets pass before the Sun?
A: There is a distinction between inner transit and outer transit.

Q: What is called an "inner transit"?
A: When a planet passes between the Earth and the Sun, this is called an inner transit.
