**Quatrain on Passing the Summer Heat (消夏絶句)**
Back drenched in sweat, head scorched by heat, coming and going without cease;
At the gate, the noon sun blazes, and a haze of sweltering dust rises.
How unexpected — the one paulownia tree I planted with my own hands
Has already grown to cast a cool shade sufficient for others to borrow.

**Writing My Feelings at the Bon Festival (中元書感)**
How many years since I last swept the graves beneath the pines?
White clouds in autumn separate me from the sky of my homeland.
Arriving as a traveler at this festive season, I am ever filled with emotion;
Reading through to the Bon Festival, my heart grows most heavy and dark.

**Improvised Verse on Returning Home at Night (夜帰口占)**
Hand in hand with my companion in verse, we find parting hard,
Deep in the night, where shall we seek to prolong our remaining joy?
The shadow of the silver toad falls upon the waters of the Western Lake,
Ten leagues of wind-swept lotus — autumn is about to turn cold.

**Moved by Reading Various Books Recording the Deeds of the Kusunoki Clan, I Happened to Compose a Long Poem (読楠氏事蹟諸書有感偶作長句)**

How flourishing was the shade of the Southern Branch (the Kusunoki clan)!
For over fifty years they protected the Imperial throne.
Not only did their meritorious deeds stand foremost among those who aided the restoration,
But the man himself was supremely fit to serve as the pillar of a king.
Moreover, his sons, nephews, retainers, and followers were all loyal and steadfast —
Searching through former ages, few could be found to equal them.
People say that Masanori [Kusunoki Masanori] betrayed and surrendered, disgracing his clan;
But I say: this lord's actions sprang from the utmost loyalty.
His father and his elder brother died one after another —
Had this lord also died, who would have been left to negotiate and resist?
He assessed the enemy's strength in three matters, rallied the troops' spirit in one battle,
With his own strength retook the capital, and time and again routed the enemy forces.
Amid shifting winds and clouds, he repeatedly brought forth extraordinary stratagems;
Like a single timber in a great mansion, he alone held it from collapsing.
Did this lord dare forget his father's dying command? He did not.
His father's military strategy — this lord understood it deeply.
Advance and retreat — what constancy could be expected in such times?
To swallow humiliation and endure shame: this is a true man.
Historical records were largely composed by the hands of traitors,
And later generations — who among them could know the grief in this lord's heart?
Have you not seen? When North and South debated peace, re-established legitimate succession, and restored proper rites and the bond between father and son —
The merit of this lord's having not died on that day becomes brilliantly evident here.
Ah, that this lord did not die on that day... *(continues on next page)*
