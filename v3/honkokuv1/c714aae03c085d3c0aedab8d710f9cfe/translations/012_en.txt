Mid-Autumn, Traveling to Sumida River with Zishou and Jiuman - Two Poems

Worldly affairs flow leisurely with nothing to worry about. Once again encountering a fine night, we set out for pure recreation. Buying fine wine to fill our wine vessels, and taking beautiful women aboard a small boat. The blue mountains over the lake are like a thousand-li dream; the bright moon east of the city marks seven years of autumn. I intend to paint this beautiful scenery to boast to friends in my hometown, but ordinary artists cannot capture it all.

A single golden wave sends wind across ten thousand li. The view from the tower surpasses even that from the boat. At Itako's reed shores autumn sounds arise, and where clouds break over the river and sky, night colors spread emptily. The beauty rivals that of Ezhou, though my verses alone pale before those of Master Shihu. Who would know that we guests lingering over poetry and wine share the same pure bliss as the immortals?

Master Shu's lifetime manuscript collection was destroyed by Zhurong (the fire god). Twenty years of painstaking effort became nothing in a single morning. Putting oneself in his place, who could not feel profound sorrow? Fortunately, there are those who remember, and now we carve and preserve these works. Like fragments of jade remaining after the fires of Kunshan, they are truly precious.

The day before New Year's Eve, Ansei 5 (1858) - Critically reviewed by Washizu Sen'mō
