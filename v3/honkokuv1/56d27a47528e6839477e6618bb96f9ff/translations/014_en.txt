[Section む] Within Saiwai-bashi Gate, the residences of Matsudaira Kai-no-kami and Ito Shuri-dayū were burned. The residence of Nambu Mino-no-kami was also burned. The Satsuma domain's soku residence was burned, with some damage. Yamashita Gate and Hibiya Gate areas suffered minor damage. [Section う] From the neighborhood watch post in Sakurada Kenbō-chō to the front row house of Matsudaira Hyōbu was burned.

To the west, in Fushimi-chō, Kaji-chō, Kubo-chō, Tazaemon-chō, Bizen-chō, Inaba-chō, Izumi-chō, and Zenzaemon-chō, there were many collapsed houses. △ From Atago-shita Nishi-no-kubo, Iikura, Azabu Ichibee-chō to Roppongi area, there was no major damage. △

Outside Hanzō Gate, the daimyo residences and townhouses suffered minor damage. △ Soto-Sakurada, inside Akasaka Gate, Miyako-zaka, and Sannō Shrine were safe. Kasumigaseki, Toranomon, and Shinbashi suffered minor damage. However, this records only the general situation in Edo, as the details are too extensive to describe fully.

Due to this great earthquake causing numerous collapsed houses throughout Edo, His Benevolent Lord (the Shogun) granted relief rice to each area, and for those who lost their homes and storehouses and had nowhere to stay, relief shelters were erected - truly a most gracious blessing for which we are grateful.

Relief shelter locations:
- Outside Saiwai-bashi Gate
- Asakusa Hirokōji
- Ueno Hirokōji
- Fukagawa Kaibe Daiku-chō
- Hachiman Shrine grounds (same area)
