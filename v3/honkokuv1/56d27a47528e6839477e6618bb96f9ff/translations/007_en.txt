Abbreviated Map of Fire Sites from the Great Edo Earthquake

On the night of October 2nd, Ansei 2 (1855), after the fourth hour, a great earthquake suddenly began shaking. Not only within four ri of Edo proper, but even the surrounding rural areas suffered massive destruction. Afterwards, fires broke out in various places, causing severe damage to mansions, temples, townhouses, storehouses, and other buildings. The number of deaths and injuries was countless. Though this was called a natural disaster, people were filled with shock and terror, all sleeping outdoors and waiting for daybreak. At the fifth hour the following morning, when fires in various locations were finally extinguished, people could rest their hearts for the first time.
