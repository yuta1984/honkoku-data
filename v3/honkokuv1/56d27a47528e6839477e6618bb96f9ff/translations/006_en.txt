From past the hour of the Snake (around 10 AM) on the 10th day of the 11th month of Ansei 1 (1854), a great earthquake began shaking. Odawara post station in Sagami Province was severely damaged, and from there, Shimoda in Izu suffered many deaths from a great tsunami. Along the Tōkaidō route: Mishima post station was severely damaged with half the lodgings burned, Numazu post station was severely damaged, Hara post station suffered some damage, Yoshiwara was severely damaged with some lodgings burned, Iwabuchi was severely damaged with half the lodgings burned, Yui and Okitsu post stations suffered some damage, Ejiri post station burned from the bridge eastward, and Fuchū post station burned from the notice board eastward. Mount Hakone suffered some landslides, but the fact that Hakone Gongen shrine remained unharmed demonstrates the divine power of the gods.
