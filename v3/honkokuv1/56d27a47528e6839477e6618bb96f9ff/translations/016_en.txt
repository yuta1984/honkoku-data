Excerpts from letters from Osaka ○ On the night of September 28th, there was an earthquake, and Hirano Bridge, Hoki-cho, and Sakai Bridge area collapsed greatly. Many people in this area were harmed. Also in Nagoya in Owari Province and Hamamatsu in Totomi Province, parts of the castle towns burned slightly. This is said to have been due to simultaneous earthquakes.

Among the theater districts of Edo that burned down, the remaining actor residences include those of Bando Shuka's son Kichiya, Nakamura Fukusuke, Ichimura Uzaemon, Morita Kanya, Onoe Kikunojuro, and two or three teahouses that survived.

First, regarding storehouses: those that collapsed greatly numbered over 65,700, those with slight damage numbered over 87,200, and those that burned are countless.

First, the dead numbered over 132,400 people, the injured over 101,000 people, and others frequenting restaurants and inns numbered over 31,200 people.

First, in Shin-Yoshiwara, the dead numbered over 1,430 people, and the injured over 2,300 people.
