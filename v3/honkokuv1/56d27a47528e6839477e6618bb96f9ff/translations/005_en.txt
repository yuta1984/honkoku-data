On the night of March 26th, Koka 4th year, at the 9th hour of night (around midnight), a great earthquake occurred,
suddenly beginning to shake. Fire broke out from the front of Zenkoji Temple in Shinano Province,
and all the town houses and temple lodgings
were burned down without exception. Particularly since it was the time of Zenkoji Temple's special exhibition,
there were many pilgrims visiting, and together with the lodgers,
the number of deaths was countless. Nearby villages were all
destroyed, Matsushiro Castle collapsed, and Iiyama suffered major collapse
with many deaths. Around Iida and Nakano areas,
and even as far as Echigo, there was great shaking. The Tanba River's
water was blocked and became a riverbed. On April
11th, the water broke through, creating a flood over one ri (about 4km) wide,
causing many deaths in nearby villages. There were
many injured people as far as the Echigo area. That Zenkoji's main hall, Nio gate, and Honganji Temple remained
safe was due to the light of the Three Honored Buddhas, truly a most sacred
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　occurrence.

On the night of June 14th, Ansei 1st year, from the 8th hour of night (around 2 PM), there was a great earthquake that destroyed the entire Ise region, but the
shrine of Amaterasu Omikami remained unharmed. Elsewhere, Yamada, Matsusaka, Tsu, Kuwana, and Yokkaichi's post towns and merchant quarters were completely destroyed, and fire
broke out from Kuwana, burning everything up to Yokkaichi. From there eastward through Owari, Mikawa, and Totomi, along the Tokaido to around Hamamatsu post town
there was shaking, and along the highway route up to around Fushimi post town, most post towns were destroyed, with fire
occurring at Minakuchi post town. West of Ise, it reached Yamato and Izumi areas, and north from Kyoto and Osaka to Tanba and Harima areas
there was shaking. In Kyoto, from the 9th hour of day (around noon) on the 14th, there was sudden thunderstorm and heavy rain with countless lightning strikes,
and after the rain stopped past the 6th hour of evening (around 6 PM), the great earthquake began shaking from the 8th hour that same night. In Osaka, shaking began from the 8th hour of night,
collapsing Lord Chikugo's warehouse, gates, longhouses, and walls, with damage in various other places.
The area up to Tennoji shook exceptionally severely, causing great damage to the vicinity. On the same 16th day, from Osaka and nearby areas came
a great tsunami killing many people. Also on the same day, great tsunamis came to various coastal locations throughout the provinces.
A great tsunami hit the Shimoda area of Izu, killing many people. However, in the Izu vicinity and other Kanto regions,
there was no earthquake.
