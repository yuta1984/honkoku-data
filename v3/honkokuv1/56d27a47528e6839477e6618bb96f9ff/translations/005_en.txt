On the night of March 26, Kōka 4 (1847), at the hour of nine (around midnight), a great earthquake occurred and suddenly began shaking. A fire broke out from in front of Zenkōji Temple in Shinshū, and all the townhouses and temple lodgings burned down completely. Since it was during the special exhibition at Zenkōji Temple, there were many pilgrims, and the number of deaths among travelers and lodgers was countless. All nearby villages collapsed, Matsushiro Castle crumbled, Iiyama suffered great collapse with many deaths. The Iida area, Nakano area, and even Echigo shook violently. The Tanba River was dammed up and became a riverbed. On April 11, the water broke through, forming a circular body of water more than one ri wide, causing many deaths in nearby villages and many injuries extending to the Echigo region. That Zenkōji's main hall, Niō gate, and Honganji Temple remained unharmed was due to the divine light of the three honored Buddhas—truly a most sacred occurrence.

From the eighth hour of night (around 2 PM) on June 14, Ansei 1 (1854), there was a great earthquake, and the entire Ise region collapsed. The shrine of Amaterasu Ōmikami was unharmed, but elsewhere Yamada, Matsusaka, Tsu, Kuwana, and Yokkaichi—all post towns and merchant quarters—collapsed completely. Fire broke out from Kuwana and burned everything up to Yokkaichi. Further east in Owari, Mikawa, and Tōtōmi, along the Tōkaidō road, shaking extended to around Hamamatsu post station, and along the highway route, most post stations collapsed up to around Fushimi. There was a fire at Minakuchi post station. West of Ise, it extended to Yamato and Izumi, and to the north from Kyoto and Osaka to Tanba and Harima regions. In Kyoto, from the ninth hour of day (around noon) on the 14th, there was sudden violent wind and heavy rain with countless lightning strikes. After the rain stopped past the sixth hour of evening (around 6 PM), the great earthquake began shaking from the eighth hour that same night. Osaka began shaking from the eighth hour of night, causing the warehouses of Lord Chikugo, as well as gates, barracks, and walls to collapse, with damage in various other places. Up to Tennōji, the shaking was particularly severe with great damage to the vicinity. On the 16th, a great tsunami from Osaka and nearby areas killed many people. Also on the same day, great tsunamis came to various coastal locations throughout the provinces. A great tsunami at Izu Shimoda killed many people. However, the Izu vicinity and other parts of Kantō experienced no earthquake.
