○Shrines and Temples, and Imperial Villa

This land was once the domain of Izu Shrine. Therefore, when Hannya-in Temple was at its height, many of its three thousand sub-temples were scattered throughout this area, and consequently various shrines were also numerous. This is why this small village has such a large number of shrines and temples.

○Yuzen Shrine: Located in Kami-cho. During the Tenpyo-shoho era (749-757), it was dedicated to Sukunahikona-no-mikoto. The Ishiwata family has traditionally overseen the religious ceremonies. There is a stone monument in front of the shrine that briefly records the history of the main shrine. The text was composed by Gen Tsugi of Shinyo, and the calligraphy appears to be by Toko Genrin of Edo. It was erected by Ishiwata Chikayoshi in September of Meiwa 7 (1770). The stone torii gate and two stone lanterns were both donated by the Lord of Kurume during the Horeki-An'ei period (1751-1781).

○Kinomiya: Located north of Kami-shuku. It enshrines Isotakeru-no-mikoto and serves as the tutelary shrine of Atami Village. It is said to have been established in Wado 3 (710). Within the precincts are two large camphor trees. They are approximately fifteen to sixteen arm-spans in circumference, with hollow trunks that can accommodate several people.

○Wada Hachiman: Located at Wada Beach at the western end of Nishiki Bay. It is traditionally said to have been a place of devotion for Minamoto no Yoriie. Today only its ancient ruins remain.

○Imamiya: Located south of Tenjin Mountain. It enshrines Kotoshironushi-no-mikoto and serves as the tutelary shrine of Wada Village. Ancient trees stand in groups, creating lush, verdant shade.

○Tenjin Shrine: Located east of Hama-cho. It enshrines Sugawara no Michizane (Kanko). According to tradition, when Michizane was exiled to Tsukushi, he carved seven portraits of himself. What is enshrined in this shrine is said to be one of them. The shrine was originally located on Tenjin Mountain in Wada, but since recent times it has been at the present location.
