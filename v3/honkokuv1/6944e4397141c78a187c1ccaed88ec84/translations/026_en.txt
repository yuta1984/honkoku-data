○Reservoir (Tame-ike): Located north of Kamijuku, beside Kinomiya Shrine. As this area is naturally rich in hot springs, cold fresh water has always been scarce here. The late friend Tanaka Heihachi frequently visited this place and, lamenting the great importance of drinking water to human health, donated one thousand five hundred yen to cover the cost of constructing a new water supply system, and consulted with the villagers about the project. The villagers were greatly pleased and further contributed donations, running pipes for more than fifteen chō from the foot of Higane-yama mountain, constructing a large pond here to store the cold water, and then using bamboo aqueducts to distribute it throughout the village. The water is clear and cool, and truly sufficient to support the health of both the villagers and the bathers who come from all directions.

○Yokoiso: At the eastern corner of Higashimachi Beach, a breakwater has been constructed to create a harbor anchorage. The view is remarkable, and it has also become a site for sea bathing and swimming.

○Ito River: Flowing from below Higane-yama, its upper reaches are called the Miya River. It passes in front of the gate of Onsen-ji Temple and, reaching Arajuku, flows into the sea.

○Hatsu River: Its upper reaches are called the Iri River. Rising from Takanosuyama, it winds around Maruyama, passes through Minokuchi village, and flows into the sea.

○Wada River: Flowing from Wadayama. The other waterways are all small streams, all flowing southward into the sea.

○Narihira Well: Located by the roadside in Shinjuku. According to the Atami Local Gazetteer by Reishūhō: "The name 'Narihira' derives from a poem composed by that courtier [Ariwara no Narihira]. Young men and women of the village would reflect their images in the well water morning and evening, using it as a means of seeking marriage matches." Nowadays, there is no longer such a custom, and no one draws water from the well for use; only a stone well frame remains to preserve the memory of the historic site.

○Nasuzawa Waterfall: Located north of Maruyama, several chō away, deep in a secluded valley. In recent years, lovers of nature have been clearing away the undergrowth and planting flowering trees, with the intention of making it a recreational viewing spot.
