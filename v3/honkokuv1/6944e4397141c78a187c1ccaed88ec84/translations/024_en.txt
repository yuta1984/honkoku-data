○Mountain Rivers and Famous Scenic Spots

The area of Atami is naturally rich in beautiful mountain and water scenery. The vicinity also has many famous scenic spots and historical sites. These must be recorded to serve the convenience of bathing guests' sightseeing. Mr. Walton said that changes in scenery also have the effect of curing one or two diseases. For example, patients with melancholy leave the city and move to open areas, touring the secluded scenery of green-shaded mountains and waters around them, and are finally cured. This is why records of mountain rivers and famous scenic spots cannot be omitted.

○Ueno Mountain: Ascended from near the hot springs of Kitano-naka in Honcho. According to the Sōsenroku (there are two travel records by Hayashi Saishū Yōtō, front and back; here we excerpt from the front record): "The woodcutter's path is like a thread. Foxes and rabbits cross tracks, pushing through old grass to ascend. Several old pine trees stand in rows at the mountain top. Below them is level ground suitable for sitting. The view is clear and open, with mountains to the right and sea to the left. Villages and fields all gather within one's finger span. It is truly an area for rich viewing. Yet it is left to weeds, with no one to appreciate it. Do people really honor the distant and despise the near? Or do places have their own destiny of prominence and obscurity?" This is indeed true.

○Wada Mountain: Located southwest of Wada Village, it stands bare. It is steep and difficult to climb.

○Nenbutsu Mountain: Connected to Wada Mountain, it lies facing the sea. One climbs from the village road several dozen steps east of Kōzen Temple. Climbing and turning right leads to the mountain top. Looking out, Ajiro and Hatsushima face each other on left and right, with Nishiki Rock jutting prominently below. The mountains of Bōsō and Sagami-Musashi appear and disappear in the distance like clouds and mist. Returning and descending to where the mountain ends, there is a thatched hut called Uomimisaki (Fish-Viewing Cape). A fisherman is always there, watching for the coming and going of fish to aid in fishing. It is also a superb view.
