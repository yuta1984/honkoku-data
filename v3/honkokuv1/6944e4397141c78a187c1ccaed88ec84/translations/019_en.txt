When taken internally, it causes mild diarrhea, promotes gastric function, facilitates digestion of food and drink, opens blockages in the liver, spleen, and peritoneum, and makes blood circulation healthy. In the records of Shaku Edon (Senkoku Gareki-shū), it is said: "If you try taking grass and flower leaves and immerse them in hot water, they wilt. If you also soak them in water for about an hour, they immediately regain their vitality and return to their original state. Those once soaked do not wilt and scatter again." ○Bathing Method and Internal Use: Although there must be differences in temperature, frequency of bathing, duration, etc., according to the differences in symptoms and physical strength or weakness, for now according to Dr. Hoffman's theory, generally the temperature should be 98 to 100 degrees Fahrenheit (i.e., 29 to 30 degrees Réaumur, 37 to 38 degrees Celsius), bathing once to two or three times daily, with duration of 15-16 minutes to 30-40 minutes being appropriate. Those taking it internally should use 4 to 12 ounces per day (since 1 ounce equals about 7 monme 5 bu 6 rin 5 mō in our country, the daily drink should be approximately 1 gō 5 shaku to 4-5 gō), taking this once or twice daily. However, it should be used alone every morning or morning and evening, or drunk with added water. After bathing, one should quickly put on clothes and never come into direct contact with outside air.
