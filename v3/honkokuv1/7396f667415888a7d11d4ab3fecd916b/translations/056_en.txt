And so he spoke, and the daughter-in-law, understanding her husband's feelings, took even greater care of her mother-in-law and devoted herself to serving her. However, the mother's heart remained unmoved, and her malice grew ever stronger.

But Hikobei was a man who could distinguish right from wrong, so he would take responsibility for his wife's mistakes and mediate on her behalf. He would also remonstrate with his mother about her wickedness as a son, and eventually make her submit to reason, so he could not allow his wife to be driven out.

At such a time, Hikobei had to go help with a celebration at his relatives' place in Edo, staying overnight. Taking advantage of his absence, over some trivial matter she flew into a rage, saying "You think you can make a fool of this mother just because your husband is away. Today I cannot bear it any longer. If you don't leave immediately, this is what will happen!" and she brandished a blade as if to cut her. The daughter-in-law was startled and fled barefoot through the light rain back to her parents' home.

The neighbors thought that this was another case of a wicked mother-in-law tormenting her daughter-in-law, and if it came to violence they would have to intervene. Though it was raining, they were considering this, but soon no harsh words could be heard, so understanding that things had quieted down, they did not go to check on the situation.

However, that very night, in the great storm, a large tree in the back fell over, Hikobei's house was smashed to pieces, and the mother-in-law was severely injured. From then on she never rose from her sickbed and departed for the netherworld. This too serves as a lesson, recorded as I heard it.

Precautions
