**Sealing the Breach**

Through the extraordinary dedication of those in charge of the Minato River embankment breach and the day-and-night efforts of firefighters and other laborers, the breach was finally sealed with sandbags (dobyō) at around midnight on the 31st. Thereafter, the torrent flowed in a straight line from the Minato River into the sea, and the floodwaters in the inundated areas gradually receded.

**Flooded Areas and Fatalities**

The following is a summary of the investigation conducted by the Kobe City Office regarding houses and other structures flooded as a result of the embankment breach:

*Flooded Town Names:*
Arata-chō 1st through 4th districts / Kami-Tachibana-dōri 3rd and 4th districts / Fukuhara-chō (entire area) / Tachibana-dōri 1st through 6th districts / Furuminato-chō 1st through 4th districts / Tamon-dōri 1st through 8th districts / Aioi-chō 1st through 5th districts / Nakamachi 1st through 6th districts / Higashi-Kawasaki-chō 1st through 7th districts

*Houses Lost and Other Figures:*
Houses completely swept away: 28 / Houses completely destroyed: 63 / Houses half-destroyed: 166 / Deaths: over 30 / Injured: 49

It should be noted that these statistics cannot yet be considered fully accurate. Furthermore, while the above lists the town names flooded due to the embankment breach, there were also a very large number of houses flooded by ordinary heavy rainfall beyond these areas — namely in the Motomachi-dōri and Hyogo Shinkawa areas — totaling some 1,800 or more households. This alone conveys how extraordinarily heavy the rainfall was on this occasion.

**◯ Flooding in the Rural Districts of the Prefecture**

Surveying the flood damage across the entire prefecture, while floods have not been uncommon in the past, one of this magnitude is extremely rare. Restricting the account to rural district matters, the general situation is as follows:

**Arima District:** Due to the storm of August 30th, the Muko River rose more than 1 jō 3 shaku (approximately 4 meters), and as a result of water overflowing the embankments, the rice fields of Miwa Village (including Miwa sub-village) and Shimoтанака Village were completely transformed into lakes. The Mannen Bridge, belonging to Sanda Town along the prefectural road, was entirely swept away, cutting off traffic.

**Kawabe District:** Embankments at 20 locations, totaling 1,211 ken (approximately 2.2 km) in length, collapsed. Fifty persons were unaccounted for (dead or alive), 840 houses were swept away, and the Tsuse Village Office was also carried off by the flood. Traffic throughout the entire area was cut off. Six towns and villages in total were flooded, and the damage to roads and bridges was also extremely extensive. A follow-up survey on September 1st revealed that the Ina River had overflowed and burst in five places, with a total breach length of over 500 ken. Imastu Village, Kitagawara Village, Kita Village, and other sub-districts within Itami Town were flooded across the board, with water levels reaching 15 shaku (approximately 4.5 meters). Damage to roads and bridges was also severe.

**Kasai District:** Weather became unsettled around 7 a.m. on the 30th. By 10 a.m. there was light rain, accompanied by a sudden strengthening north wind. The rain then temporarily stopped, leaving only wind, but a renewed storm began around 4 p.m. and was most violent around 7 p.m. The winds gradually abated around 11 p.m. and ceased entirely by the morning of the 31st. As a result, rice and cotton crops suffered roughly 10% damage, while fruits and leaf tobacco were nearly completely destroyed. A considerable number of houses, storage sheds, and storehouses also collapsed; in Yamashita sub-village within Kamo Village, two persons were crushed to death when houses collapsed.

**Taki District:** The house of Kawaminami Gihei in Asashimachi sub-district of Sasayama Town was destroyed by wind damage, injuring both the householder himself and his wife. The Kenmotsu Bridge spanning the Sasayama River suffered partial damage, and the water level of the same river reached approximately 8 shaku (about 2.4 meters). Of the 13 towns comprising the entire Sasayama urban area, only Nishi-Shinmachi escaped flooding of floors (both above and below floorboards). The deepest flooding occurred in Minami-Shinmachi, Kawaramachi, Tatemachi, Nishi-machi and others, where water exceeded 1 shaku (about 30 cm) above floor level. The source of the water that flooded the town remains unclear, but it is believed most likely to have originated from mountain collapses and the destruction of Sasayama River embankments. Damage and loss of prefectural road bridges and other infrastructure was ubiquitous, and bridges in particular — not only those on local roads but even those on prefectural roads — were almost entirely destroyed.

**Inami District:** The water levels of the Kako River and the Arai River rose dramatically, with water rising over 1 jō (approximately 3 meters). In the riverside villages of Kamihagi, Heisō, Komeda and others, much of the farmland was flooded, and the lower reaches of the Arai River in particular were entirely submerged. To summarize the damage: 43 houses, storage sheds, and storehouses collapsed; 63 more suffered major structural damage; 151 chōbu of rice crops were damaged; 153 chō of farmland was flooded; and 3 bridges were damaged.
