Staggering with unsteady steps and being cursed at, in that moment of confusion, the rioters quickly crossed the river.
Whether it was the drunken tengu-like lawless behavior, or various wishful thinking of those in casual dress,
the rioter Shiro Takiei announced himself as the vanguard of the clash, which was indeed
mere empty boasting. On this side, thinking they must not be completely crushed,
they covered themselves with hats in shame and each remained silent, saying nothing.
Therefore, Fuji-ya Shobei, Sumi-ya Jingoro,
Izumi-ya Yazaemon, and Yanaba Shichizaemon who were in Nozawa, had beforehand
not only stored away gold, silver, rice, and money, but also clothing, various tools, household goods, and other

precious items in various places. They hid straw, firewood,
and other materials inside their houses with sliding doors and paper screens inlaid with gold and silver
decorations, piling them up, thinking that no matter how lawless these people were,
they would not set foot in temples. So they carried their precious wealth to Kintai-ji and Yakushi-ji temples,
put them in storehouses, and felt at ease. From now on, when the riots
come, they agreed on what to do, saying that if a large crowd pressed forward,
they would use roof stones as projectiles, or even kill them with guns, and such.
The people were sharpening their ancestral rusty spears with Nanmaki whetstones.
