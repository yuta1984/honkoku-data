The Matter of the Riot Crossing to Kawanishi, Along with the Interrogation and Debate at Urakawa

In the meantime, a sudden large force of about 2,400 to 2,500 mounted warriors emerged at the back alleys of Nakagomi-guchi around early October. Villages in all directions were stirring, and this great force, mixed with those wearing straw raincoats and hats, broke through the middle of the earthen bridge to cross over. On the opposite bank, violent men were making a commotion, and five or six men who had pressed forward without gaps were hiding behind houses, waiting in ambush, saying "If they cross the river, we'll kick them down." At such a time, if they didn't cross, when would they raid the grain merchants? A man called "Kirizumi" (Charcoal Cutter), who usually cut wood, brandished his blade and fled, swinging it freely. In an instant, he approached halfway across the small bridge and rushed out all at once. Following behind him, one rider said, "The cold river wind against bare skin, the sound of agitated souls goes thud-thud-thud," and "There they come!" Upon seeing this, they tumbled and rolled away in flight. While they scattered and hid stealthily, [the rioters] crossed over in the confusion.
