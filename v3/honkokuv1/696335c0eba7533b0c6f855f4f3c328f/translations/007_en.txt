What has become habitual is waiting in the eight districts, buying sugar and candy,
deceiving those who come crying that their wives are coming,
making excuses about helping them escape, but even though they wait at Saguchi to be helped,
the upper fields, and indeed the middle fields, or rather the lower fields of Okubo and
Takamachi, whether in the countryside or not, children of only twelve or thirteen years
were left behind, and now they must surely be lamenting in their villages.
Wherever they go, they borrow lodging, and Katsuma and Kitagawa
lead their blind parents to take shelter in field huts,

picking up grain stalks, cutting the lower small field for father,
cutting the middle small field for mother,
cutting the upper small field for their own sustenance, stopping by a nearby temple
to rest in the shade, even at Yuhara for a moment moistening their throats and
resting their bodies. The master priest saw them and said:
