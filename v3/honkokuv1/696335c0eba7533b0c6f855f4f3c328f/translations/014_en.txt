Then they opened the storehouse, set aside only what was needed for their own household use, and decided to lend out all the remaining grain. They provided over a hundred bags of barley, rice, and miscellaneous grains without even taking promissory notes, saying that when times improved, repayment could be made then, thus offering assistance with peace of mind. Those who provide such material and spiritual aid are all admirable people who should serve as models. Following this example, temples and private homes in the nearby areas of Anahara, Takano-machi, Nakoodagiri, Jōchi, and Ōhinata also provided assistance according to their respective abilities - some giving one piece of silver each, others giving three to, two to, or five shō to three shō of rice and unhulled rice. Many people provided such aid.
