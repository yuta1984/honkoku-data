And everyone continued, shouting "Immediately destroy the houses where banners are erected!" As they took the lead, all advanced together. When the banner procession eventually reached Sakamoto post station, they destroyed two establishments - Shimizuya Seisuke and Sukehachi - like fire and water, raised victory cries with great momentum, and upon returning to count their numbers, found they had grown to over two hundred people. With the aforementioned banner at the front, they shouted from all sides, "Everyone come out! Those who don't come out will be destroyed, we'll set fires and burn everything down!" Half went up to Usui, and half headed toward Iriyama and Dezo Pass.

Now, in Karuizawa, Edo officials had established lodgings for the construction work on Usui Pass, so there was no particular disturbance. They simply called out through the town, "Everyone in town come out!" threatening that those who didn't join them would have their houses set on fire. Through Kutsukake, Oiwake, and up to Odai, they simply negotiated with the local authorities, obtaining written agreements that rice would be sold at 1.5 go per 100 mon, cracked grain at 2.5 go, hulled barley at 3 go, and unhulled barley at 5 go, and passed through peacefully.
