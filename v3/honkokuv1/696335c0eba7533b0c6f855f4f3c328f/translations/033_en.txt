They brought out winter sake, sweet mirin wine, and other liquors, and though they tried drinking them for the first time, they said "these wines cannot be drunk." When they cut the hoops of the six-shaku barrels, the sake flowed out like a rushing waterfall, making even the sea disappear. One person shouldered a ladle from a sake half-barrel and sang: "This is about the vigilance of people who have accumulated money, and we are people called robbers. Due to my parent's poverty, I had a strange dream one night. I was taught that if I go out to Nozawa market and drink sake, I can become wealthy, and following that teaching, I have been taking and bringing things.

Gradually, I have become wealthy. Here is something strange - I am one who comes to every market to drink sake. Though I can hide the scars on my shaved forehead, my complexion never changes, and the surroundings tremble. When people ask my name, I am said to be a village headman living in that village. Today, I intend to join the Shinyo meeting and cause that disturbance." At the banks of the Shinyo river, they had rice cooked, and until the night ended, they called friends to gather before the food chest and waited, striking the bottom of the sake barrels they wore over themselves.
