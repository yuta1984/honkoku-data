They smashed and broke down eaves and pillars, tied rough ropes around them and pulled them down. They spoke recklessly to sword-bearing men, telling them to carry Buddhist ritual implements and other outrageous things. They trampled through two or three places in Hanasakurai, here and there, scattering like cherry blossoms in a storm as they moved toward Shimoneri. In Shimoneri, Kiuchi Zenbei, who possessed thousands of ryō in old and new gold coins and had long been a wealthy man, also heard about the situation in the Nozawa area and came out to the village border to greet them, preparing food distribution and waiting. Thinking it would be shameful to say "I have none" if they demanded money, he placed three hundred ryō in new gold coins in a chest in his living room, while hiding his remaining money and household treasures in an underground storehouse, and waited. Then a large crowd of ruffians came in, devoured the food in a disorderly manner, and the pre-arranged villains all rose up at once, kicked and scattered the trays and bowls, and taking advantage of hitting the sliding doors and paper screens, smashed and destroyed them. The household members all fled and scattered. They smashed all the money, chests, kimonos, and boxes to pieces, ransacked everything, and even entered the underground storehouse to search out and take every last coin.
