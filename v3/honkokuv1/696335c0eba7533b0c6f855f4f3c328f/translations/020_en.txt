The Beginning of the Shinshū Uprising《Annotation: Text from Hiragana Seisuki》
It was the 2nd day of the 10th month of Tenmei 3 (1783), year of the rabbit, when Mount Asama suddenly erupted. Due to poor harvests, various provinces were in distress, and everyone was about to cause an uprising under the command of ignorant people. In the main force, foolish leaders joined and caused disturbances heading toward Usui Pass. In the flanking force, gluttonous and ill-mannered people surged toward the mountain entry. Among those who followed were characters like Shirō the troublemaker from the bare mountaintop with his beard like a demon, Kotarō the drunkard from Takase, and the pushy Samusa.

The general was a bare-footed, naked fool named Berabo Kanetori, and their total force amounted to over two hundred mounted men. Wearing stars above their heads, they were a traveling band that caused disturbances day and night. Using their practiced methods to make a living, they would realize "this place has connections too" and beg for leftover food to eat. Crossing rivers, they would take mountain paths; crossing mountains, they would reach river rapids.
