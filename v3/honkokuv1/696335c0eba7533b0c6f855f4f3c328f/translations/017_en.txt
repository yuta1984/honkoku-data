With such momentum, and without any restraint toward the castle town, they proceeded to Annaka post station, where they thoroughly destroyed the establishments of Yanagiya Yoshishichi, Komaya Ichizaemon, Kikuya Katsuemon, and Maruya Rokusuke, causing an extraordinary uproar. The castle was greatly alarmed, and the officials gathered to hold a council. Since it was a peasant uprising, they decided to suppress it by every possible means: they closed the gates at both ends of the town, prohibiting entry and exit, dispatched additional guards to the Yokogawa checkpoint, and sent express messengers to Edo with full details of the situation.

While all this was going on, dawn had already quietly begun to break, the bell rang out, and they turned back to Matsuida, completely destroyed Ōnoya Sōbei's establishment, and then pressed on to the home of grain merchant Nakajima Denbei above the Goryō slope, thoroughly smashing the main house, tenement buildings, and several storehouses, rampaging violently. With no sense of discernment whatsoever, they followed wherever the feet of those in front led, heading in the direction of Myōgi, until the sun had already set.
