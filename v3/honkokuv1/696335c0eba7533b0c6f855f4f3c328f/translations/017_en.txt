With such momentum, showing no restraint even toward the castle town, they proceeded to Annaka post station and thoroughly destroyed the establishments of Yanagiya Gishichi, Komaya Ichizaemon, Kikuya Katsuemon, and Maruyā Sukuroku, causing such an extraordinary commotion that even those in the castle were alarmed. The officials gathered for consultation. Since this was a peasant uprising, they decided to use all possible means to suppress it: they closed the gates at both ends of the town to prohibit entry and exit, sent additional guards to the Yokogawa checkpoint, and dispatched an express messenger to report the details to Edo.

While this was happening, dawn was already breaking with the sound of bells ringing together. The mob then returned to Matsuidamachi and completely destroyed Onoya Sobei's establishment. From there, they proceeded to assault the residence of grain merchant Nakajima Dembei on Goryosaka slope, thoroughly destroying his main house and numerous storehouses. Without any discrimination or reasoning, following wherever the vanguard's feet led them toward Myogi, by the time day had grown dark, their lanterns and torches scattered like stars here and there in disorder, and originally for food...
