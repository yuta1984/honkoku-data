Although they said they would gradually sell out the agreed-upon grain supplies, the stocks became scarce and prices rose high again as before, so that all people were about to suffer from famine. Because of this, the villages fell into turmoil, and on the night of September 29th, they assembled forces to the east of Matsuida to hold a council, planning to attack those who were hoarding grain in Shinshū and Jōshū provinces, seize the grain, and vent their anger. However, since this riot was like the work of tengu (supernatural beings), there was no proper leader, so there was no established law or

order. Approximately 800 people gathered in madness, taking Heaven's force as their general. With no one to give commands, they heard rumors that Jōsuke, the village headman of Isobe village, had bought and hidden about 100 bales of rice and grain, so they pressed forward directly, smashed doors, sliding screens, and partitions to pieces, and broke down the doors of the storehouse. From there they immediately proceeded to Itahana post station and similarly destroyed the grain shops of Gizaemon and Ihachi. However, despite being such a large crowd, they were extremely violent, and with no one to direct the scattered groups, they were as if ascending to heaven.
