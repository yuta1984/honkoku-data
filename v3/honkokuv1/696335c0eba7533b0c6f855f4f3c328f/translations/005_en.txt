Everyone scattered in all directions, and at the end of Kiyokawa, Onara could not unite properly.
Even passing through Tanokuchi was troublesome, trying to ward off the world's disasters.
Kami-Nakagomi was resentful - with three parts appropriate and inappropriate, living extravagantly from above
and surpassing those below. Now, like the sanekadzura vine mentioned in the poem by Minister of the Right Sanjo,
both sanekadzura and tokorokadzura vines have been completely dug up. Whether in Tokaichi
or Hatsukamachi, there is no Irisawa in this sorrowful world. Traveling to distant lands,
slowly and gradually climbing to higher ground year after year at Hirabayashi, everything becomes a painful sight.
The salvation from Ohinata-kami, arrows, and mansions are not just three or four in number.
So in those mountains, Kawakubo, and Hatakenaka as well,
people fall and roll about. O Takakura Sobara! Even entering through Hanaoka-guchi,
Shimo-Umise is truly like the realm of hungry ghosts. Though trying to drink at Higuchi,
the water is bitter. Whether it's someone's trail ahead or behind, rather than
living to see such misery, it would be better to enter Anahara with Iritakaiwa Banagashi.
People do not know, only Fudeiwa remains steadfast in heart, quietly
walking with weakening steps. There are no village mediators, and pitiful Koumi and Oyasawa too
cast away their Kasahara hats and departed. Their destination is Ichinnosawa.
