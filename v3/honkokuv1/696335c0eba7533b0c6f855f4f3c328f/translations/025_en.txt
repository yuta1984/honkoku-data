They ended up being escorted from village to village. At dawn on October 3rd, the guide crouched in Jiroemon's yard holding a bonten (ritual pole), saying "This riot is not what we desire. If you don't guide us, we'll burn everything down." Having no choice, he agreed, but soon a large crowd burst in, destroyed everything beyond endurance, and returned making a commotion, demanding immediate guidance to Hiraga. Hiraga was territory of the Matsumoto domain, with only one magistrate, so he wondered what to do. He thought that if there were any disrespect toward the government office, even peasants could not escape punishment, but since this was merely civilian unrest, there was no need for strict prevention. He sent a messenger to Matsumoto Castle to report this situation.

Thus the riot moved toward Hiraga, with crowds so dense they made the main road seem narrow. They called out "Guide us to Shugetsuin Temple in Nakagomi village from here," putting the bonten at the front.
