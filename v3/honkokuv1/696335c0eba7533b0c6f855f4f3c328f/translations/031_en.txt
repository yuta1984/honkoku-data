"This is a sacred temple hall - what ruffians are these? Withdraw immediately!" he shouted. From among the crowd, a mild-mannered man stepped forward and said, "Not so, reverend sir. While the head priest of this temple may bear no fault, there is no doubt that miscellaneous goods from the Kadoya and Yanaba shops have been stored here. If you hand them over properly, that would be fine, but if you refuse, we won't acknowledge this as a temple. You monks, don't try to make excuses - we'll smash you one by one."

When they cursed "How about it? How about it?", the priest became very angry: "What unreasonable people you are! The head priest is away in Edo, the retired priest and deputy are away on business in distant places, and only I remain as caretaker. I have no recollection of storing goods from residences or fish weirs. Not even a tea bowl - this monk cannot justify any such claim." Before he could finish speaking, the crowd pressed in: "There, there! Seize that monk and make him confess! Strike him, beat him!" A great number gathered around, trampling and kicking him. "You scoundrel! Now, did you store things or not? Tell the truth honestly! If you're stubborn, we'll split your monk's head like a melon - speak up, speak up!" Under this pressure, he cried "Ah, then I'll tell you, I'll tell you! There's no doubt we stored them [...]"
