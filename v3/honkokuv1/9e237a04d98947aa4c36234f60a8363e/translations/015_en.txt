The figure on the right shows the interior of the front entrance of a two-*ken* (approximately 3.6 meters) storehouse (*kura*), illustrating how cross-bracing (*sujichiigai*) made from boards split to one-*shaku* width has been inserted and nailed into notches cut in each pillar. In the case of construction by competitive tender (*nyūsatsu fushin*), very inexpensive rough timber and inferior wood are used as pillars without planing, so the carpenter's labor is not excessive. Nevertheless, when cross-bracing in an X-pattern is driven into all four interior walls as shown in the figure, the structural framework becomes as solid as a great bedrock, and will not shake even in the most severe earthquake. As a result, the walls suffer not even the slightest damage. Therefore, there is no fear of the fire spreading to the storehouse even in violent winds or great fires. I myself witnessed a close friend's storehouse, built in this manner, remain completely intact during the great earthquake of the recent Year of the Rabbit.
