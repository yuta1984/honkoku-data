First, the structural framework (tatejichi) should be built through the conventional competitive bidding construction process (nyūsatsu fushin), manufactured as inexpensively as possible. After the walls are attached, they should then be reinforced. The method is as follows: following the same approach used when constructing the frame of hinged earthen doors (tsuchido) for storehouses (dozō), use planks of one sun in thickness and eight sun in width to make firm notch cuts into all the pillars on the left, right, front, back, top, and bottom of the house. As shown in the figure on the left, diagonal bracing (sujikaі) should be driven in firmly in a cross pattern (+), and large nails weighing 300 me or more should be strictly nailed in — three nails per pillar. If done in this manner, even in the case of a two-story tile-roofed building, the structure will be as solid as a rock, and there will be no concern whatsoever of warping or bending in any violent earthquake or fierce storm. The general outline should be understood through the figure on the left.

(Marginal note: Regarding the method of notching for diagonal bracing — cut seven bu deep into the pillar, and three bu into the plank. All subsequent [instructions] should follow this same method.)
