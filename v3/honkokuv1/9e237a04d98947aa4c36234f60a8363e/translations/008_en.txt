The figure on the right shows a two-story house with diagonal bracing (sujikaі) driven in at two locations in a cross pattern (+) beneath the horizontal weatherboards (shitami) on the left and right sides of the building. On all sides — front, back, left, and right — wherever walls and weatherboards are to be attached, the diagonal bracing should be applied in this same manner, and the weatherboards should then be nailed over the surface. Furthermore, if it proves difficult to drive in the diagonal bracing from the exterior side depending on the location, one may instead make notch cuts from the interior side and cover the surface with fitted boarding (itahame). This should be adapted according to the structure and layout of each individual house.

○ For the frontage (omote maguchi) of townhouses facing the street, it may appear that there is no suitable location to attach diagonal bracing on the interior. However, if the building is two stories, the bracing should be notched and driven in a cross pattern in the space between the top of the sliding door lintel (kamoi) and the threshold (shikii) of the second-floor partition doors. If the building is a single-story structure (hiraya), the bracing should be driven in a cross pattern in the space between the top of the lintel and the perimeter beam (jimawari no keta). If there are walls or door recesses (tobukuro) on the left or right sides, diagonal bracing must always be notched and driven in a cross pattern. Likewise, in places where it is difficult to install from the exterior, it should be done from the interior. The general outline of this method should be understood through the figure on the left.
