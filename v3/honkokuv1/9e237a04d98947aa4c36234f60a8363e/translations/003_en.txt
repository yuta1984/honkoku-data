It is explained that when one sets up a device where an iron implement is attracted [to a magnet] and arranges it like an alarm clock mechanism to test [the magnet's pull], it would be sufficient to detect the precursor signs of an earthquake. However, since one only learns of it at the very moment the earthquake is about to occur, and without knowing whether it will be mild or severe, and with no time to escape, what benefit could this possibly provide? Furthermore, it is said that thirty days before an earthquake, the water in wells rises and begins to bubble; however, this has not yet been conclusively tested. It is also said that well water suddenly takes on a salty taste, but this too varies depending on the location and may not always be the case. It is indeed a fact that when a major earthquake has already occurred, well water rises and gushes forth, but this does not serve as an advance warning. Moreover, it is said that a little before a major earthquake is about to occur, the sky becomes overcast in all directions with a humid, rain-like atmosphere, with dense clouds forming yet no rain falling, and that a violent earthquake will surely follow; but since the earthquake reportedly occurs almost immediately after these clouds appear, this too amounts to nothing more than knowing at the very moment it happens, and is of no use whatsoever in escaping the disaster. Other methods — such as divination, physiognomy readings, ink color [omens], sacred lot-drawing, and the rustling of bamboo grass — are all empty theories and none are worth taking seriously.
