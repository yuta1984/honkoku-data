●Burned Alive to a Charred Ruin (continued from previous page)

(Hirose Saneyoshi) saw the fire advancing toward him and urged his household to work at removing the furniture, managing to get roughly half of it out. However, by this point the fire had already surrounded the house on all sides, and accepting that there was no more to be done, the household members each made their way to their respective places of refuge. Saneyoshi, however, resolved to make one more trip to retrieve furniture, and plunged alone into the smoke and entered the building — a decision that would prove fatal. He immediately became dizzy and collapsed on the spot, falling beneath the burning and crumbling sliding doors and partitions. Though badly burned, he somehow crawled desperately to the front entrance, where he was fortunately spotted by a police officer and carried to the Tobe sub-station. His entire body was without a single patch of unburned skin; his ears and nose had lost their recognizable form, and he was festering like a sufferer of "tenkeibyo" (leprosy). He was then placed on a wooden stretcher-board and transported to the Jūzen Hospital on Nogeyama, where he received treatment from the deputy director, Dr. Shibukawa, and seven assistants. After treatment, he responded to the medical staff's questions by giving his address and name, and informed them that he had a relative named Kobayashi on Jizō Hill. The Tobe sub-station dispatched someone to summon this Kobayashi, and Saneyoshi's wife Hide (age 28) was duly notified. At around 2:30 a.m. on the 13th, Hide arrived at the hospital and collapsed weeping beside her husband's sickbed. Despite all medical efforts, Saneyoshi passed away at 5:00 a.m. the same morning.

●The Tragic Fate of the Tsuta-za Theater　　　（See illustration）

The Tsuta-za had recently been presenting moving picture shows (motion pictures), but had concluded its scheduled run and was closed at the time of the fire. Upon hearing that a fire had broken out nearby, the theater owner Saitō Chōsaburō (age 63), who lived next door at 1-chōme, No. 1, Yoshida-chō, along with hired hand Higuchi Isaburō (age 19), a man of unknown surname named Yasutarō (age 19), a tea-server of unknown surname named Keijirō (age 27), dressing room attendant Tanabe Kumakichi (age 43), his wife Mine (age 37), and the head usher Takeuchi Seijirō (age 54) — seven people in total — rushed to the theater to retrieve and secure the theater's props and equipment. While they were absorbed in their work, the third floor of the facing exhibition hall, the Hinode-kan, burned and collapsed, and the raging flames quickly spread in all directions, cutting off their escape routes. All seven met a pitiful end.
