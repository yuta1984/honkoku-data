Generally speaking, there is water in the earth, and there is also fire. When yin and yang are disturbed and qi is released accordingly, mountains may burn, and hot springs may emerge. Truly, even though heaven and earth, sun and moon, stars and earth pass through the years, they do not show signs of aging. Looking down upon the entire world, good fortune is bestowed upon good people, while punishment and calamity are brought down upon evil people. This is truly something to fear and be cautious about. It is essential to restrain oneself, correct one's conduct, and avoid extraordinary lightning strikes, fire disasters, and water calamities.

Autumn of Ansei 3 Tatsu (1856) - Recorded by Ichimuan Kochō-sō

○ Days corresponding to Taihaku-sei (Venus) are good for all purposes: moving, shop openings, weddings, coming-of-age ceremonies, celebrations, consultations, negotiations, prayers to gods and Buddha, and beginning scholarly and martial arts pursuits. These are good days for departures and long journeys.

○ Days corresponding to Shinsei (Mercury) are days when both good and bad, large and small matters become magnified. Therefore, it is good to pray to gods and Buddha. For long journeys, departures, consultations, and boarding ships, one should be cautious and mindful of both good and bad fortune. These are days when matters become significant - days requiring vigilance.

● Days corresponding to Keiwaku-sei (Mars) require caution in all matters. One must not be careless about anything. Whether bathing, launching boats on rivers, or handling fire, one must not be negligent. Consultations, disputes, binding matters, long journeys, and departures all require attention. These are inauspicious days for any purpose. Be very careful.

The method for using these stars: For long months, count down from the top - 1st, 2nd, 3rd day in sequence. Also count down from the top - 11th, 12th day to determine. For short months, count up from the bottom - 1st, 2nd, 3rd day gradually toward the top. Also count up from the bottom - 11th, 12th day.
