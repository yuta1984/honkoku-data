As for the Japanese reading (wakun) of the character 鬼 as "oni," Minamoto no Shitagō's Wamyō Ruijushō states: "Oni (於邇) is a phonetic shift from the sound of 'in' (陰, yin)." The Go-on (Chinese reading in the Wu dialect) of the character 陰 is "on." Since "on" and "oni" are phonetically close, "oni" became the Japanese reading. Since the so-called ki (鬼) is the spirit (rei) of yin, why would it have a strange or fearsome physical form?

Long ago, someone asked Master Cheng (Cheng Yi) the following: "In the Book of Changes (Yi/易), there is the expression 'knowing the essence and form (seijō) of ki and shin (demons and spirits).' Does this mean that ki and shin have actual forms?" Master Cheng answered: "Indeed, they do." The questioner then asked further: "If they already have form and appearance (seijō), does that mean ki and shin exist as real things?" He answered: "The ki and shin described in the Yi refer to the workings of creation and transformation (zōka)." The questioner asked again: "What causes famous mountains and great rivers to raise clouds and bring rain?" He answered: "This is nothing more than the steaming and rising of ki (vital energy/気)." The questioner asked yet again: "When one performs a ritual offering (matsuri), is a deity present?" Master Cheng answered: "It is simply that…"
