They do not possess any particularly fearsome appearance. The passage in *The Tales of Ise* (*Ise Monogatari*) stating that a demon devoured the Second Ward Consort (Nijō no Kisaki) in one bite is actually a metaphorical description of how the Minister of Horikawa, the Major Counselor Kunitsune, heartlessly took her back. Furthermore, "yaksha" and "rakshasa demons" are actually names of countries. The *Bianzheng* (*Heterodoxy Refuted*) states: the so-called yakshas and rakshasa demons refer to place names in the western regions, far from China, beyond the reach of civilization and moral influence; therefore, many strange creatures are born there, and it is said that some lack even the qualities of human beings. Generally speaking, those who eat human flesh are not uncommon — during the Warring States period, exhausted and starving soldiers reportedly ate human flesh. This can also be understood from observing how tigers and wolves devour each other's flesh. In China, individuals known as Suima, Shubou, and Zhu Can are said to have delighted in steaming and eating young children (as recorded in *Wuzazu*). Beyond these, figures from the Five Dynasties period — Zhang Congjian, Yan Zhen, and Dugu Zhuang — all allegedly stole young children from the common people and used them as food.
