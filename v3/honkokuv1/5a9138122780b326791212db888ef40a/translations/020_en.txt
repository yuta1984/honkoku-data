**"Yagi Ryū no Maki" (The Dragon Volume of Yagi) — One Complete Volume, with an Illustrated Chart of Nanpin Ōrai Hanpuku**

Contents include: the origins of trade; the origins of competition; the distinction between trade and competition; account-balancing and the origins of the market; how to assess the overall state of the market; how to assess the advances, retreats, substance, and function of the market; establishing the rules of buying and selling; matters of unexpected timing and unfounded rumors; reading the intentions of market participants; a discussion of the relationship between market prices and various writings. The book argues that of ten people who engage in speculative trading, nine will suffer losses while only one will profit, and examines the fundamental nature of these gains and losses. It includes the illustrated chart of Nanpin Ōrai Hanpuku (the method of averaging down losses through repeated transactions). The principle of Nanpin is this: "Nan" (難) refers to the difficulty of suffering losses; "Hei" (平) refers to averaging out those difficulties. If one follows the methods of profit during profitable times, and the Nanpin method during times of loss, victory is inevitable. The chart is included with detailed instructions on how to apply it. By making deep use of this book, one will naturally arrive at the state of complete mastery — winning in a hundred battles out of a hundred.

**"Tenmon Chūsei Fūu Kō" (A Study of Astronomical Middle Stars and Weather) — Printed on Both Sides of a Single Sheet**

This work presents the Middle Star Chart (Chūsei-fu), instructions on how to observe the stars, methods for using stellar observation to assess fair and rainy weather and auspicious or inauspicious omens, methods for reading the almanac to assess the fortune of each year, and methods for making predictions about the following year starting from the winter solstice — determining the length of fair or rainy periods for three-day or six-day intervals using one's own hand as a guide. It also covers reading lamp flames, cloud formations, and atmospheric vapors to assess weather conditions and auspicious or inauspicious signs.
