Various shrines and temples suffered damage. Within the same temple (Sensoji), there were Shochi-in, Choju-in, Shozo-in, Jumyo-in, Shofuku-in, Chiko-in, Baimei-in, Jisso-in, Shoju-in, Kinzo-in, Kanchi-in, and Nion-in, and behind these there were commoner tenements that were all severely damaged. The Kannon main hall, mountain gate, bell tower, Zuishin gate, Raijin gate, three shrines, and Nembutsu hall were safe. The five-story pagoda's finial was slightly bent.

From Higashi Bridge along the river to Sanya Canal, buildings remained but were burned. However, many houses collapsed. Within this area, Matsuchiyama was safe. From Sanya Canal to Imado Bridge, about half a cho burned. The area around Ginza suffered fire damage, with extremely many collapsed houses. The eta-machi in the same area remained. From Hensho-ji and the adjacent Sosen-ji to Masaki Inari, many houses were severely damaged, and the ryoriya (restaurants) called Kawaguchi and Yanagiya collapsed. Similarly, in Sanya-cho, Asakusa-cho, the continuing Nakamura-cho, Kozukahara-cho, and Senju-juku, there were many severely damaged houses up to the great bridge. There were also many fire losses in Senju. This portion should be issued as an addendum later.
