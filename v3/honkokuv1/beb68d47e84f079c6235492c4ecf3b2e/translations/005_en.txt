On the left side of Komagata-chō, everything south of the river burned completely from the corner in front of the restaurant called Hatsufuji. In this area, not a single storehouse remained. Suwa-chō on the south side of the river burned. Kurofune-chō burned. At the same location, a plasterer's storehouse remained. At the right corner, half of a rice merchant's establishment remained. The riverside area of Miyoshi-chō remained. To the south, the fire stopped at Omaya-gashi. Kuramae-dōri was safe.

② From Shitaya Shichiken-chō, fires broke out, and both the Bingo-sama and Izumo-sama residences suffered major damage. Daishō-ji, Kōshō-ji, and Shingyō-ji temples were similarly affected. Across from them, Izumo-sama's upper residence was half-burned, but facing Shichiken-chō, from Reiun-ji's lower residence southward, the townhouses, Kyūshō-ji, Chūkō-ji, Komiyakawa, Hōchō, Hina, Myōken-ji, Shōkei-ji, and Tōen-ji all burned. Kitsurugawa was half-burned. To the south of the same location was Kaya-chō ni-chōme, and facing it were Shōgō-ji and Kōan-ji temples, while to the east Kyōshō-ji temple burned. The fire stopped at this location. This was the area from the middle of Kaya-chō ichi-chōme to ni-chōme.
