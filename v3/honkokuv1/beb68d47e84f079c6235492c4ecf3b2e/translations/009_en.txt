The mansions, Yanagi-cho, Gusoku-cho, Sumi-cho, and Zaimoku-cho up to the 8th district riverbank were burned. Some storehouses remained in this area. From Kyobashi to Shinbashi, both feudal lord mansions and townhouses suffered major damage. In the areas of Tsukiji Odawara-cho South, Iida-cho, and former Yanagiwara-cho, there was severe destruction with the most collapsed houses. The main hall of Nishi Hongan-ji Temple was safe, though several buildings in the temple compound were severely damaged. The lord's mansions in this area suffered major damage with many collapsed storehouses.

Twelve: In Teppozu Jikken-cho, Lord Matsudaira Awaji's residence burned down. Many houses collapsed in Akashi-cho. In Funamatsu-cho, Lord Hosokawa Noto-no-kami was safe, but there was much other major damage. Tsukuda Island suffered major damage but no fires. From Hon-Hatchobori northward, along the main street, Hatchobori group mansions, Kayaba-cho, and Yakushi-do were safe. Townhouses in all directions suffered many collapses and damage.

Thirteen: Reigan Island's Shio-cho, Yokkaichi-cho, Shirogane-cho, Okawa riverside, and Minami-Shinbori burned. Lord Matsudaira Echizen-no-kami's middle mansion remained. Minato-cho and Nagasaki-cho suffered major damage. In the northern area of the same location, at Kita-Shinbori, Lords Kuze, Izu, and Doi suffered minor damage. Lord Tayasu was safe, but others suffered major damage. At Gyotoku riverbank, Koami-cho, Tokan-bori, the mansions of Lords Sakai, Koda, Hayashi, Kishu, Mikawa, Inoue Sendai, and Hori Izumo, as well as Ginza and the area from Jinzaemon-cho to Ningyo-cho street and Hatago-cho vicinity suffered major damage. Similarly, the opposite areas of Kobune-cho, Horie-cho, Hon-fune-cho, and Setomono-cho were completely destroyed.
