**Master of the house:**
"Last year, when they came to sell rice cakes, I ate as many as thirteen, but they frequently contained stones and sand, and they were really quite terrible. This year's rice cakes are splendidly made — delicious, delicious, delicious!"

**Maidservant:**
"That is only to be expected, sir. This year's rice cakes are made with excellent rice to begin with, and on top of that, 'Rabbit' diligently pounded with the pestle [literally: raised the pestle finely], and the reason there are no stones or sand in them is that we didn't leave it to the hired rice-cake pounders — instead, they were pounded with good vigorous shaking, just like an earthquake!"

○ An Explanation of Earthquakes and Great Waves (Tsunamis)

Earthquakes are caused by the two forces of yin and yang. In their most violent manifestations, they collapse mountains, fill in the sea, and overturn people's homes. That is to say, when the yang force is suppressed underground and cannot emerge, and the yin force is pressed in and cannot rise, it finally has no choice but to burst forth and cause trembling and shaking. In spring and summer, the yang energy rises from within the earth, while the yin energy sinks into the earth; in autumn and winter, the yin energy floats up from within the earth while the yang energy submerges and sinks within it.
