Takadaka 600+ koku, 31 ri from Edo - Kawashima Village

Takadaka 800+ koku, 17 ri from Edo - Kitamaki Village

These two villages are located along the Agatsuma River in the same province. On the 8th at around 10 AM, a debris flow (yamatsunami) caused mud, rocks, volcanic stones, and other materials to surge forth in great quantities. From Kawashima Village to the Mokuhashi checkpoint and Kitamaki Village, all houses and fields were completely swept away. Although some houses remained in the mountainous areas, the number of people swept away is unknown. We can only speculate that there are likely no survivors, though perhaps those who had gone out for farm work or to gather fodder might have survived, but this too is uncertain. Even if anyone did survive, reports indicate they would now be facing death from thirst and starvation.

Regarding the four locations on the Nakasendō - Karuizawa, Kutsukake, Oiwake, and Itahana - due to the great eruption of Mount Asama, there were tremors and thunder and lightning, and from the night of the 7th of this month, large stones and sand accumulated to approximately 1 shaku 1 sun in depth. As for Karuizawa post station, from the night of the same day, burning stones and sand fell, landing on house roofs, and the entire post station was completely burned down. Regarding injuries and deaths, Endō Kichiemon has reported that further investigation will be conducted. The above is hereby reported.
