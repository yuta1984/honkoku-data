The volcanic ash accumulated to a depth of three to four sun (about 9-12 cm), and from around noon on the 7th, it became dark as night. The rumbling did not cease and the ash fell even more heavily, causing five townhouses in the castle town to collapse. On the 8th at the hour of the Monkey (around 2-4 PM), the Tone River suddenly flooded, and volcanic stones rushed into the rice paddies and fields, but soon the water level returned to normal. Fortunately, there were no problems within the castle, and there were no injuries to people or horses. This report came from my retainers in the territory, so I am first reporting this matter. I will submit a detailed report later. The above.
July 11th

One: A separate report from the same person is as follows:
The Usui River in Kozuke Province suddenly flooded on the 8th of this month at the hour of the Monkey, washing away both the wooden bridge and the checkpoint. Therefore, the guards have been relocated to Kanai Village in the same area. This report came from the retainers stationed there. I am first reporting this matter. The above.
July 11th

One: The report submitted to Magistrate Endo Hyoemon is as follows:
　　Humbly submitting this petition through the post station relay

One: Since the end of last month, Mount Asama in Shinano Province has been shaking and raining down volcanic ash on several occasions. On the night of the 5th, it accumulated to a thickness of about five bu (approximately 15 cm). From six o'clock on the night of the 6th, it erupted tremendously.
