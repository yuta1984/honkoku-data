Due to flooding, both rice fields and newly developed fields were submerged. Although the water gradually receded, it remained pooled in low-lying areas for several days, causing rice and grass to rot, while upland fields suffered from withering. Furthermore, from around 6 o'clock in the morning on the 6th of this month until the evening of the 7th, volcanic ash occasionally fell on the fields. From the night of the 8th, the ashfall became particularly heavy, and by the 9th, approximately 3-4 sun (9-12 cm) had accumulated, causing severe damage once again to the rice and grass that had been restored. This matter has been reported by the retainers stationed in that area. Although the total damage assessment will be reported after the harvest, I hereby submit this preliminary report.
