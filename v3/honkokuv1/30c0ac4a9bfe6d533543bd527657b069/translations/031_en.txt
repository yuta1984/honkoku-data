In the year Dingchou (the first year of Taichu of Han), the sun had retreated to the 20th degree of the Dou lunar mansion. In the 9th year of Taiyuan of Jin (Jiashen), the sun had retreated to the 17th degree of Dou. In the 10th year of Yuanjia of (Liu) Song (Guiyou), the sun had retreated to the 14th degree of Dou. In the 12th year of Kaiyuan of Tang (Jiazi), the sun had retreated to the 10th degree of Dou. In the 4th year of Qingli of Song (Jiashen), the sun had retreated to the 5th degree of Dou. In the 4th year of Emperor Duzong (Wuchen), the sun had retreated to the 4th degree and 2 minutes of Dou. In the 1st year of Zhizheng of Yuan (Xinsi), the sun had retreated to the 10th degree of Ji. In the 17th year of Hongwu (Jiazi), the sun had retreated to the 7th degree of Ji. In the 3rd year of Jiajing (Jiashen), at dusk the lunar mansion Shi was at the meridian, and the sun had retreated to the 3rd degree of Ji. At the winter solstice of the 40th year of Wanli (Renzi), the sun was still at 3 degrees, 19 minutes, and 19 seconds of Ji on the ecliptic, and at 4 degrees, 4 minutes, and 25 seconds of Ji on the equator. The inner path opening (inner intersection) was at the 1st degree of Bi, and the outer path opening (outer intersection) was at the initial degree of Zhen. From that point to the 16th year of Chongzhen (Guiwei), over 31 years, the difference amounted to only 1 minute and 35 seconds.

[Interlinear note] This difference is a progression (advance), not a retreat. Tang Daowei measured the winter solstice of the Shunzhi Gengzi year and found the sun to be at 3 degrees and 29 minutes of Ji.

From Jiajing Jiashen to Chongzhen Guiwei — 120 years — the sun was still advancing and had not yet retreated. This shows that the subtle movements of the celestial path also have variations in speed, and cannot be fixed by any set number of years.

[Interlinear note] Jie Zi (Jie Xuan) says: The precession of the equinoxes is not merely a matter of unequal speed. You Zi (You Yan) made detailed measurements and found that there are cases where, after advancing, it retreats again.

Now, counting back from Chongzhen Guiwei to Qingli Jiashen — 600 years — the sun had shifted by more than 12 degrees, yielding a rate of 1 degree per 50 years. From Qingli Jiashen back to Kaiyuan Jiazi — 321 years — the sun had shifted by 5 degrees, yielding a rate of 1 degree per 64 years. From Kaiyuan Jiazi back to the Han Taichu Dingchou — 827 years — the sun had shifted by 10 degrees, yielding a rate of 1 degree per 83 years. From Taichu Dingchou back to the Zhuangxiang Wang Renzi year — 145 years — the sun had shifted by 2 degrees, yielding a rate of 1 degree per 73 years. From Zhuangxiang Wang Renzi back to Emperor Yao's Jiachen year — 2,028 years — the sun had shifted by 28 degrees, yielding a rate of 1 degree per 72 years. This is the result of a detailed, segmented analysis.
