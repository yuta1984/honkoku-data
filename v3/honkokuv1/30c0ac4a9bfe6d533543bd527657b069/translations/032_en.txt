Using the complete calendar to calculate: from Chongzhen Guiwei (1643 CE) going back to Emperor Yao's year Jiachen, spanning 3,920 years, a total difference of 58 degrees arises. That is, a difference of one degree occurs every 68 years, 7 months, 13 days, and 3 hours. In approximately 25,100-some years, a total difference of 365 degrees and a fraction accumulates, constituting one full circuit of the heavens, and the phenomenon returns to its original starting point.

[Interlinear note: You Xiong (游熊) says: this calculation takes the lunar mansion Xu (虚宿, Empty) as the starting point for measurement, thus in 25,000-some years it returns to Xu's original position. If one takes the mansion Dou (斗宿, Dipper) as the starting point, then likewise in 25,000-some years it also returns to Dou's original position.]

I further calculated: from Chongzhen Guiwei, going back to Emperor Ku's (帝嚳) year Jiazi, spanning 4,020 years, only a difference of 57 degrees arises. Approximately one year produces a difference of slightly less than one minute, nine seconds; every 70 years and 6 months produces a difference of one degree; and in 25,735 years, the difference amounts to one full circuit of the heavens. This is arrived at by treating the whole period together and then dividing.

[Interlinear note: You Xiong says: the reason the calculations of the various eminent scholars of ancient and modern times diverge — some higher, some lower — is simply that their measurements of degrees were not sufficiently precise. However, even this method of treating the whole and dividing also yields differing results.]

The calculations of scholars of successive dynasties each have their own basis. However, as for methods of computing the precession: scholars such as Guo Shoujing (郭守敬), Xu Heng (許衡), and Wang Xun (王恂) measured gnomon shadows and verified solar terms. They set the solar year (周歳) at 365 days, 24 minutes, and 25 seconds, and the full circuit of the heavens at 365 degrees, 75 seconds and 25 minutes. Comparing the larger and smaller values, there is a difference of 1 minute and 50 seconds; accumulating 66-plus years produces a regression of one degree. This they established as the "annual precession" (歳差).

Zhenyi (振之), also known as Master Li (李子), stated that the fixed stars also have movement at times. However, their movement is so gradual that within a hundred years the accumulated difference is not yet large; but in 49,000 years, a difference of one full circuit arises. Lianru (良孺), Master Xiong (熊公), held that in 25,000-some years one full circuit of difference accumulates. He stated that the eastward-moving heaven uses the ecliptic (黄道) as its axis, and not only has an east-west difference, but additionally has a north-south difference. Mizhi (密之), Master Fang (方公), stated that the degrees traversed by the fixed stars diverge from the degrees of the static heaven (静天), and thus the actual solar year and the sun's cycle come to differ from the degrees of the fixed stars. The sun, in the course of one year, moves from west to east through the 28 lunar mansions, covering 365 and one-quarter degrees, yet still produces a surplus difference of over one minute, so that in 60-some years a difference of one degree arises.
