The sea stretches flat for miles, like a blue plate. This claim that the spherical earth theory contradicts what we can actually see is the same kind of unprecedented great delusion. Human eyesight absolutely cannot extend beyond ten ri. When one goes out to the seashore at Shinagawa in Edo and looks across at both provinces of Bōsō, even though it is less than twenty ri across the sea, the borders of those lands cannot be seen clearly - they only appear blue like mountains. This is only on clear days without clouds or mist; when there is haze over the sea, one cannot see even two or three ri ahead. How then could anyone see from the top of the earth to the horizon in a single glance? Though they utter such absurd nonsense to refute the spherical earth theory, like stones thrown in the dark, not one hits its mark. Astronomy has surveying and measuring instruments, and is not empty theorizing like Buddhism. The matter of the spherical earth with people living all around it that they doubt was not established through speculation while sitting still. Western foreigners have sailed the surrounding seas, traveled east and west, and actually observed the round form of the earth. Regarding the four continents around Mount Sumeru that they speak of - perhaps because no wheel-turning sage king has appeared - not one person has ever drifted there. It is completely one-sided. For Westerners, the earth's rotation is a matter of direct observation, leaving no room for doubt. The accounts of Columbus and Vasco da Gama are recorded in the Zhifang Waiji.
