The towns in the area suffered major damage, and fires broke out on top of that, as described below.

**[Goshuin-chi (Shogunate-certified domain) / Zenkoji Temple Estate]**

- The interior framework and construction of the Nyorai-dō (Main Hall) suffered major damage.
- The Sanmon (Main Gate) and Kyōzō (Sutra Repository) suffered minor damage.
- The Nyorai-kyōsho (offering hall for the Nyorai) collapsed.
- One shrine (miya) and one subsidiary shrine (sha) within the temple precincts suffered major damage.
- The Niōmon (Deva Gate) and two shrines within the precincts were destroyed by fire.
- The residence of the Hongan Shōnin (Chief Priest of Daihongan), along with all adjoining structures, was completely destroyed by fire.
- The Daikanjin, Manyakudō, Gomadō, Shōtendō, domestic Buddha hall, palace-style sanctuary, sitting room, living quarters, and adjoining structures all suffered major damage.
- The inner service quarters, seven storehouses (dozō), watchtower, and main gate all collapsed.
- Forty-six sub-temple lodgings (bō) within the temple complex were destroyed by fire.
- One storehouse within the temple complex collapsed.
- Ten residences belonging to the Hongan Shōnin and the servants of the Daikanjin collapsed; eight of these were subsequently destroyed by fire.
- Two temples within the temple estate were destroyed by fire.
- At one location within the temple estate, the sitting room and kitchen quarters collapsed.
- Three hermitages (an) within the temple estate were destroyed by fire.
- Of two shrines within the temple estate, one was destroyed by fire.
- The Bishamondō (Hall of Bishamonten) and its offering hall collapsed.
- Thirty-five private residences collapsed.
- Additionally: two subsidiary shrines within the precincts collapsed; two wayside tea houses collapsed; one location was destroyed by fire.

- Deaths among those within the temple complex and among the Hongan Shōnin's and Daikanjin's attendants: **138 people** (including 5 of priestly rank)
  - Male: 54
  - Female: 69

- **2,350 townhouses collapsed**, of which **2,194 were destroyed by fire**.
- **Deaths among townspeople: 1,319**, of whom 624 were male and 695 were female.
- **Deaths among travelers: approximately 1,020**.
  - Note: Among those lodging at the temple complex and at inns, some inns had all their residents perish. The survival status of some lodging travelers remains unknown.

There are many injured persons, but none whose livelihoods are so severely affected as to be unable to carry on.
Thirty-five huts belonging to eta and hinin (outcast communities) were destroyed by fire.

　　*Reported on the 16th day of the 4th month.*

**[Goshuin-chi / Arayas Village / Iizuna Shrine Domain]**

- The gate entrance and front hall of Shinmu (shrine administrator) Jinshirō Nishina collapsed.
- The main residence of the same (Jinshirō Nishina) was half-collapsed.
- One storehouse of the same was half-collapsed.
- Forty private residences in the same (Iizuna Shrine) domain collapsed.
- Three residences were half-collapsed.
- One storage shed collapsed.
- Mountain and slope collapses occurred at six locations.
- Three peasants were crushed to death (one male, two female).
- Three peasants were injured (two male, one female).

Fields and paddies experienced ground fissures and land displacement at several dozen locations.　　*Respectfully submitted.*
