One: It was perhaps at the beginning of the Kansei era that a relevant book compiled by Lord Negishi Hizen-no-kami, while he was still serving as *Otomeyaku* (a senior administrative official), contained the following record — noting that Mount Fuji erupted during the Hōei era, and that Mount Asama erupted and trembled at the beginning of the Tenmei era. Between Hōei and Tenmei was a span of over seventy years; and from Tenmei to the current Ansei era is likewise over seventy years. The movements of heaven and earth are the workings of fire veins (*kamyaku*), and these things operate on periodic cycles. Similarly, human lifespans take roughly seventy to eighty years as the ordinary span of life. In all things, change has its appointed time — perhaps there are matters even beyond the power of gods and buddhas.

One: According to the *Tsūzoku Nendaiki* (Popular Chronological Record) — In the 92nd year of the reign of Emperor Kōan, the sixth human sovereign, in the sixth month of the year *kōshin*, there was a sudden trembling, and within a single night Mount Fuji appeared. In the fifth year of the reign of Emperor Kōrei, the seventh sovereign, in the year *kinoto-i*, the waters of Lake Ōmi (Lake Biwa) filled up overnight. Such wondrous events in ancient times are recorded in old chronicles. That within a mere six years a mountain could appear in a single night, and that a lake could form overnight in ancient times, is something that all people find difficult to believe.

Emperor Jinmu rose from Hyūga Province, subjugated Kyushu, gradually conducted eastern campaigns and conquered the provinces of San'in, San'yō, and Nankai, reached the Kinai region, and established the capital in Yamato Province. The eastern provinces were relatively slow to become civilized. The Ōshū region in particular broadly resisted imperial rule, requiring repeated eastern campaigns, but it is said that after Sakanoue no Tamuramaro appeared in the reign of Emperor Monmu, the eastern provinces gradually and fully came to submit to imperial rule. Perhaps it is from this process that (our understanding of) the geography of those regions came to be described. That mountains collapse, land sinks, or mountains erupt due to the upheavals of heaven and earth — these are things that people have frequently seen and heard even in recent times.
