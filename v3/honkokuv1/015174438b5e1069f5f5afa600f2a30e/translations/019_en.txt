Item: Suifu (Mito Domain) — Nearly nine-tenths of the domain retainers' residences collapsed, with many deaths.
　　Toda Chūdayū, Fujita Toranosuke, and Aizawa Kakuzō perished. Many other deaths were also reported.
　　It is said that over three hundred men of samurai rank and above died. The domain elder (karō), the lord's personal attendant (sobayōnin), Fujita (the Confucian official), and Aizawa were all pillars of the lord's administration — their loss is truly to be mourned.

Item: Many of Hotta's tenement buildings were heavily damaged.
　　Nine male servants died. Nagura Yajibei rushed immediately to the scene and treated the injured. The following morning, when a person was dug out from the rubble who appeared to be dead, Yajibei examined him and said, "This one looks like he could yet live," and treated him, whereupon the man revived and recovered.

Item: Nishi-no-Maru Shita御厩 (stables) — Suwabe Hachijūrō
　　A new two-story building had just been completed, and many guests had gathered to celebrate. Four or five serving women had also come, and a banquet was in full swing. The building collapsed catastrophically, sending Suwabe falling from the second floor to his death. Several guests were also reported injured. A subordinate official and his wife both died instantly. A Hatchōbori doshin (police constable) who happened to be present at the gathering that night also died on the spot.

Item: Hori, Lord of Mino Province
　　He was caught in the earthquake while traveling. As his party passed beneath the tenement buildings of Sasaki, Lord of Ōmi Province, the kago bearers (rikisha) were killed instantly.

Item: Matsumoto Jūrobei — His son died instantly.
(Inner Secretariat)

Item: Tatsuta Rokusuke — His entire household, including Rokusuke himself, died instantly. He was the son of Tatsuda Iwatarō.
(Accounts Office)

Item: Murai Gorō — Died while lodging in the Yoshiwara pleasure district. [A Chinese-style memorial inscription reads:] "An upright and composed samurai breathed his last in this place. His name shall not fade for a thousand years."

(Onari-dōri)

Item: Hori, Lord of Tamba Province
　　[Marginal note: His wife was near childbirth; both mother and newborn were crushed to death in the delivery. The following day, both were barely given burial; their memorial temple is Ryōunji in Ueno.]
　　His wife died instantly. As she was a woman in the late stages of pregnancy (about to give birth), when the earthquake began and the door lintel (kamoi) was about to fall, she held the lintel up with both hands and managed to get all the serving women out to safety without exception. Just as she herself was about to escape afterward, the house collapsed and she died instantly.
