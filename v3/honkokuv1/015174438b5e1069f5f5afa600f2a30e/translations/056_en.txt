Matsudaira Tamba-no-kami

As previously reported, in my domain of Matsumoto, Chikuma District, and Azumi District in Shinano Province, from around the Hour of the Boar (approximately 10 PM) on the night of the 24th day of the 3rd month, there was a severe earthquake, followed by repeated tremors thereafter. The damage is as follows:

- Various parts of the castle grounds outside the main fortifications, including residences and fallen walls
- Walls fallen at samurai residences and storehouses (dozō)
- Two completely collapsed storehouses in the castle town
- Two half-collapsed storehouses in the castle town
- Two completely collapsed storage sheds
- Agricultural land equivalent to over 579 koku rendered wasteland
- Ground fissures: 89 locations (total length: 6,445 ken)
- Road damage: 137 locations (total length: 30,233 ken)
- Landslides: 1,477 locations
- Locations where valley water was dammed up by landslides, creating pools: 41 locations
- Fallen trees: 28,408
- Irrigation channel collapses: 71 locations (total length: 900 ken)
- Houses flooded due to the damming of the Sai River: 28 households
- Completely collapsed houses in rural areas: 396 households
- Half-collapsed houses in rural areas: 761 households
- Completely collapsed village shrines: 3 locations
- Half-collapsed village shrines: 1 location
- Completely collapsed worship halls (haiden): 4 locations
- Completely collapsed temples: 3 locations
- Half-collapsed temples: 2 locations
- Completely collapsed storehouses: 91 locations
- Completely collapsed storage sheds: 79 locations
- Half-collapsed storage sheds: 74 locations
- Completely collapsed village communal storehouses (gōgura): 3 locations
- Completely collapsed public notice boards (kōsatsuba): 2 locations
- Completely collapsed checkpoint stations (kuchidome bansho): 2 locations
- Deaths (men and women combined): 67 persons
- Injured persons: 5
- Horses that died: 34

The above is as reported. Regarding the assessment of agricultural damage in koku, we will report this at a later date. We hereby submit this preliminary report. Respectfully submitted.

14th day of the 5th month

On behalf of the Lord of Ueda Castle, Kozuke District, Shinano Province,
Matsudaira Iga-no-kami,
submitted by his retainer Kataoka Kōrinosuke
