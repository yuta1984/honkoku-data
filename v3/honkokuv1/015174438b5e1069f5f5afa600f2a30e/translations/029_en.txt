I felt suspicious about this, thinking that perhaps the landslide site at Saigawa had collapsed. When I sent someone to inspect the river course, they returned and reported that there was definitely nothing to worry about. While the villages seemed peaceful, river banks can change suddenly and are unpredictable, so as a precaution I called together laborers and summoned people from the disaster-affected villages to inquire about the situation and explain matters regarding temporary repairs of flood damage and rice planting methods. Around 11 PM that same night, the water suddenly rose, and when it was announced loudly from village to village that floodwaters had reached the Tanba-jima area, I was greatly startled and immediately evacuated. The laborers I had summoned, as well as the village officials who had gathered, all departed, leaving only two officials behind.

The innkeeper Hanshichi was in a state of panic,
so I had those two men carry armor, while my retainers carried official documents and lanterns as we quickly evacuated with all my assistants to Sansai village in my domain, which was about twenty chō away on higher ground. Looking back along the way, I saw that water had entered all the villages. When we got close to Sansai village, floodwaters arrived, and for over one chō we had to run through water before finally reaching higher ground. If we had delayed any longer during our retreat, we truly would have met with water disaster - it was an extremely dangerous situation. Climbing up the mountainside of that village to observe, I saw that the entire Tanba-jima and Kawanaka-jima area was under water. Though it was a moonlit night, the extent of the floodwaters could not be measured and the flooding was so sudden that the villagers had no time to evacuate, especially since it was just before dinner time. Throughout the night, I ordered the construction of three raft-like structures from the timber of collapsed houses and prepared three boats, made portable meals, and waited for dawn while dispatching various rescue teams. We rescued without exception all those who were on rooftops in the floodwaters or had climbed trees, as well as those who had fled to higher ground. The people from villages in my domain were taken to Sansai village, and though it was another domain, I consulted with nearby Jindai post station to house them in temples, providing food support for ten days. When I inspected the villages along the river, the water level was over three jō deep with strong currents creating whirlpools and rushing flows.
