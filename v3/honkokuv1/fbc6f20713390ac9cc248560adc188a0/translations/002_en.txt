...extending to the provinces of Yamashiro, Kawachi, Izumi, Settsu, Wakasa, and Tamba — in the blink of an eye, across twenty-two provinces, mountains crumbled and the earth split open, with deaths and injuries numbering in the tens of thousands beyond counting. Inquiring in detail into this great earthquake, the province of Owari's Ōno District, whose highest peak is Mount Haku, had already been rumbling and trembling since the evening of the 25th, growing gradually more intense. The villages at the foot of the mountains had no peace of mind, and then in the early dawn of the 28th, heaven and earth shook all at once — flames and fire burst forth with a roar — before one could even cry out in alarm, how terrifying!

East Ōtani, Nishineyamura, Hosono, Nagashima — these villages and hamlets were transformed, across an area of three ri in every direction, into a flat expanse of lake water five to ten jō in depth, and houses, people, and horses all fell to the bottom, losing their lives without exception. The total count of the dead was approximately 2,785. The number of houses destroyed within Aichi Prefecture was approximately over 16,000. The number of collapsed buildings in the city of Nagoya was 2,588. Brick-built public facilities, machinery plants, and weaving companies were scattered in pieces — and yet, amid all this, the great castle tower of Owari Nagoya suffered not even a single broken roof tile. How rare in the world, what a wonder this time! Yan-re-he!
