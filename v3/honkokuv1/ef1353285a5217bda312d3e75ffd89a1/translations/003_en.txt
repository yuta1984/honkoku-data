(Continued from the previous page) At Zenkōji Temple, the main hall, the main gate, and the Daikanjin were unharmed; however, the Niōmon (Temple Gate), Honganji, twelve temples of the Chūshū sect, and many other temples suffered damage. In the districts of Ōmonchō, Gochō, and Gondōchō, and various other locations, buildings collapsed due to the earthquake and subsequently caught fire. In the town of Inariyama, all houses collapsed and were subsequently destroyed by fire. In Yahata, only a small number of townhouses collapsed, with some remaining standing, and no fire broke out. The Yahata Shrine was unharmed. Iiyama Castle, along with its castle town, suffered great damage from the earthquake, though no fire broke out. In Shinmachi, all houses collapsed, and subsequently fire broke out, followed by flooding — truly a catastrophic disaster. In Matsushiro, while some houses did collapse, the damage was reported to be relatively light. Other areas with comparatively light damage included Ueda, Matsumoto, Aida-gumi, Omi-gumi, Kawate-gumi, Ueno-gumi, Naruai-gumi, Hotaka-gumi, and the southern districts; as well as Suwa, Saku, Ina, and the areas from Zenkōji toward Echigo Province. The Matsukawa-gumi and Ōmachi-gumi areas also suffered lighter damage, as did Itoigawa (on the border with Echigo to the north of Shiro), Togakushi, Yamanaka Shinmachi, and the deep mountain areas. It was also said that damage in the Kantō region was minimal.

Furthermore, among the areas in the vicinity that suffered significant damage from the great earthquake — with collapsed houses and major destruction — were Kawanakajima, and in the mountain areas: Ōoka, Nakamaki, and Minamimaki. Additionally, in Kamijō, the Zen temple Gen'an'yōji had its main hall completely destroyed but suffered no flooding. Unshūji of the same sect had its ground collapse, caught fire, and was subsequently flooded. The village of Shimojin suffered a major ground collapse and flooding. Kōzenji Temple in Tanaka (a Zen temple) was half-destroyed. Tenshūji Temple in Miyahira-gumi, Imaizumi village (of the same sect) had its main hall completely destroyed. In Makinoshima, a large number of houses collapsed.
