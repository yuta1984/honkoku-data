Ayaniwa Village in the mountains to the south of the Sai River — this suffered only minor flood damage. From here, villages with major flooding and houses swept away include: Taira Village, then Murayama; on the northern mountain side of the Sai River: Anadaira Village, Wazawa, Shimonagai, Daianji Village, Sasadaira, Sewaki, Hōgyoku, Koichi, Kubodera Village, Koshibami Village, up to Kakuzawa. From here, villages on the north bank of the river: Fukiage Village, Ichi Village, Araki Village, Nakano Gosho Village, up to Kado, Shimonoto Village, and three other villages.

Among the villages of Kawanakajima affected by the flooding: Komatsubara Village, Yotsuya Village, Imazato Village, Dannohara Village, Imai Village, Komorisawa Village, Tambajima Village, Shimo-Hikano [Hikanna] Village, Naka-Hikano Village, Kami-Hikano Village, Kutsumachi Village, Mitsusawa Village, Umisawa Village, Kitahara Village, Minamihara Village, Okada Village, Yokogomi Village, Takada Village, Shibasawa Village, Gohei Village, up to Kado, Ai Village, Hirota Village, Tobe Village, Kita-Tobe Village, Kojimada Village, Ainojima Village, Ushijima Village, Mamejima Village, Yokodōri Village, Mizusawa Village, Kinebuchi Village, Nakazawa Village, Uminuma Village, Komori Village, Tōfukuji Village, Shimo-Yokota Village — between this Shimo-Yokota Village and Tambajima Village is a distance of approximately two ri (roughly 8 km). Within this stretch, water entered the river channel, and the flooding and sweeping away of houses was immense.

That the floodwaters were thus driven all the way to the great sea of Echigo (the Sea of Japan) was a calamity of unprecedented severity. Among the flooded and devastated areas, it appears that some places outside the Matsushiro domain were also included; the authorities (lords/magistrates) of each respective territory conducted inspections, and relief measures were provided accordingly...
