Those with relatively small temperature differences between winter and summer are called maritime climate. For example, England's average summer temperature is 63 degrees, and its average winter temperature is 37 degrees, with a difference of only 26 degrees. However, in Beijing, the temperature difference between the two seasons reaches 56 degrees. Thus, Britain's climate belongs to the maritime type, while Beijing's climate is of the continental type.
