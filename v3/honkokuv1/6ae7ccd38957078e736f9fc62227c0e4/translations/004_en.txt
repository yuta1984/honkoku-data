We should discuss the recently published works. These include: *Bowu xinpian* [A New Treatise on Natural Philosophy] (which presents five proofs for the roundness of the Earth), *Diqiu shuolüe* [A Brief Account of the Terrestrial Globe] (which presents four proofs), *Tangtian* [Researches on the Heavens] (which presents three proofs), *Liuhe congtan* [Miscellany of the Universe], and other such works. Their general purport is largely the same. Therefore, I shall now discuss their errors based on the text of the *Diqiu shuolüe*, as follows.

The *Diqiu shuolüe* (by Williams [徫理哲] of the United States) states: "The people of high antiquity mostly believed that the Earth was flat and neither rotated nor moved. They merely observed that the sun rose from the east and set in the evening, and the moon rose from the east and set at dawn, and gave the matter no particular thought."

This is a statement mocking the Chinese "Gaitian" (Canopy Heaven) theory and the Indian Mount Sumeru cosmology. The reason is that both Sanskrit and Chinese theories of the heavens hold that the Earth is flat and immobile, and that the sun and moon revolve horizontally above the Earth's surface. This stands in great contradiction to his theory of a spherical Earth. Thus he first seeks to demolish the teachings of the ancient sages — this is his slanderous speech.

The *Diqiu shuolüe* further states: "Today's astronomers, having examined the true principles of the matter, inform the populace that the Earth is not flat, but is round (tuányuán), in the shape of a ball." (The *Bowu xinpian* says it is "round like an orange.")

Here, when it speaks of "today's astronomers," there are in fact two theories. The first is the geocentric theory; the second is the heliocentric theory. The geocentric theory was brought into China during the Wanli reign period of the Ming dynasty (corresponding to the Tenshō and Keichō eras in our imperial country) by Johann Adam Schall von Bell (湯若望), Giacomo Rho (羅雅谷), Matteo Ricci (利瑪竇), Giulio Aleni (艾儒略), and others who entered China. According to this theory, the Earth hangs in space without moving, while the sun, moon, and stars revolve around it to create day and night. This is referred to as the "old theory," and Westerners have now abandoned it and no longer use it. The second theory, heliocentrism, holds that the sun is positioned at the center, with Venus and Mercury revolving around it, then the Earth revolving beyond them, and Jupiter, Mars, Saturn, and other planets revolving still further out. The fixed stars located in the outermost region are all immobile. The moon revolves around the Earth while together with the Earth also revolving around the sun.
