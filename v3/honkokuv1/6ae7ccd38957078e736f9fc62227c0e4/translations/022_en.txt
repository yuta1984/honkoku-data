**[Right page main text]**
At the edge, the boundary between light and shadow becomes clear, and for the first time the full shape of the handball (as a shadow) becomes visible. Furthermore, when an octagonal board of approximately two or three *sun* (~6–9 cm) in diameter is attached to the top of the same bamboo pole and stood upright on the ground, again only a circular shadow is cast on the ground, with its edges indistinct and diffuse. As the board is gradually lowered closer to the ground, the octagonal shape of the board gradually becomes apparent. That is to say, when the shadow is cast from a great distance, both the shadow of the round handball and the shadow of the octagonal board are cast on the ground in the same circular form, and their edges are diffuse and indistinct.

The boundary between the dark and bright areas is not distinct. Considering this, whether it is the sun illuminating the Earth and a shadow appearing on its far side, or the sun illuminating a handball and producing a shadow on its far side — although there is a difference in the size of the shadows, in the principle that an object illuminated by the same sun produces a shadow on the side facing away from the light, the underlying principle is exactly the same.
