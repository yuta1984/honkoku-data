It should be understood that they are the smallest forces. Therefore, among the attractive forces, the sun is established as having great force, the Earth and moon as having medium force, and the various planets as having small force.

In "Hakubutsu Shinpen" (New Compilation of Natural Philosophy), the "Theory of Tides Following the Moon" states: Morning high tide and evening low tide never fail to keep their schedule. (omitted) Now, regarding the power of attraction, the sun's force is the greatest, and the moon's force comes next. However, tidal waters necessarily follow the moon and rise because the moon and Earth are closest to each other. Therefore, its attractive force is also close. Someone asks: "If tidal waters are drawn by the moon's force, why do they become twice as large as usual during new and full moons?" The answer is: "During new and full moons, the sun and moon meet in conjunction. At this time, the sun and moon combine their forces and unite their power in attraction. Therefore, the tide rises longer and fuller." (omitted) When it comes to the time after the first and third quarters, the sun and moon divide their forces. Therefore, the high tide is not as great as before. It seems the moon's attractive force is ten parts, while the sun's attractive force is three parts. At this time, the three parts of force are actually reduced.

"Liuhe Congtan" (Six Unions Miscellany) also discusses this matter similarly, saying: When the moon orbits the Earth, it attracts seawater. The place facing the moon swells and becomes tide.
