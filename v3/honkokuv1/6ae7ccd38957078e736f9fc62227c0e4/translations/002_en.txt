New Treatise on Protecting the Dharma, Volume 2
　　　　　　　　　　　　　Composed by Shōkoku Dōjin

　　　Chapter on Refuting Heterodoxy

In the work "Bì Tiānzhǔ Jiào Shū" (A Refutation of the Catholic Faith), citing the doctrines advanced by Western Christianity (Catholicism), it is stated: "To worship and make offerings to heaven, earth, the sun, the moon, bodhisattvas, gods, buddhas, and such things, is to violate the great commandments of the Lord of Heaven, thereby incurring deep sin before the Lord of Heaven," and so forth. Heaven, earth, the sun, and the moon are objects of reverence shared by all three traditions (Confucianism, Buddhism, and Shinto). Yet these Christians seek to abolish the rules and norms of those three traditions. (Note: The Ming dynasty work "Pòxié Jí" [Collection Refuting Heterodoxy] and, more recently, "Kiyu Shōgen" [Minor Words of Idle Anxiety] both discuss this matter, and should be consulted.) Although they desired to spread their heterodox doctrines, there were none who believed them. Therefore, they fabricated the theory of the "two spheres known to Heaven" [i.e., the heliocentric/spherical earth theory], and established what they presented as empirical, measured evidence. (Note: By "establishing empirical and measured evidence," they mean the following: since the space between heaven and earth is vast, it is difficult for people to verify such claims directly. Therefore, they display all manner of ingenious and curious instruments to dazzle and confuse people, or they use precise astronomical calculations to deceive learned scholars. Because this is presented as a rational theory demonstrated visibly before one's very eyes, people find it impossible not to believe it.) That they use this to lure and draw in the people of the world is something already discussed previously.
