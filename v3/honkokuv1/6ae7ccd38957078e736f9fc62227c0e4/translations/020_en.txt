At times, a black object obscures and covers the moon, causing the moon to lose its light. This is called a lunar eclipse. This black object is the shadow of the Earth (chiqiu/地球). The reason for this is that when the sun and moon come to directly opposite positions, and the Earth lies in the middle blocking sunlight, a long, pointed black shadow inevitably extends behind the Earth. Therefore, the sun's light cannot directly reach the moon's body. This is called the "Earth's shadow" (地影). It is said that when the intersection of the moon's orbital path (the white road/白道) enters this Earth shadow, the moon loses its light. Thus, when the sun illuminates the Earth and the moon enters the shadow cast on the far side, the moon becomes covered by the Earth's shadow. Observing how this Earth shadow covers the moon, its shape is perfectly round (団円). Since this great Earth produces such a perfectly circular shadow, it is offered as proof that this great Earth is spherical in shape.
