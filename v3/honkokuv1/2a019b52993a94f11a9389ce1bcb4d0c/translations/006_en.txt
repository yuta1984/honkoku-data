The liquid from sample B was boiled to evaporate the water, and when the volume was reduced by half, crystals formed on the surface. This was sodium chloride. Its weight was 65 sen 9 bu 9 rin. Sulfuric acid was added to the remaining liquid, which was boiled and dried solid, then heated over fire. It was extremely difficult to melt, confirming it to be potassium. Its weight was 8 bu.

The solid matter from sample C was treated with acetic acid, and the dissolved portion was boiled and dried, yielding a small amount of calcium. The portion that did not dissolve in acetic acid was treated with hydrochloric acid, and the supernatant was boiled and dried solid, yielding alumina. A small amount of earthy matter that did not dissolve in hydrochloric acid settled at the bottom of the container, which upon examination was found to be silica. The proportions of these three substances cannot be determined in detail.
