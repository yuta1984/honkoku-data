【Difference in the Speed of the Moon's Motion (continued)】

Before and after [the initial point of the cycle], the daily motion exceeds the mean motion of thirteen degrees, thirty-six minutes and a fraction by approximately one degree and thirty minutes. At the half-revolution point, the motion falls short of this mean motion by approximately one degree and thirty minutes. At the quarter-revolution point, the motion equals the mean motion, with neither excess nor deficit. Like the sun's motion, this is also divided into four stages: during the "fast-beginning" (疾初) and "slow-ending" (遅末) phases, the motion exceeds the mean; during the "fast-ending" (疾末) and "slow-beginning" (遅初) phases, the motion falls short of the mean. Each of the four quadrants takes six days and eighty-eight units of time (刻), totaling twenty-seven days and fifty-five units—this is one complete cycle of the moon's fast-and-slow motion. This cycle is called "nyūten" (入転, entry into the anomalistic cycle). The first day is called the "first day of nyūten," followed by the second and third days, with the final day called "tenshū" (転終, end of the cycle). All of these are names assigned within the framework of the Nine Paths (九道), and collectively refer to the moon's motion along the White Path (白道).

【The White Path (白道): Also Called the Nine Paths (九道)】

Regarding the rules governing the moon's right-revolving orbit: the moon travels thirteen degrees, thirty-six minutes and a fraction per day, completing one revolution inside and outside the ecliptic in twenty-seven days and twenty-one units of time. Along this path there are distinctions known as the Nine Paths (九道). The central path is the ecliptic (黄道), and the moon travels in and out of the ecliptic by six degrees on each side, following an oblique course. This oblique motion is called "yūrin" (游輪, wandering wheel). Within this, the southern region is called the "Vermilion Path" (朱道), the northern region the "Black Path" (黒道), the eastern region the "Azure Path" (青道), and the western region the "White Path" (白道). The central ecliptic is a single line, while the remaining paths—Azure, Red, White, and Black—each split into two paths inside and outside the ecliptic, forming eight paths in total.
