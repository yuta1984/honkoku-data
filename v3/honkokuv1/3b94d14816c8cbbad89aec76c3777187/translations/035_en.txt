What was described above regarding precession are all theories from ancient times. In the Tianjing Huowen (Celestial Classic Questions), it states that precession is the rightward motion of fixed stars. This is a modern theory with substantial foundation. Therefore, scholars since the Ming dynasty all discuss the rightward motion of fixed stars. Since that heaven is extremely high and distant, with calculations showing orbital motion spanning over twenty thousand years, and since the beginning of calendrical methods we have not even observed half a cycle of the fixed stars, it is natural that the constant for precession cannot be determined.

　　Time Difference
The arrival of day and night comes early in eastern countries and late in western regions. When east and west are separated by approximately 140 li, the eastern country experiences dawn one ke earlier and sunset one ke earlier. This is called time difference. The timing of solar and lunar eclipses follows the same principle. Moreover, the twenty-four seasonal divisions and the degrees of solar positions change year by year, so the time divisions of the twenty-four seasons are also not identical throughout history. This is also called time difference. Furthermore, the duration of day and night varies among the four seasons. Or when territories are separated north and south, the duration of day and night are not the same. This is also called time difference.
