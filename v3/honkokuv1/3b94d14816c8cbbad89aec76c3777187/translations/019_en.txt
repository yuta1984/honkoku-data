Therefore, there is nothing in heaven, above ground, or below ground that is not composed of the qi of the Five Elements. Heaven generates qi, and on earth it becomes matter, with this qi flowing in between. The qi of heaven and the matter of earth are unified and never separate from each other. The five qi are cold, heat, dryness, humidity, and wind—namely, the four seasons and five qi of spring, summer, autumn, winter, and the earth element period. Warm, hot, cool, cold, and flourishing qi are always present, and there is no place on earth without these four seasonal five qi. However, due to the difference between the orthodox and the deviant, there are variations in the abundance and intensity of these qi. Because of this, the calendars of different countries are not the same. Some countries establish one year as four seasons, some divide one year into three seasons, or in temperate countries, one year is established as eight qi. In constantly warm regions,

the time of extreme heat and great heat is considered summer, the times of slightly milder heat before and after are spring and autumn, and the time when warm and cool qi arrive is winter. Therefore, with two springs, two summers, two autumns, and two winters, one year is completed. This is a country of eight qi. Countries in the hot temperate zone lack the extreme cold of orthodox qi, and countries in the cold temperate zone lack the extreme heat of orthodox qi.

Regarding the Length of Day and Night and Time Divisions
In the heavens, there is no day and night. Although it is always day, since the sun constantly illuminates half of the earth's sphere, from when the sun rises in the east until it sets in the west is day, and when it sets in the west at dusk, the sunlight creates a shadow on the earth, and when the earth's surface becomes dark, this is called night.
