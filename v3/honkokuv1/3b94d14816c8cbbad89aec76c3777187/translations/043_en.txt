**Catalog of Books Published by Bunkindō**
(Kawachiya Tasuke, Karamonochō, Shinsaibashi, Osaka)

· *Xiaoxue* [Elementary Learning] (with katakana readings, 2 large volumes)
　Although several editions exist in the world, many contain errors in reading. This edition has been corrected to serve as an aid for basic reading practice.

· *Tōin Wage* [Japanese Glossary of Chinese Pronunciation] (2 volumes)
　Records without omission the methods of learning Chinese pronunciation, poetry, musical notation for the koto, and other words in daily use.

· Master Hakuseki's *Kishinjron* [Treatise on Demons and Spirits] (hiragana edition, 2 volumes)
　Discusses in detail the various theories found in the classics and histories, and argues clearly about their merits and faults.

· *Igaku Shomon* [First Questions on Learning] (by Master Shūnan, hiragana edition, 2 volumes)
　This work thoroughly discusses the principles of learning, debates on Confucianism, Buddhism, and Shinto, the rise and fall of successive dynasties in Japan and China, and the strengths and weaknesses of various Confucian scholars, greatly resolving the confusion of beginning students. Written so that even children can read it easily.

· *Tengaku Shiyō* [Essentials of Astronomy] (by Nishimura Tōri, 4 volumes)
　Written in katakana, recording the profound principles of astronomy in an easy-to-understand manner.

· *Kōkyō Keiten Yoshi* [Teacher's Companion to the Classic of Filial Piety] (1 volume)
　Reading guides in kana are provided in the upper column to make it easy even for young children to read, while detailed commentary in hiragana is placed beside the main text to make the meaning of the passages easy to understand. Even those without formal education or literacy can study without a teacher.

· *Shikwashi* (2 complete volumes)　　*Shōkyō Kōgi* [Lectures on the Book of Documents] (with commentary, 8 complete volumes)

---

· *Gazu Saiyūdan* [Illustrated Account of Travels to the West] (by Master Shiba Kōkan of Edo, self-illustrated, 5 volumes)
　A travel account from Edo to Nagasaki. In this work, the master records the curious tales and rare accounts from the places he visited, written in hiragana with illustrations. It records interesting subjects such as whale hunting in the western seas and the Chinese quarter in Nagasaki.

· *Kaiseī Naniwamaru* [Revised Naniwamaru] (6 volumes)
　A comprehensive listing of town names in Osaka, directories of merchants, famous places throughout the province, and all other matters, recorded without omission.

· *Osaka Miyatera Meguri* [Tour of Osaka Shrines and Temples] (small volume, 1 volume)　　*Osaka Jisha Junpaiki* [Record of Pilgrimage to Osaka Shrines and Temples] (1 volume)

· *Osaka Meisho Hitori Annai* [Self-Guide to Famous Places in Osaka] (small volume, 1 volume)　　*Shitennōji Garan Ki* [Record of Shitennōji Temple Complex] (1 volume)

· *Shinsen Ise Saiken Ki* [New Detailed Guide to Ise] (1 volume)
　Records in detail road distances, famous places, historical sites, and shrine pilgrimages along the route.

· *Ryōmen Nendaiki* [Two-Sided Chronicle] (accordion-fold book, 1 volume)
　The foremost hiragana chronicle, recording in detail the notable events year by year from the Age of the Gods through the imperial reigns, from the first year names up to the present.

· *Taisei Nendai Kōki* [Comprehensive Grand Chronicle] (accordion-fold book, 1 volume)
　Records Japanese and Chinese history arranged in upper and lower columns, distinguished by red and black ink to make the chronology easy to follow. Records in detail the rise and fall of successive dynasties, remarkable events, and strange occurrences, covering the eras of the Twenty-One Histories, military chronicles, and the Twenty-one Imperial Poetry Anthologies. An overview chronicle of Japan and China indispensable for scholars.

· *Enseki Zasshi* [Miscellany of Flint and Stone] (6 volumes)
　A collection of essays by Master Kyokutei Bakin, discussing popular beliefs and traditions of Japan and China — an entertaining read.
