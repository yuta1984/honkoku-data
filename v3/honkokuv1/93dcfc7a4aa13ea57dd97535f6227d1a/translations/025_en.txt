The confrontation was terrifying. Already on the early morning of May 24th, they prepared for battle formation, with Black Cloud and Horizontal Cloud forming the vanguard. They raised signal fires and beat large drums thunderously, advancing to attack as far as Kumoba-ga-hara. The deity of the pass saw this and ordered the bird messenger to quickly inform Mount Hakuun through express messengers.

At Mount Hakuun, everyone panicked and made a commotion. They gathered several tengu individually and deployed them to Usui Pass to defend, as could be seen. However, realizing that their small force would be difficult to withstand the attack, they pondered military strategy and devised clever schemes. After several days had passed, on June 27th, they decided in council to advance again. Since there was internal communication from Usui to Mount Hakuun, they urgently informed the deities of Haruna, Akagi, and other gods of Kōzuke and Agatsuma provinces, requesting reinforcements. Each deity brought their war fans, untied the mouths of their wind bags, and with forces numbering in the hundreds of thousands, they stretched from Ōzasa to Mount Nakao and the peaks of Usui Pass like the teeth of a comb.
