It cannot be helped; as everyone knows of worldly matters, they were variously restrained to defer to the times, and reluctantly withdrew. Furui Gongen then spoke: "I stand on the border between the two provinces and take no one's side, but as the deity of Kozuke has said, since Mount Asama is in my neighborhood, I am plagued every single day by its smoke and find it most disagreeable." He then also raised the matter of gambling at Hakuun Mountain, but upon hearing a mocking remark that there were also gods fond of gambling in Shinano Province, Chikatsु interjected, "One who is fond of gambling — could it surely be Yawata, or perhaps myself? What one does not do in plain sight is also difficult to achieve on the path of virtue." Taking Furui-dono's pointed remarks lightly, as if hanging a torn hood over one's ears, and becoming exceedingly angry, someone said, "No, that is a mistake — it is not about you, there is another matter to criticize."
