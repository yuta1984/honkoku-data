A pillow made of one's elbow during a sorrowful sleep under the traveler's sky
Here, around the autumn of the first year of Tenmei (1781), the year of the rat, there was a wandering monk who despised fame and profit, loathed this sorrowful world, and sought to escape the dust of secular life. Having no fixed dwelling in any province, he was a cloud-and-water practitioner of uncertain residence.
He was over sixty years of age, traveled through various provinces with a bamboo staff, crossed the Ina road from Mount Akiba in Tōtōmi Province, practiced asceticism at Kinugasaki, then visited Suwa Shrine with a private wish.
