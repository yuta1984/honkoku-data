Those who perish, and those fearful things that are unrelated to good and evil, continue to exist.
Here in purity, here in tranquility, playing in the garden of the gods,
in mere stillness and vastness, guarding the dwelling place of virtue. Though the
customs of the present time are said to be youthful, ministers are not truly ministers, and fathers,
though they are fathers, do not have children who are truly children. Though the gods wish to protect,
the people do not accept; though Buddha intends to save, the masses are faithless.
Those above are not truly above, those below are not truly below. What a
detestable floating world this is. Thus, from the beginning of the Meiwa era, heaven and earth have manifested various

supernatural phenomena to instruct the people, yet the stubborn, foolish, and faithless
people engage only in various frivolous theories, and there are none who dare apply their
hearts seriously. Sometimes a comet appeared in the eastern sky,
and afterwards a great white star appeared in the north. Also,
in the summer of Meiwa 7 (1770), year of the Tiger, a star pierced through the moon, and in the autumn of An'ei 8
(1779), year of the Boar, there was a rumbling sound from the southeast, and on the second day of the tenth month,
the sky became hazy and ash fell like rain.
Afterwards, there were such things as the appearance of auspicious stars and gold and silver stars.
