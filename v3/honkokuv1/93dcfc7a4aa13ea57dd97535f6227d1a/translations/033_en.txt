The earth collapsed and shook, vegetation withered and fell, and the scene was such that even the battles between King Tang and the tyrant Zhou, or the battles of our imperial court in ancient times, could hardly surpass this terrible spectacle.

As the night of the seventh day fell, the Agatsuma and Gunma forces secretly circled around to the Ōsasa entrance, attempting to attack uphill. They climbed the narrow paths here and the steep cliffs there, struggling until they finally crept near the Kambara area. Just as all forces had assembled and were making preparations to launch their uphill attack, unexpectedly the Manzawa and Shirane forces, having planned this beforehand, fired their prepared stone fire arrows and discharged their large cannons. Hidden forces everywhere rose up at once, firing consecutive artillery barrages in their attack.

The Eastern Sea forces stationed at Asama raised great signal fires. The eruption was so violent that it seemed the southern seas would dry up, raining down mud, shaking from the thirty-three heavens above to the depths of Naraku and Abi hell below. Heaven seemed to shatter and earth to collapse, as if this were the time of cosmic conflagration when the world itself would be destroyed.
