As prices continued to rise, not only Kōzuke Province but also Shinano Province saw an incalculable number of people perish from starvation. Lords and local magistrates showed compassion in various places, making provisions for relief and loans to the extent that their hands could reach, but given the sheer number of those affected, it was far from sufficient. People sold their cooking pots and cauldrons to pay for a morning meal, sold their hoes to pay for an evening meal, and eventually sold off every last household item until there was nothing left to sell. They dug up kudzu roots and bracken roots, consumed every last sprout of buckwheat, buckwheat noodles made from its flowers, and even the withered leaves of datsuma, stripping the land bare. They resorted to eating roasted rice-bran cakes, dumplings made from wheat bran, mushroom porridge, and straw cakes—and yet even these were exhausted. With no recourse left, some abandoned their elderly and children and set out with no clear destination. Others carried children on their backs or in their arms, led parents by the hand, and set off abandoning their homes and fields, taking to the road as their only fate in this fleeting world. Along the roads, abandoned children and those who had collapsed lay everywhere, a sight too painful to behold.
