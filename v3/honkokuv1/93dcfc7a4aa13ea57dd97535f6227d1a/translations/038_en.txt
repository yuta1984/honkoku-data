Not only in Kozuke Province but even in Shinano Province, countless people died of starvation. The feudal lords and local administrators showed mercy in various places, providing relief and loans to the extent possible, but with so many people in need, their efforts could not reach everyone. People sold their cooking pots for breakfast and their hoes for dinner, selling all their household goods without exception. They dug up kudzu and dioscorea roots, as well as bracken roots, gathered buckwheat sprouts and flowers, paulownia young leaves and even dead leaves until everything was exhausted. They ate parched rice, rice bran, wheat bran dumplings, mushrooms, korasen, and straw rice cakes until now they had exhausted all means. Some abandoned their elderly and children and wandered off into an uncertain future. Others carried or held their children, pulling their parents by the hand or foot, abandoning their homes and rice fields to go forth into the floating world. Abandoned children and collapsed people lay in the streets - a sight too terrible to behold, and the land had become a realm of lawlessness. A person from a mountain village, in his idle moments, composed a poem on the theme of "specialties of a famine year."
