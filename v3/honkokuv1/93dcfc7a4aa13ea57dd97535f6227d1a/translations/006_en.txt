Not knowing what province this was during his wandering pilgrimage, as evening approached and he sought lodging, wandering here and there, he encountered what appeared to be a shrine priest wearing an eboshi court cap and hitatare robe. Seeing the monk's condition, the priest asked, "Are you a traveling monk seeking lodging?" The monk replied, "Indeed, I would like to borrow lodging anywhere, no matter what kind of place it might be, to pass the night." The priest said, "If that is so, please come with me," and led the way.

The destination they reached was, strangely, beyond a silver seaside, with reeds and pine-cypress growing thickly, long gates and vermillion great gates extending deep into the sky, gleaming brightly as stars moved across the sacred fence. Looking up, he saw "Dōjin-kan" (Gate of the Same Dust) written on the gate's plaque - whose brushwork could this unprecedented calligraphy be? Led by his guide through the gate, after a short while there stood what could be called magnificent structures, piled high and adorned with silver and gold, with jeweled roof tiles arranged in rows. It was like the Epang Palace of Qin or the Hōrai Palace. From what seemed to be an inner entrance hall, he was guided along corridors and shown to a side room.
