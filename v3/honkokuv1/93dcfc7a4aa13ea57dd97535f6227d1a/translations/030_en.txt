The great general's attire for the day of departure consisted of a hitatare armor with seikai-nami yasurabu pattern, wearing a Mito shin-uta helmet, carrying a five-monme silver sword, with eastern-style gauntlets and shin guards decorated with gold dust extending to his hands, riding a magnificent horse with thick leg hair adorned with a saddle bearing official tax stamps, equipped with ryōgae-sagari tassels and modern dyed reins, wearing the prestigious sword of annual provincial service, and with the deity Jōzen-genan as the vanguard, he commanded: "No matter how many besieging forces there may be, those who do not serve His Majesty's interests shall not advance even one step east of Usui Pass," and waited in readiness.

When the call for battle on the fifth day of the seventh month was announced, conch shells blew loudly, war drums were beaten, and they advanced to Usui Pass. The Kantō forces also positioned themselves at Sakamine with Niōmon as their rear guard, and both sides raised their battle cries in unison, with arrow-shooting shouts that shook heaven and earth, scattering sparks from dusk onward.
