After being guided deeper into the interior and waiting for a while, two elegant children aged fourteen or fifteen brought fragrant thin tea in silver tea bowls and golden tea pots, serving him with great care. Afterward, the evening meal was beautifully arranged with various delicacies. Looking toward the palace quarters, it seemed there was a rare guest, as lanterns were lit in rows, creating a very lively atmosphere. After some time, the shrine attendant who had guided him earlier appeared and said, "My lord wishes to meet with the traveling monk and hear various stories," and immediately led him to the great hall. Looking far into the distance, it was mysteriously deep and profound, with silver candles emitting pure smoke. The space seemed as vast as a thousand tatami mats, with numerous court officials in various formal attire sitting in dignified rows. Looking closely, there were people with long noses, people with pointed beaks, people with wings, and people with dragon heads. The one who appeared to be the divine lord wore black court dress with a golden dragon crown.
