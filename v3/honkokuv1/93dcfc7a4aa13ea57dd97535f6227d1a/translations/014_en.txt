The shrines of Eta and Nagakura (note: Eta is located in the Kawakami Gosho-daira area, and Nagakura is in the Kutsukake area) have only their names remaining, with nothing substantial left. Although Otomo-sama fortunately survives, it has been relegated to barely existing in a corner of Mochizuki, living in a wretched dwelling like a rented house at the edge of the Kirigaya and Ontake shrines. Even in such circumstances, sitting proudly in the lowest seat is regrettable, but dismissing this sentiment, Tanokuchi Shinkai declared, "No, that thinking is misguided. The claim that Eta, Nagakura, and Otomo have only their names remaining from ancient times to now while being enshrined within our shrine grounds is false. Mochizuki is a subsidiary shrine, and our shrine is definitely the main shrine," speaking with a changed countenance. Otomo retorted, "There is no shrine other than ours," leading to mutual arguing of "No, that's not so" and "No, it is so," nearly escalating into a major disturbance. At this point, Aiso Myojin intervened, bringing Otomo Myojin to his side and attempting to calm the situation, saying, "Even if we speak of ancient matters, even Nikko Gongen and ourselves have had our ancestral shrine lands taken away. While we find it disagreeable to watch from the sidelines, it is the way of the times."
