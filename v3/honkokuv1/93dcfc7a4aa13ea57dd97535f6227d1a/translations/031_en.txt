They fought, but there was no clear victor, so that day's battle ended in a draw, and they waited for the next day.

At dawn on July 6th, the battle began around 6 AM (the hour of the snake), and from evening they raised smoke signals such as senba-baka (thousand horse fire) and ransei (scattered stars), shot stone crossbows, and attacked with lightning-like force. The Kanto forces gradually broke their encirclement and retreated to Domine peak. The Asama forces pressed their advantage and attacked, causing the main force to break and retreat to Sakamoto.

The Asama forces pushed forward to Domine peak and set up camp. The Kanto forces climbed to Tsumugi-nagahara and looked out at the enemy's distant position, where they saw millions of evil demons each spewing flames and baring their teeth of greed and lawlessness. Their numbers were beyond calculation. They reported this situation to Mount Hakuun. After deliberation at Mount Hakuun, it appeared that the majority of Asama forces had turned toward the main attack. This meant that only the general seemed to remain at Asama's main castle. Therefore, they secretly attacked from Agatsuma County's Umanokami-dake Ozasa entrance.
