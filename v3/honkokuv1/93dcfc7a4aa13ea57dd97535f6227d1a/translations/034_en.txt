With great force they attacked, causing the main force of the Kanto army to be thoroughly defeated. The flowing blood carried wooden pestles, and they fled in complete disarray all the way to the area around Tago Midori. The Azuma forces that headed toward the rear attack were surrounded from all directions. When they tried to flee back, they encountered the bottomless Tone River - where the old monk Sunada Awa-no-nyudo and Oyama River, great rivers flowing from both sides with towering rocks dozens of fathoms high, so deep that one could not see the middle or bottom. Like the mountain gorges at the foot of Mount Emei in Tang China, where both banks narrow and contract - into this place, Oyama Hangan Tokijo was ordered to hurl fire-stones spanning thirty-five to thirty-six ken, quickly piling up burning sand. In a short time, water accumulated like a great sea, and for four to five ri the area became like the bottom of purgatory with boiling water, a sea of molten iron where countless people burned to death. Those who barely managed to flee here and there and reached the downstream area, thinking they had found safety, suddenly found that burning sand released all at once, with fire-stones thrown and scattered like juggling balls, pushed out enough to dam the Tone River, rising twenty [fathoms high]
