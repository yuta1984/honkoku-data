The Assembly of Demon Gods at Mount Asama

When the three springs had already passed and it had become the summer of the Year of the Rabbit in Tenmei 3 [1783], the eightfold clouds over Mount Asama were cut away using the sharp sickle of the Yakigama, and a divine mandate was issued saying "Be aware of all things that have come to pass." With this, Mount Asama at last became clear and bright once more.

Mount Asama brooded deeply, thinking: "This mountain of mine has since ancient times been a sacred ground free from all outside interference, yet I have been made to receive such punishment — this is entirely the doing of Shirakumoyama. I shall clear this resentment." Summoning its subordinate demons, it spoke of its fury to Kokumō Dōji and Yokogumo Dōji, and then commanded Kafū Dōji and Ikazuchi Dōji to send word to the demons of all the provinces. First, being nearby, there came Manzawa Tarō, Shirane Gorō, the descendants of Momiji of Mount Togakushi, Wakuraba, the heir of Shuten Dōji of Senjōgatake in Tamba Province known as Shurando, the descendants of Ibaraki Dōji called Subaraki, and the great Takamandōji of Mount Suzuka in Ise Province.
