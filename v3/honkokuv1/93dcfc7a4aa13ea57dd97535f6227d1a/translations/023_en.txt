The Gathering of Demons and Spirits at Mount Asama

After three years had passed and it became the summer of the third year of Tenmei (1783), the Year of the Rabbit, the eight-fold clouds over Mount Asama were cut away with sharp burning sickles, and with the divine pronouncement "let all things be known," Mount Asama gradually became clear and bright.

Mount Asama pondered deeply: "This mountain of mine has been a sacred place forbidden to entry since ancient times, yet I have received such punishment entirely due to the machinations of Mount Hakuun. I shall clear this resentment." And so it summoned its demon subordinates. It spoke of its anger to Black Cloud Boy and Horizontal Cloud Boy, then immediately commanded Base Wind Boy and Thunder Boy to spread word among the demons of all provinces.

First, being nearby, came Manzawa Taro, Shirane Goro, the descendants of the autumn leaves of Mount Togakushi, Wakuraba, Sake-ran Boy who was a descendant of the sake-drinking demon of Mount Senjodake in Tanba Province, Tsuburaki who was a descendant of the victorious Ibaraki Boy, Takaman Boy of Mount Suzuka in Ise Province, and other renowned demons from various provinces and lands. Opening their eyes of avarice and jealousy, baring their fangs of the ten evils and five grave sins...
