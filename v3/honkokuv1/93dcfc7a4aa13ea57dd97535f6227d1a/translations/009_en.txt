[Seated among them were also the deities of] Nakanodake, Arani, Myogi, and Hakone Shrine, along with the divine spirits of Usui, Kanra, Tago, and Midori districts, all assembled and seated in order. On the right side, with Togakushi-san Daigongen in the seat of honor, were: Asama-san Daigongen, Tateshina and Yashio Daigongen, Kawakami Kinminesan Daigongen, Kawanakajima Hachiman-gu, Tanokuchi Shinkai Daimyojin, Chikatsu Daimyojin, Yawata-juku Hachiman-gu, and the shrine deities of Matsubara and Yochi, as well as those of Uminokuchi, Shiratori, Komoro Gion, Ueda Omiya, Nezumi Myojin, Nishinomiya, and Shioda Shrine — and further, the deities of Sanada, Hakusan, Mochizuki, Otomo, and Kasugane, extending all the way to the tutelary deities (ubusuna-gami) of villages throughout Saku and Chiisagata districts — all seated in proper court dress with their robes arranged.

The presiding deity spoke: "The reason I have summoned all the gods together tonight is that there is a matter of divine oracle (kotoyosashi) from the deities of Izumo, concerning which I wish to hold a divine council (kamubakari). It is for this purpose that I have invited all of you here. Let me first present the matter in full."
