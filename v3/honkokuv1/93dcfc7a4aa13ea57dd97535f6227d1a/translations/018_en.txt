In Tsukushi there was Hikosan Buzenbo, in Shikoku there was Shiramine no Sagamibo, Daisen no Hakibo, Iizuna no Saburo, Fuji Taro, and Omine's Reizeniki with his faction, Kurama's Sojibo, Daiyuzan's Doryubo, Akiba's Sanjakubo and Rokushakubo, and extending to Hyakushakubo and Senjibo - countless arrogant and wicked konoha-tengu (leaf tengu) gathered together and deliberated on various schemes.

Hoshibo spoke: "During this recent gathering at Suwa shrine, the gods of Shinano united to accuse us of wrongdoing and mocked us in various places. Moreover, Mount Asama's proclamation contained outrageous slander that greatly insulted us - such treatment is truly infuriating. Speaking of our origins, our father was called Myozen Gekko-o, and our mother was called Konpira Yasha-fujin, and they had eighteen princes. Of these, eight entered the Buddhist path, while the remaining ten princes rode on white foxes and descended to this land of Akitsushima during the reign of Emperor Kinmei, the thirtieth human sovereign, landing at Naniwa bay in Settsu province. Thereafter, the arts of Eijutsu-tengu spread to Go province..."
