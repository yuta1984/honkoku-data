[The text continues from the previous page regarding the catfish legend:] A stone nail (peg) is used to pin it down. This is the "Kaname-ishi" (keystone) at Kashima Shrine in Hitachi Province. It is said that, fearing the great catfish would cause massive earthquakes, divine power was used to do this — which seems for the most part to be wild and unfounded. Let us hear the true principles behind this.

In answer: Generally speaking, thunder and earthquakes share the same principle. Since the sky above is hollow (void), sound can be heard a hundred *ri* away without the ground shaking. Since the interior of the earth is solid (full), the earth moves violently. The substance of the earth is the settled dregs of the primordial *ki* (energy/vital force) that have congealed, and around it there are pores (holes) through which *ki* circulates — like a beehive, for example. The energies of water and fire lie dormant within it. When this *ki* wishes to expand but cannot, it shakes the earth and bursts outward.

In general, the way yin and yang ascend and descend between heaven and earth is as follows: at the winter solstice, *ichiyo raifuku* (the return of yang) occurs; yang gradually increases until the fourth month, when yang is fully abundant and yin completely retreats. The fourth month is therefore called the month of "solitary yang, no yin." In the fifth month, one yin is born; by the tenth month, yin alone is pure and there is no yang. The month name "no-yang month" (*kannazuki*) derives from this. By the eleventh month, yang returns, beginning again as before.

Because these two energies enter and exit and travel back and forth in this way, in winter — even though there is little yang above — since there is yang below, well water is warm and the soil is moist. In summer, since yin is scarce above and abundant below, well water is cold.

When the two energies exchange, in spring and summer, when yang rises from within the earth and yin descends to hide, it is normal (*jun*) for the rising energy to emerge first and the sinking energy to enter afterward. In autumn and winter, when yin emerges and yang retreats, it is likewise normal for the emerging energy to come out first and the sinking energy to enter afterward.

However, when the energy that is about to sink arrives first and closes the pores of the earth, the energy that wishes to emerge is blocked and cannot expand. It congeals and coils beneath the ground, and ultimately presses forward and struggles until it bursts through the resisting energy and emerges — at this moment, an earthquake occurs. It is the two energies failing to harmonize beneath the earth. When this is powerful, it produces transformations such as splitting the earth and lifting the sea.

Great earthquakes occur when there is no wind, the sky is clear, and all is still. When there is wind and rain, those forces cause some of the subterranean energy to be released little by little, and so major earthquakes do not occur.

This is just as stated in the *Guoyu* ("Discourses of the States"), "Zhou Discourse" (*Zhouyu*): "When yang is suppressed and cannot emerge, and yin is trapped and cannot evaporate — this gives rise to earthquakes."

As for the explanation involving catfish, it is like something said by a person who answered that Japan's gods have no territory — someone who does not understand the vastness of heaven and earth. In China, India, and the Netherlands alike, there would have to be large and small catfish respectively...
