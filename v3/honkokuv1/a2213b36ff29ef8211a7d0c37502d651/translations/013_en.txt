○ Why Snow Falls Early on High Mountains
Question: Why does snow fall early on snowy mountains? There shouldn't be a reason for the tops of snowy mountains to be especially cold. Why does snow fall particularly early there?
Answer: Inside great plateaus, even in midsummer there is necessarily freezing, and within great oceans there is black light at night. This is due to true li and true kan. In the extreme yang of li there is true yin, and in the extreme yin of kan there is true yang. Also, high mountains are close to heaven's cold zones, and qi conversely condenses clouds. Moreover, cold and warmth differ by location - even on flat land, Hokuetsu has much snow while Nanki has little snow.

○ Hail (Arare) and Sleet (Mizore)
Question: Hail and sleet are types of snow. However, sometimes they become snow, other times hail and sleet with different forms. What is the reason for this?
Answer: This also has the same principle as snow. Sleet is rain droplets that should fall as rain but are sealed by yin cold. Sometimes when there is wind in heaven, what should become snow is blown and hardened into hail. When the sky is cold and wind blows, hail and sleet fall for this reason. Both are places where rain-moisture qi condenses due to wind and cold. Sometimes it becomes rain-snow, and when that snow encounters yang qi in heaven, it disperses and becomes hail and sleet.
