The number of dead was immense, and the sound of crying and screaming filled the streets. Furthermore, fires broke out from collapsed buildings in various places. After the Hour of the Ox (around 2 a.m.), a tsunami struck, and many people and horses in Bōsō perished. The inner waterways filled completely, and the receding waves came four times. From this point on, numerous aftershocks occurred. Sagami Province's Odawara was particularly devastated, with approximately 2,300 dead. From Odawara to Shinagawa, 15,000 people perished; in Bōshū, 100,000; and in Edo, 30,000.
