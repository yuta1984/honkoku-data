○Taki-no-yu (Waterfall Hot Spring)
Treatment: Head rush, headaches, dizziness, various eye diseases, leprosy, bruises,
namazu (a type of eczema), scabies, syphilitic sores, phlegm, various skin toxins, and others
Various swellings; breaks up all blood coagulations and hardened masses
The spring nature is cooling. Its efficacy in opening meridians and circulating qi and blood surpasses all other springs.
It is particularly effective for blurred vision, poor eyesight, and deafness. However, prolonged bathing is not recommended.

○Washi-no-yu (Eagle Hot Spring)
Treatment: Roughly the same as Wata-no-yu
Gonorrhea, hemorrhoids, rectal prolapse, gallstones

The origin of this spring: when the hot spring was not yet known to the world, an eagle came daily for about thirty days,
soaking its feet before returning. Local people found this strange and observed that the bird had an injured leg, apparently
shot by a gun. After continuing this routine for about fifty days, its leg healed completely, and it never returned. Thus

people first discovered this hot spring, developed it, and named it Eagle Spring. For blood coagulation below the waist, bruises, and sprains, it
surpasses other springs. However, it is contraindicated for leprosy, namazu, and ringworm.

○Matsu-no-yu (Pine Hot Spring)
Treatment: Roughly the same as Netsu-no-yu
Gonorrhea, hemorrhoids, rectal prolapse, skin toxins. However, contraindicated for those with leprosy.

○Chiyo-no-yu (Eternal Hot Spring)
Treatment: Various skin diseases, fetal toxins, scabies
Syphilis; expels and heals all internal toxins.
