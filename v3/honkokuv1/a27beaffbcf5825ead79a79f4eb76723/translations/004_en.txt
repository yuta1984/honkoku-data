The nature of heaven considers south as yang and north as yin. Therefore, the sun travels in the south. This is the form of heaven and earth being perfectly round and interconnected. Thus, an ancient saying states: "If the earth decreases by one shaku, then one shaku of heaven is born. Originally faceless, in which direction, north or south, would it be?" Like the yolk of an egg, because its shape is perfectly round, this is called the earth sphere. Its circumference is said to be approximately ninety thousand ri in total. There is also a proverb that says "six seas, three mountains, one flat land." This means the sea occupies six-tenths, mountains three-tenths, and flat land one-tenth. Therefore, using "埊" to form the character for "地" (earth) represents this combined meaning. Earthquakes occur when yang energy is trapped beneath yin and is compressed by yin energy, unable to ascend, whereupon the earth splits, moves, and shakes. This is because yang energy loses its proper place and yin energy fills it. There are also holes like beehives in the earth's interior, and water subsequently flows through them while yang energy constantly enters and exits. Yin and yang harmonize through this process to maintain proper balance as the normal state.
