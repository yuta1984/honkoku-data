The damage was extremely severe. On April 27th, a great tsunami struck the coast of Enshū (present-day Shizuoka), sweeping thousands of houses along with the land into the sea, with over ten thousand people losing their lives. More than thirty chō of land became sea. This area came to be called "Imagire" (present cutting), as recorded in the Ōnin-ki.

Thirty-seven years later, during the reign of the 106th Emperor Go-Nara, on the night of February 13th, Tenbun 2 (1533), there was an earthquake. At this time, it is said that a star fell and sank into the sea. There was also an earthquake in the same era's 13th year. Twenty-eight years later, during the reign of the 107th Emperor Ōgimachi, on November 29th, Tenshō 13 (1585), there was a great earthquake. Nine years later, during the reign of the 108th Emperor Go-Yōzei, in Bunroku 4 (1595),

on July 3rd around noon, the sky suddenly became overcast, whirlwinds blew intensely, and fine rain fell heavily. On the night of the 12th of the same month, there were severe earthquakes in Yamashiro, Yamato, Ōmi, Tanba, Kawachi, and Settsu provinces. Fushimi Momoyama Castle was also damaged in various places. The sound of temples, shrines, private houses, mountains and peaks collapsing was like a hundred thousand thunderclaps. At this time, the Great Buddha of Eastern Kyoto also collapsed.

Thirty-five years later, during the reigns of the 110th Emperor Go-Mizunoo and Empress Meishō, on January 7th, Kan'ei 7 (1630), there was a great earthquake in Odawara, Sagami Province.
