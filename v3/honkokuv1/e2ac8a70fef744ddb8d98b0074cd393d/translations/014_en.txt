Records from Highway Districts and Neighboring Villages

On the Tōkaidō highway, damage extends to Hodogaya, with many collapses in various places at Kanagawa-juku post station. Damage extends to Honmoku, Kanazawa, Kamakura, Enoshima, and Uraga areas. On the Nakasendō highway, damage extends to Takasaki in Kōzuke Province, with sand springs and clear water overflowing at various locations along this highway. There are many obstructions between Warabi-juku and Ōmiya. On the Kōshū highway, damage extends to Hachiōji, with no major problems on the highway. On the Nikkō highway, damage extends to Utsunomiya, with collapses along the highway route, and similar conditions around Sōka. On the Mito highway, damage extends to the Tsuchiura area, with collapses visible in various places along the highway. In the Shimōsa region, tremors were strong around Sakasai, with similar conditions around Gyōtoku and Funabashi. In Kasai domain and Nigōhan domain, there are numerous collapses. It is also heard that there are many collapses around Matsudo, Ichikawa, and Shibamata. Surveying other rural areas and urban districts, elevated lands show no particular damage, but low-lying areas appear to have sustained severe damage in most cases.

Excerpts from Letters from Osaka

On the night of September 28th, there was an earthquake, causing major collapses at Hirano Bridge, Hōki-chō, and Shijimi Bridge area in Osaka, with many people injured in these areas. It is also reported that Nagoya in Owari Province and Hamamatsu in Tōtōmi Province also had minor fires in their castle towns, with earthquakes occurring simultaneously.

On September 29th, Saruzō, younger brother of the eighth-generation Ichikawa Danjūrō, died during a performance at Osaka Wakadayū-za theater while playing the roles of Fuwa Denzaemon and the sumo wrestler Nuregami. His posthumous Buddhist name was Jitsuyo Gisen Kōan Shinji (September 29th, age twenty-one).

Among the actor residences that survived the conflagration in Edo's theater district: Bandō (Shūka's son) Kichiya, Nakamura Fukusuke, Ichimura Hazaemon, Morita Kanya, Onoe Kikujirō, and two or three tea houses.
