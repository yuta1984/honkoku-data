### Reports from Along the Highways and Nearby Provinces

Along the Tokaido highway, damage extends as far as Hodogaya, with many collapses reported throughout Kanagawa-juku. Damage is reported as far as Honmoku, Kanazawa, Kamakura, Enoshima, and Uraga. ▲ Along the Nakasendo highway, damage extends as far as Takasaki in Kozuke Province; along this road, sand welled up from the ground and spring water overflowed in numerous locations. Many disturbances occurred between Warabi-juku and Omiya. ▲ Along the Koshu Kaido highway, damage extends as far as Hachioji, with no significant incidents along most of the route. ▲ Along the Nikko Kaido highway, damage extends as far as Utsunomiya, with collapses reported along the route. The Soka area also suffered damage. ▲ Along the Mito Kaido highway, damage extends as far as Tsuchiura, with collapses visible at various points along the road. In the Shimosa area, the shaking was particularly strong near Sakai, and the areas around Gyotoku and Funabashi were similarly affected. In the Kasai domain and the Nigohanbun domain, collapses occurred in many locations. It is also heard that Matsudo, Ichikawa, and Shibamata suffered many collapses. As for other rural and urban areas surveyed, elevated land suffered no particular damage, while low-lying areas generally appear to have sustained severe damage.

### Excerpts from a Letter from Osaka

On the night of the 28th day of the 9th month, an earthquake struck, causing major collapses along Hiranobashi, Hoko-cho, and Shijimibashi-suji in Osaka, with many people in the area suffering harm. In addition, parts of the castle towns of Owari (Nagoya) and Totomi Hamamatsu also suffered minor fires. It is reported that earthquakes occurred simultaneously in these locations.

On the 29th day of the 9th month, Sarukura, the younger brother of the eighth-generation Ichikawa Danjuro, was performing the roles of Fuwa Denzaemon and the wrestler Nuregami at the Osaka Wakadayu-za theater, and died during the performance. His posthumous Buddhist name: Jitsuyo Gisen Ko (29th day of the 9th month) Anshin-shi. [A note to the left reads: "aged 21."]

Among the actor residences that survived the fires in Edo's theater district:
Bando (Shiuka's son) Yoshiya, Nakamura Fukusuke, Ichimura Uzaemon, Morita Kanya, Onoe Kikujiro, and two or three teahouses also survived.
