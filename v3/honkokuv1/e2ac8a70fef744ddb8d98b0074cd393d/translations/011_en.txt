Around Yamashiro Bridge and Dobashi, and in the Takiyama-cho area, there was only slight collapse.

《Box: 22》 The North and South Hatchobori areas had little collapse. Teppōzu had much collapse, with particularly severe damage around Funamatsu-cho. The residence of Matsudaira Awaji-no-kami (15,000 koku) burned down. All the residences in this area suffered extensive damage, while Inari Shrine was unharmed.

On Reiganjima, Minato-cho, Nagasaki-cho, Tomishima-cho, Kawaguchi-cho, Minato Bridge Street, North and South Shinbori, and Hakozaki-cho all had much collapse. South Shinbori (half the district), one side of Shio-cho, and up to Ōkawabata-cho burned down. The residence of Matsudaira Echizen-no-kami survived. From there, Koami-cho Street, Inari-bori, the area around Lord Sakai Uta-no-kami's residence, from Jinzaemon-cho to the Ginza residences, Kaigara-cho area, and Matsushima-cho area had much collapse. Hama-cho, Manabe Riverbank Street, and up to Ōhashi suffered mostly damage. The residence of Mizuno Dewa-no-kami burned down.

《Box: 23》 In Honjō, temples generally collapsed, and most of the daimyo and hatamoto residences collapsed, with few remaining unharmed. Among these, the lower residence of Matsudaira Suō-no-kami facing East Bridge burned down. From there, the Matsushima-cho area suffered many collapses, and Koume and Kawara-cho burned slightly. Also, in the Nakanogō area, many temples were destroyed. Arai-cho completely collapsed, and small residences around Ishihara, Ushi-no-gozen, the temporary residence area, and Benten-koji burned down. Also, at Hitokuchi, two places - in front of Tenmangu and Kameido-cho - burned down. The Yanagishima and Oshiage areas had many collapses in various places. Also, near Hōonji Bridge, some townhouses burned. Yoshida-cho, Yoshioka-cho, and small residences around South Warishimizu suffered greatly, with much collapse extending to near Lord Tsugaru's residence. Also, along Otakekura-mae Street, small buildings collapsed greatly. Also, on East Ryōgoku and Tatekawa Street, townhouses on both banks, front and back, suffered many collapses, including Futatsume and Midori-cho first district.
