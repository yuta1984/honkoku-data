The second district burned down. Nishi-Hirano-cho, Fuku-cho, and Sankaku-yashiki (triangular mansion) - all these areas completely collapsed. The areas around Koza-bashi bridge and Amiuchi-ba (net-casting ground) suffered minor damage. The Saga-cho area collapsed severely, and Aikawa-cho burned down. From Kumai-cho, O-cho, Nakajima-cho to Oshima-cho and Nakajima-cho burned down. Along Fukuyoshi-cho street and Ogawa-cho, near Hachiman-bashi bridge, three houses survived in the midst of the fire. Hamaguri-cho partially survived, while Kuroe-cho and Sankura-ji temple burned down. Eitai-ji temple gate area and Yamamoto-cho Nishi-yokocho burned down. From Hamaguri-cho Kaneko-yokocho, commonly called "Naka-ura," the mansion of Matsudaira Dewa-no-kami burned down. From there, two or three houses survived near "Gatakuri-bashi" bridge, where the fire stopped. This fire burned from three locations simultaneously. Another location was Wakura, where Kita-Honjo daichi, Saka-no-cho, and other daichi areas burned down. Additionally, about one square district of Kuroe-cho burned down.

We inspected the burned areas as described above and examined them thoroughly to ensure nothing was omitted. However, recording the number of collapsed storehouses and such would be fictitious and not useful. We have investigated the truth regarding both mansion districts and urban areas, collecting only reliable information to record here.
