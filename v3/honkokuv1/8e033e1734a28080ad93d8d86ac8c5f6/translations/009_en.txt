This "geyser" is located approximately three *chō* from the coast and approximately four hundred *shaku* above sea level; the form of its vent is indeed that of a true geyser, erupting hot water and steam alternately at fixed intervals, its steam possessing an extremely powerful expansive force.

In addition to this periodically erupting spring, there are several other springs whose characteristics are more or less similar to it, as listed below.

**(3) Muen-no-yu (Salt-Free Bath)**　Temperature: 206.6 degrees. This bath contains no saline content and is a simple spring containing a small amount of sulfur. It is therefore of limited efficacy. **(4) Furo-no-yu (Bath Spring)**　Temperature: 206.6 degrees. The spring water contains a large quantity of saline content.

**(5) Kawara-yu**　Temperature: 212 degrees. Spring quality: same as above.

**(6) Midori-no-yu (Green Bath)**　Temperature: 185 degrees. Spring quality: same as above.

All of these mineral springs contain small quantities of hydrogen sulfide and have deposited large quantities of manganese oxide, manganese peroxide, and silicic acid sediment (*odomi*).

**Medical and Therapeutic Uses** [Based on the account of Dr. Hoffmann, a medical doctor from Germany]

The principal component of this hot spring is common salt (*shokuyen*/sodium chloride), with small amounts of "chloro-potassium" (potassium chloride) and "chloro-magnesium" (magnesium chloride). The other constituents are minimal and insufficient to produce any particular therapeutic effect. Based on long accumulated experience, and comparing it with the most closely similar springs in Europe, the principal therapeutic uses are indicated as follows:

○ First, regarding bathing: one should bathe once or twice daily in water at a temperature of 98 to 100 degrees. If the water is excessively hot, one must not cool it by adding other water; rather, one should allow it to cool gradually on its own until it reaches the desired temperature.

　・Scrofula (*ruireki* [tuberculous lymphadenitis]) in children and the various conditions arising therefrom
　・Rheumatism and chronic gout [the highest tolerable temperature for the patient is recommended; generally up to 104 degrees]
　・In cases of inflammatory exudate, promotes the reabsorption of exudate after the inflammation has already subsided
　・Has the effect of promoting reabsorption in cases of extravascular fluid leakage and ordinary edema
　・Beriberi accompanied by edema
　・Neuralgia of the skin

○ Second, regarding drinking: four to twelve *gō* should be taken once or twice daily — that is, each morning, or morning and evening — either taken straight or diluted with well water.

　・Chronic gastric catarrh and chronic diarrhea
　・Chronic catarrh of the throat and bronchi
　・Chronic catarrh of the bladder
　・Chronic catarrh of the vagina and uterus [to be used in combination with bathing and vaginal douching]
　・Chronic catarrh of the bile duct
　・Melancholia (*utsuyū-shō*) arising from chronic gastric and intestinal catarrh

The above ailments, especially those in which organ function is sluggish, or those in which the skin or mucous membranes are lax, should be treated through both drinking and bathing.
