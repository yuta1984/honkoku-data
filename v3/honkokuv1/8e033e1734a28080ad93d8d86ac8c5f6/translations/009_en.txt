The shape of its spring opening is truly that of a genuine geyser, periodically ejecting hot water and steam alternately at fixed intervals, with the steam possessing extremely powerful tension.

Besides the periodic geyser, there are various other springs with somewhat similar characteristics, listed below:

(1) Me-no-yu (Eye Spring): Temperature 122°F, spring quality same as source spring
(2) Shin-yu (New Spring)
(3) Muen-no-yu (Non-saline Spring): Temperature 206.6°F. This spring contains no salt and has a small amount of sulfur, making it a simple spring. Therefore, its efficacy is mild. Its therapeutic effects are good for weakness, skin diseases, uterine diseases, chronic diseases, etc.
(4) Furo-no-yu (Bath Spring): Temperature 206.6°F, spring quality contains considerable salt
(5) Kawara-yu (River Spring): Temperature 212°F, spring quality same as above
(6) Midori-no-yu (Green Spring): Temperature 185°F, spring quality same as above

All these mineral springs contain small amounts of hydrogen sulfide and deposit large quantities of manganese oxide, manganese peroxide, and silicic acid sediments.

Medical Therapeutic Effects [According to German physician Dr. Hoffmann]

The main component of this hot spring is salt, containing small amounts of potassium chloride and magnesium chloride. Other elements are minimal and insufficient to provide separate therapeutic effects. Based on long-experienced European hot springs with the most similar properties, the main therapeutic effects are as follows:

○First, bathing method: Bathe once or twice daily in water at 98-100°F. If the water is too hot, do not cool it by adding other water, but let it cool gradually to the appropriate temperature. For children's glandular diseases and related symptoms; rheumatism and chronic gout [requiring as high a temperature as the patient can tolerate, generally up to 104°F]; for inflammatory exudation, after inflammation has subsided, it promotes absorption of exudates. It has the effect of promoting absorption in extravascular fluid leakage and ordinary edema. For those with beriberi accompanied by edema; skin neuralgia.

○Second, drinking method: Use 4 to 12 gō daily, once or twice, either alone each morning or morning and evening, or mixed with well water. For chronic gastric catarrh and chronic diarrhea; chronic throat and bronchial catarrh; chronic bladder catarrh; chronic vaginal and uterine catarrh [combined with bathing and vaginal irrigation]; chronic pancreatic duct catarrh; melancholia arising from chronic gastric and intestinal catarrh. For the above diseases, especially those with sluggish organ function, and for relaxed skin or mucous membranes, use as both drink and bath.

Same (location) Ōyu: Contains small amounts of alkali and hydrogen sulfide gas, with a rotten egg-like odor. ○For chronic rheumatism, various skin diseases, syphilis, lead poisoning and mercury poisoning.
