Hot Spring (Kamifunabara-no-yu): One and a half ri to Yoshina, three ri to Shuzenji, ten ri to Atami. Temperature 109.4 degrees. The spring water contains sulfur and nitrate gases, with a slightly salty taste. Therapeutic effects are good for scabies, bruises, and injuries.

The various mineral springs from Hatake and below mentioned above are located in or on the banks of the Kano River, and are nothing more than simple hot springs containing only small amounts of mineral components.

Kimitake District, Kona: Two ri and twenty-six cho to Shuzenji, five and a half ri to Atami, five ri and twenty-four cho to Ashinoyu. Temperature 131 degrees. The spring quality is ordinary simple spring water, and its therapeutic effects are equal to those of Atami's non-saline springs, so its efficacy is limited.

Same district, Shuzenji: Two ri and twenty-six cho to Kona, three ri and ten cho to Yoshina, five and a half ri to Mishima Station, eight ri to Atami. Although the spring water contains sodium carbonate, since the amounts of salt and sodium sulfate exceed this, it is classified as alkaline saline spring. The vicinity of Kona and Shuzenji consists of mountain valleys through which the Katsura River flows. The Katsura River flows through wide rock crevices, and the valley bottom becomes very narrow at Shuzenji. There are hot springs on both sides of this area, some on the banks of the Katsura River and others in the river itself. The area where these hot springs are scattered extends no more than nine cho in length, and the distance from the riverbed does not exceed sixty shaku. A table of the names and temperatures of these mineral springs is listed below.

(1) Shin-yu: Temperature 120.2 degrees. This spring emerges from rock crevices, and the bathhouse was built by carving out the rock.
(2) Ishi-no-yu: 145.2 degrees.
(3) Hako-no-yu: 145.2 degrees, contains small amounts of hydrogen sulfide.
(4) Sugi-no-yu: 142 degrees, thirty paces from the Katsura River bank.
(5) Shin-no-yu: 154 degrees, four paces from the riverbank.
(6) Tokko-no-yu: 140 degrees, emerges from rock crevices in the center of the Katsura River.
(7) Kawara-no-yu: 147.2 degrees.
(8) Hana-no-yu: 134.6 degrees, contains traces of hydrogen sulfide.
(9) Fuji-no-yu: 105.8 degrees, same spring quality as above, approximately twenty paces from the riverbank.
(10) Meiji Reisen: 156 degrees, even the most distant is eighty to one hundred paces from the riverbank. Same spring quality as above.
(11) Kikuen-no-yu: 140 degrees, contains small amounts of hydrogen sulfide.
(12) Nodaya: 130 degrees.
(13) Tatsu-no-yu: 160 degrees, emerges from riverbed rocks near Tokko-no-yu.

These mineral springs have temperatures ranging from 106 to 160 degrees as described above. Upon detailed investigation of the reason, the original spring temperature would be 160 degrees. However, due to insufficient sealing of the conduits and mixing with cold water near the Katsura River, a difference of 86 degrees has eventually developed between the hottest and coldest springs.
