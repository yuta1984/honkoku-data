**Same (Local) (Analyzed) Ara-yu**　Located in Shiobara Village. East-southeast of Ikari Village, approximately 1.5 *ri* to the west. Spring quality contains sulfurous vapor. Recommended for hernia (*senki*) and scabies, among other conditions.

**Same (Local) Furumachi**　Located in Naka-Shiobara Village. Spring quality contains aluminous and saline vapors. Recommended for hernia and sword cuts, among other conditions. ○ Sumaki-taki-yu: spring quality contains aluminous vapor. Recommended for rush of blood to the head (*nobose*).

**Same (Local) Fukuwatado**　Located in Shimo-Shiobara Village. Spring quality contains aluminous vapor; recommended for beriberi, hernia, and similar conditions. ○ Hataorido: spring quality same as above; recommended for hernia. ○ Shiogama: contains aluminous and saline vapors; recommended for hemorrhoidal conditions. ○ Shio-yu: spring quality same as above. ○ Ōtsuna: contains aluminous vapor; recommended for hernia and scabies.

　(Analyzed) The mineral springs of Shiobara Village are highly acidic and contain green vitriol (*ryokuban*; ferrous sulfate). The large number of springs possessing astringent properties provides evidence that this area is the remains of an ancient volcano — as is the case with places such as Kusatsu.

**Same (Miscellaneous) Taki-no-yu**　Located in Taki Village. Spring quality is extremely hot, clear and transparent, contains aluminous vapor, and has no odor or taste. Recommended for scabies.

**Same (Local) Yunishigawa**　Located in Yunishigawa Village. Contains sulfuric acid, aluminous vapor, and iodine ("Yojīmu"). Recommended for hernia, hemorrhoidal conditions, scabies, and similar ailments.

**Same (Local) Kawamata**　Located in Kawamata Village. Spring quality contains sulfurous-aluminous vapor. Recommended for hernia and similar conditions. ○ Nikkōzawa: spring quality same as above; recommended for hernia and sword cuts. ○ Yuzawa: same as above.

**Same (Analyzed) (Local) Shika-no-yu**　Located in Yumoto Village. Temperature: 172 degrees. Contains a large quantity of aluminum sulfate and a small quantity of hydrogen sulfide. ○ (Local) Recommended for eczema and eye diseases.

**Same (Local) Nasu Gyōnin-yu**　Located in Yumoto Village. Spring quality contains sulfurous vapor and is extremely hot; recommended for scabies and similar conditions. ○ Gosho-yu and Kami-yu — also known as Takao-mata-yu — both have the same spring quality and therapeutic effects as above.

**Same (Analyzed) Nasu**　Located in Nasu Village. Temperature: 172 degrees. Spring quality contains large quantities of free sulfuric acid, aluminum sulfate, iron, and common salt, giving it an acidic and astringent taste. ○ The abundant sulfurous powder scattered in the vicinity of this area was likely produced in ancient times through volcanic activity.

**Same (Analyzed) Sando-goya-yu**　Located in Itamuro Village. Temperature: 131 degrees. Spring quality contains ferrous carbonate, giving it an iron taste. Those suffering from anemia should drink it. However, those with a weak stomach should not drink it. ○ Therapeutic effects: anemia, anemia arising from external injuries, debility caused by hemorrhoidal conditions and nosebleeds, chlorosis (*iōbyō*; green sickness), anemia with gastric weakness, chronic edema arising from anemia, melancholia (*fusagi-byō*), uterine diseases, nervous disorders, chronic diarrhea, leukemia, amyloid degeneration of the spleen, scurvy.

**Same (Analyzed) Itamuro-yu**　Located in Itamuro Village. Temperature: 102 degrees. Spring quality and therapeutic effects similar to those of Ikaho in Kōzuke Province. See leaf 26.
