**Agatsuma District (Surveyed) Kusatsu (草津)**　Twenty *ri* from Takasaki. Contains sulfuric acid and iron; the flavor is astringent and sour.

- (1) Beriberi Bath (*Kakke-no-yu*)　Temperature ranging from 117 to 126 degrees.
- (2) Cotton Bath (*Wata-no-yu*)　Same, 116 to 118 degrees.
- (3) Pine Bath (*Matsu-no-yu*)　Same, 106 to 115 degrees.
- (4) Kompira Bath (*Konpira-yu*)　Same, 108 to 118 degrees.
- (5) Jewel Bath (*Tama-no-yu*)
- (6) Lapis Lazuli Bath (*Ruri-no-yu*)
- (7) White Sand Bath (*Shirasu-no-yu*)
- (8) Niikawa Bath (*Niikawa-no-yu*)　These four baths no longer exist at present.
- (9) Nakazawa Teahouse Bath (*Nakazawa-jaya-no-yu*)　Temperature ranging from 100 to 108 degrees.
- (10) Eye-Washing Bath (*Meara-yu*)　Probably contains no acidic gas.

The mineral properties of these springs, with the exception of the Eye-Washing Bath, should all be similar to the following six springs. They are distinguished only by their high concentration of acidic gas and their astringent properties.

- (11) Honorable Seat Bath (*Oza-yu*)　Temperature 151 to 158 degrees; contains sulfur, iron gas, and a great quantity of acidic gas.
- (12) Eagle Bath (*Washi-no-yu*)　Same, 120 to 124 degrees. Spring quality same as above.
- (13) Heat Bath (*Netsu-no-yu*)　Same, 194 degrees. Spring quality same as above.
- (14) Jizō Bath (*Jizō-yu*)　Same, 118 to 126 degrees. Spring quality same as above.
- (15) Waterfall Bath (*Taki-no-yu*)　Same, 109 to 117 degrees. Spring quality same as above.
- (16) Hawk Bath (*Taka-no-yu*)　Temperature unclear. Spring quality same as above.

The principal components of all these springs are sulfuric acid and aluminum sulfate (*ryōban-do*), and owing to their exceptionally powerful astringent, antiseptic, and purgative properties, they are effective for the following conditions:

> Leprosy (*raibyo*)　Scrofula (*ruireki*)　Chronic syphilis　Congenital syphilitic toxin (*taidoku*)　Blood corruption　Ulcers　Scabies　Gonorrhea　Eye diseases　Nervous diseases　Dizziness (vertigo)　Rheumatism

Those suffering from the above conditions should benefit from both internal and external use. However, the dosage per occasion must not exceed half a *san* to one *san* (small cup).
