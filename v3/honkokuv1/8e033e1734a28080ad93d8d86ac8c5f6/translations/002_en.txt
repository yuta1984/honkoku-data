Preface
Whether within or outside the country, it can be said that hot spring bathing has become truly popular in recent years. When I was previously dispatched to the Ōu and Shin'etsu regions on official business, I frequently inspected places where mineral springs emerged for geological surveys, and in all regions I was astonished by the extremely large number of hot spring bathers. The reason why bathers congregate in such numbers must surely be due to the efficacy of each spring. However, those who bathe do not necessarily have detailed knowledge of the properties of the mineral springs before bathing. Therefore, I have compiled this book to present the properties of each spring, appending the diseases for which they are indicated, to provide some reference for bathers and also to serve as an aid in understanding the geology of our country.

April, Meiji 13 (1880)　　　　　　Compiler's note
