**Yamakoshi District (Ra) Yuuratsu (Yūratsu)**
There are two springs here. One is at a temperature of 102°F [approximately 39°C] and is somewhat larger; the other is at 95°F [approximately 35°C]. The spring water contains no gas but does contain iron. The water flows from alluvial soil (alluvial earths).

### Guidelines for Therapeutic Treatment Using Mineral Springs
*(Excerpted from Issue No. 2 of the Journal of the Sanitary Bureau, Ministry of the Interior)*

In general, mineral springs are commonly classified and named according to their respective properties.

While all hot springs within a given class share the same general characteristics, each individual spring must also possess special secondary components (a term used in contrast to the primary component). For example, an alkaline spring may contain large quantities of calcium sulfate and calcium carbonate as secondary components, and may additionally contain common salt as a second secondary component.

Furthermore, it may also contain a small amount of iron. This is not limited to alkaline springs alone; saline springs, sulfurous springs, chalybeate springs, and thermal springs also necessarily possess numerous secondary components.

Therefore, when one wishes to send a patient to a mineral spring to treat their illness, one must first carefully examine both the patient's constitution (their innate physical predisposition) and the nature of the spring (its composition), without exception. The patient's "constitution" refers to their habitual predisposition to conditions such as rheumatism, scrofula, gout, or stubborn skin diseases; while the "nature" of the spring refers to whether its primary and secondary components are suitable for the patient's constitution and current ailment.
