The springs of Shūzenji, compared to simple thermal springs, offer an additional degree of therapeutic benefit for skin diseases and the like; moreover, among the mineral springs of Izu Province, their medicinal efficacy is second only to that of Atami. Their therapeutic effects are similar to those of Komono Hot Spring in Mie District, Ise Province (see Leaf 5).

**Same (District) — Doi (Toi)**

Three and a half *ri* from Yoshina; six *ri* from Shūzenji; ten *ri* from Atami. Located in three places.

- **Ōyu (Great Bath)**: Contains sulfurous vapor; recommended for eye diseases. Cold in winter.
- **Koyu (Small Bath)**: Same spring quality. Recommended for hernia-like abdominal pain, sword/blade wounds, and scabies.
- **Mabu-no-yu (Mine Bath)**: Same spring quality. Recommended for hernia-like abdominal pain, muscle spasms, bruising, fractures, and similar conditions.

**Naka District (District) — Ōsawa, also known as Yunose-no-yu**

The spring quality contains sulfurous vapor. Recommended for blade wounds, tetanus, eye diseases, and similar conditions.
