General Principles

1. This book is compiled by extracting and translating the section on Japanese mineral springs from the French book titled "De la Nature" written by Dr. Goeltz, an instructor at the Yokohama Pharmaceutical School, and by inserting materials extensively excerpted from other books. Although the mineral springs mentioned in the book number over 500 cases, it is still not complete. Moreover, those that have undergone detailed testing are extremely few compared to the total number of mineral springs nationwide, so the purpose is to gradually add and revise content. Please do not fault its omissions and errors.

1. All temperatures shown in the book use the Fahrenheit scale.

1. The purpose of attaching symbols to each spring name is to certify the most reliable sources and to provide reference for readers.

1. The symbol "ゲ" indicates the opinion of Dr. Goeltz, instructor at Yokohama Pharmaceutical School
1. "ラ" indicates surveys by Benjamin Smith Lyman, American geologist and mining engineer
1. "マ" indicates the opinion of Dr. Georg Martin, German instructor at Tokyo Pharmaceutical School
1. "ド" indicates the opinion of Bergerus van Dowalz, instructor at Osaka Pharmaceutical School
1. "リ" indicates the opinion of instructor Little
1. "シ" indicates therapeutic effects noted by Siebold
1. "地" indicates excerpts from "Essentials of Japanese Geography"
1. "一" indicates excerpts from "Prefecture Administrative Overview"
1. "雑" indicates surveys by various other scholars
