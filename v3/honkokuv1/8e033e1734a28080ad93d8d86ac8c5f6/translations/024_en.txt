The rocks of this area are somewhat decomposed metamorphic rocks of a greenish-gray color [Kamuikotan Group]. Hot springs are found at twelve locations; their temperatures and approximate volumes of water discharged per minute are listed below.

○ Ōmiya-ya-no-yu: temperature 140 degrees, discharge approximately 2 *koku* 0.5 *shō*. ○ Arima-yu: 138.2 degrees, approximately 1 *koku* 5 *tō*. ○ Yonekura-yu: 138.2 degrees, large volume. ○ Mukai-taki: 134.6 degrees at the pipe mouth. ○ Yadoya-no-yu: same as above, approximately 8 *tō* 7 *shō*. ○ Ana-yu: same as above, approximately 2 *tō* 5 *shō*. ○ Sō-yu: same as above. ○ Fujiya-no-yu: 132.8 degrees, approximately 3 *tō* 7 *shō*. ○ Chōjiya-yu: 131 degrees, small volume. ○ Kitsune-yu (Fox Bath): 129.2 degrees, small volume. ○ Furutaki and Shintaki: 116.6 degrees. ○ Mujina-yu (Badger Bath): 104 degrees, approximately 3 *tō*.

All of these hot springs are of roughly the same quality; they have no odor or taste, contain no precipitates, sulfur, or gas, and merely contain a slightly whitish substance consisting of silica and calcium carbonate.

---

**Same (Local) (Lyman) Yuhara**　Located in Yuhara Village. Recommended for scabies, hernia, phlegm and dyspnea (*tanshō*), and similar conditions. ○ There are two springs on the riverbank: one at a temperature of 113 degrees, the other at 143.5 degrees. The discharge from each is approximately 5 *tō* per minute. The spring quality contains no sulfurous vapor and only a minimal amount of gas, with a small amount of precipitate that appears to be a white calcareous substance.

**Same (Local) (Lyman) Ashinomaki**　Springs in Ashinomaki Village emerge from between rocks along the Tsurunuma River. There are three springs: Spring A at 106 degrees, Spring B at 109.5 degrees, Spring C the same. The spring water is tasteless and contains no sulfurous vapor, with only a minimal white saline precipitate. Recommended for eye diseases, beriberi, and similar conditions. ○ On the opposite bank of this spring, there is a small spring called Oya-yu, which is approximately the same temperature.

**Same (Local) Yunomata (Yushimata)**　Located in Yunomata Village. Temperature: 160 degrees. Recommended for apoplexy, beriberi, sword cuts, bruises, sores and swellings, syphilis, and similar conditions. ○ Ishi-yu (Stone Bath): temperature 120 degrees.

**Same (Local) Tokusa**　Located in Tokusa Village. Temperature: 130 degrees. Spring quality contains sulfurous vapor. Recommended for scabies and similar conditions.

**Same (Official) Yuno-kami Village**　Tate-yu: temperature 110 degrees. ○ Kiri-yu: same. ○ Saru-yu (Monkey Bath): temperature 105 degrees.

---

**Ōnuma District — (Official) Hayato**　Located in Hayato Village. Spring quality contains saline vapor; the taste is astringent and briny. Recommended for accumulation of *ki* (vital energy), sword cuts, and arm pain. Temperature: 95 degrees. No bathing establishment has yet been built.

**Same (Official) Gojōshiki Village**　Spring quality contains common salt; recommended for muscular and bone pain, deficient cold (*kyorō*), colic, and similar conditions. ○ Ara-yu: temperature 120 degrees. ○ Ōzawa-yu: 130 degrees. ○ Shimo-no-yu: 116 degrees.

**Same (Official) Oya**　Located in Oya Village. Temperature: 90 degrees. Taste carries an acidic quality; recommended for eye diseases, syphilis, and similar conditions.

**Same (Official) Tamanashi**　Springs in Tamanashi Village emerge from between rocks along the Nojiri River. Temperature: 90 degrees. Briny taste. Recommended for various sores.

**Same (Official) Hachimachi**　Springs in Hachimachi Village emerge from the bank of the Nojiri River. Temperature: 75 degrees. Briny taste.

**Same (Official) Yukura**　Springs emerge from between rocks along the bank of the Tadami River in Honna Village. Temperature: 95 degrees. Briny taste. Recommended for hemorrhoidal conditions.

**Same (Official) Ōshio**　Located in Ōshio Village. Temperature: 70 degrees. Briny taste. Recommended for syphilis.

**Same (Official) Sunagohara Village**　Two springs. ○ Kami-yu (Upper Bath): temperature 113 degrees; mildly briny. Recommended for swellings, retained fluids (*ryūin*), and similar conditions. ○ Shin-yu: 115 degrees. Spring quality and therapeutic effects [continued on next page].
