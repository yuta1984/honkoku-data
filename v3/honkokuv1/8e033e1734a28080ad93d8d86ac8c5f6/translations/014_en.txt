Only the Miyanoshita Sankagetsu-no-yu (Three Months Hot Spring) contains a relatively large amount of salt content, so it is classified as a weak saline spring. Its therapeutic effects are accordingly numerous.

Simple springs, even though their salt content is minimal, are not without effect when bathing in them.

A. For skin diseases: effective for eczema, pruritus, lichen, etc. However, prolonged bathing (bathing for several hours) is even better. For unclean and slow-healing ulcers that do not readily heal, one should bathe frequently and cleanse the affected area.

B. For rheumatism, gout, and gouty exudation: select and use springs with high temperatures.

C. For various types of paralysis (numbness), namely rheumatic paralysis (numbness), gouty paralysis, and traumatic paralysis, when bathing for treatment, one should begin bathing only after several months have passed since a stroke.

D. Constitutional weakness, regardless of age, can be effectively treated with hot spring therapy. Also suitable for neurological diseases (not to be confused with mental illness) that accompany general debility, such as neuralgia, hypersensitivity, convulsions (spasms), and paralysis (numbness).

E. For those with general weakness, muscle relaxation, constipation and urinary retention (inability to pass stool or urine), as well as male impotence, optic nerve exhaustion, etc.

F. Effective for "chi-no-michi" (women's disorders related to menstruation and blood circulation).

Musashi Province, Tama District, Ogauchi: Located in Hara village at the border with Kai Province. The spring water contains sulfur, is lukewarm, and is suitable for contusions and sword wounds. Chichibu District.
