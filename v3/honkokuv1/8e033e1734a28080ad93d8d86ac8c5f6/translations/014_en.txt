Only the Sankagetsu-no-yu (Three-Month Bath) at Miyanoshita contains a somewhat higher quantity of salt. Therefore, it is classified as a weakly saline spring (*jakusei ganen-sen*). Its therapeutic effects are accordingly more numerous.

Even simple springs (*tanjun-sen*), although they may contain only a negligible quantity of salts, are by no means without benefit when one bathes in them.

**A. Skin Diseases**: Effective for eczema, prurigo, lichen, and similar conditions. However, prolonged bathing (bathing for several hours at a time) is even better. Furthermore, those suffering from foul and sluggishly healing ulcers that show no sign of recovery should bathe frequently and wash the affected area thoroughly.

**B. Rheumatism, Gout, and Gouty Effusions**: Springs of higher temperature should be selected and used.

**C. Various Forms of Numbness (*shibire*)**: That is, rheumatic numbness, gouty numbness, and numbness resulting from wounds — when bathing for these conditions, one should wait several months after a stroke (*chūfū*) before beginning to take baths.

**D. Constitutional Weakness**: Regardless of age — whether old or young — all benefit from spa treatment (*tōji*). It is also recommended for neurological disorders accompanying general debility (not to be confused with mental illness), neuralgia, hyperesthesia, convulsions (*hikitsuke*), and paralysis (*shibire*).

**E. General Debility** with muscular relaxation, and those suffering from constipation or urinary retention (i.e., those with no passage of bowel movements or urine), as well as male seminal insufficiency, and deterioration of the visual nerves, etc.

**F. Blood Disorders (*chi-no-michi*)**: Effective.
