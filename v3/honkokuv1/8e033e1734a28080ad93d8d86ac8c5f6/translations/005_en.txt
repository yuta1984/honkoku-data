A Study of Japanese Hot Springs
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Compiled and translated by Kuwata Tomoaki

The Five Home Provinces (Kinai)

**Yamashiro**　Soraku District (lower grade)　Dōsenbō
This is a cold spring containing a large amount of free carbonic acid. Although drinking it slightly stimulates the mouth and stomach, its taste is pleasant and it is known to greatly refresh the spirit. It is therefore commonly bottled in flasks and sold at various locations.

**Yamato**　Yoshino District (terrestrial/ground source)　Tōsenji-yu
Located in Musashi Village. The spring contains hydrogen sulfide, sulfuric acid, and other compounds, and is recommended for hernia pain, paralytic ailments, sores and skin diseases, bruises, and sword or blade wounds.

Same district (terrestrial)　Shionōha Village
Located in Shionōha Village. Contains carbonic acid, sulfuric acid, and other compounds, and is recommended for abdominal masses, hemorrhoids, syphilis, bruises, and blade wounds. ○ Kiku-no-taira, also located in the same village, has the same spring quality and therapeutic effects as above.

Same district (terrestrial)　Yanagimoto
Located in Kuwabatake Village. Contains sulfuric acid, iron oxide, and hydrogen sulfide, and is recommended for sores and skin diseases, bruises, and blade wounds.

Same district (terrestrial)　Dedani
Located in Dedani Village. Spring quality same as above. Recommended for hemorrhoids, beriberi, paralytic ailments, and vaginal discharge.
