# A Study of Japanese Hot Springs

Compiled and translated by Kuwata Tomoaki

## The Kinai Region (Five Home Provinces)

**Yamashiro Province** — Soraku District (Lower District) — Dōsenbō
[A cold spring containing a large quantity of free carbonic acid. Although drinking it mildly stimulates the mouth and stomach, it has a pleasant taste and is well known for refreshing the spirit. It is therefore commonly bottled in flasks (tokkuri) and sold throughout various locations.]

**Yamato Province** — Yoshino District (see map) — Tōsenji-yu (Tōsenji Hot Spring)
[Located in Musashi Village. The spring water contains hydrogen sulfide, sulfuric acid, and other minerals, and is recommended for hernia-type abdominal pain (senki), urinary ailments (rinshitsu), skin sores/syphilitic lesions (sōdoku), bruises (uchimi), and sword/blade wounds (kirikizu).]

Same district (see map) — Shionohа Village
[Located in Shionoha Village. Contains carbonic acid, sulfuric acid, and other minerals; recommended for abdominal accumulation/colic (senshaku), hemorrhoids (jishitsu), syphilis, bruises, and blade wounds. ○ Kiku-no-Taira: also located in the same village; spring quality and therapeutic effects are the same as above.]

Same district (see map) — Yanagimoto
[Located in Kuwabatake Village. Contains sulfuric acid, iron oxide, and hydrogen sulfide; recommended for skin sores, bruises, and blade wounds.]

Same district (see map) — Dedani
[Located in Dedani Village. Spring quality is the same as above. Recommended for hemorrhoids, beriberi (kakke), urinary ailments (rinshitsu), and vaginal discharge (koshike).]
