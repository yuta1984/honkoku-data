The villagers regularly use the spring water for cooking and preparing decoctions.

○ Oza-yu: Effective for syphilis.
○ Taki-no-yu: Recommended for blood ailments (*ketsubyō*).
○ Senki-yu: Recommended for colic (*senki*), hemorrhoids (*jishitsu*), and moist skin sores (*shissō*).
○ Kizu-yu: Effective for blade/sword wounds, burns, scabies (*kaisen*), and sore toxins (*sōdoku*).
○ Wata-yu: Primary indications same as above.

There are several other springs, each with its own therapeutic effects; the most notable are listed below.

○ Matsu-no-yu: Temperature 127 degrees; spring contains sulfur and gas. The water rises from alluvial strata; flow rate is approximately 2 *to* 5 *shō* per minute.
○ Taki-no-yu: Same, 119 degrees; contains sulfur. Rises from alluvial strata; approximately 1 *to* 3 *shō* per minute. The bedrock from which the spring water rises is the same type for all those listed below.
○ Oza-no-yu: Same, 122 degrees; all same as above.
○ Hori-no-yu: Same, 125 degrees; contains sulfur and gas. Flow rate approximately 5 *to* per minute.
○ Goten-no-yu: Same, 117 degrees; probably contains sulfur. Approximately 2 *to* per minute.
○ Isago-yu: Same, 122 degrees; approximately 7 *shō*; does not contain sulfur or gas.
○ Senki-no-yu: Same, 127 degrees; contains sulfur; approximately 3 *to*.
○ Kizu-no-yu: Same, 111 degrees; spring composition same as above; approximately 7 *shō*.
○ Me-no-yu (also known as Wata-no-yu): Same, 105 degrees; approximately 4 *to*; all others same as above.
○ Ume-no-yu: Same, 120 degrees; 1 *to* 7 *shō*; all others same as above.
○ Take-no-yu: Same, 126 degrees; approximately 1 *to* 2 *shō*; all others same as above.

**Same (Local) Shiraito (白糸)**　Located in Yuhara Village, eighteen *chō* east of Matsumoto. The spring contains sulfur and related compounds, and is effective for stroke (*chūfū*), numbness/paralysis (*shibiré*), muscular weakness (*ijaku*), gout, deficiency conditions (*kyoshō*), digestive blockage (*tsukae*), syphilis, various women's ailments, and all manner of melancholic and depressive conditions (*fusagi-byō*).

**(Manzoku/Comprehensive) Yuhara Village:** The bedrock in this area is composed of ancient volcanic rock. ○ Shiraito: Twenty *chō* from Asama; temperature 108 degrees; the spring quality is pure, tasteless, and odorless. No sulfur precipitate is observed. The water rises from alluvial strata; flow rate approximately 2 *to* 5 *shō* per minute. ○ Tearai-no-yu (also known as "Mitarashi-no-yu"): One and a half *chō* from Shiraito-no-yu; temperature 82 degrees; contains sulfurous gas; rises from alluvial strata; approximately 5 *shō* per minute. ○ Fujii-no-yu: Same, 91.4 degrees; rises from alluvial strata; flow rate approximately 1 *to* 2 *shō*. Approximately four *chō* from Shiraito and Goten-yu.

**Ina District (Local) Yamamuro (山室)**　Located in Kawai Village. The spring contains sulfurous gas, and is recommended for scabies and various skin sores.

---

**Azumi District (Local) Shirabone (白骨)**　Located in Azumi Village. Contains sulfurous gas, and is recommended for headaches, rising heat (*nobose*), retained fluids (*ryūin*), colic (*senki*), digestive blockage (*tsukae*), beriberi (*kakke*), scabies, and related conditions.

**Same (Local) Nakafusa (中房)**　Located in Ariake Village. Contains both sulfurous and alum gases, and is effective for colic.

**Same (Local) Kuzu-no-yu (葛ノ湯)**　Located in Noguchi Village, divided into approximately four sections. The spring contains sulfurous gas.
○ Moto-no-yu: Recommended for sore toxins, scabies, carbuncles and boils (*yōchō*), and bruises.
○ Gorin-tō: Recommended for stroke, dysentery (*ribyō*), consumptive exhaustion (*kyorō*), diabetes (*shōkatsu*), colic, urinary disease (*rinshitsu*), hemorrhoids, and women's blood ailments.
○ Kanatsubo-yu: Recommended for stroke, urinary disease, colic, hemorrhoids, and related conditions.
○ Hashimoto-yu: Recommended for hemorrhoids and tapeworms (*sanpaku*) in women.

**Suwa District (Local) Kami-Suwa (上諏訪)**　Located in Takashima Town. There are five springs in total; the spring quality has abundant sulfurous gas. Four of them are recommended for moist skin sores and hemorrhoids. One is extremely hot and suitable for cooking rice and brewing tea.

**Same (Local) Shimo-Suwa (下諏訪)**　Located at Shimosuwa post station. Approximately three springs.
○ Wata-yu: The spring contains sulfurous and saline gases, and is recommended for internal deficiency, hemorrhoids, and colic.
○ Ko-yu: Spring quality and therapeutic indications same as above; also recommended for scabies.
○ Tanka-no-yu: Spring quality and therapeutic indications same as above.
