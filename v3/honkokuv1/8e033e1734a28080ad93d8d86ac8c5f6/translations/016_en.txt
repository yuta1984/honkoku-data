**[Chikuma District — Inukai Hot Spring, continued from previous page]**

The villagers regularly use the water for cooking and medicinal decoctions.

○ Goza-yu (御坐湯): Effective for syphilis.
○ Taki-no-yu (滝ノ湯): Recommended for blood disorders.
○ Senki-yu (疝気湯): Recommended for colic (*senki*), hemorrhoids, and weeping skin sores.
○ Kizu-yu (疵湯): Effective for blade wounds, burns, scabies, and sore toxins.
○ Wata-yu (和多湯): Main therapeutic uses same as above.

○ Although there are several other springs, each with its own therapeutic effects, only the most notable ones are listed here.

○ Matsu-no-yu: Temperature 127°F; the spring contains sulfur and gas; springs from alluvial deposits; output approximately 2 *tō* 5 *shō* per minute.
○ Taki-no-yu: Same, 119°F; the spring contains sulfur; springs from alluvial deposits; output approximately 1 *tō* 3 *shō* per minute. The bedrock from which the spring water emerges is the same type for all springs listed below.
○ Goza-no-yu: Same, 122°F; all as above.
○ Hori-no-yu: Same, 125°F; contains sulfur and gas; output approximately 5 *tō* per minute.
○ Goten-no-yu: Same, 117°F; presumably contains sulfur; approximately 2 *tō* per minute.
○ Isago-yu (砂子湯): Same, 122°F; approximately 7 *shō*; does not contain sulfur or gas.
○ Senki-no-yu: Same, 127°F; contains sulfur; approximately 3 *tō* per minute.
○ Kizu-no-yu: Same, 111°F; spring composition same as above; approximately 7 *shō* per minute.
○ Me-no-yu (also known as Wata-no-yu): Same, 105°F; approximately 4 *tō*; all else same as above.
○ Ume-no-yu: Same, 120°F; 1 *tō* 7 *shō*; all else same as above.
○ Take-no-yu: Same, 126°F; approximately 1 *tō* 2 *shō*; all else same as above.

**Same (Local) Shiraito (白糸)**　Located in Yuhara Village, eighteen *chō* east of Matsumoto. The spring contains sulfur and other elements, and is recommended for stroke, numbness (*shibiré*), muscular weakness (*ijaku*), gout, deficiency conditions (*kyoshō*), digestive obstruction (*tsukae*), syphilis, various women's ailments, and all manner of melancholic and depressive conditions.

**(Unranked) Yuhara Village** — The rock formation in this area is composed of ancient volcanic stone.

○ Shiraito (白糸) Spring: Twenty *chō* from Asama; temperature 108°F; the spring water is pure, tasteless, and odorless, with no sulfur precipitate visible. Springs from alluvial deposits; output approximately 2 *tō* 5 *shō* per minute.
○ Tearai-no-yu (手洗ノ湯), also known as "Mitarashi-no-yu": One and a half *chō* from Shiraito Spring; temperature 82°F; contains sulfurous gas; springs from alluvial deposits; output approximately 5 *shō* per minute.
○ Fujii-no-yu (藤井ノ湯): Same, 91.4°F; springs from alluvial deposits; output approximately 1 *tō* 2 *shō*; approximately four *chō* from Shiraito and Goten Springs.

**Ina District (Local) Yamamuro (山室)**　Located in Kawai Village. The spring contains sulfurous gas, and is recommended for scabies and various skin sores.

**Azumi District (Local) Shirabone (白骨)**　Located in Azumi Village. Contains sulfurous gas, and is recommended for headaches, *nobose* (blood rushing to the head), fluid retention (*ryūin*), colic, digestive obstruction, beriberi, scabies, and other conditions.

**Same (Local) Nakafusa (中房)**　Located in Ariake Village. Contains both sulfurous and alum gases, and is effective for colic.

**Same (Local) Kuzu-no-yu (葛ノ湯)**　Located in Noguchi Village, divided into approximately four sections. The spring contains sulfurous gas.

○ Moto-no-yu: Recommended for sore toxins, scabies, carbuncles and furuncles (*yōchō*), and bruises.
○ Gorin-tō (五痳湯): Recommended for stroke, dysentery (*ribyō*), consumptive fatigue (*kyorō*), wasting-thirst disease (*shōkatsu*), colic, urinary disease, hemorrhoids, and women's blood disorders.
○ Kanatsub-yu (金壺湯): Recommended for stroke, urinary disease, colic, hemorrhoids, and other conditions.
○ Hashimoto-yu: Recommended for hemorrhoids and tapeworm (*sunpaku*) in women.

**Suwa District (Local) Kami-Suwa (上諏訪)**　Located in Takashima Town, with a total of five springs. The spring water has a high sulfurous content; four of the springs are recommended for wet skin sores and hemorrhoids; one is extremely hot and suitable for cooking rice and brewing tea.

**Same (Local) Shimo-Suwa (下諏訪)**　Located at Shimo-Suwa Station (*eki*), with approximately three springs.

○ Wata-yu (綿湯): The spring contains both sulfurous and salt gases, and is recommended for internal deficiency, hemorrhoids, and colic.
○ Ko-yu (小湯): Spring composition and therapeutic uses same as above; also recommended for scabies.
○ Tanka-no-yu (タンカノ湯): Spring composition and therapeutic uses same as above.
