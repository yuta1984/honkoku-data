**Same (Surveyed) Shima Village (四万)**　Has a saline taste due to its salt content.

The principal components of this spring are common salt, sodium sulfate (*sojūmu*), and others; by virtue of its resolvent, alterative, laxative, and antacid properties, it is effective for the following conditions:

> **[Conditions benefited by internal use]**
> Weak stomach　Indigestion ("things that do not digest well")　Anemia ("condition of insufficient blood")　Liver disease　Habitual constipation
> Those suffering from the above conditions should take it internally for benefit. The dose per occasion should be from one *san* to three *san*.

> **[Conditions benefited by external bathing]**
> Chronic skin diseases　Stubborn rheumatism　Numbness and weakness of the limbs and joints (*te-ashi-fushi-bushi*) arising from dislocation or contusion
> Those suffering from the above conditions should take external baths for benefit.

For neuralgia, both internal and external use should be employed for benefit. Dosage as above.

**Same (Local) Kazawa (鹿沢)**　Located in Tashiro Village. Spring quality unknown. Recommended for headache, colic accumulation, stroke, and retained fluids (*ryūin*). Also said to revive withered plants and grasses.

**Same (Category 1) Kawara**　Located in Kawara-yu Village. Spring quality contains calcium sulfate, alum earth, bitter earth, hydrochloric acid, and hydrogen sulfide gas. Recommended for scabies and rheumatic pain.

**Same (Category 1) Kawanaka**　Located in Matsutani Village. Contains calcium sulfate, alum earth, bitter earth, and hydrochloric alkali. Recommended for various types of ulcers and scabies.

**Same (Local) Hato-yu (Dove Bath)**　Located in Motoschuku Village. Spring quality contains sulfur and alum vapors. Recommended for sword wounds and cuts (*kinshō*).

**Same (Category □) Yuga-shima**　Located in Sarugakyō Village. Spring quality contains calcium sulfate, alum earth, and magnesium chloride. Principal indications: colic pain, scabies, rheumatism, syphilis, leprosy, and other conditions.

**Same (Local) Hōshi-tōge (法師嶺)**　Located in Nagai Village. Recommended for congestion / hot flushes (*nobose*).

**Same (Local / Analyzed) Kubo**　Located in Yujuku Village. Recommended for stroke and similar conditions. ○ *Yakushi*: recommended for eye diseases. ○ *Kotaki*: recommended for warts. (Analyzed) ○ (Category 1): Temperature 145 degrees. Spring quality contains sulfur and gas; reportedly tasteless and odorless, but with white precipitate. The spring water issues from ancient volcanic rock; its flow rate is approximately 2 *to* 5 *shō* per minute. ○ (2): Same, 131 degrees. Flow rate approximately 1 *to* 2 *shō*. Other qualities same as above. ○ (3): Same, 115 degrees. Contains no gas; all other qualities same as above.

**Same (Category □) Kami-Sawatari (上沢渡)**　Located in Kami-Sawatari Village. Spring quality: calcium sulfate, magnesium chloride, and alum earth. Recommended for syphilis, ulcers, and scabies.

**Same (Local) Manza (万座)**　Located at the foot of Manza Mountain. Contains sulfur and green vitriol (*tanban*). Recommended for retained fluids and colic.

**Same (Category 1) Hanashiki (花敷)**　Located in Iriyama Village. Contains calcium sulfate, alum earth, and iron chloride. Recommended for scabies and swellings.

**Same (Category 1) Misaka-yu**　Located in Fukiji Village. Spring quality unknown. Recommended for tapeworm (*sunpaku*), burns, and cuts.

**Same (Category 1) Fuse-yu**　Located in Fuse Village. Spring quality and therapeutic effects unknown.
