Same region, Teppōsui (Iron Gun Water): A cold spring that emerges near Yuyama-machi, located at an elevation of approximately 1,300 shaku above sea level.

The spring water is colorless and clear, but when placed in an uncovered vessel and left for several days, it produces a small amount of sediment. The components of this sediment are particularly hydrated iron oxide and ferrous oxide. When the spring water is poured, it produces some bubbles in the water. It also has an acidic reaction, and when tasted, it has an acidic flavor that stimulates the tongue, with no salty taste and a slight hydrogen sulfide odor. In summer, foreigners who come to bathe at this location all prefer to use this spring water as a refreshing beverage.

Same region, Tori-jigoku (Bird Hell): Located in Arima's Jigoku Valley, it is a small square-shaped well with a depth of approximately three shaku. According to tradition, any birds that happen to approach this place die instantly. This is the origin of the name "Bird Hell." The location is somewhat higher than the Teppōsui site and not very far from it. The bottom of the well is filled with rotted leaves and other organic matter, which emits an unpleasant odor. Gas from the well was collected in two bottles and analyzed, confirming that it was filled with a large amount of carbonic acid gas. Perhaps the cause of bird deaths is suffocation due to the abundant generation of carbonic acid. If so, one should be able to see their corpses in the well.

Same region, Mearai-yu (Eye-washing Hot Spring): A cold spring located in Tani-no-machi, somewhat lower than Arima Hot Spring. The water is slightly yellow-tinged but has no odor. It contains a large amount of free carbonic acid and is effective for eye diseases.

Same region, Cold Spring near Shimizu-an Temple

Shimizu-an is a small temple where many foreigners stay during summer. This cold spring emerges in a corner of the vegetable garden, creating the appearance of a garden pond. It is not yet utilized like Teppōsui. This spring is located at approximately 1,260 shaku above sea level. Its properties are colorless, clear, and odorless. When poured, it produces some bubbles and shows an acidic reaction. When tasted, it slightly stimulates the tongue with an acidic flavor and no salty taste.

Kawabe District, Tada-yu (also called Hirano-yu): Located in Tada-shō, about one ri from Ikeda. Being a lukewarm spring, it is heated for bathing. The spring water contains much iron and has a very salty taste.

Same district, Hitokura: Located in Hitokura near Tada, with the same temperature and spring quality as above.

Muko District, Takarazuka-yama: Located in Itoshi Village, the spring water contains salt and is good for eczema.

Ubara District, Umaga-buchi: Kumanai Village. The spring water contains much salt and acid, good for debility symptoms. No bathhouse has been opened yet.

Yabe District, Minatoyama: Located at the foot of the mountain above Minato River, the spring water contains both sulfur and alum.
