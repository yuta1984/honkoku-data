## Shimotsuke Province (下野国)

**Tsuga District (都賀郡) (Chemical Analysis / Miscellaneous Records) Chūzenji-yu, also known as Yumoto**　Located within the mountains of Nikkō. Approximately 6 *ri* northwest of Haraishi-juku, approximately 3 *ri* from Chūzenji. Spring quality contains sulfurous vapor.

**(1) Gosho-yu (Analyzed)**　Temperature: 140 degrees. Spring quality is clear, tasteless, and odorless. Recommended for all manner of wounds, scabies, bruises, hemorrhoids, and burns. Also said to have a fortifying (*kyōsō*) effect. ○ (Surveyed) Therapeutic effects are similar to those of the salt-free spring (*muenshō*) at Atami in Izu. Refer to Leaf 8.

**(2) Naka-no-yu**　Temperature: 108 or 118 degrees. Spring quality has no odor but a markedly acidic taste, and contains a large quantity of sulfur. Therapeutic effects are similar to those of the Ō-yu (Great Bath) at Atami in Izu. Refer to Leaf 9.

**(3) Taki-no-yu**　Same: 99 or 118 degrees. Tasteless and odorless. Therapeutic effects same as above.

**(4) Uba-no-yu**　Same: 103, 149, or 133 degrees. Spring quality is clear, tasteless, and odorless. Therapeutic effects same as above.

**(5) Sasa-yu**　Same: 145 or 129 degrees. Clear, odorless, with a bitter taste. Therapeutic effects same as above.

**(6) Jizai-yu**　Same: 151 or 156 degrees. Spring quality is clear with a slightly acidic taste. Also called "Ara-yu." ○ (Miscellaneous Records) Therapeutic effects: stroke, headache, intestinal parasites (*sunpaku*), phlegm, abdominal pain, and lower back pain. However, it is harmful for jaundice.

**(7) Yakushi-yu, also known as Junko-yu**　Same: 106 or 99 degrees. Therapeutic effects same as above. (Miscellaneous Records) Recommended for eye diseases, rush of blood to the head (*nobose*), intestinal parasites, scabies, hemorrhoidal disease, numbness/paralysis (*mashitsu*), lower back pain, debility (*ishaku*), abdominal pain, and stomach ailments (*kan'i*).

**(8) Hadaka-yu**　Same: 136 degrees. Spring quality is clear, contains sulfurous vapor, and has an acidic taste.

**(9) Kawara-yu**　(Surveyed) Temperature: 120 or 135 degrees. A saline spring containing a small quantity of sulfur. Should be avoided by those with syphilitic sores, epilepsy (*tenkан*), and abdominal bloating. ○ (Miscellaneous Records) Therapeutic effects: hernia (*senki*), intestinal parasites, abdominal pain, hemorrhoids, numbness/paralysis, malaria-like fever (*kaiki*), intestinal worms, phlegm, and debility. However, it should be avoided by those with jaundice.
