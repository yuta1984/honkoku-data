Springs emerging from alluvial layers. Kayabe District, Tomi: Two ri from Shikabe, one and a half ri from Ikusa River, temperature 122 degrees, spring water emerges from alluvial layers, nearly clear, with small volume.

Same district, Yuzaki: Four chō west of Washino-ki village (near the coast), cold spring with no gas, appears to contain large amounts of green vitriol. Spring water emerges from alluvial layers with small volume.

Same district, Boro: One ri toward Esashi from Otoshibe, two locations. One has temperature 91.4 degrees, the other 99 degrees. Both spring qualities contain iron (carbonic acid?) gas. Spring water emerges from alluvial layers in small quantities.

Same district, Shikabe: Located in Shikabe village, divided into five locations. All emerge on coastal sandy beaches. (1) Temperature 196 degrees, contains small amount of iron with gas bubbles rising. Large volume of spring water emerges. (2) Same 159 degrees, small emergence volume, others same as above. (3) Same 158 degrees, others same as above. (4) Same 111 degrees, others same as above. (5) Same 108 degrees, others same as above.

Same district, Notafu: Two ri toward Esashi road from Otoshibe. Cold spring containing hydrogen sulfide. Emerges from river sand in small quantities.

Same district, Upper Kusuri: Two ri from Otoshibe on Esashi highway. Temperature 86 degrees, gas bubbles rise in the spring. Others same as above.

Same district, Lower Kusuri: One hundred ken toward Otoshibe from Upper Kusuri hot spring, three locations. One has temperature 114 degrees, the other two are cold springs. Both spring qualities contain sulfur, iron and gas, emerging from alluvium layers.

Same district, Kawakumi: Three locations, half ri toward the mountains from Kawakumi. (1) Temperature about 122 degrees, spring quality contains very small amount of hydrogen sulfide, and gas bubbles are seen rising. Small amount of spring water comes from gray-green volcanic porphyry (a type of volcanic rock). (2) Temperature 117 degrees, same spring quality, small amount of spring water comes from surface sand. (3) Temperature about 104 degrees, rock from which spring water emerges is same as (1).

Same district, Esan: Located at Mount Esan, half ri from Yutanai, three and a half chō from sulfur field, with three locations. Also one large boiling spring in the sulfur mine. (1) Temperature about 131 degrees, spring quality contains hydrogen sulfide with very acidic taste and astringent properties. Small amount of spring water emerges from alluvium layer. (2) Same about 122 degrees, spring quality etc. same as above. (3) Same about 113 degrees, spring quality etc. same as above.

Same district, Nigorikawa: Located one and a half ri from the coast near Ishikura, two ri northwest of Washino-ki, emerges at four locations. Also many cold springs containing sulfur at seven chō and fourteen chō toward the sea. (1) Temperature 135 degrees, spring quality contains hydrogen sulfide, small amount of spring water emerges from alluvium layer. (2) Same 131 degrees, spring quality etc. same as above. (3) Same 124 degrees, spring quality etc. same as above. (4) Same 120 degrees, spring quality etc. same as above, gas bubbles seen rising.

Same district, Isoya: Between Shikabe village and Kumadomari village, half ri from Isoya, five locations. Spring water emerges from gray soft semi-slate. (1) Temperature 162 degrees, spring quality contains hydrogen sulfide and gas. (2) Same 156 degrees. (3) Same 153 degrees.
