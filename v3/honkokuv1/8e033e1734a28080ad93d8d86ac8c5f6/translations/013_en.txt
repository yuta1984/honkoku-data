This mineral spring contains very small amounts of minerals and should be considered simply a sulfur-containing hot spring. The therapeutic effects and properties are the same as the Oyu at Atami in Izu. Slightly stronger than Yoshina hot spring in the same province.

Same district, Ubako-no-yu: 3 ri 2 cho from Kiga, 3 ri 12 cho from Miyanoshita, 3 ri 15 cho from Dogashima, 3 ri 10 cho from Sokura. Located in Motohakone Village, temperature 113 degrees. Effective for various wounds.

Same district, Yugawara: 5 ri from Atami, 1 ri 17 cho from Izusan, 5 ri 8 cho from Odawara, located in Miyakami Village. (1) Shita-no-yu - Temperature: 122 degrees, traces of hydrogen sulfide; (2) Kawara-no-yu - Temperature: 107 degrees, contains no hydrogen sulfide at all; (3) Yakushi-no-yu - Temperature: 113 degrees, contains hydrogen sulfide; (4) Mama-no-yu - This spring mixes with cold water, making its temperature difficult to determine. These various springs are located in low-lying areas between mountains along both banks of a mountain stream, with most emerging from within the stream itself. Local people boast of their efficacy against malignant wounds, but they are merely simple hot springs containing very small amounts of hydrogen sulfide.
