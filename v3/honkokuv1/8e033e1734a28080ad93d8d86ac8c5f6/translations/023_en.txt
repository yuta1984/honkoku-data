**Iwaki Province　Shirakawa District　(Official) (Local) Kasshi (Katsushi)**　Located in Tsuruō Village. Two source springs; temperature 113 degrees. Spring quality contains common salt, iron, and sulfuric acid vapor; clear and transparent with no odor. Effective for blood loss, chlorosis, debility, retention of fluids (*ryūin*), ulcers and sores (*tadare-mono*), pulmonary disease, colicky pain, various childhood ailments (*shōni-shokkan*), dog bites, and viper stings. However, it is harmful to those suffering from syphilis. There are three bathhouses, with over 2,000 bathers per year.

**Same (Local) Egimata (Yushimata)**　Located in Yushimata Village. Eighteen *ri* northwest of Mito. Spring quality contains aluminous vapor. Recommended for apoplexy (*chūfū*), beriberi, headache, and similar conditions.

**Iwasaki District　(Local) (Official) Sabako (Mitsuhako)**　Located in Yumoto Village, 22 *ri* north of Mito and more than 1 *ri* from the castle town of Taira. Fifty-three source springs; 83 bathing tubs. Spring quality contains sulfurous and aluminous vapors. Recommended for scabies, syphilis, hemorrhoidal conditions, and similar ailments. ○ (Official) Spring quality contains common salt and hydrogen sulfide gas. There are 70 bathhouses, with approximately 5,000 or more bathers per year.

**Same (Official) Nakakura-yu**　Located in Takano Village. Temperature 44 degrees. Spring quality is alkaline in nature; recommended for hernia, hemorrhoidal conditions, tapeworm (*sunpaku*), and similar conditions. Over 3,000 bathers per year.

**Same (Official) Dōden-yu**　Temperature 47 degrees. Spring quality and therapeutic effects same as above.

**Katta District　(Local) Yukatta (Yugatta)**　Located in Miya Village, now called Tōda. Three source springs. Spring quality contains sulfurous and saline vapors. Recommended for leprosy, syphilis, apoplexy, debility from chronic illness (*kyorō*), blood disorders, eye diseases, and similar conditions.

**Same (Local) Kamasaki**　Located in Kuramoto Village. Spring quality contains sulfurous, aluminous, and saline vapors. Therapeutic effects include treatment of sword cuts, beriberi, and similar conditions.

**Same (Local) Yudo**　Located in Obara Village. Spring quality unknown. Recommended for rush of blood to the head, headache, blood disorders, and similar conditions.

**Same (Local) Shin-yu (New Spring)**　Located in Obara Village. Spring quality unknown. Recommended for hernia with accumulation (*senshaku*).

**Iwashiro Province　Aizu District　(Official) (Miscellaneous) Higashiyama　Also known as: Tennei-ji-yu or Yumoto**　Located in Yumoto Village. Spring quality contains aluminous vapor. Recommended for various sores and swellings, apoplexy, beriberi, syphilis, and similar conditions. ○ Taki-no-yu: temperature 110 degrees. ○ Kitsune-yu (Fox Bath): 96 degrees. ○ Saru-yu (Monkey Bath): 104 degrees. ○ Mujina-no-yu (Badger Bath): same temperature. ○ Kuda-no-yu: 128 degrees. ○ Sō-yu: 127 degrees. ○ Kobiki-yu: 123 degrees. ○ Ana-yu (Cave Bath): 122 degrees. ○ Sazanami-no-yu (Ripple Bath): 128 degrees. ○ Sumi-no-yu (Corner Bath): 118 degrees.

Higashiyama Hot Spring is located approximately one *ri* east of Wakamatsu, at a spot reached by ascending only a short distance up the mountain slope. More than half of the bathhouses are two- or three-storied buildings; they number over fifteen in total, all standing in rows along both banks of a clear stream called the Yukawa River, presenting a most picturesque scene. It may truly be called the finest location for escaping the summer heat. The number of bathers per year is said to reach approximately 27,000 to 28,000.
