**Tōsandō Circuit**

**Mino Province — Kamo District (Local) Kashio (鹿塩)**　Located in Kashio Village. The spring contains sulfurous gas, is mildly warm, and is effective for various skin sores and lesions.

**Kani District (Local) Shitsuki (次月)**　Located in Shitsuki Village. Spring composition and therapeutic effects: same as above.

**Toki District (Local) Soki (曽木)**　Located in Soki Village. Spring composition and therapeutic effects: same as above.

**Same (Local) Kamado (釜戸)**　Located in Kamado-juku Village. The spring contains alum, salt, and iron.

**Ena District (Local) Ozawa**　Springs up from a mountain valley stream in Iihazama Village. Mildly warm with sulfurous gas. Recommended for hemorrhoids (*jishitsu*).

**Same (Local) Kurimoto (栗元)**　Located in the mountains of Fukuoka Village. Mildly warm with sulfurous gas. The spring vent is extremely narrow. Effective for various skin sores.

**Same (Local) Shōbu (菖蒲)**　Located in Morokokubo Village. Spring composition and therapeutic effects are the same as those of the Kashio Hot Spring in Kamo District.

**Same (Local) Kutsukake**　Located in Tase Village. Spring composition and therapeutic effects: same as above.

**Hida Province — Masuda District (Local) Ōhora (大洞)**　Formerly known as Kosaka Hot Spring (*Kosaka Onsen*). Located in Ōhora, Yuya Village. Spring composition unknown. Recommended for colic, urinary disease (*rinshitsu*), hemorrhoids, scabies (*kaisen*), syphilis, bruises (*uchimi*), and sword/blade wounds (*kirikizu*), among others.

**Same (Local) Nigorikawa (濁川)**　Located in Ochiai Village. The spring contains sulfurous gas, and is recommended for blade wounds.

**Same (Local) Gero (下呂) Township**　There are two springs. One is located in Wasa Village; the other is in Yushima Village, which has been famous since ancient times. However, it was struck by a flood in the *mizunoto-u* (癸卯) year of Tenpō 14 (1843) and sank into the riverbed, making it impossible to bathe in today.

**Ōno District (Local) Miya**　Located in the mountains of Miya Village. Heated and then used for bathing.

**Yoshiki District (Local) Hirayu (平湯)**　Located in Hirayu Village. The spring water has a pleasant, salty taste, and is recommended for rheumatism and dampness ailments, scabies, and various women's ailments.

**Same (Local) Yamabushi-yu (山伏湯)**　Located in the same village. The spring contains sulfurous gas, and is recommended for colic (*senki*) and hemorrhoids (*jishitsu*).

**Same (Local) Kabata (蒲田)**　Located in Kamisaka Village. The spring contains sulfurous gas, and is recommended for wind-toxin ailments (*fūdoku*), leprosy (*raishitsu*), scabies, and various skin sores.
