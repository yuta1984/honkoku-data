**Tagata District (Sagami) — Hatake**

Three *ri* and twenty *chō* from Atami; less than two *ri* from Mishima Station; slightly more than one *ri* from Nirayama. Temperature: 98.6 degrees. This spring lies along the road connecting Atami to Kona, at the foot of the Atami Pass, situated within rice paddies. It belongs to the category of simple thermal springs and contains absolutely no gas or sediment matter.

**Same (Sagami) — Yoshina Village**

Ten *ri* from Atami; three *ri* from Shūzenji; one *ri* from Yugashima. Temperature: 105.8 degrees. Spring quality contains a large quantity of hydrogen sulfide. This mineral spring is counted among the finest bathing establishments in the province of Izu. Its therapeutic effects and properties are similar to Ōyu at Atami and Ashi-no-yu at Hakone; however, it is somewhat weaker than Ashi-no-yu.

**Same (Sagami) — Yugashima**

One *ri* from Yoshina; three and a half *ri* from Shūzenji; eleven *ri* from Atami. Springs emerge at two locations along the bank of the Kano River. No bathing facilities have yet been established.

- ○ **Nishidaira-yu**: Temperature 105.8 degrees. Spring quality and taste are similar to Kamifunabara-no-yu in the same district. Recommended for scabies.
- ○ **Sekotaki-yu**: Same, 111 degrees. Spring quality is the same as Yoshina-no-yu in the same district. Recommended for eye ailments, colic (*senshaku*), paralysis, and women's blood disorders.
