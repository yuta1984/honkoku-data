The residences of Suzuki, Ishikawa, Sakamaki, Fukui, and Yamazaki were burned. In Ueno-cho 1-chome side street, the fire was stopped at the Yoshida residence. The main halls of Toeisan (Kan'ei-ji Temple) suffered no particular damage, though some lodging quarters within the temple grounds sustained minor damage. The grounds of Shinobazu Benzaiten shrine were severely damaged, and Ikenohata Naka-cho suffered some damage. Osukiya-cho completely collapsed.

Most of the samurai residences along Tenjinshita-dori collapsed. Kaya-cho's 1st and 2nd districts collapsed and were completely burned. The mountainside areas remained but many houses collapsed. From there, Ueno Yamashita-dori was damaged. Kotoku-ji temple front street had no particular damage, while Shitaya Inari and Yanagi no Inari shrines were damaged. Generally, this area has many temples, so the damage was extensive. Abekawa-cho had moderate damage. Also, a fire broke out from Kikuyabashi Nishi-Shinji-cho and burned about half a block on both sides.

At Sanjusangen-do front, Yamamoto Nitayu's residence and the area within the bamboo fence collapsed and caught fire, with only the fenced area burning. Yasaki Inari shrine at the same location remained. This entire area was completely destroyed. Similarly, there was much collapse at the temple gates of Kaizen-ji, Gokuraku-ji, Tomyo-ji, and Genkuu-ji.

The attached lands of Higashi Monzeki were severely damaged, Banzui-in gate front was damaged, and there were many collapses around Yamabushi-cho and Yamazaki-cho. Shin-Sakamoto-cho completely collapsed. The Iriya area was severely damaged, and Shitaya Sakamoto-cho 2nd and 3rd districts burned down. The 4th district was severely damaged, Ono Terusaki Myojin shrine was slightly damaged, and there were many collapses around Negishi. Similarly, Kanasugi-cho 4th district completely collapsed with many people crushed to death. Mishima shrine and Hiyoke Kannon were severely damaged. Ryusen-ji district completely collapsed. Ryusen-ji temple, Daion-ji temple, Saitoku-ji temple, Washi Daimyojin shrine and others shook violently. From Minowa Shinmachi to Tenno-mae, there were many collapses, and all the inns in Kozukahara Nakamura-cho burned down. From there, Hashido Kawara and Senju Kamon post station suffered severe damage.

Collapsed houses: 1,721; Collapsed storehouses: 198 locations; Many deaths.

On the 14th day, the hundred-unit long house opposite the Mito domain residence outside Koishikawa gate burned sporadically. Similarly, there were many collapses around Ogisaka-cho and Mito-machi. Dentsu-in temple gate front suffered slight damage. Damage around Hakusan, Sugamo, and Komagome was slight. Dangozaka, Shitaya-naka, and Misaki-cho completely collapsed.
