○It was heard that no small number of people perished in the fires caused by the earthquake on the second day of the tenth month. Though it was a natural disaster, His Lordship felt pity for them, and on the second day of the eleventh month, memorial services (segaki) were ordered to be performed at temples of various Buddhist sects for those who died. This truly shows his benevolent grace.

The names of the aforementioned temples are recorded below:

Great memorial services were performed at the aforementioned temples.

○Furthermore, five relief shelters were constructed:
Outside Saiwai-bashi Gate, Asakusa Hirokoji, Fukagawa Seaside Shinden, Same Hachiman Precincts, Ueno Mountain Base Fire Prevention Grounds

○Toeisan Prince's shelter at the mountain base, same location
Total: Six locations

○From the night of the second until around the twentieth, there were frequent small earthquakes, and people camped outdoors every night, piling their belongings in vacant lots. To these people, the authorities provided substantial rice meals from the third to the twentieth day.

○Townspeople throughout the city, according to their means, provided charitable rice and money to their neighborhoods, their workers, those in the relief shelters, or those camping on vacant lots.

○Memorial services were held at nearby temples in many places. Though all were extensive, they are abbreviated here.
