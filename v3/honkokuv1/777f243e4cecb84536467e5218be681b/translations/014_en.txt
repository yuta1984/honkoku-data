During the Hour of the Sheep (approximately 2 PM), clouds gathered above the West Samurai Hall of the Kamakura Palace, and an earthquake occurred. On the 22nd, a military commander had a divination performed, and as the result indicated a grave event, he entered the residence of the Governor of Sagami. On the 6th day of the 9th month, during the Hour of the Ox (approximately 2 AM), a great earthquake struck. On the 8th, during the Hour of the Tiger (approximately 4 AM), another great tremor occurred. On the 11th, during the Hour of the Tiger, another tremor, along with a smaller one. Tremors also occurred on the 13th, 14th, and 16th. On the 17th, three tremors occurred. On the 21st, prayers were offered to 36,000 deities. Chikamoto presided over the earthquake ritual, and Nobusane served as the administrator.

**[Emperor Shijō / Bunryaku]** Second year (1235), 13th day of the 4th month: earthquake during the Hour of the Horse (approximately noon). 28th day of the same month: earthquake during the Hour of the Sheep (approximately 2 PM). 29th day: earthquake at the same hour. On the 8th day, prayers were offered and a decree regarding benevolent governance (tokusei) was issued.

**[Ninji]** Second year (1241), 7th day of the 2nd month: great earthquake during the Hour of the Snake (approximately 10 AM). According to an elder, a similarly great earthquake had occurred during the Kenryaku era (1211–1213). **[Referring specifically to Kenpō 1st year (1213)]** He wondered aloud whether it might have been an omen of the rebellion of Wada Saemon-no-jō Yoshimori. On the same day, two smaller tremors occurred during the Hour of the Horse and the Hour of the Rat. On the 8th, another tremor during the Hour of the Snake. Between yesterday and today, five tremors in total. **[The above is from the Azuma Kagami.]**

**[Emperor Gofukakusa / Kenchō]** Sixth year (1254), intercalary 5th month, 11th and 19th days: great earthquakes. **[From the Kō Nendai Ryakki.]**

**[Shōka]** First year (1257), 23rd day of the 8th month: great earthquake in Kamakura. A loud sound accompanied it. Not a single shrine or temple was left intact; mountains collapsed, houses fell, earthen walls and fences were entirely damaged, the earth cracked and water gushed out. Notably, near the Geba Bridge, blue flames were said to have burst forth from the cracks in the ground. On the 4th day of the 9th month, light rain fell and an earthquake occurred during the Hour of the Monkey (approximately 4 PM). Since the great earthquake of last month, the tremors had not ceased until this day.

**[Emperor Fushimi / Einin]** First year (1293), 13th day of the 4th month: great earthquake during the Hour of the Tiger (approximately 4 AM). Mountains collapsed, houses were overturned, and the number of deaths was beyond counting. Beginning with the sixteen-foot Buddha hall of Daihi-ji Temple in Kamakura, many buildings were crushed and buried; Jufuku-ji and Engaku-ji temples collapsed and burned. The devastation across various locations was too great to enumerate. The death toll was 21,024. **[From the Hōjō-ki.]**

**[Emperor Gofushimi / Shōan]** First year (1299), 25th day of the 4th month: great earthquake. The Golden Hall of Shitennō-ji Temple in Settsu Province collapsed, and the hall of Nanzen-ji Temple in eastern Kyoto also fell. Over ten thousand people died in the Kinai region (the five home provinces). **[From the Honchō Nendaiki.]**

**[Emperor Hanazono / Bunpō]** First year (1317), from the 1st month onward: successive great earthquakes in Kyoto. The pagoda of Tō-ji Temple was shaken down; Kiyomizu-dera Temple went up in flames, and even the portrait of General Tamura (Sakanoue no Tamuramaro) was consumed by fire. **[From the Hōjō-ki.]**

**[Emperor Godaigo / Shōchū]** First year (1324), 11th month: great earthquake. Chikubu Island in Lake Ōmi (Lake Biwa) partly collapsed and sank into the lake. **[From the Honchō Nendaiki.]**

Genkō First year (1331), 3rd day of the 7th month: great earthquake.
