Chronicle of Earthquakes

In ancient times during the age of gods, when Hayasusanoo-no-Mikoto was about to depart for the Land of Roots (Ne-no-kuni), he ascended to Takamagahara to bid farewell to Amaterasu-Omikami. At that time, all mountains and rivers moved, and the entire land shook. As recorded in the Kojiki, this should be considered the beginning of earthquakes.

5th year of Emperor Inkyo, 7th month, 14th day: Earthquake. 7th year of Empress Suiko, 4th month, 27th day: The earth moved and all houses were destroyed. Consequently, orders were given to all quarters to worship the earthquake deity.

1st year of Empress Kogyoku, 10th month, 8th day: Earthquake with rain. The next day, another earthquake, and that night an earthquake with wind. 24th day, midnight: Earthquake. 4th year of Emperor Tenmu, 11th month: Great earth movement. Same reign, 6th year, 6th month, 14th day: Great earthquake. Same reign, 7th year, 12th month: Great earthquake in Tsukushi Province with ground fissures 2 jo wide and over 3,000 jo long. Many village houses collapsed. At this time, there was a peasant house on a hill that was shaken out and moved to another location, but the house remained intact. The occupants did not realize their house had moved location until they were greatly surprised the next morning. The above is from the Nihon Shoki. Same reign, 11th year, 8th month, 12th day: Great earth movement. 17th day: Another earthquake, from Ruiju Kokushi.

Same reign, 13th year, 10th month, 14th day, hour of the Boar: Great earthquake. Mountains collapsed, river water gushed forth, and the destruction of government buildings, private homes, and temples and shrines throughout the provinces was countless. Many casualties among people and livestock. Hot springs in Iyo were buried, and over 500,000 kei (1 kei equals 100 se, thus over 500,000 cho) of farmland in Tosa Province became sea. Same reign, 14th year, 12th month, 10th day: Earthquake originating from the west. The above is from the Nihon Shoki.

Emperor Monmu, 1st year of Taiho, 3rd month, 24th day: Earthquake in Tanba Province for three days. Empress Genmei, 1st year of Reiki, 5th month, 25th day: Earthquake in Totomi Province with mountain collapse; the Aratama River was blocked, and after several tens of days water flowed out, submerging over 170 villages in the three districts of Fuchi, Choge, and Ishida, damaging crops. The next day, 26th day: Earthquake in Mikawa Province; 47 official storehouses and private houses were destroyed and collapsed. Emperor Shomu, 6th year of Tempyo, 4th month, 7th day: Great earthquake. Houses throughout the provinces collapsed with many crushed to death. Mountains collapsed, rivers were blocked, and ground fissures were countless. Same year, 9th month, 24th day: Great earth tremor. Same reign, 16th year, 5th month...
