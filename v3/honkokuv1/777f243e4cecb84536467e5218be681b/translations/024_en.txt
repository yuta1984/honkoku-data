The temples within the mountain compound suffered damage without exception. Shiba Shinmei Shrine remained unharmed. Starting with Seishō-ji Temple at the foot of Atago Hill, other temples sustained damage, while Atago Shrine remained safe. Among the samurai residences, those of Lord Matsudaira Oki-no-kami, Lord Yonezu, Lord Sendai, Lord Akita, Lord Tamura, and others were damaged. Similarly, Kiridōshi and the Iikura district suffered damage, while Shinmei Shrine remained unharmed. The areas around Nagai district, Nishi-no-kubo intersection, Tentoku-ji temple gate, Fukide district, and Shin-shitaya district, as well as the residences of Lord Sengoku Oki-no-kami and Lord Matsudaira Ukon-shōgen, were damaged. The residence of Lord Matsudaira Yamato-no-kami on Shiomi slope was damaged, Lord Kyōgoku outside Toranomon gate was damaged, while Konpira Shrine suffered no problems. Kubo district and Bizen district areas suffered severe damage. The residences of Lords Hotta, Mōri, Hitotsuyagi, and Mizoguchi suffered severe damage. Kanefusa district experienced some fire damage. Outside Saiwaibashi gate, around Dobashi, and in Futaba district the damage was moderate, and Karasumori Inari shrine remained unharmed. 494 houses collapsed, 83 storehouses collapsed, and there were many deaths.

㊈From the first to fourth districts of Kanasugi-bashi south street, Hon-shiba district, Ta district, Takanawa, Sengaku-ji temple and other temples were damaged, and Shinagawa post station was also damaged. From the southern fourth district onward, there was severe damage. Tōkai-ji temple, Kaian-ji temple and others were damaged. The areas around Kokugin-ya district, Nihon-enoki, and Mita suffered damage, while Kasuga Myōjin shrine remained unharmed. Matsumoto district, Akabane, and Lord Arima's residence were damaged. In the Azabu area, Ryūdo, Lord Hi-ga-kubo's residence, Ta district, and Honmura district areas were damaged. Azabu Zenpuku-ji temple suffered moderate damage, while Imai-ya district, Ichibē district and others suffered severe damage. From there, the Aoyama area had many collapsed houses - 150 houses collapsed and 14 storehouses collapsed.

㊉The guard post inside Wada-kura gate burned down. The residences of Lord Matsudaira Higo-no-kami (Aizu), Lord Matsudaira Shimōsa-no-kami, Lord Naitō and others burned. Outside Baba-saki gate, the residences of Lord Endō and Lord Matsudaira Sagami-no-kami were half-destroyed, and the fire brigade quarters burned down. Inside Sukiya-bashi gate, there was extensive collapse. The daimyo residences that suffered fire damage included those of Lord Nagai Tōtōmi-no-kami and Lord Honda Nakatsukasa-no-tayū.
