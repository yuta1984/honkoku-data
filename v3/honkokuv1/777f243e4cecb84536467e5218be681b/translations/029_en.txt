The Chaya-cho area suffered no particular damage. The nine-ring spire (kurin) of Tennoji Temple's five-story pagoda broke and was buried three feet deep in the ground. The Nezu Miyanaga-cho area was completely destroyed both inside and outside the main gate. The Nippori and Tabata areas suffered damage. The Otowa area suffered minor damage. Goji-in Temple and Mejiro Fudo suffered no particular damage. 743 houses collapsed, along with 19 storehouses.

Fifteen [Ushigome Area]: The areas outside Ushigome Gate, Ageba, and Kagurazaka suffered major damage. Kaitai-cho and Sakana-cho areas were damaged. Ichigaya Tamachi and Honmura-cho areas suffered major damage. The Koji-cho area was damaged. Hirakawa Tenmangu Shrine suffered major damage. Areas outside Yotsuya Gate, Temma-cho, and Shio-cho were damaged. The Mannen water conduit of the aqueduct collapsed and was damaged. Many buildings collapsed around Samegahashi, and there were many collapses in Akasaka front and back, Temma-cho, and Ta-cho areas. 367 houses collapsed, along with 39 storehouses.

Sixteen [East of Ryogoku Bridge, Honjo Area]: Ryogoku Bridge East, Honjo Motomachi, in front of Ekoin Temple, and Tachikawa Aioi-cho suffered major damage. A fire broke out from Midori-cho 1-chome and burned to 2-chome. 3-chome only collapsed, while 4-chome, 5-chome, and Hana-cho burned slightly before the fire stopped. On the opposite riverbank, from Hayashi-cho 1-chome to 5-chome there were many collapses. Tokuemon-cho 1-chome and 2-chome burned. 3-chome had many collapses, and all three sections of Kikukawa-cho had many collapses. Around Ogi-bashi and Fukagawa Nishi-cho, about half a block burned. The Kayaba-cho area was damaged. The Irie-cho and Shimizu-cho areas completely collapsed. Yoshida-cho and Yoshioka-cho areas suffered major damage. Most of the samurai residences in this entire area collapsed extensively. 109 houses in 83 buildings collapsed, 79 storehouses collapsed, many deaths.

Seventeen [Fukagawa Area]: A fire broke out from in front of Ohashi East Fukagawa ship warehouse and spread toward Hachiman-gawa-cho. Hagami Gongen shrine burned. The ship warehouse guards also burned. Dainichi Yokocho, Sugano, Inoue, and Makino burned. Only the fire watchtower remained at Lord Kinoshita Tosho's residence. Half of the rice granary remained. Rokken-bori Shinmei Shrine was unharmed. Another fire started from Naka-Morishita and burned Minami-Morishita Moto-cho. Yet another fire broke out from around Tokiwa-cho and the Inoue residence burned slightly. From there, the fire spread from Tokiwa-cho 1-chome to 2-chome and stopped at the Furuta residence.
