Mount Asama (Asamagatake) is the foremost high mountain of Shinano Province, with its foothills extending into Agatsuma District of Kōzuke Province. At its summit, the deity Asama Gongen and ninety-eight thousand Buddhas have been enshrined, and their miraculous powers are especially manifest. The principal Buddhist deity (honji) is Fugen Daibosatsu (Samantabhadra), and upon standing at Nakayama one faces Asama directly; hence it received the name Asama. People of all social standings — noble and common alike — visit the mountain and pay their reverent respects. However, this mountain has an abundance of sulfurous vapors, and smoke rises ceaselessly in ordinary times, causing many visitors, young and old, to be frightened. Now, in what year did this occur? In the autumn of the third year of Tenmei, the year Mizunoto-U (1783), the entire mountain erupted in fire, and it is said that sand and rocks reached several hundred ri in distance. Tracing the very origins of this disaster, from the ninth day of the fourth month of summer, great fire erupted, growing more intense day by day, differing entirely from normal conditions. On the nights of the twenty-seventh and twenty-eighth days of the sixth month of the same year, ash fell upon the neighboring provinces, and elderly farmers with concerned hearts took note of this. Then again, on the nights of the second and third days of the seventh month of the same year, ash fell once more...
