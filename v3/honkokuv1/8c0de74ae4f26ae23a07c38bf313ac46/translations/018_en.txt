When the stomach is empty, there is a risk of rushes of blood to the head (noblesse) and dizziness, so one should eat a small amount of plain food, wait a short while, and then bathe. It is advisable to take one bath around two or three o'clock in the afternoon, and another bath around eight or nine o'clock in the evening before going to bed. Bathing is not appropriate after a full meal, when the stomach is empty, when one has a cold or fever, or during heavy rain and wind. Regarding the frequency of bathing: the elderly, children, and those with weakened constitutions should limit themselves to one or two times per day, up to a maximum of three times. Even those in good health should start with three times per day as the standard. After staying for about one week, four or five times per day is acceptable, but exceeding this will not only be ineffective but may actually cause harm. One should be cautious.

Depending on one's illness and constitution, drinking the mineral water is often beneficial. The best times to drink it are before breakfast and in the afternoon on an empty stomach. The appropriate temperature is below 88 degrees Fahrenheit (31 degrees Celsius). The appropriate dose is two ounces (56 sen) per serving, but one should begin with about one ounce and gradually increase the amount. However, it should be diluted with double the amount of plain water before drinking. Excessive consumption is highly inadvisable. If the taste is unpleasant, a small amount of good-quality sugar may be added. After drinking the mineral water, one must not eat or drink anything for at least half an hour. In general, if any unusual changes occur in the body, one should promptly consult the innkeeper, seek a physician's diagnosis, and confirm whether continued consumption is appropriate before resuming. Special care and attention should be paid in the cases of the elderly, children, and pregnant women.

Both external bathing and internal consumption of the mineral water are best done in the morning. If one follows this regimen, one must necessarily rise early. By doing so, good health is naturally maintained, and moreover, one will naturally retire to bed early at night.
