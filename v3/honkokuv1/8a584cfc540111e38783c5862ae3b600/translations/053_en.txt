Takada: In the Takada region of Echigo Province, the day had been cloudy since early morning, but at 2:31:12 PM the eclipse began from the lower right of the sun, gradually progressing until 3:15:40 when the eclipse was at its most severe. Just as it was about to reach totality, white clouds again covered the solar disk for several minutes. Totality began at 3:42:25 and ended at 3:44:45, meaning the duration of totality was 2 minutes and 20 seconds. However, this total eclipse was obscured by white clouds, so neither the corona nor the prominences could be clearly observed.

Osaka: In this region, the eclipse began from the lower right of the sun at 2:20 PM, and after 3:20 PM the eclipse became severe, with the sun retaining only a small portion. At 3:30:30 PM the sun was completely restored to a full circle. In this region, there had been extensive advance publicity, so when the eclipse began, most households lit lamps and many places also lit street lamps. However, since it did not become as dark as expected, some people even showed signs of disappointment.
