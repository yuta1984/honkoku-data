# The Second Year of Ansei (1855), Tenth Month, Second Day, at the Hour of the Pig (approximately 10 p.m.)

A great earthquake struck, and shaking began. Storehouses tilted, and houses collapsed in countless numbers. The number of deaths among men and women, young and old, was beyond reckoning. At this time, fierce fires blazed up in all directions, with flames so intense they seemed to scorch the very heavens. The number of fires that broke out was initially thirty-eight, but as nearby fires merged and continued to burn, the count became thirty-two, and then twenty-seven. The fires then gradually spread further, and it was not until the Hour of the Horse (around noon) on the following third day that they were completely extinguished. The number of people who lost their lives as a result of all this was immense — those within the city of Edo who perished in an instant numbered in the tens of thousands. It was truly an event without precedent in all of history.
