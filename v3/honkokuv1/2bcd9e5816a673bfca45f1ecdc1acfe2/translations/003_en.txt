On the night of the 2nd day of the 10th month of Ansei 2 (1855), year of the Rabbit, at the fourth hour of the night, a great earthquake occurred. Many storehouses tilted and houses collapsed in great numbers, with countless deaths among men and women, young and old. At this time, fierce flames erupted from all directions, with fire scorching the heavens. Initially there were thirty-eight points of fire outbreak, but as nearby areas continued burning, this became thirty-two points, then twenty-seven points. The fire gradually spread wider, and was completely extinguished at the hour of the Horse (around noon) on the following 3rd day. Furthermore, this caused tremendous loss of human life, with tens of thousands of people in Edo losing their lives in an instant - truly an unprecedented disaster.
