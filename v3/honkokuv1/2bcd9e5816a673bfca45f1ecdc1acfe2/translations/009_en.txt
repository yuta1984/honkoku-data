【Upper Section】
○At Kinryuzan Sensoji Temple, the main hall, Nembutsu hall, sutra hall, bell tower, mountain gate, and Zuishin gate remained intact. The thunder god statue at the Thunder Gate fell over, the nine-ring finial of the five-story pagoda was bent, and various halls within the temple grounds were severely damaged. The main gate, entrance, and study of Denboin Temple were undamaged, but the kitchen quarters and other buildings were severely damaged. The temples within the precinct, townhouses in the temple quarters, and tenement houses were all completely destroyed, with many casualties.

○The principal image of Kannon Bodhisattva at Sensoji Temple was moved to the flower garden within the temple grounds.
　However, when they tried to move the bodhisattva back to the main hall after the earthquake had passed, it was too heavy to lift up. This must surely indicate that there will be a major aftershock, so they decided to leave it in the garden for about seven or eight days.

【Lower Section】
○A fire broke out around Asakusa Tamachi, with some areas below the embankment remaining partially burned. Areas that burned included: Yanaka Tennoji gate-front town, Daichi, Yamakawa-cho, both sides of Takemon-dori, Sodesuri Inari, Umamichi-dori, Kitashin-machi, and about twenty houses eastward from Henshoin Temple. Koto-no-yado and all three theaters in Saruwaka-cho were completely destroyed, along with dressing rooms, Shindo, Yabu-no-uchi, Sensoji temple sub-temple grounds, Iroha tenements, and Ioin temple grounds. The fire stopped on both sides near Zuishin gate. Minami-shindo burned to the corner, and in Hanakawado, the fire stopped in the middle of Tozawa tenements.
