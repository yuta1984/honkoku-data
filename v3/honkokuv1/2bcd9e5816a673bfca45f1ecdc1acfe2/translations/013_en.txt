○The shrine of Washi Daimyojin collapsed, and Ryusenji Temple, Daionji Temple, and Chokokji Temple were severely damaged.

○At the same location, at Saitokuji Temple of the Bukko-ji sect of Jodo Shinshu Buddhism, the main hall, kitchen quarters, and front gate collapsed, but the Taishi Hall remained intact.

○The Nakamura area of Senju Kozukahara-cho was burned.

○At Hashiba, Sozenji Temple, Tamahime Inari Shrine, Masaki Inari Shrine, and Shinmei Shrine were severely damaged. In the same area, the lower residence of Lord Sakai Utanokami, and the temples Choshoji, Hogenji, Honsho-ji, and Myoonji at Asajigahara were all severely damaged.

○A fire broke out in Imado-cho, and the mint was partially burned. The restaurant and teahouse Kawaguchi in the same area was severely damaged, as were Hachiman Shrine and other temples there. About half of Imado-cho was burned.

○The main hall of Matsuchiyama Shoten remained intact, but the temple grounds were severely damaged. Saiho-ji Temple and Bato Kannon on the embankment were severely damaged, and temples and townhouses in Shoten-cho, Shin-Torigoe, and San'ya-cho were all severely damaged.

○Hanakawado was severely damaged, but Azuma Bridge remained intact. Zaimoku-cho, Namiki-cho, Chaya-cho, and Komagata Hall were all severely damaged.

○The Rokugō residence was severely damaged and its front gate collapsed.
