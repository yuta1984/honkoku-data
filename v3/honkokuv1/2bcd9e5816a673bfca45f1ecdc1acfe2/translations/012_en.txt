○In the five districts of Shin-Yoshiwara, as soon as the earthquake rumbled, the entire pleasure quarter was shaken and collapsed, with flames blazing fiercely from all directions, turning the whole district into a conflagration. Therefore, there was no time to lower the drawbridges at the back alleys, and even when someone happened to try to lower them, the drawbridges were damaged and could not be crossed. Since only the Ōmon (Great Gate) served as the exit, people were engulfed in smoke, burned by fire, crushed by houses, or trapped by beams they could not avoid. Even those fortunate enough to survive would die by burning while helplessly waiting for the fire to reach them, if there was no one to rescue them by breaking through roofs and walls. Under such circumstances, the wounded and dead were extremely numerous. For four or five days afterward, corpses still lay scattered here and there, with people searching for parents and children - truly a sight too horrible to behold. The courtesans who died numbered 831, customers and others who had come to this place numbered over 450, and teahouse operators, various merchants, and entertainers in the quarter totaled over 1,400 - a grand total of over 2,700 deaths. Not a single storehouse remained, with only a small portion of the western embankment surviving and about fifty buildings remaining on one side.
