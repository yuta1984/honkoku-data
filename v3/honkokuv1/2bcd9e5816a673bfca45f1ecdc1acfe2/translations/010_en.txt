【Upper Section】
○At Tōeizan Kan'ei-ji Temple (Higashi Monzeki), the main hall remained intact, but about three temples within the grounds had their main halls collapse. Both the east and north gates fell down. The waiting rooms and temporary guard posts were severely damaged.

○At the same location, Sōfuku-ji, Daishō-ji, and Seikō-ji temples were all severely damaged. Many houses collapsed along Tawaramachi, Nakachō, and Morishitamachi streets.

○At Seigan-ji Temple, the main hall remained intact, but several temples within the grounds were severely damaged, and many houses in the temple's front area collapsed.

○At Nichirin-ji Temple, the main hall remained intact, but the dormitories, kitchen quarters, temples within the grounds, and the temple town suffered major collapses.

○At Kōryū-ji Temple, the main hall remained intact, but the kitchen quarters and temples within the grounds were severely damaged.

【Lower Section】
○Starting from the corner in front of a restaurant called Hatsufuji in Asakusa Komagatachō, both sides of the street burned down to Suwachō, Kurofunechō, and Kaya-dera Temple. To the east, the fire reached Oumaya riverbank and Miyoshi-chō, and no storehouses remained intact in this area.

○Okuramae Street had no particular major damage, though all the tile roofs of the storehouses were damaged.

○At Asakusa Gate, the stone walls bulged out slightly.

○The area around the residence of Lord Sakai Saemon-no-jō in Fukui-chō and the Kanda River vicinity were all severely damaged.
