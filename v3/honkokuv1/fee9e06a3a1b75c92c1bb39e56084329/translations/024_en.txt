【Text】

From Mount Asama in Shinano Province, approximately 7 to 8 ri (roughly 28–32 km) to the southeast, an eruption occurred from Mount Asama toward Mount Agatsuma (written as "Akatsuma-yama"), spewing mudflows and volcanic rocks. [Text damaged by worm-holes] Eruptions also reached Mount Agatsuma.

(Map annotation) Eruptions occurred from this location.

The Karuizawa post town was completely burned down. As for the post towns of Kutsukake and Oiwake, since the roads remain impassable even now, it is unknown what became of them.

In Kōzuke Province, Gunma District, under the jurisdiction of the magistrate (daikan) Harada Seizaemon of Komaki Village — the three villages of Minamimaki Village, Kawashima Village [and Komaki Village] in the same district were all abandoned (taiten). Seven houses remained in Minamimaki Village, but the total number of houses across the three villages amounted to approximately 600, representing an enormous disaster.

According to the report submitted by the magistrate of this river (the Agatsuma River), Harada Seizaemon, the eruptions occurred with thunderous roaring during the 6th and 7th days of the 7th month.

(Map annotation) This area contains a ford and the Mikuni Road, continuing toward Echigo Province.

This map is a copy of a diagram attached to the inspection report submitted by a retainer of Lord Matsudaira Awaji-no-kami, who was dispatched on the 7th day of the 7th month to conduct an on-site inspection.

(Map annotation) In this area, people and horses were reportedly swept away one after another.

At a location approximately 7 ri (roughly 28 km) downstream from the base of Mount Asama, sand had accumulated on boats at a rate of approximately 2 to 5 shō (roughly 45 liters) per tsubo (approximately 3.3 square meters).
