## Record of the Eruption of Mt. Asama

Since ancient times, it has been said that the years of Hinoe-Uma (丙午) and Hinoto-Hitsuji (丁未) in the sexagenary cycle invariably bring disasters. There is also the concept of "Hyakuyōkyū" (百陽九), which holds that after every hundred years, disaster will certainly occur. However, it is also said by tradition that such disasters can strike before a full hundred years, within fifty or sixty years.

Regarding the saying that "disasters occur in the years of Hinoe-Uma and Hinoto-Hitsuji," the *Suikenroku* states: "When China encounters the years of Hinoe-Uma and Hinoto-Hitsuji, disaster will certainly occur. Those of rank along the main roads should take Hinoe-Uma and Hinoto-Hitsuji as a mirror and warning. From the fifty-second year of King Zhaoxiang of Qin through the Five Dynasties, there were approximately twenty-two instances of unrest in certain years—so thinks Wenbao. Hinoe-Uma and Hinoto-Hitsuji are positioned at the center of heaven; Hei (丙) and Tei (丁) belong to the element of Fire and are both situated at the position of 'Uma' (午)." And so forth.

The *Suikenroku* (吹剣録) is a collection of essays by Yu Wenbao (兪文豹) of the Song dynasty.
