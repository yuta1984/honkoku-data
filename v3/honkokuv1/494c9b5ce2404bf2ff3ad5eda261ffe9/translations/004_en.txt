In Hei County, 3 towns and 19 villages suffered damage to 2,783 households, with over 13,000 deaths. In Kunohe County, there were 2 town areas and 5 other villages, with approximately 360 deaths. In the following Aomori Prefecture, over 1,000 houses were swept away, and the investigation of casualties has not yet been completed. Moreover, countless bodies were washed up on the sandy shores and piled up here and there. The endless smoke from cremations disappearing into the unknown, and even the sound of white waves crashing was terrifyingly ominous - truly a pitiable sight.

Great Tsunami Ballad (Part 2)
Here is one miraculous story. In a small village called Miyato in the coastal area of Kunohe County, there lived a fisherman named Nisuke. Though he lived in poverty with his wife and four children, he was always an honest man who, together with his wife, devoted himself to caring for his elderly mother. His filial piety became known to the authorities, and he received a reward from the prefectural office, continuing to live his life admirably. This year his son turned nine, and on that day Nisuke had gone out fishing at sea.
