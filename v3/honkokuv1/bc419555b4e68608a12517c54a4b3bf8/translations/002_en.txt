During the reign of Kōrei, the seventh human Emperor, in the year of Kanoto-Hitsuji (辛未), the earth of Ōmi Province split open, and overnight became a lake (Lake Biwa), while simultaneously Mount Fuji is said to have arisen. Beyond this, there are countless other heavenly wonders and mysterious occurrences. Regarding Mount Asama in Shinano Province: in the ninth year of Empress Jitō's reign, the year of Hinoe-Saru (丙申), the ascetic En no Gyōja climbed this mountain. In the mountains to the northeast there was a spring called Yanai, where a black serpent dwelt at its base, spitting poisonous water. The ascetic found this strange and made a vow of prayer to the principal deity, using a sharp sword to free (the serpent's) legs. From there he reached the peak, leveled the rocks, built a hermitage for himself, and performed his austerities. Demons came and knelt before him, and Fukuman Kokūzō Bodhisattva manifested day and night. To the northwest lies the Valley of Mugen (Endless Suffering), to the south the Peak of Swords, to the north the Demon's Cave, and to the east Tōmi-zaka (Far-View Slope). Kinkiganzan (Golden Turtle Rock Mountain) is a mountain of divine transformation and primal darkness, with an Outer Shore and an Inner Shore.
After this, for a long time there were no visitors to climb the mountain, until the ninth day of the sixth month of the fourth year of Kōan (1281)...
