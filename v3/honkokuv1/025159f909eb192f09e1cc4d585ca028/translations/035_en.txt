This strait is called the Le Maire Strait ("Maleum" Strait).

From there, the expedition entered the Great Pacific Ocean, passed through the Maluku (Moluccas) Islands ("Makoji"), sailed far to the west around the Cape of Good Hope, and then turned northward, arriving back at their home port of Seville ("Nishimiria") on September 7, 1520 (the 2nd year of Japan's Daiei era, the 1st year of the Ming Jiajing era). The total distance of the voyage was approximately 1,124 days, and this was the first circumnavigation of the entire globe. For this reason, the ship that had carried them was given the honorable name "Victoria" ("Hikutoria"), meaning "victory" — the intent being to commemorate the great achievement of battling through the waves of ten thousand leagues and successfully circumnavigating the world. It is said that this ship is still treasured and preserved in Spain to this day.

In 1533 (the 2nd year of Japan's Tenbun era, the 12th year of the Ming Jiajing era), Spaniards arrived at the country of Peru, located on the western coast of South America.

This country was vast in territory and abundant in produce, yielding great quantities of gold, silver, mercury, cinnabar, emeralds, various precious stones, bezoar stones ("sata"), sugar, cotton, various grains, and various fruits. It had an independent great king, called "Inca" ("Inkasu"), a title equivalent to "Emperor." The capital was established at Cusco ("Koshiko"), and the nation's power was at its height. The palace in which the king resided was entirely adorned with gold and silver, achieving the utmost splendor. There were also temples, and the priests of these temples were called "Charas" ("Shiyarasu"). These temples were likewise decorated with gold and silver; inside were enshrined enormous, strangely shaped divine images made of gold, declared to be the true likeness of the sun. Beside these, numerous other divine images made of gold and silver were arranged in rows. There were rituals of worship, and on occasion humans were used as sacrificial offerings.
