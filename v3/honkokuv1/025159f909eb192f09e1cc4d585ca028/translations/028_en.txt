These two islands were discovered. "Porto Santo" lies to the east, measuring five ri in length (ten Japanese ri) and one ri in width (two Japanese ri). "Madeira" lies to the west, measuring sixteen ri in length (thirty-two Japanese ri) and six ri in width (twelve Japanese ri). As both islands are extremely fertile, the land was developed and governors were appointed from the home country. The wine now produced in Madeira is of the finest quality.

Shortly thereafter, the people of Hispania (Spain) opened up the twelve "Canary" Islands to the south of these two islands and took possession of them. Among these twelve islands, the seven largest are all extremely fertile, producing great quantities of wine and sugar, and are renowned throughout the world.

In 1440 (the twelfth year of Eikyō in Japan, the fifth year of Zhengtong in Ming China), the Portuguese reached the White Cape and "Arguin" Island on the western coast of Africa (this territory now belongs to the Dutch). From this point onward, they conducted trade with places in Guinea such as the "Grain Coast" (where "Grain" means grain and "Coast" means shoreline; named so because grain is found in great abundance), the "Gold Coast" (named because gold is most plentiful there), and the "Ivory Coast" (named because the region produces ivory), among other places.

After this, the Dutch, the English [Anglians], and the Danes also crossed to these lands, establishing their own trading posts and conducting commerce with the native peoples. In 1446 (the third year of Bunan in Japan, the eleventh year of Zhengtong in Ming China), the Portuguese also reached the Green Cape (Cape Verde) on the Guinea coast and proceeded to Sierra Leone. Since this area produces a great deal of silver, they obtained much of it through trade with the native peoples.

They also spread their teachings, and the inhabitants of the country came to follow their religion. In the sea off this Green Cape (Cape Verde), there are ten islands of various sizes, producing much grain and fruit, and inhabited by many kinds of animals. The Portuguese took possession of all of these islands, and built an administrative center on the island called "Santo Yago" (Santiago), appointing a governor there.
