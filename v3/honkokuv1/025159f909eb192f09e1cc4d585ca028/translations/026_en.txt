The abundance of goods obtained through trade was truly beyond human imagination. As a result, the wealth of Judea (如徳亜) was unmatched in the world, and the city of Jerusalem was filled to the brim with gold and silver. Even to this day in the Western world, when praising extraordinarily rare wealth, King Solomon (撒刺満) is invariably cited as the prime example. However, it seems that the records made at the time were inadequate, and it is impossible to ascertain precisely which present-day countries correspond to the land of "Ophir" and the other nations with which trade was conducted. It is thought that ships of that era used only the two constellations of Ursa Major and Ursa Minor as their guide [Ursa Major encompasses the stars of Wenchang, Santai, and the Big Dipper, with its tail ending at the star Pojun (破軍星); Ursa Minor lies beside it, with its tail ending at the North Star], and since they lacked the astronomical instruments available today, they were unable to clearly record directions, locations, geography, sea routes, and so forth. Only the name "Ophir" has been passed down to us. [Regarding the country of Ophir: some say it is the present-day Sumatra; others suggest it may be in the region of Bengal, Bigu, Siam, or the islands of Java and Sumatra in the eastern Indies. Yet another view holds that Ophir is in fact Sofala, on the eastern coast of present-day Africa. Furthermore, it is said that during the time of Solomon, his ships went not only to the present-day Sofala, but from there sailed further southwest, rounding the present-day Cape of Good Hope, then northward to reach Tarshish in Europe [Tarshish being present-day Spain], and then southward and westward, passing through the present-day Strait of Gibraltar to reach the land of Peru in South America for trade. However, a geographical work by a man called "Nicolas Duin" discusses this matter and notes that it is still difficult to believe. Upon reflection, if it were truly confirmed that voyages were made at that time to the Cape of Good Hope, the Strait of Magellan, Peru, and similar places, there should be at least some trace of this in the maps of Ptolemy (フトロメウス) and others.
