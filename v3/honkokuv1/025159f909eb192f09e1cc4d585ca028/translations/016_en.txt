**First Entry**

"Zee Monster"
　(Meaning: a monster of the sea)

[Illustration present]

This monster, at the time, had an appearance very much like that of a "Monniki" (a title for a monastic official), while the rest of its body was entirely like a fish, covered with scales and a shell. According to a book on fish written by a person called "Rondeletius," this creature was observed in the sea as both male and female, and was promptly captured. The people of that time all called it "Zee Monniki." ("Zee" means sea; "Monniki" means monk — that is, it carries the meaning of "sea monk.")
