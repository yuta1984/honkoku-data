(Crispus) assisted the emperor with great merit, and all those below him submitted to his authority. However, the later empress "Fausta" fell in love with the handsome appearance of Crispus and repeatedly sought to seduce him. Crispus firmly refused her, whereupon the empress gradually became both angry and fearful. She therefore repeatedly slandered him before the emperor, saying: "Crispus is always trying to seduce and toy with me." The emperor believed this, exiled Crispus to the land of "Istria," and ordered him to die. The emperor's mother, "Helena," who had long been renowned for her wisdom and virtue, and who had always loved Crispus, grieved deeply and suspected that he had been wrongfully condemned. She secretly interrogated the empress's personal attendants and, as she had suspected, learned the truth of the matter. She then reported this to the emperor, who was overwhelmed with remorse and grief. He immediately deposed the empress and had her killed. He himself then passed away. (He reigned for thirty-one years, lived to the age of sixty-five, in the Western year 337—corresponding to the twenty-fifth year of Emperor Nintoku of our country, and the second year of the Xiankang era [Dingyu year] of Emperor Cheng of the Jin dynasty.)

The three sons born of the later empress inherited the imperial work and divided governance over Europe and the eastern countries among themselves; however, none of them was wise, and none met a good end. (Among these three sons: the eldest, "Constantius," governed Egypt and the countries of Asia; the second, "Constantine II," governed the countries of Gaul, Spain, and England, as well as half of Germania; and the youngest, "Constans," governed Italy, Africa, Egypt, and other countries. Three years later, a conflict arose with "Constans," they went to war, and "Constantine II" killed him and declared himself emperor. "Constantius," upon hearing this, raised a great army from the east and attacked him. He ultimately won the battle, capturing "Magnentius" in the city of Lyon in Gaul, crucifying him, and unifying the various countries. Nevertheless, from this point "Constantius" became increasingly arrogant, and his violent cruelty was beyond compare. Because of this, the people of the country grew resentful and rose in rebellion, deposing and killing "Constantius" and installing "Julianus" as emperor. This was in the Western year 354, the forty-second year of Emperor Nintoku of our country, and the tenth year of the Yonghe era [Jiayín year] of Emperor Mu of the Jin dynasty.)

A man named "Julianus," nephew of Emperor "Constantinus Magnus," succeeded to the imperial throne.
