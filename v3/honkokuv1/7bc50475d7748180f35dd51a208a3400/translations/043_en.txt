Kishu Great Earthquake
In Yuasa, a great earthquake began at the fifth hour of the morning on November 4th, causing houses to collapse in various places. Another great earthquake occurred from the seventh hour on the 5th, and from evening a great tsunami caused many ships to be washed ashore. In Kitamachi, Shinyashiki, Hamamachi, Nakamachi, and Daishoji, houses were swept away. All houses along the riverbank were swept away, with approximately 300 deaths and countless injuries. In Hiroura, the great earthquake at the same date and time caused four-tenths of the thousand houses to collapse, and the great tsunami on the 5th swept away all but three remaining houses, with approximately 400 deaths and countless injuries. In Tanabe, simultaneous great earthquakes and tsunamis caused three-tenths to collapse and be swept away, while the remaining seven-tenths were completely destroyed by fire, resulting in approximately 350 deaths and countless injuries. In Arida, Kamo, Osaki, Fujishiro, Nadaka, Hikata, Kuroe, Kada, and Kumano, all experienced great tsunami disasters with more than six-tenths of houses swept away. From Fujishiro to Kuroe, the main road became like a white sea. Wakayama castle town also suffered from the great earthquake and tsunami, with various districts being swept away and collapsed. In addition, countless large and small ships were washed ashore in various bays, with some deaths and many injuries.
