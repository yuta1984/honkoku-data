## Explanation of the "Lunar Lodge Month Value" (Shuku Chigetsuno Kai)

This refers to the "lodge" (shuku, one of the twenty-eight lunar mansions) that governs the auspiciousness or inauspiciousness of each month. For example, in the column for the First Month of Tenpō 5 (1834), it is written: "First Month, small month, construction stem Hinoe-Tora [C3], Mansion: Shǐ (參宿/Orion), Value Month." For the Second Month, it is abbreviated simply as: "Large month, construction stem Hinoto-U [D4], Mansion: Jǐng (井宿/Gemini)." This too is a record made for the purpose of divining the auspiciousness or inauspiciousness of each month, by assigning each of the twenty-eight lunar mansions, one by one, across the twelve months.

## Illustrated Explanation of Earthquakes

In general, the earth is formed from the dregs (residue) of primordial qi (the fundamental vital force of all things), which gather together to form substance and shape. Bound at the center of heaven, it floats solemnly in the sky without falling, and is settled and fixed along the axis running through the North and South Poles. Someone has said: "Heaven is still and does not move; it is the earth that moves and rotates." This is like riding on a boat — the boat is moving, yet one does not feel it moving; instead, looking at the shore, it appears as though the shore itself is flowing past. What appears to be the motion of heaven, and what appears to be the motion of the earth — both are explained by this principle.
