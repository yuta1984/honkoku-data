Castle town (The town is long. Upon leaving town there is a river, which is upstream of the Katsura River. Cross the earthen bridge to Kawara-machi. Going right leads to the Tajima road) ○ Kizaki Village (Asane Forest is on the left, with a road through the rice fields)
○ Fudaraku Valley Kannon Hall (Called Ide Kannon. It is a natural stone. Also serves as a rest stop)
This is the entrance to Mitono Pass, an outlying village of Kizaki Village.
▲ Mitono Pass (One ri from Sonobe)
This is said to be where Sanshō Dayū established a checkpoint
○ Shin-Mito Village ○ Furu-Mito Village
▲ Shūchi Village (One ri from Mitono Pass)
○ Hanaoka Mountain (The azaleas on this mountain are truly magnificent. The foothills are called "Fukashino." In a poem by Ikkyū:)
"At Hanaoka, coming to see in the deep of night, the hem of my robe drips with dew." There is a place called Shioda Village in this area.
○ Sone Village ○ Innai Village

▲ Hinoki Mountain Village (30 chō from Shinden)
▲ Leaving this village and going left leads to the Fukuchiyama road.
Going straight leads to worship at Ōhara Shrine (worship at this shrine is called "Ohara-mairi")
There is a route called Ayabe-goe that leads to the Haze River.
(In Ayabe there is a jinya of the Kuki clan worth 20,000 koku) Going around to Ōhara, it is seven and a half ri from this Hinokiyama to the Haze River. Even going directly to the Haze River without going around to Ōhara is six and a half ri. Since it's only a one-ri detour, one should worship at Ōhara Shrine.
