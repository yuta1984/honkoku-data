It is about 2 ri (8 km) to the Tajima road. Go right from the earthen bridge at Gochaku. 20 chō to Komura village, 25 chō from Komura to Sosaka, 1 ri from Sosaka to Daiō (there is a long field called Saikōji-no between here with no rest stops or food), 2 ri from Daiō to Tsuchikawa, 7 chō from Tsuchikawa to Yunokuchi, 50 chō from Yunokuchi to Kawabe, and 50 chō from Kawabe to Yakata (details to follow).

Ushidōzan Kokubun-ji Temple (northwest of Gochaku) is of the Shingon sect with temple lands of 30 koku. North of the temple are mounds called Ushi-zuka and Ushikai-zuka.
Yamawaki village, Asao-yama (a famous place).
Ichinoki village, Ichikawa River (ferry crossing in summer, bridge in winter; dangerous during high water). Kamiya village, Kōfu-ji village.

Himeji (1 ri from Gochaku)
This is the castle town of Lord Sakai Utanokamī (150,000 koku).
Sōsha Shrine (called Iwa Daimyōjin, enshrining Ōnamuchi-no-mikoto, with shrine lands of 151 koku).
Nine shrine families, one shrine monk. Every 21 years they construct an elaborate mountain for religious ceremonies. Annual festivals on July 13th and 15th feature the Hōden dance. Kameyama Hontoku-ji Temple (Western sect), Senba Hontoku-ji Temple (Eastern sect).
Kazari-man no sato (the coastal area south of town), Nozato village (the exit town of Himeji; going straight leads to the Tajima road).

From this Nozato, go left and cross Itasaka Pass to reach Masuisan Zuigan-ji Temple (temple lands 280 koku, Tendai sect, principal deity Yakushi, founded by Prince Shōtoku), Hiromine Shrine (shrine lands 72 koku 8 to, 25 sub-temples, enshrining Susanoo-no-mikoto, Inada-hime, and Hachiōji - said to be the original location of Kyoto's Gion Shrine), and Hakuhei-yama (Hiromine).
