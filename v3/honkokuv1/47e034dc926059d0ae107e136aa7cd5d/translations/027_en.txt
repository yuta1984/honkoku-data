People from near and far come to this place to worship and pay their respects. Furthermore, in this place there is a magnificent great temple, called a "Mosque" (meaning "the True Holy Place"). Its structure is square in shape, built of black stone, with the gates and open areas all finished in white plaster. Along the outer face runs a long colonnade, with windows and pillars all made of marble. Inside hang a great many lanterns, all very large, their heights reaching eight or nine feet, or even over ten feet in some cases. On the outer face there are six great towers, among which the tower called the "Minaret" is said to be the tallest.

The Qur'an (Al-Qoran) contains a great many fantastical and absurd passages, too numerous to count. Regarding Heaven and Hell, it states that those who are born into Heaven shall enjoy pleasures without end for all eternity, with no restraints whatsoever, supplied with all manner of delicious and fragrant food and drink, bathed in milk and waters of beautiful flowers, and housed in palaces constructed of coral, bright pearls, beautiful jade, and a hundred treasures. Those who fall into Hell shall suffer a thousand pains and ten thousand agonies of being boiled and cut asunder every single day, dying and dying again, without end for all eternity. Everything else described is of a similar nature. For these reasons, the people of the West have universally referred to Muhammad as a "False Prophet" (Valse Profeet). This means "false holy man."
