Western Miscellany Notes

Recorded by: Shō Tōyō, pen name Muyū Dōjin

An Account of the Kingdom of Siam

The ancient origins of the Kingdom of Siam (Shamu) are not clearly known. Works such as the "Dongxiyang Kao" (Investigations of the Eastern and Western Oceans) and the "Kunyu Quantu" (Complete Map of the World) all identify it as the territory of the ancient kingdom of Chitu (Red Earth Land). The "Da Ming Tongzhi" (Comprehensive Gazetteer of the Great Ming) and the "Xu Wenxian Tongkao" (Continuation of the Comprehensive Study of Literary Remains) state that this was anciently the territory of two kingdoms—Xian and Luhu—and that Xian was descended from the remnants of the Red Eyebrow rebels of the Han dynasty, who later merged to form a single kingdom. The "Saiран Igen" (Strange Words from Various Lands) suggests it may have been the kingdom of Pyu (Biao) during the Tang dynasty. It is not yet known which of these accounts is correct. At present, this kingdom is the largest among the nations of the southern regions. The land is divided into eleven provinces (dō), and its neighboring countries, including Cambodia (Kanbocha) [note: the ancient kingdom of Chenla] and Malacca (Marakka), are all subject to it.
