The one called Cang Jie (蒼頡) wrote his script vertically, and so forth. Regarding what is called "Huihui writing" (Islamic/Arabic script), the various books of the Ming dynasty all state: "Medina (黙徳那) is the ancestral homeland of the Huihui (Muslims). Originally, the king of that country, Muhammad (謨罕驀徳), was born with divine spirit, and with great virtue brought the various nations of the Western Regions into submission. Their scriptures number more than 3,600 volumes, and their script runs horizontally, with three styles: seal, cursive, and regular script. All nations of the Western World transmit and use this script." In the current regions of India, the various countries such as Mughal (莫臥児), Bengal and Siam (榜葛刺暹羅), Java (瓜哇), and Malaya (満刺加), and others including Persia (百児西亜), Arabia (亜刺比亜), Syria (西利牙), and Judea/Israel (如徳亜), all have different scripts. Among these, the "Hebrew" script is the oldest (as noted above). The scripts used in Europe include "Romense (Roman)", "Hochdeutsch (High German)", "Türk (Turkish)", "Scheijf (?)", "Teletski (?)", and others—from what I have personally seen, approximately twenty varieties in total. In addition, there are more than twenty symbolic characters used for quantities, astronomy, calendrical calculations, medicine, and similar fields. The scripts used in the various countries are generally the same, but there are cases where the sounds of the characters differ greatly from country to country. The script of the "Greek" nation is particularly distinctive in form, with very dense diacritical marks used to distinguish the various sounds. The script of "Muscovy (Moscow/Russia)" is also different from others. Some say it was originally made by combining the scripts of "Greece" and "Savoy (?)". As for the continent of Africa, the written culture of the world is extremely flourishing there, and there are likely few countries without writing. Only the great continent of America had absolutely no writing from ancient times; it is said that only in recent times, after coming under the influence of European teachings, did they come to know of writing.
