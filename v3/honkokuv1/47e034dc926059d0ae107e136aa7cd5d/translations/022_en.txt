In the West, portraits of ancient figures have been handed down in great numbers, and at the beginning of Western history books, several hundred portraits of notable individuals dating back two to three thousand years are illustrated. Among these is a portrait of the Roman Emperor Constantine II. The brief biography beneath it states: "The coins minted during the reign of this emperor still exist in the world today, and since the emperor's face appears on the coinage, it has been copied here." From this, one can understand that the other portraits too are all faithful likenesses based on actual evidence, and were by no means drawn from imagination.

　　　　On the Subject of Pointed High Platforms (Pyramids)

In the country of Egypt, there are several pointed high platforms (pyramids) carved from stone. The three largest of these each stand over fifty jō in height, with a base breadth of over sixty jō, and are said to be visible from a distance of sixty ri away. Furthermore, all of them served as royal tombs from ancient times, and were constructed to demonstrate the wealth of the nation. These too are counted among the Seven Wonders of the World. The local people, even to this day, regard them as ornaments of their country, and since they periodically carry out repairs and restorations, the structures have survived to the present day.

In general, Egypt was extraordinarily wealthy and prosperous in ancient times; the founding of the nation was perhaps second only to Arabia, and astronomy and medicine both originated in this northern land (Egypt) before being transmitted to various other countries. At the beginning of Heister's surgical text, there is an account of the origins of surgery, which records that it was transmitted from this country.
