Picture of the Aftermath of the Great Earthquake on October 28, Meiji 24

October 28, Meiji 24 (1891)
The earthquake that occurred after 6 o'clock in the morning
particularly caused severe shaking in the
Gifu, Nagoya, and Ogaki regions.
There were tremendous landslides, houses
completely collapsed, and the number of dead
may reach tens of thousands.
Separated from parents, separated from spouses,
only 2- or 3-year-old children remain alone.
The sight of them clinging to their dead
parents, crying and screaming, was
too pitiful to watch.
Rescue operations were immediately
established in various places, and
medical treatment was provided to the injured.
This can truly be called
a great earthquake rare in all of history.
