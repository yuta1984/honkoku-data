[Figure: A red circle about four characters in diameter with flame-like black patterns inside, and saw-tooth patterns around the rim]

Mars (Keiwaku-sei, the star of Tuesday) has edges like saw teeth, similar to the Sun, and has something thin and dark in the center like a lamp wick. When it appears and disappears, the saw-tooth-like features glitter and appear large.

Mars completes one revolution around the celestial sphere from west to east in 1 year, 321 days, and 90 ke (ancient time units). It moves approximately one degree every two days. This is also according to the calendrical scholars' theory of rightward rotation.

[Figure: A large circle about four characters in diameter]

Saturn (Chin-sei, the star of Saturday) is pale yellow in color, with a blue ring in the center. Day by day and moment by moment, it appears vertical and horizontal, then diagonal, or sometimes forms golden rings like this [triple circle diagram]. Outside, there are two small wandering stars that orbit around the large star, but there are stellar eclipses when they are blocked by the Sun.

Saturn completes one revolution around the celestial sphere from west to east in 29 years, 155 days, and 70 ke. It moves approximately one degree every thirty days. This is also according to the calendrical scholars' theory of rightward rotation.
