When you climb a mountain, the pole star (North Star) appears closer to the horizon. If you travel about thirty ri to the north, the North Star appears one degree higher; if you travel three hundred ri, it appears ten degrees higher. Japan is located about thirty to forty degrees from the North Pole. From there, if you travel over 1,700 ri to the north, you would see the North Pole at the zenith. The same applies when traveling toward the South Pole.
