#### Plateaus of North America

Along the eastern side of the Rocky Mountains, plateaus extend for approximately forty *ri*. Their elevation ranges from 2,000 to 3,000 feet. The Utah Plateau, situated between the Rocky Mountains and the Sierra Nevada, reaches an elevation of 4,000 to 5,000 feet. The great plateau of Mexico extends from latitude 42° north to the Isthmus of Tehuantepec, reaching an elevation of 4,000 to 5,000 feet. The plains of Guatemala and Honduras in Central America extend to the southern part of the Mexican Plateau, reportedly reaching an elevation of 6,000 feet.

#### Plateaus of South America

Among the plateaus of the world, the highest are found on this continent. The Quito Plateau straddles both north and south of the equator, reaching an elevation of 9,000 feet. The Pasco Plateau, situated between 10° and 12° south latitude, lies at an elevation of over 11,000 feet.
