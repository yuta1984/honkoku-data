**Plateaus of Australia**
This continent has two plateaus: one is located on the southern coast, and the other on the northern coast.

**Chapter 5: Lowlands (Low Plains)**

Flat land that is only slightly higher than sea level is called a lowland (tei-gen). In some places, the land lies below sea level. The land surrounding the Caspian Sea and the Aral Sea sits 83 feet below sea level. The land near the Dead Sea is said to lie 1,317 feet below sea level. The valley of the Jordan River is likewise situated below sea level. ○ Even what are called plains are not always perfectly flat; many have undulating, wave-like surfaces.

**Lowlands of Asia**
The entirety of Siberia, stretching from the Altai Mountains to the Atlantic Ocean, forms one great plain, which gradually descends as it approaches the ocean, becoming marshy wetlands.
