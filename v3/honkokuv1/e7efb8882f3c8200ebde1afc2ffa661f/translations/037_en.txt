Between the Himalayan Mountains, in the southeast of Arabia and Turkey, and in Persia and independent Tartary, there are vast plains.

**Lowlands of Europe**
The land from the English Channel to the foot of the Ural Mountains — namely, northern France, Belgium, the Netherlands, northern Germany, Denmark, Prussia, Russian-controlled Poland, and all of Russia — are all plains. ○ Between France's Gironde River and the Pyrenees Mountains, there is a vast sandy plain, densely covered with shrubs and dotted with many marshes. This continent also has numerous other plains, found in places such as Cyprus, Hungary, and Lombardy. The plain of Lombardy in particular is the most fertile land in the entire world, producing an abundance of goods.

**Lowlands of Africa**
From the foot of the Atlas Mountains to the Mediterranean Sea, there is fertile flatland. Moreover, Upper Guinea and Lower Guinea form broad plains, all of which are beautiful and fertile lands.
