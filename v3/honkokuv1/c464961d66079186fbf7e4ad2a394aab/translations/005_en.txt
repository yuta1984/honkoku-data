**Diagram of the True Node (正交) at the Autumnal Equinox**

When the true node (正交) falls at the autumnal equinox, the vernal equinox becomes the mid-node (中交).

[Contents of diagram:]
- The vernal equinox becomes the mid-node
- Celestial equator, ecliptic, North Pole (the lunar orbit is 122 degrees from the North Pole), lunar orbit, ascending/descending node (半交)
- True node (no angular difference / 無距差)

When the true node falls at the lodge (宿) of the autumnal equinox, the half-node (半交) extends 6 degrees beyond the ecliptic, and lies 30 degrees beyond the celestial equator.

**Diagram of the True Node (正交) at the Winter Solstice**

From the node of the ecliptic to the node of the lunar orbit: the angular difference (距差) with respect to the celestial equator is 14 degrees 66 minutes.

[Contents of diagram:]
- Half-node, lunar orbit
- Celestial equator; the lunar orbit is at 98 degrees (the fixed limiting degree) from the North Pole; North Pole; autumnal equinox point (正秋); true node (正交)
- The angular difference is the same as above (on the right); ecliptic

When the true node falls at the winter solstice, the lunar orbit extends 24 degrees beyond the celestial equator, and the half-node extends 6 degrees beyond the ecliptic.

At the two cardinal equinox points (spring and autumn), the position that falls short of the true intersection of the ecliptic and celestial equator by 14 and 2/3 degrees to the east or west is defined as the angular difference (距差).
