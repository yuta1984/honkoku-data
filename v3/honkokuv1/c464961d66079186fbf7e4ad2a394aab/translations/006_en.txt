**Diagram of the Summer Solstice at the Regular Intersection (正交)**

[Diagram description]
White Road (lunar path); distance differential (距差) same as before.
Autumnal equinox point (正秋).
Winter solstice becomes the Middle Intersection (中交). North Pole. Regular Intersection (正交).
Vernal equinox point (正春).
Ecliptic (黄道); distance differential same as before.

The amount by which the lunar mansion degree (宿度) falls short of fourteen and two-thirds degrees east or west is taken as the distance differential (距差).

[Lower section]
When the Regular Intersection (正交) occurs at the summer solstice, the center of the White Road enters twenty-four degrees into the interior of the equator, and at the Half Intersection (半交) it projects six degrees outside the ecliptic. The amount by which the lunar mansion degrees at the equinoctial Regular Intersections of the ecliptic and equator fall short of fourteen and two-thirds degrees east or west is taken as the distance differential.

**Collected Diagrams of the Four Cardinal Points and the Nine Roads**

The White Road (白道) is twenty-four degrees from the equator; the Blue Road (青道) is likewise twenty-four degrees from the equator; the Red Road (朱道) is eighteen degrees from the equator; the Black Road (黒道) is thirty degrees from the equator. The lunar road is six degrees from the ecliptic in each direction. The nine paths of the lunar road are in reality one; as they shift and change with the movement of the nodes and do not remain in a fixed position, they are named only by directional colors.
