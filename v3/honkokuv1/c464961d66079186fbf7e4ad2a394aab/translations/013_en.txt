**Botsu-nichi (Days of "Extinction" / Critical Days)**

Chen states: By calculating the *botsu-nichi* (days of extinction), one can determine the new moon days (*saku*) on which solar eclipses will occur. In general, when a *botsu-nichi* falls on a blank day, the 13th, 14th, 25th, 26th, or 27th day, a solar eclipse will necessarily occur on that month's new moon (*saku*). However, if [the sun] reaches the crossroads of the yin/yang calendar limit (*in'yō rekigen*) and exceeds six or eight degrees, then no eclipse will occur. Furthermore, when calculating the exact time (*koku*), if it falls in the middle of the night, this constitutes an eclipse below the horizon (*chika*), which is not recorded in the various calendars. Therefore, one follows the prescribed method (*jutsu*) to determine the precise time (*koku-gen*) and the yin/yang calendar limits.
