Chen (the author) states: In the case of the yang calendar (yōreki), the solar eclipse first begins from the southwest, reaches its maximum at due south, and the recovery occurs at the southeast. In the case of the yin calendar (inreki), the eclipse first begins from the northwest, reaches its maximum at due north, and the recovery occurs at the northeast. That is to say, what is recorded in the calendar follows the respective time intervals, and is shown to observers looking upward from the ground, indicating the directions of up, down, left, and right according to the orientation of the eclipse. As in this case of a yang calendar "band eclipse" (taishoku, an eclipse occurring at sunrise or sunset), since the direction of observation faces east, the recovery (fukki) concludes at the "lower" (eastern) position.

Eighth month: 8 bu, 4 rin, 3 mō, 6 shi, and a remainder (approximately 0.8436+)
