On the mountaintop there is a shrine called Soma Daijin, with two stone deity statues erected beside it. Recently, many people have come to worship this deity, enduring treacherous and difficult roads to make their pilgrimage without cease.

○Suriusu Rock (Mortar Rock) - This is a strange rock formation located to the south at the foot of Soma-ga-dake. Its shape resembles a mortar used as a farming tool, with a hole of more than seven shaku in the center.

○Lake Ikaho - This lake is located southwest of the same area, at the foot of Mount Ikaho Fuji. It is a famous place that appears in the Yakumo Gosho, Man'yoshu, and other poetry collections throughout the ages. In ancient times, the lake was said to be more than one ri in circumference. Today it is called the purification basin of Haruna Shrine. Mountains surround the lake on all four sides, and the Kazusa Diary states: "The names of the mountains surrounding the lake are Mount Ikaho Fuji, Himuro-ga-dake, Kamon-ga-dake, Bingushi-no-dake, Eboshi-ga-dake, and others."

The iris flowers are famous as a scenic feature of this place and are one of the eight scenic views.

○Great Waterfall and Benten Falls - Located behind Mount Ikaho Fuji at the outlet of the lake. The great waterfall is more than three ken wide, cascading down and breaking against numerous rocky edges, with the sound of water roaring like thunder. The surrounding area is covered with smooth moss and old trees, making it an incomparable place for escaping summer heat, though the road is steep and difficult.
