○ The hot spring source of Ikaho flows from numerous points within about one hundred ken (approximately 180 meters) to the east of the Yumoto Sanja shrine, and is channeled through pipes (troughs) to the inns. Notably, when withered plants and trees are submerged in this mineral spring, they revive and turn green within a short time. Furthermore, river fish raised in this hot spring grow plumper and fatter than those raised in ordinary water. These two properties are characteristics unheard of at other hot springs.

○ From early May to mid-June by the solar calendar, the hototogisu (lesser cuckoo, known as the "compassionate bird") calls both day and night. Additionally, the kajika frog (commonly called the "koma frog") lives in the Yuzawa stream and has a most beautiful voice.

○ In the Yuzawa stream there is a soil containing soda compounds, and when this soil is dissolved and used for washing, it is remarkably effective at removing oil and grime from hair.

○ The "Ikaho-ro no sohi no haribara" (the hazel fields of Ikaho) mentioned in the Yakumo Misho, the Man'yōshū, and other classical texts refers to the public land of over 53 chō, 9 tan, and 2 se in area, stretching from the foot of Futatsudake to the area known as Nishizawa. It is a place of scenic beauty with a very ancient history.

○ It appears that in former times, inkstone (suzuri) rock was quarried here, as evidenced by the place name "Ko-suzuriishi." Fossils of various plants are still produced from the land around the hot spring source.
