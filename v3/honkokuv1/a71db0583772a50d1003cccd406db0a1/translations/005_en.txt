【Lower section - Main text】
18 chō and more. At this station there is the Saitama Prefectural Office, and at the station entrance there is Mitsugi Shrine, an ancient shrine listed in the Engi-shiki. The precincts are spacious with lush green trees, so this place was made into a public park with a temporary industrial exhibition hall. They display local products from throughout the prefecture and built a cotton felt weaving workshop to teach weaving techniques to female students. Within the station there is Saitama Prefectural Middle School Teacher Training School as well as Urawa Library. In Harigaya Village, tea gardens and pear orchards have been recently established.

○ Ōmiya: A little over 2 ri
Hikawa Shrine is located beside the station. This shrine is an ancient shrine listed in the Engi-shiki and is the Ichinomiya Kanpei Taisha of Musashi Province. The deity enshrined is Susanoo-no-Mikoto. The annual festival is held on June 15th. At the station between Tenjin bridges, pickled vegetables and mustard rolls are local specialties.
