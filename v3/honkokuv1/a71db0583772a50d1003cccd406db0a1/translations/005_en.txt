Approximately 18 chō: At this station (Urawa), the Saitama Prefectural Government Office is located. At the entrance to the station stands the Mitsugijinja, an ancient shrine listed in the Engishiki. As the shrine grounds are vast and trees grow lushly, this place has been designated a public park, where a Provisional Industrial Museum (Kangyō Kari Hakubutsukan) has been established to display the products of the prefecture. Furthermore, a weaving workshop for mendantsū (wool carpet) has been constructed, where female students are taught weaving techniques. Within the station area, there is also the Saitama Prefectural Middle School and Normal School, as well as the Urawa Library.

In recent years, tea gardens and pear orchards have been opened in Harigaya village.

○Ōmiya　Approximately 2 ri:
Hikawa Shrine is located beside the station. This shrine is an ancient one listed in the Engishiki, and serves as the Ichinomiya (chief shrine) of Musashi Province, designated as a Kanpei Taisha (grand shrine receiving imperial offerings). The enshrined deity is Susanoo no Mikoto, and the annual festival is held on the 15th day of the sixth month.
