○ Tōsen-zan Iōji Temple　This temple is located at the foot of the stone steps leading to Ikaho Shrine. It belongs to the Tendai sect and is a branch temple of Shinkōji Temple in the post town of Shibukawa. The principal image (*honzon*) is Yakushi Nyorai (the Buddha of Healing), and the temple is known for its miraculous efficacy. The temple's founding patron (*kaiki*) was Kishi Chikuzen-no-kami Yasukane, a member of one of Ikaho's old established families.

○ Kōun-zan Tensōji Temple　This is a Zen Buddhist temple located on the back road through Ikaho town. Its principal image is a Yakushi Nyorai said to have appeared from the earth (*dochū shutsugen*). The founding patron was Kogure Shimōsa-no-kami Sukenori, the ancestor of the Kogure family of the same location. Within the temple precincts is the public Ikaho Elementary School.

○ Yumoto Fudōson and the Three Shrines of the Same Location　These are enshrined on a rock approximately five *chō* (roughly 545 meters) up the valley from Iōji Temple, above the *Fukiage* hot spring. The stone statue of Fudō Myōō is an ancient object of great miraculous power. The three shrines at the same location jointly enshrine the *Kotohira* deities of Sōmagadake, Futatsudake, and Yumoto, and serve as protective shrines against fire.

○ Konpira-san (Mt. Konpira)　Located to the southeast of Ikaho. Its true name is "Monokiku-yama" (Mountain of Listening), and it is recorded in such works as the *Fubokushū*, *Moshiogusa*, *Aki no Nezame*, and *Wakan Sansai Zue* as a famous place associated with the *hototogisu* (Japanese lesser cuckoo).

　　*Ikaho naru Monokiku-yama no hototogisu*
　　　*nigoranu koto ni kikoyunaru kana*　　—Ise

　　[The hototogisu of Monokiku-yama in Ikaho—
　　　how clearly it is heard, unclouded by anything]
