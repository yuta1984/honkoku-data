Ikaho Mineral Spring Illustrated Guide
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　By Shinoda Senka

○Route from Tokyo to Ikaho

○From Nihonbashi, approximately 2 ri and 27 chō: At Yushima 5-chōme, there is the Kanda Shrine and also the Shojakukan (library). At Hongō 5-chōme, there is the building of the University Medical Faculty. At Hakusan's Enjōji temple, there is the grave of Yaoya Oshichi, and at the same location stands the Hakusan Shrine.

○Itabashi　Approximately 2 ri and 9 chō: The Toda Riverbank is a famous spot for primroses (sakurasō), and the center of the Toda River marks the jurisdictional boundary between Tokyo Prefecture and Saitama Prefecture. Originally, this river was crossed by ferry, but in Meiji 8 (1875), a wooden bridge was constructed.

○Warabi　Approximately 1 ri and 9 chō: In Shirahata village there is Yakigome-zaka slope, and roasted rice (yakigome) is a local specialty.
