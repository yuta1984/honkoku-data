【Main text】
There are a telegraph branch office, established theaters, and other facilities. This is the most prosperous area along the Nakasendō highway, with 4,948 households (according to an 1880 survey). The main road from this station to Ikaho passes through both Kaneko and Shibukawa stations, which serve as routes to Niigata Prefecture. Particularly for the Shimizu Pass, a new road has been opened since 1880, making the route level and easy to travel.

It is said there are ancient stone monuments within the grounds of the Konpira shrine in the station area. ○ Kaneko [missing text] This entire region is devoted primarily to sericulture, so there are areas where one sees nothing but mulberry trees and no other vegetation.

○ Shibukawa - 2 ri and 6 chō beyond: There are ancient fortifications where Shibukawa Yoshiaki once took refuge. The sweetfish (ayu) from the Tone River are a specialty of this station, with a taste superior and more delicious than those from elsewhere.

The Pine of Imperial Grace: Located halfway along the Ikaho road, this was originally called "Ipponmatsu" (Single Pine). In July 1879, when the Empress Dowager made an imperial visit to Ikaho and rested beneath this pine tree, the accompanying courtier Madenokōji Hirofusa composed the impromptu poem: "In the shelter of the pine in the grass, for a thousand generations what remains is Your Majesty's gracious shadow." Since Kajitori, the governor of Gunma Prefecture, selected this historical account for inscription, it was carved in stone.
