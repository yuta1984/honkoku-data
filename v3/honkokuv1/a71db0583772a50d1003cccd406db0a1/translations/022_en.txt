River fish, dried goods, rental books — all ordinary daily necessities are well-stocked and available without inconvenience.

○ From the thirteenth year of Meiji (1880), all the inns together, for the benefit of those seeking cures, have made arrangements every year from the first day of November through the thirty-first day of March: to guard against the cold, each room is furnished with an open hearth (irori) and a foot warmer (kotatsu), the shoji screens are tightly fitted, glass panes are installed, and guests may enjoy the view in warmth and comfort. Room charges, furnishings, and bedding are all offered at half price; the female attendants working in the guest rooms serve without wages; rice, miso, and all other goods purchased from outside are made available at as close to cost price as possible. In all matters, guests are cared for with sincerity and honesty. Please have no concerns about the cold — come and bathe even in winter and spring, and rid yourselves of illness as soon as possible.
