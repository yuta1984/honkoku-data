○ History of Ikaho's Administrative Governance

This village was originally a proprietary estate (*sharyō*) of Ikaho Shrine. Around the middle of the Ashikaga period, it came under the domain of the Uesugi clan, and for several generations the chief retainer (*karō*) of the Nagao clan governed it while residing at Shirai Castle in the same district. Thereafter, it came under the rule of the Takeda clan of Kōshū and the Hōjō clan of Odawara at various times, but continued to be associated with the Uesugi of Shirai. After the eighteenth year of Tenshō (1590), Ii Naomasa became the lord of Minowa Castle in this province and also held governance over this village.
