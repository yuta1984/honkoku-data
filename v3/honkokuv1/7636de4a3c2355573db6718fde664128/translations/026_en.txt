【Main text on left page】
At the summer solstice, because the sun moves southward, the image of the solar disc appears high. This is because the arc of the outer surface of the air (atmosphere) is flat at that point. At the winter solstice, the sun shifts northward, and because the arc of the outer surface of the air (atmosphere) is steeply inclined, the solar disc appears low.
