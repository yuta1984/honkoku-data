Record of the Ansei Disaster Prevention

Natural disasters and changes in heaven and earth arise from the interaction of yin and yang, hardness and softness. Sometimes clouds form to bring rain, and wind generates thunder. This is just like how illnesses arise in the human body. Alas, it is the will of heaven, it is fate!

On the night of October 2nd, Ansei 2 (1855), around 10 PM, a great earthquake suddenly occurred. The massive earthquake shook and instantly destroyed storehouses strong as iron castles and all manner of houses. The number of deaths was beyond counting. What a pitiful and tragic event.

First, in the north, Senju-juku was greatly shaken and collapsed. Kozukahara suffered earthquake damage and then fire, burning completely. Shin-Yoshiwara was mostly shaken down, and though some remained, fires broke out from Kyō-chō 1st and 2nd districts, as well as from Kaku-chō, Ageya-chō, and Edo-chō 1st and 2nd districts, burning everything completely. The number of courtesans and other deaths was incalculable. However, three buildings at the corner of Edo-chō 1st district remained, six or seven buildings at one corner of Kyō-chō 1st district remained, and half of the fifty buildings on the west side remained.

From there, Doteshita-Tamachi, Takemon, North and South Umamichi, and Saruwaka-chō 1st, 2nd, and 3rd districts all burned completely. One side of Gakuya-shindō remained. Also, from Tanaka to Sanya, Asakusa-chō, Shin-Torigoe, Hashiba, and Imado were terribly destroyed, with the streets piled up like mountains, making passage difficult. The deaths were most numerous here. Also, in front of Daion-ji temple, Minowa, Kanasugi, and Sakamoto were greatly destroyed, while Negishi suffered some collapse. From there, Yanaka and the area around Kōryū-ji temple were greatly destroyed. Lord Rokugō's residence suffered some damage. Also, Shōten-chō, Yamano-yado, and Hanakawado in this area collapsed terribly.

At Kinryūzan Sensō-ji temple, the main hall was unharmed, but the five-story pagoda was slightly damaged. Also, Namiki-dōri, Muraki-chō, Hirokōji, Tahara-chō, and Naka-chō suffered slight damage, while Lord Katō Tōtōmi-no-kami's lower residence in Morishita was greatly destroyed. Fire also broke out from Komagata, burning Suwa-chō, Kurofune-chō, Miyoshi-chō, and up to Kashi-dōri, but was extinguished by the first fire brigade at Miyoshi-chō corner. From there, Okura-mae-dōri suffered slight damage, and while the East Gate ruins were unharmed, the gate collapsed. Also, in front of Kōtoku-ji in Shitaya, temple and town buildings were greatly destroyed, though some places in between remained unharmed.
