One: The star of Saturday, Chinsē (Saturn), completes one full revolution of the heavens from west to east in twenty-nine years, one hundred fifty-five days, and seventy units of time. It advances approximately one degree every thirty days.

One: The star of Thursday, Saisei (Jupiter), completes one full revolution of the heavens from west to east in eleven years, three hundred thirteen days, and forty units of time. It advances approximately one degree every ten days.

One: The star of Tuesday, Eikokusē (Mars), completes one full revolution of the heavens from west to east in one year, three hundred twenty-one days, and ninety units of time. It advances approximately one degree every two days.

One: The star of Friday, Taihakusē (Venus), completes one full revolution of the heavens from west to east together with the Sun in three hundred sixty-five days and twenty-five units of time, while also having an epicyclic motion of five hundred eighty-three days with the Sun as its axis. For further details, one should consult and measure according to the "Bekkoku Betsuden" written by Master Hokusui.
