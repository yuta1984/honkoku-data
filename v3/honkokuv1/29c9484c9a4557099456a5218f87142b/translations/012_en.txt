In the "Tianguan Shu" (Treatise on the Celestial Offices) in the Shiji (Records of the Grand Historian), or in works such as the Tenmon Zukai (Illustrated Explanation of Astronomy), the Tenmon Zusetsu (Illustrated Account of Astronomy), and the Shogaku Shinan Shō (A Primer for Beginning Students), the fortunes of harvests and the rise and fall of nations are explained based on which lunar mansions the five planets occupy—yet these predictions also fail to hit the mark. To cite one example: it is said that when Venus (the Metal Star) enters Wangliang or the Five Chariots, it is an omen of military strife, and when Mars enters them, it governs fire disasters. However, both Wangliang and the Five Chariots lie far to the north of the ecliptic, and are not within the latitudinal range through which the five planets travel. All of these claims are thoroughly deserving of ridicule.
