**Horizon** — A ring placed horizontally.
**Meridian (Shigo-ki)** — A ring placed along the north-south axis; this is the longitudinal line.
**Prime Vertical (Bōyū-ki)** — A ring placed along the east-west axis; this is the latitudinal line.
　　※ In a simplified celestial globe, this ring is omitted.

When these three rings are combined, there are six points of intersection. This is called "three rings, six conjunctions" (sanrin rokugō). Therefore, the study of celestial phenomena is also called "the study of six conjunctions" or "the study of longitude and latitude."
