One: One solar year (based on the seasonal calendar, "fushi") consists of 365 days and 25 koku.

One: One lunar year (based on the year count, "toshi") consists, on average, of 354 days. When this is subtracted from the solar year, a remainder of approximately 11 days and 25 koku is produced. These excess days are accumulated to form intercalary months (leap months). An intercalary month is determined by designating a month that contains no "chūki" (mid-term solar period).
