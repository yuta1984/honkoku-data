One: Stars that lie within thirty-six degrees of the North Pole — that is, within a diameter of seventy-two degrees — continuously revolve above the horizon and never descend below it. These are called the "Upper Regulator" (*jōki*/上規) stars. The stars of Wenchang (*Bunshō*/文昌), the Northern Dipper (*Hokuto*/北斗), Wang Liang (*Ōryō*/王良), and the Celestial Staff (*Tenhō*/天棓) are among the Upper Regulator stars.

One: Stars that lie within thirty-six degrees of the South Pole — that is, within a diameter of seventy-two degrees — are perpetually below the horizon and can never be seen from above ground. These are called the "Lower Regulator" (*geki*/下規) stars. Stars seen above the North Pole revolve from east to west, while stars seen below the North Pole revolve from west to east.
