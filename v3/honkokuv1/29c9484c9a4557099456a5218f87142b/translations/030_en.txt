**A Transmission on Measuring the Subtle Movement of the North Pole Star and Thereby Knowing the True Celestial Pole (*Hokushin*)**

First, carefully observe and fix in your mind the positions of the North Pole Star and the star *Taitei Taishi* (太帝太子). Whether *Taitei Taishi* is above or below the North Pole Star, when it has assumed the configuration shown in diagram [#1], it is moving east or west on a line parallel with the *Hokushin*, with no difference in altitude. This is the critical moment (*kokugen* 剋限) for the measurement of terrestrial latitude (*shutchi sokuryō* 出地測量). Furthermore, when the star is in the upper-eastern position relative to the North Pole Star and has assumed the configuration of diagram [#2], the North Pole Star is low (i.e., below the true *Hokushin*). Conversely, when it is in the lower-western position and has assumed the configuration of diagram [#3], the North Pole Star is high (i.e., above the true *Hokushin*). You should understand it thus.
