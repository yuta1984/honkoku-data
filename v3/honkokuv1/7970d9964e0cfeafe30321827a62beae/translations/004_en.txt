Account of the Great Earthquake, Fires, and Flooding at Zenkoji Temple in Shinano Province

On the twenty-fourth day of the third month of the fourth year of Kōka (1847), the year of Hinoto-Hitsuji (Fire Sheep), the Zenkoji Temple was in the midst of its periodic opening of the sacred image (kaichō). As a result, the temple gate and the surrounding town were bustling with visitors, and at night the countless lanterns made it as bright as broad daylight, with the area in full festive prosperity. However, that same night, at the Hour of the Boar (around 10 o'clock at night), a great earthquake suddenly struck with a roar like rolling thunder, collapsing temples and the townspeople's dwellings all at once, plunging everything into darkness. Before long, fires broke out in several places and grew into a great conflagration. The sound of weeping and wailing from every household echoed through heaven and earth. Even those who managed to escape unharmed struggled desperately to save their parents, spouses, children, and siblings, and there was no one in a position to fight the fires.
