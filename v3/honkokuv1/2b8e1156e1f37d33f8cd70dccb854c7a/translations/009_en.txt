3. Damage in Kanagawa Prefecture

1. Number of Damaged Households in Kanagawa Prefecture
The total number of damaged households in Kanagawa Prefecture, excluding households with only minor damage, was 175,000, representing 64% of existing households on the day of the earthquake. Of these, Yokohama City had 79,000 households, while Yokosuka City and rural districts had over 95,000 households. The ratio of damaged households to existing households was 80% in Yokohama City and 55% in rural districts, with the proportion of households destroyed by fire being 60% in Yokohama City and 3% in rural districts.

Among the rural districts, those with the highest proportion of damaged households were Ashigarashimo District at 96%, Miura District at 89%, and Naka District and Kuraki District at 78% to 77%. The largest numbers of households destroyed by fire were over 4,000 in Ashigarashimo District and over 1,000 in Kamakura District. 2. Affected Population in Kanagawa Prefecture
The total number of disaster victims in Kanagawa Prefecture was over 868,000 people, representing 63% of the existing population on the day of the earthquake. Of these, there were over 29,000 deaths, over 66,000 injuries, and over 3,500 missing persons, totaling over 97,000 casualties.
