Several hundred cooking utensils each — including pots, cauldrons, and stoves — were supplied by the First Division and the Imperial Guard Division, and were put into immediate use. Subsequently, as more continued to arrive from various regions, they have been distributed accordingly. The supply of milk for the consumption of infants and sick persons was also deemed extremely urgent; accordingly, arrangements were made to have the milk from Chiba Prefecture and the Livestock Experiment Station transferred to the custody of the Executive Office as much as possible, and supply commenced from the 9th. Additionally, arrangements were made for the Executive Office to purchase 10 *koku* of milk daily from the wholesalers' representative shop for distribution.

In order to restore the retail organization for rice and other daily necessities, a central agency was established to wholesale and distribute daily goods. The proposal submitted by the Chamber of Commerce — whereby influential merchants from various trade associations would be directed to collect goods and have retail merchants sell them at fixed prices — was approved along with its accompanying requests. Assistance or facilitation was to be provided with respect to the establishment of retail shops and storage facilities, free transport of goods for sale, and the extension of funds.

Furthermore, the handling of rice for sale was assigned to the Ministry of Agriculture and Commerce. Arrangements were made for the construction of warehouses to store foodstuffs unloaded at each distribution point and at Shibaura, and standard selling prices for rice and other foodstuffs were determined and communicated to the Prefecture and the City.
