They lowered fishing nets and pulled them, and over fifty people came up caught in the nets. They were too heavy to pull up, so they finally had to divide them in half to bring them to shore.

●Wanting to Save Her Brothers
An old man barely escaped but was worried and turned back to find his daughter reaching her hand out from the water calling for rescue. He quickly took her hand, and the daughter said, "Grandfather, thank you for coming. In my desire to save my two brothers, I lost one arm and one leg. I must take my leave now. If my two brothers are saved, I will have no regrets." The father pulled up both children, but the two brothers were unconscious. He made them vomit water and revived them, but the sister passed away the next morning.

●A Curse
In Kamaishi, a shop apprentice of about thirteen was kicking through the water to escape when a woman clung to him from behind. As he struggled to shake her off, the woman called out from the water, "If you don't save me, I will curse you." Somehow the apprentice alone escaped, but that voice remained in his ears and he could not forget it.

●Dead Bodies Standing Upside Down in the Ground
Karakuwa in Motoyoshi District, Miyagi Prefecture was a place where the damage was extremely severe. The day after the disaster, when district officials went to provide relief, they found someone stuck upside down in a rice field.

●An Exemplary Teacher
Kuchiuchi Taikichi, a teacher in Hakozaki, Iwate Prefecture, heard about the tsunami and fled without taking anything, but thinking that the elementary school classroom was now in danger, he turned back again and managed to move the Emperor's portrait to a safe location. Because of this, he lost his opportunity to escape, suffered serious injuries, and tragically died a loyal death.

○Imperial Relief Funds Granted
Their Majesties the Emperor and Empress, deeply moved by the extraordinary devastation in the disaster areas caused by the tsunami, granted 10,000 yen to Iwate Prefecture, 3,000 yen to Miyagi Prefecture, and 1,000 yen to Aomori Prefecture as imperial relief funds.

○Central Famine Relief Savings
The Ministry of Finance disbursed 50,000 yen to Iwate Prefecture and 10,000 yen to Miyagi Prefecture from the Central Famine Relief Savings. Further increases are planned pending investigation.

○Red Cross Society's Donation of Hospital Clothing
The Red Cross Society donated 1,500 sets of hospital clothing to Iwate Prefecture, 500 to Miyagi Prefecture, and 200 to Aomori Prefecture.

○Disaster Victims Continue to Die
Due to shortage of doctors, proper medical care cannot be provided, and disaster victims suffering in anguish continue to die everywhere. The tragic situation is beyond description.
