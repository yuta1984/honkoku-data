●Using Nets for Corpses: Around Kamaishi, since around the 17th, they have been searching for bodies using nets, and even more are being found.

●Sister's Head: A clerk from Kamaishi town hall rushed home to find a woman's head protruding from the water, trapped among debris. When pulled up, what a shock - it was only a head. After washing away the mud, it was revealed to be his own sister.

●Sannohe District, Aomori Prefecture: In Ichikawa village, a family of nine perished tragically. On the 18th, during the funeral service, the nine bodies were placed in four coffins and carried in procession to the temple - a rare and tragic sight. Oh, what sorrow!

●Sannohe District's Obunato Elementary School: On the night of the 15th, the school was suddenly swept into the sea by the tsunami with a teacher still inside, and no trace has been seen since.

●On the 19th, a body discovered at a certain location was that of a woman in her thirties, tightly clutching a nursing infant as they emerged from the sand. The maternal love that would not release her child even in death brings tears to all who hear of it.

●The tsunami struck around 8 PM on the 15th, right during dinner time. The attack must have been incredibly sudden - a body recovered from excavating a collapsed house was that of a 12-year-old boy who had died with pickled radish still in his mouth. Oh, the tragedy knows no bounds!

●The most tragic report came of a family that perished entirely with no relatives left to collect the bodies. Though officials do not neglect such matters, in urgent situations it is difficult to process hundreds of bodies quickly. With no choice, distant relatives came to collect them, but there was no one available for hire nearby. They had to place two people each (four total) in baskets normally used for sardines, making two baskets, and had an ox carry them away - truly life's greatest tragedy. The disasters are countless.

●Forty Burned to Death: In Yamada town, Higashihei District, Iwate Prefecture, over 1,000 people died, but only about 350 bodies have been discovered. Most tragic here was the burning death of disaster victims. Survivors who had narrowly escaped gathered in a remaining storehouse, but due to severe hunger and cold, they lit a fire. It spread to nearby furniture and soon the entire storehouse was ablaze. Too exhausted to extinguish the flames, they cried "Oh no!" as they were engulfed in fierce fire with no escape, and over 40 people burned to death - truly beyond description as tragic.

●Only Three in the Entire Village: Shirahama in Motoyoshi District, Miyagi Prefecture, was a village of 13 households with 99 residents, but the tsunami carried away all houses and people, leaving only three survivors.

●Mr. Kogaru-chu, a permanent committee member of Iwate Prefecture who perished in this tsunami, was from one of Kamaishi's most prominent families. He had built a house worth over 10,000 yen three or four years ago, but now there is no trace left, and not a single person survived, not even the servants.
