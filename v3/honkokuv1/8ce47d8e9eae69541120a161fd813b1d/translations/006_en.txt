Such was the story.

○At the residence of Lord Matsudaira Suruga-no-kami (35,000 koku, Iyo Imabari domain) inside Koishikawa Gate, five guests had come today, including Lord Arima Hyuga and Lord Ikeda Ukyo, who remained for a drinking party. That same night at the hour of the dog (around 8 PM), after returning home, the lord alone was singing noh songs when the earthquake struck and the mansion collapsed. Retainers Totsuka Suketayu and Koizumi Saburo Emon rushed to rescue their master, but fire broke out from the tea room and the entire household burned down. The immediate deaths and fire victims numbered 28 samurai and over 50 foot soldiers and servants.

○At the residence of Lord Matsudaira Buzen-no-kami (50,000 koku) outside Hitotsubashi, though the building collapsed all the way to the back of the entrance hall, the lord fortunately escaped disaster. A total of 96 retainers and servants, 27 women, and 11 horses died, with over 100 injured. The fire started and spread from within the residence.

○At the residence of Lord Matsudaira Sanuki-no-kami (120,000 koku) inside Koishikawa Gate, the lord was unharmed, but 33 retainers and 29 lady attendants died instantly, with over 150 injured. They camped outdoors in the garden for three days.

○At the residence of Lord Sakai Utano-kami (150,000 koku) in front of Ote Gate, though there was little collapse, fire broke out from the inner daisu room and everything burned down completely, including the storehouses. 84 retainers died instantly, and 163 female attendants in the inner quarters perished. The number of injured was countless.

○Lord Ogasawara Heibei (500 koku), a firearms commander of the advance guard in Ogawa-cho Sarugaku-cho, had received a visit from Sugiya Rokunosuke regarding a custom-ordered large cannon, and just as the order document was handed over and the guest was leaving, the earthquake struck. As they rushed toward the entrance hall, the guest quarters collapsed all at once, causing instant death. Fire from the Nakarai residence spread and burned everything down.
