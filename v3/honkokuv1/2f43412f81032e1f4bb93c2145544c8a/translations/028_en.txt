**Method** (explained with reference to the diagrams drawn below):

For example, when one wishes to measure a square area of forty *ken* on each side, with the base station (*honza*) at the center, first take a single sheet of white paper and cut it into a square of equal dimensions on all four sides, provisionally designating this as the model (scaled diagram) of a forty-*ken* square. (Note: as long as the paper is square, the actual size of the paper does not matter. For instance, if the paper surface is eight *sun* square, one may set two *bu* as one *ken*, two *sun* as ten *ken*, and eight *sun* as forty *ken*, and use this scale to measure the number of *ken* for the opening.)

As shown in the diagram, draw ink lines (*sumi*) across the paper from corner to corner in a cross (+) pattern. (These cross-shaped ink lines represent the four corners of the forty-*ken* square area.) Draw additional ink lines in the vertical direction as well, for the purpose of re-verification.
