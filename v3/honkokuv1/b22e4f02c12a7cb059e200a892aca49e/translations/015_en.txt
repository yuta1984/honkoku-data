## Section on Tōnozawa　— Twelve chō from Yumoto

### Medicinal Properties of the Hot Spring
This is a lukewarm spring, mild in sensation, classified as a cinnabar (mercuric sulfide) spring, and is primarily recommended for general recuperation and health maintenance.

Indicated conditions include: stroke/palsy, headache, anal fistula, dizziness, abdominal blockage, mouth pain, beriberi, bruising, rectal bleeding, hernia/colic, chronic bleeding, toothache, muscle pain, bone pain, kidney deficiency, gonorrhea/urinary disorders, leucorrhea, cold constitution, sword wounds, kidney deficiency, vaginal discharge, depression, chest congestion, infantile convulsions, poor blood circulation in women, phlegm accumulation, chest pain.

It is said that women who have been unable to conceive will surely become pregnant if they bathe in this spring for an extended period.

The main bath lodging of Yagobei is tastefully decorated and clean, with a spectacular view from the garden, truly elegant and unrivaled. In addition, this location offers archery (a popular indoor amusement) and various other entertainments.

**Bath Lodgings:** Motoyū Yagobei, Tamura Kyūbei, Fukuzumi Kihēji, Ichinoyū Sawauemon, Fujiya Kihachi, Takinoyu Jūuemon (this lodging has a waterfall bath)

All of the above have private indoor baths.

## Section on Miyanoshita　— One and a half ri from Tōnozawa; four to five chō from Dōgashima

### Medicinal Properties　　This is a lukewarm spring with a salty taste (chloride spring)

Indicated conditions include: headache, abdominal hardening, lower back pain, beriberi, abdominal accumulation, stroke/palsy, hernia/colic, wind ailments (colds/neuralgia), asthma, vertigo, blood clots, muscle ailments, anal fistula, sprains, vaginal discharge, bruising.

Within the bathing facilities of Naraya Hyōji, there is a waterfall called "Crescent Moon Falls" (Mikazuki no Taki). This waterfall bath has many medicinal benefits. From the guest rooms, one can gaze upon the distant "White Thread Falls" (Shiraito no Taki), which presents a magnificent view.

### Guidelines for Using the Waterfall Bath

Regarding entering the waterfall bath: Those who have come to the hot springs for the first time should not subject themselves to the waterfall on the very day of their arrival. One should soak in the bath tub (yagura) for one or two days before approaching the waterfall. If one rushes to the waterfall from the very beginning, the illness will surely be aggravated and the blood will not settle. After one or two days have passed and one wishes to approach the waterfall, one should enter the bath tub as one sees fit, warm the body thoroughly, and when sufficiently warmed, direct the waterfall at the affected area of one's ailment, or at any other location one wishes. After finishing under the waterfall, one should enter the bath again. One must not indiscriminately remain under the waterfall continuously, nor should one simply step out after the waterfall session without re-entering the bath. As the waterfall is something that agitates one's vital energy (ki), one should calm one's mind and settle one's energy down to the navel (heso) before entering.
