...○Tower Peak (also called Tendai Mountain; a place where a pagoda once stood) ○Sacred Hermitage Cave (located below Tower Peak). This place is said to be the site of Lord Yoriie's hermitage, and also said to be the place where Hōjō Tokimasa was confined. There are many ancient burial mounds in this area, which are the graves of Lord Yoriie's retainers. The Azuma Kagami records that in the first year of Genkyū (1204), in the sixth month, it was discovered that the household retainers of Sakon'e no Chūjō Zen'kaku (Yoriie) were hiding in remote places plotting rebellion, and Sagami's Kanekubo Tarō and others were dispatched to execute them immediately. The Kamakura Ōnikki states: "On the twentieth day of the seventh month of Genkyū 2 (1205), Tokimasa, at age sixty-eight, became a monk, took the Buddhist name Meishō, was confined to Shūzenji, and died at age seventy-eight on the sixth day of the first month of Kenpō 3 (1213)." The Shikken Shidai records that Tokimasa was confined to Shūzenji due to the matter of Lord Sanetomo, which was the result of the machinations of Lady Maki. The Azuma Kagami states that Tokimasa died in Hōjō District, but there is no Hōjō District among the four districts of Izu Province. ○Shigetsu Hall

(Local people call it the Sutra Hall). This place is the Complete Sutra Repository donated by the Nun Shogun Lady Masako. There were Song dynasty editions of excellent texts, but in recent years they were sent to Zōjōji Temple in Shiba by imperial command. Now only the Hōkō Hannya Haramitta Sutra in twenty-two volumes remains, and in its postscript there is the direct handwriting of the Nun Princess (Masako) stating "Placed here for the spiritual benefit of Seii Taishōgun Sakon'e no Kami Minamoto no Yoriie." ○Lord Yoriie's Tomb (located above the sutra hall). Lord Yoriie was the eldest son of Lord Yoritomo, became head of the family in Shōji 1 (1199), and eventually became Seii Taishōgun, but was killed in the hot spring bath in the seventh month of Genkyū 1 (1204) due to Tokimasa's conspiracy. The ancient tomb is a small stone pagoda. In front of it stands a memorial stele, with "Seii Taishōgun Sagen Yoriie Sonrei" written on its face, "Genkyū 1" on the left side, and "Kinoe-ne, seventh month, eighteenth day" on the right side. In recent times, local people and hot spring bathers have been lifting the upper stone of the sacred stone pagoda with their own hands...
