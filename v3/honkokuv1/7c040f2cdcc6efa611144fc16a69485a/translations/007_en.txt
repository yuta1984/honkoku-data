(Continued from previous page) ...this must have constituted an extraordinary loss. All the bridges spanning the Metoba River — the Ōhashi, Hitotsubashi, Shinkōji Bridge, Shimizu Bridge, Saiwai Bridge, and others — collapsed entirely, with only the Chitose Bridge remaining intact.

In response to this situation, the Matsumoto Town Office organized relief cooking (炊き出し / communal food distribution), while the head of Higashi-Chikuma District, Mr. Kaiguchi, and the Matsumoto Town Mayor, Mr. Sugaya, each donated twenty yen as relief funds.

**[Water Level of Two Jō and One Shaku]** The flooding in Nagano Prefecture this time exceeded the great flood of the 28th year [of Meiji, i.e., 1895] by two shaku (approx. 60 cm), and at around noon on July 21st, the water gauge at Ryōgun Bridge

**[Right Page, Lower Section]**

registered a remarkable height of two jō and one shaku (approximately 6.4 meters).

**[Passage by Timber Raft]** Due to the rising waters of the Asakawa River and several other rivers in Hatsusato Village, Nagano, Nagano Prefecture, damage to farmland was considerable. Furthermore, the embankment of Asahi Village broke for approximately thirty ken (roughly 54 meters), resulting in the loss of four to five chōbu of cultivated land, flooding of some two hundred homes, and the situation in the Akanuma district of Naganuma Village became so severe that the area could only be navigated by timber raft.
