The mountainous areas also suffered extensive damage from overflowing rivers. Only Hida and Ena remained unaffected. Details of casualties among people and livestock and the loss of houses remain unclear, likely because movement was severely restricted, communications were cut off, and all efforts were focused on saving lives and flood prevention. Approximately 60,000 homes were flooded (twice the number from the July flood), sixty major embankment breaches occurred, and minor breaches were beyond counting. The rain has still not stopped, and it is impossible at this time to predict what will happen next.

◯ The following are approximate figures known as of September 14th, listing damages at major locations: In Anpachi District, Ōgaki Town, 152 homes were washed away, 162 partially destroyed, and 111 damaged (in terms of total structures, including non-residential buildings, those suffering damage or worse numbered 1,440); deaths: 5. In the villages, 492 homes washed away, 536 collapsed, deaths: 11. Within the jurisdiction of the Imao sub-precinct, covering the town and six additional villages, 396 homes were washed away and 46 people died. In Kaisai District, 379 homes collapsed, 10 partially destroyed, 5 damaged (in terms of structures, including non-residential buildings, those suffering partial destruction or worse numbered 1,027); deaths: 51. The Takasu Town Saibetsuin and the branch court office in Imao Town were also washed away. The Takasu Police Station has no habitable building and is conducting its affairs from aboard a boat. The relatively low death toll across the above towns and villages is believed to be due to the police having issued timely warnings before the storm struck and making evacuation arrangements without losing the critical moment. Investigation is ongoing.
