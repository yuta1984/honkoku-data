In dry lands, the qi (atmospheric vapor) is sparse, thin, and low. Where it is thick and high, the reflected images appear increasingly large and elevated. Where it is thin and low, the reflected images are not particularly large, nor are they elevated to any great degree. Generally speaking, if the terrain is uneven, the state of the qi will also be uneven. Consequently, the degree to which things are affected by the meng qi (atmospheric refraction) is also uneven. If one wishes to precisely determine the degrees of solar longitude, lunar position, the five planets, and the various constellations, it is ultimately difficult to achieve accurate agreement without first determining the local atmospheric refraction differential.

My teacher, Master Sima Xiong (personal name: Mingyu), said: "In the phenomena of heaven and earth, that which ought to be fixed yet is not fixed—it is because qi veils it. In the numerical patterns of heaven and earth, that which ought to be fixed yet is not fixed—it is because events disrupt it. One who truly comprehends things will, from beginning to end, across all ages, deeply observe the changes of the times, look up to examine the atmospheric vapor, look down to understand disruptive events, and weigh their principles in the balance. Then the knowledge of celestial offices will be complete."

【Star Constellations】

Question: Among stars of the same category, some have their own distinct functions yet do not associate with others—why is this?

Answer: Among the stars, there are those that form one constellation by themselves, and those that form one constellation with two, three, or more than ten stars. Some are adjacent and affiliated with one another, while others are adjacent but not affiliated. Gang (the Pole) is affiliated with Huagai (the Canopy), forming a single constellation of eighteen stars in total. Heng (the Weighing Beam) is affiliated with Kulou (the Arsenal Pavilion), forming a single constellation of twenty-nine stars in total. Yue (the Axe) is not affiliated with Jing (the Well); Er (the Ear) is not affiliated with Bi (the Net); Kang (the Chaff) is not affiliated with Ji (the Winnowing Basket); Changsha is not affiliated with Zhen (the Chariot); and Gouling and Bijian are not affiliated with Fang (the Room). These are like subordinate officials who nonetheless hold their own independent posts. The Arrow can be affiliated with the Bow, but the Mortar cannot be affiliated with the Pestle—because the Bow and Arrow are governed by one person, while the Pestle and Mortar are governed by two. The Wild Pheasant is not affiliated with the Army Well, as the Pheasant independently guards its own domain. The Southern Gate is not affiliated with the Arsenal Pavilion, because the Southern Gate is not merely the gate of the Arsenal Pavilion. Similarly, Jishui (Accumulated Water) is not affiliated with Tianchu (the Celestial Boat), Jishi (Accumulated Corpses) is not affiliated with Daling (the Great Mound), Tianchan is not affiliated with Juanshe (the Rolled Tongue), and Xianchi and Tianhuang are not affiliated with Wuche (the Five Chariots). All of these have their own reasoned distinctions and should not be explained through mere speculation.
