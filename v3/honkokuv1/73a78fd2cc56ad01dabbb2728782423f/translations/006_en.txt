**[Inconsistency in Degree Attribution (continued)]**

Since the methods of measurement had not yet been refined, each practitioner held their own theory, and there was no established way to determine who was right or wrong. Thus, one person would classify a star as belonging to Zhen (軫), another as Jiao (角); one as Di (氐), another as Fang (房). However, the discrepancies always occur between adjacent lunar mansions, amounting to no more than a shift of a few degrees.

Now, the full circle of heaven is divided into four quadrants, each quadrant containing three palaces (zodiacal signs), and each palace divided into thirty degrees. Broadly speaking: the first degree of Aries (White Ram Palace, 戌 palace) meets the first degree of Wall (壁); the first degree of Taurus (Golden Bull Palace, 酉 palace) meets the fifth degree of Lou (婁); the first degree of Gemini (Yin-Yang Palace, 申 palace) meets the seventh degree of Mao (昴); the first degree of Cancer (Great Crab Palace, 未 palace) borders the end of Shen (参) and beginning of Jing (井); the first degree of Leo (Lion Palace, 午 palace) meets the thirtieth degree of Jing (井); the first degree of Virgo (Twin Maidens Palace, 巳 palace) meets the seventh degree of Zhang (張); the first degree of Libra (Celestial Scale Palace, 辰 palace) meets the first degree of Zhen (軫); the first degree of Scorpio (Celestial Scorpion Palace, 卯 palace) meets the first degree of Kang (亢); the first degree of Sagittarius (Man-Horse Palace, 寅 palace) meets the third degree of Fang (房); the first degree of Capricorn (Grinding Goat Palace, 丑 palace) meets the third degree of Ji (箕); the first degree of Aquarius (Precious Vase Palace, 子 palace) meets the first degree of Niu (牛); the first degree of Pisces (Twin Fish Palace, 亥 palace) meets the third degree of Wei (危).

[You Xi (游熙) says: Ordinary people only know that the Zi hour marks the beginning of the current day and the Hai hour marks its end, but they do not know that the Zi hour corresponds to the very start of the zheng-chu (正初) moment as the beginning of the day, and that the first four quarters of the night Zi hour constitute the end of the current day. For example, when Lichun (立春, the Beginning of Spring) falls in the first month and establishes the Yin direction, passing through a palace brings one to Yushui (雨水), and one then first enters the Yin palace. People today say that the second degree of Kui (奎) is in the Xu palace, the fourth degree of Wei (胃) passes through the You palace, and the seventh degree of Bi (畢) passes through the Shen palace, but all of these reflect ignorance of how palaces move in direct order yet counter to another direction, as well as ignorance of the principle of palace-division and the analysis of time periods.]

The method is: using a line pressed through the polar center extending to the degrees of the palaces around the full heavens, any star found within the line of a given palace is classified as belonging to that palace and lunar mansion.

**[The Twinkling and Trembling of Stars]**

*Question:* The movement of heavenly stars follows fixed degrees, and measurement follows fixed calculations. Yet stars sometimes flash and leap, and sometimes tremble and sway, as if they had no fixed position. Does this trembling and twinkling affect measurements and calculations?

*Answer:* The fixed positions of the jing xing (經星, fixed stars) have no principle by which they become disordered, transformed, or vary in brightness. When wind and rain are approaching, they flash and tremble — all of this is because the earth's qi (atmospheric vapors) is flying and trembling, causing their images to surge and move. However, it is not the stars themselves that are moving; it is the qi that is leaping.
