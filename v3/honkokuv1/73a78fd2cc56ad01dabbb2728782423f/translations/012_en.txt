The *Qiancao* states: "The earth is like a melon that has a stem and a navel. Through the belt of the equator, it is divided into north and south, and east and west; together with the two poles, this forms the framework of the six directions (heaven, earth, and the four cardinal directions). Just as an egg being incubated must be divided into upper and lower, and just as a round object floating on water or suspended by a thread naturally settles into a fixed orientation — the three circles and five lines (the various great circles and coordinate lines of the celestial sphere, such as the equator, ecliptic, and horizon) demonstrate that China corresponds to the 'chest' of the earth, while the western regions (Xiqian) correspond to the position of the left breast. The Central Land (China) used the hexagrams and yarrow stalks to establish ritual and music, to express the way of human nature and destiny, and achieved the great perfection of governance and culture. That it alone is 'clear, complete, and centrally correct' — can this be mere coincidence? The land directly beneath the North Pole is useless territory. Beneath the ecliptic, the spiritual vitality of people is abundant and the myriad things flourish, and China lies to the south of the equatorial belt. Heaven, earth, and humanity correspond to one another, and their workings naturally align. The configuration of the land corresponds to heaven: the whole earth responds to it, and even one particular region responds to it. [The lunar lodges] can be arranged in a flat sequence or in a circular sequence. The ancients listed them according to what the people knew. It is a pity that today there is no profound wisdom (*shinmei*) to redefine the astral territories (*bunno*) of the Central Land anew; instead, people merely follow the geographical records of the Sui and Jin dynasties, updating only the names of the current administrative districts. The ancients relied upon astral geography in divination by responding to events as they arose and grasping the subtle workings of things; it was never meant to be a fixed framework for later generations to cling to rigidly. Just as with the sixty-year cycle of heavenly stems and earthly branches (*kanshi*), astral geography too can be employed for calculation and inference. The rigid and narrow views of ordinary people are precisely what causes obstruction in all matters."

【Years and Months】

Question: It is correct to say that one year is the period in which the sun completes one revolution around the heavens. However, the moon is slower than the sun, yet thirty days are taken as one month and twelve hours (*toki*) as one day — why is this?

Answer: One year (*issai*) is the period in which the sun travels eastward along the lunar lodges and completes one revolution of the heavens. There are two ways of understanding this motion. The first: starting from a certain degree and minute of a given lunar mansion (*kyūji*), the sun travels one degree per day around the heavens and returns to its original position; this number amounts to 365 days, 24 *koku* (quarter-hours), and 21 *fun* (minutes), plus a remainder. The second: the sun comes into conjunction with a certain star in the heaven of the lunar lodges, travels one degree per day around the heavens, and again comes into conjunction with that original star; this is called the complete number of one year.
