Matsushiro castle town: Two-thirds of houses collapsed (Hanishina District)

The river dried up and became a riverbed. The downstream villages, fearing they would be hit by floods, fled to the mountains or escaped to other places. This area has weak ground, so they first headed to Ueda.

Travelers: 300 people
Deaths: Just under 500 people

Between Nagai and Hirabayashi villages, across a forested area of about 8 chō, a mountain about 80-90 ken high split in half and collapsed into the Sai River, with houses and trees remaining as they were, forming a lake upstream.

The width varies from 5-6 chō to 14-15 chō in some places.

Fifteen villages in this area were submerged underwater, and their houses were washed away.

It is said to be 6-7 ri from Nagai.
