"Chronicle of Major Earthquakes Across the Provinces: Tales for All Ages"

A great earthquake shook the land for more than two hundred days. On the fourth day of the tenth month of Hōei 4 (1707), a great earthquake struck Osaka, causing so many deaths by crushing that the bodies piled up like mountains. At this time, blue mud spurted up along the coastal areas of Kishū, Seishū, Sanshū, and Enshū, and a tsunami arose, killing several tens of thousands of people. Mount Fuji trembled and volcanic ash fell on neighboring provinces. It was at this time that Mount Hōei was formed.

○ The 115th Emperor, Emperor Nakamikado: On the 26th day of the 9th month of Kyōhō 17 (1732), an earthquake struck Nagasaki in Hizen Province. On the 19th day of the 3rd month of Kyōhō 11 (1726), an earthquake struck Echizen Province.

○ The 117th Emperor, Emperor Momozono: On the 29th day of the 2nd month of Hōreki 1 (1751), a great earthquake struck Kyoto, and the ground continued to shake until the 7th month. On the 25th day of the 4th month of the same year, in Takada, Echigo Province, the earth shook more than thirty times between the Hour of the Rooster (around 6 p.m.) and the Hour of the Ox (around 2 a.m.), causing landslides and killing more than sixteen thousand people.

○ The 121st Emperor, Emperor Ninkō: On the 12th day of the 6th month of Bunsei 5 (1822), earthquakes struck Kyoto and Osaka. On the 2nd day of the 7th month of Bunsei 13 (1830), a great earthquake struck Kyoto. On the 24th day of the 3rd month of Kōka 4 (1847), a great earthquake struck the area of Zenkōji Temple in Shinano Province, resulting in a great number of deaths.

○ On the 14th day of the 6th month of Kaei 7 (1854), a great earthquake struck provinces throughout the land. The areas of Nanto (Nara), Iga-Ueno, and Seishū suffered the most severe shaking over four days. On the 4th and 5th days of the 11th month of the same year, similar earthquakes occurred; among these, Shishū, Seishū, Kishū, and Sesshū were devastated. Tsunamis followed the earthquakes, causing an uncountable number of deaths. In Osaka alone, approximately six thousand or more people died from the tsunami.
