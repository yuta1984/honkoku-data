Regarding precautions before and after smallpox

It is said that there are various charms before smallpox, but they are not reliable. If one were to use charms, it would be extremely good to obtain wild yam (tokoro) that was displayed during the New Year's first feeding ceremony from seven houses, decoct it as usual, and bathe the child with it. The primary precaution is that during epidemic seasons, one should avoid rainy and windy weather, crowds, night walking, and long journeys, and keep this in mind. Naturally, one should be careful of seasonal heat and cold, and it is good to use medicines that dispel seasonal qi. If smallpox passes lightly, one should carefully recuperate afterward. Most importantly, if oily foods are given, they will aid residual toxins, damage the eyes, cause bad eruptions, and lead to malnutrition disorders.

Therefore, after smallpox, oily foods should be avoided for seventy-five days. Like before and after childbirth, smallpox is truly a major calamity of one's lifetime and should not be taken lightly.

Regarding protecting the eyes

If the eyes do not open from the time of smallpox, and during the drying period the eyelashes remain closed and will not open, burning bird feathers at a distance may suddenly cause them to open. Washing with rabbit droppings may miraculously open the eyes. Although various methods are said to exist, they are hard to believe.
