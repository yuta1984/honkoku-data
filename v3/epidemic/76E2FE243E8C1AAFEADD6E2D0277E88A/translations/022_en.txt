Rather than determining the quality of each individual food item, it is essential to pay attention to cooking methods and moderation. With any food, eating raw items or overeating will cause diarrhea and other intestinal and gastric diseases, inviting or causing infection.

Furthermore, foods that habitually cause diarrhea in individuals should never be consumed as they cause similar harm. Now, regarding suitable foods: grains and fresh meat from cattle, calves, sheep, and chickens are best. Duck, goose, and pork contain too much fat and are not good. Though there are strict prohibitions against fish and shellfish, since these are staple foods in coastal areas, this would cause problems. Truly fresh items need not be particularly forbidden. For vegetables, one should avoid lettuce and similar plants, and eat starchy root vegetables like potatoes (sweet potatoes and other tubers). Fish, shellfish, and vegetables - indeed, all foods of any kind - should only be consumed after being cooked by boiling, steaming, baking, roasting, or similar preparation methods. While eating small amounts of ripe fruits such as peaches, plums, pears, apples, grapes, and strawberries causes no harm, unripe ones must be avoided.
