Recently, there have been deaths from cholera in the Yokohama port city area, and since the risk of natural transmission to this area is incalculable, while self-care and health maintenance are naturally important, if one recognizes infection symptoms, one must immediately seek medical treatment without delay and promptly report this to the nearest Metropolitan Police hospital or district stations. This directive is hereby promulgated.
　　However, if one observes others afflicted with this disease, they should report it in the same manner as stated above.

○Metropolitan Police Headquarters Directive Ko No. 26《September 21, Meiji 10 (1877)》
　When a physician's diagnosis confirms cholera infection, a sign reading "Cholera Present" shall be posted at the patient's entrance, and the attending physician must immediately affix it. This directive is hereby promulgated.

○Metropolitan Police Headquarters Directive Ko No. 27《September 21, Meiji 10 (1877)》
　Regarding signs of cholera transmission, due to investigative requirements, for the time being, regardless of the type of illness, all deaths must be reported promptly to Metropolitan Police hospitals or jurisdictional stations, accompanied by a physician's diagnosis certificate. This directive is hereby promulgated.

○Notice from Metropolitan Police Bureau to All Districts《September 20, Meiji 10 (1877)》
　Regarding the quantity and availability of carbolic acid held by pharmacies within the jurisdiction, immediate investigation of said stores is required. A report with the proprietor's seal must be compiled and submitted to the Fifth Division of this headquarters by the day after tomorrow, the 22nd. This notice is hereby communicated.
