Among disinfectants, carbolic acid is considered the most effective. The recent reputation gained by disinfection methods is primarily due to this invention. Next to this is sulfurous acid gas. The use of green vitriol alone only has deodorizing capability and is insufficient for eradicating disease toxins. Carbolic acid should also be mixed with it.

Disinfectant Formulations

First: Crude carbolic acid - Contains 45 to 60 percent phenolic acid (i.e., crystalline carbolic acid), is somewhat colored and fluid, and dissolves with difficulty in water. When "tar" is formed, it emits an unpleasant odor.

Second: Carbolic acid powder - The above crude carbolic acid mixed with coarse materials such as charcoal, sand ash, sawdust, etc.

Third: Crystalline carbolic acid (i.e., "phenolic acid or phenyl alcohol") - A colorless crystalline acid that dissolves completely in water (already dissolves in 18 to 20 parts water).

Fourth: Carbolic acid water - One part crystalline carbolic acid dissolved in 100 times the amount of water.

Fifth: Carbolic acid solution - Two parts crystalline carbolic acid mixed with 100 parts water.
