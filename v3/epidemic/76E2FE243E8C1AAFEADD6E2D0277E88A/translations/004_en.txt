○Ministry of Home Affairs Record《September 19, Meiji 10 (1877)》
　Telegram reports cholera outbreak in Taniyama district, Kagoshima Prefecture. Similar outbreaks reported in Yokohama and Kanagawa Station in Kanagawa Prefecture. Therefore, it is essential to observe the health preservation methods outlined in Sanitation Bureau Report No. 5 and pay careful attention to protecting one's health.

○Ministry of Home Affairs Sanitation Bureau《September 19, Meiji 10 (1877)》
　Cholera has been spreading in Yokohama, Kanagawa Prefecture since the 5th of this month. As of the 18th, there have been a total of 42 patients: 14 deaths, 2 complete recoveries, and 26 currently under treatment, according to the report received.

○Ministry of Home Affairs Sanitation Bureau《September 20, Meiji 10 (1877)》
　In Nagasaki, cholera-like symptoms appeared from early this month, but around the 10th, true Asiatic cholera began spreading. From that day through the 17th, there were 78 patients, of whom 18 died, according to a report from the prefectural officials.

○Metropolitan Police Headquarters Document A-24《September 20, Meiji 10 (1877)》
　Guidelines for cholera prevention, as detailed in the separate volume received from the Ministry of Home Affairs, are hereby promulgated.
　　Note: The separate volume follows Ministry of Home Affairs Document B-79 and is omitted here.
