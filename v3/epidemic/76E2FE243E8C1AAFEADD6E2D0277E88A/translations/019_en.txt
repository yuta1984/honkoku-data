(Instruments) Surgical and obstetric instruments, and craftsmen's hand tools should be washed with carbolic acid solution (No. 4).

(Food) Many contaminated items should be discarded. Some items may be sufficient with disinfection methods. Follow the committee's instructions.

(Corpses) Those who died from cholera should be disposed of as quickly as possible, wrapped thoroughly in cotton soaked with carbolic acid solution (No. 5), and healthy people should not be allowed near. A large amount of carbolic acid powder (No. 2) should be placed inside the coffin, and carbolic acid solution should be poured in periodically.

(Section 2) Houses, Ships, Sickrooms, etc.

(Rooms) Rooms with cholera patients and rooms where corpses were placed should be thoroughly fumigated with carbolic acid vapor (No. 7) (crystalline acid should be used) to fill the entire room. After the patient fully recovers or after burial, first remove gold and silver vessels, paintings, colored items, silk goods, etc. from the room (these items should be subjected to appropriate disinfection methods separately), close the windows and doors tightly, light a fire in a brazier, place sulfur on top of it, and close the entrance. After fumigating for six to eight hours, remove the brazier, open the windows and doors, and after a short while, take all items in the room out into the open air and beat and dust them off. Wooden items such as ceilings and fixtures should be doused with carbolic acid (No. 4), washed with soap water, and then exposed to air.
