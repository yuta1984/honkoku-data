　　Cholera Disinfectants and Their Methods

Although the true cause of "cholera" disease and the actual conditions of its spread are not yet clear, it is an established point among various theories, admitting no further doubt, that the disease toxin lodges particularly in the vomit and diarrhea of patients.

Moreover, this vomit and diarrhea does not seem to strengthen infectious toxins immediately upon excretion. After a short time, it falls into fermentation and decay, and only then does it begin to produce a type of infectious disease. Therefore, when vomit and diarrhea are mixed with other decaying matter, the decay occurs extremely rapidly and the generation of disease toxins becomes most severe. When the vomit and diarrhea of "cholera" patients are mixed with other people's excrement and urine (such as disposal into ordinary toilets, garbage pits, sewers, etc.), this greatly intensifies the momentum of its spread.
