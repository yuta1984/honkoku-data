Methods of Using Disinfectants

When implementing disinfection methods, attention should be paid to cleaning: (First) the diarrheal matter, vomit, and clothing, utensils, paper scraps, cloth pieces, etc. contaminated by this vomit and diarrhea, as well as corpses; (Second) the rooms, ship cabins, hospitals, encampments, etc. where patients have slept and stayed; (Third) all unsanitary places such as latrines, garbage dumps, sewers, etc.

(First) Items contaminated with disease toxins

(Excreta) Vessels for receiving vomit and diarrheal matter should have more than one cup of green vitriol mixed with carbolic acid (the sixth formula) placed in them beforehand. After use, they should immediately be taken outdoors, washed clean, and the aforementioned disinfectant should be added again for future use. Vomit and diarrheal matter, water used to wash such vessels, and paper scraps and cloth pieces stained with vomit and diarrhea must not be discarded into ditches, sewers, garbage dumps, rice fields, etc. Moreover, when this vomit and diarrheal matter is mixed with other putrid matter, it particularly intensifies the cultivation of disease toxins, so it must never be thrown into ordinary latrines or mixed with the excrement and urine of healthy people.
