　　　　　Preface to Methods for Nursing Smallpox Patients
　1. This book records practical matters that households dealing with smallpox should keep in mind. It is hoped that by placing this even in remote mountain villages and isolated places far from doctors, it might serve as some assistance in saving lives during times of suffering and difficulty. Since it is intended to be easily understood by common people and women who are originally unlearned, it clarifies its meaning entirely in kana script without adorning it with elaborate rhetoric.
