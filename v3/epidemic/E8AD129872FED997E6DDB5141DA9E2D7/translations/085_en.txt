- Rock fish (meharu)　Non-toxic. There are two varieties, black and red. It may be used throughout for children, patients, and during and after smallpox
　　　　　without harm. However, the skin should not be given
　　　　　because it has strong oily properties. It is good when thoroughly cooked. Also, when grilled and mixed with soy sauce,
　　　　　it is extremely delicious. There is also what is called offshore rockfish. Those that are blackish in color and
　　　　　seven to eight inches long should not be eaten by patients after smallpox. They are toxic. Also,
　　　　　there is what is commonly called seaweed fish or ako-meharu. This is indeed a type of rockfish
　　　　　with a light flavor. Non-toxic. It may be given to patients and children
　　　　　throughout smallpox without harm. However, the skin has strong oily properties,
　　　　　so it should not be given. Its preparation is the same as meharu.
【Left Page】
- Carp (koi)　　　Non-toxic. It promotes urination and milk flow. This fish loosens the spleen and stomach,
　　　　　so it should not be given during smallpox. After twelve days following smallpox,
　　　　　it should be mixed with salted miso, thoroughly cooked, and given to both children and wet nurses.
　　　　　If one is prone to diarrhea after smallpox, avoid this.
- Crucian carp (funa)　Non-toxic. It regulates the interior, moves qi downward, and has the function of supplementing deficiency.
　　　　　However, it should not be used during smallpox. It should be given after twelve days following smallpox. If
　　　　　one is prone to diarrhea, it should be prohibited.
- Loach (dojō)　　Non-toxic. It warms the interior, benefits qi, regulates the spleen and stomach, and stops thirst.
　　　　　However, avoid using it during smallpox. After fifteen days following smallpox,
　　　　　it may be given. If one has diarrhea, it should be avoided.
