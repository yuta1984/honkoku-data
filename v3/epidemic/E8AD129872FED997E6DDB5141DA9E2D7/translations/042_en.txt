1. Once fever develops, parents and attendants should not leave the patient's side. Those with strong fever will have convulsions. At this time, quickly embrace and hold them tightly. If it becomes severe, they may experience dizziness, fainting, and become unconscious. In such cases, bear bile should be dissolved in water and used. Theriac is also somewhat beneficial. Do not randomly use various stimulating medicines. Initial convulsions are called "kyōtō" (shock smallpox) and some are good, while others called "chūaku" (middle evil) are bad. One should receive treatment under a physician's care. Also, do not massage the abdomen or back. When smallpox epidemic is spreading in society, even if someone appears depressed with strong symptoms, do not think it is convulsive disorder and apply acupuncture, moxibustion, or massage.

1. Smallpox differs from other diseases in that it is limited to just twelve days, and these twelve days are divided into four parts, called the four stages. The three days until the eruptions appear completely are called "kenten" (spot appearance), the next three days are called "kishō" (rising swelling), and the next three days are called "kannō" (pus filling). After holding pus, "shūren" (scab formation) takes three days to complete the process. However, after this there is "rakka" (scab falling) which takes three days. The initial fever may be long or short, with no fixed pattern. The later residual toxins may sometimes be absent or may persist for a long time. 1. Once it is known to be smallpox, clean clothing that will not accumulate dirt should be provided. Even for nobles and high-ranking people, if the smallpox has dense eruptions, undergarments should be made of red pongee or red cotton.
