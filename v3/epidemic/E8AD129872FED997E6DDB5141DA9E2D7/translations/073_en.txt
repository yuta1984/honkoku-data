- Chinese yam　Also called long yam, also called wild yam. It is non-toxic. During smallpox, from beginning to end,
　　　　　it should be mixed with salted miso and well-cooked before giving. Also, salt-grilled or miso-grilled
　　　　　preparations are acceptable. However, grated yam soup should be avoided. Also, those with eye diseases after smallpox
　　　　　should be prohibited from eating it. There are also Buddha's palm yam (round yam) and small yams. Their efficacy is
　　　　　the same as long yam. Also, their bulbils are called mukago. Their efficacy is the same as
　　　　　the parent plant.
- Taro　Chinese potato　Both varieties should not be given during smallpox.
　　　　　Also, for those without residual toxicity from eye diseases, after thirty-seven days, both wet nurses and children may

　　　　　be given them without harm. Mix with salted miso and cook well until soft before giving.
- Daikon radish　The leaves are also called daikon greens. Both are non-toxic. This plant has many varieties.
　　　　　It may be used in all four seasons without harm. Either boiled, or steamed, or mixed with salted miso,
　　　　　or mixed with fish, it should be given. During smallpox, it may be used from beginning to end without harm.
　　　　　It should be given to both children and wet nurses. It should not be given raw.
- Carrot　The young leaves may be well-boiled and mixed with soy sauce to give,
　　　　　it is said. Also, the root moves qi downward and loosens the interior. During smallpox,
　　　　　from the fifth to ninth day, it should not be given in large amounts. After the tenth day,
　　　　　it should be well-cooked and given to both children and wet nurses.
　　　　　There is no harm.
