It administers the two paths of blood vessels and controls the Small Intestine Prefecture. It is an extremely warm land. This place is the royal capital where the main character resides. The capital of the north is called the Kidney Palace. It governs winter and water, honors all black-colored things, and prefers salty foods. It administers the various paths of bones, marrow, teeth, and fangs, and controls the Bladder Prefecture. It is a land excellent in cold qi. The central capital is called the Spleen Palace. It governs earth and the seasonal transitions, honors all yellow-colored things, and prefers sweet foods. It administers the two paths of skin and flesh and controls the Stomach Prefecture. It is a land of warmth and humidity.

Throughout the country there are nine gates in total: two gates each for eyes, ears, and nose, plus the mouth gate, the water passage gate, and the grain passage gate. Such Human Body Kingdoms exist in countless numbers, and among them there is a distinction between female countries and male countries. Female countries follow male countries, and male countries show favor to female countries. Female countries are considered yin and male countries yang. The two countries of yin and yang unite in harmony to give birth to new countries, continuously generating life without cease. Each country has a fixed lifespan, and when the lifespan is exhausted, it perishes. Sometimes it is a hundred years, sometimes ninety or eighty years, and in decline, fifty or sixty years constitute one lifespan.

However, there are bandits called diseases that invade and torment countries, and when the degree of military campaigns goes astray, countries may perish without waiting for their natural lifespan to end. There are also distinctions of warrior, farmer, artisan, and merchant classes. Among warrior countries, some govern learning and teaching, some are devoted to martial valor and economics, and some specialize in medical arts and pharmacology. Farmer countries, though having some minor differences, generally make physical labor their duty. Artisan countries make craftsmanship their duty, and merchant countries make buying, selling, and bustling commerce their duty. Within these there are also smaller divisions of noble and base, rich and poor, strong and weak.

In former times, each country was pure and honest, but in these latter days, the national customs have all declined. Warriors, farmers, artisans, and merchants tend to neglect their own countries' duties; noble countries oppress base countries, rich countries despise poor countries, and strong countries control weak countries.
