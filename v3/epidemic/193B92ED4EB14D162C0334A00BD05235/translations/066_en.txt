The spies returned after two or three days and reported: "The enemy officers and soldiers are proud from their recent victory, and moreover, they despise and look down on us for not attacking, engaging only in games with no sign of vigilance." Hearing this, Raireki also dispelled his doubts and said, "Then tonight we shall select elite troops and launch a night attack," giving orders to all soldiers to prepare. At this time, on the medicine army's side, Junchoku gathered the generals and secretly said: "Tonight I saw smoke from cooking provisions rising from the bandit army's area. They seem to think our recent display of carelessness through our stratagem is real, and appear ready to launch a night attack. This is exactly the plan I had in mind. We shall strike down the bandit generals tonight. First, let us assign the formations." He divided several thousand cavalry each between Enju-gan and Kakkun-ka-jutsu-bu-to and sent them toward the designated signal positions.

【Left Page】
In the camp, they deliberately lit beacon fires, left the banners and insignia standing as they were, and had the soldiers remove their armor and appear to be sleeping, waiting for the enemy to approach. Meanwhile, in the bandit camp, they divided their eight thousand-plus cavalry into two forces: one force with Raireki-hare-taka as general to guard the camp, and another with Idoku-nuke-kane himself as general to launch a night attack on the medicine army. They bound their horses' bits, put gags in their mouths, and around the third watch left their camp, proceeding directly to the medicine army's front lines. Suddenly raising a great battle cry, they charged in with reckless abandon. The medicine army was greatly surprised, with none daring to fight back, abandoning their armor and provisions and fleeing helter-skelter. The bandit army overran the medicine camp in one sweep, and riding their momentum, pursued the fleeing enemy, fighting over the scattered weapons and provisions.
