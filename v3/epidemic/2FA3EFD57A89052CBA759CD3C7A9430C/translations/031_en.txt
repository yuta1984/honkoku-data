On the other hand, we observed that streptococci isolated from the cardiac blood of scarlet fever patients showed different actions against the same complement-fixing substance.
　Could we not find from this a standard point for establishing a more rational classification method for streptococci?
　This type of research, which has hardly been conducted until now, requires further long-term pursuit before declaring a definitive method. Nevertheless, from the few experiments conducted with this kind of thinking, whether in the future or past, we believe that streptococci have the same relationship as vibrios or spirilla. In other words, we think that there is not one type of streptococcus, but rather many types that should be distinguished from each other, just as cholera vibrio is distinguished from Vibrio Metchnikovi. In our opinion, streptococci encountered during the course of the same disease, for example scarlet fever, can represent many types; on the other hand, the same type of streptococcus can be encountered during the course of clinically different diseases.
