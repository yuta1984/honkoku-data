Chapter VI　Prophylactic Vaccination with Sensitized Pathogens・・・・・・・・74
　I. Immunity conferred by vaccines and sera; advantages and disadvantages. Principles of sensitized vaccines. Manufacturing techniques: plague, typhoid, cholera prophylactic vaccines. Comparison of various typhoid prophylactic vaccines.
　Sensitized rabies prophylactic vaccine. Its manufacturing method by A. Marie. Completely harmless; reliable action; rapid, lasting.
　Sensitized dysentery vaccine. Dopter's research, conclusion shows absence of toxicity, reliable immunity, rapid, lasting.
　Sensitized diphtheria vaccine. Its manufacturing method by Theobald Smith; its action harmless and lasting. Literature.
　II. Sensitized bile-treated tubercle bacilli. Research by Calmette and Guérin on prophylactic vaccination of calves. F. Meyer's experiments on commonly sensitized tubercle bacilli; weak toxicity and prophylactic efficacy; therapeutic trials in guinea pigs and humans.
　Sensitized tuberculin. Research by Vallée and Guinard in cattle and humans.
　Sensitized pneumococci. Prophylactic and therapeutic experiments by Levy and Aoki. Advantages over ordinary pneumococci.
　Sensitized streptococci. Marxer's experiments on rabbits. Trials of prophylactic and therapeutic treatment in pregnant women by Lévy and Hamm.
　Sensitized sheep pox. Research by Bridré and Boquet. Proof of reliable efficacy; limitation to sheep flocks. Compulsory vaccination with sensitized virus in sheep in Algeria and Egypt. Amount distributed from 1913 to 1926 was approximately 16 million.
　Sensitized typhoid live vaccine. Experiments by Metchnikoff and Besredka on chimpanzees.
　Sensitized paratyphoid prophylactic vaccine. Its advantages. Necessity of removing all traces of free serum from sensitized vaccines.
　Mode of action of sensitized vaccines. Comparative research by Garbat and Meyer. Literature.
