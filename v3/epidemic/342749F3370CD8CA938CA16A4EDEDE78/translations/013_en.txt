Whooping cough is a disease that develops from common cold and can be contagious, so one should pay careful attention and take preventive measures.
○This patient (a child suffering from whooping cough), when coughing, makes a distinctive sound during inhalation, producing spasmodic coughs that resemble a crow's cry, greatly startling those nearby. After expelling a large amount of sticky phlegm, the coughing stops, and during the interval when no new phlegm is produced, the patient child shows no particular signs of distress and remains as peaceful as usual.
