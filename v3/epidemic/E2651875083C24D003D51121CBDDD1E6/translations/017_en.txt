Sixteenth: The signs of non-toxic, benign pox are that its shape is round with a slightly concave top, containing white fluid, with the periphery showing a slight reddish tinge, and having absolutely no odor. This is called true cowpox, which is what should be used as vaccine material.

　　○According to one theory, the characteristic of true pox is that 2-3 days after vaccination, the area around the vaccination site becomes deep red and swells hard. According to another theory, true cowpox develops gradually after vaccination, and even around the third day, there are often cases that remain doubtful and difficult to determine. Also, from the 6th to the 12th day, the fever level is unstable, sometimes vigorous, sometimes mild. This is presumably a sign that the pox toxin has been absorbed and the whole body is responding. Also, from the 8th to the 11th day, the area around the vesicle necessarily shows a vivid, active red halo. And the disappearance of this red color also recedes repeatedly, just as when it first appears. Also, the progression is most rapid
