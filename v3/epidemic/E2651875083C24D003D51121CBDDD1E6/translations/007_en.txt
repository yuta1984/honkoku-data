When the conversation turned to vaccination, I asked: "The physicians of your country all claim that cowpox greatly surpasses human pox vaccination, and that not one child in ten million will be harmed. Chinese writings also enthusiastically praise the benefits of cowpox. However, when your ships brought cowpox vaccine in previous years and applied it to various infants in our country several times, not once did we achieve successful inoculation. The material you have brought this time is also the same - what is the reason for this? We have long considered this regrettable." Dr. Mohnike said: "Indeed, there are such circumstances. Even when the vaccine is sealed to prevent contact with outside air, sea transport invariably takes more than thirty days, and with the hurried preparations before and after, by the time we attend to matters after arriving at the trading post, fifty days cannot help but pass. Due to this, the vaccine perhaps becomes stale. This is

Even scabs that are already several months old can still produce good vaccination results. Based on this consideration, how about using scabs for cowpox as well?" Dr. Mohnike greatly agreed, and this year he imported cowpox scabs anew and first tested them on three children. One of those children indeed produced beautiful pox. The other two children did not achieve successful vaccination. Thereupon, taking that pox fluid and inoculating three children again, all developed pox. Their condition was beautiful, red, and lively - the finest quality pox. Afterward, taking this and transplanting it from one to another, spreading and multiplying it, we verified through experiment that his words contained not the slightest falsehood. Ah!
