Article 23: The period from inoculation to scab formation varies slightly depending on the nature of the case, but the duration is generally as follows:
　Day 2: A small red spot like a flea bite appears at the needle site, with the needle wound mark still visible above the spot. This red spot sometimes appears on the third day.
　
　Day 3: The red spot gradually becomes more prominent and produces minute small vesicles. These vesicles sometimes appear on the fourth day.
　
　Day 4: The small vesicles take on a somewhat pale reddish color.
　Day 5: The small vesicles gradually enlarge, the vesicle head becomes flat with raised edges and the center slightly depressed. On this day, generally mild systemic

　　reactions occur.
　Day 6: The vesicles become increasingly larger, the central depression becomes more pronounced and contains some thin fluid.
　
　Day 7: Thin fluid fills the vesicle, making it bright and luminous with a reddish tinge, the outer edge being particularly bright red.
　
　Day 8: The vesicles become increasingly bright and white, swelling with flat, transparent fluid.
　
　Days 9, 10, 11: The vesicles become increasingly larger, filled completely with transparent fluid, the central depression disappears, inflammatory red halos form around the vesicles, and systemic reactions occur to some degree, as described in Article 7.
