　　　　　Experience
1. A 3-year-old child who habitually suffered from convulsions: A Western doctor inoculated
　six sites on one arm, all of which swelled well and suppurated. On the eighth day, the child
　suddenly developed convulsions. Though left alone for a while, they did not stop, so I administered
　musk and calomel, 4 parts each, with 32 parts sugar as a powder medicine,
　divided into four doses to be taken in one day. Thereafter, the convulsions gradually
　subsided and the child recovered.
1. A 2-year-old child who habitually developed convulsions when having slight fever:
　Inoculated three sites on one arm, all swelled well and suppurated, with no
　convulsions whatsoever.
