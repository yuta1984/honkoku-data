　Method 4: Manufacture a pen-like tube from glass. Its shape and size are approximately like this needle diagram, with the lower end being tubular and the upper end almost like the tip of a writing brush. Place pox fluid inside the sharp tip, seal the narrow opening of the tip with pitch, and seal the lower opening by melting it shut. Then again place this in a "tinplate" tube, seal the tube opening, and once more place it in a wooden tube,

Apply this directly by rubbing it on the wound, or apply it to a needle head before insertion.

　Method 5: Take pox scabs and place them in a small-mouthed glass bottle, seal the opening with a glass stopper, coat and seal the rim with melted pitch, also wrap the opening externally with bladder, wrap with paper, and store in a wooden tube. However, the bottle should be small according to the amount of scabs used. It is preferable to have little empty space.
