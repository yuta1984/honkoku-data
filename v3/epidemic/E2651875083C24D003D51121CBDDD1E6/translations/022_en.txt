　　After the 14th day, some people develop small vesicles in other areas, but this causes no harm.
Article 22: Generally, if pox appears in one location, it is sufficient. However, because it may disappear or be insufficient for obtaining vaccine material, one should inoculate five or six locations.
　　○According to one theory, for a one-year-old child, six locations should be used, and the number of inoculation sites should gradually increase according to age, reaching even twenty or thirty locations. When this theory was questioned to Moore, he said that generally cowpox is sufficient to achieve full effectiveness if it develops in one location, and inoculating dozens of locations is truly useless.
