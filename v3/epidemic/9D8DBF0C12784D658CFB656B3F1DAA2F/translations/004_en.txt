However, since it takes approximately one month to transport the vaccine from Java to Nagasaki, the potency of the vaccine weakens or becomes completely ineffective during this time, making inoculation impossible. Therefore, its efficacy diminished and no immune response was ever achieved. Recently, however, a Nagasaki physician named Narabayashi Sōken consulted with a visiting Dutch person named Mohnike and had him bring the vaccine material. After inoculating three children in Nagasaki in the seventh month of last year (Year of the Rooster, 1857), it spread to various provinces. In the winter of the same year, in the eleventh month, the Lord of Saga implemented vaccination in his domain and had the vaccine material brought to Edo.

Since then, we too have obtained this vaccine and tried inoculating several hundred people. As discussed in Western books, we have not seen a single failure. The excellence of this cowpox is that even when several hundred people live in one room, no one except the inoculated child becomes infected, and no eruptions appear anywhere else on the body other than the inoculation site. In Western countries in ancient times, severe smallpox epidemics occurred and many children died from it, so they inoculated mild smallpox material to help them avoid severe smallpox.
