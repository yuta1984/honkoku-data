September 2nd
1. The senior retainers of the Second Palace sent word calling for one chief retainer, so Lord Kuranosuke proceeded there
1. There was official business to discuss with Matsueda Senbei, chief retainer of Hasunōike; Lord Zensaemon went to that place today. Since Senbei will return home in the near future, a modest gift of a three-shō sake barrel and one box of delicacies was brought as congratulations
1. Ureshino Zenemon was appointed to serve concurrently as steward for Lord Nishinooka Kisaburō
1. A silver merchant named Taniyama Nakazō from Nagasaki was staying at this place, so today he was summoned to Sakuraoka and received a meal in the Komatsu room. The chief administrator-advisor and treasury officials paid their respects
　　　　September 3rd
1. Incense offering on behalf of the lord to Muryōji temple; the messenger duty officer served
