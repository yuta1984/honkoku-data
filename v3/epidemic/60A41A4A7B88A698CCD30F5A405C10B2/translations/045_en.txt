Guidelines for implementing disinfection methods

Article 1: Municipalities shall prepare the following pharmaceuticals and equipment for use in disinfecting homes of infectious disease patients, and shall transport them to patient homes as necessary.
However, equipment available at the patient's home that can be used need not be transported.

1. Equipment
1. Transport vehicle or carrying equipment  2. Large tub (capable of holding three to or more)  3. Small tub  4. Horse bucket (5 sho capacity)  5. Watering can  6. Hand bucket  7. Ladle  8. Pot for boiling disinfection  9. Measuring cup (1 sho) funnel  10. Measuring glass (500 grams)  11. Stirring rod  12. Disinfection clothing and hat  13. Footwear  14. Cleaning rags and hand towels  15. Broom  16. Rake  17. Equipment for transporting waste  18. Nail puller, nails, hammer, can opener  19. Pot, firewood (with matches)  20. Hoe, saw, mat, rope, bathtub

2. Pharmaceuticals
1. Quicklime  2. Carbolic acid  3. Hydrochloric acid  4. Cresol water

3. Other necessary equipment and pharmaceuticals

Article 2: Disinfection workers must strictly observe the following items during disinfection work:
1. Must not eat, drink, or smoke
2. Must not leave the disinfection area without permission
3. When hands, feet, disinfection clothing, etc. become contaminated with pathogens, they must be disinfected immediately
4. Must take care not to scatter dust and debris
