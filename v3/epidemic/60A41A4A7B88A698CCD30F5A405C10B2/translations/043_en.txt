(2) Ceiling disinfection should be performed only when it is recognized that contamination by pathogens has occurred due to mass occurrence of flies or similar circumstances.

(Ho) Clothing, bedding, fabric pieces, etc. should be subjected to steam disinfection, and other equipment and items should also be appropriately disinfected.

(5) Toilets, toilet fixtures, sewage tanks, sewage ditches, garbage pits, and wells (water tanks)

(I) For toilet ceilings, interior walls (including window screens), footboards, partitions, urinals, doors and their knobs, carbolic acid water should be sufficiently sprayed or wiped with the same medicinal solution.

On the underside of footboards, surfaces, intake openings and covers, interior wainscoting, exterior wainscoting, and foundations, carbolic acid water should be sprayed.

For disinfection of feces pits and urine pits, quicklime powder should be used. However, lime milk may also be used depending on the contents.

Toilet fixtures should be disinfected with lime milk.

The contents of washbasins should be poured directly into feces pits or urine pits, but this should be done before disinfecting the waste pools.

Washbasins and washing areas should be disinfected with carbolic acid water.

Washing ladles and towel racks should be disinfected with carbolic acid water.

Toilet brooms, dusters, sandals, paper boxes (including paper), and hand towels should be immersed in carbolic acid water or incinerated.

(Ro) For disinfection of sewage tanks and sewage ditches, quicklime powder or lime milk should be used.

Garbage pits should be disinfected with lime milk, and their contents should be incinerated.

(Ha) Wells (water tanks) should be disinfected with quicklime powder regardless of whether they are for drinking water or general use.
