(Otsu) Degree of disinfection for typhus, smallpox, scarlet fever, and diphtheria

(1) Kitchen (2) Rooms with little risk of pathogen contamination (3) Rooms with some risk of pathogen contamination
The above should follow the procedures of (Ko)

(4) Rooms with high risk of pathogen contamination
(I) Pillars, thresholds, corridors, and tatami mats should be sprayed with carbolic acid water or wiped with the same medicinal solution as in (Ko)
(Ro) Disinfection of clothing, bedding, fabric pieces, and other equipment and items should follow the procedures of (Ko)

(Ha) Doors, shoji screens, fusuma sliding doors, walls, ceilings, header beams, and the backs of plaques should all be sprayed with carbolic acid water. However, hanging scrolls, plaque surfaces, and other items at risk of damage should be thoroughly exposed to sunlight.
