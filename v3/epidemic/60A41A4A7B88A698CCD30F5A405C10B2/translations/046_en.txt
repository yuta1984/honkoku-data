Article 3: When arriving at a patient's home, before entering the house, one must first put on disinfection clothing and hat and change footwear.

Article 4: Before beginning disinfection, investigate the following items to determine the degree and order of disinfection, thereby ensuring nothing is overlooked:
1. Number of family members and cohabitants, and their ages (young and old)  2. Severity of illness  3. Pattern of disease outbreak  4. Room where patient lay down and got up  5. Furniture and articles that came into contact with patient  6. Those who engaged in nursing care  7. Toilet used by patient and toilet equipment and diapers used  8. Vessels and places where waste was washed  9. Places where waste was buried or discarded  10. Contaminated furniture and articles  11. Presence or absence of leftover food and drink  12. Other matters deemed necessary

Article 5: Along with the investigation in the previous article, prepare the following items:
1. Boiling disinfection  2. Hot water for dissolving pharmaceuticals and wiping, etc.  3. Disinfectants  4. Bath water for bathing during and after completion of disinfection

Article 6: Toilets and objects currently contaminated with pathogens should generally be treated by the following methods. However, those who have engaged in this disinfection must not be allowed to engage in disinfection of other areas unless they have first disinfected their hands, feet, and other parts.
1. Toilets
(A) Add lime milk equal to one-fourth or more of the amount of excrement to the cesspool and stir carefully
(B) Pour sufficient lime milk around the cesspool
(C) Floor, walls, pillars, doors of the toilet (pay particular attention to door handles)
