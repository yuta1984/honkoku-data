Route of transmission: Transmission occurs through direct contact with patients, or indirectly through utensils, toys, letters, air, etc. The entry points of the pathogen are likely the respiratory system, skin wounds, and digestive tract mucosa, especially the throat.

Age of infection: Children aged 2 to 7 years are most commonly affected by this disease. Adults may also become infected.

Symptoms: Incubation period - 1 to 7 days

Onset: Begins with chills or shivering, then body temperature reaches 39 to 40 degrees Celsius, pulse increases, accompanied by nausea, vomiting, palpitations, general fatigue, and headache. The patient complains of throat pain and suffers from difficulty swallowing. When examining by opening the mouth, pronounced redness and swelling of the throat are observed.

Next, the characteristic red rash of this disease appears on the neck and spreads throughout the body within 1-2 days. However, the chin and nose areas remain pale. During this period,

examining the condition of the tongue reveals it to be deep red with an uneven surface, resembling a strawberry tongue. The rash persists for 3-4 days before gradually beginning to fade, and after one week the skin returns to its normal appearance, followed by desquamation (often membrane-like). Body temperature gradually decreases along with the fading of the rash. Thus, complete recovery occurs at the end of the fourth week after onset.

Immunity: When contracting this disease once, immunity is usually acquired, but sometimes infection occurs 3-4 times.
