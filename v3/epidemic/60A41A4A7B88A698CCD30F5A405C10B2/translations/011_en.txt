Definition of Infectious Diseases
　Infectious diseases refer to ailments that occur when living microorganisms (extremely minute organic bodies that can only be recognized through a microscope) enter the human body or animal body and develop and multiply, presenting more or less severe general symptoms.

　Microorganisms　Bacteria (plant) ... such as typhoid bacillus and dysentery bacillus
　　　　　　　　　Protozoa (animal) ... such as "malaria parasites" which cause "malaria," and "dysentery amoeba" which causes dysentery prevalent in tropical regions
