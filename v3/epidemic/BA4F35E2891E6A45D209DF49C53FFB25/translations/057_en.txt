The Hashimoto family originates from the Takeda clan of Honshu. During the Juei era, Yasuda Yoshisada was appointed to the position of maki (ranch/pasture administrator) in Enshu Province. Among his clan members, those who received fiefs in Hashimoto village took this as their family name. In medieval times, a person called Geki moved to live in the old capital of Ishu Province, personally received the transmission of Chisokusai Tokumoto, and became a physician. This is considered the ancestor of Hakuju. Therefore, several surviving writings of Tokumoto are preserved in Hakuju's house. Zen'ya, a descendant of Geki, moved to live in Ichikawa village. From this point, four generations later came Hakuju. Hakuju once sought instruction from Takamuro Shozan. His son named Un is my son-in-law. Therefore, I am not an outsider to Hakuju. Here, Hakuju has grievances regarding smallpox and measles...
