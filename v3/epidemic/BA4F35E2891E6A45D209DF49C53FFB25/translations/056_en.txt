The transmission of measles is no different from smallpox, so prevention methods are also no different from smallpox. However, while smallpox constantly circulates through countries without cease, measles is transmitted from foreign countries each time, generally with epidemics occurring at intervals of about twenty years. Therefore, if one can prevent just three or four epidemics in a lifetime, even a life destined to die from measles could maintain a lifespan of sixty to seventy years, making it easier to prevent than smallpox.

As recorded in the above articles, if one pays attention only during times of smallpox and measles epidemics, these are diseases that one will definitely never contract in one's lifetime and can easily avoid. Since ancient times, no one has explained even a word about smallpox and measles being completely due to contagious miasma. The falsehood in medical texts that they are merely seasonal epidemics caused by celestial movements that trigger fetal toxins has finally become a common custom in society, making it a major calamity once in a human lifetime. Medicine is not limited to only giving medicine to the sick; there is also the teaching of ancient people about treating those who are not yet ill. Without regard for my own shallow learning, I only hope that people will come to know the evil of the custom of becoming familiar with smallpox and that it will become a custom to avoid smallpox.
