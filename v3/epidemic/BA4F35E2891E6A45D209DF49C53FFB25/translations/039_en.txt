In Japan, with each epidemic, everyone falls ill completely and the toxic qi dissipates, so it comes from foreign countries. For this reason, when preventing smallpox, prevention of measles is naturally included within it. Kokuji Dandoku-ron Appendix
　　　On the Smallpox Deity

Worshipping the smallpox deity was completely unknown in ancient times. Smallpox first came to our imperial court in the autumn of Tenpyō 7, year of the metal boar, during Emperor Shōmu's reign. Since then, each time it spread there were ceremonies to ward off epidemics such as era changes, great amnesties, offerings to various shrines, and great purifications, but there was never any worship of a smallpox deity. Indeed, from the Shoku Nihongi cited in the previous section through the Northern and Southern Courts—over six hundred years—the absence of any mention of a smallpox deity in numerous records is clear evidence. I believe that because this malevolent disease spreads and causes tremendous deaths, people came to think that if they revered and worshipped it as a deity, they might more easily escape this calamity—no small benefit.
