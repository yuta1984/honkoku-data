…and rescue the people of that land. The Analects state: "To kill a man in order to bring peace to the people — such killing is permissible. To chastise a state while showing love toward its people — such chastisement is permissible."

Four: Should war arise, and those who delight in disorder come to attack our land, it is a situation in which one has no choice but to respond. In such circumstances, through the internal harmony and unity of the people, the countless subjects who have pledged their loyalty to the lord must together with their ruler stand firm even unto death, united without a single divided heart. Building upon this unwavering solidarity, one must further make use of geographical advantage, construct fortifications, and devote oneself fully to military preparedness — when one fights under such conditions, defeat must not come to pass. As commentary states: "It is only when a state's governance and administration falls into disorder and becomes vulnerable that an enemy commander will make his move."

【Left Page】

The foolish commanders of this world do not understand the importance of the harmony and unity of the people. In times of peace, they utterly forget the possibility of war and disorder; they do not maintain a force of soldiers proportionate to their needs; and when a sudden crisis arrives, they fall into a panic like a man startled by water flooding into his ear while asleep. Thereupon they resort to battle, wondering vaguely if perhaps they might win. This is no different from trying to make a rootless tree burst into bloom and bear fruit — utterly futile.
