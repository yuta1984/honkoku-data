**Article Seven: Signal Flags**

▲ Five flags are assigned to the advance guard, five to the center formation (naka-sonae), and five to the bannermen (hatamoto). The signal colors are established as follows: a black flag for moats and ditches (horikiri); a blue flag for thickets (shigemi); a yellow flag for marshes (numa); a white flag for rivers (kawa); and a red flag when the enemy appears (teki-de). These five colors are established as signals in advance. The advance guard raises the appropriate colored flag to inform the center formation, the center formation in turn raises its signal flag to inform the bannermen, the bannermen receive the signal, and in this way each unit responds in succession down the line.

---

**Article Eight: Raising Signal Fires (Noroshi)**

【Left Page】

▲ Signal fires (noroshi) are likewise a form of signal that can be used at any time. During the day, they are observed by their smoke; at night, by the light of their flames. Wolf dung and moxa (mogusa) are combined together. With this mixture, the smoke rises swiftly and to a great height. On a mountain ridge or elevated ground that is clearly visible from the bannermen's position, one piles straw, chaff and dry brush, and firewood, then places the above mixture within it and sets it alight.

---

**Article Nine: Rope Cords (Hoso-biki)**

▲ Every soldier in the entire force shall have one length of blue cord (ao-hoso-biki) attached to their waist. It is used for setting up shelters, crossing rivers, scaling castle walls, and all manner of tasks.
