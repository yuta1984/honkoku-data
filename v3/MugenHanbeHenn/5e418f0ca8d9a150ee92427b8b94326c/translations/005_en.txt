**[Right Page]**

(Continuing the commentary) It is not limited to kings alone — those who bring ruin upon themselves are exceedingly many.

**Article Two:** There are always many in this world who destroy their own country, not through defeat in battle against an enemy, but through their own actions. This is solely because they make the pursuit of profit and personal gain their primary concern. Profit is truly the beginning of disorder. When one acts out of the pursuit of profit, resentment multiplies greatly. When one follows the principle of Heaven, even without seeking profit, one will naturally find that profit is not absent. Yet when one takes profit as the foundation and does not know the true path of righteousness, great harm follows. In any case, the realm and the state do not belong to any single person.

**[Left Page]**

The realm belongs, broadly speaking, to every person within it. One should share all matters of the realm and state together with the people: when the people suffer, the ruler suffers; when the people prosper, the ruler prospers. If one loves the people of the nation as one's own children, then the people in turn will hold their ruler dear, as they would a father or mother. They will look up to their ruler as they look up to the sun and moon. It is by making the joys of those above and below equal that one who gains the realm and the state may hold it for long. But when one monopolizes the profits of the realm and believes the realm belongs to oneself alone, one loses the realm and the state through divine retribution. How sorrowful this is.
