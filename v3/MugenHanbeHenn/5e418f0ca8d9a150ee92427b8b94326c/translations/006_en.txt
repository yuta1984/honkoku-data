【Right Page】

How lamentable! The Analects (Lun Yu) state: "If the ten thousand regions have committed transgressions, let the guilt rest upon my person alone." When the people of the realm fall into suffering and are condemned to punishment despite having committed no wrongdoing, this is not the fault of the people — it is the sole transgression of the ruler. Thus, a wise and virtuous ruler is earnest in reproaching himself while being lenient in reproaching others. Furthermore, it is said: "If the four seas fall into poverty and distress, the blessings of Heaven shall come to a permanent end." This passage warns that if one fails to rectify the suffering and misery of the people throughout the realm, one acts against the mandate of Heaven; consequently, the blessings of Heaven grow thin, the ruler's Heaven-granted fortune comes to a permanent end, and the realm and state shall be lost. The warnings contained therein are without limit.

【Left Page】

Three: Should war arise, if the commander of an enemy state delights in wickedness and torments the countless people of that land — indulging in the way of profit and selfish desire, accumulating splendor and glory for himself alone, sparing no expense in his extravagance, exploiting the people, levying various forms of additional corvée labor upon the farmers, inflating land assessments and crop inspections, demanding harsh tax collection even when the standing crops fall short of expectations, causing tens of thousands to suffer hunger and cold, bringing many to death despite having committed no crime, and exhausting the populace through hasty and severe punishments and governance — such that the people lament and grieve, wondering on what day they shall finally be delivered from ruin, one should (take the opportunity to) destroy that wicked commander, and (deliver) the people…
