**Secret Transmission of Military Law, Volume One**
**The Scroll of Great Peace**

When a ruler is wise and virtuous, there is nothing that will prevent the realm from being governed and at peace. Therefore, one who possesses benevolence has no enemies. A true gentleman does not engage in battle. Yet if he must fight, he will certainly prevail. He does not attack enemies out of personal interest or selfish desire, but strikes down those who act against the principle of Heaven. He punishes the tyrants Jie and Zhou, who have been abandoned by both Heaven and humanity — mere solitary despots. Nevertheless, it is not that Tang and Wu executed them. Rather, it is Jie and Zhou themselves who bring about their own ruin. The commentary states: "Kings Jie and Zhou..."
