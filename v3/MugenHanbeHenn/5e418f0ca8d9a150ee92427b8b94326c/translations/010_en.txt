【Right Page】

…Even if a person happens to be a favorite or intimate of the commander, those who bend the proper way of things, disregard the troubles they cause to others, and pursue the path of self-interest and greed for their own personal gain—as well as those who cause the commander to be despised by the people—should be made to commit ritual suicide (seppuku). Make the people prosperous and at ease; show care and compassion even for widowers, widows, orphans, and those living alone; ensure that there is no one reduced to destitution by hunger and cold; and above all, make it so that the people of the world think "there is no lord but this one." Do this in such a way that even from within enemy territory, people defect and pledge loyalty to your side. If you go to war thereafter, you shall not suffer defeat.

Article Seven: There are those who wage war relying on the Timeliness of Heaven (ten no toki). The Timeliness of Heaven refers to choosing auspicious days and hours, determining favorable directions, and making the yin and yang of the four seasons one's foremost consideration…

【Left Page】

…One surrounds the enemy castle and attacks it, yet is unable to take it, spending days in vain and remaining locked in a prolonged stalemate. However, even the Timeliness of Heaven cannot match the Advantage of Terrain (chi no ri). The various elements that constitute the Advantage of Terrain are as follows: the topography is favorable; the castle is steep and formidably fortified; the stone walls are high; the palisades and watchtowers are well-constructed; the moats are deep and layered in multiple rings; bows, firearms, and other weapons and horse equipment are plentiful; provisions are fully stocked; and even the water supply is inexhaustible. Yet even fighting with the Advantage of Terrain, if the hearts of the soldiers are scattered and divided, in the end there are those who abandon the castle, flee, and surrender.
