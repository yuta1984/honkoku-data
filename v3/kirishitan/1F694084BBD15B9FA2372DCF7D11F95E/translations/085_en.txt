The enemies boarded ships that samurai from Arie village were taking to return home, and went back to Arie village. On the same day, when they pressed forward to the rear guard, they set fire to Teppo-machi (Gun Town) and attacked the tower gate in great numbers. At the tower gate, a man named Ikuma Sukenosjo was killed in battle.

Also, many peasants from Hisankai said they would "join our side" and took refuge in the castle, but for some reason, they took about sixty guns and long-handled weapons and fled from behind the walls beside the tower gate to join the enemy side. Because of this, the people of Sankai village were investigated, and over two hundred people were gathered at the prison gate.

According to the tales of the elders of Arima, on the morning of October 26th, they departed from Shimabara and attacked down toward Fukae village, sending Tazaemon, the village headman of Antoku village, as a messenger to the village headman of Fukae village, saying
