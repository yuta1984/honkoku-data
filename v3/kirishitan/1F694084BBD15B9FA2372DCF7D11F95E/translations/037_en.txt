The Shimabara Rebellion Matsukura Record states: "In mid-October, between Arima in the Matsukura Nagato-no-kami domain of Takaki District, Hizen Province, and Amakusa in Higo Province, there is an island called 'Ishima' located just over three ri across the sea, with about twenty houses. However, Christians from within Amakusa, in the Terasawa Hyōgo-no-kami domain, come to this island for consultations. At that time, Ōyano Shirō, the son of Amakusa Jinbei, though only sixteen years old and young, possessed exceptional abilities surpassing all others, and his various techniques were like supernatural arts. Nineteenth day, Kinoe-Tora
Another Shimabara Record states: "Around the nineteenth day of the tenth month, about one hundred peasants gathered in Kuchinotsu village to chant religious prayers. Deputy Yamauchi Koemon and his son Umasuke were both in Kuchinotsu at that time. Umasuke went out on an excursion and heard about this, hurried back and reported this matter to his father Koemon. Koemon was shocked and called the village headman, ordering him to quickly prohibit this.
