Due to such misgovernment, in recent years Christians secretly appeared again in his domain, and those who had definitely converted from Christianity in the past all secretly returned to their faith. Not knowing this at all, years passed, and later it led to a great disturbance in the realm, resulting in his own destruction.

The Christian chronicle states: Around mid-November of Kan'ei 14 (1637), the Shimabara-Amakusa rebellion arose frequently, spreading like pulling teeth from a comb, with urgent messengers and post horses crossing paths day and night without distinction. Investigating its origin, in the land of 60,000 koku granted to Matsukura Nagato-no-kami, he conducted private land surveys and assessed it at 120,000 koku,

imposing tax rates of 50-60% and greedily extracting tribute unreasonably. The peasants, due to years of hard labor, had no children or livestock to sell that year, and wondering what they could use to sustain their lives, rather than face starvation, they rose in rebellion, intending to leave a record for all future generations. They made a person called Amakusa Shirō their general, had him preach Christian doctrine, and devoted themselves single-mindedly to their faith. If anyone voiced opposition, they would cut them down, and they made waist swords, hat insignia, protective amulets, and things called "kurusu" (crosses).
