Those who do not understand this principle and say that practicing good deeds is difficult, even if they call themselves Christians, do so because they have not yet tried and experienced the profound virtuous matters of our Lord Christ's incarnation and passion, and the strong divine power that flows from grace.

Therefore, what do you think it means that our Lord Christ was incarnated, shed His blood, and died through various sufferings? It was solely to level sins and make the path of goodness easy. Pointing to this, as seen in Matthew chapter 1, the angel told Saint Joseph:

"She will bear a son, and you shall call his name Jesus, for he will save his people from their sins." (Matthew 1)

Since the young lord to be born from Saint Mary is the Lord who will deliver all mankind from various evils, He should be called Jesus. Jesus means "helper" in our hearts. Also, the prophet Isaiah in chapter 40 discusses the incarnation of the Helper and declares, "Then the crooked paths shall be made straight, and the rough ways made smooth." This is nothing other than the principle that the path of goodness, which was difficult to practice until that time, would become easy to practice through the Helper's divine power.

Moreover, through the Lord's incarnation, He established and gave us the precious seven sacraments and the grace that flows from them, as well as what is called "Orbils u de Infuza" - the foundation of good works planted by this grace. All of this serves to preserve His commandments easily, lighten the burden of practicing good, bring joy in times of calamity, and strength in times of hardship...
