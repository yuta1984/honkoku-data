　　　Section 2: Answering those who raise doubts about the above matters

○You might say that as seen in Job 16, even upon good people there remain some stains hidden in corners. Listen well to this answer. In the same words of Job it appears "it is as if nothing." Therefore, what remains is not at all for the sake of disaster, but becomes a means to raise up good; not for wrongdoing, but merely as a foundation for wearing a crown; not to become a seed of evil rebellion, nor for falling, but rather to open up fortune. In essence, the remaining stains are for the purpose of reflecting on bodily dangers, harboring humility, and becoming the honor of God's grace and heavenly joy, so their virtue is abundant and profound.

For example, just as even beasts, when they become attached to and serve humans, bring benefit, so too when evil desires are well controlled, they become a great means for seeking good.
