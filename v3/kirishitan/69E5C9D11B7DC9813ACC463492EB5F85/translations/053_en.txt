As the Apostle Saint Paul writes in Romans Chapter 8: "Who then can separate us from the love of Christ? Tribulation? Or distress? Or famine? Or nakedness? Or peril? Or persecution? Or the sword?"

Who could separate us from Christ's great love? Even with the sufferings of hunger, thirst, poverty, disasters, and sword-trials, how could they tear us away? Truly, neither the power of all angels, nor the hardships of death, nor heaven, nor earth, nor hell, nor the power of any other created things can completely separate us from God's great love, as He declares.

Behold, when Saint Lawrence was placed upon the gridiron and said "What cool flames these are!" - what did this mean? It was because he was burning deeply within the flames of divine love. As Chrysostom's words tell us, true love that thinks upon God considers nothing difficult, does not find suffering painful, does not find heavy things burdensome - no death or sword-trial can overcome such perfected love. Moreover, love is like good golden armor - it cannot be pierced by thrusting or shooting, it regards danger as nothing, and makes one unafraid of death. In general, it is clear that love conquers all things.

Such perfected love not only easily overcomes competing hardships, but actually desires to receive even more for the sake of the One we love. Therefore, all people who have attained goodness burn with the desire for martyrdom, and though they deeply wish to shed their blood for the One who shed His blood (Christ), when this wish cannot be fulfilled, they become the sword-bearers of their own bodies, tormenting themselves with the swords of hunger, thirst, cold, heat, hardships, and such, to gain a little...
