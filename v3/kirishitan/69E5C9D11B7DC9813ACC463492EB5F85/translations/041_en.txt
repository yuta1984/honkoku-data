"Seek the Lord while he may be found, call upon him while he is near." This appears in Isaiah 55. Though we cannot see that Lord, now He is near, ready to hear our petitions. Know that if you do not seek His grace now, even when the time of judgment comes and you see Him, He will not listen to our cries at all.

Chapter 3: Concerning Those Who Rely on God's Mercy
　　　　　but Do Not Cease from Sin

Here there are people who, taking God's mercy as their foundation and relying on Christ's Passion, do not reform their evil conduct and live at ease. This delusion must be dispelled. There are those who say, "God hung on the cross precisely for sinners. Since He is truly a Lord of vast mercy, how could He abandon us?" This is also true, but you use that divine compassion as a refuge for doing evil. The Lord hung on the cross in order to destroy sin, yet you make it a means for committing sin. Since it is life given to you by the Lord, even if you had a hundred thousand lives, you should offer them all completely. Yet instead, you begrudge even this brief dewdrop of life and commit outrages against Him - how can this be?

Consider this: The suffering of the Passion that our Lord, who is our helper, endured at the time of His death is exceeded by our present sins, which bring evil to His precious Passion...
