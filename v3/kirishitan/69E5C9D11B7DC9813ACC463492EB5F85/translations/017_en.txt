　　　　　Part One: Here we refute the excuses that evil people make for not turning to good

　　　　Chapter One: Answers to those who postpone the matter of
　　　　　　　　　　rejecting evil and turning toward good

Now, through the teachings of the first volume, most people should understand the reasoning for undertaking virtuous practices in their hearts. However, because evil people have deep karmic obstacles, they concern themselves only with wickedness, avoid the path of virtue, and make unreasonableness into reasonableness. Pointing to this, *Solomon said, "He who seeks to break with a friend constantly seeks pretexts for doing so" (Proverbs 18), and likewise, evil people never fail to seek pretexts in their respective hearts in order to distance themselves from God. Some people think to postpone virtuous practices and delay them until their final moment. Some people understand the path of virtue as only tasteless and difficult, become accustomed to this view, and make no progress.
