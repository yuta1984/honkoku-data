If one simply polishes the Buddha that is inherently present within us, it would be difficult to become a Buddha even after passing through eternity. For example, though crystal stone is said to have light, it does not become a jewel unless it is ground and polished with diamond powder. For example, a mirror is without thought, so it does not retain the forms that appear in it. Human beings too have an original mind that is no less clear than a mirror, but they retain the reflected phenomena and become deluded by the three poisons: greed, anger, and ignorance, as well as pride. In Zen, the saying "arising and falling together with all phenomena, yet not

The Tendai teaching of "three thousand realms in a single moment of consciousness" also takes as its essence that there are three thousand delusions within the one mind. Though this does not specifically concern ordinary people, while there are many who encounter a teacher and attain enlightenment, few are those who truly open up. It is like a one-wheeled cart.
