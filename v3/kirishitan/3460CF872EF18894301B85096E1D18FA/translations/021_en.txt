He reached the extreme of old age and attained high rank. His eldest son was appointed as Matsukura Nagato-no-kami to succeed him without incident, and the gratitude for this was beyond counting. He served faithfully in Edo, never neglecting his duties at court. At one time, among his gunners, he selected a man of moderate cunning and entrusted all matters to this one person alone. Not only matters of provincial administration, but even hereditary retainers and traditional family retainers were made to follow his commands. Regarding this man's abilities, they would secretly discuss nothing but profit, multiplication of gains, and clever schemes from morning till night. As expected, before long disputes arose, and forty-four or forty-five mounted retainers withdrew, all becoming masterless samurai. From then on, this man's influence grew even stronger, and he acted as if he were worth a thousand men. Though there may have been private regrets, it is said that there was not a single person who would catch his eye, pull at his sleeve, and warn him about anything.
