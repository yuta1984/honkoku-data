They opened the gates, formed spear screens, rushing in and out, scattering sparks two or three times, but when openings were exploited, heads were taken from one end to the other. As they were pressed back into the inner bailey, fire arrows were shot from all directions like falling rain. With nowhere to place themselves, they rushed out only to be skewered, cut through, and pursued. Within about two hours the castle fell. Corpses piled up like mountains. Taken by all forces, when the number of heads was recorded, it was 15,000-16,000. Including those who burned to death, were cut down, women and children, the total was over 40,000.
