【Right Page】
How could they sustain their lives? Rather than dying of starvation anyway, they would raise a rebellion and leave it recorded for posterity until the end of time. They made a man called Amano Shirō their general, had him preach Christian doctrine, and devoted themselves single-mindedly to their faith. If anyone voiced dissent, they would cut them down. They used crosses as waist ornaments, hat insignia, and protective charms. Taking advantage of Nagato-no-kami's absence while in Edo attendance, they staged uprisings not only in the castle town but throughout a radius of two to three ri. Having thoroughly prepared provisions, miso, salt, gunpowder and other supplies, they worked day and night to fortify the old castle of Shimabara.

【Left Page】
Generally speaking, this castle had the rough sea to the east and marshes with tidal flows to the west where even horses' hooves could not find footing, with steep rocky cliffs rising on the northern front. Within an area of about eighteen chō long and ten chō wide, they dug triple moats, carved gun emplacements into the hillside, erected a small keep in the main bailey, and constructed outer towers, projecting courtyards, defensive spikes, portable shields, and secondary and tertiary baileys according to their design, then barricaded themselves inside. Their forces numbered thirty-four to thirty-five thousand foot soldiers. Among them were four to five hundred bows capable of shooting even descending arrows, and deer, rabbits and other running creatures, [as well as birds in] the sky...
