Those who remained by the banner that Yoda had erected, considering that there were options regarding that terrain, withdrew to the lower tier. Also, although Nakano Chūzaemon, the banner magistrate of the vanguard, was wounded, the rōnin Okamoto Yasuemon advanced the banner toward the main citadel. However, since Okamoto was also wounded, the left-hand banner did not enter the inner citadel.

Tachibana Sakon-no-shōgen Tadashige's memorandum states: "Due to bad weather, the general assault planned for the 26th was postponed and rescheduled for the 28th. However, on the 27th, Arima Gemba reported that 'the enemy has withdrawn from Nabeshima's outer citadel.' Since Gemba reported what he had observed from his position, when light infantry were sent out from Nabeshima's forces to confirm, it was indeed found that they had withdrawn. Lord Izu and Lord Samon also observed from Nabeshima's artificial hill and, seeing that the enemy had withdrawn from Nabeshima's outer citadel, ordered that siege works be constructed against the outer citadel and the river embankment. Immediately thereafter, both envoys went to Hida-no-kami's location, and Lord Etchū also came, conveying the orders from Lord Izu and Lord Samon: 'Since the enemy has withdrawn from Nabeshima's outer citadel, advance the siege works.' Even if the enemy should resist when advancing the siege works, Lord Etchū declared, 'Even though our numbers are few, it matters not—station siege work guards in shifts.' Both Lord Etchū and Hida-no-kami agreed, summoned me, and conveyed these instructions, so I moved the siege works of the third bailey closer. Should there be combat with the enemy when advancing siege works against the outer citadel..."
