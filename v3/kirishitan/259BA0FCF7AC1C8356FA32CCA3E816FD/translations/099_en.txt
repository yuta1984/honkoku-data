"It was improper to let them climb up from there," said Tazaemon, and he drove them all down completely. Hachirōemon's foot soldiers wore haori with stepped patterns of ladders and white. Then, Lord Shimozono Sanjūrō and Lord Sugiwara Shiheē, who had been stationed by the Shogunate, heard of this and especially came to provide reassurance. At that time, when Lord Shiheē was climbing and became thirsty, Tazaemon offered water from the water bottle that he had his servant carry at his waist, which greatly satisfied the lord. Lord Sugiwara bore a red pampas grass standard, and Lord Shimozono bore a purple pointed standard. It is said that they all bore only decorative mother garments.

Also stated: From early morning of the same day, they assembled forces separated by the stone wall on the side of the lookout at the main castle entrance. From within the side area, they threw stones and cast out swords and short swords, defending strongly. At that time, Tazaemon, Hyōemon, and Jinemon gathered together and stood lined up near the base of the wall, when stones were thrown diagonally, striking Tazaemon's cheek guard and mouth protection, bouncing back and stopping. Kuchidan'emon was struck on the middle part of his head with something flat, which was crushed, but none of these hit their bodies, and soon swords and short swords were being thrown out from within.
