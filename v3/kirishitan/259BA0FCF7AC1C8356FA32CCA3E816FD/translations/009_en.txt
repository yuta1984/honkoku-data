They opened holes to peer into the outer bailey, and since they could see all the way through to the castle interior, Arima Gemba-no-kami's advance guard continued firing muskets. The enemy in the outer bailey could not endure this and retreated to the second bailey. Regarding this matter, the samurai of Gemba-no-kami's advance guard sent a messenger to Nabeshima Shinano-no-kami. Furthermore, when the enemy abandoned and withdrew from the outer bailey, the besieging forces approached Nabeshima's camp position saying, "We have long desired to capture the outer bailey. Now that it has been abandoned, please take it if you wish." Nabeshima sent a messenger reporting this, asking "May we attach our approach works up to the palisades of the outer bailey?" The reply was, "Please consider this carefully. I will accompany Toda Saemon, and after inspecting the situation later, I will give appropriate orders." Then Ujitetsu accompanied Shin-

ano-no-kami, and when they climbed the large earthwork mound in front of the camp, they saw it was vacant. "Has the outer bailey truly been abandoned? Let us send men in to investigate. There are probably earthen gun emplacements and such," the two generals ordered. Five or six light infantrymen entered and climbed onto the earthworks, but as they were dismantling the earthworks, one or two were shot and killed by flanking fire, so orders were given to withdraw the light infantry.

On the way back, they stopped directly at Saemon's camp, gathered the hereditary retainers present at the siege, and held a consultation about tomorrow's general assault. At Nabeshima's advance position, the masterless samurai Tasaka Yozaemon and Shinohara Genemon, Shinano's retainers Tashiro Gentazaemon and the same Betsuemon, and four or five others agreed "Let us go observe the interior of the outer bailey" and entered, going into the second bailey
