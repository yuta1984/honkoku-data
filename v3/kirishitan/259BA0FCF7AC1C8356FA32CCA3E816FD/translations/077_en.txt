Also, a man named Mamiya Saburōzaemon served as a banner attendant under Arima Genba-no-kami on the 28th, and together with Den Sōzaemon performed incomparable deeds. That Sōzaemon left behind written certificates from Hizen province.

Also, Nakamura Gorōzaemon had once served Furuta Hyōbu-shōyū, but after Hyōbu-shōyū's death, there was a succession dispute between the adopted son and biological son. Because he was particularly close to the adopted son Hikosaburō, he sided with Hikosaburō and ruined himself, becoming a masterless samurai for a long time. Later he attached himself to Fukushima Saemon-no-jō, but when Saemon-no-jō was stripped of his domain, he again became a rōnin and lived in the Settsu province area. Hearing reports that the Hizen rebellion was intense, he went to that campaign and served under Seibei, the eldest son of Inoue Chikugo-no-kami. He planned tactical coordination for the castle assault, and his conduct in attacking the castle in concert with Seibei showed him to be a true warrior. It was heard that during the Osaka campaigns as well, he had been besieged and distinguished himself at Makishima, demonstrating the skill of an experienced warrior.
