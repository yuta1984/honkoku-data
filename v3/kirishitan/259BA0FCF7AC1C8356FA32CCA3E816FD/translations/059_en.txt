The memorandum of Nishimura Gohei states: "On the 27th day of the second month, from the ni-no-maru entrance at Nabeshima Shinano-no-kami-sama's assigned sector, one person broke in. Everyone at our camp positions watched this with suspicion, and then another person broke in and immediately raised smoke. Seeing this, [we] ran back to the huts, put on armor, went to Takumi's place, and waited for Samon-sama to appear. In the meantime, various forces broke in and pressed forward to concentrate on the main citadel. Samon-sama entered from the san-no-maru entrance through Etchū-no-kami-sama's troop assembly point, passed through the san-no-maru, and set up his camp stool within the ni-no-maru. The troops remained within the ni-no-maru, while the others stayed as far back as the san-no-maru.

Next, the Hirado forces pressed their attack. The second and third citadels were filled only with corpses. That night the main citadel held out, but until the castle fell on the following 28th day, [we] pressed into the ni-no-maru and spent the night there."

The memorandum of Amakusa Arima-hara states: "On the 27th day of the second month, [forces] suddenly broke in from the outer citadel, so when Sakon-sama's entire unit also broke in from the san-no-maru, bamboo bundle shields were deployed first. When a man named Mizutani Jiemon appeared, Jidayū Kinosuke came out together with him. When they climbed over the san-no-maru wall, Kinosuke Jidayū was first, Banno Tazaemon was second to break in, and Jidayū Kinosuke was third to break in.
