Izu-no-kami and Samon came out to observe, thinking that rebels might be lurking front and back. They sent one or two gunners up onto the earthworks of the outer bailey and placed one or two earth-filled bags there, but there was not a single rebel in the outer bailey. From the west, from the direction of Amakusa-maru, they shot down one ashigaru (foot soldier) who had climbed up onto the earthworks with gunfire. Indeed, there was not a single rebel in the outer bailey. However, the outer bailey was a small enclosure, and capturing it would serve no purpose. In any case, tomorrow they would launch a general assault to force the surrender of the castle and take it over - this is what both Izu-no-kami and Samon thought. So they ordered Nabeshima's forces to place bamboo bundles up to the fence of the outer bailey, and everyone withdrew to Samon's camp headquarters. Meanwhile, Nabeshima's vanguard forces

were preparing to construct siege works up to the fence of the outer bailey when Sakakibara Hida-no-kami and his son, who had been assigned as scouts to Nabeshima's forces, charged into the outer bailey. Nabeshima's vanguard forces saw this and thought that if they fell behind the envoy Hida-no-kami, and moreover if they allowed Hida-no-kami and others to be killed, it would be a disgrace for Nabeshima. So they all charged in at once. They immediately stormed into the second bailey and set fire to the huts. When the main forces saw this, without distinction between vanguard and rear guard, without orders, and without time to take up their weapons, they all rushed in at once.
