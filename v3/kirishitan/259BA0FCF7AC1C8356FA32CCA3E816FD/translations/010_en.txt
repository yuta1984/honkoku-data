Then Sakakibara Saemon arrived with about five or six men, both upper and lower ranks. Likewise, Hida-no-kami had been previously appointed as inspector (metsuke) for Nabeshima's forces. At this time, as they were about to enter the area around the large earthwork, Hida-no-kami's attendants spotted Saemon entering the outer bailey and reported "Saemon-dono has entered the castle," which surprised Hida-no-kami. He rushed over and entered the outer bailey, ordering "Nabeshima's advance troops, charge in formation!" Since this was an order from the inspector, they acknowledged it without hesitation and immediately all mounted the assault.

Among Nabeshima's mounted retainers, Ishii Yazaemon achieved first entry, and Denemon was second. Among the light infantry, Ishizaki Gorozaemon was first to set fire to the huts in the second bailey, and light infantryman Tashiro Shirozaemon was second to set fires, burning them down. The circumstances of that time are remembered by Nabeshima's unit commanders Nakano Takumi and Oki Hyobu. From then on, military forces from all directions - front, back, left, and right - immediately entered and pressed forward to the vicinity of the main keep, with the entire force closing in to the base of the palisades. However, from within the castle came arrows, musket fire, stones, timber, mud, ash, sand, foul water, and mats and straw set on fire and thrown down - such fierce defense that it was difficult for our forces to enter the main keep. Since Izu-no-kami and Saemon were commanding generals, they established defensive positions in the second and third baileys with their men, and were called the main camp by the various armies.
