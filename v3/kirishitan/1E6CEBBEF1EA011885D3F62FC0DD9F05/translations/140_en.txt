Disciple: Is there a shortcut to properly examine one's conscience?

Master: Certainly there is. First, one should set aside time to reflect on the places where one has been in the past and the actions one has taken with people one has confessed with - what did one do in the places where one stayed, what did one say to the people with whom one confessed? One should examine whether there are any errors regarding the Ten Commandments, the Seven Deadly Sins, the additional sins that may have been overlooked, and the fourteen works of mercy. Since this is a matter of primary importance, one should be prepared not to treat it carelessly.
