According to the "Yukōki" (Record of Brave Deeds), around this time, those who received the imperial command and took positions again were: Terasawa Hyōganokami, Matsubara Uemonza, Hosokawa Etchūnokami, Ogasawara Ukon-dayū, the same Shinano-no-kami, Arima Zaemonza, Matsudaira Tangonokami, Mizuno Hyūganokami, and Matsudaira Ōsuminokami - nine commanders in total. The combined military forces numbered approximately 100,000 men. However, the Satsuma forces came rushing with Ōsuminokami's chief retainer Shimazu Bunzen, Mihara Zaemon, and others accompanying them. Lord Izunokami designated the attack positions for the various generals, and Tadasato's encampment remained as originally planned. On the same 8th day, Lord Izunokami summoned liaison officers from the various houses. There were ninja among Matsubara Ōsuminokami's forces, and there was a request to issue identification tokens so that these men could move back and forth in front of the siege works.

The aforementioned tokens were to be received and distributed to those on siege duty, as was ordered. Tsukiyo Saburō returned to the main camp and reported Lord Izunokami's instructions. That evening, two messengers named Yamaguchi Tōbei and Honda Shinemon brought tokens bearing the seal of Ōsuminokami's retainer Mihara Zaemon. These tokens from Mihara were then distributed to the ashigaru commanders of both vanguards, Horio Magobei and Ueda Chūemon.
