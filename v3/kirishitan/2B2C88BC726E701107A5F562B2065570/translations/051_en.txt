According to another account, on New Year's Day when Lord Jūzō was wounded, Matsuura Nitayū was also wounded by musket fire. Since he was said to be Jūzō's retainer, thinking he would be hit by muskets while carrying him on his shoulder, Ueno Jiemon held him in front and withdrew. This man had been assigned to Lord Jūzō by Lord Andō Chifuku. Afterward, he was ordered to serve as a messenger to Lord Nabeshima, so he went to the arrow platform, completed his messenger duties, and returned.

According to the Matsudaira clan's memorandum, on the morning of New Year's Day, while it was still dark before dawn, they gave signals as described above, and the entire force had to attack at once. Among them, without mistaking the signal and attacking immediately, Hyōbu-shōyū, the son of Arima Gemba-no-kami, led the assault on the walls as general. However, the rebels in the castle defended densely and fought back, causing many wounded and dead among the attacking forces, making it impossible to break through, so they retreated. Seeing this, Matsudaira Jinsaburō thought that since Arima's forces had become cowardly, they would not be able to break through. Therefore, he would attack a position slightly to the side of and close to the demaru (outer bailey) and break into the demaru. He attacked with single-minded determination, but when he approached close to the walls, he was shot in the left kneecap by a musket. As expected, this was exceptional...
