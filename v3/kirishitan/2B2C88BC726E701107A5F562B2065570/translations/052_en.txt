Jinsaburō also fell heavily to the ground. Seeing this, his retainers rushed to help and retreated. Jinsaburō and his retainers also had many wounded. In Hosokawa Etchū-no-kami's retainer band, there was an excellent samurai general named Yokoyama Sukenoshin who had been assigned to Jinsaburō. Seeing Jinsaburō attack, Yokoyama Sukenoshin and Itō Jūnojō—who was also a Hosokawa samurai general—charged up to the walls, but both were killed in action. Among the retainers of both Sukenoshin and Jūnojō, there were also many wounded. Hosokawa's samurai general Ideta Kunai was also wounded. Terao Sasuke sustained no injuries. Makino Denzō and Hayashi Tamba-no-kami also followed Jinsaburō,

but they could not break into the castle, so they positioned bamboo shields and remained there. As evening approached, they withdrew to their camp positions.

According to another account, on New Year's Day, Itakura Naizen-shō and Ishigaya Jūzō, seeing that Arima Gemba-no-kami's forces had become cowardly, said "Let us go forward" and went forward by themselves, just the two of them, into the midst of the cowardly Arima forces. Near the walls of the third bailey, the general Naizen-shō was shot through the middle of his torso by a musket, the bullet penetrating completely through, and he died instantly without lasting even a moment.
