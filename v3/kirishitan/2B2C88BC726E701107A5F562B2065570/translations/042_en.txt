Taking only the name of descendants as the vanguard

On New Year's Day of the fifteenth year of Kan'ei (1638), year of the Tiger, during the general assault, Lord Itakura Naizen-no-shō and Inspector Ishigaya Jūzō placed lanterns on both artificial hills, and when they fired stone arrows, war cries arose at once. They commanded that all three formations should simultaneously attack and storm the position at the late hour of the Tiger, but they attacked at the wrong time. From within the castle, they also fired muskets, spears, long swords, stones, and projectiles in defense. Lord Arima's forces collapsed at the early hour of the Rabbit, and Lord Nabeshima's forces also collapsed at this time. Seeing this, Lord Naizen-no-shō commanded Lord Arima's forces with only his personal retainers, saying "Do not retreat, return, return!" and advanced ahead of the entire

force to beneath the wall, where those in the castle waited, took careful aim, and fired muskets. Alas, they struck Lord Naizen-no-shō again, and he was finally killed. Truly, everyone felt his noble intention to advance all armies and capture the position at once.

For this reason, at the same time Lord Ishigaya Jūzō and Lord Matsudaira Jinzaburō were hit by musket fire and wounded. Matsudaira's forces attacked as ordered, reached beneath the wall and fought, not retreating until the hour of the Snake, but some among the forces broke and fled across their path because Lord Kuroda Uemon-no-suke's foot soldiers had collapsed upon them.
