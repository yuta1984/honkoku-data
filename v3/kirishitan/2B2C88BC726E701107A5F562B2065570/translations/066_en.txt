Zuoka Ichiro'emon was also with Naiki, and at this time he spoke to Itou: "In that case, for the rear guard of this day, among the high-ranking retainers it should be Inaji Nabenosuke, and among the lower-ranking retainers it should be Itou Sakichi." Then Arima Naiki went before Tadasato and said, "Please encourage the advance guard. The fact that we have broken through their formation and pressed forward this far is truly a reasonable strategy. It cuts to the bone how regrettable it would be to make this strategy futile by failing to capture the position," and he shed tears as he spoke at length. At this point, Sasa Shinzaemon said to Yamamura Hikoshiro (marginal note: later called Gentayu), "Our leader Watase Shogen was wounded this morning, and we do not know the condition of his wound. There is word that he is now below the artificial hill.

I would like you to accompany me to see his wound condition and return while we can." Hikoshiro replied, "Even if done casually, returning would create incalculable future difficulties. Please abandon this visit." Shinzaemon deeply trusted these words. That Hikoshiro was at that time only nineteen years old, yet he showed such prudent consideration. There were others unlike him - when the advance guard withdrew from near the castle and all gathered at the hatamoto, about twenty soldiers of the advance guard, perhaps having lost sight of their banners and flags, crossed the Takebashi bridge in retreat.
