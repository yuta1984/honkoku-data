Also recorded: Matsuura Daigaku, Asatani Matauemon, Hisaie Hyōbuzaemon, Hisakawa Sōzaemon, and Arima were stationed at Nagasaki guard duty, and these personnel went to Nagasaki. Jō Jūrōzaemon also went to Nagasaki together with them. Asatani Matauemon brought twenty retainer foot soldiers from his group.

According to the "Nagasaki Record": With the intention of observing the castle's condition from the direction of Hayasaki and firing cannons and stone fire arrows, they moved one Dutch ship and four Chinese ships to that location. A Dutch sailor climbed up and was surveying the situation with a telescope when, conversely, stone fire arrows were fired from Shirō's side, surprising him so that he fell backward and died. On the Chinese ships were Hamada pilot, Terajima Taniroku, Nagaoku Nishi, and others including cannon and stone fire arrow shooters.

Also recorded: Until this time, the Dutch had been entering the port at Hirado. However, as a strategy to fire stone fire arrows from the naval forces at Hara Castle, one Dutch ship was sent to sail around the seaside of Hara Castle and was supposed to fire stone fire arrows into the castle. But because the castle was high, they could not accomplish this task as desired from aboard the ship. Therefore, they landed and occasionally fired stone fire arrows, but without any remarkable results, and soon returned to Hirado.
