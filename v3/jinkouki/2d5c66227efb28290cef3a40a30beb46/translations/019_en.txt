※1 tan 3 se 24 bu
[Left hexagon]
[Upper side] 12 ken [Middle horizontal direction] 18 ken [Lower side] 3 ken
[Upper part in reverse direction] 15 ken [Lower part] 18 ken
　By the calculation method, when adding 18 ken to 12 ken, it becomes 30 ken. When divided by two,
　it becomes 15 ken. When multiplying this by the length of 15 ken,
　it becomes 225 tsubo. Setting this aside, and when adding 3 ken to the horizontal
　18 ken, it becomes 21 ken.
　When divided by two, it becomes 10 ken and a half. When multiplying this by the length of 18 ken,
　it becomes 189 tsubo. Adding this to the right-side 225 tsubo,
　it becomes 414 tsubo. This is divided by the field method of 3.

[Left page]
※○4 se 24 bu
[Right concave quadrilateral]
[Upper right side, in reverse direction] 24 ken [Lower right side] 12 ken
[Middle horizontal direction] 2 ken [Lower side] 18 ken
　By the calculation method, divide 2 ken by two to get 1 ken. When
　multiplying this by the length of 24 ken, it becomes 24 tsubo.
　Also, when adding 18 ken to 2 ken, it becomes 20 ken.
　Dividing by two gives 10 ken. When multiplying this by the length of 12 ken,
　it becomes 120 tsubo. When adding this to the right-side 24 tsubo,
　it becomes 144 tsubo. When dividing this by the field method of 3,
　it should be known as 4 se 24 bu.
