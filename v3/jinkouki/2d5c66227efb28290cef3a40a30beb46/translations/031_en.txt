Chapter 29: Calculating the volume of measuring containers
　　〇With new measures: 1 to 2 sho 2 go 8 shaku
　　〇With old measures: 1 to 2 sho 7 go 4 shaku
[Figure at bottom right]
[Top surface] 1 shaku 3 sun　[Side] 7 sun　[Bottom] inner bottom diameter 1 shaku 1 sun
　The calculation method: combine the diameter at the top
　and the diameter at the bottom, making 2 shaku 4 sun in total.
　Divide this by two to get 1 shaku 2 sun. Square this
　to get 144. Multiply this by the height of 7 sun to get
　1008. Multiply this by the circular coefficient 79 to get
　79632. When this is divided by the Kyō-sho coefficient 64827,
　the result is known to be 1 to 2 sho 2 go 8 shaku.

[Left page]
　※When calculating with old measures, as shown on the right,
　　add the top diameter to the bottom diameter
　　to get 2 shaku 4 sun. Divide by two to get 1 shaku 2 sun.
　　Square this, then multiply by the height, and then
　　multiply by the circular coefficient 79, then multiply by 16
　　to know the result as 1 to 2 sho 7 go 4 shaku.
　　　The above calculation is how to calculate for general approximation.
　　　For containers that taper from rim to bottom, there are
　　　slight differences from this visual calculation method.
　　　The true calculation requires oral transmission.
