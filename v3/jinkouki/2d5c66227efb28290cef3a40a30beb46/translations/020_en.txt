※○1 tan 7 se 3 bu
[Right figure]
[Left side] Length 24 ken [Middle vertically] Length 33 ken [Right side] 24 ken
[Horizontally] Width 18 ken
　By the calculation method, adding 24 ken to the length of 33 ken makes 57 ken.
　When divided by two, it becomes 28 ken and a half. When multiplying this by
　18 ken, it becomes 513 tsubo. When
　dividing this by the field method of 3, it should be known as 1 tan 7 se 3 bu.
　　The above land survey calculations copied here are important for reference.
　　Although everyone has the same mindset, this is generally how it is
　　for the purpose of teaching beginners.
