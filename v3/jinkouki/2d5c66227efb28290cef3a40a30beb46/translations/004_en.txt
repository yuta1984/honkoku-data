[Inside the diagram of the tree] ○ Middle length: seven and a half ken


[Left page]
　In the surveying method, fold the inside into a square shape,
　and also fold corner to corner, hang
　a small stone with string from above to the bottom corner,
　and observe at the place where the metal fittings
　of the upper corners meet. From the established point to the root
　of the tree, when measuring by striking with a measuring rod,
　there are seven ken. When adding the sitting height of three shaku
　to this, the length of the tree is said to be seven and a half ken.
