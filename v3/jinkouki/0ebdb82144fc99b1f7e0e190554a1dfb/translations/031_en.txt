"Chapter 19" On the Buying and Selling of Silk and Cotton
○Regarding the length measurements (jō, shaku, sun) of silk and cotton: according to the money [calculations] in Chapter 9, one shaku and two sun in gofuku [fabric trade] equals one shaku.
○Cotton costs four monme and five bu of silver per tan [bolt]. When the length is two jō and five shaku, how much silver per one shaku? It equals one bu and eight rin per shaku. First, place four monme and five bu on the right, divide by two jō and five shaku, and you get one bu and eight rin per shaku.
Also, multiplying four monme and five bu by four gives one bu and eight rin. The reason for multiplying by four is that for division by twenty-five, multiplying by four is acceptable in either case.
○When cotton fabric is eight shaku and five sun long, the price is five monme per tan. How much silver does this fabric amount to? One monme and seven bu of silver. First, multiply eight shaku and five sun by five monme to get 425. Multiply this by four to get one monme and seven bu. This multiplication by four is the calculation method for division by twenty-five. Also, calculate this fabric of eight shaku and five sun using the calculation for two jō and eight shaku per tan.
○When you have one monme and two bu of silver, with cotton at five monme per tan and length calculated as two jō and eight shaku, how much cotton fabric can this silver buy? One jō, seven shaku, nine sun, and two bu. Divide this by five monme to get one jō, seven shaku, nine sun, and two bu.
Whether silk or cotton, understand that the calculation method of division and multiplication is the same according to the length of one tan.
