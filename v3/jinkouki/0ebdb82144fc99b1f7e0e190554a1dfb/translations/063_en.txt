**Chapter 16: Four people sharing three horses on a six-ri journey**
○When four people share three horses for a six-ri journey, how many ri does each person ride?
♪Each person rides four and a half ri. They ride one and a half ri at a time, then change in rotation.
First, one person rides a horse for one and a half ri then dismounts. One person rides a horse for three ri then dismounts. One person rides a horse for four and a half ri then dismounts. One person walks the first one and a half ri, then rides from the one-and-a-half ri mark to the end.
The person who rode one and a half ri rides again from the three-ri mark to the end. The person who rode three ri rides from the four-and-a-half ri mark to the end. The calculation method: first, multiply the six-ri distance by three horses to get eighteen ri total. Dividing this eighteen ri by four people gives four and a half ri per person. Then, dividing this four and a half ri by three horses gives one and a half ri, so they change horses every one and a half ri.
