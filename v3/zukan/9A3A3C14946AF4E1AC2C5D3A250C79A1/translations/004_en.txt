According to the "Yamato Honzō" (Japanese Pharmacopoeia):
　　Rōbai (wax plum) - Osaka dialect
　　　　　　　　Kara-ume (Chinese plum) - also called Ran-bai (orchid plum)

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　According to "Gunsho":
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Rōbai, also Kyūei-bai, Japanese name: Karamume

　　I say:
　　　The original Chinese name was huang-mei (yellow plum), and later
　　　it came to be called la-mei (wax plum). The "Wakan Sansai Zue"
　　　states that rōbai or ōbai is called Nan-
　　　kyoku-bai (Nanjing plum). The "Bencao Gangmu" states that rōbai is
　　　not originally of the plum family. It blooms at the same time as plums, and its fragrance is
　　　also similar, and its color resembles beeswax, hence
　　　the name. Its fragrance does not resemble plums but resembles that of orchids,
　　　so it has the name ran-bai (orchid plum). The "Sansai Zu-
　　　e" says the yellow color resembles small plum flowers, but I have not yet
　　　seen this. See also the detailed account in Winter Section, Volume 1.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　The "Honzō" classifies it under shrubs
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Flowering trees

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　9th day of the middle month, true sketch
