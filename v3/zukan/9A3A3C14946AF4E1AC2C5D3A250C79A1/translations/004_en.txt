The "Yamato Honzō" states:
　　　Rōbai (winter sweet)　Osaka dialect
　　　　　　　Kara-ume (Chinese plum), also Ran-bai (orchid plum)
　　　　　　　(Kara-ume)

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　The "Gunsho" states:
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Rō (wax), also Kyūei-bai (Japanese name) Karamume

　　　　　　I (Baien) say:
　　　　　　　The original Chinese name was Kōbai (yellow plum), and in later generations
　　　　　　　it came to be called Rōbai. The "Wakan Sansai Zue"
　　　　　　　states "Rōbai, also Kōbai, called Nan-
　　　　　　　kyō-bai (Nanjing plum)." The "Bencao Gangmu" states "Rōbai is
　　　　　　　not originally of the plum family. It has fragrance at the same time as plums,
　　　　　　　and blooms at nearly the same time, and its color resembles beeswax, hence
　　　　　　　it was so named." Its fragrance does not resemble that of plums but resembles the fragrance of orchids,
　　　　　　　therefore it has the name Ran-bai (orchid plum). The "Sancai Tu-
　　　　　　　hui" says "its yellow color resembles small plums," but this I have not yet
　　　　　　　seen. Furthermore, details are in volume one of the winter section.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　In the "Honzō" it is listed under shrub categories

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Two varieties of Rōbai　Bunka 9, year of Mizunoe-Inu, early spring
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Drawn from life on the 9th day of mid-month
