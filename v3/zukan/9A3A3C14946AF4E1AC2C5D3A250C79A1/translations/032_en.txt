There are several varieties of Japanese coptis. The Kaga chrysanthemum-leaf coptis is of superior grade, the Nikko three-leaf variety is of medium grade, the Nikko narrow-leaf variety is of inferior grade, the celery-leaf broad-leaf variety is of medium grade, the Izu celery-leaf small-leaf variety is of medium grade, and the Ligusticum-leaf variety from various provinces is of medium grade. Besides these, there is one variety with Japanese-style leaves of medium grade, but its place of origin is still unknown.

I say that coptis is produced in Kanazawa in Kaga Province, Echizen, Sado, Echigo, Ōshū, and Ushū. Those from Shinshū and elsewhere are commonly called "shrimp coptis." However, the Kaga product is called "chrysanthemum-leaf," and when I later made drawings, they appeared quite different. What is called "celery-leaf coptis" is precisely this. The pharmacopoeia states that it "blooms yellow flowers in the fourth month and bears fruit in the sixth month," but this differs greatly from the actual seasons.
