Contemplation
What is recorded in Honzō Kōmoku states that the leaves of Ōson resemble those used for epilepsy and also resemble purple placenta, but this is a different plant. The form of Ōson in Wakan Sansai Zue also differs, treating Ōson as hollyhock (Tachi-aoi), with one name having two varieties, Ōson being one variety and another being a separate variety, same as recorded in Honkō.

As recorded in Bukō Sanbutsu-shi:
Plantain-leaved mountain lily bulb (katakuri)

As recorded in Imyō Bunrui-shū:
Ōson Katakako flower
Japanese name: Tsuchihari Also called Suhari grass
Honzō's Ōson Explanation of names: Called drought lotus, etc.

My investigation
Tsuchihari blooms in the fourth month and is different from Kōtako.
Ōson is commonly called hollyhock, also known as eren grass.

In common parlance:
Katakuri Katako Initial lily Bundai-yuri Gonbei-rō
This may depend on the region.
In Asahiyama-shi Bunkai: drought lotus Katako, Katakuri, ○○ lily
These have one or two flowers resembling coral lily with similar coloring, so other nomenclature is unclear. The root resembles lotus but is thin, like a five or six-year-old child's finger, about one inch long, etc.
