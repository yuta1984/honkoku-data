## Shin'ten'ō (信天翁) — Known as "Rai" and in Tsukushi dialect as "Ahōdori" or "Bakatsutori"; alternate name: Shin'ten'en (信天縁)

*(Based on the Jikkai [Collected Explanations] section for "Pelican" in the Bencao Gangmu [本草綱目])*

**Measurements:**
- Wings: 3 shaku 5 sun 2 bu (approx. 106 cm)
- Neck: 6 sun 3 bu (approx. 19 cm)
- Bill: 4 sun 4 bu (approx. 13 cm)
- Bill tip to tail: 2 shaku 8 sun (approx. 85 cm)
- Wingtip to wingtip (wingspan): 7 shaku 6 sun 1 bu (approx. 230 cm)
- Body length: 1 shaku 2 sun 4 bu (approx. 38 cm)
- Body width: 5 sun 7 bu (approx. 17 cm)

---

The *Chuogeng Lu* (輟耕録) by Tao Jiucheng states: "At the border between Ying Prefecture and Mo Prefecture, above the ponds and marshes called Tangbo, there live two kinds of birds. One resembles the swan (*koku*); its color is a true blue-grey, and its bill is long. It stands motionless at the water's edge, and when a fish passes beneath it, it seizes it at once. Even if no fish appears all day long, it does not change its position. It is called *Shin'ten'en* ('Trust-in-Heaven Connection'). The other resembles a duck; it runs about among the water, making a constant chattering sound (*shabushabu-zen*) as it probes through rotten grass, mud, and sand, endlessly searching for food without a moment's rest. It is called *Manga* ('Aimless Drawing/Wanderer'). The *Shin'ten'en*, though it appears to have no particular skill, passes the day just as *Manga* does, yet shows no sign of hunger, and its body grows ever more robust. Such is the difference in nature between these two birds."

The *Shin'ten Roku* (信天録) states: "This bird has a season when its feathers fall out, during which time it cannot fly. Thus it is frequently captured by people. Furthermore, during this period it is unable to seek food and may therefore die of starvation. Its wings fold in three sections — it is a remarkable bird."

---

On the 6th day of the 5th month of Tenpō 3 (1832), the year of *Mizunoe-Tatsu* (壬辰), at Koishikawa Baba: drawn from life from a specimen captured by a servant of Kitamura Yamon, a resident of that same place.
