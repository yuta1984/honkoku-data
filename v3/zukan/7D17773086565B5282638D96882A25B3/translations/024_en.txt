【Hybrid Duck (Aiкamo / Kamo-ahiro)】

(From a reference text:)
The 鶩 (ahiru/domestic duck, i.e., duck) is called "家鴨" (kachō, domestic duck).

It is incorrect to render the character 鴨 as "kamo" (wild duck); 鴨 in fact refers to the ahiru (domestic duck). The domestic duck (ahiru) is uniform in nature and has no particular sub-varieties. However, its plumage varies widely in color. The crossbreed between the wild duck (kamo/fu) and the domestic duck is called "Aikamo" (合鴨, hybrid duck). In ponds, reservoirs, or marshes where wild ducks tend not to linger long, releasing many hybrid ducks will cause wild ducks to come in great numbers and remain without leaving—because they regard the hybrid ducks as their own companions. The white duck, along with its eggs, is said to be effective as a medicine for stroke (chūshō). The black duck contains an internal poison and is said to be capable of killing people. During the summer months, few people eat bird meat, and the Grey Heron (aosagi) is considered a delicacy. In the city markets, duck meat is often fraudulently sold as grey heron meat. Duck meat is flavorful in summer, though it is inferior to wild duck (kamo/fu).

Obtained and drawn from life on the 27th day of the first month, year of Tsuchinoe-Inu (戊戌), Tenpō 9 [1838].
