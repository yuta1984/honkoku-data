**[Japanese Quail / Uzura]**
*Uzura* (Quail)
　　When July arrives, it is called "*Sōshū*" (Early Autumn [quail]);
　　from August onward, it is called "*Hakutō*" (White Tang [quail]).
　　This bird originally came from foreign lands. There is also a variety called the "Ryūkyū Quail," which is slightly different.

○ *Uzura no ko* (young quail)　○ *Ra-uzura* (quail chick)
　　*(Both are names for juvenile birds.)*

Sent by Mr. Henmi of Sendagi.
22nd day of the 10th month, year Mizunoto-Mi (Kissi),
Drawn from life.

---

On the quail — This bird has three styles of beginning its call:
　　○ *Kuwa-tō*　○ *Cho-tō*　○ *Koki-tō*

The bird's body shape falls into three types:
　　○ *Ebi-ze* (Shrimp back)　○ *Kani-ze* (Crab back)　○ *Yamagata-ze* (Mountain-shape back)

The beak falls into three types:
　　○ *Mame-hashi* (Bean beak)　○ *Shiinomi-hashi* (Chinquapin-nut beak)　○ *Kusari-hashi* (Rotten/Decayed beak)

The neck falls into three types:
　　○ *Tsuru-kubi* (Crane neck)　○ *[Unclear character]-kubi*　○ *Inoko-kubi* (Boar neck)

The tail falls into three types:
　　○ *Sashi-o* (Pointed tail)　○ *Han-sashi-o* (Half-pointed tail)　○ *Ebi-o* (Shrimp tail)

The plumage pattern falls into three types:
　　○ *Shiro-fu* (White markings)　○ *Aka-fu* (Red markings)　○ *Hoke-fu* (Faded/Pale markings)

Quail are brought to Edo (*Tōbu*) from Kōshū (Kai Province), Sunshū (Suruga Province), the Nanbu domain of Ōshū (Northern Honshū), and Tanshū (Awaji Island).
Among those who appreciate quail, there is considerable connoisseurship regarding their calls.
For more details, refer to *Tori-shō Yasuko*.
Furthermore, the *Huainanzi* states: "Toads (*hikigaeru*) transform into quail."
