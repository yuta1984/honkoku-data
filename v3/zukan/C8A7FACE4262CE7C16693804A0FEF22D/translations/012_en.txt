Nunohiki

　Illustration recorded in Sanbutsu Shasei
　According to the same book, it grows in forests and fields
　in late autumn. The gills are thick and white, slightly
　tinged with brown color, with fine
　edges at the base. The shape resembles nameko mushrooms
　but is more fleshy, with extremely short stems.
　It extends in one cluster, reaching several hundred stems.
　The taste is slightly bitter.
　This is boiled and seasoned with soy sauce and miso
　for eating. Also, when sun-dried and made into
　soup, it is extremely good, it is said.
