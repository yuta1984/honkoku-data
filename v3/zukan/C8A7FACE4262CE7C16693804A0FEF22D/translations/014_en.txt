Inu-name-suzuki (Dog name-suzuki)

　Illustration recorded in
　Sanbutsu Shasei. The same book states that it resembles
　newly grown name-suzuki in form and
　color but is somewhat thicker, and the taste
　is slightly inferior.


Shōko-take

　Illustration recorded in Shomotsu Shasei.
　The same book states it grows under
　tall trees after autumn rains.
　From one base, four to five stems grow
　in clusters.
　Some also grow individually. The cap spreads to two or
　three sun, or about one shaku
　in size. Tea-brown in color, with the underside and stem all having white
　hair. The texture is tough and
　the taste is sour.
