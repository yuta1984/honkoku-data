Inu-namesuzuki (Dog namesuzuki)

　Illustration recorded in
　Sanbutsu Shasei. According to the same book, it resembles
　the young namesuzuki in shape and
　color but is somewhat more enlarged, and the taste
　is said to be slightly inferior.


Shōkotari

　Illustration recorded in Sanbutsu Shasei.
　According to the same book, it grows
　under tall trees after autumn rains.
　Four or five stems cluster together
　from a single base. There are also
　specimens that grow singly. The cap spreads to two or
　three sun or about one shaku. It is tea-
　brown in color, with the underside and stem all white and
　covered with hair. The texture is tough and
　the taste is said to be sour.
