Koushitake　　　　　　　　　

　Illustration recorded in the Kanka. According to the Fungi History,　　　　　
　the cap spans only about the size of a coin,　　
　the flesh is thick and short,　　　　　　　　
　the surface is deep red with a slight　　　　　　　　
　purple tinge,　　　　　　　
　the underside is deep yellow with small　　　　　　　　　
　needle-like perforations,　　　　　　　　
　the stem is pale reddish-yellow with　　　　　　　　　
　fine white striations.　　　　　　　
　It grows between trees and bamboo　　　　　　　　
　after the rainy season.　　　　　　　　
　It is said not to decay even after a long time.

A Variety

　Illustration recorded in the Fungi History. According to the same book,
　in the fifth month of the Jinshin year, it grew
　in the grounds of Zōjō-ji Temple.
　When it contacts the roots of
　ophiopogon beneath it
　and damages them, the white color suddenly
　changes to indigo,
　and after some time
　returns to white again,
　it is said.
