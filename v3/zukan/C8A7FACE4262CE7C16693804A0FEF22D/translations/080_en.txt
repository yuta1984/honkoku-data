Stone Ear Fungus
　Iwatake

　Illustration recorded in the Mushroom History. According to the same book, it resembles kikurage (wood ear fungus) but is thinner and softer, like cotton. The surface is black with fine hairs, and the underside is bluish-white or tinged with a slight purple color. It grows in clusters, layer upon layer, shaped like cockscomb seaweed. The taste is somewhat similar to kikurage, but the flavor is inferior. People from Kii Province say that after heavy snow on steep rocks in deep mountains, it immediately grows when exposed to sunlight.
