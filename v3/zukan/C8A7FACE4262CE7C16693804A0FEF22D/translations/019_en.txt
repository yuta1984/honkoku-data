Ashitaka-shimeji

　Illustration recorded in
　Shobutsu Shasei. In Sanbutsu Shasei,
　it resembles shimeji but has long
　stems, with a brown surface or
　some specimens of pale reddish-brown color.
　The underside is white with rubbed patterns.
　There is one variety with small caps and pale
　yellow coloring with long stems that are slightly white.
　The texture is extremely soft.
　Both types should not be eaten,
　it is said.
