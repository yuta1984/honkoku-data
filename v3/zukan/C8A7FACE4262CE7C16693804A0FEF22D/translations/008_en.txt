Beni-take (Red mushroom)
An illustration recorded in Kanka, similar to the form shown in Kinshi, somewhat reddish-tinged and red in color, with luster. Both cap and stem are pure white. Found everywhere in shady, moist ground. Though said to be poisonous, it does not harm people. Some say that if the outer skin is removed before eating, it is not poisonous. This is attributed to Matsuoka Gentatsu.
In the Kōwa Shinfu it states: "Those near the eastern capital are mostly poisonous mushrooms. They are probably momi-take." One should choose carefully.
