Tiger Bolete

　The illustration recorded in the Mushroom History
　The same book states it resembles the black bolete
　but has a yellow surface with black
　spots, just like the spotted
　pattern of a tiger. The underside is
　white, and the stem is reddish-brown with scaly
　patterns. The same
　book classifies it among poisonous
　mushrooms.



Black Bolete

　The illustration recorded in Natural History Sketches
　The Mushroom History states it has a black cap with
　a white underside forming honeycomb-like pores,
　the stem has wrinkled patterns and is white in color,
　sticky and smooth. It grows in the mountains
　during autumn. The same book classifies it
　among poisonous mushrooms.
