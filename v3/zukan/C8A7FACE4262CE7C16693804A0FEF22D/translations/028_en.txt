Torafu-iguchi (Tiger-spotted bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Kinshi" (Fungus History).
The same book states that "it resembles the kuro-iguchi (black bolete)
but has a yellow surface with black
spots, just like the spotted
pattern of a tiger. The underside is
white, and the stem is reddish-brown with scale-like
patterns." The same
book classifies it among the poisonous
fungi.

Kuro-iguchi (Black bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Shomotsu Shasei" (Sketches of Various Things).
The "Kinshi" states that "it has a black cap with
a white underside forming honeycomb-like holes.
The stem has wrinkled patterns and is white in color,
sticky and slippery. It grows in the mountains
during autumn." The same book classifies it among poisonous
fungi.
