Maitake (Dancing mushroom)

　Illustration from "Kanka." According to the "Kinshi" (Mushroom History), the shape resembles ginkgo leaves and is large, with short, thick, solid stems. They grow in layers upon layers, reaching a height of over 1 shaku. Some reach 2-3 shaku in height, which is considered most extraordinary. The surface and stem are grayish-black, and there are also yellow, black, and purple varieties. The base does not separate, produces gills, and has wrinkled patterns. The color is pure white as if rubbed. When boiled, it immediately changes to black color, becoming soft and having a light sweet taste, though with a slight odor. When dried, it becomes edible. It is also very good, and when simply salt-preserved, it is fragrant, clean, and deliciously fresh. It should be ranked next to wakame seaweed. It grows on decayed stumps and roots in the mountains after autumn rains.
