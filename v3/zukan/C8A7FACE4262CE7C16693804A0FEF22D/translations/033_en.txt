Bakoshin (Mushroom)　　　Futetake
　According to Yamato Honzō, it grows on trees during winter and spring. It grows
　in overlapping layers like human fingers lined up. It is poisonous. The taste is reddish. Those growing on beech trees are
　poisonous unless exposed to cold months. According to Kōsei Yōran, the method for cultivating this is to cut decayed
　paper mulberry wood into pieces several sun long, bury them in soil, and pour rice washing water over them, then they will grow within three days.
