Makoshin (Puffball Mushroom)　　　Futetake
　According to Yamato Honzō, it grows on trees during winter and spring. It grows in overlapping layers like human fingers arranged in a row. It is poisonous. The taste is red/reddish. Those growing on beech trees are poisonous when exposed to cold months. According to Kōsei Yōran, the method of cultivation is to cut decayed mulberry wood into pieces of several sun, bury them in soil, and pour the white water from washing rice over them, and they will grow within three days.
