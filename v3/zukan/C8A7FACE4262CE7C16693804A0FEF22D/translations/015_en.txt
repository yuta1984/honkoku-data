Tsuru-take

　Illustration recorded in
　Sanbutsu Shasei. In the Mushroom
　History, when it first grows,
　it emerges from the ground in
　the shape of a chicken egg,
　gradually the stem
　lengthens, the protective membrane splits,
　and the cap spreads. The size
　is like a basin or bowl,
　the texture is thin, the stalk is tall,
　and its color is pure white
　with a slight tinge of
　red. The name probably
　represents its shape.

　It is sweet, smooth, and delicious. Mountain people gather it as a premium
　delicacy. They also dry and store it to treat diarrhea.
　It is found in tree-rich areas of various mountains in Hitachi Province.


Tsuru-take

　Illustration recorded in
　Shobutsu Shasei. In the Mushroom
　History, both cap and stem are pale
　black, with features like
　the feathers of a brown-eared bulbul. The stem
　is very thick, and the cap shape
　is like an inverted basin.
　It has the same name but is
　a different species.
