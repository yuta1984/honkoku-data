Tsurutake

　Illustration recorded in Sanbutsu Shasei.
　According to Kinshi (Mushroom Records), when it first grows,
　it emerges from the ground in
　the shape of an egg, then the stem
　gradually lengthens, the protective membrane splits,
　and the cap spreads to become as
　large as a basin. Its texture is
　thin with tall legs,
　and its color is pure white
　with a slight tinge of
　red. The name probably
　represents its shape.
　　　　　　　　　　　　It is sweet, smooth, and delicious. Mountain people
　　　　　　　　　　　　gather it as a superior
　delicacy. They also dry and preserve it to treat diarrhea.
　It is said to be found in wooded areas of various mountains in Hitachi Province.


Tsurutake

　Illustration recorded in Shobotsu Shasei.
　According to Kinshi,
　the cap and stem are all pale
　black, resembling the feathers
　of a quail. The stem is
　very thick, and the cap shape is
　like an inverted basin.
　It is said to be a different species
　with the same name.
