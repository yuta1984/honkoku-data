Tsuru-take

　Illustration recorded in Sanbutsu Shasei. According to Kinshi, when it first grows, it emerges from the ground in the shape of a chicken egg, then gradually the stem lengthens, the protective membrane splits, and the cap spreads. Its size is like that of a basin, with a thin texture and tall stalk. Its color is pure white with a slight reddish tinge. The name probably represents its form. It is sweet, smooth, and delicious. Mountain people gather it as a superior delicacy. They also dry and store it to treat diarrhea. It is said to be found in tree-rich areas of various mountains in Hitachi Province.


Tsuru-take

　Illustration recorded in Shobutsu Shasei. According to Kinshi, there is a type where both cap and stem are pale black, like the feathers of a sōkei bird. The stem is very thick, and the cap shape is like an inverted basin. It is said to be a different species with the same name.
