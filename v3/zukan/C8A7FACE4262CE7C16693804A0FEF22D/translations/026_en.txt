Numeri-iguchi (Slippery bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Shin'yō Kinpu" (Shin'yō Fungus Compendium).
The same book states that it "resembles
jikari-hō but is somewhat different, with a bland taste
unsuitable for eating. It is named
because its cap is smooth."
The "Kinshi" (Fungus History) records: "It grows among
clusters of cogon grass. It is reddish-brown with
stickiness, and is also good. The outer membrane is
removed before eating. People in Kanazawa,
Kaga call this shibatake
(brushwood mushroom)."
