Inu-nuno-hiki (Dog nuno-hiki)

　Illustration recorded in
　Sanbutsu Shasei. Similar to nuno-
　hiki but with longer stems,
　the cap head has a slight brown
　tinge, and a single stem
　protrudes distinctively. The taste is bitter
　and not delicious.


Ji-name-suzuki

　Illustration recorded in Sanbutsu Shasei.
　The same book states it grows in tree
　shade during autumn. The form resembles sensho
　and is viscous and slippery, with a tea-brown colored
　surface and fine gills on the underside. The gills and
　stem are pure white and glossy. The taste
　is light, delicious, and good.
