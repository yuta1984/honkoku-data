Inunohiki

　Illustration recorded in Sanbutsu Shasei.
　Resembles nunohiki
　but has longer legs,
　and the cap head has a slight brownish
　tinge, with one stem
　particularly protruding. The taste is bitter and
　said to be unpalatable.


Jinamesuzuki

　Illustration recorded in
　Sanbutsu Shasei. According to the same book, it grows in tree
　shade during autumn. In shape it resembles zenimatsu,
　being sticky and smooth. The surface is tea-brown,
　with fine folds at the base. The folds and
　stem are pure white and lustrous. Its taste
　is said to be light and delicious.
