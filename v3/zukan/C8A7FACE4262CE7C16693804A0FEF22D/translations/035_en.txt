Mukitake

　Illustration recorded in "Kanka"
　Also mentioned in "Kinshi" as having a form
　resembling ginkgo leaves, but
　compared to that, the texture is thin,
　and the color is yellowish-white with a slight
　blackish tinge. It grows on
　decayed stumps of hackberry and
　willow trees. It has a mild, sweet, smooth, and delicious taste.
　Bleached and dried ones are said to be
　rarely sold in Edo.
