Mukitake (A type of mushroom)

　Illustration recorded in Kanka
　Also mentioned in Kinshi (Mushroom Records), its shape
　compared to scattered
　ginkgo leaves, but the texture is thin
　and the color is yellowish-white with a slight black
　tinge. It grows on
　decayed stumps of hackberry and
　willow trees. Mild, sweet, smooth and delicious.
　Bleached and dried ones are rarely
　sold in Edo, it is said.
