Botan-take (Peony mushroom)

　Illustration recorded in "Shomotsu Shasei." In "Kinshi" it states that "several stems from one base
　grow in clusters.
　It somewhat resembles the form of young branches."
　The same book quotes "Shomotsu
　Shasei" saying "It grows in
　dry areas in the mountains during autumn. Its form
　resembles flower
　petals growing in layers and clusters. When
　prepared with ginger vinegar and eaten,
　the taste is delicious." One variety with yellow stems and
　white caps has
　a tender texture and
　is suitable for eating.


Memimi-take (Woman's ear mushroom)

　Illustration recorded in
　"Shomotsu Shasei." "Kinshi" quotes
　the same book saying "During autumn, it grows
　on decayed wood,
　stumps, and in the shade of trees.
　It resembles the peony fungus
　and is pale yellow in color." There are also
　white-colored ones,
　with a brittle texture, somewhat
　like seaweed
　roots.
