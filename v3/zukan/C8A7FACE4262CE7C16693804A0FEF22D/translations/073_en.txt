Chikujoku (Bamboo fungi)

　　　Grows on the roots of old bamboo around Aoyama in Musashi Province, with a shape 
　　　resembling wood ear mushrooms (kikurage) or oyster mushrooms, and is white in color.


One variety

　　　Grows on dwarf bamboo during the long rains of the fourth and fifth months, 
　　　appearing between the joints. The shape resembles wood ear mushrooms and is very 
　　　pliable, falling off when touched. It is yellow-red in color, and there are no 
　　　reports of it being eaten.
