Tetsuhare　　Makusotake (Dung mushroom)

　According to the Fungi History, it prefers to grow
　in moist and filthy
　places. It grows most abundantly in places with
　much horse dung, hence this
　name was given. The cap is extremely
　thin, with the center slightly luminous, and the stem is thin and
　hollow. The surface is grayish-black, and the stems are all
　white. When touched by hand,
　the poison causes a smell to rise and brings about swelling and pain.
　Common people always call this
　"tetsuhare" (hand swelling), it is said.
