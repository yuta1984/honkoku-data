Tengutake (Fly agaric)

　Illustration recorded in Kinshi (Mushroom History). According to the same book,
　the cap is large and the stem is long, with a separate part at the base,
　sometimes forming honeycomb-like holes,
　or creating fine needle-like perforations.
　There are pure white ones, ones that are white
　with a grayish tinge,
　gray-black ones with white spotted patterns,
　and ones with yellowish bases.
　When crushed, it has a foul odor,
　and in the seventh and eighth months, it preferentially grows
　among old graves. Its poison is extremely potent, and it is said that
　if mistakenly eaten, it will kill a person.
