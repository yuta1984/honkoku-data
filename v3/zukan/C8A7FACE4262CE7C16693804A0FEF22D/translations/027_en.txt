Awai-guchi (Pale bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Shin'yō Kinpu" (Shin'yō Fungus Compendium).
The same book states that "tea ceremony practitioners favor it for cooking.
The surface is yellow-red, and the underside has
honeycomb-like holes."

Motobuto-iguchi (Base-thick bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Shin'yō Kinpu."
The same book states that "it is also
named Shikō-bō. The flesh is gray and the taste is bland."
It also notes that "varieties with
bitter taste are poisonous."

Yamadori-iguchi (Copper bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Shin'yō Kinpu."
The same book states that
"the surface is
tea-brown, resembling the feathers of a yamadori
(copper pheasant), hence
the name. The underside is gray
and the taste is bland and delicious."
