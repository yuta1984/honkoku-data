Oni-iguchi (Demon bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Shomotsu Shasei" (Sketches of Various Things).
The "Kinshi" states that "it has a cap that is
very thick, with grayish texture and reddish-brown patterns
like a net, and the underside is pale black.
The stem is reddish-brown and grows in
moist places in the mountains.
There are also specimens where both the cap and stem are pure black,
with small protrusions on the
surface."


Oni-iguchi (Demon bolete)

The illustration recorded in the "Shomotsu Shasei."
The "Kinshi" states that "the flesh is
thick and fleshy, the surface is yellow-brown, the underside is
tea-colored with honeycomb-like
holes. The stem is gray with deep yellow
wrinkled patterns resembling scales.
It grows in mountain forests."
The same book classifies it among the
poisonous fungi.
