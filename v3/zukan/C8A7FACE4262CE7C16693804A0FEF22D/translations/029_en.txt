Demon Bolete

　The illustration recorded in Natural History Sketches.
　The Mushroom History states it has a cap that is
　extremely thick, with grayish texture and reddish-brown patterns
　forming a net-like appearance, with a pale black underside.
　The stem is reddish-brown and grows in
　moist places in the mountains.
　It also states there are varieties where both cap and stem are pure black,
　with an uneven
　surface texture.


Demon Bolete

　The illustration recorded
　in Natural History Sketches. The Mushroom History states the flesh is
　thick and fleshy with a yellow-brown surface, the underside is
　tea-colored with honeycomb-like pores,
　and the stem is gray with deep yellow
　wrinkled patterns resembling scales.
　It grows in mountain forests,
　according to the text. The same book classifies it
　among poisonous mushrooms.
