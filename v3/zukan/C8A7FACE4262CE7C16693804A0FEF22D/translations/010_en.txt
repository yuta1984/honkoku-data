Shōgen-shi

Illustration recorded in Shin'yō Kinpu. According to the same book, the cap is thick and the stem is long, with a reddish-brown surface and white underside. The taste is delicious when roasted. According to local people, a certain monk from the nearby Jōken-ji temple first gathered and ate this mushroom, hence the name.
