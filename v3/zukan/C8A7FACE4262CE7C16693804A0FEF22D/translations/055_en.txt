Awatake (Foam Mushroom)

　Illustration recorded in
　the Shobutsu Shasei (Natural Object Sketches).
　According to the Fungi History, it grows in
　mountains during autumn. It is abundant in
　places with red clay soil. The cap is deep yellow
　with a slight brownish tinge, and has
　tortoiseshell patterns on the top. It does not produce
　bottom gills but forms a honeycomb-like
　structure. The substance is thin and the stem is long.
　It is said that eating this causes people to
　suffer from diarrhea and vomiting.
