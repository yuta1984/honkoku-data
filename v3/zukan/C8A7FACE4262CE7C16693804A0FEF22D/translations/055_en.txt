Awa-take (Bubble mushroom)

　Illustration recorded in
　the Shobutsu Shasei. According to the Kinshi, it grows
　in the mountains during autumn. It is abundant in
　places with red soil. The cap is deep yellow
　with a slight brownish tinge, and has
　tortoise shell patterns on the top. It does not produce
　tei-ran, and forms honeycomb-like
　structures. The flesh is thin and poor quality.
　It is said that eating this causes people to have
　diarrhea and vomiting.
