　Sasatake　Sasamotashi　《Rikushū, Ushū provinces》

　The illustration is the same as that recorded in the Kinshi (Mushroom Records). In that same book,
　small bamboo is called "sasa." This
　fungus grows abundantly in small bamboo groves,
　hence the name. The texture is thin with long stems, the surface is
　ash-white with a whitish appearance and slightly tough.
　It has a fresh, delicious taste and fragrance. It somewhat resembles
　moisture-loving varieties, and there are also pure white, yellow, slightly
　brown, and pale reddish-brown colored ones. They grow
　abundantly in the seventh and eighth months. Some grow in clusters
　of several stems. From this point onward, although the
　form differs greatly from the previous entry, since they
　grow in bamboo groves, I record them here.
