Chichi-take (Milk mushroom)

According to an illustration recorded in Kanka and Kinshi, it often grows in places where trees and woods are luxuriant. It is light yellow or light reddish-brown in color, with a cap that is white and slightly tinged with yellow. It resembles shiitake but is somewhat thinner. When damaged, it produces a thick white sap. When soaked in water, this immediately stops. It is sweet and smooth with a fishy smell. It is made into soup and served at meals. It is very delicious. Dried and stored ones are said to be especially excellent.
