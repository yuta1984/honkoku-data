Awatake, Ahafuki
　　　Awafuku (from Kazusa Province)
　Illustration recorded in the Kanka.
　According to the Fungi History, the cap spans five to six sun,
　the stem is thin and short, the surface is yellow-black, and there are also
　pale purple varieties. The underside is yellow with
　honeycomb-like pores. Where this fungus grows,
　slugs are extremely numerous. Perhaps the qi mutually attract and
　influence each other. Common people say its poison harms
　humans, but due to its bitter astringent taste,
　it is something one cannot bear to eat.


Makusotake

　Illustration recorded in the Shomotsu Shasei.
　According to the Fungi History, the surface is large and the stem short,
　the bottom forms honeycomb-like pores. The surface is
　yellow-brown and sticky like lacquer, while the bottom
　and stem are both white, but after some time
　they change to black. If one mistakenly
　eats this, it can kill a person. It grows abundantly
　in the mountains of Mikawa Province.
