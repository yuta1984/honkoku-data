Yuki-take (Snow mushroom)

　Illustration recorded in "Shomotsu Shasei."
　"Kinshi" quotes the same book stating:
　"It resembles maitake with a round
　form, no wrinkled patterns on the back, growing in clusters from one base,
　with stems all growing obliquely.
　Its color is pure white like
　snow, hence the name. When boiled, it
　turns gray. It grows in
　dry areas of mountain forests during autumn. There are also blue-
　yellow and gray varieties,
　but they are not suitable for eating."
