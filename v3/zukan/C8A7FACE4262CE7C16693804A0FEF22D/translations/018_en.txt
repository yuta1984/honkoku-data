Kōtake

　Illustration recorded in Shobutsu Shasei. According to Kinshi (Mushroom History),
　the cap spreads to
　two to three sun in size, with large
　specimens reaching four to five sun.
　The surface has purplish-white spots.
　The stem length is over one shaku.
　The stem and volva have
　pure white undersides. When first growing,
　the white spots are very dense,
　gradually elongating and gradually
　becoming sparse. If eaten,
　it causes vomiting. It grows
　in mountains and fields in the eighth and ninth
　months,
　　　it is said.
