　Shobutsu Shasei


Kou-take 

　Illustration recorded in Shobutsu Shasei. According to the Kinshi (Fungal Records), the cap spreads to a diameter of two to three sun, with larger specimens reaching four to five sun. The surface has purplish-white spots. The stem is over one shaku in length. Both the stem and gills are all pure white. When first emerging, the white spots are very dense, but as it gradually grows, they gradually become sparse. Eating this causes vomiting. It grows in mountains and fields in the eighth and ninth months, it is said.
