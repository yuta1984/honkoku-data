The *Nishiki-gai* (brocade shell) is the king of elegant shells for refined appreciation. It can be found in various regions, but nothing surpasses those that come from the shores of Tango and Tajima provinces. In Kishū (Kii Province), they are rare, and those occasionally found there are poor in color. There exists a book that records this shell under the name *Nadeshiko-gai* (pink shell). While it could indeed be called a *Nadeshiko-gai*, it is in fact a different shell from the one properly known by that name.

In general, fine shells wash ashore most abundantly in spring. In seas with many rocks, small shells are broken and thus scarce. Good shells are found on rough shores of fine sand. The seas of Settsu are mostly warm and sheltered as an inland sea, so no fine shells are found there.

When one visits the shores of Wakanoura, Arahama, and the northern beach of Awashima at Kada in Kishū, shells are plentiful. The shores of Senshu (Izumi Province) yield no fine shells. Takeno-ura in Tajima Province (*Tanshū*) has many fine shells. I have heard that Taikohama and Kotohikihama in Tango also have good shells; however, I myself have

**[Left page]**

not been there, and the shells I have were received from those who gathered them. I have also been told that fine shells exist in Kumano in Kishū, and I have seen some given to me. Beyond these places, there are surely rare and fine shells to be found in distant regions, but since I have not gone there myself, and those who have made the journey brought none back, I cannot say.

In Kamakura (Yukinoshita) in Sagami Province, and around Enoshima, there are many shops selling shells, and it has long been said that fine shells in great numbers can be found in this area.

I visited Wakanoura in Kishū during the Hōei and Shōtoku eras, and again this year. Beyond this, as years pass, things change greatly, and there are many cases where what I had heard about the names of shells does not match what I actually found.
