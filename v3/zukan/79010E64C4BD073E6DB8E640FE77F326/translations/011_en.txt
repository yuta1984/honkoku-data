Cuscuta (toshishi・nenashikadura - dodder) "Bencao Gangmu"
It grows in fields and coastal areas. In summer, a single thread-like growth emerges, and after reaching about two to three sun in length, it leans against grasses and trees, wrapping around them in a clockwise direction like thread. It is a thread-like vine. As the vine gradually wraps around, its root withers and shrivels while spreading more extensively. Therefore, it is called "rootless wisteria" in the "Medical Orthodox Manual" (Igaku Seiden). Its stem is yellow-green with a slight reddish tinge and has purple spots. It has no leaves but produces branch vines. Even if it grows when there are no plants around, it cannot sustain life and immediately withers and shrivels. It is a parasitic plant that wraps around and attaches to plants, stealing their life force to survive. Therefore, tender grasses lose their natural properties because of it. In autumn, it produces white stems and opens small white flowers. At the tip of small tube-like flowers are five petals with yellow stamens inside. They are only about one bu in size.
