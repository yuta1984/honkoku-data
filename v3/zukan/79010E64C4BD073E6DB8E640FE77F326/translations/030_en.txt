　　　　Corn (Kōraikivi) "Bencao Gangmu"
Seeds are sown in spring. The seedlings resemble sorghum but are larger. During summer and autumn, flowers appear at the stem tops forming ears, occasionally bearing seeds. Bracts grow between the leaves, producing fine white-purple whiskers. These are also flowers. Several seeds form within the bracts. When they ripen, the seedlings wither. There are plants with both types of flowers, which I describe below.

Mountain Walnut (Ogurumi) "Bencao Gangmu" "Walnut Jijie" Cultivated in various places. After autumn the leaves fall and new leaves grow in spring. There are fine hairs. Flowers emerge from the scars of leaf stems on old branches and hang down. They are blue-yellow in color with no fruit, falling immediately.
