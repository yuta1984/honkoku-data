Chencang walnut (himegurumi) "Same book" has drooping flowers that cluster at the tips of old branches, bearing no fruit and falling immediately. The flowers at the fruit head are the same as mountain walnut (onigurumi), and its fruit is thin with few wrinkles. Walnut (chōsenkurumi) is a Korean variety and is rare. I omit its description.

The Bencao Gangmu Jijie states: Shizhen said that corn seeds come from the western lands. Those who plant the seeds are also rare. Both the seedlings and leaves resemble sorghum but are plump and dwarf, also resembling Job's tears seedlings. Three to four feet tall, flowering in the sixth and seventh months forming ears like blighted wheat. From the heart of the seedling emerges a separate bract shaped like a palm fish. White whiskers emerge from the top of the bract and hang down. When ■ occurs, the bract breaks and seeds emerge, clustering together grain by grain. The seeds are also as large as millet seeds, yellow-white in color, and can be eaten roasted. When roasted, white flowers break apart, resembling the state of roasted glutinous rice husks.
