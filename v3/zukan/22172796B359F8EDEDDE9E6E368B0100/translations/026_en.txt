The Zōho Tashikihen (Augmented Encyclopedia), in the section on fragrant grasses, states:
　　　Peony (Botan)　Japanese name: Fukami-gusa (Deep-seeing grass), also
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　called Hatsuka-gusa (Twenty-day grass)
　　　　　Alternative names: Riko (mouse aunt) [Honzōkyō]　Kokkyū (deer leek) [same work]
　　　　　Hyakuryōkin (hundred ryō gold) [Tang Materia Medica]　Tree peony
　　　　　[Kōmoku]　Kaō (flower king)


　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Three varieties of peony　Tenpō 3, year of the wood rooster, 25th day
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Painted from life at Kagakudō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Flower and grass section





In Ouyang Yongshu's "Peony Catalog,"
over ninety varieties of peony names
are recorded. The varieties currently possessed in Japan
are extremely numerous and difficult to capture with the brush.
