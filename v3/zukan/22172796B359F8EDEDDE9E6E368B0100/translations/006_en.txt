In the Zōho Tashikihen (Augmented Multilingual Guide), under the category of tall trees, it states:
　　　Suiyō (Water Willow) 《Japanese name》Kawayagi 《Alternative name》Seiryū 《Compendium》Hoyō 《Kokon Commentary》

　　　《Augmented alternative names》Horyū 《Erya》Ho-i 《Pronunciation: i》Iryū 《Kokon Commentary》
　　　　　Kanho 《Pronunciation: Kanho》

　　　　　○Kawayagi blooms in early spring
　　　　　　Being silver-white colored and having hair-like features,
　　　　　　it is called Inoko-yanagi (puppy willow)


　　　　　　　　　I say:
　　　　　　　　　　There is also a tree of the same type called Tei-ryū. This too is called Kawaya-
　　　　　　　　　　nagi as an alternative name. It is called Tei-ryū (Kyoryū). In the Yamato Honzō's
　　　　　　　　　　commentary on willows, it states "Suiyō, also called Horyū."
　　　　　　　　　　This is precisely it. Ryūjo (willow catkins) are not willow flowers but
　　　　　　　　　　cotton attached to the outside of the fruit. These are the so-called
　　　　　　　　　　Enoko-yanagi and Meharu-yanagi. The Horyū mentioned in the
　　　　　　　　　　Book of Songs is this. In recent years, foreign doctors favor the use of
　　　　　　　　　　something called Kina, which was brought by the Dutch, and its efficacy is
　　　　　　　　　　said to be very good. Kinaen is a salt extracted from the resin of the Kina tree.
　　　　　　　　　　Although Kina corresponds to our domestic Suiyō,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　it is said that its bark has no effect whatsoever. Tei-ryū is also
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　called Kawayagi. Tei refers to Miharu-yanagi, which is
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　a different plant. Could Kina perhaps be the bark of
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　this tree?

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Late month of early spring, Seiyū year
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　8th day, drawn from life


The Chikkin-shō states:
　　　Ingen-yanagi　　　The leaves are large and deeply green like willow
　　　　　　　　　　　This is the correct one

The same record:
　　　Midori-yanagi　　　　Used in ikebana, it is the mature form of Enoko-yanagi
　　　　　　　　　　　This is yet another different plant. The leaves are slightly smaller and the wood appears the same
　　　　　　　　　　　but differs in having many flowers. Used for hedging.
　　　　　　　　　　　This is also Suiyō. Tei-ryū is a separate species.
　　　　　　　　　　　Tei-ryū is the weeping willow. What is simply called yanagi is the
　　　　　　　　　　　weeping willow.
