The "Zōho Tashikihen" (Supplement to the Multi-Knowledge Compilation), in the section on tall trees, states:
　　Water Yang (willow)　Japanese name: kawaya (river willow)　Alternative names: blue willow, Kōmoku: cattail willow, Kokon-chū
　　　　　　Name: nagi
　　　　　　Supplement alternative names: cattail willow (Erya), cattail poplar (Erya pronunciation), poplar willow (Kokon-chū)
　　　　　　Kanho (pronounced as maruba)
　　　　　　　　　○River willows bloom in early spring
　　　　　　　　　　Being silver-white with hair, they are called
　　　　　　　　　　puppy willows　　　　　　　　　Tree category
　　　　　　　　　　　　　(willow)

　　　　　　　　　I say:
　　　　　　　　　　There is also a tree of the same category called teiyū (tamarisk willow). This is also called kawaya and
　　　　　　　　　　nagi but is a separate species. It is called teiyū (tamarisk willow). In "Yamato Honzō,"
　　　　　　　　　　the commentary on willow states "water yang, also called cattail willow."
　　　　　　　　　　This is exactly it. Willow floss is not the willow flower but
　　　　　　　　　　cotton attached to the outside of the fruit. This is the so-called
　　　　　　　　　　enoko willow, meharu willow. Hair
　　　　　　　　　　emerges. This is cattail willow. In recent years, barbarian doctors favor using
　　　　　　　　　　something called kinana, which comes from the Red Hair people (Dutch). Its efficacy is
　　　　　　　　　　said to be very good. Kinaen is a salt extracted from the resin of kinana.
　　　　　　　　　　Though kinana is equated with our country's water yang,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　the bark's efficacy is said to be completely absent. Tamarisk willow is also
　　　　　　　　　　　　　called kawaya and nagi. Tei refers to sanshun willow, which is
　　　　　　　　　　　　　a different species. Could kinana be the bark of this tree?

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　8th day of late early spring, Teiyū year
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Drawn from life

"Chikinkō" states:
　　　Ingen willow　　　The leaves are large, and the green is substantial like river willow
　　　　　　　　　　　This is indeed naturally correct

Same record:
　　　Midori willow　　　Used in ikebana, it is the mature form of enokoyonagi
　　　　　　　　　　　This is again a different species. The gray (color) is small, and though the tree appears
　　　　　　　　　　　similar, it differs and has many flowers. Used for hedges.
　　　　　　　　　　　This is also water yang. Tamarisk willow is a separate variety.
　　　　　　　　　　　Tamarisk willow is weeping willow. What is simply called willow
　　　　　　　　　　　is weeping willow.
