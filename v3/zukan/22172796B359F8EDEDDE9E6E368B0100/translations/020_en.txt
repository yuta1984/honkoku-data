The "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia), Fragrant Herbs section states:
　　　Spring orchid　　Commonly called: hokuri





Various texts state:
　　　Hōshunsaki (spring orchid)
　　　　　Also: spring orchid ("Chikinsho")


　　　I say:
　　　　The "Yamato Honzō" records hakuri. This is a mountain plant
　　　　resembling the secluded orchid, with grooved leaves that are triangular-like.
　　　　The flower also resembles the secluded orchid and has a subtle fragrance. Thus, in the "Kyokutei
　　　　Saijiki" (Seasonal Record), Spring section, there is mention of spring orchid flowers called "hakuri."
　　　　One should know that the hakuri in the "Honzō" is the spring orchid.
　　　　This matches the explanations given.


　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Observed on the 7th day of early Koshō, 8th year of Bunsei, year of the Rooster
