The "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia), Fragrant Herbs section, states:
　　　Spring orchid (Hokuri)　　Commonly called　hokuri
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　【Miscellaneous Grass】




Various books state:
　　　Herald of spring (Shunran)
　　　　　Also spring orchid (from Chikinsho)


　　　I say:
　　　　The "Yamato Honzō" records Hakuri. This is a mountain grass
　　　　resembling the secluded orchid with grooved leaves that appear triangular
　　　　The flowers also resemble the secluded orchid and have a subtle fragrance. Therefore, Kyōtei's
　　　　"Saijiki" (Seasonal Records), Spring section, contains spring orchid flowers and reads them as
　　　　Hakuri. One should know that the Hakuri in "Honzō" is the spring orchid.
　　　　The explanations correspond well.
