　　　　　　　　　　The Kiriishi-ju (Uguisunoki tree) puts out buds before all other trees, beginning in the twelfth lunar month.
　　　　　　　　　　It blooms at the start of spring (Risshun). Its fruit ripens in the third month. The Bencao states that the ■tao [unreadable] is unripe in the third month. The Ode to the Shu Capital states that the "vermilion ■" ripens in spring, yet [it actually] ripens in the fourth month.
　　　　　　　　　　The Kiriishi ripens from the beginning of the third month. Among tree fruits, those that bear fruit in the third month are the forerunners of all fruits. It was perhaps named so because this flower blooms at the same time the warbler first sings — so says the respected scholar Kaibara.

【Seal】 Miscellaneous Trees

The Bencao designates the plant with leaves smaller than those of the Qiu (catalpa) as 榎 (Enoki).
Upon examination, however, the Enoki tree is not of the Qiu family.
Therefore, the practice in Japan of reading 榎 as "E"
is likely an error. The Man'yōshū and the Junwamyōshō
both read the character 榎 as "E," but the source of this reading
is unclear. ○ There are two varieties of Enoki. One variety is called Muku-Enoki.
It is used for making shields for military encampments and battles, serving as protection against firearms and arrows.
The 榎 (Enoki) is said to be used for making horse saddles.
