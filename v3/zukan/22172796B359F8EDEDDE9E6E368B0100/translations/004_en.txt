## Hōjakusō (宝鐸草 / Fairy Bells)

**According to the Honzō scholars:** Manjutak (万寿竹; *Disporum* sp.) — [identified as] Hōjakusō

**Hōjakusō** (宝鐸草; *Disporum smilacinum* or related species)

> **According to the Saijiki (almanac/seasonal record):** "Hōjaku-hana" (Treasure-bell flower)

[Category: Herbaceous plants]

Sketched from life on the twenty-fifth day of the Kosei month (third lunar month), in the year Kinoe-Saru (甲申, 1844).

## Ezo Sumire (蝦夷菫 / Hokkaido Violet)

**According to the Chikinsho (地錦抄):** Ōsumire-sō (大菫草; Large violet)

**According to the Gunsho (various classical texts):** The character "菫" (*kin*) is not the same as the character for "Sumire" (violet). The character 菫 refers to *Kyūkin* (芨菫; a different plant altogether), etc.

**Ezo Sumire** (蝦夷菫; Ezo/Hokkaido Violet)

**According to the Honzō scholars:** Kokinsō (胡菫草)

Sketched from life on the twenty-ninth day of the Kyōshō month (second lunar month), in the same year (1844).
