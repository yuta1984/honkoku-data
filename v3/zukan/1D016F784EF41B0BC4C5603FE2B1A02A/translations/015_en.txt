Scutellaria (Ōgon) grows in Shiki, Senkoku, and Enkō. Today it exists in all the nearby prefectures of Senshoku, Katō, and Kōsei.
The seedling length is over one shaku, the stem is coarse like chopsticks. The leaves grow in clusters from the ground in four directions,
resembling purple grass. The height is about one shaku, and there are also single-stemmed varieties. The leaves are slender and blue-green, growing in opposing pairs.
In the sixth month, purple flowers bloom. The roots are yellow like Anemarrhena, with coarse texture and length of four to five sun. In the second and eighth months,
the roots are harvested, dried in the sun, and used. According to the Gofu Honzō, Scutellaria is also named Intō, with one name being Nai-
kyo. In the second month it produces red-yellow leaves, growing in opposing pairs on four sides. The stem interior is hollow, sometimes square or round.
Height is three to four shaku. Flowers are purple-red. In the fifth month the fruits are black, roots are yellow. Harvested from the second to ninth months.
Other names include Fuchō, Kūchō, Naikyo, Ōbun, Keigon,
and Dofu 《Marginal note: Taste is bitter, greatly cold, non-toxic. Treats bone steaming, cold-heat, back-and-forth, intestinal-stomach dysfunction. Treats boils, expels pus, treats breast abscesses and back eruptions》

Flower Strawberry (Hana-ichigo)
The flower shape resembles the thousand-petaled flowers of Edo cherry blossoms. In the center there is
turmeric color. The leaves resemble the leaves of mountain rose grass.
