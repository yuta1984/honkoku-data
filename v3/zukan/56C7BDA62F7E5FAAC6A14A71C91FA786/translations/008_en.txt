One variety
　Camphor chrysanthemum (ryūnō-kiku)

　This is a white chrysanthemum that grows wild in the mountains. It is found in the mountains of Mino, Ōmi, Shimotsuke Ashio, Musashi Tamagawa, and various other provinces. The variety from Tanba Kameyama has large flowers. It grows to about two shaku in height. The leaves are small and the fragrance resembles camphor. This is the camphor chrysanthemum recorded in the Kangxi Chrysanthemum Manual, also called kogindai (small silver platform). This is a type of white chrysanthemum as described by Su Song.

Autumn chrysanthemum　Medium chrysanthemum
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　This variety is called asahi

　The form resembles sweet chrysanthemum and grows four to five shaku tall. There are also Chinese varieties, and Japanese varieties are extremely numerous. Because the flower colors change when planted from seed, the number of their names cannot be known. This is described in detail in the Chrysanthemum Classic. In China, the variety names are listed in works such as Fan Chengda's Chrysanthemum Manual, Kangxi Chrysanthemum Manual, Flower History Left Volume, Comprehensive Fragrant Manual, and Secret Flower Mirror.
