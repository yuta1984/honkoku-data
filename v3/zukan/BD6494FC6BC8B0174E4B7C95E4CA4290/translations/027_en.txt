## Kakitsubata (燕子花 / Rabbit-ear Iris / *Iris laevigata*)　【Category: Flowering Plants】

---

From *Yamato Honzō* (Great Japanese Materia Medica):

　　Kakitsubata (燕子花, "swallow flower") — Two color varieties exist.

　　Since ancient times, popular custom has equated *tojaku* (杜若) with *kakitsubata*, but this is incorrect.
　　*Tojaku* refers to the plant known as *yabumiōga* (藪茗荷, *Pollia japonica*).
　　Its Japanese name is *Kaoyobana* (皃吉花, "beautiful-faced flower"), which appears in the *Yūzōgyoku* (由蔵玉).

---

From *Wamyō Ruijushō* (Japanese Names Classified and Annotated):

　　Gekisō (劇草) = kakitsubata. In Su Jing's commentary on the *Bencao* (Materia Medica), it states: "Gekisō is also known by the name *barin* (馬藺, *Iris lactea*)."
　　Japanese name: Kakitsubata (加木豆波太)

---

From *Wakan Sansai Zue* (Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia), section on aquatic plants:

　　　　Kakitsubata (燕子花) — Japanese reading: Kakitsubata (加岐豆波太)

　　The *Zhangzhou Fuzhi* (漳州府志, Gazetteer of Zhangzhou Prefecture) states: "The purple flowers closely resemble swallows in form; each stem bears several petals. The people of Zhang call it *Ziyan* (紫燕, 'purple swallow')."

　〇 The *Wamyō Shōshō* uses *Gekisō* (劇草) and reads it as *kakitsubata*, or in some cases reads *tojaku* (杜若) as *kakitsubata* — both of these are incorrect. *Gekisō* is in fact *barin* (馬藺, *Iris lactea*).
