**Second day of the fourth month of summer, Kōshin year (1824)**
**Drawn from life in the rear garden**

【Vegetable Category】

---

**From the *Yamato Honzō* (Great Japanese Materia Medica):**

**Daikon Radish (蘿蔔, *luóbo*)**
Also called 萊菔 (*Raifuku*) and 温菘 (*Onsō*).
Japanese name: *Ōhone*

---

**Varieties of daikon radish, recorded here:**

**From the *Qunfang Pu* (群芳譜, "Register of Many Fragrant Plants"):**

Water Radish (*Morikuchi Daikon*) — Pickled in sake lees in Morikuchi, Kawachi Province.
The *Honzō* states that it is the same plant as the one called "Manzingluobo" (蔓菁蘿蔔).

**From the *Jiangyin County Gazetteer* (江陰縣志):**

Yanghua Radish (Three-Month Radish; *Mochi Daikon*) — A variety of the Three-Month Radish; its leaves spread along the ground as it grows. The root is large.

Summer Radish and Winter Radish.

Siam Radish (*Shamuro Daikon*) — Its seeds came from Siam (present-day Thailand).
In the capital (Kyoto), it is called *Ingen-na* (bean greens).
The leaves are large; the root is red, and the flesh has a reddish swirling pattern.
Called *Uzu Daikon* (swirling radish); the center of the leaves is red.

**From the *Jiuhuang Bencao* (救荒本草, "Materia Medica for Relief of Famines"):**

Wild Radish (*Noshū Hatano Daikon*) — From Hatano in Sagami Province. In the western regions, it is called "small daikon."

Ibuki Radish — Grows wild on Mt. Ibuki in Ōmi Province (present-day Shiga Prefecture). The tip of the root resembles a mouse's tail. Also called "Mouse Radish" (*Nezumi Daikon*).

Purple Radish, Red Radish, Black Radish.

Satsuma Radish — Larger than the common daikon, with a thick skin.

Miyashige Radish — Produced in Owari Province (present-day Aichi Prefecture).

**From *Honzō Waka* (本草和解, "Japanese Commentary on the Materia Medica"):**

**Daikon Radish (蘿蔔, *Rabu*)**
Alternative names: 萊菔 (*Raifuku*), 蘆菔 (*Rohi*), 雹突 (*Hōtotsu*), 紫花菘 (*Shikasō*), 温菘 (*Unsō*), 土酥 (*Toso*)
Japanese name: *Ōhone*

---

**From the *Wamyō Ruijushō* (和名類聚抄, "Japanese Names Classified and Collected"):**

温菘 (*Kōhone*) — The *Shokkyō* (Food Classic) of Yǔ Xī states: "温菘 (pronounced *shū*); Japanese name: *Ōhone*."

From the same text:

葍 (*Ōhone*) — The *Erya Jizhu* (Collected Commentary on the Erya) states: "葍 (pronounced *fuku*; Japanese name *Ōhone*; commonly written with the two characters 大根 [*daikon*])."
The *Kenmyōen* states: "萊菔 (upper character pronounced *rai*)."
The *Honzō* (Materia Medica) states: "蘆菔 (pronounced *gan*)."
Mèng Shēn's *Shokkyō* states: "蘿菔 (upper character pronounced *ra*; now considered to be 萊)."
All of "萊菔," "蘿菔," etc. are common names for 葍.

---

**From the *Imyō Bunruishō* (異名分類抄, "Classified Collection of Alternative Names"):**

Alternative names for daikon: *Kakamikusa*, *Suzushiro* (one of the Seven Spring Herbs of *Jinjitsu*), *Konohana*, and 蘆菔 (*Konohana*)
