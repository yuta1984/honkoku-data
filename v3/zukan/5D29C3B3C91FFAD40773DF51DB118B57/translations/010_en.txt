When examining pharmacological texts about honey, there are four types: stone honey, tree honey, earth honey, and domestic honey. For medicinal use, nothing surpasses high-quality tree honey. Bees make their nests in hollow spaces near the roots of large trees, opening small holes in warm southern locations for entry and exit. This is common in deep mountains where no human footprints exist. What people nowadays often raise in their homes originally came from taking these wild colonies, called domestic honey. In Kumano, Kii Province, many people raise them as an industry. What is called mountain honey is of the highest quality. Others are produced from various provinces. Those from Tosa are considered good quality. After years, it solidifies like congealed white sand, becomes imperishable, and has a rich, sweet taste. Fresh honey has the fragrance of plum blossoms, with an aftertaste that overflows beyond words. Moreover, products from Nabeshima and Omura in Hizen Province have an exquisite rich taste and are presented to the shogunate annually, being prepared for official use. Generally, honey bees make honey from late spring to late autumn, going out and coating flower stamens on their legs, bringing back flower nectar, packing it in their combs, and fermenting it into honey, much like brewing sake. In winter, fearing wind and cold, they hibernate in their combs. During that time, they eat the honey they previously fermented and stored as food. Honey is the bees' food, so when harvesting honey, one must leave food for them and not take everything. If everything is taken, they will starve to death.

To make this honey, they use large trees in high mountains that have naturally rotted and become hollow inside over many years, or nests built between large stones that have been there for years. Woodcutters discover these and in early winter cut the trees or drill through stones to extract the nests and obtain honey - this is the so-called tree honey and stone honey. These are collectively called mountain honey. The name "mountain honey" already appears in Zongshi's writings. This is of extremely high quality.
