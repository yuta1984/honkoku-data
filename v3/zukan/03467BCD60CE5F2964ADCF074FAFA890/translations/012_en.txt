Jūyaku (Ten medicines)　Found abundantly in any location. In spring, it produces shoots from old roots. Height is seven to eight
　sun, and those in fertile soil can reach one to two shaku. Leaves grow alternately. The shape is round
　with pointed tips, closely resembling sweet potato leaves. When cut, it has an odor that is
　unbearable. Around the fourth and fifth months, it produces flowers on the stem. White four-petaled flowers, with two petals
　large and two petals small. In the center, yellow stamens gather in a circle with a length of five to six
　bu. They are like the stamens of Begonia grandis flowers. There are also varieties with seven to eight petals, and some with a thousand petals.
　There are also varieties with red and white spots on the leaves. All of them have shoots that
　wither after autumn.
　　　　　　Main properties
　　△Acrid and warm, with slight toxicity. Treats rectal prolapse, and when decocted and taken internally, treats gonorrhea.
