**Japanese name for Sekikōju (石香薷 / Rock Elsholtzia):**
　Nezumiabura　　　(name used in Ōshū / northern Honshū)
　Inuararaki　　　　(name from the *Engishiki*)
　Inue　　　　　　　(name from the *Wamyōshō*)
　Naginata-kōju　　(name used in the western provinces)

It is not incorrect to call this plant "Naginata-kōju" (Long-halberd Fragrant Herb). The name "Naginata (長刀, halberd)" comes from the way the flower spike bends and curves sideways, resembling the shape of a naginata (a Japanese pole weapon with a curved blade). There are several varieties of Kōju (香薷).

Kōju comes in two varieties: large-leaved and small-leaved. The large-leaved variety is this one (Naginata-kōju). The small-leaved variety is Sekikōju (石香薷, Rock Elsholtzia), which grows abundantly on rocks in the mountains; because it grows on rocks, its leaves are all thin. Among the varieties are Naginata-kōju and Ha-kōju; the variety whose flower spike resembles that of knotweed (Polygonum/Persicaria) is called "Naginata."
