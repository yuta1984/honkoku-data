## Autumn Shitōsai (Senbon'yari — *Leibnitzia anandria*)

**[Baien's commentary]**
　That which opens its flowers in spring is called *Shitōsai* (匙頭菜, *Hitōzai*);
　that which sends up a flower stalk in autumn but does not open its flowers is called *Senbon'yari* (千本鎗, "Thousand Spears").
　　　　《As recorded in the *Chikinsho*》

　　　　　　23rd day of the 7th month, autumn, Year of Jussshi (Inu-ne / 1802 or 1815) — Drawn from life
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　【Herb Category】

---

*Senbonyari* (千本槍) is none other than this *Shitōsai* (匙頭菜).

In spring, it puts forth seedlings in the first month, and flowers in the third month. Its flowers closely resemble those of the dandelion (*Tampopo*, 蒲公英), though considerably smaller, and are pinkish-white in color. After the flowers fall, it produces downy seed-heads (絮, *ho*). The seedlings grow most vigorously as summer approaches, and by the sixth month, the plant sends up several dozen or more flower stalks. The form of the plant is as shown in the illustration. The buds, poised as if about to open, in fact never open, and simply cease. It is from this characteristic form that the name "Senbon'yari" (Thousand Spears) was given. There is no other plant that flowers twice in a single year. The plant's proper (spring) flowers are recorded in Volume 3 of the *Spring Section*. It is one species with two names, and the variation (*henka*) that belongs to this single species is rare.

As for the *Chikinsho* — the explanation given therein has not yet been fully verified. Baien (Chokkyaku) cultivated several kinds of grasses in his own garden plot, and by examining each plant from the form of its flowers, he arrived at the proper assignment of names with accuracy. The plant that may rightfully be called a "remarkable species" (*kishu*) is surely this *Shitōsai* (Senbon'yari).
