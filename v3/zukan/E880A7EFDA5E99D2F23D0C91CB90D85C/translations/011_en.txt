*(Continued from the previous page)* When the cedar leaves are removed and the bees are allowed to come and go freely, they settle comfortably into the box and will not flee.

**Domesticated Hive Bee (家蜂 / Mitsubachi)**

**Collected Commentaries:** Li Shizhen states: "One variety, kept by households in containers, is called the domestic bee (家蜂). They are uniformly small and slightly yellow, and their honey is invariably rich and delicious."

○ The *Chōtei Zōho Tōshukō Chifū Zensho* (重訂増補陶朱公致富全書), Volume 2, states: "During the season when rapeseed flowers are in full bloom, bees are collected from old cavities in the mountains and fields. Either a basket woven from thorny branches is made, or a watertight wooden box (water box) is constructed. Both ends are sealed with mud, and one or two holes are opened to allow the bees to pass in and out. A separate small door is made, which is opened regularly to sweep the interior clean and prevent other creatures from encroaching. Place it near the eaves,

removing spider webs, and keeping away wasps, ground bees, and wood-boring insects. In the ninth and tenth months, when the weather gradually turns cold and all flowers have been exhausted, one should leave sufficient honey for the bees to consume during the winter months, and harvest only the surplus to produce honey and beeswax. In the third month of spring, clean the hive and resume care using the same method as before. If kept for a long time, the colony will thrive. In each hive, only one queen bee should be retained. When the queen bee is about to divide the colony and a swarm flies out, scatter crushed earth over them to collect them, and place them in a separate box."
