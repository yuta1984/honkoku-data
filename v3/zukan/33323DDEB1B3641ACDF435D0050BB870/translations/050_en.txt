Edo cherry is also a variant of this Kirigayatsu. However, Kirigayatsu scatters readily without lingering attachment to falling petals, which is pleasant. Edo cherry is not inferior in flower quality but does not scatter, instead withering while attached to the branch. In this regard, Kirigayatsu is considered superior. Elder Kassho also called it the finest cherry variety, with white color slightly tinged with crimson and the longest stems. Also, at Jion-ji temple in Sakai, Senshu Province, there is

a cherry called Kurumaga-eshi, but this is not a fine variety.
