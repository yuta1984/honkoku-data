Lotus Leaf (Kayō)　　Lotus Leaf


　According to Ranzan's theory, the small leaves that emerge first are called "zeni-ha" (coin leaves), which is termed Kasen 《Shakumei》. The leaves that emerge next are somewhat larger and float on the water surface, called "mizu-ha" (water leaves), which is termed Gūka 《Shakumei》. The leaves that emerge after that have long stems and rise above the water surface, called "tachi-ha" (standing leaves), which is termed Kika 《Shakumei》. These emerge as stems in pairs from the nodes of the lotus root, with one being a leaf and the other being a flower.

Lotus Leaf Center (Kabana)

　This is the center of the lotus leaf where the stem attaches.
