One variety
　　　　Oyama-renge (Japanese Big-leaf Magnolia)
　　　　　　　　　　　It is said to have been brought from China during the Enpō era
　　　　　　　　　　　It grows into a tall tree. The leaves resemble those of mokuren (magnolia) but are thicker
　　　　　　　　　　　The flower shape resembles that of hō-no-ki (Japanese big-leaf magnolia) but is somewhat smaller
　　　　　　　　　　　and white in color. The leaf shape quite resembles kikō (balloon flower) blossoms
　　　　　　　　　　　The fruit also resembles that of hō-no-ki. The bark is white in color and has a
　　　　　　　　　　　bitter and pungent taste with a fragrant aroma. If the root bark were collected and used,
　　　　　　　　　　　it would likely be effective, but because it is rare, it is difficult to use
