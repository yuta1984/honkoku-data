One variety
　　　　Oyama-renge (Large Mountain Lotus)

It is said that this plant was brought from China during the Enpō era (1673-1681).
It grows into a tall tree, with leaves resembling those of the magnolia but thicker. The flower shape resembles that of the Japanese magnolia but is somewhat smaller and white in color. The leaf shape is quite similar to balloon flower blossoms.
The fruit also resembles that of the Japanese magnolia but is smaller. The bark is white in color, with a bitter and pungent taste and aromatic qualities. If the root bark were collected and used, it would likely be effective, but since it is rare, the leaves cannot be used.
