Pinellia (Arisaema)
Mountain ginseng

Tennamiso (In Kaga Province, this is a corrupted form of the word Tennansho/Arisaema)

It grows in shaded areas of deep mountains in Shimotsuke Province's Nikko, Hitachi Province's Tsukuba, and other locations. It has one root and one stem, which is light green with white spots. The leaves consist of nine or eleven leaflets per stem, while smaller plants around the periphery have three to five leaflets.

[Left page]
The flower emerges from the fork between two leaves. The base is tubular and the tip resembles a single petal of a lotus flower. Its shape is similar to Pinellia, green in color with purple-black vertical stripes. The center is round with a long pistil. The tip does not become hair-like but is yellowish-white. This pistil bears fruit at its tip. When ripe, it turns red and resembles the fruit of Kadsura japonica (hinankazura). It grows close to the ground. Large roots are about one sun in length, somewhat flattened, with subsidiary roots on the sides being rare. According to Su Song's explanation, Arisaema "forms seeds in clusters like pomegranate seeds, red in color." This corresponds to that description.
