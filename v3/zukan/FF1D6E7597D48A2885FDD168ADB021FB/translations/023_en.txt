Arisaema (Tennansho)
　Wild ginseng

Tennamiso (a dialect from Kaga Province, which is a linguistic corruption of "Tennansho")

It grows in the shaded areas of deep mountains in Shimotsuke Province's Nikko, Hitachi Province's Tsukuba, and other locations. One stem grows from one root, pale green in color with white spots. The leaves number 9 or sometimes 11 per stem. Smaller specimens with subsidiary roots around them have 3 to 5 leaves.

【Left Page】
The flower emerges from the fork between two leaves. The base is tubular and the tip resembles a single lotus petal. The shape is similar to Pinellia, green in color with purple-black vertical stripes. The center is round with long stamens, without hair-like appendages at the tip. The color is pale yellowish-white. This stamen produces fruit at its tip. When ripe, it turns red and resembles the fruit of southern schisandra (Hinankazura). The root that grows down into the ground, when large, is about one inch long, slightly flattened, and rarely has subsidiary roots attached to the side. According to Su Song's description, Arisaema "produces fruit in spikes resembling pomegranate seeds and red in color" - this is exactly that plant.
