Female Sea Bream (Otai)

The "Yamato Honzō" states: "Sea bream has both male and female. The male has a pale black color with horns on its back. The female is red in color, beautiful in form, with the best nature and taste, superior to the male."

Sea Bream - At Kujūkuri, they are caught in March using rope boats. The bait is sardines.

The "Sancai Tuhui" and "Shijing" state: "Sea bream is sweet and cool, resembling crucian carp but with red fins. The Japanese name is Tahi."
The "Minshu Nansan" states: "The spiny-finned fish resembles crucian carp but is larger, with red-purple fins. Some call it 'strange,' 'auspicious,' or 'red,' all referring to how its fins differ in color from other fish."

Upon consideration, common people call flat things "flat (tahira)." Since this is not recorded in our country's "Sancai Tuhui" and other works, it can be understood that this was a rare item in China. In recent times, these exist in the seas of Ningbo, Zhejiang Province. Presumably, when Chinese ships frequently come to Japan in spring and summer, dolphinfish attach to the ships and come along. During autumn and winter return voyages, local sea bream attach to ships and many cross to China.

Sea bream resembles crucian carp in shape but is flattened, with scales and fins of pale reddish-white color. When separated from the tide, they turn red, with fins becoming particularly crimson. The flesh is white and delicious, considered the finest quality in our country. Throughout the four seasons, many are found in all provinces, with red varieties being most common. Generally one to two feet long, the small ones measuring one to two inches are called "kasuko bream." Those caught in the inland seas of Settsu and Izumi are collectively praised as "front fish." The products of Akashi Bay in Harima Province are also excellent. In northern regions around Niigata, large specimens of three to four feet exist. Those from the western seas around Tsushima are short in form with large eyes. None of these match the taste of Settsu and Harima sea bream. Popular tradition says: "Western sea bream that cross Awa Naruto in spring and summer to enter Harima and Settsu regions develop large bones."
