Busshōjin (Buddha's Palm Ginseng) 《Note: Isei Kenbunki》　Kanonigekusa 《Note: Wamyōshō》

This is bamboo-jointed ginseng. It exists in mountains throughout all provinces. The shoots and leaves resemble the Korean variety. It grows in the shaded northern slopes of deep mountains. The root resembles izui and has joints. The taste is bitter. The fruit is round, green when unripe, and turns red when ripe.
