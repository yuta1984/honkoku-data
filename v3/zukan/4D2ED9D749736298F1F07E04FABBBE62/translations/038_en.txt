Atractylodes lancea (Sōjutsu) - This is the Chinese variety. The leaves have no divisions and resemble those of Inula japonica, being hard and growing clasping the stem. The flowers are white in color, with the same shape as Atractylodes japonica. The roots are like a string of beads, with pale yellow flesh. When broken, they contain much resinous sap. This is a superior grade.

There are very pale yellow-brown spots.

One variety - This is also said to be a Chinese variety. The leaves resemble those of Chaenomeles (quince), being deep green in color with a lustrous appearance. The leaves do not clasp the stem. The roots resemble those of Japanese Atractylodes lancea and the flowers are white.
