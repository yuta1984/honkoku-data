## Rehmannia Flower / Yamato Rehmannia
### Komatome-gusa (Horse-stopping Grass)

　　　　　　　　1st望 day of the 4th month, Mizunoe-uma year — Copied

---

【Medicinal Herb】

**As stated by Kan (環):**
Those with yellow flowers are considered the genuine variety. The medicinal potency of this plant's flowers is thin (mild). There are also those with white flowers, which are called "white rehmannia" (白地黄). In Kōchi-gun, Yamato Province, there is a village called Jioumura, which is said to have been the first place to produce rehmannia in ancient times; even now, those from that area surpass those from other places.

---

**As stated in the *Jiuhuang Bencao*:**
The seedling of rehmannia is popularly called "Babami" (婆婆嬭, "grandmother's milk").

Northern people call it "Gyūnyūshibana" (牛嬭子花, "cow's milk flower").

**As stated by Honzōka (herbalists):**
Komatome-gusa (胡面莾)

**As stated in the "Moist Herbs" section of the *Zōho Tashiki-hen*:**
Rehmannia (地黄, Chiwō) — Japanese name: Saohime (左於比米).
[Alternate names] ■ (Ko, read as "Ko/To"), Ki (芑, read as "Ki/起"), Chizui (地髄, "marrow of the earth") [from the *Bencao Jing*].
　　　　　　　※The same names are also recorded in the *Wamyō Ruijushō*, and are therefore omitted here.

## Bletilla / Chinese Ground Orchid (Shiran)

**As stated in the Mountain Herbs section of *Wakan Sansai Zue*:**
Bletilla (Shiran / Shikei). Alternate names: Hakkyū (白給), Kankon (甘根), Renkyūsō (連及草).
[Tang-era Chinese pronunciation: Bekkyū] — In the *Konkōmyō-kyō* (Suvarṇaprabhāsa Sūtra), it is called "Gōdatsura Kasshita" (岡達羅喝悉多).
Commonly known as Shiran (紫蘭, "purple orchid"), also called Shikei (紫蕙, "purple orchid").

△ Upon reflection: Bletilla is commonly also called "Shikei." Its flowers are reddish-purple in color, resembling orchid flowers, and so on.
