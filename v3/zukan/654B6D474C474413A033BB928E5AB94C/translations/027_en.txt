**Kanzō (Hemerocallis / Daylily)**　　Alternative name: Rokusō (鹿葱)
　　Drawn with leaves　　　　　　　　【Vegetable category】

　　　　Transcribed during Zuihin (fifth month), Mizunoe-uma year,　　　【Medicinal herb category】
　　　　　　on the last fifth day

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Yamato Honzō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　recorded in the 【Flowering Plants】 section

---

**Kango Shō** (Glossary of Chinese Terms) states:
　　Japanese name: Wasure-gusa (わすれぐさ, "Forgetting Grass")

**Kemmeien** (Dictionary of Names) states:
　　Kanzō (萱草)
　　Also known as: Bōyū (忘憂, "Dispelling Sorrow")

In my personal library, it is written as 藼草 (kanzō),
and also as 蕿草, 諼草, and 忘憂草 — all referring to the same plant.

---

　　　　　　　Also in my personal library:
　　　　　　　Ginan-sō (冝男草)　　Kijo-sō (妓女草)

---

　　　　　　　I (Baien) observe:
　　　　　　　　　The Bencao Gangmu (Compendium of Materia Medica) states that kanzō
　　　　　　　　　comes in three varieties: red, yellow, and purple. However,
　　　　　　　　　the purple-flowered variety remains uncertain to me.
　　　　　　　　　That said, kanzō does indeed come in several varieties;
　　　　　　　　　I myself have observed six kinds.
