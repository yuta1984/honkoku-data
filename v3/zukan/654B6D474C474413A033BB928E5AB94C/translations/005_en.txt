【Giboshi (Hosta)】

According to "Yamato Honzō" (Japanese Herbal):
　　Yùzān Flower (Jade Hairpin Flower)
　　　　Alternative name: Hakukaku (White Calyx)
　　　In Kyoto, it is called "Korai Giboshi" (Korean Giboshi)
　　　In Tsukushi (Kyushu), it is called "Giboshi"
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Recorded in the year Kōshin (Kanoe-tatsu)
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Copied in mid-May

　　Gin-hōju (Silver Pearl)　Details unknown
　　　　Chinese name: Yùzān (Jade Hairpin)　※From "Saijiki" (Almanac)


From "Zōki" (Stored Records):
　Hakukaku-senkwa (White Crane Immortal Flower)
　　Also listed as "Sōka" (Onion Flower)
　　Whether this refers to "Hitomoshi"
　　requires further investigation
　　Notably, "Sō" (onion/葱) is also read as 《Hitomoshi》
　　There is also the common name
　　"Kibōshi"


　　　　According to "Zōho Tashiki-hen" (Expanded Encyclopedia of Knowledge), Poisonous Plants section:
　　　　Yùzān (Gyokusan)　《Japanese name》　《Upon current examination: Korobi, commonly called Gibōshi》　《Additional alternative name from expanded edition》Hakukaku-sen (White Crane Immortal)
