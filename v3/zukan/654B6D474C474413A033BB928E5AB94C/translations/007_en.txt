Shagan (Blackberry lily): According to the Classification Notes on Alternative Names, the Kogoshūi states "pressed with heavenly pressing grass and fanned with bird fans," which is commonly called hiōgi (fan iris) today.

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　with fine spots on the petals. This is not shagan. Shagan is

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　flower form resembles iris flowers but smaller, grayish-white

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　butterfly flower. Dōan distinguished these

　　　　　There are three names for shagan. The Buddhist shagan is
　　　　　　a fierce beast resembling a blue-yellow dog that eats people
　　　　　　and can climb green trees well. What Ruan Gong called shagan overlooking layered
　　　　　cities refers to a tree, particularly tall ones. The Hon-
　　　　　zō's shagan is an herb, namely what people
　　　　　cultivate today. The character "sha" is pronounced with the "shin-ya" cut, sounding like
