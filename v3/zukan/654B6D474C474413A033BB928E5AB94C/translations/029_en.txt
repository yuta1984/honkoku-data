Senshun-ra (Chinese: Cut Spring Gauze) {{Japanese name: gamuhi}} Alternative name: Senhō-ra (Cut Red Gauze)
{{Senshura}} Also called Ganpi (Eyelid). Hyakkyō states:

According to folk legend, when Dharma the Great Master was facing the wall for nine years, not wanting to sleep, he cut off his own eyelids and discarded the upper and lower lids onto the soil, whereupon this plant grew. Its flowers are flesh-red in color.
Because of this resemblance, it is called Ganpi (Eyelid).

Yamato Honzō states:
Tochū (Mayumi) Called masaki
The leaves resemble orange leaves but are thick, and throughout the four seasons
do not wither. Height does not
exceed six feet, used for hedging. When the fruit ripens
it splits and has red seeds inside, called masaki
etc. The vines of masaki grow
separately. Both leaves and fruit are the same. Vine-type
masaki. Round ○ masaki vines are the same
plant. One variety of hibari-masaki is not a vine.
Also there are thin-leaf ○ white-leaf varieties.
Tochū is mayumi (spindle tree). Those that produce cotton fiber when the stem
is broken all
bear the name tochū,
it is said.
