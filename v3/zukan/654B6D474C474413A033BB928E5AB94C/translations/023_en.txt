Crape Myrtle (Hyakujitsukō)　Purple crape myrtle, tickle tree related to
　　　　Monkey slip (saruberi)
　　　Copied in the 6th month of Kanoto-mi year

The Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia, Shrub section records:
　　　Hundred-day red (crape myrtle)　Tickle tree
　　　　　　　　Purple crape myrtle flower
　　　　From early July to September there are
　　　　flowers, and the light purple-red flowers reflect
　　　　in mountain valleys, hence named hundred-day red


　　　　　　　　　　　　Baien states:
　　　　　　　　　　　　The Three Talents Encyclopedia shrub section
　　　　　　　　　　　　lists hundred-day red and monkey slip separately, but these are one plant
　　　　　　　　　　　　The purple crape myrtle's bark is smooth and soft,
　　　　　　　　　　　　so it's called monkey slip, and the flower is called
　　　　　　　　　　　　hundred-day red. Its flowering period lasts
　　　　　　　　　　　　long, reaching a hundred days, hence
　　　　　　　　　　　　the name. In China there are blue-colored flowers
　　　　　　　　　　　　and white flowers, but in Japan
　　　　　　　　　　　　there are none other than red-purple colored ones
