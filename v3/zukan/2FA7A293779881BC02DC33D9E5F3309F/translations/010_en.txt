Galangal (kōryōkyō)　　Alpine iris　Galanga《marginal note: Japanese｜Dutch》

【Left page】
This is a Chinese species. It does not wither in winter. Its form resembles myoga, with a height of three to four shaku (approximately 90-120cm). The leaves are thick and glossy. It forms spikes of four to five sun (approximately 12-15cm), with flowers opening in pairs of calyxes per node. The shape resembles iris but is somewhat larger and pale red in color. The fruit is red and resembles mountain ginger seeds. This is called red cardamom. It corresponds to Shizhen's explanation. The root resembles mountain ginger but is enlarged.
