Mountain Ginger (sankyō)
　Flowering myoga

It grows in Izu Province's Takada District, as well as in Ise Province, Kii Province, Iyo Province, Suruga Province, and other regions. The leaves resemble ginger.

【Left page】
There are slight hairs. The stem is reddish-purple in color, over one shaku (30cm) in height. It does not wither in winter. Flowers bloom between the stems. The shape resembles alpine iris but is shorter and smaller, red in color. The fruit is round and red. The root resembles ginger but is thin and hard.
