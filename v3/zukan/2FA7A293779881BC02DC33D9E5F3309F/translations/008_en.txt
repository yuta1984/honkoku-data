Iris (tojaku)　Blue alpine iris

In Nagasaki it is called "wa no kōryōkyō" (Japanese galangal). Its form resembles alpine iris, about one shaku (30cm) in height. The stem is green, and being in a warm region, it withers in winter. The flower shape also resembles galangal, white in color with a pale red center. Both leaves and roots have a pleasant fragrance.

【Left page】
The root is yellow and tastes pungent. According to Hongjing's theory, "the leaves resemble ginger and have texture, similar to galangal but finer, with a pungent aromatic taste." Also, in "Guangdong Xinyu" it states: "Fresh grass fruit - people often cultivate it to use as spice. It is indeed iris, not the grass fruit found among vegetables. Its seedlings resemble cardamom, flowering in the third month forming spikes. The color is white with slight red. In the fifth and sixth months it bears fruit. Its root is superior to the leaves, with a pungent taste and warming properties that can ward off miasmatic qi." This is it. The predecessors' identification of it as Japanese iris or other plants are all errors. There is a separate detailed examination.
