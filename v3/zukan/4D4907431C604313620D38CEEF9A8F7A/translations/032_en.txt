【Garden Pea (Endō-mame)】

Wandou (豌豆; garden pea)

　　According to Shokkyō:
　　　　Nomame
　　　　Hiramame
　　　　Entōmame — there is also a variety with white flowers

Copied laterally from a specimen shared in the lower decade of Kosен (third lunar month), in the year Mizunoto-Hitsuji (癸未, 1823), in Katsushika
[Seal] Grain varieties (穀類)

According to Wamyō Ruijushō:
　　Noramame (野豆)　— The Honzōso states: "Wandou is also known by the name Yatō (野豆); its Japanese name is Noramame (乃良末女)"

According to the Supplemented Tashiki-hen, section on Legumes (菽豆類):
　Endou (豌豆)
　　Japanese name: Noramame (乃羅末米)
　　Alternative names:
　　　Koto (胡豆) — from Shūi (拾遺)
　　　Jūshuku (戎菽) — from Erya (尓雅)
　　Additional alternative names (from the supplemented edition):
　　　Kaikotsu-tou (回鶻豆) — Liaozhi and Yinshan Zhengyao write it as "Huihui-dou" (回回豆)
　　　Hitsutou (畢豆) — Tang History and Cui Shi's Yueling write it as "Bi-dou" (蹕豆); also Aozusho (青小豆) from Qianjin (千金)
　　　Aomandara-mame / Seihantou (青斑豆) — from Belu (別録); and Marui (麻累)
