## 【*Asebi* (馬酔木; *Pieris japonica*, Japanese andromeda)】

**According to the *Wakan Sansai Zue* (和漢三才図会; *Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia*), in the section on Shrubs (*Kanboku-rui*):**

　　*Asehonoki* (馬酔木) [Chinese-derived reading: *Asemi*] — [Japanese name:] *Asemi* (阿世美); in common parlance also called *Asebo* (アセボ).

---

**According to the *Bencao Gangmu* (本草綱目; *Compendium of Materia Medica*):**

　　*Shinboku* (梫木; *Ashibi* / *Ashimi*) [common name: *Asebo*]　*Masu-iki* / *Basuiki* (馬酔木; "horse-intoxicating tree")　[source: *Wamyō-shō* (和名抄)]

---

　　　　　　　　　　　　The *Yamato Honzō* states: if a horse eats the leaves of this plant, it will die.
　　　　　　　　　　　　Furthermore, the *Chikinsho* states: if a horse eats *Masu-iki* (馬酔木), it becomes intoxicated.
