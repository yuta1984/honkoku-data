　　　According to Bencao Gangmu, scallions have leaves that grow straight outward with hollow centers. When first sprouting they are called "scallion needles,"
　　　the leaves are called "scallion green," the skin is called "scallion robe," the stem is called "scallion white," and the juice inside the leaves is called
　　　"scallion ran." They can be seeded and planted.
　　　Winter scallions (also called compassionate scallions, frozen scallions, or great shrine scallions) decline in summer and flourish in winter
　　　Chinese scallions (also called original scallions) have very hard stems, do not yet bloom in spring but form clusters of blue-white color
　　　According to the commentary in Wakan Sansai Zue, they are called "one character" (hitomoji) because the leaves are round with no branches
　　　Probably winter scallions and Chinese scallions are originally the same - seeds are planted in the tenth month, leaves are harvested and eaten in the first month, and can be
　　　cut repeatedly, so they are commonly called "cut scallions" (kariki). The so-called Chinese scallions are these. After experiencing frost, the taste becomes extremely
　　　sweet and delicious, with warming properties that can dispel cold qi. These are the so-called winter scallions. Though one plant, they seem like many varieties
