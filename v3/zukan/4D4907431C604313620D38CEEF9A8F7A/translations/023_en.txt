According to Bencao Gangmu, onions have leaves that grow straight outward and are hollow inside. When first sprouting they are called onion needles,
the leaves are called onion green, the outer skin is called onion robe, the stem is called onion white, and the juice in the leaves is called
onion ran. They can be seeded and cultivated.
Winter onion (also called compassionate onion, frozen onion, or large shrine onion) declines in summer and flourishes in winter.
Chinese onion (also called original onion) has hard stems, and in spring before the flowers open they form clusters of blue-white color.
According to the explanation in Wakan Sansai Zue, because the leaves are round with no branches, they are called hitomoji (one character).
Essentially winter onion and Chinese onion are originally the same thing; seeds are planted in the tenth month, leaves are harvested and eaten in the first month, and can be
harvested repeatedly, so they are commonly called kari-gi (cutting onion). This is the so-called Chinese onion. After experiencing frost the taste becomes extremely
sweet and delicious, with warming properties that can dispel cold qi. This is the so-called winter onion. Though one plant, it appears to be many varieties.
