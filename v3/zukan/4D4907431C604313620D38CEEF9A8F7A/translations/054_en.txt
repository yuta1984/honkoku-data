Tennansho (Tennanseu)
　Alternative names　Kosho (Tiger Palm)　Kokotsu (Tiger Fat)　Oni-konnyaku (Demon Konjac)
　Japanese name　Oosoi
◦Mountain Konjac　Yama-konnyaku

　　　　In Honzo it is called kosho, small ones are called yuhatsu
　　　　Its shape is like konjac. It grows in spring from perennial roots. It resembles nan-
　　　　sei with extremely thin stems, three leaves growing per stem. It resembles han-
　　　　ge leaves but is larger. The flower resembles nansei but differs
　　　　　　　　It looks like a stirrup, hence the Japanese name Musashi-abumi
　　　　　　　　It is said the autumn fruit becomes vermillion-like
　　　　　　　　This is different. This plant has one flower
　　　　　　　　per stem and grows leaves like a palm. It closely
　　　　　　　　resembles konjac. It is called nansei

【Seal】Medicinal herb

Yamato Honzo says:
　　Shizhen says small ones are called yuhatsu. This is one variety.
　　Now observing, in the mountains there are small ones
　　resembling nansei. These should be yuhatsu. Yuhatsu being
　　a type of nansei is clear.
　◦Honzo Komoku states that tennansho blooms in May, resembling a snake's
　　head and is yellow in color. The rest, fruit and root all match
　　Tennansho is indeed the kosho of the original classic《Small ones are named yuhatsu》
　　Ancient prescriptions often used kosho, not mentioning tennansho
　　《The name tennansho began from the Tang period》Kosho is named for the leaf shape resembling it
　　Not because of the root. Nansei has round, white roots shaped like
　　the Southern Star constellation, hence named nansei
　◦Genshu respectfully says: What common people call tennansho is mountain kon-
　　jac. Nansei does not produce a mouse tail from the flower center but produces round beads like
　　the size of a little finger. Leaf stems are paired and
　　flowers emerge from their fork. Mountain konjac has one stem, one flower and is a separate plant

　　Kinoe-saru year, Kosho month, 15th day
　　Mountain forest sketching from life

Wakan Sansai Zue commentary states: Kosho is the original name. Wamyo-sho also has
kosho but lacks tennansho. Now conversely, there are no knowledgeable people about kosho. Presumably this is a variant
emerging from kosho subspecies named tennansho《One plant, different variety, different name》Recently also
producing variants《Such as kanki-so, musashi-abumi》. Since kosho and tennansho resemble each other but are
distinct. Also nansei has two types. Those with light blue flower clusters like mouse tails
do not exceed one foot in height. They are just like spoons or Buddhist halos. Those without mouse tails are about two feet
high. These are naturally one group, two species《Sansai Zue and Honzo necessarily show separate illustrations of kosho and tennansho, clearly treating them as two different plants》
