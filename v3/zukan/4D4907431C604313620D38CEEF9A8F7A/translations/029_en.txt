○According to Honkō, there are two types of kunugi. One type grows in clusters, and the large ones are named daki (kashiwa), also called large-leaf oak, with one name boku, kunugi.
○Kunugi, saku, ichii, hō, nara (nara) - various explanations are mixed in annotations. Now establishing separate sections to record each.
　　Kunugi (donguri, large nara), hō (kashiwa), nara (nara) all have similar leaves with distinctions of size. Kunugi is halfway between hō and nara.
　　Saku (hahaso) belongs to the same category as these three types but is a different thing, though the leaves are similar but not identical. Saku and ichii are
　　two names for one tree. Kunugi, saku-ichii, hō, kunugi, botanical (nara) wood, buna - one category. Their leaves wither and fall in winter. ○What Cao calls the new intersection of saku leaves should be considered.
