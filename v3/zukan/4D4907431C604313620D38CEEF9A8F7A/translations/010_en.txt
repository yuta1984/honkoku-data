Copied in the third month of Kanoto-Mi year

Tsutsuji (azalea) - called Dōdan

According to Wakan Sansai Zue, Fragrant Trees section:
Pine - Shōyō, also pine yellow, commonly called midori. Japanese name is matsu
Pine does not change color despite frost and snow, compared to a steadfast heart, called the tree of constancy

According to Wamyō Ruijū:
Pine - In the Book of Han, green pine is used as a tree
The reverse of shōyō, also appears written as banyan
In Tang Rhymes, the Japanese name is matsu

According to Complete Book of Agriculture, pine is the chief of a hundred trees, written with the tree radical and the character for "duke"
There are two-needle, three-needle, and five-needle varieties that do not change color through the four seasons

Yamato Honzō:
Pine - Both Japan and China appreciate this. Under thousand-year-old pines there is poria fungus
Above it is dodder, and at the roots is amber. All are good medicines
Pine resin is refined and applied to bowstrings. It has many other uses in ointments
"Matsu" means "to preserve," a tree that preserves longevity for a long time
The Records of the Grand Historian, Tortoise Strategy, says "Pine and cypress are chiefs among a hundred trees and guard gates and neighborhoods"
According to Records of the Grand Historian, when the First Emperor of Qin climbed Mount Tai, violent wind and rain arose and he rested under a tree
He then enfeoffed that tree as Fifth Rank Official. From the beginning of history it was not said what tree it was
But Ying Shao first said it was a pine, so it received the rank of official
