Violet (sumō-kusa), pronunciation: kin, Bitter Violet, Bitter Mallow, Japanese name: Sumire, commonly called Wrestling Grass
According to Su Gong in Honzō Kōmoku, violet vegetable (namely sumire), Liu Yuxi said that according to Shuowen, violet has roots like shepherd's purse,
leaves thin like smartweed, etc. This refers to bitter violet, and ancient people reversed the word order, just as they call licorice "greatly bitter"
In children's games, they pick them for tugging contests; when pulled, the one whose calyx breaks off loses, hence the name
Wrestling Grass. Among the three varieties of Bindweed, Violet, and Knife Grass, Violet is most famous.
