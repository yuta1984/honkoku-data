　　Violet (pronounced Kin)　Bitter Violet　Bitter Mallow　Japanese name　Sumire
　　　　　　　　　　　　Commonly called　Wrestling Grass

According to Honzō Kōmoku, Su Gong said that violet vegetable (namely Sumire). Liu Yuxi said that according to Shuowen, violet has roots like shepherd's purse and leaves thin like willow, etc. This refers to bitter violet, which is ancient inverted language (reverse speech), like calling licorice "great bitter."
Children play by picking two pods as hooks, and when pulling against each other, the side that cuts and drops the calyx is the loser, hence it is called Wrestling Grass. Among the three types - morning glory child, violet, and sword grass - violet is the most famous.

According to Wakan Sansai Zue, Accumulated Snow Grass is commonly
called Fence-piercer. The illustration in Honzō Shinzoku showing branches
is incorrect. The reading as Tsubokusa in Wamyōshō is also incorrect. Tsubo-grass is listed separately.

investigated.
