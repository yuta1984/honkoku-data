【Seal】Flowering plants

According to Wakan Sansai Zue, Wet Grass section:
　　Violet　Bitter Violet　Bitter Mallow　Japanese name: Su-mi-re, commonly called Sumo Grass

According to Honzō Kōmoku by Su Gong: Violet vegetable is (namely Sumire). Liu Yuxi said that according to Shuowen, violet has roots like shepherd's purse and leaves as thin as willow. Calling it "bitter violet" is an ancient reversed expression, like calling licorice "great bitter" (I say that what is described as having willow-like leaves was previously illustrated).
Children playfully pick two stems to hook together,
pulling against each other until the calyx breaks off - the one that breaks loses. Therefore it is
called Sumo Grass. Among the three types - morning glory, violet, and sword grass - violet is the most renowned.
