【Note: Rengyō (連翹; Forsythia, *Forsythia suspensa* / *F. viridissima*)】

**From the *Wakan Sansai Zue* (和漢三才図会; "Illustrated Sino-Japanese Encyclopedia"), section on Moisture-Loving Herbs (湿草類):**

　Wa-renkyō ("Japanese Renkyō / Japanese Forsythia"; 倭連翹)
　　The *Bencao* (本草) states that the "Small Renkyō" (小連翹) originates in the southern regions. What is called "Renkyō" in Japan today is precisely this plant. The variety that was the original "Renkyō" in Japan (本朝) once existed but is no longer found. Throughout the various provinces, people now simply refer to it as "Renkyō" (連翹).

　　〔Author's note〕Upon reflection, what the *Bencao* calls "Small Renkyō" (小連翹) is, in fact, an herbaceous plant (草本). It is the one known as "Kusayōyō" (草義陽). This is the point where the Japanese and Chinese accounts of Renkyō converge and correspond with each other. The nature of the Japanese Renkyō warrants further investigation.

**From the *Honzō Ikkagen* (本草一家言; "Personal Discourse on the Herbals"):**

　Renkyō (連翹)　　(Re-entry / appearing again)

　　The Small Renkyō (小連翹) has drooping branches.
　　The Large Renkyō (大連翹) has upright branches.
