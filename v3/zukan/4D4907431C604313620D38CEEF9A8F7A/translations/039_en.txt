【Note: Rengyō (連翹; *Forsythia* sp. / Japanese Forsythia)】

**From the *Wakan Sansai Zue* (和漢三才図会; *Illustrated Encyclopedia of Japan and China*), section on Aquatic Herbs (湿草類):**

　　**Wa-Renkyō (倭連翹; "Japanese Forsythia")**

　　　　The *Honzō* (本草; Chinese materia medica) states that "Shō-Renkyō (小連翹; Small Forsythia) originates from the southern regions." What is known as such in Japan today is precisely this plant. The domestically-produced (*honchō*) variety of Renkyō once existed in Japan but is no longer found. Throughout the various provinces, it is simply called "Renkyō" (連翹).

　　　　　　　[Annotation by the author, Baien]: Upon my own consideration written above: what the *Honzō* calls "Shō-Renkyō" (小連翹; Small Forsythia) is a herbaceous plant, also referred to as "Kusagiyō" (草義陽). It is in this regard that the Chinese and Japanese accounts of Renkyō converge. The matter of *Wa-Renkyō* (倭連翹; Japanese Forsythia) warrants further investigation.

**From the *Honzō Ikkagen* (本草一家言; *Personal Opinions on Materia Medica*):**

　　Renkyō (連翹) — [repeated entry]

　　　　Shō-Renkyō (小連翹; Small Forsythia): its branches droop downward.
　　　　Dai-Renkyō (大連翹; Large Forsythia): its branches grow upward.

---

**From the *Honzō Wage* (本艸和解; *Japanese Interpretations of Materia Medica*):**

　　Alternative names: Ren (連), Ikyō (異翹), Kanrenshi (旱蓮子), Range (蘭華), Sanren (三廉)
　　The root is called *Renshō* (連軺); also named *Chikkon* (竹根).
　　　　　　Japanese name: Itachigusa (伊多知久左; "Weasel Herb")

　　　　　　　　　[Note:] This accords well with the description of Renkyō in the *Kyūkō Honzō* (救荒本草; *Materia Medica for Relief from Famine*).
