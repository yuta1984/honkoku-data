## 【Shidariyanagiー(枝垂柳; Weeping Willow, *Salix babylonica*)】

**According to the *Jikinsho* (地錦抄; *Compendium of Garden Plants*):** *Shidariyanagiー* (独榣柳; Weeping Willow)　[Category:] Garden Trees (園木)

---

**According to the *Yamato Honzō* (大和本草; *Japanese Materia Medica*):**

　　*Ryūka* (柳花; "willow flower") / *Yōryū* (楊柳; "poplar-willow")

　　　　"Yōryū" is the common general name; when used specifically, it refers to the *Shidariyanagiー* (weeping willow).
　　　　Li Shizhen (時珍) states: "The *yōryū* is pliant and drooping, flowing downward; hence it is called *ryū* (柳; willow)."
　　　　This is precisely the *yōryū* (楊柳 / *Shidariyanagiー*, weeping willow).

---

Sketched from nature at the *Ukantel* (雨閑亭; "Rain Leisure Pavilion"), on the ninth day of the latter part of *Kyōshō* (夾鐘; second lunar month), year Kinoe-Saru (甲申) [Bunsei 7, 1824].

---

**According to the *Wamyō Ruijushō* (和名類聚抄; *Japanese Names Classified and Assembled*):**

　　*Ryū* (柳; *Shidariyanagiー*)
　　　　[The *Kenmeien* (兼名苑) states:] "Willow (*ryū*) has the alternative name *shōyō* (小楊; 'small poplar')."
　　　　The pronunciation of 柳 (*ryū*) is the *fanqie* spelling "kaku no han" (加久ノ反; combining initial *ka* + final *ku*).
　　　　Japanese reading: *Shitariyanarakiー* (之太里夜奈良木)

---

**According to the *Zoho Tashikiden* (増補多識編; *Expanded Nomenclature Compendium*), in the section on Tall Trees (喬木類):**

　　*Ryū* (柳; willow)
　　　[Japanese names:] *Shistari* / *Shidari* (志多利), *Yanagiー* (也那岐)
　　　[Alternative names:] *Shōyō* (小楊; "small poplar") [source: *Setsumun* (説文; *Shuowen Jiezi*)]；　*Yōryū* (楊柳)；　*Nikuridamoku* (尼倶律陀木) [source: Buddhist scriptures]

---

This species [the weeping willow] is particularly numerous [in variety]. The author *Kotobuki* (寿; Baien Chokkyaku) remarks:

　〇 *Ryū* (柳; *Shitariyanagiー*)
　　　*Yōryū* (楊柳) refers to the *yō* (楊; poplar; round-leafed willow, *maruba-yanagiー*, whose branches do not droop).
　　　*Suiyō* (水楊; "water willow") refers to *kawa-yanagiー* (カワヤナキ; river willow) and *inoko-yanagiー* (イノコヤナキ).
　　　*Hakuyō* (白楊; "white poplar") refers to *hako-yanagiー* (ハコヤナキ; box poplar / *Populus sieboldii*).
　　　*Ifu* (栘枎) refers to *shide-yanagiー* (シデヤナキ; hop-hornbeam willow).
　　　*Teiryū* (檉柳) is the same as *Gyoryū* (御柳; tamarisk, *Tamarix chinensis*).
　　　*Kiryū* (杞柳) refers to *kobu-yanagiー* (コブヤナギ; basket willow / *Salix koryanagi*), and so on.

[Seal: Flowering Trees (花木)]

## 【Higan-zakura (彼岸桜; Higan Cherry, *Prunus subhirtella* / *Cerasus subhirtella*)】

　　*Sōshi-kaidō* (帚子海棠; "broom-branch crabapple") — [specifically,] the upright-branching form — *Hikan-zakura* (ヒカンザクラ; Higan cherry)

The sixteenth day (望一日) of the beginning of *Senso* (洗初; the opening of the same season, i.e., the third lunar month [*Kyōshō*]), during continuous rains (*rinmu*): sketched from nature.
