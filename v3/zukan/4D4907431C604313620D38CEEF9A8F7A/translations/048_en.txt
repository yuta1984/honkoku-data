According to Yamato Honzō:
　　　Kiran grass

According to Igen: There was a farmer who saw a snake that had been wounded.
One snake held a grass in its mouth and placed it on the wound, and after one day passed,
the injured snake departed. The farmer then took that grass and
used it to treat snake wounds, and all were effective, so it was named snake-holding grass.

According to Honzō Kōmoku, female indigo is namely
the root of snake-holding plant. There is another female indigo, named
sparrow gourd. There are two types of female indigo, differing only
between vine-growing and grass-growing varieties.
