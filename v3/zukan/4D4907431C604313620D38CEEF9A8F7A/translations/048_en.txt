　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　This corresponds to wolf fang, examining the illustration in Chikinjō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Based on its form, it is called jakagai (snake street)

According to Igon: "A farmer saw an injured snake
One snake held a plant in its mouth and applied it to the wound, and after some days
the injured snake departed. The farmer then took that plant
to treat wounds, and named it jakagai-sō (snake street grass)"

According to Honzō Kōmoku: "Joshō (female blue) is
the root of jakagai." There is another joshō, named
jakuhyō. Joshō has two types, distinguished only
between vine-growing and grass-growing varieties
