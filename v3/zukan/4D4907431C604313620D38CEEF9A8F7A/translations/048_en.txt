## 【*Jakanso* (蛇莟草) / *Kiransō* (キラン草); *Ajuga* sp., Lamiaceae】

> [Marginal note: "The leaf shape is too different for this to be classified among the *Hebiichigo* (strawberry-type plants)."]

---

### According to the *Chikinsho* (地錦抄):

　　*Jakanso* (蛇莟草; "Snake-含 Herb")
　　[Annotation: The *Chikinsho* states that the character read *kan* here is not 含 (*kan*, "to hold in the mouth") but rather 莟 (*kan*, "bud"). The same book identifies this plant with the symbol ○ as *Hebiichigo* (ヘビイチゴ; False Strawberry, *Duchesnea indica*).]

[Seal: Medicinal Herbs (*Yakusō*)]　[The above entry is recorded here.]

Sketched from life on the eighth day of the middle period of *Kosen* (姑洗; third lunar month),
in the year *Kōshin* (甲申) [Bunsei 7, 1824].

　　Could this be the illustration in the *Chikinsho*?
　　Its form is described as *Jakan* (蛇莟).

### According to the *Yamato Honzō* (大和本草):

　　*Kiransō* (キラン草)

---

### According to the *Ien* (異苑):

A village elder (*denpu*) observed a snake that had been wounded.
Another snake took a certain herb in its mouth and applied it to the wound.
After a day had passed, the wounded snake recovered and went on its way.
The village elder then gathered that herb and used it to treat snake-bite wounds, with effective results in every case.
For this reason, the herb came to be called *Jakanso* (蛇莟草; "Snake's Poultice Herb").

---

### According to the *Bencao Gangmu* (本草綱目):

*Njosei* (女青; *Cynanchum cathayense* or related species) is the root of *Jakan* (蛇莟).
There is a separate plant also called *Njosei*, known as *Jakuhyō* (雀瓢; "Sparrow Gourd").
There are two varieties of *Njosei*, differing only in whether they grow as a vine (*tōsei*) or as a ground herb (*sōsei*).

---

### *Jakan* (蛇莟)

[Japanese names]: *Uzumamekusa* (宇豆末米久左). Also known as *Kiransō* (幾羅牟左宇).

　　[Additional alternative names]:
　　　*Jakan* (蛇莟)　[cited from the *Honzōkyō* (本草経; *Shennong Bencao Jing*)]
　　　*Ija* (威蛇)　[cited from the *Taimei* (大明; *Rihua Zhu Jia Bencao*)]
　　　*Shōryūge* (小龍芽; "Small Dragon Sprout")　[cited from the *Gangmu* (綱目)]
　　　*Shihairyūga* (紫背龍牙; "Purple-backed Dragon's Fang")　[cited from the *Zukyō* (図経; *Bencao Tujing*)]
