【KOBUSHI / JAPANESE MAGNOLIA and MOKUREN / LILY MAGNOLIA】
《Shin'i (Xinyi)》

According to Honzō Wage (Annotated Japanese Herbal):
　　Alternate names: Shinkei (辛雞), Kōtō (侯桃), Bōboku (房木), Mokushitsu (木筆)
　　Keishun (迎春)
　　Japanese name: Kobushi (己部志)

[Seal] Medicinal wood

Copied from life on the twenty-first day of Kisaragi (second month), Mizunoe-Uma year.

　　Recorded in the Yamato Honzō,
　　　Flowering Trees section.

According to Wamyo Ruiju-shō:
　　《Shin'i (Yamaarara-ki) / Kobushi-hashikami》

According to崔禹錫 (Choe U-seok)'s Shokukyō (Food Classic):
　　Shin'i — Japanese name: Yamaarara-ki (夜末阿良々木)
　　　　　　Alternate name: Kobushi-hashikami (古不之波之可美)

From the Chōmonjū (Collection of Tales Heard):
　　"Having eaten my fill and cradling an aching head as I left—
　　　the flowers of the kobushi, too, cause such pain!"
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　— Chūin Sōzu

The author (Ju) states:
　There are several varieties of shin'i: Ōyama-renge (玉蘭花, Magnolia sieboldii),
　Hakurenge (白木蘭, white magnolia), Mokuren (紫木蓮, lily magnolia),
　Shite-kobushi (石喬木), and the Hō (朴) tree — all are
　related species of shin'i.
