**From the *Yamato Honzō* (大和本草; "Japanese Herbals"):**

　Kusa-mokuke (草木瓜) [Shidomī]

　　　　　　　(From the same record:)
　Kusa-mokuke grows to a height of one to two *shaku* (approximately 30–60 cm) and is commonly found in the wild. Its flowers are red, and the wood bears thorns. The fruit is small. In the Musashino area, a plant called Shidomī — a type of Kusa-mokuke — is found; its fruit is approximately the size of a Higo plum (*Higo-ume*). Local people (土民) use its sourness (as a souring agent / vinegar substitute). Compared to the *karin* (榠櫨; Chinese quince, *Pseudocydonia sinensis*), its leaves and fruit are similarly shaped but far smaller, it is said. This description corresponds well to the present plant. Two additional varieties are listed in the same source, but [the author] has not yet seen them. They are recorded in the "Mokuke" (木瓜; quince) section of that work.
