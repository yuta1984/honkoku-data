【KODEMARI / JAPANESE SNOWBALL (Spiraea cantoniensis)】
　　　　　　　　　　　　　【Flowering Tree】

Fěntuán Huā (Powder Ball Flower)　Japanese names: Kotemari, Susukake　(from Jikkinshō; also recorded in Shoryūki)
　Chinese name:
　　Obihana (Sash Flower / 御帯花)
　　　Recorded on the 16th day of the third month (Kōshin/姑洗), Mizunoeto-Uma year

Yamato Honzō:
　　Suzukake (also called Kotemari)

From Chaseki Sōkashū (Tea Ceremony Flower Arrangement Collection):
　　Máyè Xiùqiú (Hemp-leaved Embroidered Ball)
　　(Kotemari)


【KINSENKA / CALENDULA (Calendula officinalis)】
【Flowering Herb】

Saijiki (Almanac):
　Kinsenka (Gold Coin Flower)　Kingaika (Gold Lid Flower)
　　　　Chōshunkiku (Everlasting Spring Chrysanthemum) (Senjin Fumoto-hi)
　　　Various alternative names exist

　Copied on a spring day in the Mizunoeto-Uma year

According to Yamato Honzō:
　　This is the Kinsenka (gold coin flower). The flower blooms most
　　prolifically in spring, and as it continues blooming through all four seasons, it is also called
　　　　　Jōshunka (Ever-Spring Flower).

According to Kyūkō Yafu:
　　Kinsen'ji (金銭児) (Kinsenka)

According to Wamyō Ruijushō:
　　Kinsenka (Gold Coin Flower)
　　　Emperor Jianwen of Liang wrote a rhapsody (fu) on the gold coin flower.
　　　The gold coin flower is commonly known as Komuronuan (古無軟).
