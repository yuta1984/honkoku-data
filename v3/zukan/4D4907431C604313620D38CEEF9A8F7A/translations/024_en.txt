【KODEMARI (Japanese Snowball / Spiraea cantoniensis)】
　　　　　　　　　　　　　【Flowering Tree】

Fēntuán Flower (粉団花)　Japanese names: Kotemari, Susukake　(Listed in Jijinshō, and also in Shoryūki)
　　Chinese name:
　　　Odaibana (御帯花 / "Honorable Sash Flower")
　　　　　17th day of the third month (Gassen/Koshō), Mizunoe-Uma year

From Yamato Honzō:
　　Suzukake (also called Kotemari)


From Chaseki Sōkashū:
　　Máyè Xiùqiú (麻葉繍毬, "Hemp-leaf Embroidered Ball") (Kotemari)


【KINSENKA (Pot Marigold / Calendula officinalis)】
【Flowering Herb】

From Saijiki:
　　Kinsenka (金錢花 / "Gold Coin Flower")　Kingaika (金蓋花 / "Gold Lid Flower")
　　　　　Chōshungiku (長春菊 / "Everlasting Spring Chrysanthemum") (Chisujifumoto-bi)
　　　Various alternative names exist

　　Written in the second month (Chūshun) of the Mizunoe-Uma year

According to Yamato Honzō:
　　This is the Kinsen flower. The flower blooms most
　　vigorously in spring, and continues blooming through all four seasons, so it is also called
　　　　　Jōshunka (常春花 / "Everlasting Spring Flower").

According to Kyūkō Yapu:
　　Kinsen'ji (金銭児, Kinsenka)

According to Wamyō Ruijushō:
　　Kinsenka (金銭花)
　　　Emperor Jianwen of Liang composed a fu (rhapsody) on the Kinsenka
　　　The gold coin (金銭) is colloquially called "Komunan" (古無軟)
