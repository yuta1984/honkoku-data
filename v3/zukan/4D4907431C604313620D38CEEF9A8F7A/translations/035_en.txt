**Sō (菘) / Na — Flowers**　[Hatakena (畑菜; Field Greens)]

According to Shoku-kyō (食競/食鑒):
　"Sō (菘) is called Na (菜). The interchangeable character is 菜 (na)."

Re-sketched on the 19th day of the same spring (癸未).

[Seal: Vegetable category (菜類)]

According to the Supplemented Tashiki-hen, section on Pungent Vegetables (葷菜類):
　Sō (菘; Shō)
　　Japanese names: Kohone (古保祢 / コホネ), also Takana (多加那 / タカナ); upon reflection [the author notes]: Ukina (宇幾那 / ウキナ)
　　Additional alternative name: Hakusai (白菜; Chinese cabbage)

　According to Bencao Gangmu: Sō (菘) endures winter and wilts late; it can be seen throughout all four seasons. It has the steadfast quality of pine (松 matsu). Its roots are firm and small; there are two varieties.

　The author states: In ancient times, sō (菘) was called Takana (タカナ). Nowadays, it is commonly referred to simply as Na (菜; "greens"). What is today called Takana (タカナ) is what the herbals refer to as Yúntái (蕓薹; *Brassica napus* / rapeseed).
