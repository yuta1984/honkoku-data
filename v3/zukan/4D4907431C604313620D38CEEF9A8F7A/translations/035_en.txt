According to Zōho Tashikihen, Pungent Vegetables section:
　　Cabbage (shō) (Japanese name: formerly kohone, also takana; now upon examination, ukina)
　　　　(Supplementary alternative name) Chinese cabbage

　　In Honzō Kōmoku it states: "Cabbage endures winter and withers late. It can be seen in all four seasons. Like pine, its roots are firm and small. There are two varieties."
　　I say: "In ancient times, cabbage was called takana. Now it is commonly called na (vegetable). What is now called takana is what the Honzō refers to as untai."
