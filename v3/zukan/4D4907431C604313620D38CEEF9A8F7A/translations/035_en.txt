According to the Supplemented Tashiki-hen, section on Pungent Vegetables (葷菜類):
　Sō / 菘 (Shō)
　　Japanese name: Kohone (古保祢; Kohone), or Takana (多加那; Takana). On reflection, also referred to as Ukina (宇幾那).
　　Additional alternative name: Hakusai (白菜; Chinese cabbage)

According to the Bencao Gangmu (本草綱目):
　Sō (菘) endures through winter and withers late; it can be seen throughout all four seasons. It has the steadfastness of a pine tree (i.e., it does not wither in winter). Its roots are firm and small. There are two varieties.

[The author, Baien, states:]
　I observe that in former times, "Sō (菘)" was called Takana. Today it is commonly referred to simply as "Na (菜)." What is today called Takana corresponds to what the Bencao (本草) refers to as Yúntái (蕓薹; rapeseed/Brassica napus).
