　Horse-先蒿

According to Yamato Materia Medica:
　Mouse yeast (Gogyō) grass
　　One name Buddha's ear grass
　　Japanese name mother-child grass
　Some consider it yellow flower wormwood
　　To consider it evil wormwood is incorrect

According to Tashiki Hen Wet Grass Section:
　Mouse yeast grass (Sokiku), Japanese name now examined as Hookikusa　Alternative name Heartless (Mushin) grass (Separate Record)
　Supplementary alternative names: Rice yeast (Beikiku) (Compendium), Mouse ear (Soni) (Separate Record), Buddha's ear (Butsuni) grass (Dharma Manifestation), Fragrant thatch (Hau) (Collected Remnants), Yellow (Kō) wormwood (Collected Compilation), Mushroom mother (Zōni) (Song Emperor Huizong has a poem about mushroom mother)

In ancient Japan, on the third day of the third month, grass cakes were made using mouse yeast grass
Therefore, common people call it cake grass
