【DANDELION】
Dandelion (Hokōei) - According to Saijiki (Seasonal Records):
　　　　○ Leather grass (Tsutsumikusa)　　　○ Yellow flowers are called yellow leather nail, yellow flower ground nail
　　 　　White leather nail (Hakuyutei) refers to white flowers, as recorded in Kyūkō Yafu (Famine Relief Field Guide)

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　23rd year of Mizunoto-Hitsuji, 26th day of 3rd month
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Copied again faithfully

According to Kyūkō Honzō (Famine Relief Materia Medica):
　Bobotei vegetable (Tanpopo) - Also called yellow flower sprout

According to Wamyō Ruijushō (Japanese Names Classified Collection):
　Dandelion grass - Called dandelion grass in materia medica, Japanese name is fuchina, also called tana
　　　　　One name is kōjoku grass - Upper part pronounced like egō, lower part like nuzu

According to Zōho Tashiki Hen Jūkatsu-rui (Supplemented Polyglot Soft Plant Types):
　Dandelion (Hokōei) Japanese name: Tana, now considered to be Tanpopo　Alternative names: Golden hairpin grass (from Kōmoku)
　　　Supplemented alternative names: Kōjoku grass (pronounced kō)
　　　Yellow flower ground nail (Ōkachichō) (colloquial), Wild duck dandelion (Fukōei) (from Senkin), Servant dandelion (Bokukōei) (from Zukei)
　　　　　　　Turtle dove flower (Hokkōei) (from Gyokusatsu), Dandelion nail (Hokōchō) ○ Ground gallbladder grass (Chitansō)

According to Honzō Kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica):
　Those with purple flowers are named large nail grass
　Similar ones without flowers are
　named ground gallbladder grass

According to Nōsei Zensho (Complete Agricultural Administration):
　Called bobotei vegetable
　Its flowers open in the morning and
　wilt from noon onwards, every day
　like this to endure
　winter

【CUDWEED】
According to Kankaishokukyō:
　  Mouse yeast grass (Hahakogusa) - Pronounced hahako grass, also
　　　　called five-element mugwort (gogyōyomogi), one of the seven herbs of Human Day

According to Imyō Bunruishō (Alternative Names Classification):
　Now examining Honzō Kōmoku's mouse yeast (hahako) name explanation
　Buddha's ear, mouse ear, etc., it is ear vegetable
　In Wamyōshō as anroshi (hahako), in Honzō Ruihen as
　Horse-先蒿
　Also added to the seven Human Day herbs as gogyō, also five-element mugwort (gogyō)
　I say that ear vegetable is a separate species
　　　to be copied later

According to Yamato Honzō:
　  Mouse yeast (gogyō) grass
　　Also called Buddha's ear grass
　　Japanese name mother-child grass
　Some consider it
　　yellow flower mugwort
　but calling it
　　evil mugwort is incorrect

According to Tashiki Hen Wet Grass Types:
　Mouse yeast grass (sokiku) Japanese name: now considered hoohikusa　Alternative names: Heartless grass (Betsuroku)
　Supplemented alternative names: Rice yeast (beikiku) (Kōmoku), mouse ear (soni) (Betsuroku), Buddha's ear grass (butsunisō) (Hōshō), fragrant grass (kōbō) (Shūi), yellow mugwort (kōkō) (Kaihen), jōbo (mushroom mother) - There is a poem about jōbo by Emperor Huizong of Song

In ancient times, Japan used mouse yeast grass to make grass rice cakes on March 3rd
Therefore, Japanese folk call it rice cake grass
　　　　　　　　23rd year of Mizunoto-Hitsuji, spring, 27th day of 3rd month, faithfully copied
