【Hanazuō (Chinese Redbud / Cercis chinensis)】

According to Yamato Honzō:
　　　Shikei (Suhōgi)
　　　　Hanasuhō

According to the Revised and Expanded Tashikiden, Shrub Category:
　　　Shikei (Purple Redbud)　Japanese name: uncertain　Alternative names: Shishu〔Shiyi supplement〕, its bark is named Nikkō (Flesh Red)〔Bencao Gangmu〕　Additional alternative name in revised edition: Nikushō

Same year, same month, twenty-third day
Painted from life

According to Wakan Sansai Zue (Illustrated Encyclopedia of Japan and China), Shrub Category:
　　　Shikei (Suhōnoki)
　　　　Commonly called Sōboku

Although commonly called Sōboku, it is not the true Sōboku (Sappan wood/Caesalpinia sappan).
The explanations and investigations in the Bencao Gangmu (Compendium of Materia Medica) regarding the leaves, fruit, and form all match well.

　Yamato Honzō states: Sōboku 〔alternative name〕 Sōmoku 〔Japanese name〕 Subau
　The Wamyō Ruijushō states:
　　　Regarding sōbō, Sū Jìng's commentary on Materia Medica says: Sōbō〔written in Tōin (Tang rhyme dictionary) as "hō," with the same pronunciation as "hō"〕,
　　　commonly called "subō," used by people for dyeing. 〔I say:〕 A certain person claims that this plant does not exist in Japan, and in Japanese usage, sōbō refers to the purple redbud (Shikei-ka). What is called Shikei-ka refers to the grass known as Genkashū (a type of Daphne). The Yamato Honzō states that nowadays, what is separately and commonly called the Shikei tree is a small tree, distinct from the true Sōboku tree. It is noted that the Shikei tree and Genka are two entirely different plants.
