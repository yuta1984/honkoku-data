In my opinion, treating yama-tsutsuji and eizankō as one thing in herbal medicine
has no basis. That they are originally one thing can be known from the Tashiki-hen. Eizankō has
deep red flowers. When blooming, they fill the mountains. Is this how it got its name? Mountain azalea
is a separate variety, later distinguished with different names becoming one name? Mountain - later books should still be consulted.
