According to Kyūkō Honzō:
　　　Jitsusō-ji Tree
　　　　Named in Honzō
　　　　　　　as Sanshuyu

　　　　　　　　　　Yamato Honzō
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　In Kyoto dialect called Nawashiro-gumi. In spring, when
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　preparing rice seedbeds, the fruit ripens. Chinese people say this is indeed
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　sanshuyu. According to my examination, it also matches well with the Chikin-shō section.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　However, there is also another variety called Nawashiro-gumi. Indeed,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　those later named with "shuyu" are Go-shuyu (Evodia),
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Shoku-shuyu (Edible Evodia), and San-shuyu (Cornus). Gumi (Elaeagnus),
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　and Mokuhanke are all of the same family.
