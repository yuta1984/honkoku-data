## Sanshuyu (山茱萸 / Cornelian Cherry, *Cornus officinalis*)

---

**According to *Honzō Wage* (本草和解):**

　Alternative names: Shokusan-sō (蜀酸棗), Nikku-sō (肉棗), ■-jitsu (■実; one character illegible), Keisoku (鶏足 / "chicken foot"), Soshi (鼠失)
　Japanese names: Sawagumi (左波共美), Yamagumi (夜麻久美)
　　It is said to closely resemble the *Goumi* (胡頽子 / *Elaeagnus multiflora*, called *Higumi*).

---

**According to *Zōsho-ki* (蔵書記):**

　Sekisō (石棗 / "stone jujube") — also known as Sanshuyu (山茱萸)
　　Japanese names: Yamagumi (ヤマクミ), Karagumi (カラクミ)

---

The 17th day of the first month, year Kinoe-Saru (甲申), Bunsei 7 (1824).
I begin writing this compilation of illustrations and records, copying faithfully and with sincerity.

[Seal: 薬木 / Medicinal Tree]

---

**According to *Kyūkō Honzō* (救荒本草):**

　Jitsusō-ji-ju (実棗児樹 / "Fruit jujube seedling tree")
　　In the *Honzō* (本草 / pharmacopoeia), it is named Sanshuyu (山茱萸).

---

**According to *Yamato Honzō* (大和本草):**

　In the Kyoto dialect, it is called *Nawashirokumi* (ナハシロクミ / "seedbed gumi"). Its fruit ripens exactly at the time when seedbeds (nawashiro) are prepared in spring. People from China say: "This is precisely the Sanshuyu (山茱萸)." Upon my own investigation, this accords well with the entry in *Chikinsho* (地錦抄). However, there is yet another variety called *Nawashiro-gumi*, which I shall transcribe later. Those plants called *Shūyu* (茱萸) include: *Go-shuyu* (呉茱萸), *Shoku-shuyu* (食茱萸), and *San-shuyu* (山茱萸); while *Goumi* (胡頽子) and *Moku-hanka* (木半夏) are all of the same kindred as *Kumi* (クミ).
