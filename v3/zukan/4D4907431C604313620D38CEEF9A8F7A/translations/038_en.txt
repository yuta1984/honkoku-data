【Note: Sanshuyu (山茱萸; Japanese Cornelian Cherry, *Cornus officinalis*)】

**Sanshuyu (Mountain Cornelian Cherry)**　　According to the *Honzō Wage* (本草和解):
　　Alternative names: Shokusunsō (蜀酸棗), Nikusō (肉棗), ■-mi (■実), Keisoku (鶏足), Soshi (鼠失)
　　Japanese names: Sahagumi (左波共美), Yamagumi (夜麻久美)
　　　　Note: It closely resembles the Goumi / Higumi (胡頽子; *Elaeagnus multiflora*).

According to the *Zōsho-ki* (蔵書記):
　　Sekisō (石棗) — also known as Sanshuyu (山茱萸)
　　Japanese names: Yamagumi (ヤマグミ), Karagumi (カラグミ)

---

According to the *Kyūkō Honzō* (救荒本草; *Materia Medica for Relief from Famine*):
　　[This plant is called] Jitsusōjiju (実棗児樹).
　　In the *Honzō* (本草; the main materia medica), it is named Sanshuyu (山茱萸).

---

According to the *Yamato Honzō* (大和本草):
　　In the dialect of Kyoto, it is called *Nawashirogumi* (苗代茱萸; "seedbed goumi"), because its fruit ripens precisely when the rice seedbeds (苗代) are prepared in spring. People from China say that this is indeed the true Sanshuyu (山茱萸). Upon my own investigation, this accords well with the descriptions in the *Chikinsho* (地錦抄) as well. However, there is also a separate species called *Nawashirogumi*, which I will sketch later. Among plants bearing the name "Shūyu" (茱萸), there are: Goshūyu (呉茱萸; *Tetradium ruticarpum*), Shokushūyu (食茱萸; *Zanthoxylum ailanthoides*), and Sanshuyu (山茱萸; *Cornus officinalis*). Goumi (胡頽子; *Elaeagnus multiflora*) and Mokuhanка (木半夏; *Elaeagnus* sp.) are all related members of the *gumi* (グミ) family.
