【GARLAND CHRYSANTHEMUM (type of edible chrysanthemum)】

According to Zoho Tashiki-hen, Pungent Vegetables category:
　　Garland chrysanthemum (Tōkō) [Japanese name] not clear　[Supplement alternative name] Artemisia

In Shokukyō it says:
　Garland chrysanthemum (Tōkō)
　　　Korean chrysanthemum [common name]
　　　Spring chrysanthemum　Endless grass [historical reference omitted]
　　　Copied on the fifth day of mid-spring, Mizunoe-Uma year

[Seal] Vegetables
[Seal] Medicinal herbs

I say:
In Materia Medica
　Wild chrysanthemum, also called bitter herb (Gui)
　This is spring chrysanthemum. It is not
　the wild chrysanthemum of shepherd's purse artemisia, etc. This theory is
　incorrect. Wild chrysanthemum blooms small yellow
　flowers in autumn. Garland chrysanthemum blooms in spring and
　resembles chrysanthemum, hence its name. The shape of flowers and leaves
　differs greatly.
