## 【MITSUMATA (Oriental Paperbush)】

[Category: □ type]

According to Jikin Shō:
　Mitsumata (Three-forked plant)
　　The bark of this tree is taken and used
　　to make paper; the finest paper
　　is made from this bark, and so forth.

　　　Mid-second month (February), Year of Rinjō (壬午, 1822)

According to Bencao Gangmu (Honzō Kōmoku):
　Jiéxiāng (結香, Mitsumata willow)　Bōshū (Suō Province)
　　Juzufusa　Sānzhōu (Mikawa Province)

## 【UME (Japanese Plum/Apricot)】

Flowering tree

Tenth day of the last ten-day period of the intercalary first month, after Rinjō year (1822)
Written in haste (Tonsha)

According to Yamato Honzō:
　Ume: In the Man'yōshū
　　　Mumeno hana (flower of the plum)
　Also read as Umeno hana
　The plum blossom alone opens before
　all other flowers under heaven. Therefore:
　　It is called "the leader of a hundred flowers" (Hyakkaburai)
　　and "elder brother of flowers" (Hanakei)

The matter of calling plum "Kōbunboku" (tree that loves literature)
comes from the Jū-kyo-chū (Records of Daily Activities) of the Jin dynasty.
In Japanese custom it is said:
The name "Kōbunboku" refers to the superb
fragrance and elegance of the white plum blossom.

According to Wamyō Ruijūshō:
　Ume: As noted in the commentary to Erya, Ume is read "Mokubai no han" (反切reading)
　Japanese name: U-me (宇女)

　called "Kōbai" (Yellow plum, 黄槑)
　　First month (Taisō), after Rinjō year (1822)
