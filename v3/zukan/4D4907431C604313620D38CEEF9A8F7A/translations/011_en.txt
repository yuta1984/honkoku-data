According to Yamato Honzō:
Plum - In the Man'yōshū
Mume no hana
Also read as Ume no hana
Plum blossoms bloom alone, preceding
all flowers in the world, therefore
among a hundred flowers
they are called the elder brother of flowers

That plum is called kōbunboku (literature-loving tree) appears in
the Qijuzhu of the Jin dynasty. In Japanese custom,
what is called kōbunboku refers to
the fragrant blossoms of white plum
