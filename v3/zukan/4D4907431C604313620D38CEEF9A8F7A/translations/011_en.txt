【PLUM】
Flowering tree

Mizunoe-uma year, intercalary first month, last ten days
hastily copied

According to Yamato Honzō:
Plum　In the Man'yōshū
Mume no hana
Also read as Ume no hana
Plum blossoms bloom alone, preceding
all flowers under heaven, therefore called
Chief of a hundred flowers
Elder brother of flowers

Calling plum the "tree beloved by scholars" comes from
the起居注 of Jin dynasty. Japanese custom says
that what is called the "tree beloved by scholars" refers to
the fragrant excellence of white plum

According to Wamyō Ruijushō:
Plum　According to Erya commentary, plum (reverse of mokuhai)
Japanese name　Ume
