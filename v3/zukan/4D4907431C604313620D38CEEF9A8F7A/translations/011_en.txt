According to Yamato Honzō:
Plum - In the Man'yōshū
"mume no hana" (plum blossoms)
Also read as "ume no hana"
Plum blossoms alone open
before all other flowers in the world, therefore
called the elder brother
of a hundred flowers

The reason plum is called "kōbunboku" (literature-loving tree) appears in
the Jin Dynasty's Qijuzhu. What Japanese custom calls
"kōbunboku" refers to the
fragrant blossoms of white plum
