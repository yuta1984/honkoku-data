According to Yamato Honzō:
Plum - In the Man'yōshū
there are readings as
Mume no hana
and also Ume no hana
Plum blossoms alone bloom
ahead of all flowers in the world,
therefore called
the elder brother of
a hundred flowers

Calling the plum "kōbun-boku" (literature-loving tree) appears in
the Records of Daily Life of the Jin dynasty. What Japanese folk call
kōbun-boku refers to the
fragrant blossoms of white plum
