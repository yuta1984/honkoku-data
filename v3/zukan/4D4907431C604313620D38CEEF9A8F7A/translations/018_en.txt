【FLOWERING CRABAPPLE】
Yamato Honzō
　Flowering crabapple

　　In Jia Dan's Hundred Flowers Compendium of Tang
　　it is called the immortal among flowers
　　and the people of China call it
　　a famous friend among flowers

○Wakan Sansai Zue, Mountain Fruits section states:
　Crabapple pear　Kaie
　Tang pronunciation
　　Haitanrii

　　　　In general, flowering trees named with "sea"
　　　　all come from overseas

[Seal] Flowering tree

Copied on the fifth day of the first half
of Yayoi month, Mizunoe-Uma year

Shen Li's Crabapple Compendium states that only
those of purple brocade color are called
crabapples, the rest are merely pear blossoms

Ju states:
　What the world calls crabapple flowers are those that
　are illustrated. Their flowers are pale pink and
　resemble apples. Those called crabapples have
　thick stems and large flowers. Those with flowers of varying
　pink color and red stems that hang long and drooping
　are called drooping silk (suishi) crabapples, considered the finest
　crabapple flowers. There is also one variety called Du Zimei
　crabapple, which people commonly call small apple.
　The flowers are pale pinkish-white and bear
　very many fruits. There is also one variety called
　Western Prefecture crabapple. The crabapples planted in recent times generally
　do not bear many fruits.

There are several varieties of chi. Maple trees
○Tang maples are each separate varieties, and their
leaves are all different. Maple trees are
rare in Japan, but there are
large trees at Mount Nikko. In Edo's tree nursery
Gunpō-en there are large trees.
I have not seen them elsewhere.
