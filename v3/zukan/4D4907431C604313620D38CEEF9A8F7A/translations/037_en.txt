## Shitō (紫藤) — Flower of the Wisteria (Fuji / 不之)

---

**According to the Supplemented *Tashiki-hen* (増補多識編), section on Climbing Plants (蔓草類):**

　Shitō (紫藤 / Purple Wisteria)
　　《Japanese name》Upon my consideration: *Fuji* (布知 / フヂ); also *Nohana* (乃波那 / ノハナ)

---

**According to *Wakan Sansai Zue* (和漢三才図会), section on Climbing Plants (蔓草類):**

　Shitō (紫藤) — *Fuji*; Chinese pronunciation: Zǐ Téng (ツウ・テアン)
　　Also called: Shōtōtō (招豆藤)
　　Japanese name: *Fuchi* (布知 / フヂ)
　　The single character "藤" (tō/fuji) alone is used, with its native Japanese reading (*kun-yomi*) serving as the name.

---

**According to *Wamyō Ruijushō* (和名類聚抄):**

　*Fuji* (藤 / フヂ)
　　The commentary to the *Erya* (爾雅) states: "藟" is a *fanqie* spelling (反字; a method of indicating pronunciation using two characters), and is also written as "蘽."
　　Japanese name: *Fuchi* (布知 / フヂ)

---

**According to *Imina Bunruishō* (異名分類抄):**

　Alternative names for *Fuji* (藤):
　　Futagikusa (二季草), Murasakikusa (ムラサキクサ; "purple herb"), Matsumigusa (松見草; "pine-viewing herb"), Sanokata no Hana (サノカタノ花), Matsunakusa (マツナクサ)

---

**According to *Nanpō Sōmokujō* (南方草木状), section on Trees (木類):**

　*Shitō* (紫藤 / Wisteria)
　　The leaves of the purple wisteria are slender and long; its stem resembles bamboo; its root is extremely hard and solid, and is covered with multiple layers of bark. Its flowers are white and its seeds (fruit) are black. If placed in wine (sake), it will not decay even after two or three thousand years. When its stem is cut and placed in the soot of smoke, and time passes, it turns purple; its fragrance is such that it can be used to summon divine spirits (i.e., to cause deities to descend).
