Yamato Honzō
　　Elaeagnus (Kotai-shi)　Commonly called Higumi
　　(Gumi)　　I say this is the true nursery gumi
　　　　　　In Wamyō-shō it is called Moronari

Honzō Wake　Sawa-gumi

　　　Farmers know the time to work the fields
　　　by seeing when this fruit ripens, hence it is called
　　　nursery elaeagnus

　　　　　According to my investigation:
　　　　　　Cornus also ripens when nurseries are prepared according to Honzō. Chinese people say
　　　　　　this is indeed cornus. However, the illustration corresponds to brocade ground
　　　　　　and records nursery gumi. Does cornus also ripen during nursery time?
　　　　　　This is not yet known. The truth about this tree, or perhaps gardeners explain it
　　　　　　Yamato Honzō says elaeagnus (Higumi) trees are six to seven feet tall, with soft branches
　　　　　　Leaves resemble pear but are long and narrow. Front is blue, back is white. Flowers bloom in second and first months
　　　　　　Shape like cloves, hanging abundantly. This matches the tree in question
　　　　　　Should cornus also be called nursery gumi? This should be investigated
