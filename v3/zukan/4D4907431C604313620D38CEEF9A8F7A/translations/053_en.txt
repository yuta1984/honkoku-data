【AKEBI (Akebia quinata)】

Honzō Wage says:
　　Mokutsu [Akebia]: Other names: Tsūsō, Fushichōō, Mannentō (Evergreen vine)
　　　　　　　　　The fruit is called Enfuku
　　　　　　Japanese name: Akebikazura (アケビカズラ)

【Seal】Medicinal herb

Wamyō Ruijushō says:
　　Tsūsō (Akebikazura): The Wamyō Honzō says, citing the commentary of Tao Hongjing:
　　　　　　The stem has fine holes, and the two ends are connected through to each other.
　　　　　　Japanese name: Akebikazura

Yamato Honzō says:
　　Mokutsu (Akebikazura): It is a vine plant. The vine is long, large, and firm. The vine
　　　　　　itself is the mokutsu. The leaves and stems are called
　　　　　　tsūsō. The kinome-zuke of Kurama is tsūsō.

Kinoe-Saru year, end of Koshitsu (third month), three days before the end
