【AKEBI (Chocolate Vine)】
Honzō Wakai states:
　　　Mokutsu (Akebia)　Alternative names: Tsūsō, Fushiteiō, Mannen-fujii
　　　　　　　　　The fruit is called Enfuku
　　　　　　Japanese name: Akekinkadzura

【Seal】Medicinal herb

Wamyō Ruijushō states:
　Tsūsō (Akebi-kadzura)
　　Wamyō Honzō states, in Tao Hongjing's annotation:
　　The stem has fine holes, with both ends communicating
　　Japanese name: Akehikatora

Yamato Honzō states:
　Mokutsu (Akebi-kadzura)
　　It is a climbing vine. The vine is long, large and hard. The vine
　　itself is mokutsu. The leaves and stem are called
　　tsūsō. The kinome-zuke (pickled tree buds) of Kurama is tsūsō.

Kinoe-Saru year, Koshō month, 3rd day
