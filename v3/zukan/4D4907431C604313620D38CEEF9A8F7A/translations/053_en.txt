Wamyō Ruijushō says:
　　　Tsūsō (Akebi-kazura)　Wamyō Honzō says, in Tao Hongjing's annotation it states
　　　　　　　The stem has fine pores that connect through both ends
　　　　　　　Japanese name "Akehikatsuira"

Yamato Honzō says:
　　　Mokutsu (Akebi-kazura)　It is a climbing plant. The vine is notably large and sturdy. The vine
　　　　　　　　　　is mokutsu itself. The leaves and stem are called tsūsō
　　　　　　　　　　The kinome-zuke of Kurama is tsūsō

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kinoe-Saru year, Koshō month, before the 3rd day
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Painted from life
