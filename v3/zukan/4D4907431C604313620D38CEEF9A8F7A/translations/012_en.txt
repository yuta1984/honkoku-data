Recorded in Wakan Sansai Zue, Five Fragrant Fruits section:
　Plum (sound・branch)　槑 (ancient form)　Japanese name ume
　(Chinese pronunciation　Mui)　　　　Now called mume

According to Sansai Zue, there are four precious qualities of plum: Value the rare, not the abundant; value the tender, not the lean; value the contained bud, not the fully opened flower.

○According to Kakurin Gyokurotsu, in ancient times they spoke of fruit but not flowers; all the beautiful fragrance of flowers was praised in Song dynasty poetry and literature.
○In our country anciently, what was called "flower" was plum. From the middle ancient period onward, what is simply called "flower" is cherry blossom.
○According to Shoku Nihongi, Emperor Shōmu in the tenth year of Tenpyō, seventh month, pointed to a plum tree before the palace and said: "Since last spring I have desired to appreciate this tree, but have not yet had occasion to enjoy it. Each of you should compose poetry about the plum tree." Thirty literati all composed poems with spring sentiment.

In the darkness of a spring night, the plum blossoms are wondrous—
Though their color cannot be seen, how can their fragrance be hidden?

According to Yamato Honzō, Plum Record section:
Those growing wild that have not undergone cultivation
are called river plum (kōbai)
Named straight-trunk plum
Or these are called wild plum

Baekje's Wani called the plum "this flower." Kan-shin (Sugawara no Michizane) always loved plum, leaving behind the name "flying plum." This derives from the Jin Records' term "literature-loving tree." Tachibana no Naomoto's poem contains the epithet "nightingale-lodge plum." Saigyō's poem contains "future-wish plum." Such examples cannot be fully enumerated.
