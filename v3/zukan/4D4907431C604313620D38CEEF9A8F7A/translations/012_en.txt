【PLUM】

Recorded in Wakan Sansai Zue, Five Fragrant Fruits section
　　Plum (Ume, Chinese pronunciation Mui)　(Sound: branch)　Bai (ancient)
　　　　　　　　Japanese name: Ume
　　　　　　　　Now called Mume

　According to Sansai Zue:
　The plum has four noble qualities: it values rarity over abundance, age over youth, leanness over corpulence, and being in bud over being in full bloom.

○According to Kakurin Gyokurotsu: In ancient times, people spoke of fruit and prosperity, not of flowers, and did not speak of the beauty and fragrance of flowers. By the Song Dynasty, these were celebrated in poetry and prose.

○In our country, in ancient times, when people spoke of "flowers," they meant plum. From the medieval period onward, when people simply say "flowers," they mean cherry blossoms.

○According to Shoku Nihongi: Emperor Shomu, in the seventh month of Tempyo 10, pointed to the plum trees in front of the palace and said, "Since last spring I have wanted to enjoy these trees, but have not yet had the chance to appreciate them." He commanded each person to compose poems about plum trees. Thirty literati all composed poems about spring:
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　The darkness of a spring night is mysterious, plum blossoms—
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Though their color cannot be seen, can their fragrance be hidden?

【Seal】Flowering tree

Baekje's Wang In called the plum "this flower." Kansin (Sugawara no Michizane) always loved plum and left behind the name "Flying Plum." Following the words from the Jin Chronicles, there is the designation "tree that loves literature." From Tachibana no Naomoto's poem comes the title "Warbler Inn Plum." From Saigyo's poem comes the "Please Stay" plum. Such examples are too numerous to count.

According to Yamato Honzo, Plum Catalog section:
