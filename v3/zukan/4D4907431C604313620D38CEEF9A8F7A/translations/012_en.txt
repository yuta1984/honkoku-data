　(Tang pronunciation: mui)　　　Now called mume

According to Sansai Zue, plums have four noble qualities: valuing rarity over abundance, valuing tenderness over age, valuing thinness over fatness, valuing buds over opened blossoms.

○According to Kakurin Gyokuryū, in ancient times people valued fruit over flowers and didn't speak of flowers. Only after fully appreciating the beautiful fragrance of flowers did the Song dynasty begin composing poetry about them.

○In our country, what was anciently called "flower" was the plum. Since the medieval period, what is simply called "flower" is the cherry blossom.

○According to Shoku Nihongi, Emperor Shōmu in the 10th year of Tenpyō, 7th month, pointed to a plum tree in front of the palace and said: "Since last spring I have wished to admire this tree but have not yet had the opportunity for appreciation." He commanded thirty literati to each compose poems about plum trees, and all composed poems with spring sentiment.

　　　　The darkness of a spring night is mysterious - plum blossoms,
　　　　　　though their color cannot be seen, can their fragrance be hidden?

According to Yamato Honzō, listed in Plum Catalog:

are called river plums (kō-bai)
Named straight-trunk plums (chokkyaku-bai)
Some call these wild plums (ya-bai)

Wang In of Baekje called the plum "this flower." The god of learning (Sugawara no Michizane) always loved plums and left behind the name "Flying Plum." This derives from the Jin Records mentioning the designation "literature-loving tree." Tachibana no Naomoto's poem contains the title "Warbler-lodge Plum," and Saigyō's poem contains "Future-wish Plum" - such examples cannot be enumerated completely.
