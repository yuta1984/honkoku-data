Sunaheso (Sand Navel)
Lives in shallow water areas along the seashore. Its body burrows into the sand, with tentacles swaying in the water above like seaweed. When small insects enter among them, it wraps around and eats them. This is a type of what is commonly called "Inoshiri" or "Shirigodama."

【Lower Section】
When this creature hears the sound of human footsteps, it immediately contracts and retreats into the sand. When you dig in the sand and obtain a mass, it looks like a lump of earth. It has the odor of sea cucumber. Because its shape resembles a navel, it has this name. It is abundant in places called Etomo, Mitsuishi, and Konbumui in Matsumae. When the tentacles are opened, they are beautiful like flowers.
