River bats live in waterside areas and dwell under bridges. Their form is small and their color is black. However, when they spread their wings,
they expand to six or seven sun. Their eyes are small and difficult to see clearly.

Cold-calling Insect (Kango-chū) 《Note: The Bencao Gangmu originally categorizes this under birds. In reality, it is neither bird nor beast. In winter months it hibernates and resembles insect-kind. Thus it received the character for "insect."》
《Note: The Kakuchi Kyōgen has the name "cold-calling insect." Because it has the name of an insect, it is included together in this volume. I have a separate investigation with detailed analysis that should be referenced.》

This is a type of bat whose form closely resembles [regular bats] but is somewhat larger. By nature it constantly hangs upside down. It greatly fears severe cold. It has flesh-wings
that wrap and cover its body as protection against cold. It has four legs. Its front legs are
at the midpoint of the flesh-wings. Each left and right has one digit, with one claw at the digit's tip. It is like an iron hook. It also resembles an eagle's talon.
If you startle and provoke it with a small bamboo stick, it extends its hooks to fight and resist. Its condition is like something
angry by itself. It dwells hidden in warm rocky caverns, and at night emerges to fly and seek food. In severe
winter it hibernates. It hangs upside down without moving. At this time its fur falls out and it becomes no different from a naked body. It endures cold and hunger.
It cries out without ceasing. It eats its own droppings. After excreting, it eats them again. Therefore the droppings greatly
accumulate in one place, forming sticky lumps like candy. It is found on Yaeyama, an island belonging to Ryukyu. 《Note: Located in the southwestern sea of Chuzan, separated by a distance of 200 ri.》
