Observed on the 9th day of the 8th month of an unspecified year: A type of Hō (stink bug) with a form resembling a bell cricket but slender. Not foul-smelling.
Only the front two legs are thick and black.

Kōramushi　Hō (stink bug)
Kataginu-kake
In Aizu dialect: Baibukurin
Generally having a foul odor, this name
is also applied to things that should
be despised and avoided

【Upper Left Page】
Chinshō (stink bug)　Legs are long, antennae are short
　The proboscis is long and folds inward, invisible from the back. It is
　like a cicada's proboscis. Has blue vapor (odor) and is extremely foul-smelling

【Lower Left Page】
A type of Hō (stink bug) that is a highly poisonous insect. It is an insect that feeds on the leaves of the doku-e (poison plant) tree. Its entire
body is hard with armor, and there are two types: vermillion and cinnabar colored. All black parts
shine beautifully in deep blue or purple. Its
poison is more severe than that of blister beetles (hanmyō)
