Planthopper (Unka) - Called "Katsubo" in Tosa Province, "Nukabai" in Satsuma Province, and "Seseri" in Izumo and Hizen Provinces.
They swarm and fly under the eaves, moving up and down like people pounding rice. Or they fly in circles resembling a millstone. People use them to predict rain and clear weather. The children's song "Katsubo's rice pounding" refers to this, as it often catches people's attention. In the area around the Yodo River in Yamashiro Province, when the sky is cloudy, these insects swarm and fly, appearing like smoke and mist from a distance. When people encounter them while walking, they enter the eyes, nose, and mouth, causing considerable harm.

Kongō-san - Also called "Botsu-shi," known as "Buto." This is the "Mōshi" from the Honzō Kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica). Its form resembles a bee, about one bu in size, black with a shine. It lurks day and night, and when it stings people, it causes itchy swelling and forms pustules lasting several days - it is called a poisonous insect.

Dragonfly
This creature catches flies and sucks their bodily fluids. It does not harm people when it approaches them.
