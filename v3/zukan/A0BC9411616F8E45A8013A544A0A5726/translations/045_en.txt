According to the "Bokkyaku Kisai" (Ink Guest's Rhinoceros Whisk), there is an insect that resembles a cicada in shape but is small and flat, preferring to hide in roofs, walls, and among books. It has two long legs in front like crab claws; when touched from behind, it moves sideways, and when touched from the front, it moves backward. A scholar named Zheng playfully poked it repeatedly with his finger to observe its movement, when suddenly he was stung and lay in pain for several days. He was treated by a good physician and recovered. The doctor said this was called "evil sà" and would kill a person if left untreated.

Upon investigation, what is illustrated below on the right should be the so-called "evil sà." The name "atobisari" is the same as "sha-nuo-zi," but in reality it is not a type of sha-nuo-zi, but rather a separate species of small insect.

　　　　　　　　　　　Atobisari
　　　　　　　　　　　A type of sha-nuo-zi found occasionally in old medicines,
　　　　　　　　　　　shaped like a mite, extremely small,
　　　　　　　　　　　unbearable to observe with the naked eye, therefore
　　　　　　　　　　　drawn using a microscope
