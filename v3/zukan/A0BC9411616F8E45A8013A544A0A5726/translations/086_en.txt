【Note/Label】
　　Senchūfu (Thousand Insects Manual) - Manuscript copy in three volumes
　　　　Written by Kurimoto Zuiken in the eighth year of Bunka [1811]

Although various natural history books contain
sections on plants and animals,
there are very few works in the world that
compile insect species, so this was written
to fill that void.

【Below the note】
Showa ■■ year, September
At the main residence in
Takinogawa, Tokyo City
Grandson Itō Tokutarō, respectfully and sincerely

【Left edge of right page】
The above explanation is in the handwriting of my grandfather, Professor Itō Keisuke (Kinka). On April 16th, Meiji 15 [1882], at the celebration of the teacher's eightieth birthday held at Shinobazu Pond in Ueno, Tokyo, this three-volume manuscript copy of "Senchūfu" was exhibited among the books and materials on display. This is the teacher's explanation that accompanied the book at that time.
