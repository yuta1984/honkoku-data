A type of lizard - Kanāhebi (Japanese grass lizard), with a thin form and long tail, runs swiftly along tree branches. It inhabits Dōkan-yama. The ordinary stone dragon (common lizard) has a beautiful purple-green tail and lives only in stone walls around gardens, running swiftly on the ground but never running on tree branches. This creature differs in nature from that one.

The "Kokon-chū" states: "The gecko (eitei), also called ryūshi (dragon child), skillfully climbs trees to catch and eat cicadas. The very long and slender ones are these."

Fish fry - The newly born of the Japanese giant salamander
Found in mountain streams and valley waters that flow into Lake Biwa (Tankai), in places where fallen leaves have accumulated during spring months. Around the head area, three pine needle-like hairs grow on each side. When they mature and grow large, they reach two to three shaku in length. This is what is called mountain anglerfish or hanzaki.
