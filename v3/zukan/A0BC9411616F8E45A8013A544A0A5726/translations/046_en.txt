Morning glory borer insect　Drawn on the second day of the eighth month of Kinoe-Inu year
People generally think that no insects ever attach to the stems and leaves of morning glory, but in places with poor air circulation where many leaves are layered together, this insect may occasionally appear. This insect becomes extremely large over the course of months, with some reaching over three sun in length. If not removed early, it will eat and break the flower buds, preventing flowers from blooming and causing great damage. This is a type of caterpillar.
