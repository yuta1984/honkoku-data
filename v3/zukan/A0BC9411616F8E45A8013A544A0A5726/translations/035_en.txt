There is no need to search for it elsewhere. It is one of Japan's native products that brings honor to the country. There is a method for cooking sea cucumber. If the method is lost, it becomes hard and tough, inedible, and the taste is also not good. The method is: soak in water for one night, boil in hot water for one hour, then soak in water again for one night, and boil again for one hour. When it becomes soft enough to be cut when pinched with chopsticks, put it into broth and boil until the flavor penetrates. There is also another method called tender boiling. Soak in lukewarm water in a copper pot for one night, then the next morning add a handful of rice bran to one sho of hot water and boil for one hour, or two hours if it's a short day. This completes the seasoning and cooking process.
