【Upper Right Page】
Hō (stink bugs)　Two species
　Both emit an extremely blue odor when angered
　Like the scent of grass fruit (Japanese pepper)

In Shimotsuke dialect called Hiraga-mushi. The edge has a black ring, the border is cinnabar-colored, the inside is white, and the center is
black. The surrounding black eyes are outlined in brown. Inside the black ring is white
with wavy star shapes
five at a time. Along the back
runs a black line with eight white
stars lined up.
Legs and antennae are black.

【Lower Right Page】
Hō (stink bug)　Commonly called Hanniu. When touched, it produces the scent of grass fruit (Japanese pepper). In autumn months
it falls from the Phellodendron trees. It resembles the shape of a warrior wearing
a shoulder garment (kataginu).

【Upper Left Page】
Tsubafuki-mushi (spittle bug)　Also called Awa-mushi. Attaches to various grasses and trees.
　Especially abundant on roses. When it metamorphoses, it resembles a leafhopper.

　Like a small cicada, when you try to catch it, it jumps and flies five to six feet.
　Also called Sagoromo-semi. Those with red tails are males. At night
　they have light, said to be like firefly light.

【Lower Left Page】
Mimizuku　A type of leafhopper, an unusual variety.
　The white parts of its wings are transparent, resembling
