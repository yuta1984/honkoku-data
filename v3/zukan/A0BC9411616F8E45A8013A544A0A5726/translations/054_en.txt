Hanmyō (blister beetle) - I examined two types from old imported specimens. According to the "Honzō Genji" (Origins of Materia Medica), hanmyō is a beetle that appears on soybean leaves in the 7th and 8th months. It is five to six bu long, has yellow-black spotted patterns, a bird-like abdomen and pointed proboscis, and is collected from leaves and dried in shade. "Han" refers to its color, and "myō" refers to its poison being like a spear thrust. It is commonly but erroneously called hanbyō (spotted cat).
