Fukuyoku - namely bat (koumori), a mountain bat with large ears and an entirely brown body.
　　　　What is illustrated here is a mountain bat with reddish-brown fur color.
　　　　　　Around Utsunomiya in Shimotsuke Province, there are various rare varieties of this species. There are
　　　　　　peony-headed ones with growths on their noses resembling
　　　　　　double peony flowers. What is called "chrysanthemum head" resembles the state of thousand-petaled chrysanthemums. Also, rabbit
　　　　　　bats have long ears resembling
　　　　　　rabbit ears. They are completely absent from Edo. All of these
　　　　　　are mountain bats that have grown old and large over many years.
　　　　　When they spread their wings, they span from one shaku eight sun to nearly two shaku. When flying
　　　　　at night, they resemble crows or kites. Most hang upside down
　　　　　in caves that produce stalactites. When torches are lit to illuminate them,
　　　　　they fly away in alarm. The sound of their beating wings is said to be frightening. Also,
　　　　　among hundreds or thousands, there are occasionally white ones, which people say are
　　　　　auspicious creatures that have lived a thousand years, but this is not so. It is the same as how sparrows and
　　　　　certain birds today can be pure white.
