Kyōrō (Dung beetle) - Specimen from the Tamura Motoo family collection. The back and head have a plump roundness, and there are fine striations on the shell.

Kusomushi (Dung beetle)
Born in filthy places among excrement. Its form is black and long like a silverfish (shimiмushi), and also resembles a pillbug (sowbug). Its texture is soft. One type called Yoroimushi has coarse serrated teeth protruding with horizontal discontinuous patterns, and its black shell shines with a deep blue color. This is called Yoroi-mushi (armored insect). The Erya states: "Mayflies resemble dung beetles but are longer, have horns, are yellow-black in color, and live in clusters in dung and soil." According to Ranzan's theory, "those with fine striations like a brush on their shells are mayflies."
