Kokin Autumn Songs, Volume 6
Matsumushi　　　　　　　　　　　　Tsurayuki　Three poems
Wet with dew in the autumn field, for whom do so many person-awaiting insects cry?
Has the time when you said you would come already passed? In the autumn field, the voice of person-awaiting insects is sorrowful

In the autumn field, with no one seeking lodging, for whom do so many matsumushi cry?
When evening comes and the person-awaiting insects cry, this solitary body's longing only grows stronger
Do the white waves piling jewels in the rushing waterfall cry out the sound of the flowing water?

The above poem was composed with a hidden theme　　　　　Empress of Gojō
If it were said that my matsumushi cry in the autumn field, I would see the flowers while lying without picking them

《Next comes the poem "In the old hometown grown wild with shinobu grass," but since it does not differ from the Kokinshū version, it is omitted here》
《Hirotaka says: Among these very ancient poems about matsumushi, except for the hidden theme poem, all are composed about "person-waiting." Considering Lord Tadamine's words about "moon matsumushi," perhaps it was originally named "waiting insect"》

Poems about suzumushi (bell crickets)
Kokin Wakashū Volume 6
Suzumushi
When we meet occasionally, though the suzumushi are intimate, their voices can be heard
Until the time when people hunt for their sweethearts, by each maiden flower the suzumushi cry
Coming to hunt, I wander in the fields - though the suzumushi's voice is a clear guide
