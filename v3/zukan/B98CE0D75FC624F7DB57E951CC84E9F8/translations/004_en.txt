**[From the Tashiki-hen (Encyclopedic Compilation)]**

*Kusa-ishiko* (Choroki / Chinese artichoke): Alternative names: *Toyō* (Earth Pupa) [from *Yodōroku*]; *Jisan* (Earth Silkworm) [from *Nichiyō*];
　　*Kanroshi* (Sweet Dew Child) [from *Shokumotsuhonzō*]; *Tekiro* (Dripping Dew) [from *Honzō Kōmoku*]

**[From the Kyūkō Honzō (Famine Relief Materia Medica)]**

*Kanroji* (Choroki): *Hitari-neji* (left-twisted) — local dialect of Bingo Province
　　　　　　　*Choro* — local dialect of Kōtō, Shinshū Province

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Drawn from a stem cut in the rear garden,
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　on a certain day in early autumn, the year Kichū [己丑, 1829].

**[Author's note:]**
　　The *Tashiki-hen* treats *Jikaji* (地瓜兒) as an alternative name for *Kanroji* (甘露兒 / Choroki), but this is incorrect. *Jikaji* refers to the plant known as *Shirone* (or *Kusa-choroki*), which resembles Choroki but is a distinct species. It lacks the segmented, silkworm-like swellings on the roots, and instead has long hair-like roots extending from the base.
