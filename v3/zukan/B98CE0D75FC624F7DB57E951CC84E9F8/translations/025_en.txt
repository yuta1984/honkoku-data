From Yamato Honzō (Japanese Materia Medica):
　　　Yamasotetsū (Mountain Cycad)　yamasotetsū
　　　　　　　Kujaku-shida (Peacock Fern) 《marginal note: also called｜》
　　　　　　　《marginal note: kujaku-shida｜》





　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Kanoto-U year, Mushā (9th month), 15th day
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　True sketch from flourishing roots in the garden



　　　Baien says:
　　　The yamasotetsū, when its flowers bloom, has leaves shaped like a peacock's tail,
　　　hence it is called kujaku-shida (peacock fern). Kusetsu (senmai), inchi-warabi (fuewarabi), their flowers all look similar. Gaishō (gansō),
　　　keizuran, kusetsu-shida ○ onimaki-haku (onishida) ○ sekichōsei (kōberasō) ○ sekii (hitotsuha) ○ kinseisō ○
　　　All these types have flowers and fruits on the undersides of their leaves, therefore they are plants that produce seedlings where their true essence resides.
