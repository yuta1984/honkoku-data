[Hill Spatterdock (Oka-kōhone)]
The flower resembles standing spatterdock, with yellow color and slight reddish tinge. The flower stem is long with branches. The leaf arrangement resembles camellia but has round indentations and is glossy. There is reddish coloring at the base of the main root, and there are bag-like leaves at the base of branches. It blooms from April to May.

[Garden Balsam (Hōsenka)]
The flower shape resembles a bird spreading its wings. The color is red, with varieties in deep red and pure white, as well as peach and purple colors. There are also striped and speckled varieties. The leaf shape also resembles peach leaves and is long. The plant comes in various sizes, with heights ranging from three to four shaku, or one to two shaku, or four to five sun. Some have branches while others do not. It blooms from March and also from August to September. It grows in mountain villages. Its proper name is Golden Phoenix Flower (Kinpōka). It is also commonly called "bone-pulling grass."

[Safflower (Kōka)]
The leaf color is light green, and both the receptacle and stem are light colored. It has red flowers in April and May. The leaves resemble thistle but without indentations, having thorns and growing densely. The leaves have spines.

【Left page】
It is hard. The flowers are red in color, and the buds resemble thistle. They are densely surrounded by thorns and leaves, with only a few flowers opening. The stamens extend and split into five at the tips. The plant grows to about three shaku in height with many branches. The buds are numerous and attach along the leaves.

[Water Plantain (Mizuaoi)
Water Plant (Mizunagi)  
Floating Plant (Fushoku)
Marsh Bellflower (Sawa-kikyō)]
The leaf shape resembles podocarpus and is glossy. It emerges wrapping around the stem. Those with leaves resembling hollyhock are called water plantain. The flower stems stand upright with six-petaled flowers blooming in succession. There are three varieties: light colored, white, and purple. Like bellflowers, the flower tips are colored. They are lapis lazuli blue, resembling small wisteria bellflowers with deeply cut petals. The flowers bloom successively. They appear in June, July, and August.

[Egret Orchid 《Water Plantain》
White Calyx 《resembles》]
The leaves resemble bamboo grass leaves, being narrow and emerging wrapped around the stem. The flowers are snow white, shaped like small birds in flight, or resembling flying egrets. It grows beside marshes and is an aquatic plant. It appears in July and August.
