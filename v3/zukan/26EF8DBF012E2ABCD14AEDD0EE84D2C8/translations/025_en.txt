　　　　　　　　　This plant grows in various places around Hakone's Tozawa,
　　　　　　　　　Mount Togamine, the foothills of Amida Temple,
　　　　　　　　　rocky areas, hot springs, and near bridges,
　　　　　　　　　where it flourishes abundantly. It prefers moist rocky
　　　　　　　　　cliffs. Its leaves resemble those of the zelkova tree,
　　　　　　　　　being soft with a glossy appearance and
　　　　　　　　　moisture content, hence called "leaf-water" in
　　　　　　　　　Sashu Province. It resembles what is depicted in illustrations.
　　　　　　　　　Upon examination, there are three varieties. One
　　　　　　　　　is a small herb that produces several stems from one root.
　　　　　　　　　The leaves and flowers show no particular differences. One variety has large leaves
　　　　　　　　　with significantly different form and grows in various places in Toko.
　　　　　　　　　I have sketched it in Volume 4 of "Hundred Flowers, Autumn Section."
　　　　　　　　　The flowering seasons differ greatly, and even the small
　　　　　　　　　herb variety blooms with small white flowers in spring.
