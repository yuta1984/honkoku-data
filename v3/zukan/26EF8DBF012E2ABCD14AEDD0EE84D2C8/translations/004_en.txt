【Left page, upper section】
○The Kinshū Mankakoku states that while the Nine Paths lack the character for tea, there is merely the character for tea

○Regarding tea, the Eastern Kingdoms General Mirror states that from Silla Kingdom, Daeryeom was sent as to Tang, obtained the character for tea and brought it back. By royal command it was planted on Mount Jii [Tang Emperor Wenzong, Dahe 2nd year] [Japan's Junna, Tenchō 5th year]. This was the beginning of tea cultivation in Korea

○In our country, the tea ceremony began in the first year of Kōnin under Emperor Saga, preceding Korea by about ten years. Later, the monk Myōe entered Tang, obtained fine varieties, returned and planted them at Mount Togano-o [at Beiyan in Jianzhou, China]

For sencha tea, the produce of Togano-o in Jōshū and Ashikubo village in Abe district of Sunshū is considered superior. The vine tea of Hyūga and Tanba is also acceptable. Masamune in Gōshū, Shimoichi in Washū, and Fudōbō in Yoshū are not inferior. All other provinces also have these

○Generally, tea trees have their new buds picked three to four days after the Grain Rain solar term and are processed accordingly. The methods differ between Japan and China [in Japan they steam and dry, in China they roast and steam]. The finest ones are made into thick tea: Hatsumukashi, Imamukashi, Nakamukashi, Atomukashi, Takatsume, Shikishina. The finest are offered for imperial use, the next grade goes to noble and samurai houses, the next becomes bundled tea. The very finest each... [there are also grades like Kyokuketsubetsu and Giketsu] Selected leaves that don't curl and shrink are called sorori [sosori]

○Although our country's tea ceremony began during Emperor Saga's reign, its flourishing began at the Eastern Mountain Villa [Minamoto no Yoshimasa]. Selecting precious Japanese and Chinese ceramics, bowls, boxes, braziers, etc., requesting vessels and consuming tea is called sukiya (tea connoisseurship)

○The Wamyō Ruijushō states:
Tea-ming: The Erya commentary states that tea is a small tree resembling gardenia. Its leaves can be boiled for drinking. Now we call early picking "tea" and late picking "ming." Ming's alternate name is jia. The Fudoki states that jia is the name for old leaves of ming, etc.
