　　From "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Encyclopedia of China and Japan), Wet Grass Section:
　　　　　　Ketsurōsō　　Yokome-gusa　　Koshō-gusa
　　　　　　　　　　　〇Commonly called shiba (turf grass)
　　　　　　　Kōrai-shiba (Korean grass)
　　　　　　　Kara-shiba (Chinese grass)

　　　　　　　"Kyūkō" states, and Shiko's "Commentary on the Book of Han" says: Ketsurō produces vines that spread along the ground,
　　　　　　　and wherever they touch the ground, fine roots grow, interconnecting like threads, hence the name. It is commonly called koshō-
　　　　　　　gusa (drum-koto grass) because when children hold both ends and pluck it with their hands, it produces a sound from the center that resembles
　　　　　　　a koto (Japanese zither).
