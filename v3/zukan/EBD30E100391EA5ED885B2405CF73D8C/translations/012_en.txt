　　One variety
　　　　Shōbu-no-ki (Sweet flag tree) <<Called in Edo>>
　　　　Sekishōboku　　
　　　　Toritsukeshiba
　　　　　　　　　　　　Commonly found in mountains and fields. The leaves resemble
　　　　　　　　　　　　those of Lindera umbellata but are hard. In summer, small
　　　　　　　　　　　　pale yellow flowers bloom between the leaves, and it bears
　　　　　　　　　　　　fruit. When ripe, the fruit turns black, and when dried,
　　　　　　　　　　　　it becomes wrinkled and resembles pepper. The taste is
　　　　　　　　　　　　pungent, and the fragrance of the branches and leaves
　　　　　　　　　　　　resembles that of Acorus gramineus. Whether this plant
　　　　　　　　　　　　should be classified as a variety of mountain pepper is
　　　　　　　　　　　　not clear, but it is recorded here because of the pungent taste of its fruit.
