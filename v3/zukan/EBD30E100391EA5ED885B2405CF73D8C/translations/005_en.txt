Chinese Pepper (Shin-shō) - Japanese Pepper (Sanshō), Hin-shō 《Tanba region》, Native Pepper (Do-shō) 《Under the section on Sichuan Pepper》

The tree reaches a height of over one jō (approximately 3+ meters). The leaves resemble those of the soap pod tree (Gleditsia japonica), are narrow, and have fine serrations around the edges. The leaves have pale green spots. The branches produce thorns of three to four bu (about 1 cm) in length. This tree has both male and female specimens. The male forms clusters of small yellow flowers that bloom densely but do not bear fruit. The female rarely flowers but does bear fruit. The fruit is about the size of an azuki bean and has pores like yuzu citrus peel. When it ripens in autumn, it turns vermillion-black and naturally splits in two. Inside are fine black seeds that are glossy and resemble mouse eyes. Trees with a trunk circumference of three to four sun (about 9-12 cm) or more are used to make medicinal shavings. It is said to neutralize poisons. The bark has pockmarks resembling the back of a toad. This bark is called "kara-○○." It is a famous product of Mount Nikkō in Yashū Province.
