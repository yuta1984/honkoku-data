【Right Page】
　Among the several types of pepper (sanshō), the one that comes from Mount Takiji in Asakura Village, Tajima Province is called
　Asakura sanshō. It is also called Jōkō sanshō and is of superior quality. It should be used medicinally. Currently this
　variety is abundant in many places. The tree height is about one jō (approximately 3 meters), and those without thorns on branches and trunk are considered superior quality.
　Even those with thorns sometimes bear fruit, but these are considered second grade. The leaves resemble those of Qin
　pepper (shin-shō), being round, small, and thick with a glossy surface, raised wrinkle patterns, and white spots. At the end of spring,
　small yellow flowers grow in clusters. Male plants do not bear fruit, while female plants bear fruit without flowers.
　The shape resembles Evodia rutaecarpa (go-shuyu), being round, and compared to Qin pepper, the pericarp is thicker and larger. When ripe, it
　turns red with stomata, and the pericarp naturally splits in two. Inside are
　small black seeds with a glossy surface.
　They look like mouse eyes and are called shōmoku (pepper seeds). The taste is very pungent and the aroma is fragrant.
