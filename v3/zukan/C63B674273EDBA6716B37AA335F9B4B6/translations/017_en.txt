Yōro (Weigela hortensis, Garden Weigela) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. A small tree commonly found in mountains. Leaves emerge in spring with fine serrated edges and grow in opposite pairs. In summer, it opens tubular flowers between the leaves. The flowers are light pink in color, and there are also white varieties. Later it produces pods.

Rōbai (Chimonanthus praecox, Wintersweet) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. It is a product of foreign lands. The tree height exceeds one jō (approximately 3+ meters). Leaves emerge in spring, being pointed, hard, and rough to the touch. In winter, it opens deep yellow flowers with nine petals. It resembles plum blossoms. Inside, there are small petals of deep purple color divided into nine parts. After flowering, it rarely produces pods with black seeds inside. These seeds are deeply toxic. If mistakenly consumed, they cause sudden vomiting and severe diarrhea.
