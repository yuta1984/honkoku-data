Genkwa (genka｜shigenji) 《Honzō kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica)》
　　　Has great toxicity. It is a small tree that does not exceed two to three shaku in height. Opens flowers in spring. Four-petaled, light purple in color, clustering densely on each branch, rarely forming fruit. Leaves emerge after flowering ends. Has fine hairs. Takes root well even from cuttings. If these leaves are crushed and thrown into pond water, all fish will die. Therefore it has the name "fish poison" (Tsūga). Those fish must never be eaten. Has great toxicity.

Jōka (jōka｜kikogambi) 《Honzō kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica)》
　　　Has toxicity. A small tree, three to four shaku tall. Branches and leaves grow opposite each other, leaves resemble genkwa leaves but are thinner. In autumn, branches divide at the tips, opening four-petaled yellow tubular flowers. Resembles genkwa. Also, kogambi has leaves similar to genkwa leaves but alternate and densely packed. Between summer and autumn, small four-petaled white flowers open at branch tips. Alternatively, gambi used for papermaking reaches over one jō in height and has large leaves. Small four-petaled flowers cluster and bloom. The tube is white, petals yellow. These are all varieties of the same species.
