Kyōchikutō (Oleander) 《Secret Mirror of Flowers・Japanese and Chinese Common Names》
　　　Has toxicity. It is a product of warm countries and fears cold. In winter, frost and snow should be avoided. Height is five to six shaku. The leaves are elongated and roughly similar to bamboo leaves in shape but slightly narrower. In summer, it opens pale pink flowers at the branch tips. There are several calyxes in one cluster. To propagate this by cutting, during summer use bamboo tubes to grip the nodes of branches, and if nurtured with fertile soil, roots will immediately form. Cut and plant them.

Mokuririro (Leucothoe grayana) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. It is a small tree that grows in mountains. It has many branches that grow alternately. The leaf surface is light green, the underside is pale white, and they grow alternately. In summer it opens flowers at the tips of new branches, resembling spikes. After flowering, it produces fruit. If you pick these leaves, rub them, and put them in your nose, you will immediately sneeze. Also, if placed in latrines, all insects will die. Some say that if you hang these leaves in latrines, you can avoid dysentery.
