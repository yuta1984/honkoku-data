In'u (Skimmia japonica var. intermedia f. repens) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in mountains. It is a small tree that does not exceed two or three shaku in height. The leaves grow alternately in front, back, left and right positions, with red-colored petioles. It opens white flowers at the branch tips, tinged with brown. After flowering, it produces fruit. After autumn, the fruit turns red and is larger than nandina berries.

Sekinan (Rhododendron) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in deep mountains. The leaves do not wither in winter, but it considerably fears the cold. In spring it produces new leaves and opens pale pink flowers at the branch tips, clustering together. The form of each individual flower is roughly like that of mountain azalea flowers. There are also varieties with different leaves.
