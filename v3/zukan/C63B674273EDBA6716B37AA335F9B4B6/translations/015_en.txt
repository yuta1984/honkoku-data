Teitō (Erythrina species) 《Compendium of Materia Medica, Paulownia Collection Commentary》
　　　Has toxicity. It is a product of southern countries and fears cold. If its wood is cut and planted in spring, it immediately takes root. The leaves resemble paulownia but have wrinkles, are thick and round, with serrated edges. In summer, it branches at the branch tips, opens five-petaled vermillion-red flowers, and extends long stamens. It settles down when autumn arrives.
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　It endures for a long time.

Eibō (Euonymus alatus, Burning Bush) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has mild toxicity. Grows in mountains. In spring, it produces new branches with arrow-feather-like projections on four sides. The leaves are opposite, turn red in autumn, and immediately wither and fall. In summer, it opens small yellow-green flowers between the leaves and produces fruit. After autumn, they split open. They are red in color. This fruit has deep toxicity. Small children should be cautious.
