Kongōsan (Fatsia japonica, Japanese Aralia) 《Jibutsu Konshu》
　　　Has toxicity. The leaves are divided into eight or nine lobes and have a glossy appearance. The tree height is seven to eight shaku (approximately 2.1-2.4 meters). In winter, it opens small white flowers at the branch tips, blooming in dense clusters. After flowering, it produces fruit that turns purple-black when ripe. When split open, purple juice emerges. Should avoid consuming with fish.

【Left Page】
Sōboku (Aralia elata, Japanese Angelica Tree) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has mild toxicity. Grows in mountains. The branches and trunk are entirely covered with many sharp thorns. In spring, it produces leaves at the branch tips. The stems also have sharp thorns. In autumn, they wither and fall. It opens small white flowers between the leaves and produces fruit. If mistakenly chewed, it damages the teeth. There is something called "medara," which is one variety of this plant.
