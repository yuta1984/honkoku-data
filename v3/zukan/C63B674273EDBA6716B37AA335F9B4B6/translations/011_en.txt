Koshōnoki 《Zuikō｜Chinese name unknown》《Honzō kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica)》 one variety
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in deep mountains. A small tree whose form resembles ketkō (Edgeworthia papyrifera, paper bush), with branches dividing into three or four forks. In spring, flowers bloom at the tips of old branches. Light yellow with four petals, clustering like zuikō flowers (Daphne odora flowers). Forms fruit after flowering. When ripe in summer, it turns red like wolfberry. If mistakenly eaten, causes agony for about half a day. Its taste is extremely pungent, hence it usurps the name of pepper (koshō).

Jōzan (jōzan｜Orixa japonica) 《Honzō kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica)》
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in mountains. A small tree whose leaves resemble tea leaves, glossy and smooth with veins. Has a very strong odor. Opens flowers in spring. Four petals, green in color, growing in clusters. Forms fruit after flowering, shaped like corbicula clams. Also, Kaishū jōzan (Clerodendrum trichotomum) 《Jōzan shūkai｜Shūgōdō gunhōfu》grows in mountains and throughout villages. Has a very strong odor and is toxic. In Japanese folk practice, insects from it are collected and used as medicine for children's kan (digestive disorders).
