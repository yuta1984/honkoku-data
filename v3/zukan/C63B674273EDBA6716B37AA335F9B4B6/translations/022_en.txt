Ōshitō (Vernicia cordata, Tung Oil Tree) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has strong toxicity. It grows wild in mountains. The leaves emerge in spring, are three-pointed, and have no serrated edges. In summer, it opens five-petaled white flowers. It produces fruit with three vertical ridges and three compartments inside. Each compartment contains one seed. The toxicity of these seeds is extremely strong.

Taro (U｜Imo) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　"U" is the general term for taro. Purple taro 《Taro Collective Commentary》has mild toxicity. In spring, old tubers are planted, preferring wetlands. The stem is purple-colored, and long ones reach five to six shaku in length. The leaves are green, and the parent tuber is large. Occasionally flowers bloom in autumn. They emerge from between the leaves of early and late varieties, producing light yellow petals at the stem tip. The shape roughly resembles that of Pinellia ternata flowers, with stamens inside. Later it produces fruit. From summer to autumn, child tubers develop beside the parent tuber. Blue taro 《same as above》also has mild toxicity. Its stem is blue-green with a white powdery coating. The leaves are no different from purple taro. Occasionally flowers bloom in autumn, resembling purple taro flowers. It produces many child tubers.
