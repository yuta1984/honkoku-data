Garlic (Taisan｜Ninniku) 《Bencao Gangmu》
　　　It has toxicity. It grows in mountains and fields. The leaves are larger than those of wild garlic (nobiru). In summer, it first produces purple-colored
　　　fruits at the stem tips, and white flowers bloom among them. The roots, stems, leaves, and fruits all have a strong odor. If the juice
　　　gets into the eyes, it causes pain and illness. Eating too much causes blindness, produces phlegm, and
　　　turns hair white. When eaten together with honey, it becomes highly toxic and can
　　　kill people.

【Left Page】
Wild Garlic (Sansan｜Nobiru) 《Bencao Gangmu》
　　　It has mild toxicity. It grows everywhere in mountains, plains, and flat lands. The leaves are slender and have a very strong odor. The root bulbs are smaller than those of garlic (ninniku).
　　　In early summer it extends stems, opens white flowers, and bears fruit. Small garlic (Seisan｜Kobiru) 《Bencao Gangmu・Garlic Collection》
　　　also has mild toxicity. It is wild garlic (nobiru) that has been planted in gardens and cultivated. It is not a different species.
　　　Also, scallion (Gai｜Gyōjabiru) 《Bencao Gangmu》 has no toxicity. However, it should not be eaten raw in the third and fourth months.
