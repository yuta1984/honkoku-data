Pumpkin (Bannankan｜Tōnasu) 《Qunfang Pu》
　　　It has toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. The vines, stems, and leaves all have hairs. In midsummer it opens yellow flowers and bears melons.
　　　Eating too much damages the blood. Pregnant women should not eat it. The flat-shaped ones are called Nankan (Kabocha)
　　　《Bencao Gangmu》, and the round ones without ridges and red in color are called Kōnankan (Akoda) 《Runan Pushi》. It should not be eaten
　　　together with pepper. It is extremely toxic. All
　　　food combination taboos are
　　　recorded separately in one volume.

【Left Page】
Green Onion (Seisō｜Nebuka) 《Zhenjiang Fuzhi》
　　　It has mild toxicity. The round leaves are hollow inside, with pointed tips and a strong odor. In early summer it extends stems, and at the stem tips
　　　small white flowers cluster together forming balls, later bearing fruit. It should not be eaten together with honey, jujube, or chicken.
　　　People taking Rehmannia should avoid it. Pregnant women should not eat it. A proverb says
　　　that eating it during pregnancy will cause the child to develop body odor. All pungent vegetables should be avoided.
　　　Those harvested and eaten during the plum rain season are called Kansō (Karigi) 《Bencao Gangmu・Onion Collection》. Winter onion (Wakegi)
　　　《same as above》 is a different variety that produces no flowers or fruit. It has no toxicity.
