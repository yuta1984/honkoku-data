Sōryō (Trachycarpus fortunei, Chinese Windmill Palm) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. It has no branches and grows straight upward, grows slowly and is long-lived. The leaf stalks are triangular, and there is fibrous bark at their base. Each time it grows, it adds one layer. It does not wither throughout the four seasons. In summer, it produces bracts at the base of the leaf stalks and opens flowers. They are yellow and very densely packed. After flowering, it produces fruit.

Beech Tree 《Chinese name unknown》
　　　Has toxicity. It grows in deep mountains. Its leaves resemble oak leaves and are round. It sheds leaves in winter. In summer it blooms and produces fruit. The mushroom that grows on this tree is called Buna-take (beech mushroom). Its shape resembles shiitake mushrooms, but the stem does not emerge from the center. Some people mix this with shiitake mushrooms when selling them, which is very wrong. It is a poisonous variety. If mistakenly eaten raw, it causes laughter. Generally, there are poisonous varieties among fungi and mushrooms. This book does not list them all.
