False Hellebore (Riro) 《Honzo Komoku》
　　　Has great toxicity, and there are two types. One type grows from old roots in spring. The leaves
　　　generally resemble young palm fronds with vertical striations. At the base there is something like
　　　palm bark that wraps around the stem.
　　　In summer, it extends a single stalk from the center, branching at the end to open small flowers. Six petals, purple-black color.
　　　After flowering, it bears fruit. This is the "Sōkan-riro" (palm grass) 《alternative name for False Hellebore》. If mistakenly consumed,
　　　it causes sudden vomiting and severe diarrhea. Also, if the root is powdered and put in the nose, it causes sneezing. One type
