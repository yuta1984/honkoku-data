Shinboku (Pieris japonica, Japanese Pieris) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. It grows in mountains. The leaves are elongated with serrated edges. It does not wither through winter and produces new leaves in spring. They are reddish in color. Its flowers are white, form spikes, and droop downward. Later it produces fruit, all of which mature facing upward. When cattle and horses eat these leaves, they become as if intoxicated. Therefore, the Japanese name is "basuiboku" (horse-intoxicating tree). When deer eat it, they shed their antlers out of season. When people smell the decoction of this plant, they develop headaches.

Mukuanji (Sapindus mukorossi, Chinese Soapberry) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has mild toxicity. It grows in deep mountains. The leaves grow several to one stem and fall in winter. In summer it opens small white flowers at branch tips and produces fruit. When ripe, it turns yellow. Its seeds are black, perfectly round, and extremely hard. The flesh outside the seed is taken and soaked in water, called "shabon" (soap), and children play with this. This is very inadvisable.
