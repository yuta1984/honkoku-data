Konjac (Kujaku｜Konnyaku-imo) 《Bencao Gangmu》
　　　It has toxicity. When old tubers are planted in summer, they grow immediately. The stems are green with purple-black spots. At the tip,
　　　the stem branches, and several leaves attach to one stem. Flowers grow from the center. They are black, and the petal tips are initially
　　　pointed, but after flowering the points disappear. The root is like a potato tuber. When processed for
　　　food consumption, it is called konnyaku 《Wuli Xiaoshi》. People who have not had
　　　smallpox should not eat it. It triggers epilepsy. Children are forbidden
　　　to eat it. It aggravates the five kinds of malnutrition.

Eggplant (Ka｜Nasubi) 《Bencao Gangmu》
　　　It has toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. When the leaves are young, they are purple, the stems are also purple, later becoming green with thorns. From summer
　　　to autumn, it opens light purple flowers with six to seven petals, or sometimes eight to nine petals, and bears eggplants.
　　　The fruiting position is not fixed; they grow in the middle of stems, with purple-black color and luster. The calyx has thorns.
　　　The seeds are like pepper seeds. People suffering from malaria should not eat them. Generally, after autumn,
　　　the toxicity becomes deeper. The Gangmu states: "Li Yanfei says that eating too much after autumn damages the eyes. Shizhen says that according to the 'Shengsheng Bian': The nature of eggplant is cold and purgative; eating too much necessarily causes abdominal pain and diarrhea. In women it can damage the uterus." Especially its
　　　variants or double-fruited ones have deep toxicity. They must not be eaten. A folk saying
　　　goes: "Autumn eggplants taste delicious, so mothers-in-law hate their daughters-in-law and don't let them eat them."
