Purple Wisteria (shitō｜fuji) 《Honzō kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica)》
　　　Has mild toxicity. Grows in mountains and dense forests. It is a climbing vine, and large specimens can become tree-like. Leaves emerge in spring. The flowers are purple and hang downward; long ones can reach five to six shaku in length. After flowering, it forms pods. There are also white-flowered varieties and ones called "natsu-fuji" (summer wisteria), which are of the same species. Details are given in the Seifu (Nature Records).

Caesalpinia (unjitsu｜sarutori-ibara) 《Honzō kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica)》
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in mountains and fields. The tree height reaches over one jō and spreads like a vine. The branches and trunk have very many sharp thorns. Leaves emerge in spring. The shape resembles soap pod tree leaves. The stem has many thorns. At evening time, they close together. In summer, yellow flowers grow consecutively at the branch tips, forming spikes. Later it forms pods. When ripe, they turn chestnut shell color, with seeds inside. If one mistakenly eats these flowers, one will immediately become mad and run about frantically.
