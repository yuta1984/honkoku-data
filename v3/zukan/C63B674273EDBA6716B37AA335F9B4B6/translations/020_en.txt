Urushi (Toxicodendron vernicifluum, Japanese Lacquer Tree) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. It grows abundantly in mountains and in the groves of village settlements. The leaves are like those of the Chinese mahogany (Toona sinensis) but with fewer leaflets and no serrated edges. It opens flowers in summer and produces fruit, which ripens in autumn. The sap from the tree bark is extracted and processed to make lacquer for coating objects. There is also "haze-urushi" (wax tree), which is one variety of this species.

Rō-no-ki (Wax Tree) 《A variety of lacquer tree | Chinese name unknown》
　　　Has toxicity. It grows abundantly in mountains and along roadsides. The form of the tree resembles that of the lacquer tree. The leaves also resemble those of the lacquer tree but are slightly smaller. It opens flowers in autumn and produces flattened fruit. The seeds are harvested to manufacture wax.
