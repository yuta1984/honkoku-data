Spinach (Haryō | Hōrensō) 《Bencao Gangmu》
　　　Has mild toxicity. Seeds are sown in autumn. The leaves initially have no points, but later develop one point, with two points on the sides, or sometimes four points. When the stem grows, those two or four points cease to develop. The leaves are soft and thick. The stem root is red in color. In summer it extends its stem. There are male and female plants. The male produces flowers but does not bear fruit. The female does not produce flowers but bears fruit. It has two spines. It should not be eaten together with mustard or eel. It conflicts with gallnuts, so iron solution (teeth blackening) should be avoided. Ancient texts say that eating it on a day when iron solution has been applied will lead to death.

Tobacco (Ensō | Tabako) 《Bencao Huiyan》
　　　Has toxicity. Originally transmitted from barbarian countries. Seeds are sown in spring. The leaves are light green, alternately arranged, and have fine hairs. Height is three to four shaku. In autumn it extends its stem and opens several flowers at the tip. They resemble sesame flowers. They form tubes with five petals of light pink color. After flowering, it bears fruit. Although it is commonly used without aversion, its qi is fierce and the smoke penetrates the stomach inappropriately. If used frequently, it causes vomiting from rising qi and diarrhea.
