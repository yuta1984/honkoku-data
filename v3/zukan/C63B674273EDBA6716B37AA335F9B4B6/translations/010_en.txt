Zuigyosō (suigyosō｜Fujiu tsugi - Buddleja) 《Honzō kōmoku (Compendium of Materia Medica)》
　　　Has minor toxicity. It is a small tree with opposite leaves. In autumn, it extends branches and opens flowers. Light purple, forming tubes, blooming diagonally like spikes. Forms fruit after flowering. If these leaves are picked and thrown into a pond, all fish will die. Those fish must never be eaten.

Mokuhon ōseiyo (mokuhon ōseiyō｜poison tree) Kōfun (hook kiss) 《Honzō kōmoku Kōfun seigo (Compendium of Materia Medica, Corrections on Gelsemium)》
　　　Has great toxicity. Grows abundantly in mountains. The tree reaches five to six shaku in height. Leaves grow opposite each other, leaf stalks are red in color, and the leaf surface has three longitudinal veins. In summer it produces flowers forming spikes of reddish-white color that do not fully bloom. Later it forms flattened fruit that turns red when ripe. If mistakenly eaten, it leads to death. If birds eat this fruit, they die instantly. Also, if these leaves are picked, crushed, and mixed into rice to feed to rats, they die instantly. It is an extremely fearsome thing.
