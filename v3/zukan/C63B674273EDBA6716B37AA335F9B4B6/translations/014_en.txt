Mōsō (Illicium anisatum) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in mountains. The leaves do not wither even after passing through winter and have a fragrance. Opens flowers in spring. They are white-yellow colored, roughly like wax plum flowers. After flowering, it produces fruit. It has a foul odor and deep toxicity. According to the Japanese Interpretive Dictionary, "shikimi" is an abbreviation of "ashikimi" (evil tree). If mistakenly eaten together with purple sugar, it can lead to death. It is also toxic to fish.

Yōtekichoku (Rhododendron molle) 《Compendium of Materia Medica》
　　　Has great toxicity. It is a mountain product. Compared to mountain azalea, the leaves are longer and larger, and their shape roughly resembles rhododendron leaves. Opens yellow flowers in spring. Also called "ki-tsutsuji."
　　　　　　　　　　　　　According to the Compendium's explanatory notes: "When sheep eat its leaves, they suffer convulsions and die, hence the name."
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Renge-tsutsuji (Japanese azalea) is one variety of this species.
