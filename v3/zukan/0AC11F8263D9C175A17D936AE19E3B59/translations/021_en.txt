【Right page】
Soyogo-take (Holly mushroom)

　Illustration found in
　Shin'yō Kinfu. According to the same book,
　its shape resembles
　Oshōnin (a type of mushroom)
　but the taste is inferior.
　The surface is deep green, similar
　to the color of soyogo
　tree leaves, hence
　the name.

【Right bottom】
Ume-take　Bai-shin (from Goken-shi)
　　Baiju-shin (from Go-jin-fu)

　Illustration found in Kinshi. According to the same book, it rarely
　grows under old plum trees (omitted). It is delicious and
　eaten. Soft, smooth, fresh and tasty, with very strong fragrance. It appears during
　summer and autumn. There is one variety of pale red color
　that is somewhat larger in form but considerably inferior in fragrance and taste.

【Left page】
Ume-take

Illustration found in
Shin'yō Kinfu. According to the same book,
it does not grow on plum trees
but grows on the ground. The origin
of the name ume-take is not clear.
The taste is not good and is considered
inferior quality.
