Shikin (Purple Corydalis) 《Murasakikeman》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has mild toxicity. It grows here and there under trees and in shaded areas. It germinates in autumn, grows close to the ground in winter, and has leaves that resemble celery leaves but are thin and soft, with purple-colored stems. In spring it extends stems about one shaku (30cm) tall and opens purple flowers at the top in clustered growth. The shape resembles the flowers of Engosku (Corydalis). Afterward it forms pods, and when they ripen, the pods naturally split open when touched and the seeds fly out. After summer, both the shoots and roots wither together.

Kikeman 《A variety of purple corydalis | Chinese name unknown》
It has toxicity. It grows shoots after autumn in tree shade, roadside, or in the shadows between stone walls and hedges, and does not wither in winter. The leaves are like celery leaves and have a whitish color. When cut, yellow sap emerges with a foul odor. In spring it extends stems and opens yellow flowers. They resemble the flowers of Engosku (Corydalis).
