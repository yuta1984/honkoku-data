【Right Page】
Kansui (sweet spurge | Euphorbia kansui) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in mountains and fields in spring. Newly emerged leaves are red in color, resembling Taigeki (Euphorbia pekinensis) but shorter.
　　　At the stem tip five leaves grow, dividing into five stems, with two leaves opposing at the ends, dividing into two stems, with two leaves growing at the top
　　　and opening flowers. Yellow-purple in color, resembling Taigeki flowers and quickly bearing
　　　fruit. Also similar to Taigeki.

【Left Page】
Zokusuishi (caper spurge | Euphorbia lathyris) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has toxicity. The newly sprouted thin leaves grow in alternating opposite pairs. Reaching the upper part, five leaves grow and the stem divides. When
　　　picked, white sap emerges. At the stem end it again divides the stem, producing two leaves and opening flowers.
　　　Purple-yellow in color. Bears fruit after flowering. From the side also two leaves grow, opening flowers and bearing fruit
　　　just as initially. The fruit is round and flat with three ridges, and when ripe
　　　splits into three parts with seeds inside.
