【Right Page】
Euphorbia kansui [Natsu-tōdai]　Compendium of Materia Medica
　It contains poison. It grows in mountains and fields in spring. The newly sprouted leaves are reddish in color, resembling Taka-tōdai but shorter. At the stem tip, five leaves emerge and divide into five stems, at whose ends two leaves grow in pairs dividing into two stems, with two leaves emerging at the top and blooming flowers. They are yellow-purple in color, resembling the flowers of Taka-tōdai, and quickly bear fruit. This is also like Taka-tōdai.

【Left Page】
Euphorbia lathyris [Horuto-sō]　Compendium of Materia Medica
　It contains poison. The newly sprouted thin leaves grow alternately opposite each other. Reaching the upper part, five leaves emerge and the stem divides. When picked, white sap emerges. At the stem tip, the stem also divides producing two leaves and blooming flowers. They are purple-yellow in color, and after flowering bear fruit. From the side, two leaves also emerge, blooming flowers and bearing fruit as in the beginning. The fruit is round and flat with three ridges, and when ripe splits into three parts with seeds inside.
