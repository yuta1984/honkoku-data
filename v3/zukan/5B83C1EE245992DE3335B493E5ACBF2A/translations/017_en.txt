Euphorbia helioscopia [Toudaigusa]　Compendium of Materia Medica
　It has mild toxicity. It grows by the seaside in autumn and develops in spring. The leaves grow alternately and attach densely. They roughly resemble the leaves of purslane, being thin with fine serrated edges. The leaves exude white sap. At the stem tip, five leaves grow oppositely, dividing into five stems and blooming small four-petaled flowers.

Pinellia ternata [Karasu-bishaku]　Compendium of Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. In spring it grows everywhere in gardens. It bears three leaves at the tip of the leaf stem. It roughly resembles the leaves of arrowhead. The flower grows separately, with the stem tip itself being the flower. There are stamens in the center. It is like the flower of Arisaema. If one mistakenly eats this, the throat immediately becomes severely irritated. There are varieties with different leaf shapes, as well as Saizhou-hange (Dai-hange) and Tou-hange, etc. These are all varieties of the same species.
