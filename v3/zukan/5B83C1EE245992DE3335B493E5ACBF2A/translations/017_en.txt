Zequi (marsh spurge | Euphorbia helioscopia) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has mild toxicity. Grows by the seaside in autumn, growing tall by spring. Leaves are alternate and densely
　　　attached. Generally resembles the leaves of purslane but is thin, with fine serrated edges. Its leaves exude white sap.
　　　At the stem top, five leaves grow opposite each other, dividing into five stems and opening four-petaled small flowers.

【Right Page】
Banxia (crow-dipper | Pinellia ternata) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in various places in gardens during spring. Bears three leaves at the end of the leaf stem. Generally resembles
　　　the leaves of arrowhead. Flowers grow separately, with the stem top itself being the flower. There are stamens in the center,
　　　like the flower of Tiannanxing (Arisaema). If mistakenly eaten, the throat immediately becomes acrid and severe.
　　　Sometimes there are those with different leaf shapes, and there are also Qizhou banxia (large banxia) "Banxia Fufang" and toubanxia,
　　　etc., which are varieties of the same species.
