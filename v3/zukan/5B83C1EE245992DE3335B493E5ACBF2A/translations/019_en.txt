Hōsen (garden balsam | Impatiens balsamina) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has mild toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. Both leaves and stems are soft, with nodes on the stem. In summer, it blooms
　　　flowers. There are red, white, variegated, and double-flowered varieties. The shape resembles a bird with wings. After flowering,
　　　it produces fruit. When twisted, it splits lengthwise and curls back. If mistakenly eaten together with vinegar,
　　　it is extremely toxic. There is also something called "horagaisō." It grows in deep mountains. There are varieties with yellow flowers (kitsurifune) and purple flowers (yokomurasaki),
　　　which are of the same species.

【Left Page】
Yakan (blackberry lily | Iris domestica) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has toxicity. Produces leaves in spring. When first growing, resembles the leaves of Iris sanguinea and has a whitish powder coating. In summer, at the stem
　　　tip, it branches and opens flowers. Has six petals with purple spots. Some are yellow, and there are also those without spots,
　　　and those with different leaf shapes.
