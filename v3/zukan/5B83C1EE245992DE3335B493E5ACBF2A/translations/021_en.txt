Rhubarb (Rheum) - Compendium of Materia Medica, Japanese-Chinese Common Names
　It is poisonous. There is no Japanese native variety; Chinese species are introduced and cultivated. Its seeds are difficult to germinate. It should be planted by cutting the roots. Its leaves resemble paulownia leaves, being broad without serrated edges. In summer, it extends its stem to reach five to six shaku in height, with alternately arranged leaves, and opens flowers at the tip forming spikes. It bears fruit after flowering.

Farfugium (Tsuwabuki) - Zhengzi Tong
　It is poisonous. It thrives in shaded, moist ground. Its leaves do not wither through winter and produce young leaves in spring. Like butterbur leaves but thicker, deep green in color with a glossy surface. In autumn, it extends a stem from the center, branching at the stem head and opening yellow flowers. It must not be eaten together with sugar. It is highly poisonous.
