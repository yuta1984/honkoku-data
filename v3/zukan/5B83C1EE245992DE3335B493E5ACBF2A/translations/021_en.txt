Daiō (rhubarb | Chinese rhubarb) "Bencao Gangmu" "Wakan Tsūmei"
　　Has toxicity. There are no native Japanese varieties, so Chinese varieties are introduced and cultivated. The seeds are difficult to germinate. It should be cultivated
　　by cutting and planting the roots. Its leaves resemble paulownia leaves and are broad, without serrated edges. In summer, it sends up stems reaching five to six shaku in height,
　　with alternate leaves, and opens flowers at the tips forming spikes.
　　After flowering, it produces fruit.

【Left Page】
Takugo (Japanese silver leaf | Farfugium japonicum) "Zhengzitong"
　　Has toxicity. Suitable for shady, moist ground. The leaves do not wither even through winter and produce young leaves in spring.
　　Like butterbur leaves but thicker, deep green in color with a glossy surface. In autumn, it sends up stems from the center,
　　branching at the stem tip and opening yellow flowers. It should not be eaten
　　together with sugar. It is highly toxic.
