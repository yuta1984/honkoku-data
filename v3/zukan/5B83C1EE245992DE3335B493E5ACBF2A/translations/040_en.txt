Chiyū-kinren (Earth-Emerging Golden Lotus) 《Zazensō》《Secret Transmission Flower Mirror》
It has toxicity. It grows in valleys and watery marshes of deep mountains. It produces buds from winter and opens flowers in early spring. Its height is about two to three inches. Its shape resembles the flower of Arisaema (wild konjac) but is shorter, with a purple-black color and a white center tinged with red. After flowering, it produces leaves. The shape resembles the leaves of Hosta but is round and thick. It bears fruit that turns red when ripe, like the fruit of Arisaema.

Batōrei (Aristolochia) 《Tsumbokusa》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It is a climbing vine that produces leaves in spring, which have hairs. It opens flowers in summer that are green with a purple tinge. In autumn it bears fruit shaped like bells. Inside there are flat seeds.
