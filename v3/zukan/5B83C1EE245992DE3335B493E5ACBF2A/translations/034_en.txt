Kanshū (Male Fern) 《Hosobakijinoo》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It grows in deep mountains. From a single root it produces several leaves, with each leaf bearing dozens of small leaflets. The length is one to two shaku. It is deep green in color and does not wither even through winter. In summer it separately produces one or two thin brownish stems shaped like leaves. These are actually its flowers. According to one theory, if women take these leaves, they will not become pregnant. If this is true, one must not use it carelessly. Also, what is called "kusasotetsu" (bracken fern) is one variety of this plant.

Sekishaishisha (Red Messenger) 《Kuchinawajaago》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It grows abundantly in shaded areas on the sides of valleys in deep mountains. The stem is slanted and does not stand upright. The upper part of the root is deep reddish in color. The leaves resemble those of the Chinese hackberry and grow alternately. In summer it opens small white flowers between the leaves, blooming in dense clusters. When snakes overeat, if they consume these leaves, the food is immediately digested.
