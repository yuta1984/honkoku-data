Aconitum (Uzu, Ibusu) "Compendium of Materia Medica"
　　　It has great toxicity. There are two types of this: those that grow wild in mountains are called Grass Aconitum (Yamadori-kabuto). The poison is extremely
　　　potent. In spring, it produces shoots from perennial roots. Height is three to four shaku. The stems are weak and do not stand upright. The leaves are
　　　forked and grow alternately. In autumn, flowers bloom at each leaf interval. Deep blue-green color, generally in clusters of
　　　two to three flowers. The shape roughly resembles a court musician's bird-cap hat. There are also dark purple and white varieties, and the white
　　　ones are called hanazuru. Those currently planted in households are called River Aconitum (Kabutosō). The toxicity is
　　　somewhat less than Grass Aconitum. The leaves are thick, deep green with a glossy surface. The stems are strong and straight
