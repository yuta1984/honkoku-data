Monkshood (uzu/torikabuто) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　It has great toxicity, and there are two types. Those that grow wild in mountains are called grass monkshood (yama-torikabuто). The poison is extremely
　　　potent. In spring, shoots grow from perennial roots. Height is three to four shaku. The stems are weak and do not stand upright. The leaves
　　　branch and grow alternately. In autumn, flowers bloom from each leaf interval. Deep blue-green color, usually in bunches of
　　　two or three flowers. The shape roughly resembles the bird helmet of court musicians. There are also dark purple and white varieties. The white
　　　ones are called hanazuru. Those now cultivated in households are called river monkshood (kabuтоsо̄). The poison is
　　　somewhat less potent than grass monkshood. The leaves are thick, deep green with a glossy surface. The stems are strong and straight
