Mōkon (Ranunculus japonicus) - Compendium of Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. It grows from perennial roots in spring. The leaf surface has spots. It opens yellow five-petaled flowers. The white-flowered variety is called "ginpōge." There are also double-flowered varieties. This is one of its types. If eaten by mistake, it causes madness and vomiting of blood.

Suikoshō (Persicaria hydropiper) - Famine Relief Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. It grows at waterside areas in fields and plains. The leaves do not wither even through winter, are pale green in color, and have hairs. In spring, it extends its stem and opens flowers. The flowers are five-petaled and yellow, producing fruits after flowering.
