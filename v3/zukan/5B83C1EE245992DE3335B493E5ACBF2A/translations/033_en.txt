Yaekanzō (Double Daylily) - Compendium of Materia Medica, a variety of daylily
It is poisonous. In spring it produces leaves from perennial roots. In summer it extends stems from the center and opens double-petaled reddish-yellow flowers.

Sakuchō (Sambucus) [Niwatoko] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It is poisonous. It grows in fields. The leaves resemble those of elderberry but are slightly longer and thicker, growing in opposite pairs. It has an odor. It grows three to four shaku tall. In summer it opens white flowers at the stem tips, later forming fruits.
If eaten by mistake, it causes sudden vomiting and violent diarrhea. Some people also gather the young shoots of elderberry to eat as seasoned vegetables, but this is extremely inadvisable. It is poisonous and causes sudden violent diarrhea. One must not eat it carelessly. This is the woody form of sakuchō. It is also called moku-sakuchō (woody sambucus).
