Yaekanzō (Double Day Lily) 《One variety of Kansō (Day Lily)《Compendium of Materia Medica》》
It has toxicity. In spring it produces leaves from perennial roots. When summer arrives, it extends stems from the center and opens double-petaled reddish-yellow flowers.

Sakuchō (Elder) 《Niwatazu》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It grows in fields and wild areas. The leaves resemble those of elderberry but are slightly longer and thicker, growing in opposite pairs. It has an offensive odor. The height is three to four shaku. In summer it opens white flowers at the stem tips, and later produces fruit.
If one mistakenly eats this, they will suddenly vomit and have severe diarrhea. There are also people who pick the young shoots of elderberry《Compendium of Materia Medica》and eat them as seasoned vegetables, but this is extremely inadvisable. It has toxicity and causes sudden severe diarrhea. One must not eat it carelessly. The woody type of sakuchō is called by the alternative name "moku-sakuchō" (woody elder).
