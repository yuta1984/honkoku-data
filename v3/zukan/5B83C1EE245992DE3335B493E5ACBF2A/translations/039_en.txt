Kojō (Japanese Knotweed) 《Itadori》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It grows from perennial roots in spring. Initially it resembles bamboo shoots, with round stems that are hollow inside. The leaves grow alternately, and in autumn it forms spikes between the leaves with small white flowers clustering densely. The fruit resembles rhubarb in shape. If pregnant women consume it, their children will develop body odor.

Ichiben-ren (Single-petal Lotus) 《Mizubashō》《Secret Transmission Flower Mirror》
It has toxicity. It grows beside ponds and marshes in deep mountains. The leaves and stems are short, soft, and light green in color. In the early or late period of late spring, short stems grow from between the leaves and open single-petaled white flowers. They are roughly shaped like the tip of a spoon. There are green stamens in the center. Some also have black spots on the leaf surface, which are a variety of this species.
