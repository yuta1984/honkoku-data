Hanjō (spotted staff | mamushisou/viper grass) "Bencao Gangmu, Konnyaku Shūkai"
　　　Has great toxicity. Grows in spring in shaded areas of deep mountains. Its root is a round bulb, with one stem growing independently. Has purple-black
　　　mottled spots. Has two stems - one large stem and one small stem. Its shape is like Arisaema
　　　and is easily confused with it. If one mistakenly eats this, it is as fearsome as Arisaema.

Mandara-ge (mandala flower | Korean morning glory) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. Resembles eggplant leaves and is light green in color, with soft stems.
　　　Opens flowers in autumn. The shape resembles morning glory flowers
　　　but with a long tube, white color, and white pistils. When the flowers fall,
　　　it bears fruit with thorns. When one flower ends,
　　　another flower opens at the tip.
　　　Each branch behaves in this
　　　manner. If one mistakenly
　　　eats this,
　　　one immediately goes mad. It is to be
　　　feared. However, if the poison is neutralized,
　　　there is no harm.
