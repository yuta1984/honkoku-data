【Right Page】
Kinyō Kōfun (celery-leafed hook-kiss | Giant Water Parsley) "Jinkui Yaolue"
　　　Has great toxicity. Grows in ponds and marshes in spring. Leaves resemble Byakushi (Angelica dahurica). Opens flowers in summer. Resembles carrot
　　　flowers, white in color and very small, clustering at the stem top. The stem interior is hollow, and large specimens
　　　reach three to four shaku (about 90-120cm). If one mistakenly eats this, it leads to death. It is a fearsome
　　　thing.

【Left Page】
Taigeki (great spurge | Euphorbia pekinensis) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has great toxicity. Newly emerged leaves are light red in color, shaped roughly like Hypericum leaves and grow alternately. When
　　　picked, white sap emerges. At the stem tip it divides into five stems, producing three leaves, and also at the top two or three stems develop
　　　and produce two leaves. Opens flowers in summer. Four-petaled, light purple in color, tinged with yellow. Bears fruit after flowering
　　　.
