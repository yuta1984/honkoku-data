Gyokusan (Hosta) 《Taugiboushi》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It is cultivated in various places for cut flowers. In spring it produces leaves with vertical lines. In summer it extends stems and opens white flowers. It has six petals and long stamens. It withers without fully opening. After flowering, it forms pods containing small black seeds inside. Shigaku (Purple Calyx) 《Giboushi》《Jonan Garden History》 and Shoushi (Small Purple) 《Sajigibow》《Collection of Fragrant Plants》, called kudamatama no kanzashi and others, are varieties of this species.

Yorou (Henbane) 《Omekikusa》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has great toxicity. It grows in secluded valleys of deep mountains. In spring it produces seedlings from perennial roots, which are purple-black in color and turn light green as they grow. The height is one to two shaku. The leaves resemble those of Shouriku (pokeweed) but are narrow and small. It opens tubular flowers between the leaves. The tips have five petals and resemble Shashin (bellflower). After flowering, it bears fruit. The roots are like those of Sanhikai (wild yam). If eaten by mistake, it causes madness and running about wildly. Hence it has the name "hashiridokoro" (running place).
