Shanglu (pokeweed | Phytolacca acinosa) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has toxicity. Grows in shady, moist ground. Leaves resemble young tobacco leaves, pointed, with no
　　　hair. In summer, at the stem tips, opposite to the leaf stems, white flowers bloom and form spikes. Should not be
　　　eaten together with water dropwort. Also, those that bloom purple flowers are called Sekishō "Same Book Collected Explanations." The poison is extremely
　　　deep. Must never be eaten.　　　　There are also those with yellow flowers, which are varieties of the same species.

【Left Page】
Dairyō (large knotweed | Clematis terniflora) "Jiuhuang Bencao"
　　　Has toxicity. It is a vine that produces leaves in spring. At each node, two stems grow in pairs, with one stem having
　　　two leaves at the base and three leaves at the tip, using these leaf stems to wrap around trees and bamboo. In autumn, it opens white flowers that cluster together. If mistakenly
　　　chewed, it damages the teeth.
