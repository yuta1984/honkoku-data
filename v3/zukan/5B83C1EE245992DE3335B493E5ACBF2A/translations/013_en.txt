Mansei Kōfun (vine-growing hook-kiss | poison ivy) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has great toxicity. Common in mountains. Vine-like with aerial roots, creeping over trees and rocks. Leaf stems are red
　　　in color, with three leaves per stem. Turns red in autumn then falls. In summer, opens small yellow flowers between leaves
　　　and bears fruit. The skin has fine hairs.
　　　Roughly resembles the fruit of Eleutherococcus. If one mistakenly eats this,
　　　the tongue and lips putrefy, leading to death.
　　　It is a fearsome thing.

【Left Page】
Sōhon Ōseiyō Kōfun (herbaceous Polygonatum-leafed hook-kiss | swamp nightshade) "Bencao Gangmu Kōfun Seigo"
　　　Has great toxicity. Grows in deep mountains. In spring, produces shoots from perennial roots. Leaves resemble Polygonatum (Solomon's seal) and are soft
　　　and pliable, with three to four leaves growing alternately. Has vertical veins and is purple in color. Stems are light red. Four-petaled flowers hang down between leaves, light
　　　green in color with black centers. Bears fruit after flowering. If one mistakenly eats this, the tongue immediately splits
　　　and lips putrefy, leading to death. If these leaves are picked and mixed into rice and fed to mice, they die instantly. It is a fearsome
　　　thing. The woody Polygonatum-leafed hook-kiss is mentioned in the tree section of the later volume.
