Kinugasasou (Silk Umbrella Plant) 《Zaokyu (Compendium of Materia Medica) | Chinese name unknown》 One variety
It has toxicity. It is a mountain product. In spring, it produces one stem from one root. Height is about one shaku. At the top of the stem, it produces eight or nine leaves in an umbrella-like shape. In summer, it produces a stem from the center and opens one flower. It has ten petals of light purple color with deep purple patterns. It hangs yellow stamens. The details of the Japanese variety of zaokyu are not yet clear.

Hakurakukai (Bocconia) 《Takenikusa》《Compendium of Materia Medica, Castor Bean Appendix》
It has great toxicity. It grows in the mountains. In spring, it produces shoots from old roots, with round stems that have no joints and are hollow inside. Height reaches five to six shaku. The leaves resemble the shape of chrysanthemum leaves but without points, and their size approaches one shaku. Both stems and leaves are white-green in color, and when cut, they emit red-yellow juice. In summer, it opens small white flowers at the top of the stem, forming spikes. Later it produces pods that are flat and thin with pointed ends on both sides, containing small black seeds inside.
This plant is used for boiling bamboo.
