Enbi (Iris) 《Ichhatsu》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It is cultivated here and there in gardens. The leaves do not wither even through winter and produce new leaves in spring. They resemble the young leaves of shakan (blackberry lily), are soft, and do not stand upright. From the center it extends a stem and opens six-petaled purple flowers. There are also white-flowered varieties, which are one type of this plant.

Gyūhen (Cow Tablet) 《Reijinsō》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It grows beside valleys in mountainous areas. The leaves resemble those of sōuzu (mountain aconite) and have serrated edges. In summer it extends stems with alternately arranged leaves, and in autumn opens flowers at the stem tips. They are pale purple in color, and bear fruit after flowering.
