Yukimochisou (snow rice cake plant) "A variety of Arisaema | Chinese name unknown"
　　　Has great toxicity. Grows in spring in shaded valleys of deep mountains. The independently growing stem has no spots. The leaves are three
　　　leaflets on one stem and are light green. The flower grows at the stem top, is white with a round pistil in the center. Its shape roughly resembles a Buddha's
　　　halo and covers the pistil. Its toxicity is the same as Arisaema and is to be feared.

Koshou (tiger palm | Urashimasou) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　　Has great toxicity. Grows in spring in shaded areas of deep mountains. Similar to Arisaema, its root is a round bulb. The leaves first
　　　emerge like those of Arisaema but are long, with white spots on the stem. From within the flower emerges a long pistil
　　　that reaches the ground. If one mistakenly eats this, it is as fearsome as Arisaema.
