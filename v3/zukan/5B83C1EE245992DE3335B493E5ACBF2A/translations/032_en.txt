Kiren (Encelia) [Menamomi] - Compendium of Materia Medica
It has minor toxicity. It grows wild in various places from perennial roots. The branches and leaves are opposite with serrated edges, light green in color with hair. In autumn it divides its branches and opens small yellow flowers. Its calyx is divided into five parts and is sticky.

It has minor toxicity. It also grows on dry land. In spring it extends its stems. The leaves are alternate and have an odor. When the hangesho season arrives, the three leaves at the stem tip turn white. At its top it opens small white flowers forming spikes.
