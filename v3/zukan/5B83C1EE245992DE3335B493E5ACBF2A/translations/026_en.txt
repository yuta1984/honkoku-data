Cannabis (Taima | Asa) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　Cannabis is a general term. There are distinctions between male hemp (oasa) and female hemp (measa). Their characteristics will be mentioned later. Seeds are sown in spring. Height reaches
　　six to seven shaku (approximately 180-210cm), and the leaves are toxic. The outer bark of seedlings is peeled to make hemp fiber.
　　Hemp stalks are white and light, hollow inside. They are burned to ash and
　　added to gunpowder. Hemp seeds are used for food. They are toxic. Those that enter the soil
　　have strong toxicity and can kill
　　people. In autumn, both seedlings and roots
　　wither.

Castor Bean (Hima | Tougoma) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　Has mild toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. The stem is bamboo-like with a hollow interior, reaching eight to nine shaku (approximately 240-270cm), and is powder-green in color.
　　　Leaves are alternate, six-lobed with serrated edges, and bear white powder. Flowers are produced
　　　　between the leaves at the stem tips. They are yellow and dense. Bears fruit with many spines. Seeds have black spots.
　　　　　These seeds are pressed to make oil for ink paste. It is not
　　　　　　a high-quality product.
