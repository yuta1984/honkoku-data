Taima (Hemp) - Compendium of Materia Medica
　Taima is a general term. There are distinctions between male hemp (o-asa) and female hemp (me-asa). Seeds are sown in spring. It reaches a height of six to seven shaku, and the leaves are poisonous. The outer bark of the seedlings is peeled off to make fiber.
　The hemp stalks (asa-gara) are white and light, hollow inside. They are burned to ash and added to gunpowder. Hemp seeds (asa-no-mi) are used for food but are poisonous. Those that enter the soil are highly toxic and can kill people. In autumn, both the plants and roots wither.

Hima (Castor Bean) - Compendium of Materia Medica
　It has mild toxicity. Seeds are sown in spring. The stem is like bamboo, hollow inside, reaching eight to nine shaku in height, and is powder-green in color.
　The leaves grow alternately, are six-lobed with serrated edges, and have a whitish powder coating. Flowers grow between the leaves at the stem tips. They are yellow and fine. It produces fruits with many spines. The seeds have black spots.
　These seeds are pressed to make oil for printing ink. This is not a high-quality manufacturing method.
