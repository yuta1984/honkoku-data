Wild Taro (Yau | Ishiimo) "Bencao Gangmu Taro Appendix"
　　Has strong toxicity. A product of warm countries and coastal areas, fears cold. Produces leaves in summer. Similar to white taro but
　　has luster. Stem is shorter than white taro. Root also resembles white taro but is larger. If mistakenly
　　　　　consumed, causes distress and agitation, and in severe cases leads to death. A fearsome plant.
　　　　　　Hence it has the names Dokuimo (poison taro) and Kuwazuimo (inedible taro).

【Left Page】
Mountain Sagittaria (Sanjiko | Mugikuwai) "Bencao Gangmu"
　　Has mild toxicity. Grows in mountains and fields. Leaves resemble those of Sandaigasa (umbrella plant). When young,
　　one root produces one leaf; when the root ages, it produces two leaves. Extends a stem from the center with small
　　leaves growing in opposite pairs at the end, opening flowers at the top. Six-petaled and white, with purple stripes on the outside. After flowering, produces fruit.
