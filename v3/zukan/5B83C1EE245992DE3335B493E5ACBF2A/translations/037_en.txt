Baryō (Horse Knotweed) 《Inutade》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. In spring it grows beside fields and ditches. There are many varieties. It lacks pungent flavor and is not suitable for food consumption. The one described here reaches a height of three to four shaku (about 90-120cm), has enlarged leaves with black spots on the leaf surface, opens small fine white flowers forming spikes in autumn, produces fine seeds after flowering, and the shoots wither in winter.

Bōki (Coastal Glehnia) 《Botanbaōfū》《Compendium of Materia Medica》
It has toxicity. It grows on seashores in warm regions. The leaves resemble peony leaves with smooth texture, and the leaf surface has a whitish powder coating. In summer it reaches a height of three to four shaku (about 90-120cm), opening white flowers at each branch tip in dense clusters. It resembles the flowers of dong quai (Angelica sinensis) or chuanxiong (Ligusticum chuanxiong). It produces fruit after flowering.
