Kyūi (Chloranthus serratus) - Compendium of Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. It grows in shaded areas of mountains. It produces seedlings in spring. It grows to about one shaku in height. The stem has joints. At the stem tip, two leaves face each other, with four leaves arranged alternately, and two stems grow from the top, opening white flowers. Some have white flowers like threads, called "mayuhakesō." This is one of its varieties.

Shibi (Campsis grandiflora) - Compendium of Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. It is a vine with aerial roots that grows by leaning on trees. It produces leaves in spring with deep serrations. It opens flowers in summer. The flowers form tubes with five-lobed petals that are round and pointed at the tips, reddish-yellow in color. After flowering, it produces pods. If the dew from this flower enters the eyes, it immediately causes illness. Pregnant women should also avoid the fragrance of this flower.
