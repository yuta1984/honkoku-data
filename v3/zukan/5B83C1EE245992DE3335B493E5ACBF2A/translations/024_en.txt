Sekiryūzei (Ranunculus sceleratus) - Compendium of Materia Medica
　It is poisonous. It grows in rice paddies from autumn through winter. The leaves resemble those of mōkon (buttercup) but are hairless and glossy. They are yellow-green in color. In spring, it extends its stem to a height of one to two shaku, branches out, and opens five-petaled yellow flowers. The flowers resemble those of mōkon but are smaller and glossy. It produces fruits afterward.

Shikisai (Houttuynia cordata)
　It has mild toxicity. It grows everywhere. It has a foul odor. Its leaves are initially purple in color, and the stem is reddish-purple. Alternatively, there are varieties where the leaf surface is green and the leaf underside is purple. In summer, it opens four-petaled white flowers. The stem has nodes.
