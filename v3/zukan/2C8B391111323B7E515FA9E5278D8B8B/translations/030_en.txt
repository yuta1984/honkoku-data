Honzō Zufu Volume 41
　　　　　　　Eastern Capital　Written by Iwasaki Tsunemasa

　　Grain Section

Shoku (稷)　　kibi　urukibi (non-glutinous millet)

　There are two types: glutinous and non-glutinous. Shizhen says "The sticky type is called sho (黍), and the non-sticky type is called shoku (稷)." The Honchō Shokukan states that small millet (kokibi) does not taste good.

Sho (黍)　　mochikibi (glutinous millet)
　The shape resembles shoku, the ears have a whitish tinge, there are no long branches on the ears, and the seeds cluster and form at the tips.
