Year Hinoe-inu, end of rainy season
Written on the 23rd day

In "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Encyclopedia of China and Japan), vine plants section, and
"Sōmoku Shifu" (Poetry Collection of Plants and Trees) it states:
　　Setsukakō (Snow-under-red)

Herbalists say:
　　Hakuei (White nightshade)
　　　I say:
　　　　The confusion with the same name
　　　　is as explained in the
　　　　Hakuei section of the previous volume

Kan (author's name) says:
　The common people all call Setsukakō
　Kamojōgo (Hiyodorijōgo - Solanum lyratum). This is a name for Hakuei,
　so perhaps two species share one name. However, in "Tashikihen" 
　and "Wamyōshō," Hakuei
　has no phonetic reading for "kamo" (duck), and various texts have not yet
　　　　corrected the grass character. Setsukakō is also called
　Kamojōgo.
