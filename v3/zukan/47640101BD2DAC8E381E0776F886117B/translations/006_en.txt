Written on the 23rd day of the end of the rainy season, year Hinoe-inu

In the "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Encyclopedia of Chinese and Japanese Things), vine grass category, and "Sōmoku Shifu" (Poetry Collection of Plants and Trees):
　　Sekkakou (Snow-under Red)

Herbalists say:
　　Hakuei (White Nightshade)

I say:
　　About the confusion with the same name,
　　explained in the section on Hakuei
　　in the previous volume

Kan says:
　The common people all call Sekkakou
　Kamojōgo (Hiyodorijōgo - Solanum lyratum). This is a name for Hakuei, and
　perhaps two varieties share one name. However, in the
　Tashikihen and Wamyōshō, Haku-
　ei lacks the reading "kamo," and in various texts
　　　　the plants are not properly classified. Sekkakou is also
　called Kamojōgo.
