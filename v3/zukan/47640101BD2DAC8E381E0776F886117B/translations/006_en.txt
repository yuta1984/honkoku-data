Written/illustrated on the twenty-third day of the late rainy season (end of the plum rains) in the year Hinoe-inu (Bingxu).

From the "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Japanese-Chinese Encyclopedia), section on climbing plants, and from the "Sōmoku Shifu" (Poetic Compendium of Plants and Trees):
　　**Setsukakō (雪下紅 / "Red Beneath the Snow")**

Herbalists (Honzō scholars) refer to this plant as:
　　Hakuei (白英 / *Solanum lyratum*)

The author states:
　　The confusion arising from sharing the same name is explained in the entry on "Hakuei" in the previous volume.

"Kan" (the author's pseudonym or a cited commentator) states:
　In popular Japanese usage, Setsukakō is universally called "Hiyotori-jōgo" (鴨上戸). This name is shared with Hakuei — could it be that two different species share a single name? However, in both the "Tashikiben" (多識篇) and the "Wamyōshō" (和名抄), the reading of "kamo/hiyodori" does not appear under the entry for Hakuei, and the matter has not yet been settled against the various herbal texts. Setsukakō is also referred to as "Hiyotori-jōgo."
