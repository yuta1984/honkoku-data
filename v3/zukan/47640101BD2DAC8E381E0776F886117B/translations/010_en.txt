Baien states:
　What the common people call "Tachihana" is this plant. Tachibana is
　the general name for citrus fruits and is different from this. Since citrus fruits are
　called "Tachihana," this is called "Karatachibana."
　In Tsukushi dialect it is called "yabukanshi," which is very
　easily confused. Yabukanshi is a lowland tree that
　resembles Karatachibana but has different leaves; the flowers and fruits are most similar.

The Zoho Chikin-sho states that kara-tachibana is a different plant. It refers to the hereditary tachibana
of the citrus family. Though Kara-tachibanoka is commonly called tachibana,
it does not use the character for tachibana.

Gokei grows abundantly everywhere in mountains and fields. It grows in the
plains of Sugamo and Tokumaru in Musashi Province. The leaves, stems, and flowers are shade-
dried and powdered, then taken with hot water. It effectively treats diarrhea and is
especially effective for dysentery. It can also be made into decoctions or ground fine and formed into pills.
All are effective. Hence it is called "Gen-no-shōko" (reliable remedy). Though listed in materia medica among poisonous
plants, it is not toxic. For diarrhea, this single ingredient
may be taken alone. Limit the frequency. The Yamato Honzo also
records its efficacy.

The Jiuhuang Bencao states: Qianniuer (Gen-no-shōko), Douniuer 《Alternative names: Rengesō, Hōtsukehana, Mikoshigusa》
　　Biantu (Fūro) 《Alternative names: Haikuwasō, Umegaesō》

The Wakan Sansai Zue and various books state:
　Gokei, Qianniuer, and Fūrō are treated as three different things, all recorded confusingly.
　These three are one species. The true Biantu is a separate species altogether.
