The Yamato Honzo states:
　Spicebush (Karatachibana)
　　　　　Dialect of Musashi Province: Tachibana
　　　　　○Kyoto dialect: Karatachibana
　　　　　○Chikushi dialect: Yabukarashi

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Sixth day of early Rinsho, Year of Hinoe-inu
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Copied

　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Listed in Yamato Honzo [Garden Trees]

Tamaki states:
　What the common people call "tachibana" is this plant. The tachibana (orange tree) is
　a general term for citrus species and is different from this. Since citrus is called
　tachibana, this is called spicebush (karatachibana).
　In the Chikushi dialect it is called yabukooji, which is very
　easily confused. Yabukooji is a lowland tree, and while similar to
　spicebush, the leaves differ, though the flowers and fruit are most
　similar.

The Zoho Chikin-sho states that Chinese citrus is a different plant, referring to the daidai orange.
It is a type of citrus. Though spicebush is commonly called tachibana,
it does not have the character for "orange."

Wild geranium grows abundantly in various mountains and fields. It grows in
the plains of Sugamo and Tokumaru in Musashi Province. The leaves, stems, and flowers are shade-
dried and powdered, then taken with hot water. It cures diarrhea and is
most effective for dysentery. It can also be made into decoctions or ground into fine powder for pills.
All are effective. Therefore it is called Gen-no-seuko. Though listed in materia medica as a toxic
plant, it is non-toxic. For diarrhea, this wild geranium alone
should be taken. The frequency should be limited. This is also recorded in Yamato Honzo for its
efficacy.
