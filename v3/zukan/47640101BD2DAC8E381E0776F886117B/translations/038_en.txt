The Tashikihen, in its section on hemp, barley, and rice plants, states:
Tōzu (sword bean, Japanese name: nata mame)
Alternative name: Kyōkenzu (narrow sword bean)

A "tō" (sword/blade) refers to what those who cut firewood call a "nata" (hatchet).
This bean is so named because its shape resembles it.

The Kyūkō Honzō states:
Tōzu byō (sword bean sprouts)

Teii year, intercalary 3rd month, 29th day
Sketched from my vegetable garden

【Seal】Herbaceous plants
