I (Mōri Baien) say:
　The Kyūkō's classification of seikō as inchin is incorrect.
　Inchin and seikō are different species. Inchin has leaves that are
　bluish-white like inchin and has no fragrance. Seikō has green leaves that are
　thick and luxuriant with a slight fragrance. The Wakan Sansai Zue states that seikō
　has no branching, but this is not detailed. Seikō collected on the 6th day of the 6th month
　can be prepared into divine medicine as seen in the Honzō
