Winter Mallow Seeds

Early intercalary Rinshō (6th month) of the Year of the Fire Boar
On the 12th day, drawn from life
in the Sōsha garden

Common people say there are two types of winter mallow seeds. One type has wrinkled surfaces on the leaves,
and the leaves are not flat. This is called okanori (land laver). It is also called winter mallow seed.
The genuine variety has flat leaves without wrinkled surfaces and has a glossy appearance. Its seeds are
used in medicine. There is also another type called winter mallow (kan-aoi). It belongs to the family of mokō and futaba-aoi.
Winter mallow seeds grow abundantly along the Kanagawa highway in Musashi Province near Ōjōji temple.
The okanori grass is not found there.
