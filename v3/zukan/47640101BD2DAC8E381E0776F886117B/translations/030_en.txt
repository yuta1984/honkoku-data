【Seal】Grass category

The geographical record states:
　Ryojo, Daigeki, Takushitsu, and Kansui all have the same appearance but
　have differences. Daigeki blooms yellow-purple flowers in spring. Kansui and Takushitsu
　are all similar, but Daigeki has no branching stems, while Kansui has branching stems.
　Takushitsu is also similar; Takushitsu has hair on its leaves, while Kansui is smooth.
　The grass is also not thick. Ryojo resembles Takushitsu, about three to four feet tall, with slightly
　hairy stems. Each has the same appearance yet differs, making it difficult to distinguish these
　four varieties. Some call this Daigeki, but that is incorrect. Daigeki is a
　category. They should be compared and examined together. Ryojo has two types: spring Ryojo and
　autumn Ryojo.
