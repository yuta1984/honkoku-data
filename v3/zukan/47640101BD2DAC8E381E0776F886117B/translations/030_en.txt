【Seal】 Grass category

Kan (the author) states:
　Ryojo, Taigeki (Euphorbia pekinensis), Takushitsu (Euphorbia helioscopia), and Kanzui (Euphorbia kansui) all share similar forms, yet
　there are differences. Taigeki opens yellow-purple flowers in spring. Kanzui and Takushitsu
　are similar in this regard, but Taigeki has no branching forks, while Kanzui has branching forks.
　Takushitsu is also similar; Takushitsu has hairy leaves, while Kanzui is smooth.
　The plant itself is not particularly robust. Ryojo resembles Takushitsu, grows three to four shaku tall, and has
　slightly hairy stems. Though each shares similar forms, they are distinct, and the ones most
　difficult to tell apart are likely these four species. Some call it Taigeki, but that is incorrect. Taigeki is a broader
　category. One should compare them carefully. Ryojo has two varieties: Spring Ryojo and
　Autumn Ryojo.
