Fifteenth day before the three hottest periods of Rinsho (6th lunar month), Hinoe-inu year

The Tashikihen, in the mountain plants section, states:
　　　Mokko (Saussurea costus)　Japanese name: saumokusa (grass-tree-grass)
　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　waremokau (we-hair-fragrance)
　　　　　　Alternative names: Mikko (Betsuroku), Aomokko (Kokei)
　　　　　　　Gomokko (Zukei), Nanmokko (Komoku)

　　　　　　　　　　　　○The sutra states it as kuha-bukko　(Wakan Sansai Zue)


　　　　　　　　　The Honzo Komoku records only the root, but the leaf forms
　　　　　　　　　recorded in the Honzo-zu, Sansai Zue, Somboku Gafu, etc.
　　　　　　　　　differ, and because there are many joints, it is difficult to distinguish clearly. What I have illustrated at Kansho is this
