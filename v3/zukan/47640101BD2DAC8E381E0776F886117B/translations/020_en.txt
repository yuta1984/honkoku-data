The "Jikkinshō" states:
　　Ōyama renka (Great Mountain Lotus Flower)

Various books state:
　　Gyokurenka (Jade Lotus Flower), Japanese name: Ōyama renge

Appearing in "Chaseki Sōkashū" (Tea Ceremony Flower Arrangement Collection):
　　Ōyama renka, Tennyo-ka (Celestial Maiden Flower)



　　　　　　　　It is incorrect to consider gyokurenka as ōyama renka. Gyokurenka is
　　　　　　　　mokuren (magnolia). The "Jikkinshō" treats gyokurenka as mokuranka
　　　　　　　　and lists ōyama renka separately. Ōyama renka is a type of magnolia,
　　　　　　　　a separate species that grows in Ōmine in Yamato Province, hence
　　　　　　　　receiving the name "Great Mountain." Since the flowers bloom in summer,
　　　　　　　　it is clearly a different species from magnolia. Most varieties of kobushi
　　　　　　　　(magnolia kobus) bloom in spring. There is one variety whose flower stamens
　　　　　　　　are purple in color, which is planted in the garden.
