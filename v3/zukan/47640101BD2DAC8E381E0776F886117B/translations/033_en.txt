Herbalists say:
　Baimu (Fritillaria) 《Those with buckwheat-like leaves are called Ubayuri》
"Yamato Honzō" in the section on miscellaneous plants says:
　Gobō-yuri (Shika-kakure-yuri) 《Dialect of country folk in western lands》
"Sado Kokushi" says:
　Yamakabu (Mountain turnip)

"Bukō Sanbutsu-shi" says:
　Soba-ba Baimu (Ubayuri)

【Seal】Miscellaneous plants

5th day of the first period of the three hot spells, intercalary Rinshō month, Year of the Fire Boar
Copied from actual plants grown in the medicinal garden

"Sado Kokushi" says: "The leaves of yamakabu resemble those of burdock or taro, and the roots resemble lilies. In the 6th and 7th months it blooms with pale reddish-white flowers, and around the 8th month it forms seeds. The shape resembles iris flowers," and so forth. What is called "pale red flowers" does not quite match the description in "Yamato Honzō." This is the gobō-yuri. In "Chikinkō" it says "after flowering there are no leaves," but the ubayuri I have drawn now has leaves when flowering. Could there be another type of ubayuri?
