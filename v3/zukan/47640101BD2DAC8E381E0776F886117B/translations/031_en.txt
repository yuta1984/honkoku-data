As told by the plant shop:
　　　Hitsujiran (Unohana/Goat Lily)　　also called　Hitsujisō

This flower opens at the Hour of the Goat (未, approximately 2 p.m.) and wilts at night, which is why it received the name "Hitsuji" (Goat). Its roots resemble those of an orchid (ran), hence the name Hitsujiran. Both of these are common names. There is also a plant called Nikkō Kisuge (Nikkō Yellow Daylily), which is what the herbals refer to as Setsutei-ka (Festival Garden Flower). It is broadly similar but is a distinct species.
