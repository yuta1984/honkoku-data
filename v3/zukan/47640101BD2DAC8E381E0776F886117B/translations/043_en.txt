【Indian Pipe, Aeginetia】
The Wakan Sansai Zue (Japanese-Chinese Encyclopedia) states in its section on mountain plants:
　Grass Cistanche (sōjuyō)　《Alternative names: Orobanche (tsuchi-akebi), Kuraitō | Flower Cistanche》
　　◦《Alternative names: Ghost plant | | Tsuyuzaemon》

I say:
　There is separately what is called Chikubasō, which is what is called grass cistanche.
　There is also what is called Nanban-kiseru, which is a type of grass cistanche.
　According to the Bencao Gangmu (Compendium of Materia Medica), grass cistanche sends up stems in late spring, the stems are round and white in color, and its roots resemble those of flesh cistanche. The white color refers to what has been depicted here. Flesh cistanche belongs to the Cynomorium family.

The Bencao Gangmu states:

　Small Qin Wang plant

The Chikinsho states:
　Turtle shell plant (kikkōsō) 《Alternative names: ◦Ichiyakusō　◦Suzufurirannoaoi》

Because its leaves resemble the pattern of a turtle's shell,
it was probably named thus. In the Bukō Sanbutsu-shi (Record of Edo Products),
deer hoof plant is considered to be turtle shell plant, which corresponds to
the deer hoof plant in the Bencao Gangmu according to this theory.
