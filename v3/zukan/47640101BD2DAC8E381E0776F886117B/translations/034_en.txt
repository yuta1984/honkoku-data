【ICHIBI (Velvet Leaf)】
"Wakan Sansai Zue" in the section on wetland plants says:
　Jingma (Ichihi) 　Jing《sound ■》 Yi《same》
　　　　　Qing《same》 White hemp

Commonly using the characters for "Ichio"
Called "Ichio"

"Yamato Honzō" says:
　Jingma (Kosaiha) 《Latin: Althaea Theophrastis floris luteo》

"Jiuhuang Bencao" says:
　Qingzi (Ichihi) 《Commonly called Qing-manjū》
　The description of jingma in "Benkō" matches in detail with "Wakan Sansai Zue"
　Much cultivated in the western provinces of Hyūga and Satsuma. It is a product of Kyūshū. Those
　whose leaves resemble sesame must be a different variety. "Benkō" says "resembling paulownia
　leaves, blooming yellow flowers in the 6th and 7th months." The bark is spun into cloth. Also
　when the stem is soaked in sulfur to make lamp wicks, it catches fire very quickly.
　"Yamato Honzō" lists ichihi and jingma separately, and what is called ichi
　kore is the commercial hemp of Kyūshū. There are two types: jingma. This one is
　much used.
