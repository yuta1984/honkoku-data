【Ichibi (莔麻, *Abutilon theophrasti*, Chinese Jute/Velvetleaf)】

From the "Wakan Sansai Zue" (Illustrated Encyclopedia of Japan and China), section on Hygrophilous Plants:
　Ichibi (莔麻)　莔 〈pronunciation: ■〉　䔛 〈same〉
　　　　　檾 〈same〉　White Hemp (白麻)

Commonly written with the characters "市尾," and called "Ichibi."

From the "Yamato Honzō" (Great Japanese Herbal, end section):
　Ichibi (莔麻) (Kosaiha [scholarly/Latin name])　◦ "Alter Theophrastei Floreri Liutio" [Latin]

From the "Kyūkō Honzō" (Famine Relief Herbal):
　Keishi (檾子, Ichibi)　〔Commonly called "Morokoshi-manjū" (檾饅頭)〕

---

　The "Honzō Kōmoku" (Compendium of Materia Medica) states that the description of the form of Ichibi corresponds in detail with that given in the "Wakan Sansai Zue."
　It is widely cultivated in Hyūga and Satsuma in the western provinces, and is a local product of Kyushu. Some say that its
　leaves resemble sesame, but those would likely be a separate species. The "Honzō Kōmoku" states that the leaves resemble those of the paulownia (kiri),
　and that it blooms with yellow flowers in the 6th and 7th months. Its bark/fibers can be woven into cloth, and furthermore,
　its stems can be soaked in sulfur to make fire-starters (saidō/kindling torches), which catch fire very quickly.
　In the "Yamato Honzō," "Ichibi" and "Ichibi (莔麻)" are listed separately; what is called
　"Ichibi (伊知此)" refers to two varieties found in Kyushu — the commercial hemp (shōma) and the Ichibi (莔麻). The latter
　is the one more commonly used.
