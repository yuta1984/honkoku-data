Baien says:
　According to herbalism, lilies with white flowers are medicinal; yama-yuri are maki-tan (tiger lilies) which are not medicinal but used for cooking
　They are merely eaten as vegetables. Lily roots are white-yellow in color, all have bitter taste and are barely edible
　Lilies produce bulblets below the root, and after the flowers fall, long fruits develop. Tiger lilies do not
　produce bulblets below the root but form purple-black fruits between the leaves. When these are planted, they grow well
　In three years they become food. In ancient times, there were not many types of lilies. In recent years, since people admire flowers
　The varieties are extremely numerous. I don't know how many tens of varieties there are, but they reach over a hundred. Among their flowers, some face
　upward, some face the ground, some face sideways. Those facing heaven are of the Tatsuta-tōri lily type, those facing
　the ground are Korean kasa lilies, those facing sideways are of the Hakata lily type
