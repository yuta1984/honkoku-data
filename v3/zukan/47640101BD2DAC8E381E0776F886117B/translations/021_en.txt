The "Chikinsho" states:
　　Hamabirukaho (濱葍子苗, Beach Morning Glory)


　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　Drawn from life with faithful intent, 11th day of the 5th month, Year of the Boar (Hinoto-I)


　　　　In the "Yamato Honzō," it is written that the Koshibana (皷子花)
　　　　has two varieties. One variety has round leaves
　　　　and short vines — this is that variety.
　　　　It is not listed separately in the "Honzō" (Chinese Materia Medica).
　　　　The other variety, which has double-layered blossoms (thousand-petaled),
　　　　is called "Tenshi Botan" (纏枝牡丹, Twining-branch Peony).
　　　　This variety I have not yet seen.
