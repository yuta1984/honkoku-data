"Kyūkō Honzō" says: Winter mallow (Kan-aoi)
"Dōya-fu" says: Mallow (Fuyu-aoi)

"Tashiki-hen" says:
　Mallow《Japanese name: Aohi. Ancient name: Aohi》
　　《Alternative name》Dew mallow
　　　Smooth vegetable (Kwaisai)

　Upon examination, mallow sown in the first month is called spring
　mallow, that sown in the sixth or seventh month is called autumn
　mallow, and that sown in the eighth or ninth month is called winter mallow.
　Shizhen's explanation is unclear, but autumn mallow is namely yellow hibiscus
　(Sarukoma・Tororo-aoi). Winter mallow is exactly this. Spring mallow has not yet been
　determined. Each is a separate species and is not sown in all four seasons.
　According to herbal texts, this involves sowing mallow in autumn, covering and nurturing it,
　passing through winter and reaching spring to bear seeds. Hence it is called winter mallow seed.
　This survives winter, likely in warm countries. In "Nōgyō
　Zensho," winter mallow seed is called small mallow. That which has
　serrated leaves and flowers like plum blossoms that are reddish-purple
　is coin mallow. True winter mallow is a different plant.

Intercalary Rinshō (6th month) of the Year of the Fire Boar
Written on the 28th day
【Seal】Grass category

In common households it is called:
　Grass hibiscus
　　Also known as: Land laver (Okanori・Norikusa)

My observation:
　The leaves are dried and thoroughly roasted,
　mixed with soy sauce and eaten. The leaves
　resemble the aroma of Asakusa laver. Hence
　it got this name. Since I ate it, it has never
　disappeared from the garden. Also, the leaves of hollyhock
　are roasted and eaten. Their aroma is
　quite similar.
