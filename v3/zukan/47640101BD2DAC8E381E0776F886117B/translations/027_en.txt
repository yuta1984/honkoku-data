From "Sansai Zue" and
Gao Lian's "Flower Catalog": They record
the "Tenshi botan" (twining peony), and there is a theory that
this is likely the tessen flower.
In "Bencao" xuanhua (bindweed), Shizhen says that the thousand-petaled variety
is commonly called "Tenshi botan" (silk-twining peony). This is
the thousand-petaled variety of the drumstick flower. The "Flower Catalog" lists xuanhua and tenshi
botan separately, distinguishing between single-petaled and thousand-petaled varieties.
There is also something called "windmill flower," which is a different species
but of the same family as tessen, being another variety.
"Bencao" under mountain plants mentions "tessen grass"
but its form is not clear. Tenshi botan was
not known in our country and did not exist, but recently flower
gardeners have used the thousand-petaled tessen flower
to create tenshi botan. Both tenshi botan and the thousand-petaled tessen
are commonly called "yuki-okoshi" (snow-raiser).
