Meeting and kicking - this should be a rare technique of swift footwork. During the reign of Emperor Kameyama, there was a ceremonial kemari game where the lay priest and Grand Counselor Tameyo performed a chrysanthemum ball technique by doubling his body and returning. He kicked once in the garden with the tip of his court cap, which reached the Emperor's admiration. There was an imperial decree asking "Does Tameyo kick without effort?" This was said to be unforgettable. Truly, it was reasonable that both high and low nobles, and people of all ranks were astonished.

1. On the Extended Foot Technique
When a ball is kicked far away and one chases to kick it but still cannot reach it, one should extend the right foot, kneel with the left knee, without fully sitting on the ground, hold the right hand under the sleeve and slightly touch the ground, extend the left foot forward and rise up. No matter how this ball cuts away or rises high, one should kick it low and straight, immediately rise again, and with one more standing step, kick it into the hanging area - this is excellent. The Oral Transmission Collection states that stepping the left foot once closer and extending with the right foot is most appropriate.
