The "naoshi-jin" (corrector) gave fine paper and thin paper to worthy people for the thousand-day kemari prayer completion, and gave costumes to the samurai retainers. This is indeed one type of reward. The late ■-sō Zen monk also, when there was kemari at Enman-in, kicked skillfully and received as a prize an ox called "yukiuchi" (snow-beater), as Masaiku related. In recent years, since there are no rewards given for skillful play, either publicly or privately, there would be no point at all in cultivating good or bad techniques.
