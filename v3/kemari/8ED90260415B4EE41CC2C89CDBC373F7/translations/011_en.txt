One makes a peak below and casts one's eyes on the ball, returning and coordinating to both left and right to kick - this is an extremely demanding and important foot technique. To let the ball pass by and return with body and waist held straight is quite easy. The left return is particularly beautiful, being a foot technique that includes posture and bowing gestures. It should be executed very carefully. For the left return ball, one decides in advance to return and kicks up. Place the right foot not in the path of the left foot's emergence, use the left foot to emerge, place the right foot and finish the return with the left foot alone, handling both left and right sleeves beautifully so they harmonize and resonate, returning together to kick. This is an unprecedented foot technique where one doubles the body and returns when the ball has retreated. The footwork at this time should be particularly secret. The right return ball, while being the same thing, has no coordination and is also easy. Regarding that retreated right edge, also kicking a ball high up at the eaves by turning toward a right return and stepping with footwork going "ton-ton" appears quite interesting. At that time, one should gradually step the three-beat footwork backward and finish the return with the third step to kick. Also, when the ball hits a lower branch that has retreated and moves slightly backward, one returns...
