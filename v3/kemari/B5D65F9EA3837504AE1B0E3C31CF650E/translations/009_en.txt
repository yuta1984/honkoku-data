There is a renowned kemari court called Hatake no niwa (Field Garden). When I saw it in my youth long ago, there were four old cherry trees. Emperor Go-Fushimi's kemari games were also played at this venue. There, they dug to a depth of one jō, buried salt five shaku deep at the bottom, and placed well-sifted soil on top. Even during droughts, there is moisture, and [unclear reference to ash]. Even during long spring rains, if one waits for clear intervals, the water drains quickly and kemari gatherings can soon be held. Later, when the Okamoto Lay Priest Regent's household constructed an elaborate garden, upon inspection, I saw that they similarly dug about one jō deep, laid roof tiles at the bottom, and placed sifted soil on top. The household servants were discussing in the garden that the wood was handled by the magistrate. This method of water drainage is also supreme. Though it is a pure matter, it is said that Fifth Avenue Major Counselor Kunitsuna rose in rank because he excellently supervised kemari courts. The details are separate matters, so there is no need to record them. What is called "net" refers to hemp woven together, dyed blue, and hung and placed beside screens or standing portable screens. There should be secret teachings about this.
