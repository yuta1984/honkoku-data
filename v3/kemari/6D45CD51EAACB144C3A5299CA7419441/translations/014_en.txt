Even when the timing doesn't match, one should keep stepping back and forth, floating up and wandering around. Then, when it becomes time (to kick the ball), one should make a sound, step forward, and perform this three-beat technique. One must never step twice with the same foot. When this happens, it is said that the footwork is incorrect.

One: Regarding the extended foot technique
The extended foot technique involves putting one's heart into following the falling ball, throwing one's body forward, advancing the foot, bending the knee, and pulling the shoe to

kick. The way of extending is: if one has advanced and positioned the right foot first, when kicking, one braces with the left foot and kicks with the right. One puts strength into the kicking foot and throws the body forward. Stopping the body and extending only the foot is not at all the extended foot technique—it is bad footwork. If one extends well, the next foot should necessarily return and face to kick. Kicking while remaining extended is terrible.
