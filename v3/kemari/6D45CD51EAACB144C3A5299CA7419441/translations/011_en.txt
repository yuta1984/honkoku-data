【Right Page】
If one abandons one's own natural abilities and poorly understands the learning of others' strengths, it can become a great mistake.

One: Regarding physical deportment, first it is good to have no bad habits. The eyes should not look frightening, the facial expression should be approachable yet not frivolous, the hand posture should be neither rigid nor unsightly, the body should be supple without clenching the fists, the torso should be straight without being stiff, the chest should be held naturally without being shortened, the waist should not crouch low, the feet should be positioned weakly, and when kicking the ball one should avoid pigeon-toed or bow-legged stances, with the feet being light.

【Left Page】
Overall, the body should be slightly upright, with the head slightly forward and the feet positioned toward the back when standing in formation. During practice, chasing around and kicking in a wide garden or such places is improper. If one does so, the movements inevitably become crude, crouching low and developing bad habits. In this way, one cannot kick well. From the waist up should be supple, from the waist down should be straight and strong. The ball going left and right is due to weak feet that turn inward or outward. Hand position is of primary importance. One should well understand this principle...
