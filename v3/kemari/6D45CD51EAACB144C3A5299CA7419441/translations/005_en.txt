If it is a narrow garden, one should plant trees calculating appropriately in general proportion. The terrain should be prepared without elevation differences, without stones, and not hard. If trees are planted after the garden construction is completed, it may damage the garden. Therefore, generally speaking, garden construction should be done to a good degree, and trees should be planted first.

1. Regarding Suspension Trees (Kake-gi)
The formal kake consists of cherry, willow, cockscomb tree, and pine. Trees may be planted with spacing of up to 2 jō 2-3 shaku between them. In the past,

there were exceptionally wide [spacings] as well, but recently this is not done.
However, the size of trees and spacing should be calculated appropriately according to the width of the garden. For narrow gardens, trees may be planted with spacing of 1 jō 8-9 shaku between them. However, spacing less than 2 jō is outside what the rules prescribe, but even if it is merely an abbreviated arrangement, it must be planted out of necessity.
