Throughout the provinces, there is one magistrate for each district. This is called "naishо" and referred to as "danna-san" (master). Although they possess abundant wisdom as district magistrates, since it depends on each district's discretion, there are no fixed rules for the entire country, and everything is scattered and disorganized. There are many things that are completely incomprehensible to Japanese eyes. This is said to be the national custom. Also, in each district there is an elderly woman who stands under the danna-san and manages everything. This is called a "yarite" (procuress). This official wears one pouch

【Left Page】
in front as a precious item. Depending on how the district is managed, the contents of this pouch either fatten or shrink. Sometimes it swells because of good management, and sometimes it swells because of bad management. When the mouth of the pouch appears to smile, that person's mouth also appears to smile; when the pouch does not swell, that person's cheeks puff up. In any case, one might say that a yarite's leather purse without substance is as worthless as a kappa's head without water. However, this is something incomprehensible to Japanese eyes.
