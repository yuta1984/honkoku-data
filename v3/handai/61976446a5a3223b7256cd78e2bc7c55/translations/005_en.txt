Indeed, the sumo matches feature competitors in red, white, purple, and pale yellow, each vying with their own allure and fragrance. Purple overwhelms vermillion, while nocturnal yellow deceives white. Thirty-two forms and eighty varieties of appeal compete with each other in a hundred different charms.

As the referee gradually raises his fan, the spectators cry "Will the West win? No, no, will the East win?" and even the railings shake with the excitement of the partisan supporters. The patrons' commentary covers everything from beginning to end, and with each changing bout and its critiques, the author finds himself like one standing beneath a lighthouse in darkness, unable to tell whether this spectacle will meet with the approval of the distinguished audience. Today is the opening day.
